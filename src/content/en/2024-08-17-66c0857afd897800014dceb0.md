Bali continues to draw attention for over-tourism, particularly in South Bali, where excessive tourist numbers have led to overcrowding. Since 2023, Bali has been listed by the World Travel & Tourism Council as a hotspot for over-tourism, alongside Athens, Paris, and Phuket.

![峇里島南部已經過度旅遊（overtourism）了！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fe05333dc536a7417e3945911d0ae12b.png)

South Bali is already suffering from over-tourism!

In early 2024, Bali once again became a focal point when foreign tourists and local travelers were forced to walk to Ngurah Rai International Airport due to severe road congestion (the Christmas and New Year holiday period ranks as the second busiest peak season for crowds and traffic in Bali. Here’s a short video showing the situation on the highway to the airport on December 29, 2023). Indonesia's Minister of Tourism, Sandiaga Uno, acknowledged the excessive number of tourists in South Bali and plans to divert them to the northern, western, and eastern regions to alleviate pressure, with measures to be announced in September.

> Further reading: [Bali Actually Has Five Peak Seasons! How to Travel Smartly and Avoid the Crowds?](/blog/2024-06-04-665ef28dfd89780001adfa98/)

![峇里島南部包含西岸的長谷、水明漾、庫塔、金巴蘭、烏魯瓦圖；和東岸的努沙杜瓦、沙努爾。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_116100a5cd534e0ae2d1707ef11bbe1f.png)

South Bali includes the western coastal areas of Canggu, Seminyak, Kuta, Jimbaran, and Uluwatu, as well as the eastern coastal areas of Nusa Dua and Sanur.

Additionally, the Bali Airport Rail Link project is set to begin construction this September (refer to [Bali Transportation Improvement Plan: Bali Light Rail to Start Construction in September](/blog/2024-07-22-669e605afd8978000115575b/)). Authorities have issued warnings that severe traffic congestion may occur in the Kuta and Seminyak areas.
