## Major Overhaul of Indonesia's Visa Extension System

**Bottom line**: Starting **May 29, 2025**, Indonesia's visa extension process has shifted to a two-step system: 'online application + in-person verification.' First, go to evisa.imigrasi.go.id, select 'extend my visa,' and fill out the form online. Within about 2 business days, you'll receive a notification and must visit the nearest immigration office in person for fingerprinting and document verification. **Senior citizens, individuals with disabilities, pregnant and nursing women, and travelers in emergency situations** can apply for a fast-track lane, completing the process in a single visit.

Indonesia's immigration authority has officially implemented a new visa extension process starting May 29, 2025. This system combines online registration with in-person processing, applicable to immigration offices nationwide, including Bali, and affects tourists, long-term residents, business visa holders, and social-cultural visa holders.

## Overview of the New Process: Online + In-Person Combined

### Step One: Online Application

All visa extension applicants must first visit the official website [evisa.imigrasi.go.id](http://evisa.imigrasi.go.id) to register an account and select 'extend my visa.' The entire site is available in English and supports both mobile and desktop use.

### Step Two: In-Person Visit to Immigration Office for Verification

After completing the online application, applicants will receive a notification within a maximum of two business days. They must then visit the nearest immigration office in person to submit documents and undergo biometric procedures, such as photo-taking and fingerprinting.

---

## Special Circumstances and Fast-Track Service

Applicants in the following categories may apply for fast-track service:

- Senior citizens
- Individuals with disabilities
- Pregnant and breastfeeding women
- Travelers in emergency situations

In these cases, the entire process can be completed in a single visit, with immigration officers assisting in filling out documents and completing fingerprinting.

---

## Why Did the Indonesian Immigration Office Change the System?

The Indonesian government noted a sharp increase in illegal visa violations in early 2025, handling 2,201 cases from January to April—a 36.71% rise compared to 1,610 cases during the same period in 2024. The new system aims to better regulate visa usage and reduce illegal activities.

---

## What Documents Should Applicants Prepare?

Although the authorities do not require additional documents, you may need to answer the following questions during the actual process:

- What is your purpose of stay in Indonesia?
- Do you have a departure plan afterward?
- What is your residential address in Indonesia?
- Do you have proof of financial means?

It is recommended to bring a travel itinerary, accommodation proof, and return flight ticket for reference.

## Eligible Visa Types and Fees

The most common tourist visas are:

**Visa on Arrival (VoA)**

- Apply at the airport upon arrival
- Valid for 30 days
- Extension fee: IDR 500,000

**Electronic Visa on Arrival (eVoA)**

- Apply online in advance
- Also eligible for the new hybrid extension system
- Extension fee: IDR 500,000

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. If I've already extended my visa online, do I still need to visit the immigration office in person?

Yes, the new system combines online and in-person steps, so all visa extension applicants must visit the immigration office in person.

### 2. What questions will immigration ask me?

Officials may ask about your purpose of stay, duration, and financial capability in Indonesia. It's recommended to bring supporting documents.

### 3. Can someone else handle it for me?

Currently, you must go in person, especially for biometric data collection.

### 4. How many days do I have to complete the visa extension?

From online registration, you usually need to complete the verification step at the immigration office within 2 business days.

### 5. What happens if I don't follow the new rules?

Failure to comply may result in fines, detention, or deportation.
