## Family Travel Tips

### 1. **Climate & Clothing**

Located inland, Ubud has a cooler climate compared to the coastal areas, but during the summer months (June to August), it’s still dry with plenty of sunshine. During winter breaks, Bali is in its rainy season, so brief showers are common. We recommend packing the following for your kids:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hats and sunglasses
- High-SPF sunscreen
- Comfortable walking shoes (ideal for rice field hikes or exploring attractions)
- Rain gear/raincoat (to stay dry during light showers)
- Insect repellent (mosquitoes are more active during the rainy season)

If you plan to visit nearby mountainous areas (such as Mount Batur or Mount Kintamani), be prepared for cooler mornings and evenings with a light jacket or warm layers. For more details, check out our [Mount Batur Sunrise Trekking Guide](/blog/2024-10-18-66d31f77fd897800011851c1/).

### 2. **Health and Safety**

- **Mosquito Protection**: Ubud's natural environment has many mosquitoes. It's recommended to use locally purchased insect repellent and choose accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly. It's advisable to buy bottled water or use boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Prepare common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness medicine, and band-aids. For more details, refer to: [Essential Medications for Traveling to Bali, Indonesia? How to Buy Insect Repellent and Sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/) There are clinics in Ubud city, but for serious cases, it's recommended to go to international hospitals in southern Bali.

***

### 3. **Transportation and Getting Around**

Transportation in Ubud is mainly by private car. It's recommended to book reputable car rental services and prepare safety seats for younger children. Roads in Ubud city are narrow, and some attractions (like rice terraces or temples) require walking. It's advisable to choose lightweight baby carriers or strollers. If the child is young, avoid long car rides and choose attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel.

### 4. **Dining Options**

Ubud offers a diverse range of restaurants, from local cuisine to international dishes. Recommendations:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavy-flavored foods (like babi guling).
- Many restaurants offer kids' meals, and you can also opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the taste suits children.

### 5. **Cultural Etiquette**

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, where locals place great importance on traditional etiquette. Teach your children to respect local customs, such as:

- Wear a sarong and remain quiet when entering temples.
- Avoid touching religious items or statues without permission.
- Always ask for consent before taking photos of locals.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

**Is Ubud suitable for traveling with kids?**

Ubud is a family-friendly destination with rich cultural attractions, natural scenery, and kid-friendly facilities such as zoos, rice field views, and family-friendly restaurants.

**What kid-friendly activities are available in Ubud?**

We've compiled a list of 54 kid-friendly activities in Ubud. Click [here](/blog/2024-01-10-659cf4a5fd89780001d6fb69/) to see them.

**Are there accommodations in Ubud with water slides?**

Currently, there are none known. If you're specifically looking for family resorts with water slides, check out our list of 15 family resorts with water slides. Click [here](/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/) to view them.

**What's the weather like in Ubud? What clothes should I pack?**

Ubud has a warm and humid climate. It's advisable to pack light, breathable clothing, sun protection, and rain gear to handle sudden showers.

**Is the food in Ubud safe for children?**

Ubud offers a variety of restaurants serving both international and local cuisine. It's recommended to choose restaurants with good hygiene standards and be cautious about drinking water safety.

**Is transportation in Ubud convenient for traveling with children?**

For families, transportation in Ubud mainly consists of taxis/ride-hailing services and private car hires. It's recommended to opt for private car services for convenience and safety when traveling with children.

**Are there medical facilities in Ubud? What if a child gets sick?**

Ubud has clinics and pharmacies, but for serious cases it's recommended to go to international hospitals in southern Bali. Travel insurance is also advised.

**Do attractions in Ubud require tickets? Are there discounts for children?**

Most attractions require tickets, usually free for children under 6, with discounted rates for ages 6-12 at some places.

**Is Ubud stroller-friendly?**

Most areas in Ubud have uneven roads, so using a baby carrier or lightweight stroller is recommended, avoiding crowded or rough areas.
