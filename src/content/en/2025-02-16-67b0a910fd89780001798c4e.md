## Family Travel Tips

### 1. Climate and Attire

Ubud, located inland on Bali, enjoys cooler temperatures compared to the coastal areas. However, during the summer months (June to August), it remains dry with plenty of sunshine. In contrast, Bali's winter coincides with the rainy season, making brief showers a common occurrence during outings. It's advisable to prepare the following for children:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hat and sunglasses
- High SPF sunscreen
- Comfortable walking shoes (suitable for rice field hikes or exploring attractions)
- Rain gear/raincoat (to stay dry during light showers)
- Insect repellent (especially important during the rainy season when mosquitoes are more active)

If planning to visit surrounding mountainous areas (such as Mount Batur or Mount Kintamani), be prepared for significant temperature differences between day and night. Bring a light jacket or warm clothing. Friends in need can refer to the [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/blog/2024-10-18-66d31f77fd897800011851c1/) for more information.

### 2. Health and Safety

- **Mosquito Protection**: Ubud's natural environment has many mosquitoes. It's recommended to use locally purchased insect repellent and choose accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly. It's advisable to buy bottled water or use boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Prepare common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal medicine, motion sickness pills, and band-aids. For reference: [What essential medicines/products should you bring for a trip to Bali, Indonesia? How to buy insect repellent/sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/) There are clinics in Ubud city, but for serious cases, it's recommended to go to international hospitals in southern Bali.

***

### 3. Transportation & Getting Around

Transportation in Ubud primarily relies on private car hire. It's advisable to book reputable car services and prepare safety seats for younger children. Roads in Ubud town are relatively narrow, and some attractions (like rice terraces or temples) require walking, so opting for lightweight baby carriers or strollers is recommended. For families with very young children, avoid long car journeys and choose attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel time.

### 4. Dining Options

Ubud offers a diverse range of dining options, from local delicacies to international cuisine. Recommendation:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned foods (such as babi guling, roasted pork rice).
- Many restaurants offer kids' menus, and you can also opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the flavors suit your child's palate.

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, where locals place great importance on traditional etiquette. It's essential to teach children to respect local customs, such as:

- Wear a sarong and remain quiet when entering temples.
- Do not touch religious artifacts or statues without permission.
- Always ask for locals' consent before taking photos.

**What's the weather like in Ubud? What clothes should I pack?**

Ubud has a warm and humid climate. It's recommended to pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sun protection, and rain gear to handle sudden showers.

**Is the food in Ubud safe for children?**

Ubud offers a wide range of restaurants serving both international and local cuisine. It's advisable to choose establishments with good hygiene standards and to be cautious about drinking water safety.

**Is Ubud convenient for transportation? Is it suitable for traveling with children?**

For families visiting Ubud, transportation options mainly include taxis/ride-hailing services and private car hires. It's recommended to opt for private car hire services for easier travel with children and to ensure safety.

**Are there medical facilities in Ubud? What if my child gets sick?**

Ubud has clinics and pharmacies, but for serious cases it's recommended to go to international hospitals in southern Bali. We also advise purchasing travel insurance.

**Do attractions in Ubud require entrance fees? Are there discounts for children?**

Most attractions require an entrance fee, with children under 6 usually admitted free. Some places offer discounted tickets for children aged 6 to 12.

**Is Ubud stroller-friendly?**

Most areas in Ubud have uneven roads, so it's recommended to use a baby carrier or lightweight stroller, and avoid using them in crowded or rough areas.
