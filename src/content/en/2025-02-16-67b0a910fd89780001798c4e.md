## Family Travel Tips

### 1. **Weather and Clothing**

Located inland in Bali, Ubud has a cooler climate compared to the coastal areas. However, during the summer months (June to August), it is still the dry season with plenty of sunshine. During winter, Bali is in the rainy season, and short showers are common. It is recommended to prepare the following for your children:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hats and sunglasses
- High-SPF sunscreen
- Comfortable walking shoes (suitable for rice terrace hikes or exploring attractions)
- Rain gear/raincoat (to stay dry during light showers)
- Insect repellent (especially during the rainy season when mosquitoes are more active)

If planning to visit nearby mountainous areas (such as Mount Batur or Mount Kintamani), be prepared for cooler temperatures in the morning and evening by bringing a light jacket or warm clothing. For those interested, refer to the [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/blog/2024-10-18-66d31f77fd897800011851c1/).

### 2. Health and Safety

- **Mosquito Protection**: Ubud's natural environment has many mosquitoes. It's recommended to use locally purchased insect repellent and choose accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly. It's advisable to buy bottled water or use boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Prepare common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness medicine, and band-aids. For reference: [Essential Medications and Supplies for Bali Travel: How to Buy Insect Repellent and Sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/) There are clinics in Ubud city, but for serious cases, it's recommended to visit international hospitals in southern Bali.

### 3. Transportation and Mobility

Transportation in Ubud is primarily via private car hire. It's advisable to book reputable car services and prepare safety seats for younger children. Roads in Ubud city are narrow, and some attractions (like rice terraces or temples) require walking. It's recommended to bring lightweight baby carriers or strollers. For younger children, avoid long car rides and choose attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel.

### 4. Dining Options

Ubud offers a diverse range of dining options, from local cuisine to international dishes. Recommendations:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavy dishes (like babi guling, roasted suckling pig).
- Many restaurants offer kids' menus, and international chain restaurants are also a safe bet for child-friendly meals.

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, where locals place great importance on traditional etiquette. Teach children to respect local customs, such as:

- Wear a sarong and remain quiet when entering temples.
- Avoid touching religious items or statues without permission.
- Always ask for consent before taking photos of locals.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

**Is Ubud suitable for traveling with children?**

Ubud is a great destination for family travel, offering rich cultural attractions, natural scenery, and child-friendly facilities such as zoos, rice field views, and family-friendly restaurants.

**What other activities are suitable for children in Ubud?**

We've compiled a list of 54 activities in Ubud that are perfect for kids. Click [here](/blog/2024-01-10-659cf4a5fd89780001d6fb69/) to explore.

**Are there accommodations in Ubud with water slides?**

Currently, none are known. If you're specifically looking for family resorts with water slides, check out our curated list of 15 resorts. Click [here](/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/) for more details.

**What is the weather like in Ubud? What clothing should I prepare?**

Ubud has a warm and humid climate. It's recommended to pack light, breathable clothing, sun protection, and rain gear to prepare for sudden showers.

**Is the food in Ubud safe for children?**

Ubud offers many restaurants serving both international and local cuisine. It's advisable to choose establishments with good hygiene standards and be cautious about drinking water safety.

**Is transportation convenient in Ubud? Is it suitable for traveling with children?**

For families, transportation in Ubud mainly consists of taxis/ride-hailing services and private car hires. Opting for a private car service is recommended for convenience and safety when traveling with children.

**Are there medical facilities in Ubud? What if a child gets sick?**

Ubud has clinics and pharmacies, but for serious conditions, it's advisable to visit international hospitals in southern Bali. Purchasing travel insurance is also recommended.

**Do attractions in Ubud require entrance fees? Are there discounts for children?**

Most attractions require entrance fees, with children under 6 usually admitted free. Some places offer discounted tickets for children aged 6-12.

**Is Ubud stroller-friendly?**

Most areas in Ubud have uneven roads, so it's recommended to use a baby carrier or lightweight stroller, and avoid using them in crowded or rugged areas.
