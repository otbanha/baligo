At the end of April, we embarked on an 11-day free travel journey to Bali and Lombok. This trip remains unforgettable to this day. While planning this adventure, I referred to many travelogues and suggestions from experienced travelers, knowing that trip planning is no easy task. Therefore, I hope to take this opportunity to share some recommended spots we visited, along with short videos of each location, to help you better feel the local atmosphere and decide whether to include these spots in your own itinerary.

Our journey took us to five main areas: Jimbaran, Uluwatu, Ubud, the Gili Islands, and Kuta. In this article, I will focus on the first three areas—Jimbaran, Uluwatu, and Ubud—sharing recommended spots and personal reviews (these recommendations and reviews are purely personal opinions, and I welcome any differing thoughts for discussion).

## Recommended Spots in Bali

### Sunset Seafood Feast at Jimbaran Beach

![金巴蘭海鮮（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_37baf48952137cd3a9cb784f0511bf09.jpg)

Jimbaran Seafood (provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Recommendation Rating: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 2.5 hours**

Jimbaran Beach is renowned as one of the 'World's Top Ten Sunset Beaches,' and its scenery is truly impeccable. Whether it's the ocean view or the sunset, both are breathtakingly beautiful. However, the prices at the restaurants here can be quite hard to swallow. This was our first meal after landing, and the three of us ended up spending over USD$160, which left us marveling at how expensive it was.

While dining on the beach in the evening, bands will play around the tables and ask for tips, which can feel a bit like emotional blackmail. Therefore, if you plan to dine here, it's advisable to check restaurant reviews beforehand to decide whether it's worth it. The beauty of Jimbaran Beach is definitely worth seeing, but you need to be more cautious when choosing where to eat.

### GWK Cultural Park in Jimbaran

![神鷹廣場（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c27b44be953aec6137d489e2f5b0efa3.jpg)

Garuda Wisnu Kencana Square (Photo by Ting-Yu Yeh)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Stay: 3 hours**

GWK Cultural Park is a highly worthwhile attraction. The majestic statue of Vishnu, visible from the airport, is 30 meters taller than the Statue of Liberty, leaving a deep impression. The entire park is grand and solemn, offering a sense of tranquility and reverence as you stroll through it. Enjoying a coconut drink while exploring the park is a delightful experience. The souvenir shop inside is large and well-stocked, perfect for shopping enthusiasts. Additionally, the air-conditioned restrooms next to the park provide a refreshing escape from the heat. GWK Cultural Park is not only visually stunning but also well-equipped, making it a must-visit destination.

### Uluwatu Temple Kecak Fire Dance

![Kecak Fire Dance](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_96343c5b07b3ceae31d0c3e6b4fd9e97.jpg)

Kecak Fire Dance

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1.5 hours**

The Kecak Fire Dance performance at Uluwatu Temple narrates Balinese mythological stories, attracting nearly 2,000 spectators, creating a spectacular scene. While watching this performance, you can enjoy the captivating dance alongside the breathtaking crimson sunset, offering an unforgettable visual and cultural experience.

It’s recommended to familiarize yourself with the story outline beforehand to better immerse yourself in the performance; otherwise, you might find it boring if you don’t understand it. Note that the fire dance segment lasts only about 5 minutes during the climax at the end, so don’t confuse it with street performer fire dances in Taiwan.

The Kecak Fire Dance performance at Uluwatu Temple is a visual feast combining culture and natural beauty. While the fire dance portion is brief, the overall atmosphere and quality of the performance make it well worth watching.

### Uluwatu's Oneeighty Club Cliffside Bar

![Oneeighty Day Club（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3895f7963249a801fbac88cd56bf66a4.jpg)

Oneeighty Day Club (Photo provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Duration: 3.5 hours**

Oneeighty Club Cliffside Bar is an extravagant venue perched on a cliff, featuring a photogenic pool where you could easily spend 2 hours snapping pictures. The entrance fee can be offset against purchases, making it feel like good value for money. The food here is among the tastiest in the Uluwatu area and is highly recommended.

However, remember to wash your hands after swimming before handling food, as it's easy to contract 'Bali Belly' (traveler's diarrhea)—a lesson I learned the hard way during my visit.

Oneeighty Club Cliffside Bar attracts many visitors with its unique location and high-quality food, making it an excellent spot to relax and capture stunning photos. Just be mindful of hygiene to ensure it doesn't affect your travel experience.

### Ubud Monkey Forest

![Monkey Forest（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_861edde63ee2d4e8aebd1aed3d0f8a43.jpg)

Monkey Forest (Photo by Yeh Ting-Yu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 2 hours**

The Sacred Monkey Forest is a charming attraction filled with artistic statues, caves, and totems, making visitors feel like they're on an adventure. The number of monkeys here even surpasses the tourists, and their free-spirited behavior is fascinating—we witnessed smoking monkeys, cola-drinking monkeys, and even monkeys seemingly under the influence.

When visiting the Sacred Monkey Forest, it's essential to follow the park's monkey rules to avoid any attacks. Additionally, there's a fun service where you can pay USD$3 to have a monkey hold your phone for a selfie. Although it's a trick shot, the photos look incredibly realistic, adding a lot of fun to your trip.

The Sacred Monkey Forest is a culturally rich and surprising destination, perfect for animal lovers and adventure seekers.

### Penglipuran Village in Ubud

![彭里普蘭村莊（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3be9f1e248a2509af6c7a87483488ba1.jpg)

Penglipuran Village (Photo provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Duration: 2 hours**

Penglipuran Village is renowned as the cleanest village in the world. Every household here is open to visitors, allowing tourists to step inside and gain a deeper understanding of the locals' daily lives and simple customs. The architecture and street scenes in the village are highly distinctive, making every shot a picturesque moment, especially the village's iconic split gates, which are a must-see photo spot.

As most visitors here are locals, the village maintains an authentic local charm without excessive commercialization. The villagers are friendly and hospitable, offering a warm sense of community.

Penglipuran Village is a highly recommended destination, where you can not only enjoy beautiful natural scenery but also experience genuine local culture, making it a rich and unforgettable travel experience.

### Tegallalang Rice Terraces in Ubud

![Tegallalang Rice Field（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_273e9bca32d9e28d429843236d543aab.jpg)

Tegallalang Rice Field (Photo by Yeh Ting-Yu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Duration: 2.5 hours**

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, the Tegallalang Rice Terraces are a stunning UNESCO World Heritage site. Here, you can marvel at the cascading rice fields and even experience the thrill of a swing ride. Although the 2-minute swing ride costs around USD$13, the breathtaking photos you can take here, especially in a long dress, make it worth every penny.

Additionally, there are numerous other photo spots, such as bird nests and heart-shaped decorations, perfect for capturing memorable moments. However, be mindful that the high humidity might cause headaches after prolonged exposure, so it's advisable to take breaks and stay hydrated.

The Tegallalang Rice Terraces not only boast magnificent scenery but also offer a variety of experiential activities, making it an excellent journey that combines nature and adventure, well worth a visit.

### Ubud Coffee Plantations

**Recommendation Index: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1 hour**

In Ubud, there are many civet coffee plantations. We chose one recommended by our driver, which offered a tasting experience of 16 different flavors of tea and coffee. The tasting experience was indeed enjoyable, allowing us to try a variety of beverages with different flavors.

However, after returning home and doing some research, we found that these plantations have received quite a few negative reviews. The main issue is that the products they sell are not consistent with the ones tasted during the experience. Therefore, it is recommended to do some homework and check related reviews before visiting these coffee plantations to avoid unpleasant shopping experiences.

### Ubud Jungle Gym

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1.5 hours**

The jungle gym at Ubud Wapa Di Ume offers an extremely unique fitness experience with a 360-degree panoramic view of Ubud's natural landscape. Nestled between rice fields and jungle, it allows you to exercise amidst stunning natural beauty—an experience I consider truly rare!

Although the gym equipment is limited, the low foot traffic means you can practically have the place to yourself, enjoying a private and tranquil workout space. This unique atmosphere makes the entire fitness process more comfortable and focused.

The jungle gym at Ubud Wapa Di Ume not only provides fitness facilities but also allows you to relax your mind and body in a natural setting, making it an ideal spot to escape the hustle and bustle and find peace.
