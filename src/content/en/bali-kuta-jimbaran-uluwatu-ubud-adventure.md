At the end of April, we embarked on an 11-day free travel journey to Bali and Lombok. This trip remains unforgettable to this day. While planning this journey, I referred to many travelogues and suggestions from experienced travelers, knowing that planning a trip is no easy task. Therefore, I hope to take this opportunity to share some recommended spots we visited, along with short videos of each location, to help everyone better feel the local atmosphere and decide whether to include these spots in their own itineraries.

During this trip, we visited five main areas: Jimbaran, Uluwatu, Ubud, the Gili Islands, and Kuta. In this article, I will focus on the first three areas—Jimbaran, Uluwatu, and Ubud—sharing recommended spots and personal reviews (these recommendations and reviews are purely personal opinions, and I welcome discussions and exchanges of different ideas).

## Recommended Spots in Bali

### Sunset Seafood Dinner at Jimbaran Beach

![金巴蘭海鮮（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_37baf48952137cd3a9cb784f0511bf09.jpg)

Jimbaran Seafood (provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Recommendation Index: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Stay Duration: 2.5 hours**

Jimbaran Beach is renowned as one of the 'Top Ten Sunset Beaches in the World,' and its beauty is truly unparalleled. Whether it's the ocean view or the sunset, everything is breathtakingly stunning. However, the prices at the restaurants here can be a bit hard to swallow. This was our first meal after landing, and the three of us ended up spending over USD$160, which left us marveling at the high cost.

While dining on the beach in the evening, bands often play around the tables and ask for tips, which can feel a bit like emotional extortion. Therefore, if you plan to dine here, it’s advisable to check restaurant reviews beforehand to decide whether it’s worth it. The scenery at Jimbaran Beach is definitely worth seeing, but you’ll need to be more cautious when choosing where to eat.

### GWK Cultural Park in Jimbaran

![神鷹廣場（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c27b44be953aec6137d489e2f5b0efa3.jpg)

Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park (Photo provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 3 hours**

GWK Cultural Park is a must-visit attraction. The majestic statue of Vishnu, visible even from the airport, stands 30 meters taller than the Statue of Liberty, leaving a lasting impression. The entire park is grand and solemn, offering a sense of tranquility and reverence as you stroll through it. Sipping coconut water while exploring the park is a delightful experience. The souvenir shop inside is vast and well-stocked, perfect for shopaholics. Additionally, the air-conditioned restrooms next to the park provide a refreshing escape from the heat. GWK Cultural Park is not only visually stunning but also well-equipped, making it an unmissable destination for travelers.

### Uluwatu Temple Kecak Fire Dance

![Kecak Fire Dance](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_96343c5b07b3ceae31d0c3e6b4fd9e97.jpg)

Kecak Fire Dance

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1.5 hours**

The Kecak Fire Dance at Uluwatu Temple narrates Balinese mythological tales, attracting nearly 2,000 spectators with its grand spectacle. While watching this performance, you can enjoy both the captivating dance and the stunning crimson sunset, creating an unforgettable visual and cultural experience.

It's recommended to familiarize yourself with the story outline beforehand to better immerse yourself in the performance; otherwise, you might find it boring if you don't understand what's happening. Note that the fire dance segment lasts only about 5 minutes during the climax at the end—don't confuse it with street performer fire shows in Taiwan.

The Kecak Fire Dance performance at Uluwatu Temple is a visual feast combining culture and natural beauty. Although the fire dance portion is brief, the overall atmosphere and quality of the performance make it well worth watching.

### Uluwatu Oneeighty Club Cliffside Bar

![Oneeighty Day Club（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3895f7963249a801fbac88cd56bf66a4.jpg)

Oneeighty Day Club (provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 3.5 hours**

Oneeighty Club Cliffside Bar is an extravagant venue perched on a cliff, featuring a photogenic pool that can easily occupy two hours of your time just for snapping pictures. The entrance fee can be offset against your purchases, making it feel like good value for money. The food here is among the tastiest in the Uluwatu area and is definitely worth trying.

However, remember to wash your hands after swimming before handling food to avoid contracting 'Bali Belly' (traveler's diarrhea), a lesson I learned the hard way during my visit.

Oneeighty Club Cliffside Bar attracts numerous visitors with its unique location and high-quality food. It's an excellent spot to relax and capture stunning photos, but be mindful of hygiene to ensure a pleasant travel experience.

### Ubud Monkey Forest

![Monkey Forest（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_861edde63ee2d4e8aebd1aed3d0f8a43.jpg)

Monkey Forest (Photo by Ting-Yu Yeh)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 2 hours**

The Sacred Monkey Forest is a quaint and charming attraction filled with artistic statues, caves, and totems, making visitors feel like they're on an adventure. The number of monkeys here even surpasses the tourists, and their free-spirited behavior is fascinating. We witnessed some peculiar scenes, including monkeys smoking, drinking cola, and even appearing to be 'high.'

When visiting the Sacred Monkey Forest, it's essential to follow the park's monkey rules to avoid any attacks. Additionally, there's a fun service where you can pay USD$3 to have a monkey hold your phone and take a selfie. Although it's a trick shot, the photos look incredibly realistic and add a lot of fun to the trip.

The Sacred Monkey Forest is a culturally rich and surprising destination, perfect for animal lovers and adventure seekers.

### Penglipuran Village, Ubud

![彭里普蘭村莊（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3be9f1e248a2509af6c7a87483488ba1.jpg)

Penglipuran Village (Photo provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Stay: 2 hours**

Penglipuran Village is renowned as one of the cleanest villages in the world. Every household here is open to visitors, allowing tourists to step inside and gain a deeper understanding of the locals' daily lives and simple way of living. The architecture and street scenes in the village are incredibly distinctive, making every shot a picturesque moment. The village's iconic split gates are a must-see photo spot.

Since most visitors are locals, the village retains an authentic charm without excessive commercialization. The villagers are friendly and hospitable, offering a warm sense of community.

Penglipuran Village is a highly recommended destination, where you can not only enjoy beautiful natural scenery but also experience genuine local culture. It’s a rich and unforgettable travel experience.

### Tegalalang Rice Terrace

![Tegallalang Rice Field（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_273e9bca32d9e28d429843236d543aab.jpg)

Tegalalang Rice Field (Photo by Yeh Ting-Yu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Duration: 2.5 hours**

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, the Tegalalang Rice Terrace is a stunning UNESCO World Heritage site. Here, you can marvel at the cascading rice terraces and even try the thrilling swing activity. Although the 2-minute swing experience costs around USD$13, the breathtaking photos you can take here, especially in a long skirt, make it worth every penny.

Additionally, there are numerous other photo spots, such as bird nests and heart-shaped decorations, perfect for capturing memorable moments. However, be mindful that the high humidity can sometimes cause headaches, so it’s advisable to take breaks and stay hydrated.

The Tegallalang Rice Terraces not only boast stunning scenery but also offer a variety of experiential activities, making it an excellent journey that combines nature and adventure, well worth a visit.

### Ubud Coffee Plantation

**Recommendation Index: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1 hour**

In Ubud, there are many civet coffee plantations. We chose one recommended by our driver, which offered a tasting experience of 16 different types of tea and coffee. The tasting experience was indeed enjoyable, allowing us to try a variety of flavors in one go.

However, upon returning home and doing some research, we found that these plantations have quite a few negative reviews. The main issue is that the products they sell do not match the samples tasted. Therefore, it is advisable to do your homework and check related reviews before visiting these coffee plantations to avoid an unpleasant shopping experience.

### Ubud Jungle Gym at Hotel

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1.5 hours**

The jungle gym at Ubud Wapa Di Ume offers an extremely unique fitness experience with a 360-degree panoramic view of Ubud's natural landscape. Nestled between rice fields and jungle, it allows you to exercise amidst stunning natural scenery—an experience I consider truly rare!

Although the gym equipment is limited, the low foot traffic means you can practically have the place to yourself, enjoying a private and tranquil workout space. This unique atmosphere makes the entire fitness process more comfortable and focused.

The jungle gym at Ubud Wapa Di Ume not only provides fitness facilities but also allows you to relax your mind and body in a natural setting, making it an ideal spot to escape the hustle and bustle and find peace.
