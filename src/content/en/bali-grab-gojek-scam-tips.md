When traveling in Bali, many tourists opt for ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek for short-distance transportation. These services, booked through apps, offer the advantage of transparent pricing. However, tourists sometimes encounter unscrupulous drivers that can turn their journey unpleasant. Here are two real stories sharing common ride-hailing tricks, hoping everyone can be more cautious during their travels to avoid similar troubles.

## Story 1: Airport Price Hike Trick

[Provided by Mr. Hsu, owner of Mr. Lobster Restaurant](https://www.facebook.com/hsu.dick.7) The owner of Mr. Lobster Restaurant often helps Taiwanese tourists who haven’t downloaded ride-hailing apps to book rides. Once, a guest in Canggu needed to go to the airport and asked for his assistance. The owner booked the first car through Grab, which responded quickly, but upon seeing the destination was the airport, the driver bypassed Grab and directly called the owner, demanding IDR 400,000, while the system quoted around IDR 100,000. The owner was furious and immediately canceled the order. He booked a second car, only to face the same situation—this time the driver asked for IDR 300,000. The owner canceled again and finally found a driver who didn’t hike the price on the third attempt, but the whole process dragged on for nearly an hour.

Additionally, another time, a guest arrived at the restaurant via Gojek, and the driver called the owner demanding a commission. The owner ignored it, and the driver, not receiving the commission, later left one-star reviews for the restaurant on Google Maps using two different accounts.

**Tips and Advice**: In Bali, especially when in a hurry, such as heading to the airport, always book ride-hailing services in advance and be prepared to book multiple times if necessary. Most drivers are good, but there are a few unscrupulous ones—stay vigilant.

## Story 2: Charter Car Sales Pitch Trick

[Provided by Ms. Yeh Ting-Yu](https://www.facebook.com/groups/baligo/permalink/1546686959211877/) Ms. Yeh and her friends had an unpleasant experience during their first time using Grab. After getting in the car, the driver was overly enthusiastic, constantly chatting with them and steering the conversation toward nearby attractions and activities, aggressively promoting charter car services in an attempt to bypass Grab’s transparent pricing system.

That day, they only planned to visit the Tegallalang Rice Terrace in the afternoon to play on the swings, so they politely declined the driver’s offer. When it was time to pay, the driver claimed he had no change and suggested they let him drive them back, offsetting the return fare with the change. They agreed, but on the return trip, the driver suddenly claimed the fare was three times the original amount. When questioned, he blamed traffic congestion, but checking the real-time fare on the Grab App showed no difference from the initial trip.

After sensing their skepticism, the driver began to lower his price, only to eventually revert to the original fare and insist on taking them back. The tourists stood their ground, demanding their exact change, prompting the driver to retrieve the coins from his bag. This encounter left them questioning the integrity of some drivers, though fortunately, subsequent drivers provided satisfactory service.

**Tips and Recommendations**: When using ride-hailing services like Grab, remain vigilant if drivers attempt to upsell package deals. If a driver insists on cash payment, avoid falling into the trap of not receiving exact change. Additionally, documenting any unpleasant experiences and promptly reporting them to the platform can help improve service quality.
