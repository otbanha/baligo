When traveling in Bali, many tourists opt for ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek for short-distance transportation. These services, booked through apps, offer the advantage of transparent pricing. However, tourists sometimes encounter unscrupulous drivers that can turn their journey unpleasant. Here are two real stories sharing common ride-hailing tricks, hoping everyone can be more cautious during their travels to avoid similar troubles.

## Story 1: Airport Price Hike Trick

[Provided by Mr. Hsu, owner of Mr. Lobster Restaurant (https://www.facebook.com/hsu.dick.7)] The owner of Mr. Lobster Restaurant often helps Taiwanese tourists who haven't downloaded ride-hailing apps to book rides. Once, a guest in Canggu needed to go to the airport and asked him to book a ride. The owner booked the first car through Grab, which responded quickly, but upon seeing the destination was the airport, the driver contacted the owner directly instead of through Grab, demanding IDR 400,000 while the system quoted around IDR 100,000. The owner was furious and immediately canceled the booking. He tried again, but the second driver asked for IDR 300,000. After canceling again, he finally found a driver who didn't hike the price on the third attempt, but the whole process took nearly an hour.

Additionally, once when a guest arrived at the restaurant via Gojek, the driver called the owner demanding a commission. The owner ignored him, and the driver, not receiving the commission, later left one-star reviews for the restaurant on Google Maps using two different accounts.

**Tips and Advice**: In Bali, especially when in a hurry, such as going to the airport, always book your ride-hailing service in advance and be prepared to book multiple times if necessary. Most drivers are good, but there are a few unscrupulous ones, so be cautious.

## Story 2: Car Rental Upsell Trick

[Provided by Ms. Yeh Ting-Yu (https://www.facebook.com/groups/baligo/permalink/1546686959211877/)] On their first time using Grab, Ms. Yeh and her companions had an unpleasant experience. After getting in the car, the driver was overly enthusiastic, constantly chatting with them and steering the conversation towards nearby attractions and activities, strongly recommending car rental services, trying to bypass Grab's transparent pricing system.

That day, they only planned to visit the Tegallalang Rice Terrace Swing in the afternoon, so they politely declined the driver's offer. When it was time to pay, the driver claimed he had no change and suggested they let him drive them back, offsetting the fare with the change. They agreed, but on the return trip, the driver suddenly tripled the fare. When questioned, he blamed traffic congestion, but checking the real-time fare on the Grab App showed no difference from the initial trip.

After sensing their skepticism, the driver began to haggle with himself and eventually quoted the original price, insisting on taking them back. The tourists stood their ground, demanding their exact change, prompting the driver to reluctantly retrieve the coins from his bag. This encounter left them questioning the integrity of some drivers, though fortunately, subsequent drivers provided satisfactory service.

**Tips and Recommendations**: When using ride-hailing services like Grab, remain vigilant if drivers attempt to upsell private car rentals. If a driver insists on cash payment, avoid falling into the trap of not receiving exact change. Additionally, document any unpleasant experiences during your trip and promptly report them to the platform to help improve service quality.
