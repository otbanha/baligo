**Bottom line**: The cheapest months to visit Bali are the **low season from January to April and early October to mid-December** (excluding holidays like Lunar New Year). During these periods, flights and accommodations are discounted, and attractions are less crowded. Three key money-saving tips: **rent a scooter or share a private car** instead of taking taxis, eat at **local eateries (Warung)** instead of tourist restaurants, and **book water activities and spa packages online** for discounted rates.

Dreaming of Bali but worried about the cost? By mastering the best travel timing and money-saving tricks, you can enjoy the 'Island of the Gods' on a small budget. This guide breaks down the cheapest months, accommodation deals, transportation options, and food choices, so you can experience Bali deeply even in the low season.

## **Key Bali Travel Expenses**

When planning a low-budget Bali trip, consider these major expenses:

- **Flights**: Book early and be flexible with dates to save the most.
- **Accommodation**: Lowest rates during the low season from January to May and September to early December.
  (Excluding holidays like Lunar New Year; see [here](/en/blog/bali-peak-season-travel-tips/))
- **Transportation**: Renting a scooter is cheapest, or consider a private car with driver, ideal for groups.
- **Food**: Local eateries (Warung) and street stalls offer the best value.
- **Activities**: Book water sports or spa packages online for discounts.
- **Miscellaneous**: Budget for souvenirs, tips, and parking fees.

## **Bali Low Season: The Most Affordable Months**

![峇里島淡季：最划算的月份](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7d538c747ad9deaaf455df6252953555.png)

### **Benefits of the Low Season**

- Low season: **January to April, early October to mid-December**
- **Fewer crowds**: Attractions are quieter, perfect for slow travel.
- **Lower prices**: Hotels, flights, and activities are discounted.

### **Rainy Season Travel Tips**

- Rain is **mostly brief showers** and won't affect most plans.
- Greener scenery and fresher air.
- Pack a lightweight raincoat or waterproof jacket.

***

## **Accommodation Deals & Booking Tips**

- **Off-peak discounts**: Many hotels and villas offer off-season deals.
- **Booking platforms**: Lock in deals early on sites like Agoda.
- **Best timing**: February–May and September–early December see noticeably lower rates.

## **Money-Saving Flight Booking Tips**

- **Book early**: At least 6 months in advance can save you a lot.
- **Snag deals at online travel fairs**: [China Airlines ITF Online Travel Fair](https://2025ttektf.china-airlines.com) runs until November 20, 2025, with Taipei–Bali flights starting from USD$382.
- **Avoid peak seasons**: June–August summer break, late December Christmas–New Year holidays, and Lunar New Year see the highest fares.
- **Fly on weekdays**: Tuesdays and Wednesdays are usually cheaper than weekends.
- **Connecting flights**: Transiting via Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, Bangkok, or Vietnam is more budget-friendly than direct flights.

## **Affordable & Exciting Activities**

![平價又精彩的旅遊活動](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8d0c0ccb0c0ee3d51d0ff197bed234ac.png)

- **Cultural experiences**: Visit Tanah Lot and Uluwatu Temple with low entrance fees.
- **Dance performances**: Opt for discounted combo tickets for Kecak fire dance shows.
- **Local markets**: Ubud Traditional Market is a paradise for cheap souvenirs and snacks.
- **Free beaches**: Most beaches like Kuta, Seminyak, and Nusa Dua are free to enter.
- **Water activities**: Book package tours online in advance for better deals.

## **Bali Budget Eats & Daily Expenses**

- **Local Warungs**: Authentic Nasi Goreng and satay skewers at half the price of tourist restaurants.
- **Street Stalls**: Cheap, flavorful snacks with local character.
- **Save on Drinks**: Bring a reusable water bottle and refill for free at your accommodation or cafes.
- **Snack Shopping**: Buy affordable fruits and nuts at supermarkets or traditional markets.

## **Money-Saving Transport Tips in Bali**

- **Rent a Scooter**: Around IDR 100,000–250,000 per day—flexible and cheap.
- **Public Transport**: Buses and Bemo minibuses are the cheapest, but run infrequently.
- **Private Car Hire**: Best value for groups of 2 or more—includes a driver.

### **FAQ**

## **1. What is the cheapest time to visit Bali?**

The low season runs from January to May and September to early December, offering the cheapest accommodation and flights. However, avoid specific consecutive holidays.

## **2. Is it inconvenient to visit Bali during the rainy season?**

Rain is mostly brief showers that don't affect most itineraries, and the scenery is even more beautiful.

## **3. What are the cheapest ways to get around Bali?**

Renting a scooter is the most budget-friendly option. If traveling in a group, consider hiring a car with a driver.

## **4. Is Eating Cheap in Bali?**

Dining at a local Warung is very affordable, with a meal costing around USD$1–2.

## **5. How to Score Cheap Flights to Bali?**

Book tickets 6 months in advance, avoid peak season, and make good use of price comparison sites and connecting flights. **Don't miss out on online travel fair deals**—these great offers are too good to pass up!
