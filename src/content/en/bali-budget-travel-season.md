## **Bali Travel Budget Highlights**

When planning a budget trip to Bali, consider the following major expenses:

- **Flights**: Booking early and being flexible with dates saves the most money.
- **Accommodation**: Prices are lowest during the off-season from January–May and September–early December.
  (Excluding holidays like Lunar New Year, see [here](/blog/2024-06-04-665ef28dfd89780001adfa98/))
- **Transportation**: Renting a scooter is the most economical, or consider hiring a private car with a driver for groups.
- **Dining**: Local warungs and street food stalls offer the best value.
- **Activities**: Book water sports or spa packages online for discounts.
- **Miscellaneous**: Don’t forget to budget for souvenirs, tips, and parking fees.

## **Money-Saving Tips for Booking Flights**

- **Book Early**: Save significantly by booking at least 6 months in advance.
- **Take Advantage of Online Travel Fairs**: [China Airlines ITF Online Travel Fair](https://2025ttektf.china-airlines.com) runs until November 20, 2025, with Taipei-Bali flights starting at USD$382.
- **Avoid Peak Seasons**: Prices are highest during summer vacation (June–August), Christmas-New Year holidays (late December), and Lunar New Year.
- **Travel on Weekdays**: Flights on Tuesdays and Weddays are usually cheaper than weekends.
- **Consider Connecting Flights**: Flights via Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, Bangkok, or Vietnam are often more affordable than direct flights.

## **Affordable and Exciting Activities**

## **Budget-Friendly Food and Daily Expenses in Bali**

- **Local Warung Restaurants**: Enjoy authentic dishes like Nasi Goreng and satay skewers at prices half or less than tourist restaurants.
- **Street Food Stalls**: Affordable and full of local flavor.
- **Save on Drinks**: Bring your own water bottle and refill for free at accommodations or cafes.
- **Snacks**: Find affordable fruits and nuts at supermarkets or traditional markets.

- **Private Car Hire**: Most cost-effective for groups of 2 or more, including a driver.

## **FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions**

### **1. What is the cheapest month to visit Bali?**

The off-season from January to May and September to early December offers the cheapest accommodation and flights. However, avoid specific long holiday periods.

### **2. Is it inconvenient to visit Bali during the rainy season?**

Rain is mostly short showers and doesn't affect most itineraries. In fact, the scenery becomes even more beautiful.

### **3. What are the cheapest ways to get around Bali?**

Renting a scooter is the most economical. For groups, hiring a private car with a driver is a good option.

### **4. Is eating out in Bali affordable?**

Eating at local Warungs is very affordable, with meals costing around $1–2 USD.

### **5. How can I find cheap flights to Bali?**

Book tickets 6 months in advance, avoid peak seasons, and make use of comparison websites and connecting flights. Don’t miss out on great deals during **online travel fairs**!
