[This morning's USD exchange rate: 16550] Today's (April 12) **Bali** news summary is as follows:

### ✈️ Flight Alert: Volcanic Eruption Impact

- **Remote volcanic ash disruption**: Due to the ongoing eruption of **Mount Lewotobi Laki-Laki** on Flores Island, several airlines including Jetstar, Virgin Australia, and AirAsia have canceled or delayed flights between Australia and the outer islands since yesterday (4/12).
- **Airport status**: Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) is currently **operating normally**, but flight departures depend on each airline's assessment of ash cloud density along flight paths. Passengers scheduled to fly today are strongly advised to **check the latest flight status before heading to the airport**.

### 🗞️ Travel Practices & Social Trends

- **Waste Crisis and Appeal**: Bali's TPA Suwung landfill has stopped accepting organic waste, leading to incidents of garbage burning in some areas. Officials and lawmakers today issued a stern appeal to cease burning waste to prevent the release of toxic dioxins that could harm residents, especially pregnant women and children. They recommend temporarily storing waste at home and implementing proper sorting practices, urging against extreme measures.

### 🌤️ Weather Forecast

- **Today's Overview**: **Bali** will experience sunny skies turning to partly cloudy today.
- **Temperature and Rainfall**: Temperatures range between **26°C - 32°C**. The chance of precipitation is **25%**.

### 🚗 Transportation & Environment: Construction Zone Alert

- **Subway Construction Impact**: Due to subway construction, the main road from the airport to **Canggu** and **Seminyak** has seen an increase in construction barriers. During peak hours, it's recommended to allow at least 1.5 to 2 hours for travel time.
- **New Scooter Rental Regulations**: Police have reiterated that foreign tourists must possess a **valid International Driving Permit (IDP)** to ride scooters. Additionally, renting from unregistered individuals is strictly prohibited, as vehicles may be impounded at any time.
