### 🗞️ Today's Headlines: The 13th Penglipuran Village Cultural Festival Grandly Opens

- The island's most beautiful village hosts a major celebration: the 13th Penglipuran Village Cultural Festival has officially launched, featuring a stunning hundred-person traditional bamboo ensemble performance. This traditional village, once honored as the world's most beautiful and cleanest village, is now showcasing its annual cultural tourism event. The opening day featured a breathtaking hundred-person traditional bamboo instrument ensemble, a sacred Balinese traditional blessing parade, and a vibrant market of local handmade crafts and cuisine. Organizers say this year's festival focuses entirely on sustainable tourism and community cultural preservation, with events spanning several days—perfect for independent travelers currently vacationing on the island to add to their itinerary.
- 📄 Tourist Hot Topic: Strong Southeast Wind and Wave Safety, and Extreme Dry Season Land Temperature Drop
- Strong winds and waves in southern coastal waters; be cautious of ferry changes for island hopping: The Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysical Agency (BMKG) has issued a strong wind hazard warning. Due to the southeast monsoon, swells in southern coastal areas and inter-island channels are currently running at 1.5 to 2.5 meters, with strong hidden rip currents near shore. Independent travelers planning to take speedboats to Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, or Lombok today should confirm schedules with boat operators in advance, maintain a safe distance when taking photos on coastal rocks, and never cross red flag warning zones.
- Dry season cold spell continues, with morning lows in central and northern highlands dropping to 16°C: Affected by a strong dry cold air mass from the Australian continent, early morning temperatures in Bangli Regency and along the Kintamani volcano route have dropped to 16–19°C. The large temperature difference between day and night makes afternoon winds in the mountains feel biting cold. Independent travelers planning to rent cars or scooters to head north for sunrise over the next two days should dress in layers and bring windproof jackets to avoid catching a cold.
