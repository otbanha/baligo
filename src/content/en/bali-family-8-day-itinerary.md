***

**Key Highlights**: This is a real-life travelogue of a family of 6 adults and 1 child (2 years old) over eight days and seven nights, covering **Jimbaran, Uluwatu, Nusa Lembongan, Sanur, Ubud, Mount Batur sunrise, Tanah Lot, and Seminyak**. Two practical lessons: ① Visa on arrival is safer than e-visa (to avoid fines from filling errors; on-site queue took about 10 minutes, but credit card payments incur a 2% surcharge); ② **Baggage claim may take longer than visa processing** (they waited nearly 2 hours, missing the Jimbaran sunset), so plan your itinerary with buffer time.

Thanks to the "[Go Bali Go Facebook Group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/baligo)" for pre-trip help. I hope my info helps others too.

## Travel Info & Pre-Trip Preparation

Trip Dates: May 18 - May 25

This Bali trip was a family vacation with 6 adults and 1 child (a two-year-old boy).

### Flights

We chose China Airlines' direct flight, with round-trip tickets costing around USD$419 per person. Since we booked more than three months in advance, we got a good discount.

### Visa

We opted for a visa on arrival, which incurred an extra 2% fee when paying by card. We initially considered applying for an e-visa, but it required filling out a lot of information, and we saw reports of people being fined for writing 'TAIWAN' before departure. Worried about making a mistake, we decided to go with the visa on arrival instead. The process for the visa on arrival in Bali was relatively simple, with multiple counters and about five people in each line. We were lucky and completed the visa process in about ten minutes. However, our luck ran out at baggage claim—we waited nearly two hours for our luggage, missing the chance to watch the sunset in Jimbaran.

> [1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim Full Walkthrough](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

### SIM Card

We bought two XL Telecom SIM cards in advance from the highest-rated seller on Shopee. However, we heard that XL Telecom's signal is generally poor, so we decided to keep some budget to buy another SIM card locally. As it turned out, XL Telecom's signal was indeed very weak, making internet access nearly impossible. Later, we bought a Telkomsel SIM card locally—24GB for about USD$12—and finally got a stable connection.

### Currency Exchange

We exchanged New Taiwan Dollars for US Dollars in Taiwan first, then exchanged US Dollars for Indonesian Rupiah upon arriving in Bali. We chose exchange counters that provided receipts and were officially certified. In practice, the rates at all legitimate exchange counters were similar; only unlicensed shops without receipts offered better rates, but we were wary of scams and stuck with the official ones. We noticed that the exchange rate was best between noon and around 3 PM, and sometimes it worsened in the afternoon.

How to choose a legitimate exchange counter? Check out: [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/en/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)

### Airport to Jimbaran Hotel

We used Grab to book two cars, and the wait time was about 20 minutes. Checking prices on the spot, we found Grab cheaper than Gojek and also cheaper than the airport transfer booked in advance on Klook (a HiAce-style car costs about USD$23). Considering the visa-on-arrival process might cause delays, we opted to hail a ride on the spot. The total cost for two Grab cars was around USD$13, but you'll need to wait on-site—decide for yourself. In comparison, booking a private car for airport pickup is the priciest option, as the driver only makes one trip and rents the car, without being able to take on other routes. Unless you have other destinations to visit that same day, a private car is not recommended.

### Itinerary

Since we wanted to visit all the major attractions, we planned our itinerary in advance and then found a private driver to match our schedule.

## Saturday, May 18 - DAY 1: Exploring Jimbaran

We departed from Taiwan at 9:10 AM, and after a flight of over five hours, arrived in Bali at 2:40 PM.

### FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach

Upon arrival, we headed straight to FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach to drop off our luggage. This hotel is very close to Jimbaran Beach, just a short walk away. The rooftop features an infinity pool and a children's splash area, making it a great environment for family trips.

### Jimbaran Beach

We originally planned to watch the sunset at Jimbaran Beach, but due to a long wait for luggage at the airport, we missed the beautiful sunset. Still, the scenery on the beach was delightful, and we got to try the local specialty: grilled corn. This grilled corn is a sweet corn with a buttery flavor—sweet and delicious, leaving a lasting impression.

### Dinner - Lia Cafe

For dinner, we chose Lia Cafe, which charges an additional 10% service fee and accepts credit cards. While waiting for our food, a group of local singers performed songs based on your nationality, but they aggressively demanded tips and showed displeasure if we gave too little, which soured our dining experience. (Editor's note: This band is not affiliated with the restaurant. For their performance, a tip of 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah for your group would suffice and keep everyone happy!)

### Private Driver

We hired a private driver multiple times during our trip, including on May 19 and from May 21 to May 24. Initially, we searched online for recommended drivers, but most offered only small cars. Since we were a group of seven with luggage, we needed a larger vehicle. After posting our itinerary in an English-language Bali group, we found a driver who offered a 12-hour service at a price cheaper than what others charged for 8 hours. His fee covered everything: driver, fuel, tolls, and parking, plus a bottle of water per person each day. The driver was incredibly friendly, with 15 years of experience, and his driving skills were excellent—even on the narrowest roads. He spoke good English and answered our questions without being overly chatty, maintaining a professional demeanor. We were very satisfied with his service. On the last day, we asked for his contact info, and he gladly shared it. He even offered to introduce us to Chinese-speaking drivers if needed. For [Gdbokink driver](https://www.facebook.com/Bokink/), it was his first time driving guests who changed areas daily, but we were thoroughly pleased with his service.

## Post-Trip Reflections

This trip was mainly planned for the adults, but we still made time for water activities for both kids and adults every day. We didn't opt for high-end villas or resorts since our packed schedule left little time to enjoy them—no need to splurge! Instead, we chose well-reviewed, budget-friendly hotels.

If you're not particularly interested in temples, consider cutting some temple visits to free up time for massages, water fun, and shopping. However, we approached this trip as a once-in-a-lifetime experience, so we packed in all the famous sights. Overall, it was a rich and unforgettable journey. 😆

**Family Feedback**: Traffic in Bali is incredibly congested. While we managed to visit almost all the major attractions, the itinerary shouldn't be too tight. Aside from staying in a "haunted house," everything else was fine. Next time, we might skip some temples for more relaxing activities. As for shopping, the experience of inflated prices followed by haggling wasn't great.

**Would We Visit Bali Again?**: Bali's beaches are beautiful but fall slightly short compared to other island destinations. Still, its unique cultural landscape is well worth exploring! However, the world is vast, and we want to see every corner—so we probably won't return to Bali. But the memories will stay with us forever.

## 行程後記與心得

此次行程主要依大人為主，但每天還是有安排給小孩和大人玩水的時間。我們沒有選擇入住高級的villa或度假村，因為行程安排得很緊湊，沒時間好好享受，所以就不多花錢啦！我們主要選擇的是評價好、性價比高的飯店入住。

如果對廟宇不是特別感興趣，可以考慮刪減一些相關行程，這樣就會有更多的時間去享受按摩、玩水和逛街的樂趣。不過，我們這次是抱著可能一生只來一次的心態，硬是把所有知名景點都安排得滿滿的，整體而言是一次非常豐富而難忘的旅程。😆

**同行家人感受**：峇里島的交通真的非常壅塞，雖然這次幾乎把所有知名景點都遊覽了一遍，但行程不能排得太緊密。此次行程除了住到「鬼屋」以外，其他安排都還不錯。未來可以考慮刪減一些寺廟的行程，改為更放鬆的活動。至於購物的部分，感覺開價過高再讓人殺價的體驗並不太好。

**是否再訪峇里島**：峇里島的沙灘雖然美，但相比其他海島的沙灘還是略遜一籌。不過，峇里島獨具一格的人文風景還是非常值得一遊！然而，世界這麼大，我們想去探索世界各個角落，或許不會再訪峇里島，但是峇里島的回憶將永遠留在我們心中。
