## Travel Information & Preparation

Trip Dates: May 18 - May 25

This Bali trip was a family vacation with 6 adults and 1 child (a 2-year-old boy).

### Flights

We chose China Airlines' direct flight, with round-trip tickets costing around USD$420 per person. Booking more than three months in advance secured us a good deal.

### Visa

We opted for a visa on arrival, which incurs an additional 2% fee for credit card payments. Initially, we considered applying for an electronic visa, but it required filling out extensive information, and we saw reports of people being fined for listing TAIWAN before departure. Concerned about making mistakes, we decided to go for the visa on arrival instead. The process in Bali was relatively straightforward, with many counters available, each handling about five people at a time. We were lucky and completed the visa process in about ten minutes. However, our luck ran out when it came to retrieving our luggage—we waited nearly two hours, missing the chance to watch the sunset at Jimbaran.

> [1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

### SIM Cards

We pre-purchased two XL Telecom SIM cards from the highest-rated seller on Shopee. However, we heard that XL Telecom's signal is generally poor, so we decided to keep some budget aside for other SIM cards locally. Indeed, XL Telecom's signal was terrible, almost unusable. Later, we bought Telkomsel SIM cards locally, which cost about USD$12 for 24GB, finally providing us with stable internet access.

### Currency Exchange

We exchanged USD in Taiwan before heading to Bali, then exchanged it for Indonesian Rupiah locally. We chose exchange counters that provided receipts and certifications. In reality, the rates at legitimate exchange counters are quite similar, with only some unlicensed shops offering better rates. However, fearing scams, we stuck to legitimate counters. We noticed that the exchange rates were more favorable from noon to around 3 PM, sometimes worsening in the afternoon.

How to choose a legitimate currency exchange? Check out: [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)

### Airport to Jimbaran Hotel

We used Grab to book two cars, with a wait time of about 20 minutes. On-site searches showed that Grab was cheaper than Gojek and also cheaper than the airport transfer we pre-booked on Klook (about USD$23 for a van). Considering potential delays with the visa on arrival, we chose to book cars on the spot. The cost for two Grab cars was about USD$13, but you need to wait for the cars, so weigh your options. In comparison, airport pick-up by private car is the most expensive option, as the driver only makes one trip and rents the car, unable to take on other trips unless you have other destinations planned for the day, making it not recommended unless necessary.

### Itinerary

Since we wanted to visit all the famous attractions, we planned our itinerary in advance and then found a private driver to accommodate our schedule.

## Saturday, May 18th DAY 1 Exploring Jimbaran

Our flight departed from Taiwan at 9:10 AM, and after more than five hours of flying, we arrived in Bali at 2:40 PM.

### FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach

Upon arrival, we headed straight to FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach to drop off our luggage. This hotel is conveniently located close to Jimbaran Beach, just a short walk away. The rooftop features an infinity pool and a children's play area, making it an ideal spot for family vacations.

### Jimbaran Beach

We had planned to watch the sunset at Jimbaran Beach, but due to a long wait for our luggage at the airport, we missed the beautiful sunset. Nevertheless, the scenery at the beach was still delightful, and we enjoyed the local specialty of grilled corn. The corn here is sweet and buttery, leaving a lasting impression.

### Dinner - Lia Cafe

For dinner, we chose Lia Cafe, which charges an additional 10% service fee and accepts credit card payments. While waiting for our meal, a local band performed songs based on our nationality. However, they aggressively demanded tips, and their displeasure when given too little somewhat marred our dining experience. (Note: This band is not affiliated with the restaurant. Typically, a tip of 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah would suffice to keep everyone happy!)

## May 19th, Sunday DAY 2 Uluwatu Tour

### Breakfast - Bull's Coffee Jimbaran

Our first stop was Bull's Coffee Jimbaran, where we wanted to try their famous avocado sandwich, but unfortunately it wasn't available that day. This café accepts card payments and has a comfortable atmosphere, making it a great place to start the day.

### Breakfast - Warung Nasi Ayam Ibu Oki

Afterwards, we had another breakfast—Bali's famous chicken rice at Warung Nasi Ayam Ibu Oki. The chicken rice here was delicious and authentic, leaving a deep impression on us.

### Pantai Batu Barak

After breakfast, we headed to Pantai Batu Barak, a great spot for photography. The natural scenery here is stunning, perfect for taking photos and checking in.

### Pandawa Beach

Next, we went to Pandawa Beach. Although the beach itself wasn't particularly beautiful, we enjoyed kayaking here, which added a lot of fun to the experience.

### One Eighty Bali Cliff Bar

For lunch, we chose One Eighty Bali, a cliffside bar where the food is relatively affordable. We ordered quite a few dishes just to meet the minimum spend. Besides the delicious food, there's also an infinity pool where you can enjoy the stunning views while dining. It's advisable to book in advance, and they accept card payments.

We visited Besakih Temple, where the entrance fee was quite expensive at USD$10 (as it includes the mandatory guide fee). ## Post-Trip Reflections and Insights

This trip was primarily planned with adults in mind, but we still allocated time each day for both children and adults to enjoy water activities. We chose not to stay in high-end villas or resorts because our schedule was packed, leaving little time to fully enjoy such accommodations, so we decided not to spend extra money on them. Instead, we selected hotels with good reviews and high value for money.

If you're not particularly interested in temples, consider cutting back on related activities to free up more time for massages, water fun, and shopping. However, we approached this trip with a 'once-in-a-lifetime' mindset and packed our itinerary with all the famous spots. Overall, it was an incredibly rich and unforgettable journey. 😆

**Family's Take**: Bali's traffic is notoriously congested. While we managed to visit almost all the famous attractions this time, it's important not to schedule too tightly. Aside from staying in a 'haunted house', everything else was well arranged. In the future, we might consider reducing temple visits in favor of more relaxing activities. As for shopping, the experience of high initial prices followed by haggling wasn't particularly enjoyable.

**Would We Visit Bali Again?**: While Bali's beaches are beautiful, they fall slightly short compared to other island destinations. However, Bali's unique cultural landscape is definitely worth experiencing! That said, the world is vast, and we're eager to explore its many corners. We might not return to Bali, but the memories will forever remain in our hearts.
