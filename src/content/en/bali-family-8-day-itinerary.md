Facebook group member [Mei Chen](https://www.facebook.com/mei.co.nz/) shares her family's eight-day, seven-night Bali travel diary, specially provided for us to edit and repost:

***

**Key Highlights**: This is a real-life travel diary of a family of 6 adults and 1 child (2 years old) over eight days and seven nights, covering **Jimbaran, Uluwatu, Nusa Lembongan, Sanur, Ubud, Mount Batur sunrise, Tanah Lot, and Seminyak**. Two practical lessons: ① Visa on arrival is safer than e-visa (to avoid fines from filling in wrong info; on-site queue took about 10 minutes, but credit card payments incur an extra 2% fee); ② **Baggage claim may take longer than getting the visa** (they waited nearly 2 hours, missing the Jimbaran sunset), so plan your itinerary with buffer time.

Thanks to the "[Go Bali Go Facebook Group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/baligo)" for the pre-trip help. I hope my info can also be useful to everyone.

## Travel Info & Pre-Trip Preparation

Trip Dates: May 18 - May 25

This Bali trip was a family vacation with 6 adults and 1 child (a two-year-old boy).

### Flights

We chose China Airlines' direct flights, with round-trip tickets costing about USD$419 per person. Since we booked more than three months in advance, we got a good deal.

### Visa

We opted for a visa on arrival, which costs an extra 2% fee if paid by card. We initially considered an e-visa, but it required filling out a lot of information, and we saw reports before departure of someone being fined for writing 'TAIWAN' on the form, so we decided to go with the visa on arrival to avoid mistakes. The visa on arrival process in Bali was relatively straightforward, with multiple queue counters, each with about five people. We were lucky and completed the visa process in about ten minutes. However, our luck ran out at baggage claim—we waited nearly two hours for our luggage, missing the chance to watch the sunset in Jimbaran.

> [1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: Disembark → Customs → Baggage Claim Full Walkthrough](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

### SIM Card

We pre-ordered two XL SIM cards from the highest-rated seller on Shopee. However, we heard that XL's signal is generally poor, so we set aside a budget to buy another SIM card locally. As it turned out, XL's signal was indeed terrible, making internet access nearly impossible. We later bought a Telkomsel SIM card locally—24GB for about USD$12—and finally got a stable connection.

### Currency Exchange

We exchanged some US dollars in Taiwan first, then converted them to Indonesian rupiah upon arriving in Bali. We chose official exchange offices that provide receipts and are certified. In practice, the rates at all legitimate exchange offices were similar; only unlicensed shops without receipts offered slightly better rates, but we were worried about scams, so we stuck with the official ones. We noticed that rates were better from noon to around 3 PM, and sometimes worsened in the afternoon.

How to choose a legitimate exchange office? Check out: [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/en/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)

### Airport to Jimbaran Hotel

We booked two cars via Grab, with a wait time of about 20 minutes. On-the-spot pricing showed Grab was cheaper than Gojek and also cheaper than the airport transfer we pre-booked on Klook (a HiAce-style van was about USD$23). Considering potential delays with the visa on arrival, we decided to book on the spot. The total cost for two Grab cars was around USD$13, but you have to wait for the cars on-site—something to weigh. In comparison, booking a private airport transfer is the priciest option, as the driver only makes one trip and rents the car, with no other rides to combine. Unless you have other destinations to visit that day, a private transfer is not recommended.

### Itinerary

Since we wanted to visit all the major attractions, we planned our itinerary in advance and then hired a private driver to follow our schedule.

## Saturday, May 18 – Day 1: Exploring Jimbaran

We departed from Taiwan at 9:10 AM, and after a flight of over five hours, arrived in Bali at 2:40 PM.

### FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach

Upon arrival, we headed straight to FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach to drop off our luggage. The hotel is conveniently located within walking distance of Jimbaran Beach. The rooftop features an infinity pool and a children's splash area, making it a great choice for family trips.

### Jimbaran Beach

We originally planned to watch the sunset at Jimbaran Beach, but due to a long wait for our luggage at the airport, we missed the beautiful sunset. Still, the beach scenery was delightful, and we got to try the local specialty—grilled corn. The corn was sweet and buttery, delicious and memorable.

### Dinner – Lia Cafe

For dinner, we chose Lia Cafe, which charges an additional 10% service fee and accepts credit cards. While waiting for our food, a local band performed songs based on our nationality, but they aggressively demanded tips. Giving too little visibly upset them, which made the dining experience somewhat unpleasant. (Editor’s note: This band is not affiliated with the restaurant. Typically, a tip of 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah for the group is enough to keep everyone happy!)

### Private Driver

We hired a driver multiple times during our trip, including on May 19 and from May 21 to May 24. We initially searched online for recommended drivers, but most offered only small vehicles. Since we were a group of seven plus luggage, we needed a larger car. After posting our itinerary in an English-language Bali community group, we found a driver who provided 12-hour service at a price cheaper than what others charged for 8 hours. His rate included all costs—driver fee, fuel, tolls, and parking—and he even provided one bottle of water per person daily. The driver was very friendly, with 15 years of experience, and his driving skills were excellent, even on very narrow roads. His English was good enough to answer our questions, but he didn't chat excessively—very professional. We were extremely satisfied with his service. On the last day, we asked for his contact information, and he gladly shared it. He even offered to introduce us to Chinese-speaking drivers if needed. For [Gdbokink driver](https://www.facebook.com/Bokink/), this was his first time serving guests who changed areas daily, but we were very happy with his service.

## Post-Trip Reflections

This trip was mainly planned for adults, but we still set aside time each day for both kids and adults to enjoy the water. We didn't choose high-end villas or resorts because our schedule was packed, leaving little time to relax—so we didn't want to overspend! Instead, we opted for well-reviewed, cost-effective hotels.

If you're not particularly interested in temples, consider cutting some temple visits to free up more time for massages, water activities, and shopping. However, we approached this trip as a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity, so we packed in all the famous sights. Overall, it was a rich and unforgettable journey. 😆

**Family Feedback**: Traffic in Bali is really congested. Although we visited almost all the famous spots, the itinerary couldn't be too tight. Aside from staying in a 'haunted house,' everything else was fine. In the future, we might cut some temple visits and replace them with more relaxing activities. As for shopping, the experience of high initial prices followed by haggling wasn't great.

**Would We Visit Bali Again?**: While Bali's beaches are beautiful, they fall slightly short compared to those on other islands. However, Bali's unique cultural landscape is still worth a visit! But the world is vast, and we want to explore every corner—so we probably won't return to Bali. Still, the memories will stay with us forever.
