## Travel Info & Pre-Trip Prep

Trip dates: May 18 - May 25

This Bali trip was a family vacation with 6 adults and 1 child (a two-year-old boy).

### Flights

We chose China Airlines' direct flights, round-trip tickets cost about USD$419 per person. Since we booked more than three months in advance, we got a good deal.

### Visa

We opted for the visa on arrival and paid by card, which incurred an extra 2% fee. We initially considered applying for an e-visa, but it required filling out a lot of information, and we saw reports of people being fined for writing 'TAIWAN' incorrectly, so we decided to go with the visa on arrival to avoid mistakes. The visa-on-arrival process in Bali was relatively straightforward, with multiple queue counters, each with about five people. We were lucky and completed the visa process in about ten minutes. However, our luck ran out at baggage claim—we waited nearly two hours to get our luggage, missing the chance to watch the sunset in Jimbaran.

> [1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim Full Walkthrough](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

### SIM Card

We bought two XL SIM cards from a highly-rated seller on Shopee in advance. But since XL's signal is reportedly poor, we set aside some budget to buy another SIM card locally. As it turned out, XL's signal was indeed terrible, and we could barely use the internet. We later bought a Telkomsel SIM card locally—24GB for about USD$12—and finally got a stable connection.

### Currency Exchange

We exchanged New Taiwan Dollars for US Dollars in Taiwan first, then converted to Indonesian Rupiah upon arriving in Bali. We chose exchange offices that provided receipts and were certified. In reality, the rates at all legitimate exchange offices were similar; only unlicensed shops offered better rates, but we were worried about scams, so we stuck with reputable ones. We noticed that rates were best between noon and 3 PM, and sometimes worsened in the afternoon.

How to choose a legitimate exchange office? Check out: [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)

### Airport to Jimbaran Hotel

We booked two cars via Grab, with a wait time of about 20 minutes. A quick search showed that Grab was cheaper than Gojek and also cheaper than the airport transfer we pre-booked on Klook (a HiAce-style van was about USD$23). Given potential delays with the visa on arrival, we decided to book on the spot. The two Grab cars cost around USD$13 in total, but you have to wait for them—so weigh the pros and cons. In comparison, a private airport transfer is the most expensive option, as the driver only makes one trip and rents the car, with no other rides allowed. Unless you have other destinations to visit that day, a private transfer is not recommended.

### Itinerary

Since we wanted to visit all the major attractions, we planned our itinerary in advance and then hired a private driver to match our schedule.

## Saturday, May 18 – DAY 1: Exploring Jimbaran

We departed from Taiwan at 9:10 AM, and after a flight of over five hours, arrived in Bali at 2:40 PM.

### FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach

Upon arrival, we headed straight to FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach to drop off our luggage. This hotel is very close to Jimbaran Beach, within walking distance. The rooftop features an infinity pool and a children's splash area, making it an ideal choice for family trips.

### Jimbaran Beach

We originally planned to watch the sunset at Jimbaran Beach, but due to a long wait for luggage at the airport, we missed the beautiful sunset. Still, the beach scenery was delightful, and we enjoyed local grilled corn. The corn here is sweet, buttery, and unforgettable.

### Dinner – Lia Cafe

For dinner, we chose Lia Cafe, which charges an additional 10% service fee and accepts credit cards. While waiting for our food, a local band performed songs based on our nationality, but they aggressively demanded tips. Giving too little made them visibly unhappy, which soured our dining experience. (Editor's note: This band is not affiliated with the restaurant. A tip of 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah for the group is usually enough to keep everyone happy!)

## Sunday, May 19 – Day 2: Uluwatu Adventure

### Breakfast - Bull's Coffee Jimbaran

Our first stop was Bull's Coffee Jimbaran, where we wanted to try their famous avocado sandwich, but unfortunately it wasn't available that day. The café accepts card payments and has a cozy atmosphere, making it a great place to start the day.

### Breakfast - Warung Nasi Ayam Ibu Oki

Afterwards, we had another breakfast—Bali's chicken rice at Warung Nasi Ayam Ibu Oki. The chicken rice was delicious and authentic, leaving a lasting impression on us.

![Mei Chen提供，左至右：Pantai Batu Barak/Pandawa Beach/Oneeighty](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_80fde13911e7805a8faa34c5e34bfc1d.jpg)

### Pantai Batu Barak

After breakfast, we headed to Pantai Batu Barak, a great spot for photos. The natural scenery here is stunning, perfect for snapping pictures.

### Pandawa Beach

Next, we visited Pandawa Beach. While the beach itself wasn't particularly beautiful, we enjoyed kayaking there, which added a fun element to the visit.

### One Eighty Bali Cliff Bar

For lunch, we chose the One Eighty Bali cliff bar. The food here is relatively affordable, and we ordered quite a few dishes just to meet the minimum spend. Besides the delicious food, there's also an infinity pool where you can enjoy the views while eating. It's recommended to book in advance, and credit cards are accepted here.

We visited Besakih Mother Temple, and the entrance fee was quite expensive at about USD$10 (because it includes a mandatory guided tour fee). ## Post-Trip Reflections and Takeaways

This trip was mainly planned for adults, but we still made time every day for both kids and adults to enjoy the water. We didn’t choose to stay in high-end villas or resorts because our itinerary was packed tight, leaving little time to truly relax, so we decided not to splurge! Instead, we opted for well-reviewed, cost-effective hotels.

If you’re not particularly interested in temples, consider cutting back on some temple-related activities to free up more time for massages, water fun, and shopping. However, since we approached this trip as a once-in-a-lifetime experience, we crammed in all the famous sights. Overall, it was a rich and unforgettable journey. 😆

**Family members’ thoughts**: Traffic in Bali is really congested. Although we managed to visit almost all the famous spots, the itinerary can’t be too tight. Aside from staying in a ‘haunted house,’ everything else was fine. In the future, we might cut down on temple visits and opt for more relaxing activities. As for shopping, the experience of inflated prices followed by haggling wasn’t great.

**Would we revisit Bali?**: Bali’s beaches are beautiful, but they fall slightly short compared to those on other islands. Still, Bali’s unique cultural landscape is definitely worth exploring! However, the world is vast, and we want to discover every corner of it, so we probably won’t return to Bali. But the memories of Bali will forever stay in our hearts.
