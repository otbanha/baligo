### Mobile Phones and Internet:

When traveling in Bali, mobile phones and internet connectivity are essential. There are several ways to ensure you stay connected: First, you can purchase a local SIM card from telecom operator stores or stalls, which is a cheap and convenient way to get internet access and stay online wherever you go. Alternatively, you can buy SIM cards or mobile data packages online through websites like Klook or auction sites, which offer a variety of SIM card options to suit your needs and budget.

In Bali, ensuring your phone can connect to the internet is crucial. It helps you easily find information, stay in touch with family and friends, use navigation apps, and more, making your trip more convenient and enjoyable.

> Further reading: [Bali Travel Essentials: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/)

### Voltage and Plugs:

The voltage standard in Indonesia and Bali is **220 volts (220V)**, which is important information, especially for travelers planning to bring electrical appliances. Before deciding which appliances to bring, make sure they support the international standard of 220 volts and have the appropriate plug type to ensure they can be used normally in Indonesia and Bali!

### Airport Pickup:

For your first trip to Bali, you have several options to arrange airport pickup services to ensure a smooth journey to your destination. Here are some common pickup options:

1. **Hotel Airport Transfer Service**:
2. - Some hotels in Bali offer airport transfer services. You can inquire with your hotel when booking accommodation to see if they provide this service. It's a convenient and reassuring option, as the hotel will arrange a private car to pick you up, eliminating any worries about transportation upon arrival.
3. **Airport Taxi/Ride-Hailing Services**:
4. - At Bali Airport, you can easily find taxi stands, including official taxi services from taxi companies. After exiting the airport, you can locate the pickup lounges for Gojek or Grab, where you can use ride-hailing services. These taxis usually have fixed rates, making them a convenient and quick option.
5. **Pre-Booked Private Transfer Service**:
6. - Before arriving in Bali, you can book a private transfer service online. These services are typically provided by local car rental drivers or online platforms. You can choose the vehicle type and pickup time in advance to ensure a smooth arrival experience. This is often the most cost-effective option.

### Beware of Street Vendors/Brokers:

Many vendors and brokers in Bali specifically target tourists, so be cautious.

### Safety Tips:

While Bali is generally safe, always keep an eye on your belongings. Avoid walking down deserted alleys, don’t use your phone openly on the streets, and be wary of unsolicited conversations from Middle Eastern individuals.

### Respect Local Culture:

Bali is a Hindu region, so be mindful of etiquette when visiting temples. Avoid wearing overly revealing clothing.

## **Additional Tips for Traveling with Children of All Ages**

- Bring your child’s favorite toys and activities on the plane to keep them entertained.
- In the hot weather, ensure your child stays hydrated and protected from the sun.
- Bali’s climate is hot during the day and cooler in the evening, so consider packing a light jacket for your child.
- Before visiting temples or other religious sites, teach your child to respect local culture.
- Learn some basic Indonesian phrases, such as "thank you." Check out Jay’s video on how to pronounce them here: (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VuG8s-u_I0Q&t=106s)
