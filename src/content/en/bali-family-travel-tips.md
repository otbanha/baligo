### Visa/Electronic Customs Declaration/Tourist Tax:

Currently, a visa is required to enter Indonesia! Travelers from Taiwan can apply for an **e-Visa** or **Visa on Arrival**, with a maximum stay of 30 days. The fee is IDR 500,000, approximately USD$16 or NT$1,000.

Everyone must also fill out and submit the electronic customs declaration and tourist tax. Please refer to the link below:

> Read More: [Indonesia/Bali Arrival Guide - From Landing, Customs, to Baggage Claim](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

The advantage of applying for an e-Visa in advance is that you can skip the queues after landing and use the automated gates, bypassing immigration passport checks. However, children under 6 years old cannot use the automated gates, but there are dedicated counters to assist them (see: [Guide to Visas for Children Entering Indonesia/Bali](/en/blog/bali-child-visa-guide/))

### Voltage and Plugs:

Indonesia and Bali use a standard voltage of **220 volts (220V)**. This is important information, especially for travelers planning to bring electrical appliances. Before packing, make sure your devices support the international standard of 220V and the specific plug type, so you can use them properly in Indonesia and Bali!

### Airport Pickup: 

When arriving in Bali for the first time, you have several options for arranging airport pickup to reach your destination smoothly. Here are some common choices:

1. **Hotel Pickup Service**:
2. - Some hotels in Bali offer pickup services. You can ask about this when booking your accommodation. It's a convenient and worry-free option—the hotel will send a car to pick you up, so you don't have to worry about transportation upon arrival.
3. **Airport Taxi/Ride-Hailing**:
4. - At Bali's airport, you can easily find taxi stands with official taxi company services. After leaving the terminal, you can also find Gojek or Grab pickup lounges to use ride-hailing services. These taxis usually have fixed rates and are a quick, convenient option.
5. **Pre-Booked Private Transfer**:
6. - Before arriving in Bali, you can book a private transfer online through local drivers or platforms. You can choose your vehicle type and pickup time in advance, ensuring someone is there to greet you upon arrival. This is often the most cost-effective option.

### Safety First:

Bali is generally safe, but keep an eye on your belongings. Avoid walking down empty alleys, don't constantly hold your phone on the street, and ignore Middle Eastern men who try to strike up conversations.

### Respect Local Culture:

Bali is a Hindu region, so dress modestly when visiting temples and observe proper etiquette.

## **Extra Tips for Traveling with Kids of All Ages**

- Bring your child's favorite toys and activities on the plane to keep them happy.
- In the hot weather, make sure kids drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen.
- Bali's climate is hot during the day and cool in the evening; if you're concerned, pack a light jacket for the little ones.
- Before visiting temples or other religious sites, explain to your child the importance of respecting local culture.
- Learn a few basic Indonesian phrases, like "thank you." Check out Xiao Jie's [video on how to pronounce it](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VuG8s-u_I0Q&t=106s)
