Bali has always been a popular destination for travelers, and visa issues remain a key concern for many. In this article, we explore Bali's latest [Multiple Entry Visa] (D1 Visa) introduced in 2024, explaining how to obtain it and its related conditions.

## Introduction to Bali's New Multiple Entry Visa (D1 Visa)

The D1 Visa is Bali's newest multiple entry visa, offering a longer stay duration and greater flexibility compared to the previous D212 Visa. Holders of the D1 Visa can stay in Bali for up to 180 days and enter and exit Indonesia an unlimited number of times.

## D1 Visa Duration

The D1 Visa is initially valid for 60 days and can be extended twice, allowing for a total stay of 60 + (60 X 2) = 180 days. There are no restrictions on the number of entries and exits during this 180-day period.

## D1 Visa Requirements

To obtain the D1 Visa, applicants must prepare the following documents:

1. A scanned copy of your passport, with a validity of at least 18 months.
2. The name of your first hotel in Bali.
3. Bank statements from the last three months, showing a balance of at least USD$2,000.
4. A clear photo against a plain background, ensuring your selfie is clearly visible.

The D1 visa cannot be applied for individually, but the process is actually very simple. You just need to provide the aforementioned documents and apply through an agent.
