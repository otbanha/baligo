Maluku (the Maluku Islands) is located in the eastern region of Indonesia, geographically:

- East of Sulawesi Island
- West of Papua
- Marked in yellow on the map
- Composed of multiple islands, including the two provinces of North Maluku (Maluku Utara) and Maluku

Central Maluku (Maluku Tengah) is located within Maluku Province and is one of its regencies (Kabupaten). This area is situated in the Maluku Islands of eastern Indonesia, known for its natural landscapes and cultural diversity, making it an ideal choice for travelers who love exploring off-the-beaten-path destinations.

---

## 1. **Geographical Location and Climate** 🌦

Central Maluku encompasses several islands, primarily **Seram Island** and many surrounding smaller islands. These islands are nestled in the waters where the Indian Ocean and Pacific Ocean converge, featuring a tropical rainforest climate that is warm and humid year-round, perfect for diving and marine activities.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57c6d073011f69505b098fcad7c34d6f.jpg)

---

## 2. **Natural Landscapes** 🏝️

Central Maluku is a paradise for nature enthusiasts, with its pristine tropical rainforests and crystal-clear seas attracting countless adventurers. Here are some must-see natural attractions:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad1e695a7be1c5cad8bfce79b7be2668.jpg)

- **Manusela National Park**  
  Manusela National Park is a key attraction on Seram Island, boasting dense rainforests and rich biodiversity, making it a dream destination for hikers and wildlife observers. It is also home to many endemic bird and plant species.
- **Ora Beach**  
  Known as Indonesia's version of the "Maldives," Ora Beach features pristine white sand, calm blue lagoons, and coral reefs perfect for snorkeling, making it especially suitable for visitors who enjoy water activities.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cf25a9f62b17efd19365e6e7abf3564.jpg)

- **Inland Tropical Rainforest**  
  The rainforest on Seram Island remains in its pristine state, perfect for hiking, camping, and observing many unique flora and fauna found only in this region.
- **Pantai Ngurtafur**  
  Features a white sandbar that stretches into the sea, resembling a long white ribbon. The sandbar can extend up to 2 kilometers.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a7de8d6e803d45ae699dbe6b6fd12be9.jpg)

## 3. **Culture & History** 🛖

The Maluku Islands were once world-famous as the "Spice Islands," attracting Portuguese, Dutch, and British traders in the 16th century who competed to control the clove and nutmeg trade. Today, these islands still preserve diverse cultures and rich historical relics.

- **Local Tribal Culture**  
  The central Maluku region is predominantly inhabited by Malay and Papuan ethnic groups, each with their own traditional dances, music, and rituals, showcasing strong local cultural characteristics.
- **Spice Trade Relics**  
  Many historical sites, such as Dutch colonial buildings and spice plantations, continue to attract history enthusiasts.

---

## 4. **Transportation** 🚤

The most common way to reach central Maluku is by flying to Ambon, the capital of Maluku Province, and then taking a boat or domestic flight to Seram Island and other islands. Due to limited infrastructure in the area, travelers are advised to plan ahead.

---

## 5. **Travel Tips** 🎒

- **Best Time to Visit**: The ideal travel season is from May to October, when the weather is clear and perfect for outdoor activities.
- **Travel Experience**: It’s recommended to stay for at least 5-7 days to explore the main attractions and enjoy a leisurely island experience.
- **Essential Gear**: Since some areas are remote, it’s advisable to carry basic medical supplies and sufficient cash, as ATMs are scarce.
