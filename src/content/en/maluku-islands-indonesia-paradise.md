**Bottom Line**: The Maluku Islands, often called the 'budget-friendly Maldives,' are primarily composed of islands like Seram. With a tropical rainforest climate, they offer year-round warmth perfect for diving and water activities. Situated east of Sulawesi and west of Papua, they are less accessible than Bali, making them ideal for travelers seeking to avoid crowds and explore off-the-beaten-path island gems.

Maluku is located in eastern Indonesia, geographically:

- East of Sulawesi
- West of Papua
- Marked in yellow on the map
- Comprising multiple islands, including the provinces of North Maluku (Maluku Utara) and Maluku

Central Maluku (Maluku Tengah), located within Maluku Province, is a regency (Kabupaten) in the region. This area, situated in the Maluku Islands of eastern Indonesia, is known for its natural landscapes and cultural diversity, making it an ideal choice for travelers who love exploring off-the-beaten-path destinations.

---

## 1. **Location and Climate** 🌦

Central Maluku encompasses several islands, primarily **Seram Island** and many surrounding smaller islands. These islands are surrounded by waters where the Indian and Pacific Oceans meet, with a tropical rainforest climate that is warm and humid year-round, ideal for diving and water activities.

## 2. **Natural Landscapes** 🏝️

Central Maluku is a paradise for nature lovers, with its pristine tropical rainforests and crystal-clear seas attracting countless adventurers. Here are some natural highlights not to be missed:

![2. 自然景觀](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad1e695a7be1c5cad8bfce79b7be2668.jpg)

- **Manusela National Park**  
  Manusela National Park is a key attraction on Seram Island, featuring dense rainforests and rich biodiversity, making it a dream destination for hiking and wildlife enthusiasts. It is also home to many endemic bird species and plants.

![2. 自然景觀](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cf25a9f62b17efd19365e6e7abf3564.jpg)

- **Ora Beach**  
  Dubbed the Indonesian version of the Maldives, Ora Beach boasts pristine white sands, calm blue lagoons, and coral reefs perfect for snorkeling, especially appealing to water sports lovers.

![2. 自然景觀](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a7de8d6e803d45ae699dbe6b6fd12be9.jpg)

---

- **Inland Tropical Rainforest**  
  The rainforests on Seram Island remain in their pristine state, ideal for hiking, camping, and observing many plants and animals found only in this region.

- **Pantai Ngurtafur**  
  Features a white sandbar stretching into the sea, shaped like a long white ribbon. The sandbar can extend up to 2 kilometers.

## 3. **Culture and History** 🛖

---

The Maluku Islands were once the world-famous 'Spice Islands,' attracting Portuguese, Dutch, and British arrivals in the 16th century as they vied for control of the clove and nutmeg trade. Today, these islands still preserve diverse cultures and rich historical sites.

- **Local Tribal Culture**  
  The residents of Central Maluku are primarily of Malay and Papuan descent, each with their own traditional dances, music, and ceremonies, showcasing strong local cultural characteristics.

---

- **Spice Trade Relics**  
  Many historical sites, such as Dutch colonial buildings and spice plantations, continue to attract history enthusiasts.

## 4. **Transportation** 🚤

The most common way to reach Central Maluku is to fly to Ambon, the capital of Maluku Province, and then take a boat or domestic flight to Seram Island and other islands. Due to the region's relatively limited infrastructure, travelers are advised to plan ahead.
