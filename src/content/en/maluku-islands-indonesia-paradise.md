Maluku (the Maluku Islands) is located in the eastern region of Indonesia, geographically:

- East of Sulawesi Island
- West of Papua
- Marked in yellow on the map
- Composed of multiple islands, including two provinces: North Maluku (Maluku Utara) and Maluku

Central Maluku (Maluku Tengah) is located within Maluku Province and is one of its regencies (Kabupaten). Situated in the eastern Maluku Islands of Indonesia, this area is renowned for its natural landscapes and cultural diversity, making it an ideal choice for travelers who love exploring off-the-beaten-path destinations.

---

## 1. **Geography and Climate** 🌦

Central Maluku encompasses several islands, primarily including **Seram Island** and many surrounding smaller islands. These islands are surrounded by waters where the Indian Ocean and Pacific Ocean converge, featuring a tropical rainforest climate that is warm and humid year-round, perfect for diving and marine activities.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57c6d073011f69505b098fcad7c34d6f.jpg)

---

## 2. **Natural Landscapes** 🏝️

Central Maluku is a paradise for nature lovers, with its pristine tropical rainforests and crystal-clear waters attracting countless adventurers. Here are some must-mention natural attractions:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad1e695a7be1c5cad8bfce79b7be2668.jpg)

- **Manusela National Park**  
  Manusela National Park is a key attraction on Seram Island, boasting dense rainforests and rich biodiversity, making it a dream destination for hikers and wildlife enthusiasts. It is also home to many endemic bird and plant species.
- **Ora Beach**  
  Known as Indonesia's version of the Maldives, Ora Beach features pristine white sand, calm blue lagoons, and coral reefs perfect for snorkeling, making it especially suitable for visitors who enjoy water activities.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cf25a9f62b17efd19365e6e7abf3564.jpg)

- **Inland Tropical Rainforest**  
  The rainforest on Seram Island remains in its pristine state, ideal for hiking, camping, and observing unique flora and fauna found only in this region.
- **Pantai Ngurtafur**  
  Features a white sandbar extending into the sea, resembling a long white ribbon. The sandbar can stretch up to 2 kilometers.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a7de8d6e803d45ae699dbe6b6fd12be9.jpg)

## 3. **Culture & History** 🛖

The Maluku Islands, once world-renowned as the 'Spice Islands,' attracted Portuguese, Dutch, and British traders in the 16th century, who competed to control the clove and nutmeg trade. Today, these islands still preserve diverse cultures and rich historical sites.

- **Local Tribal Culture**  
  The central Maluku region is primarily inhabited by Malay and Papuan ethnic groups, each with their own traditional dances, music, and rituals, showcasing strong local cultural characteristics.
- **Spice Trade Relics**  
  Many historical sites, such as Dutch colonial buildings and spice plantations, continue to attract history enthusiasts.

---

## 4. **Transportation** 🚤

The most common way to reach central Maluku is by flying to Ambon, the capital of Maluku Province, and then taking a boat or domestic flight to Seram Island and other islands. Due to limited infrastructure in this region, travelers are advised to plan ahead.

---

## 5. **Travel Tips** 🎒

- **Best Time to Visit**: May to October is the ideal travel season, with clear weather perfect for outdoor activities.
- **Travel Experience**: Recommend staying for at least 5-7 days to explore major attractions and enjoy leisurely island time.
- **Essential Gear**: As some areas are remote, it’s advisable to carry basic medical supplies and sufficient cash, as ATMs are scarce.
