Our Bali trip began with great anticipation as we explored Ubud's cultural atmosphere, enjoyed leisurely moments at Atlas Beach Club, and tasted delicious local street food at night markets. From the moment we arrived on a red-eye flight, our family was captivated by Bali's charm. This travelogue will take you through our 6-day, 5-night itinerary, sharing the beauty and surprises of each day.

## **Day 1: Red-Eye Flight and First Glimpse of Ubud**

We planned a 6-day, 5-night family trip to Bali, opting for Scoot's red-eye flight. After a 2-hour layover in Singapore, we had breakfast and explored Changi Airport. We arrived in Bali around 10:00 AM and started our adventure immediately, wasting no time!

Since we had purchased a SIM card in Taiwan beforehand, we quickly contacted our private driver, Mr. Awan from Bali Holiday, upon arrival.

> Further reading: [Bali Travel Essentials: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/)

He was very friendly, communicated fluently in English, and drove a highly recognizable pink car that was easy to spot anywhere. After getting in the car, we first asked him to take us to exchange currency, then headed to Ubud. Along the way, we visited the beautiful Tegenungan Waterfall and stopped by a supermarket to buy snacks and drinking water.

## **Day 2: River Tubing Adventure & Exploring Ubud**

On the second day, we went on a river tubing adventure, which was arranged by Awan. We bought tickets on-site, and the price was consistent with the listed price. We had considered purchasing tickets in advance on a double K website, but I thought that would limit our flexibility. Initially, we thought this activity would be mild, but it turned out to be quite fun and slightly challenging. My husband and kids had a great time, making it perfect for family fun!

https://youtu.be/ZEkv46_-_mM

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Cave River Tubing Adventure: A Family Adventure at Nukuwera River Tubing](/blog/nukuwera-river-tubing-adventure/)

![叢林鞦韆。（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_86291fefaa0646e19596de72aa7330bd.jpg)

Next, we headed to the rice terrace. The local swing guys were exceptionally skilled at photography, using techniques like slow motion, video, and portrait mode to capture beautiful moments. They also knew exactly when to take the best shots, far surpassing my husband's photography skills! 🤣

In the afternoon, we happily visited the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary, where we were surrounded by monkeys. Afterwards, we headed to Ubud Market for some shopping, and the bargaining process was quite fun! However, most of the items in the market were repetitive, so I usually chose to buy from vendors whose faces seemed more congenial to me.

## **Day 3: Safari Zoo and Night Market Experience**

![Safari Zoo. （趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d58684925c87a20eb4035406378406a1.jpg)

Safari Zoo. (Provided by Zhao Kangkang)

On the third day, we visited Safari Zoo. Although it was a bit far from Ubud, it was definitely worth the trip for families with children! The animals in the zoo seemed very relaxed, allowing us to get closer to them. There was a cute animal area where you could buy tickets to feed the little animals, and the children couldn't get enough of it. During our tour, we saw other tourists picking up rabbits for photos, which made the rabbits look helpless. This gave me a chance to teach my children to respect animals.

At noon, we happened to catch a tiger show, which left me amazed. It was my first time observing tigers so closely, and they weren't in glass cages—they even interacted with the staff. It was truly a sight worth seeing!

Besides animal viewing, the park also hosts a variety of animal performances, blending joy and nature into one experience. Check out [our detailed introduction here](/blog/bali-safari-night-stay/). If you're looking for better value, consider [staying at the zoo](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=109155).

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to a local night market. We walked around the market first, asking him to introduce various foods, and then he helped us order from the vendors (since some vendors didn't speak English). We enjoyed dishes like roasted pork rice, chicken meatball noodles, chicken satay, and pork satay, and bought a bunch of local desserts to enjoy back at our accommodation. All of this cost less than USD$10, making it a very affordable and fun experience!

### **Day 4: Exploring Tanah Lot Temple and Canggu Market**

On the fourth day, we visited the famous Tanah Lot Temple. Fortunately, it was low tide, allowing us to walk down to the beach. The weather was perfect, and every shot we took was postcard-worthy, perfect for a computer wallpaper.

![海神廟（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5af7bceb17915318e93173bfc2bc2052.jpg)

Tanah Lot Temple (Photo by Zhao Kangkang)

In the afternoon, we headed to Canggu and strolled through the Love Anchor Market. However, it was a bit disappointing. We had hoped to find some unique souvenirs, but the items here were quite similar to those in Ubud Market. So, it’s not really worth making a special trip here (there aren’t many places to explore nearby, and prices are higher).

{{block:canggu}}

In the evening, we went to a nearby beach and tried the [internet-famous grilled corn](https://youtu.be/HKMalFa6zMw). It lived up to its reputation! A stick of grilled corn costs only 20K IDR, which is both delicious and affordable.

## **Day 5: Relaxing at Atlas Beach Club**

On the fifth day, we spent the entire day at Atlas Beach Club.

![Atlas（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_27217d256133aaf6efab3d64131dcdff.jpg)

Atlas Beach Club (Photo by Zhao Kangkang)

I purchased tickets in advance on Klook. Upon arrival, we simply scanned the QR code, and they prepared our entry passes and guided us to our seats—very convenient. Atlas Beach Club is truly worth recommending. There’s a shallow pool specifically for children and a massive infinity pool right next to the beach, where you can go down and play in the waves. The ticket includes a certain amount of food or drinks, and you only need to pay extra if you exceed the limit.

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to the nearby Seminyak shopping district for more retail therapy. I noticed that international brands tend to be quite expensive in Bali, so I recommend supporting local brands instead — they offer great quality at affordable prices!

## **Day 6: Final Shopping and Farewell to Bali**

On our last day, Awan took us to a massive local shopping spot similar to a duty-free store (Krisna Toko Oleh Oleh Kas Bali) as per our request. While the items here aren’t particularly refined, they have everything you could think of — from food and clothing to home decor and jewelry. This was our final stop to pick up all the souvenirs we hadn’t bought yet, and we ended the trip very satisfied with a cart full of treasures.

![我們的戰利品（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6da6ad309b85087a2074fa2e43f4d3e4.jpg)

Our haul (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

This was our first time in Bali, and although I had watched many videos before the trip and was somewhat prepared, Bali’s beauty still exceeded my expectations! The traffic and pace of life here are very laid-back. Even though there’s often congestion, you rarely hear honking — everyone is incredibly patient! Except for the roads near the airport, which have clear markings, the road planning elsewhere is quite casual. Sometimes a two-way road suddenly turns into a one-way, leaving you completely baffled 😂. Therefore, I strongly recommend hiring a private car for family trips (or groups of three or more) as it’s more convenient and safer.

## **Recommended Driver: Bali Holiday Awan**

Finally, I’d like to recommend our private car driver for this trip — Awan from Bali Holiday. From the very first day, we felt his warmth and enthusiasm. Although I had prepared an itinerary for him before the trip, there were times when we changed plans due to fatigue or time constraints, and he was always flexible in adjusting and offering helpful suggestions, such as which route was smoother or how much time to allocate to certain attractions.

![我們很滿意的包車司機（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad7a43d71b2ee96f978fe92dfbedcb54.jpg)

Our very satisfied private car driver (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

Additionally, on two occasions, I asked him to arrange for a masseuse to come to our Villa, and he worked hard to coordinate the timing for us. Overall, Awan is an excellent private car driver. Though he’s not part of a big company, his personal touch made us feel truly cared for, and my family got along with him very well! If I ever have the chance to visit Bali again, I’ll definitely choose him as our driver, and I highly recommend him to anyone looking for a private car driver!
