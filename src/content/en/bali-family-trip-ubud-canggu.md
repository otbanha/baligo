Our Bali trip began with high expectations as we explored Ubud's cultural atmosphere, enjoyed leisurely moments at Atlas Beach Club, and tasted delicious local street food. From the moment we arrived on a red-eye flight, our family was captivated by Bali's charm. This travelogue will take you through our 6-day, 5-night itinerary, sharing the beauty and surprises of each day.

## **Day 1: Red-Eye Flight and First Glimpse of Ubud**

We planned a 6-day, 5-night family trip to Bali, opting for Scoot's red-eye flight. After a 2-hour layover in Singapore, we had breakfast and explored Singapore's airport. We arrived in Bali around 10:00 AM and immediately started our adventure, wasting no time!

Since we had purchased a SIM card in Taiwan beforehand, we quickly contacted our private driver, Mr. Awan from Bali Holiday, upon arrival.

> Further reading: [Bali Travel Essentials: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)

He was very friendly, spoke fluent English, and drove a highly recognizable pink car that was easy to spot anywhere. After getting in the car, we first asked him to take us to exchange currency, then headed to Ubud. Along the way, we visited the beautiful Tegenungan Waterfall and stopped by a supermarket to buy snacks and drinking water.

## **Day 2: River Tubing Adventure & Ubud Exploration**

On the second day, we headed out for a river tubing adventure, which was arranged by Awan. We bought tickets on-site, and the price was consistent with the listed rate. We considered purchasing tickets in advance through a booking website, but I felt that would limit our flexibility. Initially, I thought the activity would be quite mild, but it turned out to be incredibly fun and slightly challenging! My husband and kids had a blast, making it a perfect family activity!

https://youtu.be/ZEkv46_-_mM

Next, we visited the rice terraces. The local swing operators were incredibly skilled at photography, using techniques like slow motion, video, and portrait mode to capture stunning shots. They also knew exactly when to take the perfect photo, far surpassing my husband's photography skills! 🤣

In the afternoon, we happily visited the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary, where we were surrounded by curious monkeys. Afterwards, we headed to Ubud Market for some shopping, and the bargaining process was quite entertaining! However, most of the products at the market were repetitive, so I usually chose to buy from vendors whose vibes resonated with me.

## **Day 3: Safari Zoo & Night Market Experience**

![Safari Zoo. （趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d58684925c87a20eb4035406378406a1.jpg)

Safari Zoo. (Photo provided by Zhao Kangkang)

On the third day, we visited Safari Zoo. Although it's a bit far from Ubud, it's definitely worth a visit for families with children! The animals at the zoo seemed very relaxed, allowing us to get closer to them. There was a cute animal zone where you could purchase tickets to feed the little animals, and the kids couldn't get enough of it. During our visit, we saw other tourists picking up rabbits for photos, which made the rabbits look helpless. This gave me a chance to teach my children about respecting animals.

At noon, we happened to catch a tiger show, which left me amazed. It was my first time observing tigers so closely, and they weren't in glass cages—they even interacted with the staff. It was truly a sight worth seeing!

In addition to animal viewing, the park hosts a variety of animal performances, blending fun and nature into one exciting experience. If you're looking for better value, consider [staying at the zoo](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=109155).

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to a local night market for an authentic experience. At the market, we first walked around and asked him to introduce us to various foods. Then, he helped us order from the vendors (since some didn't speak English). We enjoyed dishes like roasted pork rice, chicken meatball noodles, chicken satay, and pork satay, and even bought a bunch of local desserts to enjoy back at our accommodation. All of this cost less than USD$10, making it a very affordable and fun experience!

### **Day 4: Exploring Tanah Lot Temple and Canggu Market**

On the fourth day, we visited the famous Tanah Lot Temple. Fortunately, it was low tide that day, allowing us to walk down to the beach. The weather was perfect, and every shot we took was postcard-worthy, easily good enough to be a computer wallpaper.

![海神廟（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5af7bceb17915318e93173bfc2bc2052.jpg)

Tanah Lot Temple (Photo by Zhao Kangkang)

In the afternoon, we headed to Canggu and strolled through the Love Anchor Market. However, it was a bit disappointing. We had hoped to find some unique souvenirs, but the items here were quite similar to those at Ubud Market. So, it’s not really worth making a special trip here (there aren’t many nearby attractions, and prices are higher).

{{block:canggu}}

In the evening, we went to a nearby beach and tried the [internet-famous grilled corn](https://youtu.be/HKMalFa6zMw). It definitely lived up to its reputation! A stick of grilled corn costs only 20K IDR, which is both delicious and affordable.

## **Day 5: Relaxing at Atlas Beach Club**

On the fifth day, we spent the entire day at Atlas Beach Club.

![Atlas（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_27217d256133aaf6efab3d64131dcdff.jpg)

Atlas Beach Club (Photo by Zhao Kangkang)

I purchased the tickets in advance on Klook. Upon arrival, we simply scanned the QR code, and they prepared our admission tickets and guided us to our seats—very convenient. Atlas Beach Club is truly worth recommending. There’s a shallow pool specifically for kids, as well as a huge infinity pool right next to the beach, where you can go down and play in the waves. The ticket includes a certain amount of food or drinks, and you only need to pay extra if you exceed the limit.

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to the Seminyak shopping district for some retail therapy. I noticed that international brands in Bali tend to be quite expensive, so I recommend supporting local brands instead — they offer great quality at affordable prices!

## **Day 6: Final Shopping and Farewell to Bali**

On our last day, Awan took us to a large duty-free-style shopping spot (Krisna Toko Oleh Oleh Kas Bali) at our request. While the items here weren’t particularly refined, they had everything from food and clothing to home decor and jewelry. This was our final stop to pick up all the souvenirs we hadn’t bought yet, and we ended the trip very satisfied with a cart full of treasures.

![我們的戰利品（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6da6ad309b85087a2074fa2e43f4d3e4.jpg)

Our loot (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

This was our first time in Bali, and although I had watched many videos before the trip and felt somewhat prepared, Bali’s beauty still exceeded my expectations! The traffic and pace of life here are very laid-back. Even though there’s often congestion, you rarely hear honking — everyone is incredibly patient! Except for the roads near the airport, which have white lines, the road planning elsewhere is quite casual. Sometimes a two-way road suddenly becomes one-way, leaving you completely baffled 😂. That’s why I strongly recommend hiring a private car for family trips (or groups of three or more) — it’s more convenient and safer.

## **Recommended Driver: Bali Holiday Awan**

Finally, I want to recommend our private driver for this trip — Awan from Bali Holiday. From the moment we met him on the first day, we felt his warmth and enthusiasm. Even though I had prepared an itinerary for him beforehand, there were times when we changed plans due to fatigue or timing, and he was always flexible in adjusting and offering helpful advice, such as which route would be smoother or how much time to allocate to certain attractions.

![我們很滿意的包車司機（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad7a43d71b2ee96f978fe92dfbedcb54.jpg)

Our very satisfied private driver (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

Additionally, on two occasions, I asked him to arrange for massage therapists to come to our villa, and he worked hard to coordinate the timing for us. Overall, Awan is an excellent private driver. Though he’s not part of a big company, his kindness made us feel very welcome, and my family got along with him wonderfully! If I ever have the chance to visit Bali again, I’ll definitely choose him as my driver, and I highly recommend him to anyone looking for a private driver!
