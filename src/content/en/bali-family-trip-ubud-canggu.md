For this Bali trip, we set off with great anticipation, exploring the cultural atmosphere of Ubud, enjoying leisurely moments at Atlas Beach Club, and tasting delicious local street food. From the arrival on a red-eye flight, our family was enchanted by Bali's charm. This travelogue will guide you through our 6-day, 5-night itinerary, sharing the beauty and surprises of each day.

## **Day 1: Red-Eye Flight and First Exploration of Ubud**

We planned a 6-day, 5-night family trip to Bali, opting for Scoot's red-eye flight. After a 2-hour layover in Singapore, we had breakfast and explored Singapore Airport. Arriving in Bali around 10:00 AM, we started our adventure immediately, wasting no time!

Since we had purchased a SIM card in Taiwan beforehand, we quickly contacted our private driver, Mr. Awan from Bali Holiday, upon arrival.

> Further reading: [Bali Travel Essentials: How to Get a Bali SIM Card/eSIM/Roaming?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/)

He was very friendly, communicated fluently in English, and his bright pink car was easily recognizable wherever we went. After boarding, we first asked him to take us to exchange currency, then headed to Ubud. Along the way, we visited the beautiful Tegenungan Waterfall and stopped by a supermarket to buy snacks and drinking water.

## **Day 2: River Tubing & Ubud Adventure**

On the second day, we went on a river tubing adventure, which was arranged by Awan. We bought tickets on-site, and the price was consistent with the listed rate. We had considered purchasing tickets in advance on a double K website, but I thought that would limit our flexibility. Initially, we thought this activity would be mild, but it turned out to be quite fun and slightly challenging. My husband and kids had a great time, making it a perfect family activity!

https://youtu.be/ZEkv46_-_mM

Next, we headed to the rice terraces. The local swing operators were incredibly skilled at photography. They not only used slow motion, video, and portrait modes to capture beautiful moments, but they also expertly timed their shots, far surpassing my husband's photography skills! 🤣

In the afternoon, we happily visited the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary, where we were surrounded by curious monkeys. Afterwards, we headed to Ubud Market for some shopping, and the bargaining process was quite entertaining! However, many of the items in the market were repetitive, so I usually chose to buy from vendors whose vibes resonated with me.

## **Day 3: Safari Zoo & Night Market Experience**

![Safari Zoo. （趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d58684925c87a20eb4035406378406a1.jpg)

Safari Zoo. (Photo provided by Zhao Kangkang)

On the third day, we visited Safari Zoo. Although it was a bit far from Ubud, it’s definitely worth a visit for families with children! The animals in the zoo seemed very relaxed, allowing us to get closer to them. There was a cute animal zone where you could buy tickets to feed the little animals, and the kids couldn’t get enough of it. During our visit, we saw other tourists picking up rabbits for photos, which made the rabbits look helpless. This gave me the opportunity to teach my children about respecting animals.

At noon, we stumbled upon a tiger show, which left me utterly amazed. It was my first time observing tigers so closely, and they weren’t behind glass cages—they even interacted with the staff. It was truly a sight worth seeing!

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to a local night market for an authentic experience. At the market, we first walked around and had him introduce us to various foods. Then, he helped us order from the vendors (since some didn’t speak English). We enjoyed dishes like roast pork rice, chicken meatball noodles, chicken satay, and pork satay, and even bought a bunch of local desserts to enjoy back at our accommodation. All of this cost less than USD$10, making it a highly affordable and fun experience!

### **Day 4: Exploring Tanah Lot Temple and Canggu Market**

On the fourth day, we visited the famous Tanah Lot Temple. Fortunately, it was low tide, allowing us to walk down to the beach. The weather was perfect, and every photo we took was postcard-worthy, easily suitable as a computer wallpaper.

![海神廟（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5af7bceb17915318e93173bfc2bc2052.jpg)

Tanah Lot Temple (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

In the afternoon, we headed to Canggu and strolled through the Love Anchor Market. However, we were somewhat disappointed. We had hoped to find some unique souvenirs, but the items here were quite similar to those in Ubud Market. Therefore, this place isn’t worth making a special trip (there aren’t many nearby attractions, and prices are higher).

{{block:canggu}}

In the evening, we visited a nearby beach and tried the [internet-famous grilled corn](https://youtu.be/HKMalFa6zMw). It lived up to its reputation! A stick of grilled corn costs only 20K IDR, which is both delicious and affordable.

## **Day 5: Relaxing at Atlas Beach Club**

On the fifth day, we spent the entire day at Atlas Beach Club.

![Atlas（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_27217d256133aaf6efab3d64131dcdff.jpg)

Atlas (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

I purchased tickets in advance on Klook. Upon arrival, we simply scanned the QR code, and they prepared our entry passes and guided us to our seats—very convenient. Atlas Beach Club is truly worth recommending. There’s a shallow pool dedicated to children and a massive infinity pool right next to the beach, where you can go for a splash. The ticket includes a certain amount of food or drinks, and you only pay extra if you exceed the limit.

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to the Seminyak shopping district for some retail therapy. I noticed that international brands tend to be more expensive in Bali, so I recommend supporting local brands instead — they offer great quality at affordable prices!

## **Day 6: Final Shopping and Farewell to Bali**

On our last day, Awan took us to a large duty-free-style shopping spot (Krisna Toko Oleh Oleh Kas Bali) as per our request. While the items here weren’t particularly refined, they had everything from food and clothing to home decor and jewelry. We stocked up on all the souvenirs we hadn’t bought yet, and with a cart full of treasures, we ended our trip feeling very satisfied.

![我們的戰利品（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6da6ad309b85087a2074fa2e43f4d3e4.jpg)

Our loot (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

This was our first time in Bali, and although I had watched many videos beforehand, the island’s beauty still exceeded my expectations! The traffic and pace of life here are very laid-back. Even though there’s often congestion, you rarely hear honking — everyone’s patience is incredible! Except for the roads near the airport, which have clear markings, the rest of the road planning is quite casual. Sometimes a two-way street suddenly becomes one-way, leaving you utterly confused 😂. That’s why I strongly recommend hiring a private car for family trips (or groups of three or more) — it’s much more convenient and safer.

## **Recommended Driver: Bali Holiday Awan**

Finally, I want to recommend our private driver for this trip — Awan from Bali Holiday. From the very first day, we felt his warmth and enthusiasm. Although I had prepared an itinerary for him beforehand, there were times when we changed plans due to fatigue or timing, and he was always flexible in adjusting and offering helpful advice, such as which route was smoother or how much time to allocate for certain attractions.

![我們很滿意的包車司機（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad7a43d71b2ee96f978fe92dfbedcb54.jpg)

Our very satisfied private driver (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

Additionally, on two occasions, I asked him to arrange for a masseuse to come to our villa, and he worked hard to coordinate the timing for us. Overall, Awan is an excellent private driver. Though he’s not part of a big company, his personal touch made us feel very welcome, and my family got along with him wonderfully! If I ever have the chance to return to Bali, I’ll definitely choose him again, and I highly recommend him to anyone looking for a private driver!
