On this Bali trip, we set off with great excitement, exploring the cultural charm of Ubud, enjoying the leisurely time at Atlas Beach Club, and tasting delicious local夜市 snacks. From the moment our red-eye flight landed, our family was drawn to Bali's magic. This travelogue walks you through our 6-day, 5-night itinerary, sharing the beauty and surprises of each day.

## **Day 1: Red-Eye Flight and First Glimpse of Ubud**

We planned a 6-day, 5-night family trip to Bali, choosing a red-eye flight with Scoot. After a 2-hour layover in Singapore, we grabbed breakfast and explored the airport. Arriving in Bali around 10:00 AM, we hit the ground running, wasting no time!

Since we bought a SIM card in Taiwan beforehand, we quickly got in touch with our private driver for the trip—Mr. Awan from Bali Holiday.

> Read more: [[Essential for Bali Travel] How to Get a Bali SIM Card/eSIM/Roaming?](/en/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/)

He was incredibly friendly, spoke perfect English, and his car was a highly recognizable pink vehicle, easy to spot anywhere. After getting in, we asked him to take us to exchange money, then headed to Ubud. Along the way, we first visited the beautiful Tegenungan Waterfall, and then stopped at a supermarket for snacks and drinking water.

## **Day 2: River Tubing and Ubud Adventure**

On the second day, we headed out for a river tubing adventure, which Awan helped us book. We bought tickets on-site, and the price matched the listed rate. We considered pre-purchasing tickets on the Klook or KKday websites, but I felt that would offer less flexibility in timing. I initially thought this activity would be quite gentle, but it turned out to be surprisingly fun and a bit challenging. My husband and kids had a blast—it's perfect for a family outing!

https://youtu.be/ZEkv46_-_mM

Next, we visited the rice terrace attraction. The local swing operators were incredibly skilled at photography—they used slow motion, video, and portrait modes to capture stunning shots, and they had a knack for timing the perfect moment. Their skills far surpassed my husband's photography abilities! 🤣

In the afternoon, we happily visited the Sacred Monkey Forest, where we were surrounded by monkeys. Then we headed to the Ubud Market for some shopping—the bargaining process was so much fun! However, many of the items in the market were repetitive, so I usually chose to buy from shops that felt more appealing to me.

## **Day 3: Safari Zoo and Night Market Experience**

![Safari Zoo. （趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d58684925c87a20eb4035406378406a1.jpg)

Safari Zoo. (Photo by Zhao Kangkang)

On the third day, we visited Safari Zoo. Although it's a bit far from Ubud, it's definitely worth a visit for families with kids! The animals in the zoo all seemed very relaxed, bringing us closer to them. There was a petting zone where you could buy tickets to feed the small animals, and the kids were reluctant to leave. During our tour, we saw other visitors picking up rabbits to take photos, which made the rabbits look helpless. This gave me a chance to teach the kids about respecting animals.

Around noon, we happened to catch a tiger show that truly amazed me. It was my first time observing tigers up close, and they weren't in glass enclosures—they even interacted with the staff on site. It was a sight worth seeing!

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to a local night market. There, we first walked around and had him introduce the various foods, then he helped us order from the stalls (since some vendors didn't speak English). We enjoyed dishes like babi guling (roast pork rice), chicken meatball noodles, chicken satay, and pork satay, and also bought a bunch of local desserts to take back to our accommodation. The total cost was less than USD$10—a very affordable and fun experience!

### **Day 4: Tanah Lot Temple and Canggu Market Stroll**

On the fourth day, we visited the famous Tanah Lot Temple. Lucky for us, it happened to be low tide, so we could walk down to the shore. The weather was gorgeous—every snapshot looked like a masterpiece, easily worthy of a desktop wallpaper.

![海神廟（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5af7bceb17915318e93173bfc2bc2052.jpg)

Tanah Lot Temple (Photo by Zhao Kangkang)

In the afternoon, we headed to Canggu and browsed the Love Anchor Market. However, it was a bit of a letdown. We had hoped to find some unique souvenirs, but the items were pretty similar to what you'd see at Ubud Market. So honestly, it's not worth a special trip (there's not much else to explore nearby, and prices are higher).

{{block:canggu}}

As evening fell, we went to a nearby beach and tried the [influencer-recommended grilled corn](https://youtu.be/HKMalFa6zMw)—it lived up to the hype! One ear of corn cost just 20K IDR, delicious and a great deal.

## **Day 5: A Leisurely Day at Atlas Beach Club**

On the fifth day, we spent the entire day at Atlas Beach Club.

![Atlas（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_27217d256133aaf6efab3d64131dcdff.jpg)

Atlas (Photo by Zhao Kangkang)

I bought tickets in advance on Klook. When we arrived, we just scanned the QR code, and they prepared our entry passes and assigned someone to show us to our seats—super convenient. Atlas Club is truly worth recommending. They have a shallow pool for kids and a massive infinity pool, right next to the beach where you can dip your toes in the waves. The ticket included a certain credit for food or drinks; anything beyond that was extra.

[Klook Booking Link](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=898838&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80472-atlas-beach-club-admission-bali%2F)

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to the nearby Seminyak shopping district for more retail therapy. I noticed that international brands in Bali are generally overpriced, so I recommend supporting local brands instead—they offer great quality at reasonable prices!

## **Day 6: Last-Minute Shopping and Farewell to Bali**

On our final day, at our request, Awan took us to a massive, duty-free-like shopping center called Krisna Toko Oleh Oleh Kas Bali. While the items there aren't exactly refined, they have everything you could need—from food and clothing to home decor and accessories. At this stop, we picked up all the souvenirs we hadn't bought yet, filling the car with our haul and ending the trip on a very satisfying note.

![我們的戰利品（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6da6ad309b85087a2074fa2e43f4d3e4.jpg)

Our haul (photo by Zhao Kangkang)

This was our first time in Bali, and even though I had watched plenty of videos before leaving and thought I was prepared, the island's beauty still exceeded my imagination! The traffic and pace of life here are incredibly laid-back. Even though traffic jams are common, you rarely hear honking—everyone has amazing patience! Aside from the white lines on the roads near the airport, road planning elsewhere is quite casual; sometimes a two-way street suddenly becomes one-way, leaving you scratching your head 😂. That's why I strongly recommend that for family trips (or groups of three or more), hiring a private driver is much more convenient and safer.

## **Recommended Driver: Bali Holiday Awan**

Finally, I want to give another shout-out to our driver for this trip—Awan from Bali Holiday. From the very first day we met him, we felt his warmth and helpfulness. Even though I had prepared an itinerary for him before departure, we often changed plans on the fly due to fatigue or time constraints. He was always flexible in adjusting our schedule and offered great advice, like which routes were smoother and how long we should spend at each spot.

![我們很滿意的包車司機（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad7a43d71b2ee96f978fe92dfbedcb54.jpg)

Our highly satisfying driver (photo by Zhao Kangkang)

Also, on two days I asked him to help arrange massage therapists to come to the villa, and he did his best to coordinate the timing for us. Overall, Awan is a fantastic private driver. Although he's not part of a big company, his warmth and personal touch made us feel truly cared for, and my family got along with him wonderfully! If I ever have the chance to return to Bali, I will definitely choose him again, and I highly recommend him to anyone looking for a private driver!
