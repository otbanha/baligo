**Key Takeaways**: This is a real-tested itinerary from the peak season of July, covering **Seminyak → Uluwatu → Jimbaran → Ubud**. Two most useful tips: ① Drivers in Bali often refuse cross-region trips or hike up prices midway—**full-day private car hire** (with driver Komang WikaSutresna, contact info at the end) is the most hassle-free solution; ② Arrive at Oneeighty Dayclub **within the first hour of opening** to snag front-row ocean views, and have your driver pre-book Uluwatu Kecak fire dance tickets to skip the queue.

> The following content was provided by group member [Lian Yuzhun](https://www.facebook.com/groups/baligo/permalink/1574999756380597/) for us to reorganize:

This time, we traveled to Bali in July to attend a friend's wedding. Travel information about Bali is quite overwhelming, and it took us two to three months to plan the itinerary.

![【水明漾-烏魯瓦圖-金巴蘭-烏布】峇里島自由行完整行程分享](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a22d6c37c8a63306b663eaf1af023ba8.jpg)

During the pre-trip planning, we found that local traffic conditions were chaotic, with drivers often refusing cross-region passengers or hiking up prices midway. To avoid these hassles, we decided to hire a private driver, which not only saved us the trouble of finding rides but also made our trip smoother and more relaxing. Our driver was Komang WikaSutresna (introduced at the bottom). In the end, we felt that choosing a private driver was a very wise decision!

## Day 1:

![水明漾夕陽。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6dc896b39c10af9bac23d825129c22b9.jpg)

Seminyak sunset. Provided by Lian Yuzhun

Since we heard traffic in Seminyak is very congested, we chose to walk around on our own in the morning without a driver. We arranged to meet our driver in the evening to take us from Seminyak to our villa in Uluwatu. Komang arrived early at the meeting point and waited for us. We originally planned to go out for dinner on our own after arriving at the villa, but Komang checked Google Street View on the way and found that the road from the villa to the main street was very dark at night and unsafe to walk. In the end, when we reached the villa, he waited at the door until we checked in and then drove us to dinner. This thoughtful service made us feel truly warm.

A quick note: Uluwatu Temple is full of monkeys—more aggressive than those at Chai Mountain. They’ll snatch things from both men and women, and you’ll even see lots of broken glasses on the ground. So it’s best not to bring any valuables when you go there.

## Day 3:

This day we attended a wedding, so we didn’t arrange Komang’s car service.

## Day 4:

Today’s itinerary took us from Jimbaran to Ubud. Along the way, we found that the restaurant we planned to visit was temporarily closed. Komang immediately called to confirm, and also checked the waterfall’s official website, which turned out to be closed due to the heavy rain the day before (even though it’s the dry season). But Komang quickly offered us new suggestions, helping us smoothly get through the day’s plans. After visiting the waterfall, we strolled around Ubud town, and finally asked Komang to drop us off for a massage to end the day.

Also, at Gefecon Villa Ubud, where we stayed that night, there was a Taiwanese supervisor who was incredibly warm and helpful—she was happy to answer any questions.

## Day 5:

![聖泉寺。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dbc6e9fddb7c4642b0a7e4bfee0a2182.jpg)

Tirta Empul Temple. Photo by Lien Yu-Chun

Today's route formed an O-shape, starting at Tirta Empul Temple, then heading to a café with volcano views. We originally planned to visit AKASA Specialty Coffee, but Komang recommended Montana Del Café instead, saying Taiwanese travelers seem to love it. Although the weather was bad and we couldn't see the volcano, the Korean-style croissants there were absolutely delicious!

https://youtube.com/shorts/fiYs5GKdINU

![Montana Del Café可惜天氣不好。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8bba492232d63a05b16658986210416d.jpg)

Montana Del Café — too bad the weather wasn't cooperating. Photo by Lien Yu-Chun

Finally, we visited Tegallalang Rice Terrace. The entrance fee doesn't include the swing ticket — if you want to ride, you need to buy a ticket at the gate. There are also special outfits available for rent, making for stunning photos. Although it's a bit of an Instagram hotspot, strolling through the rice terraces and listening to the screams of people on the swings was still a lot of fun.

https://youtu.be/op78Dk8CjL4

> Check this out: [The Ultimate Ubud Guide: 100 Ways to Deeply Explore Ubud | Discover Ubud's Charm](/en/blog/ubud-guide/)

After the tour, we asked Komang to take us to our favorite restaurant of the trip — Kebun Bistro. Unfortunately, their famous lamb chops were sold out, but the steak recommended by the staff was equally delicious! We ordered it medium-rare, and when cut, there was no blood — the meat was incredibly tender, and even the sides were tasty. Since it gets busy, it's best to make a reservation ahead of meal times. (Already added to the [Ubud Guide Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

## Day 6:

In the morning, we set off from Ubud to Tanah Lot. You may need to wade through water to reach the temple, so it's best to wear water-friendly shoes and shorts. I've heard the sunset view is stunning, but unfortunately, our midday schedule meant we couldn't stay to see it.

> [Bali Travel Must-Visit: Tanah Lot Temple – Detailed Guide on Tides and Nearby Attractions](/en/blog/bali-tanah-lot-guide/)

When we arrived at Tanah Lot, we realized we had accidentally taken the key from the previous night's hotel. Komang immediately said he could return it for us in a couple of days (after getting approval from the hotel's supervisor). We were so grateful to him!

After Tanah Lot, we headed to Canggu for some shopping and a beach stroll. We tried Blacklist Coffee Roasters, recommended by Feli, and it turned out to be our favorite coffee of the entire Bali trip.

To wrap up the trip, we invited Komang to join us for dinner to thank him for his care over the past few days.

## Recommended Private Driver Service

In summary, for first-time visitors to Bali who want to explore attractions but feel a bit lost, hiring a private driver is a great choice. Not only do you get professional advice, but the route planning is also much easier, and you don't have to worry about luggage.

![推薦包車](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fd9d65047cadfd58a1e16cce702fbb1.jpg)

Our driver, Komang WikaSutresna, is a very friendly person. When he heard we were attending a wedding, he was thrilled and even shared his own wedding video from six months ago, along with a video call to his adorable son. Komang speaks good English and is very attentive—you can ask him anything during the trip, and he'll patiently answer.

Komang takes every guest seriously. Throughout our journey, he was incredibly thoughtful: at each destination, he would get out of the car to explain how to explore the area, and when we went to the spa, he greeted the staff to ensure our needs were well taken care of. Although language barriers prevented deep conversations, communication about the itinerary was never an issue. We highly recommend Komang to everyone!
