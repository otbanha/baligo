> The following content was provided by group member [Lian Yuzhun](https://www.facebook.com/groups/baligo/permalink/1574999756380597/) and rearranged by us:

This time, we chose to visit Bali in July to attend a friend's wedding. The travel information for Bali is quite extensive, and it took us two to three months to finalize our itinerary.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a22d6c37c8a63306b663eaf1af023ba8.jpg)

During the pre-trip planning period, we discovered that local transportation is very chaotic, with drivers often refusing to take passengers across regions or raising prices midway. To avoid these issues, we decided to hire a private driver. This not only reduced the hassle of finding transportation but also made our journey smoother and more secure. Our driver was Komang WikaSutresna (introduced at the end). Ultimately, we felt that hiring a private driver was a very wise decision!

## Day 1:

![水明漾夕陽。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6dc896b39c10af9bac23d825129c22b9.jpg)

Seminyak Sunset. Provided by Lian Yuzhun

Having heard that Seminyak's traffic is very congested, we chose to walk around casually in the morning without hiring a car. We arranged for our driver to pick us up from Seminyak in the evening to take us to our villa in Uluwatu. Komang arrived early at the agreed location to wait for us. We initially planned to go out for dinner ourselves after arriving at the villa, but Komang checked Google Street View on the way and found that the road from the villa to the main street was very dark at night and not safe to walk. Finally, when we arrived at the villa, he waited at the entrance for us to check in and then took us out to eat. This thoughtful service made us feel very warm.

## Day 2:

In the morning, we visited Oneeighty Dayclub, which we highly recommend. We arrived within an hour of opening, so we secured a front-row seat with an ocean view.

Next, we headed to Blue Point Beach to watch the surfers perform, which was truly eye-opening.

### Jie's Blue Point Beach Introduction:

Afterwards, Komang recommended we visit Uluwatu Temple to watch a traditional dance performance. He even had his friend reserve tickets for us in advance, allowing us to spend more time at Blue Point Beach without having to rush to buy tickets.

![烏魯瓦圖美景。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2ffbc76870b45338de0935d3e72d256.jpg)

The stunning views of Uluwatu. Photo provided by Lian Yuzhun

A quick reminder: Uluwatu Temple has many monkeys, more aggressive than those at Chai Mountain. Both men and women can get robbed, and you can even see remnants of stolen glasses on the ground. So, it's best not to bring any valuables when visiting.

## Day Three:

This day was dedicated to attending a wedding, so we didn't arrange Komang's car service.

## Day 4:

Today's itinerary took us from Jimbaran to Ubud. Along the way, we discovered that the restaurant we planned to visit was temporarily closed. Komang immediately helped us by calling to confirm and even checked the waterfall's official website, only to find that it was also closed due to heavy rain the previous day (despite it being the dry season). However, Komang quickly offered us alternative suggestions, ensuring we still had a great day. After visiting the waterfall, we strolled around Ubud town center and ended the day with a massage, courtesy of Komang's recommendation.

Additionally, the supervisor at Gefecon Villa Ubud, where we stayed that night, was a very friendly and helpful Taiwanese lady. She was always ready to assist with any questions we had.

> Gefecon Villa Ubud [Agoda Special Offer](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1770500)

She also recommended we buy souvenirs at Bintang Supermarket Ubud, where we later found the same nuts selling for four times the price at the airport.

## Day 5:

![聖泉寺。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dbc6e9fddb7c4642b0a7e4bfee0a2182.jpg)

Tirta Empul Temple. Photo provided by Lian Yuzhun

Today's route was a loop, starting at Tirta Empul Temple, then heading to a café for volcano views. We initially planned to visit AKASA Specialty Coffee, but Komang recommended Montana Del Café instead, noting that Taiwanese visitors seem to prefer it. Although the weather obscured the volcano view, the Korean croissants here were exceptionally delicious!

![Montana Del Café可惜天氣不好。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8bba492232d63a05b16658986210416d.jpg)

Unfortunately, the weather wasn't great at Montana Del Café. Shared by Lian Yuzhun.

Finally, we visited Tegallalang Rice Terrace. The entrance ticket doesn't include the swing ride, so if you want to try it, you'll need to purchase a ticket at the entrance. There are also special outfits available for rent inside, which make for great photos. Although this place leans towards being an Instagram hotspot, strolling through the rice terraces and listening to the screams of people on the swings was quite enjoyable.

> You can refer to this: [Complete Ubud Bali Guide: 100 Ways to Deeply Explore Ubud's Charm](/blog/ubud-guide/)

After the tour, we asked Komang to take us to our favorite restaurant of the trip - Kebun Bistro. Although the famous lamb chops were sold out, the steak recommended by the staff was also incredibly delicious! We ordered it medium rare, and when cut, there was no blood, the meat was very tender, and even the side dishes were tasty. Since the restaurant gets very busy, it's advisable to make a reservation during peak dining hours. (Added to the [Ubud Guide Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

## Day Six:

In the morning, we departed from Ubud to Tanah Lot Temple. Walking to Tanah Lot might require wading through water, so it's recommended to wear shoes and shorts that can get wet. We heard that the sunset view is beautiful, but unfortunately, our schedule had us there at noon, so we missed it.

When we arrived at Tanah Lot, we realized we had accidentally taken the hotel key from the previous night. Komang immediately offered to return it for us in a couple of days (with the hotel manager's approval), for which we were very grateful!

After finishing our visit to Tanah Lot, we headed to the Canggu area for some shopping and a beach walk. We tried Blacklist Coffee Roasters, recommended by Fei Li, and it turned out to be our favorite coffee of the entire Bali trip.

> Please refer to: [Perfect Canggu Bali Adventure Map: 80 Must-Visit Spots in Canggu](/blog/canggu-guide/)

At the end of our itinerary, we invited Komang to join us for dinner as a thank you for taking care of us over the past few days.

## Recommended Private Car Hire

In summary, for first-time visitors to Bali who want to explore attractions but lack direction, hiring a private car is an excellent choice. Not only do you get professional advice, but route planning becomes more convenient, and you don't have to worry about luggage.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fd9d65047cadfd58a1e16cce702fbb1.jpg)

Our driver, Komang WikaSutresna, is an extremely friendly person. When he heard we were attending a wedding, he was delighted and shared a short video of his own wedding from six months ago, even video chatting with his adorable son. Komang's English is quite good, and he's very attentive—any questions during the trip can be directed to him, and he'll patiently answer them.

Komang takes every guest very seriously. Throughout our journey these past few days, he has been incredibly attentive. At each destination, he would get out of the car to explain how best to explore. When we went for spa treatments, he would greet the staff and ensure our needs were properly taken care of. Although deep conversations were limited due to language barriers, there were no issues with communication regarding the itinerary. We highly recommend Komang to everyone!
