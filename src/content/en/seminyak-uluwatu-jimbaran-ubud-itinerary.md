This time, we chose to visit Bali in July to attend a friend's wedding. The travel information for Bali is quite extensive, and it took us two to three months to finalize our itinerary.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a22d6c37c8a63306b663eaf1af023ba8.jpg)

During the planning phase, we discovered that local transportation is very chaotic, with drivers often refusing to take passengers across regions or raising prices midway. To avoid these issues, we decided to hire a private driver. This not only reduced the hassle of finding transportation but also made our journey smoother and more secure. Our driver was Komang WikaSutresna (introduced at the end). In the end, we felt that hiring a private driver was a very wise decision!

## Day 1:

![水明漾夕陽。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6dc896b39c10af9bac23d825129c22b9.jpg)

Seminyak Sunset. Provided by Lian Yuzhun

Having heard that Seminyak's traffic is very congested, we chose to walk around casually in the morning without hiring a driver. We arranged for Komang to pick us up from Seminyak to our villa in Uluwatu in the evening, and he arrived early at the agreed location. We initially planned to go out for dinner ourselves after arriving at the villa, but Komang checked the route using Google Street View and found that the path from the villa to the main street was very dark at night and not safe to walk. Finally, when we arrived at the villa, he waited at the entrance for us to check-in and then took us out for dinner. This thoughtful service made us feel very warm.

## Day 2:

In the morning, we visited Oneeighty Dayclub, which we highly recommend. We arrived within an hour of opening, so we secured front-row seats with an ocean view.

Afterwards, Komang recommended we visit Uluwatu Temple to watch a traditional dance performance. He even had his friend reserve tickets for us in advance, allowing us to spend more time at Blue Point Beach without having to rush to buy tickets.

A quick reminder: Uluwatu Temple has many monkeys, more aggressive than those at Chai Mountain. Both men and women can be targeted, and we even saw many remnants of stolen glasses on the ground. So, it's best not to bring any valuables when visiting.

## Day 3:

This day was dedicated to attending a wedding, so we didn't arrange Komang's car service.

## Day 4:

Today's itinerary took us from Jimbaran to Ubud. Along the way, we discovered that the restaurant we planned to visit was temporarily closed. Komang immediately helped us by calling to confirm and also checked the waterfall's official website, only to find that it was also closed due to heavy rain the previous day (even though it was the dry season). However, Komang quickly offered us alternative suggestions, ensuring we had a smooth day. After visiting the waterfall, we strolled around Ubud town center and finally asked Komang to take us for a massage, ending the day on a relaxing note.

Additionally, the manager at Gefecon Villa Ubud, a Taiwanese lady, was extremely kind and helpful. Feel free to ask her any questions you might have.

> Gefecon Villa Ubud [Agoda Special Offer](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1770500)

She also recommended we shop for souvenirs at Bintang Supermarket Ubud, where we later found the same nuts selling for four times the price at the airport.

## Day 5:

![聖泉寺。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dbc6e9fddb7c4642b0a7e4bfee0a2182.jpg)

Tirta Empul Temple. Provided by Lian Yuzhun

Today's route was a loop, starting at Tirta Empul Temple and then heading to a café to view the volcano. We initially looked up AKASA Specialty Coffee, but Komang recommended Montana Del Café instead, mentioning that Taiwanese visitors seem to prefer it. Although the weather didn't allow us to see the volcano, the Korean croissants here were absolutely delicious!

![Montana Del Café可惜天氣不好。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8bba492232d63a05b16658986210416d.jpg)

Montana Del Café was a bit disappointing due to the bad weather. Provided by Yu-Chun Lien.

Finally, we visited Tegallalang Rice Terrace. The entrance ticket doesn't include the swing ride, so if you want to try it, you'll need to purchase a ticket at the entrance. Inside, you can also rent special outfits for photos, which turn out beautifully. Although this place leans towards being an Instagram hotspot, strolling through the rice fields and listening to the screams of people on the swings is quite enjoyable.

> You can refer to: [Complete Ubud Bali Guide: 100 Ways to Deeply Explore Ubud｜Discover the Charm of Ubud](/blog/2023-11-17-655054b1fd897800011d4d2c/)

After the tour, we asked Komang to take us to our favorite restaurant of the trip - Kebun Bistro. Although the famous lamb chops were sold out, the steak recommended by the staff was incredibly delicious! We ordered it medium rare, and it was perfectly cooked with no blood, very tender, and even the side dishes were excellent. Due to the high number of customers, it's advisable to make a reservation during peak dining hours. (Added to the [Ubud Guide Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

## Day Six:

In the morning, we headed from Ubud to Tanah Lot Temple. Walking to Tanah Lot might require wading through water, so it's recommended to wear shoes and shorts that can get wet. It's said to be beautiful at sunset, but unfortunately, our schedule had us there at noon, so we missed it.

When we arrived at Tanah Lot, we realized we had accidentally taken the hotel key from the previous night. Komang immediately offered to return it for us in a couple of days (with the hotel manager's approval), for which we were very grateful!

After visiting Tanah Lot, we went shopping and walked along the beach in the Canggu area. We tried Blacklist Coffee Roasters, recommended by Fei Li, and it turned out to be our favorite coffee of the entire Bali trip.

> Please refer to: [Perfect Canggu Bali Adventure Map: 80 Must-Visit Spots in Canggu](/blog/2023-02-11-64db6b7efd897800013a9815/)

At the end of the trip, we invited Komang to join us for dinner to thank him for taking care of us over the past few days.

## Recommended Private Car Hire

In summary, for first-time visitors to Bali who want to explore attractions but lack direction, hiring a private car is an excellent choice. Not only do you get professional advice, but route planning becomes much more convenient, and you don't have to worry about luggage.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fd9d65047cadfd58a1e16cce702fbb1.jpg)

Our driver, Komang WikaSutresna, is an extremely friendly person. When he heard we were here for a wedding, he was delighted and shared a short video of his own wedding from six months ago, even video chatting with his adorable son. Komang's English is quite good, and he's very attentive—any questions during the trip can be directed to him, and he patiently answers them all.

Komang takes every guest very seriously. Throughout our journey these past few days, he has been incredibly meticulous. At each destination, he would get out of the car to explain how best to explore the area. When we went for a spa, he would greet the staff and ensure our needs were properly taken care of. Although language barriers prevented deeper conversations, communication about the itinerary was completely seamless. We highly recommend Komang to everyone!
