This time, we traveled to Bali in July to attend a friend's wedding. The travel information for Bali is quite overwhelming, and it took us two to three months to plan the itinerary.

![【Seminyak - Uluwatu - Jimbaran - Ubud】Complete Bali Itinerar](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a22d6c37c8a63306b663eaf1af023ba8.jpg)

During our pre-trip planning, we discovered that local traffic is chaotic, with drivers often refusing cross-region passengers or hiking up prices midway. To avoid these hassles, we decided to hire a private driver, which not only saved us the trouble of finding transportation but also made our trip smoother and more worry-free. Our driver was Komang WikaSutresna (introduced at the bottom). In the end, we felt that choosing a private driver was a truly wise decision!

## Day 1:

![水明漾夕陽。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6dc896b39c10af9bac23d825129c22b9.jpg)

Seminyak sunset. Provided by Lian Yuzhun

Having heard that Seminyak's traffic is extremely congested, we chose to explore on foot in the morning without a driver. We arranged with our driver to pick us up from Seminyak in the evening and take us to our villa in Uluwatu. Komang arrived early at the meeting point and waited for us. We originally planned to go out for dinner on our own after reaching the villa, but Komang checked Google Street View along the way and noticed that the road from the villa to the main street was very dark at night and unsafe to walk. In the end, when we arrived at the villa, he waited at the door until we checked in, then drove us to dinner. This thoughtful service made us feel truly warm.

A quick heads-up: Uluwatu Temple is full of monkeys—more aggressive than the ones at Chai Mountain. They'll snatch things from both men and women, and you'll even see plenty of shattered glasses on the ground. So it's best not to bring any valuables when you go.

## Day 3:

We attended a wedding today, so we didn’t arrange Komang’s private car service.

## Day 4:

Today’s itinerary took us from Jimbaran to Ubud. Along the way, we found that the restaurant we planned to visit was temporarily closed. Komang immediately called to confirm and also checked the waterfall’s official website, which revealed it was closed due to the heavy rainstorm the day before (even though it’s the dry season). However, Komang quickly offered us new suggestions, allowing us to smoothly complete the day’s plans. After visiting the waterfall, we returned to Ubud town for a stroll and finally asked Komang to drop us off for a massage, wrapping up the day.

Also, at Gefecon Villa Ubud where we stayed that night, there was a Taiwanese supervisor who was incredibly warm and helpful—she was happy to answer any questions we had.

## Day 5:

![聖泉寺。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dbc6e9fddb7c4642b0a7e4bfee0a2182.jpg)

Tirta Empul Temple. Photo by Lian Yuzhun.

Today's route formed an O-shape, starting at Tirta Empul Temple, then heading to a café with volcano views. We originally planned to visit AKASA Specialty Coffee, but Komang recommended Montana Del Café instead, saying Taiwanese travelers seem to love it. Although the weather was too cloudy to see the volcano, the Korean-style croissants here were absolutely delicious!

https://youtube.com/shorts/fiYs5GKdINU

![Montana Del Café可惜天氣不好。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8bba492232d63a05b16658986210416d.jpg)

Montana Del Café — too bad the weather wasn't cooperating. Photo by Lian Yuzhun.

Finally, we visited the Tegallalang Rice Terrace. The entrance fee doesn't include the swing ticket — if you want to try it, buy a ticket at the gate. There are also special outfits available for rent, perfect for photos. Though it's a bit of an Instagram hotspot, strolling through the rice terraces and listening to the screams of swing riders was surprisingly enjoyable.

https://youtu.be/op78Dk8CjL4

> For more inspiration: [The Ultimate Ubud Guide: 100 Ways to Dive into Ubud's Charm](/blog/ubud-guide/)

After the tour, we asked Komang to take us to our favorite restaurant of the trip — Kebun Bistro. Unfortunately, their famous lamb chops were sold out, but the steak recommended by the staff was equally amazing! We ordered it medium-rare, and when cut, there was no blood — the meat was incredibly tender, and even the sides were delicious. Since it gets busy, I recommend booking ahead during meal times. (Already added to the [Ubud Guide Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

## Day 6:

In the morning, we set off from Ubud to Tanah Lot Temple. You may need to wade through water to reach the temple, so wear water-friendly shoes and shorts. I've heard the sunset view is stunning, but unfortunately, our schedule placed us there at noon, so we missed it.

[Bali Must-Visit Attractions: Detailed Guide to Tanah Lot Temple, Including Tides and Nearby Sights](https://gobaligo.id/blog/bali-tanah-lot-guide/)

When we arrived at Tanah Lot, we realized we had accidentally taken the key from our previous night's hotel. Komang immediately said he could return it for us in a couple of days (after getting approval from the hotel supervisor). We were truly grateful for his help.

After finishing at Tanah Lot, we headed to Canggu for some shopping and a beach stroll. We tried Blacklist Coffee Roasters, recommended by Fei Li, and it became our favorite coffee spot during this Bali trip.

At the end of the trip, we invited Komang to join us for dinner to thank him for his care over the past few days.

## Recommended Private Driver Service

In summary, for first-time visitors to Bali who want to explore attractions but lack direction, hiring a private driver is an excellent choice. Not only do you get professional advice, but route planning is also more convenient, and you don't have to worry about luggage.

![Recommended Private Driver Service](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fd9d65047cadfd58a1e16cce702fbb1.jpg)

Our driver, Komang Wika Sutresna, is a very friendly person. When he heard we were attending a wedding, he was delighted and even shared his own wedding video from six months ago, along with a video call with his adorable son. Komang's English is decent, and he is very attentive. Feel free to ask him any questions during the trip—he will patiently answer them.

Komang takes every guest seriously. Throughout our journey, he was extremely meticulous: at each destination, he would get out of the car to guide us on how to explore, and when we went to the spa, he would greet the staff to ensure our needs were well taken care of. Although language barriers prevented deep conversations, communication regarding the itinerary was never an issue. We highly recommend Komang to everyone!
