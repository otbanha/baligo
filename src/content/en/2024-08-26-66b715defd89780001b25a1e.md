Bali, this tropical paradise, has become more than just a vacation destination for me. It's a place where I always discover new surprises with each visit. During this trip, I tried some luxurious accommodations and several well-known restaurants, while also experiencing the challenges of local transportation. I hope my experiences can provide useful tips and inspiration for your own Bali adventure.

## Accommodation Experience

During this trip, I stayed at two very different hotels—Peppers Villa, which exudes luxury, and the historic Hilton Hotel. Each had its own unique charm and highlights, but there were also some unexpected surprises and disappointments. Let me take you through my experiences at these two hotels.

### Peppers Villa (3 Nights)

![Peppers Seminyak 大推！(June Lin提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6f9f85b8a7491479d13ae7c0c626b00e.jpg)

Peppers Seminyak is highly recommended! (Contributed by June Lin)

We chose Peppers Villa, which recently joined the Accor group, located in the heart of Seminyak. It was very convenient for both shopping in the city and heading to the beach. There are 56 villas in total, each designed to be extremely comfortable, offering a sense of luxury yet warmth. The service was impeccable, with every detail taken care of from check-in to check-out. The rooms were spacious and bright, fully equipped, making it feel like a relaxing home away from home. The breakfast exceeded expectations, with fresh ingredients and excellent cooking. Additionally, their room service was reasonably priced, and the food quality was good, making it one of the highlights of my stay. Overall, I would rate this stay 99 out of 100.

### Hilton Hotel Nusa Dua (2 Nights)

![Hilton Nusa Dua, 五星的價格，不及格的硬體設施（June Lin 提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_999f7ae586125ba8183e3108a025fce5.jpg)

Hilton Nusa Dua, Five-Star Prices, Subpar Facilities (Provided by June Lin)

Nine years ago, I stayed at this Hilton hotel in the Nusa Dua resort area. This revisit, however, left me somewhat disappointed. Firstly, the pool water quality made me uncomfortable—it was so murky that even my son refused to swim. Secondly, the beach sand seemed to have diminished. I recall burying someone in this sand years ago, but now I couldn’t find that same feeling. Although the breakfast selection was abundant, this experience significantly lowered my impression of the hotel, and I wouldn’t consider staying here again. I rate this stay 60 out of 100.

## Dining Experiences

Bali’s culinary scene is equally exciting, ranging from Instagram-worthy restaurants to traditional local eateries, each with its unique charm. During this trip, we deliberately selected several highly recommended restaurants to try, and each left a deep impression on me, from the decor and ambiance to the taste of the dishes.

### The Cave

![The Cave餐廳（June Lin提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b691df9fca528e5219964aca698c0e60.jpg)

The Cave Restaurant (Provided by June Lin)

On this trip, we tried several Instagram-worthy restaurants, and The Cave (inside The Edge) was one of them. The cost is around USD$130 per person, which isn't cheap. However, if you're looking for a unique atmosphere, it's worth a visit. The restaurant's decor is quite distinctive, but given the high price, I think one experience is enough.

### Oneeighty Day Club

![Oneeighty Day Club（June Lin提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_443efccc3319ae3e31364d532139dc17.jpg)

Oneeighty Day Club (provided by June Lin)

This time, we specifically booked the VIP area at Oneeighty Day Club and got front-row seats, which felt great. The reservation fee plus meal cost was around USD$50 per person. Although the price is on the higher side, the food was surprisingly delicious, and the service was excellent. The infinity pool here is quite deep at 1.7 meters, so we could only take a few beautiful photos by the poolside before focusing on enjoying the meal. Overall, I would rate this dining experience a 95 out of 100.

> Reservation and payment methods, video introduction: [Exploring Oneeighty Day Club: Bali's Cliffside Pool with Stunning Views](/blog/2023-12-07-6571846ffd89780001fe97ee/)

### Cretya Ubud

Cretya Ubud is Ubud's famous swing restaurant, and the food was much better than I expected, with flavors and textures that exceeded my imagination. As for the swing photos, this place has become a global hotspot for women looking to take Instagram-worthy shots. Early in the morning, the area around the swing is packed with people eager to take photos. I suggest not challenging the farthest swing; the smaller swings nearby actually make for better photos. Our group ended up taking a bunch of hilarious, terrified-looking photos, which was quite funny. I would rate the food 90 out of 100 and the Instagram photo experience 70 out of 100.

### Ku De Ta

![Ku De Ta（June Lin提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_255fda84dc588535e81c5ffc71aebfbf.jpg)

Ku De Ta (provided by June Lin)

Ku De Ta is one of Seminyak's beachfront restaurants where you can enjoy delicious food while watching the stunning sunset. The service is excellent, and the live music complements the atmosphere perfectly, making it a relaxing experience. After your meal, you can take a stroll on the beach. The dinner prices are very reasonable, costing around $16-19 USD per person, which is a great deal compared to what you might pay in Taiwan for similar quality. I give this restaurant a 90 out of 100.

### Potato Head

Potato Head is another restaurant in Seminyak and was the biggest surprise of my trip. We visited on a Saturday and enjoyed Indonesian and Balinese dishes, each of which was incredibly delicious and exceeded my expectations. The beach next to the restaurant is also beautiful, making it the highlight of my culinary journey. The average cost per person is around $32 USD, though I can't remember the exact price, but it was definitely worth it. I give this restaurant a 99 out of 100.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6f15aa6fe69a04c67c5c8622b55bb1ab.png)

### Warung Nia

Warung Nia is a very popular pork rib restaurant in downtown Seminyak. When we went for lunch, there were a bunch of foreigners taking a cooking class inside. The restaurant is semi-open-air with no air conditioning, so the environment was a bit stuffy. As for the pork ribs, I found them a bit too sweet, and overall, the experience was just average. I give this restaurant an 80 out of 100.

### Koral

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bda877aa3ae4ea0760efc8f0b0bd4b4.jpg)

Koral is a high-end restaurant located within the Kempinski Hotel. We had reserved three tables because of our large group, but upon arrival, we were told that two of our tables had been canceled. The staff loudly claimed they had contacted me two days prior and I hadn't responded. This was very frustrating because I was either at the beach, by the pool, or enjoying meals—who has time to check emails? Moreover, their attitude was very arrogant. If you're not a white person dressed in designer clothes, I suggest you don't subject yourself to this kind of treatment. In the end, I argued with them in English, and their attitude softened, but the overall experience was terrible. I give this restaurant a 0 out of 100.
