**Bottom line**: Penglipuran Village, located in the Gianyar highlands of eastern Bali, has been an official tourism village since 1988 and is consistently listed among the **UNWTO's Best Tourism Villages**. Its highlights include the best-preserved traditional architectural settlement, a warm home-visit culture with locals, and a mysterious giant bamboo forest behind the village. Ideal for travelers seeking an authentic Balinese traditional experience and strong photo opportunities, it's about a 1-hour drive from Ubud.

Bali, one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations, draws countless visitors with its stunning beaches, cultural heritage, and unique atmosphere. Yet, hidden within this breathtaking island lies a gem: **Penglipuran Village**, waiting to be explored.

## **Background of Penglipuran Village**

Nestled in eastern Bali, far from the hustle and bustle, Penglipuran Village is celebrated as one of the island's best-preserved traditional communities. Its significance lies in maintaining original Balinese culture and a unique way of life. Immersed in Balinese traditions, it's a priceless treasure of Bali.

### **One of the World's Best Tourism Villages**

Since becoming an official tourism village in 1988, Penglipuran has been consistently listed among the **UNWTO's Best Tourism Villages**. Centered on 'safety, cleanliness, and deep culture,' it stands as a model for Bali's tourism transformation.

## **Attractions and Activities in Penglipuran Village**

One of the greatest charms of Penglipuran Village lies in its well-preserved traditional architecture and layout. Here, you'll feel the passage of time, as if stepping back into an ancient Balinese community. The winding streets and old houses exude a rich Balinese atmosphere that is truly awe-inspiring.

Penglipuran Village is also a venue for traditional Balinese ceremonies and festivals. Visitors have the chance to participate in and observe local rituals, experiencing authentic Balinese religious culture. Additionally, you can immerse yourself in the daily lives of the villagers, admire their traditional crafts and activities, and gain a deeper understanding of this unique community.

## **A Lush Bamboo Village**

Not far from Kintamani, nestled among rolling hills and countryside, lies Penglipuran Village—a serene and charming place. The village structure remains nearly intact, with orderly stone-paved roads and ancient traditional buildings intertwined with meticulously manicured gardens. The village continues its traditional building methods, with a particular emphasis on bamboo. Almost everything in the village—kitchens, grand gates and doorways, furniture, roofs, and entrances—is woven from abundant plant materials. Notably, distinctive creations like bamboo roofs have become unique hallmarks of this small community, hard to find elsewhere.

## **Eco-Friendly and Beautiful**

Yet, what’s most stunning is the village’s beauty, reflected in its cleanliness. This is because environmental awareness is an integral part of daily life here, passed down through generations. Neither locals nor visitors litter, and smokers can only light up in designated areas. Penglipuran Village holds a special title: along with Giethoorn in the Netherlands and Mawlynnong in India, it is recognized as one of the three cleanest villages in the world.

In June 2025, the village once again received the **Kalpataru Lestari Appreciation Award** from Indonesia’s Ministry of Environment, an honor specifically for communities and individuals with outstanding contributions to environmental sustainability.

**Village Chief Wayan Budiarta** said, 'This honor is the result of the entire village’s long-term efforts to protect the environment. Our culture of cleanliness has been passed down through generations.'

## **A Timeless Experience**

Visitors can come to this village year-round, but the best time to visit is around the Galungan religious festival. During these days, the village is adorned with special decorations called "penjor," hung on bamboo poles. At these special moments, you can also witness young girls in traditional Balinese attire gracefully carrying offerings on their heads as they head to the village's Hindu temple, like a ceremonial stage.

## Tasting Loloh Cem-cem

![品嚐Loloh Cem-cem](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cf5b527a6abdf1c3481af814009eb8d9.jpg)

Loloh Cemcem is a traditional drink from Penglipuran Village. Cemcem leaves are one of the main specialties of Penglipuran Village. Locals turn these leaves into a fresh, health-boosting beverage.

This green health drink is often thought to taste bitter, as many believe cemcem leaves naturally produce bitterness when processed into food. In fact, this idea is wrong. When you drink Loloh Cemcem, you won't taste any bitterness because the palm sugar syrup balances it out.

During preparation, cemcem leaves are mixed with other plants like castor leaves, betel leaves, and coral tree leaves. These four types of leaves are mashed and then steeped in warm water. After that, cinnamon and palm sugar syrup are added to neutralize the bitterness. To enhance the drink's flavor, some vendors add coconut meat. Adding ice makes it even more refreshing! It's the perfect thirst-quencher in Bali's hot weather—your thirst will vanish instantly.

Loloh Cemcem is not just a cooling drink; it also offers various health benefits, such as lowering high blood pressure and improving digestive issues. Because of these benefits, visitors to Penglipuran Village often try Loloh Cemcem. It's also easy to find—almost every resident sells it, usually right in front of their homes. A bottle of Loloh Cemcem is very affordable, so you can enjoy it without worrying about the price.

## Tips for Visiting Penglipuran Village

When visiting Penglipuran Village, it's crucial to respect local culture and customs. Dressing appropriately and behaving politely will help you interact well with the villagers. Please note that you need to ask for permission from locals before taking photos.

We strongly recommend hiring a local guide, which will give you a deeper understanding of the village's history and traditions. The guide can provide insightful explanations, share the village's stories, and answer any questions you may have.

In Penglipuran Village, you can stroll through the peaceful alleys, admire traditional-style houses, neatly trimmed gardens, and the friendly smiles of the locals. The residents have always taken pride in respecting the environment and culture, so the entire village maintains a high level of cleanliness and pristine beauty.

As you explore the village, be sure to visit the local temples and cultural heritage sites to learn about the Balinese people's religious beliefs and traditions. You can also sample authentic Balinese cuisine at local stalls, letting your taste buds experience the unique flavors of this land.

Whether you're seeking cultural immersion or pure adventure, Penglipuran Village is a destination not to be missed. This ancient Balinese community transports you to a unique time and space, allowing you to experience the profound depth of Balinese culture.

Entry requires a ticket. Prices are 25,000 IDR for Indonesians and 50,000 IDR for foreigners.

## **📚 FAQ - Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Where is Penglipuran Village?**

Penglipuran Village is located in the Bangli Regency of Bali, Indonesia, near the active volcano area of Lake Batur.

### **Why is Penglipuran Village called the cleanest village in the world?**

The villagers have long practiced strict waste management and environmental protection measures, with no visible trash anywhere in the village and streets kept spotlessly clean.

### **Can you stay overnight in Penglipuran Village?**

The village primarily offers local homestays, providing simple and comfortable accommodations that require advance booking.

### **Can you visit the bamboo groves in Penglipuran Village?**

Yes. Some bamboo groves are open to visitors, and guided tours are available to explain their sustainable management model.

### **Is there an entrance fee for the village?**

Yes, visitors need to pay a small entrance fee, which serves as a funding source for the village's environmental protection and maintenance.
