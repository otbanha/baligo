As one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations, Bali attracts countless visitors with its stunning beaches, cultural heritage, and unique atmosphere. Yet, on this breathtaking island, there's a hidden treasure waiting to be explored: **Penglipuran Village**.

## **The Background of Penglipuran Village**

Located in eastern Bali, far from the hustle and bustle of the city, Penglipuran Village is renowned as one of the best-preserved traditional communities in Bali. Its special significance lies in maintaining original Balinese culture and a unique way of life. The village is steeped in Balinese culture, making it a valuable treasure of the island.

### **Recognized as One of the World's Best Tourism Villages**

Since becoming an official tourism village in 1988, Penglipuran has consistently been listed by the **United Nations World Tourism Organization (UNWTO)** as one of the world's best tourism villages. With its core values of 'safety, cleanliness, and cultural depth,' it has become a model for Bali's tourism transformation.

## **Attractions and Activities in Penglipuran Village**

One of Penglipuran Village's greatest charms lies in its well-preserved traditional architecture and layout. Here, you'll feel as though you've stepped back in time, transported to an ancient Balinese community. The village's winding streets and historic houses showcase the rich Balinese culture, leaving visitors in awe.

Penglipuran Village is also a venue for traditional Balinese ceremonies and festivals. Visitors have the opportunity to participate in and observe local rituals, experiencing authentic Balinese religious culture. Additionally, you can immerse yourself in the daily lives of the villagers, appreciating their traditional crafts and activities, thereby gaining a deeper understanding of this unique community.

## **The Lush Bamboo Village**

Not far from Kintamani, nestled among rolling hills and countryside, lies Penglipuran Village, a serene and charming place. The village's structure remains largely intact, with orderly stone-paved roads and ancient traditional buildings interspersed with meticulously maintained gardens. This village continues to uphold traditional architectural methods, particularly making extensive use of bamboo. Almost everything in the village, from kitchens to grand gates and doorways, furniture, roofs, and even the main gates, is woven from abundant plant materials. Notably, many distinctive creations, such as bamboo roofs, have become unique symbols of this small community, hard to find elsewhere.

## **Eco-Friendly and Beautiful**

However, what is most stunning about the village is its beauty, reflected in its cleanliness. This is because environmental consciousness is an integral part of daily life here, passed down through generations. Both locals and visitors refrain from littering, and smokers are restricted to designated areas. Penglipuran Village holds the special distinction of being one of the three cleanest villages in the world, alongside Giethoorn in the Netherlands and Mawlynnong in India.

In June 2025, the village was once again honored with the **Kalpataru Lestari Appreciation Award** by the Indonesian Ministry of Environment, an accolade that recognizes communities and individuals who have made outstanding contributions to environmental sustainability.

**Village Head Wayan Budiarta** stated, 'This honor is the result of the long-term efforts of the entire village in maintaining the environment. Our culture of cleanliness has been passed down through generations.'

## **A Journey Through Time**

Visitors can come to the village year-round, but the best time to visit is around the Galungan religious festival. During these days, the village is adorned with special decorations called 'penjor,' which are hung on bamboo poles. On these special occasions, you can also witness young girls dressed in traditional Balinese attire, gracefully carrying offerings on their heads as they make their way to the village's Hindu temple, as if part of a ceremonial stage.

## Tasting Loloh Cemcem

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cf5b527a6abdf1c3481af814009eb8d9.jpg)

Loloh Cemcem is a traditional drink from Penglipuran Village. Cemcem leaves are one of the main specialties of Penglipuran Village. Local residents turn these leaves into a fresh beverage that is beneficial for health.

This green health drink is often thought to have a bitter taste. Many believe that cemcem leaves inherently produce bitterness when used in food. In fact, this notion is incorrect. When you drink Loloh Cemcem, you won't experience bitterness because palm sugar syrup balances out the bitter flavor.

During the preparation process, cemcem leaves are mixed with other plants such as physic nut leaves, betel leaves, and coral tree leaves. These four types of leaves are crushed and then soaked in warm water. Afterward, cinnamon and palm sugar syrup are added to neutralize the bitterness of the leaves. To enhance the drink's flavor, some sellers intentionally add coconut meat. Adding ice cubes makes it even more refreshing! This is the perfect thirst-quenching drink for Bali's hot weather. Your thirst will vanish instantly.

Loloh Cemcem is not just a refreshing drink; it also offers numerous health benefits. For example, it can help lower high blood pressure and improve digestive issues. Because of these advantages, visitors to Penglipuran Village often try Loloh Cemcem. This drink is also easy to find. Almost all residents of Penglipuran Village sell it, usually right in front of their homes. A bottle of Loloh Cemcem is very affordable, so you can try it without worrying about the price.

## Tips for Visiting Penglipuran Village

When visiting Penglipuran Village, it is crucial to respect local culture and customs. Dressing appropriately and behaving politely will help you interact well with the villagers. Please note that photography requires the consent of local residents.

We strongly recommend hiring a local guide, as this will allow you to gain deeper insights into the village's history and traditions. The guide will provide you with a more profound understanding, explain the village's stories, and answer any questions you may have.

In Penglipuran Village, you can stroll through peaceful alleys, admire traditional-style houses, meticulously trimmed gardens, and the friendly smiles of local residents. The villagers take pride in respecting the environment and their culture, which is why the entire village remains exceptionally clean and retains its original beauty.

While exploring the village, don't forget to visit local temples and cultural heritage sites to learn about Balinese religious beliefs and traditions. Additionally, you can savor authentic Balinese cuisine at local stalls, allowing your taste buds to experience the unique flavors of this land.

Whether you're seeking cultural immersion or pure travel adventure, Penglipuran Village is a destination not to be missed. This ancient Balinese community will transport you to a unique time and space, allowing you to experience the profound depth of Balinese culture.

Admission tickets are required to enter. The price is $2 for Indonesians and $3 for foreigners.

## **📚FAQ Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Where is Penglipuran Village located?**

Penglipuran Village is located in Bangli Regency, Bali, Indonesia, near the active volcano Mount Batur and Lake Batur area.

### **Why is Penglipuran Village known as the cleanest village in the world?**

The villagers have long practiced strict waste management and environmental protection measures, with no visible trash in the village and streets kept spotlessly clean.

### **Can I stay overnight in Penglipuran Village?**

The village mainly offers local homestays, providing simple and comfortable accommodations that require advance booking.

### Can visitors explore the bamboo forest in Penglipuran Village?

Yes. Some bamboo forests are open to visitors, and guided tours are available to introduce their sustainable management practices.

### Is there an entrance fee for the village?

Yes, visitors need to pay a small entrance fee, which serves as funding for the village's environmental protection and maintenance.
