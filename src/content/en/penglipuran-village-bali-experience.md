As one of Indonesia’s most beloved travel destinations, Bali draws countless visitors with its stunning beaches, cultural heritage, and unique atmosphere. Yet hidden within this breathtaking island lies a true gem: **Penglipuran Village**, waiting to be explored.

## **The Background of Penglipuran Village**

Nestled in eastern Bali, far from the noise and bustle of the city, Penglipuran Village is celebrated as one of the island’s most intact traditional communities. Its special significance lies in preserving original Balinese culture and a unique way of life. Immersed in Balinese traditions, this village is a priceless treasure of Bali.

### **One of the World’s Best Tourism Villages**

Since becoming an official tourism village in 1988, Penglipuran has been consistently listed among the **World Tourism Organization (UNWTO)** Best Tourism Villages. With a core focus on “safety, cleanliness, and deep culture,” it stands as a model for Bali’s tourism transformation.

## **Penglipuran Village Attractions and Activities**

One of the greatest charms of Penglipuran Village is its well-preserved traditional architecture and layout. Here, you’ll feel the passage of time, as if stepping back into an ancient Balinese community. The winding streets and age-old houses exude a rich Balinese atmosphere that is truly awe-inspiring.

Penglipuran Village is also a venue for traditional Balinese ceremonies and festivals. Visitors have the opportunity to participate in and observe local rituals, experiencing authentic Balinese religious culture. Additionally, you can immerse yourself in the daily lives of the villagers, admire their traditional crafts and activities, and gain a deeper understanding of this unique community.

## **A Verdant Bamboo Village**

Not far from Kintamani, nestled among rolling hills and countryside, lies Penglipuran Village—a serene and delightful place. The village structure remains nearly intact, with orderly stone-paved roads and ancient traditional buildings intertwined with meticulously manicured gardens. This village continues its traditional building methods, making particular use of bamboo. Almost everything in the village—from kitchens and grand gates to doorways, furniture, roofs, and entrances—is woven from abundant plant materials. Notably, many distinctive creations, such as bamboo roofs, have become unique hallmarks of this small community, hard to find elsewhere.

## **Eco-Friendly and Beautiful**

Yet, what is most stunning is the village's beauty, reflected in its cleanliness. This is because environmental awareness is an integral part of daily life here, passed down through generations. Neither locals nor visitors litter, and smokers can only smoke in designated areas. Penglipuran Village holds a special title: along with Giethoorn in the Netherlands and Mawlynnong in India, it is recognized as one of the three cleanest villages in the world.

In June 2025, the village once again received the **Kalpataru Lestari Appreciation Award** from Indonesia's Ministry of Environment, an award specifically honoring communities and individuals with outstanding contributions to environmental sustainability.

**Village Chief Wayan Budiarta** stated, 'This honor is the result of the long-term efforts of all villagers to maintain the environment. Our culture of cleanliness has been passed down through generations.'

## **A Timeless Experience**

Visitors can come to this village year-round, but the best time to visit is around the Galungan religious festival. During these days, the village is adorned with special decorations called 'penjor,' hung on bamboo poles. In these special moments, you can also witness young girls in traditional Balinese attire, carrying offerings on their heads as they gracefully make their way to the village's Hindu temple, like a ceremonial stage.

## Tasting Loloh Cem-cem

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cf5b527a6abdf1c3481af814009eb8d9.jpg)

Loloh Cemcem is a traditional drink from Penglipuran Village. Cemcem leaves are one of the main specialties of the village. Locals turn these leaves into a fresh, health-boosting beverage.

This green, healthy drink is often thought to be bitter. Many believe that cemcem leaves naturally produce a bitter taste when used in food. In fact, that idea is wrong. When you drink Loloh Cemcem, you won't taste any bitterness because the palm sugar syrup balances it out perfectly.

During preparation, cemcem leaves are mixed with other plants like physic nut leaves, betel nut leaves, and coral tree leaves. These four types of leaves are pounded together and then steeped in warm water. After that, cinnamon and palm sugar syrup are added to neutralize the bitterness. To enhance the flavor, some vendors also add coconut meat. Adding ice makes it even more refreshing—this is the ultimate thirst-quencher in Bali's hot weather, and your thirst will vanish instantly.

Loloh Cemcem is not just a cooling drink; it also offers several health benefits, such as lowering high blood pressure and improving digestive issues. Because of these advantages, visitors to Penglipuran Village often try Loloh Cemcem. It's also very easy to find—almost every resident in the village sells it, usually right in front of their homes. A bottle of Loloh Cemcem is very affordable, so you can enjoy it without worrying about the price.

## Tips for Visiting Penglipuran Village

When visiting Penglipuran Village, it's essential to respect local culture and customs. Dressing appropriately and behaving politely will help you interact well with the villagers. Please note that you should ask for permission before taking photos.

We highly recommend hiring a local guide, which will give you a deeper understanding of the village's history and traditions. The guide can provide insightful explanations, share stories about the village, and answer any questions you may have.

In Penglipuran Village, you can stroll through quiet lanes, admire traditional-style houses, well-manicured gardens, and the friendly smiles of the locals. The residents have always taken pride in respecting their environment and culture, so the village remains exceptionally clean and retains its pristine beauty.

While exploring the village, don't forget to visit the local temples and cultural heritage sites to learn about Balinese religious beliefs and traditions. Also, be sure to sample authentic Balinese cuisine at local stalls, letting your taste buds experience the unique flavors of this land.

Whether you're seeking a cultural experience or pure travel adventure, Penglipuran Village is a destination not to be missed. This ancient Balinese community transports you to a unique time and space, letting you feel the deep heritage of Balinese culture.

Entry requires a ticket. Prices are 25,000 IDR for Indonesians and 50,000 IDR for foreigners.

## **📚 FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Where is Penglipuran Village?**

Penglipuran Village is located in the Bangli Regency of Bali, Indonesia, near the active volcano and Lake Batur area.

### **Why is Penglipuran Village called the cleanest village in the world?**

Villagers have long practiced strict waste management and environmental measures. There is no visible trash anywhere in the village, and the streets are spotless.

### **Can You Stay Overnight in Penglipuran Village?**

The village mainly offers homestay accommodations, providing a simple and comfortable experience. Advance booking is required.

### **Can You Visit the Bamboo Forest in Penglipuran Village?**

Yes. Some bamboo groves are open to visitors, and guided tours are available to explain their sustainable management model.

### **Is There an Entrance Fee for the Village?**

Yes, visitors need to pay a small entrance fee, which goes toward funding the village's environmental protection and maintenance.
