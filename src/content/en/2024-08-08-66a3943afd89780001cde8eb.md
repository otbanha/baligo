This trip to Bali not only immersed me in the island's breathtaking scenery but also treated me to an unforgettable seafood feast at Mr. Lobster. From lobster to marble goby fish, every bite was a delight. Here’s a glimpse into my culinary experience:

## Customer A: Mr. Lobster Restaurant

This is a dining experience you must try!

Before my trip, I stumbled upon this Taiwanese-owned restaurant in a Bali community group, which convinced me to give it a try. Upon arrival, we ordered lobster, crab, and Thai shrimp. Each bite was exceptional—the lobster meat was tender and sweet, the crab was rich and flavorful, and the Thai shrimp’s roe had a delicate aroma that was irresistible. Two lobsters, two crabs, and 600g of Thai shrimp cost only around $32 to $65, making it an incredible value!

What’s more, the owner generously delivered their signature lobster porridge to my accommodation. Before leaving Bali, I enjoyed this dish at the airport for just $5, and it was unbelievably delicious! This restaurant is absolutely worth a visit!

## Customer B: Highly Recommended Seafood

https://youtube.com/shorts/0pk0syQh5sw

**Mantis Shrimp** In Bali, I tried mantis shrimp that was nearly 40cm long. Its meat was tender and sweet, even surpassing lobster in flavor. Sharing one between two people was just perfect.

**Marble Goby** Although marble goby is more expensive than grouper, its flesh is incredibly tender and virtually boneless, making it very easy to eat. In Taiwan, a marble goby costs around $20, but here it's only half that price.

**Langoustine** Bali's langoustines come from the sea, and their meat is delightfully springy, rivaling that of larger wild lobsters. At around $7 each, you're better off enjoying eight langoustines here - the total meat yield is double that of a large lobster.

**Thai Shrimp** While Bali's Thai shrimp are smaller than those in Taiwan, their flesh is juicier and closer in texture to grass shrimp.

**Flower Crab** We tried the flower crab here - though not large in size, the belly was firm and deliciously sweet. The owner also offers cooked food delivery, with a bowl of langoustine porridge costing just around $5.

### Restaurant Summary

Here, you can enjoy seafood on-site or opt for live seafood or cooked food delivery. If it's not too much trouble, we recommend dining at the restaurant directly.

## Customer C: Warm Hospitality from the Owner

Many thanks to the lobster restaurant owner for their warm hospitality yesterday. We truly felt the big-hearted care and warmth of Taiwanese looking out for Taiwanese.

The average cost per person for this meal was less than $30, making it excellent value for money. Our table featured six dishes including Thai shrimp, lobster, squid, stonefish, plus a delicious soup. The lobster was sweet and juicy, the stonefish tender and smooth - truly a feast for the senses. Every dish was a delightful surprise. We're deeply grateful for this culinary experience - it was truly an unforgettable banquet.
