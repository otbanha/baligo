During my visit to Bali, I was completely captivated by the island's breathtaking scenery. At Mr. Lobster Restaurant, I indulged in an exquisite seafood feast that left me in awe. From succulent lobster to delicate marble goby, every bite was a delightful surprise. Here's a glimpse into my culinary journey:

## Customer A: Mr. Lobster Restaurant

This is a culinary experience that comes highly recommended!

Before our trip, I stumbled upon this Taiwanese-owned restaurant in a Bali Facebook group, which convinced us to give it a try. Upon arrival, we ordered lobster, crab, and Thai shrimp. Each bite was absolutely delightful—the lobster meat was tender and sweet, the crab was incredibly flavorful, and the subtle aroma of the Thai shrimp roe was irresistible. For two lobsters, two crabs, and 600 grams of Thai shrimp, we spent only around $32 to $65 USD, making it an incredible value!

What's particularly noteworthy is that the owner generously delivered their signature lobster porridge to my accommodation. Before leaving Bali, I had the chance to savor this dish at the airport for just $5, and it was incredibly delicious! This restaurant is definitely worth a try!

## Customer B: Highly Recommended Seafood Delights

https://youtube.com/shorts/0pk0syQh5sw

**Mantis Shrimp** In Bali, I had the chance to try mantis shrimp that were nearly 40cm long. The meat was tender and sweet, even surpassing the sweetness of lobster. Sharing one between two people was just the right amount.

**Marble Goby** Although marble goby is priced higher than grouper, its flesh is exceptionally delicate and boneless, making it very easy to eat. In Taiwan, a marble goby costs around $20, but here it's only half that price.

**Langoustines** Bali's langoustines come from the sea, boasting a springy texture that rivals that of larger wild lobsters. At around $6 USD each, it's actually more economical to enjoy eight langoustines here - you'll get twice the meat compared to a single large lobster.

**Thai Shrimp** While the Thai shrimp in Bali may be smaller than those found in Taiwan, their flesh is juicier and has a texture closer to that of grass shrimp.

**Flower Crab** Here we savored flower crabs. Though not large in size, their bellies were firm and the meat was sweet and delicious. The owner also offers cooked food delivery service, with a bowl of lobster porridge costing only about $5 USD.

### Restaurant Summary

Here, you can not only enjoy fresh seafood on-site but also opt for live seafood or cooked dishes delivered to your door. However, if you don't mind the effort, we recommend dining directly at the restaurant for the best experience.
