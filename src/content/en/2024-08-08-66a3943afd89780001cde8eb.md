This trip to Bali not only allowed me to fully immerse myself in the island's breathtaking scenery, but also indulged my taste buds at Mr. Lobster Restaurant, where I savored an array of exquisite seafood. From lobster to marble goby, every bite was a delightful surprise. Here's a glimpse into my culinary journey during this feast:

## Customer A: Mr. Lobster Restaurant

This is a culinary experience that comes highly recommended!

Before our trip, I stumbled upon this Taiwanese-owned restaurant in a Bali Facebook group, which convinced us to give it a try. Upon arrival, we ordered lobster, crab, and Thai prawns. Each bite was a delightful surprise—the lobster meat was tender and sweet, the crab was incredibly flavorful, and the subtle aroma of the Thai prawn roe was simply irresistible. For two lobsters, two crabs, and 600 grams of Thai prawns, we spent only around $32 to $65 USD—an absolute steal!

What's particularly worth mentioning is that the owner generously delivered their signature lobster porridge right to my accommodation. Before leaving Bali, I had the chance to savor this lobster porridge at the airport for just $5, and it was incredibly delicious! This place is definitely worth a try!

## Customer B: Highly Recommended Seafood Delights

https://youtube.com/shorts/0pk0syQh5sw

**Mantis Shrimp** In Bali, I had the chance to savor a mantis shrimp that was nearly 40 cm long. Its flesh was delicate and sweet, even surpassing the sweetness of lobster. Sharing one between two people was just the right amount.

**Marble Goby** Although marble goby is more expensive than grouper, its flesh is incredibly tender, with no small bones, making it very easy to eat. In Taiwan, a marble goby costs around $20, but here it's only half that price.

**Baby Lobster** The baby lobsters in Bali are sourced from the sea, boasting a firm and springy texture that rivals that of larger wild lobsters. At around USD$6 each, it's actually more economical to indulge in eight baby lobsters here – you'll get twice the meat compared to a single large lobster.

**Thai Shrimp** The Thai shrimp in Bali may be smaller than those in Taiwan, but their flesh is juicier and has a texture closer to that of grass shrimp.

**Flower Crab** Here, I tried the flower crab. Although not large in size, its belly was firm, sweet, and delicious. The owner also offers cooked food delivery service, with a bowl of lobster porridge costing just around USD$5.

### Restaurant Summary

Here, seafood can not only be enjoyed on the spot, but you also have the option of ordering live seafood or cooked dishes for delivery. If you don't mind the hassle, it's recommended to dine directly at the restaurant.

## Customer C: Warm Welcome from the Owner

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_70793e8257b18b6ad0ebe8b35fa45423.jpg)

Many thanks to the lobster restaurant owner for the warm hospitality yesterday. I truly felt the big-heartedness and warmth of Taiwanese people looking out for each other.

The average cost per person for this meal was less than USD$32, which was truly great value for money. The table was laden with six dishes including Thai shrimp, lobster, squid, stonefish, and more, accompanied by a pot of delicious soup. The lobster was sweet and juicy, the stonefish tender and smooth - each bite was a delight that lingered on the palate. Every dish was a delightful surprise. I'm deeply grateful for this culinary experience; it was truly an unforgettable feast.
