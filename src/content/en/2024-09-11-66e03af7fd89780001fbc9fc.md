## Background and Motivation Behind the Ban

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3480635877a3cd735d124f721b373446.png)

Southern Bali, including Denpasar, Badung, Gianyar, and Tabanan (collectively known as Sarbagita), has become a popular area for tourism development in recent years. However, with the rapid growth of tourism, local farmland and natural landscapes are at risk of being overdeveloped. To address these challenges, Bali's Acting Governor, Sang Made Mahendra Jaya, has proposed a ban aimed at suspending the construction of all new hotels, villas, nightclubs, and beach clubs in the southern region.

The initial implementation period for the ban is one to two years, but it may be extended up to ten years depending on the situation. This measure aims to prevent irresponsible development from damaging the local natural environment and cultural heritage, while also laying the foundation for more sustainable tourism development in the future.

## Specific Content and Scope of the Policy

This regulation not only targets the construction of hotels and resorts but also includes measures to protect local farmland. In recent years, with the rise of tourism, many rice fields originally used for agriculture have been converted to commercial purposes. This has not only led to a reduction in local agricultural production but also disrupted Bali's unique rural landscape. Therefore, this regulation aims to prevent the over-commercialization of rice fields and regulate local land use conversion.

Specifically, current developers who wish to convert farmland to commercial use must submit an application through the Online Single Submission (OSS) system, with the final decision made by the central government. This limits the authority of the local Balinese government in land development, further increasing the risk of environmental damage.

## Support and Challenges Behind the Regulation

## **法規背後的支持與挑戰**

The policy has received strong support from the Minister of Maritime Affairs and Investment, Luhut Binsar Pandjaitan, who believes that this initiative will not only help protect Bali's cultural and natural heritage but also promote sustainable tourism development. Minister Luhut stated, 'We must preserve Bali's uniqueness to ensure that future tourism brings greater long-term benefits to local communities.'

However, the implementation of this policy faces numerous challenges. Local tourism operators and developers worry that this ban could negatively impact economic growth in the southern regions, especially as global tourism has yet to fully recover from the pandemic.

## **Potential Impacts and Outlook**

Once implemented, the ban will have profound effects on tourism in southern Bali. Firstly, new hotel and resort projects will have to halt, reducing the supply of tourism facilities in the area and potentially driving up prices for existing accommodations.

Secondly, for developers looking to invest in southern Bali, this policy will undoubtedly increase their investment risks. If the ban is extended to ten years, many planned projects will be forced to cancel or postpone, creating long-term economic impacts.

However, in the long run, the implementation of this ban may encourage Bali's tourism industry to shift toward a more sustainable development model. By limiting overdevelopment, Bali can protect its unique natural and cultural resources, laying a more solid foundation for future tourism.

## **Addressing the Challenges of Overdevelopment**

In response to the challenges posed by tourism overdevelopment, the Bali government has proposed a series of measures. In addition to restricting new construction projects, the government plans to strengthen the management of existing tourism facilities to ensure they meet environmental standards and minimize negative impacts on local communities.

Furthermore, the government will promote cultural and eco-tourism initiatives, encouraging visitors to explore Bali's natural beauty and traditional culture, thereby reducing pressure on popular tourist areas. This approach also helps boost the economic development of local communities, enabling them to benefit more from tourism.

## **Conclusion: The Future of Sustainable Tourism Development**

Overall, Bali's upcoming new tourism development regulations mark a significant turning point in addressing the issue of overdevelopment in the tourism sector. By restricting new construction projects and protecting farmland and natural landscapes, the Balinese government aims to create a more sustainable environment for future tourism development.

While this policy may pose certain economic challenges in the short term, in the long run, it will help preserve Bali's unique culture and natural resources, ensuring the region continues to attract visitors from around the world.

The future development of Bali will depend on finding a balance between tourism growth and environmental protection, a common challenge faced by many global tourist destinations.
