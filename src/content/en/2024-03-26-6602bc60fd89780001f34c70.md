Bali has long been a popular destination for Taiwanese travelers, and recently more visitors have discovered the convenience of using credit cards locally. However, some tourists have found themselves paying 5% more than expected when settling their bills! Why does this happen? It's likely because they've fallen into the 'DCC (Dynamic Currency Conversion)' trap!

When using your credit card in Bali, if asked which currency to pay in, always choose to pay in Indonesian Rupiah (pay with Rupiah), never select Taiwanese Dollars. While 'Dynamic Currency Conversion' offers cardholders the choice to pay in either local currency or their home currency, many mistakenly believe paying in Taiwanese Dollars is more advantageous. In reality, they often discover upon returning to Taiwan that the exchange rate was quite unfavorable, especially for larger purchases or department store transactions.

Opting to pay in your home currency may settle the transaction at the day's exchange rate, but the conversion already includes additional service fees (beyond the standard 1.5% overseas transaction fee). Therefore, Bali visitors should always choose to pay in local currency when using credit cards.

Many experienced travelers have shared their experiences, including online hotel bookings, unanimously recommending that all foreign transactions should be settled in local currency to avoid unnecessary losses.
