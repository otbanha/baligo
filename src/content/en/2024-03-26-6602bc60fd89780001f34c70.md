Bali has always been a popular destination for Taiwanese travelers, and recently more visitors have discovered that using credit cards is a convenient option there. However, some tourists have found themselves paying 5% more than expected when settling their bills! Why does this happen? It's likely because they've fallen into the 'DCC (Dynamic Currency Conversion)' trap!

When using credit cards in Bali, if asked which currency to pay in, always choose to pay in Indonesian Rupiah (pay with Rupiah), never select Taiwanese dollars. While 'Dynamic Currency Conversion' offers cardholders the choice to pay in local currency or their card-issuing country's currency, many mistakenly believe paying in Taiwanese dollars is more advantageous. In reality, they often discover upon returning to Taiwan that the exchange rate was quite unfavorable, especially for larger purchases or department store transactions.

Choosing to pay in your card-issuing country's currency may settle at the day's exchange rate immediately, but the conversion already includes additional service fees (not the 1.5% overseas transaction fee). Therefore, Bali visitors should always choose to pay in local currency when using credit cards.

Many experienced travelers have shared their experiences, including online hotel bookings, unanimously recommending always choosing local currency for all overseas transactions to avoid unnecessary losses.
