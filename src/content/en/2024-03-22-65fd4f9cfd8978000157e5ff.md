## GWK Cultural Park

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fe7847c15db88a7191a1f728c33d7bbe.jpg)

GWK Cultural Park is one of the few government-supported popular attractions in Bali, located near Jimbaran. It is renowned for its grand sculptures, spectacular architecture, and integration of Balinese culture. The most famous feature is the 121-meter-tall statue combining Shiva and Garuda, the fourth tallest in the world. The park also hosts various cultural activities and performances, where you can enjoy traditional Balinese dance and music. Inside the park, there's a cultural center offering in-depth insights into Balinese culture.

## Pura Besakih: The Mother Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_145940526f93b2b01a3a93bf4e89daf3.jpg)

Pura Besakih is one of Bali's most significant landmarks and the island's largest and holiest Hindu temple complex, located on the slopes of Mount Agung. Known as the 'Mother Temple,' it serves as the spiritual heart of Bali, featuring multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

The grand temple complex is nestled between lush valleys and verdant rice fields, offering breathtaking views. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere, explore the intricate temple architecture, and experience the sacred ambiance. Pura Besakih is also a central location for many of Bali's religious and cultural events, including festivals and ceremonies.

## Tirta Empul Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2671370a86c84863c3c5f1ca502e8e1e.jpg)

Beyond Bali's most famous Tirta Empul Temple, there are two other hidden holy water temples nestled in quiet corners, not too far apart, awaiting explorers. These sacred sites not only allow you to experience Bali's unique religious culture but also offer a sincere and tranquil bathing experience in a more secluded environment.

Here are two more holy water temples we recommend for you:

**Dalem Pingit Holy Spring** is a relatively hidden temple. This temple not only boasts unique architectural styles but also its holy spring is deeply revered by local devotees. In this tranquil environment, you can feel the sincerity and peace of Bali's primal religion. Dalem Pingit is famous for its beautiful waterfall holy spring.

**Pura Gunung Kawi Holy Spring** is a temple renowned for its rock carvings. In addition to ancient statues, there is also a hidden holy spring where devotees and visitors can bathe for blessings. This serene temple area is filled with mystery, making you feel as if you've traveled through time to experience Bali's ancient sanctity.

These two different holy water temples each have their unique charm and cultural depth, providing relatively secluded bathing environments where you can find peace and purification within Bali's sacred atmosphere.

## Tanah Lot Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4627595c4f7e1a1a61c20ebb613256f0.jpg)

Bali's Tanah Lot is a famous landmark, known for its magnificent seaside temple. Built on a massive rock, this temple is one of Indonesia's most renowned Hindu temples. Every sunset, visitors gather here to admire the spectacular view of the setting sun, making it one of Bali's top ten famous landmarks.

However, due to its popularity, if you choose to visit in the evening, be mindful of the traffic conditions when traveling to and from the site.

## Pura Ulun Danau Water Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7b504fc83d34c26e674b57a1c762bf58.jpg)

Pura Ulun Danau, completed in 1633, is located on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali, also known as the 'Bratan Water Temple'. Since Lake Bratan is a primary source of irrigation for central Bali, Pura Ulun Danau was once a site for ceremonies honoring Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers. Additionally, the 11-tiered Pura Ulun Danau is also a temple dedicated to Shiva and Parvati, with a statue of Buddha enshrined within, making it a temple that blends Hinduism and Buddhism.

Interestingly, this Balinese water temple is also featured on Indonesian currency, specifically on the second-largest denomination in circulation. 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah is approximately USD$3.

## Jatiluwih Rice Terrace

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6b26a35c2746bdb8c6ba7f898bc97822.jpg)

The terraced landscapes surrounding Ubud are one of Bali's highlights, presenting a picturesque and magnificent scene that has earned UNESCO World Heritage status. Ubud has two main rice terrace areas:

1. Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular terrace areas in Ubud. The unique layout of this terrace area, with its winding paths weaving through lush greenery, vividly showcases nature's vitality. Here, rolling waves of rice fields create a moving agricultural poem, blending natural beauty with farming culture.
2. Another less commercialized terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, though it's further from Ubud—about a 90-minute drive from Ubud Palace. However, it offers a more authentic experience of terrace beauty!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn when the sun just rises. At this moment, the golden sunlight spills over the terraces, outlining layers of shadows and presenting an intoxicating play of light and shadow. This is the favorite moment for photography enthusiasts and tourists, as the rice terraces seem immersed in a golden dream.
