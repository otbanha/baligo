Bali has always been a popular destination for Taiwanese travelers, and recently more visitors have discovered that using credit cards is a convenient option there. However, some tourists have found themselves paying 5% more than expected when settling their bills! Why does this happen? It might be because they've fallen into the 'DCC (Dynamic Currency Conversion)' trap!

When you use your credit card in Bali, if asked which currency to pay in, always choose to pay in Indonesian Rupiah (pay with Rupiah), and never select Taiwanese Dollars. Dynamic Currency Conversion offers cardholders the choice to pay in either the local currency or their card-issuing country's currency. Many mistakenly believe that paying in Taiwanese Dollars is more economical, but in reality, they often find the exchange rate unfavorable when they return to Taiwan, especially with larger purchases or department store transactions.

Opting to pay in your card-issuing country's currency may settle at the day's exchange rate immediately, but the conversion already includes an additional service fee (not the 1.5% overseas transaction fee). Therefore, visitors to Bali must always choose to pay in the local currency when using credit cards.

Many experienced travelers have shared their stories, including booking accommodations online, unanimously recommending that all foreign transactions should be settled in the local currency to avoid unnecessary losses.
