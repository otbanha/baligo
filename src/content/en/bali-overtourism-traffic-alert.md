Bali continues to face concerns over overtourism, particularly in the southern regions, where overcrowding due to excessive tourists has become a pressing issue. Since 2023, Bali has been listed by the World Travel & Tourism Council as a hotspot for overtourism, alongside Athens, Paris, and Phuket.

![峇里島南部已經過度旅遊（overtourism）了！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fe05333dc536a7417e3945911d0ae12b.png)

Southern Bali is already suffering from overtourism!

In early 2024, Bali once again made headlines as foreign and local travelers were forced to abandon their vehicles and walk to Ngurah Rai International Airport due to severe traffic congestion on the access roads (the Christmas and New Year holiday period ranks as the second busiest peak season in Bali in terms of crowds and traffic. Here’s a short video showing the situation on the highway to the airport on December 29, 2023). Indonesia’s Minister of Tourism, Sandiaga Uno, acknowledged the overcrowding in southern Bali and plans to divert tourists to the northern, western, and eastern regions to alleviate the pressure, with related measures to be announced in September.

![峇里島南部包含西岸的長谷、水明漾、庫塔、金巴蘭、烏魯瓦圖；和東岸的努沙杜瓦、沙努爾。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_116100a5cd534e0ae2d1707ef11bbe1f.png)

Southern Bali includes the western areas of Canggu, Seminyak, Kuta, Jimbaran, and Uluwatu; and the eastern areas of Nusa Dua and Sanur.

Additionally, the Bali Airport Rail project is set to begin construction this September (refer to [【Bali Transportation Improvement Plan Part 1】Bali Light Rail Construction to Begin in September](/blog/bali-lrt-construction-update/)), and authorities have issued warnings of potential severe traffic congestion in the Kuta and Seminyak areas.
