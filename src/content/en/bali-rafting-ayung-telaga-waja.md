## Rafting FAQ

![Rafting FAQ](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a1b29221626f7514c7a6dc6fb7797722.jpg)

### **1. Which rivers in Bali are suitable for rafting?**

Bali has two main rivers famous for rafting:

- **Ayung River:** Located in the Ubud area, ideal for beginners, with views of carvings on the river cliffs and jungle scenery along the way.
- **Telaga Waja River:** Known for its higher level of excitement, perfect for adventure-seeking rafting enthusiasts.

### **2. Is rafting suitable for children?**

Yes, Bali rafting is suitable for children aged 6/7 and above, or seniors under 65, depending on the operator's specific age requirements.

### **3. Do you need to be able to swim for rafting?**

No swimming skills are required, though knowing how to swim is definitely a plus.

### **4. Can people with disabilities join rafting activities?**

For safety reasons, those who are completely immobile (e.g., with both legs disabled) are not allowed to participate. However, those with a single leg disability can join.

### **6. Will rafting be canceled if it rains?**

Generally, rafting activities are not affected by rain, and it can even be more fun in the rain. However, if heavy rain poses a risk to participants' safety, the activity will be canceled with a full refund, or you can reschedule for another day.

### **7. How long does a typical rafting trip take?**

The duration depends on the river and itinerary chosen. Typically, the rafting itself takes about 2 to 3 hours, and including transfers, preparation, and rest time, the whole experience may take 4 to 6 hours.

### **8. What shoes should you wear for rafting?**

It is recommended to wear well-secured sandals or specialized water sports shoes to ensure safety and comfort on slippery surfaces.

### **9. Can I bring a camera or camcorder for rafting?**

Yes, but please note the following:

- **Waterproof gear:** Use a waterproof camera or a waterproof pouch for your phone.
- **Hands-free:** Consider using an action camera with a helmet or chest mount.
- **Safety first:** Follow the guide's instructions and choose the right moment to shoot, without compromising your or others' safety.

### **11. What is the best time for rafting?**

Bali's dry season, typically from April to October, is the best time for rafting. The weather is more stable, and river water levels are moderate, suitable for all levels of rafting experience.

### **13. How much does a rafting trip cost?**

Costs vary depending on the rafting company, the river chosen, and the services included in the package. Generally, all-inclusive packages that cover transfers, equipment rental, and meals are more expensive. It's best to research and compare prices and services from different companies in advance.

### **14. How can I protect my personal belongings during rafting?**

Valuables like cash, phones, cameras, and watches should be stored or left in a safe place—it's not recommended to bring them on the raft. If you need to wear glasses, secure them with a rubber band to prevent them from falling off.

### **15. What preparations are needed before rafting?**

- **Health Check:** Make sure you're in good health and fit for rafting.\n- **Safety Briefing:** Listen to the instructor's safety instructions, including paddling techniques and emergency procedures.\n- **Warm-Up:** Do some light stretching to prevent injuries.

### **16. What should I do if the raft capsizes?**

Stay calm, quickly surface to avoid swallowing water, and keep your head up while wearing a life jacket. Wait for rescue without panicking, and follow the instructor's directions.

### **17. Are there shower and changing facilities after rafting?**

Most rafting companies provide shower and changing facilities at the finish point for guests. It's recommended to bring a change of clothes to freshen up after the activity.

### **18. Is a meal included in the rafting activity?**

Many rafting trips include a meal after the activity, depending on the operator and package chosen. It's best to confirm whether meals are included when booking, along with the type and quality of food.

### **19. What should I bring for a rafting activity?**

- **Sun protection:** Use a high-SPF, waterproof sunscreen to protect your skin from UV rays.
- **Change of clothes:** Bring a set of clean clothes to change into after the activity.
- **Towel:** For drying off after the activity.
- **Waterproof bag:** To store personal items and keep them dry.

### **20. How difficult is the rafting activity?**

Rafting in Bali varies in difficulty, suitable for participants of different skill levels. When booking, it's advisable to check the river's difficulty grade with the operator to choose the experience that best suits you.

### **21. Are there rest breaks during the rafting activity?**

On longer rafting trips, there are usually scheduled breaks where participants can rest on the riverbank, enjoy the scenery, and rehydrate.

### **22. What are the minimum and maximum age limits for the rafting activity?**

Generally, rafting is suitable for healthy individuals aged 6/7 and above, and under 65. Different operators may have varying age restrictions, so it's best to confirm when booking.

### **23. Is rafting suitable for beginners?**

Yes, many rafting trips in Bali are suitable for beginners. Professional guides provide detailed safety instructions and basic technique guidance before departure, ensuring participants can enjoy the rafting experience safely and with pleasure.

### **24. Is photography service available during rafting?**

Many rafting operators arrange photographers during the activity to capture participants' exciting moments. After the activity, you can choose to purchase these photos as souvenirs. It's recommended to ask about this service and its cost when booking.

### **25. Is rafting suitable for groups?**

Yes, rafting is ideal for groups such as families, friends, or corporate teams. It not only enhances cooperation and teamwork but also allows everyone to share the thrill and fun together. ### 26. Where is it safe to keep my passport and money while rafting?

When rafting in Bali, it's important to keep your passport and money safe to avoid dropping or getting them wet. Here are a few secure storage options:

✅ Use lockers provided by the operator 🔐

Most rafting operators provide lockers where you can secure your valuables. However, it's best to confirm in advance whether they are free or if you need to bring your own small lock.

✅ Leave them with your driver 🚗

If you have a private driver you trust, you can ask them to hold your valuables temporarily—a popular choice among travelers. Still, it's wise to carry a small amount of cash on you just in case.

✅ Bring a waterproof bag 💦

If you prefer to keep your passport, cash, or phone with you, use a high-quality waterproof bag, such as a waterproof waist pack, phone pouch, or dry bag. These can be worn around your neck, across your body, or strapped to your waist to protect items from splashes or accidental drops.
