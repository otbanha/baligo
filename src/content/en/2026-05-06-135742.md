Seminyak's **Jalan Kayu Aya** has a more famous name—**Eat Street**. Along this short two-kilometer stretch, over 50 restaurants, bars, and cafes are densely packed, offering everything from Aussie brunch, French pastries, and Latin American tacos to jazz cocktail bars, champagne lounges, and nightclubs, covering almost every global flavor.

This article is curated based on actual Google reviews, helping you **categorize Eat Street's establishments by type**, paired with brief descriptions of each spot. You can also use the Google Map to quickly find the perfect choice without endless scrolling.

# ☕ Brunch & Cafes

This is the most competitive section of Eat Street and Seminyak's most beloved dining style among foreign tourists.

***

## 🥇 Sisterfields

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1778075395701-sisterfields-reviews.jpg)

**Address:** Jl. Kayu Cendana No. 7

The most renowned Australian-style brunch cafe on Eat Street, bar none. It's packed as soon as it opens, with weekend waits of 20-45 minutes. The interior is bright and spacious, filled with natural light, exuding the vibe of a Melbourne coffee shop.

Must-try signature dishes: **Truffle Scrambled Eggs**, **Bacon Benedict**, **Acai Berry Bowl**, and various hearty burgers. Coffee is supplied by Sibling Espresso, ensuring consistent quality. Prices are slightly higher than nearby competitors, but the consistent flavors and friendly service make it worth it. Perfect for travelers looking to enjoy a proper brunch.

***

<!-- shuffle-start -->

## Revolver Espresso

**Address:** Jl. Kayu Aya No. Gang 51 (turn into the alley from the main street)

Opened in 2011, it's considered one of Bali's pioneers in specialty coffee. Hidden in a small alley, you'll find a dark entrance leading to a retro-industrial decor theme centered around cowboy guns—dark wood, exposed brick walls, and leather seats.

They roast their own coffee beans, excelling in Flat Whites and pour-overs, with all-day brunch also available. Popular menu items include **Birria Toastie** and chocolate hot cross buns. A must-visit for coffee enthusiasts, but arrive early to secure a seat.

***

## Monsieur Spoon

**Address:** Jl. Kayu Cendana No. 2

Founded in 2012 by two Parisian cousins, this French bakery and café has multiple branches in Bali. The Seminyak location sits next to Sisterfields, often overlooked by passersby, making it an underrated hidden gem.

Their signature item is the butter croissant, but the **truffle scrambled egg croissant sandwich** is also a must-try, with its rich truffle aroma earning rave reviews. It’s more affordable than Sisterfields or Revolver, and you rarely have to wait for a table. Highly recommended for fans of French pastries.

***

## Sisterfields’ Neighbor: BAKED. Seminyak

**Address:** Near Sisterfields area
A modern bakery café specializing in handmade bread and desserts, perfect for travelers looking for an elegant afternoon tea.

***

## Aviary Bistro

**Address:** Inside Seminyak Square

Located within Seminyak Square mall, this cozy, air-conditioned spot offers an all-day brunch menu at reasonable prices with consistent service quality. It’s a great option for a meal after shopping.

***

## The Junction House

**Address:** Near Jl. Kayu Cendana
A casual-style café restaurant offering brunch to dinner, with cozy decor, perfect for travelers who want to dine leisurely.

***

## Bangkok Kitchen

**Address:** Near Jl. Kayu Cendana
A Thai cuisine restaurant, popular among the Thai options on Eat Street, known for its curry and fried noodles, with affordable prices.

## 🥇 Ginger Moon Canteen

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1778075441071-ginger_moon.webp)

**Address:** Jl. Kayu Aya No. 7

Established in 2012, this is a legendary 'old-timer' on Eat Street, positioning itself as a Modern Asian Canteen that emphasizes shared dining culture. The decor features colonial-style elements, creating a lively yet elegant atmosphere.

Must-try dishes: **Black Pepper Crab, Snow Crab, Peking Duck with Pancakes**, **Ayam Betutu (Balinese Spiced Roast Chicken)**, **Crab Dumplings**. Chef Dean insists on sourcing organic ingredients from local farms. While some recent reviews suggest a slight decline in quality, it remains one of Eat Street's most worthwhile restaurants to queue for.

## Chandi

**Address:** Jalan Kayu Aya No. 72

One of the oldest Pan-Asian restaurants on Eat Street, Chandi uses organic ingredients and local Balinese produce. Signature dishes include **Miso Caramelised Butterfish**, **Hot Stone Satay Platter**, and Prawn Satay with Tamarind Sauce. Afternoons feature Happy Hour, making it a perfect spot to leisurely enjoy afternoon tea with cocktails.

***

## Cafe Bali

**Address:** Jl. Kayu Aya (Laksmana)

A restaurant brimming with old Bali charm, decorated with antique tableware, vintage fabrics, and traditional Indonesian architectural elements to create a 'Warung retro vibe.' The menu offers everything from Asian to international cuisine, with daily specials providing varied options. With its affordable prices, it's a great choice for those looking to dine comfortably without breaking the bank.

# 🍕 Western & International Cuisine

***

<!-- shuffle-start -->

## Italian Job Bali

Specializing in Italian cuisine, their pizza is the star of the menu, and the pasta dishes also receive high praise. Among the many options on Eat Street, it's a reliable and affordable choice for Italian fare.

***

## Asparagus Seminyak

Asparagus is the restaurant attached to Amadea Resort, serving breakfast, lunch, and dinner. The all-day menu emphasizes organic and locally sourced ingredients, with plenty of vegetarian and vegan options. Many reviews highlight their **buffet breakfast** as one of the best in Seminyak—offering a wide variety of Indonesian and Western dishes, made-to-order eggs, a pancake station, and fresh juices. The portions are generous, and the quality is consistently excellent, with some reviewers calling it the "breakfast champion of Seminyak." In the evenings, live music adds to the ambiance, and the service staff receives glowing praise (often mentioning servers like Febri, Lanang, and Rama). Overall, it offers excellent value for money on Eat Street.

***

# 🍹 Bars & Nightlife

***

## 🥇 La Favela

**Address:** Jl. Laksamana Oberoi No. 177X

The most legendary nightlife landmark on Eat Street. Originally converted from a small shop in 2012, it blends elements of Brazilian favelas, Indonesian jungles, and London underground bars. Artificial waterfalls, ponds, arched bridges, and vintage bicycle collections intertwine, with every corner telling a story.

During dinner hours (7:00 PM–11:00 PM), the focus is on Latin American cuisine, with tacos, tostadas, and Wagyu steak receiving high praise. After 11:00 PM, it transforms into a three-story nightclub, with each floor featuring different music styles—from pop and hip-hop to reggaeton. The crowd peaks after midnight. Women usually enter for free, while men arriving after 9:00 PM pay IDR 200,000–250,000 (includes one drink).

Overall: **Perfect atmosphere, great cocktails, slightly inconsistent service**, but missing it means you haven’t truly experienced Seminyak.

***

## 🥇 Red Carpet Champagne Bar

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1778075518821-red-carpet-100.jpg)

**Address:** Jl. Kayu Aya No. 42

Opened in 2009, this is one of the oldest nightlife spots on Eat Street, boasting Indonesia’s largest champagne collection (over 100 varieties). The staff, dressed in retro uniforms, resemble "champagne flight attendants." The venue plays nostalgic 70s–90s music videos all night, occasionally offering complimentary canapés. The cocktails are top-notch, with the **Espresso Martini** receiving endless praise. The atmosphere is extravagant and fun, never pretentious, making it a favorite among travelers aged 30 and above.

# 📍 Practical Tips

- **Eat Street = Jalan Kayu Aya**, the most vibrant stretch runs from Seminyak Square to Coco Super Market.
- Eat Street gets extremely crowded on weekend evenings, **recommend dining before 19:00**, head out for clubbing after 22:00.
- La Favela and Red Carpet Champagne Bar are right across the street from each other, so you can visit both.
- Many restaurants accept reservations (especially Ginger Moon and Chandi), recommend booking in advance for weekends.
- **Parking** has always been a pain point on Eat Street, recommend using Gojek/Grab to get there.
