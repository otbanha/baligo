### ✈️ Bali Airport Congestion Continues During Peak Hours; Arrive 3 Hours Early for International Flights

With the summer travel season in full swing, roads around Ngurah Rai International Airport remain congested during morning and evening peak hours. In addition to increased passenger traffic inside the terminal, vehicle flow to and from the airport has also grown significantly. For international flights, it's recommended to arrive at least 3 hours early and allow extra travel time to and from the airport to avoid missing your flight.

### 🏝️ July Dry Season Marks Peak Travel Period; Popular Spots See Surge in Crowds

This is currently the most popular travel season in Bali, with stable weather and minimal rainfall—perfect for island hopping, diving, snorkeling, and hiking. However, popular spots like Ubud, Seminyak, Canggu, and Nusa Penida are seeing a noticeable increase in crowds and accommodation demand. It's advisable to book hotels and activities well in advance.

### ⚠️ Book Private Drivers, Spas, and Popular Restaurants Early During Peak Season

As summer vacation begins, not only are hotel occupancy rates rising, but bookings for private drivers, popular spas, beach clubs, and trendy restaurants are filling up fast. Sunset dining spots and services near major attractions, in particular, should be reserved at least a few days ahead to avoid last-minute disappointment.

### 📊 Bali Continues to Promote High-Quality Tourism, Encourages Visitors to Follow Local Guidelines

The Bali provincial government is continuing its 'Quality Tourism' initiative, aiming to balance tourism development with cultural preservation and environmental sustainability. Officials have once again reminded foreign visitors to follow traffic rules, respect religious customs, dress appropriately, and adhere to all travel regulations to help maintain Bali's tourism quality.

### 🎉 Lovina Festival Kicks Off on July 24

North Bali's annual highlight, the Lovina Festival, will take place from July 24 to 26, featuring traditional dance, live music performances, a food market, and local cultural showcases. If you're planning a trip to Lovina for dolphin watching or exploring North Bali soon, consider adding this event to your itinerary for a taste of local culture that's distinct from South Bali.
