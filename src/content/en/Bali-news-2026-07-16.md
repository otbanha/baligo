### ✈️ Bali Airport Congestion Continues; Arrive 3 Hours Early for International Flights
As the summer travel peak season kicks in, roads around Ngurah Rai International Airport remain congested during morning and evening rush hours. In addition to the surge in passengers inside the terminal, traffic flow to and from the airport has also increased significantly. For international flights, it's recommended to arrive at least 3 hours early and allow extra travel time to and from the airport to avoid missing your flight.

### 🏝️ July Dry Season Marks Peak Travel Period; Popular Spots See Crowds Surge
This is currently the most popular travel season in Bali, with stable weather and little rainfall—perfect for island hopping, diving, snorkeling, and hiking. However, hotspots like Ubud, Seminyak, Canggu, and Nusa Penida are seeing a notable increase in crowds and accommodation demand. It's advisable to book hotels and activities well in advance.

### ⚠️ Book Private Drivers, Spas, and Popular Restaurants Early During Peak Season
Since the start of summer break, not only have hotel occupancy rates risen, but bookings for private drivers, popular spas, beach clubs, and trendy restaurants are filling up fast. For sunset dining spots and services near major attractions, it's best to reserve at least a few days ahead to avoid last-minute disappointment.

### 🎉 Lovina Festival Kicks Off on July 24

The annual Lovina Festival in North Bali will be held from July 24 to 26, featuring traditional dances, musical performances, a food market, and local cultural showcases. If you're planning a trip to Lovina to see dolphins or explore North Bali soon, consider adding this event to your itinerary for a taste of local culture distinct from South Bali.
