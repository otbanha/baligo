### ✈️ Bali Airport Congestion Continues; Arrive 3 Hours Early for International Flights

With the summer peak season in full swing, roads around Ngurah Rai International Airport remain congested during morning and evening rush hours. In addition to increased passenger traffic inside the terminal, vehicle flow to and from the airport has also grown significantly. For international flights, it's recommended to arrive at least 3 hours early and allow extra travel time to and from the airport to avoid missing your flight.

### 🏝️ July Dry Season Hits Peak Travel Period; Popular Spots See Surge in Crowds

This is currently the most popular travel season in Bali, with stable weather and minimal rainfall—perfect for island hopping, diving, snorkeling, and hiking. However, hotspots like Ubud, Seminyak, Canggu, and Nusa Penida are seeing a noticeable increase in crowds and accommodation demand. It's advisable to book hotels and activities well in advance.

### ⚠️ Book Private Drivers, Spas, and Popular Restaurants Early During Peak Season

As summer vacation begins, not only are hotel occupancy rates rising, but bookings for private drivers, popular spas, beach clubs, and sought-after restaurants are filling up fast. Sunset dining spots and services near major attractions, in particular, should be reserved several days ahead to avoid last-minute disappointment.

### 📊 Bali Continues to Promote High-Quality Tourism, Encourages Visitors to Follow Local Guidelines

The Bali provincial government continues to promote its 'Quality Tourism' policy, aiming to balance tourism development, cultural preservation, and environmental sustainability. Officials have once again reminded foreign tourists to follow traffic rules, respect religious customs, dress appropriately, and adhere to various travel regulations to help maintain Bali's tourism quality.

### 🎉 Lovina Festival Kicks Off on July 24

The annual Lovina Festival in North Bali will be held from July 24 to 26, featuring traditional dances, musical performances, a food market, and local cultural showcases. If you're planning a trip to Lovina for dolphin watching or exploring North Bali, consider adding this event to your itinerary for a taste of local culture distinct from South Bali.
