For readers planning to visit Bali at the end of this year or early next year, have you seen our latest announcement on the [Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/1DCH98qzUf/) from Indonesia's BMKG (Meteorological, Climatological, and Geophysical Agency)? It's a warning about heavy rainfall from late December to early January, a period that deserves special attention during the annual rainy season.
As a long-term resident of Indonesia and frequent traveler to Bali, I've compiled a guide to help you plan your trip for January-February 2027.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_900409df53579a6382a92eb5f137d800.jpg)

## 🌧 Rainfall to Reach 300–500mm: Late December to Early January is the Peak of the Rainy Season

According to BMKG's forecast, Indonesia will enter a period of intensified rainfall from late December, with the most significant precipitation expected between **December 28 and January 10**, accompanied by frequent showers and thunderstorms.
Generally, the weather during this period is as follows:
- Occasional sunny mornings
- Increased rainfall from noon to afternoon
- Possible rain again in the evening to night, depending on cloud conditions
This isn't the 'worst storm of the year,' but it is indeed the 10–14 days during the rainy season that require heightened awareness.

## ⚠️ **What Travelers Should Be Aware Of: Not Dangerous, But Be Prepared**

Bali is still enjoyable during the rainy season, but this period does bring some noticeable impacts, including:
- Flight delays: Afternoon thunderstorms often cause temporary flight suspensions or delays.
- Unstable sea conditions: Large waves and poor visibility affect snorkeling, speedboat rides, and island-hopping activities.
- Waterlogging and localized flooding in low-lying areas: Especially in certain parts of Denpasar or coastal roads.
- Variable road conditions in mountainous areas: Occasional landslides or muddy paths on waterfall routes and mountain trails.
For travelers, these aren't dangerous enough to cancel your trip, but they do require adjusting your 'travel pace' and 'expectations.'

## 🌤 Don't Misunderstand! There Are Still Sunny Days During the Rainy Season, and You Can Have a Great Time

I know many people get nervous when they see 'heavy rainfall' and '300–500mm,' thinking the entire trip will be soaked.
But Bali's rainy season isn't continuous downpours; it's 'lots of brief showers + afternoon thunderstorms.'
The actual experience is usually:
- Sunny mornings with clear blue skies in some areas
- Weather changes around noon, with intermittent rain
- Stable evenings perfect for dining, shopping, and photography
So what travelers really need to do is '**make the most of the good weather in the morning**.'

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f65b6856f52789decc1d59a008ae02ed.jpg)

## 🎒 **Travel Recommendations for Bali in January-February 2027**

Here are some recommendations based on years of experience and BMKG's latest weather alerts:

### - Avoid island hopping trips from New Year's to January 10th

This includes trips to Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Penida, and Gili Trawangan, as boat cancellations are common and seasickness can be severe.

### - Schedule beach and outdoor activities for the morning

If you want to photograph rice terraces, waterfalls, Tanah Lot Temple, or Monkey Forest, mornings offer much higher success rates.

### - Prepare waterproof gear

Essential items include lightweight raincoats, waterproof bags, sandals, and quick-dry clothing - lifesavers during the rainy season.

### - Maintain flexibility in your itinerary

Don't pack every day full of activities, so you won't panic if sudden heavy rains occur.

### - Rainfall becomes more predictable from mid-January through February

Rain will still fall, but the rhythm feels more like a typical rainy season, not as intense as during New Year's.

## 🌈 Bali in the Rainy Season is Actually Quite Charming

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_30a080584a5be323dabd95d7c05fdcf7.jpg)

Honestly, I have a love-hate relationship with Bali during this time. After a rain shower, the entire island feels cleansed: the air is crystal clear, greenery is lush, waterfalls are majestic, and Ubud's rice fields resemble oil paintings. With fewer tourists than in peak season and cheaper accommodations, this period can be even more enjoyable for those who love slow travel, capturing natural scenery, indulging in good food, and spa treatments.

The rainy season is never an obstacle to travel, just a reminder to adjust your pace.
