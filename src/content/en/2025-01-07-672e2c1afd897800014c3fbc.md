# **Answers to Frequently Asked Questions About Bali Travel**

Whether you're planning your first trip to Bali or have been several times, many people have basic questions about this enchanting island. Here are some commonly asked questions to help you better understand Bali and plan your perfect itinerary!

***

## 1. Where is Bali? How do I get there?

Bali is an island belonging to Indonesia, located in Southeast Asia. The island is surrounded by sea, with Java to the west and Lombok to the east. Due to its unique characteristics and diverse landscapes, Bali has become a popular vacation destination in Southeast Asia, attracting travelers from all over the world every year.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e14497d695ce945e47d1b8738cd5faa6.gif)

There are direct flights from countries such as Taiwan, Hong Kong, China, Malaysia, Singapore, Vietnam, South Korea, Japan, Australia, and more.

PS. EVA Air and China Airlines from Taiwan offer direct flights.

## 2. What language is spoken in Bali?

In Bali, locals primarily use Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia), the official language of Indonesia. However, due to Bali's unique culture, locals also speak Balinese, a language widely used in daily life and considered the mother tongue for some Balinese residents. Balinese is mainly used within families, religious ceremonies, and traditional activities, playing a significant role in local religious and community life.

***

## 3. Do People in Bali Speak English?

Generally speaking, Bali's tourism industry is highly developed, and many locals working in tourism-related jobs can speak basic English. Whether it's restaurants, hotels, shops, or attractions, most staff can communicate in simple English, especially in southern areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Jimbaran. However, there may still be some language barriers when conversing with elderly locals or residents in remote villages. Some older generations may only speak Balinese.

For travelers, learning a few simple Indonesian phrases like "Hello" (Halo) and "Thank you" (Terima kasih) can make communication more fun and harmonious.

## 4. What Currency is Used in Bali?

The currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Travelers can easily exchange currency at airports, banks, hotels, or currency exchange offices in the city. It's recommended to carry some cash for use in small shops, street stalls, or traditional markets, as this allows for more flexibility with small payments.

## 5. What is Bali's Climate Like? When is the Best Time to Visit Bali?

Located near the equator, Bali has a **tropical monsoon climate**, characterized by warm and humid weather year-round. The climate is divided into two main seasons: **dry season** and **wet season**, making Bali suitable for travel throughout the year. However, the travel experience may vary slightly depending on the season.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e7fac42f701496457dff00feb523003.png)

**Dry Season (May to October)**

The dry season is Bali's peak tourist season. During this time, temperatures are moderate, humidity is lower, and there is plenty of sunshine with clear skies. Daytime temperatures usually range between **27°C to 32°C**, with slightly cooler evenings. This is the ideal time for outdoor activities such as beach sunbathing, snorkeling, diving, and hiking, as well as water sports and capturing beautiful sunsets.

**Wet Season (November to April)**

The rainy season is Bali's low season. Although there is more rainfall during this period, the rain is usually brief and intermittent, not lasting for several consecutive days. Daytime temperatures are similar to the dry season, but humidity is higher with frequent rainfall. Temperatures still hover around **26°C to 30°C**. Rainfall during this time mainly occurs from afternoon to evening, while mornings and nights remain suitable for various activities.

https://youtube.com/shorts/bqtCP1YoKCM

Bali's average temperature typically ranges between 26-32°C. During the rainy season, it can sometimes feel cooler. For packing, it's recommended to focus on short-sleeve clothing. There's no need for heavy jackets—just bring a light jacket or windbreaker.

## 7. Is Bali better suited for independent travel or group tours?

**Advantages of independent travel:**

- Greater flexibility in itinerary, allowing for in-depth exploration based on interests
- Generally more cost-effective than group tours
- Ability to relax and travel at your own pace
- More diverse dining options

However, independent travel requires thorough preparation:

- Transportation arrangements are complex, with limited public transport options. Consider private car hire or renting a vehicle.
- Attractions are spread out and route planning can be challenging. Research distances between sites and optimal visiting order.
- Consider whether accommodation areas match your needs (e.g., Kuta's lively atmosphere vs. Ubud's artistic vibe).
- Be aware of weather conditions, food hygiene, and local etiquette.

**Advantages of joining a tour group:**

- Eliminates hassle of arranging transportation and accommodation
- Includes guide commentary and assistance
- Well-planned itinerary suitable for time-limited travelers
- No need to worry about language barriers

**Consider these factors when making your choice:**

1. Language skills - English is widely used in tourist areas, but complete lack of foreign language skills suggests joining a tour
2. Trip duration - Short stays are better suited for tour groups to maximize sightseeing
3. Budget planning - Independent travel allows flexible spending adjustments, while tours are mostly all-inclusive
4. Travel style - Prefer in-depth exploration or quick sightseeing

The cost of living in Bali is actually quite flexible, depending on your travel style and spending habits:

1. **Budget Options:**
2. - If you choose to eat street food (such as Nasi Goreng, Mie Goreng, or Bali's buffet-style Nasi Campur), the cost of three meals a day might only be around USD$6～10.

4. - Bali's luxury villas, top-tier restaurants, or high-end beach clubs are relatively expensive, with costs comparable to those in major international cities.

1. **Situations Suitable for Credit Cards:**
2. - High-end restaurants, shopping malls, five-star hotels, beach clubs, and similar venues usually accept international credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, Amex).

- Many car rental companies and travel agencies accept credit card payments, but may charge additional fees (approximately 1.5-3%).

3. **Situations where cards are not suitable:**
4. - Street vendors, small local restaurants (Warung), traditional markets, and remote areas may only accept cash.

- Some restaurants have a minimum spending requirement and may not accept cards for amounts below a certain threshold.

5. **Recommendations:**
6. - **Prepare Indonesian Rupiah in cash:** Cash is most convenient for small transactions or expenses, especially in remote areas or street shopping.

## 38. Are Bali's traditional dance performances worth watching?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_82b8c46b8f153469d536f48be8633bf8.jpg)

When visiting Bali, experiencing the local traditional dances is an absolute must! Balinese dance is not just an art form but also carries profound religious and cultural significance. Through exquisite costumes, graceful movements, and vivid expressions, these dances perfectly encapsulate Bali's unique cultural essence.

Here are a few traditional Balinese dances worth watching:

1. **Kecak Dance**

This is one of Bali's most iconic traditional dances, also known as the 'Monkey Dance.' Inspired by the Hindu epic Ramayana, dancers sit in a circle and chant 'cak cak cak' to mimic a monkey army, creating a highly dramatic atmosphere. The most famous Kecak Dance performances take place at sunset at Uluwatu Temple, with the golden sunset and stunning ocean views as the backdrop, offering a feast for both the eyes and ears.

[Top 6 Must-See Kecak Fire Dance Locations in Bali](/blog/2024-12-12-675ad354fd89780001c09f24/)

2. **Barong Dance**

The Barong Dance tells the story of the battle between good and evil, featuring the benevolent mythical creature 'Barong' and the evil 'Rangda,' symbolizing balance and harmony. Dancers wear elaborate costumes and showcase intricate body language, making this performance particularly popular among family travelers.

3. **Legong Dance**

The Legong Dance is considered the most elegant dance in Bali, often performed by young girls who interpret moving mythological stories through intricate hand movements and eye contact. Known for its fast-paced rhythm and precise movements, this dance is typically performed at the Ubud Palace or local art villages, making it a favorite among art enthusiasts.

The Barong Dance and Legong Dance can be enjoyed at the indoor stage of Balerung Stage. For details, please see [here](/blog/2024-09-19-66eb78bafd89780001edcec6/).

**Reasons to Watch**

1. **Cultural Experience**: The dance is closely tied to Bali's religious rituals, offering a deeper understanding of local culture.
2. **Visual Delight**: Performances are usually accompanied by traditional music, featuring exquisite costumes and delicate movements, making it a feast for the eyes.
3. **Family-Friendly**: Most dance performances are suitable for all ages, ensuring fun for families and couples alike.
