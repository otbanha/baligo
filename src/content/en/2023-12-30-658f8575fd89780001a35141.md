## What is Nyepi (Day of Silence)?

Nyepi (pronounced "Nyeh-pee") is the Balinese New Year, a day for introspection, cleansing the soul, and a unique religious and cultural festival. On this day, Balinese residents engage in absolute meditation and reflection, and the entire island falls into silence. This symbolizes the events of the past year, offering people an opportunity to cleanse their minds and reflect on themselves. Bali's Nyepi is the only festival in the world where an entire island is shut down and activities cease to honor and adhere to traditional values. Watch Xiao Jie's introduction:

Nyepi is a time for self-dialogue and seeking inner peace. It is a deeply meaningful part of Balinese culture, not only allowing people to reflect spiritually but also showcasing Bali's profound religious beliefs and cultural traditions.

Why are there such customs on Nyepi? The Balinese believe that keeping the island silent on this day will make demons and evil spirits think the place is uninhabited, thus avoiding calamity. Prohibiting going outside ensures people are not discovered or disturbed by evil spirits. Banning the lighting of fires prevents flames from attracting evil spirits.

## What can and cannot be done during Nyepi?

On Nyepi Day, Bali residents adhere to strict traditional rules. They meditate and pray at home, refraining from using any electrical appliances, phones, computers, or entertainment devices, and even remain silent. All residents must follow these four prohibitions, which also apply to tourists:

1. Amati Geni: Prohibition of using fire or light, including electricity.
2. Amati Karya: Prohibition of work, all commercial activities cease.
3. Amati Lelunganan: Prohibition of travel, all transportation halts.
4. Amati Lelanguan: Prohibition of entertainment and noise.

Tourists are also expected to respect this tradition by staying indoors and maintaining silence.

On Nyepi Day, you cannot go out, so choosing a medium to large resort will offer more activities.

Not only are all shops, restaurants, supermarkets, and convenience stores closed, but some villages and neighborhoods also patrol the streets to ensure everyone complies with the rules. Tourists who do not wish to comply may receive warnings, and in severe cases, could be deported.

Therefore, on this day, you cannot go out! Fortunately, the hotels and resorts where you stay usually provide essential services, allowing you to enjoy a peaceful time indoors, use the WiFi, or swim quietly. Facilities and activities are limited on this day; for example, water slides cannot be used, music cannot be played, and noisy activities are strictly prohibited. After dark, even inside the hotel, curtains must be drawn to prevent light from leaking outside, adhering to Nyepi Day regulations.

On the night of this day, if the weather is clear and cloudless, remember to step outside (within the resort, not outside!) to admire Bali's starry sky without any light pollution. It's a truly breathtaking experience!

## The Monster Parade on the Eve of Nyepi

The day before Nyepi is famous worldwide for a carnival known as the 'Monster Parade,' where you can witness Bali's unique traditional monsters. This colorful parade involves noisy street processions to drive away monsters and evil spirits, preparing for the new year. Many shops, supermarkets, and restaurants close early in the evening, so friends planning to shop or dine today should be prepared! The parade, which starts in the evening, passes through several villages and main roads. The climax of the parade takes place at a square in Denpasar called 'Lapangan Puputan,' where many elaborately crafted large monsters gather for people to admire. ## Transportation Reminder

On Nyepi Day, all transportation across Bali will come to a halt. Airports, ports, and public transport will be closed, and roads will be blocked. This is to ensure people adhere to Nyepi traditions, so plan your itinerary accordingly and stay in a suitable place during this time. We recommend choosing medium to large resorts where you can comfortably spend the day indoors.

Besides Nyepi Day itself, starting from noon the day before Nyepi, Bali will implement traffic controls in various areas to facilitate the smooth running of the evening Ogoh-Ogoh parade. These two days are not ideal for long-distance travel! If you arrive in Bali on the day before Nyepi, you might face the predicament of having to 'walk' to your hotel or struggling to reach it due to traffic restrictions.

## Special Circumstances for Nyepi in 2025

In 2025, Nyepi Day will be followed by 'Eid al-Fitr'. The Eid holiday will start at the end of March and last for a whole week. Eid al-Fitr is one of Indonesia's most important holidays, celebrating the end of Ramadan for Muslims, but it's also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, similar to our Chinese Lunar New Year.

Expect Bali to start filling up with local Indonesians from March 26th, peaking on March 28th when the holiday begins, and remaining crowded for about two weeks. This marks the first of Bali's five peak seasons, so it's advisable to avoid popular tourist spots during this time!

## ****FAQ: Common Questions About Bali's Nyepi****

### ****1. Can I leave my hotel on Nyepi day?****

No. Both locals and tourists are required to stay indoors, and all public facilities and transportation on the island will be suspended.

### ****2. Will Bali's airport close during Nyepi?****

Yes, Ngurah Rai International Airport will halt all flight operations, with no arrivals or departures permitted.

### ****3. Is there an admission fee for the Ogoh-Ogoh parade?****

No. The Ogoh-Ogoh parade is a free public event that you can enjoy without any charge.

### ****4. Will hotels provide food service on Nyepi day?****

Yes. Most hotels will offer basic meal services, though some facilities may have restricted access.

### ****5. Can I use the internet during Nyepi?****

Typically, mobile data cannot be used, but Wi-Fi within hotels remains accessible.
