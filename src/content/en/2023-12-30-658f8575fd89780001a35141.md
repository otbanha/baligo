## What is Nyepi (Day of Silence)?

Nyepi (pronounced "Nyeh-pee") is the Balinese New Year, a day for introspection, cleansing the soul, and a unique religious and cultural festival. On this day, Balinese residents engage in absolute meditation and reflection, and the entire island falls into silence. It symbolizes the events of the past year, offering people a chance to cleanse their minds and reflect on themselves. Bali's Nyepi is the only festival in the world where an entire island shuts down and ceases all activities to honor and uphold traditional values. Watch Xiao Jie's introduction:

Nyepi is a time for self-dialogue and seeking inner peace. It is a deeply meaningful part of Balinese culture, allowing people to reflect spiritually and showcasing Bali's profound religious beliefs and cultural traditions.

Why are there such customs on Nyepi? The Balinese believe that keeping the island silent on this day will make demons and evil spirits think the place is uninhabited, thus avoiding calamity. The prohibition on going out ensures that people are not discovered or harassed by evil spirits. The ban on lighting fires prevents the flames from attracting evil spirits.

## What can and can't you do on Nyepi?

On Nyepi Day, the Balinese people adhere to strict traditional customs. They stay at home in silent meditation and prayer, refraining from using any electrical appliances, phones, computers, or entertainment devices, and even avoiding speech. All island residents must follow these four prohibitions, which also apply to tourists:

1. **Amati Geni**: Prohibition of using fire or light, including electricity.
2. **Amati Karya**: Prohibition of work, with all commercial activities halted.
3. **Amati Lelunganan**: Prohibition of travel, with all transportation suspended.
4. **Amati Lelanguan**: Prohibition of entertainment and noise.

Tourists are also expected to respect this tradition by staying indoors and maintaining silence.

## The Ogoh-Ogoh Parade on the Eve of Nyepi

The day before Nyepi is famous worldwide for a carnival known as the "Ogoh-Ogoh Parade," where you can witness Bali’s unique traditional monsters. This colorful parade features people marching noisily through the streets to drive away evil spirits and prepare for the new year. Many shops, supermarkets, and restaurants close early in the evening, so those planning to shop or dine should be prepared! The parade, which begins in the evening, passes through several villages and main roads. The climax of the parade takes place at a square in Denpasar called "Lapangan Puputan," where many elaborately crafted large monsters gather for people to admire. ## Transportation Reminder

On Nyepi Day, all transportation in Bali will come to a halt. Airports, ports, and public transportation will be closed, and roads will be blocked. This is to ensure people observe Nyepi traditions, so plan your itinerary accordingly and stay in a suitable place during this time. We recommend choosing a mid-to-large-sized resort to comfortably spend this day indoors.

Apart from Nyepi Day itself, traffic control will begin in multiple areas across Bali from noon the day before Nyepi to facilitate the smooth running of the evening Ogoh-Ogoh parade. Therefore, these two days are not ideal for long-distance travel! If you arrive in Bali the day before Nyepi, you might face the predicament of having to walk to your hotel or struggling to reach it due to traffic restrictions.

## Special Circumstances for Nyepi Day 2025

In 2025, Nyepi Day will be followed by Eid al-Fitr. The Eid holiday will start at the end of March and last for a whole week. Eid al-Fitr is Indonesia's most important holiday, celebrating the end of Ramadan for Muslims, but it's also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, equivalent to our Chinese Lunar New Year.

Bali is expected to see an influx of local Indonesians starting from March 26th, peaking on March 28th when the holiday begins, and remaining crowded for about two weeks. This marks the first peak season among Bali's five major high seasons, so it's advisable to avoid popular tourist spots!

## **FAQ: Common Questions About Nyepi in Bali**

### **1. Can I leave the hotel on Nyepi day?**

No. Neither locals nor tourists are allowed to go outside, and all public facilities and transportation on the island will be suspended.

### **2. Will Bali Airport close during Nyepi?**

Yes, Ngurah Rai International Airport will suspend all flights, with no arrivals or departures.

### **3. Do I need a ticket to join the Ogoh-Ogoh parade?**

No. The Ogoh-Ogoh parade is a free public event, and you can watch it freely.

### **4. Will hotels provide meal services on Nyepi day?**

Yes. Most hotels will provide basic dining services, but some facilities may have restricted usage.

### **5. Can I use the internet during Nyepi?**

Generally, mobile networks cannot be used, but Wi-Fi within the hotel will still be available.
