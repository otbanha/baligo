## Your Guide to Gili Trawangan

The Gili Islands consist of three small islands: Gili Air, Gili Meno, and Gili Trawangan (collectively referred to as "Gili ATM"). While these islands are not far from Bali, they are actually closer to Lombok.

### Limited Chinese Travel Information on the Gili Islands

Currently, there’s relatively little Chinese travel information online about the Gili Islands, especially Gili Trawangan, so most visitors here are Europeans and Americans. However, these islands boast unique charm and abundant natural resources, making them well worth exploring.

### Plan a 3~4 Day Relaxation Getaway!

If you’re vacationing in Bali, consider setting aside 3~4 days to unwind on Gili Trawangan.

Gili Trawangan (commonly called T Island) is a dreamy and vibrant holiday destination. Its stunning scenery and laid-back atmosphere will help you forget your worries and fully enjoy your vacation. Just a 1.5-hour boat ride from Bali will take you to this beautiful island.

### Enchanted Carriage Kingdom, Romantic Sunsets, Party Island

T Island is renowned for its romantic sunsets and vibrant party atmosphere, making it a coveted vacation paradise. The island prohibits motor vehicles, allowing only bicycles and horse-drawn carriages, adding a unique tranquility and simplicity to the locale.

### Gili Trawangan Island Travel Guide

- ****Area Guide****: T Island is divided into the East Coast, South Coast, West Coast, Northwest Coast, Northeast Coast, and Central Area, each offering unique charms and a wealth of activities to explore.
- ****Getting There****: Numerous boat services are available from Bali to T Island, offering a variety of convenient and fast options.
- ****Local Transportation****: Cars are banned on the island; the primary modes of transport are bicycles and horse-drawn carriages, promoting eco-friendly and sustainable tourism.
- ****Recommended Spots****: T Island boasts many exquisite restaurants and cafes, perfect for a culinary adventure.
