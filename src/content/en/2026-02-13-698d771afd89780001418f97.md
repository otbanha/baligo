When people think of Bali's beach resorts, Kuta often comes to mind first, but **Sanur was actually Bali's original seaside resort area**. It's low-key, elegant, and unassuming, yet has always maintained its charm.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_56f887fcf84745b1d038cc5391594203.jpg)

And now, Sanur is quietly undergoing a transformation.

In recent years, it has not only preserved its peaceful and family-friendly atmosphere but has also significantly upgraded towards being 'modern, efficient, and clean.' Honestly, if you haven't been to Sanur in a while, you'll be pleasantly surprised when you return.

## Sanur's New Identity: A Medical and Wellness Tourism Zone 🏥🌿

Sanur has been designated as a **Special Economic Zone for Medical and Wellness Tourism**, featuring the world-class **Bali International Hospital**.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_885b670ebe416d1159e92918145534e4.jpg)

What does this mean?

👉 Future Sanur aims to become Southeast Asia's premier destination for health and healing vacations

👉 Perfect not just for relaxation but also for combining check-ups, medical treatments, and long-term wellness stays

From my observations, this positioning perfectly matches Sanur's character - quiet, mature, and refined, rather than being a bustling and noisy resort area.

***

## Transportation and Cityscape Upgrades 🚗🚌

Honestly, one of Sanur's biggest pain points in the past was transportation and sidewalks.

### Previous issues:

- Noticeable traffic congestion
- Severe illegal parking
- Damaged sidewalks
- Pushing a stroller felt like an off-road challenge

But recent improvements have made a real difference:

### ✅ Complete renovation of Jalan Danau Tamblingan sidewalk

https://youtu.be/J6mjw5k2lzY?si=qoVEwlncFrnqNvkm

Sanur's busiest main road is now smoother and cleaner, with stricter enforcement against illegal parking, making the entire street look much fresher.

Now, hopping between coffee shops and taking walks in this area is much more comfortable, without worrying about twisting an ankle.

### ✅ Electric Shuttle Buses Become Permanent Service

## Cleaner Beach Environment 🌊

While Sanur isn't as prone to massive plastic waste influxes as Kuta, heavy rains and strong winds can still bring debris ashore. Now, local tourism operators, cleaning teams, and traditional villages are collaborating to increase patrol and cleaning frequency.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9abcbc0ac1493db0c28dcd921a2cf2ff.jpg)

What's the result?

👉 Coastline is cleaner than before

👉 Safer for strolls and family activities

## Sanur Beachwalk Still Reigns Supreme 👣

https://youtu.be/laRb_l8BdLM

If you ask me what's most irreplaceable about Sanur?

It's definitely the **extra-long Beachwalk**.

Stretching from Mertasari Beach all the way to Pantai Indah Sanur, nearly the entire coastline features wide, well-paved walkways.

This means:

- Wheelchair-friendly
- Stroller-friendly
- Senior-friendly
- Morning jog-friendly

This is why Sanur has always been one of the favorite areas for families and mature travelers.

***

## Why is it especially suitable for last-minute trips now? 💰✈️

Several practical factors make Sanur more attractive right now:

- Weak Indonesian Rupiah exchange rate
- Lower prices during the off-season
- Wide selection of resorts and villas
- Short drive from the airport to Sanur (about 20 minutes with smooth traffic)
- Convenient access to Nusa Penida and Nusa Lembongan

Plus, with infrastructure upgrades completed, the overall travel experience is moving towards a more mature direction.

## My Observations and Takeaways

Despite undergoing modernization, Sanur has retained its signature **tranquil and laid-back vibe**. Unlike the hustle and bustle of Kuta or Canggu, Sanur is known for its family-friendly, slow-paced atmosphere, which is the core reason it has maintained its appeal over time. Sanur has never been a 'viral' destination; it's more like a place that **draws you back again and again**.

It doesn’t boast extravagant parties or wild nightlife, but it offers:

- Consistent quality
- Quiet beaches
- Gentle morning light at sunrise
- And increasingly better urban planning

{{block:sanur}}

If your last impression of Bali is still stuck on Kuta, it’s really worth giving Sanur a fresh chance.

Sometimes, what makes you want to book a flight immediately isn’t some new hotspot, but the feeling that **a familiar place has gotten even better**.

And that’s exactly what Sanur feels like now. 🌅
