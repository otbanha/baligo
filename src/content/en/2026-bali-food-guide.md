Bali is not only home to stunning beaches and rich culture but also a paradise for food lovers! This time, we’ll take you deep into Bali’s most representative 10 must-try dishes, from traditional main courses to street snacks, each bursting with Indonesia’s unique spice flavors.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b027619db0a88baf3fea8f8c63193a9d.jpg)

## 🍖 Classic Main Courses

### 1. Babi Guling (Roast Suckling Pig)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4bda84a271f14b852043e10008699dc3.jpg)

A signature dish you absolutely can’t miss in Bali! A whole suckling pig is stuffed with spices and slow-roasted, resulting in golden crispy skin and tender, juicy meat. It’s usually served with rice, crispy skin, sausages, and sweet potatoes, offering a perfect blend of spices in every bite.

### 2. Nasi Campur Bali (Balinese Mixed Rice)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_af1ea75fd01be79bb6fc4b742a352357.jpg)

This is Bali’s version of mixed rice, a plate that combines the essence of various sauces, grilled meats, and Indonesian mixed rice. It typically includes rice, fried chicken or grilled meat, vegetables, peanuts, shrimp crackers, and more, allowing you to savor multiple flavors in one dish!

### 3. Betutu (Spiced Roast Chicken/Duck)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3be3ad88c261f5abe0846db474c3d02c.png)

A traditional Balinese slow-roasted dish, where a whole chicken or duck is marinated with spices, wrapped in banana leaves, and slow-roasted for hours. The rich spices fully penetrate the meat, leaving an unforgettable taste.

### 4. Jimbaran Seafood

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_936c1dc67021ff7ed3d35d7002572a20.jpg)

Jimbaran Beach is famous for its seafood barbecues, where fresh seafood paired with a stunning sunset creates an unforgettable dining experience. Freshly caught fish, shrimp, lobster, and other seafood are grilled and drizzled with special sauces, making it a must-try romantic dining experience in Bali.

## 🍢 Street Food Picks

### 5. Sate Lilit

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b96032c8d4ab3b3ca235a5f349e93f34.jpg)

A unique Balinese-style skewer, Sate Lilit is made by wrapping seasoned minced meat around lemongrass or bamboo sticks before grilling. The aroma is irresistible, and you won’t be able to stop at just one!

### 6. Pisang Goreng

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_da510f477a3b99474e4e823a2e97cbf8.jpg)

Pisang Goreng, or fried bananas, is one of Bali’s most popular street snacks. Bananas are coated in batter and fried until golden and crispy, creating a simple yet addictive treat that’s crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

### 7. Klepon

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_069db0e5cc1da39c0aba61f76f04ddec.jpg)

This adorable green glutinous rice ball is a traditional Balinese dessert. Filled with palm sugar and coated with grated coconut, biting into it releases a burst of sweet syrup that’s satisfying without being overly cloying.

### 8. Jajanan Pasar (Traditional Market Snacks)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_27d9ed090f2e0d6d77985588b30b22e2.png)

Bali's traditional markets are filled with a variety of colorful pastries and snacks. Traditional sweets like rainbow cakes and coconut cakes are bursting with local flavors, making them the perfect way to experience the island's culture.

### 9. Lawar (Mixed Meat and Vegetable Salad)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b63c90ffa74ac14509bd2a00bb1cedb3.jpg)

Despite its name suggesting a cold dish, Lawar is actually a traditional mix of vegetables, coconut meat, spices, and meat. Its refreshing and appetizing flavor, combined with rich spices, makes it an ideal starter.

## ☕ Must-Try Drinks

### 10. Kopi Bali (Bali Coffee)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f3c741d3dfe2aeb90d97b02560dfe140.jpg)

Bali's coffee beans grow in volcanic soil, giving them a uniquely rich flavor. When in Bali, don't miss the chance to savor freshly roasted local coffee while enjoying the stunning rice terrace views—a perfect way to wrap up your culinary journey! **A notable example**: **Kintamani Coffee** is a famous label from this region.

## 💡 Food Tips

**Best Dining Times**: Local restaurants are usually busiest between 11:30 AM - 2:00 PM and 6:00 PM - 9:00 PM
**Price Range**: Street food costs around $1 - $3; main dishes at restaurants range from $3 - $10
**Spice Alert**: Indonesian cuisine is often spicy. If you're sensitive to spice, remember to say "Tidak pedas" (not spicy)
