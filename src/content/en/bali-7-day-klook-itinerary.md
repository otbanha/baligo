## 🛬 Day 1: Arrival in Bali - Relaxation Mode Activated!

After arriving at Denpasar Airport, we took our pre-booked airport transfer to our hotel in Sanur, located on Bali's east coast. Sanur is a tranquil coastal town in southeastern Bali, known for its relaxed atmosphere and slow pace of life, making it a favorite among family travelers and long-term visitors. With its long beachfront promenade and sunrise beach, Sanur is perfect for morning walks, cycling, and swimming. Unlike the bustling Kuta, it exudes a relaxing elegance.

We stayed at Griya Santrian, a beachfront resort where you can step onto the sand just a few steps from the pool - perfect for water lovers and beach enthusiasts. The resort maintains traditional Balinese architectural style with serene garden landscapes. It also offers spa services, yoga classes, and ocean-view dining, making it ideal for travelers wanting to enjoy the charm of 'slow living' in Sanur.

## 📸 Day 2: Klook's 'East Bali Instagram Hotspots Tour' 🌋🌴

Today's highlight is Klook's 【Bali East Instagrammable Spots Day Tour】, our first Klook experience of this trip. This tour can be booked for just two people, and importantly, offers Chinese-speaking guides — perfect for first-time visitors like us who want to dive deeper into Bali's culture!

We departed promptly at 7:30 AM from our hotel, with private transfers making everything super convenient. After a nap en route, we arrived at our first stop: the famous **Lempuyang Temple**, also known as the 'Gates of Heaven'. Taking those iconic symmetrical mirror photos here was absolutely dreamy! 📷⛩️ Our Chinese guide also shared the sacred history of this temple, helping us appreciate the local religious culture beyond just snapping photos.

Next, we visited **Swing Heaven**, which isn't just about swinging — there are multiple Instagrammable spots for stunning photos 🌺. After our photo session, the guide helped us find the perfect angles — truly a pro!

By midday, we arrived at the serene **Tirta Gangga Water Palace**, once a royal bathing place. The ponds are filled with koi fish, and you can buy fish food to feed them or stroll across the stepping stones — a delightful experience! 🐟💦

Then, we explored the majestic **Taman Ujung Water Palace**, a less crowded spot with a relaxed atmosphere, perfect for quietly admiring the harmonious blend of architecture and water.

Our final stop was the beautiful **Virgin Beach**, a hidden gem far from the crowds. With its crystal-clear waters and white sand, and few locals around, it's ideal for unwinding, sunbathing, and sipping coconut water 🌞🥥.

## 🌿 Day 3: Half-Day Ubud Exploration & Spa Time

After waking up naturally today, we checked out from Sanur and headed to the Ubud Market for a leisurely stroll. We indulged in Ubud's famous crispy roast pork rice, followed by a 90-minute Balinese essential oil massage at a spa.

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, surrounded by rice paddies, forests, and traditional villages, making it an ideal destination for those seeking nature, art, and spiritual experiences. In Ubud, you can join yoga classes, visit craft villages, immerse yourself in Balinese temple culture, and relax in numerous high-quality villas.

How can you visit Bali without enjoying a villa!? We stayed at Ayuterra Resort, a villa that made me reluctant to leave the pool🏞️. The room was stylish and spacious, with an outdoor private infinity pool facing the Ayung River gorge, every moment felt like a postcard📸. Spending the evening stargazing in the pool and waking up to birdsong made it the most relaxing and romantic night of my Ubud journey❤️. Highly recommended for honeymooners or couples celebrating special occasions!

## 🌿 Day 4: Klook "Ubud Highlights Private Day Tour" | Culture & Nature All-In-One, Unlock Ubud's Essence in a Day!

On this day, we booked the "Ubud Highlights Private Day Tour" on Klook, which also offers Chinese-speaking guides. However, we were accompanied by an excellent English-speaking driver, **Yogi**, who guided us through the entire itinerary. He was very patient, planned the most convenient and less crowded routes, and even took care of all the parking fees at each attraction, so we didn't have to pay extra—truly thoughtful!

We departed from the hotel at **08:30**, first stopping at the stunning **Tegallalang Rice Terraces**, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The layered rice paddies resembled green waves, and the scenery was breathtakingly beautiful, making every photo look like a dreamy postcard!🌾📷

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_55c19053b01f715dbb5fe2d0dc3a3e72.jpg)

Next, we experienced Ubud's classic **jungle swing**. The moment we swung out was thrilling and fun, with dense tropical rainforests and valleys below, making us feel like we were flying in a movie scene!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_81b50f6d3f97aacd54da321c458cacce.jpg)

Our third stop was the **Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary**, a natural habitat for Balinese long-tailed monkeys. Although the monkeys are lively and curious, as long as you don't feed or provoke them, there's basically no danger. The guide also taught us how to maintain a safe distance from the monkeys, making it a unique and interesting experience 🐒🌳

For lunch, our driver Yogi picked out a restaurant with an amazing view where we could enjoy our meal while overlooking the rice fields — the atmosphere was perfect (thanks to his excellent planning!).

After lunch, we headed to **Tirta Empul Temple**, a spot I was really looking forward to. The holy spring water in the temple is said to have purifying energy, and many local devotees and tourists immerse themselves in the pools to receive blessings. Although I didn’t step into the water, sitting quietly and observing this scene of devotion brought me a sense of peace 🙏💧.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8b762a26998eb9109cd20d800e686b26.jpg)

Our final stop was the popular **Tegenungan Waterfall** in Ubud. The waterfall is truly spectacular, with its roaring waters creating a powerful scene. However, it was quite crowded with tourists, and many people were playing in the water or taking photos, which made it feel a bit commercialized to me. I didn’t stay too long, but this might vary depending on personal preferences.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99403f7db64395a7fa81d169116fa952.jpg)

We returned to the hotel around **17:00**, wrapping up a nearly 10-hour journey that was both fulfilling and relaxing. A special thanks to our driver **Yogi**, who was incredibly kind and thoughtful — he planned the itinerary perfectly and helped us avoid peak crowds. Highly recommended!

Every hotel here feels like a mini-city, with excellent security, clean streets, and an upscale vibe — it’s like Bali’s version of a wealthy neighborhood, perfect for travelers looking to completely unwind and relax.

We stayed at Mulia Resort, a well-known luxury hotel on the island that offers exceptional value. On this day, we chose to join the 【Uluwatu Cliff Temple Kecak Fire Dance & Sanur Beach Private Tour】, with the option of a Chinese-speaking guide. ### ⛱️ 09:00 Departure → 11:00 Padang Padang Beach

Our first stop was the hidden gem—**Padang Padang Beach**. Surrounded by lush cliffs, reaching this beach requires navigating through a narrow rocky path, adding a sense of adventure! The beach is small but boasts fine white sand and crystal-clear blue waters, making it a perfect spot for swimming and photography 🌊📸

### 🍴 12:30 Lunchtime

We chose our own lunch spot, and Tawan thoughtfully recommended a restaurant with ocean views. Enjoying grilled fish with coconut water while watching surfers ride the waves was pure bliss～

### 🏝️ 14:00 Blue Point / Suluban Beach

Next, we headed to **Suluban Beach**, also known as "Blue Point Beach," a secret paradise for many surfers. This beach is surrounded by massive caves, and during low tide, you can walk deep into the caverns to explore small pools—it’s incredibly mysterious and full of adventure! It truly feels like discovering a "secret hideout for adults" 🏄‍♂️

### 🛕 16:30 Uluwatu Temple

This ancient temple perched on a cliff is not only one of Bali’s six sacred temples but also an excellent spot to admire the majestic views of the Indian Ocean. The monkeys here are playful and adorable, so it’s advised to keep your belongings secure (sunglasses and hats, especially!). With our guide, we learned about the history and religious significance of Uluwatu Temple, gaining a deeper understanding of the locals’ reverence for nature and the divine.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f221761e8c64cd4b7a6f2dcc5e6f1859.jpg)

### 🔥 18:00 Kecak Dance Performance (Highlight: **Skip the Line!**)

This performance was truly one of the highlights of our trip! The Kecak Dance doesn’t rely on any instruments; instead, dozens of shirtless men create a rhythmic "chak-chak-chak" chant, accompanied by fire dancing and interactive scenes that bring the classic tale of the Indian epic *Ramayana* to life! The performance begins at sunset and ends with bursts of fire and cheers from the crowd—the atmosphere was absolutely electrifying 🔥🔥🔥

## Day Tour FAQ

### 1. Q: How many people are needed to form a group for private guided tours on Klook?

A: Yes! Most private guided tours on Klook **require only 2 people to form a group, do not merge with other groups, and even solo travelers can join**, so you don't need to worry about gathering enough people. Whether it's couples, friends, or families, everyone can easily enjoy a dedicated driver-guide, making the itinerary more flexible.

### 2. Q: Can I choose a Chinese-speaking guide?

A: **Some tours offer the option of a Chinese-speaking guide, so please pay special attention to the 'Language' field when booking.** If a Chinese-speaking guide is not available, most tours come with an English-speaking guide or a local driver. You can also contact the local driver via WhatsApp in advance to communicate your needs.

### 3. Q: Does the tour include tickets, lunch, or parking fees?

A: Each itinerary varies slightly. Some include admission tickets and lunch, but most will specify "meals at your own expense" or "admission fees not included." For example, our Ubud day tour doesn't require additional parking fees at any attractions, but lunch is self-paid. We recommend reading the details carefully or consulting customer service before departure.

### 4. Q: Will the driver accompany us into the attractions?

A: Yes! Most tours are "guided private car services," where the driver will accompany you into attractions to provide explanations, take photos, and assist with ticket purchases. However, if you choose the English-speaking driver option, you may explore some attractions on your own.

### 5. Q: Can the itinerary be customized? Can we change the route midway?

A: Generally not, but some private tours offer more flexibility. You can inquire with customer service or the driver in advance to see if minor adjustments (such as swapping an attraction or extending the stay) are possible, depending on traffic and time constraints.

### 6. Q: If we stay in different areas, like Ubud or Jimbaran, can we still get pick-up service?

A: Yes, but the pick-up range and fees may vary by area. Most tours offer "free pick-up" in areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Sanur, and Ubud. Other areas may require an additional fee or confirmation of availability.

### 7. Q: Will the day tour feel rushed?

A: Klook's tours are designed to be "moderately paced," lasting about 8 to 10 hours. While there are many attractions scheduled, private tours aren't rushed, and the driver will adjust the route to avoid crowds. For example, we arrived early for the Kecak Fire Dance performance, skipping the queue and enhancing the experience!

### 8. Q: Do we need to prepare cash?

A: It's highly recommended to carry some Indonesian Rupiah in cash. While some attractions and restaurants accept credit cards, places like Ubud Traditional Market, fire dance tickets, and temple donations still require cash.

### 9. Q: Will there be traffic jams? Is there buffer time?

A: Traffic peaks in Bali can lead to congestion, but Klook drivers usually avoid peak hours or adjust routes to bypass heavy traffic.

### 10. Q: How can I contact the driver or guide?

A: Klook will provide driver contact information before your trip. We recommend downloading WhatsApp, the most commonly used communication app in Bali, to confirm pickup times and guide language preferences in advance.

### 11. Q: Are there more day tours available?

A: Klook offers [over 100 Bali day tours](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1049626&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Fdestination%2Fc8-bali%2F%3Fpage%3D1%26frontend_id_list%3D10). We suggest choosing highly-rated tours based on your accommodation area.
