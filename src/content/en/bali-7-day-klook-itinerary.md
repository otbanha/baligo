## 🛬 Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Time to Unwind!

After landing at Ngurah Rai Airport, we took a pre-booked airport transfer to our hotel in Sanur on Bali's east coast. Sanur is a tranquil coastal town in southeastern Bali, known for its laid-back atmosphere and slow pace of life—a favorite among families and long-stay travelers. It boasts a long beachfront promenade and sunrise beaches, perfect for morning walks, cycling, and swimming. Unlike bustling Kuta, Sanur exudes a relaxing, elegant charm.

https://youtu.be/SpcgZ4iK7qs

We stayed at Griya Santrian, a beachfront resort where you can step from the pool right onto the sand—ideal for water lovers and beach bums. The architecture retains traditional Balinese style, with serene garden landscapes. The hotel also offers spa services, yoga classes, and an ocean-view restaurant, perfect for travelers wanting to embrace the 'slow living' charm of Sanur.

## Day Tour FAQ

### 1. Q: How many people are needed for a private guided tour on Klook?

A: Most private guided tours on Klook require **just 2 people to start, no group sharing, and even solo travel is fine**—no need to worry about gathering a group. Whether you're a couple, friends, or a family, you can easily enjoy a private driver-guide with more flexible itineraries.

### 2. Q: Can I choose a Chinese-speaking guide?

A: **Some tours offer a Chinese-speaking guide option—please check the 'Language' field when booking.** If a Chinese guide isn’t available, English-speaking guides or local drivers are usually provided. You can also contact the local driver in advance via WhatsApp to discuss your needs.

### 3. Q: Do the tours include entrance fees, lunch, or parking fees?

A: Details vary by tour. Some include entrance fees and lunch, but most will specify 'meals at your own expense' or 'entrance fees not included.' For example, our Ubud day tour didn’t require any extra parking fees at any of the attractions, but lunch was at our own cost. We recommend reading the details carefully or contacting customer service before departure.

### 4. Q: Will the driver accompany us into the attractions?

A: Yes! Most itineraries are "guided private car tours," where the driver accompanies you to attractions to explain, take photos, and assist with ticket purchases. However, if you choose the English-speaking driver option, you may need to explore some attractions on your own.

### 5. Q: Can the itinerary be customized? Can I change the route midway?

A: Generally, no, but some private tours offer more flexibility. You can ask customer service or the driver in advance if minor adjustments are possible (such as swapping a specific attraction or extending your stay), but this depends on traffic and time availability on the day.

### 6. Q: If we're staying in different areas, like Ubud or Jimbaran, can we still be picked up?

A: Yes, but the pickup range and fees may vary by area. Most itineraries offer "free pickup" in areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Sanur, and Ubud. Other areas may require an additional fee or confirmation of availability.

### 7. Q: Will the day tour feel rushed?

A: Klook's itineraries are generally "moderately paced," lasting about 8 to 10 hours a day. Although there are many attractions scheduled, private tours don't feel rushed, and the driver will help adjust the route to avoid crowds. For example, we arrived early for the Kecak fire dance performance and didn't have to queue for entry—a huge plus!

### 8. Q: Do I need to bring cash?

A: It's highly recommended to carry some Indonesian rupiah in cash. While some attractions and restaurants accept cards, places like Ubud's traditional market, fire dance tickets, and temple donations still require cash.

### 9. Q: Will there be traffic jams? Is time built in for that?

A: Traffic in Bali can get congested during peak hours, but Klook drivers usually avoid busy times or adjust the route order to steer clear of jams.
