## 🛬 Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Chill Mode Activated!

After landing at Ngurah Rai Airport, we took a pre-booked airport transfer to our hotel in Sanur on Bali's east coast. Sanur is a serene coastal town in southeastern Bali, known for its laid-back atmosphere and slow pace—a favorite among families and long-stay travelers. It boasts a long beachfront promenade and sunrise beaches, perfect for morning walks, cycling, and swimming, offering a relaxing elegance unlike bustling Kuta.

https://youtu.be/SpcgZ4iK7qs

We stayed at Griya Santrian, a beachfront resort where you can step from the pool right onto the sand—ideal for water lovers and beach bums. The architecture retains traditional Balinese style, with lush garden landscapes. The hotel also offers spa services, yoga classes, and an ocean-view restaurant, perfect for those wanting to savor the 'slow living' charm of Sanur.

Next, we headed to **Suluban Beach**, also known as 'Blue Point Beach,' a secret paradise for many surfers. ## Day Tour FAQ

### 1. Q: How many people are needed to form a group for private guided tours on Klook?

A: Yes! Most private guided tours on Klook require **just 2 people to start, no group merging, and even solo travel is fine**—no need to worry about gathering a group. Whether you're a couple, friends, or a family, you can easily enjoy a dedicated driver-guide with more flexible itineraries.

### 2. Q: Can I choose a Chinese-speaking guide?

A: **Some tours offer a Chinese-speaking guide option; please pay special attention to the 'Language' field when booking.** If a Chinese-speaking guide is not available, most tours come with an English-speaking guide or a local driver. You can also contact the local driver in advance via WhatsApp to discuss your needs.

### 3. Q: Do the tours include entrance fees, lunch, or parking fees?

A: Details vary by tour. Some tours include entrance fees and lunch, but most will specify 'meals at your own expense' or 'entrance fees not included.' For example, our Ubud day tour did not require any additional parking fees at any of the attractions, but lunch was at our own expense. We recommend reading the details carefully or contacting customer service before departure.

### 4. Q: Will the driver accompany us into the attractions?

A: Yes! Most tours are **guided private charters**, where the driver will accompany you into the attractions to provide explanations, take photos, and assist with ticket purchases. However, if you choose the English-speaking driver option, you may explore some attractions on your own.

### 5. Q: Can the itinerary be customized? Can I change the route midway?

A: Generally, no, but some private tours offer more flexibility. You can ask customer service or the driver in advance if minor adjustments are possible (such as swapping a specific attraction or extending your stay), but this depends on the day's traffic and time availability.

### 6. Q: If we're staying in different areas, like Ubud or Jimbaran, can we still get picked up?

A: Yes, but the pickup range and fees may vary by area. Most tours offer 'free pickup' in areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Sanur, and Ubud. Other areas may require an additional fee or need confirmation for pickup.

### 7. Q: Will the day tour feel rushed?

A: Klook's tours are generally 'moderately paced,' lasting about 8 to 10 hours. Although there are many stops, private tours don't rush you, and the driver will adjust the route to avoid crowds. For example, we arrived early before the Kecak fire dance performance and got in without queuing—a huge plus!

### 8. Q: Do I need to bring cash?

A: It's highly recommended to bring some Indonesian rupiah in cash. While some attractions and restaurants accept cards, places like Ubud's traditional market, fire dance tickets, and temple donations still require cash.

### 9. Q: Will there be traffic jams? Is time built in for that?

A: Bali's peak traffic can cause jams, but Klook's drivers usually avoid busy departure times or adjust the route order to steer clear of congestion.

### 10. Q: How do I contact the driver or guide?
