## 🛬 Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Relaxation Mode Activated!

After arriving at Denpasar Airport, we took our pre-booked airport transfer to our hotel in Sanur on Bali's east coast. Sanur is a peaceful coastal town in southeastern Bali, known for its relaxed atmosphere and slow pace of life, making it a favorite among family travelers and long-term visitors. With its long beachfront promenade and sunrise beach, Sanur is perfect for morning walks, cycling, and swimming. Unlike the bustling Kuta, Sanur exudes a relaxing elegance.

We stayed at Griya Santrian, a beachfront resort where you can walk from the pool straight onto the sand - perfect for water lovers and beach enthusiasts. The architecture maintains traditional Balinese style, with tranquil garden landscapes. The hotel also offers SPA, yoga classes, and ocean-view restaurants, ideal for travelers wanting to enjoy the 'slow life' charm of Sanur.

## 📸 Day 2: Klook's 'Bali East Instagram Tour' 🌋🌴

Today's highlight is Klook's 【East Bali Instagrammable Day Tour】, our first Klook activity of this trip. The tour can be arranged for just two people, and the best part is that you can choose a Chinese-speaking guide. It's perfect for first-time visitors like us who want to dive deeper into the local culture!

We departed promptly at 7:30 AM from our hotel, with private car transfers making everything super convenient. After a quick nap on the road, we arrived at our first stop: the famous **Lempuyang Temple**, also known as the 'Gates of Heaven'. Taking that classic symmetrical mirror shot here was absolutely dreamy! 📷⛩️ Our Chinese guide also explained the sacred origins of the temple, allowing us to appreciate the local religious culture beyond just snapping photos.

Next, we visited **Swing Heaven**, which isn't just about swinging but also features multiple Instagrammable spots for stunning photos 🌺. After taking pictures, our guide even helped us find the perfect angles—his photography skills were top-notch!

By noon, we arrived at the serene **Tirta Gangga Water Palace**, a former royal bathing complex. The pools were filled with koi fish, and we could buy fish food to feed them or stroll across the stepping stones—it was such a delightful experience! 🐟💦

Then, we explored the majestic **Taman Ujung Water Palace**, a quieter spot with a relaxed atmosphere, perfect for quietly admiring the harmonious blend of architecture and water.

Our final stop was **Virgin Beach**, a hidden gem far from the crowds. With its crystal-clear waters and white sand, and few locals around, it was ideal for unwinding, sunbathing, and sipping coconut water 🌞🥥.

## 🌿 Day 3: Half-Day Ubud Exploration + Spa Time

After waking up naturally today, we checked out from Sanur and headed to Ubud Market for a leisurely stroll. We enjoyed Ubud's famous crispy roasted pork rice, followed by a 90-minute Balinese aromatherapy massage at a spa.

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, surrounded by rice fields, forests, and traditional villages. It's the perfect place to seek nature, art, and spiritual experiences. In Ubud, you can join yoga classes, visit craft villages, immerse yourself in Balinese temple culture, and relax in numerous high-quality villas.

How can you visit Bali without enjoying a villa!? We stayed at Ayuterra Resort, a villa where I couldn't tear myself away from the pool🏞️. The room was stylish and spacious, with a private infinity pool overlooking the Ayung River gorge—every moment felt like a postcard📸. Soaking in the pool under the stars at night and waking up to birdsong in the morning made it the most relaxing and romantic night of my Ubud trip❤️. Highly recommended for honeymooners or couples celebrating special occasions!

## 🌿 Day 4: Klook "Ubud Highlights Private Day Tour" | Culture & Nature All-In-One, Unlocking Ubud's Essence in a Day!

Today, we booked Klook's "Ubud Highlights Private Day Tour." While Chinese-speaking guides were an option, we were accompanied by an amazing English-speaking driver, **Yogi**, who guided us through the entire itinerary. He was incredibly patient, planned the most efficient route to avoid crowds, and even took care of parking fees at each spot—no extra charges needed. Truly thoughtful!

We departed from the hotel at **08:30**, with our first stop being the stunning **Tegallalang Rice Terraces**, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The layered rice fields resembled green waves, and the scenery was breathtaking—every photo looked like a dreamy postcard! 🌾📷

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_55c19053b01f715dbb5fe2d0dc3a3e72.jpg)

Next, we experienced Ubud's classic **jungle swing**. The moment we swung out, it was both thrilling and fun, with lush tropical rainforests and valleys below—it felt like flying in a movie scene!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_81b50f6d3f97aacd54da321c458cacce.jpg)

Our third stop was the **Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary**, a natural habitat for Balinese long-tailed monkeys. While the monkeys are lively and curious, as long as you don't feed or provoke them, there's no danger. Our guide even taught us how to maintain a safe distance from them—a unique and fascinating experience 🐒🌳.

For lunch, our driver Yogi took us to a restaurant with an amazing view overlooking rice fields - the atmosphere was perfect (thanks to his excellent planning!)

After lunch, we visited **Tirta Empul Temple**, a spot I was really looking forward to. The holy spring water here is said to have purifying energy, and many local devotees and tourists immerse themselves in the pools for blessings. While I didn't enter the water, sitting quietly and observing this scene of devotion brought me an unexpected sense of peace 🙏💧

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8b762a26998eb9109cd20d800e686b26.jpg)

Our final stop was Ubud's popular **Tegenungan Waterfall**. The waterfall is truly spectacular with its roaring waters, but it felt a bit too crowded and commercialized for my taste, with many people playing in the water and taking photos. I didn't stay long, but it really depends on personal preference!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99403f7db64395a7fa81d169116fa952.jpg)

We returned to our hotel around **17:00** after this nearly 10-hour journey, which was both fulfilling and relaxing. Special thanks to our driver **Yogi**, who was super nice - not only did he plan the itinerary perfectly, but he also helped us avoid peak crowds. Highly recommended!

## 🔥 Day 6: Uluwatu Cliff Kecak Fire Dance & Suluban Beach Private Tour｜A Journey That Shakes Your Senses and Soul!

On this day, we opted for the 【Uluwatu Cliff Temple Kecak Fire Dance & Suluban Beach Private Tour】, which offers Chinese-speaking guides. Our driver and guide was the incredibly cool **Mr. Tawan**! He was passionate and street-smart, deeply familiar with the history and culture of each stop. Whether it was the meaning behind the Kecak dance, the architecture of Uluwatu Temple, or the formation of the beaches, he provided rich explanations. 🌟🌟🌟🌟🌟 This journey became even more complete because of him!

### ⛱️ 09:00 Departure → 11:00 Padang Padang Beach

Our first stop was the hidden gem — **Padang Padang Beach**. Surrounded by lush cliffs, reaching this beach requires navigating through a narrow rocky path, adding a sense of adventure! The beach is small but boasts fine white sand and crystal-clear blue waters, making it a perfect spot for swimming and photography 🌊📸

### 🍴 12:30 Lunchtime

We chose our own lunch spot, and Tawan thoughtfully recommended a restaurant with ocean views. Enjoying grilled fish with coconut water while watching surfers ride the waves was the epitome of chill～

### 🏝️ 14:00 Blue Point / Suluban Beach

Next, we headed to **Suluban Beach**, also known as "Blue Point Beach," a secret paradise for many surfers. This beach is surrounded by massive caves, and during low tide, you can walk deep into the caverns to explore small pools—it’s mysterious and full of adventure! It truly feels like discovering a "secret base for adults" 🏄‍♂️

### 🛕 16:30 Uluwatu Temple

This ancient temple perched on a cliff is not only one of Bali’s six most sacred temples but also an excellent spot to admire the majestic views of the Indian Ocean. The monkeys here are playful and cute, so it’s advised to keep your belongings secure (sunglasses and hats, especially!). With our guide, we learned about the history and religious significance of Uluwatu Temple, gaining a deeper understanding of the locals’ reverence for nature and the divine.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f221761e8c64cd4b7a6f2dcc5e6f1859.jpg)

### 🔥 18:00 Kecak Dance Performance (Highlight: **Skip-the-line entry!**)

This performance was truly one of the highlights of our trip! The Kecak Dance doesn’t rely on any instruments but instead uses the rhythmic chanting of "chak-chak-chak" by dozens of shirtless men to create an atmosphere. Combined with the dancers’ fire performances and interactive segments, it brings the classic scenes of the Indian epic *Ramayana* to life! The show begins at sunset and ends with bursts of fire and cheers from the crowd—it’s absolutely electrifying 🔥🔥🔥

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3ae76de3e2f7cbfb46b702bcaa7a626e.jpg)

## Frequently Asked Questions About Day Tours

### 1. Q: How many people are needed to form a private guided tour on Klook?

A: Yes! Most private guided tours on Klook require **a minimum of 2 people, no group merging, and even solo travelers can join**, so you don’t need to worry about gathering enough people. Whether it’s couples, friends, or families, everyone can easily enjoy a private driver-guided tour with more flexibility.

### 2. Q: Can I choose a Chinese-speaking guide?

A: **Some tours offer the option of a Chinese-speaking guide, so please pay special attention to the 'Language' field when booking.** If a Chinese-speaking guide is not available, most tours provide English-speaking guides or local drivers. You can also contact the local driver via WhatsApp in advance to discuss your needs.

### 3. Q: Does the tour include tickets, lunch, or parking fees?

A: Each tour has slightly different details. Some tours include entrance tickets and lunch, but most will specify "meals at your own expense" or "entrance fees not included." For example, our Ubud day tour doesn't require additional parking fees at any attractions, but lunch is at your own expense. We recommend reading the details carefully or consulting customer service before departure.

### 4. Q: Will the driver accompany us into the attractions?

A: Yes! Most tours are "**guided private tours**," where the driver will accompany you into the attractions, provide explanations, take photos, and assist with ticket purchases. However, if you choose the English-speaking driver option, you may explore some attractions on your own.

### 5. Q: Can the itinerary be customized? Can we change the route midway?

A: Generally, no, but some private tours offer more flexibility. You can inquire with customer service or the driver in advance about minor adjustments to the route (e.g., replacing an attraction or extending the stay), but this depends on traffic and time constraints on the day.

### 6. Q: If we stay in different areas, such as Ubud or Jimbaran, can we still get pick-up service?

A: Yes, but the pick-up range and fees may vary depending on the area. Most tours offer "free pick-up" in areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Sanur, and Ubud. Other areas may require an additional fee or confirmation of availability.

### 7. Q: Will the day tour feel rushed?

A: Klook tours are designed to have a "moderate pace," lasting about 8–10 hours. While there are many attractions scheduled, private tours aren't rushed, and the driver will help adjust the route to avoid crowds. For example, we arrived early before the Kecak Fire Dance performance, skipping the queue and greatly enhancing the experience!

### 8. Q: Do we need to prepare cash?

A: It's highly recommended to carry some Indonesian Rupiah in cash. While many attractions and restaurants accept card payments, places like Ubud Traditional Market, fire dance tickets, and temple donations often require cash.

### 9. Q: Will there be traffic jams? Is there buffer time?

A: Traffic congestion is common during peak hours in Bali, but Klook drivers usually avoid traveling during busy times or adjust the route sequence to bypass traffic.

### 10. Q: How can I contact the driver or tour guide?

A: Klook will provide the driver's contact information before your trip. We recommend downloading WhatsApp, as it's the most commonly used communication method in Bali. You can confirm pickup times and guide languages in advance.

### 11. Q: Are there more day tours to choose from?
