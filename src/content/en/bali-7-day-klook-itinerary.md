## 🛬 Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Relaxation Mode Activated!

After landing at Denpasar Airport, we took our pre-booked airport transfer to our hotel in Sanur, located on Bali's east coast. Sanur is a tranquil coastal town in southeastern Bali, known for its relaxed atmosphere and slow pace of life, making it a favorite among families and long-term visitors. Sanur boasts a long seaside promenade and sunrise beach, perfect for morning walks, cycling, and swimming. Unlike the bustling Kuta, Sanur exudes a calming elegance.

We stayed at Griya Santrian, a beachfront resort where you can step onto the sand just a few steps from the pool - ideal for water lovers or beach enthusiasts. The architecture retains traditional Balinese style, and the garden scenery is serene and comfortable. The hotel also offers spa services, yoga classes, and a sea-view restaurant, making it perfect for travelers looking to enjoy the charm of 'slow living' in Sanur.

## 📸 Day 2: Klook 'Bali East Instagrammable Spots Tour' 🌋🌴

Today's highlight is Klook's 【Bali East Instagrammable Day Tour】, our first Klook experience of this trip. This tour can be arranged for just two people, and the best part is that you can opt for a Chinese-speaking guide — perfect for first-time visitors like us who want to dive deeper into the local culture!

We departed promptly at 7:30 AM from our hotel, with private transfers making everything super convenient. After a quick nap en route, we arrived at our first stop: the famous **Lempuyang Temple**, also known as the 'Gates of Heaven'. Capturing the iconic symmetrical mirror shot here was absolutely dreamy! 📷⛩️ Our Chinese guide also shared the sacred origins of this temple, allowing us to appreciate not just the photos but also the local religious culture.

Next, we headed to **Swing Heaven**, which isn’t just about swinging — there are multiple Instagrammable spots to capture stunning photos 🌺. After snapping away, our guide helped us find the perfect angles — a true photography pro!

By midday, we arrived at the serene **Tirta Gangga Water Palace**, a former royal bathing complex. The ponds are filled with koi fish, and you can buy fish food to feed them or stroll across the stepping stones — a delightful experience! 🐟💦

Then, we visited the majestic **Taman Ujung Water Palace**, a quieter spot with a relaxed atmosphere, perfect for quietly admiring the harmonious blend of architecture and water.

Our final stop was my personal favorite: **Virgin Beach**, a hidden gem far from the crowds. With its crystal-clear waters and pristine white sand, and few locals around, it’s ideal for unwinding, sunbathing, and sipping coconut water 🌞🥥.

## 🌿 Day 3: Half-Day Free Exploration in Ubud + Spa Time

After waking up naturally today, we checked out from Sanur and headed to Ubud Market for a leisurely stroll. We enjoyed Ubud's famous crispy roast pork rice, followed by a 90-minute Balinese aromatherapy massage at a spa.

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, surrounded by rice paddies, forests, and traditional villages, making it an ideal place to seek nature, art, and spiritual experiences. In Ubud, you can join yoga classes, visit craft villages, immerse yourself in Balinese temple culture, and relax in numerous high-quality villas.

How can you visit Bali without enjoying a villa!? We stayed at Ayuterra Resort, a villa that made me reluctant to leave the pool🏞️. The room was stylish and spacious, with an outdoor private infinity pool overlooking the Ayung River Gorge, every moment felt like a postcard📸. Soaking in the pool under the stars at night and waking up to birdsong in the morning made it the most relaxing and romantic night of my Ubud journey❤️. Highly recommended for honeymooners or couples celebrating special occasions!

## 🌿 Day 4: Klook "Ubud Hotspots Private Day Tour" | Culture & Nature All-In-One, Unlock Ubud's Essence in a Day!

On this day, we booked the 【Ubud Hotspots Private Day Tour】 on Klook, which also offers Chinese-speaking guides. However, this time we were accompanied by an excellent English-speaking driver, **Yogi**, who guided us through the entire itinerary. He was very patient, planned the most efficient route avoiding crowds, and even took care of parking fees at each spot beforehand, so we didn't need to pay extra—truly thoughtful!

We departed from the hotel at **08:30**, first heading to the stunning UNESCO World Heritage site—**Tegallalang Rice Terraces**. The layered rice paddies resembled green waves, offering breathtaking views that looked like scenes from a postcard no matter where you stood!🌾📷

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_55c19053b01f715dbb5fe2d0dc3a3e72.jpg)

Next, we experienced Ubud's classic **jungle swing**. The moment you swing out, it's both thrilling and fun, with dense tropical rainforests and valleys below, making you feel like you're flying in a movie scene!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_81b50f6d3f97aacd54da321c458cacce.jpg)

Our third stop was the **Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary**, a natural habitat for Balinese long-tailed monkeys. Although the monkeys are lively and curious, as long as you don't feed or provoke them, there's generally no danger. The guide also taught us how to maintain a safe distance from the monkeys, making it a unique and interesting experience 🐒🌳

For lunch, our driver Yogi picked out a restaurant with an incredible view overlooking rice paddies — the perfect setting for a relaxing meal (thanks to his impeccable planning!).

After lunch, we headed to **Tirta Empul Temple**, a spot I’d been looking forward to visiting. The holy spring water here is said to have purifying energy, and many local devotees and tourists immerse themselves in the pools to receive blessings. While I didn’t take part, simply sitting nearby and observing the devotion brought a sense of calm 🙏💧.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8b762a26998eb9109cd20d800e686b26.jpg)

Our final stop was the popular **Tegenungan Waterfall** in Ubud. The waterfall is undeniably impressive, with its roaring waters and dramatic scenery. However, it felt a bit too crowded and commercialized for my taste, with many visitors swimming and taking photos. I didn’t stay long, but it’s worth checking out depending on your preferences.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99403f7db64395a7fa81d169116fa952.jpg)

We returned to the hotel around **17:00**, wrapping up a nearly 10-hour journey that was both fulfilling and relaxing. A huge shoutout to our driver **Yogi** — he was incredibly kind, expertly planned the itinerary, and helped us avoid peak crowds. Highly recommended!

## 🔥 Day 6: Uluwatu Cliff Kecak Dance & Suluban Beach Private Tour｜A Journey That Shakes the Senses and Soul!

On this day, we opted for the 【Uluwatu Temple Kecak Dance & Suluban Beach Private Tour】, which offers Chinese-speaking guides. Our driver and guide was the incredibly cool **Mr. Tawan**! He was both passionate and street-smart, deeply knowledgeable about the history and culture of each stop. Whether it was the meaning behind the Kecak dance, the architecture of Uluwatu Temple, or the formation of the beaches, he provided rich and insightful explanations. 🌟🌟🌟🌟🌟 This journey became even more complete because of him!

### ⛱️ 09:00 Departure → 11:00 Padang Padang Beach

Our first stop was the hidden gem—**Padang Padang Beach**. Surrounded by lush cliffs, reaching this beach requires navigating through a narrow rocky path, adding a sense of adventure! The beach is small but boasts fine white sand and crystal-clear blue waters, making it a perfect spot for swimming and photography 🌊📸

### 🍴 12:30 Lunchtime

We chose our own lunch spot, and Tawan thoughtfully recommended a restaurant with ocean views. Enjoying grilled fish with coconut water while watching surfers ride the waves was the epitome of chill～

### 🏝️ 14:00 Blue Point / Suluban Beach

Next, we headed to **Suluban Beach**, also known as "Blue Point Beach," a secret paradise for many surfers. Surrounded by massive caves, during low tide, you can walk deep into the caverns and explore small pools, making it a mysterious and adventurous spot! It truly feels like discovering a "secret hideout for adults" 🏄‍♂️

### 🛕 16:30 Uluwatu Temple

This ancient temple perched on a cliff is not only one of Bali's six major holy temples but also offers a stunning view of the Indian Ocean. The mischievous monkeys here are adorable, but be sure to keep your belongings secure (sunglasses and hats included!). With our guide, we learned about the history and religious significance of Uluwatu Temple, gaining a deeper understanding of the locals' reverence for nature and the divine.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f221761e8c64cd4b7a6f2dcc5e6f1859.jpg)

### 🔥 18:00 Kecak Fire Dance Performance (Highlight: **Skip the Line Entry!**)

This performance was truly one of the highlights of our trip! The Kecak Dance doesn’t rely on any instruments but instead uses the rhythmic chanting of dozens of bare-chested men creating a "chak-chak-chak" sound. Combined with fire dancing and interactive scenes, it brings the classic tale of the Indian epic *Ramayana* to life! The show begins at sunset and ends with fiery sparks and cheering crowds, creating an incredibly powerful atmosphere 🔥🔥🔥

## Day Tour FAQ

### 1. Q: How many people are needed to form a group for private tours on Klook?

A: Yes! Most private tours on Klook require **only 2 people to form a group, no merging with other groups, and even solo travelers are welcome**, so you don't need to worry about gathering enough people. Whether it's couples, best friends, or families, everyone can easily enjoy a dedicated driver and guide, making the itinerary more flexible.

### 2. Q: Can I choose a Chinese-speaking guide?

A: **Some tours offer the option of a Chinese-speaking guide, so please pay special attention to the 'Language' field when booking.** If a Chinese-speaking guide is not available, most tours provide an English-speaking guide or a local driver. You can also contact the local driver via WhatsApp in advance to discuss your needs.

### 3. Q: Are tickets, lunch, or parking fees included in the tour?

A: Each itinerary has slight differences. Some include entrance tickets and lunch, but most will specify "meals at your own expense" or "entrance fees not included." For example, our Ubud day tour doesn't require additional parking fees at any attractions, but lunch is self-funded. We recommend reading the details carefully or consulting customer service before departure.

### 4. Q: Will the driver accompany us into the attractions?

A: Yes! Most tours are "guided private car services," where the driver will accompany you into the attractions, provide explanations, take photos, and assist with ticket purchases. However, if you choose the English-speaking driver option, you may explore some attractions on your own.

### 5. Q: Can the itinerary be customized? Can we change the route midway?

A: Generally, no, but some private tours offer more flexibility. You can inquire with customer service or the driver in advance about minor adjustments to the route (e.g., swapping an attraction or extending the stay), but this depends on traffic and time constraints on the day.

### 6. Q: If we stay in different areas, such as Ubud or Jimbaran, can we still get pick-up service?

A: Yes, but the pick-up range and fees may vary by area. Most tours offer "free pick-up" in areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Sanur, and Ubud. Other areas may incur additional charges or require confirmation.

### 7. Q: Will the day tour feel rushed?

A: Klook's tours are typically "moderately paced," lasting about 8 to 10 hours. While there are many attractions scheduled, private tours are not rushed, and the driver will adjust the route to avoid crowds. For example, we arrived early before the Kecak fire dance performance, avoiding long queues and enhancing the experience!

### 8. Q: Do we need to prepare cash?

A: It’s highly recommended to carry some Indonesian Rupiah in cash. While many attractions and restaurants accept card payments, places like Ubud Traditional Market, fire dance tickets, and temple donations often require cash.

### 9. Q: Will there be traffic jams? Is there buffer time included?

A: Traffic congestion is common during peak hours in Bali, but Klook drivers usually avoid traveling during busy times or adjust the route to bypass traffic.

### 10. Q: How can I contact the driver or guide?

A: Before your trip, Klook will provide the driver’s contact information. We recommend downloading WhatsApp, as it’s the most commonly used communication method in Bali. You can confirm pickup times, guide languages, and other details in advance.

### 11. Q: Are there more day tours to choose from?

A: Klook offers [over 100 Bali day tours](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1049626&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Fdestination%2Fc8-bali%2F%3Fpage%3D1%26frontend_id_list%3D10). We suggest selecting highly-rated tours based on the area you’re staying in.
