**Times are changing, and so is Bali's tourism model.**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f407dfb77204d31628755b4581422ffb.jpg)

In the past, cash was almost the only payment method accepted in Bali, with only a few high-end establishments accepting credit cards, and even traveler's checks were sometimes required. Now, those days are history.

With the rapid development of global fintech, Bali is gradually implementing **digital payment and e-ticketing systems** at major tourist attractions, offering international travelers a more convenient, secure, and transparent spending experience.

---

## **Digital Payments Become the New Norm in Bali Tourism**

Travelers heading to Bali now need to prepare **international credit cards, debit cards, or travel debit cards** more than ever before. While cash still plays an important role in some areas, the island has clearly accelerated its digital transformation this year, especially at officially managed tourist sites.

This shift is driven by three main reasons.

First, Bali must align with the global trend of digital banking and cashless payments. Second, most international travelers already prefer card or mobile payments. Finally, digital systems significantly improve **financial transparency and tax management efficiency**.

---

## **Buleleng Regency in North Bali Speeds Up E-Ticketing Equipment Implementation**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d040c6ecb3da6b3c59def471fea05ead.jpg)

The Buleleng Tourism Office in North Bali recently announced that it is actively promoting **electronic ticketing and digital payment systems** at major tourist attractions.

I Gede Dody Sukma Oktiva Askara, the head of the tourism office, stated that the office currently only has 13 electronic ticketing devices, which are insufficient to fully support all officially taxed tourist sites.

Therefore, the tourism office has planned to apply for funding through the Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) program of the Bali Regional Development Bank (BPD) by **2026** to acquire more devices. Buleleng Regency actually needs about **40 devices** to fully cover 25 official tourist sites.

---

## **Tourist Season Arrives, Hotel Occupancy Rates in North Bali Soar**

Askara pointed out that North Bali is expected to see significant tourism growth by 2026. Currently, about **90% of popular hotels are confirmed to be fully booked during the holiday season**.

He added that Christmas and New Year have always been peak travel periods, with visitor numbers typically surging from the end of the year to the beginning of the new year, making the implementation of more efficient digital systems imperative.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b49e6706c63f705f973909cf267ea78a.jpg)

---

## **Why is North Bali Only Now Gaining International Attention?**

Buleleng Regency spans the northern and northwestern parts of Bali and has long remained relatively under the radar. Most international tourists still concentrate in the southern Badung Regency, such as around Kuta, Seminyak, and Ubud.

However, North Bali actually boasts rich and diverse tourism resources.

**Pemuteran Village** is renowned for its community involvement and marine conservation efforts and was recently **selected by the United Nations World Tourism Organization as one of the world's best tourism villages**.

Moreover, the **Munduk** region boasts pristine waterfalls, lush tropical rainforests, and refreshing mountain landscapes, making it an ideal destination for nature and eco-tourism enthusiasts.

---

## **Gateway to West Bali National Park**

Buleleng Regency also serves as the primary gateway to **West Bali National Park**. This national park is home to wild deer, monitor lizards, diverse marine life, and the critically endangered **Bali Starling**.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e540f3bf22505603cc755ea2e04cfbf1.jpg)

With comprehensive upgrades in digital payments and e-ticketing, North Bali is gradually transforming into a new destination that combines nature, ecology, and smart tourism.

**Do I still need to carry a lot of cash in Bali?**

Not necessarily. While cash is still used in some areas, popular tourist spots widely support card and digital payments.

**Is North Bali suitable for first-time visitors to Bali?**

Absolutely, especially for travelers who love nature, eco-tourism, and less crowded itineraries.

**What are the benefits of e-ticketing for travelers?**

It reduces queuing time, enhances ticket purchasing efficiency, and ensures price and transaction transparency.

**What payment methods are most convenient to bring?**

A combination of international credit cards and a moderate amount of Indonesian Rupiah cash is ideal.

**Why is North Bali particularly noteworthy in 2026?**

With fully booked accommodations, upgraded infrastructure, and comprehensive digital payment integration, the overall tourism quality has significantly improved.
