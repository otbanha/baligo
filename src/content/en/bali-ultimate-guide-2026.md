## 🛂 Must-Do Before Departure

### 1. Passport & Visa: Confirm 2 Months in Advance

Your passport must have **at least 6 months of validity remaining**. Indonesian customs are very strict about this requirement — even one day expired will get you denied.

**There are three visa options:**

- **Visa-Free Entry**: Taiwan passport holders can stay up to 30 days visa-free (but must complete an online declaration)
- **eVOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival)**: Apply online before departure, approval takes about 2-3 business days, allows a 30-day stay, **most recommended for first-timers**
- **VoA (Visa on Arrival)**: Apply upon arrival in Bali, may take 15 to 30 minutes in line

**Strongly recommend choosing eVOA** because:

- You can use the automated immigration gates upon arrival (no need to wait in line for 30 minutes)
- It’s only $5 cheaper, but the time saved is worth so much more
- Customs policies are getting stricter by 2026, so being prepared avoids any issues

### 2. Travel Insurance: Seriously, Don’t Skip It

Bali's medical standards are not as good as Taiwan's. Hospitalization there could cost USD$16,000-32,000. Additionally, Bali has many mosquito-borne diseases.

**Insurance Coverage Essentials:**

- Accidental injury (common for hiking and water activities)
- Medical expenses (including overseas hospitalization)
- Trip interruption/lost luggage

### 3. Flight Planning: Avoid Peak Periods to Save 1/3 of Your Budget

**When are prices highest?**

- Summer and winter breaks (July, August)
- Chinese New Year (late January-February)
- Christmas holidays (mid-December to early January)
- Qingming Festival & Labor Day holidays (early April)

**When are prices lower?**

- May-June (end of rainy season, before peak season)
- September-October (just after peak season)
- November (start of rainy season)

The best time to buy tickets is usually **3-6 weeks in advance**. Avoid the first flight of the day - flights departing between 2-5 PM are often 20-30% cheaper.

💡 **Common Misconception for Beginners**: "Budget airlines are cheaper." The truth is, budget airlines charge extra for luggage and seat selection, which might not make them cheaper than traditional airlines. Direct flights with Starlux, China Airlines, or EVA Air could be more cost-effective.

### 4. Bali Tourist Levy

Starting in 2024, every foreign tourist entering Bali will need to pay a tourist levy of IDR 150,000.

**How to Pay?**

- Simplest: Pay online before departure (balilevy.baliprov.go.id)
- Next Option: Pay directly at the airport upon arrival (dedicated counters available)
- Not Recommended: Wait until you're at a tourist spot to pay, as random checks may occur

Be sure to keep the receipt, as you may be asked to show it at tourist sites.

### 6. Packing Checklist: What to Bring and What to Skip

**Essentials (you’ll actually use these):**

- Sunscreen (SPF 50+ or higher)
- Mosquito repellent (lotion works better than spray)
- Light rain gear (frequent showers during the rainy season)
- Basic medication (cold medicine, stomach medicine, anti-diarrheal)
- Light jacket (air conditioning indoors can be chilly)
- Passport copy (in case of loss)

**Don't Bring (You Can Buy It Cheaply in Bali):**

- Face masks (cheap jelly masks are everywhere)
- Cosmetics (plenty of affordable brands)
- Daily necessities (hotels provide them, supermarkets have them too)

**Prohibited Items (Customs Will Confiscate or Fine):**

- Anything drug-related
- Meat products (even instant noodles from Taiwan need ingredient checks)
- Alcoholic beverages exceeding 5 liters
- Wildlife products

### 8. Voltage & Plugs: 220V, Bring the Right Adapter

Bali uses **220V voltage**, while Taiwan uses 110V.

**Smart Tips:**

- Bring a universal adapter (get one that supports multiple countries)

## 🗺️ Key Points for Itinerary Planning

### 9. How Many Days Are Ideal? First-Timers Should Aim for 5-7 Days

**Less than 3 days:** Only enough time to explore the south (Kuta, Seminyak), which is a pity
**5-7 days:** South + Ubud, experiencing 60% of Bali
**10+ days:** Enough to explore the east, west, and outer islands

For first-timers, I highly recommend **6 days and 5 nights** — it’s the perfect duration without feeling rushed.

## ✈️ Arriving in Bali

### 14. The Correct Process from Landing to Exiting the Airport

![14. The Correct Process from Landing to Exiting the Airport](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1778988530785-_5.png)

**Don’t wander around—follow these steps:**

1. **Follow the 'Arrival' signs** (not Departure)
2. **E-Visa holders use the automated gates** (fast track)
3. **Visa on Arrival holders queue at the Visa on Arrival counter** (apply for visa)
4. **Pass through immigration control** (get passport stamped)
5. **Collect luggage at the carousel** (look for your flight number)
6. **Fill out the AIAC customs declaration form** (online version only)
7. **Pass through customs** (usually just a quick glance)
8. **Pay the Bali tourism tax** (if not paid in advance)

**The entire process takes 30-60 minutes if there’s no queue**, so don’t stress.

### 16. Stomach Issues and Digestive Problems

https://youtu.be/GivvqCXp11A

**The most common issue in Bali is Bali Belly (traveler's diarrhea).**

**Prevention is better than cure**:

- Drink only bottled water (widely available in supermarkets and inexpensive)
- Avoid raw foods from street vendors (be cautious with fresh salads and fruit platters)
- Foods cooked at high temperatures are generally safe

**If you do get sick**:

- Bring anti-diarrheal medication (it's safer to bring it from home)
- Drink electrolyte beverages (available in Bali supermarkets)
- Rest for half a day; symptoms usually improve within a day

## 💡 Local Practical Knowledge

### 17. How to Convert Currency?

**Quick Conversion Formula**: Remove the last three zeros from the Indonesian Rupiah amount, then multiply by 2 to get an approximate value in New Taiwan Dollars.

**Example:**

- 100,000 IDR → 100 × 2 = NT$200
- 500,000 IDR → 500 × 2 = NT$1,000
- 1,000,000 IDR → 1000 × 2 = NT$2,000

💡 With this formula, you won't be shocked by 'OMG, why is it a 5-digit number?' when ordering food.

### 18. How to Tip?

[https://youtube.com/shorts/oBLpQ-MH4kY](https://youtube.com/shorts/oBLpQ-MH4kY)

**Indonesia doesn't have a 'mandatory tipping' culture, but 'gratitude tips' are very welcome.**

- **Hotel Bellboy**: USD 1-2 or 20,000 IDR
- **SPA Masseuse**: 10% of the bill (or directly give 20,000~50,000)
- **Restaurant Waiter**: 10% of the bill (usually, restaurants already add a 4-10% service charge, so this can be omitted)
- **Taxi Driver**: Round up (give change if you have it, otherwise don't worry)
- **Tour Guide/Private Driver**: 50,000~100,000 per day

**The simplest rule**: Always tip when someone helps you. It doesn't have to be much, but it's greatly appreciated.

### 23. Cultural Taboos and Respect

**Religious Sensitive Areas:**

- Wear a sarong when entering temples (usually provided for free)
- Do not touch or climb sacred statues
- Traditionally, women should not enter temples during menstruation (though most temples don't check)
- If you see a ceremony, take photos from a distance and don't disturb

**Daily Etiquette:**

- Eat and pass items with your right hand
- Don't point your feet at others
- Remove shoes when entering homes
- Show respect to elders

**Dress Code:**

- Bikinis are fine at the beach
- Cover shoulders and knees when entering temples or villages
- Polo shirts are acceptable for men in formal settings

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How many days are ideal for a first trip to Bali?

**A:** 5-7 days is the best choice for beginners. Less than 3 days will feel rushed and limit you to the southern areas; more than 10 days are needed to explore the entire island. **6 days and 5 nights** allows you to experience both the south (beaches, shopping) and the north (culture, mountains) comfortably without overexertion.

### Q: What’s the budget for a Bali free-and-easy trip?

👉 [**Detailed Budget Calculator**](/bali-budget-calculator/)

### Q: Can I use Taiwanese dollars in Bali?

**A:** No. Bali only uses **Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)**. Taiwanese dollars are not accepted locally, and it's difficult to find places to exchange them. We recommend bringing US dollars to Bali and exchanging them for Rupiah there.

### Q: Is Bali safe? Will I get robbed or scammed?

**A:** Overall, Bali is safer than many cities in Southeast Asia. Robberies are rare, and scams mainly target careless tourists (such as black market currency exchange or fake drivers). As long as you follow basic safety advice—avoid walking alone in secluded alleys at night, don't use black market currency exchange, and verify driver identities—you'll likely have no issues.

### Q: Can I visit Bali during the rainy season?

**A:** Absolutely. During the rainy season (November-March), it's usually **just 1-2 hours of afternoon showers**, and attractions remain open. In fact, the rainy season has advantages: fewer tourists, hotels are 40-50% cheaper, rice terraces are at their greenest, and Spas are more relaxing. Many seasoned travelers specifically choose the rainy season to visit. The downsides are unpredictable mountain weather and potential cancellations of some outdoor activities.

👉 [**Complete Rainy Season Guide**](/blog/2026-bali-indoor-activities/)

### Q: Do I need to apply for a visa before my first trip to Bali? Can I get a visa on arrival?

**A:** There are two options for Taiwanese passport holders:

1. **eVOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival)** (apply online 2-3 days before departure, **highly recommended**)
2. **Visa on Arrival** (apply at the airport, around USD$39)

The advantage of the eVOA is that you can go directly through the automated immigration gates upon arrival (no need to wait in the 45-minute manual queue).

Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia enjoy 30-day visa-free entry.

### Q: Should I tip in Bali? How?

**A:** Tipping is not mandatory in Indonesia, but it will make service staff very happy. Suggested tipping amounts:

- Porter: 20,000 IDR
- Spa massage: 50,000 IDR
- Restaurant: No need to tip if service charge is already included
- Taxi: Round up the fare
- Private driver/guide: 50,000\~100,000 IDR per day

The key is to "tip those who help you," and the amount doesn't need to be large—attitude matters most.

### Q: Where is the best area to stay for first-time visitors to Bali?

**A:** It depends on your travel style:

- **Kuta**: Lively, affordable, popular with backpackers
- **Seminyak**: Boutiques, upscale dining, Instagrammable spots
- **Canggu**: Cafes, youthful vibe, surfing
- **Ubud**: Culture, wellness, away from the crowds
- **Nusa Dua**: Five-star hotels, family-friendly, resorts
- **Jimbaran**: Sunsets, seafood, romantic dinners

For beginners, we recommend combining **Seminyak + Ubud** to experience both beach and culture.
