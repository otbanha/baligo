The Indonesian Immigration Office announced new regulations adjusting visa fees on **December 17, 2024**, covering all categories from short-term tourist visas to permanent residence visas. Fees will be calculated incrementally based on the length of stay. This article will help you understand how these changes may affect travelers planning to visit Bali and other parts of Indonesia.

![Important Updates on Bali Long-Term Stay/Tourist Visa Fees: ](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4665dca7b9f0b0d9da1e5f9572d12a25.png)

---

## 📌 **30-Day Tourist Visa Fees Remain Unchanged**

The most popular 30-day tourist visa (Visa on Arrival, VOA) and electronic tourist visa (eVoA) fees remain at **IDR 500,000** (approximately USD$16).

The VOA remains the top choice for short-term visitors, ideal for holidaymakers or foreign travelers on brief stays. Applying for the eVoA through the official website not only allows for advance visa processing but also grants access to the fast-track immigration system at Bali's Ngurah Rai International Airport, significantly reducing entry check times—perfect for **families, groups of friends**, or couples traveling together.

## ✅ **More Convenient Online Visa Extension Service**

Previously, extending a 30-day visa required three in-person visits to the immigration office:

1. Submit the application
2. Provide fingerprints
3. Collect the visa

Now, extensions can be **completed entirely online** for an additional fee of **IDR 500,000** (approximately USD$16), saving time and reducing travel-related hassles.

---

## 📈 **Other Visa Fee Adjustments: Challenges for Long-Term Stayers**

For expats or digital nomads hoping for extended stays, the visa fee adjustments are more significant. For example:

- **90-day visa**: Fee is **IDR 1,500,000** (approximately USD$48)
- **180-day Socio-Cultural Visa**: Fee is **IDR 2,000,000** (approximately USD$65)
- **KITAP (Permanent Residence Visa) fee increased from IDR 1,750,000** to **IDR 5,000,000**, nearly tripling the cost.

For foreign residents, these fee adjustments undoubtedly represent a major change and have sparked considerable discussion.

---

## 🛡️ **Other Reforms and Enforcement Measures by Immigration**

Indonesian immigration authorities have recently strengthened enforcement, particularly in Bali, conducting strict investigations into foreigners abusing the visa system.

- **From January to November 2024**: Bali's Ngurah Rai Immigration Office denied entry to **1,062 foreigners** and delayed the departure procedures of **407 travelers**.
- **178 individuals were deported**, and over **230 foreigners were detained**.

These measures by immigration authorities demonstrate their commitment not only to optimizing visa services but also to actively preventing system abuse to ensure fairness and order.

---

## 🔍 **Future Outlook: How Will the VFS Global Partnership Impact Visa Applications?**

In November 2024, Indonesian immigration authorities announced a partnership with global visa processing company VFS Global, planning a comprehensive update to the visa application system by 2025. According to Immigration Director Silmy Karim, this initiative aims to optimize visa application processes through digital solutions, further attracting international visitors to Indonesia.

Future changes may include adjustments to visa categories and new application models, with specific details to be announced later.

## 🎯 **What should travelers planning to visit Bali be aware of?**

- If your trip is for **short-term tourism (within 30 days)**, visa fees remain unchanged, and the application and renewal processes have become more convenient.
- If you plan to **stay for a medium to long term**, it's essential to understand the changes in visa fees and conditions in advance and budget accordingly.
- With the optimization of the visa system and strengthened enforcement, it is recommended that travelers always comply with local laws and regulations to avoid unnecessary trouble.

Whether for a short vacation or a long-term stay, Bali remains a charming travel destination. Start planning your trip now! 🌴✈️
