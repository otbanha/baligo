**Bottom Line**: This is a side-by-side comparison of 12 Bali accommodations filmed by travel blogger Camilla Johannesen, covering five areas—Canggu, Seminyak, Jimbaran, Ubud, and Nusa Penida—across three price tiers. For **luxury**, she recommends Nanuku House in Canggu (a four-bedroom private villa) and Ayana Resort in Jimbaran (with 14 pools); for **mid-range**, Ubud's Villa Yapoon (a six-bedroom villa surrounded by rice paddies); and for **budget-friendly**, Saka Boutique Hotel on Nusa Penida and Anjin Sapoy in Canggu. Simply match your budget and preferred area with the list below.

Bali is famous for its stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage, drawing countless visitors each year. This popular Indonesian destination offers a wide variety of accommodations, from luxurious resorts to affordable inns, ensuring that whether you seek opulence or simplicity, you'll find the perfect place to stay.

In the YouTube video below, travel blogger Camilla Johannesen takes viewers on a tour of the best accommodation options in Bali. She showcases everything from luxury resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses, catering to all travel preferences and budgets.

In the video, she introduces 12 recommended accommodations in areas like Canggu, Ubud, Jimbaran, and Nusa Penida, showcasing options from luxury resorts to affordable homestays, with something for every travel style and budget:

For luxury stays, Camilla visits the stunning Nanuku House, a four-bedroom villa in Canggu featuring a massive swimming pool, an outdoor kitchen, and full-time staff.

She also highlights the Ayana Resort, which spans 220 acres of lush tropical gardens and boasts an unforgettable 14 pools. We have a dedicated guide for Ayana Resort: [【Bali Ayana】Ayana Bali Accommodation Guide》](/en/blog/ayana-resort-bali-guide/)

In Ubud, Camilla stays at Villa Yapoon, a six-bedroom villa surrounded by rice paddies, offering a serene and tranquil experience. She also visits the beautiful Bhumian Ri Retreat Center, a perfect sanctuary for yoga retreats and meditation.

For mid-range options, she showcases Charming Sea Lovely on Nusa Penida, offering stunning volcano views, and Vince Sky Hotel in Seminyak, which boasts a prime location with a rooftop terrace and hot tub.

Additionally, Camilla also explored budget-friendly accommodation options, such as Anjin Sapoy, a seaside hotel in Canggu, and Evitel Hotel in Ubud, which boasts stunning rooftop views. On Nusa Penida, she stayed at Saka Boutique Hotel and La Mogi Cottage, offering comfortable rooms at surprisingly affordable prices.

Throughout her journey, Camilla detailed the features, facilities, and surroundings of each accommodation, helping viewers make informed choices based on their preferences and budgets.
