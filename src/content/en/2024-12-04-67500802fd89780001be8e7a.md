Bali is famous for its tropical climate and year-round sunshine, but with the arrival of the rainy season each year, the island enters a distinctly different seasonal experience. If you're planning to visit Bali during the rainy season, the following tips will help you prepare ahead of time and ensure a seamless journey.

## Bali Rainy Season: Timing and Weather Characteristics

Bali's rainy season typically begins in late October and lasts until late March or early April. During this period, rainfall is frequent and concentrated, often occurring as short but intense thunderstorms in the early morning or afternoon. Currently, NOAA (CPC) predicts a 71% chance of La Niña conditions between October and December 2025, which could lead to longer and heavier rainfall this rainy season.

Normally, Bali experiences rainfall for about 1 to 2 hours daily, but this year, rain may persist from daytime into the evening. Therefore, travelers should pay close attention to daily weather updates.

## Impacts of the Rainy Season on Bali

1. **Local Flooding and Road Issues**  
   Several major tourist areas in Bali, such as Seminyak, Kuta, Jimbaran, and Melasti Beach, have reported localized flooding.  
   Additionally, inland regions like Singaraja in the north have experienced severe flooding and landslides due to heavy rains. These disasters not only affect road accessibility but may also lead to temporary closures of attractions.
2. **Emergency Measures for Public Facilities**  
   The Bali government has deployed mobile water pumps and emergency teams to address flooding issues. For example, in the Badung region, four mobile pumps and 20 road maintenance personnel are working tirelessly to manage flood conditions. Travelers should stay updated on local announcements and follow emergency guidelines.

## Travel Tips for the Rainy Season

### **1. Pack Appropriate Gear**

- **Waterproof Raincoat and Dry Bag**  
  When traveling during the rainy season, it's advisable to carry a **plastic raincoat**, which is readily available in local markets at affordable prices. Additionally, bringing a **waterproof bag** is crucial to protect electronic devices like phones and cameras.
- **Spare Clothes and Shoes**  
  After prolonged outdoor activities, having a set of dry spare clothes can keep you comfortable. It's also recommended to choose slip-resistant and waterproof shoes to navigate slippery surfaces.

### **2. Smart Transportation and Itinerary Planning**

- **Avoid Rush Hour**  
  During the rainy season, flooded roads can lead to traffic congestion, especially during the peak tourist season from **mid-December to early January**. We suggest leaving at least twice as early as the arrival time shown on Google Maps.
- **Use Motorcycles Cautiously**  
  If you choose to ride a motorcycle yourself, avoid driving during **heavy rainfall** or when roads are **severely flooded**. When encountering standing water or strong currents, stopping to find a safe shelter is wise. Locals are usually very friendly to travelers seeking refuge.
- **Hire a Professional Driver**  
  If you're not familiar with local traffic conditions, opting for a private car service in Bali is recommended. Drivers have more experience navigating floods and road conditions, helping you save time and enhance safety.

### **3. Flexible Activity Choices**

Rainy days don't mean the end of your itinerary. Here are some activities suitable for the rainy season:

- **Adventure Activities**: Join an ATV off-road adventure or waterfall tour, which can be even more thrilling in the rain.
- **Indoor Cultural Experiences**: Explore the **Agung Rai Museum of Art (ARMA)** in Ubud or take a silver jewelry-making workshop in **Seminyak**.
- **Café Hopping**: Rainy days are perfect for leisurely exploring Bali's unique cafés, such as the hidden forest café in **Ubud**.

### **4. Stay Alert and Monitor the Weather**

- **Get Real-Time Weather Updates**  
  Follow the official Instagram account of the Bali Meteorological, Climatological, and Geophysical Agency (BMKG) [here](https://www.instagram.com/infobmkg/), where they post daily English weather forecasts for tourists.
- **Be Aware of Surrounding Safety**  
  During the rainy season, incidents like falling trees, landslides, and potholes become more frequent. Extra caution is needed when traveling in forests or mountainous areas.

## **The Importance of Travel Insurance**

Whether it's floods, landslides, or other sudden natural disasters, comprehensive travel insurance is a strong safety net for travelers in Bali. Choosing a plan that covers transportation delays, property loss, and medical support ensures you can handle any unexpected situations with ease.
