YouTuber Yo's recently shared his Bali travel experience, introducing his journey in a detailed way, allowing viewers to better feel the unique charm of Bali.

He mentioned that his previous impression of Bali was not good, as his brief stay in Kuta made him feel that the place was overly commercialized and lacked a leisurely atmosphere. However, after the pandemic, he decided to visit Bali again, putting aside his prejudices to fully experience everything the island has to offer.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1077d118b89b6bd830c9309d87b96fb4.png)

During this trip to Bali, Yo's stayed at a hotel in Ubud, surrounded by beautiful rice fields and away from the noisy main roads. The hotel offered a variety of services, including SPA, yoga, afternoon tea, and even motorcycle rentals, all of which Yo's found very satisfying.

In this trip, he was particularly looking forward to exploring Bali's natural attractions more deeply. He enjoys riding motorcycles, which allows him to freely visit various places, experience the changing scenery, and get close to the daily lives of locals. Along the way, the spectacular terraced rice fields left a deep impression on him. Yo's introduced these terraces as one of Bali's most representative landscapes, attracting countless tourists to take photos.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_495aa1662385c20f01156b9864b60899.png)

Yo's also shared his understanding of the Subak system, an agricultural technique behind the terraced fields. This system has been in place since the 9th century, ingeniously distributing water resources through channels to ensure each rice plant gets sufficient water. The success of this rice cultivation system is also closely tied to local community cooperation. This system was listed as a World Cultural Heritage in 2012.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_da9b776860b93747057974aec1cbedbe.png)

During his journey, Yo's also visited several waterfalls in the island's remote areas. He enjoys the tranquility and healing that nature brings in these less crowded places. While exploring the waterfalls, he occasionally encountered local vendors, such as sisters selling durian. He stopped by the roadside to taste the local durian, finding it much cheaper and more delicious than elsewhere.

Yo's trip to Bali not only allowed him to rediscover the beauty and charm of this land but also gave him a deeper understanding of the lives and history of the local residents. His video shows viewers a more authentic and richer Bali.
