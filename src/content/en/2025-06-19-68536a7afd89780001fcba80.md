The Indonesian Immigration Department recently announced major visa reforms aimed at combating illegal activities and streamlining the visa process. Complex visa categories will be consolidated into a clearer, unified framework.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_64d9be4decc22cde17ee06eb3a9c4b74.png)

### Details of Visa Category Consolidation

Visa Index numbers 133 to 110 have been merged, covering purposes such as tourism, business, medical treatment, and official visits. The new system makes it easier for applicants to choose the appropriate visa, reducing difficulties and errors in the application process.

---

## C7C Visa: A New Pathway for Cultural and Skill Exchange

Director General of Immigration Yuldi Yusman highlighted that the C7C visa is a groundbreaking policy allowing foreigners to showcase non-musical professional skills in Indonesia, including:

- Magic performances
- Fan meet-and-greets
- Cooking demonstrations by professional chefs

This visa encourages legitimate skill exchange activities, creating new opportunities for cultural interaction.

---

## Investor Visas E28F and E28G: Targeting the New Capital Nusantara

For the new capital being established in Kalimantan, the Indonesian government has introduced:

- **E28F Visa**: Specifically for foreign investors in the new capital

- **E28G Visa**: Designed for investors representing overseas patent companies establishing branches in Indonesia

---

This initiative paves the way for attracting global capital and accelerating infrastructure development.

## **Work Visas Streamlined from 31 Types to 6**

Under the old system, there were 20 types of work visas (E23B to E23W) for foreign professionals sponsored by companies, now consolidated into:

- **E23 Visa**: For foreign experts sponsored by companies

Additionally, two new visa types have been introduced for non-corporate sponsoring organizations:

---

- **E23U** and **E23V**: For educational institutions, foundations, and other non-profit organizations

## **eVoA Electronic Visa on Arrival: New Changes in Extended System**

---

### **What is eVoA?**

eVoA is an **online arrival visa application system** introduced by the Indonesian government, suitable for travelers from specific countries. There's no need to visit an embassy beforehand—simply apply and pay online to use it upon arrival in Indonesia.

- **Cost**: IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$32)
- **Validity**: 30 days
- **Extension**: Can be extended once for an additional 30 days (maximum stay of 60 days)

---

### **Who is eVoA Suitable For?**

The following travelers are most suited to use the eVoA for entering Indonesia:

- **Short-term tourists**
- **Business visitors**
- **Short-term social visits (e.g., family visits)**
- **Those who wish to extend their stay without needing long-term residency**

**Eligible Countries for eVoA** include Taiwan, Hong Kong, Japan, the United States, Australia, and most European countries (check the latest list on immigration.go.id for confirmation).

---

### **How to Apply for eVoA? Follow These Steps:**

1. **Visit the Official Indonesian Immigration eVoA Website** (<https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/>)
2. Select the application type (Electronic Arrival Visa)
3. Fill in personal details and passport information
4. Upload passport photo and personal photo
5. Pay IDR 500,000 using a credit card or international payment method
6. Receive the e-visa PDF file, which you can **present upon arrival**

✅ **Friendly Reminder**: Ensure your passport has at least 6 months of validity and that you hold a return or onward flight ticket.

---

### **eVoA Extension and Hybrid Application System Explained**

Starting May 29, 2025, the Indonesian Immigration Bureau will implement a new **Hybrid Application System**, which is crucial for travelers looking to extend their eVoA!

🔁 **Extension Application Process:**

1. **Submit an extension application online** (via <https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/>)
2. Schedule and visit the nearest Indonesian Immigration Office in person
3. Complete the following procedures:
4. - Verify original documents (e.g., passport, entry proof)
   - Take photos and fingerprint scans (biometrics)
   - Some cases may require an interview
5. Upon approval, you will receive **an additional 30-day stay permit**

---

### **Why the Shift to a Hybrid Application System?**

The Indonesian Immigration Bureau noted that the previous online-only visa extension process led to some foreigners **abusing residency rules or working illegally**. To enhance control quality and policy transparency, the current system combines online and in-person procedures to increase visa integrity and protect local order.

---

### **Practical Advice: Apply for Visa Extensions Early!**

Since the hybrid application requires an in-person visit, travelers needing extensions are advised to apply **7–10 days** in advance to avoid overstaying, fines, or deportation.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

### **Do I Need to Reapply After the Indonesian Visa Number Merger?**

No. Existing valid visas remain effective, and future applicants will need to follow the new classification system.

### Where can I apply for a C7C visa?

You can apply through the official website of the Indonesian Immigration Office or at Indonesian overseas representative offices.

### Can I extend my eVoA online?

Yes, but you must visit the immigration office in person to complete the verification process as per the new policy.

### Has it become more difficult to apply for a work visa after the merger?

No. The new system simplifies the process, making it easier to apply with clearer information.

### What qualifications are needed for investing in Indonesia's capital project?

You need to demonstrate investment intent and relevance to the project, and apply for the corresponding E28F or E28G visa.
