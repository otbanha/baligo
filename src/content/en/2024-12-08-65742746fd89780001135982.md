According to a December 8 report from the Central News Agency, Indonesia is considering implementing a visa-free policy for visitors from 20 countries, including Taiwan.

![赴印尼/峇里島旅遊將享有免簽待遇](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_003229f502db910ed37023c9fd244dfb.png)

Traveling to Indonesia/Bali Will Soon Be Visa-Free

Currently, only citizens of other ASEAN member states can enter Indonesia without a visa. The Indonesian government is actively promoting inbound tourism and is therefore considering expanding the visa-free policy. Sandiaga Uno, Minister of Tourism and Creative Economy, stated in an interview with CNN Indonesia that the list of countries under consideration is based on the number of visits by their citizens to Indonesia.

He mentioned the United States, Japan, China (including Hong Kong), India, the United Arab Emirates, several European countries, and Taiwan. He said this policy will encourage high-spending and high-quality travelers. The government may discuss the visa-free policy next month while also considering whether Indonesian travelers can receive similar treatment in other countries.

Under current regulations, visitors from Taiwan or Hong Kong can apply for a visa on arrival or pay for an electronic visa on arrival (e-VOA), costing approximately NT$1,000 (Rp. 500,000). The document is valid for 30 days and can be extended once.
