**Bottom Line**: Ayana Bali spans 90 hectares and is divided into **4 hotel zones with different luxury levels** (Ayana Resort, RIMBA, Villas, etc.), featuring **19 restaurants and bars, 14 pools, an 18-hole golf course**, and the iconic **Rock Bar, which requires advance booking** to secure the best sunset seats. Plan for 2-3 days to fully enjoy the diverse facilities within the same complex.

Bali's Ayana Bali is a sprawling resort complex beloved by travelers, covering 90 hectares. On this vast property, Ayana Bali offers a rich array of facilities and activities for an unforgettable getaway. The entire complex includes 19 restaurants and bars, 14 different pools (check out all pool images on the [official website](https://www.ayana.com/bali/facilities/)), an 18-hole golf course, a kids' club, sunset yoga, the famous Rock Bar, a private beach, and more—all set against unparalleled ocean views.

https://youtube.com/shorts/twUxgOqdq3E

Within the Ayana Bali complex, there are four distinct hotel zones, each representing a different level of luxury. Think of them as four separate five-star resorts with varying price points and styles, spread across different parts of the Ayana Bali grounds. They are:

- **Ayana Resort**: The heart of Ayana, offering comfortable rooms and suites along with numerous facilities and activities.
- **Ayana Villas**: An excellent choice for those seeking more privacy and luxury, with private villas for a serene and exclusive experience.
- **Ayana Segara**: The newest addition to Ayana, offering a closer connection to the sea for your stay.
- **Rimba by Ayana**: Another standout option under the Ayana umbrella, with diverse accommodations and amenities. Though not beachfront, it still delivers a luxurious resort experience.

No matter which hotel zone you choose, you'll have access to all facilities and activities across the entire Ayana Bali resort, ensuring an unparalleled vacation. Ayana Bali is renowned not only for its luxurious accommodations and refined amenities but also for its stunning coastline and exceptional service.

Let's start with an introduction to Ayana Resort.

## Ayana Resort

![Ayana Resort Ocean View Suite](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_254ad6da007ff427eb99377385b6f4c9.jpg)

Ayana Resort Ocean View Suite

Ayana Resort is the largest resort within Ayana Bali. It boasts over 500 rooms and suites, located in the prime area of the complex with the most convenient access. Room and suite sizes range from 50 to 200 square meters, accommodating 2 to 6 guests.

The rooms and suites at Ayana Resort are decorated in traditional Balinese style, spacious and comfortable. Each room is equipped with modern amenities, including LCD TV, free Wi-Fi, and a minibar. Some suites also feature a private pool, balcony, or terrace.

Ayana Resort offers a wide range of public facilities, including 9 restaurants, 4 swimming pools, a spa, fitness center, yoga pavilion, and kids' club. It is a mid-range option among the four accommodation choices.

RIMBA by Ayana

### Bonus Highlight 1: Rock Bar

The Rock Bar mentioned above is a world-renowned sunset bar offering food, cocktails, and entertainment. Perched on Jimbaran Beach, it's part of Ayana Resort. Its name comes from the fact that it's suspended on a modest rocky cliff. Rock Bar offers unparalleled views.

![Rock Bar](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e33cd71a7fbf9ba40bb409f8a9b799d0.jpg)

Rock Bar is open to the public. All guests can enter for free, but non-Ayana guests cannot reserve tables and must queue on-site—arriving too late may mean no seats available!

Here are some tips for booking Rock Bar:

- Non-guests are advised to arrive at the venue and queue by 3:30 or 4:00 PM.
- Guests can check available seating through the [Rock Bar official website](https://www.ayana.com/bali/dining/rock-bar-bali/) or the [TableCheck app](https://www.tablecheck.com/en/ayana-resort-and-spa-rockbar/reserve/message?menu_lists[]=63b515a4bbd3110045eca51e&utm_source=ayanawebsite).
- Non-guests have a minimum spend at Rock Bar.

A detailed guide to Rock Bar is here: [Jimbaran Rock Bar: How to Reserve? Complete Experience Guide!](/en/blog/bali-rock-bar-reservation-guide/)

### Bonus Highlight 5: SAKA Museum

Beyond its breathtaking natural beauty and top-tier service, the Ayana resort now boasts a cultural treasure! The newly opened SAKA Museum in 2024 is a museum dedicated to Bali's rich culture, offering visitors an in-depth look into the island's history, traditions, and art through various exhibits and interactive installations.

![加分亮點5: SAKA Museum](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3d01909c96a70fef9e0966181009f5d4.jpg)

**Museum Highlights**

- **Saka Calendar:** The SAKA Museum takes its name from Bali's ancient Saka calendar, which is deeply tied to many of the island's festivals and ceremonies.
- **Cultural Exhibits:** The museum houses a rich collection of Balinese artifacts, including sculptures, paintings, and textiles, offering a glimpse into the intricacy and uniqueness of Balinese traditional crafts.
- **Interactive Experiences:** Beyond static displays, the museum features numerous interactive installations, allowing visitors to personally experience Balinese traditions, such as making traditional instruments or learning Balinese dance.
- **Architectural Design:** The museum's architecture blends modern and traditional elements, creating a comfortable and artistic environment for exploration.

**Why Visit the SAKA Museum?**

- **Deep Dive into Balinese Culture:** If you're curious about Balinese culture, the SAKA Museum is an absolute must-visit.
- **Enrich Your Travel Experience:** Beyond soaking up the sun and hitting the beach, a visit to the museum offers a deeper understanding of this beautiful island.

The SAKA Museum is not only a new highlight of the Ayana Resort complex but also a shining gem of Balinese culture. If you're staying at Ayana, be sure to take some time to visit the SAKA Museum—you'll walk away with plenty of inspiration.

### Bonus Highlight 14: Cashless Payment Model

The entire AYANA Bali resort has now fully transitioned to a cashless payment system, offering travelers the primary benefits of 'security' and 'convenience.' Guests no longer need to carry large amounts of Indonesian rupiah while swimming or lounging on the beach, completely eliminating worries about lost wallets or damaged bills, while also avoiding potential scams at off-site currency exchange shops. Whether using credit cards, mobile payments, or charging expenses to their room number (Room Charge), this seamless payment process allows guests to immerse themselves in the vacation atmosphere without being interrupted by tedious cash counting.

Furthermore, the fully digital payment system significantly enhances the resort's operational efficiency and billing transparency. With contactless card swiping or QR code scanning, checkout at restaurants and shops becomes much faster, and guests can clearly track every expense through electronic invoices when checking out. In an era of heightened hygiene awareness, reducing physical currency contact adds an extra layer of health protection to the journey. This model not only aligns with modern digital travel trends but also makes the service at this top-tier resort feel more streamlined and contemporary.
