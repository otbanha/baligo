**Bottom line first**: Ayana Bali spans 90 hectares and is divided into **4 hotel zones with different luxury levels** (Ayana Resort, RIMBA, Villas, etc.), featuring **19 restaurants and bars, 14 pools, an 18-hole golf course**, and the iconic **Rock Bar requires advance booking** to secure the best sunset seats. It's ideal for a 2-3 day stay to fully enjoy the diverse facilities within the resort.

Ayana Bali in Bali is a sprawling resort complex beloved by travelers, covering 90 hectares. Across this vast property, Ayana Bali offers a rich array of facilities and activities for an unforgettable vacation. The resort boasts 19 restaurants and bars, 14 different pools (check out all pool images on the [official website](https://www.ayana.com/bali/facilities/)), an 18-hole golf course, a kids' club, sunset yoga, the famous Rock Bar, a private beach, and more—all set against unparalleled ocean views!

https://youtube.com/shorts/twUxgOqdq3E

Within the Ayana Bali complex, there are four distinct hotel zones, each representing a different level of luxury. Think of them as four five-star resorts with varying price points and styles, spread across different parts of the Ayana Bali property. They are:

- **Ayana Resort**: The heart of Ayana, offering comfortable rooms and suites along with extensive facilities and activities.
- **Ayana Villas**: For those seeking more privacy and luxury, Ayana Villas is an excellent choice, featuring private villas for a serene and exclusive experience.
- **Ayana Segara**: The newest addition to the Ayana resort, offering a closer connection to the sea.
- **Rimba by Ayana**: Another outstanding option under the Ayana umbrella, with diverse accommodations and amenities. Though not beachfront, it still delivers a luxurious resort experience.

No matter which hotel zone you choose, you'll have access to all facilities and activities across the entire Ayana Bali resort, ensuring an unparalleled vacation. Ayana Bali is renowned not only for its luxurious accommodations and refined amenities but also for its stunning coastline and exceptional service.

Let's start with an introduction to Ayana Resort.

## Ayana Resort

![Ayana Resort Ocean View Suite](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_254ad6da007ff427eb99377385b6f4c9.jpg)

Ayana Resort Ocean View Suite

Ayana Resort is the largest resort within Ayana Bali. It features over 500 rooms and suites, located in the prime area of the complex with the most convenient access. Room and suite sizes range from 50 to 200 square meters, accommodating 2 to 6 guests.

The rooms and suites at Ayana Resort are decorated in traditional Balinese style, spacious and comfortable. Each room is equipped with modern amenities, including LCD TV, free Wi-Fi, and a minibar. Some suites also feature a private pool, balcony, or terrace.

Ayana Resort offers a wide range of public facilities, including 9 restaurants, 4 swimming pools, a spa, a fitness center, a yoga pavilion, and a kids' club. It is a mid-range option among the four accommodation choices.

## RIMBA by Ayana

![RIMBA by Ayana](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1ba6522188802a2fe5be4dda97ad3939.jpg)

RIMBA by Ayana

Rimba means 'forest' in Indonesian, and the Rimba hotel area is not by the sea but nestled within a lush tropical rainforest environment.

RIMBA by AYANA is the best value accommodation within Ayana Bali, as guests can share all of Ayana Bali's facilities. This includes the resort's 19 restaurants and bars, 14 pools, private beach, Rock Bar, kids' club/playground, and more. This means RIMBA guests can easily explore everything Ayana Resort has to offer while enjoying a more affordable stay.

Although the RIMBA hotel area is more secluded, its luxurious accommodations are in no way inferior. It's especially great for families with kids, and with access to all of Ayana Resort's shared facilities, it's a highly attractive option. Whether you're visiting Bali for a honeymoon, a romantic getaway, or simply seeking great value, RIMBA by AYANA, with its lowest room rates, is worth considering.

## **Bonus Highlights:**

At Ayana Resort in Bali, the experience goes beyond five-star accommodations—it's a sensory luxury journey. The following 14 bonus highlights elevate your stay even further:

### Bonus Highlight 1: Rock Bar

The Rock Bar mentioned above is a world-renowned sunset bar offering food, cocktails, and entertainment. Located on Jimbaran Beach, it's part of the Ayana Resort. Its name comes from the fact that it's perched on a low rock cliff. The Rock Bar offers unparalleled views.

![Rock Bar](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e33cd71a7fbf9ba40bb409f8a9b799d0.jpg)

The Rock Bar is open to the public. All guests can enter for free, but non-Ayana guests cannot reserve tables and must queue on-site—arriving too late might mean no seats!

Here are some tips for booking the Rock Bar:

- Non-guests are advised to arrive at the venue and queue by 3:30 or 4:00 PM.
- Guests can check available seating via the [Rock Bar official website](https://www.ayana.com/bali/dining/rock-bar-bali/) or the [TableCheck app](https://www.tablecheck.com/en/ayana-resort-and-spa-rockbar/reserve/message?menu_lists[]=63b515a4bbd3110045eca51e&utm_source=ayanawebsite).
- Non-guests have a minimum spend at Rock Bar.

For a detailed guide on Rock Bar, check out: [Jimbaran Rock Bar: How to Book? Complete Experience Guide!](/en/blog/bali-rock-bar-reservation-guide/)

### Bonus Highlight 5: SAKA Museum

Beyond its stunning natural scenery and top-tier service, the Ayana resort complex now boasts a cultural treasure! The newly opened SAKA Museum in 2024 is a museum dedicated to Bali's rich culture, offering visitors an in-depth look into the island's history, traditions, and art through various exhibits and interactive installations.

![加分亮點5: SAKA Museum](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3d01909c96a70fef9e0966181009f5d4.jpg)

**Museum Highlights**

- **Saka Calendar:** The SAKA Museum takes its name from Bali's ancient Saka calendar, which is closely tied to many of the island's festivals and ceremonies.
- **Cultural Exhibits:** The museum houses a rich collection of Balinese artifacts, including sculptures, paintings, and traditional attire, offering a glimpse into the exquisite craftsmanship and uniqueness of Balinese traditions.
- **Interactive Experiences:** Beyond static displays, the museum features numerous interactive installations that allow visitors to personally experience Balinese culture, such as making traditional instruments or learning Balinese dance.
- **Architectural Design:** The museum's architecture blends modern and traditional elements, creating a comfortable and artistic environment for exploration.

**Why Visit the SAKA Museum?**

- **Dive into Balinese Culture:** If you're curious about Balinese culture, the SAKA Museum is an absolute must-visit.
- **Enrich Your Travel Experience:** Beyond soaking up the sun and hitting the beach, a visit to the museum offers a deeper understanding of this beautiful island.

The SAKA Museum is not only a new highlight of the AYANA resort complex but also a true gem of Balinese culture. If you're staying at Ayana, be sure to carve out some time to explore it—you'll leave with a wealth of new insights.

### Bonus Highlight 6: Kisik Seafood and Grill

![加分亮點6：Kisik Seafood and Grill](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9ab04c004d2ebae2b2b108dba26c53fc.jpg)

The grilling here is perfectly timed—never over-charred—preserving the natural flavors of the ingredients so every bite bursts with the sweetness of the sea.

One of the biggest highlights of **Kisik Seafood and Grill** is its unbeatable sunset view, identical to the one at Rock Bar. Perched on a cliffside, the restaurant isn't directly on the beach, but its floor is covered in fine sand, creating a strong resort vibe that makes you feel like you're dining right by the shore. As the sun sets, golden light spills across the ocean, pairing beautifully with exquisite cuisine and attentive service for an unforgettable dining experience.

What's more, the restaurant is just steps from **Rock Bar**, the famous cliffside bar that's a must-visit for many travelers. If time allows, grab a drink at **Rock Bar** first to soak in the stunning ocean views, then head over to **Kisik Seafood and Grill** for a lavish seafood feast—the perfect way to cap off your Bali adventure!

### Bonus Highlight 14: Cashless Payment System

The entire Ayana Bali resort has now fully transitioned to a cashless payment system, offering travelers the primary benefits of 'security' and 'convenience.' Guests no longer need to carry large amounts of Indonesian rupiah while swimming or lounging on the beach, eliminating worries about lost wallets or damaged bills, while also avoiding potential scams at off-site currency exchange shops. Whether using credit cards, mobile payments, or charging expenses to their room (Room Charge), this seamless payment process allows guests to fully immerse themselves in the vacation atmosphere without interruptions from tedious cash counting.

Furthermore, the fully digital payment system significantly enhances the resort's operational efficiency and billing transparency. With contactless card swiping or QR code scanning, checkout at restaurants and shops becomes much faster, and guests can clearly track every expense through electronic invoices when checking out. In an era of heightened hygiene awareness, reducing physical currency contact adds an extra layer of health protection to the journey. This model not only aligns with modern digital travel trends but also makes the service at this top-tier resort feel more streamlined and contemporary.
