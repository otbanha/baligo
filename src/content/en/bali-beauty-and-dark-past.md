**Key Highlights**: This video shares YouTuber Yo's reflections on revisiting Bali—staying at a **hotel surrounded by rice paddies in Ubud** (with SPA, yoga, afternoon tea, and scooter rental services) offers an escape from Kuta's commercial hustle; riding a scooter to explore the Tegallalang Rice Terraces provides an up-close look at the **Subak irrigation system**, a tradition dating back to the 9th century and recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2012. The journey also captures a tragic local story learned during a traffic jam on the way to Kuta.

YouTuber Yo recently shared his travel experience in Bali, using a delicate approach to recount his journey, helping viewers better appreciate the island's unique allure.

He mentioned that his previous impression of Bali was not favorable, as a brief stay in Kuta had left him feeling the area was overly commercialized and lacking a relaxed vibe. However, after the pandemic, he decided to revisit Bali, setting aside his preconceptions to fully experience everything the island has to offer.

![重新認識峇里島：絕美的度假勝地，曾經卻發生一場惡夢](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1077d118b89b6bd830c9309d87b96fb4.png)

During this trip to Bali, Yo stayed at a hotel in Ubud, surrounded by beautiful rice paddies and far from the noisy main roads. The hotel offered a rich array of services, including SPA, yoga, afternoon tea, and even scooter rentals, all of which left Yo highly satisfied.

On this trip, he was especially eager to explore Bali's natural attractions more deeply. He enjoyed riding a scooter, which gave him the freedom to visit various spots, take in the changing scenery, and get close to the daily lives of locals. Along the way, the stunning rice terraces left a deep impression on him. Yo noted that these terraces are one of Bali's most iconic landscapes, drawing countless tourists to snap photos.

![重新認識峇里島：絕美的度假勝地，曾經卻發生一場惡夢](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_495aa1662385c20f01156b9864b60899.png)

Yo also shared his understanding of the farming technique behind the terraces—the Subak system. This system, in practice since the 9th century, uses intricate channels to distribute water across different fields, ensuring each rice plant gets enough water. The success of this rice cultivation system is also closely tied to local community cooperation. It was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2012.

![重新認識峇里島：絕美的度假勝地，曾經卻發生一場惡夢](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_da9b776860b93747057974aec1cbedbe.png)

During his journey, Yo also visited several waterfalls in the outlying areas. He enjoyed the peace and healing nature offered by these less crowded spots. While exploring the waterfalls, he occasionally encountered local vendors, like sisters selling durian. He stopped by the roadside to taste the local durian, finding it much cheaper and more delicious than elsewhere.

Yo's recent trip to Bali not only gave him a fresh appreciation for the island's beauty and charm, but also deepened his understanding of the local people's lives and history. His video offers viewers a more authentic and richly layered portrait of Bali.
