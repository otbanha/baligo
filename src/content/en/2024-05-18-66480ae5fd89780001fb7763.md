Recently, a group member named Mike Chiu encountered some issues while applying for Indonesia's electronic visa (e-VOA). He sought advice in our Facebook group. Mike mentioned that he correctly entered his place of birth as 'TAIWAN' on the application form and had double-checked the accuracy. However, upon receiving the final electronic visa, he noticed that his place of birth was displayed as 'Talwan', with the 'i' mistakenly changed to an 'l'. Unsure if this was acceptable, Mike asked whether he needed to re-edit the application or how he should proceed.

In response, another traveler, Wei Chih Wen, advised Mike to first note down the electronic visa number, then write to the relevant department explaining the need to correct the place of birth error and patiently await a response. Wei Chih Wen also mentioned that he had encountered a similar situation before and successfully received a corrected response.

Ultimately, Mike Chiu followed the advice and quickly received an official response, resolving the issue smoothly. This experience serves as a reminder that if you encounter similar problems during the visa application process, you can contact the official authorities for corrections and confirmations to avoid affecting your travel plans.

## What Should You Do Specifically?

Seeking assistance from the Indonesian Immigration Office is the only solution. It is recommended that you contact them during working hours via the [Indonesian Immigration Office website](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/) using the Contact Us feature (located at the top right corner of the homepage).
