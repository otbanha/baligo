# Topics of Interest for Bali Tourists + News Roundup (July 19, 2026)

Below is a summary of recent travel updates most relevant to visitors, helping those planning a trip to Bali quickly grasp the latest developments.

## ✈️ Ngurah Rai Airport Continues to Expand International Direct Routes, Enhancing Traveler Convenience

Airport operator InJourney Airports has announced ongoing efforts to add more international direct routes and improve terminal facilities to meet growing global travel demand. For travelers, this means more direct flight options in the future, which also helps ease congestion during peak hours.

## ⚠️ Crocodile Sighting Reported in Bali Strait, Officials Warn Against Approaching Suspicious Waters

Indonesian media reports indicate that crocodile sightings have recently been reported again in the Bali Strait near Komodo Island. Authorities have reminded locals and tourists to avoid approaching river mouths, mangrove areas, and coastal waters where wildlife activity may occur. Do not enter the water or get close for photos out of curiosity. For water activities, always follow operator announcements and on-site safety instructions.

## 📊 Summer Peak Season Occupancy Expected to Rise 10–12%, Popular Accommodations Still Recommended to Book Early

The Indonesian Hotel and Restaurant Association (PHRI) estimates that hotel occupancy in Bali during this summer peak season (June–July) will increase by about 10–12% compared to regular periods. While some areas still have vacancies, popular beach resorts and family-friendly hotels remain in high demand. Travelers with upcoming plans are advised to complete their bookings as early as possible.
