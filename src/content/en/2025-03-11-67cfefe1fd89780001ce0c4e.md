According to statistics from Bali's International Airport Immigration Office, the number of international arrivals and departures in January 2025 increased by 22% compared to the same period in 2024. Alexander Maxwell, Head of Information and Communication Technology at the Immigration Office, revealed that a total of 611,603 people arrived in Bali in January 2025, while 632,833 departed. Typically, January sees more departures than arrivals as tourists return home after Christmas and New Year holidays.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf32e66b76078f0a296d4db9e4cd0f1b.png)

Maxwell stated that foreign tourists remain the primary source of arrivals, reaching 540,122, a 27% year-on-year increase. The number of Indonesian citizens arriving also grew by 30%, totaling 53,490, while crew members accounted for 17,991, a 20% increase.

In January 2025, Bali's main international tourist source countries remained similar to previous years, with the top 10 being:

1. Australia (139,577 visitors)
2. China (61,984 visitors, including Hong Kong and Taiwan)
3. India (36,514 visitors)
4. South Korea (35,172 visitors)
5. Russia (26,794 visitors)
6. Malaysia (21,771 visitors)
7. United Kingdom (20,569 visitors)
8. United States (18,627 visitors)
9. Singapore (15,942 visitors)
10. Japan (12,112 visitors)

Bali Governor Wayan Koster noted that tourism significantly impacts Indonesia's overall economy, even during the off-season. In 2024, Bali contributed IDR 107 trillion to the national tourism sector, accounting for 44% of foreign exchange earnings. Koster emphasized that Bali's economy heavily relies on tourism and stressed the need for economic restructuring to balance tourism and non-tourism sectors.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c921dc4310098a99930838894970f765.png)

Tourism accounts for 66% of Bali's revenue. While Koster expressed optimism about the increase in international tourists over the past 12 months, he voiced concerns about the slow growth in domestic tourism demand. In 2024, Bali welcomed only 9.4 million domestic tourists, far below the pre-pandemic level of over 10.5 million annually.

Koster analyzed that the slow recovery of domestic tourism is due to Indonesia's incomplete economic recovery and high domestic airfare prices, which not only affect Indonesian citizens but also discourage international tourists from exploring other parts of the country. High domestic flight costs pose a significant barrier for budget-conscious travelers.

Koster分析，國內旅遊復甦緩慢的原因包括國家經濟未完全恢復及國內機票價格高昂，這不僅影響印尼民眾，也阻礙國際遊客探索印尼其他地區的意願。高額的國內航班成本對追求性價比的遊客來說是一大障礙。
