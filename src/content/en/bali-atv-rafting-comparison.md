When people think of Bali, most imagine beaches, temples or spas, but this time my three friends and I decided to try something different. After much comparison, we chose the 'ATV Ride + Ayung River Rafting' combo package, which turned out to be the most thrilling and unforgettable day of our Bali trip!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_41c737cdbc148a72f531b97d2acd93c1.jpg)

## Itinerary Overview: The Perfect Land & Water Adventure

This well-designed day tour combines two completely different adventure experiences - water rafting and land ATV riding. The morning features 2 hours of Ayung River rafting, followed by an Indonesian buffet lunch in the forest, and then 1.5 hours of ATV riding in the afternoon. The whole itinerary runs from 8am to 5pm, making for a truly fulfilling day.

## Morning Experience: Exploring Ayung River Rafting

### Safety Briefing & Equipment Preparation

At 8:30am sharp, the operator's shuttle bus arrived on time. I must commend their transportation arrangement - air-conditioned private cars with comfortable seats made the 30-minute ride completely fatigue-free. Upon arrival at the rafting starting point, our professional guide first gave a detailed safety briefing, including how to properly wear life jackets, paddling techniques, and emergency response methods.

All safety equipment was complete and in good condition, including waterproof helmets, life jackets and paddles. The guide also carefully checked everyone's equipment to ensure proper wear, which made us feel very secure.

### Rafting Process: A Water Adventure Full of Surprises

The Ayung River rafting route is ingeniously designed, lasting about 2 hours with moderate difficulty. It features thrilling rapids as well as calm scenic stretches. The biggest surprise was discovering several hidden waterfalls along the way! These waterfalls are nestled in dense tropical rainforests, accessible only by bamboo raft, making them truly hidden gems that most tourists never get to see.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_78f83f6f2bd34de9e53e3f59a235f6ab.jpg)

One waterfall was particularly spectacular, about 15 meters high, with water cascading straight down the cliff face. In the sunlight, we could even see a rainbow. The guide stopped here for about 10 minutes, allowing us to take photos. My friend Sarah was initially nervous, but she got so excited upon seeing these beautiful sights, exclaiming in delight throughout the journey.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_12e5e214ed2fc2788efeef3a2bd801a8.jpg)

The most thrilling part of the rafting was passing through several Class III rapids. The rubber raft sped through the turbulent waters, splashing everywhere, and we all got soaked but had an amazing time. Kevin even joked that it was more exciting than water rides at amusement parks!

## Lunchtime: A Feast in the Forest

After the rafting, we arrived at the dining area deep in the forest. The setting was truly stunning, surrounded by lush greenery, with fresh air and the chirping of birds. The open-air design made it feel like we were dining in the embrace of nature.

The buffet was quite extensive, featuring classic Indonesian fried rice (nasi goreng), satay skewers, Indonesian chicken curry, various vegetable dishes, and fresh tropical fruits. The food quality was surprisingly good, especially the satay—the meat was tender and the peanut sauce was incredibly flavorful. All four of us were thoroughly satisfied, replenishing our energy for the afternoon ATV adventure.

Before the meal, there were shower facilities available, which was a thoughtful touch, allowing us to change into clean clothes and prepare for the afternoon activities.

## Afternoon Challenge: ATV Off-Road Adventure in the Countryside

### ATV Safety Training and Test Drive

At 1:30 PM, we arrived at the ATV activity base. Just like in the morning, the professional coach first gave a detailed safety briefing and operational instructions. Operating the ATV isn't difficult—mainly controlling the throttle and brakes—but the coach still carefully explained turning techniques and precautions for uphill and downhill riding.

Everyone gets a chance to test drive for a few minutes at the practice area to ensure familiarity with the controls before officially setting off. The safety equipment, including helmets and safety boots, is of excellent quality.

### Countryside Adventure: Exploring Bali's Most Beautiful Rural Areas

The ATV route mainly covers the Bongkasa Pertiwi and Taman areas, which preserve the most authentic rural scenery of Bali. Our route includes rice field paths, bamboo forest tunnels, traditional villages, and hill trails, each section offering different landscapes and challenges.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cb111d28d880b9d215e5593dce74447a.jpg)

The most impressive part was crossing the terraced rice fields, with emerald green rice stalks swaying in the wind and distant mountains forming a picturesque rural scene. We also passed through several traditional Balinese villages, witnessing the daily lives of locals. The children waved excitedly at us, creating a very heartwarming atmosphere.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_284e63ef9aa86b0f3931cc37de500341.jpg)

The most thrilling part of the route was the hill trail, with some steep slopes and bumpy stone paths. My friend Amy was a bit nervous at first but quickly adapted and even started enjoying the sense of achievement from conquering difficult terrain. The funniest moment was when Kevin almost flipped his ATV at a muddy turn, but he managed to stabilize it quickly, and we all burst into laughter at his mishap.

### Natural Scenery: Discovering Another Side of Bali

The ATV ride allowed us to venture deep into Bali's inland areas, revealing landscapes completely different from the coast. Here, we encountered dense tropical rainforests, clear streams, and small waterfalls hidden in the valleys. The ecosystem along the way was rich, with sightings of various tropical birds and butterflies, and even a few wild monkeys.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_311b1eb06b6d96b8434a358a1fa59c69.jpg)

The most memorable part was passing by a small stream where the coach let us stop to rest and take photos. The water was crystal clear, surrounded by pristine tropical vegetation with no artificial traces. This untouched beauty was truly refreshing and gave us a whole new perspective on Bali.

## Practical Information and Tips

### Transportation Arrangements

If you choose a package that includes transportation, I highly recommend it. The driver was punctual and professional, eliminating any worries about transportation. It took about 30 minutes to reach our destination from our hotel in Ubud.

### Essential Packing List

Based on our experience, here are the must-have items:

- Swimwear and change of clothes (at least two sets)
- Waterproof bag (to protect your phone and camera)
- Sunscreen (SPF 50 or higher)
- Towel
- Water shoes or sandals (ones that can get wet)
- Personal medication
- Cash (for souvenir photos and small expenses)

- Download WhatsApp: This is crucial! Local operators primarily use WhatsApp for communication, including confirming pickup times and locations.
- Age Restrictions: ATV activities require participants to be at least 12 years old and accompanied by an adult.
- Weather Conditions: During the rainy season, river currents can be stronger, so it's advisable to check the weather beforehand.
- Insurance: Basic insurance is included in the package.

### Cost Breakdown

The package we chose included all necessary items: professional guides, all safety equipment, buffet lunch, shower facilities, bottled water, and basic insurance. Additional costs mainly covered souvenir photos and tips for the guides.
