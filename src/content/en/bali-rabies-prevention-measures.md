The veterinary unit in Badung Regency, Bali recently reported several bite incidents caused by rabies-infected dogs. Earlier this month, one of our group members was bitten by a stray dog in the Kuta area, spending USD$5,806 on rabies vaccines. The incident was even featured on TV news. Please see [the report here](/en/blog/bali-rabies-vaccine-cost/).

## **South Kuta Area Designated as Enhanced Monitoring Zone**

Bali authorities have designated Nusa Dua, Benoa, Jimbaran, and parts of Canggu as 'Enhanced Animal Disease Control Zones'. These areas see high tourist traffic and increasing numbers of stray animals, necessitating enhanced public health control measures.

## **Vaccination Campaigns and Volunteer Participation**

Upon detection of rabies-positive cases, local authorities swiftly initiate mass vaccination programs. For instance, on July 10th, dogs around the Jimbaran Polytechnic Institute and Pratama Street area were vaccinated. Volunteer groups are also assisting in capturing, neutering, and vaccinating animals.

## **Preventive Measures for Travelers and Pet Owners**

Travelers to Bali and local pet owners should follow these basic rules: vaccinate pet dogs annually, don't let them roam alone, and avoid contact with unfamiliar or stray animals. If bitten or scratched, immediately wash the wound with warm water and soap for at least 10 minutes, and seek medical attention for vaccination as soon as possible. The National I.G.N.G. Ngoerah General Hospital in Denpasar offers subsidized vaccines, providing convenience for travelers.

## **Monkeys Can Also Be Virus Carriers**

At attractions like the Monkey Forest in Ubud or Uluwatu Temple, visitors often interact with monkeys. However, these wild animals may carry rabies, so it's advised not to feed, touch, or approach them. Keeping a safe distance from animals is an important step in preventing disease transmission.

## **Thanks to Expert Efforts, Bali Remains a Safe Travel Paradise**

Through the joint efforts of animal health departments and volunteers, rabies prevention in Bali's major resort areas has entered an active control phase. As long as basic safety recommendations are followed, travelers can still safely enjoy Bali's natural scenery and cultural charm.

## **Frequently Asked Questions FAQ**

**What should I do if bitten by a dog in Bali?**  
Immediately wash the wound with soap and warm water for 10-15 minutes, then go to the nearest medical center as soon as possible to receive a rabies vaccine.

**Can monkeys transmit rabies?**  
Yes. Monkeys can also be carriers of the virus, and keeping your distance is the best prevention method.

**Do dogs need to be vaccinated every year?**  
Yes. According to veterinary recommendations and local regulations, dogs should be vaccinated annually to ensure protection.

**Which areas in Bali are currently high-risk zones?**  
Southern Kuta areas, including Nusa Dua, Jimbaran, Benoa, and parts of Canggu, are all under enhanced epidemic control.

**Can I get vaccine cost reimbursement from travel insurance?**  
Some travel insurance plans cover rabies vaccine costs. It's recommended to confirm your policy details before departure.

**What if I don't have enough money to pay for the rabies vaccine?**  
Most large international hospitals in Bali (such as **BIMC**, **Siloam**) and clinics specializing in foreigner services (like **Unicare**, **Nusa Medica**) accept international credit cards (Visa, Mastercard).  
Rabies vaccines usually require multiple doses (e.g., days 0, 3, 7, 14, 28). You **don't need to pay for all doses at once** - you can pay for just the first dose (day 0).

**How much does the first rabies shot cost?**  
For the first shot, you'll need to prepare around IDR 1,500,000 – 2,200,000 (approximately USD$48 - $71). The most expensive part is the Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG). If the doctor determines your wound is a Category 3 exposure (involving bleeding or deep bites), in addition to the vaccine, you'll need to receive the immunoglobulin injection. This medication is dosed based on body weight, and the total cost could reach IDR 10,000,000 – 25,000,000 (approximately USD$323 - $806 or more).
