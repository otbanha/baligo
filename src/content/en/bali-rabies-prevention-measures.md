The veterinary unit in Badung Regency, Bali recently reported several cases of bites from dogs infected with rabies; earlier this month, one of our group members was bitten by a stray dog in the Kuta area, spending USD$5,806 on rabies vaccinations. This incident was even featured on TV news. Please see [the report here](/blog/2025-07-04-68677128fd8978000174d48a/).

## **South Kuta Area Designated as Enhanced Monitoring Zone**

Bali authorities have designated Nusa Dua, Benoa, Jimbaran, and parts of Canggu as 'Enhanced Animal Disease Control Zones.' These areas see high tourist traffic and an increasing population of stray animals, necessitating improved public health control measures.

## **Vaccination Campaigns and Volunteer Involvement**

Once a rabies-positive case is detected, local authorities quickly launch large-scale vaccination efforts. For example, on July 10th, dogs around the Jimbaran Polytechnic Institute and the Pratama Street area were vaccinated. Additionally, volunteer groups are assisting with trapping, neutering, and vaccination efforts.

## **Preventive Measures for Travelers and Pet Owners**

Travelers to Bali or local pet owners should follow several basic rules: vaccinate pet dogs annually, do not let them roam alone, and avoid unfamiliar animals and stray dogs. If bitten or scratched, immediately wash the wound with warm water and soap for at least 10 minutes, and seek medical attention for vaccination as soon as possible. The National I.G.N.G. Ngoerah General Hospital in Denpasar offers subsidized vaccines, making it convenient for travelers.

## **Monkeys Can Also Be Virus Carriers**

At attractions like the Monkey Forest in Ubud or Uluwatu Temple, visitors often interact with monkeys. However, these wild animals may carry rabies virus, so it's advised not to feed, touch, or approach them. Maintaining distance from animals is an important step in preventing disease transmission.

## **With Experts' Efforts, Bali Remains a Safe Paradise for Travelers**

Through the joint efforts of the Animal Health Department and volunteers, rabies prevention in Bali's major resort areas has entered an active control phase. As long as basic safety recommendations are followed, travelers can still safely enjoy Bali's natural scenery and cultural charm.

## **Frequently Asked Questions FAQ**

**What should I do if bitten by a dog in Bali?**  
Immediately wash the wound with soap and warm water for about 10-15 minutes, then quickly go to the nearest medical center to receive a rabies vaccine.

**Can monkeys transmit rabies?**  
Yes. Monkeys can also be carriers of the virus, and keeping your distance is the best prevention method.

**Do dogs need to be vaccinated every year?**  
Yes. According to veterinary advice and local regulations, dogs should be vaccinated once a year to ensure protection.

**Which areas in Bali are currently high-risk zones?**  
Southern Kuta areas, including Nusa Dua, Jimbaran, Benoa, and parts of Canggu, are all enhanced epidemic control zones.

**Can I get reimbursement for vaccine costs from travel insurance?**  
Some travel insurance plans cover rabies vaccine costs. It's recommended to confirm the details of your policy before departure.

**What if I don't have enough money to pay for the rabies vaccine?**  
Most large international hospitals in Bali (such as **BIMC**, **Siloam**) and clinics specializing in serving foreigners (like **Unicare**, **Nusa Medica**) accept international credit cards (Visa, Mastercard).  
Rabies vaccines usually need to be administered in multiple doses (e.g., days 0, 3, 7, 14, 28). You **don't need to pay for all doses at once**; you can pay for the first dose (day 0) first.

**How much does the first shot cost?**  
For the first shot, you'll need to prepare approximately IDR 1,500,000 – 2,200,000 (around USD$100 - $150). The most expensive part is the rabies immunoglobulin (RIG). If the doctor determines that your wound is a Category 3 exposure (involving bleeding or deep bites), in addition to the vaccine, you'll also need to receive the 'immunoglobulin.' This part is dosed according to body weight, and the total cost can be as high as IDR 10,000,000 – 25,000,000 (around USD$650 - $800 or more).
