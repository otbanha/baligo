### 20. Steps from Landing to Exiting the Airport:

After landing in Bali, follow these steps in order to complete your arrival procedures:

1. Follow the signs to disembark and enter the terminal.
2. Follow the 'Arrival' signs to the immigration hall.
3. Apply for a Visa on Arrival (VoA): If you haven't applied for an e-Visa in advance, queue at the 'Visa on Arrival' counter to pay. Those with an eVisa/eVoA can skip this step.
4. Immigration inspection (passport stamping) queue: Those with a VoA need to queue for inspection.
5. Electronic clearance: Those who have applied/paid for an eVisa in advance can skip the manual stamping line and use the 'automatic gate'.
6. Collect luggage: After passing immigration/automatic gates, head to the carousel for your flight number.
7. Prepare your health declaration and customs declaration form in advance (now integrated into All Indonesia).
8. Pay the Bali tourism tax.

### 23. Understanding Local Currency Conversion:

Bali uses the Indonesian Rupiah. Currently, 1 USD exchanges for around 16,000 IDR. Compared to the New Taiwan Dollar, the rate is roughly 1:500, meaning 1 TWD equals 500 IDR; 1,000 TWD × 500 equals 500,000 IDR.

A quick way to convert Indonesian Rupiah is to drop the last three zeros and multiply by 2, which gives you roughly the amount in New Taiwan Dollars.

### 27. Watch Your Bill:

When dining in Indonesia, keep an eye on the extra charges on your bill. The food and beverage tax is added on top, usually 11%, which goes to the government. Many restaurants also add a service charge of 4% to 10%. So if a menu item is priced at 100,000 Indonesian Rupiah, your final bill will be 100,000 IDR + 11% tax + the service fee. Be sure to factor in these extras when ordering to avoid overspending.
