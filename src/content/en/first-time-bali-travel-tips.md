### 20. From Landing to Exiting the Airport:

After arriving in Bali, you'll need to follow these steps for immigration procedures:

1. Follow instructions to disembark and enter the airport.
2. Follow the 'Arrival' signs to the immigration hall.
3. Apply for Visa on Arrival (VoA): If you haven't applied for an e-visa in advance, queue at the 'Visa on Arrival' counter to pay. Those with eVisas/eVoAs can skip this step.
4. Immigration check (passport stamping) queue: VoA holders need to queue for inspection.
5. E-gate: Those who have applied/paid for eVisa in advance can use the 'automatic gates' without queuing for manual stamping.
6. Collect luggage: After passing immigration/automatic gates, proceed to the baggage carousel according to your flight number.
7. Prepare health declaration and customs form (integrated into All Indonesia)
8. Pay Bali tourism tax

### 23. How to Convert Local Currency:

The currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah. Currently, one US dollar can be exchanged for approximately 16,000 Indonesian Rupiah. Compared to the New Taiwan Dollar, the exchange rate is roughly 1:500, meaning one NTD can be exchanged for 500 Indonesian Rupiah; 1,000 NTD ✖️ 500 equals 500,000 Indonesian Rupiah.

A quick conversion method is to remove the last three zeros from the Indonesian Rupiah amount, then ✖️ 2, which will give you an approximate amount in NTD.

### 27. Watch Out for Additional Charges:

When dining in Indonesia, be mindful of additional fees on your bill. Indonesian dining tax is added separately, usually at 11%, which goes to the government. Additionally, many restaurants charge an extra 4% to 10% service fee. So, if a menu item costs 100,000 IDR, your final bill will be 100,000 IDR + 11% tax + the corresponding service fee. Be sure to check these extra charges when ordering to avoid overspending.
