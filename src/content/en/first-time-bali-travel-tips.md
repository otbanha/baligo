### 20. From Landing to Exiting the Airport:

Upon arriving in Bali, you'll need to follow these steps for immigration procedures:

1. Follow instructions to disembark and enter the airport.
2. Follow the 'Arrival' signs to the immigration hall.
3. Apply for Visa on Arrival (VoA): If you haven't applied for an eVisa in advance, queue at the 'Visa on Arrival' counter to pay the fee. Skip this step if you already have an eVisa/eVoA.
4. Immigration check (passport stamping) queue: Those with VoA need to queue for inspection.
5. E-gate: Those who have applied/paid for eVisa in advance can use the 'automatic gates' without queuing for manual stamping.
6. Collect luggage: After passing immigration/automatic gates, proceed to the baggage carousel according to your flight number.
7. Prepare health declaration and customs form (integrated into All Indonesia)
8. Pay Bali tourism tax

### 23. How to Convert Local Currency:

The currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah. Currently, one US dollar can be exchanged for around 16,000 Indonesian Rupiah. Compared to the New Taiwan Dollar, the exchange rate is approximately 1:500, meaning one NT dollar can be exchanged for 500 Indonesian Rupiah; 1,000 NT dollars ✖️ 500 equals 500,000 Indonesian Rupiah.

A quick conversion method is to remove the last three zeros from the Indonesian Rupiah amount, then ✖️ 2, which will give you an approximate amount in NT dollars.

### 27. Pay Attention to Total Costs:

When dining in Indonesia, be mindful of additional charges. The dining tax in Indonesia is added separately, usually 11%, which goes to the government. Additionally, many restaurants add a service charge of 4% to 10%. So, if a menu item costs 100,000 IDR, your total payment will be 100,000 IDR + 11% tax + the corresponding service charge. Be sure to note these additional fees when ordering to avoid exceeding your budget.
