Every moment spent in Uluwatu feels like a slice of paradise, and we're not exaggerating! Uluwatu, Bali, is severely underrated, and today, we'll take you through its top 10 attractions and must-do activities.

## Number 10: Visit Uluwatu Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b953b86f0cdb3e5f162eec96ac861d2d.jpg)

Uluwatu Temple is considered one of Bali's most famous temples, alongside Tanah Lot and Besakih Temple. Perched 17 meters above the Indian Ocean on a cliff, it's one of Bali's three most renowned temples, embodying the foundation of Balinese spirituality. Beyond its religious significance, visitors are drawn by the breathtaking ocean views and stunning sunsets. Be cautious of the mischievous monkeys, known as the most unruly in Southeast Asia—remove your glasses and jewelry before visiting.

## Number 9: Watch the Kecak Fire Dance

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8e1e2cb735b2c791a156b870f1022afc.jpg)

Visitors to Uluwatu Temple can also enjoy the famous Kecak Fire Dance. This unique Balinese dance doesn't rely on musical instruments but is accompanied by a chorus of men chanting rhythmically. Like all Balinese dances, it tells a story through movement. The best place to watch this fire dance is on the stage at Uluwatu Temple, where you can marvel at the sunset behind the dancers.

## Number 8: Enjoy the Sunset at Single Fin

![Single Fin日落美景](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7913efd720450c575668b4f446c9e488.jpg)

Single Fin Sunset Views

Perched on the southwestern coastline, Uluwatu offers one of Bali's most spectacular sunset views over the Indian Ocean. Watching the sunset in front of Uluwatu Temple is an unparalleled experience. ## #6: Explore Suluban Beach

While Suluban Beach (also known as Blue Point Beach) isn't ideal for sunbathing, it's worth visiting for its mysterious caves!

{{video1}}

This is a popular spot for surfers who use the cave to access the wild waves. You'll need to descend a long staircase and pass through a natural limestone cave to reach the shore. This unique beach is perfect for photos, and you can watch surfers heading out to sea.

## #5: Visit GWK Cultural Park

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6c3c61665afdc5719285358d3e0c4bd9.jpg)

Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park is Indonesia's largest cultural park, dedicated to the Hindu god Wisnu and his mount, the mythical bird Garuda. The park features Indonesia's tallest statue of Wisnu riding Garuda, a major highlight. At this cultural site, you can also shop and enjoy Balinese dance performances.

## Fourth Place: Balangan Viewpoint

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc2912da85b54c0124095c6a7ac446b1.jpg)

Balangan Cliff Sunset Point is a captivating location renowned for its stunning seaside views. It's one of the best spots to witness Bali's spectacular sunsets, where you can watch the sun gradually sink below the horizon, leaving behind unforgettable beauty. Additionally, you can enjoy hiking through the rocks, immersing yourself in nature's beauty, or simply relaxing on the beach.

This is also an excellent location for wedding photography, with many couples choosing Balangan Cliff Sunset Point to capture precious memories. In this romantic setting, you can seize beautiful moments with your loved one, creating eternal imprints of your love.

## Third Place: Visit Green Bowl Beach

Green Bowl Beach is one of Uluwatu's most secluded beaches, a haven for those seeking solitude. Its fine white sand, crystal-clear blue waters, and serene atmosphere make it unforgettable. The beach stretches 30 meters, hidden beneath 75-meter-high cliffs. At the base of the cliffs, there are two large limestone caves for shelter. However, reaching this spot requires descending over 300 steep steps.

{{video2}}

The best time to visit is during low tide, which is also ideal for snorkeling. There are no vendors selling drinks or food on the beach, so remember to bring plenty of water.

## Second Place: Tanah Barak Cliff Scenic Road / Blue Road

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7ae1b58d8b51712366caf4610c73688a.jpg)

Bali's Tanah Barak Cliff (also known as Pantai Batu Barak) is a hidden gem that has recently become a popular attraction. This newly opened beach features stunning blue waters and colorful rocks, making it unique. Besides the magnificent sea views, the road here is carved through massive limestone cliffs, with the azure sea in the distance and towering rock walls on either side creating a visually striking experience. You can also climb the central rocks for better photo angles. Tanah Barak is about a 30-minute drive from the airport, and admission requires a ticket (currently Rp8,000 for adults, Rp3,000 for children). It is open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

## Number One: Surfing and Diving

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f237c3322eff064a02804f4e6cffcc22.jpg)

With its excellent surf spots along the Uluwatu coastline, this area is a surfer's paradise and one of the main attractions drawing visitors to Uluwatu. There are numerous surf camps and lesson centers nearby, including Uluwatu Beach, Padang Padang Beach, Bingin Beach, Green Bowl Beach, and Suluban Beach, to name just a few. Take Uluwatu Beach, for example, which is considered Uluwatu's biggest draw. Besides the famous cliffside temple, it's also a popular spot for visitors to experience surfing and various water sports. You can comfortably watch the sunset on the beach or enjoy delicious meals at nearby restaurants, undoubtedly captivated by the beach's enchanting charm. Whether you're looking to fully experience the thrill of surfing or simply want to relax and watch, this is definitely a must-visit destination.
