If you want to stay right in the heart of Seminyak, where you can walk everywhere and return to a rooftop pool to watch the sunset, then Cross Paasha Bali Seminyak, a four-star design boutique hotel, is worth considering. Formerly known as U Paasha, it was renamed Cross Paasha but remains at the same location on Jl. Laksmana No. 77, in the core area of Kayu Aya Street. All 99 rooms are suite-level, spacious, and strongly designed, making it one of the higher value-for-money options in Seminyak.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231500443-roof1.jpg)

***

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

Cross Paasha is located in Seminyak's most prime Laksmana area, known as 'Eat Street.' Just a two-minute walk from the hotel is Seminyak Square shopping center, and Seminyak Village Mall is only a three-minute walk away. Daily shopping, restaurants, and cafes are almost all within walking distance.

As for the beach, Seminyak Beach is about a 10 to 15-minute walk, which isn't very close but is still acceptable; a Grab ride takes about 5 minutes. The surrounding attractions are also plentiful: Petitenget Beach and Pura Petitenget temple are a five-minute drive, La Lucciola seaside restaurant and Ku De Ta are a 10-minute walk, and Potato Head Beach Club is a three-minute drive.

The overall vibe of the Seminyak area is a mix of boutique shopping and nightlife, with a high density of restaurants, bars, and fashion shops on the streets. If you want to enjoy a refined urban experience in Bali, this location is almost unbeatable.

***

## 🏨 Facilities

Upon entering the lobby, the first impression is unlike traditional Balinese-style resorts—high ceilings, geometric brick walls, streamlined furniture, combining industrial style with modern design tastefully. Guests are greeted with a complimentary welcome drink upon check-in. The lobby bar area is dimly lit and cool, attracting mainly younger guests and couples.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231575224-lobi.webp)

The hotel's biggest highlight is the **Rooftop Pool**. This pool is long and spacious, surrounded by palm trees, sun loungers, and parasols. On a clear evening, you can overlook the Seminyak cityscape and even glimpse the sea. There's a poolside bar for cocktails, and Happy Hour reportedly offers a buy-one-get-one-free deal, making it the social hub of the hotel.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231634104-roof3.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231634525-roof2.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231635037-roof5.jpg)

The restaurant ALCOVE offers a buffet breakfast with Asian and Western options, rotating daily with live egg stations, pancakes, Indonesian fried rice, etc.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231683899-canteen1.webp)

The hotel also features a gym with basic equipment. A unique policy is the **24-hour check-in system**: your stay starts exactly 24 hours from your check-in time, with flexible check-out times, not bound by the traditional 12 PM check-in/11 AM check-out rules, which is very friendly for travelers with uncertain flight schedules.

***

## 🛏️ Room Types

All 99 rooms at Cross Paasha are designated as Suites, positioning them a step above standard rooms. Starting from 55 square meters, the largest room spans up to 110 square meters, offering exceptional spaciousness for the price point.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231736986-86495544.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231737371-558542199.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231738211-683edcbff56e61d844944a683b65f028.webp)

The decor blends industrial chic with modern design: light wood furniture, concrete floors, hexagonal mirrors, and other details create a clean, minimalist aesthetic, steering clear of traditional Balinese carved wood decor. Each room features a private balcony, separate seating area, flat-screen TV, minibar, free Wi-Fi, and air conditioning. The bathrooms are open-plan, equipped with rain showers.

Room selection tip: **The higher the floor, the better**. Lower-floor rooms facing the street may occasionally suffer from road noise, while higher floors offer noticeably better soundproofing and more expansive views. For couples traveling together, we recommend opting for a high-floor balcony suite with a view.

***

## 👥 Ideal Guests

Ideal for **young couples or friends traveling together**, especially first-time visitors to Seminyak who want easy access to shopping, dining, and nightlife. The location is the hotel's strongest asset, allowing guests to explore most attractions on foot without relying on Grab rides. Families are also welcome, with a children's pool and childcare services available. However, those traveling with infants or seeking a quieter environment should pay extra attention to room floor selection.

***

## 💬 Guest Reviews

A long-term repeat guest on Tripadvisor shared that this was their fifth stay, and they found it consistently worthwhile. They particularly praised the spaciousness of the rooms and the staff's attentive service, noting that they always felt well taken care of. (Source: Tripadvisor)

Another guest mentioned in their Agoda review that the breakfast here isn't your typical buffet but rather a restaurant-style à la carte experience. Located in the heart of Kayu Aya Street, everything is within walking distance. They were pleasantly surprised by the room's spaciousness, which is rare at this price point.

***

## ⚠️ Honest Drawbacks

A few things to note. First, some reviews point out minor issues like mold spots or aging walls in older rooms (the hotel has since changed management and is reportedly making improvements). Second, noise from the street can be an issue for lower-floor rooms, especially with motorcycles and nightclub music in Seminyak at night. Third, the rooftop pool is fully exposed to the sun, with limited shaded areas, making it less ideal for prolonged use during midday or summer.
