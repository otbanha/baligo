If you want to stay right in the heart of Seminyak, where you can walk everywhere and return to a rooftop pool for sunset views, then Cross Paasha Bali Seminyak, a four-star design boutique hotel, is worth considering. Formerly known as U Paasha, this hotel is located at Jl. Laksmana No. 77, in the core area of Kayu Aya Street. All 99 rooms are suite-level, spacious, and strongly designed, making it one of the best value options in Seminyak.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231500443-roof1.jpg)

***

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

Cross Paasha is situated in Seminyak's prime Laksmana area, known as 'Eat Street.' Just a two-minute walk from the hotel is Seminyak Square shopping center, and Seminyak Village Mall is only a three-minute walk away. Daily shopping, restaurants, and cafes are all within walking distance.

As for the beach, Seminyak Beach is about a 10 to 15-minute walk, which isn't too close but still manageable. A Grab ride takes roughly 5 minutes. Nearby attractions are plentiful: Petitenget Beach and Pura Petitenget temple are a five-minute drive, La Lucciola ocean-view restaurant and Ku De Ta are a 10-minute walk, and Potato Head Beach Club is a three-minute drive away.

The overall vibe of Seminyak is a mix of boutique shopping and nightlife, with a high density of restaurants, bars, and fashion shops on the streets. If you're looking for a refined urban experience in Bali, this location is almost unbeatable.

***

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

Upon entering the lobby, the first impression is unlike traditional Balinese-style resorts—high ceilings, geometric brick walls, and streamlined furniture combine industrial and modern design tastefully. Guests are greeted with complimentary welcome drinks, and the lobby bar area features dim lighting and a cool atmosphere, attracting mainly younger guests and couples.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231575224-lobi.webp)

The hotel's biggest highlight is the **Rooftop Pool**. This pool is long and spacious, surrounded by palm trees, sun loungers, and umbrellas. On clear evenings, you can overlook Seminyak's cityscape and even catch a glimpse of the ocean. The poolside bar offers cocktails, and Happy Hour reportedly has a buy-one-get-one-free deal, making it the hotel's social hub.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231634104-roof3.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231634525-roof2.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231635037-roof5.jpg)

The restaurant ALCOVE serves Asian and Western breakfast buffets, with daily rotating dishes including made-to-order egg stations, pancakes, and Indonesian fried rice.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231683899-canteen1.webp)

The hotel also features a gym with basic equipment. A unique policy is the **24-hour check-in system**: Your stay starts exactly 24 hours from your check-in time, offering complete flexibility for departure times, free from the traditional 12 PM check-in/11 AM check-out constraints. This is particularly friendly for travelers with unpredictable flight schedules.

***

## 🛏️ Room Types

Cross Paasha's 99 rooms are all Suites, a step above standard rooms. Starting at 55 square meters and going up to 110 square meters for the largest suite, the space is quite generous for the price point.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231736986-86495544.jpg)

The decor follows a light industrial style with modern design elements: plenty of light wood furniture, concrete floors, hexagonal mirrors, and other details that create a clean, crisp look—far from the traditional Balinese carved wood aesthetic. Each room features a private balcony, a separate seating area, a flat-screen TV, a minibar, free Wi-Fi, and air conditioning. The bathrooms are open-plan with rain showers.

Room selection tip: **The higher the floor, the better**. Lower-floor rooms facing the street may occasionally suffer from road noise, while higher floors offer better soundproofing and more expansive views. For couples traveling together, we recommend booking a high-floor balcony suite.

***

## 👥 Ideal Guests

This place is ideal for **young couples or friends traveling together**, especially first-time visitors to Seminyak who want easy access to shopping, dining, and nightlife. The location is the hotel's biggest asset—most activities are within walking distance, so you won't need to rely on Grab rides. Families are also welcome, as the hotel has a kids' pool and childcare services. However, if you're traveling with infants or toddlers or prefer a quieter environment, pay extra attention to your room's floor level.

***

## 💬 Guest Reviews

A long-time returning guest on Tripadvisor shared that this was their fifth stay, and they always find it worth it. They particularly praised the spacious rooms and the staff's attentive service, saying they always feel well taken care of. (Source: Tripadvisor)

Another guest mentioned in their Agoda review that the breakfast here isn't your typical buffet but more like a restaurant-style dining experience. Located in the heart of Kayu Aya Street, everything is within walking distance. The room's spaciousness was unexpectedly impressive for the price.

***

## ⚠️ Honest Downsides

A few things to note. First, some reviews mention minor issues like mildew spots or aging walls in older rooms (the hotel has since changed management and is reportedly working on improvements). Second, noise from the street can be an issue for lower-floor rooms—Seminyak's streets are lively with motorcycles and nightclub music at night. Third, the rooftop pool gets full sun exposure during the day, with limited shaded areas, making it less ideal for lounging in the summer or midday heat.
