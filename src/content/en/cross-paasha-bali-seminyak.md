If you want to stay right in the heart of Seminyak, where you can walk everywhere and return to a rooftop pool to watch the sunset, then Cross Paasha Bali Seminyak, a four-star design boutique hotel, is worth considering. Formerly known as U Paasha, it was renamed Cross Paasha but remains at the same location at Jl. Laksmana No. 77, in the core area of Kayu Aya Street. All 99 rooms are suite level, spacious and strongly designed, making it one of the better value-for-money options in Seminyak.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231500443-roof1.jpg)

***

## 📍 Location and Surroundings

Cross Paasha is located in the most prestigious Laksmana area of Seminyak, known as the 'Eat Street'. The hotel is just a two-minute walk from Seminyak Square shopping center and a three-minute walk from Seminyak Village Mall, with almost all daily shopping, restaurants, and cafes within walking distance.

As for the beach, Seminyak Beach is about a 10 to 15-minute walk, which is not very close but still acceptable; a Grab ride takes about 5 minutes. The surrounding attractions are also rich: Petitenget Beach and Pura Petitenget temple are a five-minute drive away, La Lucciola seaside restaurant and Ku De Ta are a 10-minute walk, and Potato Head Beach Club is a three-minute drive.

The overall vibe of the Seminyak area is a mix of boutique shopping and nightlife, with a high density of restaurants, bars, and fashion shops on the streets. If you want to enjoy a refined urban travel experience in Bali, this location is almost unbeatable.

***

## 🏨 Facility Introduction

Walking into the lobby, the first impression is not like a traditional Balinese-style resort—high ceilings, geometric brick walls, streamlined furniture, combining industrial style with modern design tastefully. Upon check-in, there is a complimentary welcome drink, and the lobby bar area is dimly lit, creating a cool atmosphere, attracting mainly young people and couples.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231575224-lobi.webp)

The biggest highlight of the hotel is the **Rooftop Pool**. This pool is long enough, with ample space, surrounded by palm trees, sun loungers, and parasols. On a good evening, you can overlook the Seminyak city area and even glimpse the sea. There is a poolside bar where you can order cocktails, and during Happy Hour, there is reportedly a buy-one-get-one-free offer, making it the social center of the hotel.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231634104-roof3.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231634525-roof2.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231635037-roof5.jpg)

The restaurant ALCOVE offers a rotating selection of Asian and Western breakfast buffet items daily, including made-to-order omelets, pancakes, and Indonesian fried rice.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231683899-canteen1.webp)

The hotel also features a gym with basic but sufficient equipment. A particularly unique policy is the **24-hour check-in system**: Your stay begins exactly at the time you check in and lasts for a full 24 hours, allowing for completely flexible check-out times. This is especially convenient for travelers with unpredictable flight schedules, as it eliminates the traditional 12 PM check-in and 11 AM check-out constraints.

***

## 🛏️ Room Overview

Cross Paasha's 99 rooms are uniformly referred to as Suites, positioning them a step above standard rooms. Starting at 55 square meters and going up to 110 square meters for the largest option, the rooms are notably spacious for the price point.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231736986-86495544.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231737371-558542199.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231737832-1c93851cb69bca784431d5ea40507f08.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231738211-683edcbff56e61d844944a683b65f028.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231738613-45807d9b3c69fd21a57adfb2190c610a.webp)

The decor blends light industrial and modern design elements: abundant light wood furniture, concrete floors, hexagonal mirrors, and other details create a clean, streamlined aesthetic—quite different from the traditional Balinese carved wood style. Each room features a private balcony, a separate seating area, a flat-screen TV, a minibar, free Wi-Fi, and air conditioning. The bathrooms are open-concept with rain showers.

Room Selection Tip: **The higher the floor, the better**. Lower-floor rooms facing the street may occasionally experience noise disturbances, while higher floors offer noticeably better soundproofing and more expansive views. For couples traveling together, we recommend directly booking a high-floor balcony suite.

***

## 👥 Ideal Guests

This hotel is best suited for **young couples or friends traveling together**, especially first-time visitors to Seminyak who want convenient access to shopping, dining, and nightlife. Location is this hotel's greatest strength—most activities are within walking distance, eliminating the need to rely on Grab rides. Families can also enjoy their stay here, as the hotel offers a children's pool and childcare services. However, those traveling with infants or seeking a quieter environment should pay extra attention to room floor selection.

***

## 💬 Guest Reviews

A long-time repeat guest on Tripadvisor shared that this was their fifth stay, and they found it consistently worthwhile. They particularly praised the spaciousness of the rooms and the attentive service from the staff, noting that they always felt well taken care of. (Source: Tripadvisor)

Another guest mentioned in an Agoda review that the breakfast here isn't your typical buffet but more like a restaurant-style ordering experience. Located at the heart of Kayu Aya Street, everything you need is within walking distance. The rooms are surprisingly spacious, which is quite rare for this price range.

***

## ⚠️ Honest Drawbacks

There are a few points to note. First, some reviews have pointed out minor issues like mold spots or aging walls in older rooms (the hotel has reportedly changed management and is gradually improving). Second, noise can be an issue for rooms on lower floors facing the street, as Seminyak's roads can get noisy at night with motorcycles and club music. Third, the rooftop pool is exposed to full sun throughout the day with limited shaded areas, making it less ideal for extended stays by the pool during summer or midday.
