# 1. Trip.com Hidden Search Tricks: Three Features to Uncover Stealth Deals

## 1. Enable Price Alerts

Many people open the site and search repeatedly every day.

But you really don’t have to.

For example:

Taipei ➜ Bali

Travel Dates:

2026/9/1 – 2026/10/30

Once you’ve entered the details, just tap the bell icon for price alerts.

Then, whenever the price drops, the app will instantly send you a push notification.

Here’s my usual mental price range:

- Direct flight: under USD$387
- Connecting flight: under USD$258

Sometimes airlines suddenly release tickets, and you can really snag an unexpected deal.

## 2. Make the Most of the Low-Price Calendar Feature

This is one of my favorite tools. Many people automatically pick a Friday departure and Sunday return.

But airlines know exactly what travelers are thinking. So those dates are usually the most expensive.

When you actually search, you’ll often find:

| Date | Price |
| --- | --- |
| Depart Friday | USD$597 |
| Depart Saturday | USD$574 |
| Depart Tuesday | USD$435 |

Just three days apart, but the price difference is over USD$160.

If your schedule allows, try to avoid weekends and popular holidays.

# 2. Traditional Direct Flights vs. Budget Transfers: How to Choose in 2026?

Many first-time visitors to Bali often ask:

"Is a direct flight worth it?"

The answer really depends on your time and budget.

| Flight Strategy | Representative Airlines | Pros | Cons | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Premium Direct | EVA Air, China Airlines, STARLUX | Arrive in about 5–5.5 hours, meals & luggage included | Higher price | Families with kids, elderly travelers |

# 3. After Snagging a Cheap Flight, Don't Overlook These Three Hidden Costs

## 1. Budget Airline Baggage Fees Can Cost More Than the Ticket

Many people get thrilled when they score a super cheap flight.

But on the way back, they end up buying:

- Coffee beans
- Shrimp crackers
- Snacks
- Wood carvings
- Essential oils

And their luggage ends up way over the weight limit.

Buying baggage allowance at the airport is usually much pricier than adding it in advance.

It's best to confirm your return baggage needs right after booking.

## 2. Don't Cut Your Layover Too Short

My personal advice:

Leave at least 2 hours or more.

Especially at:

- Kuala Lumpur (KUL)
- Singapore (SIN)

These airports are huge and packed during peak season.

If your first flight is delayed, it can easily cause problems down the line.
