When visiting Ubud, Bali, besides admiring the terraced rice fields and exploring ancient temples, you absolutely can't miss the culinary experiences here! From traditional Indonesian cuisine to creative Western-style restaurants, from hidden rice field cafes to bustling market street food, Ubud gathers Bali's most exciting food culture. This guide selects Ubud's must-try restaurants and local delicacies, ensuring your taste buds enjoy an unforgettable Bali journey!

## Boni Bali Restaurant (Google Rating 4.3⭐) 👉🏼Map: [Immerse in Scenic Views](/map/ubud/)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5046f26f284c95d89a998876cafa9852.jpg)

Enjoying traditional Indonesian cuisine while overlooking the stunning Tegallalang Rice Terraces is one of Bali's greatest pleasures.

The Tegallalang Rice Terraces, located in northeastern Bali, are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Spanning 2,500 hectares, these terraces consist of thousands of stepped rice fields that cascade towards the horizon. Under the sunlight, the terraces display a mesmerizing palette of golden, green, and brown hues.

Boni Bali Restaurant, situated on the edge of the Tegallalang Rice Terraces, offers traditional Indonesian cuisine. Visitors can savor classic dishes such as Nasi Goreng, Sate Ayam, and Rendang here.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2cc396ec6670466a36f49416c315f046.jpg)

This place can be arranged as the final stop of a day spent strolling through Ubud's bustling area, where you can relax and take beautiful photos. In the evening, there's even an open-air cinema showing movies!

If you want to swim at Folk Pool & Garden, a single SUN LOUNGER costs IDR 150,000, which is about USD$10. They will provide you with a towel, allowing you to swim and take photos here.
