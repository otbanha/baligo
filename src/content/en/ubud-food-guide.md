**Bottom line**: In Ubud, focus on three experiences—for **Instagram-worthy vibes**, head to **Taman Dedari** (Google 4.6⭐, giant fairy statue + afternoon tea); for **waterfall views**, go to **D'Tukad River Club** (a treehouse day club atop Tegenungan Waterfall with 360-degree waterfall views); for **local classics**, stick with famous dirty duck rice and pork rib joints. Below is a full list organized by rating and setting.

When you come to Ubud, Bali, beyond admiring the rice terrace scenery and exploring ancient temples, you absolutely can't miss the food experiences here! From traditional Indonesian dishes to creative Western restaurants, from cafés hidden among the rice paddies to street food in lively markets, Ubud brings together the most vibrant culinary culture of Bali. This guide curates must-eat restaurants and local foods in Ubud, so your taste buds can also enjoy an unforgettable Bali journey!

## Boni Bali Restaurant (Google Rating 4.3⭐) 👉🏼 Map: [Immerse in Scenic Beauty](/map/ubud/)

![Boni Bali Restaurant (谷歌評價4.3) 地圖：沈浸山水美景](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5046f26f284c95d89a998876cafa9852.jpg)

Enjoying traditional Indonesian cuisine while overlooking the stunning Tegalalang Rice Terraces is one of the great pleasures of traveling to Bali.

Located in northeastern Bali, the Tegalalang Rice Terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage site. Spanning 2,500 hectares, they consist of thousands of stepped rice paddies cascading to the horizon. Under the sunlight, the terraces shimmer in shades of gold, green, and brown, creating a breathtaking sight.

Boni Bali Restaurant sits on the edge of the Tegalalang Rice Terraces, serving traditional Indonesian dishes. Guests can enjoy classics like Nasi Goreng, Sate Ayam, and Rendang.
