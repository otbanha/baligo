When you come to Ubud, Bali, besides admiring the rice terrace scenery and exploring ancient temples, you absolutely can't miss the food experience here! From traditional Indonesian cuisine to creative Western restaurants, from cafés hidden among the rice fields to street food at bustling markets, Ubud brings together the most exciting culinary culture of Bali. This guide selects must-eat restaurants and local foods in Ubud, letting your taste buds enjoy an unforgettable Bali journey!

## Boni Bali Restaurant (Google Rating 4.3⭐) 👉🏼 Map: [Immersed in Scenic Beauty](/map/ubud/)

![Boni Bali Restaurant (Google Rating 4.3) Map: Immersed in Sc](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5046f26f284c95d89a998876cafa9852.jpg)

Enjoying traditional Indonesian cuisine while overlooking the stunning Tegallalang Rice Terraces is one of the great pleasures of traveling to Bali.

Located in northeastern Bali, the Tegallalang Rice Terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage site. Spanning 2,500 hectares, they consist of thousands of stepped rice paddies that cascade layer upon layer to the horizon. Bathed in sunlight, the terraces shimmer in a vibrant palette of gold, green, and brown, creating an unforgettable spectacle.

Boni Bali Restaurant sits on the edge of the Tegallalang Rice Terraces, serving traditional Indonesian cuisine. Here, guests can savor classic dishes like Nasi Goreng, Sate Ayam, and Rendang.
