When visiting Ubud, Bali, besides admiring the stunning rice terraces and exploring ancient temples, you absolutely can’t miss the culinary experiences here! From traditional Indonesian dishes to creative Western cuisine, from hidden rice field cafes to bustling market street food, Ubud offers the best of Bali’s food culture. This guide curates Ubud’s must-try restaurants and local delicacies, ensuring your taste buds enjoy an unforgettable Bali journey!

## Boni Bali Restaurant (Google Rating 4.3⭐) 👉🏼Map: [Immerse in Scenic Beauty](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5046f26f284c95d89a998876cafa9852.jpg)

Enjoying traditional Indonesian cuisine while overlooking the Tegallalang Rice Terraces is one of the great pleasures of visiting Bali.

The Tegallalang Rice Terraces, located in northeastern Bali, are a UNESCO World Heritage site. Spanning 2,500 hectares, these terraces consist of thousands of stepped rice fields, layered and stretching to the horizon. Under the sunlight, the terraces display a spectrum of colors—golden, green, brown—creating a breathtaking view.

Boni Bali Restaurant is situated right by the Tegallalang Rice Terraces and offers traditional Indonesian dishes. Visitors can enjoy classic dishes such as Nasi Goreng, Sate Ayam, and Rendang here.
