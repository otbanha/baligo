When visiting Ubud, Bali, besides admiring the stunning rice terraces and ancient temples, you absolutely can't miss the culinary experiences here! From traditional Indonesian cuisine to creative Western-style restaurants, from hidden rice field cafes to vibrant street food in bustling markets, Ubud brings together the most exciting food culture in Bali. This guide curates the must-try restaurants and local delicacies in Ubud, ensuring your taste buds enjoy an unforgettable Bali journey!

## Boni Bali Restaurant (Google Rating 4.3⭐) 👉🏼Map: [Immerse in Scenic Beauty](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5046f26f284c95d89a998876cafa9852.jpg)

Enjoying Indonesian traditional cuisine while overlooking the Tegallalang Rice Terraces is one of the great pleasures of traveling in Bali.

The Tegallalang Rice Terraces, located in northeastern Bali, are a UNESCO World Heritage site. Spanning 2,500 hectares, the terraces consist of thousands of stepped rice fields, layered and stretching to the horizon. Under the sunlight, the terraces display a spectrum of colors including gold, green, and brown, creating a breathtaking view.

Boni Bali Restaurant is situated on the edge of the Tegallalang Rice Terraces and offers Indonesian traditional cuisine. Visitors can savor classic dishes such as Nasi Goreng, Sate Ayam, and Rendang here.
