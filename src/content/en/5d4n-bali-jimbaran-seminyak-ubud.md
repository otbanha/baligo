## Day 3

We left Jimbaran and headed to Seminyak, a lively and stylish area. We didn't pack the day with too many sights, instead focusing on food, shopping, and leisurely strolls to slowly soak up Seminyak's unique vibe, so different from other parts of Bali.

For lunch, we stopped at **Kilo Kitchen**, a renowned fusion restaurant that artfully blends European, Latin American, and Asian culinary elements. Every dish has its own distinct character.

Our favorite was the **Crab Dumplings**—thin, delicate wrappers with a sweet, bouncy shrimp-paste-like filling that's incredibly satisfying. They're practically a must-order. The **Beef Tongue Tacos** were also memorable, with the tongue braised until incredibly tender and layered beautifully with the corn tortillas. The **Squid Ink Risotto** was rich with seafood aroma, and the **Rendang Beef Pasta** cleverly combines classic Indonesian rendang spices with pasta for a surprisingly perfect match. If you enjoy trying unique flavors, I think Kilo Kitchen is worth a meal.

After eating, we went to **Bintang Supermarket** to shop for souvenirs. It's a popular spot recommended by many independent travelers, with a very comprehensive selection of goods—from snacks, coffee, and seasonings to daily necessities, you can find almost everything.

This time, we picked up **Woca chocolate**, Indonesian beer, **Marubali coconut chips**, civet coffee, Indonesian instant noodles, and other popular items. After browsing, our main takeaway was that if you're short on time, you can buy most of your souvenirs in one go here without needing to visit multiple places—super convenient for itinerary planning.

Next, we continued to **Seminyak Village** for some shopping. While it's not a huge mall, the environment is comfortable and the brands are stylish, making it a perfect spot for a leisurely afternoon.

At **Sensatia Botanicals**, we bought facial scrub and cleanser. Their natural plant-based skincare products are highly recommended by many travelers and are a popular Balinese brand. After that, we browsed a few clothing stores and finally found a café to sit down, enjoy some coffee, cool off with the AC, and organize our haul.

If you're not looking for serious retail therapy, I think spending **1 to 1.5 hours** at Seminyak Village is just right—enough time to browse the shops, sip a coffee, and soak up Seminyak's relaxed yet trendy vacation atmosphere.

After exploring Seminyak, the next highlight of the day was checking into our second hotel!

This time we stayed at **Berry Amour Romantic Villas**, with a nightly rate of around USD$230. This villa is designed for a romantic getaway, perfect for couples, newlyweds, or honeymooners.

During check-in, the hotel arranged a small, ceremonial welcome activity. The staff asked us to put bracelets on each other and even provided crowns for photos. Though a bit shy, it added a special memory to our stay.

Once the formalities were done, a staff member drove us to our villa in a golf cart. From the moment we stepped inside, we felt a sense of seclusion and relaxation, cut off from the outside world.

The villa came with a private pool, a small kitchen, and a soaking tub for unwinding. What we loved most was that the bathroom was indoors, unlike the previous villa's semi-outdoor setup—no worries about wind, rain, or bugs, making it much more comfortable.

If we had to choose between the two villas on this trip, we personally preferred **Berry Amour Romantic Villas**. The overall layout, comfort, and romantic atmosphere suited our tastes better.

## Day Four

On the fourth day, we arranged a full-day private car service to explore different spots in central Bali. But before heading out, we couldn't miss the floating breakfast offered by the villa!

Although the breakfast at this villa wasn't quite as much to our taste as the previous one, staying in a villa makes trying a floating breakfast at least once highly recommended. Placing the tray on the pool, enjoying breakfast while snapping photos—it really adds a ceremonial vacation vibe. That said, purely in terms of the food, we actually liked the congee the most; its flavor was surprisingly good and really boosted the meal.

After breakfast, we officially kicked off our day's car tour.

Our first stop was **Sangeh Monkey Forest**.

When it comes to Bali's monkey forests, most travelers first think of the Sacred Monkey Forest in Ubud, but this time we specifically chose Sangeh Monkey Forest. Its biggest advantage is that there are far fewer visitors, making the experience less crowded and allowing for a more relaxed interaction with the monkeys.

As soon as we entered the area, a local guide accompanied us, handing out a pack of peanuts before leading us deeper into the forest. He didn't just show us around—he taught us how to properly feed the monkeys, explained their habits, and shared many small details to be mindful of when interacting with them.

The most fun part was that the guide would occasionally make specific sounds to attract the monkeys. Before long, monkeys would jump onto our shoulders, arms, or nearby trees, giving us plenty of chances to interact and take photos. The whole experience felt very natural, and you could tell the guide was quite familiar with these monkeys.

![第四天](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548550073-mixcollage-27-jun-2026-03-16-pm-9129.jpg)

We really loved this itinerary—the entire experience lasted about an hour, not too long and not rushed. If you want to avoid the crowds at the Ubud Monkey Forest, I think this is a great alternative worth considering.

The entrance fee this time was about **USD$10**, and we also bought a souvenir photo taken at the park, plus a tip for the guide, which added another **USD$6**. Overall, we felt it was well worth it and one of the most memorable spots on this trip.

After leaving **Sangeh Monkey Forest**, we continued on to one of the most popular areas on this journey—Ubud.

At noon, we stopped for lunch at **Sun Sun Warung**, a budget-friendly Indonesian restaurant recommended by many travelers. The setting is simple, but the food is impressive and very affordable.

We ordered their signature **Crispy Duck Rice** and **Mie Goreng (Indonesian Fried Noodles)**, and both were satisfying. The crispy duck had a crunchy skin while the meat remained tender, and it paired beautifully with Indonesian chili sauce for extra depth. The fried noodles were rich with Southeast Asian aromas, savory and flavorful without being too greasy. For the two of us, the total bill was only about **USD$16**—great value for Ubud. If you enjoy authentic Indonesian cuisine, I highly recommend giving it a try.

After eating, we strolled around Ubud's town center, wandering along the lively streets and passing by the famous **Ubud Palace**. The streets are lined with unique shops, handicraft stores, and cafés, giving the area a wonderful vacation vibe.

However, the weather that day was scorching hot, and we soon decided to find a place to cool off.

We stopped at **Tukies Coconut Shop**, a well-known dessert spot specializing in coconut treats. Although the shop has no air conditioning, it offers a variety of coconut-based drinks and desserts, perfect for a refreshing break on a hot afternoon.

This time, we ordered **coconut ice, a coconut lemon drink, and an American coffee**. The icy coconut beverages were incredibly refreshing and instantly chased away the heat. If you're sweating from exploring Ubud, I highly recommend popping in for a drink and a rest.

After resting, we discovered another shop selling **Kopi Luwak (civet coffee)**. The most special part was that we could actually interact with the real civet cats up close!

![第四天](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548311880-luwak.jpg)

The civet cats in the shop were much larger than I expected, weighing about **10 kilograms**. The staff even let us hold one for photos, which was quite a unique experience.

Of course, since we were there, we also ordered a cup of civet coffee to try. It was incredibly smooth, not too bitter, and overall very pleasant to drink—checking off another must-do experience for many visitors to Bali.

After walking around for a while, our feet started to protest, so we headed to the nearby **Ubud Traditional Spa** to relax.

This time we chose the **Rice Farmer Massage** package, which includes a **60-minute Rice Farmer Massage** plus a **30-minute foot massage**, priced at around **USD$26** per person.

![第四天](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548370433-spa.jpg)

(Provided by Dr. Huang)

The massage therapist's pressure wasn't as precise as the SPA we experienced a few days earlier, but the overall service, ambiance, and price were excellent. For Ubud's city center, the value for money is quite high. If you're planning a full day of shopping and sightseeing in Ubud, ending with a relaxing massage before heading back to the hotel makes for a very comfortable itinerary.

After finishing our Ubud itinerary, we returned to Seminyak, ready to cap off the fulfilling day with a great meal.

For dinner, we chose **Pison**, a popular Western-style restaurant with a diverse menu. In the evening, there was a live band performing, creating a very relaxed atmosphere—perfect for a leisurely dinner with friends or a partner.

As soon as we sat down, we ordered the shop's signature drink, **Avocado Espresso**. I was a bit skeptical about the combination of avocado and espresso at first, but one sip was truly surprising—the creamy richness of the avocado paired unexpectedly well with the coffee's aroma. I highly recommend giving it a try if you get the chance.

For our main courses, we ordered **Steak with Truffle Mushroom Sauce** and **Truffle Carbonara Pasta**. Both were excellent, with a rich truffle aroma that wasn't overpowering, and the flavors had great depth. Paired with the live band performance, it made for an especially relaxing dinner and one of the most memorable meals of the trip.

## Day Five

Before we knew it, we had arrived at the last day of our Bali trip.

We didn't plan much for this day. After checking out, we asked our driver **Benny** to take us to a local eatery he recommended: **Warung Babi Guling Pak Malen**.

This is a famous roast suckling pig restaurant, and around noon it was nearly packed, clearly beloved by both locals and tourists.

We ordered a serving of their signature roast suckling pig rice. At first glance, it reminded me of Taiwan's Chiayi turkey rice—simple, with a plate of rice topped with sliced meat and sides. It cost less than **USD$3**, which was really affordable.

However, the meat we got was a bit cool, so the texture wasn't as good as expected, and overall it wasn't particularly impressive. Of course, everyone's experience is different, so if you're nearby, it's still worth checking out this famous local spot.

***

On the way to the airport, Benny took out a traditional Indonesian liquor he highly recommended called **Arak** and let us try some.

Seeing it in a plastic bottle, my first reaction was a bit skeptical about its origins (laughs), but Benny said it's a common traditional liquor brewed by locals.

Arak is traditionally made by fermenting and distilling ingredients like coconut palm sap. It has a subtle rice and alcohol aroma—personally, I find it somewhat similar to Taiwan's millet wine. It goes down smoothly without being too harsh, surprisingly quite enjoyable, and it's another way to experience Bali's local culture.

***

This **five-day, four-night Bali free-and-easy trip** truly left us with many wonderful memories.

Although we visited during the **December rainy season**, we only encountered heavy rain for about one day, which didn't affect our itinerary much. We didn't deliberately pack our schedule full each day but instead left plenty of time to relax, making the trip feel even more comfortable.

Looking back, these five days only scratched the surface of Bali, yet they've already made us fall deeply in love with this island.

The food here is so delicious you'll crave it again, the SPA massages remain a great value, and the Monkey Forest and civet cat interactions were both fascinating. The two villas we stayed in each had their own unique charm and are highly recommended.
