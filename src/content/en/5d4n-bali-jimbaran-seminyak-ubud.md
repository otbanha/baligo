## Day 3

We left Jimbaran and headed to Seminyak, a lively and stylish area. Instead of packing in many sights, we focused on food, shopping, and leisurely strolls, slowly soaking up Seminyak's unique charm that sets it apart from other areas.

For lunch, we stopped at **Kilo Kitchen**, a well-known fusion restaurant that artfully blends European, Latin American, and Asian culinary elements. Every dish has its own character.

Our favorite was the **Crab Dumplings**—thin, delicate wrappers with a sweet, bouncy shrimp-paste-like filling that's incredibly satisfying. They're practically a must-order. The **Beef Tongue Tacos** were also memorable, with tender braised tongue layered over corn tortillas for rich texture. The **Squid Ink Risotto** was packed with seafood aroma, while the **Beef Rendang Pasta** surprisingly paired Indonesian rendang spices with Italian noodles perfectly. If you enjoy trying different flavors, I'd say Kilo Kitchen is worth a meal.

After lunch, we headed to **Bintang Supermarket** to pick up souvenirs. It's a popular shopping spot among independent travelers, with a wide selection of goods—from snacks, coffee, and seasonings to daily essentials.

This time, we bought **Woca chocolate**, Indonesian beer, **Marubali coconut chips**, civet coffee, Indonesian instant noodles, and other popular items. Our biggest takeaway: if you're short on time, you can grab most of your souvenirs in one stop here, saving you from running around—super convenient for planning.

Next, we continued to **Seminyak Village** for some shopping. Though not a huge mall, it's comfortable and has stylish brands, perfect for a relaxed afternoon.

At **Sensatia Botanicals**, we picked up facial scrub and cleanser. Their natural botanical skincare line is highly recommended by many travelers and is a popular Bali brand. After browsing a few clothing stores, we found a café to sit down, enjoy some coffee, cool off with AC, and sort through our haul.

If you're not into serious shopping, I'd suggest spending **1 to 1.5 hours** at Seminyak Village—just enough to browse shops, sip coffee, and soak up Seminyak's laid-back yet trendy resort vibe.

After exploring Seminyak, it was time for one of the day's most anticipated activities: checking into our second hotel!

This time we stayed at **Berry Amour Romantic Villas**, with a nightly rate of around USD$226. This property focuses on a romantic getaway atmosphere, making it perfect for couples, newlyweds, or honeymooners.

During check-in, the hotel arranged a small welcome ceremony full of ritual. The staff asked us to put bracelets on each other and even provided crowns for us to wear while taking photos. Though a bit shy-making, it added a special memory to our stay.

After completing the formalities, a staff member drove us to our villa in a golf cart. From the moment we stepped inside, we felt a sense of seclusion from the outside world.

The villa came with a private pool, a small kitchen, and a small soaking tub where we could relax. What we liked most was that the bathroom was indoors, unlike the previous villa which had a semi-outdoor setup. This meant no worries about wind, rain, or mosquitoes while showering—much more comfortable overall.

If we had to choose between the two villas we stayed in on this trip, we personally preferred **Berry Amour Romantic Villas**. The overall layout, comfort, and romantic ambiance suited our tastes better.

## Day Four

On the fourth day, we arranged a full-day private car charter to explore different spots in central Bali. But before heading out, we couldn't miss the floating breakfast offered by the villa!

Although the villa's breakfast overall didn't quite suit our tastes as much as the previous one, staying in a villa makes trying a floating breakfast highly recommended. Placing the tray on the pool, enjoying breakfast while snapping photos—it really adds a ceremonial feel to the vacation. That said, purely in terms of the food, we actually liked the congee the most. Its flavor was surprisingly good, which definitely boosted the breakfast's appeal.

After eating, we officially kicked off the day's charter itinerary.

Our first stop was **Sangeh Monkey Forest**.

When it comes to Bali's monkey forests, most travelers first think of the Sacred Monkey Forest in Ubud. But this time, we specifically chose Sangeh Monkey Forest. Its biggest advantage is the relatively smaller number of visitors, making the experience less crowded and allowing for a more relaxed interaction with the monkeys.

Upon entering the area, a local guide accompanied us, handing out a bag of peanuts before leading us deep into the forest. The guide didn't just show the way—they taught us how to properly feed the monkeys, explained their habits, and shared many small details to watch out for when interacting with them.

The most fun part was that the guide would occasionally make specific sounds to attract the monkeys. Before long, monkeys would voluntarily jump onto shoulders, arms, or nearby trees, giving us plenty of opportunities for interaction and photos. The whole process felt natural, and you could tell the guide was very familiar with these monkeys.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548550073-mixcollage-27-jun-2026-03-16-pm-9129.jpg)

(Provided by Dr. Huang)

We really loved this experience. The whole tour lasted about an hour—not too long, and not rushed at all. If you want to avoid the crowds at the Ubud Monkey Forest, I think this is a great alternative worth considering.

The entrance fee was about **USD$10**, and we also bought a souvenir photo taken at the park, plus a tip for the guide, which came to around **USD$6**. For the overall experience, we felt it was well worth it and one of the most memorable spots on this trip.

After leaving **Sangeh Monkey Forest**, we continued on to one of the most popular areas on this trip—Ubud.

For lunch, we stopped at **Sun Sun Warung**, a budget-friendly Indonesian restaurant recommended by many travelers. The setting is simple, but the food is impressive and very affordable.

We ordered their signature **Crispy Duck Rice** and **Mie Goreng (Indonesian fried noodles)**—both were satisfying. The crispy duck had a crunchy skin while the meat remained tender, and it paired perfectly with Indonesian chili sauce for extra depth. The Mie Goreng was full of rich Southeast Asian aromas, savory and flavorful without being too greasy. For two people, the total bill was only about **USD$16**, which is great value for Ubud. If you love authentic Indonesian cuisine, I highly recommend stopping by.

After lunch, we took a leisurely stroll through Ubud's lively streets, passing by the famous **Ubud Palace** along the way. The streets are lined with unique shops, handicraft stores, and cafés, giving the whole area a wonderful vacation vibe.

However, the weather that day was scorching hot, and we soon decided to find a place to cool off.

We stopped at **Tukies Coconut Shop**, a well-known coconut dessert spot. Although there was no air conditioning, they offered a variety of coconut-based drinks and desserts—perfect for a refreshing break on a hot afternoon.

We ordered **coconut ice, coconut lemonade, and an American coffee**. The icy coconut drinks were incredibly refreshing and instantly chased away the heat. If you're sweating from exploring Ubud, I highly recommend popping in for a drink and a rest.

After our break, we discovered a shop selling **Kopi Luwak (civet coffee)**. The best part? We could actually interact with real civet cats up close!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548311880-luwak.jpg)

The civet cats at the shop were much larger than I expected, weighing about **10 kilograms**. The staff even let us hold them for photos, which was quite a unique experience.

Of course, since we were there, we also ordered a cup of civet coffee to try. It was incredibly smooth, with a mild bitterness, and went down easily. It felt like checking off a must-do experience for many visitors to Bali.

After walking around, our feet started protesting, so we headed to the nearby **Ubud Traditional Spa** to unwind.

This time, we chose the **Rice Farmer Massage** package, which includes a **60-minute Rice Farmer Massage** plus a **30-minute foot massage**, priced at about **NT$800 (USD$26)** per person.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548370433-spa.jpg)

(Provided by Dr. Huang)

The massage therapist's pressure wasn't as precise as the SPA we experienced a few days earlier, but the overall service, ambiance, and price were excellent. For Ubud's city center, the value for money is quite high. If you're planning a full day of shopping and sightseeing in Ubud, ending with a relaxing massage before heading back to the hotel makes for a very comfortable itinerary.

After wrapping up our time in Ubud, we returned to Seminyak, ready to cap off this fulfilling day with a great meal.

For dinner, we chose **Pison**, a popular Western-style restaurant with a diverse menu and live band performances in the evening. The atmosphere was very relaxed, perfect for a leisurely dinner with friends or a partner.

As soon as we sat down, we ordered the restaurant's signature drink, **Avocado Espresso**. I was initially skeptical about the combination of avocado and espresso, but one sip was truly surprising—the creamy richness of the avocado paired unexpectedly well with the coffee. I highly recommend giving it a try if you get the chance.

For our main courses, we ordered the **steak with truffle mushroom sauce** and the **truffle carbonara pasta**—both were impressively executed, with a rich truffle aroma that wasn't overwhelming, and the flavors had great depth. Paired with a live band performance, it made for an especially relaxing dinner and became one of the most memorable meals of the trip.

## Day 5

Before we knew it, we had reached the last day of our Bali trip.

We didn't pack too much into the schedule. After checking out, we asked our driver **Benny** to take us to a local eatery he recommended: **Warung Babi Guling Pak Malen**.

This is a well-known roast suckling pig restaurant, and around noon it was nearly full—clearly a favorite among both locals and tourists.

We ordered their signature roast suckling pig rice. At first glance, it reminded me of Taiwan's Chiayi turkey rice—simple, with a plate of rice topped with sliced meat and sides. It cost less than **USD$3**, which was really affordable.

However, the meat we had was slightly cool, so the texture wasn't as good as expected, and overall it didn't blow us away. Of course, everyone's experience is different—if you happen to be nearby, it's still worth checking out this famous local spot.

***

On the way to the airport, Benny even brought out a traditional Indonesian liquor he highly recommended called **Arak** for us to try.

When I first saw it in a plastic bottle, I honestly had my doubts about its origins (laughs), but Benny said it's a common traditional liquor brewed by locals.

Arak is traditionally made by fermenting and distilling ingredients like coconut flower nectar. It has a subtle rice and alcohol aroma, and to me, it felt a bit like Taiwan's millet wine—smooth and not too harsh, surprisingly quite enjoyable. It was also a great way to experience another facet of Balinese local culture.

***

This **five-day, four-night Bali free-and-easy trip** truly left us with many wonderful memories.

Although we visited during the **December rainy season**, we only encountered heavy rain for about one day, which didn't affect our itinerary much. We didn't cram our schedule full this time, instead leaving plenty of time to relax, which made the trip even more enjoyable.

Looking back, these five days only let us explore a small part of Bali, yet we've already fallen deeply in love with this island.

The food here makes you want to savor it again, the SPA massages remain a high-value indulgence, and the Monkey Forest and civet cat interactions were both fascinating. The two villas we stayed in each had their own charm and are highly recommendable.
