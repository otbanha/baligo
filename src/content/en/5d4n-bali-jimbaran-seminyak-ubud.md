## Day 3

We left Jimbaran and headed to Seminyak, a lively and stylish area. Instead of packing in many attractions, we focused on food, shopping, and leisurely strolls, slowly soaking up Seminyak's unique charm that sets it apart from other areas.

For lunch, we went to **Kilo Kitchen**, a well-known fusion restaurant that masterfully blends European, Latin American, and Asian culinary elements. Every dish has its own distinct character.

Our favorite was the **Crab Dumplings**—thin, tender wrappers with a filling that tasted like sweet, bouncy shrimp paste, making them an absolute must-order. The **Beef Tongue Taco** was also memorable, with the tongue braised until incredibly tender and paired with a corn tortilla for rich layers of flavor. The **Squid Ink Risotto** was packed with a deep seafood aroma, while the **Rendang Beef Pasta** creatively combined Indonesia's classic rendang spices with Italian pasta, surprisingly harmonious. If you enjoy trying different flavors, I'd say Kilo Kitchen is worth a meal.

After eating, we headed to **Bintang Supermarket** to pick up souvenirs. It's a popular shopping spot recommended by many independent travelers, with a wide selection of goods—from snacks, coffee, and seasonings to daily necessities, you can find almost everything.

This time, we bought **Woca chocolate**, Indonesian beer, **Marubali coconut chips**, civet coffee, Indonesian instant noodles, and other popular items. After browsing, our biggest takeaway was that if you're short on time, you can grab most of your souvenirs in one stop here without needing to visit multiple places—super convenient for itinerary planning.

Next, we continued to **Seminyak Village** for some shopping. While it's not a huge mall, the environment is comfortable and the brands are stylish, making it perfect for a leisurely afternoon.

At **Sensatia Botanicals**, we bought facial scrub and cleanser. Their natural botanical skincare products are highly recommended by many travelers and are a popular Bali brand. After that, we browsed a few clothing stores and finally found a café to sit down, enjoy a coffee, cool off with the air conditioning, and sort through our purchases.

If you're not looking for serious shopping, I'd suggest spending **1 to 1.5 hours** at Seminyak Village—just enough to browse the shops, grab a coffee, and soak up Seminyak's relaxed yet trendy vacation vibe.

After exploring Seminyak, it was time for one of the most anticipated activities of the day—checking into our second hotel!

We stayed at **Berry Amour Romantic Villas**, with a nightly rate of around USD$230. This hotel is all about a romantic vacation vibe, perfect for couples, newlyweds, or honeymooners.

During check-in, the hotel arranged a small, ceremonial welcome activity. The staff asked us to put bracelets on each other and even prepared crowns for us to wear while taking photos. Though a bit shy, it added a special memory to our stay.

After completing the formalities, a staff member drove us to our villa in a golf cart. From the moment we stepped inside, we felt a sense of seclusion from the outside world, perfect for a vacation.

The villa came with a private pool, a small kitchen, and a small soaking tub for relaxation. What pleased us most was that the bathroom was indoors, unlike the previous villa's semi-outdoor design, so we didn't have to worry about wind, rain, or mosquitoes while showering—making it much more comfortable overall.

If we had to choose between the two villas we stayed in during this trip, we personally prefer **Berry Amour Romantic Villas**. Its overall layout, comfort, and romantic atmosphere suited our tastes better.

## Day 4

On the fourth day, we arranged a full-day private car service to explore various attractions in central Bali. But before setting off, we couldn't miss the floating breakfast offered by the villa!

Although the breakfast at this villa wasn't quite as satisfying as the previous one overall, since we were staying at a villa, I'd still highly recommend trying the floating breakfast at least once. Placing the tray on the pool, enjoying breakfast while snapping photos—it truly adds a sense of vacation ritual. However, purely in terms of the food, we actually liked the congee the most; its flavor was surprisingly good, which really boosted the meal's appeal.

After breakfast, we officially kicked off our private car itinerary for the day.

Our first stop was **Sangeh Monkey Forest**.

When it comes to monkey forests in Bali, most travelers immediately think of the Sacred Monkey Forest in Ubud, but this time we specifically chose Sangeh Monkey Forest. Its biggest advantage is that there are far fewer visitors, making the experience less crowded and allowing for a more relaxed interaction with the monkeys.

Upon entering the park, a local guide accompanied us, handing out a bag of peanuts and leading us deep into the forest. The guide didn't just show the way; they also taught us how to properly feed the monkeys, explained their behavior, and shared many tips on interacting with them safely.

The most fun part was that the guide would occasionally make specific sounds to attract the monkeys. Before long, monkeys would jump onto our shoulders, arms, or nearby trees, giving us plenty of opportunities for interaction and photos. The whole process felt very natural, and it was clear the guide was quite familiar with the monkeys.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548550073-mixcollage-27-jun-2026-03-16-pm-9129.jpg)

(Photo provided by Dr. Huang)

We really loved this experience. The entire tour lasted about an hour—not too long, and never feeling rushed. If you want to avoid the crowds at Ubud's Sacred Monkey Forest, I think this is a great alternative worth considering.

The entrance fee was about **USD$10**, and we also bought a commemorative photo taken at the park, plus a tip for the guide, which added up to roughly another **USD$6**. Overall, we felt it was well worth it and one of the most memorable spots on this trip.

After leaving **Sangeh Monkey Forest**, we continued on to one of the most popular areas of the trip—Ubud.

For lunch, we stopped at **Sun Sun Warung**, a budget-friendly Indonesian restaurant recommended by many travelers. The setting was simple, but the food was impressive and very reasonably priced.

We ordered the signature **Crispy Duck Rice** and **Mie Goreng (Indonesian Fried Noodles)**, and both were satisfying. The crispy duck had a crunchy skin while the meat remained tender, and pairing it with Indonesian chili sauce added extra depth. The Mie Goreng was rich with Southeast Asian aromas—savory and flavorful without being too greasy. For two people, the total came to just about **USD$16**, which is great value for Ubud. If you enjoy authentic Indonesian cuisine, I highly recommend stopping by.

After eating, we strolled through Ubud's town center, wandering along the lively streets and passing by the famous **Ubud Palace**. The streets are lined with unique shops, handicraft stores, and cafés, giving the area a wonderful vacation vibe.

However, the weather that day was scorching hot, so after a short walk, we decided to find a place to cool down.

We headed to **Tukies Coconut Shop**, a well-known coconut dessert specialty store. Although there was no air conditioning, they offered a variety of coconut-based drinks and desserts, perfect for a refreshing break on a hot afternoon.

We ordered **coconut ice, a coconut lemon drink, and an American coffee**. The icy coconut beverages were incredibly refreshing and instantly chased away the heat. If you're sweating from exploring Ubud, I highly recommend stopping in to rehydrate and rest.

After our break, we discovered a shop selling **Kopi Luwak (civet coffee)**. The most unique part? We could actually interact with real civet cats up close!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548311880-luwak.jpg)

The civet cats in the shop were much larger than I expected—around **10 kilograms** each. The staff even let us hold one for photos, which was quite a special experience.

Of course, since I was there, I also ordered a cup of civet coffee to try. The texture was incredibly smooth, with a mild bitterness, making it quite pleasant to drink. It also fulfilled one of those experiences many people want to have when visiting Bali.

After walking around for a while, my feet started protesting, so we headed to the nearby **Ubud Traditional Spa** to unwind.

This time, we chose the **Rice Farmer Massage** package, which includes a **60-minute Rice Farmer Massage** plus a **30-minute foot massage**, priced at about **USD$26** per person.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548370433-spa.jpg)

(Provided by Dr. Huang)

The massage therapist's pressure wasn't as precise as the spas we tried earlier in the trip, but the overall service, ambiance, and price were excellent. For Ubud's city center, the value for money is quite high. If you're planning a full day of shopping and sightseeing in Ubud, ending with a relaxing massage before heading back to the hotel makes for a wonderfully comfortable itinerary.

After wrapping up our time in Ubud, we returned to Seminyak, ready to cap off this fulfilling day with a great meal.

For dinner, we chose **Pison**, a popular Western-style restaurant with a diverse menu. In the evening, they had a live band performing, creating a very relaxed atmosphere—perfect for a leisurely dinner with friends or a partner.

Once seated, we ordered the restaurant's signature drink, **Avocado Espresso**. At first, it was hard to imagine the combination of avocado and espresso, but one sip was truly surprising. The rich, creamy avocado paired unexpectedly well with the coffee's aroma—highly recommend giving it a try if you get the chance.

For our main courses, we ordered the **Steak with Truffle Mushroom Sauce** and the **Truffle Carbonara Pasta**. Both were excellent—the truffle aroma was rich but not overpowering, with well-layered flavors. Paired with the live band's performance, the dinner felt especially delightful and became one of the most memorable meals of the trip.

## Day 5

Before we knew it, we had arrived at the last day of this Bali trip.

We didn’t pack much into the schedule. After checking out, we asked our driver **Benny** to take us to a local eatery he recommended: **Warung Babi Guling Pak Malen**.

This is a well-known roast suckling pig restaurant, and around noon it was nearly packed—clearly a favorite among both locals and tourists.

We ordered their signature roast suckling pig rice. At first glance, it reminded me of Chiayi turkey rice in Taiwan—simple, with a plate of rice topped with sliced meat and sides. One portion cost less than **USD$3**, which is really affordable.

But this time, the meat was a bit cool, so the texture wasn’t as good as expected, and overall it didn’t blow us away. Of course, tastes vary—if you happen to be nearby, it’s still worth checking out this famous local spot.

***

On the way to the airport, Benny even brought out a traditional Indonesian liquor he highly recommended called **Arak** for us to try.

When we saw it in a plastic bottle, our first reaction was a bit skeptical (laughs), but Benny said it’s a common homemade traditional spirit among locals.

Arak is traditionally made by fermenting and distilling ingredients like coconut blossom nectar. It has a subtle rice and alcohol aroma, somewhat reminiscent of Taiwan's millet wine. It goes down smoothly without being too harsh, surprisingly quite enjoyable—a great way to experience another facet of Balinese local culture.

***

This **five-day, four-night Bali independent trip** truly left us with many wonderful memories.

Although we visited during the **December rainy season**, we only encountered heavy rain for about one day, which didn't disrupt our plans much. We didn't cram our schedule full but instead left plenty of time to relax, making the trip even more enjoyable.

Looking back, these five days only scratched the surface of Bali, yet we've already fallen deeply in love with this island.

The food here is so delicious you'll crave it again, the spa massages remain a high-value indulgence, and the Monkey Forest and civet cat interactions were both fascinating. The two villas we stayed in each had their own charm and come highly recommended.
