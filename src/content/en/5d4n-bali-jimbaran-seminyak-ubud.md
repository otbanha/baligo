## Day Three

We left Jimbaran and headed to Seminyak, a lively area full of trendy vibes. We didn't pack the day with too many sights; instead, we focused on food, shopping, and leisurely strolls to slowly soak up Seminyak's unique charm, so different from other parts of the island.

For lunch, we stopped by **Kilo Kitchen**, a well-known fusion restaurant that artfully blends European, Latin American, and Asian culinary elements. Every dish had its own distinct character.

Our favorite was the **Crab Dumplings**—thin, delicate wrappers with a filling that tasted like sweet, bouncy shrimp paste. The texture was delightful, making it an almost must-order item. Besides that, the **Beef Tongue Taco** was also memorable; the tongue was braised until incredibly tender, and pairing it with the corn tortilla created a rich, layered experience. The **Squid Ink Risotto** was packed with a deep seafood aroma, while the **Rendang Beef Pasta** cleverly combined Indonesia's classic rendang spices with Italian pasta, resulting in a surprisingly harmonious dish. If you enjoy trying different flavor profiles, I think Kilo Kitchen is worth a meal.

After eating, we headed to **Bintang Supermarket** to pick up souvenirs. It's a shopping spot often recommended by independent travelers, with a very comprehensive selection of goods—from snacks, coffee, and seasonings to everyday items, you can find almost everything.

This time, we bought popular items like **Woca chocolate**, Indonesian beer, **Marubali coconut chips**, civet coffee, and Indonesian instant noodles. After browsing, our biggest takeaway was that if you're short on time, you can grab most of your souvenirs in one stop here without needing to run around to multiple places—super convenient for itinerary planning.

Next, we continued to **Seminyak Village** for some shopping. While it's not a huge mall, the environment is comfortable and the brands have a nice, quality feel—perfect for a relaxed afternoon.

At **Sensatia Botanicals**, we bought a facial scrub and cleanser. Their natural plant-based skincare products are frequently recommended by travelers and are a popular Balinese brand. After that, we browsed a few clothing stores and finally settled into a café to rest, sipping coffee and enjoying the air conditioning while sorting through our haul.

If you're not looking for serious retail therapy, I think spending **1 to 1.5 hours** at Seminyak Village is just right—enough time to leisurely explore the shops, grab a coffee, and soak up Seminyak's relaxed yet stylish vacation atmosphere.

After exploring Seminyak, we moved on to one of the most anticipated parts of the day—checking into our second hotel!

This time we stayed at **Berry Amour Romantic Villas**, with a nightly rate of around USD$230. This villa is all about a romantic getaway vibe, perfect for couples, newlyweds, or honeymooners.

During check-in, the hotel arranged a small, ceremonial welcome activity. The staff had us put bracelets on each other and even provided crowns for photos. It was a bit shy-making, but it added a special touch to the experience.

Once the formalities were done, a staff member drove us to our villa in a golf cart. From the moment we stepped inside, it felt like a secluded retreat away from the outside world.

The villa came with a private pool, a small kitchen, and a soaking tub nearby for relaxation. What we loved most was that the bathroom was indoors, unlike the previous villa’s semi-outdoor setup. No worries about wind, rain, or bugs—much more comfortable overall.

If we had to choose between the two villas on this trip, we personally preferred **Berry Amour Romantic Villas**. The layout, comfort, and romantic atmosphere suited our tastes better.

## Day 4

On Day 4, we arranged a full-day private car charter to explore different spots in central Bali. But before heading out, we couldn't miss the villa's floating breakfast!

Although the breakfast at this villa wasn't quite as much to our taste as the previous one, staying at a villa makes trying a floating breakfast highly recommended. Placing the tray on the pool, enjoying the meal while snapping photos—it really adds a ceremonial vacation vibe. That said, purely in terms of the food, we liked the congee the most; its flavor was surprisingly good and really boosted the breakfast experience.

After eating, we officially kicked off the day's charter itinerary.

Our first stop was **Sangeh Monkey Forest**.

When it comes to Bali's monkey forests, most travelers first think of Ubud's Sacred Monkey Forest, but this time we specifically chose Sangeh Monkey Forest. Its biggest advantage is that there are far fewer visitors, making the experience less crowded and allowing for more relaxed interactions with the monkeys.

Upon entering the park, a local guide accompanied us, handing out a bag of peanuts before leading us deep into the forest. The guide didn't just show the way—they taught us how to properly feed the monkeys, explained their habits, and shared many tips on interacting with them safely.

The most fun part was when the guide made specific sounds to attract the monkeys. Before long, monkeys would jump onto our shoulders, arms, or nearby trees, giving us plenty of chances to interact and take photos. The whole experience felt natural, and you could tell the guide was very familiar with these monkeys.

![第四天](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548550073-mixcollage-27-jun-2026-03-16-pm-9129.jpg)

We absolutely loved this itinerary—the entire experience lasted about an hour, which felt neither too long nor rushed. If you're looking to avoid the crowds at the Ubud Monkey Forest, I think this is a great alternative worth considering.

The entrance fee this time was about **USD$10**, and we also bought a commemorative photo taken at the park, plus a tip for the guide, which came to another **USD$6**. Overall, we felt it was well worth it and one of the most memorable spots on this trip.

After leaving **Sangeh Monkey Forest**, we continued on to one of the most popular areas of the journey—Ubud.

For lunch, we stopped at **Sun Sun Warung**, a budget-friendly Indonesian restaurant recommended by many travelers. The setting is simple, but the food is impressive and very reasonably priced.

We ordered the signature **Crispy Duck Rice** and **Mie Goreng (Indonesian Fried Noodles)**—both were satisfying. The crispy duck had a crunchy skin while the meat remained tender, and pairing it with Indonesian chili sauce added extra depth. The Mie Goreng was rich with Southeast Asian aromas, savory and flavorful without being too greasy. For two people, the bill came to just about **USD$16**, which is great value for Ubud. If you enjoy authentic Indonesian cuisine, I highly recommend giving it a try.

After eating, we strolled casually through Ubud's streets, wandering along the lively lanes and passing by the famous **Ubud Palace**. The main street is lined with unique shops, handicraft stores, and cafes, giving the area a wonderful vacation vibe.

However, the weather that day was scorching hot, and before long, we decided to find a place to cool down.

We headed to **Tukies Coconut Shop**, a well-known dessert spot specializing in coconut treats. Though the shop has no air conditioning, it offers a variety of coconut-based drinks and desserts—perfect for a refreshing break on a hot afternoon.

This time, we ordered **coconut ice, a coconut lemon drink, and an American coffee**. The icy coconut beverages were incredibly refreshing and instantly chased away the heat. If you're sweating it out while exploring Ubud, I highly recommend stopping by to rehydrate and take a rest.

After resting, we stumbled upon a shop selling **Kopi Luwak (civet coffee)**. What made it special was that we could actually interact with real civets up close.

![第四天](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1782548311880-luwak.jpg)

The civets in the shop were much larger than I expected—around **10 kilograms** each. The staff even let us hold them for photos, which was quite a unique experience.

Of course, since we were there, we ordered a cup of civet coffee to try. It was incredibly smooth, not too bitter, and went down easily—checking off another must-do for many visitors to Bali.

After walking around, our feet started to protest, so we headed to the nearby **Ubud Traditional Spa** for some well-deserved relaxation.

We chose the **Rice Farmer Massage** package, which includes a **60-minute Rice Farmer Massage** plus a **30-minute foot massage**, priced at about **USD$26** per person.

The massage therapist's pressure wasn't as precise as the spas we'd visited earlier, but the overall service, ambiance, and price were excellent—offering great value for Ubud's city center. If you're planning a full day of shopping and sightseeing in Ubud, ending with a relaxing massage before heading back to the hotel makes for a wonderfully comfortable itinerary.

After wrapping up our time in Ubud, we returned to Seminyak, ready to cap off the fulfilling day with a delicious meal.

For dinner, we chose **Pison**, a popular Western restaurant with a diverse menu and live band performances in the evening. The overall vibe was very relaxed, making it a great spot to enjoy a leisurely meal with friends or a partner.

As soon as we sat down, we ordered the restaurant's signature drink, **Avocado Espresso**. I was a bit skeptical about the combination of avocado and espresso at first, but one sip was truly surprising—the creamy richness of the avocado paired unexpectedly well with the coffee's aroma. I highly recommend giving it a try if you get the chance.

For our main courses, we ordered the **Steak with Truffle Mushroom Sauce** and the **Truffle Carbonara Pasta**. Both dishes were impressive, with a rich truffle aroma that wasn't overpowering and well-layered flavors. Paired with the live band performance, it made for an especially relaxing dinner and one of the most memorable meals of the trip.

## Day 5

Before we knew it, it was the last day of our Bali trip.

We didn't pack the day with too many plans. After checking out, we asked our driver **Benny** to take us to a local eatery he recommended: **Warung Babi Guling Pak Malen**.

This is a well-known suckling pig restaurant, and around noon it was almost completely full, showing how popular it is with both locals and tourists.

We ordered a signature roast suckling pig rice. At first glance, it reminded me of Taiwan's Chiayi turkey rice—simple, with a plate of rice topped with sliced meat and sides. It cost less than **USD$3**, which was really affordable.

However, the meat we got was a bit cool, so the texture wasn't as good as expected, and overall it wasn't particularly impressive. Of course, everyone's experience is different, so if you're nearby, it's still worth checking out this famous local spot.

***

On the way to the airport, Benny also brought out a traditional Indonesian liquor called **Arak** that he highly recommended for us to try.

When I first saw it in a plastic bottle, I honestly had my doubts about its origins (laughs), but Benny said it's a common traditional liquor brewed by the locals.

Arak is traditionally made by fermenting and distilling ingredients like coconut flower nectar. It has a subtle rice and alcohol aroma—personally, it reminded me a bit of Taiwan's millet wine. Smooth and not too harsh, it was surprisingly enjoyable and felt like another way to experience Bali's local culture.

***

This **five-day, four-night Bali trip** truly left us with so many wonderful memories.

Although we visited during the **December rainy season**, we only encountered heavy rain for about one day, which didn't disrupt our plans much. We didn't cram the schedule full either, leaving plenty of time to relax, which made the trip feel even more comfortable.

Looking back, we only explored a small part of Bali in those five days, yet it was enough to make us fall deeply in love with this island.

The food here is so delicious you'll crave it again, the SPA massages remain a fantastic value, and the Monkey Forest and civet cat interactions were both a lot of fun. The two villas we stayed in each had their own charm and are highly recommended.
