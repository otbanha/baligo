## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Tabanan's Nuanu Creative City Opens Up Strategically, Welcoming a New Public Park Landmark This Summer

- **CEO Confirms 'Nuanu Park' Will Open Within Weeks, Creating a More Accessible, No-Barrier Public Space**:
Located along the coast north of Canggu, the sprawling 44-hectare green tech and art hub—**Nuanu Creative City—has announced the official opening of a new public park, 'Nuanu Park,' this summer, serving as the first stop for visitors**. In an interview, Nuanu CEO Lev Kroll stated that with the recent completion of Luna Beach Club, Aurora Media Park, and the Southern Hemisphere's first wolf sanctuary lodge, the new park aims to make the creative city 'more open, everyday-friendly, and easily accessible to the public,' shedding its past image of being overly serious, closed-off, or highly commercialized.
- **2026 Bali International Photography Festival (FOTO Bali) Is Now Underway at Nuanu**:
As a strategic prelude to this summer's grand opening, **Nuanu Creative City is currently hosting the island's only major international photography event—the 2026 FOTO Bali Festival (running from June 3 to 7)**. This year, the organizers have garnered significant international acclaim, receiving nearly 700 photography submissions from 80 countries worldwide. A renowned curatorial team has selected 36 top artists from 24 countries to showcase their work across the park under the core theme 'Afterimage.' Daily entry tickets start at an affordable 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah, which already includes access to Nuanu's grounds, making it a top cultural pick for travelers exploring Tabanan in early June.

## 📄 Topics of Interest to Travelers: Player Preparations for the September World Pickleball Championship (WPC)

- **Ushering in a New Wave of 'Racket Sports Tourism'! The 5th WPC Pickleball World Championship Lands in Sanur**:
The world's fastest-growing sport, blending tennis and badminton—**the 'World Pickleball Championship (WPC)' will be held from September 20 to 26 at the Liga Tennis Sports Club in Sanur**. Organizers reveal this will be the largest edition in the event's history, expected to attract a wave of international professional players and hundreds of social fitness-loving independent travelers to Sanur.
- **Sanur's Infrastructure Gets a Major Upgrade, Becoming a Key Hub for High-End 'Sports and Wellness Tourism' on the Island**:
Sports analysts point out that the WPC's choice of Sanur is largely due to the area's recent infrastructure upgrades, including new shuttle bus systems, revamped coastal promenades, and a mature international medical and wellness zone. The arrival of this high-profile event not only brings a new wave of quality long-stay visitors during Sanur's traditional low season in September but also steadily positions Bali's southeastern coast as a globally competitive benchmark destination for 'sports and wellness vacations (Wellcation).'
