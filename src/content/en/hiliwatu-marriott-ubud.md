On January 15, 2026, Ubud, Bali, welcomed a striking new landmark—**Hiliwatu, Bali Ubud**, the latest addition to Marriott International's Tribute Portfolio in Indonesia. Nestled in the village of Bresela, about 9 kilometers north of Ubud, this hotel introduces a fresh chapter to Bali's boutique hotel scene with its unique architectural design, deep cultural connections, and commitment to sustainability.

![烏布新地標開幕：Hiliwatu 萬豪精選酒店打造峇里島奢華新體驗](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b650a19390bad70b88c0d6c9c06bd150.jpg)

## The Story Behind the Name

The name "Hiliwatu" itself is rich with local meaning. In Balinese, "Hili" means "hill" and "Watu" means "stone." This name not only reflects the natural topography of the hotel's location but also symbolizes a profound connection to the earth—a core principle that drives the entire design philosophy of the property.

## Unique Architecture and Spatial Design

### A Breathtaking Arrival Experience

Unlike the traditional entrances of most hotels, Hiliwatu features a dedicated **helipad**, allowing guests to take in 360-degree panoramic views of Ubud from the air, redefining the very concept of "arrival." As the helicopter descends, guests are treated to unfolding vistas of layered rice terraces, lush tropical rainforests, and the distant, hazy silhouette of volcanoes.

### A 24-Meter-High Lobby

Stepping into the hotel, the first thing that catches the eye is the **stunning 24-meter-high lobby**. This space features sculptural architectural language paired with cascading water features, where light and shadow dance across the water's surface, creating an atmosphere that is both serene and awe-inspiring. The designers have skillfully blended modern architectural aesthetics with traditional Balinese elements, allowing every visitor to feel the space's spirituality and power.

### The Pavilion: An Open-Air Chapel

The resort features a versatile open-air space called **The Pavilion**, arguably one of the most thoughtfully designed spots on the property. In the early morning, it serves as a serene sanctuary for yoga enthusiasts; at dusk, it transforms into a romantic dining venue; and for couples dreaming of a Bali wedding, it offers a magical ceremony setting.

### Accommodation: 38 Unique Units

Spanning 26,000 square meters, the resort houses only **38 guest units**, ensuring ample privacy for every guest. Accommodation options include:

- **24 Suites** – ideal for couples or small families
- **12 One-Bedroom Villas** – offering independent space and privacy
- **1 Three-Bedroom Villa** – perfect for large families or groups of friends
- **1 Four-Bedroom Villa** – top-tier space and luxury experience

### Culinary Delights: From Bali to the Mediterranean

Hiliwatu's dining options showcase a deep respect for local ingredients and a pursuit of culinary artistry. All restaurants emphasize the use of locally sourced seasonal produce, supporting sustainable agriculture.

**Tapis Restaurant**

Focused on presenting the essence of traditional Balinese cuisine, allowing guests to savor the most authentic local flavors.

**Nira Restaurant**

A clever fusion of Balinese and Mediterranean flavors, with seating facing west—an ideal spot to watch the sunset. Imagine savoring a blend of Eastern and Western culinary highlights against the backdrop of an orange-red twilight glow; what a wonderful experience that would be.

**Omber (Signature Restaurant)**

As the resort's signature restaurant, Omber is renowned for its Mediterranean grills and classic steak dishes. Premium cuts of meat, expert cooking techniques, and carefully paired wines deliver an ultimate indulgence for the palate.

**Pool Bar**

Overlooking the sprawling terraced rice fields, sipping a signature cocktail here makes time seem to slow down.

- Mineral therapy – harnesses the natural energy of minerals for deep healing
- Crystal meditation – uses the energy fields of crystals for mindful practice

## Deep Cultural Immersion: More Than Just a Tourist

Perhaps the most touching aspect of Hiliwatu is the deep connection it has forged with the local community. This is not just a luxury hotel, but a **bridge for cultural exchange**. Guests can participate in:

- **Traditional Craft Workshops** – Learn Balinese traditional crafts from local artisans
- **Village Visits** – Gain insight into the daily life of Balinese people
- **Religious Ceremony Experiences** – Observe or take part in unique Balinese rituals
- **Sustainable Agriculture Exploration** – Discover Bali's traditional terraced rice farming system
The hotel also showcases works by emerging Balinese artists, bringing art not just into galleries but into every corner of daily life.

## A Commitment to Sustainability

In an era where climate change and environmental protection are increasingly important, Hiliwatu has integrated sustainability into its core philosophy from the very beginning of its design. From the choice of building materials and energy use to its collaboration model with the local community, everything reflects a respect for both the environment and culture.

## Conclusion

The opening of Hiliwatu, Bali Ubud, injects new vitality into Bali's boutique hotel market. It not only offers a luxurious stay but, more importantly, demonstrates how to maintain respect for local culture and care for the environment while pursuing excellence.
For travelers who want to deeply explore Balinese culture, seek balance for body and mind, yet still enjoy modern comforts, this is undoubtedly a perfect choice. Whether you gaze at the sky from the 24-meter-high lobby or meditate quietly by the poolside among the rice terraces, you'll discover that the true meaning of luxury lies not in material abundance, but in a profound connection with nature, culture, and yourself.
