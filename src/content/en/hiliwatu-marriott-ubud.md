On January 15, 2026, Ubud, Bali welcomed a striking new landmark—**Hiliwatu, Bali Ubud**, the latest addition to Marriott International’s Tribute Portfolio in Indonesia. Nestled in the village of Bresela, about 9 kilometers north of Ubud, this hotel introduces a fresh chapter to Bali’s boutique hotel scene with its unique architectural design, deep cultural connections, and commitment to sustainability.

![New Ubud Landmark Opens: Hiliwatu, a Marriott Tribute Portfo](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b650a19390bad70b88c0d6c9c06bd150.jpg)

## The Story Behind the Name

The name 'Hiliwatu' itself is rich with local meaning. In Balinese, 'Hili' means 'hill' and 'Watu' means 'stone.' This name not only reflects the natural topography of the hotel’s location but also symbolizes a profound connection to the earth—a core principle that shapes the entire design philosophy.

## Unique Architecture and Spatial Design

### A Breathtaking Arrival Experience

Unlike traditional hotel entrances, Hiliwatu features a dedicated **helipad**, allowing guests to take in 360-degree panoramic views of Ubud from the air, redefining the very concept of 'arrival.' As the helicopter descends, layers of rice terraces, lush tropical rainforests, and the distant silhouette of volcanoes unfold before your eyes.

### A 24-Meter-High Lobby

Stepping into the hotel, the first thing that captures your attention is the **stunning 24-meter-high lobby**. This space features sculptural architectural elements paired with cascading water features, where light dances across the water’s surface, creating an atmosphere that is both serene and awe-inspiring. The designers have masterfully blended modern architectural aesthetics with traditional Balinese elements, allowing every visitor to feel the space’s spirituality and power.

### The Pavilion: An Open-Air Chapel

The resort features a versatile open-air space called **The Pavilion**, arguably one of the most thoughtfully designed elements of the entire property. In the early morning, it serves as a tranquil sanctuary for yoga enthusiasts; at dusk, it transforms into a romantic dining venue; and for couples dreaming of a wedding in Bali, it offers a truly enchanting ceremony setting.

### Accommodation: 38 Unique Units

Spanning 26,000 square meters, the entire resort houses only **38 guest units**, ensuring every guest enjoys ample privacy. Accommodation options include:

- **24 Suites** – ideal for couples or small families
- **12 One-Bedroom Villas** – offering independent space and privacy
- **1 Three-Bedroom Villa** – perfect for larger families or groups of friends
- **1 Four-Bedroom Villa** – the ultimate in space and luxury

### Culinary Delights: From Bali to the Mediterranean

Hiliwatu's dining options showcase a deep respect for local ingredients and a pursuit of culinary artistry. All restaurants emphasize the use of locally sourced, seasonal ingredients, supporting sustainable agriculture.

**Tapis Restaurant**

Dedicated to presenting the essence of traditional Balinese cuisine, allowing guests to savor the most authentic local flavors.

**Nira Restaurant**

A clever fusion of Balinese and Mediterranean flavors, with seating facing west—an ideal spot to watch the sunset. Imagine savoring a blend of Eastern and Western culinary delights as the sky glows in shades of orange and red. What a magical experience that would be.

**Omber (Signature Restaurant)**

As the hotel's flagship restaurant, Omber is renowned for its Mediterranean grills and classic steak dishes. Premium cuts of meat, expert cooking techniques, and carefully paired wines deliver an ultimate indulgence for the palate.

**Pool Bar**

Overlooking the endless terraced rice paddies, sipping a signature cocktail here makes time seem to slow down.

**A Healing Journey for Body and Soul: Samya Wellness**

Modern travelers increasingly seek balance and restoration for mind, body, and spirit. Samya Wellness at Hiliwatu offers a comprehensive wellness experience:

- Mineral therapy – harnesses the natural energy of minerals for deep healing
- Crystal meditation – uses the energy fields of crystals for mindful practice
- Outdoor yoga classes – practice yoga in nature's embrace, connecting with the earth and sky

## Deep Cultural Experience: More Than Just a Tourist

What may be most moving about Hiliwatu is the deep connection it has forged with the local community. It’s not just a luxury hotel—it’s a **bridge for cultural exchange**.
Guests can participate in:

- **Traditional craft workshops** – Learn Balinese crafts from local artisans
- **Village visits** – Gain insight into the daily life of Balinese people
- **Religious ceremony experiences** – Observe or take part in Bali’s unique rituals
- **Sustainable agriculture exploration** – Discover Bali’s traditional rice terrace farming system
The hotel also showcases works by emerging Balinese artists, bringing art beyond galleries and into everyday life.

## Commitment to Sustainability

In an era where climate change and environmental protection are increasingly important, Hiliwatu has embedded sustainability into its core philosophy from the very beginning. From the choice of building materials and energy use to its collaboration with local communities, every aspect reflects a deep respect for the environment and culture.

The opening of Hiliwatu, Bali Ubud, injects new energy into Bali’s boutique hotel scene. It offers not only a luxurious stay but also shows how to honor local culture and care for the environment while pursuing excellence.
For travelers who want to deeply explore Balinese culture, seek balance for body and soul, yet still enjoy modern comforts, this is a perfect choice. Standing beneath the 24-meter-high lobby gazing at the sky, or meditating by the pool beside the rice terraces, you’ll realize that true luxury isn’t about material abundance—it’s about a profound connection with nature, culture, and yourself.
