## The Story Behind the Name

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b650a19390bad70b88c0d6c9c06bd150.jpg)

The name 'Hiliwatu' itself is rich in local meaning. In Balinese, 'Hili' means 'hill,' and 'Watu' means 'stone.' This name not only reflects the natural landscape of the hotel's location but also symbolizes a profound connection to the earth—a core concept of the hotel's design philosophy.

## Unique Architecture and Spatial Design

### An Impressive Arrival Experience

Unlike traditional hotel entrances, Hiliwatu features a dedicated **helicopter landing pad**, allowing guests to enjoy a 360-degree panoramic view of Ubud from the air, redefining the very concept of 'arrival.' As the helicopter descends, guests are greeted by terraced rice fields, lush tropical rainforests, and the faint outlines of distant volcanoes.

### A Grand 24-Meter-High Lobby

Upon entering the hotel, guests are immediately struck by the **spectacular 24-meter-high lobby**. This space employs sculptural architectural language, complemented by layered water features where light and shadow dance on the surface, creating an atmosphere that is both serene and awe-inspiring. The designer masterfully blends modern architectural aesthetics with traditional Balinese elements, allowing every visitor to feel the spirituality and power of the space.

### The Pavilion Open-Air Chapel

Within the resort lies a versatile open space called **The Pavilion**, perhaps one of the most thoughtfully designed areas in the entire property. At dawn, it serves as a serene yoga sanctuary; by dusk, it transforms into a romantic dining venue; and for couples dreaming of a Bali wedding, it offers a magical ceremony setting.

### Accommodation Options: 38 Unique Units

Spanning 26,000 square meters, the resort features only **38 guest units**, ensuring ample privacy for every visitor. The accommodation choices include:
- **24 suites** - Perfect for couples or small families
- **12 one-bedroom villas** - Offering independent space and privacy
- **1 three-bedroom villa** - Ideal for larger families or groups of friends
- **1 four-bedroom villa** - The pinnacle of space and luxury experience

### Culinary Delights: From Bali to the Mediterranean

Hiliwatu's dining options showcase a deep respect for local ingredients and a pursuit of culinary artistry. All restaurants emphasize the use of locally sourced, seasonal produce, supporting sustainable agricultural practices.

**Tapis Restaurant**

Focuses on presenting the essence of traditional Balinese cuisine, allowing guests to savor the most authentic local flavors.

**Nira Restaurant**

Skillfully blends Balinese and Mediterranean flavors, with seating facing west for an exceptional sunset view. Imagine enjoying a fusion of Eastern and Western culinary delights amidst the orange hues of the setting sun—what a splendid experience that would be.

**Omber (Signature Restaurant)**

As the hotel's signature restaurant, Omber is renowned for its Mediterranean grill and classic steak dishes. Carefully selected meats, professional cooking techniques, and thoughtfully paired wines create an ultimate dining experience for the palate.

**Pool Bar**

Overlooking the sprawling rice terrace landscape, sipping a specially crafted cocktail here makes time seem to slow down.

## Deep Cultural Experiences: Beyond Tourism

What is perhaps most touching about Hiliwatu is its deep connection with the local community. This is not just a luxury hotel, but a **bridge for cultural exchange**.
Guests can participate in:
- **Traditional Craft Workshops** - Learning Bali's traditional crafts from local artisans
- **Collaborative Village Visits** - Gaining in-depth understanding of Balinese daily life
- **Religious Ceremony Experiences** - Observing or participating in Bali's unique religious rituals
- **Sustainable Agriculture Exploration** - Understanding Bali's traditional rice terrace farming system
The hotel also showcases works by emerging Balinese artists, integrating art into every corner of daily life rather than confining it to galleries.

## Commitment to Sustainable Practices

In today's world where climate change and environmental protection are increasingly emphasized, Hiliwatu has incorporated sustainability into its core philosophy from the very beginning of its design. From the choice of building materials and energy usage to its collaboration model with local communities, it demonstrates respect for both the environment and culture.

## Practical Information

* Official Opening Date: January 15, 2026
* Location: Bresela Village, Gianyar Region, Bali (approximately 9 km north of Ubud)
* Number of Rooms: 38 units

* Official Website: www.hiliwatubaliubud.com

## Conclusion

The opening of Hiliwatu, Bali Ubud injects new vitality into Bali's boutique hotel market. It not only offers a luxurious accommodation experience but, more importantly, showcases how to maintain respect for local culture and environmental care while pursuing excellence.
For travelers who wish to deeply explore Balinese culture, seek physical and spiritual balance, and yet not give up modern comforts, this is undoubtedly a perfect choice. When you stand in the 24-meter-high lobby gazing at the sky or meditate quietly by the poolside amidst terraced fields, you will discover that the true meaning of luxury lies not in material accumulation but in profound connections with nature, culture, and oneself.
