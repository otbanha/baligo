## The Story Behind the Name

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b650a19390bad70b88c0d6c9c06bd150.jpg)

The name 'Hiliwatu' itself is rich in local meaning. In Balinese, 'Hili' means 'hill,' and 'Watu' means 'stone.' This name not only reflects the natural landscape of the hotel's location but also symbolizes a profound connection to the earth—a core concept of the hotel's design philosophy.

## Unique Architecture and Spatial Design

### A Stunning Arrival Experience

Unlike traditional hotel entrances, Hiliwatu features a dedicated **helicopter landing pad**, allowing guests to enjoy a 360-degree panoramic view of Ubud from the air, redefining the very concept of 'arrival.' As the helicopter descends, guests are greeted with terraced rice fields, lush tropical rainforests, and the faint outline of distant volcanoes.

### A 24-Meter High Grand Lobby

Upon entering the hotel, guests are immediately struck by the **grand lobby soaring 24 meters high**. This space employs sculptural architectural language paired with cascading water features, where light dances on the water's surface, creating a serene yet awe-inspiring atmosphere. The designer skillfully blends modern architectural aesthetics with traditional Balinese elements, allowing every visitor to feel the spirituality and power of the space.

步入酒店，首先映入眼簾的是\*\*高達24米的壯觀大廳\*\*。這個空間採用雕塑般的建築語彙，搭配層叠式的水景設計，光影在水面上流轉，營造出既寧靜又震撼的氛圍。設計師巧妙地將現代建築美學與峇里島傳統元素融合，讓每一位訪客都能感受到空間的靈性與力量。

### The Pavilion Open-Air Chapel

Within the resort lies **The Pavilion**, a versatile open-air space that is perhaps one of the most ingenious designs in the entire property. In the early morning, it serves as a serene spot for yoga enthusiasts; by dusk, it transforms into a romantic dining venue; and for couples dreaming of a Bali wedding, it offers a magical setting for their special day.

### Accommodation Choices: 38 Unique Units

Spanning 26,000 square meters, the resort features only **38 guest units**, ensuring ample privacy for every guest. Accommodation options include:

- **24 suites** - Perfect for couples or small families
- **12 one-bedroom villas** - Offering independent space and privacy
- **1 three-bedroom villa** - Ideal for larger families or groups of friends
- **1 four-bedroom villa** - Top-tier space and luxurious experience

### Culinary Delights: From Bali to the Mediterranean

Hiliwatu's dining options showcase a deep respect for local ingredients and a pursuit of culinary artistry. All restaurants emphasize the use of locally sourced, seasonal produce, supporting sustainable agricultural development.

**Tapis Restaurant**

Focused on presenting the essence of traditional Balinese cuisine, allowing guests to savor the most authentic local flavors.

**Nira Restaurant**

Skillfully blending Balinese and Mediterranean flavors, with seating facing west, making it an ideal spot to enjoy the sunset. Imagine savoring a fusion of Eastern and Western culinary delights amidst the orange hues of the setting sun—what a delightful experience that would be.

**Omber (Signature Restaurant)**

As the hotel's signature restaurant, Omber is renowned for its Mediterranean grill and classic steak dishes. With premium meat selections, expert cooking techniques, and carefully paired wines, it offers an ultimate feast for the taste buds.

**Pool Bar**

Overlooking the sprawling rice terrace landscape, sipping a specially crafted cocktail here makes time seem to slow down.

## Deep Cultural Experience: More Than Just a Tourist

What might be most touching about Hiliwatu is its deep connection with the local community. It's not just a luxury hotel, but a **bridge for cultural exchange**. Guests can participate in:

- **Traditional Craft Workshops** - Learn Bali's traditional crafts from local artisans
- **Collaborative Village Visits** - Gain deep insights into the daily lives of Balinese people
- **Religious Ceremony Experiences** - Observe or participate in Bali's unique religious rituals
- **Sustainable Agriculture Exploration** - Understand Bali's traditional rice terrace farming system
The hotel also showcases works by emerging Balinese artists, integrating art not just in galleries but into every corner of daily life.

## Commitment to Sustainability

In an era where climate change and environmental protection are increasingly important, Hiliwatu has incorporated sustainability as a core concept from its initial design. From material selection and energy usage to collaboration models with local communities, it demonstrates respect for both the environment and culture.
