## **Bali police quickly solve case, remind tourists to stay alert**

Bali police recently confirmed they have tracked down and arrested an Indonesian suspect in a theft case. The suspect allegedly stole a tourist's handbag on **Tuesday, February 4**, at **Pererenan Beach**.

**Pererenan Beach is located in Bali's famous Canggu area**, which was just named the 'Coolest Neighborhood in the World for 2024' by TimeOut magazine. Yet despite its stunning scenery, local police remind tourists to stay safety-conscious and avoid theft of personal items.

## **Russian tourist falls victim to theft, police solve case within hours**

The victim, a **31-year-old Russian tourist** identified by initials **GK**, was robbed on **Tuesday, February 4**, at Pererenan Beach. She had left her handbag on the sand while walking to another area, only to find it missing upon return.

The handbag contained:

- **iPhone 12 Pro Max**
- **Three ID cards**
- **Apple AirPods**
- **Cash**

Police estimate the **total loss at approximately IDR 15,000,000 (around USD$970)**.

GK immediately filed a report at the **Mengwi Police Unit**, and police quickly launched an investigation.

Within just **a few hours**, authorities successfully arrested the suspect—a **21-year-old man identified as FISD** from **East Java**. At the time, he was working at a **construction site office in Seseh-Cemagi village**. Police swiftly moved in, apprehended him, and recovered some of the stolen items.

---

## **Suspect Confesses, Faces Up to 5 Years in Prison**

According to **Mengwi Police Chief I Ketut Adnyana TJ**, the **police criminal investigation team acted quickly and successfully identified the suspect**. He stated:

> **"The suspect has confessed to the crime. Based on the victim's report, he stole an iPhone 12 Pro Max and Indonesian rupiah cash, while the handbag and other items were discarded in a public toilet at Pererenan Beach."**

Police confirmed that **the suspect will be charged under Article 362 of the Indonesian Criminal Code, facing a maximum of 5 years in prison.**

## **Travel Safety Tips: How to Prevent Theft?**

Although the items in this case were successfully recovered and Bali police acted swiftly, this incident still serves as a reminder to all travelers:

### **When traveling in Bali, always keep a close eye on your belongings and surroundings!**

Although Bali is one of the world's most popular travel destinations, **opportunistic thieves** can still be found at hotspots like beaches, cafes, restaurants, and beach clubs. Travelers must stay vigilant and **never let valuables out of sight**.

If you unfortunately experience theft, be sure to:

✅ **File a report with the local police immediately**

✅ **Notify the Pecalang (local traditional security team)**

✅ **Provide details of the lost items and the location of the incident**

Many theft cases can be solved quickly, but the key is whether the victim reports it promptly, so the police can act swiftly to prevent the suspect from fleeing or disposing of stolen goods.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **1. Is Bali a safe travel destination?**

Yes, Bali is one of the world's most popular travel destinations and generally has good public safety. However, opportunistic crime can still occur at popular spots, so be sure to keep your personal belongings secure.

### **2. What should I do if I get robbed in Bali?**

Report the incident to the local police immediately and notify the **Pecalang (traditional security team)**. Acting quickly can increase the chances of recovering your belongings.

### **3. Which areas are prone to theft?**

Travelers should be especially cautious at **beaches, cafes, restaurants, nightclubs, beach clubs, and markets**, as these places are prime targets for pickpockets.

### 4. What is an 'Opportunistic Thief'?

An 'opportunistic thief' refers to someone who doesn't plan a crime in advance but seizes a chance to steal when they spot an opportunity. These thieves typically don't target specific individuals; instead, they randomly choose careless victims and quickly snatch items when the person isn't paying attention.
