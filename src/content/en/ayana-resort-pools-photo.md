YouTuber "Bird of Paradise" stays at Bali's most popular Ayana Resort and shares in her YouTube video six of the resort's 14 stunning swimming pools. Her video showcases the unique styles and beautiful landscapes of these pools, attracting the attention and admiration of many viewers. These pools are not just places for swimming and relaxation but also perfect spots for capturing beautiful photos and creating unforgettable memories.

AYANA Resort is a charming destination located on Jimbaran Bay in southern Bali. The resort boasts its own private beach and offers multiple hotel brands at different price points, including RIMBA, SEGARA, RESORT, and VILLA, catering to travelers with various needs and budgets. No matter which brand you choose, you can enjoy all the facilities and services within the resort.

Among them, RIMBA is a great choice for budget-conscious travelers, allowing full access to all pools and facilities within the resort. The newly built SEGARA offers a fresh experience, providing more privacy and luxury. For those seeking higher-end accommodations, consider staying at VILLA, the resort's top-tier hotel brand offering luxurious stays and services.

## The Viral H2O / River Pool

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776081381855-pools-beach-ayana-river.jpeg)

Unlike the previous experience at Ayana's River Pool (H2O Pool) where you could take photos freely without queuing, new rules have been implemented as mentioned in the video __VID0__:

***

### 📸 The Queuing Atmosphere: Like a Timed Competition

Ayana's River Pool has strict photography rules, typically open from **6:30 AM to 9:00 AM (or 9:30 AM)**. This period feels like a "timed, limited, and numbered" photo challenge:

- **1-2 minutes per person**: Staff members are on-site with timers, and once time's up, you must step aside—creating a tense atmosphere.
- **Draw a number and wait for your turn**: You can't just show up and start shooting; you need to draw a number first. If you arrive around **7:45 AM**, expect to wait about **30 minutes**.
- **The waiting area feels like a photo-sharing session**: While nervously waiting, people exchange tips on the best angles and poses, making the scene both fun and lively. Some visitors joked that the timer often leaves men scrambling.

***

### 💁‍♀️ Don’t Worry About Taking Photos Alone—Staff Are Super Professional

If you're traveling solo or worried about your companion's photography skills, don’t fret—**the staff are more than happy to assist**, and they’re surprisingly skilled:

- They’ll guide you on posing, lighting, and even run around to capture shots from different angles.
- Many visitors later exclaimed, "The photos turned out even better than I expected!"

***

### 🗓️ Want to Snap the Perfect Shot? Keep These Tips in Mind

Based on recent visitor experiences, follow these suggestions to ensure a successful photo session:

1. **Wake up early!**
Aim to arrive between **6:30 AM and 7:30 AM**. If you show up at 8:00 AM, you might end up waiting an hour or even miss your chance.
2. **Where’s the most convenient place to stay?**
The River Pool is located in Ayana's **VILLAS area**. However, even if you’re staying at the more budget-friendly **RIMBA** or **SEGARA**, you can easily reach it via the resort’s free shuttle, which runs every 10 minutes.
3. **Make the most of your 1-2 minutes**
When it’s your turn, stay calm and have 1-2 poses in mind beforehand. Save reference photos on your phone to show the staff—it’s the most efficient way to communicate.
4. **Photography tips**
Use **Live Photos** or burst mode to avoid missing the perfect shot due to blinking or bad angles—after all, there’s no second chance.

***

### ⚠️ A Friendly Reminder

The poolside can be extremely slippery. Multiple guests have already cautioned about being extra careful, as there have been instances where people, including women, have slipped and fallen. While capturing the perfect photo is important, safety should always come first.
