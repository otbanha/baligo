From January 11 to 17, 2026, our family embarked on a laughter-filled trip to Bali. Before departure, many friends warned me about the rainy season and traffic congestion. Now that the journey is over, it's time to share my firsthand experience and feedback.

First, regarding the **traffic congestion** everyone is concerned about, my conclusion is: **it’s much better than expected!** With a larger group, we opted for a private 14-seater minibus. Throughout the trip, we only encountered slow-moving traffic once for about two hours when entering tourist-heavy urban areas. Otherwise, we moved around smoothly. This made me realize that **choosing private transportation and planning routes wisely** can effectively avoid most traffic nightmares.

![暴雨來襲，趕快開車離開海邊！！經過一些低窪地區，路面都變成河流](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f4f9f96d6c813ec68ba40960047d0ffa.jpg)

When the heavy rain hit, we quickly drove away from the beach! Passing through some low-lying areas, the roads turned into rivers.

Next, let’s talk about the **challenges and surprises of the rainy season**. Yes, we experienced intermittent rain on five out of seven days. However, the rainy season isn’t entirely inconvenient—it brought several benefits: cooler and more comfortable temperatures, lush greenery, and fewer tourists, avoiding crowded scenes. Of course, sudden downpours required quick adaptation (like the one in the photo that had us fleeing the beach in a hurry, which now seems hilarious 🤣🤣). Interestingly, weather forecasts are more of a reference. For example, in Kuta, despite receiving a **heavy rain warning**, we mostly enjoyed sunny skies during our two-day stay, with only occasional evening showers. This taught us that in Bali, it’s better to stay flexible with plans rather than worry about the weather. Our strategy was to always have **rainy-day alternatives** ready, such as indoor attractions, museums, art galleries, or markets, keeping the itinerary adaptable while the sound of rain became a soothing backdrop to our journey.

> **Quick Reference:** [Bali Rainy Season: What to Do When It Rains? 60 Rainy-Day Alternatives for Bali](/blog/2026-bali-indoor-activities/)

Of course, there were some adjustments to be made: urban traffic isn’t very pedestrian-friendly, crossing the street felt like playing a real-life game of "Frogger"; and the countless zeros on Indonesian Rupiah notes nearly overheated my brain’s CPU during currency conversions ♨️

## Sharing Our Itinerary:

### **Day 1: Arrival in Ubud, A Relaxing Prelude at the Villa**

After a long flight, we smoothly landed in Bali. The immigration process was quicker than expected, and after collecting our luggage, we met up with our pre-booked private car driver. As we drove away from the airport's hustle and bustle, the scenery gradually shifted from modern buildings to lush fields and intricate shrines, reminding us that we had truly stepped onto the "Island of the Gods."

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_872fd38d85050291bdc0602b42d36dd9.jpg)

Upon arriving at our booked **Villa** in Ubud, the fatigue from the journey was instantly washed away by the view before us. The Villa was nestled in greenery, featuring a private pool and an open-air gazebo. The children cheered at the sight of the pool, while the adults quickly unpacked, eager to brew a pot of tea and sit in the pavilion to soak in Ubud's unique tranquility. As night fell, accompanied by the natural symphony of insects and frogs, we concluded our relaxed first day, recharging for the adventures ahead.

### **Day 4: Exploring the Rustic Villages and Coastal Scenery of the East Coast**

Today, we venture to the eastern part of Bali to experience a more primitive and tranquil side.

- **Tenganan Pegringsingan Village:** This is a well-preserved ancient village (Bali Aga) with a unique social structure and traditions. The village streets are neatly arranged, serene and solemn. We saw villagers weaving the famous double-sided cloth "Geringsing," a complex craft with ancient colors. There is no commercial hustle here, only a profound sense of history. Walking through it feels like a journey through time.
- **Tirta Gangga Water Palace:** Next, we visited the former royal water palace. This is **a paradise for photography enthusiasts**! The exquisite ponds, fountains, stone carvings, and water lilies complement each other beautifully, and you can see plump koi fish in the ponds. Jumping across the stepping stones in the water is a fun experience for both adults and children. The entire garden is filled with Zen and a refreshing atmosphere, truly enchanting.

Many locals and tourists enjoy leisurely picnics and kite flying here, creating a relaxed and carefree atmosphere. We quietly waited for the sunset here, experiencing a coastal vibe completely different from Kuta Beach.

## **Travel Memo and Practical Tips**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2a859b507a199d6500bc5d3d47023c43.png)

Based on our firsthand experience, we've summarized the following tips for friends planning to visit in the future:

1. **Currency Exchange:** Locally, **the exchange rate for USD to IDR is usually better than using NT$ or other currencies**. It's advisable to carry USD cash and exchange it at reputable money changers in the city. **Quick Reference:**[Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide: What currency is used in Indonesia/Bali? How to get the best exchange rate? Where to exchange money?](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)
2. **Internet:** The **DJB physical SIM card** we used had very stable signals, ensuring smooth internet access throughout our trip for navigation, communication, and information lookup, proving to be quite reliable. **Quick Reference:**[【Bali Travel Essentials】How to set up Bali internet/SIM card/eSIM?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/)
3. **Shopping Price Comparison:** **Supermarket prices aren't necessarily cheaper than those at vendors**, especially for souvenirs. Vendors usually allow bargaining, while supermarkets have fixed prices. It's recommended to compare between the two based on the type of goods before purchasing.
4. **Transportation:** For short-distance travel within the city or spontaneous rides, **Gojek or Grab ride-hailing apps are very convenient and affordable**, serving as a good alternative to private car rentals. **Quick Reference:**[Grab or Gojek? Comprehensive Guide to Registering, Using, and Recharging Gojek and GoPay in Indonesia/Bali](/blog/indonesia-gojek-gopay-guide/)
5. **Mosquito and Insect Protection:** Bali has **quite a few flies and mosquitoes**, especially near fields or during the evening. **Insect repellent is an absolute daily necessity**, so be sure to carry it with you and reapply regularly. **Quick Reference:**[What essential medicines/medications should you bring for Indonesia/Bali travel? How to buy insect repellent/sunscreen?](/blog/bali-travel-medicine-checklist/)
6. **Gastrointestinal Health (Tested Effective):** To avoid stomach discomfort from water and food changes, we strictly followed these points, and no one in our group experienced gastrointestinal issues:
7. - **Take one probiotic pill on an empty stomach every morning**.

- **Always use bottled water for drinking and brushing teeth**. 
- Be extra careful when showering, **avoid accidentally ingesting tap water**. 
- When dining out, use **alcohol wipes to clean utensils** (especially those for children). 
- When choosing restaurants, prioritize **larger establishments with good hygiene and reputation**. 
- Always wash hands with **soap/hand sanitizer** before eating.
