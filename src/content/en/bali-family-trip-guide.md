From January 11 to 17, 2026, our family embarked on a laughter-filled trip to Bali. Before departure, many friends warned me about the rainy season and traffic congestion. Now that the journey is over, it's time to share my real experiences and feedback.

First, regarding the **traffic congestion** that everyone is concerned about, my conclusion is: **it's much better than expected!** With a large group, we opted for a private 14-seater minibus. Throughout the trip, we only encountered slow-moving traffic for about two hours when entering tourist-heavy areas. The rest of the time, we moved smoothly. This made me realize that **choosing private transportation and planning routes wisely** can effectively avoid most traffic nightmares in Bali.

![暴雨來襲，趕快開車離開海邊！！經過一些低窪地區，路面都變成河流](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f4f9f96d6c813ec68ba40960047d0ffa.jpg)

When heavy rain hits, quickly drive away from the beach!! Passing through some low-lying areas, the roads turned into rivers.

Next, let's talk about the **challenges and surprises of the rainy season**. Yes, during our seven-day trip, we experienced intermittent rain on five days. However, the rainy season isn't all bad—it brings cooler, more comfortable temperatures, lush greenery, and fewer tourists, avoiding crowded situations. Of course, sudden downpours require quick thinking (like the scene in the attached photo where we 'fled' from the beach during a heavy rain, which now seems hilarious 🤣🤣). Interestingly, weather forecasts can only be taken as a reference. For example, in Kuta, despite receiving a **heavy rain warning**, we mostly enjoyed sunny days with only occasional evening showers. This taught us that in Bali, instead of worrying about the weather, it's better to maintain flexibility in our plans. Our strategy was to always have **rainy day alternatives** ready, such as indoor attractions, museums, galleries, or markets, keeping our itinerary flexible and turning the sound of rain into white noise accompaniment during our journey.

> **Quick Reference:** [Bali Rainy Season: What to Do When It Rains in Bali? 60 Rainy Day Alternatives for Bali Travel](/blog/2024-01-06-6598c6fffd89780001047d76/)

Of course, there were also some aspects of the trip that required adjustment: city traffic isn't very pedestrian-friendly, crossing the street felt like playing a real-life version of 'Frogger'; the countless zeros on Indonesian Rupiah notes also nearly overloaded my brain's CPU during currency conversions ♨️

## Sharing Our Itinerary:

### **Day 1: Arrival in Ubud, A Relaxing Start at the Villa**

After a long flight, we landed smoothly in Bali. The immigration process was quicker than expected, and after collecting our luggage, we met up with our pre-booked private car driver. As we drove away from the airport's hustle and bustle, the scenery gradually shifted from modern buildings to lush fields and intricate shrines, reminding us that we had truly arrived on the "Island of the Gods."

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_872fd38d85050291bdc0602b42d36dd9.jpg)

Upon arriving at our booked **Villa** in Ubud, the fatigue of the journey was instantly washed away by the view before us. The Villa was nestled in greenery, featuring a private pool and an open-air gazebo. The kids cheered at the sight of the pool, while the adults hurriedly unloaded the luggage, eager to brew a pot of tea and sit in the pavilion to soak in Ubud's unique tranquility. As night fell, we ended our relaxing first day to the natural symphony of insects and frogs, recharging for the adventures ahead.

Watching locals and international visitors perform the ritual bathing in the spring pools with focused and devout expressions brought a sense of peace to our hearts. The kids were curious about this "queueing in water" ritual, and we took the opportunity to explain its religious significance, making it a valuable cultural experience.

The day's itinerary was full of contrasts and surprises, from forests to rice terraces, sacred trees to royal palaces, experiencing Bali's multifaceted charm.

We strictly adhered to the "no eating, no direct eye contact, no touching" guidelines, observing their behaviors from a safe distance, with the kids both nervous and excited. Thankfully, the monkeys here were much tamer! Still, it served as a vivid lesson on respecting wildlife.

### **Day 4: Exploring the Rustic Villages and Coastal Scenery of the East Coast**

On this day, we ventured to the eastern part of Bali to experience a more primitive and tranquil side.

- **Tenganan Pegringsingan Village:** This is a well-preserved ancient village (Bali Aga) with a unique social structure and traditions. The streets are neatly arranged, exuding a serene and solemn atmosphere. We witnessed villagers weaving the famous double-sided cloth "Geringsing," showcasing intricate craftsmanship and ancient colors. There's no commercial hustle here, only a profound sense of history. Walking through the village feels like a journey through time.
- **Tirta Gangga Water Palace:** Next, we visited the former royal water palace. This is a **paradise for photography enthusiasts**! The exquisite ponds, fountains, stone carvings, and water lilies create a picturesque scene, with plump koi fish swimming in the ponds. Jumping across the stepping stones in the water is a fun experience for both adults and children. The entire garden is filled with Zen-like tranquility and coolness, making it utterly charming.

- **Bukit Asah Desa Bugbug Seaside:** Finally, we headed to this hidden gem on the east coast. This isn't a sandy beach but a grassy hillside (Bukit) overlooking the sea. The view is incredibly expansive, offering a panoramic vista of the azure waters and rocky reefs. Many locals and tourists leisurely picnic and fly kites here, creating a relaxed atmosphere. We quietly waited for the sunset here, experiencing a coastal vibe vastly different from Kuta Beach.

## **Travel Notes & Practical Tips**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2a859b507a199d6500bc5d3d47023c43.png)

Based on our firsthand experience, we’ve summarized a few tips for future travelers:

1. **Currency Exchange:** In Bali, **exchanging USD for Indonesian Rupiah usually offers a better rate than using NT$ or other currencies**. We recommend carrying USD cash and exchanging it at reputable money changers in the city. **Quick Reference:** [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide: What Currency to Use? How to Get the Best Exchange Rate? Where to Exchange?](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)
2. **Internet:** We used **DJB's physical SIM card**, which provided stable signals throughout the trip, ensuring smooth navigation, communication, and information searches—highly reliable. **Quick Reference:** [Bali Travel Essentials: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card/eSIM?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)
3. **Shopping Comparison:** **Supermarket prices aren’t always cheaper than street vendors**, especially for souvenirs. Vendors often allow bargaining, while supermarkets have fixed prices. We recommend comparing prices based on the type of item before purchasing.
