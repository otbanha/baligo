## 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Environmental Challenges and Holiday Calm

- **Suwung Landfill Ban Triggers Waste Crisis**: Since April, Bali's largest landfill, Suwung, has stopped accepting two-thirds of organic waste to enforce source separation. However, due to insufficient supporting facilities, waste piles and illegal burning have emerged across the island. Authorities reiterated that the site will fully close on August 1, pushing waste management pressures to their peak.
- **Peaceful Labor Day Conclusion**: Yesterday (5/1), Labor Day activities concluded peacefully with health walks, affordable markets, and entertainment performances at Renon Square in Denpasar, attended by approximately 1,200 participants.
- **Major Breakthrough in Ukrainian Tourist Murder Case**: Bali police recently solved a kidnapping and murder case targeting a 28-year-old Ukrainian man. Six foreign suspects using multiple passports have been identified, and Interpol has issued red notices.
