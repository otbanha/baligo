### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Environmental Challenges and Holiday Calm

- **Suwung Landfill Ban Triggers Waste Crisis**: Since April, Bali's largest landfill, Suwung, has stopped accepting two-thirds of organic waste to enforce source separation. However, due to insufficient supporting facilities, garbage piles and illegal burning have appeared across the island. Authorities reiterated that the site will fully close on August 1, when waste management pressure will peak.
- **Labor Day Ends Peacefully**: Yesterday (5/1), Labor Day activities did not see large-scale protests but were held peacefully at Renon Square in Denpasar with health walks, affordable markets, and entertainment performances, attended by about 1,200 representatives.
- **Major Breakthrough in Ukrainian Tourist Murder Case**: Bali police recently solved a kidnapping and murder case targeting a 28-year-old Ukrainian man, identifying six foreign suspects using multiple passports. Red notices have been issued through Interpol.
