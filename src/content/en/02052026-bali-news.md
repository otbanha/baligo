## 🗞️ Top Stories: Environmental Challenges and a Quiet Holiday

- **Suwung Landfill Ban Sparks Garbage Crisis**: Since April, Bali's largest landfill, Suwung, has stopped accepting organic waste—which accounts for two-thirds of its intake—in an effort to enforce source separation. However, inadequate supporting infrastructure has led to waste accumulation and illegal burning across the island. Officials reiterate that the site will fully close on August 1, when waste management pressure will peak.
- **Labor Day Passes Peacefully**: Yesterday's (May 1) Labor Day events saw no large-scale protests, but instead featured peaceful activities like health walks, bargain markets, and entertainment performances at Renon Square in Denpasar, with around 1,200 representatives participating.
- **Major Breakthrough in Ukrainian Tourist Murder Case**: Bali police recently announced they have cracked a kidnapping and murder case targeting a 28-year-old Ukrainian man. Six foreign suspects, who entered the country using multiple passports, have been identified, and Interpol has been requested to issue Red Notices.
