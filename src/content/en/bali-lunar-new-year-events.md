### 2. **Culinary Experiences**

During Lunar New Year, restaurants and hotels in Bali offer special menus that combine traditional Chinese cuisine with local flavors:

- **Reunion Dinners**: Many luxury hotels like [AYANA Bali](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=6504) and [Mulia Bali](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=6532982) host Chinese reunion dinners featuring dishes such as yee sang, Peking duck, and longevity noodles, symbolizing wealth and longevity. ([Reference 1](https://www.ayana.com/zh/bali/offers/chinese-new-year-2025/), [Reference 2](https://www.themulia.com/bali/lunar-new-year-2025))
- **Afternoon Tea and Dim Sum**: AYANA Bali’s Damar Terrace offers a Lunar New Year-themed afternoon tea paired with exquisite Chinese dim sum. ([Reference](https://www.ayana.com/zh/bali/offers/chinese-new-year-2025/))
- **Street Food**: In places like Denpasar and Singaraja, you can enjoy street food that blends Chinese and Balinese flavors, such as dumplings and roast duck. ([Reference](https://dijiwasanctuaries.com/magazine/things-to-do-in-chinese-new-year-bali-2025-celebrations-festivities-and-culinary-delights))

### 3. **Cultural and Art Activities**

Bali's Lunar New Year celebrations go beyond just food, offering rich cultural experiences:

- **Traditional Music and Dance**: At the Kang Xi Temple in Tugu Bali Hotel, visitors can enjoy guzheng performances and lion dances, followed by an 8-course traditional family feast. ([Source](https://blog.tuguhotels.com/lunar-legends-celebrating-the-wood-snake-year-at-the-historic-kang-xi-temple/))
- **Festive Decorations and Markets**: The streets of Denpasar are adorned with red lanterns and festive decorations, creating a vibrant holiday atmosphere. Traditional handicrafts and holiday gifts can be purchased at the markets. ([Source](https://dijiwasanctuaries.com/magazine/things-to-do-in-chinese-new-year-bali-2025-celebrations-festivities-and-culinary-delights))

- **February 1-2**: Short Film & Photography Competition (shooting locations include Nusantara Parade, Catur Muka area, and Puputan Badung Square).
- **February 1**: Nusantara Parade (15:00-17:00), featuring opening ceremony, traditional cheongsam fashion show, lion dance, martial arts performances, and food festival.
- **February 2**: Cosplay Competition (16:00) and Closing Ceremony (17:00-22:00), including cultural performances and Lunar New Year special programs.
- **Other Activities**: Blood donation drive (08:30-13:00) and mass fitness exercise (from 16:00).
**Location**: Catur Muka Statue and Puputan Badung Square, Denpasar.
**Food Festival**: Offers a variety of Lunar New Year and Indonesian traditional delicacies.
