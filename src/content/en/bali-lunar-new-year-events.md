Here’s a curated guide to Lunar New Year celebrations in Bali for 2025. If you and your family are visiting Bali during this festive season, you can also experience the New Year atmosphere in a foreign land!

### 1. **Lunar New Year Celebrations**

On January 29, 2025, Bali will welcome the Lunar New Year. The local Chinese community and tourism industry have prepared a variety of celebratory activities, including traditional cultural performances, culinary feasts, and festive decorations:

- **Lion Dance Performances**: One of the most popular traditional activities during Lunar New Year, symbolizing the expulsion of evil spirits and the ushering in of good fortune. Catch spectacular lion dance performances at venues like [Mulia Bali](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=6546761) and [Courtyard Bali Seminyak Resort](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=624034). ([Reference](https://en.tempo.co/read/1964175/6-ultimate-ways-to-celebrate-chinese-new-year-2025-in-bali))
- **Temple Blessings**: Chinese temples in Bali, such as Klenteng Caow Eng Boi and Satya Dharma Temple, will be adorned with red lanterns and money trees, hosting blessing ceremonies that blend Chinese and Balinese cultural elements. ([Reference](https://dijiwasanctuaries.com/magazine/things-to-do-in-chinese-new-year-bali-2025-celebrations-festivities-and-culinary-delights))
- **Fireworks Displays**: In places like Singaraja, fireworks will light up the night sky, symbolizing the expulsion of bad luck and the welcoming of good fortune. ([Reference](https://dijiwasanctuaries.com/magazine/things-to-do-in-chinese-new-year-bali-2025-celebrations-festivities-and-culinary-delights))

---

### 2. **Culinary Experiences**

During Lunar New Year, restaurants and hotels in Bali offer special menus that combine traditional Chinese cuisine with local flavors:

- **Reunion Dinners**: Many luxury hotels like [AYANA Bali](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=6504) and [Mulia Bali](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=6532982) host Chinese reunion dinners, featuring dishes such as yee sang, Peking duck, and longevity noodles, symbolizing wealth and longevity. ([Reference 1](https://www.ayana.com/zh/bali/offers/chinese-new-year-2025/), [Reference 2](https://www.themulia.com/bali/lunar-new-year-2025))
- **Afternoon Tea and Dim Sum**: AYANA Bali’s Damar Terrace offers a Lunar New Year-themed afternoon tea, complete with exquisite Chinese dim sum. ([Reference](https://www.ayana.com/zh/bali/offers/chinese-new-year-2025/))
- **Street Food**: In places like Denpasar and Singaraja, you can savor street food that blends Chinese and Balinese flavors, such as dumplings and roast duck. ([Reference](https://dijiwasanctuaries.com/magazine/things-to-do-in-chinese-new-year-bali-2025-celebrations-festivities-and-culinary-delights))

### 3. **Cultural and Art Events**

Bali's Lunar New Year celebrations go beyond just food, offering rich cultural experiences:

- **Traditional Music and Dance**: At the Kang Xi Temple in Tugu Bali Hotel, visitors can enjoy guzheng performances and lion dances, followed by an 8-course traditional family banquet. ([Reference](https://blog.tuguhotels.com/lunar-legends-celebrating-the-wood-snake-year-at-the-historic-kang-xi-temple/))
- **Festive Decorations and Markets**: The streets of Denpasar are adorned with red lanterns and festive decorations, creating a vibrant holiday atmosphere. Traditional handicrafts and holiday gifts can be purchased at the markets. ([Reference](https://dijiwasanctuaries.com/magazine/things-to-do-in-chinese-new-year-bali-2025-celebrations-festivities-and-culinary-delights))

### 4. **Family-Friendly Activities**

For families traveling to Bali, resorts offer specially designed activities for children:
