## **Traveling in Bali: Cash or Credit Card?**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_832db431875e09d43fff04ac9260f412.jpg)

Bali is a beloved vacation destination for travelers from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia. While using credit cards for payments at luxury hotels, unique restaurants, and spa treatments may seem convenient, it actually hides several risks. Issues like credit card fraud, high transaction fees, and merchant scams are not uncommon. If not handled carefully, a pleasant holiday can quickly turn into a financial nightmare.

This article will provide a detailed analysis of the potential risks of using credit cards in Bali and offer practical safety tips to help you enjoy your trip while protecting your wallet!

---

## **1. Credit Card Fraud and Scam Risks**

### **1. Skimming and Counterfeit Card Production**

Some businesses or ATMs in Bali may have skimming devices installed by criminal groups. When you swipe your card, fraudsters can steal your credit card information and use it to create counterfeit cards for unauthorized transactions.

- **High-Risk Locations**: Small roadside shops, night markets, non-chain restaurants, and remote ATMs.
- **Prevention Tips**:
  - Try to use credit cards at large chain stores (e.g., supermarkets, international brand hotels).
  - When using ATMs, prioritize machines located inside banks and avoid standalone ATMs.

### **2. Unsafe Payment Environments**

Some restaurants or merchants still use outdated card machines, or even manually record credit card numbers, which could lead to your card information being stolen.

- **Recommendations**:
- - If a merchant asks to 'manually enter your card number' or 'take a photo of your credit card', refuse immediately.
  - Switch to cash or mobile payments (like Apple Pay, Google Pay, or Indonesia's GoPay) to reduce risk.

## **2. High Fees and Exchange Rate Traps**

### **1. Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) Exploitation**

When paying by card, some merchants may ask, 'Would you like to pay in Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) or New Taiwan Dollar (TWD)?' If you choose TWD, the system will automatically use a very poor exchange rate and add a service fee.

- **Correct Approach**:
- - **Insist on paying in Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)**, letting your card issuer handle the currency conversion, which is usually more favorable.
  - Before swiping, confirm the currency displayed on the POS machine to avoid forced DCC.

### **2. Foreign Transaction Fees**

Most credit cards typically charge **1.5%-3%** for foreign transactions, which can add up to a significant expense over time.

- **Solutions**:
- - Apply for a credit card with 'no foreign transaction fees' before traveling, such as Taishin FlyGo or E.SUN Multi-Currency Debit Card.
  - For large purchases, consider splitting the payment to avoid high single-transaction fees.

## **3. Additional Charges and Mandatory Tips**

In Bali, some businesses may:

- **Automatically add a service charge (5%-10%)**: Common in restaurants and spas.

- **Request additional cash tips**: Even after paying by card, cash tips may still be demanded.

**How to Handle**:

- Confirm whether the bill includes taxes and service charges before paying by card.

- If faced with unreasonable charges, request corrections on the spot or dispute the charge with your bank.

## **4. Risks of Pre-authorization**

Hotels, car rental companies, or dive shops may request a "pre-authorization" to freeze a deposit (often 2-3 times the room rate). If not properly released, this can lead to:

- **Credit limit being occupied**, affecting subsequent purchases.

---

## 5. Technical Issues Leading to Transaction Failures

In some areas of Bali, unstable internet connections may cause:

- **Failed transactions that still result in charges**
- **Duplicate charges**

**How to Handle It?**

- Immediately check your bank app or SMS notifications to confirm transaction status.
- Keep your receipt and contact your bank to dispute any incorrect charges if necessary.

Even credit cards that normally work perfectly can encounter unexpected issues in Bali. ## **Ultimate Tips for Safe Credit Card Use**

1. **Use Cash as Primary, Credit Cards as Backup**: Use cash for small purchases and credit cards for larger ones.
2. **Enable Instant Transaction Notifications**: Monitor every transaction through your bank app or SMS.
3. **Carry More Than One Credit Card**: Keep them separate to ensure you have a backup if one is lost.
4. **Prioritize Chip Cards or Mobile Payments**: These are safer than magnetic stripe cards.
5. **Report Fraud Immediately**: Save your bank’s overseas emergency contact number before traveling.
6. **If Your Card Doesn’t Work Overseas**: Some cards require international usage activation—confirm with your bank before your trip.

---

## **Conclusion: Smart Spending, Peaceful Vacation**

Bali’s credit card environment isn’t as secure as Taiwan’s, but with the right payment strategies, you can significantly reduce risks. We recommend using **cash and electronic payments** as your primary methods, with credit cards as backup. Always monitor your transaction records to ensure a convenient and worry-free vacation!
