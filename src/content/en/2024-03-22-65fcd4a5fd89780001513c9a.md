## Supplement: Should you apply sunscreen or insect repellent first? The correct order is to apply sunscreen first, followed by insect repellent. Here's why: Sunscreen should be applied first because it needs to evenly adhere to the skin to form a protective barrier against UV rays. Sunscreen typically needs to be applied 15-30 minutes before going outside to allow time for absorption. Insect repellent should be applied afterward because its active ingredients (like DEET or other repellents) need to evaporate into the air to be effective. If you apply insect repellent first and then sunscreen, the sunscreen might dilute or cover the repellent, reducing its effectiveness. **Pro tip:** After applying sunscreen, wait a few minutes for it to absorb slightly before applying insect repellent. Ideally, leave 5-10 minutes between applications. Some products may indicate they shouldn't be mixed with sunscreen, so check the instructions beforehand. If you need to reapply sunscreen, remember to reapply insect repellent as well. This ensures both products work at their best, keeping you protected from both sun and mosquitoes!
