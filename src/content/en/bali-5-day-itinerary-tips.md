**Bottom Line First**: In a 5-day, 4-night Bali itinerary, after subtracting travel days at the start and end, you actually have less than 3 full days to explore. Try to get up early to avoid traffic jams. Key pre-trip preparations: e-Visa on arrival, tourist tax, eSIM (buy it the day before and it's ready the next day), and a 220V hairdryer (for drying your hair after taking water photos). This Bali trip was only 5 days and 4 nights, but honestly, after taking out the travel time on the first and last days, the actual play time felt way too short! ## Pre-Trip Preparations

1. **e-Visa on Arrival**: Remember to apply for the e-Visa on arrival before heading to Bali.
2. **Tourist Tax**: Be sure to pay the tourist tax.
3. **Customs Declaration**: Fill out the arrival form.
4. **Currency Exchange**: It's recommended to bring US dollars and exchange them for Indonesian Rupiah locally.
5. **Sun Protection**: The sun in Bali is very strong; if you forget sunscreen, you'll get tanned easily.
6. **Internet**: Using an eSIM is recommended. I bought one from 'Global Travel Expert' on Shopee, and the connection was stable except in remote mountain areas. Buy it the day before and it's ready the next day—super convenient.
7. **Water Photos**: If you plan to take nice photos after playing in the water, remember to bring a hairdryer that supports 220V.
8. **Massage Experience**: Every massage place feels like a lottery. Luckily, we had great experiences every time, even at random roadside shops.
9. **Photo Tips**: To get those Instagram-worthy shots, study poses in advance and have your travel buddy practice their photography skills. I'm the one who's terrible at taking photos.
10. **Tipping Etiquette**: Bali is a country where tipping is customary. A basic tip is around 10,000 IDR (about USD$0.30). Avoid giving coins or very small bills, as it's considered more polite to give proper notes.
11. **Power Adapter**: Remember to bring a power adapter, or check with your hotel if they can lend you one.
12. **Extra Taxes**: Some restaurants and massage places charge additional taxes, and using a credit card may incur an extra 3% fee.

**Underwater Walking**

Our first activity was underwater walking. While it sounded fun, the actual experience was a bit disappointing. When we first put on the helmet, it leaked water without any warning, and the water pressed directly against our faces, which was quite frightening. While walking underwater, we had to hold onto the railing, but the scenery wasn't particularly impressive.

**Parasailing**

Next up was parasailing. This activity was truly amazing, especially the sense of freedom we felt while soaring through the air. Perhaps because we had a great chat with the instructor, the whole experience was even more enjoyable. Parasailing took us from the air down to the water and back up again, making the back-and-forth process a lot of fun.

**Donut Ride**

Finally, we tried the donut ride (towable tube). It's said that the donut ride in Taiwan is more thrilling, especially if you sit in the back row for extra fun. Besides this, Nanwan offers a variety of other water activities to suit different preferences.

**Light Snacks and Tea**

After the activities, light snacks and tea are provided, allowing everyone to take a short break after all the excitement.

**Reminders**

- **Price Negotiation**: Activity prices are often set high, so it's best to haggle or purchase discounted tickets online in advance.
- **Photo Fees**: If your itinerary includes photography services, these come at an extra cost. The vendor will provide a set of cloud photos and recorded video.
