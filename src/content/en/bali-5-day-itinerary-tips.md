## Pre-Trip Preparations

1. Electronic Visa on Arrival: Remember to apply for an electronic visa on arrival before heading to Bali.
2. Tourist Tax: Be aware of paying the tourist tax.
3. Fill in the Arrival Form: Complete the arrival form.
4. Currency Exchange: It's advisable to bring USD and exchange it for Indonesian Rupiah locally.
5. Sun Protection: Bali's sun is very strong; forgetting sunscreen can easily lead to sunburn.
6. Internet Usage: It's recommended to use an Esim card. I purchased an Esim card from 'Global Travel Expert' on Shopee, which was stable unless in remote mountainous areas. It's very convenient as it can be used the next day after purchase.
7. Water Activities and Photos: If planning to take photos after water activities, remember to bring a 220V hair dryer.
8. Massage Experience: No matter which massage shop you choose, it feels like a lottery. Fortunately, our massage experiences were always good, even at randomly chosen roadside shops.
9. Photography Tips: To take influencer-worthy photos, it's recommended to research poses in advance and have your travel companion practice photography skills. I'm the one who doesn't take bad photos.
10. Tipping Etiquette: Bali is a country accustomed to tipping; the basic tip is usually around 10,000 Rupiah (approximately USD$0.65). Avoid giving coins or very small bills as it's more polite.
11. Charging Adapter: Remember to bring a charging adapter or ask the hotel if they can lend one.
12. Additional Taxes: Some restaurants and massage shops may charge additional taxes, and credit card payments may incur a 3% surcharge.

- Time: 13:30

**Sea Walking**

Our first activity was sea walking. While it sounded exciting, the actual experience was somewhat disappointing. When we first put on the helmets, water leaked in without anyone informing us, causing the water to press against our faces, which was quite frightening. Walking underwater required holding onto railings, but the scenery wasn't particularly stunning.

**Parasailing**

Next up was parasailing. This activity was truly fantastic, especially the feeling of freedom while flying through the air, which was unforgettable. Perhaps because we had a great conversation with the instructor, the whole process was even more enjoyable. The parasailing experience involved being pulled from the air into the water and then ascending again, making for a fun back-and-forth.

**Donut Ride**

Finally, we tried the donut (towed inflatable) activity. It's said that the donut rides in Taiwan are more thrilling, especially when sitting in the back row for an even more exciting experience. Besides this, Nusa Dua offers a variety of other water sports to choose from, catering to different preferences.

**Snacks and Tea**

After the activity, snacks and tea are provided, allowing everyone to take a short break after enjoying the thrilling rides.

**Reminders**

- **Price Negotiation**: The initial prices for activities are quite high, so it's advisable to bargain or purchase discounted tickets online in advance.
- **Photography Fees**: If photography services are included in your package, this comes at an additional cost. The vendor will provide a set of cloud photos and video footage.

Editor's Note: Visitors should note that water sports in Nusa Dua are quite popular, so it's recommended to book in advance to avoid price hikes on the spot. Booking ensures you have ample opportunity to experience all the water sports you're interested in, while also avoiding extra costs at the venue. At Nusa Dua Beach, let's set sail together and enjoy this paradise of water sports, offering you a sea adventure filled with laughter and excitement.
