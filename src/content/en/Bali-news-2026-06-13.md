## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Dual Surge in Fuel and Diesel Triggers Transportation Inflation; Island-Wide Crackdown on Illegal Villas

- **Attention self-drive and car rental travelers! Premium gasoline surges 32%, while high-grade diesel remains frozen but at historic highs**: According to the latest financial report cited by *Bali.live*, Indonesia's state oil company Pertamina has officially implemented a major price hike for non-subsidized fuels. The most commonly used fuel for self-drive cars and scooters on the island, **Pertamax (RON 92) premium gasoline, has skyrocketed by 32.1%**, jumping from IDR 12,300 per liter to **IDR 16,250 per liter** (around IDR 16,650 in some areas after local taxes). This significantly increases fuel budgets for independent travelers ahead of the peak summer season. However, the **Dexlite (IDR 23,000 per liter) and Pertamina Dex (IDR 24,800 per liter) high-grade diesel series**, widely used in mid-to-large family vans (like the Toyota HiAce) and large tour buses, have been announced as **fully frozen in this adjustment**, with no price increase. Although diesel hasn't seen a new hike and subsidized fuels (Pertalite and Biosolar) remain frozen, tourism economists warn that since most compliant self-drive cars and newer rental vehicles require the now-32%-more-expensive Pertamax gasoline, and high-grade diesel prices are already at historic highs, the combined effect will put significant inflationary pressure on overall vehicle and rental costs during this year's peak tourist season.
- **Villas Association (BVA) Teams Up with Government for Major Crackdown! Comprehensive Sweep and Seizure of Thousands of Illegal Vacation Villas**: With vacation villas on the island booming in areas like **Canggu**, Seminyak, and Uluwatu over the past few years, violations of building and business permits have hit record highs. **The Bali Villas Association (BVA) Chairman Ismoyo Sumarlan officially stated today that they are fully cooperating with a cross-departmental government task force to conduct a sweeping crackdown on thousands of illegally operated private villas across the island.** The enforcement focuses on two main areas: first, strictly checking for violations of building permits (PGB/IMB) and local spatial planning regulations (e.g., building on greenbelt protected areas or too close to cliffside beaches); second, rigorously auditing tax compliance. The BVA points out that there are numerous underground operators—often foreigners who long-term lease local land, privately renovate properties, and list them on international platforms for subletting—without declaring local taxes (PHR) at all. This not only severely erodes tax revenue used for infrastructure repairs but also poses significant fire safety risks and invalidates travel insurance for unsuspecting tourists.
