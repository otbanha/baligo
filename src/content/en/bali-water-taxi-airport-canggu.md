Dear Bali enthusiasts, does the thought of traveling from the airport to Canggu conjure up images of being stuck in endless traffic? Great news! The Indonesian government has officially announced the construction of a 'Water Taxi' route directly from **Ngurah Rai International Airport** to **Canggu**, promising to avoid the frustrating land traffic, **reaching your destination in just 30 minutes**!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775968380470-water-taxi-di-bali-hotel-bali-batiqa-hotels_ts6wi.jpg)

## 🚤 Why Build This Waterway?

According to Indonesian Transportation Minister Dudy Purwagandhi, this project aims to integrate Bali's land, sea, and air transportation, primarily targeting the severe traffic congestion in tourist areas. Especially the route between the airport and Canggu, currently one of the most congested hotspots, affects both domestic and international tourists alike. During peak hours, driving can take **1 to 2 hours**, but traveling by sea only takes **30 minutes**, significantly saving time.

## 📅 When Will Construction Begin and Launch?

- **Estimated Investment**: 12.1 trillion Indonesian Rupiah (approximately USD$775 million)
- **Construction Start**: August 2026
- **Completion**: July 2027
- **Operating Unit**: State-owned company PT ASDP Indonesia Ferry

Feasibility studies and detailed engineering designs are currently underway, with docks and breakwaters to be built along the route, stretching from the coast near the airport to Berawa Beach in Canggu.

## ⚠️ Don't Get Too Excited Yet: This Isn't the First Announcement of a Water Taxi

Actually, as early as **August 2025**, the local government announced that this project was in its final stages, even stating it would be operational by **early 2026**. Clearly, the timeline has been pushed back. However, this time a contractor has been selected, and with a state-owned company in charge, **there's a real chance it will come to fruition in 2027**.

## 💡 Current Best Alternative: Private Transfers

Until the water taxi service launches, the most reliable way to get from the airport to Canggu is still **private transfers**. This allows you to avoid the hassle of hailing a cab and the risks of price negotiations.

## ❓ FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

**1. What will the water taxi fare likely be?**
Official pricing has not been announced yet. Specific numbers will only be available after the feasibility study is completed. It is generally expected to be slightly higher than regular taxis, but considering the time saved and increased comfort, it will still be attractive.

**2. Will the water taxi operate 24 hours?**
Detailed operating hours have not been announced. Considering sea conditions and nighttime navigation safety, initial operations may be limited to daytime hours. Actual details will need to wait for further official announcements.

**3. If I’m traveling to Bali before 2027, what’s the fastest way to get from the airport to Canggu?**
Currently, the most recommended method is booking a **private transfer** (like MyBaliTrips mentioned in the article) or using ride-hailing apps like **Gojek / Grab**. To save money, you can take a taxi to a nearby transfer point and switch vehicles, but this will take longer. Avoiding peak evening hours (4:00 PM - 8:00 PM) can also reduce traffic congestion.

**4. Will the water taxi be affected by waves or weather conditions?**
Yes. Since it travels through open waters, operations may be suspended during heavy waves, rainstorms, or typhoons for safety reasons. Breakwaters will be built along the route to mitigate wave impact, but severe weather may still affect operations.

**5. Besides the airport-to-Canggu route, will other routes be opened in the future?**
Currently, the government is only prioritizing this most congested route. If successful and well-received, future expansions to other popular tourist areas like **Kuta, Seminyak, Nusa Dua**, and other coastal regions are possible.

We hope this water taxi project can truly be realized by 2027, so travelers heading to Canggu won’t have to endure endless traffic jams! Until then, prepare your patience (or book your transfer in advance)!
