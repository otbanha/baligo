## **What’s the Difference Between Rabies Vaccine and Immunoglobulin? Is There a Big Cost Gap?**

Many people are confused about these two "rabies shots," but their functions and prices differ greatly:

### Rabies Vaccine

- Purpose: Stimulates the body to actively produce antibodies
- Applicable: Can be used for pre-travel preventive vaccination or administered on schedule after a bite
- Doses: Preventive requires three doses, post-bite requires five doses
- Cost: Approximately $32〜81 USD per dose

### Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG)

- Purpose: Provides immediate antibodies to bridge the gap before vaccines take effect
- Timing: Administered immediately after a bite, the sooner the better
- Expensive: Dosage based on body weight, total cost can exceed USD$3,200
- Prices are even higher in places like Indonesia, as seen in Mr. Guo's case which cost USD$6,000

> ✅ Recommendation: Getting preventive vaccines before departure can significantly reduce treatment costs and risks

## **Why Are Medical Costs in Indonesia Higher Than Other Southeast Asian Countries?**

While rabies treatment costs in the Philippines and Thailand are relatively low, Indonesia's related treatment fees are surprisingly high, possibly due to:

- Rabies immunoglobulin mostly needs to be imported, leading to high costs
- Foreign tourists are often charged higher-tier medical fees
- Local private healthcare system lacks price transparency

Therefore, planning for health and insurance before traveling to Indonesia is an essential investment.

According to the latest news in 2024, Bali has recently seen multiple suspected and confirmed cases of rabies, raising concerns.

In Batuan Village, Sukawati, Gianyar Regency, a dog suspected of having rabies bit five people in just a few days, including two children. The dog exhibited clear abnormal behavior: disorientation, excessive excitement, and strong aggression. However, local authorities did not handle the situation promptly, and on May 29th, the dog even broke glass, injuring several people.

At the same time, in Melaya village, Jembrana, a black-and-white stray dog attacked eight people, including a cat.

According to data from Bali's health authorities, as of now:

- **Over 39,000 cases** of suspected rabies-related animal bites have been reported this year
- **90% were caused by dogs**
- **268 people have been confirmed infected**, with **5 deaths**

## **Pre-travel health preparation checklist ✅**

1. **Get pre-travel rabies vaccinations (3 doses)**
2. **Purchase travel medical insurance that covers animal bites and infectious diseases**
3. **Avoid contact with unfamiliar or stray animals**
4. **Research nearby medical facilities offering immunoglobulin at your accommodation location**

> Prevention is better than cure, especially in areas with limited medical resources!

## **Frequently Asked Questions FAQ**

### 1. Is it necessary to get the rabies vaccine?

If you're traveling to high-risk areas like Indonesia, Southeast Asia, or Africa, it's strongly recommended to get vaccinated preventively. Compared to the pain and high costs afterward, the vaccine is a life-saving investment.

### 2. Can I skip the rabies immunoglobulin?

If you haven't received preventive vaccination, **you must get the immunoglobulin along with the vaccine after being bitten**. Missing the golden period could be life-threatening.

### 3. Can you get rabies in Taiwan?

Although Taiwan is considered a non-endemic area, there have been records of wild animals (like ferret-badgers) carrying the virus. Avoiding contact with wild animals is still essential.

### 4. Is there a chance of recovery once rabies symptoms appear?

Extremely low. Once rabies symptoms appear, the mortality rate is almost 100%. Therefore, all preventive measures should be completed before symptoms develop.

### 5. Are there side effects from the vaccine?

Most people only experience mild discomfort such as localized redness, fever, and fatigue, which falls within an acceptable range.
