## **Bali Enters Pre-Festival Period: Police Launch Large-Scale Traffic Inspection 'Zebra Operation'**

Bali welcomes the important festival **Galungan (November 18-20)** in November, followed by the Christmas and New Year holidays in December, making the streets busier than usual. To prevent an increase in accidents, local police have initiated the annual traffic control **'Zebra Operation'** in advance.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8885ac4311d8bdda3c5e0fd40566a5d6.jpg)

At popular intersections, you'll notice increased police presence, with nearly **1,700 law enforcement officers** deployed for inspections, particularly targeting foreign tourists riding scooters.

Police stated that one of the key focuses of this crackdown is the traffic safety of foreign tourists, especially those accustomed to driving on the right side or lacking riding experience.

---

## **No Experience? Don't Ride! Police Strongly Urge Tourists to Avoid Riding Scooters Independently**

With Bali's busy traffic and ever-changing road conditions, police remind: **Tourists without riding experience should not attempt to ride scooters on their own.**

Police Commissioner Turmudi, representing Bali's Traffic Police Bureau, stated:

> 'Foreign nationals unfamiliar with two-wheeled vehicles or lacking experience are strongly advised against riding scooters independently. This is to protect the safety of both tourists and local residents.'

In addition to safety considerations, he also mentioned that this measure could reduce losses incurred by rental companies due to accidents. For renting to beginners, the police suggest:

**→ Professional drivers should accompany them to avoid risks and disputes.**

---

## **[Large Rental Companies Must Provide Training: From Control Introduction to Four Basic Training Items]**

To enhance the quality of tourism, the police encourage large motorcycle rental businesses to provide **mandatory basic training or short-term courses**.

For example, BaliMotion offers free training, including:

- Explanation of control buttons and vehicle functions
- Practical starting operations
- Four basic driving exercises
- Duration of approximately 1.5 – 2 hours

These trainings can significantly reduce the risks for beginners and make tourists feel more confident while riding.

---

## **[Inspection Actions Continue to Expand: Nighttime Raids in Canggu Target Unlicensed Riders and Those Without Helmets]**

Recently, nighttime raids have also been conducted in the Canggu area, with police setting up surprise checkpoints to catch those without helmets and other violations.

No fines were issued during this operation, as the police mainly gave **verbal warnings**, hoping to remind tourists to improve their safety awareness.

---

## **Increase in Foreign Tourist Accidents: 142 Cases, 21 Deaths in 2024**

According to police statistics, there were **142 traffic accidents** involving foreigners in 2024, a **35% increase** compared to 2023.

The data is quite alarming:

- **21 foreign fatalities**
- **3 serious injuries**
- **171 minor injuries**
- **Total damages of approximately 211 million IDR (around USD$13,000)**

The police noted a slight decrease in accidents for 2025 but still urge travelers to strictly follow traffic regulations.

## **FAQ**

**Do Foreigners Need an International Driver's License to Ride Scooters in Bali?**

Yes, a valid international driver's license along with the license from your home country is required, and the police conduct random checks.

**Can tourists with no motorcycle experience rent scooters?**

Yes, you can rent one, but the police strongly advise against riding it yourself and recommend renting through companies that provide drivers.

**Will I be fined for not wearing a helmet?**

Usually yes, but during certain traffic campaigns, warnings may be issued instead.

**Do I need insurance before renting a scooter?**

It's highly recommended to purchase travel insurance that covers traffic accidents.

**Is Canggu a hotspot for enforcement?**

Yes, due to the high concentration of foreign tourists, police often conduct surprise checks in this area.
