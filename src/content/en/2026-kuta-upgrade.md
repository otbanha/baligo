Bali's most famous tourist area, Kuta, is about to undergo a dramatic transformation! To improve the walking experience for visitors and solve the long-standing traffic congestion issues, the Badung authorities have announced a major infrastructure upgrade plan.

If you're planning a trip to Bali soon or still remember Kuta's streets as 'narrow sidewalks' and 'chaotic street parking,' then this news is definitely worth your attention.

## Plan Highlights: Creating Pedestrian-Friendly Spaces

According to the latest development blueprint, Kuta's redevelopment plan will focus on the following core aspects:

### 1. Sidewalks Widened to 4 Meters

Currently, Kuta's sidewalks are often narrow and cluttered with obstacles. The new plan aims to widen sidewalks to approximately **4 meters** in the first phase, with greenery added along the way, allowing visitors to stroll comfortably in a lush environment.

### 2. Underground Wiring

To beautify the cityscape and enhance safety, the tangled overhead power and communication lines will be moved underground. This will clean up Kuta's skyline, ensuring your photos are no longer interrupted by wires!

### 3. Eliminate Street Parking, Establish Centralized Parking Zones

For a long time, cars and motorcycles parked haphazardly along the streets have been a major cause of traffic bottlenecks. Authorities plan to build **centralized parking areas**, after which street parking on main roads will be prohibited, effectively returning the streets to pedestrians.

### 4. Introduce Electric Public Transportation

To compensate for the transportation needs after eliminating street parking, Kuta will introduce **electric public transport**. Dedicated lanes or prominent colored road markings may even be implemented to ensure smooth and eco-friendly traffic flow.

***

## Coastal Area Upgrade: 8-Meter-Wide Scenic Promenade

In addition to street renovations, Kuta's coastline is undergoing reinforcement projects to prevent erosion. Once completed, authorities plan to construct an **8-meter-wide coastal promenade** along the shore. This promenade will be divided into:

- **Pedestrian and Jogging Zone**
- **Bicycle Lane**

This means that cycling and jogging along Kuta Beach will become a delightful experience in the future.

***

## When Will It Start?

This ambitious renovation project is expected to officially commence in **2026**. Although construction may bring temporary inconveniences, in the long run, it will transform Kuta from a congested traffic hotspot into a modern, green, and pedestrian-friendly international tourist destination.

Let's look forward to a more beautiful and cleaner Kuta in Bali!
