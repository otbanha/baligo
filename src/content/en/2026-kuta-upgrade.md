Update: 2026/07/03

**Key Highlights**: In 2026, Badung authorities kick off Kuta's environmental revamp—**sidewalks widened to 4 meters, power lines buried underground, street parking banned on main roads** (replaced by centralized parking lots), introduction of electric public transport, and an 8-meter-wide coastal promenade. For travelers: Kuta will be more walkable, but **those driving or renting scooters can no longer park on the street**—use designated parking zones instead.

Bali's most famous tourist area, Kuta, is about to undergo a dramatic transformation! To enhance the walking experience for visitors and tackle long-standing traffic congestion, Badung authorities have announced a major infrastructure upgrade plan.

If you're planning a trip to Bali soon, or if your memory of Kuta's streets is still stuck on 'narrow sidewalks' and 'chaotic street parking,' this news is definitely worth your attention.

## Plan Highlights: Creating a Pedestrian-Friendly Space

According to the latest development blueprint, Kuta's redevelopment plan focuses on the following core aspects:

### 1. Sidewalks Widened to 4 Meters

Currently, Kuta's sidewalks are often narrow and cluttered. The new plan aims to widen them to about **4 meters** in the first phase, adding green belts along the way so visitors can stroll comfortably in a lush environment.

### 2. Power Lines Buried Underground

To beautify the cityscape and improve safety, the tangled overhead power and communication lines will be moved underground. This will give Kuta a cleaner skyline, free from the distraction of wires in photos!

### 3. Eliminating Street Parking, Introducing Centralized Parking Zones

For years, haphazard roadside parking by cars and motorbikes has been a major cause of traffic bottlenecks. Authorities plan to establish **centralized parking lots**, after which street parking on main roads will be banned, fully returning the streets to the people.

### 4. Introducing Electric Public Transit

To meet transportation needs after the removal of street parking, Kuta will introduce **electric public transport**. There may even be dedicated lanes or brightly colored road markings to ensure smooth and eco-friendly traffic.

***

## Waterfront Upgrade: An 8-Meter-Wide Scenic Promenade

Beyond street renovations, Kuta's coastline is also undergoing reinforcement to prevent erosion. Once completed, authorities plan to build an **8-meter-wide coastal promenade** along the shore. This promenade will be divided into:

- **Pedestrian and jogging zone**
- **Dedicated bicycle lane**

This means that cycling and jogging along Kuta Beach will become a true pleasure in the future.

***

## When Will This Happen?

This ambitious redevelopment project is expected to break ground in **2026**. While construction may cause temporary inconvenience, in the long run, it will transform Kuta from a congested traffic hotspot into a modern, green, and pedestrian-friendly international tourist destination.

Let's look forward to a more beautiful and cleaner **Bali** Kuta!
