Bali's most famous tourist area, Kuta, is about to undergo a dramatic transformation! To improve the walking experience for visitors and solve the long-standing traffic congestion issues, the Badung authorities have announced a major infrastructure upgrade plan.

If you're planning a trip to Bali soon, or if your impression of Kuta's streets still revolves around 'narrow sidewalks' and 'chaotic street parking,' then this news is definitely worth your attention.

## Plan Highlights: Creating Pedestrian-Friendly Spaces

According to the latest development blueprint, Kuta's redevelopment plan will focus on the following core aspects:

### 1. Sidewalks Widened to 4 Meters

Currently, Kuta's sidewalks are often narrow and cluttered with obstacles. The new plan aims to widen the sidewalks to approximately **4 meters** in the first phase, adding greenery along the way to allow visitors to stroll comfortably in a lush environment.

### 2. Underground Wiring

To beautify the cityscape and enhance safety, the previously tangled overhead power and communication lines will be moved underground. This will clean up Kuta's skyline, ensuring that your photos are no longer interrupted by wires!

### 3. Eliminate Street Parking, Establish Centralized Parking Zones

For a long time, the haphazard parking of cars and motorcycles along the streets has been a major cause of traffic bottlenecks. Authorities plan to build **centralized parking facilities**, after which street parking on main roads will be prohibited, effectively returning the streets to pedestrians.

### 4. Introducing Electric Public Transportation

To compensate for the transportation needs arising from the elimination of street parking, Kuta will introduce **electric public transport**. There may even be dedicated lanes or prominent colored road markings to ensure smooth and eco-friendly traffic flow.

***

## Upgrading the Coastal Area: An 8-Meter-Wide Scenic Promenade

In addition to street renovations, Kuta's coastline is undergoing reinforcement projects to prevent erosion. Once completed, authorities plan to construct an **8-meter-wide coastal promenade** along the shore. This promenade will be divided into:

- **Pedestrian and Jogging Zones**
- **Dedicated Bicycle Lanes**

This means that cycling and jogging along Kuta Beach will become a delightful experience in the future.

***

## When Will It Start?

This ambitious renovation project is expected to officially commence in **2026**. While construction may bring temporary inconveniences, in the long run, it will transform Kuta from a congested traffic hotspot into a modern, green, and pedestrian-friendly international tourist destination.

Let's look forward to a more beautiful and cleaner Kuta in Bali!
