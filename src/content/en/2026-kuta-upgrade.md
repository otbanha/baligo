**Key Highlights**: In 2026, Badung authorities are launching an environmental transformation for Kuta—**sidewalks widened to 4 meters, underground power lines, complete elimination of street parking on main roads** (replaced with centralized parking lots), introduction of electric public transport, and an additional 8-meter-wide coastal promenade along the shoreline. Practical impact for tourists: Kuta will become more walkable, but **those driving or renting scooters can no longer park randomly on the streets**—please use designated parking zones.

Bali's most iconic tourist area, Kuta, is about to undergo a dramatic transformation! To enhance the walking experience for visitors and tackle long-standing traffic congestion, the Badung authorities have announced a major infrastructure upgrade plan.

If you're planning a trip to Bali soon, or if your memory of Kuta's streets is still stuck on 

narrow sidewalks

 and 

chaotic street parking

, then this news is definitely worth your attention.

## Plan Highlights: Creating a Pedestrian-Friendly Space

To beautify the cityscape and improve safety, the tangled overhead power and communication lines will be moved underground. This will give Kuta a cleaner skyline, free from wires ruining your photos!

### 3. Eliminating Street Parking, Introducing Centralized Parking Zones

For years, haphazard parking of cars and scooters along the streets has been a major cause of traffic bottlenecks. Authorities plan to establish **centralized parking lots**, after which street parking on main roads will be banned, fully returning the streets to the people.

### 4. Introducing Electric Public Transit

To meet transportation needs after eliminating street parking, Kuta will introduce **electric public transport**. There may even be dedicated lanes or brightly colored road markings to ensure smooth and eco-friendly traffic.

***

## Waterfront Upgrade: An 8-Meter-Wide Scenic Promenade

Beyond street renovations, Kuta's coastline is undergoing reinforcement works to prevent erosion. Once completed, authorities plan to build an **8-meter-wide coastal promenade** along the shore. This promenade will be divided into:

- **Pedestrian and jogging zone**
- **Dedicated bicycle lane**

This means cycling and jogging along Kuta Beach will become a true pleasure in the future.

***

## When Will It Start?

This ambitious renovation project is expected to officially begin construction in **2026**. While the construction period may bring temporary inconvenience, in the long run, it will transform Kuta from a congested traffic hotspot into a modern, green, and pedestrian-friendly international tourist destination.

Let's look forward to a more beautiful and cleaner **Bali** Kuta!
