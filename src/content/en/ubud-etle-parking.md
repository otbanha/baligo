Anyone who's visited Ubud knows how narrow the roads are. With many tourists and vehicles, some people still insist on parking directly by the roadside — well, starting April 28th, the police are getting serious.

### This Enforcement Goes Beyond Stickers, Directly Issuing Electronic Tickets

Previously, illegal parking in Ubud might have just earned you a warning sticker, and you could continue parking without consequence. But now, things have changed.

Ubud police, in collaboration with the Road Agency, Gianyar Regency Transportation Department, and Bali's traditional community security (pecalang), have launched a crackdown on the main thoroughfare, Jalan Raya Ubud. This time, it's not just about warning stickers — vehicles in violation will directly receive electronic fine notifications through the ETLE system (Indonesia's Electronic Traffic Law Enforcement system).

In this operation, 15 vehicles were officially issued electronic tickets, while another 25 received warning stickers.

### Ubud's Traffic Congestion is Getting Worse

Honestly, Ubud's traffic situation in recent years can only be described as 'increasingly outrageous.' The narrow streets were never meant to handle the volume of traffic, and with cars parked haphazardly along the roadside, the usable road width is effectively halved.

Ubud Police Chief I Wayan Putra Antara also admitted that using shoulders and roads as free parking spaces is one of the direct causes of local traffic congestion. He stated that such enforcement actions will be regularly conducted in the future.

It's worth mentioning that before taking action, the police specifically reminded law enforcement officers to remain calm. Besides issuing tickets, they should also explain to drivers the actual impact of illegal parking on traffic. I think this approach is correct - relying solely on fines isn't enough. Only when people truly understand that 'your parking method has immobilized hundreds of vehicles behind you' is there a chance to change habits.

### Driving in Ubud? Take Parking Seriously

If you're planning to drive or rent a car in Ubud, this is worth noting in advance:

- Don't park on the roadside for convenience, especially on the main thoroughfare Jalan Raya Ubud
- There are several designated parking areas in downtown Ubud - it's recommended to park properly before walking into the city center
- ETLE electronic fines are a real-name system - it's not just stickers, they will actually track you down

Honestly, Ubud's traffic problems can't be solved overnight, but stricter law enforcement is actually good news for tourists who want to enjoy shopping. With fewer cars blocking the middle of the road, walking around becomes much smoother.
