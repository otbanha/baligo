### 🗞️ Today's Headlines: The century-old Galungan Festival kicks off today alongside the island's move to '100% mandatory waste sorting'

https://www.instagram.com/reel/DIzIuRBSQ9X/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link&igsh=NTc4MTIwNjQ2YQ==

- **Welcoming Ancestral Spirits! The biggest Hindu festival, Galungan, kicks off across the island today**: According to today's front-page coverage by *Bali Post*, the island celebrates Galungan Day, the most sacred and grandest traditional Hindu festival occurring every 210 days. From **Canggu** and **Seminyak** to Ubud, every household has erected tall Penjor bamboo poles adorned with handmade coconut leaf decorations and offerings, transforming the island into one of the most exotic and mystical landscapes of the year. Residents across the island will don stunning white and colorful traditional Batik attire, bringing their families to temples for prayers, while streets will come alive with Barong dance processions to ward off evil spirits. The state electricity company (PLN) has issued a safety reminder, urging residents and self-driving tourists to maintain a safe distance of at least 2.5 meters from overhead power lines when admiring or erecting Penjor poles, to prevent power outages caused by strong winds or moisture. Additionally, some local shops and drivers may adjust their hours due to returning home for village ceremonies, so independent travelers should confirm their plans in advance.
- **Central and local governments join forces: Island-wide launch of '100% mandatory waste sorting' green era**: To tackle the severe environmental capacity issues, Indonesia's Minister of Environment (Men LH) and the Governor held a high-level joint meeting in Denpasar today, **signing and announcing the official launch of 'Bali's 100% mandatory waste sorting movement (Gerakan Bali 100 Persen Memilah Sampah)'**. The central and local governments across nine regencies and cities (including Badung Regency and Denpasar City) have reached a rigid strategic agreement. All hotels, resorts, fine dining restaurants, and traditional communities on the island must implement strict 'organic and non-organic waste separation' at the source. Officials emphasized that non-compliance will result in garbage trucks refusing to collect waste, aiming to quickly reduce the heavy burden on landfills across the island.

### 📄 Tourist Concerns: 300 kg of trash cleared from Seminyak Beach and World Cup late-night party ban

- **Turn the Tide! Indonesian digital payment giant teams up with maritime authorities to clear 317 kg of waste from Seminyak's Petitenget Beach**: According to today's report by *Pasardana*, as a flagship environmental activity ahead of the 2026 Ocean Impact Summit (OIS), Indonesian digital wallet provider DANA partnered with the Ministry of Maritime Affairs and Fisheries (KKP) **to launch a large-scale 'Turn the Tide' beach cleanup at the popular Pantai Petitenget in Seminyak**. The event mobilized hundreds of volunteers from local universities, expat communities, and beachfront businesses. **In just a few hours, they removed 317.3 kg of plastic bottles, discarded fishing nets, and marine debris from the sand and shoreline**. Organizers urged international tourists visiting coastal spots like Seminyak and Kuta to practice plastic-free, slow travel to help protect the fragile marine ecosystem.
- **Island-wide World Cup 2026 celebrations! Provincial government cracks down: Civil servants banned from arriving late due to late-night game viewing**: As the **2026 FIFA World Cup in the US, Canada, and Mexico kicks off**, football fever has swept across Bali. Sports bars and beach clubs in major tourist areas are packed with local and international fans watching matches late into the night. Due to time zone differences, many key games air from late night to early morning on the island. The provincial government today issued a playful yet stern warning to all civil servants (ASN), **explicitly stating that no official may use 'watching late-night live broadcasts' as an excuse for arriving late to work or compromising administrative efficiency**. The government will tighten attendance checks during the tournament.
