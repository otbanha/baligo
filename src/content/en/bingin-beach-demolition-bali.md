Bali's Uluwatu Bingin Beach, a favorite among tourists and surfers, is set for a major transformation. The government announced that over 40 structures in the area, due to illegal land occupation and violations of spatial planning regulations, will be forcibly demolished starting July 21, 2025.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e640f066067761e239d9a5078ca6aaf9.jpg)

Businesses in the Bingin area have been receiving formal notices from the Badung Regency government over the past two months, urging them to rectify building structures and land occupation practices. However, the government ultimately deemed most structures non-compliant and decided to proceed with legal action.

Satpol PP (Bali Civil Police) stated that investigations revealed these structures were mostly illegally built without proper permits, and some even occupied state-owned land.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d544e0f8149e61f7127439067bf1ae52.jpg)

Badung Regent Adi Arnawa emphasized that residents and business owners were well aware that these structures were built on non-private land and must take responsibility for the outcome. He stressed the government's firm stance, fully supporting the demolition recommendations based on investigations by the provincial government and Satpol PP.

He also called on the public to avoid unnecessary interference or incitement against the action, stating, 'Our community is well aware of the land they were using. Now, it's just about restoring the land to its proper use.'

The government highlighted that businesses had been notified through various means, including letters, public notices, and WhatsApp messages, to prevent claims of ignorance as an excuse.

An anonymous Bingin Beach business owner lamented that the area was once just a fishing village, and significant resources had been invested in developing facilities as tourism grew. He expressed frustration, 'If we were violating regulations, why didn't the government stop us over the past 30 years? Now that everything is being demolished, shouldn't the government also reflect on its actions?'

The demolition affects 45 structures, including guesthouses, restaurants, tourist facilities, and beach villas, involving illegal land occupation, environmental and spatial regulation violations, and impacts on natural landscape conservation areas.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ):

1. **Why is the government demolishing buildings at Bingin Beach?**  
   The government states that these structures violate spatial planning laws and land use regulations, with some illegally occupying state-owned land, hence the enforcement of demolition.
2. **When did the demolition begin?**  
   The demolition officially started on July 21, 2025, and will continue until all illegal structures are cleared.
3. **Can objections or appeals be made to the government?**  
   Several businesses have already negotiated with local authorities but failed to reach an agreement. The government has stated that it will no longer accept new objection proposals.
4. **Will tourism be affected?**  
   In the short term, tourism activities at Bingin Beach may be limited due to the demolition. In the long run, it will help create a legal and safe tourism environment.
5. **What types of buildings are being demolished?**  
   Including homestays, beach villas, restaurants, small hotels, and other tourism-related structures, totaling 45 buildings, all suspected of illegal construction or land occupation.
