**Bottom line**: The cheapest place to buy souvenirs in Bali is Denpasar's **Kumbasari Art Market**—the island's largest **wholesale** art market (wholesale prices are significantly lower than retail in tourist areas). You'll find handicrafts, paintings, sculptures, and clothing galore, open daily until **5 PM**. Pair it with the adjacent Taman Kumbasari riverside park and the evening **Pasar Kreneng night market** (the island's largest) for a full day of shopping.

### Introducing Kumbasari Art Market, Bali's Largest Wholesale Art Market

On the second day of our Bali free and easy trip, we were all set to embark on a shopping adventure. Let me share our plan: we headed out leisurely around noon, first stopping for a hearty lunch at the small restaurant run by our Bali driver Gede Arimbawa and his wife. The meal left us energized and ready for the adventure ahead!

In the afternoon, we visited Kumbasari Art Market, Bali's largest wholesale art market (open until 5 PM daily). This market is a dream to explore, packed with Balinese-style handicrafts, paintings, and souvenirs. Whatever you're looking for, you'll find it here—from paintings to sculptures and all kinds of handmade goods, the variety is incredible. Whether you're buying something for yourself or picking out a special gift for friends and family, this is an absolute must-visit spot.

Next, we headed to Taman Kumbasari, a wonderfully laid-back riverside park. For a detailed introduction, check out the link here.

In the evening, we also visited Bali's largest night market, Pasar Kreneng. This market brings together all kinds of snacks, clothing, and handicrafts—it's like an upgraded version of a Taiwanese night market! The street food here is so delicious you'll find yourself lingering, and every bite is pure satisfaction.

At night, we returned to the riverside park, where the lights twinkled and the scenery was stunning. Standing there, gazing at Bali under the night sky, it felt like stepping into a dream world.
