## 🗞️ Today's Headlines:

- **Strengthened Accommodation Management Policies**: The Ministry of Tourism reaffirmed its commitment to further optimize the management and governance of **Bali's** accommodation industry through collaboration with stakeholders, ensuring the quality and service standards of lodging on the island to meet the anticipated global tourism demand of over 7 million visitors in 2026.
- **Major Traffic Reform in Canggu**: To thoroughly address the island's worst traffic congestion, authorities announced a new **traffic flow plan** for Canggu. Key measures include converting some core roads to **one-way streets**, optimizing intersection turning restrictions, and intensifying enforcement against illegal parking. This change is expected to significantly impact traffic flow between Batu Bolong and Berawa; visitors heading to the area in the near future should allow extra travel time.
- **Progress on North Bali Connectivity**: The provincial government announced it is accelerating the promotion of transportation optimization plans connecting the southern tourist zone to Buleleng, as a key part of regional balanced development, aiming to disperse the overcrowded tourist crowds in the south.

## 📄 Topics of Concern for Tourists: Safety Alerts and Entry Procedures

- **Cliff Safety Alert**: Following the recent helicopter rescue incident at the Uluwatu cliffs, police and the Search and Rescue Agency (Basarnas) issued an official notice today, urging tourists to strictly follow warning signs and avoid entering undeveloped steep paths.
- **Market Price Transparency**: Due to increased police oversight, basic commodity prices at major traditional markets remain stable. If tourists encounter unusually high prices while shopping, they can check for local regulatory labels.
- **Digital Entry Verification**: Airport inspection authorities remind that the **e-CD (Electronic Customs Declaration)** and the **150,000 IDR tourism tax** receipt are the core items for inspection. Starting today, the '**Levy Vouchers**' secondary inspection area in front of the arrivals hall will operate strictly; those who have not paid will be directed to counters to pay. Travelers are advised to avoid the airport's unstable Wi-Fi and save a screenshot of the QR Code before boarding.
