When traveling in Bali, many tourists opt for short-distance transportation using ride-hailing apps like Grab or Gojek. These apps offer the advantage of transparent pricing. However, tourists sometimes encounter unscrupulous drivers, which can turn their journey unpleasant. Here are two real stories sharing common ride-hailing tricks, hoping everyone can be more cautious during their travels and avoid similar troubles.

## Story 1: Airport Price Hike Trick

[Provided by Mr. Hsu, owner of Lobster King Restaurant](https://www.facebook.com/hsu.dick.7) The owner of Lobster King Restaurant often helps Taiwanese tourists who haven’t downloaded ride-hailing apps to book rides. Once, a guest in Canggu needed to go to the airport and asked him to help book a ride. The owner booked the first car through Grab, and the driver responded quickly. However, upon seeing the destination was the airport, the driver didn’t contact through Grab but instead called the owner directly, demanding IDR 400,000, while the system quoted around IDR 100,000. The owner was furious and immediately canceled the order. He booked a second car, but the same thing happened—the driver asked for IDR 300,000. The owner canceled again and finally found a driver who didn’t hike the price on the third attempt, but the whole process dragged on for nearly an hour.

Additionally, another time, a guest arrived at the restaurant via Gojek, and the driver called the owner demanding a commission. The owner ignored it, and since the driver didn’t get the commission, he later left two one-star reviews for the restaurant on Google Maps using two different accounts.

**Takeaway and Advice**: In Bali, especially when in a hurry, such as heading to the airport, always book your ride-hailing service in advance and be prepared to book multiple times if necessary. Most drivers are good, but there are a few unscrupulous ones, so stay vigilant.

## Story 2: Charter Car Upsell Trick

[Provided by Ms. Yeh](https://www.facebook.com/groups/baligo/permalink/1546686959211877/) Ms. Yeh and her friends had an unpleasant experience when they first used Grab. After getting in the car, the driver was overly enthusiastic, constantly chatting with them and steering the conversation toward nearby attractions and activities, strongly recommending charter car services in an attempt to bypass Grab’s transparent pricing system.

That day, they only planned to visit Tegalalang Rice Terrace in the afternoon to swing, so they politely declined the driver’s suggestion. When it was time to pay, the driver claimed he had no change and suggested they let him drive them back, deducting the fare from the change. They agreed, but on the return trip, the driver suddenly said the fare was three times the original amount. When they questioned why, the driver blamed traffic congestion, but checking the real-time fare on the Grab App showed no difference from the initial trip.

The driver only started negotiating the price after sensing their skepticism, eventually offering the original fare and insisting on taking them back. The tourists stood their ground, demanding their exact change, prompting the driver to finally produce the correct amount from his bag. This encounter left them questioning the integrity of some drivers, though fortunately, subsequent drivers provided satisfactory service.

**Insights and Recommendations**: When using ride-hailing services like Grab, remain vigilant if drivers attempt to upsell private car services. If a driver insists on cash payment, avoid falling into the trap of not having exact change. Additionally, document any unpleasant experiences during your trip and promptly report them to the platform to help improve service quality.
