When traveling in Bali, using ride-hailing apps like Grab or Gojek is a popular choice for short-distance transportation among many tourists. These apps offer the advantage of transparent pricing. However, tourists sometimes encounter unscrupulous drivers that can turn their journey unpleasant. Here are two real stories sharing common ride-hailing tricks, hoping everyone can be more cautious during their travels to avoid similar troubles.

## Story 1: Airport Price Hike Trick

[Provided by Mr. Lobster Restaurant owner Hsu Ping-Hui](https://www.facebook.com/hsu.dick.7): The owner of Mr. Lobster Restaurant often helps Taiwanese tourists who haven’t downloaded ride-hailing apps to book rides. Once, a guest in Canggu needed to go to the airport and asked for his assistance. The owner booked the first car through Grab, and the driver responded quickly. However, upon seeing the destination was the airport, the driver bypassed Grab and directly called the owner, demanding IDR 400,000, while the app quoted around IDR 100,000. The owner was furious and immediately canceled the booking. He tried again, but the second driver asked for IDR 300,000. After canceling again, the owner finally found a driver who didn’t hike the price on the third attempt, but the whole process took nearly an hour.

Additionally, another time, a guest arrived at the restaurant via Gojek, and the driver called the owner demanding a commission. When the owner ignored the request, the driver, failing to get the commission, later left two one-star reviews for the restaurant on Google Maps using two different accounts.

****Takeaway and Advice****: In Bali, especially when in a hurry (e.g., heading to the airport), always book your ride-hailing car in advance and be prepared to book multiple times if necessary. Most drivers are good, but there are a few unscrupulous ones, so stay cautious.

## Story 2: Car Rental Upsell Trick

[Provided by Ye Ting-Yu](https://www.facebook.com/groups/baligo/permalink/1546686959211877/): On their first time using Grab, Ms. Ye and her group had an unpleasant experience. After getting in the car, the driver was overly enthusiastic, constantly engaging them in conversation and steering the topic toward nearby attractions and activities, aggressively pushing car rental services to bypass Grab’s transparent pricing system.

That day, they only planned to visit the Tegallalang Rice Terrace Swing in the afternoon, so they politely declined the driver’s offer. When it was time to pay, the driver claimed he had no change and suggested they let him drive them back, using the change to offset the return fare. They agreed, but on the return trip, the driver suddenly tripled the fare. When questioned, he blamed traffic congestion, but checking the real-time fare on the Grab App showed no difference from the initial trip.

When the driver noticed their skepticism, he started negotiating his own price, but ultimately quoted the original fare and offered to take them back. The tourists insisted on getting their exact change, and only then did the driver retrieve the coins from his bag. This experience left them questioning the professionalism of some drivers, though thankfully subsequent drivers provided better service.

****Tips and Recommendations****: When using ride-hailing apps like Grab, remain cautious if drivers try to upsell services like private car rentals. If a driver insists on cash payment, avoid falling into the trap of not receiving exact change. Additionally, documenting any unpleasant experiences during your trip and promptly reporting them to the platform can help improve service quality.
