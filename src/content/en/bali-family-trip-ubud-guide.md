The first two nights were spent at Aloft Kuta, connecting to GWK Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park and Uluwatu attractions. 🏞️ **Why Choose Omma Bali?**

Most waterfall spots require a hike to reach, but Omma Bali stands out for its convenience:

1. **Private Elevator to the Day Club**: Skip the tiring downhill trek—perfect for lazy days and family trips.
2. **Stunning Views**: Sit in the restaurant and overlook the magnificent waterfall, enjoying nature with ease.
3. **Multiple Ways to Play**: Choose to enter the pool (with minimum spend), admire the waterfall, or walk down to the base for photos.

https://youtu.be/MmqfkXw211k?si=lRF-nUKK6GXJ-wKm

🍹 **Omma Bali: Pricing Info**

- Non-alcoholic drinks and meals are reasonably priced, but alcoholic beverages are on the higher end 🍸.
- **Minimum spend rule**: The closer your seat is to the pool or waterfall, the higher the minimum spend. If you're just stopping by briefly, there are also regular seats with no minimum spend.

👣 **A Quick Tip for Seeing the Waterfall**

It's just a 2-3 minute walk from Omma Bali to the waterfall—super easy. If you'd rather not move, the restaurant's seating offers a full view of the waterfall, with its dramatic cascade bringing a refreshing sense of calm 💦.

This garden restaurant is just about 100 meters from our stay at **Bali Bohemia Huts**. If you've just visited the Monkey Forest, you can easily reach it from Gate 3—super convenient.

🏡 **Restaurant Features & Atmosphere**

- **Garden design**: The restaurant has indoor seating in a traditional stilt house and an open-air area in the back, where you can enjoy views of lush green rice terraces in a serene setting.
- **Tradition & entertainment**: The restaurant occasionally offers Balinese traditional dance performances and cooking classes, but it's best to check with them in advance as schedules are irregular.
- **Avoiding crowds**: If you're not a fan of the bustling crowds at Ubud's famous Bebek Bengil, this is a much quieter and more relaxing spot for crispy duck.

🍴 **Must-Try Dish: Signature Crispy Duck**

- **Highlights:**
  Half a duck pre-marinated in spices, deep-fried to a golden, crispy skin that crackles with every bite. The fat is perfectly balanced—not greasy at all. Even the bones are easy to crunch, making it impossible to stop at just one piece.
- **Duck Texture:**
  While the duck meat itself is a bit on the dry side, pairing it with the restaurant's three house-made sauces adds layers of flavor. Each sauce has a different level of spiciness, with a fresh and delightful taste that will have you licking your fingers for more.

🌟 **Other Food Options**

Though the restaurant is best known for its crispy duck, the other dishes are also impressive:

1. **Lemon Chicken:**
   Tender chicken paired with a tangy lemon sauce and soft potato balls—flavorful and satisfying.
2. **Spaghetti Carbonara:**
   Rich, creamy sauce with a strong milky flavor, perfectly cooked pasta—simple yet satisfying.
3. **Nasi Campur:**
   A classic Indonesian dish with a mix of traditional sides. Word has it the version here is incredibly authentic and worth a try.

🍹 **Drink Recommendations**

- **Mango Smoothie:** Rich fruit flavor with a silky texture—perfect for cooling down.
- **Coconut Juice:** Fresh, natural coconut water—refreshing and thirst-quenching.
- **Ice Tea:** Light and palate-cleansing, pairs well with any meal.

💰 **Price Guide**

- **Crispy Duck:** 140,000 IDR (approx. USD$5)
- **Lemon Chicken:** 60,000 IDR (approx. USD$2)
- **Spaghetti Carbonara:** 60,000 IDR (approx. USD$2)
- **Mango Smoothie:** 35,000 IDR (approx. USD$1)
- **Coconut Juice:** 27,000 IDR (approx. USD$1)
- **Ice Tea:** 18,000 IDR (approx. USD$1)

Laka Leke Restaurant perfectly blends traditional Balinese garden charm with classic cuisine. Whether it's the spacious and comfortable dining setting, the distinctive traditional stilt-house design, or the signature crispy duck's irresistible crunch, this place is well worth a visit.

If you want to avoid the crowds at Ubud's popular restaurants without missing out on classic Balinese flavors, add this understated yet amazing spot to your itinerary right away!

📍**Location & Transportation Tips:**

Located near Bali Bohemia, just a 100-meter walk from Monkey Forest Gate 3. It’s recommended to visit the Monkey Forest in the morning and then walk directly to dine here.

❗**Important Notes**: Upon entry, you can pick up a multilingual paper guide (including Chinese). A full loop of the sanctuary takes about 30–60 minutes. Mornings are less crowded with more monkeys, making it ideal for an early visit.

🐒 **Tips for Interacting with Monkeys**

Although the monkeys in the forest are relatively gentle, please follow these rules:

1. **Do not stare directly into the monkeys' eyes**
2. **Do not bring plastic bags or plastic bottles** (monkeys may snatch them)
3. **Do not touch or disturb the monkeys**
4. **Do not feed the monkeys**

Looking up, you can see monkeys leaping through the treetops; underfoot, a forest path blanketed with banyan roots; all around, sacred Hindu temples. Monkeys and temples coexist in harmony—this serene atmosphere is incredibly healing.

🏛️ **A 17th-Century Ancient Temple**

Within the grounds stands a Hindu temple built in the 17th century. Although you can't enter, the moss-covered temple walls alone are already breathtaking.

📸 **The Secret to Monkey Selfies**

Staff on-site offer professional monkey-feeding photo services, which require an additional photo ticket costing 50,000 IDR (about USD$3). The staff will help capture fun moments like monkeys jumping onto your shoulders or feeding them—safe and efficient, well worth a try.

**⚠️ Note: Do not feed the monkeys on your own to avoid dangerous behavior!**

**About Pura Tirta Empul: Sacred Springs and Ancient Beliefs**

The temple is named after the holy springs that surround it, featuring over a dozen different spouts, each symbolizing a distinct prayer intention, such as 'cleansing the soul,' 'warding off misfortune,' 'healing ailments,' and 'banishing nightmares.' After completing the purification ritual in the springs, local devotees enter the temple to pray, demonstrating their devotion to the divine.

**Visitor Information & Fees**

Visitors need to purchase tickets to enter Tirta Empul Temple, and the fees are quite affordable:

- **Adult ticket: 50,000 IDR** (approximately USD$2)
- **Child ticket: 25,000 IDR** (approximately USD$1)

The ticket includes borrowing a traditional colored sarong, which is required for entering the temple. However, note that the colored sarong cannot be used for entering the water. If you want to experience the **holy water purification ritual**, you'll need to pay additional fees:

- Green sarong rental fee: 10,000 IDR (approximately USD$0.3)
- Locker rental fee: 15,000 IDR (approximately USD$0.5)

These small fees are not only affordable but also allow you to deeply experience this unique traditional culture.

**How to Participate in the Holy Water Purification Ritual?**

The holy water purification ritual may seem simple, but it follows a specific order and rules:

1. **Prepare the green sarong and locker**, change clothes, and head to the holy water pool.
2. Facing the pool, start from the **left pool**, purifying at the first spout in sequence.
3. **Skip the second-to-last and third-to-last spouts** (these are reserved for offerings to the deceased).
4. After finishing the left side, move to the **right pool**.
5. At the right pool, you only need to pray at the first spout.

At each spout, follow these steps for prayer:

- Press your palms together and make a wish facing the spout.
- Wet your hair with the holy water and sprinkle the water over your entire body.

The cool spring water instantly washes away the fatigue of the journey, leaving you feeling light in body and soul. It's a brand-new cultural experience, absolutely worth trying!

**Touristy yet still retaining a pure, local warmth**

Although Tirta Empul has become a popular tourist attraction, with Western visitors in green sarongs performing purification rituals in the pools—a rather amusing scene—the temple still preserves its genuine local warmth. The staff are friendly and welcoming, with no exorbitant extra charges, leaving you feeling nothing but goodwill and smiles.

⚠️ **Friendly reminder: Respect the local culture**

When experiencing the purification ritual, be sure to maintain a solemn and respectful attitude, as many local devotees are still engaged in sincere prayers and bathing. Avoid excessive photography that might disturb others.

**Recommended nearby attraction: Pura Gunung Kawi**

**The Story Behind Kopi Luwak**

Kopi Luwak, known as **Civet Coffee** in English, gets its name from the process where civet cats eat coffee cherries, and the beans undergo enzymatic digestion before being excreted. This quirky method has earned it the nickname 'cat poop coffee.'

As one of the main producers of Kopi Luwak, Bali is home to numerous tourist farms where visitors can observe and taste the coffee. Most farms don't charge an entry fee and offer free samples of coffee and tea, a setup often called a **Coffee/Tea Plantation Tour**. But this free experience often comes with a catch—staff enthusiastically promote their products, which can feel a bit pushy.

**Exploring Satria Agrowisata: A Blend of Knowledge and Fun**

Upon arriving at **Satria Agrowisata**, we were greeted by a lush botanical garden filled with fruit trees, coffee plants, and spices like mangosteen, Arabica coffee, and ginger. This garden gave us a fascinating glimpse into the raw ingredients behind coffee.

Next, the farm staff guided us through the process of making Kopi Luwak, explaining how the coffee beans are selected and cleaned from civet droppings. Surprisingly, even with just a couple of visitors, the staff patiently explained everything with great enthusiasm and friendliness.

**A Surprise Tasting of 13 Free Beverages**

After a brief introduction to coffee-making, the staff brought out **13 different drinks** for us to sample for free, including various coffees, teas, and specialty beverages—each with its own unique flavor and benefits. Sitting under the dense canopy of trees, sipping these drinks was not only a treat for the palate but also a feast for the eyes, making it a truly relaxing experience.

**One of the Highlights: Bali CatPoo Chino**

On a hillside, a striking sign read the amusing name 'Bali CatPoo Chino'—a playful twist on 'cappuccino' (cat=cat, poo=excrement), and a key attraction for civet coffee. Intrigued, we ordered a cup of civet coffee, priced at **50,000 Indonesian Rupiah** (about USD$3), to taste the legendary brew firsthand. For coffee novices like us, it didn't taste much different from regular black coffee, but it was certainly a unique experience.

**Shopping Area & Tips**

Toward the end of the tour, you'll enter the merchandise area, where shelves are packed with products like coffee powder, spiced teas, and dried fruit. Although online reviews had warned that the flavors of take-home items might not be as rich as those sampled on-site, the staff's enthusiastic sales pitch still won us over. I ended up buying a pack of avocado coffee powder for **130,000 Indonesian Rupiah** (about USD$4), which I considered a small 'admission fee' for the free tour.

**Special Recommendation: Other Options**

If you find the pushy shopping atmosphere uncomfortable, consider visiting **Bali Pulina** instead. According to online sources, the entrance fee there is **100,000 Indonesian Rupiah** (about USD$3), which includes coffee and tea tastings along with snacks, offering a more relaxed and pleasant vibe. The scenery there is also said to be stunning, providing a different kind of experience.

**A Unique Memory of a Cup of Civet Coffee**

Taking a moment during the journey to savor a cup of civet coffee—though for coffee novices like us, the nuances might be hard to discern—was still a worthwhile experience. Witnessing the production process and learning the story behind the beans added depth to the visit.
