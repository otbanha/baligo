## Trip Planning Tips

### 1. Overpacking Your Itinerary

**Mistake**: Many travelers underestimate the size of Bali and try to visit all the attractions in a short time, resulting in a rushed schedule that prevents them from fully enjoying each location.

Although Bali is an island, it is actually quite large and diverse, offering unique charms from the beaches in the south to the mountains in the north. When planning your itinerary, choose areas based on your stay duration:

- **Within a week**: Select 1-2 main accommodation bases to minimize frequent moves.
- **Two weeks**: Cover 3-4 accommodation bases with reasonable travel time.
- **Three weeks or more**: Plan up to 5-6 locations, but avoid making the schedule too heavy.

Try to reduce frequent accommodation changes to avoid wasting time packing and moving, which can leave you exhausted.

### 3. Scheduling Island Travel at the End

**Mistake**: Many travelers plan their trips to the Nusa Islands (Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Ceningan, Nusa Penida) or the Gili Islands towards the end of their journey. However, if bad weather causes ferry cancellations, it could disrupt your return plans.

To avoid delays in your return due to weather-related ferry cancellations, it's advisable to schedule these island trips in the middle or early part of your journey. This way, even if delays occur, they won't affect your overall travel plans.

## Safety and Health

To ensure your Bali trip is safe and comfortable, it’s crucial to avoid common health and safety pitfalls. Additionally, respecting local culture and eco-tourism ethics can help you connect more deeply with Bali. Below are some common mistakes and suggestions regarding safety, health, and cultural etiquette.

### 7. Bank Card Safety

**Mistake**: Using credit cards for large purchases without considering the risk of fraud.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0648b8fa6386a0758be5f8b1fa666c66.jpg)

Certain areas in Bali may have higher risks of credit card fraud, so it's recommended to protect your funds with the following methods:

- **Use a debit card with limited funds**: Keep your debit card balance below USD$300 to minimize potential losses from fraud.

- **Online bank transfers**: For larger payments or expenses, transfer funds via online banking to avoid keeping high balances on your card.

- **Use e-wallets**: Whenever possible, use digital payment methods like Gojek to reduce the need for physical card usage.

### 8. Mosquito Protection

**Mistake**: Ignoring the threat of mosquitoes.

Dengue fever is not uncommon in Bali, so mosquito protection is crucial. Here are some essential items and tips:

**Mistake**: Ignoring Bali's rapid weather changes during the rainy season without proper preparation.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c86655f684007466ca064fac6b244c4f.png)

Bali's rainy season typically occurs from November to April. While it doesn't rain heavily every day, the weather changes quickly, often shifting suddenly from sunny skies to heavy downpours.

- **Carry a raincoat**: A lightweight raincoat is more practical than an umbrella, as rainstorms come swiftly and are accompanied by strong winds.
- **Plan morning activities**: The weather is usually more stable in the morning, so it's advisable to schedule outdoor activities before noon to avoid sudden afternoon showers.

### 10. Monkey Safety Guidelines

https://youtube.com/shorts/tpPWsvsH-Eo

**Mistake**: Ignoring the potential danger of monkeys by carrying valuables or food while interacting with them.

In certain areas of Bali, such as Uluwatu (Uluwatu Temple) and the Monkey Forest in Ubud, monkeys are common "residents." While they may appear cute, they can be aggressive, especially when interested in valuables.

- **Avoid carrying valuables or food**: Monkeys are particularly drawn to food and shiny objects, so keep such items out of sight.
- **Avoid eye contact**: Making eye contact with monkeys can be dangerous, as some may perceive it as a challenge.
- **Protect glasses and phones**: Monkeys often snatch items like glasses and phones from tourists, so keep them secure in monkey-populated areas.
- **Maintain distance**: While monkeys may seem friendly, remember they are wild animals and should be observed from a safe distance.

## Cultural Etiquette

### 11. Respect Local Culture

> **Mistake**: Ignoring local religious and cultural etiquette, leading to disrespectful behavior.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2051bf25b8c56908609a59e3c8151f2a.jpg)

Bali is a cultural hub of Hinduism, where locals place great importance on religious rituals and traditions. Respecting their customs is crucial for visitors. Here are some key cultural etiquettes:

- **Temple Dress Code**: When entering temples, dress modestly and wear a sarong (temples often provide these for free).
- **Avoid Stepping on Offerings**: On Bali's streets, you'll often see small basket-like offerings. These are daily offerings made by locals, so be careful not to step on them.

Further Reading: [Understanding Bali's Offering Rituals: Canang Sari](/blog/2024-07-16-66964aa7fd89780001c7051c/)

- **Taboo Behaviors**: Do not touch someone's head, as it is considered disrespectful in local culture. Additionally, using your left hand to pass items is seen as impolite.
- **Remove Shoes Indoors**: When entering a local's home or certain shops, remove your shoes as a sign of respect.
- **Learn Basic Greetings**: Learning a few simple phrases in the local language, such as "thank you," can help you build rapport with locals.
  - Indonesian "Thank You": **Terima kasih**
  - Balinese "Thank You": **Suksma**

### 12. Ethical Animal Tourism

> **Mistake**: Participating in unethical animal tourism activities that harm animals.

Bali offers many animal-related tourist activities, but many involve inhumane treatment of animals, sparking significant controversy. To protect wildlife, it's recommended to avoid the following activities:

- **Zoos and Marine Parks**: Many animals in zoos and marine parks are kept in confined spaces, unable to move freely.
- **Elephant Parks**: Some parks offer elephant rides, but these elephants are often mistreated and in poor health. Bali has banned elephant riding island-wide since 2026.
- **Wild Animal Performances**: Wild animals in performances are often subjected to cruel training to perform unnatural behaviors.

Support activities that respect animals' natural habitats, such as:

- **_Dolphin Watching in Lovina_**: Here, dolphins roam freely in open waters rather than being confined to aquariums.
- **_Turtle Watching in Natural Habitats_**: Join organizations or activities that support wildlife conservation to observe wild turtles without interfering with their natural lives.

## **_Practical Tips_**

When traveling in Bali, being well-prepared and understanding local conditions can make your trip more enjoyable and help avoid potential issues. Here are some practical tips for traveling in Bali to help you have a smoother vacation.

### 13. Currency Exchange

> **_Mistake_**: Exchanging cash before departure or at the airport.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e33228060f6e7d878900e7d041abfc80.png)

- **_Exchange After Arrival_**: Refer to the '[Bali Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)' to learn how and where to exchange currency.
- **_Avoid Carrying Large Amounts of Cash_**: Carrying too much cash increases risk. Using a debit card or electronic payments is safer.

### 14. Bargaining Tips

> **_Mistake_**: Accepting the first price offered at markets or street vendors without understanding local bargaining culture.

- **_Prices Are Negotiable for Non-Fixed Items_**: In many open-air markets and small stalls in Bali, prices are not fixed, and you can bargain appropriately.
- **_Avoid Accepting the First Offer_**: Sellers often start with a higher price. Try to negotiate a lower price and purchase only when both parties are satisfied.
- **_Support the Local Economy_**: While bargaining is common, avoid excessively lowballing prices. Respect local vendors to support their livelihoods.

### 15. Weather Awareness

> **Mistake:** Ignoring Bali's weather changes and not checking the forecast in advance.

- **Follow BMKG (Bali Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysical Agency) on their** [**official Instagram account**](https://www.instagram.com/infobmkg/): They post daily English travel weather forecasts.
- **Use the Accuweather App:** Often provides more accurate predictions than Google.
- **Check weather on Windy.com:** Great for Bali's weather forecasts, especially for wave and wind speed information.
- **Morning activities are ideal:** Bali's weather is usually more stable in the morning, making it perfect for outdoor activities.
- **Afternoons are prone to rain:** Especially during the rainy season, short showers are common in the afternoon, so plan indoor activities or rest during this time.

### 16. Packing Tips

> **Mistake:** Bringing unnecessary clothing or ignoring the local weather conditions.

Based on Bali's climate and activity needs, here are the essentials:

- **Lightweight and breathable clothing:** Due to Bali's hot and humid climate, pack light and airy outfits to stay comfortable in the heat.
- **Swimwear:** Whether or not you plan to stay near the beach, swimwear is a must, as Bali offers countless beautiful beaches and pools to enjoy.
- **Simple flats or sandals:** Most activities in Bali, like exploring markets, beach walks, or temple visits, can be comfortably done in flats or sandals.
- **Light jacket for mountainous areas:** If you're heading to Ubud or Munduk, evenings can be cooler, so a light jacket will keep you comfortable.

### 17. Motorcycle Safety

> **Mistake**: Ignoring motorcycle safety measures.

- **Always wear a helmet**: Whether you're driving yourself or riding as a passenger, wearing a helmet is mandatory. It's not just a legal requirement but also a crucial safety measure.
- **Request a spare helmet when booking rides**: When using ride-hailing services like Gojek or Grab for motorbike rides, always ask the driver to provide you with a helmet.
- **Follow traffic rules**: The road conditions and traffic regulations in Bali may differ from your home country. Drive cautiously and adhere to local traffic laws.

### 18. Ignoring Local Food Safety

> **Mistake**: Not paying attention to food hygiene and casually eating at street stalls or unclean places.

https://youtu.be/GivvqCXp11A

- **Choose clean restaurants or well-reviewed street stalls**: Bali offers many delicious local dishes and restaurants, but not all places meet hygiene standards. Opt for restaurants with good cleanliness practices or stalls recommended by locals.
- **Be cautious about drinking water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly. Always choose bottled water or ensure the restaurant provides safe drinking water.
- **Avoid raw or undercooked food**: Especially in remote areas or unfamiliar restaurants, raw or undercooked food can pose a risk of stomach discomfort. Prioritize cooked meals.
- **Carry stomach medicine**: If you have a sensitive stomach, carry common medications like anti-diarrheal pills or probiotics for emergencies.
