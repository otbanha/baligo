## **Bali Travel: On-Arrival Visa or E-Visa?**

Bali, Indonesia's most famous tourist destination, attracts visitors from around the world every year. For foreign travelers from non-ASEAN countries, entering Bali requires either applying for an on-arrival visa or obtaining an e-visa in advance. So, which option is better?

### **On-Arrival Visa**

The on-arrival visa is processed at Bali's international airport upon arrival. The application fee is IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$16), valid for 30 days, extendable once, allowing a maximum stay of 60 days.

The advantage of the on-arrival visa is its simplicity. Upon arrival at Bali's international airport, travelers need only present a passport valid for at least six months, a return ticket or onward ticket to another country, and payment of IDR 500,000 in cash (specific currencies accepted, [see here](/blog/indonesia-bali-visa-evoa-faq/)) or credit card (accepted card types [see here](/blog/indonesia-bali-visa-evoa-faq/)).

However, the downside of the on-arrival visa is the unpredictable processing time. It could be quick, or you might end up waiting in line for hours. Some travelers have mentioned, "Applying for an e-visa is too troublesome, and getting an on-arrival visa at immigration only took about ten minutes." However, **this is a matter of luck**! Others have spent over an hour waiting in line for their on-arrival visa. Those who breeze through are truly fortunate!

If your flight coincides with arrivals from Europe or America, where most travelers don't have e-visas, the queues for manual payment and visa issuance can be extremely long. After getting the visa, you'll still need to queue again for passport stamping at immigration. Is it worth wasting precious vacation time? The choice is yours!

### **eVisa**

The eVisa is a visa that travelers can apply for online through the official website of the Indonesian Immigration Directorate before departure. The application fee for the eVisa is IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$16), valid for 30 days and can be extended once, allowing a maximum stay of 60 days. However, additional handling fees apply for credit card payments. [See here for more details](/blog/indonesia-bali-visa-evoa-faq/)

The advantage of the eVisa is its straightforward application process, which can be completed within 14 days before departure by following these steps:

1. [Fill out the form on the website](https://molina.imigrasi.go.id/) (<--This is the only official website! Beware of fake sites online)
2. A passport valid for more than six months
3. Upload the passport (photo page) and a photo (a clear photo taken with a phone is acceptable)
4. Prepare a credit card for payment (Online payments require Visa, Mastercard, or JCB credit cards. Up to five people can apply/use one credit card. Amex cards are not accepted! The final amount after surcharge is Rp.519,500)

The processing time for the eVisa is short, usually taking only a few minutes to complete. The benefit of obtaining the eVisa in advance is that you can avoid the queue for visa on arrival at the airport. Starting from 2024, you can also use the completely queue-free electronic gates (see [Bali's Automated Immigration System with Facial Recognition Launched in 2024: eVisa Holders Can Expedite Clearance](/blog/2024-bali-automated-immigration/))

Both visa on arrival and eVisa have their pros and cons, and travelers can choose the appropriate method based on their needs and preferences. If travelers do not want to apply for a visa in advance or spend too much time researching online visa applications, they can opt for a visa on arrival. If travelers want to save time queuing for visa processing after landing, they can choose the eVisa.
