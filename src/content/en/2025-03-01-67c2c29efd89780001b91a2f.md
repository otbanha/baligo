### Is Bali Considering a Daily Tourism Tax—Will It Become the 'Next Bhutan'?

Bali is contemplating introducing a **daily tourism tax** for foreign visitors, inspired by Bhutan's approach, with the goal of attracting 'high-quality' tourists and boosting government revenue.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8577e4982baadfd2f9b3e465a7b5c8ea.png)

**Puspa Negara**, President of the Bali Tourism Association, emphasized the need to establish a tourist screening system similar to Bhutan's. He also plans to develop local tourist attractions and enhance human resources. His initiative may gain support from the local government and lay the foundation for future tourism policies.

As early as **2023**, Bali's Governor **Wayan Koster** approved this plan as a measure to control tourist flow. He believes it will not only effectively curb **overtourism** but also preserve the local environment and culture.

### Bhutan's 'Sustainable Development Fee' Serves as a Reference Point

Since **September 2022**, all visitors entering Bhutan have been required to pay a **$100** daily 'Sustainable Development Fee,' which is expected to increase to **$200** per day by **2027**.

If Bali adopts a similar policy, it could be a **nightmare** for short-term vacationers and expatriates alike. Imagine having to pay **thousands of dollars** extra simply because of foreign status—this would be a significant burden for long-term residents.

### The 'High-Quality Tourist' Policy May Transform Bali's Landscape

The concept of attracting 'high-quality tourists' has become a goal for government officials in major tourist destinations worldwide. However, tourism is a complex ecosystem, and excessive intervention may disrupt the existing balance, potentially leading to the loss of its original tourism value.

Bali attracts global visitors not only because of its stunning natural landscapes, surfing spots, and cultural charm but also due to its inclusive and diverse community atmosphere, fostering harmonious interactions between locals and foreign visitors.

However, a significant increase in daily tourism taxes could alter Bali's social structure, potentially resulting in:

- Small local businesses and family-run guesthouses struggling to survive, possibly being replaced by luxury hotel chains
- Digital nomads and long-term residents moving to more affordable destinations like nearby Lombok
- Once-bustling beach clubs, bars, and cultural markets becoming deserted, leading to the disappearance of the island's vibrant social atmosphere

Would Bali still be the soulful and lively paradise that visitors cherish?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7817b31199234c7f040aeea7339a27e9.png)

### Bali Currently Has an 'Entry Fee'

In fact, since 2023, Bali has implemented a one-time entry fee of IDR 150,000 (approximately USD$10) for foreign tourists. Compared to Bhutan's daily tourism tax, this fee remains negligible and does not significantly impact most visitors.

### Will Bali Increase Its Tourism Tax Burden in the Future?

Although discussions about raising tourism taxes have occurred multiple times within the Bali government, Indonesia's Tourism Minister, Sandiaga Uno, explicitly opposed the proposal to increase the daily tourism tax to USD$50 in 2024, believing it could affect Bali's appeal as a tourist destination.

Whether Bali will follow Bhutan's model of implementing a daily tourism tax policy in the future remains to be seen, depending on further government decisions and societal responses.

## 📌 5 Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Does Bali currently charge a tourism tax?

Yes, Bali has implemented a one-time entry fee for foreign tourists starting in 2023, set at 150,000 Indonesian Rupiah (approximately USD$10).

### 2. Will Bali's tourism tax increase?

There is no confirmed news yet, but the government is discussing raising the fee, potentially adopting a daily tourism tax policy similar to Bhutan's model.

### 3. Why is Bali considering a daily tourism tax?

This initiative aims to address the issue of overtourism, improve the quality of visitors, and increase government revenue to protect cultural and environmental resources.

### 4. How will the tourism tax impact Bali's tourism industry?

If the tax is set too high, it could lead to the closure of small businesses, a loss of long-term residents, and negatively affect the diversity of local communities and the tourism sector.

### 5. What is Bhutan's 'Sustainable Development Fee'?

Starting in 2022, Bhutan implemented a daily tourist fee of $100, with plans to increase it to $200 per day by 2027, as a source of funding for environmental protection and infrastructure development.
