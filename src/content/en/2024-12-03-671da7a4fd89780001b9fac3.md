## **September 5: Spectacular Nusa Penida Day Trip and Snorkeling Experience**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_732ed238988d475c0faa6b704e8e9383.jpg)

Today's highlight was a day trip to **Nusa Penida**. We set off early in the morning, and our private driver even arranged for his girlfriend Indah to be our assistant guide, accompanying us throughout the trip. She helped us choose good seats on the boat, took photos for us, and assisted with keeping our belongings safe. Upon arriving at Nusa Penida, we visited the island's stunning spots, such as **Broken Beach** and **Kelingking Beach**, where the sapphire-like clear blue waters were truly awe-inspiring.

The subsequent snorkeling activity was an unforgettable experience. We visited three snorkeling spots and marveled at the diverse marine life. Although we didn't see any manta rays in Manta Bay and got stung by a small jellyfish, causing a slight sting, this unique experience became another memorable part of our journey. To our surprise, we encountered a pod of dolphins jumping on our boat ride, saving us the planned trip to North Bali to see dolphins—a truly unexpected gift.

This trip to Nusa Penida, from the island's stunning landscapes to the underwater wonders, truly allowed us to appreciate the natural beauty of Bali.

> Editor's Note: Siobak: Balinese Cuisine from Singaraja

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5c5fd5848222adbc7cbc5b7ebac95bdf.jpg)

Siobak is a traditional dish from Singaraja, made from pork. Its name originates from Chinese culture (char siu), perfectly blending local and Chinese flavors. Since the local population practices Hinduism, beef is considered sacred, making pork a popular choice.

This dish is seasoned with spices, with the pork being boiled at low temperature to remove any gaminess, then mixed with cloves, shallots, garlic, and other spices, resulting in a unique flavor.

Whether in Singaraja or at specific restaurants in Denpasar, you can savor this delicacy. If you have the chance to visit Bali, be sure to try the distinctive flavor of Siobak!

Early in the morning, the staff at Ubud Element prepared a birthday cake and softly sang 'Happy Birthday,' giving us an incredibly heartwarming surprise in the early hours of Bali. After breakfast, we took a short break and then visited the **Tegalalang Rice Terraces**. The area was meticulously arranged like a painting, and despite the crowds and the rule prohibiting bringing water inside, the terraces remained a perfect spot for capturing beautiful photos.

Next, we headed to Bali's famous **Tanah Lot** temple. This sacred temple sits on a rocky outcrop in the sea and can only be approached during low tide. We followed the crowd as we walked closer, with the sea breeze gently brushing against us and the waves softly lapping at the rocks, sending sprays of water into the air. The majestic and sacred scene left us breathless. Despite the number of visitors, the temple's mysterious aura still filled us with awe. We approached the temple, lightly touched some holy water, and prayed for peace and blessings.

In the evening, we arranged a special birthday dinner for her at **Cuca Restaurant**. Located in Jimbaran, the restaurant was illuminated by flickering candlelight and offered a charming night view. We enjoyed a creative tapas-style meal, and the restaurant thoughtfully prepared a small birthday gift, creating a romantic and warm atmosphere. We specifically ordered a set menu called "Nicholas Tse's Favorite," thinking it must be something cool, but... well, let's keep it mysterious (laughs). ![（張家偉提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1033d331528078fc690724949582df98.jpg)

As our trip neared its end, the surprises continued. We started the day by visiting **GWK Cultural Park** (Garuda Wisnu Kencana), known for its giant Garuda statue and rich displays of traditional Balinese dance performances. The park hosts different dance shows almost every hour, showcasing the beauty of Balinese culture. We watched two or three performances, each unique in its own way. Before each show, we used ChatGPT to translate the descriptions and take photos, which helped us better understand the mythological stories behind the performances, adding to our enjoyment.

For lunch, we chose **Waroeng De Dusun**, recommended by our driver. This restaurant serves delicious and affordable Indonesian-style Chinese cuisine. The flavors were refreshing and satisfying, definitely not a disappointment.

In the afternoon, we drove to **Uluwatu Temple**. Be careful of the monkeys along the way! These monkeys are known for their cleverness and cunning, and they can quickly snatch sunglasses, hats, or even hand sanitizer from tourists. We witnessed a monkey 'unlocking' a tourist's belongings and happily chewing on them, requiring the staff to 'redeem' them with food. It's best to leave valuables in the car to avoid trouble.

The entire performance was accompanied only by the rhythmic chanting of dozens of dancers, creating a captivating background sound. As the performance neared its end, a spectator proposed to his beloved amidst the flames, blending the surprise proposal with the classic performance, making it a memorable highlight of our trip.

## **Important PS: Arriving in Bali – From Smooth Customs to Luggage Troubles**

One thing worth mentioning is that our arrival in Bali initially seemed very smooth. We had already completed our visa application online and paid the tourism tax beforehand, and the customs process was surprisingly quick and efficient. Aside from a slight delay of 3 to 5 minutes during the new health declaration step, the overall procedure was straightforward and reassuring. However, the first hiccup of our journey quietly unfolded at the baggage carousel.

At the time, like other passengers, we anxiously waited for our luggage. Nearly an hour later, our suitcase finally slid out slowly from the conveyor belt. When I saw my luggage, I quickly grabbed it, and everything appeared normal on the surface, so I left the airport without a second thought. However, upon arriving at the hotel and opening the suitcase, I discovered that the zipper had been pried open, and even the TSA lock was damaged. A small amount of loose change and a golden trinket (though not real gold) were missing. Fortunately, important documents and credit cards were still intact, which gave me some relief. Still, I learned a valuable lesson from this experience: always carry valuables with you!

### **Seeking Help and Kindness: Driver Dwik’s Warm Support**

After discovering the tampered luggage, I immediately contacted the Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Surabaya, the airport ground handling company, and VietJet Air’s customer service. The three parties kept shifting responsibility among themselves, leaving us exhausted. The Taipei office suggested we approach the ground handling company, who then redirected us to the airline’s customer service. In the end, we received no substantial help.

At this point, our private driver, Dwik, showed incredible warmth and kindness. Upon learning about the situation, he felt deeply apologetic, even blaming himself for not taking better care of our luggage. Dwik proactively noted the incident’s details and prepared to file a police report. Although we ultimately decided against reporting it due to time constraints, Dwik’s sincerity and responsible attitude deeply touched us. He even stayed up all night, continuously discussing the matter with fellow drivers and contacting travel companies to see if similar cases had occurred. His efforts made us feel supported and cared for in an unfamiliar environment.
