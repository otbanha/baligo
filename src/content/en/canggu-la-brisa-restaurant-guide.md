It's hard to imagine that an island roughly twice the size of New Taipei City is home to tens of thousands of hotels/villas and even more restaurants. This place is simply that popular for tourism!

Among the many restaurants, this one hailed as 'the most beautiful restaurant in Bali' is definitely not to be missed!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a516952fa874238cf08ef63be006c56d.jpg)

La Brisa is located on the foreshore of Echo Beach in Canggu, Bali's current hottest tourist spot (you can check out Xiao Jie's [Guide to 80 Attractions in Canggu](/blog/2023-02-11-64db6b7efd897800013a9815/)). It's one of Bali's most famous beaches, offering absolutely stunning, endless culinary delights and genuine Balinese hospitality.

La Brisa is more than just a restaurant - it's a beach club. The concept is right there in the name - La Brisa, meaning the wild, enchanting, and pleasantly soothing sea breeze. Built using reclaimed wood from over 500 old fishing boats and professionally hand-decorated, La Brisa Bali stands today as one of the island's most stylish and eco-conscious venues, exuding authentic Balinese charm while serving up some truly mouthwatering food and cocktails.

![La Brisa景觀 + 本人照騙](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_11aec55b3776eca04aea8a96c7fc8fb1.png)

La Brisa scenic view + my photo deception

The menu here is specially crafted for all seafood lovers, with the sole purpose of offering local produce at reasonable prices. The fish served daily at La Brisa is freshly caught by Balinese fishermen, and nearly all ingredients used in the kitchen are sourced from Bali's local products.

This place is a work of art in itself, impossible to describe with mere words. One must experience it firsthand to fully understand and appreciate its beauty and authenticity.

Stepping into La Brisa feels like being invited into a home – specifically, a humble fisherman's abode in Bali. Handcrafted decor and marine-themed elements abound, with sand, seashells, antique buoys, handmade fishing rods, woven grass baskets, fishing nets, and intricately crafted chairs, benches, and swings all blending together to create a magical illusion that transports you back in time.

It's more than just a place. It's an experience. Whether it's the refreshing mornings, sunny afternoons, or the golden hours of sunset, all the best that Bali has to offer can be found here.

What a treat to enjoy delicious food in such a wonderful setting while admiring the beautiful sunset!

## How to Make a Reservation at La Brisa?

**Online Reservation**
- Visit [La Brisa's official website](https://labrisa-bali.com/) and click the "Reserve" button.
    - Select the date, time, seating type, and number of people.
    - Fill in your name, email address, phone number, and other information.
    - Click the "Submit" button to complete your reservation.
**Phone Reservation**
- Call La Brisa's phone number: 0811-3946-666.
    - Provide your name, email address, phone number, reservation date, time, seating type, and number of people.
    - Follow the instructions from the customer service representative to complete your reservation.
**On-Site Reservation**
- If you decide to visit spontaneously on the same day, you can also make a reservation on-site. However, La Brisa does not accept reservations during peak hours from 4 PM to 7 PM daily.

**Minimum Spend**

La Brisa has minimum spending requirements for certain seating types. On weekdays, the minimum spend ranges from IDR 400,000 for two people (averaging IDR 200,000 per person) to IDR 1,800,000 for ten people (averaging IDR 180,000 per person), depending on the seating type or category. The minimum spend can be used to purchase food or drinks.

**Additional Information**

- La Brisa offers towel rental service for IDR 50,000 per towel (approx. USD$3).
- La Brisa provides vegetarian/vegan options.
- La Brisa is family-friendly and features a shallow swimming pool.
