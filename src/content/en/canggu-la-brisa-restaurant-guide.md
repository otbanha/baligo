It’s hard to imagine that an island roughly twice the size of New Taipei City is home to tens of thousands of hotels/villas and several times more restaurants. This place is simply too popular for tourism!

Among so many restaurants, this one, hailed as 'Bali's Most Beautiful Restaurant,' is a must-visit!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a516952fa874238cf08ef63be006c56d.jpg)

La Brisa is located on the foreshore of Echo Beach in Canggu, Bali's hottest tourist spot (check out [Canggu 80 Spots Guide](/blog/canggu-guide/)). It’s one of Bali’s most famous beaches, offering absolutely stunning, endless culinary delights and genuine Balinese warmth.

La Brisa is not just a restaurant; it’s also a beach club. Its concept is written in its name—La Brisa, meaning wild, charming, and pleasantly soothing sea breeze. Built using recycled wood from over 500 old fishing boats and professionally hand-decorated, La Brisa Bali is one of the island’s most stylish and eco-friendly venues, exuding authentic Balinese character and serving mouthwatering food and cocktails.

![La Brisa景觀 + 本人照騙](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_11aec55b3776eca04aea8a96c7fc8fb1.png)

La Brisa Scenery + My Photo Trick

The menu here is specially designed for all seafood lovers, with the sole purpose of offering local products at reasonable prices. The fish served daily at La Brisa is freshly caught by Balinese fishermen, and nearly all ingredients used in the kitchen are sourced locally.

The venue itself is a work of art, impossible to describe in mere words. One must experience it firsthand to fully understand and appreciate its beauty and authenticity.

As soon as you step into La Brisa, you feel invited into a home—a humble Balinese fisherman’s home. Handcrafted decor and marine-themed elements like sand, shells, antique buoys, handmade fishing rods, grass baskets, fishing nets, handwoven chairs, benches, and swings all blend together to create a magical illusion, transporting you back in time.

It's not just a place. It's an experience. Whether it's a refreshing morning, a sunny afternoon, or the golden hour of sunset, you can find all the best things Bali has to offer here.

Enjoying delicious food in such a wonderful setting while admiring the sunset view is truly a delight!

## How to Make a Reservation at La Brisa?

- **Online Reservation**
    - Visit [La Brisa's official website](https://labrisa-bali.com/) and click the "Reserve" button.
    - Select the date, time, seating type, and number of people.
    - Fill in your name, email address, phone number, and other details.
    - Click the "Submit" button to complete your reservation.
- **Phone Reservation**
    - Call La Brisa at 0811-3946-666.
    - Provide your name, email address, phone number, reservation date, time, seating type, and number of people.
    - Follow the instructions from the customer service representative to complete your reservation.
- **On-Site Reservation**
    - If you're visiting spontaneously, you can also make a reservation on-site. However, La Brisa does not accept reservations during peak hours from 4 PM to 7 PM daily.

**Minimum Spending**

Some seating types at La Brisa have a minimum spending requirement. On weekdays, the minimum spending ranges from IDR 400,000 for two people (averaging IDR 200,000 per person) to IDR 1,800,000 for ten people (averaging IDR 180,000 per person), depending on the seating type or category. The minimum spending can be used for food or beverages.

**Other Information**

- La Brisa offers towel rental services at IDR 50,000 per towel.
- La Brisa provides vegetarian/vegan options.
- La Brisa is family-friendly and features a shallow swimming pool.
