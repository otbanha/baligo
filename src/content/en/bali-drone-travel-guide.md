**Bottom line**: You can bring a drone to Bali, but **batteries cannot be checked in—they must be carried in your hand luggage**, removed from the drone and placed in a fireproof protective case. The drone body (with the battery removed) can be either checked or carried on. Be sure to check lithium battery regulations before departure to avoid having your luggage rejected.

Are you planning a trip to Indonesia or Bali and worried about whether you can bring your drone? Don't worry—the answer is yes! You can bring your drone to Bali! But before you pack it in your suitcase, there are a few rules you need to follow.

First, **batteries cannot be checked in**! You must place the batteries in your carry-on luggage, not in your checked suitcase. This prevents airlines from rejecting your luggage for safety reasons due to lithium batteries.

Second, batteries must be removed from the drone and placed in a fireproof protective case to prevent fire caused by short circuits during the flight. The case also reduces the risk of battery damage from impact.

Finally, the drone body (with the battery removed) can be placed in your checked luggage or carried on the plane with you in your hand luggage. If you put it in checked luggage, be careful to avoid damage from bumps and pressure.

Additionally, if your drone **weighs more than 2 kg**, according to Indonesian regulations, you must [register with the Indonesian Ministry of Communication and Information Technology](https://hubud.dephub.go.id/hubud/website/) and obtain a permit to legally operate the drone in Indonesia.

### Frequently Asked Questions

## Can drone batteries be checked in luggage?

No. Batteries must be removed from the drone and placed in a fireproof protective case, then carried in your hand luggage onto the plane. They cannot be placed in checked baggage, as per airline lithium battery safety regulations.

## Should the drone body be checked in or carried as hand luggage?

After removing the batteries, the drone body can be placed in checked luggage or carried as hand luggage. If you choose to check it in, make sure to pack it carefully to avoid damage from bumps or pressure.

## How heavy does a drone need to be to require registration in Indonesia?

Drones weighing over 2 kilograms must be registered with the Ministry of Communication and Information Technology and obtain a permit to operate legally in Indonesia, according to local regulations.
