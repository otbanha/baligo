It's hard to imagine that an island roughly twice the size of New Taipei City is home to tens of thousands of hotels/villas and several times more restaurants. This place is simply that popular for tourism!

Among this vast array of dining options, you definitely shouldn't miss this restaurant hailed as 'Bali's Most Beautiful Restaurant'.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a516952fa874238cf08ef63be006c56d.jpg)

La Brisa is located on the foreshore of Echo Beach in Canggu, Bali's hottest tourist spot right now (you can check out Jay's [Canggu 80 Attractions Guide](/blog/2023-02-11-64db6b7efd897800013a9815/)). It's one of Bali's most famous beaches, offering absolutely stunning, endless culinary delights and genuine Balinese hospitality.

La Brisa is more than just a restaurant; it's a beach club. Its concept is right there in the name - La Brisa, meaning the wild, enchanting, and pleasantly soothing sea breeze. Built using recycled wood from over 500 old fishing boats and professionally hand-decorated, today's La Brisa is one of Bali's most stylish and eco-friendly venues, exuding authentic Balinese character and serving up some mouthwatering dishes and cocktails.

![La Brisa景觀 + 本人照騙](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_11aec55b3776eca04aea8a96c7fc8fb1.png)

La Brisa Scenery + My Photo

The menu here is specially designed for all seafood lovers, with the sole purpose of offering local products at reasonable prices. The fish served daily at La Brisa is caught by Balinese fishermen, and nearly all ingredients used in the kitchen are locally sourced from Bali.

The venue itself is a work of art, impossible to fully describe in mere words. One must experience it firsthand to truly understand and appreciate its reputation and authenticity.

As soon as you step into La Brisa, you feel like you've been invited into a home. It's the humble abode of a Balinese fisherman. Handcrafted decorations and marine-themed elements like sand, shells, antique buoys, handmade fishing rods, grass baskets, fishing nets, handwoven chairs, benches, and swings all blend together to create a wonderful illusion that transports you back in time.

It's not just a place. It's an experience. Whether it's a refreshing morning, a sunny afternoon, or the golden hour of sunset, all the best things Bali has to offer can be found here.

Enjoying the beautiful sunset while indulging in delicious food in such a fantastic setting is truly a delight!

## How to Make a Reservation at La Brisa?

- **Online Reservation**
  - Visit [La Brisa's official website](https://labrisa-bali.com/) and click the "Reserve" button.
  - Select the date, time, seating type, and number of people.
  - Fill in your name, email address, phone number, and other information.
  - Click the "Submit" button to complete the reservation.
- **Phone Reservation**
  - Call La Brisa at 0811-3946-666.
  - Provide your name, email address, phone number, reservation date, time, seating type, and number of people.
  - Follow the instructions from the customer service representative to complete the reservation.
- **On-Site Reservation**
  - If you're visiting spontaneously, you can also make a reservation on-site. However, La Brisa does not accept reservations during peak hours from 4 PM to 7 PM daily.

**Minimum Spending**

Some seating types at La Brisa have a minimum spending requirement. On weekdays, the minimum spending ranges from IDR 400,000 for two people (average IDR 200,000 per person) to IDR 1,800,000 for ten people (average IDR 180,000 per person), depending on the seating type or category. The minimum spending can be used for food or drinks.

**Other Information**

- La Brisa offers towel rental services at IDR 50,000 per towel.
- La Brisa provides vegetarian/vegan options.
- La Brisa is family-friendly and features a shallow swimming pool.
