You might find it hard to imagine that an island roughly twice the size of New Taipei City is home to tens of thousands of hotels and villas, and several times that number of restaurants. This place is just that popular for tourism!

Among the many restaurants, this one, acclaimed as 'the most beautiful restaurant in Bali,' is definitely not to be missed!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a516952fa874238cf08ef63be006c56d.jpg)

La Brisa is located on the foreshore of Echo Beach in Canggu, one of Bali's hottest tourist spots right now (you can check out Xiao Jie's [Canggu 80 Attractions Guide](/blog/2023-02-11-64db6b7efd897800013a9815/)). It's one of Bali's most famous beaches, offering absolutely stunning, endless culinary delights and genuine Balinese hospitality.

La Brisa is not just a restaurant, but also a beach club. Its concept is encapsulated in its name - La Brisa, meaning the wild, enchanting, and delightfully soothing sea breeze. Built using reclaimed wood from over 500 old fishing boats and adorned with expert craftsmanship, La Brisa Bali stands today as one of the most stylish and eco-conscious venues on the island, exuding authentic Balinese charm while serving up some truly mouthwatering cuisine and cocktails.

![La Brisa景觀 + 本人照騙](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_11aec55b3776eca04aea8a96c7fc8fb1.png)

La Brisa's scenic views + my Instagram-worthy shots

The menu here is specially crafted for all seafood lovers, with the sole purpose of offering local products at reasonable prices. The fish served daily at La Brisa is freshly caught by Balinese fishermen, and nearly all ingredients used in the kitchen are sourced from Bali's local produce.

This place is a work of art in itself, impossible to describe with mere words. One must experience it firsthand to fully understand and appreciate its beauty and authenticity.

Stepping into La Brisa feels like being invited into a home. It's the humble abode of a Balinese fisherman. Handcrafted decorations and marine-themed elements like sand, seashells, antique buoys, handmade fishing rods, woven baskets, fishing nets, and artisanal chairs, benches, and swings all blend together to create a magical illusion that transports you back in time.

It's not just a place. It's an experience. Whether it's the refreshing mornings, sunlit afternoons, or the golden hours of sunset, all the best things Bali has to offer can be found here.

Enjoying a delicious meal in such a wonderful setting while taking in the sunset view is truly a delightful experience!

## How to Make a Reservation at La Brisa?

**Online Reservation**
- Visit [La Brisa's official website](https://labrisa-bali.com/) and click the "Reserve" button.
    - Select the date, time, seating type, and number of people.
    - Fill in your name, email address, phone number, and other information.
    - Click the "Submit" button to complete your reservation.
**Phone Reservation**
- Call La Brisa at 0811-3946-666.
    - Provide your name, email address, phone number, reservation date, time, seating type, and number of people.
    - Follow the instructions from the customer service representative to complete your reservation.
**Walk-in Reservation**
- If you decide to visit on the same day, you can also make a reservation on-site. However, La Brisa does not accept reservations during peak hours from 4 PM to 7 PM daily.

**Minimum Spending**

La Brisa has minimum spending requirements for certain seating types. On weekdays, the minimum spend ranges from IDR 400,000 for two people (averaging IDR 200,000 per person) to IDR 1,800,000 for ten people (averaging IDR 180,000 per person), depending on the seating type or category. The minimum spend can be used towards food or beverages.

**Additional Information**

- La Brisa offers towel rental service at IDR 50,000 per towel.
- La Brisa provides vegetarian/vegan options.
- La Brisa is family-friendly and features a shallow swimming pool.
