## Family Travel to Bali: Complete Guide to Children's Visas

Taking your family on a dream vacation to Bali is a goal for many parents. However, visa questions often cause headaches before departure, especially queries like "What visa does my two-year-old need?" and "Does the whole family have to queue for a visa on arrival together?" This article will answer all your questions, ensuring your family trip starts smoothly right from entry!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9b12e310451ce513776f9b8ae272b6ee.png)

## Can a Two-Year-Old Apply for an e-Visa?

**Good news! Two-year-olds can apply for Indonesia's electronic visa (e-VOA).**

Indonesia's e-visa system has no age restrictions, meaning infants, toddlers, and adults alike can apply for an e-visa online before departure. This allows you to prepare e-visas for your entire family (including your two-year-old) in advance, saving significant time at the airport.

### Advantages of the e-Visa

- **Apply in advance**: Complete the application from home, avoiding crowded airport queues
- **Time-saving convenience**: Eligible travelers can use automated immigration lanes, completing entry in under three minutes
- **Same cost**: The e-visa fee is the same as the visa on arrival, at **IDR 500,000**
- **30-day validity**: Can be extended once, allowing a maximum stay of 60 days

## Should the Whole Family Choose e-Visa or Visa on Arrival?

### Recommendation: Apply for E-Visas for the Whole Family

While it's theoretically possible to apply for visas on arrival for the entire family, **we strongly recommend that all family members apply for e-visas in advance**. Here's why:

## **Disadvantages of Visa on Arrival:**

- Requires queuing at the airport upon arrival, with uncertain wait times
- During peak seasons or when multiple flights arrive simultaneously, queues can take 30 minutes to over an hour
- Standing in long lines with tired children can make the start of your trip exhausting
- Payment must be made in cash or by card on-site

### **Advantages of E-Visa:**

- Peace of mind with everything prepared in advance
- Use of automated immigration gates (applicable for children aged 6 and above), making the process quick and convenient
- Even if your child is under 6 and cannot use automated gates, there are dedicated fast-track lanes
- Avoid long airport queues with children

## Practical Experience Sharing: Arrival at Bali Airport

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf7e7e7e9e394b75fc46a3b7bffd0242.png)

Based on actual experiences shared by community members, the arrival process at Bali Airport is now very modern and user-friendly. Here’s what to expect when arriving with children:

### Automated Immigration Gates (Applicable for Ages 6 and Above)

Currently, **children aged 6 and above** can use the automated immigration gates at the airport. If your child is 6 or older, congratulations! Your entire family can use the automated gates, completing the process in less than three minutes!

Airport staff are very friendly. Upon disembarking, they will ask if you've applied for an e-visa and guide you to the automated immigration clearance area. The process is simple: scan your passport, facial recognition, and you're through.

### What about children under 6?

If your child is under 6 years old (for example, 2 years old), although they cannot use the automated clearance, **there's no need to worry at all**!

Practical experience shows:

- Parents can quickly pass through automated clearance first
- Customs officers will immediately assist, taking the child to a **dedicated counter**
- This dedicated counter is highly efficient, **completely queue-free**
- Parents wait nearby, and it's completed within minutes
- Overall, it's still much faster than the whole family queuing for visa-on-arrival

A parent visiting Bali shared: 'When customs officers saw that only one child in our family couldn't use automated clearance, they immediately took the child to a dedicated counter nearby. The efficiency was remarkable, with no queuing at all.'

## Steps to Apply for an E-Visa

1. **Apply Online**: Visit the [Official Indonesian Immigration E-Visa Website](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/)
2. **Fill in Details**: Including passport information for all family members
3. **Upload Photo**: A passport-style digital photo is required
4. **Online Payment**: **IDR 500,000** per person, payable by credit card\*\*(card fees apply)\*\*
5. **Receive Visa**: Usually received within 12 hours, remember to print or save it on your phone

### **Pre-Departure Must-Dos**:

1. Make sure to complete your eVisa application first. Refer to [here](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/) for further instructions to avoid scam websites.
2. Complete the All Indonesia Arrival Card three days before departure and **screenshot the QR Code** for reference. See our compiled [FAQ](/blog/2025-08-14-689db40ffd89780001eb2f3e/).
3. **Pay the Bali Tourism Tax**: IDR 150,000 per person (approximately USD$5). How to pay? See the [Tourism Tax](/blog/2024-01-03-65955989fd89780001a95927/) explanation.

### Reminder

- Apply at least 7 days before departure to allow processing time
- Ensure each family member (including infants) has a valid passport with over 6 months validity
- Save the e-Visa PDF file as you'll need to present it upon arrival

## Final Recommendations

**For family trips with children under six:**

✅ **Get e-Visas for the whole family** - Saves time and effort, no need to queue at the airport
✅ **Prepare in advance** - Complete applications before departure for peace of mind
✅ **Utilize automated gates** - Fast entry for members over 6, dedicated lanes for under 6
✅ **Don't worry** - Airport staff are friendly and professional, ready to assist families with children
