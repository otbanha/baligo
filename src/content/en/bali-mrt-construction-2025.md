After the official launch of Bali's first phase Light Rail Transit (LRT) project on September 4, 2024, with a ceremony held at Kuta Central Parking, the Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) construction plan will commence excavation in April 2025, marking a major breakthrough in local transportation infrastructure. Next, we will explore in detail the various phases of Bali MRT, the construction process, and its future impact on tourism and local residents.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99819bd6538fcfc0eb8fcc36f0c1005b.jpg)

## Background of MRT

**Origins of Bali MRT Project:**

The Bali MRT project aims to address the increasingly severe traffic issues, especially congestion during peak tourist seasons. Several years ago, the local government began preparations for this major infrastructure project, but due to funding and technical challenges, it had not been launched. Now, with active participation from international investors, the project is finally being implemented.

## Project Concerns and Investor Confidence

**How to Attract International Investors:**

Acting Governor of Bali Province, Mahendra Jaya, expressed initial concerns about this massive project, fearing it might not attract sufficient investors. However, with enthusiastic responses from international investors, these concerns have gradually diminished. Many international consortia have shown strong interest in the Bali MRT project and are willing to provide funding and technical support.

## Contribution from Dubai's Minister of Energy

**Sources of Investment Funds:**

During his visit to Bali, the Energy Minister of Dubai pledged to provide 75% of the funding for this project, making it more financially feasible. These funds will primarily be used to purchase high-tech Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs) and cover related construction costs.

## Technical Specifications of TBM Machines

**Equipment for Bali's Metro System:**

This project will utilize eight large Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs), six more than those used for Jakarta's metro system. The TBMs for Bali will have a diameter of 7.2 meters, surpassing Jakarta's 6 meters, meaning Bali's metro tunnels will be more spacious. These machines are scheduled to arrive in Bali in April 2025, when tunnel excavation will commence.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_639d9f2561697e36de04070031810cf0.jpg)

## Depth of Underground Tunnels

**Specific Data on Tunnel Construction:**

According to the plan, Bali's metro tunnels will be dug approximately 20 to 30 meters underground. Due to Bali's unique geological conditions, the construction team will conduct detailed geological surveys to ensure the stability and safety of the tunnels. The tunnel construction will cover major urban areas of Bali, with the first phase connecting Denpasar to Kuta and extending to Bali's international airport.

## First Phase of Construction

**Areas Involved and Mileage:**

The first phase of the metro line will cover Denpasar city center, the Kuta area, and Bali International Airport, spanning approximately 15 kilometers. The primary goal of this phase is to provide a rapid commuting solution, alleviating traffic pressure for both tourists and local residents in these bustling areas. Metro stations will be strategically located in major tourist zones such as Kuta, Legian, and Denpasar city center, ensuring easy access for visitors and locals alike.

## Phases Two, Three, and Four

**Long-term Development Plan:**

Following the completion of the first phase, metro construction will gradually expand into the second, third, and fourth phases. The second phase will extend to popular tourist destinations like Nusa Dua and Sanur, offering broader transportation options. The third phase will cover Bali's northern and eastern regions, aiming to boost development in these areas and attract more visitors. Finally, the fourth phase will encompass the entire island of Bali, forming a comprehensive metro network.

## Project Timeline

**Estimated Completion Dates and Challenges:**

According to the Bali government's plan, the first phase of the metro construction is expected to be completed by 2030. While this project is highly ambitious, the government and relevant departments anticipate numerous challenges, particularly in construction progress, technical difficulties, and funding allocation. However, the government remains confident in the project's future and is committed to ensuring its timely completion.

## Impact on Local Transportation

**Alleviating Traffic Congestion:**

Bali has long struggled with traffic congestion, especially during peak tourist seasons. With the metro in operation, both locals and tourists will be able to travel more quickly and sustainably, significantly reducing road traffic pressure. Once the first phase is operational, the metro will become the ideal mode of transportation for reaching the airport and major tourist attractions.

## Environmental Impact

**Reduction in Carbon Emissions:**

As a clean mode of transportation, the metro system will significantly reduce carbon emissions in Bali. Currently, local transportation heavily relies on private cars and motorcycles, which exert considerable pressure on the environment. The operation of the metro will encourage more people to opt for public transportation, thereby reducing fuel consumption and lowering Bali's overall carbon footprint.

## Metro Fare and Benefits for Local Residents

**Will Local Residents Ride for Free?**

According to the Bali government's preliminary plan, local residents are expected to enjoy discounted fares and may even ride the metro for free during specific periods. This will greatly alleviate their travel costs. The government hopes this initiative will encourage more locals to choose the metro system, reducing reliance on private cars and motorcycles.

## Tourist Fare Policy

**Suggested Cost for Weekly Pass:**

For tourists vacationing in Bali, the government is considering introducing discounted weekly and monthly passes, allowing them to use the metro more frequently at an economical rate. Based on initial projections, the price for a weekly pass may be set between 150,000 to 200,000 Indonesian Rupiah, offering great convenience for tourists frequently traveling between different attractions.

## Anticipated Challenges and Solutions

**Challenges Facing Bali's MRT Project:**

While the MRT construction plan is highly anticipated, it faces several challenges, including land acquisition, traffic diversions during construction, and potential delays. Additionally, as the project involves significant financial and technical requirements, effectively managing resources and ensuring construction quality will be key to its success.

## The Significance of MRT for Bali's Future

**Long-term Impact on Tourism and Economy:**

The completion of Bali's MRT will not only greatly enhance local transportation infrastructure but also provide a significant boost to tourism. With improved transportation conditions, more tourists will be willing to choose Bali as a vacation destination and may stay longer. Furthermore, the construction of the MRT will create numerous job opportunities and promote local economic development. In the long term, the MRT system will contribute to a more sustainable future for Bali, establishing it as a core transportation hub for this tourist hotspot.
