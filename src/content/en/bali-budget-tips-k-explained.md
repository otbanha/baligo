**When spending in Indonesia/Bali, you often see a 'K' after numbers - what does it mean?**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9cd0124f7272cfa32cf8ca9be2d1e01e.jpg)

Bali, this picturesque Indonesian gem, attracts visitors from around the world. On this beautiful island, in addition to stunning natural landscapes and cultural heritage, tourists often notice a unique spending habit - a 'K' frequently appears after amount numbers. This article will explain the meaning of this convention and introduce the importance of understanding this amount format when spending in Bali.

### **Understanding the Format**

The 'K' actually stands for 'thousand', representing one thousand in amounts. In daily spending in Bali, when you see a 'K' after a price, it means the amount has been abbreviated to represent numbers in the thousands. This formatting helps simplify large numbers, making prices easier to read and understand. For example, 200K represents 200,000 Indonesian Rupiah, while 80K means 80,000 Indonesian Rupiah.

In actual spending, understanding this format is crucial. It will help you more accurately assess and control your expenses, avoiding misunderstandings due to unfamiliarity with price formats.

### **What's Spending Like in Indonesia/Bali?**

In Indonesia or Bali, there are many common expenses - here are some examples:

1. Accommodation: On average, mid-range hotels cost around 400K-800K IDR per night, while luxury resorts can be 1000K IDR and above.

2. Dining: Meals at simple, unadorned restaurants may cost you 20K-100K IDR, while upscale restaurants can be more expensive.
3. Transportation: Short taxi or motorcycle rides typically range from 20K-100K IDR, while full-day car rentals may cost between 600K-800K IDR.

### **Managing Expenses**

To better manage your expenses in Bali, here are some suggestions:

1. Use currency conversion apps: With currency conversion, you can easily convert prices to your local currency to better assess expenses. For NT$ conversion, a simple method is to multiply the number before "K" by 2 to get an approximate NT$ amount. For example, 200K is roughly NT$400; 35K is about NT$70. Give it a try!

2. Carry cash: Some places may not accept credit cards or may charge additional fees. Carrying some local currency can help you better handle such situations.

When traveling in Bali, understanding the 'K' price format will be crucial for better managing your expenses, making budgets, and avoiding misunderstandings. This simplified numerical notation makes amounts easier to comprehend, helping you enjoy your time on this beautiful island while maintaining financial control. Whether you're savoring delicious local cuisine or immersing yourself in stunning beach scenery, you'll be able to confidently choose expenditures that suit you.
