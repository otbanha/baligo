**When spending in Indonesia/Bali, you often see a 'K' after numbers - what does it mean?**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9cd0124f7272cfa32cf8ca9be2d1e01e.jpg)

Bali, this picturesque Indonesian gem, attracts visitors from around the world. On this beautiful island, besides stunning natural landscapes and cultural heritage, tourists will notice a unique spending habit - the frequent use of 'K' after monetary amounts. This article will explain the meaning of this convention and introduce the importance of understanding this currency format when spending in Bali.

### **Understanding the Format**

The 'K' actually stands for 'thousand', representing one thousand in monetary terms. In daily spending in Bali, when you see a 'K' after a price, it means the amount has been abbreviated to show figures in the thousands. This formatting helps simplify large numbers, making prices easier to read and understand. For example, 200K represents 200,000 Indonesian Rupiah, while 80K means 80,000 Indonesian Rupiah.

Understanding this format is crucial in actual spending. It will help you more accurately assess and control your expenses, avoiding misunderstandings due to unfamiliarity with price formats.

### **What is spending like in Indonesia/Bali?**

In Indonesia or Bali, there are many common expenses. Here are some examples:

1. Accommodation: On average, mid-range hotels cost around 400K-800K IDR per night, while luxury resorts can cost 1000K IDR or more.

3. Transportation: Short taxi or motorcycle rides typically range from 20K-100K IDR, while full-day car rentals can cost between 600K-800K IDR.

### **Managing Expenses**

To better manage your expenses in Bali, here are some tips:

1. Use a currency conversion app: Due to currency conversion, you can easily convert prices into your local currency to better assess expenses. For converting to USD, a simple method is to multiply the number before "K" by 0.06 to get an approximate USD amount. For example, 200K is roughly USD$12; 35K is about USD$2. Give it a try!

2. Carry cash: Some places may not accept credit cards or may charge additional fees. Carrying some local currency can help you handle such situations.

When traveling in Bali, understanding the 'K' price format will be crucial for better managing your expenses, making budgets, and avoiding misunderstandings. This simplified numerical notation makes amounts easier to comprehend, helping you enjoy your time on this beautiful island while keeping your finances under control. Whether you're savoring delicious local cuisine or immersing yourself in stunning beach scenery, you'll be able to confidently choose expenditures that suit your needs.
