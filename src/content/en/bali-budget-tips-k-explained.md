**When shopping in Indonesia or Bali, you often see a 'K' after numbers—what does it mean?**

**Bottom line**: The 'K' in Bali's price tags stands for 'thousand'—**200K = Rp 200,000**, **80K = Rp 80,000**. It's a shorthand for Indonesian Rupiah, not an actual currency unit. Understanding this conversion helps you avoid misjudging prices (e.g., mid-range hotels cost around 400K-800K/night, while luxury resorts start at 1000K and up).

Bali, this picturesque Indonesian gem, draws travelers from around the globe. Beyond its stunning natural landscapes and cultural heritage, visitors will notice a unique shopping habit on this beautiful island—a 'K' often appears after price figures. This article explains the meaning of this convention and highlights the importance of understanding this pricing format when spending in Bali.

## **Understanding the Format**

This 'K' actually stands for 'thousand,' representing one thousand in monetary amounts. In everyday spending in Bali, when you see a 'K' after a price, it means the amount has been abbreviated to indicate figures in the thousands. This formatting simplifies large numbers, making prices easier to read and grasp. For example, 200K equals 200,000 Indonesian Rupiah, and 80K means 80,000 Rupiah.

In real-world spending, understanding this format is crucial. It helps you more accurately assess and manage your expenses, avoiding misunderstandings due to unfamiliar pricing conventions.

## **What's the cost of living in Indonesia/Bali?**

In Indonesia or Bali, there are many common expenses. Here are some examples:

1. Accommodation: On average, mid-range hotels cost around 400K-800K IDR per night, while upscale resorts start at over 1000K IDR.

## **Managing Expenses**

To better manage your expenses in Bali, here are some tips:

1. Use a currency conversion app: With currency exchange, you can easily convert prices to your home currency to better assess costs. For Taiwanese dollars, there's a simple trick: double the number before the 'K' to get an approximate amount in TWD. For example, 200K is roughly TWD 400; 35K is about TWD 70. Give it a try!

2. Carry cash: Some places may not accept credit cards or may charge extra fees. Having some local currency on hand can help you handle such situations.
