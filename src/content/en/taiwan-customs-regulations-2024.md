According to the latest regulations from the Ministry of Finance's Customs Administration, starting from June 26, 2024, the duty-free allowance for luggage items brought in by inbound passengers has been increased from NT$20,000 to NT$35,000, allowing tourists to shop to their heart's content! But do you know which items are prohibited from being brought into Taiwan? Many people may not be aware, so to avoid having items confiscated by customs, it's crucial to pay attention to Taiwan's entry regulations to avoid unnecessary expenses!

## Duty-Free Allowance Increased to NT$35,000

Starting June 26, the duty-free allowance for luggage items brought in by inbound passengers has been raised from NT$20,000 to NT$35,000, benefiting those traveling abroad for summer shopping. Additionally, according to the current 'Regulations on the Inspection and Duty-Free Release of Luggage Items Brought in by Inbound Passengers,' personal or household luggage items, excluding controlled items, tobacco, and alcohol, are subject to a NT$20,000 duty-free allowance per person, with amounts exceeding this requiring declaration. However, this does not apply to those engaged in obvious commercial activities or frequent travelers with violation records.

★For the latest information, please refer to:

- [Taipei Customs, Customs Administration, Ministry of Finance - Entry Declaration Notice](https://web.customs.gov.tw/taipei/multiplehtml/3392)

## Taiwan Entry Regulations and Restrictions

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c1d6ac4830b9bdc7166a59c589adf9fb.png)

### Food Items

- Raw milk, fresh milk: Not allowed; milk roll cakes are also prohibited
- Fresh, refrigerated, frozen, vacuum-packed cooked meat: Not allowed
- Fresh truffles: Not allowed
- Dried meat, meat floss: Prohibited regardless of packaging, including vacuum-packed
- Foie gras: Glass jars are not allowed, but metal packaging is permitted
- Canned goods, instant noodles: Allowed
- Mentaiko, salmon roe: Allowed
- Fish floss: Allowed
- Long-life milk, powdered milk, cheese, yogurt: Allowed
- Soft cans: Allowed, if labeled as high-temperature sterilized
- Rice: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Tea leaves: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Dried shiitake mushrooms: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Dried chili peppers: Allowed, up to 6 kg
- Cooked peanuts, garlic: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Bird's nest: Allowed, with a market value not exceeding NT$20,000; declaration required if exceeded

### Cosmetics and Skincare Products

Non-medicated cosmetic products (including hair dyes, perms, sunscreen, whitening products) such as masks, eye creams, lip masks, toners, and serums are allowed up to 12 bottles per type, with a total limit of 36 bottles. Items with a total value exceeding USD$645 must be declared for taxation.

### Contact Lenses

If exceeding the following personal use limits, you must apply for "Medical Devices for Personal Use" from the Taiwan Food and Drug Administration and declare them to customs.

1. Daily disposable contact lenses: 60 pieces per prescription, limited to one brand and two prescriptions.
2. Corrective lenses: No more than one pair.

### Tobacco and Alcohol

Each adult traveler aged 20 or older (starting January 1, 2023, the age limit for carrying alcohol is adjusted to 18, while the age limit for tobacco remains 20) is allowed to carry the following quantities of tobacco and alcohol products. These quantities include all tobacco and alcohol products in both carry-on and checked luggage (including those purchased at duty-free shops, onboard flights, abroad, or brought out and back into the country). For more details, please refer to the Taipei Customs Office of the Ministry of Finance's "Tobacco and Alcohol" section.

* Tobacco:

- Duty-free allowance (no declaration required): 200 cigarettes or 25 cigars or 1 pound of tobacco.
- Limited quantity (declaration required): 5 cartons (1,000 cigarettes) or 125 cigars or 5 pounds of tobacco.

(★Reminder: According to the Tobacco Hazards Prevention Act, all e-cigarettes and heated tobacco products that have not passed health risk assessment reviews are completely prohibited from being manufactured, imported, sold, supplied, displayed, advertised, or used. When shopping abroad, remember that regardless of quantity, these items cannot be imported or carried into the country, whether for gifts or personal use, and may result in penalties. They also cannot be used as a means of profit. If discovered, violators may face fines of up to USD$161,290 under Article 26 of the Tobacco Hazards Prevention Act, and the goods will be confiscated and destroyed.)

►For related information, please visit the Tobacco Control Section of the Health Promotion Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare.

*Alcohol:

- Duty-free allowance (no declaration required): Total of 1 liter
- Limited quantity (with declaration): Total of 5 liters (but limited to 1 liter for alcohol from mainland China, which is not open for import)

►Explanation of duty-free allowance:

1. If not exceeding the duty-free allowance: No need to declare to customs.
2. If exceeding the duty-free allowance: Must declare to customs at the red channel, and alcohol products within the limited quantity can be released after inspection and tax payment.

►Explanation of limited quantity:

1. If not exceeding the limited quantity: After deducting the duty-free allowance, the remaining will be taxed and released.
2. If exceeding the limited quantity: Must attach a liquor import business license, fill out an import declaration form according to "Article 17 of the Customs Act", and handle the importation in the name of the company, or return the excess portion.

### Pharmaceuticals

For regulations regarding bringing personal medications upon entry, please refer to the following:

*Tablets and capsules: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, packs, bags) per type, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, packs, bags). *Over-the-counter Western medicines: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks) per type, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks). *General prescription Western medicines: Limited based on whether a doctor's prescription or proof document is carried (injectable products must have a prescription or proof document):

1. Without carrying: Limited to a 2-month supply.
2. With carrying: Not exceeding the prescribed reasonable amount, with a maximum of a 6-month supply.

(★All three items above require applying for a "Personal Medication Import Permit" from the Taiwan Food and Drug Administration, and must be declared to customs upon entry.)

*Controlled prescription Western medicines: Must carry a doctor's prescription or proof document, limited to treating one's own illness, not exceeding the prescribed amount, with a maximum of a 6-month supply.

(★Must apply for a "Declaration for Carrying Controlled Drugs In and Out of the Republic of China" from the Taiwan Food and Drug Administration, and declare to customs upon entry and exit.)

*Traditional Chinese Medicine:

1. Herbal materials: Maximum of 1 kg per type, with a total weight not exceeding 12 kg.
2. Oral TCM preparations: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks) per dosage form, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks), and a total weight not exceeding 1.2 kg.

(★Must apply for a "Special Import Permit for Personal Use of Traditional Chinese Medicine" from the Taiwan Food and Drug Administration, and declare to customs upon entry.)

### Cash and Jewelry

If carrying amounts exceeding the limit, please complete the "Customs Declaration Form" and "Registration Form for Passengers or Crew Members Carrying Cash, Securities, Gold, or Other Items Inbound/Outbound" or use the Single Window for Customs, Trade, and Port Affairs for "Online Pre-Declaration Registration" → Proceed through the red channel for customs declaration and clearance.

*New Taiwan Dollar (NTD): Limit of USD$3,226 (NT$100,000). Excess amounts must be returned (stored by the passenger at customs and taken out upon departure).

*Chinese Yuan (CNY): Limit of USD$645 (CNY 20,000). Excess amounts must be returned (stored by the passenger at customs and taken out upon departure).

*Foreign Currency: Equivalent to USD$10,000. Full amount allowed.

*Bearer Securities: Equivalent to USD$10,000. Full amount allowed.

*Gold: Equivalent to USD$20,000. For total values exceeding USD$20,000, an "Import Permit" must be applied for from the Bureau of Foreign Trade, Ministry of Economic Affairs (Tel: 02-23510271) before completing customs procedures.

*Diamonds/Gems/Platinum exceeding personal use: Equivalent to USD$500,000. For total values exceeding USD$20,000, an "Import Permit" must be applied for from the Bureau of Foreign Trade, Ministry of Economic Affairs (Tel: 02-23510271) before completing customs procedures.

## Prohibited Items

- Stun guns, flashlight-style stun guns
- Tear gas sprays, pepper sprays, anti-wolf sprays
- Small knives, folding knives, throwing darts, stun guns, etc.
- Various narcotics, possession of drug raw materials, prohibited or manufacturing materials for producing or converting drugs
- Marijuana, THC, or marijuana-related products (CBD requires an import permit)
- Counterfeit goods
- Various meat products and meat floss (including ham, jerky, sausages, etc.), beef, lamb, pork, duck, goose, etc., are all prohibited
