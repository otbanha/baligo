According to the latest regulations from the Ministry of Finance's Customs Administration, starting from June 26, 2024, the duty-free allowance for luggage items brought in by inbound travelers has been increased from NT$20,000 to NT$35,000, allowing tourists to shop to their heart's content! But do you know which items cannot be brought into Taiwan? Many people may not be aware, so to avoid having items confiscated by customs, it's essential to pay attention to Taiwan's entry regulations to avoid wasting money!

## Duty-Free Allowance Increased to NT$35,000

Starting from June 26, the duty-free allowance for luggage items brought in by inbound travelers has been increased from NT$20,000 to NT$35,000, benefiting those traveling abroad for summer shopping. Additionally, according to the current "Regulations on the Declaration and Duty-Free Allowance of Luggage Items for Inbound Travelers," each traveler can bring in personal or household items duty-free up to NT$20,000, excluding controlled items, tobacco, and alcohol. Amounts exceeding this limit must be declared. However, this does not apply to those who clearly engage in commercial activities or frequently cross borders with violation records.

## Taiwan Entry Restrictions

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c1d6ac4830b9bdc7166a59c589adf9fb.png)

### Food Items

- Raw milk, fresh milk: Not allowed, including raw milk roll cakes
- Fresh, refrigerated, frozen, vacuum-packed cooked meat: Not allowed
- Fresh truffles: Not allowed
- Dried meat, meat floss: Prohibited regardless of packaging, including vacuum-packed
- Foie gras: Glass jars not allowed, but metal packaging is permitted
- Canned food, instant noodles: Allowed
- Mentaiko, salmon roe: Allowed
- Fish floss: Allowed
- Long-life milk, powdered milk, cheese, yogurt: Allowed
- Soft canned food: Allowed, if labeled as high-temperature sterilized
- Rice: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Tea leaves: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Dried shiitake mushrooms: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Dried chili peppers: Allowed, up to 6 kg
- Cooked peanuts, garlic: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Bird's nest: Allowed, market value must not exceed NT$20,000; exceeding requires declaration

### Cosmetics and Skincare Products

Non-medicated cosmetics and skincare products (including hair dyes, perms, sunscreen, whitening products) are limited to a maximum of 12 bottles per item, with a total combined limit of 36 bottles. Any single item exceeding a total value of USD$645 must be declared for taxation.

### Contact Lenses

If exceeding the following personal use limits, you must apply for 'Medical Devices for Personal Use Only' from the Taiwan Food and Drug Administration and declare them to customs.

1. Daily disposable contact lenses: 60 lenses per prescription, limited to 1 brand and 2 prescriptions.
2. Corrective lenses: No more than 1 pair.

### Tobacco and Alcohol

Each adult traveler aged 20 or above (starting January 1, 2023, the age limit for bringing alcohol into Taiwan has been lowered to 18, while the age limit for tobacco remains at 20) is allowed to carry the following quantities of tobacco and alcohol products. These quantities include all tobacco and alcohol products in both carry-on and checked luggage (including duty-free purchases, in-flight purchases, overseas purchases, and items taken out of Taiwan and brought back). For more details, please refer to the Taipei Customs Office's 'Tobacco and Alcohol' section.

*Tobacco:

- Duty-free allowance (no declaration required): 200 cigarettes or 25 cigars or 1 pound of tobacco.
- Limited quantity (declaration required): 5 cartons (1,000 cigarettes) or 125 cigars or 5 pounds of tobacco.

(★Reminder: According to the Tobacco Hazards Prevention Act, the manufacture, import, sale, supply, display, advertising, and use of all e-cigarettes and heated tobacco products that have not passed health risk assessment reviews are completely prohibited. When shopping abroad, remember that regardless of quantity, these items cannot be imported or brought into Taiwan, whether for gifting or personal use, and may result in penalties. They also cannot be used as a means of profit. If discovered, violators may face fines of up to USD$16,129 under Article 26 of the Tobacco Hazards Prevention Act, and the goods will be confiscated and destroyed.)

►For related information, please visit the Tobacco Control Section of the Health Promotion Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare.

＊Alcoholic Beverages:

- Duty-free allowance (no declaration required): Total of 1 liter
- Limited quantity (declaration required): Total of 5 liters (but limited to 1 liter for alcoholic beverages from Mainland China that are not open for import)

►Explanation of duty-free allowance:

1. If not exceeding the duty-free allowance: No need to declare to customs.
2. If exceeding the duty-free allowance: Must declare to customs via the red channel, and alcoholic products within the limited quantity can be released after inspection and tax payment.

►Explanation of limited quantity:

1. If not exceeding the limited quantity: After deducting the duty-free allowance, release upon tax payment.
2. If exceeding the limited quantity: Must attach the alcoholic beverage import business license, fill out the import declaration form according to "Article 17 of the Customs Act", handle customs clearance in the name of the company, or handle the excess part for return shipment.

### Pharmaceuticals

For regulations regarding bringing personal medications when entering the country, please refer to the following:

*Pills and capsules: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, packs, bags) per type, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, packs, bags). *Over-the-counter Western medicines: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks) per type, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks). *General prescription Western medicines: Limits based on whether you carry a doctor's prescription or proof (injectable products require a prescription or proof):

1. Without carrying: Limited to a 2-month supply.
2. With carrying: Not exceeding the prescribed reasonable amount, with a maximum of a 6-month supply.

(★All three items above require applying for a "Personal Medication Import Permit" from the Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, and must be declared to customs upon entry.)

*Controlled prescription Western medicines: Must carry a doctor's prescription or proof, limited to treatment of personal illness, not exceeding the prescribed amount, with a maximum of a 6-month supply.

(★Must apply for a "Declaration Form for Patients Carrying Controlled Drugs In and Out of the Republic of China" from the Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, and declare to customs upon entry and exit.)

*Traditional Chinese Medicine:

1. Herbal materials: Maximum of 1 kg per type, with a total weight not exceeding 12 kg.
2. Oral TCM preparations: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks) per dosage form, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks), and a total weight not exceeding 1.2 kg.

(★Must apply for a "Special Import Permit for Personal Use of Traditional Chinese Herbal Materials and Preparations" from the Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, and declare to customs upon entry.)

### Cash and Jewelry

If carrying amounts exceeding the limit, please complete the '[Customs Declaration Form](https://taipei.customs.gov.tw/singlehtml/2851?cntId=cus2_179094_2851)' and the '[Registration Form for Carrying Cash, Securities, Gold, and Other Items by Passengers or Service Personnel](https://taipei.customs.gov.tw/singlehtml/2851?cntId=cus2_179096_2851)', or proceed with '[Online Pre-Declaration Registration](https://portal.sw.nat.gov.tw/APGW/MI50!goToMI50INDEX?request_locale=zh_TW)' via the Single Window for Customs, Trade, and Port Services. After declaration at the red channel, proceed through customs.

*New Taiwan Dollar (NTD): Limit of USD$3,200 (NTD 100,000). Amounts exceeding this limit must be returned (stored by the passenger at customs and taken out upon departure).

*Chinese Yuan (CNY): Limit of USD$3,200 (CNY 20,000). Amounts exceeding this limit must be returned (stored by the passenger at customs and taken out upon departure).

*Foreign Currency: Equivalent to USD$10,000. Can be carried in full.

*Bearer Securities: Equivalent to USD$10,000. Can be carried in full.

*Gold: Equivalent to USD$20,000. For total values exceeding USD$20,000, an '[Import Permit](https://www.trade.gov.tw/ProcessList/ProcessList.aspx?nodeID=4366&pid=3)' must be applied for from the Bureau of Foreign Trade, Ministry of Economic Affairs (Tel: 02-23510271) before completing customs procedures.

*Diamonds/Gems/Platinum Beyond Personal Use: Equivalent to USD$500,000. For total values exceeding USD$20,000, an '[Import Permit](https://www.trade.gov.tw/ProcessList/ProcessList.aspx?nodeID=4366&pid=3)' must be applied for from the Bureau of Foreign Trade, Ministry of Economic Affairs (Tel: 02-23510271) before completing customs procedures.

## Prohibited Items

- Stun guns, flashlight-style stun devices
- Tear gas sprays, pepper sprays, anti-wolf sprays
- Small knives, folding knives, throwing darts, stun devices
- Various narcotics, possession of drug raw materials, prohibited or manufacturing substances for producing or converting narcotics
- Marijuana, THC, or marijuana-related products (CBD requires an import permit)
- Counterfeit goods
- Various meat products and meat floss (including ham, jerky, sausages, etc.), beef, lamb, pork, duck, goose, etc., are all prohibited
