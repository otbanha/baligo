Taking from the community, I’m here to give back!

This summer, our family of six finally set foot on Bali again. It’s been 23 years since our last family trip here. This island, hailed as the 'Island of the Gods,' remains as enchanting as ever, though the number of tourists has noticeably increased, and the tourism facilities have become more diverse. Our family was thoroughly satisfied with this 6-day, 5-night Bali trip. Below, I’ve compiled our pre-trip preparations, itinerary overview, and insights on hiring a private guide for your reference.

***

## ※ Pre-Trip Preparations

### 1. Air Tickets

Eager to immerse ourselves in this paradise, we opted for EVA Air’s direct flights (BR255／BR256), with a flight duration of approximately 5.5 hours.

### 2. (Very Important!) Visa, Entry, and Related Applications

Includes visas (eVOA／VOA), ~~eCD electronic customs declaration, health information card (SSHP)~~ (now integrated into [AIAC](/blog/2025-08-14-689db40ffd89780001eb2f3e/)), and tourism tax.

**Pro Tips (From Personal Experience):**

For all online applications, it's recommended to operate during the Indonesian government's daytime working hours for smoother system performance and fewer technical issues.

**(1) Visa: eVOA / VOA**

To save time upon arrival, I chose to apply for the eVOA (electronic Visa on Arrival) before departure, allowing me to use the automated immigration gates directly after landing.

- Required documents: Scanned passport page (JPG/PDF), passport photo (JPG), accommodation address, flight information, credit card
- Fee: IDR 500,000 per person (approx. USD$16)
- Official application website: <https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/> <--- Only official link
- Application guide: Many tutorial articles are available in forums, such as [【2026 Edition】Using "All Indonesia" Arrival Card for Bali Entry｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

**PS:**

The visa is valid for 30 days, so it's advisable to apply about 7 days before departure.

With eVOA, you can use the automated gates; I usually print a copy as backup.

**(2) eCD Electronic Customs Declaration & SSHP Health Declaration**

- Required documents: Passport details, eVOA number, flight and accommodation information
- Application website: "All Indonesia" integrated arrival system <https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/>
- Fee: Free
- Tutorial reference: [【2026 Edition】Using "All Indonesia" Arrival Card for Bali Entry｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

**PS:** Please save the QR code on your phone or print a copy for verification.

(3) Tourism Tax

- Required documents: Passport information, credit card
- Application method: Download the Love Bali App or visit <https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/home>
- Fee: IDR 150,000 per person (approximately USD$9)

**PS:** Similarly, save the QR code for verification.

(4) Currency Exchange

It’s recommended to exchange USD$100 in Taiwan first, then convert it to Indonesian Rupiah in Indonesia for a better rate. Local exchange rates vary based on USD denominations—$100 and $50 bills get the best rates, while $20, $10, $5, and $1 bills may have slightly lower rates.

## ※ Itinerary Overview & Travelogue

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1ddc462d2d0248effc0fb73f6691b047.png)

### **Day 1: Arrival in Bali → Jimbaran Beach Sunset & Seafood Dinner**

- **Attractions**: Currency exchange, Jimbaran sunset
- **Dining**: Dinner at Malafi Cafe Jimbaran
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

Upon arrival in Bali, Betty was already waiting at the airport with a sign, her warm smile instantly easing the fatigue from the long flight. We first headed to the villa to drop off our luggage, then straight to **Jimbaran Beach**. After 23 years, the sunset at Jimbaran remains as breathtaking as ever, with the aroma of seafood grilling wafting from the neatly lined stalls on the beach. Dinner was enjoyed at **Malafi Cafe Jimbaran**, savoring fresh seafood accompanied by live music, sitting together on the beach, feet in the sand, gazing at the orange-hued sunset sea view, filled with happiness.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3e474fcc7e6a5290032324cbaa76ba48.png)

For the first two nights, our villa accommodation offers options from one to four bedrooms. Those interested can refer to [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952).

### **Day 2: Blue Point Cliff Road → Uluwatu Temple → Relaxing Spa**

- **Attractions**: Water activities, Blue Point Cliff Road, Uluwatu Temple, Sakar Jepun Spa
- **Dining**: Lunch at WARUNG LAOTA, dinner at a market near the hotel
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f24fff8b05526923028a41d85303f88f.jpg)

Betty took us along the **Blue Point Cliff Road**, where the azure sea contrasted sharply with the steep cliffs, and many couples were taking wedding photos along the way, creating a romantic scene. Next, we visited **Uluwatu Temple**, marveling at the ancient Hindu architecture and exquisite stone carvings. In the afternoon, we arranged for **Sakar Jepun Spa**, where all six of us enjoyed traditional Balinese massages to relieve fatigue and recharge for the upcoming itinerary.

### **Day 3: Safari Park → Countryside Dinner**

- **Attractions**: Taman Safari Bali, Ayung River Rafting, Sukawati Market
- **Dining**: Lunch at Joni's Garden Restaurant (Dirty Duck Rice), Dinner at Freddies Resort & Villas
- **Accommodation**: [Freddies Resort & Villas Ubud by Kanaya Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23125492)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4a04261efc67f7ef8c6413d589e1ae9c.jpg)

**Taman Safari Bali** is a favorite among children, offering close encounters with elephants, tigers, and other animals. You can also take a safari ride into the wild area, experiencing the vastness of an African savanna. Dinner is served at **Freddies Resort & Villas**, where the serene environment and exquisite cuisine make for a perfect evening.

### **Day 4: Ubud Rafting → Art Village → Rice Terrace Swing → BBQ Ribs**

- **Attractions**: Ubud Rafting, Penglipuran Village, Tegallalang Rice Terrace (Jungle Swing)
- **Dining**: Lunch included with rafting, Dinner at iiga warung (BBQ Ribs)
- **Accommodation**: [Freddies Resort & Villas Ubud by Kanaya Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23125492)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9896437fb78834627a8728b3cc73f5b5.jpg)

In the morning, head to **Ayung River Rafting**, where the lush tropical rainforest scenery and gentle currents make it perfect for family fun—you can even jump in for a swim! Next, visit **Penglipuran Village**, a traditional Balinese art village rich in cultural heritage. The **Tegallalang Rice Terrace** jungle swing is a must-visit spot for Instagram-worthy photos, and our family couldn't resist taking a turn. Soaring high above the emerald-green rice terraces was both thrilling and breathtaking. Dinner at **iiga warung** featured delicious BBQ ribs with a sweet and savory sauce that we still reminisce about.
