Giving back to the community that has given so much!

This summer, our family of six finally set foot on Bali again. It had been 23 years since our last family trip here. This island, known as the 'Island of the Gods,' remains as enchanting as ever, though the number of tourists has noticeably increased, and the tourist facilities have become more diverse and abundant. Our family was very satisfied with this 6-day, 5-night Bali free-and-easy trip. Below, we’ve compiled our pre-trip preparations, itinerary overview, and insights on hiring a private guide for your reference.

## ※ Pre-Trip Preparations

### 1. Air Tickets

Eager to immerse ourselves in this paradise, we opted for a direct flight with EVA Air (BR255／BR256), which takes about 5.5 hours.

### 2. (Very Important!) Visa, Entry, and Related Applications

Includes visa (eVOA／VOA), ~~eCD electronic customs declaration, health information card (SSHP)~~ (now integrated into [AIAC](/blog/bali-evisa-customs-health-declaration/)), and tourism tax.

**Pro Tips (From Personal Experience):**

For all online applications, it's best to operate during the Indonesian government's regular working hours. The system runs more smoothly, and you're less likely to encounter strange technical issues.

**(1) Visa: eVOA / VOA**

To save time upon arrival, I chose to apply for the eVOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival) before departure. This allows you to go directly through the automated immigration gates after landing.

- Required documents: Scanned passport page (JPG/PDF), passport photo (JPG), accommodation address, flight information, credit card
- Fee: IDR 500,000 per person (approx. USD$31)
- Official application website: <https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/> <--- Only official link
- Application guide: There are many tutorial articles in the community, such as [【2026 Edition】Bali, Indonesia Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

**PS:**

The visa is valid for 30 days. It's recommended to apply about 7 days before departure.

With eVOA, you can use the automated gates. I usually print a paper copy as backup.

**(2) eCD Electronic Customs Declaration, SSHP Health Declaration**

- Required documents: Passport information, eVOA number, flight and accommodation details
- Application website: 'All Indonesia' integrated entry system <https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/>
- Fee: Free
- Tutorial reference: [【2026 Edition】Bali, Indonesia Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

**PS:** Please save the QR Code on your phone or print a copy for verification.

(3) Tourism Tax

- Required documents: Passport information, credit card
- Application method: Download the Love Bali App or visit the website <https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/home>
- Fee: IDR 150,000 per person (approximately USD$9)

**PS:** Similarly, please save the QR Code for verification.

(4) Currency Exchange

It’s recommended to exchange USD$100 in Taiwan first, then convert it to Indonesian Rupiah in Indonesia for a better rate. In Indonesia, the exchange rate varies based on the USD denomination—$100 and $50 bills get the best rates, while $20, $10, $5, and $1 bills may have slightly worse rates.

## ※ Itinerary Overview & Travelogue

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1ddc462d2d0248effc0fb73f6691b047.png)

### **Day 1: Arrival in Bali → Jimbaran Beach Sunset & Seafood Dinner**

- **Attractions**: Currency exchange, Jimbaran sunset
- **Dining**: Dinner at Malafi Cafe Jimbaran
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

Upon arriving in Bali, Betty was already waiting at the airport with a sign, her warm smile instantly easing the fatigue from the long flight. We first headed to the villa to drop off our luggage, then rushed straight to **Jimbaran Beach**. After 23 years, the sunset at Jimbaran remains as breathtaking as ever, with the aroma of seafood grilling wafting from the neatly lined stalls on the beach. Dinner was enjoyed at **Malafi Cafe Jimbaran**, savoring fresh seafood accompanied by live band music. Sitting together on the beach, feeling the fine sand beneath our feet and gazing at the orange-hued sunset over the sea, we were filled with happiness.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3e474fcc7e6a5290032324cbaa76ba48.png)

For the first two nights, our villa offered options ranging from one to four bedrooms. Those interested can refer to [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952).

### **Day 2: Blue Cliff Road → Uluwatu Temple → Relaxing Spa**

- **Attractions**: Water activities, Blue Cliff Road, Uluwatu Temple, Sakar Jepun Spa
- **Dining**: Lunch at WARUNG LAOTA, dinner at a nearby market
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f24fff8b05526923028a41d85303f88f.jpg)

Betty led us along the **Blue Cliff Road**, where the azure sea contrasted sharply with the steep cliffs. Along the way, we saw many couples taking wedding photos, creating a romantic scene. Next, we visited **Uluwatu Temple**, marveling at the ancient Hindu architecture and intricate stone carvings. In the afternoon, we enjoyed a traditional Balinese massage at **Sakar Jepun Spa**, relieving our fatigue and recharging for the upcoming itinerary.

### **Day 3: Safari Park → Countryside Dinner**

- **Attractions**: Taman Safari Bali, Ayung River Rafting, Sukawati Market
- **Dining**: Lunch at Joni's Garden Restaurant for Dirty Duck Rice, Dinner at Freddies Resort & Villas
- **Accommodation**: [Freddies Resort & Villas Ubud by Kanaya Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23125492)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4a04261efc67f7ef8c6413d589e1ae9c.jpg)

**Taman Safari Bali** is a favorite among children, offering close encounters with elephants, tigers, and other animals. You can also take a safari ride into the wild area, experiencing the vastness of an African savanna. Dinner is enjoyed at **Freddies Resort & Villas**, where the serene environment and exquisite cuisine make for a perfect evening.

### **Day 4: Ubud Rafting → Art Village → Rice Terrace Swing → Grilled Pork Ribs**

- **Attractions**: Ubud Rafting, Penglipuran Village, Tegallalang Rice Terrace (Jungle Swing)
- **Dining**: Lunch included with rafting, Dinner at iiga warung (Grilled Pork Ribs)
- **Accommodation**: [Freddies Resort & Villas Ubud by Kanaya Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23125492)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9896437fb78834627a8728b3cc73f5b5.jpg)

Start the morning with **Ayung River Rafting**, where the beautiful tropical rainforest scenery and gentle currents make it perfect for family fun—you can even jump in for a swim! Next, visit **Penglipuran Village**, a traditional Balinese art village rich in cultural heritage. The **Tegallalang Rice Terrace** jungle swing is a must-visit for Instagrammers, and our family couldn't resist queuing up for a turn. Soaring high above the lush green terraces was both thrilling and breathtaking. Dinner at **iiga warung** featured delicious grilled pork ribs with a sweet and savory sauce that we still reminisce about.
