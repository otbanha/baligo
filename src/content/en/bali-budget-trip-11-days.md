## **Day 1 (January 1, Wed) — Arrival in Bali, Start of Vacation Mode**

At 6 PM, we arrived at our Ubud hotel, officially starting our journey. Bali's night is filled with tropical charm, with a faint floral scent in the air, instantly making us feel relaxed and joyful. After dropping off our luggage, we walked out to the bustling streets for dinner, with plenty of restaurants to choose from. We rested early to prepare for the next day's adventure.

## **Day 2 (January 2, Thursday) — Jungle Exploration & Rafting Adventure**

### **ATV Ride & Rafting Experience**

- **Meeting Time**: 9:15 AM
- **Why We Recommend It**: Perfect for adventure-loving travelers!

In the morning, we drove ATVs through jungles, rice fields, and small streams. The scenery along the way was breathtaking, and the instructor patiently guided us on safety, making it easy even for beginners. Next, we headed to the river for rafting, which lasted about 2 hours. We recommend wearing light clothing or swimwear since you’re bound to get wet! During the rafting, the tropical rainforest scenery along the turquoise river was mesmerizing, and we even spotted monkeys jumping in the trees, which was a delightful surprise.

## **Day 3 (January 3, Friday) — Mount Batur Sunrise Jeep Tour**

### **Mount Batur Sunrise Jeep Tour**

**Cost**: USD$141 for 2 people (booked via Klook)

**Meeting Time**: 2:50 AM

The highlight of the day was the Mount Batur sunrise tour. We rode in a jeep through rugged mountain roads, admiring the starry sky along the way. Upon reaching the summit, the sky gradually brightened, and as the sun slowly rose, the entire island of Bali was bathed in warm morning light—a truly breathtaking sight.

## **Day 4 (January 4, Saturday) — Ubud Culture & Market Exploration**

![聖猴公園](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a5d878122e2993e138a29dc7b0cc9935.jpg)

The fare is around $4 for a 20-minute ride, allowing you to appreciate the artistic beauty of Bali's traditional architecture.

### **Ubud Market**

Within walking distance, the market offers handmade crafts, clothing, and accessories worth purchasing, and bargaining is possible.

The entrance fee is about $9 USD, and the lake reflects the traditional architecture, creating a picture-perfect postcard scene.

### **Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary**

The entrance ticket is $16 USD, and the visit takes about 1 hour. The monkeys are quite mischievous, so be sure to keep your belongings secure.

## **Day 5 (January 5, Sun) — Bali Safari and Marine Park**

### **Bali Safari Zoo**

**Ticket Price**: USD$98 per person (credit card payment)

**Tour Duration**: Depart at 9 AM, return by 4 PM

![野生動物園](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_67233c741fc10e5b047c021c1c9298ee.jpg)

Wildlife Park

Bali Safari Zoo is an ideal destination for families and animal lovers, and we spent an entire day exploring the park. In addition to seeing large animals like elephants and lions, there are many interactive activities, such as feeding giraffes and taking photos with tiger cubs. The safari bus ride through the park offers an up-close experience with various animals, which was truly awe-inspiring.

For families with children, this is a highly recommended activity—kids will absolutely love it. The zoo also offers decent dining options, and after having lunch inside the park, we continued exploring until 4 PM before reluctantly heading back to our hotel.

**Entrance Fee**: IDR 150,000

**Driving Time**: Approximately 1 hour 30 minutes from Ubud hotels

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple is a must-visit destination. This temple is situated by the lake, with its clear waters creating a picturesque scene that feels like stepping into a fairy tale. Here, we also experienced holding and feeding civets, which are incredibly adorable and perfect for taking memorable photos with family or friends. On-site, you can also try civet coffee, priced at around IDR 71,500 per cup. Its rich aroma makes it a unique experience not to be missed!

## **Day 8 (January 8, Wednesday) — Tanjung Benoa Water Sports Adventure**

### **Tanjung Benoa Water Sports**

**Cost**: 2,700,000 IDR

On this day, we headed to Tanjung Benoa for water sports activities, including snorkeling, canoeing, and banana boat rides. A word of caution: we didn’t book tickets in advance through Klook, which resulted in much higher prices on-site, becoming a minor regret of the trip. We chose **Flying Fish** and **Parasailing**, spending a total of 2,700,000 IDR (approximately USD$175 for two people)...

If you plan to participate in water sports at Tanjung Benoa, don’t make the same mistake I did by not researching beforehand and ending up paying several times more! **Be sure to book tickets online in advance**—not only will you save a significant amount, but you’ll also avoid being overcharged on-site.
