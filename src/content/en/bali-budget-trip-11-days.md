## **Day 2 (January 2, Thursday) — Jungle Exploration and Rafting Adventure**

### **ATV and Rafting Experience**

- **Meeting Time**: 9:15 AM
- **Recommended Reason**: Perfect for adventure lovers!

In the morning, we rode ATVs through jungles, rice fields, and streams. The scenery was breathtaking, and the instructors were patient with safety guidance, making it easy even for beginners. Next, we headed to the river for a 2-hour rafting session. It’s best to wear light clothing or swimwear because you’re guaranteed to get wet! Along the way, the lush tropical rainforest along the azure riverbanks was mesmerizing, and we even spotted monkeys jumping in the trees, which was a delightful surprise.

## **Day 3 (January 3, Friday) — Mount Batur Sunrise Jeep Tour**

### **Mount Batur Sunrise Jeep Tour**

**Cost**: USD$141 for 2 people (booked via Klook)
**Meeting Time**: 2:50 AM

The highlight of the day was the Mount Batur sunrise tour. We rode a jeep through rugged mountain roads, admiring the starry sky along the way. Upon reaching the summit, the sky gradually brightened, and as the sun slowly rose, the entire island of Bali was bathed in warm morning light—a truly breathtaking sight.

## **Day 5 (January 5, Sun) — Bali Safari & Marine Park**

### **Bali Safari & Marine Park**

**Ticket Cost**: USD$98 per person (paid by card)
**Itinerary Time**: Depart at 9 AM, return at 4 PM

![野生動物園](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_67233c741fc10e5b047c021c1c9298ee.jpg)

Wildlife Park

Bali Safari & Marine Park is a perfect destination for families and animal lovers, and we spent the entire day exploring the park. In addition to seeing large animals like elephants and lions, there are many interactive activities, such as feeding giraffes and taking photos with tiger cubs. The safari bus ride, in particular, allows you to observe various animals up close, offering an incredibly immersive experience.

For families with children, I highly recommend this activity—the kids will absolutely love it. The park also offers decent dining options, and after having lunch there, we continued exploring until 4 PM, reluctantly returning to our hotel.

## **Day 6 (January 6, Mon) — Nature & Culture Tour: Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, Tirta Empul, and Tegallalang Rice Terraces**

### **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple**

  **Entrance Fee**: $10
  **Driving Time**: Approximately 1 hour 30 minutes from Ubud hotels

![水神廟 / 超軟超可愛的麝香貓](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_677e8fafd966063cdb53b173ed3256d0.jpg)

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple / Super Soft and Adorable Civets

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple is a must-visit destination. This temple is situated by the lake, reflecting on the clear waters, creating a picturesque scene that feels like stepping into a fairy tale. Here, we also experienced holding civets for photos and feeding them. These little creatures are incredibly cute, making it perfect for taking memorable photos with family or friends. On-site, you can also try civet coffee, priced at around $5 per cup. The aroma is rich and unique, making it an experience you shouldn't miss!

### **Tirta Empul Temple**

  **Entrance Fee**: $7

Tirta Empul Temple is a famous religious site in Bali, where many locals come to perform bathing rituals for blessings. Although we didn't participate in the holy water bathing, just observing the locals' rituals allowed us to feel the strong religious atmosphere. If you're particularly interested in religious culture, this place is worth a visit; if time is limited, this attraction can be arranged accordingly.

**Admission Fee**: USD$7

![德哥拉朗梯田](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_633e3a95afc0cd64f57f4c94319ffe5b.jpg)

Tegalalang Rice Terraces

In the afternoon, we headed to the Tegalalang Rice Terraces, also known as Alas Harum, one of Bali's most iconic natural landscapes. ## **Day 7 (January 7, Tuesday) — Tanah Lot Temple and Cliffside Bar Sunset**

### **Tanah Lot Temple**

**Admission Fee**: $10 USD

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f7365ccab1629758df838b22b7d55cb3.jpg)

In the morning, we set out early to avoid traffic and arrived smoothly at Tanah Lot Temple. This structure sits on a rock in the sea, and during high tide, the entire temple is surrounded by water, creating a breathtaking and unique sight. It's an unmissable landmark in Bali. We took many photos and felt the beauty of nature intertwined with religion.

## **Day 8 (January 8th, Wednesday) — Tanjung Benoa Water Sports Adventure**

### **Tanjung Benoa Water Sports**

  **Cost**: IDR 2,700,000

On this day, we headed to Tanjung Benoa for water sports, including snorkeling, canoeing, and banana boat rides. A word of caution: we didn’t book tickets in advance through Klook, which led to significantly higher prices on-site, becoming a minor regret of the trip. We chose **Flying Fish** and **Parasailing**, spending a total of IDR 2,700,000 (approximately USD$175 for two)...

If you plan to participate in Tanjung Benoa water sports, don’t make the same mistake I did by not researching beforehand and ending up paying much more! **Be sure to book online in advance**, not only to save a significant amount but also to avoid being overcharged on-site.
