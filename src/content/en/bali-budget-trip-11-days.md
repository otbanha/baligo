## **Day 1 (Jan 1, Wed) — Arrival in Bali, Vacation Mode On**

At 6 PM, we arrived at our hotel in Ubud, officially kicking off this trip. After dropping off our luggage, we stepped out onto the lively streets to find dinner—there were plenty of restaurants to choose from. We turned in early to rest up for the next day's adventure.

## **Day 2 (January 2, Thursday) — Exploring the Jungle and White-Water Rafting**

### **ATV Riding and Rafting Adventure**

- **Meeting Time**: 9:15 AM
- **Why We Recommend It**: Perfect for adventure-loving travelers!

In the morning, we drove ATVs through the jungle, rice paddies, and streams. The scenery along the way was stunning, and the instructor patiently guided us on safety, making it easy even for beginners to get the hang of it. Next, we headed to the river for about 2 hours of rafting. I recommend wearing light clothing or a swimsuit, because you'll definitely get wet! During the rafting, the tropical rainforest along the crystal-clear blue river was a feast for the eyes, and we even spotted monkeys jumping in the trees from time to time—a delightful surprise.

## **Day 5 (January 5, Sun) — Bali Safari & Marine Park**

### **Bali Safari Zoo**

  **Ticket Price**: USD$98 per person (paid by card)
  **Schedule**: Departs at 9:00 AM, returns at 4:00 PM

![野生動物園](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_67233c741fc10e5b047c021c1c9298ee.jpg)

Wildlife Park

Bali Safari Zoo is a fantastic spot for families and animal lovers—we spent the whole day there. Besides seeing large animals like elephants and lions, the park offers interactive activities such as feeding giraffes and taking photos with baby tigers. The safari tram ride, in particular, lets you observe various animals up close, making for an incredibly immersive experience.

If you're traveling with kids, I highly recommend this itinerary—they'll have a blast. The zoo also has decent dining options; we had lunch on-site and continued exploring until we reluctantly headed back to the hotel at 4 PM.

## **Day 6 (January 6, Monday) — Nature & Culture Tour: Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, Tirta Empul Temple & Tegallalang Rice Terraces**

### **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple**

**Admission Fee**: 150,000 Indonesian Rupiah

![水神廟 / 超軟超可愛的麝香貓](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_677e8fafd966063cdb53b173ed3256d0.jpg)

**Drive Time**: About 1 hour 30 minutes from your hotel in Ubud

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple / Super soft and adorable civet cats

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple is a must-visit attraction. Nestled by the lake, the temple reflects off the clear waters, creating a picturesque scene that feels like stepping into a fairy tale. Here, we also got to hold and feed the adorable civet cats—perfect for snapping photos with family or friends. On-site, you can also taste civet coffee, priced at around 71,500 Indonesian Rupiah per cup. It's rich in aroma and truly unique—an experience not to be missed!

## **Day 8 (January 8, Wednesday) — South Bay Water Sports Adventure**

### **South Bay Water Sports**

  **Cost**: 2,700,000 Indonesian Rupiah

On this day, we headed to South Bay for water sports, including snorkeling, canoeing, and banana boat rides. A key note: we didn't book tickets in advance on Klook, which led to much higher prices on-site—a small regret of the trip. We chose **Flying Fish** and **Parasailing**, costing the two of us a total of 2,700,000 IDR (about USD$174 for two)...

If you're planning water sports at South Bay, don't make the same mistake I did by not researching beforehand, which cost us several times more! **Be sure to book tickets online in advance**—not only will you save a lot of money, but you'll also avoid being overcharged on-site.
