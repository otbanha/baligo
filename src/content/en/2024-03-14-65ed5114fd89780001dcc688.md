## Recommended Activities & Experiences

### Surfing at Kuta Beach

1. One-on-one coaching includes an hour of instruction and an hour of free practice. While the teaching style is a bit rougher compared to Taiwan, it’s still a good experience overall. However, the amount of trash on Kuta Beach is quite noticeable.
2. To avoid sunburn, we scheduled our surfing session for around 9 AM. During this time, we were the only group out there, making the experience quite enjoyable.
3. During surfing, there’s no need to worry about your valuables—just hand them over to the private driver.
4. A dedicated photographer captured photos and videos of us, allowing us to preserve these precious memories.
5. Changing rooms and showers are also available (additional fees apply).

### Sunset Beach Horseback Riding

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6ff1e2744d79be91e88a8775750847dc.png)

1. Through online booking, it costs approximately $55 per person, providing one-on-one coaching where the instructor leads the horse.
2. The experience was quite enjoyable, with each instructor assisting by taking photos and videos using our own phones.
3. However, all activities carry certain risks. Towards the end of the experience, I was suddenly thrown off the horse. Fortunately, I had maintained control of the horse throughout and possess a certain level of athletic ability. As I fell from the horse, I partly jumped off myself, thus avoiding serious injury. My coach apologized profusely, and in the end, their staff drove me back on a small motorcycle. After discussing with the vendor, they mentioned this was the first time such an incident had occurred and wanted to understand exactly what happened. However, I only recall the horse suddenly starting to buck, leaving me feeling helpless, and the coach was equally at a loss.
4. The scenery here is truly stunning, and compared to Taiwan, the prices are quite affordable. The only thing to note is safety; indeed, all activities carry certain risks, making travel insurance essential.

### Sunrise Jeep Tour

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d007f0cd9a6adf529354c1ea176d2bd4.jpg)

Contributed by fellow traveler Li Ly

1. Booked through KLOOK for around USD$48 per person, including transfers.
2. At 2:50 AM, driver Dita arrived punctually at the hotel lobby for pickup. Although he drove fast, he maintained safety. When I forgot my water in the car on the return trip, he kindly brought it back to the hotel.
3. Jeep driver Suda was very friendly, always giving advance notice before turns and steep sections, ensuring we were holding on securely before proceeding. He also enthusiastically helped take photos.
4. Due to bad weather, we missed the sunrise. The driver mentioned that on clear mornings, you can enjoy a sky full of stars before dawn. It was a bit disappointing not to experience it this time, but I’d love to go again!
5. Remember to dress warmly and bring a hat, as it gets very cold in the early morning at high altitudes.

## Bali Accommodation Recommendations & Insights

![Inara Alas Harum](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dde839ff80add28fe06b81c2eba394f3.jpg)

Inara Alas Harum

This time we stayed at three places, and one of them, [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751), left a deep impression on us. Although it's located in the jungle, away from bustling areas (but Arie ensures safe transportation), the staff were incredibly friendly. Any requests could be made through WhatsApp, and even though our English wasn't great, it wasn't an issue as they patiently translated for us. Despite being in the jungle, the environment was exceptionally clean and tidy. Among the three places we stayed, this was the cleanest, and we didn't encounter any bugs.

On our third day, we stayed at a well-established villa in Jimbaran, but it turned out to be a nightmare. The air conditioning was noisy and ineffective, the water pressure was low, and there were many millipedes both outside and inside the room. Fortunately, they offered a complimentary breakfast from 07:00 to 12:00 with a wide variety of options, and we tried several dishes that were all delicious. Additionally, they provided a free afternoon tea from 16:00 to 17:00, which could be ordered to the room and was equally delightful (those spring rolls left us wanting more). The dinner (at our own expense) was also very tasty, though we didn't have any alcohol.

Swimming in the jungle pool at [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) was incredibly refreshing, and they even had a hot tub, though it's a shame they don't offer spa services. Overall, we'd definitely stay at [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) again on our next trip to Bali. With nightly rates under $50, we felt like we were truly on vacation.

## About Tipping

Regarding tipping, we usually give based on how we feel. Basically, we tip for assistance with luggage and room service. For restaurants that don't include a service charge, we'll tip if the service is good, and even for those that do include a service charge, we'll still tip if the service is excellent. As for spa treatments, regardless of whether a service charge is included, we always tip if the experience is comfortable and enjoyable.

Additionally, we also gave tips to surfing instructors, jeep drivers, and shuttle drivers. However, for the horseback riding experience, I didn't leave a tip because I fell off the horse.
