## Recommended Activities & Insights

### Surfing at Kuta Beach

1. The one-on-one coaching session included one hour of instruction and one hour of free practice. While the teaching style was slightly rougher compared to what you'd find in Taiwan, it was still a good experience overall. However, the amount of trash on Kuta Beach was quite noticeable.
2. To avoid sunburn, we scheduled our surfing session for around 9 AM. During this time slot, we were the only group out on the water, which made for an excellent experience.
3. During surfing, there was no need to worry about our valuables, as we simply handed them over to our private driver for safekeeping.
4. A dedicated photographer was on hand to capture photos and videos of us, allowing us to preserve these precious memories.
5. Changing rooms and showers were also available (additional fees applied).

### Sunset Beach Horseback Riding

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6ff1e2744d79be91e88a8775750847dc.png)

1. By booking online, it costs around USD$55 per person and includes a one-on-one instructor who will lead the horse.
2. The experience was quite enjoyable, as each instructor helped take photos and videos using our own phones.
3. However, all activities come with certain risks. Towards the end of the experience, I was suddenly thrown off the horse. Fortunately, I had been holding onto the horse firmly and have decent athletic ability, so when I fell, I partly jumped off and partly was thrown, resulting in minimal injury. My instructor kept apologizing, and eventually, their staff took me back on a small scooter. After discussing with the vendor, they mentioned this was the first time such an incident had occurred and wanted to understand exactly what happened. However, all I remember is the horse suddenly started bucking, leaving both me and the instructor feeling helpless.
4. The scenery here is truly stunning, and compared to Taiwan, the prices are quite affordable. The only thing to be mindful of is safety, as all activities inherently carry some level of risk, making travel insurance essential.

### Sunrise Jeep Adventure

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d007f0cd9a6adf529354c1ea176d2bd4.jpg)

Contributed by fellow traveler Li Ly

1. Booked through KLOOK for around USD$48 per person, including transfers.
2. At 2:50 AM, driver Dita arrived punctually at the hotel lobby for pickup. Although he drove fast, he maintained safety. When I forgot my water bottle in the car on the return trip, he kindly brought it back to the hotel.
3. Jeep driver Suda was very friendly, always informing us in advance about turns and steep sections, ensuring we were holding on securely before proceeding. He also diligently helped take photos.
4. Due to bad weather, we couldn't see the sunrise. The driver mentioned that on clear mornings, we could have enjoyed a sky full of stars. It was a bit disappointing, but I'd love to try again!
5. Remember to dress warmly and bring a hat, as it gets very cold in the early morning at high altitudes.

## Bali Accommodation Recommendations & Insights

![Inara Alas Harum](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dde839ff80add28fe06b81c2eba394f3.jpg)

Inara Alas Harum

During our trip, we stayed at three different places, and one that left a lasting impression was [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751). Although it's nestled in the jungle and not in a bustling area (but Arie ensures safe transportation), the staff were incredibly friendly. Any requests could be made via WhatsApp, and even with our limited English, communication wasn't an issue as they patiently translated. Despite being in the jungle, the environment was exceptionally clean and tidy—out of the three places we stayed, this was the cleanest, and we didn't encounter any bugs.

On the third day, we stayed at a well-established villa in Jimbaran, but it turned out to be a nightmare. The air conditioner was noisy and ineffective, the water pressure was low, and there were many millipedes both outside and inside the room. Fortunately, they offered a complimentary breakfast from 7:00 AM to 12:00 PM with a wide variety of options, and we tried several dishes that were all delicious. Additionally, they provided a free afternoon tea from 4:00 PM to 5:00 PM, which could be ordered to the room and was equally delightful (those spring rolls were unforgettable). The dinner (at our own expense) was also very tasty, although we didn't have any alcohol.

Swimming in the jungle at Inara Alas Harum was incredibly refreshing, and they even have a hot pool, though it's a shame they don't offer spa services. Overall, we would definitely stay at Inara Alas Harum again on our next trip to Bali. With nightly rates under USD$50, we felt fully immersed in the vacation vibe.

## About Tipping

When it comes to tipping, we usually give based on our mood. Essentially, we tip for assistance with luggage and room service. At restaurants without a service charge, we tip if the service is good, and even at places that already include a service charge, we still tip for excellent service. As for spa treatments, regardless of whether a service charge is included, we always tip if the experience was relaxing and enjoyable.

Additionally, we also gave tips to surfing instructors, jeep drivers, and shuttle drivers. However, I didn't leave a tip for the horseback riding experience since I fell off the horse.
