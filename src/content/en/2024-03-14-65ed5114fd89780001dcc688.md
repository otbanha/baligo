## Activity Recommendations & Experiences

### Surfing at Kuta Beach

1. One-on-one coaching, including one hour of instruction and one hour of free practice. Although the teaching was a bit rougher compared to Taiwan, it was still a good experience. However, Kuta Beach was quite littered.
2. To avoid sunburn, we scheduled our surfing session for 9 AM. At this time, we were the only group, which made for a great experience.
3. During surfing, there was no need to worry about our valuables as we simply handed them over to our hired driver.
4. A professional photographer was available to capture photos and videos, allowing us to preserve precious memories.
5. Changing rooms and showers were also provided (additional fees applied).

### Sunset Beach Horseback Riding

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6ff1e2744d79be91e88a8775750847dc.png)

1. Booked online for around $55 per person, includes one-on-one coaching with the instructor leading the horse.
2. The experience was quite enjoyable, with each instructor helping to take photos and videos using our phones.
3. However, all activities carry some risk. Towards the end of the ride, I was suddenly thrown off the horse. Fortunately, I had been holding onto the horse tightly and have decent athletic ability, so I managed to roll away and wasn't seriously injured. The instructor apologized profusely and their staff drove me back on a scooter. When discussing with the operator, they said this was the first time such an incident had occurred and wanted to understand exactly what happened, but I only remember the horse suddenly bucking and feeling helpless, as did the instructor.
4. The scenery here is truly beautiful, and compared to Taiwan, the price is very reasonable. The only thing to note is safety - in fact, all activities carry some risk, so travel insurance is essential.

### Sunrise Jeep Tour

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d007f0cd9a6adf529354c1ea176d2bd4.jpg)

Contributed by fellow traveler Li Ly

1. Booked through KLOOK for around $48 per person, including pickup.
2. Driver Dita arrived promptly at the hotel lobby at 2:50 AM. Although he drove fast, he maintained safety. I forgot my water in the car on the way back and he kindly returned it to the hotel.
3. Jeep driver Suda was very friendly, notifying us before turns and steep sections and making sure we were holding on securely before proceeding. He also diligently helped take photos.
4. Due to bad weather, we didn't get to see the sunrise. The driver said that if the weather was good, we could have seen a sky full of stars in the early morning. It was a bit disappointing that we didn't get to experience that this time, but I would definitely want to go again!
5. Remember to dress warmly and bring a hat, as it gets very cold in the high mountains in the early morning.

## Bali Accommodation Recommendations & Insights

![Inara Alas Harum](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dde839ff80add28fe06b81c2eba394f3.jpg)

Inara Alas Harum

During our stay, we tried three different accommodations, and one that left a lasting impression was [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751). Although it’s nestled in the jungle and not in a bustling area (but Arie ensures safe transportation), the staff were incredibly friendly. We could easily communicate our needs via WhatsApp, and even with our limited English, they patiently translated for us. Despite being in the jungle, the environment was impeccably clean—the cleanest of the three places we stayed—and we didn’t encounter any bugs.

On the third day, we stayed at an older villa in Jimbaran, but it turned out to be a nightmare. The air conditioner was noisy and ineffective, the water pressure was low, and there were millipedes both outside and inside the room. The silver lining was their complimentary breakfast from 7:00 AM to 12:00 PM, which offered a variety of delicious options we tried multiple times. They also provided free afternoon tea from 4:00 PM to 5:00 PM, which we could enjoy in our room—equally delightful (the spring rolls were unforgettable). The dinner (at our own expense) was also tasty, though we didn’t drink.

Swimming in the jungle pool at [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) was incredibly refreshing, and they even had a hot tub, though they didn’t offer spa services. Overall, we’d definitely stay at [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) again on our next trip to Bali. At less than $50 per night, it gave us a true sense of vacation bliss.

## About Tipping

When it comes to tipping, we usually give based on our mood. Generally, we tip for luggage assistance and room service. For restaurants without a service charge, we tip if the service is good, and even for those with a service charge, we still tip if the service is exceptional. For spa experiences, regardless of whether a service charge is included, we tip if the experience is comfortable and enjoyable.

We also tip surf instructors, jeep drivers, and transfer drivers. However, I didn’t tip for the horseback riding experience because I fell off the horse.
