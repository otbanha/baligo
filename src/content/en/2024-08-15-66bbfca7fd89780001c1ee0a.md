Bali is a classic beach club destination, much like trendy cafes and scenic restaurants. The island is dotted with cool party spots where you can enjoy endless ocean, sand, and sunshine to the beats of world-class DJs and delicious cocktails. If you visit Bali without experiencing a beach club, your vacation is incomplete. These clubs not only offer unparalleled views and a relaxing atmosphere but also allow you to enjoy gourmet food and music under stunning sunsets, making it the best way to experience Bali's passion and energy.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b156a1d76f404299fe1d74af57247cd2.jpg)

Seminyak offers a great mix of relaxed and elegant beach clubs, as well as vibrant and charming venues. If it's your first time in Bali, this is an excellent choice since most beach clubs here are easily accessible.

If you're a true party enthusiast, you'll love the beach clubs in Canggu, which provide endless opportunities to dance from morning till night. If you're looking for a more relaxed beach vibe (e.g., with family), the southern regions of Bali might be a better fit. Uluwatu and Jimbaran both offer dreamy seaside retreats where you can enjoy fantastic ocean views, delicious food, and expertly crafted cocktails.

However, with so many options, it can be hard to decide. That's why we've compiled a list of Bali's hottest beach clubs, whether you prefer dancing on tables or lounging on bean bags. The best part? None of these clubs charge an entry fee!

### **_Potato Head Beach Club_**

Potato Head Beach Club has long been one of Bali's most popular beach clubs, combining stylish design, vibrant activities, and delicious cuisine to attract visitors from around the world. Whether you want to relax on a sunbed or immerse yourself in a lively nighttime party, there's something here for everyone.

The design of Potato Head Beach Club blends local and modern styles, with a focus on sustainability and eco-friendliness. It features a large swimming pool surrounded by spacious sunbathing areas and luxurious cabanas overlooking the ocean. At night, the venue transforms into a vibrant social hub with DJ sets, live music performances, and movie screenings, making it a center of nightlife.

- **_Minimum Spend_**: Varies by area; sunbeds start at Rp. 500,000 (~USD$33)
- **_Menu Price Range_**: Main dishes start at Rp. 100,000 (~USD$6.60); drinks start at Rp. 75,000 (~USD$5)
- **_Address_**: Jl. Petitenget No.51B, Seminyak, Kuta Utara, Kabupaten Badung, Bali 80361
- **_Opening Hours_**: Mon-Sun 10am-2am
- **_Website_**: [Visit Website](https://potatohead.co)

### **_Ku De Ta_**

Since its opening in 2000, Ku De Ta has been one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs. Its open design and expansive ocean views have earned it international acclaim, attracting celebrities and fashionistas alike.

Ku De Ta's menu offers a range of modern European and Asian cuisine, with all ingredients carefully selected for freshness and quality. The cocktails here are also renowned for their innovation and sophistication, whether you're looking for a refreshing daytime drink or an evening cocktail.

- **_Minimum Spend_**: None
- **_Menu Price Range_**: Main dishes start at Rp. 150,000 (~USD$10); drinks start at Rp. 85,000 (~USD$5.70)
- **_Address_**: Jl. Kayu Aya No.9, Seminyak, Kuta Utara, Kabupaten Badung, Bali 80361

With spacious sunbathing areas, diving platforms, and exciting activities, it attracts countless young people and party enthusiasts.

Mrs Sippy's menu primarily focuses on Mediterranean flavors, offering fresh seafood, grilled meats, and delicious salads. The drink selection is equally impressive, ranging from fresh juices to creative cocktails.

As the sun begins to set, you'll have the best seats to witness the magnificent ocean sunset! When evening drinks roll around, indulge in their cocktail bar! Adorable Tiki cups filled with delicious cocktails make for an unparalleled island experience!

It's only about 20 minutes from the airport. You can step off the plane, slip into your bikini, and sip a martini in no time! Azul Beach Club is a must-visit destination, offering the unique charm of Bali.

## Dress Code and Entry Fee Guide

If you're planning to visit Bali's beach clubs—which I highly recommend—knowing a few tips beforehand can make your experience even better. Here are some practical suggestions to help you make the most of your day:

1. **_Check Opening Hours_**
   Some beach clubs may temporarily close for private events, so it's best to confirm their availability on your planned date.
2. **_Special Events and Tickets_**
   Special events like parties or music festivals may require advance ticket purchases, which is a wise choice as prices on the day can be significantly higher.
3. **_How Entry Fees Are Used_**
   Many beach clubs include a food and beverage credit with their entry fee to maximize revenue. While this is still cost-effective, the primary goal is to ensure profitability for daybeds and limited seating.
4. **_Restricted Items_**
   You may be required to leave all food and drinks, including bottled water, at the entrance.
5. **_Dress Code_**
   Most beach clubs have a minimum dress code—no tank tops or T-shirts with beer logos, and some venues require shoes (not flip-flops) after 4 PM. Enforcement varies by season and crowd, with women generally having more lenient requirements than men.
6. **_Security Checks_**
   You'll go through security checks upon entry, including inspections of backpacks, shopping bags, and handbags.
7. **_Reserve Daybeds_**
   If you want to secure a daybed at a popular club or a prime spot for sunset, it's best to book in advance.
