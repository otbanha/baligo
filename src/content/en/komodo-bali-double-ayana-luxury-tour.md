## Komodo National Park: A Must-Visit at Least Once in a Lifetime

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784818112469-d2.jpg)

When it comes to Komodo, the most anticipated sight is undoubtedly the world's only wild Komodo dragon.

Seeing these ancient giants, which have survived for millions of years, roaming freely in the national park is a thrill that no zoo can match. Guided by professional rangers, you can safely learn about their ecology and habits.

## Check In at the Dreamy Pink Beach

Truly natural pink beaches are extremely rare worldwide, and Komodo's Pink Beach is one of them.

Pink sand meets crystal-clear blue waters, creating a dreamy gradient under the sunlight. Whether you're snapping photos or taking a stroll, it feels like stepping into a postcard—a must-visit beach on many travelers' bucket lists.

## Swimming with Giant Manta Rays

The Komodo waters offer world-class snorkeling conditions.

With glassy, crystal-clear seas and vibrant coral reefs, the highlight is encountering graceful giant manta rays at Manta Point. Watching them glide effortlessly is an unforgettable travel memory for many.

## Padar Island's Three-Colored Bays: Breathtaking Beauty

Many first discover Komodo through the iconic landscape of Padar Island.

From the viewpoint, you can take in three bays in different colors at once, set against a unique volcanic terrain. This world-class vista is Komodo's most iconic photo spot.

## No Group Tours, Just Real Travel
