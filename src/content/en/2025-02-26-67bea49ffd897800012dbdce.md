With the rapid development of mobile technology, the Indonesian government is preparing to introduce a series of new mobile communication regulations that will significantly impact foreigners in Bali.

Among these, the adoption of eSIM technology is seen as a positive advancement, but the new policy limiting the number of SIM cards may complicate mobile phone usage for foreigners in Indonesia.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c092bff8309cf706ba1895ecc3468d9d.jpg)

## 🔍 eSIM Technology Becomes a New Trend in Indonesian Mobile Communication

The Ministry of Communication and Digital Technology is pushing telecom operators to accelerate the transition from physical SIM cards to eSIMs. This new bill is expected to be passed soon.

Currently, major telecom providers like Telkomsel and Indosat already offer eSIM services, but due to the lack of unified standards, consumers still face many difficulties in using them.

The new bill will introduce a standardized process for eSIM activation and usage, while also enhancing personal data protection and reducing fraud risks.

### 👉 Advantages of eSIM include:

- No need to replace physical SIM cards; activation can be completed online
- Convenient for travelers to pre-purchase and use immediately, eliminating the hassle of physical SIM cards
- Enhanced user privacy protection and device security

## ⚠️ Impact of SIM Card Quantity Restrictions on Foreigners

According to statistics, Indonesia currently has 314 million registered SIM cards, but only about 280 million active users.

The surplus SIM cards are largely attributed to foreigners in Bali, who frequently switch SIM cards to bypass IMEI registration and phone blocking restrictions.

## 📌 Background on IMEI Blocking:

- Imported phones that have not been declared and taxed will be blocked if used on Indonesian telecom networks for more than 90 days.
- Workaround: Foreigners purchase new SIM cards to reactivate the IMEI, thereby extending the phone's usability.

📋 How Will the New Policy Change Things?

- Closing the IMEI loophole: Prohibiting the resetting of IMEI registration by swapping SIM cards, preventing undeclared phones from long-term use.
- SIM card quantity restrictions: Strict control over the number of SIM cards each user can register, with specific limits yet to be announced.
- Enhancing data security: Standardizing eSIM registration processes to prevent SIM card misuse or illegal sales.
