During our vacation in Bali, my travel companion and I wanted to create more beautiful memories. Among the many travel options, we decided to book a special tour - the "Bali Professional Photographer Day Tour." This package not only includes sightseeing but also features a professional photographer accompanying us throughout the day, using both cameras and drones to capture every precious moment. For us, who wanted stunning photos without missing out on firsthand experiences, this arrangement was perfect!

## Pre-Departure Preparation

In the first few days of our Bali trip, we stayed at the Sheraton Bali Kuta Resort, and the location couldn’t have been better! Right next door was the Beachwalk Shopping Center, filled with various brand stores and restaurants, keeping us happily occupied. Even better, just across the street was Kuta Beach, where we spent our days learning to surf and feeling the thrill of the Indian Ocean waves. In the evenings, we enjoyed breathtaking sunsets from the resort’s seaside pool. At night, we strolled through the lively streets, savoring a variety of local and international cuisines. Kuta’s nightlife gave us a taste of Bali’s youthful and vibrant side.

The day before our tour, we received a confirmation message from our photographer and guide, Udin, via WhatsApp, detailing the pickup time and location. The Bali Photographer Day Tour offers three itineraries: **East Bali**, **Ubud and Waterfalls**, and **North Bali**. After discussing with my travel companion, we chose the "Ubud and Waterfalls" route, allowing us to experience Bali’s most iconic cultural sites and natural beauty.

## The Journey Begins - Departure from Kuta at 8:00 AM

At 8:00 AM sharp, our photographer and guide, Udin, arrived at our hotel in Kuta to pick us up. This professional photographer not only captured our moments but also served as our guide and driver, making the entire trip more convenient and personalized.

As we drove away from Kuta, our journey to Ubud began. The scenery gradually transformed from coastal cityscapes to lush countryside vistas, heightening our excitement for the upcoming adventure.

## First Stop: Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary

Upon arriving at the Sacred Monkey Forest, our photographer purchased our entrance tickets, which were included in the package service. Stepping into this sacred forest, we were immediately awestruck by the dense primordial jungle and ancient stone carvings.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cc3f496a03ce0af43e1dac90c9f6bda4.jpg)

Here, the mischievous monkeys became the perfect supporting characters in our photos. Our photographer skillfully used professional equipment to capture precious moments of our interactions with the monkeys. The ancient temple structures, surrounded by lush greenery, appeared particularly mystical, with every angle offering a perfect backdrop. We spent nearly an hour exploring this enchanting place.

## Second Stop: Tegallalang Rice Terraces

After leaving the monkey forest, we arrived at the famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces. Our photographer again handled the entrance tickets, allowing us to fully enjoy this green wonder without any worries.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c9715ea0e3f5452bc5324c6403d129ca.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0a56ec0011c1598693f0ffae9cb5a748.jpg)

Standing on the viewing platform, we marveled at the layered rice fields stretching like green stairways to the horizon. Our photographer took full advantage of drone capabilities, capturing the terraces' grandeur from various heights and angles. We also strolled through the fields, letting the photographer capture our natural expressions as we immersed ourselves in this breathtaking landscape.

## Lunch Break

Near the Tegallalang Rice Terraces, we chose a local restaurant for lunch. While the meal was an additional expense, savoring authentic Balinese cuisine while enjoying views of the terraces made this meal particularly memorable. Our photographer also took this opportunity to show us some of the stunning shots they had captured.

## Third Stop: Tibumana Waterfall

After lunch, we headed to the final stop of our day's itinerary - Tibumana Waterfall.

Located a bit away from Ubud, this distance allowed us to enjoy the serene beauty with fewer crowds. After a short nap in the car, we arrived at the parking lot. Our tour package included tickets to all attractions, so we simply followed Udin. As we walked along the trail through the lush tropical rainforest, the sound of the waterfall grew clearer. Tibumana Waterfall appeared like silver threads cascading from the sky into the emerald pool below.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0f90bb6512bf564843f7ec10f5422a89.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cc0b6e8b9a4393a670407aa8edbc6aeb.jpg)

Here, the photographer made full use of the drone's capabilities, capturing the waterfall's full grandeur from above, while also documenting our various poses in front of the falls with the camera. The clear pool reflected our joyful smiles, and every photo was brimming with vibrant energy.

## Return Journey - 15:00 Back to Kuta

At 3 PM, we began our journey back to Kuta. In the car, the photographer shared amusing anecdotes from past shoots: 'Last week, a couple got engaged at the waterfall, and the monkey almost snatched the ring...'

Throughout the trip, the photographer not only professionally captured our precious moments but also fluently introduced the history and cultural background of each site in English, making this journey more than just sightseeing—it was a deep cultural experience.
