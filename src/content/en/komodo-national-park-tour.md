## **Origin: Meeting the Travel Agency**

This day tour was arranged through a local travel agency in Komodo. Since the three of us originally planned to charter a private boat in Labuan Bajo but couldn't gather enough people, we decided to join their standard speedboat day tour. This journey allowed us to experience Indonesia's unique natural wonders and the warm hospitality of the locals, so we wanted to share our experience.

## **Getting to Komodo: How to Arrive**

Komodo National Park is located east of Bali, about a one-hour flight away. Since there are no direct flights, you first need to take a domestic flight from Bali's DPS Airport to Labuan Bajo's LBJ Airport, then depart from Labuan Bajo port for the island hopping day tour. Komodo is famous not only as the sole habitat of Komodo dragons but also for having one of Asia's few pink beaches—these sights are truly eye-opening.

## **Trip Overview & Costs**

⚠️ **Specific times and locations may be adjusted slightly due to weather and sea conditions.**

This standard one-day tour covers six main attractions and costs approximately USD$97 (prices may vary due to exchange rate fluctuations and new government taxes; final pricing is subject to the travel agency's quote). The itinerary is as follows:

- **6:00** Hotel pickup and transfer to the harbor
- **8:00** Stop 1: Summit Padar Island
- **10:00** Stop 2: Enjoy the scenery at Pink Beach
- **11:20** Stop 3: Komodo dragon adventure (guided trek)

https://youtu.be/n1hpBG9cXa8

(Photo by Astrid Chang)

- **12:20** Lunch on board
- **13:30** Stop 4: Stroll and snorkel at Taka Makasar (Crescent Moon Bay)
- **14:30** Stop 5: Manta ray snorkeling (Manta Spot)
- **15:30** Stop 6: Stroll and snorkel at Kanawa Island

After the tour, you'll return to the harbor around 16:00-17:00. ASRI Labuan Bajo also offers two-day, one-night trips and itineraries that include sunset viewing—the bat sunset at Kalong Island is said to be spectacular.

## **Travel Experience: Dreamlike Scenery and Cultural Charm**

![晚霞的顏色一直在變化，讓我們驚呼連連（Astrid Chang提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_40f0480ac46b1ba00e62103f7ad6647b.jpg)

The sunset colors kept shifting, leaving us in awe (Provided by Astrid Chang)

The biggest highlight of this Komodo island-hopping trip was the breathtaking scenery. Compared to Bali, it's less crowded and more serene, with every beach feeling like a pristine paradise. The sunset we witnessed at the Ayana hotel was undoubtedly the most beautiful sight of the trip, as if we had stepped into a magical world.

**👍 Why We Recommend This Local Tour Operator:**

1. **Professional Service**: Arda, the owner of the local travel agency, treats guests with great courtesy and patience, especially Taiwanese visitors, offering meticulous service. We contacted him via Line, and he responded very quickly, with well-thought-out itinerary arrangements.
2. **Attentive and Considerate**: The boat crew and guides were very attentive to each guest's condition. On this trip, we were lucky to have a Chinese-speaking guide—there are very few Chinese-speaking guides locally, so it was a pleasant surprise.

In summary, this one-day island-hopping tour of Komodo National Park was an unforgettable experience. Beyond the stunning natural scenery, the professional service from the travel agency added greatly to the journey. If you also want to explore this relatively untouched paradise, consider their services!
