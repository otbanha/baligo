**Bottom line**: Komodo National Park lies east of Bali, with **no direct flights**. You'll need to fly from Bali's DPS Airport to Labuan Bajo's LBJ Airport (about a 1-hour flight), then take a boat from the local harbor for the island hopping day tour. If you can't gather enough people, consider joining a standard speedboat tour offered by travel agencies, which covers Pink Beach and close-up Komodo dragon observation.

## **How It Started: Meeting the Tour Agency**

This day tour was arranged through a local travel agency in Komodo. Since the three of us friends originally wanted to charter a private boat for a customized itinerary on Labuan Bajo Island but couldn't gather enough people, we decided to join their standard speedboat day tour. This journey allowed us to experience Indonesia's unique natural wonders and the warm hospitality of the locals, so we wanted to share our experience.

## **Getting to Komodo: How to Reach**

Komodo National Park is located east of Bali, about a one-hour flight away. Since there are no direct flights, you first take a domestic flight from Bali's DPS Airport to Labuan Bajo's LBJ Airport, then depart from Labuan Bajo harbor for the island hopping day tour. Komodo is famous not only as the sole habitat of Komodo dragons but also for having one of Asia's few pink beaches—all sights that are truly eye-opening.

## **Trip Overview & Costs**

⚠️ **Specific times and locations may be adjusted slightly due to weather and sea conditions.**

This standard one-day tour includes six main attractions and costs approximately USD$97 (prices may vary due to exchange rate fluctuations and new government taxes; final pricing is subject to the tour operator's quote). The itinerary is as follows:

- **6:00** Pick up from hotel and depart for the harbor
- **8:00** Stop 1: Summit Padar Island
- **10:00** Stop 2: Enjoy the views at Pink Beach
- **11:20** Stop 3: Komodo dragon adventure (hiking observation)

https://youtu.be/n1hpBG9cXa8

(Photo by Astrid Chang)

- **12:20** Lunch on board
- **13:30** Stop 4: Stroll and snorkel at Taka Makasar (Crescent Moon Bay)
- **14:30** Stop 5: Snorkel with manta rays (Manta Spot)
- **15:30** Stop 6: Stroll and snorkel at Kanawa Island

After the tour, you'll return to the harbor around 16:00-17:00. ASRI Labuan Bajo also offers two-day, one-night trips and itineraries that include sunset viewing—the bat sunset at Kalong Island is said to be spectacular.

## **Travel Diary: Dreamy Scenery & Local Charm**

![晚霞的顏色一直在變化，讓我們驚呼連連（Astrid Chang提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_40f0480ac46b1ba00e62103f7ad6647b.jpg)

The sunset colors kept shifting, leaving us in awe (Provided by Astrid Chang)

The highlight of this Komodo island-hopping trip was the breathtaking scenery. Compared to Bali, it's less crowded and more serene—every beach felt like a pristine paradise. The sunset we saw at the Ayana hotel was undoubtedly the most beautiful of the trip, as if we had stepped into a magical world.

**👍 Why the Local Tour Operator Recommends This:**

1. **Professional Service**: Arda, the owner of the local tour operator, is polite and patient with guests, especially those from Taiwan, offering meticulous service. We contacted him via Line, and he responded very quickly with well-organized itineraries.
2. **Attentive and Considerate**: The boat crew and guides were very attentive to each traveler's needs. On this trip, we were lucky to have a Chinese-speaking guide—such guides are rare locally, so it was a pleasant surprise.

In summary, this one-day island-hopping tour in Komodo National Park was an unforgettable experience. Beyond the stunning natural scenery, the professional service from the tour operator added greatly to the journey. If you want to explore this relatively untouched paradise, consider booking with them!
