## Electronic Visa on Arrival (eVoA): Apply Before Departure, Valid for 90 Days After Issuance

---

The Indonesian Electronic Visa on Arrival (eVoA) is a must-have document for most travelers heading to Indonesia, especially for foreigners entering for tourism purposes.

The eVoA must be applied for in advance on the [official Indonesian website](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/), and it is usually issued immediately. The visa is valid for 90 days from the issue date, but it’s crucial to note that you must complete both your entry and exit procedures within these 90 days—overstaying is not permitted.

It’s recommended to apply within one week before your departure to ensure processing time and avoid applying too early and missing the usage period.

## Electronic Customs Declaration (eCD): Fill Out Within Three Days Before Departure

---

## 電子報關（eCD）：出發前三天內填寫

The eCD (Electronic Customs Declaration) is the [official online customs declaration form](https://ecd.beacukai.go.id/) for Indonesia. All travelers, regardless of whether they have items to declare, are required to fill it out. This declaration form includes information on whether you are carrying foreign currency, plants, animals, alcohol, cigarettes, or other items that need to be declared.

The time restriction for filling out the form is very clear: it must be completed **within three days before departure**. If filled out earlier, the system will not allow you to select the date. Once completed, you will receive a QR Code, which must be presented to customs officers for scanning upon arrival.

It is recommended to fill out the form 2 to 3 days before your flight to avoid any issues that might delay your entry process.

## Health Declaration (SSHP): Fill Out Truthfully Within 3 Days Before Arrival

[SSHP (Surat Sektor Health Protocol)](https://sshp.kemkes.go.id/) is a health declaration established by Indonesia for public health purposes. It includes information on whether you have experienced symptoms (such as cough or fever) in the past 21 days, whether you have been in contact with confirmed cases, and your overall health status.

This declaration must be completed **within three days before arrival**, and information must be provided truthfully. While most airports no longer enforce temperature checks or health certificates, this form may be rechecked in case of changes in the pandemic situation or special circumstances.

It is recommended to save the completed SSHP as a PDF or take a screenshot for future reference.

## Tourist Levy: Pay Before Arrival, Valid for a Single Entry

Starting from 2024, all international travelers visiting Bali (limited to Bali only) will be required to pay a Tourist Levy. This fee is [a management charge imposed by the Indonesian government to enhance the local tourism environment](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/).

The fee is **150,000 Indonesian Rupiah (approximately USD$10)**, and upon payment, you will receive a QR Code that must be scanned upon entry. **This fee must be paid anew for each entry and is valid for single use only.**

It is recommended to complete this process one week before departure to avoid last-minute hassles at the airport that could disrupt your travel plans.

## Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

**Q1: Can I apply for eVoA upon arrival if I haven't done so beforehand?**

A: It is advisable to apply for the online visa in advance. While you can apply for a visa on arrival at the airport, the process is slower, involves longer queues, and there is no guarantee of successful issuance.

**Q2: Does the eCD Customs Declaration Form need to be printed?**

A: No need to print. After completing the form, you'll receive a QR Code - simply show it on your phone for scanning.

**Q3: Does the SSHP Health Declaration check body temperature or vaccine proof?**

A: Currently, temperature checks and vaccine proofs are not mandatory in most cases. However, health information must be filled in the form, so it's recommended to provide accurate details and keep the receipt.

**Q4: Can the tourism tax be paid on-site?**

A: There are payment counters at Bali International Airport, but online payment is faster and more convenient, avoiding queues and waiting times.

**Q5: Do I need to pay the tourism tax every time I enter Bali?**

A: Yes. The tourism tax is a fee required for each entry and does not have multiple-use validity.

## Reminders

Before traveling to Indonesia or Bali, it's recommended to prepare the following documents and confirm their completion:

- Approved eVoA electronic visa
- QR Code for the eCD electronic customs declaration
- Screenshot or PDF of the completed SSHP health declaration
- Proof of tourism tax payment and QR Code (only required for Bali entry)
- Return flight ticket
- Travel insurance (depending on airline or entry requirements)

With all documents ready, you can breeze through immigration and enjoy your tropical island getaway with peace of mind!
