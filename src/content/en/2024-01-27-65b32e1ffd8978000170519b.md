Maintaining good health while traveling is the most important topic! Bali, this fascinating land, is filled with incredible scenery and memorable experiences. However, as you explore this beautiful destination, remember to protect your health, as daily consumption of drinking water and food hygiene here require extra attention. If you feel unwell during your trip, the entire journey could be ruined!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3e6c20db282b94567bf42043a94cbc99.png)

## The Importance of Water Quality

Tap water in Bali is not safe to drink directly! To ensure your body functions well, do not overlook the quality of drinking water. In daily life, we often neglect the importance of water, but during travel, it is the foundation of your health.

Therefore, it is recommended to choose bottled water or professionally filtered water sources. This clean water is not just a thirst-quenching liquid but also a guardian of your health. It is the first step in safeguarding your health, allowing you to confidently embark on your exploration of this beautiful land.

## Beware of 'Bali Belly'

'Bali Belly' is a term that sounds somewhat frightening, referring to gastrointestinal issues you might encounter in Bali, which can further lead to diarrhea, vomiting, fever, and general weakness. These symptoms can ruin your fun and affect your travel experience.

## Be Careful with Ice

The "ice" mentioned in the video above, this seemingly harmless little thing, could potentially hide health risks! In Bali, the water source for ice is not always reliable. Therefore, in your drinks, choose to avoid ice or ensure it comes from a trustworthy source.

## Be Careful When Brushing Your Teeth Too!

Even when brushing your teeth, pay extra attention to the quality of water. Tap water in Bali may not be suitable for brushing teeth as it could contain bacteria or other contaminants. Therefore, use bottled or filtered water for brushing and rinsing.
