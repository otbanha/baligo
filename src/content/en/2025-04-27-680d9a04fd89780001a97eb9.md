But **can these loots be smoothly brought back to Taiwan?** Recently, group member Yang Mei asked this very practical question, along with sharing her impressive photo of shopping haul in Bali.

This article will help us understand Taiwan Customs' regulations together, avoiding accidental pitfalls. Shop happily when departing, and pass through customs worry-free when returning home!

***

## Items That Can Be Brought Back to Taiwan ✅

Judging from Yang Mei's photo, most products fall into the following categories, **which are allowed by Taiwan Customs**:

- ✅ **Cookies, potato chips, pineapple cakes** (like Kusuka, GuriBee)
- ✅ **Coconut chips**
- ✅ **Instant snack noodles** (Enak, Mie Sedaap, **pay attention to ingredients**)
- ✅ **Dried seafood products** (like JUHU dried squid)
- ✅ **Bottled sauces** (like chili sauce)
- ✅ **Toothpaste, skincare products, hair oils** (like Sensodyne toothpaste, Aloe Vera gel)
- ✅ **Nuts, candies, chocolates**

從Yang Mei的照片來看，大多數的商品屬於以下類別，**符合台灣海關允許攜帶的範圍**：

- ✅**餅乾、洋芋片、鳳梨酥**（如Kusuka、GuriBee）
- ✅**椰子脆片**（Coconut Chips）
- ✅**即食零食泡麵**（Enak、Mie Sedaap，**小心注意成分**）
- ✅**風乾海鮮類產品**（如JUHU魷魚乾）
- ✅**瓶裝醬料**（如辣椒醬）
- ✅**牙膏、護膚品、護髮油**（如Sensodyne牙膏、Aloe Vera凝膠）
- ✅**堅果類、糖果類、巧克力**

As long as the products are **unopened, factory-sealed, and clearly labeled**, and the quantity is for 'personal use', they can usually be brought into Taiwan without issues.

***

## Special Note: Pork Products 🚫

**Important! Taiwan has very strict regulations prohibiting food products containing pork**, including but not limited to:

- 🚫 Pork jerky, pork floss, pork crackers, fried pork skin
- 🚫 Foods labeled with Pork, Lard, or Pork Extract
- 🚫 Instant noodles or crackers with pork broth or meat particles

**If caught, the minimum fine is USD$6,450!** (and the second offense can go up to USD$32,250)

https://youtu.be/4EzO1rpSMic?si=YKEu2SMJVTzSzwjO

(**The last three pork-related items mentioned by Xiao Jie cannot be brought in!**)

So, for snacks like Enak or Mie Sedaap, remember to carefully check the ingredient labels. 👉 **If you see words like 'Pork/babi' (the Indonesian word for pork), it's best to finish them in Bali and not bring them back to Taiwan.**

A tip: When you return to your hotel, take out the instant noodle packaging and carefully inspect the ingredients. If in doubt, don't risk bringing it.

***

## Can I Bring Dried Squid? 🐙

Yang Mei also specifically asked: 'JUHU is processed and dried squid slices, can I bring them?'

✅ The answer is: Yes!

The reasons are:

- Processed squid slices are **dried seafood**, not meat products.
- The packaging is intact and the ingredients are clearly labeled.
- It is a **commercially processed product**, not fresh produce.

As long as the quantity is reasonable, you can go through the general channel when entering Taiwan, no special declaration is needed.

**Reminder**: If you bring a lot (more than 20 boxes), it might look like it's for commercial use and could be subject to inspection, so it's recommended to control the quantity.

***

## Tips for Bringing Bali Souvenirs Back to Taiwan ✈️

Here are a few tips to help you return to Taiwan with peace of mind:

1. **Group dry goods together for easier customs inspection.**
2. **Avoid bringing back any products containing meat at all costs.**
3. **When in doubt, declare it—better safe than sorry.**
