However, **can these treasures be smoothly brought back to Taiwan?** Recently, group member Yang Mei asked this very practical question, along with an impressive photo of her Bali shopping haul.

## Items Allowed to Bring Back to Taiwan ✅

Judging from Yang Mei's photo, most products fall into the following categories, **which comply with Taiwan Customs' allowance**:

***

- ✅**Cookies, potato chips, pineapple cakes** (like Kusuka, GuriBee)
- ✅**Coconut chips**
- ✅**Instant snacks and noodles** (Enak, Mie Sedaap, **pay attention to ingredients**)
- ✅**Dried seafood products** (like JUHU dried squid)
- ✅**Bottled sauces** (like chili sauce)
- ✅**Toothpaste, skincare products, hair oils** (like Sensodyne toothpaste, Aloe Vera gel)
- ✅**Nuts, candies, chocolates**

As long as the products are **factory-sealed, unopened, with clear ingredient labeling**, and in quantities for 'personal use', they can usually be brought into Taiwan without issues.

- ✅**餅乾、洋芋片、鳳梨酥**（如Kusuka、GuriBee）
- ✅**椰子脆片**（Coconut Chips）
- ✅**即食零食泡麵**（Enak、Mie Sedaap，**小心注意成分**）
- ✅**風乾海鮮類產品**（如JUHU魷魚乾）
- ✅**瓶裝醬料**（如辣椒醬）
- ✅**牙膏、護膚品、護髮油**（如Sensodyne牙膏、Aloe Vera凝膠）
- ✅**堅果類、糖果類、巧克力**

只要是**原封未拆、工廠密封包裝、成分標示清楚**的商品，且數量以「自用」為原則，通常都能順利入境台灣。

## Important Note: Pork Products 🚫

**Heads up! Taiwan has very strict regulations** prohibiting food products containing pork ingredients, including but not limited to:

- 🚫 Pork jerky, pork floss, pork crackers, fried pork skin
- 🚫 Foods labeled with Pork, Lard, Pork Extract
- 🚫 Instant noodles or crackers with pork broth or meat particles

**If caught, the minimum fine is USD$645!** (and the penalty increases to USD$3,226 for repeat offenses)

https://youtu.be/4EzO1rpSMic?si=YKEu2SMJVTzSzwjO

(**The last three pork-related items mentioned by Xiao Jie cannot be brought back!**)

So, for snacks like Enak or Mie Sedaap, remember to carefully check the ingredients. 👉 **If you see words like 'Pork/babi' (the Indonesian word for pork), it's best to finish them in Bali and not bring them back to Taiwan.**

A small tip: After returning to your hotel, take out the instant noodle packaging and carefully inspect the ingredients. If in doubt, don't risk bringing it.

***

## Can I Bring Dried Squid? 🐙

Yang Mei also asked: 'Can I bring JUHU, which is processed dried squid slices?'

✅ The answer is: Yes!

Here's why:

- Processed squid is classified as **dried seafood**, not a meat product.
- The packaging is intact with clear ingredient labeling.
- It's considered a **commercially processed product**, not fresh seafood.

As long as the quantity is reasonable, you can go through the regular customs channel when entering Taiwan without special declaration.

**Reminder**: If you're carrying a large quantity (more than 20 boxes) that appears to be for commercial use, you might be subject to inspection, so it's recommended to keep the quantity moderate.

***

## Tips for Bringing Bali Souvenirs Back to Taiwan ✈️

Here are some tips to make your return to Taiwan smoother:

1. **Group dried goods together for easier customs inspection.**
2. **Never risk bringing back any products containing meat ingredients.**
3. **When in doubt, declare it proactively for safety.**
