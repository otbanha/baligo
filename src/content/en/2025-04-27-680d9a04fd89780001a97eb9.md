However, can these spoils of shopping be smoothly brought back to Taiwan? Recently, fellow traveler Yang Mei asked this very practical question, along with sharing her impressive post-shopping haul photos from Bali.

---

This article will help us understand Taiwan Customs' regulations, avoid any pitfalls, shop happily when departing, and return home smoothly!

## Items That Can Be Brought Back to Taiwan ✅

Looking at Yang Mei's photos, most of the products fall into the following categories, which are allowed by Taiwan Customs:

- ✅ Cookies, potato chips, pineapple cakes (like Kusuka, GuriBee)
- ✅ Coconut chips
- ✅ Instant snack noodles (Enak, Mie Sedaap, be careful with ingredients)
- ✅ Dried seafood products (like JUHU dried squid)
- ✅ Bottled sauces (like chili sauce)
- ✅ Toothpaste, skincare products, hair oils (like Sensodyne toothpaste, Aloe Vera gel)
- ✅ Nuts, candies, chocolates

---

## Special Notice: Pork Products 🚫

**Here's the important part! Taiwan has very strict regulations prohibiting food products containing pork, including but not limited to:**

- 🚫 Pork jerky, pork floss, pork crackers, fried pork rinds
- 🚫 Foods labeled with Pork, Lard, or Pork Extract
- 🚫 Instant noodles or crackers with pork broth or meat particles

**If caught, the minimum fine is USD$6,450!** (And the penalty increases to USD$32,250 for a second offense)

(**The last three pork-related items mentioned by Xiao Jie cannot be brought back!**)

So, for snacks like Enak or Mie Sedaap, remember to carefully check the ingredients. 👉 **If you see words like 'Pork/babi' (the Indonesian word for pork), it's best to finish them in Bali and not bring them back to Taiwan.**

A small tip: After returning to your hotel, take out the instant noodle packaging and carefully inspect the ingredients. If in doubt, don't risk bringing it.

---

## Can dried squid be brought back? 🐙

Yang Mei also specifically asked: 'Can JUHU, which is processed and dried squid slices, be brought back?'

✅ The answer is: Yes!

The reasons are:

- Processed squid products are **dried seafood** and do not fall under meat products.
- Packaging is intact with clear ingredient labeling.
- They are considered **commercial processed goods**, not fresh products.

As long as the quantity is reasonable, you can pass through the general customs channel when entering Taiwan without needing to declare them.

**Reminder**: If you're carrying a large quantity (more than 20 boxes), which may appear to be for commercial use, you might be subject to inspection. Therefore, it's recommended to keep the quantity in check.

---

## Tips for Bringing Bali Souvenirs Back to Taiwan ✈️

Here are a few tips to make your return to Taiwan smoother:

1. **Focus on dried goods, as they are easier for customs to inspect.**
2. **Avoid bringing back any products containing meat ingredients at all costs.**
3. **When in doubt, declare the items proactively to stay safe.**
