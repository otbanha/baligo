## Major Overhaul of Indonesia's Visa Extension System

Effective May 29, 2025, the Indonesian Immigration Office introduces a new visa extension process integrating online registration and in-person verification, applicable across the country including Bali, impacting tourists, long-term residents, business, and socio-cultural visa holders.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_05dab2ad74b02830d37c34b349f59630.jpg)

---

## Overview of the New Process: Online + In-Person

### Step 1: Online Application

All visa extension applicants must first register an account on the official website [evisa.imigrasi.go.id](http://evisa.imigrasi.go.id) and select 'extend my visa'. The website offers an English interface and supports both mobile and desktop operations.

### Step 2: In-Person Verification at Immigration Office

After completing the online form, applicants will receive a notification within two business days to visit the nearest immigration office in person to submit documents and undergo biometric procedures (e.g., photo and fingerprinting).

---

## Special Cases and Fast-Track Services

Applicants who fall under the following categories may apply for the expedited process:

- Senior citizens
- Individuals with disabilities
- Pregnant and nursing women
- Emergency travelers

In these cases, the entire process can be completed in a single visit, with immigration officers assisting in filling out documents and completing fingerprint recognition.

---

## Why is the Indonesian Immigration Office changing the system?

The Indonesian government has noted a significant increase in illegal visa violations in early 2025, with 2,201 cases processed from January to April, a 36.71% rise compared to 1,610 cases during the same period in 2024. The new system aims to more effectively manage visa usage and reduce illegal activities.

---

## What documents should applicants prepare?

Although no additional documents are officially required, applicants may need to answer the following questions during the actual process:

- What is the purpose of your stay in Indonesia?
- Do you have a subsequent departure plan?
- What is your residential address in Indonesia?
- Do you have proof of financial means?

It is recommended to bring relevant documents such as travel itineraries, accommodation proof, and return flight tickets for reference.

## Visa Types and Fees

The most common tourist visa is:

**Visa on Arrival (VoA)**

- Can be applied for upon arrival at the airport
- Valid for 30 days
- Extension fee is IDR 500,000

**Electronic Visa on Arrival (eVoA)**

- Can be applied for online in advance
- Also applicable for extension under the new hybrid system
- Extension fee is IDR 500,000

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. Do I still need to visit the immigration office in person if I've already extended my visa online?

Yes, the new system requires a combination of online and in-person processing. All visa extenders must visit the immigration office in person.

### 2. What questions will the immigration officer ask me?

Officials may inquire about your purpose in Indonesia, duration of stay, and financial capability. It is advisable to bring basic supporting documents.

### 3. Can someone else handle this for me?

Currently, applicants must appear in person, especially for biometric data collection.

### 4. How many days do I have to complete the visa extension?

Starting from online registration, you typically need to complete the verification process at the immigration office within 2 business days.

### 5. What happens if I don't comply with the new regulations?

Failure to follow the regulations may result in fines, detention, or deportation.
