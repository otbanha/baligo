I've always been a scaredy-cat who wouldn't even ride roller coasters. Every time I saw photos of others high in the sky, I felt both envious and terrified. This time in Bali, I finally gathered the courage to challenge myself to my first paragliding experience! Honestly, my hands were shaking when I made the reservation, and all sorts of terrifying scenarios kept popping into my head: What if I suddenly fell? What if the wind was too strong? But looking back now, all those worries were completely unnecessary~

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cc96f660086124cb45fc31d80b083263.jpg)

Choosing Timbis Beach in Uluwatu was absolutely the right decision! Not only is it Bali's most famous paragliding spot, but the professional coaching team and comprehensive safety measures made me feel completely at ease for my first experience.

But preparing to challenge myself to my first paragliding experience still left me super nervous, especially since we had to take off from the edge of a cliff. However, after the actual experience, I realized—**it really wasn't as scary as I imagined**, and the scenery was so beautiful that it made me forget my fear!

## **🚁 Paragliding Experience Process**

1. **Hotel Pickup & Check-in**
2. - The driver arrived on time in the morning, and it took about 1 hour to reach Timbis Beach.
   - After arriving, I signed a safety agreement, and the coach explained the process (mainly in English, but gestures helped me understand).
3. **Gear Up & Takeoff Instructions**
4. - The coach helped me put on internationally certified safety harnesses and a helmet, including a GoPro for recording the entire flight.
5. **Takeoff! The Thrilling Moment at the Cliff Edge**
6. - **No running required!** The coach led me to the slope at the cliff edge, adjusted the parachute lines, and **with a gentle push when the wind came**, we were instantly airborne!
7. **10 Minutes in the Air, Enjoying the Breathtaking Ocean Views**
8. - The coach controlled the direction, and all I had to do was enjoy the scenery and take photos!
   - **Below me was the Indian Ocean**, with water so blue it seemed to glow, and I could see the entire cliff of Uluwatu—absolutely stunning!
   - The coach asked if I wanted **"a bit of excitement?"** and then suddenly spun us around, giving me the feeling of riding a roller coaster—so much fun!
9. **Landing on the Beach, Receiving Photos & Videos**
10. - The landing was smoother than I expected, and it was over as soon as my feet touched the ground.
    - The staff transferred the GoPro footage and photos to my phone—**make sure to check if they saved properly!**

## **⚠️ Tips & Suggestions**

✅ **Pre-Trip Preparation**

- **Attire**: Wear **sneakers**; avoid sandals or flip-flops.
- **Sun Protection**: The UV rays at high altitudes are intense—don't forget sunscreen!
- **Weight Limit**: Only those between 40-110 kg can fly; too light or too heavy won't work.
- **Age Limit**: 5-66 years old

❌ **What Not to Bring**

- **Backpacks or handbags** (they can affect balance; small items can go in pockets).
- **Selfie sticks or items that are easy to drop**.

💡 **Other Tips**

- **Weather plays a big role**: Paragliding relies on wind, so flights may be canceled if it's too calm or raining. **Leave some buffer time**.
- **If you're scared, let your instructor know**: They’re experienced and can fly more smoothly.

### **🌟 Review: Safer and More Fun Than Expected!**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_967be453cf97a01700bb55bec2eb3271.jpg)

I thought it would be terrifying, but it was **super stable**, like floating gently in the air. The instructor was great and even pointed out the scenery in English. **The most breathtaking part was seeing the Indian Ocean from above—that shade of blue is unforgettable!**

If you like thrills, ask your instructor for some spins; if you’re afraid of heights, a smooth flight is just as amazing. **Highly recommended for paragliding beginners**—it’s not as scary as you think, and the views are worth it!

**Overall, highly recommended!** I’d love to do it again next time I’m in Bali. 🪂💙
