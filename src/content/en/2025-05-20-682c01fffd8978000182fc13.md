## 🛬 Day 1: Arrival in Bali - Relaxation Mode Activated!

After arriving at Denpasar Airport, we took our pre-booked airport transfer to our hotel in Sanur on Bali's east coast. Sanur is a tranquil coastal town in southeastern Bali, known for its relaxed atmosphere and slow pace of life, making it a favorite among family travelers and long-term visitors. Sanur boasts a long beachfront promenade and sunrise beach, perfect for morning walks, cycling, and swimming. Unlike the bustling Kuta, Sanur exudes a relaxing elegance.

We stayed at Griya Santrian, a beachfront resort where you can step onto the sand just a few steps from the pool - perfect for water lovers and beach enthusiasts. The architecture retains traditional Balinese style, with serene garden landscapes. The hotel also offers SPA, yoga classes, and ocean-view dining, making it ideal for travelers wanting to enjoy the charm of "slow living in Sanur".

## 📸 Day 2: Klook's "East Bali Instagrammable Tour" 🌋🌴

Today's highlight is Klook's 【East Bali Instagrammable Day Tour】, our first Klook experience of this trip. The tour can be arranged for just two people, and the best part is that you can choose a Chinese-speaking guide - perfect for first-time visitors like us who want to gain deeper insights!

We departed promptly at 7:30 AM from our hotel with private transfers, which was super convenient. After a nap during the ride, we arrived at our first destination: the famous **Lempuyang Temple**, also known as the 'Gates of Heaven'. Taking the classic symmetrical mirror photos here was absolutely dreamy! 📷⛩️ Our Chinese guide also explained the sacred origins of this temple, allowing us to not just take photos but also understand the local religious culture.

Next, we visited **Swing Heaven**, which isn't just about swings - there are multiple Instagrammable spots to capture stunning photos 🌺. After taking photos, our guide helped us with angles - their photography skills were top-notch!

At noon, we arrived at the spiritual **Tirta Gangga Water Palace**, originally a royal bathing place. The pools are filled with koi fish, and you can buy fish food to feed them or walk across the stepping stones - it's quite fun! 🐟💦

Then we visited the majestic **Taman Ujung Water Palace**. This spot was less crowded with a relaxed atmosphere, perfect for quietly appreciating the blend of architecture and water features.

Our final stop was my favorite: **Virgin Beach**. This hidden gem is far from the crowds, with crystal clear water and white sand. There aren't many locals around, making it ideal for relaxing, sunbathing, and drinking coconut water 🌞🥥.

We returned around 5:30 PM. The whole day was packed with activities but never felt rushed. We didn't have to worry about directions or transportation - it was completely hassle-free!

## 🌿 Day 3: Half-Day Free Exploration in Ubud + Spa Time

After waking up naturally today, we checked out from Sanur and headed to Ubud Market for a leisurely stroll. We tried Ubud's famous crispy roast pork rice and then enjoyed a 90-minute Balinese aromatherapy massage at a spa.

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, surrounded by rice fields, forests, and traditional villages. It's an ideal place to seek nature, art, and spiritual experiences. In Ubud, you can join yoga classes, visit craft villages, immerse yourself in Balinese temple culture, and relax in numerous high-quality villas.

How can you visit Bali without experiencing a villa!? We stayed at Ayuterra Resort, a villa that made me never want to leave the pool🏞️. The room design is stylish and spacious, with an outdoor private infinity pool overlooking the Ayung River Gorge. Every moment felt like a postcard📸. Soaking in the pool under the stars at night and waking up to bird songs in the morning made it the most relaxing and romantic night of my Ubud trip❤️. Highly recommended for honeymooners or couples celebrating special occasions!

## 🌿 Day 4: Klook "Ubud Hotspots Private Day Tour" | Culture & Nature All-In-One, Unlock Ubud's Highlights in One Day!

On this day, we booked the 【Ubud Hotspots Private Day Tour】 on Klook. Again, we could choose a Chinese-speaking guide, but this time we were accompanied by an excellent English-speaking driver, **Yogi**. He was very patient, planned the most convenient and less crowded route for us, and even took care of all the parking fees at each spot beforehand—no extra charges needed. Really thoughtful!

We **departed from the hotel at 08:30** and first visited the stunning **Tegallalang Rice Terraces**, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The layered rice fields looked like green waves, and the scenery was breathtaking. Every photo taken here felt like a dreamy postcard! 🌾📷

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_55c19053b01f715dbb5fe2d0dc3a3e72.jpg)

Next, we experienced the classic **Ubud Swing**. The moment you swing out is both thrilling and fun, with dense tropical rainforest and valleys below—it felt like flying in a movie scene!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_81b50f6d3f97aacd54da321c458cacce.jpg)

Our third stop was the **Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary**. This is the natural habitat of Balinese long-tailed monkeys. Although the monkeys are lively and curious, don't worry—as long as you don't feed or provoke them, there's basically no danger. The guide also taught us how to maintain a safe distance from the monkeys, making it a unique and interesting experience 🐒🌳

For lunch, our driver Yogi took us to a restaurant with an amazing view where we enjoyed our meal overlooking rice fields — the atmosphere was perfect (thanks to his excellent planning!).

After lunch, we headed to **Tirta Empul Temple**, a spot I was really looking forward to. The holy spring water in the temple is said to have the power to purify the soul, and many local devotees and tourists immerse themselves in the pools to receive blessings. While I didn’t step into the water, sitting quietly and observing this scene of devotion brought me a sense of peace 🙏💧.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8b762a26998eb9109cd20d800e686b26.jpg)

Our final stop was the popular **Tegenungan Waterfall** in Ubud. The waterfall is truly spectacular, with roaring waters and a breathtaking sight. However, it felt a bit too crowded and commercialized for my taste, with many people playing in the water and taking photos, so I didn’t stay long. This part really depends on personal preference!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99403f7db64395a7fa81d169116fa952.jpg)

We returned to the hotel around **17:00**, wrapping up a nearly 10-hour journey that was both fulfilling and relaxing. A huge thanks to our driver **Yogi**, who was incredibly kind and thoughtful — he planned the itinerary perfectly and helped us avoid peak crowds. Highly recommended!

On the fifth day, we arrived in Nusa Dua.

If Sanur is a tranquil beach town and Ubud is a place of natural spirituality, then Nusa Dua is synonymous with luxury✨. Every hotel here feels like a mini-city, with excellent security, clean streets, and an upscale vibe — it’s the perfect destination for travelers looking to completely unwind and indulge.

We stayed at Mulia Resort, a well-known luxury hotel that offers exceptional value. The breakfast buffet here is hailed as 'Bali’s best' — the variety and quality of the dishes are truly impressive, making every morning feel like a gourmet festival. The resort itself is expansive, featuring multiple pools, a kids’ club, and a spa. The staff are highly trained, friendly, and courteous, creating a premium vacation experience that makes you think, 'I’ll definitely stay here again'🌟.

## 🔥 Day 6: Uluwatu Cliff Kecak Fire Dance & Sanur Secret Beach Private Tour｜A Journey That Shakes the Senses and Soul!

On this day, we opted for the 【Uluwatu Cliff Temple Kecak Fire Dance & Sanur Beach Private Tour】, with the option of a Chinese-speaking guide. Our driver and guide this time was the incredibly cool **Mr. Tawan**! He was both passionate and street-smart, deeply knowledgeable about the history and culture of each stop. Whether it was the meaning behind the Kecak dance, the architecture of Uluwatu Temple, or the formation of the beaches, he provided rich and insightful explanations. 🌟🌟🌟🌟🌟 Thanks to him, this journey felt complete!

### ⛱️ 09:00 Departure → 11:00 Padang Padang Beach

Our first stop was the hidden gem—**Padang Padang Beach**. Surrounded by lush cliffs, reaching this beach required navigating through a narrow rocky path, adding a sense of adventure! Though small, the beach boasts fine white sand and crystal-clear blue waters, making it a perfect spot for swimming and photography 🌊📸

### 🍴 12:30 Lunchtime

We chose our own lunch spot, and Tawan thoughtfully recommended a restaurant with ocean views. Enjoying grilled fish with coconut water while watching surfers ride the waves was pure bliss～

### 🏝️ 14:00 Blue Point Beach / Suluban Beach

Next, we headed to **Suluban Beach**, also known as "Blue Point Beach," a secret paradise for many surfers. This beach is surrounded by massive caves, and during low tide, you can walk deep into the caverns to explore small pools—it's mysterious and full of adventure! It truly feels like discovering a "secret hideout for adults" 🏄‍♂️

### 🛕 16:30 Uluwatu Temple

This ancient temple perched on a cliff is not only one of Bali's six sacred temples but also an excellent spot to admire the majestic views of the Indian Ocean. The monkeys here are mischievous and adorable, so be sure to keep your belongings secure (sunglasses and hats, especially!). With our guide, we learned about the history and religious significance of Uluwatu Temple, gaining a deeper understanding of the locals' reverence for nature and the divine.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f221761e8c64cd4b7a6f2dcc5e6f1859.jpg)

### 🔥 18:00 Kecak Fire Dance Performance (Highlight: **Skip-the-line entry!**)

This performance was truly one of the highlights of our trip! The Kecak Dance doesn't rely on any instruments; instead, dozens of shirtless men create a rhythmic "chak-chak-chak" chant, accompanied by dancers interacting with fire, bringing scenes from the Indian epic *Ramayana* to life! The show began at sunset and ended with blazing flames and cheers from the crowd—the atmosphere was absolutely electrifying 🔥🔥🔥

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3ae76de3e2f7cbfb46b702bcaa7a626e.jpg)

## Day Tour Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

### 1. Q: How many people are needed to form a private guided tour on Klook?

A: Yes! Most private guided tours on Klook require **a minimum of 2 people, no group merging, and even solo travelers can join**, so there's no need to worry about gathering enough people. Whether it's couples, friends, or families, everyone can easily enjoy a dedicated driver-guide with more flexible itineraries.

### 2. Q: Can I choose a Chinese-speaking guide?

A: **Some tours offer the option of a Chinese-speaking guide, so please pay special attention to the 'Language' field when booking.** If a Chinese-speaking guide is not available, English-speaking guides or local drivers are often provided. You can also contact the local driver via WhatsApp in advance to discuss your needs.

### 3. Q: Does the tour include tickets, lunch, or parking fees?

A: Each itinerary varies slightly. Some include entrance fees and lunch, but most will specify "meals at your own expense" or "entrance fees not included." For example, our Ubud day tour doesn't require additional parking fees at any attractions, but lunch is self-funded. We recommend reading the details carefully or consulting customer service before departure.

### 4. Q: Will the driver accompany us into the attractions?

A: Yes! Most tours are "guided private car services," where the driver will accompany you into attractions to provide explanations, take photos, assist with ticket purchases, etc. However, if you choose the English-speaking driver option, you may explore some attractions on your own.

### 5. Q: Can the itinerary be customized? Can we change the route midway?

A: Generally not, but some private tours offer more flexibility. You can inquire with customer service or the driver in advance about minor adjustments (e.g., replacing an attraction or extending the stay), but this depends on traffic and time constraints on the day.

### 6. Q: If we stay in different areas, such as Ubud or Jimbaran, can we still get pick-up service?

A: Yes, but pick-up areas and fees may vary. Most tours offer "free pick-up" in areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Sanur, and Ubud. Other areas may incur additional charges or require confirmation.

### 7. Q: Will the day tour feel rushed?

A: Klook's tours are designed to be "moderately paced," lasting about 8–10 hours. While many attractions are included, private tours aren't rushed, and the driver will adjust the route to avoid crowds. For example, we arrived early for the Kecak Fire Dance performance, avoiding long queues and enhancing the experience!

### 8. Q: Do we need to prepare cash?

A: It's highly recommended to carry some Indonesian Rupiah cash. While many attractions and restaurants accept credit cards, places like Ubud Traditional Market, fire dance tickets, and temple donations still require cash.

### 9. Q: Will there be traffic jams during the trip? Is there buffer time?

A: Bali's peak traffic hours can lead to congestion, but Klook drivers usually avoid these times or adjust the route sequence to bypass heavy traffic.

### 10. Q: How can I contact the driver or guide?

A: Klook will provide the driver's contact information before your trip. We recommend downloading WhatsApp, as it's the most commonly used communication method in Bali. You can confirm pickup times and guide languages in advance.
