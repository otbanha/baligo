## 🛬 Day 1: Arrival in Bali—Relaxation Mode Activated!

After landing at Denpasar Airport, we took our pre-booked airport transfer to our hotel in Sanur, located on Bali's east coast. Sanur is a tranquil seaside town in southeastern Bali, known for its relaxed atmosphere and slow pace of life, making it a favorite among families and long-term visitors. Sanur boasts a long beachfront promenade and a sunrise beach, perfect for morning walks, cycling, and swimming. Unlike the bustling Kuta, Sanur exudes a calming elegance.

We stayed at Griya Santrian, a beachfront resort just steps away from the sand—ideal for water lovers and beach enthusiasts. The resort maintains traditional Balinese architecture with serene garden landscapes. It also offers spa services, yoga classes, and a sea-view restaurant, making it perfect for travelers looking to embrace the 'slow life' charm of Sanur.

## 📸 Day 2: Klook's 'Eastern Bali Instagram Hotspots Tour' 🌋🌴

Today's highlight was Klook's 【Eastern Bali Instagram Hotspots Day Tour】, our first Klook activity of the trip. With just two people needed to form a group, and the option of a Chinese-speaking guide, it was perfect for first-time visitors like us who wanted to delve deeper into the local culture!

We departed promptly at 7:30 AM from our hotel, with private transport making everything super convenient. After a nap en route, we arrived at our first stop: the famous ****Lempuyang Temple****, also known as the 'Gates of Heaven'. Capturing the classic symmetrical mirror image here was absolutely dreamy! 📷⛩️ Our Chinese guide also explained the sacred origins of the temple, enriching our experience beyond just photography.

Next, we visited ****Swing Heaven****, which offers more than just swings—there are multiple Instagram-worthy spots for stunning photos 🌺. After snapping pics, our guide helped with angles, showcasing top-notch photography skills!

By midday, we arrived at the serene ****Tirta Gangga Water Palace****, once a royal bathing site. The ponds are filled with koi fish, and you can buy feed to interact with them or stroll across the stepping stones—it's quite delightful! 🐟💦

Then, we explored the majestic ****Taman Ujung Water Palace****. Less crowded and with a relaxed atmosphere, it's ideal for quietly appreciating the harmonious blend of architecture and water.

Our final stop was the ****Virgin Beach****, a hidden gem away from the hustle and bustle. With crystal-clear waters and white sands, and few locals around, it's perfect for unwinding, sunbathing, and sipping coconut water 🌞🥥.

## 🌿 Day 3: Ubud Half-Day Free Exploration & Spa Time

After waking up naturally today, we checked out from Sanur and headed to Ubud Market for a leisurely stroll. We tried Ubud's famous crispy roast pork rice and then enjoyed a 90-minute Balinese aromatherapy massage at a spa.

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, surrounded by rice fields, forests, and traditional villages. It's the ideal place to immerse yourself in nature, art, and spiritual experiences. In Ubud, you can join yoga classes, visit craft villages, experience Balinese temple culture, and relax in numerous high-quality villas.

How can you visit Bali without indulging in a villa!? We stayed at Ayuterra Resort, a villa where I couldn't tear myself away from the pool🏞️. The room was stylish and spacious, with a private infinity pool overlooking the Ayung River gorge – every moment felt like a postcard📸. Spending the evening stargazing in the pool and waking up to birdsong made it the most relaxing and romantic night of my Ubud trip❤️. Highly recommended for honeymooners or couples celebrating special occasions!

## 🌿 Day 4: Klook's "Ubud Highlights Private Day Tour" – Culture & Nature All in One, Unlocking Ubud's Essence in a Day!

On this day, we booked Klook's 【Ubud Highlights Private Day Tour】, which also offers Chinese-speaking guides. However, we were accompanied by an amazing English-speaking driver, ****Yogi****, who guided us through the entire itinerary. He was incredibly patient, planned the most efficient route to avoid crowds, and even handled all the parking fees in advance – no extra charges needed. Such thoughtful service!

We departed from the hotel at ****08:30**** and our first stop was the stunning UNESCO World Heritage-listed ****Tegallalang Rice Terraces****. The layered rice fields resembled green waves, creating a breathtakingly picturesque scene – every angle felt like a postcard! 🌾📷

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_55c19053b01f715dbb5fe2d0dc3a3e72.jpg)

Next, we experienced Ubud's classic ****jungle swing****. The moment you swing out, it's both thrilling and exhilarating, with lush tropical rainforests and valleys below – it felt like flying in a movie scene!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_81b50f6d3f97aacd54da321c458cacce.jpg)

Our third stop was the **Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary in Ubud**, a natural habitat for Bali's long-tailed macaques. While the monkeys are lively and curious, there's no need to worry as long as you don't feed or provoke them. Our guide taught us how to maintain a safe distance from the monkeys, making it a unique and fascinating experience 🐒🌳.

For lunch, our driver Yogi picked a restaurant with an incredible view overlooking rice fields. The atmosphere was perfect (thanks to his excellent planning!).

After lunch, we visited **Tirta Empul Temple**, a spot I was particularly looking forward to. The temple's holy spring is believed to have purifying energies, and many local devotees and tourists immerse themselves in the pools for spiritual cleansing. While I didn't enter the water, sitting quietly and observing this scene of devotion brought me a sense of inner peace 🙏💧.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8b762a26998eb9109cd20d800e686b26.jpg)

Our final stop was the popular **Tegenungan Waterfall** in Ubud. The waterfall is truly spectacular, with its roaring waters creating a breathtaking sight. However, it was quite crowded with tourists swimming and taking photos, which made it feel a bit commercialized. I didn't stay long, but it's worth visiting depending on your preferences.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99403f7db64395a7fa81d169116fa952.jpg)

We returned to the hotel around **17:00**, concluding a nearly 10-hour journey that was both fulfilling and relaxing. A special shoutout to our driver **Yogi**, who was incredibly kind and thoughtful. He expertly planned our itinerary and helped us avoid peak crowds—highly recommended!

On this day, we opted for the 【Uluwatu Cliff Temple Kecak Fire Dance & Suluban Beach Private Tour】, which offers Chinese-speaking guides. ### ⛱️ 09:00 Departure → 11:00 Padang Padang Beach

Our first stop was the hidden gem—****Padang Padang Beach****. Surrounded by lush cliffs, reaching this beach requires navigating through a narrow rocky path, adding a sense of adventure! The beach is small but boasts fine white sand and crystal-clear blue waters, making it a perfect spot for swimming and photography 🌊📸.

### 🍴 12:30 Lunchtime

We chose our own lunch spot, and Tawan kindly recommended a restaurant with a sea view. Enjoying grilled fish with coconut water while watching surfers ride the waves was the epitome of chill～

### 🏝️ 14:00 Blue Point Beach (Suluban Beach)

Next, we headed to **Suluban Beach**, also known as "Blue Point Beach," a secret paradise for many surfers. This beach is surrounded by massive rock caves, and during low tide, you can walk deep into the caves to explore small pools—it feels mysterious and adventurous! It’s like discovering a "secret hideout for adults" 🏄‍♂️

### 🛕 16:30 Uluwatu Temple

This ancient temple perched on a cliff is not only one of Bali’s six major temples but also offers a stunning view of the Indian Ocean. The monkeys here are playful and cute, so keep your belongings secure (sunglasses and hats, especially!). With our guide, we learned about the history and religious significance of Uluwatu Temple, gaining a deeper understanding of the locals’ reverence for nature and the divine.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f221761e8c64cd4b7a6f2dcc5e6f1859.jpg)

### 🔥 18:00 Kecak Dance Performance (Highlight: **Skip the Line!**)

This performance was truly one of the highlights of our trip! The Kecak Dance doesn’t rely on any instruments—instead, dozens of shirtless men chant "chak-chak-chak" to create the rhythm. Combined with the dancers’ fire performances and interactive scenes, it brings the classic story of the Indian epic *Ramayana* to life! The show began at sunset and ended with sparks flying and the crowd cheering—it was absolutely mesmerizing 🔥🔥🔥

## Day Tour FAQ

### 1. Q: How many people are needed to form a group for private guided tours on Klook?

A: Yes! Most private guided tours on Klook require ****only 2 people to form a group, no merging with other groups, and even solo travelers can join****, so no need to worry about gathering enough people. Whether it's couples, friends, or families, everyone can easily enjoy a dedicated driver-guide, making the itinerary more flexible.

### 2. Q: Can I choose a Chinese-speaking guide?

A: ****Some tours offer the option of a Chinese-speaking guide, so please pay special attention to the 'Language' field when booking.**** If a Chinese-speaking guide is not available, most tours come with an English-speaking guide or a local driver. You can also contact the local driver via WhatsApp in advance to discuss your needs.

### 3. Q: Are tickets, lunch, or parking fees included in the tour?

A: Each tour varies slightly. Some include tickets and lunch, but most specify "meals at your own expense" or "tickets not included." For example, our Ubud day tour doesn't require additional parking fees at any attractions, but lunch is self-paid. We recommend reading the details carefully or contacting customer service before departure.

### 4. Q: Will the driver accompany us into the attractions?

A: Yes! Most tours are "guided private car services," where the driver will accompany you into the attractions, provide explanations, take photos, and assist with ticket purchases. However, if you choose the English-speaking driver option, you may explore some attractions on your own.

### 5. Q: Can the tour be customized? Can we change the route midway?

A: Generally not, but some private tours offer more flexibility. You can inquire with customer service or the driver in advance about minor adjustments (e.g., replacing an attraction or extending the stay), but this depends on traffic and time constraints.

### 6. Q: If we stay in different areas, such as Ubud or Jimbaran, can we still get pick-up service?

A: Yes, but pick-up areas and fees may vary. Most tours offer "free pick-up" in areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Sanur, and Ubud. Other areas may require additional fees or confirmation of availability.

### 7. Q: Will the day tour feel rushed?

A: Klook tours are designed to be "moderately paced," lasting about 8–10 hours. While there are many attractions scheduled, private tours aren't rushed, and the driver will adjust the route to avoid crowds. For example, we arrived early before the Kecak Fire Dance performance, skipping the queue and greatly enhancing the experience!

### 8. Q: Do I need to bring cash?

A: It's highly recommended to carry some Indonesian Rupiah in cash. While many attractions and restaurants accept cards, places like Ubud Traditional Market, fire dance tickets, and temple donations typically require cash.

### 9. Q: Will there be traffic jams? Is there buffer time?

A: Traffic peaks in Bali can lead to congestion, but Klook drivers usually avoid peak hours or adjust the route sequence to bypass heavy traffic.

### 10. Q: How can I contact the driver or guide?

A: Klook will provide the driver's contact information before your trip. We recommend downloading WhatsApp, as it's the most commonly used communication method in Bali. You can confirm pickup times and guide languages in advance.

### 11. Q: Are there more day tours to choose from?
