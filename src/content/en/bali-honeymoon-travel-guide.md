For our honeymoon trip to Bali, I referred to many experiences shared by fellow travelers. Now, I’ve compiled a few noteworthy points from this journey, hoping it will be helpful for those planning to visit.

Trip Dates: May 19 - 23, 2024

## Visa/Tourism Tax

First and foremost, the most important thing is the visa. We applied for the electronic visa about three weeks in advance, following instructions from fellow travelers and YouTube tutorials, step by step filling out the form. We received the electronic visa immediately after submission. Remember to print it out, as it will be checked at customs. Don’t take chances—make sure to pay the tourism tax online as well. Upon arrival, allow about an hour from immigration to baggage claim to be safe. After collecting your luggage, you’ll need to scan a pre-filled electronic customs declaration QR code to proceed.

## Currency Exchange

Due to our itinerary, we exchanged currency directly at the airport (USD to IDR). While the exchange rate isn’t as favorable as outside, we only exchanged a small amount, so it wasn’t a big concern. The money we exchanged was mainly used for tips and paying drivers in cash. Wherever possible, we used credit cards (remember to choose the local currency rate), such as when getting spa treatments or dining at beachside restaurants. We also asked in advance if card payments were accepted, which made things convenient by not having to carry too much cash and avoiding potential issues with unfamiliar currency values.

## Transportation

Navigating Bali's roads can be tricky, so it's advisable to hire a driver or arrange for private transportation. Traffic congestion is mainly concentrated near the airport, while other areas are relatively better. I'd like to share our experience with a local driver named Budi, whom we met on our first day through a KKday airport transfer booking. We found him to be excellent, and for the subsequent days' short-distance transfers, we contacted him directly via WhatsApp in advance.

{{block:包車}}

Budi is friendly and trustworthy. During the drive, feel free to ask him anything you're curious about Bali. Although his English has a local accent, it's generally understandable, and communication was pleasant. We chatted in Chinese among ourselves, and Budi remained quiet, focusing on driving, which made us feel very comfortable. After a positive airport transfer experience, we chose him for our other days' transportation as we had already built trust. Even when we inquired late at night (early morning), he always responded quickly and arrived earlier than the agreed time. Budi's schedule is quite packed, but he mentioned he would handle transfers as long as the destinations aren't too remote.

**Dinner**: For two evenings, we randomly chose restaurants at Jimbaran and Seminyak beaches for dinner. ## Tipping

After researching online, we found mixed opinions about tipping culture in Indonesia. Our experience was that sometimes staff seemed surprised and even reluctant to accept tips. They would only take them when we expressed our gratitude for their service. Generally, we gave IDR 10,000 for rides and luggage handling, and an additional IDR 10,000 in cash to service staff when settling restaurant bills. At hotels, helpful staff who took many photos for us declined tips. Personally, I believe tipping isn't mandatory but is a polite gesture. If you do tip, try to avoid coins as they're considered less polite. (Editor's note: The largest Indonesian coin is IDR 1,000, worth about USD$0.06)

## SAVA SPA at Indigo

![圖左：SAVA Spa / 圖右：SPA 結束小小休息區。Angel Wu提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7e6548e1f0331b60456a40ecb933e7c8.jpg)

Left: SAVA Spa / Right: Small relaxation area after SPA. Photo provided by Angel Wu

At Indigo Hotel, we chose the SAVA SPA service. The elegant environment and complete facilities were impressive, especially the 90-minute hot stone massage we experienced.

When starting the hot stone massage, the therapist will continuously check if the temperature of the stones is comfortable for you, ensuring it doesn't feel too hot, which is very considerate. Throughout the process, the therapist's skilled techniques allowed us to fully relax, experiencing deep relief and relaxation.

SAVA SPA only has six double massage rooms, so it's essential to book in advance to enjoy their premium services. The spa services here are provided by an award-winning professional team, with each therapist rigorously trained to ensure every guest receives the best experience.

In addition to hot stone massage, SAVA SPA offers a variety of other spa treatments, such as aromatherapy massage, deep tissue massage, and herbal therapies, each designed to meet different customer needs. Whether you're looking to relieve stress, ease muscle tension, or simply relax, SAVA SPA has the perfect option for you.

Overall, SAVA SPA is an excellent place for relaxation. The elegant environment, thoughtful service, and professional techniques made our time here very enjoyable. We recommend booking in advance to ensure you can experience their high-quality spa services.
