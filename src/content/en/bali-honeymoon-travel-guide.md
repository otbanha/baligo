For our honeymoon trip to Bali, I referred to many experiences shared by fellow travelers. Now, I’ve compiled a few noteworthy points from this journey that I hope will be helpful for those planning to visit.

Travel Dates: May 19 - May 23, 2024

## Visa/Tourism Tax

First and foremost, the most important thing is the visa. We applied for the electronic visa about three weeks in advance, following instructions from fellow travelers and YouTube tutorials, and filled out the form step by step. We received the electronic visa immediately after submission. Remember to print it out, as it will be checked at customs. Don’t take chances—the tourism tax must also be paid online. Upon arrival, it’s best to allow about an hour from immigration to baggage claim. After collecting your luggage, you’ll need to scan a pre-filled electronic customs declaration QR code to proceed.

## Currency Exchange

Due to our itinerary, we exchanged currency directly at the airport (USD to Indonesian Rupiah). While the exchange rate isn’t as good as outside, we only exchanged a small amount, so it wasn’t a big concern. The money we exchanged was mainly used for tips and paying drivers in cash. For places that accept card payments, we used cards (remember to choose the local currency option). For example, when going for spa treatments or dining at beachside restaurants, we asked in advance if card payments were accepted. This made it convenient for us to avoid carrying too much cash and eliminated potential issues due to unfamiliarity with the currency.

## Transportation

Bali's roads can be complex, so it's recommended to hire a car or arrange private transportation based on your itinerary. Traffic congestion is mainly concentrated near the airport, while other areas are relatively better. I'd like to share about a local driver named Budi, whom we met on our first day through a KKday airport transfer booking. We found him to be excellent, and for the next few days' short-distance transfers, we directly contacted him via WhatsApp in advance.

{{block:包車}}

Budi is friendly and trustworthy. During the drive, you can ask him anything you're curious about regarding Bali. Although his English has a local accent, it's generally understandable, and communication is pleasant. When we chatted in Chinese among ourselves, Budi wouldn't interrupt, quietly driving instead, which made us feel very comfortable. After a good airport transfer experience, we chose him for our other days' transportation as we had already established trust. Even when inquiring very late (early morning), he always responded quickly and arrived earlier than the agreed time. Budi's schedule is quite packed, and he said he would provide transfers as long as the location isn't too remote.

## Tipping

After researching online, we found mixed opinions about tipping culture in Indonesia. Our experience was that sometimes when we offered tips, the recipients were surprised and somewhat hesitant to accept. They would only take it when we expressed our gratitude for their service. Generally, we gave IDR 10,000 for car rides and luggage handling; we also gave an extra IDR 10,000 in cash to service staff when settling meal bills. At hotels, helpful staff who took many photos for us also indicated that tipping was unnecessary. Personally, I feel tipping is not mandatory but a gesture of courtesy. If you decide to tip, try to avoid coins as it's considered less polite. (Editor's note: The largest coin denomination in Indonesia is IDR 1,000, approximately USD$0.06)

## SAVA SPA at Indigo

![圖左：SAVA Spa / 圖右：SPA 結束小小休息區。Angel Wu提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7e6548e1f0331b60456a40ecb933e7c8.jpg)

Left: SAVA Spa / Right: Small rest area after SPA. Provided by Angel Wu

At Indigo Hotel, we chose the services of SAVA SPA. The environment is elegant, and the facilities are excellent, especially the 90-minute hot stone massage we experienced, which was truly memorable.

When starting the hot stone massage, the therapist continuously checks if the stone temperature is comfortable, ensuring it doesn't feel too hot - a very thoughtful touch. Throughout the process, the therapist's skilled technique allowed us to completely relax, experiencing deep relief and relaxation.

SAVA SPA only has six double massage rooms, so advance booking is essential to enjoy their premium services. The spa treatments are provided by an award-winning professional team, with each therapist undergoing rigorous training to ensure every guest receives the best experience.

In addition to hot stone massage, SAVA SPA offers various other spa treatments such as aromatherapy massage, deep tissue massage, and herbal therapies, each catering to different customer needs. Whether you want to relieve stress, ease muscle tension, or simply relax your mind and body, SAVA SPA provides the perfect choice.

Overall, SAVA SPA is an excellent place for complete relaxation. The elegant environment, thoughtful service, and professional techniques made our time here truly enjoyable. We recommend booking in advance to ensure you can experience their premium spa services.
