Travel Dates: May 19 - 23, 2024

## Visa/Tourism Tax

First and foremost, the most important thing is the visa. We applied for the electronic visa about three weeks in advance, following instructions from fellow travelers and YouTube tutorials, step by step filling out the form. We received the electronic visa immediately after submission. Remember to print it out, as it will be checked at customs. Don’t take chances—make sure to pay the tourism tax online as well. Upon arrival, it’s advisable to allocate about an hour from immigration to baggage claim. After collecting your luggage, you’ll need to scan a pre-filled electronic customs declaration QR code to proceed.

## Currency Exchange

Due to our itinerary, we exchanged currency directly at the airport (USD to IDR). Although the exchange rate isn’t as favorable as outside, we only exchanged a small amount, so it wasn’t a big concern. The money we exchanged was mainly used for tips and paying drivers in cash. For places that accept card payments, we opted to pay by card (remember to choose the local currency rate). For example, when going for a spa or dining at beachside restaurants, we asked in advance if card payments were accepted. This way, we avoided carrying too much cash and the potential hassle of unfamiliar currency values.

## Transportation

Bali's roads can be quite complex, so we recommend hiring a car or arranging private transportation based on your itinerary. Traffic congestion is mainly concentrated near the airport, while other areas are relatively better. Here, we'd like to share our experience with a local driver named Budi, whom we met on our first day through a KKday airport transfer booking. We found him to be excellent, and for the next few days' short-distance transfers, we contacted him directly via WhatsApp in advance.

{{block:包車}}

Budi is friendly and trustworthy. During the drive, feel free to ask him anything you're curious about Bali. Although his English has a local accent, it's generally understandable, and communication was pleasant. We chatted in Chinese among ourselves, and Budi remained quiet, focusing on driving, which made us feel very comfortable. After a good airport transfer experience, we chose him for our other days' transportation as we had already built trust. Even when we inquired late at night (early morning), he always responded quickly and arrived earlier than the agreed time. Budi's schedule is quite packed, and he mentioned he would provide transfers as long as the locations weren't too remote.

**Dinner**: For two evenings, we randomly chose restaurants at Jimbaran and Seminyak Beach for dinner. In Indonesia, besides the listed price, there’s usually an additional tax of up to 21% (11% government tax + 5%～10% service charge). Some restaurants include taxes in their prices, while others don’t, so it’s best to confirm with the establishment before dining. At a beach restaurant in Jimbaran, we opted for a basic meal package that included a bucket of rice, a vegetable dish, shrimp, fish, shellfish, satay, a side dish, and two beers, along with various sauces. This package cost around USD$23, which was very reasonable for two people.

## Tipping

After checking online, we found mixed opinions about tipping culture in Indonesia. Our experience was that sometimes when we offered tips, the recipients were surprised and hesitant to accept. They would only take it when we expressed our gratitude for their service. Generally, we gave IDR 10,000 for car rides and luggage handling, and an additional IDR 10,000 in cash to service staff when dining out. At the hotel, helpful staff took many photos for us and also indicated that tipping was unnecessary. Personally, I feel that tipping is not mandatory but is a gesture of courtesy. If you do tip, try to avoid coins as it is considered less polite. (Editor's Note: The largest coin denomination in Indonesia is IDR 1,000, approximately USD$0.06)

## SAVA SPA at Indigo

![圖左：SAVA Spa / 圖右：SPA 結束小小休息區。Angel Wu提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7e6548e1f0331b60456a40ecb933e7c8.jpg)

Left: SAVA Spa / Right: Small rest area after SPA. Provided by Angel Wu.

At the Indigo Hotel, we chose the services of SAVA SPA. The environment is elegant and the facilities are excellent, especially the 90-minute hot stone massage we experienced, which was truly memorable.

When starting the hot stone massage, the therapist will continuously check if the temperature of the stones is comfortable for you, ensuring it doesn't feel too hot, which is very considerate. Throughout the process, the therapist's skilled techniques allowed us to fully relax, experiencing deep relief and relaxation.

SAVA SPA has only six double massage rooms, so it's essential to make a reservation in advance to enjoy their premium services. The spa services here are provided by an award-winning professional team, with each therapist rigorously trained to ensure every guest receives the best experience.

In addition to hot stone massage, SAVA SPA offers a variety of other spa treatments, such as aromatherapy massage, deep tissue massage, and herbal therapies, each catering to different customer needs. Whether you're looking to relieve stress, ease muscle tension, or simply relax your mind and body, SAVA SPA provides the perfect option.

Overall, SAVA SPA is an excellent place for complete relaxation. The elegant environment, thoughtful service, and professional techniques made our time here very enjoyable. It's highly recommended to book in advance to ensure you can experience their high-quality spa services.
