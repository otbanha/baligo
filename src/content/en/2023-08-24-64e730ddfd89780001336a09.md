**When spending in Indonesia/Bali, you often see a 'K' after numbers. What does it mean?**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9cd0124f7272cfa32cf8ca9be2d1e01e.jpg)

Bali, this picturesque Indonesian gem, attracts visitors from around the world. On this beautiful island, in addition to stunning natural landscapes and cultural heritage, tourists will notice a unique spending habit—the frequent use of 'K' after numerical amounts. This article will explain the meaning behind this practice and highlight the importance of understanding this price format when spending in Bali.

### **Understanding the Format**

The 'K' stands for 'thousand,' representing a thousand in monetary terms. In daily spending in Bali, when you see a 'K' after a price, it means the amount has been abbreviated to indicate thousands. This formatting helps simplify large numbers, making prices easier to read and understand. For example, 200K represents 200,000 Indonesian Rupiah, and 80K means 80,000 Indonesian Rupiah.

Understanding this format is crucial in practical spending. It will help you more accurately assess and manage your expenses, avoiding misunderstandings due to unfamiliar price formats.

### **What is Spending Like in Indonesia/Bali?**

In Indonesia or Bali, there are many common expenses. Here are some examples:

1. Accommodation: On average, mid-range hotels cost around IDR 400K-800K per night, while luxury resorts can be IDR 1000K or more.

3. Transportation: Short taxi or motorcycle rides typically range from IDR 20K-100K, while full-day car rentals can cost between IDR 600K-800K.

### **Managing Expenses**

To better manage your expenses in Bali, here are some tips:

1. Use currency conversion apps: With currency conversion, you can easily translate prices into your local currency to better assess costs. For TWD conversions, a simple trick is to multiply the number before "K" by 2 to get an approximate TWD amount. For example, 200K is roughly TWD 400; 35K is about TWD 70. Give it a try!

2. Carry cash: Some places may not accept credit cards or may charge additional fees. Having local currency on hand helps you navigate these situations.

When traveling in Bali, understanding the 'K' price format is crucial for better managing your expenses, budgeting effectively, and avoiding misunderstandings. This simplified numerical notation makes amounts easier to comprehend, helping you enjoy your time on this beautiful island while maintaining financial control. Whether you're savoring delicious local cuisine or immersing yourself in stunning beach views, you'll be able to confidently choose expenditures that suit your needs.
