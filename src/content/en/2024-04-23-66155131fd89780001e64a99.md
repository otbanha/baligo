Inspired by the community, I'd like to give back by sharing my experience of traveling solo to Bali for 8 days in March. Since there's limited information online about women traveling alone in Bali, I'm here to share my itinerary and insights from this solo trip.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6152213cb48cec104b8463302a71617d.jpg)

In Bali, it's quite common to see foreign women traveling solo. Based on my experience, as long as you maintain basic safety precautions—like not getting into cars with strangers and avoiding eye contact or conversation with individuals who may harass women on the streets—most locals are genuinely warm, friendly, and safe to be around.

First, let me share my 8-day solo travel itinerary in Bali:

## **Saturday, March 17**

**09:15-14:45** Arrive in Bali by flight. I had pre-booked airport transfer service through Klook, and the driver waited patiently at the airport for up to 2 hours. After getting in the car, you can ask the driver to take you to a money changer first. I went directly to BMC Money Changer to exchange currency. Then we headed to tonight's accommodation at [The Eight](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23144116) resort.

> Further Reading: [Indonesia/Bali Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)

On Nusa Penida, the only option is to hire a private car. I booked my ferry tickets and private car package through Klook, and overall, I was very satisfied. The car rental company contacted me in advance to confirm the itinerary and helped facilitate communication with the driver. Upon disembarking, the driver met me and even helped carry my luggage. Throughout the journey, he was very attentive as a guide. When I wanted to climb down to Kelingking Beach, he followed me all the way down, for which I was truly grateful. At each stop, he would take photos and report back to the company, which made me, traveling solo as a woman, feel much more at ease. I’m really thankful for this driver, who helped carry my luggage up and down, accompanied me for photos, and was very responsible throughout the trip.

For transportation, aside from pre-booked private transfers for airport pickups and trips to Nusa Penida Island, we primarily used Grab and Gojek for rides. It was extremely convenient, and most drivers were quiet, allowing us to relax and rest during the journey.

{{block:包車}}

## **Summary of Experience**

Overall, I found Bali to be an ideal destination for solo travel photography. The spacious attractions allow for comfortable tripod setup and shooting. Except for airport transfers and Nusa Penida car hires, nearly all my stunning photos were taken using a tripod and remote shutter release. Even in situations where tripods couldn't be used - like uneven terrain or waterfall areas - locals often offer reasonably priced photography services, which is incredibly thoughtful.

The natural scenery in Bali is absolutely stunning! This solo trip gave me unforgettable experiences and allowed me to witness the warm hospitality of the locals. Traveling alone isn't scary at all - in fact, it enabled me to fully immerse myself in nature and exotic culture. If I have the chance again, I'd love to revisit this dreamlike island! Looking forward to sharing more travel stories with everyone.
