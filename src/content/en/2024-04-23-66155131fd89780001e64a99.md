I want to give back to the community by sharing my experience. In March, I traveled solo to Bali for 8 days. Since there's not much information online about women traveling alone in Bali, I decided to share my itinerary and insights with everyone.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6152213cb48cec104b8463302a71617d.jpg)

Actually, in Bali, you can see many foreign women traveling alone. Based on my experience, as long as you maintain some basic precautions, such as not getting into strangers' cars and avoiding eye contact or conversation with people who harass women on the street, most locals are warm, friendly, and safe.

Let me share my 8-day solo trip itinerary in Bali:

## **Saturday, March 17**

**09:15-14:45** Arrived in Bali by plane. I pre-booked airport transfer service on Klook, and the driver waited patiently at the airport for 2 hours. After getting in the car, I asked the driver to take me to a money changer first, so I went directly to BMC Money Changer. Then, I headed to [The Eight](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23144116) resort where I stayed for the night.

> Further reading: [Indonesia/Bali Travel Money Exchange Guide](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)

I went to a nearby supermarket to buy mosquito repellent, adapters, sunglasses, and summer swimwear. I bought a summer swimsuit at [niconico swimwear](https://niconicoswimwear.com/). The staff there were friendly and didn't push for sales, and they had a wide variety of styles.

## **Sunday, March 18**

10:30 AM - 4:30 PM: One Eighty. This place offers abundant facilities, stunning scenery, and high-quality dining. We recommend getting the VIP pass for 6 hours.

> Further reading: [Exploring Oneeighty Day Club: Bali's Cliffside Pool with Breathtaking Views](/blog/2023-12-07-6571846ffd89780001fe97ee/)

**17:30** Arrived at The Blue Point Beach. Be mindful of the tides before heading out. On this day, high tide was at 2 PM, and by 5 PM, the water level was still high, making it difficult to take photos.

> Further reading: [Exploring Bali's Hidden Gems: 6 Secret Beaches in Bali & How to Check Tides](/blog/2023-08-22-64e4c0c9fd897800014e31bf/)

**18:00-19:30** Moved to La Terrazza Uluwatu for dinner and to enjoy the view. The restaurant is situated in a prime location, offering panoramic views of the sunset and the silhouette of Uluwatu Temple.

## **Monday, March 19**

**10:30** Visited KIOA Bali, a newly opened brunch spot. Highly recommend their breakfast platter, which features unique and delicious sauces.

**12:00 PM**: Experienced a traditional Balinese SPA at Natura Organics Spa. The interior decor is relaxing, the rice field views are beautiful, and the massage techniques are excellent. You can make reservations via WhatsApp.

**3:00 PM**: Called a Grab to head to our accommodation in Ubud, [Jani's Place Cottage](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=2166288).

**6:00 PM**: Dine at Kagemusha Japanese Restaurant. Delicious food in a beautiful setting.

## **Tuesday, March 20**

**09:00** Start the day by visiting Abian Desa Rice Terraces to enjoy the scenic views.

**11:00** Have lunch at Tis Cafe, a dreamy and adorable restaurant.

**Afternoon** Stroll through Ubud Market and the Royal Palace.

> Further reading: How to explore Ubud Market and the Royal Palace? [Click here](/blog/2023-08-16-64dcdf93fd8978000186a9dd/)

**18:00** Dinner at L'Osteria Italian Restaurant.

## **Wednesday, March 21**

**08:00** Head to Kanto Lampo Waterfall for photos and sightseeing. Professional photo service costs approximately USD$3 per session.

> Further reading: [Don't miss this spot for stunning waterfall photos in Bali? Kanto Lampo Waterfall](/blog/2020-11-06-64db6b71fd897800013a9435/)

**12:00** Return to accommodation for a midday rest.

**Afternoon** Visit Saba Beach, a less popular beach with some litter on the shore but clear waters perfect for photography. The area is exposed to strong sunlight with no shade, so it's best to visit between 4:00 PM and 6:30 PM.

**Dinner** Dine at SHOOQ Restaurant. The shawarma is highly recommended, with all sauces served separately allowing you to customize according to your taste.

**12:00** Have lunch at Sushi Island Restaurant.

**14:00-17:00** Head to Kelingking Beach, the most famous spot on Nusa Penida

> Further reading: [Bali - Complete Guide to Nusa Penida: Map, Ferry, Accommodation, Transportation, Private Car, Activities, Photography, Attractions](/blog/2023-08-16-64db6b82fd897800013a9942/)

On Nusa Penida, private car hire is the only option. I booked ferry tickets and private car service through Klook and was very satisfied overall. The car hire company contacted me beforehand to confirm the itinerary and facilitated communication with the driver. Upon disembarking, the driver met me and even helped carry my luggage. Throughout the trip, he was a dedicated guide, even accompanying me down to Kelingking Beach, for which I was truly grateful. At each stop, he took photos to report back to the company, which made me, a solo female traveler, feel much more at ease. I'm really thankful for the driver's assistance, from carrying luggage to accompanying me for photos - he was very responsible.

**05:00** Wake up early and head to the treehouse spot to wait for sunrise

**After sunrise** Continue exploring the dreamy Diamond Beach

**Noon** Revisited Penida Colada Beach Bar for lunch

**13:00** Returned to the port to wait for the shuttle boat back to the main island

**14:40** Called a taxi to head to Le Cielo Villa in Seminyak for accommodation

## **Saturday, March 24**

**07:30** Woke up early

**08:30** Enjoyed a floating breakfast at Le Cielo Villa for photos

**11:00** Had our final Bali spa experience at Lluvia Spa, then took the shuttle directly to the airport to prepare for our journey home.

Before heading to the airport, treat yourself to a mid-range, highly-rated spa experience at Lluvia Spa in Seminyak.

For transportation, apart from pre-booked airport transfers and the trip to Nusa Penida, I used Grab or Gojek for all other rides. It was incredibly convenient, and most drivers were quiet, allowing me to relax during the journey.

## Final Thoughts

Overall, I found Bali to be an ideal destination for solo travel and photography. The spacious attractions allowed me to freely set up my tripod for self-portraits. Except for airport transfers and the Nusa Penida car hire, almost all my stunning photos were taken using a tripod and remote. Even in situations where a tripod wasn’t feasible, like uneven terrain or waterfall settings, locals often offered reasonably priced photography services, which was very thoughtful.

Bali’s natural scenery is breathtaking! This solo trip gave me unforgettable experiences and allowed me to witness the warm hospitality of the locals. Traveling alone isn’t intimidating—it allowed me to fully immerse myself in nature and exotic charm. I’d love to revisit this dreamy island given the chance! I look forward to sharing more travel stories with everyone.
