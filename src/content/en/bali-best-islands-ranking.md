## Bali Climbs to Top 3! Travel + Leisure Reveals 2025 Best Islands Ranking

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fd614936412bc49943234a0f0b484811.jpg)

In the prestigious Travel + Leisure magazine's 2025 "World's Best Islands" survey, Bali secured the 3rd position, once again showcasing its immense appeal on the global tourism stage. This marks Bali's eleventh consecutive year on the list, standing out among hundreds of thousands of reader votes, demonstrating how its cultural charm and natural beauty continue to deeply move travelers worldwide.

---

## Why Does Bali Attract Global Travelers?

Bali is not only one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations but also a sacred island that combines natural scenery with spiritual culture. From its religious traditions featuring over 10,000 temples to UNESCO-recognized rice terraces and volcanic landscapes, every corner holds unique stories and energy.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8eeac6deb511c17075b54478c049814f.jpg)

Whether you want to stay in world-class luxury resorts or choose budget-friendly local guesthouses, Bali has it all. Many accommodations offer high-quality services and stunning views at prices far more affordable than other international hotspots, **offering exceptional value**, making it a top choice recommended by both budget-conscious and high-end travelers.

## Bali's Cultural Highlights

Bali's charm lies not only in its natural beauty but also in its rich cultural heritage:

- **Traditional dances like the Barong and Kecak fire dance**
- **Daily rituals and ceremonies that permeate every aspect of life**
- **The locals' reverence for nature and spirits**, which shapes their unique philosophy of life

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2664ad6d94007006dec606128661dd90.jpg)

All these elements blend together to form Bali's unique philosophy of 'Tri Hita Karana,' perfectly harmonizing the divine, humanity, and nature.

## Bali Reigns Supreme in Asia-Pacific

In addition to ranking among the top three globally, Bali recently triumphed over Thailand's Koh Samui and Vietnam's Phu Quoc in Travel + Leisure's Asia-Pacific Luxury Travel Awards, earning the title of **Best Island in Asia-Pacific**. This award, focused on the luxury travel market, further proves Bali's dual appeal of accessibility and high-end allure, catering to travelers of all types.

## Detailed World Rankings

### 1. Paros Island (Paros, Greece)

- Features quintessential Greek blue-and-white villages, especially the charming town of Naoussa.
- Known for its mushroom, chickpea, and seafood dishes.
- Home to numerous high-end resorts, but the true luxury lies in the boundless Aegean Sea.

**Score: 96.50**

---

### 2. Palawan Island (Palawan, Philippines)

- Pristine and natural, boasting stunning limestone cliffs and coral reefs.
- Secluded white sand beaches make it a paradise for divers.
- The Underground River National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site, offers awe-inspiring caves and ancient forests.

**Rating: 93.60**

---

### 3. Bali, Indonesia

- Bali boasts a rich culture with over 10,000 temples and stunning rice terraces.
- The newly opened Anantara Ubud Bali Resort blends modern and traditional designs.
- A single trip can take you across beaches, mountains, and cultural villages.

**Rating: 93.25**

---

### 4. Ischia, Italy

- A renowned Italian spa destination famous for its over 100 natural hot springs.
- The coastal towns offer charming scenery, combining sea views with wellness retreats.
- Luxury spa hotels like Mezzatorre attract global celebrities.

**Rating: 93.25**

---

### 5. Fiji Islands

- Comprising over 300 islands, Fiji features spectacular white sand beaches and tropical landscapes.
- Its multicultural fusion gives local cuisine a unique flavor.
- A cultural and natural paradise in the South Pacific.

**Rating: 93.20**

---

### 6. Maldives

- Islands scattered across the Indian Ocean, making it a top choice for honeymoons and luxury vacations.
- Features private islands and overwater villas, perfect for diving and snorkeling.
- Local culture is also worth exploring, such as learning Dhivehi or enjoying traditional afternoon tea.

**Rating: 92.65**

---

### 7. Galápagos Islands (Ecuador)

- Rich in flora and fauna, including Galápagos penguins, giant tortoises, and marine iguanas.
- A paradise for eco-tourism enthusiasts.
- Explore multiple islands through boat tours to observe wildlife.

**Rating: 91.23**

---

### 8. Hvar & Dalmatian Islands (Croatia)

- Hvar is renowned for its luxury resorts and vibrant nightlife.
- The island boasts lavender fields, olive groves, and wineries.
- The Dalmatian coastal islands are breathtakingly beautiful and steeped in history.

**Rating: 91.09**

---

### 9. Madeira (Portugal)

- A renowned wine region, also featuring natural coastal pools and stunning forests.
- Diverse terrain perfect for hiking and seaside relaxation.
- Unique culture blending African and Portuguese influences.

**Rating: 89.93**

---

### 10. Mallorca, Spain

- Combines nightlife, cuisine, and serene mountain landscapes.
- Explore towns and harbors via vintage trams.
- Ideal for family vacations, romantic honeymoons, or solo retreats.

**Rating: 89.54**

---

### 11. Crete, Greece

- Greece's largest island, with history tracing back to the Minoan civilization.
- Features majestic mountains and ancient fishing villages, a paradise for archaeology enthusiasts.
- Beaches and the city of Heraklion each offer unique charms.

**Rating: 89.49**

---

### 12. Phuket, Thailand

- A famous beach destination rich in history and culture.
- Old town architecture showcases a blend of Chinese and Portuguese styles.
- Temples, night markets, and local cuisine are visitor favorites.

**Rating: 89.47**

---

### 13. Vancouver Island (Canada)

- Close to the city yet filled with pristine forests and rugged coastlines.
- Victoria boasts British architecture and museums.
- Ideal for nature adventures and luxury eco-tourism.

**Rating: 89.31**

---

### 14. Koh Samui (Thailand)

- Filming location for 'The White Lotus,' featuring stunning bays and forests.
- Stay in five-star hotels or explore inland waterfalls.
- Rich in temples and local culinary culture.

**Rating: 89.31**

---

### 15. Anguilla

- Home to 33 world-class beaches, such as Shoal Bay.
- Combines gourmet cuisine with signature Caribbean hospitality.
- Resorts like Cap Juluca are top choices for luxury.

**評分：89.21**

---

### 16. Milos, Greece

- A lesser-known Greek island, tranquil and enchanting.
- Features traditional fishing villages and unique white rock formations.
- Ideal for travelers seeking to avoid crowds.

**Rating: 89.11**

---

### 17. Great Barrier Reef Islands, Australia

- Includes famous Whitsunday Islands and rainforest-covered islands.
- Serves as the gateway to explore the world's largest coral reef system.
- Perfect for low-carbon, eco-friendly tourism.

**Rating: 88.95**

---

### 18. (Tie) Azores, Portugal

- Located in the North Atlantic, consisting of 9 volcanic islands.
- Each island offers hot springs, wineries, and mountain trails.
- Considered a hidden gem by Europeans.

**Rating: 88.89**

---

### 18. (Tie) Dominica

- Unlike typical Caribbean islands, it boasts mountains, waterfalls, and hot springs.
- Morne Trois Pitons National Park is a World Heritage Site.
- Perfect for travelers who love adventure and nature.

**Rating: 88.89**

---

### 20. Cape Breton Island (Canada)

- A place where Indigenous, Scottish, and Acadian cultures intertwine.
- Spectacular highland landscapes and trails like the Skyline Trail are must-visits.
- Food and seafood are also highlights.

**Rating: 88.79**

---

### 21. Maui (USA)

- Combines the best of Hawaii's beaches, volcanoes, and tropical rainforests.
- The Road to Hana is one of the most famous scenic drives.
- Ideal for family and couple vacations.

**Rating: 88.26**

---

### 22. Sardinia (Italy)

- Rich in Mediterranean history with cuisine influenced by multiple cultures.
- Best known for Costa Smeralda (Emerald Coast) and Costa Verde dunes.
- Explore the ancient Nuragic civilization ruins.

**Rating: 88.22**

---

### 23. Bora Bora (French Polynesia)

- World-renowned for its crystal-clear lagoon and overwater bungalows.
- Volcanic terrain and coral reefs create a tropical paradise.
- Synonymous with luxury and romance.

**Rating: 88.11**

---

### 24. St. Lucia

- The iconic Pitons volcanic peaks symbolize the island.
- Romantic resorts and rich natural landscapes attract couples.
- Excellent cuisine and historical culture.

**Rating: 87.56**

---

### 25. Island of Hawaii (USA)

- The largest Hawaiian island, featuring active volcanoes and black sand beaches.
- Hilo's local culture and Kona coffee are highly praised.
- Volcanoes National Park offers stunning scenic experiences.

**Rating: 87.37**
