## Bali Climbs to World's Top 3! Travel + Leisure Reveals 2025 Best Islands Ranking

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fd614936412bc49943234a0f0b484811.jpg)

In the prestigious Travel + Leisure magazine's 2025 "World's Best Islands" selection, Bali secured the 3rd position, once again demonstrating its immense appeal on the international tourism stage. This marks Bali's eleventh consecutive year on the list, standing out among hundreds of thousands of reader votes, showcasing its cultural charm and natural beauty that continues to captivate travelers worldwide.

---

## Why Does Bali Attract Global Travelers?

Bali is not only one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations but also a sacred island that combines natural landscapes with spiritual culture. From its religious traditions featuring over 10,000 temples to its UNESCO-recognized terraced rice fields and volcanic landscapes, every corner holds unique stories and energy.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8eeac6deb511c17075b54478c049814f.jpg)

Whether you're looking to stay in world-class luxury resorts or budget-friendly local guesthouses, Bali has it all. Many accommodations offer high-quality services and stunning views at prices far more affordable than other international hotspots, **offering exceptional value**, making it a top choice for both budget-conscious and high-end travelers.

## Bali's Cultural Highlights

Bali's charm lies not only in its natural beauty but also in its rich cultural heritage:

- **Traditional dances like Barong and Kecak Fire Dance**
- **Daily rituals and ceremonies that permeate every aspect of life**
- **The locals' reverence for nature and deities**, shaping a unique philosophy of life

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2664ad6d94007006dec606128661dd90.jpg)

All of this blends into Bali's distinctive "Tri Hita Karana" philosophy, harmoniously uniting the divine, humans, and nature.

## Bali Reigns Supreme in the Asia-Pacific Region

In addition to ranking among the top 3 globally, Bali recently surpassed Thailand's Koh Samui and Vietnam's Phu Quoc in the *Travel + Leisure* Asia-Pacific Luxury Travel Awards to become the **Best Island in the Asia-Pacific Region**. This award, focused on the luxury travel market, further proves Bali's dual appeal of accessibility and exclusivity, catering to travelers of all types.

## Detailed World Rankings

### 1. Paros, Greece

- Features classic Greek blue-and-white villages, especially the beautiful town of Naoussa.
- Renowned for its mushroom, chickpea, and seafood dishes.
- Home to numerous high-end resorts, but the true luxury lies in the endless Aegean Sea.

**Score: 96.50**

---

### 2. Palawan, Philippines

- Naturally pristine, with spectacular limestone cliffs and coral reefs.
- Hidden white sand beaches make it a paradise for diving enthusiasts.
- The Underground River National Park, a World Heritage site, boasts stunning caves and ancient forests.

**Score: 93.60**

---

### 3. Bali, Indonesia

- Rich in Balinese culture, with over 10,000 temples and magnificent rice terraces.
- The newly opened Anantara Ubud Bali Resort blends modern and traditional designs.
- A single trip can span beaches, mountains, and cultural villages.

**Score: 93.25**

---

### 4. Ischia, Italy

- A renowned Italian hot spring destination, famous for over 100 natural thermal springs.
- Coastal towns offer charming scenery, combining sea views with wellness tourism.
- Luxury hot spring hotels like Mezzatorre attract global celebrities.

**Rating: 93.25**

---

### 5. Fiji Islands

- Comprising over 300 islands, featuring stunning white sand beaches and tropical landscapes.
- A blend of diverse cultures, with unique local cuisine.
- A cultural and natural paradise in the South Pacific.

**Rating: 93.20**

---

### 6. Maldives

- Islands scattered across the Indian Ocean, a top choice for honeymoons and luxury vacations.
- Private islands and overwater villas, perfect for diving and snorkeling.
- Local culture is also worth exploring, such as learning Dhivehi or enjoying traditional afternoon tea.

**Rating: 92.65**

---

### 7. Galápagos Islands, Ecuador

- Rich in flora and fauna, including Galapagos penguins, giant tortoises, and marine iguanas.
- A paradise for eco-tourism enthusiasts.
- Explore multiple islands by boat to observe wildlife.

**Rating: 91.23**

---

### 8. Hvar & Dalmatian Islands (Croatia)

- Hvar is renowned for luxury resorts and vibrant nightlife.
- The island features lavender fields, olive groves, and vineyards.
- The Dalmatian coast boasts stunning islands rich in history.

**Rating: 91.09**

---

### 9. Madeira (Portugal)

- Famous for its wine, natural coastal pools, and breathtaking forests.
- Diverse terrain ideal for hiking and seaside relaxation.
- Unique culture blending African and Portuguese influences.

**Rating: 89.93**

---

### 10. Mallorca (Spain)

- Combines nightlife, gourmet cuisine, and serene mountain landscapes.
- Explore towns and harbors via vintage trams.
- Perfect for family vacations, romantic getaways, or solo retreats.

**Rating: 89.54**

---

### 11. Crete, Greece

- Greece's largest island with history dating back to the Minoan civilization.
- Features stunning mountains and ancient fishing villages, a paradise for archaeology enthusiasts.
- Beaches and the city of Heraklion each have their unique charm.

**Rating: 89.49**

---

### 12. Phuket, Thailand

- A famous beach destination rich in history and culture.
- Old town architecture showcases a blend of Chinese and Portuguese styles.
- Temples, night markets, and cuisine are visitor favorites.

**Rating: 89.47**

---

### 13. Vancouver Island, Canada

- Close to urban areas yet filled with pristine forests and rugged coastlines.
- Victoria city boasts British-style architecture and museums.
- Ideal for nature adventures and luxury eco-tourism.

**Rating: 89.31**

---

### 14. Koh Samui, Thailand

- Filming location for the movie 'The White Lotus,' featuring beautiful bays and forests.
- Stay in five-star hotels or explore inland waterfalls.
- Rich in temples and local culinary culture.

**Rating: 89.31**

---

### 15. Anguilla

- Home to 33 world-class beaches, such as Shoal Bay.
- Combines exquisite cuisine with signature Caribbean hospitality.
- Luxury resorts like Cap Juluca are top choices.

**Rating: 89.21**

---

### 16. Milos, Greece

- A lesser-known Greek island, tranquil and enchanting.
- Features traditional fishing villages and unique white rock formations.
- Ideal for travelers seeking to avoid crowds.

**Rating: 89.11**

---

### 17. Great Barrier Reef Islands, Australia

- Includes the famous Whitsunday Islands and rainforest islands.
- Serves as the gateway to explore the world's largest coral reef system.
- Ideal for low-carbon and eco-tourism.

**Rating: 88.95**

---

### 18. (Tie) Azores, Portugal

- Located in the North Atlantic Ocean, consisting of 9 volcanic islands.
- Each island features hot springs, wineries, and forest trails.
- Considered a hidden gem among Europeans.

**Rating: 88.89**

---

### 18. (Tie) Dominica

- Unlike typical Caribbean islands, it boasts mountains, waterfalls, and hot springs.
- Morne Trois Pitons National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
- Perfect for travelers who love adventure and nature.

**Rating: 88.89**

---

### 20. Cape Breton Island, Canada

- Features a rich history blending Indigenous, Scottish, and Acadian cultures.
- Spectacular highland landscapes and trails like the Skyline Trail are must-visits.
- Local cuisine and seafood are also highlights.

**Rating: 88.79**

---

### 21. Maui (USA)

- Combines the best of Hawaii's beaches, volcanoes, and tropical rainforests.
- The Road to Hana is one of the most famous scenic drives.
- Ideal for family and couples' travel.

**Rating: 88.26**

---

### 22. Sardinia (Italy)

- Rich in Mediterranean history with cuisine influenced by multiple cultures.
- Best known for Costa Smeralda (Emerald Coast) and Costa Verde dunes.
- Explore the ancient Nuragic civilization ruins.

**Rating: 88.22**

---

### 23. Bora-Bora (French Polynesia)

- World-famous for its crystal-clear lagoons and overwater bungalows.
- Volcanic terrain and coral reefs create a tropical paradise.
- Synonymous with luxury and romance.

**Rating: 88.11**

---

### 24. St. Lucia

- The iconic Pitons volcanic peaks symbolize the island.
- Romantic resorts and rich natural landscapes attract couples.
- Excellent cuisine and historical culture are also highlights.

**Score: 87.56**

---

### 25. Island of Hawaii, USA

- The largest Hawaiian island, featuring active volcanoes and black sand beaches.
- Hilo's local culture and Kona coffee are highly praised.
- Volcanoes National Park offers stunning scenic experiences.

**Score: 87.37**
