Indonesian President Joko Widodo recently announced that citizens from 13 countries can enjoy visa-free entry into Indonesia. This policy is based on the principle of reciprocity and considerations for Indonesia's national economic interests, aiming to boost national revenue and support domestic economic development.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ff6eba9dd0a64a0c697d3f34391751e8.png)

According to a report by *detikFinance*, this new policy has been included in Presidential Regulation No. 95 of 2024 and will officially take effect on August 29, 2024. The regulation states: 'Citizens of countries granted visa-free access may enter Indonesian territory without a visa,' but they must enter through designated immigration checkpoints and can stay for a maximum of 30 days.

It is important to note that this 30-day stay cannot be extended or converted into other types of residence permits.

President Joko Widodo has also instructed the Minister of Law and Human Rights to evaluate the visa-free policy at least every six months, adding or removing countries from the list as needed. In specific cases, such as national security or public health concerns, the minister has the authority to suspend the policy.

Here is the list of 13 countries granted visa-free entry to Indonesia:

1. Brunei
2. Philippines
3. Cambodia
4. Laos
5. Malaysia
6. Myanmar
7. Singapore
8. Thailand
9. Vietnam
10. Timor-Leste
11. Suriname
12. Colombia
13. Hong Kong

The 13 countries on this visa-free list are primarily concentrated in Southeast Asia, expanding on the existing 'ASEAN visa-free' framework and including a few other nations. However, countries previously mentioned as potential candidates for visa-free access—such as the United States, Saudi Arabia, Australia, the Netherlands, China, and Germany—were not included in this list.

Additionally, countries like the United Kingdom, South Korea, France, Japan, Russia, India, New Zealand, Spain, Italy, the United Arab Emirates, and Taiwan were also excluded from this visa-free arrangement. Travelers from these countries will still need to follow the current visa application process. However, with the policy being evaluated every six months, whether these countries will be added to the visa-free list in the future remains to be seen and discussed.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_05d8056aa79596f2f9108999a2456902.jpg)

According to current regulations, Taiwanese citizens can apply for a Visa on Arrival (VOA) or an Electronic Visa on Arrival (eVOA) when entering Indonesia. The fee is IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$16), allowing a stay of 30 days with the option to extend once.

Following President Joko Widodo's directive, Indonesia's Minister of Law and Human Rights will evaluate the visa-free policy every six months. During these evaluations, discussions will take place regarding the potential addition of more countries to the visa-free list. This has sparked interest in which countries might be included next, particularly China and Taiwan, given their significant economic and tourism market potential for Indonesia. Whether these countries will be added to the visa-free policy will be a key topic in future inter-ministerial discussions.
