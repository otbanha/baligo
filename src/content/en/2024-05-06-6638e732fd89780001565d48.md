Our group member James Lin recently visited Bali with his family. They applied for an e-visa online beforehand. Upon arriving at Bali Airport, they quickly passed through the automated immigration area, completing the entry process in just 5 minutes.

Based on his observation, if you don't apply for an e-visa in advance and choose to get a visa on arrival at the airport, you'll need to queue for at least 30-45 minutes to complete the visa process. After obtaining the visa, you'll still need to get your passport stamped at the immigration counter, which he estimates would take about another 1.5 hours.

James Lin's experience shows that if you're traveling with children, seniors over 65, or people with mobility issues, you can directly get a visa on arrival at the airport as there are dedicated lanes that don't require long waits. However, for adults without children, it's recommended to apply for an e-visa online in advance to save considerable time.

Another netizen, Zhou Jiuren, shared his experience of choosing to get a visa on arrival when entering Jakarta. At that time, he found himself in a queue of about 20 people. He had to patiently wait there. Two hours later, it was finally his turn to complete the entry process. However, he was very disappointed with the efficiency, feeling that the entire process was extremely time-consuming.

## Why do I hear that the visa on arrival is fast?

This comes from the experiences of some group members. They were fortunate to quickly complete their visa on arrival after landing, and didn't encounter long queues at immigration. Such situations are indeed enviable, but it's mostly a matter of luck!

If you encounter a large crowd going through immigration, the situation will be completely different. Waiting in line at the airport for over two hours not only tests travelers' patience but also means wasting precious relaxation time.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_75e0b7b75b7ad28994890343c6c92319.png)

Regarding paying for the visa on arrival at the airport, travelers' experiences vary. Some report that payment is very quick with almost no queue, while others mention that during peak travel seasons or when many flights arrive simultaneously, waiting times can be long, sometimes exceeding an hour.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9c5c856be3a0a3b3d83a2591980933e3.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ee9b6ab6d9b455c0a1cc65c4ddcb5b6e.jpg)

It's highly recommended to apply for the **electronic visa on arrival (e-VOA)** before departure. Why? Because when traveling, many things are unpredictable, especially the situation upon arrival at the airport. If you've already obtained your e-VOA, you can proceed directly through immigration upon landing in Bali, saving time on queuing for payment and filling out forms, and avoiding potential hassles due to language barriers or requests for tips.

Additionally, a pickup driver mentioned: "**Better do e-Visa... Sometimes taking long time to wait for visa on arrival in airport.**" This advice is very valuable as local drivers frequently interact with travelers and are well-informed about airport conditions. Their suggestions are usually based on practical experience and are worth considering.

---

### Conclusion: It depends, but preparing in advance is safer

Considering the above points, my recommendation is: **If you wish for a smoother journey and want to avoid unnecessary waits and troubles, it's best to apply for the e-VOA in advance.** While paying for the visa on arrival is an option, since many things are unpredictable when traveling, being prepared beforehand always offers peace of mind.

Of course, if you enjoy adventure or have flexible arrival times, you can opt for a visa on arrival. However, be mentally prepared for potential queues and waiting times.
