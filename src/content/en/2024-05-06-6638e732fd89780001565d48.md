Our group member James Lin recently visited Bali with his family. They had obtained their e-visa online beforehand. Upon arrival at Bali Airport, they swiftly passed through the automated immigration area, completing the entry process in just 5 minutes.

According to his observations, those who choose to get an on-arrival visa at the airport instead of preparing an e-visa in advance would need to queue for at least 30-45 minutes to complete the visa process. After obtaining the on-arrival visa, they would then have to wait for immigration stamping, which he estimates would take about another 1.5 hours.

James Lin's experience suggests that families with children, seniors over 65, or those with mobility issues can opt for the on-arrival visa at the airport as there are dedicated lanes available, avoiding long queues. However, for adults without children, it's advisable to apply for an e-visa online beforehand to save considerable time.

Another netizen, Zhou Jiuren, shared his experience of opting for an on-arrival visa upon entering Jakarta. He found himself in a queue of about 20 people and had to patiently wait. Two hours later, it was finally his turn to complete the entry procedures. However, he was very disappointed with the efficiency, finding the entire process extremely time-consuming.

## Why have I heard that the on-arrival visa is quick?

Based on the experiences of some group members, they were fortunate to quickly obtain their visa on arrival after landing and didn't encounter long queues at immigration. Such a situation is indeed enviable, but it's largely a matter of luck!

If you encounter a large crowd going through immigration, the situation becomes entirely different. Waiting in line at the airport for over two hours not only tests travelers' patience but also means wasting precious vacation relaxation time.

Regarding the issue of paying for the visa on arrival at the airport, travelers' experiences vary significantly. Some report that the payment process is very quick with almost no queue, while others note that during peak travel seasons or when many flights arrive simultaneously, waiting times can be long, sometimes exceeding an hour.

We strongly recommend applying for the **Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA)** before departure. Why? Because when traveling, many things are unpredictable, especially the situation upon arrival at the airport. If you've already obtained your e-VOA, you can proceed directly through immigration upon arrival in Bali, saving time on queuing for payment and form filling, and avoiding potential issues due to language barriers or unexpected demands for tips.

Additionally, a pickup driver mentioned: '**Better do e-Visa... Sometimes taking long time to wait for visa on arrival in airport.**' This advice is highly valuable as local drivers frequently interact with travelers and are well-informed about airport conditions. Their suggestions are typically based on practical experience and worth considering.

***

### Conclusion: It depends, but preparation is safer

Considering the above points, my recommendation is: **If you want your journey to go more smoothly and avoid unnecessary waiting and hassle, it's best to apply for the e-VOA in advance**. While paying for the visa on arrival is an option, since many things are unpredictable when traveling, being prepared in advance is always more reassuring.

Of course, if you enjoy adventure or have flexible arrival times, you can opt to process your visa on arrival at the airport. However, remember to mentally prepare for possible queuing situations.

Therefore, we still recommend applying for an electronic visa in advance to enjoy the convenience of automated immigration clearance.
