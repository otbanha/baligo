According to Indonesian law, those caught possessing drugs face severe penalties. Whether it's a small or large quantity, offenders could be sentenced to several years in prison. Even worse, those convicted of drug trafficking may face the harshest punishment—the death penalty.

These severe penalties are not just empty threats. In fact, there have been multiple cases of drug traffickers being executed in Indonesia. Even some traffickers from Taiwan have not been spared, ultimately facing tragic fates. These cases not only demonstrate the Indonesian government's zero-tolerance attitude towards drug crimes but also serve as a strong warning to potential traffickers worldwide.

For travelers planning to visit Bali or other parts of Indonesia, it is crucial to understand and abide by these strict laws to avoid danger. Possessing or selling drugs threatens personal freedom and safety, and it is absolutely not worth the risk. Even some prescription drugs that are legal in Taiwan or Hong Kong might be considered illegal in Indonesia!
