According to Indonesian law, those caught possessing drugs face severe penalties. Whether it's a small or large quantity, individuals may be sentenced to several years in prison. Moreover, if convicted of drug trafficking, one could face the harshest punishment—the death penalty.

These strict penalties are not just empty threats; in fact, there have been multiple cases of drug traffickers executed in Indonesia. Even some Taiwanese drug traffickers have not been spared, ultimately facing tragic fates. These cases demonstrate the Indonesian government's zero-tolerance attitude toward drug crimes and serve as a strong warning to potential traffickers worldwide.

For travelers planning to visit Bali or other parts of Indonesia, it is crucial to be aware of and comply with these strict laws to avoid danger. Possessing or selling drugs threatens personal freedom and safety, and it is absolutely not worth the risk. Even some prescription drugs that are legal in Taiwan or Hong Kong may be considered prohibited substances in Indonesia!
