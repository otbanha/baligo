When people think of Bali, the first things that come to mind are its beautiful beaches and stunning natural landscapes. However, Bali's cultural heritage is equally rich! The Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) is the perfect place to immerse yourself in Balinese traditional culture. As the historical residence of Bali's royal family, this palace displays Balinese architectural styles dating back to the 19th century and preserves the history and traditional arts of the Balinese monarchy.

## Location and How to Get to Ubud Royal Palace

The Ubud Royal Palace is located in the heart of Ubud, Bali, at 8 Jalan Raya Ubud, in the central area of Gianyar Regency. Its convenient location makes it easily accessible. It takes about an hour to drive from Denpasar city center, or you can take the Trans Dewata bus, choosing the K4B route, which passes through several key attractions in Ubud, making it easier for travelers to reach the palace.

## Open Areas and Features of the Palace

It is said that the interior of Ubud Royal Palace is not open to the public.

It’s important to note that although the Ubud Royal Palace covers a vast area, only a small portion is open to the public. Visitors can mainly explore the outer courtyards and some palace buildings. However, even this limited section offers a glimpse of the beauty of traditional Balinese architecture. The intricate carvings and incorporation of Balinese religious and cultural symbols make it a perfect spot for photography enthusiasts to capture Bali's charm.

## Highlight of Ubud Royal Palace: Art Performances

As a paradise for culture enthusiasts, the Ubud Royal Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the mystical 'Kecak Fire Dance.' These performances are among Bali's most iconic artistic events, attracting visitors from around the world. Tickets cost 100K IDR, and the performances usually take place between 7 PM and 8 PM on Thursdays, making it an ideal way to enjoy a visual feast under the sunset glow. For travelers looking to delve deeper into Balinese culture, this is a rare and unforgettable experience.

## Ubud Palace Visiting Information

Ubud Palace does not charge an entrance fee, and visitors can freely explore the open areas. However, tickets are required to attend art performances, and it is recommended to arrive early to secure good seats for the full show. The palace is open daily from 7 AM to 5 PM, giving visitors ample time to slowly explore this historically rich site.

## Exploring Ubud Palace and Surrounding Attractions: A Perfect Blend of Culture, Shopping, and Nature

Ubud is not only home to the historically rich palace but also offers many nearby attractions to explore, including the bustling Ubud Art Market, the beautiful Saraswati Temple, the trendy Monkey Forest Road shops, and the nature-rich Monkey Forest. Here, whether you're immersing yourself in local culture, enjoying shopping, or connecting with Bali's natural beauty, your Ubud trip will be unforgettable!

### 1. Ubud Art Market 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Located opposite Ubud Palace, the Ubud Art Market is a vibrant hub of Balinese traditional crafts. Here, you'll find a wide variety of handmade jewelry, wood carvings, paintings, traditional clothing, and more, each showcasing the island's rich artistic heritage. Don't forget to bargain while shopping to snag your favorite souvenirs at a better price!

### 2. Saraswati Temple (Pura Taman Saraswati) 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Dedicated to the goddess of wisdom, Saraswati, the temple features pink lotus flowers set against golden walls, creating a picturesque scene that attracts many visitors for photos. This serene spot is perfect for slowing down and experiencing Bali's unique spiritual atmosphere.

### 3. Monkey Forest Road Shopping (Jalan Monkey Forest) 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Monkey Forest Road is one of Ubud's main shopping streets, lined with unique shops, trendy boutiques, cafes, and restaurants. Along this street, you'll find local handicraft stores as well as shops selling Bali's natural skincare products, with coconut oil and handmade soaps being particularly popular. When you need a break, relax at one of the street-side cafes and enjoy a cup of rich Balinese coffee.

### 4. Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Nature lovers should not miss the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary! Located in southern Ubud, this forest is home to hundreds of long-tailed macaques. Visitors can observe the monkeys' daily activities up close, adding a fun element to their journey. The forest also features ancient stone carvings and temple ruins, adding a mysterious atmosphere to this natural reserve. However, visitors should be cautious with their belongings, as the monkeys are known to snatch food! 🐒

## Ubud Palace FAQ

**1. What are the opening hours of Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Traditional dance performances are held in the evening, usually starting around 7:30 PM.

**2. What is the ticket fee for Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime admission to Ubud Palace is free. However, tickets are required for the evening traditional dance performances, costing approximately IDR 100K (around USD$7). Prices may vary depending on the performance. It is recommended to [book in advance](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=944134&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80603-ubud-kecak-fire-dance-show-tickets-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Da2deaa6927).

**3. Can visitors access all areas of Ubud Palace?**

- Not all areas are open to the public. Only a small portion of the main buildings and courtyards are accessible to visitors, while other sections remain private residences for the royal family and their guests.

**4. What are some nearby attractions worth visiting around Ubud Palace?**

- Several popular attractions are located near Ubud Palace, including Ubud Market, Saraswati Temple (Pura Taman Saraswati), Monkey Forest Road, and the famous Sacred Monkey Forest. These sites are close to each other, making them ideal for a day trip.

**5. Can visitors wear shorts and tank tops to Ubud Palace?**

- While there is no strict dress code for Ubud Palace, it is recommended to dress modestly and respectfully to honor local culture. Religious sites or temples often require more covered attire, and some locations may provide sarongs for visitors to use.

**6. What types of traditional dance performances are held at the palace?**

- Ubud Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the famous Barong Dance and Legong Dance. These performances showcase Bali's rich cultural heritage, blending unique music and dance artistry, making them highly worth watching.

**7. Can I take photos inside Ubud Palace?**

- Photography is allowed in the palace's open courtyard areas. However, it's recommended to respect local culture by avoiding the use of flash when photographing performances or religious structures. If local ceremonies or events are taking place, please take photos discreetly as a sign of respect.

**8. What are the transportation options to Ubud Palace?**

- You can choose to rent a scooter or hire a private car. Bali also offers ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek. If you're staying in central Ubud, the palace is within walking distance, making it very convenient.

**9. Are there recommended restaurants or cafes near the palace?**

- There are many well-known restaurants and cafes near Ubud Palace, offering traditional Balinese cuisine and creative dishes, making them excellent choices for visitors to relax and dine.

**10. Do I need to book in advance to visit Ubud Palace?**

- No booking is required for daytime visits. However, if you wish to attend the evening traditional dance performances, it's advisable to purchase tickets in advance to secure your spot, especially during peak tourist seasons when tickets may sell out quickly.

**11. Does the royal family of Bali still reside in Ubud Palace?**

- Yes, Ubud Palace remains the residence of the royal family of Bali. Although they no longer hold political power, the royal family continues to live in the palace and plays a vital role in preserving traditional culture, participating in numerous religious and cultural activities.
