When people think of Bali, the first things that come to mind are its beautiful beaches and stunning natural landscapes. However, Bali's cultural heritage is equally rich! Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) is the ideal destination to immerse yourself in Bali's traditional culture. As the historical residence of Bali's royal family, this palace displays Balinese architectural styles from the 19th century and reflects the history and traditional arts of Bali's royalty.

## Location and How to Get to Ubud Royal Palace

Ubud Royal Palace is located in the heart of Ubud, Bali, at No. 8 Jalan Raya Ubud, in the central area of Gianyar Regency. It is very conveniently situated. It takes about an hour to drive from Denpasar city center. If using public transportation, you can take the Trans Dewata bus on the K4B route, which passes through several key attractions in Ubud, making it easier for travelers to reach the palace.

## Open Areas and Features of the Palace

It is said that the interior of Ubud Royal Palace is not open to the public.

It’s important to note that although Ubud Royal Palace covers a vast area, only a small portion is open to visitors. Tourists can mainly explore the outer courtyards and some palace buildings. However, even this limited section offers a glimpse of the beauty of traditional Balinese architecture. The intricate carvings and abundant religious and cultural symbols make it a perfect spot for photography enthusiasts to capture Bali's charm.

## Highlight of Ubud Royal Palace: Art Performances

As a paradise for culture enthusiasts, Ubud Royal Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the mystical 'Kecak Fire Dance.' These performances are among Bali's most iconic artistic events, attracting visitors from around the world. Tickets cost 100K IDR, and the performances usually take place between 7 PM and 8 PM on Thursday evenings, making it a perfect visual feast to enjoy under the sunset. For those looking to delve deeper into Balinese culture, this is a rare and unforgettable experience.

## Visiting Information for Ubud Palace

Ubud Palace does not charge an entrance fee, and visitors can enter the open areas for free. However, if you wish to enjoy an art performance, you will need to purchase tickets, and it is recommended to arrive early to secure a good seat for the entire show. The palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM, giving visitors ample time to slowly explore this historic site.

## Exploring Ubud Palace and Surrounding Attractions: A Perfect Blend of Culture, Shopping, and Nature

Ubud is not only home to the historic palace but also offers many surrounding attractions to explore, including the bustling Ubud Art Market, the beautiful Water Palace, the trendy shops along Monkey Forest Road, and the nature-rich Monkey Forest. Here, whether you're experiencing local culture, enjoying shopping, or immersing yourself in Bali's natural ecology, your trip to Ubud will be unforgettable!

### 1. Ubud Art Market 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Located opposite Ubud Palace, Ubud Art Market is a vibrant marketplace filled with traditional Balinese goods. The market offers a wide variety of items, including handmade jewelry, wood carvings, paintings, and traditional clothing, each showcasing the local artistic atmosphere. Remember to haggle appropriately while shopping to get your desired souvenirs at a better price!

### 3. Monkey Forest Road Shopping (Jalan Monkey Forest) 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Monkey Forest Road is one of Ubud's main shopping streets, lined with unique shops, fashionable boutiques, cafes, and restaurants. The street features both local handicraft stores and shops selling Bali's natural skincare products, with items like coconut oil and handmade soaps being particularly popular. When you're tired from shopping, you can take a break at one of the street's cafes and enjoy a cup of rich Balinese coffee.

### 4. Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Nature lovers should not miss the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary! Located in southern Ubud, this forest is home to hundreds of long-tailed macaques, where visitors can observe the monkeys' daily activities up close, adding fun to their journey. The forest also features ancient stone carvings and temple ruins, adding a mysterious atmosphere to this nature reserve. However, visitors need to be extra careful with their belongings here, as the monkeys are known to snatch food! 🐒

## Ubud Palace FAQ

**1. What are the opening hours of Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace opens daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Traditional dance performances are held at specific times in the evening, usually starting around 7:30 PM.

**2. What is the ticket fee for Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime admission to Ubud Palace is free. For the evening traditional dance performances, tickets are required and cost approximately IDR 100K (around USD $6). Prices may vary depending on the performance. It is recommended to [book in advance](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=944134&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80603-ubud-kecak-fire-dance-show-tickets-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Da2deaa6927)
- What does the 'K' in 100K mean? Find out [here](/blog/2023-08-24-64e730ddfd89780001336a09/)

**3. Are all areas of Ubud Palace accessible to visitors?**

- Not all areas are open to the public. Only a small portion of the main buildings and courtyards are accessible to visitors, while other areas are private residences for the royal family and their guests.

**4. What are some nearby attractions worth visiting?**

- Several popular attractions are located near Ubud Palace, including Ubud Market, Saraswati Temple (Pura Taman Saraswati), Monkey Forest Road, and the famous Sacred Monkey Forest. These sites are close to each other, making them ideal for a day trip.

**5. Can I wear shorts and tank tops when visiting Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace does not have strict dress codes, but it is recommended to dress neatly and respectfully to honor local culture. When visiting religious sites or temples, more modest clothing is usually required, and sarongs are often provided for visitors.

**6. What types of traditional dance performances are held at the palace?**

- The Thursday evening dance performances at Ubud Palace include Bali's famous traditional dances such as the Barong Dance and Legong Dance. These dances showcase Bali's rich cultural heritage, blending unique music and dance artistry, making them highly worthwhile to watch.

**7. Can I take photos inside Ubud Palace?**

- Photography is allowed in the palace's open courtyard areas, but it's recommended to respect local culture by avoiding flash photography during performances or near religious structures. If local ceremonies or events are taking place, please take photos discreetly as a sign of respect.

**8. What are the transportation options to Ubud Palace?**

- You can choose to rent a scooter or hire a private car. Bali also offers ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek. If you're staying in central Ubud, the palace is within walking distance, making it very convenient.

**9. Are there recommended restaurants or cafes near the palace?**

- There are many well-known restaurants and cafes near Ubud Palace, offering traditional Balinese cuisine and creative dishes, making them excellent choices for visitors to relax and dine.

**10. Do I need to book in advance to visit Ubud Palace?**

- No booking is required for daytime visits, but if you want to watch the evening traditional dance performances, it's advisable to purchase tickets in advance to secure a seat, especially during peak tourist seasons when tickets may sell out quickly.

**11. Does the Balinese royal family still reside in Ubud Palace?**

Yes, Ubud Palace remains the residence of Bali's royal family. Although they no longer hold actual political power, the royal family still lives within the palace and plays an important role in preserving traditional culture, participating in many religious and cultural activities.
