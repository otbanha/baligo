When people think of Bali, they often first think of beautiful beaches and stunning natural landscapes, but Bali's cultural heritage is equally rich! Ubud Palace (Ubud Royal Palace/Puri Saren Agung) is the perfect place to experience Bali's traditional culture. As the residence of Bali's royal family throughout history, this palace showcases Balinese architectural styles from the 19th century and bears witness to the history and traditions of Bali's royalty.

## Location and How to Get to Ubud Palace

Ubud Palace is located in the heart of Ubud, Bali, with the specific address at 8 Jalan Raya Ubud, in the central area of Gianyar Regency. It is very conveniently located. It takes about an hour to drive from Denpasar city center. If using public transportation, you can take the Trans Dewata bus, choosing the K4B route, which passes through several important attractions in Ubud, making it easier for travelers to reach the palace.

It is said that the interior of Ubud Palace is not open to the public.

It's important to note that although Ubud Palace covers a vast area, only a small portion is open to the public. Therefore, visitors mainly can explore the outer courtyards and some palace buildings. However, even this limited area is enough to give visitors a glimpse of the beauty of traditional Balinese architecture. The intricate carvings and the incorporation of numerous Balinese religious and cultural symbols make this a fantastic spot for photography enthusiasts to capture the essence of Bali.

## Highlight of Ubud Palace: Art Performances

As a paradise for culture enthusiasts, Ubud Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the mystical 'Kecak Fire Dance.' These performances are among Bali's most representative artistic activities, attracting numerous visitors from around the world. The ticket price is 100K Indonesian Rupiah, and the performances usually take place between 7 PM and 8 PM on Thursday evenings, making it a perfect visual feast to enjoy under the sunset glow. For visitors looking to delve deeper into Balinese culture, this is a rare and valuable experience.

## Ubud Palace Visiting Information

Ubud Palace does not charge an entrance fee, and visitors can enter the open areas for free. However, if you wish to enjoy an art performance, tickets must be purchased, and it is recommended to arrive early to secure a good seat for the entire show. The palace is open daily from 7 AM to 5 PM, giving visitors ample time to slowly explore this historically rich site.

## Exploring Ubud Palace and Surrounding Attractions: A Perfect Blend of Culture, Shopping, and Nature

Ubud is not only home to the historically rich palace but also offers many surrounding attractions to explore, including the bustling Ubud Art Market, the beautiful Saraswati Temple, the trendy shops along Monkey Forest Road, and the nature-rich Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary. Here, whether you're immersing yourself in local culture, enjoying shopping, or connecting with Bali's natural ecology, your Ubud trip will be unforgettable!

### 1. Ubud Art Market 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Located opposite Ubud Palace, Ubud Art Market is a traditional Balinese market filled with local charm. The market offers a wide variety of goods, including handmade jewelry, wood carvings, paintings, and traditional clothing, each showcasing the island's rich artistic heritage. ### 3. Monkey Forest Road Shopping 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Nature lovers should not miss the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary! Located in southern Ubud, this forest is home to hundreds of long-tailed macaques, where visitors can observe the monkeys' daily activities up close, adding fun to their journey. The forest also features ancient stone carvings and temple ruins, adding a mysterious atmosphere to this nature reserve. However, visitors should be especially careful with their belongings, as the monkeys are known to snatch food! 🐒

## Ubud Palace FAQ

**1. What are the opening hours of Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Traditional dance performances are held in the evening, usually starting around 7:30 PM.

**2. How much is the entrance fee to Ubud Palace?**

- Admission to Ubud Palace during the day is free. For the evening traditional dance performances, tickets cost approximately IDR 100K (around USD $7), though prices may vary depending on the show. It’s recommended to [book in advance](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=944134&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80603-ubud-kecak-fire-dance-show-tickets-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Da2deaa6927).

**3. Can visitors access all areas of Ubud Palace?**

- Not all areas are open to the public. Only a small portion of the main buildings and courtyards are accessible to visitors, while other areas are reserved as private residences for the royal family and their guests.

**4. What are some nearby attractions worth visiting?**

- There are several popular attractions near Ubud Palace, including Ubud Market, Saraswati Temple (Pura Taman Saraswati), Monkey Forest Road, and the famous Sacred Monkey Forest. These spots are close to each other, making them ideal for a day trip.

**5. Can I wear shorts and tank tops to Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace does not have strict dress code requirements, but it's recommended to dress neatly and respectfully to honor local culture. When visiting religious sites or temples, modest clothing is usually required, and some locations may provide sarongs for visitors to use.

**6. What types of traditional dance performances are held at the palace?**

- The dance performances at Ubud Palace every Thursday evening include famous Balinese traditional dances such as the Barong Dance and Legong Dance. These performances showcase Bali's rich cultural heritage, blending unique music and dance art forms, making them highly worth watching.

**7. Can I take photos inside Ubud Palace?**

- Photography is allowed in the palace's open courtyard areas, but it's recommended to respect local culture by avoiding the use of flash when photographing performances or religious facilities. If local ceremonies or events are taking place, please take photos discreetly to show respect.

**8. What are the transportation options to Ubud Palace?**

- You can choose to rent a scooter or hire a private car. Bali also offers ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek. If you're staying in central Ubud, the palace is within walking distance, making it very convenient.

**9. Are there any recommended restaurants or cafes near the palace?**

- There are many well-known restaurants and cafes near Ubud Palace, offering both traditional Balinese cuisine and creative dishes, making them excellent choices for visitors to relax and dine.

**10. Do I need to make a reservation in advance to visit Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime visits require no reservation, but if you want to watch the evening traditional dance performances, it's recommended to purchase tickets in advance to secure your spot, especially during peak tourist seasons when tickets may sell out quickly.

**11. Does the Balinese royal family still reside in Ubud Palace?**

- Yes, Ubud Palace remains the residence of the Balinese royal family. Although they no longer hold political power, the royal family continues to live in the palace and plays a vital role in preserving traditional culture, participating in numerous religious and cultural events.
