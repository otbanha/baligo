Tip: Should you apply sunscreen or mosquito repellent first? The correct order is **apply sunscreen first, then mosquito repellent**. Here’s why: Sunscreen should be applied first because it needs to form an even protective layer on the skin to effectively block UV rays. Sunscreen usually needs to be applied 15-30 minutes before going outside to allow time for absorption. Mosquito repellent should be applied afterward because its active ingredients (such as DEET or other repellents) need to evaporate into the air to work effectively. If you apply repellent before sunscreen, the sunscreen may dilute or cover the repellent, reducing its effectiveness. **Pro Tips:** After applying sunscreen, wait a few minutes for it to absorb before applying mosquito repellent. Ideally, leave a 5-10 minute gap between applications. Some products may indicate they shouldn’t be mixed with sunscreen, so check the instructions before use. If you need to reapply sunscreen, remember to reapply mosquito repellent as well. This ensures both products work optimally, keeping you protected from both sunburn and mosquito bites!
