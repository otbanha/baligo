Tip: Should you apply sunscreen or insect repellent first? The correct order is **apply sunscreen first, then insect repellent**. Here's why: Sunscreen should be applied first because it needs to evenly adhere to the skin to form a protective barrier, effectively blocking UV rays. Sunscreen typically needs to be applied 15-30 minutes before going outside to allow time for absorption. Insect repellent should be applied afterward because its active ingredients (like DEET or other repellents) need to evaporate into the air to be effective. If you apply insect repellent before sunscreen, the sunscreen may dilute or cover the repellent, reducing its effectiveness. **Pro Tip:** After applying sunscreen, wait a few minutes for it to absorb before applying insect repellent. Ideally, leave a 5-10 minute gap between applications. Some products may indicate they shouldn't be mixed with sunscreen, so check the instructions beforehand. If you need to reapply sunscreen, remember to reapply insect repellent as well. This ensures both products work optimally, keeping you protected from both sun and bugs!
