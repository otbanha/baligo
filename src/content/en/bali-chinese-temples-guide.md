Bali is famous for its Hindu temples, but scattered across the island are also several Chinese Buddhist temples filled with Eastern charm. These temples not only showcase the historical traces of Chinese immigrants in Bali but also serve as unique corners for travelers seeking tranquility and cultural fusion.

Below, we introduce six temples/Buddhist temples in Bali worth visiting 👇

***

### 🏮 **1. Vihara Dharmayana | Kuta**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c97c1a79a14cdb7a094a888ce573dae2.jpg)

Tucked away in the bustling streets of Kuta, **Vihara Dharmayana** is one of Bali's oldest Buddhist temples, established in 1876 with over 200 years of history. The temple is surrounded by a small pond inhabited by turtles, while the main hall features vibrant red pillars, dragon sculptures, and large red lanterns, exuding a strong Chinese ambiance. The serene atmosphere here contrasts sharply with the lively beach outside, making it an excellent spot to find a moment of peace in the heart of Kuta.

***

### 🌿 **2. Vihara Dharma Giri Temple | Tabanan, West Bali**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2b5cbc2609a02a0e190331f413afd26.jpg)

Located in the mountainous region of Tabanan in West Bali, **Vihara Dharma Giri Temple** is most famous for its **giant reclining Buddha statue**. The temple is divided into three levels, each with distinct designs and functions, and features tranquil gardens and viewing platforms. With its fresh air and beautiful scenery, visitors can freely wander around and experience the peaceful Buddhist atmosphere, making it a must-visit destination for many travelers exploring West Bali.

***

### 🕊 **3. Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery | Banjar, North Bali**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c227915a15c932da1e5530467e36dfcc.jpg)

Situated in Banjar, North Bali, **Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery** was built in the 1960s and is **one of Bali's largest Buddhist temples**. This temple is also an active monastery where monks practice daily chanting and meditation. The architecture blends Balinese traditions with Buddhist styles, offering views of Lovina's coastline. It is not only a pilgrimage site for Buddhists but also an ideal place for travelers to meditate and escape the hustle and bustle of daily life.

***

### 🕍 **4. Vihara Buddha Dharma | Seminyak**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_202f5bcbe1e52ec63840a101dcb7620e.jpg)

Located in Seminyak, **Vihara Buddha Dharma** was completed in 2007 and is a **modern and contemporary Buddhist temple**. Although it has a relatively short history, it has become an important place of worship for both local and international Buddhists. The temple is clean, bright, and bustling with incense, making it a convenient and serene spot for travelers seeking spiritual cleansing in Seminyak.

***

### 🏯 **5. Vihara Buddha Guna | Nusa Dua**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c74636fc69cbff76a2df2c287979e8ac.gif)

Situated in Nusa Dua, **Vihara Buddha Guna** is part of the renowned **Puja Mandala multi-religious complex**. This complex symbolizes Indonesia's spirit of religious harmony, housing five major religious sites: a Hindu temple, a Catholic church, a Protestant church, a mosque, and this Buddhist temple. Vihara Buddha Guna features elegant architecture, fragrant incense, and a solemn atmosphere, embodying Bali's ethos of "coexistence of different faiths."

***

### 🏮 **6. Vihara Satya Dharma Temple | Benoa Harbour**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2f1db13b9d1b0b61ad8eeede51d4507.jpg)

Located near **Benoa Harbour**, **Vihara Satya Dharma Temple** is one of Bali's most magnificent Chinese Buddhist temples. The temple's architecture is grand and striking, with upturned eaves, crimson pillars, and golden statues creating a visually stunning effect. Upon entering, visitors are immersed in a strong Chinese cultural atmosphere.

The temple primarily enshrines **Mazu (Heavenly Holy Mother)**, along with various Guanyin Bodhisattvas and guardian deities. It serves as an important spiritual center for the local Chinese community, especially during major festivals such as Lunar New Year, Ghost Festival, and Guanyin's Birthday.

The temple is also ideal for photography and quiet contemplation—the morning light shining on the red tiles and golden Buddha statues creates a particularly solemn scene. Due to its proximity to the harbor, many cruise passengers visit before or after their voyages to pray for a safe and smooth journey.

***

## 💡 **Tips for Visiting Buddhist Temples:**

- Dress modestly before entering the temple.
- Avoid pointing the soles of your feet toward Buddha statues or altars.
- Maintain a peaceful mindset and avoid loud noises.
- Traditionally, women during menstruation or within six weeks postpartum are advised not to enter the temple.
