**Bottom Line First**: The top recommendation among Bali's six Chinese Buddhist temples is **Vihara Dharmayana**, tucked away in the alleys of Kuta. Founded in 1876 and over 200 years old, it's one of Bali's oldest Buddhist temples—a perfect spot to find a moment of peace amid the bustling city. In West Bali's Tabanan, Vihara Dharma Giri Temple is famous for its giant reclining Buddha statue.

Bali is renowned for its Hindu temples, but the island is also dotted with several Chinese Buddhist temples rich in Eastern character. These temples not only showcase the historical footprint of Chinese immigrants in Bali but also offer travelers unique corners for quiet reflection and cultural fusion.

Here are six Bali temples worth visiting 👇

***

### 🏮 **1. Vihara Dharmayana｜Kuta**

![1. 法光寺（Vihara Dharmayana）｜庫塔](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c97c1a79a14cdb7a094a888ce573dae2.jpg)

Hidden in the lively alleys of downtown Kuta, **Vihara Dharmayana** is one of Bali's oldest Buddhist temples, founded in 1876 and over 200 years old. The temple is surrounded by a small pond where turtles live, while the main hall features vibrant red pillars, dragon sculptures, and large red lanterns, creating a strong Chinese atmosphere. It's serene and peaceful here, a stark contrast to the noisy beaches outside—an ideal escape for a moment of calm in the heart of Kuta.

***

### 🌿 **2. Vihara Dharma Giri Temple｜Tabanan, West Bali**

![2. 法吉禪寺（Vihara Dharma Giri Temple）｜塔巴南區，西峇里島](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2b5cbc2609a02a0e190331f413afd26.jpg)

Located in the mountainous Tabanan region of West Bali, **Vihara Dharma Giri Temple** is best known for its **giant reclining Buddha statue**. The temple is built on three levels, each with distinct designs and functions, and features tranquil gardens and viewing platforms. With fresh air and beautiful scenery, visitors can freely wander and soak in the peaceful Buddhist atmosphere—it's a deliberate stop for many heading to West Bali.

***

### 🕊 **3. Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery｜Banjar, North Bali**

![3. 梵天寺（Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery）｜班嘉區，北峇里島](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c227915a15c932da1e5530467e36dfcc.jpg)

Situated in the Banjar area of North Bali, **Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery** was built in the 1960s and is a **large Buddhist temple on the island**. It also functions as an active monastery where monks chant and meditate daily. The architecture blends Balinese traditions with Buddhist styles, and offers sweeping views of the Lovina coastline. It's not only a pilgrimage site for Buddhists but also an ideal retreat for travelers seeking meditation and escape from the hustle and bustle.

***

### 🕍 **4. Vihara Buddha Dharma｜Seminyak**

![4. 八菩薩佛法寺（Vihara Buddha Dharma）｜水明漾](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_202f5bcbe1e52ec63840a101dcb7620e.jpg)

Located in Seminyak, the **Eight Bodhisattva Dharma Temple**, completed in 2007, is a **modern and contemporary Buddhist temple**. Though not steeped in history, it has become a key site for both local and international Buddhists to gather and worship. The temple is clean, bright, and filled with the scent of incense, offering a peaceful and convenient retreat for travelers seeking spiritual cleansing in Seminyak.

***

### 🏯 **5. Vihara Buddha Guna Temple｜Nusa Dua**

![5. 佛陀功德寺（Vihara Buddha Guna）｜努沙杜瓦](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c74636fc69cbff76a2df2c287979e8ac.gif)

Situated in Nusa Dua, **Vihara Buddha Guna Temple** is part of the renowned **Puja Mandala multi-religious complex**. This complex symbolizes Indonesia's spirit of religious harmony, housing five places of worship: a Hindu temple, a Catholic church, a Protestant church, a mosque, and this Buddhist temple. With its elegant architecture, drifting incense, and serene atmosphere, Vihara Buddha Guna beautifully embodies Bali's ethos of 'coexisting faiths.'

***

### 🏮 **6. Baoan Temple / Vihara Satya Dharma Temple｜Benoa Harbour, Bali**

![6. 保安宮/薩迦法寺（Vihara Satya Dharma Temple）｜峇里島港口區（Benoa Harbour](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2f1db13b9d1b0b61ad8eeede51d4507.jpg)

Located beside **Benoa Harbour**, Vihara Satya Dharma Temple is **one of Bali's most ornate Chinese Buddhist temples**. Its grand architectural style features sweeping eaves, vermilion pillars, and golden statues that create a stunning visual impact, immersing visitors in a rich Chinese cultural ambiance the moment they step inside.

The temple primarily enshrines **the Heavenly Mother**, along with multiple Guanyin Bodhisattvas and guardian deities, serving as a vital spiritual center for the local Chinese community during major festivals such as Lunar New Year, the Ghost Festival, and Guanyin's birthday.

It's also an excellent spot for photography and quiet prayer—morning light streaming over the red tiles and golden Buddha statues creates a particularly solemn scene. Thanks to its proximity to the harbor, many cruise passengers stop by before boarding or after disembarking to pray for a safe and smooth journey.

***

## 💡 **Tips for Visiting Buddhist Temples:**

- Dress modestly before entering the temple.
- Avoid pointing your feet toward Buddha statues or altars.
- Maintain a peaceful mindset and refrain from loud talking.
- Traditionally, women are advised not to enter temples during menstruation or within six weeks after childbirth.
