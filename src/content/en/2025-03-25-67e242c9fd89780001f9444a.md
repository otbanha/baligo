As a globally popular tourist destination, Bali has recently introduced stricter regulations for foreign tourists. The Bali Provincial Government officially issued **Announcement No. 7 of 2025**, clearly outlining tourist behavior guidelines, including mandatory obligations, prohibited actions, and penalties for violations, to preserve local culture and the environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e38d91165546796db77a86f37dbb3076.png)

## **Key Points of the New Regulations: Civilized Tourism, Heavy Penalties for Violations**

Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** emphasized that the new regulations aim to ensure foreign tourists respect local culture and reduce inappropriate behavior. Although similar regulations were issued in 2023, this update is more detailed to address various issues that have arisen over the past year and a half.

## **What Tourists Must Comply With**

1. **Respect Religious Culture**
2. - Maintain decorum when entering temples or visiting religious artifacts; do not desecrate them.
   - Observe etiquette and avoid interfering during local祭祀 ceremonies.
3. **Hire Licensed Tour Guides**
4. - When sightseeing in Bali, tourists must be accompanied by a licensed guide (or [professional private driver](/blog/2024-07-07-668aaea7fd89780001981840/)) to ensure understanding of local customs and environmental regulations.
5. **Transaction and Payment Rules**
6. - Use only officially authorized currency exchange services (see [here](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/) for details).
   - Transactions should be conducted in Indonesian Rupiah.

## **Transportation and Behavior Guidelines**

- **Driving Regulations**:
- - Helmets are mandatory when riding motorcycles, and a valid international or local driver’s license is required.
  - Drunk driving, overloading, or dangerous driving is prohibited.
  - When renting a car, ensure it is provided by a legal rental company.
- **Accommodation Restrictions**:
- - Stay only in government-approved accommodations and adhere to specific regulations at various tourist sites.

## **Prohibited Actions, Violators Will Be Penalized**

**Religious Sites**:
- Entry to temple core areas is prohibited for non-worship purposes.
- Climbing sacred trees or engaging in disrespectful behavior is strictly forbidden.
**Environmental Protection**:
- Littering, especially water pollution, is prohibited.
- The use of single-use plastic items is restricted. Visitors are advised to avoid using plastic bags, straws, and disposable utensils. Bringing reusable bags or containers is recommended.
**Behavioral Conduct**:
- Offensive language or inappropriate behavior is not allowed.
- Engaging in commercial activities or illegal work without permission is prohibited.

The governor warns that **violators will face legal consequences**, including fines, deportation, or even criminal charges. If residents encounter tourists violating these rules, they can report them by calling the hotline: **081-287-590-999**.

These new regulations take effect immediately, aiming to enhance tourism quality while protecting Bali's cultural and natural environment. Travelers planning to visit must adhere to these rules to avoid legal issues that could disrupt their trip.
