As a globally popular tourist destination, Bali has recently implemented stricter regulations for foreign tourists. The Bali Provincial Government officially issued **Circular No. 7 of 2025**, clearly outlining tourist behavior guidelines, including mandatory obligations, prohibited actions, and penalties for violations, to preserve local culture and the environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e38d91165546796db77a86f37dbb3076.png)

## **Key Points of the New Regulations: Civilized Tourism, Heavy Penalties for Violations**

Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** emphasized that the new regulations aim to ensure foreign tourists respect local culture and reduce inappropriate behavior. Although similar regulations were issued in 2023, this update is more detailed to address various issues that have arisen over the past year and a half.

## **What Tourists Must Comply With**

1. **Respect Religious Culture**
2. - When entering temples or visiting religious relics, maintain solemnity and avoid desecration.
   - If encountering local ceremonial rituals, observe proper etiquette and avoid interference.
3. **Hire Licensed Tour Guides**
4. - When sightseeing in Bali, tourists must be accompanied by a licensed guide (or a [professional private driver](/blog/2024-07-07-668aaea7fd89780001981840/)) to ensure understanding of local customs and environmental regulations.
5. **Transaction and Payment Rules**
6. - For currency exchange, use only officially authorized institutions (see [here](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/) for details).
   - Use Indonesian Rupiah for transactions.

## **Transportation and Behavioral Guidelines**

- **Driving Regulations**:
- - Motorcyclists must wear helmets and possess a valid international or domestic driver’s license.
  - Drunk driving, overloading, or dangerous driving is prohibited.
  - When renting a car, choose vehicles provided by legal rental companies.
- **Accommodation Restrictions**:
- - Only stay in government-approved accommodations and adhere to specific regulations at various attractions.

## **Prohibited Actions, Violators Will Be Penalized**

- **Religious Sites**:
- - Entry to the inner sanctum of temples is prohibited for non-worship purposes.
  - Climbing sacred trees or engaging in disrespectful behavior is forbidden.
- **Environmental Protection**:
- - Littering, especially water pollution, is strictly prohibited.
  - The use of single-use plastic products is restricted. Visitors are advised to avoid items like plastic bags, straws, and disposable utensils. Bringing reusable bags or containers is recommended.
- **Behavior**:
- - Offensive language or improper conduct is not allowed.
  - Engaging in commercial activities or illegal work without permission is prohibited.

The governor warns that **violators will face legal consequences**, including fines, deportation, or even criminal charges. If the public notices any violations, they can report them by calling the hotline: **081-287-590-999**.

These new regulations take effect immediately, aiming to enhance tourism quality while protecting Bali's cultural and natural environment. Travelers planning to visit must adhere to these rules to avoid legal issues that could disrupt their trip.
