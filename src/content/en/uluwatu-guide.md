Uluwatu is located at the southern tip of Bali, covering a vast area that encompasses the entire southern region of the island. The name 'Uluwatu' is quite intriguing: 'Ulu' means 'the end of the land,' while 'Watu' stands for 'rock.' The terrain here is primarily composed of rocks, resulting in a rugged and varied landscape. This area is characterized by massive sea cliffs, rolling hills, and small knolls.

Uluwatu is renowned for its stunning cliffside views, hidden beaches nestled between cliffs, Balinese temples perched on cliffs, and world-class surfing spots. Although the beaches here are not vast, they are among the most beautiful on the island!

### Uluwatu Temple (📌[Location Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1caV88xn9M89ulNo9cVhNF6dyakGSo0g&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_73f1698ba36e1b9c8e46a30af016165b.jpg)

Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali's most famous seaside temples, perched atop towering cliffs in the Uluwatu region, offering breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean. Built in the 11th century, it is one of the oldest temples on the island. Beyond its religious significance, Uluwatu Temple is renowned for its stunning cliffside sunset views, attracting large crowds of visitors every evening.

A word of caution: the monkeys here are quite mischievous, so be careful with items in your hands, backpacks, and even sunglasses—they might try to snatch them!

### Pandawa Beach/Tanah Barak Cliff Scenic Road/Blue Road (📌[Location Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1caV88xn9M89ulNo9cVhNF6dyakGSo0g&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e249047f24fcaf820748a7f940fc9d01.jpg)

Pandawa Beach (also known as Tanah Barak Cliff) in Bali is a hidden gem that has recently become a popular destination. This newly opened beach is unique for its stunning blue waters and colorful rock formations. The road leading to the beach is carved through massive limestone cliffs, offering breathtaking views of the azure sea and towering rock walls on either side, creating a dramatic visual impact. Pandawa Beach is about a 30-minute drive from the airport and requires an entrance fee. It is open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
