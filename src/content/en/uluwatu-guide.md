Located at the southern tip of Bali, Uluwatu covers a vast area, encompassing the entire southern region of the island. The name 'Uluwatu' is quite intriguing: 'Ulu' means 'the end of the land,' while 'Watu' translates to 'rock.' The terrain here is predominantly rocky, resulting in a rugged and varied landscape characterized by large sea cliffs, rolling hills, and small knolls.

Uluwatu is renowned for its stunning cliffside views, secluded beaches nestled between cliffs, Balinese temples perched on precipices, and world-class surfing spots. Although the beaches here are not expansive, they are among the most beautiful on the island of Bali!

### Uluwatu Temple (📌[Location Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1caV88xn9M89ulNo9cVhNF6dyakGSo0g&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_73f1698ba36e1b9c8e46a30af016165b.jpg)

Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali's most famous seaside temples, perched dramatically on a towering cliff in the Uluwatu region, overlooking the majestic Indian Ocean. Built in the 11th century, it is one of the oldest temples on the island. Beyond its religious significance, Uluwatu Temple is renowned for its breathtaking cliffside sunset views, attracting crowds of visitors every evening.

A word of caution: the monkeys here are quite mischievous! Be careful with items in your hands, backpacks, and even sunglasses or glasses, as they might get snatched.

Pandawa Beach in Bali (also known as Tanah Barak Cliff) is a hidden gem that has recently become a popular attraction.
