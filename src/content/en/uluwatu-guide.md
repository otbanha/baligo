Uluwatu is located at the southern tip of Bali, covering a vast area that encompasses the entire southern region of the island. The name "Uluwatu" is quite intriguing: "Ulu" means "the end of the land," while "Watu" translates to "rock." The terrain here is predominantly rocky, creating a landscape full of dramatic cliffs, hills, and rugged formations shaped by massive sea rocks.

Uluwatu is renowned for its breathtaking cliffside views, hidden beaches nestled between cliffs, iconic Balinese temples perched on cliffs, and world-class surfing spots. Although the beaches here are relatively small, they are among the most beautiful on the island!

Here are the must-visit attractions for your Uluwatu getaway:

### Uluwatu Temple (📌[Location Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1caV88xn9M89ulNo9cVhNF6dyakGSo0g&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_73f1698ba36e1b9c8e46a30af016165b.jpg)

Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali's most famous seaside temples, perched on a towering cliff in the Uluwatu region, offering breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean. Built in the 11th century, it is one of the oldest temples on the island. Beyond its religious significance, Uluwatu Temple is renowned for its stunning cliffside sunset views, attracting countless visitors every evening.

A word of caution: the monkeys here are quite mischievous, so be careful with items in your hands, backpacks, and even sunglasses—they might get snatched!

### Pandawa Beach/Tanah Barak Cliff Cliff Road/Blue Road (📌[Location Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1caV88xn9M89ulNo9cVhNF6dyakGSo0g&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e249047f24fcaf820748a7f940fc9d01.jpg)

Pandawa Beach in Bali (also known as Tanah Barak Cliff) is a hidden gem that has recently become a popular attraction. This newly opened beach boasts stunning blue waters and colorful rocks that make it truly unique. In addition to the magnificent ocean views, the road leading here is carved through massive limestone cliffs, creating a dramatic visual impact with the azure sea in the distance and towering rock walls on either side of the road. Pandawa Beach is about a 30-minute drive from the airport and requires an entrance fee. It is open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
