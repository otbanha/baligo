## What Is Indonesia's 30-Day Visa on Arrival?

Indonesia's 30-day visa on arrival is a short-term visa for travelers from many countries, allowing a stay of up to 30 days upon entry. It's typically processed at airports or seaports upon arrival and is suitable for tourism, visiting family, or other short-term stays.

## How to Apply for the Visa on Arrival

Applying for Indonesia's 30-day visa on arrival is relatively straightforward. First, head to the visa application counter after arriving at the airport. You'll need to prepare the following documents and fees:

- A valid passport (with at least 6 months of validity)
- A return ticket or onward journey ticket
- Visa application fee (usually paid in cash or by credit card, around USD$35)

## Speed Up Immigration with an e-VoA

The Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VoA) is a visa obtained through an online application process, eliminating the need to queue for a visa on arrival at the airport. Applicants simply complete the online application before departure, provide necessary personal information and documents, and pay the visa fee. Once approved, applicants receive an email notification and can present the e-visa directly for clearance upon arrival. This method not only saves time but also greatly enhances the convenience of entry, making the journey smoother.

## Validity of the Visa on Arrival

Indonesia's 30-day visa on arrival is valid for 30 days from the date of entry. It's important to note that this 30-day period **includes both the day of entry and the day of departure**, so you effectively have a full 30 days to stay.

## Can You Leave on the 30th Day?

According to Indonesian visa regulations, you must depart before the visa expires. Therefore, the 30th day is the last day you can leave. If you plan to depart on the 30th day, make sure your flight time is before the visa expiration to avoid any unnecessary complications.

## Consequences of Overstaying

If you overstay, you will face a fine of **1,000,000 Indonesian Rupiah per day** (approximately USD$70). Additionally, you may face more serious legal consequences, such as being banned from re-entering the country. It is therefore advisable to leave before the visa expires to avoid any legal issues.

## Options for Extending Your Stay

If you wish to extend your stay in Indonesia, you can apply for a visa extension. Typically, you can extend it for another 30 days, but you must visit the local immigration office or apply online before your current visa expires, submitting the required application forms and fees.
