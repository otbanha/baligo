## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Regional Governance and Academic Events

- **National Population Forum Held at Udayana University**: The '2026 National Population Dialogue,' organized by Indonesia's National Population and Family Planning Board (BKKBN), concluded successfully last weekend. Key discussions focused on 'inclusive population development' and 'rural dividends,' emphasizing that Bali should serve as a demonstration area for village economies (such as the KDMP cooperative) to bridge the urban-rural wealth gap.
- **ISI Bali Exam Season Crowds**: The Indonesian Institute of the Arts Bali (ISI Bali) enters the final week of the 2026 academic year UTBK-SNBT entrance exams today (4/27), with over 3,600 test-takers flooding the campus. Tourists passing through the area around the arts institute in Denpasar in the coming days should be aware of brief traffic surges in the morning and afternoon.
- **North Bali Airport Regains Attention**: The central government reaffirmed during a recent meeting that the North Bali International Airport project in **Buleleng** has been reinstated on the priority list. Final land assessments are scheduled for completion in 2026, with the long-term goal of alleviating congestion at the southern airport and achieving balanced tourism development across the island.

## 📄 Topics of Interest to Tourists: Transportation and Fee Details

- **Water Taxi Plan**: The Ministry of Transportation unveiled a new proposal today, tasking the state-owned ferry company (ASDP) with developing a water taxi route connecting the airport to Canggu. Construction is expected to begin in August 2026, aiming to solve the current 1-2 hour ground travel time by offering a sea route alternative.

- **Tourist Tax 'Secondary Checkpoint'**: Starting today, the airport (DPS) has officially set up a '**Levy Vouchers**' secondary inspection lane between the arrival hall and the pick-up area. Tourists who have not yet paid the **150,000 Indonesian Rupiah** tourist tax will be required to pay on the spot via QRIS scan, with no way to avoid it. It is recommended that travelers prepare in advance.

- **Underground Drilling Update**: The tunnel boring machine for Bali's Subway Phase 1 is currently operating 30 meters underground. Officials reiterate that construction fully complies with cultural preservation regulations, with no impact on the surface landscape. However, large vehicles may be entering and exiting around some drilling sites, so please be mindful of traffic safety.
