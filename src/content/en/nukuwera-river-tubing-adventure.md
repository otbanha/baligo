Bali is not only known for its stunning beaches and temples but also hides many surprising adventure activities. This time, our family chose the Nukuwera & Pandawa Cave River Tubing experience, which turned out to be one of the most unforgettable highlights of our six-day, five-night family trip to Bali. Unlike traditional rafting, cave river tubing combines historical exploration with water adventure, making it a safe and fun activity for the whole family, allowing everyone to enjoy the unique charm of Bali. Let me share this thrilling and laughter-filled cave river tubing experience!

## Preparation and Booking

Before the trip, we found Nukuwera Adventure, a professional cave river tubing company, through a recommendation in the [Ubud Guide](/en/blog/ubud-guide/). The guide mentioned that this was an adventure where you could experience Bali's historical WWII caves while tubing! This adventurous description immediately caught our attention.

The booking process was quite simple. We reserved the activity for four people through [Klook](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1064752&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F53430-ubud-cave-tubing-adventure-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DActivity.TopNavigation.SelectLanguage%26clickId%3D7271ea9df1). Price-wise, it was about $50 per person, regardless of age, and included hotel transfers, tubing, lunch, and ATV. I thought it was a great deal for a full day of fun!

**Pre-Trip Tips**:

- Book 1-2 weeks in advance, especially during peak seasons
- Bring swimwear, sunscreen, and a change of clothes
- Suitable for children aged 7 and above, making it a perfect family activity
- No swimming skills required

## Starting the Day's Adventure

On the day of the activity, the shuttle arrived promptly at 10 AM at our accommodation, [Bidadari Private Villas & Retreat](/en/blog/bidadari-private-villas-ubud/). This serene retreat in the outskirts of Ubud was our carefully chosen place to stay. It took about 15 minutes to drive from Ubud to Nukuwera's base. The base had well-equipped changing rooms and lockers, allowing us to store our belongings securely.

Afterwards, we boarded their shuttle bus to head further upstream to the starting point. Along the way, our guide shared the historical background of this man-made river. It turns out that during World War II, Japanese troops had established bases in these caves, and now these historical sites have become part of the adventure.

After getting off the shuttle bus, we had to descend into the valley to reach the starting point of the rafting. Here, the staff warmly welcomed us and provided each person with rafting gear, including life jackets, safety helmets, specially designed rafting tubes (with very comfortable seating), waterproof bags (for carrying small personal items), and more.

The guide handed us over to the rafting leader, who gave us a brief safety briefing in English. This included instructions on the correct sitting posture, how to steer, and what to do when hearing commands in the caves, such as lying flat. It was particularly reassuring that they emphasized this route was specifically designed for families, with gentle yet fun currents, making it perfect for first-time participants and children.

## Safety Measures and Family-Friendly Design

As a parent with children, my primary concern was the safety of the activity. Nukuwera excelled in this aspect. They assigned one leader for every group of 4-6 people, meaning our family of four had our own dedicated leader. They also provided appropriately sized life jackets and more stable rafting tubes for the kids.

It’s worth mentioning that this activity has been recommended by many family travel bloggers as a "**safer alternative to traditional rafting**" due to its relatively gentle currents and the constant attention from the leaders. Our 7-year-old daughter was initially a bit scared, but with the leader’s encouragement and professional guidance, she quickly fell in love with the activity and even insisted on doing it again!

## Lunch Break

After the tubing, we changed into dry clothes and were taken to Nukuwera's bamboo-built restaurant perched on the cliff edge overlooking the river valley. Despite the scorching sun, the cool breeze flowing through the valley brought refreshing air, perhaps mingled with the valley's moisture, making us forget we were in the tropical midday heat.

After lunch, we rested at our table. My son had already fallen asleep, too comfortable, as the gamelan music played by the restaurant flowed like water in our ears. Half-closing my eyes, I counted the light spots filtering through the bamboo roof, savoring this rare moment of tranquility.

## **ATV Adventure Highlights**

"Ready to get muddy?" Our guide suddenly appeared at our table, and the four of us boarded the shuttle again to the ATV starting point.

This 90-minute ATV adventure was full of surprises! Following our professional leader coach, we navigated through Bali's most authentic rural landscapes. The ATV convoy initially moved slowly through traditional villages, passing by temples with Balinese thatched roofs, where local children peeked out from the porches waving enthusiastically. The emerald rice fields swayed like waves in the wind, occasionally startling a few egrets that drew graceful arcs across the blue sky.

The coach led us into natural water puddles formed after the rain, and the cool mud instantly splashed all over us! On the red dirt paths marked with tire tracks, we learned how to balance the vehicle, with screams and laughter echoing through the jungle whenever the tires skidded.

The itinerary includes several scenic stops: taking photos by a small waterfall in the valley, overlooking the terraced rice fields, with rainbows faintly visible in the mist, like marks of adventure.

Every turn brings surprises, every journey has its story — this is undoubtedly the most vivid way to experience the rural beauty of Bali!

Want to fully document the excitement of Nukuwera River Tubing? These experienced guides not only know the best angles for every turn and cave but also how to capture the most thrilling moments of adventure. They’ll snap dynamic shots of you navigating rapids, dramatic scenes of entering mysterious caves, and, most importantly, natural interactions of your family laughing together.

## Practical Tips and Precautions

Based on our personal experience, here are some practical suggestions for travelers planning to experience Nukuwera River Tubing:

- **Best Season**: The dry season from April to October is the ideal time to visit, with stable water flow and clear weather
- **Recommended Attire**: Waterproof clothing + non-slip sandals
- **Change of Clothes**: Prepare one set of dry clothes if only participating in cave tubing; an additional set is recommended for ATV activities
- **Photography Gear**: Action cameras like GoPro can be used, but ensure they are securely fastened or let the guide handle the filming
- **Valuables**: Most items can be locked in the base storage lockers; carry only a small amount of cash for tipping guides and leaders
- **Health Considerations**: Requires descending stairs and walking down to the river valley; consider this carefully if bringing elderly family members
