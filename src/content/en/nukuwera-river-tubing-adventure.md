Bali is not only home to enchanting beaches and temples but also hides many surprising adventure activities. This time, our family chose the Nukuwera & Pandawa Cave River Tubing experience, which undoubtedly became one of the most unforgettable highlights of our six-day, five-night family trip to Bali. Unlike traditional rafting, cave river tubing combines historical exploration with aquatic adventure, ensuring a safe and fun experience for the whole family. Here, let me share this thrilling and laughter-filled cave river tubing adventure!

Before departure, we found Nukuwera Adventure, a professional cave river tubing company, recommended in the [Ubud Guide](/blog/ubud-guide/). The guide mentioned that this was a rafting adventure through Bali's historical Japanese army caves! This adventurous description immediately captivated us.

The booking process was quite simple. Price-wise, it was about USD$50 per person, regardless of age, which included hotel transfer, river tubing, lunch, and ATV. I think it's quite a bargain for a full day of fun!

**Preparation Tips**:

- It is advisable to book 1-2 weeks in advance, especially during peak seasons
- Prepare swimwear, sunscreen, and a change of clothes
- Suitable for children aged 7 and above, making it a perfect family activity
- No swimming skills required

## Starting the Day's Adventure

On the day of the activity, the shuttle arrived promptly at 10 AM at our stay, [Bidadari Private Villas & Retreat](/blog/bidadari-private-villas-ubud/). This tranquil retreat in the outskirts of Ubud was our carefully chosen accommodation. It took about a 15-minute drive from Ubud to reach the Nukuwera base. The base was equipped with complete changing rooms and lockers, allowing us to store our personal belongings securely.

Afterwards, we boarded their shuttle bus to head further upstream to the starting point. Along the way, our guide shared the historical background of this man-made river. It turns out that during World War II, Japanese troops had established bases in these caves, and now these historical sites have become part of the adventure.

After getting off the shuttle bus, we had to descend into the valley to reach the starting point of the rafting. Here, the staff warmly greeted us and provided each person with rafting gear, including life jackets, safety helmets, specially designed rafting tubes (with very comfortable seating), and waterproof bags (for carrying small personal items).

The guide handed us over to the rafting leader, who gave us a brief safety briefing in English. This included instructions on the correct sitting posture, how to control the direction, and what to do when hearing commands in the caves, such as lying flat. It was particularly reassuring that they emphasized this route was specifically designed for families, with gentle yet fun currents, making it very suitable for first-timers and children.

## Safety Measures and Family-Friendly Design

As a parent with children, my primary concern was the safety of the activity. Nukuwera did an excellent job in this regard. They assigned one leader for every 4-6 people, so our family of four had a dedicated leader. They also provided appropriately sized life jackets and more stable rafting tubes for the kids.

It's worth mentioning that this activity has been recommended by many family travel bloggers as a "**safer alternative to traditional rafting**" because the currents are relatively gentle, and the leaders closely monitor the entire process. Our 7-year-old daughter was initially a bit scared, but with the leader's encouragement and professional guidance, she quickly fell in love with the activity and even insisted on doing it again!

## Lunch Break

After the tubing, we changed into dry clothes and were taken to Nukuwera's bamboo-built restaurant perched on the edge of a river valley cliff. Despite the blazing sun, the cool breeze flowing through the valley kept us refreshed, perhaps carrying a hint of the river's moisture, making us forget we were in the tropical midday heat.

After lunch, we rested at our table. My son had already fallen asleep, too comfortable, while the gamelan music played by the restaurant flowed like water by our ears. I half-closed my eyes, counting the light spots filtering through the bamboo roof, savoring this rare moment of tranquility.

## **ATV Adventure Highlights**

"Ready to get muddy?" Our guide suddenly appeared by our table, and the four of us boarded the shuttle again to the ATV starting point.

This 90-minute ATV adventure was full of surprises! We followed our professional guide through the most authentic rural landscapes of Bali. The ATV convoy started slowly, passing through traditional villages, past temples with Balinese thatched roofs, where local children would peek out from the porches and wave enthusiastically. The emerald green rice fields swayed like waves in the wind, occasionally startling a few egrets, which drew graceful arcs in the blue sky.

Our guide led us into natural water puddles formed after the rain, and the cool mud instantly splashed all over us! On the red dirt trails filled with tire tracks, we learned how to control the balance of our vehicles, with screams and laughter echoing through the jungle whenever the tires slipped.

The itinerary includes several scenic stops: taking photos by the small waterfall in the valley, overlooking the terraced rice fields, with rainbows faintly visible in the mist, like marks of adventure.

Every turn brings surprises, every journey has its story—this is undoubtedly the most vivid way to experience the rural beauty of Bali!

## Practical Tips and Precautions

Based on our personal experience, here are some practical suggestions for travelers planning to try Nukuwera River Tubing:

- **Best Season**: The dry season from April to October is the ideal time to visit, offering stable water flow and sunny weather.
- **Attire Recommendation**: Wear swimwear and non-slip sandals.
- **Change of Clothes**: Prepare a dry set of clothes if you're only participating in cave tubing; for ATV adventures, it's best to bring an additional set.
- **Photography Gear**: You can use action cameras like GoPro, but ensure they are securely fastened or let the guide handle the photography.
- **Valuables**: Most items can be locked in the base lockers; carry only a small amount of cash for tipping the guide and leader.
- **Health Considerations**: Be prepared to walk down stairs to reach the river valley; consider the physical condition of elderly family members.
