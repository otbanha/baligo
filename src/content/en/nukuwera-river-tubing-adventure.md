Bali is not only home to stunning beaches and temples but also hides many surprising adventure activities. This time, our family chose the Nukuwera & Pandawa Cave River Tubing experience, which turned out to be one of the most memorable highlights of our six-day, five-night family trip to Bali. Unlike traditional rafting, cave river tubing combines historical exploration with aquatic adventure, making it a safe and fun activity for the whole family. Here, I’ll share our thrilling and laughter-filled cave river tubing experience!

## Preparation and Booking

Before the trip, we found Nukuwera Adventure, a professional cave river tubing company, through a recommendation in the [Ubud Guide](/blog/2023-11-17-655054b1fd897800011d4d2c/). The guide mentioned that this was an adventure where you could experience Bali’s historical Japanese military caves while tubing! This adventurous description immediately caught our attention.

The booking process was straightforward. We reserved our trip for four people through [Klook](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1064752&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F53430-ubud-cave-tubing-adventure-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DActivity.TopNavigation.SelectLanguage%26clickId%3D7271ea9df1). The cost was approximately USD$48 per person, regardless of age, and included hotel transfers, tubing, lunch, and ATV. I thought it was a great deal for a full day of fun!

**Pre-Trip Tips**:

- Book 1-2 weeks in advance, especially during peak seasons
- Bring swimwear, sunscreen, and a change of clothes
- Suitable for children aged 7 and above, making it a perfect family activity
- No swimming skills required

## Starting the Day’s Adventure

On the day of the activity, the shuttle arrived promptly at 10 AM at our accommodation, [Bidadari Private Villas & Retreat](/blog/2025-01-17-6789be68fd89780001c3ec93/). This serene retreat in the outskirts of Ubud was our carefully chosen lodging. From Ubud, it was about a 15-minute drive to the Nukuwera base. The base had well-equipped changing rooms and lockers, allowing us to store our belongings securely.

Afterwards, we boarded their shuttle bus to head further upstream to the starting point. Along the way, our guide shared the historical background of this man-made river. It turns out that during World War II, Japanese troops had established bases in these caves, and now these historical sites have become part of the adventure.

After getting off the shuttle bus, we had to descend into the valley to reach the starting point of the rafting. Here, staff warmly greeted us and provided each person with rafting equipment, including life jackets, safety helmets, specialized rafting tubes (with very comfortable seat designs), waterproof bags (for carrying small personal items), and more.

The guide handed us over to the rafting team leader, who gave us a brief safety briefing in English. This included the correct sitting posture, how to steer, and techniques for responding to instructions in the caves, such as lying flat when told to do so. What was particularly reassuring was their emphasis that this route was specially designed for families, with gentle yet fun currents, making it perfect for first-timers and children.

## Safety Measures and Family-Friendly Design

As a parent with children, my biggest concern was the safety of the activity. Nukuwera did an excellent job in this regard. They assigned one team leader for every 4-6 people, so our family of four had a dedicated leader. They also provided appropriately sized life jackets and more stable rafting tubes for the kids.

It’s worth mentioning that this activity has been recommended by many family travel bloggers as a "**safer alternative to traditional rafting**" because the currents are relatively gentle and there’s constant attention from the team leader. Our 7-year-old daughter was initially a bit scared, but with the encouragement and professional guidance of the leader, she quickly fell in love with the activity and even insisted on doing it again!

## Lunch Break

After the tubing, we changed into dry clothes and were taken to Nukuwera's bamboo restaurant built on the edge of a river valley cliff. Despite the blazing sun, the cool breeze from the valley kept us refreshed, perhaps carrying a hint of the river's moisture, making us forget we were in the tropics at noon.

After lunch, we rested at our table. My son had already fallen asleep, lulled by the comfort. The gamelan music played by the restaurant flowed like water in our ears. I half-closed my eyes, counting the light spots filtering through the bamboo roof, savoring this rare moment of tranquility.

## **ATV Adventure Highlights**

"Ready to get muddy?" Our guide suddenly appeared by our table, and we four hopped back onto the shuttle to the ATV starting point.

This 90-minute ATV adventure was full of surprises! We followed our professional guide through the most authentic Balinese countryside scenery. The ATV convoy started slowly through traditional villages, passing by temples with Balinese thatched roofs, where local children peeked out from porches to wave enthusiastically. The emerald rice fields swayed like waves in the wind, occasionally startling a few egrets that drew graceful arcs across the blue sky.

Our guide led us into natural puddles formed after the rain, and the cool mud instantly splashed all over us! On the red dirt trails marked with tire tracks, we learned how to control the balance of our vehicles. The screams and laughter caused by skidding tires echoed through the jungle.

The itinerary includes several scenic stops: taking photos by a small waterfall in the valley, overlooking the terraced rice fields, with rainbows faintly visible in the mist like marks of adventure.

Every turn brings surprises, every journey has its story — this is undoubtedly the most vivid way to experience the rural beauty of Bali!

## Practical Tips and Precautions

Based on our personal experience, here are some practical suggestions for travelers planning to try Nukuwera River Tubing:

- **Best Season**: The dry season from April to October offers the best experience with stable water flow and sunny weather
- **Clothing Recommendations**: Swimwear + non-slip sandals
- **Change of Clothes**: Prepare one set of dry clothes if only participating in cave tubing; an additional set is recommended for ATV activities
- **Photography Gear**: Sports cameras like GoPro can be used, but ensure they are securely fastened or let the guide handle the photography
- **Valuables**: Most items can be locked in base lockers; only carry a small amount of cash for guide and leader tips
- **Health Considerations**: Requires walking down stairs to reach the river valley; consider this carefully if bringing elderly family members
