## 2025 Bali Country/Visitor Statistics

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99a8db2673d75441daa04e66ea7fd9aa.jpg)

This table is actually worth a closer look.

According to official data, the total number of foreign visitors flying directly to Bali in 2025 is close to **7 million**, and the source structure has quietly undergone some changes that 'don't quite align with our intuition'.

First, something Taiwanese travelers will relate to. Taiwanese visitors to Bali have remained stable for nearly **20 years**. The annual number has hovered around **120,000**, neither surging nor significantly declining. This is very 'Taiwanese' — not chasing trends, not being swayed by hype, just quietly returning if they like it.

On the other hand, what used to be a prominent presence of **mainland Chinese tourists** has now dwindled to about **one-third** compared to its peak. This shift is noticeable in airports, hotel structures, and even the language preferences of tour guides.

Next, a phenomenon I find quite interesting. Australia has long been synonymous with 'foreign tourists in Bali'. With numerous flights, proximity, and a compatible vacation culture, it has consistently ranked first. However, this time, European visitors have officially surpassed Australia to become Bali's largest source of tourists.

This isn't a sudden surge but the result of gradual accumulation.

This also reflects the changes observed in Bali in recent years:

- Long-term travelers are increasing, digital nomads are on the rise, and the off-season is no longer as quiet.
- Europeans' travel habit of 'staying longer once they arrive' is changing the rhythm of the entire island.

## Here's another key point many people ask about: How to choose the EVOA electronic visa on arrival?

Currently, the most common stumbling block for general travelers isn't whether to get a visa, but 'which one to choose'.

The most common options now are B1 and C1:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dd1d15ae602a759d1d7e072cbd771d4d.jpg)

**Quick Reference:** [Complete Guide to Indonesia/Bali eVisa/eVOA: Application Steps and Precautions All in One Place!](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/)

### B1 Visa (Tourist Visa)

- Valid for 30 days
- According to latest news, **short-term pure tourism does not require proof of financial means**

This visa has a very straightforward purpose:

It's for tourism, vacationing, relaxing, taking photos, hitting the beaches, and enjoying great food.

But it **is not suitable for**:

Business meetings, contract signings, investment inspections, and medical-related activities.

### C1 Visa (Visit Visa)

- Allows a stay of 60 days
- Requires uploading proof of funds exceeding USD$2,000 from the last 3 months (PDF)

The focus of the C1 visa is not 'travel' but 'visiting'.

Includes exchanges, inspections, and non-employment business contacts, but still prohibits working.

## How should regular tourists choose?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d26a3e513ac8df95013e940223bf7017.png)

The answer is actually quite simple.

If your itinerary includes

- ✔ Travel
- ✔ Vacation
- ✔ Pure relaxation
- ✔ Travel not exceeding 30 days

**Choosing the B1 – Tourist Visa is completely sufficient.**

C1 is more suitable for those with complex travel purposes, longer stays, or requiring formal 'visit purposes'.

Otherwise, preparing too much information just adds unnecessary hassle. However, it's crucial to note that **you must have your return flight ticket and accommodation details ready**! If you're randomly checked and deemed suspicious in your travel intentions, you may be asked to show proof of financial means or even denied entry! After all, there are quite a few cases of illegal overstays in Bali.

## Bali Visa FAQ

### Q1: What type of visa do general tourists need for Bali?

If your purpose is simply tourism, vacation, relaxation, eating, and drinking, **just choose B1 (Tourist Visa)** without overthinking it. This is currently the choice for most short-term tourists.

### Q2: Does the B1 visa require proof of financial means?

According to the latest information, **the B1 visa for short-term tourism purposes does not require uploading proof of financial means**. However, it's still advisable to keep basic return flight and accommodation details handy, just in case.

### Q3: If I want to stay longer in Bali, which visa should I choose?

If you wish to stay for more than 30 days or your trip includes visiting friends, exchanges, or non-employment-related inspections, then you might consider **C1 (Visit Visa)**, which allows a stay of 60 days but requires uploading proof of financial means of over US$2,000 for the last 3 months (PDF).

### Q4: What is the biggest difference between B1 and C1?

In simple terms:

- B1 = Pure tourism
- C1 = Non-employment related visits

B1 is not suitable for business meetings, contract signings, or investment-related activities;

C1 emphasizes visits, exchanges, and inspections, but similarly, no work is allowed.

### Q5: Can I use a tourist visa to view properties or discuss collaborations?

Officially, it is not recommended to use B1 for any business or investment-related activities. If your trip involves 'business-related contacts,' C1 would be a safer choice.

### Q6: Can EVOA be applied for in Taiwan in advance?

Yes. Now EVOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival) can be applied for online before departure,

making the entry process much smoother and reducing the need to wait in long queues.

**Quick Reference:** [Complete Guide to Indonesia/Bali eVisa/eVOA: Application Steps and Precautions All in One Place!](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/)

### Q7: Can the EVOA be extended?

Yes. The B1 visa can generally be extended once for an additional 30 days, following the regulations. However, if you know from the start that you'll be staying for a long time, opting for the C1 visa directly might be more convenient.

### Q8: Is a return ticket mandatory for entering Bali?

Officially, it's 'recommended to have a departure ticket.' In practice, most travelers aren't individually checked, but it's better to be prepared for peace of mind.

### Q9: What if I choose the wrong visa?

Changing your visa after entry is relatively complicated. The best approach is to select the correct one before departure.

If you're just traveling, don't overthink it—the **B1 – Tourist Visa** is sufficient.

## Final Reminder

Bali's visa regulations occasionally undergo minor adjustments. Checking the latest announcements 1–2 weeks before departure is the safest approach. Based on the experiences of most travelers, choosing the right visa makes entering Bali quite straightforward.
