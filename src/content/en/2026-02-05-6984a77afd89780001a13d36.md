## Here's another important point many people ask about: How to choose the EVOA electronic visa on arrival?

Currently, what most travelers get stuck on isn't whether to get a visa, but 'which one to choose'.

The most common options now are B1 and C1:

### B1 Visa (Tourist Visa)

- Allows a stay of 30 days
- According to the latest news, ****short-term pure tourism does not require proof of financial means****

The positioning of this visa is straightforward:

It's for traveling, vacationing, relaxing, taking photos, hitting the beaches, and enjoying good food.

But it ****is not suitable for****:

Business meetings, contract signings, investment inspections, and medical-related activities.

### C1 Visa (Visit Visa)

- Allows a stay of 60 days
- Requires uploading a financial proof (PDF) showing a minimum of USD$2,000 from the last 3 months

The focus of the C1 visa is not 'leisure' but 'visiting'.

It includes exchanges, inspections, and non-employment related business contacts, but still does not permit working.

## Bali Visa FAQ

### Q1: What type of visa do general tourists need for Bali?

If your purpose is solely tourism, vacation, relaxation, and leisure, ****simply choose B1 (Tourist Visa)****, no need to overthink it. This is currently the choice for most short-term tourists.

### Q2: Does the B1 visa require proof of financial means?

According to the latest information, ****the B1 visa for short-term tourism purposes does not require uploading proof of financial means****. However, it's still advisable to keep basic return flight and accommodation details on hand, just in case.

### Q3: If I want to stay longer in Bali, which visa should I choose?

If you wish to stay for more than 30 days at a time, or if your trip includes visiting friends, exchanges, or non-employment-related inspections, then consider ****C1 (Visit Visa)****, which allows a stay of 60 days but requires uploading proof of financial means of over US$2,000 for the last 3 months (PDF).

### Q4: What is the biggest difference between B1 and C1?

In simple terms:

- B1 = Tourism
- C1 = Non-employment visit

B1 is not suitable for business meetings, contract signings, or investment-related activities;

C1 emphasizes visits, exchanges, and inspections, but still prohibits work.

### Q5: Can I use a tourist visa to view properties or discuss collaborations?

Officially, it is not recommended to use B1 for any business or investment-related activities. If your trip involves 'business-related contact,' C1 would be a safer choice.

### Q6: Can EVOA be applied for in Taiwan in advance?

Yes. EVOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival) can now be applied for online before departure.

The entry process will be much smoother, and you won't have to wait in long queues.

### Q7: Can EVOA be extended?

Yes. The B1 visa type can generally be extended once for 30 days as per regulations. However, if you know you'll be staying longer from the start, opting for C1 might be more convenient.

### Q8: Is a return ticket mandatory for entering Bali?

Officially, it's 'recommended to have a departure ticket.' In practice, most travelers aren't individually checked, but it's better to be prepared for peace of mind.

### Q9: What if I chose the wrong visa?

Changing visas after entry is relatively complicated. The best approach is to select the correct one before departure.

If you're just traveling, don't overthink it—the ****B1 – Tourist Visa**** is perfectly suitable.

## Final Reminder

Bali's visa regulations occasionally undergo minor adjustments. It's safest to quickly verify the latest announcements 1–2 weeks before departure. Based on most travelers' recent experiences, choosing the right visa makes entering Bali quite straightforward.
