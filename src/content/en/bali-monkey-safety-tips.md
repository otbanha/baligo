Bali's monkeys are quite unique, closely tied to local Hindu beliefs. Due to the reverence for the monkey god Hanuman, locals generally respect monkeys and even actively feed them. Ubud's 'Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary' is a protected area and paradise specifically created for them.

{{block:戶外}}

A single monkey may seem cute, but when there are two or more, you need to be extra cautious - we've personally witnessed three monkeys teaming up to attack a grown man. Monkeys also frequently steal, snatch items, or cause destruction, with similar incidents happening constantly.

When traveling to Bali, be sure to guard against monkeys: avoid direct eye contact, don't carry food or shiny objects. In protected areas, follow the guides' instructions, remain quiet and keep your distance.

Monkeys can be seen almost everywhere in Bali. Always stay alert, keep your belongings secure, and stay out of monkeys' reach. While interacting with monkeys can be fun, safety should always come first.

Keep these points in mind, and you can safely enjoy your Bali trip. Have a pleasant journey, and we look forward to hearing about your adventures!
