**Bottom line**: The biggest threat in Bali isn’t snakes or wild animals—it’s **monkeys**. A single one may look cute, but **two or more can team up to attack** (we once saw three monkeys gang up on an adult man). They also frequently snatch sunglasses, food, and other belongings. Prevention tips: don’t bring food or shiny items, avoid direct eye contact, keep your distance, and follow the guide’s instructions in protected areas.

Bali’s monkeys are unique and closely tied to local Hindu beliefs. Because the 'monkey god Hanuman' is highly revered, locals generally respect monkeys and even feed them. Ubud’s 'Sacred Monkey Forest' is a dedicated sanctuary and playground for them.

A single monkey may look cute, but when two or more appear, stay alert—we once saw three monkeys attack an adult man together. Monkeys also frequently steal, snatch items, or cause damage; such incidents are common.

When traveling in Bali, be sure to watch out for monkeys: avoid direct eye contact, and don’t bring food or shiny items. In protected areas, follow the guide’s instructions, stay quiet, and keep your distance.
