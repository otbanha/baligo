Bali's monkeys are quite unique, closely tied to local Hindu beliefs. Due to the reverence for 'Hanuman the Monkey God', locals generally respect monkeys and even actively feed them. Ubud's 'Sacred Monkey Forest' is a protected sanctuary and playground created specifically for them.

{{block:戶外}}

A single monkey may seem cute, but with two or more, you need to be on high alert - we've personally witnessed three monkeys teaming up to attack a grown man. Monkeys also frequently steal, snatch items, or cause destruction, with similar incidents happening constantly.

When traveling to Bali, be sure to guard against monkeys: avoid direct eye contact, don't carry food or shiny objects. In protected areas, follow the guides' instructions, stay quiet and keep your distance.

Monkeys can be found almost everywhere in Bali. Always stay vigilant, secure your belongings, and keep them out of monkeys' reach. While interacting with monkeys can be fun, safety should always come first.

Keep these points in mind, and you can enjoy your Bali trip with peace of mind. Have a wonderful journey, and we look forward to hearing about your adventures!
