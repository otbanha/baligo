Komodo is famous for its giant lizard inhabitants, but the judging team emphasized that its appeal goes beyond these unique reptiles. From dramatic cliffs to exotic beaches, breathtaking natural landscapes abound—especially the pink beach, where white sand blends with red coral fragments to create a dreamy gradient.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8f912459f69f089f45717f5762ab0364.png)

The national park offers abundant options for water sports enthusiasts, with its coral reefs and marine biodiversity cited as must-see attractions. Snorkeling or diving here feels like being in a giant aquarium.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5a64eb10e3c1da7349d5a23729583014.jpg)

The 'Time Out Travel' editorial team highlighted that visiting Komodo is not just a visual treat but also a rare opportunity to explore an ecological treasure trove. UNESCO has listed it as a World Heritage site, recognizing its natural landscapes and unique ecosystem. The best travel season is from April to October, when the weather is clear and the sea is calm, ideal for diving activities.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_66bce95b0348817b1e72c3c0177af421.jpg)

## Bonus: Other Selected Landmarks Among Asia's Most Beautiful Spots

---

▍Punakha Valley, Bhutan Located in western Bhutan, Punakha Valley is stunning with its emerald green terraced fields. A recommended side trip is to Khamsum Yulley Namgyal Chorten, a four-story Buddhist stupa housing exquisite artworks.

▍Ha Giang Province, Vietnam

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a48d3efe90362f72bb1aa44f7e269534.jpg)

▍越南河江省

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5b34565bdbeb482d284c6f8c69d260b1.jpg)

For those seeking pristine mountain views away from the crowds, this is an excellent choice. The winding road weaves through terraced valleys and towering peaks, as if moving through a traditional Chinese landscape painting.

▍Zhangye Danxia, China

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b9c114cb5c7e46f5ba8554dfd056559f.jpg)

This geological park in Gansu Province features colorful hills with striking stripes of deep red, golden yellow, and emerald green. These astonishing textures are natural masterpieces formed over millions of years through erosion and tectonic movements.

▍Three Whale Rocks, Thailand

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f29165c6dd242cb362160e72104df10e.jpg)

The 'Three Whale Rocks' consist of three massive rock formations resembling a pod of whales, surrounded by endless tropical rainforests. On clear days, you can even glimpse the Phu Khao Khouay mountain range in Laos.
