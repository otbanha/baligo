Indonesia's Minister of Tourism recently announced plans to push for legislative changes that would restore visa-free entry for visitors from 20 of the most frequent visiting countries. This news has garnered widespread attention, especially among frequent visitors to Bali, who are eagerly anticipating this development.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_df4c423f689ad952426bcbfc63faea8f.png)

### History of Indonesia's Tourist Visa

For many years, visitors from Australia, New Zealand, the United States, most European countries, as well as Taiwan and China, could enjoy visa-free entry to Indonesia for up to 30 days. However, since reopening its borders post-pandemic, visitors from these countries have had to pay a visa fee of IDR 500,000, which has been an additional expense for many frequent travelers.

### Current Visa Policy

Currently, Indonesia offers a 30-day visa that can be applied for upon arrival or through an electronic visa application. This policy allows visitors from 97 countries to engage in tourism, business meetings, and visiting friends and family.

### Impact of Visa Fees on Travelers

The increase in visa fees has forced many travelers to consider this additional expense when planning their trips. This not only affects travel budgets but also impacts visitors' overall impression of Bali. Many travelers have expressed that they would visit Indonesia more frequently if the visa-free policy were reinstated.

### Potential Changes to Visa-Free Policy

Tourism and Creative Economy Minister Sandiaga Uno has proposed a visa-free policy aimed at restoring visa-free access for visitors from countries including Australia, Taiwan, China, Japan, South Korea, the United States, the United Kingdom, India, France, Germany, Qatar, the United Arab Emirates, Saudi Arabia, the Netherlands, Russia, New Zealand, Italy, and Spain. This policy will allow tourists from these countries to once again enjoy the benefit of not having to pay visa fees.

### Potential Economic Impact

The reinstatement of the visa-free policy is likely to significantly boost Indonesia's tourism revenue. More tourists mean increased spending, which will undoubtedly stimulate the local economy. Particularly for tourist hotspots like Bali, the rise in tourism income will drive the development of related industries.

### Predictions from Tourism Experts

Tourism experts believe that if the visa-free policy is implemented, the number of visitors to Bali will increase significantly. This will not only aid in the local economic recovery but also enhance Bali's competitiveness in the international tourism market.

### Preparing for the New Policy

For tourists planning to visit Bali, it is crucial to understand and adapt to the new visa policy. Adjusting travel budgets and preparing necessary documents in advance will help ensure smooth entry procedures.

### Bali Tourism Tax

In addition to visa fees, tourists are required to pay a Bali tourism tax of IDR 150,000. This fee is primarily used for environmental protection, cultural heritage preservation, and improving tourism infrastructure. Tourists can pay this fee upon arrival at the airport or through the Love Bali app and website.

### Challenges in Policy Implementation

Despite the proposal for a visa-free policy by the Minister of Tourism and Creative Economy, its final implementation still faces numerous challenges. Government departments need to coordinate various interests to ensure the smooth rollout of the policy.

### Traveler Opinions and Reactions

Currently, most tourists welcome the restoration of the visa-free policy. They believe it will significantly reduce travel costs, allowing them to visit Bali more frequently. Many travelers have already begun adjusting their travel plans to accommodate potential policy changes.

### Conclusion

Overall, the restoration of the visa-free policy will be a major boon for Bali's tourism industry. It will not only help attract more international visitors but also promote the development of the local economy. In the future, with the further implementation of the policy, Bali is expected to usher in a new peak tourism season.
