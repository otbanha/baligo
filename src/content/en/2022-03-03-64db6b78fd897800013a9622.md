Exploring Bali's Beauty: How Did Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream End Up in Denpasar, Bali?!

Just a 30-minute drive from the airport, this riverside park located between Badung Traditional Market and Kumbasari Art Market is officially named 'Taman Kumbasari.' However, locals call it 'Taman Tukad Korea' (Korean River Park) due to its striking resemblance to Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream.

Once a polluted waterway, it has now transformed into a captivating park, showcasing Bali's impressive river revitalization efforts. Here, you can experience the harmonious blend of nature and modernity, with its winding pathways, pavilions, and serene atmosphere that make you feel like you're strolling along Cheonggyecheon in Korea.

In this hidden corner of Bali, you can wander along the riverside, admire the refreshing scenery, and soak in the harmonious ambiance. This picturesque spot has become a favorite relaxation destination for both locals and tourists.
