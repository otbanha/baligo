When it comes to the soul of Bali, **Ubud** is undoubtedly the cultural center you can't miss. Beyond its rice terraces, yoga, and artistic vibe, Ubud Market is a place where every traveler lingers. Filled with handicrafts and local artworks, it’s a place where you can marvel at the Balinese love for detail and creativity as you stroll through. Today, let’s explore what you should buy at Ubud Market! 🌿

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4afef2016a21557b5908bae9c5c70f79.jpg)

***

### 🧶1. Handmade Textiles

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_218f0d194c432ade542611ace179bde4.jpg)

At Ubud Market, you’ll be drawn to a traditional Balinese fabric called **Endek**. This handmade woven cloth is a source of pride for the Balinese, featuring vibrant colors and intricate patterns that exude ethnic charm. You can use it as a tablecloth, shawl, or even take it home as a decorative piece. Each piece of Endek is like a work of art, embodying the warmth of Balinese culture and the soul of its craftsmanship.

***

### 🧣2. Sarong: A Versatile and Culturally Rich Fabric

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_37f79c58c6862ad429cb1e686e6615f4.png)

The sarong is one of Bali's most iconic souvenirs. This traditional fabric is not only affordable but also highly versatile, serving as:

- Beach towel
- Tote bag or backpack
- Baby carrier
- Tablecloth or sofa cover
- DIY clothing like skirts, tops, or shorts

Some sarongs are made using the traditional **Batik technique**, with colors and motifs that carry local stories and cultural symbolism. When shopping, you can ask the vendors about the meanings behind different patterns, adding value to your collection and keepsake. This isn’t just a piece of fabric—it’s an extension of Balinese culture.

***

### 👕3. Bali-Style T-shirts

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4c17893fefca873287d8967a8fb63949.jpg)

At Ubud Market, you’ll find T-shirts everywhere printed with **'Bali' or Barong patterns**. Soft fabric and casual designs make them perfect for personal use or as gifts. Prices are usually affordable, and the designs are full of island vibes. Wearing one feels like bringing a piece of Bali’s sunshine home ☀️.

***

### 🪵4. Wooden Carvings: Lifelike Traditional Art

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2dbb0b57c27c67f42d3c1f9bd259fb9c.jpg)

Bali's wood carving craftsmanship is world-renowned, with Ubud and Tegallalang being the cradle of this art form. From **symbols of peace, trees of life, mythological figures to elephant sculptures**, each piece is crafted by skilled local artisans, showcasing intricate details and vibrant vitality.

Beyond traditional wood carvings, there are also toy-like wooden collectibles that are both fun and stylish!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d8efdab6446270abc68c86a25529abfc.png)

Visitors can even sign up for wood carving workshops to create their own souvenirs, experiencing the charm of this ancient art firsthand.

### 🎨5. Local Paintings

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dbe855a474b5a5cf9786d05e24c820b6.png)

Ubud has long been a hub for artists. In the markets, you can find many paintings **depicting Balinese life, natural landscapes, or abstract themes**. Most of these works are created by local painters, each telling its own unique story. You don't have to buy large oil paintings; small canvases or hand-painted cards are also perfect for collecting or gifting.

***

### 💍6. Handmade Silver Jewelry

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_72b61db47b0441a0b11437e94330ad1d.png)

Bali's silver jewelry is equally famous, especially in Ubud's markets. Whether it's **bracelets, rings, necklaces, or earrings**, each piece is meticulously crafted by artisans, featuring delicate lines and diverse designs. If you prefer minimalist or ethnic styles, you're sure to find something that suits your taste here.

**Join a silver jewelry-making class**: [Immerse in Balinese Culture: Don't Miss This Silver Craft Workshop Day Tour](/blog/bali-silver-workshop/)

***

### 🧺7. Balinese Handicrafts

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_45771c55bc31c1317e3fea935860e721.png)

In addition to fabrics and accessories, Ubud Market offers a variety of **bamboo and rattan products**, such as bags, hats, and baskets. These items are not only stylish but also highly practical, making them perfect for everyday use or as heartfelt souvenirs from your journey. When selecting, opt for pieces with sturdy structures and tight weaves for better durability and aesthetics.

***

### 🏡8. Exotic Home Decor

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_aef6507b2d960d9de93bb987f44150e2.png)

Ubud Market is also a treasure trove for unique home items. You’ll find many **Bali-inspired tropical and exotic home decorations**, such as handmade rattan photo frames, scented candle holders, bamboo lampshades, wooden trays, and wall hangings. These pieces showcase the beauty of natural materials and can infuse your home with the relaxed, tropical vibe of Bali. Even a small decorative item can evoke memories of sunlit days and rice fields.🌴

***

### 🎁9. Small Souvenirs: Classic Choices for Gifts and Keepsakes

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1b64458b40cb2a01da5bf20f8d3fb7c8.png)

If you’re looking to bring home practical and compact souvenirs, consider the following:

- Handmade keychains
- Woven bracelets
- Aromatic incense sticks

These items are not only affordable but also widely available in various art markets, making them excellent gifts for friends and family back home. Though small, they are brimming with the essence of Bali.
