When it comes to the soul of Bali, **Ubud** is undoubtedly an unmissable cultural hub. Beyond its rice terraces, yoga, and artistic ambiance, Ubud Market is a place where every traveler lingers. Packed with handicrafts and local artworks, it’s impossible not to marvel at the Balinese love for detail and creativity as you explore. Today, let’s dive into what you should buy at Ubud Market! 🌿

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4afef2016a21557b5908bae9c5c70f79.jpg)

***

### 🧶1. Handmade Textiles

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_218f0d194c432ade542611ace179bde4.jpg)

At Ubud Market, you’ll undoubtedly be drawn to **Endek**, a traditional Balinese fabric. This handwoven textile, bursting with vibrant colors and intricate patterns, is a source of pride for the Balinese. You can use it as a tablecloth, scarf, or even take it home as a decorative piece. Each Endek is like a work of art, embodying the warmth of Balinese culture and the soul of its craftsmanship.

***

### 🧣2. Sarong: A Versatile and Culturally Rich Fabric

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_37f79c58c6862ad429cb1e686e6615f4.png)

The sarong is one of Bali's most iconic souvenirs. This traditional fabric is not only affordable but also serves multiple purposes, such as:

- Beach towel
- Tote bag or backpack
- Baby carrier
- Tablecloth or sofa cover
- DIY transformation into skirts, tops, or shorts

Some sarongs are made using the traditional **Batik technique**, with colors and motifs that carry local stories and cultural symbolism. When shopping, ask the vendors about the meanings behind different patterns to add depth to your collection and its commemorative value. It’s not just a piece of fabric—it’s an extension of Balinese culture.

***

### 👕3. Bali-Themed T-Shirts

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4c17893fefca873287d8967a8fb63949.jpg)

At Ubud Market, you’ll find T-shirts everywhere featuring **"Bali" lettering or Barong patterns**. Soft, casual, and affordable, these shirts are perfect for personal use or as souvenirs. Their island-inspired designs make you feel like you’re bringing a piece of Bali’s sunshine home with you ☀️.

***

### 🪵4. Wooden Carvings: Lifelike Traditional Art

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2dbb0b57c27c67f42d3c1f9bd259fb9c.jpg)

Bali's wood carving craftsmanship is world-renowned, with Ubud and Tegallalang being important birthplaces of this art form. From **symbols of peace, trees of life, mythological characters to elephant sculptures**, each piece is crafted by skilled local artisans, showcasing intricate details and vibrant life.

In addition to traditional wood carvings, there are also toy-like wooden collectibles that are both fun and stylish!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d8efdab6446270abc68c86a25529abfc.png)

Visitors can even sign up for wood carving workshops to create their own souvenirs and experience the charm of this ancient art.

***

### 🎨5. Local Paintings

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dbe855a474b5a5cf9786d05e24c820b6.png)

Ubud has long been a gathering place for artists. In the markets, you can find many paintings **depicting Balinese life, natural landscapes, or abstract themes**. Most of these works are created by local painters, each with its own unique story. You don't have to buy large oil paintings; small canvases or hand-painted cards are also great for collecting or gifting.

***

### 💍6. Handmade Jewelry

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_72b61db47b0441a0b11437e94330ad1d.png)

Bali's silver jewelry is equally famous, especially in Ubud markets where the selection is vast. Whether it's **bracelets, rings, necklaces, or earrings**, each piece is meticulously crafted by artisans, featuring delicate lines and diverse designs. If you prefer minimalist or ethnic styles, you're sure to find something that suits you here.

**Join a jewelry-making class**: [Immerse in Balinese Culture: Don't Miss This Silver Craft Workshop Day Tour](/blog/2025-06-30-685b8c4ffd89780001f4fc01/)

***

### 🧺7. Balinese Handicrafts

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_45771c55bc31c1317e3fea935860e721.png)

In addition to fabrics and accessories, Ubud Market offers a wide variety of **bamboo and rattan products**, such as bags, hats, and baskets. These items are not only stylish but also highly practical. They are perfect for everyday use and make heartfelt souvenirs from your travels. Opt for pieces with sturdy structures and tight weaves for greater durability and aesthetic appeal.

***

### 🏡8. Exotic Home Decor

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_aef6507b2d960d9de93bb987f44150e2.png)

Ubud Market is also a treasure trove for unique home decor items. You'll find many pieces that embody **Bali's tropical and exotic charm**, such as handmade rattan picture frames, scented candle holders, bamboo lampshades, wooden trays, and wall hangings. These creations showcase the beauty of natural materials and can infuse your home with Bali's relaxed vibe. Even a small decorative piece can evoke memories of sunlit days and rice field scents.🌴

***

### 🎁9. Compact Souvenirs: Classic Choices for Gifts and Collections

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1b64458b40cb2a01da5bf20f8d3fb7c8.png)

If you're looking to bring home practical and compact mementos, consider the following:

- Handmade keychains
- Woven bracelets
- Scented incense sticks

These items are not only affordable and widely available in various art markets, but they also make excellent gifts for friends and family back home. Though small, they are brimming with Bali's unique charm.

### ✨Tips

Most items at Ubud Market are handmade, and **bargaining is part of the local shopping culture**. Remember to negotiate with a smile and a relaxed attitude—it’s one of the most enjoyable interactions during your travels.
