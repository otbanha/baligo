### 🧶1. Handmade Textiles

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_218f0d194c432ade542611ace179bde4.jpg)

At Ubud Market, you’ll undoubtedly be drawn to **Endek**, a traditional Balinese fabric. This handwoven textile, bursting with vibrant colors and intricate patterns, is a source of pride for the Balinese. You can use it as a tablecloth, scarf, or even take it home as a decorative piece. Each piece of Endek is like a work of art, embodying the warmth and craftsmanship of Balinese culture.

### 🧣2. Sarong: Versatile and Culturally Rich Fabric

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_37f79c58c6862ad429cb1e686e6615f4.png)

The sarong is one of Bali's most iconic souvenirs. This traditional fabric is not only affordable but also serves multiple purposes, such as:

- Beach towel
- Tote bag or backpack
- Baby carrier
- Tablecloth or sofa cover
- DIY transformation into skirts, tops, or shorts

Some sarongs are made using the traditional **Batik technique**, with colors and motifs that tell local stories and cultural symbols. When shopping, ask the vendors about the meanings behind the patterns—it adds both collectible and sentimental value. This isn’t just fabric; it’s an extension of Balinese culture.

### 🪵4. Wooden Carvings: Lifelike Traditional Art

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2dbb0b57c27c67f42d3c1f9bd259fb9c.jpg)

Bali’s woodcarving craftsmanship is world-renowned, and Ubud along with Tegallalang are key hubs for this art form. From **symbols of peace, trees of life, mythological figures to elephant sculptures**, each piece is crafted by skilled local artisans, showcasing intricate details and vitality.

In addition to traditional woodcarvings, there are also collectible woodcarvings that resemble toys - both fun and stylish!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d8efdab6446270abc68c86a25529abfc.png)
