## 🤍 Day 2: A Day of Thrilling Challenges! Rafting, Off-Roading, and a Healing Massage 💦🛶🧖‍♀️

The second day's itinerary was undoubtedly the most exhilarating day of the entire trip! We arranged two of Ubud's most popular outdoor activities—rafting and ATV off-roading—perfect for friends who love adventure and want to experience the charm of nature!

### 🔹 Rafting Experience | A 2-Hour Bali Adventure on the River 🚣‍♀️

![Yi Chen Lai 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7c2a5b3b454771a11ceb77a7496135df.jpg)

Provided by Yi Chen Lai

The entire rafting activity lasts about 2 hours, passing through tropical jungles, cliffs, and waterfalls along the way—the scenery is spectacular! However, here are a few important reminders:

- **You will definitely get completely soaked** (really not "maybe," but "definitely" 😂)
- Remember to **bring a change of clothes**; the shop provides towels, but it's always best to bring your own
- It's recommended to wear **water shoes** or sandals with good coverage because you have to **descend a long 15-minute staircase** from the meeting point to reach the river, and you have to climb back up afterward—the stairs are no joke!

But the moment you jump into the cool river water after sweating is incredibly refreshing—it's like being baptized by nature～✨

## 🤍 Day 4: Market Shopping, Water Palace Stroll, Sunset Beach Club Extravaganza 🛍️🏯🌅

On our fourth day in Ubud, we planned a more relaxed and leisurely stroll, starting with market shopping, visiting the Water Palace, and then heading south to the popular Finns Beach Club. It was a fascinating day transitioning from tradition to fashion!

### 🔹 Ubud Market: A Bargain Hunter's Paradise 🧣👛

We started our morning by exploring the Ubud Market, a must-visit classic spot in Ubud filled with handicrafts, wood carvings, rattan bags, sarongs, jewelry, and more. When shopping, vendors typically quote tourist prices, so remember that bargaining is an essential skill!

### 🔹Taman Saraswati Temple: A Romantic Hidden Gem Next to the Market🌺

Right next to the Ubud Market is the stunning **Taman Saraswati Temple**, one of the few temples in Bali built over a lotus pond. While it used to be free to enter, you now need to purchase a ticket for **IDR 60,000** (about USD$4) per person and wear traditional attire before entering, so keep that in mind!
