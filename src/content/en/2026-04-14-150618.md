【Today's USD Exchange Rate: 16700】Here’s a summary of today’s (4/15) **Bali** news and practical information for tourists:

### 🗞️ Today’s Highlights: Law Enforcement & Diplomatic Updates

- **Expanded Enforcement Operation**: The Ngurah Rai Immigration Office arrested 10 foreigners suspected of violations today during the "Wira Waspada 2026" operation in areas like Kuta and Kerobokan. Those detained included Russians involved in online prostitution, Nigerians and Ugandans with forged documents, and several visa overstayers. Authorities emphasized zero tolerance for illegal activities and encouraged the public to report via hotlines.
- **Resolution of South Korea Travel Advisory Controversy**: Following the controversy over South Korea’s inclusion of Bali in its travel advisory due to safety concerns, the South Korean embassy officially apologized today, clarifying it was a communication error. The advisory has been revised to emphasize Bali’s safe travel environment.
- **Cross-Border Crime Crackdown**: Police held a press conference in Denpasar today, announcing the bust of an international cocaine smuggling network linked to online operations, showcasing their commitment to combating transnational crime.

### 🌤️ Weather Forecast

- **Today’s Overview**: Partly cloudy with temperatures ranging between **26°C - 32°C** in lowland areas.

### 📄 Topics of Interest for Tourists: Flights & Safety

- **Volcanic Ash Cloud Relief**: Flight delays caused by the eruption of Mount Lewotobi have significantly improved. International flights, including Virgin Australia, Jetstar, Singapore Airlines, and Malaysia Airlines, have **resumed normal operations** today. Two affected island airports have also reopened.
- **Entry Reminder**: Strict checks are currently in place at airport customs, particularly for the **IDR 150,000 tourism tax** and **e-CD electronic customs declaration** QR codes.
- **Crowd Alert**: Tourist arrivals have surged recently, with nearly 1 million visitors recorded as of 4/10. Airport transfers and traffic congestion in popular areas are severe, so travelers are advised to allow ample time when heading from the airport to southern accommodation zones.
