【Current USD Exchange Rate: 16700】Today's (4/15) news and practical information for tourists in **Bali** are summarized below:

### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Law Enforcement and Diplomatic Developments

- **Expanded Crackdown Operation**: The Ngurah Rai Immigration Office today arrested 10 foreigners suspected of violations during the "Wira Waspada 2026" operation in Kuta, Kerobokan, and other areas. Among them were Russians involved in online prostitution, Nigerians and Ugandans with forged documents, and several visa overstayers. Authorities emphasized zero tolerance for illegal activities and encouraged the public to report via hotlines.
- **Resolution of South Korea Travel Advisory Controversy**: Regarding the earlier controversy where South Korea included Bali in its travel advisory due to security concerns, the South Korean embassy today formally apologized and clarified it as a communication error. The advisory has been revised to emphasize that Bali remains a safe travel destination.
- **Cross-border Crime Bust**: Police held a press conference in Denpasar today, announcing the bust of a cocaine smuggling case linked to an international network, demonstrating their commitment to combating transnational crime.

### 🌤️ Weather Forecast 

- **Today's Overview**: Partly cloudy, with temperatures ranging between **26°C - 32°C** in the lowlands.

### 📄 Topics of Concern for Tourists: Flights and Safety

- **Volcanic Ash Cloud Relief**: Flight delays caused by the eruption of Mount Lewotobi have significantly improved. International flights, including Virgin Australia, Jetstar, Singapore Airlines, and Malaysia Airlines, have **resumed normal operations** today. The two affected island airports have also reopened.
- **Entry Reminder**: Currently, airport customs inspections are strict, particularly regarding the **IDR 150,000 tourism tax** and the QR Code for **e-CD electronic customs declaration**.
- **Crowd Warning**: The number of incoming tourists has surged recently, with data from 4/10 showing nearly 1 million arrivals. Airport transfers and traffic congestion on popular routes are severe, so it is recommended to allow ample time when traveling from the airport to southern accommodation areas.
