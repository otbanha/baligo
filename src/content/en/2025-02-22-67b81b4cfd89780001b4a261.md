## Family Travel Tips

### 1. Climate and Attire

Sanur, located in the southeastern part of Bali, enjoys a warm and pleasant climate. During the summer months (June to August), it's the dry season with plenty of sunshine and refreshing sea breezes. However, during winter holidays, Bali enters its rainy season, and brief showers are common. It's advisable to prepare the following for children:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hat and sunglasses
- High SPF sunscreen (especially for beach activities)
- Comfortable sandals or flip-flops (ideal for beach outings)
- Rain gear/raincoat (to stay dry during light showers)
- Insect repellent (essential during the rainy season when mosquitoes are more prevalent)
If you plan to visit nearby attractions (such as Mount Batur or Ubud), pack a light jacket as temperatures can vary significantly between morning and evening.

***

### 2. Health and Safety

- **Sun Protection & Bug Repellent**: The beaches in Sanur have intense sunlight, so it's recommended to reapply sunscreen regularly. While Sanur has fewer mosquitoes, it's still wise to carry insect repellent.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly. Opt for bottled water or use boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Pack essential medications like fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness tablets, and band-aids. For reference: [What essential medicines should you bring for a trip to Bali, Indonesia? How to buy insect repellent and sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/) Sanur city center is home to Bali's largest and newest international hospital.

***

### 3. Transportation and Getting Around

Sanur offers relatively convenient transportation. It's recommended to book reputable private car services and prepare safety seats for younger children. The town's flat roads are perfect for walking or cycling, with many hotels providing complimentary bike rentals. For families with young children, choosing attractions closer to your accommodation can help avoid lengthy car rides.

***

### 4. Dining Options

Sanur offers a diverse range of dining options, from local delicacies to international cuisine. Recommendation:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned foods.
- Many restaurants offer kids' menus, and opting for international chain restaurants can ensure the flavors are suitable for children.

***

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

Although Sanur is more modernized, it still preserves Bali's traditional culture. Teach children to respect local customs, such as:

- Wear a sarong and maintain silence when entering temples.
- Do not touch religious artifacts or statues without permission.

## Top 10 Family-Friendly Activities in Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5e82dc6665e39c659dd66b7a031d7c99.jpg)

## Frequently Asked Questions about Sanur

**Is Sanur Suitable for Traveling with Children?**

Sanur is an ideal destination for family vacations, featuring calm beaches, family-friendly hotels, and a variety of outdoor activities such as cycling, sand play, and cultural experiences.

**What other kid-friendly activities are available in Sanur?**

We've compiled dozens of kid-friendly activities in Sanur, including snorkeling, visits to turtle conservation centers, and hands-on craft experiences. Click [here](/blog/2024-02-11-65bf2f75fd89780001dbf162/) for detailed information.

**Do accommodations in Sanur have water slides?**

Some family resorts offer water slides, perfect for kids who love playing in the water. We've selected a few family-friendly hotels with water slides. Click [here](/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/) to learn more.

**What's the weather like in Sanur? What clothes should I pack?**

Sanur enjoys warm and humid weather year-round, with average daytime temperatures ranging from 27-32°C. It's recommended to pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sun protection, hats, and rain gear to prepare for possible afternoon thunderstorms.

**Is the food in Sanur safe for children?**

Sanur boasts a variety of restaurants offering both international cuisine and local dishes, with some establishments providing children's menus. It's advisable to choose restaurants with good reviews and high hygiene standards. Always opt for bottled water and avoid consuming raw or undercooked foods.

**Is Sanur convenient for transportation? Is it suitable for traveling with children?**

Sanur offers convenient transportation options, primarily relying on taxis, ride-hailing services like Grab, and private car hires. For families traveling with children, we recommend opting for private car services to ensure a more comfortable journey. You can also request child safety seats in advance.

**Are there medical facilities in Sanur? What if my child gets sick?**

Sanur has several clinics and pharmacies suitable for addressing minor ailments. For more advanced medical services, you can visit the newly opened international hospital, Bali International Hospital, located in the Sanur area of Bali, which is expected to officially open by the end of 2025. The hospital is situated within The Sanur Special Economic Zone, a 41.26-hectare integrated health and tourism area designed to provide world-class medical services and wellness experiences.

**Do attractions in Sanur require entrance fees? Are there discounts for children?**

Most public beaches in Sanur are free to access, but some cultural attractions or activities may require tickets. Typically, children under 6 can enter for free, while those aged 6-12 may qualify for discounted tickets. It's recommended to confirm details in advance.

**Is Sanur stroller-friendly?**

Sanur's streets and coastal walkways are relatively flat, making them stroller-friendly, especially along Sanur Beach Walk, where you can easily push a stroller. It's perfect for family strolls and leisurely sightseeing.

**Must-Visit Attractions Around Sanur**
