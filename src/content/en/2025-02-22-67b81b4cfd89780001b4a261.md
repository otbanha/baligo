## Family Travel Tips

### 1. Climate and Clothing

Located in the southeastern part of Bali, Sanur enjoys warm and pleasant weather. During the summer months (June to August), it’s the dry season with plenty of sunshine and cool sea breezes. However, during the winter months, Bali is in the rainy season, so brief showers are common. Here’s what to pack for your kids:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hats and sunglasses
- High-SPF sunscreen (especially for beach activities)
- Comfortable sandals or flip-flops (ideal for beach outings)
- Rain gear or raincoats (to stay dry during light showers)
- Insect repellent (mosquitoes are more active during the rainy season)
If you plan to visit nearby attractions like Mount Batur or Ubud, bring a light jacket as temperatures can drop in the mornings and evenings.

***

### 2. Health and Safety

- **Sun Protection and Mosquito Repellent**: The beach areas in Sanur are intensely sunny, so it's recommended to reapply sunscreen regularly. Although Sanur has fewer mosquitoes, it's still advisable to carry insect repellent.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly; opt for bottled water or boiled water instead.
- **Medical Preparedness**: Bring common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness medicine, and band-aids. For more details, check out: [Essential Medications for Bali Travel: What to Bring? How to Buy Mosquito Repellent and Sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/) Sanur city has Bali's largest and newest international hospital.

***

### 3. Transportation and Getting Around

Transportation in Sanur is relatively convenient. It's recommended to book reputable private car services and prepare safety seats for younger children. The streets in Sanur are flat, making it suitable for walking or cycling, and many hotels offer free bike rentals. If traveling with young children, choose attractions closer to your accommodation to avoid long drives.

### 4. Dining Options

Sanur offers a diverse range of dining options, from local cuisine to international dishes. Recommendations:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned foods.
- Many restaurants offer kids' menus, and international chain restaurants are also a safe bet for child-friendly meals.

***

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

Although Sanur is more modernized, it still retains Bali's traditional culture. Teach children to respect local customs, such as:

- Wear a sarong and maintain silence when entering temples.
- Do not touch religious items or statues without permission.

## Sanur Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Is Sanur suitable for traveling with children?**

Sanur is a family-friendly destination with calm beaches, child-friendly hotels, and a variety of outdoor activities such as cycling, playing in the sand, and cultural experiences.

**What other activities are suitable for children in Sanur?**

We’ve compiled dozens of activities in Sanur that are perfect for kids, including snorkeling, visits to turtle conservation centers, and hands-on craft experiences. Click [here](/blog/2024-02-11-65bf2f75fd89780001dbf162/) for detailed information.

**Do accommodations in Sanur have water slides?**

Some family-friendly resorts offer water slides, perfect for kids who love water play. We've curated a selection of family hotels with water slides—click [here](/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/) to learn more.

**What is the weather like in Sanur? What should I pack?**

Sanur enjoys warm and humid weather year-round, with daytime temperatures averaging 27-32°C. It's recommended to pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sunscreen, a hat, and rain gear to prepare for possible afternoon thunderstorms.

**Is the food in Sanur safe for children?**

Sanur offers a variety of restaurants serving international cuisine and local dishes, with some establishments providing kids' menus. It's advisable to choose well-reviewed restaurants with good hygiene standards and to drink bottled water while avoiding raw or undercooked foods.

**Is transportation convenient in Sanur? Is it suitable for traveling with children?**

Sanur has convenient transportation options, primarily relying on taxis, ride-hailing services like Grab, and private car hires. For families with children, hiring a private car is recommended for a more comfortable travel experience, and you can request a child safety seat in advance.

**Are there medical facilities in Sanur? What if my child gets sick?**

Sanur has several clinics and pharmacies suitable for minor ailments. For more advanced medical care, the newly opened Bali International Hospital, located in Sanur, is expected to officially launch by the end of 2025. Situated within the Sanur Special Economic Zone, a 41.26-hectare integrated health and tourism area, the hospital aims to provide world-class medical services and wellness experiences.

**Do attractions in Sanur require entrance fees? Are there discounts for children?**

Most public beaches in Sanur are free to access, but some cultural attractions or activities require tickets. Children under 6 usually enter for free, while those aged 6-12 may qualify for discounted tickets. It's recommended to confirm details in advance.

**Is Sanur stroller-friendly?**

Sanur's streets and coastal walkways are relatively flat, making them stroller-friendly, especially along the Sanur Beach Promenade. It's an easy push, perfect for family strolls and leisurely sightseeing.

**Must-visit attractions near Sanur**

From Sanur, a 30-minute speedboat ride will take you to two major islands off Bali's east coast. Check out the [Nusa Penida Guide](/blog/2023-08-16-64db6b82fd897800013a9942/) / [Nusa Lembongan Guide](/blog/2024-10-23-66adfb9afd89780001f62df1/)
