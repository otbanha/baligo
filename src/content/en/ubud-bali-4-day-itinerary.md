Ubud is an essential stop for anyone visiting Bali. This YouTube influencer [[Jits into the Sunset](https://www.youtube.com/@JitsIntoTheSunset)] shares videos online introducing this cultural hub.

## Arriving in Ubud

Upon arriving in Ubud, they first dined at Wild Fire, a highly-rated vegetarian restaurant. The serene ambiance and vibrant dishes, especially the signature Gado-Gado salad with peanut sauce over fresh vegetables and legumes, left a refreshing and appetizing impression, making them instant regulars.

## Sacred Monkey Forest

The next morning, they ventured to Ubud's most famous attraction - the Sacred Monkey Forest. Home to over 700 wild monkeys, they had the rare opportunity to observe these agile and adorable creatures up close, watching them play and interact in ways reminiscent of our ancestors. However, an amusing incident occurred: while they were engrossed in watching the monkeys, a mischievous little monkey opened their backpack and stole a wallet containing cash and bank cards! Fortunately, staff arrived promptly, lured the monkey away with food, and safely retrieved the wallet. This unexpected event added a thrilling and unforgettable twist to their adventure.

## Countryside Stroll

After leaving the monkey forest, they followed a small path into the countryside surrounding Ubud. The scenic route was filled with lush, green landscapes exuding peace and tranquility. They eventually reached a serene rice field, feeling as though they had stepped into a paradise, reminiscent of their travels in Costa Rica.

## Art Market

Strolling through Ubud not only offers natural scenery but also immerses you in the heart of Bali's art and culture. They explored the bustling art markets, admiring various handicrafts like wood carvings, silk, and ceramics. The wife experienced the most relaxing massage of her life at a temporary massage pavilion, completely rejuvenating her body and mind.

## Purification at Tirta Empul Temple

Their final stop was Tirta Empul, Bali's most sacred water temple. Visiting on a full moon night, they witnessed a large number of local devotees coming to bathe and purify themselves, showcasing unique Hindu traditions. In this thousand-year-old historical site, they were deeply moved by the devout atmosphere and touched by the temple's hospitality, which included offering free candies and fruits.
