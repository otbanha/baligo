**Bottom line**: A recommended 4-day Ubud itinerary—dine at the vegetarian restaurant Wild Fire (try their signature Gado-Gado salad), visit the Sacred Monkey Forest (**watch your belongings; monkeys may snatch items from your bag**—buy tickets in advance to skip the queue), and take a leisurely stroll through the countryside around the Campuhan Ridge Walk. With a relaxed pace, you can enjoy four full days without switching hotels.

Ubud is a must-see for every Bali traveler. This YouTube influencer [[Jits into the Sunset](https://www.youtube.com/@JitsIntoTheSunset)] shares videos online that introduce this cultural hub.

## Arriving in Ubud

Upon arriving in Ubud, they first dined at Wild Fire, a highly-rated vegetarian restaurant. The setting is serene, the food is vibrant, and their signature Gado-Gado salad—fresh vegetables and beans drizzled with peanut sauce—is refreshing and appetizing, winning them over instantly.

## Sacred Monkey Forest

The next morning, they headed to Ubud's most famous attraction—the Sacred Monkey Forest. Home to over 700 wild monkeys, it offers a rare chance to observe these agile, adorable creatures up close as they chase, play, and interact, vividly displaying instincts not far from our own. But a funny incident occurred: while they were focused on the monkeys, a cheeky little monkey opened their backpack and stole a wallet containing cash and bank cards! Luckily, a staff member quickly lured the monkey away with food and safely retrieved the wallet. It made for a surprising and unforgettable travel experience.

## Countryside Stroll

Leaving the monkey forest, they followed a path into the countryside around Ubud. The scenery was lovely, with lush green rice fields all around, exuding a peaceful and leisurely vibe. They eventually reached a quiet rice paddy that felt like a hidden paradise, reminding them of their previous trip to Costa Rica.

This path, known as the Campuhan Ridge Walk, is covered in a dedicated article: [Just a short walk from Ubud center lies a paradise like this? An introduction to the Campuhan Ridge Walk](/en/blog/ubud-campuhan-ridge-walk/)

## Art Market

Strolling through Ubud not only offers stunning natural scenery but also places you at the heart of Bali's art and culture. They explored the bustling art market, admiring a variety of handcrafted items like wood carvings, silk, and ceramics. The wife even experienced the most relaxing massage of her life at a temporary massage pavilion, completely unwinding both body and mind.

## Purification at Tirta Empul

For their final stop, they visited Bali's most sacred temple, Tirta Empul. It was a full moon night, and the temple was filled with local devotees coming to bathe and purify themselves, showcasing the unique Hindu faith. In this thousand-year-old heritage site, they were deeply moved by the devout atmosphere and touched by the temple's warm hospitality, offering free candies and fruits.
