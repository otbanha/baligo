Indonesia's Minister of Tourism recently announced plans to push for legislative changes that would restore visa-free entry for visitors from 20 countries that frequently visit Indonesia. This news has sparked widespread attention, especially among frequent visitors to Bali, who are eagerly anticipating this change.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_df4c423f689ad952426bcbfc63faea8f.png)

### The History of Indonesia's Tourist Visa

For many years, visitors from Australia, New Zealand, the United States, most European countries, as well as Taiwan and China, could enjoy visa-free entry to Indonesia for stays of up to 30 days. However, since Indonesia reopened its borders after the pandemic, visitors from these countries have had to pay a visa fee of IDR 500,000, which has been an additional expense for many frequent travelers.

### Current Visa Policy

Currently, Indonesia offers a 30-day visa, which visitors can apply for upon arrival or through an electronic visa application. This policy allows visitors from 97 countries to engage in tourism, business meetings, and visiting family and friends.

### The Impact of Visa Fees on Travelers

The increase in visa fees has forced many travelers to consider this additional expense when planning their trips. This not only affects travel budgets but also influences visitors' overall impression of Bali. Many travelers have expressed that if the visa-free policy is reinstated, they would visit Indonesia more frequently.

### Potential Changes to the Visa-Free Policy

Tourism and Creative Economy Minister Sandiaga Uno has proposed a visa-free policy aimed at reinstating visa-free entry for visitors from countries including Australia, Taiwan, China, Japan, South Korea, the United States, the United Kingdom, India, France, Germany, Qatar, the UAE, Saudi Arabia, the Netherlands, Russia, New Zealand, Italy, and Spain. This policy will allow tourists from these countries to once again enjoy visa-free travel without paying visa fees.

### Potential Economic Impact

The reinstatement of the visa-free policy could significantly boost Indonesia's tourism revenue. More tourists mean increased spending, which will undoubtedly stimulate the local economy. Particularly for tourist hotspots like Bali, the rise in tourism income will drive the development of related industries.

### Predictions from Tourism Experts

Tourism experts believe that if the visa-free policy is implemented, the number of visitors to Bali will increase significantly. This will not only aid in the recovery of the local economy but also enhance Bali's competitiveness in the international tourism market.

### Travelers' Preparation for the New Policy

For travelers planning to visit Bali, understanding and adapting to the new visa policy is crucial. Adjusting travel budgets and preparing necessary documents in advance will help ensure smooth entry procedures.

### Bali Tourism Tax

In addition to visa fees, tourists are required to pay a Bali tourism tax of IDR 150,000. This fee is primarily used for environmental protection, cultural heritage preservation, and improving tourism infrastructure. Visitors can pay this tax upon arrival at the airport or through the Love Bali app and website.

### Challenges in Policy Implementation

Although the Minister of Tourism and Creative Economy has proposed a visa-free policy, its final implementation still faces numerous challenges. Government departments need to coordinate various interests to ensure the smooth rollout of the policy.

### Traveler Opinions and Reactions

Currently, most tourists welcome the reinstatement of the visa-free policy. They believe it will significantly reduce travel costs, allowing them to visit Bali more frequently. Many travelers have already started adjusting their travel plans to accommodate potential policy changes.

### Conclusion

Overall, the reinstatement of the visa-free policy will be a major boost for Bali's tourism industry. It will not only help attract more international visitors but also promote local economic development. In the future, as the policy is further implemented, Bali is expected to experience a new peak in tourism.
