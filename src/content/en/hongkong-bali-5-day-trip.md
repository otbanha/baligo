**Key Highlights**: This 5-day, 4-night itinerary from Hong Kong goes: **Day 1 Canggu (Tanah Lot) → Day 2 Jimbaran & South Bay (seafood sunset dinner) → Day 3 Uluwatu & Ubud → Day 4 Ubud → Day 5 Canggu**, perfect for travelers from Hong Kong and Macau who want to experience both the west coast and southern hotspots of Bali in one trip.

"[Hea Travel & Food](https://www.youtube.com/@hea_travel)" is a YouTuber from Hong Kong who frequently shares food and travel videos on her channel. This time, she traveled to Bali, Indonesia, and brought us an exciting 5-day travel guide.

## Day 1: Canggu

![Hea遊美食在海神廟欣賞了壯麗的海景，並了解了當地宗教文化](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_852a221e388b61983529c8eb8f87d3e2.jpg)

Hea Travel & Food admired the stunning sea views at Tanah Lot and learned about the local religious culture.

Her first stop was Tanah Lot Temple in Canggu, Bali. Built on the coast, this temple is said to be the home of the sea god. She shared a folk tale about Tanah Lot: long ago, a fisherman went out to sea and encountered a fierce storm. His boat was swallowed by giant waves, and he went missing. Worried for his safety, the villagers prayed sincerely. Suddenly, the temple's main gate opened, revealing a secret passage leading to the ocean floor. Believing this was a sign from the sea god, the villagers passed through the tunnel and eventually found the fisherman at the bottom of the sea. After returning to the village, the fisherman shared the beauty he had seen underwater. From then on, Tanah Lot Temple in Bali has been regarded as a sacred place connected to the underwater world.

Pura Tirta Empul, the Holy Water Temple, is one of the six most sacred temples in Bali and an important religious site. Hea Travel Food received a holy water blessing here, praying for good luck and blessings.

> Editor's note: In Ubud, besides the well-known Pura Tirta Empul, there are two other holy spring temples tucked away in quiet corners, all within easy reach, waiting for explorers to discover.

> Here are two other holy spring temples we recommend:

> **Dalem Pingit Holy Spring** is a relatively secluded temple. Not only does it feature unique architecture, but its sacred spring is also deeply revered by local devotees. Dalem Pingit is especially known for its beautiful waterfall-fed holy spring.

> **Pura Gunung Kawi Holy Spring** is a temple renowned for its rock carvings. In addition to ancient statues, it also hides a sacred spring where devotees and visitors can bathe to receive blessings.

> These two distinct holy spring temples each offer their own unique charm and cultural depth, while providing a relatively serene bathing environment where you can find peace and purification in Bali's sacred atmosphere.

> Hea遊美食's description of Bali:

> Bali is an island in Indonesia, located in the eastern part of the country. It boasts many famous attractions, including Tanah Lot Temple, Tirta Empul Temple, Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, Ubud Palace, Ubud Market, rice terraces, volcanoes, and stunning beaches and resorts. There are direct flights from Hong Kong to Bali with Cathay Pacific or Hong Kong Airlines, taking about 5 hours.
> Let me introduce what you can do in Bali! First, Bali offers breathtaking natural landscapes. Its coastline is dotted with beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters, making it a paradise for vacations. You can relax under the sun, enjoy beach activities like surfing, kayaking, or diving, and explore the fascinating underwater world.
> Additionally, Bali boasts majestic mountains and lush greenery. You can join captivating hikes through rice paddies, jungles, and waterfalls to admire Bali's unique natural beauty.
> Bali's cultural heritage is also one of its attractions. The island is rich in traditional Indonesian religion and art, with numerous temples, palaces, and traditional art performances. You can visit stone and wood carving workshops, enjoy Balinese dance and music performances, and experience the local culture.
> Moreover, Bali has a thriving yoga and spiritual tourism community. You can take yoga classes, meditation workshops, or visit spiritual temples to seek inner peace and balance.
> Of course, Bali also offers abundant food and shopping experiences. You can taste local Balinese cuisine like Babi Guling (roast pork) and grilled seafood, and explore local arts and crafts such as traditional textiles, wood carvings, and silver jewelry.
> In summary, Bali is a travel destination with stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and a relaxing atmosphere. You can engage in various activities and experiences—whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or cultural exploration, Bali can meet your expectations.
