When traveling in Bali, heat exhaustion (Heat Exhaustion/Indonesian: Kelelahan panas) is quite common due to the hot and humid climate. Besides the standard advice of seeking shade and drinking plenty of water, Bali has some unique local remedies to help you recover quickly:

## 1. Local "Cooling" Drinks

Bali has many traditional herbal drinks (called Loloh) that are highly effective for cooling down and rehydrating:

- **Loloh Cemcem (Herbal Drink):** This is Bali's most authentic cooling drink, especially in Ubud or Penglipuran. It’s green in color, with a sweet, sour, and slightly salty taste, made from **Cemcem leaves** (a local herb), palm sugar, cinnamon, and tamarind. Locals believe it effectively relieves "internal heat" (Panas Dalam).
- **Kelapa Muda (Fresh Coconut Water):** This is the easiest-to-find natural electrolyte. Opt for "green coconut" (Kelapa Hijau), which has lower sugar content and is rich in electrolytes. After drinking, ask the vendor to scrape out the coconut flesh for you—it has a moisturizing effect.
- **Jamu Kunyit Asam (Turmeric Tamarind Drink):** This bright yellow herbal drink is available in supermarkets and roadside stalls. Turmeric has anti-inflammatory properties, while tamarind helps cool the body.

## 2. Local Food Remedies

- **Bubur Kacang Hijau (Mung Bean Porridge):** This warm or iced mung bean porridge is commonly sold at local warungs (roadside stalls). Mung beans are widely recognized in tropical countries as a cooling food that helps detoxify and promote urination.
- **Tamarind Candy:** If you feel slightly nauseous or dizzy, eating a tamarind candy can stimulate saliva production and alleviate discomfort.

## 3. Unique Local Therapies

- **Boreh Herbal Body Wrap:** While traditional Boreh is more warming and detoxifying, some SPAs offer "Cooling Boreh," which uses cucumber, aloe vera, or sandalwood to quickly cool down overheated skin and repair sunburn.
- **Balinese Massage:** After mild heatstroke, blood circulation may become sluggish. Once your body temperature has slightly dropped, a gentle massage can help relax tense muscles (but avoid it if you’re still running a high fever).

## 4. Modern Bali Travel Solution: IV Drip Therapy

In Bali's tourist areas (such as Canggu, Seminyak, or Ubud), "IV Therapy" has become very popular. If you're severely dehydrated and extremely fatigued, professional hospitals like **BIMC** or **Siloam** even offer [home visit services](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1197571&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F170717-24-7-doctor-on-call-bali-at-unicare-clinic-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Def8fdeaa30), where doctors will administer an IV drip containing saline, vitamin C, and electrolytes directly to you. This can significantly alleviate heatstroke symptoms within an hour.

### 💡 Emergency Guide

If you experience **confusion, a body temperature exceeding 40°C, or inability to sweat**, this could indicate severe heatstroke. Seek medical attention immediately.

## Can You Get a Massage or Spa After Heatstroke?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1120e315e89abf9baad7f4e4f333bee6.png)

Whether you can get a massage or spa treatment after heatstroke depends on your current **physical condition** and the **type of treatment**. In Bali's tropical climate, incorrect massage techniques may worsen heatstroke symptoms, so extra caution is needed.

Here are detailed recommendations for massage and spa treatments after heatstroke:

### 1. When Should You Absolutely Avoid Massage?

If you're in the **acute phase** of heatstroke and experiencing the following symptoms, avoid massage at all costs:

- **Fever or high body temperature:** Massage promotes blood circulation and accelerates metabolism, which can further increase body temperature and worsen heat exhaustion.
- **Severe dehydration, dizziness, or vomiting:** Your body is extremely weak at this point, and even mild stimulation from massage may cause blood pressure fluctuations, leading to fainting.
- **Severe sunburn:** Bali's intense sunlight often causes sunburn alongside heatstroke. Massaging damaged skin can cause excessive friction, leading to pain or inflammation.

### 2. Post-Recovery (After Normal Body Temperature Returns) Massage Recommendations

Once you've rested in a cool place, rehydrated with electrolytes, and your body temperature has returned to normal, you may opt for a SPA session, but keep these precautions in mind:

- **❌ Avoid Heat Therapies:** Absolutely avoid entering **saunas**, **steam rooms**, or undergoing **hot stone massages**.

- **❌ Steer Clear of Traditional Boreh:** Traditional **Bali** Boreh herbal wraps typically contain 'warming' ingredients like ginger and cloves, aimed at expelling cold, which would only exacerbate heatstroke symptoms.

- **✅ Opt for 'Cooling' Treatments:** Look for wraps based on **Aloe Vera**, **Cucumber**, or **Sandalwood** (Cooling Wrap). These help physically cool down the skin and replenish moisture.

- **✅ Gentle Acupressure:** Light massage on the head or neck can alleviate headaches caused by heatstroke, but ensure the pressure is very gentle.
