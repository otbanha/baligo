## 1. Local Cooling Drinks

Bali has many traditional herbal drinks (called Loloh) that are highly effective for cooling down and rehydrating:

- **Loloh Cemcem (Herbal Drink):** This is Bali's most authentic cooling remedy, especially popular in Ubud or Penglipuran. It’s green in color, with a sweet, sour, and slightly salty taste, made from **Cemcem leaves** (a local herb), palm sugar, cinnamon, and tamarind. Locals believe it effectively relieves "internal heat" (Panas Dalam).
- **Kelapa Muda (Fresh Coconut Water):** This is the easiest natural electrolyte source. Opt for "green coconut" (Kelapa Hijau), which has lower sugar content and high electrolytes. After drinking, ask the vendor to scrape out the coconut flesh, which has a moisturizing effect.
- **Jamu Kunyit Asam (Turmeric Tamarind Drink):** This bright yellow herbal drink is available in supermarkets and roadside stalls. Turmeric has anti-inflammatory properties, while tamarind helps cool the body.

## 2. Local Food Remedies

- **Bubur Kacang Hijau (Mung Bean Porridge):** Warungs (local food stalls) often serve this porridge, either warm or iced. Mung beans are widely recognized in tropical countries as a cooling food that helps detoxify and promote urination.
- **Tamarind Candy:** If you feel slightly nauseous or dizzy, sucking on tamarind candy can stimulate saliva production and ease discomfort.

## 3. Unique Local Therapies

- **Boreh Herbal Body Wrap:** While traditional Boreh is more about warming and detoxifying, some SPAs offer "Cooling Boreh" using cucumber, aloe vera, or sandalwood. Applying it to overheated skin can quickly cool and repair sunburn.
- **Balinese Massage:** After mild heatstroke, blood circulation may worsen. Once your body temperature has slightly dropped, a gentle massage can relax tense muscles (avoid if you’re running a high fever).

- **Boreh 藥草敷體：** 雖然傳統的 Boreh 偏向溫熱排濕，但有些 SPA 提供「Cooling Boreh」，使用黃瓜、蘆薈或檀香，敷在發燙的皮膚上能快速降溫並修復曬傷。
- **Balinese Massage（峇里島式按摩）：** 輕微中暑後，血液循環可能變差。等體溫稍降後，進行一次輕柔的按摩可以放鬆緊繃的肌肉（但若正處於高燒狀態請避免）。

## 4. Modern Bali Travel "Solution": IV Drip Therapy

In Bali's tourist areas (such as Canggu, Seminyak, or Ubud), "IV Therapy" has become increasingly popular. If you're severely dehydrated and extremely fatigued, professional hospitals like **BIMC** or **Siloam** offer services, with some Bali doctors even providing [home visits](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1197571&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F170717-24-7-doctor-on-call-bali-at-unicare-clinic-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Def8fdeaa30) to administer IV drips containing saline, vitamin C, and electrolytes. This can significantly alleviate heatstroke symptoms within an hour.

### 💡 Emergency Guide

If you experience **confusion, a body temperature exceeding 40°C, or an inability to sweat**, it could be severe heatstroke. Seek medical attention immediately.

## Can You Get a Massage or Spa After Heatstroke?

![Can You Get a Massage or Spa After Heatstroke?](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1120e315e89abf9baad7f4e4f333bee6.png)

Whether you can get a massage or spa treatment after heatstroke depends on your **current physical condition** and the **type of treatment**. In Bali's tropical climate, improper massage may worsen heatstroke symptoms, so caution is essential.

Here are detailed recommendations for massage and spa treatments after heatstroke:

### 1. When Should You "Absolutely Avoid" Massage?

If you're in the **acute phase** of heatstroke and experiencing the following symptoms, avoid massage at all costs:

- **Fever or high body temperature:** Massage promotes blood circulation and accelerates metabolism, which can further raise body temperature and exacerbate heat exhaustion.
- **Severe dehydration, dizziness, or vomiting:** Your body is extremely weak at this stage, and even mild stimulation from massage can cause blood pressure fluctuations, potentially leading to fainting.
- **Severe sunburn:** Bali's intense sunlight often accompanies heatstroke. Massaging sunburned skin can cause excessive friction, leading to pain or inflammation.

### 2. Post-Recovery (After Body Temperature Normalizes) Massage Recommendations

After resting in a cool place, rehydrating, and restoring electrolytes, and once your body temperature has returned to normal, you may opt for a SPA session, but with the following precautions:

- **❌ Avoid Heat Therapies:** Absolutely avoid entering **Saunas**, **Steam Rooms**, or undergoing **Hot Stone Massages**. 
- **❌ Steer Clear of Traditional Boreh:** Traditional **Bali** Boreh herbal wraps typically contain ingredients like ginger and cloves, which are "warming" in nature and intended to dispel cold. For someone suffering from heatstroke, this would only exacerbate the condition.
- **✅ Opt for "Cooling" Treatments:** Look for wraps based on **Aloe Vera**, **Cucumber**, or **Sandalwood** (Cooling Wrap). These help physically cool the skin and replenish moisture.
- **✅ Gentle Acupressure:** Light massage on the head or neck can alleviate headaches caused by heatstroke, but ensure the pressure is very gentle.

### 3. Special Tips for SPA in Bali

Before booking a massage in Bali, it's advisable to communicate with the therapist:

1. **Inform About Your Condition:** Clearly state that you are experiencing heat exhaustion ("I have heat exhaustion") and ask them to adjust the pressure or avoid heating rituals.
2. **Change Essential Oils:** Avoid oils with a spicy sensation. Instead, choose **Peppermint**, **Lavender**, or **Lemongrass**, which can also help cool and refresh through their aromas.
3. **Stay Hydrated:** Drink plenty of water before and after the SPA. Bali SPA shops usually offer ginger tea; after heatstroke, ask if they have **room temperature water** or **coconut water** as an alternative.

***

**In summary:** If you are still feverish or dizzy, rest first, drink coconut water, and even consider a doctor's [home visit service](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1197571&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F170717-24-7-doctor-on-call-bali-at-unicare-clinic-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Def8fdeaa30) for an IV Drip; once your strength returns, scheduling an **Aloe Vera Soothing Treatment** would be a better choice.
