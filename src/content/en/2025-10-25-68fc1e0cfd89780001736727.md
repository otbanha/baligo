## Introduction to Bali Bird Park: A Tropical Paradise of Birdsong and Blooms

Located in Gianyar Regency in central Bali, **Bali Bird Park** has been one of the island's most popular nature education destinations since its opening in the 1990s. Spanning approximately **2 hectares**, the park features lush tropical plants and water landscapes, with carefully designed ecological zones that recreate the natural habitats of birds from around the world.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9d386245f4b2e5ff72a2aa378b5a1a8a.jpg)

Currently, the park houses over **1,300 birds, representing more than 250 species**, including rare birds native to Indonesia, Africa, South America, and Australia, making it an ideal spot for observation, photography, and educational experiences.

## Eight Themed Ecological Zones

Bali Bird Park is divided into eight main habitat zones, each designed to replicate the native environment with region-specific climates and vegetation.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_aea4c2b914a235be16f57e09e219a799.jpg)

1. **Archipelago Zone**  
   Features birds from Sumatra, Kalimantan, and Java, including hornbills, golden-necked doves, and serpent eagles.
2. **Papua Rainforest Zone**  
   One of the most enchanting zones, showcasing the magnificent **Bird of Paradise**, cassowaries, and blue-crowned pigeons.
3. **Australian Outback Zone**  
   Home to lively kookaburras and rainbow lorikeets, this is a favorite interactive area for children.
4. **African Savannah Zone**  
   Displays African grey parrots, hornbills, and flamingos, alongside African wetland ecosystems.
5. **South America Zone**  
   Gathers vibrant macaws, toucans, and Amazonian jungle birds.
6. **Bali Starling Sanctuary**  
   A breeding and conservation base for Bali's national bird, the **Bali Mynah**.
7. **Bird Nursery**  
   Showcases the hatching and rearing process of birds, offering a chance to witness chicks breaking out of their shells.
8. **Komodo Experience**  
   One of the few places in Bali to observe **Komodo dragons (Varanus komodoensis)** up close.

## Must-See Star Birds

- 🕊️ **White-Crested Hornbill**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_94078312d545c3cd0dd092a4eedc6eee.jpg)

Known for its snowy white crest and glossy black body, elegant and mysterious.

- 🦉 **Eagle Owl**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f7fbcada14c7aafed7a094d8650699e5.jpg)

One of the largest owl species in the world, with prominent ear tufts that create a striking appearance.

- 🐦 **Bali Starling**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_add94c706a8547ea70a305ae78e01f0a.jpg)

A symbolic bird of Bali, characterized by its white plumage and blue eye-ring. It is critically endangered with a dwindling population.

## Education and Conservation Mission

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d4c7abe03b460c0714238ead3e94ed93.jpg)

Bali Bird Park plays a vital role in **environmental education and wildlife conservation**. The park features a research and breeding center and collaborates with various international conservation organizations to promote:

- Artificial breeding and reintroduction programs for the Bali Starling

The park regularly hosts nature education activities for children, teaching them how to care for animals and respect nature.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Can tickets be purchased on-site?**

Yes, but it's recommended to [book online for discounted tickets](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1148651&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F169-bali-bird-park-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3D8555ce542e) and fast-track entry.

**2. Can I take photos inside the park?**

Photography is allowed freely, but professional photography services within the park require an additional fee.

**3. How long does the average visit take?**

Approximately 3 to 4 hours. If including all shows, it's recommended to plan for a half-day visit.

**4. Can I bring my own food?**

No. The park has dining facilities with a wide range of options.

**5. Can I really see the Bali Starling?**

Yes. The park has a dedicated breeding area where visitors can observe this rare bird species up close.
