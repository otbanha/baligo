## Family Travel Tips

### 1. **_Climate and Clothing_**

Bali has a tropical climate, and during summer (June to August), it's the dry season with hot and intense sunlight. Prepare lightweight, breathable clothing, sun hats, sunglasses, and high-SPF sunscreen for your kids. During winter, Bali experiences the rainy season, so be prepared for occasional showers.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7c08d86c6df883cdd0f82bbf4a41a31c.png)

If you plan to hike or take a jeep to see the sunrise on a volcano, bring warm clothing regardless of the season. For more details, check out our [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/blog/2024-10-18-66d31f77fd897800011851c1/).

### 2. **_Health and Safety_**

- **_Mosquito Protection_**: Bali has many mosquitoes, so it's recommended to use local mosquito repellent and choose accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **_Drinking Water_**: Avoid tap water; opt for bottled or boiled water.
- **_Medical Preparation_**: Bring essential medications like fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness medicine, and band-aids. For more information, see: [Essential Medications and Supplies for Bali Travel](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/).

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fe90182feb7b0f11bdd7ee503a63a11.png)

### 3. **_Transportation and Getting Around_**

Bali's traffic can be chaotic, so it's recommended to book a reputable private car service and prepare child safety seats. For younger children, avoid long car rides and choose attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel time.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d7d530bfe7d4a2f4a1ffbbb9f5e99b7.jpg)

### 4. **_Dining Options_**

Bali offers a wide variety of dining options, but children's stomachs can be sensitive. It's advisable to choose restaurants with good hygiene standards and avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned dishes. For example, Bali's famous roast pig (babi guling) is often very spicy and may not be suitable for kids!

Additionally, many restaurants offer children's menus, and you can also opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the flavors are kid-friendly.

### 5. **_Cultural Etiquette_**

Bali residents are friendly and warm, but the local culture places great emphasis on etiquette. Teach your children to respect local customs, such as maintaining silence when entering temples and avoiding touching religious items.

## **_Eight Family-Friendly Activities in Seminyak:_**

Seminyak is one of Bali’s most stylish areas, perfect for families who enjoy modern amenities and beach activities.

### **_1. Surfing Adventure_**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_acd3def2c723eb7ba857e888a6b2173f.jpg)

Bali is one of the world’s best destinations for family surfing fun! In Seminyak, **_Seminyak Beach_** and **_Double Six Beach_** are not only paradise for surfers but also ideal spots for family bonding. With gentle waves and a gradual shoreline, these beaches are perfect for kids and parents to experience the thrill of surfing together!

Here, you’ll find many professional **_family-friendly surf schools_**. The instructors are not only experienced but also skilled at ensuring kids enjoy surfing safely. Schools provide equipment for all ages, from small boards for kids to professional ones for adults!

**_Family Surfing Tips:_**

1. **_Beginner Courses:_** If it’s your family’s first time surfing, sign up for beginner lessons. Coaches will start with the basics, making it easy for everyone to get started.
2. **_Family Challenges:_** Parents and kids can compete to see who can stand the longest or ride the farthest, adding fun and interaction!
3. **_Beach Games:_** If surfing gets tiring, enjoy building sandcastles or playing frisbee on the beach while soaking up the sun and sea breeze.

Both kids and adults can find their own surfing rhythm here, creating unforgettable family memories!

### 2. Bali's Aquatic Wonderland – Waterbom Bali

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4a40d018acfd204d09d0db5b3cc0c0dc.jpg)

**_Waterbom Bali_**, located about 40 minutes from Seminyak, is a massive water park and one of Asia's most popular! It's not just a great place for adults to relax but also a dream paradise for kids! Spanning **_3.8 hectares_**, the park features **_26_** meticulously designed water slides and various aquatic attractions, ensuring the whole family stays entertained all day!

**_Family Fun Highlights:_**

1. **_Thrilling Water Slides_**: For family members who love a challenge, try the high-speed slides like 'Climax' or 'Smash Down 2.0' and feel the rush of adrenaline!
2. **_Relaxing Lazy River_**: If you want to unwind, the whole family can float along the **_Lazy River_** on inflatable tubes, enjoying a leisurely drift.
3. **_Kids' Zone_**: The park also has shallow pools and mini slides specially designed for children, ensuring they have a safe and fun time!

Yes! Bali is very suitable for traveling with babies or toddlers, and you can even [hire a babysitter](/blog/2023-11-06-6544370afd89780001e1546c/).

Many restaurants provide kids' menus and family-friendly seating.

**_3. Should I bring a stroller to Bali?_**

If your child is young, it's recommended to bring a lightweight stroller, though some areas have uneven terrain where a baby carrier might be more practical.

**_4. What are Bali's medical facilities like?_**

Major cities like Kuta, Seminyak, and Sanur have international hospitals that can handle travel emergencies.
