## Family Travel Tips

### 1. **Climate and Attire**

Bali has a tropical climate, with the dry season occurring during summer vacation (June to August), characterized by hot weather and intense sunlight. It's advisable to prepare lightweight, breathable clothing, sun hats, sunglasses, and high-SPF sunscreen for children. During winter break, Bali is in the rainy season, and short showers are common, so be prepared for occasional rain when traveling.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7c08d86c6df883cdd0f82bbf4a41a31c.png)

For those planning to watch the sunrise on Mount Batur, whether hiking or taking a jeep, it's essential to prepare warm clothing for the volcano ascent. If needed, please refer to the [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/blog/2024-10-18-66d31f77fd897800011851c1/).

### 2. Health and Safety

- **Mosquito Protection**: Bali has a lot of mosquitoes, so it's recommended to use locally effective mosquito repellent and choose accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly; opt for bottled water or boiled water instead.
- **Medical Preparation**: Bring common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness medicine, and band-aids. For more details, check out: [Essential Medications and Supplies for Bali, Indonesia: How to Buy Mosquito Repellent and Sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fe90182feb7b0f11bdd7ee503a63a11.png)

### 3. Transportation and Getting Around

Bali's traffic can be quite chaotic, so it's advisable to book a reputable private car service and prepare a safety seat for your child. If your child is very young, avoid long car rides and opt for attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel time.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d7d530bfe7d4a2f4a1ffbbb9f5e99b7.jpg)

### 4. Dining Options

Bali offers a diverse range of dining options, but children's stomachs can be more sensitive. It's advisable to choose restaurants with good hygiene standards and avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned dishes. For example, Bali's famous roast pig dish (babi guling) is mostly very spicy and may not be suitable for young children!

Additionally, many restaurants offer kids' menus, and you can also opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the flavors suit your children's tastes.

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

The people of Bali are friendly and warm, but local culture places great emphasis on etiquette. Teach children to respect local customs, such as maintaining silence when entering temples and refraining from touching religious items casually.

Seminyak is one of Bali's most stylish areas, perfect for families who enjoy modern amenities and beach activities.

### **1. Surfing Adventure**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_acd3def2c723eb7ba857e888a6b2173f.jpg)

Bali is one of the world's best surfing destinations for family fun! Especially at Seminyak's **Seminyak Beach** and **Double Six Beach**, these spots are not just a paradise for surfers, but also perfect for family bonding. With gentle slopes and mild waves, it's an ideal place for kids and parents to experience the joy of surfing together!

Here, you'll find numerous professional family-friendly surfing schools. The instructors are not only highly experienced but also particularly skilled at ensuring children can enjoy the thrill of surfing in a safe environment. The schools provide surfing equipment suitable for the whole family, ranging from small boards for kids to professional boards for adults - they've got it all covered!

**Tips for Family Surfing:**

1. **Beginner Surfing Lessons**: If it's your family's first time trying surfing, you can sign up for beginner lessons. The instructor will start with basic techniques to help everyone get started easily.
2. **Parent-Child Challenge**: Parents and kids can compete together to see who can stand the longest or ride the farthest, adding fun and interaction!
3. **Beach Games**: If you get tired from surfing, you can build sandcastles or play frisbee on the beach while enjoying the sunshine and sea breeze.

Whether you're a child or an adult, everyone can find their own surfing rhythm here and create unforgettable family memories!

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

**1. Is Bali suitable for traveling with infants or toddlers?**

Yes! Bali is an excellent destination for traveling with infants or toddlers. You can even [hire a babysitter](/blog/2023-11-06-6544370afd89780001e1546c/).

**2. Do Bali restaurants offer kids' meals?**

Many restaurants offer kids' menus and family-friendly seating.

**3. Do I Need to Bring a Stroller to Bali?**

If traveling with young children, it's recommended to bring a lightweight stroller, though some areas may have uneven terrain where a baby carrier might be more practical.

**4. What are the medical facilities like in Bali?**

Major cities like Kuta, Seminyak, and Sanur have international hospitals that can handle travel emergencies.
