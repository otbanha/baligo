## Family Travel Tips

### 1. **Climate & What to Wear**

Bali has a tropical climate, and during summer (June to August), it's the dry season with hot and intense sunlight. Prepare lightweight, breathable clothing, sun hats, sunglasses, and high-SPF sunscreen for your kids. During winter, Bali experiences the rainy season, so be prepared for occasional showers.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7c08d86c6df883cdd0f82bbf4a41a31c.png)

If you plan to hike or take a jeep to see the sunrise on a volcano, bring warm clothing regardless of the season. For more details, check out our [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/blog/2024-10-18-66d31f77fd897800011851c1/).

### 2. **Health & Safety**

- **Mosquito Protection**: Bali has many mosquitoes, so we recommend using local insect repellent and choosing accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid tap water; opt for bottled or boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Bring essential medications like fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness tablets, and band-aids. For more details, check out: [Essential Medications & Supplies for Bali Travel](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fe90182feb7b0f11bdd7ee503a63a11.png)

### 3. **Transportation & Getting Around**

Bali's traffic can be chaotic, so it's recommended to book a reputable private car service and prepare child safety seats. For younger children, avoid long car rides and choose attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel time.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d7d530bfe7d4a2f4a1ffbbb9f5e99b7.jpg)

### 4. **Dining Options**

Bali offers a wide variety of dining options, but children's stomachs can be sensitive. It's advisable to choose restaurants with good hygiene standards and avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned dishes. For example, Bali's famous roast pig (babi guling) is often very spicy and may not be suitable for kids!

Additionally, many restaurants offer children's menus, and you can also opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the flavors are kid-friendly.

### 5. **Cultural Etiquette**

Bali residents are friendly and warm, but the local culture places great emphasis on etiquette. Teach your children to respect local customs, such as maintaining silence when entering temples and avoiding touching religious items.

Seminyak is one of Bali’s most stylish areas, perfect for families who enjoy modern amenities and beach activities.

### **1. Surfing Adventure**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_acd3def2c723eb7ba857e888a6b2173f.jpg)

Bali is one of the world's best destinations for family-friendly surfing! Especially at **Seminyak Beach** and **Double Six Beach**, these spots are not only a paradise for surf enthusiasts but also perfect for families to enjoy together. The gentle waves and gradual shoreline make it ideal for kids and parents to experience the thrill of surfing!

Here, you can find many professional **family-friendly surf schools**, where instructors are not only experienced but also know how to keep kids safe while having fun. The schools provide surfing gear for the whole family, from small boards for kids to professional ones for adults—everything you need!

**Family Surfing Tips:**

1. **Beginner Lessons**: If it’s your family’s first time surfing, sign up for beginner lessons. Coaches will start with the basics, making it easy for everyone to get the hang of it.
2. **Family Challenge**: Parents and kids can compete to see who can stand the longest or ride the farthest, adding some interactive fun!
3. **Beach Games**: If surfing gets tiring, you can always build sandcastles or play frisbee on the beach while enjoying the sun and sea breeze.

Both kids and adults can find their own surfing rhythm here, creating unforgettable family memories!

### 2. Bali's Aquatic Wonderland – Waterbom Bali

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4a40d018acfd204d09d0db5b3cc0c0dc.jpg)

**Waterbom Bali**, located about 40 minutes from Seminyak, is a massive water park and one of Asia’s most popular! It’s not just a great place for adults to unwind but also a dreamland for kids. Spanning **3.8 hectares**, the park features **26** meticulously designed water slides and various aquatic attractions, ensuring the whole family stays entertained all day long!

**Family Highlights:**

1. **Thrilling Slides**: For thrill-seekers, try the high-speed slides like "Climax" or "Smash Down 2.0" and feel the rush of adrenaline!
2. **Relaxing Lazy River**: If you want to take it easy, float along the **Lazy River** on inflatable tubes and enjoy a leisurely ride.
3. **Kids’ Zone**: The park also has a dedicated shallow area and mini slides for children, ensuring they can have fun safely!

Many restaurants provide kids' menus and family-friendly seating.

If your child is young, it's recommended to bring a lightweight stroller, though some areas have uneven terrain where a baby carrier might be more practical.

**4. What are the medical facilities like in Bali?**
