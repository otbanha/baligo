> The following content is adapted from fellow traveler [Sandra Yun](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/R9sqkkpWfnrB6Bzr/)'s travelogue:

This was a trip for six of us girls, chosen in September 2023 to visit Bali and Komodo Island. We were initially full of anticipation for a fulfilling holiday, but little did we know that the journey would be filled with surprises and challenges. Although it was only a short 6-day 5-night trip, and we didn't manage to cover all the planned activities, everyone had a great time. Unfortunately, on the fourth day, we all came down with gastroenteritis, which significantly reduced our stamina, and by 8:00 PM, we were already resting in bed. Despite this, the trip was still very rewarding for everyone. Below, we share some of our actual experiences, hoping they will be helpful for those planning to visit!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e63cd0875f45cb04b02c52f665b32f47.jpg)

## 1. Recommended Private Driver: Gede Arimbawa

What moved us the most during this trip, besides the beautiful scenery, was our private driver, Gede Arimbawa! He was not only thoughtful but also very kind and enthusiastic. He respected our itinerary and gave us practical advice at the right times, making our days in Bali very smooth. Whether it was reminding us of traffic times or helping us book restaurants and activities, his service was incredibly reassuring. We highly recommend such a great driver! 🥰

## 2. Airport Arrival: EVOA Fast Track

The entry process was quite smooth; we used the EVOA fast track and encountered no obstacles at all, and the customs didn't ask any questions. However, waiting for our luggage was a lengthy process, taking nearly an hour!

> Editor's Note: For the latest Bali entry procedures, please refer to [2025/08 Indonesia Bali Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Entry Card｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

## 3. Airport Wi-Fi is Terrible!

As soon as we arrived at Bali Airport, we noticed that the Wi-Fi was incredibly weak, likely due to the high number of people connected at the time. Fortunately, one of our travel companions had roaming service from Chunghwa Telecom, otherwise, we wouldn't have known how to contact our driver. If you plan to purchase a local Telkomsel SIM card, it costs around $10 to $16 USD, which includes 14GB of data. However, the signal can still be weak in some remote areas, such as the Labuan Bajo island-hopping region.

Further reading: [Essential Guide: How to Get Internet/SIM Card in Bali](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)

## 4. Traffic Conditions in Bali

During our trip, we found that traffic congestion in busy areas was really severe! Especially between 1 PM and 7:30 PM, places like Uluwatu, Ubud, and Jimbaran experienced particularly bad traffic. Although Google Maps estimates might seem short, the actual travel time often needs to be multiplied by 2.5 to 3 times. We recommend avoiding long-distance travel during these hours.

Editor's note: [Don't Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/2024-03-19-65ebd5ecfd89780001cf6c8b/)

## 5. Spa Booking Tips

If you plan to enjoy a Spa in Bali, it's best to book in advance via WhatsApp or ask your private driver to help with the booking. Especially for highly-rated Spa places, there's a high chance they'll be fully booked if you try to walk in. If you ask the driver to book for you, they might need to pay a deposit upfront, so consider carefully before canceling, otherwise, remember to return the deposit to the driver.

## 6. Island Transportation Arrangements

Since our itinerary also included island hopping, we only had three days in Bali. However, we chose to visit three different areas, resulting in spending every day stuck in traffic. If you want to explore more places, we recommend staying in the same area for 1-2 days to avoid wasting precious time on the road.

## 7. Big Price Differences for Water Activities

During our trip, we tried sea walking and discovered that buying tickets on-site was twice as expensive as getting them through our driver! The on-site menu price was around USD$58, but we only paid USD$26 through our driver. While we're not sure if this applies to all water activities, it seems that booking in advance or arranging through a driver can save you a lot.

## 8. Weather and Queuing at Rock Bar

If you're planning to visit Rock Bar, keep an eye on the weather! On the day we went, it was a bit cloudy, so we missed the sunset, but we barely had to wait in line. We arrived at 6 PM and found plenty of empty seats, allowing us to enjoy the ocean view peacefully. Even without the sunset, the stunning sea view was mesmerizing.

## 9. Observations on Tipping

In Bali, we noticed that many restaurants would return our first tip and only accept it when we offered it a second time. However, tipping housekeeping staff, porters, or activity instructors always brought big smiles!

## 10. Souvenirs: Bali Banana

If you're looking for souvenirs, Bali Banana is a great option. There are several stores in the city, but note that while there are three branches at the airport, they are located at the domestic arrival/departure areas and the international departure area outside the terminal. If you plan to buy at the airport before your flight, you'll need to walk to these locations. It's better to purchase them on your way to the airport on your last day in Kuta.

## 12. The Importance of Carrying Stomach Medicine

This bout of gastroenteritis really drove home the importance of always carrying stomach medicine when traveling! The first three days were fine, but on the fourth day, whether it was an infection or something we ate, everyone started vomiting and had diarrhea. Thankfully, we had prepared medication, and everyone bounced back the next day.

> Editor's note: These six ladies are professional nurses who brought their own prescription medication. For most people, over-the-counter medicine like what's mentioned in the video above should suffice.

## 13. Beware of motorcycle taxi scams!

In Ubud, we encountered some motorcycle taxis (motor taxis) that tried to overcharge us. We had booked two rides, but they took us to the wrong place and then charged us again when we arrived at the correct destination. This is likely the 'white plate motorcycle scam' that many travelers have mentioned. Always confirm the route and price beforehand.
