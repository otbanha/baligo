> Adapted from fellow traveler [Sandra Yun](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/R9sqkkpWfnrB6Bzr/)'s travelogue:

This was a trip for six of us girls, chosen in September 2023 to visit Bali and Komodo Island. We were initially filled with anticipation for a fulfilling holiday, but unexpectedly, the journey was full of surprises and challenges. Although it was only a short 6-day 5-night trip and we didn't manage to cover all the planned activities, everyone had a great time. Unfortunately, on the fourth day, we all came down with gastroenteritis, which drastically reduced our stamina, and by 20:00 we were already resting in bed. Despite this, the trip was still very rewarding for everyone. Below, we share some of our actual experiences, hoping to be of help to future travelers!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e63cd0875f45cb04b02c52f665b32f47.jpg)

## 1. Recommended Private Car Driver: Gede Arimbawa

What moved us the most during this trip, besides the beautiful scenery, was our private car driver, Gede Arimbawa! He was not only thoughtful but also very kind and enthusiastic. He respected our itinerary and gave practical advice at the right times, making our days in Bali very smooth. Whether reminding us of travel times or helping to book restaurants and activities, his service was incredibly reassuring. We highly recommend such a great driver! 🥰

## 2. Airport Entry: EVOA Fast Track

The entry process was quite smooth; we used the EVOA fast track and encountered no obstacles at all, and the customs didn't ask any questions. However, waiting for our luggage was a lengthy process, taking nearly an hour!

> Editor's Note: For the latest Bali entry procedures, please refer to [2025/08 Indonesia Bali Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Entry Card｜Understand the Latest Process in 1 Minute: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

## 3. Airport Wi-Fi is Terrible!

As soon as we arrived at Bali Airport, we noticed the Wi-Fi was incredibly weak, likely due to the high number of users. Fortunately, one of our travel companions had roaming service from Chunghwa Telecom, otherwise we wouldn't have known how to contact our driver. If you plan to purchase a local Telkomsel SIM card, prices range from $10 to $16 USD, including 14GB of data. However, the signal remains weak in some remote areas (like the Labuan Bajo island-hopping region).

Further reading: [Essential Bali Travel Guide: How to Get a Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)

## 4. Bali's Traffic Situation

During our trip, we found that traffic congestion in busy areas was really severe! Especially between 1 PM and 7:30 PM, places like Uluwatu, Ubud, and Jimbaran experienced particularly bad traffic. Although Google Maps estimated short travel times, the actual journey often took 2.5 to 3 times longer. We recommend avoiding long-distance travel during these hours.

> Editor's note: [Don't Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/2024-03-19-65ebd5ecfd89780001cf6c8b/)

## 5. Spa Booking Tips

If you plan to enjoy a Spa in Bali, it's best to book in advance via WhatsApp or ask your private driver to make the reservation. Especially for highly-rated Spa spots, walking in without a reservation often means no availability. If you ask the driver to book, they might need to pay a deposit upfront, so consider carefully before canceling, or remember to return the deposit to the driver.

> Further Reading: [Best SPA Recommendations in Bali, Organized by Area for Easy Reference](/blog/2023-01-22-64db6b7efd897800013a97ed/)

## 6. Island Transportation Arrangement

Since our itinerary included island hopping, we only had three days in Bali. However, we chose to visit three different areas, resulting in spending each day stuck in traffic. If you want to explore more places, we recommend staying in the same area for 1-2 days to avoid wasting precious time on the road.

## 7. Big Price Differences in Water Activities

This time we tried sea walking and discovered that buying tickets on-site was twice as expensive as getting them through our driver! The on-site menu price was around USD$58, but we only paid USD$26 through our driver. While we're not sure if this applies to all water activities, it seems that booking in advance or arranging through a driver can save you a lot.

## 8. Weather and Queue Situation at Rock Bar

If you're planning to visit Rock Bar, keep an eye on the weather! On the day we went, it was a bit cloudy, so we didn't get to see the sunset, but it meant we hardly had to queue. We arrived at 6 PM and found plenty of empty seats, allowing us to quietly enjoy the ocean view. Even without the sunset, the beautiful sea view was still mesmerizing.

## 9. Observations on Tipping

In Bali, we noticed that many restaurants would return our first tip and only accept it when we gave it a second time. However, tipping housekeeping staff, porters, or activity instructors always brought big smiles!

> Further reading: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/blog/2024-05-07-663a126bfd89780001390b63/)

## 10. Souvenirs: Bali Banana

If you're looking for souvenirs, Bali Banana is a great choice. There are several stores in the city, but it's important to note that while there are three branches at the airport, they are located at the domestic arrival/departure areas and the international departure area outside the airport. If you plan to buy them at the airport before your flight, you'll need to walk to the store. You can also purchase them on your way to the airport on your last day in Kuta.

## 12. The Importance of Carrying Stomach Medicine

This bout of gastroenteritis really drove home the importance of always carrying stomach medicine when traveling! For the first three days, we had no issues with our meals, but on the fourth day, whether it was due to an infection or something unclean we ate, everyone ended up vomiting and having diarrhea. Fortunately, we had prepared with medication, and everyone bounced back by the next day.

> Editor's note: These six ladies are professional nurses who brought their own prescription medications. For most people, over-the-counter medicines like those mentioned in the video above should suffice.

## 13. Beware of the Motorcycle Taxi Scam!

In Ubud, we encountered some motorcycle taxis (motor taxi) that were haggling over prices. We had booked two rides, but they took us to the wrong place and then charged us again when we arrived at the correct destination. This is likely the unlicensed motorcycle scam that many travelers have mentioned. Always confirm the route and price beforehand to avoid such situations.
