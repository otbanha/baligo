## What is Automated Immigration?

Beginning in 2024, applying for an electronic Visa on Arrival (eVoA) not only eliminates the need to queue for VoA (Visa on Arrival) but also bypasses the immigration stamping process! The time saved allows you to fully immerse yourself in Bali!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_afba78c72d520b7b245a856eed8aeefb.png)

Starting in January 2024, Bali implemented this automated immigration system. It is expected to add 50 more automated gates in the coming months, bringing the total to 80 units at Bali's international terminal for faster immigration processing (60 gates for arrivals; 20 for departures).

## Immigration Procedures After Landing

The standard procedure is:

> Disembark the plane ➡️ Queue for Visa on Arrival ➡️ Queue for passport inspection and stamping by immigration ➡️ Pass through immigration ➡️ Collect luggage ➡️ Show QR Code at customs ➡️ Pay tourism tax

For those who follow us and know to [apply for an electronic visa](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/) and [pay the tourism tax](/blog/2024-01-03-65955989fd89780001a95927/) in advance, their immigration process becomes:

> Disembark the plane ➡️ Pass through automated immigration ➡️ Collect luggage ➡️ Show QR Code at customs ➡️ Pay tourism tax

Not only do you skip two queues, but you also avoid potential questions or hassles from immigration officers!

## Essential Requirements for Using Automated Immigration

Does this convenient service pique your interest? To use this super-fast automated immigration clearance system, you need to meet the following two essential requirements:

1. You must possess a valid **Electronic Visa on Arrival (eVoA)**, which is a prerequisite for automated immigration clearance at Bali Airport. You can apply for and obtain this visa online before arriving in Bali to ensure a smooth clearance process.
2. You must be at least 6 years old. The automated immigration system is only applicable to passengers aged 6 and above. Passengers under 6 years old will need to be guided by customs officers to a dedicated counter for clearance.

## Don't Take Random Photos Inside the Airport

Lastly, a reminder to everyone (applicable to airports worldwide) that taking photos at immigration or customs can lead to significant trouble. It is generally not recommended to take photos in these areas, primarily due to security and privacy concerns. Airports are highly secure zones, and photography might be misinterpreted as a challenge to security measures or a potential threat. Additionally, photographing immigration or customs facilities could violate laws or regulations, especially in more sensitive areas. Maintaining respect and adhering to local laws is the best way to avoid issues, so it's best to refrain from taking photos to prevent unnecessary complications.
