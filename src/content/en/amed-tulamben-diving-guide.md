## The Perfect Blend of Bali’s Volcanic Majesty and Tranquil Shores

**Bottom line**: Amed and Tulamben, located on Bali’s northeast coast, are about a **2.5–3.5 hour drive** from the airport. These hidden gems, far from the tourist crowds, are famous for the USAT Liberty wreck dive and black sand beaches. On clear days, you can see three volcanoes—Mount Agung, Mount Abang, and Mount Batur—all at once. Perfect for a 1–2 day deep dive or serene sea-view getaway.

Nestled on Bali’s northeast coast, Amed and Tulamben offer a world apart from the bustling tourist hubs. Here, you won’t find the lively energy of Kuta or the artistic vibe of Ubud. Instead, you’ll discover a tranquil fishing village atmosphere and breathtaking natural scenery.

From Ngurah Rai International Airport, the drive takes about 2.5 to 3.5 hours, winding through villages, forests, and rice terraces—a scenic road trip in itself.

## Dive into the Underwater World, Dance with Sunrise and Sunset

What makes Amed and Tulamben most captivating is their crystal-clear waters and diverse marine life. This is a paradise for diving enthusiasts—whether you're a seasoned diver or a complete beginner, you'll find waters that suit your level.

### Scuba Diving in Tulamben

Tulamben's USAT Liberty Wreck is one of the world's most famous dive sites. With moderate depth and excellent visibility, you can dance alongside colorful tropical fish while getting up close to this historic wreck, now embraced by the ocean.

### Chasing Sunrise / Sunset / Sea Fishing with Jukung

https://youtube.com/shorts/0f-eAQ78no4

Beyond diving and snorkeling, there's one unmissable experience: heading out to sea on a traditional local fishing boat called a **Jukung**. This small wooden boat with outriggers has been used by local fishermen for generations and is the most authentic way to explore the Amed coastline.

In the early morning, many travelers choose to take a Jukung out to **chase the sunrise**. As the boat gently glides away from shore, you'll watch the sun rise slowly from the horizon, casting golden light across the sea and painting the world in soft, warm orange hues—a moment of tranquility and grandeur that's truly unforgettable.

In the late afternoon, you can experience a **Jukung sunset cruise**. Unlike the crisp morning air, the sea breeze at sunset is gentle and slightly cool. As the sun slowly sinks behind the volcano, the sky and sea are dyed in shades of pink, purple, gold, and orange—an incredibly dreamy scene.

If you're interested in fishing village culture, you can also opt for a **sea fishing experience**. Many local fishermen will take you out to sea, letting you experience their daily rhythm firsthand. After catching fish, a local restaurant can prepare them for you, giving you a true 'catch-and-cook' seafood dinner.

Due to the terrain, it's fascinating that Sunset Point faces west.

In Amed, the most popular spot to watch the sunset is undoubtedly **Sunset Point Amed Bali**. This viewpoint on a hillside offers a perfect vantage point, with an unobstructed view of the entire coastline and distant views of the volcano and small fishing village—a true 'sunset sanctuary' in the hearts of many travelers.

As the sun slowly sinks behind the volcano, the sky transforms from deep blue to shades of orange-red and violet-pink, mirrored by the sea in a dreamlike scene. With the surrounding tranquility and minimal commercial development, it's a place where you can truly quiet your mind and connect with nature.

Near the viewpoint, a few small cafés and bars let you sip a cold drink while quietly watching the sunset, enjoying a peaceful and leisurely time. Last time, Xiao Jie went to [this spot](https://www.sunsetpointamedbali.com/)—the food was just okay, but the stunning views were priceless!

If you're visiting Amed, be sure to save an evening for Sunset Point—the sunset here is absolutely worth the wait.

## Village-Style Seafood Delights

Amed and Tulamben were originally traditional fishing villages, so fresh seafood naturally takes center stage on the local menu.

### 9 Notable Restaurants in the Area (📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing))

**1. Warung Agung Amed**

A cozy family-run restaurant serving a fusion of Chinese, Indonesian, and seafood dishes. Popular recommendations include daily fresh catch, corn fritters, and fish curry, plus must-try desserts like caramelized pineapple ice cream and black rice pudding. Sometimes the owner even puts on a live music performance!

**2. Warung Enak Amed**

A budget-friendly favorite among locals and travelers alike, offering delicious Balinese pancakes, BBQ, and freshly grilled fish. The lava cake is a hidden dessert gem—recommended for solo indulgence!

**3. Restaurant Bukit Segara**

A beachfront restaurant specializing in daily fresh catches and a diverse menu of Asian and European cuisine. Prices are mid-to-high, but the service, quality, and stunning views make it a worthwhile splurge.

**4. Galanga Amed**

A cozy Asian fusion restaurant serving Balinese, Indonesian dishes, and healthy smoothie bowls. The relaxed atmosphere makes it a great spot for lunch, dinner, and dessert.

**5. di Lipah Warung**

A traditional seafood eatery known for its signature Pepe Ikan (grilled fish in coconut leaves), with highly praised desserts too. Though it lacks a view, the food and warm service leave a lasting impression.

**6. Gusto Resto Amed**

A homey restaurant with a comfortable vibe, specializing in Mediterranean and Indonesian fusion cuisine. Recommended dishes include fresh salads, rendang chicken, and seared tuna steak—perfect for group dining.

**7. Green Melon Warung**

Focused on healthy cooking with fresh ingredients at affordable prices. Try the BBQ tuna and squid dishes paired with Balinese salad, plus the adorable banana leaf cone rice. Cash only.

**8. Trattoria Amed**

An authentic Italian restaurant with a romantic atmosphere and excellent service. Their pizzas are incredibly popular, and they also feature a professional wine cellar and well-maintained restrooms—a true paradise for pizza lovers.

**9. Sails Restaurant**

Boasting stunning sea views reminiscent of a Greek island. Recommended dishes include banana leaf-wrapped fish, lamb patties, and frozen margaritas. The atmosphere is fresh, the staff are friendly, and they offer a complimentary shuttle service.

## Transportation

A special note: **most drivers based in the south are reluctant to take a one-way trip to Amed and return empty**, especially during peak season when it's even harder to find a ride. ## Essential Tips Before You Go

https://youtube.com/shorts/XS1j72uEtTY

Since Amed and Tulamben are relatively remote, with infrastructure less developed than in southern tourist areas, travelers should be well-prepared before heading there:

- **Cash is king**: ATMs are scarce in the area and may not accept foreign cards, so withdraw enough cash before diving or hiking activities.
- **Bring basic medications**: Local medical facilities are limited—pack stomach medicine, pain relievers, and insect repellent.
- **Book transportation in advance**: If you need a chartered car or driver, it's best to reserve ahead.
- **Limited internet**: Signal can be spotty in some areas, so consider getting a local SIM card or portable WiFi.
- **Protect the marine ecosystem**: Avoid touching coral and use reef-safe sunscreen to help preserve this precious ocean.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**Are Amed and Tulamben suitable for beginner divers?**

Yes, the sea conditions here are stable and the currents are gentle, making it especially suitable for beginner divers. Local dive schools also offer introductory courses.

**Can I go directly from Ubud or Kuta?**

Yes, but it's recommended to hire a private driver. The drive takes about 2.5 to 3 hours, and you can arrange stops at other attractions along the way.

**What's the best time to visit?**

The dry season (May to October) is the best time to visit, with clear skies and crystal-clear waters perfect for water activities.

**Do local restaurants offer vegetarian options?**

Most restaurants can provide vegetarian options, but the choices are limited. It's best to inform them in advance and confirm.

**What other attractions are nearby?**

If you have extra time, I recommend visiting three stunning spots in the area:

![FAQ 常見問題解答](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ed589a98d59d6d3d1de01faf3eb0b822.jpg)

- **Lempuyang Temple (The Gate of Heaven)**: Perched on a mountainside, famous for its majestic volcanic backdrop and sacred stone gate, it's a paradise for photography enthusiasts.
- **Tirta Gangga**: A former royal water palace, where stone carvings and koi fish create a mesmerizing scene amidst the pools — a perfect spot for a stroll and photos.
- **Ujung Water Palace**: An ancient palace blending European and Balinese styles, surrounded by pools and lush greenery, exuding a serene and historically rich atmosphere.
