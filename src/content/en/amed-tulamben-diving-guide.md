## The Perfect Blend of Bali's Volcanic Majesty and Tranquil Coastline

Located in Bali's northeast, Amed and Tulamben offer a world apart from the bustling tourist areas. Here, you won't find the lively buzz of Kuta or the artistic vibe of Ubud. Instead, you'll be immersed in the peaceful atmosphere of fishing villages and breathtaking natural landscapes.

![阿貢火山（Gunung Agung）  峇里島最高、最具宗教與文化意義的活火山，海拔約 3,031 公尺；上古火山殘留的 Gunung Abang，海拔約 2,151 公尺；巴杜爾火山（Gunung Batur）  是一座活火山錐，海拔約 1,717 公尺](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_38b4f9c2c400c72b9f94875f5af8aeaa.jpg)

Mount Agung: Bali's highest and most culturally significant active volcano, standing at approximately 3,031 meters; Mount Abang: a remnant of an ancient volcano, reaching about 2,151 meters; Mount Batur: an active volcanic cone, approximately 1,717 meters tall.

https://youtube.com/shorts/Hce-BK5I-NU

From this serene black sand coastline, you can take in views of three of Bali's famous volcanoes: Mount Agung, Mount Abang, and Mount Batur. When the clouds part, all three volcanoes come into view simultaneously, creating a scene straight out of a mythical world.

The journey from Ngurah Rai International Airport takes about 2.5 to 3.5 hours by car, passing through villages, forests, and rice terraces, making it a scenic road trip.

## Dive into the Underwater World and Dance with Sunrise and Sunset

The most captivating aspects of Amed and Tulamben are undoubtedly their crystal-clear waters and diverse underwater ecosystems. This area is a paradise for diving enthusiasts, offering suitable spots for both seasoned divers and beginners alike.

### Scuba Diving in Tulamben

The USAT Liberty Wreck in Tulamben is one of the world's renowned diving sites. With its moderate depth and excellent visibility, you can dance alongside colorful tropical fish while admiring this historic shipwreck embraced by the ocean.

### Jukung: Chasing Sunrises, Sunsets, and Fishing Adventures

https://youtube.com/shorts/0f-eAQ78no4

Beyond diving and snorkeling, there's one unique experience you absolutely cannot miss — sailing on a traditional **Jukung** boat. These small wooden boats with outriggers have been used by local fishermen for generations and offer the most authentic way to explore the Amed coastline.

In the early morning, many visitors choose to board a Jukung to **chase the sunrise**. As the boat slowly glides away from the shore, you'll witness the sun rising above the horizon, casting golden light across the sea. The world is bathed in soft, warm hues of orange, creating a moment of serenity and grandeur that’s unforgettable.

In the evening, you can experience a **Jukung sunset cruise**. Unlike the crisp morning air, the evening breeze is gentle and cool. As the sun slowly dips behind the volcano, it paints the sky and sea in shades of pink, purple, and gold, creating a truly magical scene.

If you're interested in fishing village culture, you can also opt for a **fishing experience**. Many local fishermen take visitors out to sea, allowing you to experience their daily rhythm firsthand. After catching fish, you can have them cooked at a local restaurant for a fresh, 'catch-to-table' seafood dinner.

### Sunset Point (📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing))

![由於地形的關係，很神奇的是Sunset Point這個角度面向西邊](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5b312da0651b1112f4c52963c74fa9ee.jpg)

Due to its unique terrain, Sunset Point offers a magical westward-facing view.

In Amed, the most popular spot to watch the sunset is undoubtedly **Sunset Point Amed Bali**. This hillside viewpoint offers an unparalleled vantage point, showcasing the entire coastline, distant volcanoes, and the quaint fishing village below. It’s a 'sunset sanctuary' for many travelers.

As the sun slowly sinks behind the volcano, the sky transforms from deep blue to shades of orange, red, and pink, reflecting beautifully on the sea. The serene surroundings, free from commercial distractions, make it a perfect place to truly unwind and connect with nature.

Near the viewpoint, there are several small cafes and bars where you can sip on a cold drink while quietly admiring the sunset, enjoying a peaceful and leisurely moment. The last time Jay visited was [here](https://www.sunsetpointamedbali.com/). The food was average, but the view was priceless!

If you come to Amed, don't forget to save an evening for Sunset Point—the sunset here is absolutely worth the wait.

## Seafood Delights

Amed and Tulamben are traditional fishing villages, so fresh seafood naturally takes center stage on the dining tables here.

### 9 Popular Restaurants in the Area (📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing))

**1. Warung Agung Amed**

A cozy family-style restaurant offering a fusion of Chinese, Indonesian, and seafood dishes. Popular recommendations include the daily fresh fish, corn fritters, and fish curry, along with must-try desserts like caramel pineapple ice cream and black rice pudding. Sometimes, the owner even performs live music!

**2. Warung Enak Amed**

A budget-friendly spot loved by both locals and tourists, serving delicious Balinese pancakes, BBQ, and freshly grilled fish. The lava cake is a hidden gem of a dessert—perfect for indulging solo!

**3. Restaurant Bukit Segara**

A seaside restaurant specializing in daily fresh catches and diverse Asian-European fusion cuisine. While prices lean towards the higher end, the exceptional service and quality, coupled with stunning views, make it a worthwhile dining investment.

**4. Galanga Amed**

A cozy Asian fusion restaurant offering Balinese, Indonesian dishes, and healthy smoothie bowls. With a relaxed atmosphere, it's a great spot for lunch, dinner, and desserts.

**5. di Lipah Warung**

A traditional seafood eatery famous for its Pepe Ikan (fish grilled in coconut leaves), with desserts also receiving high praise. Though lacking in views, the food and warm service leave a lasting positive impression.

**6. Gusto Resto Amed**

A comfortable family-style restaurant focusing on Mediterranean and Indonesian fusion dishes. Fresh salads, Rendang chicken, and pan-seared tuna steak are highly recommended, making it an excellent choice for group dining.

**7. Green Melon Warung**

Known for its healthy cooking, this spot offers fresh ingredients at affordable prices. BBQ tuna and squid dishes paired with Balinese salad are must-tries, along with the adorable banana leaf cone rice. Cash only.

**8. Trattoria Amed**

An authentic Italian restaurant with a romantic atmosphere and excellent service. Their pizzas are extremely popular, and with a professional wine cellar and well-maintained restroom facilities, it's a paradise for pizza lovers.

**9. Sails Restaurant**

Boasting stunning ocean views, it feels like a Greek island getaway. Recommended dishes include fish wrapped in banana leaves, lamb patties, and frozen margaritas. The atmosphere is refreshing, the staff is friendly, and they even offer complimentary shuttle service.

### **Café Recommendation: Rimba Café**

Rimba Café is a rare find in Ubud, combining natural aesthetics with creative light meals. Surrounded by greenery, the atmosphere is fresh and relaxing. They specialize in handmade desserts, smoothie bowls, coffee, and healthy light meals, making it a favorite among travelers and yoga enthusiasts.

The coffee here rivals city-level quality, paired with homemade baked goods like banana cake or coconut pudding, making customers return for more. They also offer vegetarian and plant-based meal options, making it an ideal spot for brunch or an afternoon relaxation.

If you're looking for a place in Amed to slow down and enjoy a good cup of coffee, **Rimba Café** is definitely a great choice.

## Transportation

Travel to Amed requires advance planning, as it is located in the northeastern corner of Bali, far from the airport or southern tourist areas (like Kuta and Seminyak), with a driving time of approximately 2.5 to 3.5 hours. It is recommended to book **private car transfers** directly.

Please note that **most drivers operating in the south are reluctant to make one-way trips to Amed and return empty**, especially during peak tourist seasons when it’s harder to find a ride. Therefore, the best approach is to hire **local drivers from Amed**, who are more willing to provide service and familiar with the area.

## Essential Pre-Trip Notes

https://youtube.com/shorts/XS1j72uEtTY

As Amed and Tulamben are relatively remote, the infrastructure is not as developed as in the southern tourist areas. Therefore, travelers should be well-prepared before departure:

- **Cash is king**: ATMs are scarce in the area, and not all accept foreign cards. It's advisable to withdraw enough cash before heading out for hiking or diving activities.
- **Bring basic medications**: Local medical facilities are limited, so carry essentials like stomach medicine, painkillers, and insect repellent.
- **Book transportation in advance**: If you need a private car or driver, it's best to arrange this ahead of time.
- **Limited internet**: Signal can be unstable in some areas, so consider getting a SIM card or portable WiFi.
- **Protect marine life**: Avoid touching coral and use reef-safe sunscreen to preserve this precious marine environment.

## FAQ

**Are Amed and Tulamben suitable for beginner divers?**

Yes, the sea conditions here are stable with gentle currents, making it particularly suitable for diving beginners. Local dive schools also offer introductory courses.

**Can I go directly from Ubud or Kuta?**

Yes, but it's recommended to hire a private car for the trip, which takes about 2.5 to 3 hours. You can also arrange stops at other attractions along the way.

**What is the best time to visit?**

The dry season (May to October) is the ideal time to visit, with sunny weather and clear waters perfect for water activities.

**Do local restaurants offer vegetarian options?**

Most restaurants can provide vegetarian dishes, but the selection is limited. It's advisable to inform them in advance and confirm.

**What other attractions are nearby?**

If you have more time, we recommend visiting these three stunning nearby attractions:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ed589a98d59d6d3d1de01faf3eb0b822.jpg)

- **Lempuyang Temple (Gates of Heaven)**: Located on the mountainside, famous for its majestic volcanic backdrop and sacred stone gates, it's a paradise for photography enthusiasts.
- **Tirta Gangga**: A former royal water garden, the stone sculptures and koi fish in the pools create a mesmerizing scene, making it an excellent spot for strolling and photography.
- **Ujung Water Palace**: An ancient palace blending European and Balinese styles, surrounded by pools and greenery, offering a serene atmosphere rich with historical ambiance.
