## The Perfect Blend of Bali's Volcanic Majesty and Serene Coastline

Located in Bali's northeast, Amed and Tulamben offer a world apart from the bustling tourist areas. Here, you won't find the lively atmosphere of Kuta or the artistic vibe of Ubud. Instead, you'll be immersed in the peaceful ambiance of fishing villages and breathtaking natural landscapes.

From this tranquil black sand coastline, you can enjoy views of three of Bali's famous volcanoes: Mount Agung, Mount Abang, and Mount Batur. When the clouds clear, all three volcanoes come into view simultaneously, creating a scene straight out of a mythical world.

The journey from Ngurah Rai International Airport takes approximately 2.5 to 3.5 hours, passing through villages, forests, and rice terraces, making for a scenic road trip.

## Dive into the Underwater World, Dancing with Sunrise and Sunset

The most captivating aspects of Amed and Tulamben are undoubtedly the crystal-clear waters and diverse underwater ecosystems. This is a paradise for diving enthusiasts, whether you're a seasoned diver or just starting out, you'll find waters that suit your level.

### Scuba Diving in Tulamben

The USAT Liberty Wreck in Tulamben is one of the world's renowned dive sites. With its moderate depth and excellent visibility, you can dance alongside colorful tropical fish while admiring this historic wreck that has been embraced by the ocean.

### Jukung Chasing Sunrise/Sunset/Fishing

https://youtube.com/shorts/0f-eAQ78no4

Besides diving and snorkeling, there's one unique experience you absolutely cannot miss — sailing on the traditional local fishing boat called **Jukung**. This small wooden boat with outriggers has been used by local fishermen for generations and is the most authentic way to explore the Amed coastline.

In the early morning, many tourists choose to board a Jukung to **chase the sunrise**. As the boat slowly glides away from the shore, you'll witness the sun gradually rising from the horizon, casting golden light across the sea, painting the world in soft warm orange hues. The tranquility and grandeur of that moment are unforgettable.

In the evening, you can experience the **Jukung sunset cruise**. Unlike the fresh morning air, the sea breeze at sunset is gentle and slightly cool. As the sun slowly sinks behind the volcano, it paints the sky and sea in shades of pink, purple, gold, and orange, creating a dreamlike scene.

If you're interested in fishing village culture, you can also opt for the **fishing experience**. Many local fishermen will take tourists out to fish, allowing you to experience their daily rhythm firsthand. After catching fish, local restaurants can prepare them for you, offering a truly 'catch-and-cook' seafood dinner.

### Sunset Point (📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing))

![由於地形的關係，很神奇的是Sunset Point這個角度面向西邊](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5b312da0651b1112f4c52963c74fa9ee.jpg)

Due to the terrain, it's fascinating how Sunset Point faces west.

In Amed, the most popular spot to watch the sunset is undoubtedly **Sunset Point Amed Bali**. This hillside viewpoint offers an unparalleled panoramic view of the coastline, with distant views of the volcano and small fishing villages, making it a 'sunset sanctuary' for many travelers.

As the sun slowly sinks behind the volcano, the sky transforms from deep blue to shades of orange, red, purple, and pink, reflecting beautifully on the sea. The serene surroundings, free from commercial hustle, make it a place where you can truly quiet your mind and connect with nature.

Near the viewpoint, there are a few small cafes and bars where you can sip on a cold drink while quietly admiring the sunset, enjoying a peaceful and leisurely moment. The last time Xiao Jie visited, he went [here](https://www.sunsetpointamedbali.com/). The food was average, but the view was priceless!

If you come to Amed, don’t forget to save an evening for Sunset Point—the sunset here is absolutely worth the wait.

## Village-Style Seafood Delights

Amed and Tulamben are traditional fishing villages, so fresh seafood naturally takes center stage on the dining table here.

### 9 Famous Restaurants in the Area (📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing))

**1. Warung Agung Amed**

A cozy family-style restaurant offering a fusion of Chinese, Indonesian, and seafood dishes. Popular recommendations include the daily fresh fish, corn fritters, and fish curry, along with must-try desserts like caramelized pineapple ice cream and black rice pudding. Sometimes, the owner even performs live music!

**2. Warung Enak Amed**

A budget-friendly spot loved by both locals and tourists, serving delicious Balinese pancakes, BBQ, and freshly grilled fish. The lava cake is a hidden gem of a dessert—perfect for indulging!

**3. Restaurant Bukit Segara**

A seaside restaurant specializing in daily fresh catches and diverse Asian-European fusion cuisine. While prices are mid-to-high range, the exceptional service, quality, and stunning views make it a worthwhile dining investment.

**4. Galanga Amed**

A cozy and intimate Asian fusion restaurant offering Balinese, Indonesian dishes, and healthy smoothie bowls. With a relaxed atmosphere, it's a great spot for lunch, dinner, or dessert.

**5. di Lipah Warung**

A traditional seafood eatery famous for its Pepe Ikan (fish grilled in coconut leaves) and well-loved desserts. Though lacking in views, the delicious food and warm service leave a lasting impression.

**6. Gusto Resto Amed**

A comfortable family-style restaurant focusing on Mediterranean-Indonesian fusion dishes. Recommended dishes include fresh salads, Rendang chicken, and seared tuna steak, making it an excellent choice for group dining.

**7. Green Melon Warung**

Known for its healthy cooking, this spot offers fresh ingredients at affordable prices. Must-tries include BBQ tuna and squid dishes paired with Balinese salad, and the adorable banana leaf cone rice. Cash only.

**8. Trattoria Amed**

An authentic Italian restaurant with a romantic atmosphere and excellent service. Their pizzas are extremely popular, complemented by a professional wine cellar and well-equipped restroom facilities - a true paradise for pizza lovers.

**9. Sails Restaurant**

Boasting stunning sea views reminiscent of Greek island style. Highly recommend the banana leaf-wrapped fish, lamb patties, and frozen margaritas. The atmosphere is refreshing, staff is friendly, and they offer complimentary shuttle service.

### **Café Recommendation: Rimba Café**

Rimba Café is a rare find in Ubud, combining natural style with creative light meals. Surrounded by greenery, the atmosphere is fresh and relaxing. Specializing in handmade desserts, smoothie bowls, coffee, and healthy light meals, it's popular among travelers and yoga enthusiasts.

The coffee quality rivals that of city-level cafes, paired perfectly with homemade baked goods like banana cake or coconut milk pudding - guaranteed to make you a regular. They also offer vegetarian and plant-based options, making it an ideal spot for brunch or afternoon relaxation.

If you're looking for a place in Amed to slow down and enjoy a good cup of coffee, **Rimba Café** is definitely a great choice.

## Transportation

Planning transportation to Amed in advance is recommended, as Amed is located in the northeastern corner of Bali, far from the airport or southern tourist areas (like Kuta and Seminyak), with a drive time of approximately 2.5 to 3.5 hours. It’s advisable to book **private car transfers** directly.

A special reminder: **Most drivers operating in the south are reluctant to make one-way trips to Amed and return empty-handed**, especially during peak tourist seasons when it’s harder to find rides. ## Essential Pre-Trip Notes

https://youtube.com/shorts/XS1j72uEtTY

Since Amed and Tulamben are relatively remote, their infrastructure is not as developed as the southern tourist areas. Therefore, travelers should prepare thoroughly before departure:

- **Cash is king**: ATMs are scarce in the area, and foreign cards may not be accepted. It’s advisable to withdraw enough cash before hiking or diving activities.
- **Bring basic medications**: Local medical facilities are limited, so carry essentials like stomach medicine, painkillers, and insect repellent.
- **Book transportation in advance**: If you need a private car or driver, it’s best to arrange it early.
- **Limited internet**: Signal can be unstable in some areas, so consider getting a SIM card or portable WiFi.
- **Protect marine life**: Avoid touching coral and use reef-safe sunscreen to treat this precious ocean with care.

## FAQ

**Are Amed and Tulamben suitable for beginner divers?**

Yes, the sea conditions here are stable with gentle currents, making it particularly suitable for diving beginners. Local dive schools also offer introductory courses.

**Can I go directly from Ubud or Kuta?**

Yes, but it's recommended to hire a private car for the trip, which takes about 2.5 to 3 hours. You can also arrange stops at other attractions along the way.

**What is the best time to visit?**

The dry season (May to October) is the best time to visit, with clear skies and pristine waters perfect for water activities.

**Do local restaurants offer vegetarian options?**

Most restaurants can provide vegetarian options, but the choices are limited. It's advisable to inform them in advance and confirm.

**What other attractions are nearby?**

If you have more time, I recommend visiting these three stunning attractions nearby:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ed589a98d59d6d3d1de01faf3eb0b822.jpg)

- **Lempuyang Temple (Gates of Heaven)**: Located on the mountainside, famous for its majestic volcanic backdrop and sacred stone gates, it's a paradise for photography enthusiasts.
- **Tirta Gangga**: A former royal water garden, the scenery of stone sculptures and koi fish in the ponds is mesmerizing, making it an excellent spot for walking and photography.
- **Ujung Water Palace**: An ancient palace blending European and Balinese styles, surrounded by pools and greenery, offering a serene and historically rich atmosphere.
