## The Importance of Water Quality

Bali's tap water is not safe to drink directly! To ensure your body functions well, never overlook the quality of your drinking water. In daily life, we often underestimate the importance of water, but during your travels, it's the foundation of your health for the entire trip.

Therefore, it's recommended to choose bottled water or professionally filtered water sources. This bottle of clean water is not just a liquid to quench your thirst—it's a guardian of your health. It's the first step in protecting your well-being, allowing you to confidently explore this beautiful land.

## Beware of 'Bali Belly'

'Bali Belly' is a term that sounds a bit scary—it refers to the gastrointestinal issues you might encounter in Bali, which can lead to diarrhea, vomiting, fever, and overall weakness. These symptoms can completely ruin your fun and impact your travel experience.

## Watch Out for Ice Cubes

As mentioned in the video above, ice cubes—those seemingly harmless little things—can actually pose a hidden health risk! In Bali, the water source for ice isn't always reliable. So when ordering drinks, opt for no ice, or make sure the ice comes from a trusted source.

## Be Careful When Brushing Your Teeth, Too!

Even when brushing your teeth, pay close attention to water quality. Tap water in Bali may not be safe for brushing, as it can contain bacteria or other contaminants. So always use bottled or filtered water for brushing and rinsing.
