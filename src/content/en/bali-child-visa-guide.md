## Bali Family Travel: Complete Guide to Children's Visas

Taking a family vacation to Bali is a dream trip for many families. However, visa issues often cause headaches for parents before departure, especially questions like 'What visa does my two-year-old need?' and 'Does the whole family need to queue for a visa on arrival together?' This article will answer all your questions, ensuring your family trip starts smoothly right from entry!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9b12e310451ce513776f9b8ae272b6ee.png)

## Can a Two-Year-Old Apply for an Electronic Visa?

**Good news! Two-year-olds can apply for Indonesia's electronic visa (e-VOA).**

Indonesia's electronic visa system has no age restrictions. Whether it's a baby, toddler, or adult, everyone can apply for an electronic visa online before departure. This means you can pre-arrange electronic visas for your entire family (including your two-year-old), significantly saving time queuing at the airport.

### Advantages of the Electronic Visa

- **Apply in advance**: Complete the application at home before departure, avoiding crowded airport queues
- **Time-saving and convenient**: Directly use automated immigration clearance upon arrival (for eligible individuals), completing entry within three minutes
- **Same cost**: The electronic visa fee is the same as the visa on arrival, both **IDR 500,000**
- **30-day validity**: Can be extended once, allowing a total stay of up to 60 days

## Should the Whole Family Choose Electronic Visa or Visa on Arrival?

### Recommendation: Apply for E-Visas for the Whole Family

While it's theoretically possible for the entire family to apply for visas on arrival, **it's highly recommended that all family members apply for e-visas in advance**. Here's why:

## **Disadvantages of Visa on Arrival:**

- Requires queuing at the airport upon arrival, with uncertain waiting times
- During peak seasons or multiple flight arrivals, queues can take 30 minutes to over an hour
- Long queues with tired children can make the start of your journey exhausting
- Cash or card payment required on the spot

### **Advantages of E-Visas:**

- Peace of mind with everything prepared in advance
- Use of automated immigration gates (applicable for children aged 6 and above), fast and convenient
- Even if children under 6 cannot use automated gates, there are dedicated fast-track lanes
- Avoid long queues at the airport with children

## Practical Experience Sharing: Arrival at Bali Airport

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf7e7e7e9e394b75fc46a3b7bffd0242.png)

Based on actual experiences shared by community members, the arrival process at Bali Airport is very modern and user-friendly. Here’s what to expect when arriving with children:

### Automated Immigration Gates (Applicable for Ages 6 and Above)

Currently, **children aged 6 and above** can use the airport's automated immigration gates. If your child is 6 or older, congratulations! The whole family can use the automated gates, completing the process in less than three minutes!

Airport staff are very friendly. Upon disembarking, they will ask if you have applied for an e-visa and guide you to the automated immigration clearance area. The process is simple: scan your passport, facial recognition, and you're through.

### What about children under 6?

If your child is under 6 (e.g., 2 years old), although they cannot use the automated clearance, **there's no need to worry at all**!

Practical experience shows:

- Parents can quickly pass through automated clearance
- Customs officers will immediately assist, taking the child to a **dedicated counter**
- This dedicated counter is highly efficient, **with no queue at all**
- Parents wait beside the counter, and it's completed within minutes
- Overall, it's much faster than the entire family queuing for a visa on arrival

A parent visiting Bali shared: 'The customs officer saw that only one child in our family couldn't use automated clearance and immediately led the child to a dedicated counter nearby. The efficiency was very high, with no queue at all.'

## Steps to Apply for an E-Visa

1. **Apply Online**: Visit the [Official Indonesian Immigration E-Visa Website](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/)
2. **Fill in Details**: Including passport information for all family members
3. **Upload Photo**: An electronic passport-sized photo is required
4. **Online Payment**: **IDR 500,000** per person, payable by credit card\*\*(transaction fees apply)\*\*
5. **Receive Visa**: Usually received within 12 hours, remember to print or save it on your phone

### **Pre-Departure Must-Dos**:

1. Ensure you complete the e-Visa application first. Refer to [here](/blog/bali-evisa-application-guide/) for further instructions to avoid scam websites.
2. Complete the All Indonesia arrival card three days before departure and **screenshot the QR Code** for reference. See our compiled [FAQ](/blog/bali-evisa-customs-health-declaration/).
3. **Pay the Bali Tourism Tax**: IDR 150,000 per person (approx. USD$5). How to pay? See the [Tourism Tax](/blog/2026-bali-tourism-tax-guide/) guide.

### Reminder

- Apply at least 7 days before departure to allow processing time
- Ensure every family member (including infants) has a valid passport with at least 6 months validity
- Save the PDF file of your e-Visa, as you'll need to present it upon arrival

## Summary Recommendations

**For family trips with children under six:**

✅ **Get e-Visas for the whole family** - Save time and effort, avoid airport queues
✅ **Prepare in advance** - Complete applications before departure for peace of mind
✅ **Utilize automated immigration** - Fast entry for members aged 6+, dedicated lanes for under 6
✅ **Don't worry** - Airport staff are friendly and professional, ready to assist families with children
