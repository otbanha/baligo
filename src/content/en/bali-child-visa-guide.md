## Complete Guide to Children's Visas for Bali Family Trips

Taking a family vacation to Bali is a dream for many families. However, visa issues often cause headaches for parents before departure, especially questions like "What visa does my two-year-old need?" and "Does the whole family need to queue for a visa on arrival?" This article will answer all your questions, ensuring your family trip starts smoothly right from entry!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9b12e310451ce513776f9b8ae272b6ee.png)

## Can a Two-Year-Old Apply for an Electronic Visa?

**Good news! Two-year-olds can apply for Indonesia's electronic visa (e-VOA).**

Indonesia's electronic visa system has no age restrictions. Whether it's an infant, toddler, or adult, everyone can apply for an electronic visa online before departure. This means you can prepare electronic visas for your entire family (including your two-year-old) in advance, significantly saving time queuing at the airport.

### Advantages of Electronic Visa

- **Apply in advance**: Complete the application at home before departure, avoiding crowded airport queues
- **Time-saving and convenient**: Eligible travelers can use automated gates upon arrival, completing entry within three minutes
- **Same cost**: The electronic visa fee is the same as visa on arrival, both **IDR 500,000**
- **30-day validity**: Can be extended once, allowing a maximum stay of 60 days

## Should the Whole Family Choose Electronic Visa or Visa on Arrival?

### Recommendation: Apply for E-Visas for the Whole Family

While it's theoretically possible to apply for visas on arrival for the entire family, **we strongly recommend that all family members apply for e-visas in advance**. Here's why:

## **Disadvantages of Visa on Arrival:**

- Requires queuing at the airport upon arrival, with uncertain waiting times
- During peak seasons or when multiple flights arrive simultaneously, queues can take 30 minutes to over an hour
- Dragging tired children through long queues makes for a stressful start to the trip
- Cash or card payment required on-site

### **Advantages of E-Visas:**

- Peace of mind with everything prepared in advance
- Use of automated immigration gates (applicable for children aged 6 and above) for quick and convenient processing
- Even if children under 6 cannot use automated gates, there are dedicated fast-track lanes
- Avoid the hassle of long queues with children at the airport

## Practical Experience Sharing: Arrival at Bali Airport

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf7e7e7e9e394b75fc46a3b7bffd0242.png)

Based on actual experiences shared by community members, the arrival process at Bali Airport is now very modern and user-friendly. Here's what to expect when arriving with children:

### Automated Immigration Gates (Applicable for Ages 6 and Above)

Currently, **children aged 6 and above** can use the airport's automated immigration gates. If your child is 6 or older, congratulations! The whole family can use automated immigration, completing the process in less than three minutes!

Airport staff are very friendly. As soon as you disembark, someone will ask if you've applied for an e-Visa and guide you to the automated immigration area. The process is simple: scan your passport, facial recognition, and you're through.

### What about children under 6?

If your child is under 6 years old (for example, 2 years old), although they cannot use automated immigration, **there's absolutely no need to worry**!

Practical experience shows:

- Parents can quickly enter through automated immigration
- Customs officers will immediately assist, taking the child to a **dedicated counter**
- This dedicated counter is highly efficient, **with no need to queue at all**
- Parents wait beside the counter, and it's completed within minutes
- Overall, it's still much faster than the whole family queuing for a visa on arrival

A parent visiting Bali shared: 'The customs officer saw that only one child in our family couldn't use automated immigration, and immediately led the child to a dedicated counter nearby. The efficiency was very high, with no queuing at all.'

## Steps to Apply for an e-Visa

1. **Apply online**: Visit the [Official Indonesian Immigration e-Visa Website](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/)
2. **Fill in details**: Including passport information for all family members
3. **Upload photo**: A digital passport-style photo is required
4. **Online payment**: **IDR 500,000** per person, payable by credit card\*\*(card fees apply)\*\*
5. **Receive visa**: Usually received within 12 hours, remember to print or save on your phone

### **Pre-Departure Essentials**:

1. Make sure to complete the e-Visa application first. Refer to [here](/blog/bali-evisa-application-guide/) for further instructions to avoid scam websites.
2. Complete the All Indonesia Arrival Card three days before departure and **screenshot the QR Code** for reference. See our compiled [FAQ](/blog/bali-evisa-customs-health-declaration/).
3. **Pay the Bali Tourism Tax**: IDR 150,000 per person (approx. USD$5). How to pay? See the [Tourism Tax](/blog/2026-bali-tourism-tax-guide/) guide.

### Reminder

- Apply at least 7 days before departure to allow processing time
- Ensure each family member (including infants) has a valid passport with over 6 months validity
- Save the e-Visa PDF file as you'll need to present it upon arrival

## Summary Recommendations

**For family trips with children under six:**

✅ **Get e-Visas for the whole family** - Saves time and effort, no need to queue at the airport
✅ **Prepare in advance** - Complete applications before departure for peace of mind
✅ **Utilize automated immigration** - Fast entry for members over 6, dedicated lanes for under 6
✅ **Don't worry** - Airport staff are friendly and professional, ready to assist families with children
