At 6 PM, we arrived at our Ubud hotel, officially starting our journey. Bali's night is full of tropical charm, with a faint floral scent in the air, instantly making us feel relaxed and joyful. ## **Day 2 (January 2, Thursday) — Jungle Exploration and Rafting Adventure**

### **ATV Ride and Rafting Experience**

- **Meeting Time**: 9:15 AM
- **Why We Recommend It**: Perfect for adventure lovers!

In the morning, we drove ATVs through jungles, rice fields, and small streams. The scenery along the way was breathtaking, and the instructors patiently guided us on safety, making it easy even for beginners. Next, we headed to the river for rafting, which lasted about 2 hours. We recommend wearing light clothing or swimwear because you’re definitely going to get wet! During the rafting, the lush tropical rainforest scenery along the blue river was mesmerizing, and we even spotted monkeys jumping in the trees occasionally, which was a delightful surprise.

## **Day 3 (January 3, Friday) — Mount Batur Sunrise Jeep Tour**

### **Mount Batur Sunrise Jeep Tour**

**Cost**: USD$141 for 2 people (booked via Klook)

**Meeting Time**: 2:50 AM

The highlight of the day was the Mount Batur sunrise tour. We rode in a jeep through rugged mountain roads, admiring the starry sky along the way. Upon reaching the summit, the sky gradually brightened, and as the sun slowly rose, the entire island of Bali was bathed in warm morning light—a truly breathtaking sight.

For about $4, enjoy a 20-minute ride and immerse yourself in the artistic beauty of Bali's traditional architecture.

## **Day 5 (January 5, Sun) — Bali Safari & Marine Park**

### **Bali Safari Zoo**

**Ticket Price**: USD$98 per person (credit card payment)

**Tour Duration**: Depart at 9 AM, return at 4 PM

![野生動物園](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_67233c741fc10e5b047c021c1c9298ee.jpg)

Wildlife Park

Bali Safari Zoo is an ideal destination for families and animal lovers, and we spent the entire day exploring the park. In addition to seeing large animals like elephants and lions, there are many interactive activities, such as feeding giraffes and taking photos with tiger cubs. The highlight was riding the park's safari bus, which allowed us to observe various animals up close—a truly awe-inspiring experience.

Families with children are highly recommended to include this in their itinerary, as kids are sure to have a blast. The zoo also offers decent dining options, and after lunch, we continued exploring until 4 PM, reluctantly returning to our hotel.

## **Day 6 (January 6, Monday) — Nature & Culture Tour: Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, Tirta Empul Temple, and Tegallalang Rice Terraces**

### **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple**

**Entrance Fee**: $10

**Travel Time**: Approximately 1 hour 30 minutes drive from Ubud hotels

![水神廟 / 超軟超可愛的麝香貓](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_677e8fafd966063cdb53b173ed3256d0.jpg)

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple is a must-visit destination. This temple is situated by the lake, reflecting beautifully on the clear waters, creating a picturesque scene that feels like stepping into a fairy tale. Here, we also experienced holding and feeding civet cats for photos—these adorable creatures are perfect for capturing memories with family or friends. On-site, you can also try civet coffee, priced at around $5 per cup, which is rich in aroma and offers a unique experience not to be missed!

**Entrance Fee**: $7

**Entrance Fee**: IDR 150,000

## **Day 8 (January 8, Wednesday) — Nusa Dua Water Sports Adventure**

### **Nusa Dua Water Sports**

**Cost**: $180 USD

On this day, we headed to Nusa Dua for water sports, including snorkeling, kayaking, and banana boat rides. A word of caution: we didn’t book our tickets in advance through Klook, which resulted in much higher prices on-site, becoming a slight regret of our trip. We chose **Flying Fish** and **Parasailing**, and the two of us spent a total of 2,700,000 IDR (approximately $180 USD for two)...

If you plan to participate in water sports at Nusa Dua, don’t make the same mistake I did by not researching beforehand and ending up paying several times more! **Be sure to book your tickets online in advance**, not only to save a significant amount of money but also to avoid being overcharged on-site.
