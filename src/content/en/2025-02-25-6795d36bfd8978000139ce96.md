## **Day 1 (January 1, Wednesday) — Arrival in Bali, Starting Vacation Mode**

At 6 PM, we arrived at our Ubud hotel, officially starting this journey. Bali's night is full of tropical charm, with a faint floral fragrance in the air, instantly making us feel relaxed and happy. After dropping off our luggage, we walked out to the bustling streets for dinner, with many restaurants to choose from. We rested early to prepare for the next day's adventure.

## Day 2 (January 2nd, Thursday) — Jungle Exploration and Rafting Adventure

### ATV Off-Roading and Rafting Experience

- **Meeting Time**: 9:15 AM
- **Why We Recommend It**: Perfect for adventure seekers!

In the morning, we drove ATVs through jungles, rice fields, and small streams. The scenery along the way was breathtaking, and the instructors patiently guided us on safety, making it easy even for beginners. Next, we headed to the river for rafting, which lasted about 2 hours. We recommend wearing light clothing or swimwear since you’re guaranteed to get wet! During the rafting, the lush tropical rainforest scenery along the turquoise river was mesmerizing, and we even spotted monkeys jumping in the trees—a delightful surprise!

## Day 3 (January 3rd, Friday) — Mount Batur Sunrise Jeep Tour

### **Mount Batur Sunrise Jeep Tour**

**Cost**: USD$141 for 2 people (booked via Klook)

**Meeting Time**: 2:50 AM

The highlight of the day was the Mount Batur sunrise tour. We rode a jeep through rugged mountain roads, admiring the starry sky along the way. Upon reaching the summit, the sky gradually brightened, and as the sun slowly rose, the entire island of Bali was bathed in warm morning light—a truly breathtaking sight.

## **Day 4 (January 4, Saturday)—Exploring Ubud Culture and Markets**

The fare is approximately $4 for a 20-minute ride, allowing you to appreciate the artistic beauty of Bali's traditional architecture.

Within walking distance, the market offers handicrafts, clothing, and accessories worth purchasing, with room for negotiation.

The entrance fee is about $10 USD. The lake reflects the traditional architecture, creating a picturesque scene straight out of a postcard.

The entrance ticket costs $16 USD, and the visit lasts about an hour. The monkeys are quite mischievous, so be sure to keep your belongings secure.

## Day 5 (January 5th, Sunday) — Bali Safari Zoo

### Bali Safari Zoo

**Admission Fee**: USD$98 per person (credit card payment)

**Tour Duration**: Depart at 9:00 AM, return at 4:00 PM

![野生動物園](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_67233c741fc10e5b047c021c1c9298ee.jpg)

Wildlife Safari

Bali Safari Park is an ideal destination for families and animal lovers. We spent an entire day exploring the park. In addition to seeing large animals like elephants and lions, there are many interactive activities such as feeding giraffes and taking photos with tiger cubs. The safari bus tour was particularly impressive, allowing us to observe various animals up close.

Families with children are highly recommended to include this in their itinerary—kids will absolutely love it. The park also offers decent dining options. After having lunch inside the park, we continued exploring until 4:00 PM before reluctantly returning to our hotel to rest.

## **Day 6 (January 6, Monday)—Nature and Culture Tour: Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, Tirta Empul Temple, and Tegallalang Rice Terraces**

### **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple**

**Entrance Fee**: IDR 150,000

**Driving Time**: Approximately 1 hour 30 minutes from Ubud hotels

![水神廟 / 超軟超可愛的麝香貓](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_677e8fafd966063cdb53b173ed3256d0.jpg)

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple / Super Soft and Adorable Civets

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple is a must-visit destination. This temple is situated by the lake, reflecting on the clear waters, creating a picturesque scene that feels like stepping into a fairy tale. We also experienced holding civets for photos and feeding them—these little creatures are incredibly cute, perfect for capturing memories with family or friends. On-site, you can also try civet coffee, priced at around IDR 71,500 per cup. Its rich aroma makes it a unique and unforgettable experience!

### **Tirta Empul Temple**

**Entrance Fee**: IDR 100,000

Tirta Empul Temple is a renowned religious site in Bali, where many locals come to perform purification rituals. Although we didn’t participate in the holy water bathing, simply observing the locals’ ceremonies allowed us to feel the profound religious atmosphere. If you’re particularly interested in religious culture, this place is worth a visit. However, if time is limited, you can adjust your itinerary accordingly.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace

## **Day 7 (January 7, Tuesday) — Tanah Lot Temple and Cliffside Bar Sunset**

### **Tanah Lot Temple**

**Entrance Fee**: 150,000 IDR

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f7365ccab1629758df838b22b7d55cb3.jpg)

We set out early in the morning to avoid traffic and arrived smoothly at Tanah Lot Temple. This structure sits on a rock in the sea, and during high tide, the entire temple is surrounded by water, creating a breathtaking and unique view. It's a must-see landmark in Bali. We took many photos and felt the beauty of nature and religion intertwined.

### Rock Bar

## Day 8 (January 8, Wednesday) — Nusa Dua Water Sports Adventure

### **Nusa Dua Water Sports**

**Cost**: IDR 2,700,000

On this day, we headed to Nusa Dua for some water sports, including snorkeling, kayaking, and banana boat rides. A word of caution: we didn’t book tickets in advance through Klook, which resulted in paying significantly more on-site—a small regret of our trip. We chose **Flying Fish** and **Parasailing**, spending a total of IDR 2,700,000 (approximately USD$180 for two people)...

If you plan to participate in water sports in Nusa Dua, don’t make the same mistake I did by not researching beforehand and ending up paying several times more! **Be sure to book your tickets online in advance**. Not only will you save a lot of money, but you’ll also avoid being overcharged on-site.
