## 📄 Topics of Concern for Tourists: Health Warnings and Safety Tips

- **May 'Tropical Disease' Alert**: Due to lingering stagnant water after the rainy season, health authorities warn that May is a peak period for **Dengue Fever**. Travelers active during dawn and dusk should be sure to use insect repellent. Additionally, sporadic **Measles** cases have been reported recently; long-term visitors are advised to check their vaccination status.
- **Outdoor Activity Safety: Beware of Wild Macaques**: Several incidents of macaques snatching personal belongings from tourists have recently occurred at Uluwatu and the Monkey Forest. Officials remind visitors to secure glasses, hats, and small electronic devices before entering these areas, and to avoid eye contact with or feeding the monkeys to prevent scratches or bites.
- **Weekend Life Reminder: Watch Out for 'Alcohol Scams'**: The risk of 'fake alcohol' targeting foreign tourists persists. Police advise travelers to avoid purchasing suspiciously low-priced Arak (local liquor) mixed drinks, and recommend consuming only at reputable restaurants and bars, while keeping an eye on drink preparation.
- 🏗️ **Airport Update: Bali's Ngurah Rai Airport Arrival Area to Be Redesigned**: To cope with growing tourist traffic and reduce queue congestion, Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) plans to redesign its arrival area. The project will expand space for Autogates and optimize traffic flow, aiming to improve clearance efficiency and comfort for passengers after landing.
- 🚨 **Entry Reminder: Jakarta Airport Strengthens Health Screening**: In response to Hantavirus risks, Jakarta's Soekarno-Hatta International Airport (CGK) has intensified health checks for arriving passengers. Since Jakarta is a major transit point for entering **Bali**, travelers are reminded to cooperate with thermal scanners at the airport and proactively report symptoms like fever, headache, or difficulty breathing to health quarantine officers.


## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Infrastructure Funding and Cultural Preservation

- **Details of IDR 1.27 Trillion Infrastructure Funding Announced**: The Ministry of Public Works and Housing (PUPR) has announced the allocation of IDR 1.27 trillion for 206 infrastructure projects in Bali in 2026. Beyond road improvements, the focus will be on **flood control facilities** and **coastline protection** to address climate change-related sea level rise risks, directly impacting coastal tourist areas like Kuta and Seminyak.
- **Ancient Traditional Arts Preservation Program Launched**: To counter the impact of overtourism on culture, Bali's Cultural Office and local communities have initiated a 'Traditional Heritage Revival Program.' This involves digital recording and local training for **Gamelan** music and specific ceremonial dances, ensuring tourism revenue flows back into cultural preservation efforts.
- **Medical Tourism Facilities Scaling Up**: Construction of the international hospital in the Sanur Special Economic Zone (KEK Sanur) is ahead of schedule. Authorities expect to bring in more international specialists in the second half of 2026, aiming to attract 'medical tourists' seeking high-quality dental and cosmetic services, marking further diversification of Bali's industrial structure.
