### 📄 Topics of Concern for Travelers: Health Alerts and Safety Guidelines

- **May 'Tropical Disease' Alert**: Due to standing water remaining after the rainy season, health authorities warn that May is the peak period for Dengue Fever. Travelers are advised to use mosquito repellent during dawn and dusk activities. Additionally, sporadic cases of Measles have been reported recently, and long-term visitors are recommended to confirm their vaccination status.
- **Outdoor Activity Safety: Beware of Wild Monkeys**: Recent incidents of monkeys snatching tourists' belongings have occurred in Uluwatu and Monkey Forest. Officials remind visitors to secure glasses, hats, and small electronic devices before entering these areas and to avoid eye contact or feeding monkeys to prevent scratches or bites.
- **Weekend Life Reminder: Beware of 'Alcohol Scams'**: The risk of counterfeit alcohol targeting foreign tourists persists. Police advise travelers to avoid purchasing unusually cheap Arak (local liquor) mixed drinks and recommend consuming only at reputable restaurants and bars, ensuring drinks are prepared within sight.
- 🏗️ **Airport Update: Ngurah Rai Airport Arrival Area Redesign**: To accommodate increasing tourist traffic and reduce queue congestion, Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) plans to redesign the arrival area. The project will expand the space for automated gates (Autogates) and optimize flow configurations, aiming to enhance post-landing customs efficiency and comfort.
- 🚨 **Entry Reminder: Jakarta Airport Enhances Health Monitoring**: In response to Hantavirus risks, Soekarno-Hatta International Airport (CGK) in Jakarta has strengthened health screenings for incoming passengers. As Jakarta is a major transit point to Bali, travelers are reminded to cooperate with temperature checks and report symptoms like fever, headache, or breathing difficulties to health quarantine officers.

### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Construction Funding and Cultural Preservation

- **IDR 1.27 Trillion Infrastructure Funding Details Announced**: The Ministry of Public Works and Housing (PUPR) announced an allocation of IDR 1.27 trillion for 206 infrastructure projects in Bali in 2026. Beyond road improvements, the focus will be on flood prevention facilities and coastline protection to address rising sea levels due to climate change, directly impacting coastal tourist areas like Kuta and Seminyak.
- **Ancient Traditional Art Preservation Program Launched**: To counter the impact of overtourism on culture, the Bali Cultural Agency and local communities have initiated the 'Traditional Heritage Revival Program'. This includes digital documentation and local training for arts like Gamelan music and specific ceremonial dances, ensuring tourism revenue directly supports cultural preservation.
- **Medical Tourism Facilities Scaling Up**: The construction of the international hospital in the Sanur Special Economic Zone (KEK Sanur) is ahead of schedule. Officials expect to introduce more international specialists in the latter half of 2026, aiming to attract 'medical tourists' seeking high-quality dental and cosmetic services, marking further diversification in Bali's industry structure.
