## 1. Waterbom Bali｜Asia's Best Water Park, Thrills and Cool Fun Combined

**📍 Address:** Jl. Kartika Plaza, Kuta

![](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3367ef3d85a6593784914b7333f8c98f.jpg)

If there's one family attraction in Bali you can't miss, Waterbom tops the list. Awarded 'Asia's Best Water Park' by TripAdvisor for years, this 3.8-hectare water kingdom sits in the heart of Kuta, set against a tropical garden backdrop, creating a world of screams and laughter for both kids and adults.

The park features over 20 water slides, from gentle lazy rivers for toddlers to adrenaline-pumping vertical drop slides like 'Smashdown' for teens. Every ride is carefully designed by age group. The Lazy River lets parents drift leisurely while keeping an eye on the kids, while the 'Climax' slide plunges at a near-vertical angle—a perfect test of courage for adventurous adults.

The park is fully accessible, with ample lockers, changing rooms, and nursing spaces. Dining options are plentiful, and even sunscreen rentals are available. This isn't just a water park—it's a perfect stage for the whole family to laugh and play under the tropical sun.

**Travel Tip**: Arrive at opening time to avoid the afternoon crowds. Pre-purchase tickets through platforms like Klook for discounts.

## 2. Taman Safari Bali｜Bali's Largest Wildlife Park, an African Savannah Dream Among the Beasts

**📍 Address:** Jl. Prof. Dr. Ida Bagus Mantra No.KM. 19,8, Gianyar

![](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_48a0d20a925461edf1ebf06084a8e220.jpg)

If your child has dreamed of seeing lions, giraffes, and zebras strolling leisurely right before their eyes, Taman Safari Bali is where that dream comes true. This sprawling wildlife park in Gianyar is Bali's largest and most species-rich zoo, designed after the African savannah. Visitors ride through vast open animal zones, coming face-to-face with wildlife just a window away.

The park is divided into several themed areas: the **Drive-Through Safari** is the core experience—as your vehicle slowly rolls through, giraffes poke their long necks into the windows looking for food, and kids' excited gasps fill the air. The **Walking Zone** features various shows, including animal stunt performances, sea lion shows, and bird of prey displays—each one keeps the audience spellbound.

**Travel Tip:** Taman Safari and the adjacent Marine Safari Bali offer a two-day combo ticket, letting you enjoy one park each day—incredible value! See details below.

## 3. Marine Safari Bali｜Right Next to Taman Safari! A Marine Wonderland with a Two-Day Combo Ticket

**📍 Location:** Right next to Taman Safari Bali, Gianyar

![3. Marine Safari Bali｜就在 Taman Safari 旁！海洋奇境兩日聯票超值玩法](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1780205183105-website-banner-scaled.webp)

What many visitors don’t know is that right next to Taman Safari Bali lies a hidden marine-themed park—**Marine Safari Bali**. The two parks are so close that the official ticket office offers a **two-day combo ticket**: spend the first day exploring the African savanna at Taman Safari, and the second day diving into the marine world at Marine Safari. This way, you unlock both land and sea adventures in one trip, and it’s much more budget-friendly than buying separate tickets.

Marine Safari Bali focuses on rich marine life exhibits, featuring several immersive zones where kids can get up close with coral reef fish, tropical jellyfish, and various marine ecosystems. Interactive areas allow children to touch some marine creatures, fostering awareness and affection for ocean conservation.

For families who love the ocean, instead of rushing to the other side of the island, it’s better to spend two days in Gianyar and fully enjoy this “land and sea combo package.”

**Travel Tip:** The two-day combo ticket can be booked through authorized agency platforms. It’s recommended to visit Marine Safari first (open Tuesday to Sunday), then Taman Safari.

## 4. Mason Elephant Park｜A Heartwarming Moment Bathing with Elephants

**📍 Location:** Jl. Elephant Park, Taro, Tegallalang, Gianyar

![4. Mason Elephant Park｜與大象共浴的感動時刻](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1780205293049-elephant.jpeg)

In the tranquil village of Taro, north of Ubud, lies a green sanctuary dedicated to Bali’s rare Sumatran elephants—Mason Elephant Park. This is more than just a tourist attraction; it’s a conservation refuge that shelters dozens of Sumatran elephants, providing them with vast, free-roaming habitats.

Families with kids can enjoy a variety of immersive activities here: **elephant feeding** is the most popular among children—handing a banana to an elephant's trunk brings a mix of gentleness and awe that no photo can fully capture; **elephant feeding** and **elephant bathing** let families step into shallow water together to scrub the elephants, with laughter and splashes filling the air.

The park also features a tourist farm, a scenic restaurant, and a handicraft market, making it easy to arrange a half-day or full-day leisurely itinerary.

**Travel tip:** It's best to visit in the morning when the animals are most active. Some experiences require advance booking, and you can search for packages on Klook.

## 5. Bali Bird Park｜600 Rare Bird Species, a Tropical Fantasy Journey Among Feathers

**📍 Address:** Jl. Serma Cok Ngurah Gambir, Singapadu, Gianyar

![](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7391a524afd01acee5b8276e6d4c1a76.jpg)

Located near Ubud, Bali Bird Park is one of Southeast Asia's largest bird parks, home to over 1,000 birds from more than 250 species, including the breathtaking Birds of Paradise, toucans, flamingos, and the endemic **Bali Starling**—a rare species with fewer than 100 individuals worldwide, which receives the best breeding protection here.

Strolling through the park, tropical plants and exotic birds weave a natural tapestry. The daily **flying raptor show** lets eagles and owls swoop overhead, filling children's faces with pure wonder; the **parrot feeding experience** allows colorful birds to perch on kids' arms, creating the most unforgettable family photo of the trip.

Adjacent to Bali Reptile Park (see below), you can buy a combined ticket to explore both theme parks in half a day.

**Travel tip:** Wear long sleeves to prevent mosquito bites, and visit in the morning when the weather is cooler for the best experience.

## 6. Bali Reptile Park｜Snakes Around Your Neck, Monitors in Sight – A Reptile Wonderland for the Brave

**📍 Address:** Adjacent to Bali Bird Park, Singapadu, Gianyar

![6. Bali Reptile Park｜蛇纏頸、巨蜥現身——勇敢者的爬蟲奇境](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1780206204799-_2026-05-31_12.42.57.png)

Right next to Bali Bird Park, Bali Reptile Park is another strange world that both frightens and fascinates kids. The park houses Bali's most comprehensive collection of reptiles, including various pythons, cobras, monitor lizards (close relatives of Komodo dragons), colorful geckos, and tortoises. Just approaching the display tanks is enough to make the faint-hearted's pulse race.

The most popular experience here is having the staff help **drape a python over your shoulders for a photo** — the coolness of the snake's body and the tension of its muscles against your skin create an indescribably unique sensation, and it's the moment that makes kids both scream and laugh. After bravely completing it, you'll see that proud, triumphant grin on your child's face.

Guides accompany you throughout, explaining each animal's habits and conservation status in detail, turning this 'courageous adventure' into a vivid natural education lesson.

## 7. Bali Exotic Marine Park｜Dive into the Blue and Dance with Dolphins in a Dreamy Underwater World

**📍 Address:** Tanjung Benoa, Nusa Dua

![](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779966642880-mixcollage-28-may-2026-06-10-pm-4374.jpg)

If you had to pick the most moving experience for a family trip to Bali, the dolphin interaction at Bali Exotic Marine Park would top the list. Located along the southeastern coast in Tanjung Benoa, this marine park is famous for its **incredibly close dolphin encounters**, where you can slip into a swimsuit, step into the water, and swim alongside these graceful ocean spirits in the same crystal-blue sea.

**Swim with Dolphins** is the park's flagship experience: well-trained dolphins swim right up to you, letting you touch their smooth skin, and under the trainer's guidance, you can even feel the exhilarating rush of being pushed through the water by a dolphin. The moment children come face-to-face with a dolphin in the water is often the brightest, most unforgettable highlight of their entire Bali trip.

Beyond dolphin interactions, the park also features sea lion shows, underwater observation pools, and various marine ecology exhibits, allowing families to deepen their respect and love for ocean life while having fun.

**Travel Tip:** Dolphin interaction slots are limited, so it's highly recommended to book in advance through online platforms. Some activities have age and height restrictions, so be sure to check before booking.

Bali Zoo is the largest comprehensive zoo on the island and a top weekend destination for local families. Home to over 500 animals, including Sumatran tigers, orangutans, giraffes, and zebras, the zoo is set against a lush tropical backdrop, creating a natural environment that blends education with entertainment.

One of the biggest highlights for families is the **Breakfast with Orangutans**—enjoy a traditional Balinese breakfast in an open-air restaurant while orangutans roam freely on nearby platforms, occasionally coming close to say hello. It’s the perfect way to start the day with joy. Other popular activities include **elephant interactions**, **tiger feeding**, and various animal encounters, ensuring kids of all ages find their own adventure.

The zoo also features well-equipped dining facilities and a children’s play area, making it ideal for a full day of relaxed family fun.

## Practical Tips for Planning a Family Trip to Bali

Bali's family-friendly attractions are spread out, so it's best to plan based on your accommodation: if you're staying in **Kuta / Seminyak / Sanur**, prioritize Waterbom, AeroXSpace, and Trans Studio; if you're around **Ubud**, visit Bali Bird Park, Bali Reptile Park, Bali Zoo, Mason Elephant Park, and Taman Safari in one go for maximum efficiency.

Also, Bali often has brief afternoon showers, so it's wise to schedule high-energy outdoor activities (like water parks and zoos) in the morning, and opt for indoor experiences or a swim back at the hotel in the afternoon. This keeps kids' energy and mood at their best.

May every family that sets foot on this island create the most precious travel memories through the pure laughter of their children.

Always check each attraction's official website for age and height restrictions before you go.

Some authorized booking platforms (like Klook) also occasionally offer package deals. It's best to pre-purchase through official or trusted platforms, as combo tickets may not be available at the on-site ticket booth. The two parks are adjacent, so staying in Gianyar or around Ubud for two days is the most convenient arrangement.

**Q3: Where are Bali's animal parks concentrated?** Bali Bird Park, Bali Reptile Park, Bali Zoo, and Taman Safari / Marine Safari are all located in **Gianyar Regency**, close to each other, making it easy to plan a 2-3 day 'animal-themed itinerary' with Ubud as your home base.

**Q4: Is Waterbom suitable for bringing infants and toddlers?** Yes. Waterbom has a dedicated shallow pool and gentle slides for toddlers, with shaded rest areas nearby so parents can take turns playing with their little ones. The park offers stroller rentals, and the changing rooms and nursing facilities are well-equipped, making it very family-friendly for those with babies.

**Q5: Is the dolphin interaction at Bali Exotic Marine Park safe?** Yes, it's safe. All dolphins in the park are accompanied by professional trainers throughout the interaction, which is strictly regulated to prohibit rough touching or chasing. Even young children can participate in some interactive experiences with adult supervision (be sure to check age restrictions in advance).

**Q6: What's the best time of year to visit Bali's family attractions?** The **dry season from April to October** offers the highest chance of sunny weather, making it ideal for water parks and outdoor zoos. If your trip falls during the rainy season (November to March), plan indoor attractions like Trans Studio Bali as a rainy-day backup, and schedule outdoor activities for the morning to avoid afternoon thunderstorms.

**Q7: Can I buy tickets online for these attractions? Is it cheaper?** Most attractions can be pre-purchased through platforms like **Klook**, **Kkday**, and **Trip.com**, typically offering 10-30% discounts compared to on-site prices, plus saving you time waiting in line. It's highly recommended to buy tickets in advance, especially for Waterbom and the dolphin experience at Bali Exotic Marine Park, as spots are often limited during peak season.

**Q8: Is the elephant riding experience at Mason Elephant Park controversial? Is it suitable for kids?** Mason Elephant Park positions itself as a **conservation park** rather than a pure entertainment venue. The park emphasizes that it houses injured or orphaned elephants that cannot be returned to the wild, providing them with spacious living areas. Low-intensity interactive experiences like feeding and bathing are generally considered more humane. Elephant riding has now been completely banned.

**Q9: Can you visit multiple attractions in one day?** It's generally recommended to **schedule at most 1–2 attractions per day** to ensure kids have enough energy and emotional capacity to enjoy each place. Taman Safari + Marine Safari is an exception and is better suited for a two-day trip. For large-scale parks like Waterbom or Trans Studio, it's best to dedicate an entire day to them.

**Q10: What should you prepare for Bali's theme parks?** Essentials include: **sunscreen** (especially important for water parks), **insect repellent** (for outdoor zoos), **a lightweight raincoat or foldable umbrella** (for afternoon showers), **a change of clothes** (for water parks or dolphin interactions), and **snacks and water** (some attractions have high food and drink prices). Credit cards are accepted at most major parks, but it's wise to carry some Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) just in case.
