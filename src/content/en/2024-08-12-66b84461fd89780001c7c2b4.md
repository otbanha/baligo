Our Bali trip began with high expectations as we explored the cultural essence of Ubud, relaxed at Atlas Beach Club, and indulged in local night market delicacies. From the moment we arrived on a red-eye flight, our family was enchanted by Bali's charm. This travelogue will guide you through our 6-day, 5-night journey, sharing the highlights and surprises of each day.

## **Day 1: Red-Eye Flight and First Glimpse of Ubud**

We planned a 6-day, 5-night family trip to Bali, opting for a red-eye flight with Scoot. After a 2-hour layover in Singapore, we had breakfast and explored Singapore's airport. Arriving in Bali around 10:00 AM, we wasted no time and started our adventure immediately!

Since we had purchased a SIM card in Taiwan beforehand, we quickly contacted our private driver, Mr. Awan from Bali Holiday, upon arrival.

> Further reading: [【Essential for Bali Travel】How to Set Up Internet/SIM Card in Bali?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)

He was very friendly, communicated fluently in English, and drove a highly recognizable pink car that was easy to spot anywhere. After boarding, we first asked him to take us to exchange currency, then headed to Ubud. Along the way, we visited the beautiful Tegenungan Waterfall and stopped by a supermarket to buy snacks and drinking water.

## **Day 2: River Tubing & Ubud Adventure**

On the second day, we headed out for a river tubing adventure, which was arranged by Awan. We bought tickets on-site, and the price was consistent with the listed rate. We considered purchasing tickets in advance on the KK website, but I thought that would limit our flexibility. Initially, we expected the activity to be mild, but it turned out to be quite fun and slightly challenging! My husband and kids had a great time, making it a perfect family activity!

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Cave River Tubing Adventure: A Family Adventure at Nukuwera River Tubing](/blog/2025-06-09-68463dcbfd897800011c6f06/)

![叢林鞦韆。（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_86291fefaa0646e19596de72aa7330bd.jpg)

Next, we visited the rice terraces. The local swing operators were incredibly skilled at photography, using techniques like slow motion, video, and portrait mode to capture stunning shots. They also had a knack for timing the perfect shot, far surpassing my husband's photography skills! 🤣

In the afternoon, we happily visited the Sacred Monkey Forest, where we were surrounded by monkeys. Later, we strolled through Ubud Market, and the bargaining process was quite entertaining! However, many of the items in the market were repetitive, so I usually chose to buy from vendors whose vibes resonated with me.

## **Day 3: Safari Zoo and Night Market Experience**

![Safari Zoo. （趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d58684925c87a20eb4035406378406a1.jpg)

Safari Zoo. (Photo provided by Zhao Kangkang)

On the third day, we visited the Safari Zoo. Although it's a bit far from Ubud, it's definitely worth a visit for families with children! The animals in the zoo seemed very relaxed, allowing us to get closer to them. There's a cute animal zone where you can buy tickets to feed the little animals, and the kids couldn't get enough of it. During our tour, we saw other visitors picking up rabbits for photos, which made the rabbits look helpless. This gave me a chance to teach my children to respect animals.

At noon, we stumbled upon a tiger show, which left me amazed. It was my first time observing tigers so closely, and they weren't in glass cages—they even interacted with the staff. It was truly a sight to see!

Besides animal viewing, the park hosts a variety of animal performances, blending fun and nature. Check out [our detailed guide here](/blog/2023-07-25-64db6b8cfd897800013a9ab1/). If you're looking for better value, consider [staying at the zoo](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=109155).

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to a local night market. We walked around first, asking him to introduce various foods, and then he helped us order from the vendors (since some didn't speak English). We enjoyed roasted pork rice, chicken meatball noodles, chicken satay, pork satay, and bought a bunch of local desserts to enjoy back at our accommodation. All of this cost less than USD$10, making it a very affordable and fun experience!

### **Day 4: Tanah Lot Temple and Canggu Market Stroll**

On the fourth day, we visited the famous Tanah Lot Temple. Fortunately, it was low tide, allowing us to walk down to the seaside. The weather was perfect, and every shot we took was postcard-worthy, easily good enough to be a computer wallpaper.

![海神廟（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5af7bceb17915318e93173bfc2bc2052.jpg)

Tanah Lot Temple (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

In the afternoon, we headed to Canggu and explored the Love Anchor Market. However, it left us somewhat disappointed. We had hoped to find unique souvenirs, but the items here were quite similar to those in Ubud Market. So, this place isn’t worth making a special trip for (there aren’t many nearby attractions, and prices are higher).

In the evening, we went to a nearby beach and tried the [internet-famous grilled corn](https://youtu.be/HKMalFa6zMw). It lived up to its reputation! At just 20K per ear, it was both delicious and affordable.

## **Day 5: Relaxing at Atlas Beach Club**

On the fifth day, we spent the entire day at Atlas Beach Club.

![Atlas（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_27217d256133aaf6efab3d64131dcdff.jpg)

Atlas (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

I had purchased tickets in advance on Klook. Upon arrival, I simply scanned the QR code, and they prepared our entry passes and guided us to our seats—very convenient. Atlas Beach Club is highly recommended. It features a shallow pool for kids and a massive infinity pool right next to the beach, where you can wade into the waves. The ticket includes a certain amount of food or drinks, and you only pay extra if you exceed the limit.

## **Day 6: Final Shopping and Farewell to Bali**

On the last day, Awan took us to a large local shopping spot similar to a duty-free store (Krisna Toko Oleh Oleh Kas Bali) as per our request. Although the products here weren't particularly refined, they had everything from food and clothing to home decor and jewelry. At this stop, we bought all the souvenirs we hadn't picked up yet, and we were very satisfied with our full cart of treasures, marking a perfect end to our trip.

![我們的戰利品（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6da6ad309b85087a2074fa2e43f4d3e4.jpg)

Our loot (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

This was our first time in Bali. Before departure, I had watched many videos and was somewhat prepared, but Bali's beauty still exceeded my expectations! The traffic and lifestyle here are very laid-back. Although there are frequent traffic jams, you rarely hear honking, and everyone is incredibly patient! Except for the roads near the airport, which have white lines, the road planning elsewhere is quite casual. Sometimes a two-way street suddenly becomes one-way, which can be confusing 😂. Therefore, I strongly recommend that for family trips (or groups of three or more), hiring a private car is more convenient and safer.

## **Recommended Driver: Bali Holiday Awan**

Finally, I want to recommend our private car driver again—Awan from Bali Holiday. From the first day we met, we felt his kindness and enthusiasm. Although I had prepared an itinerary for him before departure, there were times when we changed plans due to fatigue or time constraints, and he was very flexible in adjusting and giving appropriate advice, such as which route was smoother or how long to stay at certain attractions.

![我們很滿意的包車司機（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad7a43d71b2ee96f978fe92dfbedcb54.jpg)

Our satisfied private car driver (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

Additionally, on two days, I asked him to arrange for massage therapists to come to our Villa, and he worked hard to coordinate the timing for us. Overall, Awan is an excellent private car driver. Although he's not part of a large company, his warmth made us feel very comfortable, and my family got along with him very well! If I have the chance to visit Bali again, I will definitely choose this driver again, and I highly recommend him to friends looking for a private car driver!
