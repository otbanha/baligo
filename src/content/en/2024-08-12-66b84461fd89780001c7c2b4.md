Our Bali trip began with high expectations as we explored Ubud's cultural atmosphere, relaxed at Atlas Beach Club, and tasted delicious local street food at night markets. From the moment we arrived on a red-eye flight, our family was captivated by Bali's charm. This travelogue will take you through our 6-day, 5-night itinerary, sharing the beauty and surprises of each day.

## **Day 1: Red-Eye Flight and First Glimpse of Ubud**

We planned a 6-day, 5-night family trip to Bali, opting for Scoot's red-eye flight. After a 2-hour layover in Singapore, we had breakfast and explored Changi Airport. Arriving in Bali around 10:00 AM, we wasted no time and started our adventure immediately!

Since we had purchased a SIM card in Taiwan beforehand, we quickly contacted our private driver, Mr. Awan from Bali Holiday, upon arrival.

> Further reading: [Essential for Bali Travel: How to Get a Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)

He was very friendly, communicated fluently in English, and his bright pink car was easily recognizable wherever we went. After boarding, we first asked him to take us to exchange currency, then headed to Ubud. Along the way, we visited the beautiful Tegenungan Waterfall and stopped by a supermarket to buy snacks and drinking water.

## **Day 2: River Tubing & Ubud Adventure**

On the second day, we went on a river tubing adventure, which was arranged by Awan. We bought tickets on-site, and the price was consistent with the listed rate. We had considered purchasing tickets in advance through a booking website, but I thought it would limit our flexibility. Initially, we expected the activity to be mild, but it turned out to be quite fun and slightly challenging. My husband and kids had a great time, making it a perfect family activity!

https://youtu.be/ZEkv46_-_mM

Next, we headed to the rice terraces. The local swing operators are incredibly skilled at photography, using techniques like slow motion, video, and portrait mode to capture stunning shots. They also have a knack for timing the perfect shot, far surpassing my husband's photography skills! 🤣

In the afternoon, we happily visited the Monkey Forest and were surrounded by curious monkeys. Afterwards, we headed to Ubud Market for some shopping, where the bargaining process was quite entertaining! However, many of the items in the market were repetitive, so I usually chose to buy from vendors whose vibes resonated with me.

## **Day 3: Safari Zoo & Night Market Experience**

![Safari Zoo. （趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d58684925c87a20eb4035406378406a1.jpg)

Safari Zoo. (Photo provided by Zhao Kangkang)

On the third day, we visited Safari Zoo. Although it was a bit far from Ubud, it was definitely worth the trip, especially for families with children! The animals in the zoo seemed very relaxed, allowing us to get closer to them. There was a cute animal zone where you could buy tickets to feed the little animals, and the kids couldn’t get enough of it. During our visit, we saw other tourists picking up rabbits for photos, which made the rabbits look helpless. This gave me a chance to teach my children about respecting animals.

At noon, we stumbled upon a tiger show, which left me completely amazed. It was my first time seeing tigers up close, and they weren’t in glass cages—they even interacted with the staff. It was truly a sight to behold!

In addition to animal viewing, the park hosts a variety of animal performances, blending fun and nature into one unforgettable experience. If you’re looking for better value, consider [staying at the zoo](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=109155).

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to a local night market for a unique experience. At the market, we first walked around and had him introduce us to various foods. Then, he helped us order from the vendors (since some didn’t speak English). We enjoyed dishes like roasted pork rice, chicken meatball noodles, chicken satay, and pork satay, and even bought a bunch of local desserts to enjoy back at our accommodation. All of this cost less than USD$10, making it a highly affordable and fun experience!

### **Day 4: Tanah Lot Temple and Canggu Market Stroll**

On the fourth day, we visited the famous Tanah Lot Temple. Fortunately, it was low tide that day, allowing us to stroll along the beach. The weather was perfect, and every shot we took was postcard-worthy, easily good enough to be a computer wallpaper.

![海神廟（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5af7bceb17915318e93173bfc2bc2052.jpg)

Tanah Lot Temple (Photo provided by Zhao Kangkang)

In the afternoon, we headed to Canggu and explored the Love Anchor Market. However, we were somewhat disappointed. We had hoped to find some unique souvenirs, but the items here were quite similar to those in Ubud Market. So, this place isn’t really worth a special trip (there aren’t many nearby attractions, and prices are higher).

{{block:canggu}}

In the evening, we went to a nearby beach and tried the [internet-famous grilled corn](https://youtu.be/HKMalFa6zMw). It definitely lived up to the hype! At just 20K per ear, it was both delicious and affordable.

## **Day 5: Relaxing at Atlas Beach Club**

On the fifth day, we spent the entire day at Atlas Beach Club.

![Atlas（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_27217d256133aaf6efab3d64131dcdff.jpg)

Atlas (Photo provided by Zhao Kangkang)

I pre-purchased tickets on Klook, and upon arrival, all I had to do was scan the QR code. They prepared our entry passes and guided us to our seats—very convenient. Atlas Beach Club is truly worth recommending. There’s a shallow pool for kids, a massive infinity pool, and the beach is right next door, perfect for wading in the waves. The ticket includes a certain amount of food or drinks, and you only need to pay extra if you exceed the limit.

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to the Seminyak shopping district for some retail therapy. I noticed that international brands tend to be quite expensive in Bali, so I recommend supporting local brands instead — they offer great quality at affordable prices!

## **Day 6: Final Shopping Spree and Farewell to Bali**

On our last day, Awan took us to a massive local shopping spot similar to a duty-free store (Krisna Toko Oleh Oleh Kas Bali) as per our request. While the items here weren’t particularly refined, they had everything you could think of — from food and clothing to home decor and jewelry. This was our final stop to pick up all the souvenirs we hadn’t bought yet, and we ended up with a car full of treasures, wrapping up our trip on a very satisfying note.

![我們的戰利品（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6da6ad309b85087a2074fa2e43f4d3e4.jpg)

Our haul (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

This was our first time in Bali, and although I had watched plenty of videos beforehand and thought I was prepared, Bali’s beauty still exceeded all expectations! The traffic and pace of life here are very laid-back. Even though there’s often congestion, you rarely hear honking — everyone is incredibly patient! Except for the roads near the airport, which have clear markings, the road planning elsewhere is quite casual. Sometimes a two-way street suddenly becomes one-way, leaving you utterly confused 😂. That’s why I strongly recommend hiring a private driver for family trips (or groups of three or more) — it’s much more convenient and safer.

## **Recommended Driver: Bali Holiday Awan**

Finally, I want to recommend our private driver for this trip — Awan from Bali Holiday. From the very first day, we felt his warmth and enthusiasm. Even though I had prepared an itinerary for him beforehand, there were times when we changed plans due to fatigue or timing, and he was always flexible, adjusting accordingly and offering helpful advice, like which route was smoother or how much time to allocate to certain attractions.

![我們很滿意的包車司機（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad7a43d71b2ee96f978fe92dfbedcb54.jpg)

Our very satisfied private driver (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

Additionally, on two occasions, I asked him to arrange for massage therapists to come to our villa, and he worked hard to coordinate the timing for us. Overall, Awan is an excellent private driver. Though he’s not part of a big company, his personal touch made us feel truly cared for, and my family got along with him wonderfully! If I ever have the chance to visit Bali again, I’ll definitely choose him as our driver, and I highly recommend him to anyone looking for a private driver!
