Our Bali trip began with great anticipation as we explored the cultural charm of Ubud, enjoyed leisurely moments at Atlas Beach Club, and savored delicious local street food. From the moment we arrived on a red-eye flight, our family was captivated by Bali's allure. This travelogue will take you through our 6-day, 5-night itinerary, sharing the beauty and surprises of each day.

## Day 1: Red-Eye Flight and First Glimpse of Ubud

We planned a 6-day, 5-night family trip to Bali and chose to fly on a red-eye flight with Scoot. After a 2-hour layover in Singapore, we had breakfast and explored Singapore's airport. We arrived in Bali around 10:00 AM and started our adventure immediately, wasting no time!

Since we had purchased a SIM card in Taiwan beforehand, we quickly contacted our private driver for this trip—Mr. Awan from Bali Holiday.

> Further reading: [Essential for Bali Travel: How to Get a Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/).

He was very friendly, communicated fluently in English, and his bright pink car was easily recognizable wherever we went. After getting in the car, we first asked him to take us to exchange currency, then headed to Ubud. Along the way, we visited the beautiful Tegenungan Waterfall and stopped by a supermarket to buy snacks and drinking water.

## ****Day 2: River Tubing & Ubud Adventure****

On the second day, we went on a river tubing adventure, which was arranged by Awan. We bought tickets on-site, and the price was consistent with the listed rate. We considered purchasing tickets in advance on a double-K website, but I thought that would limit our flexibility. Initially, we expected the activity to be mild, but it turned out to be quite fun and slightly challenging. My husband and kids had a great time, making it a perfect family activity!

Quick Reference: [Bali Cave River Tubing Adventure: A Family Adventure at Nukuwera River Tubing](/blog/2025-06-09-68463dcbfd897800011c6f06/)

![叢林鞦韆。（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_86291fefaa0646e19596de72aa7330bd.jpg)

Jungle Swing. (Photo provided by Zhao Kangkang)

Next, we visited the rice terraces. The local swing operators were incredibly skilled photographers, using techniques like slow motion, video, and portrait mode to capture stunning shots. They expertly timed the best moments for photos, far surpassing my husband's photography skills! 🤣

In the afternoon, we happily visited the Sacred Monkey Forest, where we were surrounded by monkeys. Later, we went shopping at Ubud Market, and the bargaining process was quite entertaining! However, many items in the market were repetitive, so I usually chose to buy from vendors whose faces I found more appealing.

## ****Day 3: Safari Zoo and Night Market Experience****

![Safari Zoo. （趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d58684925c87a20eb4035406378406a1.jpg)

Safari Zoo. (Provided by Zhao Kangkang)

On the third day, we visited the Safari Zoo. Although it's a bit far from Ubud, it's absolutely worth a visit for families with children! The animals in the zoo seemed very relaxed, allowing us to get closer to them. There's a cute animal area where you can buy tickets to feed the little ones, and the kids couldn't get enough of it. During the tour, we saw other visitors picking up rabbits for photos, which made the rabbits look helpless. This gave me a chance to teach the kids about respecting animals.

At noon, we stumbled upon a tiger show, which left me amazed. It was my first time seeing tigers up close without glass barriers, and they even interacted with the staff. It was truly a sight to behold!

Besides animal viewing, the park also hosts various animal performances, creating a joyful and immersive experience. For more details, check out [our in-depth guide](/blog/2023-07-25-64db6b8cfd897800013a9ab1/). If you're looking for better value, consider [staying at the zoo](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=109155).

In the evening, we asked Awan to take us to a local night market. We first walked around, asking him to introduce various foods, and then he helped us order from the vendors (since some didn't speak English). We enjoyed dishes like roast pork rice, chicken meatball noodles, chicken satay, and pork satay, and even bought a bunch of local desserts to enjoy back at our accommodation. All of this cost less than USD$10, making it a very affordable and fun experience!

### ****Day 4: Tanah Lot Temple and Canggu Market Stroll****

On the fourth day, we visited the famous Tanah Lot Temple. Fortunately, it was low tide, allowing us to walk down to the beach. The weather was perfect, and every shot was a postcard-worthy scene, ideal for a computer wallpaper.

![海神廟（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5af7bceb17915318e93173bfc2bc2052.jpg)

Tanah Lot Temple (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

In the afternoon, we headed to Canggu and explored the Love Anchor Market. However, we were somewhat disappointed as we had hoped to find unique souvenirs, but the items here were quite similar to those in Ubud Market. Therefore, it's not necessary to make a special trip here (there aren't many places to visit nearby, and prices are higher).

As evening approached, we visited a nearby beach and tried the [internet-famous grilled corn](https://youtu.be/HKMalFa6zMw), which lived up to its reputation! A stick of grilled corn costs only 20K IDR, both delicious and affordable.

## ****Day 5: Relaxing Time at Atlas Beach Club****

On the fifth day, we spent the entire day at Atlas Beach Club.

![Atlas（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_27217d256133aaf6efab3d64131dcdff.jpg)

Atlas (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

I purchased tickets in advance on Klook, and upon arrival, we just scanned the QR code. They prepared our entry tickets and arranged for someone to guide us to our seats, which was very convenient. Atlas Club is truly worth recommending. There's a shallow pool dedicated to children and a large infinity pool right next to the beach, where you can go down to wade in the waves. The ticket includes a certain amount of food or drinks, and you only need to pay extra if you exceed the limit.

## ****Day 6: Final Shopping and Farewell to Bali****

On the last day, Awan took us to a large local shopping spot similar to a duty-free store (Krisna Toko Oleh Oleh Kas Bali) as per our request. Although the products here weren't particularly refined, they had everything from food and clothing to home decor and jewelry. At this stop, we bought all the remaining souvenirs we needed, and we were very satisfied with our full cart of treasures, marking a perfect end to our trip.

![我們的戰利品（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6da6ad309b85087a2074fa2e43f4d3e4.jpg)

Our spoils (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

This was our first time in Bali, and although I had watched many videos before the trip and was somewhat prepared, Bali's beauty still exceeded my expectations! The traffic and the pace of life here are very laid-back. Although there are frequent traffic jams, you rarely hear honking, and everyone has incredible patience! Except for the roads near the airport, which have white lines, the road planning elsewhere is very casual. Sometimes a two-way street suddenly becomes a one-way street, leaving you puzzled 😂. Therefore, I strongly recommend hiring a private car for convenience and safety, especially for family trips or groups of three or more.

## ****Recommended Driver: Bali Holiday Awan****

Finally, I want to recommend our private car driver for this trip—Awan from Bali Holiday. From the first day we met, we felt his kindness and enthusiasm. Although I had prepared an itinerary for him before the trip, there were times when we changed our plans due to fatigue or timing, and he was very flexible in adjusting and giving appropriate advice, such as which route was smoother or how long to spend at certain attractions.

![我們很滿意的包車司機（趙康康提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad7a43d71b2ee96f978fe92dfbedcb54.jpg)

Our very satisfied private car driver (provided by Zhao Kangkang)

Additionally, on two days, I asked him to arrange for massage therapists to come to our Villa, and he did his best to coordinate the timing for us. Overall, Awan is an excellent private car driver. Although he doesn't work for a large company, his warmth made us feel very comfortable, and my family got along with him very well! If I have the chance to visit Bali again, I will definitely choose him as our driver again, and I highly recommend him to anyone looking for a private car driver!
