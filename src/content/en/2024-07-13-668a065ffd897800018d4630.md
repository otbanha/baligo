## 1️⃣ **Exchanging Indonesian Rupiah**

During this trip, each of us exchanged around USD$320, which was just enough for the 7-day, 6-night itinerary, and even allowed us to buy quite a few souvenirs. Therefore, I recommend exchanging USD$300 to USD$350 beforehand and supplementing with card payments. Many restaurants and massage shops accept card payments, which allows for flexible budgeting.

> [Complete Guide to Currency Exchange for Indonesia/Bali Travel: What currency is used? How to exchange money most cost-effectively? Where is the best place to exchange money?](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)

> [How much cash to prepare for a Bali trip?](/blog/2023-09-11-64fdaddefd89780001bdb780/)

## 2️⃣ **Internet**

I recommend purchasing a local SIM card after arriving in Bali, as it’s cheaper and offers more stable connectivity (this might be a personal feeling). The airport provides free Wi-Fi, so there’s no need to worry about contacting your driver. The SIM card we purchased locally offered 18GB of data per person, which was more than enough for frequent social media use, YouTube streaming, and app downloads over the seven days. So, you can choose a data plan based on your usage needs.

> [Essential for Bali Travel: How to Get a SIM Card in Bali](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)

## 3️⃣ **Visa, Tourist Tax, and Customs**

Remember to apply for your visa online before departure. You can also complete your customs declaration online three days in advance to avoid long queues at the airport and save time.

> [Starting 2026: Indonesia Bali Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Declaration System｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

## 4️⃣ **Transportation**

In Bali, apart from hiring a private driver, most short-distance travel or food delivery services rely on apps like Grab or Gojek. Make sure to set up your account in Taiwan (you'll need a Taiwanese phone number to receive verification codes) so you can use these services smoothly upon arrival.

> [Must-Download Apps for Traveling in Indonesia/Bali: Complete Guide to Gojek](/blog/2023-08-20-64e187dffd897800017304fe/)

## ⚠️ **Private Drivers Are Essential!**

Online information can be scattered, and we were completely disoriented when we arrived in Bali. Thankfully, our driver Komang WikaSutresna was a jack-of-all-trades. No matter what we asked, he always found the best value-for-money solutions. It seemed like he knew someone everywhere on the island, and he could always coordinate things for us—truly amazing!

When we first arrived in Bali, we asked Komang directly about the best places to buy SIM cards and exchange money, and he took us there right away. The money exchange place seemed to be one of his trusted spots, so the process was incredibly fast. The SIM card shop even offered free drinks—such great service!

![潘奕勳提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4c2d1ab0f9e7203c313606371c0d77c9.jpg)

Bali's traffic can be chaotic, so for safety-conscious travelers like us, choosing a careful and steady driver is crucial. Luckily, Komang drove very cautiously, which made us feel at ease. Before heading to Uluwatu for a performance, my glasses were snatched by a monkey, causing us to miss part of the show. However, seeing our disappointment, Komang went out of his way to ask the 'White Monkey General' from the fire dance performance, allowing us to take photos with him (seeing the Monkey General up close was truly impressive XDD). This close encounter with local traditional culture became a very special memory for us.
