## 1️⃣ **Exchanging Indonesian Rupiah**

During our trip, each of us exchanged around USD$320, which was just enough for the 7-day, 6-night itinerary, and we even bought quite a few souvenirs. Therefore, I recommend exchanging USD$300 to $350 beforehand and supplementing with card payments. Many restaurants and massage parlors accept card payments, allowing you to manage your budget flexibly.

> [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide: What currency is used in Indonesia/Bali? How to get the best exchange rate? Where is the best place to exchange money?](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)

> [How much cash should you prepare for a Bali trip?](/blog/2023-09-11-64fdaddefd89780001bdb780/)

## 2️⃣ **Internet**

It's recommended to purchase a local SIM card after arriving in Bali, as it's cheaper and offers more stable connectivity (this might be a personal opinion). The airport provides free Wi-Fi, so there's no need to worry about not being able to contact your driver. The SIM card we purchased locally offered a total of 18GB per person, which was more than enough for seven days, even with frequent use of social media, watching YouTube videos, and downloading apps. Therefore, you can choose a data package that suits your needs based on your usage.

> [【Essential for Bali Travel】How to Get a Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)

## 3️⃣ **Visa, Tourist Tax, and Customs**

Remember to apply for your visa online before departure. You can also complete your customs declaration online three days in advance to avoid long queues at the airport and save time.

> [2026 Indonesia Bali Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Declaration System｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

## 4️⃣ **Transportation**

In Bali, apart from hiring a private driver, most short-distance travel or food delivery services are accessed through the Grab or Gojek apps. Make sure to set up your account in Taiwan (you'll need a Taiwanese phone number to receive the verification code) so you can use it smoothly once you arrive.

> [Essential apps to download for traveling in Indonesia/Bali: A complete guide to Gojek](/blog/2023-08-20-64e187dffd897800017304fe/)

## ⚠️ **Private Drivers Are Essential!**

Because information online is very scattered, we were still disoriented even after arriving in Bali. Fortunately, our driver, Komang WikaSutresna, was a jack-of-all-trades. No matter what we asked, he always found us the best value-for-money options. It seemed like he knew someone everywhere in Bali, and he could always help us coordinate things wherever we went—truly amazing!

When we first arrived in Bali, we asked Komang directly about the best places to buy SIM cards and exchange money, and he took us there right away. The money exchange place seemed to be one he knew well, so the process was very quick, and the SIM card shop even offered free drinks—service was excellent!  
  
Bali's traffic can be chaotic, and for safety-conscious travelers like us, choosing a careful and steady driver is crucial. Luckily, our driver Komang was very cautious, which made us feel at ease. Before heading to Uluwatu for a performance, my glasses were snatched by a monkey, causing us to miss part of the show. However, seeing our disappointment, Komang went out of his way to ask the White Monkey General of the fire dance performance, allowing us to take photos with him (seeing the Monkey General up close was truly awe-inspiring XDD). This close encounter with local traditional culture was a very special memory for us.
