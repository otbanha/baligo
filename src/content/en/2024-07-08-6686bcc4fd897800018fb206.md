## Pre-Trip Preparation

1. Visa on Arrival: Before heading to Bali, remember to apply for an electronic visa on arrival.
2. Tourism Tax: Be sure to pay the tourism tax.
3. Fill Out Arrival Form: Complete the arrival form.
4. Currency Exchange: It’s recommended to bring USD and exchange it for Indonesian Rupiah locally.
5. Sun Protection: Bali’s sun is very intense. If you forget sunscreen, you’ll easily get tanned.
6. Internet Usage: Consider using an eSIM card. I purchased the 'Global Travel Expert' eSIM card on Shopee, and unless you’re in remote mountainous areas, the internet speed is stable. Buy it the day before, and it’s ready to use the next day—super convenient.
7. Water Activities & Photos: If you plan to take photos after water activities, remember to bring a 220V hair dryer.
8. Massage Experience: No matter which massage place you choose, it feels like a lottery. Luckily, we’ve always had great experiences, even at random roadside shops.
9. Photography Tips: To capture Instagram-worthy photos, research poses beforehand and have your travel companion practice their photography skills. I’m the one who avoids taking bad shots.
10. Tipping Etiquette: Bali is a country where tipping is customary. The basic tip is around 10,000 IDR (about USD$0.65). Avoid giving coins or very small bills—it’s more polite.
11. Power Adapter: Remember to bring a power adapter, or check with your hotel if they can lend you one.
12. Additional Taxes: Some restaurants and massage places may charge extra taxes, and credit card payments might include a 3% surcharge.

- Time: 1:30 PM

**Sea Walking**

Our first activity was sea walking. While it sounded exciting, the actual experience was somewhat disappointing. When we first put on the helmets, water seeped in without any warning, pressing directly against our faces, which was quite frightening. Walking underwater required holding onto a railing, and the scenery wasn't particularly stunning.

**Paragliding**

Next up was the paragliding activity. This experience was truly amazing, especially the unforgettable sense of freedom we felt while soaring through the air. Perhaps it was because we had such great conversations with our instructor that made the whole process even more enjoyable. The paraglider would descend from the sky into the water and then ascend again - this back-and-forth motion was incredibly fun.

**Donuts**

Finally, we tried the donut (tube) ride. It's said that the donut rides in Taiwan are more thrilling, especially when sitting in the back seats. Besides this, there are various other water activities available at South Bay to cater to different preferences.

**Snacks and Tea**

After the activity concludes, light refreshments and tea will be provided, allowing everyone to relax and unwind after the thrilling experience.

**Important Notes**

- **Price Negotiation**: Activity prices tend to be quoted high, so it's advisable to bargain or purchase discounted tickets online in advance.
- **Photography Fees**: If your itinerary includes photography services, this will incur an additional charge. Vendors typically provide a set of cloud-stored photos and the recorded footage.

## Recommended Private Drivers

When traveling in Bali, finding a good private driver is crucial, especially when you're exhausted and struggling with English translations. We highly recommend Mang Loco (https://www.facebook.com/komang.rika.501/), a Balinese who previously worked in Taiwan. He speaks fluent and amusing Chinese, learned from indigenous people, and has a friendly personality, making you feel like he's a helpful friend.

### Tip:

1. Itinerary Planning: You can roughly plan the attractions you want to visit and let the driver help arrange the route.
2. Cost Communication: Discuss in advance the costs for longer distances and overtime fees.
3. Meal Invitation: Drivers usually don't dine with guests, but if you like your driver, you can invite him and treat him to a meal.
