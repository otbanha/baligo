## Pre-Departure Preparation

1. E-Visa on Arrival: Remember to apply for the e-visa on arrival before heading to Bali.
2. Tourism Tax: Be aware of paying the tourism tax.
3. Fill in the Arrival Form: Complete the arrival form.
4. Currency Exchange: It's advisable to bring USD and exchange it for Indonesian Rupiah locally.
5. Sun Protection: Bali's sun is very strong; forgetting sunscreen can easily lead to sunburn.
6. Internet Usage: It's recommended to use an Esim card. I purchased the 'Global Travel Expert' Esim card on Shopee, and unless you're in remote mountainous areas, the internet speed is stable. You can buy it one day and use it the next, very convenient.
7. Water Activities and Photos: If you plan to take beautiful photos after water activities, remember to bring a 220V hairdryer.
8. Massage Experience: No matter which massage shop you choose, it feels like a lottery. Fortunately, our massage experiences were all great, even at randomly chosen roadside shops.
9. Photography Tips: To take stunning photos, it's recommended to research poses beforehand and practice shooting techniques with your travel companion. I'm the one who doesn't take bad photos.
10. Tipping Etiquette: Bali is a country where tipping is customary. The basic tip is around 10,000 Rupiah (approximately USD$0.65), and it's more polite not to give coins or small bills.
11. Power Adapter: Remember to bring a power adapter or ask the hotel if they can lend one.
12. Additional Taxes: Some restaurants and massage shops may charge additional taxes, and there might be a 3% surcharge for credit card payments.

- Time: 13:30

**Sea Walking**

Our first activity was sea walking. While it sounded exciting, the actual experience was somewhat disappointing. When putting on the helmet, water leaked in without warning, pressing against my face and causing considerable fear. Walking underwater required holding onto a railing, but the scenery wasn't particularly impressive.

**Parasailing**

Next was parasailing. This activity was truly fantastic, especially the unforgettable feeling of freedom while flying through the air. Perhaps because we had a great conversation with the instructor, the whole experience was even more enjoyable. The parasail pulled us from the air into the water and back up again, making for a fun back-and-forth experience.

**Banana Boat**

Finally, we tried the banana boat (towed inflatable) activity. It's said that banana boat rides in Taiwan are more thrilling, especially when sitting in the back. Besides these, Nusa Dua offers a variety of other water activities to cater to different preferences.

**Snacks and Tea**

After the activity ends, light refreshments and tea will be provided, allowing everyone to take a short break after enjoying the thrilling experience.

**Reminders**

- **Price Negotiation**: Activity prices are often quoted high, so it's recommended to negotiate or purchase discounted tickets online in advance.
- **Photography Fees**: If photography services are included in the itinerary, these come at an additional cost. The vendor will provide a set of cloud photos and recorded videos.

Editor's Note: Visitors should note that water activities in Nusa Dua are quite popular, so it's advisable to book in advance to avoid on-the-spot price hikes. Booking ensures you have ample opportunities to experience all the water activities you desire while avoiding costly additional fees on-site. At Nusa Dua Beach, let's set sail together and enjoy this paradise of water activities, offering you an adventurous and laughter-filled sea journey.

## Recommended Private Driver

When traveling in Bali, finding a good private driver is crucial, especially when you're tired and translating English can be exhausting. We recommend [Mang Loco](https://www.facebook.com/komang.rika.501/), a Balinese who once worked in Taiwan. He learned Chinese fluently and amusingly from indigenous people, and his friendly personality makes him feel like a helpful friend.

### Tips:

1. Itinerary Planning: You can roughly plan the attractions you want to visit and let the driver arrange the route for you.
2. Cost Communication: Discuss longer trips and overtime fees in advance.
3. Meal Invitation: Drivers usually don't eat with guests, but if you like your driver, you can invite him to join you for a meal.
