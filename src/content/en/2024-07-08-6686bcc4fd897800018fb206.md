Although this Bali trip was only 5 days and 4 nights, it felt like there was too little time for actual activities after deducting travel time on the first and last days! ## Pre-Trip Preparations

1. Electronic Visa on Arrival: Remember to apply for an electronic visa on arrival before traveling to Bali.
2. Tourist Tax: Be aware of paying the tourist tax.
3. Fill in the Arrival Form: Complete the arrival form.
4. Currency Exchange: It is recommended to bring USD and exchange it for Indonesian Rupiah locally.
5. Sun Protection: Bali's sunlight is very intense, and forgetting sunscreen can easily lead to sunburn.
6. Internet Usage: It is recommended to use an Esim card. I purchased the 'Global Cross-Border Travel Expert' Esim card on Shopee, and the internet speed was stable except in remote mountainous areas. It is very convenient as you can buy it one day and use it the next.
7. Taking Photos After Water Activities: If you plan to take photos after water activities, remember to bring a 220V hair dryer.
8. Massage Experience: Every massage experience feels like a lottery. Fortunately, our massage experiences were all good, even at randomly chosen roadside shops.
9. Photography Tips: To take Instagram-worthy photos, it is recommended to research poses in advance and practice photography skills with your travel companions. I am the one who is not good at taking photos.
10. Tipping Etiquette: Bali is a country where tipping is customary. The basic tip is usually around 10,000 Rupiah (approximately USD$0.65), and it is more polite not to give coins or very small bills.
11. Power Adapter: Remember to bring a power adapter or ask the hotel if they can lend one.
12. Additional Taxes: Some restaurants and massage shops may charge additional taxes, and there might be a 3% surcharge for credit card payments.

****Sea Walking****

Our first activity was sea walking. While it sounded exciting, the actual experience was somewhat disappointing. When putting on the helmet, water leaked in without warning, pressing against my face and causing fear. Walking underwater required holding onto a railing, and the scenery wasn’t particularly stunning.

****Parasailing****

Next up was the parasailing activity. This activity was truly amazing, especially when we were flying through the air - that feeling of freedom was unforgettable. Perhaps it was because we had such a great chat with our instructor that made the whole experience even more enjoyable. The parasailing took us from the air down to the water and back up again - this back-and-forth motion was incredibly fun.

****Donuts****

Finally, we tried the donut (banana boat) activity. It's said that the donut rides in Taiwan are more thrilling, especially when sitting in the back row. Besides this, there are many other water activities available at Nanwan to satisfy different preferences.

****Snacks and Tea****

After the activities, snacks and tea were provided, allowing everyone to take a short break after all the excitement.

****Reminders****

- ****Price Negotiation****: The initial prices for activities are quite high, so it's recommended to bargain or purchase discounted tickets online in advance.
- ****Photo Fees****: If there's a photography service included in the activity, this comes at an additional cost. The vendor will provide a set of cloud photos and video footage.

Editor's Note: Visitors should be aware that water activities at Nanwan are quite popular. It's recommended to book in advance to avoid on-the-spot price hikes. Booking ensures you have ample opportunity to experience all the water activities you want while avoiding additional expensive fees on-site. At Nanwan Beach, let's set sail together and enjoy this paradise of water activities, bringing you an adventurous and laughter-filled sea journey.

Editor’s Note: Pandawa Beach in Bali (also known as Tanah Barak Cliff) is a hidden gem that has recently become a popular spot. This newly opened beach is stunning with its turquoise waters and colorful rock formations. Besides the magnificent ocean views, the road leading to the beach is carved through massive limestone cliffs, creating a dramatic visual impact. You can also climb the rocks in the middle for better photo angles. Pandawa Beach is about a 30-minute drive from the airport, and an entrance ticket is required (currently Rp8,000 for adults and Rp3,000 for children). It’s open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

## Recommended Private Driver

When traveling in Bali, finding a good private driver is crucial, especially when you're tired and translating English becomes a hassle. We recommend [Mang Loco](https://www.facebook.com/komang.rika.501/), a Balinese who once worked in Taiwan. He learned Chinese fluently and amusingly from indigenous people, and his friendly personality makes him feel like a helpful friend.

### Tips:

1. Itinerary Planning: You can roughly plan the attractions you want to visit and let the driver arrange the route for you.
2. Cost Communication: Discuss longer distances and overtime fees in advance.
3. Meal Invitation: Drivers usually don't eat with guests, but if you like your driver, you can invite him to join you for a meal.
