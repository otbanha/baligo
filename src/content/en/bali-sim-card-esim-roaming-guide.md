While Bali hotels offer free Wi-Fi, mobile internet remains essential when you're out exploring, whether for navigation, research, social media updates, or photo sharing.

There are three main ways to get mobile internet in Bali:

First, rent a 4G hotspot; Second, use international roaming; Third, purchase a local SIM card in Bali and top up for internet access.

## Using International Roaming

The second option is to use international roaming, which has become quite affordable these days. It's recommended to check with your telecom provider for their international roaming packages, as they usually offer multiple options. The advantage of using your original number for roaming is that you don't need to change SIM cards or rent a WiFi hotspot, saving you a lot of hassle.

## Purchasing a Local SIM Card

For those staying longer in Bali or requiring more data usage, it's advisable to get a local SIM card and purchase a data package, which is more cost-effective. Just bring your passport to register, and it's best to have your driver take you to avoid being scammed.

SIM cards and data packages purchased at the airport are the most expensive, with some people paying up to three times the normal price! Therefore, it's recommended to go to an outside store. Since they need to register your passport, having your driver take you ensures you'll be brought to a legitimate store, preventing issues like identity theft or overcharging.

In Bali, Telkomsel, the state-owned Indonesian telecommunications company, offers the best signal coverage for local SIM cards, for your reference.
