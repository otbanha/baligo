## Bali Tourism Reaches New Heights in 2024

![峇里島今年之前的國際觀光客人數](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d81b29ecc544bc3b5de481cb82ab455b.png)

### Overview of Bali's International Tourist Data

According to data from Indonesia's Central Statistics Agency, Bali welcomed 7.75 million international tourists from January to the end of July 2024. Pudji Ismartini, Deputy Director of Distribution and Service Statistics at the Central Statistics Agency, stated, 'The number of foreign tourist visits by July 2024 is the highest since 2020.'

Many tourists from around the world choose Bali as their vacation destination to enjoy its sunshine, beaches, and rich cultural activities.

Bali's pleasant climate and friendly community make it a dream destination for many, attracting a significant number of international expatriates.

## Tourist Statistics for July 2024

### Monthly Tourist Growth Trend

In July 2024, Bali experienced its largest tourist growth in recent years. A total of 1.31 million international tourists arrived at Ngurah Rai International Airport, marking a 9.42% increase from June 2024 and a 16.91% increase from July 2023.

### Main Tourist Source Countries

Tourists from Malaysia, Australia, China, and Singapore are the primary international visitors to Bali. In July 2024, Bali welcomed 177,000 Malaysian tourists, followed closely by 166,000 Australian visitors. Chinese tourists totaled 126,000 (including Taiwan), while Singaporean visitors numbered 102,000.

## Bali Airport Operations

### The Bustling Ngurah Rai International Airport

Ngurah Rai International Airport has become the main gateway for international tourists arriving in Bali, experiencing its busiest period in history in 2024.

### The Role of Soekarno-Hatta Airport

Located in Jakarta, Soekarno-Hatta Airport also plays a crucial role in international travel, with the two airports collectively handling 90% of the country's international arrivals and departures.

## Travel Behavior and Trends

### Average Length of Stay

New data reveals that tourists vacationing in Bali stay an average of 8.1 nights. Visitors from Southeast Asian countries stay for an average of 3.67 nights, while African tourists have the longest average stay at 15.74 nights.

### Stay Patterns by Country

Visitors from different countries show significant variations in their length of stay in Bali, reflecting their travel plans and needs.

## Measures to Enhance Service Quality at Bali Airport

### Immigration Service Optimization

To accommodate the increasing number of tourists, Bali Airport has been continuously improving its immigration services to ensure smooth customs clearance for travelers.

### Impact of the Indonesia-Africa Forum

During the Indonesia-Africa Forum held from September 1st to 3rd, 2024, Bali Airport set up dedicated immigration check counters to facilitate easier entry for attendees and visitors.

## Policy Changes and Future Challenges

### Updates on Tourism Development Policies

Last week, at the Indonesia Quality Tourism Conference, Luhut Binsar Pandjaitan, the Coordinating Minister for Maritime Affairs and Investment, announced a series of new policies aimed at better managing the impacts of overtourism. These policies are designed to promote sustainable tourism development, ensuring the benefits for both tourists and local communities.

## Cultural Preservation and Sustainable Development

### The Importance of Preserving Bali's Culture

Minister Pandjaitan emphasized the importance of maintaining Bali's culture, noting that without it, Bali would no longer be a paradise island.

### Advancing Sustainable Tourism

To achieve sustainable tourism development, the government and businesses need to work together to promote environmental and cultural preservation measures.
