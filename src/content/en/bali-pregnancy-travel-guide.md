> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Liu Huici](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/UxWzce2FRdK2gmsT/):

**Key Takeaways**: The key to traveling during pregnancy is **thorough preparation and a flexible itinerary**—arrange a Chinese-speaking driver, e-visa, tourist tax, and customs declaration online in advance. On departure day, prepare health certificates and essential medications (stomach and cold medicine). This 5-day, 4-night route is **Sanur → Nusa Lembongan → Ubud → Canggu**, with a chill pace and no rigid schedule, so you can still enjoy your trip during pregnancy.

This Bali trip was an exciting first-time experience for me! I'm very grateful to the friends who shared their tips in the [Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide), which helped me find an amazing Chinese-speaking driver. I'll introduce him at the end of the article.

## Pre-Departure Essentials:

1. **Excellent Chinese-Speaking Driver**
   Having a Chinese-speaking driver on this trip meant I never had to worry about transportation. His Chinese was very fluent, and he not only took us to all the popular local spots but also flexibly adjusted the itinerary based on time and traffic, making the journey smoother.
2. **Confirm Itinerary with Driver**
   Before departure, remember to communicate your daily schedule with the driver in advance. The driver will offer professional advice based on your needs, ensuring the best experience at each attraction.
3. **Exchange TWD to USD**
   Before leaving, remember to exchange some New Taiwan Dollars to US Dollars, then exchange them for Indonesian Rupiah upon arrival in Bali. This is the most cost-effective way to exchange money.
4. **E-Visa**
   Don't forget to apply for your Indonesian e-visa in advance. This will save you time queuing at airport immigration and make the start of your trip smoother.
5. **Tourist Tax**
   Bali charges a tourist tax for incoming visitors. It's recommended to pay it online in advance to avoid cumbersome procedures upon arrival.
6. **Customs Declaration Form**
   Remember to fill out the customs declaration form online three days before departure. This will save you a lot of time when entering the country.
7. **Health Certificate and Tickets**
   On departure day, remember to get your tickets and fill out the health certificate to ensure all documents are in order, avoiding last-minute panic at the airport.
8. **Prepare Essential Medications**
   Although this trip wasn't long, it's still a good idea to pack some basic medications just in case, such as stomach and cold medicine.

These preparations may seem tedious, but they all contribute to a smoother journey, allowing me to fully enjoy every moment in Bali!

## Day 1 Itinerary:

**Arrive in Bali at 15:15**
The plane touched down right on schedule at Bali's international airport, kicking off an eagerly anticipated journey!

**Present Completed Health Certificate**
After disembarking, the first step was to show the pre-filled health certificate. This process was quick and smooth, giving us a sense of Bali's efficient pandemic protocols.

**Present Completed E-Visa**
Next up was presenting the e-visa. Since we had it ready in advance, we skipped the queues and used facial recognition for fast clearance—super convenient and saved us a lot of hassle!

**Present Customs Declaration Form**
The final step was showing the customs declaration form, which we also completed online beforehand. The entire entry process went incredibly smoothly.

**Wait for Luggage**
The immigration process was so fast that waiting for our luggage ended up taking the longest! 😂 But once we had our bags, we felt much more relaxed.

**Meet the Driver**
As soon as we exited the airport, our pre-booked driver was already waiting outside. Seeing him made the whole trip feel more grounded—ready to start Bali's culinary adventure!

## Day 2 Itinerary (Nusa Lembongan Adventure):

**Morning Hotel Pickup by Driver**
Early in the morning, our private driver arrived right on time at our hotel to pick us up. Today's destination was the stunning Nusa Lembongan, and we couldn't contain our excitement!

**Buying a Nusa Lembongan Tour at the Sanur Pier**
Once we reached the pier in Sanur, we bought a day tour to Nusa Lembongan on the spot. There were plenty of options, and we chose one that included snorkeling and sightseeing—excellent value for money.

**Thoughtful Arrangement of a Chinese-Speaking Guide**
The best part of the tour was that they arranged a Chinese-speaking guide to accompany us throughout the day, explaining everything along the way. This made our trip so much easier and more enriching—no language barriers, and we got deeper insights into the local landscapes and culture.

**Exciting Activities: Snorkeling, Devil's Tears, Dream Beach, Mangrove Ecology**
We snorkeled at a spot off Nusa Lembongan, enjoying crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine life—colorful tropical fish swimming all around us, absolutely breathtaking! Then we visited the famous 'Devil's Tears,' where massive waves crashed against the rocks, sending spray flying—a dramatic and awe-inspiring sight. Next came Dream Beach, which truly lived up to its name with its fine white sand and brilliant blue water, perfect for total relaxation. Finally, we explored the mangrove ecological area, getting up close with this natural wonder. The whole itinerary was rich and varied.

### ❤️ Reflections:

1. **Prepare your clearance documents in advance!**
   During this trip, I noticed many travelers handling their paperwork on the spot, causing long lines and chaos. So be sure to prepare health certificates, e-visas, customs declarations, and other documents ahead of time to breeze through immigration smoothly.
2. **Bali has many attractions, but traffic is heavy—don't overpack your itinerary!**
   Bali's sights are incredibly diverse, but traffic can be intense, especially during peak hours. I recommend not cramming your schedule too tightly to avoid wasting time on the road. The best experience here is simply to chill and soak in the local pace of life.
3. **Haggle at market stalls!**
   When shopping at markets, always bargain! For example, I bought a coin purse at Love Anchor, haggling the price from 320,000 IDR down to 140,000 IDR, and at another stall, I even got it for 100,000 IDR. So don't be shy—negotiate boldly! 😅
4. **Poor English and three months pregnant, but lucky to have a local who speaks Chinese**
   During this trip, I was three months pregnant and worried about my English skills, but luckily I met a local driver named Kang Ge (Wayan Suantara) who speaks Chinese. His thoughtful help made the entire journey stress-free, especially with route planning and attraction recommendations.
5. **Souvenir recommendation: BALI BANANA banana cake**
   Don't forget to grab a box of BALI BANANA banana cake before heading home—it's a signature Balinese souvenir with a soft, sweet, and delicious texture, available at the airport. You can buy one to try upon arrival, and if you like it, stock up more. They even offer free packing service for convenience.
