## Essential Preparations Before Departure:

1. **An Amazing Chinese-Speaking Driver**  
   Having a Chinese-speaking private driver made this trip stress-free. His fluent Chinese not only took us to popular local spots but also adjusted the itinerary flexibly based on time and traffic, ensuring a smooth journey.
2. **Confirm Itinerary with the Driver**  
   Before departure, make sure to communicate daily plans with the driver in advance. The driver will offer professional suggestions based on your needs to ensure the best experience at each destination.
3. **Exchange NT Dollars to USD**  
   Before leaving, exchange some USD and convert it to Indonesian Rupiah upon arrival in Bali. This is the most cost-effective way to handle currency exchange.
4. **E-Visa**  
   Don’t forget to apply for Indonesia’s e-visa in advance to save time at the airport immigration queue and start your journey smoothly.
5. **Tourism Tax**  
   Bali charges a tourism tax for inbound travelers. It’s recommended to pay it online in advance to avoid hassle upon arrival.
6. **Customs Declaration Form**  
   Three days before departure, remember to fill out the customs declaration form online to save time during immigration.
7. **Health Declaration and Flight Ticket**  
   On the day of departure, ensure you have your flight ticket and complete the health declaration form to avoid last-minute panic at the airport.
8. **Prepare Essential Medications**  
   Although this trip isn’t long, it’s wise to bring basic medications like stomach medicine and cold medicine, just in case.

These preparations may seem tedious, but they ensure a smoother journey, allowing me to fully enjoy every moment in Bali!

## Day 1 Itinerary:

**Arrival in Bali 15:15**  
The plane landed on time at Bali International Airport, officially kicking off our much-anticipated journey!

**Present Completed Health Declaration**  
After disembarking, the first step was to present the pre-filled health declaration. This process was completed quickly, giving us a sense of Bali's smooth health protocols.

**Present Completed eVisa**  
Next was presenting the eVisa. Since we had prepared it in advance, we didn’t need to queue and used facial recognition technology for swift clearance—super convenient and hassle-free!

**Present Customs Declaration Form**  
The final step was presenting the customs declaration form, which was also completed online beforehand, making the entire immigration process seamless.

**Wait for Luggage**  
The clearance was so fast that waiting for our luggage ended up taking the longest time 🤣! But once we had our bags, we felt much more relaxed.

**Meet the Driver**  
As soon as we exited the airport, our pre-booked driver was waiting for us. Seeing him instantly made the trip feel more solid—ready to begin Bali’s culinary adventure!

**Dinner: Naughty Nuri's Warung, Sanur (Delicious Pork Ribs)**

## Day 2 Itinerary (Nusa Lembongan Adventure):

**Driver Pickup at Hotel in the Morning**  
Early in the morning, our private driver arrived punctually at our hotel to pick us up. Today’s adventure was heading to the beautiful Nusa Lembongan, and we were full of anticipation!

**Purchase Nusa Lembongan Tour at Sanur Pier**  
Upon arriving at Sanur Pier, we purchased a day trip to Nusa Lembongan on the spot. There were many options available, and we chose a package that included snorkeling and sightseeing, which offered great value.

**Thoughtful Arrangement: Chinese-Speaking Guide**  
The best part of the tour was that they arranged a Chinese-speaking guide for us. The guide accompanied us throughout the trip, providing explanations and ensuring a smooth and enriching experience. We didn’t have to worry about language barriers and gained deeper insights into the local natural landscapes and culture.

**Exciting Activities: Snorkeling, Devil’s Tear, Dream Beach, Mangrove Forest**  
At Nusa Lembongan’s snorkeling spot, we enjoyed the crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine life. Colorful tropical fish swam around us, creating a truly mesmerizing experience! Next, we visited the famous Devil’s Tear, where massive waves crashed against the rocks, creating a spectacular and awe-inspiring sight. Later, we arrived at Dream Beach, which lived up to its name with its fine white sand and turquoise waters—a perfect place to unwind. Finally, we explored the mangrove forest, getting up close to this natural wonder. The entire itinerary was diverse and fulfilling.

## Day 3 Itinerary (Ubud Day Trip):

**White Water Rafting Adventure**  
Our Ubud adventure began with thrilling white water rafting. Navigating through Bali's magnificent tropical rainforests and drifting along crystal-clear rivers, we felt the excitement of the rapids, making it a top choice for adventure enthusiasts. The entire experience was filled with surprises and challenges, leaving an unforgettable impression!

**Instagrammable Cafe: Swing Photos**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57b0b41ac469b22436f739a53fe74f4f.jpg)

After rafting, we arrived at a very famous Instagrammable cafe known for its giant swing. Here, we could swing while enjoying the stunning views of the rice fields. The locals are really skilled at photography, and the atmosphere made every photo look like a postcard. It's truly a great spot for photos and check-ins.

![Tebasari Resto, Bar & Lounge](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_de5feafe85fbc690903411bfb7bf280f.jpg)

**Classic Bebek Bengil (Dirty Duck)**  
For lunch, we went to the famous Bebek Bengil restaurant in Ubud. This dish is a classic Ubud delicacy—crispy duck skin paired with tender duck meat, complemented by various local sauces, making it absolutely mouthwatering! It's definitely a must-try dish in Bali.

**Ubud Palace**  
After lunch, we visited the historic Ubud Palace. The palace's architecture is distinctly Balinese, with intricate carvings and elaborate decorations that left us in awe, immersing us in the local culture and history.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2cb88da21144a9adcc0b17e6c870ab0a.jpg)

**Ubud Market**  
Next, we strolled through Ubud Market, which is filled with various handicrafts, clothing, and souvenirs. The market's lively atmosphere was infectious, and we picked up a few locally made souvenirs to remember this trip by.

### ❤️ Reflections:

1. **Prepare Your Travel Documents in Advance!**  
   During this trip, I noticed many travelers only started handling their documents at the last minute, causing long queues and chaos. Make sure to prepare your health certificates, e-visas, and customs declarations ahead of time to ensure a smoother entry process.
2. **Bali Has Many Attractions, but Traffic Can Be Heavy—Don’t Overpack Your Itinerary!**  
   While Bali offers a diverse range of attractions, traffic can be intense, especially during peak hours. It’s best not to over-schedule your days to avoid wasting too much time in transit. The best way to experience Bali is to chill and embrace the local pace of life.
3. **Always Haggle at Markets!**  
   When shopping at markets, don’t forget to haggle! For example, I managed to negotiate a coin purse down from USD$10 to USD$5 at Love Anchor, and even got another item down to USD$3. There’s always room for negotiation, so don’t be shy—speak up! 😅
4. **Limited English + Three Months Pregnant—Thankfully, Locals Who Speak Chinese Helped!**  
   During this trip, despite being three months pregnant and worried about my limited English, I was fortunate to have the help of a local driver, Kang Ge (Wayan Suantara), who speaks Chinese. His thoughtful assistance made the journey much smoother, especially with route planning and attraction recommendations.
5. **Souvenir Recommendation: BALI BANANA Banana Cake**  
   Don’t forget to pick up a box of BALI BANANA banana cake before heading home—it’s a signature Balinese souvenir with a soft, sweet texture. You can find it at the airport. Try it upon arrival, and if you like it, stock up for your return. They even offer free packing services, making shopping more convenient.
