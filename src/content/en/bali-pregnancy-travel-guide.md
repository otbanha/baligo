> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Liu Huici](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/UxWzce2FRdK2gmsT/):

**Key Takeaways**: The key to traveling during pregnancy is **thorough preparation and a flexible itinerary**—arrange a Chinese-speaking private driver, e-visa, tourist tax, and customs declaration online in advance, and prepare health certificates and essential medications (stomach medicine, cold medicine) on departure day. This 5-day, 4-night route is **Sanur → Nusa Lembongan → Ubud → Canggu**, with a chill pace and no rigid schedule—pregnancy travel can still be thoroughly enjoyable.

This Bali free-and-easy trip was an exciting first-time experience for me! I'm very grateful to the friends who shared tips in the [Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide), which helped me find an amazing Chinese-speaking private driver—I'll introduce him in detail at the end of the article.

## Essential Preparations Before Departure:

1. **Excellent Chinese-Speaking Driver**
   Having a Chinese-speaking private driver on this trip meant I never had to worry about transportation. His Chinese was very fluent, and he not only took us to all the popular local spots but also flexibly adjusted the itinerary based on time and traffic conditions, making the journey smoother.
2. **Confirm Itinerary with the Driver**
   Before departure, remember to communicate the daily schedule with the driver in advance. The driver will offer professional advice based on our needs, ensuring the best experience at each attraction.
3. **Exchange TWD to USD**
   Before leaving, remember to exchange some New Taiwan Dollars to US Dollars, then exchange them for Indonesian Rupiah upon arrival in Bali. This is the most cost-effective way to handle currency.
4. **E-Visa**
   Don't forget to apply for the Indonesian e-visa in advance—this saves time queuing at airport immigration and ensures a smooth start to the trip.
5. **Tourist Tax**
   Bali charges a tourist tax for incoming visitors. It's recommended to pay it online in advance to avoid cumbersome procedures upon arrival.
6. **Customs Declaration Form**
   Fill out the customs declaration form online three days before departure to save time during entry.
7. **Health Certificate and Ticket**
   On departure day, remember to obtain your ticket and fill out the health certificate to ensure all documents are complete and avoid last-minute panic at the airport.
8. **Prepare Essential Medications**
   Although this trip wasn't long, it's still wise to pack some basic medications just in case, such as stomach medicine and cold medicine.

These preparations may seem tedious, but they all contribute to a smoother journey, allowing me to fully enjoy every moment in Bali!

## Day 1 Itinerary:

**Arrival in Bali at 15:15**
The plane touched down right on time at Bali's international airport, marking the official start of our long-awaited adventure!

**Present Completed Health Certificate**
After disembarking, the first step was to show our pre-filled health certificate. This process was quick and smooth, showing that Bali's pandemic protocols are still running efficiently.

**Present Completed E-Visa**
Next up was presenting our e-visa. Since we had it ready in advance, we didn't need to queue—just used facial recognition technology to breeze through immigration. It was incredibly convenient and saved us a lot of hassle!

**Present Customs Declaration Form**
The final step was showing our customs declaration form, which we had also completed online beforehand. As a result, the entire entry process went very smoothly.

**Wait for Luggage**
We cleared customs so fast that waiting for our luggage ended up taking the longest time 🤣! But once we had our bags, we felt much more relaxed.

**Meet the Driver**
As soon as we exited the airport, our pre-booked driver was already waiting for us. Seeing him made the whole trip feel more grounded and real! Time to start our culinary journey through Bali.

### ❤️ Reflections:

1. **Make sure to prepare your entry documents in advance!**
   During this trip, I noticed many travelers only started handling their documents on-site, leading to long queues and chaos. So be sure to prepare health certificates, e-visas, customs declaration forms, and other documents in advance to get through immigration faster and smoother.
2. **Bali has many attractions, but traffic is heavy—don't overpack your itinerary!**
   Bali's attractions are incredibly diverse, but traffic is also heavy, especially during peak hours. It's best not to cram your schedule too full to avoid wasting too much time on the road. The best experience here is simply to chill and soak in the local pace of life.
3. **Remember to haggle at market stalls!**
   When shopping at markets, always bargain! For example, the coin purse I bought at Love Anchor went from the original price of 320 to 140, and at another stall, I even got it down to 100. So there's definitely room to negotiate—don't be shy, just go for it! 😅
4. **With limited English and being three months pregnant, I was grateful for a local who spoke Chinese**
   During this trip, though I was three months pregnant and worried about my English skills, I was lucky to meet a local driver named Kang Ge (Wayan Suantara) who speaks Chinese. His thoughtful help made the entire journey so reassuring, especially with route planning and attraction recommendations—he offered plenty of practical advice.
5. **Souvenir recommendation: BALI BANANA banana cake**
   Before heading home, don't forget to grab a box of BALI BANANA banana cake. It's a signature souvenir from Bali—soft, sweet, and delicious—and it's available at the airport. You can buy one to try upon arrival, and if you like it, buy more to take back. They even offer free packing service for easy shopping.
