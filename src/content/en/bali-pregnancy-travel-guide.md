**Key Takeaways**: The key to traveling during pregnancy is **thorough preparation and flexible scheduling**—arrange a Chinese-speaking private driver, e-visa, tourist tax, and customs declaration form online in advance. On departure day, prepare health certificates and essential medications (stomach medicine, cold medicine). This 5-day, 4-night route was **Sanur → Nusa Lembongan → Ubud → Canggu**, with a chill pace and no rigid itinerary—pregnancy travel can still be thoroughly enjoyable.

This Bali trip was an exciting first-time experience for me! I'm very grateful to the friends who shared tips in the [Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide), which helped me find an amazing Chinese-speaking private driver. I'll introduce him in detail at the end of the article.

## Pre-Departure Essentials:

1. **Excellent Chinese-Speaking Driver**
   Having a Chinese-speaking private driver on this trip meant I never had to worry about transportation. His Chinese was very fluent, and he not only took us to all the popular local spots but also flexibly adjusted the itinerary based on time and traffic, making the journey smoother.
2. **Confirm Itinerary with Driver**
   Before departure, be sure to communicate your daily schedule with the driver in advance. The driver will offer professional advice based on your needs, ensuring the best experience at each destination.
3. **Exchange TWD to USD**
   Before leaving, remember to exchange some New Taiwan Dollars for US Dollars, then convert to Indonesian Rupiah once in Bali. This is the most cost-effective way to exchange money.
4. **E-Visa**
   Don't forget to apply for Indonesia's e-visa in advance—this saves time queuing at airport immigration and ensures a smooth start to your trip.
5. **Tourist Tax**
   Bali charges a tourist tax for incoming visitors. It's recommended to pay online in advance to avoid cumbersome procedures upon arrival.
6. **Customs Declaration Form**
   Three days before departure, remember to fill out the customs declaration form online to save time when entering the country.
7. **Health Certificate and Tickets**
   On departure day, make sure to get your tickets and fill out the health certificate to ensure all documents are complete and avoid last-minute airport chaos.
8. **Prepare Essential Medications**
   Although this trip wasn't long, it's still wise to pack basic medications just in case, such as stomach medicine and cold medicine.

These preparations may seem tedious, but they all contribute to a smoother journey, allowing me to fully enjoy every moment in Bali!

## Day 1 Itinerary:

**Arrived in Bali at 3:15 PM**
The plane touched down right on schedule at Bali International Airport, officially kicking off our long-awaited adventure!

**Present Completed Health Certificate**
After disembarking, the first step was to show the health certificate we had filled out in advance. This process was quick and smooth, giving us a sense that Bali's pandemic protocols are still well-organized.

**Present Completed E-Visa**
Next came showing our e-visa. Since we had prepared it beforehand, we didn't need to queue—we used facial recognition technology to breeze through customs. This was incredibly convenient and saved us a lot of hassle!

**Present Customs Declaration Form**
The final step was presenting the customs declaration form, which we had also completed online. As a result, the entire entry process went very smoothly.

**Wait for Luggage**
The customs clearance was so fast that waiting for our luggage ended up taking the longest time 🤣! But once we had our bags, we felt much more relaxed.

**Meet the Driver**
As soon as we exited the airport, our pre-booked driver was already waiting outside. Seeing him made the whole trip feel more grounded! We were ready to start our culinary journey in Bali.

### ❤️ Reflections:

1. **Make sure to prepare all entry documents in advance!**
   During my trip, I noticed many travelers scrambling to handle paperwork on the spot, causing long queues and chaos. So be sure to prepare your health certificate, e-visa, customs declaration, and other documents ahead of time to breeze through immigration smoothly.
2. **Bali has plenty of attractions, but traffic is heavy—don't overpack your itinerary!**
   Bali's attractions are incredibly diverse, but traffic can be intense, especially during peak hours. It's best not to cram your schedule too tightly to avoid wasting time on the road. The real charm here is to chill and soak in the local pace of life.
3. **Don't forget to haggle at market stalls!**
   When shopping at markets, always bargain! For instance, I bought a coin purse at Love Anchor—originally priced at IDR 320, I got it down to IDR 140, and at another stall, even to IDR 100. So there's definitely room to negotiate—don't be shy, just go for it! 😅
4. **My English isn't great and I'm three months pregnant—thankfully, a local who speaks Chinese helped out**
   During this trip, despite being three months pregnant and worried about my English skills, I was lucky to meet a local driver named Kang Ge (Wayan Suantara) who speaks Chinese. His thoughtful assistance made the entire journey stress-free, especially with route planning and attraction recommendations—he offered tons of practical advice.
5. **Souvenir recommendation: BALI BANANA banana cake**
   Before heading home, don't forget to grab a box of BALI BANANA banana cake—a signature Bali souvenir with a soft, fluffy texture and sweet flavor. It's available at the airport. You can buy some to try upon arrival, and if you like it, stock up more for home. They even offer free packing service, making shopping a breeze.
