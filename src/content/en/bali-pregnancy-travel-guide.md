## Must-Do Preparations Before Departure:

1. **An Excellent Chinese-Speaking Driver**  
   Having a Chinese-speaking private driver made this trip stress-free. His fluent Chinese allowed him to take us to all the popular spots while flexibly adjusting the itinerary based on time and traffic, ensuring a smooth journey.
2. **Confirm Itinerary with the Driver**  
   Before departure, make sure to communicate your daily plans with the driver. They’ll provide professional suggestions based on your needs to ensure the best experience at each destination.
3. **Exchange USD Beforehand**  
   Remember to exchange some USD before your trip and convert it to Indonesian Rupiah once in Bali. This is the most cost-effective way to handle currency exchange.
4. **E-Visa**  
   Don’t forget to apply for Indonesia’s e-visa in advance. This saves time at immigration and ensures a smoother start to your journey.
5. **Tourism Tax**  
   Bali charges a tourism tax for incoming travelers. It’s recommended to pay this online in advance to avoid hassle upon arrival.
6. **Customs Declaration Form**  
   Three days before departure, fill out the customs declaration form online to save time during immigration.
7. **Health Declaration and Flight Ticket**  
   On the day of departure, ensure you have your flight ticket and complete the health declaration form. Having all documents ready avoids last-minute stress.
8. **Prepare Basic Medications**  
   Although this trip isn’t long, it’s wise to bring basic medications like stomach and cold medicine, just in case.

These preparations might seem tedious, but they make the journey smoother, allowing me to fully enjoy every moment in Bali!

## Day 1 Itinerary:

**Arrival in Bali at 15:15**  
The plane landed right on schedule at Bali International Airport, officially marking the start of our highly anticipated journey!

**Present Completed Health Declaration**  
After disembarking, the first step was to present the pre-filled health declaration. This step was completed quickly, giving us a sense of Bali's smooth and efficient health protocols.

**Present Completed E-Visa**  
Next up was presenting the e-visa. Since we had prepared it in advance, we didn’t need to queue and used facial recognition technology for a swift passage through immigration. This process was incredibly convenient and saved us a lot of hassle!

**Present Customs Declaration Form**  
The final step was presenting the customs declaration form, which we had also completed online beforehand. As a result, the entire entry process went very smoothly.

**Wait for Luggage**  
The immigration process was so fast that waiting for our luggage ended up taking the longest time 🤣! But once we had our bags, we felt much more relaxed.

**Meet the Driver**  
As soon as we exited the airport, our pre-booked driver was already waiting for us. Seeing him instantly made the trip feel more solid and organized. Ready to begin Bali’s culinary adventure!

**Dinner: Naughty Nuri's Warung, Sanur (Delicious Pork Ribs)**

## Day 2 Itinerary (Nusa Lembongan Adventure):

**Driver Pickup at the Hotel**  
Early in the morning, our private driver arrived promptly at our hotel to pick us up. Today's adventure was all about exploring the beautiful Nusa Lembongan, and we were filled with anticipation!

**Purchase Nusa Lembongan Tour at Sanur Pier**  
Upon arriving at Sanur Pier, we purchased a day trip to Nusa Lembongan on the spot. There were many options available, and we chose a package that included snorkeling and sightseeing, offering great value for money.

**Thoughtful Arrangement: Chinese-Speaking Guide**  
One of the best parts of the tour was the inclusion of a Chinese-speaking guide who accompanied us throughout the day, providing explanations and making the journey more enjoyable and informative. We didn't have to worry about language barriers and gained deeper insights into the local natural landscapes and culture.

**Exciting Activities: Snorkeling, Devil's Tear, Dream Beach, Mangrove Forest**  
At the snorkeling spot in Nusa Lembongan, we marveled at the crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine life, surrounded by colorful tropical fish—truly breathtaking! Next, we visited the famous Devil's Tear, where massive waves crashed against the rocks, creating a spectacular and awe-inspiring sight. Later, we arrived at Dream Beach, which lived up to its name with its fine white sand and turquoise waters—a perfect place to unwind. Finally, we explored the mangrove forest, getting up close with the local natural wonders. The day was packed with diverse and exciting experiences.

## Day 3 Itinerary (Ubud Day Trip):

**White Water Rafting Adventure**  
Our Ubud adventure began with thrilling white water rafting. Navigating through Bali's magnificent tropical rainforests and drifting along crystal-clear rivers, we felt the excitement of the rapids—a must-do for adventure enthusiasts. The entire experience was filled with surprises and challenges, making it unforgettable!

**Instagram-Worthy Cafe: Swing Photos**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57b0b41ac469b22436f739a53fe74f4f.jpg)

After rafting, we visited a famous Instagram-worthy cafe known for its giant swing. Here, we could swing while enjoying the breathtaking view of lush rice fields. The locals are truly skilled at photography, and the atmosphere made every photo look like a postcard. It's definitely a perfect spot for photos and social media check-ins.

![Tebasari Resto, Bar & Lounge](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_de5feafe85fbc690903411bfb7bf280f.jpg)

**Classic Bebek Bengil (Dirty Duck)**  
For lunch, we headed to Ubud's famous Bebek Bengil restaurant. This dish is a classic Ubud delicacy, featuring crispy duck skin paired with tender duck meat and a variety of local sauces. It's absolutely mouthwatering and a must-try when in Bali!

**Ubud Palace**  
After lunch, we visited the historic Ubud Palace. The architecture is distinctly Balinese, with intricate carvings and elaborate decorations that left us in awe. It was a wonderful way to immerse ourselves in the local culture and history.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2cb88da21144a9adcc0b17e6c870ab0a.jpg)

**Ubud Market**  
Next, we strolled through the bustling Ubud Market, filled with handmade crafts, clothing, and souvenirs. The lively atmosphere was infectious, and we picked up a few locally made keepsakes to remember our trip by.

### ❤️ Reflections:

1. **Prepare Your Entry Documents in Advance!**  
   During this trip, I noticed many travelers handling their documents on-site, leading to long queues and chaos. Make sure to prepare your health certificates, e-visas, and customs declarations in advance for a smoother entry process.
2. **Bali Has Many Attractions, but Traffic Can Be Heavy—Don’t Overpack Your Itinerary!**  
   Bali offers diverse attractions, but traffic can be intense, especially during peak hours. It's best not to overpack your schedule to avoid wasting time in traffic. The best way to experience Bali is to chill and embrace the local pace of life.
3. **Always Haggle at Markets!**  
   When shopping at markets, don’t forget to haggle! For example, I bought a coin purse at Love Anchor for USD$5, down from the original USD$10, and even got another for USD$3. There’s always room for negotiation, so don’t be shy—speak up! 😅
4. **Limited English + Three Months Pregnant, but Thankfully Met a Local Who Speaks Chinese**  
   During this trip, despite being three months pregnant and worried about my limited English, I was fortunate to meet Kang Ge (Wayan Suantara), a local driver who speaks Chinese. His thoughtful assistance made the journey stress-free, especially with route planning and attraction recommendations.
5. **Souvenir Recommendation: BALI BANANA Banana Cake**  
   Don’t forget to pick up a box of BALI BANANA banana cake before heading home—it’s a signature Balinese souvenir with a soft, sweet texture. You can find it at the airport. Try it upon arrival, and if you like it, stock up before leaving. They even offer free packing services, making shopping more convenient.
