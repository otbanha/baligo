## Essential Preparations Before Departure:

1. **An Excellent Chinese-Speaking Driver**
   Having a Chinese-speaking private driver on this trip meant I never had to worry about transportation. His Mandarin was very fluent, and he not only took us to all the popular local spots but also flexibly adjusted the itinerary based on time and traffic, making the journey much smoother.
2. **Confirm the Itinerary with the Driver**
   Before departure, make sure to communicate the daily schedule with your driver in advance. The driver will offer professional advice based on your needs, ensuring you get the best experience at each attraction.
3. **Exchange New Taiwan Dollars for US Dollars**
   Before leaving, remember to exchange some New Taiwan Dollars for US Dollars, then convert them to Indonesian Rupiah once you arrive in Bali. This is the most cost-effective way to handle currency exchange.
4. **Electronic Visa**
   Don't forget to apply for Indonesia's electronic visa in advance. This will save you time waiting in line at the airport upon arrival, making the start of your trip smoother.
5. **Tourist Tax**
   Bali charges a tourist tax for incoming visitors. It's recommended to pay it online in advance to avoid cumbersome procedures upon arrival.
6. **Customs Declaration Form**
   Three days before departure, remember to fill out the customs declaration form online. This will save you a lot of time when entering the country.
7. **Health Certificate and Flight Ticket**
   On the day of departure, make sure to get your flight ticket and fill out the health certificate. Ensure all documents are complete and correct to avoid last-minute panic at the airport.
8. **Prepare Basic Medications**
   Although this trip wasn't very long, it's still a good idea to pack some basic medications just in case, such as stomach medicine and cold medicine.

These preparations may seem tedious, but they all help make the trip smoother, allowing me to fully enjoy every moment in Bali!

## Day 1 Itinerary:

**Arrived in Bali at 3:15 PM**
The plane landed right on time at Bali International Airport, kicking off a journey full of anticipation!

**Present completed health certificate**
After getting off the plane, the first step was to show the pre-filled health certificate. This process was quick and smooth, giving us a sense that Bali's pandemic protocols are still well-organized.

**Present completed e-visa**
Next, we showed our e-visa. Since we had prepared it in advance, we didn't need to queue and used facial recognition technology to clear customs quickly. This step was super convenient and saved us a lot of hassle!

**Present customs declaration form**
The final step was to show the customs declaration form, which we had also completed online beforehand. As a result, the entire entry process went very smoothly.

**Wait for luggage**
The customs clearance was so fast that waiting for our luggage ended up taking the longest time 🤣! But once we got our bags, we felt much more relaxed.

**Meet the driver**
As soon as we exited the airport, our pre-booked driver was waiting outside. Seeing him made the whole trip feel more grounded! Ready to start our culinary adventure in Bali.

### ❤️ Reflections:

1. **Make sure to prepare all clearance documents in advance!**
   During this trip, I noticed many travelers handling their paperwork only upon arrival, leading to long queues and chaos. So be sure to prepare health certificates, e-visas, customs declaration forms, and other documents ahead of time to ensure a smoother and faster entry.
2. **Bali has many attractions, but traffic is heavy—don't overpack your itinerary!**
   Bali's attractions are incredibly diverse, but traffic is also intense, especially during peak hours. I recommend not cramming your schedule too tightly to avoid wasting too much time on the road. The best experience here is simply to chill and soak in the local pace of life.
3. **Remember to haggle at market stalls!**
   When shopping at markets, always bargain! For example, the coin purse I bought at Love Anchor went from the original price of 320 to 140, and another vendor even offered it for 100, so there's definitely room to negotiate. Don't be shy—speak up! 😅
4. **Poor English and three months pregnant, but lucky to have a local who speaks Chinese**
   During this trip, though I was three months pregnant and worried about my English, I was fortunate to meet a local driver named Kang Ge (Wayan Suantara) who speaks Chinese. His thoughtful help made the entire journey very reassuring, especially with route planning and attraction recommendations—he gave plenty of practical advice.
5. **Souvenir recommendation: BALI BANANA banana cake**
   Before heading home, don't forget to grab a box of BALI BANANA banana cake—a signature souvenir from Bali. It's soft, sweet, and delicious, and available at the airport. You can buy one to try upon arrival, and if you like it, buy more to take back. They also offer free packing service, making shopping even more convenient.
