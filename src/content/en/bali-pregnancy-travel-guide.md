> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Liu Huici](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/UxWzce2FRdK2gmsT/):

**Key Takeaways**: The key to traveling during pregnancy is **thorough preparation and a flexible itinerary**—book a Chinese-speaking private driver, handle e-visa, tourist tax, and customs declaration online in advance, and prepare health certificates and essential medications (stomach medicine, cold medicine) on departure day. This 5-day, 4-night route was **Sanur → Nusa Lembongan → Ubud → Canggu**, with a chill pace and no rigid schedule—pregnancy travel can still be thoroughly enjoyable.

This Bali trip was an exciting first-time experience for me! I'm very grateful to the friends who shared tips in the [Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide), which helped me find an amazing Chinese-speaking private driver—I'll introduce him in detail at the end of the article.

## Essential Preparations Before Departure:

1. **An Excellent Chinese-Speaking Driver**
   Having a Chinese-speaking private driver on this trip meant I never had to worry about transportation. His Chinese was very fluent, and he not only took us to all the popular local spots but also flexibly adjusted the itinerary based on time and traffic, making the journey smoother.
2. **Confirm the Itinerary with the Driver**
   Before departure, remember to communicate the daily schedule with the driver in advance. The driver will offer professional advice based on our needs, ensuring the best experience at each attraction.
3. **Exchange TWD for USD**
   Before leaving, exchange some New Taiwan Dollars for US Dollars, then convert to Indonesian Rupiah upon arrival in Bali. This is the most cost-effective way to exchange money.
4. **E-Visa**
   Don't forget to apply for Indonesia's e-visa in advance—this saves time queuing at airport immigration and makes the start of your trip smoother.
5. **Tourist Tax**
   Bali charges a tourist tax for incoming visitors. It's recommended to pay online in advance to avoid cumbersome procedures upon arrival.
6. **Customs Declaration Form**
   Fill out the customs declaration form online three days before departure—this saves a lot of time when entering the country.
7. **Health Certificate and Flight Ticket**
   On departure day, remember to get your flight ticket and fill out the health certificate to ensure all documents are complete and avoid last-minute airport chaos.
8. **Prepare Essential Medications**
   Although this trip wasn't long, it's still wise to pack basic medications just in case, such as stomach medicine and cold medicine.

These preparations may seem tedious, but they all contribute to a smoother journey, allowing me to fully enjoy every moment in Bali!

## Day 1 Itinerary:

**Arrival in Bali at 15:15**
The plane touched down right on schedule at Bali International Airport, kicking off a journey full of anticipation.

**Present Completed Health Certificate**
After disembarking, the first step was to show the health certificate we had filled out in advance. This process was quick and smooth, giving us a sense that Bali's pandemic protocols are still well-organized.

**Present Completed E-Visa**
Next up was presenting our e-visa. Since we had already taken care of it beforehand, we didn't have to wait in line and used facial recognition technology to breeze through customs. This step was incredibly convenient and saved us a lot of hassle!

**Present Customs Declaration Form**
The final step was showing the customs declaration form, which we had also completed online. As a result, the entire entry process went very smoothly.

**Wait for Luggage**
Customs clearance was so fast that waiting for our luggage ended up taking the longest time 🤣! But once we had our bags in hand, we felt much more relaxed.

**Meet Up with Driver**
As soon as we exited the airport, our pre-booked driver was already waiting outside. Seeing him made the whole trip feel much more grounded! We were ready to start our culinary adventure in Bali.

## Day 2 Itinerary (Nusa Lembongan Adventure):

**Hotel Pickup by Driver in the Morning**
Early in the morning, our private driver arrived at our hotel right on time to pick us up. Today's plan was to head to the stunning Nusa Lembongan—we were full of anticipation!

**Buying a Nusa Lembongan Tour at Sanur Pier**
Once we reached the pier in Sanur, we bought a day trip to Nusa Lembongan on the spot. There were plenty of options available, and we chose one that included snorkeling and sightseeing—great value for money.

**Thoughtful Arrangement of a Chinese-Speaking Guide**
The best part of the tour was that they arranged a Chinese-speaking guide for us, who accompanied and explained everything throughout the trip. This made our journey much easier and more enriching—no language barriers, and we got to learn more about the local natural scenery and culture.

**Highlights: Snorkeling, Devil's Tears, Dream Beach, Mangrove Ecosystem**
We enjoyed crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine life at the snorkeling spots on Nusa Lembongan, with colorful tropical fish swimming all around us—truly breathtaking! Then we visited the famous "Devil's Tears," where massive waves crash against the rocks, sending up sprays of water in a dramatic and awe-inspiring display. Next, we headed to Dream Beach, which lived up to its name with soft white sand and stunning blue water, perfect for total relaxation. Finally, we explored the mangrove ecosystem, getting up close to the area's natural wonders. The whole trip was rich and varied.

### ❤️ Reflections:

1. **Get your clearance documents ready in advance!**
   During this trip, I noticed many travelers handling paperwork on the spot, causing long lines and chaos. So, make sure to prepare health certificates, e-visas, customs declarations, and other documents ahead of time for a smoother entry.
2. **Bali has many attractions, but traffic is heavy—don’t overpack your itinerary!**
   Bali’s sights are incredibly diverse, but traffic can be intense, especially during peak hours. I recommend keeping your schedule light to avoid wasting time on the road. The best experience here is simply to chill and soak in the local pace of life.
3. **Haggle at market stalls!**
   When shopping at markets, always bargain! For instance, I bought a coin purse at Love Anchor, haggling from the original price of 320,000 IDR down to 140,000 IDR, and at another stall, even down to 100,000 IDR. So there’s always room to negotiate—don’t be shy, just go for it! 😅
4. **Poor English and three months pregnant—thankfully, a local who speaks Chinese helped out**
   During this trip, despite being three months pregnant and worried about my English skills, I was lucky to meet a local driver named Kang (Wayan Suantara) who speaks Chinese. His thoughtful assistance made the whole journey stress-free, especially with route planning and attraction choices, offering plenty of practical tips.
5. **Souvenir recommendation: BALI BANANA banana cake**
   Before heading home, don’t forget to grab a box of BALI BANANA banana cake—a signature Balinese souvenir with a soft, sweet, and fragrant texture. It’s available at the airport. You can buy a box to try upon arrival, and if you like it, stock up more for home. They even offer free packing service, making shopping a breeze.
