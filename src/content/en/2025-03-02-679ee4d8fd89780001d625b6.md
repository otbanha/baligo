## ****Visa Process****

I opted for the electronic visa application. Traveling with elders and kids often takes more time, so I prefer to prepare everything in advance. Not only did I handle the visa, but I also paid the tourism tax in Taiwan to minimize procedures upon arrival.

Bali’s customs process is now quite electronic, but I overlooked that the exit declaration had also moved online. As a result, I had to quickly fill it out on my phone at the airport, which took about ten minutes. Fortunately, my family had already learned to stay relaxed, and this little hiccup didn’t dampen the mood on our first day. Instead, it helped everyone embrace the flexibility and spontaneity of travel.

Although there were some minor bumps along the way, this trip taught me how important it is to stay flexible and patient when traveling with family. I’ll continue sharing more details and insights about this Bali trip.

## ****Flight Arrangements****

During the Lunar New Year period, regardless of the destination, based on my decade of experience, the best strategy is to start closely monitoring flight prices a year before your intended departure date. Once you spot a good deal, don't hesitate waiting for travel fairs or airline promotions, as ticket prices during this period tend to only increase.

Originally planning a trip to Australia, after discussing with my family in March, we decided to switch our destination to Bali. While searching for Lunar New Year flights, I unexpectedly found EVA Air's round-trip business class tickets to Bali priced at just over NT$35,000, while economy class tickets were already approaching NT$20,000. Comparatively, the business class price seemed particularly worthwhile, especially considering the nearly five-hour flight to Bali - offering increased comfort without being prohibitively expensive.

## Local Transportation

Public transportation in Bali isn't well-developed, so there are mainly three ways to get around: private car hire, taxis, or renting a car/motorcycle.

1. Private Car Hire: This is the most common and convenient option, especially suitable for families with elderly or children. Private hire allows flexible scheduling according to your itinerary, with drivers often doubling as guides who can recommend local spots and restaurants.
2. Taxis: For short distances, taxis are a good choice. It's recommended to use local ride-hailing apps like Grab or Gojek for transparent pricing and convenience.
3. Car/Motorcycle Rental: For independent travelers, renting a car or motorcycle can be a great option. However, be aware that Bali's traffic can be chaotic, and driving habits differ from Taiwan, so this is best suited for experienced drivers.

We opted for private car hire this time, making our itinerary more relaxed and allowing family members to rest well during drives, conserving energy for upcoming activities. While Bali's transportation isn't as convenient as big cities, with proper planning, your journey can still be smooth and enjoyable.

### Taxis and Traffic Conditions

We visited during the rainy season, which is similar across countries. When the weather is good, ordering food delivery or taxis is smooth, but once it rains, everything changes. Therefore, on days without a private car, avoid planning trips that are too far—30 minutes of travel time is already the limit. I suggest including popular attractions in your private car itinerary; otherwise, calling a taxi for the return trip can lead to overcharging, and you might not even get a ride in bad weather.

We experienced an exaggerated situation with food delivery during the rain: we ordered at 11 AM, but by 1 PM, no driver had accepted the order. Eventually, Grab gave up and asked me to cancel the order! Thankfully, we had bought enough instant noodles, vegetables, and eggs at the supermarket the day before, so we didn’t go hungry.

Bali’s traffic situation isn’t ideal, mainly because the road planning is quite haphazard. Often, places that look close on the map require five times the travel time due to winding routes (sometimes I really wanted to just climb over the walls!).

### Grab Ride Experience

Most Grab drivers we encountered were great, but we did have one driver who asked us to cancel the order upon pickup so he wouldn’t have to pay platform fees. When we arrived at the destination, he jokingly asked, ‘No tip?’ In such situations, it’s best to stay calm, as every place has its own culture and habits.

## Accommodation Arrangements

This trip was all about relaxation, so I focused on staying in villas. Browsing through Airbnb felt like taking a tour of luxury mansions, with so many extravagant villas to choose from. It took me about three days to snap out of the dreamy options and start considering our actual needs.

### Latest Update: December 2025

In Bali, the more luxurious villas tend to be located in more remote areas, which can be less convenient than staying in the city. Therefore, based on my family's preferences and needs, I tried to strike a balance between 'luxury' and 'convenience.' The villas I booked this time were all located in areas where Grab food delivery or taxis were easily accessible, ensuring we could conveniently handle meals and transportation when needed.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_16220458feb59e44fa83e1669a7981f0.jpg)

However, this accommodation experience also taught me some lessons. Many villas in Bali are designed with outdoor or semi-outdoor common spaces (like living rooms, dining areas, and kitchens) to bring guests closer to nature. While this design adds to the vacation vibe, it also comes with the downside of more mosquitoes, especially for someone like me who attracts them. Next time, I'll pay more attention and try to choose villas with air-conditioned and enclosed common spaces, so I can enjoy a comfortable environment without the mosquito troubles.

### ****Booking Process****

The booking process went very smoothly this time. Bali's villa booking system is now operated by many small and medium-sized teams, and the communication and service quality are quite good. If your English is proficient, you can directly communicate with hosts through platforms like Airbnb, and you'll usually receive prompt and detailed responses.

In terms of accommodation arrangements, this villa stay left our entire family very satisfied. Especially enjoying the leisurely vacation vibe in a private space, it's really suitable for family trips with elders and children.

## ****Dining Experience****

Bali's water is quite hard, which was somewhat painful for a tea lover like me. Fortunately, a friend living in Bali reminded me that even bottled water from supermarkets doesn't make tea as good as in Taiwan. She suggested I bring tea bags made from crushed leaves or stems, which would be more acceptable. Although I brought some nice red oolong tea leaves, after trying the tea bags I brought from Taiwan on the first day, I completely gave up and didn't even use the travel tea set I brought.

### Local Food

In terms of food, I tried local street vendors a couple of times, like crispy pork rice and satay, but soon gave up. The locals' standards for ingredient quality differ from our family's, so I mostly chose restaurants frequented by foreigners. These restaurants usually cater to foreign tastes, even modifying local dishes to be safer and more friendly, especially for those who don't like spicy food.

### Dairy Products and Desserts

A friend traveling with us is a big fan of dairy products and was eager to find fresh local milk. It’s important to note that Bali doesn’t have a dairy industry, so there are no locally produced dairy products. If you’re looking for milk that meets Taiwanese standards, you’ll only end up wasting money. Compared to twenty years ago when Bali only offered powdered and long-life milk, there are now some products labeled as 'Fresh milk,' but they’re far from ideal when consumed plain—they’re better suited for adding to coffee or tea.

Similarly, Bali doesn’t produce flour, and the locals’ taste for sweets isn’t particularly Westernized, nor is the quality prioritized. Therefore, I don’t recommend spending time or money searching for good Western-style desserts or bread here. This is Bali, not Korea or Japan, so expectations for such foods should be adjusted accordingly.

## Transportation and Itinerary Planning

As mentioned earlier, Bali's road design lacks comprehensive planning, resulting in traffic congestion for more than two-thirds of the day. Therefore, if you have plans for the day, especially for attractions more than an hour's drive from your accommodation, ****it is absolutely recommended**** to depart around 7 or 8 in the morning. Otherwise, just the traffic alone can drain half of your energy, making the entire day's itinerary exhausting.

When arranging your itinerary, try to group attractions in the same area together and avoid the whimsical idea of covering more than 40 kilometers in a single day! Because Bali's roads are narrow and it coincides with the Lunar New Year, which is Bali's [third busiest season of the year](/blog/2024-06-04-665ef28dfd89780001adfa98/), it's common for a journey of less than 40 kilometers to take two hours. Don't pack your schedule too tightly just because the distance looks short on the map, as this will only leave you stuck in traffic, completely losing the joy of traveling.

### Family Travel Recommendations

For family trips, especially when traveling with seniors and children, I highly recommend adopting the '****one day of private car hire, one day of rest****' model. This not only allows everyone enough time to rest and relax between activities but also avoids fatigue caused by long travel times.

For example, on the day of private car hire, you can arrange visits to farther attractions or popular activities, such as Ubud Market, rice terraces, or Tanah Lot Temple; on the rest day, you can choose to enjoy the private pool at your villa, indulge in a spa treatment, or leisurely dine at nearby restaurants. This pace not only ensures comfort for the seniors but also provides children with ample time to unwind and rest.

## ****Massage Experience****

Originally, my expectations for massages in Bali were not high. In my impression, whether it's a common or luxurious massage, the pressure tends to be too light for Taiwanese people, as if gently caressing the muscles. For Taiwanese who are accustomed to deep tissue massages, such pressure always feels insufficient. Even when selecting the strongest massage intensity and repeatedly urging the masseuse to increase the pressure, the effect remains limited.

During my time in Ubud, I decided to try the Deep Tissue Massage at Nata Spa. The pressure was indeed stronger than the typical Balinese massage, and overall it was quite comfortable. I even dozed off for most of the session. While it was a significant improvement from the usual 'light touch' massages, it still felt like it didn't quite hit the spot. However, my private tour guide had mentioned that massages in Kuta and Seminyak are more impressive, and Ubud's offerings are relatively average, so I wasn't disappointed.

### A Pleasant Discovery: Hot Bamboo SPA

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c72ed5775df8b39abb816fd5c7abd11d.jpg)

After arriving in Canggu, I started searching for nearby massage places and stumbled upon 'Hot Bamboo SPA.' Reading the reviews, I was immediately drawn to its unique offerings. One of their treatments involves using heated bamboo, hence the name 'Hot Bamboo.' The reviews were overwhelmingly positive, especially regarding the intensity, claiming it to be the strongest massage in Bali.

So, I decided to give it a try! However, this SPA turned out to be unexpectedly popular. It's one of the few places that requires a deposit upon booking, and payment can only be made in cash or via bank transfer to an Indonesian account. Credit cards aren't accepted, so cash is the only option. Fortunately, with the help of my private driver, I managed to transfer the deposit and secure my booking.

### Simple Yet Professional Choices

Compared to other massage places with overwhelming options, Hot Bamboo SPA's menu is refreshingly simple, offering only three types of massages:

1. ****Relaxing Bali Aga**** (Gentle and Relaxing)
2. ****Hot Bamboo**** (Heated Bamboo Massage)
3. ****Strong Barefoot**** (Intense Foot Massage)

The intensity ranges from light to strong, and you can choose between 60, 90, or 120-minute sessions. The decor is clean and minimalist, with bamboo and natural linen tones creating a soothing and natural ambiance.

### The Strong Barefoot Massage Experience

Without hesitation, we opted for the strongest option, the 'Strong Barefoot' massage, and booked a 2-hour session. The pressure was indeed spot-on—firm yet gentle. While I initially considered asking the masseuse to ease up during the back massage, within a minute, I felt completely relaxed and even started drifting off to sleep. I couldn't bear to ask them to lighten the pressure!

This massage experience completely changed my perspective on Balinese massages. The professional and unique techniques at Hot Bamboo SPA truly provided a holistic relaxation for mind, body, and soul. If you prefer a firmer touch, this SPA is definitely worth trying!

This family trip to Bali, though challenging with elders and children, was filled with unforgettable memories and valuable experiences. From flights and accommodations to transportation, dining, and massages, every aspect deepened my understanding that travel is not just about exploring the world, but also about creating cherished moments with family.

Bali's charm lies in its diversity and inclusivity. Whether it's luxurious villas, unique massage experiences, or natural landscapes, everyone can find their own way to relax. While the traffic and local cuisine require some adaptation, with proper planning and flexibility, these small challenges can make the journey even more enriching.

This trip taught me that traveling with family isn't about packing the itinerary, but ensuring everyone feels comfortable and happy. Bali's slow pace and warm hospitality provided the perfect space for relaxation. I hope this travelogue offers practical insights for future travelers to Bali and that our next journey will bring even more beautiful memories with family.
