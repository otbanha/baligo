Nusa Lembongan (also known as Nusa Lembongan) and its neighboring islands—Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida—draw a niche group of travelers with their pristine blue waters, laid-back coastal village vibe, and unique island adventures. However, these three islands near Bali have now become must-visit destinations. Whether you choose a day trip to Nusa Lembongan and Nusa Ceningan or a deeper exploration of Nusa Penida over two days, these islands will add endless color to your Bali vacation.

Nusa Lembongan is a small island located southeast of Bali, accessible by a 30-minute boat ride from Sanur Harbor. With an area of just 8 square kilometers, its main economic activities are tourism and seaweed farming.

When visiting Nusa Lembongan, you'll experience a relaxing atmosphere completely different from the hustle and bustle of Kuta, Seminyak, or Canggu beaches. Here, you can choose to actively explore the island (island hopping) and participate in activities, or simply unwind by the pool or at a restaurant at your accommodation. It's a great way to take a short break and recharge before returning to your daily routine.

## The Enchanting Attractions of Nusa Lembongan

Lembongan Island covers an area of approximately 6.15 square kilometers and can be reached by speedboat in just 30 minutes. For travelers with limited time, it's an ideal choice as it can be fully explored in a single day, unlike Nusa Penida which requires at least two days to visit the main attractions on its east and west coasts. Lembongan is also the most developed of the three islands, offering a variety of resorts, restaurants, and beach clubs where visitors can easily enjoy sunbathing on the beach.

Upon arriving at Lembongan Island, visitors are usually taken to the main areas of Jungut Batu Beach or Mushroom Bay, which are home to numerous accommodations and restaurants. If you plan to stay overnight, it's essential to book your accommodation in advance. This way, you can start exploring as soon as you drop off your luggage.

## Beach Exploration

Heading south by bike, you'll discover some interesting beaches like Sandy Bay, Devil's Tear, and Dream Beach. Sandy Bay is the best spot for sunset viewing, so it's recommended to save it for last.

![Sandy Bay有個beach club](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_60e81c23058acdc6be657d2a801fb230.jpg)

Sandy Bay has a beach club

Devil's Tear, known in Chinese as 'The Devil's Tears,' is a rocky cliff that meets the ocean. Standing here, you can enjoy a panoramic view of the sea from above. When the timing is right, large waves crash against the rocks, creating spectacular sprays, making it a perfect Instagram photo spot. However, be cautious not to stand too close, as sudden large waves can be very powerful. There have been unfortunate incidents where people were swept into the sea, so extreme care is advised!

Dream Beach is a popular beach outside the main area of the island and tends to be a bit crowded. A morning visit is more pleasant. Walking to the rocky areas, you can find interesting photo spots like rock formations or small caves. Additionally, there's a cliff diving spot here, but it's best to consult local guides to ensure safety.

## Mangrove Tour

Heading north, you'll arrive at the tranquil mangrove area of Lembongan Island. This area is typically less crowded than the southern regions, making it perfect for those seeking peace and quiet.

Upon arriving at Lembongan Island, you'll find various ways to explore these enchanting mangroves. You can rent a canoe and leisurely paddle through the serene waterways, immersing yourself in the soothing embrace of the mangroves while listening to the natural symphony of bird calls and sea breezes. For a deeper understanding of this ecosystem, consider joining a small-group guided boat tour.

Generally, when tourists arrive at Lembongan Island, locals enthusiastically recommend various mangrove boat tours. It's advisable to opt for a small-group private boat tour, typically accommodating 3-4 people with an experienced boatman. If you can negotiate the price to below IDR 100,000 per person, it's considered reasonable. During the half-hour journey, you can closely admire the stunning scenery of the pristine mangroves, observe various waterbirds, and even spot crabs scurrying among the roots.

Special tip: The **best time to visit** is during **low tide**, as the mangrove roots are fully exposed, presenting the most captivating view. The boatman can also navigate through lower water levels, allowing you to delve deeper into this unique ecosystem.

## A Relaxing Day

Spending a leisurely afternoon at beachfront restaurants in the Jungut Batu or Mushroom Bay area is incredibly comfortable. There are many reasonably priced seaside restaurants here. Since these are also the main points of arrival and departure, with the ports located here, you can observe the bustling activity during the day.

## Surfing

If you can't resist the allure of surfing, there are three popular surf spots near Jungut Batu Beach: Playgrounds, Lacerations, and Shipwrecks. You can find instructors and rental services at the surf center on Jungut Batu Beach.

## Crossing the Yellow Bridge to Nusa Ceningan

Nusa Ceningan is connected to Lembongan Island by the famous Yellow Bridge, and it boasts some stunning attractions. You can enjoy the bright turquoise waters at Blue Lagoon, Secret Beach, and Mahana Point. Some of these spots offer cliff jumping challenges. The jump at Mahana Cliff is about 15 meters high, and it's best to check the tides before jumping to avoid injury during low tide. ## Best Spots for Diving and Snorkeling

For adventurers who love the underwater world, Lembongan Island and its neighboring islands, Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, offer a wealth of diving and snorkeling spots. Mangrove Point is the closest snorkeling spot to Lembongan Island's shore, perfect for those who want a quick glimpse of the underwater beauty. There are also multiple snorkeling and diving locations around Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, such as the famous Manta Point, where you can swim alongside giant and graceful manta rays—an unforgettable experience.

A half-day snorkeling trip around Lembongan Island, including hotel pickup, snorkeling gear, boat fees, and three dive spots, costs approximately 150,000 IDR (USD$10) per person.

## Water Activities and Adventures

In addition to diving and snorkeling, Nusa Lembongan offers a variety of water activities to cater to different interests. You can try water skiing, stand-up paddleboarding (SUP), banana boat rides, or even experience the unique sensation of underwater walking with Seawalker. Additionally, you can join an island-hopping boat tour to admire the island's stunning beauty from different perspectives, enjoy close encounters with the water, and feel the charm of the ocean.

## Transportation

The best way to explore Nusa Lembongan and Nusa Ceningan is by renting a bicycle or scooter. Like Nusa Penida, these islands do not have access to ride-hailing platforms like Gojek or Grab. If you prefer not to ride, another unique transportation option on the island is the local "modified pickup truck shuttle service."

![當地的 pick-up truck](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_38697b346c428fcbc1d2bff17b0e777d.jpg)

Local pickup trucks

Transportation Costs on Nusa Lembongan:

- Motorcycle rental: 75,000～100,000 IDR per day (150～200 TWD/$4.6~6.6 USD)
- Pick-up truck: 50,000 IDR one way (100 TWD/$3 USD)
- Private car: Limited options on Nusa Lembongan, more expensive, 800,000 IDR for 8 hours (1,600 TWD/$51 USD)

Renting a motorcycle is not only economical but also allows you to freely explore every corner of the island at your own pace. The pick-up truck shuttle service offers a unique local experience, especially suitable for travelers who are not accustomed to riding or carrying more luggage.

****Friendly Reminder****: When renting a motorcycle, please ensure the vehicle is in good condition and remember to get a receipt. If opting for the pick-up truck shuttle, it's advisable to inquire about the price in advance and confirm the destination with the driver to avoid miscommunication.

## Future Development of Nusa Lembongan

Nusa Lembongan will become Bali's most digitally connected tourist destination. Through the 'Digital Island Nusa Lembongan' initiative, the local government and businesses aim to enhance the tourism experience and boost the local economy by implementing a digital payment system. This project includes transportation, dining, accommodation, and shops, fully supporting cashless payments. Additionally, the Central Bank of Indonesia will provide relevant financial education to integrate digital technology into daily life. This initiative is expected to improve the quality of tourism on Nusa Lembongan, increase visitor stay duration, and promote the growth of local income (PAD) in the Klungkung Regency.

In the future, Nusa Lembongan will become Bali's most digitally connected tourist destination. Through the 'Digital Island Nusa Lembongan' initiative, the island will gradually implement a fully digital payment system for ferry tickets, local transportation, attraction tickets, hotels, restaurants, and shops, making it easier for travelers to plan and arrange their trips. Additionally, the enhancement of digital infrastructure will address current signal blind spots, ensuring stable network connectivity across all areas. By driving these changes, Nusa Lembongan will become a world-class tourist destination, extending visitor stay durations and integrating the local community into the digital economy, promoting regional economic growth and improving quality of life.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) about Nusa Lembongan

### 1. Is Nusa Lembongan worth visiting?

Absolutely! Nusa Lembongan is beloved by visitors for its tranquil atmosphere. Compared to Bali, it's more serene and offers plenty of attractions to explore. If you include nearby Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, you can easily plan a week-long itinerary. Nusa Lembongan boasts spectacular marine activities like swimming with manta rays and cliff jumping. The island also has many beautiful beaches to enjoy.

### 2. What is Nusa Lembongan famous for?

Nusa Lembongan is known for its Yellow Bridge, seaweed farms, white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, stunning sunsets, captivating scenery, quality cafes, and nearby Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida. These are the main highlights that attract visitors.

### 3. Is it better to visit Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan?

Both islands are worth visiting! But if you can only choose one, Nusa Lembongan is more developed with better facilities and more activities. If time permits, we recommend visiting both islands to experience their unique charms.

### 4. How long does the ferry from Bali to Nusa Lembongan take?

The ferry ride is short, taking only about 30-45 minutes from Bali to Nusa Lembongan.

### 5. ****How many days should you spend on Nusa Lembongan?****

You can explore the main attractions of Nusa Lembongan in a single day, but if you want to leisurely enjoy the island's beauty, we recommend staying for 3 days. If you also wish to explore Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, you can plan for around 4 days. Staying at least one night on the island allows you to truly experience the stunning sunset and relaxed island atmosphere.

### 6. ****Is Nusa Lembongan suitable for family trips?****

Nusa Lembongan offers many family-friendly activities, such as snorkeling, beach play, and sunset watching, making it ideal for traveling with children. The island's facilities are relatively well-developed, catering well to family vacations.

### 7. ****Is Nusa Lembongan suitable for digital nomads?****

Although Nusa Lembongan's internet connectivity is weaker compared to Bali, digital infrastructure has been improving in recent years. Some areas already have stable internet, making it suitable for short-term work or digital nomadism.

### 8. ****What are the must-see attractions on Nusa Lembongan?****

Besides the Yellow Bridge and beautiful beaches, 'Devil's Tears' on Nusa Lembongan is a famous natural attraction. The waves crashing against the cliffs create spectacular water sprays, making it a must-see.

### 9. ****How to get to Nusa Lembongan from Bali?****

You can reach Nusa Lembongan by taking a ferry from Sanur Harbor in Bali. There are multiple ferries daily, making transportation very convenient.

### 10. ****What is the best time to visit Nusa Lembongan?****

The dry season (April to October) is the best time to visit Nusa Lembongan. During this period, the weather is sunny, and the sea is clear, making it ideal for outdoor activities like snorkeling and island hopping.

## Tips

1. ****Book transportation and accommodation in advance****: As Nusa Lembongan has seen increasing numbers of tourists, it's advisable to book your transportation and accommodation early to avoid last-minute hassles.
2. ****Carry cash****: While there are a few ATMs on the island, they sometimes run out of cash. Carrying enough Indonesian Rupiah ensures a smooth trip.
3. ****Use sunscreen****: The island's sun is intense, so make sure to apply sunscreen regularly to avoid sunburn.
4. ****Follow eco-friendly practices****: Please take care of the marine environment by not littering and adhering to local eco-friendly rules to protect this beautiful tropical paradise.
