Suluban Beach, also known as 'Blue Point Beach,' is a uniquely charming hidden beach tucked away in Bali's Bukit Peninsula. Featuring stunning sea caves and limestone cliffs, it's a paradise for surfers. If you're looking to escape the crowds, Suluban Beach is undoubtedly one of Bali's most distinctive and mysterious destinations.

### 1. How to Get to Suluban Beach

Suluban Beach is located in the southern part of Bukit Peninsula, about an hour's drive from Seminyak and Kuta. It's recommended to hire a private car, as there is no public transportation available.

- **Location:** Uluwatu
- **Navigation:** Search for "Suluban Beach" or "Blue Point Beach"

Upon arrival, follow the stone steps downhill. It takes about a 10-minute walk to reach the beach, passing by shops, stalls, and tunnel-like rock crevices. This journey feels like an adventure, so wear comfortable shoes.

### 2. Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Suluban Beach is during the dry season (May to October), when the sun is plentiful and the waves are stable, making it perfect for enjoying the scenery and surfing. It's also recommended to visit in the morning to avoid the crowds that gather on the beach in the afternoon.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c69315ce69c0a6c8aeb911ea549cdd0a.jpg)

### Main Activities

1. **Sea Cave Exploration:** The limestone terrain of Suluban Beach has created numerous natural caves, making it an excellent backdrop for photography and adventure. During low tide, you can walk through the caves and capture mysterious photos.
2. **Surfing Paradise:** The waves here are perfect for surfing, especially for experienced surfers. Around Blue Point, there are many surf rental shops where you can easily find equipment or hire a local instructor. Note that the wave height is suitable for intermediate to advanced surfers, and beginners might find it challenging.
3. **Sunset Viewing:** The sunset views at Suluban Beach are spectacular. You can enjoy the sunset while sipping on a drink or having a meal at one of the nearby cliffside bars.
4. **Cliffside Bars:** There are several cliffside bars and cafes near Suluban Beach, including the famous Single Fin. These bars offer stunning ocean views and are perfect for enjoying lunch or dinner. Visitors can watch the waves crashing against the cliffs while relaxing here.

### Travel Tips

- **Tide Times:** It’s recommended to check the tide times. During low tide, the beach area expands, allowing for more exploration on foot. During high tide, water levels rise into the caves, so it’s best to avoid them. Check tide times [here](https://www.tideschart.com/Indonesia/Bali/Kota-Denpasar/Uluwatu/).
- **Sun Protection:** Although Suluban Beach has some shaded areas, prolonged exposure to the sun is common. It’s advisable to bring sunscreen, a hat, and water.
- **Travel Light:** The paths here are narrow, so carrying a small bag and traveling light is more convenient. The stone steps leading down to the beach are steep, so wearing sneakers or sandals is recommended—avoid flip-flops.

### Conclusion

What makes Suluban Beach special is its unique terrain and sense of seclusion. As you descend the winding, narrow stone steps and gradually enter a path between rock crevices, it feels like stepping into a hidden paradise. Unlike typical flat, open beaches, Suluban Beach is surrounded by dramatic limestone cliffs, forming natural tunnels and caves. The waves crashing against the rocks create a spectacular and mysterious atmosphere.

Suluban Beach is perfect for travelers seeking raw, natural beauty. At low tide, a soft, fine sandy beach emerges beneath the caves, offering visitors a serene stroll amidst the majestic rock formations. These caves are not only a paradise for photographers but also add layers of charm to the beach, making it feel like a hidden utopia.

Additionally, Suluban Beach is known as 'Blue Point Beach' due to its deep, crystal-clear waters that shimmer like sapphires, especially under the sunlight. The powerful waves here attract surfers from around the globe, with many seasoned surfers coming to challenge themselves. This unique combination makes Suluban Beach a mysterious and enchanting hidden gem in Bali.
