Suluban Beach, also known as 'Blue Point Beach,' is tucked away in Bali's Bukit Peninsula, a uniquely charming secret beach. Here, you’ll find stunning sea caves, limestone cliffs, and a paradise for surfers. If you’re looking to escape the crowds, Suluban Beach is undoubtedly one of Bali’s most distinctive and mysterious destinations.

### 1. How to Get to Suluban Beach

Suluban Beach is located in the southern part of Bukit Peninsula, about an hour’s drive from Seminyak and Kuta. It’s recommended to hire a private car, as there is no public transportation available.

- **Location:** Uluwatu
- **Navigation:** Search for 'Suluban Beach' or 'Blue Point Beach'

Once you arrive, follow the stone steps down for about a 10-minute walk to reach the beach. You’ll pass by some shops and stalls, as well as tunnel-like rock crevices. This journey feels like an adventure, so wear comfortable shoes.

### 2. Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Suluban Beach is during the dry season (May to October), when the sun is abundant and the waves are stable, making it ideal for enjoying the scenery and surfing. Additionally, it's recommended to visit in the morning to avoid the crowded beach in the afternoon.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c69315ce69c0a6c8aeb911ea549cdd0a.jpg)

### Main Activities

1. **Sea Cave Exploration**: The limestone terrain of Suluban Beach has created numerous natural caves, providing a perfect backdrop for photography and adventure. During low tide, you can walk through the caves and capture mysterious photos.
2. **Surfing Haven**: The wave conditions here are excellent for surfing, especially for experienced surfers. There are many surf rental shops around Blue Point where you can easily find equipment or hire a local instructor. Note that the wave height is suitable for intermediate to advanced surfers, and beginners might find it challenging.
3. **Sunset Viewing**: The sunset at Suluban Beach is spectacular, and you can enjoy it while sipping drinks and dining at nearby cliffside bars.
4. **Cliffside Bars**: There are several cliffside bars and cafes near Suluban Beach, including the famous Single Fin, offering stunning ocean views perfect for lunch or dinner. Visitors can enjoy the dramatic scene of waves crashing against the cliffs.

### Travel Tips

- **Tide Times**: It's advisable to check the tide times. During low tide, the beach area expands, allowing for more exploration on foot. During high tide, water levels rise into the caves, so it's best to avoid them. Check tide times [here](https://www.tideschart.com/Indonesia/Bali/Kota-Denpasar/Uluwatu/).
- **Sun Protection**: Although Suluban Beach has some shaded areas, prolonged exposure to the sun calls for sunscreen, hats, and water.
- **Travel Light**: The paths here are narrow, so carrying a small bag and traveling light is more convenient. The stone steps down to the beach are steep, so wearing sports shoes or sandals is recommended over flip-flops.

### Conclusion

What makes Suluban Beach special is its unique terrain and sense of seclusion. As you descend the winding, narrow stone steps and enter a path between rock crevices, it feels like stepping into a hidden realm. Unlike typical flat, open beaches, Suluban Beach is surrounded by dramatic limestone cliffs, forming natural tunnels and caves. The waves crashing against the rocks create white sprays, adding a majestic yet mysterious atmosphere.

Suluban Beach is particularly suited for travelers seeking raw and natural beauty. During low tide, a soft and fine sandy beach emerges beneath the caves, offering visitors a chance to stroll and experience the tranquility and grandeur of these natural rock formations. These caves are not only a paradise for photography enthusiasts but also add layers of depth to the beach, making it feel like a hidden utopia.

Moreover, Suluban Beach is also known as 'Blue Point Beach' due to its deep, crystal-clear waters that shimmer like sapphires, especially breathtaking under the sunlight. The powerful waves here attract surfers from around the world, with many experienced surfers challenging themselves in these waters. This unique combination makes Suluban Beach a mysterious and surprising beach gem in Bali.
