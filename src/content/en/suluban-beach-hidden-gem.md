Suluban Beach, also known as 'Blue Point Beach,' is tucked away in Bali's Bukit Peninsula, a distinctive and secretive beach destination. Not only does it boast stunning sea caves and limestone cliffs, but it's also a paradise for surfers. If you're looking to escape the hustle and bustle, Suluban Beach is undoubtedly one of Bali's most unique and mysterious spots.

### 1. How to Get to Suluban Beach

Suluban Beach is located in the southern part of Bukit Peninsula, about an hour's drive from Seminyak and Kuta. It's recommended to hire a private car as there is no public transportation available.

- **Location:** Uluwatu
- **Navigation:** Search for 'Suluban Beach' or 'Blue Point Beach'

Upon arrival, descend the stone steps and walk for about 10 minutes to reach the beach, passing by shops and stalls, as well as tunnel-like rock crevices. This journey is full of adventure, so wear light and comfortable shoes.

### 2. Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Suluban Beach is during the dry season (May to October), when the sun is plentiful and the waves are stable, making it ideal for enjoying the scenery and surfing. It's also recommended to visit in the morning to avoid the crowded beach in the afternoon.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c69315ce69c0a6c8aeb911ea549cdd0a.jpg)

### Main Activities

1. **Sea Cave Exploration**: The limestone terrain of Suluban Beach has created many natural caves, providing a perfect backdrop for photography and adventure. During low tide, you can walk through the caves and capture mysterious photos.
2. **Surfing Paradise**: The wave conditions here are excellent for surfing, especially for experienced surfers. There are many surf rental shops around Blue Point, where you can easily find equipment or hire a local instructor. Note that the wave height is suitable for intermediate to advanced surfers, and beginners might find it challenging.
3. **Sunset Watching**: The sunset views at Suluban Beach are spectacular. You can enjoy a drink and meal at a nearby cliffside bar while watching the sunset.
4. **Cliffside Bars**: There are several cliffside bars and cafes near Suluban Beach, including the famous Single Fin. These bars offer stunning ocean views, perfect for enjoying lunch or dinner. Visitors can watch the waves crash against the cliffs in a dramatic scene.

### Travel Tips

- **Tide Times**: It's recommended to check the tide times. During low tide, the beach area is larger, allowing for more exploration; during high tide, water levels rise into the caves, so it's best to avoid them. Check tide times [here](https://www.tideschart.com/Indonesia/Bali/Kota-Denpasar/Uluwatu/).
- **Sun Protection**: Although Suluban Beach has some shaded areas, prolonged exposure to the sun means you should bring sunscreen, a hat, and water.
- **Travel Light**: The paths here are narrow, so carrying a small bag and traveling light will be more convenient. The stone steps down to the beach are steep, so it's advisable to wear sneakers or sandals rather than flip-flops.

### Conclusion

What makes Suluban Beach special is its unique terrain and sense of seclusion. As you descend the winding, narrow stone steps and gradually enter a path between rock crevices, it feels like stepping into a hidden paradise. Unlike typical flat, open beaches, Suluban Beach is surrounded by dramatic limestone cliffs, forming natural tunnels and caves. The waves crashing against the rocks create a spectacular and mysterious atmosphere.

Suluban Beach is particularly suited for travelers seeking raw and natural beauty. During low tide, a soft, fine sandy beach emerges beneath the caves, where visitors can stroll and experience the tranquility and grandeur of these natural caverns. These caves are not only a paradise for photography enthusiasts but also add layers of texture to the beach, making it feel like a hidden utopia.

Moreover, Suluban Beach is also known as 'Blue Point Beach' due to its deep, crystal-clear waters that shimmer like sapphires, especially breathtaking under the sunlight. The powerful waves here attract surfers from around the world, with many experienced surfers challenging themselves in these waters. This unique combination makes Suluban Beach a mysterious and surprising beach gem in Bali.
