## **Pre-Trip Preparation**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_77d2a3cab5d6865647f5e4179987a87c.jpg)

### Visa/Electronic Customs Declaration/Tourism Tax:

Currently, a visa is required to enter Indonesia! Taiwanese travelers can apply for an **eVisa** or a **Visa on Arrival**, with a maximum stay of 30 days. The fee is IDR 500,000, approximately USD$31.

Additionally, everyone must complete/pay the electronic customs declaration/tourism tax. Please refer to the link below:

> Further Reading: [Indonesia/Bali Entry Process - From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2023-12-07-65717449fd89780001fda950/)

The advantage of applying for an eVisa in advance is that you can skip the queue entirely upon arrival and use the automated immigration gates, bypassing passport checks. However, children under 6 cannot use the automated gates but have dedicated counters for assistance (see: [Quick & Easy! 2024 Bali Automated Immigration Process & Guide](/blog/2024-02-06-65c1defcfd89780001f79bfd/))

### Mobile Phones and Internet:

When traveling in Bali, having access to your mobile phone and the internet is essential. There are several ways to ensure connectivity: First, you can purchase a local SIM card, which is an affordable and convenient way to get internet access, allowing you to use the internet anytime, anywhere. You can buy these SIM cards at local telecom operator stores or stalls. Second, you can also purchase SIM cards or mobile data plans online. Websites like Klook or auction sites offer a variety of SIM card options, allowing you to choose a package that fits your needs and budget.

In Bali, ensuring your phone can connect to the internet is crucial. It helps you easily find information, stay in touch with family and friends, use navigation apps, and more, making your trip more convenient and enjoyable.

### Voltage and Plugs:

The standard voltage in Indonesia and Bali is **220 volts (220V)**, which is important information for travelers planning to bring electrical appliances. Before deciding which appliances to bring, make sure your devices support the international standard of 220 volts and have the appropriate plug type to ensure they can be used normally in Indonesia and Bali!

### Airport Transfers:

When traveling to Bali for the first time, you have several options for arranging airport transfers to ensure a smooth journey to your destination. Here are some common transfer options:

1. **Hotel Transfer Service**:

2. - Some hotels in Bali offer transfer services. You can inquire with your hotel when booking accommodation to see if they provide this service. This is a convenient and reliable option, as the hotel will send a dedicated vehicle to pick you up, eliminating the need to worry about transportation arrangements upon arrival.

3. **Airport Taxi/Ride-Hailing Services**:

4. - At Bali Airport, you can easily find taxi stands, including official taxi services from taxi companies. After exiting the airport, you can locate the pickup lounges for Gojek or Grab, where you can use ride-hailing services. These taxis usually have fixed rates, making them a convenient and quick option.

5. **Pre-Booked Private Transfer Service**:

6. - Before arriving in Bali, you can book a private transfer service online. These services are typically provided by local drivers or online platforms, allowing you to choose the vehicle type and pickup time in advance to ensure a personalized welcome upon arrival. This is often the most cost-effective option.

### Beware of Vendors/Brokers:

Bali has many vendors/brokers who target tourists, so be cautious.

### Pay Attention to Safety:

Bali is generally safe, but it's still important to keep an eye on your belongings. Avoid walking into deserted alleys, don't constantly use your phone on the street, and don't engage in conversations with Middle Eastern individuals.

### Respect Local Culture:

Bali is a Hindu region, so be mindful of etiquette when visiting temples and avoid wearing overly revealing clothing.

## **Additional Tips for Children of All Ages**

- Bring your child's favorite toys and activities on the plane to keep them entertained.
- In hot weather, ensure your child drinks plenty of water and stays protected from the sun.
- Bali's climate is hot during the day and cooler in the evening, so consider packing a light jacket for your child if you're concerned.
- Before visiting temples or other religious sites, teach your child to respect local culture.
- Learn some basic Indonesian phrases, such as 'thank you.' Check out Jay's [video on how to pronounce it](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VuG8s-u_I0Q&t=106s)
