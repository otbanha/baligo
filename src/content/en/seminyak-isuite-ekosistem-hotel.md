If you've searched for hotels in Petitenget, you know there's no shortage of options—but few combine strong design, great location, and a satisfying stay like iSuite by Ekosistem. With only 21 rooms, this boutique design hotel opened in April 2025, backed by Ekosistem, known for eco-retreats and villas in Bali. This is their first boutique hotel, and its thoughtful design reflects their expertise.

***

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

Located at Jl. Petitenget No. 19X, in the heart of Seminyak, this area is familiar to Bali regulars: Petitenget Street is right outside, Potato Head Beach Club is a short walk away, and nearby spots include Sarong Asian Fusion Restaurant, Shiro Sushi (right next door), and Amici Club across the street. Batu Belig Beach is an 8-minute walk, and Petitenget Temple is just 7 minutes away.

Petitenget’s vibe strikes a balance between Seminyak’s refined nightlife and Canggu’s laid-back charm—not overly touristy or noisy, yet convenient for dining, cafes, spas, and convenience stores. Perfect for travelers who want accessibility without the hustle and bustle.

## 🛏️ Room Types

All 21 rooms are suite-style. The rooms are quite spacious, with the most eye-catching feature being the freestanding egg-shaped bathtub placed right in the center of the room. The tub is large enough for two people, and the hot water supply is stable. The bathroom is separated from the bedroom by louvered sliding doors, striking the perfect balance between privacy and openness, making it comfortable even when staying with friends.

The room amenities are more extensive than expected: complimentary minibar (though not restocked), unlimited glass-bottled mineral water, snacks and fruits, dual sinks, bidet toilet, rain shower plus handheld showerhead, smart TV, JBL Bluetooth speaker (with built-in alarm clock, a practical design), universal power outlets, and even a bottle of mineral water specifically for brushing teeth. The room's lighting ambiance has been particularly praised by guests, with overall quality and attention to detail far exceeding other options in the same price range.

The only minor drawback: the rooms don’t have physical "Please Clean" or "Do Not Disturb" signs, but the front desk will verbally ask if you need housekeeping when you head out each day. It’s manageable once you get used to it, though first-time guests might need some adjustment. Some guests also mentioned that figuring out the blackout curtains takes a bit of trial and error, so if you’re sensitive to light, it’s worth confirming the operation with the front desk upon check-in.

## 👥 Ideal Guests

This place is perfect for travelers who value design and prefer understated elegance over sprawling villas. It’s especially suitable for couples or honeymooners—the combination of the egg-shaped bathtub and spa discounts feels like a package deal. It’s also great for friends traveling together, as the rooms are spacious enough to avoid interfering with each other. It’s not particularly recommended for those with infants or toddlers, as the pool requires children under 12 to be accompanied by an adult, and the overall atmosphere leans toward tranquility.

***

## 💬 Guest Reviews

A guest from Singapore mentioned that every aspect of this hotel—from the lobby ambiance to the room design and breakfast quality—left her impressed. She particularly loved the afternoon tea service delivered to her room, feeling it offered great value for money. She’s already marked it as her top choice for her next trip to Bali. (Source: Agoda ⭐ 10/10)

Another guest described it as the most design-forward hotel they’ve stayed at in Bali. They were pleasantly surprised when the hotel’s backup power system kept everything running smoothly during a Bali-wide blackout, ensuring their experience remained uninterrupted. They praised the hotel for its thorough preparation. (Source: Trip.com 9.8/10)
