If you've searched for hotels in Petitenget, you know the area isn't short on options—but few manage to combine strong design, a great location, and a stay you won't regret. iSuite by Ekosistem is one of those rare exceptions. With only 21 rooms, it opened in April 2025, following a boutique design ethos. The Ekosistem group, known for eco-retreats and villas in Bali, has launched their first boutique hotel here. Just hearing this background tells you they didn't open this hotel casually.

***

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

Located at Jl. Petitenget No. 19X, in the heart of Seminyak, this area is familiar to Bali regulars: Petitenget Street is right outside, Potato Head Beach Club is just a few minutes' walk away, and nearby are Sarong Asian Fusion Restaurant, Shiro Sushi (right next door, super convenient), and Amici Club across the street. A short 8-minute walk takes you to Batu Belig Beach, and Petitenget Temple is just 7 minutes away.

The vibe of Petitenget lies between Seminyak's refined nightlife and Canggu's laid-back atmosphere—it's not a noisy tourist zone that keeps you awake, but restaurants, cafes, spas, and convenience stores are all within walking distance. For travelers who want "convenience without too much hustle," the location is a definite plus.

## 🛏️ Room Types

All 21 rooms are suite-style. The rooms are quite spacious, with the most eye-catching feature being the freestanding egg-shaped bathtub - placed right in the center of the room. The tub is large enough for two people, with stable hot water. The bathroom is separated from the bedroom by louvered sliding doors, striking the perfect balance between privacy and openness, making it comfortable even when staying with friends.

The amenities are more abundant than expected: complimentary mini-bar (though not refilled), unlimited glass-bottled mineral water, snacks and fruits, double sinks, bidet toilet, rain shower plus handheld showerhead, smart TV, JBL Bluetooth speaker (with built-in alarm clock - a very practical design), universal power outlets, and even a bottle of mineral water specifically for brushing teeth. The room's lighting ambiance has been particularly praised by guests, with overall quality and details far exceeding options at this price point.

The only minor drawback: rooms don't have 'Please Clean' or 'Do Not Disturb' signs, but the front desk will verbally ask if you want your room cleaned when you leave each day. It's fine once you get used to it, but first-time guests might need some adjustment. Some guests have mentioned needing to figure out the blackout curtains, so if you're light-sensitive, it's recommended to confirm the operation method with the front desk upon check-in.

## 👥 Ideal Guests

Most suitable for travelers who value design and prefer subtle sophistication over large villas. Perfect for couples or honeymooners - the egg-shaped bathtub combined with spa discounts is practically a package deal. Also suitable for friends traveling together, with ample space and no interference. Not particularly recommended for those with infants or toddlers, as the pool requires adult supervision for children under 12, and the overall atmosphere leans towards tranquility.

***

## 💬 Guest Reviews

A guest from Singapore mentioned that every aspect of this hotel, from the lobby ambiance to room design and breakfast quality, amazed her. She particularly found the afternoon tea served in the room to be excellent value for money, and has already listed it as her top choice for her next Bali trip. (Source: Agoda ⭐ 10/10)

Another guest said this was the most design-forward hotel they've stayed at in Bali. When Bali experienced a power outage, the hotel's self-contained power system ensured uninterrupted service, earning praise for the hotel's thorough preparation. (Source: Trip.com 9.8/10)
