On this magical island of Bali, with the increasing trend of international tourists, the acceptance of credit cards has become more widespread. As a traveler, you might consider using credit cards to cover your expenses. However, using credit cards while traveling in Bali has its pros and cons, let's explore them together.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_aa29ee99c92f61f5ed2131d9230f0941.jpg)

## **Advantages of Using Credit Cards:**

1. **Convenience and Speed:** Whether you're at a five-star hotel or most tourist restaurants, using a credit card allows for quick payments, saving you the hassle of carrying large amounts of Indonesian Rupiah and dealing with change.
2. **Security:** Compared to carrying large amounts of cash, credit cards are more secure and reliable. Even if your credit card is lost or stolen, you can report it immediately and block the card to prevent misuse by criminals.
3. **Emergency Funds:** During your travels, you might encounter situations where you run out of funds. In such cases, a credit card can serve as a temporary emergency fund, helping you out of a tight spot.
4. **Rewards:** Some credit cards offer travel rewards or foreign transaction rebates, meaning you can enjoy additional benefits or save on expenses during your trip, making your travel experience more enjoyable.

## **Disadvantages of Using Credit Cards:**

1. **Currency Conversion Fees:** When you use a credit card in Bali, if your card is settled in a foreign currency, you might incur currency conversion fees. These fees are calculated based on the local and issuing bank's exchange rates, usually converting the foreign currency to your card's settlement currency, and may include additional handling fees.
2. **Foreign Transaction Fees:** Some credit card issuers charge additional fees for foreign transactions. These fees are usually a fixed percentage of your payment when using a credit card in Bali and may vary based on your transaction amount. Although these fees might be relatively small, they can add up if you use your credit card frequently during your trip.
3. **Other Fees:** In some cases, there might be other additional fees, such as early repayment fees or penalties for exceeding your credit limit. These fees are usually based on your credit card's terms and conditions, so make sure to understand your card's fees and policies before traveling.
4. **Limited Acceptance:** While most large hotels, shops, and restaurants accept credit cards, you might encounter situations where smaller businesses, local eateries, street vendors, or remote areas do not accept credit cards. In such cases, you might need to carry cash.
5. **Security Risks:** Although credit cards are relatively secure, there is still a risk of fraud or data theft. To minimize risks, ensure you use your credit card in secure places and monitor your transaction records closely to detect any anomalies promptly.
6. **Overspending:** Using a credit card might lead to a loss of control over your spending, resulting in overspending. Use your credit card cautiously to avoid accumulating excessive debt during your trip, which could affect your travel experience.
7. **Unpredictable Situations:** Even credit cards that usually work fine might encounter unexpected issues in Bali. Our Facebook group once shared a member's experience: a credit card that worked fine for several days suddenly failed at a high-end restaurant when it was time to pay. Understand the associated currency conversion fees and foreign transaction charges, and choose the most suitable payment method based on your needs. Sometimes, carrying some Indonesian Rupiah cash can be more convenient and cost-effective. Therefore, diversifying your payment methods during your trip might be a wise choice.
