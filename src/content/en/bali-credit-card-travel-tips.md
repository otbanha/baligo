**Bottom line**: Credit cards are widely accepted at **large hotels, shops, and restaurants in Bali**, offering convenience, security, and the chance to earn rewards. However, be mindful of **overseas transaction fees and currency conversion charges**, which can add up. **Street vendors, local eateries, and remote areas mostly only accept cash**. It's best to use a mix of credit cards and cash, and make sure to have enough cash on hand—some travelers have found themselves in a tight spot when their card suddenly stopped working and they had no cash.

In the magical land of Bali, as international tourism continues to grow, credit card acceptance has become increasingly widespread. As a traveler, you might consider using credit cards to cover your expenses. However, using credit cards in Bali has its pros and cons—let's dive in.

## **Benefits of Using Credit Cards:**

1. **Convenience and Speed:** Whether you're at a five-star hotel or a popular tourist restaurant, credit cards allow for quick payments, saving you the hassle of carrying large amounts of Indonesian rupiah and dealing with loose change.
2. **Security:** Compared to carrying a lot of cash, credit cards are safer and more reliable. Even if your card is lost or stolen, you can report it immediately and block it to prevent unauthorized use.
3. **Emergency Funds:** During your trip, you might run into a cash shortage. A credit card can serve as a temporary emergency fund to help you out of a tight spot.
4. **Earn Rewards:** Some credit cards offer travel rewards or overseas transaction rebates, meaning you can enjoy extra perks or save money on your trip, making it even more enjoyable.

## **Drawbacks of Using Credit Cards:**

1. **Currency Conversion Fees:** When you use a credit card in Bali, if your card is settled in a foreign currency, you may incur currency conversion fees. These fees are based on the exchange rate between the local currency and your card's settlement currency, and often include an additional service charge.
2. **Overseas Transaction Fees:** Some card issuers charge extra fees for overseas transactions. These are usually a fixed percentage of each transaction and can vary. While they may seem small individually, they can add up if you use your card frequently during your trip.
3. **Other Fees:** In some cases, there may be additional charges, such as early repayment fees or penalties for exceeding your credit limit. These depend on your card's terms and conditions, so be sure to review them before you travel.
4. **Limited Acceptance:** While most large hotels, shops, and restaurants accept credit cards, smaller businesses, local eateries, street vendors, and remote areas may not. You'll need cash in those situations.
5. **Security Risks:** Although credit cards are relatively safe, there's still a risk of fraud or data theft. To minimize this, use your card in secure locations and monitor your transactions closely for any suspicious activity.
6. **Overspending:** Paying with a credit card can make it easy to lose track of your spending, leading to overspending. Use your card wisely to avoid racking up debt and ruining your travel experience.
7. **Unpredictable Issues:** Even a card that works perfectly at home can run into problems in Bali.
