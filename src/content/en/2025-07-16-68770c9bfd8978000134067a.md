Tukad Badung Riverside, also known as Tukad Badung Riverside, stands as one of the most successful urban renewal projects in Denpasar, Bali, under the 'Green City, Friendly Community' initiative in recent years. This approximately 1-kilometer-long, 4-meter-wide artificial waterway was once a turbid and foul-smelling drainage ditch. However, starting in 2020, through the collaborative efforts of the Bali Provincial Government, municipal authorities, and the community, it has been thoroughly cleaned, redesigned, and transformed into a public space that combines modern aesthetics with community functionality.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9bcc97b4941d0def4b645b5e493dc7ac.jpg)

This trail is affectionately called 'Taman Tukad Korea' primarily because its overall design inspiration comes from Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream in South Korea. It not only mimics the orderly riverbank design and lighting arrangements but also incorporates local cultural elements, allowing visitors to experience a unique charm that blends Eastern aesthetics with tropical ambiance.

The entire area is themed around 'Night Aesthetics'. As night falls, LED lights form a colorful ribbon along the riverbank, transforming every corner from the bridge and railings to lanterns and waterfall backdrops into Instagram-worthy spots. Particularly in the main waterfall area, lotus-shaped light installations complement the gently flowing artificial waterfall, creating a deeply romantic atmosphere that requires no filters for perfect photos.

Not only that, the riverbanks are lined with an abundance of wild cherry blossoms and tropical plants. During the rainy season and blooming period, the entire trail feels like a natural garden. It's perfect for family strolls during the day, while evenings and nights transform it into a paradise for couples and photography enthusiasts.

Beyond its landscape design, Tukad Badung Riverside emphasizes 'life participation.' You can find a bamboo chair by the river, savor hot grilled corn and satay, sip on refreshing passion fruit juice, and watch street performers play traditional gamelan instruments or local pop music. The area often hosts craft markets and street painters, allowing you to feel the city's creative pulse.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c5169911503e79727e1b2f6ff164cd5f.jpg)

In late 2023, the local government established an 'Ecological Education Corner' near the Kunbassari Market bridge section. Through graphic displays, it educates visitors about river ecosystems and water conservation while they rest. Future plans include an outdoor stage, community performance area, and small ecological pond to enhance the integration of education and tourism functions.

The trail facilities are well-maintained, including clean public restrooms, a waste sorting system, guide signs, Wi-Fi hotspots, and basic first aid stations. For families with children, this is a safe, vehicle-free outdoor space ideal for activities. Young people are particularly enthusiastic about filming TikTok videos or short vlogs in front of the decorative walls and reflective river.

The Kumbasari Art Market and Badung Market along the riverbanks are the best places to explore the daily life of Denpasar. Here, you can find local handicrafts, religious offerings, spices, textiles, and souvenirs, making it an excellent spot to experience authentic Balinese culture.

## Introduction to Kumbasari Art Market:

Transportation-wise, it takes about 30-40 minutes by taxi from Ngurah Rai International Airport to reach here, and it's easily accessible on foot from central Denpasar. Its extremely convenient location makes it an ideal spot to include in your itinerary as one of the best places for evening and nighttime activities.

Taman Tukad Korea showcases not only the aesthetic transformation of urban space, but also the result of joint participation by residents and the government, making Denpasar city more humanized and suitable for walking and living. Such public spaces not only enhance the quality of life for local residents, but also allow visiting travelers to discover that Bali, beyond its sunshine and beaches, has such a city attraction filled with human warmth and cultural atmosphere.

**Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ):**

1. **Is Tukad Badung Riverside free to enter?**
   Yes, it is currently open to the public and tourists free of charge.
2. **What is the best time for photography?**
   The best time to capture the light and shadow effects is between 5:30 PM and 6:30 PM.
3. **Are food and drinks available for purchase?**
   Yes, there are several stalls along the riverside selling Balinese snacks and fresh juices.
4. **How to get to Tukad Badung?**
   It takes about 30 minutes by taxi from the airport; walking from the city center only takes a few minutes.
5. **Is it suitable for family travel?**
   Very suitable, with a dedicated children's water fountain area, but parental supervision is recommended.
