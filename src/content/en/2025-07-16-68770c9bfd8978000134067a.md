Tukad Badung Riverside, also known as Tukad Badung Riverside, is one of the most successful urban renewal projects in Denpasar, Bali, under the 'Green City, Friendly Citizens' initiative. This approximately 1-kilometer-long, 4-meter-wide artificial waterway was once a muddy and foul-smelling drainage ditch. However, since 2020, through collaboration between the Bali provincial government, municipal authorities, and the community, it has been thoroughly cleaned, redesigned, and transformed into a modern aesthetic and community-oriented public space.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9bcc97b4941d0def4b645b5e493dc7ac.jpg)

The reason this walkway is nicknamed 'Taman Tukad Korea' is primarily due to its design inspiration from Seoul's Cheonggyecheon. It not only mimics the orderly riverbank design and lighting layout but also incorporates local cultural elements, creating a unique charm that blends Eastern aesthetics with tropical vibes.

The entire area is themed around 'Night Aesthetics,' with LED lights forming colorful ribbons along the riverbank after dark. From bridges and railings to lanterns and waterfall backdrops, every corner becomes a photo-worthy spot. The main waterfall area, adorned with lotus-shaped lights and a gently flowing artificial waterfall, exudes a romantic atmosphere without the need for filters.

Moreover, the riverbanks are lined with wild cherry blossoms and tropical plants, making the entire walkway feel like a natural garden during the rainy and blooming seasons. It's perfect for family strolls during the day and becomes a paradise for couples and photography enthusiasts in the evening and at night.

Beyond its scenic design, Tukad Badung Riverside emphasizes 'community engagement.' You can sit on a bamboo chair by the river, enjoy hot grilled corn and satay, sip on chilled passion fruit juice, and watch street performers play traditional gamelan or local pop music. There are also regular craft markets and street artists, allowing you to feel the city's creative pulse.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c5169911503e79727e1b2f6ff164cd5f.jpg)

In late 2023, the local government established an 'Ecology Education Corner' near the Kumbasari Market bridge section, showcasing river ecology and water conservation knowledge through visuals and text. This allows visitors to learn while relaxing. Future plans include an outdoor stage, community performance area, and small ecological pond, enhancing the integration of education and tourism.

The walkway facilities are well-equipped, including clean public restrooms, waste sorting systems, guide signs, Wi-Fi hotspots, and basic first aid stations. For families with children, it's a safe, vehicle-free outdoor activity space. Young people, meanwhile, are enthusiastic about filming TikTok videos or vlogs in front of the decorated walls and reflective river.

The Kumbasari Art Market and Badung Market along the riverbanks are the best places to explore daily life in Denpasar. Here, you can find local handicrafts, religious offerings, spices, textiles, and souvenirs, making it an excellent spot to experience authentic Balinese culture.

## Introduction to Kumbasari Art Market:

In terms of transportation, it takes about 30-40 minutes by taxi from Ngurah Rai International Airport to get here, and it's an easy walk from the center of Denpasar. The location is extremely convenient, making it one of the best spots to include in your itinerary for evening and nighttime activities.

Taman Tukad Korea showcases not only the aesthetic transformation of urban space but also the collaborative efforts of residents and the government, making Denpasar more humane and suitable for walking and living. Such public spaces not only enhance the quality of life for local residents but also allow visitors to discover that Bali has more to offer than just sun and beaches—there are also city spots filled with warmth and cultural charm.

Transportation is also very convenient. From Ngurah Rai Airport, it's about a 20-kilometer drive, and taxis can take you directly to Badung Market. It's just a 5-minute walk from Puputan Margarana Square in the city center.

Tukad Badung Riverside is not just a beautiful spot for night views; it's also a successful case of urban renewal. What was once a neglected space has been transformed into a public area enjoyed by both the community and tourists, allowing people to rediscover the gentle side of Denpasar.

**Frequently Asked Questions FAQ:**

1. **Is Tukad Badung Riverside free to enter?**  
   Yes, it is currently free and open to all residents and tourists.
2. **What is the best time for photography?**  
   The best time for capturing light and shadow effects is between 5:30 PM and 6:30 PM.
3. **Are food and drinks available for purchase?**  
   Yes, there are several stalls along the riverbank selling Balinese snacks and fresh juices.
4. **How to get to Tukad Badung?**  
   It's about a 30-minute taxi ride from the airport; just a few minutes' walk from the city center.
5. **Is it suitable for family travel?**  
   Very suitable, with a dedicated water fountain area for children, but parental supervision is recommended.
