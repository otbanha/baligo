Tukad Badung Riverside, also known as Tukad Badung Riverside, is one of the most successful urban renewal projects in Denpasar, Bali, under the 'Green City, Friendly Citizens' initiative. This 1-kilometer-long, 4-meter-wide artificial waterway was once a murky, foul-smelling drainage ditch. However, starting in 2020, through collaboration between the Bali provincial government, municipal authorities, and the community, it has been thoroughly cleaned, redesigned, and transformed into a modern, aesthetically pleasing public space with community functions.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9bcc97b4941d0def4b645b5e493dc7ac.jpg)

The pathway is affectionately called 'Taman Tukad Korea' because its design draws inspiration from Seoul's Cheonggyecheon. It not only mimics the orderly riverbank design and lighting arrangements but also incorporates local cultural elements, creating a unique charm that blends Eastern aesthetics with tropical vibes.

The entire area is themed around 'nighttime aesthetics.' After dark, LED lights form colorful ribbons along the riverbank, turning every corner—from bridges and railings to lanterns and waterfall backdrops—into a photo hotspot. The main waterfall area, adorned with lotus-shaped lights and a gently flowing artificial waterfall, exudes a romantic atmosphere that requires no filters for stunning photos.

Moreover, the riverbanks are lined with wild cherry blossoms and tropical plants, making the pathway feel like a natural garden during the rainy and blooming seasons. It's perfect for family strolls during the day and becomes a paradise for couples and photography enthusiasts in the evening and night.

Beyond its scenic design, Tukad Badung Riverside emphasizes 'life participation.' You can sit on bamboo chairs by the river, savor hot grilled corn and satay, sip on chilled passion fruit juice, and enjoy street performances of gamelan or local pop music. Handicraft markets and street artists are also regular features, allowing you to feel the city's creative pulse.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c5169911503e79727e1b2f6ff164cd5f.jpg)

In late 2023, the local government established an 'Ecology Education Corner' near the Kumbasari Market bridge, showcasing river ecology and water conservation knowledge through visuals and text. This allows visitors to learn while relaxing. Future plans include outdoor stages, community performance areas, and small ecological ponds to enhance the educational and tourism experience.

The pathway is well-equipped with clean public restrooms, waste sorting systems, guide signs, Wi-Fi hotspots, and basic first aid stations. For families with children, it's a safe, vehicle-free outdoor playground. Young people, meanwhile, love filming TikTok videos or vlogs against the backdrop of decorated walls and reflective river surfaces.

The Kumbasari Art Market and Badung Market along the riverbanks are ideal spots to explore Denpasar's daily life. Here, you can find local handicrafts, religious offerings, spices, textiles, and souvenirs, making it a great place to experience authentic Balinese culture.

**Frequently Asked Questions FAQ:**

1. **Is Tukad Badung Riverside free to enter?**
   Yes, it is currently open to the public and tourists free of charge.
2. **What is the best time for photography?**
   The best time for capturing light and shadow effects is between 5:30 PM and 6:30 PM.
3. **Are food and drinks available for purchase?**
   Yes, there are several stalls along the riverbank selling Balinese snacks and fresh juices.
4. **How to get to Tukad Badung?**
   It's about a 30-minute taxi ride from the airport; walking from the city center takes just a few minutes.
5. **Is it suitable for family trips?**
   Very suitable, with a dedicated children's water play area, though parental supervision is recommended.
