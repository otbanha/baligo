Tukad Badung Riverside, also known as Tukad Badung Riverside, stands as one of the most successful urban renewal projects in Denpasar, Bali, under the 'Green City, Friendly Citizens' initiative in recent years. This approximately 1-kilometer-long, 4-meter-wide artificial waterway was once a muddy and foul-smelling drainage ditch. However, since 2020, through the collaborative efforts of the Bali provincial government, municipal authorities, and local communities, it has been thoroughly cleaned, redesigned, and transformed into a modern aesthetic public space with community functions.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9bcc97b4941d0def4b645b5e493dc7ac.jpg)

This trail is affectionately known as 'Taman Tukad Korea' primarily because its overall design inspiration comes from Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream in South Korea. Not only does it mimic the orderly riverbank design and lighting arrangements, but it also incorporates local cultural elements, creating a unique charm that blends Eastern aesthetics with tropical ambiance.

The entire area is themed around 'Nighttime Aesthetics.' After dark, LED lights form a colorful riverside ribbon, turning every corner—from the bridge railings to lanterns and waterfall backdrops—into a photo-worthy spot. The main waterfall area, adorned with lotus-shaped lights and complemented by a gently flowing artificial waterfall, exudes a romantic atmosphere so vivid that no filters are needed for perfect shots.

Not only that, the riverbanks are lined with an abundance of wild cherry blossoms and tropical plants. During the rainy season and blooming periods, the entire trail feels like a natural garden. It's perfect for family strolls during the day, while evenings and nights transform it into a paradise for couples and photography enthusiasts.

Beyond landscape design, Tukad Badung Riverside emphasizes 'life participation.' You can find a bamboo chair by the river, savor hot grilled corn and satay, sip on refreshing passion fruit juice, and watch street performers play traditional gamelan instruments or local pop music. The area also features permanent craft markets and street artists, allowing you to feel the city's creative pulse.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c5169911503e79727e1b2f6ff164cd5f.jpg)

At the end of 2023, the local government established an 'Ecological Education Corner' near the Kumbasari Market bridge area. Through illustrations and texts, it showcases river ecology and water conservation knowledge, allowing visitors to gain insights while resting. Future plans include setting up an outdoor stage, community performance area, and a small ecological pond, enhancing the integration of education and tourism.

The trail facilities are well-maintained, including clean public restrooms, waste sorting systems, navigation signs, Wi-Fi hotspots, and basic first aid stations. For families with children, this is a safe, vehicle-free outdoor activity area. Young people are enthusiastic about shooting TikTok videos or short vlogs in front of the decorated walls and reflective river.

The Kumbasari Art Market and Badung Market along the riverbanks are the best places to explore the daily life of Denpasar. Here, you can find local handicrafts, religious offerings, spices, textiles, and souvenirs, making it an excellent spot to experience authentic Balinese culture.

**Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ):**

1. **Is Tukad Badung Riverside free to enter?**
   Yes, it is currently open to the public and tourists free of charge.
2. **What is the best time for photography?**
   The best time to capture the light and shadow effects is between 5:30 PM and 6:30 PM.
3. **Are food and drinks available for purchase?**
   Yes, there are several stalls along the riverside selling Balinese snacks and fresh juices.
4. **How to get to Tukad Badung?**
   It takes about 30 minutes by taxi from the airport; walking from the city center only takes a few minutes.
5. **Is it suitable for family travel?**
   Very suitable, with a dedicated water fountain area for children, but parental supervision is recommended.
