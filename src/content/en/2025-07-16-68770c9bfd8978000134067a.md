Tukad Badung Riverside, also known as Tukad Badung Riverside, stands as one of the most successful urban renewal achievements in Denpasar, Bali, under the recent 'Green City, Friendly Citizens' initiative. This approximately 1-kilometer-long, 4-meter-wide artificial waterway, once a turbid and foul-smelling drainage ditch, has been transformed since 2020 through the collaborative efforts of the Bali provincial government, municipal authorities, and local communities. It has been thoroughly cleaned, redesigned, and developed into a modern aesthetic public space with community functions.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9bcc97b4941d0def4b645b5e493dc7ac.jpg)

This trail is affectionately known as 'Taman Tukad Korea' primarily because its overall design inspiration comes from Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream in South Korea. Not only does it emulate the orderly riverbank design and lighting arrangements, but it also incorporates local cultural elements, creating a unique charm that blends Eastern aesthetics with tropical ambiance.

The entire area is themed around 'Nighttime Aesthetics,' where LED lights form a colorful ribbon along the riverbank after dark. From the bridge railings to lanterns and waterfall backdrops, every corner becomes a photo-worthy spot. Particularly in the main waterfall area, lotus-shaped lights complement the gently flowing artificial waterfall, creating a romantic atmosphere that requires no filters for stunning photos.

Moreover, the riverbanks are lined with wild cherry blossoms and tropical plants. During the rainy season and blooming period, the entire trail feels like a natural garden. It's perfect for family strolls during the day, while evenings and nights transform it into a paradise for couples and photography enthusiasts.

Beyond its landscape design, Tukad Badung Riverside emphasizes 'life participation.' You can find a bamboo chair by the river, savor piping hot grilled corn and satay, sip on refreshing passion fruit juice, and watch street performers play traditional gamelan instruments or local pop music. The area also regularly hosts craft markets and street artists, allowing you to feel the city's creative pulse.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c5169911503e79727e1b2f6ff164cd5f.jpg)

In late 2023, the local government established an 'Ecological Education Corner' near the Kumbasari Market bridge section. Through graphic displays, it educates visitors about river ecosystems and water conservation while they rest. Future plans include setting up an outdoor stage, community performance area, and small ecological pond to enhance the integration of education and tourism.

The trail facilities are well-maintained, featuring clean public restrooms, a waste sorting system, clear signage, Wi-Fi hotspots, and basic first aid stations. For families with children, this is a safe, vehicle-free outdoor space ideal for activities. Younger visitors are particularly drawn to capturing TikTok videos or short vlogs in front of the decorative walls and reflective river.

The Kumbasari Art Market and Badung Market along the riverbanks are the best places to explore the daily life of Denpasar. Here, local handicrafts, religious offerings, spices, textiles, and souvenirs are sold, making it an excellent spot to experience authentic Balinese culture.

## Introduction to Kumbasari Art Market:

Getting there is convenient - it's about a 30-40 minute taxi ride from Ngurah Rai International Airport, and easily accessible on foot from downtown Denpasar. Its prime location makes it an ideal spot to include in your itinerary as one of the best places for evening and nighttime activities.

Taman Tukad Korea showcases not only the aesthetic transformation of urban space, but also the collaborative efforts of residents and the government, making Denpasar City more human-centric and pedestrian-friendly. This public space has not only enhanced the quality of life for local residents but also allowed visiting travelers to discover that Bali offers more than just sun and beaches—there are also urban attractions brimming with warmth and cultural charm.

**Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ):**

1. **Is Tukad Badung Riverside free to enter?**
   Yes, it is currently free for all visitors and locals.
2. **What is the best time for photography?**
   The best time to capture the lighting effects is between 5:30 PM and 6:30 PM.
3. **Are food and drinks available?**
   Yes, there are several stalls along the riverside offering Balinese snacks and fresh juices.
4. **How to get to Tukad Badung?**
   It takes about 30 minutes by taxi from the airport; walking from the city center only takes a few minutes.
5. **Is it family-friendly?**
   Very family-friendly, with a dedicated water fountain play area for children, though parental supervision is recommended.
