Our 6-day, 5-night Bali adventure has come to a successful conclusion! We're incredibly grateful to the helpful online community and the wealth of information provided, which made traveling with our two children both relaxing and enjoyable. The trip went smoothly!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_46eaba429c96b06a7d14c92bea5677e0.jpg)

First, let's talk about our preparations and customs process:

✔️ **E-Visa:** Upon arrival, we noticed that the queue for visa-on-arrival took about 20 minutes. Fortunately, thanks to the updated e-visa website shared in the community, we saved a lot of time! Using the e-visa was much more convenient, allowing us to start our journey sooner.

Further reading: [Everything You Need to Know About Landing, Customs, and Exiting the Airport](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/) (2025 latest edition)

✔️ **Currency Exchange:** Initially, we exchanged USD$200, as most places accepted card payments. However, towards the end, we realized we didn't have enough cash to pay for our driver's car rental service, so we exchanged an additional USD$150 at the hotel. This experience taught us that while card payments are convenient, it's essential to keep an eye on cash needs, especially when using car rental services, to ensure it doesn't disrupt our plans!

> Further Reading: [How Much Cash Should You Prepare for Your Bali Trip?](/blog/2023-09-11-64fdaddefd89780001bdb780/)

Next is our detailed itinerary, which we arranged as follows:

## **Day 1**

After arriving in Bali, we headed straight to our accommodation at Bidadari Villa. Along the way, our driver Agus kindly stopped for us to purchase a SIM card and exchange currency. We opted for the most affordable 12GB data plan, which cost around USD$16. The internet speed in Bali was quite stable, and the data allowance was sufficient for our entire trip, whether we were navigating or sharing our travel experiences online.

In the evening, we walked about 8 minutes to Natys Restaurant Ubud for dinner. While the prices weren't exactly cheap, the quality of the food was excellent, with both taste and presentation meeting high standards. The service was attentive, and the restaurant's atmosphere was perfect for a relaxing family dinner.

![Natys Restaurant Ubud 用餐](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_14e6f10810dcbe116db5e67a5c771e2e.jpg)

Dining at Natys Restaurant Ubud

Our first day went smoothly. Despite feeling a bit tired from the journey, the comfortable accommodation and delicious meal quickly put us in a vacation mood, and we looked forward to the days ahead.

## Day Two

![我們在Nukuwera玩River Tubing](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6568e81ae3a73a6d63bd5d98eebe2e75.jpg)

We went river tubing at Nukuwera

Today was a day trip around Ubud by private car. Our first stop was Nukuwera & Pandawa River Tubing, recommended by Jason. The package included lunch, and the tubing experience was family-friendly. We had a great time with our kids, who thoroughly enjoyed their time in the water.

Introduction to Nukuwera River Tubing: [Bali Cave River Tubing Adventure: A Family-Friendly Journey with Nukuwera River Tubing](/blog/2025-06-09-68463dcbfd897800011c6f06/)

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary**

![野生的猴子很容易就暴走，最好保持距離](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9b47f1081db7082131841f0d5a6803c9.jpg)

Wild monkeys can be unpredictable, so it's best to keep a safe distance

The kids were fascinated by the monkeys, but we remained cautious as these creatures can sometimes be mischievous

Next, we visited **Ubud Palace** to soak in the local cultural atmosphere. Afterward, we made a special stop at **KUO CUSINE** to purchase some locally made jams. The variety was impressive, and bringing a few jars home as souvenirs was a great idea

Dinner was more spontaneous since we were traveling with kids, so our schedule was relaxed. We found **Warung Pulau Kelapa** through a quick Google search, and thankfully, the food was excellent. Although a bit pricey, the ambiance was perfect for a family dinner. For us, a comfortable dining environment and convenient location mattered more than the cost, allowing us to enjoy a stress-free meal

![Warung Pulau Kelapa豬肋排、沙爹、印尼炒飯](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f03637f17c9aaf0b53884be36d55b084.jpg)

Overall, today's itinerary wasn't packed, but it was filled with family-friendly fun, leaving everyone satisfied

## **Day Three**

Today was our travel day to **Bali Safari & Marine Park**. Along the way, we asked our driver Benny to take us to **Ayoo-Ayoo** for their famous burgers. With a perfect 5-star rating on Google, we had high expectations, and it didn't disappoint! The burgers were delicious—juicy, flavorful, and generously topped. It was a delightful culinary experience for the whole family

![Ayoo Ayoo](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1b22614e00a6c81c0cbe97cccd84c7c3.jpg)

Ayoo Ayoo

> Editor's Note: I've tried at least 50 different burgers in Bali, and almost none were disappointing - each one was delicious!

Next, we stopped by a nearby supermarket to pick up some essentials before arriving at the zoo and checking in. That evening, we chose to dine at **Tsavo Lion Restaurant** within the park, which offers the unique experience of enjoying your meal while watching lions through glass windows! The kids were thrilled, making the entire dining experience both fun and unforgettable.

![Bali Safari Zoo](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9d3eacc77a4c925c1e1acc7690259fa5.jpg)

Bali Safari Zoo

After dinner, there was a photo service available outside the restaurant where you could take pictures with the animals. We opted for this service, and the photographer took numerous photos for us, including ones of mom and brother, dad and sister, and the whole family of four. The best part was that the entire package, including all digital files and one A4-sized printed photo in a beautiful album, cost only around USD$23. The photo quality was excellent, and everyone looked great in the pictures. We felt it was a fantastic value for capturing these memories!

![孩子們在動物園玩得很開心！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d2a9c9a4213a421155d1dfde20dfb81f.jpg)

Although today's itinerary was mostly about moving from place to place, it was filled with surprises and warm family moments.

## **Day Four**

Today we moved from Bali Safari to Westin Nusa Dua. Along the way, we asked our driver Agus to help us find local snacks, including Siobak (roast pork) and Martabak (stuffed pancake). Agus not only found us a suitable small shop but also carefully communicated with the shop owner to ensure we weren't overcharged as tourists. This thoughtful service really warmed our hearts.

> Further reading: [Top 10 Must-Try Foods in Bali](/blog/2023-05-02-64db6b81fd897800013a98e3/)

Next, we asked our driver about buying souvenirs and visited Krisna and The Balinese. When recommending Krisna, Agus specifically reminded us that if we needed to go elsewhere, we could tell him anytime, allowing us to decide our itinerary freely. This reminded me of a travel experience over a decade ago when I clearly stated I didn't want to shop but was still forcibly taken to several shopping factories by the driver. I was really angry at the time. This experience made me feel very satisfied and relaxed because Agus completely respected our wishes.

For dinner, we enjoyed burgers and Indian cuisine at Velada Restaurant within the resort. The restaurant environment was very comfortable, and the food was delicious. The burger was flavorful, and the Indian cuisine was a delightful surprise with its perfect use of spices, making it a perfect end to our fourth day of the trip.

Overall, today's itinerary allowed us to experience the warmth and thoughtfulness of the locals, making our trip even more enjoyable and memorable.

## **Day Six**

On our final day, we headed to the airport, once again relying on Agus for transportation. We are deeply grateful for his and Benny's patience and understanding towards our children's noise during the rides, which made our journey smoother.

For lunch, we opted for **Made's Warung** at the airport. Our farewell meal naturally included the nostalgic **Nasi Goreng** and **Mie Goreng**. These classic Indonesian fried rice and noodles never fail to satisfy, accompanying our emotions as we bid farewell to Bali👋, perfectly concluding our trip.

Throughout the journey, we not only enjoyed stunning scenery and delicious food but also created many beautiful memories with our children, which will remain cherished experiences in our hearts.
