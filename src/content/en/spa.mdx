## **First-Time Spa Visitor’s Guide**

### What type of treatment should I choose?

**Massage (Urut)** — Best for: First-timers and budget-conscious travelers
- Traditional Balinese massage: Deep tissue work, improves circulation, $5-20
- Swedish massage: Relaxation-focused, gentler pressure, $15-30
- Hot stone massage: Therapeutic warmth, reduces tension, $20-40

**Water Therapy (Spa)** — Best for: Luxury seekers and skincare enthusiasts
- Body scrub + facial: Deep cleansing and rejuvenation, $30-80
- Aromatherapy with oils: Stress relief and relaxation, $40-100
- Hydrotherapy treatments: Using water jets and herbs, $50-150

### Traditional vs. Modern Spa: Which Should You Choose?

**Traditional Balinese Spa:**
- Emphasis on healing and wellness
- Uses natural ingredients (coconut, rice, herbs)
- Focus on pressure points and energy balance
- Usually in smaller, intimate settings
- Price: $5-40

**Modern Spa Resort:**
- Luxury facilities with contemporary design
- Scientific skincare treatments with premium products
- Multiple treatment options in one location
- Often includes amenities (pools, saunas, steam rooms)
- Price: $50-300+

**Pro Tip:** Many travelers do both! Start with a budget traditional massage in Ubud to experience authentic Balinese healing, then splurge on a luxury spa experience in Jimbaran or Seminyak.
