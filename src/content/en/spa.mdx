## **First-Time Spa Visitor Guide**

### What Treatment Should I Choose?

**Massage (Urut)** — Best for: First-time visitors and budget-conscious travelers
- Traditional Balinese Massage: Deep tissue work to boost circulation, $5-20
- Swedish Massage: Gentle pressure for relaxation, $15-30
- Hot Stone Massage: Therapeutic heat to ease muscle tension, $20-40

**SPA** — Best for: Luxury seekers and skincare enthusiasts
- Body Scrub + Face Mask: Deep exfoliation and rejuvenation, $30-80
- Aromatherapy with Essential Oils: Stress relief and relaxation, $40-100
- Hydrotherapy: Water-based massage and herbal oils, $50-150

### Traditional Balinese SPA vs. Modern Resort SPA: Which to Choose?

**Traditional Balinese SPA:**
- Emphasizes healing and wellness
- Uses natural ingredients (coconut, rice, herbs)
- Focuses on acupressure and energy balance
- Typically small, intimate settings
- Price: $5-40

**Modern Resort SPA:**
- Luxurious facilities with contemporary design
- Science-based skincare treatments with premium products
- Wide range of treatments in one location
- Often includes pools, saunas, and steam rooms
- Price: $50-300+

**Pro Tip:** Many travelers try both! Start with an affordable traditional massage in Ubud for an authentic Balinese healing experience, then splurge on a luxury SPA in Jimbaran or Seminyak.
