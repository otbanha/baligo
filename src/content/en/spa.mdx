## **First-Time SPA Visitor Guide**

### What Treatment Should I Choose?

**Massage (Urut)** — Best for: First-timers and budget-conscious travelers
- Traditional Balinese massage: Deep tissue work to boost circulation, $5-20
- Swedish massage: Relaxation-focused with gentle pressure, $15-30
- Hot stone massage: Therapeutic heat to relieve muscle tension, $20-40

**SPA Treatments** — Best for: Luxury seekers and skincare enthusiasts
- Body scrub + mask: Deep cleansing and rejuvenation, $30-80
- Aromatherapy: Stress relief and relaxation, $40-100
- Hydrotherapy: Using water massage and herbal oils, $50-150

### Traditional Balinese SPA vs Modern Resort SPA: Which to Choose?

**Traditional Balinese SPA:**
- Emphasizes healing and wellness
- Uses natural ingredients (coconut, rice, herbs)
- Focuses on pressure points and energy balance
- Typically in small, intimate settings
- Price: $5-40

**Modern Resort SPA:**
- Luxurious facilities with contemporary design
- Science-based skincare treatments using premium products
- Wide range of treatments available in one location
- Typically includes swimming pool, sauna, and steam room
- Price: $50-300+

**Pro Tip:** Many travelers try both! Start with an affordable traditional massage in Ubud for an authentic Balinese healing experience, then treat yourself to a luxurious SPA in Jimbaran or Seminyak.
