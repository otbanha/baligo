## **First-Time SPA Visitor Guide**

### What Treatment Should I Choose?

**Massage (Urut)** — Ideal for: First-time visitors and budget-conscious travelers
- Traditional Balinese Massage: Deep tissue massage, promotes circulation, $5-20
- Swedish Massage: Focused on relaxation, gentle pressure, $15-30
- Hot Stone Massage: Therapeutic heat, relieves muscle tension, $20-40

**Spa Treatments** — Ideal for: Luxury seekers and skincare enthusiasts
- Body Scrub + Mask: Deep cleansing and rejuvenation, $30-80
- Aromatherapy: Relieves stress and promotes relaxation, $40-100
- Hydrotherapy: Uses water massage and herbal oils, $50-150

### Traditional Balinese SPA vs Modern Resort SPA: Which to Choose?

**Traditional Balinese SPA:**
- Focuses on healing and wellness
- Uses natural ingredients (coconut, rice, herbs)
- Emphasizes acupressure and energy balance
- Typically small and intimate settings
- Price: $5-40

**Modern Resort SPA:**
- Luxurious facilities with contemporary design
- Scientific skincare treatments using high-end products
- Multiple treatment options in one location
- Typically equipped with swimming pools, saunas, and steam rooms
- Price: $50-300+

**Expert Tip:** Many travelers try both! Start with an affordable traditional massage in Ubud to experience authentic Balinese healing, then indulge in a luxurious SPA experience in Jimbaran or Seminyak.
