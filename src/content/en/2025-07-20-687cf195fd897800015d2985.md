The veterinary department in Badung Regency, Bali recently reported several cases of rabies-infected dog bites. Earlier this month, one of our tour group members was bitten by a stray dog in the Kuta area, spending USD$5,806 on rabies vaccinations. The incident was even featured on local news. Please see [the report here](/blog/2025-07-04-68677128fd8978000174d48a/).

## South Kuta Area Designated as Enhanced Monitoring Zone

Bali authorities have designated Nusa Dua, Benoa, Jimbaran, and parts of Canggu as 'Enhanced Animal Disease Control Zones'. These areas see high tourist traffic and increasing numbers of stray animals, necessitating enhanced public health control measures.

## Vaccination Campaigns and Volunteer Participation

Upon detection of rabies-positive cases, local authorities swiftly initiate mass vaccination programs. For instance, on July 10th, vaccinations were administered to dogs around Jimbaran Polytechnic and the Pratama Street area. Volunteer groups are also assisting with trapping, neutering, and vaccination efforts.

## Preventive Measures for Travelers and Pet Owners

Travelers to Bali and local pet owners should follow these basic rules: vaccinate pet dogs annually, prevent them from roaming alone, and avoid contact with unfamiliar or stray animals. If bitten or scratched, immediately wash the wound with warm water and soap for at least 10 minutes, then seek medical attention promptly for vaccination. The National I.G.N.G. Ngoerah General Hospital in Denpasar offers subsidized vaccines, providing convenience for travelers.

## Monkeys May Also Serve as Virus Carriers

At attractions like the Monkey Forest or Uluwatu Temple in Ubud, tourists often interact with monkeys. However, these wild animals may carry rabies, so it's advised not to feed, touch, or approach them. Maintaining distance from animals is an important step in preventing disease transmission.

## ****Thanks to Experts' Efforts, Bali Remains a Safe Tourist Paradise****

Through the joint efforts of the animal health department and volunteers, rabies prevention in Bali's major resort areas has entered an active control phase. As long as basic safety recommendations are followed, travelers can still safely enjoy Bali's natural scenery and cultural charm.

## ****Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)****

****What should I do if bitten by a dog in Bali?****  
Immediately wash the wound with soap and warm water for about 10-15 minutes, then go to the nearest medical center as soon as possible to receive a rabies vaccine.

****Can monkeys transmit rabies?****  
Yes, they can. Monkeys are also potential carriers of the virus, and keeping your distance is the best prevention method.

****Do dogs need to be vaccinated every year?****  
Yes. According to veterinary recommendations and local regulations, dogs should be vaccinated annually to ensure protection.

****Which areas in Bali are currently high-risk zones?****  
Southern Kuta areas, including Nusa Dua, Jimbaran, Benoa, and parts of Canggu, are under enhanced epidemic control.

****Can I get reimbursement for vaccine costs through travel insurance?****  
Some travel insurance plans cover rabies vaccine costs. It's recommended to confirm your policy details before departure.

****What if I don't have enough money to afford the rabies vaccine?****  
Most large international hospitals in Bali (such as BIMC, Siloam) and clinics specializing in foreigner services (like Unicare, Nusa Medica) accept international credit cards (Visa, Mastercard).  
Rabies vaccines usually require multiple doses (e.g., days 0, 3, 7, 14, 28). You don't need to pay for all doses at once; you can pay for the first shot (day 0) initially.

****How much does the first rabies shot cost?****  
For the first shot, you'll need to prepare approximately IDR 1,500,000 – 2,200,000 (around USD$48 - $71). The most expensive part is the rabies immunoglobulin (RIG). If the doctor determines your wound is a Category 3 exposure (involving bleeding or deep bites), in addition to the vaccine, you'll need to receive immunoglobulin injections. This treatment is dosed according to body weight and can cost as much as IDR 10,000,000 – 25,000,000 (approximately USD$323 - $806 or more).
