Bali isn't entirely without tissues or toilet paper, but you might struggle to find them when you need them. This is because in Southeast Asian countries, they typically use a device known as the 'bum gun'—a water sprayer—to clean themselves after using the toilet, often using their hands.

For many, this method of cleaning might come as a shock and be hard to accept. However, this approach is actually more hygienic than using toilet paper. Many studies show that washing with water is more effective at removing residue and reduces the risk of skin irritation and infections.

Some public restrooms and restaurants in Bali don't provide toilet paper. If you can't get used to the 'bum gun' method, it's best to carry your own toilet paper.

Additionally, Bali's sewage system can't handle toilet paper or wet wipes, so remember to dispose of them in the trash bin provided.

Encountering different cultures and lifestyles during travel is a rich and valuable experience. While the 'bum gun' culture might feel unfamiliar and surprising, being open to and respecting local customs will make your journey even more enriching.
