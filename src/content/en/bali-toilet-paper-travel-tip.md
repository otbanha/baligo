Bali isn't devoid of tissues or toilet paper, but you might struggle to find them when you need them most. This is because in Southeast Asian countries, they typically use a device called a 'bum gun' - spraying water while cleaning manually.

For many, this cleaning method may come as a shock and be hard to accept. However, this method is actually cleaner and more hygienic than using toilet paper. Many studies show that washing with water is more effective at removing residue while reducing skin irritation and infection risks.

Some public restrooms and restaurants in Bali don't provide toilet paper. If you can't accept using the 'bum gun' method, it's best to carry your own toilet paper.

Additionally, Bali's sewage system can't handle toilet paper and wet wipes, so please remember to dispose of them in the nearby trash bin after use.

Encountering different cultures and lifestyles during travel is an enriching and valuable experience. While the 'bum gun' culture may feel unfamiliar and surprising, being open to accepting and respecting local customs will make your journey even more colorful.
