Nuanu Creative City is a 44-hectare creative complex located in Tabanan, Bali (south of Tanah Lot Temple), founded in 2020 by Russian tech entrepreneur Sergey Solonin with a total investment of USD$100 million. The ambitious project's name, **Nuanu**, derives from Balinese, meaning '**in progress**,' symbolizing the ever-evolving nature of this creative city. Solonin launched this project after relocating to Bali five years ago, aiming to create an ecosystem where art, technology, and nature coexist harmoniously.

## Founding Philosophy and Core Principles

Nuanu Creative City is rooted in the traditional Balinese philosophy of **Tri Hita Karana**, which emphasizes harmonious balance between humans and gods, humans and others, and humans and nature. The landmark **THK Tower** (Tri Hita Karana Tower), designed by architect Arthur Mamou Mani and constructed using reclaimed wood, embodies this philosophy. This spiral tower is not only a visual centerpiece but also a symbol of Nuanu's spiritual core.

He positions Nuanu as an **experiential park**, blending education, wellness, and sustainable architectural living, aiming to become a model for future utopias.

## Sustainability and Environmental Commitment

The most distinctive feature of Nuanu Creative City is its unwavering commitment to environmental sustainability. Across its 44-hectare site, developers have pledged to preserve two-thirds (approximately 70%) as natural landscape, with only 30% allocated for construction. Specific environmental initiatives include:

- **Tree Planting Program**: Planting 15,000 trees in educational areas to enhance biodiversity and carbon sequestration
- **Waste Management**: Recycling 70% of waste generated within the complex
- **Ecosystem Protection**: Avoiding the cutting down of large trees, relocating them to other areas within the complex when necessary
- **Mangrove Conservation**: Protecting and expanding the mangrove ecosystem, replanting mangrove seedlings and native coastal tree species
- **Zero Emission Transition**: Gradually adopting zero-emission vehicles to reduce carbon footprint

These measures set Nuanu apart from the prevalent overdevelopment seen in Bali, positioning it as a model project for sustainable development.

## Community Engagement and Global Vision

Nuanu is not just a physical space but a vibrant creative community. Each project partner commits 5% of their monthly revenue to the Nuanu Social Fund, which supports reforestation or social initiatives. The fund has already backed various community projects, such as establishing a computer education center in Beraban Village and preserving traditional Balinese music.

Solonin envisions Nuanu's future as: "Over the next decade, Nuanu will become a dynamic global hub, attracting the world's most innovative minds to live, collaborate, and visit. It will be a dynamic community for visionaries, artists, and changemakers, living and working in thoughtfully designed spaces that inspire creativity."

Nuanu Creative City commenced operations in July 2024, hosting a series of international events including the Suara Art Music Festival, CoinFest Web3 Festival, and DEEP Wellness Festival, further cementing its status as a nexus of innovation and culture.

This creative city represents a new paradigm in urban development, demonstrating that modern development can address contemporary issues while respecting local traditions, inviting people to join in the journey towards a sustainable future. As one observer noted, Nuanu is not just a tourist destination in Bali, but a "space to live, work, and create, a city of the future."
