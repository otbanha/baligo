### Travel Dates: February 6-9, 2025

This was my first trip to Bali, and although it was only four days long, it was filled with surprises and moving experiences. I’m especially grateful for the valuable advice provided by many experienced travelers when I asked questions online last September, which made planning this trip much smoother. To give back to the community, I’d like to share detailed insights and recommendations from this trip, hoping to provide some reference for those planning to visit Bali in the future.

### 1. **Mara River Safari Lodge: A Unique Experience of Sleeping with Animals**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b29252ee62cf7e762ff1f447761b8fa7.jpg)

During this trip, my 7-year-old child’s favorite part was **Mara River Safari Lodge**. This is a resort located within a zoo, offering not only convenience but also close encounters with various animals. Every morning, we enjoyed breakfast while watching lions through the glass windows—a truly special experience. The lions seemed accustomed to visitors’ gazes, often posing in various positions as if they were “striking a pose” for us. My child was completely captivated and thrilled.

Additionally, taking the safari bus was very convenient, allowing us to easily observe a variety of animals. The kids couldn’t stop exclaiming in excitement, fully immersed in this vibrant environment. If you’re traveling with children, this is definitely a must-visit destination.

### 2. **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple: A Serene Spiritual Haven**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bbe079ae357378ab17d4b8d0b2fcadd3.jpg)

For seniors aged 70, **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple** is the most relaxing place. The environment here is incredibly tranquil, surrounded by mountains with a lake as calm as a mirror and a gentle breeze that refreshes the soul. We strolled leisurely, sipped coffee, and enjoyed the peace and beauty of nature. The misty mountain scenery feels like stepping into a fairyland, and the elders particularly loved this escape from the hustle and bustle, finding it a perfect place to unwind completely.

### 3. **Tanah Lot: Majestic Sea Erosion Wonders**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e939c789a7f931dc80650833c7e66e7c.jpg)

**Tanah Lot** is one of Bali's iconic landmarks, featuring stunning sea arches and platforms that perfectly match our imagination of Bali. Although the seaside sun can be intense, watching the waves crash against the rocks is truly awe-inspiring. Friends visiting here are advised to bring hats or umbrellas and come in the evening to avoid the heat and enjoy a beautiful sunset.

### 5. **Tirta Empul Temple: A Dual Surprise of Purification Rituals and Presidential Palace**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4f849d9c235d59a137fbef5de65d6f96.jpg)

**Tirta Empul** is a place filled with religious atmosphere, where we witnessed many locals performing bathing rituals. The scene was both solemn and novel. However, compared to the bathing rituals, I was personally more intrigued by the presidential palace on the hill. This building is steeped in history, and the surrounding environment is exceptionally beautiful, making it worth spending some time to savor.

### 7. **Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park: Majestic Yet Sparse**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9ff7c54eabc3f3383b36804884d88bd9.jpg)

The **Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park** covers a vast area, with the most striking feature being the enormous Garuda statue, whose height and scale are truly awe-inspiring. The statue is visible from the airport, highlighting its grandeur. However, the park's facilities seem somewhat incomplete, giving a sense of emptiness. It is suggested that visitors arrange a brief stop here to admire the statue before moving on to other attractions.

### 8. **Private Driver: The Unsung Hero of the Trip**

A significant part of the success of this trip is owed to our private driver **Xiao Wu** and his wife **Betty Pingky**. With five people in our group and a lot of luggage, Xiao Wu specially arranged a large vehicle to ensure our comfort during the journey. His driving skills were very steady, with no sudden brakes or jerks, allowing the child with a cold and the elderly to rest well in the car.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3143a1eb899bf827d37ad8851ac6b8d4.jpg)

Moreover, Xiao Wu was very familiar with Bali's road conditions, perfectly avoiding traffic jams and congested routes, making our itinerary smoother. Whether it was the elderly needing a restroom break or the child constantly asking "Are we there yet?", he always managed to arrive at the destination at the right time, keeping the family atmosphere harmonious. His Chinese was very fluent, with a standard accent, making it easy to chat with the elderly and children, adding warmth to the entire journey.

It is worth mentioning that Betty Pingky was very attentive before the trip, helping us confirm the hotel packages and adjusting the itinerary according to our needs. Even during the trip, when we casually mentioned a certain attraction, Xiao Wu would take a detour to show us, which was a thoughtful service that touched us deeply.

### 9. **Why No Traffic Jams?**

During this trip, we hardly encountered any traffic jams, which pleasantly surprised me. Upon reflection, there are a few possible reasons:

1. **Clever Timing**: We visited **Tanah Lot** and **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple** during off-peak hours, choosing to go around lunchtime when the roads were less crowded.
2. **Driver’s Expertise**: Xiao Wu, our driver, was very familiar with Bali’s roads. When heading to **Tirta Empul** and **Jimbaran**, he took some back roads, avoiding the congestion on main thoroughfares.
3. **Personal Tolerance for Traffic**: Having lived in Jakarta for a long time, I’m used to endless traffic jams. In comparison, Bali’s traffic felt like paradise.

These factors combined made our journey smoother and significantly improved my impression of Bali.

### 10. **Few Photos: Busy Taking Care of Family**

We took far fewer photos during this trip than expected, mainly because after getting out of the car, I was almost always busy taking care of my 7-year-old child and 70-year-old elder. The child was lively and needed constant supervision, while the elder required timely rest and assistance. Although we missed out on some great shots, ensuring that the whole family had a fun and safe time felt more important than taking photos. After all, the essence of travel lies in creating memories, not just checking in for social media.

### 11. **A Small Regret About the Villa Pool**

![示意圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b77f1b401df4485747072419ea8c5e64.jpg)

The villa we stayed in was beautiful, but the only downside was that the pool was 1.5 meters deep, which was too deep for the child to enjoy playing in the water. This also made it hard for the adults to fully relax, as we had to constantly watch over the child’s safety. For future villa bookings, I recommend checking the pool depth, especially for families with children. Opting for a shallow pool or a children’s pool would be more suitable.

### Summary and Rating

Our family trip to Bali left us thoroughly satisfied, especially with the professional service of our private driver and Bali's stunning natural beauty, which made a deep impression on us. We initially thought Bali's traffic would be as congested as Jakarta's, but we hardly encountered any traffic jams, greatly enhancing our travel experience. If I were to rate this trip out of 100, I'd give it a **95**. The only downside was the depth of the villa's swimming pool.

Although this trip was busy—so much so that we didn't even have time for a massage (I made up for it right after returning to Jakarta)—at least the whole family found Bali enjoyable. Both the kids and the elders had a great time, making all the effort worthwhile. The essence of travel isn't about perfection but about the companionship and memories created with family.

---

### Recommendations and Suggestions

- **Private Driver**: Highly recommend **Betty Pingky** and her husband **Mr. Wu**. Their attentive service and fluent Chinese make them ideal for family trips.
- **Itinerary Planning**: Tailor your itinerary based on the ages and interests of family members. For example, families with children might enjoy more zoo or rice terrace activities, while elders may prefer serene spots like the Water Temple.
- **Accommodation Choices**: When selecting a villa, be sure to check the pool depth, especially important for families with young children.
- **Transportation Arrangements**: Avoid peak hours and choose drivers familiar with local roads to significantly enhance your travel experience.

This Bali trip has left our family with wonderful memories, and we look forward to visiting this enchanting island again in the future!
