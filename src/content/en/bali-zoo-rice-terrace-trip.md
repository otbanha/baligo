### Travel Dates: February 6-9, 2025

This was my first trip to Bali, and although it was only four days long, it was filled with surprises and heartfelt moments. I’m especially grateful for the valuable advice many experienced travelers shared online last September, which made planning this trip much smoother. To give back to the community, I’d like to share my detailed experience and recommendations, hoping to provide some insights for those planning a trip to Bali.

### 1. **Mara River Safari Lodge: A Unique Experience of Sleeping with Animals**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b29252ee62cf7e762ff1f447761b8fa7.jpg)

During this trip, my 7-year-old child’s favorite part was **Mara River Safari Lodge**. This resort is located within a zoo, making it not only convenient but also allowing close encounters with various animals. Every morning, we enjoyed breakfast while watching lions through the glass windows—a truly unique experience. The lions seemed accustomed to visitors, often posing in specific spots as if they were “striking a pose” for us. My child was completely captivated and thrilled.

Additionally, taking the safari tour was incredibly convenient, offering easy views of various animals. The kids couldn’t stop exclaiming in excitement, fully immersed in this vibrant environment. If you’re traveling with children, this is definitely a must-visit destination.

### 2. **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple: A Serene Spiritual Haven**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bbe079ae357378ab17d4b8d0b2fcadd3.jpg)

For seniors aged 70, **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple** is the most relaxing place. The environment here is incredibly tranquil, surrounded by mountains, with the lake surface as calm as a mirror and a gentle breeze that refreshes the soul. We strolled leisurely, sipping coffee, and soaking in the peaceful beauty of nature. The misty mountain scenery feels like stepping into a fairyland, and the elders particularly love this escape from the hustle and bustle, finding it a perfect place to unwind completely.

### 3. **Tanah Lot: Majestic Sea Erosion Wonders**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e939c789a7f931dc80650833c7e66e7c.jpg)

**Tanah Lot** is one of Bali’s iconic landmarks, where the sea erosion arches and platforms are truly spectacular, perfectly matching our imagination of Bali. Although the seaside sun can be intense, watching the waves crash against the rocks is an awe-inspiring experience. Friends visiting here are advised to bring hats or umbrellas and consider arriving in the evening to avoid the midday heat and enjoy the beautiful sunset.

### 7. **Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park: Majestic Yet Sparse**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9ff7c54eabc3f3383b36804884d88bd9.jpg)

**Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park** covers a vast area, with the most striking feature being the colossal Garuda statue, whose height and scale are truly awe-inspiring. The statue is visible from the airport, highlighting its grandeur. However, the park's facilities seem somewhat incomplete, giving it a sparse feel. It is recommended to make a brief stop here to admire the statue before moving on to other attractions.

### 8. **Private Driver: The Unsung Hero of the Trip**

The success of this trip is largely thanks to our private driver **Xiao Wu** and his wife **Betty Pingky**. With five people in our group and a lot of luggage, Xiao Wu arranged a spacious vehicle to ensure our comfort throughout the journey. His driving was smooth and steady, with no sudden stops or jerks, allowing the children and elderly to rest well in the car.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3143a1eb899bf827d37ad8851ac6b8d4.jpg)

Moreover, Xiao Wu is very familiar with Bali's road conditions, skillfully avoiding traffic congestion and ensuring our itinerary ran smoothly. Whether it was the elderly needing a restroom break or the children constantly asking 'Are we there yet?', he always managed to arrive at the destination at the right time, maintaining a harmonious atmosphere for the entire family. His Chinese is fluent and his tone is clear, making it easy for him to chat with both the elderly and the children, adding warmth to the journey.

It's worth mentioning that Betty Pingky was very attentive before the trip, helping us confirm hotel packages and adjusting the itinerary based on our needs. Even during the trip, when we casually mentioned a certain attraction, Xiao Wu would go out of his way to take us there. This thoughtful service truly touched us.

### 9. **Why No Traffic Jams?**

During this trip, we hardly encountered any traffic jams, which pleasantly surprised me. Upon reflection, there are a few possible reasons:

1. **Perfect Timing**: We visited **Tanah Lot** and **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple** during off-peak hours, choosing to go around lunchtime when the roads were less crowded.
2. **Driver’s Expertise**: Xiao Wu, our driver, knew Bali’s roads well. When heading to **Tirta Empul Temple** and **Jimbaran**, he took smaller routes, avoiding the main roads’ congestion.
3. **Personal Tolerance for Traffic**: Having lived in Jakarta for a long time, I’m used to endless traffic jams. In comparison, Bali’s traffic felt like paradise.

These factors combined made our journey smoother and significantly improved my impression of Bali.

### 10. **Few Photos: Busy Taking Care of Family**

There were far fewer photos from this trip than expected, mainly because after getting out of the car, I was almost always busy taking care of my 7-year-old child and 70-year-old elder. The child was lively and needed constant supervision, while the elder required timely rest and assistance. Although I missed out on some great shots, ensuring the whole family had a fun and safe time felt more important. After all, the essence of travel lies in creating memories, not just capturing them for social media.

This also made it hard for adults to fully relax, as we had to constantly watch over the child. For future villa bookings, I recommend confirming the pool depth, especially for families with kids. Opting for shallow pools or children’s pools would be more suitable.

### Summary and Rating

Our family trip to Bali left us thoroughly satisfied, especially with the professional service of our private driver and the island's stunning natural beauty, which made a deep impression on us. We initially expected Bali's traffic to be as congested as Jakarta's, but we hardly encountered any traffic jams, greatly enhancing our travel experience. If I were to rate this trip out of 100, I'd give it **95 points**, deducting only for the depth issue of the villa pool.

Although this trip was busy, leaving no time even for a massage (I made up for it right after returning to Jakarta), at least the whole family found Bali enjoyable. Both the kids and the elders had a great time, making all the effort worthwhile. The essence of travel lies not in perfection but in the companionship and memories created with family.

---

### Recommendations and Suggestions

- **Private Driver**: Highly recommend **Betty Pingky** and her husband **Mr. Wu**. Their attentive service and fluent Chinese make them ideal for family trips.
- **Itinerary Planning**: Tailor your itinerary based on the age and interests of family members. For example, families with children might enjoy more zoo or rice terrace activities, while elders might prefer serene spots like the Water Temple.
- **Accommodation Choice**: When selecting a villa, always check the pool depth, especially important for families with young children.
- **Transportation Arrangements**: Avoid peak hours and choose a driver familiar with local roads to significantly enhance your travel experience.

Our trip to Bali has left our family with wonderful memories, and we look forward to visiting this enchanting island again in the future!
