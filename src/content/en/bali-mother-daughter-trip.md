> The following content is provided by fellow traveler [Zhang Pingping](https://www.facebook.com/zhang.p.ping.3/) and she has agreed to let us adapt it.

This itinerary is mainly based on suggestions from fellow travelers in our group and my own personal experience, aiming to provide detailed feedback for everyone. During this trip, we stayed at three different hotels, two nights each, hired a private car for three days, and had a pure airport transfer service on the last day. Below, I’ll share some key points and insights from this journey.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_31e20a9205edd79820f5422844919002.jpg)

## Driver Recommendation

For this trip, we chose Fovix Ketut, a highly recommended driver in our group, and I sincerely recommend him to anyone in need. Although my English is very limited, I managed to get through these days smoothly thanks to Ketut’s assistance. Here, I also want to remind everyone not to be afraid of language barriers—making good use of translation apps and the few words you know is enough.

> Further reading: [【Bali Guide】What to Do If You Want to Visit Bali but Don’t Speak English?](/blog/bali-travel-tips-bad-english/)

One thing to remind everyone about when booking a driver service is to be trustworthy and not stand up the driver casually, as this is the foundation of mutual trust. We communicated thoroughly in advance, and my itinerary was basically adjusted by Ketut, who even recommended the Spa for us.

## Arriving at Bali Airport

We had already completed the customs declaration, visa, and tourism tax procedures electronically in Taiwan, so everything went very smoothly upon arrival at Bali Airport. There are multiple stages of electronic clearance, and the process is very fast—it took less than ten minutes. The only slightly frustrating part was waiting for the luggage, which took about 30 minutes. Some passengers from the same flight got their luggage much earlier, but ours got stuck on the conveyor belt, sometimes due to the belt getting jammed.

When going through customs after collecting your luggage, choose the lane with the fewest people, and you can exit the airport in less than three minutes by showing the QR code.

> Further Reading: [2026 Edition] Indonesia Bali Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card | 1-Minute Guide to Latest Procedures: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

## Itinerary and Transportation

Originally, I thought this trip wouldn't be too packed and would be relaxing, but Bali's traffic congestion really kept us out late every day. I recommend leaving plenty of time to deal with traffic conditions to avoid affecting your itinerary.

## Internet Usage

My mom and I used an eSIM and a physical SIM card respectively, both from XL. My eSIM frequently lost connection, especially when I urgently needed translation, which was frustrating, forcing me to rely on the few words I could recall. My mom's physical SIM card, however, worked smoothly throughout. Over the seven days, I mainly relied on hotel WiFi and ended up using less than 3GB of data.

## May 29th Private Car Itinerary

### Arrival in Bali

This day's itinerary started with our landing. First, we exchanged money and did some supermarket shopping. Then, we went to a somewhat expensive but very famous pork rib restaurant—Naughty Nuri's—and enjoyed a delicious meal.

### Accommodation and Spa

After dining, we headed straight to our accommodation in Ubud, [Purana Boutique Resort](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1197693). This hotel is nestled in the jungle near the center of Ubud, full of natural ambiance. Although there are many bugs, the staff are very friendly, and the rooms are clean and comfortable with particularly good beds—highly recommended! The hotel offers shuttle service to the Coco supermarket near the Sacred Monkey Forest, which is very convenient. I suggest staying here, but skip the hotel breakfast as it's not tasty, just nicely presented XD

▶[Book Purana Boutique Resort, view more room types](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1197693)

We enjoyed two delightful spa sessions during our Ubud trip. The first was on the night we arrived, when our driver took us to Jaens Spa Ubud, just two minutes from our hotel. The spa quality was excellent and reasonably priced. The second session was booked through our hotel the next day.

### Exploring Ubud Town

On the second day of our stay, we took the hotel shuttle to Coco Supermarket and then walked to the Sacred Monkey Forest. The monkeys here are very mischievous, and we even experienced a thrilling monkey robbery incident. Afterward, we continued to explore the Ubud Market, strolling around and sampling local delicacies. Although we didn’t have a private car that day, our driver Ketut was still very attentive, sending us a message the night before to remind us of shopping precautions, worried that we might get scammed.

### Uluwatu Temple

Next, we arrived at Uluwatu Temple. The monkeys here are quite aggressive, so Ketut accompanied us like a bodyguard throughout, sweating profusely as he helped shoo away the monkeys and took photos for us. We were especially grateful that he carried my mom’s heavy bag, which felt like a rock. Just moments before, we witnessed a tourist’s phone being snatched by a monkey and thrown off the cliff, never to be seen again. This made us even more appreciative of Ketut’s presence and protection.

### Buying Fruit

![張萍萍提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4e911106eea2855ae9b63b329f382.jpg)

Contributed by Zhang Pingping

In Bali, my favorite fruit is mangosteen. Knowing our preference, Ketut took advantage of our spa time to visit several places in search of fresh mangosteen. He even bought some for us himself, as locals get it at half the price, but we insisted on paying him. As for durian, we didn’t come across any of good quality, and Ketut proved to be more professional than the vendors. He inspected the durian, and upon finding it subpar, he clearly communicated with the vendor on our behalf, ensuring we didn’t end up with bad fruit. He truly solved problems from our perspective.

### Seafood Dinner with Sunset Views

In the evening, Ketut took us to a highly cost-effective seafood restaurant—Menega Cafe. Here, we enjoyed a beautiful sunset view. Ketut checked the bill for us to ensure we weren’t overcharged. My mom ordered lobster and two large crabs, along with a set meal that included lobster and fish, totaling around USD$100. Even with three of us, including the driver, we couldn’t finish the food and ended up taking leftovers home. The restaurant staff, seeing that we were already full, kindly advised against serving the live fish my mom had ordered, as it would truly be too much to finish.

## June 4th: Transfer Back to Taiwan

On this day, we prepared to return to Taiwan. Since Ketut had to attend to his daughter's matters, he arranged for his brother to take us back to the airport.

### Summary and Reflections

I hope this feedback helps friends planning to visit Bali. Due to space limitations, many details couldn't be fully elaborated, but feel free to exchange ideas (o^^o). There were two restaurants not originally planned, which Ketut spontaneously took us to, and both were excellent. Unfortunately, I don't know their names (Editor's note: We have already requested Fovix Ketut's supplementary information).

On the contrary, the two trendy restaurants we visited—SunSun and Sacred Rice—didn't meet our expectations. The flavors were mediocre, not premium, nor particularly enjoyable.

### Recommended Private Driver

Regarding private drivers, I carefully selected several options from my shortlist and was fortunate to ultimately choose Fovix Ketut. Ketut treated my mother as if she were his own, constantly showing concern with phrases like 'Be careful, Mom' and 'How are you, Mom?' In turn, my mother cared for him as if he were her own son. In just a few days, we built a nice rapport.

Ketut is a cheerful driver in his early thirties with three children. He deeply loves his wife and always beams with joy when talking about her and their daughters. He works diligently to serve his clients, putting in great effort to support his family. I highly recommend him to Taiwanese friends who want to practice their English skills and enjoy adventurous independent travel—you can confidently choose Fovix Ketut.

It's worth noting that most drivers in Bali have wives and children, and they also need to take care of their families. Sometimes, unforeseen family emergencies may prevent them from working. I believe this isn't their intention, so let's empathize and understand their situation!
