> The following content is provided by group member [Zhang Pingping](https://www.facebook.com/zhang.p.ping.3/) and has been adapted with her consent.

This trip was mainly based on suggestions from the group’s community and my own personal experience, aiming to provide a detailed review. During this trip, we stayed at three hotels, two nights each, and hired a private driver for three days, plus a pure airport transfer on the last day. Let me share some highlights and insights from the journey.

## Driver Recommendation

The driver we chose this time was Fovix Ketut, highly recommended by the group. I sincerely recommend him to anyone in need. Although my English is extremely poor, I managed to get through these days smoothly, thanks to Ketut’s help. I’d like to tell everyone here: don’t be afraid of language barriers—using translation apps and the few words you know is enough.

> Further reading: [【Bali Guide】What to Do If Your English Isn’t Good for Bali?](/blog/bali-travel-tips-bad-english/)

One thing to remind everyone about booking a driver: be punctual and don’t stand the driver up—it’s the foundation of mutual trust. We communicated thoroughly in advance, and Ketut helped adjust most of my itinerary, even recommending the spa.

## Arriving at Bali Airport

We had already completed the electronic procedures for customs declaration, visa, and tourist tax in Taiwan, so everything went very smoothly upon arrival at Bali Airport. There were many e-gates, and the process was very fast—it took less than ten minutes. The only slightly frustrating part was waiting for our luggage, which took about 30 minutes. Some passengers on the same flight got their bags early, but ours got stuck on the carousel, sometimes because the luggage belt jammed.

After collecting your luggage, head to the least crowded customs lane—scanning your QR code gets you out of the airport in under three minutes.

> Read more: [[2026 Edition] Indonesia Bali Entry: Using the "All Indonesia" Arrival Card｜Master the Latest Process in 1 Minute: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

## Itinerary & Transportation

I initially thought this trip wasn't too packed and would be relaxing, but Bali's traffic jams really kept us running late every day. I highly recommend leaving plenty of buffer time for traffic to avoid disrupting your plans.

## Internet Usage

My mom and I used an eSIM and a physical SIM card respectively, both from XL. My eSIM kept dropping signal, especially when I desperately needed translation—it was so frustrating that I had to scramble to recall basic English words. Meanwhile, my mom's physical SIM worked smoothly throughout. Over the seven days, I mainly relied on hotel WiFi and used less than 3GB of data.

## May 29: Private Car Day Trip

### Arriving in Bali

That day, our itinerary kicked off right after landing. First, we exchanged currency and stocked up at the supermarket. Then, we headed to a somewhat pricey but very famous pork rib restaurant—Naughty Nuri's—for a delicious pork rib meal.

https://youtu.be/UoGlRyAdyB8

### Accommodation & Spa

After lunch, we headed straight to our accommodation in Ubud, Purana Boutique Resort. Nestled in the jungle near Ubud's center, this hotel is full of natural vibes. Although there were quite a few bugs, the staff were incredibly friendly, and the room was clean and comfortable—the bed was especially cozy, highly recommended! The hotel offers a shuttle service to the Coco supermarket near the Sacred Monkey Forest, which is very convenient. I suggest staying here, but skip the hotel breakfast—it doesn't taste great, just looks pretty on the plate XD

### Exploring Ubud Town

On our second day, we took the hotel shuttle to Coco supermarket and then walked to the Sacred Monkey Forest. The monkeys there were quite mischievous—we even had a scary encounter where one tried to snatch something from us. Afterward, we wandered through the Ubud Market, browsing and sampling local food. Although we didn't hire a car that day, our driver Ketut still looked out for us, sending a message the night before with shopping tips to help us avoid getting ripped off.

### Tanah Barak

Our first stop today was Tanah Barak. I'm not one for artsy photo poses, so Ketut couldn't stop laughing while taking my pictures. He must have been thinking, 'This lady can't even jump but insists on doing jump shots'—it was absolutely hilarious.

### Uluwatu Temple

Next, we arrived at Uluwatu Temple. The monkeys here are incredibly aggressive, so Ketut stayed by our side like a bodyguard the entire time, sweating profusely as he shooed them away and took photos for us. We were especially grateful that he carried my mom's bag, which felt as heavy as a rock. Just moments earlier, we witnessed a tourist's phone being snatched by a monkey and thrown off the cliff into the sea, never to be seen again. This made us even more thankful for Ketut's company and protection.

### Buying Fruit

![張萍萍提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4e911106eea2855ae9b63b329f382.jpg)

Provided by Zhang Pingping

In Bali, my favorite fruit is mangosteen. Knowing our preference, Ketut went out of his way to find fresh mangosteens while we were at the spa, visiting several places. He even bought them for us out of his own pocket, since locals can get them at half the price, but we insisted on paying him back. As for durian, we didn't come across any good ones. Ketut was more knowledgeable than the vendors themselves. He would check the quality of the durian, and if it wasn't up to par after opening, he'd explain it to the seller on our behalf, ensuring we didn't buy bad fruit. He truly had our best interests at heart.

### Seafood Dinner with Sunset Views

In the evening, Ketut took us to a fantastic value seafood restaurant—Menega Cafe. It offers stunning sunset views. Ketut helped us double-check the bill to make sure we weren't overcharged. My mom ordered lobster and two large crabs, plus a set meal that included lobster and fish, costing around NT$3,000 (approximately USD$97). The three of us, including our driver, couldn't even finish it all and ended up taking leftovers. When the staff saw we were full, they kindly suggested skipping the live fish my mom had ordered, as it would have been too much.

## June 4: Airport Transfer Back to Taiwan

On this day, we were heading back to Taiwan. Since Ketut had to take care of his daughter's matters, he asked his brother to pick us up and take us to the airport.

### Summary and Reflections

I hope this feedback helps those planning a trip to Bali. Due to space constraints, many details can't be fully covered here—feel free to reach out and chat (o^^o). There were two restaurants not originally on our itinerary that Ketut spontaneously took us to, and both were fantastic—unfortunately, I don't know their names (Editor's note: We've since requested Ketut's supplementary info).

On the other hand, the two trendy restaurants we visited—SunSun and Sacred Rice—didn't suit our taste. The food was average, not high-quality, and just not good.

### Recommended Private Driver

When it came to choosing a private driver, I carefully picked several candidates and was ultimately lucky to go with Fovix Ketut. Ketut treated my mom like his own mother, constantly checking in with 'Watch your step, Mom' or 'How are you, Mom?' throughout the trip. My mom, in turn, looked after him like a son. In just a few days, we built a wonderful bond.

Ketut is a sunny driver in his early 30s with three kids. He adores his wife, and every time he talks about her and his daughter, he beams with joy. He works incredibly hard to serve his guests, doing everything he can to earn a living for his family. I highly recommend him to my Taiwanese friends who want to practice their English and enjoy adventurous solo travel—you can confidently choose Fovix Ketut.

It's worth noting that most drivers in Bali have wives and children and need to support their families. Sometimes, they may face unexpected family emergencies that prevent them from working. I believe this is never their intention, so let's be understanding and empathetic toward their situation!
