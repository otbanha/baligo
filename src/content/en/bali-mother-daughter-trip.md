> The following content was provided by fellow traveler [Zhang Pingping](https://www.facebook.com/zhang.p.ping.3/) and adapted with her permission

This itinerary was primarily based on recommendations from fellow travelers in our group and my own personal experiences, aiming to provide detailed feedback for everyone. During this trip, we stayed at three different hotels, two nights each, hired a private car for three days, and arranged airport transfer service for the last day. Below, I'll share some highlights and insights from our journey.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_31e20a9205edd79820f5422844919002.jpg)

## Driver Recommendation

We chose Fovix Ketut, a highly recommended driver from our travel group, and I wholeheartedly recommend him to anyone in need. Despite my extremely limited English proficiency, we managed to get through these days smoothly, thanks to Ketut's assistance. Here, I want to emphasize: don't be afraid of language barriers - using translation apps and your limited vocabulary is enough.

> Further Reading: [【Bali Guide】What to Do If You Want to Visit Bali but Don't Speak English?](/blog/bali-travel-tips-bad-english/)

One thing to remind everyone about booking driver services: always keep your word and don't stand up your driver. This is the foundation of mutual trust. We communicated thoroughly in advance, and Ketut basically helped me adjust my itinerary. He even recommended the Spa.

## Arriving at Bali Airport

We had already completed the electronic procedures for customs declaration, visa, and tourism tax in Taiwan, so everything went very smoothly upon arrival at Bali Airport. There are multiple electronic customs clearance points, and the process was very fast - completed in less than ten minutes. The only slightly frustrating part was waiting for our luggage, which took about 30 minutes. Some passengers from the same flight got their luggage much earlier, but ours got stuck on the conveyor belt, sometimes due to jams.

When going through customs after collecting luggage, choose the lane with the fewest people. Showing the QR code, you can exit the airport in less than three minutes.

> Further Reading: [【2026 Edition】Using the 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card in Bali｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

## Itinerary and Transportation

Originally, I thought this trip wouldn't be too packed and would be relaxing, but the traffic in Bali really kept us out late every day. I recommend leaving plenty of time to deal with traffic conditions to avoid affecting your itinerary.

## Internet Usage

My mom and I used an eSIM and a physical SIM card respectively, both from XL. It turned out that my eSIM frequently lost signal, especially when I urgently needed translation, which was quite frustrating and forced me to rely on the few words I could recall. On the other hand, my mom's physical SIM card worked smoothly throughout. During these seven days, I mainly relied on hotel WiFi and ended up using less than 3GB of data.

## May 29th Private Car Itinerary

### Arrival in Bali

On this day, our itinerary started right after landing. First, we exchanged money and did some grocery shopping. Then, we headed to a somewhat expensive but very famous pork rib restaurant—Naughty Nuri's, where we enjoyed a delicious meal.

### Accommodation and Spa

After dining, we went straight to our accommodation in Ubud, [Purana Boutique Resort](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1197693). This hotel is nestled in the jungle near the center of Ubud, full of natural ambiance. Although there are quite a few bugs, the staff are very friendly, and the rooms are clean and comfortable, with particularly good beds—highly recommended! The hotel offers shuttle service to the Coco supermarket near the Sacred Monkey Forest, which is very convenient. I suggest staying here, but avoid the hotel's breakfast as it doesn't taste good, despite the attractive presentation XD

▶[Book Purana Boutique Resort, view more room types](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1197693)

We enjoyed two delightful spa experiences during our Ubud trip. The first was at Jaens Spa Ubud, just a 2-minute walk from our hotel, recommended by our driver upon arrival. The spa quality was excellent and reasonably priced. The second was arranged through our hotel the following day.

### Exploring Ubud Town Center

On our second day, we took the hotel shuttle to Coco Supermarket and then walked to the Sacred Monkey Forest. The monkeys were quite mischievous, and we even experienced a startling 'monkey robbery' incident. Afterwards, we continued exploring Ubud Market, casually browsing and sampling local delicacies. ### Uluwatu Temple

Next, we arrived at Uluwatu Temple. The monkeys here are quite aggressive, so Ketut accompanied us like a bodyguard throughout, sweating profusely, shooing away monkeys, and even taking photos for us. We were especially grateful that he carried my mom's bag, which was as heavy as a rock. Just moments before, we witnessed a tourist's phone being snatched by a monkey and thrown off the cliff, never to be seen again. This made us even more appreciative of Ketut's company and protection.

### Buying Fruits

![張萍萍提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4e911106eea2855ae9b63b329f382.jpg)

Provided by Zhang Pingping

In Bali, my favorite fruit is mangosteen. Knowing our preference, Ketut took advantage of our spa time to visit several places in search of fresh mangosteen. He even bought some for us himself, as locals get them at half the price, but we insisted on paying him. As for durian, we didn't come across any of good quality, and Ketut proved to be more professional than the vendors. He would inspect the durian, and if it wasn't up to par, he would clearly explain to the vendor, preventing us from buying subpar fruit. He truly stood by our side to solve the problem.

### Seafood Dinner with Sunset View

In the evening, Ketut took us to a highly cost-effective seafood restaurant—Menega Cafe, where we could enjoy a beautiful sunset view. Ketut helped us check the bill to ensure we weren't overcharged. My mom ordered lobster and two large crabs, plus a set meal that included lobster and fish, totaling around USD$100. Even with three of us, including the driver, we couldn't finish the food and ended up taking the leftovers with us. The restaurant staff, seeing that we were full, kindly advised us against serving the live fish my mom had ordered, as it would indeed be too much to consume.

## June 4th: Transfer Back to Taiwan

We prepared to return to Taiwan on this day. Since Ketut had to attend to his daughter's matters, he asked his brother to take us back to the airport.

### Summary and Reflections

I hope this feedback helps those planning to visit Bali. Due to space limitations, many details couldn't be fully covered, but feel free to share your thoughts (o^^o). There were two restaurants that weren't part of our original plan, which Ketut took us to on the spot, and both were excellent. Unfortunately, I don't know their names (Note: We have already requested Fovix Ketut's supplementary information).

On the contrary, the two trendy restaurants we visited—SunSun and Sacred Rice—didn't meet our expectations. The flavors were mediocre, not premium, and not particularly tasty.

### Recommended Private Driver

Regarding private drivers, I carefully selected several options and was fortunate to choose Fovix Ketut. Ketut treated my mother as if she were his own, constantly showing concern with phrases like 'Be careful, Mom' and 'How are you, Mom?' In turn, my mother cared for him like her own son. In just a few days, we built a strong bond.

Ketut is a cheerful driver in his early 30s with three children. He deeply loves his wife and always beams with joy when talking about her and their daughters. He works diligently to serve his clients, striving hard to support his family. I highly recommend him to Taiwanese friends who want to practice their English skills and enjoy adventurous independent travel—you can confidently choose Fovix Ketut.

It's worth noting that most drivers in Bali have wives and children and need to care for their families. Sometimes, unexpected family emergencies may prevent them from working. I believe this is not their intention, so let's empathize and understand their situation!
