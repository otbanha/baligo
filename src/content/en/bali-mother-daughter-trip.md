This itinerary is based on suggestions from fellow travelers in our community and my own personal experience, aiming to provide detailed feedback. During this trip, we stayed at three different hotels, two nights each, hired a private car for three days, and had a pure airport transfer service on the last day. Below, I’ll share some key points and insights from this journey.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_31e20a9205edd79820f5422844919002.jpg)

## Driver Recommendation

For this trip, we chose Fovix Ketut, a highly recommended driver in our community, and I sincerely recommend him to anyone in need. Although my English is extremely limited, we managed to get through these days smoothly, thanks to Ketut’s assistance. Here, I’d like to remind everyone not to fear language barriers—utilizing translation apps and your few remaining words is enough.

> Further reading: [Bali Travel Tips: What to Do If Your English Isn’t Good?](/blog/bali-travel-tips-bad-english/)

One thing to note when booking a driver service is to always keep your word and not stand up the driver, as this is the foundation of mutual trust. We communicated thoroughly in advance, and Ketut helped adjust my itinerary, even recommending the Spa.

## Arrival at Bali Airport

We had already completed the electronic customs, visa, and tourism tax procedures in Taiwan, so everything went smoothly upon arrival at Bali Airport. There are multiple steps for electronic clearance, and it’s very fast—completed in less than ten minutes. The only slight frustration was waiting for our luggage, which took about 30 minutes. Some passengers from the same flight got their luggage much earlier, but ours got stuck on the conveyor belt, sometimes due to jams.

When going through customs after collecting your luggage, choose the lane with the fewest people, and you’ll be out of the airport in less than three minutes by showing your QR code.

> Extended Reading: [2026 Edition] Bali, Indonesia Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card | 1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

## Itinerary Planning and Transportation

Initially, I thought our schedule wasn't too packed and would be relaxing, but Bali's traffic jams really kept us out late every day. I recommend leaving plenty of time to deal with traffic conditions to avoid disrupting your plans.

## Internet Usage

My mom and I used an eSIM and a physical SIM card respectively, both from XL. My eSIM frequently lost connection, especially when I urgently needed translation, which was frustrating—I had to rely on the few words I could recall. Meanwhile, my mom's physical SIM worked smoothly throughout. Over the seven days, I mainly relied on hotel WiFi and ended up using less than 3GB of data.

### Exploring Ubud Town

On the second day of our stay, we took the hotel shuttle to Coco Supermarket and then walked to the Sacred Monkey Forest. The monkeys here are very mischievous, and we even experienced a startling incident where they tried to steal from us. Afterward, we continued exploring Ubud Market, casually browsing and sampling local delicacies. Although we didn’t have a private car that day, our driver Ketut still cared for us, sending a message the night before to remind us of shopping tips and warning us about potential scams.

### Uluwatu Temple

Next, we arrived at Uluwatu Temple. The monkeys here are quite aggressive, so Ketut accompanied us throughout like a bodyguard, sweating profusely as he helped shoo away the monkeys and took photos for us. We were especially grateful that he carried my mom's heavy bag, which felt like a rock. Just moments before, we witnessed a tourist's phone being snatched by a monkey and thrown off the cliff into the sea, which made us even more appreciative of Ketut's presence and protection.

### Buying Fruits

![張萍萍提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4e911106eea2855ae9b63b329f382.jpg)

Provided by Zhang Pingping

In Bali, my favorite fruit is mangosteen. Knowing our preference, Ketut took advantage of our spa time to visit several places in search of fresh mangosteen. He even bought some for us himself, as locals get them at half the price, but we insisted on paying him. As for durian, we didn't come across any of good quality, but Ketut proved to be more professional than the vendors. He would inspect the durian's quality, and if it wasn't good upon opening, he would clearly explain to the vendor, preventing us from buying inferior fruit. He truly solved problems from our perspective.

### Seafood Dinner with Sunset Views

In the evening, Ketut took us to a highly cost-effective seafood restaurant—Menega Cafe, where we could enjoy a beautiful sunset. Ketut helped us check the bill to ensure we weren't overcharged. My mom ordered lobster and two large crabs, along with a set meal that included lobster and fish, totaling around USD$100. Even with three of us, including the driver, we couldn't finish the food and ended up taking leftovers. The restaurant staff, seeing we were full, kindly advised against serving the live fish my mom had ordered, as it would truly be too much.

On this day, we prepared to return to Taiwan. Since Ketut had to attend to his daughter's matters, he asked his brother to take us back to the airport.

### Summary and Thoughts

I hope this feedback helps those planning to visit Bali. Due to space limitations, many details couldn't be covered, but feel free to share your thoughts (o^^o). ### Recommended Private Driver

Regarding private drivers, I carefully selected several options and was fortunate to choose Fovix Ketut. Ketut treated my mother as if she were his own, constantly showing concern with phrases like 'Be careful, Mom' and 'How are you, Mom?' In turn, my mother cared for him like her own son. In just a few days, we developed a wonderful bond.

Ketut is a cheerful driver in his early 30s with three children. He adores his wife and always beams with happiness when talking about her and their daughters. He works diligently to serve his clients, putting in great effort to support his family. I highly recommend him to Taiwanese friends who want to practice their English skills and enjoy adventurous independent travel. You can confidently choose Fovix Ketut.

It’s worth noting that most drivers in Bali have wives and children to care for. Sometimes, unexpected family emergencies may arise, making it difficult for them to work. I believe this isn’t their intention, so let’s empathize and understand their situation!
