This time, we chose to visit Bali in July to attend a friend's wedding. The travel information for Bali is quite extensive, and it took us two to three months to finalize our itinerary.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a22d6c37c8a63306b663eaf1af023ba8.jpg)

During the pre-trip planning period, we discovered that local transportation is very chaotic, with drivers often refusing to take passengers across regions or raising prices midway. To avoid these issues, we decided to hire a private driver, which not only reduced the hassle of finding transportation but also made our journey smoother and more secure. Our driver was Komang WikaSutresna (introduced at the bottom). In the end, we felt that choosing a private driver was a very wise decision!

## Day 1:

![水明漾夕陽。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6dc896b39c10af9bac23d825129c22b9.jpg)

Seminyak Sunset. Provided by Lian Yuzhun

Having heard that Seminyak's traffic is very congested, we chose to walk around casually in the morning without hiring a driver. We arranged with our driver to pick us up from Seminyak in the evening and take us to our villa in Uluwatu. Komang arrived early at the agreed location to wait for us. We initially planned to go out for dinner on our own after arriving at the villa, but Komang checked the route using Google Street View and found that the path from the villa to the main street was very dark at night and not safe to walk. Finally, when we arrived at the villa, he waited at the entrance for us to check-in and then took us out for dinner. This thoughtful service made us feel very warm.

## Day 2:

In the morning, we visited Oneeighty Dayclub, which we highly recommend. We arrived within an hour of opening, securing a front-row seat with an ocean view.

Later, Komang recommended we visit Uluwatu Temple to watch a traditional dance performance. He even had his friend reserve tickets for us in advance, allowing us to spend more time at Blue Point Beach without worrying about buying tickets early.

A quick reminder: Uluwatu Temple is home to many monkeys, more aggressive than those on Chai Mountain. Both men and women can get robbed, and you’ll even see remnants of stolen glasses on the ground. So, it’s best not to bring any valuables when visiting.

## Day 3:

This day was dedicated to attending a wedding, so we didn’t arrange Komang’s private car service.

## Day 4:

Today's itinerary took us from Jimbaran to Ubud. Along the way, we discovered that the restaurant we planned to visit was temporarily closed. Komang immediately helped us confirm this and checked the waterfall's official website, only to find that it was also closed due to heavy rain the previous day (despite it being the dry season). However, Komang quickly offered us alternative suggestions, ensuring our day went smoothly. After visiting the waterfall, we strolled around Ubud town center and ended the day with a relaxing massage arranged by Komang.

Additionally, at Gefecon Villa Ubud where we stayed that night, there was a Taiwanese manager who was extremely kind and helpful. Feel free to ask her any questions you might have.

> Gefecon Villa Ubud [Agoda Special Offer](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1770500)

She also recommended Bintang Supermarket Ubud for souvenir shopping. Later, we saw the same nuts being sold at four times the price at the airport.

## Day 5:

![聖泉寺。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dbc6e9fddb7c4642b0a7e4bfee0a2182.jpg)

Tirta Empul Temple. Photo provided by Lian Yuzhun.

Today's route formed an 'O' shape, starting at Tirta Empul Temple and then heading to a café for volcano views. Initially, we had planned to visit AKASA Specialty Coffee, but Komang recommended Montana Del Café instead, noting that Taiwanese visitors seem to prefer it. Although the weather obscured our view of the volcano, the Korean croissants here were exceptionally delicious!

![Montana Del Café可惜天氣不好。連育諄提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8bba492232d63a05b16658986210416d.jpg)

Unfortunately, the weather wasn't great at Montana Del Café. Provided by Yu-Chun Lien.

Finally, we visited Tegallalang Rice Terrace. The entrance ticket doesn't include the swing ride - you'll need to purchase that separately at the gate. There are also special outfits available for rent that make for beautiful photos. While this spot leans towards being an Instagram hotspot, wandering through the rice paddies and hearing the screams of swing riders was thoroughly enjoyable.

> You might find this useful: [Complete Ubud, Bali Guide: 100 Ways to Deeply Explore Ubud's Charm](/blog/2023-11-17-655054b1fd897800011d4d2c/)

After our tour, we asked Komang to take us to our favorite restaurant of the trip - Kebun Bistro. Although their famous lamb chops were sold out, the steak recommended by the staff was absolutely delicious! We ordered it medium rare, and it was perfectly cooked - tender with no blood, and even the side dishes were excellent. Since it's quite popular, we recommend making reservations during peak dining hours. (Added to [Ubud Guide Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

## Day 6:

We departed from Ubud in the morning heading to Tanah Lot Temple. You might need to wade through water to reach the temple, so wear shoes and shorts that can get wet. We heard the sunset views are spectacular, but unfortunately our schedule had us there at midday.

When we arrived at Tanah Lot, we realized we'd accidentally taken our hotel key from the previous night. Komang immediately offered to return it for us in a couple days (with the hotel manager's approval) - we were so grateful!

After Tanah Lot, we went shopping and strolled along the beach in Canggu, trying Blacklist Coffee Roasters recommended by Fei Li. This turned out to be our favorite coffee of the entire Bali trip.

> Check out: [Perfect Canggu, Bali Adventure Map: 80 Must-Visit Spots in Canggu](/blog/2023-02-11-64db6b7efd897800013a9815/)

To conclude our trip, we invited Komang to join us for dinner as a thank you for taking such good care of us these past few days.

## Recommended Private Car Hire

In summary, for first-time visitors to Bali who want to explore some attractions but lack direction, hiring a private car is an excellent choice. Not only do you get professional advice, but the itinerary planning is also more convenient, and you don't have to worry about luggage.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fd9d65047cadfd58a1e16cce702fbb1.jpg)

Our driver, Komang WikaSutresna, is an incredibly friendly person. When he heard we were here for a wedding, he was delighted and even shared a short video of his own wedding from six months ago, along with a video chat with his adorable son. Komang's English is quite good, and he is very attentive. You can ask him any questions during the trip, and he will patiently answer them.

Komang takes every guest very seriously. Throughout our journey these past few days, he has been extremely attentive. At each destination, he would get out of the car to explain how to best enjoy the place. When we went for a spa, he would greet the staff and ensure our needs were well taken care of. Although we couldn't have deep conversations due to language barriers, there were no issues with communication regarding the itinerary. We highly recommend Komang to everyone!
