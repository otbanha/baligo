If there's one hotel in Bali that represents the pinnacle of luxury accommodations, it's undoubtedly the Bvlgari Resort Bali.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776583391475-021c17e65dc09e40627bfafe07bbf491.webp)

This resort, personally crafted by the Italian luxury jewelry brand Bulgari, sits atop a limestone cliff at the southern tip of the Bukit Peninsula, 160 meters above sea level. Step out of your villa to be greeted by the endless Indian Ocean; enjoy an aperitivo in the evening as the sunset paints the entire sky orange. Calling it one of Bali's top accommodations would be an understatement.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776583423005-da796fc23397d30a15e6a595c13c48cb.webp)

Just at the end of March, BLACKPINK's Lisa—also Bulgari's global brand ambassador—quietly celebrated her 29th birthday here. Her Instagram post featuring an infinity pool with sunset and expansive ocean views was confirmed by media to be the scenery of Bvlgari Resort Bali. Such celebrity endorsement speaks volumes about the caliber of this place.

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776583518454-eb47f8e0655de85da41eccefdc9575ef.webp)

Bvlgari Resort Bali is located in the Pecatu area, commonly known as Uluwatu. It's about a 20-minute drive from Uluwatu Temple and Jimbaran Beach, approximately 10 km from Balangan Beach, and around 45 minutes from the airport.

This area is the prime location of southern Bali's Bukit Peninsula, surrounded by Bali's best surf spots and cliffside bars. There's a wealth of options within walking or short driving distance:

- **Uluwatu Temple** — Bali's most iconic Hindu temple, the cliffside Kecak dance performance is a must-see
- **Padang Padang Beach** — World-renowned surf spot and filming location for 'Eat, Pray, Love'
- **Balangan Beach** — Relatively quiet, stunning white sand beach
- **Single Fin** — Uluwatu's iconic cliffside bar, bustling during sunset
- **Alila Villas Uluwatu** — Top luxury competitor in the same area, located nearby

{{block:uluwatu}}

This area isn't for shopping or the bustling vibe of Seminyak. But if you're seeking tranquility, spectacular natural scenery, and a high-end experience away from tourist crowds, the Bukit Peninsula is one of Bali's best choices.

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

The resort spans 8.4 hectares and consists of 59 independent villas and mansions, blending Italian style with traditional Balinese architectural elements, surrounded by frangipani trees. Entering the gates feels like stepping into another world—quiet, private, with design touches around every corner.

Must-experience facilities include:

**Serpenti Pool Club**: The main pool was recently redesigned into the snake-themed 'Serpenti Pool Club,' a tribute to Bvlgari's iconic motif, offering a visually stunning experience. The pool overlooks the Indian Ocean, with an infinity edge that's hard to replicate elsewhere in Bali.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776583588168-77182_16030212010040383642.webp)

**Il Ristorante Luca Fantin**: Helmed by an Italian resident chef, this creative Italian fine dining restaurant combines refined cooking techniques with artistic presentation, offering one of the few authentic high-end Italian dining experiences in Bali. The breakfast quality is also widely regarded as one of the most lavish on the island.

**The Bvlgari Bar**: Every evening at sunset, guests can enjoy complimentary Italian aperitivo (appetizers with pre-dinner drinks) while seated by the cliffside watching the sunset. This complimentary experience is actually quite valuable.

## 👥 Ideal For

**Honeymoon couples** will find this place irresistible, with its cliffside views and private pool villas. **Celebrities or business travelers** seeking exclusivity and privacy will also find it perfect. The Mansion room type is a dream option for **large families or corporate groups**, offering privacy comparable to a standalone villa.

If you're a backpacker or a traveler focused on value for money, this isn't for you—starting at around USD$1,300 per night for the entry-level option. But if you're planning to splurge just once in your lifetime, Bvlgari Resort Bali is worth it.
