Indonesia and Bali operate on a **220-volt (220V)** standard, crucial information for travelers planning to bring electrical appliances. Before deciding which devices to bring, ensure your equipment supports the international 220V standard for proper use in Indonesia and Bali.

Besides voltage, socket types are another important consideration. Indonesia uses European-style two-round-pin sockets. Without the appropriate adapter, many hotel sockets may be unusable.

## Do You Need an Adapter?

You can purchase suitable adapters at local supermarkets, known as 'Adaptor' in Indonesian (pronounced: ah-da-pu-tou-er). Alternatively, you can ask your hotel if they provide adapters, though not all hotels do. It's best to buy one yourself at a local supermarket to ensure availability - they're inexpensive, costing around NT$30-40 (about USD$1).

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cee2923f799f1f91f4f50a8eee72a53d.jpg)

Typically, you'll need at least one adapter plus a **220V-compatible** multi-socket extension cord to ensure all your devices can be used in Indonesia and Bali. Remember, when purchasing extension cords, avoid cheap options and choose reliable-looking brands for safer use of your electronics.

## How to Prepare USB Chargers?

Most USB chargers (like phone and tablet chargers) that modern travelers bring abroad already support '100V-240V wide voltage.' As long as you confirm that the input voltage (Input) range marked on the charger includes 220V, you can use it directly in Bali without needing an additional transformer. Before your trip, check the small print on the charger—if it says 'Input: 100-240V,' it’s universally compatible. If it only says 110V, you’ll need to replace it.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a1558c540adf790fc52dc3f5badfe3ae.jpg)

If you’re preparing a multi-port extension cord like the one shown above, first confirm that it supports the 110~220V range. Second, check the plug type—those bought in Taiwan typically have two flat prins, so you’ll also need the adapter mentioned earlier to ensure compatibility in different hotels/villas. Lastly, if your devices support PD or QC fast charging, opt for a high-power charger to significantly reduce charging time and travel anxiety!

## Where to Buy a 220V Multi-Port Extension Cord?

If you’re looking for a 220V multi-port extension cord, it might be hard to find in physical stores, but there are plenty of options online. Many e-commerce platforms and electronics specialty stores offer various 220V multi-port extension cords or cords with multiple USB ports. You can easily find products that meet your needs on these platforms.

## Can I Bring My Own Hair Dryer or Curling Iron?

Most hotels provide hair dryers for guests, though some may not.

It’s recommended to contact your hotel before departure to confirm whether they provide a hair dryer. If they don’t, or if you prefer using a specific brand or model, consider the solutions mentioned earlier, such as purchasing a local 220V hair dryer or using a transformer.

If you bring your own hair dryer, note that those bought in Taiwan typically only support 110V. Using them in Indonesia and Bali can cause issues, as the standard voltage here is 220V. A 110V hair dryer won’t function properly and may even get damaged due to voltage mismatch.

In such cases, the safest and most convenient solution is to purchase a 220V-compatible hair dryer locally. This ensures it works properly in Indonesia and Bali while avoiding damage from voltage mismatch.

If you insist on using your own hair dryer or curling iron, you can also consider using a transformer or converter. A transformer can convert 220V to 110V, but make sure to use one **that provides sufficient wattage (W) and meets safety standards** to avoid damaging your device or posing a safety risk.

For travelers heading to Indonesia and Bali, understanding the voltage and socket information is crucial. **Avoid using cheap chargers of unknown origin, as inferior products can easily overheat at 220V, posing safety risks**. Prepare appropriate adapters and multi-socket extension cords to ensure your devices function properly, allowing you to fully enjoy your vacation without worrying about electrical compatibility issues!
