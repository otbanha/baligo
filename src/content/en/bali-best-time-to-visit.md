When planning a trip to Bali, one of the most frequently asked questions is: "When is the best time to go?" Although Bali's climate is divided into dry season and rainy season, the actual conditions can be more variable than we might imagine.

## **Bali's Seasons**

The dry season typically runs from April to October, during which the weather is sunny and pleasant, making it an excellent time for travel. The rainy season lasts from November to April of the following year, but this is not a hard and fast rule. In this tropical region, weather forecasts often show discrepancies, and sometimes we find ourselves in heavy rain while the weather app still displays sunshine.

## **Surprises of the Rainy Season**

However, even during the rainy season, there’s no need to worry too much. Tropical thunderstorms usually pass quickly during the day and don’t last long. Although the dry season is considered the best time to visit, the rainy season also has its advantages, such as fewer tourists, lower accommodation prices, and cooler weather. When it rains, find a place to shelter and enjoy a cup of coffee—it usually clears up soon!

## **Rainfall in Bali**

Don’t forget that rainfall in Bali varies by region. Although November to March is the wetter period, we’ve visited during this time and only encountered light, brief showers that usually lasted less than an hour. This might be because most of the rainfall is convective thunderstorms, and conditions differ from one area to another.

So, you don’t need to ask "**Is it raining in Bali/Will it rain in Bali?**" anymore! The chance of rain across the entire island is actually quite low; sunny weather in one area doesn’t mean the same for other regions!

## **Whenever You Visit, Be Prepared**

Whenever you choose to visit Bali, always carry rain gear and stay aware of weather changes. Your journey may include some surprising weather fluctuations, but this is part of Bali's charm. We hope you enjoy the unique allure and wonderful moments this beautiful island offers, no matter when you visit!

## Rainy Season Precautions

The rainy season in Bali requires special attention from travelers. Here are eight precautions to keep in mind during Bali's rainy season:

1. **Carry Waterproof Gear**: Include raincoats, umbrellas, and waterproof backpacks to handle sudden showers.
2. **Choose Appropriate Transportation**: Rainy conditions can make roads slippery, so opting for safer transportation like private cars or taxis is better than motorcycles.
3. **Allow Extra Travel Time**: Rain can cause traffic delays or congestion, so planning extra time can help avoid schedule disruptions.
4. **Avoid Beach Areas**: Heavy rain can increase wave intensity and make the water murky, posing risks, so staying away from beaches is a safer choice.
5. **Pay Attention to Local Warnings**: Local governments and tourism agencies often issue warnings about typhoons or floods. Travelers should stay informed and follow local advice.
6. **Avoid Hiking or Trekking**: Mountain trails can become slippery and muddy during the rainy season, increasing danger, so it's best to avoid such activities.
7. **Avoid Visiting Nearby Islands**: Traveling by boat from Bali to nearby islands during the rainy season may lead to significant delays or cancellations due to rough seas, disrupting your plans.
8. **Avoid Rafting**: Pre-booked rafting trips may be canceled by operators due to fast-flowing rivers, posing safety risks.
