When planning a trip to Bali, one of the most frequently asked questions is: "When is the best time to go?" Although Bali's climate is divided into dry and wet seasons, the reality can be more unpredictable than we imagine.

## **Bali's Seasons**

The dry season typically runs from April to October, during which the weather is sunny and pleasant, making it an ideal time for travel. The wet season lasts from November to April, but this is not a strict rule. In this tropical region, weather forecasts often show discrepancies, and sometimes we find ourselves in heavy rain while the forecast on our phones still shows clear skies.

## **Surprises of the Wet Season**

However, even during the wet season, there’s no need to worry too much. Tropical thunderstorms usually pass quickly during the day and don’t last long. Although the dry season is considered the best time to visit, the wet season has its advantages, such as fewer tourists, lower accommodation prices, and cooler weather... etc. When it rains, find a place to shelter and enjoy a cup of coffee—it usually clears up soon!

## **Rainfall in Bali**

Don’t forget that rainfall in Bali varies by region. Although November to March is the wetter period, we’ve visited during this time and only encountered light, brief showers that usually lasted less than an hour. This might be because most of the rainfall is convective thunderstorms, and conditions differ from one area to another.

So, you don’t need to ask anymore, "Is it raining in Bali?" or "Will it rain in Bali?" because the chance of rain across the entire island is actually quite low; sunny weather in one area doesn’t mean the same for other regions!

## **Whenever You Visit, Be Prepared**

No matter when you choose to visit Bali, always carry rain gear and stay mindful of weather changes. Your journey might bring some unexpected weather fluctuations, but that’s part of Bali’s charm. We hope you enjoy the unique allure and wonderful moments this beautiful island has to offer, no matter the season!

## Rainy Season Precautions

The rainy season in Bali requires travelers to be extra cautious. Here are eight things to keep in mind during Bali’s rainy season:

1. **Carry Waterproof Gear**: Include raincoats, umbrellas, and waterproof backpacks to handle sudden downpours.
2. **Choose Appropriate Transportation**: Wet roads can be slippery, so safer options like private cars or taxis are better than motorbikes.
3. **Allow Extra Travel Time**: Rainy weather can cause traffic delays, so plan extra time to avoid disruptions to your schedule.
4. **Avoid Beach Areas**: Heavy rain can lead to rough waves and murky waters, increasing danger. It’s safer to stay away from beaches.
5. **Pay Attention to Local Warnings**: Local governments and tourism agencies often issue warnings about typhoons or floods. Travelers should stay informed and follow local advice.
6. **Avoid Hiking or Trekking**: Mountain trails can become slippery and muddy during the rainy season, increasing risks. It’s best to avoid such activities.
7. **Avoid Visiting Nearby Islands**: During the rainy season, boat trips to nearby islands may face delays or cancellations due to poor sea conditions, disrupting your plans.
8. **Avoid Rafting**: Pre-booked rafting trips may be canceled due to dangerous river currents or safety concerns.
