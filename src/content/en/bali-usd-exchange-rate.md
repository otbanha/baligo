Many travelers in Bali face currency exchange issues, especially on social media where people often ask: Is there a difference in exchange rates between large and small USD bills?

This article will start with an overview of exchange rates, analyze the USD exchange rate situation in Bali, and provide the best currency exchange strategies.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9bb247d0e05bae1d63368331539e85c8.png)

In Bali, currency exchange spots are everywhere, from airports to hotels to city centers. However, exchange rates vary. Therefore, it's best to understand the local exchange rates before exchanging money.

### Common USD Denominations

In Bali, common USD denominations include $100, $50, $20, $10, and $5. Different denominations may have varying exchange rates when converting currency.

### Exchange Rate Differences Between Large and Small USD Bills

Generally, when exchanging USD in Bali, Money Changers categorize USD into three types: large bills ($100, $50) usually have better exchange rates; small bills ($20, $10, $5) have worse rates; and $1 bills have the worst rates.

Here is the exchange rate table for Bali as of May 1, 2024. When exchanging currency, we should look at the 'buying' price. Let's take a look:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b3e9800865e70d4ff80928aed2404b40.png)

As shown in the table, the exchange rate for $100 and $50 bills is the same (15,875), while the cash price difference for $20, $10, and $5 bills is 20 Indonesian Rupiah (15,855). If you have $1 bills, compared to $100/$50 bills, the difference is 275 (15,600), which is the worst exchange rate.

## Currency Exchange Guide

### Does It Really Make a Big Difference?

When exchanging currency, we usually think that carrying $100 and $50 bills will get us the most Indonesian Rupiah. However, if you look at it differently, suppose you bring $1,000 in $20, $10, and $5 bills to exchange for Indonesian Rupiah, compared to $100 and $50 bills, you lose 20 Rupiah (about $0.04) per dollar exchanged. For $1,000, you lose a total of $40...

Now, suppose you bring 1,000 $1 bills to Bali for exchange, compared to larger bills, you lose 275 Rupiah (about $0.55) per dollar exchanged. For $1,000, you lose a total of $550... It seems like an amount you can afford, right!
