Traveling to Indonesia or Bali requires a visa (VISA). There are two methods to apply for a visa. For a comparison of these two methods, please refer to: [Bali Travel Visa Guide: Visa on Arrival vs Electronic Visa, Which is Better for You?](/blog/2023-12-05-656f160bfd89780001e07ea9/)

## There are many benefits to applying for an electronic visa (eVoA) to enter Indonesia/Bali!

Steps to prepare documents for electronic visa application:

1. **Scan or photograph passport page**: Ensure the passport page is clearly visible without glare. Save it as a JPG or PDF file.
2. **Passport photo**: If you don't have a passport photo, take a selfie against a plain wall. Make sure the photo is high resolution and save it as a JPG.
3. **Credit card**: Have your credit card ready for any necessary payments.
4. **Hotel/villa address**: Prepare the address of your accommodation in Bali for filling out the application form.
5. **Flight ticket PDF**: Have your flight ticket PDF ready to confirm your arrival and departure dates.

Visit the official Indonesian Immigration website for application: <https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/>

Now, applying for an electronic visa no longer requires creating an account first, and you can apply for up to 5 people at once. Prepare your documents and start your electronic visa application.

## Troubles encountered when uploading passport photos

Many users have reported difficulties during the passport photo upload step. First, we need to ensure our photos meet the official website requirements:

### Key Points in Example 1:

1. Color photo in JPEG/JPG/PNG format

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8d4f513b24a0c87fae92aee293c19920.jpg)

2. Minimum size of 400x600

3. File size must not exceed **2MB**

4. Proper composition

**Pay special attention to the third point above!** Nowadays, smartphone cameras have increasingly higher resolutions, resulting in larger photo file sizes. If you're not careful, a casual snapshot can easily exceed 2MB. Whether you're reshooting a photo or taking a new one, most will surpass the website's upload limit and fail to pass!

If you're unfamiliar with adjusting your phone's settings or don't know how to reduce file size, here's a handy trick. You can use messaging apps like Line, which almost everyone has, to send the photo to a family member or friend and ask them to send it back. These apps usually compress photos, reducing the original file size to a few hundred KB without losing quality.
