## Exact End Date of the Rainy Season?

According to the latest official news released by the Indonesian Meteorological Agency (BMKG) from March to early April 2026, there is **no nationwide 'exact end date'** (e.g., a specific month or day). Instead, predictions are made in phases based on different 'seasonal zones' (ZOM).

Here’s a summary of the exact timelines mentioned in media reports:

### **Key Timeline for Seasonal Transition**

According to BMKG’s head, Teuku Faisal Fathani, the climatic turning points for 2026 are as follows:

- **End of Rainy Season / Start of Dry Season:**  **April:** Approximately **16.3%** of regions, starting from the eastern **Nusa Tenggara**, will enter the dry season first.
    - **May:** Around **26.3%** of areas, including parts of **Bali** and Java, will transition into the dry season.
    - **June:** The remaining **23.3%** of regions will follow.
- **Peak of Dry Season:** About **61.4%** of Indonesia will reach the driest peak in **August 2026**.

### **Why Isn’t There an Exact Date?**

BMKG points out that the seasonal transition in Indonesia progresses from east to west, resulting in varying dates:

- **Eastern regions (e.g., Lombok, Sumbawa):** The dry season begins as early as early April.
- **Central regions (e.g., Bali, East Java):** The transition is expected to complete between mid-to-late April and early May.
- **Western regions (e.g., Sumatra, West Java):** The dry season may not fully arrive until late May or June.

### **Special Climate Warning for 2026**

News media has highlighted the differences this year compared to previous years:

- **Early Arrival:** Nearly half (46.5%) of the country will experience an **earlier** start to the dry season.
- **Extreme Dryness:** With the weak La Niña phenomenon ending in February and the potential for a **weak El Niño** in July, BMKG predicts this year's dry season will be longer and drier than in 2025.
- **Transition Warning:** Currently, early April is in the **Pancaroba (transition period)**, and while the rainy season is ending, there is still a chance of intense thunderstorms, strong winds, and even hail in the afternoons. BMKG advises the public to remain vigilant for sudden extreme weather during this transition.

If you're planning activities in **Bali** or Java, media reports quoting BMKG suggest that **after mid-April**, the weather will stabilize significantly, with rainfall frequency dropping sharply, marking the start of the ideal season for outdoor activities.

## 📅 2026 Indonesia Seasonal Transition FAQs

#### Q1: When is the 2026 rainy season in Indonesia expected to end?

A: According to Indonesia's Meteorological Agency (BMKG), the end of the rainy season varies by region. About 16.3% of the country will enter the dry season in April, while most regions (around 50%) will complete the transition between May and June. Overall, this year's dry season is arriving earlier than usual.

#### Q2: Which areas will enter the dry season first?

A: The climate transition will begin in eastern Indonesia and progress westward.
First phase (April): Nusa Tenggara Islands (such as Lombok, Komodo).
Second phase (May): Bali, East Java, and Central Java.
Third phase (June): West Java, Sumatra, and Kalimantan.

#### Q3: Will this year's dry season have any special characteristics?

A: This year is expected to be an **"extremely dry" year. With the end of the La Niña phenomenon and the potential emergence of a weak El Niño, BMKG predicts that around 46.5% of regions will experience an earlier start to the dry season**, with rainfall below the annual average.

#### Q4: Is it suitable to visit Java or Bali now (early April)?

A: We are currently in the Pancaroba (transitional season). Although the rainy season is coming to an end, atmospheric conditions remain unstable, with the possibility of brief but intense thunderstorms in the afternoon. It is recommended to schedule outdoor activities in the morning and stay alert for sudden strong winds or heavy rain warnings.

#### Q5: What is the best month for travel in 2026?

A: Mid to late April: Ideal for travelers who enjoy lush green scenery but prefer less rain.

May to September: The most stable weather period across Indonesia, with the lowest chance of rainfall, making it the golden time for visiting Bali's beaches or climbing Java's volcanoes (such as Bromo, Ijen).

#### Q6: What should I be aware of during this travel period?

A: 1. Sun protection and hydration: As the dry season approaches, UV radiation will significantly increase, so be sure to protect yourself from the sun.

2. Temperature Alert: During the dry season, nighttime temperatures in Java's mountainous regions can drop significantly (even approaching 0°C), so hikers should prepare adequate warm clothing.
