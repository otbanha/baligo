## Activity Recommendations & Tips

### Surfing at Kuta Beach

1. One-on-one coaching, including one hour of instruction and one hour of free practice. While it's a bit less refined than lessons in Taiwan, it's still a decent experience. However, Kuta Beach really has quite a lot of trash.
2. To avoid sunburn, we started surfing around 9 AM. At that time, we were the only group, making for a great experience.
3. During surfing, there's no need to worry about your valuables—just leave them with your driver.
4. A staff member helped take photos and videos of us, so we could keep precious memories.
5. Changing clothes and rinsing off are also available (for an additional fee).

### Sunset Beach Horseback Riding

![夕陽海灘騎馬](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6ff1e2744d79be91e88a8775750847dc.png)

1. Booked online, about USD$55 per person, with one-on-one coaching where the instructor leads the horse.
2. The experience was quite good—each instructor used our phones to take photos and videos.
3. However, all activities come with some risk. Near the end, I was suddenly thrown off the horse. Luckily, I kept a firm grip on the reins and have decent athletic ability, so when I flipped over, I was half-thrown and half-jumped off, avoiding serious injury. My instructor kept apologizing, and eventually, a staff member gave me a ride back on a small scooter. When I talked to the business, they said this was the first time it had happened, so they wanted to understand what exactly occurred. But I only remember the horse suddenly started bucking—I felt helpless, and so did the instructor.
4. The scenery here is truly beautiful, and the prices are quite reasonable compared to Taiwan. The only thing to watch out for is safety—all activities carry some risk, so travel insurance is essential.

### Sunrise Jeep Tour

![日出吉普車](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d007f0cd9a6adf529354c1ea176d2bd4.jpg)

Provided by group member Li Ly

1. Booked through KLOOK, about USD$48 per person, including transfers.
2. At 02:50 AM, driver Dita arrived at the hotel lobby on time for pickup. Though he drove fast, he still kept it safe. I left my water in the car on the way back, and he kindly returned it to the hotel.
3. The jeep driver Suda was very friendly, giving advance notice on turns and steep sections, making sure we were holding on tight before moving, and took photos for us seriously.
4. Due to bad weather, we didn't get to see the sunrise. The driver said if the weather were good, we could also see a sky full of stars in the early morning. It was a bit of a shame we missed out, but I'd love to go again!
5. Remember to dress warmly, preferably with a hat, as it gets very cold on the mountain in the early morning.

## About Tipping

When it comes to tipping, we usually go with the flow. We generally tip for luggage assistance and room service. At restaurants without a service charge, we tip if the service is good, and at those that already include a service charge, we still tip if the service is excellent. For spas, regardless of whether a service charge is included, we tip if the experience is comfortable and enjoyable.

We also tip surf instructors, jeep drivers, and transfer drivers. However, for the horseback riding experience, I didn't tip because I fell off the horse.
