## Activity Recommendations & Experiences

### Surfing at Kuta Beach

1. One-on-one coaching, including one hour of instruction and one hour of free practice. Although slightly rougher than teaching in Taiwan, it was still a good experience. However, Kuta Beach does have a significant amount of trash.
2. To avoid sunburn, we scheduled our surfing session to start at 9 AM. At this time, we were the only group, making for a great experience.
3. During surfing, there's no need to worry about important personal items; just hand them over to the car driver.
4. There was someone dedicated to taking our photos and videos, allowing us to keep precious memories.
5. Changing clothes and showering facilities were available (additional payment required).

### Sunset Beach Horseback Riding

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6ff1e2744d79be91e88a8775750847dc.png)

1. Booked online for about USD$55 per person, includes a one-on-one coach who will lead the horse.
2. The experience was quite good, with each coach helping to take photos and videos using our phones.
3. However, all activities carry some risk. Towards the end of the experience, I was suddenly thrown off the horse. Fortunately, I had been holding onto the horse and have some athletic ability, so when I fell, I half-jumped away and wasn't seriously injured. The coach kept apologizing, and their staff took me back on a scooter. After communicating with the vendor, they said this was the first time such an incident had occurred and wanted to understand what happened, but I only remember the horse suddenly starting to buck, and I felt helpless, as did the coach.
4. The scenery here is truly beautiful, and compared to Taiwan, the price is quite reasonable. The only thing to note is safety, as all activities carry some risk, so travel insurance is necessary.

### Sunrise Jeep Tour

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d007f0cd9a6adf529354c1ea176d2bd4.jpg)

Provided by fellow traveler Li Ly

1. Booked through KLOOK for about USD$48 per person, including pickup.
2. At 2:50 AM, driver Dita arrived punctually at the hotel lobby for pickup. Although he drove fast, he maintained safety. I forgot my water in the car on the way back, and he kindly returned it to the hotel.
3. Jeep driver Suda was very friendly, notifying us in advance of turns and steep sections and ensuring we were holding on before proceeding. He also diligently helped take photos.
4. Due to bad weather, we couldn't see the sunrise. The driver mentioned that if the weather were good, we could have seen a sky full of stars in the early morning. It was a bit disappointing, but I would like to go again!
5. Remember to wear more layers and bring a hat, as it gets very cold in the early morning at high altitudes.

## Bali Accommodation Recommendations & Insights

![Inara Alas Harum](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dde839ff80add28fe06b81c2eba394f3.jpg)

Inara Alas Harum

During our stay, we checked into three places, and one of them, [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751), left a lasting impression on us. Although it’s nestled in the jungle and not in a bustling area (but Arie ensures safe transportation), the staff were incredibly friendly. Any requests could be made via WhatsApp, and even with our limited English, communication was smooth as they patiently translated for us. Despite being in the jungle, the environment was impeccably clean—out of the three places we stayed, this was the cleanest, and we didn’t encounter any bugs.

On the third day, we stayed at an older villa in Jimbaran, but it turned out to be a nightmare. The air conditioning was noisy and ineffective, the water pressure was weak, and there were millipedes both outside and inside the room. Fortunately, they offered complimentary breakfast from 7:00 AM to 12:00 PM with a variety of options, and we tried several dishes that were delicious. Additionally, they provided free afternoon tea from 4:00 PM to 5:00 PM, which could be enjoyed in the room and was equally delightful (the spring rolls were unforgettable). The dinner (at our own expense) was also tasty, though we didn’t indulge in any drinks.

Swimming in the jungle pool at [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) was incredibly refreshing, and there was even a hot tub, though unfortunately, they didn’t offer spa services. Overall, we’d definitely stay at [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) again on our next trip to Bali. With rates under USD$50 per night, we felt a true sense of vacation.

## About Tipping

Regarding tipping, we usually give based on our mood. Generally, we tip for assistance with luggage and room service. At restaurants without a service charge, we tip if the service is good, and even at restaurants with a service charge, we tip if the service is exceptional. For spa treatments, regardless of whether a service charge is included, we tip if the experience is comfortable and enjoyable.

Additionally, we tip surf instructors, jeep drivers, and transfer drivers. However, for the horseback riding experience, I didn’t tip because I fell off the horse.
