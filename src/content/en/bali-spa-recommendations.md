## Activity Recommendations & Experiences

### Surfing at Kuta Beach

1. One-on-one coach, including one hour of instruction and one hour of free practice. Although a bit rougher compared to teaching in Taiwan, it's still a good experience. However, Kuta Beach has a significant amount of trash.
2. To avoid sunburn, we scheduled our surfing session to start at 9 AM. At this time, we were the only group, making for a great experience.
3. During surfing, there's no need to worry about important personal items; just hand them over to the car rental driver.
4. There's a dedicated person to help take photos and videos of us, allowing us to keep precious memories.
5. Changing clothes and rinsing off are also provided (additional fees apply).

### Sunset Beach Horseback Riding

![Sunset Beach Horseback Riding](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6ff1e2744d79be91e88a8775750847dc.png)

1. Booked online, costing around USD$55 per person, includes a one-on-one instructor who leads the horse.
2. The experience was quite enjoyable, with each instructor helping to take photos and videos using our phones.
3. However, all activities carry some risk. Towards the end of the experience, I was suddenly thrown off the horse. Fortunately, I had been holding onto the horse and had some athletic ability, so when I fell, I half-jumped away and wasn't seriously injured. My instructor kept apologizing, and their staff took me back on a scooter. After communicating with the provider, they said this was the first time such an incident had occurred and wanted to understand what happened. All I remember is the horse suddenly started bucking, and I felt helpless, as did the instructor.
4. The scenery here is truly beautiful, and compared to Taiwan, the price is quite reasonable. The only thing to note is safety, as all activities carry some risk, so travel insurance is essential.

### Sunrise Jeep Tour

![Sunrise Jeep Tour](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d007f0cd9a6adf529354c1ea176d2bd4.jpg)

Provided by fellow traveler Li Ly

1. Booked through KLOOK, costing around USD$48 per person, including transportation.
2. At 2:50 AM, driver Dita arrived punctually at the hotel lobby for pickup. Although he drove fast, he maintained safety. I forgot my water in the car on the way back, and he specially returned it to the hotel.
3. Jeep driver Suda was very friendly, notifying us in advance of turns and steep sections and ensuring we were holding on securely before proceeding. He also diligently helped take photos.
4. Due to bad weather, we couldn't see the sunrise. The driver mentioned that if the weather were good, we could have seen a sky full of stars in the early morning. It was a bit disappointing, but I would love to go again!
5. Remember to dress warmly and bring a hat, as it gets very cold in the early morning at high altitudes.

## Bali Accommodation Recommendations & Insights

![Inara Alas Harum](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dde839ff80add28fe06b81c2eba394f3.jpg)

Inara Alas Harum

During our stay, we checked into three places, and one of them, [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751), left a lasting impression on us. Although it's located in the jungle and not in a bustling area (but Arie ensures safe transportation), the staff were incredibly friendly. Any requests could be made via WhatsApp, and even with our limited English, there were no issues as they patiently translated for us. Despite being in the jungle, the environment was spotless and tidy—out of the three places we stayed, this was the cleanest, with no bugs in sight.

On the third day, we stayed at a well-established villa in Jimbaran, but it turned out to be a nightmare. The air conditioner was noisy and ineffective, the water pressure was low, and there were millipedes everywhere, both outside and inside the room. Fortunately, they offered a complimentary breakfast from 7:00 to 12:00 with a variety of options, and we tried several dishes that were all delicious. Additionally, they provided a free afternoon tea from 16:00 to 17:00, which could be enjoyed in the room and was equally delightful (especially the spring rolls). The dinner (at our own expense) was also tasty, though we didn't drink.

Swimming in the jungle at [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) was incredibly refreshing, and there was also a hot tub, though unfortunately, no spa services were available. Overall, we would definitely stay at [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) again on our next trip to Bali. With rates under USD$50 per night, we felt fully immersed in a vacation vibe.

## About Tipping

Regarding tipping, we usually give based on our mood. Generally, we tip for assistance with luggage and room service. At restaurants without a service charge, we tip if the service is good, and even at places with a service charge, we still tip if the service is excellent. For spa treatments, regardless of whether a service charge is included, we tip if the experience is comfortable and enjoyable.

Additionally, we also tip surf instructors, jeep drivers, and transfer drivers. However, for the horseback riding experience, I didn't tip because I fell off the horse.
