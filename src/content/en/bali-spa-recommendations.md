## Activity Recommendations & Tips

### Surfing at Kuta Beach

1. One-on-one coaching, including one hour of instruction and one hour of free practice. Though a bit rough compared to lessons in Taiwan, it's still a decent experience. However, Kuta Beach really has quite a lot of trash.
2. To avoid sunburn, we started surfing around 9 AM. At that time, we were the only group, making for a great experience.
3. During surfing, no need to worry about your valuables—just leave them with the driver.
4. A dedicated person took photos and videos of us, helping us capture precious memories.
5. Changing clothes and rinsing off are also available (for an additional fee).

### Sunset Beach Horseback Riding

![夕陽海灘騎馬](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6ff1e2744d79be91e88a8775750847dc.png)

1. Booked online, about USD$55 per person, with one-on-one coaching where the instructor leads the horse.
2. The experience was quite enjoyable—each instructor used our phones to take photos and videos.
3. However, all activities come with some risk. Near the end, I was suddenly thrown off the horse. Luckily, I kept a firm grip on the reins and have some athletic ability, so when I flipped over, I was half-thrown and half-jumped off, avoiding serious injury. My instructor kept apologizing, and eventually, a staff member gave me a ride back on a small scooter. When I talked to the business, they said this was the first time it happened, so they wanted to understand what went wrong. But I only remember the horse suddenly bucking—I felt helpless, and so did the instructor.
4. The scenery here is truly beautiful, and the prices are quite affordable compared to Taiwan. The only thing to watch out for is safety—all activities carry some risk, so travel insurance is a must.

### Sunrise Jeep Tour

![日出吉普車](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d007f0cd9a6adf529354c1ea176d2bd4.jpg)

Photo courtesy of group member Li Ly

1. Booked via KLOOK, about USD$48 per person including transfers.
2. At 02:50 AM, driver Dita arrived promptly at the hotel lobby for pickup. Though he drove fast, he still kept it safe. I left my water in the car on the way back, and he kindly returned it to the hotel.
3. Jeep driver Suda was very friendly, giving advance notice before turns and steep sections, making sure we were holding on tight before moving, and took photos for us seriously.
4. Due to bad weather, we didn't see the sunrise. The driver said on clear mornings, you can also see a sky full of stars before dawn. It was a pity we missed out this time, but I'd love to go again!
5. Remember to dress warmly and bring a hat, as it gets very cold on the mountain in the early hours.

## Bali Accommodation Recommendations & Insights

![Inara Alas Harum](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dde839ff80add28fe06b81c2eba394f3.jpg)

Inara Alas Harum

We stayed at three places this time, and one of them, [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) [Trip](https://hk.trip.com/hotels/bali-hotel-detail-109668691/inara-alas-harum/?Allianceid=6817581&SID=311634184&trip_sub1=&trip_sub3=D18793707) [Booking](https://booking.tpm.li/AUV4lN4h), left a lasting impression. Although it's nestled in the jungle, away from the bustling areas (but Arie will ensure you get there safely), the staff are incredibly friendly. Any request can be made via WhatsApp, and even with our limited English, there was no issue—they patiently translated everything. Despite being in the jungle, the environment was spotless; among the three places we stayed, this was the cleanest, with no bugs at all.

On the third day, we stayed at an older villa in Jimbaran, but it was a nightmare. The air conditioning was noisy and not cold, the water pressure was weak, and there were many millipedes both outside and on the room floor. On the bright side, they offered a free breakfast from 07:00 to 12:00 with a wide selection—we tried several dishes and they were all delicious. They also provided a free afternoon tea from 16:00 to 17:00, which could be ordered to the room and was equally tasty (the spring rolls left us wanting more). The dinner (at our own expense) was also excellent, though we didn't have any drinks.

Swimming in the jungle at [Inara Alas Harum](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=41581751) [Trip](https://hk.trip.com/hotels/bali-hotel-detail-109668691/inara-alas-harum/?Allianceid=6817581&SID=311634184&trip_sub1=&trip_sub3=D18793707) [Booking](https://booking.tpm.li/AUV4lN4h) was incredibly refreshing, and there was also a hot tub, though unfortunately no spa services were available. ## About Tipping

When it comes to tipping, we usually go by how we feel. Generally, we tip for luggage assistance and room service. For restaurants without a service charge, we tip if the service is good, and for those that already include a service charge, we also tip if the service is excellent. As for spas, regardless of whether a service charge is included, we tip if the experience is comfortable and enjoyable.

Additionally, we also tip surf instructors, jeep drivers, and transfer drivers. However, for the horseback riding experience, I didn't tip because I fell off the horse.

## Driver Recommendation

![團友 Li Ly 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8ea91cd233adf18da19de12f41184e3c.jpg)

Provided by group member Li Ly

For transportation, we hired Arie for the entire 7-day, 6-night trip (except for 2/28 when Arie needed to go home to help with a festival). We highly recommend him. Here are his strengths:

1. Fluent in English (we prefer drivers who don't understand Chinese, as it makes chatting more relaxed).
2. Very thoughtful and polite (he helps with umbrellas when it rains, opens doors, handles luggage, etc.).
3. The car is new, clean, and smells pleasant (he washes the car inside and out every morning, showing his professionalism).
4. Acts like a personal assistant (the day before departure, while in Taiwan, we tried to book Double-Six Rooftop via WhatsApp but got no response; after asking Arie, he immediately helped us secure the reservation and called to book various spas).
5. Offers itinerary suggestions but fully follows guests' preferences.
6. Makes you feel at ease and safe.
7. Drives smoothly.
8. Recommends delicious local street food (even for someone like me with a sensitive stomach, I had no issues after the second day).
9. Transparent and reasonable pricing (the service was so good that we gave 20-30% tips daily, yet it still felt like great value).
10. He engages warmly in conversation but doesn't disturb when it's not needed (when he saw other friends sleeping, he proactively turned off the car music).
11. Basically, if you have any needs or curiosity, just ask him—he almost always delivers!

We visited Bali six years ago (an eight-person small group organized by a travel agency), but the experience was poor, mainly due to the driver-guide. Although he spoke Chinese, communication was nearly impossible, which made us realize how crucial a good driver is for a trip—they can make the journey more complete and relaxing. Because Arie was so attentive, we gave him 20-30% tips every day.
