## Activity Recommendations & Experiences

### Surfing at Kuta Beach

1. One-on-one coaching, including one hour of instruction and one hour of free practice. Although a bit rougher compared to teaching in Taiwan, it was still a good experience. However, the amount of trash at Kuta Beach was quite significant.
2. To avoid sunburn, we scheduled our surfing session to start at 9 am. At this time, we were the only group, making the experience quite nice.
3. During surfing, there was no need to worry about important personal belongings as we could leave them with our private driver.
4. There was a dedicated person to help take photos and videos of us, allowing us to keep precious memories.
5. Changing clothes and showering facilities were also provided (additional fees required).

### Sunset Beach Horseback Riding

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6ff1e2744d79be91e88a8775750847dc.png)

1. Booked online for about USD$55 per person, includes a one-on-one instructor who leads the horse.
2. The experience was quite enjoyable, with each instructor helping to take photos and videos using our phones.
3. However, all activities carry some risk. Towards the end of the experience, I was suddenly thrown off the horse. Fortunately, I had been holding onto the horse tightly and have some athletic ability, so when I fell, I half-jumped and half-was thrown, minimizing injury. My instructor kept apologizing, and their staff eventually took me back on a scooter. After discussing with the operator, they said this was the first time such an incident had occurred and wanted to understand what happened, but I only remember the horse suddenly starting to buck, leaving me and the instructor both feeling helpless.
4. The scenery here is truly beautiful, and compared to Taiwan, the price is very reasonable. The only thing to note is safety, as all activities inherently carry some risk, so travel insurance is essential.

### Sunrise Jeep Tour

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d007f0cd9a6adf529354c1ea176d2bd4.jpg)

Contributed by fellow traveler Li Ly

1. Booked through KLOOK for about USD$48 per person, including pickup and drop-off.
2. At 2:50 AM, driver Dita arrived punctually at the hotel lobby for pickup. Although he drove fast, he maintained safety. I forgot my water bottle in the car on the return trip, and he kindly brought it back to the hotel.
3. Jeep driver Suda was very friendly, notifying us before sharp turns and steep sections, ensuring we were holding on securely before proceeding, and diligently helping with photos.
4. Due to bad weather, we couldn’t see the sunrise. The driver mentioned that on clear mornings, you can also enjoy a sky full of stars. It was a bit disappointing to miss out this time, but I’d love to try again!
5. Remember to dress warmly and bring a hat, as it gets very cold in the early morning at high altitudes.

## Bali Accommodation Recommendations & Insights

![Inara Alas Harum](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dde839ff80add28fe06b81c2eba394f3.jpg)

Inara Alas Harum

During our stay, we tried three different accommodations, and one that left a lasting impression was Inara Alas Harum. Although it's nestled in the jungle and not in a bustling area (but Arie ensures safe transportation), the staff were exceptionally friendly. Any requests could be made via WhatsApp, and even with our limited English, communication wasn't an issue as they patiently translated. Despite being in the jungle, the environment was impeccably clean and tidy—it was the cleanest of the three places we stayed, with no bugs in sight.

On the third day, we stayed at a well-established villa in Jimbaran, but it turned out to be a nightmare. The air conditioning was noisy and ineffective, the water pressure was low, and there were many millipedes both outside and inside the room. Fortunately, they offered a complimentary breakfast from 07:00 to 12:00 with a variety of options, and we tried several dishes that were delicious. Additionally, they provided a free afternoon tea from 16:00 to 17:00, which could be enjoyed in the room and was equally delightful (especially the spring rolls). The dinner (at our own expense) was also tasty, though we didn't drink.

Swimming in the jungle pool at Inara Alas Harum was incredibly refreshing, and there was also a hot tub, though unfortunately, they didn't offer spa services. Overall, we would definitely consider staying at Inara Alas Harum again on our next trip to Bali. With nightly rates under USD$50, we felt a strong sense of vacation bliss.

## About Tipping

Regarding tipping, we usually give based on our mood. Essentially, we tip for luggage assistance and room service. At restaurants without a service charge, we tip if the service is good, and at those with a service charge, we also tip if the service is excellent. For spa services, regardless of whether a service charge is included, we tip if the experience is comfortable and enjoyable.

Additionally, we tip surf instructors, jeep drivers, and transfer drivers. However, for the horseback riding experience, I didn't tip because I fell off the horse.

## Driver Recommendation

![團友 Li Ly 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8ea91cd233adf18da19de12f41184e3c.jpg)

Provided by group member Li Ly

For transportation during our 7-day, 6-night trip (except on February 28th when Arie needed to go home to assist with a festival), we had Arie as our private driver, and we highly recommend him. Here are his strengths:

1. Fluent in English (we prefer drivers who don’t understand Chinese, as it makes conversations more relaxed).
2. Extremely thoughtful and polite (he would help with umbrellas, opening doors, and carrying luggage when it rained).
3. The car is new, clean, and smells pleasant (he cleans the car inside and out every morning, showcasing his professionalism).
4. Acts like a personal assistant (the day before departure, we wanted to book Double-Six Rooftop from Taiwan, but the restaurant didn’t respond via WhatsApp. After asking Arie, he immediately helped with reservations and even called to book various SPAs).
5. Offers itinerary suggestions but fully respects the guests’ preferences.
6. Makes you feel secure and safe.
7. Drives smoothly.
8. Recommends delicious local snacks (even for someone like me with a sensitive stomach, I had no issues the next day).
9. Transparent and reasonable pricing (the service was so good that we tipped 20-30% daily, yet it still felt like great value).
10. Engages in friendly conversation but doesn’t intrude when unnecessary (when he noticed other friends sleeping, he proactively turned off the car music).
11. Basically, if you have any needs or curiosities, just ask him—he’s almost always able to deliver!

Six years ago, we visited Bali on a small group tour organized by a travel agency, but the experience wasn’t great, especially due to the driver-guide. Although he spoke Chinese, communication was nearly impossible, which made us deeply appreciate the importance of a good driver in making a trip complete and relaxing. Because Arie was so thoughtful, we tipped him 20-30% daily.
