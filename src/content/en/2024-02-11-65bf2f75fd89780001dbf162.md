## The History of Sanur

Sanur is a historic coastal town on Bali, with a history stretching back hundreds of years. In Bali's history, Sanur was once an important port and fishing village, attracting ships from the Indonesian archipelago and other countries for trade. This made Sanur a center for commercial and cultural exchange on Bali.

Following Indonesia's independence in the mid-20th century and the start of national development, Bali began to flourish during the era of Indonesia's founding father Sukarno, with Sanur marking the beginning of Bali's tourism industry! The island's first five-star hotel was established in Sanur.

Today, Sanur, with its naturally protected reefs, calm waters, and gently sloping golden beaches, has developed into a family vacation paradise. Many foreigners from around the world choose this quiet, unpretentious town for retirement.

### Water Activities

In Sanur, the number one activity is water sports! Canoeing and Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP) are two of the most popular water activities here. You can enjoy the serene ocean atmosphere on calm waters or challenge your balance and strength. This beautiful sea will bring you both fun and adventure, ensuring a delightful time.

Video of Jay canoeing

Both canoes and paddleboards can be rented from vendors along the beach, with many concentrated around Pantai Karang. This area also has numerous local vendors selling swimwear, floating devices, water guns, beach toys, and sand tools (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). In 2023, the rental price for both canoes and paddleboards was 25K IDR per hour, approximately USD$2.

### Beach Walk

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d124800f990b3fd545451b4c77015a4f.jpg)

When it comes to Sanur, one cannot overlook its expansive beach walk stretching over four kilometers! This lengthy coastal path is one of Sanur's most distinctive features, perfect for strolling, jogging, or renting a bike to leisurely ride along the flat path with your children. The beach on one side of the walkway, with its varied length and scenery, ensures you'll find a stretch of sand all to yourself, complete with large trees and lovely shaded spots, free from smoking tourists! ### Museum Le Mayeur （📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a7eadec18bd85e377d455169550dbd25.jpg)

The Museum Le Mayeur is located in the former home of Belgian painter Adrien-Jean Le Mayeur and his Balinese wife Ni Pollok. The story of this couple fills the place with a romantic and historical atmosphere. Situated at the northern end of Sanur Beach, this charming small museum showcases many of Le Mayeur's paintings, vividly capturing Bali's landscape and way of life before mass tourism took hold. Although the museum's maintenance has been a topic of debate in recent years, it remains a captivating spot for visitors who appreciate Balinese art and culture. Walking through this historic home, you can not only admire Le Mayeur's deep affection for Bali and his artistic interpretations but also feel the passage of time in Sanur.

### Deep-Sea Fishing

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e5a751129b55192d84a6e6780bfca72e.jpg)

This fishing adventure departs from Sanur, offering an exciting deep-sea fishing experience. You’ll have the chance to catch a variety of prized fish, including grouper, snapper, and colorful tropical species.

Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced angler, the activity provides fishing gear, bait, and expert guides to ensure you have a memorable time.
