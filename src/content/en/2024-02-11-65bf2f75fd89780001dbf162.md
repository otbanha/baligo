## The History of Sanur

Sanur is a historic coastal town on Bali, with roots dating back hundreds of years. In Bali's history, Sanur was once a significant port and fishing village, attracting ships from across the Indonesian archipelago and beyond for trade. Over time, it became one of Bali's hubs for commerce and cultural exchange.

Following Indonesia's independence in the mid-20th century and the start of national development, Bali began to flourish during the era of Indonesia's founding father, Sukarno. Sanur marked the beginning of Bali's tourism industry, hosting the island's first five-star hotel.

Today, Sanur boasts calm, gently sloping golden beaches protected by natural reefs, making it a family-friendly vacation paradise. Many expatriates from around the world choose this quiet, unpretentious town for retirement.

### Water Activities

In Sanur, the number one activity is water sports! Canoeing and Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP) are two of the most popular water activities here. You can enjoy the serene ocean atmosphere on calm waters or challenge your balance and strength. This beautiful sea will bring you both fun and challenges, ensuring a delightful time.

Xiao Jie’s canoeing video

Canoes and paddleboards can be rented from vendors along the beach, with many concentrated around Pantai Karang. This area is also bustling with local vendors selling swimwear, flotation devices, water guns, beach toys, and sand tools (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). In 2023, the rental cost for both canoes and paddleboards is 25K per hour, approximately USD$1.

### Beachfront Promenade

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d124800f990b3fd545451b4c77015a4f.jpg)

When it comes to Sanur, one cannot overlook its over 4-kilometer-long beachfront promenade! This extensive coastal walkway is one of Sanur's most distinctive features. You can stroll along the shops lining the path, go for a jog, or rent a bicycle to leisurely ride with your children on the flat trail. The beach on one side of the promenade, with its varying lengths and changes along the way, ensures you'll find a stretch of sand to call your own, complete with large trees and lovely shaded spots—and no smoking tourists nearby! You can lay down a mat and relax in your private area.

Strolling through this former residence, you can not only admire Le Mayeur's deep affection and artistic interpretation of Bali but also feel the historical moments that belong to Sanur.
