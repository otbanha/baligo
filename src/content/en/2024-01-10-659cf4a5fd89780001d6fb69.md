Ubud is famous for its cultural depth and natural beauty, which greatly appeals to adult travelers. However, for younger children, visiting historical sites or simply admiring the scenery might not be as engaging.

This guide specially curates the most family-friendly spots and activities in Ubud, ensuring kids have fun while parents also enjoy their vacation. From children's theme parks to DIY craft workshops, there's plenty to keep the little ones entertained. Meanwhile, attractions like Ubud Palace and Monkey Forest offer special experiences for the whole family. Let's explore what Ubud has to offer for families:

## Family Activities

Best activities for families in Ubud:

### Alas Harum （👉 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 1 and up

Near the famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces in Ubud lies a family-friendly destination called Alas Harum. Located about a 20-minute drive from Ubud's city center, it's best known for its stunning rice terrace views.

Beyond the iconic terraces, Alas Harum offers outdoor activities perfect for families, such as flying fox, swings, and the latest sky biking. Kids can enjoy thrilling adventures while parents relax in the serene surroundings, making for memorable family moments.

Alas Harum is not just an adventure park; it also carries a rich cultural heritage. Surrounding the park are numerous temples, statues, and historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal place to explore Ubud, combining stunning scenery, thrilling activities, and rich cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market (👉🏼 Map: [Central Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 0 and above

At the Ubud Market, children can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. The market is filled with a variety of handicrafts, from intricate carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural charm. Colorful fabrics are woven into various patterns and prints, each reflecting the exquisite craftsmanship and rich historical heritage of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove, where children can observe, smell, and even experience Bali's unique art and craft techniques firsthand. They can witness the finesse of ceramic art, feel the delicacy of wood carving, and learn how local residents preserve and pass down Bali's cultural traditions through weaving and handicrafts.

At this market, children can not only purchase unique souvenirs but also appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art through interactions with local artisans. Such experiences will fill them with curiosity about the world and enrich their educational journey.

### Ubud Skywalk (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Consider carefully if you're bringing preschoolers—it can be tiring if they need to be carried!

Suitable for ages: 8 and above

Just a short walk from Ubud Palace lies the Campuhan Ridge Walk, known as the 'Skywalk of Ubud.' Easily accessible from the palace, this hidden gem near the heart of Ubud offers tranquility and stunning natural scenery for you and your family. Situated along a ridge, the trail provides breathtaking 360-degree views, allowing you to escape the hustle and bustle of the city.

This is a perfect family-friendly destination where both children and parents can immerse themselves in the beauty of nature. As you stroll along the path, you'll be surrounded by serene landscapes, free from traffic noise, with only the sounds of nature and picturesque views awaiting you.

### Bali's New Monkey Forest: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉 Map: [Popular Spots Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River immerses you in Bali's pristine natural beauty! The crystal-clear Petanu River winds through the forest, while artificial waterfalls add a refreshing ambiance.

Here, you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying fox bats, all while relaxing in traditional Balinese gazebos, listening to the soothing sounds of flowing water and embracing nature's tranquility.

Now open for visits! Hours: Daily 9 AM - 6 PM (last entry at 5 PM) Special price: Adults $5, Children $3

Escape the crowds and experience the unique melukat water purification ritual, immersing yourself in Bali's spirituality and natural beauty!

Travel tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, choose this spot—the monkeys here are gentler and won't attack. In Ubud, the monkeys are more aggressive and may snatch items.

### Kid-Friendly River Tubing

Suitable for ages: 4 and up

While rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it carries certain risks that depend on river conditions. For a safer water adventure, cave tubing/river tubing (as shown in the video above) offers a more secure and family-friendly alternative.

It's worth noting that many caves in the Ubud area date back to Japan's brief occupation of Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces constructed various defensive structures on the island, including bunkers, caves, and tunnels. These underground spaces were later repurposed by local residents or government agencies, some transformed into tourist attractions while others were adapted for irrigation purposes. These caves stand as witnesses to historical periods and have become integral parts of Bali's history and culture.

Whether rafting or cave tubing, both Ubud water activities offer unforgettable experiences for adventurers. They allow you to feel the thrill of river challenges while enjoying nature's tranquility and vitality. These activities enable interaction with both nature and history, enriching your journey with profound experiences and lasting memories.

## Tips for Family Travel in Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some considerations for family travel in Ubud:

- **_Book family-friendly hotels or resorts_** Ubud offers many accommodations, with plenty designed specifically for families. These often include childcare services, kids' clubs, and swimming pools.
- **_Consider hiring a private car_** A private car is a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings at your own pace, stopping for photos and attractions along the way.
- **_Be mindful of sun protection_** Bali's sun is intense, so bring sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, and protective clothing, and reapply sunscreen frequently to prevent sunburn.
- **_Pay attention to food hygiene_** Children have weaker immune systems, so avoid letting them drink water of uncertain quality or eat food from street vendors.
- **_Be prepared for weather changes_** Bali's weather can be unpredictable, with sudden rain showers, so carry rain gear and pack light jackets for the kids.

These are some basic precautions to keep in mind. We hope you and your children have a fun and safe time exploring Ubud, Bali!
