Ubud is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and natural landscapes, making it highly attractive to adult travelers. However, for younger children, visiting historical sites or admiring scenery can indeed be somewhat dull.

This guide specially curates the most family-friendly spots and activities in Ubud, ensuring kids have a blast while parents also enjoy their vacation. From children's theme parks to DIY craft workshops, there's plenty to keep the little ones engaged; while places like Ubud Palace and Monkey Forest offer unique family experiences. Let's explore what Ubud has to offer for families:

## Family Activities

Top family-friendly activities in Ubud:

### Alas Harum （👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 1+

Near Ubud's famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces lies a family-friendly destination called 'Alas Harum'. Located about 20 minutes from Ubud's city center, it's best known for its stunning rice terrace views.

Not only does it offer the famous terrace landscapes, but also outdoor activities perfect for families, such as flying fox, swings, and the latest sky biking. Kids can enjoy thrilling adventures here while parents relax in the serene environment, creating wonderful family memories together.

Alas Harum is not just an adventure park; it also carries a rich cultural heritage. The surrounding area is dotted with temples, statues, and other historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal place to explore Ubud, combining stunning scenery, thrilling activities, and rich cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market （👉🏼 Map: [Central Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 0 and up

In the Ubud Market, children can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. The market is filled with a variety of handicrafts, from exquisite carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural charm. Colorful fabrics are woven into various patterns and prints, each exuding the craftsmanship and rich historical heritage of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove, where children can observe, smell, and even experience Bali's unique art and craft techniques firsthand. They can marvel at the intricacies of ceramic art, feel the delicacy of wood carving, and learn how local residents preserve and pass down Bali's cultural traditions through weaving and handicrafts.

In this market, children can not only purchase unique souvenirs but also appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art through interactions with local artisans. Such experiences will fill them with curiosity about the world and enrich their educational journey.

### Bali’s New Monkey Forest: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River immerses you in Bali's pristine natural beauty! The crystal-clear Petanu River winds through the forest, while man-made waterfalls add a refreshing ambiance to the environment.

Here, you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying fox bats, all while relaxing in traditional Balinese pavilions, listening to the flowing water, and soaking in nature's tranquility.

Now open for visits! Hours: Daily 9 AM - 6 PM (last entry at 5 PM) Special price: Adults $5, Children $2.50

Escape the crowds and experience the unique melukat water purification ritual, embracing Bali's spirituality and natural beauty!

Friendly tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, this is a great spot as the monkeys here are gentler and won't attack. Monkeys in Ubud tend to be more aggressive and may snatch items.

### Kid-Friendly River Tubing

https://youtube.com/shorts/Z_vDb7AbMKg

Suitable for ages: 4 and up

While white-water rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it carries certain risks and safety depends heavily on river conditions. For a safer water activity experience, cave tubing or river tubing (as shown in the video above) is a more family-friendly option.

It's worth noting that many of the caves in the Ubud area date back to the brief period of Japanese occupation in Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces constructed various military installations on the island, including bunkers, caves, and tunnels. These structures were later repurposed or modified by local residents or government agencies. Some have been turned into tourist attractions, while others serve irrigation purposes. These caves stand as witnesses to a historical period and have become part of Bali's cultural heritage.

Whether it's rafting or cave tubing, both water activities in Ubud offer unforgettable experiences for adventurers. They allow you to feel the thrill of challenge in the river while enjoying the tranquility and vitality bestowed by nature. These activities enable you to interact with both nature and history, adding rich experiences and lasting memories to your journey.

## Tips for Family Travel in Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some considerations for family travel in Ubud:

- **Book Family-Friendly Hotels or Resorts** Ubud offers many hotels and resorts, many of which are designed for families. These often include childcare services, kids' clubs, and swimming pools.
- **Consider Hiring a Private Car** Hiring a car is a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings at your own pace, stopping for photos and attractions along the way.
- **Sun Protection** Bali's sun is intense. Bring sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, and protective clothing, and reapply sunscreen frequently to avoid sunburn.
- **Food Hygiene** Children have weaker immune systems, so avoid letting them drink water of uncertain quality or eat food from street vendors.
- **Weather Changes** Bali's weather can be unpredictable, with sudden rain showers. Always carry rain gear and prepare light jackets for your children.

These are some basic precautions to keep in mind. We hope both you and your children can enjoy a fun and safe time in Ubud, Bali!
