## **Bali's Seasons**

The dry season typically runs from April to October, a period when the sun shines brightly and the weather is pleasant, making it an excellent time for travel. The rainy season lasts from November to April, but this is not a hard and fast rule. In this tropical region, weather forecasts often have discrepancies, and sometimes, even when you're in the midst of heavy rain, your phone's weather app might still show sunny skies.

## **Surprises of the Rainy Season**

However, even during the rainy season, there's no need to worry too much. Tropical thunderstorms usually pass quickly within the day and don't last long. Although the dry season is hailed as the best time, the rainy season also has its advantages, such as fewer tourists, usually lower accommodation prices, and cooler weather...etc. When it rains, find a place to take shelter, enjoy a cup of coffee, and usually, the rain will clear up soon!

## **Rainfall in Bali**

Don't forget, rainfall in Bali varies by region. Although the period from November to March tends to be wetter, we've visited during this time and only encountered light, brief showers that usually lasted less than an hour. This is likely due to the convective nature of the thunderstorms and the differing conditions across various areas.

So, you don't need to keep asking 'Is it raining in Bali/Will it rain in Bali?' because the chance of rain across the entire island is actually quite low; sunny weather in one area doesn't guarantee the same in others!

## **Be Prepared Whenever You Visit**

Whenever you choose to visit Bali, always carry rain gear and stay alert to weather changes. You might encounter some surprising weather fluctuations during your journey, but this is part of Bali's charm. We hope you enjoy the unique allure and wonderful moments that Bali offers at any time!

## Precautions During the Rainy Season

The rainy season in Bali is a time when travelers need to be particularly cautious. Here are eight precautions for the Bali rainy season:

1. **Carry waterproof gear**: Including raincoats, umbrellas, and waterproof backpacks to handle sudden showers.
2. **Choose appropriate transportation**: Wet and slippery roads during the rainy season make safer options like private cars or taxis more suitable than motorcycles.
3. **Allow extra travel time**: Rainy conditions can cause traffic jams or delays, so allowing extra time can prevent disruptions to your schedule.
4. **Avoid beach areas**: Heavy rain can increase wave size and make the sea murky, posing dangers, so it's safer to avoid beaches.
5. **Heed local warnings**: Local governments and tourism organizations often provide warnings about typhoons or floods; travelers should pay close attention and follow local advice.
6. **Avoid hiking or trekking**: Mountainous areas can become slippery and muddy during the rainy season, increasing risks, so it's best to avoid such activities.
7. **Avoid trips to outer islands**: During the rainy season, boat trips from Bali to outer islands may face delays or cancellations due to poor sea conditions, significantly affecting planned itineraries.
8. **Avoid rafting**: Scheduled rafting trips may be dangerous due to fast-flowing rivers or canceled by operators for safety reasons.
