When planning a trip to Bali, one of the most frequently asked questions is: 'When is the best time to go?' While Bali's climate is divided into dry season and rainy season, the actual conditions can be more variable than we might imagine.

## **Bali's Seasons**

The dry season typically runs from April to October, during which the sun shines brightly and the weather is pleasant, making it an excellent time for travel. The rainy season lasts from November to April of the following year, but this is not a hard and fast rule. In this tropical region, weather forecasts often show discrepancies, and sometimes we find ourselves in heavy rain even when the forecast on our phones shows sunny skies.

## **Surprises of the Rainy Season**

However, even during the rainy season, there's no need to worry too much. Tropical thunderstorms usually pass quickly during the day and don't last long. Although the dry season is considered the best time to visit, the rainy season also has its advantages, such as fewer tourists, lower accommodation prices, and cooler weather... etc. When it rains, find a place to take shelter, enjoy a cup of coffee, and usually, the rain will stop soon!

## **Rainfall in Bali**

Don't forget, rainfall in Bali varies by region. Although November to March is the wetter period, we've also visited during this time and only encountered light and brief showers, usually lasting less than an hour. This might be because most of the rainfall is convective thunderstorms, and conditions differ from one area to another.

So, you don't need to ask '**Is it raining in Bali/Will it rain in Bali**' anymore! The chance of it raining across the entire island is actually quite low; sunny weather in one area doesn't mean the same conditions apply elsewhere!

## **Whenever You Visit, Be Prepared**

Whenever you choose to visit Bali, always carry rain gear and stay alert to weather changes. Your journey might include some surprising weather fluctuations, but that's part of Bali's charm. We hope you enjoy the unique allure and wonderful moments this beautiful island offers, no matter when you visit!

## Rainy Season Precautions

The rainy season in Bali requires special attention from travelers. Here are eight precautions to keep in mind:

1. **Carry Waterproof Gear:** Include raincoats, umbrellas, and waterproof backpacks to handle sudden showers.
2. **Choose Appropriate Transportation:** Wet and slippery roads during the rainy season make private cars or taxis safer options than motorcycles.
3. **Allow Extra Travel Time:** Rainy weather can cause traffic delays, so plan additional time to avoid disruptions to your itinerary.
4. **Avoid Beach Areas:** Heavy rain can increase wave size and make the water murky, posing risks. Staying away from beaches is a safer choice.
5. **Pay Attention to Local Warnings:** Local governments and tourism agencies often issue warnings about typhoons or floods. Travelers should stay informed and follow local advice.
6. **Avoid Hiking or Trekking:** Mountain trails can become slippery and muddy during the rainy season, increasing danger. It's best to avoid such activities.
7. **Avoid Visiting Nearby Islands:** Traveling by boat from Bali to nearby islands during the rainy season may lead to delays or cancellations due to rough seas, disrupting your plans.
8. **Avoid Rafting:** Pre-booked rafting trips may be canceled due to fast-flowing rivers, posing safety risks.
