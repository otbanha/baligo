## 📍 【Top Value Choice】April Qingming Festival —— A Spiritual Retreat After the Calm

**Why go at this time?** It's a super smart timing! Right after Indonesia's busiest and most crowded homecoming period for Eid al-Fitr (which ends around late March in 2026). As Indonesians return to work and international tourists aren't as crazy as during peak season, Bali enters a short but precious period of tranquility.

- **Weather**: The golden transition between wet and dry seasons
  **1. Farewell to Rain, Welcome the Sun** Bali's rainy season typically ends in late March. By April, rainfall decreases significantly, and any showers are usually quick afternoon thunderstorms that clear up within half an hour, leaving sunny skies perfect for your plans.
  **2. Lower Humidity, More Comfort** Compared to the muggy and humid January and February, April brings fresher air. Although temperatures remain between **25°C - 32°C**, the lower humidity makes outdoor activities much more comfortable.
- **Local Events**: April 3 is Good Friday, and April 5 is Easter, both national holidays in Indonesia. While locals are off, tourists can enjoy fewer crowds from domestic travelers.
- **Budget Analysis**: Airfare and accommodations often see a slight dip post-Eid al-Fitr, offering great value for money. (Check out our accommodation recommendations here)

### 📍 【Relaxation First】May Labor Day —— A Global Island Escape

**Why go at this time?** May 1st, Labor Day, is an international holiday, meaning not only are you on vacation, but Bali is also in holiday mode. The island's vibe during this period is incredibly relaxed and lively.

  **為什麼選這時候去？** 5 月 1 日勞動節是國際共通假，這意味著不只你在放假，**峇里島**當地也是放假模式。這段期間島上的 vibe 會非常放鬆且熱鬧。

- **Climate Advantage**: May marks the official start of the dry season in Bali. Rainfall decreases further compared to April, and almost every day is perfect for outdoor activities, eliminating the need for rainy-day plans. The sky is as blue as a filter, and the lower humidity makes it ideal for beach picnics and sunset watching. From May onwards, the surf conditions on Bali's west coast become ideal, making it a paradise for surf enthusiasts.
- **Budget Analysis**: Airfare prices remain stable, and it's recommended to book villas in popular areas three months in advance. (For accommodation recommendations that won't disappoint, check here.)

### 📍 Third Stop: 【Cultural Feast】June Dragon Boat Festival Holiday —— The Highlight of Sun and Celebrations

**Why choose this time to go?** If you enjoy deep cultural experiences, June 2026 is undoubtedly the most exciting month in Bali, bar none!

- **Climate Advantage**: The chance of rain is extremely low, with almost every day offering clear blue skies, eliminating worries about outdoor plans being disrupted by rain. Influenced by Australian cold air, Bali in June has sunshine but refreshing, non-sticky air accompanied by cool sea breezes. The average temperature ranges from **24°C - 30°C**, making it the least humid period. With no rain to wash away sediment, the sea is at its most transparent blue, making it the best time of the year for **snorkeling and scuba diving**.
- **Local Events**:
- - \*\*6/17 Galungan\*\*: The entire island will be submerged in elaborate bamboo decorations (Penjor), celebrating the victory of good over evil.
    - **6/13 - 7/11 Bali Arts Festival**: A month-long arts festival takes place in Denpasar.
    - **6/18 - 6/20 Pandawa Beach Festival**: Music and market activities will be held at the famous Pandawa Beach.
- **Budget Analysis**: Due to the numerous festivals, accommodations may be slightly more competitive, so it's advisable to secure your preferred lodging in advance. (For recommended accommodations, see here.)
