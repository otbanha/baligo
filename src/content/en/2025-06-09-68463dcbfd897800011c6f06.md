Bali is not only known for its stunning beaches and temples but also hides many surprising adventure activities. This time, our family chose the Nukuwera & Pandawa Cave River Tubing experience, which turned out to be one of the most unforgettable highlights of our six-day, five-night family trip to Bali. Unlike traditional rafting, cave river tubing combines historical exploration with water adventure, making it a safe and fun activity for the whole family. Here, let me share this thrilling and laughter-filled cave river tubing experience!

Before the trip, we found Nukuwera Adventure, a professional cave river tubing company, through a recommendation in the [Ubud Guide](/blog/2023-11-17-655054b1fd897800011d4d2c/). The guide mentioned that this was a tubing adventure where you could explore Bali's historical Japanese war caves! This adventurous description immediately caught our attention.

The booking process was quite simple. The price was around USD$48 per person, regardless of age, and included hotel pickup, river tubing, lunch, and ATV. I think it's quite a good deal for a full day of fun!

**Pre-Trip Tips**:

- Book 1-2 weeks in advance, especially during peak seasons
- Bring swimwear, sunscreen, and a change of clothes
- Suitable for children aged 7 and above, making it a perfect family activity
- No swimming skills required

## Starting the Day's Adventure

On the day of the activity, the shuttle arrived punctually at 10 AM at our accommodation, [Bidadari Private Villas & Retreat](/blog/2025-01-17-6789be68fd89780001c3ec93/). This serene retreat in the outskirts of Ubud was our carefully chosen lodging. From Ubud, it took about 15 minutes to reach the Nukuwera base. The base had well-equipped changing rooms and lockers, allowing us to store our belongings securely.

Afterwards, we boarded their shuttle bus heading further upstream to the starting point. Along the way, our guide shared the historical background of this man-made river. It turns out that during World War II, Japanese troops had established bases in these caves, and now these historical sites have become part of the adventure.

After getting off the shuttle bus, we had to descend into the valley to reach the rafting starting point. Here, staff warmly greeted us and provided each person with rafting equipment, including life jackets, safety helmets, specially designed rafting tubes (with very comfortable seating), waterproof bags (for carrying small personal items), and more.

The guide handed us over to the rafting team leader, who gave us a brief safety briefing in English. This included instructions on proper sitting posture, how to steer, and what to do when hearing commands in the caves, such as lying flat. It was particularly reassuring when they emphasized that this route was specifically designed for families, with gentle yet fun currents, making it perfect for first-timers and children.

## Safety Measures and Family-Friendly Design

As a parent with children, my primary concern was the activity's safety. Nukuwera excelled in this aspect. They assigned one team leader for every 4-6 people, meaning our family of four had a dedicated leader. They also provided appropriately sized life jackets and more stable rafting tubes for the kids.

It's worth mentioning that this activity has been recommended by many family travel bloggers as a "safer alternative to traditional rafting" due to its gentler currents and constant supervision by team leaders. Our 7-year-old daughter was initially a bit scared, but with the leader's encouragement and professional guidance, she quickly fell in love with the activity and even begged to go again!

## Lunch Break

After tubing, we changed into dry clothes and were taken to Nukuwera's bamboo restaurant built on the cliffside overlooking the river valley. Despite the blazing sun, the cool breeze flowing through the valley brought refreshing relief, perhaps carrying a hint of moisture from the river, making us forget we were in the tropical midday heat.

After lunch, we rested at our table. My son had already fallen asleep from the comfort, and the gamelan music playing in the restaurant flowed like water by our ears. I half-closed my eyes, counting the light spots filtering through the bamboo roof, savoring this rare moment of tranquility.

## **ATV Adventure Highlights**

"Ready to play in the mud?" Our guide suddenly appeared by the table, and the four of us boarded the shuttle again to the ATV starting point.

This 90-minute ATV adventure was full of surprises! We followed our professional guide through Bali's most authentic countryside scenery. The ATV convoy started slowly winding through traditional villages, passing by Balinese temples with thatched roofs, where local children peeked out from porches to wave enthusiastically. The emerald rice fields swayed like waves in the wind, occasionally startling a few egrets that traced elegant arcs across the blue sky.

Our guide led us charging into natural puddles formed after the rain, with cool mud instantly splashing all over us! On the red dirt trails marked with tire tracks, we learned how to control the ATV's balance, with screams and laughter echoing through the jungle whenever the tires slipped.

The itinerary includes several scenic stops: taking photos by the small waterfall in the valley, overlooking the terraced rice fields, with rainbows appearing and disappearing in the mist like marks of adventure.

Every turn brings surprises, every journey has its story - this is undoubtedly the most vivid way to experience Bali's rural beauty!

## Practical Tips and Precautions

Based on our personal experience, here are some practical suggestions for travelers planning to try Nukuwera River Tubing:

- **Best Season**: The dry season from April to October offers the best experience with stable water flow and sunny weather
- **Attire Recommendation**: Waterproof clothing and non-slip sandals
- **Change of Clothes**: Prepare a dry set of clothes if only participating in cave tubing; an additional set is recommended for ATV activities
- **Photography Gear**: Action cameras like GoPro can be used, but ensure they are securely fastened or let the guide handle the filming
- **Valuables**: Most items can be locked in the base storage lockers; carry only a small amount of cash for tipping the guide and leader
- **Health Considerations**: Be prepared to walk down stairs to reach the river valley; consider the physical condition of elderly companions
