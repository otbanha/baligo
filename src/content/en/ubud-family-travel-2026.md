## Family Travel Tips

### 1. **Climate & Clothing**

Located inland, Ubud has a cooler climate compared to the coastal areas, but during the summer months (June to August), it remains dry with plenty of sunshine. During winter breaks, Bali is in the rainy season, so brief showers are common. We recommend packing:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hats and sunglasses
- High SPF sunscreen
- Comfortable walking shoes (ideal for rice field hikes or exploring attractions)
- Rain gear/raincoat (to stay dry during light showers)
- Insect repellent (especially during the rainy season when mosquitoes are more prevalent)

If planning to visit nearby mountainous areas (such as Mount Batur or Mount Kintamani), be prepared for cooler temperatures in the morning and evening with a light jacket or warm layers. For more details, check out the [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/blog/batur-sunrise-hiking-guide/).

### 2. **Health and Safety**

- **Mosquito Protection**: Ubud's natural environment has many mosquitoes, so it's recommended to use locally purchased insect repellent and choose accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly; opt for bottled water or boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Prepare common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness medicine, and band-aids. For reference: [What Essential Medicines Should You Bring for a Trip to Bali? How to Buy Insect Repellent and Sunscreen?](/blog/bali-travel-medicine-checklist/) There are clinics in Ubud city, but for serious cases, it's advisable to visit international hospitals in southern Bali.

***

### 3. **Transportation and Getting Around**

Transportation in Ubud mainly relies on private car hires. It's recommended to book reputable car services and prepare safety seats for younger children. Roads in Ubud city are narrow, and some attractions (like rice terraces or temples) require walking, so lightweight baby carriers or strollers are advisable. For younger children, avoid long car rides and choose attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel time.

### 4. **Dining Options**

Ubud offers a diverse range of dining options, from local cuisine to international dishes. Recommendations:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavy dishes (like babi guling, roasted pork).
- Many restaurants offer kids' menus, and international chain restaurants are also a safe bet for child-friendly flavors.

**Quick Reference:**

- [Top 10 Foods to Try in Bali](/blog/bali-travel-tips/)

### 5. **Cultural Etiquette**

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, where locals place great importance on traditional etiquette. Teach your children to respect local customs, such as:

- Wear a sarong and maintain silence when entering temples.
- Avoid touching religious items or statues without permission.
- Always ask for consent before taking photos of locals.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

**Is Ubud suitable for traveling with children?**

Ubud is a family-friendly destination, offering rich cultural attractions, natural scenery, and child-friendly facilities such as zoos, rice field views, and family-friendly restaurants.

**What kid-friendly activities are available in Ubud?**

We've compiled a list of 54 kid-friendly activities in Ubud. Click [here](/blog/ubud-with-kids-guide/) to see them.

**Are there accommodations in Ubud with water slides?**

Currently, none are known. If you're specifically looking for family resorts with water slides, check out our list of 15 family resorts with water slides. Click [here](/blog/bali-family-resorts-water-slides/) for more details.

**What's the weather like in Ubud? What clothes should I pack?**

Ubud has a warm and humid climate. It's recommended to pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sun protection, and rain gear to handle sudden showers.

**Is the food in Ubud safe for kids?**

Ubud offers a variety of restaurants serving both international and local cuisine. It's advisable to choose restaurants with good hygiene standards and be cautious about drinking water safety.

**Is transportation in Ubud convenient? Is it suitable for traveling with kids?**

For families, transportation in Ubud mainly consists of taxis/ride-hailing services and private car hires. We recommend opting for private car services for convenience and safety when traveling with kids.

**Are there medical facilities in Ubud? What if my child gets sick?**

Ubud has clinics and pharmacies, but for serious cases, it's recommended to go to international hospitals in southern Bali. Travel insurance is also advised.

**Do Ubud attractions require tickets? Are there discounts for children?**

Most attractions require tickets, usually free for children under 6, with discounted rates for ages 6-12 at some places.

**Is Ubud stroller-friendly?**

Most areas in Ubud have uneven surfaces. It's recommended to use a baby carrier or lightweight stroller, avoiding crowded or rugged areas.
