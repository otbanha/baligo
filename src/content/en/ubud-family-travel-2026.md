## Family Travel Tips

### 1. **Climate & Clothing**

Located inland, Ubud has a cooler climate compared to Bali’s coastal areas. However, during the summer months (June to August), it’s still dry season with plenty of sunshine. During winter breaks, Bali is in its rainy season, so brief showers are common. Here’s what to pack for your kids:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hats and sunglasses
- High-SPF sunscreen
- Comfortable walking shoes (for rice field hikes or exploring attractions)
- Rain gear/raincoat (to stay dry during light showers)
- Insect repellent (especially important during the rainy season)

If planning to visit surrounding mountain areas (such as Mount Batur or Mount Kintamani), be prepared for significant temperature differences between day and night. Bring a light jacket or warm clothing. For those interested, refer to our [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/blog/batur-sunrise-hiking-guide/)

### 2. Health & Safety

- **Mosquito Protection**: Ubud's natural environment has many mosquitoes. We recommend using locally purchased insect repellent and choosing accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly. Purchase bottled water or use boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Prepare common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal medicine, motion sickness pills, and band-aids. Refer to: [Essential Medicines for Bali Travel - How to Buy Insect Repellent/Sunscreen?](/blog/bali-travel-medicine-checklist/) There are clinics in Ubud city, but for serious cases, it's recommended to go to international hospitals in southern Bali.

***

### 3. Transportation & Getting Around

Private car hire is the main mode of transportation in Ubud. We recommend booking reputable car services and preparing child safety seats for younger children. Ubud's city roads are narrow, and some attractions (like rice terraces or temples) require walking. Choose lightweight baby carriers or strollers. For younger children, avoid long car rides and select attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel time.

### 4. Dining Options

Ubud offers a diverse range of dining options, from local delicacies to international cuisine. Recommendations:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned dishes (such as babi guling, roasted suckling pig).
- Many restaurants offer kids' menus, and you can also opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the flavors suit your child's palate.

Quick Reference:

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

As the cultural heart of Bali, Ubud places great importance on traditional customs. Teach your children to respect local traditions, such as:

- Wearing a sarong and maintaining silence when entering temples.
- Avoiding touching religious objects or statues without permission.
- Asking for consent before taking photos of locals.

The park features diverse marine exhibits including sharks, sea turtles, and tropical fish. Each exhibit area provides detailed explanations to spark children's curiosity about the ocean. Regularly scheduled marine knowledge lectures offer accessible introductions to various marine species.

Family-friendly facilities include ample shaded areas, children's restrooms, and dining spaces, ensuring comfort throughout your visit. Convenient parking and easy access make it an ideal destination for a half-day trip.

Tips: Bring towels and a change of clothes, and don't forget sunscreen. Due to the park's popularity, advance booking is recommended to avoid waiting. Suitable for children aged 3 and above.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

**Is Ubud suitable for traveling with children?**

Ubud is a great destination for family travel, offering rich cultural attractions, natural scenery, and family-friendly facilities such as zoos, rice field views, and kid-friendly restaurants.

**What other activities are suitable for children in Ubud?**

We've compiled a list of 54 activities in Ubud that are perfect for kids. Click [here](/blog/ubud-with-kids-guide/) to check them out.

**Are there accommodations in Ubud with water slides?**

Currently, none are known. If you're specifically looking for family resorts with water slides, you can explore our curated list of 15 family resorts featuring water slides. Click [here](/blog/bali-family-resorts-water-slides/) for more details.

**What is the weather like in Ubud? What clothing should I prepare?**

Ubud has a warm and humid climate. It's recommended to pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sun protection, and rain gear to prepare for sudden showers.

**Is the food in Ubud safe for children?**

Ubud offers a variety of international and local cuisine. It's recommended to choose restaurants with good hygiene standards and pay attention to drinking water safety.

**Is transportation in Ubud convenient? Is it suitable for traveling with children?**

For families, transportation in Ubud mainly consists of taxis/ride-hailing services and private car hires. It's advisable to opt for private car hire for convenience and safety when traveling with children.

**Are there medical facilities in Ubud? What if a child gets sick?**

Ubud has clinics and pharmacies, but for serious cases, it's recommended to go to international hospitals in southern Bali. It's also advisable to purchase travel insurance.

**Do attractions in Ubud require entrance fees? Are there discounts for children?**

Most attractions require entrance fees, with children under 6 usually admitted free. Some places offer discounted tickets for children aged 6-12.

**Is Ubud stroller-friendly?**

Most areas in Ubud have uneven surfaces. It's recommended to use a baby carrier or a lightweight stroller and avoid crowded or rugged areas.
