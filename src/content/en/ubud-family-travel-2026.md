## Family Travel Tips

### 1. **Climate and Clothing**

Ubud is located inland in Bali, making its climate cooler than the coast. During summer break (June to August), it's the dry season with plenty of sunshine, while winter break falls in the rainy season, often bringing brief showers. Pack the following for your kids:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hat and sunglasses
- High-SPF sunscreen
- Comfortable walking shoes (for rice field treks or exploring sights)
- Rain gear or raincoat (handy for light rain)
- Insect repellent (more bugs in the rainy season)

If you plan to visit nearby highland areas like Mount Batur or Kintamani, temperatures can vary significantly between morning and evening, so bring a light jacket or warm layers. For those interested, check out the [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/en/blog/batur-sunrise-hiking-guide/).

### 2. Health & Safety

- **Mosquito Protection**: Ubud's natural environment has many mosquitoes. It's recommended to use locally purchased insect repellent and choose accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly. Opt for bottled water or boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Pack common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrhea medicine, motion sickness pills, and band-aids. For reference: [What essential medications and supplies should you bring for a Bali trip? How to buy mosquito repellent and sunscreen?](/en/blog/bali-travel-medicine-checklist/) There are clinics in Ubud, but for serious cases, head to international hospitals in southern Bali.

***

### 3. Transportation & Getting Around

Transportation in Ubud mainly relies on private car hire. It's advisable to book a reputable service and prepare a car seat for younger children. Roads in Ubud are narrow, and some attractions (like rice terraces or temples) require walking, so bring a lightweight baby carrier or stroller. If your child is very young, avoid long drives and choose attractions close to your accommodation to minimize back-and-forth travel.

### 4. Dining Options

Ubud offers a diverse range of dining options, from local cuisine to international dishes. Recommendations:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavy foods (like babi guling, roasted suckling pig).
- Many restaurants offer kids' menus, or you can opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the food suits your child's taste.

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, and locals place great importance on traditional etiquette. Teach children to respect local customs, such as:

- Wear a sarong and remain quiet when entering temples.
- Do not touch religious objects or statues.
- Ask for permission before taking photos of locals.

### 11. Bali Exotic Marine Park — A Must-Visit Ocean Park for Families

![11. 峇里島海豚樂園 Bali Exotic Marine Park — 親子必訪海洋樂園](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779866556839-_2026-05-27_14.22.19.png)

Letting kids interact closely with dolphins is the highlight of Bali Exotic Marine Park. Located near Ubud, this family-oriented marine park features rich marine life exhibits, educational programs, and interactive experiences, making it a perfect choice for a family trip.

Swimming with dolphins is the park's most popular activity. Under the guidance of professional trainers, children can safely enter the pool to play with these intelligent dolphins. It's not just fun—it's a valuable lesson in marine ecology, teaching kids about dolphin behavior and conservation.

The park also features exhibits of sharks, sea turtles, tropical fish, and other marine life, each with detailed explanations to spark children's curiosity about the ocean. Regularly scheduled marine knowledge talks offer engaging, easy-to-understand introductions to various sea creatures.

Family-friendly facilities include plenty of shaded areas, children's restrooms, and dining options, ensuring your little ones stay comfortable throughout the visit. With convenient parking and easy access, it's an ideal half-day outing.

Friendly reminder: Bring towels and a change of clothes, and don't forget sunscreen. The park is very popular, so book ahead to avoid waiting on-site. Suitable for children aged 3 and up.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

**Is Ubud suitable for traveling with kids?**

Ubud is a great destination for family trips, offering rich cultural attractions, natural scenery, and kid-friendly facilities such as a zoo, rice terrace views, and family-oriented restaurants.

**What other kid-friendly activities are there in Ubud?**

We've compiled 54 kid-friendly activities in Ubud—click [here](/en/blog/ubud-with-kids-guide/) to check them out.

**Are there any accommodations in Ubud with water slides?**

Not that we know of. If you're specifically looking for family resorts with water slides, check out our curated list of 15 family resorts with water slides. Click [here](/en/blog/bali-family-resorts-water-slides/).

**What's the weather like in Ubud? What kind of clothing should I pack?**

Ubud has a warm, humid climate, so it's best to pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sunscreen, and rain gear to handle sudden showers.

**Is the food in Ubud safe for children?**

Ubud has many restaurants offering both international and local cuisine. It's advisable to choose places with good hygiene standards and be mindful of drinking water safety.

**Is transportation in Ubud convenient? Is it suitable for traveling with kids?**

For families, transportation in Ubud mainly relies on taxis, ride-hailing services, and private car hires. It's recommended to opt for a private car service to make traveling with kids easier and safer.

**Are there medical facilities in Ubud? What if a child gets sick?**

Ubud has clinics and pharmacies, but for serious cases, it's better to head to an international hospital in southern Bali. It's also wise to purchase travel insurance.

**Do attractions in Ubud require tickets? Are there discounts for children?**

Most attractions require tickets. Typically, children under 6 enter for free, and some places offer discounted tickets for kids aged 6-12.

**Is Ubud stroller-friendly?**

Most areas of Ubud have uneven roads, so it's recommended to use a baby carrier or a lightweight stroller, and avoid using them in crowded or rugged spots.
