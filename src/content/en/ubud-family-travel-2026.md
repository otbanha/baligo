## Family Travel Tips

### 1. Climate and Attire

Ubud is located inland in Bali, offering a cooler climate compared to the coastal areas. However, during the summer vacation period (June to August), it remains part of the dry season with plenty of sunshine during the day. In contrast, during the winter break, Bali enters its rainy season, making short showers a common occurrence during outings. It's advisable to prepare for children:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hat and sunglasses
- High SPF sunscreen
- Comfortable walking shoes (suitable for rice field hikes or exploring attractions)
- Rain gear/raincoat (don't let light rain stop you)
- Insect repellent (mosquitoes are more active during the rainy season)

If you're planning to visit the surrounding mountainous areas (such as Mount Batur or Mount Kintamani), be prepared for significant temperature differences between day and night. It's advisable to bring a light jacket or warm clothing. For those interested, please refer to our [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/blog/2024-10-18-66d31f77fd897800011851c1/) for more details.

### 2. Health and Safety

- **Mosquito Protection**: Ubud's natural environment has a lot of mosquitoes, so it's recommended to use locally purchased insect repellent and choose accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly; it's advisable to buy bottled water or use boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Prepare common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness medicine, and band-aids. For reference: [What essential medications and products should you bring for a trip to Bali, Indonesia? How to buy insect repellent and sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/) There are clinics in Ubud city, but for serious conditions, it's recommended to go to an international hospital in southern Bali.

***

### 3. Transportation and Getting Around

Transportation in Ubud primarily revolves around private car hires. It's advisable to book reputable car services and ensure safety seats are prepared for younger children. The roads in central Ubud are narrow, and some attractions (like rice terraces or temples) require walking, so opting for lightweight baby carriers or strollers is recommended. For families with very young children, it's best to avoid long car journeys and choose attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel time.

### 4. Dining Options

Ubud offers a diverse range of dining options, from local cuisine to international dishes. Recommendation:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned dishes (such as babi guling roast pork).
- Many restaurants offer kids' menus, or you can opt for international chain restaurants to ensure flavors suit children's tastes.

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali, where locals place great importance on traditional etiquette. Teach your children to respect local customs, such as:

- Wear a sarong and maintain silence when entering temples.
- Do not touch religious artifacts or statues without permission.
- Always ask for local consent before taking photographs.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

**Is Ubud suitable for traveling with children?**

Ubud is an ideal destination for family travel, offering a wealth of cultural attractions, natural scenery, and family-friendly facilities such as zoos, rice field landscapes, and kid-friendly restaurants.

**Does Ubud have accommodations with water slides?**

**What's the weather like in Ubud? What clothes should I pack?**

Ubud's climate is warm and humid, so it's advisable to pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sun protection, and rain gear to handle sudden showers.

**Is Ubud's Food Safe for Children?**

Ubud offers a variety of restaurants serving both international and local cuisine. It's advisable to choose establishments with good hygiene standards and to be cautious about drinking water safety.

**Is Ubud convenient for transportation? Is it suitable for traveling with children?**

For families traveling in Ubud, transportation options mainly include taxis/ride-hailing services and private car rentals. It's recommended to opt for private car rental services for convenience when traveling with children and to ensure safety.

**Are there medical facilities in Ubud? What if my child gets sick?**

Ubud has clinics and pharmacies, but for serious conditions it's recommended to go to international hospitals in southern Bali. We also advise purchasing travel insurance.

**Do attractions in Ubud require entrance fees? Are there discounts for children?**

Most attractions require an entrance fee, with children under 6 usually admitted free. Some places offer discounted tickets for children aged 6 to 12.

**Is Ubud stroller-friendly?**

Most areas in Ubud have uneven road surfaces. It's recommended to use a baby carrier or lightweight stroller, and avoid crowded or rugged areas.
