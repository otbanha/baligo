This 10-day, 9-night self-guided trip to Bali with my girlfriend (from June 26 to July 5, 2024) was filled with unforgettable experiences. Before departure, we gathered valuable travel information from the '[Bali/Indonesia Travel Guide](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide)' platform. To give back, I’d like to share our journey and especially recommend our private driver, Ketut. We’re incredibly grateful for his warm hospitality and guidance, which made our Bali adventure truly memorable.

## First Encounter: Warmth and Attention to Detail

Upon arriving at Denpasar Airport, we contacted Ketut. He’s a cheerful and always-smiling young man who immediately impressed us with his thoughtfulness. From helping with our luggage to opening car doors, his small gestures gave us a fresh perspective on Bali. On the way to our villa, Ketut took us to a highly-rated currency exchange spot and shared insights into Bali’s religious culture and local customs. Through his explanations, we learned that Bali is the only Hindu-majority region in Indonesia, blending local traditions and beliefs. This unique faith system, which includes ancestor worship, nature reverence, and devotion to deities, felt truly special.

## Unexpected Surprises During the Dry Season

Our trip coincided with Bali’s dry season, offering pleasant weather without the humidity and heat we’re used to in Taiwan. It was the peak of the tourist season, but not overly crowded, ensuring a comfortable travel experience. However, we did encounter a few rainy days, missing some sunset and sunrise views. These small regrets have become reasons for us to return in the future.

## Despite a Cold, Still Meticulously Planning

Although Ketut felt unwell on the second day of our visit to Bali, he still arranged for another driver, Manx Liega, from his professional tour guide team. Liega has a completely different personality from Ketut. Although he doesn't actively engage in conversation with us, his planning of the itinerary for the attractions is equally excellent. His silence doesn't affect his professionalism; instead, it allows us to feel more tranquility and comfort while enjoying the beautiful sea views.

## An Encounter at Tanah Lot Temple

During our visit to Tanah Lot Temple (Pura Tanah Lot), we encountered many wild macaques. While sharing stories about the monkeys, Ketut proactively helped us fend off the mischievous primates that often steal items from tourists, such as hats, flip-flops, and glasses. Just as Ketut finished his tales, a macaque suddenly appeared on the stone path, attempting to snatch a white tourist's flip-flop. Fortunately, it didn't succeed. This mix of frustration and slight panic added a humorous twist to our journey.
