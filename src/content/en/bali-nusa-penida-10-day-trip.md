This 10-day, 9-night self-guided trip to Bali with my girlfriend took place from June 26 to July 5, 2024. Before departure, we gathered valuable travel information through the platform '[Bali/Indonesia Travel Guide](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide).' As a way to give back, we’d like to share our experience and especially recommend our private driver, Ketut. We’re deeply grateful to this enthusiastic driver and guide who warmly welcomes Taiwanese travelers, making our Bali journey truly unforgettable.

## First Encounter: Warmth and Thoughtfulness

As soon as we arrived at Denpasar Airport, we contacted Ketut. He’s a cheerful and always-smiling young man. Upon meeting us, he immediately helped with our luggage and even opened the car door for us—small gestures that gave us a completely different impression of Bali. On the way to our villa, Ketut took us to a highly-rated currency exchange spot and shared insights about Bali’s religious culture and local customs. Through his explanations, we learned that Bali is the only Hindu-majority region in Indonesia, blending local traditions and practices. This gave us a fresh perspective on Balinese Hinduism, with its unique belief system that includes ancestor worship, nature worship, and devotion to deities—something we found truly special.

## Unexpected Surprises During the Dry Season

Our trip coincided with Bali’s dry season, offering pleasant weather without the humidity and heat we’re used to in Taiwan. It was the peak of the tourist season, but not overly crowded, ensuring a comfortable travel experience. However, we did encounter a few rainy days, which meant missing out on some sunset and sunrise views. These small regrets have become reasons for us to return in the future.

## Despite a Cold, Still Meticulous in Planning

Although Ketut felt unwell on our second day in Bali, he still arranged for another driver, Manx Liega, from his professional tour guide team. Liega has a completely different personality from Ketut. While he doesn't initiate much conversation, his planning of the scenic routes is equally excellent. His silence doesn't affect his professionalism; instead, it allows us to enjoy the beautiful sea views with more tranquility and comfort.

## An Encounter at Tanah Lot Temple

During our visit to Tanah Lot Temple, we encountered many wild macaques. While sharing stories about the monkeys, Ketut proactively helped us fend off the mischievous macaques that often steal items from tourists, such as hats, flip-flops, and glasses. Just as Ketut finished telling these tales, a macaque suddenly appeared on the stone path, attempting to snatch a white tourist's flip-flop. Fortunately, it didn't succeed. This mix of frustration and slight panic added a humorous twist to our journey.
