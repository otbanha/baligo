This time, my girlfriend and I took a 10-day, 9-night self-guided trip to Bali (June 26 to July 5, 2024). Before departing, we gathered a wealth of valuable travel tips from the platform "[Bali/Indonesia Travel Guide](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide)", so we wanted to give back by sharing our experience and especially recommending our private driver, Ketut. A big thank you to this warm-hearted driver and guide who warmly welcomed Taiwanese travelers and made our Bali trip even more unforgettable.

## First Encounter: Warmth and Attention to Detail

As soon as we arrived at Denpasar Airport, we contacted Ketut. He's an incredibly friendly young man who always wears a smile. Right from the start, he helped us with our checked luggage and even opened the car door for us—such thoughtful gestures that gave me a completely different impression of Bali. During the drive to our villa, Ketut took us to a highly-rated currency exchange spot and shared insights about Bali's religious culture, customs, and traditions along the way. Through Ketut's explanations, we learned that Bali is the only region in Indonesia that practices Hinduism, blending it with local traditions and customs, which gave us a fresh perspective on Balinese Hinduism. This unique belief system, including ancestor worship, nature worship, and reverence for deities, felt truly special to us.

## Pleasant Surprises in the Dry Season

Our trip coincided with Bali's dry season, offering comfortable, pleasant weather without the humidity and stickiness of Taiwan. It was the peak of the tourist season, climbing toward its high point. Visiting during this time, while not the busiest, meant a relatively good travel experience without feeling overcrowded. However, we did encounter a few days of rain, missing out on some chances to watch sunsets and sunrises. These small regrets have become reasons for us to return in the future.

## Despite a Cold, He Still Planned with Care

Although Ketut fell ill on our second day in Bali, he still arranged for another driver, Manx Liega, from his professional team of guides. Liega had a completely different personality from Ketut—he wasn't very talkative, but his route planning was just as excellent. His quiet demeanor didn't affect his professionalism; instead, it allowed us to enjoy the beautiful sea views with a greater sense of peace and comfort.

## An Unexpected Encounter at Tanah Lot

During our visit to Tanah Lot (Pura Tanah Lot), we encountered many wild macaques. Ketut shared stories about the monkeys while proactively shooing away those that came too close—these monkeys are known to snatch items from tourists, such as hats, slippers, and glasses. Just as Ketut finished recounting their antics, a monkey suddenly appeared on the stone path and tried to steal a pair of slippers from a Western tourist, though it didn't succeed. It was a mix of helplessness and mild alarm, adding a playful twist to our journey.
