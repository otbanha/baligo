This self-guided trip to Bali with my girlfriend lasted ten days and nine nights (June 26 to July 5, 2024). Before departure, we gathered valuable travel information through the platform '[Bali/Indonesia Travel Guide](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide).' To give back, we’d like to share our experience and especially recommend our private driver, Ketut. We’re deeply grateful to this enthusiastic driver and guide who warmly welcomed Taiwanese tourists, making our Bali journey truly unforgettable.

## First Encounter: Warmth and Thoughtfulness

As soon as we arrived at Denpasar Airport, we contacted Ketut. He’s a very warm and always-smiling young man. Upon meeting, he proactively helped us with our luggage and even opened the car door for us, which gave us a completely different impression of Bali. On the way to our villa, Ketut took us to a highly-rated currency exchange spot and introduced us to Bali’s religious culture and local customs along the way. Through his explanations, we learned that Bali is the only Hindu-majority region in Indonesia, blending local traditions and practices. This gave us a fresh understanding of Balinese Hinduism, with its unique belief system encompassing ancestor worship, nature worship, and devotion to deities, which felt truly special to us.

## Unexpected Surprises During the Dry Season

Our trip coincided with Bali’s dry season, offering pleasant weather without the humidity and heat we’re used to in Taiwan. It was the peak of the tourist season, but not overly crowded, ensuring a relatively high-quality travel experience. However, we did encounter a few days of rain, missing some opportunities to watch sunsets and sunrises. These small regrets have become reasons for us to revisit in the future.

## Despite a Cold, Still Meticulously Planning

Although Ketut felt unwell on our second day in Bali, he still arranged for another driver, Manx Liega, from his professional tour guide team. Liega has a completely different personality from Ketut. While he doesn't actively engage in conversation with us, his planning of the scenic routes is equally excellent. His silence doesn't affect his professionalism; instead, it allows us to enjoy the beautiful sea views with more tranquility and comfort.

## An Encounter at Tanah Lot Temple

During our visit to Tanah Lot Temple, we encountered many wild macaques. While sharing stories about the monkeys, Ketut proactively helped us fend off the mischievous primates that often steal items from tourists, such as hats, flip-flops, and glasses. Just as Ketut finished his tales, a macaque suddenly appeared on the stone path, attempting to snatch a white tourist's flip-flop. Fortunately, it didn't succeed. This mix of frustration and slight panic added a humorous twist to our journey.
