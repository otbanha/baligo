This travelogue was contributed by group member [Serena](https://www.facebook.com/100004740466944/), documenting a family adventure in Bali with two young children. During the trip, they spent seven wonderful days with their guide and driver, Xiao Zhong (Pacung Bali). Through a detailed itinerary and attraction recommendations, they explored every corner of Bali, savored local cuisine, enjoyed a variety of water activities, and created many precious memories and beautiful photos.

**Bottom Line**: For a 7-day, 6-night trip with 5 adults and 2 kids, we recommend staying at **Beachwalk Residence in Kuta** (a three-bedroom apartment with Beachwalk Shopping Center right downstairs, ideal for large families). The itinerary covers Jimbaran beach dining, South Bali/Uluwatu attractions, and is perfect for families with elderly members and children who need more space and convenient amenities.

## Afterword

Here are some top recommendations for must-try foods and must-visit spots in Bali, along with a few things to avoid:

1. **Natys Restaurant**: Don't miss their seafood platter and red wine braised beef! They've recently opened branches in many locations, ensuring consistent quality.
2. **Tanjung Benoa Water Sports**: Highly recommend the underwater walking activity for diving beginners. Though it's a man-made attraction, it offers a unique experience. Skip Turtle Island—it's quite boring and lacks distinctive animals. Also, be sure to haggle; you can get 20-40% off.

   [Reserve Tanjung Benoa Banana Boat Combo](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=804752&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F9106-banana-boat-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3D461f80f005)
3. **Uluwatu Cliff Road**: The scenery here is stunning, perfect for photos. Visit in the morning for fewer crowds.
4. **Uluwatu Temple, Tanah Lot Temple, and Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park**: All worth a visit. Uluwatu Temple and Tanah Lot Temple offer beautiful views and great photo ops. At Garuda Wisnu Kencana, if your budget allows, take the shuttle to the statue area to avoid heatstroke.
5. **Ubud Swing**: Features various activities like zip-lining and sky bikes. Nearby, there's a fantastic pork rib fried noodle restaurant, but it's a bit hidden—ask my private driver for directions.
6. **La Brisa**: If you plan to visit the bar at the far end, don't bring water in the car—the restaurant will require you to pour it all out.
7. **Beachwalk Residence**: For family trips, consider staying at [Beachwalk Residence](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=25918345) [Trip](https://hk.trip.com/hotels/bali-hotel-detail-78158227/hai-tan-man-bu-gong-yu/?Allianceid=6817581&SID=311634184&trip_sub1=&trip_sub3=D18793707) [Booking](https://booking.tpm.li/Smp4FEqy). It offers family-style apartment units, within walking distance to shopping malls and close to the beach—great value for money.
8. **Traffic is heavy during Chinese New Year**: Travel times were 30 minutes to 1.5 hours longer than Google Maps estimates, especially to Ubud, where the narrow roads near the palace cause severe congestion.
9. **Rainy season tips**: Since Chinese New Year falls during Bali's rainy season peak, save this guide for rainy days: [Bali Rainy Season: What to Do When It Rains? 60 Indoor Activities in Bali](/en/blog/2026-bali-indoor-activities/)
