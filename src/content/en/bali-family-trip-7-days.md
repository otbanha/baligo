## Epilogue

Here are some must-try and must-visit recommendations for Bali, along with some pitfalls to avoid:

1. **Natys American Restaurant**: Don’t miss their seafood platter and beef stew! They’ve recently opened branches in many places, ensuring quality.  
2. **Tanjung Benoa Water Sports**: Highly recommended for beginners to try the underwater walking activity. Although it’s a man-made attraction, it’s a unique experience. Avoid Turtle Island, as it’s quite boring and lacks interesting animals. Also, remember to bargain for discounts of 20-40%.

3. **Uluwatu Cliff Road**: The scenery here is stunning, perfect for photos. It’s best to visit in the morning when there are fewer people.  
4. **Uluwatu Temple, Tanah Lot Temple, Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park**: These places are all worth visiting. Uluwatu Temple and Tanah Lot Temple offer beautiful views and great photo opportunities. As for Garuda Wisnu Kencana, if your budget allows, take the shuttle to the statue area to avoid heatstroke.  
5. **Ubud Swing**: This place offers various entertainment facilities, including zip lines and sky bikes. There’s also a fantastic pork rib fried noodle restaurant nearby, though it’s a bit hidden—ask my driver for directions.  
6. **La Brisa**: If you’re heading to the innermost bar, remember not to bring water inside, as the restaurant will ask you to pour it out.  
7. **Beachwalk Residence**: For family trips, consider staying at [Beachwalk Residence](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=25918345). They offer family-friendly apartment-style rooms, just a short walk from the shopping mall and close to the beach, offering great value for money.  
8. **Traffic during Lunar New Year**: Expect delays of 30 minutes to 1.5 hours longer than the estimated time calculated by Google Maps. This is especially true for trips to Ubud, which take even longer than expected. The roads near the palace are narrow, leading to severe traffic congestion.  
9. **Rainy season contingency**: Since the Lunar New Year falls during Bali’s rainy season, save this guide for rainy days: [Bali Rainy Season: 60 Indoor Activities for Rainy Days](/blog/2026-bali-indoor-activities/)
