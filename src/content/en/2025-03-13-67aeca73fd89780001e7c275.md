### Bali Travel Experience & Recommendations

> The following content is adapted from fellow traveler [Chingju Wang](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/193Gv3vLVV/)’s travelogue:

### Travel Dates: February 6-9, 2025

This was my first trip to Bali, and although it was only four days long, it was filled with surprises and moving moments. I’m especially grateful for the valuable advice from many experienced travelers when I asked questions online last September, which made planning this trip much smoother. To give back to everyone’s help, I’d like to share my detailed travel experience and recommendations, hoping to provide some insights for those planning to visit Bali in the future.

### 1. Mara River Safari Lodge: A Unique Experience of Sleeping with Animals

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b29252ee62cf7e762ff1f447761b8fa7.jpg)

During this trip, our 7-year-old child’s favorite part was the **Mara River Safari Lodge**. This is a resort located within a zoo, and staying here not only offers convenience but also allows close encounters with various animals. Every morning, we enjoyed breakfast while watching lions through the glass windows—a truly unique experience. The lions seemed accustomed to visitors’ gazes, often posing in various spots as if “posing for photos,” which kept our child completely captivated and thrilled.

Additionally, taking the safari tram was very convenient, allowing us to easily observe various animals. The kids couldn’t stop exclaiming with excitement on the tram, fully immersed in this vibrant environment. If you’re traveling with children, this is definitely a must-visit destination.

### 2. **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple: A Serene Spiritual Haven**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bbe079ae357378ab17d4b8d0b2fcadd3.jpg)

For seniors aged 70, **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple** is the most relaxing place. The environment here is incredibly tranquil, surrounded by mountains with a lake as calm as a mirror, and a gentle breeze that refreshes the soul. We strolled leisurely, sipping coffee, and soaking in the peacefulness of nature. The misty mountain scenery felt like stepping into a fairyland, and the elders particularly enjoyed this escape from the hustle and bustle, finding it a perfect place to unwind completely.

### 3. **Tanah Lot: Majestic Sea Erosion Wonders**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e939c789a7f931dc80650833c7e66e7c.jpg)

**Tanah Lot** is one of Bali's iconic landmarks, where the sea-eroded gate and platform are truly spectacular, perfectly matching our imagination of Bali. Although the seaside sun can be intense, watching the waves crash against the rocks is utterly awe-inspiring. Friends visiting here are advised to bring hats or umbrellas and consider coming in the evening to avoid the midday heat and enjoy the beautiful sunset.

### 7. **Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park: Majestic Yet Sparse**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9ff7c54eabc3f3383b36804884d88bd9.jpg)

**Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park** covers a vast area, with the most striking feature being the enormous Garuda statue, whose height and scale are truly awe-inspiring. Visible even from the airport, its grandeur is undeniable. However, the park's facilities seem somewhat incomplete, giving it a sparse feel. It's advisable to make a brief stop here to admire the statue before moving on to other attractions.

### 8. **Private Driver: The Unsung Hero of the Trip**

A significant part of this trip's success is owed to our private driver **Xiao Wu** and his wife **Betty Pingky**. With five people in our group and a lot of luggage, Xiao Wu arranged a large vehicle to ensure our comfort throughout the journey. His driving was very steady, with no sudden brakes or jerks, allowing the sick child and elderly to rest well in the car.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3143a1eb899bf827d37ad8851ac6b8d4.jpg)

Moreover, Xiao Wu's familiarity with Bali's road conditions allowed him to perfectly avoid traffic congestion, making our itinerary smoother. Whether it was the elderly needing a restroom break or the child constantly asking 'Are we there yet?', he always managed to arrive at the destination at the right time, maintaining a harmonious atmosphere for the entire family. His fluent Chinese, with standard intonation, made it easy for him to chat with both the elderly and children, adding warmth to the journey.

It's worth mentioning that Betty Pingky was very attentive before departure, helping us confirm hotel package deals and adjusting the itinerary according to our needs. Even during the trip, when we casually mentioned a certain attraction, Xiao Wu would go out of his way to take us there. Such thoughtful service truly touched us.

### 9. **Why Wasn't There Traffic?**

During this trip, we hardly encountered any traffic, which pleasantly surprised me. Upon reflection, there might be a few reasons:

1. **Clever Timing**: We visited **Tanah Lot** and **Ulun Danu Beratan Temple** during off-peak hours, choosing to go around lunchtime when the roads were less crowded.
2. **Driver's Expertise**: Xiao Wu, our driver, was very familiar with Bali's roads. When heading to **Tirta Empul Temple** and **Jimbaran**, he took some back roads, avoiding the congestion on main thoroughfares.
3. **Personal Tolerance for Traffic**: Having lived in Jakarta for a long time, I'm used to endless traffic jams. In comparison, Bali's traffic felt like paradise.

These factors combined made our journey much smoother and significantly improved my impression of Bali.

### 10. **Why There Are Few Photos: Busy Taking Care of Family**

There are far fewer photos from this trip than expected, mainly because after getting out of the car, I was almost always busy taking care of my 7-year-old child and 70-year-old elder. The child is lively and always needs supervision, while the elders require timely rest and assistance. Although there are fewer beautiful photos, ensuring that the whole family had a fun and safe time felt more important than taking pictures. After all, the essence of travel is about creating memories, not just checking in.

### 11. **A Small Regret About the Villa Pool**

![示意圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b77f1b401df4485747072419ea8c5e64.jpg)

Illustration

The villa we stayed in was beautifully located, but the only regret was that the pool was 1.5 meters deep, which was too deep for the child to enjoy playing in the water. This also made it hard for the adults to fully relax, as we had to constantly watch over the child's safety. In the future, when choosing a villa, it's essential to confirm the pool's depth, especially for families with children. Opting for a shallow area or a children's pool would be more suitable.

### Summary and Rating

Our family trip to Bali left us thoroughly satisfied, especially thanks to the professional service of our private driver and Bali's stunning natural beauty, which left a deep impression on us. We initially expected Bali's traffic to be as congested as Jakarta's, but surprisingly, we hardly encountered any traffic jams, which greatly enhanced our travel experience. If I were to rate this trip out of 100, I'd give it a **95**. The only downside was the depth of the villa's swimming pool.

Although this trip was quite busy—so much so that we didn't even have time for a massage (I made up for it right after returning to Jakarta)—at least the whole family found Bali enjoyable. Both the kids and the elders had a great time, making all the effort worthwhile. The essence of travel isn't about perfection, but about the companionship and memories created with family.

---

### Recommendations and Tips

- **Private Driver**: Highly recommend **Betty Pingky** and her husband **Mr. Wu**. Their attentive service and fluent Chinese make them ideal for family trips.
- **Itinerary Planning**: Tailor your itinerary based on the age and interests of family members. For example, families with children might enjoy more zoo or rice terrace activities, while elders may prefer serene spots like the Water Temple.
- **Accommodation**: When choosing a villa, always confirm the pool depth, especially if traveling with children.
- **Transportation**: Avoid peak hours and opt for drivers familiar with local routes to significantly enhance your travel experience.

This Bali trip has left our family with wonderful memories, and we look forward to visiting this enchanting island again in the future!
