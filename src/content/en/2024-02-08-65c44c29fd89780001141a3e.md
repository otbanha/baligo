Bali is one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations, attracting millions of visitors from around the world each year. Due to the development of tourism, English has gradually become a common language in Bali.

![峇里島有來自各個國家、不同人種/母語的外國人](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_af3cffb9f72b6ef11a499c8c3fa47d3f.jpg)

Bali is home to foreigners from various countries, ethnicities, and native languages.

Why has English become a common language in Bali? Here are some reasons:

- Many hotels, restaurants, and shops in Bali cater to foreign tourists, so staff often speak English.
- The Balinese government promotes English education to enhance the island's international competitiveness.
- Many Balinese people believe learning English can help them secure better job opportunities.

Therefore, when traveling in Bali, even if you don’t speak Indonesian, you can usually communicate in English.

People from different countries communicate with each other in English.

## What to Do If You Don’t Speak English Well?

For travelers from non-English-speaking countries, visiting Bali might present some language challenges, but don’t worry! In this article, I’ll share some methods to help you overcome language barriers and make your trip to Bali smoother and more enjoyable.

### Using Translation Apps 🌍 Must-Have Travel Apps for 2026

### 1. Google Translate: The All-Purpose Essential

This app is practically a staple on everyone's phone. Its most powerful feature is the "instant camera translation"—just point your camera at a sign or menu, and the Chinese translation appears directly on the screen.

- **Why we recommend it:** Supports the widest range of languages, and with offline maps paired with offline translation packs, you can survive even without internet.
- **Must-use feature:** Point the camera at text for instant replacement—no need to type a single word.

### 2. VoiceTra (沃译通): Your Travel Companion in Japan

If you're planning a trip to Japan, this app developed by a Japanese government research institute is a must-download. Its voice recognition is extremely accurate and optimized for travel scenarios, producing very natural Japanese translations.

- **Why we recommend it:** Super simple interface with just one microphone button.
- **Highlight:** Displays "reverse translation" so you can confirm if the app misunderstood you, avoiding awkward miscommunications.
- (A little shoutout to Voicetra—it's really handy! Even translating your speech into Indonesian is quite accurate.)

### 3. Naver Papago: The Top Choice for K-Wave Enthusiasts

When traveling in Korea, Google Translate can sometimes feel a bit stiff. That's where Papago comes to the rescue. Developed by Korea's national search engine NAVER, it handles Korean tone and honorifics flawlessly.

- **Why we recommend it:** The most localized app for Korea, with beautifully formatted photo translations.
- **Thoughtful touch:** Built-in categories for common travel conversations (like shopping, hotels, and transportation)—no need to even open your mouth.

### 4. DeepL: The Most Natural AI Translation

If you need to translate lengthy articles or formal conversations (such as discussing business or long-term rental contracts in **Bali**), DeepL's semantic logic is widely recognized as the most 'human-like,' avoiding any awkward machine-like tone.

- **Why We Recommend It:** Extremely high accuracy in semantics, producing very fluent translations.
- **Note:** Better suited for long texts or complex needs, with an interface leaning towards professional use.

## 💡 Tips: How to Use It More Effectively?

- **Download Offline Packs Before Traveling:** Prevent being disconnected in places like airports, mountainous areas, or areas with poor signal.
- **Use Voice Input:** When communicating with locals, simply hold the microphone to speak, then play the translated audio directly for them.

We hope these tools make your journey smoother!

### Familiarize Yourself with Common Travel Scenarios

Before heading to Bali, familiarizing yourself with common travel situations and useful phrases will help you navigate various scenarios, such as seeking help, ordering food, and shopping.

For example:

**Seeking Help:**

1. Excuse me, do you speak English?
2. I'm lost, can you help me?
3. I want to go to XXX, can you tell me how to get there?

**Ordering Food:**

1. I'd like to order a XXX.
2. Can you make it not spicy?

**Shopping:**

1. How much is this?
2. Can you make it cheaper?
3. I'd like to buy this.
