When people think of Bali, they often imagine beautiful beaches and stunning natural landscapes, but Bali's cultural heritage is equally rich! The Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) is the perfect destination to immerse yourself in Bali's traditional culture. As the historical residence of Bali's royal family, this palace exemplifies Balinese architectural styles from the 19th century and preserves the history and artistic traditions of Bali's royalty.

## Location and How to Get to Ubud Royal Palace

The Ubud Royal Palace is located in the heart of Ubud, Bali, at 8 Jalan Raya Ubud, in the central area of Gianyar Regency. It is very conveniently situated. It takes about an hour to drive from Denpasar city center. If using public transportation, you can take the Trans Dewata bus on the K4B route, which passes through several key attractions in Ubud, making it easier for travelers to reach the palace.

## Open Areas and Features of the Palace

It is said that the interior of Ubud Royal Palace is not open to the public.

It's important to note that although the Ubud Royal Palace covers a large area, only a small portion is open to visitors. Guests can mainly explore the outer courtyards and some of the palace buildings. However, even this limited section offers a glimpse into the beauty of traditional Balinese architecture. The intricate carvings and abundant religious and cultural symbols make it a perfect spot for photography enthusiasts to capture Bali's charm.

## Highlight of Ubud Royal Palace: Art Performances

As a paradise for culture enthusiasts, the Ubud Royal Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the mystical 'Kecak Fire Dance.' These performances are among Bali's most iconic artistic events, attracting visitors from around the world. Tickets cost 100K IDR, and the performances usually take place between 7 PM and 8 PM on Thursdays, making it an ideal way to enjoy a visual feast under the sunset. For those looking to delve deeper into Balinese culture, this is a rare and unforgettable experience.

## Visiting Information for Ubud Palace

Ubud Palace does not charge an entrance fee, and visitors can freely explore the open areas. However, tickets are required to enjoy art performances, and it's recommended to arrive early to secure good seats for the entire show. The palace is open daily from 7 AM to 5 PM, providing ample time for visitors to slowly discover this historically rich site.

## Exploring Ubud Palace and Surrounding Attractions: A Perfect Blend of Culture, Shopping, and Nature

Ubud is not only home to the historic palace but also offers numerous nearby attractions to explore, including the bustling Ubud Art Market, the beautiful Saraswati Temple, the trendy shops along Monkey Forest Road, and the nature-rich Monkey Forest. Whether you're immersing yourself in local culture, enjoying shopping, or connecting with Bali's natural ecosystem, your trip to Ubud will be unforgettable!

### 1. Ubud Art Market 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Located opposite Ubud Palace, the Ubud Art Market is a vibrant showcase of traditional Balinese culture. Here, you'll find a wide array of goods, including handmade jewelry, wood carvings, paintings, and traditional clothing, each reflecting the local artistic atmosphere. ### 2. Saraswati Temple (Pura Taman Saraswati) 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

### 3. Monkey Forest Road Shopping (Jalan Monkey Forest) 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Monkey Forest Road is one of Ubud's main shopping streets, lined with unique shops, trendy boutiques, cafes, and restaurants. This street features both local handicraft stores and shops selling Bali's natural skincare products, with items like coconut oil and handmade soap being particularly popular. When you're tired from shopping, you can take a break at one of the street-side cafes and enjoy a cup of rich Balinese coffee.

### 4. Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Nature lovers should not miss the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary! Located in southern Ubud, this forest is home to hundreds of long-tailed macaques, where visitors can observe the monkeys' daily activities up close, adding fun to their journey. The forest also contains ancient stone carvings and temple ruins, adding a mysterious atmosphere to this nature reserve. However, visitors should be especially careful with their belongings here, as the monkeys might try to snatch food! 🐒

## Ubud Palace FAQ

**1. What are the opening hours of Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace opens daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Traditional dance performances are held on specific evenings, usually starting around 7:30 PM.

**2. How much is the entrance fee for Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime admission to Ubud Palace is free. For the evening traditional dance performances, tickets are required and cost approximately IDR 100K (around USD $6). Prices may vary depending on the performance. It is recommended to [book in advance](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=944134&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80603-ubud-kecak-fire-dance-show-tickets-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Da2deaa6927)

**3. Can I access all areas of Ubud Palace?**

- Not all areas are open to the public. Only a small portion of the main buildings and courtyards are accessible to visitors, while other sections are private residences for the royal family and their guests.

**4. What are some nearby attractions worth visiting?**

- There are several popular attractions near Ubud Palace, including Ubud Market, Pura Taman Saraswati (Ubud Water Palace), Monkey Forest Road, and the famous Sacred Monkey Forest. These sites are close to each other, making them ideal for a day trip.

**5. Can I wear shorts and tank tops in Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace does not have strict dress code requirements, but it's recommended to dress neatly and respectfully to honor local culture. When visiting religious sites or temples, modest clothing is usually required, and some locations may provide sarongs for visitors to use.

**6. What types of traditional dance performances are held at the palace?**

- Every Thursday evening, Ubud Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances, including famous dances such as the Barong Dance and Legong Dance. These performances showcase Bali's rich cultural heritage, blending unique music and dance art forms, making them highly worth watching.

**7. Is photography allowed inside Ubud Palace?**

- Photography is permitted in the palace's open courtyard areas. However, it's advised to respect local culture by avoiding the use of flash when photographing performances or religious structures. If local ceremonies or events are taking place, please take photos discreetly as a sign of respect.

**8. What are the transportation options to Ubud Palace?**

- You can choose to rent a scooter or hire a private car. Bali also offers ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek. If you're staying in central Ubud, the palace is within walking distance, making it very convenient.

**9. Are there recommended restaurants or cafes near the palace?**

- There are many well-known restaurants and cafes near Ubud Palace, offering both traditional Balinese cuisine and creative dishes, making them excellent choices for visitors to relax and dine.

**10. Do I need to make a reservation in advance to visit Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime visits don't require reservations, but if you want to watch the evening traditional dance performances, it's recommended to purchase tickets in advance to secure your spot, especially during peak tourist seasons when tickets may sell out quickly.

**11. Does the Balinese royal family still reside in Ubud Palace?**

- Yes, Ubud Palace remains the residence of the Balinese royal family. Although they no longer hold actual political power, the royal family members still live in the palace and play an important role in preserving traditional culture, participating in many religious and cultural activities.
