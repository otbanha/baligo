When people think of Bali, beautiful beaches and stunning natural landscapes come to mind, but the island's cultural richness is equally impressive! Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) is an excellent place to experience traditional Balinese culture. As the historic residence of Bali's royal family, this palace showcases Balinese architectural styles dating back to the 19th century and bears witness to the history and traditional arts of the Balinese royalty.

## Location & How to Get to Ubud Royal Palace

Ubud Royal Palace is located in the heart of Ubud, at No. 8 Jalan Raya Ubud, in the center of Gianyar Regency, making it very convenient to reach. It's about a one-hour drive from downtown Denpasar. If using public transport, you can take the Trans Dewata bus on the K4B route, which passes through several key attractions in Ubud, making it easy for travelers to reach the palace.

The intricate carvings, rich with Balinese religious and cultural symbols, make it a perfect spot for photography enthusiasts to capture the essence of Bali.

## Highlights of Ubud Palace: Art Performances

As a haven for culture lovers, Ubud Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the mystical "Kecak Fire Dance." These performances are among Bali's most iconic artistic events, drawing visitors from around the world. Tickets cost 100K IDR, and the show typically runs from 7:00 PM to 8:00 PM on Thursdays, offering a visual feast best enjoyed under the sunset glow. For travelers eager to delve into Balinese culture, this is an unforgettable experience.

## Visiting Information for Ubud Palace

Ubud Palace does not charge an entrance fee, and visitors can explore the open areas for free. However, tickets are required to enjoy the art performances, and it's recommended to arrive early to secure good seats for the entire show. The palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM, giving visitors ample time to leisurely explore this historically rich venue.

## Ubud Palace FAQ

**1. What are the opening hours of Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Traditional dance performances are held at specific times in the evening, usually starting around 7:30 PM.

**2. How much is the entrance fee for Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime admission to Ubud Palace is free. To watch the evening traditional dance performances, you need to purchase a ticket, which costs around IDR 100K (approximately USD$6). Prices may vary depending on the show. It's recommended to [book in advance](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=944134&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80603-ubud-kecak-fire-dance-show-tickets-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Da2deaa6927).

**3. Can you enter all areas of the Ubud Palace?**

- Not all areas are open to the public. Only a small portion of the main buildings and courtyards are accessible to visitors; the rest are private residences for the royal family and their guests only.

**4. What attractions are worth visiting near Ubud Palace?**

- There are several popular spots near Ubud Palace, including the Ubud Market, Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati), Monkey Forest Road, and the famous Sacred Monkey Forest. These attractions are close to each other, making them ideal for a day trip.

**5. Can I wear shorts and tank tops inside Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace does not have strict dress codes, but it's recommended to dress neatly and respectfully to honor local culture. When visiting religious sites or temples, more modest clothing is usually required, and some places provide sarongs for visitors to use.

**6. What types of traditional dance performances are held at the palace?**

- The dance performances at Ubud Palace every Thursday night include famous Balinese traditional dances such as the Barong Dance and Legong Dance. These performances showcase Bali's rich cultural heritage, blending unique music and dance art, and are well worth watching.

**7. Can I take photos inside Ubud Palace?**

- You can take photos in the public courtyard areas of the palace, but it's recommended to respect local culture by not using flash when photographing performances or religious facilities. If a local ceremony or event is taking place, keep a low profile while shooting to show respect.

**8. What are the ways to get to Ubud Palace?**

- You can rent a scooter or hire a private car. Bali also offers ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek taxis. If you're staying in central Ubud, the palace is within walking distance, making it very convenient.

**9. Are there any recommended restaurants or cafes near the palace?**

- There are many well-known restaurants and cafes near Ubud Palace, offering traditional Balinese cuisine and creative dishes. They are great spots for visitors to rest and dine.

**10. Do I need to book in advance to visit Ubud Palace?**

- No reservation is needed for daytime visits, but if you want to watch the traditional dance performances in the evening, it's recommended to buy tickets in advance to secure a seat, especially during peak tourist season when tickets can sell out quickly.

**11. Does the Balinese royal family still live in Ubud Palace?**

- Yes, Ubud Palace is still the residence of the Balinese royal family. Although they no longer hold actual political power, the royal family members continue to live in the palace and play an important role in preserving traditional culture, participating in many religious and cultural activities.
