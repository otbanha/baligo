When people think of Bali, they often imagine beautiful beaches and stunning natural landscapes, but Bali's cultural heritage is equally rich! Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) is the ideal destination to immerse yourself in Bali's traditional culture. As the historical residence of Bali's royal family, this palace displays Balinese architectural styles from the 19th century and preserves the history and artistic traditions of Bali's royalty.

## Location and How to Get to Ubud Royal Palace

Ubud Royal Palace is located in the heart of Ubud, Bali, at 8 Jalan Raya Ubud, in the central area of Gianyar Regency. It is easily accessible, with a drive from Denpasar city center taking about an hour. For those using public transportation, the Trans Dewata bus, specifically the K4B route, passes through several key attractions in Ubud, making it convenient for travelers to reach the palace.

## Open Areas and Features of the Palace

It is said that the interior of Ubud Royal Palace is not open to the public.

It's important to note that while Ubud Royal Palace covers a vast area, only a small portion is open to visitors. Guests can explore the outer courtyards and some palace buildings. Even this limited access offers a glimpse into the beauty of traditional Balinese architecture. The intricate carvings and abundant religious and cultural symbols make it a photographer's paradise for capturing Bali's essence.

## Highlight of Ubud Royal Palace: Art Performances

As a haven for culture enthusiasts, Ubud Royal Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the mystical Kecak Fire Dance. These performances are among Bali's most iconic artistic events, attracting visitors from around the world. Tickets cost 100,000 IDR, and the performances usually take place between 7 PM and 8 PM on Thursdays, offering a visual feast under the glow of the setting sun. For those looking to delve deeper into Balinese culture, this is an unforgettable experience.

## Visiting Information for Ubud Palace

Ubud Palace does not charge an entrance fee, and visitors can freely explore the open areas. However, tickets are required to watch art performances, and it's recommended to arrive early to secure good seats for the show. The palace is open daily from 7 AM to 5 PM, giving visitors ample time to slowly explore this historically rich site.

## Exploring Ubud Palace and Surrounding Attractions: A Perfect Blend of Culture, Shopping, and Nature

Ubud is not only home to the historic palace but also offers many nearby attractions to explore, including the lively Ubud Art Market, the beautiful Saraswati Temple, the trendy shops along Monkey Forest Road, and the nature-rich Monkey Forest. Whether you're immersing yourself in local culture, enjoying shopping, or connecting with Bali's natural environment, your trip to Ubud will be unforgettable!

### 1. Ubud Art Market 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Located opposite Ubud Palace, Ubud Art Market is a vibrant hub of traditional Balinese culture. Here, you'll find a wide variety of goods, including handmade jewelry, wood carvings, paintings, and traditional clothing, each showcasing the island's rich artistic heritage. Remember to bargain when shopping to snag your favorite souvenirs at a better price!

### 2. Saraswati Temple (Ubud Water Palace) 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Dedicated to the goddess of wisdom, Saraswati, the temple's pink lotus flowers and golden walls create a mesmerizing scene, attracting many visitors for photo opportunities. This serene spot is perfect for slowing down and experiencing Bali's unique spiritual atmosphere.

### 3. Shopping on Monkey Forest Road (Jalan Monkey Forest)🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Monkey Forest Road is one of Ubud's main shopping streets, lined with unique shops, fashionable boutiques, cafes, and restaurants. This street features both local handicraft stores and shops selling Bali's natural skincare products, with coconut oil and handmade soaps being particularly popular. When you're tired from shopping, you can take a break at one of the street-side cafes and enjoy a cup of rich Balinese coffee.

### 4. Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Nature lovers should not miss the Sacred Monkey Forest! Located in southern Ubud, this forest is home to hundreds of long-tailed macaques. Visitors can observe the monkeys' daily activities up close, adding a fun element to their journey. The forest also contains ancient stone carvings and temple ruins, adding a mysterious atmosphere to this nature reserve. However, visitors should be especially careful with their belongings here, as the monkeys are known to snatch food!🐒

## Ubud Palace FAQ

**1. What are the opening hours of Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Traditional dance performances are held on specific evenings, usually starting around 7:30 PM.

**2. How much is the entrance fee to Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime admission to Ubud Palace is free. However, tickets are required for the evening traditional dance performances, costing approximately IDR 100K (around USD$6). Prices may vary depending on the performance. It's recommended to [book in advance](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=944134&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80603-ubud-kecak-fire-dance-show-tickets-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Da2deaa6927)
- What does 100K mean? Check out [this guide](/blog/bali-budget-tips-k-explained/)

**3. Are all areas of Ubud Palace accessible to visitors?**

- Not all areas are open to the public. Only a small portion of the main buildings and courtyards are accessible to tourists, while other sections remain private residences for the royal family and their guests.

**4. What are some nearby attractions worth visiting around Ubud Palace?**

- Several popular attractions near Ubud Palace include Ubud Market, Saraswati Temple (Pura Taman Saraswati), Monkey Forest Road, and the famous Sacred Monkey Forest. These sites are conveniently close, making them ideal for a day trip.

**5. Can I wear shorts and tank tops to Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace does not have strict dress code requirements, but it is recommended to dress neatly and respectfully to honor local culture. When visiting religious sites or temples, modest clothing is usually required, and some locations may provide sarongs for visitors to use.

**6. What types of traditional dance performances are held at the palace?**

- The dance performances at Ubud Palace every Thursday evening include famous traditional Balinese dances such as the Barong Dance and Legong Dance. These performances showcase Bali's rich cultural heritage, blending unique music and dance art, and are highly worth watching.

**7. Can I take photos inside Ubud Palace?**

- Photography is allowed in the palace's open courtyard areas, but it is recommended to respect local culture by avoiding the use of flash when photographing performances or religious facilities. If local ceremonies or events are taking place, please take photos discreetly as a sign of respect.

**8. What are the transportation options to Ubud Palace?**

- You can choose to rent a scooter or hire a private car. Bali also offers ride-hailing services such as Grab or Gojek. If you are staying in central Ubud, the palace is within walking distance, making it very convenient.

**9. Are there recommended restaurants or cafes near the palace?**

- There are many well-known restaurants and cafes near Ubud Palace, offering traditional Balinese cuisine and creative dishes, making them great options for visitors to relax and dine.

**10. Do I need to make a reservation in advance to visit Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime visits don't require reservations, but if you want to watch the evening traditional dance performances, it's recommended to purchase tickets in advance to secure your seat, especially during peak tourist seasons when tickets may sell out quickly.

**11. Does the Balinese royal family still live in Ubud Palace?**

- Yes, Ubud Palace remains the residence of the Balinese royal family. Although they no longer hold actual political power, the royal family members still live in the palace and play an important role in preserving traditional culture, participating in many religious and cultural activities.
