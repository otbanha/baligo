When people think of Bali, the first things that come to mind are its beautiful beaches and stunning natural landscapes. However, Bali's cultural heritage is equally rich! The Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) is the ideal destination to immerse yourself in Bali's traditional culture. As the historical residence of Bali's royal family, this palace exemplifies Balinese architectural styles from the 19th century and preserves the history and artistic traditions of Bali's royalty.

## Location and How to Get to Ubud Royal Palace

The Ubud Royal Palace is located in the heart of Ubud, Bali, at 8 Jalan Raya Ubud, in the central area of Gianyar Regency. It is easily accessible, with a drive of about one hour from Denpasar city center. If using public transportation, you can take the Trans Dewata bus on the K4B route, which passes through several key attractions in Ubud, making it convenient for travelers to reach the palace.

## Open Areas and Features of the Palace

It is said that the interior of Ubud Royal Palace is not open to the public.

It’s important to note that while the Ubud Royal Palace covers a vast area, only a small portion is open to visitors. Guests can mainly explore the outer courtyards and some palace buildings. However, even this limited access offers a glimpse into the beauty of traditional Balinese architecture. The intricate carvings and abundant incorporation of Balinese religious and cultural symbols make this a perfect spot for photography enthusiasts to capture the essence of Bali.

## Highlight of Ubud Royal Palace: Art Performances

As a paradise for culture enthusiasts, the Ubud Royal Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the mystical Kecak Fire Dance. These performances are among Bali's most iconic artistic events, attracting visitors from around the world. Tickets cost 100K IDR, and the shows typically run from 7 PM to 8 PM on Thursdays, making it a perfect way to enjoy a visual feast under the glow of sunset. For those looking to delve deeper into Balinese culture, this is a rare and unforgettable experience.

## Visiting Information for Ubud Palace

Ubud Palace does not charge an entrance fee, and visitors can freely explore the open areas. However, tickets are required to attend art performances, and it is recommended to arrive early to secure a good seat for the entire show. The palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM, giving visitors ample time to slowly explore this historically rich site.

## Exploring Ubud Palace and Surrounding Attractions: A Perfect Blend of Culture, Shopping, and Nature

Ubud is not only home to the historically rich palace but also offers many nearby attractions to explore, including the lively Ubud Art Market, the beautiful Saraswati Temple, the trendy shops along Monkey Forest Road, and the nature-rich Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary. Here, whether you're immersing yourself in local culture, enjoying shopping, or connecting with Bali's natural environment, your Ubud experience will be unforgettable!

### 1. Ubud Art Market 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Located opposite Ubud Palace, Ubud Art Market is a vibrant bazaar filled with traditional Balinese charm. The market offers a wide variety of goods, including handmade jewelry, wood carvings, paintings, and traditional clothing, each showcasing the local artistic flair. Remember to bargain while shopping to take home your favorite souvenirs at a better price!

### 2. Saraswati Temple (Pura Taman Saraswati) 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Commonly known as 'Ubud Water Palace,' Saraswati Temple is just a few minutes' walk from Ubud Palace and is renowned for its beautiful lotus pond and elegant architecture. This temple is dedicated to Saraswati, the goddess of wisdom, and its pink lotus flowers set against golden walls create a captivating scene, attracting many visitors for photo opportunities. It is a serene spot, perfect for slowing down and experiencing Bali's unique spiritual atmosphere.

### 3. Monkey Forest Road Shopping 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Monkey Forest Road is one of Ubud's main shopping streets, lined with unique shops, fashionable boutiques, cafes, and restaurants. The street features both local handicraft stores and shops selling Bali's natural skincare products, with coconut oil and handmade soaps being particularly popular. When you're tired from shopping, you can take a break at one of the street-side cafes and enjoy a cup of rich Balinese coffee.

### 4. Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary 🗺️[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

Nature lovers should not miss the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary! Located in southern Ubud, this forest is home to hundreds of long-tailed macaques, where visitors can observe the monkeys' daily activities up close, adding fun to their journey. The forest also contains ancient stone carvings and temple ruins, adding a mysterious atmosphere to this nature reserve. However, visitors need to be especially careful with their belongings, as the monkeys might try to snatch food! 🐒

## Ubud Palace FAQ

**1. What are the opening hours of Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Traditional dance performances are held at specific times in the evening, usually starting around 7:30 PM.

**2. What is the admission fee for Ubud Palace?**

- Visiting Ubud Palace during the day is free. However, if you wish to attend the traditional dance performance in the evening, you need to purchase tickets, which cost approximately IDR 100K (around USD $3). Prices may vary depending on the performance. It is recommended to [book in advance](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=944134&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80603-ubud-kecak-fire-dance-show-tickets-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Da2deaa6927)
- What does the 'K' in 100K mean? Check out [here](/blog/bali-budget-tips-k-explained/)

**3. Can I access all areas of Ubud Palace?**

- Not all areas are open to the public. Only a small portion of the main buildings and courtyards are accessible to visitors, while other areas are private residences for the royal family and their guests.

**4. What are some nearby attractions worth visiting around Ubud Palace?**

- There are several popular attractions near Ubud Palace, including Ubud Market, Saraswati Temple (Pura Taman Saraswati), Monkey Forest Road, and the famous Sacred Monkey Forest. These attractions are close to each other, making them ideal for a day trip.

**5. Is it acceptable to wear shorts and tank tops when visiting Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace does not enforce strict dress codes, but it is recommended to dress neatly and respectfully to honor local culture. When visiting religious sites or temples, it is usually required to wear more covered clothing, and some locations may provide sarongs for visitors to use.

**6. What types of traditional dance performances are held at the palace?**

- Ubud Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including famous dances such as the Barong Dance and Legong Dance. These performances showcase Bali's rich cultural heritage, blending unique music and dance artistry, making them highly worth watching.

**7. Is photography allowed inside Ubud Palace?**

- Photography is permitted in the palace's open courtyard areas. However, it is advised to respect local culture by avoiding the use of flash when photographing performances or religious structures. If local ceremonies or events are taking place, please take photos discreetly as a sign of respect.

**8. What are the transportation options to Ubud Palace?**

- You can choose to rent a scooter or hire a private car. Bali also offers ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek taxis. If you are staying in central Ubud, the palace is within walking distance, making it very convenient.

**9. Are there any recommended restaurants or cafes near the palace?**

- There are many well-known restaurants and cafes near Ubud Palace, offering traditional Balinese cuisine and creative dishes, making them excellent choices for visitors to relax and dine.

**10. Do I need to book in advance to visit Ubud Palace?**

- No booking is required for daytime visits. However, if you wish to watch the traditional dance performances in the evening, it's recommended to purchase tickets in advance to secure your spot, especially during peak tourist seasons when tickets may sell out quickly.

**11. Does the Balinese royal family still reside in Ubud Palace?**

- Yes, Ubud Palace is still the residence of the Balinese royal family. Although they no longer hold political power, the royal family members continue to live in the palace and play a vital role in preserving traditional culture, participating in numerous religious and cultural events.
