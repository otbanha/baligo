When people think of Bali, beautiful beaches and stunning natural landscapes often come to mind first, but the island's cultural richness is equally impressive! Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) is the ideal spot to immerse yourself in traditional Balinese culture. As the historic home of Bali's royal family, the palace displays Balinese architectural styles from the 19th century and reflects the history and traditional arts of the Balinese monarchy.

## Location and How to Get to Ubud Royal Palace

Ubud Royal Palace is located in the heart of Ubud, at No. 8 Jalan Raya Ubud, in the center of Gianyar Regency, making it very accessible. It takes about an hour by car from downtown Denpasar. For public transport, you can take the Trans Dewata bus on the K4B route, which passes through several key Ubud attractions, making it convenient for travelers to reach the palace.

## Highlight of Ubud Royal Palace: Art Performances

As a paradise for culture lovers, Ubud Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the mysterious 'Kecak Fire Dance.' These performances are among the most iconic artistic events in Bali, drawing visitors from around the world. Tickets cost 100K Indonesian Rupiah, and the event typically runs from 7:00 PM to 8:00 PM every Thursday, offering a visual feast under the sunset glow. For travelers eager to delve into Balinese culture, this is an unforgettable experience.

## Ubud Palace Visitor Information

Ubud Palace does not charge an entrance fee, and visitors can explore the open areas for free. However, tickets are required to enjoy the art performances, and it's recommended to arrive early to secure a good seat for the entire show. The palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM, giving visitors ample time to leisurely explore this historically rich site.

## Ubud Palace FAQ

**1. What are the opening hours of Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Traditional dance performances are held on specific evenings, usually starting around 7:30 PM.

**2. How much is the entrance fee for Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime admission to Ubud Palace is free. For the evening traditional dance performances, you need to purchase a ticket, which costs around IDR 100K (approximately USD$6). Prices may vary depending on the show. **3. Can you enter all areas of Ubud Palace?**

- Not all areas are open to the public. Only a small portion of the main buildings and courtyards are accessible to visitors, while the rest serve as private residences for the royal family and are restricted to them and their guests.

**4. What attractions are worth visiting near Ubud Palace?**

- There are several popular spots near Ubud Palace, including Ubud Market, Pura Taman Saraswati (Ubud Water Palace), Monkey Forest Road, and the famous Sacred Monkey Forest. These attractions are close to each other, making them ideal for a day trip.

**5. Can you wear shorts and tank tops inside Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace does not have strict dress codes, but it's recommended to dress neatly and respectfully to honor local culture. When visiting religious sites or temples, more modest clothing is usually required, and some places provide sarongs for visitors to use.

**6. What types of traditional dance performances are held at the palace?**

- The dance performances at Ubud Palace every Thursday evening feature famous Balinese traditional dances, such as the Barong Dance and Legong Dance. These dances showcase Bali's rich cultural heritage, blending unique music and dance artistry, and are well worth watching.

**7. Can you take photos inside Ubud Palace?**

- You can take photos in the public courtyard areas of the palace, but it's advisable to respect local culture by not using flash when photographing performances or religious facilities. If a local ceremony or event is in progress, shoot discreetly to show respect.

**8. How can I get to Ubud Palace?**

- You can rent a scooter or hire a private car. Ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek taxis are also available in Bali. If you're staying in central Ubud, the palace is within walking distance, making it very convenient.

**9. Are there any recommended restaurants or cafes near the palace?**

- There are many well-known restaurants and cafes near Ubud Palace, offering traditional Balinese cuisine and creative dishes. They are great spots for visitors to rest and dine.

**10. Do I need to book in advance to visit Ubud Palace?**

- No reservation is needed for daytime visits. However, if you want to watch the traditional dance performances in the evening, it's advisable to buy tickets in advance to secure a seat, especially during peak tourist season when tickets can sell out quickly.

**11. Does the Balinese royal family still live in Ubud Palace?**

- Yes, Ubud Palace remains the residence of the Balinese royal family. Although they no longer hold political power, the royal family still lives in the palace and plays an important role in preserving traditional culture, participating in many religious and cultural events.
