**Bottom Line**: Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) is located at No. 8 Jalan Raya Ubud in the city center, showcasing **Balinese architectural styles from the 19th century**. It's about a **1-hour drive** from downtown Denpasar, or you can take the Trans Dewata bus **K4B route**. It's perfect to include as a stop on a sightseeing tour of Ubud city center.

When people think of Bali, beautiful beaches and stunning natural landscapes often come to mind, but the island's cultural richness is equally impressive! Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) is the perfect place to experience traditional Balinese culture. As the historic residence of Bali's royal family, this palace showcases Balinese architectural styles dating back to the 19th century and bears witness to the history and traditional arts of the Balinese royalty.

## Location and How to Get to Ubud Royal Palace

Ubud Royal Palace is located in the heart of Ubud, at No. 8 Jalan Raya Ubud in the Gianyar regency, making it very convenient to reach. It's about a one-hour drive from downtown Denpasar. For public transport, you can take the Trans Dewata bus on the K4B route, which passes through several key Ubud attractions, making it easy for travelers to get to the palace.

## Accessible Areas and Highlights of the Palace

It is said that the interior of Ubud Royal Palace is not open to the public.

It's important to note that while the palace grounds are extensive, only a small portion is open to visitors. As a result, guests can mainly explore the outer courtyards and some of the palace buildings. However, even this limited area is enough to offer a glimpse into the beauty of traditional Balinese architecture. The buildings feature intricate carvings infused with Balinese religious and cultural symbols, making it a fantastic spot for photography enthusiasts to capture the essence of Bali.

## Highlight of Ubud Royal Palace: Art Performances

As a paradise for culture lovers, the Ubud Palace hosts traditional Balinese dance performances every Thursday evening, including the mystical 'Kecak Fire Dance.' These performances are among the most iconic artistic events in Bali, drawing visitors from around the world. Tickets cost 100K Indonesian Rupiah, and the event typically runs from 7:00 PM to 8:00 PM every Thursday, offering a visual feast best enjoyed under the sunset glow. For travelers eager to delve into Balinese culture, this is an unforgettable experience.

## Ubud Palace Visitor Information

Ubud Palace does not charge an entrance fee, and visitors can explore the open areas for free. However, tickets are required to enjoy the art performances, and it's advisable to arrive early to choose a good seat for the entire show. The palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM, giving visitors ample time to leisurely explore this historically rich venue.

## Ubud Palace FAQ

**1. What are the opening hours of Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace is open daily from 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Traditional dance performances are also held at specific times in the evening, starting around 7:30 PM.

**2. How much is the entrance fee to Ubud Palace?**

- Daytime visits to Ubud Palace are free. For the traditional dance performances held in the evening, you need to purchase a ticket, which costs around IDR 100K (approximately USD$6). Prices may vary by performance. It's recommended to book in advance: the grand fire dance show every Thursday can be booked via [Klook](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1336040&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80603-ubud-kecak-fire-dance-show-tickets-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Da2835b3f21); the daily Legong dance performances during peak season at Ubud Palace can be booked via [Trip](https://hk.trip.com/things-to-do/detail/108153384?Allianceid=6817581&SID=311634184&trip_sub1=&trip_sub3=D18620695).

It's recommended to arrive at least 30 minutes early to secure front-row seats or a spot with a good view.

**3. Can you enter all areas of Ubud Palace?**

- Not all areas are open to the public. Only a small portion of the palace's main buildings and courtyards are accessible to visitors; the rest are private residences for the royal family and their guests only.

**4. What attractions are worth visiting near Ubud Palace?**

- There are several popular attractions near Ubud Palace, including Ubud Market, Pura Taman Saraswati (Ubud Water Palace), Monkey Forest Road, and the famous Sacred Monkey Forest. These spots are close to each other and perfect for a day trip.

**5. Can you wear shorts and a tank top inside Ubud Palace?**

- Ubud Palace does not have strict dress code rules, but it's recommended to dress neatly and respectfully to honor local culture. When visiting religious sites or temples, you're usually expected to wear more covering clothing, and some places provide sarongs for visitors.

**6. What types of traditional dance performances are held at the palace?**

- Every Thursday evening, Ubud Palace hosts traditional Balinese dances such as the Barong Dance and Legong Dance. These performances showcase Bali's rich cultural heritage, blending unique music and dance artistry, and are well worth watching.

**7. Can I take photos inside Ubud Palace?**

- You can take photos in the palace's open courtyard areas, but it's advisable to respect local culture by not using flash during performances or near religious structures. If a local ceremony or event is taking place, keep a low profile when taking photos to show respect.

**8. What transportation options are available to get to Ubud Palace?**

- You can rent a scooter or hire a private car. Ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek are also available in Bali. If you're staying in central Ubud, the palace is within walking distance, making it very convenient.

**9. Are there any recommended restaurants or cafes near the palace?**

- There are many well-known restaurants and cafes near Ubud Palace, offering traditional Balinese cuisine and creative dishes. They are great spots for visitors to rest and enjoy a meal.

**10. Do I need to book in advance to visit Ubud Palace?**

- No reservation is needed for daytime visits. However, if you want to watch the evening traditional dance performances, it's recommended to buy tickets in advance to secure a seat, especially during peak tourist season when tickets can sell out quickly.

**11. Are there still Balinese royals living inside the Ubud Palace?**

- Yes, the Ubud Palace remains the residence of the Balinese royal family. Although they no longer hold actual political power, the royal family still lives within the palace and plays an important role in preserving traditional culture, participating in many religious and cultural ceremonies.
