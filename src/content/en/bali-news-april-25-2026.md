## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Disaster Recovery and Conservation Efforts

![今日要聞：災後重建與保育行動](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1777039875036-club-med-bali-nusa-dua-terbakar_01-696x464.jpg)

- **Club Med Bali Fire Update and Operations**: A fire broke out yesterday at Club Med Bali in Nusa Dua.
Cause: Preliminary investigations suggest the fire originated from a deep fryer in the kitchen. Staff were preparing breakfast when the oil overheated and ignited. Despite attempts to extinguish it with fire extinguishers, the flames quickly spread to the wooden ceiling and thatched roof (Sirap).
Casualties: According to the latest statements from ITDC and local police, there were no injuries or fatalities.
Damage: The fire destroyed approximately 5,000 square meters of a two-story building, including the Agung restaurant on the second floor, as well as a boutique, meeting rooms, and the accounting office on the first floor.
- **'Row for R.O.L.E 5.0' Charity Rowing Race**: This morning (April 25), a large charity rowing event is taking place in the waters between **Sanur and Nusa Dua**. The event aims to raise funds for the Zero Waste Center and promote marine ecosystem conservation. Due to the race, there will be a higher concentration of kayaks in the southern waters this morning; please exercise caution during water activities.
- **2026 Tourism Goal: 'Quality Over Quantity'**: The Bali Tourism Board officially announced today that the international tourist target for 2026 is set at **6.63 million arrivals**. Officials stated they will reduce reliance on low-cost tour groups and shift focus toward promoting high-end cultural and in-depth nature tourism. Specific strategies will be unveiled at next week's travel expo.

## 📄 Tourist Concerns: Weekend Crowds and Entry Reminders

- **Seminyak Weekend Party Alert**: Today marks the 13th anniversary of Motel Mexicola, a landmark Mexican restaurant in Seminyak. A large influx of people is expected in the area starting in the evening. Travelers planning to dine nearby are advised to leave an hour earlier to avoid getting stuck in traffic.
- **Flight and Entry Status**: All flights at Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) are **operating normally today**. Recent entry queues have been heavy. Immigration authorities remind travelers that checks for the **IDR 150,000 tourism tax** have been intensified. Please ensure you complete payment via 'Love Bali' before arrival and save the QR code to avoid issues downloading it at the airport due to poor signal.
- **Preventive Hotel Safety Inspections**: Following the Club Med fire incident, several high-end hotels in the Nusa Dua area are stepping up internal fire safety inspections. Some areas may see brief drills or security personnel moving about, but this will not affect guest stays.
