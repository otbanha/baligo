### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Post-Disaster Recovery and Conservation Efforts

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1777039875036-club-med-bali-nusa-dua-terbakar_01-696x464.jpg)

- **Club Med Bali Fire Aftermath and Operations**: A fire broke out yesterday at Club Med Bali in Nusa Dua.
Cause of the Fire: Preliminary investigations suggest the fire originated from a deep fryer in the kitchen. Staff were preparing breakfast when the oil overheated and ignited. Although employees attempted to extinguish the flames with fire extinguishers, the fire quickly spread to the wooden ceiling and thatched roof (Sirap).
Casualties: According to the latest statements from ITDC and local police, there were no casualties.
Extent of Damage: The fire destroyed approximately 5,000 square meters of a two-story building, including the Agung restaurant on the second floor, as well as the boutique, meeting rooms, and accounting office on the first floor.
- **"Row for R.O.L.E 5.0" Charity Rowing Event**: This morning (4/25), a large charity rowing event took place in the waters from **Sanur to Nusa Dua**. The event aims to raise funds for the Zero Waste Center and increase awareness of marine conservation. Due to the event, there was a higher concentration of canoes in the southern waters this morning; please be cautious when engaging in water activities.
- **2026 Tourism Goal "Quality Over Quantity"**: The Bali Tourism Board officially announced today that the target for international visitors in 2026 is set at **6.63 million**. The authorities plan to reduce reliance on budget tours and instead promote high-end cultural and nature-focused tourism. Specific strategies will be unveiled at next week's tourism exhibition.

### 📄 Visitor Concerns: Weekend Crowds and Entry Reminders

- **Seminyak Weekend Party Alert**: Today marks the 13th anniversary celebration of the iconic Mexican restaurant Motel Mexicola in Seminyak. Expect large crowds in the area starting from the evening. Visitors planning to dine nearby are advised to leave an hour early to avoid traffic congestion.
- **Aviation and Entry Status**: Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) is operating **normally** today. With increased inbound traffic, the immigration department reminds travelers that the **IDR 150,000 tourism tax** verification rate has been heightened. Ensure you complete the payment via "Love Bali" before arrival and save the QR Code to avoid issues downloading it at the airport due to poor signal.
- **Hotel Preventive Safety Checks**: In light of the Club Med fire incident, several high-end hotels in the Nusa Dua area are currently enhancing their internal fire safety inspections. There may be brief drills or safety personnel moving around certain areas, but this will not affect guest accommodations.
