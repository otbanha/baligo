## 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Post-Disaster Recovery and Conservation Efforts

![Today's Highlights: Post-Disaster Recovery and Conservation ](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1777039875036-club-med-bali-nusa-dua-terbakar_01-696x464.jpg)

- **Club Med Bali Fire Aftermath and Operations**: Located in Nusa Dua, Club Med Bali experienced a fire yesterday.
Cause of the fire: Preliminary investigations suggest the source may have been a deep fryer in the kitchen. Staff were preparing breakfast when the oil overheated and ignited. Although employees attempted to extinguish the flames with fire extinguishers, the fire quickly spread to the wooden ceiling and thatched roof (Sirap).
Casualties: According to the latest statements from ITDC and local police, there were no injuries.
Extent of damage: The fire destroyed approximately 5,000 square meters of a two-story building, including the Agung restaurant on the second floor, as well as the boutique, meeting rooms, and accounting office on the first floor.
- **"Row for R.O.L.E 5.0" Charity Rowing Event**: This morning (4/25), a large charity rowing event took place in the waters from **Sanur to Nusa Dua**. The event aims to raise funds for the Zero Waste Center and increase awareness of marine conservation. Due to the event, there was a higher concentration of canoes in the southern waters this morning; please be cautious when engaging in water activities.
- **2026 Tourism Goal: "Quality Over Quantity"**: The Bali Tourism Board officially announced today that the target for international visitors in 2026 is set at **6.63 million**. Officials stated they will reduce reliance on low-cost tour groups and shift focus toward promoting high-end cultural and nature-based tourism. Specific strategies will be unveiled at next week's tourism expo.

## 📄 Visitor Concerns: Weekend Crowds and Entry Reminders

- **Seminyak Weekend Party Alert**: Today marks the 13th anniversary celebration of the iconic Mexican restaurant Motel Mexicola in Seminyak. A large influx of visitors is expected in the area from the evening. Those planning to dine nearby are advised to depart an hour earlier to avoid traffic congestion.
- **Flight and Entry Status**: Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) is operating **normally** today. Due to recent high entry volumes, the immigration department has increased checks for the **IDR 150,000 tourism tax**. Visitors are reminded to complete payment via "Love Bali" before arrival and save the QR Code to avoid issues downloading it at the airport due to poor signal.
- **Hotel Preventive Safety Inspections**: Following the Club Med fire incident, several high-end hotels in the Nusa Dua area are currently enhancing their internal fire safety inspections. Brief drills or safety personnel movements may occur in some areas, but guest accommodations will not be affected.
