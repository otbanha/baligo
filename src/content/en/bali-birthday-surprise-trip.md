## **September 5: Spectacular Nusa Penida Day Trip and Snorkeling Experience**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_732ed238988d475c0faa6b704e8e9383.jpg)

> Editor’s Note: Siobak: A Balinese Dish from Singaraja

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5c5fd5848222adbc7cbc5b7ebac95bdf.jpg)

Siobak is a traditional dish from Singaraja, made with pork. Its name originates from Chinese culture (siu yuk, meaning roasted pork), perfectly blending local and Chinese flavors. Since Hinduism is prevalent in Bali, where beef is considered sacred, pork has become a popular choice.

The dish is seasoned with spices, and the pork is boiled at a low temperature to remove any gaminess. It’s then mixed with cloves, shallots, garlic, and other spices, creating a unique flavor.

You can find this dish in Singaraja or at specific restaurants in Denpasar. If you have the chance to visit Bali, don’t miss out on trying the distinctive taste of Siobak!

We followed the crowd as we walked closer, with the sea breeze gently brushing against us and the waves softly lapping at the rocks, splashing water everywhere. The majestic and sacred scene left us breathless. Despite the number of tourists, the mysterious atmosphere of Tanah Lot still filled us with awe. ## **September 10: GWK Cultural Park & Kecak Fire Dance Tour**

For lunch, we chose **Waroeng De Dusun**, recommended by our driver. This restaurant serves delicious and affordable Chinese-Indonesian fusion cuisine. It definitely didn't disappoint - the flavors were refreshing and satisfying.

In the afternoon, we drove to **Uluwatu Temple**. Be careful of the monkeys along the way! These clever and mischievous creatures are known to steal (or swiftly snatch) tourists' sunglasses, hats, and even hand sanitizer! We witnessed monkeys 'unlocking' people's belongings and happily chewing on them, requiring staff to 'redeem' the items with food. It's best to leave valuables in the car to avoid trouble.

As evening fell, we found seats to watch the famous **Kecak Fire Dance**. This performance combines Balinese mythology with traditional dance, presented on a semicircular tiered stage. The visual effect of being surrounded by fire dancers is truly mesmerizing. The entire performance has no musical accompaniment, only dozens of dancers chanting the powerful 'Kecak' rhythm, creating a captivating background sound. This unique performance completely immerses the audience. At the end of the show, a spectator proposed to his lover amidst the flames, blending this surprise proposal with the classic performance, creating a memorable moment in our journey.

## **Important PS: Arriving in Bali – From Smooth Customs to Luggage Troubles**

One thing worth mentioning is that our arrival in Bali initially seemed very smooth. We had already applied for our visas online and paid the tourism tax beforehand, and the customs process was surprisingly quick and efficient. Aside from a slight delay of 3 to 5 minutes during the new health declaration step, the overall procedure was straightforward and reassuring. However, the first hiccup of our journey quietly unfolded at the baggage carousel.

At the time, like other passengers, we anxiously waited for our luggage. Nearly an hour later, we finally saw our suitcase slowly sliding down the conveyor belt. When I spotted my luggage, I quickly grabbed it. Everything seemed fine on the surface, so we left the airport without a second thought. However, upon arriving at the hotel and opening the suitcase, I discovered that the zipper had been pried open, and the TSA lock was destroyed. A small amount of loose change and a gold-colored trinket (though not real gold) were missing. Fortunately, important documents and credit cards were still intact, which gave me some relief. Still, I learned a valuable lesson from this experience: always carry valuables with you!

In the end, we received no substantial help.

Although we ultimately decided against reporting it due to time constraints, Dwik’s sincerity and responsible attitude deeply moved us. He even stayed up all night, continuously discussing the matter with fellow drivers and contacting the travel company to see if similar incidents had occurred. His efforts made us feel supported and cared for in an unfamiliar environment.
