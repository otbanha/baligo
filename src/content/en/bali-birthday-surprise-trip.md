This trip to Bali was originally meant to celebrate my girlfriend's birthday, and also to take a brief escape from my busy expat work in Vietnam, giving us both a chance to catch our breath. Lately, we've both been overwhelmed by work stress, and when we spotted a deal from VietJet Air, we decisively booked round-trip tickets from Ho Chi Minh City to Bali, seizing the chance to unwind a little. This time, we chose tickets with 20 kg of checked luggage and also upgraded to emergency exit row seats, which came out to less than USD$200 per person for the airfare alone—an incredible value!

(A quick tip: At the time of writing, VietJet Air still has plenty of zero-fare ticket promotions, so interested folks should keep an eye out!)

On departure day, we arrived at the airport smoothly and completed check-in. VietJet Air's emergency exit row seats are truly worth recommending—the legroom is so spacious you can fully stretch your legs, and the seatbacks recline, making the entire flight experience noticeably more comfortable! However, there was a small hiccup along the way: the passenger behind me was a tall Western man whose knees kept bumping into my seatback due to his cramped space, which was a bit uncomfortable for me. But aside from that little issue, the overall flight was still enjoyable.

(VietJet Air pro tip: Choose row 11 seats, and you'll be able to stretch your legs straight without bumping into the person in front!)

## **September 4: Welcome flower garlands and a first taste of spa massage**

As soon as we stepped out of Bali's airport, our private driver Dwik warmly greeted us with welcome flower garlands and thoughtfully handed us cold water, instantly making us feel cool and refreshed! One of the reasons we chose Dwik was that he drives a 2024 Toyota new SUV—spacious and quiet inside, making it especially relaxing for a quick nap on the go. Dwik speaks fluent American English, and when we asked, we learned he used to work on cruise ships between the US and Mexico, which explains his strong service mindset.

Afterward, we headed to a nearby currency exchange point to get local cash, where the rate was much better than at the airport. Then, on Dwik's recommendation, we visited **Calma Spa** for a two-hour spa relaxation session, and through Dwik, we even got a better price than the menu listed. This spa experience was truly relaxing, leaving us completely soothed from head to toe.

## **September 5: Spectacular Nusa Penida Day Trip & Snorkeling**

![9月5日：壯觀的Nusa Penida一日遊與浮潛體驗](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_732ed238988d475c0faa6b704e8e9383.jpg)

Today's highlight was a day trip to **Nusa Penida**. We set off early, and our private driver even arranged for his girlfriend Indah to join as an assistant guide. She accompanied us throughout, helping pick good seats on the boat, taking photos, and looking after our belongings. On Nusa Penida, we visited stunning spots like **Broken Beach** and **Kelingking Beach**, where the crystal-clear turquoise water sparkled like sapphires—a truly breathtaking sight.

The snorkeling that followed was an unforgettable experience. We visited three snorkeling spots and marveled at the diverse marine life. Although we didn't spot manta rays at Manta Bay and got a slight sting from a small jellyfish, this unique experience became another memorable part of the trip. Even more surprising, we encountered a pod of dolphins leaping near our boat, saving us a planned trip to North Bali to see them—a truly unexpected gift.

This Nusa Penida trip, from its stunning landscapes to the underwater wonders, made us truly appreciate Bali's natural beauty.

> Editor’s note: Siobak: A Balinese specialty from Singaraja

![9月6日：火山日出與豬肉料理之旅](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5c5fd5848222adbc7cbc5b7ebac95bdf.jpg)

Siobak is an authentic dish from Singaraja, made from pork. Its name traces back to Chinese culture (siu yuk, or roasted pork), blending local and Chinese flavors seamlessly. Since Hinduism is widely practiced here, beef is considered sacred, making pork a popular choice.

The meat is seasoned with spices, boiled at low heat to remove any gamey taste, then mixed with cloves, shallots, garlic, and other aromatics for a unique flavor profile.

You can find this dish in Singaraja or at select restaurants in Denpasar. If you ever visit Bali, be sure to try Siobak’s distinctive taste!

Early in the morning, the staff at Ubud Element specially prepared a birthday cake and softly sang the birthday song, giving us an incredibly heartwarming surprise in the Bali dawn. After breakfast, we took a short rest and then went to see the **Tegalalang Rice Terraces**. The terraces were beautifully arranged like a painting. Despite the crowds and the rule that no water bottles are allowed inside, the entire landscape was still a perfect spot for taking stunning photos.

Next, we headed to Bali's famous sea temple, **Tanah Lot**. Perched on a rocky islet in the ocean, this sacred temple can only be reached during low tide. We joined the crowd and walked closer, with the sea breeze gently brushing against us and waves lapping softly at the rocks, sending up sprays of water. The majestic and holy sight left us breathless. Despite the many tourists, the temple's mystical atmosphere still inspired awe. We approached the shrine, lightly touched some holy water, and prayed for peace and blessings.

In the evening, we arranged a special birthday dinner for her at **Cuca Restaurant**. Located in Jimbaran, the restaurant offered flickering candlelight and a lovely night view. We enjoyed creative tapas-style dishes. The staff thoughtfully prepared a small birthday gift, making the atmosphere both romantic and warm. We specially ordered a set meal called "Nicholas Tse's Favorite" from the menu, thinking it would be cool—but well, let's just keep it mysterious (laughs). It was one of those experiences you know! ## **September 10: GWK Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park & Kecak Fire Dance Tour**

![（張家偉提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1033d331528078fc690724949582df98.jpg)

(Provided by Zhang Jiawei)

As the trip neared its end, the surprises kept coming. We headed to the **GWK (Garuda Wisnu Kencana) Cultural Park** in the morning. This park is famous not only for its giant Garuda statue but also for its rich lineup of traditional Balinese dance performances. Almost every time slot features a different dance show, showcasing the beauty of Balinese culture. We watched two or three performances in a row, and before each one, we used ChatGPT to translate and read the descriptions, which helped us better understand the mythological stories behind the dances—adding a lot to the enjoyment.

For lunch, we chose **Waroeng De Dusun**, recommended by our driver. This restaurant serves delicious and affordable Indonesian-style Chinese cuisine—no regrets here, with light and satisfying flavors.

In the afternoon, we drove to **Uluwatu Temple**. Watch out for the monkeys along the way! These monkeys are famously clever and sneaky, known for snatching (or swiftly grabbing) sunglasses, hats, and even hand sanitizer from tourists. We saw one monkey "unlock" a visitor's belongings and munch on them with relish, only to have staff trade food to get them back. So, it's best to leave valuables in the car to avoid trouble.

![（張家偉提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_430945d058d78bbef4733cf6ac83c202.jpg)

(Photo by Jiawei Zhang)

As dusk fell, we found seats to watch the renowned **Kecak Fire Dance**. This performance blends Balinese mythology with traditional dance, staged on a semicircular tiered platform. The visual impact of being surrounded by fire is breathtaking. There are no musical instruments—just dozens of dancers chanting a deep, rhythmic "kecak-kecak" in a vocal tradition passed down orally, creating a mesmerizing backdrop. As the show neared its end, a spectator proposed to their partner amidst the flames, blending this surprise with the classic performance into a delightful highlight of the trip.

## **【Important PS】Arriving in Bali: From Smooth Customs to Luggage Troubles**

I have to mention that after landing in Bali, everything seemed to go smoothly at first. We had already applied for our visas online and paid the tourist tax, so passing through customs was surprisingly quick and easy. Apart from a slight delay of 3 to 5 minutes at the new health declaration checkpoint, the whole process was straightforward and reassuring. However, the first hiccup of our trip quietly unfolded at the baggage carousel.

At the time, like other travelers, we anxiously waited for our luggage. Nearly an hour later, our suitcases finally emerged slowly from the conveyor belt. When I spotted mine, I quickly grabbed it—everything looked fine on the surface, so I headed straight out of the airport. But when we arrived at the hotel and I opened the suitcase, I discovered the zipper had been pried open and even the TSA lock was broken. A small amount of loose change and a tiny gold trinket (not real gold, thankfully) were missing. Luckily, all important documents and credit cards were still there, which was a relief. Still, this experience taught me a lesson: always keep valuables on you!

### **Seeking Help and Kindness: Driver Dwik's Warm Support**

After discovering the tampered luggage, I immediately contacted the Taiwanese office in Surabaya, the airport ground handling company, and Vietjet Air's customer service. All three kept passing the buck, leaving us exhausted. The Taiwanese office suggested we talk to the ground handling company, which then shifted the blame to the airline's customer service—round and round with no real help in sight.

That's when our private driver, Dwik, showed incredible warmth and kindness. Upon hearing what happened, he felt deeply sorry, almost as if he had failed to look after our belongings. Dwik proactively noted the time of the incident and prepared to file a police report. Although we ultimately decided not to go to the police due to time constraints, his sincerity and sense of responsibility touched us deeply. He even stayed up all night, chatting with fellow drivers and contacting travel agencies to see if similar incidents had occurred, making us feel supported and cared for in an unfamiliar place.
