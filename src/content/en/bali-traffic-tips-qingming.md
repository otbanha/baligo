During Taiwan's Qingming holiday in April, taking just 3 days off can give you a long 9-day vacation. Many plan to travel abroad during this time, but Indonesia/Bali might not be the best destination for the 2025 Qingming holiday!

This is because nearly 90% of Indonesia's population practices Islam. Among all Islamic holidays, Eid al-Fitr is as significant as our Lunar New Year. According to the Islamic calendar, **Eid al-Fitr in 2025 coincides exactly with our Qingming holiday**

Following Nyepi Day on March 29, 2025, Eid al-Fitr begins. The Eid holiday starts in late March and lasts for a full week. As Indonesia's most important holiday, while it celebrates the end of Ramadan, it's also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, much like our Chinese Lunar New Year

Based on past experience, Bali will likely start seeing an influx of Indonesians from various regions starting March 24, with crowds lasting about two weeks. This marks **the first peak season among Bali's five major peak periods** - a time we recommend avoiding for your Bali trip!

During the more than two weeks of Eid al-Fitr in 2023, Bali's airport handled 1.17 million passengers and 7,554 flights. For reference, Bali's total annual visitor count is over 6 million. You can imagine that during this period, it's not just traffic that's congested - even people might be packed!

If you're certain this is the only time you can visit Bali, or if you've already booked your tickets and must go, carefully follow these guidelines to avoid unnecessary disruptions to your trip:

## **Flights and Accommodation**

Due to the high demand for accommodations during the annual Eid al-Fitr holiday in Bali, prices for flights and hotels tend to rise. It is recommended to book in advance to secure better prices and options.

## **Visa**

If you hold a Republic of China (Taiwan) passport, you can apply for a visa on arrival, which costs 500,000 Indonesian Rupiah (approximately USD$31).

For those planning to apply for an electronic visa, please apply early! During the Eid al-Fitr holiday, which lasts about nine days, the Indonesian immigration office usually closes online applications for electronic visas. This is because government offices are also on a long holiday, and everyone can only apply for visas on arrival, potentially leading to long queues! Those who have already obtained an electronic visa will not be affected and can still enjoy smooth automatic clearance.

## Transportation

Will Bali experience heavy traffic during the Qingming Festival and Eid al-Fitr? Should I visit tourist spots?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9749c24c3bb6a64ec5579ecf7b89dc27.png)

Here are some specific suggestions for navigating Bali's traffic during this special period:

**Plan your route in advance**

> Try to avoid visiting famous attractions as traffic congestion could be severe! If you still want to go, it's recommended to use Google Maps or other map apps to plan your route before departure. You can check traffic conditions and choose the optimal route.

**Allow ample time**

> During the Qingming Festival and Eid al-Fitr, traffic will be more congested, so you need to allocate sufficient time. When using Google Maps to estimate travel time, it is recommended to add a 50% buffer during normal times (if Google Maps shows one hour, estimate one and a half hours); during the Qingming holiday, add at least double the time.

**Avoid traveling during peak hours**

> Bali's traffic peak usually starts around noon and continues into the evening. If you can avoid traveling during these times, you can reduce the likelihood of encountering traffic congestion.

**Opt for Private Car Hire**

> The best mode of transportation in Bali is hiring a private car. For more details, check out the '[Complete Guide to Bali Private Car Hire - Costs, Booking Methods, Advantages Analysis](/blog/bali-private-car-hire-guide/)'.

## **Beware of the Rainy Season**

The Qingming Festival-Eid al-Fitr period falls at the end of Bali's rainy season, where it might not rain at all or rain continuously for several days. When visiting Bali during the rainy season, always have a backup plan for sudden rain.
