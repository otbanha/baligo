During Taiwan's Qingming holiday in April, taking 3 days off can give you a 9-day vacation. Many plan to travel abroad during this time, but Indonesia/Bali might not be the best destination for the 2025 Qingming holiday!

This is because nearly 90% of Indonesia's population practices Islam. Among all Islamic holidays, Eid al-Fitr is as significant as our Lunar New Year. According to the Islamic calendar, **Eid al-Fitr in 2025 coincides with our Qingming holiday**.

Following Nyepi Day on March 29, 2025, Eid al-Fitr begins. The Eid holiday starts at the end of March and lasts for a whole week. While Eid celebrates the end of Ramadan, it's also a time for Indonesians to gather with family and friends, much like our Lunar New Year.

Based on past experience, Bali will likely see an influx of Indonesians starting March 24, with crowds lasting about two weeks. This marks **the first of Bali's five peak seasons**, a period we recommend avoiding for your Bali trip!

During the more than two weeks of Eid al-Fitr in 2023, Bali Airport handled 1.17 million passengers and 7,554 flights. For reference, Bali's annual tourist count is over 6 million. You can imagine not just traffic jams, but possibly even 'people jams' during this period!

If you must visit Bali during this time or have already booked your tickets, please carefully follow these guidelines to minimize disruptions to your trip:

## **Flights and Accommodation**

Due to the high demand for accommodations during Eid al-Fitr in Bali each year, both airfare and hotel prices tend to rise. It is advisable to book in advance to secure better prices and options.

## **Visa**

If you hold a Republic of China (Taiwan) passport, you can apply for a visa on arrival, which costs IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$31).

For those planning to apply for an e-visa, please do so early! During the Eid al-Fitr holiday period, which lasts about nine days, the Indonesian immigration office typically closes the online e-visa application system. This is because government offices are also on a long holiday, and everyone can only apply for a visa on arrival, potentially leading to long queues! Those who have already obtained an e-visa will not be affected and can still enjoy smooth automatic clearance.

## Transportation

Will Bali experience heavy traffic during the Qingming Festival-Eid al-Fitr period? Should I visit tourist spots?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9749c24c3bb6a64ec5579ecf7b89dc27.png)

Here are some specific suggestions for navigating Bali's traffic during this special period:

**Plan your route in advance**

> Try to avoid visiting famous attractions as traffic congestion could be severe! If you still want to go, we recommend using Google Maps or other map apps to plan your route before departure. You can check traffic conditions and choose the optimal route.

**Allow ample time**

> During the Qingming Festival-Eid al-Fitr period, traffic will be more congested, so you need to allow ample time. When using Google Maps to estimate travel time, it is recommended to add an extra 50% buffer time under normal circumstances (if Google Maps shows one hour, plan for one and a half hours); during the Qingming holiday period, add at least double the time.

**Avoid traveling during peak hours**

> Bali's traffic peaks usually start around midday and continue into the evening. If you can avoid traveling during these times, you are less likely to encounter traffic congestion.

> The best mode of transportation in Bali is a private car. For more details, check out "[Complete Guide to Bali Private Car Tours - Costs, Booking Methods, Advantages Analysis](/blog/bali-private-car-hire-guide/)".
