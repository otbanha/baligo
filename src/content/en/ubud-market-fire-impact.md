On the afternoon of August 17, a fire broke out in the basement of Ubud Market, lasting from noon until evening without being extinguished. This fire has left 1,033 vendors unable to continue their business.

The fire began to spread to the market basement at 13:00 local time, and by evening, more than ten fire trucks had been dispatched to the scene, yet the fire remained uncontrollable.

Gianyar Regional Secretary I Dewa Gede Alit Mudiarta personally inspected the firefighting process on-site. He stated, "We are doing our utmost to extinguish the fire, prioritizing the prevention of loss of life, followed by addressing the vendors' merchandise losses. As the firefighting is still ongoing, we cannot yet determine the specific extent of the damage."

According to available data, there are a total of 1,033 vendors in the market, with approximately 400 operating in the basement (the origin of the fire), who are the directly affected vendors.

Dewa Alit added, "Regarding the relocation and other issues concerning the vendors, we will discuss them in a meeting. Currently, our focus is on extinguishing the fire as quickly as possible to prevent it from spreading to other areas."

As of now, the cause of the fire is still under investigation. It is reported that the fire may have originated from the motorcycle parking area in the basement.
