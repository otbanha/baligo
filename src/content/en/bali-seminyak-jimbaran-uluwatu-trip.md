**Bottom line**: Skip Ubud and follow this refined route—**Seminyak (stay + food) → Canggu's Finns Beach Club → Tanah Lot Temple (sunset) → Jimbaran/Uluwatu**—and you'll still have an incredible time. Key highlights include The Citta Luxury Residence (a pool villa in Seminyak), WAHAHA's pork ribs (salty, so don't overdo it), and visiting Tanah Lot Temple at low tide to walk the rocky path.

## The Citta Luxury Residence

As soon as we arrived in Bali, we headed straight to this stunning accommodation in Seminyak—The Citta Luxury Residence. The space is incredibly spacious, clean, bright, and filled with natural light. The pool is surrounded by beautiful frangipani trees, making you feel like you're in paradise.

![林彥瑋提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_edf58f2a40844210086a19a06c06b3c9.jpg)

Provided by Yanwei Lin

How can you visit Bali without staying in a villa like this? The staff were incredibly thoughtful and attentive, making us feel right at home. The floor-to-ceiling glass doors open directly to the pool—a true luxury.

After dinner out, we returned to find the staff had thoughtfully lit mosquito coils, ensuring a peaceful night's sleep. This is a place I'll never forget, and I'd genuinely love to return ❤️.

## WAHAHA Grilled Ribs

WAHAHA is a well-known grilled rib restaurant in Bali. While the sauce pairing isn't particularly impressive, the meat is quite tender. Due to the climate, the flavors lean salty—worth a try, but if you overdo it, you might wake up looking like a pig the next day 😂.

## Finns Beach Club

![Finns Beach Club](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ec287902e4ed7c7e446d1ab5965f3b76.jpg)

Finns Beach Club

Located in Bali's most famous Canggu area, Finns Beach Club sits in one of Bali's trendiest districts. It's packed with pools, beaches, and bars, offering a mix of music, restaurants, and food. You can jog, lounge all day, and even admire muscular guys and bikini-clad beauties—truly a great spot.

But that's what I thought (ahem), only to find a bunch of uncles and older guys on the prowl, along with some girls in barely-there bikinis or with lips plumper than Kim Kardashian's, swaying around. I ended up sitting by the beach, sipping a cold beer and nibbling on some snacks, calling it a day (I'm too old for this 🚬).

## Pura Tanah Lot

![林彥瑋提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_39c3d1b80460e3a16f0d50ba87211547.jpg)

Photo by Lin Yanwei

The rock of Tanah Lot Temple only reveals a path connecting to the mainland during low tide. The cave beneath the temple is said to be home to sea snakes, the temple's guardians. The sunset terrace next to the temple is a prime spot for watching the sunset.

The weather was absolutely perfect that day, and we were lucky enough to walk out to Tanah Lot after the tide receded, strolling along while soaking in the stunning sunset. The scenery here is breathtaking from every angle, truly worth visiting again and again.

## Odysseys Surf School

Surfing was hands down the craziest yet most rewarding experience of this trip. I took a two-hour lesson at Odysseys Surf School, with one instructor patiently guiding me the entire time.

![林彥瑋提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d2b6fff6e58831ef52a3f8832240efbf.jpg)

Provided by Lin Yanwei

For beginners, it's easy to pick up quickly. The shop thoughtfully provides surfboards, leg ropes, and wetsuits, and the changing rooms are spacious and clean, with lockers, showers, and fresh towels—so girls have nothing to worry about.

Best of all, their photos are absolutely stunning, gorgeous, and amazing—no need to fret about capturing memories. This school truly takes care of every concern for women, and I highly recommend it 👍🏻👍🏻👍🏻.

During the lesson, the most frequent commands I heard were:

- 🤦🏽‍♂️ Don't keep staring down at the sea, look ahead!
- 🤦🏽‍♂️ Go go go go, stand up now!
- 🤦🏽‍♂️ Paddle hard!

Every time I stood up, I could feel the instructor pushing hard from behind, and all that effort finally paid off. Even though I ended up bruised all over and my skin got super dark, this surfing experience was way more fun than 100%.

If you're planning to surf in Bali, you must find Odysseys Surf School - Bali!

## Ku De Ta Bali

![Ku De Ta Bali](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ab0a3a5ac054d66ee5265ded695c92bc.jpg)

Ku De Ta Bali is crowned one of Bali's most beautiful and chill beach bars. It's huge, perched right by the sea, letting you soak up the ocean breeze and listen to the waves while enjoying fine drinks and food—truly a life pleasure.

It offers indoor, outdoor, and beachfront areas, serving dinner, snacks, and a variety of drinks that rival five-star hotels. Ku De Ta is located in a lively area full of restaurants, bars, nightclubs, and shops—you can wander around shopping, then come here to chill with a drink.

Although the price is slightly higher than other places, it is absolutely worth it. Travelers to Bali must come here to sit and soak in its unique charm.

## Uluwatu Temple

![林彥瑋提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7a12e9e42959f6278b3e29a31a1d34b6.jpg)

Provided by Lin Yanwei

Uluwatu Temple, also known as Pura Luhur Uluwatu, is perched on a dramatic cliff. I visited once a few years ago, and coming back this year, I still felt its grandeur and beauty.

The temple stands on a cliff overlooking the vast Indian Ocean. Although I wasn't here for the sunset this time, the daytime view of the sky merging with the sea was still unforgettable. Our guide took us on a thorough tour, with plenty of photo opportunities and angles to capture.

Aside from a few cheeky monkeys that nearly snatched my sunglasses, everything was breathtakingly beautiful. This visit to Uluwatu Temple once again deeply impressed me with the charm of this ancient and sacred place.

## Jimbaran Beach Sunset

The most unforgettable moment was watching the sunset at Jimbaran Beach. It's just a five-minute walk from the villa, super convenient. We strolled along the shore, feeling the waves, and took in the stunning sunset—golden and shimmering, with the water sparkling. I couldn't resist snapping photos.

![林彥瑋提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_14604a13fbe71c200d16472cf9245cbf.jpg)

Photo by Lin Yanwei

Under this breathtaking scenery, I have to praise my guide once again. He carried my bag, snapped candid shots while giving directions, and even worried about my boyfriend getting his pants dirty, so he took off his own slippers for my boyfriend to sit on. His dedication moved me to tears, and every single photo turned out stunningly beautiful.

## Sundara by Four Seasons

The Four Seasons' dinner restaurant, Sundara—I have to say, from the moment you step through its grand entrance, you're instantly enveloped in a luxurious vibe. With a seaside location, stunning views, and a live band performance, every detail—the atmosphere, service, and environment—is a perfect 💯.

![Sundara by Four Seasons](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4a1c285ebde315bf580e113c530ea376.jpg)

But I'm truly sorry to say, the food was so terrible that I can't even rate it. This is a place you'd want to linger and enjoy the romantic sea views, but unfortunately, we left after just an hour. Chef, please step it up! Despite the high prices, the food quality was deeply disappointing.

Throughout the entire dinner, what struck me most was that the bread and butter were barely edible. Truly disappointing.

## Cuca

![林彥瑋提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6fc7a41afe1e55583fdb17392f461539.jpg)

Provided by Lin Yanwei

Cuca was my last meal before leaving Bali. I heard it's a restaurant recommended by Nicholas Tse, and every dish was impressive. What stood out the most was probably their large pearl oysters. The service was excellent, the food was delicious, and the drinks were very creative.

If you enjoy light meals and don't mind a menu-less dining experience, Cuca is definitely a place to consider.
