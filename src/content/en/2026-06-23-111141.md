One of the easiest things to overlook before traveling abroad is packing your luggage. Every year, many travelers have items confiscated at the airport, face boarding delays, or even incur fines due to bringing prohibited items.

According to international aviation safety regulations and the latest requirements from various airlines, there are a few particularly tricky items to watch out for in 2026.

## 🔋 Power Banks & Bluetooth Earbuds Must Be in Your Carry-On

Electronic products containing lithium batteries, such as:

- Power banks
- Bluetooth earbuds
- Camera batteries
- Phones, tablets
- Handheld fans with built-in lithium batteries

Should all be placed in your carry-on luggage and never in checked baggage. The main reason is that if a lithium battery overheats or catches fire, it's easier to handle immediately in the cabin.

Handheld fans are a common pitfall in recent years; if they have a built-in lithium battery, they must also be carried on.

## 💧 Liquids Over 100ml Must Be Checked

Lotions and emulsions under 100ml: can be carried in hand luggage.\nShampoos, sunscreens, and skincare products over 100ml: recommended to pack in checked baggage.

Liquids carried in hand luggage usually need to be placed in a clear zip-lock bag for easy security screening.

## ☂️ Selfie sticks and tripods: watch the length

Folding umbrellas are generally allowed in hand luggage.

Selfie sticks and tripods longer than 60cm may need to be checked by some airlines, so it's best to confirm the airline's rules before departure.

## 🔥 Lighters: only one allowed

Typically, only one regular lighter is allowed, and it must be kept on your person or in your hand luggage—never in checked baggage.

## 🍖 Don't bring pork floss, sausages, or egg yolk pastries back to Taiwan

Foods containing meat products, such as:\n- Pork floss pastries\n- Sausages\n- Cured meat\n- Egg yolk pastries with meat

- 肉鬆餅
- 香腸
- 臘肉
- 含肉蛋黃酥

Bringing them back to Taiwan is considered high-risk behavior, and if caught, you could face heavy fines, so it's best not to bring any at all.

## ✅ Quick Summary

The three most common mistakes:

1. Putting power banks in checked luggage.
2. Placing liquids over 100ml in carry-on bags.
3. Bringing meat products back to Taiwan.

Additionally, regulations may vary slightly by airline, so it's recommended to check the official airline website before departure. Checking your luggage in advance not only helps you avoid being stopped at security but also makes your journey smoother from the moment you check in. ✈️😊
