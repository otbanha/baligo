Located in the central part of Bali, Ubud is the heart of its tourism and culture. With a history dating back to the 8th century, Ubud has grown into one of Bali's most renowned tourist destinations.

The scenery here is distinctly different from other parts of the island, known for its unique religious, artistic, and natural beauty. The cultural atmosphere of this mountain town is intoxicating, making Ubud an excellent spot for capturing Instagram-worthy photos. Ancient temples, spectacular rice terraces, and ubiquitous Balinese architecture all showcase captivating visual charm. Every corner of Ubud feels like a unique piece of art, allowing you to experience the city's profound soul and rich history.

### Ubud Water Palace (👉🏼 Map: [Central Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

The Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati) is just a three-minute walk from the Ubud Royal Palace. Hidden behind a Starbucks, it’s often overlooked by tourists but boasts breathtaking beauty. Compared to the Ubud Royal Palace, this temple is quieter and more mysterious, offering an excellent photography spot to capture unique and exquisite moments.

The Ubud Water Palace is renowned for its distinctive water features, located at the end of a lotus pond walkway. Its architecture is imbued with Balinese artistic style, with mysterious stone carvings and structures that reflect the region's deep cultural heritage. Walking here feels like traveling through time, immersed in ancient myths and religious beliefs.

### Monkey Forest (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Ubud Monkey Forest is a treasure trove where nature and culture intertwine. As you step into this lush green jungle, monkeys play around you, making you feel like a part of nature itself. You can interact with these clever little creatures and experience the wonder of coexisting with nature.

However, visitors should remain alert when entering the Monkey Forest, as these mischievous monkeys can be quite playful. These curious little animals might climb all over you or even snatch away your glasses or belongings. Therefore, it’s important to interact with them cautiously, avoiding carrying food or conspicuous items to prevent attracting their attention.

### World Heritage Rice Terrace Landscape (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b32736a5ef98517bd7b4ebd87af9506f.jpg)

The rice terrace landscapes around Ubud are one of Bali's highlights, a breathtaking scene that has earned UNESCO World Heritage status. This area not only showcases nature's wonders but also exemplifies the harmonious coexistence between humans and nature, making it a must-visit destination in Bali.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular rice terrace areas in Ubud. The unique layout of these terraces, with winding paths weaving through them, creates a green canvas that vividly displays nature's vitality. Here, the rolling waves of rice fields form a moving agricultural poem, blending natural beauty with farming culture.

Another less commercialized rice terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, though it's farther from Ubud, taking about 90 minutes by car from Ubud Palace. However, it offers a more authentic experience of the rice terraces' beauty!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At this moment, golden sunlight bathes the terraces, creating rich layers of shadows and presenting an enchanting play of light and shadow. It's a favorite time for photographers and visitors alike, as the terraces seem to be immersed in a golden dream.

### Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

The Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area is located in the Buleleng Regency in northern Bali, renowned for its spectacular thousand-year-old sacred tree. This massive Kayu Putih tree (meaning 'white wood' in Indonesian) is estimated to be over a millennium old, with a trunk so thick it takes dozens of people to encircle it, and its towering canopy covers a vast area. Local residents regard this ancient tree as a sacred entity, believing it possesses spiritual powers, and devotees often come here to pray and perform traditional rituals.

This sacred tree is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the harmonious coexistence of Balinese culture and nature. Beneath the tree, there is a small shrine adorned with traditional black-and-white checkered cloth (poleng), signifying its religious importance. Visitors can feel the sacred and serene atmosphere here, appreciating this ancient life that has witnessed centuries of Bali's history. The surrounding natural environment is tranquil, making it a unique spot to experience Balinese spirituality and natural beauty.

## 【Around Ulun Danu Beratan Temple】

### Bali's Ulun Danu Temple （👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcbb190a64dc4d8d05b92348d7fda9c.jpg)

Ulun Danu Temple (Pura Ulun Danau), completed in 1633, is located on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali. Some also refer to it as "Bratan Water Temple." Since Lake Bratan serves as the primary water source for irrigation in central Bali, Ulun Danu Temple was once used as a ceremonial site to worship Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers in Bali. Additionally, the 11-tiered Ulun Danu Temple was built in honor of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, and a statue of Buddha is also enshrined here, making it a temple that blends Hinduism and Buddhism.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf3c1b5963a5c33d38b37b9e3375ccf4.jpg)

Interestingly, this Balinese water temple is featured on Indonesian currency, specifically on the second-largest denomination in circulation. 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah is approximately equivalent to USD$3.

### Pura Besakih Mother Temple （👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

Pura Besakih, the Mother Temple, is one of Bali's largest and most sacred Hindu temples, located on the slopes of Mount Agung. This temple is considered the spiritual heart of Bali and is revered as the 'Mother Temple.' It houses multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

This magnificent temple complex is nestled between beautiful valleys and lush rice fields, offering breathtaking views. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere, explore the intricate temple architecture, and experience the sacred ambiance. Pura Besakih hosts many of Bali's religious and cultural events, such as festivals and ceremonies.

In 1963, Mount Agung erupted, killing 1,700 people, yet the lava flowed just meters away from the temple, sparing Pura Besakih. This event is seen by the Balinese as a miracle, demonstrating the power of the gods while preserving their faith. The temple holds profound religious significance and cultural value, attracting visitors from around the world. Visitors are required to adhere to dress codes and respect local religious customs to honor this sacred site.
