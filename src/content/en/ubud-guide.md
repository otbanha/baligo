### Ubud Water Palace (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](/map/ubud/))

The Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati), located just a three-minute walk from the Ubud Royal Palace, is a hidden gem tucked away behind a Starbucks, often overlooked by tourists yet boasting breathtaking views. Compared to the Ubud Royal Palace, this temple is more serene and mysterious, offering an excellent spot for photography where you can capture unique and exquisite moments.

The Ubud Water Palace is renowned for its distinctive water features, situated at the end of a lotus pond pathway. Its architecture is rich in Balinese artistic style, with mystical stone carvings and structures reflecting the deep cultural roots of the area. Strolling here feels like traveling through time, immersed in the influence of ancient myths and religious beliefs.

### Monkey Forest (📍 Map: [Ubud Center](/map/ubud/))

Ubud's Monkey Forest is a treasure where nature and culture blend seamlessly. Step into this lush sea of greenery, and monkeys frolic around you as if you've become part of the natural world. You can interact with these clever little creatures, experiencing a magical connection with nature.

However, since the monkeys here are quite mischievous, visitors should stay alert when entering the forest. These curious little animals might climb all over you, or even snatch or steal your glasses, personal items, and more. So, it's wise to interact cautiously with the monkeys—avoid bringing food or obvious items that might attract their attention.

### World Heritage Rice Terrace Views (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Spots Around Ubud](/map/ubud/))

![世界遺產梯田景觀（地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b32736a5ef98517bd7b4ebd87af9506f.jpg)

The rice terraces around Ubud are a highlight of Bali, offering a breathtaking landscape like a painting, and they've earned UNESCO World Heritage status. This terraced area not only showcases nature's wonders but also highlights the beautiful harmony between nature and humanity, making it an unmissable travel destination in Bali.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular terraced areas near Ubud. Its layout is unique, with winding paths between the terraces weaving a green canvas that vividly displays nature's vitality. Here, rice paddies ripple like waves, creating a moving agricultural poem that blends natural beauty with farming culture.

Another less commercialized terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, but it's farther from Ubud—about a 90-minute drive from Ubud Palace as a starting point—yet it offers a more pristine experience of the terraced landscape!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At this moment, golden sunlight spills over the terraces, casting rich layers of shadow and creating a mesmerizing play of light. This is the favorite time for photography enthusiasts and travelers, as the terraces seem immersed in a golden dream.

### Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](/map/ubud/))

![Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan 神木風景區（地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2c3bc45f0626ebf69733b0745a47a021.jpg)

Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan, located in Buleleng Regency in northern Bali, is famous for its magnificent thousand-year-old sacred tree. This giant white bark tree (Kayu Putih, meaning 'white wood' in Indonesian) is estimated to be over a thousand years old, with a trunk so thick it takes dozens of people to encircle it, and a towering canopy covering a vast area. Locals regard this ancient tree as sacred, believing it possesses spiritual power, and devotees often come here to pray and hold traditional ceremonies.

This sacred tree is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the harmonious coexistence of Balinese culture and nature. A small shrine beneath the tree, adorned with traditional black-and-white checkered cloth (poleng), highlights its religious significance. Visitors can feel a sacred and serene atmosphere here, appreciating this ancient life that has witnessed centuries of change in Bali. The surrounding natural environment is tranquil, making it a unique spot to experience Bali's spiritual culture and natural beauty.

By the way, many of the caves excavated in the Ubud area date back to Japan's brief occupation of Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces carried out construction projects on the island, including building bunkers, caves, and tunnels for defensive purposes. These caves and tunnels were often used for military operations.

Even after Japan's occupation ended, these caves and tunnels were sometimes repurposed or renovated by local residents or government agencies. Some were turned into tourist attractions, while others were converted into irrigation channels. These caves bear witness to a bygone historical era, and today, they form part of Bali's rich history and culture.

## [Around Pura Ulun Danau]

### Pura Ulun Danau Bratan (📍 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](/map/ubud/))

![峇里島水神廟 （地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcbb190a64dc4d8d05b92348d7fda9c.jpg)

Completed in 1633, Pura Ulun Danau Bratan sits on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali and is also known as the "Bratan Water Temple." Since Lake Bratan is the main irrigation source for central Bali, this temple once served as a venue for ceremonies honoring Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers. Additionally, the 11-tiered Pura Ulun Danau is dedicated to Shiva and Parvati, and a statue of Buddha is also enshrined here, making it a temple that blends Hinduism and Buddhism.

![峇里島水神廟 （地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf3c1b5963a5c33d38b37b9e3375ccf4.jpg)

Interestingly, this Balinese water temple is featured on Indonesia's currency—specifically the 50,000 rupiah note, the second-largest denomination in circulation, worth about USD$2.

### Pura Besakih (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](/map/ubud/))

Pura Besakih is one of Bali's largest and holiest Hindu temples, located on the slopes of Mount Agung. This temple is considered the spiritual heart of Bali and is revered as the 'Mother Temple.' It features multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

This magnificent temple complex sits amidst beautiful valleys and lush green rice terraces, offering stunning scenery. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere, exploring the intricate temple architecture and sacred ambiance. Pura Besakih hosts many of Bali's religious and cultural events, such as festivals and ceremonies.

In 1963, the eruption of Mount Agung killed 1,700 people, yet lava flowed just meters away from the temple, sparing Pura Besakih. This was seen as a miracle by the Balinese, a sign that the gods displayed their power without destroying faith. The temple holds profound religious significance and cultural value, attracting visitors from around the world. Guests must adhere to dress codes and respect local religious customs to honor this sacred site.
