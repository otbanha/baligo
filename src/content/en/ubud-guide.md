Located in central Bali, Ubud is the heart of the island's tourism and culture. With a rich history dating back to the 8th century, it has become one of Bali's most famous destinations.

Its landscape is distinctly different from other parts of the island, renowned for its unique blend of religion, art, and natural beauty. The cultural atmosphere of this hill town is intoxicating, and Ubud is a prime spot for capturing Instagram-worthy shots. Ancient temples, stunning rice terraces, and Balinese architecture at every turn create an irresistible visual allure. Every corner of Ubud feels like a unique work of art, letting you sense the city's deep soul and rich history.

### Ubud Water Palace (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](/map/ubud/))

The Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati), located just a three-minute walk from the Ubud Royal Palace, is a hidden gem tucked away behind a Starbucks, often overlooked by tourists yet boasting breathtaking beauty. Compared to the Ubud Royal Palace, this temple is more serene and mysterious, offering an excellent spot for photography where you can capture unique and exquisite moments.

The Ubud Water Palace is renowned for its distinctive water features, situated at the end of a lotus pond pathway. Its architecture is rich in Balinese artistic style, with mysterious stone carvings and structures reflecting the deep cultural roots of the area. Strolling here feels like traveling through time, immersed in the influence of ancient myths and religious beliefs.

### Monkey Forest (📍 Map: [Ubud Center](/map/ubud/))

Ubud's Monkey Forest is a treasure trove where nature and culture blend seamlessly. Stepping into this lush sea of greenery, monkeys play around you as if you've become part of the natural world. You can interact with these clever little creatures, experiencing the wonder of coexisting with nature.

However, because the monkeys here are quite mischievous, visitors should stay alert when entering the Monkey Forest. These curious little animals might climb all over you, or even snatch your glasses, belongings, and other items. So, it's wise to interact cautiously with the monkeys—avoid bringing food or obvious valuables to prevent drawing their attention.

### World Heritage Rice Terrace Views (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](/map/ubud/))

![世界遺產梯田景觀（地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b32736a5ef98517bd7b4ebd87af9506f.jpg)

The rice terraces around Ubud are a highlight of Bali, offering a breathtaking, painting-like landscape that has earned UNESCO World Heritage status. This terraced area not only showcases nature's wonders but also highlights the harmonious coexistence of nature and humanity, making it an unmissable travel destination in Bali.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the easiest and most popular terraced areas to reach from Ubud. Its unique layout features winding paths between the terraces that weave together like a green canvas, vividly displaying nature's vitality. Here, rice paddies ripple in the wind, creating a moving agricultural poem that blends natural beauty with farming culture.

Another less commercialized terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, but it's farther from Ubud—about a 90-minute drive from the Ubud Palace as a starting point. However, it offers a more pristine experience of the terraces' beauty!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At this moment, golden sunlight spills over the terraces, casting rich layers of shadow and creating a mesmerizing play of light. This is the favorite time for photographers and travelers, as the terraces seem immersed in a golden dream.

### Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](/map/ubud/))

![Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan 神木風景區（地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2c3bc45f0626ebf69733b0745a47a021.jpg)

The Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area is located in northern Bali's Buleleng Regency, renowned for its spectacular thousand-year-old sacred tree. This massive white bark tree (Kayu Putih, meaning 'white wood' in Indonesian) is estimated to be over a thousand years old, with a trunk so thick it takes dozens of people to encircle it, and a towering canopy that covers a vast area. Local residents regard this ancient tree as sacred, believing it possesses spiritual power, and devotees often come here to pray and hold traditional ceremonies.

This sacred tree is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the harmonious coexistence of Balinese culture and nature. A small shrine is set beneath the tree, adorned with traditional black-and-white checkered cloth (poleng), highlighting its religious significance. Visitors can feel a sacred and serene atmosphere here, admiring this ancient life that has witnessed centuries of Bali's history. The surrounding natural environment is tranquil, making it a unique spot to experience Bali's spiritual culture and natural beauty.

However, it's worth noting that rafting carries certain risks, and safety depends on river conditions. For a safer water activity, cave tubing (or river tubing, as shown in the video above) offers a more secure alternative.

Incidentally, many of the caves excavated in the Ubud area date back to Japan's brief occupation of Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces carried out construction projects on the island, including building bunkers, caves, and tunnels for defensive purposes. These caves and tunnels were often used for military operations.

Even after Japan's occupation ended, these caves and tunnels were sometimes repurposed or renovated by local residents or government agencies. Some were transformed into tourist attractions, while others were converted into irrigation channels. These caves bear witness to a bygone historical era, and today, they form part of Bali's history and culture.

Back to the main topic: these two water activities in Ubud—rafting and river tubing—offer unforgettable experiences for explorers, letting you feel the thrill of challenge in flowing waters while enjoying the tranquility and vitality bestowed by nature.

## [Around the Water Temple]

### Bali's Ulun Danu Beratan Temple (📍 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](/map/ubud/))

![峇里島水神廟 （地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcbb190a64dc4d8d05b92348d7fda9c.jpg)

Completed in 1633, Pura Ulun Danu Beratan Temple sits on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali, and is also known as the 'Bratan Water Temple.' Since Lake Bratan is the main irrigation source for central Bali, this temple once served as a venue for ceremonies honoring Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers. Additionally, the 11-tiered Ulun Danu Beratan Temple is dedicated to Shiva and Parvati, and a statue of Buddha is also enshrined here, making it a fusion of Hinduism and Buddhism.

![峇里島水神廟 （地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf3c1b5963a5c33d38b37b9e3375ccf4.jpg)

Remarkably, this water temple in Bali is featured on Indonesian currency—it appears on the second-largest denomination in circulation, the 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah note, which is worth about USD$2.

### Toya Devasya Hot Spring Water Park (📍 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](/map/ubud/))

![Toya Devasya溫泉水上樂園 （地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d938aab64c88210c3c4550474c7836f5.jpg)

As a volcanic island, Bali is home to one of its most famous hot springs: Toya Devasya. This hot spring water park, built on the shores of Lake Batur, is one of the island's most popular attractions. It is renowned for its natural hot springs, water park, and stunning lake views.

### Pura Besakih (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](/map/ubud/))

Pura Besakih, one of Bali's largest and most sacred Hindu temples, sits on the slopes of Mount Agung. Considered the heart of Balinese religion, it is revered as the 'Mother Temple.' The complex features multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

This magnificent temple complex is set amidst beautiful valleys and lush green rice terraces, offering stunning scenery. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere, exploring the intricate temple architecture and sacred ambiance. Pura Besakih hosts many of Bali's religious and cultural events, such as festivals and ceremonies.

In 1963, the eruption of Mount Agung killed 1,700 people, yet the lava flow passed just meters from the temple, sparing Pura Besakih. This is seen as a miracle by the Balinese, a sign that the gods displayed their power without destroying faith. The temple holds profound religious significance and cultural value, attracting visitors from around the world. Guests must adhere to dress codes and respect local religious customs to show reverence for this sacred site.
