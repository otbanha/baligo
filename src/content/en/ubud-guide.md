The scenery here is distinctly different from other parts of the island, known for its unique religious, artistic, and natural beauty. The cultural atmosphere of this mountain town is enchanting, making Ubud an excellent spot for Instagram-worthy photos. Ancient temples, stunning rice terraces, and ubiquitous Balinese architecture all contribute to its captivating visual appeal. Every corner of Ubud feels like a unique piece of art, allowing you to experience the city's profound soul and rich history.

### Ubud Water Palace (👉🏼 Map: [Central Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

The Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati) is located just a three-minute walk from Ubud Palace. Hidden behind a Starbucks, this corner is often overlooked by tourists but boasts breathtaking scenery. Compared to Ubud Palace, this temple is more serene and mysterious, offering an excellent photography spot where you can capture unique and exquisite moments.

The Ubud Water Palace is renowned for its distinctive water features, situated at the end of a lotus pond walkway. The architecture of the palace is imbued with Balinese artistic style, with mysterious stone carvings and structures that showcase the deep cultural heritage of the region. Walking here feels like traveling through time, immersed in ancient myths and religious beliefs.

### Monkey Forest (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

Ubud Monkey Forest is a treasure where nature and culture intertwine. As you step into this lush green jungle, monkeys play around you, making you feel like a part of nature itself. You can interact with these clever little creatures and experience the wonder of coexisting with nature.

However, because the monkeys here are quite mischievous, visitors should stay alert when entering the Monkey Forest. These curious little animals might climb all over you or even steal or snatch items like glasses or personal belongings. Therefore, visitors should interact with the monkeys cautiously, avoiding carrying food or obvious items that might attract their attention.

### World Heritage Rice Terrace Landscape (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b32736a5ef98517bd7b4ebd87af9506f.jpg)

The rice terrace landscapes around Ubud are a major highlight of Bali, offering a breathtaking scene that has earned UNESCO World Heritage status. This area not only showcases nature's wonders but also highlights the harmonious coexistence of humans and nature, making it a must-visit destination in Bali.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular rice terrace areas in Ubud. The unique layout of these terraces, with winding paths weaving through them, creates a green canvas that vividly displays nature's vitality. Here, the rolling waves of rice fields form a moving agricultural poem, blending natural beauty with farming culture.

Another less commercialized rice terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, though it's farther from Ubud. Starting from Ubud Palace, it takes about 90 minutes by car, but it offers a more pristine view of the terraces!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At this moment, golden sunlight bathes the terraces, creating rich layers of shadows and a mesmerizing play of light. This is the favorite time for photographers and visitors alike, as the terraces seem to be immersed in a golden dream.

### Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2c3bc45f0626ebf69733b0745a47a021.jpg)

The Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area, located in Buleleng Regency in northern Bali, is renowned for its spectacular thousand-year-old sacred tree. This massive Kayu Putih tree (meaning 'white wood' in Indonesian) is estimated to be over a millennium old, with a trunk so thick it takes dozens of people to encircle it, and its towering canopy covers a vast area. Local residents regard this ancient tree as a sacred entity, believing it possesses spiritual powers, and often come here to pray and perform traditional rituals.

This sacred tree is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the harmonious coexistence of Balinese culture and nature. Beneath the tree, there is a small shrine adorned with traditional black-and-white checkered cloth (poleng), signifying its religious importance. Visitors can feel the sacred and serene atmosphere while admiring this ancient lifeform that has witnessed centuries of Bali's history. The surrounding natural environment is tranquil, making it a unique spot to experience Balinese spiritual culture and natural beauty.

However, it's worth noting that river rafting carries certain risks, and safety depends on river conditions. For a safer water activity experience, cave tubing/river tubing (as shown in the video above) is a more secure alternative.

By the way, many of these caves excavated in the Ubud area can be traced back to Japan's brief occupation of Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces conducted construction projects on the island, including building bunkers, caves, and tunnels for defensive purposes. These caves and tunnels were often used for military purposes.

Even after Japanese rule ended, these caves and tunnels were sometimes repurposed or modified by local residents or government agencies. Some were converted into tourist attractions, while others were transformed into irrigation channels. These caves bear witness to past historical periods and, today, their existence has become part of Bali's history and culture.

## 【Around Ulun Danu Beratan Temple】

### Bali's Ulun Danu Temple （👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcbb190a64dc4d8d05b92348d7fda9c.jpg)

Ulun Danu Temple (Pura Ulun Danu) was completed in 1633 and is located on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali. It is also known as the "Bratan Water Temple." Since Lake Bratan is the primary source of irrigation for central Bali, Ulun Danu Temple was once used as a ceremonial site to worship Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers in Bali. Additionally, the 11-tiered Ulun Danu Temple was built to honor Shiva and Parvati, and a statue of Buddha is also enshrined here, making it a temple that blends Hinduism and Buddhism.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf3c1b5963a5c33d38b37b9e3375ccf4.jpg)

Interestingly, this Balinese water temple is also featured on Indonesian currency, specifically on the second-largest denomination in circulation. 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah is approximately USD$3.

### Pura Besakih Mother Temple (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

Pura Besakih, known as the Mother Temple, is one of Bali's largest and most sacred Hindu temples, located on the slopes of Mount Agung. This temple is considered the spiritual center of Bali and is revered as the 'Mother Temple.' It features multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

This magnificent temple complex is nestled between beautiful valleys and lush rice fields, offering breathtaking scenery. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere, explore the intricate temple architecture, and experience the sacred ambiance. Pura Besakih hosts numerous religious and cultural events in Bali, such as festivals and ceremonies.

In 1963, Mount Agung erupted, killing 1,700 people, but the lava flowed just meters away from the temple, leaving Pura Besakih unscathed. This event is seen as a miracle by the Balinese, believed to be a divine demonstration of power without destroying faith. The temple holds profound religious significance and cultural value, attracting visitors from around the world. Visitors are required to adhere to dress codes and respect local religious customs to honor this sacred site.
