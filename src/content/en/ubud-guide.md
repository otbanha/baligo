The scenery here is distinctly different from other parts of the island, known for its unique blend of religion, art, and natural beauty. Ancient temples, stunning rice terraces, and Balinese architecture at every turn create an irresistible visual appeal. Each corner of Ubud feels like a unique work of art, letting you experience the city’s deep soul and rich history.

### Ubud Water Palace (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

The Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati), located just a three-minute walk from the Ubud Royal Palace, is a hidden gem tucked away behind a Starbucks, often overlooked by tourists yet boasting breathtaking beauty. Compared to the Ubud Royal Palace, this temple is more serene and mysterious, offering an excellent spot for photography where you can capture unique and exquisite moments.

The Ubud Water Palace is renowned for its distinctive water features, situated at the end of a lotus pond walkway. The architecture of the Ubud Water Palace is also rich in Balinese artistic style, with mysterious stone carvings and structures reflecting the deep cultural roots of the area. Wandering here feels like traveling through time, immersed in the influence of ancient myths and religious beliefs.

### Monkey Forest (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

Ubud Monkey Forest is a treasure trove where nature and culture blend seamlessly. Stepping into this lush sea of greenery, monkeys play around you as if you've become part of the natural world. You can interact with these clever little creatures and experience the wonder of coexisting with nature.

However, because the monkeys here are quite mischievous, visitors should stay alert when entering the Monkey Forest. These curious little animals may climb all over you, or even snatch your glasses or other belongings. So, it's wise to interact cautiously, avoid bringing food or flashy items, and keep a low profile to prevent drawing their attention.

### Folk Pool & Gardens: A Serene Pool Club Hidden in Bustling Ubud

![Folk Pool & Gardens 隱藏在烏布鬧區中的幽靜Pool Club](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2cc396ec6670466a36f49416c315f046.jpg)

### World Heritage Rice Terrace Landscape (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![世界遺產梯田景觀（地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b32736a5ef98517bd7b4ebd87af9506f.jpg)

The rice terraces around Ubud are a major highlight of Bali, offering a stunning landscape that feels like a painting and has earned UNESCO World Heritage status. This terraced area not only showcases nature's wonders but also highlights the beautiful harmony between nature and humans, making it an unmissable destination in Bali.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular rice terrace areas near Ubud. Its unique layout features winding paths between the terraces that weave together like a green canvas, vividly displaying nature's vitality. Here, the rice paddies ripple in the breeze, creating a moving agricultural poem that blends natural beauty with farming culture.

Another less commercialized rice terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, but it's farther from Ubud—about a 90-minute drive from the Ubud Palace as a starting point. However, it offers a more authentic experience of the terraces' raw beauty!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At this moment, golden sunlight spills over the terraces, creating rich layers of shadow and a mesmerizing play of light. This is the favorite time for photography enthusiasts and visitors, as the terraces seem immersed in a golden dream.

The Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area, located in Buleleng Regency in northern Bali, is famous for its magnificent thousand-year-old sacred tree. This giant Kayu Putih tree (meaning 'white wood' in Indonesian) is estimated to be over a thousand years old, with a trunk so thick it takes dozens of people to encircle it, and a towering canopy that covers a vast area. Local residents regard this ancient tree as sacred, believing it possesses spiritual power, and devotees often come here to pray and hold traditional ceremonies.

This sacred tree is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the harmonious coexistence of Balinese culture and nature. A small shrine beneath the tree, adorned with traditional black-and-white poleng cloth, highlights its religious significance. Visitors can feel a sacred and serene atmosphere here, appreciating this ancient life that has witnessed centuries of Bali's history. The surrounding natural environment is tranquil, making it a unique spot to experience Bali's spiritual culture and natural beauty.

During World War II, Japanese forces carried out construction projects on the island, including building bunkers, caves, and tunnels for defensive purposes. Some were turned into tourist attractions, while others were converted into irrigation channels. These caves bear witness to a bygone historical era and today remain part of Bali's history and culture.

### Pura Ulun Danau Bratan (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![峇里島水神廟 （地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcbb190a64dc4d8d05b92348d7fda9c.jpg)

Completed in 1633, Pura Ulun Danau Bratan sits on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali, and is also known as the 'Bratan Water Temple.' Since Lake Bratan is the main irrigation source for central Bali, this temple once served as a venue for ceremonies honoring Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers. Additionally, the 11-tiered Pura Ulun Danau is a temple dedicated to Shiva and Parvati, and a statue of Buddha is also enshrined here, making it a fusion of Hindu and Buddhist traditions.

![峇里島水神廟 （地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf3c1b5963a5c33d38b37b9e3375ccf4.jpg)

### Pura Besakih (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

Pura Besakih, the Mother Temple, is one of Bali's largest and most sacred Hindu temples, located on the slopes of Mount Agung. Considered the spiritual heart of Bali, it is revered as the 'Mother Temple.' The complex features multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

This magnificent temple complex sits amidst beautiful valleys and lush green rice terraces, offering stunning scenery. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere, exploring the intricate temple architecture and sacred ambiance. Pura Besakih hosts many of Bali's religious and cultural events, such as festivals and ceremonies.

In 1963, Mount Agung erupted, killing 1,700 people, yet the lava flow passed just meters from the temple, sparing Pura Besakih. This was seen as a miracle by the Balinese, a sign that the gods displayed their power without destroying faith. The temple holds profound religious significance and cultural value, attracting visitors from around the world. Guests must adhere to dress codes and respect local religious customs to show reverence for this sacred site.
