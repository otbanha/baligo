Ubud is located in central Bali and serves as the heart of its tourism and culture. With a rich history dating back to the 8th century, it has become one of Bali's most famous travel destinations.

The scenery here is strikingly different from other parts of the island, renowned for its unique blend of religion, art, and natural beauty. The cultural atmosphere of this hill town is enchanting, making Ubud a prime spot for capturing Instagram-worthy photos. Ancient temples, stunning rice terraces, and Balinese architecture at every turn create an irresistible visual appeal. Each corner of Ubud feels like a unique work of art, allowing you to experience the city's profound soul and rich history.

### Ubud Water Palace (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

The Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati), located just a three-minute walk from the Ubud Royal Palace, is a hidden gem tucked behind a Starbucks, often overlooked by tourists yet boasting breathtaking beauty. Compared to the Ubud Royal Palace, this temple is more serene and mysterious, offering an excellent spot for photography to capture unique and exquisite moments.

The Ubud Water Palace is renowned for its distinctive water features, situated at the end of a lotus pond walkway. The architecture of the Ubud Water Palace is also rich in Balinese artistic style, with mysterious stone carvings and structures reflecting the deep cultural heritage of the area. Strolling here feels like traveling through time, immersed in ancient myths and religious influences.

### Monkey Forest (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

Ubud Monkey Forest is a treasure where nature and culture blend seamlessly. Stepping into this lush sea of greenery, monkeys frolic around you as if you've become part of nature itself. You can interact with these clever little creatures and experience the wonder of coexisting with the wild.

However, because the monkeys here are quite mischievous, visitors should stay alert when entering the Monkey Forest. These curious little animals may climb all over you, or even snatch your glasses or other belongings. Therefore, it's wise to interact with them cautiously, avoid bringing food or flashy items, and not attract their attention.

### World Heritage Rice Terrace Views (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![World Heritage Rice Terrace Views ( Map: Popular Attractions](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b32736a5ef98517bd7b4ebd87af9506f.jpg)

The rice terraces around Ubud are a highlight of Bali, offering a breathtaking landscape like a painting and earning UNESCO World Heritage status. This terraced area not only showcases nature's wonders but also highlights the harmonious coexistence of nature and humanity, making it an unmissable destination in Bali.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular terraced areas near Ubud. Its unique layout features winding paths through the terraces, weaving a green canvas that vividly displays nature's vitality. Here, rice paddies ripple like waves, creating a moving agricultural poem that blends natural beauty with farming culture.

Another less commercialized terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, but it's farther from Ubud—a 90-minute drive from Ubud Palace as a starting point—yet it offers a more pristine experience of the terraced landscape!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At that moment, golden sunlight spills over the terraces, creating rich layers of shadow and a mesmerizing play of light. This is a favorite time for photographers and visitors, as the terraces seem immersed in a golden dream.

### Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area ( Map: Popul](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2c3bc45f0626ebf69733b0745a47a021.jpg)

The Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area is located in Buleleng Regency, northern Bali, and is famous for its magnificent thousand-year-old sacred tree. This giant Kayu Putih tree (meaning 'white wood' in Indonesian) is estimated to be over a thousand years old, with a trunk so thick it takes dozens of people to encircle it, and its towering canopy covers a vast area. Local residents regard this ancient tree as sacred, believing it possesses spiritual power, and devotees often come here to pray and hold traditional ceremonies.

This sacred tree is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the harmonious coexistence of Balinese culture and nature. A small shrine at its base is adorned with traditional black-and-white checkered cloth (poleng), highlighting its religious significance. Visitors can feel a sacred and serene atmosphere here, appreciating this ancient life that has witnessed centuries of Bali's history. The surrounding natural environment is tranquil, making it a unique spot to experience Bali's spiritual culture and natural beauty.

These caves bear witness to a bygone historical era, and today, they remain part of Bali's history and culture.

## [Around Pura Ulun Danu Bratan]

### Pura Ulun Danu Bratan (Bali's Water Temple) (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![Pura Ulun Danu Bratan (Bali's Water Temple) ( Map: Popular A](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcbb190a64dc4d8d05b92348d7fda9c.jpg)

Completed in 1633, Pura Ulun Danau (Ulun Danu Temple) sits on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali and is also known as the "Bratan Water Temple." Since Lake Bratan is the main irrigation source for central Bali, this temple once served as a venue for ceremonies honoring Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers. Additionally, the 11-tiered Pura Ulun Danau is dedicated to Shiva and Parvati, and a statue of the Buddha is enshrined here, making it a temple that blends Hinduism and Buddhism.

![Pura Ulun Danu Bratan (Bali's Water Temple) ( Map: Popular A](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf3c1b5963a5c33d38b37b9e3375ccf4.jpg)

Interestingly, this Balinese water temple is featured on Indonesia's currency, appearing on the second-largest denomination in circulation: the 50,000 Indonesian rupiah note, which is worth about USD$2.

### Pura Besakih (Mother Temple) (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

Pura Besakih is one of the largest and holiest Hindu temples in Bali, perched on the slopes of Mount Agung. Considered the spiritual heart of the island, it is revered as the 'Mother Temple.' The complex features numerous shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

This magnificent temple complex sits amidst lush valleys and verdant rice terraces, offering breathtaking scenery. Visitors can soak in the ancient religious atmosphere, explore the intricate temple architecture, and feel the sacred ambiance. Pura Besakih hosts many of Bali's religious and cultural events, including festivals and ceremonies.

In 1963, Mount Agung erupted, killing 1,700 people, yet the lava flow stopped just meters from the temple, sparing Pura Besakih. This was seen as a miracle by the Balinese, a sign that the gods displayed their power without destroying faith. The temple holds deep religious significance and cultural value, drawing visitors from around the world. Guests must follow dress codes and respect local religious customs to honor this sacred site.
