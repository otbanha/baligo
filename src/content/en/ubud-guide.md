Ubud is located in central Bali and serves as the heart of its tourism and culture. With a rich history dating back to the 8th century, it has grown into one of Bali's most famous travel destinations.

The scenery here is distinctly different from other parts of the island, renowned for its unique blend of religion, art, and natural beauty. The cultural atmosphere of this mountain town is enchanting, making Ubud a prime spot for capturing Instagram-worthy photos. Ancient temples, stunning rice terraces, and Balinese architecture at every turn showcase an irresistible visual charm. Every corner of Ubud feels like a unique work of art, allowing you to experience the city's profound soul and rich history.

### Ubud Water Palace (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

The Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati), located just a three-minute walk from the Ubud Royal Palace, is a hidden gem tucked away behind a Starbucks, often overlooked by tourists yet boasting breathtaking beauty. Compared to the Ubud Royal Palace, this temple is more serene and mysterious, offering a fantastic spot for photography to capture unique and exquisite moments.

The Ubud Water Palace is renowned for its distinctive water features, situated at the end of a lotus pond pathway. Its architecture is rich in Balinese artistic style, with mysterious stone carvings and structures reflecting the deep cultural heritage of the area. Strolling here feels like traveling through time, immersed in ancient myths and religious influences.

### Monkey Forest (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

Ubud Monkey Forest is a treasure trove where nature and culture blend seamlessly. Stepping into this lush sea of greenery, monkeys play around you as if you've become part of the natural world. You can interact with these clever little creatures and experience the wonder of coexisting with nature.

However, because the monkeys here are quite mischievous, visitors should stay alert when entering the Monkey Forest. These curious little animals may climb all over you, or even snatch your glasses or other belongings. Therefore, it's wise to interact with the monkeys cautiously, avoid bringing food or obvious items, and steer clear of attracting their attention.

### Folk Pool & Gardens: A Serene Pool Club Hidden in Bustling Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2cc396ec6670466a36f49416c315f046.jpg)

In the heart of Ubud, Bali—amid countless shops, unique art pieces, delicious and healthy international cuisine, and rejuvenating yoga and spa experiences—there lies a hidden paradise: Folk Pool & Garden.

This isn't like a typical restaurant where people just eat and leave. It's different—unique and spacious. You can spend an entire day here, and it's a great spot for photos!

### World Heritage Rice Terrace Views (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b32736a5ef98517bd7b4ebd87af9506f.jpg)

The rice terraces around Ubud are a highlight of Bali, offering a stunning landscape like a painting and earning UNESCO World Heritage status. This terraced area not only showcases nature's wonders but also highlights the harmonious coexistence of nature and humanity, making it one of Bali's must-visit destinations.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the easiest and most popular terraced areas to reach from Ubud. Its unique layout features winding paths through the terraces, weaving a green canvas that vividly displays nature's vitality. Here, rice paddies ripple like waves, creating a moving agricultural poem that blends natural beauty with farming culture.

Another less commercialized rice terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, but it's farther from Ubud—a 90-minute drive from Ubud Palace as a starting point—yet it offers a more pristine experience of terrace beauty!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At this moment, golden sunlight spills over the terraces, outlining rich layers of shadow and creating a mesmerizing play of light. This is the favorite time for photography enthusiasts and visitors, as the terraces seem immersed in a golden dream.

### Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2c3bc45f0626ebf69733b0745a47a021.jpg)

The Sacred Tree Scenic Area (Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan) is located in the Buleleng Regency of northern Bali, famous for its magnificent thousand-year-old sacred tree. This giant Kayu Putih tree (meaning 'white wood' in Indonesian) is estimated to be over a thousand years old, with a trunk so thick it takes dozens of people to encircle it, and a towering canopy that covers a vast area. Local residents regard this ancient tree as a sacred being, believing it possesses spiritual power, and devotees often come here to pray and hold traditional ceremonies.

This sacred tree is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the harmonious coexistence of Balinese culture and nature. A small shrine beneath the tree, adorned with traditional black-and-white poleng cloth, highlights its religious significance. Visitors can feel the sacred and tranquil atmosphere here, appreciating this ancient life that has witnessed centuries of Bali's history. The surrounding natural environment is serene, making it a unique spot to experience Bali's spiritual culture and natural beauty.

However, it's worth noting that rafting carries certain risks, and safety largely depends on river conditions. For a safer water activity, cave tubing (river tubing, as shown in the video above) is a great alternative.

By the way, many of these caves in the Ubud area date back to the brief Japanese occupation of Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces carried out construction on the island, building bunkers, caves, and tunnels for defensive purposes. These caves and tunnels were often used for military operations.

Even after the Japanese occupation ended, these caves and tunnels were sometimes repurposed or renovated by locals or government agencies. Some were turned into tourist attractions, others into irrigation channels. These caves bear witness to a bygone era and today form part of Bali's history and culture.

Back to the point: these two water activities in Ubud—rafting and river tubing—offer adventurers unforgettable experiences, letting you feel the thrill of the challenge while enjoying the tranquility and vitality of nature.

## Around Pura Ulun Danau Bratan

### Pura Ulun Danau Bratan (Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcbb190a64dc4d8d05b92348d7fda9c.jpg)

Completed in 1633, Pura Ulun Danau Bratan sits on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali, often called the 'Bratan Water Temple.' As Lake Bratan is the primary irrigation source for central Bali, this temple once hosted ceremonies dedicated to Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers. Beyond that, the 11-tiered temple is also built for Shiva and Parvati, with a statue of Buddha enshrined within—a unique fusion of Hinduism and Buddhism.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf3c1b5963a5c33d38b37b9e3375ccf4.jpg)

Remarkably, this Balinese water temple appears on Indonesia's currency, on the second-largest denomination in circulation: the 50,000 rupiah note, worth about USD$3.

### Toya Devasya Hot Spring Water Park (Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d938aab64c88210c3c4550474c7836f5.jpg)

As a volcanic island, Bali boasts one of its most famous hot springs at Toya Devasya. This hot spring water park, set on the shores of Lake Batur, is one of the island's top attractions, renowned for its natural thermal pools, water park, and stunning lake views.

The hot spring water park covers 2.5 hectares and features seven hot spring pools at different temperatures. ### Pura Besakih (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://gobaligo.id/map/ubud/))

Pura Besakih is one of Bali's largest and most sacred Hindu temples, located on the slopes of Mount Agung. This temple is considered the heart of Balinese religion and is revered as the 'Mother Temple.' It features multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

This magnificent temple complex sits amidst beautiful valleys and lush green rice terraces, offering spectacular views. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere, exploring intricate temple architecture and sacred vibes. Pura Besakih hosts many of Bali's religious and cultural events, such as festivals and ceremonies.

In 1963, Mount Agung erupted, killing 1,700 people, yet the lava flow passed just meters away from the temple, sparing Pura Besakih. This was seen as a miracle by Balinese, a sign that the gods showed their power without destroying faith. The temple holds deep religious significance and cultural value, attracting visitors from around the world. Guests must follow dress codes and respect local religious etiquette to honor this sacred site.
