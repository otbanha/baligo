Located in the heart of Bali, Ubud is the cultural and tourism core of the island. With a history dating back to the 8th century, Ubud has evolved into one of Bali's most renowned tourist destinations.

The scenery here is distinctly different from other parts of the island, celebrated for its unique religious, artistic, and natural beauty. The cultural atmosphere of this mountain town is enchanting, making Ubud a perfect spot for Instagram-worthy photos. Ancient temples, stunning rice terraces, and ubiquitous Balinese architecture all contribute to its captivating visual appeal. Every corner of Ubud feels like a unique piece of art, allowing you to experience the city's profound soul and rich history.

### Ubud Water Palace (👉🏼 Map: [Central Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVO4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

The Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati) is located just a three-minute walk from the Ubud Royal Palace. Hidden behind a Starbucks, this spot is often overlooked by tourists but boasts breathtaking beauty. Compared to the Ubud Royal Palace, this temple is more serene and mysterious, offering an excellent photography spot where you can capture unique and exquisite moments.

The Ubud Water Palace is renowned for its distinctive water features, situated at the end of a lotus pond pathway. Its architecture is imbued with the artistic style unique to Bali, with mysterious stone carvings and structures that reflect the deep cultural roots of the region. Walking here feels like traveling through time, immersed in ancient myths and religious beliefs.

### Monkey Forest (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Ubud Monkey Forest is a treasure trove where nature and culture intertwine. As you step into this lush green jungle, monkeys play around you, making you feel like part of nature itself. You can interact with these clever little creatures and experience the wonder of coexisting with nature.

However, because the monkeys here are quite mischievous, visitors should stay alert when entering the monkey forest. These curious little animals might climb all over you or even steal items like glasses or personal belongings. Therefore, visitors should interact with the monkeys cautiously, avoiding carrying food or obvious items that might attract their attention.

### World Heritage Rice Terrace Landscape (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b32736a5ef98517bd7b4ebd87af9506f.jpg)

The rice terrace landscapes around Ubud are a major highlight of Bali, offering a breathtaking scene that has earned UNESCO World Heritage status. This area not only showcases the wonders of nature but also highlights the harmonious coexistence between humans and nature, making it a must-visit destination in Bali.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular rice terrace areas in Ubud. The layout of this terrace is unique, with winding paths between the fields creating a green canvas that vividly displays the vitality of nature. Here, the rolling waves of rice form a moving agricultural poem, blending natural beauty with farming culture.

Another less commercialized rice terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, though it's farther from Ubud, taking about 90 minutes by car from Ubud Palace. However, it offers a more authentic experience of the rice terraces!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At this moment, golden sunlight bathes the terraces, creating rich layers of shadows and presenting a mesmerizing play of light and shadow. This is the favorite time for photographers and tourists, as the terraces seem to be immersed in a golden dream.

### Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Scenic Area (👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

The Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Scenic Area is located in the Buleleng Regency in northern Bali, renowned for its spectacular thousand-year-old sacred tree. This massive Melaleuca tree (Kayu Putih, meaning 'white wood' in Indonesian) is estimated to be over a millennium old, with a trunk so thick it would take dozens of people to encircle it, and its towering canopy covers a vast area. Local residents regard this ancient tree as a sacred entity, believing it possesses spiritual power, and it is often visited by devotees for blessings and traditional ceremonies.

This sacred tree is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the harmonious coexistence between Balinese culture and nature. Beneath the tree stands a small shrine adorned with traditional black-and-white checkered cloth (poleng), signifying its religious significance. Visitors here can immerse themselves in a sacred and tranquil atmosphere while admiring this ancient lifeform that has witnessed centuries of Bali's historical changes. The serene natural surroundings make it a unique destination to experience Balinese spirituality and natural beauty.

By the way, many of these caves excavated in the Ubud area can be traced back to Japan's brief rule over Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces carried out construction projects on the island, including building bunkers, caves, and tunnels for defense. These caves and tunnels were often used for military purposes.

Even after Japanese rule ended, some of these caves and tunnels were repurposed or modified by local residents or government agencies. Some were turned into tourist attractions, while others were converted into irrigation channels. These caves bear witness to past historical periods, and today, their existence has become part of Bali's history and culture.

### Bali's Ulun Danu Beratan Temple （👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcbb190a64dc4d8d05b92348d7fda9c.jpg)

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple (Pura Ulun Danau), completed in 1633, is located on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali. Some also refer to it as "Bratan Water Temple." Since Lake Bratan is a primary source of irrigation for central Bali, Ulun Danu Beratan Temple was once a site for ceremonies honoring Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers in Bali. Additionally, the 11-tiered Ulun Danu Beratan Temple was built for Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, and a statue of Buddha is also enshrined here, making it a temple that blends Hinduism and Buddhism.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf3c1b5963a5c33d38b37b9e3375ccf4.jpg)

Notably, this Balinese water temple is featured on the Indonesian currency, specifically on the second-largest denomination currently in circulation—50,000 Indonesian Rupiah, which is approximately USD$3.

The temple holds profound religious and cultural significance, attracting visitors from around the world.
