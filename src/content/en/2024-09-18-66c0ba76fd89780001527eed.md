For our first night, we chose to dine at **Cafe Wayan** in Ubud. This restaurant exudes a strong Balinese vibe, with a beautiful setting filled with traditional local charm. We ordered a steak, which was truly delicious—tender and juicy. However, the service was a bit slow, likely because we arrived late, but overall, the dining experience was satisfying.

After dinner, we returned to our accommodation for the night—**Komaneka at Rasa Sayang**. This hotel was as beautiful as the online reviews described, with both the room design and overall atmosphere providing great comfort. Its location is also very convenient, right next to Monkey Forest Road, surrounded by numerous attractions and restaurants, making it ideal for sightseeing and relaxation.

## August 14: Exploring Ubud and Surroundings

On the second day, we set off at 9:30 AM with a private car for a full-day tour. Our first stop was **Ritatkala Cafe**, a coffee shop located near a volcano with absolutely stunning views. From here, you can gaze out at the entire volcanic landscape. However, the food was just average, nothing particularly impressive, but the ambiance and visual feast more than made up for it.

Provided by Oscar Lin

Next, we visited a **coffee plantation** recommended by our driver. The setting was incredibly relaxing, surrounded by lush greenery, and we even had the chance to take photos with civet cats, which delighted the kids. What made it even better was that our driver didn't push any products on us, allowing us to enjoy the experience freely.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. The crowd wasn't as overwhelming as we had expected, perhaps because we arrived early. We asked our driver to guide us through the entire blessing ritual, which was deeply solemn and sacred. Our driver was very attentive, helping us record videos and take photos, preserving precious memories.

Our final stop was **Tis Cafe**. The food here was still just average, but the relaxed atmosphere and the fact that there was no minimum charge made it enjoyable. However, to see the rice terraces, you had to climb a bit, and only a small section was visible, which might be disappointing for those expecting a grand view.

In the afternoon, we once again hired a private car to visit **Uluwatu Temple** (Pura Luhur Uluwatu) to watch the famous Kecak fire dance. The drive from Ubud took about three hours, and our driver was very steady, making us feel safe. Upon arrival, the cliffside temple was truly breathtaking, and the combination of the spectacular scenery with the fire dance made the long journey completely worthwhile.

In the evening, we headed to **Bali Collection** for dinner. This large shopping center features numerous restaurants and shops, making it a perfect spot for families to dine and shop.

After a full day of activities, we checked into **Ulu Segara Luxury Suites & Villas**. While the hotel shows some signs of age, the pool and beach views are stunning, and the spacious rooms were a pleasant surprise, ensuring a comfortable night's stay.

On the fourth day, we visited **Finns Club**, a beach-themed club. We had reserved seats in advance, and the evening featured fire dance performances and fireworks, creating a lively atmosphere reminiscent of a large outdoor nightclub. However, I personally feel that if you just want to experience the vibe, there's no need to book seats ahead of time—simply walk in during the evening for more flexibility and less expense.

## August 17: The Evening Beauty of Oneeighty Dayclub and Rock Bar

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f862c21bb5cb84ebefb16ef3d47bab26.jpg)

On our final day, we embarked on a full-day tour with a private car. Our first stop was Oneeighty Dayclub, an upscale club perched on a cliff. The atmosphere here was truly relaxing, the food was delicious, and they strictly controlled the number of guests, so it never felt crowded. Compared to Finns Club, I much preferred the vibe here—it was an overall delightful experience.

Next, we headed to Rock Bar on Bali's southwest coast. The sunset view here was unforgettable, especially when the lights came on in the evening, creating an even more enchanting ambiance. We ordered a few drinks and slowly savored the stunning scenery—it was undoubtedly the most romantic moment of our Bali trip.
