Our Bali journey began on August 13th, as our family flew from Singapore to this captivating island filled with excitement. The plane landed promptly at 6:45 PM, and we immediately felt Bali's warm atmosphere. After disembarking, we took a private car to Ubud, ready to start our vacation.

## August 13th: Arrival in Ubud, Enjoying Food and Accommodation

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3590d59e09bb1e649fce6f5a8026fef.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

For our first night, we chose **Cafe Wayan** in Ubud for dinner. This restaurant exudes a strong Balinese vibe, with a beautiful setting full of traditional charm. We ordered a steak, which was truly excellent—tender and juicy. However, the service was a bit slow, likely because we arrived late, but the overall dining experience was satisfying.

After dinner, we returned to our accommodation for the night—**Komaneka at Rasa Sayang**. This hotel lived up to its online reviews, offering a beautiful design and a comfortable atmosphere. Its location is also very convenient, situated near Monkey Forest Road with plenty of attractions and restaurants nearby, making it ideal for sightseeing and relaxation.

## August 14: Exploring Ubud and Surrounding Attractions

On the second day, we set off at 9:30 AM with a private car for a full-day tour. Our first stop was **Ritatkala Cafe**, a coffee shop located near the volcano with absolutely stunning views. From here, you can gaze at the entire volcanic landscape. However, the food was just average, nothing particularly impressive, but the atmosphere and visual experience more than made up for it.

Provided by Oscar Lin

Next, we visited the **coffee plantation** recommended by our driver. The environment here was very relaxing, surrounded by lush greenery, and we even had the chance to take photos with civet cats, which delighted the kids. What made us even more satisfied was that the driver didn’t push any products on us, which made the experience feel very comfortable.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. The crowd wasn’t as overwhelming as we had expected, perhaps because we arrived early. We asked the driver to guide us through the entire blessing ritual, which was deeply solemn and sacred. The driver also thoughtfully recorded videos and took photos for us, creating precious memories.

Our final stop was **Tis Cafe**. The food here was still just average, but the relaxed atmosphere made up for it. However, to see the rice terraces, you need to climb a bit, and only a small section is visible. For those expecting a grand view of the terraces, it might be a bit disappointing.

## August 15: Strolling Through Ubud Town and Visiting Uluwatu

On the morning of the third day, we leisurely strolled around **Ubud**, where there are many interesting small shops and markets offering local handicrafts and souvenirs.

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_107bf410383ac8c4239e99ca81014ced.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

In the afternoon, we hired a car again to head to **Uluwatu** (Pura Luhur Uluwatu) to watch the famous fire dance performance. The drive from Ubud took about three hours, and the driver was very steady, making us feel safe throughout the journey. Upon arrival, the cliffside temple was truly breathtaking, and the combination of the spectacular scenery with the fire dance made the long trip feel completely worthwhile.

> Editor's note: What is the fire dance about? Watch here: [Kecak Fire Dance: Bali's Must-See Spectacular Performance (with Ticket Info)](/blog/2024-01-29-65b6e533fd89780001f51eaf/)

In the evening, we went to **Bali Collection** for dinner. This is a large shopping center with multiple restaurants and shops, making it a great spot for families to dine and shop.

After wrapping up the day's activities, we checked into **Ulu Segara Luxury Suites & Villas**. Although the hotel is a bit dated, the pool and beach views are stunning, and the rooms are surprisingly spacious, ensuring we had a comfortable night.

## August 16: Nightlife at Finns Club

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_584ebd67376cd348c0cb9464f6f6e987.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the fourth day, we visited **Finns Club**, a beach-themed club. We had reserved seats in advance, and in the evening, there were fire dance performances and fireworks, creating an atmosphere akin to a large outdoor nightclub, very lively. However, personally, I think if you just want to experience the vibe here, you don’t need to book seats in advance. Simply walking in during the evening would be more flexible and cost-effective.

## August 17: The Twilight Beauty of Oneeighty Dayclub and Rock Bar

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f862c21bb5cb84ebefb16ef3d47bab26.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the last day of our trip, we hired a car for a full day of exploration. Our first stop was **Oneeighty Dayclub**, a high-end club perched on a cliff. The environment here was truly relaxing, the food was delicious, and they strictly controlled the number of guests, so it never felt crowded. Compared to Finns Club, I preferred the atmosphere here—it was thoroughly enjoyable.

> [Introduction and Pricing for Oneeighty Dayclub](/blog/2023-12-07-6571846ffd89780001fe97ee/)

Afterward, we headed to **Rock Bar**, located on Bali’s southwest coast. The sunset view here was unforgettable, especially when the lights came on in the evening, enhancing the bar’s enchanting atmosphere. We ordered a few drinks and slowly savored the beautiful scenery—it was undoubtedly the most romantic moment of our Bali trip.
