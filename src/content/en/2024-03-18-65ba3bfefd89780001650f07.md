## Day 3 to Day 4: Sanur or Nusa Penida

From Seminyak or Canggu, it takes about an hour by car to reach the eastern coast of southern Bali. Depending on your preferences, you can choose [Option 1] to explore the beach vibes of Sanur or [Option 2] head to Nusa Penida to admire its stunning natural landscapes and underwater world.

### Option 1: Sanur

- Sanur is a laid-back beach town on Bali.
- Sanur boasts a serene and beautiful beach with a several-kilometer-long beachfront promenade, making it an ideal spot for sunbathing and leisurely walks.
- The beach promenade is lined with a variety of coastal restaurants, shops, and markets blending exotic flavors with Balinese charm, where you can purchase local handicrafts and souvenirs.
- Sanur Beach offers spectacular sunrise views, perfect for early risers to enjoy Bali’s tranquility and beauty.
- Sanur is also a hotspot for water activities, where you can try kayaking, paddleboarding, windsurfing, and more.
- The harbor for island hopping is located here, making it convenient to catch an early boat to Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan.

## **Day 5 to Day 6: Ubud**

About an hour's drive from Sanur brings you to Ubud, Bali's most famous cultural town.

- Before heading to Ubud, take a slight detour to visit '[Bali's Largest Art Wholesale Market](/blog/2022-03-03-64db6b78fd897800013a9661/)'.
- Ubud was once the capital of Bali's kingdom and remains the island's cultural and artistic heart.
- Ubud is renowned for its unique artistic and cultural atmosphere.
- Stay in a Ubud villa or boutique hotel to immerse yourself in its distinctive ambiance.
- Explore nearby attractions such as the Sacred Monkey Forest, UNESCO-certified rice terraces, and stunning waterfalls.

## **Days 7 to 8: Kuta or Jimbaran**

To navigate Bali's traffic challenges, we recommend spending the last one or two nights in Kuta or Jimbaran, both conveniently located near the airport. Staying in these areas ensures you can reach the airport in about 30 minutes on your final day, avoiding boredom from arriving too early or the stress of traffic delays. Whether you choose Kuta or Jimbaran, both offer stunning attractions to explore, providing the perfect conclusion to your journey.

**Option 1: Kuta**

- Kuta is one of Bali's most bustling commercial districts, offering a wide variety of shops, markets, and malls. Here, you can purchase exquisite Balinese crafts, clothing, jewelry, and more.
- Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous beaches, renowned for its golden sand and impressive waves. It's a paradise for surfers and an ideal spot for sunbathing and sunset viewing.
- Kuta boasts a diverse culinary scene, from [local traditional dishes](/blog/2023-05-02-64db6b81fd897800013a98e3/) to international cuisine. You can savor delicious dishes from around the world.
- Kuta's nightlife is vibrant, with numerous bars, clubs, and entertainment venues. After dark, you can immerse yourself in Bali's lively party culture.
- At Kuta Beach, you can participate in surfing and other water activities, as it's globally renowned as a beginner-friendly surfing spot. [Watch Xiao Jie's first surfing experience here](https://youtu.be/gECdNvG16ww).

### How Not to Plan Your Bali Itinerary

1. **Overcrowded and Rushed Schedule:** Avoid packing every day with too many attractions and activities. Instead, leave some time to relax and enjoy Bali's beautiful scenery and laid-back atmosphere. It's generally recommended to plan no more than four attractions/activities plus lunch/dinner per day.

2. **Constantly Moving Between Areas:** While Bali isn't large, traffic congestion is a serious issue. Moving between different areas can consume a lot of time and energy, leading to fatigue and inconvenience. Therefore, when planning your itinerary, try to focus on exploring and activities within one area. This will save time and energy, allowing you to delve deeper into the local culture and attractions.
