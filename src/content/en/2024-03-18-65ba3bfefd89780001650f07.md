## Day 3 to Day 4: Sanur or Nusa Penida

From Seminyak or Canggu, it's about an hour's drive to the eastern coast of southern Bali. Depending on your preferences, you can choose [Option 1] to explore the beach vibes of Sanur, or [Option 2] to visit Nusa Penida and marvel at its stunning natural landscapes and underwater world.

### Option 1: Sanur

- Sanur is a laid-back beach town on Bali, exuding a relaxed vibe.

![適合家庭度假的沙努爾攻略](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f70927bb4b24c69685305c623a8b4f7e.jpg)

- Sanur boasts a serene and beautiful beach with a several-kilometer-long beachfront promenade, making it an ideal spot for sunbathing and leisurely walks.

- The beachfront promenade in Sanur is lined with a variety of coastal restaurants, shops, and markets that blend exotic flavors with Balinese charm, where you can purchase local handicrafts and souvenirs.

- The sunrise views at Sanur Beach are spectacular, and waking up early to witness the sunrise allows you to experience Bali's tranquility and beauty.

- Sanur is also a hotspot for water activities, where you can enjoy kayaking, paddleboarding, windsurfing, and more.

- The pier for island hopping is located here, making it convenient to catch an early morning boat to Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan.

## **Day 5 to Day 6: Ubud**

About an hour's drive from Sanur brings you to Ubud, Bali's most famous mountain town.

- Before heading to Ubud, take a detour to visit [Bali's Largest Art Wholesale Market](/blog/2022-03-03-64db6b78fd897800013a9661/).
- Ubud was once the capital of Bali's ancient kingdom and remains the island's cultural and artistic heart.
- Ubud is the most uniquely artistic and culturally rich place in Bali.
- Stay in a resort villa or boutique hotel in Ubud to experience its distinctive ambiance and charm.
- Explore nearby attractions such as the Sacred Monkey Forest, UNESCO World Heritage-listed rice terraces, and stunning waterfalls.
- Participate in local cultural experiences like Ubud's handicraft workshops or traditional Balinese cooking classes.

## **Day 7 to 8: Kuta or Jimbaran**

To tackle Bali's traffic challenges, we recommend spending the last one or two nights of your trip in Kuta or Jimbaran, both conveniently located near the airport. As long as you don't stay too far north or south, you can typically reach the airport in about 30 minutes on your final day. This ensures you won't arrive too early and get bored, nor will you risk missing your flight due to traffic congestion. Whether you choose Kuta or Jimbaran, there are plenty of breathtaking spots to explore, providing the perfect ending to your journey.

**Option 1: Kuta**

- Kuta is one of Bali's most bustling commercial areas, offering a wide variety of shops, markets, and shopping centers. Here, you can purchase exquisite Balinese crafts, clothing, jewelry, and more.
- Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous beaches, renowned for its golden sand and spectacular waves. It's a paradise for surfers and an ideal spot for sunbathing and sunset viewing.
- Kuta offers a diverse range of dining options, from [local traditional dishes](/blog/2023-05-02-64db6b81fd897800013a98e3/) to international cuisine. You can savor delicious meals from around the world.
- Kuta's nightlife is vibrant, with numerous bars, clubs, and entertainment venues. After dark, you can immerse yourself in Bali's lively party culture.
- At Kuta Beach, you can participate in surfing activities, as it's globally renowned as a beginner-friendly surf spot. [Watch Jay's first surfing experience here](https://youtu.be/gECdNvG16ww).

## What Not to Do vs What to Do

### Avoid These Mistakes When Planning Your Bali Itinerary

1. **Avoid Overcrowded Itineraries:** Don't pack your days with too many attractions and activities. Instead, allow yourself time to relax and enjoy Bali's beautiful scenery and laid-back atmosphere. A general recommendation is to plan no more than four attractions/activities + lunch/dinner per day.
2. **Minimize Cross-Region Travel:** While Bali isn't large, traffic congestion is severe. Moving between regions can consume significant time and energy, leading to fatigue and inconvenience. Therefore, when planning your itinerary, try to focus on exploring and engaging in activities within one area. This saves time and energy and allows for a deeper understanding of local culture and attractions.
