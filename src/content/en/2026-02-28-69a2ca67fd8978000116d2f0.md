Planning to fly to Bali in the first half of 2026? This period is the most 'dramatic' time on the island. You might experience magical moments of complete stillness or accidentally get stuck in the traffic hell locals fear most.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_faf6335054f12d89f60b16fd7a533b9d.jpg)

To help you navigate like a pro, we've combined climate data with local festivals to create this 'March to June Travel Outlook.'

### 🌦️ Climate & Survival Guide: Transitioning from Rainy to Dry Season

***

These four months mark the critical transition from rainy to dry season:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_07409a02cddf0c7953f3992184b40d23.png)

- **March (End of Rainy Season):** Humidity can reach up to 80%. While rainfall starts to decrease, brief afternoon showers are still common.
- **April-May (Golden Transition Period):** The weather begins to dry up, with increasing chances of sunshine, making it the perfect time for water activities and photography.
- **June (Peak of Dry Season):** Influenced by the Southern Hemisphere's cold air, refreshing breezes blow, making it one of the most comfortable months of the year.

## **March: Between Extreme Bustle & Absolute Stillness**

## **3 月：在極致熱鬧與絕對靜止之間**

__VID0__

- **Weather Overview:** March marks Bali's transition from the rainy season to the dry season. Humidity remains high (around 80%), and while there are still occasional showers, they tend to pass quickly. This is when the island's vegetation is at its greenest, and the rice fields are most picturesque.
- **What to Wear:** Lightweight, breathable cotton clothing, and always carry a foldable umbrella or a light rain jacket.

**Major Festivals:**

- **March 18: Ogoh-Ogoh Parade:** A island-wide exorcism ritual with a spectacular atmosphere.
- **March 19-20: Nyepi (Day of Silence):** Balinese New Year, where the entire island comes to a standstill for 24 hours, offering a unique opportunity to experience profound stillness.
- **Note:** Late March coincides with Indonesia's Eid al-Fitr holiday, a peak travel season you should avoid! Visitors from across Indonesia flock to Bali, causing overcrowding and skyrocketing accommodation prices. If you seek tranquility, steer clear of this period or stay in a remote resort.

**Recommended Destinations for March**

- **Denpasar:** Head to Puputan Square in the evening of March 18 to witness the grandest and most authentic **Ogoh-Ogoh Parade**. (Reference: [Nyepi Day Guide 2026](/blog/2026-02-13-698f077efd89780001e7294f/))
- **Uluwatu Cliff Resorts:** Stay at a resort with stunning ocean views during **Nyepi** (March 19-20). With minimal light pollution, it's the best spot on the island to observe the "Nyepi Milky Way." (Reference: [Uluwatu Accommodation Recommendations: 13 Private Villas with Ocean Views](https://s.id/uluwatu_Stay))

## **April: A Time for Renewal**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cc16394692a6e8dd69ea0fab7f62c062.png)

- **Weather Overview:** Rainfall decreases significantly in April, with more sunny days. Water visibility improves, marking the start of the ideal season for snorkeling and diving. Temperatures range between 25°C - 32°C.
- Early April, during the Qingming holiday, is a smart time to visit! The busiest period of the year in Indonesia, Eid al-Fitr, has just ended. As locals return to work and international tourists are fewer, Bali enjoys a brief but precious period of calm.
- **What to Wear:** Summer outfits paired with a sun-protective jacket, and don’t forget your swimwear.

**Major Festivals:**

- **April 15-19: Bali Spirit Festival:** An annual gathering for yoga enthusiasts worldwide, featuring yoga, dance, and music.
- **April 4: Saraswati Day:** Observe locals' reverence and rituals honoring books and knowledge.

**Recommended Destinations for April**

- **Ubud:** This is the main venue for the **Bali Spirit Festival**. We recommend staying near the Monkey Forest for yoga classes or joining workshops at **The Yoga Barn**. (Reference: [Top 10 Yoga Retreats in Ubud, Bali: Combining Yoga Courses and Spiritual Relaxation](/blog/2024-11-26-67451f5afd89780001f002f2/))
- **Tegallalang Rice Terraces:** With reduced rainfall in April, the sunlight filtering through the lush green terraces creates a mesmerizing scene, perfect for an early morning stroll to avoid the heat. (Reference: [A Guide to Bali's Tegallalang and Jatiluwih Rice Terraces: Entrance Fees, Best Times to Visit](/blog/2024-11-15-673167e4fd897800017c44a9/))
- **Campuhan Ridge Walk:** The dry weather makes the trail less slippery, ideal for an evening walk. (Reference: [A Hidden Paradise Within Walking Distance of Ubud: Introducing Campuhan Ridge Walk](/blog/2023-10-29-653dc7f3fd89780001f4f90a/))

## **May: A Golden Month for Foodies and Outdoor Enthusiasts**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b7405a76a96c0882e108c5117afe7a87.png)

- **Weather Overview:** May is highly recommended by many travel experts. The dry season officially arrives, humidity drops significantly, and the weather feels refreshingly comfortable. The sky is at its bluest, making it the perfect time for Instagram-worthy photos.
- **Labor Day:** Take 4 days off (April 27-30) for a 9-day holiday exclusive to workers!
- **What to Wear:** Show off your summer style, with sunglasses and high-SPF sunscreen being essential.

{{video2}}

**Major Festivals:**

- **May 29-31: Ubud Food Festival:** A must-attend culinary event for food lovers.
- **May 31: Waisak Day:** Experience the solemnity and tranquility of Buddhist culture.

**Top Places to Visit in May**

- **Ubud Center:** Stay near the center to easily navigate between long-table lunches and chef lectures during the **Ubud Food Festival**. (Reference: [Ubud Accommodation Recommendations](https://s.id/UbudResorts))
- **Water Activities:** Enjoy the best weather without the peak-season crowds! (Reference: [Complete Guide to Water Activities in Bali: Beyond Snorkeling, 15 Unexpected Water Adventures](/blog/2026-01-20-694122b8fd89780001f514c9/))
- **Island Hopping/Snorkeling:** With calmer seas in May, it's the best time to visit **Nusa Lembongan** or snorkel with manta rays in **Nusa Penida**. (Reference: [Nusa Lembongan Guide](/blog/2024-10-23-66adfb9afd89780001f62df1/) / [Nusa Penida Guide](https://s.id/penida_guide))
- **Tulamben:** The dry season brings excellent underwater visibility, perfect for exploring shipwrecks. (Reference: [Bali's Northeast Hidden Gems: Complete Guide to Amed and Tulamben](/blog/2025-06-23-68578b01fd89780001dd5208/))

## **June: The Peak of Culture and Beauty**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dd2c5771be5e78e79e23c43530cfe5c2.png)

- **Weather Overview:** June offers extremely stable weather with minimal rainfall. Cool breezes from the Southern Hemisphere bring refreshing winds, and mornings and evenings can feel slightly chilly, making it the most comfortable time for hiking (e.g., Mount Batur).
  **Quick Reference:**
- Dragon Boat Festival Holiday: Take 4 days off (June 15-18) for an epic 9-day early summer trip!
- **What to Wear:** Besides summer outfits, pack a light long-sleeve or windproof jacket for cool mornings and evenings.

**Major Festivals:**

- **Bali Arts Festival (PKB) Throughout June:** Held in Denpasar, it's the ultimate showcase of traditional dance, crafts, and parades.
- **Galungan & Kuningan Festivals:** Streets adorned with spectacular Penjor bamboo decorations, creating the most festive atmosphere.

**Recommended Travel Destinations for June**

- **Werdhi Budaya Art Centre in Denpasar:** Throughout June, the **Bali Arts Festival (PKB)** features top-notch traditional dance and theater performances every afternoon and evening. (Refer to [Denpasar Guide Map](/blog/2025-03-08-67cbb3befd89780001c01e9b/))
- **Mount Batur:** June is the driest and coolest month of the year, perfect for hiking the volcano to catch the sunrise with a high chance of seeing a sea of clouds. (Refer to [Mount Batur & Lake Batur Guide | Sunrise Hike × Royal Cruise × Lakeside Café × Mountain Playground × Hot Springs](/blog/2025-10-09-68e786abfd89780001c1b9b7/))
- **Pandawa Beach:** Join the **Pandawa Beach Festival** and enjoy cultural parades and music performances by the turquoise sea. (How to explore Bali's southern tip? Check out [Bukit Peninsula Adventure Guide | From Jimbaran Sunset to Uluwatu Cliff Temple: Bali's Hidden Paradise](/blog/2024-10-31-67061b07fd897800012f87b5/))

## 💡 Editor's Smart Travel Notes

1. **Dressing Philosophy:** Bring rain gear and moisture-wicking clothes for March and April; for May and June, pack summer clothes plus a light jacket, as the cool evening breeze might give you goosebumps. Check out [Do You Need a Jacket in Bali? Bali Packing Guide: 8 Essential Items](/blog/2025-09-22-68d13539fd89780001b06dfc/)
2. **Avoid Peak Crowds:** Again, mid-to-late March's Eid al-Fitr is the **local peak season**. If possible, plan your trip after April.
3. **Booking Advice:** If you're attending the Ubud Food Festival or Spirit Festival, Ubud city center gets extremely crowded. Book accommodations at least three months in advance.
