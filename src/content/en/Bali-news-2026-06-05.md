### 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Jimbaran Welcomes Top Asian Swimming Event and Illegal Accommodation Delisting Deadline

- **Boosting Sports Tourism! 12th Asian Open Water Swimming Championships to Debut in Jimbaran in Mid-June**:
Indonesia's Ministry of Tourism and the Indonesian Water Sports Association (Akuatik Indonesia) held a joint press conference today (June 4) announcing that **Bali's Jimbaran will host the '12th Asian Open Water Swimming Championships' and the A.STREAM Series Open from June 13 to 15**. This is one of the largest open water swimming events ever held in Indonesia and Asia, expected to attract hundreds of elite athletes, coaches, and their families from 17 Asian countries (including as far as Iran). Tourism Minister Widiyanti noted that sports tourism extends visitor stays and boosts revenue for local accommodations, dining, and small businesses, aligning perfectly with the government's 'high-quality tourism' strategy.
- **Ultimatum! 1,600 Unlicensed Accommodations in Bali Face Forced Delisting from International Platforms if Permits Not Obtained by August**:
Following the tourism ministry's announcement yesterday of the 'API Digital License Verification System,' officials today released a more specific timeline and enforcement plan. Using big data, the government has **precisely identified about 1,600 short-term stays and villa operators on major international online travel agencies (OTAs) that completely lack valid business licenses**. Officials announced that the system will begin hardware integration with international platforms starting June 2026. **If these 1,600 illegal operators fail to complete all required compliance permits during the transition period, they will face full 'forced delisting' from international booking platforms starting August 1, 2026**. This move aims to thoroughly ensure the safety of international tourists and eliminate tax evasion.

### 📄 Topics of Interest to Travelers: Parliament Surrounded by Hundreds (in Support of Oversight Actions)

- **Fed Up with Overdevelopment! 200 'FOR HATI' Residents Rally at Parliament, Strongly Backing Pansus Investigation**:
Following the local parliament's 'Pansus TRAP Special Committee' announcement two days ago of a comprehensive regulatory review of the Turtle Island (Serangan) land and yacht marina development project, grassroots communities across the island erupted in strong response today. The 'Development Observer Forum (FOR HATI Bali),' composed of local leaders, academics, religious figures, university students, and traditional guardians, **gathered about 200 residents today to directly rally at the Bali Parliament building (Wantilan DPRD Bali) and submit a petition**. The crowd made it clear they fully support Pansus Special Committee Chairman I Made Supartha's determination to strictly oversee the island's spatial planning, local assets, and permit enforcement, demanding zero tolerance for all 'environmentally damaging and unapproved' large-scale developments to ensure the island's sustainable future. The 200-person rally also prompted police to deploy temporary security and traffic management around the parliament.
