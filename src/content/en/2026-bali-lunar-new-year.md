2026 is the Year of the Fire Horse in the lunar calendar, and **Bali**'s Lunar New Year (known as Imlek in Indonesia) celebrations are rich and varied, centering mainly on **February 16 (New Year's Eve)** and **February 17 (New Year's Day)**, with some festivities extending into early March for the Lantern Festival.

Here is your guide to **Bali**'s Lunar New Year events:

## 🏮 Must-See Folk Performances: Lion and Dragon Dances (Barongsai)

Lion dance performances are a highlight of the New Year in **Bali**, arranged at nearly all major malls and resorts:

### 💡 Tips for Watching Performances:

- **Arrive Early**: Lion dance shows often draw large crowds of guests and visitors, especially those in hotel lobbies. It's best to arrive **15–20 minutes** early to secure a good spot.
- **Interactive Customs**: When watching lion dances in **Bali**, you can prepare red envelopes (Angpao) to feed the lion, which is seen as a gesture of support for the performers and a way to wish for good luck (suggested amounts are IDR 50,000 or 100,000).

## ⛩️ Temple Fairs and Cultural Experiences

For a more traditional religious atmosphere, head to one of the island's Chinese temples (Klenteng):

- **Vihara Dharmayana (Kuta)**: One of the oldest Buddhist temples on the island, bustling with incense during the New Year, decorated with red lanterns, and often hosting prayer ceremonies and folk performances.
- **Vihara Satya Dharma Temple (Bao'an Gong / Sajia Fa Temple) | Benoa Harbour, Bali**: A key spiritual center for the local Chinese community during major festivals such as Lunar New Year, Ghost Festival, and Guanyin's Birthday.

## 🗓️ Key Dates to Note

- **February 16 (Monday)**: New Year's Eve, declared a joint public holiday (Cuti Bersama) by the Indonesian government, with many hotels hosting reunion dinners.
- **February 17 (Tuesday)**: Lunar New Year's Day, a public holiday with the most bustling mall activities.
- **March 3**: Lantern Festival, with lantern events in some areas.

**💡 Friendly Reminder:** During the New Year period, traffic in Bali's main tourist areas (such as Kuta and Canggu) may be heavier than usual. Be sure to book popular restaurants for New Year's Eve dinner well in advance.
