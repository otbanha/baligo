## Postscript

Here are some must-try and must-visit recommendations for Bali, along with some pitfalls to avoid:

1. ****Natys American Restaurant****: Don’t miss their seafood platter and red wine braised beef! They have branches in many locations, and the quality is guaranteed.
2. ****Tanjung Benoa Water Sports****: Highly recommended for beginners to try the underwater walking activity. Although it’s a man-made attraction, it’s a unique experience. Avoid Turtle Island, as it’s quite boring and lacks distinctive animals. Also, remember to bargain for discounts of 20-40%.

3. ****Uluwatu Cliff Road****: The scenery here is stunning and perfect for photos. It’s best to visit in the morning when it’s less crowded.
4. ****Uluwatu Temple, Tanah Lot Temple, Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park****: These places are all worth visiting. Uluwatu Temple and Tanah Lot Temple offer beautiful views and great photo opportunities. As for Garuda Wisnu Kencana, if your budget allows, take the shuttle to the statue area to avoid heatstroke.
5. ****Ubud Swing****: This place offers various entertainment facilities, including zip lines and sky bikes. Nearby, there’s also a fantastic restaurant serving pork rib fried noodles, though it’s a bit hidden—ask my driver for directions.
6. ****La Brisa****: If you’re heading to the inner bar, remember not to bring water inside, as the restaurant will ask you to pour it out.
7. ****Beachwalk Residence****: For family trips, consider staying at [Beachwalk Residence](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=25918345). They offer family-style apartments, just a short walk from shopping malls and close to the beach, making it great value for money.
8. ****Traffic during Chinese New Year****: Travel times are 30 minutes to 1.5 hours longer than what drivers estimate using Google Maps. This is especially true for trips to Ubud, which take even longer than expected! The roads near the palace are narrow, leading to severe traffic congestion.
9. ****Rainy season contingency****: Since the Chinese New Year period coincides with Bali’s rainy season, save this guide for rainy days: [Bali Rainy Season: What to Do When It Rains? 60 Backup Plans for Rainy Days in Bali](/blog/2024-01-06-6598c6fffd89780001047d76/)
