## Why We Stayed in Kuta

Although Kuta isn't ideal for a long stay (see: [The Most Convenient Place to Stay in Bali, Yet the Least Recommended! Pros and Cons of Kuta, Bali](/blog/kuta-bali-accommodation-pros-cons/)), we chose to stay here right after landing for four reasons:

1. It's the closest to the airport, so we could unwind almost immediately after the flight. Traveling with two kids, staying in Kuta—the nearest area to the airport—helps minimize post-long-haul fatigue, letting the whole family relax and start enjoying Bali's beauty as soon as possible.
2. It aligns with our plans for the next day to visit Jimbaran and Uluwatu. Kuta's prime location not only offers proximity to the airport but also easy access to other Bali attractions like Jimbaran and Uluwatu. Since we planned to head there on day two, staying in Kuta saves travel time and lets us savor more of the journey.
3. It's our first time in Bali, and we wanted to see the liveliest part of the island. Kuta is Bali's most bustling area, with vibrant streets, diverse dining and shopping options, and stunning beaches. As first-timers, we wanted to soak up Bali's energy and charm right here, experiencing the local culture and vibe.
4. We heard Kuta Beach is perfect for learning to surf, and our two boys were eager to give it a try. Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous surf spots, with beginner-friendly waves and wide sandy shores, ideal for kids wanting to experience the thrill of surfing. We hoped to witness our boys' courage and effort as they took on the waves, sharing joyful beach moments together.

After weighing these four factors, we decided to spend our first day in Bali in Kuta, finding a cozy hotel near Beachwalk Shopping Center to kick off our Bali adventure.

## Aloft Bali Kuta: A New Marriott Hotel

On the hectic first day of travel, to align with our family's goal of a 'relaxing getaway,' we didn't want to spend time on long drives. The Kuta area, just 15–20 minutes from the airport, was perfect!

Located within the Beachwalk Shopping Center, Aloft Kuta opened in April 2022. It's Marriott's younger, trendier brand, with a simple, comfortable design and a relatively affordable price point that still maintains Marriott's quality. The service is friendly, the facilities are brand-new, and the overall vibe is fantastic. Though it's right next to the mall, you have to pass through an X-ray security gate to enter or exit, so safety is solid. With only three floors, Aloft Kuta has quite a few rooms per level. Rooms deeper inside are far from the lively Kuta beachfront street, making them much quieter—perfect for a good night's sleep in the twin-bed layout.

![「Let's Play and Eat 胡志明市」現場實拍](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e310dd9b549bdb012b4b52ba5e812f36.jpg)

Live shot from 'Let's Play and Eat Ho Chi Minh City'

The room design includes a small sofa area, a balcony, and a separate wet-and-dry bathroom—overall, it's incredibly satisfying! The breakfast restaurant is bright and comfortable, with floor-to-ceiling windows letting in endless sunshine. While the breakfast selection isn't huge, it offers both Western and Balinese options, all refined and delicious. I absolutely loved the atmosphere!

The pool area is lush and green, surrounded by trees, with a refreshing sense of clarity. We stayed here for two nights, but unfortunately, one morning was rainy and overcast, and the other we had to save for surfing—because right across from the hotel is the world-famous beginner surf spot, Kuta Beach. How could we not visit it?

The most beautiful scenery is the people—at Aloft Kuta, it's all within easy reach. The overall service deserves a perfect score; isn't that exactly what a vacation needs?

## Notes & Reflections

✖️ If you don't want to travel all the way to Jimbaran for sunset, just head next door and follow the signs for *Beachwalk Shopping* to the ✨Sunset Deck✨—you can enjoy a magical Bali sunset for free 🥳🥳🥳. It was a delightful surprise during our trip, being able to soak in such a stunning sunset right near the mall, lifting our spirits instantly.

![「Let's Play and Eat 胡志明市」現場實拍](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7442b03d0a7e21248397c17556f37d93.jpg)

「Let's Play and Eat Ho Chi Minh City」Live Footage

🍽 There are restaurants on both sides of the Sunset Deck. I originally planned to grab a drink at 'Mr. Wayan, by the sea' on the left to enjoy the sunset 🍸, but all the good seats were reserved, so I just wandered around the deck 👀. Even without finding the perfect spot, strolling under the sunset and soaking in nature's beauty was a joy in itself.

🍴 The restaurant on the right, 'Raa Cha Suki & BBQ Beachwalk,' seems to have even better outdoor seating, closer to the sea. If you're interested, be sure to book in advance 📲! Next time, I'll definitely reserve a table to enjoy the perfect combo of seaside dining and sunset.

🌠 In the evening, the Sunset Deck is accompanied by soft music 🎵—sunset, stunning views, and good vibes 😊. Watching the sun set with music in the background was an incredibly uplifting moment, leaving me feeling refreshed and at peace.

👀 Bali is vast, but the roads are narrow and single-lane, so traffic jams are common 🚘. If you're too lazy to venture out for food, the Beachwalk Shopping Center is a lifesaver for satisfying hunger 💪. It offers a wide range of dining options, plus snacks and souvenirs—convenient and affordable.

🔹 1F: A massive candy wonderland 🍭 'Candylicious,' bursting with colors—a true paradise for kids 🫧 (visited). The kids couldn't get enough of this candy haven, spending a joyful time there, and I felt their happiness too.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c6082ea9e6e06a45c3157784ad9da261.jpg)

🔹 3F: 'MM Juice 🍹'—recommended by netizens for tasty juices, but the food is said to be bad (not visited). Although I heard the juices are great, we chose other restaurants; maybe I'll give it a try next time.

🔹 3F: 'Bebek Tepi Sawah' 🦆—a great choice if you want to sample authentic Balinese cuisine but are short on time (not visited). Though I didn't get to taste their food, I've heard the dishes are very authentic—definitely on my list for next time in Bali.

## Recommended Bali Private Driver

✨A great driver takes you to heaven ☁️—enjoy local food 🍽, capture stunning views 📸, and get a tour guide 📚. Highly recommend this Bali driver! 👍

📋 Our Bali trip came together so suddenly. 📸 I originally planned to just ask the hotel to help with transportation between attractions 💭, but a week before departure, a Facebook group admin from "[Bali/Indonesia Travel Guide](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide)" gave me a hand 💪, and I got a chance to step into paradise.

🏡 We stayed with Gede near KUTA. Before returning to work in his hometown, 🇺🇸 he worked on large cruise ships in the U.S., so his English is flawless 👍. 👨‍👩‍👧‍👧 Plus, Gede has two daughters (ages 8 and 10) and a 6-year-old son, so he's incredibly patient with kids 💗. For a family trip like ours, that was a huge help 🙇‍♀️.

**😊 Gede's smile is so genuine it puts you at ease; his service is spot-on, making the trip worry-free:**

🔹 Always shows up on time (or even early) for appointments

🔹 If there's any delay (due to traffic), he always notifies you in advance 📱

🔹 Safety first—he even blocks traffic when you get in or out of the car ⛑️

🔹 Upon arriving at the hotel, he's always the first to rush out and check things for you 👀

🔹 He goes out of his way to help with any questions (lost items, purchases, sharing info ℹ️)

🔹Three steps after landing, all taken care of for you: ✈️ Airport pickup, 📲 Buy a local SIM card at a shop, 💵 Currency exchange (at a legitimate, no-cheating exchange center).

🔹He'll advise against visiting tourist traps ❌ (Gede doesn't take kickbacks, so some popular spots won't let him accompany guests inside). 🔆 At some attractions, Gede will join you (locals get free entry 🎫), acting as a guide to explain the history and stories 🌇. On our trip to Uluwatu Temple/cliffs/sunset, Gede stayed close, 📸 helping with photos, 🐒 and keeping an eye out for monkeys trying to snatch our belongings 👀.

⚠️ Remember! Be a considerate traveler—Gede is a driver, ✖️ not a personal guide. If he doesn't accompany you into attractions, that's perfectly reasonable. Don't make excessive demands; put yourself in his shoes 🫰. On this trip, we never asked Gede to join us or explain things—we went with the flow. Extra company was appreciated ❤️, but if not, that's fine and normal.

🍽️ As for meals/afternoon tea/scenic coffee ☕️, places tourists like us visit tend to be pricey 💰. Basically, Gede drops you off and leaves, waiting for your call 📲. For Gede, who doesn't earn much, these spots are sky-high 💸. We, who treat Gede like a friend, love inviting him along (he always refuses to join for meals, claiming he's full 😑, and occasionally has a drink, always picking the cheapest option 😑).
