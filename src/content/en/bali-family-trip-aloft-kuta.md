## Why We Stayed in Kuta

Although Kuta isn't the best place for a long stay (see: [The Most Convenient Place to Stay in Bali, Yet the Least Recommended! Pros and Cons of Kuta, Bali](/blog/kuta-bali-accommodation-pros-cons/)), we chose to stay here right after landing for four reasons:

1. It's the closest to the airport, so we could unwind almost immediately after the flight. Traveling with two kids, staying in the nearest area to the airport—Kuta—helps minimize post-long-haul fatigue, letting the whole family relax and start enjoying Bali's beauty sooner.
2. It aligns with our plans for Day 2, heading to Jimbaran and Uluwatu. Kuta's prime location not only offers airport proximity but also easy access to other Bali attractions like Jimbaran and Uluwatu. Since we planned to visit these spots the next day, staying in Kuta saves travel time and lets us savor more of the journey.
3. It was our first time in Bali, and we wanted to experience the liveliest part of the island. Kuta is Bali's most vibrant area, with bustling streets, diverse dining and shopping options, and stunning beaches. As first-timers, we wanted to soak up Bali's energy and warmth in Kuta, immersing ourselves in local culture and atmosphere.
4. We heard Kuta Beach is ideal for learning to surf, and our two boys were eager to give it a try. Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous surf spots, with beginner-friendly waves and wide sandy shores, perfect for kids wanting to surf. We hoped to witness our boys' courage and effort as they took on the waves, sharing joyful beach moments together.

After weighing these four factors, we decided to spend our first day in Bali in Kuta, finding a comfortable hotel near Beachwalk Shopping Center to kick off our trip.

## Choosing Accommodation in Kuta

## Aloft Bali Kuta is a new Marriott hotel

On our busy first day of travel, to stick with our family's motto of 'relaxed vacation,' we didn't want to rush or travel far. The Kuta area, just 15–20 minutes from the airport, was perfect!

Located inside the Beachwalk Shopping Center, Aloft Kuta opened in April 2022. It's Marriott's younger, trendier brand, with a simple, comfortable design and a much friendlier price point while still maintaining Marriott's quality. The service is warm, the facilities are brand new, and the overall vibe is fantastic. Even though it's right next to a shopping mall, you have to go through an X-ray security gate to enter, so safety is solid. With only three floors, Aloft Kuta has quite a few rooms per floor. Rooms at the far end are away from the hustle and bustle of Kuta's main beach road, making for a much quieter stay and a great night's sleep in the twin-bed room.

![「Let's Play and Eat 胡志明市」現場實拍](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e310dd9b549bdb012b4b52ba5e812f36.jpg)

The room design includes a small sofa area, a balcony, and a separate wet-and-dry bathroom—overall, it exceeded our expectations! The breakfast restaurant is bright and comfortable, with floor-to-ceiling windows letting in endless sunshine. The breakfast selection isn't huge, but it offers both Western and Balinese options, all refined and delicious. We absolutely loved the atmosphere!

The pool area is lush and green, surrounded by trees, with a wonderfully clear vibe. We stayed here for two nights, but unfortunately, one morning was rainy and overcast, and the other we had to save for surfing—because right across from the hotel is the world-famous (beginner-friendly) surf spot, Kuta Beach. How could we not go?

The best scenery is the people. At Aloft Kuta, you'll find warmth and kindness everywhere. The service is simply perfect—and isn't that exactly what a vacation needs?

## Notes & Reflections

✖️ If you don’t want to travel all the way to Jimbaran for sunset, just head next door. Follow the signs for “Beachwalk Shopping” and the “✨Sunset Deck✨” to enjoy a fairy-tale Bali sunset for free 🥳🥳🥳. It was a delightful surprise during our trip—being able to witness such a beautiful sunset right near the mall truly lifted our spirits.

![「Let's Play and Eat 胡志明市」現場實拍](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7442b03d0a7e21248397c17556f37d93.jpg)

“Let’s Play and Eat Ho Chi Minh City” live on-site

🍽 Restaurants line both sides of the Sunset Deck. I originally planned to grab a drink at “Mr. Wayan, by the sea” on the left and enjoy the sunset 🍸, but all the good seats were reserved. So I just wandered around the Sunset Deck 👀. Even without finding the perfect spot, strolling under the sunset and soaking in nature’s beauty was a joy in itself.

🍴 The restaurant on the right, “Raa Cha Suki & BBQ Beachwalk,” seemed to have even better outdoor seating, closer to the sea. If you’re interested, be sure to book ahead! 📲 Next time, I’ll definitely reserve a table in advance to enjoy the perfect combo of seaside dining and sunset.

🌠 In the evening, the Sunset Deck is accompanied by soft music 🎵—sunset, scenery, and good times 😊. With the music playing and the sun setting, it was an incredibly joyful and refreshing moment.

👀 Bali is vast, but the roads are narrow and single-lane, so traffic jams are common 🚘. If you really don’t feel like going out to eat, Beachwalk Shopping Center is a lifesaver for hunger pangs 💪. It offers plenty of dining options, plus snacks and souvenirs—convenient and affordable.

🔹 1F: A huge candy wonderland 🍭 “Candylicious,” colorful and vibrant—a little slice of heaven for kids 🫧 (visited). The children couldn’t get enough of this candy paradise; they had a blast, and I could feel their joy too.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c6082ea9e6e06a45c3157784ad9da261.jpg)

🔹 3F: “MM Juice 🍹”—recommended by netizens for great juice but bad food (not visited). Although I heard the juice here is excellent, we chose other restaurants. Maybe next time I’ll give it a try.

🔹 3F: “Bebek Tepi Sawah (Dirty Duck Restaurant) 🦆”—perfect for those who want to taste authentic Balinese cuisine but are short on time (not visited). Though we didn’t eat here, I’ve heard the dishes are very authentic. Next time in Bali, I’ll definitely make a point to try it.

## Recommended Bali Private Driver

✨ A great driver takes you to heaven ☁️ — local eats 🍽, stunning sights 📸, and a tour guide 📚 all in one. Highly recommend this Bali driver! 👍

📋 Our Bali trip came together quite suddenly. 📸 For transportation between attractions, 💭 I initially planned to just ask the hotel for help. But unexpectedly, a week before departure, with the help of the admin of the "[Bali/Indonesia Travel Guide](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide)" Facebook group 💪, we got a chance to step into paradise.

🏡 Gede, who lives near Kuta, worked on large cruise ships in the US 🇺🇸 before returning to his hometown, so his English is excellent 👍. 👨‍👩‍👧‍👧 Plus, Gede has two daughters (ages 8 and 10) and a 6-year-old son, so he's incredibly patient with kids 💗. For a family trip like ours, he was a huge help 🙇‍♀️.

**😊 Gede’s smile is genuine, putting you at ease; his service is impeccable, making your trip worry-free:**

🔹 Arrives on time (or even early) for scheduled pickups

🔹 If there’s a delay (e.g., traffic), he always notifies you in advance 📱

🔹 Safety first—he even shields you when getting in and out of the car ⛑️

🔹 At the hotel, he’s always the first to rush and check you in 👀

🔹 For any questions, he goes out of his way to help (lost items, shopping, sharing info ℹ️)

🔹 If you need to book tours, he’ll ask his friends to try to get you the best price 💰

🔹 He handles the three steps after landing: ✈️ airport pickup, 📲 buying a local SIM card at a shop, 💵 currency exchange (at a legitimate, no-cheat exchange center)

🔹 He advises against overpriced tourist traps ❌ (Gede doesn’t take kickbacks, so some popular spots won’t let him accompany guests inside) 🔆 He does join you at some attractions (where locals get free entry 🎫), acting as a guide to share the site’s history and stories 🔊 🌇 At Uluwatu Temple / cliffs / sunset, Gede stayed close, 📸 taking photos and 🐒 keeping an eye out so monkeys wouldn’t snatch our belongings 👀

⚠️ Remember! Be a responsible traveler. Gede is a private driver, ✖️ not a tour guide. If he doesn't accompany you into attractions, that's perfectly reasonable—please don't make excessive demands, and put yourself in his shoes 🫰 🔎 On this trip, I never expected Gede to join me for sightseeing or provide commentary. We went with the flow, and any extra companionship was a bonus we appreciated ❤️. If not, that's totally fine and normal.

🍽 As for meals, afternoon tea, or scenic coffee ☕️, the places tourists like you and me visit 💰 tend to be pricey. 🚘 Basically, Gede drops you off and leaves, waiting for your call 📲 👦🏻 For Gede, who doesn't earn much, these spots are sky-high 💸. Since we get along like friends, we love inviting him to join (he always refuses to eat with us, claiming he's full 😑, and occasionally has a drink, always picking the cheapest option 😑).
