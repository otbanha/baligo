## Why Stay in Kuta

Although Kuta isn't ideal for a long stay (see: [The Most Convenient Place to Stay in Bali, Yet the Least Recommended! What Are the Pros and Cons of Kuta?](/en/blog/kuta-bali-accommodation-pros-cons/)), here are four reasons we chose to stay in Kuta right after landing:

1. It's the closest area to the airport, so we could relax almost immediately after getting off the plane. Traveling with two kids, staying in Kuta—the nearest spot to the airport—helps minimize post-flight fatigue, letting the whole family unwind quickly and start enjoying Bali.
2. It aligned with our plan to head to Jimbaran and Uluwatu on the second day. Kuta's location is excellent—not only close to the airport but also convenient for reaching other Bali attractions like Jimbaran and Uluwatu. By staying in Kuta, we saved travel time and could focus on enjoying the journey.
3. It was our first time in Bali, and we wanted to see the most vibrant area. Kuta is Bali's liveliest district, with bustling streets, diverse dining and shopping options, and stunning beaches. As first-timers, we wanted to soak up Bali's energy and culture right in the heart of the action.
4. We heard Kuta Beach is the best place to learn surfing, and our two boys wanted to give it a try. Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous surf spots, with beginner-friendly waves and wide sandy shores—perfect for kids eager to surf. We hoped to witness our boys' courage and effort as they took on the challenge and share joyful beach moments together.

After considering these four factors, we decided to spend our first day in Bali in Kuta, and found a comfortable hotel near Beachwalk Shopping Center to kick off our Bali adventure.

## Aloft Bali Kuta is a New Marriott Hotel

On the hectic first day of flying, to align with our family's goal of a 'leisurely vacation,' we didn't want to rush between destinations. Kuta, just 15–20 minutes from the airport, was a perfect fit!

Located inside the Beachwalk Shopping Center and opened in April 2022, Aloft Kuta is a youthful brand under Marriott. Its design is simple and comfortable, with relatively affordable rates while maintaining Marriott's quality. The service is friendly, the facilities are brand new, and the overall experience is fantastic. Although it's next to the mall, entry and exit require passing through an X-ray security gate, ensuring safety. With only three floors, Aloft Kuta has many rooms per floor. Rooms deeper inside are quieter, away from the hustle and bustle of Kuta's beachfront street, ensuring a good night's sleep in the twin-bed room type.

![「Let's Play and Eat 胡志明市」現場實拍](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e310dd9b549bdb012b4b52ba5e812f36.jpg)

The room design includes a small sofa area, a balcony, and a separate wet-and-dry bathroom—overall, we were extremely satisfied! The breakfast restaurant is bright and comfortable, with floor-to-ceiling windows letting in plenty of sunlight. Although the breakfast selection isn't huge, it offers both Western and Balinese options, all refined and delicious. We absolutely loved the overall vibe!

The pool atmosphere is lush and green, surrounded by trees, offering an endless sense of clarity. We stayed here for two nights, but one morning was drizzly, and the other we saved for surfing—since right across from the hotel is the world-famous (beginner-friendly) surf spot, Kuta Beach. How could we not visit?

The best scenery is the people, and at Aloft Kuta, it's everywhere you look. The overall service deserves a perfect score—isn't that what a vacation needs?

## Notes & Reflections

✖️ If you don’t want to travel all the way to Jimbaran for sunset, just head next door and follow the ‘Beachwalk Shopping’ signs to the ‘Sunset Deck’ — you can enjoy a magical Bali sunset for free! 🥳🥳🥳 It was a delightful surprise on our trip, being able to soak in such a beautiful sunset right near the mall, lifting our spirits.

![「Let's Play and Eat 胡志明市」現場實拍](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7442b03d0a7e21248397c17556f37d93.jpg)

“Let’s Play and Eat Ho Chi Minh City” live footage

🍽 Restaurants line both sides of the Sunset Deck. I originally planned to grab a drink at “Mr. Wayan, by the sea” on the left to enjoy the sunset 🍸, but the best spots were all reserved, so I just wandered around the deck 👀. Even without finding the perfect seat, strolling under the sunset and soaking in nature’s beauty was a joy in itself.

🍴 The restaurant on the right, “Raa Cha Suki & BBQ Beachwalk,” seemed to have even better outdoor seating, closer to the sea. If you’re interested, be sure to book in advance! 📲 Next time, I’ll definitely reserve a spot to enjoy a perfect combo of seaside dining and sunset.

🌠 In the evening, the Sunset Deck is accompanied by soft music 🎵 — sunset, stunning views, and good times 😊. Watching the sun set with music in the background was an incredibly uplifting moment, leaving me feeling refreshed and at peace.

👀 Bali is vast, but the roads are narrow and single-lane, so traffic jams are common 🚘. If you’re too lazy to venture out for food, the Beachwalk Shopping Center is a lifesaver for satisfying hunger 💪. It offers not only a wide range of dining options but also plenty of snacks and souvenirs — convenient and affordable.

🔹 1F: A massive candy wonderland “Candylicious” 🍭, bursting with colors and vibrancy — a true paradise for little ones 🫧 (we visited). The kids couldn’t get enough of this candy haven; they had a blast, and I could feel their joy too.

![雜記&心得](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c6082ea9e6e06a45c3157784ad9da261.jpg)

🔹 3F: “MM Juice” 🍹 — recommended by netizens for tasty juice, but the food is reportedly bad (we didn’t visit). Although I heard the juice here is great, we chose other restaurants; maybe next time we’ll give it a try.

🔹3F: "Bebek Tepi Sawah" 🦆, a great choice for those short on time but eager to taste authentic Balinese cuisine (not visited). Though we didn't get to try their dishes, I've heard the food here is very authentic—definitely on my list for next time in Bali.

## Recommended Bali Private Driver

✨ A good driver takes you to heaven ☁️ Eat local 🍽 Capture stunning views 📸 Act as a guide 📚 Highly recommend this Bali driver 👍

📋 Our Bali trip came together last minute, and we initially planned to just ask our hotel for help with transportation between attractions 📸. But a week before departure, thanks to the admin of the '[Bali/Indonesia Travel Guide](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide)' Facebook group 💪, we got a chance at heaven.

🏡 Gede lives near Kuta and worked on large cruise ships in the US before returning home, so his English is flawless 👍. 👨‍👩‍👧‍👧 He also has daughters aged 8 and 10, and a 6-year-old son, so he's incredibly patient with kids 💗—a huge help for family travelers like us 🙇‍♀️

**😊 Gede's smile is genuine and puts you at ease; his service is top-notch, making your trip worry-free:**

🔹 If delayed (due to traffic), he always notifies you in advance 📱

🔹 Safety first—he'll even block traffic for you when you get in and out of the car ⛑️

🔹I’ll advise against visiting tourist traps ❌ (Gede doesn’t take kickbacks, so some popular spots won’t allow him to accompany you inside). 🔆 At certain attractions, Gede will join you (locals get free entry 🎫), acting as a guide to explain the history and stories 🔊. 🌇 At Uluwatu Temple, cliffs, and sunset, Gede stayed close, 📸 helping with photos, 🐒 and keeping an eye out for monkeys trying to snatch our belongings 👀

⚠️ Remember! Be a considerate traveler—Gede is a driver, ✖️ not a personal guide. If he doesn’t accompany you into attractions, it’s perfectly reasonable; don’t over-demand, put yourself in his shoes 🫰 🔎 On this trip, we never asked Gede to join us or explain things; we went with the flow. Extra company was appreciated ❤️, but its absence was just as normal
