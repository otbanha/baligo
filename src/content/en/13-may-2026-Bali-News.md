## 📄 Topics of Interest for Travelers: Entry Procedures and Environmental Rules

- **2026 Fully Digital Immigration Process Reminder**: Officials reiterate that Bali is no longer a destination for spontaneous visits. Travelers must complete the online "All Indonesia e-Arrival Card" within 72 hours before arrival. This system integrates immigration, customs, and health declarations, generating a QR code for inspection.
- **Visa (e-VOA) and Accommodation Proof Checks**: Most nationalities currently use the electronic visa on arrival (e-VOA), valid for 30 days with one possible extension. Upon entry, officers strictly check return/onward flight tickets and **official proof of first-night accommodation** (hotel or legally registered villa).
- **Waste Management Becomes a New Travel Hurdle**: Since the Suwung landfill stopped accepting organic waste in April, waste management on the island has become a regulatory priority. Commercial villas must now prove they have a legal waste disposal contract. When choosing accommodation, travelers are advised to prioritize properties with "green certification" to avoid disruptions to their vacation experience due to regional waste collection delays.
- **Major Event Alert for This Weekend**: The **BTR Ultra 2026 Trail Run** will take place from **May 15-17** in Kintamani. The event features multiple distance categories, with the finish line at the Batur Natural Hot Springs. It is expected to draw a large number of sports enthusiasts to the area. General travelers heading to Kintamani this weekend should be aware of traffic controls.
