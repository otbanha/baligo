### 📄 Hot Topics for Travelers: Entry Procedures and Environmental Guidelines

- **2026 Fully Digitalized Entry Process Reminder**: Officials reiterate that Bali is no longer a destination for spontaneous visits. Travelers must complete the online 'All Indonesia e-Arrival Card' within 72 hours before arrival. This system integrates immigration, customs, and health declarations, generating a QR code for verification.
- **Visa (e-VOA) and Accommodation Proof Checks**: Most nationalities currently use the electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA), valid for 30 days with the option to extend once. Upon entry, officers will strictly check return/onward flight tickets and **official proof of accommodation for the first night** (hotel or legally registered villa).
- **Waste Management Becomes a New Travel Hurdle**: Since the Suwung landfill stopped accepting organic waste in April, waste management on the island has become a regulatory priority. Commercial villas must now prove they have legal waste disposal contracts. Travelers are advised to prioritize accommodations with 'Green Certification' to avoid disruptions to their vacation due to delayed waste collection.
- **Large Event Alert for This Weekend**: The **BTR Ultra 2026 Trail Run** will take place from **5/15-5/17** in Kintamani. The event features various distance categories, with the finish line at the natural hot springs of Mount Batur. A large influx of sports enthusiasts is expected, so general travelers visiting Kintamani this weekend should be aware of traffic controls.

### 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Digital Transformation and New Market Trends

- **Strong Growth in Tourist Numbers for 2026**: Despite geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, Bali has seen significant growth in arrivals during the first four months of this year. Data from Ngurah Rai International Airport shows that passenger traffic from January to April has exceeded **4.5 million**, a growth of over 10% compared to the same period in 2025.
- **Deepening Regional Tourism Cooperation**: The Governor of Bali recently met with the Governor of Siquijor Province in the Philippines to share Bali's tourism development strategies in environmental protection (such as plastic reduction policies) and cultural preservation.
