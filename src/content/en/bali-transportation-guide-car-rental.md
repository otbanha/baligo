Bali, a picturesque vacation destination, attracts visitors from around the world. When planning your trip to this island, you'll inevitably face an important question: Should you hire a private car or use ride-hailing services? In this article, I'll share our experiences with both options during our Bali travels to help you make the best choice.

The safest and most convenient transportation methods in Bali are hiring a private car and using ride-hailing services. Hiring a private car means having a driver at your disposal for a set period (usually 10 or 5 hours) to take you wherever you want to go. Ride-hailing, on the other hand, involves using apps like Gojek or Grab for point-to-point transportation, similar to taxis.

## Pros and Cons of Hiring a Private Car

https://youtu.be/0fqEvAzAy9A

1. **Customized Itinerary:** Hiring a private car offers a personalized travel experience, allowing you to arrange your schedule according to your preferences and needs, with flexible timing for visiting attractions and complete control over your travel pace.
2. **Professional Guide:** Most private car drivers are local guides familiar with the culture and attractions, providing expert navigation services.
3. **Pre-Trip Planning:** The driver will discuss itinerary arrangements with you to ensure a smooth journey.
4. **Comfort:** A private car offers a private and comfortable space, allowing you to relax during your travels without external disturbances.
5. **No Waiting:** When you finish visiting a site, a simple call or message will have your driver ready to take you to the next destination, offering VIP treatment.
6. **Attentive Service:** Most drivers can also help look after your children, watch your luggage while you explore, and quickly arrange for any sudden cravings or shopping needs during the trip.
7. **Photography:** If you're lucky, some drivers may be willing to take photos for you, and some are quite skilled at it. However, it's worth noting that this is not their obligation, so encountering such a driver is a bonus.
8. **Cons:** Higher cost, typically around 600,000 IDR (USD$39) or more for a 10-hour rental (including driver, fuel, etc.).

## Pros and Cons of Ride-Hailing Services

https://youtube.com/shorts/wnhyqUekdW0

1. **Cost-Effective:** Hailing a ride (via app) is usually more economical than hiring a private car, especially for short trips or when you need transportation to and from a single attraction.
2. **Instant Availability:** You can hail a ride anytime, anywhere, without needing to book in advance, making it ideal for last-minute transportation needs.
3. **Flexibility:** You can change your itinerary and destination on the go, without being tied to a pre-arranged schedule.
4. **Avoid Traffic:** Another option is to hail a "motorbike taxi" (locally known as "ojek"), which allows you to bypass traffic congestion.
5. **Drawbacks of Hailing a Ride:**
   You might have to wait outdoors in the heat for your ride to arrive, which could take anywhere from 5 to 30 minutes.
   Most drivers are concentrated in busy areas, so if you're in or heading to a remote location, it might be harder to find a willing driver.
   Most drivers aren’t keen on chatting, and some may not speak English.
   They won’t provide services like "suddenly craving mangosteen," "wanting to try snake fruit for the first time," or "knowing where to buy small scissors."

## Private Car or Hailing a Ride? How to Choose?

If your itinerary involves long-distance travel, it might be harder to find drivers willing to accept your ride request. This is because they’d be leaving their familiar areas and risking an empty return trip. In such cases, hiring a private car might be the better option. This can save you time and energy, allowing you to focus more on enjoying your trip without constantly hailing and waiting for rides.

**You can refer to/apply the following formula to choose the most cost-effective/safe transportation method:**

- Arriving in Bali and needing currency exchange, SIM card purchase, or a trip to the supermarket ▶ Private car
- Visiting two or more attractions in a day ▶ Private car
- Itinerary includes long-distance "cross-region" travel ▶ Private car
- Popular photo spots (with long queues) ▶ Private car
- Moving within busy areas ▶ App-hailed ride
- Short-distance round trips ▶ App-hailed ride
- Heading to non-busy areas ▶ Private car
- Solo female traveler prioritizing safety ▶ Private car
- Traveling with two or more people ▶ Private car (cost-sharing makes it affordable)
- Traveling with kids and needing extra care ▶ Private car
- Wanting someone to take group photos ▶ Private car
- Changing accommodations/flight departures/traveling with luggage while visiting attractions and dining ▶ Private car
- Solo traveler moving short distances within the city ▶ App-hailed motorbike
- Unpredictable weather (rainy season) ▶ Private car
- Staying in suburban/remote areas ▶ Private car
- Not wanting to wait for rides/needing VIP treatment ▶ Private car
- Wanting to chat/need basic explanations ▶ Private car
- Wanting to meet local Balinese friends ▶ Private car

## FAQ

![獨旅選擇社團推薦司機，安全多一層保障](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9d3ba96f0b8dc80d54cf2a7eebf091b5.png)

Choosing a driver recommended by travel groups adds an extra layer of safety for solo travelers.

### 1. How much does a private car cost?

The cost of hiring a private car varies depending on the driver’s language skills, distance, and duration. Generally, a 10-hour day costs around USD$40.

### 2.How long does it take to get a ride through the App?

The waiting time for a ride is usually around 10 minutes, but it may be slightly longer during peak hours.

### 3. Do I need to bring a private driver when visiting attractions? Do I need to cover the driver's meal expenses?

Generally, it's not necessary. In most cases, the driver will wait nearby until you need their service. You don't need to cover the driver's meal expenses; they will handle it themselves.

### 4. Which ride-hailing App is cheaper?

Usually, Gojek tends to be slightly cheaper.

### 5. Can I hail a taxi on the street?

Yes, but it's not recommended. There's a high chance of encountering drivers who overcharge.
