## 📄 Southern Transport and Health Alerts

![南端交通與健康警示](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1777820274834-canggu_new_route.png)

- **Four Major Road Projects Begin in the South**: To ease the notorious traffic congestion in southern Bali, the Badung Regency government has launched four new road construction projects, including routes connecting **Pecatu to Melasti** and **Pecatu Market to the main Uluwatu road**, aimed at diverting crowds heading to Uluwatu Temple.
- **Canggu Underground Road Plan**: Targeting the most congested area of Canggu, a 6-kilometer connecting road (including an underground section) has entered the implementation phase. During construction, some sections may see construction vehicles, so travelers are advised to keep navigation updates handy.
- **Alcohol and Infectious Disease Warning**: Officials have recently reminded travelers of the risk of **methanol poisoning**, urging them not to consume cocktails of unknown origin or suspiciously low prices. Additionally, sporadic cases of **dengue fever** and **measles** are still being reported on the island, so outdoor activities call for enhanced mosquito protection.

## 🗞️ Aviation Updates and Event Changes

- **Garuda Indonesia Launches Direct Bali–Timika Flights**: Garuda Indonesia has announced that starting in May, it will operate direct flights from **I Gusti Ngurah Rai (DPS) Airport** to **Timika (TIM)** in Papua. This is part of a nationwide plan to add 53 new routes, aiming to strengthen Bali's role as a regional transport hub.
- **2026 Global Coffee Conference (IWCA) Canceled**: The International Women's Coffee Alliance (IWCA) Global Conference, originally scheduled for May 12-14 in Bali, has been officially canceled due to international flight disruptions caused by geopolitical factors. The decision was made to ensure the safety and participation rights of members from Africa, Europe, and the Americas.
- **Suwung Landfill Organic Waste Acceptance Deadline Nears**: Although the governor has temporarily reopened the Suwung landfill for organic waste, this measure only lasts until the **end of July**. Authorities reiterate that the entire island must complete source-separation transformation before the full closure in August, or waste accumulation will worsen during the peak tourist season.
