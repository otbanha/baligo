### 📄 Southern Traffic and Health Alerts

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1777820274834-canggu_new_route.png)

- **Four Major Road Projects Commence in the South**: To alleviate the traffic nightmare in the south, the Badung Regency government has initiated four new road construction projects, including routes connecting **Pecatu to Melasti** and **Pecatu Market to the main road of Uluwatu**, aiming to divert traffic heading to Uluwatu Temple.
- **Canggu Underground Road Project**: For the most congested area in Canggu, a 6-kilometer connecting road (including an underground section) has entered the implementation phase. Tourists are advised to stay updated on navigation apps as construction vehicles may be present on certain routes.
- **Alcohol and Infectious Disease Alert**: Authorities recently warned tourists about the risk of **methanol poisoning**, advising against consuming cocktails of unknown origin or those that are suspiciously cheap. Additionally, sporadic cases of **dengue fever** and **measles** have been reported on the island, so it is recommended to enhance mosquito protection measures during outdoor activities.

### 🗞️ Aviation Updates and Event Changes

- **Garuda Indonesia Launches Direct 'Bali-Timika' Flight Route**: Garuda Indonesia announced that starting in May, it will officially operate direct flights between **I Gusti Ngurah Rai (DPS) Airport** and **Timika (TIM)** in Papua. This is part of a recent plan to add 53 new routes across Indonesia, aiming to strengthen Bali's role as a regional transportation hub.
- **2026 Global Coffee Congress (IWCA) Cancelled**: The International Women's Coffee Alliance (IWCA) Global Congress, originally scheduled for May 12-14 in Bali, has been officially cancelled due to international flight disruptions caused by geopolitical tensions. This decision ensures the safety and participation rights of members from Africa, Europe, and the Americas.
- **Suwung Landfill 'Organic Waste' Receipt Deadline Countdown**: Although the governor temporarily reopened the Suwung landfill for organic waste, this measure will only last until **the end of July**. Authorities reiterated that the entire island must complete the transition to source separation by August, or waste accumulation issues will worsen during the peak tourist season.
