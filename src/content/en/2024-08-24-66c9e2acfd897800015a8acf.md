As one of Bali's most famous tourist attractions, Ubud Art Market has drawn numerous tourists to buy local art and handicrafts. However, after a destructive fire last week and its subsequent cleanup, this vibrant market will remain closed for the foreseeable future. The fire occurred on August 17, 2024, destroying the new market building and dealing a significant blow to the local economy and thousands of vendors.

## The Destructive Impact of the Fire

According to official data, the fire destroyed the market's basement and first floor, severely damaging over 400 stalls. Many vendors lost their goods irreparably, while those whose goods remained undamaged cannot retrieve them due to structural damage. Security personnel and police continue to guard the market perimeter, preventing vendors from inspecting their stalls, especially those on the first floor's east side, where water damage from firefighting efforts has made the extent of damage unpredictable.

## The Cause of the Fire

At a press conference on August 23, Commissioner Pol Jansen Avitus Panjaitan, the public relations head of Bali Police, provided an update on the fire investigation. Based on the Bali Police Fire Laboratory's findings, the fire was caused by an electrical short circuit. Police are conducting further investigations to determine if any negligence by security personnel contributed to the disaster. He emphasized that such incidents should not recur and hopes the investigation will deter those responsible.
