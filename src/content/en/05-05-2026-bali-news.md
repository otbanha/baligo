### 📄 Health Alert

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1777910220842-_.png)

- **Continued Dengue Fever Risk**: Due to climate change, the 2026 rainy season has seen scattered rainfall, potentially extending the **dengue fever** peak into May. Tourists are advised to use DEET-containing mosquito repellent, especially during dawn and dusk activities, and to watch for sudden high fevers or severe headaches.

- **Alcohol and Rabies Reminder**: Consular alerts warn tourists to be cautious of fake alcohol (methanol) poisoning incidents, recommending purchasing alcoholic beverages only from reputable sources. Additionally, stray dogs on the island still pose a rabies risk, so avoid touching or feeding wild animals.

### 🗞️ National Strategy and Environmental Challenges

- **Accelerated SEZ Development**: The government is speeding up the construction of two major Special Economic Zones (SEZs): **Kura Kura Bali** and **Sanur**. The international-standard hospital within the Sanur SEZ served over 14,000 patients in Q1 2026, with 60% being foreigners, highlighting the strong demand for medical tourism.
- **'Garbage Island' Crisis Intensifies**: Since April, the island's largest landfill, Suwung, has banned organic waste, leading to a surge in roadside garbage piles and illegal burning due to a lack of alternatives. Authorities have imposed penalties of up to 3 months imprisonment and 50 million IDR fines, but residents report a lack of waste sorting guidance. The landfill is currently open only until the end of July and will fully close in August.
- **New Financial Hub Regulations**: The government is finalizing regulations for Bali's financial SEZ, aiming to attract global investors and establish internationally competitive financial management mechanisms.
