### 📄 Health Alerts

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1777910220842-_.png)

- **Dengue Fever Risk Continues**: Due to climate changes, the 2026 rainy season has seen scattered rainfall, potentially extending the **dengue fever** peak into May. Tourists are advised to use DEET-containing mosquito repellent, especially during dawn and dusk activities, and be alert to symptoms like sudden high fever or severe headaches.

- **Alcohol and Rabies Reminder**: Consular alerts warn tourists to be cautious of fake alcohol (methanol) poisoning incidents and recommend purchasing alcoholic beverages only from reputable sources. Additionally, stray dogs on the island still pose a rabies risk; avoid touching or feeding wild animals.

### 🗞️ National Strategy and Environmental Challenges

- **Special Economic Zone (SEZ) Development Accelerates**: The government is speeding up the construction of two major SEZs: **Kura Kura Bali** and **Sanur**. The international-standard hospital in the Sanur SEZ served over 14,000 patients in Q1 2026, with 60% being foreigners, highlighting the strong demand for medical tourism.
- **'Garbage Island' Crisis Intensifies**: Since April, the island's largest landfill, Suwung, has banned organic waste disposal. Due to a lack of alternatives, roadside garbage piles and illegal burning have surged. Authorities have imposed penalties of up to 3 months imprisonment and 50 million IDR fines, but residents report a lack of waste sorting guidance. The landfill will only remain open until the end of July and will be fully closed starting August.
- **New Indonesian Financial Center Regulations**: The government is finalizing regulations for Bali's financial SEZ, aiming to attract global investors and establish a competitive international financial management system.
