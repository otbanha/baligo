## 📄 Health Advisory

![健康警示](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1777910220842-_.png)

- **Dengue Risk Persists**: Due to shifting weather patterns, the 2026 rainy season has brought scattered rainfall, potentially extending the **dengue fever** peak into May. Travelers are advised to use DEET-based insect repellent, especially during dawn and dusk, and watch for symptoms like sudden high fever or severe headaches.

> [What essential medicines and supplies to bring for Bali travel? How to buy mosquito repellent and sunscreen?](/en/blog/bali-travel-medicine-checklist/)

- **Alcohol and Rabies Warning**: Consular alerts remind travelers to be cautious of methanol poisoning from fake alcohol; only purchase alcoholic beverages from reputable sources. Additionally, stray dogs on the island still pose a rabies risk—avoid touching or feeding wild animals.

## 🗞️ National Strategy and Environmental Challenges

- **Special Economic Zone (SEZ) Development Accelerates**: The government is speeding up construction of two major SEZs: **Kura Kura Bali** and **Sanur**. The international-standard hospital in the Sanur SEZ has served over 14,000 patients in the first quarter of 2026, with 60% being foreigners, highlighting strong demand for medical tourism.
- **'Island Surrounded by Trash' Crisis Worsens**: Since April, the largest landfill in Suwung has banned organic waste, and with no alternative in place, roadside trash buildup and illegal burning have surged. Authorities have imposed penalties of up to 3 months in prison and fines of 50 million Indonesian rupiah (approx. USD$1,613), but residents report a lack of waste sorting guidelines. The landfill is only open until the end of July and will close completely in August.
- **New Financial Hub Regulations**: The government is finalizing regulations for a financial special zone in **Bali**, aiming to attract global investors and establish a competitive financial management framework.
