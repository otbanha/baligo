There are plenty of ocean-facing hotels in Uluwatu, Bali, but only one lets you literally 'live on the edge of a cliff.'

![Le Cliff Bali｜烏魯瓦圖懸崖海景第一排 浪漫蜜月 Villa 推薦 2026 - Go Bali Go](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776336998991-d983334a9253e13b11b8294497cc9420.jpeg)

Le Cliff Bali is nestled into the rocky cliffs of Uluwatu, overlooking the famous surf break at Padang Padang. Open your door to an infinity ocean view, and lie in bed to see the entire horizon. This isn't a hotel where you pay extra for an 'ocean view room'—every room here faces the sea. The average rate is around USD$223 per night, a bit pricier than Bali's average accommodation, but this view is a rare experience money can't easily buy elsewhere.

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

The hotel is located on Jalan Labuan Sait, in the heart of Uluwatu. You'll need to descend about 200 stone steps from the parking lot to reach the main property. More on that later, but first, let's talk about the surroundings.

https://youtube.com/shorts/isVxI2UEq6U

Within walking distance is Padang Padang Beach, one of Uluwatu's most famous small beaches, known for its unique rock formations, great for snorkeling and photos. Uluwatu Temple is about a 15-minute drive away—go in the evening to watch the Kecak fire dance, highly recommended. Pandawa Beach is also not far by car.

There are plenty of nearby restaurants and cafes: Single Fin Bali is famous across Bali for its sunset views, Ulu Garden serves excellent Indonesian cuisine, and Suka Espresso is a great spot for a morning coffee. Surf lessons are also available within walking distance. Overall, this area is lively, but the hotel itself feels like a secluded cliffside sanctuary.

***

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

As you descend those 200-plus stone steps from the parking lot, you'll start to question your choices—but when the entire Indian Ocean unfolds before you, all complaints will likely vanish in an instant.

![設施介紹](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776337278582-188944639.jpg)

The hotel is small, with only 10 rooms, following a boutique, intimate concept. The common areas are built right against the cliff face, featuring an outdoor terrace and a rope hammock on the rocks for lounging and watching the sea. Guests mention a suspended sun lounger made of rope, hanging directly over the ocean—one of the most memorable design features here.

The restaurant serves simple Indonesian dishes and Western cuisine. Breakfast requires choosing a room rate that includes it or purchasing it separately. Some guests have reported inconsistent food quality, so it's recommended to walk up and find nearby restaurants for breakfast. The hotel also offers room service, but if you're too lazy to go down to pick it up, watch out for the monkeys—they really do snatch things (more on that later).

Wi-Fi signal is weaker at the bottom of the cliff, which isn't surprising—just be prepared. Motorcycle rental is available on-site, making it very convenient to explore the surrounding area.

Room categories are divided into several levels: the basic Deluxe Double Room, the Deluxe Double with Balcony, and the Sea View room. All rooms face the ocean, with differences in balcony size and view angle.

The rooms feature a Balinese wooden decor style and are equipped with air conditioning, a flat-screen TV, a mini-fridge, tea and coffee making facilities, a safe, and a shower. Overall, they are clean and tidy, but the decor feels a bit dated—some guests have mentioned it feels old-fashioned, lacking the refined touch of a newer hotel.

Space is generally reported to be on the small side, so if you're two people with a lot of luggage, it might feel a bit cramped. The room layout can sometimes be visible from the balcony above, so privacy is somewhat limited—keep this in mind when choosing your room type.

## 👥 Who It's For

Best suited for surfers and sunset photography enthusiasts—it's right next to the Padang Padang surf break, and you can easily get into the water from the shore. It's also great for travelers seeking a 'non-typical Bali experience' or honeymoon couples (the kind who want a sense of seclusion). **Not suitable** for those with young children, knee problems, or mobility issues—the hotel itself recommends that children under 15 not stay here, as the stairs are a real beast.

## ⚠️ Things to Know Before You Check In

Here are a few things that need to be made clear:

- **The stone steps are no joke**: Someone counted over 200 irregular stone steps from the parking lot to the rooms. Pack light, or contact the front desk via WhatsApp in advance to arrange a porter (extra fee applies).
- **Monkeys are relentless**: The local monkeys are used to snatching things from people. Be extra careful if you're eating on the balcony with the door open.
- **Parking is currently limited**: Due to the nearby temple grounds, it's best to come by scooter or motorcycle. If you drive, you may need to park on the main road and walk.
- **Check-in 15:00–Check-out 12:00**: If you need to adjust the timing, contact the hotel in advance.
