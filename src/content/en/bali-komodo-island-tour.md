## Komodo Island Liveaboard Adventure

The sunset view from the liveaboard (provided by Tyty Chow):

Our two-day, one-night liveaboard trip to Komodo Island was truly an unforgettable experience. The comfort of the liveaboard exceeded our expectations—the sea was calm, and the gentle breeze made it incredibly relaxing (though this depends on the weather conditions). The meals on board were simple but delicious, with the grilled fish being particularly memorable.

![科摩多島船宿及美景（Tyty Chow提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a615a173436b8ee9cd7ee4f0d691c0ea.jpg)

Komodo Island liveaboard and scenic views (provided by Tyty Chow)

One of the biggest advantages of joining a liveaboard is being able to witness the sunrise at Padar Island without the hassle of back-and-forth travel like speedboat tours. Speedboat tours usually can't accommodate sunrise viewing, but liveaboards give us the opportunity to experience Padar's magnificent sunrise. Additionally, the evening sunsets and night skies left us utterly captivated.

![左：帕達爾日出/中：有成群蝙蝠飛過，可惜太暗沒拍出/右：蘭科洞穴游泳（Tyty Chow提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_501ebd9a73e18b60967656df34adeb8f.jpg)

Left: Sunrise at Padar / Middle: A swarm of bats flew by, but it was too dark to capture / Right: Swimming in Rangko Cave (Photo provided by Tyty Chow)

However, the two-day-one-night itinerary felt a bit rushed, especially when we encountered a light rain in the afternoon. The sea conditions became slightly unstable, slowing down the boat and reducing some of our planned activities. Based on this experience, I recommend friends interested in Komodo tours to opt for a three-day-two-night or longer itinerary, especially if you're into diving or snorkeling. Two days is indeed a bit too short to fully experience all the highlights of Komodo Island.
