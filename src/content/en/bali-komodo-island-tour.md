## Komodo Island Liveaboard Adventure

The sunset view from the liveaboard (provided by Tyty Chow):

Our two-day, one-night liveaboard trip to Komodo Island was truly an unforgettable experience. The comfort level of the liveaboard exceeded our expectations, with calm seas and a gentle breeze making it very pleasant (though this depends on the weather at the time). The meals on board were simple but delicious, with the grilled fish being particularly memorable.

![科摩多島船宿及美景（Tyty Chow提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a615a173436b8ee9cd7ee4f0d691c0ea.jpg)

Komodo Island liveaboard and scenic views (provided by Tyty Chow)

One of the biggest advantages of joining a liveaboard is being able to watch the sunrise at Padar Island without the hassle of back-and-forth trips like speedboat tours. Speedboat itineraries usually can't accommodate sunrise viewing, but liveaboards give us the chance to witness Padar's magnificent sunrise. Additionally, the evening sunsets and starry nights left us completely enchanted.

![左：帕達爾日出/中：有成群蝙蝠飛過，可惜太暗沒拍出/右：蘭科洞穴游泳（Tyty Chow提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_501ebd9a73e18b60967656df34adeb8f.jpg)

Left: Padar Sunrise / Center: A group of bats flew by, but it was too dark to capture / Right: Swimming at Rangko Cave (Photo by Tyty Chow)

However, the two-day-one-night itinerary felt slightly insufficient, especially when we encountered a light rain in the afternoon. The sea conditions became a bit unstable, slowing down the boat and causing some parts of the itinerary to be cut short. Based on this experience, I recommend friends interested in Komodo tours to opt for a three-day-two-night or longer itinerary, especially if you're into diving or snorkeling. A two-day trip is indeed a bit rushed for fully experiencing all the highlights of Komodo Island.
