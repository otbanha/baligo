This initiative, launched by the Denpasar Transportation Agency, is specifically designed for the Sanur resort area, differing from the existing Trans Metro Dewata bus routes. The Sanur shuttle bus will operate exclusively within the Sanur area, offering a 'hop-on, hop-off' model for tourists.

![Sanur, Bali Launches New Tourist Shuttle Bus Trial Operation](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4b99726fb49ba913d10972a6a4dbe.jpg)

The shuttle bus features an open design, allowing passengers to enjoy the scenery and natural ventilation along the route. However, some passengers have noted that the experience might be slightly affected during heavy rain or strong winds.

Passenger Putu Ardiyasa Raditya Putra shared his positive experience with the shuttle bus but pointed out that the current bus stops only have signboards and lack shelters or seating. He hopes for improved waiting facilities in the future. He added, 'Foreign tourists might be more accustomed to this mode of public transportation, as it is quite common in their countries.'

The current bus route includes:

- Mertasari Beach
- Pangembak Street
- Mertasari Street
- Danau Poso Road
- Danau Tamblingan Road
- Danau Toba Road
- The final stop is near the SMP Wisata area, adjacent to the Sanur Special Economic Zone.

![Sanur, Bali Launches New Tourist Shuttle Bus Trial Operation](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e4d803fc5df07426b0f5021662e241ee.jpg)

Regarding the fares and payment methods for the electric shuttle bus (Shuttle / Bemo Listrik) in Sanur, Bali:

![Sanur, Bali Launches New Tourist Shuttle Bus Trial Operation](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_52fb126f2de8603997d344ea4caf16c6.jpg)

### 1. Is there a fee now?

- **Fees have started:** The shuttle bus was free during the trial operation in August 2025, but it has now transitioned to formal operation and started charging fees.
- **Fare:** 
  * **General passengers:** The fare is approximately **5,000 Indonesian Rupiah** (Rp 5,000).
  * **Monthly pass:** For local employees, there is also a monthly pass option for 100,000 Indonesian Rupiah.

### 2. How to pay?

### 2. 如何支付？

This electric shuttle service operates on a **cashless payment system**, so we recommend preparing the following tools before boarding:

- **E-Money Card:** This is the most recommended and stable payment method. You can use prepaid cards issued by major Indonesian banks, such as:
  - **Mandiri e-money**
  - **BCA Flazz**
  - **BNI TapCash**
  - **BRI Brizzi**
  - **Note:** These cards can be purchased and topped up at local convenience stores like Indomaret or Alfamart.
- **Contactless Credit Card:** Some vehicles are equipped with contactless card readers and theoretically support international contactless credit cards (e.g., Visa/Mastercard). However, due to potential network connectivity issues, it’s safer to carry a physical e-money card as a backup.
- **QRIS Scan:** If you have installed local Indonesian payment apps (e.g., GoPay, OVO, Dana), you can usually pay by scanning the QRIS code on the vehicle.
- **Cash Payment:** While the official policy is cashless, drivers may occasionally accept cash. However, tourists have reported being charged higher amounts (e.g., 10,000 IDR), so electronic payment is strongly recommended.

### 3. Service Information

- **Operating Hours:** Approximately from 06:00 or 07:00 in the morning until 22:00 (with the last departure around 21:00).
- **Route:** Primarily covers Sanur’s tourist hotspots, including Jalan Danau Tamblingan, the Mertasari area, and major hotels and beach entrances.

If you’re staying in **Bali**, this is an eco-friendly and cost-effective option for short-distance travel!

This shuttle bus service trial is an innovative initiative by Bali’s authorities to address traffic congestion. Officials, including Bali’s Governor Wayan Koster, emphasize that improving the island’s transportation is crucial for enhancing residents’ quality of life and ensuring sustainable tourism development.

In addition to land-based improvements, the local government is actively developing "sea taxi" services. A recently launched sea shuttle route connects Benoa and Tanjung Benoa, reducing the travel time from about 30 minutes by car to just 10–15 minutes by sea.

In the coming months, the government plans to launch a speedboat service connecting Bali Airport and Canggu, expected to be operational by late 2025 or early 2026.

The Badung Regency government has confirmed that the plans are in the final stages of development, aiming to provide tourists with more efficient and comfortable travel options.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is this service exclusively for tourists?**

While primarily aimed at tourists, locals can also use this service.

**Are the station facilities well-equipped?**

Currently, most bus stops only have simple signage, lacking sheltered spaces and seating. Upgrades are expected in the future.

**Will this service expand to other areas?**

Currently limited to the Sanur area only. Future expansion will depend on the trial operation's success.
