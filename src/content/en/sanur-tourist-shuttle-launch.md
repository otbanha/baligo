This initiative, launched by the Denpasar Transportation Agency, is specifically designed for the Sanur resort area, differing from the existing Trans Metro Dewata bus routes. The Sanur shuttle bus will operate exclusively within the Sanur region, offering a 'hop-on, hop-off' model for tourists.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4b99726fb49ba913d10972a6a4dbe.jpg)

The shuttle buses feature an open design, allowing passengers to enjoy the scenery while benefiting from natural ventilation. However, some passengers have noted that heavy rain or strong winds might slightly affect the riding experience.

Passenger Putu Ardiyasa Raditya Putra shared his positive experience with the shuttle service but pointed out that current bus stops only have signage and lack shelters or seating. He hopes for improved waiting facilities in the future. He added, 'Foreign tourists might find this public transport mode more familiar, as it's quite common in their countries.'

The current bus route includes:

- Mertasari Beach
- Pangembak Street
- Mertasari Street
- Danau Poso Road
- Danau Tamblingan Road
- Danau Toba Road
- The final stop is near the SMP Wisata area, adjacent to the Sanur Special Economic Zone (Medical Zone)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e4d803fc5df07426b0f5021662e241ee.jpg)

Details on the electric shuttle (Shuttle / Bemo Listrik) fares and payment methods in Sanur, Bali, are as follows:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_52fb126f2de8603997d344ea4caf16c6.jpg)

### 1. Is There a Fee?

- **Fees Now Apply:** The shuttle was free during its trial operation in August 2025 but has since transitioned to full operation with fees.
- **Fare:** * **General Passengers:** Approximately **5,000 Indonesian Rupiah** (Rp 5,000) per ride.
- - **Monthly Pass:** A monthly pass is available for local employees at 100,000 Indonesian Rupiah.

### 2. How to Pay?

### 2. 如何支付？

This electric shuttle service operates on a **cashless payment system**, so we recommend preparing the following tools before boarding:

- **E-Money Card:** This is the most recommended and reliable payment method. You can use prepaid cards issued by major Indonesian banks, such as:
  - **Mandiri e-money**
  - **BCA Flazz**
  - **BNI TapCash**
  - **BRI Brizzi**
  - **Note:** These cards can be purchased and topped up at local convenience stores like Indomaret or Alfamart.
- **Contactless Credit Card:** Some vehicles are equipped with contactless card readers, which theoretically support international contactless credit cards (e.g., Visa/Mastercard). However, due to occasional network connectivity issues, it’s safer to carry a physical E-Money card as a backup.
- **QRIS Scan:** If you have installed local Indonesian payment apps (e.g., GoPay, OVO, Dana), you can usually complete payments by scanning the QRIS code on the vehicle.
- **Cash Payment:** While the official policy promotes cashless transactions, drivers may occasionally accept cash payments. However, some tourists have reported being charged higher amounts (e.g., 10,000 IDR) when paying in cash, so electronic payments are strongly recommended.

### 3. Service Information

- **Operating Hours:** Approximately from 06:00 or 07:00 in the morning until 22:00 (with the last departure around 21:00).
- **Route:** Primarily covers the tourist core areas of Sanur, including Jalan Danau Tamblingan, the Mertasari area, and major hotel and beach entrances.

If you’re staying in **Bali**, this is an eco-friendly and cost-effective option for short-distance travel!

This shuttle bus service pilot is an innovative initiative by Bali authorities to address traffic congestion. Officials, including Bali Governor Wayan Koster, have emphasized that improving island transportation is crucial for enhancing residents' quality of life and ensuring the sustainable development of tourism.

In addition to land-based improvements, the local government is actively developing "water taxi" services. A recently launched sea shuttle route connects Benoa and Tanjung Benoa, reducing the travel time from approximately 30 minutes by road to just 10–15 minutes by sea.

In the coming months, the government plans to launch a high-speed boat service connecting Bali Airport and Canggu, expected to be operational by late 2025 or early 2026.

The Badung Regency government has confirmed that related plans are in the final stages of development, aiming to provide travelers with more efficient and comfortable transportation options.

## Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

**Is this service exclusively for tourists?**

While primarily catering to tourists, locals can also utilize this service.

**Are the station facilities well-equipped?**

Currently, most bus stops feature simple signage without covered waiting areas or seating, but upgrades are anticipated in the future.

**Will this service be extended to other areas?**

At present, it's limited to the Sanur area, and future expansion will depend on the outcomes of the trial operation.
