This program, launched by the Denpasar Transportation Agency, is specifically designed for the Sanur resort area. Unlike the existing Trans Metro Dewata bus routes, the Sanur shuttle bus will operate exclusively within the Sanur region, offering a 'hop-on, hop-off' mode of travel for tourists.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4b99726fb49ba913d10972a6a4dbe.jpg)

The shuttle bus features an open-air design, allowing passengers to enjoy the scenery while benefiting from natural ventilation. However, some passengers have noted that the experience may be slightly affected during heavy rain or strong winds.

Passenger Putu Ardiyasa Raditya Putra shared his positive experience with the shuttle service but pointed out that the current bus stops only have signage and lack shelters or seating, expressing hope for more comprehensive waiting facilities in the future. He added, 'Foreign tourists might be more accustomed to this mode of public transportation, as it is quite common in their countries.'

The current bus route includes:

- Mertasari Beach
- Pangembak Street
- Mertasari Street
- Danau Poso Road
- Danau Tamblingan Road
- Danau Toba Road
- The final stop is near the SMP Wisata area, adjacent to the Sanur Special Economic Zone.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e4d803fc5df07426b0f5021662e241ee.jpg)

Regarding the electric shuttle (Shuttle / Bemo Listrik) service in Sanur, Bali, here are the details on fares and payment methods:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_52fb126f2de8603997d344ea4caf16c6.jpg)

### 1. Is There a Fee Now?

- **Fees Now Apply:** The shuttle was free during the trial operation in August 2025 but has since transitioned to full operation and started charging fees.
- **Fare:** * **General Passengers:** Single ride costs approximately **5,000 Indonesian Rupiah** (Rp 5,000).
- - **Monthly Pass:** For local employees, there is also a monthly pass option priced at 100,000 Indonesian Rupiah.

### 2. How to Pay?

### 2. 如何支付？

This electric shuttle service uses a **cashless payment system**, so we recommend preparing the following tools before boarding:

- **E-Money Card:** This is the most recommended and reliable payment method. You can use prepaid cards issued by major Indonesian banks, such as:
  - **Mandiri e-money**
  - **BCA Flazz**
  - **BNI TapCash**
  - **BRI Brizzi**
  - **Note:** These cards can be purchased and topped up at local convenience stores like Indomaret or Alfamart.
- **Contactless Credit Card:** Some vehicles are equipped with contactless card readers, which theoretically support international contactless credit cards (e.g., Visa/Mastercard). However, due to potential network connectivity issues, it’s safer to carry a physical e-money card as a backup.
- **QRIS Scan:** If you have installed local Indonesian mobile payment apps (e.g., GoPay, OVO, Dana), you can usually complete the payment by scanning the QRIS code on the vehicle.
- **Cash Payment:** While the official policy promotes cashless transactions, drivers may occasionally accept cash. However, some tourists have reported being charged higher amounts (e.g., 10,000 IDR) when paying in cash, so electronic payment is strongly recommended.

### 3. Service Information

- **Operating Hours:** Approximately from 06:00 or 07:00 in the morning until 22:00 (with the last departure around 21:00).
- **Route:** Primarily covers the tourist core areas of Sanur, including Jalan Danau Tamblingan, the Mertasari area, and major hotels and beach entrances.

If you’re staying in **Bali**, this is an eco-friendly and cost-effective option for short-distance travel!

This shuttle bus service is part of a pilot program by Bali authorities to address traffic congestion. Officials, including Bali Governor Wayan Koster, have emphasized that improving island transportation is crucial for enhancing residents' quality of life and ensuring the sustainable development of tourism.

In addition to land-based improvements, the local government is actively developing "water taxi" services. A recently launched sea shuttle route connects Benoa and Tanjung Benoa, significantly reducing the travel time from approximately 30 minutes by car to just 10-15 minutes by sea.

In the coming months, the government plans to launch a sea shuttle service connecting Bali Airport to Canggu, expected to be operational by late 2025 or early 2026.

The Badung Regency government has confirmed that related plans are in the final stages of development, aiming to provide tourists with more efficient and comfortable travel options.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is this service exclusively for tourists?**

While primarily catering to tourists, local residents can also use this service.

**Are the station facilities well-equipped?**

Currently, most bus stops only have simple signage, lacking sheltered spaces and seating. Upgrades are expected in the future.

**Will this service be extended to other areas?**

For now, it's limited to the Sanur area. Future expansion will depend on the trial operation's success.
