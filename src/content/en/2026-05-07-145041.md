### 📄 Environmental Trends & Safety Reminders

- **Energy Shortage in Labuan Bajo, Gateway to Komodo Island, Impacts Shipping**: Recent shortages of fuel (BBM) and liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) have forced the cancellation of multiple **tourist boat trips** in the area. This directly affects travelers planning to visit Komodo Island from Bali, with many already at the port stranded due to lack of fuel. Local authorities attribute the shortage to delayed land transportation logistics and a surge in demand.
- Power outages lasting several hours occurred in multiple areas of eastern and southern Bali yesterday due to maintenance work by the national power company.
- **"Environmental Literacy" Emerges as New Travel Trend**: The latest travel report highlights that in 2026, tourists increasingly value eco-friendly practices by businesses. Hotels are moving beyond simply banning plastic straws to implementing deeper systemic changes, such as on-site organic waste composting, which has become a key factor for high-end travelers when choosing accommodations.
- **Safety Alert Reminder**: Official travel guidelines continue to advise visitors to remain highly vigilant in crowded areas, safeguard personal belongings, and stay updated on the latest safety risk announcements through local media.

### 🗞️ Green Infrastructure

- **Nusa Dua ESG Transformation**: On May 5, Indonesia’s House of Representatives’ Seventh Committee inspected the Nusa Dua tourism area, praising its achievements in implementing Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) principles. The area has established a modern **integrated waste management system**, seawater desalination (SWRO), and LNG energy supply systems, aiming to become a model for sustainable tourism across Indonesia.
