Bali isn't devoid of toilet paper, but you might struggle to find it when you need it. This is because in Southeast Asian countries, they typically use a device known as the 'bum gun', which sprays water for cleaning, often accompanied by manual assistance.

For many, this method of cleaning might come as a shock and be hard to accept. However, it's actually more hygienic and cleaner than using toilet paper. Numerous studies have shown that washing with water is more effective at removing residue and reduces the risk of skin irritation and infections.

Some public toilets and restaurants in Bali do not provide toilet paper. If you're uncomfortable with using the 'bum gun', it's best to carry your own toilet paper.

Additionally, Bali's sewage system cannot handle toilet paper and wet wipes, so remember to dispose of them in the trash bin provided.

Encountering different cultures and ways of life while traveling is a rich and valuable experience. Although the 'bum gun' culture might seem strange and unexpected, being open to and respecting local customs and practices will make your journey all the more enriching.
