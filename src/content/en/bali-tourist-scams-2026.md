Many people DM me asking, 'Will I get scammed on my first trip to Bali?' Honestly, Bali is generally very safe, and the locals are warm and friendly. However, tourist areas always have their 'tricks.' To ensure your trip is filled with beautiful scenery and no traps, be extra cautious of these common scams in 2026.

# ⚠️ Tourist Traps You Should Walk Away From

## **The Magic of Money Exchange Shops**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9f72c79c18a0688540bdd57dd45f5fad.png)

Small roadside shops with outrageously high exchange rates often use 'quick counting' tricks to slip bills away unnoticed. Remember, abnormally high exchange rates almost always equal a scam. Stick to reputable stores with proper storefronts, and always count your money slowly at the counter after exchanging. Don’t rush to put it away just because you feel awkward.

## **The Taxi Meter Mystery**

Some drivers may claim their meter is broken or ask you to cancel your app booking and pay in cash. In such cases, there’s no need to argue—just find another taxi. Stick to **_Grab_, _Gojek_**, or the official **Blue Bird** to ensure transparent pricing.

## **Fake Visa Websites on Search Engines**

When searching for e-visas, the top results are often beautifully designed but unofficial agency sites that charge high service fees or even provide incorrect documents. Always verify the official Indonesian domain `.go.id` when applying to avoid unnecessary extra costs.

## **Disputes Over 'New Scratches' When Returning Vehicles**

While renting a scooter is convenient, it can be troublesome if you're accused of pre-existing damage when returning it. Always take a full video walkaround of the vehicle upon pickup, including all details and mileage, and never leave your original passport with the rental shop.

## **Fake Staff at Temple/Attraction Entrances**

At attraction entrances, people often claim there’s a special ceremony today and that you must donate or hire a guide to enter. Most temples clearly display ticket prices and rules at official ticket counters. If approached by aggressive solicitors, head straight to the official ticket booth.

## **New Internet Risks with Mobile Hotspots**

In recent years, there have been instances of strangers asking to borrow mobile hotspots under the pretext of having no internet access. This not only poses a risk of data leakage but also leaves a record of your connection if the other party engages in illegal activities. Politely refusing to share your hotspot or connect to unknown Wi-Fi is a basic step in protecting your personal privacy.
