Many people DM me asking, 'Will I get scammed on my first trip to Bali?' Honestly, Bali is generally very safe, and the locals are warm and friendly. However, tourist areas always have their 'tricks.' To ensure your trip is filled with beautiful scenery and not traps, be extra cautious of these common scams in 2026.

# ⚠️ Tourist Traps You Should Walk Away From

## **The Magic of Money Exchange Shops**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9f72c79c18a0688540bdd57dd45f5fad.png)

Small roadside shops with outrageously high exchange rates often use 'quick counting' tricks to slip bills out of sight. Remember, an abnormally high exchange rate almost always equals a scam. Stick to reputable shops with proper storefronts, and always count your money slowly at the counter after exchanging. Don’t rush to put it away just to avoid awkwardness.

## **The Taxi Meter Mystery**

Some drivers may claim their meter is broken or ask you to cancel your app booking and pay in cash. If this happens, don’t argue—just move on to the next taxi. Stick to **_Grab_, _Gojek_**, or the official **Blue Bird** to ensure transparent pricing.

## **Fake Visa Websites on Search Engines**

When searching for e-visas, the top results are often beautifully designed but unofficial agencies that charge high service fees or even provide incorrect documents. Always verify the official Indonesian domain `.go.id` when applying to avoid unnecessary extra payments.

## **Disputes Over "New Scratches" When Returning Rental Vehicles**

While renting a scooter is convenient, disputes over alleged damages when returning the vehicle can be troublesome. Always record a full video walkaround of the scooter upon pickup, including all details and mileage, and never leave your original passport with the rental shop.

## **Fake Staff at Temple/Attraction Entrances**

At attraction entrances, people often claim there’s a special ceremony, mandatory donation, or requirement for a guide to enter. Most temples clearly display ticket prices and rules at official ticket counters. If approached by aggressive solicitors, head directly to the official ticket booth.

## **New Internet Risks with Mobile Hotspots**

In recent years, there have been instances of strangers requesting to borrow mobile hotspots under the pretext of having no internet access. This not only poses a risk of data leakage but also leaves your records exposed if the other party engages in illegal activities. Politely refusing to share your hotspot or connect to unknown Wi-Fi networks is a fundamental step in protecting your personal privacy.
