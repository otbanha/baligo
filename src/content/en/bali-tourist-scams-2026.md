Many people have privately messaged me asking, 'Will I get scammed on my first trip to Bali?' Honestly, Bali is generally very safe, and the locals are warm and friendly. However, tourist areas always have their 'tricks.' To ensure your trip is filled with beautiful scenery and not traps, be extra cautious of these common scams in 2026.

# ⚠️ Tourist Traps You Should Walk Away From

## **The Sleight of Hand at Money Exchange Shops**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9f72c79c18a0688540bdd57dd45f5fad.png)

Small shops with outrageously high exchange rates often use 'quick counting' to steal bills from your blind spot. Remember, abnormally high exchange rates almost always mean a scam. It’s best to only use reputable stores with proper storefronts, and after exchanging money, always count it slowly at the counter yourself. Don’t rush to put it away just because you feel awkward.

## **The Taxi Meter Mystery**

Some drivers may claim the meter is broken or ask you to cancel your app order and pay in cash instead. In such cases, there’s no need to argue—just move on to the next taxi. Stick to using **_Grab_, _Gojek_**, or the official **Blue Bird** to ensure transparent pricing.

## **Fake Visa Websites on Search Engines**

When searching for e-visas, the top results are often beautifully designed but unofficial agencies that charge high service fees or even provide incorrect documents. Always verify the official Indonesian domain `.go.id` when applying to avoid unnecessary extra costs.

## **Disputes Over 'New Scratches' When Returning Rental Vehicles**

While renting a scooter is convenient, disputes over pre-existing damage when returning the vehicle can be troublesome. Always record a full video walkaround of the vehicle upon pickup, including all details and mileage, and never leave your passport with the rental shop.

## **Fake Staff at Temple/Attraction Entrances**

At attraction entrances, you may encounter individuals claiming that special ceremonies require donations or guides for entry. Most temples clearly display ticket prices and rules at official ticket counters. If approached by aggressive solicitors, proceed directly to the official ticket booth.

## **New Risks of Mobile Hotspots**

Recently, there have been instances of strangers asking to borrow mobile hotspots under the pretext of having no internet access. This not only poses a risk of data leakage but also leaves your records if the other party engages in illegal activities. Politely refusing to share your hotspot or connect to unknown Wi-Fi networks is a basic step in protecting your personal privacy.
