# ⚠️ Tourist Traps You Should Avoid

## **The Art of Money Counting at Magic Currency Exchange**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9f72c79c18a0688540bdd57dd45f5fad.png)

Small shops with outrageously high exchange rates often use 'quick counting' tricks to skim bills from your stack. Remember, abnormally high rates almost always mean a scam. Stick to reputable shops with proper storefronts, and always count your money slowly at the counter before leaving. Don't rush to accept the money just because you feel awkward.

## **The Taxi Meter Mystery**

Some drivers may claim their meter is broken or ask you to cancel your app booking and pay in cash. In such cases, there's no need to argue—simply move on to the next taxi. Stick to using **_Grab_**, **_Gojek_**, or the official **Blue Bird** to ensure transparent pricing.

## **New Network Risks of Mobile Hotspots**

In recent years, strangers have been asking to borrow mobile hotspots under the pretext of having no internet access. This not only poses a risk of data leakage but also leaves a record of your connection if the other party engages in illegal activities. Maintaining politeness while refusing to share your hotspot or connect to unknown Wi-Fi is a basic step in protecting your personal privacy.
