Mangosteen is one of the tropical fruits abundantly produced in Indonesia. This sweet and tangy fruit typically has a three-month harvest season in a given region.

However, due to Indonesia's vast territory, mangosteen harvests normally continue from November to April across different areas. In Indonesia, the mangosteen harvest season usually begins in Sumatra. Farmers in this province typically start harvesting mangosteens at the end of the year, from November to December. This is followed by Java/Bali from January to March.

The variation in mangosteen harvest seasons across Indonesia allows this tropical fruit to be available in markets for almost eight months of the year.

## Mangosteens in Bali

Bali is one of the main producers of mangosteens in Indonesia, accounting for 50% of the country's total production. The mangosteens grown in Bali are of excellent variety and have an exceptional taste! Therefore, when visiting Bali, you must not miss the opportunity to try this delicious fruit! In Indonesian, mangosteen is called "manggis".

## The Best Season to Buy Mangosteens

Like most fruits, mangosteens tend to be cheaper during the harvest season. During the harvest season, mangosteens cost around IDR 10,000 per kilogram. The price of this fruit, known as the "Queen of Tropical Fruits", increases when production decreases, with a price difference of three to four times.

To buy fresh and cheap mangosteens in Indonesia/Bali, the best time is usually between January and April.
