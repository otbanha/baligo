Mangosteen is one of the most extensively cultivated tropical fruits in Indonesia. This delightful fruit typically has a three-month harvest season in a given region.

However, due to Indonesia's vast geography, mangosteen harvesting generally spans from November to April across different areas. In Indonesia, the mangosteen harvest season usually begins in Sumatra. Farmers in this province typically start harvesting mangosteen at the end of the year, from November to December, followed by Java and Bali from January to March.

The variation in mangosteen harvest seasons across Indonesia ensures that this tropical fruit is available in markets for nearly eight months of the year.

## Mangosteen in Bali

Bali is one of the primary producers of mangosteen in Indonesia, accounting for 50% of the country's total production. The mangosteen varieties grown in Bali are of excellent quality with an exceptional taste! Therefore, when visiting Bali, don't miss the opportunity to savor this delicious fruit! In Indonesian, mangosteen is called "manggis."

## The Best Season to Buy Mangosteen

Like most fruits, mangosteen prices tend to be cheaper during the harvest season. During peak season, mangosteen costs around IDR 10,000 per kilogram. However, the price of this "Queen of Tropical Fruits" can increase three to four times when production decreases.

To buy fresh and affordable mangosteen in Indonesia/Bali, the best time is usually between January and April.
