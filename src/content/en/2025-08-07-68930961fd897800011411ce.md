This initiative, launched by the Denpasar Transportation Agency, is specifically designed for the Sanur resort area. Unlike the existing Trans Metro Dewata bus routes, the Sanur shuttle bus will operate exclusively within the Sanur region, offering a 'hop-on, hop-off' mode of travel for tourists.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4b99726fb49ba913d10972a6a4dbe.jpg)

The shuttle bus features an open design, allowing passengers to enjoy the scenic views while experiencing natural ventilation. However, some passengers have noted that the experience might be slightly affected during heavy rain or strong winds.

Passenger Putu Ardiyasa Raditya Putra shared his positive experience with the shuttle bus but pointed out that the current bus stops only have signboards and lack shelter facilities or seating. He expressed hope for a more comfortable waiting environment in the future. He added, 'Foreign tourists might be more accustomed to this mode of public transportation as it is quite common in their countries.'

The current bus route includes:

- Mertasari Beach
- Pangembak Street
- Mertasari Street
- Danau Poso Road
- Danau Tamblingan Road
- Danau Toba Road
- The final stop is the SMP Wisata area near the Sanur Special Economic Zone.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e4d803fc5df07426b0f5021662e241ee.jpg)

Regarding the electric shuttle service (Shuttle / Bemo Listrik) in Sanur, Bali, here are the details on fares and payment methods:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_52fb126f2de8603997d344ea4caf16c6.jpg)

### 1. Is There a Fee Now?

- **Fee Implemented:** The shuttle service was free during the trial operation in August 2025 but has now transitioned to official operation with fees.
- **Fare:**
  * **General Passengers:** Single ride costs approximately **5,000 IDR** (Rp 5,000).
  * **Monthly Pass:** For local employees, there is an option for a monthly pass priced at 100,000 IDR.

### 2. How to Pay?

### 2. 如何支付？

This electric shuttle service operates on a cashless payment system. Here's what you'll need before boarding:

- E-Money Card: This is the most recommended and reliable payment method. You can use prepaid cards issued by major Indonesian banks such as:
  - Mandiri e-money
  - BCA Flazz
  - BNI TapCash
  - BRI Brizzi
  - Note: These cards can be purchased and topped up at local convenience stores like Indomaret or Alfamart.
- Contactless Credit Card: Some vehicles are equipped with contactless card readers, theoretically supporting international credit cards with contactless functionality (e.g., Visa/Mastercard). However, due to potential network connectivity issues, it's safer to have a physical e-money card as a backup.
- QRIS Scan: If you have installed local Indonesian mobile payment apps (such as GoPay, OVO, Dana, etc.), you can usually complete payments by scanning the QRIS code on the vehicle.
- Cash Payment: While the official policy promotes cashless transactions, drivers may sometimes accept cash. However, tourists have reported being charged higher amounts (e.g., 10,000 IDR) when paying with cash, so electronic payments are recommended.

### 3. Service Information

- Operating Hours: Approximately from 06:00 or 07:00 in the morning until 22:00 (with the last departure around 21:00).
- Route: Mainly passes through the tourist core areas of Sanur, including Jalan Danau Tamblingan, the Mertasari area, and major hotels and beach entrances.

If you're staying in Bali, this is an eco-friendly and cost-effective option for short-distance travel!

This shuttle bus service trial is an innovative initiative by Bali authorities to address traffic congestion issues. Officials, including Bali Governor Wayan Koster, have emphasized that improving the island's transportation is crucial for enhancing residents' quality of life and ensuring the sustainable development of tourism.

In addition to land-based improvements, the local government is actively developing 'water taxi' services. Recently launched, the new water shuttle route connects Benoa and Tanjung Benoa, significantly reducing the travel time from approximately 30 minutes by road to just 10-15 minutes by sea.

In the coming months, the government plans to introduce a high-speed boat service connecting Bali Airport and Canggu, expected to be operational by late 2025 or early 2026.

The Badung Regency government has confirmed that related plans are in the final stages of development, aiming to provide travelers with more efficient and comfortable transportation options.

## Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

Is this service exclusively for tourists?

While primarily catering to tourists, local residents are also welcome to use the service.

**Are the bus stations well-equipped?**

Currently, most bus stops feature simple signage and lack sheltered waiting areas or seating, though upgrades are planned for the future.

**Will this service expand to other areas?**

For now, it's limited to the Sanur area. Future expansion will depend on the success of this trial operation.
