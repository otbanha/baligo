### Introduction to Kumbasari Art Market, Bali's largest wholesale art market

On the second day of our Bali trip, we were ready to embark on a shopping adventure. Here's our plan: we leisurely headed out at noon, first enjoying a hearty lunch at a small restaurant opened by our Bali driver Gede Arimbawa and his wife. Feeling energized, we were ready for the adventure ahead!

In the afternoon, we visited Kumbasari Art Market, Bali's largest wholesale art market (open until 5 PM daily). This market is fantastic to explore, offering a wide variety of Balinese handicrafts, paintings, and souvenirs. Whatever you're looking for, you'll find it here—from paintings to sculptures and handmade items, the choices are endless! Whether you're shopping for yourself or looking for a special gift for friends or family, this is definitely the place to be!

Next, we headed to Taman Kumbasari, a super scenic riverside park. For more details, check here.

In the evening, we visited Pasar Kreneng, Bali's largest night market. This market is packed with various snacks, clothes, and handicrafts—it's like an upgraded version of Taiwan's night markets! The delicious street food here will keep you coming back for more—it's absolutely mouthwatering.

We returned to the riverside park at night, where the lights sparkled, and the night view was stunning! Standing there, looking at the nighttime scenery of Bali, it felt like stepping into a dream world.

The most exciting part of today's itinerary was finding three beautiful goddess outfits at the art wholesale market featured in our video below!
