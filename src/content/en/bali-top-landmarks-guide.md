## GWK Cultural Park

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fe7847c15db88a7191a1f728c33d7bbe.jpg)

GWK Cultural Park is one of the few government-supported attractions in Bali, located near Jimbaran. It’s renowned for its grand sculptures, impressive architecture, and integration of Balinese culture. The highlight is the 121-meter-tall statue of Shiva fused with Garuda, the fourth tallest in the world. The park also hosts various cultural events and performances, where you can enjoy traditional Balinese dances and music. Inside, there’s a cultural center offering in-depth insights into Balinese heritage.

## Pura Besakih

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_145940526f93b2b01a3a93bf4e89daf3.jpg)

Pura Besakih is one of Bali's most significant landmarks and one of the largest and holiest Hindu temples on the island, located on the slopes of Mount Agung. This temple is considered the spiritual heart of Bali and is revered as the "Mother Temple," featuring multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

The grand temple complex of Pura Besakih is nestled amidst beautiful valleys and lush rice fields, offering breathtaking views. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere, explore the intricate temple architecture, and experience the sacred ambiance. Additionally, Pura Besakih serves as an important venue for many of Bali's religious and cultural events, such as festivals and ceremonies.

## Monkey Forest & Pura Taman Saraswati

## Tirta Empul Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2671370a86c84863c3c5f1ca502e8e1e.jpg)

Besides Bali's most famous Tirta Empul Temple, there are two other hidden holy water temples tucked away in quiet corners, not far from each other, waiting to be discovered. These temples not only allow you to experience Bali's unique religious culture but also offer a sincere and tranquil bathing experience in a relatively serene environment.

Here are two other sacred water temples we recommend for you:

**Dalem Pingit Sacred Water** is a relatively hidden temple. This temple not only boasts unique architectural styles but also its sacred spring is deeply revered by local devotees. In this tranquil environment, you can feel the sincerity and peace of Bali's primal religion. Dalem Pingit is famous for its beautiful waterfall spring.

**Pura Gunung Kawi Sacred Water** is a temple renowned for its rock carvings. Besides ancient statues, there is also a hidden sacred spring here, offering blessings to devotees and visitors through bathing. This serene temple area is filled with a sense of mystery, making you feel as if you've traveled through time to experience Bali's ancient sanctity.

These two different sacred water temples each have their unique charm and cultural depth, providing a relatively secluded bathing environment, allowing you to find peace and purification in Bali's sacred atmosphere.

## Tanah Lot Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4627595c4f7e1a1a61c20ebb613256f0.jpg)

Bali's Tanah Lot is a famous landmark, renowned for its magnificent seaside temple. This temple is built on a massive rock and is one of Indonesia's most famous Hindu temples. At sunset, visitors gather here to admire the stunning scenery illuminated by the setting sun, making it one of Bali's top ten famous landmarks.

However, because this place is so famous, if you choose to visit in the evening, be mindful of the transportation issues to and from.

## Pura Ulun Danau

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7b504fc83d34c26e674b57a1c762bf58.jpg)

Pura Ulun Danau was completed in 1633 and is located by the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali, also known as the "Bratan Water Temple." Since Lake Bratan is the main irrigation source for central Bali, Pura Ulun Danau was once used as a ceremonial site to worship Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers in Bali. Additionally, the 11-tiered Pura Ulun Danau is also a temple built for Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, with a statue of Buddha enshrined within, making it a temple where Hinduism and Buddhism blend.

What's particularly special is that this water temple in Bali is featured on Indonesian currency, specifically on the second largest denomination currently in circulation - the 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah note, which is approximately equivalent to USD$3.

## Jatiluwih Rice Terrace

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6b26a35c2746bdb8c6ba7f898bc97822.jpg)

The terraced landscapes surrounding Ubud are one of Bali's highlights, presenting a breathtaking scene like a painted scroll, and have been honored with UNESCO World Heritage status. There are two main rice terrace areas in Ubud:

1. Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular terrace areas in Ubud. The unique layout of this terrace area, with its winding paths between the fields, resembles a green canvas that vividly displays nature's vitality. Here, the rolling waves of rice create a moving agricultural poem, blending natural beauty with farming culture.
2. Another less commercialized terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, though it's farther from Ubud, requiring a 90-minute drive from Ubud Palace. However, it offers a more authentic experience of the terraces' beauty!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At this moment, golden sunlight bathes the terraces, creating rich layers of shadows and a mesmerizing play of light. This is the favorite time for photographers and visitors alike, as the terraces seem to be immersed in a golden dream.
