## GWK Cultural Park

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fe7847c15db88a7191a1f728c33d7bbe.jpg)

GWK Cultural Park is one of Bali’s few government-supported attractions, located near Jimbaran. Known for its grand sculptures, stunning architecture, and integration of Balinese culture, its most famous feature is the 121-meter-tall statue of Shiva fused with Garuda, the fourth tallest in the world. The park hosts various cultural events and performances, including traditional dances and music. It also features a cultural center offering insights into Balinese heritage.

## Pura Besakih

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_145940526f93b2b01a3a93bf4e89daf3.jpg)

Pura Besakih is one of Bali's most significant landmarks and the island's largest and holiest Hindu temple, located on the slopes of Mount Agung. Known as the 'Mother Temple,' it is considered the spiritual heart of Bali, featuring multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

The grand temple complex is set amidst stunning valleys and lush rice fields, offering breathtaking views. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere, explore the intricate temple architecture, and experience the sacred ambiance. Pura Besakih is also a key venue for many of Bali's religious and cultural events, such as festivals and ceremonies.

## Tirta Empul Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2671370a86c84863c3c5f1ca502e8e1e.jpg)

Besides Bali's most famous Tirta Empul Temple, there are two other hidden holy spring temples tucked away in quiet corners, all within reasonable distance, waiting to be discovered. These temples not only allow you to experience Bali's unique religious culture but also offer a sincere and tranquil bathing experience in a relatively peaceful environment.

Here are two more sacred water temples we recommend for you:

**Dalem Pingit Sacred Water** is a relatively hidden temple. This temple not only boasts unique architectural styles but also holds sacred springs deeply revered by local devotees. In this tranquil environment, you can feel the sincerity and peace of Bali's original religion. Dalem Pingit is famous for its beautiful waterfall springs.

**Pura Gunung Kawi Sacred Water** is a temple renowned for its rock carvings. In addition to ancient statues, there is also a hidden sacred spring here, offering blessings to devotees and visitors through ritual bathing. This serene temple area is filled with a sense of mystery, transporting you back in time to experience Bali's ancient sanctity.

These two different sacred water temples each have their unique charm and cultural depth, providing a relatively secluded bathing environment where you can find peace and purification within Bali's sacred atmosphere.

## Tanah Lot Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4627595c4f7e1a1a61c20ebb613256f0.jpg)

Tanah Lot in Bali is a famous landmark, renowned for its magnificent seaside temple. Built on a massive rock, this temple is one of Indonesia's most famous Hindu temples. At sunset, visitors gather here to admire the stunning scenery bathed in the setting sun, making it one of Bali's top ten famous landmarks.

However, due to its popularity, if you choose to visit in the evening, be mindful of transportation issues when returning.

## Pura Ulun Danau Ulun Danu Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7b504fc83d34c26e674b57a1c762bf58.jpg)

Completed in 1633, Pura Ulun Danau is located on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali, also known as the "Bratan Water Temple." Since Lake Bratan serves as the primary water source for irrigation in central Bali, Ulun Danu Temple was once used as a ceremonial site to worship Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers in Bali. Additionally, the 11-tiered Ulun Danu Temple was built for Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, and a statue of Buddha is also enshrined here, making it a temple that blends Hinduism and Buddhism.

Notably, this Balinese water temple is featured on the Indonesian currency, specifically on the second-largest denomination currently in circulation: 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah, which is approximately USD$3.20.

## Jatiluwih Rice Terrace

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6b26a35c2746bdb8c6ba7f898bc97822.jpg)

The terraced rice fields around Ubud are one of Bali's highlights, offering a breathtaking landscape that has earned UNESCO World Heritage status. Ubud has two main rice terrace areas:

1. Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular rice terrace areas in Ubud. The layout of this terraced region is unique, with winding paths between the fields creating a green canvas that vividly showcases nature's vitality. Here, the rolling waves of rice form a poetic blend of natural beauty and agricultural culture.
2. The less commercialized Jatiluwih Rice Terrace is farther from Ubud, requiring a 90-minute drive from Ubud Palace as a starting point, but it offers a more authentic experience of the terraced fields' beauty!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn, just as the sun rises. At this moment, golden sunlight bathes the terraces, casting rich layers of shadows and creating an enchanting play of light and shadow. This is the favorite time for photographers and visitors alike, as the terraces seem immersed in a golden dreamscape.

## Travel Map & How to Plan?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c6e1c88da233ba83e7e292988927222c.png)

If you plan to visit the "Top Ten Landmarks," we recommend arranging your itinerary as follows:

1. Start with a 3-4 day stay in Ubud to immerse yourself in the local culture and scenery;
2. Then move to Sanur for an overnight stay before heading to Nusa Penida for a day trip;
