In summary: This new policy is not about conducting a 'census' of all travelers at customs, but rather formalizing immigration officers' existing verification powers and digitizing the process. The goal is to accurately screen quality tourists while cracking down on illegal work.

## 1. Who Are the Main Targets of 'Strict Verification'?

The new system****does not apply to all entrants****, but rather combines 'targeted checks' with 'digital pre-screening':

The new regulation **does not apply to all inbound travelers**, but rather adopts a combined approach of 'targeted spot checks' and 'digital pre-screening':

- Digital pre-screening: Most reviews will be completed in the backend system when you apply for an electronic visa (e-VOA) or fill out the entry health declaration.

- General tourists: If you're planning a week-long vacation and have booked a star-rated hotel, the system will usually let you pass without much interference.

## 2. The True Purpose of 'Bank Deposit Proof'

- Combating Illegal Work: Officials primarily examine transaction records to identify regular "salary deposits" or "service payments." For them, travelers with accounts showing only expenditures and no fixed income are considered more purely "consumers."
- Deposit Amount Standards:

1. Official Recommendation: Hold $2,000 or more (approximately NT$63,000).
2. Practical Operation: For most short-term travelers, simply showing a mobile banking screenshot proving sufficient funds to cover trip expenses is acceptable.

## 3. At Customs: Primarily "Random Checks"

Immigration officers have always had the legal authority to request proof of financial means from travelers, though it was rarely enforced in the past. Starting in March, they will have clearer grounds to conduct "random checks."

- Triggers for Inspection: If officers find your attire, behavior, or responses about your itinerary suspicious, they may ask you to provide proof of financial means and a detailed itinerary on the spot.
- Itinerary Planning is Key: If you claim to stay somewhere for two weeks but cannot name a single hotel, this could be sufficient grounds for denial of entry.

## 💡 Conclusion: Should Regular Tourists Worry?

Not at all. Especially for tourists planning short trips of 5 to 10 days, as long as you are traveling legitimately and have the following documents prepared, the chance of being stopped is very low, and the customs process will be smooth:

1. Complete accommodation bookings (not just for the first day, preferably covering the entire trip).
2. Confirmed return flight tickets.

Policy Target Group: This policy mainly aims to deter long-term travelers on a tight budget who want to "work while traveling," as well as those illegally working as guides, photographers, or renters locally.

## 📋 Essential Documents for Entering Bali Starting March 2026

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_164e1666147c790e2ecfa35259c44293.png)

### Essential Documents

- Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months.
- Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA): Recommended to apply online in advance. (See [details](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/))
- Return/onward flight ticket: Ensure the date aligns with the visa's allowed stay period.

### Electronic Declaration

- [All Indonesia Arrival Card](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/) (AIAC): Fill out online within 72 hours before departure.
- Bali Tourist Levy: Pay IDR 150,000 online and save the payment receipt.

### New System Spot Check Documents

- For non-B1 tourist visas (stays exceeding 30 days), **bank statements are mandatory**: Provide bank statements from the last three months (PDF or online banking screenshots are acceptable), with a recommended minimum balance of $2,000. Officers will check for regular "salary deposits."
- Detailed travel itinerary: Must be able to specify accommodation locations and planned tourist activities.

Final reminder: Organize all electronic documents (PDFs or screenshots) in a folder on your phone. If randomly checked, proactively and cooperatively present them, and you'll usually clear customs in a minute or two. Do not alter documents, as authorities have implemented AI verification systems to detect fraud. Violators face immediate deportation and lifetime blacklisting.

## 🏛️ Bali 2026 High-Quality Tourism: FAQ on Financial Proof Checks

**Q1: Will everyone be asked to show their bank account upon entry?**

> **No.** While the policy nominally applies to all foreign travelers, in practice it involves **random spot checks**:
> >
> - **Applying for visas over 60 days (e.g., C1/D1)**: You **must** upload three months of bank statements during the application process. This is mandatory.
> - **Holding a 30-day e-VOA**: **Random spot checks** are conducted. If you have a confirmed return ticket and reservations at reputable hotels, the chances of being checked are extremely low.
> - **Key targets**: Frequent travelers to Bali (suspected of visa runs), those staying for exceptionally long periods, or individuals unable to provide a clear itinerary are the primary focus of immigration officers.

**Q2: What is the 'recommended balance' for proof of financial means?**

> **The official recommended benchmark is USD$2,000 (approximately HKD$16,000 / NT$65,000).** This is not a rigid figure, but rather a 'safe zone'. If you're only visiting for 5 days and staying at a luxury resort, having slightly less than this amount usually won't be an issue; however, if you're planning to stay for a month, this sum serves as crucial evidence that you 'won't be working illegally in the country'.

**Q3: Can I show the immigration officer my mobile banking app directly?**

> **Yes.** The authorities currently accept digital proof. In case you're randomly checked at customs, you can directly open your mobile banking app to show your real-time balance. However, the most reliable method is to keep a **PDF of your bank statement from the last three months** saved on your phone.

**Q4: Why check three months of records instead of just the current balance?**

> The authorities aim to eliminate situations where individuals 'temporarily borrow money to meet requirements.' Immigration officers will assess whether your financial flow is stable and check for frequent 'unexplained work-related deposits' (used to determine if you are already working remotely illegally in the country).

**Q5: Besides bank deposits, what other documents can serve as supplementary proof?**

> If your account balance doesn't quite meet the requirements, the following documents can also be helpful:
> >
> - **Credit Limit Proof** (to demonstrate emergency payment capability).
> - **Salary Slips**.
> - **Prepaid Hotel Itinerary Receipts** (to show that you've already covered major expenses).

**Q6: What happens if I don't cooperate with the verification or if my financial proof is insufficient?**

> According to new regulations effective in 2026, immigration officers have the authority to **deny entry and deport visitors** on the grounds of 'not meeting high-quality tourism standards' or 'posing a risk of illegal employment'.

**Q7: What about homemakers, elderly, and children without income?**

For family travel, immigration checks primarily focus on the main sponsor:

> Housewives and children: No need to provide personal bank statements. When applying for an e-VOA or during on-site checks, you can present the bank statements of your spouse or parents, along with proof of relationship (such as marriage certificate, birth certificate, or household registration photos stored on your phone for backup).
> Shared financial proof: If a single account balance significantly exceeds $2,000 (for example, a family of four with over $8,000 in the account), this will be considered as the entire group having sufficient financial resources.

**Q8: Which travelers are more likely to be questioned?**

The following situations are more likely to prompt questions from immigration officers:
> One-way ticket
> No hotel reservation
> Extended stay duration
> Unclear travel itinerary
> The immigration officer primarily aims to confirm whether the traveler has **sufficient funds and a reasonable travel plan**.

**Q9: What happens if I don't have financial proof?**

> If selected for inspection and unable to demonstrate financial capacity
> Immigration officers have the authority to:
> Conduct further questioning
> Request additional documentation
> In the most severe cases, may deny entry
> However, such situations are actually very rare.

**Q10: Essential Documents Required for Entry to Bali**

The most important documents for general travelers currently are:
✔ Passport (valid for 6 months)
✔ Return or onward flight ticket
✔ Visa (or visa on arrival)
✔ Accommodation booking confirmation
✔ Simple itinerary plan
✔ Bali tourism tax QR code
If you also prepare a **bank balance screenshot or PDF bank statement**, you'll be fully covered.
