In a nutshell: This new policy is not about conducting a 'census' of all travelers at customs, but rather formalizing and digitizing immigration officers' existing verification powers. The goal is to accurately screen quality tourists while cracking down on illegal work.

Below we break down the actual implementation details of the policy to help you understand if you might be affected:

## 1. Who Are the Main Targets of 'Strict Verification'?

The new system **does not target all entrants**, but rather combines 'targeted spot checks' with 'digital pre-screening':

- High-risk groups (key verification): Officials will pay special attention to travelers with long stays (e.g., over 30 days), frequent short-term entries, or those with illogical itineraries in the online declaration system.
- Digital pre-screening: Most reviews will be completed in the backend system when you apply for an electronic visa (e-VOA) or fill out the entry health declaration.
- General tourists: If you're planning a week-long vacation and have booked a star-rated hotel, the system will usually let you pass without much interference.

## 2. The True Purpose of 'Bank Deposit Proof'

## 2. 「銀行存款證明」的真正用意

The primary purpose of this requirement is to 'prevent illegal employment,' not to assess how wealthy you are.

- Combating illegal work: Officials mainly observe whether there are regular 'salary deposits' or 'service payments' through transaction records. For them, a traveler whose account only shows consumption expenses without fixed income is considered a more genuine 'consumer.'
- Deposit amount standards:

1. Official recommendation: Hold at least USD$2,000 (approximately NT$63,000).
2. Practical operation: For most short-term travelers, simply showing a mobile banking screenshot proving sufficient funds to cover the trip expenses is acceptable.

## 3. Customs On-site: Primarily 'Random Checks'

Immigration officers have always had the legal authority to request proof of financial means from travelers, though it was rarely enforced in the past. Starting in March, they will have clearer grounds to conduct 'random checks.'

- Triggers for inspection: If officers find your attire, behavior, or answers about your itinerary suspicious, they will ask you to present proof of financial means and a detailed itinerary on the spot.
- Itinerary planning is key: If you claim to stay somewhere for two weeks but cannot name a single hotel, this will constitute sufficient grounds for denial of entry.

## 💡 Conclusion: Should Regular Tourists Worry?

Not at all. Especially for tourists planning short trips of 5 to 10 days, as long as you are traveling legitimately and have the following documents prepared, the chance of being stopped is extremely low, and the customs process will be very smooth:

1. Complete accommodation bookings (not just for the first day, preferably covering the entire itinerary).
2. Confirmed return flight tickets.

Target audience of the policy: This policy mainly aims to deter low-budget long-term travelers who want to 'work while traveling,' as well as those illegally working as guides, photographers, or renting accommodations locally.

## 📋 Essential Documents for Bali Entry Starting March 2026

### Core Documents

- Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months.
- Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA): Recommended to apply online in advance. (See [instructions](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/))
- Return/Onward Flight Ticket: Ensure dates align with visa stay period.

### Electronic Declarations

- [All Indonesia Arrival Card](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/) (AIAC): Complete online within 72 hours before departure.
- Bali Tourist Levy: Pay IDR 150,000 online and save the payment receipt.

### New System Spot Check Documents

- Non-B1 Tourist Visa (over 30 days) **Bank Statement Required**: Recent 3-month bank statement (PDF or screenshot acceptable), with a minimum balance of USD$2,000. Officials will check for regular "salary deposits."
- Detailed Itinerary: Must be able to specify accommodation locations and travel activities.

Final Reminder: Organize electronic copies (PDFs or screenshots) of all documents in a folder on your phone. If selected for a random check, proactively and cooperatively present them, usually allowing smooth clearance within a minute or two. Do not alter documents, as authorities have implemented AI verification systems to detect fraud, with violators facing immediate deportation and lifetime blacklisting.

## 🏛️ Bali 2026 High-Quality Tourism: Financial Proof Verification FAQ

**Q1: Will everyone be asked to show bank statements upon entry?**

> **No.** While the policy nominally applies to all foreign travelers, in practice it's implemented through **random spot checks**:
> >
> - **Applying for visas over 60 days (e.g., C1/D1)**: You **must** upload three months of bank statements during the application process — this is mandatory.
> - **Holding a 30-day e-VOA**: **Random spot checks** are conducted. If you have a confirmed return ticket and bookings at reputable hotels, the chance of being checked is extremely low.
> - **Key targets**: Frequent travelers to Bali (suspected visa runners), those staying for exceptionally long periods, or individuals unable to provide a clear itinerary are the primary focus of immigration officers.

**Q2: What is the 'recommended balance' for proof of funds?**

> **The official benchmark is USD$2,000 (approx. HK$16,000 / NT$65,000).** This isn't a rigid number but rather a 'safe zone.' If you're only staying for 5 days at a luxury resort, a balance slightly below this amount may still suffice. However, if you're staying for a month, this amount serves as crucial evidence that you 'won't engage in illegal work locally.'

**Q3: Can I simply show the immigration officer my mobile banking app?**

> **Yes.** The authorities currently accept digital proof. If you're randomly checked at customs, you can directly open your mobile banking app to show your real-time balance. However, the safest approach is to keep a **PDF of your bank statements from the last three months** saved on your phone.

**Q4: Why do they check 'three months' of records? Can't they just look at the current balance?**

> Because officials want to rule out cases of **temporarily borrowing money to meet the requirement**. Immigration officers will observe whether your funds flow steadily and check for frequent 'unexplained work-related deposits' (to determine if you're already engaged in illegal remote work locally).

**Q5: Besides bank deposits, what other documents can serve as supplementary proof?**

> If your account balance doesn't quite meet the requirement, the following documents can also help:
> >
> - **Credit limit proof** (demonstrating emergency payment capability).
> - **Salary slips**.
> - **Prepaid hotel booking receipts** (showing you've already covered major expenses).

**Q6: What happens if I don't cooperate with the check or fail to provide sufficient proof of funds?**

> According to new regulations effective 2026, immigration officers have the authority to **deny entry and deport visitors** based on "failure to meet high-quality tourism standards" or "risk of illegal employment."

**Q7: What about homemakers, elderly, and children without income?**

For family travel, immigration checks typically focus on the "primary sponsor":

> Homemakers and children: No need to provide personal bank statements. When applying for e-VOA or during random checks, you can present your spouse's or parent's bank statement along with proof of relationship (such as marriage certificate, birth certificate, or household registration photos saved on your phone).
> Shared funds proof: If one account balance significantly exceeds $2,000 (e.g., $8,000+ for a family of four), this will be considered sufficient financial proof for the entire group.

**Q8: Which travelers are more likely to be questioned?**

> The following situations are more likely to prompt immigration inquiries:
> One-way ticket
> No hotel booking
> Extended stay
> Unclear travel plans
> Immigration officers mainly want to confirm that travelers **have sufficient funds and a reasonable travel plan.**

**Q9: What happens if I don't have financial proof?**

> If you're selected for inspection and cannot provide financial proof
> Immigration officers may:
> Ask further questions
> Request additional documents
> In severe cases, deny entry
> However, such situations are actually very rare.

**Q10: Essential documents for entering Bali**

> Currently, the most important documents for general travelers are:
> ✔ Passport (6 months validity)
> ✔ Return or onward ticket
> ✔ Visa (or visa on arrival)
> ✔ Accommodation booking
> ✔ Simple travel itinerary
> ✔ Bali tourism tax QR code
> If you additionally prepare a **bank balance screenshot or PDF bank statement**, you'll be fully prepared.
