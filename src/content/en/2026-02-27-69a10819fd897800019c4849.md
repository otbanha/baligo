In summary: This new policy is not about conducting a 'census' of all travelers at customs, but rather formalizing immigration officers' existing verification powers and digitizing the process. The goal is to accurately screen quality tourists while cracking down on illegal work.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a1316fab9941800e7f0a4551426ccb6e.png)

Below, we break down the practical implementation details of the policy to help you understand whether you might be affected:

## 1. Who Are the Main Targets of 'Strict Verification'?

The new system****does not apply to all entrants****, but rather combines 'targeted checks' with 'digital pre-screening':

- High-risk groups (targeted checks): Officials will pay special attention to travelers staying for extended periods (e.g., over 30 days), those frequently entering and exiting within a short time frame, or those with illogical itineraries in the online declaration system.

- Digital pre-screening: Most reviews will be completed in the backend system when you apply for an electronic visa (e-VOA) or fill out the entry health declaration.

- General tourists: If you're planning a week-long vacation and have booked a star-rated hotel, the system will usually let you pass without much interference.

## 2. The True Purpose of 'Bank Deposit Proof'

- Combating Illegal Work: Officials primarily examine transaction records to identify regular "salary deposits" or "service payments." For them, travelers with accounts showing only expenditures and no fixed income are considered more purely "consumers."
- Deposit Amount Standards:

1. Official Recommendation: Hold $2,000 or more (approximately NT$63,000).
2. Practical Operation: For most short-term travelers, simply showing a mobile banking screenshot proving sufficient funds to cover trip expenses is acceptable.

## 3. At Customs: Primarily "Random Checks"

Immigration officers have always had the legal authority to request proof of financial means from travelers, though it was rarely enforced in the past. Starting in March, they will have clearer grounds to conduct "random checks."

- Triggers for Inspection: If officers find your attire, behavior, or responses about your itinerary suspicious, they may ask you to provide proof of financial means and a detailed itinerary on the spot.
- Itinerary Planning is Key: If you claim to stay somewhere for two weeks but cannot name a single hotel, this could be sufficient grounds for denial of entry.

## 💡 Conclusion: Should Regular Tourists Worry?

Not at all. Especially for tourists planning short trips of 5 to 10 days, as long as you are traveling legitimately and have the following documents prepared, the chance of being stopped is very low, and the customs process will be smooth:

1. Complete accommodation bookings (not just for the first day, preferably covering the entire trip).
2. Confirmed return flight tickets.

Policy Target Group: This policy mainly aims to deter long-term travelers on a tight budget who want to "work while traveling," as well as those illegally working as guides, photographers, or renters locally.

## 📋 Essential Documents for Entering Bali Starting March 2026

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_164e1666147c790e2ecfa35259c44293.png)

### Essential Documents

- Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months.
- Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA): Recommended to apply online in advance. (See [details](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/))
- Return/onward flight ticket: Ensure the date aligns with the visa's allowed stay period.

### Electronic Declaration

- [All Indonesia Arrival Card](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/) (AIAC): Fill out online within 72 hours before departure.
- Bali Tourist Levy: Pay IDR 150,000 online and save the payment receipt.

### New System Spot Check Documents

- Non-B1 tourist visas (stays over 30 days) ****must provide proof of funds****: Bank statements from the last three months (PDF or online banking screenshots are acceptable), with a recommended balance of at least USD$2,000. Officers will check for regular "salary deposits."
- Detailed travel itinerary: Must specify accommodation locations and planned activities.

Final reminder: Organize all electronic documents (PDFs or screenshots) in a folder on your phone. If randomly checked, proactively and cooperatively present them, and you'll usually clear customs in a minute or two. Do not alter documents, as authorities have implemented AI verification systems to detect fraud. Violators face immediate deportation and lifetime blacklisting.

## 🏛️ Bali 2026 High-Quality Tourism: FAQ on Financial Proof Checks

****Q1: Will everyone be asked to show bank account details upon entry?****

> ****No.**** While the policy nominally applies to all foreign travelers, in practice, it's implemented through ****random spot checks****:
>
> - ****Applying for visas over 60 days (e.g., C1/D1)****: Uploading three months of bank statements is ****mandatory**** during the application process.
> - ****Holding a 30-day e-VOA****: Subject to ****random spot checks (Spot Check)****. If you have a confirmed return ticket and a booking with a reputable hotel, the chance of being checked is very low.
> - ****Key targets****: Frequent visitors to Bali (suspected of visa runs), extremely long stays, or those unable to provide a detailed itinerary are the immigration officers' primary focus.

****Q2: What is the recommended balance for financial proof?****

> ****The official benchmark is USD$2,000 (approximately HKD$16,000 / NT$65,000).**** This is not a rigid number but rather a 'safe zone.' If you're only visiting for 5 days and staying at a luxury resort, a balance slightly below this amount is usually acceptable. However, if you plan to stay for a month, this amount serves as crucial evidence that you 'will not engage in illegal work in the country.'

****Q3: Can I show the immigration officer my mobile banking app directly?****

> ****Yes.**** The authorities currently accept digital proof. If you are randomly checked at customs, you can directly open your mobile banking app to display your real-time balance. However, the safest approach is to keep a ****PDF of your bank statement from the last three months**** saved on your phone.

****Q4: Why do they check 'three months' of records? Can't they just look at the current balance?****

> Because the authorities want to rule out the possibility of ****borrowing money temporarily to meet the requirement****. Immigration officers will assess whether your financial flow is stable and check for frequent 'unexplained work-related deposits' (to determine if you are already engaging in illegal remote work in the country).

****Q5: Besides bank deposits, what other documents can be used as supplementary proof?****

> If your account balance doesn’t quite meet the requirement, the following documents can also help:
>
> - ****Credit limit proof**** (to demonstrate emergency payment capability).
> - ****Salary slips****.
> - ****Receipts for prepaid hotel bookings**** (to show you’ve already covered significant expenses).

****Q6: What happens if I refuse to cooperate with the check or fail to provide sufficient financial proof?****

> According to the new regulations effective in 2026, immigration officers have the authority to ****deny entry and deport you on the same flight**** based on 'failure to meet high-quality tourism standards' or 'risk of illegal employment.'

****Q7: What about housewives, elderly, and children without income?****

For family travel, immigration checks primarily focus on the main sponsor:

> Housewives and children: No need to provide personal bank statements. When applying for an e-VOA or during on-site checks, you can present your spouse's or parent's bank statement along with proof of relationship (such as marriage certificate, birth certificate, or household registration photos stored on your phone as backup).

> Shared financial proof: If one account balance significantly exceeds $2,000 (e.g., $8,000+ for a family of four), it will be considered sufficient funds for the entire group.

****Q8: Which travelers are more likely to be questioned?****

> The following situations may lead to immigration officer inquiries:

> One-way ticket

> No hotel booking

> Long stay duration
