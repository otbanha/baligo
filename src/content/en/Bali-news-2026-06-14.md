## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: 2026 Bali Arts Festival Grand Opening (President Absent) & New Island-Wide Fuel Prices

- **President Prabowo Unexpectedly Absent! The 48th Bali Arts Festival (PKB) Opened by the Minister of Culture, Still a Spectacular Event**: The annual cultural extravaganza eagerly anticipated by global travelers and local residents—the **48th Bali Arts Festival (Pesta Kesenian Bali / PKB) officially kicked off on the afternoon of June 13 in Denpasar**. Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto, who was originally confirmed to attend the royal review and ribbon-cutting ceremony, was unexpectedly absent. The opening ceremony was instead led by the Indonesian Minister of Culture, who conveyed the President's highest respect for the preservation of Balinese traditional culture in his speech. Although the event lacked the head of state's personal flag presentation, the atmosphere remained electric. The classic opening parade (Peed Aya), featuring 20,000 artists mobilized from all nine regencies across the province, made a stunning appearance in front of the Bajra Sandhi Monument, drawing tens of thousands of international independent travelers and locals, packing the Renon area to the brim.
- **New Price Signs at Gas Stations! Premium Gasoline Soars 32% as New Policy Takes Effect, High-Grade Diesel Frozen at Record Highs**: In line with the latest official data released by state-owned oil company Pertamina, the new policy raising **Pertamax (RON 92) premium gasoline by 32.1% has been fully implemented**. Gas stations across the island, including those in popular areas like **Canggu** and the southern region, have updated their Pertamax prices to **16,250 Indonesian Rupiah per liter** (around 16,650 Rupiah at some stations after local fuel taxes). Notably, **Dexlite high-grade diesel (23,000 Rupiah per liter), commonly used by mid-to-large tourist vans (like the Toyota HiAce), has been completely frozen** in this round of adjustments. Although diesel didn't see a new increase, it was already at historically high levels. Combined with the sharp rise in gasoline for self-drivers, transportation budgets during the peak summer season are facing significant inflationary pressure.

## 📄 Topics of Interest to Tourists: Arts Festival Venue Relocation & Impact of Illegal Villa Crackdown

- **Tourist Route Reminder: Grand Parade Concluded; All Activities Move to 'Art Centre' Starting Today (June 14)**: Following the successful conclusion of yesterday's street parade and road closures in the city center, the Cultural Office reminds international tourists that the festival's exciting programs, which will run for a full month (until July 11), will now move to the main venue—the Taman Werdhi Budaya Art Centre. From afternoon to evening, the main venue will feature non-stop traditional shadow puppet shows, masked dance performances, and handicraft markets. All road closures around the Renon area in Denpasar have been lifted, and traffic has returned to normal. Tourists heading to the Art Centre are advised to use two-wheeled scooters for better access and to avoid traffic congestion around the venue.

- **Bali Villa Association (BVA) Continues Strict Inspections; Non-Compliant Private Villas in Canggu and Southern Bali Face Tax Audits**: As the tourist season brings a surge of visitors, the Bali Villa Association (BVA), in collaboration with local law enforcement, is intensifying compliance checks on private vacation villas. Authorities are focusing on individuals in areas like Canggu and Uluwatu who are subletting properties without properly declaring local taxes (PHR) or violating spatial building permits (PGB/IMB). The BVA once again urges international independent travelers to choose reputable, fully compliant property management brands when booking entire villas, to avoid any regulatory disputes that could disrupt their vacation plans and insurance coverage.
