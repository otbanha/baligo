Here’s a summary of the anticipated **Bali news for April 21 (Tuesday)**:

### 🗞️ Top Stories: Environmental Governance and Policy Shifts

- **Countdown to 'Open Dumping' Ban**: As major landfills like Suwung undergo transformation, the central government today reiterated its strict enforcement of the 'ban on open dumping of waste' policy. The provincial government is accelerating the construction of regional waste processing centers (TPST) and urging the tourism industry and residents to implement waste sorting. This means that in the coming months, waste disposal costs in some areas may be passed on to consumers, or temporary waste accumulation may occur.
- **Tourism Market Regionalization Strategy**: In response to uncertainties in global long-haul flights, the Bali Tourism Board (Dinpar) announced today that the target for 2026 is to attract 6.62 million international tourists, with a complete shift in focus to the **Asia-Pacific region** (Australia, India, China, Japan, South Korea, and Southeast Asia). More direct flights and promotional activities targeting Asia-Pacific travelers will be introduced.

### 🌤️ Weather Forecast

- **Today’s Overview**: Today (April 21) is expected to be **cloudy with occasional showers or heavy rain**, transitioning to **cloudy** in the evening.
- **Temperature and Humidity**: Temperatures will range between **25°C - 31°C**, with a feels-like temperature of around 25°C.

### 📄 Topics of Interest for Tourists: Practical Checks and Traffic Alerts

- **Enhanced Tourism Tax Checks**: Officials reminded today that starting April, random checks for the **IDR 150,000 tourism tax** payment proof will be intensified at popular attractions (such as Uluwatu and Garuda Wisnu Kencana) and major transportation hubs. Tourists are advised to save a screenshot of the QR Code generated by 'Love Bali' on their phones to avoid delays during checks due to poor signal in mountainous areas.
- **'Bee Swarm' Phenomenon on the Sea Toll Road**: Yesterday, Bali Mandara Sea Toll Road experienced a large-scale bee migration, causing temporary traffic disruptions. Experts attribute this to the recent seasonal transition with extreme heat and wind patterns. Motorcyclists traveling on this route today should remain calm and slow down if encountering a swarm, avoiding panicked swatting on the bridge.
- **Traffic Warning**: Due to metro construction barriers, travel times between the airport and Canggu remain unstable. With rain expected this afternoon, roads may become slippery, so passengers are advised to allow at least **2.5 hours** to reach the airport.
