For many, learning to surf is one of their dreams, and it must be said that Bali is possibly one of the best places in the world to learn surfing.

## The Allure of Surfing in Bali

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ff49c7ae6f272d0f15bd093cf980b480.jpg)

Bali has been one of the world's premier surfing destinations for decades. Not only does it offer waves suitable for all skill levels, but the cost of learning to surf is quite reasonable, and the island is home to some of the most experienced surfing instructors in the world.

These instructors take pride in their teaching, and they feel deeply frustrated if they can't get a beginner to stand up on their first surfing attempt.

If you've ever wondered if surfing is for you, or if you've tried it before and want to give it another go, your next Bali vacation is the perfect time and place to try.

The best time to surf in Bali depends on your experience level. The dry season (April to October) typically offers more consistent waves, while the wet season (November to March) can produce larger waves, more suitable for experienced surfers.

### Beginners: Kuta Beach and Surroundings

For those trying surfing for the first time, Kuta Beach is the undisputed champion. This long stretch of golden sand offers consistent and gentle waves, perfect for practicing standing and balance. Numerous surf schools and board rental stands make Kuta an excellent spot for your first surfing experience.

Nearby beaches like Legian and Seminyak also offer similar beginner-friendly conditions.

### Intermediate Waves: Canggu

As your confidence grows, spots like Batu Bolong Beach in Canggu become more appealing. This iconic beach offers waves suitable for intermediate surfers. The lively atmosphere and beachfront cafes make the Old Man's area of Batu Bolong Beach a fun place to spend time, both in and out of the water.

> Canggu Guide: [What else can you do in Canggu?](/blog/canggu-guide/)

### Advanced Surfers: Uluwatu and Surroundings

For experienced riders, Bali truly becomes a surfer's paradise. Uluwatu is Bali's crown jewel, a world-renowned reef break with barrel waves over a shallow reef bottom. Watching skilled surfers carve down this long, powerful wave is a spectacular sight, but attempting it yourself requires advanced skills and respect for the ocean's power.

Other premium surf spots include Padang Padang, a beach break hidden within rocky caves, and Keramas, renowned for its long, peeling waves. These locations can get crowded with experienced surfers, so it's advisable to join a surf tour early in the morning.

## Organized by Location

Bali's surf spots offer a variety of options tailored to surfers of different skill levels. Let's explore these locations categorized by area to find the perfect spot for your surfing proficiency.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b14a8693e646e832647aded7011bfdde.jpg)

### Kuta Beach

Kuta Beach's vibrant atmosphere and beginner-friendly waves make it a top choice for surfers. This long stretch of sandy beach offers waves suitable for various skill levels, with gentle, rolling waves ideal for learning near the shore. Despite the crowds during peak season, Kuta remains Bali's quintessential surf destination, boasting stunning sunsets and a lively beach culture.

The Kuta area extends northward to Legian and Seminyak, offering the most consistent waves.

### Echo Beach

Echo Beach, located in the northern part of Canggu, is a surf haven on Bali's west coast, known for its black sand and reliable waves. Surfers flock here for the variety of breaks catering to all levels.

Intermediate surfers enjoy faster, more challenging waves, while beginners can have a pleasant experience in the gentler waves closer to shore. After a surf session, surfers can relax at beachside cafes, soaking in the laid-back atmosphere that makes Echo Beach a favorite among surfers and beach lovers alike.

### Batu Bolong Beach

Batu Bolong Beach is said to be named after a rock with a sea-eroded cave that once extended into the ocean. It is a favorite among beginners and intermediate surfers. Its rolling waves break over a sandy beach, providing an ideal environment for novices to learn.

![Jalan Batu Bolong路上的拍照景點](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_05c55e8b3f7706f9f0ef046ddda32bf5.png)

### **Kedungu Beach**

Located on Bali's west coast near Tanah Lot, Kedungu Beach offers a lesser-known but equally enchanting surfing experience. This secluded coastline is cherished by surfers seeking a more tranquil and uncrowded environment to ride the waves. The serene atmosphere and stunning natural beauty of Kedungu Beach create the perfect backdrop for a surfing adventure.

### **Medewi Beach**

Located on the west coast of Bali, Medewi Beach offers a serene and pristine surfing experience away from the island's hustle and bustle. Renowned for its long, rolling waves and laid-back atmosphere, Medewi Beach is a haven for longboarders and leisurely surfers seeking endless rides and unspoiled conditions.

### **Keramas Beach**

Situated on Bali's east coast, Keramas Beach is a cherished hidden gem among surfers, famous for its powerful waves and uncrowded lineup. Known for its fast, barreling waves, Keramas Beach provides an exhilarating surfing experience for seasoned surfers.

### Surfboard Rentals

If you already have surfing experience and just want to rent a surfboard, that's perfectly fine! In popular surfing areas like Kuta, Legian, Seminyak, and Canggu, surfboard rentals cost around $2 per hour or $5 for a full day. Of course, the more advanced the board, the higher the price.

## Beyond the Waves: The Essence of a Bali Surfing Journey

The beauty of a Bali surfing trip extends far beyond the waves. After an exhilarating surf session, unwind at charming beachfront cafes, savor delicious Balinese cuisine, and immerse yourself in the rich culture.

Explore ancient temples, enjoy captivating Kecak dance performances, or simply relax on pristine beaches.

Bali's surfing community is friendly and vibrant. Sign up for a surf camp, connect with other surfers, learn from local experts, and discover hidden breaks. With its stunning scenery, welcoming people, and world-class waves, Bali offers a truly unforgettable surfing experience.
