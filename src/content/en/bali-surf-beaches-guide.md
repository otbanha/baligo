For many, learning to surf is a dream, and it must be said that Bali is possibly one of the best places in the world to learn surfing.

## The Allure of Surfing in Bali

![The Allure of Surfing in Bali](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ff49c7ae6f272d0f15bd093cf980b480.jpg)

Bali has been one of the world's premier surfing destinations for decades. Not only does it offer waves suitable for all skill levels, but the cost of learning to surf is quite reasonable, and the island is home to some of the most experienced surfing instructors in the world.

These instructors take pride in their teaching, and they feel deeply frustrated if they can't get a beginner to stand up on their first surf.

If you've ever wondered if surfing is for you, or if you've tried it before and want to give it another go, your next Bali vacation is the perfect time and place to try.

The best time to surf in Bali depends on your experience level. The dry season (April to October) typically offers smoother waves, while the wet season (November to March) can produce larger waves, more suitable for experienced surfers.

### Beginner: Kuta Beach and Surroundings

For first-time surfers, Kuta Beach is the undisputed champion. This long stretch of golden sand offers consistent and gentle waves, perfect for practicing standing and balance. Numerous surf schools and board rental stalls make Kuta an excellent spot for your first surfing experience.

Nearby beaches like Legian and Seminyak also offer similar beginner-friendly conditions.

### Intermediate Waves: Canggu

As your confidence grows, Batu Bolong Beach in Canggu becomes more appealing. This iconic beach offers waves suitable for intermediate surfers. The lively atmosphere and beachfront cafes make Old Man's at Batu Bolong Beach a fun place to spend time, both in and out of the water.

> Canggu Guide: [What else can you do in Canggu?](/en/blog/canggu-guide/)

### Expert Surfers: Uluwatu and Surroundings

For experienced riders, Bali truly becomes a surfer's paradise. Uluwatu is Bali's crown jewel, a world-renowned reef break with barrel waves along the shallow reef bottom. Watching skilled surfers carve through this long, powerful wave is a spectacular sight, but attempting it yourself requires advanced skills and respect for the ocean's power.

Other premium surf spots include Padang Padang, a beach break hidden within rocky caves, and Keramas, renowned for its long, peeling waves. These locations can get crowded with experienced surfers, so it's recommended to join a surf tour early in the morning.

## By Location

Bali's surf spots offer a variety of options catering to surfers of different skill levels. Let's explore these locations divided by area to find the perfect spot for your surfing proficiency.

![By Location](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b14a8693e646e832647aded7011bfdde.jpg)

### Kuta Beach

Kuta Beach's lively atmosphere and beginner-friendly waves make it a top choice for surfers. This long stretch of sandy beach offers waves suitable for various skill levels, with gentle, rolling waves ideal for learning near the shore. Despite being crowded during peak season, Kuta remains Bali's quintessential surf destination, boasting vibrant sunsets and a bustling beach culture.

The Kuta area extends northward to Legian and Seminyak, offering the most consistent waves.

### Echo Beach

Echo Beach, located in the northern part of Canggu, is a surf paradise on Bali's west coast, known for its black sand and reliable waves. Surfers flock here for the variety of breaks suitable for all levels.

Intermediate surfers enjoy the faster, more challenging waves, while beginners can have a pleasant experience with the gentler waves closer to shore. After a surf session, relax at one of the beachfront cafes and soak in the laid-back atmosphere, making Echo Beach a favorite among surfers and beach lovers alike.

### Batu Bolong Beach

Batu Bolong Beach is said to be named after a rock sea cave that once extended into the ocean, making it a favorite spot for beginner and intermediate surfers. Its rolling waves break over a sandy beach, providing an ideal environment for newcomers to learn.

![Jalan Batu Bolong路上的拍照景點](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_05c55e8b3f7706f9f0ef046ddda32bf5.png)

### **Kedungu Beach**

Located on Bali's west coast near Tanah Lot, Kedungu Beach offers a lesser-known yet equally captivating surfing experience. This secluded coastline is cherished by surfers seeking a more tranquil and uncrowded environment to ride the waves. The serene atmosphere and stunning natural beauty of Kedungu Beach create the perfect backdrop for a surfing adventure.

### **Medewi Beach**

Situated on Bali's west coast, Medewi Beach provides a peaceful and unspoiled surfing experience away from the island's hustle and bustle. Known for its long, rolling waves and relaxed vibe, Medewi Beach is a haven for longboarders and laid-back surfers looking for endless rides and pristine conditions.

### **Keramas Beach**

Located on Bali's east coast, Keramas Beach is a hidden gem cherished by surfers, renowned for its powerful waves and uncrowded lineup. Known for its fast, barrel-shaped waves, Keramas offers experienced surfers an exhilarating surfing experience.

If you're already experienced in surfing and just want to rent a surfboard, that's possible too! In popular surfing areas like Kuta, Legian, Seminyak, and Canggu, surfboard rentals cost around USD$2 per hour or USD$5 per day. Of course, the more advanced the board, the higher the price.

## Beyond the Waves: The Essence of a Bali Surfing Journey

The beauty of a Bali surfing trip extends far beyond the waves. After an exhilarating surf session, unwind at charming beachfront cafes, savor delicious Balinese cuisine, and immerse yourself in the rich culture.

Explore ancient temples, enjoy captivating Kecak dance performances, or simply relax on pristine beaches.

Bali's surfing community is friendly and vibrant. Sign up for a surf camp, connect with other surfers, learn from local experts, and discover hidden breaks. With its stunning scenery, welcoming people, and world-class waves, Bali offers a truly unforgettable surfing experience.
