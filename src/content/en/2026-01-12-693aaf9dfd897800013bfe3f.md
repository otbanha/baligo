Bali is not only home to stunning beaches and rich culture but also a paradise for food lovers! This time, we'll take you deep into Bali's most representative 10 must-try dishes, from traditional main courses to street snacks, each bursting with Indonesia's unique spice flavors.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b027619db0a88baf3fea8f8c63193a9d.jpg)

## 🍖 Classic Main Courses

### 1. Babi Guling (Roast Suckling Pig)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4bda84a271f14b852043e10008699dc3.jpg)

A signature dish you absolutely can't miss in Bali! A whole suckling pig is stuffed with spices and slow-roasted until the skin is golden and crispy, while the meat remains tender and juicy. Typically served with white rice, crispy skin, grilled sausages, and sweet potatoes, every bite is a perfect blend of spices.

### 2. Nasi Campur Bali (Balinese Mixed Rice)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_af1ea75fd01be79bb6fc4b742a352357.jpg)

This is Bali's version of mixed rice, a plate that combines the essence of various sauces, grilled meats, and Balinese mixed rice. It usually includes rice, fried chicken or grilled meat, vegetables, peanuts, shrimp crackers, and more, allowing you to savor multiple flavors in one meal!

### 3. Betutu (Spiced Roast Chicken/Duck)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3be3ad88c261f5abe0846db474c3d02c.png)

A traditional Balinese slow-roasted dish, where a whole chicken or duck is marinated with spices, wrapped in banana leaves, and slow-roasted for hours. The rich spices fully penetrate the meat, leaving an unforgettable taste.

### 4. Jimbaran Seafood

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_936c1dc67021ff7ed3d35d7002572a20.jpg)

Jimbaran Beach is famous for its seafood barbecues, where fresh seafood paired with a stunning sunset creates an unforgettable dining experience. Freshly caught fish, shrimp, lobster, and more are grilled to perfection and served with special sauces, making it a must-try romantic dining experience in Bali.

## 🍢 Street Food Picks

### 5. Sate Lilit

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b96032c8d4ab3b3ca235a5f349e93f34.jpg)

A unique Balinese-style skewer, Sate Lilit is made by wrapping seasoned minced meat around lemongrass or bamboo sticks before grilling. The aromatic flavors make it impossible to stop at just one!

### 6. Pisang Goreng

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_da510f477a3b99474e4e823a2e97cbf8.jpg)

Pisang Goreng, or fried bananas, is one of Bali's most popular street snacks. Bananas coated in batter are fried until golden and crispy, offering a simple yet addictive treat that's crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

### 7. Klepon

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_069db0e5cc1da39c0aba61f76f04ddec.jpg)

This adorable green glutinous rice ball is a traditional Balinese dessert. Filled with palm sugar and coated with grated coconut, each bite releases a burst of sweet syrup, making it a delightful treat that's not overly sweet.

### 8. Jajanan Pasar (Traditional Market Snacks)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_27d9ed090f2e0d6d77985588b30b22e2.png)

Bali's traditional markets are filled with a variety of colorful pastries and snacks. Traditional sweets like rainbow cakes and coconut-flavored cakes are bursting with local flavors, making them the perfect way to experience the island's culture.

### 9. Lawar (Mixed Meat and Vegetable Salad)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b63c90ffa74ac14509bd2a00bb1cedb3.jpg)

Despite its name suggesting a cold dish, Lawar is actually a traditional mix of vegetables, coconut meat, spices, and meat. Its refreshing and appetizing flavor, combined with rich spices, makes it an ideal starter.

## ☕ Must-Try Drinks

### 10. Kopi Bali (Bali Coffee)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f3c741d3dfe2aeb90d97b02560dfe140.jpg)

Bali's coffee beans grow in volcanic soil, giving them a unique and robust flavor. When in Bali, don’t miss the chance to savor freshly roasted local coffee while enjoying the stunning rice terrace views—a perfect way to end your culinary journey! **A notable example**: **Kintamani Coffee** is a renowned product from this region.

## 💡 Food Tips

**Best Dining Times**: Local restaurants are busiest between 11:30 AM - 2:00 PM and 6:00 PM - 9:00 PM
**Price Range**: Street food costs around $1 - $3 USD; main dishes at restaurants range from $3 - $10 USD
**Spice Alert**: Indonesian cuisine is often spicy. If you’re sensitive to spice, remember to say "Tidak pedas" (not spicy)
