If you must bring them, keep them in their original packaging with clear labels and carry a doctor's prescription at all times; Taiwan customs also limits outbound health supplements to a maximum of 12 bottles of the same type and no more than 36 bottles in total.

When preparing for a trip to Indonesia or Bali, some travelers need to bring Chinese herbal powder and health supplements that are essential for their well-being. However, different countries have varying regulations on medications and supplements, and Indonesia is particularly strict compared to others. Understanding and following these rules is crucial to avoid unnecessary trouble and delays.

### Indonesia's Legal Regulations on Chinese Herbal Medicine

Indonesia has strict controls on Chinese herbal materials and preparations, especially for unidentified powder-like items brought into the country. According to Indonesia's Food and Drug Authority regulations, if you bring Chinese herbal powder without proper supporting documents, it may be considered illegal, leading to unnecessary complications.

### Taiwan's Restrictions on Bringing Health Supplements

Taiwan customs also imposes strict limits on taking health supplements out of the country. Travelers may carry a maximum of 12 bottles of the same type of supplement, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles, and all items must remain in their original packaging. Exceeding this limit may require an import/export permit from the Ministry of Health and Welfare.

### Documents to Note When Bringing Chinese Herbal Powder and Supplements into Indonesia

Each traveler should ensure that any powdered Chinese medicine or health supplements they carry are clearly labeled, and bring along a doctor's diagnosis or relevant documentation. These documents can help you explain and clear customs more smoothly if you are inspected.

### Case Study: Traveler's Chinese Medicine Seized Upon Entry

In 2019, a traveler had their unlabeled Chinese medicine powder seized upon entering Indonesia, and was forced to have it returned and was detained. This happened because they couldn't provide any supporting documents, violating Indonesian regulations.

### Risks and Consequences: Legal Liability for Violating Medicine Regulations

It is not recommended to bring Chinese medicine powder or health supplements into Bali. While it may sometimes pass through customs smoothly, if you encounter a strict inspection and cannot clearly explain or provide relevant documentation, it could lead to serious trouble. Indonesia has strict regulations on the entry of medicines and supplements, especially non-Western ones like Chinese medicine, as customs officials may not immediately recognize their ingredients or legality. If customs deems the items questionable, travelers may face fines, confiscation, or even more severe legal consequences. Therefore, unless absolutely necessary, it's best to avoid bringing such products, especially if you cannot provide a clear English prescription or official documentation.
