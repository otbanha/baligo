### Indonesia's Legal Regulations on Chinese Herbal Medicine

Indonesia has strict controls on Chinese herbal materials and preparations, especially for unidentified powder-type items brought into the country. According to Indonesia's Food and Drug Authority regulations, if you bring Chinese herbal powder without proper supporting documents, it may be considered illegal, leading to unnecessary trouble.

### Taiwan's Restrictions on Carrying Health Supplements

Taiwan customs also imposes strict limits on carrying health supplements out of the country. Travelers can bring a maximum of 12 bottles of the same type of health supplement, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles, and all items must remain in their original packaging. Exceeding this limit may require an import/export permit from the Ministry of Health and Welfare.

### Documents to Note When Bringing Chinese Herbal Powder and Health Supplements into Indonesia

Each traveler should ensure that any powdered herbal medicine or health supplements they carry are clearly labeled, and bring along a doctor's diagnosis or relevant documentation. These documents can help you explain and clear customs more smoothly if you are inspected.

### Case Study: Passenger's Herbal Medicine Seized Upon Entry

In 2019, a passenger had their unlabeled herbal powder confiscated upon entering Indonesia, and was forced to have it returned and was detained. This happened because they could not provide any supporting documents, violating Indonesia's relevant regulations.

### Risks and Consequences: Legal Liability for Carrying Medications Improperly

It is not recommended to bring powdered herbal medicine or herbal health supplements into Bali. While it may sometimes pass through customs smoothly, if you encounter a strict inspection and cannot clearly explain or provide relevant documentation, it could lead to serious trouble. Indonesia has strict regulations on the entry of medications and health supplements, especially for non-Western medicines like traditional Chinese medicine, as customs officials may not immediately recognize their ingredients or legality. If customs deems the items suspicious, travelers may face fines, confiscation, or even more severe legal consequences. Therefore, unless absolutely necessary, it's best to avoid bringing such products, especially if you cannot provide a clear English prescription or official documentation.
