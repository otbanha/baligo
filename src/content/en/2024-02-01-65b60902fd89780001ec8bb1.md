### 20. From Landing to Exiting the Airport: Procedures

Upon arriving in Bali, you'll need to follow these steps for immigration:

1. Follow instructions to disembark and enter the airport.
2. Follow 'Arrival' signs to the immigration hall.
3. Apply for Visa on Arrival (VoA): If you haven't applied for an e-Visa in advance, queue at the 'Visa on Arrival' counter to pay the fee. Those with e-Visa/eVoA can skip this step.
4. Immigration check (passport stamping) queue: VoA holders need to queue for inspection.
5. E-Gate: Those who have applied/paid for eVisa in advance can use the 'Automatic Gate' without queuing for manual stamping.
6. Collect luggage: After passing immigration/automatic gates, proceed to the baggage carousel according to your flight number.
7. Prepare health declaration and customs form (integrated into All Indonesia)
8. Pay Bali tourism tax

### 23. How to Convert Local Currency:

The currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Currently, 1 USD is roughly equivalent to 16,000 IDR. Compared to the New Taiwan Dollar (TWD), the exchange rate is approximately 1:500, meaning 1 TWD equals 500 IDR. For example, 1,000 TWD × 500 = 500,000 IDR.

A quick conversion method is to remove the last three zeros from the IDR amount and then multiply by 2, which will give you an approximate amount in TWD.

### 27. Watch Out for Additional Charges:

When dining in Indonesia, pay attention to the additional charges on your bill. The dining tax is usually added separately, typically at 11%, which goes to the government. Additionally, many restaurants add a service charge of 4% to 10%. For example, if a menu item costs 100,000 IDR, your final bill will be 100,000 IDR + 11% tax + the applicable service charge. Be mindful of these extra fees when ordering to avoid overspending.
