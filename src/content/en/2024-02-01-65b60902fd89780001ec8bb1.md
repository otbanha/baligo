### 20. From Landing to Exiting the Airport:

After arriving in Bali, you need to follow these steps for immigration procedures:

1. Follow instructions to disembark and enter the airport.
2. Follow the 'Arrival' signs to the immigration hall.
3. Apply for Visa on Arrival (VoA): If you haven't applied for an e-Visa in advance, queue at the 'Visa on Arrival' counter to pay the fee. Those with an eVisa/eVoA can skip this step.
4. Immigration check (passport stamping) queue counter: Those with VoA need to queue for inspection.
5. E-Gate: Those who have applied/paid for eVisa in advance can use the 'Automatic Gate' without queuing for manual stamping.
6. Collect luggage: After passing immigration/automatic gates, proceed to the carousel according to your flight number to collect your luggage.
7. Prepare health declaration and customs form (integrated into All Indonesia)
8. Pay Bali tourism tax

### 23. How to Convert Local Currency:

The currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah. Currently, one US dollar can be exchanged for approximately 16,000 Indonesian Rupiah. Compared to the New Taiwan Dollar, the exchange rate is roughly 1:500, meaning one New Taiwan Dollar can be exchanged for 500 Indonesian Rupiah; thus, 1000 New Taiwan Dollars ✖️ 500 equals 500,000 Indonesian Rupiah.

A quick conversion method is to remove the last three zeros from the Indonesian Rupiah amount and then ✖️ 2, which will give you an approximate amount in New Taiwan Dollars.

### 27. Pay Attention to Costs:

When dining in Indonesia, be mindful of additional charges. The dining tax in Indonesia is added separately, usually at 11%, which goes to the government. Additionally, many restaurants add a service charge of 4% to 10%. Therefore, if a menu item costs 100,000 IDR, the total amount you’ll pay at checkout will be 100,000 IDR + 11% tax + the corresponding service charge. Be sure to pay attention to these additional fees when ordering to avoid overspending.
