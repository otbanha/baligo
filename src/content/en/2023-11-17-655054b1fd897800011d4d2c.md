Located in central Bali, Ubud is the heart of the island's tourism and culture. With a history dating back to the 8th century, Ubud has grown into one of Bali's most renowned tourist destinations.

The scenery here is distinctly different from other parts of the island, known for its unique religious, artistic, and natural beauty. The cultural atmosphere of this mountain town is intoxicating, making Ubud a perfect spot for Instagram-worthy photos. Ancient temples, spectacular rice terraces, and ubiquitous Balinese architecture all showcase captivating visual charm. Every corner of Ubud feels like a unique piece of art, allowing you to experience the city's profound soul and rich history.

### Ubud Water Palace (👉🏼 Map: [Central Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

The Ubud Water Palace (Pura Taman Saraswati) is just a three-minute walk from the Ubud Royal Palace. Hidden behind a Starbucks, this often-overlooked gem offers breathtaking views. Compared to the Ubud Royal Palace, this temple is more serene and mysterious, providing an excellent photography spot to capture unique and exquisite moments.

The Ubud Water Palace is renowned for its distinctive water features, located at the end of a lotus pond walkway. Its architecture is imbued with Balinese artistic flair, with mysterious stone carvings and structures that showcase the depth of local culture. Walking here feels like traveling through time, immersed in ancient myths and religious beliefs.

### Monkey Forest (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Ubud's Monkey Forest is a treasure trove where nature and culture intertwine. As you step into this lush green forest, monkeys play around you, making you feel like a part of nature itself. You can interact with these clever little creatures and experience the wonder of coexisting with nature.

However, because the monkeys here are quite mischievous, visitors should remain alert when entering the Monkey Forest. These curious little creatures may climb all over you and even steal or snatch items like glasses or personal belongings. Therefore, visitors should interact with the monkeys cautiously, avoiding carrying food or conspicuous items to prevent attracting their attention.

### World Heritage Rice Terrace Scenery (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b32736a5ef98517bd7b4ebd87af9506f.jpg)

The rice terrace scenery around Ubud is a major highlight of Bali, presenting a magnificent landscape like a painting scroll, and has been honored with the title of World Heritage by UNESCO. This rice terrace area not only showcases the wonders of nature but also highlights the beautiful scene of harmony between nature and humans, making it an unmissable tourist destination in Bali.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace is one of the most accessible and popular rice terrace areas in Ubud. The layout of this rice terrace area is unique, with winding paths between the terraces weaving a green canvas that vividly displays the vitality of nature. On this land, the rolling rice waves form a moving agricultural poem, blending natural beauty with farming culture.

Another less commercialized rice terrace area is Jatiluwih Rice Terrace, but it's also farther from Ubud, requiring a 90-minute drive from Ubud Palace as the starting point. However, it offers a more authentic experience of the beauty of rice terraces!

The best time to capture the beauty of the rice terraces is at dawn when the sun just rises. At this moment, the golden sunlight spills over the terraces, outlining layers of shadows and presenting an intoxicating play of light and shadow. This is the favorite moment for photography enthusiasts and tourists, as the rice terraces seem immersed in a golden dream.

### Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan Sacred Tree Scenic Area (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2c3bc45f0626ebf69733b0745a47a021.jpg)

The Sacred Tree Area (Wisata Kayu Putih Bayan) is located in Buleleng Regency in northern Bali, renowned for its spectacular thousand-year-old sacred tree. This massive Kayu Putih tree (meaning 'white wood' in Indonesian) is estimated to be over a millennium old, with a trunk so thick it takes dozens of people to encircle it, and its towering canopy stretches high into the sky, covering a vast area. Local residents regard this ancient tree as a sacred entity, believing it possesses spiritual powers, and it is common to see devotees coming to pray and perform traditional rituals.

This sacred tree is not only a natural wonder but also a symbol of the harmonious coexistence of Balinese culture and nature. Beneath the tree, there is a small shrine adorned with traditional black-and-white checkered cloth (poleng), highlighting its religious significance. Visitors here can experience a sacred and serene atmosphere while admiring this ancient life form that has witnessed centuries of Bali's historical changes. The surrounding natural environment is tranquil, making it a unique spot to experience Bali's spiritual culture and natural beauty.

## 【Around Pura Ulun Danu Bratan】

### Bali's Ulun Danu Beratan Temple (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcbb190a64dc4d8d05b92348d7fda9c.jpg)

Ulun Danu Beratan Temple (Pura Ulun Danau), completed in 1633, is located on the shores of Lake Bratan in central Bali. Some also refer to it as the 'Bratan Water Temple.' Since Lake Bratan is the primary water source for irrigation in central Bali, Ulun Danu Beratan Temple was once used as a ceremonial site to worship Dewi Danu, the goddess of water, lakes, and rivers in Bali. Additionally, this 11-tiered temple was built in honor of Shiva and Parvati, and a statue of Buddha is also enshrined here, making it a temple that blends Hinduism and Buddhism.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf3c1b5963a5c33d38b37b9e3375ccf4.jpg)

Interestingly, this Balinese water temple is also featured on Indonesian currency, specifically on the second-largest denomination in circulation. 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah is approximately USD$3.

As a volcanic island, Bali is home to several hot springs, with Toya Devasya being one of the most famous. This hot spring water park is located on the shores of Lake Batur and is one of the island's most popular attractions. It is known for its natural hot springs, water park, and stunning lake views.

The water park spans 2.5 hectares and features seven hot spring pools with varying temperatures. ### Pura Besakih (Mother Temple) （👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

Pura Besakih, known as the Mother Temple, is one of Bali's largest and most sacred Hindu temples, located on the slopes of Mount Agung. It is considered the spiritual heart of Bali and features multiple shrines and altars dedicated to various deities.

This magnificent temple complex is nestled between beautiful valleys and lush rice fields, offering breathtaking views. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient religious atmosphere and explore the intricate temple architecture and sacred ambiance. Pura Besakih hosts many of Bali's religious and cultural events, such as festivals and ceremonies.

In 1963, Mount Agung erupted, killing 1,700 people, yet the lava flowed just meters away from the temple, sparing Pura Besakih. This event is seen as a miracle by the Balinese, believed to be a divine demonstration of power without destroying faith. The temple holds profound religious significance and cultural value, attracting visitors from around the world. Visitors are required to dress appropriately and respect local religious customs to honor this sacred site.
