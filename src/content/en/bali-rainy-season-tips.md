**Hello everyone, I'm a traveler who absolutely adores Bali, having visited during both the dry and rainy seasons multiple times. Many people hesitate when they hear 'Bali rainy season,' fearing constant rain and ruined plans. But that's not the case! Bali during the rainy season offers a unique charm of lush greenery, tranquility, and great value. Today, I'll share the latest comprehensive guide to Bali's rainy season, ensuring you have a fantastic time even during the wet season!**


## When is Bali's Rainy Season?

Bali's rainy season generally runs from **November to March** (sometimes starting with sporadic rains in October and ending completely by April). Specifically:

- Wettest months: December, January, February (especially January, with average rainfall exceeding 300mm)

- In 2025, the rainy season arrived early in September, but it's still concentrated after November.

- Rain characteristics: Mostly sudden tropical showers in the afternoon or evening, lasting 1-2 hours, with mornings and midday usually sunny!

Temperatures remain hot (27-31°C), and while rain cools things down, humidity increases, sometimes making it feel even more humid.

## Advantages of Visiting Bali During the Rainy Season (There Are So Many!)

1. **Fewer Crowds**: No queues at popular attractions, perfect for photos, and it feels like the whole island is yours!
2. **Budget-Friendly Deals**: Hotels, villas, and private pool villas often offer 30-50% discounts, and flights are 20-40% cheaper. Want to stay in a luxurious ocean-view villa without breaking the bank? The rainy season is the time!
3. **Stunning Scenery**: Rice fields, waterfalls, and forests turn incredibly lush, and the blue skies, white clouds, and rainbows after the rain look like a full-on filter!
4. **Surfer’s Paradise**: Big waves on the southwest coast are perfect for advanced surfers.
5. **Fresh Air**: The rain washes away the dust, making the air exceptionally clean.

## The Downsides of the Rainy Season (Honestly)

- Slippery roads—be careful when riding a scooter (consider hiring a car or using Gojek/Grab).

- Occasional localized flooding (especially in Kuta and low-lying areas).

- Sometimes the sea is murky, not ideal for snorkeling or capturing ultra-blue waters.

- Hiking (e.g., Mount Batur sunrise) is riskier, with higher chances of not seeing the sun or even cancellations.

## Must-Pack List for the Rainy Season

- Lightweight raincoat or disposable poncho (more convenient than an umbrella)

- Waterproof phone case, waterproof backpack cover

- Quick-dry clothes and sandals (don't wear leather shoes, they'll get soaked)

- Non-slip sandals or waterproof shoes

- Mosquito repellent (lots of mosquitoes during rainy season)

- Light long-sleeve shirts (for temples or cooler evenings)

## Rainy Season Transportation Tips

- Be extra careful when riding scooters! Slippery roads + puddles make it easy to crash. Beginners should consider hiring a car or using Grab/GoJek.

- Southern areas (Kuta, Seminyak, Jimbaran, Uluwatu) usually get less rain, while Ubud and mountainous regions see more rainfall.

- Don't force yourself to drive/ride in heavy rain - just find a cozy cafe to wait it out.

## Perfect Rainy Season Activities (Rain won't stop the fun!!)

1. SPA Relaxation: Rain sounds paired with aromatherapy massage — pure heaven! Prices are much cheaper than in the dry season.
2. Café Hopping: Ubud and Canggu are filled with Instagram-worthy cafés. Enjoy coffee and books while it rains outside.
3. Cooking Classes/Silver Jewelry Making/Hands-on Workshops: Indoor activities that capture the essence of Bali.
4. Shopping Spree: Explore Ubud Market, Kuta's shopping malls, and Seminyak boutiques — perfect rainy-day activities.
5. Yoga/Meditation/Healing Retreats: The serene atmosphere of the rainy season is ideal for recharging your soul.
6. Waterfall Chasing: Waterfalls like Sekumpul and Tegenungan are at their most spectacular during the rainy season.
7. Cultural Tours: Visit temples and watch traditional dance performances (many are held indoors).

## My Honest Advice

If it’s your first time in Bali, you’re worried about rain, or you’re eager to snap beach photos, I’d recommend visiting during the dry season (April to October).

But if you’ve been before, are on a budget, and prefer a quieter, more natural Bali, the rainy season will make you fall in love with a different side of the island! Staying in a villa, listening to the rain, and gazing at rice fields is incredibly soothing.

The rainy season in Bali isn’t a "second choice" but a "different flavor of Bali." Pack your raincoat, bring a flexible mindset, and set off! I guarantee you’ll fall in love with this magical island under the post-rain rainbow, just like I did. 🌧️🌈🏝️
