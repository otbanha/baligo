Ubud is famous for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes, making it a huge draw for adult travelers. But for young children, visiting ancient temples or admiring scenery can indeed get a bit dull.

This guide specially curates the best kid-friendly spots and activities in Ubud, ensuring children have a blast while parents also enjoy their vacation. From children's theme parks and DIY craft classes to let little ones burn off energy, to attractions like the Ubud Palace and Monkey Forest that offer unique family outings, let's first take a look at what Ubud has to offer for families:

## Family Activities

The best activities for families with kids in Ubud:

### Alas Harum (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![Alas Harum （地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable age: 1 year and up

Near Ubud's famous Tegalalang Rice Terraces, there's a fantastic family-friendly spot called the 'Alas Harum' complex. It's about a 20-minute drive from central Ubud and is best known for its stunning rice terrace views.

This place offers not only stunning terraced rice fields but also family-friendly outdoor activities like flying foxes, swings, and the latest sky biking. Kids can enjoy thrilling adventures here while parents relax in the peaceful surroundings, making for quality family time.

Alas Harum is more than just an adventure park—it's steeped in rich cultural heritage. The area around the park is dotted with temples, statues, and other historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal spot to explore Ubud, blending breathtaking scenery, exciting activities, and deep cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![烏布藝術市場 （地圖：烏布中心）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable age: 0 years and up

At Ubud Market, kids can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. It's filled with a dazzling array of handicrafts, from intricate carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural flair. Colorful fabrics are woven into diverse patterns and prints, each radiating the skill and rich history of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove. Children can observe, smell, and even experience Bali's distinctive art and craft techniques firsthand. They'll see the finesse of ceramic art, feel the detail of wood carving, and learn how locals use weaving and handicrafts to preserve and pass on Bali's cultural traditions.

At this market, kids can not only pick up unique souvenirs but also, through interactions with local artisans, appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art. Such experiences will spark their curiosity about the world and enrich their journey with deeper learning.

### Ubud Skywalk (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Ubud Attractions](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

### Bali's New Monkey Forest: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River is a natural paradise that immerses you in Bali's pristine beauty! The clear Petanu River winds through the forest, while man-made waterfalls add a refreshing, cool ambiance.

Here, you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying foxes, all while relaxing in traditional Balinese pavilions, listening to the flowing water and soaking in the tranquility of nature.

Now open for visits! Hours: Daily from 9 AM to 6 PM (last entry at 5 PM). Special rates: Adults 80,000 IDR, Children 40,000 IDR.

Escape the crowds and experience a unique Melukat water purification blessing ceremony, immersing yourself in Bali's spirituality and natural beauty!

A friendly tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, choose this spot—the monkeys here are gentle and won't attack. At Ubud's Monkey Forest, they tend to snatch things and are more aggressive.

## Bali Reptile Park｜Snakes Coiling Around Necks, Giant Lizards Appearing—A Reptile Wonderland for the Brave

**📍 Address:** Adjacent to Bali Bird Park, Singapadu, Gianyar

![](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1780206204799-_2026-05-31_12.42.57.png)

Right next to the Bali Bird Park, the Bali Reptile Park is another fascinating world that both frightens and captivates kids. It houses the most comprehensive collection of reptiles on the island, including various pythons, cobras, monitor lizards (close relatives of the Komodo dragon), colorful geckos, and tortoises. Just getting near the display tanks is enough to make the faint-hearted's pulse race.

The most popular experience here is having a staff member help you **drape a python over your shoulders and neck for a photo**—the cool sensation of the snake's body and the strength of its muscles transmitted through your skin create an indescribably unique feeling, and it's the challenge that makes kids both scream and laugh. After braving it, the look of proud accomplishment on their faces is priceless.

A guide accompanies you throughout, explaining each animal's habits and conservation status in detail, turning this 'courage adventure' into a vivid natural education lesson.

### Kid-Friendly River Tubing

https://youtube.com/shorts/Z_vDb7AbMKg

Suitable age: 4 years and up

While rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it carries certain risks, with safety affected by river conditions. For a safer water experience, cave tubing or river tubing (as shown in the video above) is a relatively safer and more family-friendly option.

Interestingly, many caves in the Ubud area date back to Japan's brief occupation of Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces built fortifications on the island, including bunkers, caves, and tunnels. These caves and tunnels were later repurposed or renovated by locals or government agencies—some turned into tourist attractions, others used for irrigation. They stand as witnesses to a bygone historical era and have become part of Bali's history and culture today.

Whether it's whitewater rafting or cave tubing, both of these water activities in Ubud offer adventurers an unforgettable experience. They let you feel the thrill of challenge in the river while enjoying the tranquility and vitality that nature provides. These activities allow you to interact with nature and history, adding rich experiences and lasting memories to your journey.

## Tips for Family Travel in Ubud

![烏布家庭親子旅遊的注意事項](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some tips for a family trip to Ubud:

- **Book a family-friendly hotel or guesthouse** Ubud offers many hotels and guesthouses, many of which are designed for families. These often provide amenities like babysitting services, kids' clubs, and pools.
- **Consider hiring a private driver** Hiring a private driver is a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings. It lets you travel at your own pace and stop for photos and sights along the way.
- **Protect against the sun** Bali's sun is strong, so bring sun protection like hats, sunglasses, and UV-protective clothing, and apply sunscreen frequently to prevent your kids from getting sunburned.
- **Be mindful of food and water hygiene** Children have weaker immune systems and are more prone to illness, so never let them drink water of uncertain quality, and avoid food from street vendors.
- **Watch for weather changes** Bali's weather can be unpredictable—sunny one moment and rainy the next. It's best to carry rain gear and pack light layers like a thin jacket for your kids.

These are some basic tips to keep in mind. Hope you and your kids have a fun and safe time in Ubud, Bali!
