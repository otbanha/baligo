Ubud is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and natural landscapes, making it highly attractive to adult travelers. However, for younger children, visiting historical sites or admiring scenery can be quite dull.

This guide specially curates the most family-friendly attractions and activities in Ubud, ensuring kids have fun while parents enjoy their vacation too. From children's theme parks to DIY craft workshops, there's plenty to keep kids entertained; while Ubud Palace and Monkey Forest offer unique family experiences. Let's explore what Ubud has to offer for families:

## Family Activities

Top family-friendly activities in Ubud:

### Alas Harum （👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 1+

Near Ubud's famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces lies a family-friendly destination called Alas Harum. Located about 20 minutes from Ubud center, it's best known for its rice terrace views.

Not only does it offer stunning terrace landscapes, but also kid-friendly outdoor activities like flying fox, swings, and the latest sky biking. Children can enjoy thrilling adventures while parents relax in the peaceful surroundings, creating wonderful family memories together.

Alas Harum is not just an adventure park; it also carries a rich cultural heritage. The surrounding area is dotted with temples, statues, and other historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal place to explore Ubud, combining stunning scenery, thrilling activities, and rich cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market (👉🏼 Map: [Central Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable age: 0 and above

In the Ubud Market, children can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. The market is filled with a variety of handicrafts, from exquisite carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural charm. Colorful fabrics are woven into various patterns and prints, each exuding the craftsmanship and rich historical heritage of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove, where children can observe, smell, and even personally experience Bali's unique art and craft techniques. They can see the intricacies of ceramic art, feel the delicacy of wood carving, and learn how local residents preserve and pass down Bali's cultural traditions through weaving and handicrafts.

In this market, children can not only purchase unique souvenirs but also appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art through interactions with local artisans. Such experiences will fill them with curiosity about the world and enrich their educational journey.

### Ubud Skywalk (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Consider carefully if you're bringing preschoolers; it can be tiring if they need to be carried!

Suitable age: 8 and above

Recommend a place called Campuhan Ridge Walk (also known as the Ubud Skywalk), which is easily accessible on foot from Ubud Palace. This hidden gem near the heart of Ubud offers tranquility and stunning natural scenery for you and your family. Located along a ridge, this trail provides breathtaking 360-degree views, allowing you to escape the hustle and bustle of the city.

https://youtube.com/shorts/3PmPpoHI_6c

This is a perfect destination for families, where both children and parents can immerse themselves in the beauty of nature. As you stroll along the trail, you’ll enjoy the serene atmosphere unique to the ridge. Here, there’s no traffic noise—only the sounds of nature and picturesque views await you.

### Bali's New Monkey Forest: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River immerses you in Bali's pristine natural beauty! The clear Petanu River flows through the forest, and artificial waterfalls add a refreshing ambiance.

Here you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying foxes while relaxing in traditional Balinese pavilions, listening to the soothing sounds of flowing water, and feeling the tranquility of nature.

Now open for visits! Hours: Daily 9 AM - 6 PM (last entry at 5 PM) Special price: Adults IDR 80,000, Children IDR 40,000

Escape the crowds and experience the unique melukat water purification ritual, embracing Bali's spirituality and natural beauty!

Friendly tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, choose this place—the monkeys here are gentler and won't attack. In Ubud, the monkeys are more aggressive and may snatch belongings.

### Kid-Friendly River Tubing

https://youtube.com/shorts/Z_vDb7AbMKg

Suitable for ages: 4 and above

While river rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it carries certain risks influenced by river conditions. For a safer water activity experience, cave tubing/river tubing (as shown in the video above) is a relatively safer option, ideal for family fun.

It's worth noting that many caves in the Ubud area date back to the brief Japanese occupation of Bali during World War II. During this period, Japanese forces constructed bunkers, caves, and tunnels as part of their defense infrastructure. These caves and tunnels have since been repurposed or modified by local residents or government agencies, some turned into tourist attractions, others used for irrigation. These caves bear witness to historical periods and have become part of Bali's history and culture.

Whether it's rafting or cave tubing, both water activities in Ubud offer unforgettable experiences for adventurers. They allow you to feel the thrill of challenge in the river while enjoying the tranquility and vitality bestowed by nature. These activities enable interaction with both nature and history, enriching your journey with profound experiences and memories.

### Exploring Three Major Waterfalls (👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

## Tips for Family Travel in Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some tips for family travel in Ubud:

- **Book family-friendly hotels or resorts** Ubud offers a wide range of accommodations, many of which are designed with families in mind. These hotels often provide childcare services, kids' clubs, and swimming pools.
- **Consider hiring a private car** Renting a car is a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings at your own pace, allowing you to stop for photos and sightseeing along the way.
- **Be mindful of sun protection** Bali's sun can be intense, so bring sun protection items like hats, sunglasses, and sunscreen, and apply sunscreen frequently to avoid sunburn.
- **Be cautious with food and water** Children have weaker immune systems, so avoid letting them drink water of uncertain quality or eat food from street vendors.
- **Be prepared for weather changes** Bali's weather can be unpredictable, so it's best to carry rain gear and some warm clothing like a light jacket for your children.

These are some basic tips to ensure you and your children have a safe and enjoyable time in Ubud, Bali!
