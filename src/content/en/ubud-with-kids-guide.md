## Family Activities

The best activities for families with kids in Ubud:

### Alas Harum (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Spots Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![Alas Harum （地圖：烏布周邊熱門景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 1+

Near Ubud’s famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces, there’s a fantastic family-friendly spot called the 'Alas Harum' complex. It’s about a 20-minute drive from central Ubud and is best known for its stunning rice terrace views.

This spot offers not only stunning rice terrace views but also family-friendly outdoor activities like flying foxes, swings, and the latest sky biking. Kids can enjoy thrilling adventures here while parents relax in the peaceful surroundings, making for quality family time together.

Alas Harum is more than just an adventure park—it's steeped in rich cultural heritage. The area around the park is dotted with temples, statues, and other historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal place to explore Ubud, combining breathtaking scenery, exciting activities, and a wealth of cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![烏布藝術市場 （地圖：烏布中心）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 0 and up

At Ubud Market, kids can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. The stalls are filled with a dazzling array of handicrafts, from intricate carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural flair. Colorful fabrics are woven into various patterns and prints, each radiating the exquisite skill and rich history of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove, where children can observe, smell, and even experience Bali's distinctive art and craft techniques firsthand. They can marvel at the finesse of ceramic art, feel the delicacy of wood carving, and learn how locals preserve and pass on Bali's cultural traditions through weaving and handicrafts.

At this market, kids can not only pick up unique souvenirs but also appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art through interactions with local artisans. Such an experience will spark their curiosity about the world and enrich their journey with educational insights.

### Bali's New Monkey Forest: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉🏼 Map: [Top Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River is a natural paradise that immerses you in Bali's pristine beauty! The crystal-clear Petanu River winds through the forest, while man-made waterfalls add a cool, refreshing vibe.

Here, you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying foxes, all while relaxing in a traditional Balinese pavilion, listening to the flowing water and soaking in nature's tranquility.

Now open! Hours: Daily 9 AM to 6 PM (last entry at 5 PM). Special rates: Adults 80,000 IDR, Children 40,000 IDR.

Escape the crowds and experience a unique Melukat purification blessing ritual, feeling Bali's spirituality and natural beauty!

A friendly tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, choose this spot—the monkeys here are gentler and won't attack. At Ubud's Monkey Forest, the monkeys are more aggressive and may snatch things.

After bravely completing it, you'll see that proud, triumphant grin on your child's face.

Guides accompany you throughout, explaining each animal's habits and conservation status in detail, turning this 'courageous adventure' into a vivid natural education lesson.

### Kid-Friendly River Tubing

https://youtube.com/shorts/Z_vDb7AbMKg

Ages: 4 and up

While white-water rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it does carry some risk, with safety depending on river conditions. For a safer water experience that's more suitable for families, cave tubing or river tubing (as shown in the video above) is a much safer and more family-friendly option.

It's worth noting that many caves in the Ubud area date back to Japan's brief occupation of Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces carried out construction projects on the island, including building bunkers, caves, and tunnels for defensive purposes. These caves and tunnels were later repurposed or renovated by local residents or government agencies. Some were turned into tourist attractions, while others were used for irrigation. These caves bear witness to a bygone historical era and have now become part of Bali's history and culture.

Whether it's white-water rafting or cave tubing, both of these water activities in Ubud offer unforgettable experiences for adventurers. ## Tips for Family Travel in Ubud

![烏布家庭親子旅遊的注意事項](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some tips for a family trip to Ubud:

- **Book a family-friendly hotel or resort** Ubud has many options, with many designed specifically for families. These often offer babysitting services, kids' clubs, and pools.
- **Consider hiring a private driver** This is a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings at your own pace, stopping for photos and sights along the way.
- **Protect against the sun** Bali's sun is strong. Bring sun hats, sunglasses, and UV-protective clothing, and apply sunscreen frequently to prevent sunburn.
- **Watch food and water hygiene** Children have weaker immune systems, so avoid giving them tap water of uncertain quality or food from street vendors.
- **Be prepared for weather changes** Bali's weather can be unpredictable—sunny one moment, rainy the next. Always carry rain gear and a light jacket for the kids.

These are some basic tips—hope you and your kids have a fun and safe time in Ubud, Bali!
