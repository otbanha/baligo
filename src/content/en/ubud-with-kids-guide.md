Ubud is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and natural landscapes, making it highly appealing to adult travelers. However, for young children, visiting historical sites or simply admiring the scenery can indeed become a bit dull.

This guide specially selects the best kid-friendly attractions and activities in Ubud, ensuring children have a blast while parents also enjoy the vacation. From children's theme parks and DIY craft classes that let kids burn off energy, to spots like the Ubud Palace and Monkey Forest that offer unique family experiences, here's a look at the top family activities in Ubud:

## Family Activities

The best activities for families with kids in Ubud:

### Alas Harum (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable age: 1 year and up

Near Ubud's famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces, there's a fantastic spot for families called 'Alas Harum.' It's about a 20-minute drive from central Ubud and is best known for its stunning rice terrace views.

Besides the iconic terraced scenery, it offers family-friendly outdoor activities like flying foxes, swings, and the latest sky biking. Kids can enjoy thrilling adventures while parents relax in the peaceful surroundings, making for quality family time together.

Alas Harum is more than just an adventure park—it's steeped in rich cultural heritage. The surrounding area is dotted with temples, statues, and other historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal spot to explore Ubud, combining stunning scenery, thrilling activities, and a wealth of cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 0 and up

At the Ubud Market, kids can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. It's filled with an array of handicrafts, from intricate carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural flair. Colorful fabrics are woven into a variety of patterns and prints, each radiating the skill and rich history of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove, where children can observe, smell, and even experience Bali's unique art and craft techniques firsthand. They can marvel at the finesse of ceramic art, feel the delicacy of wood carving, and learn how locals preserve and pass on Bali's cultural traditions through weaving and handicrafts.

In this market, kids can not only pick up unique souvenirs but also, through interactions with local artisans, appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art. Such an experience will spark their curiosity about the world and enrich their journey with deeper learning.

### Ubud Skywalk (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Spots Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

### New Monkey Forest in Bali: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉🏼 Map: [Top Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River is a natural paradise that immerses you in Bali's pristine beauty! The crystal-clear Petanu River winds through the forest, while man-made waterfalls add a refreshing, cool ambiance.

Here, you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying foxes, all while relaxing in traditional Balinese pavilions, listening to the flow of water and soaking in nature's tranquility.

Now open for visits! Hours: Daily 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (last entry at 5:00 PM). Special rates: Adults 80,000 IDR, Children 40,000 IDR.

Escape the crowds and experience a unique Melukat water blessing ritual, feeling the spirituality and natural beauty of Bali!

Warm tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, choose this spot—the monkeys here are gentle and won't attack. At Ubud's Monkey Forest, the monkeys are more aggressive and may snatch things.

Right next to Bali Bird Park, Bali Reptile Park is another fascinating world that both frightens and enthralls kids. The park houses Bali's most comprehensive collection of reptiles, including various pythons, cobras, monitor lizards (close relatives of Komodo dragons), colorful geckos, and tortoises. After bravely completing it, you're rewarded with that proud, triumphant look on your child's face.

A guide accompanies you throughout, explaining each animal's habits and conservation status in detail, turning this 'courageous journey' into a vivid natural education lesson.

### Kid-Friendly River Tubing

https://youtube.com/shorts/Z_vDb7AbMKg

Suitable age: 4 years and up

While rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it carries certain risks, with safety affected by river conditions. For a safer water experience, cave tubing (river tubing, as shown in the video above) is a relatively safer and more family-friendly option.

It's worth noting that many caves in the Ubud area date back to Japan's brief occupation of Bali. During World War II, Japanese troops carried out engineering projects on the island, including building bunkers, caves, and tunnels for defense. These caves and tunnels were later repurposed or renovated by local residents or government agencies. Some were turned into tourist attractions, while others were used for irrigation. These caves bear witness to a past historical period and have now become part of Bali's history and culture.

Whether it's rafting or cave tubing, both of these water activities in Ubud offer unforgettable experiences for adventurers. They let you feel the thrill of challenge in the river while enjoying the tranquility and vitality bestowed by nature. These activities allow you to interact with both nature and history, adding rich experiences and lasting memories to your journey.

## Tips for Family Travel in Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some tips for a family trip to Ubud:

- **Book a family-friendly hotel or guesthouse** Ubud offers a wide range of hotels and guesthouses, many of which are designed with families in mind. These often provide amenities such as babysitting services, kids' clubs, and swimming pools.
- **Consider hiring a private driver** Hiring a private driver is a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings. It allows you to travel at your own pace and stop for photos and sightseeing along the way.
- **Protect against the sun** The sun in Bali is intense, so bring sun protection like hats, sunglasses, and UV-protective clothing, and apply sunscreen frequently to prevent your children from getting sunburned.
- **Be mindful of food and water hygiene** Children have weaker immune systems and are more prone to illness, so avoid letting them drink water of uncertain quality and steer clear of food from street vendors.
- **Prepare for changing weather** Bali's weather can be unpredictable—sunny one moment and rainy the next. It's best to carry rain gear and pack light jackets or layers to keep your children warm.

These are some basic tips to keep in mind. We hope you and your children have a fun and safe time in Ubud, Bali!
