Ubud is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and natural landscapes, making it highly attractive to adult travelers. However, for young children, visiting historical sites or admiring scenery can indeed be somewhat dull.

This guide specially curates the most suitable attractions and activities in Ubud for family trips, ensuring children have fun while parents enjoy their vacation. For example, children's theme parks and DIY craft courses allow kids to release energy; while Ubud Palace and Monkey Forest offer unique family experiences. Let's explore what Ubud has to offer for families:

## Family Activities

Top family-friendly activities in Ubud:

### Alas Harum （👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable age: 1+

Near Ubud's famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces lies a family-friendly destination called Alas Harum. Located about 20 minutes from Ubud's city center, it's most famous for its rice terrace views.

Not only does it offer stunning terrace landscapes, but also family-friendly outdoor activities like flying fox, swings, and the latest sky biking. Kids can enjoy thrilling adventures while parents relax in the peaceful environment, creating wonderful family memories together.

Alas Harum is not just an adventure park; it also carries rich cultural heritage. The surrounding area is dotted with temples, statues, and other historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal place to explore Ubud, combining stunning scenery, thrilling activities, and enriching cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 0 and up

In the Ubud Market, children can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. The market is filled with a dazzling array of handicrafts, from exquisite carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural charm. Colorful fabrics are woven into various patterns and prints, each radiating the craftsmanship and rich historical heritage of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove, where children can observe, smell, and even personally experience Bali's unique art and craft techniques. They can marvel at the intricacies of ceramic art, feel the delicacy of wood carving, and learn how local residents preserve and pass down Bali's cultural traditions through weaving and handicrafts.

At this market, children can not only purchase unique souvenirs but also appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art through interactions with local artisans. Such experiences will fill them with curiosity about the world and enrich their educational journey.

### Ubud Skywalk (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Suitable for ages: 8 and up

Just a short walk from Ubud Palace lies a hidden gem known as the Campuhan Ridge Walk, often referred to as the 'Ubud Skywalk.' Easily accessible on foot from the palace, this trail offers a serene escape into nature, providing you and your family with breathtaking views and tranquility. Situated along a ridge, the path offers stunning 360-degree vistas, allowing you to immerse yourselves in the beauty of the landscape while leaving the city's hustle and bustle behind.

https://youtube.com/shorts/3PmPpoHI_6c

This is a perfect destination for families, where both children and parents can connect with nature. As you stroll along the trail, you’ll be surrounded by peaceful scenery, free from the noise of traffic, with only the sounds of nature accompanying you.

### Bali's New Monkey Forest: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River immerses you in Bali's pristine natural beauty! The crystal-clear Petanu River winds through the forest, while artificial waterfalls add a refreshing ambiance to the surroundings.

Here, you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying fox bats, all while relaxing in traditional Balinese gazebos, listening to the flowing water, and soaking in the tranquility of nature.

Now open for visits! Operating hours: Daily from 9 AM to 6 PM (last entry at 5 PM) Special price: Adults $5, Children $3

Escape the crowds and experience the unique melukat water purification ritual, embracing Bali's spirituality and natural beauty!

Friendly tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, this is a great spot. The monkeys here are more docile and won't attack. If you're visiting Ubud, the monkeys there are more aggressive and may snatch items.

### Kid-Friendly River Tubing

https://youtube.com/shorts/Z_vDb7AbMKg

Suitable for ages: 4 and up

While white-water rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it carries certain risks, as safety depends heavily on river conditions. For a safer water adventure, cave tubing (or river tubing, as shown in the video above) is a more family-friendly option.

Interestingly, many of the caves in the Ubud area date back to the brief period when Bali was under Japanese rule. During World War II, Japanese forces constructed bunkers, caves, and tunnels as part of their defensive infrastructure. Over time, these structures have been repurposed by locals or government authorities—some transformed into tourist attractions, others adapted for irrigation. These caves stand as witnesses to a historical era and have become an integral part of Bali's cultural heritage.

Whether you choose rafting or cave tubing, both water activities in Ubud offer unforgettable experiences for adventurers. They allow you to embrace the thrill of the river while soaking in the tranquility and vitality of nature. These activities connect you with both nature and history, enriching your journey with profound memories.

## Tips for Family Travel in Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some considerations for family travel in Ubud:

- **Book Family-Friendly Hotels or Resorts** Ubud offers many hotels and resorts, many of which are designed for families. These hotels often provide childcare services, kids' clubs, and pools.
- **Consider Hiring a Private Car** Hiring a car is a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings. It allows you to travel at your own pace and stop for photos and sightseeing along the way.
- **Be Sun-Smart** Bali's sun is intense. Bring sun protection items like hats, sunglasses, and sunscreen, and apply sunscreen frequently to prevent sunburn.
- **Be Mindful of Food Hygiene** Children have weaker immune systems and are more prone to illness. Avoid letting them drink water of uncertain quality and refrain from eating street food.
- **Be Prepared for Weather Changes** Bali's weather can be unpredictable, with sudden rain showers. Always carry rain gear and prepare some warm clothing like light jackets for your children.

These are some basic precautions to keep in mind. We hope you and your children have a fun and safe time exploring Ubud, Bali!
