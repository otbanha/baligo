Known for its rich cultural heritage and natural landscapes, Ubud is highly attractive to adult travelers. However, for younger children, visiting historical sites or admiring scenery can indeed be somewhat dull.

This guide specially selects the most family-friendly attractions and activities in Ubud, ensuring children have fun while parents enjoy their vacation too. From children's theme parks to DIY craft workshops, there's plenty to keep kids engaged; while attractions like Ubud Palace and Monkey Forest offer unique family experiences. Let's take a look at what Ubud has to offer for families:

## Family Activities

Top activities for families in Ubud:

### Alas Harum （👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 1+

Near Ubud's famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces lies a great spot for families called 'Alas Harum'. Located about 20 minutes from Ubud center, it's best known for its rice terrace views.

Beyond the famous terrace scenery, it offers family-friendly outdoor activities like flying foxes, swings, and the latest sky biking. Kids can enjoy thrilling adventures while parents relax in the peaceful surroundings, creating wonderful family memories together.

Alas Harum is not just an adventure park; it also carries a rich cultural heritage. The surrounding area is dotted with temples, statues, and other historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal place to explore Ubud, combining stunning scenery, thrilling activities, and a wealth of cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market （👉🏼 Map: [Central Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 0 and up

In the Ubud Market, children can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. The market is filled with a dazzling array of handicrafts, from exquisite carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural charm. Colorful fabrics are woven into various patterns and prints, each exuding the craftsmanship and rich historical heritage of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove, where children can observe, smell, and even personally experience Bali's unique art and craft techniques. They can witness the intricacies of ceramic art, feel the delicacy of wood carving, and learn how local residents preserve and pass down Bali's cultural traditions through weaving and handicrafts.

In this market, children can not only purchase unique souvenirs but also appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art through interactions with local artisans. Such experiences will fill them with curiosity about the world and enrich their educational journey.

### Ubud Skywalk （👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![想帶學齡前幼童要考慮一下，走不動要人家背就累了！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f041831b50b5914743f777ba9a8e4210.jpg)

Consider carefully if you're bringing preschoolers—carrying them when they get tired can be exhausting!

Suitable for ages: 8 and up

Just a short walk from Ubud Palace lies a hidden gem known as the Campuhan Ridge Walk, often referred to as the 'Ubud Skywalk.' Easily accessible on foot from the palace, this trail offers a serene escape into nature, perfect for families seeking tranquility and breathtaking scenery. Situated along a ridge, the path provides stunning 360-degree views, allowing you to immerse yourself in Bali's natural beauty while leaving the city's hustle and bustle behind.

https://youtube.com/shorts/3PmPpoHI_6c

This is an ideal spot for families, offering a chance for both kids and parents to connect with nature. As you stroll along the trail, you'll be surrounded by peaceful landscapes, free from traffic noise, with only the sounds of nature accompanying you. It's a perfect way to unwind and enjoy the beauty of Bali together.

Curious about how to get to the Skywalk, how long it takes, or the best time to visit? Check out [Jay's detailed guide](/blog/ubud-campuhan-ridge-walk/) for all the information you need.

### Bali's New Monkey Forest: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River immerses you in Bali's pristine natural beauty! The crystal-clear Petanu River flows through the forest, and an artificial waterfall adds a refreshing ambiance.

Here, you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying foxes while relaxing in traditional Balinese pavilions, listening to the soothing sounds of flowing water, and embracing the tranquility of nature.

Now open for visits! Hours: Daily from 9 AM to 6 PM (last entry at 5 PM) Special price: Adults USD$5, Children USD$3

Escape the crowds and experience the unique melukat water purification ritual, immersing yourself in Bali's spirituality and natural beauty!

Friendly tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, this is the place to go. The monkeys here are gentler and won't attack. In Ubud, the monkeys are more aggressive and may snatch items.

### Baby-Friendly River Tubing

https://youtube.com/shorts/Z_vDb7AbMKg

Suitable for ages: 4 and up

While rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it carries certain risks as safety depends on river conditions. For a safer water experience, cave tubing/river tubing (as shown in the video above) is a relatively safer option that's more suitable for family fun.

It's worth noting that many caves in the Ubud area date back to the period when Japan briefly ruled Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces carried out construction projects on the island, including building bunkers, caves, and tunnels as defensive fortifications. These caves and tunnels were later repurposed or modified by local residents or government agencies. Some have been transformed into tourist attractions, while others are used for irrigation purposes. These caves bear witness to past historical periods and have become an integral part of Bali's history and culture today.

Whether it's rafting or cave tubing, these two water activities in Ubud offer unforgettable experiences for adventurers. They allow you to feel the thrill of challenge in the river while enjoying the tranquility and vitality bestowed by nature. These activities enable you to interact with both nature and history, adding rich experiences and profound memories to your journey.

## Tips for Family Travel in Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some tips for traveling to Ubud with kids:

- **Book Family-Friendly Hotels** Ubud has many hotels designed for families, often offering childcare services, kids’ clubs, and pools.
- **Consider Private Transfers** Private transfers are a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings at your own pace, stopping for photos and attractions along the way.
- **Sun Protection** Bali’s sun is intense. Bring sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, and protective clothing, and reapply sunscreen frequently to avoid sunburn.
- **Food Hygiene** Children are more susceptible to illness. Avoid letting them drink water of uncertain quality or eat street food.
- **Weather Changes** Bali’s weather can be unpredictable. Carry rain gear and pack light jackets for your kids.

These are some basic tips to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip to Ubud with your family!
