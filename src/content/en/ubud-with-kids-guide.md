Ubud is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and natural landscapes, which are highly attractive to adult travelers. However, for young children, visiting historical sites or admiring scenery can indeed be somewhat dull.

This guide specially selects the most suitable attractions and activities for families traveling with children in Ubud, ensuring kids have fun while parents enjoy their vacation. For example, children's theme parks and DIY craft courses can help kids release energy; while Ubud Palace and Monkey Forest offer unique family experiences. Let's take a look at what Ubud has to offer for families:

## Family Activities

The most suitable activities for families in Ubud:

### Alas Harum （👉🏼Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable age: 1 year and above

Near the famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces in Ubud, there's a family-friendly place called 'Alas Harum'. It's about a 20-minute drive from Ubud city center and is most famous for its rice terrace views.

Not only does it offer the renowned terrace scenery, but also outdoor activities suitable for families, such as flying fox, swings, and the latest sky biking. Kids can experience thrilling adventures here, while parents can relax in the peaceful environment, enjoying quality family time together.

Alas Harum is not just an adventure park; it also carries rich cultural heritage. The surrounding area is dotted with temples, statues, and other historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal place to explore Ubud, combining stunning scenery, thrilling activities, and rich cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market （👉🏼 Map: [Central Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 0 and up

At the Ubud Market, children can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. The market is filled with a dazzling array of handicrafts, from exquisite carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural charm. Colorful fabrics are woven into various patterns and prints, each radiating the exquisite craftsmanship and rich historical heritage of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove, where children can observe, smell, and even personally experience Bali's unique art and craft techniques. They can witness the intricacies of ceramic art, feel the delicacy of wood carving, and learn how local residents preserve and pass down Bali's cultural traditions through weaving and handicrafts.

In this market, children can not only purchase unique souvenirs but also appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art through interactions with local artisans. Such experiences will fill them with curiosity about the world and enrich their educational journey.

### Bali's New Monkey Forest: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River immerses you in Bali's pristine natural beauty! The clear Petanu River winds through the forest, while artificial waterfalls add a refreshing ambiance to the environment.

Here, you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying fox bats while relaxing in traditional Balinese pavilions, listening to the flowing water, and feeling the tranquility of nature.

Now open for visits! Hours: Daily 9 AM - 6 PM (last entry at 5 PM) Special price: Adults $5, Children $3

Escape the crowds and experience the unique melukat water purification ritual, feeling Bali's spirituality and natural beauty!

Friendly tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, choose this place. The monkeys here are gentler and won't attack people. If you go to Ubud Monkey Forest, the monkeys are more aggressive and may snatch items.

### Kid-Friendly River Tubing

https://youtube.com/shorts/Z_vDb7AbMKg

Suitable for ages: 4 and up

While white-water rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it carries certain risks, as safety depends on river conditions. For a safer water adventure, cave tubing or river tubing (as shown in the video above) is a relatively safer option and more suitable for families.

It's worth noting that many caves in the Ubud area date back to the brief period of Japanese rule in Bali. During World War II, the Japanese military constructed bunkers, caves, and tunnels as part of their defensive fortifications. These caves and tunnels have since been repurposed or modified by locals or government agencies. Some have been turned into tourist attractions, while others are used for irrigation. These caves bear witness to a historical era and have become part of Bali's cultural heritage.

Whether it's rafting or cave tubing, both water activities in Ubud offer unforgettable experiences for adventurers. They allow you to feel the thrill of the challenge while enjoying the tranquility and vitality of nature. These activities enable you to interact with both nature and history, enriching your journey with profound memories.

## Tips for Family Travel in Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some considerations for family travel in Ubud:

- **Book family-friendly hotels or resorts**: Ubud offers many options, with numerous hotels designed specifically for families. These often include childcare services, kids' clubs, and pools.
- **Consider hiring a private car**: This is a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings at your own pace, stopping for photos and attractions along the way.
- **Sun protection**: Bali's sun is intense. Bring sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, and protective clothing, and reapply sunscreen frequently to prevent sunburn.
- **Food hygiene**: Children have weaker immune systems, so avoid letting them drink water of uncertain quality or eat food from street vendors.
- **Weather changes**: Bali's weather can be unpredictable, with sudden rain showers. Always carry rain gear and pack light jackets for the kids.

These are some basic precautions to keep in mind. We hope you and your children have a fun and safe time exploring Ubud, Bali!
