Ubud is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes, making it highly appealing to adult travelers. However, for young children, visiting historical sites or simply admiring the scenery can indeed become a bit dull.

This guide specially selects the best kid-friendly attractions and activities in Ubud, ensuring children have a blast while parents also enjoy the vacation. From children's theme parks and DIY craft workshops that let kids burn off energy, to family-friendly spots like the Ubud Palace and Monkey Forest, there's something for everyone. Let's first take a look at the family activities Ubud has to offer:

## Family Activities

The best activities for families with kids in Ubud:

### Alas Harum (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc22b17fd8a9f30fd3626ee79eab7984.jpg)

Suitable Age: 1 year and up

Near Ubud's famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces, there's a fantastic spot for families called 'Alas Harum.' It's about a 20-minute drive from central Ubud and is best known for its stunning rice terrace views.

Besides the iconic terraced scenery, it offers family-friendly outdoor activities like flying foxes, swings, and the latest sky biking. Kids can enjoy thrilling adventures while parents relax in the peaceful surroundings, making for quality family time.

Alas Harum is more than just an adventure park—it's steeped in rich cultural heritage. The surrounding area is dotted with temples, statues, and other historical sites that showcase Bali's long history and culture. For families, Alas Harum is an ideal place to explore Ubud, combining stunning scenery, thrilling activities, and a wealth of cultural experiences.

### Ubud Art Market (👉🏼 Map: [Ubud Center](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_026ba58f9cd09d22c78e23def9b1c5c2.jpg)

Suitable for ages: 0 and up

At Ubud Market, kids can immerse themselves in a world of vibrant colors. The stalls are filled with a dazzling array of handicrafts, from intricate carvings to delicate textiles, all showcasing Bali's unique cultural flair. Colorful fabrics are woven into bright patterns and prints, each radiating the skill and rich history of local artisans.

This market is a true cultural treasure trove. Children can observe, smell, and even experience Bali's distinctive art and craft techniques firsthand. They can admire the finesse of ceramic art, feel the delicacy of wood carving, and learn how locals preserve and pass on Bali's cultural traditions through weaving and handicrafts.

In this market, kids can not only pick up unique souvenirs but also, through interactions with local artisans, appreciate the beauty of culture and the value of traditional art. Such an experience will spark their curiosity about the world and enrich their journey with deeper learning.

### Ubud Skywalk (👉🏼 Map: [Popular Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Suitable for ages: 8 and up

I recommend the Campuhan Ridge Walk, often called the 'Ubud Skywalk,' which is easily reachable on foot from the Ubud Palace. This hidden gem near the heart of Ubud offers you and your family a peaceful escape into nature with stunning scenery. The trail runs along a ridge, providing breathtaking 360-degree views away from the city's hustle and bustle.

It's a perfect family-friendly spot where kids and parents can enjoy the beauty of nature together. You can take a leisurely stroll while soaking in the views and the unique tranquility of the ridge. ### Bali's New Monkey Forest: Kemenuh Monkey River (👉🏼 Map: [Top Attractions Around Ubud](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing))

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud, Kemenuh Monkey River is a natural paradise that immerses you in Bali's pristine beauty! The crystal-clear Petanu River winds through the forest, while man-made waterfalls add a refreshing, cool ambiance.

Here, you can observe long-tailed macaques, rare white monkeys, and flying foxes, all while relaxing in traditional Balinese pavilions, listening to the flowing water and soaking in nature's tranquility.

Now open to visitors! Hours: Daily 9 AM to 6 PM (last entry at 5 PM). Special rates: Adults 80,000 IDR, Children 40,000 IDR.

Escape the crowds and experience a unique Melukat water purification blessing ceremony, embracing Bali's spirituality and natural beauty!

Friendly tip: If you want to take photos with monkeys in Bali, choose this spot—the monkeys here are gentle and won't attack. At the Ubud Monkey Forest, monkeys tend to snatch things and can be more aggressive.

### Baby-Friendly River Tubing

https://youtube.com/shorts/Z_vDb7AbMKg

Suitable age: 4 years and up

While rafting is one of Ubud's most famous activities, it carries certain risks that depend on river conditions. For a safer water experience, cave tubing or river tubing (as shown in the video above) is a relatively safer option, better suited for family fun.

It's worth noting that many caves in the Ubud area date back to Japan's brief occupation of Bali. During World War II, Japanese forces carried out construction projects on the island, including building bunkers, caves, and tunnels for defense. These caves and tunnels were later repurposed or renovated by local residents or government agencies. Some were turned into tourist attractions, while others were used for irrigation. These caves bear witness to a historical era and have now become part of Bali's history and culture.

Whether it's rafting or river tubing, both water activities in Ubud offer unforgettable experiences for adventurers. They let you feel the thrill of challenge in the river while enjoying the tranquility and vitality that nature provides. These activities allow you to interact with nature and history, adding rich experiences and lasting memories to your journey.

## Tips for a Family Trip to Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a2a659ccd7ce835a19aafcb770b98d37.png)

Here are some tips for a family trip to Ubud:

- **Book a family-friendly hotel or resort** Ubud offers many hotels and resorts, many designed for families. These often include services like babysitting, kids' clubs, and pools.
- **Consider hiring a private driver** A private driver is a great way to explore Ubud and its surroundings. It lets you travel at your own pace and stop for photos and sights along the way.
- **Protect against the sun** Bali's sun is strong. Bring sun protection like hats, sunglasses, and UV-protective clothing, and apply sunscreen frequently to prevent sunburn.
- **Watch food and water hygiene** Children have weaker immune systems and can get sick easily. Avoid giving them water of uncertain cleanliness and skip food from street vendors.
- **Be prepared for weather changes** Bali's weather can be unpredictable—sunny one moment, rainy the next. Always carry rain gear and pack light jackets for the kids.

These are some basic tips. We hope you and your children have a fun and safe time in Ubud, Bali!
