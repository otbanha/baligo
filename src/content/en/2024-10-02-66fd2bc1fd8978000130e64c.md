After the official commencement of Bali's first phase Light Rail Transit (LRT) project on September 4, 2024, with a ceremony at Kuta Central Parking, the Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) construction is scheduled to start excavation in April 2025, representing a significant advancement in local transportation infrastructure. Next, we will delve into the various stages of Bali's MRT, the construction process, and its future impact on tourism and local residents.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99819bd6538fcfc0eb8fcc36f0c1005b.jpg)

## Background of the MRT

**Origins of Bali's MRT Project:**

The Bali MRT project aims to address the increasingly severe traffic issues, particularly congestion during peak tourist seasons. Several years ago, the local government began preparations for this major infrastructure project, but due to financial and technical challenges, it remained stalled. Now, with active participation from international investors, the project is finally moving forward.

## Project Concerns and Investor Confidence

**Attracting International Investors:**

Acting Governor of Bali Province, Mahendra Jaya, expressed initial concerns about the massive project, fearing insufficient investor interest. However, with enthusiastic responses from international investors, these concerns have gradually diminished. Many international consortia have shown keen interest in Bali's MRT project and are willing to provide financial and technical support.

## Contribution from Dubai's Energy Minister

**Source of Investment Funds:**

During his visit to Bali, the Energy Minister of Dubai pledged to provide 75% of the funding for this project, making it more financially viable. These funds will primarily be used to purchase high-tech Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs) and cover related construction costs.

## Technical Specifications of the TBM

**Equipment for the Bali Metro:**

This project will utilize eight large Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs), six more than those used for the Jakarta Metro. The TBMs for Bali will have a diameter of 7.2 meters, surpassing Jakarta's 6 meters, meaning the Bali Metro tunnels will be more spacious. These machines are scheduled to arrive in Bali by April 2025, when tunnel excavation will commence.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_639d9f2561697e36de04070031810cf0.jpg)

## Depth of the Underground Tunnels

**Specific Data on Tunnel Construction:**

According to the plan, the Bali Metro tunnels will be dug approximately 20 to 30 meters below ground. Due to Bali's unique geological conditions, the construction team will conduct detailed geological surveys to ensure the tunnels' stability and safety. The tunnel construction will cover major urban areas of Bali, with the first phase connecting Denpasar to Kuta and extending to Bali International Airport.

## First Phase of Construction

**Areas and Mileage Involved:**

The first phase of the metro line will cover Denpasar city center, Kuta area, and Bali International Airport, spanning approximately 15 kilometers. The primary goal of this phase is to provide a rapid commuting solution, alleviating traffic pressure for both tourists and locals in these bustling areas. Metro stations will be strategically located in major tourist zones such as Kuta, Legian, and Denpasar city center, ensuring easy access for visitors and residents alike.

## Second, Third, and Fourth Phases

**Long-Term Development Plan:**

Following the completion of the first phase, the metro construction will gradually expand into the second, third, and fourth phases. The second phase aims to extend the line to popular tourist destinations like Nusa Dua and Sanur, offering broader transportation options. The third phase will cover Bali's northern and eastern regions, with the goal of promoting development in these areas and attracting more visitors. Finally, the fourth phase will encompass the entire island of Bali, forming a comprehensive metro network.

## Project Timeline

**Estimated Completion Dates and Challenges:**

According to the Bali government's plan, the first phase of the metro construction is expected to be completed by 2030. While this project is highly ambitious, the government and relevant departments anticipate numerous challenges, particularly in construction progress, technical difficulties, and funding allocation. However, the government remains confident in the project's future and is committed to ensuring its timely completion.

## Impact on Local Transportation

**Alleviating Traffic Congestion:**

Bali has long struggled with traffic congestion, especially during peak tourist seasons. With the introduction of the metro, both locals and tourists will be able to travel more quickly and sustainably, significantly reducing road traffic pressure. Once the first phase becomes operational, the metro will serve as an ideal mode of transportation to the airport and major tourist attractions.

## Environmental Impact

**Reduction in Carbon Emissions:**

As a clean mode of transportation, the metro system will significantly reduce carbon emissions in Bali. Currently, local transportation heavily relies on private cars and motorcycles, which exert considerable pressure on the environment. The operation of the metro will encourage more people to opt for public transport, thereby reducing fuel consumption and lowering Bali's overall carbon footprint.

## Metro Fares and Benefits for Local Residents

**Will Local Residents Ride for Free?**

According to the Bali government's preliminary plan, local residents are expected to enjoy discounted fares, and may even ride for free during certain periods. This will greatly alleviate their travel costs. The government hopes this initiative will encourage more locals to choose the metro system, reducing reliance on private cars and motorcycles.

## Tourist Fare Policy

**Suggested Cost for Weekly Passes:**

For tourists vacationing in Bali, the government is considering introducing discounted weekly and monthly passes, allowing them to use the metro more frequently at a lower cost. Initial estimates suggest that weekly passes may be priced between IDR 150,000 to 200,000, offering great convenience for tourists frequently traveling between different attractions.

## Anticipated Challenges and Solutions

**Challenges Facing Bali's MRT Project:**

While the MRT construction plan is highly anticipated, it faces several challenges including land acquisition, traffic diversions during construction, and potential delays. Additionally, as the project involves significant financial and technical resources, effective management and ensuring construction quality will be crucial to its success.

## The Significance of MRT for Bali's Future

**Long-term Impact on Tourism and Economy:**

The completion of Bali's MRT will not only greatly enhance local transportation infrastructure but also provide a significant boost to tourism. With improved transportation conditions, more tourists will be inclined to choose Bali as a vacation destination and potentially extend their stays. Furthermore, the MRT construction will create numerous job opportunities and stimulate local economic development. In the long run, the MRT system will contribute to a more sustainable future for Bali, establishing it as a central transportation hub for this tourist hotspot.
