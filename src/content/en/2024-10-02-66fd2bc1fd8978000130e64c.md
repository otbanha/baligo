After the official commencement of Bali's first phase of the Light Rail Transit (LRT) project on September 4, 2024, with a ceremony at Kuta Central Parking, the Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) construction is scheduled to start excavation in April 2025. This represents a major advancement in local transportation infrastructure. We will now delve into the various phases of the Bali MRT, the construction process, and its future impact on tourism and local residents.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99819bd6538fcfc0eb8fcc36f0c1005b.jpg)

## Background of the MRT

**Origins of the Bali MRT Project:**

The Bali MRT project aims to address the increasingly severe traffic issues, particularly the congestion during peak tourist seasons. The local government began preparations for this large-scale infrastructure project several years ago, but due to financial and technical challenges, it had not been initiated. Now, with active participation from international investors, the project is finally being implemented.

## Project Concerns and Investor Confidence

**Attracting International Investors:**

Acting Governor of Bali, Mahendra Jaya, expressed initial concerns about the massive project, fearing it might not attract sufficient investors. However, with the positive response from international investors, these concerns have gradually diminished. Many international consortia have shown keen interest in the Bali MRT project and are willing to provide financial and technical support.

## Contribution from the Dubai Minister of Energy

**Source of Investment Funds:**

During his visit to Bali, the UAE Minister of Energy pledged to provide 75% of the funding for this project, making it financially viable. The funds will primarily be used to purchase advanced Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs) and cover related construction costs.

## Technical Specifications of TBM Machines

**Equipment Used for Bali Metro:**

This project will utilize eight large Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs), six more than those used for the Jakarta Metro. The TBMs for Bali will have a diameter of 7.2 meters, surpassing Jakarta's 6 meters, meaning Bali Metro tunnels will be more spacious. The equipment is scheduled to arrive in Bali in April 2025, when tunnel excavation will commence.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_639d9f2561697e36de04070031810cf0.jpg)

## Depth of Underground Tunnels

**Specific Data on Tunnel Construction:**

According to the plan, Bali Metro tunnels will be dug approximately 20 to 30 meters underground. Due to Bali's unique geological conditions, the construction team will conduct detailed geological surveys to ensure tunnel stability and safety. The tunnel construction will cover major urban areas of Bali, with the first phase connecting Denpasar to Kuta and extending to Bali International Airport.

## First Phase of Construction

**Areas Involved and Mileage:**

The initial phase of the metro line will cover Denpasar city center, Kuta area, and Bali International Airport, spanning approximately 15 kilometers. The primary goal of this phase is to provide a rapid commuting solution, alleviating traffic congestion in these bustling areas for both tourists and locals. Metro stations will be strategically located in major tourist zones such as Kuta, Legian, and Denpasar city center, ensuring convenient access for travelers and residents alike.

## Phases Two, Three, and Four

**Long-term Development Plan:**

Following the completion of the first phase, the metro construction will gradually expand into phases two, three, and four. The second phase will extend to popular tourist destinations like Nusa Dua and Sanur, offering broader transportation options. The third phase will cover Bali's northern and eastern regions, aiming to boost development and attract more visitors. Finally, the fourth phase will encompass the entire island, forming a comprehensive metro network.

## Project Timeline

**Estimated Completion Dates and Challenges:**

According to the Bali government's plan, the first phase of the metro construction is expected to be completed by 2030. While this project is highly ambitious, the government and relevant departments anticipate numerous challenges, particularly in construction progress, technical difficulties, and funding allocation. However, the government remains confident in the project's future and is committed to ensuring its timely completion.

## Impact on Local Transportation

**Alleviating Traffic Congestion:**

Bali has long struggled with severe traffic congestion, especially during peak tourist seasons. With the metro in operation, both locals and tourists will enjoy faster and more eco-friendly travel options, significantly reducing road traffic pressure. Once the first phase is operational, the metro will become the ideal mode of transportation for accessing the airport and major tourist attractions.

## Environmental Impact

**Reduction in Carbon Emissions:**

The metro system, as a clean mode of transportation, will significantly reduce carbon emissions in Bali. Currently, local traffic heavily relies on private cars and motorcycles, which puts considerable pressure on the environment. The operation of the metro will encourage more people to opt for public transport, thereby reducing fuel usage and lowering Bali's overall carbon footprint.

## Metro Fare and Local Resident Benefits

**Will Local Residents Ride for Free?**

According to the Bali government's preliminary plan, local residents are expected to enjoy discounted fares and may even ride for free during specific periods, which will greatly alleviate their travel costs. The government hopes this initiative will encourage more locals to choose the metro system, reducing reliance on private cars and motorcycles.

## Tourist Fare Policy

**Suggested Cost for Weekly Passes:**

For tourists vacationing in Bali, the government is considering introducing weekly and monthly passes to allow them to use the metro more frequently at an economical rate. Based on initial estimates, the price for a weekly pass may be set between IDR 150,000 and IDR 200,000, offering great convenience to tourists who frequently travel between different attractions.

## Anticipated Challenges and Solutions

**Challenges Facing Bali's Metro Project**

While the metro construction plan is highly anticipated, it faces several challenges including land acquisition, traffic diversions during construction, and potential delays. Additionally, as the project involves substantial funding and technical requirements, effectively managing resources and ensuring construction quality will be crucial to its success.

## The Significance of Metro for Bali's Future

**Long-term Impact on Tourism and Economy**

The completion of Bali's metro system will not only significantly enhance local transportation infrastructure but also provide a major boost to the tourism industry. With improved transportation conditions, more tourists will be willing to choose Bali as a vacation destination and potentially extend their stays. Furthermore, the metro construction will create numerous job opportunities and stimulate local economic development. In the long run, the metro system will contribute to a more sustainable future for Bali, establishing itself as the island's central transportation hub.
