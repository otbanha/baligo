### 🗞️ Today’s Highlights: Aviation Resilience & Market Shifts

- **Flight Normalization**: Despite disruptions caused by volcanic ash clouds from Flores Island, Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) is operating normally today. Official data shows that international tourist arrivals exceeded 1.6 million in the first quarter, demonstrating strong recovery despite geopolitical challenges.
- **Exploring New Markets**: The tourism industry (ASITA) is shifting its focus to Central Asian countries like Kazakhstan, aiming to position Bali as an international transit hub akin to Singapore or Bangkok. This strategy is expected to diversify visitor sources in the coming years.

### 🌤️ Weather Forecast

- **Today’s Overview**: Bali is sunny today.
- **Temperature & Humidity**: Temperatures in the southern lowlands range between **25°C - 32°C**.

### 📄 Traveler Concerns: Fees & Transportation

- **Tourist Levy Checks**: Authorities have recently intensified checks for the **Tourist Levy** of 150,000 IDR. All international travelers (including children) are reminded to pay this fee via the Love Bali website before arrival, as random checks at the airport have increased.
- **Sweet Spot for Prices**: The current period, following Easter, is a low tourist season. Reports indicate that hotel and flight prices are generally more affordable compared to the summer peak, and attractions are less crowded, making it an ideal time for slow, immersive travel.
