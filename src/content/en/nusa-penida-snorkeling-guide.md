## Can You Snorkel Everywhere in Bali?

Although Bali’s main island is surrounded by coastline, there are actually few places truly suitable for snorkeling or diving. Some of the most famous dive sites like Blue Lagoon, Amed, and Tulamben are located in the more remote eastern part, requiring over two to three hours of travel from the airport. Below is Xiao Jie’s introduction to snorkeling in Amed:

https://youtu.be/qdS8Td7QPYY?si=J-jy5ieS7-eAsl0E

To reach snorkeling or diving spots more quickly, we recommend taking a ride from the airport to Sanur, staying there for a night or two, and then catching an early morning speedboat to Nusa Penida, which takes about 30 minutes. This is a more convenient option that avoids long travel times.

For those interested in what Sanur has to offer in terms of fun and exploration, check out Xiao Jie’s "[Sanur Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=laRb_l8BdLM)".

## Crystal Bay

![Crystal Bay](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_233401d4c81f691e18d2a6149a0d9830.jpg)

Crystal Bay

Crystal Bay is one of the popular attractions on Nusa Penida, a secluded bay on the west coast of the island with a beach stretching about 200 meters. The beach is accessible by road. Besides its charming palm-fringed sandy beach, the bay is most famous for being Nusa Penida's premier snorkeling and diving spot. The water here is crystal clear (hence the name Crystal Bay). Divers praise the excellent underwater visibility, which can sometimes reach up to 30 meters.

In addition to seeing a wide variety of colorful corals and tropical fish, Crystal Bay is one of the few places in the world where you can observe the mola-mola (known as sunfish in Taiwan/Japan) up close. These massive migratory fish can weigh up to 2000 kilograms and reach lengths of 3 meters. Diving here between July and October offers a chance to encounter and swim alongside them.

## Gamat Bay

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774881880928-gamatbay1.jpeg)

Gamat Bay

Gamat Bay and Crystal Bay are hailed as the two best snorkeling spots on Nusa Penida. The shallow waters here are crystal clear, making it ideal for snorkeling and observing coral reef ecosystems.

At Gamat Bay, you can see various hard corals such as staghorn coral, flower coral, and large brain coral. Many algae like anemones and sea fans also thrive here. Small tropical fish like butterflyfish, snappers, and surgeonfish dart among the coral reefs. Occasionally, small reef sharks can also be spotted.

Another highlight is the sea turtles. Different species of sea turtles, such as black turtles and green turtles, are often observed in the shallow waters near the shore. From June to October, during the turtle nesting season, you might even witness mother turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs.

Due to its proximity to the coast and calm waters, Gamat Bay is perfect for children and beginners to snorkel. Remember to wear your life jacket, goggles, and fins, and you can safely enjoy the magical world of this underwater garden.

## Manta Point

![Manta Point](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_20761005e11f28ee9b74adf674bab312.jpg)

Manta Point

Manta Point is one of the most distinctive attractions on Nusa Penida Island, where you can get up close and personal with the giant manta rays (Manta Ray), offering a truly unique and stunning experience.

Known as 'devil fish,' the giant manta ray can grow up to 7 meters in length and weigh around 3000 kg—pretty impressive, right? With such size and weight, it's natural to feel apprehensive about approaching them, but don't worry. Despite their enormous size, manta rays are not dangerous at all. They are large but friendly towards humans. There are two types of manta rays in the world: the reef manta ray and the oceanic manta ray.

The manta rays at Manta Point on Nusa Penida Island are of the reef manta ray type (Manta alfredi). This species is the second largest after the oceanic manta ray, averaging 3 to 3.5 meters in size. These beautiful creatures are filter feeders, meaning they consume plankton, crustaceans, and small fish. Although they have long tails, they are not venomous, making swimming with manta rays very safe. In addition to their long tails, manta rays have the largest brains among bony fish. They are highly interactive with humans, making Manta Point on Nusa Penida Island the best spot for snorkelers and divers to capture close encounters with these majestic creatures.

A reminder to everyone: when we say 'interaction,' we don’t mean actually touching these wild animals! The primary reason is to protect the manta rays' skin—they are covered in a layer of mucus that helps shield their skin from environmental harm. If tourists touch them, this mucus layer can be removed, increasing the risk of skin infections for the manta rays. Although manta rays are gentle, they are still wild animals, and unauthorized touching may startle them or cause them to react instinctively, potentially harming those nearby. Most manta ray diving locations worldwide have strict regulations against touching marine life, and visitors should adhere to these rules.

If you want to encounter manta rays, the recommended months are **April to November**, especially during the dry season (April to October), when the water is clear, currents are stable, and visibility is at its best, making it the ideal time for snorkeling and diving.

Specifically, **August to October** is the peak period for encountering manta rays, as the currents during this time bring abundant plankton, attracting large numbers of manta rays to feed. **Manta Point** and **Manta Bay** on **Nusa Penida Island** in southern Bali are popular spots for observing manta rays.

Please note: The appearance of manta rays is influenced by weather and ocean currents, so sightings cannot be guaranteed 100%.

## Toyapakeh Wall Point

Toyapakeh Wall Point is actually a cliff located just below the Adiwana Warnakali Dive Center.

This dive site features a small coral plateau at a depth of 4 to 6 meters, which is the best spot for snorkeling. Beyond the coral plateau, there are two layers: the first layer is about 12 to 15 meters deep, and the second layer is around 40 meters deep. This site is very popular among divers due to its clear waters and thriving coral reefs.
