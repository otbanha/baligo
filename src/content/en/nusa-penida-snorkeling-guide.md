## Can You Snorkel Everywhere in Bali?

Although Bali's main island is surrounded by coastline, there are actually few spots truly suitable for snorkeling or diving. The most famous dive sites—Blue Lagoon, Amed, and Tulamben—are all in the remote eastern part, taking two to three hours or more by car from the airport. ## Crystal Bay

![Crystal Bay](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_233401d4c81f691e18d2a6149a0d9830.jpg)

Crystal Bay

Crystal Bay is one of the most popular spots on Nusa Penida—a secluded cove on the island’s west coast with a beach stretching about 200 meters. You can reach it by road. Besides its charming palm-fringed shoreline, this bay is best known as Nusa Penida’s premier snorkeling and diving destination. The water here is crystal clear (hence the name), offering excellent underwater visibility that can reach up to 30 meters.

At Crystal Bay, you’ll not only find a dazzling array of colorful corals and tropical fish, but it’s also one of the few places in the world where you can get up close with the ocean sunfish (mola-mola, known as manbo fish in Taiwan and Japan). These massive migratory fish can weigh up to 2,000 kilograms and grow up to 3 meters long. If you dive here between July and October, you might just encounter and swim alongside them.

## Gamat Bay

![Gamat Bay](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774881880928-gamatbay1.jpeg)

Gamat Bay

Gamat Bay and Crystal Bay are hailed as the two best snorkeling spots on Nusa Penida Island. The shallow waters here are crystal clear, perfect for snorkeling and observing coral reef ecosystems.

At Gamat Bay, you can see various hard corals, such as staghorn coral, flowerpot coral, and large brain coral. Many algae like sea anemones and sea fans thrive here as well. Small tropical fish, including double-banded butterflyfish, snappers, and surgeonfish, dart among the coral clusters. Occasionally, small reef sharks can also be spotted.

Another highlight is the sea turtles. Different species, such as black turtles and green sea turtles, can often be observed in the shallow waters near the shore. From June to October, it's the turtle nesting season, offering a chance to see mother turtles coming ashore to lay eggs.

Due to its proximity to the coast and gentle currents, Gamat Bay is ideal for children and beginner snorkelers. Just remember to wear a life jacket, mask, and fins, and you can safely enjoy the magical underwater garden.

## Manta Point

![Manta Point](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_20761005e11f28ee9b74adf674bab312.jpg)

Manta Point

Manta Point is one of the most unique spots on Nusa Penida Island. At this dive site, you can get up close and personal with giant manta rays, offering a truly extraordinary and breathtaking experience.

Commonly known as 'devil fish,' the giant manta ray can reach up to 7 meters in length and weigh around 3,000 kilograms—pretty huge, right? With such size and weight, it's natural to feel apprehensive about getting close, but don't worry. Despite their massive size, manta rays are not dangerous at all. They are large but friendly to humans. There are two species of manta rays: the reef manta and the oceanic manta.

The manta rays at Manta Point on Nusa Penida are of the reef manta type (Manta alfredi). This species is the second largest after the oceanic manta, with an average size of 3 to 3.5 meters. These beautiful creatures are filter feeders, meaning they eat plankton, crustaceans, and small fish. Although their tails are long, they are not venomous, so swimming with manta rays is very safe. Besides their long tails, manta rays have the largest brain-to-body ratio among all fish. They are very interactive with humans, making Manta Point on Nusa Penida the best choice for snorkelers and divers to capture close encounters with these magnificent rays.

Just a reminder: when we say 'contact,' we don't mean actually touching these wild creatures!! The main reason is to protect the skin of manta rays—they're covered in a layer of mucus that helps shield their skin from environmental harm. If tourists touch them, this mucus layer gets removed, increasing the risk of skin infections. Although manta rays are gentle by nature, they're still wild animals. Unauthorized touching can startle them, or they might instinctively injure people nearby. Most regions around the world where you can dive with manta rays have strict rules against touching the fish, and visitors should follow these regulations.

If you want to encounter manta rays, the recommended months are **between April and November**, especially during the dry season (April to October), when the water is clear, currents are stable, and visibility is higher—making it the best time for snorkeling and diving.

Among these, **August to October** is the peak season for seeing manta rays, as currents during this period carry abundant plankton, attracting large numbers of them to feed. In southern Bali, **Manta Point and Manta Bay on Nusa Penida Island** are popular spots for viewing manta rays.

Note: The appearance of manta rays is influenced by weather and currents, so sightings are never 100% guaranteed.

## Toyapakeh Wall Point

Toyapakeh Wall Point is actually a cliff wall, located directly below the Adiwana Warnakali Dive Center.

This dive site features a small coral terrace at a depth of 4 to 6 meters—the best spot for snorkeling. Beyond the coral terrace, it splits into two levels: the first at about 12 to 15 meters deep, and the second at around 40 meters. It's a very popular dive site due to its clear waters and thriving coral.
