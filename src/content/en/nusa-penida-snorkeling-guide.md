## Can You Snorkel Everywhere in Bali?

Although Bali's main island is surrounded by coastline, there are actually few places suitable for snorkeling or diving. Some of the most famous dive spots like Blue Lagoon, Amed, and Tulamben are located in the more remote eastern part, taking over two to three hours by car from the airport. Below is Xiao Jie's introduction to snorkeling in Amed:

https://youtu.be/qdS8Td7QPYY?si=J-jy5ieS7-eAsl0E

To reach suitable snorkeling or diving spots more quickly, it's recommended to take a car from the airport to Sanur, stay there for a night or two, and then take a speedboat early in the morning for about 30 minutes to Nusa Penida. This is a more relaxed option that avoids long travel times.

## Crystal Bay

![Crystal Bay](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_233401d4c81f691e18d2a6149a0d9830.jpg)

Crystal Bay

Crystal Bay is one of the popular attractions on Nusa Penida, a secluded bay on the west coast of the island with a beach about 200 meters long. The beach is accessible by road. Besides the charming palm-fringed beach, the bay is most famous as Nusa Penida's premier snorkeling and diving destination. The water here is crystal clear (hence the name Crystal Bay). Divers praise the excellent underwater visibility, sometimes reaching up to 30 meters.

At Crystal Bay, besides seeing a variety of colorful corals and tropical fish, it's also one of the few places in the world where you can observe ocean sunfish (mola mola, known as manbo fish in Taiwan/Japan) up close. These giant migratory fish can weigh up to 2000 kg and reach 3 meters in length. Diving here between July and October gives you a chance to encounter and swim with them.

## Gamat Bay

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774881880928-gamatbay1.jpeg)

Gamat Bay

Gamat Bay and Crystal Bay are hailed as the two best snorkeling spots on Nusa Penida. The shallow waters here are crystal clear, perfect for snorkeling and observing coral reef ecosystems.

At Gamat Bay, you can see various types of hard corals, such as staghorn coral, flower bud coral, and large brain coral. Many algae, like anemones and sea fans, also thrive here. Small tropical fish, such as butterflyfish, snappers, and surgeonfish, swim among the coral reefs. Occasionally, you might even spot small reef sharks.

Another highlight is the sea turtles. Different species, like black turtles and green turtles, can often be observed in the shallow waters near the shore. From June to October, it's turtle nesting season, offering a chance to see mother turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs.

Due to its proximity to the coast and calm currents, Gamat Bay is perfect for children and beginner snorkelers. Just remember to wear a life jacket, goggles, and fins, and you can safely enjoy the magical underwater world of this marine garden.

## Manta Point

![Manta Point](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_20761005e11f28ee9b74adf674bab312.jpg)

Manta Point

Manta Point is one of the most distinctive attractions on Nusa Penida. At this diving spot, you can get up close and personal with the giant manta rays, offering a truly unique and awe-inspiring experience.

Known as 'devil fish,' manta rays can grow up to 7 meters in length and weigh around 3,000 kilograms—quite impressive, right? Given their size and weight, it's natural to feel apprehensive about approaching them. But don't worry, despite their enormous size, manta rays are not dangerous at all. They are large but friendly towards humans. There are two types of manta rays in the world: reef manta rays and oceanic manta rays.

The manta rays at Manta Point on Nusa Penida are of the reef manta ray species (Manta alfredi). This species is the second largest, averaging 3 to 3.5 meters in length. These beautiful creatures are filter feeders, meaning they consume plankton, crustaceans, and small fish. Although they have long tails, they are not venomous, making it very safe to swim with them. Besides their long tails, manta rays have the largest brains among bony fish. They are very interactive with humans, making Manta Point on Nusa Penida the best spot for snorkelers and divers to capture close encounters with these majestic creatures.

Just a reminder: when we say 'interact,' we don't mean actually touching these wild animals! The main reason is to protect the manta rays' skin—they are covered in a layer of mucus that helps shield their skin from environmental damage. If touched by tourists, this mucus layer can be removed, increasing the risk of skin infections for the manta rays. Although manta rays are gentle, they are still wild animals, and touching them might startle them or cause them to instinctively react in a way that could harm those nearby. Most manta ray diving locations around the world have strict rules against touching the fish, and tourists should adhere to these regulations.

If you want to encounter manta rays, the recommended months are **between April and November**, especially during the dry season (April to October) when the water is clear, currents are stable, and visibility is at its best, making it the ideal time for snorkeling and diving.

**August to October** is the peak season for encountering manta rays, as the currents bring abundant plankton, attracting large numbers of manta rays to feed. **Manta Point** and **Manta Bay** on **Nusa Penida Island** in southern Bali are popular spots for observing these majestic creatures.

Please note: The appearance of manta rays is influenced by weather and ocean currents, so sightings cannot be guaranteed 100%.

## Toyapakeh Wall Point

Toyapakeh Wall Point is essentially a cliffside located just below the Adiwana Warnakali Dive Center.

This dive site features a small coral plateau at a depth of 4 to 6 meters, making it an excellent spot for snorkeling. Beyond the coral plateau, the site is divided into two layers: the first layer is about 12 to 15 meters deep, while the second layer reaches depths of around 40 meters. This site is highly popular due to its crystal-clear waters and thriving coral reefs.
