## Can You Snorkel Anywhere in Bali?

Although Bali's main island is surrounded by coastline, there are actually few spots suitable for snorkeling or diving. Some of the most famous dive spots like Blue Lagoon, Amed, and Tulamben are located in the more remote eastern part, requiring a two to three-hour drive from the airport. Below is Xiao Jie's introduction to snorkeling in Amed:

https://youtu.be/qdS8Td7QPYY?si=J-jy5ieS7-eAsl0E

To reach suitable snorkeling or diving spots more quickly, we recommend taking a car from the airport to Sanur, staying there for a night or two, and then taking a fast boat early in the morning for about 30 minutes to Nusa Penida. This is a more convenient option that avoids long travel times.

## Crystal Bay

![Crystal Bay](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_233401d4c81f691e18d2a6149a0d9830.jpg)

Crystal Bay

Crystal Bay is one of the popular attractions on Nusa Penida, a secluded bay on the west coast of the island with a beach stretching about 200 meters. The beach is accessible by road. Besides its charming palm-fringed sandy beach, this bay is most famous as Nusa Penida's premier snorkeling and diving destination. The water here is crystal clear (hence the name Crystal Bay). Divers praise the excellent underwater visibility, which can sometimes reach up to 30 meters.

In addition to seeing a wide variety of colorful corals and tropical fish, Crystal Bay is one of the few places in the world where you can observe mola-mola (also known as ocean sunfish) up close. These massive migratory fish can weigh up to 2000 kilograms and reach lengths of up to 3 meters. Diving here between July and October offers a chance to encounter and swim alongside them.

## Gamat Bay

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774881880928-gamatbay1.jpeg)

Gamat Bay

Gamat Bay and Crystal Bay are hailed as the two best snorkeling spots on Nusa Penida. The shallow waters here are crystal clear, making it ideal for snorkeling and observing coral reef ecosystems.

At Gamat Bay, you can see various hard corals such as staghorn coral, flower coral, and large brain coral. Many algae like anemones and sea fans also thrive here. Small tropical fish like butterflyfish, snappers, and surgeonfish dart among the coral reefs. Occasionally, small reef sharks can also be spotted.

Another highlight is the sea turtles. Different species of sea turtles, such as black turtles and green sea turtles, are often observed in the shallow waters near the shore. From June to October, during the turtle nesting season, you might even witness mother turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs.

Due to its proximity to the coast and calm waters, Gamat Bay is perfect for children and beginners to enjoy snorkeling. Remember to wear your life jacket, goggles, and fins, and you can safely explore the magical underwater world of this marine garden.

## Manta Point

![Manta Point](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_20761005e11f28ee9b74adf674bab312.jpg)

Manta Point

Manta Point is one of the most distinctive attractions on Nusa Penida Island. At this diving spot, you can get up close and personal with the giant manta rays (Manta Ray), offering a truly unique and breathtaking experience.

Commonly known as 'devil fish,' the giant manta ray can grow up to 7 meters in length and weigh around 3,000 kilograms. Isn't that huge? With such size and weight, it's natural to feel apprehensive about approaching them, but don't worry—despite their enormous size, manta rays are not dangerous at all. They are large but friendly towards humans. There are two types of manta rays in the world: the reef manta ray and the oceanic manta ray.

The manta rays at Manta Point on Nusa Penida Island are of the reef manta ray type (Manta alfredi). This species is the second largest, averaging 3 to 3.5 meters in length. These beautiful creatures are filter feeders, meaning they consume plankton, crustaceans, and small fish. Although they have long tails, they are not venomous, making it very safe to swim with them. Compared to other bony fish, manta rays have the largest brains. They are highly interactive with humans, making Manta Point on Nusa Penida Island the best spot for snorkelers and divers to capture close encounters with these majestic creatures.

A friendly reminder: when we say 'interaction,' we don't mean actually touching these wild creatures! The main reason is to protect the manta rays' skin—they are covered in a layer of mucus that helps shield their skin from environmental harm. If tourists touch them, this mucus layer can be removed, increasing the risk of skin infections. Although manta rays are gentle, they are still wild animals, and unauthorized touching might startle them or lead to instinctive reactions that could harm people. Most manta ray diving locations worldwide have strict rules against touching the fish, and visitors should adhere to these regulations.

If you want to encounter manta rays, the recommended months are **April to November**, especially during the dry season (April to October), when the sea is clear, currents are stable, and visibility is at its best, making it the ideal time for snorkeling and diving.

Among these months, **August to October** is the peak period for encountering manta rays, as the currents bring abundant plankton, attracting large numbers of manta rays to feed. In southern Bali, **Manta Point** and **Manta Bay** on **Nusa Penida Island** are popular spots for observing these magnificent creatures.

Please note: The appearance of manta rays is influenced by weather and ocean currents, so sightings cannot be guaranteed 100%.

## Toyapakeh Wall Point

Toyapakeh Wall Point is actually a cliff located just below the Adiwana Warnakali Dive Center.

This dive site features a small coral plateau 4 to 6 meters deep, which is the best spot for snorkeling. Beyond the coral plateau, it divides into two layers. The first layer is about 12 to 15 meters deep, and the second layer is around 40 meters deep. This is a very popular dive site due to its clear waters and abundant coral.
