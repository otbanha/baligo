**Key Highlights**: This is a tested six-day itinerary centered around **Ubud as a base**—the first night is spent at Komaneka at Rasa Sayang (conveniently located along Monkey Forest Road), with dinner at the Balinese-style Cafe Wayan. The entire trip uses a private driver, covering Ubud's attractions, dining, and accommodations in a complete arrangement, ideal for families seeking a slow-paced, immersive Ubud experience.

Our Bali trip began on August 13, as our family flew from Singapore to this captivating island with great anticipation. The plane landed punctually at 6:45 PM, and we immediately felt Bali's warm embrace. After disembarking, we took a private car to Ubud, ready to kick off our vacation.

## August 13: Arrival in Ubud, Enjoying Food and Accommodation

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3590d59e09bb1e649fce6f5a8026fef.jpg)

Oscar Lin

For our first evening, we chose **Cafe Wayan** in Ubud for dinner. This restaurant exudes a strong Balinese vibe, with a beautiful setting steeped in local tradition. We ordered a steak, and it was truly delicious—tender and juicy. However, the service was a bit slow, likely because we arrived late, but overall, the dining experience was satisfying.

After dinner, we returned to our accommodation for the night—**Komaneka at Rasa Sayang**. This hotel is as stunning as online reviews suggest, with both the room design and overall atmosphere offering great comfort. Its location is also very convenient, right along Monkey Forest Road, surrounded by attractions and restaurants, making it perfect for sightseeing and relaxation.

## August 14: Exploring Ubud and Nearby Attractions

On the second day, we set off at 9:30 AM with a private driver for a full day of exploration. Our first stop was **Ritatkala Cafe**, a coffee shop near the volcano with absolutely stunning views—you can see the entire volcanic landscape from here. However, the food was just average, nothing particularly impressive, but the atmosphere and visual experience more than made up for it.

__VID0__

Photo by Oscar Lin

Next, we visited a **coffee plantation** recommended by our driver. The environment was incredibly relaxing, surrounded by lush greenery, and we even had the chance to take photos with civet cats, which delighted the kids. What made it even better was that our driver didn't push any sales, leaving us feeling completely at ease.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. The crowd wasn't as large as we expected, perhaps because we arrived early. We asked our driver to guide us through the full purification ritual, which was solemn and sacred. He was very attentive, recording videos and taking photos for us, creating precious memories.

Our last stop was **Tis Cafe**. The food here was still average, but the place had no minimum spend and a very relaxed vibe. However, to see the rice terraces, you have to climb a bit, and you only get a glimpse of a small section—so if you're expecting a grand panoramic view, you might be a bit disappointed.

On the morning of the third day, we leisurely strolled around **Ubud town center**, where there are plenty of charming little shops and markets to pick up local handicrafts and souvenirs.

In the afternoon, we hired a driver again to head to **Uluwatu** (Pura Luhur Uluwatu) to catch the famous fire dance performance. The drive from Ubud took about three hours, but our driver was steady and reassuring. Upon arrival, the cliffside temple was absolutely breathtaking—the stunning scenery paired with the fire dance made the long journey feel completely worthwhile.

## August 16: Nightlife at Finns Club

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_584ebd67376cd348c0cb9464f6f6e987.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the fourth day, we arrived at **Finns Club**, a beach-themed club. We booked seats in advance, and in the evening there were fire dance performances and a fireworks show. The whole vibe felt like a massive outdoor nightclub—very lively. However, personally, if you just want to soak in the atmosphere, there's no need to book ahead; just walk in around dusk. That way, you have more flexibility and don't have to spend as much.

## August 17: The Twilight Beauty of Oneeighty Dayclub and Rock Bar

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f862c21bb5cb84ebefb16ef3d47bab26.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the last day, we hired a driver again for a full day of sightseeing. Our first stop was **Oneeighty Dayclub**, an upscale club perched on a cliff. The environment here was truly relaxing, the food was delicious, and they strictly control the number of guests, so it never felt crowded. Compared to Finns Club, I preferred the vibe here—it was an overall delightful experience.

https://youtu.be/Czw4ALduV14

Afterward, we headed to **Rock Bar**, located on Bali's southwestern coast. The sunset views there were truly unforgettable, especially after dark when the lights came on, making the bar's atmosphere even more enchanting. We ordered a few drinks and slowly savored the scenery—it was definitely the most romantic moment of our Bali trip.
