**Key Highlights**: This is a tested six-day itinerary, with **Ubud as the base**—the first night is spent at Komaneka at Rasa Sayang (conveniently located on Monkey Forest Road), with dinner at the Balinese-style Cafe Wayan. The entire trip uses a private car for transportation, covering Ubud's attractions, food, and accommodation in a complete arrangement, ideal for families looking to capture the rhythm of a deep, slow-paced Ubud exploration.

Our Bali trip began on August 13th, as our family flew from Singapore to this captivating island with high hopes. The plane landed right on time at 6:45 PM, and we immediately felt Bali's warm embrace. After disembarking, we took a private car to Ubud, ready to kick off our vacation.

## August 13: Arrival in Ubud, Enjoying Food and Accommodation

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3590d59e09bb1e649fce6f5a8026fef.jpg)

Oscar Lin provided

For our first night, we chose **Cafe Wayan** in Ubud for dinner. This restaurant has a strong Balinese vibe, with a beautiful setting full of local tradition. We ordered a steak, and it was truly delicious—tender and juicy. However, the service was a bit slow, probably because we arrived late, but overall, the dining experience was satisfying.

After dinner, we returned to our accommodation for the night—**Komaneka at Rasa Sayang** hotel. This hotel is as beautiful as online reviews say, with both the room design and overall atmosphere making us feel very comfortable. Its location is also very convenient, right on Monkey Forest Road, with plenty of attractions and restaurants nearby, perfect for sightseeing and relaxation.

## August 14: Exploring Ubud and Nearby Attractions

On the second day, we set off at 9:30 AM with a private driver for a full day of exploration. Our first stop was **Ritatkala Cafe**, a coffee shop near the volcano with absolutely stunning views—you can see the entire volcanic landscape from here. The food, however, was just average, nothing particularly impressive, but the atmosphere and visual feast more than made up for it.

https://youtu.be/V8YAI0JqIcw

Photo by Oscar Lin

Next, we visited a **coffee plantation** recommended by our driver. The environment was incredibly relaxing, surrounded by lush greenery, and we even had a chance to take photos with civet cats, which delighted the kids. What made it even better was that our driver didn't push any sales—it felt very easygoing and stress-free.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. The crowds weren't as heavy as we expected, perhaps because we arrived early. We asked our driver to guide us through the full purification ritual, which was solemn and sacred. He was very attentive, recording videos and taking photos for us, leaving us with precious memories.

Our last stop was **Tis Cafe**. The food here was again just average, but the place had no minimum charge and a very relaxed vibe. However, to see the rice terraces, you have to walk a bit uphill, and the view only reveals a small patch of terraces—a bit disappointing for those expecting a grand panorama.

## August 15: Strolling Through Ubud Town and a Trip to Uluwatu

On the morning of the third day, we leisurely strolled around **Ubud town center**, where there are plenty of charming little shops and markets to pick up local handicrafts and souvenirs.

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_107bf410383ac8c4239e99ca81014ced.jpg)

Photo by Oscar Lin

In the afternoon, we hired a driver again to head to **Uluwatu** (Pura Luhur Uluwatu) to watch the famous fire dance performance. The drive from Ubud took about three hours, but our driver was steady and reassuring. Upon arrival, the cliffside temple was absolutely breathtaking—the stunning scenery paired with the fire dance made the long journey feel completely worthwhile.

> Editor's note: What's the fire dance all about? Check out: [Kecak Fire Dance – A Must-See Spectacular Show in Bali (with Booking Info)](/en/blog/kecak-fire-dance-bali/)

In the evening, we headed to **Bali Collection** for dinner. This large shopping mall features a variety of restaurants and shops, making it a perfect spot for a family meal and some shopping.

https://youtu.be/3AqPucmnSLE

After wrapping up the day, we checked into **Ulu Segara Luxury Suites & Villas**. Though the hotel is a bit dated, the pool and beach views were stunning, and the rooms were surprisingly spacious, giving us a comfortable night's rest.

## August 16: Nightlife at Finns Club

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_584ebd67376cd348c0cb9464f6f6e987.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the fourth day, we headed to **Finns Club**, a beach-themed club. We booked seats in advance, and in the evening there were fire dance performances and a fireworks show. The whole vibe felt like a massive outdoor nightclub—very lively. That said, personally, if you just want to soak in the atmosphere, there's no need to book ahead; just walk in around dusk. It's more flexible that way and saves you from spending too much.

https://youtube.com/shorts/wai2lSW3vVU

## August 17: The Twilight Beauty of Oneeighty Dayclub and Rock Bar

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f862c21bb5cb84ebefb16ef3d47bab26.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the last day, we hired a driver again for a full day of sightseeing. Our first stop was **Oneeighty Dayclub**, an upscale club perched on a cliff. The setting was incredibly relaxing, the food was delicious, and they strictly control the number of guests, so it never felt crowded. Compared to Finns Club, I preferred the atmosphere here—it was a thoroughly enjoyable experience.

https://youtu.be/Czw4ALduV14

> [Oneeighty Dayclub Introduction and Pricing](/en/blog/oneeighty-dayclub-uluwatu-pool/)

After that, we headed to **Rock Bar** on Bali's southwestern coast. The sunset views there were truly unforgettable, especially after the lights came on—the whole bar became even more enchanting. We ordered a few drinks and slowly savored the scenery. It was hands down the most romantic moment of our Bali trip.

## August 18: Farewell to Bali

On our last day, we chose to soak up the final moments by the hotel pool and on the beach. In the afternoon, we flew back to Taiwan, bringing this Bali trip full of wonderful memories to a close.
