Our Bali adventure began on August 13th, as our family flew from Singapore to this captivating island brimming with anticipation. The plane touched down promptly at 6:45 PM, and we immediately felt Bali's warm embrace. After disembarking, we took a private car to Ubud, ready to kick off our vacation.

## August 13: Arriving in Ubud, Enjoying Food and Accommodation

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3590d59e09bb1e649fce6f5a8026fef.jpg)

Oscar Lin

For our first evening, we chose **Cafe Wayan** in Ubud for dinner. This restaurant exudes a strong Balinese vibe with its beautiful setting and traditional atmosphere. We ordered a steak, and it was absolutely delicious—tender and juicy. However, the service was a bit slow, likely because we arrived late. Still, the overall dining experience was satisfying.

After dinner, we returned to our accommodation for the night—**Komaneka at Rasa Sayang**. This hotel truly lives up to its glowing online reviews, with stunning room design and a cozy ambiance. Its location is incredibly convenient, right along Monkey Forest Road, surrounded by attractions and restaurants, making it perfect for sightseeing and relaxation.

## August 14: Exploring Ubud and Surrounding Attractions

On the second day, we set off at 9:30 AM with a private driver for a full day of exploration. Our first stop was **Ritatkala Cafe**, a coffee shop near the volcano with absolutely stunning views overlooking the entire volcanic landscape. The food, however, was just average—nothing particularly impressive—but the atmosphere and visual feast more than made up for it.

https://youtu.be/V8YAI0JqIcw

Photo by Oscar Lin

Next, we headed to a **coffee plantation** recommended by our driver. The surroundings were incredibly relaxing, lush with greenery, and we even had the chance to take photos with civet cats, which delighted the kids. What made it even better was that our driver didn’t push any sales, leaving us feeling completely at ease.

Afterward, we visited the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. The crowds weren’t as heavy as we expected, perhaps because we arrived early. We asked our driver to guide us through the full purification ritual, which felt solemn and sacred. He was thoughtful enough to record videos and take photos for us, capturing precious memories.

Our final stop was **Tis Cafe**. The food here was still just okay, but the lack of a minimum charge and the relaxed vibe were nice. However, to see the rice terraces, you have to climb a bit, and only a small section is visible—so if you’re expecting sweeping views, you might be a bit disappointed.

## August 15: Strolling Through Ubud Town and a Trip to Uluwatu

On the third morning, we leisurely wandered around **Ubud town**, where there are plenty of charming shops and markets to browse for local handicrafts and souvenirs.

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_107bf410383ac8c4239e99ca81014ced.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

In the afternoon, we hired a driver again to head to **Uluwatu** (Pura Luhur Uluwatu) to watch the famous fire dance performance. The drive from Ubud took about three hours, but our driver was steady and reassuring. Upon arrival, the cliffside temple was absolutely breathtaking—the stunning scenery paired with the fire dance made the long journey feel completely worthwhile.

In the evening, we headed to **Bali Collection** for dinner. This large shopping mall features a variety of restaurants and shops, making it a perfect spot for families to dine and shop together.

https://youtu.be/3AqPucmnSLE

After a full day, we checked into **Ulu Segara Luxury Suites & Villas**. Though the hotel is a bit dated, the pool and beach views were stunning, and the rooms were surprisingly spacious, giving us a comfortable night's rest.

## August 16: Nightlife at Finns Club

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_584ebd67376cd348c0cb9464f6f6e987.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the fourth day, we visited **Finns Club**, a beach-themed club. We booked seats in advance, and in the evening there were fire dance performances and a fireworks show. The whole vibe was like a massive outdoor nightclub—very lively. Personally, though, if you just want to soak in the atmosphere, there's no need to book ahead; just walk in around dusk. That way, you have more flexibility and don't have to spend as much.

https://youtube.com/shorts/wai2lSW3vVU

## August 17: The Twilight Beauty of Oneeighty Dayclub and Rock Bar

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f862c21bb5cb84ebefb16ef3d47bab26.jpg)

Oscar Lin provides

On the last day, we hired a driver again for a full day of sightseeing. Our first stop was **Oneeighty Dayclub**, an upscale club perched on a cliff. The setting was incredibly relaxing, the food was delicious, and they strictly control the number of guests, so it never felt crowded. Compared to Finns Club, I preferred the atmosphere here—it was an all-around pleasant experience.

https://youtu.be/Czw4ALduV14

> [Oneeighty Dayclub Introduction and Pricing](/blog/oneeighty-dayclub-uluwatu-pool/)

Afterward, we headed to **Rock Bar** on Bali's southwestern coast. The sunset views there were truly unforgettable, especially after dark when the lights came on—the whole bar became even more enchanting. We ordered a few drinks and slowly savored the scenery; it was definitely the most romantic moment of our Bali trip.

On our last day, we chose to spend it by the hotel pool and on the beach, savoring those final precious moments. In the afternoon, we flew back to Taiwan, concluding this Bali trip filled with wonderful memories.
