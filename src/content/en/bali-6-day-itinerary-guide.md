Our Bali journey began on August 13th as our family flew from Singapore to this captivating island with high hopes. The plane landed promptly at 6:45 PM, and we immediately felt Bali's warm atmosphere. After disembarking, we took a private car to Ubud, ready to start our vacation.

## August 13th: Arrival in Ubud, Enjoying Food and Accommodation

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3590d59e09bb1e649fce6f5a8026fef.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

For our first night, we chose to dine at **Cafe Wayan** in Ubud. This restaurant exudes a strong Balinese vibe, with a beautiful setting full of traditional charm. We ordered a steak, which was truly excellent—tender and juicy. However, the service was a bit slow, likely because we arrived late, but overall, the dining experience was satisfying.

After dinner, we returned to our accommodation for the night—**Komaneka at Rasa Sayang** hotel. Just as the online reviews suggested, the hotel was stunning. Both the room design and the overall ambiance were incredibly comfortable. Its location was also very convenient, right next to Monkey Forest Road, surrounded by numerous attractions and restaurants, making it ideal for sightseeing and relaxation.

## August 14: Exploring Ubud and Surrounding Attractions

On the second day, we set off at 9:30 AM with a private car for a full-day journey. Our first stop was **Ritatkala Cafe**, a coffee shop located near the volcano, offering breathtaking views of the entire volcanic landscape. However, the food was just average, nothing particularly impressive, but the ambiance and visual experience more than made up for it.

Provided by Oscar Lin

Next, we visited the **coffee plantation** recommended by our driver. The environment was very relaxing, surrounded by lush greenery, and we even had the chance to take photos with civet cats, which delighted the children. What made us even more satisfied was that the driver didn’t push any products on us, making the experience very comfortable.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. The crowd today wasn’t as large as we had anticipated, perhaps because we arrived early. We specifically asked the driver to guide us through the entire blessing ritual, which was very solemn and sacred. The driver was also very attentive, helping us record videos and take photos, leaving us with precious memories.

Our final stop was **Tis Cafe**. The food here was still average, but the advantage was that there was no minimum charge, and the atmosphere was very relaxed. However, to see the rice terraces, we had to climb a bit, and only a small section of the terraces was visible. For those expecting a grand view of the terraces, it might be a bit disappointing.

## August 15: Strolling Through Ubud City and Uluwatu Adventure

## 8月15日：烏布市區漫遊與烏魯瓦圖之旅

On the morning of the third day, we leisurely strolled around **Ubud**, where there are many interesting small shops and markets to purchase local handicrafts and souvenirs.

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_107bf410383ac8c4239e99ca81014ced.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

In the afternoon, we hired a car again to head to **Uluwatu** (Pura Luhur Uluwatu) to watch the famous fire dance performance. The journey from Ubud took about three hours, and the driver drove steadily, making us feel very secure. Upon arrival, the cliffside temple was truly breathtaking, and the spectacular scenery combined with the fire dance performance made the long trip well worth it.

In the evening, we went to **Bali Collection** for dinner. This is a large shopping center with multiple restaurants and shops, perfect for a family outing.

After finishing the day's activities, we checked into **Ulu Segara Luxury Suites & Villas**. Although the hotel is a bit old, the pool and beach views are stunning, and the room was surprisingly spacious, ensuring a comfortable night.

## August 16: Nightlife at Finns Club

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_584ebd67376cd348c0cb9464f6f6e987.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the fourth day, we visited **Finns Club**, a beach-themed club. We had reserved seats in advance, and in the evening, there were fire dance performances and fireworks shows. The atmosphere felt like a massive outdoor nightclub, buzzing with energy. Personally, I think if you just want to soak in the vibe, you don’t need to book seats ahead of time. Simply walking in during the evening is more flexible and saves you money.

## August 17: The Twilight Beauty of Oneeighty Dayclub and Rock Bar

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f862c21bb5cb84ebefb16ef3d47bab26.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On our final day, we hired a car for a full day of exploration. Our first stop was **Oneeighty Dayclub**, a luxurious club perched on a cliff. The environment here was truly relaxing, the food was delicious, and they strictly controlled the number of guests, so it never felt crowded. Compared to Finns Club, I preferred the vibe here—it was an overall delightful experience.

> [Introduction to Oneeighty Dayclub and Pricing](/blog/2023-12-07-6571846ffd89780001fe97ee/)

Afterward, we headed to **Rock Bar** on Bali’s southwest coast. The sunset views here were unforgettable, especially when the bar lit up at night, creating an even more enchanting atmosphere. We ordered a few drinks and slowly enjoyed the stunning scenery—it was undoubtedly the most romantic moment of our Bali trip.

On our last day, we spent time by the hotel's pool and beach, savoring these final moments of bliss. In the afternoon, we flew back to Taiwan, concluding this memorable Bali journey.
