**Key Highlights**: This is a tested six-day itinerary, with **Ubud as the base**—the first night is spent at Komaneka at Rasa Sayang (conveniently located on Monkey Forest Road), with dinner at the Balinese-style Cafe Wayan. The entire trip uses a private car for transportation, covering Ubud's attractions, food, and accommodation in a complete arrangement, ideal for families looking to capture the rhythm of a deep, slow-paced Ubud exploration.

Our Bali trip began on August 13, as our family flew from Singapore to this captivating island with great anticipation. The plane landed punctually at 6:45 PM, and we immediately felt Bali's warm and welcoming atmosphere. After disembarking, we took a private car to Ubud, ready to kick off our vacation.

## August 13: Arrival in Ubud, Enjoying Food and Accommodation

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3590d59e09bb1e649fce6f5a8026fef.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

For our first night, we chose **Cafe Wayan** in Ubud for dinner. This restaurant exudes a strong Balinese vibe, with a beautiful setting steeped in local tradition. We ordered a steak, and it was truly excellent—tender and juicy. However, the service was a bit slow, likely because we arrived late, but overall, the dining experience was satisfying.

After dinner, we returned to our accommodation for the night—**Komaneka at Rasa Sayang** hotel. This hotel is as stunning as online reviews suggest, with both the room design and overall atmosphere offering great comfort. Its location is also very convenient, right on Monkey Forest Road, surrounded by many attractions and restaurants, making it perfect for sightseeing and relaxation.

## August 14: Exploring Ubud and Nearby Attractions

On the second day, we set off at 9:30 AM with a private driver for a full day of exploration. Our first stop was **Ritatkala Cafe**, a coffee shop near the volcano with absolutely stunning views overlooking the entire volcanic landscape. The food, however, was just average—nothing particularly impressive—but the atmosphere and visual experience more than made up for it.

https://youtu.be/V8YAI0JqIcw

Photo by Oscar Lin

Next, we visited a **coffee plantation** recommended by our driver. The surroundings were incredibly relaxing, lush with greenery, and we even had the chance to take photos with civet cats, which delighted the kids. What made it even better was that our driver didn't push any sales, leaving us feeling completely at ease.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. The crowds weren't as heavy as we expected, perhaps because we arrived early. We asked our driver to guide us through the full purification ritual, which felt solemn and sacred. He was thoughtful enough to record videos and take photos for us, capturing precious memories.

Our final stop was **Tis Cafe**. The food here was again just average, but the plus side was no minimum charge and a very relaxed atmosphere. However, to see the rice terraces, you need to walk a bit uphill, and the view only reveals a small patch of terraced fields—those expecting a grand panorama might find it a bit disappointing.

## August 15: A Leisurely Stroll Through Ubud and a Trip to Uluwatu

On the morning of the third day, we leisurely strolled around **Ubud town center**, where there are plenty of charming little shops and markets to pick up local handicrafts and souvenirs.

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_107bf410383ac8c4239e99ca81014ced.jpg)

Photo by Oscar Lin

In the afternoon, we hired a driver again to head to **Uluwatu** (Pura Luhur Uluwatu) to catch the famous fire dance performance. The drive from Ubud took about three hours, but our driver was steady and reassuring. Upon arrival, the cliffside temple was absolutely breathtaking—the stunning scenery paired with the fire dance made the long journey feel completely worthwhile.

In the evening, we headed to **Bali Collection** for dinner. This large shopping mall houses a variety of restaurants and shops, making it a great spot for a family meal and some shopping.

https://youtu.be/3AqPucmnSLE

After a full day, we checked into **Ulu Segara Luxury Suites & Villas**. Though the hotel is a bit dated, the pool and beach views were gorgeous, and the room was surprisingly spacious, giving us a comfortable night's rest.

## August 16: Nightlife at Finns Club

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_584ebd67376cd348c0cb9464f6f6e987.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the fourth day, we headed to **Finns Club**, a beach-themed club. We booked seats in advance, and in the evening there were fire dance performances and a fireworks show, creating an atmosphere like a large outdoor nightclub—very lively. However, personally, if you just want to soak in the vibe, there's no need to book ahead; simply walk in around dusk. That way, you have more flexibility and don't have to spend as much.

## August 17: The Twilight Beauty of Oneeighty Dayclub and Rock Bar

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f862c21bb5cb84ebefb16ef3d47bab26.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the last day, we hired a driver again for a full day of exploration. Our first stop was **Oneeighty Dayclub**, an upscale club perched on a cliff. The setting here is truly relaxing, the food is delicious, and they strictly control the number of guests, so it never feels crowded. Compared to Finns Club, I preferred the atmosphere here—it was an overall delightful experience.

https://youtu.be/Czw4ALduV14

> [Oneeighty Dayclub Introduction and Pricing](/en/blog/oneeighty-dayclub-uluwatu-pool/)

Afterward, we visited **Rock Bar**, located on Bali's southwestern coast. The sunset views here are truly unforgettable, especially after dark when the lights come on, making the bar's ambiance even more enchanting. We ordered a few drinks and slowly savored the scenery—it was undoubtedly the most romantic moment of our Bali trip.

On our last day, we chose to soak up the final moments by the hotel pool and on the beach. In the afternoon, we flew back to Taiwan, ending this Bali trip filled with wonderful memories.
