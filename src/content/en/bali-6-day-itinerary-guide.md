Our Bali adventure began on August 13th, as our family flew from Singapore to this captivating island with high hopes. The plane landed promptly at 6:45 PM, and we immediately felt Bali's warm atmosphere. After disembarking, we took a private car to Ubud, ready to start our vacation.

## August 13th: Arrival in Ubud, Enjoying Food and Accommodation

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3590d59e09bb1e649fce6f5a8026fef.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

For our first night, we chose to dine at **Cafe Wayan** in Ubud. This restaurant exudes a strong Balinese vibe, with a beautiful setting full of traditional charm. We ordered a steak, which was truly excellent—tender and juicy. However, the service was a bit slow, likely because we arrived late, but overall, the dining experience was satisfying.

After dinner, we returned to our accommodation for the night—**Komaneka at Rasa Sayang**. This hotel lived up to its online reviews, offering a beautiful design and a comfortable atmosphere. Its location is also very convenient, situated near Monkey Forest Road, with plenty of attractions and restaurants nearby, making it ideal for sightseeing and relaxation.

## August 14: Exploring Ubud and Surrounding Attractions

On the second day, we set off at 9:30 AM, starting our full-day journey with a private car. Our first stop was **Ritatkala Cafe**, a coffee shop located near the volcano. The scenery here is absolutely breathtaking, offering a panoramic view of the entire volcanic landscape. However, the food was just average, nothing particularly outstanding, but the ambiance and visual experience more than made up for it.

Provided by Oscar Lin

Next, we visited the **coffee plantation** recommended by our driver. The environment here was very relaxing, surrounded by lush greenery. We even had the chance to take photos with civet cats, which delighted the children. What made us even more satisfied was that the driver didn’t push any products on us, making the experience very comfortable.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. The crowd wasn’t as large as we had expected, perhaps because we arrived early. We specifically asked the driver to guide us through the entire blessing ceremony, which was very solemn and sacred. The driver was also very attentive, helping us record videos and take photos, leaving us with precious memories.

Our final stop was **Tis Cafe**. The food here was still just average, but the relaxed atmosphere made up for it. However, to enjoy the view of the rice terraces, you need to climb a bit, and only a small section of the terraces is visible. For those expecting a grand view of the terraces, it might be a bit disappointing.

## August 15: Strolling Through Ubud City and Exploring Uluwatu

## 8月15日：烏布市區漫遊與烏魯瓦圖之旅

On the morning of the third day, we leisurely strolled around **Ubud**, where there are many interesting small shops and markets offering local handicrafts and souvenirs.

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_107bf410383ac8c4239e99ca81014ced.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

In the afternoon, we once again hired a car to visit **Uluwatu** (Pura Luhur Uluwatu) to watch the famous fire dance performance. The journey from Ubud took about three hours, and the driver ensured a smooth ride, making us feel very secure. Upon arrival, the cliffside temple was truly breathtaking, and the spectacular view combined with the fire dance made the long trip well worth it.

> Editor's Note: What is the fire dance about? Check out: [Kecak Fire Dance - A Must-See Spectacular Performance in Bali (with Ticket Information)](/blog/kecak-fire-dance-bali/)

In the evening, we headed to **Bali Collection** for dinner. This large shopping complex houses numerous restaurants and shops, making it an ideal spot for families to dine and shop.

After concluding the day's activities, we checked into **Ulu Segara Luxury Suites & Villas**. Although the hotel is somewhat dated, the pool and beach views are stunning, and the spacious rooms were a pleasant surprise, ensuring a comfortable night's stay.

## August 16: Nightlife at Finns Club

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_584ebd67376cd348c0cb9464f6f6e987.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the fourth day, we visited **Finns Club**, a beach-themed club. We had reserved seats in advance, and in the evening, there were fire dance performances and fireworks shows. The atmosphere felt like a massive outdoor nightclub, incredibly lively. However, I personally think that if you just want to experience the vibe here, you don’t need to book seats ahead of time. Simply walk in during the evening, which offers more flexibility and saves money.

## August 17: The Sunset Beauty of Oneeighty Dayclub and Rock Bar

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f862c21bb5cb84ebefb16ef3d47bab26.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On our final day, we embarked on a full-day tour by private car. Our first stop was **Oneeighty Dayclub**, a luxurious club perched on a cliff. The ambiance here was truly relaxing, the food was delicious, and they strictly controlled the number of guests, so it never felt crowded. Compared to Finns Club, I preferred the vibe here—it was an overall delightful experience.

> [Introduction and Costs at Oneeighty Dayclub](/blog/oneeighty-dayclub-uluwatu-pool/)

Later, we headed to **Rock Bar**, located on Bali’s southwestern coast. The sunset views here were unforgettable, especially when the lights came on in the evening, enhancing the bar’s enchanting atmosphere. We ordered a few drinks and slowly savored the stunning scenery—it was undoubtedly the most romantic moment of our Bali trip.
