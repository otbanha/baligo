Our Bali journey began on August 13th, as our family flew from Singapore to this captivating island with high expectations. The plane landed promptly at 6:45 PM, and we immediately felt Bali's warm atmosphere. After disembarking, we took a private car to Ubud, ready to embark on our vacation.

## August 13th: Arrival in Ubud, Enjoying Food and Accommodation

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3590d59e09bb1e649fce6f5a8026fef.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

For our first evening, we chose to dine at **Cafe Wayan** in Ubud. This restaurant exudes a strong Balinese vibe, with a beautiful setting filled with traditional charm. We ordered a steak, which was truly excellent—tender and juicy. However, the service was a bit slow, likely because we arrived late, but overall, the dining experience was satisfying.

After dinner, we returned to our accommodation for the night—**Komaneka at Rasa Sayang**. As the online reviews suggested, the hotel was indeed beautiful, with room designs and overall ambiance that made us feel very comfortable. Its location was also very convenient, right next to Monkey Forest Road, surrounded by numerous attractions and restaurants, making it ideal for sightseeing and relaxation.

## August 14: Exploring Ubud and Surrounding Attractions

On the second day, we set off at 9:30 AM with a private car for a full-day journey. Our first stop was **Ritatkala Cafe**, a coffee shop located near a volcano with absolutely stunning views. From here, you can gaze at the entire volcanic landscape. However, the food was just average, nothing particularly impressive, but the ambiance and visual experience more than made up for it.

Provided by Oscar Lin

Next, we visited a **coffee plantation** recommended by our driver. The surroundings were incredibly relaxing, lush and green, and we even had the chance to take photos with civet cats, which delighted the kids. What made it even better was that our driver didn’t pressure us into buying anything, which made the experience feel very comfortable.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. The crowd wasn’t as large as we had expected, perhaps because we arrived early. We specifically asked our driver to guide us through the entire blessing ritual, which was very solemn and sacred. Our driver thoughtfully recorded videos and took photos for us, leaving us with precious memories.

Our final stop was **Tis Cafe**. The food here was still just average, but the relaxed atmosphere made up for it. However, to see the rice terraces, you have to climb a bit, and only a small section is visible. For those expecting a grand view of the terraces, it might be a bit disappointing.

## August 15: Strolling Through Ubud Town and Visiting Uluwatu

On the morning of the third day, we leisurely strolled through the **Ubud city center**, where there are many interesting small shops and markets offering local handicrafts and souvenirs.

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_107bf410383ac8c4239e99ca81014ced.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

In the afternoon, we hired a car again to head to **Uluwatu** (Pura Luhur Uluwatu) to watch the famous fire dance performance. The journey from Ubud took about three hours, and the driver was very steady, making us feel safe throughout the trip. Upon arrival, the cliffside temple was truly breathtaking, and the spectacular view combined with the fire dance performance made the long journey well worth it.

> Editor's note: What is the fire dance performance about? Check out: [Kecak Fire Dance Show - A Must-See Spectacle in Bali (with Ticket Information)](/blog/kecak-fire-dance-bali/)

In the evening, we went to **Bali Collection** for dinner. This is a large shopping center with a variety of restaurants and shops, perfect for families looking to dine and shop.

After a full day of activities, we checked into **Ulu Segara Luxury Suites & Villas**. Although the hotel is a bit dated, the pool and beach views are stunning, and the room was surprisingly spacious, providing us with a comfortable night's stay.

## August 16: Nightlife at Finns Club

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_584ebd67376cd348c0cb9464f6f6e987.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On the fourth day, we arrived at **Finns Club**, a beach-themed club. We had booked seats in advance, and the evening featured fire dance performances and fireworks, creating an atmosphere akin to a massive outdoor nightclub, buzzing with energy. Personally, I think if you just want to soak in the vibe, there’s no need to book seats ahead of time—just walk in during the evening for more flexibility and less expense.

## August 17: The Sunset Beauty of Oneeighty Dayclub and Rock Bar

![Oscar Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f862c21bb5cb84ebefb16ef3d47bab26.jpg)

Provided by Oscar Lin

On our final day, we hired a car for a full day of exploration. Our first stop was **Oneeighty Dayclub**, a luxurious club perched on a cliff. The ambiance here was truly relaxing, the food was delicious, and they strictly controlled the number of guests, so it never felt crowded. Compared to Finns Club, I preferred the vibe here—it was an overall delightful experience.

> [Introduction and Pricing for Oneeighty Dayclub](/blog/oneeighty-dayclub-uluwatu-pool/)

Later, we headed to **Rock Bar** on Bali’s southwest coast. The sunset views here were unforgettable, especially when the lights came on in the evening, enhancing the bar’s enchanting atmosphere. We ordered a few drinks and slowly savored the stunning scenery—it was undoubtedly the most romantic moment of our Bali trip.
