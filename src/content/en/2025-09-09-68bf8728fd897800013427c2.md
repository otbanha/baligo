## **Many are asking: Do I need to print?**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1ff5703b8036ffd6acb543380c8cc3dc.png)

Essential documents for traveling to Bali include:

- **Electronic Visa (VOA / e-VOA)**
- **All Indonesia Arrival Card (Customs Declaration/Health Declaration)**
- **Bali Tourist Tax Certificate (QR Code)**

Official regulations state that **digital versions of these documents are sufficient**, and travelers can show them on their phones for entry.

However, many travelers have recently noticed that 👉 **immigration police are conducting random checks across Bali**, especially around tourist attractions, roads, and hotel areas. If you can't provide the necessary documents on the spot, you may face delays or even fines.

---

## **Conclusion: No need to print, but better safe than sorry!**

While printed copies aren't mandatory, **having backup prints can give you peace of mind during your trip**.

### **Why print?**

1. **Phone out of battery or no internet** → Paper copies can be presented anytime.
2. **Immigration police random checks** → Paper copies are much faster than scrolling through phone screenshots.
3. **Hotel or local service requirements** → Some places still prefer to see paper documents.

👉 Recommendation:

- Print **one A4 black-and-white copy each of your e-Visa, tourism tax receipt, and Arrival Card QR Code**.
- Carry them with you to avoid any hassles during your trip.

## **Latest Entry Process Overview**

### **1. Visa (VOA / e-VOA)**

- VOA: Apply on arrival at the airport
- e-VOA: Apply in advance at [evisa.imigrasi.go.id](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id)
- Fee: 500,000 IDR (approx. USD$32)

### **2. All Indonesia Arrival Card**

- Replaces the previous health declaration, customs form, and quarantine form
- **Free**, to be filled within 72 hours before arrival
- Upon submission, receive a **QR Code** (via Email / App)

### **3. Bali Tourism Levy**

- 150,000 IDR per foreign tourist (approx. USD$10)
- Platform: [lovebali.baliprov.go.id](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id) or **Love Bali App**
- Upon completion, receive an electronic receipt

**4. Documents to Present Upon Arrival**

- Passport, Visa (VOA / e-VOA)
- All Indonesia QR Code
- Tourism Tax Receipt

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

### **1. Can I Show Entry Documents on My Phone?**

Technically yes, but having printed copies can be more convenient if immigration officers decide to inspect your documents.

### **2. Will Immigration Officers Actually Inspect My Documents?**

Yes, currently in many tourist spots, roads, or tourist areas in Bali, immigration officers may randomly request to check your documents.

### **3. Is the All Indonesia Arrival Card Free?**

Completely free, but it can only be filled out within 72 hours before arrival.

### **4. What If I Forget to Pay the Tourism Tax?**

Payment may be required upon entry or at some attractions, so it's best to complete it in advance.

### **5. Do I need to print in color?**

No, black and white is fine as long as it's clear and legible.
