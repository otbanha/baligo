According to rainfall probability charts, we can clearly see that Bali's southern region (including areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Uluwatu, Nusa Dua, and Sanur) experiences its rainy season primarily from December to February. Around January 20th, the chance of rainfall peaks at 61%, the highest point of the year. In January, there are typically about 18 rainy days per month.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8c004965cfd469d049ae8b0e5df25cfa.png)

However, don't let these numbers scare you! Bali's rainy season doesn't mean non-stop rain all day. Usually, rainfall lasts only 1-2 hours per day. After the rain clears, you'll still have plenty of time to enjoy the sunshine and beaches.

It's worth noting that while occasional heavy rainfall may cause temporary flooding, such situations are uncommon. Most of the time, rainy season showers serve as refreshing interludes, bringing some comfort to the tropical heat.

From the charts, we can also observe an interesting trend: rainfall probability shows distinct seasonal variations. It gradually increases from Bali's spring, peaks in January, and then slowly declines. This pattern provides valuable insights for travelers planning their trips.

Additionally, it's important to mention that rainfall probability varies between northern and southern Bali. Generally, the further north you go, the higher the chance of rain. So if you're planning to visit during the rainy season, choosing the southern region might be a wise decision.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_482eb8e3cecbce3092b2dd1454b68823.png)

In summary, even during the rainy season, southern Bali remains a charming travel destination. With proper preparation, bringing rain gear, or relaxing in a luxury resort or villa, you can fully enjoy the unique seasonal charm of this place. Perhaps a brief tropical shower might even add some unforgettable memories to your journey!
