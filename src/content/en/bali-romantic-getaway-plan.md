✈️ **Bali Six-Day, Five-Night Independent Travel Journal (June 22–June 27)**

This trip was a long-awaited time for just the two of us! 👫

After leaving our child with grandma, my wife and I packed our bags with barely contained excitement, ready to board our flight to Bali. For parents who haven't had a getaway in ages, this relaxing vacation felt extra precious, and our anticipation was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Tips: Three Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we followed advice from many travel experts and applied for Indonesia's **e-VOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival)** in advance. I honestly think this step is super important—it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, here's a little trap to watch out for: my wife **accidentally entered the wrong birth date** when filling out the e-VOA 😓. As a result, she had to go through the manual visa-on-arrival counter and wait in line for **an hour and a half**. So regretful!

Besides the visa, there are two other online procedures that are also important. I recommend taking care of them before boarding to avoid any last-minute hassle upon arrival:

1. **All Indonesia Arrival Card (including e-CD Customs Declaration & SSHP Health Statement)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**

All four steps are **digital documents that can be completed online**, and they're actually quite straightforward—just have your passport copy, flight details, and payment method ready. Once done, I recommend saving the QR codes as screenshots in your phone album; presenting them for scanning upon arrival will speed things up significantly. For a detailed walkthrough, check this out: [2026 Bali Entry Changes in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Arrival Process - From Landing, Customs, to Baggage Claim](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

Next up, it's time to kick off our Bali duo adventure~ 🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arriving in Bali, Checking into the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We caught the 9:50 AM direct EVA Air flight, scheduled to land in Bali at 3:30 PM. The flight was a bit long, but after a quick nap on the plane, my mind was already switching into vacation mode.

After arriving at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we queued for the manual visa process, and by the time we cleared customs, it was already getting dark. We hopped into our pre-booked transfer and reached **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran around 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort Is Worth Two Nights!**

This hotel was hands-down the most anticipated stop of our trip! AYANA is one of Bali's most renowned resorts, boasting top-notch facilities, stunning ocean views, and a variety of signature restaurants. The absolute must-visit here is the legendary **Rock Bar Bali**—one of the world's top ten sunset bars and a wildly popular Instagram spot.

https://youtu.be/lbDfKut2J7M

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, only catching the night view, standing at the cliffside bar area, listening to the waves and watching the lights sway, the atmosphere was incredibly romantic 💫. The food was decent but a bit pricey—what you're really paying for here isn't the meal, but that magical vibe of dancing with the sea and sky.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

We stayed in a sea-view room, costing about USD$387 per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a little tip: **the AYANA resort complex is enormous**. If you choose a more budget-friendly room, you might end up in a remote area, and walking to the main facilities will leave your legs sore. I'd recommend adding a bit to your budget and picking a room closer to the main facilities for a much better experience!

The crowd for photos was already gathering early in the morning—you have to queue, and each person gets just one minute for a quick shoot. But the good news is—you can queue up again! 📷

### 🍽 **Eat a Hearty Breakfast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the buffet restaurant near the AYANA lobby. Although it was a bit pricey at about USD$30 per person, the selection was incredibly rich—featuring Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even made-to-order omelets, bread, and desserts. It was truly generous. After eating our fill, we couldn't even manage lunch, saving us a meal's budget 😂

A little side note: we left a **$2 tip** while housekeeping was cleaning, and when we returned to the room in the afternoon, the housekeeping lady had quietly prepared a little surprise—thoughtful and heartwarming, truly capturing the spirit of AYANA's service. In contrast, last time in Chiang Mai, a friend left a $10 tip and got nothing in return—guess luck is also a kind of skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to the Highland Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After a hearty breakfast and a leisurely morning soaking in AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to kick off the "mountain chapter" of our trip 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive, All for a Cup of Coffee and Stunning Views**

Our first stop of the day was a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin — **Montana Del Café**, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region of northern Bali. The drive from AYANA in Jimbaran took a whopping **two and a half hours** one way — a true mountain-crossing journey.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was totally worth it once we arrived! Montana Del Café boasts sweeping mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether you sit indoors or on the terrace, it’s like stepping into a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two slices of cheesecake, costing around USD$16 in total — pretty reasonable for a scenic café. The real highlight? That priceless feeling of sipping coffee above the clouds ✨

If you love photography or crave fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just keep in mind the long travel time — ask your driver if you can pair it with other Ubud attractions along the way, like volcanic hot springs, to make the trip more efficient.

### 🌿 **Check-in: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After wrapping up our highland adventure, we headed back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay turned out to be one of the most delightful surprises of our trip, perfectly capturing the "relaxation + romance" vibe we were after!

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcb27348f2a4fd72f4e265beed93773.jpg)

First off, this is an adults-only resort. Without the noise of children playing, the atmosphere is exceptionally serene. The staff are warm and attentive, from the welcome drink to help with luggage—every detail shows their thoughtfulness.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modern and natural elements, with large floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking lush forests. You can even hear birds chirping, insects humming, and a babbling stream—perfect for couples seeking a romantic and peaceful getaway. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so you can soak in the tub and enjoy the greenery without stepping out. Pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Check into The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak for a Convenient and Comfortable Stay**

Leaving the tranquil Ubud highlands behind, we headed to the lively beach area—**Seminyak**. This is Bali's trendy district, packed with restaurants, bars, boutique shops, and Instagram-worthy cafes. It's super convenient and perfect for travelers who want a city-meets-beach vibe.

That night, we stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, right in the heart of Seminyak. The value for money here is incredible—around **USD$148 per night**—yet the space is spacious, amenities are complete, and it even has a private pool! Within a few minutes' walk, you'll find convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage places—no need to call a ride.

## 🎉 **Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club—Worth Splurging Like a VIP for a Day!**

Having visited Bali many times, we finally made it to the long-awaited **Finns Beach Club**! As one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs, located in Canggu, the venue stretches along the beach with multiple pools, sun loungers, bars, DJ stages, and VIP cabanas. The vibe is lively and absolutely buzzing.

https://youtu.be/MpetWBEQ2Ts

However, online reviews are really mixed—some find the **service staff indifferent and prices steep**, while many others rave about the **great atmosphere, top-notch facilities, and a must-visit experience**.

From our own experience—**if you just order the cheapest sunbed package, you'll likely be overlooked**; but this time, we splurged on a VIP sofa cabana package. While it cost a pretty penny for the whole day, the treatment we received was a world apart! ✨

💡The key to getting good service really is 'spending your money in the right places'! When you opt for a high-end cabana, the security, servers, and even dancers proactively come over to greet and interact with you, instantly elevating the vibe to something like a private party.

The group next to us was also in the cabana area, but they probably didn't order much or engage, and ended up being almost completely ignored—even the dancers walked right past without a glance 😅 So, **to truly enjoy Finns, you've got to be a lavish spender for a day** (the happy kind), and only then can you really soak in its charm!

## 🛫 **Day 6: A Bittersweet Farewell, Heading Back to Taiwan**

Our short six-day, five-night Bali trip came to an end. After checking out at noon, we took a car to Ngurah Rai Airport for our return flight to Taiwan. Although the flight was scheduled to depart at 4:30 PM, we encountered an airline scheduling delay that day, and we didn't land in Taiwan until **11:00 PM** — several hours later than expected, leaving us utterly exhausted.

But hey, that's travel — full of unexpected little twists. What matters is the memories we made, and it was all worth it! 🎒

## 📝 **Five Key Takeaways from This Bali Trip, Hope They Help:**

### ✅ **1. Get Your Online Visa on Arrival in Advance!**

I can't stress this enough — **the e-VOA online visa is super important!** It saves you a ton of time waiting in line. Especially if you're traveling with kids or on a red-eye flight, applying in advance is a sign of respect for your own energy.

### 📱 **2. Always Use Gojek or Grab for Rides**

These two local ride-hailing apps let you pay by credit card, with transparent pricing and peace of mind. We once made the mistake of hailing a motorbike on the street for convenience, and got totally ripped off — they quoted us in US dollars with a markup, acting like it was perfectly normal 😓. Don't try to save a little and end up losing big.

{{block:包車}}

### 🚦 **3. Traffic Jams Are the Norm in Bali — Don't Overpack Your Itinerary!**

Especially on the routes from Jimbaran and Seminyak to Ubud, traffic jams are common. I strongly recommend planning just 1–2 key activities per day, or you'll spend the whole trip feeling like you're constantly missing out—and it'll be exhausting.

### 💵 **4. Tipping is fine, but don't let anyone guilt you into it!**

Over 75% of Bali's population works in the service industry, so tipping moderately is a polite way to show appreciation. However, you may encounter people who try to guilt-trip you or act entitled to a tip. In such cases, **you really don't need to force yourself to tip**—it's more important to set boundaries and respect yourself.

Quick reference: [Do you need to tip in Bali? How to tip? How much to tip in various situations?](/en/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google it yourself before deciding whether to go!**

If you book a private driver or guided tour, you'll often be 'conveniently taken' to this coffee factory. These places mainly push expensive civet coffee, sometimes with high-pressure sales tactics or scripted presentations. Whether it's worth a visit—well, that's for you to decide! 🧐
