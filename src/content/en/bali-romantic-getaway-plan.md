✈️ **Bali Six-Day, Five-Night Self-Guided Trip Journal (June 22–27)**

This trip was truly a long-awaited time for just the two of us! 👫

After leaving our child with grandma, my wife and I practically packed our bags in a hurry, eager to board the flight to Bali. For parents who haven't had a break abroad in a while, this relaxing vacation felt especially precious, and our excitement was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Tips: Three Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we followed advice from many travel experts and applied for Indonesia's **e-VOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival)** in advance. Honestly, this step is super important—it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, here's a little pitfall to watch out for: my wife **accidentally entered the wrong birth date** when filling out the e-VOA 😓. As a result, she had to go through the manual visa-on-arrival counter and ended up waiting in line for **an hour and a half**—such a regret!

Besides the visa, there are two other online procedures that are also important. I recommend taking care of them before boarding to avoid any last-minute hassle upon arrival:

1. **All Indonesia Arrival Card (including e-CD Customs Declaration and SSHP Health Statement)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**

All four steps are **digital documents that can be completed online**, and they're actually quite straightforward—just make sure you have a copy of your passport, flight details, and a payment method ready. Once done, I recommend saving the QR codes as screenshots in your phone album—it'll speed things up when you just need to scan them upon arrival. For a detailed walkthrough, check this out: [2026 Bali Entry Changes in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Arrival Guide - From Landing, Customs, to Baggage Claim](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

Next up, it's time to kick off our Bali duo adventure~ 🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arriving in Bali, Checking into the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We took EVA Air's direct 9:50 AM flight, scheduled to land in Bali at 3:30 PM. The flight was a bit long, but after a quick nap on the plane, my mind was already switching into vacation mode.

After arriving at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we queued for the manual visa process, and by the time we cleared customs, it was already getting dark. We hopped into our pre-booked transfer and arrived at **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran around 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort is Worth Staying for Two Nights!**

This hotel was hands-down the highlight we were most looking forward to! AYANA is one of Bali's most renowned resorts, boasting top-notch facilities, stunning ocean views, and a variety of signature restaurants. The absolute must-visit is the legendary **Rock Bar Bali**—one of the world's top ten sunset bars and an Instagram-famous spot.

https://youtu.be/lbDfKut2J7M

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, only catching the night view, standing at the cliffside bar area, listening to the waves and watching the lights sway, the atmosphere was incredibly romantic 💫. The food was decent but on the pricier side—but honestly, you're not here for the meal; you're here for that magical vibe of dancing with the sea and sky.

We stayed in a sea-view room, which cost about USD$387 per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a little tip: **the AYANA resort complex is enormous**. If you opt for a more budget-friendly room, you might end up in a far-flung area, leaving you exhausted just walking to the main facilities. I'd suggest adding a bit to your budget and choosing a room closer to the main amenities for a much better experience!

The photo crowd was already gathering early, so we had to queue up, with a one-minute time limit per person. But the good news? You can line up again and again! 📷

### 🍽 **Pig Out at Breakfast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the buffet restaurant near the AYANA lobby. Though it was a bit pricey at about USD$30 per person, the selection was incredibly rich—featuring Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even made-to-order omelets, pastries, and desserts. It was a real feast. After eating our fill, we couldn't even think about lunch, so we just saved that meal's budget 😂

A little side note: we left a **$2 tip** while housekeeping was cleaning, and when we returned to the room in the afternoon, the housekeeping lady had quietly prepared a small surprise—thoughtful and heartwarming, truly capturing the spirit of AYANA's service. In contrast, last time in Chiang Mai, a friend left a $10 tip and got nothing in return—guess luck is a skill too 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to the Highland Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After a hearty breakfast and a leisurely morning at AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to begin the "mountain chapter" of our journey 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive, All for a Cup of Coffee and Stunning Views**

Our first stop of the day was a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin — **Montana Del Café**, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region of northern Bali. The drive from AYANA in Jimbaran took a full **two and a half hours** one way — a real mountain-crossing adventure.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was totally worth it once we arrived! Montana Del Café boasts sweeping mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether you sit indoors or on the terrace, it's like stepping into a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two slices of cheesecake, costing around USD$16 in total — quite reasonable for a scenic café. The real treasure was that "sipping coffee above the clouds" feeling — absolutely priceless ✨

If you love photography or crave fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just be mindful of the long travel time, and ask your driver if you can pair it with other Ubud attractions, like volcanic hot springs, for a more efficient route.

### 🌿 **Check-in: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After wrapping up the highland stop, we drove back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay turned out to be one of the most delightful surprises of our trip, perfectly matching our desire for a "relaxing + romantic" vacation vibe!

First of all, this is an adults-only resort. Without the noise of children playing, the atmosphere is exceptionally peaceful. The staff are warm and attentive, from the welcome drink to luggage assistance—every detail shows their thoughtfulness.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modern and natural elements, with large floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking lush green mountains. You can even hear birds chirping, insects humming, and a babbling stream—perfect for couples seeking a romantic and tranquil getaway. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so even if you don't feel like going out, you can soak in the tub surrounded by greenery. It's pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Check into The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak for a Convenient and Comfortable Stay**

After leaving the serene Ubud highlands, we headed to the lively beach area—**Seminyak**. This is Bali's trendy district, packed not only with restaurants and bars but also boutique shops and Instagram-worthy cafes. It's super convenient and perfect for travelers who want a city-resort vibe.

We checked into **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, right in the heart of Seminyak. The value for money here is incredible—around **USD$148 per night**—yet the space is spacious, fully equipped, and comes with a private pool! Within a few minutes' walk, you'll find convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage places, so you don't even need to call a ride.

## 🎉 **Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club — Worth Being a Splurger for a Day!**

Having visited Bali many times, we finally made it to the much-anticipated **Finns Beach Club** on this trip! As one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs, located in Canggu, the venue stretches along the beach with multiple pools, sun loungers, bars, DJ stages, and VIP cabanas. The vibe is lively and absolutely buzzing.

https://youtu.be/MpetWBEQ2Ts

However, online reviews are really mixed — some people complain about **cold service and high prices**, while many others rave about the **great atmosphere, excellent facilities, and a must-visit experience**.

From our own experience — **if you just order the cheapest sunbed package, you'll likely be overlooked**; but this time we splurged and upgraded to a VIP sofa cabana package. Although we spent a lot over the day, the treatment we received was completely different! ✨

💡The key to getting good service really is 'spending your money in the right places'! When you choose a high-end cabana, the security, servers, and even dancers will proactively greet and interact with you, instantly elevating the vibe to something like a private party.

The group next to us was also in the cabana area, but they probably didn't order much or engage much, and they were almost completely ignored throughout — even the dancers walked right past without a glance 😅 So, **to have a blast at Finns, you need to be a splurger for a day** (the happy kind), and only then can you truly experience its charm!

## 🛫 **Day 6: A Bittersweet Farewell, Heading Back to Taiwan**

Our short six-day, five-night Bali trip came to an end. After checking out at 12:00 PM, we took a car to Ngurah Rai Airport to fly back to Taiwan. ## 📝 **Five Key Takeaways from This Bali Trip (Hope They Help!):**

### ✅ **1. Apply for Your E-Visa on Arrival Online in Advance!**

Seriously, I can't stress this enough: **the e-VOA online visa is super important!** It saves you a ton of time waiting in line. Especially if you're traveling with kids or arriving on a red-eye flight, getting it done beforehand is a sign of respect for your own energy.

### 📱 **2. Always Use Gojek or Grab for Rides**

These two local ride-hailing apps let you pay by credit card, with transparent pricing and peace of mind. We once grabbed a motorbike taxi on the street out of convenience, and got totally ripped off — the driver quoted us in US dollars and acted like it was perfectly normal 😓. Don't try to save a little and end up losing a lot.

{{block:包車}}

### 🚦 **3. Traffic Jams Are the Norm in Bali — Don't Overpack Your Itinerary!**

The routes from Jimbaran and Seminyak to Ubud are especially prone to traffic jams. I strongly recommend planning just 1–2 key activities per day, or you'll spend the whole trip feeling like you're constantly missing things — and it'll be a real struggle.

### 💵 **4. Tipping Culture: It's Okay to Tip, But Don't Let Guilt Trip You!**

Over 75% of Bali's population works in the service industry, so tipping moderately is a polite gesture of support. However, you may encounter people who use verbal pressure or act entitled to tips. In such cases, **you really don't need to force yourself to tip**—it's more important to maintain your boundaries and self-respect.

Quick reference: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/en/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Do Your Own Google Search Before Deciding!**

If you book a private driver or guided tour, you'll often be 'conveniently taken' to this coffee factory. These places mainly push expensive civet coffee, sometimes with high-pressure sales tactics or scripted explanations. Whether it's worth a visit is entirely up to you to decide 🧐
