✈️ **Bali Six-Day, Five-Night Self-Guided Trip Journal (June 22–27)**

This trip was a long-awaited time for just the two of us! 👫

After leaving our child with grandma, my wife and I packed our bags almost impatiently, ready to board our flight to Bali. For parents who haven't had a break abroad in a while, this relaxing vacation felt especially precious, and our excitement was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Tips: Three Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we followed advice from many travel experts and applied for Indonesia's **e-VOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival)** in advance. We honestly think this step is super important—it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, here's a little pitfall to watch out for: my wife **accidentally entered the wrong birth date** when filling out the e-VOA 😓. As a result, she had to go through the manual visa-on-arrival counter and ended up waiting in line for **an hour and a half**—so regretful!

Besides the visa, there are two other online procedures that are also important. We recommend taking care of them before boarding to avoid any last-minute hassle upon arrival:

1. **All Indonesia Arrival Card (including e-CD Customs Declaration and SSHP Health Statement)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**

All four steps are **digital documents that can be completed online**, and they're actually quite straightforward—just have your passport copy, flight details, and payment method ready. Once done, I recommend saving the QR codes as screenshots in your phone album, as presenting them for scanning at immigration will speed things up significantly. For a detailed guide, check this out: [2026 Bali Entry Changes in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Arrival Process - From Landing, Customs, to Baggage Claim](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

Next up, it's time to kick off our Bali duo adventure~ 🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arriving in Bali, Checking into the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We took EVA Air's direct 9:50 AM flight, expected to land in Bali at 3:30 PM. The flight was a bit long, but after a quick nap on the plane, my mind was already switching to vacation mode.

After arriving at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we queued for the manual visa process, and by the time we cleared customs, it was already getting dark. We hopped into our pre-booked transfer and arrived at **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran around 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort Is Worth Staying Two Nights!**

This hotel was hands-down the most anticipated stop of our trip! AYANA is one of Bali's most famous resorts, boasting top-notch facilities, stunning ocean views, and a variety of unique restaurants. The absolute must-visit is the legendary **Rock Bar Bali**—one of the world's top ten sunset bars and a wildly popular Instagram-worthy spot.

https://youtu.be/lbDfKut2J7M

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, only catching the night view, standing at the cliffside bar area, listening to the waves and watching the lights sway, the atmosphere was incredibly romantic 💫. The food was decent but a bit pricey—you're not really here for the cuisine, but for that magical vibe of dancing with the sea and sky.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

We stayed in a sea-view room at around USD$387 per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a tip: **the AYANA resort is enormous**. If you choose a more budget-friendly room, you might end up in a remote area, and walking to the main facilities could leave your legs exhausted. I'd recommend adding a bit more to your budget and picking a room closer to the main facilities for a better experience!

To capture those dreamy pool photos you see on Instagram, we woke up at 7 a.m. the next day and took the resort shuttle to AYANA Resort's most iconic **H2O Infinity Pool**. The crowd for photos was already gathering early in the morning—you have to queue, and each person gets just one minute to snap away. But the good news is—you can line up again! 📷

### 🍽 **Hearty Breakfast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the buffet restaurant near the AYANA lobby. Though it was a bit pricey at around USD$30 per person, the selection was incredibly rich—featuring Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even made-to-order omelets along with bread and desserts. It was well worth it. After eating our fill, we couldn't even manage lunch, so we saved a meal's budget 😂

As a little side note, we left a **$2 tip** during housekeeping, and when we returned to the room in the afternoon, the housekeeping lady had quietly prepared a small surprise—thoughtful and heartwarming, truly capturing AYANA's service spirit. In contrast, last time in Chiang Mai, a friend left a $10 tip and got nothing in return—guess luck is also a kind of skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to the Highland Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After a hearty breakfast and a leisurely morning at AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to begin the 'mountain chapter' of our trip 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive for a Cup of Coffee and Breathtaking Views**

Our first stop of the day was a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin — **Montana Del Café**, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region of northern Bali. The drive from AYANA in Jimbaran took a whopping **two and a half hours** one way — a true journey over hills and mountains.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was totally worth it once we arrived! Montana Del Café boasts sweeping mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether you sit indoors or on the terrace, it's like stepping into a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two slices of cheesecake, costing around USD$16 in total — quite reasonable for a scenic café. The real treasure, though, was that feeling of sipping coffee above the clouds — absolutely priceless ✨

If you love photography or crave fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just keep in mind the long travel time — ask your driver if you can pair it with other Ubud attractions, like volcanic hot springs, to make the trip more efficient.

### 🌿 **Stay: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After our highland adventure, we drove back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This accommodation turned out to be one of the most delightful surprises of our trip, perfectly matching our desire for a 'relaxing + romantic' getaway vibe!

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcb27348f2a4fd72f4e265beed93773.jpg)

First of all, this is an adults-only resort. Without the noise of children playing, the atmosphere is exceptionally serene. The staff are warm and attentive, from the welcome drink to luggage assistance—every detail shows their thoughtfulness.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modern and natural elements, with large floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking lush mountain forests. You can even hear birds chirping, insects humming, and a babbling stream—perfect for couples seeking a romantic and peaceful getaway. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so you can soak in the tub and enjoy the greenery without stepping out. It's pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Check into The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak for a Convenient and Comfortable Stay**

Leaving the tranquil Ubud highlands, we headed to the lively beach area—**Seminyak**. This is Bali's trendy district, packed with restaurants, bars, boutique shops, and Instagram-worthy cafes. It's super convenient and perfect for travelers who want a city-vacation vibe.

That night, we stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, right in the heart of Seminyak. The value for money is incredible—around **USD$148 per night**—yet the space is spacious, fully equipped, and includes a private pool! Within a few minutes' walk, you'll find convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage parlors, so you don't even need to call a ride.

## 🎉 **Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club — Worth Every Penny for a Day of Luxury!**

Having visited Bali many times, we finally made it to the much-anticipated **Finns Beach Club**! As one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs, it's located in Canggu. The sprawling venue faces the ocean and features multiple pools, sun loungers, bars, a DJ stage, and VIP cabanas — the vibe is lively and absolutely electric.

https://youtu.be/MpetWBEQ2Ts

That said, online reviews are really mixed. Some people complain about **cold service and high prices**, while many others rave about the **great atmosphere, top-notch facilities, and a must-visit experience**.

From our own experience — **if you just order the cheapest sunbed package, you'll likely be overlooked**. But this time, we splurged and upgraded to a VIP sofa cabana package. It cost a pretty penny for the whole day, but the treatment we received was a world apart! ✨

💡The secret to getting great service really is **spending your money in the right places**! Once you opt for a higher-tier cabana, the security, waitstaff, and even dancers will proactively come over to greet and interact with you, instantly elevating the vibe to something like a private party.

The group next to us was also in the cabana area, but they probably didn't order much or engage much, and they were almost completely ignored the entire time — even the dancers walked right past without a glance 😅 So, **if you want to have a blast at Finns, you've got to go all out and be a happy big spender for a day** — that's the only way to truly soak in the magic here!

## 🛫 **Day 6: Bittersweet Farewells, Heading Back to Taiwan**

Our short six-day, five-night Bali trip came to an end. After checking out at noon, we took a car to Ngurah Rai Airport for our return flight to Taiwan. Although the flight was scheduled for 4:30 PM, we faced an airline scheduling delay that day, and we didn't arrive in Taiwan until **11:00 PM** — several hours later than expected, leaving us utterly exhausted.

But hey, that's travel — there are always unexpected little twists. The important thing is that we came home with a treasure trove of memories. Worth it! 🎒

## 📝 **Five Key Takeaways from This Bali Trip (Hope They Help!):**

### ✅ **1. Apply for Your E-Visa on Arrival Online in Advance!**

I can't stress this enough: **the e-VOA online visa is super important!** It can save you a ton of time waiting in line. Especially if you're traveling with kids or arriving on a red-eye flight, applying in advance is a sign of respect for your own energy.

### 📱 **2. Always Use Gojek or Grab for Rides**

These two local ride-hailing apps allow you to pay by credit card, with transparent pricing and peace of mind. We once took a shortcut and hailed a motorbike on the street, only to get completely ripped off — they quoted us in US dollars with a straight face 😓. Don't try to save a little and end up losing a lot.

{{block:包車}}

### 🚦 **3. Traffic Jams Are the Norm in Bali — Don't Overpack Your Itinerary!**

The routes from Jimbaran and Seminyak to Ubud are especially prone to traffic jams. I strongly recommend scheduling just 1-2 key activities per day; otherwise, you'll spend the whole trip 'missing out' and it'll be a real struggle.

### 💵 **4. Tipping Culture: It's Okay to Tip, But Don't Let Guilt Trip You!**

Over 75% of Bali's population works in the service industry, so tipping moderately is a polite gesture of support. However, you may encounter people who use verbal pressure or act entitled to tips. In such cases, **you really don't need to force yourself to tip**—it's more important to maintain your boundaries and self-respect.

Quick reference: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How to Tip? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/en/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google It Yourself Before Deciding!**

If you book a private driver or guided tour, you'll often be 'conveniently taken' to this coffee factory. These places mainly sell overpriced civet coffee, sometimes with high-pressure sales tactics or scripted explanations. Whether it's worth a visit is entirely up to you to decide 🧐
