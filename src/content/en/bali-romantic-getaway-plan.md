✈️ **Bali Six-Day, Five-Night Independent Travel Journal (June 22–27)**

For us, this trip was a long-awaited time for just the two of us! 👫

After leaving our child with grandma, my wife and I packed our bags almost impatiently, ready to board the flight to Bali. For parents who haven't had a break abroad in a while, this relaxing vacation felt especially precious, and our excitement was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Tips: Three Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we followed advice from many travel experts and applied for Indonesia's **e-VOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival)** in advance. We honestly think this step is super important—it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, we also want to warn you about a little pitfall—my wife **accidentally entered the wrong birth date** when filling out the e-VOA 😓. As a result, she had to go through the manual visa-on-arrival process and wait in line for **an hour and a half**. So regretful!

Besides the visa, there are two other online procedures that are also important. We recommend handling them before boarding to avoid any hassle upon arrival:

1. **All Indonesia Arrival Card (including e-CD Customs Declaration and SSHP Health Statement)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**

All four steps are **digital documents that can be completed online**, and they're actually quite straightforward—just have your passport copy, flight details, and payment method ready. Once done, I recommend saving the QR codes as screenshots in your phone album for quick scanning upon arrival. For a detailed walkthrough, check this out: [2026 Bali Entry Changes in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Arrival Guide - From Landing, Customs, to Baggage Claim](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

Next up, it's time to kick off our Bali duo adventure~ 🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arriving in Bali, Checking into the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We took EVA Air's direct 9:50 AM flight, scheduled to land in Bali at 3:30 PM. The flight was a bit long, but a quick nap on the plane, and my mind was already switching into vacation mode.

After arriving at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we queued for the manual visa process, so by the time we cleared customs, it was already getting dark. We hopped into our pre-booked transfer and reached **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran around 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort Is Worth Staying for Two Nights!**

This hotel was hands-down the most anticipated stop on our trip! AYANA is one of Bali's most renowned resorts, boasting top-notch facilities, stunning ocean views, and a variety of signature restaurants. The absolute must-visit is the legendary **Rock Bar Bali**—one of the world's top ten sunset bars and a wildly popular Instagram spot.

https://youtu.be/lbDfKut2J7M

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, only catching the night view, standing at the cliffside bar area, listening to the waves and watching the lights sway, the atmosphere was incredibly romantic 💫. The food was decent but a bit pricey—you're not really here for the meal, but for that magical vibe of dancing with the sea and sky.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

We stayed in a sea-view room, costing about USD$387 per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a little tip: **the AYANA resort complex is enormous**. If you choose a more budget-friendly room, you might end up in a remote area, and walking to the main facilities will leave your legs exhausted. I recommend adding a bit more to your budget and picking a room closer to the main facilities for a much better experience!

The crowd for photos was already gathering early, with a queue and a one-minute time limit per person—but the good news is you can line up again! 📷

### 🍽 **Hearty Breakfast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the buffet restaurant near the AYANA lobby. Although it was a bit pricey at about USD$30 per person, the selection was incredibly rich—featuring Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even made-to-order omelets, bread, and desserts. It was truly generous. After eating our fill, we couldn't even think about lunch, saving us a whole meal's budget 😂

A little side note: we left a **$2 tip** while housekeeping was cleaning, and when we returned to the room in the afternoon, the housekeeping lady had quietly prepared a small surprise—thoughtful and heartwarming, truly capturing the spirit of AYANA's service. In contrast, last time in Chiang Mai, a friend left a $10 tip and got nothing in return—guess luck is also a kind of skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to the Highland Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After a hearty breakfast and a leisurely morning soaking up AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to begin the 'mountain chapter' of our trip 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive, All for a Cup of Coffee and the View**

Our first stop of the day was a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin — **Montana Del Café**, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region of northern Bali. The drive from AYANA in Jimbaran took a whopping **two and a half hours** one way — a real mountain-crossing journey.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was totally worth it once we arrived! Montana Del Café boasts sweeping mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether you sit indoors or on the terrace, it's like stepping into a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two slices of cheesecake, totaling around USD$16 — pretty reasonable for a scenic café. The real treasure was that feeling of sipping coffee above the clouds — absolutely priceless ✨

If you love photography or crave fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just keep in mind the long travel time — ask your driver if it can be paired with other Ubud stops, like volcanic hot springs, to make the trip more efficient.

### 🌿 **Check-in: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After wrapping up our highland adventure, we headed back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay turned out to be one of the most delightful surprises of our trip, perfectly matching our desire for a 'relaxing + romantic' getaway vibe!

First of all, this is an 'adults-only' resort. Without the noise of children playing, the atmosphere is exceptionally peaceful. The staff are warm and attentive, from the welcome drink to luggage assistance—every detail shows their dedication.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modern and natural elements, with large floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking the lush forest. You can even hear birds chirping, insects humming, and a babbling stream—perfect for couples seeking a romantic and tranquil escape. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so even if you don't feel like going out, you can soak in the tub and gaze at the greenery. It's pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Checking into The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak for a Convenient and Comfortable Stay**

After leaving the serene Ubud highlands, we headed to the lively beach area—**Seminyak**. This is Bali's trendy district, packed not only with restaurants and bars but also boutique shops and Instagram-worthy cafes. It's incredibly convenient and perfect for travelers wanting a city-resort vibe.

That night, we stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, right in the heart of Seminyak. The value for money here is amazing—around **USD$148 per night**—yet the space is spacious, fully equipped, and comes with a private pool! Within a few minutes' walk, you'll find convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage places, so you don't even need to call a ride.

## 🎉 **Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club – Worth Every Penny to Live Like a VIP!**

After so many trips to Bali, we finally made it to the long-awaited **Finns Beach Club**! As one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs, located in Canggu, the venue stretches right onto the sand, featuring multiple pools, sun loungers, bars, DJ stages, and VIP cabanas. The vibe is lively and absolutely electric.

https://youtu.be/MpetWBEQ2Ts

That said, online reviews are really mixed—some people complain about **cold service and high prices**, while others rave about the **great atmosphere, top-notch facilities, and a must-visit experience**.

From our own experience: **if you just order the cheapest sunbed package, you'll likely be overlooked**. But this time, we splurged on a VIP sofa cabana package, and while it cost a pretty penny for the whole day, the treatment we got was a whole different story! ✨

💡The key to getting good service really is **spending your money in the right places**! Once you opt for a high-end cabana, the security guards, waitstaff, and even dancers will come over to greet and interact with you, instantly elevating the vibe to something like a private party.

The group next to us was also in the cabana area, but they probably didn't order much or engage, so they were largely ignored the entire time—even the dancers walked right past them without a glance 😅 So, **if you want to have a blast at Finns, you've got to go all out and live like a VIP for a day** (the happy kind), and only then will you truly feel the magic here!

## 🛫 **Day 6: Bittersweet Goodbyes, Heading Back to Taiwan**

Our short six-day, five-night Bali trip came to an end. After checking out at noon, we took a car to Ngurah Rai Airport (Denpasar) for our flight back to Taiwan. ## 📝 **Five key takeaways from this Bali trip, hope they help you too:**

### ✅ **1. Get your e-VOA online visa in advance!**

I can't stress this enough — **the e-VOA online visa is a lifesaver!** It saves you tons of time waiting in line. Especially if you're traveling with kids or on a red-eye flight, applying ahead is a true act of self-care.

### 📱 **2. Always use Gojek or Grab for rides**

These two local ride-hailing apps let you pay by credit card, with transparent pricing and peace of mind. We once made the mistake of hailing a motorbike on the street for convenience, only to get ripped off — they quoted us in US dollars with an inflated price, acting like it was totally normal 😓. Don't skimp on small savings and end up with a big loss.

{{block:包車}}

### 🚦 **3. Traffic jams are the norm in Bali — don't overpack your itinerary!**

Especially on the routes from Jimbaran and Seminyak to Ubud, traffic jams are common. I strongly recommend planning just 1–2 key activities per day, otherwise you'll spend your trip constantly 'missing out' and it'll be exhausting.

### 💵 **4. Tipping is welcome, but don't let anyone guilt you into it!**

Over 75% of Bali's population works in the service industry, so tipping moderately is a gesture of courtesy and support. However, you may encounter people who try to guilt-trip you or act entitled to a tip. In such cases, **you really don't need to force yourself to tip**—it's more important to set boundaries and respect yourself.

Quick reference: [Do you need to tip in Bali? How much to tip? Tipping guide for various occasions in Bali.](/en/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google it yourself before deciding whether to go!**

If you book a private driver or guided tour, you'll often be 'conveniently taken' to this coffee factory. These places mainly push expensive civet coffee, sometimes with high-pressure sales tactics or scripted spiels. Whether it's worth a visit is for you to decide! 🧐
