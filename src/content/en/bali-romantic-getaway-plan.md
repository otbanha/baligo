✈️ **6-Day, 5-Night Bali Self-Guided Travel Diary (6/22–6/27)**

This trip was truly a long-awaited chance for just the two of us! 👫

After entrusting our child to my mother-in-law's care, my wife and I practically packed our bags in record time, eager to board our flight to Bali. For parents who haven't had a chance to travel abroad in a while, this relaxing vacation was especially precious, and our excitement was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Reminder: Three Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we followed plenty of advice from travel experts and applied for Indonesia's **Online Visa on Arrival (e-VOA)** in advance. Honestly, this step is super important—it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, here’s a little trap to watch out for—my wife **accidentally filled in the wrong birthdate** when applying for the e-VOA 😓. As a result, we had to go through the manual Visa on Arrival channel, queuing for **an hour and a half** on-site. So much regret!

Besides the visa, there are two other online procedures that are equally important. We recommend handling them before boarding to avoid any last-minute chaos upon arrival:

1. **All Indonesia Arrival Card (including e-CD Customs Declaration and SSHP Health Declaration Form)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**

All four processes above can be completed online as electronic documents, and they’re actually quite straightforward. Just make sure to prepare copies of your passport, flight information, and payment method in advance. After completing them, it’s recommended to save the QR Codes as screenshots in your phone album—it’ll make the immigration process much faster when you scan them directly. For a detailed step-by-step guide, check this out: [2026 Bali Arrival Changes Explained in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Arrival Process - From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

Next, our Bali couple’s adventure begins! 🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Check-in at the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We took EVA Air’s direct flight departing at 9:50 AM and arrived in Bali around 3:30 PM. Although the flight was long, a quick nap onboard helped us shift into vacation mode.

After landing at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we went through the manual visa process, which took some time. By the time we cleared customs, it was already late afternoon. We hopped into our pre-booked transfer and arrived at **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran by 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort is Worth Staying Two Nights!**

This hotel was definitely the highlight of our trip! AYANA is one of Bali’s most renowned resorts, offering top-notch facilities, stunning ocean views, and a variety of unique dining options. The must-visit spot here is the legendary **Rock Bar Bali**—a super popular Instagrammable location hailed as one of the "Top 10 Sunset Bars in the World."

https://youtu.be/lbDfKut2J7M

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, capturing the night view was still magical. Standing at the cliffside bar, listening to the waves, and watching the lights flicker created an incredibly romantic atmosphere 💫. The food itself was decent but slightly pricey—though what you’re really paying for here is the ambiance of dining amidst the sea and sky.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

(Johnny Wei)

We stayed in an ocean view room for about USD$387 per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a small tip: **the AYANA resort complex is massive**. If you choose a cheaper room type, you might end up in a more remote area, and walking to the main facilities can be exhausting. I recommend adding a bit more to your budget and selecting a room closer to the main facilities for a better experience!

The photo crowd arrived early, and there was a queue with a one-minute time limit per person, but the good news is—you can queue up again! 📷

### 🍽 **Enjoy a Hearty Breakfast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the all-you-can-eat restaurant next to the AYANA lobby. Although slightly pricey at around USD$29.6 per person (approximately NT$960), the options were incredibly diverse, featuring Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even freshly made omelets and bread desserts. It was truly satisfying. After such a hearty meal, we couldn't even think about lunch, effectively saving on one meal budget 😂

A little anecdote: We left a **$2 tip** while housekeeping was cleaning our room, and when we returned in the afternoon, we found that the housekeeping auntie had quietly prepared a little surprise for us. It was so thoughtful and warm, truly embodying the service spirit of AYANA. In contrast, during our last trip to Chiang Mai, a friend left a $10 tip but got nothing in return. Guess luck is also a kind of skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to the Highlands Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After enjoying a hearty breakfast and leisurely morning at AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to embark on the "mountain chapter" of our journey 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive for Coffee and Scenery**

The first stop of the day was **Montana Del Café**, a scenic coffee shop highly recommended by my cousin, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region in northern Bali. Driving from AYANA in Jimbaran took **two and a half hours** one way—truly a journey over mountains and through valleys.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was worth it once we arrived! Montana Del Café boasts stunning mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether seated indoors or on the terrace, it felt like being in a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two cheesecakes, totaling around USD$16, which is reasonable for a scenic café. The feeling of "drinking coffee in the clouds" was truly priceless ✨.

If you love photography or enjoy the fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just be mindful of the long travel time—you can ask your driver to suggest combining it with other Ubud attractions, like hot springs, for a more efficient trip.

### 🌿 **Accommodation: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After our highland adventure, we drove back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay was one of the most delightful surprises of our trip, perfectly embodying the "relaxation + romance" vibe we were looking for!

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcb27348f2a4fd72f4e265beed93773.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

First and foremost, this is an adults-only resort, so the absence of children's laughter creates an exceptionally serene atmosphere. The staff are warm and attentive, from the welcome drinks to luggage assistance, every detail reflects their thoughtfulness.

We opted for the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modern and natural elements, with floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking lush greenery. You can even hear birds chirping, insects buzzing, and streams flowing - perfect for couples seeking a romantic and tranquil getaway. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so you can soak in the tub while enjoying the greenery without leaving your room - pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Stay at The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak for a Convenient and Comfortable Night**

This is Bali's famous fashion district, packed with restaurants, bars, boutique shops, and Instagram-worthy cafes. It's super convenient and perfect for travelers looking to experience an urban resort vibe.

That night, we stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, located in the heart of Seminyak. This villa offers incredible value for money, costing only around **USD$150 per night**, yet it's spacious, well-equipped, and features a private pool! Convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage parlors are just a short walk away, eliminating the need for transportation.

## 🎉 **Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club – Being a Luxury Spender for a Day is Worth It!**

After visiting Bali so many times, we finally scheduled our long-awaited trip to **Finns Beach Club**! As one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs, located in Canggu, the venue faces the beach and features multiple pools, sun loungers, bars, DJ stages, and VIP cabanas, creating an incredibly lively and energetic atmosphere.

https://youtu.be/MpetWBEQ2Ts

However, online reviews are quite polarized. Some feel that **the staff’s attitude is cold and the prices are high**, while others believe **the atmosphere and facilities are excellent, making it worth a visit**.

Our own experience was this—**if you only opt for the cheapest lounger package, you might feel overlooked**. But this time, we upgraded to a VIP cabana package, and although it cost us a pretty penny, the treatment we received was completely different! ✨

💡 The key to good service really is **spending money in the right places**! When you choose a higher-priced cabana, security, waitstaff, and even dancers will proactively greet and interact with you, instantly elevating the atmosphere to feel like a private party.

The table next to us was also in the cabana area but didn’t order much or engage much, and as a result, they were largely ignored—even the dancers passed by without a glance 😅 So, **to fully enjoy Finns, you’ve got to embrace being a luxury spender for a day** (the happy kind) to truly experience its charm!

## 📝 **Five Key Takeaways from Our Bali Trip (Hope This Helps!)**

### ✅ **1. Apply for Your e-VOA Online Visa in Advance!**

Let me say it again: **the e-VOA online visa is super important!** It saves you a ton of time waiting in line. Especially if you're traveling with kids or on a red-eye flight, applying early is a sign of self-respect for your energy levels.

### 📱 **2. Always Use Gojek or Grab for Ride-Hailing**

These two local ride-hailing apps in Indonesia allow credit card payments, offering transparent pricing and peace of mind. Once, we tried to flag down a motorbike on the street for convenience, only to get ripped off with inflated USD prices and a 'take it or leave it' attitude 😓. Don't save a little money only to lose big.

{{block:包車}}

### 🚦 **3. Traffic Jams Are the Norm in Bali — Don’t Overpack Your Itinerary!**

Especially on routes like Jimbaran, Seminyak to Ubud, traffic jams are common. We strongly recommend planning just 1\~2 key activities per day. Otherwise, you'll spend your trip constantly 'missing out,' making the journey feel exhausting.

### 💵 **4. Tipping is Optional, Don't Feel Pressured!**

Over 75% of Bali's population works in the service industry, so tipping moderately is a polite way to show support. However, some people might use manipulative language or act entitled when asking for tips. In such cases, **you really don't need to force yourself to tip**. Maintaining boundaries and self-respect is more important.

Quick Reference: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Different Situations?](/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google It Before Deciding to Go!**

If you join a private car or guided tour, you might be 'conveniently taken' to this coffee factory. These places mainly sell expensive civet coffee, and sometimes you might encounter high-pressure sales tactics or overly pushy explanations. Whether it's worth visiting is up to you to decide. 🧐
