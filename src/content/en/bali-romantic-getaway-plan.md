✈️ **Six-Day, Five-Night Bali Self-Guided Travel Diary (6/22–6/27)**

This trip was truly a long-awaited chance for just the two of us! 👫

After entrusting our child to my mother-in-law, my wife and I packed our bags almost impatiently, ready to board our flight to Bali. For parents who haven’t had a chance to travel abroad in a while, this relaxing vacation felt especially precious, and our excitement was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Reminder: Three Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we took advice from several travel experts and applied for Indonesia’s **e-VOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival)** in advance. Honestly, this step is super important—it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, here’s a little trap to watch out for—my wife accidentally **entered the wrong birthday date** while filling out the e-VOA 😓. As a result, we had to go through the manual Visa on Arrival channel, queuing for **an hour and a half** on-site. So much regret!

Besides the visa, there are two other important online procedures to handle before boarding your flight to avoid chaos upon arrival:

1. **All Indonesia Arrival Card (including e-CD Customs Declaration and SSHP Health Declaration)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**

All four processes above can be completed online as electronic documents, which are actually not difficult to handle. Just prepare a copy of your passport, flight information, and payment method in advance. It’s recommended to save the QR Code as a screenshot in your phone album for quick scanning upon arrival. For detailed steps, check out this guide: [2026 Bali Arrival Changes Explained in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Arrival Process - From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

Next, our Bali adventure begins! 🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Check-in at the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We took EVA Air’s direct flight departing at 9:50 AM and arrived in Bali at 3:30 PM. Although the flight was long, a quick nap on the plane helped us shift into vacation mode.

After landing at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we went through the manual visa process, and by the time we cleared customs, it was already late. We hopped into our pre-booked transfer car and arrived at **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran by 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort is Worth Staying Two Nights!**

This hotel was definitely the highlight of our trip! AYANA is one of Bali’s most renowned resorts, offering top-notch facilities, stunning ocean views, and several unique restaurants. The must-visit spot here is the legendary **Rock Bar Bali**—a super popular Instagram-worthy destination hailed as one of the "Top 10 Sunset Bars in the World."

https://youtu.be/lbDfKut2J7M

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, capturing the night view was still magical. Standing at the cliffside bar, listening to the waves and watching the lights sway, the atmosphere was incredibly romantic 💫. The food was decent but slightly pricey, though the real treat here isn’t the dishes but the enchanting ambiance of dancing with the sea and sky.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

We stayed in an ocean-view room, which cost around USD$387 per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a small tip: **The AYANA resort complex is massive**. If you opt for a cheaper room, you might end up in a more remote area, and walking to the main facilities could be exhausting. We recommend spending a bit more to choose a room closer to the main facilities for a better experience!

The photo crowd had already gathered early in the morning, with a queue and a one-minute time limit per person for photos, but the good news is—you can queue up again! 📷

### 🍽 **Fill Up on Breakfast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the all-you-can-eat restaurant next to the AYANA lobby. Although slightly pricey at around USD$29.6 per person (approximately NT$960), the options were incredibly diverse, featuring Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even freshly made omelets and bread desserts. It was truly satisfying. After such a hearty meal, we couldn't even think about lunch, effectively saving on one meal budget 😂

A little anecdote: We left a $2 tip while housekeeping was cleaning our room, and when we returned in the afternoon, we found that the housekeeping auntie had quietly prepared a small surprise for us. It was thoughtful and heartwarming, truly reflecting AYANA's service spirit. In contrast, during our last trip to Chiang Mai, a friend left a $10 tip but received nothing in return. I guess luck is also a kind of skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to the Highlands Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After enjoying a hearty breakfast and leisurely morning at AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to embark on the "mountain chapter" of our journey 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive for Coffee and Scenery**

The first stop of the day was a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin — **Montana Del Café**, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region in northern Bali. Driving from AYANA in Jimbaran took a whopping **two and a half hours**, truly a journey over mountains and through valleys.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was worth it once we arrived! Montana Del Café boasts stunning mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether sitting indoors or on the terrace, it felt like being in a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two cheesecakes, totaling around USD$16, which is reasonable for a scenic café. The feeling of "drinking coffee in the clouds" was truly priceless ✨.

If you love photography or enjoy the fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just be mindful of the long travel time. You can ask your driver to suggest combining it with other Ubud attractions, like volcanic hot springs, for a more efficient trip.

### 🌿 **Accommodation: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After finishing our highland adventure, we drove back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay was one of the most delightful discoveries of our trip, perfectly matching our desire for a "relaxing + romantic" vacation vibe!

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcb27348f2a4fd72f4e265beed93773.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

First and foremost, this is an adults-only resort, so the absence of children's laughter makes the environment exceptionally tranquil. The staff are warm and attentive, from the welcome drinks to luggage assistance, every detail reflects their thoughtfulness.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modernity with nature, with floor-to-ceiling windows offering views of lush greenery. You can even hear birds chirping, insects buzzing, and the gentle flow of a stream, making it perfect for couples seeking a romantic and peaceful retreat. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so you can enjoy soaking in the tub while admiring the greenery without stepping out—pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Staying at The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak, Enjoying a Convenient and Comfortable Night**

After leaving the serene mountains of Ubud, we headed to the vibrant coastal area of **Seminyak**. This is Bali's famous fashion district, bustling with restaurants, bars, boutique shops, and Instagram-worthy cafes. The area is super convenient, making it ideal for travelers looking to experience a city resort vibe.

That night, we stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, located in the heart of Seminyak. This villa offers incredible value for money—around **USD$150 per night**—yet it’s spacious, well-equipped, and even includes a private pool! Convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage parlors are just a short walk away, eliminating the need for taxis.

Our short six-day, five-night trip to Bali has come to an end. After checking out at 12:00 PM, we headed to Denpasar Airport to catch our flight back to Taiwan. ## 📝 **Five Key Takeaways from Our Bali Trip (Hope This Helps!)**

### ✅ **1. Make Sure to Apply for Your e-VOA Online Visa in Advance!**

Let me say it again: **the e-VOA online visa is super important!** It saves a ton of time waiting in line. Especially for those traveling with kids or on red-eye flights, applying early is a sign of respect for your own energy levels.

### 📱 **2. Always Use Gojek or Grab for Ride-Hailing**

These two local ride-hailing apps in Indonesia allow credit card payments and offer transparent pricing, making things hassle-free. We once tried to flag down a motorcycle on the street for convenience, only to get ripped off with inflated USD prices and a “take it or leave it” attitude 😓. Don’t save small amounts only to lose big.

{{block:包車}}

### 🚦 **3. Traffic Jams Are a Daily Reality in Bali—Don’t Overpack Your Itinerary!**

Especially on routes like Jimbaran, Seminyak to Ubud, traffic jams are common. We strongly recommend planning only 1\~2 key activities per day. Otherwise, you’ll spend your trip constantly “missing out” and feeling stressed.

### 💵 **4. Tipping Culture: Give When Appropriate, But Don’t Feel Pressured!**

Over 75% of Bali's population works in the service industry, so tipping moderately is a polite way to show support. However, some individuals may use manipulative language or act entitled when asking for tips. In such cases, **you really don’t need to feel obligated to tip**. Maintaining boundaries and self-respect is more important.

Quick Reference: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Different Situations?](/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google It Before Deciding to Visit!**

If you’re on a private car or guided tour, you might be "conveniently" taken to this coffee factory. These places primarily sell expensive civet coffee and sometimes employ high-pressure sales tactics or overly pushy explanations. Whether it’s worth visiting is up to you to decide. 🧐
