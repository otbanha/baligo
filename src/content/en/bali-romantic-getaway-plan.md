✈️ **Six-Day, Five-Night Bali Self-Guided Travel Diary (6/22－6/27)**

This trip was truly a long-awaited escape for just the two of us! 👫

After entrusting our child to my mother-in-law, my wife and I packed our bags almost in a frenzy, eager to board our flight to Bali. For parents who haven't had a chance to travel abroad in a while, this relaxing vacation was especially precious, and our excitement was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Reminder: Three Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we took advice from many travel experts and applied for Indonesia's **Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA)** in advance. Honestly, this step is super important as it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, here’s a little trap to watch out for—my wife accidentally **entered the wrong birthday date** while filling out the e-VOA 😓 As a result, we had to go through the manual visa-on-arrival channel, queuing for **an hour and a half** on the spot. So much regret!

Besides the visa, there are two other important online procedures that you should complete before boarding to avoid any last-minute chaos upon arrival:

1. **All Indonesia Entry Card (including e-CD Customs Declaration and SSHP Health Declaration Form)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**

All four processes above can be completed online as electronic documents. It's actually not difficult to handle, as long as you prepare copies of your passport, flight information, and payment methods in advance. After completion, it's recommended to save the QR Code screenshot to your phone's photo album for faster scanning upon arrival. For detailed procedures, check this out: [Understand Bali's 2026 Entry Changes in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Entry Process - From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

Next, our Bali adventure for two begins～🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Check-in at the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We took EVA Air's direct flight departing at 9:50 AM and arrived in Bali at 3:30 PM. Although the flight was a bit long, taking a nap on the plane helped us switch into vacation mode.

After arriving at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we went through the manual visa process and it was already dark by the time we cleared customs. We boarded our pre-booked transfer car and arrived at **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran by 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort is Worth Staying for Two Nights!**

This hotel was definitely the most anticipated stop of our trip! AYANA is a very famous resort in Bali, not only for its excellent facilities but also for its stunning ocean views and multiple unique restaurants. Among them, the must-visit is the legendary **Rock Bar Bali**—one of the 'World's Top Ten Sunset Bars' and a super popular photo spot.

https://youtu.be/lbDfKut2J7M

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, only capturing the night view, standing at the cliffside bar area, listening to the waves and watching the lights sway, the atmosphere was truly romantic💫. The food itself was decent, slightly pricey, but what you're really here for is not the dishes but the atmosphere of dancing with the sea and sky.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

We stayed in an ocean-view room, which cost about USD$387 per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a small tip: **the AYANA resort complex is very large**. If you choose a cheaper room type, you might end up in a more remote area, and walking to the main facilities can be exhausting. We recommend adding a bit more to your budget and choosing a room closer to the main facilities for a better experience!

The photo crowd was already there early in the morning, with queues and a one-minute time limit per person, but the good news is—you can queue again! 📷

### 🍽 **Breakfast Feast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the all-you-can-eat restaurant next to the AYANA lobby. Although the price was slightly high at about USD$29.6 per person (approximately NT$960), the options were very diverse, including Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even freshly made omelets and bread desserts. It was very satisfying. After eating and drinking to our heart's content, we couldn't eat lunch, saving the budget for one meal 😂

A little anecdote: We left a $2 tip while housekeeping was cleaning our room, and when we returned in the afternoon, we found that the housekeeping auntie had quietly prepared a small surprise for us. It was so thoughtful and heartwarming, truly reflecting AYANA's service spirit. In contrast, when we were in Chiang Mai last time, a friend left a $10 tip but didn't receive anything in return. I guess luck is also a kind of skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to a Highland Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After enjoying a hearty breakfast and leisurely morning at AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to embark on the 'mountain chapter' of our journey 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive for Coffee and Scenery**

The first stop of the day was a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin — **Montana Del Café**, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region in northern Bali. Driving from AYANA in Jimbaran took **two and a half hours** one way, truly a journey over mountains and through valleys.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

But it was worth it! Montana Del Café boasts breathtaking mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether seated indoors or on the terrace, the scenery was like a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two slices of cheesecake, totaling around USD$16, which is reasonable for a scenic café. The feeling of 'drinking coffee in the clouds' was truly priceless ✨.

If you love photography or enjoy the fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just be mindful of the long travel time. You can ask your driver to suggest combining it with other Ubud attractions, like volcanic hot springs, for a more efficient trip.

### 🌿 **Stay: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After our highland adventure, we drove back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay was one of the most delightful discoveries of our trip, perfectly matching our desire for a 'relaxing + romantic' vacation vibe!

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcb27348f2a4fd72f4e265beed93773.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

First and foremost, this is an adults-only resort. Without the playful noises of children, the environment feels exceptionally tranquil. The staff are warm and attentive, from the welcome drinks to luggage assistance, every detail makes you feel their dedication.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modernity with nature, with large floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking lush mountains. You can even hear birds chirping, insects humming, and streams flowing, making it perfect for couples to enjoy a romantic and peaceful time. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so you can soak in the tub and enjoy the greenery without stepping out—pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Stay at The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak, Enjoy a Convenient and Comfortable Night**

After leaving the serene mountains of Ubud, we headed to the lively coastal area—**Seminyak**. This is Bali's famous fashion district, filled with restaurants, bars, boutique shops, and Instagram-worthy cafes. The area is super convenient, perfect for travelers looking to experience a city resort vibe.

That night, we stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas** in the heart of Seminyak. This villa offers incredible value for money, costing only around **USD$150** per night, yet it’s spacious, well-equipped, and comes with a private pool! Within walking distance, you’ll find convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage parlors, so there’s no need to call a car.

## 🎉 **Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club, Being a Luxury Plate for a Day is Worth It!**

After visiting Bali so many times, we finally arranged our long-awaited trip to **Finns Beach Club**! It's one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs, located in Canggu. The entire venue faces the beach, featuring multiple pools, sun loungers, bars, DJ stages, and VIP lounges, creating an incredibly lively and energetic atmosphere.

https://youtu.be/MpetWBEQ2Ts

However, online reviews about this place are quite polarized. Some feel that **the staff's attitude is cold and the prices are high**, while others believe that **the atmosphere is great, the facilities are excellent, and it's worth a visit**.

Our own experience was this—**if you only order the cheapest sunbed package, you might easily be overlooked**. But this time, we upgraded to a VIP sofa lounge package, and although we spent quite a bit of money, the treatment we received was completely different! ✨

💡The key to good service is really about **spending money in the right places**! When you opt for a higher-priced lounge, security, waitstaff, and even dancers will proactively greet and interact with you, instantly elevating the atmosphere to feel like a private party.

The table next to us was also seated in the lounge area, but perhaps they didn't order much or didn't engage much, and as a result, they were largely ignored throughout the event—even the dancers passed by without a glance 😅 So, **to truly enjoy Finns, you need to be a luxury plate for a day** (the happy kind) to fully experience its charm!

## 🛫 **Day 6: Saying Goodbye and Heading Home**

Our short six-day, five-night trip to Bali has come to an end. We checked out at 12:00 PM and headed to Denpasar Airport to catch our flight back to Taiwan. Although the flight was scheduled to depart at 4:30 PM, due to airline delays, we didn't arrive in Taiwan until **11:00 PM**, several hours later than expected, leaving us utterly exhausted.

But hey, that's travel for you—always full of unexpected little twists and turns. The important thing is that we came back with a treasure trove of memories, and it was all worth it! 🎒

## 📝 **Five Key Takeaways from Our Bali Trip (Hope This Helps!)**

### ✅ **1. Apply for Your e-VOA Online in Advance!**

Let me say it again: **the e-VOA online visa is super important!** It can save you a lot of time waiting in line. Especially if you're traveling with kids or on a red-eye flight, applying in advance is a sign of self-respect for your energy levels.

### 📱 **2. Always Use Gojek or Grab for Ride-Hailing**

These two local ride-hailing apps in Indonesia allow you to pay with a credit card, offering transparent pricing and peace of mind. We once made the mistake of hailing a motorcycle on the street for convenience, only to be ripped off with inflated USD prices and a 'take it or leave it' attitude 😓. Don't try to save a little money only to end up losing big.

{{block:包車}}

### 🚦 **3. Traffic Jams Are a Daily Reality in Bali—Don’t Overpack Your Itinerary!**

Especially on routes like Jimbaran, Seminyak to Ubud, traffic jams are common. We strongly recommend limiting yourself to 1~2 key activities per day. Otherwise, you'll spend your trip constantly 'missing out,' which can make the journey feel exhausting.

### 💵 **4. Tipping Culture: Give When Appropriate, But Don’t Feel Pressured!**

Over 75% of Bali's population works in the service industry, so tipping moderately is a polite way to show support. However, some people may try to guilt-trip you or act entitled when asking for tips. In such cases, **you really don’t need to feel obligated to tip**. Maintaining boundaries and self-respect is more important.

Quick Reference: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/blog/2024-05-07-663a126bfd89780001390b63/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google It Before Deciding to Go!**

If you’re on a private car or guided tour, you’ll often be ‘conveniently’ taken to this coffee factory. These places mainly sell expensive civet coffee, and sometimes you might encounter high-pressure sales tactics or overly pushy explanations. Whether it’s worth visiting is up to you to decide. 🧐
