✈️ **Bali Six-Day, Five-Night Self-Guided Trip Journal (June 22–27)**

This trip was a long-awaited time for just the two of us! 👫

After leaving our child with grandma, my wife and I packed our bags almost impatiently, ready to board the flight to Bali. For parents who haven't had a break abroad in a while, this relaxing vacation felt especially precious, and our excitement was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Tips: Three Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we followed advice from many travel experts and applied for Indonesia's **e-VOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival)** in advance. I honestly think this step is crucial—it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, I also want to warn you about a little pitfall—my wife **accidentally entered the wrong birth date** when filling out the e-VOA 😓. As a result, she had to go through the manual visa-on-arrival counter and waited in line for **an hour and a half**. So regretful!

Besides the visa, there are two other online procedures that are also important. I recommend handling them before boarding to avoid any last-minute hassle upon arrival:

1. **All Indonesia Arrival Card (including e-CD Customs Declaration and SSHP Health Statement)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**

All four steps are **digital documents that can be completed online**, and they're actually quite straightforward—just have your passport copy, flight details, and payment method ready. I recommend saving the QR codes as screenshots in your phone album after completing them, as presenting them for scanning at immigration will speed things up significantly. For a detailed walkthrough, check this out: [2026 Bali Entry Changes in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Arrival Guide - From Landing, Through Customs, to Baggage Claim](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

Next up, it's time to kick off our Bali duo trip~ 🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arriving in Bali, Checking into the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We took EVA Air's direct 9:50 AM flight, scheduled to land in Bali at 3:30 PM. The flight was a bit long, but after a quick nap on the plane, my mind had already switched into vacation mode.

After arriving at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we queued for the manual visa process, so by the time we cleared customs, it was already getting dark. We hopped into our pre-booked transfer and arrived at **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran around 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort Is Worth Staying for Two Nights!**

This hotel was hands-down the most anticipated stop on our trip! AYANA is one of Bali's most renowned resorts, boasting top-notch facilities, stunning ocean views, and a variety of signature restaurants. The absolute must-visit is the legendary **Rock Bar Bali**—one of the world's top ten sunset bars and a wildly popular Instagram-worthy spot.

https://youtu.be/lbDfKut2J7M

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, only catching the night view, standing at the cliffside bar area, listening to the waves and watching the lights sway, the atmosphere was incredibly romantic 💫. The food itself was decent, though a bit pricey—but honestly, you're not here for the meal; you're here for that magical vibe of dancing with the sea and sky.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

We stayed in a sea-view room, which cost about NT$12,000 (approx. USD$387) per night. While it was quite nice, here's a tip: **the AYANA resort is enormous**, so if you opt for a cheaper room, you might end up in a remote area, and walking to the main facilities could leave your legs sore. I'd suggest adding a bit to your budget and choosing a room closer to the main facilities for a better experience!

## 📸 **Day 2: Wake Up Early to Snap Photos at the Famous H2O Pool!**

To capture those dreamy pool photos you see on Instagram, we got up at 7 a.m. the next day and took the hotel shuttle to the most iconic **H2O Infinity Pool** at AYANA Resort. The crowd for photos was already gathering early in the morning—you have to queue, and each person gets just one minute for a quick snap. But the good news is—you can line up again! 📷

### 🍽 **Eat a Hearty Breakfast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the buffet restaurant near the AYANA lobby. Though it was a bit pricey at about USD$30 (approx. NT$960) per person, the selection was incredibly rich, featuring Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even made-to-order omelets along with bread and desserts—truly generous. After eating our fill, we couldn't even manage lunch, saving us a meal's budget 😂

A little side note: we left a **$2 tip** while housekeeping was cleaning, and when we returned to the room in the afternoon, the housekeeping lady had quietly prepared a little surprise—thoughtful and heartwarming, truly capturing the AYANA service spirit. In contrast, last time in Chiang Mai, a friend left a $10 tip and got nothing in return—guess luck is also a kind of skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to the Highland Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After a hearty breakfast and a leisurely morning soaking in AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to embark on the 'mountain chapter' of our journey 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive, All for a Cup of Coffee and the Views**

Our first stop of the day was a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin — **Montana Del Café**, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region of northern Bali. The drive from AYANA in Jimbaran took a whopping **two and a half hours** one way — a true journey over hills and mountains.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was totally worth it once we arrived! Montana Del Café boasts sweeping mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether you sit indoors or on the terrace, it's like stepping into a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two slices of cheesecake, totaling around USD$16 — quite reasonable for a scenic café. The real treasure, though, was that feeling of 'sipping coffee above the clouds' — absolutely priceless ✨

If you love photography or crave fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just keep in mind the longer travel time — ask your driver if you can pair it with other Ubud attractions along the way, like volcanic hot springs, to make the trip more efficient.

### 🌿 **Check-in: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After wrapping up our highland adventure, we drove back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay turned out to be one of the most delightful surprises of our trip, perfectly matching the 'relaxation + romance' vibe we were after!

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcb27348f2a4fd72f4e265beed93773.jpg)

First off, this is an adults-only resort, so without the noise of children playing, the atmosphere is especially serene. The staff are warm and attentive, from the welcome drink to help with luggage—every detail shows their thoughtfulness.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modern and natural elements, with large floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking lush mountain forests. You can even hear birds chirping, insects humming, and a babbling stream—perfect for couples seeking a romantic and peaceful getaway. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so even if you don't feel like going out, you can soak in the tub and gaze at the greenery—pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Check into The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak for a Convenient and Comfortable Stay**

Leaving the tranquil Ubud highlands behind, we headed to the lively beach area—**Seminyak**. This is Bali's trendy district, packed not only with restaurants and bars but also boutique shops and Instagram-worthy cafes. It's super convenient and perfect for travelers wanting a city-resort vibe.

That night, we stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, right in the heart of Seminyak. The value for money here is incredible—around **USD$148 per night**—yet the space is spacious, fully equipped, and comes with a private pool! Within a few minutes' walk, you'll find convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage places, so you don't even need to call a ride.

## 🎉 **Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club — Worth Being a Splurger for a Day!**

Having visited Bali many times, we finally made it to the long-awaited **Finns Beach Club** on this trip! As one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs, located in Canggu, the venue stretches out to the beach with multiple pools, sun loungers, bars, DJ stages, and VIP cabanas. The vibe is lively and absolutely buzzing.

https://youtu.be/MpetWBEQ2Ts

However, online reviews are really mixed — some people complain about **cold service and high prices**, while many others rave about the **great atmosphere, fantastic facilities, and say it's worth a visit**.

Our own experience? **If you just order the cheapest sunbed package, you'll likely be overlooked.** But this time, we splurged and upgraded to a VIP sofa cabana package. We spent a good chunk of money for the whole day, but the treatment we got was completely different! ✨

💡The key to getting good service really is **spending your money in the right place**! When you choose a high-end cabana, the security guards, servers, and even dancers will proactively come over to greet and interact with you, instantly elevating the vibe to something like a private party.

The group next to us was also in the cabana area, but they probably didn't order much or engage much, so they were almost completely ignored the whole time — even the dancers walked right past them without a glance 😅 So, **if you want to have a blast at Finns, you've got to be a splurger for a day** (the happy kind) to truly experience its charm!

## 🛫 **Day 6: A Bittersweet Farewell, Heading Back to Taiwan**

Our short six-day, five-night Bali trip came to an end. After checking out at 12:00 PM, we took a car to Ngurah Rai Airport for our return flight to Taiwan. Although the flight was scheduled to depart at 4:30 PM, we encountered an airline scheduling delay and didn't arrive in Taiwan until **11:00 PM** — several hours later than expected, leaving us utterly exhausted.

But hey, that's travel — full of unexpected little twists. What matters is the memories we made, and they were totally worth it! 🎒

## 📝 **Five Key Takeaways from This Bali Trip (Hope They Help!):**

### ✅ **1. Get Your Online Visa on Arrival in Advance!**

Seriously, I can't stress this enough: **the e-VOA is super important!** It saves you tons of time waiting in line. Especially if you're traveling with kids or arriving on a red-eye flight, applying early is a sign of respect for your own energy.

### 📱 **2. Always Use Gojek or Grab for Rides**

These two local ride-hailing apps let you pay by credit card, with transparent pricing and zero hassle. We once grabbed a motorbike taxi on the street for convenience, and got totally ripped off — the driver quoted us in US dollars with a straight face like it was totally normal 😓. Don't lose big money trying to save a little.

{{block:包車}}

### 🚦 **3. Traffic Jams Are the Norm in Bali — Don't Overpack Your Itinerary!**

The routes from Jimbaran and Seminyak to Ubud are especially prone to traffic. I strongly recommend planning just 1–2 key activities per day, or you'll spend the whole trip feeling like you're constantly "missing out" — and it'll be a real struggle.

### 💵 **4. Tipping Culture: It’s Okay to Tip, but Don’t Let Guilt Trip You!**

Over 75% of Bali’s population works in the service industry, so giving a modest tip is a polite gesture of support. However, you may encounter people who use verbal pressure or act entitled to tips. In such cases, **you really don’t need to force yourself to tip**—it’s more important to set boundaries and respect yourself.

Quick reference: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/en/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google It Yourself Before Deciding!**

If you book a private driver or guided tour, you’ll often be “conveniently taken” to this coffee factory. These places mainly push expensive civet coffee, sometimes with high-pressure sales tactics or scripted explanations. Whether it’s worth a visit is entirely up to you to decide 🧐
