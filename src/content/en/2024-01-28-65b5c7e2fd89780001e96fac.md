When planning a trip to Bali, currency exchange is an important and carefully considered preparation step. As part of Indonesia, the only currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). In this article, we'll explore how to get the best exchange rates and recommend some trustworthy exchange shops.

## Currency Exchange Methods

**Exchange USD before departure**: The most important step is to exchange your local currency for USD before departure, then bring USD to Indonesia/Bali to exchange for IDR. This method offers the best rates. If you have AUD, you can directly exchange AUD for IDR in Bali, as the rates are comparable to USD.

Why is exchanging TWD to USD, then USD to IDR better? Check out the explanation [here](/blog/2025-08-13-689c380bfd8978000178bb5e/)

Most tourists handle currency exchange upon arrival in Bali. You can ask a trusted private driver to take you to a reputable Money Changer they know - this is the best way to avoid scams!

If you're choosing your own exchange method, here are your options:

- **Bank exchange**: Bringing USD to a local bank is a safe and reliable method. Banks offer stable rates with low fees. Major cities and tourist areas in Bali have bank branches for convenient exchanges.
- **Money Changer**: Tourist areas in Bali have specialized currency exchange shops. When choosing one, ensure it's reputable with favorable rates, and check for any additional fees.
- **ATM withdrawal**: Bali has an extensive ATM network where you can withdraw cash using international credit or debit cards. This is convenient and usually uses the day's real-time rate (close to bank mid-market rates). However, the drawbacks are:
1. Taiwanese banks typically charge **overseas withdrawal fees**: around USD$2.5～5 per transaction
2. Plus **1.5%～2% overseas transaction fees** (varies by card issuer)
3. Local ATMs in Bali may charge additional **machine usage fees** (around IDR 50,000～75,000 per transaction)
4. Some ATMs have withdrawal limits, requiring multiple transactions and accumulating fees
   We recommend withdrawing larger amounts to reduce fee frequency, confirming your bank's overseas withdrawal fees before departure, and choosing reliable ATMs from major banks like BCA or Mandiri.

## **Indonesia/Bali Currency Exchange FAQ**

### **1. What currency is used in Bali?**

As part of Indonesia, Bali uses the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Only Indonesian Rupiah is accepted for transactions, so it's recommended to prepare cash in advance.

### **2. Should I bring USD to Bali?**

Yes, it's recommended to exchange your local currency for USD before departure, as USD typically offers better exchange rates in Bali. If you hold Australian dollars, you can also bring AUD directly to Bali for exchange, as AUD also offers favorable rates. Reference: [Indonesia/Bali Currency Exchange: Direct Exchange vs. Exchanging USD First, Which is Better?](/blog/2025-08-13-689c380bfd8978000178bb5e/)

### **3. Where is the best place to exchange currency?**

- **Money Changer:** Choose reputable money changers, as they usually offer better rates with low or no fees.
- **Bank:** Banks provide stable and secure exchange rates, but you may need to wait in line.
- **ATM Withdrawal:** Convenient but may incur fees. It’s recommended to use a bank card with no overseas transaction fees.

Avoid exchanging currency at the airport, as the rates are usually poor.

### **4. How to Identify Reputable Money Changers?**

- Choose authorized money changers with the green shield-shaped **PVA Berizin** logo.
- Check Google reviews and opt for establishments with high ratings and numerous comments.
- Avoid shops offering exceptionally favorable rates, as they are almost certainly scams.
- **No commission** is another way to identify legitimate operators. Before exchanging, ask "Any commission?" If they say yes, find another place.
- Our themed map lists authorized money changers licensed by **Bank Indonesia**. Check it out [here](https://gobaligo.id/map/money-changer/).

### **5. What to Pay Attention to When Exchanging Currency?**

- Count the cash on the spot and confirm the amount before leaving.
- Avoid exchanging money at night or in remote areas.
- Be aware of any additional fees or commissions.
- Keep the exchange receipt for future reference.

### **6. Can I Use Credit Cards or Mobile Payments?**

Yes, many shops, restaurants, and hotels in Bali accept credit cards (Visa, Mastercard) and mobile payments (e.g., GoPay, OVO). However, cash remains the primary payment method at small vendors or in remote areas.

### **7. Is ATM Withdrawal Convenient?**

Bali has an extensive ATM network, with machines available in major cities and tourist areas. When using international credit or debit cards for withdrawals, please note:

- Ensure your bank card supports overseas withdrawals.
- Understand the bank's fees and exchange rate differences.
- Choose ATMs with security monitoring; bank-specific ATMs are the safest. Avoid withdrawing cash at night.

### **8. Will I be charged a fee when exchanging currency?**

- **Currency exchange shops:** Some shops may charge a fee, so it's recommended to ask before exchanging.
- **Banks:** Fees are usually lower, but the exchange rate may not be as favorable as at currency exchange shops.
- **ATM withdrawals:** You may incur two fees: a local bank fee and your card issuer's overseas withdrawal fee.

### **9. How much cash should I prepare?**

It is recommended to prepare cash based on your itinerary and spending habits. Generally:

- A daily budget of approximately **1,100,000 IDR～1,280,000** (around USD$70-80) per person for meals, transportation, small purchases, and tips. You can refer to the explanation below:

### **10. What if I run out of cash?**

- Use an ATM to withdraw cash, but be mindful of fees.
- Use a credit card for cash advances, but note the higher interest and fees.
- Seek help from hotel staff, as they usually know nearby currency exchange shops or banks.
- Confirm with your card issuer before departure that your card has enabled "**Overseas Withdrawal**" and "**International Transactions**." Otherwise, it cannot be used abroad.

### **11. Should I keep receipts after exchanging currency?**

Yes, it is recommended to keep exchange receipts as proof in case of issues. Additionally, record the amount and exchange rate for each transaction to facilitate financial management.

### **12. Does the exchange rate fluctuate in Bali?**

Yes, exchange rates fluctuate with the market. It's recommended to monitor the exchange rate trends before your trip and choose a favorable time to exchange currency. You can use currency tracking tools (such as XE.com or OANDA) to stay updated on the latest rates.

### **14. Can I use other currencies in Bali?**

No, Bali only accepts Indonesian Rupiah as the transaction currency. Even though some merchants may accept US dollars, the exchange rate is usually less favorable for tourists.

### **15. Do I need to speak Indonesian when exchanging currency?**

Bali is a highly developed tourist destination, and staff at legitimate money changers can almost always communicate in **English**, so English alone is perfectly sufficient. Here are some useful phrases:

**Negotiating**

- Can you give me a better rate?
- If I exchange more, can I get a better rate?

**Confirming the Amount**

- I'd like to exchange 200 US dollars.
- How much Indonesian Rupiah will I get?
- Is there any commission or fee?

**Transaction Completed**

- Can I count the money here?
- Thank you, this looks correct.

### 17. **Credit Card Payments**

It's recommended to use a credit card that offers overseas spending rewards and has low or no foreign transaction fees, especially at large hotels or restaurants. This way, you can enjoy the favorable USD exchange rate while maintaining flexibility! A quick reminder: a group member once had their credit card skimmed at a **small supermarket**, so please use credit cards cautiously!
