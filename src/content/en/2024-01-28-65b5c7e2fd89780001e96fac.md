When planning a trip to Bali, currency exchange is an important and carefully considered preparatory step. As part of Indonesia, the only currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). In this article, we'll explore how to exchange currency in the most cost-effective way and recommend some trustworthy exchange shops.

## Currency Exchange Methods

**Exchange for USD before departure**: The most important thing is to exchange your local currency for USD before departure, then bring USD to Indonesia/Bali to exchange for IDR. The reason for this is that the exchange rate is the best. If you hold AUD, you can directly bring AUD to Bali to exchange for IDR. As Australia has always been a major tourist source country for Bali, the exchange rate is not worse than USD.

Why is exchanging TWD for USD and then USD for IDR better? Please see the explanation [here](/blog/2025-08-13-689c380bfd8978000178bb5e/)

Most tourists handle currency exchange immediately upon arrival in Bali. You can find a trustworthy private driver to take you to a reputable Money Changer they know - this is the best way to avoid scams!

If you're choosing your own exchange method, you have the following options:

- **Bank exchange**: Bringing USD to a local bank for exchange is a safe and reliable method. Banks offer relatively stable exchange rates with low fees. Major cities and tourist areas in Bali have bank branches for convenient currency exchange.

- **Money Changer**: There are many specialized currency exchange shops in Bali's tourist areas that offer foreign currency exchange services. When choosing a Money Changer, make sure to select a reputable shop with favorable rates, and be aware of any additional fees or charges.

## **Indonesia/Bali Currency Exchange FAQ**

### **1. What currency is used in Bali?**

As part of Indonesia, Bali uses the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Only IDR is accepted for transactions, so it’s advisable to prepare cash in advance.

### **2. Do I Need to Bring USD to Bali?**

Yes, it's recommended to exchange your local currency to USD before departure, as USD generally offers better exchange rates in Bali. If you hold AUD, you can also bring AUD directly to Bali for exchange, as AUD also offers favorable rates. Reference: [Indonesia/Bali Currency Exchange: Direct Exchange vs. Exchanging to USD First, Which is Better?](/blog/2025-08-13-689c380bfd8978000178bb5e/)

### **3. Where is the Best Place to Exchange Currency?**

- **Money Changers:** Choose reputable money changers, as they usually offer better rates with low or no fees.
- **Banks:** Banks provide stable and secure exchange rates, but you may need to wait in line.
- **ATM Withdrawals:** Convenient but may incur fees; it's recommended to use a card with no overseas transaction fees.

Avoid exchanging currency at the airport, as the rates are usually worse.

### **4. How to Identify Reputable Money Changers?**

- Choose authorized money changers with the green shield-shaped **PVA Berizin** logo.
- Check Google reviews and select shops with high ratings and numerous reviews.
- Avoid shops offering extremely favorable rates, as they are 100% scams.
- **No commission (no commission)** is another way to identify legitimate operators. Before exchanging, ask "Any commission?" If they say yes, consider going elsewhere.
- [Here](https://moneychangerbali.com/) is a list of "Authorized Money Changers" licensed by **Bank Indonesia**.

### **5. What to Pay Attention to When Exchanging Currency?**

- Count the cash on the spot and confirm the amount before leaving.
- Avoid exchanging currency at night or in remote areas.
- Be aware of any additional fees or commissions.
- Keep the exchange receipt for future reference.

### **6. Can I Use Credit Cards or Mobile Payments?**

Yes, many shops, restaurants, and hotels in Bali accept credit cards (Visa, Mastercard) and mobile payments (such as GoPay, OVO). However, cash remains the primary payment method at small stalls or in remote areas.

### **7. Is ATM Withdrawal Convenient?**

Bali has an extensive ATM network, with ATMs available in major cities and tourist areas. When using an international credit or debit card to withdraw cash, please note:

- Confirm that your bank card supports overseas withdrawals.
- Be aware of your bank’s fees and exchange rate differences.
- Choose ATMs with security monitoring, and bank-owned ATMs are the safest. Avoid withdrawing cash at night.

### **8. Are There Fees for Currency Exchange?**

- **Money Changers:** Some shops may charge a fee, so it’s recommended to ask before exchanging money.
- **Banks:** Fees are usually lower, but the exchange rate may not be as favorable as at money changers.
- **ATM Withdrawals:** You may incur two fees: a local bank fee and your card issuer’s overseas withdrawal fee.

### **9. How Much Cash Should I Prepare?**

It’s recommended to prepare cash based on your itinerary and spending habits. Generally:

### **10. What If I Run Out of Cash?**

- Use ATMs to withdraw cash, but be mindful of transaction fees.
- Use credit cards for cash advances, but note that interest rates and fees are higher.
- Seek assistance from hotel staff, as they usually know nearby currency exchange shops or banks.
- Before departure, confirm with your card issuer whether your card has activated "**International Withdrawals**" _**and**_ "Cross-border Transactions**" features. Otherwise, it cannot be used abroad.

### **11. Do I need to keep the receipt after currency exchange?**

Yes, it is recommended to keep the currency exchange receipt as proof in case of any issues. Additionally, record the amount and exchange rate each time for easier financial management.

### **12. Does the exchange rate in Bali fluctuate?**

Yes, exchange rates fluctuate with the market. It is advisable to monitor the exchange rate trends before departure and choose a favorable time to exchange currency. You can use exchange rate tracking tools (such as XE.com, OANDA) to stay updated on the latest rates.

### **14. Can I use other currencies in Bali?**

No, Bali only accepts Indonesian Rupiah as the transaction currency. Even though some merchants may accept US dollars, the exchange rate is usually less favorable for tourists.

### **15. Do I need to speak Indonesian when exchanging currency?**

Bali is a highly developed tourist destination, and staff at legitimate Money Changers can almost always communicate in **English**, so English alone is perfectly sufficient. Here are some common phrases:

**Bargaining**

- Can you give me a better rate?
- If I exchange more, can I get a better rate?

**Confirm Amount**

- I'd like to exchange 200 US dollars.
- How much Indonesian Rupiah will I get?
- Is there any commission or fee?

**Complete Transaction**

- Can I count the money here?
- Thank you, this looks correct.

### 17. **Credit Card Payments**

It’s recommended to use a credit card that offers overseas spending rewards and has low or no foreign transaction fees for purchases at large hotels or restaurants, as this might be more cost-effective. This way, you can enjoy favorable USD exchange rates while maintaining flexibility! A quick reminder: Some group members have experienced **credit card fraud after using their cards at small supermarkets**, so use credit cards cautiously!
