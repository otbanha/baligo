When planning a trip to Bali, currency exchange is an important preparation step that requires careful consideration. As part of Indonesia, the only currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). In this article, we'll explore how to get the best exchange rate and recommend some trustworthy exchange shops.

## Currency Exchange Methods

**Exchange USD before departure**: The most important step is to exchange your local currency for USD before departure, then bring USD to Indonesia/Bali to exchange for IDR. This method offers the best exchange rate. If you have AUD, you can directly bring AUD to Bali for exchange. As Australia is a major source of tourists for Bali, the exchange rate is comparable to USD.

Why is it better to exchange TWD to USD, then USD to IDR? Check out the explanation [here](/blog/2025-08-13-689c380bfd8978000178bb5e/)

Most tourists handle currency exchange upon arrival in Bali. You can ask a trusted private driver to take you to a reputable Money Changer they know - this is the best way to avoid scams!

If you're choosing your own exchange method, here are your options:

- **Bank exchange**: Bringing USD to a local bank is a safe and reliable method. Banks offer relatively stable rates with low fees. Major cities and tourist areas in Bali have bank branches for convenient exchange.

- **Money Changer**: Bali's tourist areas have many specialized currency exchange shops. When choosing a Money Changer, make sure to select a reputable shop with favorable rates, and check for any additional fees.

## **Indonesia/Bali Currency Exchange FAQ**

### **1. What currency is used in Bali?**

As part of Indonesia, Bali uses the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Only IDR is accepted for transactions, so it's recommended to have cash ready.

### **2. Should I bring USD to Bali?**

Yes, it's recommended to exchange your local currency for USD before your trip, as USD generally offers better exchange rates in Bali. If you have Australian dollars, you can also bring AUD directly to Bali for exchange, as AUD also has favorable rates. Reference: [Indonesia/Bali Currency Exchange: Direct Exchange vs. Exchanging USD First, Which is Better?](/blog/2025-08-13-689c380bfd8978000178bb5e/)

### **3. Where is the best place to exchange currency?**

- **Money Changer**: Choose reputable money changers, as they often offer better rates with low or no fees.
- **Banks**: Banks provide stable and secure exchange rates but may involve waiting in line.
- **ATM Withdrawals**: Convenient but may incur fees; it's recommended to use bank cards with no overseas transaction fees.

Avoid exchanging currency at the airport, as the rates are usually poor.

### **4. How to Identify Reputable Money Changers?**

- Choose authorized money changers with the green shield-shaped **PVA Berizin** logo.
- Check Google reviews and select establishments with high ratings and numerous comments.
- Avoid places offering exceptionally favorable rates, as they are almost certainly scams.
- **No commission** is another way to identify legitimate operators. Before exchanging, ask "Any commission?" If they say yes, consider going elsewhere.
- [Here](https://moneychangerbali.com/) is a list of **Authorized Money Changers** licensed by **Bank Indonesia**.

### **5. What to Pay Attention to When Exchanging Currency?**

- Count the cash on the spot and ensure the amount is correct before leaving.
- Avoid exchanging money at night or in remote areas.
- Be aware of any additional fees or commissions.
- Keep the exchange receipt for future reference.

### **6. Can I Use Credit Cards or Mobile Payments?**

Yes, many shops, restaurants, and hotels in Bali accept credit cards (Visa, Mastercard) and mobile payments (such as GoPay, OVO). However, cash remains the primary payment method at small vendors or in remote areas.

### **7. Is ATM Withdrawal Convenient?**

Bali has an extensive ATM network, with machines available in major cities and tourist areas. When using an international credit or debit card for withdrawals, please note:

- Confirm that your bank card supports overseas withdrawals.
- Be aware of your bank's fees and exchange rate differences.
- Choose ATMs with security monitoring; bank-owned ATMs are the safest. Avoid withdrawing money at night.

### **8. Will I Be Charged Fees When Exchanging Currency?**

- **Money Changers:** Some shops may charge fees, so it's best to ask beforehand.

- **Banks:** Fees are usually lower, but the exchange rate may not be as favorable as at money changers.

- **ATM Withdrawals:** You may incur two types of fees: a local bank fee and an overseas withdrawal fee from your card issuer.
