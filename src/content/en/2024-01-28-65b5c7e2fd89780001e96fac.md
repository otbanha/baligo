## Currency Exchange Methods

**Exchange USD Before Departure**: The most important thing is to exchange your local currency for USD before departure, then bring USD to Indonesia/Bali and exchange it for Indonesian Rupiah. The reason for this is that the exchange rate is the best. If you have Australian dollars, you can directly bring AUD to Bali and exchange it for Indonesian Rupiah. Since Australia has always been a major source of tourists to Bali, the exchange rate is just as good as USD.

Why is it better to exchange TWD to USD, then USD to IDR? Check out the explanation [here](/blog/2025-08-13-689c380bfd8978000178bb5e/)

Most tourists handle currency exchange upon arrival in Bali. You can ask a trusted private driver to take you to a reputable Money Changer they know - this is the best way to avoid scams!

If you're choosing your own exchange method, here are your options:

- **Bank Exchange**: Bringing USD to a local bank for exchange is a safe and reliable method. Banks offer relatively stable exchange rates and lower fees. Major cities and tourist areas in Bali have bank branches, making it convenient to exchange money.
- **Money Changer**: There are many specialized money changers in Bali's tourist areas that offer foreign currency exchange services. When choosing a money changer, make sure to select a reputable one with favorable rates, and be aware of any additional fees or charges.
- **_ATM Withdrawal_: Bali has an extensive ATM network where you can withdraw money using international credit or debit cards. This is a convenient method and usually uses the day's real-time exchange rate (close to the bank's mid-rate). However, the _drawbacks are_:

1. Taiwanese banks usually charge an **overseas withdrawal fee**: approximately USD$2.42～4.84 per transaction
2. Plus a **1.5%～2% foreign transaction fee** (depending on the issuing bank)
3. Local ATMs in Bali may also charge an additional **machine usage fee** (around IDR 50,000～75,000 per transaction)
4. Some ATMs have withdrawal limits, requiring multiple transactions and thus increasing fees
   It is recommended to withdraw larger amounts at once to reduce the number of fees, confirm your bank's overseas withdrawal fees before departure, and choose reliable ATMs from major banks like BCA or Mandiri.

## **Indonesia/Bali Currency Exchange FAQ**

### **1. What currency is used in Bali?**

As part of Indonesia, Bali uses the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Only IDR is accepted for transactions, so it's recommended to have cash ready.

### **2. Do I need to bring USD to Bali?**

Yes, it's recommended to exchange your local currency for USD before your trip, as USD generally offers better exchange rates in Bali. If you have Australian dollars, you can also bring AUD directly to Bali for exchange, as AUD also has favorable rates. Reference: [Indonesia/Bali Currency Exchange: Direct Exchange vs. Exchanging USD First, Which is Better?](/blog/2025-08-13-689c380bfd8978000178bb5e/)

### **3. Where is the best place to exchange currency?**

- **Money Changer:** Choose reputable money changers, which usually offer better rates with low or no fees.
- **Banks:** Banks provide stable and secure exchange rates but may involve waiting in line.
- **ATM Withdrawals:** Convenient but may incur fees. It's recommended to use bank cards with no overseas transaction fees.

Avoid exchanging currency at the airport, as the rates are usually poor.

### **4. How to identify reputable money changers?**

- Look for authorized money changers with the green shield-shaped **PVA Berizin** logo.
- Check Google reviews and choose establishments with high ratings and numerous comments.
- Avoid places offering extremely favorable rates, as they are almost certainly scams.
- **No commission** is another way to identify legitimate operators. Before exchanging, ask "Any commission?" If they say yes, consider going elsewhere.
- [Here](https://moneychangerbali.com/) is a list of "Authorized Money Changers" licensed by **Bank Indonesia**.

### **5. What should I pay attention to when exchanging currency?**

- Count the cash on the spot and ensure the amount is correct before leaving.
- Avoid exchanging money at night or in remote areas.
- Be aware of any additional fees or commissions.
- Keep the exchange receipt for future reference.

### **6. Can I use credit cards or mobile payments?**

Yes, many shops, restaurants, and hotels in Bali accept credit cards (Visa, Mastercard) and mobile payments (such as GoPay, OVO). However, cash remains the primary payment method at small vendors or in remote areas.

### **7. Is ATM withdrawal convenient?**

Bali has an extensive ATM network, with machines available in major cities and tourist areas. When using an international credit or debit card for withdrawals, please note:

- Confirm that your bank card supports overseas withdrawals.
- Be aware of your bank's fees and exchange rate differences.
- Choose ATMs with security monitoring; bank-owned ATMs are the safest. Avoid withdrawing money at night.

### **8. Will I be charged a fee when exchanging currency?**

- **Money changers:** Some shops may charge a fee, so it's recommended to ask before exchanging.
- **Banks:** Fees are usually lower, but the exchange rate might not be as favorable as at money changers.
- **ATM withdrawals:** You may incur two fees: a local bank fee and your card issuer's international withdrawal fee.

### **9. How much cash should I prepare?**

- **ATM Withdrawals:** You may incur two types of fees: a local bank fee and an overseas withdrawal fee from your card issuer.

### **12. Does the exchange rate in Bali fluctuate?**

Yes, exchange rates fluctuate with the market. It's advisable to monitor the rate trends before your trip and exchange currency when rates are favorable. You can use rate tracking tools like XE.com or OANDA to stay updated on the latest rates.

No, Bali only accepts Indonesian Rupiah as the transaction currency. Even though some merchants might accept US dollars, the exchange rate is usually less favorable for tourists.

Bali is a highly developed tourist destination, and staff at legitimate money changers can almost always communicate in **English**. Speaking English alone is perfectly fine. Here are some useful phrases:
