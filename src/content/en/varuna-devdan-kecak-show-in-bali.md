# Bali After Dark: Four Must-See Shows for the Whole Family, from Kids to Grandparents

When you think of Bali, it's all about sun, sand, and rice terraces. But did you know? As the sun sets, this mythical island truly begins its most stunning visual feast! Today, we're introducing four completely different 'grand shows'—from deep-sea fairy tales and aerial dances to fiery human epics. Perfect not just for couples and friends, but even grandparents and little ones will be captivated. Take notes!

***

## 🐠 Varuna Show: The Underwater World — A Magical Aqua Theater That Will Wow Grandma and Grandpa

**Best for: All ages, especially recommended for families with young children or seniors with limited mobility**

If your little ones are curious about the ocean, or if your elders prefer not to spend long hours outdoors in the wind, the **Varuna Show** in the Ubud area is the top choice! This is Bali's newest indoor aqua theater, where a dreamy story about marine ecology unfolds in a comfortable, air-conditioned space.

At the center of the stage is a giant transparent water tank. Dancers and acrobats glide through the water, accompanied by dazzling light projections and live music, bringing the underwater world right before your eyes. Even better, the theater-style tiered seating offers unobstructed views from every angle. Seniors don't have to walk far or sit in the sun to enjoy a 'dry' version of an underwater ballet. Kids' eyes will light up when they see the 'mermaids' and glowing jellyfish appear!

**Helpful Tip**: The show runs for about 60 minutes with no intermission. It's a good idea to take the family to the restroom before the show starts.

## 🪄 Devdan Show — A Visually Explosive Indonesian Cultural Carnival

**Best for: Families who love lively, spectacular scenes; kids elementary school age and up will love it most**

If you can only catch one show, many will directly recommend **Devdan—Treasure of the Archipelago**. This signature dinner show in Nusa Dua condenses the cultures of the Indonesian archipelago into a 90-minute lavish musical.

From the drum dances of Sumatra, the elegant court dances of Java, to the jungle rhythms of Borneo and the primal war dances of Papua, **paired with aerial rings, fire effects, giant props, and quick costume changes**, the pace is brisk with no dull moments. Elders will be amazed by the intricate costumes, while kids will scream and laugh at the aerial flips and sudden bursts of sparks.

**Family-friendly highlights**: On-site children's seating, interactive warm-up before the show, and photo ops with dancers afterward—full of family memories.

## 🔥 Kecak Fire Dance at Uluwatu — An Epic Cliffside Spectacle

**Best for: Families with good stamina who enjoy outdoor atmospheres (recommended for ages 6 and up)**

The final experience is Bali's most iconic and irreplicable spectacle—the **Uluwatu Kecak Fire Dance**. As the sun sets, you sit in an open-air theater perched on a cliff, facing the Indian Ocean, while dozens of bare-chested men form a circle and chant 'chak-chak-chak' in unison. No instruments, yet it's utterly mesmerizing.

The story is adapted from the Ramayana, culminating in a fierce battle between the monkey army and the demon king, ending with the 'Fire Dance'—where dancers playing monkeys leap over burning coconut husks, sending sparks flying. Paired with the crashing waves below, even a dad who usually isn't into performances will whip out his phone to capture it all.

**Tips for bringing elders and kids:**

- **Arrive at least 30 minutes early** to snag a seat in the middle-to-upper section for the best view.
- It gets windy on the cliff at dusk, so bring a light jacket for the elders.
- When the torches are lit, there's a wave of heat and smoke; kids sensitive to smoke should sit further back.

## [Bonus Feature] 🦁 GWK Barong Dance — A Story-Driven, Spectacular Lion Dance with an Epic Good vs. Evil Battle

**Best for: Families who love cultural stories and traditional arts; suitable for kids aged 3 and up.**

https://www.instagram.com/reel/DXOd_lRAfrM/?utm_source=ig_web_button_share_sheet

Saving the best for last, the most iconic **Barong Dance** in Bali takes place at the Garuda Wisnu Kencana (GWK) Cultural Park. The Barong, a mythical lion-like creature symbolizing good, is portrayed by dancers in heavy, ornate fur costumes and masks. Through exaggerated movements and expressions, they battle the evil witch Rangda—**the whole performance is like a lion dance with a complete storyline**, with no dialogue, relying entirely on gamelan music and the dancers' expressions to drive the plot.

Elders will marvel at the intricate craftsmanship of the costumes, while children will burst into laughter at the Barong's playful winks, tumbles, and monster-fighting antics. The triumphant ending where good prevails is deeply satisfying! After the show, you can take photos up close with the Barong, and kids often find it hard to leave.

**Family-Friendly Highlights**:

- Spacious venue with shaded seating, protected from sun and rain
- Performance lasts about 1 hour, with a brisk and engaging pace

**Helpful Tips**: The GWK park is huge, so arrive early to also explore the giant Garuda statue; front-row seats offer the best view of mask details, but children sensitive to loud sounds may prefer mid-to-back rows.
