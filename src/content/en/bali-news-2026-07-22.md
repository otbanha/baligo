## ⚠️ Homicide in Denpasar: Travelers Staying in Long-Term Rentals Should Stay Vigilant

A homicide occurred in South Denpasar recently, where a Singaporean man was arrested for allegedly killing his live-in girlfriend. Preliminary investigations suggest the case is a personal crime between acquaintances, not a random attack targeting tourists.

**Traveler Advice:** If you're staying in a villa, Airbnb, or long-term rental and notice anything unusual—such as being unable to reach a travel companion for an extended period, detecting strange odors, or signs of possible violence—immediately notify your accommodation provider or call Indonesian police at 110. Do not attempt to handle it yourself.

## ✈️ New Progress on North Bali International Airport Project, President Urges Acceleration

The North Bali International Airport project has seen new developments recently. According to Detik Bali, representatives of PT Pembangunan Bali Mandiri (Pembari) recently met with Indonesian President Prabowo to discuss the construction plans for the North Bali International Airport. The President expressed a desire to accelerate this major infrastructure project in response to Bali's continued tourism growth and the nearing capacity of Ngurah Rai International Airport.

The report states that the North Bali International Airport has been included in Indonesia's 2025–2029 National Medium-Term Development Plan (RPJMN). It is planned to accommodate large aircraft such as the Boeing 777 and Airbus A380, and will be driven by private investment, without using central or local government budgets.

**Traveler's Tip:** This project is still in the planning and promotion phase. For now, Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) remains the main gateway for international flights to Bali, so travelers don't need to adjust their existing itineraries, but can keep an eye on future construction progress.
