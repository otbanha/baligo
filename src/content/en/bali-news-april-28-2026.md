## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Environmental Response and Energy Dynamics

- **New Rules at Suwung Landfill to Tackle Waste Crisis**: With the **Suwung Landfill** slated for full closure this August, recent garbage collection has hit a standstill, leading to piled-up trash and burning issues on Denpasar streets. Yesterday (4/27), the Ministry of Environment released new response policies: **Suwung Landfill will now only accept organic waste two days per week**. Authorities urge communities to implement source separation to mitigate impacts on air quality and scenery in tourist hotspots.
- **Energy Supply Stability Praised**: After inspecting Denpasar yesterday (4/27), the 12th Commission of Parliament noted that compared to energy-producing regions like Sumatra, **Bali's current electricity supply is extremely stable**, with near-island-wide coverage, sufficient to support growing tourism energy demands.
