This time, I want to share my travel experience in Bali with everyone. Bali, known as the 'Island of the Gods,' attracts visitors from all over the world with its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. From visa processing to car rental services, and from major attractions to restaurants, we experienced the local charm firsthand, although we did encounter some minor hiccups. I hope my sharing can provide some reference and assistance to friends planning to visit Bali, making your journey smoother and more enjoyable.

## Visa:

After seeing many issues with electronic visas (eVOA) in the group, we decided to opt for a visa on arrival. Upon arriving at the airport, we waited in line for about 15-20 minutes before it was our turn, which was acceptable. However, even though the visa processing was relatively quick, baggage claim still took some time, leaving us feeling a bit frustrated😅. So, if you choose to get a visa on arrival, please allow ample time for possible delays.

## Car Rental Driver:

When booking car rental services, be sure not to trust online reviews too much. We booked a service based on a recommendation from a group member on Facebook for 'Brother X's Car Rental Team,' but the actual experience was not as smooth as expected🥲. The assigned driver, 'Special X,' was not very proactive, responded to messages very slowly, and was careless in arranging the itinerary. Every time we arrived at a destination, he wouldn't notify us unless we asked.

The driver said the fee included gasoline and parking, but when we went to the Cliff Highway, we were asked to pay Rp5000 for parking when buying tickets, and we ended up paying it ourselves. Since the driver was always the one communicating with the ticket seller, he couldn't have been unaware of this parking fee. This made us feel that the driver was not honest, which was very dissatisfying😅.

Moreover, on the day of checkout, the driver told us that because it was a holiday, we needed to pay an additional Rp50,000. This left us very confused, as I had confirmed the price twice before departure, and the driver had said there would be no changes. This last-minute price increase was very frustrating. Therefore, when booking car rental services, be sure to carefully check the price and terms to avoid similar situations.

### Jimbaran:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_25e33167db824cfc14c33b417c1d0709.jpg)

Provided by Li Wallis

We didn't choose to eat seafood by the beach in Jimbaran because we heard the prices were very high, with some people spending over Rp1 million on seafood. Instead, we went to Waroeng Pan Yoga for a meal. We ordered a large fish (actually two fish, probably because they ran out of big fish 😅), fried calamari rings, squid skewers, and clams, plus two drinks, all for just over Rp200,000. We had a great time, and the quality was good! However, it might be a Jimbaran specialty - the seafood was a bit charred 🤣.

## Souvenirs:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_101ac5a2786fcaa5db6ac954775e1089.jpg)

Provided by Li Wallis

The souvenirs at Ubud Market were almost identical, and nothing particularly caught our eye. Instead, we had more fun shopping at the supermarket, even though the prices were slightly higher. We stumbled upon Bali Galeria Mall, which was fantastic to explore! It has a supermarket, souvenir shops, and restaurants - everything you could need. We wanted to find a post office to send postcards, but it was already closed 🥲. So, if you plan to visit the post office, remember to go early. In the end, we bought stamps at the airport and found a mailbox.

## Other Tips

- Some restrooms require payment, and in a rush, we had to pay Rp3,000 😅😂

- Prices at the airport are very high, so we recommend buying everything you need, including souvenirs, before heading there. For example, bubble tea costs Rp65,000, a Vietnamese sandwich is Rp120,000, and a large Aqua water bottle is Rp20,000.
