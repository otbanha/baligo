This time, I want to share my travel experiences in Bali with everyone. Bali, known as the 'Island of the Gods,' attracts tourists from all over the world with its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. During this trip, we experienced everything from visa processing, private car services, to various attractions and restaurants, immersing ourselves in the local culture. Of course, we also encountered some minor incidents. I hope my sharing can provide some reference and help for friends planning to visit Bali, making your journey smoother and more enjoyable.

## Visa:

Since we saw many people encountering issues with the electronic visa (eVOA) in the group, we decided to opt for the visa on arrival. Upon arriving at the airport, we waited in line for about 15-20 minutes before it was our turn, which was relatively acceptable. However, even though the visa processing was quick, baggage claim still took some time, so we felt a bit helpless while waiting for our luggage😅. Therefore, if you choose to get a visa on arrival, please allocate sufficient time to handle possible delays.

## Private Car Driver:

When booking private car services, everyone must not overly trust online reviews. We booked a service based on a recommendation from a group member in a Facebook group called 'X Brother Private Car Driver Team,' but the actual situation was not as smooth as expected🥲. The assigned driver, 'Special X,' was not very proactive, responded to messages very slowly, and was careless in arranging the itinerary. Every time we arrived at a destination, he wouldn't notify us proactively; we had to ask to find out.

The driver said the fee included gasoline and parking, but when we went to the Cliff Road, we were asked to pay Rp5000 for parking when purchasing tickets, and this fee ultimately fell on us. Since the driver was always the one communicating with the ticket seller, he couldn't have been unaware of this parking fee. This made us feel that the driver was not honest, which was very dissatisfying😅.

On one occasion, when we were settling the bill, the driver informed us that an additional Rp50,000 would be charged due to it being a holiday. This left us quite confused, as I had specifically confirmed the price twice before departure, and the driver had assured me there would be no changes. This last-minute price hike left us feeling dissatisfied. Therefore, when booking private car services, it's crucial to carefully verify the price and terms to avoid encountering similar situations.

### Jimbaran:

We opted not to dine on seafood by the beach in Jimbaran as we heard prices were steep, with some spending over Rp1 million on seafood. Instead, we ate at Waroeng Pan Yoga, ordering a large fish (actually two, perhaps due to a shortage of big fish😅), fried calamari rings, squid skewers, and clams, plus two drinks, all for just over Rp200,000. ## Souvenirs:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_101ac5a2786fcaa5db6ac954775e1089.jpg)

Li Wallis shares

The souvenirs at Ubud Market are almost identical, with nothing particularly enticing us to buy. Instead, we found more joy browsing the supermarket, even though prices were slightly higher. We unexpectedly discovered Bali Galeria Mall, which was fantastic to explore! It has a supermarket, souvenir shops, and restaurants, everything you could need. We initially wanted to find a post office to send postcards, but it was already closed🥲. So, friends planning to visit the post office, remember to go early. In the end, we bought stamps at the airport and found a mailbox.

## Other Thoughts

- Some restrooms require payment, and when you're in a hurry, you just have to pay the Rp3,000 (~USD$0.20) 😅😂
- Prices at the airport are extremely high, so it's recommended to buy all your necessities and souvenirs before heading there. For example, bubble tea costs Rp65,000 (~USD$4), Vietnamese sandwiches are Rp120,000 (~USD$8), and large Aqua water bottles are Rp20,000 (~USD$1)
- Don't forget to bring reusable bags! We only found out **Bali doesn't provide plastic bags** when shopping at the supermarket, and ended up buying a Rp15,000 (~USD$1) reusable bag - it was really expensive 🥺.
