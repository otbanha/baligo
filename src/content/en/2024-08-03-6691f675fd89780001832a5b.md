This time, I want to share my travel experience in Bali with everyone. Known as the 'Island of the Gods,' Bali attracts visitors from around the world with its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. From visa processing to car rental services, and exploring various attractions and restaurants, we fully immersed ourselves in the local culture. Of course, we also encountered some minor hiccups. I hope my sharing can provide some reference and help for those planning to visit Bali, making your journey smoother and more enjoyable.

## Visa:

After seeing many issues with the electronic visa (eVOA) in group discussions, we decided to opt for the visa on arrival. Upon arriving at the airport, we waited in line for about 15-20 minutes before it was our turn, which was relatively acceptable. However, even though the visa process was quick, baggage claim still took some time, leaving us feeling a bit frustrated 😅. So, if you choose to get a visa on arrival, make sure to allocate enough time to handle potential delays.

## Car Rental Driver:

When booking car rental services, don’t rely too heavily on online reviews. We booked a service based on a recommendation from a group member in a Facebook group called 'X Brother Car Rental Team,' but the actual experience didn’t meet our expectations 🥲. The assigned driver, 'Special X,' was not very proactive, responded to messages slowly, and was careless in arranging our itinerary. Every time we arrived at a destination, he wouldn’t notify us unless we asked.

The driver claimed that the fee included gas and parking, but when we went to the Cliff Road, we were asked to pay Rp5000 for parking, which we ended up covering. Since the driver was the one communicating with the ticket staff, he must have known about this parking fee. This made us feel that the driver wasn’t honest, which was very disappointing 😅.

On one occasion, when we were settling the bill, the driver informed us that an additional Rp50,000 would be charged due to it being a holiday. This left us quite confused, as I had specifically confirmed the price twice before departure, and the driver had assured me there would be no changes. This last-minute price hike left us feeling dissatisfied. Therefore, when booking car rental services, it's essential to carefully verify the price and terms to avoid encountering similar situations.

### Jimbaran:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_25e33167db824cfc14c33b417c1d0709.jpg)

Li Wallis provided

We didn't choose to eat seafood by the beach in Jimbaran because we heard the prices there are very high, with some people spending over Rp1 million on seafood. Instead, we went to Waroeng Pan Yoga for a meal. We ordered a large fish (actually two fish, maybe because they were out of the big ones 😅), fried calamari rings, squid skewers, and clams, plus two drinks, all for just over Rp200,000. We enjoyed our meal, and the quality was good! However, perhaps it's a Jimbaran specialty, the seafood was a bit charred 🤣.

## Souvenirs:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_101ac5a2786fcaa5db6ac954775e1089.jpg)

Li Wallis provided

The souvenirs at Ubud Market were almost all the same, nothing particularly caught our eye to buy. On the contrary, we had more fun shopping at the supermarket, even though the prices were slightly higher. We stumbled upon Bali Galeria Mall, which was great for shopping! It has a supermarket, souvenir shops, and restaurants, everything you could need. We originally wanted to find a post office to send postcards, but it was already closed 🥲. So, friends who want to visit the post office, remember to go early. In the end, we bought stamps at the airport and found a mailbox.

## Other Tips

- Some toilets require payment, and in a hurry, Rp3,000 had to be paid 😅😂
- Prices at the airport are very high, so it's recommended to buy all necessary items and souvenirs before heading to the airport. For example, bubble tea costs Rp65,000, Vietnamese sandwiches are Rp120,000, and a large Aqua water is Rp20,000
- Don't forget to bring a reusable bag! We found out **Bali doesn't provide plastic bags** while shopping at the supermarket, and ended up buying a Rp15,000 reusable bag, which was really expensive 🥺.
