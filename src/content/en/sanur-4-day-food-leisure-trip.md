For this Bali trip, we specifically planned a sea excursion, so we chose to stay in Sanur. It's very close to the harbor, allowing us to avoid waking up too early to catch the boat. After returning from our outing, we could quickly get back to our accommodation to rest—it was incredibly convenient. We stayed here for three nights, choosing the Holiday Inn hotel. The nightly rate was about USD$87, suitable for two adults and one child, with two beds in the room that were both comfortable and spacious.

## Area Highlights

![Alice Li 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f244d738d873e23a6b050e8cb1a13952.jpg)

Alice Li shares:

In Sanur, there are relatively few Asian tourists; it's mostly Western visitors. This area is great for families and those traveling with kids, thanks to its friendly and safe atmosphere. There are dedicated bike paths, and we rented bicycles to ride to the now-famous Icon Bali, enjoying a leisurely time. Icon Bali is home to many boutiques, restaurants, and cafes, making it a wonderful place to relax and enjoy some shopping.

## Shopping & Spending

The shops in this area are mostly traditional rather than modern fashion boutiques, so it never feels crowded, and prices are quite reasonable. We strolled around slowly and felt very relaxed—perfect for unwinding.

For getting around, we mainly used Grab, with fares ranging from about $1 to $3 USD, whether by car or scooter. If you're staying in a narrow alley, I'd recommend taking a scooter for more flexibility.

## Laundry Service

![這家Sunrise Laundry很讚！Alice Li 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fa7b6b629f8919a4797b14dbb1bc91ad.jpg)

This Sunrise Laundry is fantastic! (Courtesy of Alice Li)

There's also a self-service laundromat nearby, which solved our clothing change issues over the past few days. The laundry prices are very affordable—just $4 USD for up to 5 kilograms, with staff washing, drying, and folding for you. However, they don't offer ironing services!

## Massage Experience

As for massages, Sanur also has plenty of options. A one-hour massage typically costs between $13 and $16 USD. We especially loved 'Ur Spa Sanur'—the environment is fantastic, clean, and tidy, but you need to book in advance via WhatsApp. If you're looking for a cheaper option, there's a more traditional massage parlor next door that's $2 to $3 USD less, with a decent atmosphere. No reservation is needed there—you can just walk in.

## Restaurant Recommendations

![這道錫箔紙包魚很特別！Alice Li 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e03b6d2764d647365c7e2940b232a769.jpg)

This fish wrapped in foil is amazing! Provided by Alice Li

For dining, we highly recommend Lilla Warung — their foil-wrapped fish is absolutely delicious and unforgettable. Jepun Sanur is another restaurant we wish we had discovered sooner; we wanted to go back two nights in a row. Next door, there's a famous ice cream shop that always has a long line, but it's definitely worth the wait.
