# 11 Best Seafood Restaurants on Jimbaran Beach

<!-- shuffle-start -->

## **Menega Cafe**

For years, Menega has been one of the most popular beach cafes in Jimbaran. On the surface, it doesn't look much different from other restaurants, but it consistently draws a steady stream of visitors year-round. To snag a good table before the crowds roll in (usually around 5:30 PM), it's best to arrive early. Also, placing your order as soon as possible is key—otherwise, you might face a long wait! Nearly everything on the menu is delicious. The giant prawns, spicy grilled snapper, and clams drenched in a flavorful garlic chili sauce are outstanding! You can also enjoy Jimbaran's sunset from your beachside table, making this a perfect romantic dining spot.

## **Kisik Bar and Grill at Ayana Resort and Spa**

Located within the Ayana Resort on Jimbaran Bay in Bali, Kisik Bar and Grill offers two of our favorite things: fresh, delicious seafood and stunning views of the bay. Kisik Bar and Grill serves a variety of freshly caught seafood, including large crabs, juicy oysters, and squid. The restaurant offers a set menu that includes complimentary appetizers and your choice of seafood. In addition to seafood, they also have meat and vegetarian options. For the best experience, we recommend dining outdoors to enjoy the most beautiful views!

## **Cuca Restaurant**

Cuca is famous as a tapas restaurant, but don't overlook its seafood dishes! Located near the Kayumanis Jimbaran Inn, this restaurant offers three different dining areas: a food bar, a traditional dining area, and a garden lounge. Its location allows the chef to select the freshest catch of the day, offering perfectly grilled prawns, smoked squid, and seafood rice that has become a delicious staple. Though Sundara Bali doesn't bill itself as a seafood restaurant, it consistently tops the list of the best seafood spots in Jimbaran. Located at the Four Seasons Resort, Sundara Bali blends five-star dining with a relaxed beach club vibe. ## **Jimbaran Beach Club**

If you're craving fresh, delicious seafood with stunning views of Jimbaran Bay, we highly recommend dining at Jimbaran Beach Club at Kupu Kupu Jimbaran Suites. Located right on the sands of Jimbaran Bay, it offers a modern dining area and a sunset lounge where you can savor mouthwatering seafood.

## **Warung Nyoman**

**Warung Nyoman** / Nyoman Café is a seafood spot known for its affordable, simple, and fresh offerings. The restaurant serves a variety of fresh seafood, with all dishes prepared only when you order. You can choose live lobster, large prawns, big crabs, and squid, all grilled over hot coals. Must-try items on the menu include crab with sweet soy sauce, braised fish, and fried squid!

## **Lia Café**

If you love seafood and want beautiful views without breaking the bank, you'll likely enjoy Lia Cafe! Located in a seafood dining area away from tourist bus crowds, it's less crowded. This humble restaurant may not look like much, but once you taste the seafood, you'll fall in love! Xiao Jie [dined at **Lia Café** in 2023](https://youtube.com/shorts/5gg10paCxfY), and a ten-person set meal including tax and service charge cost about USD$80, leaving everyone very satisfied!

## **Akua Bali Restaurant**

Akua is not only one of the finest seafood restaurants in Jimbaran Bay, but also a lifestyle venue that blends a bar, beach club, and restaurant! Inspired by Bali's stunning turquoise beaches, Akua is a Spanish restaurant known for delicious tapas and freshly caught seafood. The restaurant offers spacious dining areas with beautiful views of the bay.

## **Made Bagus Café Jimbaran**

This is a popular restaurant located on Jimbaran Beach. They offer various set meal combinations with clearly marked prices, and this place only adds a 10% tax at the end. The food is good and offers great value for money—definitely worth considering for anyone heading to Jimbaran. (Provided by Angel Wu)

## **Wr Mang Ayu ex Waroenk Djadoel**

Many people associate Jimbaran sunset seafood with being 'overpriced and not tasty,' right? But Wr Mang Ayu (formerly Waroenk Djadoel) completely overturns that stereotype. This small eatery was our second visit—when we first came in early 2023, we just ordered a simple meal with beer, but were deeply impressed by the affordable prices and delicious flavors. This time, we returned specifically to enjoy dinner here at sunset, and it didn't disappoint. For a set meal costing around USD$19, we got one lobster, one fish, four grilled prawns, clams, squid skewers, stir-fried vegetables, white rice, and a bottle of water. The lobster wasn't huge, but the flavor was top-notch. The owner's family was warm and welcoming, and paired with the priceless Jimbaran sunset, this meal became the perfect memory of our last night in Bali. (Provided by Li Qiuxiong)

# Tips for Choosing a Restaurant

It's important to note that Jimbaran Beach offers a wide variety of dining options, and most restaurants operate honestly. However, there are indeed a small number of dishonest vendors who try to take advantage of tourists—when they hear customers speaking English, they may bring out a menu with inflated prices. This can happen in some popular tourist spots, so it's wise to be extra careful when choosing a restaurant. Here are some tips to help you avoid such situations:

![選擇餐廳的建議](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_87c31a5431c311fed250d5a9e0ebe4a1.jpg)

1. **Research Restaurants**: Before heading to Jimbaran Beach, it's a good idea to do some online research to find well-reviewed and reputable restaurants. Reading reviews and travel site recommendations can help you identify trustworthy places.
2. **Ask About Prices**: Once seated, don't hesitate to ask about the price range or request to see the menu. This can help prevent any unpleasant surprises.
3. **Compare Prices**: If you feel a price is too high, try comparing it with other restaurants. If there's a big difference between several places, it might be a red flag. A reasonable average cost per person should be between IDR 150,000 and 300,000 (around USD$10 to $20).
4. **Use Translation Tools**: If you're unsure whether you're being treated fairly, use a translation app or tool to communicate and make sure both sides understand the prices and order.
5. **Assess on the Spot**: When choosing where to eat, you can also do a quick on-site evaluation. If you notice a restaurant's prices are significantly higher than similar ones nearby, it may be wise to think twice before sitting down.
