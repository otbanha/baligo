Recently, a group member named Mike Chiu encountered some issues while applying for an Indonesian electronic visa (e-VOA). He sought advice from everyone in our Facebook group. Mike mentioned that he correctly entered his place of birth as "TAIWAN" when filling out the application form and had double-checked the accuracy of his input. However, when he received the final electronic visa, he noticed that his place of birth was displayed as "Talwan", with the "i" mistakenly turned into an "l". Mike was unsure if this was acceptable and asked whether he needed to re-edit the application or how he should proceed.

In response, another traveler, Wei Chih Wen, advised Mike to first note down the electronic visa number, then write to the relevant department explaining the need to correct the place of birth error and patiently wait for a reply. Wei Chih Wen also mentioned that he had encountered a similar situation before and successfully received a corrected response.

Ultimately, Mike Chiu followed the advice and quickly received an official reply, resolving the issue smoothly. This experience reminds everyone that if similar problems are encountered during the visa application process, contacting the official channels for corrections and confirmations can prevent any impact on travel plans.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e14cb6937b166fd9b0d25c773930b373.png)

## What exactly should you do?

Seeking assistance from the Indonesian Immigration Office is the only solution. It is recommended to get help through the Contact Us section on the [Indonesian Immigration Office website](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/) (located at the top right corner of the homepage) during working hours.
