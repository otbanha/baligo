## Indonesia's Rainbow Sky Phenomenon

These days, a spectacular rainbow cloud phenomenon has been trending on Indonesian social media!

No filters, no editing—just a real 'rainbow cloud' appearing in the sky.

On May 1, 2026, many people in Jonggol, Bogor, Indonesia looked up and saw colorful clouds resembling the aurora—shades of pink, turquoise, lavender, and gold intertwined, soft yet strikingly vivid.

This is not a myth but a relatively rare atmospheric phenomenon: **iridescent clouds**.

When sunlight passes through a cloud containing a large number of uniformly sized tiny water droplets or ice crystals, the light undergoes **diffraction**, splitting into colors of different wavelengths, much like the iridescence on the surface of a soap bubble. Unlike a typical rainbow, it doesn’t necessarily form an arc but often appears as irregular patches of colorful clouds, blooming along the edges of the cloud.

This celestial spectacle lasted only briefly but quickly ignited Indonesian social platforms. From Twitter to TikTok and Instagram, comments like 'healed by the sky' flooded in—amid busy days, such unexpected natural beauty felt even more moving.

If you happened to look up that day, you surely wouldn’t have been able to tear your eyes away.

(Quick note: While iridescent clouds differ slightly in formation from 'circumhorizontal arcs' or 'iridescent clouds', they are equally among the most magical daytime optical phenomena.)
