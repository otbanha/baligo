## Indonesia's Rainbow Sky Phenomenon

These days, a fascinating rainbow cloud phenomenon has been trending on Indonesian social media!

No filters, no editing—just a real 'rainbow cloud' appearing in the sky.

On May 1, 2026, in Jonggol, Bogor, Indonesia, many people looked up to see colorful clouds resembling the aurora—shades of pink, turquoise, lavender, and gold, soft yet strikingly vivid.

This isn’t a myth but a relatively rare atmospheric phenomenon: **iridescent clouds**.

When sunlight passes through a large number of uniformly sized tiny water droplets or ice crystals in the clouds, the light undergoes **diffraction**, splitting into different wavelengths of color, much like the iridescence on the surface of a soap bubble. Unlike a typical rainbow, it doesn’t necessarily form an arc and often appears as irregular patches of color, blooming along the edges of the clouds.

This celestial spectacle lasted only briefly but quickly ignited Indonesian social platforms. From Twitter and TikTok to Instagram, comments like 'healed by the sky' flooded in—amid busy days, such unexpected natural beauty feels even more moving.

If you happened to look up that day, you surely wouldn’t have been able to tear your eyes away.

(Quick note: While iridescent clouds differ slightly in formation from 'circumhorizontal arcs' or 'colored clouds,' they are equally among the most enchanting daytime optical phenomena.)
