## 🌞 What's the Weather Like in Bali During Summer Vacation?

July and August during summer vacation mark Bali's dry season, with almost no rainfall. Even if occasional showers occur, they are usually brief and pass quickly, rarely disrupting travel plans. These months are considered among the most comfortable for tourism throughout the year, allowing outdoor activities to proceed smoothly almost every day.

https://youtu.be/MdQYUy31LiY?si=_oj6pEam_FE87pau

The average temperature ranges between 27-30°C, but due to humidity and sunlight, it can feel even hotter. If traveling with children or elderly family members, it's advisable to schedule a 'heat-avoidance break' around midday each day. Returning to the hotel or staying in air-conditioned rooms during this time will make the experience much more comfortable.

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Which Area in Bali is Best for Families with Kids?

During summer vacations, family travelers make up a significant portion of tourists. Many resorts in Bali feature Kids Clubs and water slides as their main attractions, allowing parents to relax without having to plan additional activities for their children.

We've specially compiled two resources: '[Hotels with Kids Clubs](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-05-14-66405077fd89780001f23b72/)' and '[Bali Resorts with Water Slides](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/)', making it convenient for families traveling with children to reference directly.

As for area selection, I most recommend the following three locations:

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but actually an excellent destination for family travel. With calm waves, clean beaches, and none of the party crowds found in Seminyak, it's just a short walk to the shore and remains peaceful at night. If you're traveling with young children or elderly family members, Sanur is a reassuringly safe and comfortable choice.

## 🏡 Accommodation Options

### Perfect for Family Getaways: Private Villa Rentals

Three generations living under one roof - that's what true family reunion is all about!

If you're planning a multi-generational family trip this summer with several households, opting for a **private villa** is actually a smarter and more thoughtful choice than booking two or three hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why do I say that? Because the benefits of renting an entire villa are far greater than you might imagine:

**✔ The whole family stays together without running between rooms**
Even if you book the same hotel, grandparents might be on the third floor while the kids are on the seventh, making it time-consuming just to gather everyone. With a private villa, the living room, kitchen, and pool are all in one place. You can chat anytime, watch the kids play in the water, and even enjoy a movie together in the living room at night, truly capturing the feeling of 'togetherness'.

**✔ High Privacy, Free from Other Guests' Disturbances**
Elderly family members dislike noisy environments, and children might worry about disturbing others—booking a private villa perfectly solves these concerns. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively yours, so kids can splash around freely without worrying about bothering neighboring guests.

**✔ Flexible Dining, Better Accommodation for Elders' Dietary Needs**
Many elders may not be accustomed to eating Indonesian cuisine for several consecutive days. Staying in a private villa with a kitchen allows you to cook porridge, blanch vegetables, or prepare simple dishes using local ingredients. Breakfast isn't restricted to hotel hours either—eat whenever you want, making it very convenient for elders.

**✔ Great Value When Split Among Group**
Bali offers a wide selection of private villas ranging from three to six or more bedrooms. When the cost is divided among families, it often works out to be more affordable than staying at a five-star hotel, while providing more space and greater freedom.
