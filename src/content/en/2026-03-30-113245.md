## 🌞 What's the Weather Like in Bali During Summer Vacation?

July and August during summer vacation mark Bali's dry season, with almost no rainfall. Even if you occasionally encounter rain, it's mostly brief showers that pass quickly, rarely affecting your travel plans. These months are considered among the most comfortable for tourism throughout the year, allowing outdoor activities to proceed smoothly almost every day.

https://youtu.be/MdQYUy31LiY?si=_oj6pEam_FE87pau

The average temperature ranges between 27–30°C, but due to humidity and sunlight, it can feel even hotter and more stifling. If traveling with children or elderly family members, it's advisable to schedule a 'heat avoidance break' around midday each day—returning to the hotel to rest or staying in air-conditioned rooms will make things much more comfortable.

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Which Area in Bali is Best for Families with Kids?

During summer vacations, family travelers make up a significant portion of tourists. Many resorts in Bali feature Kids Clubs and water slides as their main attractions, allowing parents to relax without needing to plan additional activities for their children.

As for area selection, I most recommend the following three places:

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but actually makes for an excellent family-friendly destination. With calm waves, clean beaches, and none of the party crowds found in Seminyak, it's just a short walk to the shoreline and remains peaceful at night. If you're traveling with young children or elderly family members, Sanur is a reassuringly safe and comfortable choice.

## 🏡 Accommodation Options

### Family Travel? Book a Private Villa

Three generations living together under one roof - that's what true family reunion is all about!

If you're planning a multi-generational family trip this summer with several families, opting for a **private villa** is actually a smarter and more thoughtful choice than booking two or three hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why do I say that? Because the benefits of booking an entire villa far exceed what you might imagine:

**✔ The whole family stays together without running between rooms**
Even if you book rooms in the same hotel, with grandparents on the third floor and kids on the seventh floor, just gathering everyone takes forever. With a private villa, the living room, kitchen, and pool are all in the same building. You can chat anytime, watch the kids play in the water, and even enjoy a movie together in the living room at night. It truly feels like a 'family reunion.'

**✔ High privacy, free from other tourists' disturbances**
Elderly family members dislike noisy environments, and children might worry about disturbing others—renting a private villa completely solves this issue. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively yours, so kids can splash around to their heart's content without worrying about bothering neighboring guests.

**✔ Flexible Dining Options Cater Better to Seniors' Dietary Needs**
Many seniors find it challenging to adapt to Indonesian cuisine for several consecutive days. Staying in a private villa with a kitchen allows you to prepare congee, blanched vegetables, or simple dishes using local ingredients. Breakfast isn't restricted to hotel hours either—you can eat whenever suits you, making it exceptionally senior-friendly.

**✔ Great value when costs are split**
Bali offers a wide range of private villas, from three-bedroom to six-bedroom or more. When the cost is divided among families, it often works out cheaper than staying in a five-star hotel, while providing more space and greater freedom.
