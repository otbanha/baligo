## 🌞 What's the Weather Like in Bali During Summer Vacation?

July and August, during summer vacation, mark Bali's dry season with almost no rainfall. Even if occasional showers occur, they are usually brief and quickly pass, rarely disrupting travel plans. These months are considered among the most comfortable for tourism throughout the year, allowing outdoor activities to proceed smoothly almost every day.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774870951644-f79d30d6471d3e80da7d9e4791f7771f.jpg)

The average temperature ranges between 27–30°C, but due to humidity and sunlight, it can feel even more stifling. If traveling with children or elderly family members, it's advisable to schedule a 'heat avoidance period' around midday each day—returning to the hotel to rest or staying in air-conditioned rooms will make things much more comfortable.

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but actually makes for an excellent family-friendly destination. With calm waves, clean beaches, and none of the party crowds found in Seminyak, it's just a short walk to the shore and remains peaceful at night. If you're traveling with young children or elderly family members, Sanur is a reassuringly safe and comfortable choice.

## 🏡 Accommodation Options

### Family Getaway? Go for a Private Villa

Three generations living together - that's what true family reunion is all about!

If you're planning a multi-generational family trip this summer with several households, opting for a **private villa** is actually a smarter and more thoughtful choice than booking two or three hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why do I say that? Because the benefits of renting an entire villa are far greater than you might imagine:

**✔ The whole family stays together without running between rooms**
Even if you book rooms in the same hotel, with grandparents on the third floor and kids on the seventh floor, just gathering everyone takes forever. With a private villa, the living room, kitchen, and pool are all in the same building. You can chat anytime, watch the kids play in the water, and even enjoy a movie together in the living room at night — truly capturing the essence of 'family reunion.'

**✔ High Privacy, Free from Other Guests' Disturbances**
Elderly family members dislike noisy environments, and children are afraid of disturbing others—renting a private villa completely solves this issue. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively yours, so even if the kids are splashing around energetically, there's no need to worry about bothering neighboring guests.

**✔ Flexible Dining Options, Catering Better to Elders' Dietary Needs**
Many elders may not be accustomed to eating Indonesian cuisine for several consecutive days. Staying in a private villa with a kitchen allows you to prepare congee, blanched vegetables, or simple dishes using local ingredients. Breakfast isn't restricted to hotel hours either—you can eat whenever you want, making it very accommodating for elders.

**✔ Great value when costs are split**
Bali offers a wide range of private pool villas, from three-bedroom to six-bedroom or larger options. When the cost is divided among families, it often works out to be more affordable than staying at a five-star hotel, while providing more space and greater freedom.
