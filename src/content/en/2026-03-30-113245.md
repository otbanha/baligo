## 🌞 What’s the Weather Like in Bali During Summer Break?

July and August fall in Bali’s dry season, with almost no rain. Even if you encounter occasional showers, they’re usually brief and won’t disrupt your plans. It’s one of the most comfortable months for travel, with outdoor activities almost always feasible.

Average temperatures range between 27–30°C, but the humidity and sunshine can make it feel hotter. If traveling with kids or seniors, it’s wise to schedule a midday "heat break"—returning to your hotel or staying in air-conditioned spaces will make the day much more comfortable.

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Where’s the Best Area in Bali for Families with Kids?

During summer vacations, family travelers make up a significant portion of tourists. Many resorts in Bali already feature Kids Clubs and water slides as their main selling points, so parents don’t need to worry about extra planning.

We’ve compiled two helpful resources: "[Which Hotels Have Kids Clubs](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-05-14-66405077fd89780001f23b72/)" and "[Bali Resorts with Water Slides](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/)" for families traveling with kids to easily reference.

As for choosing the best area, here are our top three recommendations:

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but is actually perfect for family travel. With calm waves, clean beaches, and no party crowds like Seminyak, it’s just a short walk to the shore and remains quiet at night. If you’re traveling with young kids or elderly family members, Sanur is a reassuring choice.

### 📍 Seminyak

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867278224-cassie-gallegos-6wcwcpwmrjy.jpeg)

Seminyak’s advantage lies in having "everything"—shopping, restaurants, beach clubs, and high-quality resorts are all available. If parents also want to enjoy their own vacation time without the entire itinerary revolving around the kids, Seminyak can balance both adult and children’s needs.

### 📍 Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867612552-tegerarang.jpeg)

Ubud offers a completely different experience. Here, there are no beaches, but instead, you'll find rice terraces, culture, forests, and plenty of family-friendly activities—and many kids actually have more fun here than at the beach because there are so many experiences to participate in.

### 📍 Nusa Dua

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775130814476-2893e5d6-29ad-4199-8041-45c2ab39bf47.jpg)

Nusa Dua is the most hassle-free choice for family vacations in Bali. This area is a closed-off resort zone with smooth, wide roads, an extremely safe and quiet environment, perfect for strolling with a stroller. The beaches here have gentle waves, allowing kids to safely play in the sand and water. The area is densely packed with five-star hotels, most of which offer excellent kids' clubs and water play facilities, enabling the whole family to start their 'staycation' mode as soon as they check in.

## 🏡 Accommodation Options

### Private Villas Are Ideal for Family Trips

Staying together is what makes a true family reunion!

If this summer trip involves multiple generations and families, booking a **private villa** is smarter and more thoughtful than reserving several hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why? Because private villas offer far more benefits than you might think:

**✔ The whole family stays together, no need to run between rooms**
Even if you book rooms in the same hotel, with grandparents on the 3rd floor and kids on the 7th floor, just gathering everyone can take forever. A private villa, however, has the living room, kitchen, and pool all in one place. You can chat anytime, watch the kids play in the water, and even enjoy a movie together in the living room at night — truly feeling the sense of 'togetherness.'

**✔ High privacy, free from other guests' disturbances**
Elderly family members dislike noisy environments, and kids might worry about disturbing others — a private villa solves this perfectly. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively yours, so kids can splash around without worrying about bothering neighbors.

**✔ Flexible dining, easier to cater to elders' dietary needs**
Many elders aren’t used to eating Indonesian food for several days in a row. A private villa comes with a kitchen, allowing you to cook porridge, blanch vegetables, or prepare simple meals with local ingredients. Breakfast isn’t tied to hotel schedules either — eat whenever you want, making it very elder-friendly.

**✔ Great value when split among families**
Bali offers a wide range of private villas, from three-bedroom to six-bedroom options. When the cost is split among families, it often turns out to be more affordable than staying at a five-star hotel, while offering more space and greater freedom.
