## 🌞 What's the Weather Like in Bali During Summer Vacation?

July and August, during summer vacation, mark Bali's dry season with almost no rainfall. Even if you occasionally encounter rain, it's usually a brief shower that quickly passes, hardly affecting your travel plans. These months are considered some of the most comfortable for tourism throughout the year, allowing outdoor activities to proceed smoothly almost every day.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774870951644-f79d30d6471d3e80da7d9e4791f7771f.jpg)

The average temperature ranges between 27–30°C, but due to humidity and sunlight, it can feel even hotter and more stifling. If traveling with children or elderly family members, it's advisable to schedule a 'heat avoidance period' around midday each day — returning to the hotel for rest or staying in air-conditioned spaces will make the experience much more comfortable.

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Where's the Best Area in Bali to Visit with Kids?

Among summer vacation travelers, families with children make up a significant portion. Many resorts in Bali feature Kids Clubs and water slides as their main attractions, allowing parents to relax without needing to plan additional activities.

As for area selection, I highly recommend the following three locations:

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but actually an ideal destination for family travel. With calm waves, clean beaches, and far from the party crowds of Seminyak, it's just a short walk to the shore and remains peaceful at night. If you're traveling with young children or elderly family members, Sanur is a reassuring choice.

## 🏡 Accommodation Options

### Top Choice for Family Trips: Private Villa Rentals

Three generations living under one roof - that's what true family togetherness is all about!

If you're planning a multi-generational family trip this summer with several families, opting for a **private villa** is actually a smarter and more thoughtful choice than booking two or three hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why do I say that? Because the benefits of renting a private villa are far greater than you might imagine:

**✔ Stay together as a family without running between rooms**
Even if you book rooms in the same hotel, grandparents might be on the third floor while the kids are on the seventh, making it time-consuming just to gather everyone. With a private villa, the living room, kitchen, and pool are all in one place. You can chat anytime, watch the kids play in the water, and even enjoy a movie together in the living room at night, truly capturing the feeling of 'togetherness.'

**✔ High privacy, free from interference by other travelers**
Seniors dislike noisy environments, and children are afraid of disturbing others—booking a private villa completely solves this issue. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively for your group, so kids can splash around without worrying about bothering neighboring guests.

**✔ Flexible Dining Options, Catering Better to Elders' Dietary Needs**
Many elders may not be accustomed to eating Indonesian cuisine for several consecutive days. With a private villa that includes a kitchen, you can easily prepare congee, blanched vegetables, or cook simple dishes using local ingredients. Breakfast isn't restricted to hotel hours either—eat whenever you want, making it very accommodating for elders.

**✔ Great Value When Split Among Group**
Bali offers a wide selection of private villas ranging from three to six or more bedrooms. When the cost is divided among families, it often turns out to be more economical than staying at a five-star hotel, while providing more space and greater freedom.
