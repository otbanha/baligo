Honestly, there’s no straightforward answer—but there are preparations you can make to ensure a smoother and smarter trip. ## 🌞 What’s the Weather Like in Bali During Summer?

July and August mark Bali’s dry season, with almost no rain. Even if you encounter occasional showers, they’re usually brief and won’t disrupt your plans. These months are among the most comfortable for travel, with outdoor activities almost always feasible.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774870951644-f79d30d6471d3e80da7d9e4791f7771f.jpg)

Average temperatures range between 27–30°C, but humidity and sunlight can make it feel hotter. If traveling with kids or seniors, consider scheduling a midday "heat break"—returning to your hotel or staying in air-conditioned spaces for comfort.

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Where’s the Best Area to Stay in Bali with Kids?

Families make up a significant portion of summer travelers to Bali. Many resorts here feature Kids Clubs and water slides as main attractions, making it easier for parents to relax without extra planning.

We’ve compiled two handy resources: "[Hotels with Kids Clubs](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-05-14-66405077fd89780001f23b72/)" and "[Bali Resorts with Water Slides](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/)" to help families with kids find the perfect stay.

When it comes to choosing an area, here are the top three recommendations:

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but is actually perfect for family travel. With calm waves, clean beaches, and no party crowds like Seminyak, it's just a short walk to the shore, and the nights are peaceful. If you're traveling with young children or elderly family members, Sanur is a reassuring choice.

### 📍 Seminyak

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867278224-cassie-gallegos-6wcwcpwmrjy.jpeg)

Seminyak's strength lies in having 'everything'—shopping, restaurants, beach clubs, and high-quality resorts are all readily available. If parents also want to enjoy their own vacation time without the entire trip revolving around the kids, Seminyak balances both adult and child needs.

> 👉 For detailed planning, see: [Bali Family Travel Guide for Summer/Winter Holidays: A Comprehensive Guide to Make Family Trips Easier – Seminyak Edition](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2025-02-15-67ac4321fd897800015b9a11/)

### 📍 Ubud

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867612552-tegerarang.jpeg)

Ubud offers a completely different experience. There are no beaches here, but instead, you'll find rice terraces, culture, forests, and plenty of family-friendly activities—many kids actually enjoy Ubud more than the beach because there are so many experiences to participate in.

## 🏡 Accommodation Choices

### Family Trips Are Best with Private Villas

Living together across three generations is what true family reunions are all about!

If this summer vacation involves multiple families spanning three generations traveling together, opting for a **private villa** is actually a smarter and more thoughtful choice than booking two or three hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why is that? Because the benefits of a private villa far exceed what you might imagine:

**✔ The whole family stays together, no need to run between rooms**
Even if booked at the same hotel, with grandparents on the third floor and kids on the seventh, just gathering everyone can take forever. A private villa, however, has the living room, kitchen, and pool all in one place, allowing for spontaneous chats, watching the kids play in the water, and even movie nights in the living room, truly capturing the essence of 'family reunion.'

**✔ High privacy, free from other travelers' disturbances**
Elders dislike noisy environments, and kids are often worried about disturbing others—a private villa completely solves this issue. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively yours, so kids can splash around without worrying about bothering neighbors.

**✔ Flexible dining, better catering to elders' dietary needs**
Many elders aren't accustomed to eating Indonesian food for several days in a row. A private villa comes with a kitchen, allowing you to cook porridge, blanch vegetables, or prepare simple meals with local ingredients. Breakfast isn't tied to hotel schedules either—eat whenever you want, making it very elder-friendly.

**✔ Shared costs mean incredible value**
Bali offers a wide range of private villas, from three-bedroom to six-bedroom options and beyond. When the cost is split among families, it often turns out cheaper than staying at a five-star hotel, while providing more space and greater freedom.
