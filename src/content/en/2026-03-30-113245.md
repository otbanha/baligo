## 🌞 What's the Weather Like in Bali During Summer Vacation?

July and August, during the summer vacation, mark Bali's dry season, with almost no rainfall. Even if you occasionally encounter rain, it's mostly brief showers that quickly pass, hardly affecting your itinerary. These months are considered among the most comfortable for tourism all year round, allowing outdoor activities to proceed smoothly almost every day.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774870951644-f79d30d6471d3e80da7d9e4791f7771f.jpg)

The average temperature ranges between 27–30°C, but it feels even hotter due to humidity and sunlight. If traveling with children or elderly family members, it's advisable to schedule a 'heat avoidance break' around midday each day. Returning to the hotel or staying in an air-conditioned room during this time will make the experience much more comfortable.

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Which Area in Bali is Best for Families with Kids?

Summer vacation travelers often include a large proportion of families with children. Many resorts in Bali feature Kids Clubs and water slides as their main attractions, allowing parents to relax without needing to arrange additional activities.

We've specially compiled two resources: '[Hotels with Kids Clubs](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-05-14-66405077fd89780001f23b72/)' and '[Bali Resorts with Water Slides](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/)', making it convenient for families traveling with children to access directly.

As for area selection, the following three places are most recommended:

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but actually an excellent area for family travel. With calm waves, clean beaches, and no party crowds like Seminyak, it's just a short walk to the shore and remains quiet at night. If you're traveling with young children or elderly family members, Sanur is a reassuring choice.

## 🏡 Accommodation Options

### Family Trips? Go for a Private Villa

Three generations living under one roof - that's what true family togetherness is all about!

If you're planning a multi-generational family trip this summer with several families, opting for a **private villa** is actually a smarter and more thoughtful choice compared to booking two or three hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why is that? Because the benefits of renting a private villa far exceed what you might imagine:

**✔ The whole family stays together without running between rooms**
Even if you book rooms in the same hotel, with grandparents on the third floor and kids on the seventh, just gathering everyone takes forever. With a private villa, the living room, kitchen, and pool are all in the same building. You can chat anytime, watch the kids play in the water, and even enjoy a movie together in the living room at night—truly capturing the essence of 'togetherness.'

**✔ High Privacy, Free from Other Guests' Disturbances**
Elderly family members dislike noisy environments, and children might worry about disturbing others—booking a private villa perfectly solves these concerns. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively yours, so kids can splash around freely without worrying about bothering neighboring guests.

**✔ Flexible Dining Options Cater Better to Seniors' Dietary Needs**
Many seniors find it challenging to adapt to Indonesian cuisine for several consecutive days. Booking a private villa with a kitchen allows you to prepare congee, blanched vegetables, or simple dishes using local ingredients. Breakfast isn't restricted to hotel hours either—you can eat whenever suits you, making it exceptionally accommodating for seniors.

**✔ Great Value When Split Among Group**
Bali offers a wide selection of private villas ranging from three to six or more bedrooms. When the cost is divided among families, it often works out to be more affordable than staying at a five-star hotel, while providing much more space and freedom.
