Participating in water activities like diving and snorkeling is a top choice for many travelers in Bali. However, since these activities often take place at the beach or on offshore islands, it's impossible to bring bags onboard, let alone a safe. Thus, 'How to store passports, wallets, and phones?' becomes a frequently asked question.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_13ba81f657671652cb965ba7cbdbbb32.jpg)

No worries! Below, we've compiled **6 safe and practical storage methods** to help you enjoy marine activities with confidence!

## **1. Keep Your Passport in the Hotel Safe**

Your passport is your most important identification document abroad. **Never bring it to the beach** or engage in any water activities with it. Most hotels and guesthouses provide safes; it's advisable to lock your passport, flight ticket copies, and extra cash inside.

If you need to register or participate in activities outside, carry a photocopy of your passport or keep a digital photo to avoid losing the original.

---

## **2. Use a Waterproof Bag or Waterproof Phone Case (Limited Use)**

If you must bring your phone or a small amount of cash, consider using a **high-quality waterproof bag (Dry Bag) or waterproof phone case**. These items are also easily purchasable locally in Bali.

However, note that while these products are marketed as waterproof, they **are not suitable for prolonged immersion in water** or deep diving. Avoid keeping important items in them for extended periods.

---

## **3. Utilize Lockers or Storage Boxes During Diving Trips**

Most dive shops or snorkeling companies provide temporary storage space for participants to store items like shoes, towels, and phones. Before using these facilities, confirm their security and avoid placing all valuables in one location.

---

## **4. Have a Trusted Private Driver? Entrust Them with Your Belongings**

If you're traveling with a private driver who is **a familiar and trustworthy local**, consider leaving some valuables (like your phone or cash) in their care.

This is quite common among travelers, especially during long trips (such as a day trip to Nusa Lembongan). However, it's still advisable to first refer to drivers with good reputations in community forums [like this guide](/blog/bali-private-car-drivers-guide/).

---

## **5. Carry Passport Copies and Cloud Backups**

For emergencies, it's recommended to scan and store copies of your passport, visa, and credit cards in the cloud (e.g., Google Drive, iCloud). This way, if anything is lost, you can quickly handle reporting or replacement procedures.

---

## **6. Travel Light to Minimize Risks**

When participating in water activities, only bring essentials like your phone, sunscreen, and a small amount of cash. Avoid wearing expensive watches, jewelry, or carrying designer bags.

The first step to safe travel is **traveling light and reducing temptations**!

## **FAQ**

### **1. Is there storage available during diving trips?**

Yes, most dive shops provide basic storage options like plastic bins or lockers. However, you are responsible for your belongings, so it's best not to bring valuables.

### **2. Can I bring my passport with me?**

It is strongly not recommended. If it accidentally falls into the sea or gets soaked, passport damage can cause significant trouble. Please store it securely in your accommodation's safe.

### **3. Are local drivers really reliable?**

Most travelers find that experienced local private drivers are usually friendly and reliable. However, it's still advisable to choose platforms with reviews and reputation, and avoid handing over all valuables on the first meeting.

### **4. What waterproof gear should I prepare?**

It's recommended to bring a dry waterproof bag, waterproof phone case, sunscreen, and amphibious shoes for convenience and practicality.

### **5. What should I do if I lose an item?**

It's advised to report it to the local police immediately. If you lose your passport, contact the **Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Indonesia (or your country's consulate)** for assistance in replacement.

**Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Jakarta**

- **Location**: Located on the 17th floor of Gedung Artha Graha building in Senayan, Central Jakarta (Jl. Jend. Sudirman Kav. 52-53)
- **Responsibilities**: Covers the entire territory of Indonesia (excluding East Timor), including West Java, Central Java, Jakarta Capital Region, Sumatra, Kalimantan, Papua, and most other areas

**Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Surabaya**

- **Location**: Jl. Indragiri No. 49, Surabaya, East Java
- **Responsibilities**: Specifically responsible for Eastern Indonesia, including East Java, Bali, Lombok, Sulawesi, Maluku Islands, and other islands in Eastern Indonesia
