Engaging in water activities like diving or snorkeling in Bali is a top choice for many travelers. However, since these activities often take place at the beach or on offshore islands, bringing bags on board is usually not possible, let alone safes. Therefore, 'How to store passports, wallets, and phones?' becomes a frequently asked question.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_13ba81f657671652cb965ba7cbdbbb32.jpg)

No worries! Below, we’ve compiled **6 safe and practical storage methods** to help you enjoy marine activities with confidence!

## **1. Keep Your Passport in the Hotel Safe**

Your passport is your most important identification document abroad, **never take it to the beach** or any water activities. Most hotels and guesthouses provide safes; it’s advisable to lock your passport, flight ticket copies, and extra cash inside.

If you need to register or participate in activities outside, carry a photocopy of your passport or have a digital copy saved to avoid losing the original.

---

## **2. Use a Waterproof Bag or Waterproof Phone Case (Limited Use)**

If you must carry your phone or a small amount of cash, it’s recommended to use a **high-quality waterproof bag (Dry Bag) or waterproof phone case**. These items are also easily purchasable locally in Bali.

However, note that while these products are marketed as waterproof, they **are not suitable for prolonged immersion in water** or deep diving. Avoid keeping important items in them for extended periods.

---

## **3. Utilize Storage Lockers or Containers During Diving Trips**

Most dive shops or snorkeling companies provide temporary storage space for participants to store items like shoes, towels, and phones. Before using these facilities, confirm their security and avoid placing all valuables in one location.

---

## **4. Have a Trusted Private Driver? Entrust Them with Your Valuables**

If you're traveling with a private car and your driver is **a familiar and trustworthy local**, consider entrusting them with some of your valuables (e.g., phone, cash).

This is very common among local travelers, especially during long trips (such as a day trip to Nusa Lembongan). However, it’s still recommended to refer to drivers with good reputations in community groups beforehand.

---

## **5. Carry Passport Copies and Cloud Backups**

To prepare for emergencies, scan and store copies of your passport, visa, and credit cards in the cloud (e.g., Google Drive, iCloud). This ensures you can quickly handle reporting or replacement procedures if they are lost.

---

## **6. Travel Light to Reduce Risks**

When participating in water activities, carry only essential items like your phone, sunscreen, and a small amount of cash. Avoid wearing expensive watches, jewelry, or designer bags.

The first step to safe travel is **traveling light and minimizing temptations**!

## **FAQ**

### **1. Is there storage available for belongings during diving trips?**

Yes, most dive shops provide basic storage solutions like plastic bins or lockers. However, you're responsible for your belongings, so it's advised not to bring valuables.

### **2. Can I bring my passport along?**

It's strongly discouraged. If it accidentally falls into the sea or gets soaked, damaging your passport could cause significant inconvenience. Please securely lock it in your accommodation's safe.

### **3. Are local drivers really reliable?**

Most travelers find that experienced local private drivers are generally friendly and reliable. However, it's still recommended to choose platforms with reviews and good reputations, and avoid handing over all valuables upon first meeting.

### **4. What waterproof gear should I prepare?**

It's advisable to bring a dry waterproof bag, a waterproof phone case, sunscreen, and amphibious shoes for convenience and practicality.

### **5. What should I do if I lose something?**

It's recommended to report to the local police immediately. If you lose your passport, contact **the Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Indonesia (or your country's consulate)** for assistance with replacement.

**Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Jakarta**

- **Location**: Located in the Gedung Artha Graha building (Jl. Jend. Sudirman Kav. 52-53, 17th floor) in Senayan, central Jakarta
- **Responsibilities**: Covers the entire territory of Indonesia (excluding East Timor), including West Java, Central Java, the Jakarta Capital Region, Sumatra, Kalimantan, Papua, and most other areas

**Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Surabaya**

- **Location**: Jl. Indragiri No. 49, Surabaya, East Java
- **Responsibilities**: Specifically responsible for eastern Indonesia, including East Java, Bali, Lombok, Sulawesi, the Maluku Islands, and other islands in eastern Indonesia
