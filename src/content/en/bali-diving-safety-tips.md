Participating in marine activities like diving or snorkeling is a top choice for many travelers in Bali. However, since these activities often take place at the beach or on islands, bringing bags onboard is usually not allowed, let alone having access to a safe. Thus, the question 'How should I store my passport, wallet, and phone?' becomes a common concern.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_13ba81f657671652cb965ba7cbdbbb32.jpg)

No worries! Below, we’ve compiled **6 safe and practical storage methods** to help you enjoy your marine adventures with confidence!

## **1. Keep Your Passport in the Hotel Safe**

Your passport is your most important identification document abroad, so **never bring it to the beach** or during any water activities. Most hotels and guesthouses provide safes, and it’s recommended to lock your passport, flight ticket copies, and extra cash inside.

If you need to register or join tours while out, carry a photocopy of your passport or save a photo of it to avoid losing the original.

---

## **2. Use a Waterproof Bag or Waterproof Phone Case (Limited Use)**

If you must bring your phone or a small amount of cash, consider using a **high-quality waterproof bag (Dry Bag) or waterproof phone case**. These items are also easy to purchase locally in Bali.

However, note that while these products are marketed as waterproof, they are **not suitable for prolonged submersion or deep diving**. Avoid keeping important items in them for extended periods.

---

## **3. Utilize Lockers or Storage Boxes During Diving Trips**

Most diving shops or snorkeling companies provide temporary storage spaces for participants to store items like shoes, towels, and phones. Before using them, ensure their security and avoid placing all valuables in one location.

---

## **4. Have a Trusted Private Driver? Entrust Them with Your Belongings**

If you're traveling with a private car and the driver is a **familiar and trustworthy local**, consider leaving some valuables (such as your phone or cash) in their care.

This is quite common among local travelers, especially during long trips (e.g., a day trip to Nusa Lembongan). However, it’s still recommended to refer to drivers with good reputations in community groups beforehand.

---

## **5. Carry Passport Copies and Cloud Backups**

To prepare for emergencies, scan and store copies of your passport, visa, and credit cards in the cloud (e.g., Google Drive, iCloud). This ensures you can quickly handle reporting or replacement procedures if they are lost.

---

## **6. Travel Light, Reduce Risks**

When participating in water activities, carry only essentials like your phone, sunscreen, and a small amount of cash. Avoid wearing expensive watches, jewelry, or designer bags.

The first step to safe travel is to **travel light and minimize temptations**!

## **FAQ**

### **1. Is there storage available during diving trips?**

Yes, most dive shops provide basic storage options like plastic bins or lockers. However, you are responsible for your belongings, so it’s best not to bring valuables.

### **2. Can I bring my passport with me?**

Strongly not recommended. If it accidentally falls into the sea or gets soaked, passport damage can cause major issues. Please store it securely in your accommodation’s safe.

### **3. Are local drivers really reliable?**

Most travelers find that experienced local drivers are usually friendly and dependable. Still, it’s advisable to choose platforms with good reviews and avoid handing over all valuables upon first meeting.

### **4. What waterproof gear should I prepare?**

It’s recommended to bring a dry waterproof bag, a waterproof phone case, sunscreen, and amphibious shoes for convenience and practicality.

### **5. What should I do if I lose an item?**

Report it to the local police immediately. If it’s your passport, contact the **Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Indonesia (or your country’s consulate)** for assistance in replacement.

**Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Jakarta**

- **Location**: Located in the Gedung Artha Graha building (Jl. Jend. Sudirman Kav. 52-53, 17th floor) in Senayan, central Jakarta
- **Responsibilities**: Covers the entire territory of Indonesia (excluding East Timor), including West Java, Central Java, the Jakarta Capital Region, Sumatra, Kalimantan, Papua, and most other regions

**Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Surabaya**

- **Location**: Jl. Indragiri No. 49, Surabaya, East Java
- **Responsibilities**: Specifically responsible for Eastern Indonesia, including East Java, Bali, Lombok, Sulawesi, the Maluku Islands, and other islands in Eastern Indonesia
