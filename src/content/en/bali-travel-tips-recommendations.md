**Bottom Line First**: My stay at two hotels had very different reviews—**Peppers Villa in Seminyak is a strong recommendation (I give it 99 out of 100)** with 56 villas, attentive service, and breakfast that exceeded expectations; **the Hilton in Nusa Dua was a letdown** with a five-star price tag but subpar facilities. If you're choosing accommodation in Seminyak, don't hesitate to pick Peppers; for a five-star stay in Nusa Dua, I suggest doing more research and comparing options.

Bali, this tropical island full of charm, is more than just a vacation destination for me—it's a place where I discover something new every time I visit. On this trip, I tried some luxurious accommodations and a few well-known restaurants, while also gaining a deep appreciation for the challenges of getting around Bali. I hope my experiences here offer some useful tips and inspiration for your own Bali trip.

## Accommodation Experience

During this trip, I stayed at two very different hotels—the luxurious Peppers Villa and the historic Hilton. Each place had its own unique character and highlights, but there were also some unexpected surprises and disappointments. Let me walk you through the details of my stay at both.

### Peppers Villa (3 nights)

![Peppers Seminyak 大推！(June Lin提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6f9f85b8a7491479d13ae7c0c626b00e.jpg)

Peppers Seminyak is highly recommended! (Provided by June Lin)

This time, we chose Peppers Villa, which recently joined the Accor group. Located in the heart of Seminyak, it's super convenient for shopping in town or heading to the beach. The property has 56 villas, each designed for ultimate comfort, offering a luxurious yet cozy feel. The service was impeccable—from check-in to departure, every detail was well taken care of. The rooms were spacious and bright, fully equipped, making it feel as relaxing as being at home. Breakfast was even better than expected, with fresh ingredients and well-prepared dishes. Their in-room dining was also reasonably priced with good quality food, making it a highlight of my stay. Overall, I give this stay a 99 out of 100.

### Hilton Nusa Dua (2 nights)

![Hilton Nusa Dua, 五星的價格，不及格的硬體設施（June Lin 提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_999f7ae586125ba8183e3108a025fce5.jpg)

Hilton Nusa Dua: Five-star price, subpar facilities (photo by June Lin)

Nine years ago, I stayed at this Hilton in the Nusa Dua resort area. This revisit left me disappointed. First, the pool water quality was so poor that even my son refused to swim. Second, the beach seemed to have less sand—I remember burying someone in the sand here years ago, but now that feeling is gone. Despite a good breakfast selection, this experience tarnished my impression of the hotel, and I wouldn't consider staying again. I'd give this stay a 60 out of 100.

## Restaurant Experiences

Bali's food scene is equally exciting, from Instagram-worthy cafes to traditional local eateries, each with its own charm. On this trip, we deliberately chose several highly-rated restaurants to try, and each left a lasting impression with its decor, ambiance, and flavors.

### The Cave

![The Cave餐廳（June Lin提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b691df9fca528e5219964aca698c0e60.jpg)

The Cave restaurant (photo by June Lin)

We tried a few Instagram-worthy restaurants this time, and The Cave (inside The Edge) was one of them. It costs about USD$129 per person, which isn't cheap. But if you want to experience a novel atmosphere, it's worth a try. The restaurant's decor is unique, but given the high price, I think one visit is enough.

### Oneeighty Day Club

![Oneeighty Day Club（June Lin提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_443efccc3319ae3e31364d532139dc17.jpg)

Oneeighty Day Club (Photo by June Lin)

This time, we specifically booked the VIP area at Oneeighty Day Club and got front-row seats—it felt great. The reservation fee plus meal cost about USD$48 per person. Despite the not-so-low price, the food was surprisingly delicious, and the service was excellent. The infinity pool here is very deep, at 1.7 meters, so we could only snap a few photos by the pool before focusing on enjoying the meal. Overall, I'd give this dining experience a 95 out of 100.

> Reservation methods, payment details, and video introduction: [Explore Oneeighty Day Club: Bali's Cliffside Infinity Pool Paradise](/en/blog/oneeighty-dayclub-uluwatu-pool/)

### Cretya Ubud

Cretya Ubud is a famous swing restaurant in Ubud, and it was much tastier than I expected—the flavors and textures of the dishes exceeded my imagination. As for the swing photos, this place has practically become a global gathering spot for women seeking Instagram-worthy shots. Early in the morning, the swings were already crowded with people wanting to take pictures. I suggest you skip the challenge of the farthest swing; the smaller ones next to it actually look better in photos. Our group ended up with a bunch of hilarious panicked shots—it was pretty funny. I'd give the food 90 out of 100 and the Instagram experience 70 out of 100.

> Further reading: [Luxury on a Budget: Cretya Ubud Pool Club and Alas Harum Spending Guide](/en/blog/cretya-ubud-alas-harum-budget-guide/)

### Ku De Ta

![Ku De Ta（June Lin提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_255fda84dc588535e81c5ffc71aebfbf.jpg)

Ku De Ta (Photo by June Lin)

Ku De Ta is one of the beachfront restaurants in Seminyak where you can enjoy delicious food while watching the stunning sunset. The service is friendly, and the live music perfectly complements the atmosphere, making it a relaxing experience. After your meal, you can take a stroll on the sand. Dinner prices are very reasonable, around USD$16–$19 per person—you'd be hard-pressed to find such quality dining in Taiwan for that price. I give this restaurant a 90 out of 100.

### Potato Head

Potato Head is another restaurant in Seminyak and turned out to be the biggest surprise of my trip. We visited on a Saturday and enjoyed Indonesian and Balinese dishes—every single one was incredibly tasty and far exceeded my expectations. The beach next to the restaurant is also beautiful, making this my number one dining experience of the trip. The cost was around USD$32 per person—I can't recall the exact price, but it was absolutely worth it. I give this restaurant a 99 out of 100.

![Potato Head](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6f15aa6fe69a04c67c5c8622b55bb1ab.png)

### Warung Nia

Warung Nia is a very popular pork rib restaurant in central Seminyak. When we went for lunch, a bunch of foreigners were taking a cooking class inside. It's a semi-open-air restaurant with no air conditioning, so it felt a bit stuffy. As for the pork ribs, I found them a little too sweet—overall, just average. I give this restaurant an 80 out of 100.

### Koral

![Koral](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bda877aa3ae4ea0760efc8f0b0bd4b4.jpg)

Koral is an upscale restaurant inside the Kempinski Hotel. Since we had a large group, we booked three tables. But when we arrived, we were told two of the tables had been canceled. The staff loudly claimed they had contacted me two days earlier and I hadn't replied. I was really annoyed—I was either on the beach, in the pool, or enjoying a meal, so who has time to check emails? Their attitude was incredibly arrogant. If you're not a white person dressed in designer brands, I'd advise you to spare yourself the trouble. In the end, I argued with them in English, and their attitude softened a bit, but the overall experience was terrible. I give this restaurant a 0 out of 100.

## Driver Services

Transportation in Bali is truly a challenge. This time, we found an English-speaking local driver named Dwik online. His English pronunciation is excellent and easy to understand. He was always punctual and an incredibly skilled driver. Even though those of us in the back seat often nervously grabbed the handrails, he would just smile and say, 'Take it easy.' Most importantly, he never took us to any sketchy shops or pushed any sales, which I really appreciated. I value honesty, and Dwik's conduct put me completely at ease.
