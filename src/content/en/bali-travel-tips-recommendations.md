**Bottom line first**: The two hotels I stayed at on this trip had completely opposite reviews—**Peppers Villa in Seminyak is a huge recommendation (I give it 99 out of 100)**: 56 villas, attentive service, and breakfast that exceeded expectations; **the Hilton Hotel in Nusa Dua was a letdown**: five-star price tag, but the facilities didn't measure up. If you're choosing accommodation in Seminyak, don't hesitate to go with Peppers; for five-star stays in Nusa Dua, I'd suggest doing more research and comparing options.

Bali, this tropical island full of charm, is more than just a vacation destination for me—it's a place where I discover new surprises every time I visit. On this trip, I tried some luxurious accommodations and a few well-known restaurants, and I also got a real taste of Bali's local traffic challenges. I hope sharing my experience gives you some useful tips and inspiration for your own Bali trip.

## Accommodation Experience

During this trip, I stayed at two very different hotels—the luxurious Peppers Villa and the historic Hilton Hotel. Each place had its own unique character and highlights, but there were also some unexpected surprises and disappointments. Let me walk you through the experience at both hotels.

### Peppers Villa (3 nights)

![Peppers Seminyak 大推！(June Lin提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6f9f85b8a7491479d13ae7c0c626b00e.jpg)

Peppers Seminyak is a huge recommendation! (Provided by June Lin)

This time, we chose Peppers Villa, which recently joined the Accor group. Located in the heart of Seminyak, it's super convenient for shopping in town or heading to the beach. There are 56 villas here, each designed to be incredibly comfortable, giving off a luxurious yet cozy vibe. The service was spot-on—from check-in to check-out, every detail was well taken care of. The rooms were spacious and bright, fully equipped, and felt as relaxing as being at home. Breakfast was even better than expected—both the freshness of the ingredients and the cooking style were satisfying. Plus, their in-room dining was reasonably priced with good food quality, making it one of the highlights of my stay. Overall, I give this stay a 99 out of 100.

### Hilton Nusa Dua (2 nights)

![Hilton Nusa Dua, 五星的價格，不及格的硬體設施（June Lin 提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_999f7ae586125ba8183e3108a025fce5.jpg)

Hilton Nusa Dua: Five-star price, subpar facilities (Photo by June Lin)

Nine years ago, I stayed at this Hilton in the Nusa Dua resort area. This revisit left me disappointed. First, the pool water quality was off-putting—so murky that even my son refused to go in. Second, the beach seemed to have less sand; I remember burying someone in the sand here back then, but now that feeling is gone. While the breakfast spread was generous, this experience tarnished my impression of the hotel, and I wouldn't consider staying again. I'd give this stay a 60 out of 100.

## Dining Experiences

Bali's food scene is equally exciting, from Instagram-worthy cafes to local eateries, each with its own charm. On this trip, we deliberately tried several highly recommended restaurants, and each left a lasting impression with its decor, ambiance, and flavors.

### The Cave

![The Cave餐廳（June Lin提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b691df9fca528e5219964aca698c0e60.jpg)

The Cave restaurant (Photo by June Lin)

This time we tried a few Instagram-worthy restaurants, and The Cave (located inside The Edge) was one of them. The cost is about USD$129 per person, which isn't cheap. But if you're looking to experience a unique atmosphere, it's worth a try. The restaurant's decor is distinctive, though given the high price, I think a one-time visit is enough.

### Oneeighty Day Club

![Oneeighty Day Club（June Lin提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_443efccc3319ae3e31364d532139dc17.jpg)

Oneeighty Day Club (Photo by June Lin)

This time we specifically booked the VIP area at Oneeighty Day Club and got front-row seats—it felt great. The reservation fee plus food cost about USD$48 per person. Despite the steep price, the food was surprisingly delicious, and the service was excellent. The infinity pool here is very deep, at 1.7 meters, so we could only snap a few photos by the poolside before focusing on enjoying the meal. Overall, I'd give this dining experience a 95 out of 100.

> Reservation methods, payment details, and video introduction: [Explore Oneeighty Day Club: Bali's Cliffside Infinity Pool with Stunning Views](/en/blog/oneeighty-dayclub-uluwatu-pool/)

### Cretya Ubud

Cretya Ubud is a famous swing restaurant in Ubud, and it was much tastier than I expected—the flavors and textures of the dishes exceeded my imagination. As for the swing photos, this place has practically become a global gathering spot for women seeking Instagram-worthy shots. Early in the morning, the swings are already crowded with people wanting to take pictures. I suggest you skip the challenge of the farthest swing; the smaller ones nearby actually look better in photos. Our group ended up with a bunch of hilarious terrified expressions, which was pretty funny. I'd give the food a 90 out of 100 and the Instagram photo experience a 70.

> Further reading: [Luxury on a Budget: Cretya Ubud Pool Club and Alas Harum Spending Guide](/en/blog/cretya-ubud-alas-harum-budget-guide/)

### Ku De Ta

![Ku De Ta（June Lin提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_255fda84dc588535e81c5ffc71aebfbf.jpg)

Ku De Ta (Photo by June Lin)

Ku De Ta is one of the beachfront restaurants in Seminyak, where you can enjoy delicious food while watching a stunning sunset. The service is friendly, and the live music perfectly complements the atmosphere, making it a relaxing experience. After your meal, you can take a stroll on the sand. Dinner prices are very reasonable, around USD$16-19 per person—you probably wouldn't get such great food in Taiwan for that price. I give this restaurant a 90 out of 100.

### Potato Head

Potato Head is another restaurant in Seminyak and was the biggest surprise of my trip. We visited on a Saturday and enjoyed Indonesian and Balinese dishes—every single one was delicious and far exceeded my expectations. The beach next to the restaurant is also beautiful, making this my number one pick of the entire food-and-drink journey. The cost per person was around USD$32, though I've forgotten the exact price, but it was absolutely worth it. I give this restaurant a 99 out of 100.

![Potato Head](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6f15aa6fe69a04c67c5c8622b55bb1ab.png)

### Warung Nia

Warung Nia is a very popular pork rib restaurant in central Seminyak. When we went at lunchtime, a bunch of foreigners were taking a cooking class there. It's a semi-open-air restaurant with no air conditioning, so it felt a bit stuffy. As for the pork ribs, I found them a little too sweet—overall, just average. I give this restaurant an 80 out of 100.

### Koral

![Koral](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bda877aa3ae4ea0760efc8f0b0bd4b4.jpg)

Koral is an upscale restaurant inside the Kempinski Hotel. Since we had a large group, we booked three tables. Upon arrival, we were told two of the tables had been canceled, and the staff loudly claimed they had contacted me two days earlier with no reply. This really annoyed me—I was either on the beach, in the pool, or enjoying a meal, so who has time to check emails? Their attitude was very arrogant. If you're not a white person dressed in designer brands, I'd advise you not to put yourself through that. In the end, I argued with them in English, and their attitude softened a bit, but the overall experience was terrible. I give this restaurant a 0 out of 100.

## Driver Service

Transportation in Bali is truly a challenge. This time, we found an English-speaking local driver named Dwik online. His English pronunciation is excellent and easy to understand, and he was always punctual with great driving skills. Even though those of us in the back seat often nervously grabbed the handrails, he would just smile and say, 'Take it easy.' Most importantly, he never took us to any shady shops or pushed sales on us, which I really appreciated. I value honesty, and Dwik's performance made me feel completely at ease.
