## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Environmental Protests and Social Rescues

- **Students Launch 'Garbage Emergency' Protest**: Yesterday morning (4/22), hundreds of students from Udayana University (Unud) gathered at Renon Square in Denpasar to protest Bali's worsening waste management crisis. Demonstrators demanded the provincial government address landfill saturation and infrastructure failures. Governor Wayan Koster personally met with student representatives, pledging to accelerate policy reforms.
- **Australian Man Rescued from Abandoned Building**: An Australian man with a severely injured leg, missing for several days, was found today in an abandoned building near Sunset Road in Legian. It is believed the man became stranded after his motorcycle was taken by a local acquaintance, and his wounds had become severely infected. Through coordination between the Australian Consulate and local police, the man agreed to receive treatment and has been taken to Denpasar Hospital.
- **Sports Tourism Shows Strong Results**: Statistics from the 'Kemala Run 2026' road race, which concluded yesterday, show the event attracted over 11,000 participants, generating significant tourism revenue for the Gianyar region. Officials plan to make such events regular to promote eastern tourism.

## 📄 Tourist Concerns: Coastal Warnings and Infrastructure

- **'King Tide' Coastal Flooding Warning**: Due to the new moon and lunar perigee, BMKG has issued a **April 17-25** 'King Tide' warning for coastal areas across the island. Areas most affected today include **Kuta, Seminyak, Canggu, and Sanur**, where seawater may overflow onto sidewalks or coastal villas. Be mindful of real-time water levels when walking or dining near the beach.
- **Transport Changes**: Due to the king tide, speedboat services to **Nusa Penida** and **Lembongan** may face temporary delays or changes in boarding points due to high dock water levels. Travelers with island plans today are advised to confirm with boat operators in advance.
- **Immigration 'Village' Inspection Operation**: Immigration authorities today launched the PIMPASA program, sending officers into communities (Villages) to collect information on foreign residents. This means long-stay tourists living in private villas or non-hotel areas will face a significantly higher chance of being asked to show valid residence permits.
