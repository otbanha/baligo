After the pandemic, much of the online information about Bali's Sunday Markets has become outdated. However, some fantastic markets are still running in 2026. We've specifically curated these genuine Sunday markets, not the ones that operate daily. Here are the Sunday markets still active in 2026:

## La Brisa Sunday Market (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1BZB_5sqY7fAPbfZJkSKFuZI7ar1UTF8U&usp=sharing))

La Brisa Sunday Market is a stunning and vibrant market in Bali, located at Echo Beach in Canggu. Every Sunday, you can find local handmade jewelry, textiles, and beauty products here.

The name La Brisa means 'sea breeze,' and the architecture here uses recycled wood from over 500 old fishing boats, meticulously decorated. Today, La Brisa is one of Bali's most famous and sustainable eco-friendly venues, exuding genuine Balinese charm. Check out our feature: [Dubbed 'Bali's Most Beautiful Restaurant': How La Brisa Stands Out Among Tens of Thousands of Dining Spots?](/blog/canggu-la-brisa-restaurant-guide/)

La Brisa Sunday Market runs every Sunday from 10 AM to 4 PM. It's an initiative supporting local farmers and small businesses, with LYD Organic Farm providing organic produce, baked goods, natural body care products, regenerative fashion, and zero-waste items.

If you're in Bali, don't miss La Brisa Sunday Market to experience sustainable decor, breathtaking views, delicious food, and a delightful time with the local community.

## Samadi Sunday Market (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing))

Samadi Sunday Market is a popular Sunday market in the Canggu area of Bali. It's located on Jalan Padang Linjong, near Echo Beach. The market is open every Sunday from 8 AM to 4 PM.

Samadi Sunday Market is renowned for its organic produce and handmade crafts. The market features a variety of stalls selling fresh fruits, vegetables, meats, fish, and grains. Additionally, there are numerous stalls offering handmade clothing, jewelry, home goods, and souvenirs.

Samadi Sunday Market is a great place to buy local products and support local businesses. It's also a wonderful spot to relax, enjoy good food, and listen to music.

## Sanur Sunday Market (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing))

Held at the beach of Mercure Resort Sanur, the monthly Sunday Market is a family-friendly event designed to encourage community participation and support local businesses and artists. The market takes place on the last Sunday of each month from 10 AM to 6 PM, offering a relaxed and enjoyable shopping and social experience.

The monthly market features numerous stalls offering a wide range of goods and services. From exquisite handicrafts to fashionable accessories, from delicious food to eco-friendly products, there’s something for everyone. This market isn’t just a shopping destination—it’s also an ideal spot for family gatherings. You can savor tasty treats, enjoy delicious food, or unwind on the beautiful beach while participating in seaside yoga sessions.

Attending this market isn’t just about shopping—it’s a way to support local businesses and artists. You can interact directly with vendors, learn the stories behind their products, and support local creativity and entrepreneurship. Doing so not only helps the local economy but also fosters community development and prosperity.

## SUNDAZE ULUWATU MARKET  (📌[Map](https://maps.app.goo.gl/wMDJDgG22d8n2JhU8))

SUNDAZE ULUWATU MARKET is located at Hatch Bali Uluwatu in the southern tip of Bali. Every Sunday from 11 AM to 6 PM, it transforms into a vibrant marketplace. Here, you can enjoy performances by talented acoustic musicians, savor delicious barbecue from local vendors, and browse unique products or tasty snacks. This market is the perfect spot to unwind and connect with friends, embodying the relaxed weekend vibe of Uluwatu!
