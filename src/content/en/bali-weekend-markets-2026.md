After the pandemic, much of the online information about Bali's Sunday Markets has become outdated. However, in 2026, some fantastic markets are still going strong. We've specifically compiled these genuine Sunday markets, not the ones that operate daily. Here are the Sunday markets we've listed that are still active in 2026:

## La Brisa Sunday Market (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1BZB_5sqY7fAPbfZJkSKFuZI7ar1UTF8U&usp=sharing))

La Brisa Sunday Market is a stunning and vibrant market in Bali, located at Echo Beach in Canggu. Every Sunday, you can find local handmade jewelry, textiles, and beauty products here.

The name La Brisa signifies 'sea breeze,' and the architecture here uses recycled wood from over 500 old fishing boats, meticulously decorated. Today, La Brisa is one of Bali's most famous and sustainable eco-friendly venues, exuding genuine Balinese charm. Check out our feature: [La Brisa: How Does It Stand Out Among Tens of Thousands of Restaurants as 'Bali's Most Beautiful Restaurant'?](/blog/canggu-la-brisa-restaurant-guide/)

La Brisa Sunday Market runs every Sunday from 10 AM to 4 PM. It's an initiative supporting local farmers and small businesses, offering organic produce from LYD Organic Farm, baked goods, natural body care products, regenerative fashion, and zero-waste products.

If you're in Bali, don't miss La Brisa Sunday Market to experience sustainable decor, breathtaking views, delicious food, and a delightful time with the local community.

## Samadi Sunday Market (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing))

Samadi Sunday Market is a popular Sunday market in the Canggu area of Bali. It's located on Jalan Padang Linjong, near Echo Beach. The market is open every Sunday from 8 AM to 4 PM.

Samadi Sunday Market is renowned for its organic produce and handicrafts. The market features a variety of stalls selling fresh fruits, vegetables, meats, fish, and grains. Additionally, there are many stalls offering handmade clothing, jewelry, home goods, and souvenirs.

Samadi Sunday Market is a great place to buy local products and support local businesses. It's also a wonderful spot to relax, enjoy good food, and listen to music.

## Sanur Sunday Market (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing))

The monthly farmers' market held at the beach of Mercure Resort Sanur is a family-friendly event designed to encourage community participation and support local businesses and artists. This market takes place on the last Sunday of every month, from 10 AM to 6 PM, offering a relaxed and enjoyable shopping and social experience.

Each month's market features numerous stalls covering a variety of goods and services. From exquisite handicrafts to fashionable accessories, from gourmet food to eco-friendly products, everyone can find something they love. This market is not just a shopping venue but also an ideal place for family gatherings. You can savor delicious food, relax on the beautiful beach, or participate in seaside yoga classes.

Attending this market is not just about shopping; it's also a way to support local businesses and artists. You can directly interact with vendors, learn the stories behind the products, and support local creativity and entrepreneurship. Doing so not only helps the local economy but also promotes community development and prosperity.

## SUNDAZE ULUWATU MARKET  (📌[Map](https://maps.app.goo.gl/wMDJDgG22d8n2JhU8))

Located at Hatch Bali Uluwatu in the southern tip of Bali, SUNDAZE ULUWATU MARKET transforms into a vibrant bazaar every Sunday from 11 AM to 6 PM. Here, you can enjoy performances by talented acoustic musicians, savor delicious barbecue from local vendors, and discover unique products or tasty snacks. This market is the perfect place to unwind and connect with friends, defining Uluwatu's laid-back weekend vibe!
