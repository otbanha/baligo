This morning's USD exchange rate: 16650. Today (4/8), Bali is in the transitional period from the rainy season to the dry season. Although April theoretically marks the start of the dry season, the Indonesian Meteorological Agency (BMKG) has issued a thunderstorm warning today: there is a 70% - 85% chance of brief but intense showers across the island (especially in the central and southern regions) from afternoon to evening.

The characteristic of these 'transitional' rains is that they come and go quickly, usually clearing up within 30 to 60 minutes, with mornings often being sunny and pleasant. Yesterday evening, Tabanan was hit by a strong tornado (Angin Puting Beliung), damaging 9 houses. Currently, rescue teams and villagers are working on cleanup and recovery. The Meteorological Agency (BMKG) continues to issue a warning for 4-meter-high waves in the southern sea, advising ferries and fishermen traveling to and from the islands to stay safe. Due to the lingering effects of yesterday's tornado in Tabanan, convective activity remains strong today. Temperatures range between 25°C - 31°C, with high humidity making it feel quite muggy. It is recommended to schedule outdoor activities in the morning and prepare for rain or plan indoor activities after 3 PM.
