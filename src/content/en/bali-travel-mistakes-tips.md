## Itinerary Planning

### 1. Overpacking Your Schedule

> **Mistake**: Many travelers underestimate the size of Bali and try to visit all the attractions in a short period, resulting in a rushed itinerary that doesn't allow for proper enjoyment of each location.

Although Bali is an island, it is vast and diverse, offering unique charms from the southern beaches to the northern mountains. When planning your itinerary, choose suitable areas based on your stay duration:

- **One week or less**: Select 1-2 main accommodation bases to avoid excessive moving around.
- **Two weeks**: Cover 3-4 accommodation bases, allowing moderate travel time.
- **Three weeks or more**: Plan up to 5-6 locations, but ensure the schedule doesn't become too overwhelming.

Try to minimize frequent changes in accommodation to avoid wasting time packing and moving, which can leave you exhausted.

**Mistake**: Many travelers schedule their Nusa Islands (Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Ceningan, Nusa Penida) or Gili Islands trips towards the end of their journey. However, if bad weather causes ferry cancellations, it could disrupt your return plans.

To avoid delays in your return due to weather-related ferry cancellations, it's advisable to schedule these island-hopping adventures in the middle or early part of your trip. This way, any potential delays won't affect your overall travel plans.

## Safety and Health

To ensure your Bali trip is safe and comfortable, it’s crucial to avoid common health and safety pitfalls. Additionally, respecting local culture and eco-tourism ethics can help you forge a deeper connection with Bali. Below are some common mistakes and suggestions regarding safety, health, and cultural etiquette.

### 7. Bank Card Security

**Mistake**: Using credit cards for large purchases without considering potential fraud risks.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0648b8fa6386a0758be5f8b1fa666c66.jpg)

Certain areas in Bali may have risks of credit card fraud, so it's recommended to protect your funds using the following methods:

- **Use a debit card with limited funds**: Keep your debit card balance below USD$300 to minimize losses in case of fraud.
- **Online bank transfers**: For large payments or purchases, transfer additional funds via online banking to avoid keeping high balances on your card.
- **Use e-wallets**: Whenever possible, use e-wallets like Gojek to reduce the use of physical cards.

### 8. Mosquito Protection

**Mistake**: Ignoring the threat of mosquitoes

Dengue fever is not uncommon in Bali, so mosquito protection is crucial. Here are some essential items and tips:

- **Insect repellent**: Carry insect repellent with you and use it frequently, especially during outdoor activities.
- **Wear long sleeves and pants**: Mosquitoes are most active from dusk to dawn, so wear protective clothing.
- **Secure doors and windows**: Ensure your accommodation's doors and windows are tightly closed, or use mosquito nets to prevent mosquitoes from entering.
- **Be aware of dengue risk**: If you feel unwell, experiencing symptoms like fever or muscle pain, seek medical attention immediately to check for dengue fever.

### 10. Monkey Safety Guidelines

https://youtube.com/shorts/tpPWsvsH-Eo

>   **Mistake**: Ignoring the potential dangers of monkeys and carrying valuables or food while interacting with them.

In certain areas of Bali, such as Uluwatu (Uluwatu Temple) and the Monkey Forest (Ubud Monkey Forest), monkeys are common 'residents'. While they may appear cute, they can be aggressive, especially towards valuables.

- **Do not carry valuables or food**: Monkeys are particularly interested in food and shiny objects, so avoid bringing these items with you.
- **Do not make eye contact**: Making eye contact with monkeys is highly dangerous, as some may perceive it as a challenge.
- **Protect glasses and phones**: Monkeys often snatch items like glasses and phones from tourists, so keep them secure in monkey areas.
- **Maintain distance**: Although monkeys are often seen as friendly, remember they are wild animals and should be kept at a safe distance.

## Cultural Etiquette

### 11. Respect Local Culture

> **Mistake**: Ignoring local religious and cultural etiquette, leading to disrespectful behavior.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2051bf25b8c56908609a59e3c8151f2a.jpg)

Bali is a cultural hub of Hinduism, where locals place great importance on religious rituals and traditions. Respecting their customs is crucial for visitors. Here are some key cultural etiquettes:

- **Temple Dress Code**: When entering temples, dress modestly and wear a sarong (temples usually offer free rentals).
- **Avoid Stepping on Offerings**: On Bali's streets, you'll often see small basket-like offerings. These are daily offerings made by locals, so be careful not to step on them.

Further Reading: [Understanding Bali's Offering Rituals: Canang Sari](/blog/2024-07-16-66964aa7fd89780001c7051c/)

- **Taboo Actions**: Do not touch someone's head, as it is considered disrespectful in local culture. Additionally, using your left hand to pass items is seen as impolite.
- **Remove Shoes Indoors**: When entering a local's home or certain shops, remove your shoes as a sign of respect.
- **Learn Basic Greetings**: Learning a few simple phrases in the local language, such as "thank you," can help you build rapport with locals.
- - Indonesian "Thank you": **Terima kasih**
    - Balinese "Thank you": **Suksma**

### 12. Ethical Animal Tourism

> **Mistake**: Participating in unethical animal tourism activities that harm animals.

Bali offers many animal-related tourist activities, but many involve inhumane treatment of animals, sparking significant controversy. To protect wildlife, it's advised to avoid the following activities:

- **Zoos and Marine Parks**: Many animals in zoos and marine parks are kept in confined spaces, unable to move freely.
- **Elephant Parks**: Some parks offer elephant rides, but these elephants are often mistreated and in poor health. Bali has banned elephant riding island-wide starting in 2026.
- **Wildlife Performances**: Wild animals in performances are often subjected to cruel training to perform unnatural behaviors.

Support activities that respect animals' natural habitats, such as:

- **Lovina Dolphin Watching**: Here, dolphins roam freely in open waters rather than being confined to aquariums.
- **Sea Turtle Watching in Natural Habitats**: Participate in organizations or activities that support wildlife conservation to observe sea turtles in the wild without interfering with their natural lives.

When traveling in Bali, being well-prepared and understanding local conditions can make your journey more enjoyable and help avoid potential hassles. Here are some practical tips for traveling in Bali to help you have a smoother vacation.

### 13. Currency Exchange

>   **Mistake**: Exchanging cash before departure or at the airport.

- **Avoid Carrying Large Amounts of Cash**: Carrying too much cash increases risk; using debit cards or electronic payments is safer.

### 14. Bargaining Tips

>   **Mistake**: Accepting the first offer at markets or street vendors without understanding local bargaining culture.

- **Negotiable Prices for Non-Fixed Items**: In many open-air markets and small stalls in Bali, prices are not fixed, and you can negotiate appropriately.
- **Avoid Accepting the First Offer**: Sellers often quote higher prices; try to lower the price reasonably and purchase only after reaching a mutually satisfactory agreement.
- **Support the Local Economy**: While bargaining is common, avoid excessively lowballing prices to respect local vendors and support their livelihoods.

### 15. Weather Awareness

> **Mistake**: Ignoring Bali's weather changes and failing to check forecasts in advance.

- **Follow BMKG (Bali Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysical Agency)** on their [official Instagram account](https://www.instagram.com/infobmkg/): They post daily English weather forecasts for tourists.
- **Use the Accuweather App**: Often provides more accurate predictions than Google.
- **Check weather on Windy.com**: Great for viewing Bali's weather forecasts, especially for wave and wind speed information.
- **Morning activities are ideal**: Bali's weather is usually more stable in the morning, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities.
- **Afternoons are prone to rain**: Especially during the rainy season, brief showers often occur in the afternoon, so it's best to schedule indoor activities or rest during this time.

### 16. Packing Tips

> **Mistake**: Bringing unnecessary clothing or overlooking the local weather conditions.

Based on Bali's climate and activity needs, here are the essentials:

- **Lightweight and breathable clothing**: Given Bali's hot and humid climate, pack thin and airy outfits to stay comfortable in the heat.
- **Swimwear**: Whether or not you plan to stay near the beach, swimwear is a must, as Bali offers countless beautiful beaches and pools to enjoy.
- **Simple flats or sandals**: Most activities in Bali, such as exploring markets, strolling on beaches, or visiting temples, can easily be done in flats or sandals.
- **Light jacket for mountainous areas**: If you plan to visit Ubud or Munduk, evenings can get cooler, so a light jacket will keep you comfortable.

### 17. Motorcycle Safety

https://youtu.be/lIV-T2jYhVk

**Mistake**: Neglecting motorcycle safety measures.

- **Always wear a helmet**: Whether you're driving yourself or riding as a passenger, wearing a helmet is mandatory. This is not only a legal requirement but also a crucial safety measure.
- **Request a spare helmet when booking a ride**: When using ride-hailing services like Gojek or Grab for motorcycle rides, always ask the driver to provide you with a helmet.
- **Follow traffic rules**: The road conditions and traffic regulations in Bali may differ from those in your hometown. Drive cautiously and adhere to local traffic laws.

### 18. Ignoring Local Food Safety

**Mistake**: Not paying attention to food hygiene and casually dining at roadside stalls or unclean places.

https://youtu.be/GivvqCXp11A

- **Choose clean restaurants or well-reviewed roadside stalls**: Bali offers many delicious local snacks and restaurants, but not all places meet hygiene standards. Opt for restaurants with good cleanliness or stalls recommended by locals.
- **Be cautious about drinking water**: Never drink tap water directly. Choose bottled water or ensure that the restaurant provides safe drinking water.
- **Avoid raw or undercooked food**: Especially in remote areas or unfamiliar restaurants, raw and undercooked food may pose a risk of stomach discomfort. Prioritize cooked food.
- **Carry stomach medicine**: If you have a sensitive stomach, carry common stomach medications like anti-diarrheal pills or probiotics for emergencies.
