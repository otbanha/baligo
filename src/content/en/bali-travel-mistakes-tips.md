After multiple explorations and observations, we've noticed that tourists often make certain mistakes when visiting Bali. These mistakes not only affect the travel experience but can also waste valuable time and money. Here are 18 common errors and practical tips to help you enjoy a more pleasant and worry-free journey.

## Itinerary Planning

### 1. Overpacking Your Schedule

> **Mistake**: Many tourists underestimate the size of Bali and try to visit all the attractions in a short time, resulting in a rushed itinerary that doesn't allow them to fully enjoy each location.

Although Bali is an island, it is vast and diverse, offering unique charms from the beaches in the south to the mountains in the north. When planning your itinerary, consider the following based on your stay duration:

- **One week**: Choose 1-2 main accommodation bases to minimize frequent moves.
- **Two weeks**: Cover 3-4 accommodation bases with moderate travel time.
- **Three weeks or more**: Arrange up to 5-6 locations, but ensure the schedule isn't overly burdensome.

Try to reduce frequent changes in accommodation to avoid wasting time packing and moving, which can leave you exhausted.

### 3. Scheduling Island Trips at the End

> **Mistake**: Many travelers plan their trips to the Nusa Islands (Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Ceningan, Nusa Penida) or the Gili Islands towards the end of their journey. However, if bad weather causes ferry cancellations, it could disrupt your return plans.

To avoid delays in your return due to weather-related ferry cancellations, it's advisable to schedule these island trips in the middle or early part of your journey. This way, even if there are delays, it won't affect your overall travel plans.

## Safety and Health

To ensure a safer and more comfortable trip to Bali, it’s crucial to avoid common health and safety pitfalls. Additionally, respecting local culture and eco-tourism ethics can help you build a deeper connection with Bali. Below are some common mistakes and suggestions regarding safety, health, and cultural etiquette.

### 7. Bank Card Security

> **Mistake**: Using credit cards for large purchases without considering the risk of fraud.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0648b8fa6386a0758be5f8b1fa666c66.jpg)

Some areas in Bali may have risks of credit card fraud, so it's recommended to protect your funds with the following methods:

- **Use a debit card with limited funds**: Keep your debit card balance below USD$300 to minimize losses in case of fraud.
- **Online bank transfers**: For large payments or purchases, transfer more funds via online banking to avoid keeping high balances on your card.
- **Use e-wallets**: Whenever possible, use e-wallets like Gojek to reduce the use of physical cards.

### 8. Mosquito Protection

> **Mistake**: Ignoring the threat of mosquitoes

Dengue fever is not uncommon in Bali, so mosquito protection is crucial. Here are some essential items and recommendations:

- **Mosquito repellent**: Carry mosquito repellent with you and use it frequently, especially during outdoor activities.
- **Wear long sleeves and pants**: Mosquitoes are most active from dusk to night, so it's advisable to wear protective clothing.
- **Secure doors and windows**: Ensure your accommodation's doors and windows are tightly closed, or use mosquito nets to prevent mosquitoes from entering.
- **Be aware of dengue risks**: If you feel unwell, such as having a fever or muscle pain, seek medical attention immediately to check for dengue fever.

### 9. Dealing with the Rainy Season

>   **Mistake**: Ignoring the rapid weather changes during Bali's rainy season and failing to prepare accordingly.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c86655f684007466ca064fac6b244c4f.png)

Bali's rainy season typically occurs from November to April. While it doesn't rain every day, the weather changes quickly, often shifting suddenly from sunny to heavy rain.

- **Carry a raincoat**: A lightweight raincoat is more practical than an umbrella, as heavy rains come suddenly and are often accompanied by strong winds.
- **Plan morning activities**: The weather is usually more stable in the morning, so it's advisable to schedule outdoor activities in the morning to avoid sudden afternoon showers.

### 10. Monkey Safety Guidelines

https://youtube.com/shorts/tpPWsvsH-Eo

>   **Mistake**: Ignoring the potential dangers of monkeys and carrying valuable items or food while interacting with them.

In certain areas of Bali, such as Uluwatu (Uluwatu Temple) and the Monkey Forest (Ubud Monkey Forest), monkeys are common "residents." While they may appear cute, they can be aggressive, especially towards valuable items.

- **Do not carry valuable items or food**: Monkeys are particularly interested in food and shiny objects, so avoid carrying them.
- **Do not make eye contact with monkeys**: Making eye contact with monkeys is highly dangerous, as some may perceive it as a challenge.
- **Protect your glasses and phone**: Monkeys often snatch items like glasses and phones from tourists, so keep them secure in monkey areas.
- **Maintain distance**: While monkeys are often seen as friendly, remember they are wild animals and keep a safe distance.

## Cultural Etiquette

### 11. Respect Local Culture

> **Mistake**: Ignoring local religious and cultural etiquette, leading to disrespectful behavior.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2051bf25b8c56908609a59e3c8151f2a.jpg)

Bali is a cultural hub of Hinduism, where locals place great importance on religious rituals and traditions. Respecting their customs is especially important for visitors. Here are some key cultural etiquettes:

- **Temple Dress Code**: When entering temples, dress modestly and wear a sarong (temples usually offer free rentals).
- **Avoid Stepping on Offerings**: On Bali's streets, you'll often see small basket-like offerings. These are daily offerings made by locals, so be careful not to step on them.

Further reading: [Understanding Bali's Offering Rituals: Canang Sari](/blog/bali-canang-sari-explained/)

- **Taboo Behaviors**: Do not touch someone's head, as it is considered disrespectful in local culture. Additionally, using your left hand to pass items is seen as impolite.
- **Remove Shoes Indoors**: When entering locals' homes or certain shops, remove your shoes as a sign of respect.
- **Learn Basic Greetings**: Learning a few simple phrases in the local language, such as "thank you," can help you build rapport with locals.
  - Indonesian "Thank you": **Terima kasih**
  - Balinese "Thank you": **Suksma**

### 12. Ethical Animal Tourism

> **Mistake**: Participating in unethical animal tourism activities that harm animals.

Bali offers many animal-related tourist activities, but many involve inhumane treatment of animals, sparking significant controversy. To protect wildlife, it's recommended to avoid the following activities:

- **Zoos and Marine Parks**: Many animals in zoos and marine parks are kept in confined spaces, unable to move freely.
- **Elephant Parks**: Some parks offer elephant rides, but these elephants are often mistreated and in poor health. Starting in 2026, Bali has banned elephant riding across the island.
- **Wildlife Performances**: Wild animals in performances are often subjected to cruel training to perform unnatural behaviors.

Support activities that respect animals' natural habitats, such as:

- **Lovina Dolphin Watching**: Here, dolphins roam freely in open waters rather than being confined to aquariums.
- **Sea Turtle Watching in Natural Habitats**: Join organizations or activities that support wildlife conservation to observe wild sea turtles without interfering with their natural lives.

## **Practical Tips**

When traveling in Bali, being well-prepared and understanding local conditions can make your trip more enjoyable and help avoid potential hassles. Here are some practical tips for traveling in Bali to help you have a smoother vacation.

### 13. Currency Exchange

>   **Mistake**: Exchanging cash before departure or at the airport.

https://youtu.be/KPGb6MmwfZw

- **Exchange After Arrival**: Refer to the "[Bali Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)" to learn how and where to exchange currency.
- **Avoid Carrying Large Amounts of Cash**: Carrying too much cash increases risk; using debit cards or electronic payments is safer.

### 14. Bargaining Tips

>   **Mistake**: Accepting the first price offered at markets or street vendors without understanding local bargaining culture.

- **Non-Fixed Price Items Are Negotiable**: In many open-air markets and small stalls in Bali, prices are not fixed, and you can bargain appropriately.
- **Avoid Accepting the First Offer**: Sellers often quote higher prices; try to lower the price reasonably and purchase only when both parties are satisfied.
- **Support the Local Economy**: While bargaining is common, avoid excessively lowballing prices to respect local vendors and support their livelihoods.

### 15. Weather Awareness

> **Mistake**: Ignoring Bali's weather changes and failing to check the forecast in advance.

- **Follow BMKG (Bali Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysical Agency)** on their [official Instagram account](https://www.instagram.com/infobmkg/): They post daily English weather forecasts for tourists.
- **Use the Accuweather App**: Often provides more accurate predictions than Google.
- **Check weather on Windy.com**: Great for viewing Bali's weather forecasts, especially for wave and wind speed information.
- **Morning activities are ideal**: Bali's weather is usually more stable in the morning, making it the best time for outdoor activities.
- **Afternoons are prone to rain**: Especially during the rainy season, short showers are common in the afternoon, so it's recommended to schedule indoor activities or rest during this time.

### 16. Packing Tips

> **Mistake**: Bringing unnecessary clothing or overlooking the local weather characteristics.

Based on Bali's climate and activity needs, here are the essentials:

- **Lightweight and breathable clothing**: Due to Bali's hot and humid climate, pack thin and airy outfits to stay comfortable in the heat.
- **Swimwear**: Whether or not you plan to stay near the beach, swimwear is a must, as Bali offers countless beautiful beaches and pools to enjoy.
- **Simple flats or sandals**: Most activities in Bali, such as exploring markets, beach walks, or visiting temples, can be comfortably done in flats or sandals.
- **Light jacket for mountainous areas**: If you plan to visit areas like Ubud or Munduk, evenings can be cooler, so a light jacket will keep you comfortable.

### 17. Motorcycle Safety

https://youtu.be/lIV-T2jYhVk

> **Mistake**: Neglecting motorcycle safety measures.

- **Always wear a helmet**: Whether you're driving yourself or riding as a passenger, wearing a helmet is mandatory. This is not only a legal requirement but also a crucial safety measure.
- **Request a spare helmet when booking a ride**: When using ride-hailing services like Gojek or Grab for motorcycle rides, always ask the driver to provide you with a helmet.
- **Follow traffic rules**: The road conditions and traffic regulations in Bali may differ from those in your hometown. Drive cautiously and make sure to adhere to local traffic laws.

> Further reading: [Essential for short trips! Hailing a motorcycle with ride apps saves time and effort!](/blog/bali-travel-app-motorcycle-taxi/)

### 18. Ignoring local food safety

> **Mistake**: Not paying attention to food hygiene and casually eating at roadside stalls or unclean places.

https://youtu.be/GivvqCXp11A

- **Choose clean restaurants or well-reviewed street vendors**: Bali offers many delicious local snacks and restaurants, but not all places meet hygiene standards. Opt for restaurants with good cleanliness practices or vendors that come recommended.
- **Be cautious with drinking water**: Never drink tap water directly. Choose bottled water or ensure that the restaurant provides safe drinking water.
- **Avoid raw or undercooked food**: Especially in remote areas or unfamiliar restaurants, raw and undercooked food can pose a risk of stomach discomfort. Prioritize fully cooked meals.
- **Carry stomach medicine**: If you have a sensitive stomach, carry common stomach medications like anti-diarrheal pills or probiotics for emergencies.

From thoughtful itinerary planning to respecting local culture and protecting the environment, every detail impacts your Bali experience.
**In summary**, whether it's your first visit or a return trip, these practical tips will help you enjoy a relaxing and delightful vacation in Bali! Remember, the essence of travel lies not only in enjoying the scenery but also in respecting local culture, supporting the local economy, and coexisting harmoniously with nature.
