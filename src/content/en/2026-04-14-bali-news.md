## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Aviation Resilience and Market Shifts

- **Flight Normalization**: Although volcanic ash clouds from Flores Island caused disruptions, Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) is operating normally today. Official data shows that despite geopolitical challenges, international visitor numbers exceeded 1.6 million in the first quarter, demonstrating strong recovery momentum.
- **Exploring New Markets**: The tourism industry (ASITA) is turning its attention to Central Asian countries like Kazakhstan, aiming to position Bali as an international transit hub similar to Singapore or Bangkok. This will bring more diverse visitor sources in the coming years.

## 📄 Topics of Interest to Travelers: Fees and Transportation

- **Tourist Levy Checks**: Authorities have recently intensified checks on the IDR 150,000 "**Tourist Levy**." All international visitors (including children) are reminded to pay in advance via the Love Bali website before arrival, as random checks at the airport have increased.
- **Price "Sweet Spot"**: It's currently the low season after Easter. Reports indicate that hotel and flight prices are generally more affordable compared to summer, and attractions are less crowded—making it the perfect time for an in-depth, slow-paced trip.
