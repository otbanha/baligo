### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Aviation Resilience and Market Shifts

- **Flight Normalization**: Although volcanic ash clouds from Flores Island caused disruptions, Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) is operating normally today. Official data shows that despite geopolitical challenges, international tourist arrivals in the first quarter exceeded 1.6 million, demonstrating strong recovery.
- **Developing New Markets**: The tourism industry (ASITA) is shifting its focus to Central Asian countries like Kazakhstan, aiming to position Bali as an international transit hub akin to Singapore or Bangkok. This is expected to bring a more diverse range of visitors in the coming years.

### 🌤️ Weather Forecast

- **Today's Overview**: **Bali** is sunny today

### 📄 Topics of Interest for Tourists: Fees and Transportation

- **Tourist Levy Inspection**: Authorities have recently intensified inspections of the 150,000 IDR **Tourist Levy**. All international tourists (including children) are reminded to pay this fee via the Love Bali official website before arrival, as random checks at the airport have increased.
- **Price "Sweet Spot"**: Currently, it's the off-season following Easter. News reports indicate that hotel and flight prices are generally more favorable compared to the summer peak, and tourist attractions are less crowded, making it an ideal time for slow, immersive travel.
