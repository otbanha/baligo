## Trip Overview

- **Travel Dates:** April 12 to April 17, 2023
- **Travelers:** 2 people (Mom + her boyfriend). I arranged everything for my mom, and they traveled on their own.

## Pre-Trip Preparation

I gathered valuable Bali travel tips from Facebook groups. Now back in Taiwan, I hope to share my mom's travel experiences to help others plan a wonderful trip.

## Foreword:

Since my mom is busy with work, I planned this trip for her. She loves photography and isn't picky about food, but she's a vegetarian, so I had to find restaurants that offer vegetarian options in advance (my mom is easygoing—she just needs to be full and eat meat-free meals).

**Visa + Customs + Tourism Tax:** I handled all their pre-trip procedures online, and they were out of the airport in about 30 minutes.

Also, when paying the tourism tax online, I noticed the first payment always fails—you need to wait a moment and try again for it to go through.

**Private Car & Guide:** I arranged a private car and guide for my mom and her group—Ani and her husband. In online communities, someone recommended a guide-plus-driver service format, and I thought it was a great idea. Luckily, I booked Ani; she and her husband served as our guide and driver.

## Day 1: Uluwatu Temple Adventure

My mom and her group kicked off their Bali adventure at Uluwatu Temple. Although the weather wasn't ideal and they missed the sunset, which was a bit of a shame, they still felt the mystical charm of this sacred temple. They wanted to buy tickets for the fire dance on-site, but unfortunately, they were all sold out, so they didn't get to see Bali's most stunning fire dance show.

## Day 2: A Fairytale Journey in Ubud

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c866025d6c35db35dc6e898935c8ca44.jpg)

Photo: Provided by Chuang Ling Chun

Ani knew my mom loves taking photos, so she recommended a swing experience—400k per person, plus a 200k fee for renting a dress—well worth it. The photographer's skills were top-notch; for about USD$39 per person (600k), you can get stunning shots like these. A tip for anyone wanting to capture that fairy-tale flying look: definitely rent the dress, as their specially designed skirts are long and voluminous, creating a flowing effect in the air.

Ubud Swing Deals Here:

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供 + 網路截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3fd5654938f7fafc790509041021cf0.jpg)

Photo: Provided by Chuang Ling Chun + Screenshot from the web

In the evening, they checked into the Mason Elephant Lodge. The close-up elephant encounters there were unforgettable. I had stayed here ten years ago, and those beautiful memories are still deeply etched in my heart. This time, they were lucky enough to get up close with the elephants. My mom said one of the most memorable experiences was when the elephants personally gave them a ride from their room to the restaurant for dinner. Even more fortunate, during their stay, they happened to be there for the owner's birthday celebration and were invited to join the dinner banquet for free.

For travelers who love elephants, this is a fantastic experience. The elephant park seems to treat its elephants well, not just as money-making tools, and its revenue goes toward Sumatran elephant conservation. Aside from elephant rides and bathing, there are no other animal performances, so concerned visitors can feel at ease. Guests can also spend USD$3 to buy fruit and feed the elephants by hand. If you want to ride an elephant to a meal, you'll need to dine at the park (either by choosing a meal-inclusive package when booking or purchasing it separately). For those who don't want to spend extra, you can still get up close with the elephants and enjoy their gentle, adorable nature. (PS. Elephant rides will be completely banned across Bali starting in 2026.)

In the evening, I arranged for them to stay at Grün Canggu Garden. When I first saw its unique design while booking, I was instantly drawn in. It feels like a treehouse—the first floor is just stairs, halfway up you'll find the bathroom and shower, the second floor is a small platform, and the third floor is the bedroom. The room design is very open: aside from the enclosed bathroom and shower, the rest is all open space. Surrounded by trees, with the sounds of insects and birds, it feels like you're immersed in nature, able to fully enjoy the leisurely vacation vibe.

## Day 6: Farewell to Bali

Since their flight was in the afternoon, my mom and the others went back to the massage shop at 10 a.m. for another session. Afterward, they returned to the villa and checked out at noon.

I booked an airport transfer service online in advance. The driver arrived early, and the price was reasonable.

> ▶Highly rated on Klook: [Bali International Airport Transfer](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=808796&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Fairport-transfers%2Fservice%2Fdps-ngurah-rai-bali-international-airport%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Dd218d2ab66)

The advantage of booking online is that you know the exact fare when you pay by card, so you don’t need to set aside cash—just spend all your Indonesian rupiah on the last day! Next time I visit Bali, I’ll definitely use this service again.

## Afterword

1. If you like mangosteen, be sure to buy some to try. At the supermarket, 12 mangosteens cost just USD$3—cheap and delicious. Mom also asked the guide to take them to a roadside stall for durian, where one durian was only USD$10. (Related article: [Bali Mangosteen Season](/blog/bali-mangosteen-season/))
2. When traveling in Bali, you’ll often encounter traffic congestion, and the road conditions aren’t great, so the estimated times on Google Maps are just for reference. (Related article: [Don’t Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/))
3. Mom and her group were very satisfied with Ani’s service, so on the last day, they gave her and her husband a little extra tip. We also tipped the luggage handlers and massage staff as a thank-you. (Related article: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How to Tip? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw))
4. When getting into the car, ask the driver or guide to take your group to exchange money—they’ll bring you to a trustworthy place. (Related article: [Independent Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/))
5. Locally, a SIM card costs USD$19 for 18GB of data, while roaming with 5GB costs USD$16. I recommend using roaming, since Mom didn’t need to look up much during the trip, and most hotels offer free Wi-Fi—5GB was more than enough. (Related article: [Essential Bali Travel Guide: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/))

Mom’s review of this trip was that she had a fantastic time and really enjoyed the fun of taking photos. She especially praised the hotels I picked—each one had its own unique charm, allowing her to capture many beautiful pictures. For her, as long as she can take plenty of photos, it’s a wonderful journey.

She felt that not seeing the sunset at Uluwatu Temple was a big regret! But after trying the swing in Ubud, her friends all complimented her photos for being stunning.

The private car tour in Bali made her feel free and convenient, and the price was very reasonable. The only downside was the food—she didn’t care for Bali’s heavy flavors and wished there were more options with less oil and salt.

This Bali trip was full of surprises and wonderful memories. Whether visiting temples, climbing volcanoes, or getting up close with elephants, every moment was unforgettable. I hope Mom’s travelogue can offer some inspiration and help for those planning a trip to Bali, and I look forward to stepping foot on this paradise island again.
