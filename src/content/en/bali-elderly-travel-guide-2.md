## Trip Overview

- **Travel Dates:** April 12 to April 17, 2023
- **Travelers:** 2 people (my mom + her boyfriend). I arranged the trip for them, and they traveled on their own.

## Pre-Trip Preparation

I gathered valuable Bali travel information from Facebook groups. Now back in Taiwan, I hope to share my mom's travel experience to help others plan a pleasant journey.

## Introduction:

Since my mom was busy with work, I arranged this trip for her. My mom is a photography enthusiast and isn't picky about food, but she is a vegetarian, so I needed to find vegetarian-friendly restaurants in advance (my mom is quite easygoing when traveling—she just needs to be full and avoid meat).

**Visa + Customs + Tourism Tax:** I helped them complete all pre-trip procedures online, and they walked out of the airport in just about 30 minutes.

Additionally, when paying the tourism tax online, I noticed that the first payment attempt often fails. You need to wait a moment and try again for it to succeed.

**Private Car Guide:** I arranged for my mom and her group to have Ani and her husband as their private car guides. In online communities, someone recommended a service format where you have both a guide and a driver, which I thought was a great idea. Luckily, I booked Ani, and she and her husband served as our guide and driver.

Their Bali adventure began at Uluwatu Temple. Although the weather wasn’t ideal and they missed the sunset, they still felt the mystical charm of this sacred temple. They had planned to buy tickets for the fire dance on-site, but unfortunately, it was sold out, so they missed Bali’s most stunning fire dance performance.

## Day 2: A Fairy Tale Journey in Ubud

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c866025d6c35db35dc6e898935c8ca44.jpg)

Knowing that my mom loves taking photos, Ani recommended a swing experience for 400k per person, plus 200k for renting a dress — totally worth it. The photographer's skills were top-notch, and for just $20 USD (600k) per person, we got stunning photos like the ones shown here. A tip for those wanting to capture ethereal flying fairy shots: definitely rent a dress. Their specially designed dresses have long, flowing skirts that create the perfect floating effect in the air.

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供 + 網路截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3fd5654938f7fafc790509041021cf0.jpg)

Photo provided by Chuang Ling Chun + Screenshot

In the evening, we stayed at Mason Elephant Lodge, where the up-close elephant encounters were unforgettable. I had stayed here ten years ago, and those beautiful memories are still deeply etched in my heart. This time, we were fortunate to get close to the elephants. My mom said one of the most memorable experiences was being transported from our room to the restaurant by an elephant during dinner. We were even luckier to encounter the owner's birthday celebration during our stay and were invited to join the dinner feast for free.

For travelers who love elephants, this is an exceptional experience. The elephant park seems to treat the elephants well, not just as money-making tools, and the park's income is used for Sumatran elephant conservation. Besides elephant rides and bathing, there are no other animal performances, so even concerned travelers can feel at ease. Additionally, visitors can spend $3 USD to buy fruits and feed the elephants themselves. If you want to experience riding an elephant to dinner, you'll need to dine at the park (you can choose a meal-inclusive package when booking or purchase separately). For those who don't want to spend extra, you can still get close to the elephants and feel their gentleness and cuteness. (PS. Elephant riding activities will be completely banned across Bali by 2026).

In the evening, I arranged for them to stay at Grün Canggu Garden. When I was booking, the unique design immediately caught my attention. It gives off a treehouse vibe, with stairs on the first floor, a bathroom halfway up, a small platform on the second floor, and the room on the third floor. The room design is very open, with only the bathroom being enclosed, while the rest of the areas are open spaces. Surrounded by trees, the sounds of insects and birds make you feel like you're immersed in nature, allowing you to fully enjoy the leisurely pace of the vacation.

Since their flight back was scheduled for the afternoon, my mom and her group returned to the massage parlor they visited the previous day at 10 AM for another session. Afterward, they went back to the villa and stayed until the 12 PM checkout time.

We had pre-booked airport transfer service online, and the driver arrived early at an affordable price.

## Postscript

1. If you love mangosteens, you must try them here. At the supermarket, 12 mangosteens cost only USD$3—cheap and delicious! My mom even asked the guide to take them to a roadside stall to buy durian, where one durian cost just USD$10. (Read more: [Mangosteen Season in Indonesia/Bali](/blog/bali-mangosteen-season/))
2. Traffic in Bali can be congested, and the road conditions aren’t great, so Google Maps’ estimated travel times should be taken with a grain of salt. (Read more: [Don’t Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/))
3. My mom and her group were very satisfied with Ani’s service, so they gave her and her husband a little extra tip on the last day. We also tipped the luggage porter and massage therapists as a gesture of gratitude. (Read more: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw))
4. Ask the driver or guide to take you to a trusted money exchange shop when you get in the car. (Read more: [Currency Exchange Guide for Independent Travelers](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/))
5. Local SIM cards cost USD$19 for 18GB of data, while roaming with 5GB costs USD$16. I recommend using roaming, as my mom didn’t need to look up much information during her trip, and most hotels offer free Wi-Fi. The 5GB was more than enough. (Read more: [Essential Guide: How to Get a Bali SIM Card?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/))

My mom thoroughly enjoyed this trip and loved the opportunity to take lots of photos. She especially praised the hotels I picked, each with its unique charm, allowing her to capture many beautiful shots. For her, a great trip is one where she can take plenty of photos.

She felt that not seeing the sunset at Uluwatu Temple was a big regret! However, after experiencing the swing in Ubud, her friends all complimented her on how beautiful her photos turned out.

Exploring Bali by private car gave her a sense of freedom and convenience, and the price was very affordable. The only downside was the food - she wasn't fond of Bali's strong flavors and wished there were more options with less oil and salt.

This Bali trip was filled with surprises and wonderful memories. Whether it was visiting temples, hiking up volcanoes, or getting up close with elephants, every moment was unforgettable. I hope Mom's travelogue can provide some reference and help for those planning a trip to Bali. Looking forward to stepping foot on this island paradise again!
