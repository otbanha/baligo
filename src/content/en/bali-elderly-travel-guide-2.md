## Trip Overview

- **Travel Dates:** April 12 to April 17, 2023
- **Travelers:** 2 people (Mom + her boyfriend). I arranged everything for my mom, and they traveled on their own.

## Pre-Trip Planning

I gathered valuable Bali travel tips from Facebook groups. Now back in Taiwan, I hope sharing my mom's travel experience can help others plan a wonderful trip.

## Foreword:

Since my mom is busy with work, I planned this trip for her. She loves photography and isn't picky about food, but she's vegetarian, so I needed to find restaurants offering vegetarian options in advance (my mom is easygoing—she just needs to be full and eat meat-free meals).

**Visa + Customs + Tourism Tax:** I handled all their pre-trip procedures online, and they walked out of the airport in just about 30 minutes.

Also, when paying the tourism tax online, I found that the first payment always fails—you need to wait a moment and try again for it to go through.

**Private Car & Guide:** I arranged a private car and guide for my mom and her group—Ani and her husband. In online communities, someone recommended a guide-plus-driver service, and I thought it was a great setup. Luckily, I booked Ani; she and her husband served as our guide and driver.

## Day 1: Uluwatu Temple Adventure

My mom and her group started their Bali adventure at Uluwatu Temple. Although the weather wasn't ideal and they missed the sunset, which was a bit of a shame, they still felt the mystical charm of this sacred temple. They had planned to buy Kecak dance tickets on-site, but unfortunately, they were all sold out, so they didn't get to see Bali's most stunning fire dance show.

## Day 2: A Magical Journey in Ubud

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c866025d6c35db35dc6e898935c8ca44.jpg)

Photo: Provided by Chuang Ling Chun

Ani knew my mom loves taking photos, so she recommended a swing experience—400k per person, plus a 200k fee for renting a dress—well worth it. The photographer's skills were top-notch; for about USD$39 per person (600k), you can get shots as stunning as these. A tip for anyone wanting that fairy-tale flying look: definitely rent the dress. Their specially made skirts are long and voluminous, creating that ethereal floating effect in the air.

Get the best deal on Ubud swings here:

> [Ubud Swing: The 8 Best Swing Photo Spots in Bali | Ubud Swing & Dress Rental Guide](/blog/ubud-jungle-swings-photo-guide/)

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供 + 網路截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3fd5654938f7fafc790509041021cf0.jpg)

Photo: Provided by Chuang Ling Chun + Screenshot from the web

In the evening, they checked into the Mason Elephant Lodge. The close-up encounters with elephants there were unforgettable. I had stayed here ten years ago, and those fond memories are still etched in my heart. This time, they were lucky enough to get up close with the elephants. My mom said one of the most memorable experiences was when an elephant personally carried them from their room to the restaurant for dinner. Even more fortunate, their stay coincided with the owner's birthday celebration, and they were invited to join the dinner banquet for free.

For travelers who love elephants, this is a fantastic experience. The elephant park seems to treat its elephants well, not just as money-making tools, and the park's revenue goes toward Sumatran elephant conservation. Aside from elephant rides and bathing, there are no other animal performances, so concerned visitors can feel at ease. Additionally, guests can spend USD$3 to buy fruit and feed the elephants by hand. If you want to experience riding an elephant to a meal, you'll need to dine at the park (either by choosing a meal-inclusive package when booking or purchasing it separately). For those who don't want to spend extra, you can still get up close with the elephants and enjoy their gentle, lovable nature. (PS. Elephant riding will be completely banned across Bali in 2026.)

In the evening, I arranged for them to stay at Grün Canggu Garden. When I first saw this place's unique design while booking, I was instantly drawn in. It feels like a treehouse: the first floor is the staircase, halfway up you'll find the toilet and bathroom, the second floor is a small platform, and the third floor is the bedroom. The room design is very open—except for the enclosed toilet and bathroom, all other areas are open spaces. Surrounded by trees, with the sounds of insects and birds, it feels like you're immersed in nature, allowing you to fully enjoy a leisurely vacation.

Since I arranged for them to stay in a villa on the last night, we hoped to check in early. That day, I had the guide Ani take them on a simple visit to Tanah Lot Temple and buy some local souvenirs.

## Day 6: Farewell to Bali

Since their flight was in the afternoon, my mom and the others went back to the massage shop from the day before at 10 a.m. for another session. Afterward, they returned to the villa and checked out at noon.

I had booked an airport transfer online in advance; the driver arrived early, and the price was reasonable.

## Afterword

1. If you like mangosteens, be sure to buy some. At the supermarket, 12 mangosteens cost just USD$3—cheap and delicious. Mom also asked the guide to take them to a roadside stall for durian, where one durian was only USD$10. (Read more: [Indonesia/Bali Mangosteen Season](/blog/bali-mangosteen-season/))
2. When traveling in Bali, you're likely to encounter heavy traffic and poor road conditions, so Google Maps estimated travel times are for reference only. (Read more: [Don't Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/))
3. Mom and her group were very satisfied with Ani's service, so on the last day, they gave her and her husband a little extra tip. We also tipped the luggage handlers and massage attendants as a token of gratitude. (Read more: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How to Tip? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw))
4. When getting into the car, ask the driver-guide to take your mom and her group to exchange money—he'll bring them to a trustworthy place. (Read more: [Free & Easy Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/))
5. Locally, a SIM card costs USD$19 for 18GB of data, while roaming with 5GB costs USD$16. I recommend using roaming, as Mom didn't need to look up much information during her trip, and most hotels offer free Wi-Fi—5GB was more than enough. (Read more: [Essential Bali Travel: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/))

Mom's verdict on this trip was that she had a blast—she really enjoyed the fun of taking photos. She especially praised the hotels I picked, saying each one had its own unique charm, allowing her to capture many beautiful pictures. For her, as long as she can snap plenty of photos, it's a wonderful journey.

She felt that not seeing the sunset at Uluwatu Temple was a big regret! But after experiencing the swing in Ubud, her friends all complimented her photos.

The private car tour in Bali made her feel free and convenient, and the price was very affordable. The only downside was the food—she didn't care for Bali's heavy flavors and wished there were more options with less oil and salt.

This Bali trip was full of surprises and wonderful memories, whether exploring temples, climbing volcanoes, or getting up close with elephants—every moment was unforgettable. I hope Mom's travelogue can offer some reference and help for those planning a trip to Bali, and I look forward to stepping foot on this paradise island again.
