## Trip Overview

- **Travel Dates:** April 12 to April 17, 2023
- **Travelers:** 2 people (Mother + her boyfriend). I arranged everything for them, and they traveled independently.

## Pre-Trip Preparation

Having gathered valuable Bali travel information from Facebook groups, now back in Taiwan, I hope to share my mother's travel experience to help others plan enjoyable trips.

## Introduction:

Since my mother was busy with work, I arranged this trip for her. My mother is a photography enthusiast and not picky about food, but she is vegetarian, so I needed to find vegetarian-friendly restaurants in advance (my mother is quite easygoing when traveling - as long as she can eat her fill without meat, she's happy).

**Visa + Customs + Tourism Tax:** I helped them complete all pre-trip procedures online, and they walked out of the airport in just about 30 minutes.

Additionally, when paying the tourism tax online, I noticed that the first payment attempt always failed. It was necessary to wait a moment and try again for it to succeed.

**Private Tour Guide:** I arranged a private tour guide for my mom and her group—Ani and her husband. In online communities, someone recommended a private car service format that includes both a guide and a driver, which I thought was an excellent arrangement. Fortunately, I booked Ani, and she and her husband served as our guide and driver.

## Day 1: Uluwatu Temple Adventure

Their Bali adventure began at Uluwatu Temple. Although the weather wasn’t ideal and they missed the sunset, they still felt the mystical charm of this sacred temple. They had planned to buy tickets for the fire dance on-site, but unfortunately, they were sold out, so they missed Bali’s most breathtaking fire dance performance.

## Day 2: A Fairy Tale Journey in Ubud

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c866025d6c35db35dc6e898935c8ca44.jpg)

Knowing my mom loves taking photos, Ani recommended a swing experience, costing $27 per person, plus $13 for renting a dress—great value for money. The photographer's skills were top-notch, and for just $20 per person (600k), you can capture stunning photos like the ones shown. A tip for those wanting to take ethereal flying photos: definitely rent a dress. Their specially designed dresses have long, flowing skirts that create a beautiful floating effect in the air.

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供 + 網路截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3fd5654938f7fafc790509041021cf0.jpg)

Photo provided by Chuang Ling Chun + Screenshot

In the evening, we stayed at Mason Elephant Lodge, where the close-up elephant experience was unforgettable. I stayed here ten years ago, and those wonderful memories are still deeply etched in my heart. This time, we were fortunate to have close encounters with elephants. My mom said one of the most memorable experiences was being transported from our room to the restaurant by an elephant for dinner. We were also lucky to attend the owner's birthday celebration during our stay and were invited to a complimentary dinner banquet.

For travelers who love elephants, this is an excellent experience. The elephant park seems to treat the elephants well, not merely as money-making tools, and the park's income is used for Sumatran elephant conservation. Besides elephant rides and bathing, there are no other animal performances, so concerned travelers can rest assured. Additionally, visitors can spend $3 to buy fruit and feed the elephants themselves. If you want to experience riding an elephant to dinner, you need to dine at the park (you can choose a meal-inclusive package when booking or purchase separately). For those who don't want to spend extra, you can still get up close with the elephants and feel their gentleness and cuteness. (PS. Elephant riding activities will be completely banned across Bali by 2026).

In the evening, I arranged for them to stay at Grün Canggu Garden. When I first saw the unique design of this place while booking, I was immediately captivated. It gives off a treehouse vibe, with stairs on the first floor, a bathroom halfway up, a small platform on the second floor, and the room on the third floor. The room design is very open, with only the bathroom enclosed, and the rest of the areas open to the surroundings. Surrounded by trees, with the sounds of insects and birds, it feels like being immersed in nature, allowing one to fully enjoy the leisurely vacation.

## Day 6: Farewell to Bali

Since their flight back was scheduled for the afternoon, my mom and her companions returned to the massage shop from the previous day at 10 AM for another session. They then went back to the villa and checked out at noon.

I had pre-booked airport transfer service online, and the driver arrived early at a reasonable price.

## Postscript

1. If you love mangosteens, you must try them. At the supermarket, 12 mangosteens cost only USD$3—cheap and delicious. My mom also asked the guide to take them to a roadside stall to buy durian, where one durian cost only USD$10. (Further reading: [Indonesia/Bali Mangosteen Season](/blog/bali-mangosteen-season/))
2. When traveling in Bali, you may encounter heavy traffic and poor road conditions, so the estimated time on Google Maps is for reference only. (Further reading: [Don't Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/))
3. My mom and her companions were very satisfied with Ani's service, so they gave her and her husband a little extra tip on the last day. We also tipped the luggage porters and massage therapists as a gesture of gratitude. (Further reading: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw))
4. Ask the driver or guide to take you to a reliable money exchange shop when you get in the car. (Further reading: [Currency Exchange Guide for Independent Travelers](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/))
5. Locally, a SIM card costs USD$19 and provides 18GB of data, while roaming with 5GB costs USD$16. I recommend using roaming services because my mom didn't need to look up information during her trip, and most hotels offer free Wi-Fi, so 5GB was more than enough. (Further reading: [Essential for Bali Travel: How to Get a Bali SIM Card/eSIM?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/))

Mom thoroughly enjoyed this trip and had a great time taking photos. She especially praised the hotels I chose, each with its unique charm, allowing her to capture many beautiful pictures. For her, being able to take plenty of photos makes for a wonderful journey.

She felt that missing the sunset at Uluwatu Temple was a big regret! However, after experiencing the swings in Ubud, her friends all complimented her beautiful photos.

The private car tour in Bali gave her a sense of freedom and convenience, and the price was very reasonable. The only downside was the food; she wasn't fond of Bali's strong flavors and wished there were more options with less oil and salt.

This Bali trip was filled with surprises and wonderful memories. Whether it was visiting temples, climbing volcanoes, or getting up close with elephants, every moment was unforgettable. We hope Mom's travelogue can provide some reference and help for those planning a trip to Bali, and we look forward to setting foot on this island paradise again.
