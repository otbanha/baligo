## Trip Overview

- **Travel Dates:** April 12 to April 17, 2023
- **Travelers:** 2 people (Mom + her boyfriend). I arranged everything for Mom, and they went on their own.

## Pre-Trip Preparation

I gathered valuable Bali travel tips from Facebook groups. Now back in Taiwan, I hope to share Mom's travel experiences to help others plan a joyful trip.

## Introduction:

**Key Takeaways**: Practical tips for planning a Bali trip for elders—① Complete **visa, customs declaration, and tourist tax online in advance**; it took only about 30 minutes to exit the airport upon arrival; ② Online tourist tax payment **often fails on the first try**—just wait a moment and retry for success; ③ If the elders are vegetarian, be sure to **confirm vegetarian restaurants** before including them in the itinerary.

Since my mom is busy with work, I arranged this trip for her. She loves photography and isn't picky about food, but she's a vegetarian, so I had to find restaurants offering vegetarian options in advance (she's easygoing—just needs to be full and meat-free).

**Visa + Customs + Tourist Tax:** I handled all their pre-trip procedures online, and they walked out of the airport in just about 30 minutes.

Also, when paying the tourist tax online, I noticed the first payment always fails—you need to wait a bit and try again for it to go through.

**Private Car & Guide:** I arranged a private car and guide for my mom—Ani and her husband. In online communities, someone recommended a guide-plus-driver service, which I thought was a great setup. Lucky for me, I booked Ani, and she and her husband served as our guide and driver.

## Day 1: Exploring Uluwatu Temple

My mom and her group started their Bali adventure at Uluwatu Temple. Although the weather wasn't ideal and they missed the sunset—a bit of a shame—they still felt the mystical charm of this sacred temple. They planned to buy Kecak dance tickets on-site, but unfortunately, they were all sold out, so they didn't get to see Bali's most stunning fire dance show.

## Day 2: A Fairy-Tale Journey in Ubud

Ani knew my mom loves taking photos, so she recommended a swing experience at 400k per person, plus 200k for a dress rental—well worth it. The photographer's skills were top-notch; for about USD$39 (600k) per person, you can get photos like these. A tip for anyone wanting that ethereal fairy look: definitely rent the dress. Their specially designed skirts are long and voluminous, creating that floating-in-the-air effect.

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供 + 網路截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3fd5654938f7fafc790509041021cf0.jpg)

Photo: Courtesy of Chuang Ling Chun + Screenshot from online

In the evening, we checked into the Mason Elephant Lodge. The close-up elephant encounters here were unforgettable. I had stayed here ten years ago, and those beautiful memories are still deeply etched in my heart. This time, we were fortunate to get up close with the elephants. My mom said one of the most memorable experiences was when the elephants personally carried them from their rooms to the restaurant for dinner. Even more fortunate, they happened to be there during the owner's birthday celebration and were invited to join the dinner banquet for free.

For travelers who love elephants, this is an amazing experience. The elephant park seems to treat the elephants well, not just as tools for profit. The park's revenue is used for the conservation of Sumatran elephants. Aside from elephant riding and bathing, there are no other animal performances, so concerned visitors can feel at ease. Additionally, guests can buy fruit for USD$3 to feed the elephants themselves. If you want to experience being taken to dinner by an elephant, you need to dine at the park (you can choose a package that includes meals when booking, or purchase it separately). For those who don't want to spend extra, you can still get up close with the elephants and feel their gentle and adorable nature. (PS. Elephant riding across all of Bali will be completely banned in 2026.)

In the evening, I arranged for them to stay at Grün Canggu Garden. When I first saw its unique design while booking, I was instantly captivated. It feels like a treehouse: the first floor is the staircase, halfway up you'll find the toilet and bathroom, the second floor is a small terrace, and the third floor is the bedroom. The room design is very open—except for the enclosed toilet and bathroom, all other areas are open spaces. Surrounded by trees and filled with the sounds of insects and birds, it feels like being immersed in nature, allowing you to fully enjoy a leisurely vacation.

Since they had an afternoon flight back, my mom and her group went back to the same massage place from the day before at 10 a.m. for another session. Afterward, they returned to the villa and checked out at noon.

## Afterword

1. If you like mangosteens, be sure to buy some. At the supermarket, 12 mangosteens cost just USD$3—cheap and delicious. My mom also asked the guide to take them to a roadside stall for durian, where one durian was only USD$10. (Related article: [Bali Mangosteen Season](/en/blog/bali-mangosteen-season/))
2. When traveling in Bali, you're likely to encounter heavy traffic and poor road conditions, so the estimated times on Google Maps are just for reference. (Related article: [Don't Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/en/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/))
3. My mom and her group were very satisfied with Ani's service, so on the last day, they gave her and her husband a little extra tip. We also tipped the luggage handlers and massage therapists as a thank-you. (Related article: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How to Tip? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw))
4. When getting into the car, ask the driver or guide to take your group to exchange money—they'll bring you to a trustworthy place. (Related article: [DIY Currency Exchange Guide](/en/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/))
5. Locally, a SIM card costs USD$19 for 18GB of data, while roaming with 5GB costs USD$16. I recommend using roaming, since my mom didn't need to look up much during the trip, and most hotels offer free Wi-Fi—5GB was plenty. (Related article: [Essential Bali Travel: How to Get Internet/SIM Card in Bali?](/en/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/))

My mom's verdict on this trip was that she had a blast and really enjoyed the fun of taking photos. She especially praised the hotels I picked—each one had its own unique charm, allowing her to snap many beautiful pictures. For her, as long as she can take lots of photos, it's a wonderful journey.

She felt that missing the sunset at Uluwatu Temple was a big regret! But after trying the swing in Ubud, her friends all complimented her photos.

The private car tour in Bali made her feel free and convenient, and the price was very reasonable. The only downside was the food—she didn't care for Bali's heavy flavors and wished there were more options with less oil and salt.

This Bali trip was full of surprises and wonderful memories, whether exploring temples, climbing volcanoes, or getting up close with elephants—every moment was unforgettable. I hope my mom's travelogue can offer some inspiration and help for those planning a trip to Bali, and I look forward to stepping foot on this paradise island again.
