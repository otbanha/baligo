## Trip Overview

- **Travel Dates:** April 12 to April 17, 2023
- **Travelers:** 2 people (Mom + her boyfriend). I arranged everything for Mom and they went on their own.

## Pre-Trip Preparation

I gathered valuable Bali travel information from Facebook groups. Now back in Taiwan, I hope to share Mom's travel experiences to help others plan enjoyable trips.

## Introduction:

Since Mom was busy with work, I arranged this trip for her. Mom is a photography enthusiast and isn't picky about food, but she's vegetarian, so I needed to find vegetarian-friendly restaurants in advance (Mom is quite flexible when traveling - as long as she can eat her fill without meat, she's happy).

**Visa + Customs + Tourism Tax:** I helped them complete all the pre-arrival procedures online, and they walked out of the airport in just about 30 minutes.

Additionally, when paying the tourism tax online, I noticed that the first payment attempt always failed. I had to wait a moment and try again for it to go through successfully.

**Private Car Guide:** I arranged for my mom and her group to have Ani and her husband as their private car guides. In online communities, someone recommended a service format that includes both a guide and a driver, which I thought was an excellent arrangement. Fortunately, I booked Ani, and she and her husband served as our guide and driver.

## Day 1: Uluwatu Temple Adventure

Their Bali adventure began at Uluwatu Temple. Although the weather wasn't ideal and they missed the sunset, they still felt the mystical charm of this sacred temple. They had hoped to buy tickets for the fire dance on-site, but unfortunately, it was sold out, so they missed Bali's most breathtaking fire dance performance.

## Day 2: A Fairyland Journey in Ubud

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c866025d6c35db35dc6e898935c8ca44.jpg)

Knowing that my mom loves taking photos, Ani recommended a swing experience for 400k per person, plus an additional 200k for renting a dress—totally worth it. The photographer's skills were top-notch, and for just $20 (600k) per person, we got stunning photos like the ones shown here. A tip for those wanting to capture ethereal flying shots: definitely rent a dress. Their specially designed dresses have long, flowing skirts that create the perfect floating effect in the air.

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供 + 網路截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3fd5654938f7fafc790509041021cf0.jpg)

Photo provided by Chuang Ling Chun + Screenshot

In the evening, we stayed at Mason Elephant Lodge, where the up-close elephant encounters were unforgettable. I had stayed here ten years ago, and those beautiful memories are still deeply etched in my heart. This time, we were fortunate to get close to the elephants, and my mom said one of the most memorable experiences was being transported from our room to the dining area by an elephant during dinner. We were even luckier to stumble upon the owner’s birthday celebration during our stay and were invited to join the dinner feast for free.

For travelers who love elephants, this is an exceptional experience. The elephant park seems to treat the elephants well, not merely as money-making tools, and the park’s income is used for Sumatran elephant conservation. Apart from elephant rides and bathing, there are no other animal performances, so concerned travelers can rest assured. Visitors also have the opportunity to spend $3 to buy fruit and feed the elephants themselves. If you want to experience riding an elephant to dinner, you’ll need to dine at the park (you can choose a meal-inclusive package when booking or purchase separately). For those who prefer not to spend extra, you can still get up close with the elephants and feel their gentleness and charm. (PS. Elephant riding activities will be completely banned across Bali by 2026).

In the evening, I arranged for them to stay at Grün Canggu Garden. When I first saw the unique design of this place while booking, I was immediately captivated. It gives off a treehouse vibe, with stairs on the first floor, a bathroom and shower halfway up, a small platform on the second floor, and the room on the third floor. The room design is very open, with only the bathroom and shower being enclosed, while the rest of the areas are open spaces. Surrounded by trees, with the sounds of insects and birds, it feels like you're immersed in nature, allowing you to fully enjoy the leisurely pace of your vacation.

Since they had a flight to catch that afternoon, my mom and her group went back to the massage parlor from the previous day at 10 AM for another session. Afterward, they returned to the villa and checked out at noon.

We had pre-booked airport transfer service online, and the driver arrived early at an affordable price.

## Epilogue

1. If you love mangosteens, make sure to try them. At the supermarket, 12 mangosteens cost just USD$3—cheap and delicious. My mom also asked the guide to take them to a roadside stall to buy durian, where one durian cost only USD$10. (Read more: [Mangosteen Season in Bali](/blog/bali-mangosteen-season/))
2. Traffic in Bali can be congested, and the roads aren’t great, so Google Maps’ estimated travel times should be taken with a grain of salt. (Read more: [Don’t Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/))
3. My mom was very satisfied with Ani’s service, so she gave her and her husband a little extra tip on the last day. We also tipped the luggage handlers and massage therapists as a gesture of gratitude. (Read more: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much?](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw))
4. Ask the driver or guide to take you to a reliable money exchange shop when you get in the car. (Read more: [Currency Exchange Guide for Independent Travelers](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/))
5. A local SIM card costs USD$19 for 18GB of data, while roaming with 5GB costs USD$16. I recommend using roaming since my mom didn’t need to look up much information during her trip, and most hotels offer free Wi-Fi—5GB was more than enough. (Read more: [Essential Guide: How to Get a SIM Card or eSIM in Bali](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/))

My mom thoroughly enjoyed this trip and loved the joy of taking photos. She especially praised the hotels I picked, each with its unique charm, allowing her to capture many beautiful shots. For her, a great trip is one where she can take plenty of photos.

She felt that missing the sunset at Uluwatu Temple was a big regret! However, after experiencing the swing in Ubud, her friends all complimented her photos.

The private car tour in Bali gave her a sense of freedom and convenience, and the price was very affordable. The only downside was the food—she wasn't fond of Bali's strong flavors and wished there were more options with less oil and salt.

This trip to Bali was filled with surprises and wonderful memories. Whether it was visiting temples, hiking up a volcano, or getting up close with elephants, every moment was unforgettable. I hope Mom's travelogue can provide some references and help for those planning a trip to Bali. I look forward to setting foot on this island paradise again.
