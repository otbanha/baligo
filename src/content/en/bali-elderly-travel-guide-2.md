## Trip Overview

- **Travel Dates:** April 12 to April 17, 2023
- **Travelers:** 2 people (Mom + her boyfriend). I arranged everything for my mom, and they traveled on their own.

## Pre-Trip Preparation

I gathered valuable Bali travel tips from Facebook groups. Now back in Taiwan, I hope to share my mom's travel experiences to help others plan a wonderful trip.

## Foreword:

Since my mom is busy with work, I planned this trip for her. She loves photography and isn't picky about food, but she's a vegetarian, so I needed to find restaurants offering vegetarian options in advance (my mom is easygoing when traveling—she just needs to be full and have no meat).

**Visa + Customs + Tourist Tax:** I completed all their pre-trip procedures online, and they walked out of the airport in just about 30 minutes.

Also, when paying the tourist tax online, I found that the first payment always fails—you need to wait a moment and try again for it to go through.

**Private Car & Guide:** I arranged a private car and guide for my mom and her group—Ani and her husband. In online communities, someone recommended a service format where the guide also drives, and I thought that was a great setup. Luckily, I booked Ani, and she and her husband served as our guide and driver.

## Day 1: Uluwatu Temple Adventure

My mom and her group started their Bali adventure at Uluwatu Temple. Although the weather wasn't ideal and they missed the sunset, which was a bit of a shame, they still felt the mystical charm of this sacred temple. They originally wanted to buy tickets for the fire dance on-site, but unfortunately, they were all sold out, so they didn't get to see the most spectacular fire dance show in Bali.

## Day 2: A Magical Journey in Ubud

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c866025d6c35db35dc6e898935c8ca44.jpg)

Photo: Provided by Chuang Ling Chun

Ani knew my mom loves taking photos, so she recommended a swing experience—400k per person, plus 200k for a dress rental, which was great value. The photographer's skills were top-notch; for about USD$39 per person (600k), you can get photos as stunning as these. I also recommend renting a dress if you want that fairy-tale flying effect—their specially made skirts are long and wide, perfect for capturing that flowing look in the air.

Ubud Swing deals here:

> [Ubud Swing: The 8 Best Swing Photo Spots in Bali | Ubud Swing & Dress Rental Guide](/blog/ubud-jungle-swings-photo-guide/)

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供 + 網路截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3fd5654938f7fafc790509041021cf0.jpg)

Photo: Provided by Chuang Ling Chun + Screenshot from the web

In the evening, they checked into the Mason Elephant Lodge. The close-up encounters with elephants there were unforgettable. I had stayed here ten years ago, and those fond memories are still etched in my heart. This time, they got to interact with the elephants up close. My mom said one of the most memorable experiences was when the elephants personally carried them from their room to the restaurant for dinner. Even more fortunate, during their stay, they happened upon the boss's birthday celebration and were invited to join the dinner banquet for free.

For travelers who love elephants, this is a fantastic experience. The elephant park seems to treat the animals well, not just as money-making tools, and the revenue goes toward Sumatran elephant conservation. Aside from elephant rides and bathing, there are no other animal performances, so concerned visitors can feel at ease. Guests can also spend USD$3 to buy fruit and feed the elephants themselves. If you want to ride an elephant to a meal, you'll need to dine at the park (either by choosing a meal-inclusive booking package or purchasing it separately). For those who don't want to spend extra, you can still get up close with the elephants and enjoy their gentle, adorable nature. (PS. Elephant rides will be completely banned across Bali by 2026.)

## Afterword

1. If you like mangosteen, be sure to buy some to try. At the supermarket, 12 mangosteens cost just USD$3—cheap and delicious. My mom also asked the guide to take them to a roadside stall for durian, where one durian cost only USD$10. (Related article: [Indonesia/Bali Mangosteen Season](/blog/bali-mangosteen-season/))
2. When traveling in Bali, you're likely to encounter heavy traffic, and the road conditions aren't great, so the estimated times on Google Maps are just for reference. (Related article: [Don't Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/))
3. My mom and her group were very satisfied with Ani's service, so on the last day, they gave her and her husband a little extra tip. We also tipped the luggage handlers and massage attendants as a token of gratitude. (Related article: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How to Tip? How Much to Tip in Various Situations in Bali?](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw))
4. Ask the driver-guide to take your mom and her group to exchange money as soon as they get in the car—he'll take them to a trustworthy place. (Related article: [Independent Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/))
5. Locally, a SIM card costs USD$19 for 18GB of data, while roaming with 5GB costs USD$16. I recommend using roaming, as my mom didn't need to look up much during her trip, and most hotels offer free Wi-Fi—5GB was more than enough. (Related article: [Essential Bali Travel: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/))

My mom's verdict on this trip was that she had a blast—she really enjoyed the fun of taking photos. She especially praised the hotels I picked, saying each one was unique and allowed her to capture many beautiful pictures. For her, as long as she can take plenty of photos, it's a wonderful journey.

She felt that not seeing the sunset at Uluwatu Temple was a big regret! But after trying the swing in Ubud, her friends all complimented her photos.

The private car tour in Bali made her feel free and convenient, and the price was very affordable. The only downside was the food—she didn't care for Bali's heavy flavors and wished there were more options with less oil and salt.

This Bali trip was full of surprises and wonderful memories, from visiting temples and climbing volcanoes to getting up close with elephants—every moment was unforgettable. I hope my mom's travelogue can offer some reference and help for those planning a trip to Bali, and I look forward to stepping foot on this paradise island again.
