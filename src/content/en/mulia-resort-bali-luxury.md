Located in Nusa Dua, Mulia Resort is your ultimate choice for a luxurious escape. This 5-star resort opened in 2012 and features 424 rooms, just about a 25-minute drive from Ngurah Rai International Airport. Check-in starts at 3 PM, and check-out is at noon. Perfect for families, children aged 2 to 11 stay free, making it an ideal destination for a family trip. The facilities and services here will make your Bali vacation truly unforgettable.

### **The Perfect Blend of Relaxation and Entertainment**

Mulia Resort offers a wide range of entertainment facilities, catering to both those seeking relaxation and nightlife enthusiasts. The resort features cozy bars and a nightclub, providing an excellent spot for guests who love vibrant evenings. After a tiring day, you can unwind with a massage at the spa, or relax in the hot tub and sauna. The resort's beautiful gardens are also ideal for a leisurely stroll. Whether you're looking to chill out or let loose, you'll find everything you need here.

### **Stay Active with Top-Notch Sports Facilities**

Mulia Resort boasts first-class sports facilities to keep you energized during your vacation. The resort has a well-equipped fitness center and tennis courts for those who enjoy a good workout. If water sports are your thing, non-motorized options are available for thrilling adventures at sea. You can also take a dip in the outdoor swimming pool or soak up the sun on the private beach. For diving enthusiasts, the resort offers diving experiences to explore the stunning underwater world of Bali.

### **Art Collection and Spatial Design at the Resort**

Mulia Resort is not only renowned for its luxurious facilities but also uniquely integrates contemporary artworks from around the world into every corner, turning the entire resort into an open-air art space. These pieces, created by artists from China, India, and beyond, are scattered throughout guest rooms, restaurants, lobbies, walkways, and pool areas, crafting a vacation experience that blends cultural sophistication with visual delight.

### **Serene Art in the Guest Rooms**

Upon entering a guest room, you're greeted by authorized paintings from Nanjing artist Yu Qiping. Yu specializes in traditional Chinese literati painting, interpreting classical aesthetics with a restrained contemporary flair. The figures in his works often immerse themselves in contemplation, surrounded by lotus flowers, still waters, or birds in flight—these paintings don't demand attention but quietly accompany you as you rest, infusing the private space with tranquility and depth. Amid Bali's vibrant vacation atmosphere, this serenity perfectly embodies the essence of true luxury: an inner calm.

### **Contemporary Creative Dialogue in the Lobby**

The Mulia Villas lobby showcases a massive work by Shanghai artist Pianzi. Born in Yunnan and based in Shanghai, Pianzi creates with a humorous and symbolic perspective, using her signature cat imagery and layered symbols to build scenes that balance imagination and order. Another piece transforms a traditional abacus into a structure of human faces, turning 'calculation' into a metaphor for the relationship between individuals and groups—a restrained discussion on commerce, order, and social rhythms presented in the space, inviting guests to ponder the systemic logic behind daily life.

### **The Power Narrative of Table8 Restaurant**

At the Table 8 Chinese restaurant within the resort, works by Guangzhou artist Huang Zhongyang reinterpret historical themes through a cross-cultural lens. His creations, centered on Empress Dowager Cixi, explore the literal and metaphorical meanings of 'reflection' through the imagery of mirrors, presenting power with subtle and delicate brushstrokes—not declaring, but questioning. The subtle interplay between Cixi and the monkey in the painting silently challenges power structures. In this space where tradition meets precision cooking, art leaves ample room for thought beyond taste.

### **A Tribute to Labor and Service on the Walkway**

Along the resort's walkway, Indian contemporary artist Valay Shende constructs human figures from metal discs and clock parts, exploring labor, collaboration, and collective identity. Inspired by Mumbai's famous dabbawala system, the body made of watch faces symbolizes time and reliability, while the stomach, shaped like a lunchbox, points to nourishment and need. This sculpture subtly captures the essence of hotel service: not a performance, but a steady and consistent fulfillment.

### **Water Blessings in the Pool Area**

In the courtyard pool, ocean pool, and oasis pool areas, three female statues inspired by Bali's water goddess Dewi Danu symbolize water, fertility, and the cycle of life. The lotus in the courtyard pool represents devotion and purity; the water bowl in the ocean pool signifies purification; the rice and water in the oasis pool embody nourishment and renewal. These custom pieces become key markers of the space, seamlessly connecting architecture, landscape, and local beliefs, allowing every moment by the pool to feel culturally and spiritually enriching.

Mulia Resort's curation philosophy emphasizes experience and depth over mere display. Art need not be understood all at once but unfolds gradually during your stay. In an era where contemporary luxury often chases expression and scale, Mulia chooses a more restrained path—art does not assert its presence but naturally blends into the space. This is true luxury: born from clear choices and values that stand the test of time.

## Real Guest Reviews

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fbe5c8d9366184e5c302970ce5259a43.jpg)

This was my first stay at this hotel, and I joined their morning yoga class with friends. We took a shuttle to an outdoor yoga space overlooking a vast grassland. The gentle morning breeze and birdsong made the entire experience incredibly relaxing. The class wasn't difficult—just basic poses, perfect for starting the day.

The instructor's English was simple and easy to follow, allowing guests from different countries to participate. Since seating is limited, make sure to arrive on time. The whole session felt deeply connected to nature, which was especially healing. The teacher was very friendly and even offered to take photos for us after class. Lucky for us, the sky was clear and blue—a perfect backdrop! If you want to greet the day in a relaxing yet energizing way, I highly recommend this morning yoga class.
