Located in Nusa Dua, Mulia Resort is undoubtedly the best choice for a luxurious vacation. This five-star resort, which opened in 2012, features 424 rooms and is conveniently located just a 25-minute drive from Bali International Airport. Check-in begins at 3 PM, and check-out is at 12 PM. Perfect for family trips, children aged 2 to 11 can stay for free, making it an ideal destination for family travel. The facilities and services here will make your Bali vacation truly unforgettable.

### **The Perfect Blend of Relaxation and Entertainment**

Mulia Resort offers a variety of entertainment facilities, catering to both relaxation and nightlife enthusiasts. The resort features cozy bars and nightclubs, providing an excellent venue for those who enjoy vibrant evenings. After a tiring day, you can indulge in a massage at the spa or unwind in the hot tub and sauna. The resort's beautiful gardens are also ideal for leisurely strolls and relaxation. Whether you're seeking tranquility or looking to let loose, the resort has all the amenities you need.

### **Stay Active with Top-notch Sports Facilities**

Mulia Resort boasts first-class sports facilities to keep you energized during your vacation. The resort offers a fully-equipped fitness center and tennis courts for those looking to break a sweat. If you're a fan of water sports, the resort provides non-motorized water activities for adventurous sea exploration. Additionally, you can swim in the outdoor pool or bask in the sun on the private beach. For diving enthusiasts, the resort offers diving experiences to explore Bali's stunning underwater world.

Mulia Resort offers a variety of transportation options to explore every corner of Bali with ease. Whether you need airport transfers or wish to participate in various tours during your stay, the resort's transportation services are at your convenience. If you prefer to drive yourself, the resort provides parking facilities and car rental services, allowing you to freely explore Bali's scenic beauty.

### **Exceptional Dining Experiences**

Mulia Resort features diverse dining facilities to satisfy all taste preferences. The resort's cafes and restaurants offer a wide selection, allowing you to enjoy authentic Indonesian cuisine and international dishes. In addition to dining in restaurants, the resort provides room service for convenient in-room meals. Daily buffet and continental breakfasts offer a delightful start to your day.

## Guest Reviews

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fbe5c8d9366184e5c302970ce5259a43.jpg)

This was my first stay at this hotel, and I joined their morning yoga class with friends. We took a shuttle to an outdoor yoga venue facing a vast grassland. The morning breeze and birdsong made the entire experience relaxing. The class wasn't difficult, focusing on basic poses, perfect for starting the day.

The instructor's English was easy to understand, making it accessible for participants from different countries. Since seating is limited, it's advisable to arrive on time. The session was deeply connected with nature, offering a therapeutic experience. The instructor was very friendly and even helped take photos afterward. Luckily, the weather was perfect with clear skies! If you want to start your day relaxed and energized, I highly recommend this morning yoga class.

***

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a453cc99c786072992ba3e62998ab4d1.jpg)

From the moment you step into the hotel, you'll feel like royalty! The staff helped with luggage, and check-in was quick and easy. The room's cleanliness was impressive—spotless surfaces, clean glass, a spacious and bright bathroom, and immaculate bedding.

The breakfast buffet was rich and delicious, with every dish leaving a lasting impression. Checkout was equally efficient without any delays. The pool was spacious, and even during the Christmas season, I could swim freely like a professional without bumping into other guests. There were no complaints about this stay—we enjoyed every moment and can't wait to return!

***

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b657ddd6500f3db4c5b5943ddc057cb6.jpg)

Our seven-day stay in early November, during the off-season, allowed the resort to maintain a serene and peaceful atmosphere. Even as more guests arrived later in our stay, it still felt incredibly comfortable, with ample personal space whether during breakfast or around the pool.

Breakfast was the highlight of the day! The restaurant offered a wide variety of international cuisines, including Balinese, Indian, Chinese, Japanese, and Western dishes. The dessert section was a must-visit, featuring an array of pastries and freshly made ice cream. Our kids even happily had ice cream for breakfast every day!

Dining experiences at other restaurants like Table 8 (Chinese) and Edogin (Japanese) were also highly satisfying, offering reasonable prices and excellent quality. However, our favorite was Soleil, where the wood-fired pizzas and Southeast Asian dishes delighted us, rivaling the best meals we’ve had in Thailand and Vietnam.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4bcfdf917a8ce2ececcbf56925a317e4.jpg)

In terms of facilities, the resort’s gym was equipped with state-of-the-art equipment but remained largely unused, making it perfect for travelers seeking a quiet workout. The multiple pools throughout the resort offered different styles, with the Ocean Pool being the most popular.

Lastly, the cost was very reasonable. Dining for three cost around USD$70 per day, which is excellent value for a high-end resort. Compared to other luxury hotels, Mulia Resort’s service, quality, and value were truly impressive.

This was our first time in Bali, and Mulia Resort provided the perfect starting point. From the food to the facilities, the service to the atmosphere, every day was incredibly relaxing and enjoyable. We will definitely return and highly recommend it to anyone seeking luxury and tranquility!
