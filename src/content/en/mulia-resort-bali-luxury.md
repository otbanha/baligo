This five-star resort opened in 2012 and features 424 rooms, just a 25-minute drive from Bali's international airport for easy access. Check-in begins at 3:00 PM, and check-out is at noon. ### **Art Collection and Spatial Design at the Resort**

Mulia Resort is not only renowned for its luxurious facilities but also uniquely integrates contemporary artworks from around the world into every corner, transforming the entire resort into an open-air art space. These pieces, created by artists from China, India, and beyond, are scattered throughout guest rooms, restaurants, lobbies, walkways, and pool areas, crafting a vacation experience rich in cultural taste and visual delight.

### **Serene Art in the Guest Rooms**

Stepping into a guest room, your eyes are greeted by authorized paintings from Nanjing artist Yu Qiping. Yu specializes in traditional Chinese literati painting, interpreting classical aesthetics with a restrained contemporary flair. The figures in his works often immerse themselves in quiet contemplation, surrounded by lotus flowers, still waters, or birds in flight—these paintings don't vie for attention but quietly accompany you as you rest, infusing the private room with tranquility and depth. Amid the vibrant energy of a Bali vacation, this serenity perfectly captures the essence of true luxury: a calm that comes from within.

### **Contemporary Creative Dialogue in the Lobby**

The Mulia Villa lobby showcases a massive work by Shanghai artist Pianzi. Born in Yunnan and based in Shanghai, Pianzi creates with a humorous and symbolic perspective, using her signature cat imagery and layered symbols to build scenes that balance imagination and order. Another piece transforms a traditional abacus into a structure composed of human faces, turning 'calculation' into a metaphor for the relationship between the individual and the collective—a restrained discussion on commerce, order, and social rhythms presented in the space, inviting guests to reflect on the systems behind everyday life.

### The Power Narrative of Table8 Restaurant

At the Table 8 Chinese restaurant in the resort, works by Guangzhou artist Huang Zhongyang reinterpret historical themes through a cross-cultural lens. His piece centered on Empress Dowager Cixi uses the imagery of mirrors to explore the literal and metaphorical meanings of 'reflection,' presenting power with subtle, delicate brushstrokes—not declaring, but questioning. The subtle interplay between Cixi and the monkey in the painting silently probes the structures of power. In this space where tradition meets precision cooking, art leaves ample room for thought beyond taste.

### A Tribute to Labor and Service on the Walkway

Along the resort's walkway, Indian contemporary artist Valay Shende constructs human forms from metal discs and clock parts, exploring labor, collaboration, and collective identity. His inspiration comes from Mumbai's famous dabbawala system—bodies made of watch faces symbolize time and reliability, while stomachs shaped like lunch boxes point to nourishment and need. This sculpture subtly captures the essence of hotel service: not a performance, but a steady, consistent fulfillment.

### Water Goddess Blessings at the Pool Area

In the courtyard pool, ocean pool, and oasis pool areas, three female statues inspired by Bali's water goddess Dewi Danu symbolize water, fertility, and the cycle of life. The lotus in the courtyard pool represents devotion and purity; the water bowl in the ocean pool signifies purification; and the rice and water in the oasis pool embody nourishment and renewal. These custom pieces become key markers of the space, organically connecting architecture, landscape, and local beliefs, allowing every moment by the pool to carry cultural and spiritual depth.

The curatorial philosophy of Mulia Resort emphasizes experience and meaning over mere display. Art doesn't need to be understood all at once but unfolds gradually during your stay. In an era where contemporary luxury often chases expression and scale, Mulia chooses a more restrained path—art doesn't assert its presence but naturally blends into the space. This is true luxury: born from clear choices and values that stand the test of time.
