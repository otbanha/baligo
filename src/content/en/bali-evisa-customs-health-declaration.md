### New Indonesia Entry Measures🇮🇩 Important Notice! Starting August 18, 2025, Full Transition to 'All Indonesia' Unified Declaration System

Attention dear travelers! Latest announcement from the Indonesian government✨ Starting **Monday, August 18, 2025**, a new integrated entry declaration platform—[All Indonesia](https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/)—will be launched, unifying customs declarations, health statements, and other declaration procedures. No more separate submissions through the old ECD (Customs) and SSHP (Health Statement) systems!

## **Common Questions About VISA:**

### **❓ Do I need to apply for a visa (VoA, Visa on Arrival)?**

Currently, citizens from 13 countries enjoy visa-free entry ([see the list here](/blog/indonesia-visa-free-countries-list/)). Citizens from over 80 other countries, including Taiwan and China, need to apply for a visa.

### **❓ Do children need to apply for a visa?**

Visa requirements apply regardless of age - even infants need to apply.

### **❓ Where can I apply for the eVisa?**

Please refer to [this guide](/blog/bali-evisa-application-guide/) for further instructions.

### **❓ Don't want to apply for an e-Visa? Where to get Visa on Arrival and what documents to prepare?**

If you haven't filled out the e-Visa application in advance, head straight to the Visa on Arrival counter after disembarking. Make sure to have:

1. A passport valid for at least six months
2. At least one blank page in your passport for the stamp
3. Return/onward flight ticket
4. Cash or credit card for payment (IDR 500,000 or equivalent in USD. Cash equivalents in EUR, GBP, AUD, SGD are also accepted)

### **❓ How long does it take for an e-Visa to be issued?**

After completing the application form with your photo and receiving a successful notification, you'll immediately receive a QR Code in your registered email.

### **❓ How early can I apply for an e-Visa?**

The standard tourist e-Visa is valid for 90 days from the date of issue; you must depart within 30 days from the date of entry.

### **❓ What's the secret to a successful online application?**

Based on community experiences, using a Gmail account and applying during business hours increases your chances of success.

### **❓ What if the system auto-fills my name+surname differently from my passport?**

According to fellow travelers, passing through customs should go smoothly without any issues.

If the system doesn't automatically fill in the information, please manually input it yourself.

### **❓Name Format Question: Should I enter my name as Given Name + Surname? Do I need to include a space?**

Since the system doesn't accept hyphens or other symbols in names, if your passport name contains a '-', you can replace it with a space or simply remove the hyphen. For example, if your passport name is 'Wang, Fa-Fa', you can manually enter it as 'Wang, Fa Fa' or 'Wang, Fafa'.

### **❓Name Order Question: Should the given name come first or the surname?**

Simply follow the format shown on your passport—this is foolproof, and no one can say you're wrong!

### **❓Place of Birth and Date of Birth**

Place of Birth and Date of Birth must match exactly what is on your passport.

### **❓What should I fill in for Issuing Country?**

If the system doesn't automatically fill it in, travelers from Taiwan can enter ROC, Taiwan, or China—all are acceptable.

### **❓**How to Convert Image Files to PDF on Your Phone?

📱【How to Convert Image Files to PDF on Your Phone】

✅ **For iPhone (iOS):**

1. Open your screenshot or photo in the **Photos** app.
2. Tap the share icon at the bottom left (a square with an arrow).
3. Swipe down → Tap **Print**.
4. Use two fingers to zoom in on the photo (enlarge the photo with your fingers).
5. A PDF preview will appear. Tap the share icon in the top right → Select **Save to Files** or **Save to iCloud Drive**.

✅ **For Android Phones:**

1. Open the image file you want to convert in the **Photos** or **Gallery** app.
2. Tap the share button in the top right → Select **Print**.
3. In the printer options, choose **Save as PDF**.
4. Tap the download icon to save the file.

### **❓ What to Do If the Final Step of Credit Card Payment Fails?**

If the payment fails, you’ll need to reapply. Sometimes switching to a different card will work (AMEX cards are not accepted; please use JCB, VISA, or Mastercard).

### **❓ Pay Attention to the Date!**

Once your eVOA visa is issued, you must use it within **90 days**, meaning you must enter Indonesia within 90 days of the visa issue date. If you do not enter within this period, the visa will automatically expire and become invalid.

When filling out the arrival date, make sure not to mistakenly enter the departure date.

### **❓ Why does Date of Issue automatically change to the application date?**

The **Date of Issue** that appears on your e-Visa is the date you applied for the eVoA, and it has no relation to the Date of Issue on your passport. Therefore, it correctly shows the date you applied for the eVoA.

What if this field is filled out incorrectly? The Date of Issue here is only for your reference regarding the application/issuance date of the e-Visa. If you have successfully applied and paid, you will pass through smoothly!

### **❓** What if Date of Issue and Place of Birth are filled out incorrectly?

If you notice any mistakes:

- Date of Issue is just for your reference regarding the entry date, so it doesn't matter if it's incorrect.
- Place of Birth, according to user reports, can be filled out as Taiwan or according to the birthplace on your passport, and you can still pass through automated immigration.

### **❓** Can children also use automated immigration?

Children aged 6 and above can also use the airport's automated immigration. Please refer to the [Guide to Indonesian/Bali Visas for Children](/blog/bali-child-visa-guide/)

### **❓ How long is the tourist visa valid? What happens if I overstay?**

The validity period of a tourist visa upon entry is 30 days, which can be extended once either at the local immigration office or [online](/blog/2024-bali-visa-extension/), allowing a maximum stay of 60 days.

Overstaying incurs a daily fine of 1,000,000 Indonesian Rupiah and is considered illegal residence, potentially leading to detention, deportation, or a ban from entering Indonesia for a specified period.

## All **Indonesia Frequently Asked Questions:**

### **❓ How early can I apply for** [All Indonesia](https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/) **?**

Since it now integrates a health declaration requiring the latest status updates, you can only fill it out three days before your flight.

### **❓ Which airports can I use** [All Indonesia](https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/) **for entry?**

Three international airports—Jakarta Soekarno-Hatta, Surabaya Juanda, and Bali Ngurah Rai—allow the use of 'All Indonesia' for a one-stop application. For other airports, please separately fill out the **eVisa**, **eCD Customs Declaration**, and **SSHP Health Declaration** (see [link](/blog/bali-entry-requirements/) for instructions).

### **❓ How can I check my application status?**

You can view the latest progress through the 'Application Status' option within [All Indonesia](https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/).

### **❓ Can I apply on behalf of family members/travel groups?**

✅ Yes! Simply fill out one application form for each family/group member. Each group is limited to a maximum of **10 people**. For groups exceeding 10, please submit an additional form.

1. **Indonesian citizens** (entering with an Indonesian passport) and **foreign travelers** (entering with a foreign passport) must submit separate declarations.

### **❓ What file formats are supported for upload?**

Only **JPEG and PNG** formats are accepted. For more details, refer to the instructions within the application.

### **❓ Which devices are supported?**

Both **PC/Mac** and **Android/iOS** devices are compatible.

### **❓ Is my personal information secure?**

The platform strictly adheres to information security standards and is used solely for immigration-related purposes.

### **❓ Is multilingual support available?**

Currently, **Indonesian, English, and Chinese** are supported. You can switch between languages in the settings.

### **❓ What to Do After Applying for eVOA/eVisa?**

Next, proceed to fill out the [All Indonesia](https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/) form.

### **❓** Is the Tourism Tax Not Integrated?

No. Since the tourism tax is only applicable in Bali and not nationwide, it must still be paid through a separate system. Refer to: [Bali Tourism Tax Guide: New 2024 Regulations, Payment Methods, and Island-Hopping Tips](/blog/2026-bali-tourism-tax-guide/)
