Have you ever wondered if you can leave Indonesia on the 30th day of your visa on arrival? This is a common question for many planning to visit Indonesia/Bali. In this article, we'll delve into the details of Indonesia's 30-day visa on arrival, answer your questions, and provide practical travel tips.

## What is Indonesia's 30-Day Visa on Arrival?

Indonesia's 30-day visa on arrival is a short-term visa available to citizens of many countries, allowing them to stay for up to 30 days after entry. This visa is typically processed upon arrival at Indonesian airports or ports and is suitable for tourism, family visits, or other short-term stays.

## The Application Process for Visa on Arrival

Applying for Indonesia's 30-day visa on arrival is relatively straightforward. Upon arrival at an Indonesian airport, proceed to the visa application counter. You'll need to prepare the following documents and fees:

- A valid passport (with at least 6 months validity)
- Return ticket or onward journey ticket
- Visa application fee (usually paid in cash or by credit card, approximately USD$35)

## Speed Up Immigration with the Electronic Visa on Arrival

The Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VoA) is a visa that can be applied for and obtained online, eliminating the need to queue for a visa on arrival at the airport. Applicants simply complete the online application before departure, providing necessary personal details and documents, and paying the visa fee. Once approved, applicants receive an email notification and can present the electronic visa directly at immigration. This method not only saves time but also significantly enhances the convenience of entry, making the journey smoother.

## Validity of Visa on Arrival

The 30-day visa on arrival for Indonesia is valid for 30 days from the date of entry. It’s important to note that these 30 days **include both the arrival and departure days**, so the actual stay duration is a full 30 days.

## Can You Leave on the 30th Day?

According to Indonesian visa regulations, you must leave before the visa expires. Therefore, the 30th day is the last day you can depart. If you plan to leave on the 30th day, make sure your flight is scheduled before the visa expiration to avoid any unnecessary issues.

## Consequences of Overstaying

If you overstay, you will face a fine of **1 million Indonesian rupiah per day** (approximately USD$70). Additionally, you may face more severe legal consequences, such as being banned from re-entering the country. Therefore, it’s recommended to depart before your visa expires to avoid any legal problems.

## Options for Extending Your Stay

If you wish to extend your stay in Indonesia, you can apply for a visa extension. Typically, you can extend for an additional 30 days, but you must visit the local immigration office or apply online before your visa expires and submit the required application forms and fees.
