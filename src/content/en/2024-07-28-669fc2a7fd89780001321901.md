## The Charm of Ubud

### Natural Beauty

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_196e1575dee8964008cb839fae2973d5.png)

Ubud is renowned for its natural beauty, nestled between rice fields and forested areas, surrounded by river gorges. In Ubud, you can enjoy lush hillsides, terraced rice fields, waterfalls, and pristine river landscapes.

### Art and Culture

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1c5aa41f7f1a617f73a2295d168fb493.jpg)

Beyond its natural beauty, Ubud is also famous for its Balinese art and culture. Most local residents in Ubud are artists, including painters, craftsmen, and dance artists. Along the streets of Ubud, you can easily find galleries, wood carving, and stone carving shops. Additionally, Ubud hosts many museums, art galleries, and souvenir shops selling local handicrafts.

Daily Balinese dance performances can be found in various parts of Ubud. Moreover, Ubud is home to numerous Balinese Hindu temples and beautiful palaces.

## Distances and Travel Times

### Distance from Bali Airport to Ubud

Bali has only one international airport, Ngurah Rai International Airport. If you're traveling from Ngurah Rai Airport to central Ubud, the distance is approximately 40 kilometers, with a driving time of about one and a half to two and a half hours.

If you're staying in the Seminyak area, traveling to central Ubud is about 32 kilometers, with an average driving time of 1 hour 30 minutes to 2 hours.

However, given the increasingly severe traffic congestion, it's advisable to allow extra buffer time for your journey!

## Best Ways to Get Around Ubud

Understanding the best transportation options is an essential part of vacation planning, so what are the best ways to get around Ubud?

Transportation in Ubud is similar to other areas in Bali, with no proper public transit system. The only options are private vehicles. Therefore, the best ways to get around Ubud are by hiring a private car or renting a scooter.

### Scooter – Ubud Transportation Guide

Scooters are the most widely used mode of transportation in Bali, including the Ubud area. Most Balinese people choose to ride scooters for their daily commutes. If you're interested in riding a scooter during your Ubud vacation, you can easily find scooter rental services.

Simply search 'Bali scooter rental' on Google, and you'll find rental services near your accommodation. You can also request the rental service to deliver the scooter to your hotel. Rental prices depend on the scooter model and rental duration, with longer rentals offering cheaper rates. Fuel is not included! So remember to fill up before long trips.

Additionally, an international driver's license is required to ride a scooter. You can obtain one in your home country or in Bali. Note that helmets must be worn at all times while riding in Bali, and be aware that traffic drives on the left, opposite to Taiwan. If you're unsure about the traffic conditions, consider alternative transportation options.

### 1. Ubud Monkey Forest

![烏布猴子森林](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9802a9d8d64470391fc74b9eaa207a8f.jpg)

Ubud Monkey Forest

The Monkey Forest is one of Ubud's must-visit attractions, located within a protected forest area filled with lush trees and long-tailed macaques. Inside the forest, you'll find the Pura Dalem Agung Padangtegal, a Balinese Hindu temple. While the wild monkeys can be dangerous, following the park's rules—such as staying calm, avoiding running, and not making direct eye contact with male monkeys—will ensure your safety. The recommended visiting time is about 1.5 hours.
