## The Charm of Ubud

### Natural Beauty

![自然美景](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_196e1575dee8964008cb839fae2973d5.png)

Ubud is renowned for its natural beauty, nestled among rice paddies and forested areas, surrounded by river valleys. In Ubud, you can enjoy lush green hillsides, terraced rice fields, waterfalls, and pristine river scenery.

### Art and Culture

![藝術與文化](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1c5aa41f7f1a617f73a2295d168fb493.jpg)

Beyond its natural beauty, Ubud is also famous for Balinese art and culture. Most local residents in Ubud are artists, including painters, craftsmen, and dancers. Along Ubud's streets, you can easily find galleries, wood carving, and stone carving shops. Additionally, Ubud boasts many museums, art galleries, and souvenir shops selling local handicrafts.

Every day, Balinese dance performances take place in various parts of Ubud. Moreover, Ubud is home to numerous Balinese Hindu temples and a beautiful royal palace.

## Distance and Travel Time

### Distance from Bali Airport to Ubud

Bali has only one international airport, Ngurah Rai International Airport. If you're traveling from Ngurah Rai Airport to central Ubud, the distance is about 40 kilometers, and the drive takes approximately 1.5 to 2.5 hours.

If you're staying in Seminyak, the distance to central Ubud is about 32 kilometers, with an average drive time of 1 hour 30 minutes to 2 hours.

However, given the worsening traffic congestion, be sure to add extra buffer time to your estimated travel duration!

## Best Ways to Get Around Ubud

Understanding the best transportation options in a tourist area is a key part of any vacation. So, what's the best way to get around Ubud?

Transportation in Ubud is similar to other tourist areas in Bali—there's no proper public transit, so your only option is private vehicles. Therefore, the best ways to get around Ubud are by hiring a private driver or renting a scooter.

### Scooter – Ubud Transportation Guide

Scooters are the most widely used mode of transport in Bali, including the Ubud area. Most locals rely on scooters for getting around. If you're interested in riding a scooter during your Ubud vacation, you can easily find scooter rental services.

Just search Google for 'Bali scooter rental,' and you'll find rental services near your accommodation. You can also ask the rental service to deliver the scooter to your hotel. Rental rates depend on the scooter model and rental duration—the longer you rent, the cheaper the daily rate. Note that the rental fee does not include fuel! So be sure to fill up the tank before any long trips.

Additionally, riding a scooter requires an International Driving Permit. You can obtain one in your home country or in Bali. Please note that you must wear a helmet at all times while riding a scooter in Bali, and get used to driving on the right side of the road, which is opposite to Taiwan. If you're unsure about the traffic conditions, consider choosing another mode of transport.

### 1. Ubud Monkey Forest

### 1. 烏布猴子森林 (Monkey Forest Ubud)

![烏布猴子森林](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9802a9d8d64470391fc74b9eaa207a8f.jpg)

Ubud Monkey Forest

The Monkey Forest is one of the must-visit attractions in Ubud, located within a protected forest filled with towering trees and long-tailed gray macaques. Inside the forest, there is also a Balinese Hindu temple called Pura Dalem Agung Padangtegal. However, the wild monkeys can be dangerous; as long as you follow the park's rules—such as not panicking, avoiding running, and not making eye contact with male monkeys—you can stay safe. A recommended visit time is about 1.5 hours.
