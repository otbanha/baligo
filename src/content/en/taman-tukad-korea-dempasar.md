Exploring Bali's beauty—how did 'Korea's Cheonggyecheon Stream Park' end up in Denpasar, Bali?!

**Bottom line**: Taman Tukad Korea (officially Taman Kumbasari) is located between Badung Traditional Market and Kumbasari Art Market in Denpasar, **just a 30-minute drive from the airport**. It earned its nickname for its striking resemblance to Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream. Once a polluted drainage ditch, it has been transformed into a riverside park with walking paths and pavilions. Perfect for a quick stop after shopping at the art market—free to visit, great for photos and relaxation.

Just a 30-minute drive from the airport, this riverside park sits between Badung Traditional Market and Kumbasari Art Market. Its official name is 'Taman Kumbasari,' but locals call it 'Taman Tukad Korea (Korean River Park)' because it closely resembles Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream (청계천).

Once a smelly drainage ditch, it has now transformed into a captivating park—Bali's river revitalization is truly impressive. Here, you can experience a blend of nature and modernity, with winding walkways, pavilions, and a relaxed atmosphere that makes you feel like you're in Korea's Cheonggyecheon.
