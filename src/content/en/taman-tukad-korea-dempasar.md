Exploring Bali's Beauty: How Did Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream End Up in Denpasar, Bali?!

Just a 30-minute drive from the airport, this riverside park located between Badung Traditional Market and Kumbasri Art Market is officially named "Taman Kumbasari (Kumbasari Park)." However, locals call it "Taman Tukad Korea (Korean River Park)" due to its striking resemblance to Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream (청계천).

Once a polluted waterway, it has now transformed into a captivating park, showcasing Bali's impressive river revitalization efforts. Here, you can experience the perfect blend of nature and modernity, with its winding pathways, pavilions, and serene atmosphere, making you feel as though you've been transported to Seoul's Cheonggyecheon.

In this hidden corner of Bali, you can stroll along the riverside, admire the refreshing scenery, and soak in the harmonious ambiance. This picturesque spot has become a favorite relaxation destination for both locals and tourists.
