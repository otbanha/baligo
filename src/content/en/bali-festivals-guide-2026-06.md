A curated guide to Bali's most anticipated festivals and events over the next month (June to early July):

## Modern Arts, Culture & Food Events ([Official Website](https://www.ubudfoodfestival.com/))

### Ubud Food Festival

- **Dates:** May 28 – May 31, 2026 (last day)
- **Location:** Taman Kuliner, Ubud
- **Highlights:** If you're in Ubud during these days, don't miss this top-tier Indonesian food and culture event! Expect bustling markets, Indonesian cooking demos, chef showcases, and engaging discussions on culinary traditions and sustainability.

### Balinale (Bali International Film Festival)

- **Dates:** June 1 – June 7, 2026
- **Location:** Denpasar
- **Highlights:** Bringing films from around the world to Bali, featuring premieres, screenings, filmmaker interviews, and panel discussions—a must for cinema and storytelling enthusiasts.

### Ubud Open Studio

- **Dates:** June 5 – June 7, 2026
- **Location:** Various art studios across Ubud
- **Highlights:** Now in its fifth year, over 60 local and international artists and designers open their private studios to the public. Ticket holders receive an exclusive Google Map to explore these creative hubs firsthand.

### Bali Arts Festival

![](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_15ea6a6aa9cd30d097d8adf78a25f3cb.png)

- **Dates:** June 13 – July 11, 2026
- **Location:** Denpasar Art Center (Taman Budaya)
- **Highlights:** This is **Bali's largest official cultural showcase, spanning an entire month**. Teams, performers, and artists from regencies across Bali take turns presenting the most authentic traditional dances, Gamelan music, theater, and visual arts—making it the perfect opportunity to deeply engage with Bali's traditional culture and arts.

## Traditional Religious Festivals

### Galungan

![](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c8fb868428b648667e4ff2774ba1d282.jpg)

- **Date:** June 17, 2026 (Wednesday)
- **Highlights:** This is one of the most important holidays in Balinese Hinduism, symbolizing the victory of good (Dharma) over evil (Adharma). During the festival, every household and street across the island is adorned with towering bamboo poles called **Penjor**, decorated with coconut leaves, rice stalks, and fabric, transforming the entire island into a stunning spectacle. Locals dress in exquisite traditional attire to pray and parade at temples, creating a deeply religious and cultural atmosphere.

### Kuningan

- **Date:** June 27, 2026 (Saturday)
- **Highlights:** This falls 10 days after Galungan, marking the day when ancestral spirits return to the heavens after their time on earth. Traditionally, locals prepare yellow rice cakes for final temple prayers in the early morning. After this day, the festivities officially conclude, and the Penjor along the streets are gradually taken down.

### Semarak Pandawa Festival

- **Dates:** June 18–20, 2026
- **Description:** A beach festival held at Pandawa Beach, featuring stage performances, guest activities, and local participation.

### Karangasem Festival

- **Date** : June 22, 2026
- **Description** : A festival showcasing the culture and tourism projects of the Karangasem region.

### **Mekotek, Munggu Village (Bamboo Pole Ritual)**

- **Date** : June 27, 2026
- **Location** : Munggu Village
- **Description** : A traditional ceremony where male villagers line up with bamboo poles and clash, aiming to ward off evil spirits and pray for health and a bountiful harvest.
