[Exchange Rate This Morning: 1 USD = 16,700 IDR] Today's (4/15) **Bali** news and practical information for tourists are summarized below:

### 🗞️ Top News: Law Enforcement and Diplomatic Updates

- **Expanded Crackdown Operation**: The Ngurah Rai Immigration Office today arrested 10 foreigners suspected of violations during the "Wira Waspada 2026" operation in Kuta, Kerobokan, and other areas. Among those arrested were Russians involved in online prostitution, Nigerians and Ugandans with forged documents, and several visa overstayers. Authorities emphasized zero tolerance for illegal activities and encouraged the public to report via hotlines.
- **Resolution of South Korea Travel Advisory Controversy**: Following the controversy over South Korea's inclusion of Bali in its travel advisory due to security concerns, the South Korean embassy today formally apologized and clarified it as a communication error. The advisory has been revised, reaffirming Bali's safe travel environment.
- **Cross-Border Crime Bust**: Police held a press conference in Denpasar today, announcing the bust of a cocaine smuggling ring linked to an international network, demonstrating their commitment to combating transnational crime.

### 🌤️ Weather Forecast 

- **Today's Overview**: Partly cloudy, with temperatures ranging between **26°C - 32°C** in lowland areas.

### 📄 Topics of Concern for Tourists: Flights and Safety

- **Volcanic Ash Cloud Relief**: Flight delays caused by the eruption of Mount Lewotobi have significantly improved. International flights, including Virgin Australia, Jetstar, Singapore Airlines, and Malaysia Airlines, have **resumed normal operations** today. Two affected island airports have also reopened.
- **Entry Reminder**: Airport customs inspections are currently strict, particularly regarding the **150,000 IDR tourism tax** and the QR Code for **e-CD electronic customs declaration**.
- **Crowd Warning**: The number of incoming tourists has surged recently, with nearly 1 million arrivals recorded on 4/10. Airport transfers and traffic congestion on popular routes are severe, so it is advised to allow ample time when traveling from the airport to southern accommodation areas.
