Here is your curated news for **Tuesday, April 21st, in Bali**:

## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Environmental Governance and Policy Shifts

- **'Open Dumping' Ban Countdown**: As major landfills like Suwung undergo transformation, the central government reiterated today that the 'ban on open waste dumping' will be strictly enforced. The provincial government is accelerating the construction of regional waste treatment centers (TPST) and urging the tourism industry and residents to sort waste. This means that in the coming months, waste disposal costs in some areas may be passed on to end consumers, or temporary waste accumulation may occur.
- **Regional Tourism Market Strategy**: In response to uncertainties in global long-haul routes, the Bali Tourism Office (Dinpar) stated today that the 2026 target is to attract 6.62 million international tourists, with a full strategic shift in focus to the **Asia-Pacific region** (Australia, India, China, Japan, South Korea, and Southeast Asia). Expect more direct flights and promotional campaigns targeting Asia-Pacific travelers in the future.

## 🌤️ Weather Forecast

- **Today's Overview**: Weather on April 21st is expected to be **cloudy with occasional showers or heavy rain**, turning **cloudy** in the evening.
- **Temperature and Humidity**: Temperatures will range between **25°C - 31°C**, with a feels-like temperature of around 25°C.

## 📄 Tourist Concerns: Practical Checks and Traffic Alerts

- **Tourist Tax Checks Intensified**: Officials reminded today that starting in April, random checks for proof of payment of the **150,000 IDR tourist tax** will be increased at popular attractions (such as Uluwatu and Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park) and major transport hubs. Tourists are advised to save the QR code generated by 'Love Bali' as a screenshot on their phones to avoid delays due to poor signal in mountainous areas during checks.
- **'Swarm' Spectacle on the Toll Road**: Yesterday, a massive bee migration briefly disrupted traffic on the Bali Mandara Toll Road. Experts attribute this to the intense heat and wind patterns during the current seasonal transition. Motorcyclists traveling on this road today should stay alert, remain calm if encountering a swarm, and reduce speed—do not panic or swat at them on the bridge.
- **Traffic Advisory**: Due to subway construction barriers, travel time between the airport and Canggu remains unpredictable. Combined with expected afternoon rain and slippery roads, travelers are advised to allow at least **2.5 hours** to reach the airport.
