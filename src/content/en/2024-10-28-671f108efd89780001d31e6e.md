Bali's traffic can be unpredictable, especially on the route from Ubud to the airport, where you might encounter religious ceremonies, sudden traffic jams, or road repairs, making this seemingly short journey hard to estimate. To ensure you don't miss your flight, this article will provide you with a complete itinerary and time management tips for traveling from Ubud to Bali International Airport, allowing your last day to be both flexible and stress-free.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_db0768c5c03e273249cef15e302ea65c.jpg)

This article will guide you through every detail, from arranging transportation to managing your time, especially focusing on the route and flexible planning from Ubud to Bali International Airport, ensuring a smooth and worry-free end to your trip.

***

## Why is the journey from Ubud to the airport hard to estimate?

The distance from Ubud to the airport is approximately 30 kilometers, and while the drive seems like it should take about an hour, the actual time can vary due to traffic conditions, road situations, or even religious ceremonies. Bali's roads are often affected by local religious activities, causing delays or detours. Choosing the right departure time and planning your route becomes crucial for a smooth trip.

***

## Pre-Departure Preparations

**Confirm your flight in advance**: Double-check your flight time the night before departure. It’s generally recommended to arrive at the airport at least 3 hours before your flight to account for potential queues or unexpected delays.

**Pack your luggage**: Try to organize your luggage the day before to ensure you don’t forget any important items in a rush. This also adds flexibility to your schedule.

**Arrange transportation**: To avoid delays caused by finding a ride, it’s best to arrange transportation in advance and confirm the pickup time. You can either arrange it through your hotel or use a reputable private car service to ensure a smooth journey.

***

## Route planning: Ubud → Kuta Area → Airport

Start your journey from Ubud to the Kuta area. Upon arrival, assess your remaining time. If you have ample time, you can engage in a brief sightseeing tour of Kuta.

***

## Avoiding Specific Areas in Kuta

While Kuta is one of the most popular bustling areas for tourists, its main streets are mostly **one-way**, narrow, and heavily trafficked, making access quite inconvenient. The most common scenario is 'easy to get in, hard to get out.' Once you're caught in traffic, it could significantly impact your schedule. It's advisable to avoid entering the area marked in red on the map during your Kuta tour to prevent delays in reaching the airport.

## Flexible Sightseeing Arrangements in Kuta

Near the Kuta area, you can selectively visit some attractions, shop for souvenirs, or dine at a restaurant. The area south of the red box and north of the airport is the most ideal location.

## The Importance of Allocating Buffer Time

It's crucial to allocate at least 3 hours of buffer time for your journey. Regardless of the situation, these 3 hours provide flexibility to your schedule and ensure you're not caught off guard by unexpected events.

***

## Using Real-Time Traffic Apps

Many real-time traffic apps can help you understand current traffic conditions. Using these apps to adjust departure times and choose the best routes can save you time more effectively. Even if you have a booked driver, it's recommended to keep the app open as a reference.

However, **never trust the estimated arrival times on traffic apps too much**! Because in Bali, there's a high chance they won't be accurate!

## Conclusion: Tips to Avoid Missing Your Flight

The journey from Ubud to the airport is full of variables, but with proper preparation, flexible scheduling, and constant use of real-time traffic apps, the trip can go smoothly. Pack your luggage early, leave enough time, and be flexible with sightseeing, allowing you to enjoy the last moments of your Bali trip.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **How long does it usually take from Ubud to the airport?**
   Normally about 1.5 to 2 hours, but it's recommended to allow 3 hours as a buffer for unexpected delays.
2. **Is it advisable to include sightseeing on the last day?**
   If time permits, consider brief sightseeing in the Kuta area, but always keep an eye on traffic conditions.
3. **What real-time traffic apps are recommended?**
   Apps like Google Maps or Waze provide real-time traffic updates and help adjust your itinerary.
4. **Why should I arrange transportation in advance?**
   Bali's traffic is unpredictable; arranging in advance avoids the risk of last-minute searches and ensures timely departure.
5. **How early should I arrive for an international flight?**
   Typically, it's recommended to arrive at the airport 3 hours before an international flight to allow time for check-in. Although Bali's international airport is relatively small, preparing for boarding more than two hours in advance is essential!
6. **If my flight is at 3:30 PM, what time should I leave Ubud?**
   2 hours at the airport + 3 hours on the road, so it's advisable to leave Ubud by 10:30 AM at the latest.
7. **Are there recommended areas to stay on the last day?**
   To avoid traffic variables on the last day, it's recommended to arrange your final Bali itinerary in Jimbaran, just south of the airport.
