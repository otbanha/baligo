## **💡 How to Avoid Common Mistakes in Indonesia Visa Applications?**

Indonesia's immigration authority recently released a series of visa application guidelines, reminding foreign travelers how to avoid common mistakes to increase approval rates. As the visa application process has fully moved online, while it's become more convenient, error rates have also risen.

The official statement says: 'Visa rejection can be frustrating, but many cases are avoidable! We'll show you how to boost your chances of success.'

Now, let's take a look at the most common mistakes in Indonesia visa applications and how to avoid them.

## **✅ Key Advice: Use the Official e-Visa Website**

Indonesia's immigration authority emphasizes that all applicants should **only use the official website** to apply for an e-Visa:

👉 [**www.evisa.imigrasi.go.id**](https://www.evisa.imigrasi.go.id/)

There are many fake websites online trying to trick applicants, so be sure to double-check that the URL is correct. If the site loads slowly, try turning off your **VPN or ad blocker**, and it's recommended to apply using a computer for the best experience.

## **📌 5 Main Reasons for Indonesia Visa Application Rejection**

### **1️⃣ Incomplete or Missing Documents**

This is one of the most common reasons for visa rejection. If the submitted materials are incomplete, immigration officers cannot verify the applicant's eligibility and must deny the application.

**Solution:**

- Carefully read the requirements for your specific visa type
- Ensure all documents are prepared, including passport, ID, financial proof, etc.
- Double-check that all required documents are complete before applying

---

### **2️⃣ Errors in the Application Form**

Indonesia's immigration authorities point out that incorrect or inconsistent information on the application form is also a common cause of rejection. For example:

❌ Wrong passport number

❌ Misspelled name

❌ Incorrect email entry

❌ Passport issue date and validity period filled incorrectly

**Solution:**

- Double-check every field before submitting
- Ensure all personal details match your passport exactly

---

### **3️⃣ Insufficient Financial Proof**

When applying for certain visa types (such as long-term visas), the immigration authorities require applicants to provide sufficient financial proof to ensure they can support themselves during their stay in Indonesia.

**Solution:**

- Prepare bank statements showing stable financial status
- Ensure your bank balance meets the visa's minimum requirements

📌 **Note:** This requirement does not apply to the eVisa on Arrival, but it does apply to other long-term visa categories.

---

### **4️⃣ Poor Travel Record**

If an applicant has a history of **overstaying, violating immigration laws, or being deported**, this may affect a new visa application.

**Solution:**

- Avoid any actions that could negatively impact your entry record.
- If you've had visa issues in the past, consult a professional visa advisor for solutions.

---

### **5️⃣ Unclear Travel Purpose**

Aside from the eVisa on Arrival, other types of visas may require applicants to provide a clear travel plan, such as work visas or student visas.

**Solution:**

- Prepare a detailed travel itinerary, including hotel bookings and flight tickets.
- For business visas, provide an invitation letter or relevant documentation.

✅ **Extension:** Can be extended for another **30 days** through the same online platform

## **🔍 6 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

### **1️⃣ How long does it take to process an Indonesian e-visa?**

Typically **3-7 business days**, but it may take longer during peak periods.

### **2️⃣ Can I apply for an e-visa using my phone?**

Yes, but it's recommended to use a **computer** to ensure the page displays properly and minimize technical issues.

### **3️⃣ If my visa is rejected, can I reapply?**

Yes, but make sure to correct all errors before resubmitting your application.

### **4️⃣ Can I use a VPN when applying for a visa?**

It's not recommended, as it may cause website loading issues and disrupt the application process.

### **5️⃣ Can I change my visa type while in Indonesia?**

Some visa types can be changed within the country, but specific conditions must be met. It's best to consult the local immigration office.
