Tirta Empul (meaning 'Holy Spring') is one of Bali's oldest and most spiritually significant water temples, located in the village of Tampaksiring, Gianyar. This temple is renowned for its Melukat purification ritual, where devotees believe the spring water has the power to cleanse negative energy.

**Highlights of the Visit:**

- Experience the traditional Melukat purification ritual
- Admire the ancient Balinese architecture and mysterious spring
- Observe local religious celebrations like Piodalan
- Reflect in the lush, serene surroundings

***

## What is the Melukat Purification Ritual? Can Non-Hindus Participate?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5ba2abc7963542e2253bb38ebb3360ef.jpg)

Melukat is a purification ritual in Balinese Hinduism, where devotees pass through 26 fountains in sequence, cleansing their spirits through splashing water and prayer.

Non-Hindus can also participate, as long as they respect the culture and etiquette.

**Participation Guidelines:**

- Wear a Sarong and sash (provided free by the temple)
- Prepare [Canang Sari](/blog/2024-07-16-66964aa7fd89780001c7051c/) (offerings), which can be purchased outside the temple
- Maintain quietness, humility, and respect
- Follow the sequence of the springs for purification (guides are available to assist)

### The Correct Process and Order of the Tirta Empul Purification Ritual

The Tirta Empul purification ritual may seem simple, but it follows a specific order and rules. Please adhere to the following steps:

1. **Dress and Preparation**: Change into the traditional green Sarong and waistband, store your belongings in the locker, then proceed to the holy spring pool area.
2. **Begin the Purification Process**: When facing the holy spring pools, **start from the left pool**, following the sequence of water spouts for prayers and purification.
3. **Skip Two Specific Water Spouts (Tirta Pengentas)**: **Skip the second-to-last and third-to-last water spouts on the left pool**. These are reserved for purification rituals for the deceased and should not be used by those not involved in funeral rites.
4. **After Completing the Left Pool, Move to the Right Pool**: Only the first water spout in the right pool needs to be used for purification; there’s no need to go through each one.
5. **Steps for Each Water Spout**:
6. - Place your hands together in prayer, silently express your wishes or prayers while facing the water spout.

- Splash the holy water on your hair, symbolizing the cleansing of distractions and fatigue.
- Sprinkle the holy water over your entire body for internal and external purification and spiritual renewal.

The cool, clear water flowing over your body feels like washing away the fatigue of travel and the worries of your heart. This ritual of body, mind, and spirit not only brings inner peace but also offers a profound and unforgettable cultural experience—definitely worth trying!

## Introduction to the Three Areas of Tirta Empul

**Outer Courtyard (Jaba Pura)**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3f2cc19c62eca50170a62be966baf1f4.jpg)

After entering the temple gate, you’ll arrive at the outer courtyard, which features a serene koi pond and lush greenery—perfect for meditation and quiet reflection.

**Central Courtyard (Jaba Tengah)**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4700e8862974289fdc9dfce583117aec.jpg)

The main area for purification rituals, featuring two large pools and 26 water springs, each representing a different spiritual aspiration.

**Significance of Key Springs:**

- Tirta Sudamala: Soul purification
- Tirta Penglukatan: Removal of negative energy
- Tirta Panegtegan: Enhancement of courage and confidence

Note: Some springs (e.g., Tirta Pengentas) are reserved for death rituals; visitors should avoid using them.

**Inner Sanctum (Jeroan)**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_623a206ab810a4a260d720844b62f5f3.jpg)

The most sacred area, housing the main temple and the source of the springs, is a place for meditation and prayer after completing the Melukat ritual. Non-Hindus should generally refrain from entering.

## Dress Code & Etiquette

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_de538df8b6d201321be97e49b49d502a.jpg)

**Required Attire:**

- Sarong and Sash are provided free of charge
- Shoulders must be covered; avoid tank tops and shorts
- Men may wear Udeng headgear (optional)

**Prohibited Actions:**

- Women on their menstrual cycle may not enter the main shrine
- Do not step on altars or offering tables
- Maintain silence and respect those performing rituals
- No littering or loud behavior

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Can non-Hindus participate in the Melukat ritual at Tirta Empul?

Yes. As long as you respect local culture and ritual etiquette, and maintain politeness, you can safely participate.

### 2. Are sarongs provided at the entrance?

Yes. Sarongs and waistbands are provided free of charge when entering the temple, so there's no need to bring your own.

### 3. Is the spring water safe to drink?

It is not recommended to drink directly. The spring water is primarily for ritual purposes and has not been purified.

### 4. Can I visit on my own without a guide?

Yes. However, if you wish to gain deeper insight into the meaning of the springs and the ritual process, it's recommended to hire a local guide.

### 5. Is photography allowed inside the temple?

Photography is permitted, but please avoid capturing people during rituals and refrain from disturbing worshippers in prayer.
