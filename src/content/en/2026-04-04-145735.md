Have you ever applied for Indonesia's e-visa online?

If so, be careful. Recently, travelers on Reddit have shared a new scam targeting those who just completed their visa applications.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775314454153-img_5278.jpeg)

## Received an "Official Email" the Day After Applying for Visa

Here's how it works: You apply for the Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA) on Indonesia's immigration website, thinking you're done, only to receive an email in your inbox a day or two later.

The email claims you need to apply for a "Mandatory Entry Declaration Card," stating you won't be allowed entry without it, urging you to click a link and make a payment.

Here's the kicker - the email contains your real name and application number. It's not some obvious spam email, but one that clearly knows what you just applied for.

Many people fall for it at this point, click through to make the payment, and are even asked to transfer money using cryptocurrency.

## Why Does It Feel So Real?

The "Arrival Declaration Card" is indeed a real thing.

Starting from September 2025, travelers entering Indonesia will need to fill out an electronic declaration called the All Indonesia Arrival Card (AIAC). Scammers are exploiting this—your uncertainty about whether it costs money or how to complete it—and posing as officials to charge fees.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775314476563-img_5279.jpeg)

But the truth is: **The AIAC declaration is completely free, with no additional fees!**

## Just remember these few things

**First, Indonesian government websites always end with `.go.id`.** When you receive an email, check the sender address and link first. If the URL doesn't match, close it immediately.

**Second, after completing your visa application, the authorities will not ask you for more money.** Any notification about "additional fees" is almost certainly fake.

**Third, the arrival declaration card is free to fill out—just go to the official All Indonesia system.** You don't need to use any links from emails.

## This isn't the first time this has happened

In 2022, fake e-VOA application websites appeared, looking almost identical to the real ones and even ranking first in Google search results, tricking many people at the time.

Scam tactics are constantly evolving. Now, they can even access your application details, making their emails look increasingly authentic. But just remember one principle: **Once the visa fee is paid, it's done. Ignore any additional charges afterward.**
