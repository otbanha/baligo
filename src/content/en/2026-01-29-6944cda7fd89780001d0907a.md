This summer, our family of six finally returned to Bali. It had been 23 years since our last family trip here. This island, known as the 'Island of the Gods,' remains as enchanting as ever, though it's noticeably more crowded now, with a richer array of tourist facilities. Our family was very satisfied with this 6-day, 5-night Bali trip. Below, we’ve organized our pre-trip preparations, itinerary overview, and insights on hiring a private driver for your reference.

## ※ Pre-Trip Preparations

### 1. Air Tickets

Eager to immerse ourselves in this paradise, we opted for a direct flight with EVA Air (BR255／BR256), which takes about 5.5 hours.

### 2. (Very Important!) Visa, Entry, and Related Applications

Includes visa (eVOA／VOA), ~~eCD electronic customs declaration, health information card (SSHP)~~ (now integrated into [AIAC](/blog/2025-08-14-689db40ffd89780001eb2f3e/)), and tourism tax.

**Heads Up (From Personal Experience):**

For all online applications, it's recommended to operate during "Indonesian government working hours" for smoother system performance and fewer technical glitches.

**(1) Visa: eVOA / VOA**

To save time upon arrival, I opted to apply for the eVOA (electronic Visa on Arrival) before departure, allowing me to use the automated immigration gates directly after landing.

- Required documents: Scanned passport page (JPG/PDF), passport photo (JPG), accommodation address, flight information, credit card
- Fee: IDR 500,000 per person (approx. USD$31)
- Official application website: <https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/> <--- Only official link
- Application guide: Many tutorial articles are available in forums, such as [【2026 Edition】Using "All Indonesia" Arrival Card for Bali Entry｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

**PS:**

The visa is valid for 30 days, and it's advisable to apply about 7 days before departure.

With eVOA, you can use the automated gates; I prefer to print a paper copy as backup.

**(2) eCD Electronic Customs Declaration, SSHP Health Declaration**

- Required documents: Passport details, eVOA number, flight and accommodation information
- Application website: "All Indonesia" integrated arrival system <https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/>
- Fee: Free
- Tutorial reference: [【2026 Edition】Using "All Indonesia" Arrival Card for Bali Entry｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

**PS:** Please save the QR code on your phone or print a copy for verification.

(3) Tourism Tax

- Required documents: Passport information, credit card
- Application method: Download the Love Bali App or visit <https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/home>
- Fee: IDR 150,000 per person (approximately USD$9)

**PS:** Similarly, please save the QR code for verification.

(4) Currency Exchange

It’s recommended to exchange USD$100 bills in Taiwan first, then convert to Indonesian Rupiah in Indonesia for better rates. Local exchange rates vary based on USD bill denominations—100 and 50 bills get the best rates, while 20, 10, 5, and 1 bills have lower rates.

## ※ Trip Overview and Travelogue

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1ddc462d2d0248effc0fb73f6691b047.png)

### **Day 1: Arrival in Bali → Jimbaran Beach Sunset and Seafood Dinner**

- **Itinerary**: Currency exchange, Jimbaran sunset
- **Dining**: Dinner at Malafi Cafe Jimbaran
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

Upon arriving in Bali, Betty was already waiting at the airport with a sign, her warm smile instantly easing the fatigue from the long flight. We first headed to the villa to drop off our luggage, then rushed straight to **Jimbaran Beach**. After 23 years, the sunset at Jimbaran remains as breathtaking as ever, with the aroma of seafood grilling from the neatly lined stalls on the beach. Dinner was enjoyed at **Malafi Cafe Jimbaran**, where we savored fresh seafood accompanied by live music. Sitting together on the beach, feeling the fine sand beneath our feet and gazing at the orange-hued sunset over the sea, we were filled with happiness.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3e474fcc7e6a5290032324cbaa76ba48.png)

For the first two nights, our villa offered options ranging from one to four bedrooms. For those interested, you can check out [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952).

### **Day 2: Blue Cliff Road → Uluwatu Temple → Relaxing Spa**

- **Itinerary**: Water activities, Blue Cliff Road, Uluwatu Temple, Sakar Jepun Spa
- **Dining**: Lunch at WARUNG LAOTA, dinner at a nearby market
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f24fff8b05526923028a41d85303f88f.jpg)

Betty took us along the **Blue Cliff Road**, where the azure sea contrasted sharply with the steep cliffs. Along the way, we saw several couples taking wedding photos, creating a romantic scene. Next, we visited **Uluwatu Temple**, marveling at the ancient Hindu architecture and intricate stone carvings. In the afternoon, we indulged in a traditional Balinese massage at **Sakar Jepun Spa**, rejuvenating ourselves for the upcoming adventures.

### **Day 3: Safari Park → Countryside Dinner**

- **Itinerary**: Taman Safari Bali, Ayung River Rafting, Sukawati Market
- **Dining**: Lunch at Joni's Garden Restaurant (Dirty Duck Rice), Dinner at Freddies Resort & Villas
- **Accommodation**: [Freddies Resort & Villas Ubud by Kanaya Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23125492)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4a04261efc67f7ef8c6413d589e1ae9c.jpg)

**Taman Safari Bali** is a favorite among children, offering close encounters with elephants, tigers, and other animals. You can also take a safari ride into the wild area, experiencing the vastness of an African savanna. Dinner is enjoyed at **Freddies Resort & Villas**, where the serene environment and exquisite cuisine make for a perfect evening.

### **Day 4: Ubud Rafting → Art Village → Rice Terrace Swing → BBQ Pork Ribs**

- **Itinerary**: Ubud Rafting, Penglipuran Village, Tegallalang Rice Terrace (Jungle Swing)
- **Dining**: Lunch included with rafting, Dinner at iiga warung (BBQ Pork Ribs)
- **Accommodation**: [Freddies Resort & Villas Ubud by Kanaya Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23125492)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9896437fb78834627a8728b3cc73f5b5.jpg)

Start the morning with **Ayung River Rafting**, where the lush tropical rainforest scenery and gentle currents make it perfect for family fun—you can even jump in for a swim! Next, visit **Penglipuran Village**, a traditional Balinese art village rich in cultural heritage. The **Tegallalang Rice Terrace** jungle swing is a must-visit spot for Instagram-worthy photos, and our family couldn't resist taking a turn. Soaring high above the emerald rice terraces was both thrilling and breathtaking. Dinner at **iiga warung** featured delicious BBQ pork ribs with a sweet and savory sauce that we still reminisce about.
