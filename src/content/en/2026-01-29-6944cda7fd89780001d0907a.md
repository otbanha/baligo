This summer, our family of six finally set foot on Bali again. It had been 23 years since our last family trip here. This island, known as the 'Island of the Gods,' remains as enchanting as ever, though the number of tourists has noticeably increased, and the tourism facilities have become more diverse and abundant. Our family was very satisfied with this 6-day, 5-night Bali free-and-easy trip. Below, we’ve compiled our pre-trip preparations, itinerary overview, and insights on hiring a private car and guide for your reference.

***

## ※ Pre-Trip Preparations

### 1. Air Tickets

Eager to dive into the embrace of this vacation paradise, we opted for EVA Air's direct flights (BR255／BR256), with a flight duration of approximately 5.5 hours.

### 2. (Very Important!) Visa, Entry, and Related Applications

Includes visa (eVOA/VOA), ~~eCD electronic customs declaration, health information card (SSHP)~~ (now integrated into [AIAC](/blog/2025-08-14-689db40ffd89780001eb2f3e/)), and tourism tax.

**Friendly reminder (from personal experience):**

For all online applications, it's recommended to operate during "Indonesian government working hours" for smoother system performance and fewer technical issues.

**(1) Visa: eVOA / VOA**

To save time upon arrival, I chose to apply for the eVOA electronic visa on arrival before departure, allowing me to use the automated immigration gates directly after landing.

- Required documents: Scanned passport page (JPG/PDF), passport photo (JPG), accommodation address, flight information, credit card
- Fee: IDR 500,000/person (approx. USD$31)
- Official application website: <https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/> <--- Only official link
- Application guide: Many tutorial articles are available in the community, such as [【2026 Edition】Using "All Indonesia" Arrival Card for Bali Entry｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

**PS:**

The visa is valid for 30 days; it's recommended to apply about 7 days before departure.

With eVOA, you can use the automated immigration gates; I prefer to print a paper copy as backup.

**(2) eCD Electronic Customs Declaration, SSHP Health Declaration**

- Required documents: Passport information, eVOA number, flight and accommodation details
- Application website: "All Indonesia" integrated entry system <https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/>
- Cost: Free
- Tutorial reference: [【2026 Edition】Using the "All Indonesia" Entry Card for Bali, Indonesia｜Master the Latest Process in 1 Minute: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

**PS:** Please save the QR code on your phone or print a copy for verification.

(3) Tourism Tax

- Required documents: Passport information, credit card
- Application method: Download the Love Bali App or visit <https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/home>
- Cost: IDR 150,000 per person (approximately USD$9)

**PS:** Similarly, save the QR code for verification.

(4) USD Exchange

It's recommended to exchange USD$100 bills in Taiwan first, then convert to Indonesian Rupiah in Indonesia for better rates. Local exchange rates vary based on USD bill denominations, with $100 and $50 bills offering the best rates, while $20, $10, $5, and $1 bills may have less favorable rates.

## ※ Itinerary Overview and Travelogue

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1ddc462d2d0248effc0fb73f6691b047.png)

### **Day 1: Arrival in Bali → Jimbaran Beach Sunset and Seafood Dinner**

- **Attractions**: Currency exchange, Jimbaran sunset
- **Dining**: Dinner at Malafi Cafe Jimbaran
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

Upon arriving in Bali, Betty was already waiting at the airport with a sign, her warm smile instantly easing the fatigue from the long flight. We headed to the villa to drop off our luggage and then straight to **Jimbaran Beach**. Twenty-three years later, the sunset at Jimbaran remains as breathtaking as ever, with the aroma of seafood grills wafting from neatly arranged stalls on the beach. We enjoyed fresh seafood at **Malafi Cafe Jimbaran**, accompanied by live band music. Sitting together on the beach, toes in the sand, with the orange-red sunset painting the sea, it was pure bliss.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3e474fcc7e6a5290032324cbaa76ba48.png)

For the first two nights, our villa offered options from one to four bedrooms. Friends in need can refer to [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952).

### **Day 2: Blue Cliff Road → Uluwatu Temple → Relaxing Spa**

- **Itinerary**: Water activities, Blue Cliff Road, Uluwatu Temple, Sakar Jepun Spa
- **Dining**: Lunch at WARUNG LAOTA, dinner at a nearby market
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f24fff8b05526923028a41d85303f88f.jpg)

Betty led us along the **Blue Cliff Road**, where the deep blue sea contrasted sharply with the steep cliffs. Many couples were taking wedding photos along the way, creating a romantic scene. Next, we visited **Uluwatu Temple**, marveling at the ancient Hindu architecture and intricate stone carvings. In the afternoon, we enjoyed a traditional Balinese massage at **Sakar Jepun Spa**, rejuvenating ourselves for the upcoming adventures.

### **Day 3: Safari Park → Countryside Dinner**

- **Itinerary**: Taman Safari Bali, Ayung River Rafting, Sukawati Market
- **Dining**: Lunch at Joni Countryside Restaurant (Dirty Duck Rice), dinner at Freddies Resort & Villas
- **Accommodation**: [Freddies Resort & Villas Ubud by Kanaya Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23125492)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4a04261efc67f7ef8c6413d589e1ae9c.jpg)

**Taman Safari Bali** was a favorite for the kids, offering close encounters with elephants, tigers, and other animals. We also took a safari ride into the wild zone, experiencing the vastness of an African savanna. Dinner at **Freddies Resort & Villas** was serene, with exquisite dishes.

### **Day 4: Ubud Rafting → Art Village → Rice Terrace Swing → Grilled Pork Ribs**

- **Itinerary**: Ubud rafting, Penglipuran Village, Tegallalang Rice Terrace (jungle swing)
- **Dining**: Lunch included with rafting, dinner at iiga warung (grilled pork ribs)
- **Accommodation**: [Freddies Resort & Villas Ubud by Kanaya Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23125492)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9896437fb78834627a8728b3cc73f5b5.jpg)

Start the morning with **Ayung River rafting**, enjoying the beautiful tropical rainforest scenery and gentle currents perfect for family fun—you can even jump in for a swim! Next, visit **Penglipuran Village**, a traditional Balinese art village rich in cultural heritage. The **Tegallalang Rice Terrace** jungle swing is a must-visit spot for Instagram-worthy photos, and our family couldn't resist queuing up to experience the thrill of swinging high above the lush green terraces. Dinner at **iiga warung** features delicious grilled pork ribs with a sweet and savory sauce that we still reminisce about.

- **Itinerary**: Stroll along Kuta Beach, Mal Bali Galeria
