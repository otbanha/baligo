This summer, our family of six finally returned to Bali. It had been 23 years since our last family trip to this island paradise known as the 'Island of the Gods.' While it remains as beautiful and enchanting as ever, the number of tourists has noticeably increased, and the tourism facilities have become more diverse and abundant. ## ※ Pre-Trip Preparations

### 1. Air Tickets

Eager to immerse ourselves in this vacation paradise, we opted for a direct flight with EVA Air (BR255／BR256), with a flight duration of approximately 5.5 hours.

### 2. (Very Important!) Visa, Entry, and Related Applications

Includes visa (eVOA/VOA), ~~eCD electronic customs declaration, health information card (SSHP)~~ (now integrated into [AIAC](/blog/2025-08-14-689db40ffd89780001eb2f3e/)), and tourism tax.

**Friendly reminder (based on personal experience):**

For all online applications, it is recommended to operate during "Indonesian government working hours" for smoother system performance and fewer technical issues.

**(1) Visa: eVOA / VOA**

To save time upon arrival, I chose to apply for the eVOA electronic visa on arrival before departure, allowing me to go directly through the automated gates after landing.

- Required documents: Scanned passport page (JPG/PDF), passport photo (JPG), accommodation address, flight information, credit card
- Cost: 500,000 IDR per person (approx. USD$31)
- Official application website: <https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/> <--- Only official link
- Application guide: Many tutorial articles are available in the community, such as [【2026 Edition】Using the 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card for Bali Entry｜1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

**PS:**

The visa is valid for 30 days, so it is recommended to apply about 7 days before departure.

With eVOA, you can use the automated gates. I prefer to print a paper copy as backup.

**(2) eCD Electronic Customs Declaration, SSHP Health Declaration**

- Required documents: Passport information, eVOA number, flight and accommodation details
- Application website: 'All Indonesia' Entry System <https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/>
- Fee: Free
- Tutorial reference: [【2026 Edition】Using 'All Indonesia' Entry Card for Bali, Indonesia｜1-Minute Guide to Latest Process: From Landing → Customs → Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

**PS:** Save the QR Code on your phone or print a copy for verification.

**(3) Tourism Tax**

- Required documents: Passport information, credit card
- Application method: Download Love Bali App or use website <https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/home>
- Fee: IDR 150,000/person (approx. USD$9)

**PS:** Similarly, save the QR Code for verification.

**(4) USD Exchange**

It's recommended to exchange USD$100 bills in Taiwan first, then convert to Indonesian Rupiah in Indonesia for better rates. Local exchange rates vary based on bill denominations, with $100 and $50 bills offering the best rates, while $20, $10, $5, and $1 bills have less favorable rates.

### **Day 1: Arrival in Bali → Jimbaran Beach Sunset & Seafood Dinner**

- **Attractions**: Currency exchange, Jimbaran sunset
- **Dining**: Dinner at Malafi Cafe Jimbaran
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

Upon arriving in Bali, Betty was already waiting at the airport with a welcoming sign, her warm smile instantly easing the fatigue from the long flight. We first headed to the villa to drop off our luggage, then rushed straight to **Jimbaran Beach**. Twenty-three years later, Jimbaran's sunset remains as breathtaking as ever, with the aroma of seafood grilling stalls neatly lining the beach. Dinner at **Malafi Cafe Jimbaran** featured fresh seafood accompanied by live music. Sitting together on the sand, toes in the fine grains, with the orange-red sunset over the sea before us, the moment was filled with happiness.

### **Day 2: Blue Cliff Road → Uluwatu Temple → Relaxing Spa**

- **Attractions**: Water activities, Blue Cliff Road, Uluwatu Temple, Sakar Jepun Spa
- **Dining**: Lunch at WARUNG LAOTA, dinner at a nearby market
- **Accommodation**: [Lumbini Luxury Villas and Spa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=432952)

Betty led us along the **Blue Cliff Road**, where the deep blue sea contrasted sharply with the steep cliffs. Along the way, many couples were taking wedding photos, creating a romantic atmosphere. We then visited **Uluwatu Temple**, an ancient Hindu structure with intricate stone carvings that left us in awe. In the afternoon, we treated ourselves to a traditional Balinese massage at **Sakar Jepun Spa**, rejuvenating our bodies and preparing for the adventures ahead.

### **Day 4: Ubud Rafting → Art Village → Rice Terrace Swing → BBQ Ribs**

- **Itinerary**: Ubud Rafting, Penglipuran Village, Tegallalang Rice Terrace (Jungle Swing)
- **Dining**: Lunch included with rafting, Dinner at iiga warung (BBQ Ribs)
- **Accommodation**: [Freddies Resort & Villas Ubud by Kanaya Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23125492)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9896437fb78834627a8728b3cc73f5b5.jpg)

We started the day with **Ayung River Rafting**, enjoying the lush tropical rainforest scenery and calm waters perfect for family fun—some of us even jumped in for a swim! Next, we explored **Penglipuran Village**, a traditional Balinese art village rich in cultural heritage. The **Tegallalang Rice Terrace** jungle swing was a must-visit Instagram spot, and our family couldn't resist taking a turn, soaring above the emerald-green terraces—thrilling and breathtaking. Dinner at **iiga warung** featured mouthwatering BBQ ribs, with flavors that lingered long after the meal.
