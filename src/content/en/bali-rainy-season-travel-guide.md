### When interpreting the data in this chart, please note the following points:

## **Average Rainfall Probability**

**"Average Rainfall Probability"** refers to the likelihood of rainfall occurring in a specific area and time period, usually expressed as a percentage. For example, if the rainfall probability for a certain area is forecasted to be 30%, it means that under similar conditions, it has rained in that area 30% of the time in the past. It's important to note that rainfall probability does not indicate the duration or intensity of the rainfall, nor does it mean that it will rain 30% of the time in that area. When interpreting rainfall probability, understand that it only represents the likelihood of precipitation occurring, and is unrelated to the extent of precipitation.

## **Rainy Days**

**"Rainy Days"** refers to the number of days in which at least one rainfall event occurs. Typically, a daily rainfall of 0.1 mm or more is considered a rainfall event. Therefore, rainy days count the total number of days with rainfall over a certain period. It's important to note that rainy days do not reflect the intensity or duration of the rainfall. For example, if a location experiences rainfall on 5 days in a month, with each rainfall exceeding 0.1 mm, then the number of rainy days for that month is 5.

## **Explanation of Temperature**

"Average temperature" refers to the actual measured air temperature, while "feels like" temperature is how hot or cold the human body perceives it to be, influenced by humidity, wind speed, and sunlight. For example, at the same 30°C, higher humidity makes it feel more stifling, while wind makes it feel cooler.

## **Travel Suitability Index**

"Travel Suitability Index" is a comprehensive recommendation based on factors such as monthly rainfall probability, rainy days, average temperature, and tourist crowds. A higher score indicates more stable weather, fewer tourists, and better conditions for outdoor activities. For instance, June and September score 100%, representing dry, comfortable weather with fewer tourists, making them ideal for travel. December scores only 50% due to frequent rainfall and crowded Christmas holidays, making indoor activities more recommended. This index helps travelers quickly assess whether the weather and crowd levels are ideal for their chosen month.

## **It Rarely Rains All Over the Island**

In Bali, rainfall distribution is influenced by geography, with eastern and northern regions experiencing more rain, and mountainous areas being wetter than lowlands. Therefore, even during the rainy season, some areas may enjoy sunny weather while others experience rainfall. It is very rare for the entire island to be rainy simultaneously.
