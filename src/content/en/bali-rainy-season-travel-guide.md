### When interpreting the data in this chart, please note the following points:

## **Average Rain Probability**

**"Average Rain Probability"** refers to the likelihood of rainfall occurring in a specific area and time period, usually expressed as a percentage. For example, if the forecast predicts a 30% chance of rain in a certain area, it means that under similar conditions, it has rained 30% of the time in the past. It's important to note that rain probability does not indicate the duration or intensity of rainfall, nor does it mean that it will rain 30% of the time in that area. When interpreting rain probability, understand that it only represents the likelihood of precipitation occurring, and is not related to the amount of precipitation.

## **Rainy Days**

**"Rainy Days"** refers to the number of days with at least one rainfall event. Typically, a day is considered rainy if the rainfall reaches 0.1mm or more. Therefore, rainy days count the total number of days with rainfall during a certain period. Note that rainy days do not reflect the intensity or duration of rainfall. For example, if a location experiences rainfall exceeding 0.1mm on 5 days in a month, then the number of rainy days for that month is 5.

## **Temperature Explanation**

"Average temperature" refers to the actual measured air temperature, while "feels like temperature" is how hot or cold the human body perceives it to be, influenced by humidity, wind speed, and sunlight. For example, at the same 30°C, higher humidity makes it feel more stifling, while wind makes it feel cooler.

## **Travel Suitability Index**

"Travel Suitability Index" is a comprehensive recommendation based on factors such as monthly rainfall probability, number of rainy days, average temperature, and tourist crowds on the island. A higher score indicates more stable weather and fewer tourists, making it ideal for outdoor activities. For instance, June and September score 100%, representing dry, comfortable weather with fewer tourists, making them the best months to visit. December scores only 50% due to frequent rainfall and crowded Christmas holidays, making indoor activities more recommended. This index helps travelers quickly assess whether the weather and crowd levels are ideal for their visit.

## **It Rarely Rains Across the Entire Island**

In Bali, rainfall distribution is influenced by topography, with eastern and northern regions experiencing more rain, and mountainous areas being rainier than flatlands. Therefore, even during the rainy season, some areas may enjoy sunny weather while others experience rainfall. It is very rare for the entire island to be raining simultaneously.
