If you want to stay right in the center of Seminyak, where you can walk everywhere and return to a rooftop pool to watch the sunset, then Cross Paasha Bali Seminyak, a four-star design boutique hotel, is worth considering. Formerly known as U Paasha, it was renamed Cross Paasha but remains at Jl. Laksmana No. 77, in the heart of Kayu Aya Street. All 99 rooms are suite-level, spacious, and strongly designed, making it one of the higher value options in Seminyak.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231500443-roof1.jpg)

***

## 📍 Location and Surroundings

Cross Paasha is located in the prime Laksmana area of Seminyak, known as 'Eat Street.' Just a two-minute walk from the hotel is Seminyak Square shopping center, and Seminyak Village Mall is only a three-minute walk away. Daily shopping, restaurants, and cafes are all within walking distance.

As for the beach, Seminyak Beach is about a 10 to 15-minute walk, which isn't too close but is still manageable; a Grab ride takes about 5 minutes. The surrounding area is rich with attractions: Petitenget Beach and Pura Petitenget temple are a five-minute drive away, La Lucciola seaside restaurant and Ku De Ta are a 10-minute walk, and Potato Head Beach Club is a three-minute drive.

The overall vibe of Seminyak is a mix of boutique shopping and nightlife, with a high density of restaurants, bars, and fashion stores on the streets. If you're looking to enjoy a refined urban experience in Bali, this location is almost unbeatable.

***

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

Stepping into the lobby, the first impression is unlike traditional Balinese-style resorts — with its high ceilings, geometric brick walls, and streamlined furniture, the industrial and modern design elements blend together quite tastefully. Upon check-in, complimentary welcome drinks are offered. The lobby bar area is dimly lit with a cool atmosphere, primarily attracting younger guests and couples.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231575224-lobi.webp)

The hotel's biggest highlight is the **Rooftop Pool**. This pool is long and spacious, surrounded by palm trees, lounge chairs, and sun umbrellas. On a clear evening, you can gaze over the Seminyak cityscape and even catch a glimpse of the sea. Adjacent to the pool is a bar where you can order cocktails, and during Happy Hour, there's reportedly a buy-one-get-one-free deal, making it the social hub of the hotel.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231634104-roof3.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231634525-roof2.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231635037-roof5.jpg)

The restaurant ALCOVE offers a breakfast buffet with both Asian and Western options, rotating daily. Features include a live egg station, pancakes, and Indonesian fried rice.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231683899-canteen1.webp)

The hotel also includes a gym with basic equipment. A particularly unique policy is the **24-hour check-in system**: your stay begins exactly 24 hours from your check-in time, with flexible check-out, not bound by the traditional 12 PM check-in/11 AM check-out rules. This is very accommodating for travelers with uncertain flight schedules.

***

## 🛏️ Room Overview

Cross Paasha's 99 rooms are uniformly called Suites, positioning them a step above standard rooms. Starting from 55 square meters, the largest room can reach up to 110 square meters, offering considerable space for the price.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231736986-86495544.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231737371-558542199.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231737832-1c93851cb69bca784431d5ea40507f08.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231738211-683edcbff56e61d844944a683b65f028.webp)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776231738613-45807d9b3c69fd21a57adfb2190c610a.webp)

The decor follows a light industrial and modern design theme: plenty of light wood furniture, concrete floors, and details like hexagonal mirrors, creating a clean and crisp atmosphere, distinct from the traditional Balinese carved wood decor. Each room features a private balcony, a separate seating area, a flat-screen TV, a minibar, free Wi-Fi, and air conditioning. The bathroom is designed openly with a rain shower.

Room selection advice: **the higher the floor, the better**. Lower-floor rooms facing the street may occasionally suffer from road noise, while higher floors offer significantly better sound insulation and more expansive views. For couples traveling together, it's recommended to directly book a high-floor balcony suite with a view.

***

## 👥 Ideal For

Perfect for **young travelers visiting with friends or partners**, especially first-timers to Seminyak who want convenient access to shopping, dining, and nightlife. The location is this hotel's biggest asset—most activities are within walking distance, eliminating the need for Grab rides. It's also suitable for families, as the hotel offers a kids' pool and childcare services. However, those traveling with infants or seeking a quieter environment should pay extra attention to room floor selection.

***

## 💬 Guest Reviews

A long-term repeat guest on Tripadvisor shared that this was their fifth stay, and it was worth it every time. They particularly praised the spacious rooms and the staff's attentive service, saying they always felt well taken care of. (Source: Tripadvisor)

Another guest on Agoda mentioned that breakfast here isn't the typical buffet but more like a restaurant-style dining experience. Located at the heart of Kayu Aya Street, everything is within walking distance, and the room's spaciousness was unexpectedly impressive for the price.

***

## ⚠️ Honest Downsides

A few things to note. First, some reviews mention minor issues like mold spots or aging walls in older rooms (the hotel has recently changed management and is reportedly making improvements). Second, noise can be an issue in lower-floor rooms facing the street, especially at night with motorcycles and club music. Third, the rooftop pool gets full sun exposure during the day with limited shaded areas, making it less ideal for extended poolside stays in summer or midday heat.
