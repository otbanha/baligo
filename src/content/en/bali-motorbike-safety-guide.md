**Bottom line**: The biggest insurance claim trap for scooter accidents in Bali is this—**without both an International Driving Permit (IDP) and a valid Taiwanese motorcycle license, insurance companies can outright deny your claim**, leaving you to pay all medical costs out of pocket. To avoid this risk, the safest option is to **use private car hire or Gojek/Grab ride-hailing**. If you insist on riding yourself, make sure to get your IDP before you go and confirm your policy covers scooter accidents.

Recently, a member of our Facebook group, Poon, shared his travel experience in Bali, highlighting the importance of travel safety, especially the risks of riding a scooter. He urged other travelers to be extra cautious while exploring Bali.

## Scooter Riding: Convenience and Danger Go Hand in Hand

Bali’s stunning scenery and diverse culture draw countless visitors, and scooters have become the go-to mode of transport for exploring this beautiful island. Their flexibility lets travelers zip between attractions and dodge heavy traffic. But this convenience also comes with hidden dangers.

Poon’s experience reminds us that while scooters are handy, in an unfamiliar place—especially with complex road conditions and traffic rules different from your home country—the risk of accidents rises sharply.
