If you visit Bali, don't miss the Kecak Fire Dance performance! It's an unforgettable experience that will stay with you forever!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f8c6296d8f2f09b45c9843546731b432.jpg)

## A Breathtaking Performance

Here's a brief introduction to this dramatic and awe-inspiring Kecak Fire Dance:

A visit to Bali wouldn't be complete without witnessing some of its most captivating traditional performances. Alongside the Barong and Janger dances, another dazzling show you absolutely shouldn't miss is the traditional Kecak Fire Dance, known as "卡恰火舞" in Chinese. This is one of Bali's artistic masterpieces, presented in the form of dance and musical theater.

The most famous Kecak Fire Dance in Bali takes place at the open-air amphitheater on the cliff overlooking the sea in Uluwatu. It's held at sunset, relying entirely on natural daylight. The performance begins at dusk and continues into the night, illuminated only by flickering bamboo torches.

What makes this dance particularly unique is that it doesn't use any artificial backdrops or musical instruments. The focus is entirely on a concentric circle of about 50-60 shirtless men wearing distinctive Balinese sarongs, sitting cross-legged around a central set of torches.

Unlike other traditional Balinese performances that are accompanied by a gamelan orchestra, the Kecak Fire Dance features a male choir representing a monkey army. They continuously chant "Cak! Cak! Cak!" or "Keh-Chak" in polyrhythmic voices throughout most of the performance. This astonishing vocal orchestra is led by a soloist who directs the high and low tones while also serving as the narrator. Over time, this creates a dramatic wall of sound against which the performance unfolds.

## The Story

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f2406be4ab4d6ea6026d450f8c08440e.jpg)

The Kecak Fire Dance performance at Uluwatu is a traditional Balinese dance drama that draws its storyline from the Indian epic, the Ramayana. This performance focuses on the tale of Prince Rama rescuing his wife, Sita.

The story goes like this: Prince Rama lives happily with his beautiful wife, Sita, until one day she is kidnapped by the evil demon king Ravana. To rescue his wife, Prince Rama enlists the help of the monkey god Hanuman and his army of monkeys. In the end, amidst flames and dance, Rama defeats Ravana, saves Sita, and brings her safely home.

During the performance, actors dressed in traditional costumes chant "cak cak cak" in the background, with flames adding to the mystical atmosphere. This fire dance not only tells the legendary story of heroism but also immerses the audience in the unique cultural charm of Bali.

## Origins of the Kecak Fire Dance

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a406dcf7d7e0aeedce7bcc6494100b63.jpg)

The Kecak Fire Dance is said to originate from an ancient Balinese ritual called Sanghyang, which was performed as a form of exorcism or to ward off evil spirits, with dancers entering a trance-like state. The dance first appeared in 1930 when Balinese dancer Wayan Limbak collaborated with German painter Walter Spies to incorporate the legendary story of the Ramayana into a dramatic version of the Sanghyang performance. They took this innovative creation on a world tour, which helped popularize the dance.

The Kecak Fire Dance is regularly performed in many places across Bali. However, the best place to witness this spectacular performance is Uluwatu, where two dance shows are held daily against the backdrop of a stunning sunset.
