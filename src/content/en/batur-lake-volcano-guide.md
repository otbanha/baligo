## **The Majestic and Serene Batur Caldera Lake**

![The Majestic and Serene Batur Caldera Lake](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a5e37e35b2726428b7743348685d03c0.jpg)

Nestled in the Kintamani region, Lake Batur is a vast volcanic crater lake formed by an ancient eruption. Sitting at an elevation of about 1,000 meters and surrounded by layered mountain ridges, it looks like a living painting when mist swirls at dawn.

In Balinese culture, Lake Batur is considered the home of the goddess Dewi Danu, a sacred symbol of water and fertility, and a vital water source for Bali's agriculture. The lakeside Pura Ulun Danu Batur temple honors her and serves as a key religious center. Annual ceremonies are held here to pray for favorable weather and bountiful harvests.

Watching the sunrise from the summit of Mount Batur is a must-do for many travelers. As the sun slowly breaks through the clouds, golden light reflects off the lake, and the whole world seems to stand still—only the heartbeat of nature remains.

## Exploring the Sacred Site: Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur Water Temple

![Exploring the Sacred Site: Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur Water](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1781672235683-dscf3056_1200.webp)

### **Bali Has More Than One Water Temple**

When it comes to water temples in Bali, most people think of **Pura Ulun Danu Beratan on Lake Bratan**—that iconic temple reflected on the lake's surface. But few know that on **Lake Batur** lies another equally important, yet more mysterious and serene water temple: **Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur**.

#### **More Than a Lake—An 'Inland Sea'**

This temple sits right beside the magnificent Lake Batur. In Balinese philosophy, this caldera lake is considered a sacred 'inland sea' (Segara means ocean), serving as a vital source of life-giving water for the island. Compared to the grandeur and fame of Pura Ulun Danu Beratan, Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur feels more humble and profound—it directly honors Dewi Danu, the goddess of water and fertility, who protects the life-giving waters of the entire caldera lake.

#### **A Small Yet Mighty Pearl of a Temple**

Rather than a grand complex, this temple feels like a secluded pearl. Its main structure is built on a solid rock bed beneath the lake, connected to the land by a wooden bridge—at high tide, it truly appears to float on the water. The most striking feature is the towering **11-tiered Meru tower** (multi-tiered shrine), a number symbolizing the highest sacred status in Bali, dedicated exclusively to the water goddess Dewi Danu. Standing here, you'll feel a spiritual power woven from tranquility and solemnity.

#### **Atmosphere and Practical Info**

This temple sees relatively few visitors, offering an incredibly peaceful and serene atmosphere—perfect for travelers who love exploring hidden gems and escaping the crowds. Visiting at dawn or dusk especially reveals the temple's subtle beauty.

**Ticket info:** Approximately 20,000 to 25,000 Indonesian rupiah (about USD$1-2)

## **Hiking or Sunrise Jeep Tour**

### 🔥 **Hiking to Catch the Sunrise**

![Hiking to Catch the Sunrise](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_faaf8d67d5e06d033ea24d05e691b831.png)

Want to feel a true sense of conquest? Joining a sunrise hike up Mount Batur is a classic adventure! You'll typically set off in the early hours, taking about 2–3 hours to reach the summit. As the first rays of light break across the sky, the entire lake and surrounding peaks shimmer in golden hues—a breathtaking sight that leaves you speechless.

## 🌅 **Sunset Jeep Safari Experience**

Not a fan of waking up before dawn? No worries! Besides the sunrise experience, Mount Batur also offers a **sunset jeep tour**, letting you conquer the volcano at a more leisurely pace. Departing around midday, you'll skip the hike and ride a private off-road jeep directly to the prime sunset viewpoint, where you can watch the sun slowly sink behind the mountains in a spectacular display.

This experience is perfect for:

- **Travelers who dislike early mornings**: It's more relaxed and avoids the crowds.
- **Photography enthusiasts**: Comes with a professional jeep driver who doubles as a photographer, capturing every stunning moment.
- **Those who love a laid-back pace**: Enjoy panoramic views at a Kintamani café before heading to the volcano viewpoint.

The entire tour lasts about 8 hours and includes hotel pickup and drop-off, attraction entrance fees, an English-speaking driver-guide, professional photography services, and snacks and bottled water.

![Royal Voyage: A Dual Experience on Lake Batur](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3ca74bdad12e693945d91b0c5b5888cf.jpg)

Watching the sun slowly rise over Mount Batur is one of the most awe-inspiring memories for any traveler. After your hike, try the emerging lake activity—the **Royal Songan Cruise on Lake Batur**.

This approximately 60–90 minute lake cruise will take you through:

- **Trunyan Village** — the origin village of the Bali Aga culture;
- **Dewi Danu Statue** — the goddess symbol guarding the volcanic lake;
- **Panoramic views of Mount Batur and Mount Abang** — taking in the majestic mountain scenery.

The cruise features live cultural performances, tea service, and panoramic seating, making it perfect for romantic couples and family trips.

## **Transportation & Tips**

- From Ubud, the drive takes about 1.5 hours. You can hire a private driver or join a tour.
- The dry season (May–October) offers stable weather, making it the best time for photos.
- Remember to bring a jacket and sunscreen, as the mountain climate can be unpredictable.

Absolutely! With professional drivers, it's both safe and thrilling throughout.

**5. Is Lake Batur suitable for family trips?**

Most private drivers can arrange a day trip for you.
