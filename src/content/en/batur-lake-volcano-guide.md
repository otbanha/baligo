## **The Majestic and Serene Lake Batur**

![壯麗與靜謐共存的巴杜爾火山湖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a5e37e35b2726428b7743348685d03c0.jpg)

**Bottom line**: Lake Batur sits in the Kintamani highlands at an elevation of about **1,000 meters**. Besides the famous Mount Batur sunrise trek, the lakeshore hides a lesser-known gem—**Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur Water Temple** (distinct from the famous one on Lake Bratan)—perfect for a one-day car charter itinerary combining sunrise trekking, a boat cruise, lakeview cafés, and volcanic hot springs.

Nestled in the Kintamani region, Lake Batur is a massive volcanic caldera lake formed by an ancient eruption. At roughly 1,000 meters above sea level, it's surrounded by layered mountain ranges, often veiled in mist at dawn, like a living, breathing painting.

In Balinese culture, Lake Batur is considered the dwelling place of the goddess Dewi Danu, a sacred symbol of water and fertility, and a vital source for irrigating Bali's agriculture. The Pura Ulun Danu Batur temple on its shores is dedicated to her and serves as a key religious center. Annual ceremonies are held here to pray for good weather and bountiful harvests.

Watching the sunrise from the summit of Mount Batur is a must-do for many travelers. As sunlight slowly pierces the clouds, casting golden rays across the lake, the whole world seems to pause, leaving only the heartbeat of nature.

## Exploring the Sacred Site: Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur Water Temple

![探訪聖地：Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur 水神廟](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1781672235683-dscf3056_1200.webp)

### **Bali Has More Than One Water Temple**

When it comes to water temples in Bali, most people think of **Pura Ulun Danu Beratan** on Lake Bratan—that iconic temple reflected on the lake's surface. But few know that on **Lake Batur** lies another equally important, yet more mysterious and serene water temple: **Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur**.

#### **More Than a Lake—It's an 'Inland Sea'**

This temple sits right beside the majestic Lake Batur. In Balinese philosophy, this caldera lake is considered a sacred 'inland sea' (Segara means sea), serving as a vital source of life-giving water for the island. Compared to the ornate and famous Pura Ulun Danu Beratan, Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur feels more humble and profound—it directly honors Dewi Danu, the goddess of water and fertility, who protects the life-giving waters of the entire caldera lake.

#### **A Small but Mighty Pearl of a Temple**

Rather than a grand complex, this temple feels like a secluded pearl. Its main structure is built on a solid rock bed beneath the lake, connected to the shore by a wooden bridge—at high tide, it truly appears to float on the water. The most striking feature is the towering **11-tiered Meru tower** (multi-level shrine), a number that in Bali symbolizes the highest sacred status, dedicated specifically to the water goddess Dewi Danu. Standing here, you'll feel a spiritual power woven from tranquility and solemnity.

#### **Atmosphere and Practical Info**

This temple sees far fewer visitors, offering an incredibly peaceful and serene atmosphere—perfect for travelers who love exploring hidden gems and escaping the crowds. Visiting at dawn or dusk is especially rewarding, as the temple reveals its subtle beauty in the soft light.

**Ticket info:** Approximately 20,000 to 25,000 Indonesian Rupiah (about USD$1-2)

## 🌅 **Sunset Jeep Safari Experience**

Not a fan of waking up before dawn? No worries! Besides the sunrise experience, Mount Batur also offers a **sunset jeep tour**, allowing you to conquer the volcano at a more relaxed pace. Departing around midday, you'll skip the hike and ride a private off-road jeep directly to the prime sunset viewpoint, where you can watch the sun slowly sink behind the mountains in a spectacular display.

This experience is perfect for:

- **Travelers who hate early mornings**: It's more relaxed and avoids the crowds.
- **Photography enthusiasts**: A professional jeep driver doubles as a photographer, capturing every stunning moment.
- **Those who love a leisurely pace**: Enjoy panoramic views at a Kintamani café before heading to the volcano viewpoint.

The entire tour lasts about 8 hours and includes hotel pickup and drop-off, attraction entrance fees, an English-speaking driver-guide, professional photography services, and snacks and bottled water.

## **Transportation & Tips**

- From Ubud, it's about a 1.5-hour drive. You can hire a private driver or join a tour.
- The dry season (May–October) offers stable weather, making it the best time for photos.
- Bring a jacket and sunscreen—the mountain climate can be unpredictable.
