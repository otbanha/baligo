## **The Majestic and Serene Lake Batur**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a5e37e35b2726428b7743348685d03c0.jpg)

Nestled in the Kintamani region, Lake Batur is a vast volcanic caldera lake formed by an ancient eruption. Sitting at an elevation of about 1,000 meters, it is surrounded by layered mountains, often shrouded in mist at dawn, like a living painting.

In Balinese culture, Lake Batur is considered the dwelling place of the goddess Dewi Danu, a sacred symbol of water and fertility, and a vital source for irrigating Bali's agriculture. The lakeside Pura Ulun Danu Batur temple honors her and serves as a key religious center. Annual ceremonies are held here to pray for favorable weather and bountiful harvests.

Watching the sunrise from the summit of Mount Batur is a must-do for many travelers. As sunlight slowly pierces the clouds, casting golden rays across the lake, the world seems to stand still, leaving only the heartbeat of nature.

## Exploring the Sacred Site: Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur Water Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1781672235683-dscf3056_1200.webp)

### **Bali Has More Than One Water Temple**

When people think of Bali's water temples, most picture Pura Ulun Danu Beratan on Lake Bratan—the iconic temple reflected on the water. But few know that on Lake Batur, there is an equally important, yet more mysterious and serene water temple: Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur.

#### **Not Just a Lake, but an 'Inland Sea'**

This temple sits right next to the stunning Lake Batur. In Balinese philosophy, this caldera lake is considered a sacred 'inner sea' (Segara means ocean), serving as a vital source of life-giving water for the island. Compared to the ornate and famous Pura Ulun Danu Bratan, Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur feels more humble and profound—it is directly dedicated to Dewi Danu, the goddess of water and fertility, who protects the life-giving waters of the entire caldera lake.

#### **A Small but Mighty Pearl of a Temple**

Rather than a grand complex, this temple feels like a secluded pearl. Its main structure is built on a solid rock bed beneath the lake's surface, connected to the shore by a wooden bridge—at high tide, it truly appears to float on the water. The most striking feature is the towering **11-tiered Meru tower** (multi-tiered shrine), a number that in Bali symbolizes the highest sacred status, dedicated exclusively to the water goddess Dewi Danu. Standing here, you'll feel a spiritual power woven from both tranquility and solemnity.

## **Hiking or Sunrise Jeep Tour**

### 🔥 **Hiking at Sunrise**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_faaf8d67d5e06d033ea24d05e691b831.png)

Want a true sense of achievement? Joining a sunrise hike up Mount Batur is a classic adventure! You'll typically set off before dawn, taking about 2–3 hours to reach the summit. As the first rays of light break across the sky, the entire lake and surrounding peaks shimmer in golden hues—a breathtaking sight that will leave you speechless.

## 🌅 **Sunset Jeep Safari Experience**

Not a morning person? No problem! Besides the sunrise experience, Mount Batur also offers a **sunset jeep tour**, letting you conquer the volcano in a more relaxed way. Departing around midday, you'll skip the hike and ride a private off-road jeep directly to the prime sunset viewpoint, where you can watch the sun slowly sink behind the mountains in a spectacular display.

This experience is especially perfect for:

- **Travelers who dislike early mornings**: More relaxed and less crowded
- **Photography enthusiasts**: Comes with a professional jeep driver who doubles as a photographer, capturing every exciting moment
- **Those who prefer a leisurely pace**: Enjoy panoramic views at a Kintamani café before heading to the volcano viewpoint

The entire tour lasts about 8 hours and includes hotel pickup and drop-off, entrance fees, an English-speaking driver-guide, professional photography services, and snacks and bottled water.

Watching the sun rise from Mount Batur is one of the most awe-inspiring memories for travelers. After your hike, why not try a new lake activity—the **Royal Songan Cruise on Lake Batur**.

This 60- to 90-minute cruise takes you through:

- **Trunyan Village** — the origin village of the Bali Aga culture;
- **Dewi Danu Statue** — a symbol of the goddess guarding the volcanic lake;
- **Panoramic views of Mount Batur and Mount Abang** — offering a sweeping vista of majestic peaks.

The cruise features live cultural performances, tea service, and panoramic seating, making it perfect for romantic couples and family trips.

## **Transportation & Tips**

- It takes about 1.5 hours by car from Ubud. You can hire a private driver or join a tour.
- The dry season (May–October) offers stable weather, making it the best time for photos.
- Remember to bring a jacket and sunscreen, as the mountain climate can be unpredictable.
