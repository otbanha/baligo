Finally, I arrived in Bali this summer, a place I've always dreamed of visiting. This trip had many moments that matched my expectations, along with some unexpected surprises and reflections that I'd like to share with everyone:

## Weather

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3121af7a76e9ab9aeae87f42ed213a36.jpg)

The weather in Bali wasn't as hot as I had imagined. Rainstorms and sunshine often appeared on the same day. The rain could be torrential, occurring every one or two days, but the wind wasn't too strong. I recommend bringing a light jacket, as it can get a bit chilly when it rains. When engaging in outdoor activities, always carry rain gear to avoid sudden downpours disrupting your plans. Additionally, sunscreen is essential, as the UV rays are still very strong when the sun is out, despite the unpredictable weather.

## Aesthetics and Decor

The shops, aesthetics, and decor in Bali are quite impressive, especially in some hotels and yoga studios, where bamboo and bamboo weaving are skillfully used to decorate roofs and chandeliers. While Vietnamese bamboo weaving is more intricate, Indonesian designs are more creative and grand. I particularly loved the bamboo chandeliers here, which provided a lot of inspiration for future home decor. These unique design styles not only reflect local cultural characteristics but also convey a sense of beauty that combines nature and modernity. During your travels, take plenty of photos to capture these beautiful designs as inspiration for future home decorations.

## Transportation

Bali's traffic is indeed quite chaotic, with hardly any traffic lights to be seen. Cars and motorcycles share the same lanes, and dedicated pedestrian walkways are almost nonexistent. Walking around can be nerve-wracking, as you constantly worry about being hit if you're not careful. It's advisable to wear sturdy shoes, as there are also many puddles to navigate. The traffic order here is vastly different from other cities, and it takes some time to adjust when you first arrive. When choosing transportation options, it's a good idea to make use of ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek, which are both convenient and safe, reducing the risks associated with walking.

## Plants

Bali's vegetation is even more lush than you might imagine, making it a paradise for plant lovers. Everywhere you look, you'll see vast stretches of greenery and various exotic plants, many of which you might be seeing for the first time in your life. The island's botanical gardens and nature reserves are must-visit attractions, where you can not only admire rare plants but also learn a lot about tropical flora. Don't forget to bring your camera along to capture these verdant landscapes.

## Insects

Bali is also home to a lot of insects, moths, and ants. The locals have a harmonious attitude towards these creatures, often coexisting peacefully with them. It's common to see offerings placed outside shops for the insects to feed on. Encountering insects during a spa session isn't unusual, and locals typically don't kill them but instead catch them by hand 🪰. This approach to nature is quite fascinating and reflects the locals' respect for life. During your travels, it's advisable to carry insect repellent, especially when engaging in outdoor activities, to avoid getting bitten.

## Accessory Shops

Bali's accessory shops are incredibly appealing, such as Blink, where almost every store makes you want to step inside and browse. There are also many local designer brands that incorporate natural elements into their designs, offering great value for money. These accessories are not only beautifully designed but also rich in local cultural essence, making them excellent choices for souvenirs or gifts. When purchasing accessories, it's worth engaging with the shop owners to learn the stories behind each piece, which adds to the uniqueness of these items.

## Transportation

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dafbbe49d363e8f35135436c504badf2.png)

Using Grab or Gojek for taxis is very convenient, and most drivers are polite. A 20-minute ride typically costs around USD$3, and for longer distances, you can opt for a chartered car service. Chartered car services are highly flexible, with drivers usually tailoring the route to your needs, allowing you to make the most of your time and visit more attractions. Before setting off, it's a good idea to discuss your itinerary with the driver to ensure you can smoothly reach each destination.

## Yoga Apparel

I initially thought yoga clothes here would be cheap, but they turned out to be quite expensive. Well-known brands like Indigo Luna start at around USD$65-100, while the yoga apparel downstairs at Alchemy costs over USD$160. At these prices, Lululemon seems like a better deal. Although the prices are high, the quality and design of these brands' yoga wear are excellent. If you find a style you love, it might still be worth the investment. When shopping, compare a few stores to find the best fit and price for you.

## Yoga Classes

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6968936fa4c0c6ca57750fc1614ccd39.jpg)

Yoga Barn offers many classes, but the facilities are a bit outdated, perhaps due to overly high expectations. There are plenty of flashy classes, but fewer traditional yoga sessions. The free yoga classes at hotels, however, are quite good. If you're interested in yoga, try out different studios to find the best classes and instructors for you. Bali offers a wide variety of yoga courses, catering to everyone from beginners to advanced practitioners.

## Food

Bali's cuisine mainly features pork ribs, fried noodles, and fried rice. Those who love vegetable juices will adore the fresh juices here—super fresh, perfect for two glasses a day. The ingredients are fresh, and the flavors are unique, making it worth trying. While enjoying the food, take the time to learn about the local culinary culture and experience different taste sensations. Visit local specialty restaurants to not only enjoy authentic dishes but also immerse yourself in the rich cultural atmosphere.

I recommend trying different spas during your trip to relax and enjoy the leisurely pace of life here. The warmth and hospitality of the locals make you feel truly welcomed, whether you're at a restaurant, shop, or tourist spot. This friendly atmosphere adds many wonderful memories to your journey.
