Finally made it to Bali this summer, a place I've always dreamed of visiting. This trip had many moments that matched my expectations, as well as some unexpected surprises and reflections that I'd like to share:

## **Weather**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3121af7a76e9ab9aeae87f42ed213a36.jpg)

Bali's weather wasn't as hot as I imagined, especially since heavy rain and bright sunshine often occur on the same day. The rain can be torrential, happening once every day or two, but the wind isn't too strong. I recommend bringing a light jacket, as it can get chilly when it rains. When engaging in outdoor activities, always carry rain gear to avoid sudden downpours disrupting your plans. Additionally, sunscreen is essential, as UV rays are still very strong even when the weather is unpredictable.

## **Aesthetics and Decor**

The shops, aesthetics, and decor in Bali are quite impressive, especially in some hotels and yoga studios that excel in using bamboo and woven bamboo to decorate roofs and chandeliers. Vietnamese bamboo weaving is more intricate, but Indonesian designs are more creative and grand. I really loved the bamboo chandeliers here, and they provided a lot of inspiration for my future home decor. These unique design styles not only reflect local cultural characteristics but also convey a sense of beauty that blends nature with modernity. During your travels, don't forget to take plenty of photos to capture these beautiful designs as inspiration for future home decoration.

## **Transportation**

Bali's traffic is notoriously chaotic, with almost no traffic lights, mixed lanes for cars and motorcycles, and barely any dedicated sidewalks. Walking on the streets can be nerve-wracking, as you constantly worry about being hit. It's advisable to wear sturdy shoes, as puddles are everywhere. The traffic system here is quite different from other cities, and it takes some time to adjust when you first arrive. For transportation, it's recommended to use ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek, which are convenient and safe, reducing the risks of walking.

## **Plants**

Bali's vegetation is even more lush than you might imagine, making it a paradise for plant lovers. Everywhere you look, you'll see vast stretches of greenery and unique plants, many of which you might be seeing for the first time in your life. The island's botanical gardens and nature reserves are must-visit spots, where you can not only admire rare plants but also learn a lot about tropical flora. Don't forget to bring your camera to capture these verdant landscapes during your trip.

## **Insects**

Bali is also home to a lot of insects, moths, and ants. The locals have a harmonious approach to dealing with bugs, often leaving offerings outside their shops for the little creatures. It's not uncommon to encounter insects during a spa session, and locals usually handle them by catching them bare-handed 🪰. This respectful attitude towards nature is fascinating and reflects the locals' reverence for life. During your trip, it's advisable to carry insect repellent, especially when engaging in outdoor activities, to avoid bites.

## **Jewelry Shops**

Bali's jewelry shops are incredibly charming, such as Blink, where almost every store makes you want to step inside. There are also many local designer brands that incorporate natural elements into their designs, offering great value for money. These pieces are not only beautifully crafted but also infused with local cultural essence, making them perfect souvenirs or gifts. When shopping for jewelry, take the time to chat with the shop owners to learn the stories behind each piece, which adds to their uniqueness.

## **Transportation**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dafbbe49d363e8f35135436c504badf2.png)

Using Grab or Gojek for taxis is very convenient, and most drivers are polite. A 20-minute ride typically costs around USD$3, and for longer distances, you can hire a private car. Private car services are flexible, with drivers usually tailoring the route to your needs, allowing you to make the most of your time and visit more attractions. Before setting off, discuss your itinerary with the driver to ensure you can smoothly reach each destination.

## **Yoga Apparel**

I initially thought yoga clothes here would be cheap, but they turned out to be quite expensive. Well-known brands like Indigo Luna start at around USD$65-100, while the yoga apparel at Alchemy downstairs costs over USD$160. At these prices, Lululemon seems like a better deal. Although the prices are high, the quality and design of these brands' yoga wear are excellent. If you find a style you love, it’s worth the investment. When shopping, it’s a good idea to compare a few stores to find the best style and price for you.

## **Yoga Classes**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6968936fa4c0c6ca57750fc1614ccd39.jpg)

Yoga Barn offers a wide variety of classes, but the facilities are a bit outdated, which might be disappointing if your expectations are high. There are many flashy classes, but fewer traditional yoga sessions. The free yoga classes at hotels are surprisingly good. If you’re interested in yoga, try out different studios to find the one that suits you best. Bali offers a diverse range of yoga classes, catering to everyone from beginners to advanced practitioners, ensuring there’s something for every level.

## **Food**

Bali’s cuisine mainly features pork ribs, fried noodles, and fried rice. If you love vegetable juices, you’ll adore the fresh juices here—super refreshing, perfect for two glasses a day. The ingredients are fresh, and the flavors are unique, making the food worth trying. While enjoying the local cuisine, take the time to learn about the island’s culinary culture and savor the diverse tastes. Don’t miss out on local specialty restaurants, where you can enjoy authentic dishes and immerse yourself in the cultural atmosphere.

## **People and Services**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3215b9db05b9e4049dd8c6ef5859a350.jpg)

Most people here are very polite and friendly, and it’s safe to walk around at night. The spa services are excellent, affordable, and offer great value for money. I recommend trying different spas during your trip to relax and enjoy the leisurely pace of life here. The warmth and hospitality of the locals make the experience even more memorable. Whether you’re at a restaurant, shop, or attraction, you’ll feel their kindness and attentiveness, adding a special touch to your journey.

## Conclusion

This trip to Bali was filled with many surprises and moving moments. I hope my sharing can help everyone better prepare and enjoy the wonderful times in Bali. Whether it's exploring natural beauty, experiencing local culture, or relaxing your mind and body, Bali can meet all your needs. I hope you can also spend a beautiful time in this dreamlike place, just like I did.
