## **Paramount Pictures Ventures into Bali｜Kerthi Bali Semesta Park Set to Open in 2026**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e1514a7e3f77a9ddd98d7ba1560bd96.jpg)

Bali is stepping into a new era of tourism. Located in the **Jembrana Regency** of West Bali, the **Kerthi Bali Semesta Park (KBS Park)** project is now in its main construction phase and is expected to **officially open in 2026**. This theme park is a collaboration between **Paramount Pictures** and **Indonesian company PT Kios Ria Kreas (KIOS)**, aiming to merge international entertainment brands with Balinese culture, making it Indonesia's first integrated film and cultural theme park.

***

## **Park Theme: A Perfect Blend of Hollywood Entertainment and Balinese Legends**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2bdc5bdefc0003786f5f2c91e844831b.jpg)

Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is designed with two main thematic areas:

- **International Film and Entertainment Zone**: Showcasing iconic characters and brands from Paramount, such as **SpongeBob**, **Dora**, and **Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles** from **Nickelodeon**, creating immersive experiences and family-friendly facilities.
- **Balinese Mythology and Culture Zone**: Inspired by Balinese legends like **Barong and Rangda**, this area will use technology and stage performances to bring island myths to life, allowing visitors to experience both the thrill of Hollywood and the depth of Balinese culture.

Additionally, the park will feature a **Craft and Cultural Education Center**, offering workshops in traditional dance, handicrafts, and music, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the essence of Balinese culture.

***

## **Sustainability and Eco-Design: Creating a Green Tourism Landmark**

Kerthi Bali Semesta Park will be Bali's first theme park built around the core concept of **sustainable development**. The entire park will incorporate:

- **Solar Power Systems**
- **Rainwater Harvesting and Recycling Technology**
- **Electric Transportation Vehicles**
- **Ecological Green Zone Conservation Areas**

Parts of the park will be designated as **'Green Zones'**, preserving rare flora and fauna ecosystems, creating an environment where entertainment coexists with nature.

Jembrana Regent **Kembang Hartawan** stated: 'Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is a symbol of new economy that combines tradition and innovation. We hope Jembrana becomes a model for sustainable development.'

***

## **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway: The Lifeline Connecting Future Tourism**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d34afd400c1c24665aedfd62011a78c5.jpg)

To support the massive tourism demand and infrastructure, the Indonesian government is simultaneously advancing the **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway (part of the Jagat Kerthi Bali Project)**, with **over 20% of preliminary work already completed**.

This **96.8-kilometer-long** highway will connect **Gilimanuk Port** with the **Mengwi area**, effectively linking West Bali and South Bali's main tourism zones. Once completed, travel time from Denpasar to Jembrana will be reduced from 4-5 hours to **approximately 2 hours**, making it more convenient for visitors to reach the park and surrounding areas.

Along the route, there will be:

- **Rest stops and viewing platforms**
- **Theme pavilions showcasing Balinese mythology**
- **Electric vehicle charging stations and renewable energy facilities**

Additionally, the highway construction will also promote the **upgrading of Gilimanuk Port**, creating new waterfront promenades and docks, connecting Bali with Java, and strengthening tourism and logistics links.

***

## **Economic and Social Benefits: Transforming Jembrana's Future**

The Indonesian Ministry of Tourism estimates that after the opening of Kerthi Bali Semesta Park, **Jembrana could attract up to 5 million visitors annually**, creating **over 25,000 job opportunities** across various sectors including tourism, services, transportation, environmental management, and cultural education.

In addition, alongside the development of the park, Jembrana will also introduce:

- **Two five-star hotels and resorts**
- **A golf course and sports academy**
- **Gilimanuk waterfront promenade and a new pier**

This will not only boost the local tourism economy but also significantly improve residents' quality of life and upgrade infrastructure.

***

## **Jembrana's New Role: A Model for Sustainable Tourism in Bali**

Jembrana has long been considered one of Bali's slowest-developing regions. However, with the dual advancement of **Kerthi Bali Semesta Park** and the **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway**, the area is set to transform into a **cultural, technological, and ecological tourism hub**.

Kembang Hartawan emphasized, "We hope this project is not just about construction but also a spiritual renewal. We aim to make Jembrana a model where tradition and modernity coexist, and culture and nature progress together."

The official opening is scheduled for **2026**, with construction progressing steadily.

The park integrates Paramount Pictures' cinematic characters with Balinese mythological culture, and includes educational experiences and ecological conservation areas.

**5. How to get to the park from Denpasar?**

In the future, visitors can take the Gilimanuk-Mengwi Expressway directly to Jembrana, with a travel time of just about two hours.
