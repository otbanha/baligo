## **Paramount Pictures Enters Bali｜Kerthi Bali Semesta Park Expected to Open in 2026**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e1514a7e3f77a9ddd98d7ba1560bd96.jpg)

Bali is stepping into a new era of tourism. The **Kerthi Bali Semesta Park (KBS Park)** project, located in the **Jembrana Regency** of West Bali, has entered its main construction phase and is expected to **officially open in 2026**. This theme park is a collaboration between **Paramount Pictures** and **Indonesian company PT Kios Ria Kreas (KIOS)**, aiming to combine international entertainment brands with Balinese culture, making it Indonesia's first integrated park blending film and culture.

***

## **Park Theme: Hollywood Entertainment × Balinese Legends Perfectly Merged**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2bdc5bdefc0003786f5f2c91e844831b.jpg)

Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is designed with two main themed areas:

- **International Film and Entertainment Zone**: Showcases Paramount's iconic characters and brands, such as **SpongeBob**, **Dora**, and **Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles** from **Nickelodeon**, creating immersive experiences and family-friendly facilities.
- **Balinese Mythology and Culture Zone**: Inspired by Balinese legends like **Barong and Rangda**, it uses technology and stage performances to recreate island myths, allowing visitors to experience both the excitement of Hollywood and the depth of Balinese culture.

Additionally, the park will establish a **Craft and Cultural Education Center**, offering workshops in traditional dance, handicrafts, and music, allowing visitors to experience the essence of Balinese culture.

***

## **Sustainability and Eco-Design: Creating a Green Tourism Landmark**

Kerthi Bali Semesta Park will be Bali's first theme park built with **sustainable development** as its core philosophy. The entire park will feature:

- **Solar Power Systems**
- **Rainwater Harvesting and Recycling Technology**
- **Electric Transportation Vehicles**
- **Ecological Green Zone Conservation Areas**

Parts of the park will be designated as "**Green Zones**" to preserve rare flora and fauna, creating an environment where entertainment coexists with nature.

Jembrana Regent **Kembang Hartawan** stated: "Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is a symbol of new economy that combines tradition and innovation. We hope Jembrana becomes a model for sustainable development."

***

## **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway: The Lifeline Connecting Future Tourism**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d34afd400c1c24665aedfd62011a78c5.jpg)

To support the massive tourism demand and infrastructure, the Indonesian government is simultaneously advancing the **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway (part of the Jagat Kerthi Bali Project)**, with **over 20% of the preliminary work** already completed.

This **96.8-kilometer-long** highway will connect **Gilimanuk Port** with the **Mengwi area**, effectively linking West Bali and South Bali's main tourism zones. Once completed, the travel time from Denpasar to Jembrana will be reduced from 4-5 hours to **approximately 2 hours**, making it more convenient for visitors to access the park and surrounding areas.

Along the route, there will be:

- **Rest stops and viewing platforms**
- **Pavilions showcasing Balinese mythological stories**
- **Electric vehicle charging stations and renewable energy facilities**

Additionally, the highway construction will also promote the **upgrade and transformation of Gilimanuk Port**, creating new waterfront promenades and docks, connecting Bali with Java, and strengthening tourism and logistics links.

***

## **Economic and Social Benefits: Transforming Jembrana's Future**

The Indonesian Ministry of Tourism estimates that after the opening of Kerthi Bali Semesta Park, **Jembrana could attract up to 5 million visitors annually**, creating **over 25,000 jobs** across various sectors including tourism, services, transportation, environmental management, and cultural education.

In addition, alongside the development of the park, Jembrana will also add:

- **Two five-star hotels and resorts**
- **A golf course and sports academy**
- **Gilimanuk waterfront promenade and a new marina**

This will not only boost the local tourism economy but also drive a comprehensive upgrade in residents' quality of life and infrastructure.

***

## **Jembrana's New Role: A Model for Sustainable Tourism in Bali**

Long considered one of Bali's slowest-developing regions, Jembrana is set to transform into a **cultural, technological, and ecological tourism hub** with the dual advancement of **Kerthi Bali Semesta Park** and the **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway**.

Kembang Hartawan emphasized: "We hope this project is not just about construction, but also a spiritual renewal. We aim to make Jembrana a model where tradition coexists with modernity, and culture progresses alongside nature."

***

## **FAQ**

**1. When will Kerthi Bali Semesta Park open?**

The official opening is scheduled for **2026**, with construction progressing steadily.

**2. What is the completion timeline for the Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway?**

As of now, approximately 20% of the preliminary work has been completed, with the project expected to be finished around 2026, coinciding with the opening of the theme park.

**3. What are the unique themes within the park?**

The park combines Paramount Pictures' cinematic characters with Balinese mythological culture, and includes educational experiences and ecological conservation areas.

**4. How will this project benefit local residents?**

It will create approximately 25,000 jobs, improving transportation, environmental conditions, and economic status.

**5. How to get to the park from Denpasar?**

In the future, visitors can take the Gilimanuk-Mengwi Expressway directly to Jembrana, with a drive time of about two hours.

**5. What other must-visit parks are there in Bali?**
