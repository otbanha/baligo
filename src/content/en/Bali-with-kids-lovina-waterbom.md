I had always wanted to take my daughter on a trip, just the two of us. This summer, right before she turned 8, we finally made this little wish come true.

We spent 6 days and 5 nights in Bali (June 27 to July 2), without cramming in too many 'must-see' spots. Instead, I wanted a relaxed, unhurried pace to truly enjoy exploring the world together as mother and daughter. We played at Waterbom Water Park, went dolphin watching in Lovina, strolled through the Ubud rice terraces, and watched the culturally rich Legong dance—each day brought its own surprises and memories.

Before the trip, I also referred to many experiences shared by seniors in various groups. So after this trip, I wanted to compile my actual insights into a travel diary, hoping to provide some reference for those planning a free-and-easy trip to Bali, especially for family travel.

***

## Day 1–2｜Stay in Kuta First, No Rush to Hit the Itinerary

After arriving in Bali, we checked into Kuta for two consecutive nights, and on the second day, we spent the whole day at Waterbom Water Park.

> [【Bali Must-Do for Families】Complete Guide to Waterbom Bali Water Park: Asia's Best, Slide Ratings, and Money-Saving Ticket Tips](/en/blog/waterbom-bali-water-park-guide/)

Considering the fatigue from flying and traveling with a child, I deliberately kept the first two days light. Choosing to stay near the airport made transportation easy and allowed us to gradually adjust to the travel pace without having to endure a long drive right after landing. This made the trip feel relaxed from the very start.

Looking back now, this arrangement was truly perfect for a family trip. The kids had plenty of rest time, and we adults didn't have to scramble from the start—instead, we could savor each day ahead even more.

## Day 3｜From Kuta to Lovina, Hiring a Driver Was the Best Decision

On June 29, we officially began our northward journey, heading from Kuta to Lovina.

Since the drive was quite long, we decided to hire a private driver. Through a recommendation in a travel group, we found Ani and booked two days of driving, from Lovina in the north all the way down to Ubud in the central region. Looking back now, this was truly one of the best decisions of the entire trip.

Not only did it save us the hassle of transferring between transport, but we could also set our own pace without worrying about rushing. Especially when traveling with kids, being able to stop and rest anytime made things so much easier.

Along the way, Ani shared a wealth of stories about Bali's history, religious beliefs, local culture, and everyday tales of the people. This turned the journey into more than just visiting sights—it became a slow, meaningful introduction to the island during every ride.

***

## Day 4｜Dolphin Watching in Lovina and Alas Harum, Scenery More Stunning Than Imagined

On June 30, we set out before dawn to watch dolphins at sea.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783767974106-lovina2.jpeg)

Luckily, we not only spotted pods of dolphins leaping across the surface but also enjoyed a beautiful sunrise over the ocean. As the first rays of light rose from the horizon, painting the sky in golden hues, that moment made every early wake-up call feel completely worth it.

But that day, a little hiccup happened.

While out at sea, I accidentally broke my power bank. With several more days of travel ahead and my phone needed for navigation, photos, and communication, I was really panicked.

To my surprise, Ani found out and immediately helped me look for shops along the way, even taking the initiative to talk to the owners. In the end, I managed to buy a reasonably priced power bank that met airline regulations. This seemingly small gesture made me deeply appreciate his thoughtfulness and kindness.

After leaving Lovina, we headed south to Ubud.

Along the way, my daughter insisted on visiting the Alas Harum rice terraces. Honestly, I didn't have high expectations at first—I just thought, since she wanted to go, we'd give it a try.

But the weather turned out to be fantastic. Sunlight poured over the layered terraces, turning the entire valley a lush green that looked like a painting. Every casual snapshot felt like a postcard.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783768368173-mixcollage-11-jul-2026-05-59-pm-751.jpg)

(Photo courtesy of Jean Chen)

I also have to praise Ani's photography skills. He didn't just press the shutter—he really knew how to find the right angles, capture the light, and even guided my daughter on posing. Thanks to him, we ended up with many family photos we'd never have been able to take on our own.

Also, the Alas Harum complex is bigger than I imagined—it's not just the rice terraces; the whole environment is beautifully maintained. After walking through the entire area, only one thought remained in my mind:

**"I'm so glad I included this place."**

One more tip: the free tea tasting at Alas Harum used to be complimentary, but it's now a paid experience. Ani proactively informed us about the updated pricing before we arrived and offered alternatives, with no hard selling at all—every decision was ours to make, which felt very comfortable.

## Day 5｜Check into Ubud and Enjoy the Most Classic Legong Dance

For our last two nights, we stayed in Ubud, a town brimming with art, culture, and spirituality.

If you're in Ubud, I highly recommend spending one evening at the Ubud Palace to watch the Legong Dance.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783768573781-mixcollage-11-jul-2026-06-00-pm-2889.jpg)

(Provided by Jean Chen)

> Book tickets for the grand fire dance performance at Ubud Palace every Thursday: [Klook](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1336040&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F80603-ubud-kecak-fire-dance-show-tickets-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Da2835b3f21)

During peak season, the show typically starts at 7:30 PM and ends around 9:00 PM, with tickets priced at 100K Indonesian Rupiah per person. This time, I bought tickets directly at the palace entrance.

> Book the daily Legong dance show at Ubud Palace during peak season: [Trip](https://hk.trip.com/things-to-do/detail/108153384?Allianceid=6817581&SID=311634184&trip_sub1=&trip_sub3=D18620695)

I recommend arriving at least 30 minutes early to secure front-row seats or a spot with a good view.

Accompanied by the gamelan orchestra, the dancers' delicate expressions, graceful gestures, and ornate costumes together showcase Bali's profound cultural heritage. Even without understanding the storyline, you can immerse yourself in the rich performance atmosphere—it's truly worth experiencing.

The scenery along the way, the cultural stories, and the people we met on the journey all added so much warmth to this trip.

## The trip is over, but the memories are just beginning

Our six-day, five-night journey flew by, but what we truly brought home isn't just the countless photos on our phones—it's every moment we shared together.

From chasing dolphins at sea for the first time, having so much fun at Waterbom we didn't want to go back to the hotel, strolling through lush green rice terraces, watching the Legong dance, to looking out for each other along the way and solving little hiccups together—these seemingly ordinary moments are the most precious memories we'll cherish in the future.

Maybe the true meaning of travel has never been about how many sights you see, but about seeing a bigger world together while accompanying each other, and leaving behind your own stories.

Finally, I want to thank every friend in this group who has been willing to share their experiences. Thanks to the information you've generously provided, we avoided many detours and made this mother-daughter trip much smoother.

I hope this travelogue can also offer some practical tips for the next traveler heading to Bali. If you're planning a family trip, I wish you all the best in creating your own beautiful memories in Bali.
