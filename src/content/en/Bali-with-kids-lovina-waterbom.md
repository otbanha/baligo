I had always wanted to take my daughter on a trip, just the two of us. This summer, right before she turned 8, we finally made that little dream come true.

We spent 6 days and 5 nights in Bali (June 27 to July 2), without cramming in too many 'must-see' spots. Instead, I wanted us to enjoy a relaxed, unhurried pace and savor the time exploring the world together as mother and daughter. We played at Waterbom water park, went dolphin watching in Lovina, strolled through the Ubud rice terraces, and took in the culturally rich Legong dance—each day brought its own surprises and memories.

Before the trip, I also read many tips shared by experienced travelers in online groups, so after returning, I wanted to compile my own real experiences into a travelogue, hoping to offer some useful references for those planning a Bali trip, especially families with kids.

***

## Day 1–2｜Stay in Kuta First, No Rush to Hit the Road

After arriving in Bali, we checked into Kuta for two nights, and on the second day, we spent the whole day at Waterbom water park.

Knowing that flying can be tiring, especially with a child in tow, I deliberately kept the first two days light. Choosing to stay near the airport made transfers easy and let us slowly adjust to the travel rhythm without having to endure a long drive right after landing. It made the start of the trip much more relaxing.

Looking back, this arrangement was perfect for a family trip. The kids got plenty of rest, and I didn’t have to scramble from the get-go, which made every day after that even more enjoyable.

***

## Day 3｜From Kuta to Lovina, Hiring a Driver Was the Best Decision We Made

On June 29, we officially began our northbound journey, heading from Kuta to Lovina.

Since the drive was quite long, we decided to hire a private driver. Through a recommendation in a travel group, we found Ani and booked him for two days, taking us all the way from Lovina in the north down to Ubud in the south. Looking back, this was truly one of the best decisions of the entire trip.

Not only did it save us the hassle of navigating transfers on our own, but it also let us set the pace and plan our itinerary without worrying about the clock. Traveling with kids, being able to stop and rest whenever needed made everything so much easier.

Along the way, Ani shared so much about Bali's history, religious beliefs, daily culture, and local stories, turning every drive into a chance to slowly get to know the island—not just visit its sights.

***

## Day 4｜Dolphin Watching in Lovina & Alas Harum, Scenery More Breathtaking Than Expected

On the early morning of June 30, we set out before dawn to watch dolphins at sea.

Luckily, we not only saw pods of dolphins leaping across the water but also enjoyed a stunning sunrise over the ocean. As the first rays of light rose from the horizon, painting the sky in shades of gold, it was one of those moments that made every early wake-up call completely worth it.

However, a small hiccup occurred that day.

When we went out to sea, I accidentally broke my power bank. With several more days of travel ahead, and my phone needed for navigation, photos, and communication, I was really panicked at that moment.

To my surprise, as soon as Ani found out, he immediately helped me look for shops along the way and proactively communicated with the owners. In the end, I managed to buy a power bank at a reasonable price that also met airline regulations. This seemingly small gesture made me deeply appreciate his attentiveness and kindness.

After leaving Lovina, we headed south to Ubud.

Along the way, my daughter specifically asked to visit the Alas Harum rice terraces. To be honest, I didn't have high expectations at first—I just thought, since she wanted to go, we might as well give it a try.

As it turned out, the weather was fantastic that day. Sunlight poured over the layered terraces, making the entire valley look as green as a painting—every snapshot looked like a postcard.

I also have to praise Ani's photography skills. He didn't just press the shutter; he really knew how to find the right angles and capture the light, and even guided my daughter on posing. As a result, we got many great family photos that we'd normally struggle to take on our own.

Moreover, the Alas Harum complex was much larger than I expected. Beyond the rice terraces, the entire environment was beautifully maintained. After walking through the whole area, only one thought came to mind:

**'I'm so glad we included this place.'**

One more tip: the free tea tasting at Alas Harum is now a paid experience. Ani proactively informed us about the updated pricing before we arrived and offered alternatives, without any pushy sales tactics. All decisions were left entirely to us, which made the experience feel very relaxed.

After each attraction, he was already waiting for us at the exit right on time—no need to find a car, no waiting. The entire trip flowed seamlessly.

## Day 5｜Checking into Ubud, Watching the Classic Legong Dance

For our last two nights, we stayed in Ubud, a town brimming with art, culture, and spirituality.

If you're in Ubud, I highly recommend spending an evening watching the Legong Dance at the Ubud Palace.

During peak season, performances usually start at 7:30 PM and end around 9:00 PM, with tickets priced at 100K IDR per person. I bought mine directly at the palace entrance.

I suggest arriving at least 30 minutes early to snag a front-row seat or a spot with a good view.

Accompanied by the Gamelan orchestra, the dancers' intricate eye movements, graceful gestures, and ornate costumes bring Bali's rich cultural heritage to life. Even if you don't follow the story, you'll be swept up in the powerful atmosphere—it's an experience well worth having.

## The trip is over, but the memories are just beginning

The six-day, five-night journey ended quickly, but what I truly brought home isn’t just the countless photos on my phone—it’s every moment we shared together.

六天五夜的旅程很快就結束了，但真正帶回家的，不只是手機裡滿滿的照片，而是那些一起經歷的每一個瞬間。

From chasing dolphins at sea for the first time, spending hours at Waterbom until we didn’t want to go back to the hotel, strolling through lush green rice terraces, and watching the Legong dance, to looking out for each other and solving little hiccups along the way—these seemingly ordinary moments are the most precious memories we’ll cherish in the future.

Perhaps the true meaning of travel isn’t about how many sights you see, but about growing together through companionship, discovering a bigger world, and leaving behind your own unique story.

Finally, I want to thank every friend in the community who generously shared their experiences. Thanks to the selfless information you provided, we avoided many detours and made this mother-daughter trip much smoother.

I hope this travelogue can also offer some practical tips for the next traveler heading to Bali. If you’re planning a family trip, I wish you the joy of creating your own beautiful memories in Bali.
