I had always dreamed of taking a trip with just my daughter, and this summer, as she was about to turn 8, we finally made that little wish come true.

This time we spent 6 days and 5 nights in Bali (June 27 to July 2), without cramming in too many 'must-see' attractions. Instead, we aimed for a relaxed, unhurried pace to truly savor the time exploring the world together as mother and daughter. We visited Waterbom water park, went dolphin watching in Lovina, strolled through the Ubud rice terraces, and enjoyed the culturally enchanting Legong dance—each day brought its own surprises and memories.

Before setting off, I also referred to the experiences shared by many seniors in various forums. ## Day 1–2｜Stay in Kuta first, no need to rush the itinerary

After arriving in Bali, we checked into Kuta for two consecutive nights, and on the second day, we spent the whole day at Waterbom Water Park.

Knowing how tiring flying can be, especially with a little one in tow, I deliberately kept the first two days light and easy. I chose to stay near the airport, which made getting around a breeze and let us ease into the travel rhythm without having to endure a long drive right after landing. The trip felt relaxed from the very start.

Looking back now, this arrangement was truly perfect for a family trip. The kids had plenty of rest time, and we adults didn't have to scramble from the start—instead, we could enjoy each day that followed even more.

## Day 3｜From Kuta All the Way to Lovina, Hiring a Private Driver Was the Best Decision

On June 29, we officially began our northward journey, heading from Kuta to Lovina.

Since the drive was quite long, we decided to hire a private car. Through a community recommendation, we found Ani and booked the car for two days, traveling from Lovina in the north all the way down to Ubud in the south. Looking back now, it was truly one of the best decisions of the entire trip.

Besides saving you the hassle of transferring on your own, it also lets you plan your itinerary at your own pace without worrying about rushing. Especially when traveling with kids, being able to rest and stop whenever you need makes things so much more convenient.

Along the way, Ani shared a wealth of stories about Bali's history, religious beliefs, lifestyle, and the everyday lives of locals. This turned the trip into more than just a sightseeing tour—it became a journey of slowly getting to know the island during every drive.

## Day 4｜Dolphin Watching in Lovina, Alas Harum – The Scenery Was Even More Breathtaking Than Expected

On the early morning of June 30, we set out to sea before dawn to chase dolphins.

Luckily, not only did we see pods of dolphins leaping across the ocean surface, but we also witnessed a stunning sunrise at sea. As the first rays of sunlight rose from the horizon, painting the sky in shades of gold, that moment truly made every early wake-up call worth it.

However, a little incident happened that day as well.

While out at sea, I accidentally broke my power bank. With several more days of travel ahead and my phone needed for navigation, photos, and communication, I honestly panicked a bit in that moment.

To my surprise, Ani immediately helped me look for shops along the way, proactively communicated with the owner, and eventually helped me buy a power bank that was reasonably priced and met airline carry-on regulations. This seemingly trivial act deeply moved me and made me truly appreciate his thoughtfulness and kindness.

After leaving Lovina, we headed south all the way to Ubud.

Along the way, my daughter specifically wanted to visit the Alas Harum rice terraces. To be honest, I didn't have high expectations at first—I just thought, since she wanted to go, we might as well give it a try.

Unexpectedly, the weather turned out to be absolutely perfect that day. Sunlight spilled across the layered rice terraces, painting the entire valley in a vibrant green that looked just like a painting. Every casual snapshot came out looking like a postcard.

Even more praiseworthy was Ani's photography skills — not just pressing the shutter, but truly knowing how to find the right angles and capture the light. She also guided our daughter in posing, which allowed us to capture many family photos that would have been difficult to take on our own.

## Day 5｜Check in to Ubud and Watch the Most Classic Legong Dance

For the final two nights of our journey, we checked into Ubud, a place brimming with art, culture, and spiritual vibes.

If you're in Ubud, I highly recommend spending an evening at the Ubud Palace to watch the Legong Dance.

During peak season, performances usually start at 7:30 PM and end around 9:00 PM, with tickets priced at 100K Indonesian Rupiah per person. This time, I bought my ticket directly at the palace entrance.

It is recommended to enter the venue at least 30 minutes early to have a better chance of securing front-row seats or spots with a good view.

Accompanied by the live performance of a gamelan orchestra, the dancers' intricate eye movements, graceful gestures, and ornate costumes collectively showcase Bali's profound cultural heritage. Even if you can't follow the storyline, you'll be completely immersed in the rich atmosphere of the live performance—an experience truly worth having.

## The trip may be over, but the memories are just beginning

The six-day, five-night trip ended all too quickly, but what I truly brought home wasn't just the countless photos on my phone—it was every single moment we experienced together.

From chasing dolphins at sea for the first time, spending hours at Waterbom until we didn’t want to go back to the hotel, strolling through lush green rice terraces, watching a Legong dance, to looking out for each other and solving little hiccups along the way—these seemingly ordinary moments are the most precious memories we’ll cherish in the future.

The meaning of travel may never be about how many sights you've seen, but about seeing a bigger world together and leaving behind your own stories in the process of accompanying one another.
