During Taiwan's Qingming holiday in April, taking just 3 days off can give you a 9-day vacation. Many plan to travel abroad during this time, but Indonesia/Bali might not be the best destination for the 2025 Qingming holiday!

This is because nearly 90% of Indonesia's population practices Islam. Among all Islamic holidays, Eid al-Fitr holds significance comparable to our Lunar New Year. According to the Islamic calendar, **Eid al-Fitr in 2025 coincides exactly with our Qingming holiday**

Following Nyepi Day on March 29, 2025 comes Eid al-Fitr. The Eid holiday begins at the end of March and lasts for a whole week. As Indonesia's most important holiday, while celebrating the end of Ramadan, it's also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, much like our Chinese Lunar New Year

Based on past experience, Bali will likely start seeing an influx of Indonesian visitors from March 24, with crowds lasting about two weeks. This marks **the first of Bali's five peak seasons**, a period we recommend avoiding for your Bali trip!

During the Eid al-Fitr period in 2023, Bali's airport handled 1.17 million passengers and 7,554 flights in just over half a month. For reference, Bali's total annual visitor count is over 6 million. You can imagine not just traffic jams, but possibly even people jams during this period!

If you must visit Bali during this time or have already booked your tickets, please carefully consider the following guidelines to minimize disruptions to your trip:

## **Flights and Accommodation**

Due to the high demand during the annual Eid al-Fitr holiday, both airfare and hotel prices in Bali tend to rise. It is advisable to book in advance to secure better prices and options.

## **Visa**

If you hold a Republic of China (Taiwan) passport, you can apply for a visa on arrival, which costs IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$31).

For those planning to apply for an e-visa, please do so early! During the Eid al-Fitr holiday, which lasts about nine days, the Indonesian Immigration Office typically suspends online e-visa applications as government offices are also on a long holiday. This means everyone will have to apply for a visa on arrival, potentially leading to long queues! Those who have already obtained an e-visa will not be affected and can still enjoy smooth automatic clearance.

## Transportation

Will there be heavy traffic in Bali during the Qingming Festival and Eid al-Fitr? Should I still visit tourist spots?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9749c24c3bb6a64ec5579ecf7b89dc27.png)

Here are some specific recommendations for navigating Bali's traffic during this special period:

**Plan your travel route in advance**

> Try to avoid visiting well-known tourist spots as traffic congestion can be severe! If you still wish to go, it is recommended to use Google Maps or other mapping applications to plan your route before departure. You can check traffic conditions and choose the optimal route.

**Allow Plenty of Time**

> During the Qingming Festival-Eid al-Fitr period, traffic will be heavier, so you need to allow plenty of time. When using Google Maps to estimate travel time, it's recommended to add a 50% buffer during normal times (if Google Maps shows one hour, estimate one and a half hours); during the Qingming holiday period, add at least double the time.

**Avoid Peak Travel Times**

> Bali's traffic peaks usually start around midday and continue into the evening. If you can avoid traveling during these times, you'll reduce the likelihood of encountering traffic congestion.

> The best mode of transportation in Bali is hiring a private car. Check out the '[Complete Guide to Private Car Hire in Bali - Costs, Booking Methods, Advantages](/blog/2024-01-12-65a0a163fd8978000115f37a/)' for more details.

## **Beware of the Rainy Season**

The Qingming Festival and Eid al-Fitr fall towards the end of Bali's rainy season, which can be unpredictable — it might not rain at all, or it might rain for several days in a row. When visiting Bali during the rainy season, it's essential to have backup plans for sudden downpours.
