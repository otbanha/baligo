During Taiwan's Qingming holiday in April, taking just 3 days off can give you a 9-day vacation. Many plan to travel abroad during this time, but Indonesia/Bali might not be the best destination for the 2025 Qingming holiday!

This is because nearly 90% of Indonesia's population practices Islam. Among Islamic holidays, Eid al-Fitr is as significant as our Lunar New Year. According to the Islamic calendar, Eid al-Fitr in 2025 coincides with our Qingming holiday.

In 2025, Eid al-Fitr begins right after Nyepi Day on March 29. The Eid holiday starts in late March and lasts for a full week. Eid al-Fitr is Indonesia's most important holiday, marking the end of Ramadan and a time for families and friends to gather, much like our Lunar New Year.

Based on past experiences, Bali is expected to see an influx of local Indonesians starting March 24, with crowds lasting for about two weeks. This marks the first peak season of Bali's five major tourist seasons—a time best avoided for travel!

During the 2023 Eid al-Fitr period, Bali's airport handled 1.17 million passengers and 7,554 flights in just over half a month. For reference, Bali's annual tourist count is around 6 million. You can imagine not just traffic jams but also crowded spaces during this time!

If you're certain about traveling to Bali during this period or have already booked your tickets, follow these guidelines to minimize disruptions to your trip:

## ****Flights and Hotels****

During the Eid al-Fitr holiday, accommodations in Bali are in high demand, causing flight and hotel prices to rise. It's recommended to book early to secure better prices and options.

## ****Visa****

If you hold a Republic of China passport, you can obtain a visa on arrival for 500,000 IDR (approximately USD$31).

For those planning to apply for an e-visa, please apply early! During the Eid al-Fitr holiday, which lasts about nine days, the Indonesian immigration office typically closes online e-visa applications as government offices are also on holiday. This means everyone must get a visa on arrival, potentially leading to long queues! Those who have already obtained an e-visa can still enjoy smooth automatic clearance.

## Transportation

Will Bali experience heavy traffic during Qingming Festival-Eid al-Fitr? Should I visit tourist spots?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9749c24c3bb6a64ec5579ecf7b89dc27.png)

Here are some specific suggestions for navigating Bali's traffic during this special period:

****Plan your travel route in advance****

> Try to avoid visiting famous attractions as traffic congestion could be severe! If you still wish to go, it's recommended to use Google Maps or other map applications to plan your route beforehand. Check traffic conditions and choose the optimal route.

****Allow plenty of time****

> During the Qingming Festival-Eid al-Fitr period, traffic congestion worsens, so you'll need to allocate extra time. When estimating travel time using Google Maps, add a 50% buffer during normal periods (if Google Maps shows one hour, estimate one and a half hours); during the Qingming holiday period, double the estimated time.

****Avoid traveling during peak hours****

> Bali's traffic peaks usually start around midday and continue into the evening. If you can avoid traveling during these times, you'll reduce the likelihood of encountering traffic jams.

****Opt for private car hire****

> The best way to get around Bali is by hiring a private car. Check out the '[Complete Guide to Private Car Hire in Bali - Costs, Booking Methods, and Advantages](/blog/2024-01-12-65a0a163fd8978000115f37a/)' for more details.
