During Taiwan's Qingming holiday in April, taking just 3 days off can give you a 9-day vacation. Many plan to travel abroad during this time, but Indonesia/Bali might not be the best destination for the 2025 Qingming holiday!

This is because nearly 90% of Indonesia's population is Muslim. Among all Islamic holidays, Eid al-Fitr is as significant as our Lunar New Year. According to the Islamic calendar, **Eid al-Fitr in 2025 coincides exactly with our Qingming holiday**

Following Nyepi Day on March 29, 2025, Eid al-Fitr begins. The holiday starts at the end of March and lasts for a week. As Indonesia's most important holiday, while celebrating the end of Ramadan, it's also a time for Indonesians to gather with family and friends, much like our Lunar New Year

Based on past experience, Bali will likely start seeing an influx of Indonesian visitors from March 24, with crowds lasting about two weeks. This marks **the first peak season among Bali's five major high seasons** - a period we recommend avoiding!

During the more than two weeks of Eid al-Fitr in 2023, Bali's airport handled 1.17 million passengers and 7,554 flights. For reference, Bali's total annual visitor count is just over 6 million. You can imagine not just traffic jams, but potentially even people jams during this period!

If you must visit Bali during this time or have already booked your tickets, please carefully follow these guidelines to minimize disruptions to your trip:

## **Flights and Accommodation**

As Bali's hospitality industry faces high demand during Eid al-Fitr each year, flight and hotel prices tend to rise. It's recommended to book in advance to secure better prices and options.

## **Visa**

If you hold a Republic of China (Taiwan) passport, you can apply for a visa on arrival for IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$31).

For those planning to apply for an e-visa, please do so early! During the approximately nine-day Eid al-Fitr holiday period, Indonesia's immigration office typically suspends online e-visa applications as government offices are also on extended holiday. This means everyone must apply for visas on arrival, potentially leading to long queues! Those who have already obtained their e-visa won't be affected and can enjoy smooth automated clearance.

## Transportation

Will Bali experience heavy traffic during Qingming Festival-Eid al-Fitr? Should I visit tourist spots?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9749c24c3bb6a64ec5579ecf7b89dc27.png)

Here are some specific transportation suggestions for Bali during this special period:

**Plan your route in advance**

> Try to avoid visiting famous attractions as traffic congestion may be severe! If you still want to go, it's recommended to use Google Maps or other mapping apps to plan your route beforehand. Check traffic conditions and choose the optimal route.

**Allow ample time**

> During the Qingming Festival-Eid al-Fitr period, traffic congestion increases significantly, so you'll need to allow extra time. When using Google Maps to estimate travel time, it's recommended to add 50% buffer during normal times (if Google Maps shows one hour, estimate one and a half hours); during the Qingming holiday period, double the estimated time.

**Avoid peak travel hours**

> Bali's traffic peaks usually start around noon and continue into the evening. If you can avoid traveling during these times, you'll reduce your chances of encountering traffic congestion.

**Opt for Private Car Hire**
