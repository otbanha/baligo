This 10-day, 9-night self-guided trip to Bali with my girlfriend (June 26, 2024 - July 5, 2024) was truly unforgettable. Before departure, we gathered valuable travel insights from the '[Bali/Indonesia Travel Guide](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide)' platform. To give back, we’d like to share our experience and highly recommend our private driver, Ketut. His warm hospitality and expertise made our Bali journey even more special, especially for Taiwanese travelers like us.

## First Encounter: Warmth and Attention to Detail

Upon arriving at Denpasar Airport, we contacted Ketut. He greeted us with a constant smile and genuine enthusiasm. Right from the start, he went out of his way to assist us, from carrying our luggage to opening car doors—small gestures that left a lasting impression. On the way to our villa, Ketut took us to a highly-rated currency exchange spot and shared fascinating insights about Bali’s culture and traditions. Through his explanations, we learned that Bali is the only Hindu-majority region in Indonesia, blending local customs with ancient beliefs. This unique spiritual system, which includes ancestor worship, nature reverence, and devotion to deities, felt truly special to us.

## Unexpected Surprises During the Dry Season

Our trip coincided with Bali’s dry season, offering pleasant weather without the humidity we’re used to in Taiwan. It was the peak of the tourist season, but not overly crowded, ensuring a comfortable travel experience. However, we did encounter a few rainy days, which meant missing out on some sunset and sunrise views. These small regrets have already become reasons for us to plan a return visit in the future.

## Despite a Cold, Careful Planning Continues

Although Ketut felt unwell on our second day in Bali, he still arranged for another driver, Manx Liega, from his professional tour guide team. Liega had a completely different personality from Ketut. While he didn’t initiate much conversation, his planning of the itinerary was equally impressive. His silence didn’t detract from his professionalism; instead, it allowed us to enjoy the beautiful ocean views with a greater sense of peace and comfort.

## An Encounter at Tanah Lot Temple

During our visit to Tanah Lot Temple (Pura Tanah Lot), we encountered many wild macaques. While Ketut shared stories about these monkeys, he actively helped shoo away the mischievous ones that tried to steal items from tourists, such as hats, flip-flops, and glasses. Just as Ketut finished his tales, a macaque suddenly appeared on the stone path, attempting to snatch a white tourist's flip-flop. Fortunately, it didn't succeed. This mix of frustration and slight fear added a unique and amusing twist to our journey.
