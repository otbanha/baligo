The Indonesian government offers a 'Tax Refund for Tourists' program, allowing eligible foreign tourists to reclaim Value Added Tax (VAT, currently around 11-12%) on purchases. Below are the latest official regulations (from the Indonesian Tax Office pajak.go.id, applicable in 2026), especially relevant for shopping in Bali.

### 1. **Who is eligible for a tax refund?**

- Tourists holding foreign passports (non-Indonesian citizens or permanent residents).
- Stay in Indonesia **not exceeding 60 days** (from the day of entry).
- Airline crew members **are not eligible** for tax refunds.
- Goods must be **carried out as hand luggage** at departure (cannot be checked or mailed).
- Purchases must be made **within 1 month before departure**, and goods must be taken out of Indonesia within 1 month of purchase.

**Note**: Bali's 'Tourism Tax for Foreign Tourists' (IDR 150,000) is an entry tax, **non-refundable**, and completely separate from shopping tax refunds.

### 2. **What to pay attention to when shopping?**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776321690797-untitled_design_24_.webp)

- **Only** shops displaying the **'Tax Refund for Tourists'** or **'VAT Refund for Tourists'** logos are eligible (most large department stores, brand stores, and popular areas like Kuta, Seminyak, Ubud, etc., have them).
- **Always show your passport** when shopping and inform the staff that you want a VAT refund. The store will issue a **formal tax invoice with a payment receipt** (valid tax invoice).
- **Minimum amount**:
    - Minimum tax per invoice: Rp 50,000 (approximately Rp 500,000 in total purchase).
    - Total tax from all invoices must be at least Rp 500,000 to be eligible for a refund (invoices from different stores and dates **can be accumulated**).
- Keep all invoices, packaging, and goods intact (they will be checked upon departure).

**Tip**: Not all stores display the logo, so ask the staff or check the official website [www.pajak.go.id](http://www.pajak.go.id) for a list of participating stores.

### 3. **Refund Process (Most Commonly Used in Bali)**

1. **After Shopping**: Collect all tax invoices.
2. **On Departure Day (Ngurah Rai International Airport DPS, Bali)**:

    - Pre-apply via the official app on your phone or computer: Go to [**https://vatrefundapp.pajak.go.id/**](https://vatrefundapp.pajak.go.id/) to log in or register → Create a new tax refund application → Fill in your address, departure/arrival dates → Select the invoices for refund → Choose the refund method → Take photos of the items → Submit.
    - Head to the **Tax Refund Counter**: **Location**: International Terminal, **3rd floor** (near the departure area).
    - Present: Passport, boarding pass, all tax invoices, and the physical items (for inspection).
    - Once verified by customs/tax officers, you can receive your refund.

**Tip**: Allow at least 30-60 minutes for the process, ideally after check-in and before security. Arrive early during peak times.

### 4. **Refund Methods and Limits**

- **Cash Refund** (in Indonesian Rupiah IDR): If the total refund amount is **≤ Rp 5,000,000**, you can receive cash directly at the airport.
- **Over Rp 5,000,000**: Must be transferred via bank (provide overseas bank account details, account name, SWIFT Code, etc.), credited within 1 month; or opt to receive Rp 5,000,000 in cash and forfeit the remainder.
- Refund amount = VAT portion on the invoice (minus a small handling fee).

### 5. **Common Exclusions**

- Food, beverages, tobacco products, firearms, explosives, and prohibited items.
- Services (accommodation, restaurants, car rentals, tour guides, etc.) are not eligible for refunds.
- Stores without the tax refund logo, invoices without passport details, or items not physically exported are also excluded.

### 6. **Practical Tips (Must-Read for Bali Shoppers)**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776321764381-vat_refund_11zon.jpeg)

▲Tax Refund Counter at the Airport

- Popular tax refund shopping areas: Kuta, Seminyak, Discovery Mall, Bali Collection, etc.
- The airport tax refund counter is only in the **International Terminal**; domestic transfers cannot process refunds.
- Combine multiple small invoices to meet the threshold; groups can split refunds (each person is calculated separately).
- For the latest updates, refer to the official website **pajak.go.id**, as regulations may be adjusted.

Following these steps makes tax refunds surprisingly easy! If you've purchased designer goods, souvenirs, or keepsakes during your Bali trip, remember to keep your receipts and take photos of the items. You'll be able to effortlessly claim your tax refund when departing. Happy shopping and smooth tax refunds! 🛍️
