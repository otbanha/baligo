In our [previous article](/blog/2023-11-13-65504084fd897800011c51f2/), we discussed how to safely bring your drone to Indonesia/Bali. Today, we will delve deeper into the rules and regulations you need to follow when flying drones in Indonesia/Bali.

The most important aspect is undoubtedly **safety**. Drone flight safety concerns not only yourself but also others. To this end, there are several local regulations you must adhere to:

First, the flight altitude of the drone must not exceed 150 meters. This is not only to ensure the safety of airspace but also to avoid interference with other aircraft or natural ecosystems.

Second, do not fly drones within 15 kilometers of any major airport. This regulation ensures that drones do not interfere with normal air traffic, thereby preventing safety hazards.

Third, please do not fly drones at night. The visibility is lower at night, making such operations not only dangerous but also likely to cause disturbances to those around you.

Additionally, never fly drones in crowded or densely populated areas. This can not only cause panic but also increase the risk of flight accidents.

Lastly, never fly over temples in Bali. These places are extremely important to local residents, and respecting local culture and religious beliefs is basic courtesy.

These regulations aim to ensure everyone's safety while allowing drone flying to remain an enjoyable activity. Any disregard for these rules may result in fines or even criminal charges.

Drone flying is a fascinating experience, but we must remember that responsibility and respect are equally important behind this enjoyment. Adhering to these regulations is not just an obligation, but also our way of showing respect for the local community and environment. Let's ensure everyone can enjoy this wonderful activity while protecting this enchanting paradise.
