**Bottom Line**: Five key restrictions for flying drones in Bali—**flight altitude must not exceed 150 meters**, **no-fly zone within 15 km of airports**, no night flying, no flying over crowded areas, and **no flying over temples**. Violations can lead to fines or even criminal charges, so always check if your flight location is compliant before heading out.

In [the previous article](/en/blog/bali-drone-travel-guide/), we discussed how to safely bring your drone to Indonesia/Bali. Today, we'll dive into the regulations and rules you need to follow when flying a drone here.

The most important thing is **safety**. Drone flight safety isn't just about you—it's about others too. To that end, there are several local rules you must follow:

First, your drone's flight altitude must not exceed 150 meters. This ensures air safety and prevents interference with other aircraft or natural ecosystems.

Second, don't fly your drone within 15 kilometers of any major airport. This rule is in place to prevent drones from disrupting normal air traffic and causing safety hazards.

Third, avoid flying your drone at night. Visibility is low, making it dangerous and potentially disturbing to those around you.

Also, never fly your drone over crowds or densely populated areas. This can cause panic and increase the risk of accidents.

Finally, never fly over temples in Bali. These sites are deeply important to locals, and respecting local culture and religious beliefs is basic courtesy.

### Frequently Asked Questions

## What is the drone flight altitude limit in Bali?

It must not exceed 150 meters. Flying above this height may interfere with other aircraft and the natural ecosystem, and it is also the most common violation that gets caught.

## Can I fly a drone near the airport?

No. Drone flight is prohibited within 15 kilometers of all major airports to avoid interfering with normal air traffic.

## Can I fly a drone at night to capture night views?

It is not recommended. Nighttime flying is dangerous due to low visibility and can easily disturb nearby residents. It is explicitly prohibited by local regulations.

## Can I take aerial shots of temples?

No. Flying over temples in Bali is considered disrespectful to local religious beliefs. You must avoid all temple areas, as well as crowded or densely populated zones.

## What are the consequences of illegal flying?

You could face fines or even criminal charges. Safety and respect for local culture are the core reasons for following these regulations.
