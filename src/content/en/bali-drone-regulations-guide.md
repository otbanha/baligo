In [our previous article](/blog/bali-drone-travel-guide/), we discussed how to safely bring your drone to Indonesia/Bali. Today, we will delve into the relevant rules and regulations to follow when flying drones in Indonesia/Bali.

The most important aspect is undoubtedly **safety**. Drone flight safety concerns not only yourself but also others. To ensure this, there are several local regulations you must adhere to:

First, the drone's flight altitude must not exceed 150 meters. This is to ensure aerial safety and avoid interference with other aircraft or natural ecosystems.

Second, do not fly drones within 15 kilometers of any major airport. This rule ensures that drones do not interfere with normal air traffic, preventing potential safety hazards.

Third, please do not fly drones at night. Low visibility during nighttime makes such operations dangerous and potentially disruptive to those around you.

Additionally, never fly drones in crowded or densely populated areas. This can cause panic and increase the risk of flight accidents.

Lastly, do not fly over temples in Bali. These sites hold great significance for local residents, and respecting local culture and religious beliefs is fundamental courtesy.

These regulations are designed to ensure everyone's safety while also aiming to make drone flying an enjoyable activity. Ignoring these rules may result in fines or even criminal charges.

Drone flying is a fascinating experience, yet we must remember that responsibility and respect are equally crucial behind this enjoyment. Adhering to these regulations is not just an obligation but also a way to show our respect for the local community and environment. Let's ensure everyone can enjoy this wonderful activity while protecting this enchanting paradise.
