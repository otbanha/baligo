> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Lin Jinsheng](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/16zBggKw15/):

This time, I want to share our **8-day, 7-night motorbike🛵 adventure in Bali**. Exploring every corner of the island on two wheels was both thrilling and liberating for us. The biggest takeaway and tip for riding a motorbike in Bali is: **"Yield! Yield! Yield!"** Whether at narrow T-junctions or bustling crossroads, knowing when to yield is crucial. Riding requires boldness and attentiveness—follow the rhythm of the traffic ahead, speed up when necessary, slow down when needed, and don’t hesitate. Mastering this makes riding in Bali a joyful experience!

## Staying in Kuta

**Day 1**: After arriving at our hotel in the afternoon, we wasted no time renting a motorbike to kick off our free-spirited journey. We first roamed around Kuta and Seminyak to get familiar with the roads and traffic vibe. For dinner, we chose the renowned **Wahaha Restaurant**, famous for its flavorful and tender pork ribs, perfect for hearty enjoyment. We stayed at **The Vira Bali Boutique Hotel**, conveniently located near the airport in Kuta, ideal for travelers arriving on the first day. The hotel thoughtfully offers free shuttle services and complimentary coffee and snacks from 6 PM to 10 PM, perfect for unwinding after a day of travel.

**Day 2**: We started the morning with a leisurely **half-day beach trip to Nusa Dua**. The beach here is clean, the sea water clear, and the atmosphere quieter than Kuta and Canggu, making it ideal for strolling and relaxing. In the afternoon, we visited the new large shopping mall **ICON Bali**, which combines shopping, dining, and sightseeing, offering a cool retreat from the midday heat.

https://youtu.be/hdIKOX5wghU

By evening, we headed to the famous **Asoka Market night market** in Denpasar, Bali’s largest and liveliest night market. The stalls were abundant, offering affordable and delicious local snacks. We enjoyed roast suckling pig rice, meatball soup (Bakso), and Indonesian fried rice (Nasi Goreng), each for just 25K IDR (less than USD$2), satisfying both our hunger and taste buds. We returned to Kuta for the night, recharging for the next day’s adventures.

> Editor’s Note: **Asoka Market** is often grouped with the neighboring **Kereneng Night Market**, forming a super vibrant night market area with thousands of stalls. From authentic Babi Guling (roast pork) to colorful batik fabrics, handmade utensils, snacks, and various desserts, it’s all there. Visitors start pouring in from 4 PM, entering a bustling universe of food and culture.

## Accommodation in Ubud

**Day 3**: This day marked the beginning of the main highlights of our itinerary. We left our large luggage at the hotel in Kuta, taking only two 7 kg carry-ons secured tightly with cargo straps, along with a small backpack, traveling light. We rode our scooter straight to our accommodation in Ubud, settled our luggage, and officially started our journey. Our first stop was **Pura Ulun Danu Bratan**, a lakeside temple situated at an altitude of 1200 meters. This temple appears to float on the lake, surrounded by mountains and mist, creating a dreamy postcard-like scene.

![當天的水神寺，雲霧瀰漫](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_39c5eeaf14517308ba67848462ecddf9.jpg)

The temple was enveloped in clouds and mist that day.

Next, we visited the recently popular **Bali Handara Gate**, which has become a sensation on social media. The symmetrical and solemn Balinese gate, paired with the distant green mountains, truly makes it a 'must-visit spot for Instagrammers'. Returning to Ubud, we chose **Pison Ubud** for dinner. Their signature 'Ubud Dirty Duck' lived up to its reputation, with crispy fried duck paired with aromatic rice, offering a rich and satisfying flavor.

**Day 4**: The highlight of the day was the **Mount Batur Sunrise**. We woke up at 3 AM, quickly got ready, and set off on our scooter. The 45 km journey from Ubud to the volcano took about 70 minutes. This time, we opted not to hike but instead went directly to the **Sukawana Sunrise Spot** viewpoint. Here, we could easily witness the volcanic sunrise without the exhaustion of climbing, enjoying a more relaxed experience. As the sun rose behind the clouds, casting golden light over the volcano and lake, every bit of the early morning effort felt worth it.

![巴杜爾火山旁的觀景台，清晨 5 點一開門就陸續有遊客到訪，因為地點視野極佳，是拍攝日出的熱門場景。對於不想辛苦爬山的人來說，這裡是一個輕鬆欣賞日出的好選擇，汽車貨騎機車就能直接抵達，非常方便。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cae628e2c53a24c4bfb2eac74c4ccab.jpg)

The viewpoint near Mount Batur started welcoming visitors as early as 5 AM, being a popular spot for sunrise photography due to its excellent vantage point. For those who prefer not to hike, this is a convenient alternative, easily accessible by car or scooter.

After enjoying the sunrise, we returned to the hotel for a short nap before starting the second part of our day. At noon, we explored the **Ubud Market**, where my wife happily purchased an Indonesian-style dress for just 100K IDR, a bargain with great souvenir value. In the afternoon, we visited the **Tegalalang Rice Terrace**, Ubud's most iconic rice field landscape. The layered green terraces paired with swaying coconut trees make every shot postcard-worthy.

**Day Six**: Today's itinerary was more relaxed as we visited the **Pandawa Beach** area. Once a hidden gem, it has now been developed into a complete tourist destination. Upon entering, one must not miss a special experience: purchasing tickets to ride a shuttle car (IDR 30K per person) to the so-called "Blue Road" at **Tanah Barak**. The main attraction here is the towering cliffs on both sides, described by some as "Bali's version of Japan's Tateyama Kurobe Snow Walls," but with magnificent limestone instead. This scene is particularly popular among younger visitors, offering impressive photo opportunities.

![這處壯觀的岩壁近年成為許多網紅口耳相傳的打卡景點，因為角度與光影的變化，無論怎麼拍都顯得特別好看。要前往這裡，必須先從 Pandawa Beach 入口進入，再搭乘園區內的接駁車才能抵達。沿途景色也相當迷人，抵達後更能感受到岩壁與藍天大海交織出的震撼畫面，是拍照愛好者不能錯過的美拍地點。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4f8fe37b3a9f45664aad73384dc6a559.jpg)

To reach here, visitors must first enter from Pandawa Beach and then take a shuttle within the area. The scenery along the way is equally charming, and upon arrival, the interplay of cliffs, blue sky, and ocean creates a breathtaking view, making it a must-visit spot for photography enthusiasts.

### Final Thoughts

This **8-day, 7-night Bali couple's trip** saw us navigating the island on scooters, weaving through cities, rice fields, and coastlines, creating countless unique memories. While we didn't indulge in luxurious private car tours, we embraced the local pace of life, stopping freely and exploring at will. Along the way, we shared romantic moments and overcame small challenges together, making our journey all the more meaningful. This 'budget travel' approach, for us, truly embodies the essence of travel: freedom, authenticity, and our own little adventures.

I've chosen to travel by scooter in **Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, Laos, and Sri Lanka**. This method is cost-effective, convenient, and liberating, while also avoiding the hassle of being overcharged for transportation. Over the years, I've accumulated quite a bit of experience. For me, the biggest downside is having to ride under the scorching sun, which inevitably leads to tanning or sunburn. However, the benefits of controlling the pace of the journey and exploring freely far outweigh the drawbacks.

However, I'd like to offer a special reminder to friends planning to ride scooters in Bali: **While scooters are convenient, they also come with risks**. If an accident occurs in a foreign country, whether you hit someone or are hit, the aftermath can be quite troublesome, potentially leading to high medical expenses or related compensations, making the experience more costly than beneficial. These risks must be carefully assessed. Don't overlook the importance of safety and security just to save a bit on transportation costs.
