> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by fellow traveler [Lin Jinsheng](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/16zBggKw15/):

This time, I want to share our **8-day, 7-night motorbike🛵 adventure in Bali**. Exploring every corner of the island on two wheels was an exhilarating and liberating experience for us. The biggest takeaway and trick to riding a motorbike in Bali is: **"Yield! Yield! Yield!"** Whether at narrow T-junctions or busy crossroads, knowing when to yield is crucial. Riding requires boldness and attentiveness—follow the rhythm of the traffic ahead, speed up when needed, slow down when necessary, and don’t hesitate. This ensures a safe and smooth journey. Once you master this trick, riding in Bali becomes a joyful experience!

## Accommodation in Kuta

**Day 1**: After arriving at the hotel in the afternoon, we wasted no time and rented a motorbike to kick off our free-spirited adventure. We first roamed around Kuta and Seminyak to familiarize ourselves with the roads and traffic atmosphere. For dinner, we chose the locally renowned **Wahaha Restaurant**, known for its flavorful grilled pork ribs—tender, juicy, and perfect for hearty enjoyment. We stayed at **The Vira Bali Boutique Hotel**, conveniently located in Kuta and very close to the airport, making it ideal for travelers arriving on their first day. The hotel thoughtfully offers free shuttle services and complimentary coffee and snacks from 6 PM to 10 PM, allowing guests to unwind after a busy day.

**Day 2**: In the morning, we enjoyed a leisurely **half-day beach outing in Nusa Dua**. The beach here is clean, the water is crystal clear, and the atmosphere is more serene compared to Kuta and Canggu, making it perfect for strolling and relaxing. In the afternoon, we visited the newly opened **ICON Bali**, a large shopping mall combining retail, dining, and sightseeing features. The air-conditioned mall is also a great escape from the midday heat.

https://youtu.be/hdIKOX5wghU

In the evening, we made a special trip to the famous **Asoka Market night market** in Denpasar, Bali’s largest and liveliest night market. The stalls are abundant, offering affordable and delicious local snacks. We tried roast suckling pig rice, meatball soup (Bakso), and Indonesian fried rice (Nasi Goreng), each costing only 25K IDR (less than USD$2), satisfying both our hunger and taste buds. We returned to Kuta for the night, recharging for the next day’s adventures.

> Editor’s Note: **Asoka Market** is often grouped with the neighboring **Kereneng Night Market**, forming a bustling night market area with thousands of stalls. From authentic Babi Guling (roast pork) to vibrant batik fabrics, handmade utensils, snacks, and desserts, everything is available. Visitors start pouring in from 4 PM, creating a lively universe of food and culture.

## Accommodation in Ubud

**Day 3**: This day marked the beginning of the main highlights of our itinerary. We left our large luggage at the hotel in Kuta and carried two 7 kg hand luggage, securely fastened with cargo straps, along with a small backpack, traveling light. We rode our motorbike straight to our accommodation in Ubud, settled our luggage, and then officially started our journey. Our first stop was **Pura Ulun Danu Bratan**, located at an altitude of 1200 meters. This lakeside temple appears to float on the water, surrounded by mountains and mist, creating a dreamy postcard-like scene.

![當天的水神寺，雲霧瀰漫](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_39c5eeaf14517308ba67848462ecddf9.jpg)

The temple was enveloped in clouds and mist that day.

Next, we visited the recently popular **Bali Handara Gate** on social media. The symmetrical and solemn Balinese gate, paired with the distant green mountains, truly makes it a 'must-visit spot for one-stop photo ops'. Returning to Ubud, we chose **Pison Ubud** for dinner. The signature 'Ubud Dirty Duck' lived up to its reputation, with crispy fried duck paired with spiced rice, offering a rich and satisfying flavor.

**Day 4**: The highlight of the day was the **Mount Batur Sunrise**. We woke up at 3 AM, quickly got ready, and immediately set off on our motorbike. The journey from Ubud to the volcano is about 45 km and took us about 70 minutes. This time, we opted not to hike up the mountain but went directly to the **Sukawana Sunrise Spot** viewing platform. Here, we could easily watch the sunrise over the volcano, avoiding the exhaustion of climbing while enjoying a sense of freedom. As the sun rose from behind the clouds, bathing the volcano and lake in golden light, all the early morning effort felt worthwhile.

![巴杜爾火山旁的觀景台，清晨 5 點一開門就陸續有遊客到訪，因為地點視野極佳，是拍攝日出的熱門場景。對於不想辛苦爬山的人來說，這裡是一個輕鬆欣賞日出的好選擇，汽車貨騎機車就能直接抵達，非常方便。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cae628e2c53a24c4bfb2eac74c4ccab.jpg)

The viewing platform near Mount Batur started welcoming visitors as early as 5 AM, as it offers an excellent vantage point for sunrise photography. For those who prefer not to hike, this is a convenient spot to enjoy the sunrise, accessible by car or motorbike.

**Day Six**: Today's itinerary was more relaxed as we visited the **Pandawa Beach** area. Originally a hidden gem, it has now been developed into a complete tourist zone. Upon entering, one must not miss a special experience: purchasing a ticket to ride a shuttle car (USD$2 per person) to the so-called "Blue Road" of **Tanah Barak**. The most striking feature here is the towering rock walls on both sides, described by some as "Bali's version of Japan's Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route," but with magnificent limestone instead. This scene is particularly popular among young people, making it a powerful spot for photos and social media check-ins.

![這處壯觀的岩壁近年成為許多網紅口耳相傳的打卡景點，因為角度與光影的變化，無論怎麼拍都顯得特別好看。要前往這裡，必須先從 Pandawa Beach 入口進入，再搭乘園區內的接駁車才能抵達。沿途景色也相當迷人，抵達後更能感受到岩壁與藍天大海交織出的震撼畫面，是拍照愛好者不能錯過的美拍地點。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4f8fe37b3a9f45664aad73384dc6a559.jpg)

The scenery along the way is also quite charming, and upon arrival, one can truly feel the awe-inspiring blend of rock walls, blue skies, and the vast ocean, making it a must-visit spot for photography enthusiasts.

### Final Thoughts

Our **8-day, 7-night Bali couple's trip** was filled with unique memories as we navigated the island on a scooter, weaving through cities, rice fields, and coastlines. While we didn’t opt for luxurious private car tours, we embraced the local rhythm of life, stopping freely and exploring at our own pace. Along the way, we shared romantic moments and overcame small challenges together, making the journey feel even more meaningful. For us, this 'budget-friendly' approach was the essence of travel: freedom, authenticity, and our own little adventure.

I’ve traveled by scooter in **Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, Laos, and Sri Lanka**. It’s a budget-friendly, convenient, and liberating way to explore, while also avoiding the hassle of being overcharged for transportation. Over the years, I’ve gained plenty of experience, and while the biggest downside is enduring the scorching sun—often leading to tanning or sunburn—the benefits of controlling my travel pace and exploring freely far outweigh the drawbacks.
