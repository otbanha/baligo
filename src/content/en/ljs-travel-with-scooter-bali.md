> The following content is adapted from a travel diary shared by group member [Lin Jinsheng](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/16zBggKw15/):

I'd like to share our couple's **8-day, 7-night Bali scooter 🛵 self-guided tour**. Exploring every corner of the island on two wheels was an exhilarating and liberating experience for us. The biggest tip for riding a motorbike in Bali is: **"Yield! Yield! Yield!"** Whether at a narrow T-junction or a chaotic intersection, knowing how to give way is crucial. Ride with "boldness and caution," follow the rhythm of the traffic ahead—speed up when needed, slow down when needed, and don't hesitate. This way, the entire journey is both safe and enjoyable. Once you get the hang of it, riding in Bali becomes a truly joyful experience!

## Accommodation in Kuta

**Day 1**: After arriving at the hotel in the afternoon, we wasted no time and immediately rented a scooter to kick off our self-guided adventure. We first cruised around Kuta and Seminyak to get familiar with the roads and traffic vibe. For dinner, we chose the locally famous **Wahaha Restaurant**, where their grilled pork ribs are incredibly flavorful—tender, juicy, and perfect for hearty eating. That night, we stayed at **The Vira Bali Boutique Hotel**, located in Kuta and very close to the airport, making it convenient for first-day arrivals. The hotel also offers a complimentary shuttle service and free coffee and snacks from 6 PM to 10 PM, perfect for relaxing after a day of travel.

**Day 2**: In the morning, we planned a leisurely **half-day beach trip to Nusa Dua**. The sand here is clean, the water crystal clear, and the atmosphere is much quieter than Kuta or Canggu—perfect for strolling and zoning out. In the afternoon, we visited the newest large shopping mall, **ICON Bali**, which combines shopping, dining, and sightseeing. The air-conditioned environment is also a great escape from the midday heat.

https://youtu.be/hdIKOX5wghU

By evening, we made a special trip to Denpasar's famous **Asoka Market night market**, the largest and liveliest in Bali. The stalls are abundant, with affordable and delicious local snacks. We ordered babi guling (suckling pig rice), bakso (meatball soup), and a serving of nasi goreng (Indonesian fried rice), each costing just 25K IDR (less than USD$1)—filling and satisfying. We returned to Kuta for the night, recharging for the next day's adventure.

> Editor's note: **Asoka Market** is often considered together with the adjacent **Kereneng Night Market**, forming a massive, bustling night market area with thousands of stalls. From authentic babi guling (roast pork) to colorful batik fabrics, handmade tableware, snacks, and various desserts, it has it all. Visitors start pouring in from 4 PM, stepping into a vibrant microcosm of food and culture.

## Ubud Accommodation

**Day 3**: This day marked the highlight of our trip. We left our large luggage at the hotel in Kuta, packed two 7 kg carry-ons secured with cargo straps, and carried a small backpack for a light journey. Hopping on a scooter, we headed straight to our accommodation in Ubud. After dropping off our bags, we officially kicked off the itinerary. Our first stop was **Pura Ulun Danu Bratan**, perched at an altitude of 1,200 meters. This lakeside water temple seemed to float on the lake, surrounded by mountains and mist, creating a dreamy scene straight out of a postcard.

**Day 4**: The highlight of this day was the **Mount Batur sunrise**. We woke up at 3 AM, quickly got ready, and set off on our scooter. The ride from Ubud to the volcano was about 45 km, taking roughly 70 minutes. Instead of a strenuous hike, we headed straight to the **Sukawana Sunrise Spot** viewpoint. Here, we could easily watch the sunrise over the volcano without the exhaustion of climbing, enjoying a sense of freedom. As the sun rose from behind the clouds, painting the volcano and lake in golden hues, every bit of the early wake-up felt worth it.

The sunset at Uluwatu Temple is one of the most unforgettable sights in Bali for many travelers. The temple is built into the cliffside, which drops straight into the Indian Ocean. As the sun slowly sinks below the horizon, the entire sky turns golden and orange-red, reflecting off the dramatic coastline—an atmosphere that is both romantic and sacred. Paired with the famous Kecak dance performance, it offers a powerful blend of Bali's unique culture and natural beauty.

**Day Six**: Our itinerary today was more relaxed, starting with a visit to **Pandawa Beach area**. Once a hidden secret beach, it's now a fully developed tourist zone. Upon entering, there's a unique experience not to be missed: you must buy a ticket for a shuttle car (30K IDR per person) to reach **Tanah Barak**, known as the 'Blue Road.' The main draw here is the towering limestone cliffs on both sides, which some describe as 'Bali's version of the Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route snow walls in Japan,' but made of stunning limestone instead. This spot is very popular among young people, offering impressive photo opportunities.

![這處壯觀的岩壁近年成為許多網紅口耳相傳的打卡景點，因為角度與光影的變化，無論怎麼拍都顯得特別好看。要前往這裡，必須先從 Pandawa Beach 入口進入，再搭乘園區內的接駁車才能抵達。沿途景色也相當迷人，抵達後更能感受到岩壁與藍天大海交織出的震撼畫面，是拍照愛好者不能錯過的美拍地點。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4f8fe37b3a9f45664aad73384dc6a559.jpg)

To get there, you must enter through the Pandawa Beach entrance and then take the park's shuttle. The scenery along the way is also captivating, and upon arrival, you'll be struck by the powerful image of cliffs merging with the blue sky and sea—a must-visit for photography enthusiasts.
