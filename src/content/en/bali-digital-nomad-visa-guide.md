## **Does Bali Have a Remote Worker Visa?**

Although Indonesian government officials have long been studying the possibility of a remote worker visa, they finally introduced the 'Second Home' visa last year.

While the 'Second Home' visa is valid for 5 or 10 years, its stringent requirements make it somewhat inaccessible for most remote workers. The most exaggerated condition is that you must have at least 2 billion Indonesian Rupiah (approximately USD$130,000) in an Indonesian bank account under your name as proof of funds, which cannot be touched. This visa also does not allow you to legally work for Indonesian companies or provide commercial services locally.

## The "Golden Visa" for Investors

On September 3rd, the Indonesian government launched the "Golden Visa" program aimed at attracting foreign investment and stimulating the local economy. This program requires a minimum investment of $2.5 million for a minimum period of 5 years. Applicants can obtain residency rights ranging from 5 to 10 years. Individual investors who invest $2.5 million and establish a company will receive a 5-year residency, while those who increase their investment to $5 million can extend their residency to 10 years.

For corporate applications, the minimum investment requirement is $25 million, with directors and designated personnel eligible for a 5-year residency. If the corporation doubles its investment, the residency period can also be extended to 10 years.

## **Confirming Internet Connection**

If you enjoy working from home, a reliable internet connection is crucial. Some areas in Bali may have spotty network reception, so it's important to ensure stable Wi-Fi when choosing accommodations. You can inquire in advance with hotels or guesthouses about internet speed and reliability.

## **Practical Life Tips**

You've arranged your work, accommodations, and visa. Now, let's talk about practical matters—how to manage daily life.

> **Transportation:** Rent a scooter: Renting a scooter in Bali is relatively cheap and straightforward (but remember, 'you must have an international driver's license'). You'll definitely see many people on the road without helmets, but don't forget to wear yours. Police are cracking down on helmetless riding, but more importantly, Bali's roads can be dangerous, and wearing a helmet is one way to protect yourself.

> **Using Ride-Hailing Apps like Grab and Gojek:** These apps are convenient and easy to use for booking rides, motorcycle taxis, and ordering food delivery. However, due to pressure from local taxi companies, these services may not be available at certain island attractions or remote areas.

> **Taxis:** Some taxi drivers in Bali may refuse to use the meter or claim it's 'broken' to negotiate higher fares. Whenever possible, opt for Blue Bird taxis, as their drivers always use the meter and are the most reliable.

> **Staying Connected:** Purchase a local SIM card for the most affordable internet plans and reliable coverage. Telkomsel and XL are the two best mobile network providers in Bali. Additionally, investing in a VPN service is worthwhile for connecting to public Wi-Fi networks and bypassing Indonesia's internet censorship, which blocks access to certain websites.

> **Health and Safety:** While Bali has many excellent medical facilities, it's crucial to ensure you have adequate travel health insurance that covers overseas treatment in emergencies. It's also important to note down emergency contact numbers and reputable hospitals in Bali.

> **Learning the Language:** Although many Indonesians in Bali speak English well, we recommend learning some basic Indonesian phrases. This will make it easier to make friends, navigate around, and enjoy meals at local restaurants frequented by Indonesians, which are often affordable and delicious. We mostly speak Indonesian in Bali!

> **Respecting Local Culture and Laws:** There are numerous stories of foreigners getting into trouble in Bali for disrespecting local culture or breaking laws. When in someone else's country, always respect their cultural norms and legal regulations.
