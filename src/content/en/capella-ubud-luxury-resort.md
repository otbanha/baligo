Just 12 kilometers from Ubud's center, about a 20-minute drive, lies a luxury resort hidden deep in the jungle—Capella Ubud. With its seamless integration of nature and exceptional service, this resort has drawn countless travelers seeking to immerse themselves in the natural world. The official website describes it as the ideal destination for those looking to experience Bali's pristine natural beauty. It's both a sanctuary for relaxation and a haven for creative inspiration.

## A Stunning Creation by Design Master Bill Bensley

Capella Ubud's design is inspired by world-renowned designer Bill Bensley, who weaves together diverse historical backgrounds and tales of exploration to infuse the resort with a unique cultural atmosphere. Bensley draws inspiration from the legendary exploits of Venetian explorer Marco Polo and 19th-century Danish explorer Mads Johansen Lange, particularly Lange's role as a merchant and peacemaker in Bali.

Bensley incorporates European colonial style into the design, but avoids an overly opulent approach, instead using understated details to create an elegant and mysterious ambiance. This allows Capella Ubud to strike a perfect balance between grandeur and simplicity.

## A Unique 'Tent Village' Accommodation Experience

A standout feature of Capella Ubud is its 'tent village' concept. The resort boasts 22 luxury tents, each with a unique theme named after various 19th-century professions, including baker, toy maker, carpenter, and photographer. Every tent showcases a different style, transporting guests back in time and immersing them in the stories of past trades for an unprecedented lodging experience.

These tents are exquisitely designed, offering not only luxurious comfort but also the thrill of stepping into a historical scene, making each stay a unique adventure.
