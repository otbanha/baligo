If your Bali trip is all about shopping, dining, and nightlife, and you need an affordable yet comfortable base, Seminyak Square Hotel and Villas makes perfect sense. This three-star hotel at Jl. Kayu Aya 100 is literally a hotel built above a mall—occupying the second floor and above of Seminyak Square shopping center. Step out of the elevator onto Eat Street, and downstairs you'll find a supermarket, money changer, and restaurants. This location is its biggest selling point, and the price is notably more affordable than other hotels in the same area.

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

The location is the hotel's most undeniable advantage, no question about it.

The hotel sits right above Seminyak Square shopping mall, which houses Mega Mart supermarket, a money changer, pharmacy, clothing stores, and several restaurants—so you don't need to call a Grab for any shopping or errands. Just a few steps outside, you're on Eat Street (Kayu Aya), a dense strip of restaurants, with famous spots like La Favela and Ku De Ta all within walking distance.

Seminyak Beach is about a five to ten-minute walk away, which is quite close for this part of Seminyak. Petitenget Beach is also within walking distance, roughly 400 meters away. The airport drive takes about 25 to 30 minutes. Overall, this area is the very heart of Seminyak's shopping and dining scene—staying here puts the best of Seminyak right at your doorstep.

***

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

The hotel itself is a three-star property, so the facilities aren't luxurious, but there are a few highlights worth mentioning.

The most praised feature among guests is the **rooftop pool**. It's a good-sized pool surrounded by wooden decking, with white loungers and umbrellas, offering views over Seminyak town—especially atmospheric at dusk. There's a pool bar where you can grab drinks and unwind.

For dining, the hotel has two restaurants: Aviary Restaurant serves international cuisine all day, while Braga Cafe (also known as Spicery) mainly offers breakfast, with a mix of Western and Indonesian dishes. Guests generally find the breakfast buffet generous and varied. There's also Essential SPA, offering deep tissue massage, hot stone massage, aromatherapy, and body treatments, with facilities including a thermal mineral bath.

Other convenient facilities include a 24-hour front desk, complimentary valet parking, luggage storage, an ATM, currency exchange services, and airport transfers (surcharge applies) as well as a shuttle service within the Seminyak area.

## 🛏️ Room Types

There are 41 rooms in total, with room types including Standard Room, Superior Room, Deluxe Room, Junior Suite, as well as one-bedroom and two-bedroom apartment-style units.

The overall decor is minimalist and modern, with wooden furniture paired with white walls—not particularly Balinese in style, but clean and tidy. Guests consistently praise the spaciousness of the rooms; Junior Suites and above are especially roomy, and the two-bedroom apartments feature a separate living room, dining area, and small kitchen, making them ideal for families or groups.

▲ Villa-style accommodation

A unique feature of this hotel's Villa rooms is that they are separate from the hotel located above the Seminyak shopping mall.

Each one-bedroom and two-bedroom Villa comes with its own private pool. Even the one-bedroom villas are quite spacious, while the two-bedroom ones are even larger, featuring boutique hotel-grade bedding such as Egyptian cotton sheets, a pillow menu, and plush mattresses. The layout includes a separate living room, dining area, and small kitchen, equipped with a refrigerator, microwave, and stovetop—perfect for long stays or groups who want to cook. The bathrooms include bathrobes, slippers, and toiletries, offering a noticeably higher quality than standard business-class rooms.

In short, in the same prime Seminyak city center location, these Villa rooms let you enjoy the resort feel of a private pool without having to retreat to the countryside—a combination that's quite rare among accommodation options in Seminyak.

## 👥 Who It's For

Best suited for **budget-conscious independent travelers**, especially those visiting Bali for the first time with an itinerary focused on Seminyak. The location is a major advantage, with prices that are clearly competitive compared to other properties in the same area. You can explore all of Seminyak without spending extra on transportation. For family trips, the two-bedroom apartment is a sensible choice—plenty of space and a kitchen for cooking your own meals. If you care about design or resort ambiance, this place might not meet your expectations, but if you're just looking for a clean, well-located base, it offers great value for money.

***

## 💬 Real Guest Reviews

A returning guest mentioned that the location is right at the entrance of Eat Street, making it possible to walk to everything you need. They keep coming back because the apartment is incredibly spacious, especially the two-bedroom unit, which offers unbeatable value for this price point and area.

Another guest noted that this was their third stay at the hotel. The Mega Mart supermarket downstairs is very convenient, and ordering a Grab to the airport or taking a Gojek to Canggu or Legian is a breeze. The Junior Suite comes with a small kitchen for cooking, which is especially useful for long-stay travelers.

## ⚠️ Honest Drawbacks

A few things to keep in mind. First, the hotel is right next to a busy mall and street, so rooms facing the road may have traffic and nighttime music noise. It's best to choose a higher floor or a room facing away from the street. Second, multiple guests have mentioned that the hot water takes a while to come out—you'll need to wait a bit.
