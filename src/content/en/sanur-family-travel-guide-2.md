## Family Travel Tips

### 1. Climate and Clothing

Located in the southeastern part of Bali, Sanur enjoys warm and comfortable weather. During the summer months (June to August), it’s the dry season with plenty of sunshine and cool sea breezes. However, during winter breaks, Bali is in the rainy season, and short showers are common. Here’s what to prepare for your kids:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hats and sunglasses
- High SPF sunscreen (especially for beach activities)
- Comfortable sandals or flip-flops (ideal for beach outings)
- Rain gear/raincoat (to handle light rain)
- Insect repellent (mosquitoes are more active during the rainy season)
  If you plan to visit nearby attractions (like Mount Batur or Ubud), bring a light jacket as temperatures can vary significantly between morning and evening.

***

### 2. Health and Safety

- **Sun Protection and Mosquito Repellent**: The beach areas in Sanur have strong sunlight, so it’s recommended to reapply sunscreen regularly. Although Sanur has fewer mosquitoes, it’s still advisable to carry insect repellent.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly; it’s recommended to purchase bottled water or use boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Bring common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness medicine, and band-aids. For reference: [What Essential Medicines/Products Should You Bring for a Bali Trip? How to Buy Insect Repellent/Sunscreen?](/blog/bali-travel-medicine-checklist/) Sanur’s city center is home to Bali’s largest and newest international hospital.

***

### 3. Transportation and Getting Around

Sanur offers relatively convenient transportation. It’s recommended to book reputable private car services and prepare safety seats for younger children. The streets in Sanur are flat, making it suitable for walking or cycling, and many hotels offer free bike rentals. If traveling with young children, choose attractions closer to your accommodation to avoid long car rides.

***

### 4. Dining Options

Sanur boasts a diverse range of dining options, from local cuisine to international dishes. Recommendations:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned foods.
- Many restaurants offer kids’ menus, and international chain restaurants are also a safe bet for ensuring child-friendly flavors.

***

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

Although Sanur is more modernized, it still retains Bali’s traditional culture. Teach children to respect local customs, such as:

- When entering temples, wear a sarong and maintain silence.
- Do not touch religious items or statues casually.

## Top 10 Family-Friendly Activities in Sanur

### **1. The Charm of Sanur Beach**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5e82dc6665e39c659dd66b7a031d7c99.jpg)

Sanur Beach boasts calm, shallow waters, making it perfect for children to play and splash around. Parents can rest assured letting their kids play here, as the waves are gentle and the beach is clean. Additionally, the beachside pathway is ideal for family strolls, cycling, or jogging, with numerous restaurants and cafes along the way for breaks.

### 2. Water Activities

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_96eaf38d1524e5a6a49dd46a55480bf7.png)

In Sanur, the top activity is water sports! Canoeing and Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP) are two of the most popular water activities here. You can enjoy the serene ocean atmosphere on calm waters or challenge your balance and strength. This beautiful sea will bring you both fun and challenges, ensuring a delightful time.

###

### 3. Cycling/Picnicking on the Beach Path

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0d4b00a6a1290b4b1dcef2ff8f035538.jpg)

When talking about Sanur, one cannot miss the over 4-kilometer-long beach path! This lengthy coastal path is one of Sanur's most distinctive features. You can stroll along the shops, jog, or rent bicycles to leisurely ride with your kids on the flat path. The beach on one side of the path, with its length and variety, guarantees you'll find a spot to enjoy privately—complete with large trees and lovely shade, and no smoking tourists nearby! You can lay down a mat and relax in this exclusive area.

### Prime Plaza Suites Sanur

Prime Plaza Suites Sanur boasts the highly-rated Camp Splash Kids Club, providing children with a fun and safe play area. This indoor-outdoor facility includes a children's pool, a shallow pool, and a 30-meter-long water slide for endless fun.

In addition to the water features, Camp Splash offers a mini soccer field, basketball court, and an outdoor playground with swings, slides, and seesaws. The indoor area features video games, board games, books, movies, and PlayStation to cater to children of different ages.

Camp Splash Kids Club is open daily from 9 AM to 6 PM and is free for all guests staying at Prime Plaza Suites Sanur. However, the club is not a traditional daycare center. ## Sanur Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Is Sanur suitable for traveling with kids?**

Sanur is a family-friendly destination with calm beaches, kid-friendly hotels, and a variety of outdoor activities such as cycling, sand play, and cultural experiences.

**What other activities are suitable for kids in Sanur?**

We’ve compiled dozens of kid-friendly activities in Sanur, including snorkeling, visits to turtle conservation centers, and hands-on craft workshops. Click [here](/blog/sanur-guide/) for detailed information.

**Do accommodations in Sanur have water slides?**

Some family resorts offer water slides, perfect for kids who love water play. We've selected a few family-friendly hotels with water slides—click [here](/blog/bali-family-resorts-water-slides/) to learn more.

**What's the weather like in Sanur? What should I pack?**

Sanur has a warm and humid climate year-round, with average daytime temperatures of 27-32°C. It's recommended to pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sun protection, hats, and rain gear to handle possible afternoon thunderstorms.

**Is the food in Sanur safe for children?**

Sanur offers a variety of restaurants serving international and local cuisine, with some providing kids' menus. It's advisable to choose well-reviewed restaurants with good hygiene standards, drink bottled water, and avoid raw or undercooked foods.

**Is transportation in Sanur convenient? Is it suitable for traveling with kids?**

Sanur has convenient transportation options, primarily relying on taxis, ride-hailing apps (like Grab), and private car services. For families with kids, private car services are recommended for a more comfortable experience, and child safety seats can be requested in advance.

**Are there medical facilities in Sanur? What if my child gets sick?**

Sanur has several clinics and pharmacies for minor health issues. For more advanced medical care, the newly opened Bali International Hospital, located in Sanur, is expected to be fully operational by the end of 2025. Situated within the Sanur Special Economic Zone, a 41.26-hectare integrated health and tourism area, the hospital aims to provide world-class medical services and wellness experiences.

**Do attractions in Sanur require entrance fees? Are there discounts for children?**

Most public beaches in Sanur are free to access, but some cultural attractions or activities may require tickets. Children under 6 usually enter for free, while those aged 6-12 may enjoy discounted rates. It's recommended to confirm in advance.

**Is Sanur stroller-friendly?**

Sanur's streets and coastal walkways are relatively flat, making them suitable for strollers, especially along the Sanur Beach Promenade, where it's easy to push strollers, perfect for family strolls and leisurely sightseeing.

**Must-visit attractions around Sanur**

From Sanur, a 30-minute speedboat ride will take you to two major islands off Bali's east coast. Check out the [Nusa Penida Guide](/blog/nusa-penida-guide/) / [Nusa Lembongan & Ceningan Guide](/blog/nusa-lembongan-ceningan-guide/).
