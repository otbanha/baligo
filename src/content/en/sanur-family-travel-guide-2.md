## Family Travel Tips

### 1. Climate and Clothing

Located in the southeastern part of Bali, Sanur enjoys warm and comfortable weather. During the summer months (June to August), it’s the dry season with plenty of sunshine and cool sea breezes. However, during winter breaks, Bali is in the rainy season, and short showers are common. Recommended items for kids include:

- Lightweight, breathable clothing
- Sun hats and sunglasses
- High-SPF sunscreen (especially for beach activities)
- Comfortable sandals or flip-flops (ideal for beach activities)
- Rain gear/raincoat (to stay dry during light showers)
- Insect repellent (more mosquitoes during the rainy season)
   If planning to visit nearby attractions (like Mount Batur or Ubud), prepare a light jacket as temperatures can vary between morning and evening.

***

### 2. Health and Safety

- **Sun Protection and Mosquito Repellent**: The beach area in Sanur has strong sunlight, so it's recommended to reapply sunscreen regularly. Although Sanur has fewer mosquitoes, it's still advisable to prepare insect repellent.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid drinking tap water directly; it's better to purchase bottled water or use boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Bring common medications such as fever reducers, anti-diarrheal pills, motion sickness medicine, and band-aids. For reference: [What Essential Medications/Supplies Should You Bring for a Trip to Bali, Indonesia? How to Buy Insect Repellent/Sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/) Sanur city has Bali's largest and newest international hospital.

***

### 3. Transportation and Getting Around

Sanur's transportation is relatively convenient. It's recommended to book reputable car rental services and prepare safety seats for younger children. The roads in Sanur city are flat, making it suitable for walking or cycling, and many hotels offer free bicycle rentals. If your children are young, choose attractions closer to your accommodation to avoid long car rides.

### 4. Dining Options

Sanur offers a diverse range of dining options, from local cuisine to international dishes. Recommendations:

- Choose restaurants with good hygiene standards.
- Avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned foods.
- Many restaurants offer children's menus, and you can also opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the flavors suit your child.

***

### 5. Cultural Etiquette

Although Sanur is more modernized, it still retains Bali's traditional culture. Teach your children to respect local customs, such as:

- Wearing a sarong and staying quiet when entering temples.
- Not touching religious items or statues casually.

## Sanur Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Is Sanur suitable for traveling with children?**

Sanur is a family-friendly destination with calm beaches, child-friendly hotels, and a variety of outdoor activities such as cycling, sand play, and cultural experiences.

**What other activities are suitable for children in Sanur?**

We’ve compiled dozens of activities in Sanur that are perfect for kids, including snorkeling, visits to turtle conservation centers, and hands-on craft workshops. Click [here](/blog/2024-02-11-65bf2f75fd89780001dbf162/) for detailed information.

**Do accommodations in Sanur have water slides?**

Some family resorts offer water slide facilities, perfect for kids who love water play. We’ve selected a few family-friendly hotels with water slides—click [here](/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/) to learn more.

**What's the weather like in Sanur? What should I pack?**

Sanur has a warm and humid climate year-round, with average daytime temperatures around 27-32°C. It's recommended to bring lightweight, breathable clothing, sun protection, hats, and rain gear to prepare for possible afternoon thunderstorms.

**Is the food in Sanur safe for children?**

Sanur offers many restaurants serving both international cuisine and local dishes, with some providing children's menus. It's advisable to choose well-reviewed restaurants with good hygiene standards, drink bottled water, and avoid raw or undercooked foods.

**Is transportation convenient in Sanur? Is it suitable for traveling with children?**

Transportation in Sanur is convenient, primarily relying on taxis, ride-hailing services (like Grab), and private car hires. For families with children, private car hires are recommended for a more comfortable travel experience, and child safety seats can be requested in advance.

**Are there medical facilities in Sanur? What if my child gets sick?**

Sanur has several clinics and pharmacies suitable for minor ailments. For more comprehensive medical services, the newly opened Bali International Hospital in Sanur is an option, expected to be fully operational by the end of 2025. Located within the Sanur Special Economic Zone, this hospital aims to provide world-class medical services and wellness experiences.

**Do attractions in Sanur require tickets? Are there discounts for children?**

Most public beaches in Sanur are free to access, but some cultural sites or activities may require tickets. Typically, children under 6 can enter for free, and children aged 6-12 may enjoy discounted rates. It's recommended to confirm in advance.

**Is Sanur stroller-friendly?**

Sanur's streets and coastal walkways are relatively flat, making them suitable for strollers, especially along the Sanur Beach Promenade, where you can easily push a stroller, perfect for family strolls and leisurely sightseeing.

**Must-Visit Attractions Around Sanur**
