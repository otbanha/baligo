Bali is a dream vacation destination for many travelers, combining tropical charm with unique culture, attracting countless visitors. However, choosing the right time to travel is crucial for both the quality of your trip and budget control.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3ca684e28f6adcd444bf1c7f57779da7.png)

Avoiding Bali's peak tourist seasons not only saves you money on flights and accommodations but also allows for a more peaceful travel experience—definitely a wise move. So, when exactly are Bali's peak seasons? Generally, the dry season from April to September, when rainfall is less likely, is the best time to visit Bali and thus becomes the peak tourist season. Although the rainy season may not offer ideal weather, it allows you to experience a different side of Bali while enjoying discounted flights and accommodations.

## Bali's Five Peak Tourist Seasons

In addition to seasonal distinctions, the following five special periods also attract large numbers of tourists to Bali:

### 【First Place】Eid al-Fitr

- **Eid al-Fitr Holiday in Indonesia (dates vary each year)**: Eid al-Fitr is an important Muslim holiday in Indonesia, and many Indonesians travel to Bali during this period, causing a surge in crowds. This is Bali's number one peak season—**definitely avoid this time and steer clear of the crowds**!
- The national holiday lasts about ten days. Eid al-Fitr is Indonesia's most significant holiday, celebrating the end of Ramadan for Muslims, but it’s also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, akin to Chinese New Year.
- In 2026, Bali is expected to see an influx of locals starting around March 13, with crowds lasting for about two weeks. This is the first of Bali's five peak seasons—a period we recommend avoiding!

### 【Second Place】Christmas and New Year Holidays

**Christmas-New Year Holiday (December 25 to January 1)**: Christmas and New Year are long holidays in Europe, America, Australia, and New Zealand, and also peak travel seasons in many Asian countries. The New Year holiday typically attracts global tourists, as it marks a major holiday period in most Western countries. Indonesians also try to take time off during this period. Therefore, the Christmas and New Year holidays rank second in terms of crowds and traffic. Here’s a short video showing the situation on the highway to the airport on December 29, 2023: [2023/12/29 Highway to Airport](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide/permalink/3579975912271532/). Hotel and flight prices in Bali usually surge during this period.

### **Third Place: Around Chinese Lunar New Year**

Chinese Lunar New Year is one of the most important holidays in Asia, and many Asian tourists travel to Bali during this time. Besides China and Taiwan, countries like Hong Kong, Malaysia, Singapore, South Korea, and Vietnam also have holidays ranging from two to five days. The Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia observe a one-day holiday on the first day of the Lunar New Year, and **in 2026, Indonesia will have a four-day holiday**. The Lunar New Year holiday ranks third in attracting large crowds to tourist spots.

### Fourth Place: Summer Vacation

**July to September**: During the summer vacation in the Northern Hemisphere, many families and students choose to vacation in Bali, leading to increased flight and hotel prices. Many families and students take advantage of this time to visit Bali. Flight and accommodation prices during summer vacation are never cheap!

### Fifth Place: Term Break

**Australian School Holidays and Indonesian International School Holidays (Term Break, usually a week between September 20 and October 10)**: Term breaks for Australian and Indonesian international schools also bring a wave of tourists, but the number of visitors is less than a third compared to the top four periods. I’ve taken advantage of this holiday to visit Bali several times!

## Advantages of Avoiding Peak Season

- **Save Money**: Prices for flights, accommodations, and activities are usually higher during peak season. Avoiding peak season can save you a significant amount of money.
- **More Peaceful**: Bali is crowded during peak season, and traffic jams can make you question life. Avoiding peak season allows you to enjoy a less congested travel experience.
- **More Options**: Hotels and attractions may be fully booked during peak season. Avoiding peak season gives you more choices.
