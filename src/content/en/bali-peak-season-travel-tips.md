Bali is a dream vacation destination for many travelers, combining tropical charm with unique culture, attracting countless visitors. However, choosing the right time to travel is crucial for both the quality of the trip and budget control.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3ca684e28f6adcd444bf1c7f57779da7.png)

Avoiding Bali's peak tourist seasons not only saves on budgets for flights and accommodations but also allows for a more peaceful travel experience—definitely a wise move. So, when exactly are Bali's peak seasons? Generally, the dry season with lower rainfall, from April to September, is the best time to visit Bali, making it the peak tourist season. Although the rainy season might not be ideal weather-wise, it offers a different side of Bali and discounted flights and accommodations.

## Bali's Five Peak Tourist Seasons

Apart from distinguishing peak and off-peak seasons by weather, the following five special periods also attract a surge of tourists to Bali:

### 【First Place】Eid al-Fitr

- **Eid al-Fitr Holiday in Indonesia (dates vary each year)**: Eid al-Fitr is a significant Muslim holiday in Indonesia, and many Indonesians travel to Bali during this period, causing a spike in crowds. This is the number one peak season in Bali—**definitely avoid this time to steer clear of the crowds**!
- The national holiday lasts about ten days. Eid al-Fitr is Indonesia's most important holiday, celebrating the end of Ramadan, but it's also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, akin to Chinese New Year.
- In 2026, Bali is expected to see an influx of locals starting March 13, with crowds lasting about two weeks. This is the first of Bali's five peak seasons—a holiday you should avoid visiting Bali!

### 【Second Place】Christmas and New Year Holidays

**Christmas-New Year Holiday (December 25 to January 1)**: Christmas and New Year are major holidays in Europe, America, Australia, and New Zealand, as well as peak travel seasons in many Asian countries. The New Year holiday typically attracts tourists from around the globe, as it marks a long break in most Western countries. Indonesians also try to take time off during this period. As a result, the Christmas and New Year holidays rank second in terms of crowds and traffic. Here’s a short video showing the situation on the highway to the airport on December 29, 2023: __VID0__. Hotel and flight prices in Bali usually surge during this time.

### **Third Place: Around Chinese Lunar New Year**

Chinese Lunar New Year is one of the most important holidays in Asia, and many Asian tourists visit Bali during this period. Besides China and Taiwan, countries like Hong Kong, Malaysia, Singapore, South Korea, and Vietnam also have holidays ranging from two to five days. The Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia observe a one-day holiday on the first day of the Lunar New Year, and **in 2026, Indonesia will have a four-day holiday**. The Lunar New Year holiday ranks third in attracting large crowds to tourist destinations.

### Fourth Place: Summer Vacation

**July to September**: During the summer vacation in the Northern Hemisphere, many families and students choose to vacation in Bali, driving up flight and hotel prices. Summer vacation has never been cheap for flights and accommodations!

### Fifth Place: Term Break

**Australian School Holidays and Indonesian International School Holidays (Term Break, usually a week between September 20 and October 10)**: Term breaks in Australian and Indonesian international schools also bring a wave of tourists, but the number of visitors is less than a third of the fourth-place peak. I’ve taken advantage of this holiday to visit Bali several times!

## Advantages of Avoiding Peak Season

- **Save Money**: Flight tickets, accommodations, and activities are usually more expensive during peak season. Avoiding peak season can save you a lot of expenses.
- **More Peaceful**: Bali is crowded during peak season, and traffic jams can make you question life. Avoiding peak season allows you to enjoy a less congested travel experience.
- **More Options**: Hotels and attractions may be fully booked during peak season. Avoiding peak season gives you more choices.
