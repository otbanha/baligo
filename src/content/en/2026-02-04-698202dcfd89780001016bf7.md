## ⛩️ 廟會與文化體驗

For a more traditional religious atmosphere, visit the Chinese temples (Klenteng) on the island:

- **Vihara Dharmayana (Kuta)**: One of the oldest Buddhist temples on the island, bustling with incense during the New Year, adorned with red lanterns, and often hosting祈福 ceremonies and folk performances.
- **Vihara Satya Dharma Temple (Benoa Harbour)**: An important faith center for the local Chinese community, hosting major festivals such as Lunar New Year, Ghost Festival, and Guanyin's Birthday.

## 🗓️ Important Dates to Remember

- **February 16th (Monday)**: Lunar New Year's Eve, declared a public holiday (Cuti Bersama) by the Indonesian government, with many hotels hosting reunion dinners.
- **February 17th (Tuesday)**: Lunar New Year's Day, a public holiday, with the most concentrated mall activities.
- **March 3rd**: Lantern Festival, with lantern events held in some areas.

**💡 Friendly Reminder:** During the Lunar New Year period, traffic in **Bali**'s main tourist areas (such as Kuta and Canggu) may be more congested than usual. It is recommended to make reservations for popular restaurants' New Year's Eve dinners well in advance.
