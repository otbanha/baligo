Bali Travel Guide Part 6: Jimbaran Guide

**Bottom Line First**: Jimbaran is located south of Bali's airport, about a 15–20 minute drive away. It's best for travelers who want to "watch the sunset, eat seafood, and stay in 4- or 5-star resorts." Three must-dos: **watch the sunset at Jimbaran Beach**, **enjoy grilled fish and lobster at beachfront seafood restaurants** (see our [10 no-regret seafood restaurant recommendations](/en/blog/jimbaran-seafood-restaurants-list/) to avoid tourist traps), and **shop and dine at Samasta Lifestyle Village**. This article rounds up 30 attractions and activities, complete with a savable Google Maps guide.

Jimbaran, a slice of paradise in Bali, is world-famous for its stunning beach sunsets and seafood. But what else is worth exploring here? Follow in the footsteps of "[Xiao Jie Indonesia](http://bit.ly/youtube-jason)" as we take you through Jimbaran in this blog, uncovering the activities you can enjoy and discovering this Balinese paradise.

## GWK Culture Park: Bali's Cultural Treasure (👉🏼 Map: [Must-Visit Spots in Jimbaran](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1D3sjMfTtG3_4I3AR2Ljl4Hh2bQFn9-U&usp=sharing))

![GWK Culture Park：峇里島的文化寶庫（地圖：金巴蘭必訪景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b7bbdc8f5e366d675157e3db28abb0c6.jpg)

GWK Culture Park (Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park) is a striking attraction in Bali, located near Jimbaran. This cultural park is renowned for its grand sculptures, impressive architecture, and fusion of Balinese culture. As you step into the park, you'll be surrounded by a series of magnificent sculptures and structures that showcase the depth of Balinese heritage. The most famous sculpture here is the colossal 121-meter-tall statue combining the Hindu god Shiva and the mythical Garuda bird, ranking as the fourth tallest statue in the world. That towering structure you see from the beaches of Seminyak and Canggu? That's this very statue!

GWK Culture Park is not just a treasure trove of art and culture but also a venue for various cultural events and performances. You can enjoy traditional dance, music, and theater that offer a deep dive into the beauty of Balinese culture. The park also houses a cultural center with in-depth information about Bali's heritage, helping visitors better understand this island gem. For a detailed guide, check out [this article](/en/blog/gwk-cultural-park-bali/).

## **Uluwatu Temple Sunset & Kecak Fire Dance Tour** (👉🏼 Map: [Must-Visit Spots in Jimbaran](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1D3sjMfTtG3_4I3AR2Ljl4Hh2bQFn9-U&usp=sharing))

![烏魯瓦圖廟夕陽＆卡恰火舞之旅（地圖：金巴蘭必訪景點）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_217afb04c28455f43d3135b503b3c6cd.jpg)

Bali's religious heritage and cultural activities reach their peak with the Uluwatu Temple and Kecak Fire Dance tour. Uluwatu Temple, one of Bali's most spectacular temples, sits atop a cliff overlooking the vast Indian Ocean. This important religious site also offers a prime spot to watch the sunset. As the sun dips below the horizon, the temple's silhouette against the fiery sky creates a breathtaking scene.

After visiting Uluwatu Temple, you can head to a Kecak Fire Dance performance, a traditional Balinese cultural show. This performance combines dance, music, and drama to tell ancient Balinese stories. It's usually held in an open-air amphitheater, where the audience sits in a semicircle, enjoying the show under the stars. What makes it special is that performers use only their voices—chanting in unison to create the unique Kecak music—instead of musical instruments, building a truly mesmerizing atmosphere.
