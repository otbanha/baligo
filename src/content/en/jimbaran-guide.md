## GWK Culture Park: Bali's Cultural Treasure (👉🏼 Map: [Must-Visit Spots in Jimbaran](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1D3sjMfTtG3_4I3AR2Ljl4Hh2bQFn9-U&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b7bbdc8f5e366d675157e3db28abb0c6.jpg)

GWK Culture Park is a captivating attraction located near Jimbaran in Bali. This cultural park is renowned for its grand sculptures, magnificent architecture, and the fusion of Balinese culture. As you step into the park, you'll be surrounded by a series of spectacular sculptures and structures that showcase the profound depth of Balinese culture. The most famous sculpture in the park is the towering 121-meter-high statue of the Hindu god Shiva fused with the Indonesian eagle Garuda, ranking as the fourth tallest giant sculpture in the world. The tower-like structure you see along the beaches of Seminyak and Canggu is this very statue!

GWK Culture Park is not only a treasure trove of art and culture but also a venue for various cultural events and performances. You can enjoy traditional dances, music performances, and dramas that offer deep insights into the beauty of Balinese culture. Additionally, the park features a cultural center providing in-depth information about Balinese culture, helping visitors better understand this gem of Bali. For a detailed guide, check out [here](/blog/2025-07-17-68785bf9fd89780001822053/)

## **Uluwatu Temple Sunset & Kecak Fire Dance Tour** (👉🏼 Map: [Must-Visit Spots in Jimbaran](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1D3sjMfTtG3_4I3AR2Ljl4Hh2bQFn9-U&usp=sharing))

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_217afb04c28455f43d3135b503b3c6cd.jpg)

Bali's religious heritage and cultural activities culminate in the Uluwatu Temple and Kecak Fire Dance tour. Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali's most spectacular temples, perched on a cliff overlooking the majestic Indian Ocean. This temple is not only an important religious site but also an excellent spot to watch the sunset. As the sun sets, the silhouette of Uluwatu Temple against the fiery sunset creates a breathtaking scene.

After visiting Uluwatu Temple, you can head to watch the Kecak Fire Dance performance, a traditional Balinese cultural show. The Kecak Fire Dance combines dance, music, and drama to narrate ancient Balinese stories. The performance usually takes place in an open-air theater, where the audience sits in a semicircular arrangement, enjoying the show under the starlit sky. A unique aspect of the performance is that the actors do not use musical instruments but instead chant the distinctive 'Kecak' music, creating a mesmerizing atmosphere.
