Tirta Empul (meaning 'holy spring') is one of Bali's oldest and most spiritually significant water temples, located in Tampaksiring village, Gianyar. This temple is renowned for its Melukat purification ritual, where devotees believe the spring water has the power to cleanse negative energy.

**Visiting Highlights:**

- Experience the traditional Melukat purification ritual
- Admire ancient Balinese architecture and mysterious springs
- Observe local religious celebrations like Piodalan
- Find peace in the lush surroundings

***

## What is the Melukat Purification Ritual? Can Non-Hindus Participate?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5ba2abc7963542e2253bb38ebb3360ef.jpg)

Melukat is a purification ritual in Balinese Hinduism, where devotees pass through 26 fountains in sequence, using the water and prayers to cleanse their spirits.

Non-Hindus can also participate, as long as they respect the culture and etiquette.

**Participation Guidelines:**

- Wear a sarong and sash (provided free by the temple)
- Prepare Canang Sari (offerings) available for purchase outside the temple
- Maintain quietness, humility, and respect
- Follow the spring sequence for purification (guides are available to assist)

### The Correct Process and Order of the Tirta Empul Purification Ritual

The Tirta Empul purification ritual may seem simple, but it follows a specific order and rules. Please adhere to these steps:

1. **Attire and Preparation**: Change into the traditional green sarong and waistband, store your belongings in the locker, then proceed to the holy spring pool area.
2. **Begin the Purification Process**: When facing the holy spring pools, **start from the left pool**, following the sequence of water spouts for prayer and purification.
3. **Skip Two Specific Spouts (Tirta Pengentas)**: **Skip the second-to-last and third-to-last spouts on the left pool**. These are reserved for purification rituals for the deceased and should not be used by those not involved in funeral rites.
4. **After Completing the Left Pool, Move to the Right Pool**: Only the first spout in the right pool needs to be used for purification; there’s no need to go through each one.
5. **Steps for Each Spout**:
6. - Place your hands together in prayer, silently express your wishes or prayers while facing the spout.

- Splash the holy water on your hair, symbolizing the cleansing of distractions and fatigue.
- Sprinkle the holy water over your entire body to seek inner and outer purification and spiritual renewal.

The cool, refreshing water flowing over your body feels as though it washes away travel fatigue and worries. This ritual of mind, body, and spirit not only brings inner peace but also offers a profound and unforgettable cultural experience—definitely worth trying!

## Introduction to the Three Main Areas of Tirta Empul

**Outer Courtyard (Jaba Pura)**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3f2cc19c62eca50170a62be966baf1f4.jpg)

Upon entering the temple gates, you’ll arrive at the outer courtyard, which features a serene koi pond and lush greenery, making it an ideal spot for meditation and reflection.

**Central Courtyard (Jaba Tengah)**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4700e8862974289fdc9dfce583117aec.jpg)

The main area for purification rituals, featuring two large pools and 26 springs, each representing a different spiritual wish.

**Significance of Key Springs:**

- Tirta Sudamala: Purification of the soul
- Tirta Penglukatan: Removal of negative energy
- Tirta Panegtegan: Enhancement of courage and confidence

Note: Some springs (e.g., Tirta Pengentas) are reserved for death rituals; visitors should avoid using them.

**Inner Sanctum (Jeroan)**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_623a206ab810a4a260d720844b62f5f3.jpg)

The most sacred area, housing the main temple and the source of the springs. It is a place for meditation and prayer after completing the Melukat ritual. Non-Hindus should refrain from entering casually.

## Dress Code & Etiquette

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_de538df8b6d201321be97e49b49d502a.jpg)

**Required Attire:**

- Sarong and Sash provided free of charge
- Shoulders must be covered; avoid tank tops and shorts
- Men may wear Udeng headband (optional)

**Prohibited Actions:**

- Women on their menstrual cycle cannot enter the main shrine
- Do not step on altars or offering tables
- Maintain silence and respect those performing rituals
- No littering or loud behavior

## Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

### 1. Can non-Hindus participate in the Melukat ritual at Tirta Empul?

Yes. As long as you respect local culture and ritual etiquette, and maintain politeness, you can participate with peace of mind.

### 2. Are sarongs provided upon entry?

Yes. Sarongs and waistbands are provided free of charge when entering the temple, so you don’t need to bring your own.

### 3. Is the spring water drinkable?

It is not recommended to drink directly. The spring water is primarily for ritual purposes and has not been purified.

### 4. Can I visit on my own without hiring a guide?

Yes. However, if you wish to gain deeper insight into the meaning of the springs and the ritual process, it’s recommended to hire a local guide.

### 5. Is photography allowed inside the temple?

Photography is permitted, but please avoid capturing people during rituals and refrain from disturbing worshippers in prayer.
