Have you started planning your international travels for 2024?

This guide introduces Taiwan's national holidays in 2024 and provides several leave strategies to help you enjoy the longest vacations with the least annual leave. In 2024, Taiwan has 116 national holidays, including four consecutive holidays of three days or more: New Year's Day, Lunar New Year, Tomb Sweeping Day, and Dragon Boat Festival. Mid-Autumn Festival and Double Ten Day fall on Thursdays, so there are no consecutive holidays.

Here are some leave strategies to help you enjoy the longest vacations with minimal annual leave:

****New Year's Day Holiday**** Originally 3 days, take leave from January 2 to January 5 to enjoy 9 days off.

****Lunar New Year Holiday**** Originally 7 days, take leave from February 5 to February 7 to enjoy 12 days off. Or take leave on February 16 and 17 for 11 days off.

****228 Peace Memorial Day**** Originally 1 day, take leave on February 26, 27, 29, and March 1 to enjoy 9 days off.

****Tomb Sweeping Day Holiday**** Originally 4 days, take leave from April 1 to April 3 to enjoy 9 days off.

****Labor Day**** Originally 1 day, take leave on April 29, 30, May 2, and 3 to enjoy 9 days off.

****Dragon Boat Festival Holiday**** Originally 3 days off, taking additional leave from June 11 to June 14 allows for a 9-day break.

****Mid-Autumn Festival**** Originally 1 day off, taking additional leave on September 16 and September 18 to September 20 allows for a 9-day break.

****Double Ten Day**** Originally 1 day off, taking additional leave from October 7 to October 9 and October 11 allows for a 9-day break.

****Best to Avoid:**** Lunar New Year is a peak tourist season in Bali, attracting visitors from around the world, especially from East Asia where Lunar New Year is considered the only long holiday of the year. Originally, February 10th, the first day of the Lunar New Year, is a national holiday in Indonesia, and February 8th coincidentally is the Ascension of Prophet Muhammad (a national holiday in Indonesia), plus the weekend, Indonesia will have flexible holidays from February 8th to February 11th. Additionally, with the Indonesian presidential election on February 14th, many Indonesians are expected to take leave from February 12th to 13th, leading to a seven-day break, which is anticipated to cause major traffic jams in Bali. Check out our [Lunar New Year Bali Family Travel Guide](/blog/2024-01-13-65a211a1fd89780001cfcd58/).

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_700e68141838114c4ae9cbc8dd92b922.png)

****Can Participate:**** February 28th to March 9th Galungan & Kuningan: This is one of Bali's most important festivals, usually lasting about 10 days, celebrating the creation of the universe and the victory of good over evil. During this festival, people offer prayers to their ancestors at home, seeking their blessings, and also hold ceremonies in temples to express reverence to the gods. During this period, schools in Bali are closed for two weeks, highlighting its significance!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_075f5585db131960b6ac37cd4a560582.png)

****Do Not Go:**** The 2024 Indonesian Eid al-Fitr holiday will start on April 8th (Friday) and last until April 15th. Eid al-Fitr is the most important holiday in Indonesia, celebrating the end of Ramadan (April 10th), but it's also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, equivalent to our Chinese Lunar New Year. Typically, Bali starts to fill up with locals from various parts of Indonesia around April 5th, making it the first peak season among Bali's five major tourist seasons. This is a holiday we recommend you avoid and not visit Bali!

****Can Attend:**** May 1-5: The 2024 Bali Spirit Festival will run from May 1st to May 5th. The Bali Spirit Festival is a music, wellness, and yoga event designed to offer participants a holistic mind-body-spirit experience. Held in Ubud, attendees can join yoga, dance, music, and healing workshops while connecting with people from around the world. The festival aims to create a global community that respects and cares for our environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_78e2b82008d7640eff35d35211a75671.png)

****Recommended Event:**** The most exciting event in September is the Bali Carnival! This annual cultural festival takes place in various locations across Bali every September. Known for its vibrant traditional dances, music, and art performances, it attracts visitors from all over the world. In recent years, more and more international participants have joined the parade! Dates are yet to be announced.

****Can Attend:**** October 3-12: Navaratri is a Hindu festival celebrating the victory of Goddess Durga. Typically held in September or October, it spans nine days and includes fasting, prayers, flower offerings, and dances. The final day, Dussehra, commemorates Prince Rama's defeat of the demon king Ravana. If you plan a trip to Bali around Taiwan's Double Ten Day, you can enjoy a nine-day holiday while immersing yourself in the sacred atmosphere of Navaratri, adding depth to your travel experience.
