Have you started planning your international travels for 2024?

This guide introduces Taiwan's national holidays in 2024 and provides strategies to maximize your vacation time with minimal leave days. Taiwan has 116 national holidays in 2024, including four consecutive holidays of three days or more: New Year's Day, Lunar New Year, Tomb-Sweeping Day, and Dragon Boat Festival. Mid-Autumn Festival and Double Ten Day fall on Thursdays, so they don't create long weekends.

Here are some strategies to help you enjoy the longest vacations with minimal leave days:

**New Year's Day Holiday**: Originally 3 days, take January 2–5 off for a 9-day break.

**Lunar New Year Holiday**: Originally 7 days, take February 5–7 off for a 12-day break. Alternatively, take February 16–17 off for an 11-day break.

**228 Peace Memorial Day**: Originally 1 day, take February 26, 27, 29, and March 1 off for a 9-day break.

**Tomb-Sweeping Day Holiday**: Originally 4 days, take April 1–3 off for a 9-day break.

**Labor Day**: Originally 1 day, take April 29, 30, May 2, and 3 off for a 9-day break.

**Dragon Boat Festival Holiday** Originally a 3-day holiday, taking additional days off from June 11 to June 14 allows for a 9-day break.

**Mid-Autumn Festival** Originally a 1-day holiday, taking additional days off on September 16 and September 18 to September 20 allows for a 9-day break.

**Double Ten Day** Originally a 1-day holiday, taking additional days off from October 7 to October 9 and October 11 allows for a 9-day break.

**Best to Avoid:** Lunar New Year is Bali's peak tourist season, attracting visitors from around the world, especially from East Asia, where Lunar New Year is considered the longest holiday of the year. Originally, February 10 (Lunar New Year's Day) is a national holiday in Indonesia, and February 8 happens to be Ascension Day of Prophet Muhammad (also a national holiday). Combined with the weekend, Indonesia has flexible holidays from February 8 to February 11. Additionally, February 14 is Indonesia's presidential election, and many Indonesians are likely to take February 12 and 13 off, creating a 7-day holiday. Expect heavy traffic in Bali! Check out our [Bali Family Travel Guide for Lunar New Year](/blog/2024-01-13-65a211a1fd89780001cfcd58/).

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_700e68141838114c4ae9cbc8dd92b922.png)

**Can Participate:** February 28 to March 9, Galungan & Kuningan: This is one of Bali's most important festivals, lasting about 10 days, celebrating the creation of the universe and the triumph of good over evil. During this festival, people honor their ancestors at home, seeking their blessings, and hold ceremonies in temples to express reverence for the gods. Schools in Bali close for two weeks during this period, highlighting its significance!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_075f5585db131960b6ac37cd4a560582.png)

**Avoid:** Indonesia's Eid al-Fitr holiday in 2024 starts on April 8 (Friday) and lasts until April 15. Eid al-Fitr is Indonesia's most important holiday, celebrating the end of Ramadan (April 10), but it's also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, akin to our Lunar New Year. Bali typically starts filling up with locals around April 5, making it the first of Bali's five peak seasons. This is a holiday you should avoid visiting Bali!

**Join us:** May 1-5, 2024: The Bali Spirit Festival kicks off on May 1 and runs through May 5. This festival of music, wellness, and yoga offers participants a holistic experience for mind, body, and soul. Held in Ubud, attendees can participate in yoga, dance, music, and healing workshops while connecting with people from around the globe. The Bali Spirit Festival aims to create a global community that respects and cares for our environment.

Known for its vibrant traditional dances, music, and art performances, it attracts visitors from all over the world. In recent years, more and more international participants have joined the parade! **Join us:** October 3-12: Navaratri is a Hindu festival celebrating the victory of Goddess Durga. Typically held in September or October, this nine-day festival involves fasting, prayers, flower offerings, and dancing. The final day, Dussehra, commemorates Prince Rama's defeat of the demon king Ravana. If you visit Bali around Double Ten Day, you can enjoy a nine-day holiday and immerse yourself in the sacred atmosphere of Navaratri, adding depth to your travel experience.
