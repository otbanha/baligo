In Bali, there are numerous currency exchange locations, from airports to hotels, and exchange offices throughout the city. However, the exchange rates can vary. Therefore, it's best to understand the local exchange rate situation before exchanging currency.

### Common USD Denominations

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9bb247d0e05bae1d63368331539e85c8.png)

In Bali, common USD denominations include $100, $50, $20, $10, and $5. Different denominations may have slight differences in exchange rates.

### Exchange Rate Differences Between Large and Small USD Bills

Generally, when exchanging USD in Bali, Money Changers categorize USD into three types: Large bills ($100, $50) tend to have better exchange rates; small bills ($20, $10, $5) have slightly worse rates; and $1 bills have the worst rates.

## Currency Exchange Guide

### Does It Really Make a Big Difference?

When exchanging currency, we often think that carrying $100 and $50 bills will get us the most Indonesian Rupiah. However, if you look at it differently, suppose you bring $1,000 in $20, $10, and $5 bills to exchange into Rupiah. Compared to $100 and $50 bills, you lose 20 Rupiah (about $0.04) per dollar exchanged. For $1,000, that's a total loss of $40...

Now, imagine you bring 1,000 $1 bills to Bali for exchange. Compared to larger bills, you lose 275 Rupiah (about $0.55) per dollar exchanged. For $1,000, that's a total loss of $550... which doesn't seem like an unbearable amount, right?
