**Key Highlights**: This seven-day, six-night travelogue connects three distinct style areas of Bali—**Seminyak** (staying at Indigo Seminyak, walking to Double Six Beach for sunset, boutique shopping), **Ubud** (cultural experiences), and **Canggu** (trendy, modern vibe). Each area has a clear character, perfect for travelers who want to experience Bali’s diverse facets without sticking to just one spot.

After the pandemic, I’ve traveled to quite a few countries, but this seven-day, six-night trip to Bali has become the one I most want to revisit. Whether in Seminyak, Ubud, or Canggu, each area has its own unique charm, offering travelers different feelings and memories.

## The Enchanting Vibe of Seminyak

First, I arrived in lively Seminyak, where the bustling, modern atmosphere is undoubtedly one of Bali’s hallmarks. I chose to stay at the **Indigo Seminyak** hotel, which boasts an excellent location—within walking distance of the famous Double Six Beach. Every evening, I’d order a drink, face the beach, and wait for the sunset. In that moment, I felt an incredibly relaxing peace. Such a leisurely life made me completely forget the daily grind—it felt like paradise.

## Cultural Experiences in Ubud

Next, I headed to Ubud, the cultural heart of Bali. This area is steeped in an artistic atmosphere and pastoral scenery, offering a stark contrast to the bustling vibe of Seminyak. My first stop was the traditional market near the Ubud Palace, where stalls overflow with everything from handmade crafts to local specialties, each one brimming with the thrill of discovery. I recommend setting aside plenty of time to 'treasure hunt,' as every stall might hold the unique souvenir you've been dreaming of.

https://youtu.be/s16gdTOjonU

Wandering through Ubud's exotic alleyways, I stumbled upon two restaurants that made it hard to leave—**Kebun Bistro** and **Kusi**. Both offer a distinct taste of Ubud through their dishes and ambiance. ## Trendy and Stylish Canggu

The final stop of our trip was Canggu, the trendiest new area in Bali. The streets are lined with a wide variety of concept stores and cafés, each with its own unique design style that makes you want to explore every one. Whether you're shopping or indulging in local cuisine, Canggu's diversity ensures every traveler finds something to love.

The **Komu Hotel** we stayed at had slightly lacking soundproofing, allowing us to 'enjoy' the nightclub's noise from our room. However, the hotel's modern design was impressive, and the overall experience was quite good—especially for travelers who appreciate contemporary aesthetics, offering great value for money.
