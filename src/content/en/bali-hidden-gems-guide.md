After the pandemic, I've flown to quite a few countries, but this seven-day, six-night trip to Bali has become the one I most want to revisit. Whether in Seminyak, Ubud, or Canggu, each area has its own unique charm, offering travelers different experiences and memories.

## The Enchanting Vibe of Seminyak

First, I headed to lively Seminyak, where the bustling, modern atmosphere is undoubtedly one of Bali's hallmarks. I chose to stay at **Indigo Seminyak**, a hotel with an unbeatable location—just a short walk from the famous Double Six Beach. Every evening, I'd order a drink, face the ocean, and wait for the sunset. In that moment, a deep sense of calm washed over me. This laid-back lifestyle made me completely forget the daily grind—it felt like paradise.

## Cultural Immersion in Ubud

Next, I headed to Ubud, the cultural heart of Bali. With its rich artistic atmosphere and pastoral landscapes, it stands in stark contrast to the bustling vibe of Seminyak. My first stop was the traditional market near the Ubud Palace, where stalls overflow with everything from handmade crafts to local specialties—every corner holds a delightful surprise. I recommend setting aside plenty of time to hunt for treasures, as each stall might hide that one-of-a-kind souvenir you've been dreaming of.

https://youtu.be/s16gdTOjonU

Wandering through Ubud's exotic alleyways, I stumbled upon two restaurants that made it hard to leave—**Kebun Bistro** and **Kusi**. Both offer a unique taste of Ubud through their dishes and ambiance. ## Trendy and Stylish Canggu

The final stop of our trip was Canggu, the hippest new area in Bali. The streets here are lined with a wide variety of concept stores and cafes, each with its own unique design style that makes you want to explore every one. Whether you're shopping or savoring food, Canggu's diversity ensures every traveler finds something they love.

The **Komu Hotel** we stayed at had slightly inadequate soundproofing, allowing us to "enjoy" the nightclub's noise from our room, but the hotel's design style was fresh and modern. Overall, the experience was quite good, especially for travelers who love contemporary design—offering great value for money.
