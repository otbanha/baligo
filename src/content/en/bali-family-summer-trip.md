Taking from the group, giving back to the group. Just returned from Bali, sharing our travel experience with everyone. During this trip, we experienced everything from immigration and transportation to weather, activities, and cuisine. We hope these insights will help with your travel planning.

## Smooth Immigration

We opted for visa-on-arrival and flew to Bali with China Airlines. After landing, the immigration process went smoothly with almost no waiting time. The immigration officers were efficient and friendly. We paid the visa fee in USD and received change in Indonesian Rupiah—simple and straightforward.

## Bali's Traffic Situation

Bali's traffic is notoriously bad, with congestion almost all day. Therefore, it's advisable not to pack your itinerary too tightly, or it can become exhausting. We prepared thoroughly before the trip but still needed to adjust our schedule flexibly to avoid travel delays affecting our experience.

## Comfortable Weather

Bali's weather is very comfortable. Although the sun is intense, it doesn't feel as harsh on the skin as Taiwan's heat. However, sunburn is still a risk, so it's essential to take sun protection measures, including applying sunscreen, wearing protective clothing, and hats.

## Resort Pool Depth

Resort pools in Bali are typically deeper compared to those in Taiwan, presenting a noticeable difference. Therefore, parents traveling with young children should pay extra attention to their safety. It's advisable to bring arm floats or other buoyancy devices to ensure the children's safety while playing in the water.

## Hard Rock Hotel's Kids Club

The Hard Rock Hotel is truly perfect for families with children. One day, we enrolled our kids in the full-day Kids Club activities while we adults went shopping at nearby malls and enjoyed spa treatments. This not only gave us a day of peace and quiet but also ensured the kids had a fantastic time. We stayed at the Hard Rock Hotel in Kuta for the last three days. The Kids Club at Hard Rock is very popular, so I recommend making reservations in advance. The hotel provides WhatsApp contact details, and you can also email to book ahead. If your plans are confirmed, booking early is safer as cancellations are allowed before 10 AM. I made the reservation upon arrival and was lucky to secure the last available spot.

## Delicious Food

The food in Bali is truly delicious, and we didn't encounter any disappointments this time. However, since our kids are sensitive to spicy food, we recommend asking the staff about the spiciness of dishes to ensure a pleasant dining experience for the children. There are many restaurants near the Hard Rock Hotel, and we randomly chose one without any issues. Within walking distance, there are two malls and numerous dining options, including Jamie Oliver's restaurant, KFC, and McDonald's at the back entrance. I genuinely think the fries at Bali's KFC are exceptionally good.

We recommend choosing the full 2-hour experience rather than the 1-hour option for a more complete adventure. We left our villa in Ubud at 9 AM, and a simple lunch was included. We finished around 1 PM. Getting to the rafting spot required walking down a long staircase, but even our kids managed it slowly without difficulty.

## Fun Hair Braiding Experience

The hair braiding experience in Bali is incredibly fun and really adds to the vacation vibe. I noticed many foreign children with colorful braids, so I decided to take my kids to try it out—it was quite special.

We got our hair braided on the street near Jamie Oliver's restaurant by Kuta Beach. This street is lined with many Spa shops offering braiding services, displaying samples of braids at their entrances. I initially thought they would use a lot of hair gel to secure the braids, but they didn't. When it was time to remove the braids, they simply cut off the extensions, which was very convenient.

## Convenient Private Car Service

This time, we opted for a private car service (driver + Chinese-speaking guide). We hired a guide who spoke Chinese, which not only solved our language barrier but also made our trip more relaxing and enjoyable. For example, we adults enjoyed three Spa sessions—once while the guide looked after the kids at our Villa, and another time when he took the kids out for food, allowing us to fully relax during our Spa time.
