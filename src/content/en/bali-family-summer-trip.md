Taking from the group, giving back to the group. Just returned from Bali, and I'd like to share my travel experiences with everyone. On this trip, we experienced everything from entry procedures and transportation to weather, activities, and food. I hope these insights help with your travel planning.

## Smooth Entry

We opted for a visa on arrival and flew to Bali with China Airlines. After landing, the entry process went very smoothly with almost no waiting time. The immigration officers were efficient and friendly. We paid the visa fee in US dollars, and they gave change in Indonesian rupiah—simple and straightforward.

## Traffic in Bali

Traffic in Bali is terrible, with congestion almost all day long. So, I recommend not overpacking your itinerary, or you'll end up exhausted. We prepared thoroughly before the trip, but we still had to adjust our plans flexibly to avoid traffic delays ruining the experience.

## Pleasant Weather

The weather in Bali is very comfortable. Although the sun is strong, it doesn't feel as harsh on the skin as Taiwan's heat. Still, it's easy to get sunburned, so be sure to take sun protection measures, including applying sunscreen, wearing protective clothing, and hats.

## Resort Pool Depths

Resort pools in Bali are typically deeper than those in Taiwan, so parents with young children need to be extra cautious. It's recommended to bring arm floats or other flotation devices to ensure kids' safety while playing in the water.

## Hard Rock Hotel's Kids Club

Hard Rock Hotel is truly perfect for families with kids. One day, we let the children join the full-day Kids Club activities while we adults went shopping at the nearby department store and enjoyed a spa. It gave us a peaceful day to ourselves, and the kids had a blast too. We stayed at Hard Rock Hotel in Kuta for the last three nights. The Kids Club is very popular, so I recommend booking in advance. The hotel provides a WhatsApp contact, and you can also email to make a reservation. If your itinerary is set, it's safer to book ahead, as cancellations are allowed before 10 AM. I booked upon arrival and was lucky to snag the last spot.

It's worth noting that the Hard Rock Kids Club has different activities each day. While photography isn't allowed inside, the best part is that lunch is included, so kids can happily spend the whole day there (cost: 250K IDR per day). This gives us adults some time to relax.

## Delicious Food

The food in Bali is absolutely delicious, and we didn't hit any duds this time. However, since kids are wary of spice, I suggest asking the staff if a dish is spicy when ordering, to avoid ruining their dining experience. There are plenty of restaurants near Hard Rock Hotel, and we picked a few at random without any disappointments. Within walking distance, there are two department stores and many dining options, with Jamie Oliver's restaurant, KFC, and McDonald's out the back door. I honestly think the fries at KFC in Bali are especially tasty.

## Thrilling Whitewater Rafting

![泛舟、OMMA Day Club（Fish Lee提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_28669c7a5c5894f46e25196b63d37e90.jpg)

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Rafting Guide (FAQ)](/blog/bali-rafting-ayung-telaga-waja/)

This time, we also tried white-water rafting in Ubud, and it was a blast. With so many options, we simply asked our driver to book for us, and he found a well-reviewed operator. I recommend choosing the full 2-hour trip instead of the 1-hour one for a more complete experience. We had two younger kids with us—one just over 5 and the other 7—and they both had a great time. The rafting itself took about 2 hours. We left our villa in Ubud at 9 AM, and after a simple lunch included in the trip, we wrapped up around 1 PM. To reach the rafting starting point, you need to walk down a long flight of stairs, but our kids managed it at their own pace without any trouble.

## Fun Hair Braiding Experience

The hair braiding experience in Bali is incredibly fun and really adds to the vacation vibe. I saw many foreign kids with colorful braids, so I took my children to try it too—it was very special.

We got our braids done on the street near Jamie Oliver’s restaurant in Kuta. This street is lined with many spa shops that offer braiding services, with sample braids displayed at their entrances. I thought they would use a lot of hair gel to secure the braids, but they didn’t. When it was time to remove them, we just cut off the fake hair parts—it was quite convenient.

## Convenient Private Car Service

This time we opted for a private car service (driver + Chinese-speaking guide). Hiring a guide who speaks Chinese not only solved the language barrier but also made our trip more relaxed and enjoyable. For instance, we adults enjoyed three spa sessions—once the guide looked after the kids at our villa, and another time he took them out to eat, allowing us to fully unwind during the spa treatments.
