**Key Takeaways**: Four tested tips for a summer family trip to Bali with kids—① Visa on arrival was processed **without long waits**, and paying in USD will get you change in Indonesian Rupiah; ② Traffic **jams all day** are the norm, so don't overpack your itinerary; ③ The weather is pleasant but **UV rays are strong**, so sun protection is a must; ④ Resort pools are **quite deep**, so be sure to bring floaties or other flotation devices for kids.

Taking from the group, giving back to the group. Just returned from Bali and want to share our travel experience. On this trip, we experienced everything from entry, transportation, and weather to various activities and food. I hope these insights help with your travel planning.

## Smooth Entry

We opted for a visa on arrival and flew to Bali with China Airlines. After landing, the entry process went very smoothly with almost no waiting time. The immigration officers were efficient and friendly. We paid the visa fee in USD, and they gave us change in Indonesian Rupiah—simple and straightforward.

## Traffic in Bali

Traffic in Bali is terrible, with congestion almost all day long. So it's best not to pack your itinerary too tightly, or you'll end up exhausted. We prepared well before the trip, but we still had to adjust our schedule flexibly to avoid travel delays affecting the experience.

## Pleasant Weather

The weather in Bali is very comfortable. Although the sun is strong, it doesn't feel as harsh on the skin as Taiwan's heat. However, you can still get sunburned easily, so be sure to take sun protection measures, including applying sunscreen, wearing protective clothing, and a hat.

## Resort Pool Depth

Resort pools in Bali are typically deeper than those in Taiwan, which is a noticeable difference. If you're traveling with children, parents need to pay extra attention to their safety. It's recommended to bring arm floats or other flotation devices to ensure kids stay safe while playing in the water.

## Hard Rock Hotel's Kids Club

Hard Rock Hotel is truly perfect for families with kids. One day, we let our children join the full-day Kids Club activities while we adults went shopping at the nearby department store and enjoyed a spa. Not only did we get a peaceful day to ourselves, but the kids also had a blast. We stayed at the Hard Rock Hotel in Kuta for the last three days. The Kids Club is very popular, so it's best to book in advance. The hotel offers WhatsApp contact, or you can email to make a reservation. If your itinerary is set, it's safer to book ahead, as cancellations are allowed before 10 AM. I booked upon arrival and was lucky to snag the last spot.

## Delicious Food

The food in Bali is absolutely delicious—we didn't encounter any duds this time. However, since our kids are wary of spicy food, it's a good idea to ask the staff if a dish is spicy when ordering, to avoid ruining their dining experience. There are many restaurants near Hard Rock Hotel, and we picked a random one without any issues. Within walking distance, there are two department stores and plenty of dining options, with Jamie Oliver's restaurant, KFC, and McDonald's at the back entrance. I honestly think the fries at KFC in Bali are especially tasty.

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Rafting Guide (FAQ)](/en/blog/bali-rafting-ayung-telaga-waja/)

This time, we also tried whitewater rafting in Ubud, and it was a blast. With so many options available, we simply asked our private driver to book for us, and he found a well-reviewed operator. I recommend choosing the full 2-hour trip rather than the 1-hour one for a more complete experience. We had two younger kids with us—one just over five and the other seven—and they both had a great time. The rafting activity lasted about two hours. We set off from our villa in Ubud at 9 AM, and after a simple lunch included in the package, we wrapped up around 1 PM. To reach the rafting starting point, you need to walk down a long flight of stairs, but our kids managed it at their own pace without any trouble.

## Fun Hair Braiding Experience

Getting hair braided in Bali is a lot of fun and really adds to the vacation vibe. I saw many foreign kids with colorful braids, so I took my children to try it too—it was quite special.

Our family got our braids done on the street near Jamie Oliver's restaurant at Kuta Beach. This street is lined with many spa shops that offer braiding services, with sample braids displayed outside. I thought they would use a lot of hair gel to secure the braids, but they didn't. When removing the braids, you just need to cut off the fake hair part—it's pretty convenient.

## Convenient Car Charter Service

This time we opted for a private car service (driver + Chinese-speaking guide). We hired a guide who speaks Chinese, which not only solved the language barrier but also made our trip more relaxed and enjoyable. For example, the adults enjoyed three spa sessions—once the guide looked after the kids at our villa, another time he took them out to eat, allowing us to fully unwind during the spa.
