## Smooth Immigration

We opted for a visa on arrival and flew to Bali with China Airlines. After landing, the immigration process was very smooth with almost no waiting time. The immigration officers were highly efficient and friendly. We paid the visa fee in USD and received change in Indonesian Rupiah, making the process simple and straightforward.

## Bali's Traffic Situation

Bali's traffic situation is terrible, with congestion almost all day long. Therefore, it's advisable not to pack your schedule too tightly, as it can be very exhausting. We prepared thoroughly before the trip, but still needed to flexibly adjust our itinerary to avoid travel delays affecting our experience.

## Comfortable Weather

Bali's weather is very comfortable. Although the sun is strong, it doesn't feel as harsh on the skin as Taiwan's heat. Nevertheless, sunburn is still a risk, so it's essential to take sun protection measures, including applying sunscreen, wearing sun-protective clothing, and hats.

## Resort Pool Depths

Resort pools in Bali are typically deeper compared to those in Taiwan, presenting a noticeable difference. Therefore, parents traveling with young children should pay extra attention to their safety. It's advisable to bring arm floaties or other flotation devices to ensure the children's safety while playing in the water.

## Hard Rock Hotel's Kids Club

Hard Rock Hotel is truly ideal for families with children. One day, we enrolled our kids in the full-day Kids Club activities while we adults went shopping at the nearby mall and enjoyed a spa session. This not only gave us a day of peace and quiet but also ensured the kids had a fantastic time. We stayed at the Hard Rock Hotel in Kuta for the last three days. The Kids Club at Hard Rock is very popular, so I recommend making reservations in advance. The hotel provides WhatsApp contact details, and you can also email to book ahead. If your itinerary is confirmed, it's safer to book early as cancellations are allowed before 10 AM. I was lucky to secure the last spot when I made the reservation at the hotel.

## Delicious Food

The food in Bali is truly delicious, and we didn't encounter any disappointments this time. However, since our kids are sensitive to spicy food, it's advisable to ask the staff about the spiciness of dishes to ensure a pleasant dining experience for the children. There are many restaurants near Hard Rock Hotel, and we randomly chose one without any issues. Within walking distance, there are two malls and numerous dining options, including Jamie Oliver's restaurant, KFC, and McDonald's at the back entrance. I genuinely think the fries at KFC in Bali are exceptionally good.

## Exciting Rafting Experience

![泛舟、OMMA Day Club（Fish Lee提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_28669c7a5c5894f46e25196b63d37e90.jpg)

Rafting and OMMA Day Club (provided by Fish Lee)

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Rafting Guide (Rafting FAQ)](/blog/bali-rafting-ayung-telaga-waja/)

This time, we also experienced rafting in Ubud, which was incredibly fun. Since there were many options, we asked our private driver to book it for us, and he found a reputable operator. We recommend choosing the full 2-hour experience rather than the 1-hour option for a more complete adventure. We had two younger children, one around 5 years old and the other 7, and they both had a great time. The rafting activity lasted about 2 hours. We left our villa in Ubud at 9 AM, and a simple lunch was included. We finished around 1 PM. Getting to the rafting site requires walking down a long set of stairs, but even our kids managed it slowly without difficulty.

## Fun Braiding Experience

The braiding experience in Bali is incredibly fun and really adds to the vacation vibe. I noticed many foreign children with colorful braids, so I decided to take my kids to try it out—it was truly special.

We got our braids done on the street by Jamie Oliver's restaurant near Kuta Beach. This street is lined with numerous spa shops offering braiding services, and they display sample braids outside their stores. I initially thought they would use a lot of hair gel to secure the braids, but they didn't. When it was time to remove the braids, we just had to cut off the extensions, which was quite convenient.

## Convenient Private Car Service

This time, we opted for a private car service (driver + Chinese-speaking guide). We hired a guide who speaks Chinese, which not only solved our language barrier but also made our trip more relaxing and enjoyable. For example, we adults enjoyed three spa sessions—once while the guide watched our kids at the villa, and another time when he took the kids out for snacks, allowing us to fully unwind during our spa time.
