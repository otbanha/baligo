## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Aviation Resilience and Middle East Route Adjustments

- **Network Recovery**: Despite ongoing turmoil in the Middle East, Bali's international visitor numbers in the first quarter still surpassed 1.6 million, showcasing the tourism market's high resilience.
- **Security Updates**: In response to recent security incidents involving foreigners, the South Korean government issued a travel advisory today. The Bali Police Department reiterated that patrols around nightclubs and villa areas have been stepped up, and reminded travelers to make use of the newly launched reporting hotline.

## 🌤️ Weather Forecast: Continued 'Yellow' Thunderstorm Warning

- **Rainfall Trends**: According to the latest forecast from **BMKG**, the entire island remains under a 'Waspada (Alert)' level today. There is a **65% - 90%** chance of moderate to heavy rain from **afternoon to evening**, with possible lightning and strong winds in some areas.
- **Temperature and Sea Conditions**: Temperatures range between **21°C - 33°C**, with very high humidity (up to 95%). Wave heights in southern waters remain at **0.5 - 2 meters**. Water sports enthusiasts are advised to head ashore before the afternoon rains for safety.

## 🚗 Traffic and Development: Subway Construction Underway

- **Subway Progress**: The long-awaited **Bali Subway (LRT)** has officially broken ground on the route from the airport to Canggu. During construction, localized traffic changes (congestion) may occur around the airport area, so allow ample transfer time.
- **New West Coast Route**: Jetstar recently launched a direct flight to Australia's Sunshine Coast. While it may not directly affect you, a significant increase in European, American, and Australian visitors is expected starting this month.

## 🌊 Environment and Travel Trends: Hidden Gems on the Rise

- **Waste Ban Impact**: Due to the Suwung landfill banning organic waste since April 1, some street collection frequencies have been slightly affected. Travelers are advised to choose hotels with good waste management systems (e.g., those with sustainability certifications).
- **Slow Travel Trend**: The 2026 travel trend is shifting toward 'deep slow travel.' Eastern areas like **Amed** and Tulamben, with their secluded atmosphere, have become the most searched hotspots recently.
