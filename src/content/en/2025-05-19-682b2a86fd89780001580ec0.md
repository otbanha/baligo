## SAKA Museum Wins Prix Versailles International Architecture Award

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8076762ae5af260562c2e8e6cfde5c76.jpg)

In 2025, the SAKA Museum in Jimbaran, Bali, was listed among the world's most beautiful museums, marking it as the sole Indonesian museum to achieve this distinction. The honor was conferred by the French organization Prix Versailles, known for its annual selection of buildings that excel in design, cultural significance, and sustainability.

Sharing this prestigious recognition were France's Grand Palais in Paris, Norway's Kunstsilo Museum, and the Cleveland Museum of Natural History in the United States.

---

## A Model of Balinese Culture and Modern Architecture

Dr. Judith E. Bosnak, the museum's director, stated, 'This award proves that Balinese culture can be modern, powerful, and capable of engaging in dialogue on the international stage.' SAKA not only boasts a modern and aesthetically pleasing exterior but also fully embodies Bali's rich cultural heritage and sustainable principles within.

---

## Highlights of the SAKA Museum

Since its opening in 2024, the SAKA Museum has garnered significant attention. TIME magazine listed it among 'The World's Greatest Places to Visit,' and the Kyoto Global Design Award praised its innovative and sustainable architectural concepts.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d01b67a39659e0d695d4520b6645f2.jpg)

### Location and Environment

SAKA is located within the renowned **AYANA Resort Bali**, situated between the cliffs of Jimbaran and the coastline, offering breathtaking natural scenery and boasting a 4.7-star rating on Google Maps.

### **Cultural Exhibitions**

- **'Kasanga: Nyepi Special Exhibition'**  
  This interactive exhibition showcases the essence of Bali's most sacred holiday, Nyepi, including its rituals, purification ceremonies, and the nighttime Ogoh-Ogoh parade.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e66e535983024aa2553685842fc80922.jpg)

- **'Subak: Bali's Ancient Irrigation System'**  
  This exhibit introduces the Subak irrigation system, a UNESCO World Heritage site, revealing the symbiotic relationship between rice fields, temples, and communities.

---

## **More Than a Museum, It's an Immersive Experience**

Beyond its exhibition halls, SAKA Museum features a theater, library, meeting spaces, a shop, and an outdoor relaxation area with ocean views. Visitors can immerse themselves in the intersection of culture and nature, leaving with lasting impressions in just a few hours.

## **FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions**

### Where is the SAKA Museum located?

SAKA is situated within the AYANA Resort Bali in Jimbaran, Bali, Indonesia.

### Is advance booking required to visit SAKA Museum?

General visits do not require reservations, but it's advisable to purchase tickets in advance during holidays or peak seasons to avoid crowds.

### Are there Chinese language tours available in the museum?

Currently, tours are primarily in English and Indonesian, but some exhibitions feature multilingual subtitles. Chinese audio guides may be introduced in the future.

### Is the museum suitable for children?

Absolutely, the interactive exhibitions make learning fun and engaging for children.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0edcd294343232378ae08cd9028fd507.jpg)

### Can I buy souvenirs at the museum?

Yes, there is a shop inside the museum offering handicrafts and souvenirs themed around Balinese culture.
