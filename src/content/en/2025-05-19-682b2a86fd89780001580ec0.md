## **SAKA Museum Wins Prix Versailles International Architecture Award**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8076762ae5af260562c2e8e6cfde5c76.jpg)

In 2025, the SAKA Museum in Jimbaran, Bali, was honored as one of the world's most beautiful museums, becoming the only Indonesian museum to make the prestigious list. This accolade was awarded by the French organization **Prix Versailles**, which annually recognizes the most architecturally stunning, culturally significant, and sustainable buildings globally.

Sharing this honor with SAKA are the Grand Palais in Paris, France, the Kunstsilo Museum in Norway, and the Cleveland Museum of Natural History in the United States.

***

## **A Perfect Blend of Balinese Culture and Modern Architecture**

Museum Director Dr. Judith E. Bosnak stated: 'This award proves that Balinese culture can be modern, powerful, and capable of engaging in dialogue on the international stage.' SAKA not only boasts a modern and aesthetically pleasing exterior but also fully showcases Bali's profound cultural heritage and sustainable philosophy within its interiors.

***

## **Highlights of SAKA Museum**

Since its opening in 2024, the SAKA Museum has garnered significant attention. **TIME Magazine** included it in its list of 'World's Greatest Places to Visit,' while the **Kyoto Global Design Awards** praised its innovative and sustainable architectural concepts.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d01b67a39659e0d695d4520b6645f2.jpg)

### **Location and Environment**

SAKA is located within the renowned AYANA Resort Bali, nestled between the cliffs and coastline of Jimbaran, offering breathtaking natural scenery. It boasts an impressive Google Maps rating of 4.7 stars.

### **Cultural Exhibitions**

- **《Canggu: Nyepi Special Exhibition》**
This interactive exhibition showcases Bali's most sacred holiday, Nyepi (Day of Silence), in its entirety. It features traditional rituals, purification ceremonies, and the nighttime Ogoh-Ogoh parade.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e66e535983024aa2553685842fc80922.jpg)

- **Subak: Bali's Ancient Irrigation System**
  This section introduces the Subak irrigation system, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site, revealing the symbiotic relationship between rice fields, temples, and local communities.

***

## More Than Just a Museum - An Immersive Experience

Beyond its exhibition halls, SAKA Museum also features a theater, library, conference spaces, shops, and an outdoor relaxation area with ocean views. Visitors can quietly experience the intersection of culture and nature here, leaving a lasting impression in just a few short hours.

## **FAQ Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Where is the SAKA Museum?**

SAKA is located within the AYANA Resort Bali in Jimbaran, Bali, Indonesia.

### **Do I need to make a reservation to visit SAKA Museum?**

No reservation is required for general visits, but it's recommended to purchase tickets in advance during holidays or peak seasons to avoid crowds.

### **Is there Chinese language guided tour available?**

Currently, the primary languages used are English and Indonesian, but some exhibitions feature multilingual subtitles. There are plans to potentially introduce Chinese audio guides in the future.

### **Is the Museum Suitable for Children?**

Highly suitable, with strong interactive elements in the exhibition, allowing children to experience fun while learning.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0edcd294343232378ae08cd9028fd507.jpg)

### **Is there any souvenir shopping available?**

Yes, the museum has a shop that sells handicrafts and souvenirs themed around Balinese culture.
