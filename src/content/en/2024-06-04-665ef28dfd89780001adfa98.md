Bali is a dream vacation destination for many travelers, combining tropical charm with unique culture, attracting countless visitors. However, choosing the right travel season is crucial for both travel quality and budget control.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3ca684e28f6adcd444bf1c7f57779da7.png)

Avoiding Bali's peak tourist seasons not only saves on airfare and accommodation budgets but also allows for a more tranquil travel experience, making it a wise choice. So, when exactly are Bali's peak seasons? Generally, the dry season with lower rainfall probability, from April to September each year, is the most suitable time to visit Bali, hence becoming the local tourist peak season. Although the rainy season weather might not be ideal, it offers a different perspective of Bali and discounted airfare and accommodation deals.

## Bali's Five Peak Tourist Seasons

Apart from distinguishing peak and off-peak seasons by weather, the following five special periods also attract a surge of tourists to Bali:

### 【First】Eid al-Fitr

- ****Eid al-Fitr Holiday in Indonesia (dates vary each year)****: Eid al-Fitr is a significant Muslim festival in Indonesia, during which many Indonesians flock to Bali for vacation, causing a sharp increase in crowds. This is Bali's number one peak season, ****definitely avoid this period to steer clear of the crowds****!
- The Eid al-Fitr national holiday lasts about ten days. It's Indonesia's most important holiday, celebrating the end of the Muslim fasting month, and is a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, equivalent to our Chinese Lunar New Year.
- In 2026, Bali is expected to start seeing an influx of local Indonesians from March 13th, with places packed with Indonesians for about two weeks. This is the first of Bali's five peak seasons, a holiday you're advised to avoid for Bali travel!

### 【Second】Christmas and New Year Holidays

****Christmas-New Year Holiday (December 25th to January 1st)****: Christmas and New Year are major holidays in Europe, America, and Australia, as well as peak travel seasons in many Asian countries. The New Year holiday typically attracts global tourists, as it's a long break for most Western countries, and Indonesians also try to take time off. Therefore, the Christmas-New Year period ranks second in terms of crowds and traffic. Here's a short video showing the situation on the highway to the airport on December 29, 2023 [link]. Hotel and flight prices in Bali usually increase during this period.

### ****【Third Place】Chinese Lunar New Year****

The Chinese Lunar New Year is one of the most important festivals in Asia, and many Asian tourists visit Bali during this time. Besides China and Taiwan, countries like Hong Kong, Malaysia, Singapore, South Korea, and Vietnam also have holidays ranging from two to five days; the Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia observe a one-day holiday on the first day of the Lunar New Year, while ****in 2026, Indonesia will have a four-day holiday****. The Lunar New Year holiday ranks third in attracting large crowds to tourist spots.

### 【Fourth Place】Summer Vacation

****July to September****: During the summer vacation in the Northern Hemisphere, many families and students choose to vacation in Bali, driving up flight and hotel prices. Many families and students take advantage of this time to visit Bali. Flight and accommodation prices during summer are never cheap!

### 【Fifth Place】Term Break

****Australian School Holidays and Indonesian International School Holidays (term break, usually a week between September 20th and October 10th)****: The term break holidays in Australia and Indonesian international schools also bring a wave of tourists, but the number of visitors is less than a third compared to the top four periods. I've taken advantage of this holiday to visit Bali several times!

## Advantages of Avoiding Peak Season

- **Save Money**: Airfare, accommodations, and activities are usually more expensive during peak season. Avoiding peak season can save you a significant amount of money.
- **More Peaceful**: Bali is crowded during peak season, and traffic jams can make you question life. Avoiding peak season allows for a smoother, less congested travel experience.
- **More Options**: Hotels and attractions may be fully booked during peak season. Avoiding peak season gives you more choices.
