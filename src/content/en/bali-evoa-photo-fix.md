Traveling to Indonesia or Bali requires a visa (VISA). There are two methods to apply for a visa. For a comparison of these two methods, please refer to: [Bali Travel Visa Guide: Landing Visa vs Electronic Visa, Which is Better for You?](/blog/bali-visa-landing-vs-electronic/)

## There are many benefits to applying for an electronic visa (eVOA) to enter Indonesia/Bali!

Steps to prepare documents for electronic visa application:

1. **Scan or photograph passport page**: Ensure the passport page is clearly visible without glare. Save it as a JPG or PDF file.
2. **Passport photo**: If you don't have one, take a selfie against a plain wall. Ensure high photo resolution and save as JPG format.
3. **Credit card**: Prepare your credit card for potential payment of related fees.
4. **Hotel/villa address**: Prepare the address of your accommodation in Bali for filling out the application form.
5. **Flight ticket PDF**: Prepare your flight ticket PDF to confirm your entry and departure dates.

Visit the official Indonesian Immigration website for application: <https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/>

Now you don't need to create an account to apply for an electronic visa, and you can apply for up to 5 people at once. Prepare your documents and start your electronic visa application.

## Troubles encountered when uploading passport photos

Many users have reported encountering various issues during the passport photo upload step. First, we need to ensure our photos meet the official website's requirements:

### Key Points in Example One:

1. File format: JPEG/JPG/PNG color photo
2. Minimum size: 400x600
3. File size must not exceed **2MB**
4. Proper composition

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8d4f513b24a0c87fae92aee293c19920.jpg)

**Pay special attention to the third point above!** With smartphone cameras becoming increasingly high-resolution, photos taken casually can easily exceed 2MB in size. Whether you're scanning photos or taking new ones, most will surpass the website's upload limit, resulting in rejection!

If you're unfamiliar with adjusting your phone's settings or unsure how to reduce file size, here's a handy trick. You can use messaging apps like Line, which almost everyone has, to send the photo to a family member or friend and have them send it back. These apps typically compress photos, reducing the original file size to just a few hundred KB without losing quality.
