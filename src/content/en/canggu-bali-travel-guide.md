## Understanding Canggu's Layout/Map

Canggu's roads may seem complicated at first glance, but once you grasp a few key points, navigating the area becomes much easier.

Canggu is primarily traversed by the bustling Jl. Raya Canggu, which we’ll refer to as 'Canggu Main Road.' If you’re visiting Canggu for a vacation, you’ll likely find yourself frequently traveling along this road, as it connects to the various beaches in the area.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bcb115402d89cc7fbbb2a73787e279fc.png)

To reach the beaches from Canggu Main Road, you’ll need to take one of the following three routes:

- Jl. Pantai Berawa (Berawa Beach Road)
- Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong (Batu Bolong Beach Road)
- Jl. Padang Lingjong (Padang Lingjong Road)

### Jl. Pantai Berawa (Berawa Beach Road)

Let's start with the rightmost road, "Jl. Pantai Berawa." This road connects to Berawa Beach, which is home to the very famous "Finns Beach Club," the most stylish beach club in the area. There's also the "Atlas Beach Fest" beach club, which opened after the pandemic. This road usually gets crowded by evening.

It's worth mentioning the public beach outside Finns Beach Club. What's special about this beach? Normally, when taking photos of people against a beach sunset, if the subject is far from the camera or phone, they usually end up as silhouettes. Without any lighting equipment and just using a phone, we managed to capture a clear, well-lit portrait with a beautiful sunset backdrop on this beach.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2f1c4750ee129a41dac2aeb26089d470.jpg)

So how was this achieved? It's because Finns Beach Club has strong lights directed toward the beach. So, during sunset, just standing anywhere on this beach can result in professional-looking photos with perfect lighting.

Not far from Finns Beach Club, Panama Kitchen & Pool is a great spot for taking photos.

Here, there's a garden pool with a South American Caribbean vibe, and the food is pretty good too. We spent a few hours here taking some photos.

### Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong (Batu Bolong Beach Road)

Located in the middle is Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong (Batu Bolong Beach Road), which boasts the highest concentration of restaurants, bars, and cafes in Canggu. It serves as the main shopping street and can be considered the downtown area of Canggu. If I were to recommend a place for first-time visitors to stay, this would be the best area in Canggu. Accommodations along Batu Bolong offer easy walking access to all the best cafes, restaurants, shops, and the beach.

On this road, you'll find 'Love Anchor,' a semi-open-air market selling exquisite handicrafts. The items are beautiful but come with a hefty price tag. However, the atmosphere here is uniquely special, with an indescribable vibe. It's a great spot for taking those artsy photos!

Continuing towards the beach, Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong leads to the popular Old Man's Bar and the sunset viewing spot at Batu Bolong Beach. Don't forget to take a photo with the mural and signboard of Oldman, which serves as a landmark of Canggu!

### Canggu Shortcut

Between the two roads mentioned earlier, Jl. Pantai Berawa (Berawa Beach Road) and Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong (Batu Bolong Beach Road), there's a secret shortcut connecting them! Locals call this path the Canggu Shortcut. It links the Batu Bolong and Batu Bewara areas, allowing for quicker commutes without having to take the often congested main road, which requires a long detour. The Canggu Shortcut provides great convenience. In 2017, it looked like this: a small path cutting through vast green rice fields, creating a feeling akin to a paradise on earth—so open and carefree!

![小傑Canggu攻略視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_36fc37858acc4c3c0e4c64442b683117.jpg)

[Jackie's Canggu Guide Video Screenshot](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aglSSIW2vZg)

However, with the rapid development of Canggu, before the pandemic hit, the Canggu Shortcut had already transformed into this: crowded with people and vehicles, lined with billboards, one house after another, and cafes built along the narrow roadside, turning the Canggu Shortcut into a small road devoid of any aesthetic appeal. The road conditions worsened, and with increasing traffic, the Canggu Shortcut became a stretch where 'someone plows the field every day'!

By the end of 2021, this shortcut was repaved with asphalt, and the road seemed a bit wider, but it might still be just as congested during certain times.

![小傑Canggu攻略視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d7c5ea859ad40ffcf173f2a18aeedb2c.jpg)

### Jl. Padang Lingjong (Padang Lingjong Road)

The third main road, Jl. Padang Lingjong, connects to Echo Beach. Recently, many shops and accommodations have concentrated in the area near the beach. There's a La Brisa Beach Club by the sea, a large place that requires online reservations to enter. The beach club's decor is stylish, making it hard not to fall in love with the casual dining atmosphere here. The interiors are built with driftwood floors and structures, planted with an abundance of coconut trees, and there's a swimming pool suitable for children. It offers a very unique charm.

## Canggu Cuisine

Having introduced Canggu's main roads and mentioned its cuisine, we can't overlook the food scene in Canggu! Canggu is also a place that emphasizes healthy eating, and among various health foods, the Smoothie Bowl has become an incredibly popular breakfast choice in recent years. Made with dairy products, vegetables, fruits, and nuts, the Smoothie Bowl is visually appealing, rich in texture, nutritious, healthy, delicious, and scientifically balanced—these adjectives perfectly describe it!

Start your day in Canggu with a Smoothie Bowl for a healthy and fat-reducing breakfast! After all, surfing while overweight won't attract any handsome guys or hot girls!

## Canggu Transportation

![小傑Canggu攻略視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_593492dd1250bf658dbf5e3cbd767052.jpg)

[Jie's Canggu Guide Video Screenshot](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aglSSIW2vZg)

Canggu has developed from small villages, and its road planning is not well-structured, with narrow streets that easily get congested. However, the atmosphere here has attracted many international investors, who have started building large resorts. But the issues of narrow roads and traffic congestion won't be resolved anytime soon, and traveling from one attraction to another often requires a detour. No wonder some say the best way to get around Canggu is to 'take off your shoes and walk along the beach'!

Besides exploring Canggu on foot, what other recommended ways are there? If you don't want to grow old stuck in traffic, you can use Indonesia's ride-hailing app 'Gojek' and its 'Go Ride' motorcycle service. This app is super convenient, the prices for rides are very low, and you don't have to worry about being overcharged since the prices are fixed. For more on how to use Gojek, refer to my previous introduction and tutorial. Gojek has an English interface, and I highly recommend it to friends visiting Indonesia!
