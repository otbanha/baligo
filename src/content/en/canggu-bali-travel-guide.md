## Understanding Canggu's Layout/Map

Canggu's road network may seem complex and confusing for first-time visitors. However, once you grasp a few key points, navigating Canggu becomes much easier.

Canggu is primarily traversed by the bustling Jl. Raya Canggu, known as 'Canggu Main Road' in Chinese. If you're vacationing in Canggu, you'll frequently travel along this road as it connects to various beaches via downward-leading paths.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bcb115402d89cc7fbbb2a73787e279fc.png)

To reach the beaches from Canggu Main Road, you'll need to take one of the following three routes:

- Jl. Pantai Berawa (Berawa Beach Road)
- Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong (Batu Bolong Beach Road)
- Jl. Padang Lingjong (Padang Lingjong Road)

### Jl. Pantai Berawa (Berawa Beach Road)

Let's start with the rightmost road, "Jl. Pantai Berawa." This road connects to Berawa Beach, home to the very fashionable "Finns Beach Club," the most stylish beach club in the area. There's also the "Atlas Beach Fest," a beach club that opened after the pandemic. This road usually gets crowded by evening.

It's worth mentioning the public beach outside Finns Beach Club. What makes this beach special? Normally, when taking photos of people against a beach sunset, those farther from the camera or phone end up as silhouettes. However, without any lighting equipment and just using a phone, we managed to capture a clear, well-lit portrait with a stunning sunset backdrop on this beach.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2f1c4750ee129a41dac2aeb26089d470.jpg)

So how was this achieved? It's because Finns Beach Club has strong lights directed toward the beach. So, during sunset, simply standing on this beach allows you to take professional-looking photos with perfect lighting.

Not far from Finns Beach Club is Panama Kitchen & Pool, a great spot for taking photos.

### Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong (Batu Bolong Beach Road)

Located in the middle of Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong (Batu Bolong Beach Road), this street boasts the highest concentration of restaurants, bars, and cafes, making it Canggu's main shopping street and essentially the heart of the town. If I were to recommend an area for first-time visitors to Canggu, this would be the best place to stay. Accommodations along Batu Bolong offer easy walking access to all the best cafes, restaurants, shops, and the beach.

On this road, you'll find 'Love Anchor,' a semi-open-air market selling exquisite handmade crafts. While the items are beautiful, they come with a hefty price tag. However, the atmosphere here is uniquely special, evoking an indescribable feeling. It's also a great spot for taking those artsy photos!

### Canggu Shortcut

Between the two roads I just mentioned—Jl. Pantai Berawa (Berawa Beach Road) and Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong (Batu Bolong Beach Road)—there's a secret shortcut known locally as Canggu Shortcut. It connects the Batu Bolong and Batu Bewara areas, allowing for quicker commutes without having to navigate the often congested Canggu main road. Canggu Shortcut offers great convenience. In 2017, it was a small path cutting through lush rice fields, creating a scene reminiscent of a paradise—open and carefree!

![小傑Canggu攻略視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_36fc37858acc4c3c0e4c64442b683117.jpg)

[Jackie's Canggu Guide Video Screenshot](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aglSSIW2vZg)

However, with the rapid development of Canggu, the Canggu Shortcut had already transformed into a congested and unattractive path before the pandemic hit. Crowds, traffic, billboards lining the narrow roads, and the continuous construction of houses and cafes along the roadside have stripped the Canggu Shortcut of any charm it once had. The road conditions worsened, and with increasing traffic, it became a stretch where accidents were a daily occurrence!

By the end of 2021, this shortcut was repaved with asphalt and seemed to have been widened slightly, though it might still experience traffic jams during certain times.

### Jl. Padang Lingjong (Padang Lingjong Road)

The third main road, Jl. Padang Lingjong, connects to Echo Beach. Recently, many shops and accommodations have concentrated in the area near the beach. La Brisa Beach Club, located by the sea, requires online reservations for entry. This beach club boasts a stylish decor, creating a relaxed dining atmosphere that's hard not to love. The club features wooden floors and structures built from driftwood, surrounded by numerous coconut trees and includes a swimming pool suitable for children, offering a unique charm.

## Canggu Transportation

![小傑Canggu攻略視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_593492dd1250bf658dbf5e3cbd767052.jpg)

[Jack's Canggu Guide Video Screenshot](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aglSSIW2vZg)

Canggu has developed from small villages, and its road planning isn't perfect, with narrow streets prone to traffic jams. However, the area's charm has attracted international investors, who have started building large resorts. But the issues of narrow roads and traffic congestion won't be resolved anytime soon, and traveling from one attraction to another often requires a long detour. No wonder some say the best way to get around Canggu is to 'take off your shoes and walk along the beach!'

Besides exploring Canggu on foot, what other recommended ways are there? If you don't want to grow old stuck in traffic, you can use Indonesia's ride-hailing app 'Gojek,' specifically its 'Go Ride' motorcycle service. This app is super convenient, the ride prices are very low, and you don't have to worry about being overcharged since the prices are fixed. For a guide on how to use Gojek, you can refer to my previous introduction and tutorial. Gojek has an English interface, and I highly recommend it to friends visiting Indonesia!
