### 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Environmental Protests and Social Rescue

- **Students Launch 'Garbage Emergency' Demonstration**: Yesterday morning (4/22), hundreds of students from Udayana University (Unud) gathered at Renon Square in Denpasar to protest Bali's worsening waste management crisis. Demonstrators urged the provincial government to address landfill saturation and infrastructure failures. Governor Wayan Koster personally met with student representatives, promising to accelerate policy reforms.
- **Australian Man Rescued from Abandoned Building**: An Australian man, severely injured and missing for several days, was found today in an abandoned building near Sunset Road in Legian. Reports indicate that the man became trapped after his motorcycle was taken by a local acquaintance, and his wounds had become severely infected. With the collaboration of the Australian consulate and local police, the man finally agreed to receive treatment and has been transferred to Denpasar Hospital.
- **Sports Tourism Shows Significant Results**: The recently concluded 'Kemala Run 2026' event attracted over 11,000 participants, bringing substantial tourism revenue to the Gianyar region. Officials plan to regularize such events to promote tourism in the eastern part of the island.

### 📄 Topics of Interest for Tourists: Coastal Warnings and Infrastructure

- **'King Tide' Coastal Flooding Alert**: Due to the new moon and lunar perigee (Perigee), BMKG has issued a 'King Tide' warning for coastal areas across the island from **4/17 - 4/25**. Today, the most affected areas include **Kuta, Seminyak, Canggu, and Sanur**, where seawater may overflow onto sidewalks or coastal villas. Visitors walking or dining near the beach should monitor water levels closely.
- **Transportation Changes**: Due to the King Tide, speedboat services between **Nusa Penida** and **Lembongan** may experience temporary delays or changes in boarding points due to high water levels at the docks. Tourists planning to visit the islands today are advised to confirm details with their boat operators in advance.
- **Immigration 'Village' Inspection Campaign**: The immigration office announced today the launch of the PIMPASA program, deploying officers to various villages to collect information on foreign residents. This means that long-term tourists staying in private accommodations or non-hotel areas are more likely to be asked to present valid residency documents.
