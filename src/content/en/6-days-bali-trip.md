> The following is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Liao Yiwen](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/1CjoK9BEFX/).

**Bottom line first**: For a six-day Bali itinerary, I recommend the route "Ubud (3 days) → Uluwatu → Nusa Lembongan." The drive from the airport to Ubud takes about **90 minutes** (the group recommends a private car for around USD$26). In Ubud, stay at **Kubu Tropis** (a 15–20 minute walk to Ubud Palace). For currency exchange, avoid **roadside money changers with suspiciously good rates**; instead, find modest shops that locals also use. The key is to book a well-reviewed local guide in advance (popular guides need early confirmation), which greatly enhances flexibility and peace of mind.

The moment the plane's tires touched down on Ngurah Rai Airport's runway, our six-day Bali adventure officially began. This wasn't just an escape from daily life—it was a meticulously crafted, warmly human sensory feast. From the spiritual Ubud in the rainforest to the majestic cliffs of Uluwatu, and finally the transparent waters of Nusa Lembongan, we spent six days gathering Bali's many facets. This travelogue will detail every step of the journey and share the key to a smooth, carefree trip: a local guide you can trust.

## Arriving in Ubud: A Lesson in Trust from the Airport

At 1:30 PM on December 12th, the tropical sun warmly embraced us as we stepped off the plane. Clearing customs, we immediately spotted the hotel's driver holding a sign with our name. Before the trip, we had asked the hotel to arrange currency exchange, and the driver was well aware. As the car left the airport, his first words weren't small talk but a valuable local tip: "You'll see many money changers on the road. Remember, if the rate looks too good, don't go in." His sincere eyes and simple English explained the risks. This kindness at first sight instantly dispelled the unease of a foreign land. Following his recommendation, we exchanged money at a modest shop frequented by locals. The 90-minute drive to Ubud flew by with conversation and first glimpses of the scenery. The pre-arranged fare of USD$26 was settled without any fuss, setting a reassuring tone for the journey.

We stayed at **Kubu Tropis Hotel**. Upon entering, the staff's genuine smiles and cool towels instantly washed away the fatigue of travel. The hotel is small but thoughtfully designed: we once happened to see an employee meticulously scrubbing the pool, no wonder the water was always crystal clear. The room was filled with a relaxing aromatherapy scent, and the open wardrobe and dedicated drying rack on the balcony were incredibly practical for travelers. The only minor drawback was the fixed showerhead, which rendered our own filtered showerhead useless. The hotel's location was excellent—a 15 to 20-minute walk to Ubud Palace, surrounded by restaurants and shops, making evening strolls and meals very convenient. This hotel became our comfortable base for exploring Ubud over the first three days.

## Core Three Days: Let the Pros Take You on a Deep Dive

![核心三日：交給專業的深度奔馳](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_00f2cd7be9f879ad828534427edb9114.jpg)

The next three days were the highlight of the trip. I entrusted the entire itinerary to a guide highly recommended in online travel groups—**Ani (Si Febri)** and her driver husband. She was so popular that I had to do things in reverse: before even buying my plane tickets in Taiwan, I contacted her to confirm her availability, like booking a star director's schedule.

This early trust paid off handsomely. Ani is not just a guide but a professional itinerary consultant. When I sent her my ideal schedule, she carefully reviewed it and offered practical advice: "Ubud city center gets very congested in the late afternoon, so move your sunset plans earlier." "The volcano sunrise and paragliding are very weather-dependent. Don't book tickets yet; we'll decide in the morning based on conditions, and I can buy them on-site for you." She always foresees blind spots that travelers miss and patiently explains the reasons. Moreover, she acts like an itinerary magician, proactively suggesting: "The road you're taking passes right by Tirta Empul Temple—it's very convenient. Want to stop by?" This flexibility and thoughtfulness added delightful surprises to our itinerary.

Our driver for these three days (arranged by Ani's team) was equally impressive. Since we were in a playful mood, we exceeded the ten-hour charter limit on two days (overtime fees were reasonable), but the driver remained smiling and uncomplaining, even helping us carry heavy luggage. Their dedication made us willingly give extra tips at the end of the trip as a direct thank you.

## Epilogue: The Blue of Nusa Lembongan and a Leisurely Return

At the end of our journey, we spent a day hopping over to **Nusa Lembongan**. We bought round-trip ferry tickets in advance on Klook (about USD$13 per person) and deliberately chose different ferry companies for a bit of variety. The island's azure skies and turquoise waters, along with the dramatic waves at "Devil's Tears," added a final splash of vibrant color to our Bali album.

https://youtube.com/shorts/exdiGPLkVjw

On the last day, we returned from Nusa Lembongan to **Sanur Harbor**. Remembering online tips that the harbor area has limited transport, we contacted Ani again to book a one-way transfer to **Beachwalk Shopping Center** in Kuta. This decision was spot-on. The driver waited punctually outside the pier, and we even asked him to make a quick detour to Bali Banana for last-minute shopping. The roughly 40-minute ride cost about USD$19, but it bought us the most precious thing when dragging luggage: ease and relaxation.

## Conclusion: More Than Just a Good Time

Six days flew by in an instant. Looking back on this trip, Bali's beauty lies not only in the spectacular landscapes of volcanoes, oceans, temples, and rice terraces, but also in the "people" we met along the way. The sincerity of hotel staff, the kindness of our driver, and the professionalism and thoughtfulness of Ani's team all wove together a warm safety net, allowing us to explore and feel without any worries.

If you ask me what the biggest takeaway was, I think it's learning the art of "entrusting." By handing over the itinerary to a trustworthy local expert, she used her knowledge and network to transform an ordinary trip into a deep, surprise-filled experience. We're already nostalgic and certain this won't be our last time. Bali, see you again; Ani, we'll count on you for the next trip!
