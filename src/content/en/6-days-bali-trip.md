> The following content is adapted from fellow traveler [Liao Yiwen](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/1CjoK9BEFX/)'s travelogue:

The moment the plane's tires touched down on Ngurah Rai Airport's runway, our six-day Bali adventure officially began. This was not just an escape from daily life, but a meticulously orchestrated sensory feast filled with human warmth. From the spiritual heart of Ubud in the rainforest to the majestic cliffs of Uluwatu, and finally floating in the transparent waters of Lembongan Island, we captured Bali's multifaceted beauty in six days. This travelogue will detail our itinerary and share the key to a smooth journey—a trustworthy local guide.

## Arrival in Ubud: A Lesson in Trust Starting at the Airport

At 1:30 PM on December 12th, the tropical sun warmly embraced us as we stepped off the plane. Exiting customs, we immediately spotted the hotel's driver holding a sign with our names. We had pre-arranged currency exchange through the hotel, and the driver was well-informed. As we drove away from the airport, his first words weren't small talk but a valuable local tip: 'You'll see many currency exchange places along the way. Remember, don't go into the ones with exceptionally good rates.' His sincere eyes and simple English explanation instantly eased our anxieties in a foreign land. Following his recommendation, we successfully exchanged currency at a modest shop frequented by locals. The ninety-minute drive to Ubud flew by with casual conversation and our first glimpses of the scenic route. The pre-agreed USD$26 fare set a reassuring tone for the journey.

We stayed at **Kubu Tropis Hotel**. Upon arrival, the staff's genuine smiles and cold towels immediately washed away the fatigue of travel. Though small, the hotel exuded thoughtfulness: we once witnessed staff meticulously scrubbing the pool, explaining why the water was always crystal clear. The room was filled with relaxing essential oil aromas, and the open wardrobe and balcony clothesline were incredibly practical for travelers. The only downside was the fixed showerhead, rendering our portable filtered showerhead useless. The hotel's prime location allowed us to reach Ubud Palace in a fifteen to twenty-minute walk, with numerous restaurants and shops nearby, making evening strolls for food particularly convenient. This hotel became our comfortable base for exploring Ubud over the next three days.

## Core Three Days: Deep Exploration with Professionals

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_00f2cd7be9f879ad828534427edb9114.jpg)

The next three days were the highlight of this trip. I entrusted my itinerary entirely to **Ani (Si Febri)**, a highly recommended tour guide from online travel communities, and her husband, who served as our private driver. Her popularity was such that I had to 'go against the grain'—before even purchasing my plane tickets in Taiwan, I contacted her to confirm her availability, much like booking a slot with a star director.

This early trust paid off handsomely. Ani wasn’t just a tour guide; she was a professional itinerary consultant. When I sent her my ideal schedule, she carefully evaluated it and offered practical advice: 'Ubud gets very congested in the evening, so we should leave earlier for the sunset.' 'The volcano sunrise and paragliding are highly weather-dependent, so don’t rush to book tickets. We’ll decide on the morning itself, and I can help you purchase them on-site.' She always anticipated blind spots that travelers might miss and patiently explained her reasoning. Moreover, she transformed into an itinerary magician, suggesting: 'The road you’re taking will pass by Tirta Empul Temple—it’s on the way, would you like to stop by?' Her flexibility and thoughtfulness added delightful surprises to our trip’s framework.

The drivers arranged by Ani's team over these three days were equally commendable. As we were in high spirits, we exceeded the standard 10-hour car rental period on two days (with reasonable overtime charges), but the drivers always maintained their smiles, never complained, and even proactively helped us with our heavy luggage. Their professionalism made us willingly offer extra tips at the end of our journey as a direct expression of our gratitude.

## Epilogue: The Blue of Nusa Lembongan and the Ease of the Return Journey

At the end of our journey, we spent a day hopping over to **Nusa Lembongan**. We pre-purchased round-trip ferry tickets on Klook (around USD$13 per person) and deliberately chose different ferry companies to add some variety to our experience. The island's azure seas and skies, along with the surging waves at 'Devil's Tears,' added the final vibrant stroke to our Bali album.

https://youtube.com/shorts/exdiGPLkVjw

On the last day, we returned from Nusa Lembongan to **Sanur Port**. Heeding online advice about the inconvenient transportation around the port, we contacted Ani again to arrange a one-way transfer service to **Beachwalk Shopping Center** in Kuta. This decision proved to be absolutely correct. The driver was waiting punctually outside the pier, and we even asked him to make a detour to Bali Banana for our final shopping. The approximately 40-minute ride cost around USD$19, but what we gained was the most precious 'leisurely ease' while dragging our luggage.

## Conclusion: More Than Just One Beautiful Experience

Six days passed in the blink of an eye. Looking back on this journey, the beauty of Bali lies not only in the feast of landscapes—volcanoes, oceans, temples, and rice terraces—but also in the 'people' we encountered along the way. The sincerity of the hotel staff, the kindness of the drivers, and the professionalism and thoughtfulness of Ani's team collectively wove a warm safety net, allowing us to explore and feel without any worries.

If you ask me what the biggest takeaway was? I think it was learning the art of 'entrustment.' By handing over our itinerary to a trustworthy local expert, she was able to use her knowledge and network to transform an ordinary trip into a deep, surprise-filled experience. We have already started to miss it and are certain this won't be the last time. Bali, until we meet again; Ani, we'll be counting on you for our next trip!
