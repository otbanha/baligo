**Bottom Line First**: For a six-day Bali itinerary, I recommend the route 'Ubud (3 days) → Uluwatu → Nusa Lembongan.' The drive from the airport to Ubud takes about **90 minutes** (the community recommends a private car for around USD$26). In Ubud, stay at **Kubu Tropis** (a 15-20 minute walk to the Ubud Palace). For currency exchange, be sure to avoid **street-side money changers with suspiciously great rates**; instead, look for humble shops that locals also use. The key is to book a reputable local guide in advance (e.g., popular guides need to have their schedules confirmed early), which can greatly enhance the flexibility and peace of mind of your trip.

The moment the plane's tires touched down on the runway at Ngurah Rai Airport, our six-day Bali adventure officially began. This was more than just an escape from daily life; it was a meticulously orchestrated, heartwarming sensory feast. From the spiritual heart of Ubud in the rainforest to the dramatic cliffs of Uluwatu, and finally drifting through the crystal-clear waters of Nusa Lembongan, we spent six days capturing the many faces of Bali. This travelogue will detail every step of our journey and share the key to a smooth and carefree trip: a local guide you can truly trust.

## Arriving in Ubud: A Lesson in Trust from the Airport

At 1:30 PM on December 12th, the tropical sun warmly embraced us as we stepped off the plane. Clearing customs, we immediately spotted the driver sent by our hotel, holding a sign with our names. Before the trip, we had asked the hotel to arrange for currency exchange, and the driver was well aware. As the car left the airport, his first words weren't small talk but a valuable piece of local advice: 'You'll see many money changers along the way. Remember, if the rate looks too good, don't go in.' His eyes were sincere as he explained the risks in simple English. This kindness at our first meeting instantly dispelled the unease of being in a foreign land. Following his recommendation, we exchanged money at a humble shop frequented by locals. The 90-minute drive to Ubud flew by as we chatted and took in the scenery for the first time. The pre-negotiated fare of USD$26 was settled without any issues, setting a reassuring tone for the entire journey.

We stayed at **Kubu Tropis Hotel**. Upon entering, the staff's genuine smiles and cool towels immediately washed away the fatigue of travel. The hotel is small but thoughtfully designed: we once happened to witness an employee meticulously scrubbing the pool, which explained why the water was always crystal clear. The room was filled with a relaxing aromatherapy scent, and the open wardrobe and dedicated drying rack on the balcony were incredibly practical for travelers. The only minor drawback was the fixed showerhead, which made our own filtered showerhead useless. The hotel's location was excellent, just a 15 to 20-minute walk to the Ubud Palace, surrounded by restaurants and shops, making evening strolls for food very convenient. This hotel became our comfortable base for exploring the Ubud area for the first three days.

## The Core Three Days: Let the Experts Take You on a Deep Dive

![核心三日：交給專業的深度奔馳](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_00f2cd7be9f879ad828534427edb9114.jpg)

The next three days were the highlight of the trip. I entrusted the entire itinerary to a guide highly recommended in online travel groups—**Ani (Si Febri)** and her husband, the driver. She was so popular that I had to take a 'reverse approach': before even buying my plane tickets in Taiwan, I contacted her to confirm her availability, like booking a star director's schedule.

This early trust paid off handsomely. Ani is more than just a guide; she's a professional itinerary consultant. When I sent her my ideal schedule, she would carefully assess it and offer practical advice: 'Ubud town gets very congested in the late afternoon, so you need to leave earlier for the sunset.' 'The volcano sunrise and paragliding are very weather-dependent. Don't book tickets in a hurry; let's decide in the morning based on the conditions. I can buy them for you on the spot.' She always foresees blind spots that travelers can't see and patiently explains the reasons. Moreover, she acts like an itinerary magician, proactively suggesting: 'The road you're taking will pass right by Tirta Empul Temple; it's very convenient. Would you like to stop by?' This flexibility and thoughtfulness added delightful surprises to our itinerary.

Our driver for these three days (arranged by Ani's team) was equally impressive. Since we got carried away with fun, we exceeded the ten-hour base limit on two days (overtime fees were reasonable), yet the driver remained smiling and never complained, even helping us carry heavy luggage. Their dedication made us willingly give extra tips at the end of the trip as a direct thank you.

## Epilogue: The Blue of Nusa Lembongan and a Leisurely Return

As the trip wound down, we spent a day hopping over to **Nusa Lembongan**. We booked round-trip ferry tickets on Klook in advance (around USD$13 per person) and deliberately chose different ferry companies for a bit of variety. The island's azure skies and sea, along with the dramatic waves at Devil's Tears, added a final splash of vibrant color to our Bali album.

https://youtube.com/shorts/exdiGPLkVjw

On the last day, we returned from Nusa Lembongan to **Sanur Harbor**. Remembering online tips that the harbor area has poor transport connections, we contacted Ani again to book a one-way transfer to **Beachwalk Shopping Center** in Kuta. It was the perfect decision. The driver was waiting punctually outside the pier, and we even asked him to make a detour to Bali Banana for some last-minute shopping. The roughly 40-minute ride cost about USD$19, but what we gained was the precious luxury of traveling with luggage—calm and unhurried.

## Conclusion: More Than Just a Good Time

Six days flew by in a flash. Looking back on this journey, Bali's beauty lies not only in the spectacular landscapes of volcanoes, oceans, temples, and rice terraces, but also in the people we met along the way. The sincerity of hotel staff, the kindness of our driver, and the professionalism and thoughtfulness of Ani's team all wove together a warm safety net, allowing us to explore and feel without any worries.

If you ask me what the biggest takeaway was, I think it's learning the art of 'entrusting.' Handing over the itinerary to a trustworthy local expert, who then uses her knowledge and network to transform an ordinary trip into a deep, surprise-filled experience. We're already missing it, and we're sure this won't be the last time. Bali, see you again; Ani, we'll leave the next trip to you too!
