The moment the plane's tires gently touched the runway at Ngurah Rai Airport, our six-day Bali adventure officially began. This was not just an escape from daily life, but a meticulously orchestrated sensory feast filled with human warmth. From the spiritual heart of Ubud in the rainforest to the majestic cliffs of Uluwatu, and finally floating in the transparent waters of Lembongan Island, we captured the multifaceted beauty of Bali in six days. This travelogue will detail the highlights of our itinerary and share the key to a smooth journey—a trustworthy local guide.

## Arrival in Ubud: A Lesson in Trust from the Airport

At 1:30 PM on December 12th, the tropical sun warmly embraced us as we stepped off the plane. Exiting customs, we immediately spotted the hotel's driver holding a sign with our names. We had informed the hotel beforehand about needing to exchange currency, and the driver was well-prepared. As we drove away from the airport, his first words were not small talk but a valuable local tip: 'You'll see many currency exchange places along the way. Remember, if the rate seems too good to be true, don’t go in.' His sincere eyes and simple English explanation instantly eased our anxiety in this unfamiliar land. Following his recommendation, we successfully exchanged currency at a modest shop frequented by locals. The ninety-minute drive to Ubud passed quickly with casual conversation and our first glimpses of the scenic route. The agreed-upon fare of USD$26 was hassle-free, setting a reassuring tone for the journey.

We stayed at **Kubu Tropis Hotel**. Upon arrival, the staff's genuine smiles and cold towels immediately washed away the fatigue of travel. Though the hotel is small, it exudes thoughtfulness: we once witnessed staff meticulously scrubbing the pool, which explains why the water is always crystal clear. The room was filled with relaxing essential oil scents, and the open wardrobe and balcony drying rack were extremely practical for travelers. The only downside was the fixed showerhead, rendering our portable filtered showerhead useless. The hotel's prime location is a fifteen to twenty-minute walk from Ubud Palace, surrounded by restaurants and shops, making evening strolls for food particularly convenient. This hotel became our comfortable base for exploring Ubud over the first three days.

## Core Three Days: Professional Deep Exploration

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_00f2cd7be9f879ad828534427edb9114.jpg)

The next three days were the highlight of this trip. I entrusted my entire itinerary to **Ani (Si Febri)**, a highly recommended guide from online travel communities, and her husband, who served as our private driver. Her popularity was such that I had to 'go against the grain'—before even purchasing my flight tickets in Taiwan, I contacted her to confirm her availability, much like booking a slot with a celebrity director.

This early trust paid off immensely. Ani wasn’t just a guide; she was a professional itinerary consultant. When I sent her my ideal schedule, she carefully evaluated it and offered practical advice: 'Ubud gets very congested in the evening; we should leave earlier for the sunset viewing.' 'The volcano sunrise and paragliding are highly weather-dependent; let’s not rush to book tickets. We’ll decide on the morning itself, and I can help you purchase them on-site.' She always anticipated blind spots that travelers might miss and patiently explained the reasons. Moreover, she transformed into an itinerary magician, suggesting: 'The road you’re taking will pass by Tirta Empul Temple; it’s on the way. Would you like to stop by?' This flexibility and thoughtfulness added delightful surprises to our trip.

The drivers arranged by Ani's team over these three days were equally commendable. Our adventurous spirit led us to exceed the standard 10-hour car hire limit on two days (with reasonable overtime charges), yet the drivers remained cheerful and complaint-free, even offering to carry our heavy luggage. Their professionalism prompted us to willingly give extra tips at the end of our journey as a direct expression of our gratitude.

## Epilogue: The Blue of Nusa Lembongan and the Ease of the Return Journey

As our journey neared its end, we spent a day exploring **Nusa Lembongan**. We booked round-trip ferry tickets via Klook for about USD$13 per person, deliberately choosing different ferry companies to vary our experience. The island's azure seas and the dramatic waves at 'Devil's Tears' added the final vivid strokes to our Bali album.

https://youtube.com/shorts/exdiGPLkVjw

On our final day, we returned from Nusa Lembongan to **Sanur Harbor**. Heeding online advice about the inconvenient transport around the harbor, we contacted Ani again to arrange a one-way transfer to **Beachwalk Shopping Center** in Kuta. This decision proved to be spot on. The driver was punctually waiting outside the pier, and we even asked him to make a detour to Bali Banana for some last-minute shopping. The approximately 40-minute ride cost us about USD$20, but the ease it brought us, especially with our luggage, was priceless.

## Conclusion: More Than Just a One-Time Wonder

Six days passed in the blink of an eye. Reflecting on this journey, the beauty of Bali lies not only in its volcanic landscapes, oceans, temples, and rice terraces but also in the people we met along the way. The sincerity of the hotel staff, the kindness of the drivers, and the professionalism and thoughtfulness of Ani's team wove a warm safety net, allowing us to explore and feel without any worries.

If asked what my greatest takeaway was, I'd say it's mastering the art of 'entrustment.' Handing over our itinerary to a trustworthy local expert, she could use her knowledge and network to transform an ordinary trip into a surprising and profound experience. We've already started to miss it and are certain this won't be our last time. Bali, until we meet again; Ani, we'll be counting on you for our next adventure!
