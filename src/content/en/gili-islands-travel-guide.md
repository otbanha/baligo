For travelers around the world, Bali feels like heaven on earth—a place that seems to have everything one could dream of. Yet even so, for those of us with an insatiable thirst for exploration, this beautiful island can still leave a lingering desire for more. That's why today I'm introducing a hidden gem not far from Bali, yet even more enchanting and dreamlike: the Gili Islands!

## About the Gili Islands

![吉利群島  蕭雅心提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_548da6ca68b9022e537bcba1e1b85b9d.jpg)

The Gili Islands consist of three small islands: lively Gili Trawangan, serene Gili Meno, and vibrant Gili Air. Locals often refer to them simply as Gili T, Gili M, and Gili A. From Padangbai port on Bali's east coast, a two-hour speedboat ride brings you to this secluded paradise.

Once you step onto the pristine white sand beaches of the Gili Islands, you'll be completely captivated by the stunning scenery. The entire island is surrounded by the vast blue Indian Ocean, serene and untouched. Motorized vehicles are completely banned on the island, leaving only three simple yet leisurely modes of transport: walking, bicycles, and horse-drawn carts.

![吉利群島交通方式只有步行/腳踏車/馬車](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2e07117bb37de261bfa7f139e2979100.jpg)

Transportation on the Gili Islands is limited to walking, cycling, or horse-drawn carts.

Everywhere you look, you'll find tangled palm groves, winding paths, and beachfront bar huts, all blending into the island's unique tropical vibe. There are three beautiful islands here, but if I had to pick just one, I'd unhesitatingly recommend Gili T.

## **Gili T**

Gili T, known as the party island, is the largest of the three, brimming with energy and enthusiasm. It's just a two-hour boat ride from Bali. Bicycles and horse-drawn carts are the main modes of transport here.

## **Fun and Experiences on the Island**

On this enchanting white sand island, you can spend a few blissful days. Although it takes some time to get here from Bali, once you set foot on land, you'll find it's truly one of a kind. Most visitors are from Europe and America, adding to its unique charm.

![吉利群島  蕭雅心提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2bed84a8a4da6caa34e8f5f9a0b1041b.jpg)

The allure of Gili T lies in its diversity. You can easily walk or cycle around the entire island—it's really small but well-equipped. Here, you'll find an outdoor cinema, pool bars, various restaurants, a yoga center, a gym, a fish market, cooking classes, diving activities, massage and hair salons, and more. The locals are always warm and welcoming, and there's even a monthly full moon party, sure to satisfy all your cravings for a vacation adventure.

**What Are Magic Mushrooms?**

"Magic mushrooms" refer to varieties of mushrooms with high levels of psilocybin, widely known as psychedelic mushrooms. They are primarily used as recreational drugs, with effects including euphoria, altered thought processes, closed- and open-eye visuals (hallucinations), changes in the perception of time, and spiritual experiences.

Hallucinations are caused by a substance called psilocybin, which activates serotonin receptors in your brain. Some say it's similar to LSD, just milder in effect.

**How Long Do the Effects of Magic Mushrooms Last?**

Like many psychedelics, the effects of magic mushrooms vary from person to person, so experiences can differ greatly from one user to another.

The effects last anywhere from 3 to 8 hours, depending on dosage, preparation method, and individual metabolism. The first 3 to 4 hours are often called the "peak," during which users experience the most vivid hallucinations and distortions of reality.

**Are Magic Mushrooms Dangerous?**

Because magic mushrooms can be found in the wild, they are often seen as a "natural drug" or a safer alternative. To some extent, this is true.

Magic mushrooms have minimal side effects. Unlike cocaine or cannabis, they do not increase the risk of mental health issues. They are also far less harmful to our internal organs than heroin or cocaine. In fact, psilocybin mushrooms have been found to benefit people suffering from depression.

The good news is that magic mushrooms are not addictive, meaning you won't become dependent on them, and you won't experience withdrawal symptoms if you stop taking them after long-term regular use.

The bad news is that the risk of a 'bad trip' still exists (read on to learn how to prevent it). Or you might accidentally eat a mushroom that looks similar but is a completely different species. For example, the angel's trumpet mushroom is highly toxic and can damage your kidneys or be fatal.

**Are Magic Mushrooms Legal?**

In Indonesia, magic mushrooms are illegal. However, the Gili Islands are considered a safe haven for seeking the euphoria induced by mushrooms. Bars openly sell magic mushrooms on their outdoor menus without fear.

Apart from their own 'island security,' the only police presence on the Gili Islands is at the harbor. I'm not sure if these enforcers are bribed to turn a blind eye, but as far as I know, no one has been arrested for selling or using magic mushrooms. Still, we don't know how long this will last.

On other islands, such as Bali and Lombok, you might also find them, but you need to be very, very careful. The person trying to sell you drugs could be an undercover cop or a scammer. Either way, it could land you in serious trouble.

**Where to Find Magic Mushrooms on Gili Trawangan**

I believe almost every bar, restaurant, or hostel on Gili Trawangan sells magic mushrooms—if not on the menu, then under the table.

Our top recommendation is a beach bar on the west coast, in front of the Sunset Beach Mediterranean restaurant, marked with 'coco xiao' on the blackboard in the upper left corner.
