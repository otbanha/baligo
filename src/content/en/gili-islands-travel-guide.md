## About the Gili Islands

![吉利群島  蕭雅心提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_548da6ca68b9022e537bcba1e1b85b9d.jpg)

The Gili Islands consist of three small islands—lively Gili Trawangan, serene Gili Meno, and vibrant Gili Air. Locals often call them Gili T, Gili M, and Gili A. From Padangbai Harbor on Bali's east coast, a two-hour speedboat ride will take you to this secluded paradise.

The moment you step onto the powdery white sands of the Gili Islands, you'll be utterly captivated by this stunning paradise. Surrounded by the vast, azure Indian Ocean, the islands feel both serene and untouched. Motorized vehicles are completely banned here, leaving only three simple yet leisurely modes of transport: walking, cycling, and horse-drawn carts.

![吉利群島交通方式只有步行/腳踏車/馬車](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2e07117bb37de261bfa7f139e2979100.jpg)

Transportation on the Gili Islands is limited to walking, cycling, and horse-drawn carts.

Everywhere you look, you'll find tangled palm groves, winding paths, and beachfront bar huts, all blending together to create the islands' unique tropical vibe. There are three beautiful islands here, but if I had to pick just one, I'd unhesitatingly recommend Gili T (Gili Trawangan).

## **Gili T**

Gili T, known as the party island, is the largest of the three, brimming with energy and enthusiasm. It's just a two-hour boat ride from Bali. The main ways to get around are by bicycle and horse-drawn cart.

## **Fun and Experiences on the Island**

On this enchanting white-sand islet, you can spend a few blissful days unwinding. Although it takes some time to get here from Bali, once you set foot on land, you'll find it's a world apart. Most visitors are travelers from Europe and America, adding to its unique charm.

![吉利群島  蕭雅心提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2bed84a8a4da6caa34e8f5f9a0b1041b.jpg)

Gili Islands | Photo by Hsiao Ya-hsin

The charm of Gili T lies in its diversity. You can easily walk or cycle around the entire island—it's truly tiny but well-equipped. Here, you'll find an outdoor cinema, pool bars, various restaurants, a yoga center, a gym, a fish market, cooking classes, diving activities, massage and hair salons, and more. The locals are always warm and welcoming, and there's even a monthly full moon party, sure to satisfy all your cravings for adventure and relaxation.

## **Scuba Diving and Snorkeling**

If you love scuba diving, Gili T is your paradise. While the waters here aren't rich in coral or marine ecosystems, you can get up close with a variety of sea creatures—even spot sea turtles while snorkeling, and sharks when diving.

## PS. About Magic Mushrooms on the Gili Islands

**What Are Magic Mushrooms?**

“Magic mushrooms” refer to varieties of mushrooms with high levels of psilocybin, commonly known as psychedelic mushrooms. They are primarily used as recreational drugs, with effects including euphoria, altered thought processes, closed- and open-eye visuals (hallucinations), changes in time perception, and spiritual experiences.

Hallucinations are caused by a substance called psilocybin, which activates serotonin receptors in your brain. Some say it’s similar to LSD, only milder in effect.

**How Long Do the Effects of Magic Mushrooms Last?**

Like many psychedelics, the effects of magic mushrooms vary from person to person, so experiences can differ greatly from one user to another.

The effects last anywhere from 3 to 8 hours, depending on dosage, preparation method, and individual metabolism. The first 3 to 4 hours are often referred to as the “peak,” during which users experience the most vivid hallucinations and distortions of reality.

**Are Magic Mushrooms Dangerous?**

Because magic mushrooms can be found in the wild, they are often perceived as a “natural drug” or a safer alternative. To some extent, this is true.

Magic mushrooms have very mild side effects. Unlike cocaine or marijuana, they do not increase the risk of mental health issues. They are also far less harmful to our internal organs than heroin or cocaine. In fact, psilocybin mushrooms have been found to benefit people suffering from depression.

The good news is that magic mushrooms are not addictive, meaning you won't become dependent on them, and you won't experience withdrawal symptoms if you stop taking them after long-term regular use.

The bad news is that there is still a risk of a 'bad trip' (read on to learn how to prevent this). Or you might accidentally consume a mushroom that looks similar but is a completely different species. For example, the angel's trumpet mushroom is highly toxic and can damage your kidneys or be fatal.

**Are magic mushrooms legal?**

In Indonesia, magic mushrooms are illegal under the law. However, the Gili Islands are considered a safe haven for seeking mushroom-induced euphoria. Bars openly sell magic mushrooms on their outdoor menus without fear.

Apart from their own 'island security,' the only police presence on the Gili Islands is at the harbor. I'm not sure if these law enforcers are bribed to turn a blind eye, but as far as I know, no one has been arrested for selling or using magic mushrooms. Still, we don't know how long this will last.

On some other islands, such as Bali and Lombok, you can also find them, but you must be very, very careful. People trying to sell you drugs could be undercover police or scammers. Either way, it will land you in a lot of trouble.

**Where to find magic mushrooms on Gili Trawangan**

I believe almost every bar, restaurant, and hostel on Gili Trawangan sells magic mushrooms—if not on the menu, then under the table.

We highly recommend the beach bar to the left front of the Sunset Beach Mediterranean restaurant on the west coast, marked with a 'coco xiao' sign on the blackboard in the top left corner.
