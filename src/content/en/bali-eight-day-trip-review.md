This was our second visit to Bali this year. From October 11 to 18, we spent an unforgettable eight days and seven nights on this 'Island of the Gods.' We chose to return because we were deeply captivated by Bali's cultural charm, natural landscapes, and authentic cuisine. During this trip, we stayed at three different hotels, each with its own unique style—from the bustling Kuta Beach area to the tranquil rice fields of Ubud, and then to the laid-back shores of Jimbaran. Each place left us with wonderful memories.

A special thanks to the group admins and fellow members who offered advice and care when my husband suffered from severe diarrhea during the trip—your support helped us get through the ordeal (I'll share our solution for overcoming Bali Belly at the end). This warmth became one of the most precious takeaways from the journey.

## Day 2: Tanah Lot Temple Visit and Leisurely Time in Seminyak

### Morning: Tanah Lot Temple

After enjoying a hearty breakfast at the hotel, we drove to one of Bali's most iconic landmarks—Tanah Lot Temple. This 16th-century Hindu temple sits atop a massive offshore rock, accessible on foot during low tide but surrounded by water at high tide, creating a picturesque scene. Standing on the shore, watching the waves crash against the reef as the temple glistens in the sunlight, the awe is indescribable. I recommend visiting in the afternoon, as the temple at sunset is breathtakingly beautiful.

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Travel Must-See: Tanah Lot Temple Detailed Guide with Tides and Nearby Attractions](/en/blog/bali-tanah-lot-guide/)

### Midday: Dining and Shopping at Seminyak Square

After leaving Tanah Lot Temple, we headed to the trendy Seminyak area. This neighborhood is packed with boutique shops, restaurants, and cafes, making it one of Bali's more modern districts. We strolled around Seminyak Square, enjoying a relaxed shopping experience. The area is filled with designer boutiques where you can find unique clothing, accessories, and home decor—perfect for travelers seeking distinctive souvenirs.

## Day 3: From Kuta to Ubud—A Different Side of Bali

### Morning: Pool Time at the Hotel

No rush today. After a leisurely breakfast at the hotel, we decided to make the most of the pool facilities. Splashing around and relaxing under the Bali sun—this is what a vacation should feel like. Lounging by the pool, we soaked up the sun and embraced the island’s warmth.

### Midday: Check-Out and Massage

After checking out at noon, we headed to a nearby spa for a traditional Balinese massage. Bali is famous for its essential oil massages, and the therapists were professional with just the right pressure. After over an hour of treatment, all our travel fatigue melted away, leaving us completely relaxed in both body and mind.

### Afternoon: Massimo Italian Restaurant & Ice Cream

After the massage, we went to Massimo Italian Restaurant for lunch. This restaurant is well-known in Bali, serving authentic Italian cuisine. The dishes were true to their origins, especially the handmade pasta and pizza, which made us feel like we were in Italy. Don't forget to try their ice cream after the meal—it's smooth and creamy, the perfect dessert for a hot day.

### Grand Lucky Supermarket Shopping

**Shopping Recommendation: Grand Lucky Supermarket**

On our way to Ubud, we made a special detour to Grand Lucky Supermarket for some shopping. This supermarket is a popular spot among tourists for buying souvenirs, with transparent and reasonable prices, so you don't have to worry about being overcharged as a tourist. The store offers a wide variety of products, from Balinese coffee, spices, cookies, and snacks to everyday essentials. We picked up quite a few local specialties here to bring back to Taiwan and share with friends and family.

### Check-in at KAAMALA Resort & Spa Ubud

## Day 4: Deep Dive into Ubud, Embracing the Essence of Balinese Culture

### Morning: Leisurely Time at the Hotel

On our first morning in Ubud, we chose to lounge around the hotel, fully enjoying the private space of the villa and the surrounding rural scenery. Sitting on the balcony, gazing at the rice paddies and listening to the distant chirping of insects and birds, this sense of tranquility away from the hustle and bustle was exactly what we came to Ubud for.

### Midday: Lunch at Bebek Tepi Sawah

**Restaurant Recommendation: Bebek Tepi Sawah**

![中午:Bebek Tepi Sawah 午餐](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_18d5fd887059ea02f0b30f3666e5d864.jpg)

Around midday, we headed to Bebek Tepi Sawah for lunch. The restaurant's name literally translates to 'Duck by the Rice Fields,' and it truly lives up to its name, nestled beside beautiful rice paddies. The restaurant is decorated in a traditional Balinese style, exuding a strong local atmosphere. We chose an outdoor seat, enjoying the refreshing rural views while savoring authentic Balinese cuisine. A gentle breeze carried the scent of rice, making this lunch a truly relaxing experience. The restaurant's signature grilled duck dish is not to be missed—the meat is tender, and the spices are perfectly balanced.

### Afternoon: Bali Reptile Park

**Attraction Recommendation: Bali Reptile Park**

![下午:Bali Reptile Park 爬蟲公園](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_be260f863fa3cf1d7f49877c02f31826.jpg)

After lunch, we visited Bali Reptile Park, a must-see for reptile enthusiasts! The entrance fee includes a full English-guided tour by the park staff, which is one of the park's biggest highlights. The guides are highly professional and enthusiastic, providing detailed explanations of each reptile's habits and characteristics. Their lively and engaging commentary is so infectious that even those who are initially a bit wary of reptiles will be won over by their passion.

What made it truly special was that during the guided tour, we had the chance to gently touch several docile reptiles, including pythons and lizards. The staff ensured safety first and taught us the proper way to handle them. This close encounter gave us a whole new appreciation and understanding of these creatures. The entire visit takes about 1.5 to 2 hours, making it perfect for a family outing.

### Evening: Stroll Through Ubud Market

After leaving the reptile park, we headed to Ubud Market. This is the liveliest area in Ubud and the best place to shop for Balinese handicrafts. The market is packed with all kinds of goods, from traditional clothing, wood carvings, silver jewelry, woven bags, to paintings. Prices here are negotiable, so it's a good idea to start bargaining at about one-third of the asking price—enjoying the haggling is part of the market experience. Tucked away in the alleys are also many cafes and small eateries, perfect for taking a break with a cup of Balinese coffee and soaking in Ubud's relaxed vibe.

## Day 5: Exploring Mount Batur Volcano and Traditional Villages

### Morning: Kintamani & Ritatkala Café

**Attraction & Restaurant Recommendation: Kintamani & Ritatkala Café**

![上午:金塔瑪尼與 Ritatkala Café](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3432f10b8a1acb758145140d33e88ac0.jpg)

Today we drove to one of Bali's famous volcanic scenic areas—Kintamani. Here, you can take in the breathtaking views of Mount Batur and Lake Batur, making it a must-visit natural attraction on the island.

We chose to dine at Ritatkala Café, a scenic coffee shop with unbeatable views. I highly recommend grabbing an outdoor seat, facing the volcano and lake, to enjoy your meal while soaking in nature's masterpiece. The chicken wings here are a standout—crispy on the outside, juicy on the inside, and perfectly paired with a special dipping sauce. Sipping hot coffee in the cool mountain air while gazing at the distant volcano is an indescribably refreshing experience.

### Afternoon: Penglipuran Village

**Attraction Recommendation: Penglipuran Village**

![下午:Penglipuran Village 傳統村落](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_534f807ca0ff379512580af984f596cc.jpg)

After leaving Kintamani, we headed to Penglipuran Village, a well-preserved traditional Balinese settlement. The village features uniform architecture that beautifully showcases Bali's traditional design and cultural heritage. What makes this place truly special is that visitors can step inside the homes—a dream come true for anyone curious about the interiors of traditional Balinese houses.

The village streets are spotless and lined with bamboo groves, creating a serene atmosphere as you stroll through. Some homes also sell handmade crafts or local specialties, and prices are negotiable, so don't hesitate to chat with the owners. We bought popsicles from one family, and the host was incredibly warm, inviting us to rest in their courtyard. Sitting in a traditional Balinese compound, feeling the gentle breeze and soaking in the tranquility, it was so relaxing that we didn't want to leave. The slow pace of life here, filled with genuine hospitality, makes it an ideal spot to experience Bali's traditional culture.

## Day 6: Southern Coastline Exploration and Cliff Views

### Morning: Pool Time at the Hotel

On our last morning in Ubud, we returned to the hotel's infinity pool to soak in the rice field views one last time. Floating in the pool, gazing out at the pastoral scenery, I felt a deep sense of peace and gratitude.

### After Checkout: Uluwatu Cliff Road

![中午退房後:烏魯瓦圖斷崖公路](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a123f697405c668b110a8f2b12af913f.jpg)

After checking out, we drove south to the Uluwatu area of Bali. Cruising along the coastal cliff road, with the deep blue Indian Ocean on one side and steep limestone cliffs on the other, the breathtaking views opened up our hearts. This stretch of road is considered one of Bali's most scenic coastlines, with several viewpoints perfect for photo stops—don't miss them.

### Afternoon: Roosterfish Afternoon Tea at Pandawa Beach

![下午:Pandawa Beach Roosterfish 下午茶](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4cfd7bde880edf48f6923248b34146d2.jpg)

We enjoyed afternoon tea at Roosterfish restaurant on Pandawa Beach. Perched right by the beach, this spot offers stunning sea views. With a cold drink and light bites in hand, sitting on the breezy terrace watching waves crash onto the sand, it was a simple yet perfect afternoon. Pandawa Beach boasts clear waters and white sand, making it one of Bali's quieter beaches—ideal for travelers looking to escape the crowds.

### Evening: Uluwatu Temple

**Attraction Recommendation: Uluwatu Temple ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!**

![傍晚:烏魯瓦圖神廟](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_65cdfff2716506cd7615d0c7611c9c66.jpg)

In the late afternoon, we headed to Uluwatu Temple, a cliffside temple that is one of Bali's most important religious sites and one of the best spots to watch the sunset. Perched on a 70-meter-high cliff overlooking the vast Indian Ocean, it exudes a majestic aura.

**Important reminder: The monkeys here are incredibly mischievous and bold!** We witnessed tourists having their glasses snatched away by these monkeys. Through long-term interaction with visitors, they've learned how to quickly grab items like glasses, hats, and sunglasses, then 'demand' food in exchange. So, when visiting, be sure to secure your belongings—use a strap for your glasses, and avoid carrying shiny accessories or food to avoid attracting their attention.

**Quick Reference:** [Uluwatu Temple / Cliffside Travel Guide in Bali: Must-See Highlights and Practical Tips](/en/blog/uluwatu-temple-travel-guide/)

Despite the need to watch out for monkeys, the scenery at Uluwatu Temple is truly breathtaking. As the sun sets, golden light spills over the sea, creating a shimmering effect, and the temple appears even more sacred and solemn in the backlight. If time permits, stay to watch the traditional Kecak Fire Dance performance, a cultural feast blending dance, music, and drama against the sunset glow.

**Quick Reference:** [Kecak Fire Dance: A Must-See Spectacular Performance in Bali (with Booking Info)](/en/blog/kecak-fire-dance-bali/)

In the evening, we dined at Ulu Cliffhouse, another cliffside restaurant with stunning ocean views. Under the starry sky, listening to the waves, we enjoyed exquisite cuisine, perfectly capping off today's exciting itinerary.

## Day 7: Relaxed Time in Jimbaran and Crazy Shopping

### Check-in at Movenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran

**Accommodation Recommendation: Movenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran**

Today we arrived at our final hotel of the trip—Movenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran, marking our second stay at this resort. Perhaps because we were returning guests, we were lucky enough to be upgraded to a pool-access room, where we could jump straight into the pool from our balcony—a convenience that felt truly luxurious.

The resort boasts multiple pool facilities, including a children's splash area and a water volleyball court, and regularly hosts various water activities, making it ideal for families. The breakfast buffet is generous and diverse, offering everything from Asian dishes to Western fare, all of excellent quality. The biggest surprise was the complimentary Chocolate Happy Hour every afternoon, featuring an array of chocolate desserts and hot chocolate drinks—a paradise for sweet tooths!

Mövenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran is a luxurious 5-star resort in the Jimbaran area, offering a serene and romantic escape that's also perfect for families. Tucked away in a corner of the Samasta shopping complex, it's quite convenient for daily needs. And guess what? This shopping center is home to an amazing pork rib specialty restaurant! ### Important Reminders

### ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!

At attractions like Uluwatu Temple and the Sacred Monkey Forest where monkeys roam, be extra cautious! We witnessed tourists having their glasses snatched right off their faces. These monkeys are incredibly clever—they grab items and then 'negotiate' for their return. Recommendations:

- Secure glasses and sunglasses with straps
- Avoid wearing shiny accessories or carrying food
- Keep your bag zipped up tight
- Maintain a safe distance from monkeys; don't attempt to touch or feed them

### ⚠️ Don't Underestimate Bali Belly!—Our Painful Firsthand Experience

During this trip, my husband suffered a severe case of Bali Belly, and the whole ordeal was quite alarming. I'm sharing the details here so that anyone planning a trip to Bali can learn from our mistake.

**Night of Day 6: Symptoms Begin**

On the very first night we checked into the Movenpick hotel, my husband suddenly complained of stomach pain and needed to use the bathroom. That night, he made three trips to the toilet, tossing and turning without a wink of sleep. At the time, we thought it was just a mild case of adjusting to the local food—little did we know it was the calm before the storm.

**Morning of Day 7: A Sharp Turn for the Worse**

The next morning, my husband woke up with a splitting headache. I reached out and touched his forehead—he was burning up with a fever! Soon after, the diarrhea intensified, and he couldn't even leave the room to grab breakfast. I had to take our child to the restaurant on my own.

However, when we returned to our room after breakfast, I was utterly shocked by what I saw. My husband was sitting weakly on the toilet, his entire upper body slumped over the sink. He told me that due to severe stomach pain and constant urges to use the bathroom, he didn't dare leave the toilet for a second. Seeing him like this, our child thought he had fainted and burst into tears. In that moment, I truly panicked.

I immediately grabbed the backup medication I had brought from Taiwan, giving him pain relievers, fever reducers, and anti-diarrhea medicine, then let him rest. Two hours later, his fever had subsided, but the diarrhea showed no signs of easing. Realizing the seriousness of the situation, I decided to seek help at a local pharmacy.

**Seeking Help at the Pharmacy**

At the pharmacy, I described my husband's symptoms to the pharmacist. The pharmacist was very professional, recommending two types of medication (the ones in the photo) and explaining the dosage and precautions in detail. I quickly bought the medicine and returned to the hotel for my husband to take.

In the afternoon, I took our child to Krisna to shop for souvenirs—partly to distract him from worrying about his dad, and partly to avoid wasting our precious travel time. When we got back to the hotel, seeing that my husband's condition had slightly improved, we finally breathed a small sigh of relief.

**A Mistaken Dinner Attempt**

That evening, we went to Bebek Bengil, a restaurant next to the hotel, for dinner. My husband was very cautious, only daring to have clear soup and plain rice, thinking it would be safe. But unexpectedly, as soon as we returned to the room, his diarrhea started again. At this point, I was truly worried and didn't know what to do, so I went online to post in a travel group for help. I'm grateful to the group admins and kind members who offered us valuable advice and warm support late into the night, helping us feel less panicked in a foreign land.

**Day 8: The Tough Journey Back to Taiwan**

On the morning of the eighth day, I brought some plain toast from the hotel restaurant for my husband, but he was still suffering from diarrhea. I began to worry whether he could make it through the flight back to Taiwan, feeling very anxious. Thankfully, he said that as long as he could sit, the pain was still bearable.

And so, we nervously headed to the airport. On the plane, my husband didn't even dare eat the in-flight meal, enduring abdominal discomfort throughout the entire flight. Finally, we landed safely in Taipei, but even after returning home, he continued to experience stomach pain and diarrhea.

**Post-Return Medical Care and Recovery**

The next morning, we immediately went to the clinic. The doctor carefully inquired about the symptoms and progression, prescribing antidiarrheal medication and probiotics. After taking the medicine, the symptoms slowly began to ease, though intermittent abdominal pain and diarrhea persisted.

During recovery, my husband dared only to eat plain white toast. Once, he tried drinking some soup, but it triggered another bout of diarrhea, scaring him off from attempting any other foods. The entire ordeal lasted six days, with the diarrhea finally stopping completely on the sixth day.

**A Surprising 'Side Effect'**

This bout of Bali Belly caused my husband's weight to drop from 78 kg to 73 kg—a loss of 5 kg! While it's certainly not a healthy way to lose weight, it could be seen as a silver lining. He joked, 'When the stomach cramps hit, I felt like I was about to give birth!'

**Cause Analysis and Reflections**

Afterward, we carefully reviewed the entire trip. My child and I remained perfectly fine, while only my husband fell ill. We suspect the following possible causes:

1. **Ice Cube Issue**: At noon on day six, my husband bought a freshly brewed iced coffee packed with ice cubes at Pepito Supermarket. The source of ice in Bali is questionable, and it may have been the culprit.
2. **Hand Hygiene**: Men sometimes slack off and don't wash their hands thoroughly before meals. My child and I, however, were diligent about it. That evening, we ate pizza with our hands, increasing the risk of infection.
3. **Probiotic Protection**: Throughout the eight-day trip, my child and I took gut-friendly probiotics every morning, which might have helped safeguard our digestive health. (Note: This is purely personal speculation, not medical advice.)

Worth mentioning, this is the second time we've encountered Bali Belly this year. During our six-day trip to Bali in January, all three of us experienced varying degrees of diarrhea, and our child even had a fever. But those symptoms were milder and resolved quickly after medication. We never expected my husband's case to be so severe, giving us a real taste of Bali Belly's power.

**Advice for All Travelers**

After this painful experience, I'd like to offer the following advice to friends planning a trip to Bali:

1. **Prepare ample gastrointestinal medication before departure**: Include anti-diarrheal medicine, fever reducers and painkillers, probiotics, and stomach remedies. Don't take chances—better to have them and not need them.
2. **Absolutely avoid drinks with ice**: This can't be stressed enough! Avoid any beverages with ice from street stalls, freshly made drinks at supermarkets, or iced drinks at restaurants. Also, make sure bottled water has a sealed cap.
3. **Wash hands thoroughly before meals**: Use soap to wash your hands properly, or use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer. Don't skip this step just because it's inconvenient.
4. **Consider taking probiotics daily**: Start taking them a few days before your trip and continue throughout your journey—it may help protect your digestive system.
5. **Be cautious with street food**: Check the hygiene conditions; if the environment looks unclean, it's better to skip it.
6. **Don't tough it out if symptoms are severe**: If you experience high fever, severe dehydration, or persistent diarrhea, seek help from a local pharmacy or see a doctor immediately. Don't wait until the next day like we did.
7. **Travel insurance is crucial**: Make sure your insurance covers overseas medical expenses, so you can ease the financial burden if you need to see a doctor.
