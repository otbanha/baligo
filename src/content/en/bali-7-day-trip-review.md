## 1️⃣ **Exchanging Indonesian Rupiah**

During our trip, each of us exchanged about USD$320, which turned out to be just enough for the 7-day, 6-night itinerary, even allowing us to buy quite a few souvenirs. Therefore, I recommend exchanging between USD$300 to USD$350 initially, supplemented by credit card payments. Many restaurants and massage parlors accept credit cards, offering flexibility in managing your budget.

> [How Much Cash Should You Prepare for a Bali Trip?](/blog/bali-travel-cash-budget-guide/)

## 2️⃣ **Internet**

I recommend purchasing a local SIM card after arriving in Bali, as it tends to be cheaper and offers more stable connectivity (though this might just be my personal experience). The airport provides free Wi-Fi, so there’s no need to worry about being unable to contact your driver. The SIM card we bought locally offered a total of 18GB per person, which was more than enough for seven days, even with frequent use of social media, watching YouTube videos, and downloading apps. So, you can choose a data plan that suits your usage needs.

> [Essential Bali Travel Guide: How to Get a Bali SIM Card/eSIM?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/)

## 3️⃣ **Visa, Tourist Tax, and Customs**

Remember to apply for your visa online before departure. You can also complete your customs declaration online three days in advance to avoid long queues at the airport and save time.

> [2026 Indonesia Bali Entry Using the 'All Indonesia' Declaration System | 1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

## 4️⃣ **Transportation**

In Bali, apart from hiring private drivers, most short-distance travel or food delivery services rely on apps like Grab or Gojek. Make sure to set up your account in Taiwan (you’ll need a Taiwanese phone number to receive the verification code) so you can use these apps smoothly once you arrive.

> [Essential App for Traveling in Indonesia/Bali: Complete Guide to Gojek](/blog/gojek-bali-guide/)

## ⚠️ **Your Private Driver is Crucial!**

Because information online is scattered, we were still completely lost even after arriving in Bali. Fortunately, our driver Komang WikaSutresna was a jack-of-all-trades—no matter what we asked, he always found us the best value-for-money options. It seemed like he knew someone everywhere in Bali, and he could always help us coordinate things wherever we went. He was absolutely amazing!

When we first arrived in Bali, we asked Komang directly about the best places to buy SIM cards and exchange money, and he took us there right away. The money exchange place seemed to be one of his connections, so the process was super quick, and the SIM card shop even offered free drinks—service was fantastic!  
  
Bali’s traffic can be chaotic, so for safety-conscious travelers like us, choosing a careful and steady driver is crucial. Luckily, Komang drove very cautiously, which made us feel at ease. Before heading to Uluwatu for a performance, my glasses were snatched by a monkey, causing us to miss part of the show. However, seeing how upset we were, Komang went out of his way to ask the Fire Dance performer, the White Monkey General, to let us take photos with him (seeing the Monkey General up close was truly shocking XDD). This close encounter with local traditional culture became a very special memory for us.
