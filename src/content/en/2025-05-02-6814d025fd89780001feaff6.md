## Bali's Sudden Blackout Leaves Entire Island in Darkness

[Provided by Han Zongliang](https://www.facebook.com/share/v/1AW8j4mkAm/)

On May 2, 2025, at 4 PM, Bali experienced a sudden power outage, plunging nearly the entire island into darkness. This major blackout significantly impacted local livelihoods, transportation, and tourism. PT PLN (Indonesia's State Electricity Company) quickly initiated repair efforts and continues to update the power supply situation.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_da277efc556c6ee025a7bfedbf8bd7e8.jpg)

---

## Power Company Responds: Submarine Cable Failure is the Main Cause

According to Gregorius Adi Trianto, Vice President of Corporate Communications at PLN, the blackout occurred around 4 PM local time due to a ****failure in the submarine cable between Java and Bali****, causing all connected generators to Bali to cease operation.

As of 10 PM that evening, approximately 80% of the area remained without power.

In the southern areas of Jimbaran and Uluwatu, not only was there no electricity, but the internet was also down.

## Where Does Bali's Electricity Come From?

Bali's own power generation capacity is limited, with the majority of electricity supplied from Java via an undersea high-voltage direct current (HVDC) cable. This crucial cable serves as the island's lifeline for stable power supply, and any technical failure could lead to widespread blackouts like the current incident.

Additionally, while Bali has several small-scale oil and gas power stations locally, these are only suitable for emergency use or as backup and cannot meet the island's full energy demands.

---

## Restoration Efforts Continue, Full Power Restoration Will Take Time

PT PLN stated that hundreds of technicians with equipment have been dispatched to affected areas to carry out phased restoration and load management. Their goal is to stabilize the system as quickly as possible and prevent similar incidents from occurring again.

Officials emphasized that full power restoration will take time and urged the public to be patient and conserve electricity.

---

## Looking Ahead: Bali's Power System Needs Diversification

This blackout highlights the risks of Bali's reliance on a single power source. Moving forward, the Indonesian government and PLN should consider the following strategies to enhance energy resilience:

- Invest in renewable energy: Diversify energy sources with solar, wind, and geothermal power.
- Strengthen local power generation: Increase microgrids and distributed energy systems.
- Enhance undersea cable redundancy: Improve backup and maintenance mechanisms for main cables.

## FAQ Section

### 1. Why did Bali experience this major power outage?

The primary cause was a malfunction in the submarine cable connecting Java and Bali, which prevented the power generators from operating.

### 2. Where does Bali get most of its electricity from?

Most of Bali's electricity comes from Java, transmitted via submarine cables.

### 3. Does Bali have its own power plants?

Yes, but they are relatively small and primarily serve as backup, unable to supply the entire island for extended periods.

### 4. How can similar incidents be prevented in the future?

It is necessary to diversify power sources, enhance local power generation facilities, and improve the backup design and response capabilities of the power grid.
