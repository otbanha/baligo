## Bali Hit by Sudden Blackout, Entire Island Plunged into Darkness

[Provided by Han Zongliang](https://www.facebook.com/share/v/1AW8j4mkAm/)

On May 2, 2025, at 4 PM, a sudden power outage in Bali left almost the entire island without electricity. This major blackout significantly impacted local livelihoods, transportation, and tourism. PT PLN (Indonesia's State Electricity Company) quickly initiated repair efforts and continued to update the power supply situation.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_da277efc556c6ee025a7bfedbf8bd7e8.jpg)

## Power Company Responds: Submarine Cable Failure Main Cause

According to Gregorius Adi Trianto, Vice President of Corporate Communications at PLN, the outage occurred around 4 PM local time due to a **failure in the submarine cable between Java and Bali**, causing all connected power generators to Bali to cease operation.

As of 10 PM that day, approximately 80% of the area remained without power.

## Where Does Bali's Electricity Come From?

Bali's own power generation capacity is limited, with most electricity coming from Java Island via a high-voltage direct current (HVDC) submarine cable. This critical cable serves as a vital lifeline for stable power supply on the island. Any technical failure could lead to large-scale blackouts like the current incident.

Additionally, while Bali has several small fuel and gas power stations locally, they can only serve emergency or backup purposes and cannot meet the island's full energy demands.

---

## Restoration Efforts Continue, Full Power Restoration Still Requires Time

PT PLN stated that hundreds of technicians with equipment have been dispatched to affected areas, carrying out phased restoration and load management. Their goal is to stabilize the system as soon as possible and prevent similar incidents from recurring.

Officials emphasized that full power restoration will take time and urged the public to remain patient and conserve electricity.

---

## Looking Ahead: Bali's Power System Needs Diversified Development

This blackout highlights the risks of Bali's reliance on a single power source. In the future, the Indonesian government and PLN should consider the following strategies to enhance energy resilience:

- Invest in renewable energy: Diversify energy sources such as solar, wind, and geothermal power.
- Strengthen local power generation: Increase microgrids and distributed energy systems.
- Submarine cable redundancy design: Improve backup and maintenance mechanisms for main cables.

## FAQ Section

### 1. Why did Bali experience this major power outage?

The main cause was a failure in the submarine cable connecting Java and Bali, which prevented the power generators from functioning.

### 2. Where does Bali's electricity primarily come from?

Most of Bali's electricity is supplied from Java Island, transmitted via submarine cables.

### 3. Does Bali have its own power plants?

Yes, but they are relatively small and primarily serve as backup, unable to supply the entire island for extended periods.

### 4. How can similar incidents be prevented in the future?

It is necessary to diversify power sources, enhance local power generation facilities, and improve the resilience and backup design of the power grid.
