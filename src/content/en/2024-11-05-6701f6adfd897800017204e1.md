### Bali Transportation Insights:

We originally planned to hire a private car for our entire stay in Bali, thinking it would be more convenient with two young children. But reality often differs from expectations! On our first day, we were shocked by the chaotic traffic on the island—traffic jams seemed to be everywhere! 🤯 If we had stuck with the private car, we probably would have wasted all our time stuck in traffic.

So, we quickly changed our strategy and decided to rent scooters as our main mode of transportation. Considering our family of four, we rented a larger scooter so that two adults and two kids could ride together. The kids tended to fall asleep easily on the scooter, so we sandwiched them between us for safety. The roads on the island weren’t exactly friendly, but renting scooters gave us much more flexibility, especially when moving between different attractions—it was so much easier and quicker! 🚴‍♀️🚴‍♂️

For destinations that were farther away, we didn’t have to worry either! We used ride-hailing services like GRAB or GO-JEK, which are very well-established in Bali. They were always available on demand, making things super convenient.

## August 9 (Friday) Tirta Empul Temple + Alas Harum Bali (Rice Terraces & Luwak Coffee) + Lunch at Cretya

Our first stop today was the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. This temple is renowned for its holy spring water, which local devotees believe has the power to purify both the mind and body. Although we are not Hindu, experiencing this unique cultural ritual firsthand felt very special. The kids were also fascinated by the temple’s mystical atmosphere and grand architecture.

Next, we visited **Alas Harum Bali**, known not only for its stunning rice terraces but also for its local specialty, **Luwak Coffee**. The terraces were breathtaking, and we took plenty of beautiful photos here. The kids were curious about the terraces, while we enjoyed a fragrant cup of Luwak coffee. The place also offers various recreational facilities, such as sky swings, making our time here relaxing and enjoyable.

For lunch, we chose to dine at **Cretya**, which boasts beautiful rice terrace views and a rich menu. Not only was the food delicious, but the serene atmosphere of the restaurant allowed us to enjoy a visual and culinary feast.

## August 10th (Sat) Kanto Lampo Waterfall + Ubud Palace + Ubud Traditional Market

Today we visited the hidden gem of **Kanto Lampo Waterfall** nestled in the jungle. The water cascades gently between rocks, creating a beautiful natural scene. The kids had a blast playing in the water, and the surrounding nature allowed us to fully appreciate Bali's ecological charm. The waterfall's flow was moderate, allowing visitors to get close and feel its refreshing coolness.

Next, we explored the **Ubud Palace** located in the heart of Ubud. This ancient palace features intricate carvings and traditional Balinese architecture, making it an excellent place to learn about local culture. Though the palace isn't large, its historical significance and architectural beauty left a deep impression on us.

Afterward, we visited the famous **Ubud Traditional Market**. While the market offers a wide variety of goods, the prices aren't particularly cheap, and the vendors tend to be somewhat indifferent, making bargaining difficult. A tip: if you're just looking for souvenirs, this might not be the best spot. However, on our way back to **Puri Padma Hotel**, we stumbled upon a smaller market where prices were much more reasonable, allowing us to pick up some affordable, high-quality handicrafts. 🎁

We encountered several individuals trying to sell us fake services, but fortunately, we had done our homework and avoided being tricked. We recommend staying vigilant in these areas and not easily trusting strangers.

Tonight we checked into the Holiday Resort Lombok in Senggigi. This hotel was impressive, with spacious rooms, beautiful garden views, and a serene beach that left our entire family very satisfied. The hotel facilities were also quite comprehensive, including a pool, restaurant, and spaces suitable for family activities, offering excellent value for money. ## August 20 (Tuesday) Coffee Shop and Traditional Market Exploration + Batuidung Hill Scenery

Today's itinerary was all about relaxation and leisure. We started at the stylish and artistic **Konyu Coffee & Space**, instantly falling in love with its unique decor and tranquil atmosphere. Their brunch was exceptionally delicious, making it the perfect spot to enjoy a cozy coffee break during our travels. Whether you're a coffee enthusiast or a brunch lover, this place is definitely worth a visit.

Next, we visited two local traditional markets: **Sindu Traditional Market** and **Cakranegara Traditional Market**. These markets were deeply local, with hardly any tourists in sight, offering a glimpse into the daily lives of Lombok residents. The prices were very affordable, making it ideal for purchasing local fruits, vegetables, or handmade crafts. We discovered many interesting items and enjoyed the experience of bargaining with locals, feeling truly immersed in their way of life.

In the afternoon, we took on the challenge of reaching the legendary **Batuidung Hill**. The journey was tough, involving winding mountain paths, but the view from the top made all the effort worthwhile. This hidden hill offers breathtaking scenery and an excellent vantage point to admire the natural beauty of the surroundings. The peacefulness and stunning views provided the perfect conclusion to our day.

![Batuidung，非常難到達的小山丘，google導不到要問在地村莊的人才知道，但爬上去景色很美](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d996aca6ecb50262a1fd75642fdd10f.jpg)

Batuidung, a hill that's quite difficult to reach—Google Maps won't guide you there, so you'll need to ask locals from nearby villages. But once you climb up, the view is absolutely beautiful.
