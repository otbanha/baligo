Our group member James Lin recently traveled to Bali with his family. They applied for an e-visa online beforehand. Upon arrival at Bali Airport, they quickly passed through the automated immigration area, completing the entry process in just 5 minutes.

According to his observation, if you don't apply for an e-visa in advance and choose to get an on-arrival visa at the airport, you'll need to queue for at least 30-45 minutes just to obtain the visa. After getting the visa, you'll still need to go through immigration for stamping, which he estimates could take about 1.5 hours.

James Lin's experience suggests that if you're traveling with children, seniors over 65, or individuals with mobility issues, you can directly apply for an on-arrival visa at the airport as there are dedicated lanes available. However, for adults without children, it's recommended to apply for an e-visa online in advance to save considerable time.

Another netizen, Zhou Jiuren, shared his experience of opting for an on-arrival visa when entering Jakarta. He found himself in a queue of about 20 people and had to patiently wait. Two hours later, it was finally his turn to complete the entry procedures. However, he was very disappointed with the efficiency, finding the entire process extremely time-consuming.

## Why do I hear that the on-arrival visa is quick?

Based on the experiences of some group members, they were fortunate to quickly obtain their visa on arrival after landing and didn't encounter long queues when getting their passports stamped at immigration. Such a situation is indeed enviable, but it's largely a matter of luck!

If you encounter a large crowd going through immigration procedures, the situation can be entirely different. Waiting in line at the airport for more than two hours not only tests the patience of travelers but also means wasting precious relaxation time.

Regarding the issue of paying for the visa on arrival, netizens' experiences vary significantly. Some report that payment on-site is very quick with almost no queue, while others note that during peak tourist seasons or when many flights arrive simultaneously, waiting times can be long, even exceeding an hour.

We strongly recommend applying for the **Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA)** before departure. Why? Because when traveling, many things are unpredictable, especially the situation upon arrival at the airport. If you have already obtained your e-Visa, you can proceed directly through immigration upon landing in Bali, saving time on queuing, paying fees, and filling out forms, and avoiding potential hassles due to language barriers or demands for tips.

Additionally, the pickup driver mentioned: '\*\*Better do e-Visa... Sometimes taking long time to wait for visa on arrival in airport.\*\*' This advice is very valuable as local drivers frequently interact with travelers and are well-acquainted with airport conditions. Their suggestions are usually based on practical experience and worth considering.

***

### Conclusion: It depends, but preparing in advance is safer

Considering the above points, my recommendation is: **If you wish for a smoother journey and to avoid unnecessary waits and hassles, it's best to apply for the Electronic Visa on Arrival in advance**. While paying for the visa on arrival is an option, since many things are unpredictable when traveling, preparing in advance always offers more peace of mind.

Of course, if you enjoy adventure or have flexible arrival times, you can opt to process your visa on arrival at the airport. However, be mentally prepared for possible queuing.
