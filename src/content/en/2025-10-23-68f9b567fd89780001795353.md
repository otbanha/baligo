If you think Bali is only about romantic beaches, sunsets, and resorts, you're missing the most touching aspect of the island—the **turtle conservation story**. Every year from April to October, mother turtles return to Bali's beaches to lay eggs, and local communities, volunteers, and foundations are dedicated to conservation, giving travelers the chance to witness the moment when baby turtles rush into the sea.

This article helps you organize the **latest turtle conservation activities and viewing spots for 2025**, covering everything from Kuta to Sanur!

## 🐢 1️⃣ BSTS — Bali Sea Turtle Society (Kuta)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_10d8cb87fd3795c13748ec3da6d89767.jpg)

📍 **Location**: Kuta Beach (next to Kuta Beach, refer to the [Kuta Guide Map](/blog/2023-12-13-657598bdfd8978000120fe20/) for exact location)

🌐 **Official Website**: <https://baliseaturtle.org/>

📅 **Opening Hours**: Daily 9:00–17:00 (activities scheduled based on hatching periods)

BSTS is one of Bali's most trusted turtle conservation organizations, primarily conducting patrols and hatching conservation on Kuta Beach. Whenever baby turtles hatch, they host free '**Baby Turtle Release**' events, allowing visitors to assist baby turtles in returning to the ocean at sunset.

🚗 **Transportation Tips**:

- About 15 minutes drive from the airport
- Direct access via Gojek/Grab or Blue Bird taxi

💡 **Pro Tip**:

Activity times vary, **please follow BSTS official IG or Facebook for updates** to avoid disappointment.

## 🐢 2️⃣ Sea Turtle Village (Sanur)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_653a7621066e1f56aaf7e49601283f11.jpg)

📍 **Location**: Sindhu Beach, Sanur. Refer to [Sanur Guide Map](/blog/2024-02-11-65bf2f75fd89780001dbf162/) for exact position.

🌐 **Official Info**: [Sea Turtle Village Sanur](https://www.hyatt.com/content/dam/hotel/propertysites/assets/regency/dpsbl/documents/en_us/home/Sea-Turtle-Village-Activities.pdf)

📅 **Opening Hours**: Approximately 8:00–17:00

This small sea turtle conservation site was voluntarily established by two local five-star hotels, Hyatt and Andaz. Though modest in size, it's perfect for family visits. You can explore small hatchery pools and display walls to learn about the journey of sea turtles from eggs to hatchlings.

🚗 **Transportation Tips**:

- About 35 minutes drive from the airport
- Recommended to take a taxi or hire a private car

💡 **Recommended Timing**:

Early morning or late afternoon visits are most comfortable, with soft sunlight perfect for photography 📸.

## 🐢 3️⃣ Sindu Dwarawati Turtle Conservation Center (Sanur)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c3a5b550721e4545254d70c3406ea9f0.jpg)

📍 **Location**: Sindhu Beach, Sanur. Refer to the [Sanur Guide Map](/blog/2024-02-11-65bf2f75fd89780001dbf162/) for exact location.

🌐 **Official Page**: [Sindu Dwarawati Turtle Conservation](https://www.instagram.com/sindu_dwarawati?utm_source=ig_web_button_share_sheet&igsh=ZDNlZDc0MzIxNw==)

📅 **Opening Hours**: Daily 8:00–17:00

Located near Sindu Beach, this site differs from Sea Turtle Village, focusing more on education and research. Volunteers provide insights into turtle species, habitats, and conservation challenges.

🚗 **Transportation Tips**:

- Walkable from main hotels in Sanur
- Approximately 35 minutes drive from the airport

💡 **Highlights**:

If traveling with children, the guided tours and interactive displays here are ideal for combining education with entertainment.

## 🐢 4️⃣ Serangan Island Turtle Conservation & Education Center (Turtle Island/Sanur)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_210d4b94f50f241c3bd9234150ff0593.jpg)

📍 **Location**: Serangan Island (off the coast of Sanur, see [Sanur Guide Map](/blog/2024-02-11-65bf2f75fd89780001dbf162/))

🌐 **Official Website**: [TCEC Serangan](https://www.instagram.com/tcecserangan?utm_source=ig_web_button_share_sheet&igsh=ZDNlZDc0MzIxNw==)

📅 **Opening Hours**: Daily 9:00–17:00

Known as Turtle Island, this conservation center is one of Bali's largest and most well-equipped facilities, featuring several large rehabilitation pools and exhibition halls. Visitors can observe various turtle species up close and learn about the balance between fishing, tourism, and conservation.

🚗 **Transportation Tips**:

- Previously accessible only by boat, Serangan Island is now connected by a bridge.

---

- Approximately 25 minutes from the airport.

## 💬 Conclusion: An Unforgettable Lesson in Life

On the beaches of Bali, watching tiny sea turtles just a few centimeters long make their way towards the deep blue Indian Ocean is a moment of emotion that words cannot fully capture.

This is not just an experience during a journey, but a voyage of connecting with nature and learning to respect life.

However, in recent years, there have been controversies surrounding 'Sea Turtle Conservation Centers'—some question whether they have become overly commercialized or if they truly adhere to conservation principles. From my personal observations, **most sea turtle conservation centers in Bali do not charge an entrance fee**; they operate mainly on donations from visitors and modest income from small-scale activities. These centers are often maintained by local communities or volunteers, driven by their commitment to conservation and education.

Remember, the process of sea turtles laying eggs on the beach is extremely fragile—**a party or an unintentional step can destroy an entire nest of turtle eggs**. Therefore, the existence of these conservation centers remains essential for protecting sea turtles and their habitats.

Perhaps not perfect, but as long as we visit with respect and support—**less criticism, more understanding and donations**—we can help sustain these efforts to protect these small lives.

🌺 Whether you're in Kuta, Sanur, or Turtle Island, don't miss out on this gentle and hopeful aspect of Bali.
