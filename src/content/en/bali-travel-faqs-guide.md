## 1. Where is Bali and how do I get there?

Bali is an island belonging to Indonesia, geographically located in Southeast Asia. Surrounded by the sea, it lies just west of Java and east of Lombok. With its unique character and diverse landscapes, Bali has become a popular vacation destination in Southeast Asia, attracting travelers from all over the world each year.

## 2. What language is spoken in Bali?

In Bali, locals primarily speak Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia), the official language of the country. However, due to Bali's unique culture, they also speak Balinese, a language commonly used in daily life and considered the mother tongue for many residents. Balinese is mainly used at home, in religious ceremonies, and during traditional activities, playing a significant role in local religious and community life.

***

## 3. Do Balinese People Speak English?

Generally speaking, Bali's tourism industry is highly developed, and many locals working in tourism-related jobs can speak basic English. Whether at restaurants, hotels, shops, or attractions, most service staff can communicate in simple English, especially in southern areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Jimbaran. However, when talking to elderly locals or residents in remote villages, there may still be some language barriers. Some older generations only speak Balinese.

For travelers, learning a few simple Indonesian phrases like "Halo" (Hello) and "Terima kasih" (Thank you) can make interactions more enjoyable and smoother.

## 4. What Currency Is Used in Bali?

The currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Travelers can easily exchange for Indonesian Rupiah at airports, banks, hotels, or currency exchange offices in the city. It's recommended to carry some cash for use at small shops, street stalls, or traditional markets, allowing for more flexible handling of small payments.

## 5. What Is the Climate Like in Bali? What Is the Best Time to Visit?

Located near the equator, Bali has a **tropical monsoon climate**, warm and humid year-round. The climate is divided into two main seasons: the **dry season** and the **rainy season**. These distinct weather patterns make Bali suitable for travel throughout the year, though the experience varies slightly depending on the season.

![5.峇里島的氣候如何？什麼月份拜訪峇里島最好？](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e7fac42f701496457dff00feb523003.png)

**Dry Season (May to October)**

The dry season is Bali's peak tourist season, with moderate temperatures, lower humidity, plenty of sunshine, and clear skies. Daytime temperatures typically range from **27°C to 32°C**, with slightly cooler evenings. This is the ideal time for outdoor activities like beach sunbathing, snorkeling, diving, and hiking, as well as for water sports and capturing stunning sunsets.

**Rainy Season (November to April)**

The rainy season is Bali’s low season. Although rainfall is more frequent during this period, showers are usually brief and intermittent, not lasting for days on end. Daytime temperatures are similar to the dry season, but humidity is higher and rain is more common. Temperatures still hover around **26°C to 30°C**. Rain typically falls in the afternoon and evening, leaving mornings and late nights open for activities.

## 7. Is it better to travel to Bali independently or with a tour group?

**Advantages of independent travel:**

- Highly flexible itinerary, allowing you to explore based on your interests
- Generally more affordable than group tours
- Lets you relax at your own pace
- Offers a wider variety of dining options

However, planning a free-and-easy trip requires quite a bit of preparation:

- Transportation can be tricky, as public transit is limited; you'll need to research private driver or car rental options.
- Attractions are spread out and route planning is challenging, requiring you to study distances and visiting order between sites.
- You'll need to consider whether the character of each accommodation area (e.g., lively Kuta, artistic Ubud) suits your needs.
- It's important to understand weather, food hygiene, and visiting etiquette.

**Advantages of joining a tour:**

- Eliminates the hassle of arranging transportation and accommodation.
- Includes a guide for explanations and assistance.
- Offers a well-planned itinerary, ideal for travelers with limited time.
- No need to worry about language barriers.

**We recommend choosing based on the following considerations:**

1. Language skills - English is common in tourist areas, but if you don't speak any foreign language, a tour is advisable.
2. Trip duration - For short stays, a tour allows you to visit key spots quickly.
3. Budget planning - Independent travel lets you adjust expenses flexibly, while tours are mostly all-inclusive with no extra worries.
4. Travel style - Whether you prefer in-depth exploration or a quick overview of attractions.

## 21. Is It Convenient to Use Credit Cards in Bali?

Bali's credit card payment environment is quite mature, but there are a few things to keep in mind:

1. **When to Use Cards:**
2. - High-end restaurants, shopping malls, five-star hotels, and beach clubs usually accept international credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, Amex).

- Many car rental companies and travel agencies also accept credit card payments, but may charge an additional processing fee (around 1.5–3%).

3. **Situations where cards are not accepted:**
4. - Street stalls, small local eateries (Warung), traditional markets, and remote areas may only accept cash.

- Some restaurants have a minimum spending amount; if your bill is below that, you won't be able to use a card.

5. **Recommendations:**
6. - **Carry Indonesian Rupiah cash:** Cash is most convenient for small transactions or tips, especially in remote areas or when street shopping.

- **Bring multi-currency credit cards:** Visa and MasterCard are the most widely accepted.
- **Use digital payments:** GoPay e-wallet is becoming increasingly popular in Bali, so consider installing the app. For more details, check out: [Complete Guide to Registering, Using, and Topping Up Gojek & GoPay in Indonesia/Bali](/en/blog/indonesia-gojek-gopay-guide/)

## 38. Are Bali's Traditional Dance Performances Worth Watching?

![38.峇里島的傳統舞蹈表演值得看嗎？](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_82b8c46b8f153469d536f48be8633bf8.jpg)

When visiting Bali, catching a traditional dance performance is an absolute must-do experience! Balinese dance is not just an art form but also deeply rooted in religious and cultural significance. Through intricate costumes, graceful movements, and vivid expressions, these dances perfectly showcase Bali's unique cultural landscape.

Here are several traditional Balinese dances worth watching:

1. **Kecak Dance**

This is one of Bali's most iconic traditional dances, also known as the 'Monkey Dance.' Inspired by the Hindu epic *Ramayana*, performers sit in a circle and chant 'chak-chak-chak' sounds to mimic a troop of monkeys, creating a truly electrifying atmosphere. The most famous venue for the Kecak dance is at Uluwatu Temple at sunset, with a backdrop of golden skies and stunning ocean views—a feast for both the eyes and ears.

[Top 6 Kecak Fire Dance Locations in Bali](/en/blog/bali-kecak-fire-dance-locations/)

2. **Barong Dance**

The Barong Dance tells a story of the struggle between good and evil, featuring the benevolent mythical beast 'Barong' battling the malevolent 'Rangda,' symbolizing balance and harmony. Dancers wear elaborate costumes and perform intricate movements, making this show especially popular with families.

3. **Legong Dance**

The Legong Dance is considered the most elegant dance in Bali, often performed by young girls who use intricate hand movements and eye contact to interpret captivating mythological stories. Known for its tight rhythm and precise movements, this dance is typically staged at Ubud Palace or local art villages, making it a favorite among art enthusiasts.

The Barong Dance and Legong Dance can be enjoyed at the indoor stage of Balerung Stage. For details, see [here](/en/blog/balerung-stage-cultural-show/).

**Why It's Worth Watching**

1. **Cultural Experience**: The dance is closely tied to Balinese religious rituals, offering you a deeper understanding of local culture.
2. **Visual Delight**: Performances are accompanied by traditional instruments, with exquisite costumes and delicate movements that are a feast for the eyes.
3. **Family-Friendly**: Most dance performances are suitable for all ages, whether you're a family with kids or a couple, everyone can find joy.
