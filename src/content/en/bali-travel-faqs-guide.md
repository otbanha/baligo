# **Answers to Common Bali Travel Questions**

Whether you're preparing for your first trip to Bali or have been there a few times, many people have some basic questions about this enchanting island. Here are a few frequently asked questions to help you better understand Bali and plan your perfect trip!

## 1. Where is Bali? How to Get There?

Bali is an island belonging to Indonesia, located in Southeast Asia. The island is surrounded by sea, with Java to the west and Lombok to the east. Due to its unique characteristics and diverse landscapes, Bali has become a popular vacation destination in Southeast Asia, attracting travelers from all over the world each year.

![1. Where is Bali? How to Get There?](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e14497d695ce945e47d1b8738cd5faa6.gif)

Direct flights are available from countries such as Taiwan, Hong Kong, China, Malaysia, Singapore, Vietnam, South Korea, Japan, Australia, and more.

PS. EVA Air and China Airlines offer direct flights from Taiwan.

## 2. What Language is Spoken in Bali?

In Bali, locals primarily speak Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia), the official language of Indonesia. However, due to Bali's unique culture, locals also speak Balinese, a language that is quite common in daily life and can be considered the mother tongue for some Balinese residents. The use of Balinese is mainly concentrated in family, religious ceremonies, and traditional activities, playing an important role in local religious and community life.

***

## 3. Do People in Bali Speak English?

Generally speaking, Bali's tourism industry is highly developed, and many locals working in tourism-related jobs can speak basic English. Whether it's restaurants, hotels, shops, or attraction staff, most can communicate in simple English, especially in southern areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Jimbaran. However, there might still be some language barriers when conversing with elderly locals or residents in remote villages. Some older generations may only speak Balinese.

For travelers, learning a few simple Indonesian phrases like "Hello" (Halo) and "Thank you" (Terima kasih) can make communication more fun and harmonious.

## 4. What Currency is Used in Bali?

The currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Travelers can easily exchange currency at the airport, banks, hotels, or currency exchange offices in the city. It's recommended to carry some cash for use in small shops, street vendors, or traditional markets, as this allows for more flexibility with small payments.

## 5. What is Bali's Climate Like? When is the Best Time to Visit Bali?

Located near the equator, Bali has a **tropical monsoon climate**, characterized by warm and humid weather year-round. The climate is divided into two main seasons: **dry season** and **_rainy season_**, making Bali suitable for travel throughout the year. However, the travel experience may vary slightly depending on the season.

![5. What is Bali's Climate Like? When is the Best Time to Vis](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e7fac42f701496457dff00feb523003.png)

**Dry Season (May to October)**

The dry season is Bali's peak tourist season. During this period, temperatures are moderate, humidity is lower, and there is plenty of sunshine with clear skies. Daytime temperatures typically range between **27°C to 32°C**, with slightly cooler evenings. This is the ideal time for outdoor activities such as beach sunbathing, snorkeling, diving, hiking, and enjoying water sports or capturing stunning sunsets.

**Rainy Season (November to April)**

The rainy season is Bali's low season. Although there's more rainfall during this period, the rain is usually brief and intermittent, rarely lasting for several consecutive days. Daytime temperatures are similar to the dry season, but humidity is higher and rainfall is more frequent. Temperatures still hover between **26°C to 30°C**. Most rainfall occurs from afternoon to evening, while mornings and nights remain suitable for various activities.

Bali's average temperature typically ranges between 26-32°C. During the rainy season, it can feel slightly cooler. When packing, we recommend focusing on short-sleeved clothing. There's no need for heavy jackets - just bring a light jacket or windbreaker.

## 7. Is Bali better for independent travel or group tours?

**Advantages of independent travel:**

- Greater itinerary flexibility, allowing for in-depth exploration based on personal interests
- Generally more cost-effective than group tours
- Ability to enjoy a leisurely vacation at your own pace
- More diverse dining options

However, independent travel requires significant preparation:

- Transportation arrangements are complex, with limited public transit options. Consider hiring a private car or renting a vehicle.
- Attractions are spread out and route planning can be challenging. Research distances between sites and optimal sightseeing order.
- Consider whether the characteristics of accommodation areas (e.g. lively Kuta, artsy Ubud) meet your needs.
- Be aware of weather conditions, food hygiene, and visiting etiquette.

**Advantages of group tours:**

- Eliminate hassle of arranging transportation and accommodation
- Have a guide for commentary and assistance
- Well-planned itineraries suitable for time-limited travelers
- No need to worry about language barriers

**Suggestions for making your choice:**

1. Language ability - English is widely used in tourist areas, but those with no foreign language skills may prefer group tours
2. Trip duration - Short stays are better suited for group tours to maximize sightseeing
3. Budget planning - Independent travel allows flexible spending, while group tours are mostly all-inclusive
4. Travel style - Prefer in-depth exploration or quick sightseeing

## 21. Is It Convenient to Use Credit Cards in Bali?

Bali’s credit card environment is quite mature, but there are a few things to note:

1. **Places Suitable for Credit Cards:**
2. - High-end restaurants, shopping malls, five-star hotels, beach clubs, and similar venues usually accept international credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, Amex).

- Many car rental companies and travel agencies accept credit card payments, but may charge additional fees (approximately 1.5-3%).

3. **Situations where credit cards are not suitable:**
4. - Street vendors, small local restaurants (Warung), traditional markets, and remote areas may only accept cash.

- Some restaurants have minimum spending requirements and may not accept credit cards for amounts below a certain threshold.

5. **Recommendations:**
6. - **Prepare Indonesian Rupiah cash:** Cash is most convenient for small transactions or minor expenses, especially in remote areas or street shopping.

- **Bring multi-currency credit cards:** Visa and MasterCard are the most widely accepted.

## 38. Are Bali's Traditional Dance Performances Worth Watching?

![38. Are Bali's Traditional Dance Performances Worth Watching](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_82b8c46b8f153469d536f48be8633bf8.jpg)

Visiting Bali, experiencing the local traditional dances is an absolute must! Balinese dance is not just an art form but also carries deep religious and cultural significance. Through exquisite costumes, graceful movements, and vivid expressions, these dances perfectly encapsulate Bali's unique cultural essence.

Here are a few traditional Balinese dances worth watching:

1. **Kecak Fire Dance**

This is one of Bali's most iconic traditional dances, also known as the 'Monkey Dance.' Inspired by the Hindu epic 'Ramayana,' dancers sit in a circle and chant 'cak cak cak' to simulate a monkey army, creating a very powerful atmosphere. The most famous venue for the Kecak Fire Dance is at Uluwatu Temple during sunset, with the golden sun and stunning ocean views providing a mesmerizing visual and auditory experience.

[Top Six Must-See Kecak Fire Dance Locations in Bali](/en/blog/bali-kecak-fire-dance-locations/)

2. **Barong Dance**

The Barong Dance tells the story of the battle between good and evil, featuring the benevolent mythical creature 'Barong' against the evil 'Rangda,' symbolizing balance and harmony. Dancers wear elaborate costumes and display intricate body language, making this performance particularly popular among family travelers.

3. **Legong Dance**

The Legong Dance is considered the most elegant dance in Bali, often performed by young girls who interpret captivating mythological stories through intricate hand movements and eye expressions. Known for its fast-paced rhythm and precise movements, this dance is typically performed at Ubud Palace or local art villages, making it a favorite among art enthusiasts.

The Barong and Legong dances can be enjoyed at the indoor stage of Balerung Stage. For more details, check [here](/en/blog/balerung-stage-cultural-show/).

**Why It's Worth Watching**

1. **Cultural Experience**: The dance is deeply intertwined with Bali's religious rituals, offering a deeper understanding of local culture.
2. **Visual Delight**: Performances are usually accompanied by traditional music, with exquisite costumes and delicate movements that are a feast for the eyes.
3. **Family-Friendly**: Most dance performances are suitable for all ages, making it enjoyable for families and couples alike.
