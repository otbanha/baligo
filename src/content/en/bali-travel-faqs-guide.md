# **Answers to Common Bali Travel Questions**

Whether you're planning your first trip to Bali or have been there several times, many people have some basic questions about this enchanting island. Here are some frequently asked questions to help you better understand Bali and plan your perfect itinerary!

***

## 1. Where is Bali? How to Get There?

Bali is an island belonging to Indonesia, located in Southeast Asia. The island is surrounded by the sea, with Java to the west and Lombok to the east. Due to its unique characteristics and diverse landscapes, Bali has become a popular vacation destination in Southeast Asia, attracting travelers from all over the world each year.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e14497d695ce945e47d1b8738cd5faa6.gif)

Direct flights are available from Taiwan, Hong Kong, China, Malaysia, Singapore, Vietnam, South Korea, Japan, Australia, and other countries.

PS. EVA Air and China Airlines from Taiwan offer direct flights.

## 2. What Language is Spoken in Bali?

In Bali, the locals primarily use Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia), which is the official language of Indonesia. However, due to Bali's unique culture, locals also speak Balinese, a language that is quite common in daily life and can be considered the mother tongue of some Balinese residents. The use of Balinese is mainly concentrated in family, religious ceremonies, and traditional activities, playing an important role in local religious and community life.

***

## 3. Do People in Bali Speak English?

Generally speaking, Bali's tourism industry is highly developed, and many locals working in tourism-related jobs can speak basic English. Whether it's restaurants, hotels, shops, or attraction staff, most can communicate in simple English, especially in southern areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Jimbaran. However, there might still be some language barriers when conversing with elderly locals or residents in remote villages. Some older generations may only speak Balinese.

For travelers, learning a few simple Indonesian phrases like "Hello" (Halo) and "Thank you" (Terima kasih) can make communication more fun and harmonious.

## 4. What Currency is Used in Bali?

The currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Travelers can easily exchange currency at airports, banks, hotels, or currency exchange offices in the city. It's recommended to carry some cash for use in small shops, street vendors, or traditional markets, as this allows for more flexibility with small payments.

## 5. What is Bali's Climate Like? What is the Best Month to Visit Bali?

Located near the equator, Bali has a **tropical monsoon climate**, characterized by warm and humid weather year-round. The climate is divided into two main seasons: **dry season** and **wet season**, making Bali suitable for travel throughout the year. However, the travel experience may vary slightly depending on the season.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e7fac42f701496457dff00feb523003.png)

**Dry Season (May to October)**

The dry season is Bali's peak tourist season. During this period, temperatures are moderate, humidity is lower, and there is plenty of sunshine with clear skies. Daytime temperatures usually range between **27°C to 32°C**, with slightly cooler evenings. This is the ideal time for outdoor activities such as beach sunbathing, snorkeling, diving, hiking, and capturing beautiful sunsets. It's also perfect for water sports.

**Wet Season (November to April)**

The rainy season is Bali's low season. Although there is more rainfall during this period, the rain is usually brief and intermittent, not lasting for several consecutive days. Daytime temperatures are similar to the dry season, but humidity is higher and rainfall is more frequent. Temperatures still remain around **26°C to 30°C**. During this time, rainfall mainly occurs from afternoon to evening, while mornings and nights are still suitable for some activities.

https://youtube.com/shorts/bqtCP1YoKCM

The average temperature in Bali usually ranges between 26-32°C. During the rainy season, it can sometimes be cooler. For packing, it's recommended to focus on short-sleeved clothing. There's no need to bring thick jackets—just a light jacket or windbreaker will suffice.

## 7. Is Bali better for independent travel or group tours?

**Advantages of independent travel:**

- Greater flexibility in itinerary, allowing for in-depth exploration based on interests
- Generally cheaper overall compared to group tours
- Ability to relax and travel at your own pace
- More diverse dining options

However, independent travel requires just as much preparation:

- Transportation arrangements are complex, public transport is underdeveloped, so consider hiring a private car or renting a vehicle
- Attractions are scattered and route planning is challenging, research distances between sites and optimal visiting order
- Consider if accommodation areas match your needs (e.g., lively Kuta, artsy Ubud)
- Be aware of weather, food hygiene, and cultural etiquette

**Advantages of Group Tours:**

- No hassle with transportation and accommodation arrangements
- Guided commentary and assistance available
- Well-planned itineraries suitable for time-limited travelers
- No language barrier concerns

**Consider these factors when choosing:**

1. Language skills - English is widely used in tourist areas, but complete beginners may prefer group tours
2. Trip duration - Short stays are better suited for group tours to maximize sightseeing
3. Budget planning - Independent travel allows flexible spending, while group tours are mostly all-inclusive
4. Travel style - Prefer in-depth exploration or quick overviews of attractions

## 18. Is Bali Expensive?

The cost of living in Bali is quite flexible, depending on your travel style and spending habits:

1. **Budget Options:**
2. - If you choose to eat street food (like Nasi Goreng, Mie Goreng, or Balinese buffet Nasi Campur), you might spend only around USD$6-10 per day on three meals.

- Public transportation and local restaurants are also very affordable, especially in areas away from tourist hotspots.

1. **Mid-Range Budget:**
2. - If you choose mid-range restaurants recommended by tourists or international chain restaurants, a meal per person costs around USD$8 to USD$16.

3. **Luxury Spending:**
4. - Bali's luxury villas, top-tier restaurants, or high-end beach clubs are relatively expensive, with costs comparable to those in international metropolises.

1. **Situations Suitable for Credit Cards:**
2. - High-end restaurants, shopping malls, five-star hotels, and beach clubs usually accept international credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, Amex).

## 38. Are Bali's Traditional Dance Performances Worth Watching?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_82b8c46b8f153469d536f48be8633bf8.jpg)

When visiting Bali, experiencing the local traditional dances is an absolute must! Balinese dance is not just an art form but also carries deep religious and cultural significance. Through intricate costumes, graceful movements, and expressive gestures, these dances beautifully encapsulate Bali's unique cultural essence.

Here are a few traditional Balinese dances worth watching:

1. **Kecak Dance**

This is one of Bali's most iconic traditional dances, also known as the "Monkey Dance." Inspired by the Hindu epic Ramayana, the dancers sit in a circle and chant "cak cak cak" to mimic a monkey army, creating a mesmerizing atmosphere. The most famous Kecak Dance performances take place at Uluwatu Temple during sunset, with the golden hues of the setting sun and the stunning ocean backdrop making it a truly enchanting visual and auditory experience.

[Top 6 Places to Watch the Kecak Fire Dance in Bali](/blog/2024-12-12-675ad354fd89780001c09f24/)

2. **Barong Dance**

The Barong Dance tells the story of the battle between good and evil, featuring the benevolent mythical creature "Barong" and the evil witch "Rangda," symbolizing balance and harmony. Dancers wear elaborate costumes and perform intricate movements, making this performance particularly popular among family travelers.

3. **Legong Dance**

The Legong Dance is considered the most elegant dance in Bali, often performed by young girls who interpret captivating mythological stories through intricate hand movements and eye expressions. Known for its precise rhythms and movements, this dance is typically performed at Ubud Palace or local art villages, making it a favorite among art enthusiasts.

The Barong Dance and Legong Dance can be enjoyed at the indoor stage of Balerung Stage. For details, please see [here](/blog/2024-09-19-66eb78bafd89780001edcec6/).

**Reasons to Watch**

1. **Cultural Experience**: The dance is closely tied to Bali's religious rituals, offering a deeper understanding of local culture.
2. **Visual Delight**: Performances are usually accompanied by traditional music, with exquisite costumes and delicate movements that are a feast for the eyes.
3. **Family-Friendly**: Most dance performances are suitable for all ages, making it enjoyable for families and couples alike.
