# **Answers to Common Questions About Bali Travel**

Whether you're planning your first trip to Bali or have been there several times, many people have basic questions about this enchanting island. Here are some frequently asked questions to help you better understand Bali and plan your perfect itinerary!

## 1. Where is Bali? How to Get There?

Bali is an island belonging to Indonesia, located in Southeast Asia. The island is surrounded by the sea, with Java to the west and Lombok to the east. Due to its unique characteristics and diverse landscapes, Bali has become a popular vacation destination in Southeast Asia, attracting travelers from all over the world each year.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e14497d695ce945e47d1b8738cd5faa6.gif)

Direct flights are available from countries such as Taiwan, Hong Kong, China, Malaysia, Singapore, Vietnam, South Korea, Japan, Australia, and more.

PS. EVA Air and China Airlines from Taiwan offer direct flights.

## 2. What Language is Spoken in Bali?

In Bali, locals primarily use Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia), the official language of Indonesia. However, due to Bali's unique culture, locals also speak Balinese, a language that is quite common in daily life and can be considered the mother tongue for some Balinese residents. Balinese is mainly used in family settings, religious ceremonies, and traditional activities, playing a significant role in local religious and community life.

***

## 3. Do People in Bali Speak English?

Generally speaking, Bali's tourism industry is highly developed, and many locals working in tourism-related jobs can speak basic English. Whether it's restaurants, hotels, shops, or attraction staff, most can communicate in simple English, especially in southern areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Jimbaran. However, there might still be some language barriers when conversing with elderly locals or residents in remote villages. Some older generations may only speak Balinese.

For travelers, learning a few simple Indonesian phrases like "Hello" (Halo) and "Thank you" (Terima kasih) can make communication more fun and harmonious.

## 4. What Currency is Used in Bali?

The currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Travelers can easily exchange currency at airports, banks, hotels, or currency exchange offices in the city. It's recommended to carry some cash for use in small shops, street stalls, or traditional markets, as this allows for more flexibility with small payments.

## 5. What is Bali's Climate Like? When is the Best Time to Visit Bali?

Located near the equator, Bali has a **tropical monsoon climate**, characterized by warm and humid weather year-round. The climate is divided into two main seasons: **dry season** and **wet season**, making Bali suitable for travel throughout the year. However, the travel experience may vary slightly depending on the season.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e7fac42f701496457dff00feb523003.png)

**Dry Season (May to October)**

The dry season is Bali's peak tourist season. During this period, temperatures are moderate, humidity is lower, and there is plenty of sunshine with clear skies. Daytime temperatures typically range between **27°C to 32°C**, with slightly cooler evenings. This is the ideal time for outdoor activities such as beach sunbathing, snorkeling, diving, hiking, and is also perfect for water sports and capturing beautiful sunsets.

**Wet Season (November to April)**

The rainy season is Bali's low season. Although there's more rainfall during this period, the rain is usually brief and intermittent, rarely lasting several consecutive days. Daytime temperatures are similar to the dry season, but humidity is higher and rainfall more frequent. Temperatures still hover between **26°C to 30°C**. Most rainfall occurs from afternoon to evening, while mornings and nights remain suitable for various activities.

https://youtube.com/shorts/bqtCP1YoKCM

Bali's average temperature typically ranges between 26-32°C. During the rainy season, it can feel slightly cooler. When packing, prioritize short-sleeved clothing – there's no need for heavy jackets, just bring a light jacket or windbreaker.

## 7. Is Bali better for independent travel or group tours?

**Advantages of independent travel:**

- Greater itinerary flexibility allows for deeper exploration based on personal interests
- Generally more cost-effective than group tours
- Ability to relax and travel at your own pace
- More diverse dining options

However, independent travel requires substantial preparation:

- Transportation arrangements are complex, with limited public transport options. Consider hiring a private car or renting a vehicle.
- Attractions are spread out, making route planning challenging. Research distances between sites and optimal visiting order.
- Consider whether the characteristics of different accommodation areas (e.g., lively Kuta, artistic Ubud) meet your needs.
- Be aware of weather conditions, food hygiene, and local etiquette.

**Advantages of joining a tour group:**

- Eliminates the hassle of arranging transportation and accommodation
- Includes guide commentary and assistance
- Well-planned itineraries suitable for time-limited travelers
- No need to worry about language barriers

**Suggestions for making your choice:**

1. Language ability - English is widely used in tourist areas, but those with no foreign language skills may prefer tours
2. Trip duration - Shorter stays may benefit from tours for efficient sightseeing
3. Budget planning - Independent travel allows flexible spending, while tours offer all-inclusive packages
4. Travel style - Preference for in-depth exploration or quick sightseeing

## 18. Is Bali Expensive?

The cost of living in Bali is actually quite flexible, depending on your travel style and spending habits:

1. **Budget Options:**
2. - If you choose to eat street food (like Nasi Goreng, Mie Goreng, or Bali's buffet-style Nasi Campur), you can get by with just USD$6-10 per day for three meals.

- Public transportation and local restaurants are also very affordable, especially in areas away from tourist hotspots.

1. **Mid-Range Budget:**
2. - If you choose mid-range restaurants recommended by tourists or international chain restaurants, a meal per person costs around USD$8 to USD$16.

- Some mid-range three-star or four-star hotels cost approximately USD$48 to USD$97 per night, offering decent facilities and services.

3. **Luxury Spending:**
4. - Bali's luxury villas, top-tier restaurants, or high-end beach clubs are relatively expensive, with costs comparable to those in major international cities.

2. - High-end restaurants, shopping malls, five-star hotels, beach clubs, and similar venues usually accept international credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, Amex).

## 38. Is it worth watching traditional dance performances in Bali?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_82b8c46b8f153469d536f48be8633bf8.jpg)

When visiting Bali, watching local traditional dances is definitely an experience not to be missed! Balinese dance is not just an art form but also carries deep religious and cultural significance. Through exquisite costumes, graceful movements, and vivid expressions, these dances perfectly encapsulate Bali's unique cultural charm.

Here are a few traditional Balinese dances worth watching:

1. **Kecak Dance**

This is one of Bali's most iconic traditional dances, also known as the 'Monkey Dance.' Inspired by the Hindu epic 'Ramayana,' dancers sit in a circle and use the sound 'cak cak cak' to mimic a monkey army, creating a very intense atmosphere. The most famous Kecak Dance performance takes place at sunset at Uluwatu Temple, with the golden sunset and stunning ocean views as the backdrop, making it a feast for both the eyes and ears.

[Top 6 Must-See Kecak Fire Dance Locations in Bali](/blog/bali-kecak-fire-dance-locations/)

2. **Barong Dance**

The Barong Dance tells the story of the eternal struggle between good and evil, featuring the benevolent mythical creature Barong battling the malevolent Rangda, symbolizing balance and harmony. Dancers adorned in elaborate costumes perform intricate movements, making this performance particularly popular among family travelers.

3. **Legong Dance**

Considered Bali's most elegant dance, the Legong Dance is typically performed by young girls who convey captivating mythological tales through delicate hand gestures and expressive eye movements. Known for its precise rhythms and intricate choreography, this dance is often showcased at Ubud Palace or local art villages, making it a favorite among art enthusiasts.

Both the Barong Dance and Legong Dance can be enjoyed at the indoor stage of Balerung Stage. For more details, check [here](/blog/balerung-stage-cultural-show/).

**Why It's Worth Watching**

1. **Cultural Experience**: The dances are deeply intertwined with Balinese religious rituals, offering a profound understanding of local culture.
2. **Visual Delight**: Performances are accompanied by traditional music, with exquisite costumes and meticulous movements that are a feast for the eyes.
3. **Family-Friendly**: Most dance performances are suitable for all ages, making them enjoyable for families, couples, and solo travelers alike.
