We chose Bali as our travel destination for this 6-day adventure. As a couple, we wanted to experience the local culture, natural beauty, and cuisine of this stunning island. Our itinerary included visits to Seminyak's beaches, the breathtaking Nusa Penida, and traditional Tampaksiring, with the assistance of a local driver to make our journey smoother. Let us share the highlights of our trip!

### Day 1

We arrived at the airport around 2:40 PM. Having applied for an e-visa in advance, the immigration process went smoothly, and we easily handled the tourism tax. However, waiting for our luggage took an hour, which made us a bit anxious.

Since Agus had a last-minute commitment, his nephew Oddy came to pick us up. First, he took us to exchange money. Then, Oddy quickly drove us to La Brisa, allowing us to enjoy dinner before sunset.

After dinner, we visited the nearby Canggu Center market to experience the lively local atmosphere.

🏠 We spent our first night at a hotel in Seminyak, ending a wonderful day.

---

### Day 2

Today was dedicated to exploring the east and west coasts of Nusa Penida, with Agus handling our tickets and including lunch 🫶🏻.

Around 6:30 AM, Agus arrived at our villa to pick us up and took us to the harbor to collect our tickets, guiding us through the process without any worries of getting lost 😌. Our itinerary included visits to the stunning Diamond Beach and Kelingking Beach. Although we had plenty of time, the trek down to the beaches was quite tiring for someone like me who isn't used to much physical activity, especially given the rough terrain—it felt like an adventure at times 😂.

After returning to Sanur Harbor in the afternoon, Agus took us for a massage session that lasted about two hours. Thanks to his negotiation skills, we enjoyed a significant discount off the menu price 👍.

### Day 3

📍 Penglipuran Village, recognized as one of the top three cleanest villages in the world. Visiting in the morning meant fewer tourists, allowing us to quietly enjoy the local scenery.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_139f7058772c6fa1200a75cee97ac9af.jpg)

Here, we also sampled local specialties—snake fruit and Loloh Cemcem, a drink similar to green grass tea, which Agus explained is made from coconut water and ground plant leaves. It has a sweet and sour taste, and while not unpleasant, it was something I couldn't quite get used to. I recommend giving it a try if you get the chance 😆.

![ Loloh Cemcem](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_191755c6fb20e4272bb5923d0a7dfab8.jpg)

Loloh Cemcem

Editor's Note: Loloh Cemcem is a traditional beverage from Penglipuran village. Cemcem leaves are one of the village's main specialties. Local residents process these leaves into a fresh drink that is beneficial for health.

This green health drink is often thought to have a bitter taste, as many believe cemcem leaves naturally produce bitterness when used in food. However, this is a misconception. When you drink Loloh Cemcem, you won't experience bitterness because palm sugar syrup balances it out.

During preparation, cemcem leaves are mixed with other plants like physic nut leaves, betel leaves, and coral tree leaves. These four types of leaves are crushed and soaked in warm water. Then, cinnamon and palm sugar syrup are added to neutralize the bitterness. To enhance the flavor, some sellers add coconut meat. Adding ice makes it even more refreshing! It's the perfect thirst-quencher in Bali's hot weather, instantly relieving your thirst.

Loloh Cemcem is not just a refreshing drink but also offers multiple health benefits, such as lowering high blood pressure and improving digestion. Because of these advantages, visitors to Penglipuran village often try Loloh Cemcem. It's also easy to find, as almost every resident sells it, usually right at their doorstep. A bottle of Loloh Cemcem is very affordable, so you can try it without worrying about the price.

📍Next, we visited ****Tirta Empul Temple****, where we had to wear sarongs to enter. We witnessed solemn traditional rituals and felt the strong religious atmosphere.

📍In the afternoon, we headed to ****Alas Harum Rice Terrace Club****. Initially, we were drawn by their pool and rice terrace swings. However, perhaps because we arrived in the afternoon, the place was crowded, and the pool was packed like dumplings in a pot, which I found a bit overwhelming. I didn't end up swimming, and the experience didn't quite meet my expectations. I felt there could be better options for the price🥲.

📍For dinner, Agus took us to a nearby restaurant to try ****pork ribs**** and ****dirty duck****, though these dishes didn't quite agree with my roommate's stomach🫠.

🏠 Returned to our bamboo villa, wrapping up a fulfilling day.

### Day 4

📍 Our **Lovina** dolphin-watching tour was arranged by Agus 🫶🏻. The night before, Agus drove us back to our villa, a journey that took about three hours to his home. By 2:30 AM, he was back to pick us up for the trip to Lovina, leaving him barely an hour of sleep—truly a testament to his dedication 🫠.

(Photo provided by Feilin Huang)

After a long wait in Lovina, we finally spotted a few adorable dolphins 🐬🐬. Though there weren’t many, the anticipation made the experience delightful.

📍 After the dolphin-watching, we headed to **Tanah Lot Temple** in Canggu, arriving around noon. Due to high tide, we couldn’t go down, so the crowd wasn’t as large as others had mentioned.

(Photo provided by Feilin Huang)

### Day 5

📍In the morning, we arrived at **Pantai Batu Barak**, a fantastic spot for photography, though it was quite crowded. There’s an entrance fee, and you’ll need to take an additional bus from the parking lot to the cliffside road—something to keep in mind! 🤣

📍At noon, we had lunch at **Malini**, where the stunning cliffside ocean views left us in awe. Enjoying our meal with such a backdrop made the experience even more delightful. While it wasn’t too crowded at midday, if you want a seat by the cliff, it’s best to book in advance, as those spots fill up quickly.

📍Next, we visited **GWK Cultural Park**, a grand and solemn park, though I found the ticket price a bit steep, so I opted not to go upstairs 🥲.

📍In the evening, we headed to **Rock Bar** to enjoy the sunset. Since we weren’t staying at Ayana, we had to meet a minimum spending requirement.

It’s worth mentioning that shortly after we arrived at Rock Bar, the sun set—luckily, we didn’t have to wait in line. However, the place was packed with visitors soaking in the sunset views. Once the sun dipped below the horizon, many people seemed to wrap up their plans, and the crowd thinned out quickly, leaving the atmosphere much more serene.

During our Bali trip, we were fortunate to meet an amazing driver—****Agus Jon Devn****. Here’s our honest and heartfelt review of our experience. ### Why recommend Agus Jon Devn?

🫶🏻 ****Customized Itinerary Planning****

We initially gave Agus a rough idea of the places we wanted to visit, and he immediately crafted the most efficient route based on our preferences. Moreover, if there were any last-minute changes, he was flexible enough to adjust on the fly, ensuring our plans remained seamless. This made our trip relaxed and stress-free, with no pressure whatsoever.

🫶🏻 ****Hidden Deals on Tours & Massages****

Agus wasn't just our driver - he helped us book various tours and massages! Surprisingly, the prices were cheaper than what we found online or on local menus. It felt like having an insider's privilege, saving us quite a bit of money.

🫶🏻 ****Excellent Time Management****

Agus was always punctual for our appointments, often arriving early. This made us both surprised and a bit embarrassed, as he was usually ready before us, waiting by the car with a smile when we arrived 🤣.

🫶🏻 ****Part-time Guide & Cultural Interpreter****

At tourist spots, Agus didn't just stay in the car - he accompanied us inside, acting as a temporary guide and enthusiastically explaining local culture and history. Although our English wasn't great and we sometimes struggled to understand, his passion still conveyed his deep love for this land. However, it's worth noting that driving is a driver's primary job, not guiding. If your driver doesn't provide tour services, that's completely normal; if they do, consider it a bonus!

🫶🏻 ****Shopping Price Negotiator****

When shopping at markets, Agus even helped us pick fruits and negotiate prices with local vendors. We often found that prices quoted to us became lower after his intervention, making us feel like we had a shopping assistant! 😆

🫶🏻 ****Unbeatable Driving Skills****

His driving skills were truly reassuring. I usually get carsick easily, but I always slept peacefully in Agus's car without any discomfort. Especially during the two days we stayed at a treehouse villa, the roads were extremely narrow and winding - what might be challenging for most drivers was no problem for Agus. His skills were truly admirable! 👍👍

🫶🏻 **Thoughtful Luggage Storage**

One day we planned a day trip to an outer island, and Agus kindly let us store our luggage in his car, allowing us to explore unburdened without carrying heavy bags. Such thoughtful service really made a difference!

🫶🏻 **Professional Photographer**

Agus also doubled as our photographer, excelling at both photos and videos, capturing many precious moments and memories for us.

🫶🏻 **Souvenir Shopping Expert**

Whenever we mentioned souvenirs we wanted to buy, Agus was always attentive. When we passed by certain shops, he would stop to let us shop, and even took the time to buy the cigarettes we mentioned wanting. His thoughtfulness truly touched us! 🥹

In summary, Agus Jon Devn wasn’t just a driver—he was like a thoughtful caretaker and friend for our trip. His professionalism, enthusiasm, and attention to detail made our Bali journey even more fulfilling and worry-free. Next time we visit Bali, we’ll definitely reach out to him again!
