This time, we chose Bali as our travel destination for a 6-day exploration. As a couple, we hoped to experience local culture, natural scenery, and cuisine on this beautiful island. During our trip, we visited Seminyak Beach, the spectacular Nusa Penida, and traditional Tampaksiring, with the assistance of a local driver to make our journey smoother. Let's share the highlights of this trip!

### Day 1

We arrived at the airport around 14:40. Since we had applied for an electronic visa in advance, the immigration process was very smooth, and the tourist tax was easily handled. Unfortunately, waiting for our luggage took an hour, which made us a bit anxious.

Since Agus had a last-minute issue, his nephew Oddy came to pick us up. First, he took us to exchange money. Then, Oddy quickly drove us to La Brisa, allowing us to enjoy dinner before sunset.

After dinner, we visited the nearby Canggu Center market to experience the lively local atmosphere.

🏠 We spent our first night at a hotel in Seminyak, ending a wonderful day.

---

### Day 2

Today was all about exploring the east and west coasts of **Nusa Penida**, with Agus handling our tickets and including lunch 🫶🏻.

Around 6:30 AM, Agus arrived punctually at our villa to pick us up, took us to the harbor to collect our tickets, and guided us on how to proceed, so there was no worry about getting lost 😌. Our itinerary for the day included the stunning beaches of **Diamond Beach** and **Kelingking Beach**. Although we had plenty of time, the trek down to the beaches was quite exhausting for someone like me who doesn’t exercise regularly, especially given the poor trail conditions—it felt like an adventure at times 😂.

After returning to **Sanur** harbor in the afternoon, Agus took us for a massage session that lasted about two hours. Thanks to the discount he negotiated beforehand, the price was significantly lower than the menu rate 👍.

🏠 We stayed at a bamboo villa located in Tampaksiring, Ubud, ending a fulfilling yet tiring day.

### Day 3

📍**Penglipuran Village**, recognized as one of the top three cleanest villages in the world. Visiting in the morning meant fewer tourists, allowing us to quietly enjoy the local scenery.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_139f7058772c6fa1200a75cee97ac9af.jpg)

Here, we also sampled local specialties—**salak** (snake fruit) and **Loloh Cemcem**, which was a bit challenging for me to adapt to. According to Agus, it’s made from coconut water mixed with ground plant leaves, offering a sweet and sour taste. While not bad, it’s definitely an acquired taste—give it a try if you get the chance 😆.

![ Loloh Cemcem](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_191755c6fb20e4272bb5923d0a7dfab8.jpg)

> Note: Loloh Cemcem is a traditional drink from Penglipuran Village. Cemcem leaves are one of the main specialties of Penglipuran Village. Local residents turn these leaves into a fresh beverage that is beneficial for health.

> This green, healthy drink is often thought to have a bitter taste. Many believe that cemcem leaves inherently produce bitterness when used in food. In fact, this is a misconception. When you drink Loloh Cemcem, you won't feel any bitterness because palm sugar syrup balances it out.

> During the preparation process, cemcem leaves are mixed with other plants such as jatropha leaves, betel leaves, and coral tree leaves. These four types of leaves are crushed and then soaked in warm water. Afterward, cinnamon and palm sugar syrup are added to neutralize the bitterness of the leaves. To enhance the flavor, some sellers intentionally add coconut meat. If ice is added, the drink becomes even more refreshing! It's the perfect thirst-quencher in Bali's hot weather. Your thirst will disappear instantly.

> Loloh Cemcem is not just a refreshing drink; it also offers various health benefits. For example, it can help lower high blood pressure and improve digestive issues. Because of these advantages, visitors to Penglipuran Village often try Loloh Cemcem. This drink is also easy to find. Almost all residents of Penglipuran Village sell it, usually right in front of their homes. A bottle of Loloh Cemcem is very affordable, so you can try it without worrying about the price.

📍During our break, Agus treated us to various types of coffee, including the famous **Kopi Luwak**💩. Unfortunately, this time, we really didn't get used to the taste😅.

📍Next, we visited **Tirta Empul Temple**, where we had to wear sarongs to enter. We witnessed solemn traditional rituals and felt a strong religious atmosphere.

📍In the afternoon, we headed to **Alas Harum Rice Terrace Club**. Initially, we were drawn by their pool and rice terrace swings, but perhaps because we arrived in the afternoon, it was quite crowded. The pool was packed like dumplings in a pot, which made me a bit uncomfortable, so I didn't go in. The actual experience didn't quite meet expectations, and I felt there could be better options for this price🥲.

📍For dinner, Agus took us to a nearby restaurant to try **pork ribs** and **dirty duck**. However, these dishes didn't quite agree with my roommate's stomach🫠.

📍晚餐時，Agus帶我們到附近的一家餐廳品嚐**豬肋排**和**髒鴨餐**，不過這些食物可能不太適合室友的胃🫠。

🏠 Returned to our bamboo villa, ending a fulfilling day.

### Day 4

📍 The **Lovina** dolphin-watching tour was arranged by Agus 🫶🏻. The night before, Agus drove us back to our villa, and it took him about three hours to return home. At 2:30 AM, he came back to pick us up for the trip to Lovina, leaving him with barely an hour of sleep. He really went above and beyond 🫠.

(Provided by Feilin Huang)

After waiting for quite some time in Lovina, we finally spotted a few adorable dolphins 🐬🐬. Although there weren’t many, the anticipation still brought us joy.

📍 After the dolphin-watching, we headed to **Tanah Lot Temple** in Canggu. We arrived around noon, but due to high tide, we couldn’t go down to the temple. As a result, the crowd wasn’t as large as some online reviews had suggested.

(Provided by Feilin Huang)

### Day 5

📍In the morning, we arrived at **Pantai Batu Barak**, an excellent spot for photography, though it was quite crowded. Entry requires a toll fee, and an additional bus ride is needed from the parking lot to the cliffside road. Consider this before visiting! 🤣

(Provided by Feilin Huang)

📍At noon, we had lunch at **Malini**, where the stunning cliffside views left us in awe. Enjoying our meal with such scenery was truly delightful. While it wasn’t too crowded at midday, if you want a seat by the cliff, it’s best to book in advance, as those spots are usually fully occupied.

(Provided by Feilin Huang)

📍Next, we visited **GWK Cultural Park**, a grand and solemn park, though I found the entrance fee a bit steep, so I didn’t go up to the higher levels 🥲.

📍In the evening, we headed to **Rock Bar** to enjoy the sunset. Since we weren’t staying at Ayana, we had to pay a minimum spend fee.

It’s worth mentioning that shortly after we arrived at Rock Bar, the sun set. Luckily, we didn’t have to wait in line. However, the place was packed with tourists enjoying the sunset. Once the sun went down, many people seemed to wrap up their visit, and the crowd dispersed quickly, making the atmosphere much more peaceful.

Both the pool and the room exceeded our expectations. The pool was spacious, giving us plenty of room to relax, especially in the evening when the atmosphere by the pool was incredibly soothing. The room was also spacious and clean, with every detail thoughtfully designed, allowing us to enjoy a high-quality rest environment during our trip. The only minor drawback was some issues with the hot water supply—it couldn’t fill the bathtub completely, and the shower’s hot water wasn’t very consistent. ### Why Recommend Agus Jon Devn?

🫶🏻 **Customized Itinerary Planning**

We initially gave Agus a rough idea of the places we wanted to visit, and he immediately crafted the most efficient route based on our preferences. Not only that, but he was also flexible enough to adjust the plan on the fly if anything changed, ensuring our trip remained smooth and stress-free. This made our journey relaxed and spontaneous, without any pressure.

🫶🏻 **Booking Tours & Hidden Spa Deals**

Agus wasn't just our driver—he also helped us book various tours and spa treatments! Surprisingly, the prices he got us were even lower than what we found online or on local menus. It felt like having an insider's privilege, saving us quite a bit of money.

🫶🏻 **Excellent Time Management**

Every time we had a scheduled pickup, Agus wasn’t just punctual—he often arrived early. This left us both amazed and a little embarrassed, as he was usually ready before us, waiting by the car with a smile when we came out 🤣.

🫶🏻 **Part-Time Guide & Cultural Ambassador**

When we arrived at attractions, Agus wasn’t just a driver—he accompanied us inside, stepping in as a guide and enthusiastically sharing local culture and history. Although our English wasn’t great and we struggled to understand everything, his passion still made us feel his deep connection to the land. That said, it’s worth noting that a driver’s main job is driving, not guiding. If your driver doesn’t offer tour services, that’s completely normal; if they do, consider it a bonus!

🫶🏻 **Helping with Shopping Bargains**

When shopping at markets, Agus even helped us pick out fruit and negotiate prices with vendors. Often, the prices quoted to us were lower after he stepped in, making us feel like we had a shopping assistant on our side! 😆

🫶🏻 **Unmatched Driving Skills**

His driving skills were truly reassuring. I usually get car sick, but I slept peacefully every time I was in Agus’s car, with no discomfort at all. Especially on the two days we stayed at a treehouse villa, where the roads were super narrow and winding—what might be a challenge for most drivers was no problem for Agus. His skills were truly impressive! 👍👍

🫶🏻 **Thoughtful Luggage Storage**

One day we planned a day trip to the outer islands, and Agus kindly let us store our luggage in his car, allowing us to travel light without carrying bulky bags. Such thoughtful service really added value to our experience!

🫶🏻 **Professional Photographer**

Agus also acted as our photographer, excelling in both photos and videos, helping us capture many beautiful memories.

🫶🏻 **Souvenir Shopping Expert**

Whenever we mentioned souvenirs we wanted to buy, Agus was always attentive. When we passed by certain shops, he would stop to let us shop, and even once took the time to buy the cigarettes we mentioned wanting. His level of thoughtfulness truly moved us! 🥹

In summary, Agus Jon Devn is not just a driver but also a thoughtful caretaker and friend during our trip. His professionalism, enthusiasm, and attentiveness made our Bali journey even more fulfilling and worry-free. We will definitely seek him out the next time we visit Bali!
