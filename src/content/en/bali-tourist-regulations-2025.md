Bali, as a globally popular tourist destination, has recently implemented stricter regulations for foreign visitors. The Bali Provincial Government officially issued **Announcement No. 7 of 2025**, clearly outlining tourist behavior guidelines, including mandatory obligations, prohibited actions, and penalties for violations, to preserve local culture and the environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e38d91165546796db77a86f37dbb3076.png)

## **Key Points of the New Regulations: Civilized Tourism, Heavy Penalties for Violations**

Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** emphasized that the new regulations aim to ensure foreign tourists respect local culture and reduce inappropriate behavior. Although similar regulations were issued in 2023, this update is more detailed to address various issues that have arisen over the past year and a half.

## **Tourists Must Comply with the Following**

1. **Respect Religious Culture**
2. - When entering temples or visiting religious relics, maintain solemnity and avoid desecration.
   - If encountering local ceremonies, observe proper etiquette and avoid disruptions.
3. **Hire Licensed Guides**
4. - When sightseeing in Bali, tourists must be accompanied by a licensed guide (or a [professional private driver](/blog/bali-private-car-drivers-guide/)) to ensure understanding of local customs and environmental regulations.
5. **Transaction and Payment Rules**
6. - For currency exchange, use officially authorized institutions (see details [here](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)).
   - Use Indonesian Rupiah for transactions.

## **Transportation and Behavior Guidelines**

- **Driving Regulations**:
- - Motorcycle riders must wear helmets and possess a valid international or domestic driver’s license.
  - Drunk driving, overloading, or reckless driving is prohibited.
  - When renting a car, choose vehicles provided by legal rental companies.
- **Accommodation Restrictions**:
- - Only stay in government-approved accommodations and adhere to specific regulations at various attractions.

## **Prohibited Actions, Violators Will Be Penalized**

- **Religious Sites**:
- - Non-worship purposes are not allowed in the temple's core areas.
  - Climbing sacred trees or engaging in disrespectful behavior is prohibited.
- **Environmental Protection**:
- - Littering is strictly prohibited, especially polluting water sources.
  - Restrictions on the use of single-use plastic products. Tourists are advised to avoid using plastic bags, straws, disposable utensils, etc., and are encouraged to bring their own reusable bags or containers.
- **Behavior**:
- - Offensive language or improper conduct is not allowed.
  - Commercial activities or illegal work without permission are prohibited.

The governor warned that **violators will face legal consequences**, including fines, deportation, or even criminal charges. If the public notices any violations by tourists, they can call the hotline: **081-287-590-999**.

This new regulation takes effect immediately, aiming to enhance the quality of tourism while protecting Bali's cultural and natural environment. Travelers planning to visit must comply to avoid legal issues that could disrupt their trip.
