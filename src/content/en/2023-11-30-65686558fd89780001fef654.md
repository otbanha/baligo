You can purchase the appropriate adapter at local supermarkets, known as 'Adaptor' in Indonesian (pronounced: ah-dah-pu-tour). Alternatively, you can ask your hotel if they provide adapters, but not all hotels offer them. It's best to buy one yourself at a local supermarket to ensure compatibility. They are inexpensive, costing around NT$30–40 (approximately USD$1).

Typically, you may need at least one adapter along with a multi-socket extension cord that supports 220V to ensure all your devices can be used in Indonesia and Bali. Remember, when purchasing an extension cord, avoid cheap options and opt for a trustworthy brand to ensure safe usage of all your appliances.

## How to Prepare Your USB Charger?

## 

Most modern USB chargers (for phones, tablets, etc.) that travelers bring abroad already support '100V-240V wide voltage.' As long as the charger's input voltage range includes 220V, it can be used directly in Bali without an additional transformer. Before your trip, check the small print on your charger—if it says 'Input: 100-240V,' it’s universally compatible. If it only indicates 110V, you’ll need to replace it.

現代人出國必帶的USB充電器（如手機、平板充電頭）大多已支援「100V-240V寬電壓」，只要確認充電器上標示的輸入電壓（Input）範圍包含220V，即可直接在峇里島使用，無需額外變壓器。出發前查看充電器上的小字，例如標有「Input: 100-240V」即可通用；若僅標示110V則需更換。

## Can I Bring My Own Hair Dryer/Curling Iron?

Most hotels provide hair dryers for guests, though some may not.

It's advisable to contact your hotel beforehand to confirm if they offer a hair dryer. If not, or if you prefer using your own specific model or brand, consider purchasing a 220V hair dryer locally or using a voltage converter.

Hair dryers purchased in Taiwan typically support only up to 110V, which poses a problem in Indonesia and Bali where the standard voltage is 220V. A 110V hair dryer not only won't function properly but could also be damaged due to voltage mismatch.

The safest and most convenient solution in such cases is to buy a 220V-compatible hair dryer locally. This ensures it works correctly in Indonesia and Bali while preventing damage from voltage incompatibility.

If you insist on using your own hair dryer/curling iron, consider using a transformer or converter. A transformer can adjust 220V down to 110V, but ensure it provides sufficient wattage (W) and meets safety standards to avoid damaging your appliance or posing a safety hazard.

For travelers heading to Indonesia and Bali, understanding these voltage and socket details is crucial. Avoid using cheap, unknown chargers as low-quality products can overheat at 220V, posing safety risks. Equip yourself with the right adapters and multi-socket extension cords to ensure your electronics function smoothly, allowing you to fully enjoy your vacation without worrying about compatibility issues!
