This afternoon, August 17, a fire started in the basement of Ubud Market and continued until evening without being extinguished. The fire has left 1,033 vendors unable to continue their businesses.

The fire began spreading in the market basement at 13:00 local time. By evening, over ten fire trucks had been dispatched to the scene, but the flames remained uncontrollable.

Gianyar Regional Secretary I Dewa Gede Alit Mudiarta personally inspected the firefighting efforts. He stated, 'We are doing our utmost to extinguish the fire, prioritizing the safety of lives first, followed by addressing the vendors' losses. As the firefighting is still ongoing, we cannot yet determine the exact extent of the damage.'

According to available data, there are 1,033 vendors in the market, with approximately 400 operating in the basement—the origin of the fire—who are directly affected.

Dewa Alit added, 'We will discuss the relocation and support for the vendors in a meeting. For now, our focus is on extinguishing the fire as quickly as possible and preventing it from spreading to other areas.'

As of now, the cause of the fire is still under investigation. It is reported that the source may have been the motorcycle parking area in the basement.
