When planning your Bali trip, booking accommodations is one of the most important steps. Bali offers a wide range of luxury hotels, exquisite villas, and affordable stays, but how do you find something that fits your budget while still being comfortable? Here are some money-saving tips to help you save big when booking accommodations in Bali.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e1458b18b30d2ce80d48b7e014546a95.png)

Want to have an amazing trip without breaking the bank? Check out these smart saving tips!

## Avoid Peak Travel Seasons

First, let’s start with choosing the right time to travel! Try to avoid visiting Bali during peak seasons, as flight tickets, accommodations, and activity prices tend to skyrocket. Opting for the off-season or less busy periods not only helps you save on flights and accommodations but also allows for a more peaceful travel experience.

So when is Bali’s peak travel season? Generally, the dry season with lower chances of rainfall is the best time to visit Bali, typically from April to September, which is also Bali’s peak travel season. What are the downsides of avoiding peak season? Check out [this guide](/blog/bali-best-time-to-visit/) to learn more about Bali’s rainy season.

Besides distinguishing peak and off-peak seasons by weather, here are five periods when Bali sees a surge in visitors, ranked by crowd/traffic volume:

1. Indonesia's Eid al-Fitr holiday is a crucial time. A large number of local Indonesian tourists flock to this paradise island during this approximately ten-day holiday. This is Bali's busiest peak season!
2. Christmas-New Year holidays typically attract global tourists, as it's a long holiday for most Western countries, and Indonesians also try to take time off. Thus, the Christmas-New Year holiday ranks second in terms of crowd and traffic. Here's a short video showing [the situation on the airport highway on December 29, 2023](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide/permalink/3579975912271532/).
3. Around Chinese New Year is a special time, with holidays ranging from two to five days in countries like China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Singapore, South Korea, and Vietnam; the Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia have a one-day holiday on the first day of the lunar new year. The Chinese New Year holiday ranks third in attracting large numbers of tourists, causing crowded tourist spots.
4. The period from July 1 to September 5 coincides with Europe's long holidays and the summer vacation for students in the Northern Hemisphere, leading to a travel trend where many families and students take this time to vacation in Bali. Summer flight tickets and accommodation prices are never cheap!
5. Australian school holidays and Indonesian international school holidays (one week between September 20 and October 10) also bring a significant number of Australian tourists and families from Indonesian international schools, who embark on trips during this period.

During these peak periods, the number of tourists surges dramatically, and prices for accommodations and attractions may rise due to high demand. Therefore, if you plan to visit Bali during these busy seasons, it's best to book in advance and prepare thoroughly to ensure a pleasant and worry-free trip.

## Choose Affordable Yet High-Value Accommodations

Accommodation can take up a significant portion of your travel budget! Bali offers countless lodging options, but don't be swayed by luxurious resorts alone. Opting for affordable yet quality accommodations can stretch your budget further. Additionally, consider sharing a room with friends—this not only reduces accommodation costs but also adds to the fun of your trip.

## Plan and Book in Advance

Planning ahead and booking accommodations early can help you secure better rates. Many hotels and websites offer discounts for early bookings, sometimes saving you up to 30%.

## **Subscribe to Promotional Emails**

Subscribe to email notifications from hotels and booking websites to stay updated on the latest promotions and special offers. Sometimes they offer limited-time deals that allow you to book at a lower price.

## **Pay in Local Currency**

When paying for accommodation with a credit card, prioritize settling in the local currency. This avoids unnecessary currency conversion fees, helping you save money to the greatest extent.
