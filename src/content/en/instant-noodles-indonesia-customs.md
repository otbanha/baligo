**Bottom line**: **Instant noodles with meat chunks cannot be brought into Indonesia** (meat products must be declared upon entry; failure to declare may result in fines or confiscation). **Milk powder, UHT milk, cheese, and other heat-sterilized packaged dairy products are allowed**, but raw milk/fresh milk is not. If the soup base of the instant noodles is in liquid form, it must also comply with the carry-on rule of no more than 100ml per packet.

Bringing Taiwanese instant noodles to Bali—especially those containing meat—is a common concern for many travelers. Instant noodles aren't just a tasty part of Taiwanese life; they're a taste of home. So, can we bring these treats on the plane and enjoy a bit of Taiwan while abroad? This article will answer that question in detail.

## Airline Regulations

Generally, most airlines don't have many restrictions on passengers bringing food on board, as long as it complies with liquid regulations (liquid volume not exceeding 100ml). Most airlines allow passengers to bring instant noodles, but note that some noodle soup packets are considered liquids, so they must be packed separately and each packet must not exceed 100ml.

Generally, carry-on luggage cannot contain the following items:

- Food/fresh produce: Fresh fruits, vegetables, and meat products cannot be brought in or out of the country. Dried meat and meat strips are also strictly regulated, and even dog food cans or instant noodles with meat chunks are not allowed on the plane.
- Dairy products: Raw milk and fresh milk cannot be brought on the plane, but packaged UHT milk, milk powder, cheese, etc., are allowed as they have been heat-sterilized.

Additionally, different airlines may have different regulations, so it's best to check your specific airline's policy before departure.

## Indonesia Entry Regulations

## 印尼入境規定

Indonesia has strict regulations on bringing food into the country, especially meat products. Any food containing meat must be declared upon entry. If found undeclared, you may face fines or confiscation. So, before bringing Taiwanese instant noodles with meat into Bali, make sure you understand and follow Indonesia's rules.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **How many packs of instant noodles can I bring?**
2. - Generally, as long as they don't contain meat, bringing a few packs shouldn't be a problem.
3. **Which instant noodles are not allowed?**
4. - Noodles with a lot of meat may be prohibited from entry.
5. **What happens if I don't declare them?**
6. - Failure to declare may result in fines or confiscation, and in severe cases, even denial of entry.
7. **Can I bring other meat-containing foods?**
8. - Other meat-containing foods also need to be declared and must comply with relevant regulations.
