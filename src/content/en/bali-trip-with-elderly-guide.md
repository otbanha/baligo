**Key Takeaways**: Tried-and-tested tips for traveling to Bali with seniors — ① Complete the health QR code, e-visa, and customs declaration online before arrival (now integrated into the All Indonesia entry card, all in one go); ② **Hire a private car for the entire trip**, communicate the itinerary with the driver in English via LINE, and arrange a Chinese-speaking guide before departure; ③ Focus the itinerary on **Ubud’s mountain scenery and culture**, keep a slow pace, and schedule ample rest time, moving to a southern beach hotel only at the end for a relaxed transition to the airport.

> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Claire Chen](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/17yjQ17JHi/):

On this Bali trip with my parents, the main focus was on mountain landscapes and local culture, so we spent most of our time in the Ubud area. Only toward the end of the trip did we gradually move toward the airport, choosing to relax at a hotel near the southern beach. The itinerary covered several must-visit spots, and I deliberately planned activities that would allow my parents to unwind, with plenty of rest time built in.

## 【Preparing for Bali Entry】

The entry process for Bali can actually be handled before departure. There are plenty of online guides for the application steps. I’ve summarized the post-customs procedures below for your reference:

**Step 1: Health QR Code Scan**

- Register your health QR code in advance here: [Click to Register](https://sshp.kemkes.go.id/register?travel_mode=Air)

**Step 2: Apply for a Visa in Advance to Avoid On-Site Queues**

- If you apply for a tourist visa in advance, you can skip the on-site queuing process: [Visa Application](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/)
- You can also submit a traveler responsibility declaration at the same time: [Love Bali](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/)

**Third Stage: Departure QR Code Customs Declaration**

- This step can be completed before departure: [Customs Declaration Portal](https://ecd.beacukai.go.id/)

With these preparations in advance, the arrival process in Bali becomes much smoother, saving you the time of filling out forms and queuing on the spot. If you're still unclear about the process, you can refer to the detailed explanation here. ## 【Communication Tips for Charter and Itinerary Planning】

The arrangement for this charter service mainly involved initial communication with the driver in English via LINE. As the departure date approached, we also invited a Chinese-speaking guide, Ani, to assist with our itinerary. When discussing with your charter driver, it's recommended to provide the following information in advance to help them better assist with planning:

(Provided by Claire Chen)

1. **Basic Information**
2. - **Number of travel days**, **hotel location**, **flight details**, and **group size**, etc., to help the driver plan the route and arrange pickups.

3. **Itinerary Planning Mode**
4. - **Preliminary Planning**: This is the approach I used. First, I browsed popular Bali itineraries in the community, sketched out a rough travel framework, and booked hotels separately. Then, I provided the accommodation details and a preliminary itinerary to the driver for confirmation and a quote.

- **Fully Hand Over to the Guide**: Suitable for those without a specific plan. Just provide the **number of travel days**, **preferences**, and **group characteristics**, such as the age range of the participants, and the guide can arrange everything accordingly.

- **Special Activity Requests**: For activities like mountain itineraries (e.g., relaxation, hiking, sunrise volcano tours) or water activities (e.g., snorkeling, diving, river rafting), sharing these details helps the guide incorporate suitable experiences into the trip.
- **Restaurant Preferences**: If you have specific dining preferences (e.g., wanting to try local cuisine or unique restaurants), let the guide know in advance so they can recommend convenient options along the route.

7. **Language Options**
   Our private drivers primarily offer English service. If you're confident in English communication, there's no need to hire a separate Chinese-speaking guide. I personally used the driver for the relaxed first and last days of my trip, while for the longer excursions in between, I hired a Chinese-speaking guide named Ani for full-day support. During pre-trip communication, you can also flexibly use ChatGPT or Google Translate to ensure both sides clearly understand each other's needs.

With this level of preparation and communication, your private car travel will go more smoothly, better aligning with your trip's needs and delivering a fulfilling Bali experience.

## 【Recommended Private Driver and Tour Guide Services for First-Time Bali Travelers】

This is my first time writing a recommendation, especially for those visiting a Southeast Asian island for the first time, particularly travelers who want to skip the hassle of detailed itinerary planning. The local drivers and guides in Bali were incredibly helpful, making the entire trip relaxed and enjoyable. Here are my tips and insights:

1. **SIM Card and Currency Exchange Needs**
   If you need a local SIM card or currency exchange, just let your driver or guide know. I had already taken care of these details in Taiwan, so I could start the trip right upon arrival.
2. **Itinerary Planning Tips**
   Some locations in Bali are quite far apart by car, so it's best to prioritize your accommodation when planning your itinerary to avoid unnecessary back-and-forth. Arranging your schedule around your lodging makes the trip much more relaxing.
3. **Activity Requests**
   If you have specific day trips or other activities in mind, feel free to discuss them with your driver or guide. They'll help arrange everything smoothly, so you can just follow along without worrying about the details.
4. **Transparent Pricing**
   The private car service fees for this trip were clear and transparent, adjusted based on the daily route and settled each day. Entrance fees and additional day trip costs were calculated separately and could be discussed and confirmed before departure.
5. **Thoughtful Company of Chinese-Speaking Guide Ani**
   On the tours Ani accompanied, she stayed with us throughout and shared insights into Bali's culture, history, and customs. This companionship was especially valuable for me and my elderly family members! Plus, if we wanted to buy souvenirs, Ani would take us directly to purchase them or buy them on our behalf based on photos we provided. It's best to let her know in advance so she can arrange it properly.
6. **Attentive Communication and Photography**
   Ani and the driver not only helped take photos throughout the trip but also communicated with hotel front desks about accommodation details, so we didn't have to worry about language barriers.
7. **Local Traffic and Driver Skills**
   Traffic in Bali can sometimes be congested, especially in the city, but the driver was very steady and reassuring even on mountain roads. We were also lucky to encounter almost no traffic jams during this trip.
8. **Companionship Like Family**
   Throughout the journey, the driver and guide cared for us like family or friends, making the trip not only smoother but also incredibly relaxing. I'm grateful for their company and support during this trip.
