On this Bali trip with my elderly parents, the main journey centered on mountain scenery and local culture, so most of our itinerary was spent in the Ubud area. Only toward the end of the trip did we gradually move toward the airport, choosing to relax at a hotel near the southern beach. This travel itinerary covered several must-visit attractions, and I deliberately planned activities that would allow the elders to unwind, with ample rest time built in.

## 【Bali Entry Preparation】

The entry process for Bali can actually be prepared before departure. There are many online guides for the application process. I've organized the steps after clearing customs below for your reference:

**Step 1: Health QR Code Scan**

- Register your health QR code in advance here: [Click to Register](https://sshp.kemkes.go.id/register?travel_mode=Air)

**Step 2: Apply for Visa in Advance to Avoid On-Site Queues**

- If you apply for a tourist visa in advance, you can skip the on-site queuing process: [Visa Application](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/)
- You can also submit the Passenger Responsibility Declaration at the same time: [Love Bali](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/)

**Third Stage: Departure QR Code Customs Declaration**

- This step can be completed before departure: [Customs Declaration Portal](https://ecd.beacukai.go.id/)

With these preparations in advance, the arrival process in Bali becomes much smoother, saving you the time of filling out forms and waiting in line on the spot. If you're still unclear about the process, you can refer to the detailed explanation here. ## 【Tips for Communicating About Charter and Itinerary Planning】

For this charter service arrangement, we first communicated with the driver in English via LINE for initial discussions. As the departure date approached, we also invited a Chinese-speaking guide, Ani, to assist with our itinerary. When discussing with your charter driver, it's recommended to provide the following information in advance to help them better assist with the planning:

![（Claire Chen提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_696ef9532aca81d419e61d7f96d61b1b.jpg)

(Provided by Claire Chen)

1. **Basic Information**
2. - **Number of travel days**, **hotel location**, **flight details**, and **group size**, etc., to help the driver plan the route and arrange pickups.
3. **Itinerary Discussion Mode**
4. - **Preliminary Planning**: This is the approach I used. First, I checked popular itinerary directions for Bali within the community, drafted a rough travel framework, and booked hotels separately. Then, I provided the accommodation details and a preliminary itinerary plan to the driver for confirmation and quotation.

- **Full Planning by the Guide**: Suitable for those without a specific plan. Just provide the **number of travel days**, **preferences**, and **group characteristics**, such as the age range of participants, and the guide can arrange accordingly.

5. **Itinerary Preferences**
6. - **Age Range of Travel Companions**: Different combinations of families, seniors, or friends can affect itinerary preferences, so it's helpful to inform the driver and guide in advance.

- **Special Activity Requests**: For example, mountain itineraries (like relaxing, hiking, or sunrise volcano tours) or water activities (such as snorkeling, diving, or river tubing). Sharing this info helps the guide add suitable experiences to the trip.
- **Restaurant Preferences**: If you have specific dining preferences (e.g., wanting to try local cuisine or unique restaurants), let the guide know in advance so they can recommend places along the route.

7. **Language Choice**
   Our private drivers primarily offer English service. If you're confident in English communication, there's no need to hire a Chinese-speaking guide. I kept the first and last days of my trip light, so the driver was enough; for the middle days with longer distances, I hired a Chinese-speaking guide named Ani for full-day company. During pre-trip communication, you can flexibly use ChatGPT or Google Translate to ensure both sides understand each other's needs.

With this kind of preparation and communication, your private car trip will go more smoothly and better align with your travel needs, ensuring a fulfilling Bali experience.

## 【Recommended Private Car and Guide Services for First-Time Bali Travelers】

This is my first time writing a recommendation, especially for those heading to a Southeast Asian island for the first time, particularly travelers who want to skip the hassle of detailed itinerary planning. The local drivers and guides in Bali were incredibly helpful, making the entire trip smooth and enjoyable. Here are my tips and insights:

1. **SIM Cards and Currency Exchange**
   If you need a local SIM card or currency exchange, just let your driver or guide know. I had already handled these details back in Taiwan, so I could start the trip right away upon arrival.
2. **Itinerary Planning Tips**
   Some locations in Bali involve long drives, so it's best to prioritize your accommodation when planning your itinerary to avoid unnecessary back-and-forth. Arranging your schedule around your stay makes the trip much more relaxing.
3. **Activity Requests**
   If you have specific day trips or activities in mind, feel free to discuss them with your driver or guide. They'll help arrange everything, so you can just follow along without worrying about the details.
4. **Transparent Pricing**
   The private car fees for this trip were clear and transparent, adjusted based on the daily route and settled each day. Ticket and extra day-trip costs were calculated separately and could be discussed and confirmed before departure.
5. **Thoughtful Company of Chinese-Speaking Guide Ani**
   Ani accompanied us throughout the itinerary, sharing insights into Balinese culture, history, and customs along the way. This companionship was especially valuable for me and my elderly family members! Also, if we wanted to buy souvenirs, Ani would take us directly to the shops or purchase items based on photos we provided. It's best to let her know in advance so she can plan accordingly.
6. **Helpful Communication and Photography**
   Ani and the driver not only helped take photos throughout the trip but also assisted with communicating accommodation details at the hotel front desk, so we never had to worry about language barriers.
7. **Local Traffic and Driver Skills**
   Traffic in Bali can be congested at times, especially in the city, but the driver was very steady and reassuring even on mountain roads. We were also lucky to encounter almost no traffic jams during this trip.
8. **Companionship Like Family**
   Throughout the journey, the driver and guide's care felt like that of family or friends, making the trip not only smoother but also incredibly relaxing. I'm grateful for their company and support during this trip.
