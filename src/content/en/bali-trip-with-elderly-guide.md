This Bali trip with my elderly parents focused mainly on mountain scenery and local culture, so most of our itinerary was spent in the Ubud area. ## 【Preparing for Bali Entry】

The entry process for Bali can actually be prepared before departure. There are many guides online for the application process. I've summarized the steps after clearing customs for your reference:

**Step 1: Health QR Code Scan**

- Register your health QR code in advance here: [Click to Register](https://sshp.kemkes.go.id/register?travel_mode=Air)

**Step 2: Apply for Visa in Advance to Avoid On-Site Queues**

- Applying for a tourist visa early can save you from queuing on the spot: [Visa Application](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/)
- Also, submit the traveler responsibility declaration: [Love Bali](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/)

**Step 3: Customs Declaration via Exit QR Code**

- This step can be completed before departure: [Customs Declaration Portal](https://ecd.beacukai.go.id/)

With these preparations in place, your arrival process in Bali will be much smoother, saving you the hassle of filling out forms and waiting in line on the spot. ## 【Tips for Communicating About Private Car and Itinerary Planning】

During this trip, the private car service arrangement began with initial communication in English with the driver via LINE. As the departure date approached, we also invited a Chinese-speaking guide, Ani, to assist with our itinerary. When discussing with your private car driver, it's recommended to provide the following information in advance to help them better coordinate:

![（Claire Chen提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_696ef9532aca81d419e61d7f96d61b1b.jpg)

(Provided by Claire Chen)

1. **Basic Information**
2. - **Number of travel days**, **hotel locations**, **flight details**, and **group size**, so the driver can help plan routes and arrange pickups.
3. **Itinerary Discussion Mode**
4. - **Preliminary Planning**: This is the approach I used. First, I browsed popular Bali itinerary ideas in online groups, sketched out a rough travel framework, and booked hotels separately. Then, I shared the accommodation info and initial itinerary plan with the driver for confirmation and a quote.

- **Full Delegation to the Guide**: Ideal for those without a specific plan. Just provide the **number of travel days**, **preferences**, and **group characteristics**, such as the age range of participants, and the guide can tailor the arrangements accordingly.

5. **Itinerary Preferences**
6. - **Age Range of Travel Companions**: Different combinations—families with kids, seniors, or friends—affect itinerary preferences, so let the driver and guide know in advance.

- **Special Activity Requests**: For example, mountain excursions (like relaxing, hiking, or sunrise volcano tours) or water activities (like snorkeling, diving, or river tubing). This info helps the guide incorporate suitable experiences into the itinerary.
- **Restaurant Preferences**: If you have specific dining preferences (e.g., craving local cuisine or unique restaurants), let the guide know in advance so they can recommend restaurants along the way.

7. **Language Options**
   Our private driver primarily offers English service. If you're confident in English communication, there's no need to hire a separate Chinese-speaking guide. On my first and last days, I kept the itinerary light and relied on the driver. For the middle days with longer trips, I arranged for a Chinese-speaking guide named Ani to accompany me throughout. During pre-trip communication, you can flexibly use ChatGPT or Google Translate to ensure both sides clearly understand each other's needs.

With this kind of preparation and communication, your private car travel will go more smoothly and better align with your trip's needs, delivering a fulfilling Bali experience.

## 【Recommended Private Car and Guide Services for First-Time Bali Travelers】

This is my first time writing a recommendation, especially for those visiting Southeast Asian islands for the first time, particularly travelers who want to skip the hassle of detailed itinerary planning. The local drivers and guides in Bali were incredibly helpful, making the entire trip relaxed and enjoyable. Here are some tips and insights from my experience:

1. **SIM Card and Currency Exchange Needs**
   If you need a local SIM card or to exchange currency, you can let your driver or guide know at any time. I had already taken care of these details back in Taiwan, so we started the trip right after arriving.
2. **Itinerary Planning Tips**
   Travel times between some locations in Bali can be long, so it's best to prioritize your accommodation when planning your itinerary to avoid unnecessary back-and-forth. Arranging your schedule around your stay can make the trip much more relaxing.
3. **Activity Requests**
   If you have specific day trips or other activities in mind, feel free to discuss them with your driver or guide. They'll help arrange everything smoothly, so you can simply follow along without worrying about the details.
4. **Transparent Pricing**
   The private car fees for this trip were clear and transparent, adjusted based on the daily route, and settled each day. Entrance fees and additional day trip costs were calculated separately and could be discussed and confirmed before departure.
5. **Thoughtful Company of Chinese-Speaking Guide Ani**
   Ani accompanied us throughout the itinerary, introducing Bali's culture, history, and customs along the way. This companionship was especially valuable for me and my elderly family members! Additionally, if we wanted to buy souvenirs, Ani would take us directly to purchase them or buy them on our behalf based on photos we provided. It's best to let her know in advance so she can plan accordingly.
6. **Attentive Communication and Photography**
   Ani and the driver not only helped take photos throughout the trip but also assisted with communicating accommodation details at hotel front desks, so we didn't have to worry about language barriers.
7. **Local Traffic and Driver Skills**
   Traffic in Bali can sometimes be congested, especially in the city, but the driver was very steady and made us feel safe even on mountain roads. We were also lucky to encounter almost no traffic jams during this trip.
8. **Companionship Like Family**
   Throughout the journey, the driver and guide's care made us feel like we were with family or friends. This not only made the trip smoother but also brought a wonderful sense of ease. I'm grateful for their company and support during this trip.
