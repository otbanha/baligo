## Trip Overview

- ****Travel Dates:**** April 12-17, 2023
- ****Travelers:**** 2 people (Mom + her boyfriend). I arranged everything for them to travel independently.

## Pre-Trip Preparation

Having gathered valuable Bali travel information from Facebook groups, now back in Taiwan, I hope to share my mother's travel experience to help others plan enjoyable trips.

## Introduction:

As my mother was busy with work, I arranged this trip for her. Being a photography enthusiast and a vegetarian who isn't picky about food, I needed to find vegetarian-friendly restaurants in advance (Mom is quite flexible when traveling, as long as she can eat her fill without meat).

****Visa + Customs + Tourism Tax:**** I helped them complete all pre-trip procedures online, and they were out of the airport in about 30 minutes.

Additionally, when paying the tourism tax online, I noticed that the first payment attempt always failed. I had to wait a moment and try again for it to succeed.

****Private Car Guide:**** I arranged a private car guide for my mom and her group, Ani and her husband. In online communities, someone recommended a private car service format that includes both a guide and a driver, which I thought was an excellent arrangement. Fortunately, I booked Ani, and she and her husband served as our guide and driver.

## Day 1: Uluwatu Temple Adventure

Their Bali adventure began at Uluwatu Temple. Although the weather wasn't ideal, and they missed the sunset, they still felt the mystical charm of this sacred temple. They had planned to buy tickets for the fire dance on-site, but unfortunately, it was completely sold out, so they missed Bali's most stunning fire dance performance.

## Day 2: A Fairytale Journey in Ubud

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c866025d6c35db35dc6e898935c8ca44.jpg)

Knowing my mom loves taking photos, Ani recommended a swing experience for $40 per person, plus $20 for renting a dress—totally worth it. The photographer’s skills were top-notch, and for just $40 (600k), you can get stunning photos like the ones shown here. A tip for those aiming for ethereal flying shots: definitely rent the dress. Their specially designed dresses have long, flowing skirts that create the perfect floating effect in the air.

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供 + 網路截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3fd5654938f7fafc790509041021cf0.jpg)

Photo provided by Chuang Ling Chun + Screenshot

In the evening, we stayed at Mason Elephant Lodge, where the up-close elephant encounters were unforgettable. We stayed here ten years ago, and those cherished memories are still deeply etched in our hearts. This time, we were fortunate to interact closely with the elephants. My mom said one of the most memorable experiences was being transported from our room to the dining area by an elephant during dinner. We were also lucky to attend the owner’s birthday celebration during our stay and were invited to join the dinner feast for free.

For elephant lovers, this is an exceptional experience. The elephant park seems to treat the animals well, not just as money-making tools, and the park’s income is used for Sumatran elephant conservation. Apart from elephant rides and bathing, there are no other animal performances, so even concerned travelers can feel at ease. Visitors also have the opportunity to spend $3 to buy fruits and feed the elephants themselves. If you want to experience riding an elephant to dinner, you’ll need to dine at the park (you can choose a meal-inclusive package when booking or purchase separately). For those who prefer not to spend extra, you can still get up close with the elephants and appreciate their gentle and adorable nature. (PS. Elephant riding activities have been completely banned across Bali starting in 2026.)

In the evening, I arranged for them to stay at Grün Canggu Garden. When I first saw the unique design of this place during booking, I was immediately captivated. It gives a treehouse-like feel, with stairs on the ground floor, a bathroom halfway up, a small platform on the second floor, and the bedroom on the third floor. The room design is very open, with only the bathroom being enclosed, while the rest of the areas are open spaces. Surrounded by trees, with the sounds of insects and birds, it feels like being immersed in nature, allowing one to fully enjoy the leisure of the holiday.

## Day Five: A Luxurious Stay on the Last Night

For the last night, I arranged for them to stay in a villa, hoping we could enter the room early. That day, I asked our guide Ani to briefly take them to visit Tanah Lot Temple and buy some local specialties.

## Day 6: Farewell to Bali

Since they had a flight to catch that afternoon, my mom and her group returned to the massage parlor from the previous day at 10 AM for one last session. Afterward, they went back to the villa and stayed until the 12 PM checkout time.

We had pre-booked airport transfer service online, and the driver arrived early. The price was very reasonable.

> ▶Highly rated on Klook: [Bali International Airport Transfers](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=808796&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Fairport-transfers%2Fservice%2Fdps-ngurah-rai-bali-international-airport%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Dd218d2ab66)

The advantage of booking online in advance is that you can pay by card and know the exact fare, so there’s no need to keep cash on hand. It’s a great way to spend all your Indonesian Rupiah on the last day! We’ll definitely use this service again on our next trip to Bali.

## Postscript

1. If you love mangosteens, make sure to try them here. At the supermarket, 12 mangosteens cost only USD$3—cheap and delicious! My mom also asked the guide to take them to a roadside stall to buy durian, which cost just USD$10 per fruit. (Read more: [Mangosteen Season in Indonesia/Bali](/blog/2024-04-12-661943c7fd897800016eaf4a/))
2. Traffic in Bali can be quite congested, and the roads aren’t in the best condition, so Google Maps’ estimated travel times should be taken with a grain of salt. (Read more: [Don’t Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/2024-03-19-65ebd5ecfd89780001cf6c8b/))
3. My mom and her group were very satisfied with Ani’s service, so they gave her and her husband a little extra tip on the last day. We also tipped the luggage handlers and massage therapists as a gesture of gratitude. (Read more: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much and When?](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw))
4. We asked the driver-guide to take my mom and her group to exchange money as soon as they got in the car—he took them to a reliable place. (Read more: [Currency Exchange Guide for Independent Travelers](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/))
5. Local SIM cards cost USD$19 for 18GB of data, while roaming with 5GB costs USD$16. I recommend using roaming since my mom didn’t need to look up much information during her trip, and most hotels offer free Wi-Fi. The 5GB was more than enough. (Read more: [Essential Guide: How to Get a SIM Card/Internet in Bali](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/))

My mom thoroughly enjoyed this trip and loved the opportunity to take lots of photos. She especially praised the hotels I chose, saying each one was unique and allowed her to capture many beautiful shots. For her, a great trip is one where she can take plenty of pictures.

Her only regret was not seeing the sunset at Uluwatu Temple! However, after experiencing the swing in Ubud, her friends all complimented her photos.

Exploring Bali by private car gave her a sense of freedom and convenience, and the price was very affordable. The only downside was the food - she wasn't fond of Bali's strong flavors and wished there were more options with less oil and salt.

This Bali trip was filled with surprises and wonderful memories. Whether it was visiting temples, hiking up volcanoes, or getting up close with elephants, every moment was unforgettable. I hope Mom's travelogue can provide some references and help for those planning a trip to Bali. I look forward to setting foot on this island paradise again.
