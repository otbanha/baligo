## Trip Overview

- **Travel Dates:** April 12 to April 17, 2023
- **Travelers:** 2 people (Mom + her boyfriend). I arranged everything for Mom and they went on their own.

## Pre-Trip Preparation

I gathered valuable Bali travel information from Facebook groups. Now that I'm back in Taiwan, I hope to share Mom's travel experience to help others plan enjoyable trips.

## Preface:

Since Mom was busy with work, I arranged this trip for her. Mom is a photography enthusiast and isn't picky about food, but she's a vegetarian, so I needed to find vegetarian-friendly restaurants in advance (Mom is quite easygoing when traveling - as long as she can eat her fill without meat, she's happy).

**Visa + Customs + Tourism Tax:** I helped them complete all pre-trip procedures online, and they walked out of the airport in just about 30 minutes.

Additionally, when paying the tourism tax online, I noticed that the first payment attempt always failed. It was necessary to wait a moment and try again for it to succeed.

**Private Car Guide:** I arranged for my mom and her group to have Ani and her husband as their private car guides. In online communities, someone recommended a service format that includes both a guide and a driver, which I thought was an excellent arrangement. Fortunately, I booked Ani, and she and her husband served as our guide and driver.

## Day 1: Uluwatu Temple Adventure

My mom's Bali adventure began at Uluwatu Temple. Although the weather wasn't ideal and they missed the sunset, they still felt the mystical charm of this sacred temple. They had planned to buy tickets for the fire dance on-site, but unfortunately, they were all sold out, so they missed Bali's most stunning fire dance performance.

## Day 2: A Fairyland Journey in Ubud

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c866025d6c35db35dc6e898935c8ca44.jpg)

Photo provided by Chuang Ling Chun

Knowing my mom loves taking photos, Ani recommended a swing experience for 400k per person, plus 200k for renting a dress—totally worth it. The photographer's skills were top-notch, and for just $20 (600k) per person, you can capture stunning photos like the ones shown. A tip for those wanting to take ethereal flying photos: definitely rent a dress. Their specially designed dresses have long, flowing skirts that create the perfect floating effect in the air.

![圖：Chuang Ling Chun提供 + 網路截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3fd5654938f7fafc790509041021cf0.jpg)

Photo provided by Chuang Ling Chun + Screenshot

In the evening, we stayed at Mason Elephant Lodge, where the close-up elephant encounters were unforgettable. I stayed here ten years ago, and those beautiful memories are still deeply etched in my heart. This time, we were fortunate to get up close with the elephants. My mom said one of the most memorable experiences was being transported from our room to the restaurant by an elephant for dinner. We were also lucky to encounter the owner's birthday celebration during our stay and were invited to join the dinner banquet for free.

For travelers who love elephants, this is an excellent experience. The elephant park seems to treat the elephants well, not just as money-making tools, and the park's income is used for Sumatran elephant conservation. Besides elephant rides and bathing, there are no other animal performances, so concerned travelers can rest assured. Additionally, visitors can spend $3 to buy fruits and feed the elephants personally. If you want to experience riding an elephant to dinner, you’ll need to dine at the park (you can choose a meal-inclusive package when booking or purchase separately). For those who don’t want to spend extra, you can still get close to the elephants and feel their gentleness and cuteness. (PS. Elephant riding activities will be completely banned across Bali by 2026).

It gives off a treehouse vibe, with stairs on the first floor, a bathroom halfway up, a small platform on the second floor, and the room on the third floor. The room design is very open, with only the bathroom being enclosed, while the rest of the areas are open spaces. Surrounded by trees, with the sounds of insects and birds, it feels like you're immersed in nature, allowing you to fully enjoy the leisure of your vacation.

Since they had a flight to catch that afternoon, my mom and her group headed back to the massage parlor from the previous day at 10 AM for one last session. They then returned to the villa and checked out at noon.

We had pre-booked airport transfer service online, and the driver arrived early at an affordable price.

## Postscript

1. If you love mangosteens, you must try them here. At the supermarket, you can get 12 mangosteens for just USD$3—cheap and delicious. My mom even asked the guide to take them to a roadside stall to buy durian, where one durian cost only USD$10. (Read more: [Mangosteen Season in Indonesia/Bali](/blog/2024-04-12-661943c7fd897800016eaf4a/))
2. When traveling in Bali, traffic congestion is common, and the roads aren’t in the best condition, so Google Maps’ estimated travel times should be taken with a grain of salt. (Read more: [Don’t Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/2024-03-19-65ebd5ecfd89780001cf6c8b/))
3. My mom and her group were very satisfied with Ani’s service, so they gave her and her husband a little extra tip on the last day. We also tipped the luggage handlers and massage therapists as a gesture of gratitude. (Read more: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw))
4. Ask your driver or guide to take you to a trusted money exchange shop as soon as you get in the car. (Read more: [Currency Exchange Guide for Independent Travelers](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/))
5. Local SIM cards cost USD$19 for 18GB of data, while roaming with 5GB costs USD$16. I recommend using roaming services since my mom didn’t need to look up much information during her trip, and most hotels offer free Wi-Fi. The 5GB was more than enough. (Read more: [Essential Guide: How to Get a Bali SIM Card or Internet?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/))

My mom thoroughly enjoyed this trip, especially the joy of taking photos. She particularly praised the hotels I chose, each with its unique charm, allowing her to capture many beautiful shots. For her, a trip is perfect as long as she can take plenty of photos.

She felt that not seeing the sunset at Uluwatu Temple was a major regret! After experiencing the swing in Ubud, her friends all praised her beautiful photos.

The private car tour in Bali made her feel free and convenient, and the price was very affordable. The only downside was the food - she didn't quite enjoy Bali's strong flavors and wished there were more low-oil, low-salt options.

This trip to Bali was filled with surprises and wonderful memories. Whether it was visiting temples, climbing volcanoes, or getting up close with elephants, every moment was unforgettable. She hopes her mother's travelogue can provide some references and help for travelers planning to visit Bali, and looks forward to setting foot on this island paradise again.
