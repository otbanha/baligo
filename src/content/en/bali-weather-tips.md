Today, I want to chat with you about a topic that might come as a surprise — why stop asking about Bali's weather?

First, although Bali only has a rainy season and a dry season throughout the year, it might not rain for weeks during the rainy season, and heavy rain could occur during the dry season. Bali's weather is unpredictable; you might wake up to sunny skies, ready to enjoy a day at the beach, only for it to pour down in the afternoon. This rapid change in weather makes it hard to predict and even harder to give an accurate answer. So, even if you ask, the answer might change at any moment.

Second, Bali's weather isn't the focus of your trip. Bali is a place filled with beautiful landscapes and rich culture. No matter the weather, you can always find plenty of interesting things to do. There's no need to spend too much energy on weather forecasts; instead, focus more on exploring this beautiful place and experiencing its culture and cuisine.

Moreover, when traveling in Bali, we also need to be aware that the weather conditions won't be the same in every area. Bali's terrain is varied, from the coast to the mountains, and the climate and weather can differ in each place. For example, inland mountainous areas might be more prone to rain than the southern coastal regions. Therefore, even on the same small island of Bali, you might notice weather changes in different areas. This is why we can't simply judge Bali's overall weather based on some people's answers; we should consider the specific location more carefully.

Finally, weather always has unpredictable factors. Even if the forecast shows a sunny day, it might suddenly start raining. In such cases, if you have fixed expectations about the weather, it might disrupt your plans and even lead to disappointment. On the contrary, if you can accept weather changes and enjoy each moment as it comes, your trip will be more enjoyable and enriching.
