### **Where is Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

Batu Barak Beach is located in the Uluwatu region of southern Bali, specifically in Kutuh Village, adjacent to the famous Pandawa Beach and Green Bowl Beach. This man-made road is nestled between limestone cliffs, offering unique natural scenery and serving as a hidden gem in Bali’s southern tip.

### **Why is it called the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road'?**

This coastal road is referred to as the 'Blue Road' because the limestone cliffs contrast beautifully with the azure sky, creating a breathtaking scene. Especially on Bali’s sunny days, the sky’s deep blue and the earthy tones of the cliffs form a striking contrast, resembling a blue path to heaven. Due to the steep cliffs flanking the road, it’s also known as the 'Cliff Road.'

### **Highlights of Batu Barak/Tanah Barak**

1. **Pristine White Sand and Unique Rock Formations**:
   The beach here features fine, white sand that feels like walking on clouds. Scattered across the beach are natural rock formations, resembling sculptures crafted by nature, adding a unique visual charm to the area.
2. **Crystal-Clear Waters and Majestic Cliffs**:
   The water is so clear that you can see the bottom, displaying a mesmerizing blue-green hue that contrasts sharply with the towering limestone cliffs, making you feel as if you’ve stepped into a painting.
3. **Tranquility and Privacy**:
   Since this place hasn’t been discovered by large crowds of tourists, the beach remains exceptionally peaceful. You can enjoy your own private time here, away from the hustle and bustle.
4. **Proximity to Pandawa Beach**:
   Batu Barak Beach is adjacent to Pandawa Beach, allowing visitors to easily walk between the two and experience different beach vibes in one trip.

### **How to Get to Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

1. **From Denpasar**:
   Take the Bali Mandara Highway toward Nusa Dua, then follow the signs to Pandawa Beach, eventually arriving at Batu Barak Beach.
2. **From Kuta**:
   The drive takes about 45 minutes, with scenic views along the way, making it ideal for self-driving or hiring a private car.
3. **From GWK Cultural Park**:
   The drive is only 20 minutes, making it very convenient.

**Note**: Due to new traffic regulations, private vehicles must park at the Lelangon parking lot, and visitors can then walk or take a shuttle to the beach. The shuttle service ensures a safer and more comfortable experience for tourists. Entrance requires a ticket, costing IDR 25,000 per person (approximately USD$2).

### **Travel Tips and Notes**

1. **Transportation**:
2. - It is recommended to hire a private car or use taxis/ride-hailing services to avoid parking difficulties.

- If driving, you can park your vehicle at nearby Pandawa Beach and then walk to Batu Barak Beach.

3. **Preparation**:
4. - Since there are no vendors on the beach, it’s advisable to bring your own food and drinking water.

- Wear comfortable shoes for walking on rocky areas.
- Bring sunscreen, hats, and plenty of water.

5. **Safety Tips**:
6. - The stairs leading to the beach are steep, so walk carefully.

- As there are no lifeguards, exercise extra caution when engaging in water activities.

7. **Best Time to Visit**:
8. - Weekday mornings are less crowded, ideal for travelers seeking tranquility.

- The sunset in the evening is particularly stunning, a must-see moment for photography enthusiasts.
