Bali, this mysterious and romantic vacation paradise, is not only known for its beaches and rice terraces but also hides many stunning secret spots. Located in Uluwatu, **Batu Barak** (also known as **Tanah Barak**) has become one of the most popular attractions in recent years. This place, referred to as the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road', attracts countless travelers with its magnificent limestone cliffs, pristine white sand beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Today, let's step into this dreamland and experience its charm!

***

### **Where is Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

Batu Barak Beach is located in the Uluwatu area of southern Bali, specifically in Kutuh Village, adjacent to the famous Pandawa Beach and [Green Bowl Beach](/blog/2023-08-15-64db6b57fd897800013a8e30/). This man-made road is hidden between limestone cliffs, offering unique natural scenery and serving as a hidden gem in Bali's southern tip.

***

### **Why is it called the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road'?**

This coastal road is called the 'Blue Road' because the limestone cliffs contrast beautifully with the azure sky, creating a magnificent scene. Especially on sunny days in Bali, the sky's deep blue and the earthy tones of the cliffs form a striking contrast, resembling a blue path to heaven. Additionally, due to the steep cliffs on both sides of the road, it is also known as the 'Cliff Road'.

***

### **Highlights of Batu Barak/Tanah Barak**

1. **Pristine White Sand and Unique Rock Formations**:
   The beach features fine, white sand that feels like walking on clouds. Scattered across the beach are natural rock formations, resembling sculptures crafted by nature, adding unique visual appeal to this shoreline.
2. **Crystal-Clear Waters and Majestic Cliffs**:
   The water is so clear that you can see the bottom, displaying a mesmerizing blue-green hue that contrasts sharply with the towering limestone cliffs, making you feel as if you're in a painting.
3. **Tranquility and Privacy**:
   As this spot hasn't been discovered by many tourists yet, the beach remains exceptionally peaceful. You can enjoy your private time here, away from the hustle and bustle.
4. **Proximity to Pandawa Beach**:
   Batu Barak Beach is adjacent to Pandawa Beach, allowing visitors to easily walk between the two and experience different beach atmospheres in one trip.

### **How to Get to Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

1. **From Denpasar**:
   Take the Bali Mandara Highway towards Nusa Dua, then follow the signs towards Pandawa Beach, finally arriving at Batu Barak Beach.
2. **From Kuta**:
   The drive takes about 45 minutes, with beautiful scenery along the way, making it ideal for self-driving or hiring a private car.
3. **From GWK Cultural Park**:
   The drive takes only 20 minutes, making it very convenient.

**Note**: Due to new local traffic regulations, private vehicles must be parked at the Lelangon parking lot, and visitors can then walk or take a shuttle to the beach. The shuttle service enhances safety and comfort for visitors. Entrance requires a ticket, priced at IDR 25,000 (approximately USD$2) per person.

### **Travel Tips and Precautions**

1. **Transportation**:
2. - It is recommended to hire a private car or use a taxi/ride-hailing service to avoid parking difficulties.

- If driving, you can park your vehicle at the nearby Pandawa Beach and then walk to Batu Barak Beach.

3. **Preparation**:
4. - Since there are no vendors on the beach, it is advisable to bring your own food and drinking water.

- Wear comfortable shoes for walking on rocky areas.
- Bring sunscreen, hats, and plenty of water.

5. **Safety Tips**:
6. - The stairs leading to the beach are steep, so please walk carefully.

- Due to the lack of lifeguards, extra caution is required when engaging in water activities.

7. **Best Time to Visit**:
8. - Early mornings on weekdays are less crowded, ideal for travelers seeking tranquility.

- The sunset views in the evening are particularly enchanting, a must-see moment for photography enthusiasts.
