## **Is Bali a Good Place for a Honeymoon?**

Yes! Bali is one of the best honeymoon destinations, offering the following advantages:

1. **Romantic Atmosphere**: Bali is considered a romantic getaway with stunning beaches, sunrise and sunset views, and luxurious resorts and private villas, allowing couples to spend unforgettable moments in a romantic setting.
2. **Diverse Activities**: Bali offers a wide range of activities, including water sports like snorkeling and diving, cultural experiences such as visiting temples and watching Balinese dance performances, and indulging in delicious cuisine and spa treatments.
3. **Beautiful Natural Environment**: Bali boasts breathtaking natural landscapes, including lush rice terraces, majestic volcanoes and waterfalls, and unique flora and fauna.
4. **Luxurious Accommodation Options**: Bali has a variety of luxurious accommodation options, from private villas to resorts and boutique hotels, allowing couples to find their ideal stay based on preferences and budget.
5. **Warm Climate**: Bali enjoys a warm tropical climate year-round, making it a perfect destination for couples to enjoy the sun and beach at any time.

## How to Get to Bali?

You can choose to take a direct flight to Bali. The capital of Bali is Denpasar (airport code: DPS). Currently, China Airlines and EVA Air offer direct flights from Taiwan to Bali. Additionally, other Asian cities like Singapore, Hong Kong, Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, and Ho Chi Minh City also have direct flight options. Make sure to check flight schedules and prices and choose the option that best fits your itinerary.

## How long should you stay in Bali?

For a honeymoon in Bali, we recommend staying at least 10 nights to explore different regions, surrounding islands, and have time for adventure and relaxation.

**Things you can do in 10 nights:**

- Discover hidden beaches in southern Bali
- Experience art and nature in Ubud
- Watch the sunrise from Mount Batur
- Visit Nusa Penida or other nearby islands
- Enjoy spa treatments and other relaxing activities

Depending on your interests and budget, you can adjust your itinerary. Here are some suggestions:

- If you love beaches, we suggest relaxing on some hidden beaches in the south.
- If you're into culture, spend more time exploring Bali's temples and villages.
- If you enjoy natural beauty, we recommend spending extra time in Ubud.
- If you're adventurous, participate in outdoor activities like hiking, snorkeling, or surfing.

## Should I Bring Cash/Should I Tip?

In Bali, credit and debit cards are widely accepted, but it's advisable to carry some cash for unforeseen needs, such as at small vendors, island tours, or tipping. Here are some specific recommendations:

- The only currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah. It's recommended to bring some US dollars in cash, which can be exchanged locally for Rupiah.
- Tipping is not mandatory, but if you receive good service, a tip of 20,000 to 50,000 Rupiah is appreciated.
- When dining in restaurants, an 11% tax and a 5% to 10% service charge will be added to your bill.
