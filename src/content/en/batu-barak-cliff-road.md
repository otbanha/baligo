Bali, this mysterious and romantic vacation paradise, hides many stunning secrets beyond its famous beaches and rice terraces. Located in Uluwatu, **Batu Barak** (also known as **Tanah Barak**) has become one of the hottest spots in recent years. This place, known as the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road', attracts countless travelers with its magnificent limestone cliffs, pristine white sand beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Today, let's step into this dreamlike place and experience its charm!

***

### **Where is Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

Batu Barak Beach is located in the Uluwatu area of southern Bali, specifically in Kutuh Village, adjacent to the famous Pandawa Beach and [Green Bowl Beach](/blog/2023-08-15-64db6b57fd897800013a8e30/). This man-made road is hidden between limestone cliffs, boasting unique natural scenery, making it a hidden gem in southern Bali.

***

### **Why is it called the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road'?**

This coastal road is called the 'Blue Road' because the limestone cliffs contrast beautifully with the azure sky, creating a magnificent scene. Especially on Bali's sunny days, the sky's deep blue and the earth's colors form a striking contrast, resembling a blue road to heaven. Additionally, due to the steep cliffs on both sides of the road, it's also known as the 'Cliff Road'.

***

### **Highlights of Batu Barak/Tanah Barak**

1. **Pristine White Sand and Unique Rock Formations**:
   The beach here features fine, white sand that feels like walking on clouds. Scattered natural rocks on the beach add unique visual charm, like sculptures crafted by nature.
2. **Crystal-Clear Waters and Majestic Cliffs**:
   The water is so clear that you can see the bottom, displaying a mesmerizing blue-green hue that contrasts sharply with the towering limestone cliffs, making you feel like you're in a painting.
3. **Tranquility and Privacy**:
   Since this place hasn't been discovered by many tourists yet, the beach remains exceptionally peaceful. You can enjoy your private time here, away from the hustle and bustle.
4. **Proximity to Pandawa Beach**:
   Batu Barak Beach is adjacent to Pandawa Beach, allowing visitors to easily walk between the two and experience different beach vibes in one trip.

### **How to Get to Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

1. **From Denpasar**:
   Take the Bali Mandara Highway towards Nusa Dua, then follow the signs to Pandawa Beach, finally arriving at Batu Barak Beach.
2. **From Kuta**:
   The drive takes about 45 minutes, with beautiful scenery along the way, perfect for self-driving or hiring a car.
3. **From GWK Cultural Park**:
   Only a 20-minute drive, making it very convenient.

**Note**: Due to new local traffic regulations, private vehicles must park at Lelangon Parking Lot, then walk or take a shuttle to the beach. The shuttle service enhances safety and comfort for visitors. Entrance requires a ticket, IDR 25,000 per person (approximately USD$2).

### **Travel Tips and Precautions**

1. **Transportation**:
   It is recommended to hire a car or use a taxi/ride-hailing service to avoid parking difficulties.
   If driving, park at the nearby Pandawa Beach and take the shuttle to Batu Barak Beach.
2. **Preparation**:
   Bring your own food and drinking water as there are few vendors on the beach.
   Wear comfortable shoes for walking on rocky areas.
   Carry sunscreen, hats, and plenty of water.
3. **Safety Tips**:
   The stairs leading to the beach are steep, so tread carefully.
   Be extra cautious during water activities as there are no lifeguards.
4. **Best Time to Visit**:
   Early mornings on weekdays are less crowded, ideal for those seeking tranquility.
   The sunset in the evening is particularly stunning, a must-see for photography enthusiasts.
5. **About the Shuttle**:
   Currently, Batu Barak (Tanah Barak) shuttle does not have a fixed schedule, operating more like an on-demand loop service.
   Shuttles usually start around 7:00 AM
   and end around 6:00 PM
   During peak times, shuttles run continuously.
