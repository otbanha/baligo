Bali, this mysterious and romantic vacation paradise, hides many stunning secrets beyond its famous beaches and rice terraces. Located in Uluwatu, **Batu Barak** (also known as **Tanah Barak**) has become one of the hottest spots in recent years. This area, referred to as the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road', attracts countless travelers with its magnificent limestone cliffs, white sandy beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Today, let's step into this dreamy place and feel its charm!

***

### **Where is Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

Batu Barak Beach is located in the Uluwatu region of southern Bali, specifically in Kutuh Village, adjacent to the famous Pandawa Beach and [Green Bowl Beach](/blog/2023-08-15-64db6b57fd897800013a8e30/). This man-made road is hidden between limestone cliffs, offering unique natural scenery and serving as a hidden gem in the southern tip of Bali.

***

### **Why is it called the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road'?**

This coastal road is called the 'Blue Road' because the limestone cliffs contrast beautifully with the azure sky, creating a magnificent scene. Especially on Bali's sunny days, the sky's blue and the earth's colors form a striking contrast, resembling a blue path to heaven. Additionally, due to the steep cliffs on both sides of the road, it is also known as the 'Cliff Road'.

***

### **Highlights of Batu Barak/Tanah Barak**

1. **Pristine White Sand and Unique Rock Formations**:
   The beach features fine, white sand that feels like walking on clouds. Scattered natural rock formations add a unique visual charm, resembling sculptures crafted by nature.
2. **Crystal-Clear Waters and Majestic Cliffs**:
   The water is crystal clear, displaying a mesmerizing turquoise hue. The towering limestone cliffs create a striking contrast, making it feel like you’re stepping into a painting.
3. **Tranquility and Privacy**:
   As this spot remains relatively undiscovered by tourists, the beach offers a serene and private atmosphere, perfect for escaping the hustle and bustle.
4. **Proximity to Pandawa Beach**:
   Batu Barak Beach is adjacent to Pandawa Beach, allowing visitors to easily explore both beaches and enjoy their distinct vibes.

### **How to Get to Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

1. **From Denpasar**:
   Take the Bali Mandara Highway toward Nusa Dua, then follow the signs to Pandawa Beach, where you’ll find Batu Barak Beach.
2. **From Kuta**:
   The drive takes about 45 minutes, offering scenic views along the way. It’s ideal for self-driving or hiring a private car.
3. **From GWK Cultural Park**:
   Just a 20-minute drive away, making it a convenient option.

**Note**: Due to new traffic regulations, private vehicles must park at Lelangon Parking Lot. Visitors can then walk or take a shuttle to the beach. The shuttle service ensures a safer and more comfortable experience. Entrance fee is IDR 25,000 per person (approximately USD$2).

### **Travel Tips and Precautions**

1. **Transportation**:
   It’s recommended to hire a private car or use a taxi/ride-hailing service to avoid parking difficulties.
   If driving, park near Pandawa Beach and take the shuttle to Batu Barak Beach.
2. **Preparation**:
   Bring your own food and drinking water, as there are few vendors on the beach.
   Wear comfortable shoes for walking on rocky areas.
   Pack sunscreen, hats, and plenty of water.
3. **Safety Tips**:
   Be cautious when navigating the steep stairs leading to the beach.
   As there are no lifeguards, exercise extra caution during water activities.
4. **Best Time to Visit**:
   Early mornings on weekdays are quieter, ideal for those seeking tranquility.
   Sunsets in the evening are particularly stunning, a must-see for photography enthusiasts.
5. **About the Shuttle**:
   Currently, Batu Barak (Tanah Barak) shuttles operate on a loop system rather than a fixed schedule.
   Shuttles typically start around 7:00 AM and end around 6:00 PM.
   During peak times, shuttles run continuously to accommodate visitors.
