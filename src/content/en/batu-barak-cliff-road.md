Bali, this mysterious and romantic vacation paradise, hides many stunning secrets beyond its famous beaches and rice terraces. Located in Uluwatu, **Batu Barak** (also known as **Tanah Barak**) has become one of the hottest spots in recent years. This place, referred to as the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road', attracts countless travelers with its magnificent limestone cliffs, pristine white sand beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Today, let's step into this dreamlike place and feel its charm!

***

### **Where is Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

Batu Barak Beach is located in the Uluwatu area of southern Bali, specifically in Kutuh Village, adjacent to the famous Pandawa Beach and [Green Bowl Beach](/blog/green-bowl-beach-bali/). This man-made road is hidden between limestone cliffs, offering unique natural scenery and serving as a hidden gem in southern Bali.

***

### **Why is it called the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road'?**

This coastal road is called the 'Blue Road' because the limestone cliffs reflect against the deep blue sky, creating a magnificent scene. Especially on Bali's sunny days, the azure sky contrasts sharply with the color of the cliffs, resembling a blue road leading to heaven. Due to the steep cliffs on both sides of the road, it is also known as the 'Cliff Road'.

***

### **Highlights of Batu Barak/Tanah Barak**

1. **Pristine White Sand and Unique Rock Formations**:
   The beach features fine white sand that feels like walking on clouds. Scattered natural rocks add unique visual charm to this beach.
2. **Crystal-Clear Waters and Majestic Cliffs**:
   The clear waters display mesmerizing shades of blue-green, contrasting sharply with the towering limestone cliffs, making you feel like you're in a painting.
3. **Tranquility and Privacy**:
   As this place hasn't been discovered by many tourists yet, the beach remains exceptionally peaceful. You can enjoy your private time here, away from the hustle and bustle.
4. **Proximity to Pandawa Beach**:
   Batu Barak Beach is adjacent to Pandawa Beach, allowing visitors to easily walk between the two and experience different beach atmospheres.

### **How to Get to Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

1. **From Denpasar**:
   Take the Bali Mandara Highway towards Nusa Dua, then follow the signs to Pandawa Beach, finally arriving at Batu Barak Beach.
2. **From Kuta**:
   The drive takes about 45 minutes with beautiful scenery along the way, perfect for self-driving or hiring a car.
3. **From GWK Cultural Park**:
   Just a 20-minute drive, making it very convenient.

**Note**: Due to new traffic regulations, private vehicles must park at Lelangon Parking Lot and then walk or take a shuttle to the beach. The shuttle service enhances safety and comfort for visitors. Entrance requires a ticket, IDR 25,000 per person (approximately USD$2).

### **Travel Tips and Notes**

1. **Transportation**:
It's recommended to hire a car or use a taxi/ride-hailing service to avoid parking difficulties.
If driving, park near Pandawa Beach and take the shuttle to Batu Barak Beach.
2. **Preparation**:
Bring your own food and drinking water as there are few vendors on the beach.
Wear comfortable shoes for walking on rocky areas.
Carry sunscreen, hats, and plenty of water.
3. **Safety Tips**:
Be cautious on the steep stairs leading to the beach.
As there are no lifeguards, extra care is needed during water activities.
4. **Best Time to Visit**:
Weekday mornings are less crowded, ideal for those seeking tranquility.
The sunset in the evening is particularly stunning, a must-see for photography enthusiasts.
5. **Shuttle Service**:
Currently, Batu Barak (Tanah Barak) shuttle does not have a fixed schedule, operating more like a loop service.
Shuttles usually start around 7:00 AM
and end around 6:00 PM
During peak times, shuttles run continuously.
