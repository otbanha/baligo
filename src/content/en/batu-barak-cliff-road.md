Bali, this mysterious and romantic vacation paradise, besides its famous beaches and rice terraces, hides many stunning secret spots. Located in Uluwatu, **Batu Barak** (also known as **Tanah Barak**) is one of the recently popular attractions. This place, known as the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road,' attracts countless travelers with its magnificent limestone cliffs, white sandy beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Today, let's step into this dreamy place and feel its charm!

***

### **Where is Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

Batu Barak Beach is located in the Uluwatu area of southern Bali, specifically in Kutuh Village, adjacent to the famous Pandawa Beach and [Green Bowl Beach](/blog/green-bowl-beach-bali/). This man-made road is hidden between limestone cliffs, offering unique natural scenery and is a hidden gem in the southern part of Bali.

***

### **Why is it called the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road'?**

This coastal road is called the 'Blue Road' because the limestone cliffs and the azure sky backdrop create a magnificent picture. Especially on Bali's sunny days, the sky's blue and the earth's colors form a striking contrast, resembling a blue road leading to heaven. Also, because the road is flanked by steep cliffs, it is also known as the 'Cliff Road.'

***

### **Highlights of Batu Barak/Tanah Barak**

1. **White Sandy Beach and Unique Rock Formations**:
   The beach here is fine and white, feeling like walking on clouds. Scattered natural rocks on the beach add unique visual charm, like sculptures crafted by nature.
2. **Crystal-Clear Waters and Majestic Cliffs**:
   The water is clear and displays a mesmerizing blue-green hue, contrasting sharply with the towering limestone cliffs, making you feel like you're in a painting.
3. **Tranquility and Privacy**:
   Since this place hasn't been discovered by many tourists yet, the beach is exceptionally quiet. You can enjoy your private time here, away from the hustle and bustle.
4. **Proximity to Pandawa Beach**:
   Batu Barak Beach is adjacent to Pandawa Beach, allowing visitors to easily walk between the two and experience different beach atmospheres in one trip.

### **How to Get to Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

1. **From Denpasar**:
   Take the Bali Mandara Highway towards Nusa Dua, then follow the signs to Pandawa Beach, and finally arrive at Batu Barak Beach.
2. **From Kuta**:
   The drive takes about 45 minutes, with beautiful scenery along the way, perfect for self-driving or hiring a private car.
3. **From GWK Cultural Park**:
   The drive takes only 20 minutes, making it very convenient.

**Note**: Due to new traffic regulations, private vehicles must park at the Lelangon parking lot, then walk or take a shuttle to the beach. The shuttle service enhances the safety and comfort of visitors. Entrance requires a ticket, costing IDR 25,000 (approximately USD$2) per person.

### **Travel Tips and Precautions**

1. **Transportation**:
It is recommended to hire a private car or use a taxi/ride-hailing service to avoid parking difficulties.
If driving, park near Pandawa Beach and take the shuttle to Batu Barak Beach.
2. **Preparation**:
As there are no vendors on the beach, bring your own food and drinking water.
Wear comfortable shoes for walking on rocky areas.
Bring sunscreen, hats, and plenty of water.
3. **Safety Tips**:
The stairs leading to the beach are steep, so walk carefully.
Due to the lack of lifeguards, be extra cautious when engaging in water activities.
4. **Best Time to Visit**:
Weekday mornings are less crowded, ideal for those seeking tranquility.
The sunset in the evening is particularly enchanting, a must-see for photography enthusiasts.
5. **Shuttle Service**:
Currently, Batu Barak (Tanah Barak) shuttle does not have a formal, public "fixed schedule" and operates more like an on-demand loop service.
Shuttles usually start around 7:00 AM
and end around 6:00 PM
During peak times, shuttles run continuously.
