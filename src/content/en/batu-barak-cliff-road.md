Bali, a paradise of mystery and romance, hides many stunning secrets beyond its famous beaches and rice terraces. Located in Uluwatu, **Batu Barak** (also known as **Tanah Barak**) is one of the most popular spots in recent years. This place, called the 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road,' captivates countless travelers with its majestic limestone cliffs, white sandy beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Today, let's explore this dreamy destination and feel its charm!

***

### **Where is Batu Barak/Tanah Barak?**

Batu Barak Beach is located in the Uluwatu area of southern Bali, specifically in Kutuh Village, adjacent to the famous Pandawa Beach and [Green Bowl Beach](/en/blog/green-bowl-beach-bali/). This man-made road is hidden among limestone cliffs, offering a unique natural landscape and serving as a hidden gem at the southern tip of Bali.

***

### **Why is it called 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road'?**

This coastal road is called the 'Blue Road' because the limestone cliffs contrast beautifully with the azure sky, creating a stunning scene. Especially on a sunny day in Bali, the vivid blue of the sky and the earthy tones of the cliffs create a striking contrast, making it feel like a blue path to heaven. Since the road is flanked by steep cliffs, it's also known as 'Cliff Road.'

![Why is it called 'Blue Road' or 'Cliff Road'?](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_30197729f12704e67d5352e5ad465799.jpg)

***

### **Highlights of Batu Barak / Tanah Barak**

1. **Pristine White Sand and Unique Rock Formations**:
   The sand here is fine and pure white, making every step feel like walking on clouds. Scattered across the beach are natural rock formations that resemble sculptures, adding a distinctive visual charm to the shoreline.
2. **Crystal-Clear Waters and Stunning Cliffs**:
   The water is so clear you can see the bottom, shimmering in mesmerizing blue-green hues that contrast beautifully with the towering limestone cliffs, making you feel like you've stepped into a painting.
3. **Peace and Privacy**:
   Since this spot remains largely undiscovered by crowds, the beach is exceptionally tranquil. You can enjoy your own private escape, far from the hustle and bustle.
4. **Close to Pandawa Beach**:
   Batu Barak Beach is adjacent to Pandawa Beach, so you can easily walk between them and experience two different beach vibes in one trip.

![Highlights of Batu Barak / Tanah Barak](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b1c0bf1ebb9e5f22f466bcb222140ba6.jpg)

***

### **How to Get to Batu Barak / Tanah Barak?**

1. **From Denpasar**:
   Take the Bali Mandara Highway toward Nusa Dua, then follow signs toward Pandawa Beach. Finally, head to Batu Barak Beach.
2. **From Kuta**:
   The drive takes about 45 minutes, with scenic views along the way—ideal for self-driving or hiring a car.
3. **From GWK Cultural Park**:
   It's just a 20-minute drive, making it very convenient.

**Note**: Due to new traffic regulations, private vehicles must park at the Lelangon parking lot, then walk or take a shuttle to the beach. The shuttle service makes the experience safer and more comfortable. Entry requires a ticket costing IDR 25,000 (about USD$1).

### **Travel Tips and Precautions**

1. **Transportation**:
   It's best to hire a car or use a taxi/ride-hailing service to avoid parking hassles.
   If driving, you can park at nearby Pandawa Beach and then take a shuttle or walk to Batu Barak Beach.
2. **What to Bring**:
   Since there are few vendors on the beach, bring your own food and drinking water.
   Wear comfortable shoes for navigating the rocky areas.
   Pack sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water.
3. **Safety Tips**:
   The stairs down to the beach are quite steep, so watch your step.
   There are no lifeguards, so be extra cautious when engaging in water activities.
4. **Best Time to Visit**:
   Weekday mornings are less crowded, perfect for those seeking peace.
   Sunset views are especially stunning, a must for photography enthusiasts.
5. **About the Shuttle**:
   Currently, the shuttle at Batu Barak (Tanah Barak) doesn't have a fixed, publicly announced schedule—it operates more like a continuous loop service.
   The shuttle typically starts around 7:00 AM
   and ends around 6:00 PM
   When it's busy, it runs back and forth almost constantly.
