If you're planning a trip to Bali and want a private pool villa without venturing to a remote area, The Maya Seminyak Villas (also known as Maya Sayang Seminyak) is a strong contender. Nestled right off Seminyak's bustling Eat Street (Kayu Aya), it's just a few minutes' walk to restaurants and shopping malls, yet once you step through the villa gates, it's instantly peaceful—a world away from the city center. With only 11 villas on the property, it's small-scale and offers a strong sense of privacy.

***

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

Located at Jl. Mertasari No. 1 in Seminyak's Laksmana district, it's tucked down a small alley next to the renowned Italian restaurant Ultimo. Step outside, and you're surrounded by Seminyak's restaurant hub. Seminyak Square shopping center is a five-minute walk away, and beachfront dining spots like La Lucciola and Ku De Ta are also within walking distance.

As for the beach, Seminyak Beach is about a 20-minute walk—a moderate distance, not right on your doorstep, but no need for a ride either.

![位置與周邊環境](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776238213668-b8c519e3606601c53285a3622679d3aa.jpg)

This area is Seminyak's most concentrated consumer zone—shopping, dining, coffee, spa, sunset views—almost everything you'd do in Bali is just a few minutes' walk or scooter ride away. The Maya's clever location capitalizes on this: close enough, yet tucked away in a quiet alley, surprisingly serene.

***

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

The property's entrance features a traditional Balinese Angkul-Angkul carved wooden gate. Beyond it, a path lined with bamboo and tropical plants leads you in, a stark and swift contrast to the street's bustle.

![設施介紹](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776238253051-62383025b5be5b788f2617facd1f0d2a.webp)

For common facilities, there's a shared outdoor pool with loungers, though most guests rarely use it since each villa has its own private pool. There's also a full-service spa, Maya Sayang Spa, offering hot stone massages, Swedish massages, aromatherapy, body scrubs, and manicures, with amenities including an indoor hot tub. The restaurant serves breakfast, lunch, and dinner, with a menu focused on Asian cuisine—Balinese, Korean, Japanese—plus Western options, and guests rate the prices as reasonable.

![設施介紹](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776238356883-37237cb64d90a6d2c9a38f40903f83e9.webp)

A nice touch: you can pre-order breakfast for a set time, and staff will deliver it directly to your villa—no need to head to the restaurant. It's a true villa experience. They also offer free bicycle rentals and a Seminyak area shuttle service, handy for those who'd rather not call a Grab. Note: there's no gym.

***

The architecture leans toward traditional Balinese style with thatched roofs and carved wooden details. The open-plan living room directly faces the pool, and the indoor-outdoor layout allows for great airflow. The bedroom is air-conditioned and comes with a mosquito-netted king bed; the bathroom is semi-outdoor with a soaking tub and separate shower. The kitchen is equipped with a fridge, microwave, and stove, making it easy for long-stay guests to cook their own meals.

To be honest, the decor is classic Balinese—not the ultra-modern, Instagram-worthy design you might see elsewhere. Some furniture and fixtures show signs of age, and you can spot a bit of wear and tear. If you're after a sleek, minimalist look, this might not be your style. But if you appreciate the charm of a traditional Balinese villa with character, this place has a unique appeal all its own.

***

## 👥 Who It's For

It's highly recommended for **couples on a honeymoon**—the private pool, garden, and peaceful setting are ideal, and the hotel is very accommodating for special arrangements like proposals (one guest noted that when their partner asked for rose petals in the pool, the staff not only delivered but also decorated the entire walkway with heart-shaped flower patterns). **Family trips** are also a great fit, with spacious rooms, a kitchen, and three-bedroom villas that offer excellent value for a group. However, travelers seeking modern design or the amenities of a large resort might want to think twice.

***

## 💬 Real Guest Reviews

A returning guest shared that the one-bedroom villa is incredibly spacious, the private pool experience is excellent, breakfast is delivered to the villa door on time, the restaurant menu is varied, and the staff are friendly and professional—overall, great value for the price.

Another TripAdvisor guest mentioned that the villa is very quiet despite being right next to the bustling Eat Street—you can't hear any outside noise from inside. The private pool gets just the right amount of shade throughout the day, and the turndown service includes lighting mosquito coils and setting up the mosquito net, a thoughtful touch that left a strong impression.

***

## ⚠️ The Honest Downsides

Let's be clear about a few real drawbacks. First, the villa is a bit dated, with some bathroom fixtures showing their age. The outdoor kitchen and semi-open bathroom areas naturally attract mosquitoes and bugs—mosquito coils are provided, but if you're sensitive to that, be prepared. Second, the living room is open-air with no air conditioning, so during Bali's hot season, it can get stuffy in the afternoon, relying only on fans. Third, some villas are close to nearby restaurants and bars, so you might hear voices or music at night—you can request a quieter villa when booking.
