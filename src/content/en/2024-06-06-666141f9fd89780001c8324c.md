Most travelers who have visited Bali and fallen in love with the island wish to delve deeper into the local lifestyle or embark on a non-touristy itinerary, but often don't know where to start. The best place to experience the rhythm of local life in a day is Denpasar, Bali's inland capital. This article will take you on a journey to explore Bali's local life, from shopping at traditional markets to strolling through beautiful riverside parks and bustling night markets. Let's step into this enchanting land and experience the most authentic Balinese charm!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_17d0b4f41e1151132e8732154d87d812.jpg)

## Art Wholesale Market: Kumbasari Art Market

First, we head to the Kumbasari Art Market (pronounced: Koom-bah-sah-ree) in Denpasar, Bali's capital. This is Bali's largest wholesale handicraft market, offering an incredible variety of handmade goods. Unlike typical tourist markets, this one primarily serves locals, so prices are very reasonable. If you're looking to buy handicrafts in bulk, this is the place to go.

## Badung Market: Pasar Badung

(Contributed by fellow traveler Fran Cisco. [Original post here](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/d9Bh4epnNiYGMsy8/)) For those who love tasting local fruits, Pasar Badung (Badung Market) is a must-visit. This bustling market is filled with vendors selling fresh fruits and other local produce. While the indoor area can be crowded and chaotic, the outdoor section offers a relaxed space to find a variety of delicious fruits.

Fran Cisco shares: (Prices may vary by season) Mangosteen IDR 10,000 /1 kg White Durian IDR 15,000/20,000/30,000 per fruit (The photo shows a 20,000 IDR durian, packed with flesh!)

Fran Cisco and his friends bought sweet mangosteens, creamy durians, salak, and duku here at very affordable prices.

In addition, Fran Cisco highly recommends a handsome durian vendor on the small road to the left of the market. The owner enthusiastically helps you pick the best fruits, making you feel his warmth and hospitality. He doesn't inflate prices for foreign tourists and offers friendly service with high-quality fruits. Even two weeks after returning to Taiwan, Fran Cisco still fondly remembers the delightful durian moments with this vendor!

It's worth noting that both Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets have many middlemen aunties who approach non-locals, guiding them around and encouraging them to buy goods at higher prices while taking a commission. To avoid being followed by these middlemen, it's best to bring your private driver along, as they can help negotiate and get better deals.

## Korea River Park: Taman Tukad Korea

Next, the river separating Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets is now a riverside park called 'Taman Tukad Korea,' locally known as 'Korea River Park' due to its design resembling Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream. Once a polluted ditch, it has been transformed by the government into a beautiful riverside park.

During the day, Taman Tukad Korea offers a pleasant view with lush trees along the riverbanks and exquisite stone carvings. At night, colorful lights illuminate the park, creating a romantic atmosphere. The park also features a floating bridge and a fountain, adding to its charm.

Besides the beautiful scenery, the park provides free Wi-Fi for visitors to share their experiences. After exploring the park, you can visit the nearby night market to enjoy various delicious snacks and experience Bali's nightlife.

## Bali's Largest Night Market: Pasar Kreneng

Finally, we arrive at 'Pasar Kreneng,' Bali's largest night market, just a few minutes' drive from the riverside park. The market is filled with stalls selling a wide variety of food and daily necessities. The lively atmosphere allows you to indulge in local delicacies and savor the satisfaction of great food. You can plan a day trip like this to experience Bali's local charm more deeply. Whether it's the handicrafts at Kumbasari Art Market, the local fruits at Badung Market, the beautiful scenery of Taman Tukad Korea, or the delicious food at Kreneng Night Market, each spot is full of surprises.
