Most travelers who have visited Bali and fallen in love with it wish to immerse themselves in local life or embark on non-touristy itineraries but don’t know where to start? The best place to experience the rhythm of local life in just one day is Denpasar, Bali’s inland capital. This article will take you deep into Balinese local life, from shopping at local markets to beautiful riverside parks and bustling night markets. Let’s step into this enchanting land and feel the most authentic Balinese charm!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_17d0b4f41e1151132e8732154d87d812.jpg)

## Wholesale Art Market: Kumbasari Art Market

First, we head to Denpasar’s 'Kumbasari Art Market' (search 'Pasar Seni Kumbasari' on Google Maps). This is Bali’s largest wholesale handicraft market, offering a wide variety of handmade goods. Unlike typical tourist markets, it primarily serves locals, so prices are very affordable. If you’re looking to buy handicrafts in bulk, this is the perfect place.

## Badung Market: Pasar Badung

(Contributed by Fran Cisco. [Original post here](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/d9Bh4epnNiYGMsy8/)) For those who love tasting local fruits, Pasar Badung (Badung Market) is a must-visit. It’s packed with vendors selling fresh fruits and other local foods. While the indoor market might feel crowded and chaotic, you can easily find delicious fruits in the outdoor area.

![Fran Cisco提供:（價格可能隨季節而有不同） 山竹 IDR 10000 /1公斤 白榴蓮 IDR 15000/20000/30000顆 （圖片是20000的，果肉爆炸多！）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99114756823a709b41f05ead1f12f4ba.jpg)

Fran Cisco’s tip: (Prices may vary by season) Mangosteen IDR 10,000/kg White Durian IDR 15,000/20,000/30,000 per fruit (The photo shows a 20,000 one, with an explosion of flesh!)

Our travel companion Fran Cisco found delicious mangosteens and creamy white durians here, along with snake fruit and salak, all at very reasonable prices.

Additionally, Fran Cisco highly recommends a friendly durian vendor on the small path to the left of the market. The vendor enthusiastically helps you pick the best fruits, making you feel warmly welcomed. He doesn't inflate prices for foreign tourists, maintains a friendly attitude, and offers high-quality fruits. Even two weeks after returning to Taiwan, Fran Cisco still fondly remembers the joyful durian moments provided by this vendor!

It's important to note that both Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets have many broker aunties doing business. They approach you if you're not a local, guiding you around to buy goods at higher prices from which they take a commission. To avoid being followed by these broker aunties, it's best to bring along your hired car driver who can help guard and bargain for you.

## Korea River Park: Taman Tukad Korea

Next, the river separating Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets is now a riverside park named 'Taman Tukad Korea,' locally known as 'Korea River Park' because its design resembles Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream. Once a polluted ditch, it has been transformed by the government into a beautiful riverside park.

During the day, Taman Tukad Korea offers pleasant scenery with shaded green trees along the riverbanks and exquisite stone carvings. At night, colorful lights illuminate the entire park, creating a romantic atmosphere. The park also features a floating bridge and a fountain, adding to its charm.

Besides the beautiful scenery, the park provides free Wi-Fi for visitors to share their experiences instantly. After visiting the park, you can explore the nearby night market to taste various delicious snacks and experience Bali's nightlife.

## Bali's Largest Night Market: Pasar Kreneng

Finally, we arrive at 'Pasar Kreneng,' Bali's largest night market, just a few minutes' drive from the riverside park. The market is bustling with stalls selling a wide range of foods and daily necessities. The lively atmosphere allows you to enjoy various local delicacies and the satisfaction they bring.
