## January

### January 1: New Year's Day (Indonesian National Holiday)

### **January 9: Siwa Ratri (Balinese Hindu Festival)**

- January 1st, New Year's Day, is a public holiday observed almost worldwide, including in Indonesia/Bali. The celebrations typically peak on December 31st with New Year's Eve festivities. During this period, Indonesians have a spontaneous "Nataru" holiday. For more details, refer to the festival introduction at the end of December.

![跨年倒數是每年元旦的高潮！在峇里島也有很多地方舉辦倒數派對喔！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_90f97b1bd0d4353fa00d0518a9fab011.png)

The countdown to the New Year is the highlight of January 1st! In Bali, there are many places hosting countdown parties!

- January 9th, Siwa Ratri, is one of the significant festivals in the Balinese calendar. Siwa Ratri emphasizes introspection, meditation, and seeking forgiveness. The rituals include staying awake for 36 hours, fasting for 24 hours, and maintaining silence for 12 hours. After this period, purification ceremonies are usually held at the beach, alongside ceremonies at some of the island's major temples.

## February

### **February 8: Ascension of Prophet Muhammad (Indonesian National Holiday)**

### **February 9 - 15: Chinese Lunar New Year Holiday**

### **February 28 - March 9: Galungan Kuningan (Balinese Hindu Festival)**

- February 8, 2024, marks the Ascension of Prophet Muhammad, a national holiday in Indonesia. This is an important Islamic festival commemorating the Prophet's ascension. On this day, Muslims gather in mosques for prayers and worship.
- February 9 - 15 is our Chinese Lunar New Year! Indonesia designates the first day of the Lunar New Year (February 10, 2024) as a national holiday. However, this year it coincides with February 8, also a national holiday (Ascension of Prophet Muhammad), so February 9 is designated as a flexible holiday. Therefore, friends in Indonesia can enjoy a four-day holiday during the Lunar New Year period (February 11 is a Sunday)! The week-long Chinese Lunar New Year holiday may seem unrelated to Bali, Indonesia, but it is also a peak travel season as many tourists from China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan take long vacations during this time!

![農曆新年假期大人放長假、小孩放寒假、還可以帶著家中長輩來峇里島避寒喔～](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_641795a1061cfdbbdd2df651788f4be8.png)

Celebrate the Lunar New Year with a long vacation for adults, winter break for kids, and bring your elders to Bali to escape the cold!

- February 28 to March 9: Galungan & Kuningan - One of Bali's most important festivals, lasting about 10 days, celebrating the creation of the universe and the triumph of good over evil. During this festival, people honor their ancestors at home, seeking their blessings, and perform ceremonies at temples to show reverence to the gods. Galungan & Kuningan also marks the beginning of the Balinese New Year according to the Pawukon calendar, signifying the day of the universe's creation. Schools in Bali close for two weeks during this period, highlighting its significance!

## December

### **25th: Christmas (Indonesian National Holiday)**

### **26th: Christmas Flexible Holiday**

- December 25th is Christmas, a national holiday in Indonesia. For Christians, Christmas is an important festival, and Indonesia has an average of nearly 10% Christians. Since Christmas and New Year's Day are close together, many Indonesians try their best to take leave to combine the two holidays into one long vacation. This gives them the opportunity to spend more time with family and friends and travel to other cities or countries. Indonesians have even coined a term called "Nataru," meaning "Christmas - New Year Holiday," combining "Natal" (Christmas) and "tahun baru" (New Year).
