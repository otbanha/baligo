## January

### January 1: New Year's Day (Indonesian National Holiday)

### **January 9: Siwa Ratri (Balinese Hindu Festival)**

- January 1st, New Year's Day, is a public holiday observed worldwide, including in Indonesia/Bali. The celebrations typically peak on December 31st with New Year's Eve festivities. During this period, Indonesians often take a spontaneous "Nataru" holiday. For more details, refer to the festival descriptions at the end of December.

![跨年倒數是每年元旦的高潮！在峇里島也有很多地方舉辦倒數派對喔！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_90f97b1bd0d4353fa00d0518a9fab011.png)

The countdown to the New Year is the highlight of January 1st! In Bali, there are many places hosting countdown parties!

- January 9th, Siwa Ratri, is one of the most important festivals in the Balinese calendar. Siwa Ratri emphasizes introspection, meditation, and seeking forgiveness. The rituals include staying awake for 36 hours, fasting for 24 hours, and maintaining silence for 12 hours. After this period, purification ceremonies are typically held on the beach, alongside rituals at some of the island's major temples.

## February

### **February 8: Isra Mi'raj (Ascension of Prophet Muhammad) (Indonesian National Holiday)**

### **February 9 - 15: Chinese Lunar New Year Holiday**

### **February 28 - March 9: Galungan Kuningan (Balinese Hindu Festival)**

- February 8, 2024, marks Isra Mi'raj, the Ascension of Prophet Muhammad, a national holiday in Indonesia. This is a significant Islamic festival commemorating the Prophet's ascension. On this day, Muslims gather in mosques for prayers and worship.
- February 9th to 15th is our Lunar New Year! Indonesia designates the first day of the Lunar New Year (February 10, 2024) as a national holiday. However, this year it coincides with February 8th, also a national holiday (Isra Mi'raj), so February 9th is designated as a flexible holiday. Therefore, friends in Indonesia can enjoy a four-day holiday during the Lunar New Year period (February 11th is a Sunday)! The week-long Chinese Lunar New Year holiday may seem unrelated to Bali, Indonesia, but it's also a peak travel season for many tourists from China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan, making it a busy time for Bali tourism!

![農曆新年假期大人放長假、小孩放寒假、還可以帶著家中長輩來峇里島避寒喔～](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_641795a1061cfdbbdd2df651788f4be8.png)

Escape the winter chill this Lunar New Year holiday with your family in Bali! Adults can enjoy a long vacation while kids are on winter break, making it the perfect time to bring the whole family together.

- February 28th to March 9th: Galungan & Kuningan — One of Bali's most important festivals, lasting around 10 days, celebrating the creation of the universe and the triumph of good over evil. During this festival, people honor their ancestors at home, seek their blessings, and hold ceremonies at temples to pay respect to the gods. Galungan & Kuningan also marks the beginning of the Balinese New Year according to the Pawukon calendar, symbolizing the day of cosmic creation. Schools in Bali close for two weeks during this period, highlighting its significance!

## April

### **April 8 - 15: Eid al-Fitr Joint Holiday (Public Holiday)**

### **April 10: Eid al-Fitr (Indonesian Public Holiday)**

- The 2024 Eid al-Fitr holiday in Indonesia will begin on Friday, April 8, and continue until April 15. Eid al-Fitr is the most important holiday in Indonesia, celebrating the end of the Muslim fasting month (April 10). It is also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, similar to the Chinese Lunar New Year. 

In 2024, the Eid al-Fitr holiday will start on Friday, April 8, and last until April 15. Eid al-Fitr is Indonesia's most significant holiday, marking the end of Ramadan (April 10). It is also a time for Indonesians to gather with family and friends, akin to the Chinese Lunar New Year. Typically, Bali starts to see an influx of local Indonesians around April 5, with crowds filling the island. This period is one of Bali's five peak seasons and the first major one. It is highly recommended to avoid traveling to Bali during this holiday!

## October

### **3rd - 12th: Navaratri (Bali Hindu Festival)**

### **12th: Dussehra (Bali Hindu Festival)**

- Navaratri is a Hindu festival celebrating the victory of Goddess Durga. ## November

### **Day 1: Diwali (Bali Hindu Festival)**

- Diwali is a Hindu festival celebrating the victory of light over darkness. This festival usually takes place in October or November each year, with the date determined by the Hindu calendar. In 2024, Diwali will be celebrated on November 1st.

## December

### **25th: Christmas (Indonesian National Holiday)**

### **26th: Christmas Flexible Holiday**

- December 25th is Christmas, a national holiday in Indonesia. For Christians, Christmas is an important festival, and Christians make up nearly 10% of Indonesia's population. Since Christmas and New Year's Day are close together, many Indonesians try to take time off to connect the two holidays into a longer break. This allows them to spend more time with family and friends and travel to other cities or countries. Indonesians have even coined a term called "Nataru," meaning "Christmas - New Year holiday," combining "Natal" (Christmas) and "tahun baru" (New Year).
