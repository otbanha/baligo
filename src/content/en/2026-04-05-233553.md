【Current USD Exchange Rate: 16475】**Bali** is currently in the transitional period between the dry and wet seasons (Peralihan). Although the island is gradually entering the dry season, the Indonesian Meteorological Agency (BMKG) has issued a **yellow alert** today, warning areas including Kuta and Ubud that there is still a **45% - 60%** chance of brief thunderstorms, strong winds, and lightning strikes in the afternoon.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775432045241-3ceb1d6524f69d9f46e44e81f7d6e105.jpg)

In terms of temperature, the daytime high is around **31°C**, with a humid feel due to 80% humidity; it drops to a comfortable **24°C** at night. The southern coast (Uluwatu, Nusa Dua) is influenced by the Australian monsoon, with winds shifting to east-southeast and wave heights of about 1-2 meters. Sea conditions are generally stable, but it is advised to avoid thunderstorm areas. Overall, the morning to noon is the golden time for outdoor activities, so it’s recommended to carry light rain gear and apply sunscreen.

Due to the Middle East conflict and international airspace instability, experts warn that Bali and Thailand will be the regions most affected by increased long-haul flight costs, and airlines have begun adjusting routes or adding fuel surcharges.

Culturally, today coincides with a challenging day in the Balinese calendar, considered unsuitable for weddings or groundbreaking. Last Saturday marked the end of **Saraswati Day**, and schools and temples across the island are still immersed in the atmosphere of knowledge worship; followed by **Pagerwesi** Day on April 8, when residents will strengthen spiritual protection and inner balance rituals. On the security front, a British man wanted by Interpol was arrested at the airport last week and is currently undergoing extradition proceedings. The Tourism Board has announced a goal to attract 6.63 million international tourists in 2026, shifting focus to promoting high-quality tourism.
