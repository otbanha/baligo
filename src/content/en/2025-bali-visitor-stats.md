'Which visa should I choose, and could I accidentally pick the wrong one?'

From the latest direct flight statistics for 2025, Bali's tourism structure is slowly evolving, but some patterns remain unexpectedly steady—like the increasing number of visitors from Singapore; Taiwanese tourists maintaining a consistent volume over two decades; and some previously dominant markets quietly shrinking.

More intriguingly, Australia, long considered Bali's largest source of visitors, has been surpassed by Europe for the first time.

In this article, I’ll first guide you through the **key trends in Bali’s foreign visitor demographics for 2025**, and then clarify the **Bali visa options and FAQs** that often confuse travelers.

No anxiety, no panic—just practical data and firsthand experience to ensure you're fully prepared before your trip.

If you're planning a trip to Bali, this guide will be incredibly useful.

## 2025 Bali Visitor Statistics by Country/Region

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99a8db2673d75441daa04e66ea7fd9aa.jpg)

This table is definitely worth a closer look.

According to official data, the total number of foreign visitors flying directly to Bali in 2025 is close to **7 million**, and there have been some subtle shifts in the source structure that might surprise you.

Let’s start with something Taiwanese travelers will relate to. Taiwanese visitors to Bali have remained steady for nearly **20 years**, hovering around **120,000** annually—no sudden spikes, no significant drops. This is very 'Taiwanese'—not chasing trends, not swayed by hype, just quietly returning year after year.

On the other hand, visitors from **Mainland China**, once a dominant force, have dropped to about **one-third** of their peak numbers. This shift is noticeable in airports, hotel demographics, and even the languages spoken by tour guides.

Here’s something I find particularly interesting. Australia has long been synonymous with Bali tourism—flights are frequent, the distance is short, and the vacation culture aligns perfectly. It’s consistently held the top spot. But this year, European visitors have officially surpassed Australians, becoming Bali’s largest source of tourists.

It’s not a sudden surge, but rather the result of gradual growth over time.

This trend reflects the changes we’ve been seeing in Bali in recent years:

- Long-term travelers are increasing, digital nomads are on the rise, and the off-season is no longer as quiet as it used to be.
- The European habit of 'staying longer once they arrive' is changing the rhythm of the entire island.

## Here's another important point many people ask about: How to choose the EVOA electronic landing visa?

Currently, the most common stumbling block for general travelers isn't whether to get a visa, but 'which one to choose'.

The most common options right now are B1 and C1:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dd1d15ae602a759d1d7e072cbd771d4d.jpg)

**Quick Reference:** [Complete Guide to Indonesia/Bali eVisa/eVOA Application: Steps and Precautions All in One Place!](/blog/bali-evisa-application-guide/)

### B1 Visa (Tourist Visa)

- Allows a stay of 30 days
- According to the latest news, **short-term pure tourism does not require proof of financial means**

The positioning of this visa is straightforward:

It's for traveling, vacationing, relaxing, taking photos, hitting the beaches, and enjoying good food.

But it **is not suitable for**:

Business meetings, contract signings, investment inspections, and medical-related activities.

### C1 Visa (Visit Visa)

- Allows a stay of 60 days
- Requires uploading a financial statement (PDF) showing a balance of at least USD$2,000 over the last 3 months

The focus of the C1 visa is not 'leisure' but 'visiting'.

It includes exchanges, inspections, and non-employment-related business contacts, but still does not permit working.

## How should regular tourists choose?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d26a3e513ac8df95013e940223bf7017.png)

The answer is actually quite simple.

If your itinerary includes:

- ✔ Traveling
- ✔ Vacationing
- ✔ Pure relaxation
- ✔ Traveling for no more than 30 days

**Choosing the B1 – Tourist Visa is completely sufficient.**

C1 is more suitable for those with multifaceted travel purposes, longer stays, or those requiring an official 'visit status'.

Otherwise, preparing too much documentation might just add unnecessary hassle. However, it's crucial to note that **you must have your return flight ticket and accommodation details ready**! If you're randomly checked and your travel motives are deemed suspicious, you might be asked to provide proof of financial means or even denied entry! After all, there are quite a few people illegally staying in Bali.

## Bali Visa FAQ

### Q1: What type of visa do general tourists need for Bali?

If your purpose is simply tourism, vacation, relaxation, eating, and playing, **just choose B1 (Tourist Visa)**, no need to overthink it. This is currently the choice for most short-term tourists.

### Q2: Does the B1 visa require proof of financial means?

According to the latest information, **the B1 visa for short-term tourism purposes does not require uploading proof of financial means**. However, it's still advisable to keep basic return flight and accommodation details on hand, just in case.

### Q3: If I want to stay in Bali longer, which one should I choose?

If you wish to stay for more than 30 days, or if your trip includes visiting friends, exchanges, or non-employment-related inspections, then you might consider **C1 (Visit Visa)**, which allows a stay of 60 days but requires uploading proof of financial means of over US$2,000 for the last 3 months (PDF).

### Q4: What is the biggest difference between B1 and C1?

In short:

- B1 = Pure tourism
- C1 = Non-employment related visits

B1 is not suitable for business meetings, contract signings, or investment-related activities;

C1 emphasizes visits, exchanges, and inspections, but also prohibits work.

### Q5: Can I use a tourist visa to view properties or discuss collaborations?

Officially, it's not recommended to use B1 for any business or investment-related activities. If your trip involves 'business contact,' C1 would be a safer choice.

### Q6: Can EVOA be applied for in Taiwan in advance?

Yes. Now EVOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival) can be applied for online before departure,

making the entry process much smoother and reducing the need to wait in long queues.

**Quick Reference:** [Complete Guide to Indonesia/Bali eVisa/eVOA Application: Steps and Precautions All in One Place!](/blog/bali-evisa-application-guide/)

### Q7: Can EVOA be extended?

Yes. The B1 visa can generally be extended once for an additional 30 days as per regulations. However, if you're certain you'll be staying for a long time, opting for the C1 visa from the start might be more convenient.

### Q8: Is it mandatory to purchase a return ticket when entering Bali?

Officially, it's 'recommended to have a departure ticket.' In practice, most travelers aren't individually checked, but it's better to be prepared for peace of mind.

### Q9: What if I chose the wrong visa?

Changing your visa after entry is relatively complicated. The best approach is to select the correct one before departure.

If you're just traveling, don't overcomplicate it—the **B1 – Tourist Visa** is sufficient.

## Final Reminder

Bali's visa regulations occasionally undergo minor adjustments. Checking the latest announcements 1–2 weeks before departure is the safest approach. Based on most travelers' experiences, choosing the right visa makes entering Bali quite straightforward.
