'Are there as many people coming to Bali now as before?'

'Which countries are sending more tourists? Which ones are sending fewer?'

'Which visa should I choose, and could I accidentally pick the wrong one?'

The truth is, the answers lie in the data.

Looking at the latest direct flight passenger statistics for 2025, Bali's tourism structure is gradually changing, but some trends remain surprisingly stable—like the increasing number of visitors from Singapore; Taiwanese tourists maintaining a consistent number year after year; and some previously prominent markets quietly shrinking.

Even more interesting is that Australia, long considered Bali's largest source of tourists, has been surpassed by Europe for the first time.

In this article, I'll first guide you through the **2025 trends in foreign visitors to Bali**, then clarify the **Bali visa options and FAQs** that often confuse travelers.

No anxiety-selling, no panic-inducing — just practical data and current entry experiences to ensure you're fully prepared before departure.

If you're planning a trip to Bali, this article will prove highly useful.

## Bali Country/Visitor Statistics for 2025

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99a8db2673d75441daa04e66ea7fd9aa.jpg)

This table is actually worth examining closely.

According to official data, the total number of foreign tourists flying directly to Bali in 2025 is close to **7 million**, with some subtle shifts in source markets that might surprise you.

Let's start with what Taiwanese travelers will notice most. Taiwanese visitors to Bali have remained stable for nearly **20 years**, hovering around 120,000 annually — no sudden spikes or significant drops. This is very 'Taiwanese' — not chasing trends, not swayed by hype, just quietly returning year after year.

In contrast, what many remember as the **mainland Chinese tourist wave** has dwindled to about **one-third** of its peak. This shift is noticeable in airports, hotel demographics, and even language offerings among tour guides.

Here's something I find particularly interesting. Australia has long been synonymous with 'Bali tourists' — frequent flights, proximity, and compatible vacation culture kept it firmly in first place. But this year, European visitors have officially surpassed Australians to become Bali's largest source market.

This isn't a sudden surge, but rather the result of gradual accumulation.

This trend aligns with changes observed in Bali over recent years:

- Long-term travelers are increasing, digital nomads are on the rise, and the off-season is no longer as quiet.
- Europeans' travel habit of 'staying longer once they arrive' is changing the rhythm of the entire island.

## Here's another key point many people ask about: How to choose the EVOA electronic visa on arrival?

Currently, what most travelers get stuck on isn't whether to get a visa, but 'which one to choose'.

The most common options right now are B1 and C1:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dd1d15ae602a759d1d7e072cbd771d4d.jpg)

**Quick reference:** [Complete Guide to Indonesia/Bali eVisa/eVOA: Application Steps and Important Notes All in One Place!](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/)

### B1 Visa (Tourist Visa)

- Allows stay of 30 days
- According to latest news, **no financial proof required for short-term pure tourism**

This visa has a straightforward purpose:

It's for tourism, vacation, relaxation, photography, beach hopping, and enjoying great food.

However, it **is not suitable for**:

Business meetings, contract signings, investment inspections, and medical-related activities.

### C1 Visa (Visit Visa)

- Allows a stay of 60 days
- Requires uploading a financial statement (PDF) showing a balance of at least USD$2,000 from the last 3 months

The focus of the C1 visa is not 'leisure' but 'visiting.'

It includes exchanges, inspections, and non-employment business contacts, but still does not permit working.

## So how should regular tourists choose?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d26a3e513ac8df95013e940223bf7017.png)

The answer is actually quite simple.

If your itinerary consists of

- ✔ Travel
- ✔ Vacation
- ✔ Pure relaxation
- ✔ Travel not exceeding 30 days

**Choosing the B1 – Tourist Visa is completely sufficient.**

C1 is more suitable for those with complex travel purposes, longer stays, or those requiring formal 'visit purposes'.

Otherwise, preparing too much information just adds unnecessary hassle. However, it's crucial to note that **you must have your return flight ticket and accommodation details ready**! If you're randomly checked and deemed suspicious in your travel motives, you might be asked to show proof of financial means or even denied entry! After all, there are quite a few cases of illegal stays in Bali.

## Bali Visa FAQ

### Q1: What type of visa do general tourists need for Bali?

If your purpose is simply tourism, vacation, relaxation, eating, and drinking, **just choose B1 (Tourist Visa)**, no need to overthink it. This is currently the choice for most short-term tourists.

### Q2: Does the B1 visa require proof of financial means?

According to the latest information, **the B1 visa for short-term tourism purposes does not require uploading proof of financial means**. However, it's still advisable to keep basic return flight and accommodation details on hand, just in case.

### Q3: If I want to stay in Bali longer, which one should I choose?

If you wish to stay for more than 30 days, or if your trip includes visiting friends, exchanges, or non-employment-related inspections, then consider **C1 (Visit Visa)**, which allows a 60-day stay but requires uploading proof of financial means of over US$2,000 for the last 3 months (PDF).

### Q4: What is the biggest difference between B1 and C1?

In simple terms:

- B1 = Pure tourism
- C1 = Non-employment related visits

B1 is not suitable for business meetings, contract signings, or investment-related activities;

C1 emphasizes visits, exchanges, and inspections, but still prohibits work.

### Q5: Can I use a tourist visa to view properties or discuss collaborations?

Officially, it is not recommended to use B1 for any business or investment-related activities. If your trip involves 'business-related contact,' C1 would be a safer choice.

### Q6: Can EVOA be applied for in Taiwan in advance?

Yes. Now EVOA (Electronic Visa on Arrival) can be applied for online before departure,

making the entry process much smoother and reducing the need to wait in long queues.

**Quick Reference:** [Indonesia/Bali eVisa/eVOA Complete Guide: Application Steps and Precautions All in One Place!](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/)

### Q7: Can EVOA be extended?

Yes. The B1 type visa can generally be extended once for 30 days according to regulations. However, if you know you'll be staying for a long time from the start, opting for C1 directly might be more convenient.

### Q8: Is it mandatory to purchase a return ticket when entering Bali?

The official regulation 'recommends having a departure ticket.' In practice, most travelers aren't individually checked, but it's better to be prepared for peace of mind.

### Q9: What if I chose the wrong visa?

Changing visas after entry is relatively troublesome. The best approach is to choose the correct one before departure.

If it's just for tourism, don't overcomplicate it—just go with the **B1 – Tourist Visa**.

## Final Reminder

Bali's visa regulations occasionally undergo minor adjustments. Checking the latest announcements 1–2 weeks before departure is the safest approach. Based on the experiences of most travelers, choosing the right visa makes entering Bali quite straightforward.
