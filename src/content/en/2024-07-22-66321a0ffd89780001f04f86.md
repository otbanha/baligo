## Day 1: Canggu

![Hea遊美食在海神廟欣賞了壯麗的海景，並了解了當地宗教文化](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_852a221e388b61983529c8eb8f87d3e2.jpg)

Hea Travel & Food admired the stunning sea views at Tanah Lot and learned about the local religious culture.

Her first stop was Tanah Lot in Canggu, Bali. This temple is built on the coast, and it is said that the sea god resides here. She shared a folk tale about Tanah Lot: Long ago, a fisherman encountered a fierce storm while out at sea, and his boat was swallowed by giant waves, causing him to disappear. The villagers, worried about his safety, prayed sincerely. Suddenly, the gates of Tanah Lot opened, revealing a secret passage to the ocean floor. Believing this to be the sea god's guidance, the villagers followed the passage and eventually found the fisherman at the bottom of the sea. After returning to the village, the fisherman shared the beautiful sights he had seen underwater. Since then, Tanah Lot has been regarded as a sacred place connected to the underwater world.

Pura Tirta Empul, one of Bali's six great temples, is an important religious site. Hea Travel Food experienced the holy spring purification ritual here, seeking blessings and good fortune.

> Editor's Note: In Ubud, besides the well-known Pura Tirta Empul, there are two hidden holy spring temples tucked away in quiet corners, waiting to be discovered.

> Here are two other holy spring temples we recommend:

> **Dalem Pingit Holy Spring** is a relatively secluded temple. This temple not only features unique architectural styles but also has a holy spring deeply revered by local devotees. Dalem Pingit is famous for its beautiful waterfall spring.

> **Pura Gunung Kawi Holy Spring** is a temple renowned for its rock carvings. In addition to ancient statues, there is also a hidden holy spring where devotees and visitors can bathe for blessings.

> Each of these two holy spring temples has its unique charm and cultural significance, offering serene bathing environments where you can find peace and purification in Bali's sacred atmosphere.

The food-filled Bali trip with Hea was full of excitement and joy, showcasing the unique charm of this beautiful island.

> Hea's introduction to Bali:

> Bali is an island in Indonesia, located in the eastern part of the country. Bali boasts many famous tourist attractions, including Tanah Lot Temple, Tirta Empul Temple, Ulun Danu Temple, Ubud Palace, Ubud Market, rice terraces, volcanoes, as well as beautiful beaches and resorts. There are direct flights from Hong Kong to Bali, operated by Cathay Pacific or Hong Kong Airlines, with a flight time of approximately 5 hours.
> Here's an introduction to what Bali has to offer: First, Bali is home to breathtaking natural landscapes. Its coastline is dotted with stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters, making it a paradise for vacationers. You can relax under the sun and enjoy beach activities like surfing, kayaking, or diving to explore the wonders of the underwater world.
> Additionally, Bali features magnificent mountains and lush greenery. You can embark on captivating hikes through rice fields, jungles, and waterfalls, taking in the island's unique natural beauty.
> Bali's cultural heritage is also a major draw. The island is rich in traditional Indonesian religion and art, with numerous temples, palaces, and traditional performances. You can visit stone and wood carving workshops, enjoy Balinese dance and music performances, and immerse yourself in local traditions.
> Moreover, Bali has a thriving yoga and spiritual tourism community. You can join yoga classes, meditation workshops, or visit spiritual temples to seek inner peace and balance.
> Of course, Bali also offers a rich culinary and shopping experience. You can savor local Balinese dishes like Babi Guling (roast pork) and grilled seafood, as well as explore local arts and crafts such as traditional textiles, wood carvings, and silver jewelry.
> In summary, Bali is a tourist destination with stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and a comfortable atmosphere. You can fully engage in various activities and experiences, whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural exploration—Bali will meet your expectations.
