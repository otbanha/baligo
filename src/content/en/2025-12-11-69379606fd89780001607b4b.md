For readers planning to visit Bali at the end of this year or early next year, have you seen our latest announcement from the Indonesian BMKG (Meteorological, Climatological, and Geophysical Agency) posted in our [Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/1DCH98qzUf/)? It's a warning about heavy rainfall from late December to early January, which is the most critical period during the annual rainy season.
As a long-term resident of Indonesia and frequent traveler to Bali, I’ve compiled a guide to help you plan your trip for January-February 2027 with more confidence.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_900409df53579a6382a92eb5f137d800.jpg)

## 🌧 Rainfall Will Reach 300–500mm: Late December to Early January is the Peak of the Rainy Season

According to BMKG’s forecast, Indonesia will enter a period of intensified rainfall starting late December, with the heaviest precipitation expected between **December 28 and January 10**, accompanied by frequent showers and thunderstorms.
Generally, the weather during this period follows this pattern:
- Occasional sunny mornings
- Increasing rain from midday to afternoon
- Possible rain again in the evening depending on cloud conditions
This isn’t the "worst storm of the year," but it’s definitely the 10–14 days during the rainy season that require extra caution.

## ⚠️ **What Travelers Should Be Aware Of: Not Dangerous, But Be Prepared**

Bali is still enjoyable during the rainy season, but this period does bring some noticeable impacts, including:
- Flight delays: Afternoon thunderstorms often cause temporary flight suspensions or delays.
- Unstable sea conditions: Large waves and poor visibility affect snorkeling, speedboat rides, and island-hopping activities.
- Waterlogging and localized flooding: Especially in certain areas of Denpasar or coastal roads.
- Variable mountain road conditions: Occasional landslides or muddy trails on waterfall routes.
For travelers, these aren’t dangers that require canceling your trip, but rather adjusting your "travel pace" and "expectations."

## 🌤 Don’t Misunderstand! There Are Still Sunny Days During the Rainy Season, and You Can Have a Great Time

I know many people get nervous when they see "heavy rainfall" or "300–500mm," thinking their entire trip will be ruined by rain.
But Bali’s rainy season isn’t continuous downpours—it’s characterized by "frequent short bursts of rain and afternoon thunderstorms."
The actual experience is usually:
- Sunny mornings with clear blue skies in some areas
- Changing weather after midday, with alternating heavy and light rain
- Stable evenings perfect for dining, shopping, and photography
So what travelers really need to do is "**make the most of the good weather in the morning**."

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f65b6856f52789decc1d59a008ae02ed.jpg)

## 🎒 **Bali Travel Tips for January-February 2027**

Here are some recommendations based on years of experience and BMKG's warnings this year:

### - Avoid island hopping trips from New Year's Eve to January 10th

This includes Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Penida, and Gili T, to prevent canceled boats or severe seasickness.

### - Schedule beach and outdoor activities for the morning

Want to capture rice terraces, waterfalls, Tanah Lot, Monkey Forest? Your chances of success are much higher in the morning.

### - Prepare waterproof gear

Lightweight raincoats, waterproof bags, flip-flops, and quick-dry clothes are lifesavers during the rainy season.

### - Keep your itinerary flexible

Don't pack every day full of activities, so you won't panic if the rain suddenly intensifies.

### - Rainfall becomes more 'predictable' after mid-January and throughout February

It still rains, but the rhythm is more like a typical rainy season, not as intense as during New Year's.

## 🌈 Bali's Rainy Season is Actually Quite Charming

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_30a080584a5be323dabd95d7c05fdcf7.jpg)

Honestly, I have a love-hate relationship with Bali during this time. After a rain shower, the entire island feels refreshed: the air is crisp, the greenery is lush, the waterfalls are spectacular, and Ubud's rice fields look like oil paintings. With fewer tourists and cheaper accommodations, this period can be even more comfortable than peak season for those who enjoy slow travel, nature photography, good food, and spa treatments.

The rainy season is never an obstacle to travel, just a reminder to adjust your pace.
