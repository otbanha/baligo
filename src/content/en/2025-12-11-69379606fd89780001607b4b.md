As a long-term resident of Indonesia and frequent traveler to Bali, I've compiled a guide to help you plan your trip for January-February 2027 with more clarity.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_900409df53579a6382a92eb5f137d800.jpg)

## 🌧 Rainfall Expected to Reach 300–500mm: Peak Rainy Season from Late December to Early January

BMKG predicts that Indonesia will enter a period of intensified rainfall by late December, with the most significant precipitation expected between **December 28 and January 10**. During this time, the weather typically follows this pattern:
- Occasional sunny mornings
- Increasing rain from noon to afternoon
- Possible evening showers depending on cloud systems
This isn't the 'worst storm of the year', but it's indeed the 10–14 days during the rainy season that require heightened awareness.

## ⚠️ **Travelers Should Note: Not Dangerous, But Be Prepared**

Bali remains enjoyable during the rainy season, but this period does bring some noticeable impacts, including:
- Flight delays: Afternoon thunderstorms often cause temporary cancellations or delays.
- Unstable sea conditions: Large waves and poor visibility affect snorkeling, speedboat rides, and island-hopping activities.
- Flooding in low-lying areas: Particularly in certain parts of Denpasar or coastal roads.
- Variable mountain road conditions: Occasional landslides or muddy paths on waterfall routes and trails.
For travelers, these aren't dangers that require canceling your trip, but rather adjusting your 'travel pace' and 'expectations'.

## 🌤 Don't Misunderstand! Sunny Days Still Exist During the Rainy Season, and You Can Still Have a Great Time

I know many people get nervous when they see 'heavy rainfall' and '300–500mm', imagining their entire trip will be spent in water.
But Bali's rainy season isn't continuous downpours—it's 'heavy, short bursts of rain + afternoon thunderstorms'.
The actual experience is usually:
- Sunny mornings with clear blue skies in some areas
- Weather turning cloudy by noon, with intermittent rain
- Stable evenings perfect for dining, shopping, and photography
So what travelers really need to do is '**make the most of the good weather in the morning**'.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f65b6856f52789decc1d59a008ae02ed.jpg)

## 🎒 **Bali Travel Recommendations for January-February 2027**

Here are recommendations based on years of experience and BMKG's warnings this year:

### - Avoid island-hopping trips from New Year's to January 10th

This includes trips to Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Penida, and Gili Trawangan, to prevent boat cancellations or severe seasickness.

### - Schedule beach and outdoor activities for the morning

Your chances of successfully photographing rice terraces, waterfalls, Tanah Lot Temple, and Monkey Forest are much higher in the morning.

### - Prepare waterproof gear

Lightweight raincoats, waterproof bags, flip-flops, and quick-dry clothing are lifesavers during the rainy season.

### - Keep your itinerary flexible

Don't pack every day full of activities, so you won't panic if the rain suddenly intensifies.

### - Rainfall becomes more 'predictable' after mid-January and throughout February

Rain still falls, but the rhythm feels more like a typical rainy season, not as intense as during the New Year period.

## 🌈 Bali in the Rainy Season is Actually Quite Charming

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_30a080584a5be323dabd95d7c05fdcf7.jpg)

Honestly, I have a love and dependence for Bali during this time.
After a rain shower, the entire island feels freshly washed: the air is crystal clear, the greenery is lush, waterfalls are spectacular, and Ubud's rice fields resemble oil paintings.
With fewer tourists than in peak season and cheaper accommodations, this period is even more comfortable for those who enjoy slow travel, photographing natural scenery, savoring good food, and indulging in spa treatments.

The rainy season has never been a hindrance to travel; it's just a reminder to adjust your pace.
