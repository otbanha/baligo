## Exposing the Mechanics of Currency Exchange Scams

This scam is actually very sophisticated and deceptive. The scenario generally goes like this:

1. You walk into a street-side currency exchange booth.
2. The clerk confirms the exchange rate with you and counts out the amount of foreign currency you wish to exchange.
3. He then pulls out a stack of Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) bills from under the counter and counts them in front of you.
4. After recounting several times, the clerk reorganizes the bills into a neat stack ready to hand over to you.
5. At that very moment, he skillfully manipulates the stack, causing some bills to fall behind the desk or into a drawer.

Even if you watch the entire process intently, you might not notice anything amiss. Many recorded videos, even when played back in slow motion, still struggle to reveal the trick.

## How to Protect Yourself from Scams?

1. **Only use authorized currency exchange locations**
   Choose to conduct transactions at banks, airports, resorts, or government-certified exchange bureaus.
2. **Familiarize yourself with Indonesian Rupiah notes**
   Different denominations and colors of bills can be confusing, especially for first-time visitors, making it easier to misunderstand the amount.
3. **Count the bills yourself at least twice**
   After receiving the bills, be sure to **personally** count them on the spot, even if others are waiting in line, do not rush to leave.

Legitimate businesses are not at all offended by this; in fact, they encourage customers to verify the amount.

## Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

**1. How does the money exchange scam in Bali work?**

The most common scam is 'quick counting and swapping,' where staff quickly hide some bills during counting, resulting in tourists receiving less money than they should without noticing immediately.

**2. Where is the safest place to exchange money in Bali?**

It's recommended to exchange currency at banks, officially authorized exchange offices (look for the 'PVA Berizin' logo), or at large hotels and airport exchange counters, as these places are more reliable.

**3. If I've been scammed, can I recover my losses?**

It varies. If you notice immediately and react on the spot, sometimes the shop might 'play dumb' and return the money. However, if you realize it later, the chance of recovery is very low. You can report it to the local Tourist Police.

**4. Which money exchange shops in Bali should I be extra cautious about?**

Avoid unlicensed roadside stalls or exchange places offering unusually favorable rates. Many scams happen at small stands advertising 'No Commission' or 'High Rate.'

**5. Can I record the money exchange process to protect myself?**

Yes. Although some staff might resist, recording in public places is legal. This can serve as evidence in case of disputes.

**6. What are some common pitfalls with Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) banknotes?**

Indonesian Rupiah banknotes come in various denominations ranging from 1,000 to 100,000, with similar colors and long numbers (many zeros), making it easy for beginners to misread the amount or denomination. Extra caution is advised.

**7. Why do some currency exchange shops offer exceptionally high rates?**

This is often a bait to attract tourists. During the actual transaction, they may use fraudulent tactics to secretly give less cash or manipulate the exchange receipt.

**8. Is using an ATM safer?**

Generally, it is safer, but avoid using ATMs in remote, poorly lit, or unmonitored locations. Some ATMs may have card skimmers installed. It’s recommended to manually shake the card slot before use or opt for ATMs inside banks or large supermarkets.

**9. Can using travel cards or credit cards completely avoid cash scams?**

It’s recommended to use a mix of cash and electronic payments during your trip, with credit cards as a backup.

**10. Where can I find Bali’s official Tourist Police?**

Tourist police offices are conveniently located in all major tourist areas across Bali. Simply search for 'Tourist Police Bali' on Google Maps to find the nearest station.
