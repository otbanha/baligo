Here’s a guide to celebrating Lunar New Year in Bali in 2025. If you and your family visit Bali during this festive season, you can also experience the New Year atmosphere in a foreign land!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_18bd7cbd5a329497c0a6de2563f31473.png)

---

### 1. Lunar New Year Celebrations

On January 29, 2025, Bali will welcome the Lunar New Year. The local Chinese community and tourism industry have prepared a variety of celebrations, including traditional cultural performances, culinary feasts, and festive decorations:

- **Lion Dance Performances**: One of the most popular traditional activities during Lunar New Year, symbolizing the expulsion of evil spirits and the ushering in of good fortune. You can catch spectacular lion dance performances at venues like [Mulia Bali](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=6546761) and [Courtyard Bali Seminyak Resort](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=624034). ([Source](https://en.tempo.co/read/1964175/6-ultimate-ways-to-celebrate-chinese-new-year-2025-in-bali))

---

- **Temple Blessings**: Chinese temples in Bali, such as Klenteng Caow Eng Boi and Satya Dharma Temple, will be adorned with red lanterns and money trees, hosting blessing ceremonies that blend Chinese and Balinese cultural elements. ([Source](https://dijiwasanctuaries.com/magazine/things-to-do-in-chinese-new-year-bali-2025-celebrations-festivities-and-culinary-delights))

- **Fireworks Displays**: Fireworks will light up the night sky in places like Singaraja, symbolizing the expulsion of bad luck and the welcoming of good fortune. ([Source](https://dijiwasanctuaries.com/magazine/things-to-do-in-chinese-new-year-bali-2025-celebrations-festivities-and-culinary-delights))

### 2. Culinary Experiences

During Lunar New Year, restaurants and hotels in Bali offer special menus that combine traditional Chinese cuisine with local flavors:

Bali's Lunar New Year celebrations go beyond just food, offering rich cultural experiences:

- **Traditional Music and Dance**: At the Kang Xi Temple in Tugu Bali Hotel, visitors can enjoy guzheng performances and lion dances, and participate in an 8-course traditional family feast. ([Reference](https://blog.tuguhotels.com/lunar-legends-celebrating-the-wood-snake-year-at-the-historic-kang-xi-temple/))
- **Festive Decorations and Markets**: The streets of Denpasar are adorned with red lanterns and festive decorations, creating a strong holiday atmosphere. Traditional handicrafts and holiday gifts can be purchased at the markets. ([Reference](https://dijiwasanctuaries.com/magazine/things-to-do-in-chinese-new-year-bali-2025-celebrations-festivities-and-culinary-delights))

### 4. **Family-Friendly Activities**

For family travelers, Bali's resorts offer activities designed specifically for children:

****Event Highlights****: Bali's Lunar New Year celebrations are rich in cultural colors and perfect for family participation. Here are the main event schedules:
- ****January 26-27****: Chinese Karaoke Competition (divided into children and adult categories).
- ****February 1-2****: Short Film and Photography Competition (shooting locations include the Nusantara Parade, Catur Muka area, and Puputan Badung Square).
- ****February 1****: Nusantara Parade (15:00-17:00), featuring an opening ceremony, traditional cheongsam fashion show, lion dance, martial arts performances, and a food festival.
- ****February 2****: Cosplay Competition (16:00) and Closing Ceremony (17:00-22:00), including cultural performances and Lunar New Year special programs.
- ****Other Activities****: Blood donation drive (08:30-13:00) and mass aerobics (starting at 16:00).
****Location****: Catur Muka Statue and Puputan Badung Square, Denpasar.
****Food Festival****: Offering a variety of Lunar New Year and Indonesian traditional delicacies.
