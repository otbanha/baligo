## **Common Questions about VISA:**

### **❓ Do I need to apply for a visa (VoA, Visa on Arrival)?**

Currently, citizens from 13 countries enjoy visa-free entry privileges ([see the list here](/blog/2024-09-04-66d83419fd89780001956ba2/)). Citizens from over 80 other countries, including Taiwan and China, need to apply for a visa.

### **❓ Do children need to apply for a visa?**

Age is not a factor for visa requirements - even infants need to apply.

### **❓ Where can I apply for an eVisa?**

Please refer to [this page](/blog/2024-02-06-65c217c5fd897800010113af/) for further instructions.

### **❓ Don't want to apply for an e-visa? Where to get a visa on arrival and what documents to prepare?**

If you haven't filled out the e-visa application in advance, head straight to the Visa on Arrival counter after disembarking. Make sure to have:

1. A passport valid for at least six months
2. At least one blank page in your passport for the stamp
3. Return/onward flight ticket
4. Cash or credit card for payment (IDR 500,000 or equivalent in USD. EUR, GBP, AUD, SGD cash also accepted)

### **❓ How long does it take to get an e-visa approved?**

Fill out the application form in detail and attach your photo. Once completed and successful, you'll immediately receive a QR Code in your registered email.

### **❓ How far in advance can I apply for an e-visa?**

The general tourist e-visa is valid for 90 days from the date of issue; you must depart within 30 days from the date of entry.

### **❓ What's the secret to a successful online application?**

Based on community members' experiences, using a Gmail account and applying during business hours increases your chances of success.

### **❓ What if the system auto-fills my name+surname differently from my passport?**

According to fellow travelers' reports, passing through customs should be smooth and problem-free.

If the system doesn't automatically populate the information, please enter it manually.

### **❓Name Format Question: Should I enter my name as given name + surname? Do I need spaces?**

Since the system doesn't accept hyphens "-" or other symbols in names, if your passport name contains a "-", you can replace it with a space or simply remove the "-". For example, if your passport name is "Wang, Fa-Fa", you can manually enter it as "Wang, Fa Fa" or "Wang, Fafa".

### **❓Name Order Question: Should the given name come first or the surname?**

Simply follow the format shown on your passport - this will never be wrong, and no one can say you're incorrect!

### **❓Place of Birth and Date of Birth**

Place of Birth and Date of Birth must match exactly with your passport.

### **❓What should I fill in for Issuing Country?**

If the system doesn't automatically populate this, Taiwanese nationals can enter ROC, Taiwan, or China - all are acceptable.

### **❓ What to Do If the Final Step of Credit Card Payment Fails?**

If the payment fails, you’ll need to reapply. Sometimes switching to a different card works (AMEX cards are not accepted; please use JCB, VISA, or Mastercard).

### **❓ Pay Attention to the Date!**

Once your eVOA visa is issued, you must use it within **90 days**, meaning you must enter Indonesia within 90 days of the visa’s issue date. If you fail to enter within this period, the visa will automatically expire and become invalid.

When filling in the arrival date, make sure not to mistakenly enter the departure date.

### **❓ Why does the Date of Issue automatically change to the application date?**

The **Date of Issue** that appears on your e-Visa is the date you applied for the eVoA, and it is not related to the Date of Issue on your passport. Therefore, it correctly shows the date you applied for the eVoA.

What if this field is filled incorrectly? The Date of Issue here is only for your reference regarding the application/issuance date of the e-Visa. If you have successfully applied and paid, you will pass through smoothly!

### **❓** What if the Date of Issue and Place of Birth are filled incorrectly?

If you notice any mistakes:

- The Date of Issue is just for your reference regarding the entry date, so it doesn't matter if it's incorrect.
- According to user reports, whether you fill in Taiwan or follow the birthplace on your passport, you can still pass through automated immigration.

### **❓** Can children also use automated immigration?

Children aged 6 and above can also use automated immigration at the airport. Please refer to the [Guide to Children's Visas for Entering Indonesia/Bali](/blog/2025-12-24-694bf0d9fd89780001214e1c/).

### **❓ How long is the tourist visa valid? What happens if it expires?**

The validity period of a tourist visa upon entry is 30 days, which can be extended once either at the local immigration office or [online application](/blog/2024-02-05-65c039affd89780001e5a3db/), allowing a maximum stay of 60 days.

Overstaying incurs a daily fine of 1,000,000 Indonesian Rupiah (approximately USD$65) and is considered illegal residence, potentially leading to detention, deportation, or a ban from entering Indonesia for a specified period.

## All **Indonesia Frequently Asked Questions:**

### **❓ How far in advance can I apply for** [All Indonesia](https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/) **?**

Since it now integrates a health declaration requiring the latest updates, you can only fill it out three days before your flight.

### **❓ Which airports allow entry using** [All Indonesia](https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/) **?**

Three international airports—Jakarta Soekarno-Hatta, Surabaya Juanda, and Bali Ngurah Rai—allow one-stop application via "All Indonesia." For other airports, please separately complete the **eVisa**, **eCD Customs Declaration**, and **SSHP Health Declaration** (see [link](/blog/2025-07-05-686902bafd89780001e3f490/) for instructions).

### **❓ How can I check my application status?**

You can view the latest progress through the "Application Status" option in [All Indonesia](https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/).

### **❓ Can I apply on behalf of family members/travel groups?**

✅ Got it! Simply fill out one application form with details for each family/group member. Each group is limited to **10 people**. For groups exceeding 10, please submit an additional form.

1. **Indonesian citizens** (entering with an Indonesian passport) and **foreign travelers** (entering with a foreign passport) must submit separate declarations.

### **❓ What file formats are supported for upload?**

Only **JPEG** and **PNG** formats are accepted. For more details, refer to the instructions within the application.

### **❓ Which devices are supported?**

Both **PC/Mac** and **Android/iOS** devices are supported.

### **❓ Is my personal information secure?**

The platform strictly adheres to cybersecurity standards and uses your data solely for immigration-related purposes.

### **❓ Is multilingual support available?**

Currently, the platform supports **Indonesian, English, and Chinese**, which can be switched in the settings.

### **❓ What to do after applying for eVOA/eVisa?**

Next, proceed to fill out the [All Indonesia](https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/) form.

### **❓** Is the tourism tax not integrated?

No. Because the tourism tax is only collected in Bali and does not apply nationwide. The tourism tax must still be paid through a separate system. Please refer to: [Bali Tourism Tax Guide: New 2024 Regulations, Payment Methods, and Island-Hopping Tips](/blog/2024-01-03-65955989fd89780001a95927/)
