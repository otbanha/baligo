For travelers who love in-depth exploration, now is the perfect time to plan a "cross-sea trip" between two islands. On one side is **Java Island**, known for its profound cultural heritage and majestic volcanoes, and on the other is **Bali**, showcasing the ultimate island charm. These two islands, separated only by a narrow strait, have distinctly different personalities.

## Exact End Date of the Rainy Season?

According to the latest official news released by the Indonesian Meteorological Agency (BMKG) from March to early April 2026, there is **no nationwide unified 'exact end date'** (e.g., a specific month and day). Instead, predictions are made in phases based on different 'Seasonal Zones' (ZOM).

Here’s a summary of the exact dates mentioned in media reports:

### **Key Timeline of Seasonal Transition**

As explained by BMKG head Teuku Faisal Fathani, the climatic turning points for 2026 are as follows:

- **End of Rainy Season / Start of Dry Season:** **April:** Approximately **16.3%** of regions, starting from **Nusa Tenggara** in the east, enter the dry season first.
    - **May:** Around **26.3%** of areas (including parts of **Bali** and Java) transition into the dry season.
    - **June:** The remaining **23.3%** of regions follow suit.
- **Peak of Dry Season:** About **61.4%** of Indonesia will reach the driest peak in **August 2026**.

### **Why Isn’t There an Exact Date?**

BMKG points out that the seasonal transition in Indonesia progresses "from east to west," resulting in varying dates across regions:

- **Eastern regions (e.g., Lombok, Sumbawa):** The dry season begins as early as early April.
- **Central regions (e.g., Bali, East Java):** The transition is expected to complete between mid-to-late April and early May.
- **Western regions (e.g., Sumatra, West Java):** The dry season may not fully set in until late May or June.

### **Special Climate Warning for 2026**

News media has highlighted the differences this year compared to previous years:

- **Early Arrival:** Nearly half (46.5%) of the country will experience an **earlier** start to the dry season.
- **Extreme Dryness:** With the weak La Niña phenomenon ending in February and the potential for a **weak El Niño** in July, BMKG predicts this year's dry season will be longer and drier than in 2025.
- **Transition Warning:** Currently, early April is in the **Pancaroba (transition period)**, and while the rainy season is nearing its end, there is still a chance of intense thunderstorms, strong winds, and even hail in the afternoons. BMKG advises the public to remain vigilant for sudden extreme weather during this transition.

If you are planning activities in **Bali** or Java, media reports citing BMKG recommend that **after mid-April**, the weather will stabilize significantly, with rainfall frequency dropping sharply, marking the official start of the outdoor activity season.

## 📅 2026 Indonesia Seasonal Transition FAQ

#### Q1: When is the rainy season expected to end in Indonesia in 2026?

A: According to BMKG forecasts, the end of the rainy season varies by region. About 16.3% of the country will enter the dry season in April, while most regions (around 50%) will complete the transition between May and June. Overall, this year's dry season is arriving earlier than usual.

#### Q2: Which areas will enter the dry season first?

A: The climate transition will begin in eastern Indonesia and move westward.
First phase (April): Nusa Tenggara Islands (e.g., Lombok, Komodo).
Second phase (May): Bali, East Java, and Central Java.
Third phase (June): West Java, Sumatra, and Kalimantan.

#### Q3: What makes this year's dry season particularly special?

A: This year is expected to be an **"extremely dry" year. With the end of the La Niña phenomenon and the potential emergence of a weak El Niño, BMKG predicts that around 46.5% of regions will experience an earlier start to the dry season**, with rainfall below the annual average.

#### Q4: Is it suitable to travel to Java or Bali now (early April)?

A: Currently, we are in the Pancaroba (transitional season). Although the rainy season is coming to an end, atmospheric conditions remain unstable, with the possibility of brief but intense afternoon thunderstorms. It is recommended to schedule outdoor activities in the morning and stay alert for sudden strong winds or heavy rain warnings.

#### Q5: What is the best month for travel in 2026?

A: Mid to late April: Ideal for travelers who enjoy lush green landscapes but prefer less rain.

May to September: The most stable weather period across Indonesia, with the lowest chance of rainfall, making it the golden time for visiting Bali's beaches or climbing Java's volcanoes (e.g., Bromo, Ijen).

#### Q6: What should travelers pay attention to during this period?

A: 1. Sun protection and hydration: As the dry season approaches, UV radiation will significantly increase, so be sure to protect yourself from the sun.

2. Temperature Alert: During the dry season, nighttime temperatures in the mountainous regions of Java can drop significantly (even approaching 0°C), so hikers should prepare adequate warm clothing.
