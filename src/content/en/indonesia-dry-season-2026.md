For travelers who love in-depth exploration, now is the perfect time to plan a "cross-sea trip" between two islands. ## The Exact End Date of the Rainy Season?

According to the latest official news released by the Indonesian Meteorological Agency (BMKG) from March to early April 2026, there is **no nationwide 'exact end date'** (such as a specific month and day), but rather a phased prediction based on various 'Seasonal Zones' (ZOM).

Here is a summary of the exact time points mentioned in media reports:

### **Key Timeline of Seasonal Transition**

According to BMKG head Teuku Faisal Fathani, the climatic turning points for 2026 are as follows:

- **Official End of Rainy Season / Start of Dry Season:**  **April:** Approximately **16.3%** of the regions enter the dry season first, starting from the eastern **Nusa Tenggara**.
    - **May:** About **26.3%** of the areas (including parts of **Bali** and Java) enter the dry season.
    - **June:** The remaining **23.3%** of the regions follow suit.
- **Peak of Dry Season:** Around **61.4%** of Indonesia will reach the driest peak in **August 2026**.

### **Why Isn't There an Exact Date?**

BMKG points out that the seasonal transition in Indonesia progresses "from east to west," resulting in varying dates:

- **Eastern regions (such as Lombok, Sumbawa):** The dry season begins in early April.
- **Central regions (such as Bali, East Java):** The transition is expected to complete between mid-to-late April and early May.
- **Western regions (such as Sumatra, West Java):** The dry season may not fully set in until late May or June.

### **Special Climate Warning for 2026**

News media has particularly highlighted the differences this year compared to previous years:

- **Early Arrival:** Nearly half (46.5%) of the country will experience an **earlier** start to the dry season.
- **Extreme Dryness:** With the weak La Niña phenomenon ending in February and the potential for a **weak El Niño** in July, BMKG predicts this year's dry season will be longer and drier than in 2025.
- **Transition Warning:** Currently in early April, during the **Pancaroba (transition period)**, although the rainy season is nearing its end, there is still a chance of intense thunderstorms, strong winds, and even hail in the afternoons. BMKG advises the public to remain vigilant for sudden extreme weather during this transition.

If you're planning activities in **Bali** or Java, media reports citing BMKG suggest that **after mid-April**, the weather will stabilize significantly, with rainfall frequency dropping sharply, marking the official start of the outdoor activity season.

## 📅 2026 Indonesia Seasonal Transition FAQ

#### Q1: When is the rainy season in Indonesia expected to end in 2026?

A: According to predictions by the Indonesian Meteorological Agency (BMKG), the end of the rainy season varies by region. Approximately 16.3% of the country will enter the dry season in April, while most regions (about 50%) will complete the transition between May and June. Overall, this year's dry season is arriving earlier than usual.

#### Q2: Which areas will enter the dry season first?

A: The climate transition will begin in eastern Indonesia and progress westward.
First phase (April): Nusa Tenggara Islands (such as Lombok, Komodo).
Second phase (May): Bali, East Java, and Central Java.
Third phase (June): West Java, Sumatra, and Kalimantan.

#### Q3: Will this year's dry season have any special characteristics?

A: This year is expected to be an **'extremely dry' year. With the end of La Niña and the potential emergence of a weak El Niño, BMKG predicts that the dry season will start earlier in about 46.5% of regions**, with rainfall below the annual average.

#### Q4: Is it suitable to travel to Java or Bali now (early April)?

A: Currently, we are in the Pancaroba (transitional season). Although the rainy season is coming to an end, atmospheric conditions remain unstable, with the possibility of brief but intense thunderstorms in the afternoon. It is recommended to schedule outdoor activities in the morning and stay alert for sudden strong winds or heavy rain warnings.

#### Q5: What is the best month for travel in 2026?

A: Mid to late April: Suitable for travelers who enjoy lush green scenery but prefer less rain.

May to September: The most stable weather period across Indonesia, with the lowest chance of rainfall, making it the golden time for visiting Bali's beaches or climbing Java's volcanoes (such as Bromo, Ijen).

#### Q6: What should I pay attention to during this travel period?

A: 1. Sun protection and hydration: As the dry season approaches, UV radiation will significantly increase, so be sure to protect yourself from the sun.

2. Temperature Alert: During the dry season, nighttime temperatures in the mountainous regions of Java can drop significantly (even approaching 0°C). Hikers should prepare adequate warm clothing.
