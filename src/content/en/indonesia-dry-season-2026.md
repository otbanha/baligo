## The Exact End Date of the Rainy Season?

According to the latest official news released by the Indonesian Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysical Agency (BMKG) between March and early April 2026, there is **no nationwide unified 'exact end date'** (such as a specific month and day). Instead, predictions are made in phases based on different 'seasonal zones' (ZOM).

Here’s a summary of the exact dates mentioned in media reports:

### **Key Timeline of Seasonal Transition**

As explained by BMKG's head, Teuku Faisal Fathani, the climatic turning points for 2026 are as follows:

- **Official End of Rainy Season / Start of Dry Season:**  **April:** Approximately **16.3%** of regions enter the dry season first, starting from the eastern **Nusa Tenggara**.
    - **May:** Around **26.3%** of areas (including parts of **Bali** and Java) transition into the dry season.
    - **June:** The remaining **23.3%** of regions follow suit.
- **Peak of Dry Season:** About **61.4%** of Indonesia will reach the driest peak in **August 2026**.

### **Why Isn’t There an Exact Date?**

BMKG points out that the seasonal transition in Indonesia progresses from east to west, resulting in varying dates across regions:

- **Eastern regions (e.g., Lombok, Sumbawa):** The dry season begins as early as early April.
- **Central regions (e.g., Bali, East Java):** The transition is expected to complete between mid-to-late April and early May.
- **Western regions (e.g., Sumatra, West Java):** The dry season may not fully set in until late May or June.

### **Special Climate Warning for 2026**

News outlets have highlighted key differences this year compared to previous years:

- **Earlier onset:** Nearly half (46.5%) of the country will experience an **earlier** start to the dry season.
- **Extreme dryness:** With the weak La Niña ending in February and a potential **weak El Niño** emerging in July, BMKG predicts a longer and drier dry season compared to 2025.
- **Transition warning:** Currently in early April, the **Pancaroba (transition period)** is underway. While the rainy season is ending, intense thunderstorms, strong winds, and even hail may still occur in the afternoons. BMKG advises the public to remain vigilant for sudden extreme weather during this transition.

If you're planning activities in **Bali** or Java, media reports citing BMKG suggest that **after mid-April**, the weather will stabilize significantly, with rainfall frequency dropping sharply, marking the official start of the outdoor-friendly season.

## 📅 2026 Indonesia Seasonal Transition FAQ

#### Q1: When is the 2026 rainy season in Indonesia expected to end?

A: According to BMKG predictions, the end of the rainy season varies by region. Approximately 16.3% of the country will enter the dry season in April, while most areas (around 50%) will complete the transition between May and June. Overall, this year's dry season is arriving earlier than usual.

#### Q2: Which areas will enter the dry season first?

A: The climate transition will begin in eastern Indonesia and progress westward.
First phase (April): Nusa Tenggara Islands (such as Lombok Island, Komodo Island).
Second phase (May): Bali, East Java, and Central Java.
Third phase (June): West Java, Sumatra Island, and Kalimantan.

#### Q3: What’s special about this year’s dry season?

A: This year is expected to be an **'extremely dry' year. With the end of the La Niña phenomenon and the potential emergence of a weak El Niño, BMKG predicts that the dry season will start earlier in about 46.5% of the regions**, with rainfall below the annual average.

#### Q4: Is it suitable to travel to Java or Bali now (early April)?

A: We are currently in the Pancaroba (transitional season). Although the rainy season is coming to an end, atmospheric conditions remain unstable, with the possibility of brief but intense thunderstorms in the afternoon. It’s recommended to schedule outdoor activities in the morning and stay alert for sudden strong winds or heavy rain warnings.

#### Q5: What is the best month to travel in 2026?

A: Mid to late April: Ideal for travelers who enjoy lush green landscapes but prefer less rainfall.

May to September: The most stable weather period across Indonesia, with the lowest chance of rainfall, making it the golden time to visit Bali’s beaches or climb Java’s volcanoes (such as Bromo and Ijen).

#### Q6: What should I pay attention to during this period?

A: 1. Sun protection and hydration: As the dry season approaches, UV radiation will significantly increase, so be sure to protect yourself from the sun.

2. Temperature Alert: During the dry season, nighttime temperatures in the mountainous regions of Java can drop significantly (even close to 0°C). Hikers should prepare adequate warm clothing.
