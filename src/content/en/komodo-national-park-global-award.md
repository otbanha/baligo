The renowned travel resource platform '[Time Out Travel](https://www.timeout.com/asia/news/the-11-most-beautiful-places-in-asia-according-to-time-out-040125)' regularly publishes global attraction rankings, recently unveiling its 'Most Beautiful Landmarks in Asia' selection. This carefully curated list of 44 spots from the world's most beautiful landmarks includes 11 locations in Asia, with Komodo National Park securing the top position on both the global and Asian lists.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8f912459f69f089f45717f5762ab0364.png)

Komodo is famous for its giant lizard habitats, but the judging team emphasizes that it's not just the unique reptiles that make it worth visiting. From rugged cliffs to exotic beaches, breathtaking natural landscapes are everywhere—especially the pink beach, which leaves travelers in awe as white sand blends with red coral fragments to create a dreamy gradient.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5a64eb10e3c1da7349d5a23729583014.jpg)

The national park offers a wealth of options for water sports enthusiasts, with its coral reefs and marine biodiversity listed as must-see attractions. Snorkeling or diving here feels like being in a giant aquarium.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_66bce95b0348817b1e72c3c0177af421.jpg)

The 'Time Out Travel' editorial team particularly notes that visiting Komodo is not only a visual treat but also a rare opportunity to explore an ecological treasure trove. UNESCO has listed it as a World Heritage Site, recognizing its natural landscapes and unique ecosystem. The best travel season is from April to October, when the weather is clear and the sea is calm, making it ideal for diving activities.

## Bonus: Other Selected Landmarks Among Asia's Most Beautiful Spots

▍Punakha Valley, Bhutan Located in western Bhutan, Punakha Valley is stunning with its emerald terraced landscapes. A recommended side trip is to Khamsum Yulley Namgyal Chorten, a four-story Buddhist stupa housing exquisite artworks.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a48d3efe90362f72bb1aa44f7e269534.jpg)

▍Ha Giang Province, Vietnam

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5b34565bdbeb482d284c6f8c69d260b1.jpg)

Seeking pristine mountain views and escaping the crowds? Winding roads traverse terraced valleys and towering peaks, as if moving through a landscape painting.

▍Zhangye Danxia, China

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b9c114cb5c7e46f5ba8554dfd056559f.jpg)

This geological park in Gansu Province features colorful hills with alternating stripes of deep red, golden yellow, and emerald green. These stunning textures are natural masterpieces formed through millions of years of erosion and tectonic movements.

▍Three Whale Rocks, Thailand

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f29165c6dd242cb362160e72104df10e.jpg)

The 'Three Whale Rocks' consist of three massive rock formations resembling a pod of whales, surrounded by endless tropical rainforests. On clear days, you can even glimpse the Phou Khao Khouay mountain range in Laos.
