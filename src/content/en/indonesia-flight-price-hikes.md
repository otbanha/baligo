Affected by the surge in international oil prices, the Civil Aeronautics Administration has announced that starting April 7, 2026, the passenger fuel surcharge for some routes (China Airlines, EVA Air, STARLUX Airlines) departing from Taiwan will significantly increase by 157%. This price hike is particularly impactful for travelers planning long-haul flights.

## How Much Are Bali and Jakarta Routes Increasing?

### ✈️ Taipei → Bali, What Kind of Route Is It?

Conclusion first👇

👉 Direct flights from Taipei to Bali (Taipei → Bali) are usually 'regional routes', not long-haul routes.
Airlines generally categorize them as follows:

#### 🟢 Regional Routes (Short / Regional Haul)

Flight time: Approximately 4～6 hours
Destinations: Within Asia (Southeast Asia, Northeast Asia)
Examples:
Taipei → Bali
Taipei → Bangkok
Taipei → Singapore
👉 The Bali flight duration is about 5 hours, fitting perfectly within this range

#### 🔴 Long-Haul Routes (Long Haul)

Flight duration: Typically over 8 hours
Destinations: Europe, America, Australia
Examples:
Taipei → Los Angeles
Taipei → Paris

According to the new rate, the fuel surcharge for flights to Bali (regional route) will increase from the original $17.5 (approximately NT$551) to $45 (approximately NT$1,417) per person, making it nearly NT$866 more expensive for a one-way trip. For a round trip, the ticket cost per person will increase by approximately NT$1,732. For a family trip, a family of four would have to pay nearly NT$6,928 more just in surcharges, which is equivalent to one or two nights' stay in a family villa. This does not include possible increases in the base fare.

## How to cope?

Facing the rising cost of flights to Bali or Jakarta, travel experts have proposed three strategies to help you save money:

![How to cope?](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775702565966-1000117789.jpg)

### Strategy 1: Utilize 'Outbound Transits' to Avoid High Direct Flight Prices

When flying to Bali, you don't necessarily have to choose expensive direct flights. Consider first taking a budget or traditional airline to regional transit hubs like Hong Kong, Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, or Singapore, then connect to flights to Bali or other cities in Indonesia.

· Saving principle: Buying tickets in segments is usually cheaper than purchasing a direct flight ticket, especially now when long-haul fuel surcharges are astonishing.
· Potential benefit: You could save NT$10,000 to NT$20,000 and also have a short stay in the transit city, enjoying two destinations in one trip.

### Strategy 2: Smartly Combine 'Low-Cost Airlines'

Not all airlines charge the same fuel surcharges. Many low-cost airlines have different fee structures, and some do not charge extra.

· Operation suggestion: When flying from Taiwan to Bali, you can first take airlines like Tigerair Taiwan, which do not charge additional fuel surcharges, to Macau, Bangkok, or Singapore, then connect to low-cost airlines like AirAsia to Bali (AirAsia usually includes surcharges in the ticket price, making the total price potentially more competitive).
· Key reminder: Be sure to add up all segment ticket prices, baggage fees, seat selection fees, etc., to know if it's truly cost-effective.

### Strategy 3: Seize the Golden Booking Period and Leverage Credit Card Benefits

This price increase is based on the "ticket issuance date" (starting from 12:00 AM on April 7). If you've already issued your ticket before this deadline, even if your departure date is during the summer vacation or long holidays months later, you'll still be subject to the lower old version of the surcharge.

· Remedial Plan: If you've missed the deadline, keep a close eye on travel benefits offered by credit cards, such as airport transfers, lounge access, or discount codes and cashback available through specific travel platforms, which can somewhat offset the increase.
