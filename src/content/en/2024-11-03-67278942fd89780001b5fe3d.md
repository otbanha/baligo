Who would have thought that while enjoying the sunset on the beautiful beaches of Bali, this serene moment could become a stage for scammers? Today, I want to share a personal experience and remind everyone to stay alert during their Bali trip to avoid falling into a scam.

***

### The 'Foreigner' Scam on the Beach

This happened on the last night of my family's trip to Bali. We were strolling along the beach, with waves gently lapping the shore and the sunset painting the sky in hues of red. The atmosphere was romantic and relaxing. However, two strangers caught our attention—a man and a woman who appeared to be from the Middle East. They greeted us warmly, asked where we were from, and shared seemingly harmless topics.

### The Scam Revealed: Starting with Currency

Their conversation gradually shifted to our currency, expressing curiosity about 'never having seen Taiwanese/Hong Kong/Malaysian banknotes.' They then opened their wallets to show stacks of US dollars, seemingly implying they were also tourists, subtly building a 'friendly trust.' Next, they asked us to show our currency, framing it as a 'cultural exchange.'

At this point, my husband reached for his wallet, ready to display our bills. However, my intuition told me something was off. As they tried to get closer to see our wallet, I quickly stopped my husband, simply telling the 'curious foreigners' that 'we’re not interested,' and we walked away. The pair seemed displeased but didn’t pursue us further.

***

### Tips to Avoid Scams

Afterward, I realized this was likely a scam tactic. The 'currency display' method is often used to lull tourists into a false sense of security, distracting them and giving scammers an opportunity to 'swap' or 'steal' their money. Although we prevented the scam this time, this experience reminded my family and me to stay alert during travels, no matter how relaxed the environment may seem.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a5e5250780fcc77664a9ee525e8da9fb.png)

This is actually a long-standing scam, and the perpetrators may be well-dressed, attractive individuals who lower your guard. In such scams, the tricksters often use distraction techniques to achieve their goals. They might make requests like 'Let’s see the currency of your country,' causing victims to relax their vigilance and open their wallets. Once the victim displays the bills or starts rummaging, the scammer moves closer, observes the location of the cash, or even pretends to be friendly by reaching out, making subtle moves like quickly grabbing a few bills.

Another common tactic is 'switching': scammers may replace the victim’s bills with similar paper or worthless foreign currency while inspecting them. By the time the victim notices the missing money, the scammer has already disappeared into the beach crowd, leaving them defenseless.

In short, the core of these scams is to distract the victim, making them unknowingly lose their belongings. So when strangers make requests to handle your valuables, always stay alert and avoid giving them any opportunity.

To ensure a safer trip to Bali, here are a few reminders:

1. **Keep your distance from strangers**: Even if they seem friendly, maintain an appropriate distance to avoid giving them close access to your belongings.
2. **Beware of 'curious' topics**: When the conversation shifts to your valuables or personal privacy, stay vigilant, respond briefly, and quickly end the discussion.
3. **Always protect your belongings**: When out and about, avoid casually displaying cash or valuables, especially in unfamiliar environments where others might see what you’re carrying.
4. **Trust your instincts**: If something feels off, trust your gut and quickly leave the situation or decline suspicious requests.
5. **These scenarios can happen anywhere**: While we encountered these scammers on the beach, they could be anywhere in Bali!
6. **Watch out for male-female pairs**: Many of these scams involve a male-female duo.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc163f9a5759d0b79c361d1cbcee0546.png)

Bali’s stunning scenery and vibrant culture are indeed captivating, but staying alert during your travels won’t diminish the fun—it will allow you to enjoy every moment with peace of mind. I hope my experience provides some safety insights for your Bali journey, helping everyone avoid these common travel traps!
