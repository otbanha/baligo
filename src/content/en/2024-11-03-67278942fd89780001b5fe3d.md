Who would have thought that while enjoying a beautiful sunset on Bali's beaches, this idyllic moment could become a stage for scammers? Today I want to share a personal experience and warn travelers to stay vigilant in Bali to avoid falling into fraud traps.

***

### The Beach 'Foreigner' Scam

This happened on our last evening in Bali with my family. We were strolling along the beach, waves gently lapping the shore, the sunset painting the sky in romantic hues. Suddenly, two strangers caught our attention - a Middle Eastern-looking man and woman. They greeted us warmly, asking where we were from and engaging in seemingly harmless conversation.

### The Scam Revealed: Starting with Currency Questions

The conversation gradually turned to our currency, and they expressed curiosity about 'never having seen Taiwanese/Hong Kong/Malaysian banknotes before'. They then opened their wallets to show stacks of US dollars, seemingly establishing a 'friendly mutual trust' as fellow tourists. Next, they asked to see our currency, framing it as a 'cultural exchange'.

At this point, my husband was reaching for his wallet to show our bills. But something felt off. As they tried to move closer to get a better look at our wallet, I quickly stopped my husband, simply telling these 'curious foreigners' that 'we're not interested', and we walked away promptly. The pair seemed displeased but didn't pursue us further.

***

### Tips to Avoid Scams

After this incident, I realized it was likely a scam. The 'show me your money' tactic is often used to make tourists let their guard down, distracting them and giving scammers the opportunity to 'swap' or 'steal' their money. Although we managed to prevent the scam this time, the experience reminded me and my family to stay vigilant while traveling, no matter how relaxed the environment may seem.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a5e5250780fcc77664a9ee525e8da9fb.png)

This is actually a long-standing scam, and the scammers might even be well-dressed, attractive individuals who lower your guard. In such scams, the perpetrators typically use distraction techniques to achieve their goal. They might ask, 'Let’s see the currency from your country,' to make the victim relax and open their wallet. Once the victim shows the money or starts rummaging, the scammer moves closer, observes where the cash is kept, or even pretends to be friendly while reaching out to touch, making subtle moves like quickly grabbing a few bills.

Another common tactic is 'switching': The scammer might ask to see your money and replace it with similar-looking paper or worthless foreign currency. By the time the victim notices the missing money, the scammer has already disappeared into the crowd on the beach, leaving you defenseless.

In short, the core of these scams is to distract the victim and steal their belongings without them noticing. So, when a stranger asks to handle your money or belongings, always stay alert and avoid giving them any opportunity.

To make your trip to Bali safer, here are a few reminders:

1. **Keep your distance from strangers**: Even if they seem friendly, maintain a safe distance to avoid giving them a chance to get close to your belongings.
2. **Be wary of 'curious' topics**: When the conversation shifts to your money or personal privacy, stay alert, respond briefly, and end the conversation quickly.
3. **Always protect your belongings**: When out and about, avoid displaying cash or valuables, especially in unfamiliar environments.
4. **Trust your instincts**: If something feels off, trust your gut and quickly remove yourself from the situation or decline suspicious requests.
5. **These scenarios can happen anywhere**: While we encountered this scam on the beach, it could happen anywhere in Bali!
6. **Watch out for male-female pairs**: Many of these scams involve a male-female duo.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc163f9a5759d0b79c361d1cbcee0546.png)

Bali’s stunning scenery and warm culture are indeed captivating, but staying vigilant during your trip won’t diminish the fun—it will actually help you enjoy every moment more safely. I hope my experience serves as a reminder for everyone traveling to Bali, helping you avoid falling into these common travel traps!
