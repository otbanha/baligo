## When "Brunch" Meets Bali Time

Honestly, when I first saw the Brisa Brunch running from 12 PM to 4 PM, I couldn’t help but laugh. This isn’t brunch—it’s a Bali-style lazy feast! A local friend winked and said, "In Bali, time is for savoring, not rushing."

KLOOK’s new "[Dining Experience at Alila Villas Uluwatu](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1063734&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F156213-dining-experience-at-alila-villas-uluwatu%2F)" lets regular folks like us, who can’t afford to stay at Alila, still indulge in a taste of luxury. So we booked a 1 PM slot, planning to savor this culinary journey all afternoon.

## A Seafood Lover’s Paradise

Kicking off with the seafood station is a smart move. The glistening Tuna Crudo sparkles like gems in the sunlight, and the moment you squeeze on lime juice, it’s like hearing the ocean sigh. The Jimbaran Shrimp carries that familiar Balinese charcoal-grilled aroma, reminding me of the sunset BBQ stalls on Jimbaran Beach—only here, the plating is ten times more refined. The standout is the octopus dish, incredibly tender, paired with a smoked red pepper sauce that had a French diner at our table exclaiming "C'est magnifique!" three times. Just as we were digging in, a live saxophonist started playing, blending lazy jazz melodies with the sound of waves, adding a romantic touch to this seafood feast.

## The Chef’s Hidden Gem

Just when we thought we'd seen every dish, the server mysteriously handed us a handwritten note: 'Today, the chef has prepared a special off-menu treat: mini beef rendang burgers.' This Indonesian classic was deconstructed into a three-layer delight: beef slow-braised for 36 hours, caramelized shallots, and homemade sambal, all tucked into charcoal buns. I secretly noticed that almost every table's guests couldn't help but squint their eyes on the first bite—a reflex of taste buds meeting a surprise.

## The Pastry Chef's Mischief

The dessert section felt like a modern art exhibition, especially the 'Cracked Chocolate Sphere.' The server pours warm passion fruit sauce tableside, watching the chocolate shell crack open like volcanic lava to reveal coconut ice cream and tropical fruits inside. The kids at the next table were utterly mesmerized, forgetting their mobile games. My personal favorite was the pandan tiramisu—a cross-border romance between Southeast Asian herbs and Italian dessert, with a rebellious deliciousness.

![甜點師的惡作劇](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9d5b33c029f609db6725ad75ff99f312.jpg)

## Digesting by the Cliff

The ultimate luxury after a feast is lingering on the ocean-facing sofa, letting the Indian Ocean breeze carry away the fullness. The server thoughtfully adjusts the sunshade angle and brings chilled towels and ginger tea. We stayed from high noon until sunset, watching CIRE Restaurant's minimalist lines gradually gilded in gold, with champagne glasses on the table reflecting the entire Bali twilight.

On the ride back, I kept replaying the memory of the sunset and crashing waves, suddenly understanding why someone once said: 'In Bali, the best seasoning is always the scenery.'
