Standing on the terrace of CIRE restaurant at Alila Villas Uluwatu, the Indian Ocean breeze gently caresses your cheeks, while the awe-inspiring cliffside seascape stretches beneath your feet. This isn't just any brunch date—it's a gastronomic marathon that lasts from noon till dusk.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9a2eb3064d9941b060c7ff0260527c5d.jpg)

### When "Brunch" Meets Bali Time

Honestly, when I first saw that "Brisa Brunch" runs from 12 PM to 4 PM, I couldn't help but laugh. This isn't brunch—it's a Bali-style leisurely feast! A local friend winked and said, "In Bali, time is meant to be savored, not rushed."

KLOOK's newly launched "[Dining Experience at Alila Villas Uluwatu](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1063734&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F156213-dining-experience-at-alila-villas-uluwatu%2F)" allows ordinary folks like us, who can't afford to stay at Alila, to indulge in a taste of luxury. So, we booked a 1 PM seat, planning to savor this culinary journey throughout the afternoon.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cd123f46c39552d4bf8ae22a5cc40d7e.png)

### A Seafood Lover's Paradise

Starting with the seafood section was undoubtedly the right move. The crystal-clear Tuna Crudo shimmered like a gemstone under the sunlight, and the moment lime juice was drizzled over it, I could almost hear the ocean sigh. The Jimbaran Shrimp carried the familiar aroma of Balinese charcoal grilling, reminding me of sunset barbecues at Jimbaran Beach—only here, the presentation was ten times more refined. The most surprising dish was the octopus, impossibly tender, paired with a smoked paprika sauce that had our French dining companion exclaiming "C'est magnifique!" three times. As we indulged, a live saxophonist began playing, blending lazy jazz melodies with the sound of waves, adding a romantic touch to this seafood feast.

### The Chef's Hidden Gem

Just when we thought we'd tried everything, the waiter mysteriously handed us a handwritten card: "Today, the chef has prepared a special Padang beef mini burger—it's not on the menu." This Indonesian classic was deconstructed into a three-layered mini feast: beef slow-cooked for 36 hours, caramelized shallots, and homemade sambal, all sandwiched in a charcoal bun. I secretly noticed that almost every table squinted their eyes upon taking the first bite—a natural reaction when taste buds encounter a delightful surprise.

### The Pastry Chef's Prank

The dessert section resembles a modern art exhibition, especially the "Shattered Chocolate Sphere." The waiter pours hot passion fruit sauce over it right at your table, watching the chocolate shell crack like molten lava, revealing coconut ice cream and tropical fruits inside. The kids at the next table were utterly mesmerized, even forgetting their mobile games. My personal favorite is the pandan tiramisu—a rebellious fusion of Southeast Asian herbs and Italian dessert, offering a uniquely delicious twist.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9d5b33c029f609db6725ad75ff99f312.jpg)

### Digesting by the Cliff

The most luxurious part of a full meal is lingering in the ocean-facing sofa seats, letting the Indian Ocean breeze carry away the fullness. The waiter thoughtfully adjusts the sunshade angle and brings chilled towels and ginger tea. We stayed from midday until sunset, watching CIRE Restaurant’s minimalist lines gradually gilded by the golden hour, with champagne glasses reflecting Bali’s twilight.

On the way back, I kept reminiscing about the sunset and the sound of the waves, suddenly understanding why people say, "In Bali, the best seasoning is always the scenery."
