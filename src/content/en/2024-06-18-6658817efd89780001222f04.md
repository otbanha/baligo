## Visa/Tourism Tax

First and foremost, the most important thing is the visa. We applied for our electronic visa about three weeks in advance, following instructions from fellow travelers and YouTube tutorials, filling out the form step by step. We received the electronic visa immediately after submission, and remember to print it out as it will be checked at customs. Don't take any chances; the tourist tax must also be paid online in advance. Upon arrival, it's advisable to allow about an hour from immigration to baggage claim to be on the safe side. After collecting your luggage, you'll need to scan a QR code from a pre-filled electronic customs declaration form before you can proceed.

## Currency Exchange

Due to our itinerary, we exchanged currency directly at the airport (USD to IDR). While the exchange rate wasn't as favorable as outside, we only exchanged a small amount, so it wasn't a big concern. The money we exchanged was mainly used for tipping and paying our driver in cash. Wherever possible, we used credit cards (remember to choose the local currency option), such as when we went for spa treatments or dined at beachfront restaurants. We always checked in advance if card payments were accepted, which made things more convenient by reducing the need to carry large amounts of cash and avoiding potential issues from unfamiliarity with the currency.

## Transportation

Navigating Bali's roads can be quite complex, so it's advisable to either hail a ride or arrange for a private car based on your itinerary. Traffic congestion is primarily concentrated around the airport area, while other regions are relatively smoother. I'd like to share about a local driver named Budi, whom we met on our first day through KKday when we booked airport transfers. We found him to be excellent, and for the subsequent short-distance transfers over the next few days, we directly contacted him in advance via WhatsApp.

Budi is friendly and trustworthy. During the drive, feel free to ask him anything you're curious about regarding Bali. Although his English carries a local accent, it's generally understandable, and the communication process is pleasant. When we chatted in Chinese among ourselves, Budi didn't interrupt and quietly drove, which made us feel very comfortable. After a good airport transfer experience, we chose him for our other days' transportation as trust had already been established. Even when we inquired very late (in the early hours), he always responded quickly and arrived earlier than the agreed time. Budi's schedule is quite packed, but he mentioned he would handle pickups and drop-offs as long as the locations aren't too remote.

**Dinner**: On two evenings, we randomly chose restaurants at Jimbaran and Seminyak Beach for dinner. In Indonesia, in addition to the listed price, there is usually a maximum of 21% tax added (11% government dining tax + 5% to 10% service fee). Some restaurants include tax in their prices, while others do not, so it's best to confirm with the establishment before dining. ## Tipping

After researching online, I found mixed opinions about tipping culture in Indonesia. From our experience, there were times when offering a tip surprised the recipient, and they seemed hesitant to accept it. They would only take it when we expressed gratitude for their service. Generally, we tipped 10,000 IDR for rides or luggage handling, and an additional 10,000 IDR in cash for restaurant staff when settling the bill. At hotels, helpful staff who took many photos for us declined tips, saying it wasn’t necessary. Personally, I feel tipping isn’t mandatory but is a gesture of courtesy. If you do tip, try to avoid giving coins, as it’s considered less polite. (Note: The highest-value coin in Indonesia is 1,000 IDR, roughly equivalent to USD$0.06.)

## SAVA SPA at Indigo

Left: SAVA Spa / Right: Relaxation area after the spa session. Provided by Angel Wu.

At the Indigo Hotel, we opted for the SAVA SPA services. The ambiance is elegant, and the facilities are top-notch, especially the 90-minute hot stone massage we experienced, which left a lasting impression.

At the start of the hot stone massage, the therapist continuously checked if the temperature of the stones was comfortable, ensuring it wasn't too hot—a thoughtful touch. Throughout the session, the therapist's skilled techniques allowed us to fully unwind, experiencing deep relaxation and relief.

SAVA SPA features only six couples' massage rooms, making advance reservations essential to enjoy their premium services. The spa treatments are provided by an award-winning professional team, with each therapist rigorously trained to ensure every guest receives the best possible experience.

In addition to hot stone massage, SAVA SPA offers a variety of other spa treatments, such as aromatherapy massage, deep tissue massage, and herbal therapies, each designed to meet the diverse needs of their clients. Whether you're looking to relieve stress, ease muscle tension, or simply relax and rejuvenate, SAVA SPA has the perfect option for you.

Overall, SAVA SPA is an excellent place for relaxation of both body and mind. The elegant environment, thoughtful service, and professional techniques made our time there truly enjoyable. I highly recommend making a reservation in advance to ensure you can experience their high-quality spa services.
