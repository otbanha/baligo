## Visa/Tourism Tax

First and foremost, the visa is crucial. We applied for our e-visa about three weeks in advance, following guides from fellow travelers and YouTube tutorials to fill out the form step by step. We received the e-visa immediately after submission—remember to print it out as it will be checked at customs. Don’t take chances; the tourism tax must also be paid online beforehand. Upon arrival, allocate about an hour from immigration to baggage claim to be safe. After collecting your luggage, you’ll need to scan a pre-filled electronic customs declaration QR code to proceed.

## Currency Exchange

Due to our itinerary, we exchanged currency directly at the airport (USD to IDR). While the exchange rate isn’t as favorable as outside, we only exchanged a small amount, so it wasn’t a big concern. The cash was mainly used for tips and paying drivers in cash. Wherever possible, we used cards (remember to choose the local currency option), such as for spa treatments and dining at beachfront restaurants. We always asked in advance if card payments were accepted, which made it convenient to avoid carrying too much cash and eliminated potential hassles with unfamiliar currency values.

## Tipping

After researching online, we found mixed opinions on tipping culture in Indonesia. Our experience was that tipping sometimes surprised the recipients, who were hesitant to accept. They would only take the tip when we expressed gratitude for their service. Generally, we tipped IDR 10,000 for rides and luggage handling, and an additional IDR 10,000 in cash to service staff when settling meal bills. At hotels, helpful staff who took many photos for us indicated that tipping was unnecessary. Personally, I feel tipping is not mandatory but a gesture of politeness. If you choose to tip, try to avoid coins as it's considered impolite. (Editor's Note: The largest Indonesian coin is 1,000 rupiah, approximately USD$0.03).

## SAVA SPA within Indigo

![圖左：SAVA Spa / 圖右：SPA 結束小小休息區。Angel Wu提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7e6548e1f0331b60456a40ecb933e7c8.jpg)

Left: SAVA Spa / Right: Small rest area after SPA. Provided by Angel Wu.

At Hotel Indigo, we chose the services of SAVA SPA. The environment is elegant and the facilities are well-maintained, especially the 90-minute hot stone massage we experienced, which left a deep impression.

During the hot stone massage, the therapist continuously checked if the temperature of the stones was comfortable, ensuring it wasn't too hot, which was very thoughtful. Throughout the session, the therapist's skilled technique allowed us to fully relax, experiencing deep relief and relaxation.

SAVA SPA has only six double massage rooms, so it's essential to book in advance to enjoy their high-quality services. The spa treatments are provided by an award-winning professional team, with each therapist rigorously trained to ensure every guest receives the best experience.

In addition to the hot stone massage, SAVA SPA offers a variety of other spa treatments, such as aromatherapy massage, deep tissue massage, and herbal therapies, each catering to different guest needs. Whether you want to relieve stress, ease muscle tension, or simply relax, SAVA SPA has the perfect option for you.

Overall, SAVA SPA is an excellent place for relaxation. The elegant environment, thoughtful service, and professional techniques made our time there very enjoyable. We recommend booking in advance to ensure you can experience their high-quality spa services.
