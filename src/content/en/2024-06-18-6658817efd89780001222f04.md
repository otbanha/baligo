Trip Dates: May 19 - 23, 2024

## Visa/Tourism Tax

First and foremost, the most important thing is the visa. We applied for the electronic visa about three weeks in advance, following instructions from fellow travelers and YouTube tutorials, and filled out the form step by step. We received the electronic visa immediately after submission, and remember to print it out as it will be checked at customs. Don’t take chances—make sure to pay the tourism tax online as well. Upon arrival, it’s advisable to allocate about an hour from immigration to baggage claim. After collecting your luggage, you’ll need to scan a pre-filled electronic customs declaration QR code to proceed.

## Currency Exchange

Due to our itinerary, we exchanged currency directly at the airport (USD to IDR). While the exchange rate wasn’t as favorable as outside, we only exchanged a small amount, so it wasn’t a big concern. The money we exchanged was mainly used for tips and paying drivers in cash. For places that accept card payments, we opted to pay by card (remember to choose the local currency rate), such as during spa treatments or dining at beachfront restaurants. We also asked in advance if card payments were accepted, which made it convenient to avoid carrying too much cash and eliminated potential issues with unfamiliar currency values.

## Transportation

Navigating Bali's roads can be tricky, so we recommend hiring a driver or arranging private transportation for your itinerary. Traffic congestion is mainly concentrated near the airport, while other areas are relatively better. We'd like to share our experience with a local driver named Budi, whom we met on our first day through a KKday airport transfer booking. We found him to be excellent, and for the next few days' short-distance transfers, we contacted him directly via WhatsApp in advance.

{{block:包車}}

Budi is friendly and trustworthy. During the ride, feel free to ask him anything you're curious about Bali. Although his English has a local accent, it's generally understandable, and communication is pleasant. When we chatted in Chinese among ourselves, Budi remained quiet and focused on driving, which made us feel comfortable. After a positive airport transfer experience, we chose him for our other days' transportation as we had already built trust. Even when we contacted him late at night (early morning), he always responded quickly and arrived earlier than the agreed time. Budi's schedule is quite packed, but he mentioned he can provide transfers as long as the location isn't too remote.

**Dinner**: For two evenings, we randomly chose restaurants on Jimbaran and Seminyak beaches for dinner. In Indonesia, besides the listed price, there’s usually an additional tax of up to 21% (11% government tax + 5% to 10% service charge). Some restaurants include taxes in their prices, while others don’t, so it’s best to confirm with the staff before dining. At a beach restaurant in Jimbaran, we opted for a basic meal set that included a bucket of rice, one vegetable dish, shrimp, fish, shellfish, satay, a side dish, two bottles of beer, and various sauces. This set cost around USD$23, which was very affordable for two people.

## Tipping

After checking online, I found that some say Indonesia has a tipping culture, while others say it doesn't. Our experience is that a few times when we tried to tip, the recipients were surprised and somewhat reluctant to accept. They would only take it when we expressed our gratitude for their service. Basically, we would give IDR 10,000 for rides and luggage handling; we would also give an additional IDR 10,000 in cash to the service staff when settling the bill. At the hotel, some helpful staff took many photos for us and indicated that tipping was not necessary. So, personally, I think tipping is not mandatory but a courtesy. If you do tip, try to avoid coins as it is considered impolite. (Editor's note: The largest coin denomination in Indonesia is IDR 1,000, about USD$0.06)

## SAVA SPA at Indigo

![圖左：SAVA Spa / 圖右：SPA 結束小小休息區。Angel Wu提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7e6548e1f0331b60456a40ecb933e7c8.jpg)

Left: SAVA Spa / Right: Small rest area after SPA. Provided by Angel Wu.

At the Indigo hotel, we opted for the services of SAVA SPA. The environment is elegant, and the facilities are excellent, especially the 90-minute hot stone massage we experienced, which was truly memorable.

When starting the hot stone massage, the therapist continuously checks if the temperature of the stones is comfortable, ensuring you don't feel overheated - a very thoughtful touch. Throughout the session, the therapist's skilled techniques allowed us to completely relax, experiencing deep relief and relaxation.

SAVA SPA only has six couple's massage rooms, so it's essential to book in advance to enjoy their premium services. The spa treatments here are provided by an award-winning professional team, with each therapist undergoing rigorous training to ensure every guest receives the best experience.

In addition to hot stone massage, SAVA SPA offers various other spa treatments such as aromatherapy massage, deep tissue massage, and herbal therapies, each designed to meet different customer needs. Whether you're looking to relieve stress, ease muscle tension, or simply relax, SAVA SPA has the perfect option for you.

Overall, SAVA SPA is an excellent place for physical and mental relaxation. The elegant environment, thoughtful service, and professional techniques made our time here truly enjoyable. We recommend booking in advance to ensure you can experience their high-quality spa services.
