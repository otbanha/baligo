# Top Topics & News Roundup for Bali Travelers (July 25, 2026)

**Slug:** `bali-news-2026-07-25`

Recent travel buzz in Bali centers on cultural events, airline services, and traffic improvements. Here are 5 key updates worth noting before your trip.

***

## 🪁 International Bali Kite Festival Takes Flight with Teams from 23 Countries in Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784890846485-festival-layang-layang-internasional-di-bali-1784800657431_169.jpeg)

The annual International Bali Kite Festival is officially underway, featuring teams from **23 countries** this year. Held at the event grounds near Sanur, the festival showcases large traditional and creative kites, alongside cultural performances and local cuisine—making it one of the most iconic cultural events of the summer.

**Traveler Tip:** If you're staying in Sanur, set aside a half-day to enjoy the kite competitions. Bring a hat, sunscreen, and plenty of water. The afternoon winds are stronger, offering the best time for kite viewing.

***

## ✈️ Garuda Indonesia Again Named One of the World's Most Punctual Airlines

According to the latest on-time performance statistics, **Garuda Indonesia** has once again been recognized as one of the world's most punctual airlines, reflecting its strong performance in flight punctuality. This is valuable information for travelers arranging connecting flights or transfers to outlying islands.

**Traveler Tip:** Even when flying with a highly punctual airline, during the peak summer season, it's recommended to arrive at Ngurah Rai International Airport at least 3 hours early for international check-in.

***

## 🏖️ Lovina Beach Upgrades Lead to Longer Stays for Many Travelers

Following recent environmental cleanups and landscape improvements, Lovina Beach has received positive feedback from many visitors. Local businesses report that some foreign tourists who originally planned to visit other attractions ended up extending their stays by several days due to the relaxed coastal atmosphere, reflecting the growing appeal of North Bali tourism.

**Traveler Tip:** If you're planning a dolphin-watching trip in Lovina, consider staying an extra night to explore hot springs, waterfalls, and other mountain attractions in North Bali at a more leisurely pace.

***

## 🚦 Kuta Traffic Improvement Plan Progresses, Officials Report Reduced Congestion

Bali officials continue to review recent traffic improvement measures implemented in Kuta's popular tourist areas, including adjustments to intersection signals, traffic flow patterns, and road management. Preliminary assessments show that congestion during peak hours has improved, and authorities may expand these measures to other tourist zones in the future.

**Traveler's Tip:** When heading to Kuta, Seminyak, or Canggu, it's still advisable to allow extra travel time during peak hours, especially for trips to and from the airport.

***

## ⚠️ Stay Vigilant About Travel Safety During Summer Peak Season—Keep Your Phone and Valuables Secure

With the surge in summer tourists, police are stepping up patrols in popular tourist areas and reminding travelers to keep an eye on valuables like phones, cameras, and bags. Avoid holding your phone in crowded areas or while riding a scooter to reduce the risk of theft.

**Traveler's Tip:** When using navigation, consider a phone mount and voice-guided directions. Store your passport in your accommodation's safe and carry only a photocopy when out and about.
