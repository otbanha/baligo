Buying souvenirs during an international trip is a way to preserve the precious memories of that journey. When you visit the beautiful island of Bali, not only can you enjoy stunning beaches and unique culture, but you can also bring back a variety of souvenirs to share with family and friends, keeping the unforgettable trip alive.

## Bali Cultural Handicrafts

Bali's handicrafts are world-renowned for their exquisite craftsmanship and unique designs. These handicrafts include:

- **Wood Carvings**: Wood carving is one of Bali's representative crafts. These carvings range from deities and animals to everyday items, all filled with intricate details and vitality, making them excellent choices for home decor.
- **Stone Carvings**: Similar to wood carvings, Bali's stone carvings showcase the artisans' exceptional skills. You can find these beautiful pieces in temples, gardens, or as indoor decorations.

![砂岩雕刻的迷你石雕](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_edcff6e24f592177be3b581f0d4042eb.png)

Sandstone-carved miniature stone sculptures

- **Textiles**: Bali's textiles are famous for their vibrant colors and traditional patterns. Whether it's sarongs, scarves, or tablecloths, they all capture the essence of local culture.
- **Handicrafts**: Such as bracelets, necklaces, and other handwoven accessories.
- **Incense**: Bring the scent of Bali home with you. For a detailed introduction, check [here](/blog/bali-art-souvenirs-guide/).
- 👜 **Bali Rattan Bags**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0da8524d97ec3c0a0ab9714a64bb7364.png)

These handmade rattan bags are known for their simple, natural, and stylish designs, making them practical whether as small carry-on bags or larger capacity options. Prices start from **Rp 50,000, with basic models around Rp 150,000, and larger or more intricately designed styles reaching up to Rp 500,000**. They are a popular choice for both gifting and personal use.

## Local Food Specialties

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_08d17f1959db86dd24612c06e5c7ebca.png)

If you're a food lover, then Bali's local specialties are not to be missed. Bringing home some local delicacies not only extends the delightful flavors of your journey but also allows you to share this joy with friends and family.

- **Coffee**: Bali's coffee is renowned for its unique flavor and high quality. Especially the coffee from Kintamani, which carries a subtle fruity aroma, is highly favored by tourists. Additionally, civet coffee is a famous product of Bali, available in various brands at almost any supermarket. However, be cautious when purchasing, as considering the scale of production and rarity, there's a high chance of encountering counterfeit products.
- **Spices**: Bali offers a wide variety of spices, from chili, white pepper, bay leaves, turmeric to cloves. These spices not only enhance the flavor of cooking but also serve as practical souvenirs.

### Salak (available at major supermarkets and fruit stalls)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5246f0141cf9c98d5f703e5b8a69248c.png)

[Lam Tak Wai] Brought a lot back to Hong Kong! Among Bali's many unique souvenirs, Salak (pronounced 'Salak' in Indonesian) is an absolute must-try. Named for its snake-like scaly skin, Salak has a crisp, sweet, and juicy flesh that is beloved by both tourists and locals.

The best variety is Salak Gula Pasir, known for its exceptional sweetness, delicate flesh, and subtle honey-like aroma, offering an excellent taste experience. Salak Gula Pasir is typically sweeter than regular Salak, making it a favorite among many.

Not only does Salak taste amazing, but it's also rich in vitamins and antioxidants, helping to boost immunity and aid digestion. This fruit is conveniently packaged and easy to carry, making it a healthy and delicious souvenir. Share this tropical delight with family and friends, letting them experience Bali's unique charm.

On your next trip to Bali, remember to bring some Salak Gula Pasir to share with loved ones, adding a sweet touch to your wonderful travel memories.

### Pia Agung (Near the Airport, Specialty Shops in Canggu)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3917493dbcbee0bb3a0c799f6c1a57ae.png)

[Provided by Anna Wei] Pia Agung is a Balinese pastry that originated from Guangdong-style mooncakes in China. Over the years, it has evolved and blended with local flavors to become a popular dessert in Bali today. Pia Agung is loved by both locals and tourists, not only as a must-have treat during festivals but also as a popular souvenir choice.

Pia Agung comes in a variety of flavors to cater to different tastes. Classic flavors include mung bean, cheese, chocolate cheese, chocolate, milk custard cheese, tiramisu, cappuccino, and durian. These flavors retain the traditional mooncake essence while incorporating Indonesian ingredients, offering a delightful surprise with every bite.

The pastry features a crispy outer layer and a sweet, rich filling that melts in your mouth, making it an irresistible dessert. Whether paired with a cup of coffee or enjoyed as an afternoon snack, Pia Agung is an excellent choice.

### **Chocolate Monggo**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a91fa60eb71f580eac358c12a830f694.jpg)

**Chocolate Monggo** is a **Belgian Artisan Chocolatier** based in Bali. The founder, originally a Belgian engineer, moved to Bali and started crafting island-inspired origin chocolates using local spices and organic cocoa.

**Features:**

- Uses premium Indonesian cocoa beans
- Bean-to-bar fine chocolate
- Various flavors: pure cocoa, mango dark chocolate, etc.
- Beautifully packaged, perfect for gifts

**Quality:** Clearly labeled cocoa percentage, a mid-to-high-end chocolate brand, and one of Bali's popular chocolate souvenirs.

**Where to Buy:** The main store in Ubud offers the most comprehensive selection and includes a glass workshop where you can watch the production process. There are also branches in Seminyak and Kuta.

**Personal Recommendation:** If you prefer less sweetness, go for the pure cocoa flavor—you won't be disappointed. I regretted not buying more after returning home! (Recommended by [一切是自己·靈魂覺醒](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/17AMwFcBD5/))
