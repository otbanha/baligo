## Balinese Cultural Handicrafts

Balinese handicrafts are renowned worldwide for their exquisite craftsmanship and unique designs. These include:

- **Wood Carvings**: Wood carving is one of Bali's signature crafts. From statues of deities and animals to everyday items, these carvings are full of detail and vitality, making them excellent home decor choices.
- **Stone Carvings**: Similar to wood carvings, Balinese stone carvings showcase the artisans' exceptional skills. You can find these beautiful pieces in temples, gardens, or as indoor decorations.

![砂岩雕刻的迷你石雕](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_edcff6e24f592177be3b581f0d4042eb.png)

Miniature sandstone carvings

- **Textiles**: Balinese textiles are known for their vibrant colors and traditional patterns. Whether it's sarongs, scarves, or tablecloths, they all let you feel the local vibe.
- **Handicrafts**: Small handwoven items like bracelets and necklaces.
- **Incense**: Bring the scent of Bali home. For a detailed guide, check [here](/en/blog/bali-art-souvenirs-guide/).
- 👜 **Balinese Woven Bags**

![峇里島文化手工藝品](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0da8524d97ec3c0a0ab9714a64bb7364.png)

These handmade woven bags are known for their simple, natural, and stylish designs. Whether you prefer a small clutch or a large tote, they are highly practical. Prices start from **Rp 50,000, with basic styles around Rp 150,000, and larger or more intricate designs reaching up to Rp 500,000**. They are a popular choice for both gifts and personal use.

## Local Food & Specialties

![美食特產](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_08d17f1959db86dd24612c06e5c7ebca.png)

If you're a food lover, Bali's specialty products are not to be missed. Bringing home local treats not only extends the flavors of your journey but also lets you share that joy with family and friends.

- **Coffee**: Balinese coffee is renowned for its unique flavor and high quality. Coffee from Kintamani, in particular, has a subtle fruity aroma that visitors love. Luwak (civet) coffee is another famous Balinese product, widely available in supermarkets under many brands. Be cautious when buying—given the scale of production and rarity, there's a high chance of purchasing counterfeit products.
- **Spices**: Bali offers a wide variety of spices, from chili peppers, white pepper, bay leaves, and turmeric to cloves. These spices not only enhance your cooking but also make practical souvenirs.
