Buying souvenirs during international travels is a way to preserve precious memories. When you visit the beautiful island of Bali, not only can you enjoy stunning beaches and unique culture, but you can also bring back a variety of souvenirs to share with family and friends, keeping this unforgettable journey alive.

## Bali Cultural Handicrafts

Bali's handicrafts are renowned worldwide for their exquisite craftsmanship and unique designs. These handicrafts include:

- **Wood Carvings**: Wood carving is one of Bali's representative crafts. These carvings range from deities and animals to everyday items, all filled with intricate details and vitality, making them excellent choices for home decor.
- **Stone Carvings**: Similar to wood carvings, Bali's stone carvings showcase the artisans' exceptional skills. You can find these beautiful pieces in temples, gardens, or indoor settings.

![砂岩雕刻的迷你石雕](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_edcff6e24f592177be3b581f0d4042eb.png)

Sandstone-carved miniature stone sculptures

- **Textiles**: Bali's textiles are famous for their vibrant colors and traditional patterns. Whether it's sarongs, scarves, or tablecloths, they all capture the local essence.
- **Handmade Accessories**: Such as bracelets, necklaces, and other handwoven items.
- **Incense**: Bring the scent of Bali home. For detailed information, check [here](/blog/bali-art-souvenirs-guide/).
- 👜 **Bali Rattan Bags

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0da8524d97ec3c0a0ab9714a64bb7364.png)

These handmade rattan bags are known for their simple, natural, and stylish designs. Whether it's a small everyday bag or a large-capacity one, they are highly practical. Prices start from **Rp 50,000, with basic models around Rp 150,000, and larger or more intricately designed styles reaching up to Rp 500,000**, making them a popular choice for both gifting and personal use.

## Local Food Specialties

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_08d17f1959db86dd24612c06e5c7ebca.png)

If you're a food lover, then Bali's specialty foods are a must-try. Bringing home some local delicacies not only extends the delightful flavors of your trip but also allows you to share this joy with friends and family.

- **Coffee**: Bali's coffee is renowned for its unique flavor and high quality. Especially the coffee from Kintamani, which carries a subtle fruity aroma, is highly favored by tourists. Additionally, civet coffee is another famous product of Bali, available in various brands at almost any supermarket. However, be cautious when purchasing, as considering the scale of production and its rarity, there's a high chance of encountering counterfeit products.
- **Spices**: Bali offers a wide variety of spices, from chili, white pepper, bay leaves, turmeric to cloves. These spices not only enhance the flavor of your cooking but also serve as practical souvenirs.

Bali Banana offers a variety of flavors, allowing you to choose according to your preferences or try them all:

### Salak (Snake Fruit) (Available at major supermarkets/fruit stalls)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5246f0141cf9c98d5f703e5b8a69248c.png)

【Lam Tak Wai】Brought a lot back to Hong Kong! Among Bali's many unique souvenirs, Salak (pronounced 'Salak' in Indonesian) is an absolute must-try. Named for its snake-like textured skin, the fruit is crisp, sweet, and juicy, loved by both tourists and locals.

The best variety is Salak Gula Pasir (Sugar Salak), which is exceptionally sweet with a delicate texture and a hint of honey aroma, offering an excellent taste. Salak Gula Pasir is usually sweeter than regular Salak, making it a favorite for many.

Salak is not only delicious but also rich in vitamins and antioxidants, helping to boost immunity and aid digestion. Conveniently packaged, it’s a healthy and tasty souvenir, allowing friends and family to enjoy Bali’s unique flavors while savoring this tropical delight.

On your next trip to Bali, remember to bring some Salak Gula Pasir to share this sweet tropical treat with loved ones, adding even more joy to your wonderful travel memories.

**PS. Fresh fruits cannot be brought into Taiwan (dried fruits are allowed)!**

### Pia Agung (Available at shops near the airport and in Canggu)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3917493dbcbee0bb3a0c799f6c1a57ae.png)

[Provided by Anna Wei] Pia Agung is a Balinese pastry that originated from Guangdong-style mooncakes in China. Over the years, it has evolved and blended with local flavors to become a popular dessert in Bali today. Loved by both locals and tourists, Pia Agung is not only a must-have during festivals but also a top choice for souvenirs.

Pia Agung comes in a variety of flavors to cater to different tastes. Classic options include mung bean, cheese, chocolate cheese, chocolate, milk cheese, tiramisu, cappuccino, and durian. These flavors retain the traditional mooncake essence while incorporating Indonesian ingredients, offering a delightful surprise with every bite.

The pastry features a crispy outer layer and a sweet, rich filling that melts in your mouth, making it an irresistible treat. Whether paired with a cup of coffee or enjoyed as an afternoon snack, Pia Agung is always a great choice.

### **Chocolate Monggo**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a91fa60eb71f580eac358c12a830f694.jpg)

**Chocolate Monggo** is a **Belgian Artisan Chocolatier** based in Bali. Founded by a Belgian engineer who moved to the island, the brand uses local spices and organic cocoa to create chocolates infused with the essence of Bali.

**Highlights:**

- Uses premium Indonesian cocoa beans
- Bean-to-bar craft chocolate
- Variety of flavors: pure cocoa, mango dark chocolate, and more
- Beautifully packaged, perfect for gifts

**Quality:** Clearly labeled cocoa percentage, positioned as a mid-to-high-end chocolate brand, and one of Bali's most popular chocolate souvenirs.

**Where to Buy:** The Ubud flagship store offers the widest selection and features a glass workshop where you can watch the chocolate-making process. Other locations include Seminyak and Kuta.
