If you're hunting for a newly opened design hotel in Canggu for 2026, **La Mewali Resort & Spa Canggu** is one of the most exciting properties to watch.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148600313-65ee973a.jpeg)

Crafted by **Ini Vie Hospitality** and officially opening in 2026, this boutique resort ditches Bali's typical tropical villa style for a **Modern Cave Retreat** concept, featuring fluid architecture, sculpted clay walls, lagoon waterscapes, and healing spaces for a completely different stay experience.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148852711-2bc3a90a.jpeg)

The standout is the resort's most talked-about **cave-style pool villa**. From the moment you step inside, you'll realize this isn't about opulent luxury—it's about being wrapped in nature and embracing a slower pace of life. If you've stayed at many Bali villas before, La Mewali might just offer something entirely fresh.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148853262-6ab292ad.jpeg)

***

## 🏝️ La Mewali's Biggest Draw: Live in a Modern Cave Pool Villa

What makes La Mewali truly captivating isn't the pool or the restaurant—it's the **One Bedroom Plunge Pool Villa with Lagoon Access**, which has become the brand's signature.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148853777-8b736aff.jpeg)

Officially styled as a **Modern Cave Retreat**, this room type draws inspiration from natural rock formations and organic curves, blending them with Balinese architectural elements to reinterpret the tranquility and embrace of a cave space.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148854629-954de5c3.jpeg)

Unlike typical Bali villas, you'll find almost no traditional right angles here. Instead, the space is defined by soft, sweeping curves. Walls feature sculpted terracotta textures, complemented by wood, natural stone, and warm lighting, giving the room a gentle, gallery-like ambiance.

By day, natural light filters in along the curved surfaces, with shadows shifting slowly over time. At night, warm amber light reflects off the walls, evoking the quiet stillness of a natural cavern. This design isn't about mimicking a cave—it's about using materials, light, and lines to craft a comfortable, layered living experience.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148855644-ae5d190c48e6ac04e580d62df4e80c90.webp)

Another delightful surprise is how seamlessly the indoor and outdoor spaces blend together.

Sliding open the large floor-to-ceiling windows reveals your private plunge pool, and just one step further connects you to the resort's lagoon, creating a visual effect of water stretching endlessly. Even just sitting on the terrace in a daze, you can feel the tranquil harmony of water and architecture.

Beyond the private pool, the room is equipped with a projector, a hammock, a king-size bed, a sculptural bathroom, and a terrace space—making it feel less like a place to just sleep for the night and more like a private vacation retreat you'd want to spend an entire day in.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148856646-cba1bab1790c7db5b5694c9a563afc0a.webp)

For couples, honeymooners, or anyone who loves capturing stylish accommodations on camera, I believe this room type is the highlight worth experiencing at La Mewali.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148857153-dccd1efb.jpeg)

## 💦 The Lagoon Is the True Soul of the Resort

Beyond the cave villas, another signature feature of La Mewali is the lagoon water landscape that runs through the entire property.

Unlike a typical large pool, this one uses a series of interconnected lagoon designs that naturally link the accommodation areas, public spaces, and restaurants, making the entire resort feel like a floating village on water.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148854208-9ae86e80.jpeg)
![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148855199-44829efa.jpeg)
![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783148856225-c46129af.jpeg)

By day, sunlight dances on the water, exuding a vacation vibe; by evening, warm lights reflect into the lagoon, creating a completely different, romantic scene.

The resort also features a swim-up bar, floating breakfasts, and several lagoon-facing lounge areas. Whether you're sipping a cocktail in the pool or strolling along the water's edge at night, it's easy to forget you're actually in bustling Canggu.

***

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

Although La Mewali is a boutique resort, its public facilities are quite comprehensive.

**Wisteria Rooftop** is a daytime rooftop restaurant serving coffee, brunch, and light bites—perfect for a leisurely start to your day.

In the evening, head to **Puri Pure** to savor modern Indonesian cuisine while enjoying lagoon views and a slow-paced dinner.

For spa lovers, **Svaha Spa La Mewali** offers treatments that go beyond traditional massage, incorporating sound healing concepts to create a more holistic relaxation experience through massage, sound, and spatial design.

The resort also features a gym, yoga space, free Wi-Fi, free parking, and a shuttle service within the Canggu area, making daily life very convenient.

## 📍 Location & Surroundings (Refer to the [Canggu Guide Map](/map/canggu/) for the exact location)

La Mewali is located on **Jl. Raya Canggu**, not far from Canggu's liveliest area, yet cleverly set back from the main traffic, making it much quieter than you might expect.

It's about a 5 to 10-minute drive to Echo Beach, while Batu Bolong and Berawa Beach are also within 10 to 15 minutes. Whether you're looking to surf, explore cafés, enjoy beach clubs, or catch the sunset, everything is conveniently close.

For a day trip, Tanah Lot is about a 20 to 30-minute drive away—a popular attraction many visitors plan to see.

# 💬 Real Guest Reviews

Guests who have stayed here generally mention that the most impressive aspects are the resort's design and lagoon views.

Many say that although the resort has just opened, the facilities are well-maintained and feel brand new, with friendly and attentive staff. The floating breakfast, evening lighting, and cave-style rooms have become the most memorable parts of their stay.

# ✍️ Our Take

[https://www.instagram.com/reel/DaMjzE7SGKz/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link&igsh=NTc4MTIwNjQ2YQ==](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DaMjzE7SGKz/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link&igsh=NTc4MTIwNjQ2YQ==)

In recent years, many new hotels have opened in Bali, but what truly leaves a lasting impression is often not how big the rooms are, but whether the property has its own unique character.

La Mewali's greatest success lies in not replicating the familiar tropical villa style, but instead crafting its own 'modern cave aesthetic.'

It doesn't literally carve rooms into caves, but uses flowing architecture, sculptural walls, natural light and shadow, and lagoon-like water features to create a quiet, relaxing, and design-driven space. What truly draws you in isn't any single amenity, but the overall atmosphere.

If you've been to Bali many times, stayed in plenty of villas, and are looking for a truly 'memorable' new hotel, I believe La Mewali is well worth adding to your next Canggu stay list.
