Most travelers who have visited Bali and fallen in love with it wish to delve deeper into the local lifestyle or embark on a non-touristy itinerary, but often don’t know where to start? The best place to spend a day experiencing the local rhythm is Denpasar, Bali’s inland capital. This article will take you on a deep dive into Bali’s local life, from shopping at traditional markets to strolling through beautiful riverside parks and exploring bustling night markets. Let’s step into this enchanting land and experience the most authentic Balinese charm!

## The Wholesale Art Market: Kumbasari Art Market

First, we head to the Kumbasari Art Market (pronounced: Koom-bah-sah-ree) in Denpasar, Bali’s capital. Search for "Pasar Seni Kumbasari" on Google Maps. This is Bali’s largest wholesale handicraft market, offering a wide variety of handmade goods. Unlike typical tourist markets, this place primarily caters to locals, so prices are very affordable. If you’re looking to buy handicrafts in bulk, this is definitely the best spot.

https://youtu.be/RRurkFC7UG4

This market not only boasts an array of handicrafts but also features many shops frequented by locals, allowing you to experience the most authentic Balinese shopping atmosphere. When visiting the market, don’t forget to bring enough cash, as many vendors don’t accept credit cards.

## Badung Market: Pasar Badung

(Contributed by fellow traveler Fran Cisco. [Original post here](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/d9Bh4epnNiYGMsy8/)) For those who love tasting local fruits, Pasar Badung (Badung Market) is a must-visit. This market is filled with vendors selling fresh fruits and other local produce. While the indoor area may feel crowded and chaotic, you can easily find a variety of delicious fruits in the outdoor section.

Group member Fran Cisco bought sweet mangosteens and juicy white durians here, along with snake fruit and salak, at very affordable prices.

Additionally, Fran Cisco highly recommends a handsome durian seller on the small road to the left of the market. The owner enthusiastically helps you pick the best fruits, making you feel warmly welcomed. He doesn't inflate prices for foreign tourists, is friendly, and the fruit quality is excellent. Even two weeks after returning to Taiwan, Fran Cisco still fondly remembers the delightful durian experience with this seller!

It's worth noting that both Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets have many middlemen aunties doing business. When they see you're not a local, they'll approach and guide you around, making you buy goods at higher prices from which they take a commission. To avoid being followed by these aunties, it's best to bring your hired driver along, who can help guard against and bargain for you.

## Korean River Park: Taman Tukad Korea

Next, the river separating Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets is now a riverside park named "Taman Tukad Korea," locally known as "Korean River Park" due to its design similarity to Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream Park. Once a smelly ditch, it has been transformed by the government into a beautiful riverside park.

https://youtu.be/gEcE2e4TFaM

During the day, Taman Tukad Korea offers pleasant scenery with shaded green trees along both banks and exquisite stone carvings. At night, colorful lights illuminate the entire park, creating a romantic atmosphere. The park also features a floating bridge and fountain, adding to its charm.

Besides the beautiful scenery, free Wi-Fi is available for visitors to easily share their experiences. After visiting the park, you can explore the nearby night market to taste various delicious snacks and experience Bali's nightlife.

## Bali's Largest Night Market: Pasar Kreneng

Finally, we arrived at Pasar Kreneng, Bali's largest night market, just a short drive from the riverside park. The stalls here offer a dazzling array of goods, from local delicacies to daily necessities. The vibrant atmosphere is truly captivating, and you can indulge in a variety of authentic Balinese street food, savoring the satisfaction that comes with every bite. For a detailed exploration of the culinary delights and a full night market experience, check out Xiao Jie's video:

https://youtu.be/FphhiUzB7gM

You can easily spend a day immersing yourself in this authentic Balinese experience, gaining a deeper appreciation for the island's charm. Whether it's the handicrafts at Kumbasari Art Market, the local fruits at Badung Market, the scenic beauty of Taman Tukad Korea, or the culinary delights at Kreneng Night Market, each spot is filled with surprises.
