Most travelers who have visited Bali and fallen in love with the island wish to delve deeper into its local lifestyle or embark on non-touristy itineraries but don’t know where to start? The best place to experience the rhythm of local life in a day is Denpasar, Bali’s inland capital. This article will take you on a deep dive into Bali’s local life, from shopping at traditional markets to exploring beautiful riverside parks and bustling night markets. Let’s step into this enchanting land and experience the most authentic Balinese charm!

## The Wholesale Art Market: Kumbasari Art Market

First, we head to the Kumbasari Art Market (pronounced: Koom-bah-sah-ree) in Denpasar, Bali’s capital. This is Bali’s largest wholesale handicraft market, offering a wide variety of handmade goods. Unlike typical tourist markets, this place primarily caters to locals, so prices are very affordable. If you’re looking to buy handicrafts in bulk, this is definitely the best spot.

This market not only boasts an array of handicrafts but also features many shops frequented by locals, allowing you to experience the most authentic Balinese shopping atmosphere. When visiting the market, don’t forget to carry enough cash, as many vendors don’t accept credit cards.

## Badung Market: Pasar Badung

(Shared by fellow traveler Fran Cisco. [Original post here](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/d9Bh4epnNiYGMsy8/)) For those who love sampling local fruits, Pasar Badung (Badung Market) is a must-visit. This bustling market is filled with vendors selling fresh fruits and other local produce. While the indoor area can be crowded and chaotic, the outdoor section offers a more relaxed experience where you can easily find a variety of delicious fruits.

Group member Fran Cisco bought sweet mangosteens and juicy white durians here, along with snake fruit and salak, at very affordable prices.

Additionally, Fran Cisco highly recommends a handsome durian seller on the small road to the left of the market. The owner enthusiastically helps you pick the best fruits, making you feel warmly welcomed. He doesn't inflate prices for foreign tourists, is friendly, and the fruit quality is excellent. Even two weeks after returning to Taiwan, Fran Cisco still fondly remembers the pleasant durian experience with this seller!

Note that both Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets have many middlemen aunties doing business. They will approach you if they see you're not a local, guiding you around and making you buy goods at higher prices while taking a commission. To avoid being followed by these aunties, it's best to bring your private driver along, who can help you negotiate and get better deals.

## Korea River Park: Taman Tukad Korea

Next, the river separating Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets is now called "Taman Tukad Korea," a riverside park locally known as "Korea River Park" due to its design similarities with Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream. Once a polluted ditch, it has been transformed by the government into a beautiful riverside park.

https://youtu.be/gEcE2e4TFaM

During the day, Taman Tukad Korea offers pleasant scenery with shady trees along both banks and exquisite stone carvings. At night, colorful lights illuminate the entire park, creating a romantic atmosphere. The park also features a floating bridge and fountain, adding to its charm.

Besides the beautiful scenery, free Wi-Fi is available for visitors to share their experiences instantly. After visiting the park, you can explore the nearby night market to taste various delicious snacks and experience Bali's nightlife.

## Bali's Largest Night Market: Pasar Kreneng

Finally, we arrived at Pasar Kreneng, Bali's largest night market, just a short drive from the riverside park. The stalls here are filled with a dazzling array of goods, offering everything from local delicacies to daily necessities. The lively atmosphere of the night market is truly captivating, and you can indulge in a variety of authentic local snacks, savoring the satisfaction that good food brings. For a detailed exploration of the culinary delights and a full account of the night market adventure, check out Xiao Jie's video:

https://youtu.be/FphhiUzB7gM

You can spend a day immersing yourself in this authentic Bali experience, allowing you to deeply appreciate the charm of this land. Whether it's the handicrafts at Kumbasari Art Market, the local fruits at Badung Market, the beautiful scenery at Taman Tukad Korea, or the delicious food at Kreneng Night Market, each spot is brimming with surprises.
