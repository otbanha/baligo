Most travelers who have been to Bali and fallen in love with it wish to delve deeper into the local life or embark on a non-touristy itinerary, but often don't know where to start? The best place to experience the rhythm of local life in just one day is Denpasar, the inland capital of Bali. This article will take you on an in-depth exploration of Bali's local life, from shopping experiences at local markets to beautiful riverside parks and bustling night markets. Let's step into this enchanting land together and feel the most authentic Balinese charm!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_17d0b4f41e1151132e8732154d87d812.jpg)

## Art Wholesale Market: Kumbasari Art Market

First, we head to the 'Kumbasari Art Market' (search 'Pasar Seni Kumbasari' on Google Maps) in Denpasar, Bali's capital. This is Bali's largest wholesale market for handicrafts, offering a wide variety of handmade goods. Unlike typical tourist markets, this place primarily serves locals, so prices are very reasonable. If you're looking to buy handicrafts in bulk, this is definitely the best spot.

## Badung Market: Pasar Badung

(Shared by fellow traveler Fran Cisco. [Original post here](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/d9Bh4epnNiYGMsy8/)) For those who love tasting local fruits, Pasar Badung (Badung Market) is a must-visit. This market is filled with various stalls selling fresh fruits and other local foods. While the interior may be crowded and chaotic, you can easily find a variety of delicious fruits in the outdoor area.

Fran Cisco and his group bought delicious mangosteens and creamy durians here, along with salak and duku, all at very affordable prices.

Additionally, Fran Cisco highly recommends a handsome durian vendor on the small path to the left of the market. The vendor enthusiastically helps you pick the best fruits, making you feel warmly welcomed. He doesn't inflate prices for foreign tourists, maintains a friendly attitude, and offers high-quality fruits. Even two weeks after returning to Taiwan, Fran Cisco and his group still fondly remember the delightful durian experience provided by this vendor!

It's important to note that both Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets have many middlemen aunties doing business. They approach you if they see you're not local, guiding you around and encouraging you to buy goods at higher prices from which they take a commission. To avoid being followed by these middlemen aunties, it's best to bring your hired driver along, as they can help guard against overcharging and negotiate prices.

## Korean River Park: Taman Tukad Korea

Next, the river separating Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets is now a riverside park named 'Taman Tukad Korea,' locally known as the 'Korean River Park' due to its design similarity to Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream. Originally a polluted ditch, it has been transformed by the government into a beautiful riverside park.

During the day, Taman Tukad Korea offers pleasant scenery with shady trees along the riverbanks and exquisite stone carvings. At night, colorful lights illuminate the entire park, creating a romantic atmosphere. The park also features a floating bridge and a fountain, adding to its charm.

Besides the beautiful scenery, the park provides free Wi-Fi for visitors to share their experiences. After visiting the park, you can explore the nearby night market to taste various delicious snacks and experience Bali's nightlife.

## Bali's Largest Night Market: Pasar Kreneng

Finally, we arrive at 'Pasar Kreneng,' Bali's largest night market, located about a few minutes' drive from the riverside park. The market is filled with stalls selling a variety of foods and daily necessities. The bustling atmosphere allows you to enjoy local delicacies and satisfy your culinary cravings.
