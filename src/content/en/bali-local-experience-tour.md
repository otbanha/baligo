Most travelers who have visited Bali and fallen in love with it hope to dive deeper into local life or take a non-touristy route, but often don't know where to start. The best place to experience the rhythm of local life in just one day is Denpasar, the inland capital of Bali. This article will take you on a deep dive into Balinese local life, from shopping at local markets to strolling through beautiful riverside parks and exploring lively night markets. Let's step into this enchanting land and feel the most authentic Bali vibes!

## Kumbasari Art Market: The Wholesale Art Market

First, we head to the Kumbasari Art Market (search on Google Maps: Pasar Seni Kumbasari) in Denpasar, the capital of Bali. This is the largest wholesale market for handicrafts in Bali, offering a wide variety of handmade items. Unlike typical tourist markets, this one primarily serves locals, so prices are very affordable. If you're looking to buy handicrafts in bulk, this is definitely the best place.

https://youtu.be/RRurkFC7UG4

This market not only boasts a dazzling array of handicrafts but also features many shops frequented by locals, allowing you to soak in the most authentic Balinese shopping atmosphere. When exploring the market, remember to bring enough cash, as many stalls don't accept credit cards.

## Pasar Badung: The Badung Market

(Contributed by fellow traveler Fran Cisco. [Original post here](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/d9Bh4epnNiYGMsy8/)) For those who love tasting local fruits, Pasar Badung is a must-visit. This market is filled with stalls selling fresh fruits and other local foods. While the interior might be a bit crowded and chaotic, you can easily find a variety of delicious fruits in the outdoor area.

![Fran Cisco提供:（價格可能隨季節而有不同） 山竹 IDR 10000 /1公斤 白榴蓮 IDR 15000/20000/30000顆 （圖片是20000的，果肉爆炸多！）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99114756823a709b41f05ead1f12f4ba.jpg)

Provided by Fran Cisco: (Prices may vary by season) Mangosteen IDR 10,000/kg, White Durian IDR 15,000/20,000/30,000 each (the one in the photo is IDR 20,000, with an explosion of flesh!)

Fellow traveler Fran Cisco and their group found sweet mangosteens and bursting white durians here, along with snake fruit, duku, and more—all at very affordable prices.

Additionally, Fran Cisco especially recommends a handsome durian seller on the small lane to the left of the market. The owner enthusiastically helps you pick the best fruit, making you feel genuine warmth and hospitality. He doesn't inflate prices just because you're a foreign tourist, is friendly, and the fruit quality is excellent. Even two weeks after returning to Taiwan, Fran Cisco and their group still fondly remember the delightful durian moments this seller gave them!

Note that both Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets have many middle-aged female touts doing business. When they see you're not a local, they'll swarm around and guide you through the market, making you buy items at higher prices while they take a commission. To avoid being followed by these touts, it's best to bring your private driver along—they can help you vet prices and bargain.

## Korea River Park: Taman Tukad Korea

Next, the river separating Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets is now a riverside park called 'Taman Tukad Korea.' Locals refer to it as 'Korea River Park' because its design resembles Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream park. Originally a smelly drainage ditch, it has been transformed by the government into a beautiful riverside park.

https://youtu.be/gEcE2e4TFaM

By day, Taman Tukad Korea offers pleasant scenery with tree-lined riverbanks and exquisite stone carvings. After nightfall, colorful lights illuminate the entire park, creating a romantic atmosphere. The park also features a floating bridge and fountain, making the view even more enchanting.

Beyond the beautiful scenery, there's free Wi-Fi for visitors to easily share their views. After exploring the park, you can also visit the night market next door to sample various delicious snacks and experience Bali's nightlife.

## Bali's Largest Night Market: Pasar Kreneng

Finally, we arrived at Pasar Kreneng, about a minute's drive from the riverside park. This is Bali's largest night market, with a dazzling array of stalls selling all kinds of food and daily necessities. The lively atmosphere is electric, and you can sample a variety of local specialties here, savoring the satisfaction that comes with great food. For a detailed food exploration and night market travelogue, check out Xiaojie's video:

https://youtu.be/FphhiUzB7gM

You can set aside a day for this kind of local Bali experience, which will give you a deeper appreciation of the island's charm. Whether it's the handicrafts at Kumbasari Art Market, the local fruits at Badung Market, the beautiful scenery of Taman Tukad Korea, or the delicious food at Kreneng Night Market, every spot is full of surprises.
