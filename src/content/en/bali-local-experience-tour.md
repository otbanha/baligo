Most travelers who have been to Bali and fallen in love with it wish to delve deeper into the local life or embark on a non-touristy itinerary, but often don't know where to start? The best place to spend a day experiencing the local rhythm is Denpasar, Bali's inland capital. This article will take you on a deep dive into Bali's local life, from shopping at local markets to beautiful riverside parks and bustling night markets. Let's step into this enchanting land and experience the most authentic Balinese charm!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_17d0b4f41e1151132e8732154d87d812.jpg)

## The Wholesale Art Market: Kumbasari Art Market

First, we head to the Kumbasari Art Market (pronounced: Koom-bah-sah-ree) in Denpasar, Bali's capital. This is Bali's largest wholesale handicraft market, offering a wide variety of handmade goods. Unlike typical tourist markets, this place primarily serves locals, so prices are very reasonable. If you're looking to buy handicrafts in bulk, this is definitely the best spot.

## Badung Market: Pasar Badung

(Contributed by fellow traveler Fran Cisco. [Original post here](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/d9Bh4epnNiYGMsy8/)) For those who love tasting local fruits, Pasar Badung (Badung Market) is a must-visit. This market is filled with vendors selling fresh fruits and other local foods. While the interior might be a bit crowded and chaotic, you can easily find a variety of delicious fruits in the outdoor area.

Fran Cisco shares: (Prices may vary by season) Mangosteen IDR 10,000 /1 kg White Durian IDR 15,000/20,000/30,000 per piece (The photo shows a 20,000 one, with an explosion of flesh!)

Fran Cisco and their group bought delicious mangosteens and creamy durians here, along with salak and duku, all at very reasonable prices.

Additionally, Fran Cisco highly recommends a handsome durian vendor on the small road to the left of the market. The vendor enthusiastically helps you pick the best fruits, making you feel warmly welcomed. He doesn’t inflate prices for foreign tourists, and his friendly attitude and high-quality fruits are truly commendable. Even two weeks after returning home, Fran Cisco still fondly remembers the delightful durian moments shared with this vendor!

It’s worth noting that both Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets have many middlewomen who approach non-locals, guiding them around and encouraging them to buy goods at higher prices while taking a commission. To avoid being followed by these middlewomen, it’s best to bring your private driver along, as they can help negotiate and ensure fair prices.

## Korea River Park: Taman Tukad Korea

Next, the river separating Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets is now a riverside park called Taman Tukad Korea, locally known as Korea River Park due to its design resembling Seoul’s Cheonggyecheon Stream. Once a polluted ditch, it has been transformed by the government into a beautiful riverside park.

During the day, Taman Tukad Korea offers a pleasant view with lush trees lining both banks and exquisite stone sculptures decorating the area. At night, colorful lights illuminate the park, creating a romantic atmosphere. The park also features a floating bridge and fountain, adding to its charm.

In addition to its scenic beauty, the park provides free Wi-Fi for visitors to share their experiences. After exploring the park, you can visit the nearby night market to sample various delicious snacks and experience Bali’s vibrant nightlife.

## Bali’s Largest Night Market: Pasar Kreneng

Finally, we arrive at Pasar Kreneng, Bali’s largest night market, just a short drive from the riverside park. The market is bustling with vendors offering a wide array of food and daily necessities. You can plan a day trip to experience Bali’s local charm deeply. Whether it’s the handicrafts at Kumbasari Art Market, the local fruits at Badung Market, the beautiful scenery of Taman Tukad Korea, or the delicious food at Kreneng Night Market, each spot is full of surprises.
