Most travelers who have visited Bali and fallen in love with the island wish to delve deeper into its local life or embark on a non-touristy itinerary but often don’t know where to start. The best place to spend a day experiencing the local rhythm is Denpasar, Bali’s inland capital. This article will take you on a deep dive into Bali’s local life, from shopping at traditional markets to exploring beautiful riverside parks and bustling night markets. Let’s step into this enchanting land and experience the most authentic Balinese charm!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_17d0b4f41e1151132e8732154d87d812.jpg)

## Art Wholesale Market: Kumbasari Art Market

First, we head to the Kumbasari Art Market (pronounced: Koom-bah-sah-ree) in Denpasar, Bali’s capital. This is Bali’s largest wholesale market for handicrafts, offering an incredible variety of handmade goods. Unlike typical tourist markets, this one primarily caters to locals, so prices are very affordable. If you’re looking to buy handicrafts in bulk, this is the place to go.

## Badung Market: Pasar Badung

(Shared by fellow traveler Fran Cisco. [Original post here](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/d9Bh4epnNiYGMsy8/)) For those who love sampling local fruits, Pasar Badung (Badung Market) is a must-visit. This bustling market is filled with vendors selling fresh fruits and other local produce. While the indoor area can be crowded and chaotic, the outdoor section offers a relaxed space to find delicious fruits.

Our travel companion Fran Cisco found sweet mangosteens and creamy durians here, along with salak and duku, all at very affordable prices.

Additionally, Fran Cisco highly recommends a handsome durian seller on the small path to the left of the market. The seller enthusiastically helps you pick the best fruits, making you feel warmly welcomed. He doesn't inflate prices for foreign tourists, maintains a friendly attitude, and offers high-quality fruits. Even two weeks after returning to Taiwan, Fran Cisco still fondly remembers the delightful durian moments provided by this seller!

It's important to note that both Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets have many middlemen aunties doing business. They approach you if they see you're not a local, guiding you around to buy goods at higher prices from which they take a commission. To avoid being followed by these middlemen, it's best to bring along your hired driver who can help guard against and negotiate prices.

## Korean River Park: Taman Tukad Korea

Next, the river separating Pasar Kumbasari and Pasar Badung markets is now a riverside park named 'Taman Tukad Korea,' locally known as the Korean River Park because its design resembles Seoul's Cheonggyecheon Stream Park. Originally a smelly ditch, it has been transformed by the government into a beautiful riverside park.

During the day, Taman Tukad Korea offers pleasant scenery with shady trees and exquisite stone decorations along both banks. At night, colorful lights illuminate the entire park, creating a romantic atmosphere. The park also features a floating bridge and a fountain, making the scenery even more enchanting.

Besides the beautiful views, the park offers free Wi-Fi for visitors to share their experiences instantly. After visiting the park, you can explore the nearby night market to taste various delicious snacks and experience Bali's nightlife.

## Bali's Largest Night Market: Pasar Kreneng

Finally, we arrive at 'Pasar Kreneng,' Bali's largest night market, just a few minutes' drive from the riverside park. The market is bustling with vendors selling a variety of foods and daily necessities. The lively atmosphere allows you to enjoy various local delicacies and the satisfaction they bring.
