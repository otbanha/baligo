## Must-Read Before You Go! Five Essential Tips for Family Travel in Bali

### 🌤️ Climate & Clothing

Located in southern Bali, Nusa Dua enjoys temperatures between 26–33°C year-round. The dry season (May to October) is the most comfortable time to visit, while the rainy season (November to April) occasionally brings brief afternoon showers, though they rarely disrupt plans.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127893271-e7cd6a46660c3237454c5f8185b741b5.jpg)

Clothing tips for kids: lightweight, breathable cotton clothes, sandals or waterproof slippers, and swimwear are essential. Sunscreen is a daily must—UV rays on the beach are stronger than you might think, and children's delicate skin requires extra care. Reapply every two to three hours.

### 💊 Health and Safety

Before departure, pack essential medications: fever reducers, stomach remedies, anti-itch creams, and band-aids. Mosquitoes in Bali are a real presence, so opt for child-friendly insect repellent and remember to apply it during outdoor activities in the evening.

For drinking water, always purchase bottled water, and even use it for brushing your teeth to avoid stomach discomfort. Large resorts in Nusa Dua generally maintain good hygiene standards, but be cautious about the source of ice when dining out.

If engaging in water activities, ensure that children have appropriately sized life jackets and verify whether the operator provides them.

### 🚗 Transportation and Getting Around

Compared to Kuta or Seminyak, Nusa Dua has more orderly traffic, but getting around Bali still largely relies on private car hires. It's advisable to book a car through your hotel or a trusted platform in advance, with daily costs ranging from 600,000–800,000 IDR (approximately USD$40–50). Drivers often double as guides, making it very convenient.

{{block:包車}}

When traveling with children, confirm whether a child safety seat is needed and arrange this with the driver or hotel beforehand.

### 🍽️ Dining Options

Nusa Dua boasts many high-end restaurants and hotel buffets, offering fresh ingredients and diverse choices, so there's no need to worry about finding suitable meals for children. When dining out, opt for busy establishments with high turnover rates to ensure food freshness.

Balinese cuisine tends to be spicy, so remember to request "No spicy, please" when ordering. Most restaurants are child-friendly, with some even offering special kids' menus.

{{block:美食}}

### 🙏 Cultural Etiquette

Bali is a highly spiritual place, where offerings and temples are ubiquitous. When visiting temples with children, please dress them in sarongs (most hotels provide these) and speak softly before entering sacred spaces to respect local religious customs.

If you occasionally see small offerings (Canang Sari) on the ground, avoid stepping on them and remind children not to touch them casually. These are part of the daily Hindu rituals of worship and are an important aspect of Balinese culture.

## Top 9 Family-Friendly Activities in Nusa Dua

### 1. 🐬 Splashing Around at Nusa Dua Beach

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127983449-f5518c75b96f9370968adbb0ae27c709.jpg)

The calm waves here make it the perfect spot for young children to play in the water. The golden sand is clean and tidy, the sea is crystal clear, and the shallow waters ensure kids can safely enjoy themselves. Along the beach, various water activity stalls offer rentals of water toys, and simply playing in the sand can keep children entertained all afternoon.

### 2. 🐢 Turtle Island × Glass-Bottom Boat × Mangrove Cruise Combo Experience

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128003311-_2026-04-02_16.11.32.png)

This itinerary is, in my opinion, the best value-for-money family activity in Nusa Dua, packing three experiences into one trip that will leave your kids thoroughly satisfied.

Departing from Tanjung Benoa, start by gliding over the clear waters in a glass-bottom boat, where you can see coral reefs and tropical fish swimming below without getting wet. Then, venture into the mangrove waterways, slowly navigating through the dense mangroves as if entering another world. Finally, arrive at Turtle Island, the highlight for kids—here, they can feed turtles up close, observe turtles of various sizes, and even hold baby turtles for photos. The island also features iguanas, various bird species, and other animals, making it a mini ecological education park.

The tour includes hotel transfers, making it suitable for children who are not familiar with water and require no prior aquatic experience. You can conveniently book through Klook for a secure and hassle-free experience.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128134418-snorkeling.webp)

The coral ecosystem off the coast of Nusa Dua is rich and vibrant, making it an ideal spot for children aged 8 and above to try introductory snorkeling courses. ### 4. 🏄 Banana Boat & Jet Ski

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128198938-water_sports.jpg)

Older children (recommended for ages 8 and up) can try the banana boat, where they sit together and are pulled at high speeds by a speedboat, occasionally flipping into the water—laughter and screams abound, capturing the essence of Bali's classic vacation vibe. Jet skiing is more suitable for teenagers, and it’s safe for parents to ride along with their kids.

### 5. 🌊 Bali Waterbom Park

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128232527-qp1.png)

Located about a 30-minute drive from Nusa Dua, Waterbom Bali is one of the highest-rated water parks in Asia. It features water slides suitable for all ages, from dedicated areas for toddlers to thrilling slides that will leave adults breathless. You can easily spend an entire day here. It’s recommended to visit on weekdays to avoid crowds, and don’t forget to bring sunscreen and a change of clothes.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128295472-6a746e42f4cc324c672b9ade957e37ce.jpg)

## Final Thoughts

For visitors, Nusa Dua is the kind of place where you can truly let go and relax. It’s not as bustling as Kuta or as remote as Ubud—it strikes the perfect balance of being vibrant yet not overwhelming.

Traveling with kids often comes with two worries: the kids not having enough fun and the parents not getting enough rest. Nusa Dua solves both. Kids can run on the beach, play in the pools, and marvel at cultural experiences, while parents can lounge on sunbeds, sip a Bintang beer, and finally catch up on that book they’ve been meaning to read for months.

This is what travelers envision as the perfect family vacation in Bali.
