## Must-Read Before You Go! Five Essential Tips for Family Travel in Bali

### 🌤️ Climate & Clothing

Located in southern Bali, Nusa Dua enjoys temperatures ranging from 26–33°C year-round. The dry season (May to October) is the most comfortable time to visit, while the rainy season (November to April) may bring occasional afternoon showers, though they rarely disrupt plans.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127893271-e7cd6a46660c3237454c5f8185b741b5.jpg)

Clothing tips for kids: lightweight, breathable cotton clothes, sandals or waterproof slippers, and swimsuits are a must. Sunscreen is essential daily—the UV rays at the beach are stronger than you might think, and children’s delicate skin requires extra care. Reapply sunscreen every two to three hours.

### 💊 Health and Safety

Before departure, pack essential medications: fever reducers, stomach medicine, anti-itch cream, and band-aids. Mosquitoes in Bali are very real, so choose a child-friendly insect repellent and remember to apply it during outdoor activities in the evening.

For drinking water, always purchase bottled water. Even for brushing your teeth and rinsing, use mineral water to avoid stomach discomfort. Large resorts in Nusa Dua generally have excellent hygiene standards, but be cautious about the source of ice when dining out.

If engaging in water activities, make sure to prepare properly sized life jackets for children and confirm whether the operator provides them.

### 🚗 Transportation and Getting Around

Compared to Kuta or Seminyak, the Nusa Dua area has more orderly traffic, but getting around Bali still largely relies on private car hires. It’s recommended to book a car in advance through your hotel or a trusted platform. The daily cost is around 600,000–800,000 IDR (approximately USD$40–50), and drivers often double as guides, making it very convenient.

{{block:包車}}

When traveling with children, confirm whether a child safety seat is needed and arrange this in advance with the driver or hotel.

### 🍽️ Dining Options

Nusa Dua offers many high-end restaurants and hotel buffets with fresh ingredients and diverse options, so there’s no need to worry about finding food for kids. When dining out, choose busy restaurants with high turnover rates to ensure food freshness.

Balinese cuisine is generally spicy, so remember to specify "No spicy, please" when ordering. Most restaurants are child-friendly, and some even offer kids' menus.

{{block:美食}}

### 🙏 Cultural Etiquette

Bali is a highly religious place, with offerings and temples visible everywhere. When visiting temples with children, please help them wear a sarong (most hotels provide them), and speak softly before entering sacred spaces to respect local religious customs.

Occasionally, you may see small offerings (Canang Sari) on the ground. Avoid stepping on them, and remind children not to touch them casually. These are daily Hindu rituals for offering to the gods and are an important part of Balinese culture.

## Top 9 Family-Friendly Activities in Nusa Dua

### 1. 🐬 Play in the Waters of Nusa Dua Beach

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127983449-f5518c75b96f9370968adbb0ae27c709.jpg)

The waves here are very calm, making it the top choice for young children to play in the water. The golden sand is clean and tidy, the sea water is clear, and the shallow areas allow children to safely enjoy the water. Along the beach, there are various water activity stalls where you can rent water toys. Just playing in the sand by the shore can keep children entertained all afternoon.

### 2. 🐢 Turtle Island × Glass-Bottom Boat × Mangrove Boat Ride Triple Experience

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128003311-_2026-04-02_16.11.32.png)

This itinerary is, in my opinion, the best value-for-money family activity in Nusa Dua, packing three experiences into one, leaving children thoroughly satisfied.

Departing from Tanjung Benoa, first take a glass-bottom boat ride across the clear sea. Through the glass bottom, you can see coral reefs and tropical fish swimming below, all without getting wet. Then, enter the mangrove waterways, slowly navigating through the dense mangroves, feeling as if you've entered another world. Finally, arrive at Turtle Conservation Island, the highlight for children—they can feed turtles by hand, observe turtles of various sizes up close, and even hold baby turtles for photos. The conservation island also has iguanas, various birds, and other animals, making it a small ecological education park.

The tour includes hotel transfers and is suitable for children with no swimming experience or water skills. You can book conveniently and securely through Klook.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128134418-snorkeling.webp)

The coral reefs near Nusa Dua are rich in marine life, making it a great spot for kids aged 8 and above to try beginner snorkeling lessons. Many water sports centers offer snorkeling classes for children, with professional instructors ensuring safety. Watching colorful tropical fish swim by is a memory your child will cherish forever.

### 4. 🏄 Banana Boat & Jet Ski

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128198938-water_sports.jpg)

Older kids (recommended for ages 8 and up) can try the banana boat, where they sit together and are pulled by a speedboat at high speeds, occasionally flipping into the water—laughter and screams guaranteed! Jet skiing is perfect for teenagers, and it’s safe for parents to ride along with their kids.

### 5. 🌊 Bali Waterbom Park

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128232527-qp1.png)

Located about a 30-minute drive from Nusa Dua, Waterbom Bali is one of the highest-rated water parks in Asia. It features slides for all ages, from toddler-friendly zones to adrenaline-pumping rides for adults. You can easily spend an entire day here. Visit on weekdays to avoid crowds, and don’t forget sunscreen and a change of clothes.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128295472-6a746e42f4cc324c672b9ade957e37ce.jpg)

Located on the Bukit Peninsula, GWK Cultural Park features a massive statue of Vishnu that will leave children awestruck. The park regularly hosts traditional dance performances and Balinese music shows, making it an excellent place for kids to learn about Hindu culture. Visiting in the evening offers a stunning sunset view with the giant golden statue as a backdrop, creating a breathtaking scene.

Let your kids experience Batik making or traditional mask painting - it's not just a trip, but a living cultural lesson. Upscale resorts in Nusa Dua often organize craft activities, or you can arrange transportation to Ubud's art studios for more in-depth workshops. The artwork your child brings home will be the most meaningful travel souvenir.

## Final Thoughts

For visitors, Nusa Dua is the kind of place where you can truly let your guard down. It’s not as bustling and noisy as Kuta, nor as remote as Ubud—it strikes the perfect balance of being vibrant without being overwhelming.

Traveling with kids often comes with two big worries: the kids not having enough fun, and the parents not getting enough rest. Nusa Dua solves both problems at once. Kids can run on the beach, splash in the pool, and marvel at cultural experiences, while parents can lounge on a sunbed, sip a Bintang beer, and finally dive into that book they’ve been meaning to read for months.

This is the picture-perfect Bali family vacation that travelers dream of.
