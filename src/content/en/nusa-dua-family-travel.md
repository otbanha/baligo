## Must-Read Before You Go! Five Key Considerations for Family Travel in Bali

### 🌤️ Climate & Clothing

Located in southern Bali, Nusa Dua experiences temperatures between 26–33°C year-round. The dry season (May to October) is the most comfortable time to visit. During the rainy season (November to April), there may be occasional afternoon showers, but they usually don't affect travel plans.

![Climate & Clothing](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127893271-e7cd6a46660c3237454c5f8185b741b5.jpg)

For children's clothing, opt for lightweight and breathable cotton outfits, sandals or waterproof slippers, and of course, don't forget swimwear. Sunscreen is a daily essential, as the UV rays at the beach are stronger than you might think. Children's delicate skin requires extra care, so it's recommended to reapply sunscreen every two to three hours.

### 💊 Health and Safety

Before departure, it's advisable to pack essential medications: fever reducers, stomach remedies, anti-itch ointments, and band-aids. Mosquitoes in Bali are a real presence, so choose a child-friendly insect repellent and remember to apply it during outdoor activities in the evening.

For drinking water, purchase bottled water, and even use mineral water for brushing your teeth to avoid stomach discomfort. Large resorts in Nusa Dua generally have excellent hygiene standards, but be cautious about the source of ice when dining out.

If engaging in water activities, ensure that children have appropriately sized life jackets and confirm whether the operator provides them.

### 🚗 Transportation and Getting Around

Compared to Kuta or Seminyak, the Nusa Dua area has more orderly traffic, but getting around Bali still largely relies on private car hires. It's recommended to book a car through your hotel or a trusted platform in advance, with daily costs ranging from 600,000–800,000 IDR (approximately USD$40–50). Drivers often double as guides, making it very convenient.

{{block:包車}}

When traveling with children, remember to confirm if a child safety seat is needed and arrange this with the driver or hotel beforehand.

### 🍽️ Dining Options

Nusa Dua boasts many high-end restaurants and hotel buffets, offering fresh ingredients and a wide variety of choices, so there's no need to worry about finding something for your child to eat. When dining out, opt for busy establishments with high turnover rates to ensure the freshness of the food.

Balinese cuisine is generally quite spicy, so remember to specifically request "No spicy, please" when ordering. Most restaurants are child-friendly, with some even offering kids' menus.

{{block:美食}}

### 🙏 Cultural Etiquette

Bali is a highly religious place, with offerings and temples visible everywhere. When visiting temples with children, please dress them in sarongs (most hotels provide these) and speak softly before entering sacred spaces to respect local religious customs.

If you occasionally see small offerings (Canang Sari) on the ground, avoid stepping on them and remind children not to touch them casually. These are part of the daily Hindu rituals of offering to the gods and are an important aspect of Balinese culture.

## Top 10 Family-Friendly Activities in Nusa Dua

### 1. 🐬 Play in the Waters of Nusa Dua Beach

![1. Play in the Waters of Nusa Dua Beach](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127983449-f5518c75b96f9370968adbb0ae27c709.jpg)

The waves here are very calm, making it the top choice for young children to play in the water. The golden sand is clean and tidy, the sea water is clear, and the shallow areas allow children to safely enjoy the water. Along the beach, there are various water activity stalls where you can rent different water toys. Just playing in the sand by the shore can keep children happy for an entire afternoon.

### 2. 🐢 Turtle Island × Glass-Bottom Boat × Mangrove Boat Tour Triple Experience

![2. Turtle Island × Glass-Bottom Boat × Mangrove Boat Tour Tr](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128003311-_2026-04-02_16.11.32.png)

This itinerary is hands down the best value-for-money family activity in Nusa Dua, packing three experiences into one trip that will leave your kids thoroughly satisfied.

Departing from Tanjung Benoa, you'll first glide across the crystal-clear waters on a glass-bottom boat, where you can admire the coral reefs and tropical fish swimming below without getting wet. Then, venture into the mangrove waterways, slowly navigating through the dense mangroves, feeling as if you've entered another world. Finally, arrive at Turtle Island, the highlight for kids — they can feed turtles up close, observe turtles of various sizes, and even hold baby turtles for photos. The conservation island also features iguanas, various bird species, and other animals, making it a mini ecological education park.

The entire itinerary includes hotel transfers, making it suitable for children with no swimming experience and requiring no prior water activities knowledge. You can book through Klook for convenience and peace of mind.

### 10. Bali Exotic Marine Park｜Dive into the Blue, Dance with Dolphins in a Dreamy Underwater World

![](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779966642880-mixcollage-28-may-2026-06-10-pm-4374.jpg)

If there's one experience that stands out in family travel to Bali, it's the dolphin interaction at Bali Exotic Marine Park. Located along the southeastern coast in Tanjung Benoa, this marine park is renowned for its **up-close dolphin encounters**, allowing visitors to don swimsuits and dive in to swim alongside these graceful marine creatures in the crystal-clear waters.

**Swim with Dolphins** is the park's flagship experience: trained dolphins will swim close, letting you gently touch their smooth skin, and even, with the guidance of trainers, experience the thrill of being pushed forward by dolphins. The moment children come face-to-face with dolphins in the water often becomes the brightest memory of their entire Bali trip.

Beyond dolphin interactions, the park also features sea lion shows, underwater observation pools, and various marine ecology educational displays, deepening families' respect and care for marine life amidst laughter.

**Travel Tip:** Dolphin interaction spots are limited, so it's highly recommended to book in advance through online platforms. Some interaction programs have age and height restrictions, so please confirm before booking.

## Final Thoughts

For visitors, Nusa Dua is the kind of place where you can truly let your guard down. It's not as bustling and noisy as Kuta, nor as remote as Ubud — it strikes that perfect balance of being vibrant without being overwhelming.

When traveling with kids, there are two main concerns: the kids not having enough fun, and the parents not getting enough rest. Nusa Dua solves both problems at once. Kids can run on the beach, play in the pool, and marvel at cultural experiences, while parents can lounge on deck chairs, sip a Bintang beer, and finally catch up on that book they've been meaning to read for six months.

This is what travelers envision as the perfect family vacation in Bali.
