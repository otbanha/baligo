## Must-Read Before You Go! 5 Key Tips for Family Travel in Bali

### 🌤️ Climate and What to Wear

Nusa Dua is located in southern Bali, with year-round temperatures ranging from 26–33°C. The dry season (May to October) is the most comfortable time to visit, while the rainy season (November to April) brings occasional brief afternoon showers that usually don't disrupt plans.

![氣候與穿著](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127893271-e7cd6a46660c3237454c5f8185b741b5.jpg)

Clothing tips for kids: Light, breathable cotton clothes, sandals or waterproof slippers, and swimsuits are a must. Sunscreen is an everyday essential—the UV rays on the beach are much stronger than you'd think, and children's delicate skin needs extra care. Reapply every two to three hours.

### 💊 Health and Safety

Before you go, it's a good idea to pack some basic medications: fever reducers, stomach medicine, anti-itch cream, and band-aids. Mosquitoes are a real thing in Bali, so bring kid-friendly mosquito repellent and remember to spray it on when you're outdoors in the late afternoon.

For drinking water, buy bottled water—even use it for brushing teeth and rinsing your mouth to avoid stomach issues. Large resorts in Nusa Dua generally have excellent hygiene standards, but when eating out, be mindful of the ice source.

If you plan any water activities, make sure to get a properly fitting life jacket for your child and confirm that the operator provides one.

### 🚗 Transportation and Getting Around

Compared to Kuta or Seminyak, the Nusa Dua area has more orderly traffic, but getting around Bali still largely relies on hiring a private driver. It's best to book a car in advance through your hotel or a trusted platform; the daily rate is around 600,000–800,000 IDR (about USD$19–26), and drivers often double as guides, which is very convenient.

{{block:包車}}

When traveling with kids, remember to check if a child safety seat is needed and arrange it with the driver or hotel in advance.

### 🍽️ Dining Options

Nusa Dua has many upscale restaurants and hotel buffets with fresh ingredients and plenty of choices, so you won't have to worry about your kids finding something to eat. When eating out, choose busy places with high table turnover—they tend to have fresher food.

Balinese cuisine is generally quite spicy, so be sure to specify 'No spicy, please' when ordering. Most restaurants are kid-friendly, and some even offer children's menus.

{{block:美食}}

### 🙏 Cultural Etiquette

Bali is a deeply religious place, with offerings and temples随处可见. When visiting temples with children, help them wear a sarong (most hotels provide them), speak softly before entering sacred spaces, and respect local religious customs.

If you see small offerings (Canang Sari) on the ground, don't step on them, and remind children not to touch them. These are part of daily Hindu rituals and an important aspect of Balinese culture.

## Top 10 Family Activities in Nusa Dua

### 1. 🐬 Playing in the Waters at Nusa Dua Beach

![1. 努沙杜瓦海灘戲水](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127983449-f5518c75b96f9370968adbb0ae27c709.jpg)

The waves here are very calm, making it the top choice for playing in the water with young children. The golden sand is clean and tidy, the seawater is clear, and the shallow areas allow kids to play safely. Along the beach, there are stalls offering various water toys for rent, and just playing in the sand can keep children happy all afternoon.

### 2. 🐢 Turtle Island × Glass-Bottom Boat × Mangrove Cruise Combo Experience

![2. 海龜島 × 玻璃底船 × 紅樹林遊船三合一體驗](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128003311-_2026-04-02_16.11.32.png)

I think this is the best value-for-money family activity in Nusa Dua, packing three experiences into one trip that kids absolutely love.

Starting from Tanjung Benoa, you first board a glass-bottom boat gliding over the clear sea. Through the glass bottom, you can see coral reefs and tropical fish swimming below without getting wet. Then, you enter the mangrove waterways, slowly weaving through dense mangroves, feeling like you've entered another world. Finally, you arrive at Turtle Island, a conservation area where kids go wild—they can hand-feed turtles, observe turtles of all sizes up close, and even hold baby turtles for photos. The conservation island also has iguanas, various birds, and other animals, making it a small ecological education park.

The entire itinerary includes hotel transfers, making it perfect for children who can't swim at all—no prior water experience needed. You can book through Klook for convenience and peace of mind.

### 10. Bali Exotic Marine Park｜Dive into the Blue, a Dreamy Underwater World Dancing with Dolphins

![](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779966642880-mixcollage-28-may-2026-06-10-pm-4374.jpg)

If there's one experience in Bali that truly touches the heart during a family trip, it's the dolphin interaction at Bali Exotic Marine Park. Located in Tanjung Benoa on the southeastern coast, this marine park is renowned for its **ultra-close dolphin encounters**, allowing visitors to put on swimsuits, get into the water, and swim alongside these graceful ocean spirits in the same crystal-blue sea.

**Swim with Dolphins** is the park's flagship experience: well-trained dolphins swim right up to you, letting you touch their smooth skin, and under the trainer's guidance, you can even feel the exhilarating speed of being pushed through the water by a dolphin. The moment children come face-to-face with a dolphin in the water is often the brightest highlight of their entire Bali trip.

Beyond dolphin interactions, the park also features sea lion shows, underwater observation pools, and various marine ecology educational exhibits, allowing families to deepen their respect and love for ocean life amidst the fun.

**Travel Tip:** Dolphin interaction slots are limited, so it's highly recommended to book in advance through online platforms. Some activities have age and height restrictions, so please confirm before booking.

## Final Thoughts

For travelers, Nusa Dua is the kind of place where you can truly let your heart rest. It’s not as lively and noisy as Kuta, nor as remote as Ubud—it strikes the perfect balance of being rich in experiences without being exhausting.

When traveling with kids, there are two main worries: the kids not having enough fun, and the parents not getting enough rest. Nusa Dua solves both problems at once. The kids run along the beach, splash in the pool, and marvel at cultural experiences, while the parents lounge on deck chairs, sip a cold Bintang beer, and finally crack open that book they’ve been meaning to read for six months.

This is the ultimate vision of a family trip to Bali for any traveler.
