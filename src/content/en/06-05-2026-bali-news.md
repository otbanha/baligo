### 📄 New Travel Routes and Safety Guidelines

![raw image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6727b9f6f57b7ae2c320641b63c0569c.jpg)

- **2026 Travel Trends: Slow Travel and the Rise of the East**: This year, tourists are increasingly avoiding crowded areas and turning towards more culturally rich, in-depth itineraries. The popularity of eastern destinations such as **Amed (Black Sand Beach)**, **Sidemen (known as the early Ubud)**, and **Penglipuran Village** has significantly risen, making them ideal for travelers seeking tranquility and natural landscapes.

- **Water Taxi Schedule**: The highly anticipated "Airport-Canggu-Uluwatu" water taxi project is currently scheduled to commence in **August 2026**, with construction expected to continue until 2027. This project aims to bypass severe ground traffic congestion by significantly reducing travel time via sea routes.
- **Health and Safety Double Alert**:
    - **Food Safety**: Authorities have once again reminded travelers to be cautious of the risk of food and drinks being spiked. Do not leave beverages unattended or accept drinks from strangers.
    - **Vaccine Recommendation**: If planning to stay in rural areas for more than a month, consult a doctor about whether to get vaccinated against **Japanese Encephalitis**.

### 🗞️ Law Enforcement Storm and Garbage Issues

- **"Operation Dharma Dewata" Concludes**: The Bali Immigration Office announced the completion of a large-scale patrol from April 15 to May 4, detaining **62 foreigners**. This operation aimed to maintain tourism order, uncovering cases of illegal overstays and activities inconsistent with visa purposes (such as online illegal services).
- **Hotel Industry Unites Against Garbage**: In response to the impending closure of the Suwung Landfill, several top resorts have recently launched a joint initiative to actively assist in waste sorting and disposal in popular tourist areas, reinforcing Bali's image as a sustainable tourism destination.
