### 📄 New Travel Routes and Safety Guidelines

![raw image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6727b9f6f57b7ae2c320641b63c0569c.jpg)

- **2026 Travel Trends: Slow Travel and the Rise of the East**: This year, tourists are increasingly avoiding crowded areas and opting for more culturally immersive experiences. The popularity of eastern destinations like **Amed (Black Sand Beach)**, **Sidemen (known as the early Ubud)**, and **Penglipuran Village** has significantly risen, making them ideal for travelers seeking tranquility and natural landscapes.

- **Water Taxi Schedule**: The highly anticipated water taxi project connecting the airport, Canggu, and Uluwatu is set to commence in **August 2026**, with completion expected by 2027. This initiative aims to bypass severe land traffic congestion by significantly reducing travel time via sea routes.
- **Health and Safety Alerts**:
    - **Food Safety**: Authorities have reiterated the risk of food and drink tampering, advising travelers to never leave their beverages unattended or accept drinks from strangers.
    - **Vaccination Advice**: For those planning to stay in rural areas for over a month, consulting a doctor about the **Japanese Encephalitis** vaccine is recommended.

### 🗞️ Law Enforcement and Waste Management

- **Conclusion of Operation Dharma Dewata**: Bali's immigration office announced the completion of a large-scale patrol from 4/15 to 5/4, resulting in the detention of **62 foreigners**. The operation aimed to maintain tourism order, uncovering cases of illegal overstays and activities inconsistent with visa purposes (such as unauthorized online services).
- **Hotel Industry Unites Against Waste**: In response to the impending closure of the Suwung landfill, several top resorts have recently launched a joint initiative to actively assist in waste sorting and management in popular tourist areas, reinforcing Bali's image as a sustainable tourism destination.
