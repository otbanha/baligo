When it comes to Canggu, most people first think of surfing, cafes, and beach clubs. It has always been a haven for backpackers and digital nomads, with relatively few high-end accommodation options.

But in November 2025, things quietly changed.

Regent, the top-tier brand under IHG (InterContinental Hotels Group), opened Indonesia's first Regent Hotel along the Batu Bolong coastline. This isn't just another five-star hotel in Canggu—it's an upgrade for the entire area's accommodation standards.

***

### 📍 Location & Surroundings

Regent Bali Canggu is located at **Jl. Pantai Batu Bolong No. 93**, directly facing the black sand coastline.

While the address says Canggu, more precisely, it’s situated between Batu Bolong and Echo Beach. This stretch is the most atmospheric part of Canggu—not the overcrowded touristy kind, but a relaxed vibe dotted with cafes, boutiques, and beach clubs.

Agoda’s location rating is a solid **9.5/10**, which speaks volumes.

Within walking distance, you'll find Echo Beach (a popular surf spot) and Batu Bolong Beach. Heading north, you can explore the quieter coffee culture area of Pererenan. The surrounding area is packed with renowned restaurants and beach clubs, so you don't need to go far to enjoy great food.

The only thing to note is that driving here from the airport during afternoon traffic can take **over two hours**. One guest reported leaving the airport at 6 PM and taking a full 150 minutes to arrive. This isn't a hotel issue but rather a reality of Canggu's location.

### 🏨 Facilities Overview

The entire resort was designed by **WATG Architects**, with interiors by **Hirsch Bedner Associates (HBA)**. The blend of stone, teak, and marble, combined with Bali's traditional roof styles and modern geometric elements, avoids tacky tropical luxury and instead exudes restrained elegance. Every room comes equipped with a teak bathtub!

The **pool area** is one of the most frequently mentioned highlights by guests. It's expansive, featuring several large pools and private cabanas for relaxation. One guest described it as 'a serene oasis in the bustling heart of Canggu,' a description I find spot-on.

**Regent Spa** is the first globally under the Regent brand—no marketing spin, it's truly a world-first. With 7 treatment rooms (including a couples' suite), sauna, cold plunge pool, treatments blending traditional Balinese herbal therapies with modern skincare technology, plus yoga classes and a 24-hour gym.

**Dining options** are another major highlight of this hotel. There are **6 dining venues**, two of which are helmed by Michelin-starred chef Andrew Walsh:

- **Sazón**: Mediterranean-inspired, focusing on grilled seafood, slow-cooked meats, and flatbreads, with a shared menu concept
- **Cure Bali** (opening early 2026): An extension of Walsh's Singapore flagship, Cure, combining Asian ingredients with European techniques
- **Taru**: Authentic Indonesian cuisine, featuring rendang sapi, grilled prawns with pete beans, and East Javanese soto ayam
- **Beach House**: A beachfront bar perfect for cocktails and sunbathing
- **Pool Bar** and **The Lounge**: Snacks and coffee by the pool for ultimate relaxation

### 👥 Ideal For

**Honeymooners** are undoubtedly the perfect fit here, with private pool villas, Regent Spa, and a Michelin-starred chef restaurant—this combination hits all the right notes. **Travelers who want to stay in a top-tier property in Canggu without venturing to Uluwatu** will also find this spot ideal, thanks to its great location and strong sense of local life—it’s not one of those isolated resorts. For surf enthusiasts looking for a bit of luxury, you can walk straight to Echo Beach for a surf session and return to high-end accommodations in the evening.

***

### 💬 Guest Reviews

One guest who stayed in October 2025 described the pool area as unexpectedly expansive, with several large independent pools and direct beach access, creating a vibe that felt like "a hidden oasis in the bustling Canggu."

Another traveler shared that breakfast was mostly à la carte, with different signature dishes rotating daily. The coffee quality was excellent, and the overall experience felt closer to "a high-end boutique hotel" rather than a standard chain hotel.

It’s worth noting that seasoned travelers pointed out that compared to other top-tier options in Bali at the same price point (like Four Seasons Jimbaran or Six Senses Uluwatu), the entry-level rooms here offer relatively lower value for money, as those places include private pool villas within the same budget. This is an honest reminder, especially if your budget falls in the $400–$600 USD/night range—be sure to compare room types carefully before deciding.
