Thanks to group member Yang Mei for sharing photos of her bed full of spoils 🎁

Every time returning from Bali to Taiwan, my suitcase is always packed with various souvenirs - potato chips, chili sauce, dried squid, and a bunch of local snacks, each one hard to miss!

But **can these spoils be smoothly brought back to Taiwan?** Recently, group member [Yang Mei](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/12GG3morMXe/) asked this very practical question, along with sharing her spectacular post-shopping photos from Bali.

This article will help us understand Taiwan Customs' regulations together, avoiding accidental mistakes, so we can shop happily when departing and pass through customs with peace of mind when returning!

***

## Items That Can Be Brought Back to Taiwan ✅

From Yang Mei's photos, most products fall into the following categories, **which comply with Taiwan Customs' allowed items**:

- ✅**Cookies, potato chips, pineapple cakes** (like Kusuka, GuriBee)
- ✅**Coconut chips**
- ✅**Instant snack noodles** (Enak, Mie Sedaap, **pay attention to ingredients**)
- ✅**Dried seafood products** (like JUHU dried squid)
- ✅**Bottled sauces** (like chili sauce)
- ✅**Toothpaste, skincare products, hair oils** (like Sensodyne toothpaste, Aloe Vera gel)
- ✅**Nuts, candies, chocolates**

As long as the products are **factory-sealed, clearly labeled**, and in quantities for 'personal use', they can usually be smoothly brought into Taiwan.

***

## Special Notice: Pork Products 🚫

**Important Reminder! Taiwan has very strict regulations** prohibiting pork-containing products, including but not limited to:

- 🚫 Pork jerky, pork floss, pork crackers, fried pork skin
- 🚫 Foods labeled with Pork, Lard, or Pork Extract
- 🚫 Instant noodles or snacks containing pork broth or meat particles

**If caught, the minimum fine is USD$6,450!** (And the penalty increases to USD$32,250 for repeat offenses)

https://youtu.be/4EzO1rpSMic?si=YKEu2SMJVTzSzwjO

(**The last three pork-related items mentioned by Xiao Jie cannot be brought back!**)

So, for snacks like Enak or Mie Sedaap, remember to carefully check the ingredients. 👉 **If you see words like 'Pork/babi' (the Indonesian word for pork), it's best to finish them in Bali and not bring them back to Taiwan.**

A small tip: After returning to your hotel, take out the instant noodle packaging and carefully inspect the ingredients. If in doubt, don’t risk bringing it.

***

## Can I Bring Dried Squid? 🐙

Yang Mei also specifically asked: 'Can I bring JUHU, which is processed and dried squid slices?'

✅ The answer is: Yes!

Here's why:

- Processed squid is considered **dried seafood**, not a meat product.
- The packaging is intact with clear ingredient labeling.
- It falls under **commercially processed goods**, not fresh produce.

As long as the quantity is reasonable, you can go through the regular customs channel when entering Taiwan without special declaration.

**Reminder**: If you're carrying a large amount (more than 20 boxes) that appears to be for commercial use, you may be subject to inspection. It's recommended to keep quantities reasonable.

***

## Tips for Bringing Bali Souvenirs Back to Taiwan ✈️

Here are some tips to make your return to Taiwan smoother:

1. **Group dried goods together for easier customs inspection.**
2. **Avoid bringing back any products containing meat ingredients.**
3. **When in doubt, declare it proactively.**
