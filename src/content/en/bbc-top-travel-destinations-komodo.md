Around this time each year, travel media outlets start rolling out their 'must-visit lists.' This time, BBC's 2026 edition, '[The 20 best places to travel in 2026](https://www.bbc.com/travel/article/20251209-the-20-best-places-to-travel-in-2026),' features a striking image of Komodo Island on its main visual—it instantly caught my attention. Having spent a long time in Bali, seeing a nearby destination recognized by international media always makes me take a closer look.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784430073193-suza_lelic_496255714_18501143596054015_1825470138392597452_n.jpg)

## Komodo National Park Turns 45

The list notes that Komodo National Park will celebrate its 45th anniversary in 2026, with a new wave of conservation programs set to launch. A more practical change is that getting there has become easier—Labuan Bajo now has direct flights from Singapore and Kuala Lumpur, so you no longer have to navigate complicated routes like before.

In recent years, many people have been combining Bali and the Komodo Islands into a single trip. One offers island resort vacations and cultural experiences, while the other features wild Komodo dragons and pink sand beaches—two completely different vibes that surprisingly complement each other. Infrastructure has indeed been improving, with airports, ports, and accommodations far better than a few years ago.

## Other Places on the List

Beyond Komodo, this list spans five continents, with each destination chosen for distinct reasons—it's not just a random collection of popular spots.

In Asia and the Middle East, Abu Dhabi is emerging as a cultural hub in the region with several new museums in the Saadiyat Cultural District. Japan's Ishikawa Prefecture hopes visitors will return to explore the Noto Peninsula, boosting post-earthquake reconstruction through tourism, while also enjoying Kenrokuen Garden and local sake. As for Phnom Penh, Cambodia—I spent eight years there before moving to Indonesia, so it holds a special place in my heart. Now, with a new airport and riverside renovations, it's transformed from a mere transit point into a destination worth exploring.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784430595493-pp.jpeg)

▲Phnom Penh Riverside, Cambodia

In Europe: Scotland's Hebrides Islands offer white sandy beaches and the ancient Callanish Standing Stones, paired with whisky culture—perfect for a leisurely exploration. Montenegro adds hiking routes in the Prokletije Mountains alongside the Bay of Kotor. Portugal's Guimarães celebrates the 25th anniversary of its UNESCO World Heritage status while also being named European Green Capital. Finland's Oulu, one of the 2026 European Capitals of Culture, sits near the Arctic Circle and focuses on art festivals and saunas.

In the Americas: Chile's Colchagua Valley stretches from the Andes to the Pacific, featuring wineries, stargazing, and colonial towns. Oregon's coast in the USA is ideal for a slow road trip, with cliffs and lighthouses as highlights. Philadelphia will host a series of celebrations and events for the 250th anniversary of American independence. The colonial zone of Santo Domingo in the Dominican Republic has just been renovated, just in time for the 2026 Central American and Caribbean Games. Canada's Slocan Valley will open the 'Japanese Canadian Heritage Trail' to commemorate WWII history.

Oceania and Africa: Rarotonga in the Cook Islands boasts a blue lagoon and rich Polynesian culture, while Aitutaki is even quieter. Australia's Uluru has a new trail that lets you walk through the desert while learning about the Anangu people's culture. Kenya's Samburu is recognized for its rare wildlife safaris, elephant rescue center, and stargazing.

Other highlights include Roman ruins in Algeria, a whale and dolphin sanctuary in Loreto, Mexico, beaches and the historic city of Colonia del Sacramento in Uruguay, and eco-tourism on the Osa Peninsula in Costa Rica—each with its own unique appeal.

### Final Thoughts

Looking at the full list, what struck me most was Komodo making the cut. Indonesia's tourism marketing in recent years has indeed become more internationally savvy—not just posting scenic photos, but weaving conservation and sustainability into the narrative. I think that's the right direction. If you're still deciding where to go this year, consider adding Indonesia to your shortlist—once you've had your fill of Bali, a quick flight to Labuan Bajo to see the Komodo dragons offers a completely different experience.
