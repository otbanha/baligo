However, due to its relatively remote location, visiting Nusa Penida may require detailed research and planning. In this article, we'll provide you with a travel guide from Bali to Nusa Penida, along with the latest information to help you plan your trip.

## Introduction to Nusa Penida

Nusa Penida is an offshore island of Bali, reachable by a 45-minute boat ride from the main island. It is famous for its untouched natural beauty, featuring dramatic cliffs, sea caves, beaches, and other fascinating landscapes that create a paradise-like scene against the backdrop of azure waters. The word 'Nusa' in Indonesian means 'island,' so Nusa Penida is also translated as 'Penida Island,' 'Perida Island,' or directly transliterated as 'Nusa Penida.'

## How to Get to Nusa Penida

There are two ways to get to Nusa Penida from Bali. First, let's look at traveling independently. The most common way is to take a ferry from Sanur Port on Bali's east coast. Sanur Port is about 30 minutes from Kuta and 1 hour from Canggu. You can book your ferry ticket in advance and head to Sanur Port. From there, you can take the ferry to Nusa Penida. While this is an economical option, it does require time and effort to plan.

For those traveling from Bali to Nusa Penida, you should know that the fast boats depart from one location: Sanur, located on Bali's southeast coast, about 40 minutes from Kuta and 80 minutes from Canggu. The new port is called Pemelisan Harbor.

### ****How to get from Ubud to Nusa Penida?****

Traveling from Ubud to Nusa Penida is more complicated due to the greater distance. Currently, there are no speedboat tickets available for booking.

However, you can arrange transportation from Ubud to the port in Sanur. Driving/taking a car takes about an hour, but frequent traffic jams can make it longer.

### ****What is it like to take a boat from Sanur Port to Nusa Penida?****

When taking a speedboat from Sanur to Nusa Penida, it's essential to book your ticket in advance. If you arrive at the port without a pre-booked ticket, you'll be surrounded by 'enthusiastic' salespeople offering some very absurd prices.

Once you're on board, the journey from Sanur Port to Nusa Penida takes about 45 minutes. The old 'slow boats' no longer cater to tourists as they were somewhat dangerous and have even capsized in the past. Depending on the weather and sea conditions, it could be a calm, short boat ride or a wild rollercoaster on the ocean!

### ****Luggage Restrictions****

Speedboats from Bali to Nusa Penida usually have luggage restrictions, allowing a maximum of two suitcases with a total weight of 30 kg. However, they don't actually weigh your luggage. They place it on the deck and cover it with a large waterproof tarp to avoid getting splashed.
