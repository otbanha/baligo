According to Indonesian law, those caught possessing drugs face severe penalties. Whether it's a small or large amount, being found with drugs by the police could result in several years of imprisonment. Even more severe, if convicted of drug trafficking, one could face the harshest penalty—death.

Such severe punishments are not just empty threats; in fact, there have been multiple cases of drug traffickers being executed in Indonesia. Even some traffickers from Taiwan have not been spared, ultimately facing dire consequences. These cases not only demonstrate the Indonesian government's zero-tolerance policy towards drug crimes but also serve as a strong warning to potential traffickers worldwide.

For travelers planning to visit Bali or other regions in Indonesia, it is crucial to be aware of and comply with these strict laws to avoid danger. Possessing or selling drugs can threaten personal freedom and safety, and it is absolutely not worth the risk. Moreover, some prescription drugs that are legal in Taiwan and Hong Kong might be considered illegal drugs in Indonesia!
