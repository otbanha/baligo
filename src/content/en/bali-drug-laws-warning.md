According to Indonesian law, those caught possessing drugs face severe penalties. If discovered by the police with drugs, whether in small or large quantities, one could be sentenced to several years in prison. Moreover, if convicted of drug trafficking, the most severe punishment—death—may be imposed.

Such harsh penalties are not just empty threats; in fact, there have been multiple cases of drug traffickers being executed in Indonesia. Even some traffickers from Taiwan have not been spared, facing extremely dire consequences. These cases not only demonstrate the Indonesian government's zero-tolerance policy towards drug crimes but also send a strong warning to potential drug traffickers worldwide.

For travelers planning to visit Bali or other regions in Indonesia, it is crucial to be aware of and comply with these strict laws to avoid danger. Possessing or selling drugs threatens personal freedom and safety, and it is absolutely not worth the risk. Moreover, some prescription drugs that are legal in Taiwan and Hong Kong could be considered illegal drugs in Indonesia!
