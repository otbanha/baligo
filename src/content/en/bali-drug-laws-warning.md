**Bottom Line**: Indonesia has a zero-tolerance policy on drugs. Possession can lead to **years in prison**, and drug trafficking carries a maximum penalty of **the death sentence**. Several foreign nationals (including Taiwanese) have been executed in Indonesia for drug offenses. Be aware: some prescription drugs legal in Taiwan/Hong Kong (like sleeping pills) may be considered illegal in Indonesia—always verify the legality of your medications before traveling.

Indonesia takes an extremely harsh stance on drug issues, and Bali, as one of its most famous tourist destinations, is no exception. Drugs are seen as a major social problem, and the Indonesian government enforces strict legal measures to combat drug-related crimes.

Under Indonesian law, anyone caught with drugs faces severe penalties. Whether it's a small or large amount, being found in possession can lead to several years in prison. Even worse, if convicted of drug trafficking, you could face the harshest punishment—the death penalty.

These severe penalties are no empty threat; multiple drug traffickers have already been executed in Indonesia. Even traffickers from Taiwan have not been spared, meeting tragic ends. These cases not only underscore Indonesia's zero-tolerance policy toward drug crimes but also serve as a stark warning to potential traffickers worldwide.

For travelers planning a trip to Bali or other parts of Indonesia, it's essential to understand and comply with these strict laws to avoid serious danger. Possessing or trafficking drugs can threaten your personal freedom and even your life—it's absolutely not worth the risk. Moreover, some prescription medications that are legal in Taiwan or Hong Kong may be considered illegal drugs in Indonesia.
