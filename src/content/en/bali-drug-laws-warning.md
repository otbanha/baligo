According to Indonesian law, those caught possessing drugs face severe penalties. Whether it's a small or large amount, possession can lead to several years of imprisonment. Even more severe, those convicted of drug trafficking may face the harshest punishment—death.

These strict penalties are not just empty threats; in fact, several drug traffickers have already been executed in Indonesia. Even some traffickers from Taiwan have not been spared, ultimately facing tragic consequences. These cases not only demonstrate the Indonesian government's zero-tolerance policy towards drug crimes but also serve as a strong warning to potential traffickers worldwide.

For travelers planning to visit Bali or other parts of Indonesia, it is crucial to be aware of and adhere to these strict laws to avoid danger. Possessing or selling drugs can threaten personal freedom and safety, and it is absolutely not worth the risk. Even some prescription drugs that are legal in Taiwan and Hong Kong may be considered illegal in Indonesia!
