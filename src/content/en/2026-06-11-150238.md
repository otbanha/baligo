### Today's Top Story: The Double-Edged Economic Impact of the Rupiah Plunging Past 18,000 and Surging Fuel Costs

- **Rupiah plunge and non-subsidized fuel cost surge reshape tourist spending patterns**: According to an in-depth financial report by *The Bali Sun* today, the Indonesian rupiah has recently broken through the historic psychological barrier of **1 USD to 18,000 IDR** against major currencies like the US dollar and Australian dollar. This financial upheaval is a massive 'consumption dividend' for international tourists on the island, significantly boosting the purchasing power of foreign currency holders. Many Australian and European tourists in Sanur and Canggu have decided on the spot to extend their stays and increase spending on high-end spas and restaurants. However, this plunge is a double-edged sword: due to global oil price hikes from Middle East tensions, Indonesia's state oil company has sharply raised prices for two types of non-subsidized gasoline (by nearly a third). This directly drives up procurement costs for imported ingredients and raw materials at the island's fine dining restaurants and star-rated hotels, while also indirectly pushing up international and domestic airfares. Travel expert Sukawati warns that although international tourists can now enjoy local accommodations at cheaper rates, as local transportation fleets and imported dining costs are gradually eroded by inflation, future independent travel cost structures may shift toward 'extremely affordable accommodation and dining, but pricier car rentals and flights.'

### 📄 Tourist Hot Topic: Road Closure Guide for Tomorrow's Arts Festival

- **Tomorrow (June 13), the 'Bali Arts Festival' grand opening will trigger large-scale road closures in Denpasar's Renon area starting at 12:00 PM**: The Cultural Office and Traffic Police Headquarters have issued the core traffic security notice for tomorrow. To welcome the 20,000 artists arriving in the morning and the confirmed presence of Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto, **major arterial roads around the Bajra Sandhi Monument in central Denpasar (such as Jl. Raya Puputan) will be fully closed and vehicle-restricted starting at 12:00 PM on Saturday, June 13**. Organizers strongly urge all tourists planning to attend the grand opening parade (Peed Aya) tomorrow afternoon to use two-wheeled motorbikes (Grab/Gojek) instead, and to get off and walk inside before the control lines to completely avoid the expected hours-long gridlock of private cars in the surrounding area.
