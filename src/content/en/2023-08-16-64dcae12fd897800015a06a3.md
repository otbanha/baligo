Shopping is often a key part of any travel itinerary. When shopping during your travels, convenience and safety are crucial considerations. While focusing on the items you wish to purchase, it's easy to become less vigilant. This can increase the risk of losing money due to dropped wallets or pickpocketing.

To make travel shopping more convenient and secure, try the following tips:

### 1. Carry an Appropriate Amount of Cash

Carrying an appropriate amount of cash is the first step to making your holiday shopping safer and more convenient. However, carrying too much cash can introduce unnecessary risks! Additionally, carrying just the right amount can help prevent overspending, as people with excessive cash tend to be more wasteful.

To ensure a safer and more comfortable trip, it's advisable to separate your cash from important documents. This minimizes the risk of losing both money and documents.

### 2. Separate Cash and Important Documents

Cash is typically used for everyday expenses, while important documents like tickets or passports are only needed at specific times. It's recommended to store cash and documents separately to avoid losing everything due to carelessness.

### 3. Carry a Bag for Valuables

When traveling abroad, it's essential to carry a small bag to store valuable items. This provides greater convenience and security, protecting these valuables while keeping them separate from shopping items.

Make sure the bag you carry is smaller than your main backpack, so you won't feel inconvenienced. Additionally, wear the bag across your chest or waist to store valuable items, making it easy to keep an eye on your belongings at all times.

### 4. Prioritize Cashless Payment Methods

During your vacation, prioritize using cashless payment methods whenever possible. This simplifies shopping and reduces the need to carry large amounts of cash.

For cashless payments, travelers in Indonesia or Bali can consider using the e-wallet GoPay. GoPay is a service under Indonesia's super app, GoJek. With GoPay, you can make payments at nearly 90% of merchants in tourist areas across Indonesia or Bali.

Why is GoJek considered a 'super app'? Using GoJek, travelers can not only book rides for short-distance travel but also order food delivery to their hotels and make payments through the GoPay e-wallet—three services most commonly used by travelers. Additionally, topping up your GoPay balance is incredibly simple! You can do it through ATMs in Indonesia/Bali, convenience stores, or even by giving cash to GoJek drivers to recharge for you. Another option is linking GoPay to your credit card, which saves even more hassle.
