Shopping is often a key activity in travel itineraries. When shopping during travels, convenience and safety are crucial aspects to pay attention to. While focusing on desired purchases, one might become less vigilant, increasing the risk of losing money due to dropped wallets or pickpocketing.

To enhance the convenience and safety of travel shopping, consider these tips:

### 1. Carry an Appropriate Amount of Cash

Carrying a suitable amount of cash is the first step towards safer and more convenient holiday shopping. However, carrying too much cash can introduce unnecessary risks! Additionally, having just enough cash can prevent overspending, as people tend to be more wasteful when they carry excessive amounts.

For a safer and more comfortable travel experience, it's advisable to separate cash from important documents. This minimizes the risk of losing both money and essential papers.

### 2. Separate Storage for Cash and Documents

Cash is typically used for everyday expenses, while documents like tickets or passports are needed only at specific times. Storing cash and documents separately can prevent the loss of both due to carelessness.

### 3. Carry a Bag for Collectible Items

When traveling abroad, it's essential to carry a small bag to store valuable items. This provides greater convenience and security, protecting these valuables while also separating shopping items from precious belongings.

Make sure the bag you carry is smaller than your main backpack, so you won't feel inconvenienced. Additionally, wear the bag across your chest or waist to store valuables, allowing you to keep an eye on your precious items at all times.

### 4. Prioritize Non-Cash Payment Methods

During your vacation, try to prioritize non-cash payment methods whenever possible. This simplifies shopping and eliminates the need to carry large amounts of cash.

For non-cash payments, travelers in Indonesia or Bali can consider using the e-wallet GoPay. GoPay is a service under Indonesia's super app, GoJek. Using GoPay allows you to make payments at nearly 90% of merchants in tourist areas across Indonesia or Bali.

Why is GoJek considered a 'super app'? With GoJek, travelers can not only book rides for short-distance travel but also order food delivery to their hotels and make payments using the GoPay e-wallet—three services most commonly used by travelers. Moreover, topping up your GoPay balance is incredibly simple! You can do it through ATMs in Indonesia/Bali, convenience store chains, or even by giving cash to GoJek drivers who can top up for you. Another option is linking GoPay to your credit card, saving you even more hassle.
