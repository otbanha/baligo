## **Traveling in Bali: Cash or Credit Card?**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_832db431875e09d43fff04ac9260f412.jpg)

Bali is a favorite vacation destination for travelers from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia. Whether it's luxury hotels, unique restaurants, or spa treatments, using credit cards seems convenient, but it actually hides many risks. Issues like credit card fraud, high transaction fees, and merchant scams are frequently reported. If not handled carefully, a pleasant holiday can turn into a financial nightmare.

This article will provide a detailed analysis of the potential risks of using credit cards in Bali and offer practical safety tips to help you protect your wallet while enjoying your trip!

---

## **1. Credit Card Fraud and Scam Risks**

### **1. Skimming and Counterfeit Card Production**

Some merchants or ATMs in Bali may be equipped with skimming devices by criminal groups. When you swipe your card, fraudsters can steal your credit card information and duplicate it into counterfeit cards for fraudulent transactions.

- **High-risk locations**: Small roadside shops, night markets, non-chain restaurants, and remote ATMs.
- **Prevention methods**:
  - Try to use credit cards at large chain stores (such as supermarkets and international brand hotels).
  - When using ATMs, prioritize machines located inside banks and avoid standalone ATMs.

### **2. Unsafe Payment Environments**

Some restaurants or merchants still use outdated card machines or manually record credit card numbers, which could lead to your card information being stolen.

- **Recommendations**:
- - If a merchant requests 'manual card number entry' or 'taking a photo of your credit card', refuse immediately.
  - Switch to cash or mobile payments (like Apple Pay, Google Pay, or Indonesia's GoPay) to reduce risks.

## **II. High Fees and Exchange Rate Traps**

### **1. Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) Exploitation**

When paying by card, some merchants may ask, 'Would you like to pay in Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) or New Taiwan Dollar (TWD)?' If you choose TWD, the system will automatically use a very poor exchange rate and add extra fees.

- **Correct Approach**:
- - **Insist on paying in Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)**, letting your card issuer handle the currency conversion, which is usually more favorable.
  - Before swiping, confirm the currency displayed on the POS machine to avoid forced DCC.

### **2. Foreign Transaction Fees**

Most credit cards typically charge **1.5%-3%** for foreign transactions, which can add up to a significant expense over time.

- **Solutions**:
- - Apply for a credit card with 'no foreign transaction fees' before traveling, such as Taishin FlyGo or Yuanta Multi-Currency Debit Card.
  - For large purchases, consider splitting the payment across multiple transactions to avoid high single-transaction fees.

---

## **3. Additional Charges and Mandatory Tips**

In Bali, some businesses may:

- **Automatically add service charges (5%-10%)**: Common in restaurants and spas.
- **Request additional cash tips**: Even after paying by card, you may still be asked to pay cash.

**How to handle it**:

- Check if the bill includes taxes and service charges before paying by card.
- If you encounter unreasonable charges, request correction on the spot or dispute the charge with your bank.

## **4. Pre-authorization Risks**

Hotels, car rental companies, or dive shops may request a "pre-authorization" to freeze a deposit (possibly 2-3 times the room rate). If not properly released, it could lead to:

- **Credit limit being occupied**, affecting subsequent purchases.
- **Delayed refunds**, or even overcharging.

**Safety tips**:

- Use cash for deposits to avoid pre-authorization disputes.
- If you must use a card, request the merchant to cancel the pre-authorization on the spot upon checkout and keep the receipt.

---

## **5. Technical Issues Leading to Transaction Failures**

In some areas of Bali, unstable internet connections may cause:

- **Failed credit card transactions that still result in charges.**
- **Duplicate charges.**

**How to Handle It?**

- Immediately check your bank app or SMS notifications to confirm the transaction status.
- Keep the credit card receipt and contact your bank to dispute the charge if necessary.

Even credit cards that normally work perfectly can encounter unexpected issues in Bali. ## **Ultimate Tips for Safe Credit Card Use**

1. **Cash First, Credit Card as Backup**: Use cash for small purchases and credit cards for larger ones.
2. **Enable Instant Transaction Notifications**: Monitor every transaction through your bank app or SMS.
3. **Carry Two or More Credit Cards**: Keep them separate to avoid being stranded if one is lost.
4. **Prioritize Chip Cards or Mobile Payments**: These are safer than magnetic stripe cards.
5. **Report Fraud Immediately**: Save your bank’s overseas emergency contact number before traveling.
6. **Credit Card Not Working Abroad**: Some cards require international usage activation—confirm with your bank before departure.

---

## **Conclusion: Smart Spending, Peaceful Vacation**

Bali’s credit card environment isn’t as secure as Taiwan’s, but with the right payment strategies, you can significantly reduce risks. During your trip, try to use **a mix of cash and electronic payments**, with credit cards as a backup. Always monitor your transaction records to ensure a convenient and worry-free vacation!
