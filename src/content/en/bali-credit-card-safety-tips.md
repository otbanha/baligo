## **Traveling in Bali: Cash or Credit Card?**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_832db431875e09d43fff04ac9260f412.jpg)

Bali is a favorite vacation destination for travelers from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia. Whether it's luxury hotels, unique restaurants, or spa treatments, using credit cards may seem convenient, but it actually hides many risks. Issues like credit card fraud, high transaction fees, and merchant scams are not uncommon. If not handled carefully, a pleasant holiday can quickly turn into a financial nightmare.

This article will provide a detailed analysis of the potential risks of using credit cards in Bali and offer practical safety tips to help you protect your wallet while enjoying your trip!

---

## **1. Credit Card Fraud and Scam Risks**

### **1. Skimming and Counterfeit Card Production**

Some merchants or ATMs in Bali may be compromised by criminal organizations with skimming devices. When you swipe your card, fraudsters can steal your credit card information and create counterfeit cards for unauthorized transactions.

- **High-risk locations**: Small roadside shops, night markets, non-chain restaurants, and remote ATMs.
- **Prevention methods**:
  - Try to use credit cards at large chain stores (e.g., supermarkets, international brand hotels).
  - When using ATMs, prioritize machines located inside banks and avoid standalone ATMs.

### **2. Unsafe Payment Environments**

Some restaurants or merchants still use outdated card machines, or even manually record credit card numbers, which could lead to your card information being stolen.

- **Recommendation**:
  - If a merchant requests to "manually enter your card number" or "take a photo of your credit card," refuse immediately.
  - Switch to cash or mobile payments (such as Apple Pay, Google Pay, or Indonesia's GoPay) to reduce risks.

## **2. High Fees and Exchange Rate Traps**

### **1. Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) Exploitation**

When paying by card, some merchants may ask, "Would you like to pay in Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) or New Taiwan Dollars (TWD)?" If you choose TWD, the system will automatically convert the amount using a very poor exchange rate and add additional fees.

- **Correct Approach**:
  - **Insist on paying in Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)**, letting your card issuer handle the currency conversion, which is usually more favorable.
  - Before swiping, confirm the currency displayed on the POS machine to avoid forced DCC.

### **2. Foreign Transaction Fees**

Most credit cards typically charge **1.5%-3%** for foreign transactions, which can add up to a significant expense over time.

- **Solution**:
  - Apply for a credit card with "no foreign transaction fees" before traveling, such as Taishin FlyGo or Yuanta Multi-Currency Debit Card.
  - For large purchases, consider splitting the payment into multiple transactions to avoid high fees on a single transaction.

---

## **3. Additional Charges and Mandatory Tips**

In Bali, some businesses may, without prior notice:

- **Automatically add a service charge (5%-10%)**: Common in restaurants and spas.
- **Request additional cash tips**: Even after paying by card, you may still be asked to pay in cash.

**How to Handle It**:

- Before swiping your card, confirm whether the bill includes taxes and service charges.
- If you encounter unreasonable charges, you can request an immediate correction or dispute the charge with your bank.

## **4. Pre-authorization Risks**

Hotels, car rental companies, or dive shops may sometimes require a "pre-authorization" to freeze a deposit (which could be 2-3 times the room rate). If not properly released, this could lead to:

- **Credit limit being tied up**, affecting subsequent purchases.
- **Delayed refunds**, or even overcharging.

**Safety Tips**:

- Use cash for deposits to avoid pre-authorization disputes.
- If you must use a card, ask the merchant to cancel the pre-authorization on the spot when checking out, and keep the receipt.

---

## **5. Technical Issues Leading to Transaction Failures**

Unstable internet connections in some areas of Bali may result in:

- **Failed transactions but still being charged**.

- **Duplicate charges**.

**What to Do?**

- Immediately check your bank app or SMS notifications to confirm the transaction status.
