At the end of April, we embarked on an 11-day free & easy trip to Bali and Lombok. This journey remains unforgettable to this day. While planning this trip, I referred to many travelogues and suggestions from experienced travelers, knowing that trip planning is no easy task. Therefore, I hope to take this opportunity to share some of our recommended attractions, along with short videos of each spot, to help everyone better experience the local atmosphere and decide whether to include these places in their own itineraries.

During this trip, we visited five main areas: Jimbaran, Uluwatu, Ubud, the Gili Islands, and Kuta. In this article, I will focus on the first three areas—Jimbaran, Uluwatu, and Ubud—sharing our recommended attractions and personal reviews (these recommendations and reviews are purely based on personal experiences, and I welcome any differing opinions for discussion).

## Recommended Attractions in Bali

### Jimbaran Beach Sunset Seafood Dinner

![金巴蘭海鮮（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_37baf48952137cd3a9cb784f0511bf09.jpg)

Jimbaran Seafood (provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Recommendation Rating: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 2.5 hours**

Jimbaran Beach is renowned as one of the 'World's Top 10 Sunset Beaches,' and its scenery is truly impeccable. Whether it's the ocean view or the sunset, both are breathtakingly beautiful. However, the prices at the restaurants here can be quite hard to swallow. This was our first meal after landing, and the three of us ended up spending over USD$160, which left us astonished by the high costs.

While dining on the beach in the evening, bands often perform around the tables and request tips, which can feel a bit like emotional extortion. Therefore, if you plan to dine here, I recommend checking restaurant reviews beforehand to decide whether it's worth it. The stunning views at Jimbaran Beach are worth seeing, but you’ll need to be more cautious when choosing where to eat.

### GWK Cultural Park in Jimbaran

![神鷹廣場（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c27b44be953aec6137d489e2f5b0efa3.jpg)

Garuda Wisnu Kencana Square (Photo by Ting-Yu Yeh)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Duration: 3 hours**

GWK Cultural Park is a must-visit attraction. The majestic statue of Vishnu, visible even from the airport, stands 30 meters taller than the Statue of Liberty, leaving a lasting impression. The park itself is grand and solemn, offering a sense of tranquility and reverence as you stroll through it. Sipping coconut water while exploring the park is a delightful experience. The souvenir shop here is extensive and well-stocked, making it perfect for shopping enthusiasts. Additionally, the air-conditioned restrooms nearby provide a refreshing escape from the heat. GWK Cultural Park is not only visually stunning but also well-equipped, making it an unmissable destination.

### Uluwatu Temple Kecak Fire Dance

![Kecak Fire Dance](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_96343c5b07b3ceae31d0c3e6b4fd9e97.jpg)

Kecak Fire Dance

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1.5 hours**

The Kecak Fire Dance performance at Uluwatu Temple narrates Balinese mythological tales, attracting nearly 2,000 spectators, creating a spectacular scene. While watching this performance, you can enjoy the exquisite dance and the magnificent crimson sunset, offering a dual visual and cultural experience that is unforgettable.

It is recommended to familiarize yourself with the story outline before watching, as it will help you immerse in the context; otherwise, you might find it boring. Notably, the fire dance segment lasts only about 5 minutes at the climax, and it should not be confused with fire dance performances by street artists in Taiwan.

The Kecak Fire Dance performance at Uluwatu Temple is a visual feast combining culture and natural beauty. Although the fire dance segment is brief, the overall atmosphere and performance quality are worth experiencing.

![Oneeighty Day Club（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3895f7963249a801fbac88cd56bf66a4.jpg)

Oneeighty Day Club (provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 3.5 hours**

Oneeighty Club Cliff Bar is an extravagant venue built on a cliff, featuring a stunning pool perfect for photography—just taking photos can easily occupy 2 hours. The entrance fee can be offset against purchases, making it feel like good value for money. The food here is considered quite delicious for the Uluwatu area and is definitely worth trying.

However, remember to wash your hands after swimming before handling food, as it's easy to contract 'Bali Belly' (traveler's diarrhea)—a lesson I learned the hard way during my visit.

Oneeighty Club Cliff Bar attracts many tourists with its unique location and high-quality food. It's a great place to relax and capture beautiful photos, but hygiene should be prioritized to avoid ruining your travel experience.

### Ubud Monkey Forest

![Monkey Forest（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_861edde63ee2d4e8aebd1aed3d0f8a43.jpg)

Monkey Forest (provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 2 hours**

The Sacred Monkey Forest is a quaint attraction filled with artistic statues, caves, and totems, making visitors feel like they're on an adventure. The number of monkeys here even surpasses that of tourists, and their free-spirited behavior is fascinating. We witnessed some peculiar sights, such as smoking monkeys, cola-drinking monkeys, and even monkeys appearing to be under the influence.

When visiting the Sacred Monkey Forest, it's essential to follow the park's monkey rules to avoid any attacks. Additionally, there's an amusing service where you can pay USD$3 to have a monkey hold your phone for a selfie. Although it's a trick shot, the photos look incredibly realistic, adding a lot of fun to your trip.

The Sacred Monkey Forest is a place rich in cultural atmosphere and full of surprises, making it perfect for animal lovers and adventure seekers.

### Penglipuran Village in Ubud

![彭里普蘭村莊（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3be9f1e248a2509af6c7a87483488ba1.jpg)

Penglipuran Village (Photo provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 2 hours**

Penglipuran Village is hailed as the cleanest village in the world. Every household here is open to visitors, allowing tourists to step inside and gain a deeper understanding of the locals' daily lives and simple lifestyle. The architecture and street scenes in the village are highly distinctive, with every shot being a picturesque moment. Particularly, the village's Yin-Yang gates are a must-see photo spot.

As most visitors are locals, this place retains its authentic charm without excessive commercialization. The villagers are friendly and hospitable, offering a warm and welcoming atmosphere.

Penglipuran Village is a highly recommended destination, where you can not only enjoy stunning natural landscapes but also immerse yourself in genuine local culture, making it a rich and unforgettable travel experience.

### Tegallalang Rice Terraces in Ubud

![Tegallalang Rice Field（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_273e9bca32d9e28d429843236d543aab.jpg)

Tegallalang Rice Field (Photo provided by Yeh Ting-Yu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 2.5 hours**

The Tegallalang Rice Terraces are just a 20-minute drive from Ubud and are a magnificent UNESCO World Heritage site. Here, you can admire the stunning layered rice terraces and experience the thrilling swing activity. Although the 2-minute swing experience costs nearly USD$13, the breathtaking photos taken here in long dresses make it feel worth every penny.

Additionally, there are many other photo spots here, such as bird nests and heart-shaped decorations, perfect for capturing memorable moments. However, it's important to note that the high humidity can cause headaches after prolonged exposure, so it's advisable to take breaks and stay hydrated.

The Tegallalang Rice Terraces not only offer spectacular views but also provide a variety of experiential activities, making it an excellent journey that combines nature and adventure, well worth a visit.

### Ubud Coffee Plantation

**Recommendation Index: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1 hour**

In Ubud, there are many civet coffee plantations. We chose one recommended by our driver, which offered a tasting experience of 16 different types of tea and coffee. The tasting experience was indeed enjoyable, allowing us to try a variety of flavors in one go.

However, upon returning home and conducting some research, we found that these plantations have received quite a few negative reviews. The main issue is that the products they sell do not match the samples tasted. Therefore, it's advisable to do thorough research and check reviews before visiting these coffee plantations to avoid unpleasant shopping experiences.

### Jungle Gym at Ubud Hotel

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1.5 hours**

The jungle gym at Ubud Wapa Di Ume offers an exceptionally unique fitness experience with a 360-degree panoramic view of Ubud's natural landscape. Nestled between rice fields and jungle, it allows you to exercise amidst stunning natural scenery—an experience I find truly rare!

Although the gym equipment is limited, the low foot traffic means you can practically have the place to yourself, enjoying a private and tranquil workout space. This unique atmosphere makes the entire fitness process more comfortable and focused.

The jungle gym at Ubud Wapa Di Ume not only provides fitness facilities but also allows you to relax your mind and body in a natural setting, making it an ideal spot to escape the hustle and bustle and find peace.
