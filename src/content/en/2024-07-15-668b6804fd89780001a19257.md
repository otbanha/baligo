At the end of April, we embarked on an 11-day free trip to Bali and Lombok. This journey remains unforgettable to this day. When planning this trip, I referred to many travelogues and suggestions from predecessors, knowing that planning a journey is not easy. Therefore, I hope to take this opportunity to share some of the attractions we visited, along with short videos of each spot, to help everyone feel the local atmosphere and decide whether to include these attractions in their own itinerary.

Our journey took us to five main areas: Jimbaran, Uluwatu, Ubud, the Gili Islands, and Kuta. In this article, I will focus on the first three areas—Jimbaran, Uluwatu, and Ubud—sharing recommendations and personal reviews of the attractions (these recommendations and reviews are purely personal opinions, and I welcome discussions and exchanges if you have different thoughts).

## Recommended Attractions in Bali

### Sunset Seafood Dinner at Jimbaran Beach

![金巴蘭海鮮（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_37baf48952137cd3a9cb784f0511bf09.jpg)

Jimbaran Seafood (provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Recommendation Index: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Stay Duration: 2.5 hours**

Jimbaran Beach is renowned as one of the 'World's Top Ten Sunset Beaches,' and its beauty is truly unparalleled. Both the seascape and the sunset are breathtakingly stunning. However, the prices at the restaurants here can be quite hard to swallow. This was our first meal after landing, and the three of us ended up spending over $160, which left us marveling at the steep prices.

While dining on the beach in the evening, bands often play around the tables and request tips, which can feel a bit like emotional blackmail. Therefore, if you plan to dine here, it's advisable to check restaurant reviews beforehand to decide whether to enter. The scenic beauty of Jimbaran Beach is worth seeing, but you need to be more cautious when choosing a restaurant.

### GWK Cultural Park in Jimbaran

![神鷹廣場（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c27b44be953aec6137d489e2f5b0efa3.jpg)

Garuda Wisnu Kencana Square (provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Recommendation Index: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 3 hours**

GWK Cultural Park is a highly worthwhile attraction. The majestic statue of Vishnu, visible from the airport, stands 30 meters taller than the Statue of Liberty, leaving a deep impression. The entire park is grand and solemn, offering a sense of tranquility and reverence as you stroll through it. Enjoying a coconut drink while exploring the park is a delightful experience. The souvenir shop within the park is extensive and well-stocked, perfect for shopping enthusiasts. Additionally, the air-conditioned restrooms next to the park provide a refreshing respite from the heat. GWK Cultural Park is not only visually stunning but also well-equipped, making it a must-visit destination.

### Uluwatu Temple Kecak Fire Dance

![Kecak Fire Dance](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_96343c5b07b3ceae31d0c3e6b4fd9e97.jpg)

Kecak Fire Dance

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1.5 hours**

The Kecak Fire Dance performance at Uluwatu Temple narrates Balinese mythological tales, attracting nearly 2,000 spectators in a truly spectacular setting. While watching the performance, you can enjoy both the captivating dance and the breathtaking crimson sunset, creating an unforgettable visual and cultural experience.

It's recommended to familiarize yourself with the story outline beforehand to better immerse yourself in the performance; otherwise, you might find it boring if you don't understand what's happening. Note that the fire dance segment only lasts about 5 minutes during the climax, so don't confuse it with street performer fire shows in Taiwan.

The Kecak Fire Dance performance at Uluwatu Temple is a visual feast combining culture and natural beauty. While the fire dance portion is brief, the overall atmosphere and quality of the performance make it well worth watching.

### Uluwatu Oneeighty Club Cliff Bar

![Oneeighty Day Club（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3895f7963249a801fbac88cd56bf66a4.jpg)

Oneeighty Day Club (provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 3.5 hours**

Oneeighty Club Cliff Bar is an extravagant venue built on a cliff, featuring a photogenic pool where you can easily spend 2 hours taking pictures. The entrance fee can be offset against purchases, making it feel like good value for money. The food here is among the tastiest in the Uluwatu area and is definitely worth trying.

However, remember to wash your hands after swimming before handling food, as it's easy to contract 'Bali Belly' (traveler's diarrhea)—a lesson I learned the hard way during my visit.

Oneeighty Club Cliff Bar attracts many visitors with its unique location and high-quality food, making it a great spot to relax and capture stunning photos. Just be mindful of hygiene to avoid ruining your travel experience.

### Ubud Monkey Forest

![Monkey Forest（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_861edde63ee2d4e8aebd1aed3d0f8a43.jpg)

Monkey Forest (Photo by Ting-Yu Yeh)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Duration: 2 hours**

The Sacred Monkey Forest is a charming and ancient site filled with artistic statues, caves, and totems, making it feel like an adventure. The number of monkeys here even surpasses the tourists, and their free-spirited behavior is fascinating. We witnessed some peculiar scenes, including monkeys smoking, drinking cola, and even appearing to be 'high.'

When visiting the Sacred Monkey Forest, it's essential to follow the park's monkey rules to avoid any attacks. Additionally, there's a fun service where you can pay USD$3 to have a monkey hold your phone for a selfie. Although it's a trick shot, the photos look incredibly realistic and add a lot of fun to your trip.

The Sacred Monkey Forest is a culturally rich and surprising destination, perfect for animal lovers and adventure seekers.

### Penglipuran Village in Ubud

![彭里普蘭村莊（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3be9f1e248a2509af6c7a87483488ba1.jpg)

Penglipuran Village (Photo provided by Ye Tingyu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Duration: 2 hours**

Penglipuran Village is renowned as one of the cleanest villages in the world. Every household here is open to visitors, allowing tourists to step inside and gain a deeper understanding of the locals' daily lives and simple way of living. The architecture and streetscapes in the village are highly distinctive, making every shot a picturesque scene. The village's iconic split gates are a must-see photo spot.

As most visitors here are locals, the village retains its authentic charm without excessive commercialization. The villagers are friendly and hospitable, offering a warm sense of community.

Penglipuran Village is a highly recommended destination. Not only can you enjoy stunning natural scenery, but you can also immerse yourself in genuine local culture, making it a rich and unforgettable travel experience.

### Ubud Tegallalang Rice Terraces

![Tegallalang Rice Field（葉庭瑜提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_273e9bca32d9e28d429843236d543aab.jpg)

Tegallalang Rice Field (Photo provided by Yeh Ting-Yu)

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Recommended Duration: 2.5 hours**

Just a 20-minute drive from Ubud city center, the Tegallalang Rice Terraces are a stunning UNESCO World Heritage site. Here, you can marvel at the cascading rice terraces and even try the thrilling swing activity. Although the 2-minute swing experience costs around USD$13, the breathtaking photos you can take here, especially in a long dress, make it worth every penny.

Additionally, there are numerous other photo spots like bird nests and heart-shaped decorations, perfect for Instagram-worthy shots. However, be mindful that the high humidity can sometimes cause headaches, so it's advisable to take breaks and stay hydrated.

The Tegallalang Rice Terraces not only boast breathtaking scenery but also offer a variety of experiential activities, making it an excellent journey that combines nature and adventure—definitely worth a visit.

### Ubud Coffee Plantations

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1 hour**

In Ubud, there are many civet coffee plantations. We chose one recommended by our driver, which offered a tasting experience of 16 different types of tea and coffee. The tasting experience was indeed enjoyable, allowing us to try a variety of flavors in one go.

However, after returning home and doing some research, we found that these plantations have received quite a few negative reviews. The main issue is that the products they sell differ from what is offered during the tasting. Therefore, it’s advisable to do your homework and check reviews before visiting these coffee plantations to avoid an unpleasant shopping experience.

### Ubud Jungle Gym at Hotel

**Rating: 🌟🌟🌟🌟**

**Duration: 1.5 hours**

The jungle gym at Ubud Wapa Di Ume offers a truly unique fitness experience with 360-degree panoramic views of Ubud's natural landscape. Nestled between rice fields and jungle, it allows you to exercise amidst stunning natural scenery—an experience I found exceptionally rare!

Although the gym equipment is limited, the low foot traffic means you can practically have the place to yourself, enjoying a private and tranquil workout space. This unique atmosphere makes the entire fitness process more comfortable and focused.

The jungle gym at Ubud Wapa Di Ume not only provides fitness facilities but also allows you to relax in a natural environment, making it an ideal spot to escape the hustle and bustle and find peace.
