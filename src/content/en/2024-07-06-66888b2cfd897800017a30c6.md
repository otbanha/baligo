The Ministry of Finance's Customs Administration has announced new regulations effective June 26, 2024, increasing the duty-free allowance for incoming travelers' luggage from $645 to $1,129, allowing tourists to shop freely! But do you know which items are prohibited from being brought into Taiwan? Many people might not be aware, so to avoid having your items confiscated by customs, it's crucial to understand Taiwan's entry regulations to prevent unnecessary losses!

## Duty-Free Allowance Increased to $1,129

Effective June 26, the duty-free allowance for incoming travelers' luggage has been raised from $645 to $1,129, benefiting Taiwanese traveling abroad for summer shopping. Additionally, according to the current 'Regulations on the Inspection and Tax Exemption of Luggage Items for Incoming Travelers,' personal or household luggage items, excluding controlled substances, tobacco, and alcohol, are subject to a $645 duty-free allowance per person, with amounts exceeding this requiring declaration. However, this does not apply to those engaged in obvious commercial activities or frequent travelers with violation records.

## Taiwan Entry Restrictions

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c1d6ac4830b9bdc7166a59c589adf9fb.png)

### Food Items

- Raw milk, fresh milk: Not allowed, including raw milk roll cakes
- Fresh, refrigerated, frozen, vacuum-packed cooked meats: Not allowed
- Fresh truffles: Not allowed
- Dried meat, meat floss: Prohibited regardless of packaging, including vacuum packaging
- Foie gras: Glass jars not allowed, but metal packaging is permitted
- Canned goods, instant noodles: Allowed
- Mentaiko, salmon roe: Allowed
- Fish floss: Allowed
- Shelf-stable milk, powdered milk, cheese, yogurt: Allowed
- Soft canned goods: Allowed, if labeled as high-temperature sterilized
- Rice: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Tea leaves: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Dried shiitake mushrooms: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Dried chili peppers: Allowed, up to 6 kg
- Cooked peanuts, garlic: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Bird's nest: Allowed, with a market value not exceeding $645; amounts exceeding this must be declared

### Cosmetics and Skincare Products

Non-medicated cosmetics and skincare products such as face masks, eye creams, lip masks, toners, and serums (including hair dyes, perms, sunscreens, and whitening products) are limited to a maximum of 12 bottles per item, with a total of no more than 36 bottles. Items with a total value exceeding $645 USD must be declared for customs.

### Contact Lenses

If you exceed the following personal use limits, you must apply for 'Medical Devices for Personal Use Only' from the Food and Drug Administration of the Ministry of Health and Welfare and declare them to customs.

1. Daily disposable contact lenses: 60 pieces per prescription, limited to one brand and two prescriptions.
2. Orthokeratology lenses: no more than one pair.

### Tobacco and Alcohol

Each adult traveler aged 20 or older (starting January 1, 2023, the age limit for carrying alcohol is adjusted to 18, while the age limit for tobacco remains 20) is allowed to carry the following quantities of tobacco and alcohol products. These limits include all tobacco and alcohol products in both carry-on and checked luggage (including duty-free purchases, onboard purchases, overseas purchases, and items taken out and brought back into the country). For more details, please refer to the Taipei Customs Office's 'Tobacco and Alcohol' guidelines.

＊Tobacco:

- Duty-free allowance (no declaration required): 200 cigarettes or 25 cigars or 1 pound of tobacco.
- Limited quantity (requires declaration): 5 cartons (1,000 cigarettes) or 125 cigars or 5 pounds of tobacco.

(★Reminder: According to the Tobacco Hazards Prevention Act, the manufacture, import, sale, supply, display, advertising, and use of all e-cigarettes and heated tobacco products that have not passed health risk assessment reviews are completely prohibited. When shopping abroad, remember that regardless of quantity, these items cannot be imported or carried into the country, whether for gifting or personal use, and may result in penalties. They also cannot be used for profit. If discovered, violators may face fines of up to $161,290 USD under Article 26 of the Tobacco Hazards Prevention Act, and the goods will be confiscated and destroyed.)

►For related information, please visit the E-cigarette Control Section of the Health Promotion Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare.

*Alcohol:

- Duty-free allowance (no declaration required): Total of 1 liter
- Import limit (declaration required): Total of 5 liters (but for alcohol from Mainland China, which is not open for import, the limit is 1 liter)

►Explanation of duty-free allowance:

1. If not exceeding the duty-free allowance: No need to declare to customs.
2. If exceeding the duty-free allowance: Must declare to customs via the red channel and can be released after inspection and tax payment within the import limit.

►Explanation of import limit:

1. If not exceeding the import limit: After deducting the duty-free allowance, tax will be levied and the goods will be released.
2. If exceeding the import limit: Must attach an alcohol import business license, fill out an import declaration form according to Article 17 of the Customs Act, and complete the customs clearance in the name of the company, or return the excess portion.

### Medications

For regulations on bringing personal medications when entering the country, please refer to the following:

* Tablet or capsule supplements: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, packs, bags) per type, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, packs, bags). * Over-the-counter Western medicines: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks) per type, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks). * General prescription Western medicines: Limited based on whether a doctor's prescription or documentation is carried (injectable products require a prescription or documentation):

1. Without prescription: Limited to a 2-month supply.
2. With prescription: Not exceeding the prescribed reasonable amount, with a maximum of a 6-month supply.

(★ For all three categories above, you must apply for a "Personal Medication Import Permit" from the Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, and declare it to customs upon entry.)

* Controlled prescription Western medicines: Must carry a doctor's prescription or documentation, limited to treating the individual's condition, not exceeding the prescribed amount, with a maximum of a 6-month supply.

(★ You must apply for a "Declaration for Patients Carrying Controlled Drugs In and Out of the Republic of China" from the Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, and declare it to customs upon entry and exit.)

* Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM):

1. TCM herbs: Limited to 1 kg per type, with a total weight not exceeding 12 kg.
2. Oral TCM preparations: Limited to 12 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks) per type, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks), and a total weight not exceeding 1.2 kg.

(★ You must apply for a "Special Import Permit for TCM Herbs and Preparations for Personal Use" from the Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, and declare it to customs upon entry.)

### Cash and Jewelry

If carrying amounts exceeding the limit, please fill out the 'Customs Declaration Form of the Republic of China' and the 'Registration Form for Passengers or Service Personnel Carrying Cash, Securities, Gold, Items, etc. Inbound and Outbound' or use the 'Online Pre-Registration' at the Single Window for Trade and Port Services. Proceed through customs via the red channel after declaration.

* New Taiwan Dollar (NTD): Limit of USD$3,226 (NTD100,000). Excess must be returned (to be sealed by the passenger at customs and carried out upon departure).

* Chinese Yuan (CNY): Limit of USD$3,226 (CNY20,000). Excess must be returned (to be sealed by the passenger at customs and carried out upon departure).

* Foreign Currency: Equivalent to USD$10,000. Can be carried in full.

* Bearer Securities: Equivalent to USD$10,000. Can be carried in full.

* Gold: Equivalent to USD$20,000. For values exceeding USD$20,000, an 'Import Permit' must be applied for from the Bureau of Foreign Trade, Ministry of Economic Affairs (Tel: 02-23510271) before customs clearance.

* Diamonds/Gemstones/Platinum exceeding personal use: Equivalent to USD$500,000. For values exceeding USD$20,000, an 'Import Permit' must be applied for from the Bureau of Foreign Trade, Ministry of Economic Affairs (Tel: 02-23510271) before customs clearance.

## Prohibited Items

- Stun guns, flashlight stun guns
- Tear gas sprays, pepper sprays, anti-wolf sprays
- Small knives, folding knives, throwing stars, stun guns, etc.
- All types of narcotics, possession of drug raw materials, prohibited or manufactured for production or conversion into narcotics
- Marijuana, THC, or marijuana-related products (CBD requires an import permit)
- Counterfeit goods
- All types of meat products and meat floss (including ham, dried meat, sausages, etc.), beef, lamb, pork, duck, goose, etc. are prohibited
