According to the latest regulations from the Ministry of Finance's Customs Administration, starting June 26, 2024, the duty-free allowance for inbound passengers' luggage has been increased from NT$20,000 to NT$35,000, allowing tourists to shop to their heart's content! But do you know which items are prohibited from being brought into Taiwan? Many people might not be aware, so to avoid having your items confiscated by customs, it's crucial to pay attention to Taiwan's entry regulations to avoid unnecessary expenses!

## Duty-Free Allowance Increased to NT$35,000

Starting June 26, the duty-free allowance for inbound passengers' luggage has been raised from NT$20,000 to NT$35,000, benefiting those traveling abroad for summer shopping. Additionally, according to the current 'Regulations on the Inspection and Duty-Free Allowance of Inbound Passengers' Luggage,' passengers bringing personal or household items, excluding controlled items, tobacco, and alcohol, are entitled to a NT$20,000 duty-free allowance. Amounts exceeding this limit must be declared. However, this does not apply to those engaged in obvious commercial activities or frequent travelers with violation records.

★For the latest information, please refer to:

- [Bureau of Foreign Trade, Ministry of Economic Affairs <Detailed Export Restriction List>](https://fbfh.trade.gov.tw/fh/ap/downLoadListf.do)
- [Taipei Customs, Customs Administration, Ministry of Finance - Entry Declaration Notice](https://web.customs.gov.tw/taipei/multiplehtml/3392)

## Taiwan Entry Regulations and Restrictions

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c1d6ac4830b9bdc7166a59c589adf9fb.png)

### Food Items

- Raw milk, fresh milk: Not allowed, including raw milk roll cakes
- Fresh, refrigerated, frozen, vacuum-packed cooked meat: Not allowed
- Fresh truffles: Not allowed
- Dried meat, meat floss: Prohibited regardless of packaging, including vacuum-packed
- Foie gras: Glass jars not allowed, but metal packaging is permitted
- Canned food, instant noodles: Allowed
- Mentaiko, salmon roe: Allowed
- Fish floss: Allowed
- Long-life milk, powdered milk, cheese, yogurt: Allowed
- Soft canned food: Allowed, if labeled as high-temperature sterilized
- Rice: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Tea leaves: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Dried shiitake mushrooms: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Dried chili peppers: Allowed, up to 6 kg
- Cooked peanuts, garlic: Allowed, up to 1 kg
- Bird's nest: Allowed, market value must not exceed NT$20,000; exceeding amounts must be declared

### Cosmetics and Skincare Products

Non-medicated cosmetics and skincare items (including hair dyes, perms, sunscreens, and whitening products) are limited to 12 units per type, with a total maximum of 36 units. Any single item with a total value exceeding USD$645 must be declared for customs.

### Contact Lenses

If exceeding the following personal-use limits, you must apply for "Medical Devices for Personal Use Only" from the Food and Drug Administration of the Ministry of Health and Welfare and declare them to customs.

1. Daily disposable contact lenses: 60 lenses per prescription, limited to one brand and two prescriptions.
2. Corrective lenses: No more than one pair.

### Tobacco and Alcohol

Each adult traveler aged 20 or older (starting January 1, 2023, the age limit for carrying alcohol is adjusted to 18, while the age limit for tobacco remains 20) is allowed to carry the following quantities of tobacco and alcohol products. These quantities include all tobacco and alcohol products in both carry-on and checked luggage (including duty-free purchases, onboard purchases, overseas purchases, and items taken out and brought back into the country). For more details, please refer to the Taipei Customs Office of the Ministry of Finance's "Tobacco and Alcohol" section.

*Tobacco:

- Duty-free allowance (no declaration required): 200 cigarettes or 25 cigars or 1 pound of tobacco.
- Limited quantity (declaration required): 5 cartons (1,000 cigarettes) or 125 cigars or 5 pounds of tobacco.

(★Reminder: According to the Tobacco Hazards Prevention Act, the manufacture, import, sale, supply, display, advertising, and use of all e-cigarettes and heated tobacco products that have not passed health risk assessment reviews are completely prohibited. When shopping abroad, remember that regardless of quantity, these items cannot be imported or carried into the country, whether for gifts or personal use, and may result in penalties. They also cannot be used for profit. If caught, violators may face a maximum fine of USD$161,290 under Article 26 of the Tobacco Hazards Prevention Act, and the goods will not be returned and will be destroyed.)

►For related information, please visit the E-cigarette Control Section of the Health Promotion Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare.

＊Alcohol:

- Duty-free allowance (no declaration required): Total of 1 liter
- Limited quantity (declaration required): Total of 5 liters (but limited to 1 liter for alcohol from Mainland China, which is not open for import)

►Explanation of duty-free allowance:

1. If not exceeding the duty-free allowance: No need to declare to customs.
2. If exceeding the duty-free allowance: Must declare to customs via the red channel for clearance. Alcohol and tobacco products within the limited quantity can be released after inspection and tax payment.

►Explanation of limited quantity:

1. If not exceeding the limited quantity: After deducting the duty-free allowance, the goods will be taxed and released.
2. If exceeding the limited quantity: Must attach an alcohol import business license, fill out an import declaration form according to "Article 17 of the Customs Act", and complete customs clearance in the name of the company, or arrange for the excess portion to be returned.

### Medicines

For regulations regarding bringing personal medications upon entry, please refer to the following:

*Tablets and capsules: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, packs, bags) per type, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, packs, bags). *Over-the-counter Western medicines: Maximum of 12 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks) per type, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks). *General prescription Western medicines: Quantity limits based on whether a doctor's prescription or proof document is carried (injectable products must have a prescription or proof document):

1. Without carrying: Limited to a 2-month supply.
2. With carrying: Not exceeding the prescribed reasonable amount, with a maximum of 6 months' supply.

(★For all three items above, you must apply for a "Personal Medication Import Permit" from the Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, and declare it to customs upon entry.)

*Controlled prescription Western medicines: Must carry a doctor's prescription or proof document, limited to treatment for the individual, not exceeding the prescribed amount, with a maximum of 6 months' supply.

(★You must apply for a "Declaration Form for Patients Carrying Controlled Drugs In and Out of the Republic of China" from the Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, and declare it to customs upon entry and exit.)

*Traditional Chinese Medicine:

1. Herbal materials: Limited to 1 kg per type, with a total weight not exceeding 12 kg.
2. Oral TCM preparations: Limited to 12 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks) per dosage form, with a total not exceeding 36 bottles (boxes, cans, tubes, sticks), and a total weight not exceeding 1.2 kg.

(★You must apply for a "Special Import Permit for Traditional Chinese Medicine and Preparations for Personal Use" from the Food and Drug Administration, Ministry of Health and Welfare, and declare it to customs upon entry.)

### Cash and Jewelry

If carrying amounts exceeding the limit, please fill out the [Customs Declaration Form](https://taipei.customs.gov.tw/singlehtml/2851?cntId=cus2_179094_2851) and the [Registration Form for Carrying Cash, Securities, Gold, and Other Items by Passengers or Service Personnel](https://taipei.customs.gov.tw/singlehtml/2851?cntId=cus2_179096_2851), or use the [Online Pre-Declaration Registration](https://portal.sw.nat.gov.tw/APGW/MI50!goToMI50INDEX?request_locale=zh_TW) on the Single Window for Trade and Port Services. Proceed through the red channel to declare to customs before passing through.

*New Taiwan Dollars: Limit of USD$3,200. Excess amounts must be returned (to be stored by customs and carried out upon departure).

*Chinese Yuan: Limit of USD$650. Excess amounts must be returned (to be stored by customs and carried out upon departure).

*Foreign Currency: Equivalent to USD$10,000. Full amount allowed.

*Bearer Securities: Equivalent to USD$10,000. Full amount allowed.

*Gold: Equivalent to USD$20,000. For total values exceeding USD$20,000, an [Import Permit](https://www.trade.gov.tw/ProcessList/ProcessList.aspx?nodeID=4366&pid=3) must be applied for from the Bureau of Foreign Trade, Ministry of Economic Affairs (Tel: 02-23510271) before completing customs procedures.

*Diamonds/Gems/Platinum exceeding personal use: Equivalent to USD$500,000. For total values exceeding USD$20,000, an [Import Permit](https://www.trade.gov.tw/ProcessList/ProcessList.aspx?nodeID=4366&pid=3) must be applied for from the Bureau of Foreign Trade, Ministry of Economic Affairs (Tel: 02-23510271) before completing customs procedures.

## Prohibited Items

- Stun guns, flashlight-style stun guns
- Tear gas sprays, pepper sprays, personal defense sprays
- Small knives, folding knives, throwing darts, stun guns, etc.
- All types of narcotics, possession of drug raw materials, prohibited or used for manufacturing or converting into narcotics
- Marijuana, THC, or marijuana-related products (CBD requires an import permit)
- Counterfeit goods
- All types of meat products and meat floss (including ham, jerky, sausages, etc.), beef, lamb, pork, duck, goose, etc. are prohibited
