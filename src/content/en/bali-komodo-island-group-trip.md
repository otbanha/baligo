> The following content is adapted from fellow traveler [Sandra Yun](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/R9sqkkpWfnrB6Bzr/)'s travelogue:

This was a trip for six girls, chosen to take place in September 2023 in Bali and Komodo Island. We were all looking forward to a fulfilling holiday, but little did we know that the journey would be filled with surprises and challenges. Although it was only a short 6-day, 5-night trip and we didn't manage to complete all the planned activities, everyone had a great time. Unfortunately, on the fourth day, the entire group came down with gastroenteritis, which significantly reduced our stamina, and by 8 PM, we were already resting in bed. Despite this, the trip was still very rewarding for everyone. Below, we share some of our actual experiences, hoping they will be helpful for those planning to visit!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e63cd0875f45cb04b02c52f665b32f47.jpg)

## 1. Recommended Private Car Driver: Gede Arimbawa

What moved us most during this trip, besides the beautiful scenery, was our private car driver, Gede Arimbawa! He was not only thoughtful but also very kind and enthusiastic. He respected our itinerary and gave us practical advice at the right times, making our days in Bali very smooth. Whether it was reminding us about traffic times or helping us book restaurants and activities, his service was incredibly reassuring. We highly recommend such a great driver! 🥰

## 2. Airport Arrival: EVOA Fast Track

The entry process was quite smooth; we used the EVOA fast track and encountered no obstacles at all, and the customs didn't ask any questions. However, waiting for our luggage was a lengthy process, taking nearly an hour!

> Editor's Note: For the latest Bali entry procedures, please refer to [2025/08 Indonesia Bali Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card｜1 Minute to Understand the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Collecting Luggage Full Guide](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

## 3. Airport Wi-Fi is Terrible!

As soon as we arrived at Bali Airport, we noticed that the Wi-Fi was incredibly weak, likely due to the high number of users connected at the time. Fortunately, one of our travel companions had roaming service from Chunghwa Telecom, otherwise, we wouldn’t have known how to contact our driver. If you plan to purchase a local Telkomsel SIM card, it costs around USD$10 to USD$16 and includes 14GB of data, but the signal can still be weak in remote areas like Labuan Bajo.

Further reading: [Essential Guide to Bali Internet/SIM Cards](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/)

## 4. Traffic Conditions in Bali

During our trip, we found that traffic congestion in the busy areas was really severe! Especially between 1 PM and 7:30 PM, places like Uluwatu, Ubud, and Jimbaran experienced exaggerated traffic jams. Although Google Maps estimated short travel times, the actual journey often took 2.5 to 3 times longer. We recommend avoiding long-distance travel during these hours.

Editor’s note: [Don’t Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/)

## 5. Spa Booking Tips

If you plan to enjoy a Spa in Bali, it’s best to book in advance via WhatsApp or ask your driver to help with the booking. Especially for highly-rated Spa places, there’s a high chance of no availability if you go without a reservation. If you ask the driver to book, they might need to pay a deposit upfront, so consider carefully before canceling, or remember to return the deposit to the driver.

> Further Reading: [Top-Rated Spas in Bali: Organized by Area for Easy Reference](/blog/spa/)

## 6. Island Transportation Arrangements

Since our itinerary included island hopping, we only had three days in Bali. However, we chose to visit three different areas, resulting in spending every day stuck in traffic. If you want to explore more places, we recommend staying in the same area for 1-2 days to avoid wasting precious time on the road.

## 7. Big Price Differences for Water Activities

During our trip, we tried sea walking and discovered that buying tickets on-site was twice as expensive as getting them through our driver! The on-site menu price was around USD$58, but we only paid USD$26 through our driver. While we're not sure if this applies to all water activities, it seems that booking in advance or arranging through a driver can save you a lot.

## 8. Weather and Queuing at Rock Bar

If you're planning to visit Rock Bar, make sure to check the weather! On the day we went, it was a bit cloudy, so we didn't get to see the sunset, but this meant we hardly had to wait in line. We arrived at 6 PM and found plenty of empty seats, allowing us to enjoy the ocean view in peace. Even without the sunset, the stunning sea view was still mesmerizing.

## 9. Observations on Tipping

In Bali, we noticed that many restaurants would return the first tip we gave, only accepting it when we offered it again. However, tipping housekeeping staff, porters, or activity instructors always brought big smiles!

> Further Reading: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

## 10. Souvenirs: Bali Banana

If you're looking for souvenirs, Bali Banana is a great choice. There are several stores in the city, but be aware that while there are three branches at the airport, they are located at the domestic arrival/departure areas and the international departure area outside the terminal. If you plan to buy at the airport to take on your flight home, you'll need to walk to purchase them. It's better to buy them on your way to the airport on your last day in Kuta.

## 12. The Importance of Carrying Stomach Medicine

This bout of gastroenteritis taught us a valuable lesson: always pack stomach medicine when traveling! The first three days went smoothly, but on the fourth day, whether it was due to an infection or something unclean we ate, we all ended up vomiting and having diarrhea. Thankfully, we had prepared with medication, and everyone recovered their energy the next day.

> Editor's Note: These six ladies are professional nurses who brought their own prescription medication. For most people, over-the-counter medicine like what's mentioned in the video above should suffice.

## 13. Beware of Motorcycle Taxi Scams!

In Ubud, we encountered some motorcycle taxi drivers who tried to overcharge us. We had booked two rides, but they took us to the wrong locations and then charged us again when we arrived at the correct destinations. This is likely what other travelers have referred to as the 'white plate motorcycle scam.' Be sure to confirm the route and price beforehand to avoid such situations.
