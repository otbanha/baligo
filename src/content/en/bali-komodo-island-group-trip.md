> The following content is adapted from fellow traveler [Sandra Yun](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/R9sqkkpWfnrB6Bzr/)'s travelogue:

This trip was for six girls, chosen in September 2023 to visit Bali and Komodo Island. We were initially full of anticipation for a fulfilling holiday, but unexpectedly, the journey was filled with surprises and challenges. Although it was only a short 6-day 5-night trip and we didn't complete all the planned activities, everyone had a great time. Unfortunately, on the fourth day, we all came down with gastroenteritis, which significantly reduced our energy levels, and by 20:00, we were already resting in bed. Despite this, the trip was still very rewarding for everyone. Below, we share some of our actual experiences, hoping they will be helpful for those planning to visit!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e63cd0875f45cb04b02c52f665b32f47.jpg)

## 1. Recommended Private Driver: Gede Arimbawa

What moved us the most during this trip, besides the beautiful scenery, was our private driver, Gede Arimbawa! He was not only thoughtful but also very kind and enthusiastic. He respected our travel arrangements and gave practical advice at the right times, making our days in Bali go very smoothly. Whether it was reminding us about travel times or helping to book restaurants and activities, his service was incredibly reassuring. We highly recommend such a great driver! 🥰

## 2. Airport Arrival: EVOA Fast Track

The entry process was quite smooth; we used the EVOA fast track and encountered no obstacles at all, and the customs didn't ask any questions. However, waiting for our luggage was a lengthy process, taking nearly an hour!

> Editor's note: For the latest Bali entry procedures, please refer to [2025/08 Indonesia Bali Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card｜Understand the Latest Process in 1 Minute: From Landing to Customs to Collecting Luggage Full Guide](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

## 3. Airport Wi-Fi is Terrible!

As soon as we arrived at Bali Airport, we noticed the Wi-Fi was incredibly weak, likely due to the high number of people connected at the time. Fortunately, one of our travel companions had roaming service from Chunghwa Telecom, otherwise we wouldn't have known how to contact our driver. If you plan to purchase a local Telkomsel SIM card, it costs around USD$10 to USD$16 and includes 14GB of data, but the signal can still be weak in remote areas like Labuan Bajo.

Further reading: [Essential Bali Travel Guide: How to Get a Bali SIM Card/ESIM/Roaming?](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/)

## 4. Traffic Conditions in Bali

During our trip, we found that traffic congestion in busy areas was really severe! Especially between 1 PM and 7:30 PM, places like Uluwatu, Ubud, and Jimbaran experienced particularly exaggerated traffic jams. Although Google Maps estimates short travel times, the actual journey often takes 2.5 to 3 times longer. We recommend avoiding long-distance travel during these hours.

Editor's note: [Don't Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/)

## 5. Spa Booking Tips

If you plan to enjoy a Spa in Bali, it's best to book in advance via WhatsApp or ask your private driver to make the reservation. Especially for highly-rated Spa places, there's a high chance of no availability if you go without a reservation. If you ask the driver to book, they might need to pay a deposit upfront, so consider carefully before canceling, otherwise remember to return the deposit to the driver.

> Further Reading: [Essential Guide: Top-Rated Spas in Bali Organized by Area for Easier Searching](/blog/spa/)

## 6. Island Transportation Arrangement

Since our itinerary also included island hopping, we only had three days in Bali. However, we chose to visit three different areas, resulting in spending every day in traffic. If you want to explore more places, we recommend staying in the same area for 1-2 days to avoid wasting precious time on the road.

## 7. Wide Price Range for Water Activities

During our trip, we tried underwater walking and discovered that buying tickets on-site was twice as expensive as getting them through our driver! The on-site menu price was around USD$58, but we only paid USD$26 through our driver's arrangement. While we're not sure if this applies to all water activities, it seems that booking in advance or going through a driver can save you a lot.

## 8. Weather and Queue Situation at Rock Bar

If you're planning to visit Rock Bar, make sure to check the weather! On the day we went, it was a bit cloudy, so we didn't get to see the sunset, but it also meant we hardly had to wait in line. We arrived at 6 PM and found plenty of empty seats, allowing us to quietly enjoy the ocean view. Even without the sunset, the stunning sea view was still mesmerizing.

## 9. Observations on Tipping

In Bali, we noticed that many restaurants would return the first tip we gave, only accepting it when we offered it a second time. However, giving a small tip to housekeeping staff, porters, or activity instructors always brought big smiles!

> Further reading: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

## 10. Souvenirs: Bali Banana

If you're looking for souvenirs, Bali Banana is a great choice. There are several stores in the city, but note that while there are three branches at the airport, they are located at the domestic arrival/departure areas and the international departure area outside the airport. If you plan to buy at the airport before your flight, you'll need to walk to purchase them. It's better to buy them on your way to the airport on your last day in Kuta.

## 12. The Importance of Carrying Stomach Medicine

This bout of gastroenteritis really drove home the importance of always carrying stomach medicine when traveling! The first three days were fine, but on the fourth day, whether it was an infection or something unclean we ate, everyone ended up vomiting and having diarrhea. Thankfully, we had prepared medication, and everyone bounced back the next day.

> Editor's note: These six ladies are professional nurses who brought their own prescription medication. For most people, over-the-counter medicine like what's mentioned in the video above should suffice.

## 13. Beware of Motorcycle Taxi Scams!

In Ubud, we encountered some motorcycle taxi drivers who tried to overcharge us. We had booked two rides, but they took us to the wrong place and then charged us again when we reached the correct destination. This is likely the 'white plate motorcycle scam' that many travelers have mentioned. Always confirm the route and price beforehand.
