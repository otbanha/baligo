**Key Takeaways**: Real experience from a 6-person trip to Bali & Komodo over 6 days and 5 nights — ① **Heavy traffic in tourist areas** (especially Uluwatu/Ubud/Jimbaran from 1 PM to 7:30 PM; Google Maps estimates should be multiplied by 2.5–3x); ② **Airport Wi-Fi is weak** — recommend roaming or a local SIM card (Telkomsel around USD$10–16 with 14GB); ③ Baggage claim can take nearly an hour, longer than immigration itself; ④ The whole group got gastroenteritis at one point — bring stomach medicine.

> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Sandra Yun](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/R9sqkkpWfnrB6Bzr/):

This was a trip for the six of us girls, and we chose to visit Bali and Komodo in September 2023. We were initially looking forward to a fulfilling vacation, but little did we know the journey would be full of surprises and challenges. Although it was only a short 6-day, 5-night trip and we didn't manage to complete our entire itinerary, everyone had a fantastic time. Unfortunately, on day four, the whole group came down with gastroenteritis, which instantly drained our energy — by around 8 PM, we could only rest in bed. Even so, this trip left us with so many wonderful memories. Here are some of our real experiences, hoping they'll be helpful for travelers heading there soon!

## 1. Private Driver Recommendation: Gede Arimbawa

What touched us most on this trip, besides the stunning scenery, was our private driver Gede Arimbawa! He was not only thoughtful but also incredibly kind and warm-hearted. He respected our itinerary and offered practical advice at the right moments, making our days in Bali go smoothly. Whether it was reminding us about travel times or helping us book restaurants and activities, his service gave us total peace of mind. Highly recommend this wonderful driver! 🥰

## 2. Airport Arrival: EVOA Fast Track

The entry process was quite smooth — we used the EVOA fast track and encountered no issues at all, with customs officers not asking a single question. However, waiting for our luggage was a long ordeal, taking nearly an hour!

Editor's note: For the latest Bali entry procedures, please refer to [2025/08 Bali Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card | 1-Minute Guide: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/)

## 3. Airport Wi-Fi Is Terrible!

When we arrived at Bali's airport, we found the Wi-Fi was incredibly weak—probably because too many people were connected. Luckily, a friend in our group had Chunghwa Telecom's roaming service; otherwise, we wouldn't have known how to contact our driver. If you plan to buy a local Telkomsel SIM card, it costs around USD$10 to USD$16, including 14GB of data. However, in some remote areas (like the Labuan Bajo island-hopping zone), the signal is still weak.

Further reading: [【Essential for Bali Travel】How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card?](/en/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/)

## 4. Traffic in Bali

During this trip, we found that traffic jams in busy areas are really severe! Especially between 1 PM and 7:30 PM, places like Uluwatu, Ubud, and Jimbaran have particularly bad congestion. Although Google Maps estimates short travel times, the actual journey often takes 2.5 to 3 times longer. We recommend avoiding long-distance travel during these hours.

Editor's note: [Don't Trust Google Maps in Bali!](/en/blog/bali-google-maps-warning/)

## 5. Spa Booking Tips

If you plan to enjoy a spa in Bali, it's best to book in advance via WhatsApp or ask your private driver to help with reservations. Especially for highly-rated spas, showing up on the spot often means no availability. If you ask your driver to book, they may need to pay a deposit upfront, so think carefully before canceling—otherwise, remember to reimburse the driver.

Further reading: [【Essential】Top-Rated Bali Spa Recommendations by Area for Easy Searching](/en/blog/spa/)

## 6. Getting Around the Island

Since our trip also included island hopping, we only had three days on Bali. Yet we chose to visit three different areas, and ended up spending every day stuck in traffic. If you want to explore more spots, it's best to stay 1–2 days in the same area so you don't waste precious time on the road.

## 8. Weather and Queuing at Rock Bar

If you're planning to visit Rock Bar, keep an eye on the weather! The day we went was a bit overcast, so we didn't see the sunset, but it also meant we barely had to queue. We arrived at 6:00 PM and found plenty of empty seats, allowing us to quietly enjoy the ocean view. Even without the sunset, the stunning seascape was still mesmerizing.

## 9. Observations on Tipping

In Bali, we noticed that many restaurants would return the tip we gave the first time, only accepting it when we offered a second time. However, if you tip housekeeping staff, porters, or activity instructors, they are very happy to receive it!

> Read more: [Do You Need to Tip in Bali? How to Tip? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/en/blog/bali-tipping-guide-driver-spa/)

## 10. Souvenirs: Bali Banana

If you're looking for souvenirs, Bali Banana is a great choice. There are several shops in the city center. Note that while the airport has three branches, they are located at the domestic arrivals/departures area and the international departures area outside the terminal. If you plan to buy them at the airport to take on your flight home, you'll need to walk to get them! You can also purchase them on Kuta Street on your last day before heading to the airport.

## 12. The Importance of Bringing Stomach Medicine

This bout of gastroenteritis made us realize how crucial it is to pack stomach medicine when traveling! The first three days, we ate without any issues, but on the fourth day—whether from an infection or something we ate—everyone ended up with vomiting and diarrhea. Luckily, we had medication on hand, and by the next day, we were all back to normal.

https://youtu.be/GivvqCXp11A

Editor's note: These six ladies are professional healthcare workers who brought their own prescription medications. For most people, the over-the-counter medicines mentioned in the video above will suffice.

## 13. Beware of the Motorcycle Scam!

In Ubud, we encountered some motor taxi drivers who haggled prices. We booked two rides, but they took us to the wrong places and charged us again when we finally reached the correct destinations. This is likely the white-plate motorcycle scam that netizens often mention—be sure to confirm the route and price in advance.
