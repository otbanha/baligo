## Monkey Forest

Located in the heart of Ubud's old town, the Monkey Forest is a popular attraction that combines wild monkey populations with Hindu temple culture. After navigating through a maze of intricate alleyways, visitors are greeted by a lush green canopy formed by towering ancient trees, creating a natural shaded corridor flanked by various tropical plants, crafting an independent oasis of greenery. Walking down the entrance steps, one can immediately spot several wild monkeys frolicking in the trees.

These monkeys, not particularly large, are covered in black or brown fur, displaying remarkable agility and unpredictable movements that often captivate onlookers. The most delightful sight is watching mother monkeys with their babies, either clinging to their backs or peeking out from beneath them, their playful antics bringing smiles to faces. Occasionally, the monkeys engage in playful tussles on the branches, likely scrambling for food.

During their visit, they witnessed the intelligence and cunning of these monkeys firsthand. A black monkey suddenly leapt from a tree, accurately landing on a tourist and swiftly snatching a pair of valuable earrings before disappearing into the forest. This incident serves as a reminder for visitors to remain vigilant, as the monkeys have a keen interest in shiny jewelry.

Beyond the charming monkeys, the forest also houses a Hindu Sakralan temple, featuring various hand-carved stone statues and sculptures that are quite distinctive. Further along the path, visitors can find a small pond and fountain, beside which stands an old banyan tree, creating a serene atmosphere. Though small, the Monkey Forest encapsulates the unique natural and religious cultural essence of Ubud, making it a worthwhile stop to savor.

## Ubud Traditional & New Market

After leaving the tranquil Monkey Forest, they quickly walked to the Ubud Traditional Market. This long-standing market primarily sells local fresh produce and various daily necessities. Upon entering, visitors are greeted by a long corridor lined with stalls selling clothing, crafts, and other souvenir items. Inside the market, numerous food stalls and vendors offer fresh produce like fruits, vegetables, seafood, and meats. However, the market's open and aged structure exposes visitors to the harsh sun, making it somewhat stifling. To address this, a new market was built nearby, offering a more comfortable shopping environment. This new market is fully enclosed, bright, and well-ventilated, with stalls organized into themed sections like clothing, home decor, and handicrafts. Shopping here is a more pleasant and relaxed experience.

In the new market, they discovered several practical and interesting handicrafts, such as intricately woven fruit baskets with various patterns, which were quite unique. They also found beautiful wooden carvings with surf motifs, coconut carvings, and dyed fabric hangings, showcasing a wide array of exotic styles. Although prices were slightly higher than in the old market, they felt the superior craftsmanship justified the investment.

## Serayu Pottery

Leaving the bustling market behind, they headed to another renowned Ubud attraction, Serayu Pottery, which specializes in pottery making, painting, and various DIY activities, set in a tranquil and pleasant environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b41e6268aabe4e4c156ff8807840bee3.jpg)

Upon entering the compound, visitors are greeted by a natural oasis of intertwined green trees and vibrant pottery jars, creating an idyllic atmosphere. Next to the classroom stands an exhibition hall filled with exquisite ceramic pieces painted by students, serving as inspiring examples. Before the class begins, staff members provide a detailed introduction to the properties of clay and key considerations in pottery making, emphasizing the care needed from kneading to shaping. Then comes the hands-on session: participants select a simple pottery jar as their canvas, while the instructor demonstrates various decorating techniques like glaze dripping, color blending, and pattern outlining, making the process both fun and engaging. During the painting process, the host also shares the rich history and artistic essence of pottery, enriching the experience. After the class, the completed pieces require high-temperature firing to transform them into lasting works of art.

The staff will mail the finished pieces back in a few weeks, allowing participants to relive the pottery experience.

## Alas Harum Attraction

The highlight of this journey was visiting the Alas Harum theme attraction, a rare blend of traditional Balinese architecture, tropical recreational facilities, and coffee-making experiences. At the entrance, visitors are greeted by a grand Balinese-style wooden gate adorned with intricately carved shrines and statues, evoking the grandeur of a royal palace. Beyond the gate lies a garden landscape composed of Balinese architectural styles, exuding a serene and leisurely atmosphere. The attraction is divided into several themed areas:

1. Experience Zone: This area features various family-friendly interactive facilities, with the most eye-catching being the world's largest outdoor swing, standing nearly 9 meters tall. Additionally, there are thrilling activities like zip lines and rope courses, offering plenty of excitement.

2. Pool Zone: Comprising a large semi-open swimming pool, this area is perfect for cooling off, with lounge chairs by the poolside. A spacious poolside bar serves a variety of drinks and tropical fruits.

3. Exhibition Halls and Workshops: One section showcases local coffee craftsmanship and origins, where visitors can sample authentic civet coffee. Staff explain the entire process from harvesting to roasting and brewing, making it both educational and entertaining. Another area offers hands-on workshops for bamboo weaving, wood carving, and painting.

4. Performance Areas: The attraction includes themed performance zones featuring traditional art dances and percussion music, with daily shows for visitors to enjoy.

Alas Harum seamlessly blends traditional culture with modern entertainment, offering a diverse and enriching experience that leaves visitors captivated.
