## Monkey Forest

Nestled in the heart of Ubud, the Monkey Forest is a popular attraction that blends wild macaques with Hindu temple culture. After winding through a maze of narrow lanes, you're greeted by a sprawling canopy of lush greenery, where towering trees form a natural shaded corridor lined with tropical vegetation—a verdant oasis all its own. As you descend the entrance steps, you'll immediately spot several wild monkeys swinging and playing in the trees.

These monkeys are not overly large, with sleek black or brown fur, incredibly agile and quick, their movements unpredictable and captivating. The most delightful sight is watching mother monkeys with their babies—tiny ones clinging to their backs or peeking out from under their bellies, so lively and cute you can't help but smile. Occasionally, you'll see them tumbling and wrestling in the branches, a playful scramble for food.

During your walk, you'll also witness their intelligence and cunning. One black monkey suddenly leaped from a tree, landing precisely on a tourist, snatching a pair of valuable earrings from her ear before darting away. Clearly, these monkeys have a keen "interest" in shiny jewelry—a reminder for visitors to stay vigilant.

Beyond the charming monkeys, the forest also houses a Hindu temple, Pura Dalem Agung Padangtegal, featuring intricately carved stone statues and sculptures. Following the path deeper in, you'll find a small pond and fountain, with an ancient banyan tree standing by the water's edge, exuding a serene, natural ambiance. Though compact, the Monkey Forest encapsulates Ubud's unique blend of nature and religious culture—a stop worth savoring.

## Ubud Traditional & New Market

After escaping the hustle and bustle of the Monkey Forest, they soon walked to the Ubud Traditional Market. This long-standing market mainly sells fresh local ingredients and various daily necessities. At the entrance, a long corridor lined with stalls offers clothing, handicrafts, and other souvenirs. Stepping inside, you'll find rows of food stalls and vegetable vendors selling fresh produce like fruits, vegetables, seafood, and meat. However, due to the market's open and aging structure, the scorching sun beats down directly, making it feel stuffy and hot. As a result, a new market was built nearby to meet tourists' needs for a better shopping environment. This new market is fully enclosed, spacious, and well-ventilated, with stalls organized by theme, such as clothing, home decor, and handicrafts. Shopping here feels bright and relaxing, adding a layer of comfort.

At the new market, they discovered several practical and interesting handicrafts, such as woven fruit baskets crafted into various patterns, which were quite unique. They also saw many beautiful wooden carvings with surf motifs, coconut carvings, and dyed fabric hangings, offering an endless variety with an exotic flair. Although the prices were slightly higher than at the old market, they felt the craftsmanship was exquisite and well worth the purchase.

## Serayu Pottery Ceramic Studio

Leaving the bustling market behind, they headed to another famous Ubud attraction, Serayu Pottery, which specializes in DIY experiences like pottery making and ceramic painting in a serene and pleasant setting.

![Serayu Pottery 陶藝教室](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b41e6268aabe4e4c156ff8807840bee3.jpg)

Upon entering the complex, they were greeted by lush, intertwined trees and abundant ceramic pots, creating a natural, idyllic atmosphere. Next to the studio, there's an exhibition hall displaying many beautifully painted ceramic works by students, serving as reference samples. Before the class, the staff gave a detailed introduction to the properties of clay and precautions for crafting, emphasizing the care needed from kneading to shaping. Then it was time to get hands-on: they chose a plain ceramic pot as their canvas, and the teacher demonstrated various decorative techniques, such as glaze dripping, color blending, and pattern sketching, which was quite fun. During the painting process, the host also shared the rich history and artistic essence of pottery, enriching their experience. After the class, the finished pieces need to be fired at high temperatures to become permanent works.

The staff will mail the finished pieces back a few weeks later, allowing them to relive this pottery experience time and again.

## Alas Harum Attraction

The highlight of this trip was visiting Alas Harum, a themed attraction that brings together various traditional Balinese architectural styles, tropical recreational facilities, and coffee-making experiences—a rare amusement park. At the entrance, you're greeted by a grand wooden gate carved in Balinese style, with intricately carved shrines and towering statues exuding a majestic aura, as if you're approaching the gates of a palace. Passing through the gate, you're met with a garden landscape composed of Balinese architecture, radiating a serene and leisurely atmosphere. The park is divided into several themed areas:

1. Experience Zone: This area features various family-friendly interactive facilities, with the standout being the world's largest outdoor swing, towering nearly 9 meters high. In addition, there are thrilling attractions like zip lines and rope courses, offering plenty of excitement.

2. Pool Area: Comprising a large semi-open swimming pool with lounge chairs along the edge, it's perfect for cooling off and having fun. Next to the pool, there's a large water bar serving a variety of drinks and tropical fruits.

3. Exhibition Halls and Workshops: One section showcases local coffee craftsmanship and origins, where visitors can sample authentic civet coffee. Staff explain the entire process from picking and roasting to hand-brewing, making it lively and educational. Another area is a hands-on workshop offering experiences like bamboo weaving, wood carving, and painting with local artisans.

4. Performance Area: The park features performance zones with different themes, such as traditional dance and percussion music, with daily showtimes for visitors to enjoy.

Alas Harum perfectly blends traditional culture with modern entertainment, offering a diverse and rich experience that leaves you wanting to stay longer.

## Leisurely Moments

After four days of sightseeing, they often set aside some leisurely time to relax. The villa itself is a highlight—lounging by the pool or lying on an outdoor rocking chair, savoring the sun and gentle breeze, is already an unparalleled pleasure. Sometimes they stroll to the nearby streets, experiencing the slow-paced life of Ubud, and occasionally pick a boutique restaurant for a meal, enjoying a touch of luxury in simplicity.

Finally, they recommend staying in Ubud for at least four days, experiencing just one attraction per day without rushing, fully immersing yourself in the tranquil and charming atmosphere of this town and savoring the relaxed pace of Ubud life.
