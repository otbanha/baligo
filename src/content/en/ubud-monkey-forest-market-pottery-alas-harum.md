## Monkey Forest

Located in the heart of Ubud, the Monkey Forest is a popular attraction that blends wild monkey troops with Hindu temple culture. After winding through a maze of narrow lanes, you're greeted by a sprawling canopy of emerald green, where towering trees form a natural shaded corridor lined with tropical vegetation, creating a lush, secluded oasis. As you descend the entrance steps, you'll immediately spot several wild monkeys swinging and playing in the trees.

These monkeys are medium-sized, with black or brown long fur, incredibly agile and nimble, their movements unpredictable and fascinating to watch. The most delightful sight is seeing mother monkeys carrying their babies—some clinging to their backs, others peeking out from under their bellies, their lively and adorable antics sure to bring a smile. Occasionally, you'll see a troop suddenly tumbling and wrestling in the branches, likely scrambling for food.

During your walk, you'll also witness the monkeys' intelligence and cunning. One black monkey suddenly leaped from a tree, landing precisely on a tourist, snatching a pair of valuable earrings from her ear before darting away. Clearly, these monkeys have a keen "interest" in shiny jewelry—a reminder for visitors to stay vigilant.

Beyond the charming monkeys, the forest also houses a Hindu temple called Pura Dalem Agung Padangtegal, featuring intricately carved stone statues and sculptures that are quite distinctive. Following the path deeper in, you'll find a small pond and fountain, with an ancient banyan tree standing by the water, creating a serene and harmonious atmosphere. Though small, the Monkey Forest encapsulates Ubud's unique blend of nature and religious culture, making it a stop worth savoring.

## Ubud Traditional & New Market

After leaving the tranquil Monkey Forest, they quickly walked to the Ubud Traditional Market. This long-standing market primarily sells fresh local ingredients and various daily necessities. At the entrance, a long corridor lined with stalls offers clothing, handicrafts, and other souvenirs. Stepping inside, they found rows of food stalls and vegetable vendors selling fresh produce like fruits, vegetables, seafood, and meat. However, the market's open and aging layout leaves it exposed to the scorching sun and wind, making it feel somewhat stuffy. As a result, a new market was later built nearby to meet visitors' needs for a better shopping environment. This new market is fully enclosed, spacious, and well-ventilated, with stalls organized by theme, such as clothing, home decor, and handicrafts. Shopping here feels bright, relaxed, and far more comfortable.

At the new market, they discovered several practical and interesting handicrafts, such as woven fruit baskets crafted into various intricate patterns—quite unique. They also spotted many beautiful surf-themed wood carvings, coconut carvings, and dyed fabric hangings, offering an endless variety with an exotic flair. Although prices were slightly higher than at the old market, they felt the exquisite craftsmanship made them well worth the purchase.

## Serayu Pottery Art Class

Leaving the bustling market behind, they headed to another famous Ubud attraction, Serayu Pottery, known for its DIY experiences like pottery making and ceramic painting, all set in a serene and pleasant environment.

![Serayu Pottery Art Class](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b41e6268aabe4e4c156ff8807840bee3.jpg)

Upon entering the grounds, they were greeted by lush, intertwined trees and an array of pottery jars, creating a natural, idyllic atmosphere. Next to the classroom, an exhibition hall displayed beautifully painted ceramic works by students, serving as inspiration. Before the class, staff gave a detailed introduction to the properties of clay and key crafting tips, emphasizing care from kneading to shaping. Then it was time to get hands-on: they chose a plain clay pot as their canvas, while the instructor demonstrated various decorative techniques like glaze dripping, color blending, and pattern sketching—quite fascinating. During the painting process, the host also shared the rich history and artistic essence of pottery, enriching their experience. After the class, the finished pieces needed to be fired at high temperatures to become permanent works.

The staff would mail the finished pieces back a few weeks later, allowing them to relive this pottery experience time and again.

## Alas Harum Attraction

The highlight of this trip was visiting Alas Harum, a themed attraction that combines various traditional Balinese architectural styles, tropical recreational facilities, and coffee-making experiences—a rare amusement park. At the entrance, they saw a grand wooden gate carved in Balinese style, with intricately carved shrines and giant statues exuding majesty, like the gateway to a palace. Passing through the gate, they were greeted by a garden landscape composed of Balinese architecture, radiating a leisurely and carefree vibe. The park is divided into several themed areas:

1. Experience Zone: This area features a variety of family-friendly interactive facilities, with the most eye-catching being the world's largest giant outdoor swing, standing nearly 9 meters high. In addition, there are thrilling attractions like zip lines and rope courses, offering plenty of excitement and fun.

2. Pool Area: Comprising a large semi-open wading pool with loungers by the edge, it's perfect for cooling off and splashing around. Next to the pool is a large swim-up bar serving a range of drinks and tropical fruits.

3. Exhibition Halls & Workshops: One section showcases local coffee craftsmanship and origins, where visitors can sample authentic civet coffee. Staff explain the entire process from picking and roasting to pour-over brewing, making it both engaging and educational. Another area features hands-on workshops, offering experiences in bamboo weaving, wood carving, and painting with local artisans.

4. Performance Areas: The park also features themed performance zones, showcasing traditional dance and percussion music, with different showtimes daily for guests to enjoy.

Alas Harum perfectly blends traditional culture with modern entertainment, offering a rich and diverse experience that leaves visitors enchanted and reluctant to leave.
