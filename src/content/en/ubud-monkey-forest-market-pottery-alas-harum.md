## Monkey Forest

Located in the heart of Ubud's ancient town, the Monkey Forest is a popular attraction that combines wild monkeys with Hindu temple culture. After navigating through a maze of intricate alleyways, visitors are greeted by a stretch of lush greenery, formed by towering trees that create a natural shaded corridor, flanked by various tropical plants, crafting an independent oasis. Walking down the entrance stairs, one can immediately spot several wild monkeys frolicking in the trees.

These monkeys are not particularly large, with black or brown long fur, and are extremely agile and unpredictable, making them a delight to watch. The most amusing sight is seeing mother monkeys with their babies, either clinging to their backs or peeking out from under their bellies, their playful antics bringing smiles to faces. Occasionally, the monkeys can be seen tumbling together in the branches, likely scrambling for food.

During their walk, they also witnessed the intelligence and cunning of the monkeys. A black monkey suddenly leaped from a tree, accurately landing on a tourist, swiftly snatching a pair of valuable earrings from her ear before fleeing. It's clear that these monkeys have a keen 'interest' in shiny jewelry, reminding visitors to always stay alert.

Beyond the adorable monkeys, the forest also houses a Hindu temple, Sakralan, featuring various hand-carved stone statues and sculptures, each with its unique charm. Further along the path, a small pool and fountain can be seen, with an old banyan tree standing by, creating a serene atmosphere. Though small, the Monkey Forest encapsulates Ubud's unique natural and religious cultural essence, making it a stop worth savoring.

## Ubud Traditional & New Market

After leaving the tranquil Monkey Forest, they quickly walked to the Ubud Traditional Market. This is a long-standing traditional market mainly selling local fresh produce and various daily necessities. Upon entering, visitors are greeted by a long aisle lined with stalls selling clothing, crafts, and other souvenirs. Inside the market, shops and vendors selling fresh produce like vegetables, fruits, seafood, and meat are abundant. However, the market's open and aged structure exposes visitors to the harsh sun, making it somewhat stifling. To address this, a new market was built nearby, offering a better shopping environment. This new market is fully enclosed, bright, and well-ventilated, with vendors organized into themed sections like clothing, home decor, and handicrafts. Shopping here is a more comfortable and pleasant experience.

In the new market, they discovered several practical and interesting handicrafts, such as woven fruit baskets with intricate patterns, quite unique. They also saw many beautiful surf-themed wood carvings, coconut carvings, and dyed fabric hangings, showcasing a variety of exotic styles. Although prices are slightly higher than in the old market, they felt the quality of the crafts justified the purchase.

## Serayu Pottery

Leaving the bustling market behind, they headed to another famous Ubud attraction, Serayu Pottery, known for its pottery making, painting, and various DIY activities, set in a serene and pleasant environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b41e6268aabe4e4c156ff8807840bee3.jpg)

Upon entering the park, one is greeted by lush trees with intricate roots and vibrant pottery jars, creating a natural atmosphere reminiscent of a utopia. Next to the classroom, there is an exhibition hall filled with exquisite ceramic pieces painted by students, serving as reference examples. Before the class began, the staff provided a detailed introduction to the properties of clay and the precautions needed during the crafting process, from kneading to shaping, emphasizing the importance of care and attention. Then, it was time to get hands-on. They first selected a simple pottery jar as their canvas, and the teacher demonstrated various decorating techniques such as glaze dripping, color blending, and pattern outlining, which were quite fascinating. During the painting process, the host also shared the long history and artistic significance of pottery, enriching their knowledge. After the class, the completed works needed to be fired at high temperatures to become permanent pieces.

The staff will mail the finished pieces back in a few weeks, allowing participants to relive the pottery experience.

## Alas Harum Attraction

The highlight of this trip was visiting the Alas Harum themed attraction. This place combines various traditional Balinese architectural styles, tropical recreational facilities, and coffee-making experiences, making it a rare and diverse amusement park. At the entrance, visitors are greeted by a grand wooden gate adorned with intricate carvings of shrines and statues, exuding a majestic aura akin to entering a royal palace. Beyond the gate lies a garden landscape composed of Balinese architectural styles, exuding a relaxed and leisurely atmosphere. The park is divided into several themed areas:

1. Experience Zone: This area features various interactive facilities for families, with the most eye-catching being the world's largest outdoor swing, standing nearly 9 meters tall. Additionally, there are thrilling attractions like zip lines and rope courses, providing plenty of excitement.

2. Pool Area: Comprising a large semi-open swimming pool, complete with lounge chairs, it's perfect for cooling off. Beside the pool, there's a large water bar offering a variety of drinks and tropical fruits.

3. Exhibition Halls and Classrooms: One area showcases local coffee craftsmanship and origins, where visitors can taste authentic civet coffee. Staff explain the entire process from harvesting to roasting and brewing, making it both informative and engaging. Another area is a workshop offering hands-on experiences in bamboo weaving, wood carving, and painting.

4. Performance Area: The park also features different themed performance zones, such as traditional art dances and percussion music, with varying showtimes daily.

Alas Harum seamlessly blends traditional culture with modern entertainment, offering a rich and diverse experience that leaves visitors enchanted.
