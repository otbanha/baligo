## #10: Visit Uluwatu Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b953b86f0cdb3e5f162eec96ac861d2d.jpg)

Uluwatu Temple is considered one of Bali’s most iconic temples, standing alongside Tanah Lot and Besakih Temple. Perched 17 meters above the Indian Ocean on a steep cliff, Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali’s three most famous temples and a cornerstone of Balinese spirituality. Beyond its religious significance, visitors are drawn to its breathtaking ocean views. It’s also a prime spot to witness stunning sunsets. Be cautious, though—the monkeys here are notoriously mischievous and are known as some of the most unruly in Southeast Asia, so keep your glasses and jewelry secure.

## #9: Watch the Kecak Fire Dance

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8e1e2cb735b2c791a156b870f1022afc.jpg)

Visitors to Uluwatu Temple can also enjoy the famous Kecak Fire Dance. This unique Balinese dance doesn’t rely on musical instruments but instead features a chorus of men chanting rhythmically. Like all Balinese dances, it tells a story through movement. The best place to watch this mesmerizing performance is on the stage at Uluwatu Temple, where you can marvel at the sunset backdrop behind the dancers.

## 6th Place: Explore the Hidden Suluban Beach

Although Suluban Beach (also known as Blue Point Beach) isn't ideal for sunbathing, it's worth a visit just to see the mysterious cave!

__VID0__

This is a popular spot for surfers who use the cave to access the wild waves. You'll need to descend a long staircase and pass through a natural limestone cave to reach the shore. This unique beach is perfect for photo ops, and you can also watch surfers heading out to sea.

## 5th Place: Visit GWK Cultural Park

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6c3c61665afdc5719285358d3e0c4bd9.jpg)

Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park is Indonesia's largest cultural park, dedicated to the Hindu deity Wisnu and his mount, the mythical bird "Garuda". The park features Indonesia's tallest statue of Wisnu riding Garuda, a major highlight. In this cultural haven, you can also shop and enjoy Balinese dance performances.

## Fourth Place: Balangan Viewpoint

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc2912da85b54c0124095c6a7ac446b1.jpg)

Balangan Cliff Sunset Point is a captivating spot renowned for its stunning coastal views. It’s one of the best places to witness Bali’s breathtaking sunsets, where you can watch the sun gradually sink below the horizon, leaving behind unforgettable beauty. Additionally, you can enjoy hiking through the rocks, immersing yourself in nature, or simply relaxing on the beach.

This is also an excellent location for wedding photography, with many couples choosing Balangan Cliff Sunset Point to capture precious memories. In this romantic setting, you can seize beautiful moments with your loved one, creating lasting impressions of your love.

## Third Place: Visiting Green Bowl Beach

Green Bowl Beach is one of Uluwatu's most secluded beaches, yet it's a paradise for those seeking peace and solitude. The fine white sand, crystal-clear blue waters, and serene, cool atmosphere make it unforgettable. The beach stretches 30 meters, hidden beneath 75-meter-high cliffs. At the base of the cliffs, there are two large limestone caves for shelter. However, reaching this spot requires descending over 300 steep steps.

{{video2}}

The best time to visit is during low tide, which is also ideal for snorkeling. There are no beverages or meals sold on the beach, so remember to bring plenty of drinking water.

## Second Place: Tanah Barak Cliff Road/Blue Road

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7ae1b58d8b51712366caf4610c73688a.jpg)

Bali's Tanah Barak Cliff (also known as Pantai Batu Barak) is a hidden gem that has recently become a popular attraction. This newly opened beach features stunning blue waters and colorful rocks that make it unique. Besides the magnificent sea views, the road here is carved out of massive limestone cliffs, with the azure sea in the distance and towering rock walls on either side, creating a dramatic visual impact. You can also climb the rocks in the middle for better photo angles. Tanah Barak is about a 30-minute drive from the airport, and admission requires a ticket (currently Rp8,000 for adults, Rp3,000 for children). It's open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

## Number One: Surfing and Diving

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f237c3322eff064a02804f4e6cffcc22.jpg)

With its high-quality surf spots along the Uluwatu coastline, this area is a dream paradise for surfers and a major draw for visitors to Uluwatu. Nearby, there are numerous surf camps and instructional courses at beaches like Uluwatu Beach, Padang Padang Beach, Bingin Beach, Green Bowl Beach, and Suluban Beach, among many others. Take Uluwatu Beach, for example, which is hailed as the biggest attraction in Uluwatu. Besides the famous cliffside temple, it's also a popular spot for visitors to experience surfing and various water sports. You can comfortably watch the sunset on the beach or enjoy delicious meals at nearby restaurants, undoubtedly captivated by the beach's enchanting charm. Whether you're looking to fully immerse yourself in the thrill of surfing or simply enjoy the view, this is a must-visit destination.
