Every moment spent in Uluwatu feels like a slice of paradise, and we're not exaggerating! Uluwatu, Bali, is severely underrated, and today, we'll take you through its top 10 attractions and must-do activities.

## Number 10: Visit Uluwatu Temple

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b953b86f0cdb3e5f162eec96ac861d2d.jpg)

Uluwatu Temple is considered one of Bali's most famous temples, alongside Tanah Lot and Besakih Temple. Perched on a 17-meter-high cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean, it's one of Bali's three most renowned temples, embodying the cornerstone of Balinese spirituality. Beyond its religious significance, visitors are drawn by the breathtaking ocean views. You can also enjoy stunning sunset views here. Be cautious of the mischievous monkeys, known to be the most unruly in Southeast Asia, so remember to remove your glasses and jewelry.

## Number 9: Watch the Kecak Fire Dance

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8e1e2cb735b2c791a156b870f1022afc.jpg)

Visitors to Uluwatu Temple can also enjoy the famous Kecak Fire Dance. This unique Balinese dance doesn't rely on musical instruments but is accompanied by a large group of men chanting in unison. Like all Balinese dances, it tells a story through movement. The best place to watch the fire dance in Bali is on the stage at Uluwatu Temple, where you can admire the dancers against the backdrop of a stunning sunset.

## Number 8: Enjoy the Sunset at Single Fin

![Single Fin日落美景](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7913efd720450c575668b4f446c9e488.jpg)

Single Fin Sunset Views

Located on the southwest coastline, Uluwatu offers breathtaking sunset views over the Indian Ocean from its cliffs, making it one of Bali's best spots to watch the sunset. ## 6th Place: Explore the Hidden Suluban Beach

Although Suluban Beach (also known as Blue Point Beach) isn't ideal for sunbathing, it's worth a visit just to see the mysterious cave!

__VID0__

This is a popular spot for surfers who use the cave to access the wild waves. You'll need to descend a long staircase and pass through a natural limestone cave to reach the shore. This unique beach is perfect for photo ops, and you can also watch surfers heading out to sea.

## 5th Place: Visit GWK Cultural Park

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6c3c61665afdc5719285358d3e0c4bd9.jpg)

Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park is Indonesia's largest cultural park. It honors the Hindu god Wisnu and his mount, the mythical bird "Garuda." The park features Indonesia's tallest statue of Wisnu riding Garuda, which is a major highlight. In this cultural haven, you can also shop and enjoy Balinese dance performances.

## Fourth Place: Balangan Viewpoint

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc2912da85b54c0124095c6a7ac446b1.jpg)

Balangan Cliff Sunset Point is a captivating location renowned for its stunning coastal views. It is one of the best spots to witness Bali's spectacular sunsets, where you can watch the sun gradually sink below the horizon, leaving behind unforgettable scenery. Additionally, you can enjoy hiking through the rocks, immersing yourself in the beauty of nature, or simply relax on the beach.

This is also an excellent location for wedding photography, with many couples choosing Balangan Cliff Sunset Point to capture precious memories. In this romantic setting, you can seize beautiful moments with your loved one, creating lasting imprints of your love.

## Third Place: Visit Green Bowl Beach

Green Bowl Beach is one of Uluwatu's most secluded beaches, yet it's a haven for visitors seeking peace and solitude. The fine white sand, crystal-clear blue waters, and serene, cool atmosphere make it unforgettable. The beach stretches 30 meters, hidden beneath 75-meter-high cliffs. At the base of the cliffs, there are two large limestone caves for shelter. However, reaching this spot requires descending over 300 steep steps.

{{video2}}

The best time to visit is during low tide, which is also ideal for snorkeling. There are no vendors selling drinks or food on the beach, so remember to bring plenty of water.

## Second Place: Tanah Barak Cliff / Blue Road

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7ae1b58d8b51712366caf4610c73688a.jpg)

Bali's Tanah Barak Cliff (also known as Pantai Batu Barak) is a hidden gem that has recently become a popular attraction. This newly opened beach features stunning blue waters and colorful rocks that make it unique. Besides the magnificent sea views, the road here is carved out of massive limestone cliffs, with the azure sea in the distance and towering cliffs on both sides of the road creating a dramatic visual impact. You can also climb the rocks in the middle for better photo angles. Tanah Barak is about a 30-minute drive from the airport, and admission requires a ticket (currently Rp8,000 for adults and Rp3,000 for children). It's open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

## Number One: Surfing and Diving

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f237c3322eff064a02804f4e6cffcc22.jpg)

With its excellent surf spots along the Uluwatu coastline, this area is a dream destination for surfers and a major draw for visitors. There are numerous surf camps and lesson centers nearby, such as Uluwatu Beach, Padang Padang Beach, Bingin Beach, Green Bowl Beach, and Suluban Beach, among many others. Take Uluwatu Beach, for example—it's considered the biggest attraction in Uluwatu. Besides the famous cliffside temple, it's also a popular spot for surfing and various water activities. You can comfortably watch the sunset on the beach or enjoy delicious meals at nearby restaurants, undoubtedly captivated by the beach's enchanting charm. Whether you're looking to experience the thrill of surfing or simply want to relax and watch, this is a must-visit destination.
