## Accommodation

We chose two amazing villas for this trip, marking the first time our entire family stayed in a villa together. The kids were thrilled and excited about this unique accommodation experience.

### Seminyak Accommodation

We stayed at The Dusun Villa, which boasts an excellent location near Jalan Kayu Aya Street (Editor's note: What's special about this street? Check out [Jie's introduction](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/oz_QUWvCK_8)).

The pool was clean and spacious, and the villa offered a golf cart reservation service to Seminyak Square, which was very convenient for families with children. The villa's interior design was elegant and comfortable, featuring a spacious living room and outdoor dining area. The kids could play freely in the garden while parents enjoyed a relaxing time.

> ▶[Check availability, room types, and prices for The Dusun Villa](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=64314)

The Dusun Villa is featured in our article '[22 Stunning Bali Villas in Seminyak](/blog/2024-04-04-660e6e92fd89780001e6047e/)'

### ****Ubud Accommodation****

We stayed at Tanah Gajah, a Resort by Hadiprana

Here, you can book to watch the Kecak Dance without having to jostle through crowds. The stay includes a lavish afternoon tea and breakfast. The resort has a relaxed atmosphere, and the staff are warm and friendly, especially towards children. The entire resort is nestled among rice fields and lotus ponds, offering breathtaking views that make you feel like you're in a paradise.

## Tips

- ****Pre-trip research on tipping culture:****
- - Daily private driver: IDR 50,000~100,000
  - Luggage: IDR 10,000/50,000 (approx. USD$0.65/3)
  - Bedside/restaurant: IDR 10,000/20,000 (approx. USD$0.65/1)
  - Pre-tip 10% for massages
  - "KEEP THE CHANGE": No need for change, as locals often struggle to provide change for amounts below IDR 10,000.

- ****Villa accommodation recommendation:**** If you have kids who love playing in the water, we strongly recommend staying in a Villa. You can ask your private driver to arrange for large inflatables from local vendors or book through Klook—the kids will have a blast.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a1dc699ccad5ec44b57b56c8328810f8.jpg)

> Klook [Large Inflatable Rental Service](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=775806&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Factivity%2F4837-pool-float-rental-bali%2F)

- ****Transportation tip:**** July and August are peak seasons, and traffic jams are common after 11:30. We recommend planning no more than 2-3 attractions per day for a fulfilling experience.
- ****Nusa Penida tour experience:**** Although the roads are bumpy, the kids treated it like a bumper car ride, making it feel especially fun. Since our kids are all elementary school age, we didn’t participate in snorkeling activities, but even a day trip to the west coast was well worth it.

The local culture and charm of Bali are truly captivating, whether it’s the temples, water activities, or natural landscapes—each holds a unique allure. This trip gave us a deeper understanding and appreciation of Bali, and we look forward to visiting again!
