Recently, the new 182-meter glass elevator project on Nusa Penida's cliffs has attracted significant attention. This project not only alters the local scenery but has also ignited intense debate among residents and visitors. This article provides a comprehensive overview of the controversial project's background, design, economic impact, and environmental concerns.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_505b89f8f349d60e5f1110515d942a6d.jpg)

## Background of the Nusa Penida Cliff Elevator Project

### Overview

The cliff elevator project at Kelingking Beach on Nusa Penida was first proposed in 2023, aiming to enhance the tourist experience by providing direct access from the cliff top to the famous Kelingking Beach.

### Project Announcement

The project was publicly announced in 2023, immediately drawing significant attention and sparking both support and opposition from various stakeholders.

## Design and Functionality of the Cliff Elevator

### The 182-Meter Glass Elevator

The project plans to construct a 182-meter-high glass elevator shaft directly connecting the cliff-top viewpoint with Kelingking Beach.

### Viewing Platform Design

The design proposal includes multiple viewing platforms and walkways along the elevator shaft, allowing visitors to enjoy the magnificent ocean views from various heights.

## Reasons Supporting Economic and Tourism Development

### Support from Local Leaders

Many local leaders support this project, believing it will boost economic development, create jobs, and enhance tourism.

### Economic and Employment Opportunities

This project is expected to generate numerous job opportunities for local residents and attract more tourists, further stimulating the local economy.

## Environmental Impact and Local Community Concerns

### Opposition from Environmental Groups

Environmental groups and many local residents are concerned that this project may cause irreversible damage to the environment, including impacts on cliff ecosystems and local wildlife.

### Recent Environmental Disaster Cases

The recent construction landslide incident at Pemutih Peactu Beach in Uluwatu has raised concerns about potential similar issues with the Nusa Penida Cliff Elevator project.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4d35f44916ca506e943964111b19c803.png)

## Project Commencement and Future Development

### Groundbreaking Ceremony

The project officially broke ground at the end of 2023, with regional leaders, local communities, and private investors attending the ceremony.

### Project Construction Timeline

Construction has now officially begun and is expected to be completed and open to the public within the next few years.

## The Role of Chinese Investment Companies

### Investment and Construction Team

This project is jointly developed by Chinese investment company PT Bina Nusa Properti (PT BNP) and the local Karang Dawa Traditional Banjar, with at least 50% of the construction workforce sourced from local labor.

### Local Collaboration

PT BNP emphasizes collaboration with local communities and commits to adhering to strict environmental standards during construction.

## Government Oversight and Regulatory Compliance

### Statement from the Bali Tourism Board

In 2023, Tjok Bagus Pemayun, Head of the Bali Tourism Board, expressed hope that the Klungkung Regency government would strictly oversee the project's construction process to ensure compliance with all regulations.

### Oversight Measures

The government emphasized that stringent oversight measures would be implemented to ensure the project does not negatively impact Bali's image.

## Current Controversies in Tourism Development

### Spatial Planning Issues

In recent weeks, an increasing number of leaders and tourism stakeholders have expressed concerns about overdevelopment and spatial planning issues.

### Voices from the Local Community

Many local residents believe that excessive development could harm the area's natural beauty and traditional way of life.

## Other New Projects

### Construction of a New Residential Area

Recently, a new residential development project near Kelingking Beach has also begun, sparking public attention and controversy.

### Development Permits and Land Use

The construction permit for this residential area was first submitted in 2019. Land clearing and preliminary landscaping have already been completed, with groundbreaking expected in the coming weeks.
