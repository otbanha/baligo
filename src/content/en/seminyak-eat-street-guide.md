Seminyak's **Jalan Kayu Aya** is better known by its catchier name—**Eat Street**. Along this two-kilometer stretch, over 50 restaurants, bars, and cafes are densely packed, offering everything from Aussie brunch and French pastries to Latin American tacos, jazz cocktail bars, champagne lounges, and nightclubs—essentially a global culinary tour.

This article organizes Eat Street's establishments **by category**, based on actual Google reviews, complete with brief descriptions of each spot. Paired with Google Maps, it helps you find your perfect match without endless scrolling.

# ☕ Brunch & Cafes

This is Eat Street's most competitive category and Seminyak's favorite dining style among foreign visitors.

***

## 🥇 Sisterfields

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1778075395701-sisterfields-reviews.jpg)

**Address:** Jl. Kayu Cendana No. 7

The most renowned Aussie-style brunch cafe on Eat Street, hands down. Always packed from opening, with weekend waits of 20–45 minutes. Bright, spacious interiors with ample natural light evoke Melbourne cafe vibes.

Must-try dishes: **Truffle Scrambled Eggs**, **Bacon Benedict**, **Acai Berry Bowl**, and hearty burgers. Coffee is supplied by Sibling Espresso, consistently high quality. Slightly pricier than nearby competitors, but worth it for the reliable flavors and friendly service. Perfect for travelers looking to indulge in a proper brunch.

***

<!-- shuffle-start -->

## Revolver Espresso

**Address:** Jl. Kayu Aya No. Gang 51 (turn into the alley from the main street)

Established in 2011, it's considered one of the pioneers of specialty coffee in Bali. Hidden in a small alley, walk through the dark entrance to find a retro industrial decor with a cowboy gun theme - dark wood, exposed brick walls, and leather seats.

They roast their own coffee beans, excelling in Flat White and pour-over coffee. All-day breakfast is also available. Popular menu items include **Birria Toastie** and chocolate hot cross buns. A must-visit for coffee lovers, but arrive early to secure a seat.

***

## Monsieur Spoon

**Address:** Jl. Kayu Cendana No. 2

Founded in 2012 by two Parisian cousins, this French bakery and cafe has multiple branches in Bali. The Seminyak location is next to Sisterfields, often overlooked by passersby, making it an underrated hidden gem.

Their signature is the butter croissant, and the **Truffle Scrambled Egg Croissant Sandwich** is highly praised for its truffle aroma. More affordable than Sisterfields or Revolver, and rarely requires waiting. Highly recommended for lovers of French pastries.

***

## Chandi

One of the oldest pan-Asian restaurants on Eat Street, featuring organic ingredients and local Balinese produce. Signature dishes include **Miso Caramelised Butterfish**, **Hot Stone Satay Platter**, and **Shrimp Satay with Tamarind Sauce**. # 🍕 Western & International Cuisine

***

<!-- shuffle-start -->

## Italian Job Bali

Specializing in Italian cuisine, their pizzas are the signature dish, and their pasta dishes also receive favorable reviews. Among the many options on Eat Street, it's a solid and reliable choice for Italian fare.

***

## Asparagus Seminyak

Asparagus is the restaurant attached to Amadea Resort, serving breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Their all-day menu emphasizes organic and locally sourced ingredients, with plenty of vegetarian/vegan options. Many reviews specifically highlight their **buffet breakfast** as one of the best in Seminyak—offering a wide range of Indonesian and Western dishes, made-to-order eggs, a pancake station, and fresh juices. The portions are generous, and the quality is consistently good, with some reviews even calling it the "breakfast champion of Seminyak." Evening features live music, and the staff receives excellent reviews (often praised by name, such as Febri, Lanang, and Rama). Overall, it offers great value for money on Eat Street.

***

## Sumak Turkish Cuisine

A rare Turkish cuisine restaurant in Bali, their kebabs and mezze platters are popular choices, offering authentic flavors that attract adventurous diners.

***

## Salazon Bali

Specializing in Spanish/Mediterranean flavors, the atmosphere is relaxed, with a wide selection of cocktails, making it a great spot for post-dinner drinks.

***

## Buzo Seminyak – Sushi, Pizza & Itameshi

A fusion of Japanese and Italian cuisine (Itameshi refers to Japanese-Italian fusion), offering diverse options like sushi and pizza, making it one of the creative culinary highlights on Eat Street.

***

## Thaikhun Bali

A chain Thai restaurant brand offering standardized flavors, perfect for travelers craving Thai food but unsure where to go. The vibrant green decor is eye-catching.

***

## Shawarma Beirut Seminyak

A Lebanese-style shawarma spot, standing out as a unique Middle Eastern cuisine option on Eat Street. The meat is juicy and portions are generous.

***

## Mykonos Greek Restaurant Bali

A Greek restaurant serving Mediterranean flavors like Tzatziki and grilled meat platters. The decor features the iconic Greek blue-and-white style, creating a bright and cheerful atmosphere.

## Corner House Bali

# 🍹 Bars & Nightlife

## 🥇 La Favela

**Address:** Jl. Laksamana Oberoi No. 177X

The most legendary nightlife landmark on Eat Street. Originally a small shop transformed in 2012, it blends elements of Brazilian favelas, Indonesian jungles, and London underground bars, featuring artificial waterfalls, ponds, arched bridges, and vintage bicycle collections—every corner tells a story.

During dinner hours (7:00 PM–11:00 PM), the focus is on Latin American cuisine, with tacos, tostadas, and Wagyu steak receiving high praise. After 11:00 PM, it transforms into a three-story nightclub, each floor with a different music style—from pop and hip-hop to reggaeton. The peak crowd arrives after midnight. Women usually enter for free, while men arriving after 9:00 PM pay IDR 200,000–250,000 (includes one drink).

***

Overall: **perfect atmosphere, decent cocktails, slightly inconsistent service**, but skipping it means you haven't truly experienced Seminyak.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1778075518821-red-carpet-100.jpg)

## 🥇 Red Carpet Champagne Bar

Opened in 2009, this is one of the oldest nightlife venues on Eat Street, boasting the largest champagne collection in all of Indonesia (over 100 varieties). The staff, dressed in retro uniforms, resemble 'champagne flight attendants.' The place plays nostalgic music videos from the 70s to 90s all night long, occasionally offering complimentary canapés. The cocktails are top-notch, with the **Espresso Martini** receiving endless praise. The atmosphere is extravagantly fun and completely unpretentious, making it a great spot for travelers aged 30 and above.

***

## Seken Bali | Jazz and Cocktails

Known for its live jazz performances paired with exquisite cocktails, this is the most uniquely charming small bar on Eat Street. Highly recommended for travelers looking to escape noisy dance floors and quietly enjoy music and fine drinks.

***

## Malverde Tequileria & Nightclub

A nightclub themed around tequila and Mexican cocktails, located right next to La Favela. It's a great alternative when La Favela gets too crowded. The atmosphere leans Latin American, with energetic music.

## KUUKI Rooftop Dining

One of the few restaurants on Eat Street with rooftop seating, offering a view of the street below while enjoying dinner or cocktails. The ambiance is particularly enchanting when the night breeze blows.

## Pool & Cafe Upaasha

## Nirvana Lounge Bali

# 📍 Practical Tips

- **Eat Street = Jalan Kayu Aya**, the most vibrant stretch runs from Seminyak Square to Coco Super Market.
- Eat Street gets extremely crowded on weekend evenings, **recommend dining before 7 PM**, head out for clubbing after 10 PM.
- La Favela and Red Carpet Champagne Bar are directly across the street from each other, you can visit both.
- Many restaurants accept reservations (especially Ginger Moon and Chandi), recommend booking in advance for weekends.
- **Parking** has always been a pain point on Eat Street, suggest using Gojek/Grab to get there.
