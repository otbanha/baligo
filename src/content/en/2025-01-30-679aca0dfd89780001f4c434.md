During air travel, managing carry-on luggage is a challenge every passenger faces. CiCi, a flight attendant with six years of experience at a U.S. airline, has observed and accumulated extensive practical knowledge on how passengers handle their carry-ons. Here, she shares some common mistakes and solutions to help you breeze through boarding and enjoy a comfortable flight.

## About the Author

- **Flight Attendant CiCi has six years of experience at a U.S. airline**  
  Over her six-year career, CiCi has witnessed countless passengers making mistakes with their carry-on luggage, which not only affected their travel experience but also inconvenienced other passengers and crew.  
- **Hands-on observation and accumulated practical experience**  
  Through years of observation and experience, CiCi has identified common issues and solutions to help passengers avoid these mistakes.

## Main Issue: Overweight Luggage

- **Most common mistake: Carrying overweight luggage that doesn’t fit in the overhead bin**  
  Many passengers overlook weight restrictions, resulting in luggage that’s too heavy to fit in the overhead bin.  
- **Consequences:**  
  - **Delays in boarding**: When passengers struggle to stow their luggage, it often delays the boarding process, affecting other travelers.  
  - **Increased workload and safety risks for flight attendants**: Flight attendants must assist with these bags, adding to their workload and potentially creating safety hazards.  
- **Suggested solutions:**  
  - **Wear bulky clothing like sweatshirts and boots**: Wearing heavier items can effectively reduce luggage weight.  
  - **Avoid packing large clothing in your suitcase**: Packing bulky clothes increases luggage size and weight; wear them or place them in checked baggage instead.

## Quantity Restrictions

- **Common airline policies:**  
  - **Limit of two carry-on items**: Most airlines allow passengers to bring two carry-on items, typically a small rolling suitcase and a handbag.  
- **Consequences of violation: Additional checked baggage fees**  
  If passengers exceed the limit, airlines may require them to check some items and charge extra fees.

## Item Placement Issues

- ****Common Mistake: Essential Items Not Properly Organized****  
  Many travelers fail to properly organize items needed during the flight when packing their luggage, leading to frequent opening of bags and causing inconvenience.
- ****Suggestions for Improvement:****
  - ****Place items needed during the flight (books, phone, headphones, etc.) in easily accessible locations****: It's recommended to keep these items in the outer compartments of your carry-on or suitcase for quick access.
  - ****Avoid burying frequently used items deep inside your luggage****: Stashing commonly used items deep within your suitcase makes them harder to reach. Keep them in easily accessible spots instead.

---

## V. Special Reminders

- ****The Importance of Bringing Food on Long Flights:****
  - ****Prepare for potential turbulence****: Turbulence is a common occurrence on long flights, and having some food on hand can help passengers maintain their energy during bumpy periods.
  - ****Prevent potential lack of meal service****: Sometimes flights may not provide meal service due to various reasons. Bringing food ensures passengers' basic dietary needs are met.
  - ****Ensure basic dietary needs****: Packing simple snacks like crackers or fruit can provide necessary energy boosts during the flight.

We hope these tips help you better manage your carry-on luggage and enjoy a pleasant flight experience.
