Taking from the group, giving back to the group. Just returned from Bali and wanted to share our travel experience. We experienced everything from immigration procedures and transportation to weather, various activities, and local cuisine. We hope these insights will help with your travel planning.

## Smooth Immigration

We opted for a visa on arrival and flew to Bali with China Airlines. After landing, the immigration process went very smoothly with almost no waiting time. The immigration officers were efficient and friendly. We paid the visa fee in USD and received change in Indonesian Rupiah—a simple and straightforward process.

## Bali's Traffic Situation

Bali's traffic is notoriously bad, with congestion almost all day long. Therefore, it's advisable not to pack your itinerary too tightly, as it can be exhausting. We prepared thoroughly before the trip but still had to flexibly adjust our schedule to avoid travel delays affecting our experience.

## Comfortable Weather

The weather in Bali is very comfortable. Although the sun is intense, it doesn't feel as harsh on the skin as Taiwan's heat. Nonetheless, sunburn is still a risk, so it's essential to take sun protection measures, including applying sunscreen, wearing protective clothing, and using hats.

## Depth of Resort Pools

Resort pools in Bali are typically deeper compared to those in Taiwan, which is a noticeable difference. Therefore, if traveling with children, parents need to pay extra attention to their safety. It's recommended to bring arm floats or other buoyancy devices to ensure the safety of kids while they play in the water.

## Hard Rock Hotel's Kids Club

Hard Rock Hotel is truly ideal for families with children. One day, we enrolled our kids in the full-day Kids Club activities while we adults went shopping at the nearby mall and enjoyed a spa session. This not only gave us a peaceful day but also ensured our kids had a blast. We stayed at the Hard Rock Hotel in Kuta for the last three days. The Kids Club at Hard Rock is very popular, so I recommend booking in advance. The hotel provides WhatsApp contact details, and you can also make reservations via email. If your plans are confirmed, booking early is safer as cancellations are allowed before 10 AM. I was lucky to secure the last spot when I booked upon arrival at the hotel.

## Delicious Food

The food in Bali is truly delightful, and we didn't encounter any disappointments this time. However, since our kids are sensitive to spicy food, we recommend asking the staff about the spiciness of dishes to ensure a pleasant dining experience for the children. There are many restaurants near Hard Rock Hotel, and we randomly chose one without any issues. Within walking distance, there are two malls and numerous dining options, including Jamie Oliver's restaurant, KFC, and McDonald's at the back entrance. I genuinely think the fries at KFC in Bali are exceptionally good.

## Thrilling Rafting Experience

![泛舟、OMMA Day Club（Fish Lee提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_28669c7a5c5894f46e25196b63d37e90.jpg)

Whitewater Rafting, OMMA Day Club (provided by Fish Lee)

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Whitewater Rafting Guide (FAQ)](/blog/2025-03-28-67e62aa8fd89780001888620/)

This time, we also experienced whitewater rafting in Ubud, which was incredibly fun. Since there were many options, we directly asked our private driver to book it for us, and he found a reputable operator. We recommend choosing the full 2-hour experience rather than the 1-hour one for a more complete adventure. We had two younger children, one just over 5 years old and the other 7, and they both had a great time. The rafting activity lasted about 2 hours. We departed from our Villa in Ubud at 9 AM, and a simple lunch was included. We finished around 1 PM. Getting to the rafting spot required walking down a long set of stairs, but our kids managed it slowly without any difficulty.

## Fun Braiding Experience

The braiding experience in Bali is incredibly fun and really captures that vacation vibe. I noticed many foreign children with colorful braids, so I decided to take my kids for the experience too—it was quite special.

We got our braids done on the street by Jamie Oliver's restaurant near Kuta Beach. This street is lined with many Spa shops offering braiding services, and they display samples of braids at their entrances. I initially thought they would use a lot of hair gel to secure the braids, but they didn't. When it came time to remove the braids, we just had to cut off the extensions, which was quite convenient.

## Convenient Private Car Service

This time, we opted for a private car service (driver + Chinese-speaking guide). We hired a guide who speaks Chinese, which not only solved our language barrier but also made our trip more relaxing and enjoyable. For instance, we adults enjoyed three Spa sessions. Once, the guide looked after our kids at the Villa, and another time, he took the kids out for a meal, allowing us to fully relax during our Spa time.
