## Smooth Arrival

We opted for a visa on arrival, flying with China Airlines to Bali. Upon landing, the immigration process was smooth with almost no waiting time. The immigration officers were efficient and friendly. We paid the visa fee in USD and received change in Indonesian Rupiah, making the process straightforward and hassle-free.

## Bali's Traffic Conditions

Traffic in Bali is notoriously bad, with congestion almost all day. Therefore, it's advisable not to pack your schedule too tightly, as it can be exhausting. Despite thorough preparation, we still needed to flexibly adjust our itinerary to avoid travel delays impacting our experience.

## Comfortable Weather

The weather in Bali is very comfortable. Although the sun is intense, it doesn't feel as harsh on the skin as the heat in Taiwan. Nonetheless, sunburn is still a risk, so it's essential to take sun protection measures, including applying sunscreen, wearing protective clothing, and hats.

## Resort Pool Depths

Resort pools in Bali are typically deeper than those in Taiwan, presenting a noticeable difference. Therefore, if traveling with children, parents should pay special attention to their safety. It is advisable to bring arm floats or other buoyancy devices to ensure the safety of children while playing in the water.

## Kids Club at Hard Rock Hotel

Hard Rock Hotel is really suitable for families with children. One day, we let the kids participate in the Kids Club activities for the whole day, while we adults went shopping at the nearby mall and enjoyed a SPA. This not only gave us a quiet day but also made the kids very happy. We stayed at Hard Rock Hotel Kuta for the last three days. The Kids Club at Hard Rock is very popular, so it's recommended to make reservations in advance. The hotel provides WhatsApp contact information, and you can also make reservations via email. If your itinerary is confirmed, booking in advance is safer because they allow cancellations before 10 AM. I made the reservation upon arrival at the hotel and was lucky to get the last spot.

## Delicious Food

The food in Bali is truly delicious, and we didn't encounter any disappointments this time. However, since children are sensitive to spicy food, it's advisable to ask the staff about the spiciness of dishes to ensure a pleasant dining experience for the kids. There are many restaurants near Hard Rock Hotel, and we randomly chose one without any issues. Within walking distance, there are two department stores and numerous dining options, including Jamie Oliver's restaurant, KFC, and McDonald's at the back entrance. I genuinely think the fries at KFC in Bali are exceptionally good.

## Exciting Rafting Experience

![泛舟、OMMA Day Club（Fish Lee提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_28669c7a5c5894f46e25196b63d37e90.jpg)

Rafting, OMMA Day Club (provided by Fish Lee)

*****Quick Reference:***** [Bali Rafting Guide (Frequently Asked Questions)](/blog/2025-03-28-67e22aa8fd89780001888620/)

This time, we also experienced rafting in Ubud, which was incredibly fun. Since there were many options, we asked our private driver to book for us, and he found a reputable operator. We recommend choosing the full 2-hour experience rather than the 1-hour option for a more complete adventure. We have two younger children, one just over 5 years old and the other 7, and they both had a great time. The rafting activity lasted about 2 hours. We departed from our villa in Ubud at 9 AM, and a simple lunch was included. We finished around 1 PM. Getting to the rafting site involves walking down a long set of stairs, but even our kids managed it slowly without difficulty.

## Fun Braiding Experience

The braiding experience in Bali is incredibly fun and really sets the vacation mood. I noticed many foreign kids with colorful braids, so I decided to take my children to try it out—it was quite special.

We got our braids done on the street by Jamie Oliver’s restaurant in Kuta Beach. This street is lined with numerous spa shops offering braiding services, and they display sample braids at their entrances. I initially thought they’d use a lot of hair gel to secure the braids, but they didn’t. Removing the braids was easy—just cut off the fake hair part, which was very convenient.
