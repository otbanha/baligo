Taking from the group, giving back to the group. Just returned from Bali, sharing our travel experience with everyone. During this trip, we experienced everything from immigration and transportation to weather, activities, and cuisine. We hope these insights will help with your travel planning.

## Smooth Immigration

We opted for a visa on arrival and flew to Bali with China Airlines. After landing, the immigration process went very smoothly with almost no waiting time. The immigration officers were efficient and friendly. We paid the visa fee in USD and received change in Indonesian Rupiah—the process was straightforward and hassle-free.

## Bali's Traffic Situation

Traffic in Bali is notoriously bad, with congestion throughout the day. Therefore, it’s advisable not to overpack your itinerary, as it can be exhausting. We prepared thoroughly before the trip, but still had to flexibly adjust our plans to avoid travel delays affecting our experience.

## Comfortable Weather

The weather in Bali is very pleasant. While the sun is intense, it doesn’t feel as harsh on the skin as Taiwan’s heat. However, sunburn is still a risk, so it’s essential to take sun protection measures, including applying sunscreen, wearing protective clothing, and using hats.

## Depth of Resort Pools

Resort pools in Bali are typically deeper compared to those in Taiwan, presenting a noticeable difference. Therefore, if traveling with children, parents should pay extra attention to their safety. It's advisable to bring arm floats or other buoyancy devices to ensure the safety of children while they play in the water.

## Hard Rock Hotel's Kids Club

The Hard Rock Hotel is truly ideal for families with children. One day, we enrolled our kids in the all-day Kids Club activities while we adults went shopping at the nearby mall and enjoyed a spa session. This not only gave us a peaceful day but also ensured the kids had a fantastic time. We stayed at the Hard Rock Hotel in Kuta for the last three days. The Kids Club at Hard Rock is very popular, so I recommend booking in advance. The hotel provides a WhatsApp contact option, and you can also make reservations via email. If your itinerary is confirmed, booking early is safer since cancellations are allowed before 10 AM. I made the reservation upon arrival at the hotel and was lucky to secure the last available spot.

## Delicious Food

The food in Bali is truly delightful, and we didn't encounter any disappointments this time. However, since our kids are sensitive to spicy food, we recommend asking the staff about the spiciness of dishes to ensure a pleasant dining experience for the children. There are many restaurants near the Hard Rock Hotel, and we randomly chose one without any issues. Within walking distance, there are two malls and numerous dining options, including Jamie Oliver's restaurant, KFC, and McDonald's at the back entrance. I genuinely think the fries at Bali's KFC are exceptionally good.

We departed from our villa in Ubud at 9 AM, and a simple lunch was included. We finished around 1 PM. Getting to the rafting site requires walking down a long staircase, but even our kids managed it slowly without difficulty.

## Fun Hair Braiding Experience

The hair braiding experience in Bali was very fun and gave a strong vacation vibe. I saw many foreign kids with braids in various colors, so I took my children to try it out too—it was quite special.

We got our hair braided on the street by Jamie Oliver's restaurant near Kuta Beach. There are many spa shops along this street offering braiding services, with samples displayed at their entrances. I thought they would use a lot of hair gel to secure the braids, but they didn't. Removing the braids was convenient—just cut off the fake hair part.

## Convenient Private Car Service

This time we opted for a private car service (driver + Chinese-speaking guide), and we hired a guide who speaks Chinese. Not only did this solve our language barrier issues, but it also made our trip more relaxed and enjoyable. For instance, we adults indulged in three spa sessions during this trip. Once, our guide helped look after the kids at our villa, and another time, he took them out for snacks, allowing us adults to fully unwind and enjoy our spa time.
