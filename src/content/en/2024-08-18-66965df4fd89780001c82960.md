## Smooth Immigration

We opted for a visa on arrival and flew to Bali with China Airlines. After landing, the immigration process was very smooth with almost no waiting time. The immigration officers were highly efficient and friendly. We paid the visa fee in USD and received change in Indonesian Rupiah, making the process simple and straightforward.

## Bali's Traffic Situation

Bali's traffic is notoriously bad, with congestion almost all day. Therefore, it's advisable not to pack your itinerary too tightly, as it can be exhausting. We prepared thoroughly before our trip but still needed to flexibly adjust our schedule to avoid travel delays affecting our experience.

## Comfortable Weather

Bali's weather is very comfortable. Although the sun is intense, it doesn't feel as harsh on the skin as Taiwan's heat. Nevertheless, sunburn is still a risk, so it's essential to take sun protection measures, including applying sunscreen, wearing sun-protective clothing, and hats.

## Resort Pool Depth

Resort pools in Bali are typically deeper compared to those in Taiwan, which is a noticeable difference. Therefore, if traveling with children, parents need to pay extra attention to their safety. It's recommended to bring arm floaties or other flotation devices to ensure the safety of kids while they play in the water.

## Hard Rock Hotel's Kids Club

The Hard Rock Hotel is truly ideal for families with children. One day, we enrolled our kids in the all-day Kids Club activities while we adults went shopping at nearby malls and enjoyed spa treatments. This not only gave us a peaceful day but also ensured the kids had a blast. We stayed at the Hard Rock Hotel in Kuta for the last three days. The Kids Club at Hard Rock is very popular, so I recommend making reservations in advance. The hotel provides WhatsApp contact details, and you can also email to book ahead. If your plans are set, booking early is safer as cancellations are allowed before 10 AM. I booked upon arrival and was lucky to secure the last available spot.

## Delicious Food

The food in Bali is incredibly delicious, and we didn't encounter any disappointments this time. However, since kids are sensitive to spicy food, it's advisable to ask the staff about the spiciness of dishes to ensure a pleasant dining experience for them. There are many restaurants near the Hard Rock Hotel, and we randomly chose one without any issues. Within walking distance, there are two malls and numerous dining options, including Jamie Oliver's restaurant, KFC, and McDonald's at the back entrance. I genuinely think the fries at Bali's KFC are exceptionally good.

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Whitewater Rafting Guide (FAQ)](/blog/2025-03-28-67e62aa8fd89780001888620/)

This time, we also experienced whitewater rafting in Ubud, which was incredibly fun. Since there are many options, we asked our private driver to book it for us, and he found a reputable operator. I recommend choosing the full 2-hour experience rather than the 1-hour one for a more complete adventure. We have two younger kids, one just over 5 years old and the other 7, and they both had a great time. The rafting activity lasted about 2 hours. We left our villa in Ubud at 9 AM, and a simple lunch was included. We finished around 1 PM. Getting to the rafting spot requires walking down a long set of stairs, but even our kids managed it slowly without difficulty.

## Fun Braiding Experience

The braiding experience in Bali is incredibly fun and really adds to the vacation vibe. I noticed many foreign children with colorful braids, so I decided to take my kids to try it out too—it was quite special.

We got our braids done on the street by Jamie Oliver's restaurant near Kuta Beach. This street is lined with many Spa shops that offer braiding services, and they display samples of braids at their entrances. I initially thought they would use a lot of hair gel to secure the braids, but they actually didn't. When removing the braids, you just need to cut off the fake hair part, which is quite convenient.

## Convenient Private Car Service

This time, we opted for a private car service (driver + Chinese-speaking guide). We hired a Chinese-speaking guide who not only helped us overcome language barriers but also made our trip more enjoyable and relaxing. For instance, we adults enjoyed three Spa sessions—once while the guide looked after our kids at the Villa, and another time when he took the kids out for a meal, allowing us to fully relax during our Spa time.
