At the end of June 2024, I returned from Bali, and this trip was filled with memories and surprises. For me, this was my second visit to Bali; the first time was to obtain my Open Water diving certification. That trip was very hectic, as the diving course took five days, leaving little time to explore other places. However, this time, I traveled with my family, and it had been twenty years since my last visit. Bali's development has astonished me—not only has it become more prosperous, but the number of Indonesians and tourists has more than tripled compared to two decades ago. Here are some unforgettable places and experiences from our journey:

### Bali's Traffic Situation

Traffic in Bali has always been a challenge. Frequent congestion often reduces speeds to 10 km/h, making it difficult to plan our itinerary. We often had to patiently wait in traffic jams, but if we weren't in a hurry, we took it as an opportunity to enjoy the scenery along the way!

### Taxis and Gojek

Twenty years ago, calling a taxi in Bali cost at least USD$20, which made me reluctant to leave the hotel after returning, as the transportation expenses were painful. Now, things have improved with the Gojek ride-hailing app, making transportation costs more affordable and convenient, greatly enhancing our travel experience.

### Bali International Airport

Bali's airport is new and very aesthetically pleasing. However, due to its limited capacity during peak seasons, the check-in process can take a long time, often over an hour. Therefore, it's recommended to arrive early for check-in when departing to avoid delays.

> August 2025 News: [Bali Airport's New Luggage Inspection System Now Live!](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/14Hjcko4sKG/)

### Airport Shopping

Inside the airport, there's a small Oakley stall selling sunglasses and backpacks. Although the selection is limited, prices are cheaper than in Taiwan. Based on my experience, branded goods at the airport are authentic, making shopping here relatively reliable. Additionally, Billabong surfwear is also cheaper at the airport compared to Taiwan, tempting me to buy more.

### Bali's Coffee and Chocolate

Bali produces coffee and chocolate, with JungleGold Bali offering many unique flavors. Although somewhat expensive, the packaging is exquisite, making it perfect as a gift to bring home. We visited Satria Coffee Plantation, where various spices are grown, and we saw how cocoa fruits grow on trees—a rare experience for us.

### Civet Coffee

![Hung Min Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dff855853b4eddf3442497de4b2e98ac.jpg)

Provided by Hung Min Lin

At Satria Coffee Plantation, we also tried civet coffee. Due to the unique and somewhat unsettling production process of civet coffee, our group of four and our guide shared a set by asking for extra cups. Personally, I didn't find the civet coffee particularly aromatic, but the experience was enlightening. The products sold in the plantation are slightly expensive, but seeing the staff's hard work in maintaining the place, we bought a pack of coffee powder to show our support.

### Pork Ribs Culinary Journey

During our time in Bali, we indulged in a variety of delicious foods. The grilled pork ribs at Naughty Nuris were particularly impressive, and we visited both their Ubud and Sanur locations. The Sanur branch is larger and even has air-conditioned rooms, making it very comfortable. Fat Chow Bali in Kuta also served up some fantastic dishes—we ate there twice, and although we had to queue each time, the wait was definitely worth it.

### Indonesian Cuisine

Our guide, Mr. Liega, recommended Warung Kampung for Indonesian food. The dishes were delicious and affordable, with freshly made durian juice costing just USD$1 per glass. It gave us a real taste of local culinary culture.

### Bali Zoo

Although the zoo's ticket price was on the higher side at around USD$32 per person, we found it to be worth it once inside. The zoo combines Bali's unique architectural designs, making it exceptionally beautiful. During our visit, we rode in an air-conditioned safari bus, which kept us comfortable despite the heat, making the ticket price feel justified.

### Nightlife Experience

Hard Rock Cafe Bali continues the Indonesian nightclub vibe with live band performances every night at 9:30 PM. The music was extremely loud, and we left shortly after arriving due to the overwhelming volume.

### White Water Rafting

We also participated in Bali's white water rafting. Although the price was steep at nearly USD$65 per person, it included an all-you-can-eat local meal. Our guide helped us secure a 20% discount. While the river wasn't too dangerous, navigating the stone steps required some physical effort. My 76-year-old mother managed the experience successfully.

### Manta Ray Spotting at Nusa Penida

To see manta rays at Nusa Penida, it's best to stay on the island the night before. If staying elsewhere, such as Kuta, you'd need to wake up as early as 5 AM. We ultimately skipped this activity to avoid the early start.

### Bali's Culture

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d2bcc00956186161b74a5936edb7dc14.png)

In Bali, every household has its own temple, and the walls are adorned with beautiful stone carvings. Our guide, Mr. Fovix, explained that locals pray twice a day, and no building in a home can be taller than the temple, as a sign of respect to the gods. This is also why most places in Bali don't have tall buildings.

### Conclusion

For those who love ocean views, beaches, and rice field landscapes, Bali is a place you'll want to visit again and again. On my next trip, I plan to explore local markets and experience the daily life of the locals. The basic wage for locals is around USD$180 (2024), which offers a different perspective on life. Through the locals' way of living, we might gain a deeper appreciation for the spirituality of this beautiful land. We are very grateful to our two guides, Mr. Fovix and Mr. Liega, who provided us with many valuable suggestions. Mr. Fovix is a meticulous guide with excellent English skills, while Mr. Liega is honest and also proficient in English. They took us to many spots frequented by locals, helping us save a lot of money. Every time we ate, we invited them to join us, but they always politely declined or ordered simple dishes. We are very thankful for their attentive care, leaving us with perfect memories.
