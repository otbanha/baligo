At the end of June 2024, I returned from Bali, and this trip was filled with memories and surprises. For me, this was my second visit to Bali—the first was to obtain my Open Water diving certification. That trip was very tight, as the diving course took five days, leaving little time to explore elsewhere. However, this time, I traveled with my family, and it had been twenty years since my last visit. Bali's development has amazed me—not only has it become much more prosperous, but the number of locals and tourists has more than tripled compared to two decades ago. Here are some unforgettable places and experiences from our journey:

### Bali's Traffic Situation

Bali's traffic has always been a challenge. The constant congestion often reduces speeds to 10 km/h, making it difficult to plan schedules. We frequently had to patiently wait in traffic jams, but if we weren't in a hurry, we took it as an opportunity to enjoy the scenery along the way!

### Taxis and Gojek

Twenty years ago, calling a taxi in Bali could cost upwards of USD$20, which made me reluctant to leave the hotel after returning, as the transportation expenses were painful. Now, things have improved with the Gojek ride-hailing app, making transportation costs more affordable and convenient, greatly enhancing our travel experience.

> [Essential apps to download for traveling in Indonesia/Bali: A complete guide to Gojek](/blog/2023-08-20-64e187dffd897800017304fe/)

### Bali International Airport

Bali's airport is new and very aesthetically pleasing. However, due to its limited capacity still unable to handle the massive crowds during peak season, the check-in process can take quite a long time, often over an hour. Therefore, it's recommended to arrive at the airport early for check-in when departing to avoid delays in your itinerary.

> August 2025 News: [Bali Airport's New Luggage Inspection System Goes Live!](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/14Hjcko4sKG/)

### Airport Shopping

Inside the airport, there's a small stall selling Oakley sunglasses and backpacks. Although the selection is limited, the prices are cheaper than in Taiwan. Based on my experience, the branded goods at the airport should be genuine, so shopping here is relatively reliable. Additionally, Billabong surfwear is also cheaper at the airport compared to Taiwan, making it hard to resist buying a few extra pieces.

### Bali's Coffee and Chocolate

Bali produces coffee and chocolate, with JungleGold Bali offering many unique flavors. Although somewhat expensive, the packaging is exquisite, making it perfect as a souvenir to bring home as gifts. We visited Satria Coffee Plantation, where various spices are grown, and we got to see how cocoa pods grow on trees, which was a rare experience for us.

### Civet Coffee

![Hung Min Lin提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dff855853b4eddf3442497de4b2e98ac.jpg)

Provided by Hung Min Lin

At Satria Coffee Plantation, we also experienced civet coffee. Due to the unique and somewhat unsettling production process of civet coffee, our group of four and our guide asked for extra cups to share a set. Although I personally didn't find the civet coffee particularly aromatic, the experience was quite enlightening. The products sold in the plantation are slightly expensive, but seeing the staff's hard work in maintaining the place, we bought a pack of coffee powder to show our support.

### Pork Ribs Culinary Journey

We indulged in many delicious foods during our trip to Bali. The grilled pork ribs at Naughty Nuri's were absolutely stunning — we visited both their Ubud and Sanur locations. The Sanur branch is larger and even has air-conditioned rooms, making it very comfortable. Fat Chow Bali in Kuta also served incredibly tasty food. We ate there twice during our stay in Kuta, and although we had to wait in line each time, it was definitely worth it.

### Indonesian Cuisine

Our guide, Mr. Liega, introduced us to Warung Kampung, a restaurant serving delicious Indonesian food at reasonable prices. A glass of freshly blended durian juice cost just USD$1, allowing us to immerse ourselves in the local culinary culture.

### Bali Zoo

Although the zoo's admission fee is relatively high, costing over USD$30 per person, we discovered that it combines Bali's unique architectural designs, making it exceptionally beautiful. During our visit, we rode an air-conditioned safari bus, which kept us comfortable despite the hot weather. This made us feel that the ticket price was justified.

### Jimbaran Beach Restaurants

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_44cc47df3248d9b0ec1e2f48f78c0678.png)

Jimbaran's beachfront restaurants are generally pricey, but our guide recommended Menega Cafe, which offers more affordable options. Although it was cloudy that day and we missed the sunset, having three generations of our family dining together abroad made for a precious moment we truly cherished.

### Souvenir Shopping

For souvenirs, we chose Krisna, where the peanuts and dried fruits were both delicious and inexpensive, making it hard to resist buying extra to bring home.

### Nightlife Experience

HardRock Cafe Bali continues the Indonesian nightclub vibe, with live band performances every night at 9:30 PM. The music was extremely loud, and we left shortly after arriving due to the overwhelming volume.

### Rafting Adventure

We also participated in Bali's rafting activity. Although it was relatively expensive at nearly USD$65 per person, it included unlimited local food. Our guide helped us secure a 20% discount. While the river wasn't too dangerous, navigating the stone steps required some physical effort. My 76-year-old mother managed to complete the experience successfully.

### Manta Ray Spotting in Nusa Penida

To see manta rays in Nusa Penida, it's best to stay on the island the night before. If staying elsewhere, like Kuta, you’d need to wake up as early as 5 AM. Since we didn’t want to wake up that early, we ultimately decided to skip this activity.

Sanur is a perfect destination for family vacations, ideal for parents looking to relax while keeping their kids entertained—a true Balinese gem.

### Bali's Culture

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d2bcc00956186161b74a5936edb7dc14.png)

In Bali, every household has its own temple, adorned with beautiful stone carvings on the walls. Our guide, Mr. Fovix, explained that locals pray twice daily, and no building in the home can be taller than the temple, as a sign of respect to the gods. This is also why most areas in Bali don't have tall buildings.

### Conclusion

For those who love ocean views, beaches, and rice field landscapes, Bali is a place you'll want to visit again and again. On my next trip, I plan to explore local markets and experience the daily life of the locals. With a basic monthly wage of around USD$180 (as of 2024), this lifestyle offers a unique perspective. Through the locals' way of life, we might gain a deeper appreciation for the spirituality of this beautiful land. We are immensely grateful to our two guides, Mr. Fovix and Mr. Liega, who provided invaluable advice. Mr. Fovix is a meticulous and fluent English-speaking guide, while Mr. Liega is honest and also proficient in English. They took us to many spots frequented by local tourists, helping us save on costs. Every time we ate, we invited them to join us, but they always politely declined or ordered simple dishes. We are deeply thankful for their attentive care, which left us with perfect memories.
