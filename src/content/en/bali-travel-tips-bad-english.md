Bali is one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations, attracting millions of visitors from around the world each year. Due to the development of tourism, English has gradually become a common language in Bali.

![峇里島有來自各個國家、不同人種/母語的外國人](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_af3cffb9f72b6ef11a499c8c3fa47d3f.jpg)

Bali is home to foreigners from various countries and different ethnicities/languages.

Why has English become a common language in Bali? Here are some reasons:

- Many hotels, restaurants, and shops in Bali cater to foreign tourists, so their staff often speak English.
- The Balinese government is also promoting English education to enhance the island's international competitiveness.
- Many Balinese people believe that learning English can help them secure better job opportunities.

Therefore, when traveling in Bali, even if you don’t speak Indonesian, you can usually communicate in English.

## What to Do If You Don’t Speak English Well?

For travelers from non-English-speaking countries, visiting Bali might pose some language challenges, but don’t worry! In this article, I’ll share some methods to help you overcome language barriers and make your trip to Bali smoother and more enjoyable.

### Translation Apps 🌍 Must-Have Travel Tools for 2026

### 1. Google Translate: The All-Rounder Essential

This app is practically a staple on everyone's phone. Its most powerful feature is the "instant camera translation"—just point your camera at a sign or menu, and Chinese text instantly appears on the screen.

- **Why it's great:** Supports the widest range of languages, and with offline maps paired with offline translation packs, you can survive without internet.
- **Must-use feature:** Real-time text replacement through the camera—no need to type a single word.

### 2. VoiceTra (沃译通): Your Japan Travel Companion

If you're planning a trip to Japan, this app developed by a Japanese government research institute is a must-download. Its voice recognition is incredibly accurate and optimized for travel scenarios, delivering highly authentic Japanese translations.

- **Why it's great:** Super simple interface—just one microphone button.
- **Highlight:** It shows a "reverse translation" to confirm whether the app misunderstood you, avoiding awkward miscommunications.
- (A little shoutout to Voicetra—it’s super handy! Even translating your speech into Indonesian is quite accurate.)

### 3. Naver Papago: The Go-To for K-Culture Fans

When traveling to Korea, Google Translate can sometimes feel a bit stiff. That’s where Papago comes to the rescue. Developed by Korea’s leading search engine NAVER, it handles Korean tone and honorifics flawlessly.

- **Why it's great:** Best localized for Korea, with beautifully formatted photo translations.
- **Thoughtful touch:** Built-in categories for common travel phrases (like shopping, hotels, and transportation)—no need to even speak.

### 4. DeepL: The Most Natural AI Translation

If you need to translate lengthy articles or more formal conversations (such as discussing business or long-term rental contracts in **Bali**), DeepL's semantic logic is currently recognized as the most "human-like," without any awkward machine-like tone.

- **Why We Recommend It:** Extremely high accuracy in semantics, producing very fluent translations.
- **Note:** More suitable for long texts or complex needs, with an interface leaning towards professional use.

## 💡 Tips: How to Use It More Smoothly?

- **Download Offline Packages Before Traveling:** Prevent disconnection in airports, mountainous areas, or places with poor signal.
- **Combine with Voice Input:** When communicating with locals, hold down the microphone to speak directly, then play the translation for them.

We hope these tools make your journey smoother!

### Familiarize Yourself with Common Travel Scenarios

Before heading to Bali, familiarizing yourself with common travel scenarios and useful phrases will help you navigate various situations, such as seeking help, ordering food, and shopping.

For example:

**Seeking Help:**

1. Excuse me, do you speak English?
2. I'm lost, can you help me?
3. I want to go to XXX, can you tell me how to get there?

**Ordering Food:**

1. I'd like to order a XXX.
2. Can you make it not spicy?

**Shopping:**

1. How much is this?
2. Can you make it cheaper?
3. I'd like to buy this.
