Have you ever dreamed of sleeping in a wooden cabin surrounded by raw jungle in Bali, stepping out your door to your own private pool with layered tropical greenery in the distance? Moonlit Jungle is that place. This is a boutique wooden villa run by a private owner, boasting an Agoda rating of 9.7 and a five-star Google review—one of the top-rated accommodations in the area. It's small and refined, not for those seeking the lively vibe of a large resort, but if what you crave is stillness, jungle, and a pool entirely your own, it's nearly flawless.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783940104263-797675711.jpg)

▲There are only four villas in total, each with its own private pool

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

Moonlit Jungle is located along Jl. Raya Kintamani in the village of Manukaya. This area might be unfamiliar to most travelers, but its location is surprisingly advantageous.

The biggest highlight is that it's just a 5-minute drive from two of Bali's most important sacred sites: **Tirta Empul Temple** (a must-visit for purification rituals) and **Gunung Kawi Temple** (ancient rock-cut shrines with a powerful aura). Being able to visit these in the morning and return to your private pool in the afternoon is a luxury not every accommodation can offer.

About 20 minutes south, you'll reach the Tegallalang Rice Terraces, and a bit further is central Ubud. For travelers looking to base themselves in Tampaksiring and explore northern Ubud, the route is very convenient. However, keep in mind that Manukaya is a small mountain village with no convenience stores or supermarkets nearby. There's **Pangkon Bali Resto** for a nearby meal, but options are limited. It's a good idea to stock up on snacks and water before you arrive, or plan your meals around your outings.

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783940327439-fa28bf4886a8e41fec0b04760d99a98d.webp)

Moonlit Jungle's concept is clear: this isn't a large resort. There's no restaurant, no spa, no gym, and no lobby to lounge in. Its core is the wooden cabin itself and that private pool.

Each cabin is completely independent, with a total of four outdoor pools—one per villa—so you never share with other guests. Privacy is truly top-notch. The cabins are built mainly from natural wood, with a simple, rustic design that avoids over-the-top Instagram styling. But the authentic feeling of being immersed in the jungle is spot-on—the texture of wood as the wind blows through, the symphony of cicadas and frogs as your soundtrack. You might find it hard to even finish a book while sitting by the pool.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783940537465-797536557.jpg)

Breakfast is included, with a complimentary Western-style meal served daily from 8:00 AM to 11:00 AM. The property also offers a shuttle service, which is a nice bonus for guests who haven't rented their own transport.

The room type centers on the 'Private Pool Jungle View Bungalow,' with each standalone villa featuring its own private pool and terrace, offering direct jungle views. Inside, guests will find a living area, air conditioning, a flat-screen TV, and basic bathroom amenities, all designed with natural wood tones for an earthy, organic feel.

A key note: **no extra beds or baby cots are available**, and children aged 7 and above are charged as adults. This accommodation is ideal for couples or honeymooners staying as two. If traveling with young children, be sure to confirm with the host before booking.

This is undoubtedly a top pick for **honeymooners and couples**—private pools, jungle villas, and stellar guest ratings make it a near-perfect romantic escape. It's also great for travelers seeking **total seclusion for a few days**, or those planning to use Tampaksiring as a base to explore the spiritual hidden gems of northern Ubud.

## 💬 Guest Reviews

As a newly built property in 2025, Moonlit Jungle has already amassed a wealth of positive feedback.

One guest raved about the service: the manager not only helped with currency exchange, taxi bookings, and scooter rentals but also offered free tastings of new dishes—calling it the best service they've ever experienced. On clear days, the views are simply breathtaking.

Another guest described it as an 'unbelievable experience'—the most beautiful place they've ever stayed, with stunning views, modern and stylish rooms, and a strong treehouse vibe. They noted that it's about a 40-minute drive from central Ubud, but the scenic route is full of pleasant surprises, and they can't wait to return with friends and family.

Some travelers also pointed out practical considerations: the jungle setting makes nights cooler, and the pool water can be chilly, though the jacuzzi offers a warm alternative. The location is far from town and cafes, so if you don't want to ride out, you can order Grab delivery or room service. Scooter rental costs 175,000 IDR per day, and a 20-minute ride takes you to Kintamani, with views of Mount Batur, Mount Abang, and Mount Agung along the way—an unexpected bonus.
