Have you ever dreamed of sleeping in a wooden cabin surrounded by pristine jungle in Bali, stepping out your door to your own private pool with layers of tropical greenery in the distance? Moonlit Jungle is that place. This boutique wooden villa, run by a private owner, boasts an Agoda rating of 9.7 and a five-star Google rating, making it one of the top-rated accommodations in the area. Small and refined, it’s not for those craving the lively vibe of a large resort, but if you’re after peace, jungle, and a pool that’s entirely your own, it’s nearly flawless.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783940104263-797675711.jpg)

▲There are only four villas in total, each with its own private pool

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

Moonlit Jungle sits along Jl. Raya Kintamani in the village of Manukaya—a spot that may feel unfamiliar to most travelers, but its location is actually a major advantage.

The biggest highlight is that it’s just a 5-minute drive from two of Bali’s most sacred sites: **Tirta Empul Holy Water Temple** (a must-visit for purification rituals) and **Gunung Kawi Temple** (ancient rock-cut shrines with a powerful aura). Being able to visit these in the morning and return to soak in your private pool by afternoon is a luxury not every accommodation can offer.

About 20 minutes south takes you to the Tegallalang Rice Terraces, and a bit further brings you to central Ubud. For travelers looking to base themselves in Tampaksiring and explore northern Ubud, the route planning is very convenient. However, keep in mind that Manukaya is a small mountain village with no convenience stores or supermarkets nearby. There’s **Pangkon Bali Resto** for a quick meal, but options are limited. It’s wise to stock up on snacks and water before arriving, or plan your meals around outings to nearby attractions.

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783940327439-fa28bf4886a8e41fec0b04760d99a98d.webp)

Moonlit Jungle’s positioning is clear: this is not a large resort. There’s no restaurant, no spa, no gym, and no lobby to lounge in. Its essence lies in the wooden cabins themselves and the private pools.

Each cabin stands independently, with a total of four outdoor pools across the property—one per villa, so you never share with other guests. Privacy is truly top-notch. The cabins are built mainly from raw wood, with a natural, understated design. There’s no forced Instagram aesthetic, but the immersive jungle experience is spot-on—the texture of wind through the wood, the soundtrack of cicadas and frogs blending together, and the kind of tranquility that makes it hard to even finish a book by the pool.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1783940537465-797536557.jpg)

Breakfast is included, offering a complimentary Western-style meal served daily from 8:00 AM to 11:00 AM. The property also provides a shuttle service, a nice bonus for travelers who haven’t rented their own car.

The core offering is the 'Jungle View Wooden Cabin with Private Pool.' Each standalone cabin features its own private pool and terrace, with direct jungle views. Inside, you’ll find a living area, air conditioning, a flat-screen TV, and basic bathroom amenities. The design revolves around natural wood, exuding an earthy, organic vibe.

A key note: **no extra beds or baby cots are available**, and children aged 7 and above are charged as adults. This setup is ideal for couples on a romantic getaway. If you’re traveling with young children, be sure to confirm with the host before booking.

This is a top pick for **honeymooners and couples**—private pools, jungle cabins, and stellar guest ratings make it a near-perfect romantic escape. It’s also great for **travelers seeking a few days of total seclusion**, or those planning to use Tampaksiring as a base to explore the spiritual hidden gems of northern Ubud.

## 💬 Guest Reviews

As a newly built accommodation in 2025, Moonlit Jungle has already accumulated a wealth of positive reviews.

One guest raved about the service: the manager not only helped with currency exchange, calling taxis, and arranging scooter rentals, but also let them try new dishes for free, calling it the best service they had ever experienced. On clear days, the views are simply breathtaking.

Another guest described it as an 'unforgettable experience'—the most beautiful place they had ever stayed, with stunning views, modern and stylish rooms, and a strong treehouse vibe throughout. They added that while it's about a 40-minute drive from central Ubud, the scenic route is full of pleasant surprises, and they can't wait to return with friends and family.

Some travelers also noted practical considerations: the jungle terrain makes nights cooler, and the pool water is on the chilly side, though the jacuzzi offers a warm alternative. The location is far from town and cafes, so if you don't want to ride out, you can order Grab delivery or use room service. Scooter rental is 175,000 IDR per day, and a 20-minute ride takes you to Kintamani, where you can enjoy views of three volcanoes—Mount Batur, Mount Abang, and Mount Agung—an unexpected bonus.
