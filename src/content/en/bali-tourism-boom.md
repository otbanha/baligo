According to statistics from Bali's International Airport Immigration Office, the number of arrivals and departures at Bali International Airport in January 2025 increased by 22% compared to the same period in 2024. Alexander Maxwell, Head of Information and Communication Technology at the Immigration Office, revealed that a total of 611,603 people arrived in Bali in January 2025, while 632,833 departed. Typically, January sees more departures than arrivals as tourists return home after Christmas and New Year holidays.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bf32e66b76078f0a296d4db9e4cd0f1b.png)

Maxwell stated that foreign tourists remain the main arrivals, totaling 540,122, a 27% increase year-on-year. The number of Indonesian citizens arriving also rose by 30%, reaching 53,490, while crew members increased by 20% to 17,991.

In January 2025, Bali's main international tourist sources remained similar to previous years, with the top 10 being:

1. Australia (139,577)
2. China (61,984, including Hong Kong and Taiwan)
3. India (36,514)
4. South Korea (35,172)
5. Russia (26,794)
6. Malaysia (21,771)
7. United Kingdom (20,569)
8. United States (18,627)
9. Singapore (15,942)
10. Japan (12,112)

Bali Governor Wayan Koster pointed out that tourism significantly impacts Indonesia's overall economy, even during the off-season. In 2024, Bali contributed IDR 107 trillion to the national tourism industry, accounting for 44% of foreign exchange earnings. Koster emphasized that Bali's economy is highly dependent on tourism and that future economic structural transformation is needed to balance tourism and non-tourism development.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c921dc4310098a99930838894970f765.png)

Tourism accounts for 66% of Bali's revenue. Koster expressed optimism about the increase in international tourists over the past 12 months but voiced concerns about the slow growth in domestic tourism demand. In 2024, Bali welcomed only 9.4 million domestic tourists, far below the pre-pandemic level of over 10.5 million annually.

Koster analyzed that the slow recovery of domestic tourism is due to the incomplete recovery of the national economy and high domestic airfare prices, which not only affect Indonesian citizens but also hinder international tourists' willingness to explore other regions of Indonesia. The high cost of domestic flights poses a significant barrier for budget-conscious travelers.

Koster分析，國內旅遊復甦緩慢的原因包括國家經濟未完全恢復及國內機票價格高昂，這不僅影響印尼民眾，也阻礙國際遊客探索印尼其他地區的意願。高額的國內航班成本對追求性價比的遊客來說是一大障礙。
