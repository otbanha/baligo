When you think of Bali, images of turquoise waters, emerald rice terraces, and exotic temples probably come to mind. But the 'Island of the Gods' has a famously... spirited traffic scene. Narrow roads, heavy traffic, and motorbikes weaving through everywhere.

So the first challenge when planning your Bali trip is: **Should you hire a private driver or use ride-hailing?**

### Frequently Asked Questions

## Which is more cost-effective: private driver or ride-hailing?

For single destinations or short trips within the city, Grab/Gojek is the most budget-friendly (point-to-point pricing). If you're visiting multiple spots in a day, crossing regions, or heading to the Ubud highlands, a private driver (starting from around Rp 600,000 / 10 hours) is more cost-effective—it saves you from repeated waiting and the risk of being rejected.

## Should I use ride-hailing or a private driver for sunset at Jimbaran?

A private driver is recommended. During sunset hours, demand for rides surges, leading to long waits or no availability. A private driver can arrive early and take you directly to the best viewing spot, and you won't have to worry about finding a ride back afterward.

## How should I get around when traffic is heavy in Canggu?

Canggu's roads are congested with many one-way streets. For short distances, it's best to walk or book a motorbike Grab/Gojek (GoRide) for more flexibility; for longer trips across areas, consider hiring a private driver and allow extra buffer time.
