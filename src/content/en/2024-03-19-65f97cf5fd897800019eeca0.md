Bali's monkeys are quite unique, closely tied to local Hindu beliefs. As the 'Divine Monkey Hanuman' is highly revered, residents generally respect monkeys and even actively feed them. Ubud's 'Sacred Monkey Forest' is a protected sanctuary and playground specifically created for them.

{{block:戶外}}

A single monkey may seem cute, but be extra cautious when there are two or more - we've personally witnessed three monkeys teaming up to attack a grown man. Monkeys also frequently steal, snatch items, or cause destruction, with similar incidents occurring constantly.

When traveling to Bali, always be wary of monkeys: avoid direct eye contact, don't carry food or shiny objects. In protected areas, follow guide instructions, maintain quiet and distance.

Monkeys can be found almost everywhere in Bali. Always stay alert, keep your belongings secure, and stay out of monkeys' reach. While interacting with monkeys can be fun, safety should always come first.
