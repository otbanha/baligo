Monkeys in Bali are a unique phenomenon of this land. With Hinduism playing a significant role here, and Hanuman, the Monkey God, being one of the deities revered by Balinese people, most locals hold monkeys in high regard, even feeding them. In Ubud, there's a monkey sanctuary called the Sacred Monkey Forest, which serves as a paradise for these creatures.

While a single monkey may appear cute, be cautious when you see two or more! We've personally witnessed three monkeys ganging up on a grown man. This made us realize how aggressive monkeys can become in groups. Additionally, monkeys are known for stealing, snatching items, and even causing damage - incidents like these are frequently reported.

Therefore, when traveling to Bali, please be wary of monkey behavior. Besides avoiding direct eye contact with monkeys, we should also be careful not to carry food or shiny objects that might attract their interest. Moreover, when visiting monkey sanctuaries in Bali, always follow the guides' instructions, remain quiet, and keep your distance.

The truth about monkeys

No matter where you are in Bali, monkeys can appear almost anywhere. So before going anywhere, stay alert and don't give monkeys any opportunity. Make sure your belongings are securely protected and out of monkeys' reach. While interacting with monkeys can be a wonderful experience, remember that safety always comes first.

While exploring Bali, keep these tips in mind and stay vigilant, and you'll be able to enjoy a safe and pleasant journey. We look forward to hearing about your adventures - happy travels!
