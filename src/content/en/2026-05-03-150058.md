### 📄 Southern Traffic & Health Alerts

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1777820274834-canggu_new_route.png)

- **Four Major Road Projects Commence in the South**: To alleviate traffic congestion in the south, the Badung Regency government has launched four new road construction projects, including routes connecting **Pecatu to Melasti** and **Pecatu Market to the main road of Uluwatu**, aiming to divert traffic heading to Uluwatu Temple.
- **Canggu Underground Road Project**: A 6-kilometer connecting road (including partial underground sections) has entered the implementation phase to address the most congested area in Canggu. Tourists are advised to stay updated on navigation changes during construction.
- **Alcohol & Infectious Disease Alerts**: Authorities recently warned tourists about the risk of **methanol poisoning** and advised against consuming cocktails of unknown origin or suspiciously low prices. Additionally, sporadic cases of **dengue fever** and **measles** have been reported on the island, so enhanced mosquito protection is recommended for outdoor activities.

### 🗞️ Aviation Updates & Event Changes

- **Garuda Indonesia Launches Direct 'Bali-Timika' Flight**: Garuda Indonesia announced the commencement of direct flights between **I Gusti Ngurah Rai (DPS) Airport** and **Timika (TIM)** in Papua starting May. This is part of Indonesia's recent addition of 53 new routes, aiming to strengthen Bali's role as a regional transportation hub.
- **2026 Global Coffee Congress (IWCA) Cancelled**: The International Women’s Coffee Alliance (IWCA) Global Congress, originally scheduled for May 12-14 in Bali, has been officially cancelled due to international flight disruptions caused by geopolitical tensions. This decision ensures the safety and participation rights of members from Africa, Europe, and the Americas.
- **Suwung Landfill Organic Waste Deadline Countdown**: Although the governor temporarily reopened the Suwung Landfill for organic waste, this measure will only last until **the end of July**. Authorities reiterated that the entire island must complete the transition to source separation by August, or waste accumulation issues will worsen during the peak tourist season.
