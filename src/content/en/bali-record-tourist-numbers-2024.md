As mid-2024 approaches, Bali is experiencing its busiest year for international tourism on record. New data shows that by the end of July 2024, the number of international tourists visiting Bali has already surpassed the total for the entire year of 2019, pre-pandemic. This 'Island of the Gods' is attracting a large number of vacationers, digital nomads, expats, and international investors.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f8066c45927d327a922bd2c5a987a2c7.jpg)

## Bali Tourism Hits New High in 2024

### Overview of Bali's International Tourist Data

According to data from Indonesia's Central Statistics Agency, Bali welcomed 7.75 million international tourists from January to the end of July 2024. Pudji Ismartini, Deputy Director of Distribution and Service Statistics at the Central Statistics Agency, stated, 'The number of foreign tourist visits by July 2024 is the highest since 2020.'

## Key Drivers of Bali's Tourism Market

### Vacationers

Many tourists from around the world choose Bali as their vacation destination to enjoy its sunshine, beaches, and rich cultural activities.

### Digital Nomads

Bali has become a paradise for digital nomads, attracting a large number of professionals who work online with its reliable internet connectivity and stunning natural environment.

### Expatriates

Bali's pleasant climate and welcoming community make it a dream destination for many, drawing a significant number of international expatriates.

### International Investors

With the booming tourism industry, many international investors have recognized the business opportunities in Bali, engaging in various investments.

## Tourist Statistics for July 2024

### Monthly Tourist Growth Trend

In July 2024, Bali experienced its largest tourist growth in recent years. A total of 1.31 million international tourists arrived at Ngurah Rai International Airport, marking a 9.42% increase from June 2024 and a 16.91% increase from July 2023.

### Main Tourist Source Countries

Malaysia, Australia, China, and Singapore are the primary sources of international tourists to Bali. In July 2024, Malaysia contributed 177,000 visitors to Bali, closely followed by 166,000 Australian tourists. The total number of Chinese tourists (including Taiwan) reached 126,000, while Singapore accounted for 102,000 visitors.

## Bali Airport Operations

### Busy Ngurah Rai International Airport

Ngurah Rai International Airport has become the main gateway for welcoming international tourists to Bali, experiencing its busiest period in history in 2024.

### The Role of Soekarno-Hatta Airport

Located in Jakarta, Soekarno-Hatta Airport also plays a significant role in international travel, with the two airports collectively handling 90% of the country's international air traffic.

## Travel Behavior and Trends

### Average Length of Stay

New data reveals that tourists vacationing in Bali stay for an average of 8.1 nights. Visitors from Southeast Asian countries stay for an average of 3.67 nights, while African tourists have the longest average stay at 15.74 nights among all nationalities.

### Stay Patterns by Country

Visitors from different countries show significant variations in their length of stay in Bali, reflecting their travel plans and needs.

## Measures to Enhance Service Quality at Bali Airport

### Immigration Service Optimization

To cope with the increasing number of tourists, Bali Airport continuously improves its immigration services to ensure smooth customs clearance for travelers.

### Impact of the Indonesia-Africa Forum

During the Indonesia-Africa Forum held from September 1st to 3rd, 2024, Bali Airport set up dedicated immigration check counters to facilitate a more convenient entry process for attendees and visitors.

## Policy Changes and Future Challenges

### Updates on Tourism Development Policies

Luhut Binsar Pandjaitan, the Coordinating Minister for Maritime Affairs and Investment, publicly explained at last week's Indonesia Quality Tourism Conference that a series of new policies would be formulated to better control the impacts of overtourism. These policies aim to promote sustainable tourism development, ensuring the interests of both tourists and local communities.

## Cultural Preservation and Sustainable Development

### The Importance of Preserving Bali's Culture

Minister Pandjaitan emphasized the importance of maintaining Bali's culture, noting that without it, Bali would no longer be a paradise island.

### Advancing Sustainable Tourism

To achieve sustainable tourism development, the government and businesses need to work together to promote environmental and cultural conservation measures.
