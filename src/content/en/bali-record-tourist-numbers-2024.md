Overview of Bali's International Tourist Data

According to Indonesia's Central Bureau of Statistics, Bali welcomed 7.75 million international tourists from January to July 2024. Pudji Ismartini, Deputy Director of Distribution and Service Statistics at the Central Bureau, stated: 'The number of foreign tourist visits up to July 2024 is the highest record since 2020.'

## July 2024 Tourist Statistics

### Monthly Visitor Growth Trend

In July 2024, Bali experienced its largest tourist growth in recent years. A total of 1.31 million international tourists arrived at Ngurah Rai International Airport, marking a 9.42% increase from June 2024 and a 16.91% increase from July 2023.

### Main Tourist Source Countries

Malaysia, Australia, China, and Singapore are the primary sources of international tourists to Bali. In July 2024, Malaysia contributed 177,000 visitors to Bali, closely followed by 166,000 Australian tourists. The total number of Chinese tourists, including those from Taiwan, was 126,000, while Singapore accounted for 102,000 visitors.

## Bali Airport Operations

### Busy Ngurah Rai International Airport

Ngurah Rai International Airport has become the main gateway for welcoming international tourists to Bali and experienced its busiest period in history in 2024.

### The Role of Soekarno-Hatta Airport

Soekarno-Hatta Airport in Jakarta also plays a significant role in international travel, with the two airports combined handling 90% of the country's international arrivals and departures.

## Travel Behavior and Trends

### Average Length of Stay for Tourists

New data reveals that tourists vacationing in Bali stay an average of 8.1 nights. Visitors from Southeast Asian countries stay an average of 3.67 nights, while African tourists have the longest average stay at 15.74 nights among all countries.

### Stay Patterns of Tourists by Country

Visitors from different countries show significant variations in their length of stay in Bali, reflecting their travel plans and needs.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9dc09529c8ade0bee152f19eaf786d94.jpg)

## Bali Airport's Measures to Enhance Service Quality

### Immigration Service Optimization

To accommodate the increasing number of tourists, Bali Airport continuously improves its immigration services to ensure smooth customs clearance for travelers.

### Impact of the Indonesia-Africa Forum

During the Indonesia-Africa Forum held from September 1st to 3rd, 2024, Bali Airport set up dedicated immigration check counters to facilitate easier entry for attendees and visitors.

## Policy Changes and Future Challenges

### Updates on Tourism Development Policies

Luhut Binsar Pandjaitan, the Coordinating Minister for Maritime Affairs and Investment, announced at last week's Indonesia Quality Tourism Conference that a series of new policies will be formulated to better manage the impacts of overtourism. These policies aim to promote sustainable tourism development, ensuring the interests of both visitors and local communities.

## Cultural Preservation and Sustainable Development

### The Importance of Preserving Balinese Culture

Minister Pandjaitan emphasized the importance of preserving Balinese culture, noting that without it, Bali would no longer be a paradise island.

### Advancing Sustainable Tourism

To achieve sustainable tourism development, the government and businesses need to work together to promote environmental and cultural conservation measures.
