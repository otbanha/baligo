## Sanur's History

Sanur is a historic coastal town on Bali, with a history dating back centuries. In Bali's past, Sanur was a key port and fishing village, attracting ships from across the Indonesian archipelago and beyond for trade. Over time, it became one of Bali's commercial and cultural exchange hubs.

After Indonesia gained independence in the mid-20th century and began nationwide development, Bali's tourism took off under President Sukarno—and Sanur was where it all started! The island's first five-star hotel was built right here in Sanur.

Today, thanks to its natural reef protection, Sanur boasts calm, gently sloping golden beaches, making it a family-friendly haven. Many expats from around the world also choose to retire in this quiet, unpretentious town, far from the nightlife and hustle.

### Water Activities

In Sanur, the top activity is water sports! Canoeing and stand-up paddleboarding (SUP) are two of the most popular water activities here. You can glide across the calm sea, soaking in the peaceful ocean atmosphere, or challenge your balance and strength. This beautiful stretch of sea offers both fun and challenge, ensuring a delightful time.

Xiao Jie's canoeing video

https://youtu.be/Xp5gyMslrVE?si=mPledHphy4xTtRUO

Canoes and paddleboards can be rented from vendors on the beach, mostly concentrated around Pantai Karang. At this marked location on the map, you'll find many local vendors selling swimwear, floaties, water guns, water toys, and sand digging tools (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). In 2023, both canoes and paddleboards cost 25K IDR per hour, about USD$1.

![Bopel Beach 免費的沙灘遊樂場](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2d0f7048e37c3a787f43c41df39ade22.jpg)

Bopel Beach is another family-friendly area in Sanur (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). It features a playground on the sand and offers rental umbrellas and lounge chairs. Public restrooms and changing rooms are also available.

At Mertasari Beach, the exposed sand stretches for hundreds of meters during low tide!

### Beach Promenade

![Beach Promenade](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d124800f990b3fd545451b4c77015a4f.jpg)

You can't talk about Sanur without mentioning its over four-kilometer-long beach promenade! This lengthy coastal walkway is one of Sanur's most distinctive features. Stroll along the shops, walk, jog, or rent a bike and ride leisurely with your kids on the flat path. The beach on one side, with its varying length and scenery, guarantees you'll find a spot to call your own—shaded by huge trees with lovely cool spots, and no smokers nearby! Lay down a mat and relax in your private slice of paradise.

You might also consider joining one of these two half-day tours.

Museum Le Mayeur is located in the former home of Belgian painter Adrien-Jean Le Mayeur and his Balinese wife, Ni Pollok. Their story fills this place with a romantic and historical atmosphere. Strolling through this former residence, you can not only appreciate Le Mayeur's deep affection and artistic interpretation of Bali but also feel the historical essence of Sanur's past.
