## Sanur's History

Sanur is a historic coastal town on Bali, with a history dating back centuries. In Bali's past, Sanur was an important port and fishing village, attracting ships from across the Indonesian archipelago and beyond for trade. Over time, it became one of Bali's commercial and cultural exchange hubs.

After Indonesia gained independence in the mid-20th century and began nationwide development, Bali's tourism industry took off under President Sukarno—and Sanur was where it all started! The island's first five-star hotel was built right here in Sanur.

Today, thanks to its natural reef protection, Sanur boasts calm, gently sloping golden beaches, making it a family-friendly paradise. Many expats from around the world also choose to retire in this quiet, unpretentious town, far from the nightlife and hustle.

### Water Activities

In Sanur, the top activity is water sports! Canoeing and stand-up paddleboarding (SUP) are two of the most popular water activities here. You can glide across the calm, flat sea to soak in the peaceful ocean atmosphere, or challenge your balance and strength. This beautiful stretch of water brings both fun and challenge, ensuring a delightful time.

Video of Xiao Jie canoeing

https://youtu.be/Xp5gyMslrVE?si=mPledHphy4xTtRUO

Canoes and paddleboards can be rented from vendors on the beach, mostly concentrated around Pantai Karang. At this spot on the map, you'll find many local vendors selling swimsuits, floaties, water guns, water toys, and sand-digging tools (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). In 2023, both canoes and paddleboards cost 25K IDR per hour, about USD$1.

![Bopel Beach 免費的沙灘遊樂場](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2d0f7048e37c3a787f43c41df39ade22.jpg)

Bopel Beach is another family-friendly area in Sanur (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)), featuring a playground on the sand and rentals for umbrellas and lounge chairs. Public restrooms and changing rooms are also available.

![Mertasari Beach 退潮的時候露出的沙灘有數百公尺！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_629c7393918cd56ab168b8a899176d9a.jpg)

At Mertasari Beach, the exposed sand stretches for hundreds of meters during low tide!

Another area is Mertasari Beach at the southernmost tip of Sanur (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). Many local vendors rent out sand toys, swim rings, float boards, kayaks, stand-up paddleboards, jet skis, and more. Prices are reasonable, and unlike other parts of Southeast Asia, rental disputes are rare.

### Beach Promenade

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d124800f990b3fd545451b4c77015a4f.jpg)

No mention of Sanur is complete without its over four-kilometer-long coastal promenade! This lengthy beachfront walkway is one of Sanur's most distinctive features. You can browse the shops along the path, take a stroll, go for a jog, or rent a bike and leisurely ride with your kids on the flat trail. On one side of the promenade, the beach offers plenty of length and variety, ensuring you'll find a spot all to yourself—with big trees, lovely shade, and no smokers nearby! Lay down a mat and relax in your own private slice of paradise.

Museum Le Mayeur is housed in the former home of Belgian painter Adrien-Jean Le Mayeur and his Balinese wife, Ni Pollok. Their love story fills this place with romance and history. Strolling through this historic home, you can appreciate Le Mayeur's deep affection for Bali through his art, while also soaking in a piece of Sanur's past.
