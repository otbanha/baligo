## The History of Sanur

Sanur is a historic coastal town on Bali, with a history stretching back centuries. In Bali's past, Sanur was an important port and fishing village, attracting ships from across the Indonesian archipelago and beyond for trade. Over time, it became a hub of commerce and cultural exchange on the island.

After Indonesia gained independence in the mid-20th century and began nationwide development, Bali's tourism industry took off under President Sukarno—and Sanur was where it all began! The island's first five-star hotel was built right here in Sanur.

Today, thanks to its natural reef protection, calm waters, and gently sloping golden beaches, Sanur has evolved into a family-friendly vacation haven. Many expats from around the world also choose to retire in this quiet, unpretentious coastal town, far from the neon lights and bustling crowds.

### Water Activities

In Sanur, the top activity is water sports! Canoeing and stand-up paddleboarding (SUP) are two of the most popular water activities here. You can glide across the calm, serene sea and soak in the peaceful ocean atmosphere, or challenge your balance and strength. This beautiful stretch of water offers both fun and a bit of a challenge, ensuring a delightful time.

Video of Xiao Jie Canoeing

https://youtu.be/Xp5gyMslrVE?si=mPledHphy4xTtRUO

Canoes and paddleboards can be rented from vendors along the beach, mostly concentrated around Pantai Karang. At the spot marked on the map, you'll find many local vendors selling swimwear, floaties, water guns, water toys, and sand-digging tools (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). In 2023, both canoe and SUP rentals cost 25K IDR per hour, about USD$1.

Bopel Beach is another family-friendly area in Sanur (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)), featuring a playground on the sand, with umbrellas and lounge chairs available for rent. There are also public restrooms and changing rooms.

### Beach Promenade

![海灘步道](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d124800f990b3fd545451b4c77015a4f.jpg)

Speaking of Sanur, you can't miss its over 4-kilometer-long coastal promenade! This lengthy walkway is one of Sanur's most distinctive features. Stroll along the shops, walk, jog, or rent a bike to ride leisurely with your kids on the flat path. The beach on one side, with its varying stretches and scenery, ensures you'll find a spot all to yourself—with big trees, lovely shade, and no smokers nearby! Lay down a mat and relax in your own private slice of paradise.

### Museum Le Mayeur (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing))

![Museum Le Mayeur （地圖）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a7eadec18bd85e377d455169550dbd25.jpg)

Museum Le Mayeur is housed in the former home of Belgian painter Adrien-Jean Le Mayeur and his Balinese wife, Ni Pollok. Their love story fills this place with a romantic and historic atmosphere. Located at the northern end of Sanur Beach, this charming small museum showcases many of Le Mayeur’s paintings, which vividly capture Bali’s landscape and life before mass tourism took hold. Although the museum’s maintenance has sparked some debate in recent years, it remains a captivating spot for visitors who love Balinese art and culture. Strolling through this former home, you can not only admire Le Mayeur’s deep affection for Bali and his artistic interpretation but also soak in a piece of Sanur’s history.
