## The History of Sanur

Sanur is a historic coastal town in Bali, with a history dating back hundreds of years. In Bali's history, Sanur was once an important port and fishing village, attracting ships from the Indonesian archipelago and other countries for trade. Over time, Sanur became one of Bali's hubs for commerce and cultural exchange.

Following Indonesia's independence in the mid-20th century, the country embarked on various development projects. Bali began to flourish during the era of Indonesia's founding father, Sukarno, and Sanur marked the starting point of Bali's tourism industry! The island's first five-star hotel was established in Sanur.

Today, Sanur, blessed with its natural reef protection, boasts calm waters and gently sloping golden beaches, making it a perfect destination for family vacations. Many expatriates from around the world choose this quiet, unpretentious town as their retirement haven.

### Water Activities

In Sanur, the number one activity is water sports! Canoeing and Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP) are two of the most popular water activities here. You can easily enjoy the serene ocean atmosphere on calm waters or challenge your balance and strength. This beautiful sea will bring you both fun and challenges, ensuring a delightful time.

Canoes and SUP boards can be rented from vendors along the beach, with many concentrated around Pantai Karang. This area also has numerous local vendors selling swimwear, flotation devices, water guns, beach toys, and sand tools (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). In 2023, the rental price for both canoes and SUP boards is 25K IDR per hour, approximately USD$2.

Museum Le Mayeur is located in the former home of Belgian painter Adrien-Jean Le Mayeur and his Balinese wife Ni Pollok. Walking through this historic home, you can not only admire Le Mayeur's deep affection and artistic interpretation of Bali but also feel the historical essence of Sanur.
