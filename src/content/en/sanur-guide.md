## The History of Sanur

Sanur is a historic coastal town on Bali, with a history stretching back hundreds of years. In Bali's history, Sanur was once an important port and fishing village, attracting ships from the Indonesian archipelago and other countries for trade. This made Sanur one of Bali's centers for commercial and cultural exchange.

Following Indonesia's independence in the mid-20th century and the start of national development, Bali began to flourish during the era of Indonesia's founding father, Sukarno. Sanur was the starting point for Bali's tourism industry! The island's first five-star hotel was established in Sanur.

Today, Sanur, with its naturally protected reef, boasts calm waters and gently sloping golden beaches, making it a haven for family vacations. Many foreigners from around the world choose this quiet, unpretentious town for their retirement.

### Water Activities

In Sanur, the number one activity is water sports! Canoeing and stand-up paddleboarding (SUP) are two of the most popular water activities here. You can easily enjoy the serene ocean atmosphere on calm waters or challenge your balance and strength. This beautiful sea will bring you both fun and challenges, ensuring a delightful time.

Canoes and SUP boards can be rented from vendors along the beach, with many concentrated around Pantai Karang. This area is also bustling with local vendors selling swimwear, flotation devices, water guns, beach toys, and sand tools (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). In 2023, the rental price for both canoes and SUP boards was 25K per hour, approximately USD$1.

Bopel Beach’s Free Beach Playground

Bopel Beach is another family-friendly area in Sanur (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). Here, you’ll find a beachside playground, along with rental options for umbrellas and lounge chairs. Public restrooms and changing rooms are also available.

Mertasari Beach’s Expansive Sandbanks at Low Tide

Another area worth visiting is Mertasari Beach, located at the southernmost end of Sanur (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)). The prices are reasonable, and unlike some other Southeast Asian destinations, rental disputes are rare here.

### Beachfront Promenade

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d124800f990b3fd545451b4c77015a4f.jpg)

When it comes to Sanur, one cannot overlook its over four-kilometer-long beachfront promenade! This extensive coastal walkway is one of Sanur's most distinctive features. You can stroll along the shops beside the path, go for a jog, or rent a bicycle to leisurely ride with your children on the flat trail. On one side of the promenade lies the beach, which, with its length and variety along the way, ensures you'll find a stretch of sand to call your own—complete with large trees and lovely shaded spots, and without any smoking tourists nearby! You can lay down a mat and relax in this private area.

### Museum Le Mayeur （📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a7eadec18bd85e377d455169550dbd25.jpg)

Museum Le Mayeur is located in the former home of Belgian painter Adrien-Jean Le Mayeur and his Balinese wife Ni Pollok. The story of this couple fills the place with a romantic and historical atmosphere. Situated at the northern end of Sanur Beach, this charming small museum showcases many of Le Mayeur's paintings, which vividly depict Bali's landscape and lifestyle before the rise of mass tourism. Although the museum's maintenance has been controversial in recent years, it remains an enchanting spot for visitors who appreciate Balinese art and culture. Strolling through this historic home, you can not only admire Le Mayeur's deep affection and artistic interpretation of Bali but also feel the historical essence of Sanur.
