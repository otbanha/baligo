## The History of Sanur

Sanur is a historic coastal town in Bali, with roots dating back hundreds of years. In Bali's history, Sanur was once a significant port and fishing village, attracting ships from across the Indonesian archipelago and beyond for trade. Through this, Sanur became one of Bali's hubs for commerce and cultural exchange.

Following Indonesia's independence in the mid-20th century, the country embarked on various development initiatives. Bali began to flourish during the era of Indonesia's founding father, Sukarno, and Sanur marked the starting point of Bali's tourism industry! The island's first five-star hotel was established in Sanur.

Today, Sanur boasts a naturally protected reef, calm waters, and gently sloping golden beaches, making it a family-friendly vacation paradise. Many expatriates from around the world choose to retire in this peaceful, unpretentious town.

### Water Activities

In Sanur, the number one activity is water sports! Canoeing and Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP) are two of the most popular water activities here. You can easily enjoy the serene ocean atmosphere on calm waters or challenge your balance and strength. This beautiful sea will bring you fun and challenges, ensuring a delightful time.

Both canoes and paddleboards can be rented from vendors on the beach, with many concentrated around Pantai Karang. This area also has numerous local vendors selling swimwear, flotation devices, water guns, water toys, and sand tools (📌[Map](/map/sanur/)). The rental price for canoes and paddleboards in 2023 is 25K per hour, approximately USD$1.

### Beachfront Promenade

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d124800f990b3fd545451b4c77015a4f.jpg)

When it comes to Sanur, one cannot overlook its iconic 4-kilometer-long beachfront promenade! This extensive coastal walkway is one of Sanur's most distinctive features. You can stroll along the shops lining the path, go for a jog, or rent a bicycle to leisurely ride with your kids on the flat trail. On one side of the promenade lies the beach, which, thanks to its length and varied scenery, ensures you'll find a secluded spot all to yourself. ### Museum Le Mayeur （📌[Map](/map/sanur/)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a7eadec18bd85e377d455169550dbd25.jpg)

Museum Le Mayeur is located in the former home of Belgian painter Adrien-Jean Le Mayeur and his Balinese wife Ni Pollok. The story of this couple fills the place with a romantic and historical atmosphere. Situated at the northern end of Sanur Beach, this charming small museum showcases numerous paintings by Le Mayeur, vividly capturing the essence and lifestyle of Bali before the rise of mass tourism. Although the museum's maintenance has been a topic of debate in recent years, it remains a captivating spot for visitors who appreciate Balinese art and culture. Strolling through this former residence, you can not only admire Le Mayeur's deep affection and artistic interpretation of Bali but also feel the historical moments that belong to Sanur.
