Due to soaring international oil prices, the Civil Aeronautics Administration has announced that starting April 7, 2026, fuel surcharges for passenger flights departing from Taiwan on national carriers (China Airlines, EVA Air, Starlux Airlines) will increase by 157%. This significant hike will particularly impact travelers planning trips to Indonesia, especially those heading to the popular vacation destination of Bali.

## How Much Have Bali and Jakarta Routes Increased?

According to the new rate, the fuel surcharge for flights to Bali (classified as a long-haul route) will increase sharply from $45.5 per person to $117 per person, making a single trip nearly $73 more expensive. For a round trip, the ticket cost per person surges by about $145. For a family vacation, a family of four would have to pay nearly $580 extra just in surcharges, not including potential increases in base ticket prices.

## How to Respond?

With airfare to Bali or Jakarta becoming increasingly expensive, travel experts have proposed three strategies to help you save on costs:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775702565966-1000117789.jpg)

### Strategy 1: Utilize 'Connecting Flights' to Avoid High Direct Flight Prices

When flying to Bali, you don't necessarily have to opt for expensive direct flights. Consider taking a budget or traditional airline to regional hubs like Hong Kong, Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, or Singapore first, then connect to flights heading to Bali or other Indonesian cities.

В· Money-saving principle: Booking flights in segments is often cheaper than purchasing a direct flight ticket from a national carrier, especially now with the staggering fuel surcharges on long-haul routes.
В· Potential benefits: You could save between USD$323 to USD$645, plus enjoy a short stopover in the connecting city, effectively visiting two destinations in one trip.

### Strategy 2: Smart Pairing with 'Low-Cost Airlines'

Not all airlines charge the same fuel surcharge. Many low-cost carriers have different fee structures, with some not charging it at all.

В· Travel Tip: When flying from Taiwan to Bali, consider taking airlines like Tigerair Taiwan that don't charge extra fuel surcharges to destinations such as Macau, Bangkok, or Singapore. Then connect to budget airlines like AirAsia for your flight to Bali (AirAsia usually includes surcharges in ticket prices, potentially offering more competitive total fares).
В· Key Reminder: Always calculate the total cost including all segment fares, baggage fees, and seat selection charges to determine if it's truly a good deal.

### Strategy 3: Seize the Golden Booking Period and Maximize Credit Card Benefits

This price increase is based on the 'ticket issuance date' (starting from 12:00 AM on April 7th) as the threshold. If you have already issued your ticket before the deadline, even if your departure date is several months later during summer vacation or a holiday period, you will still be subject to the older, lower surcharge.

В· Contingency Plan: If you've already missed out, keep a close eye on travel benefits offered by your credit card, such as airport transfers, lounge access, or discount codes and cashback available through specific travel platforms. These can help offset some of the price increases.
