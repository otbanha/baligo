Due to soaring international oil prices, the Civil Aeronautics Administration has announced that starting April 7, 2026, fuel surcharges for certain routes (China Airlines, EVA Air, Starlux Airlines) departing from Taiwan will increase by 157%. This price hike will significantly impact travelers planning long-haul flights.

## How Much Are Bali and Jakarta Routes Increasing?

### ✈️ Taipei → Bali: What Type of Route Is It?

Here’s the conclusion first 👇

👉 Direct flights from Taipei to Bali (Taipei → Bali) are typically classified as "regional routes," not long-haul routes.
Airlines generally categorize them as follows:

#### 🟢 Regional Routes (Short / Regional Haul)

Flight duration: Approximately 4–6 hours
Destinations: Within Asia (Southeast Asia, Northeast Asia)
Examples:
Taipei → Bali
Taipei → Bangkok
Taipei → Singapore
👉 The Bali flight takes about 5 hours, fitting squarely within this category

#### 🔴 Long-Haul Routes (Long Haul)

Flight Duration: Typically over 8 hours
Destinations: Europe, America, Australia
Examples:
Taipei → Los Angeles
Taipei → Paris

According to the new rate, the fuel surcharge for flights to Bali (regional route) will increase from $17.5 (approximately NT$551) to $45 (approximately NT$1,417) per person, making a single trip nearly NT$866 more expensive. For a round trip, the ticket cost per person will increase by approximately NT$1,732. For a family trip, a family of four will have to pay nearly NT$6,928 more just in surcharges, which is roughly equivalent to one or two nights' stay in a family villa. This does not include possible increases in the base fare.

## How to Cope?

Facing the rising cost of flights to Bali or Jakarta, travel experts have proposed three strategies to help you save money:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775702565966-1000117789.jpg)

### Strategy 1: Utilize "Stopover Flights" to Avoid High Direct Flight Prices

When flying to Bali, you don't necessarily have to choose expensive direct flights. Consider taking budget or traditional airlines to regional hubs such as Hong Kong, Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, or Singapore, and then connect to flights to Bali or other cities in Indonesia.

· Saving Principle: Buying tickets in segments is usually cheaper than purchasing a direct flight ticket, especially now when long-haul fuel surcharges are staggering.
· Potential Benefit: You could save NT$10,000 to NT$20,000 and also have a short stay in the transit city, allowing you to visit two places in one trip.

### Strategy 2: Smartly Combine "Low-Cost Airlines"

Not all airlines charge the same fuel surcharges. Many low-cost airlines have different fee structures, and some do not charge extra.

· Operation Suggestion: When flying from Taiwan to Bali, you can first take airlines like Tigerair Taiwan, which do not charge additional fuel surcharges, to Macau, Bangkok, or Singapore, and then connect to low-cost airlines like AirAsia to Bali (AirAsia usually includes surcharges in the ticket price, making the total price potentially more competitive).
· Key Reminder: Be sure to calculate all segment ticket prices, baggage fees, seat selection fees, etc., to know if it's truly cost-effective.

### Strategy 3: Take Advantage of the Booking Window and Credit Card Benefits

This price increase is based on the "ticket issuance date" (starting from 12:00 AM on April 7th). If you completed your booking before the deadline, even if your departure date is during the summer vacation or holiday season months later, you will still be subject to the older, lower surcharge.

· Remedy: If you missed the window, keep a close eye on travel benefits offered by credit cards, such as airport transfers, lounge access, or discount codes and cashback available through specific travel platforms, which can help offset some of the price increase.
