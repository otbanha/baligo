Starlink, the satellite internet service under Elon Musk's SpaceX, has finally launched in Indonesia. This marks a major shift for the country's internet landscape. As a satellite-based service, Starlink is designed to deliver high speed, low latency, and wide coverage—especially in regions where conventional internet is hard to come by.

## **What Is Starlink?**

Starlink is a satellite internet service created by SpaceX, providing high-speed connectivity through a constellation of low Earth orbit satellites. These satellites orbit the Earth to form a global network, enabling users in remote areas to enjoy stable internet access.

## **Why Choose Starlink?**

### High Speed

Starlink promises speeds comparable to fiber-optic connections, ensuring a smooth online experience no matter where you are.

### Low Latency

The design of low Earth orbit satellites gives Starlink significantly lower latency than traditional satellite internet, which is especially important for real-time applications like online gaming and video conferencing.

### Wide Coverage

Since it's satellite-based, Starlink can reach remote and rural areas where traditional wired or wireless internet services are often hard to come by.

## **Starlink's Three Plans in Indonesia**

![Starlink 在印尼的三個套餐](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a1028050853b28dee096aa9f86ada818.png)

### Residential Plan

The Residential plan is the most affordable option, costing 750,000 Indonesian Rupiah per month. This plan is ideal for families living in residential areas, offering high speed and low latency for everyday internet needs.

### Jelajah Plan

The Jelajah plan costs 990,000 Indonesian Rupiah per month and is designed for those who frequently travel or work in remote areas. This plan guarantees high-speed connectivity even in changing environments.

### Kapal Plan

The Kapal plan targets users on vessels, providing global connectivity at a monthly fee of 4,345,000 Indonesian Rupiah, with a data cap of 50 GB. There's also a premium option at 17,160,000 Indonesian Rupiah per month (1 TB of data).

## **Starlink Hardware**

The Starlink hardware kit costs approximately IDR 7,800,000, which includes the antenna for receiving satellite signals and the accompanying router.

## **How to Order Starlink**

### Visit the Official Website

First, go to the Starlink official website: <https://www.starlink.com/id>

### Enter Your Service Address

Scroll down on the site to the service address section and enter, for example, "Indonesia."

### Choose a Plan

The website will show availability in your area; select the plan that suits your needs.

### Submit Your Order

Fill in your contact and shipping information, then update the address.

## **Payment Methods and Delivery Time**

The website will display the monthly service fee, hardware costs, and a shipping and handling fee of 345,000 Indonesian Rupiah. Choose your payment method (only Visa and Mastercard credit or debit cards are accepted), and expect delivery within 1–2 weeks.

## **Self-Installation Guide**

Users can install the equipment themselves by following the instructions in the Starlink app, which includes the WiFi router, a 15-meter Starlink cable, power cord, and base.

## **Experience Using Starlink**

Many early users report that Starlink’s speed and stability far exceed expectations, especially in areas with weak network coverage. It handles everything from home entertainment to remote work with ease.

## **Starlink vs. Traditional Internet Services**

Compared to traditional internet services, Starlink offers better coverage and speed, particularly in remote areas. However, the hardware costs and monthly fees are relatively high, so it’s important to consider your personal needs and budget.

## **The Future of Starlink**

In the future, Starlink plans to launch more satellites to further improve service quality and coverage, and may introduce more specialized packages tailored to businesses and specific needs.
