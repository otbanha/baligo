> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Sannie Lin](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/KZWh2fnM6upSM3uq/):

**Key Takeaways**: For a six-day, five-night family trip to Bali, the on-site queue for a visa on arrival took about **20 minutes**—we recommend getting an e-visa in advance to save time. When exchanging currency, don’t just exchange $200 USD—while credit cards are convenient, **private driver fees often require cash**, so it’s best to exchange more from the start (in our case, we needed an extra $150 USD).

Our six-day, five-night Bali trip has come to a perfect end! We’re so grateful to the helpful members of the online community and the wealth of information shared, which made this family adventure with two little ones smooth, relaxing, and truly enjoyable.

![帶著孩子看世界：六天五夜峇里島親子旅行的旅程安排與小貼士](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_46eaba429c96b06a7d14c92bea5677e0.jpg)

Here’s a rundown of our preparation and customs process:

✔️ **E-Visa**: Upon arrival, we noticed the visa-on-arrival queue took about 20 minutes. Luckily, the group provided the new e-visa website, which saved us a lot of time! Using the e-visa was much more convenient and let us start our trip sooner.

Further reading: [Your Guide from Landing to Customs to Exiting the Airport](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/) (2025 Latest Edition)

✔️ **USD Exchange**: We initially exchanged $200 USD, planning to use credit cards wherever possible. But by the end, we ran out of cash to pay our driver for the private car service, so we exchanged another $150 USD at the hotel. This experience taught us that while cards are handy, you still need to keep cash needs in mind—especially when using private drivers—to avoid any hiccups in your itinerary!

> Further reading: [How Much Cash Should You Bring for a Bali Trip?](/en/blog/bali-travel-cash-budget-guide/)

Here's our detailed itinerary, arranged as follows:

## **Day 1**

After arriving in Bali, we headed straight to our stay at **Bidadari Villa**. Along the way, our driver Agus kindly took us to buy a SIM card and exchange currency. We opted for the cheapest 12GB SIM card, which cost around USD$16. The internet speed in Bali was quite stable, and the data was sufficient for our entire trip, whether for navigation or sharing travel moments online.

## **Day 2**

![我們在Nukuwera玩River Tubing](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6568e81ae3a73a6d63bd5d98eebe2e75.jpg)

Today was a full-day private tour in Ubud. Our first stop was the **Nukuwera & Pandawa River Tubing**, recommended by [Xiao Jie](http://bit.ly/youtube-jason). Lunch was included, and the whole tubing experience was perfect for families. Even with kids, we had a blast, and the little ones absolutely loved their time in the water.

![野生的猴子很容易就暴走，最好保持距離](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9b47f1081db7082131841f0d5a6803c9.jpg)

Next, we visited **Ubud Palace** to soak in the local cultural vibe. Afterward, we made a special stop at **KUO CUSINE** to pick up some local specialty jams. They had a great variety, and grabbing a few bottles as souvenirs was a nice idea.

Dinner was more spontaneous. Since we had the kids with us, we kept the schedule pretty relaxed. We found **Warung Pulau Kelapa** on Google on a whim, and luckily the food was good. The prices were a bit high, but the atmosphere was lovely and very family-friendly. For us traveling with kids, a comfortable dining setting and convenient location matter more than the cost, making it easy to enjoy the evening meal.

## **Day 3**

Today was our moving day, heading to the **Bali Safari Park**. Along the way, we asked our driver Benny to take us to try the burgers at **Ayoo-Ayoo**. This place has a five-star rating on Google, so we went there out of curiosity, and it really didn't disappoint! The burgers tasted amazing—the meat was tender and juicy, and the toppings were generous. It was a truly satisfying food experience for the whole family.

![Ayoo Ayoo](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1b22614e00a6c81c0cbe97cccd84c7c3.jpg)

Ayoo Ayoo

> Editor's note: The editor has tried at least 50 different burger joints in Bali and has almost never had a bad experience—every single one was delicious!

Next, we stopped by a nearby supermarket to stock up on some essentials before arriving at the park and checking in. That evening, we chose to dine at the **Tsavo Lion Restaurant** inside the park. The highlight of this restaurant is that you can enjoy your meal while watching lions through a glass window! The kids were thrilled, and the whole dining experience was both fun and unique.

![Bali Safari Zoo](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9d3eacc77a4c925c1e1acc7690259fa5.jpg)

After dinner, there was a photo opportunity with animals outside the restaurant. We opted for this service, and the photographer took many photos—shots of mom and the older brother, dad and the younger sister, and a family portrait of all four. The best part was that the entire set included all digital files, plus one A4-sized print with a beautiful album, all for just around USD$23. The photo quality was excellent, and everyone in the family looked great. We felt it was an incredible value for capturing such memorable moments!

Although today's itinerary was mostly about moving from one place to another, it was filled with surprises and warm family moments.

## **Day 4**

Today we moved from **Bali Safari** to **Westin Nusa Dua**. Along the way, we asked our driver Agus to help us find local snacks, including **Siobak (roast pork)** and **Martabak (stuffed pancake)**. Agus not only found suitable small shops for us but also carefully communicated with the shop owners to prevent us from being overcharged as tourists. This thoughtful service made us feel truly touched.

> Read more: [Top 10 Foods in Bali](/en/blog/bali-top-10-foods/)

Next, we asked the driver about buying souvenirs and went to **Krisna** and **The Balinese** to shop. When Agus recommended Krisna, he specifically reminded us that if we needed to go anywhere else, we could let him know anytime, allowing us to decide our own itinerary. This reminded me of a travel experience over a decade ago, when I clearly stated I didn't want to shop but was still forced by the driver to visit several souvenir factories. I was really furious back then. This time, the experience left me feeling very satisfied and relaxed because Agus fully respected our wishes.

For dinner, we dined at the resort's **Velada Restaurant**, enjoying burgers and Indian cuisine. Overall, today's itinerary allowed us to experience the warmth and thoughtfulness of the locals, making our trip even more enjoyable and unforgettable.

## **Day 6**

On our final day, we headed to the airport, once again relying on Agus for the ride. We're deeply grateful for his and Benny's patience and tolerance with our kids' noise in the car, which made our journey much smoother.

For lunch, we grabbed a bite at **Made's Warung** in the airport. For our farewell meal, we had to order the nostalgic **Nasi Goreng** and **Mie Goreng**. These classic Indonesian fried rice and noodles never get old, accompanying our mood as we bid goodbye to Bali 👋, bringing this trip to a perfect close.

Throughout the entire trip, we not only enjoyed beautiful scenery and delicious food but also created many wonderful memories with our children—treasured experiences that will stay with us forever.
