> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Sannie Lin](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/KZWh2fnM6upSM3uq/):

**Key Takeaways**: For a 6-day, 5-night trip to Bali with kids, the on-site visa-on-arrival queue took about **20 minutes**—it’s better to apply for an e-visa in advance to save time. When exchanging money, don’t just exchange USD$200—while card payments are convenient, **private driver fees often require cash**, so it’s wise to exchange more upfront (in this case, an additional USD$150 was needed).

Our 6-day, 5-night Bali trip has come to a perfect end! We’re so grateful to the helpful community members and the wealth of information shared, which made this family trip with two kids smooth, relaxed, and truly enjoyable.

![帶著孩子看世界：六天五夜峇里島親子旅行的旅程安排與小貼士](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_46eaba429c96b06a7d14c92bea5677e0.jpg)

Let’s start with our preparation and customs process:

✔️ **E-Visa:** Upon arrival, we noticed the visa-on-arrival queue took about 20 minutes. Luckily, the new e-visa website shared by the community saved us a lot of time! Using the e-visa was much more convenient, allowing us to start our journey sooner.

Further reading: [Your Guide from Landing to Customs to Exiting the Airport](/en/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/) (2025 Latest Edition)

✔️ **USD Exchange:** We initially exchanged USD$200, planning to use cards wherever possible. But by the end, we ran out of cash to pay our driver for the private car service, so we exchanged another USD$150 at the hotel. This taught us that while card payments are convenient, you still need to keep an eye on cash needs—especially when using private drivers—to avoid any hiccups in your itinerary!

> Read more: [How much cash should you bring for a trip to Bali?](/en/blog/bali-travel-cash-budget-guide/)

Next, here’s our detailed itinerary:

## **Day 1**

After arriving in Bali, we headed straight to our stay at **Bidadari Villa**. Along the way, our driver Agus kindly took us to buy a SIM card and exchange currency first. We chose the cheapest 12GB SIM card, which cost about USD$16. The internet speed in Bali was quite stable, and the data was sufficient for our entire trip—whether for navigation or sharing travel moments online, it worked smoothly.

## **Day 2**

Dinner was a spontaneous affair. Since we had the kids with us, we kept the schedule pretty relaxed. We found **Warung Pulau Kelapa** on Google on a whim, and luckily the food was good. Though a bit pricey, the ambiance was lovely and very family-friendly. For us traveling with children, a comfortable dining setting and convenient location matter more than the cost—it made for an easy and enjoyable dinner.

## **Day 3**

Today was our moving day, heading to **Bali Safari Park**. Along the way, we asked our driver Benny to take us to try the burgers at **Ayoo-Ayoo**. This place has a five-star rating on Google, so we went there out of curiosity, and it did not disappoint! The burgers were fantastic—tender, juicy meat with rich toppings. It was a truly satisfying food experience for the whole family.

![Ayoo Ayoo](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1b22614e00a6c81c0cbe97cccd84c7c3.jpg)

Ayoo Ayoo

> Editor's note: I've tried at least 50 different burgers in Bali, and almost none of them were disappointing—every single one was delicious!

Next, we stopped by a nearby supermarket to stock up on essentials, then arrived at the park and checked in. That evening, we had dinner at the park's **Tsavo Lion Restaurant**, which is famous for letting you dine while watching lions through a glass window! The kids were thrilled, and the whole meal was both fun and unique.

![Bali Safari Zoo](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9d3eacc77a4c925c1e1acc7690259fa5.jpg)

## **Day 4**

Today we moved from **Bali Safari** to **The Westin Resort Nusa Dua**. Along the way, we asked our driver Agus to help us find local snacks, including **Siobak (roast pork)** and **Martabak (stuffed pancake)**. Agus not only found suitable small eateries but also carefully communicated with the shop owners to prevent us from being overcharged as tourists. This thoughtful service made us feel truly warm.

> Read more: [Top 10 Bali Food Recommendations](/en/blog/bali-top-10-foods/)

Next, we asked the driver about buying souvenirs, and we went to **Krisna** and **The Balinese** to shop. When Agus recommended Krisna, he specifically reminded us that if we needed other places, we could let him know anytime, allowing us to decide our own itinerary. This reminded me of a travel experience over a decade ago, when I clearly stated I didn't want to shop but was still forced by a hired driver to visit several shopping factories—I was really furious at the time. This experience left me feeling very satisfied and relaxed, because Agus fully respected our wishes.

For dinner, we dined at **Velada Restaurant** within the resort, enjoying burgers and Indian cuisine. It was a perfect end to our fourth day of the trip.

Overall, today's itinerary let us experience the warmth and thoughtfulness of the locals, making our journey even more enjoyable and memorable.

## **Day Six**

On the last day, our itinerary was to head to the airport, and we once again asked Agus to drive us. We're very grateful for his and Benny's patience and tolerance with our kids' noise in the car, which made our trip much smoother.

For lunch, we settled at **Made's Warung** in the airport. For our final farewell meal, we had to order the nostalgic **Nasi Goreng** and **Mie Goreng**. These classic Indonesian fried rice and fried noodles never get old, accompanying our mood as we bid goodbye to Bali 👋, marking a perfect end to this journey.

Throughout the trip, we not only enjoyed beautiful scenery and delicious food but also created many wonderful memories with our children, which will become an unforgettable and precious experience in our hearts.
