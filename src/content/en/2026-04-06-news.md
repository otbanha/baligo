【Today's USD Exchange Rate: 16475 IDR】 **Bali** is currently in the "transitional period (Peralihan)" between the wet and dry seasons. Although the island is gradually entering the dry season, Indonesia's Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysical Agency (BMKG) has issued a **yellow alert** today, warning that areas including Kuta and Ubud still have a **45% - 60%** chance of brief afternoon thunderstorms, strong winds, and lightning risks.

Temperature-wise, daytime highs reach around **31°C**, feeling muggy due to humidity levels as high as 80%; at night, it cools to a comfortable **24°C**. The southern coast (Uluwatu, Nusa Dua) is influenced by the Australian monsoon, with winds shifting to east-southeast and waves around 1-2 meters—sea conditions are generally stable, but it's best to stay away from thunderstorm areas. Overall, the morning to midday period is the golden window for outdoor activities; it's advisable to bring lightweight rain gear and apply plenty of sunscreen.

Due to Middle East conflicts and international airspace disruptions, experts warn that Bali and Thailand will be the regions most affected by rising long-haul flight costs, with airlines already adjusting routes or adding fuel surcharges.

Culturally, today falls on a challenging day in the Balinese calendar, considered inauspicious for weddings or groundbreaking ceremonies. Last Saturday marked the end of **Saraswati Day**, with schools and temples across the island still immersed in the atmosphere of knowledge worship; next, on April 8, **Pagerwesi** will be observed, where locals strengthen spiritual protection and inner balance through rituals. In terms of security, a British man wanted by Interpol was arrested at the airport last week and is currently undergoing extradition proceedings. The tourism board has announced a target of attracting 6.63 million international visitors in 2026, shifting focus toward promoting high-quality tourism.
