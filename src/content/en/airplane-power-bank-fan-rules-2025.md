## ✈️ Portable Charger (Power Bank) Regulations

![Portable Charger (Power Bank) Regulations](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0f872f41d331409831d5203d1221cea7.png)

---

According to the International Air Transport Association (IATA) and most airline regulations, the key factor is the 'Wh' value, which will be explained at the end:

- **Below 100Wh**: Can be carried in hand luggage without airline approval.
- **100Wh to 160Wh**: Requires airline approval, with a maximum of two per passenger.
- **Above 160Wh**: Prohibited from being carried on board.
- **Unmarked Capacity**: If the power bank does not indicate its capacity or cannot be calculated from other parameters, it is prohibited from being carried on board.

Generally, power banks with a capacity below 20,000mAh usually comply with regulations and are often rated below 100Wh.

Additionally, power banks must be placed in carry-on luggage and are strictly prohibited in checked baggage.

## 🌬️ Battery-Operated USB Neck Fan Regulations

The regulations for carrying USB neck fans primarily depend on the type of battery they use:

隨身USB風扇和掛脖風扇的攜帶規定主要取決於其使用的電池類型：

- **Lithium Batteries**:
- - Can be carried in carry-on luggage but are prohibited in checked baggage.
  - It is recommended to choose products with international certifications and ensure the battery capacity is within regulated limits.
- **Dry Batteries**:
- - Can be carried in carry-on or checked luggage.
  - Pay attention to airline restrictions on the number of dry batteries.
- **Lead-Acid Batteries**:
- - Prohibited in both carry-on and checked luggage.
  - Due to their instability, lead-acid batteries can swell or even explode from impact or pressure changes, posing a threat to aviation safety.

---

## ✅ Tips and Reminders

- **Before Purchase**: Choose products with clear labeling of battery type and capacity. Avoid purchasing items with unclear origins or labels.
- **Before Departure**: Check the latest regulations of your airline to ensure your items comply.
- **Security Check**: Place battery-containing devices in your carry-on luggage for easier inspection during security checks.

---

In summary, power banks and battery-operated portable fans/neck fans can be carried on planes if they meet relevant regulations. To ensure a smooth journey, it’s recommended to thoroughly understand and comply with airline rules before traveling.

## 🔋 A Simple Explanation of What Wh Is

**Wh** stands for **Watt-hour**, which refers to the **rated energy capacity** of a battery. This value measures how much energy a battery can store and is a crucial factor in aviation transportation regulations.

Wh (Watt-hour) = Voltage (V) × Capacity (Ah)

For example:

- If a power bank is **3.7V × 10,000mAh (which is 10Ah)**,
- Its energy capacity is: **3.7V × 10Ah = 37Wh**.

Such a capacity is **completely allowed on planes** because it falls below the 100Wh limit.

---

### ✈️ Why do airlines restrict Wh?

Devices containing lithium batteries with excessively high capacity pose risks of overheating, expansion, and even fire in high-altitude environments. Therefore, aviation safety regulations impose clear limits on Wh values.
