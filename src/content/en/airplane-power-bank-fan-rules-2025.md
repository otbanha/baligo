When traveling by air, carrying portable chargers (power banks) and battery-operated neck fans requires adherence to specific aviation safety regulations. Here’s a guide for these items:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0f872f41d331409831d5203d1221cea7.png)

---

## ✈️ Portable Charger (Power Bank) Regulations

According to the International Air Transport Association (IATA) and most airlines, the key factor is the "Wh" value, which will be explained at the end:

- **Below 100Wh**: Can be carried in hand luggage without airline approval.
- **100Wh to 160Wh**: Requires airline approval, with a maximum of two per passenger.
- **Above 160Wh**: Prohibited on board.
- **Unmarked Capacity**: If the portable charger does not indicate its capacity or cannot be calculated through other parameters, it is prohibited on board.

Generally, portable chargers with a capacity below 20,000mAh usually comply with regulations, and their rated capacity is mostly below 100Wh.

Additionally, portable chargers must be placed in hand luggage and are strictly prohibited in checked baggage.

## 🌬️ Battery-Operated USB Neck Fan Regulations

The regulations for carrying USB neck fans mainly depend on the type of battery they use:

- **Lithium Batteries**:
- - Can be carried in hand luggage but are prohibited in checked baggage.
  - It is recommended to choose products with international certification and ensure the battery capacity is within regulated limits.
- **Dry Batteries**:
- - Can be carried in hand luggage or checked baggage.
  - Pay attention to airline restrictions on the number of dry batteries.
- **Lead-Acid Batteries**:
- - Prohibited in both hand luggage and checked baggage.
  - Due to their instability, lead-acid batteries can expand or even explode from impact or pressure changes, posing a threat to aviation safety.

---

## ✅ Tips and Reminders

- **Before Purchase**: Choose products with clear labeling of battery type and capacity, and avoid items with unknown sources or unclear labeling.
- **Before Departure**: Check the latest regulations of your airline to ensure your items comply.
- **Security Check**: Place battery-containing devices in your hand luggage for easier inspection during security checks.

---

In summary, power banks and battery-operated portable fans/neck fans can be carried on planes if they meet relevant regulations. However, to ensure a smooth journey, it is recommended to thoroughly understand and comply with airline regulations before traveling.

---

## 🔋 A Simple Explanation of What Wh Is

**Wh** refers to the "**Rated Energy Capacity (Watt-hour)**" of a battery, abbreviated as Wh. This value measures how much energy a battery can store and is a crucial basis for aviation transportation regulations.

Wh (Watt-hour) = Voltage (V) × Capacity (Ah)

For example:

- If a power bank is **3.7V × 10,000mAh (which is 10Ah)**,
- Its energy capacity is: **3.7V × 10Ah = 37Wh**.

Such a capacity is **completely allowed on planes** as it is below the 100Wh limit.

---

### ✈️ Why Do Airlines Limit Wh?

Devices containing lithium batteries pose risks of overheating, swelling, or even catching fire in high-altitude environments if their capacity is too high. Therefore, aviation safety regulations impose clear limits on Wh values.
