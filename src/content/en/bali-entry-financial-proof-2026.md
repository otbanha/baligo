Analysis of Bali's 'High-Quality Tourism' Policy for 2026: New Financial Proof and Itinerary Verification System Starting March

In summary: This new policy is not about conducting a 'census' of all travelers at customs, but rather formalizing and digitizing immigration officers' existing verification powers. The goal is to accurately select quality tourists while cracking down on illegal work.

__VID0__

Below, we break down the practical implementation details of the policy to help you understand if you will be affected:

## 1. Who Are the Main Targets of 'Strict Verification'?

The new system **does not target all entrants**, but rather combines 'spot checks' with 'digital pre-screening':

- High-risk groups (focused checks): Officials will pay special attention to travelers staying for long periods (e.g., over 30 days), those frequently entering and exiting in a short time frame, or those with illogical itineraries in the online declaration system.
- Digital pre-screening: Most reviews will be completed in the backend system when you apply for an electronic visa (e-VOA) or fill out the health declaration form.
- General tourists: If you are planning a one-week vacation and have booked a star-rated hotel, the system will usually let you pass without much interference.

The primary purpose of this requirement is to "prevent illegal employment," not to assess how wealthy you are.

- Combating illegal work: Officials mainly monitor bank statements to observe regular "salary deposits" or "service payments." To them, a traveler whose account only shows consumption expenses without fixed income is considered a purer "consumer."
- Deposit amount standards:

1. Official recommendation: Hold more than USD$2,000 (approximately NT$63,000).
2. Practical operation: For most short-term travelers, showing a mobile banking screenshot proving sufficient funds to cover the trip expenses is usually acceptable.

## 3. Customs on-site: Primarily "random checks"

Immigration officers have always had the legal authority to request proof of financial means from travelers, though it was rarely enforced in the past. Starting in March, they will have clearer grounds to conduct "random checks."

- Triggers for inspection: If officials find your attire, behavior, or responses about your itinerary suspicious, they may request on-the-spot proof of financial means and detailed travel plans.
- Itinerary planning is key: If you claim to stay somewhere for two weeks but cannot name a single hotel, this could be sufficient grounds for denial of entry.

## 💡 Conclusion: Should general tourists worry?

Not at all. Especially for tourists planning short trips of 5 to 10 days, as long as you are traveling legitimately and have the following documents prepared, the likelihood of being stopped is extremely low, and the customs process will be very smooth:

1. Complete accommodation booking confirmations (not just for the first day, preferably covering the entire itinerary).
2. Confirmed return flight tickets.

Policy target group: This policy mainly aims to deter low-budget long-term travelers who want to "work while traveling" and those illegally working as guides, photographers, or renting accommodations locally.

## 📋 Essential Documents for Bali Entry Starting March 2026

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_164e1666147c790e2ecfa35259c44293.png)

### Core Documents

- Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months.
- Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA): Recommended to apply online in advance. (See [guide](/blog/bali-evisa-application-guide/))
- Return/Onward Flight Ticket: Ensure the date matches the visa stay period.

### Electronic Declarations

- [All Indonesia Arrival Card](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/) (AIAC): Complete online within 72 hours before departure.
- Bali Tourist Levy: Pay IDR 150,000 online and save the payment receipt.

### New Inspection Documents

- Non-B1 Tourist Visa (over 30 days) **Mandatory Bank Statement**: Recent 3-month bank statement (PDF or online banking screenshot), recommended balance of at least USD$2,000. Officials will check for regular "salary deposits".
- Detailed Travel Itinerary: Must be able to specify accommodation locations and activities.

Final Reminder: Organize all electronic copies (PDFs or screenshots) of the above documents in a folder on your phone. If randomly inspected, proactively and cooperatively present them, and you should clear customs smoothly within a minute or two. Do not alter documents, as authorities have implemented AI verification systems to detect fraud, and violators will face immediate deportation and lifetime blacklisting.

## 🏛️ Bali 2026 High-Quality Tourism: Financial Proof Verification FAQ

**Q1: Will everyone be asked to show bank account details upon entry?**

> **No.** While the policy nominally applies to all foreign travelers, in practice it's implemented through **random checks**:
> >
> - **Applying for visas over 60 days (e.g., C1/D1)**: You **must** upload three months of bank statements during the application process — this is mandatory.
> - **Holding a 30-day e-VOA**: **Random spot checks** are conducted. If you have a confirmed return ticket and reservations at reputable hotels, the chance of being checked is extremely low.
> - **Key targets**: Frequent travelers to Bali (suspected visa runners), those staying for exceptionally long periods, or individuals unable to provide specific itineraries are the primary focus of immigration officers.

**Q2: What is the 'recommended balance' for financial proof?**

> **The official benchmark is USD$2,000 (approx. HKD$16,000 / NT$65,000).** This isn't a rigid number but rather a 'safe zone.' If you're only staying for 5 days at a luxury resort, having slightly less than this amount is usually acceptable. However, if you're staying for a month, this amount serves as crucial evidence that you 'won't engage in illegal work locally.'

**Q3: Can I just show the immigration officer my mobile banking app?**

> **Yes.** The authorities currently accept digital proof. If you're randomly checked at customs, you can directly open your mobile banking app to display your real-time balance. However, the safest approach is to keep a **PDF of your bank statements for the past three months** saved on your phone.

**Q4: Why do they check 'three months' of records? Can't they just look at the current balance?**

> This is to rule out cases of **borrowing money temporarily to meet the requirement**. Immigration officers will assess whether your financial flow is stable and check for frequent 'unexplained work-related deposits' (to determine if you're already engaged in illegal remote work locally).

**Q5: Besides bank deposits, what other documents can be used as supplementary proof?**

> If your account balance doesn't quite meet the requirement, the following documents can also help:
> >
> - **Credit limit proof** (demonstrating emergency payment capability).
> - **Salary slips**.
> - **Receipts for prepaid hotel bookings** (showing you've already covered major expenses).

**Q6: What happens if I don't cooperate with the check or if my financial proof is insufficient?**

> According to new regulations effective in 2026, immigration officers have the authority to **deny entry and deport travelers** based on 'failure to meet high-quality tourism standards' or 'risk of illegal employment.'

**Q7: What about housewives, elderly, and children without income?**

For family travel, immigration checks typically focus on the 'primary sponsor':

> Housewives and children: No need to provide personal bank statements. When applying for an e-VOA or during on-site checks, they can present the bank statements of their spouse or parents, along with proof of relationship (such as marriage certificates, birth certificates, or household registration photos stored on their phone).
> Shared financial proof: If one account balance significantly exceeds $2,000 (e.g., $8,000 or more for a family of four), it will be considered sufficient financial proof for the entire group.

**Q8: Which travelers are more likely to be questioned?**

> The following situations are more likely to prompt inquiries from immigration officers:
> One-way ticket
> No hotel booking
> Long stay duration
> Unclear travel itinerary
> Immigration officers mainly aim to confirm whether travelers **have sufficient funds and a reasonable travel plan**.

**Q9: What happens if I don’t have financial proof?**

> If you’re selected for inspection and cannot provide financial proof
> Immigration officers may:
> Ask further questions
> Request additional documents
> In the most severe cases, deny entry
> However, such situations are extremely rare.

**Q10: Essential documents currently required for entering Bali**

> Currently, the most important documents for general travelers are:
> ✔ Passport (valid for 6 months)
> ✔ Return or onward flight ticket
> ✔ Visa (or visa on arrival)
> ✔ Accommodation booking
> ✔ Simple travel itinerary
> ✔ Bali tourism tax QR code
> If you also prepare a **bank balance screenshot or PDF bank statement**, you’ll be fully prepared.
