Analysis of Bali's 'High-Quality Tourism' Policy for 2026: New Financial Proof and Itinerary Verification System Starting March

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a1316fab9941800e7f0a4551426ccb6e.png)

In a nutshell: This new policy is not about conducting a 'census' of all travelers at customs, but rather about 'codifying' and 'digitizing' the existing verification powers of immigration officers. The goal is to accurately screen quality tourists while cracking down on illegal work.

https://youtu.be/7JIqxDIyDF0

Below, we break down the practical implementation details of the policy to help you understand whether you will be affected:

## 1. Who Are the Main Targets of 'Strict Verification'?

The new system **does not apply to all entrants**, but rather combines 'targeted checks' with 'digital pre-screening':

- High-risk groups (focused checks): Officers will pay special attention to travelers with long stays (e.g., over 30 days), frequent short-term entries, or those whose itinerary logic in the online declaration system appears unreasonable.
- Digital pre-screening: Most reviews will be completed in the backend system when you apply for an e-VOA or fill out the health declaration form.
- General tourists: If you are planning a week-long vacation and have already booked a star-rated hotel, the system will usually let you pass without much interference.

## 2. The Real Purpose of 'Bank Deposit Proof'

The primary purpose of this requirement is to 'prevent illegal employment,' not to assess how wealthy you are.

- Combating illegal work: Officials primarily scrutinize bank statements to observe regular 'salary deposits' or 'service payments.' To them, a traveler with only consumption expenses and no fixed income is considered a more genuine 'consumer.'
- Deposit amount standards:

1. Official recommendation: Hold USD$2,000 (approximately NT$63,000) or more.
2. Practical operation: For most short-term travelers, simply showing a mobile banking screenshot proving sufficient funds to cover the trip expenses is adequate.

## 3. Customs on-site: Primarily 'random checks'

Immigration officers have always had the legal authority to request proof of financial means from travelers, though it was rarely enforced in the past. Starting in March, they will have clearer grounds to conduct 'random checks.'

- Triggers for inspection: If officials find your attire, behavior, or responses about your itinerary suspicious, they will ask you to provide proof of financial means and a detailed itinerary on the spot.
- Itinerary planning is crucial: If you claim to stay somewhere for two weeks but cannot name a single hotel, this constitutes sufficient grounds for denial of entry.

## 💡 Conclusion: Should general tourists worry?

Not at all. Especially for tourists planning short trips of 5 to 10 days, as long as you are traveling legitimately and have the following documents prepared, the likelihood of being stopped is minimal, and the immigration process will be very smooth:

1. Complete accommodation booking confirmations (not just for the first day, preferably covering the entire trip).
2. Confirmed return flight tickets.

Policy target group: This policy primarily aims to deter low-budget long-term travelers who want to 'work while traveling,' as well as those illegally working as guides, photographers, or renting accommodations locally.

## 📋 Essential Documents for Bali Entry Starting March 2026

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_164e1666147c790e2ecfa35259c44293.png)

### Core Documents

- Passport: Must be valid for at least 6 months.
- Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA): Recommended to apply online in advance. (See [guide](/blog/bali-evisa-application-guide/))
- Return/Onward Flight Ticket: Ensure dates align with visa duration.

### Electronic Declarations

- [All Indonesia Arrival Card](/blog/bali-all-indonesia-arrival-guide/) (AIAC): Complete online within 72 hours before departure.
- Bali Tourist Levy: Pay IDR 150,000 online and save the payment receipt.

### New Random Check Documents

- Non-B1 Tourist Visa (over 30 days) **Mandatory Bank Statement**: Recent 3-month bank statement (PDF or online banking screenshot), recommended balance of at least USD$2,000. Officers will check for regular "salary deposits."
- Detailed Itinerary: Must be able to specify accommodation locations and travel activities.

Final Reminder: Organize all electronic copies (PDFs or screenshots) of the above documents in a folder on your phone. If randomly checked, proactively and cooperatively present them, and you should clear customs within a minute or two. Do not alter documents, as authorities have implemented AI systems to detect fraud, and violators face immediate deportation and lifetime blacklisting.

## 🏛️ Bali 2026 High-Quality Travel: Financial Proof Verification FAQ

**Q1: Will everyone be asked to show bank account details upon entry?**

> **No.** While the policy nominally applies to all foreign travelers, in practice it's implemented through **random spot checks**:
> >
> - **Applying for visas over 60 days (e.g., C1/D1)**: You **must** upload three months of bank statements during application — this is mandatory.
> - **Holding a 30-day e-VOA**: **Random spot checks** are conducted. If you have confirmed return tickets and bookings at reputable hotels, the chance of being checked is extremely low.
> - **Key targets**: Frequent visitors to Bali (suspected of visa runs), extremely long stays, or those unable to provide specific itineraries are the immigration officers' main focus.

**Q2: What is the 'recommended balance' for financial proof?**

> **The official benchmark is USD$2,000 (approx. HKD$16,000 / NT$65,000).** This isn't a rigid number but rather a 'safe zone.' If you're only visiting for 5 days but staying at luxury resorts, a balance slightly below this amount is usually acceptable. However, if you're staying for a month, this amount serves as crucial evidence that you 'won't engage in illegal work locally.'

**Q3: Can I just show the immigration officer my mobile banking app?**

> **Yes.** The authorities currently accept digital proof. If you're randomly checked at customs, you can directly open your mobile banking app to show your real-time balance. However, the safest approach is to keep a **PDF of your bank statements for the past three months** saved on your phone.

**Q4: Why do they check 'three months' of records? Can't they just look at the current balance?**

> Because the authorities want to rule out cases of **temporary borrowing to meet requirements**. Immigration officers will observe whether your cash flow is stable and check for frequent 'unexplained work-related deposits' (to determine if you're already working illegally remotely in the country).

**Q5: Besides bank deposits, what other documents can serve as supplementary proof?**

> If your account balance doesn't quite meet the requirement, the following documents can also help:
> >
> - **Credit limit proof** (demonstrating emergency payment capability).
> - **Salary slips**.
> - **Prepaid hotel booking receipts** (showing you've already covered major expenses).

**Q6: What happens if I don't cooperate with the check or if my financial proof is insufficient?**

> According to the new regulations in 2026, immigration officers have the authority to **deny entry and deport** individuals based on "failure to meet high-quality tourism standards" or "risk of illegal employment."

**Q7: What about homemakers, elderly, and children with no income?**

For family travel, immigration checks typically focus on the "primary sponsor":

> Homemakers and children: No need to provide personal bank statements. When applying for an e-VOA or during on-site checks, you can present the bank statement of your spouse or parent, along with proof of relationship (such as marriage certificate, birth certificate, or household registration photos stored on your phone).
> Shared financial proof: If one account balance significantly exceeds $2,000 (e.g., $8,000 for a family of four), this will be considered sufficient financial proof for the entire group.

**Q8: Which travelers are more likely to be questioned?**

> The following situations are more likely to prompt questions from immigration officers:
> One-way ticket
> No hotel booking
> Long stay
> Unclear travel itinerary
> Immigration officers mainly want to confirm that travelers **have sufficient funds and a reasonable travel plan**.

**Q9: What happens if I don’t have financial proof?**

> If you are checked and cannot provide financial proof
> The immigration officer has the right to:
> Ask further questions
> Request additional documents
> In the most severe case, deny entry
> However, such cases are actually very rare.

**Q10: Essential documents for entering Bali**

> Currently, the most important documents for general travelers are:
> ✔ Passport (valid for 6 months)
> ✔ Return or onward flight ticket
> ✔ Visa (or visa on arrival)
> ✔ Accommodation booking
> ✔ Simple travel itinerary
> ✔ Bali tourism tax QR code
> If you also prepare a **bank balance screenshot or PDF bank statement**, you’re essentially fully prepared.
