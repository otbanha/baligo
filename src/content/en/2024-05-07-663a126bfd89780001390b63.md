## Additional Tips

**Can I tip in currencies other than Indonesian Rupiah?**

When tipping service staff in Bali, it's usually most convenient to use Indonesian Rupiah. This is because the Rupiah is the only currency officially used in Indonesia and is widely accepted. Service staff can easily use Rupiah for their daily expenses without incurring additional currency conversion fees.

However, if you only have other currencies like USD or EUR, it's generally acceptable to tip service staff with these. In some tourist hotspots, such as hotels or tourist attractions, major foreign currencies may be accepted. But be aware that service staff might need to go through extra steps to convert foreign currency into Rupiah, which could cause some inconvenience or additional costs.

To ensure hassle-free tipping, it's advisable to carry some Indonesian Rupiah in cash during your stay in Bali. This ensures you can provide local currency immediately when tipping, without worrying about currency conversion or other issues.
