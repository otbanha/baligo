## Additional Notes

**Can I tip in currencies other than Indonesian Rupiah?**

When tipping service staff in Bali, it's most convenient to use Indonesian Rupiah. This is because the Rupiah is the sole currency in circulation locally and is widely accepted and used. Service staff can easily use the Rupiah to cover their expenses without incurring additional currency conversion fees.

However, if you only have other currencies such as US dollars or euros, you can usually still tip service staff. In some tourist hotspots like hotels or attractions, they may accept other major currencies. But please note that service staff might need to take extra steps to convert foreign currency into Indonesian rupiah, which could lead to some inconvenience or additional fees.

To ensure convenience and peace of mind when tipping, it's recommended to carry some Indonesian Rupiah in cash during your stay in Bali. This way, you can promptly offer local currency when tipping is needed, without worrying about currency conversion or other issues.
