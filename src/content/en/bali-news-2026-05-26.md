## 1. World Bank Warns Bali Faces Five Key Challenges ⚠️

The World Bank recently issued a stern warning to Indonesia, highlighting that Bali is facing five critical issues. Without swift and effective action, this premier Indonesian tourist destination could face long-term risks. During a meeting with Bali Governor Wayan Koster, Carolyn Turk, the World Bank's Country Director for Indonesia, emphasized five urgent challenges: sewage treatment and recycling, waste management, traffic mobility, clean water supply, and electricity supply in parts of Bali. These issues not only threaten Bali's tourism-dependent economy but also directly impact residents' quality of life and access to basic services. Governor Koster stated that infrastructure development directly affects the sustainability of Bali's tourism industry and requires serious, well-planned policies.

(Source: antaranews.com / VNA, May 25, 2026)

## 2. Bali's Tourism Boom Sparks Debate on Sustainability and Public Spaces 🌴

Bali's tourism industry is rapidly growing, particularly in areas like Canggu and Uluwatu, where infrastructure strain, worsening traffic congestion, and diminishing public spaces are increasingly becoming focal points in local discussions about overtourism. Lev Kroll, President of Nuanu Creative City, pointed out that many development projects focus solely on short-term commercial value without considering the long-term impacts on communities, infrastructure, culture, and the environment. He emphasized that Bali is not just a tourism product but a living cultural ecosystem with a unique identity. As tourism investments continue to pour in, developers and policymakers face growing pressure to address infrastructure strain, environmental sustainability, and the long-term effects of rapid commercial expansion.

(Source: thebalisun.com, May 22, 2026)

## 3. Bali Steps Up Immigration Patrols Amid Rising Crimes Involving Foreign Tourists 👮

Bali's immigration authorities have recently increased patrols in response to a rise in crimes involving foreign tourists. This move aims to maintain local order and tourism safety, ensuring a secure environment for all visitors and residents. Immigration officials stated that they will work closely with local law enforcement to crack down on illegal activities and conduct stricter checks on foreign tourists' visas and residency status. Authorities urge all tourists to comply with local laws and regulations, respect local cultural customs, and collectively uphold Bali's reputation as a safe tourist destination.

(Source: thebalisun.com, May 22, 2026)

## 4. Balinese Artisans Transform Driftwood into Exportable Art and Furniture 🎨

Balinese artists and craftsmen are transforming driftwood collected from beaches into unique art pieces and furniture, successfully exporting them to international markets. This creative industry not only showcases Bali's rich artistic talent but also provides economic opportunities for local communities. These driftwood creations blend natural aesthetics with exquisite craftsmanship, earning them popularity among international buyers. This sustainable approach to art also reflects Bali's commitment to environmental protection, giving new life to discarded materials while promoting the island's cultural and artistic identity.

(Source: thebalisun.com, May 22, 2026)

## 5. Bali Promotes Electric Vehicle Adoption to Ease Traffic Congestion and Air Pollution 🚗

To address growing traffic congestion and air pollution, the Balinese government is actively promoting the adoption of electric vehicles. This policy aims to encourage residents and tourists to adopt greener transportation methods, reducing reliance on fossil fuels. The government plans to provide subsidies and infrastructure support, such as expanding charging stations, to accelerate the transition to electric vehicles. This initiative not only helps improve Bali's air quality and traffic conditions but also aligns with its sustainable tourism development goals, contributing to a greener and more livable island.

(Source: antaranews.com, May 25, 2026)
