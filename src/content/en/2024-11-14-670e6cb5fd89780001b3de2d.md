## Essential Preparations Before Departure:

1. **An Amazing Chinese-Speaking Driver**  
   Having a Chinese-speaking private driver made this trip stress-free. His fluent Chinese allowed him to take us to all the popular spots, and he adjusted the itinerary flexibly based on time and traffic, ensuring a smooth journey.
2. **Confirm Itinerary with the Driver**  
   Before departure, make sure to communicate your daily plans with the driver. He’ll provide professional suggestions based on your needs to ensure the best experience at each destination.
3. **Exchange NT$ to USD**  
   Before leaving, exchange some NT$ to USD and then convert it to Indonesian Rupiah in Bali. This is the most cost-effective way to handle currency exchange.
4. **E-Visa**  
   Don’t forget to apply for Indonesia’s e-visa in advance. This saves time during airport immigration and makes the start of your journey smoother.
5. **Tourism Tax**  
   Bali charges a tourism tax for incoming travelers. It’s recommended to pay it online in advance to avoid hassle upon arrival.
6. **Customs Declaration Form**  
   Three days before departure, fill out the customs declaration form online to save time during immigration.
7. **Health Declaration and Flight Ticket**  
   On the day of departure, ensure you have your flight ticket and complete the health declaration form to avoid last-minute panic.
8. **Prepare Basic Medications**  
   Although this trip isn’t long, it’s still wise to bring basic medications like stomach and cold medicine, just in case.

These preparations might seem tedious, but they ensure a smoother journey, allowing me to fully enjoy every moment in Bali!

## Day 1 Itinerary:

**Arrival in Bali at 15:15**  
The plane landed right on time at Bali International Airport, officially kicking off our highly anticipated journey!

**Present completed health declaration**  
After disembarking, the first step was to present our pre-filled health declaration. This process was completed quickly, giving us a smooth first impression of Bali's pandemic protocols.

**Present completed e-visa**  
Next, we presented our e-visa. Since we had prepared it in advance, we bypassed the queues and used facial recognition technology for swift clearance. This was incredibly convenient and hassle-free!

**Present customs declaration form**  
The final step was presenting our customs declaration form, which we had also completed online beforehand. As a result, the entire immigration process went very smoothly.

**Wait for luggage**  
The clearance process was so fast that waiting for our luggage ended up taking the longest time 🤣! But once we had our bags, we felt much more relaxed.

**Meet the driver**  
As soon as we exited the airport, our pre-booked driver was waiting for us. Seeing him instantly made the trip feel more solid and organized! Ready to begin our culinary adventure in Bali.

**Dinner: Naughty Nuri's Warung, Sanur (Delicious Pork Ribs)**

## Day 2 Itinerary (Nusa Lembongan Adventure):

**Driver Pickup at Hotel**  
Early in the morning, our private driver arrived punctually to pick us up from the hotel. Today's adventure takes us to the beautiful Nusa Lembongan—full of anticipation!

**Purchase Nusa Lembongan Tour at Sanur Pier**  
Upon arriving at Sanur Pier, we purchased a day trip to Nusa Lembongan on the spot. There were many options available, and we chose a package that included snorkeling and sightseeing, offering excellent value.

**Thoughtful Chinese-Speaking Guide**  
The highlight of the tour was the thoughtful arrangement of a Chinese-speaking guide who accompanied us throughout the day, providing explanations and ensuring a smooth, enriching experience. No language barriers meant we could dive deeper into the local natural landscapes and culture.

**Exciting Activities: Snorkeling, Devil's Tear, Dream Beach, Mangrove Forest**  
At Nusa Lembongan's snorkeling spot, we marveled at the crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine life, surrounded by colorful tropical fish—truly breathtaking! Next, we visited the famous 'Devil's Tear,' where massive waves crashed against the rocks, creating a spectacular and awe-inspiring sight. Then, we arrived at Dream Beach, living up to its name with its fine white sand and turquoise waters—pure relaxation. Finally, we explored the mangrove forest, getting up close to this natural wonder. The day was packed with diverse and unforgettable experiences.

## Day 3 Itinerary (Ubud Day Trip):

**White Water Rafting Adventure**  
Our Ubud adventure kicked off with thrilling white water rafting. Navigating through Bali's magnificent tropical rainforests and drifting along crystal-clear rivers, we felt the exhilaration of the rapids—a must-do for adventure enthusiasts. The entire experience was filled with surprises and challenges, making it unforgettable!

**Instagram-Worthy Cafe: Swing Photography**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57b0b41ac469b22436f739a53fe74f4f.jpg)

After rafting, we visited a very famous Instagram-worthy cafe known for its giant swing. Here, we could swing while enjoying the breathtaking view of the rice fields. The locals are really skilled at photography, and the atmosphere made every photo look like a postcard. It's truly a great spot for photo ops and social media check-ins.

**Classic Bebek Bengil (Dirty Duck)**  
For lunch, we headed to Ubud's famous Bebek Bengil restaurant. This dish is a classic Ubud delicacy, featuring crispy duck skin paired with tender duck meat and a variety of local sauces. It's a must-try when in Bali!

**Ubud Palace**  
After lunch, we visited the historic Ubud Palace. The palace's architecture is distinctly Balinese, with intricate carvings and elaborate decorations that leave visitors in awe, offering a deep sense of the local culture and history.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2cb88da21144a9adcc0b17e6c870ab0a.jpg)

**Ubud Market**  
Next, we strolled through Ubud Market, which is filled with handicrafts, clothing, and souvenirs. The market's lively atmosphere was infectious, and we picked up a few local souvenirs to remember our trip by.

### ❤️ Reflections:

1. **Prepare Your Entry Documents in Advance!**  
   During this trip, I noticed many travelers only started handling their documents at the last minute, causing long queues and chaos. Make sure to prepare your health certificate, e-visa, customs declaration, and other documents ahead of time to ensure a smoother entry process.
2. **Bali Has Many Attractions, but Traffic Can Be Heavy — Don’t Overpack Your Itinerary!**  
   Bali offers a diverse range of attractions, but traffic can be intense, especially during peak hours. It's best not to overpack your schedule to avoid wasting too much time in traffic. The best way to experience Bali is to chill and soak in the local pace of life.
3. **Remember to Haggle at Markets!**  
   When shopping at markets, always haggle! For example, I bought a coin purse at Love Anchor, negotiating the price down from USD$10 to USD$4, and even got another item down to USD$3. There's definitely room for negotiation, so don't be shy — speak up! 😅
4. **Limited English + Three Months Pregnant, Thankfully Met a Local Who Speaks Chinese**  
   During this trip, despite being three months pregnant and worried about my limited English, I was fortunate to meet a local driver, Kang Ge (Wayan Suantara), who speaks Chinese. His thoughtful assistance made the entire journey very reassuring, especially with route planning and attraction recommendations.
5. **Souvenir Recommendation: BALI BANANA Banana Cake**  
   Don’t forget to pick up a box of BALI BANANA banana cake before heading home — it's a signature Balinese souvenir with a soft, sweet flavor. You can find it at the airport. Try it upon arrival, and if you like it, buy more to take back. They even offer free packaging services, making shopping more convenient.
