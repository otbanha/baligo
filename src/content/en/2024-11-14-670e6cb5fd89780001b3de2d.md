## Essential Preparations Before Departure:

1. **An Amazing Chinese-Speaking Driver**  
   Having a Chinese-speaking private driver for this trip completely eliminated my transportation worries. His Chinese is very fluent, not only taking us to all the popular local attractions but also flexibly adjusting the itinerary based on time and traffic conditions, making the journey smoother.
2. **Confirming the Itinerary with the Driver**  
   Before departure, remember to communicate the daily itinerary with the driver in advance. The driver will provide professional suggestions based on our needs to ensure the best experience at each attraction.
3. **Exchanging NTD to USD**  
   Before departure, remember to exchange some USD and then convert it to Indonesian Rupiah after arriving in Bali. This is the most cost-effective way to exchange money.
4. **Electronic Visa**  
   Don't forget to apply for Indonesia's electronic visa in advance, which can save time queuing at the airport upon arrival and make the start of the journey smoother.
5. **Tourism Tax**  
   Bali charges a tourism tax for incoming tourists. It's recommended to pay it online in advance to avoid cumbersome procedures upon arrival.
6. **Customs Declaration Form**  
   Three days before departure, remember to fill out the customs declaration form online, which can save a lot of time upon entry.
7. **Health Certificate and Air Tickets**  
   On the day of departure, remember to obtain your air tickets and fill out the health certificate to ensure all documents are complete and accurate, avoiding last-minute panic at the airport.
8. **Preparing Essential Medications**  
   Although this trip isn't long, it's still good to prepare some basic medications just in case, such as stomach medicine and cold medicine.

These preparations may seem tedious, but they all contribute to a smoother journey, allowing me to fully enjoy every moment in Bali!

## Day 1 Itinerary:

****Arrival in Bali at 15:15****  
The plane landed right on schedule at Bali International Airport, marking the exciting start of our journey!

****Presenting Completed Health Declaration****  
After disembarking, the first step was to present our pre-filled health declaration. This process went smoothly, showcasing Bali's efficient health protocols.

****Presenting Completed eVisa****  
Next up was presenting our eVisa. Since we had prepared it in advance, we bypassed the queues and used facial recognition technology for a swift entry—super convenient and hassle-free!

****Presenting Customs Declaration Form****  
The final step was presenting our customs declaration form, which we had also completed online beforehand, making the entire immigration process seamless.

****Waiting for Luggage****  
The immigration process was so quick that waiting for our luggage ended up taking the longest time 🤣! But once we had our bags, we felt much more at ease.

****Meeting Our Driver****  
As soon as we exited the airport, our pre-booked driver was waiting for us. Seeing him instantly made the trip feel more secure! Ready to embark on Bali's culinary adventure.

****Dinner: Naughty Nuri's Warung, Sanur (Delicious Pork Ribs)****  
For our first meal in Bali, we chose Naughty Nuri's Warung in Sanur, famous for its signature pork ribs. It lived up to its reputation—tender meat paired with a secret sauce that awakened our taste buds. Absolutely delicious! A must-visit spot on any Bali itinerary.

## Day 2 Itinerary (Nusa Lembongan Exploration):

****Driver Pickup at Hotel in the Morning****  
Early in the morning, our private driver arrived punctually at our hotel to take us to our destination for the day: the beautiful Nusa Lembongan. We were filled with anticipation!

****Purchase Nusa Lembongan Tour at Sanur Pier****  
Upon arriving at Sanur Pier, we purchased a day tour package to Nusa Lembongan on the spot. There were many options available, and we chose one that included snorkeling and sightseeing, offering excellent value for money.

****Thoughtful Arrangement of Chinese-Speaking Guide****  
One of the highlights of the tour was the inclusion of a Chinese-speaking guide who accompanied us throughout the day, providing explanations and making the experience more seamless and enriching. This eliminated any language barriers and deepened our understanding of the local natural landscapes and culture.

****Exciting Activities: Snorkeling, Devil's Tear, Dream Beach, Mangrove Ecosystem****  
At the snorkeling spot on Nusa Lembongan, we marveled at the crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine life, surrounded by colorful tropical fish. Next, we visited the famous 'Devil's Tear,' where massive waves crashed against the rocks, creating a spectacular and awe-inspiring sight. We then headed to Dream Beach, which lived up to its name with its fine white sand and turquoise waters, offering complete relaxation. Finally, we explored the mangrove ecosystem, getting up close with the local natural wonders. The day was packed with diverse and memorable experiences.

## Day 3 Itinerary (Ubud Day Trip):

****White Water Rafting Adventure****  
Our Ubud adventure began with thrilling white water rafting. We navigated through Bali's magnificent tropical rainforest, drifting on clear river waters, and experienced the excitement of rapids—a must-do for adventure lovers. The entire journey was filled with surprises and challenges, making it unforgettable!

****Instagram-Worthy Cafe: Swing and Snap****

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57b0b41ac469b22436f739a53fe74f4f.jpg)

After rafting, we visited a highly popular Instagram-worthy cafe known for its giant swing. While swinging, we enjoyed breathtaking views of the lush rice fields. The locals are truly skilled at photography, and the entire ambiance made every photo look like a postcard. It's an ideal spot for capturing memorable moments.

![Tebasari Resto, Bar & Lounge](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_de5feafe85fbc690903411bfb7bf280f.jpg)

****Classic Bebek Bengil (Dirty Duck Rice)****  
For lunch, we headed to Ubud's famous Bebek Bengil restaurant. This dish is a Ubud classic, featuring crispy duck skin paired with tender meat and complemented by a variety of local sauces. It's a must-try delicacy in Bali!

****Ubud Palace****  
After lunch, we visited the historic Ubud Palace. The architecture is distinctly Balinese, with intricate carvings and elaborate decorations that left us in awe, immersing us in the local culture and history.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2cb88da21144a9adcc0b17e6c870ab0a.jpg)

****Ubud Market****  
Next, we explored the bustling Ubud Market, filled with handmade crafts, clothing, and souvenirs. The lively atmosphere was infectious, and we picked up a few unique local mementos to remember our trip by.

### ❤️ Reflections:

1. ****Make sure to prepare your entry documents in advance!****  
   During my trip, I noticed many travelers waited until the last minute to handle their documents, causing long queues and chaos at the entry points. So, be sure to prepare your health certificates, e-visas, and customs declarations beforehand to ensure a smoother and faster entry process.
2. ****Bali has many attractions, but traffic can be heavy—don’t overpack your itinerary!****  
   Bali offers a diverse range of attractions, but traffic can be intense, especially during peak hours. I recommend keeping your schedule flexible to avoid wasting too much time in transit. The best way to experience Bali is to chill and soak in the local pace of life.
3. ****Don’t forget to haggle at the markets!****  
   When shopping at local markets, always negotiate the price! For example, I bought a coin purse at Love Anchor, originally priced at $10, but managed to bring it down to $5. At another stall, I even got it for $3. So, there’s definitely room for bargaining—don’t be shy, just go for it! 😅
4. ****Limited English + pregnancy—thankfully, a local who speaks Chinese helped me out!****  
   During this trip, being three months pregnant and worried about my limited English, I was fortunate to meet Wayan Suantara, a local driver who speaks Chinese. His thoughtful assistance made the entire journey stress-free, especially with route planning and attraction recommendations.
5. ****Souvenir recommendation: BALI BANANA banana cake****  
   Don’t forget to grab a box of BALI BANANA banana cake before heading home—it’s a signature Bali souvenir! Soft, sweet, and delicious, it’s available at the airport. Try it upon arrival, and if you like it, stock up for your return. They even offer free packing services, making shopping hassle-free.
