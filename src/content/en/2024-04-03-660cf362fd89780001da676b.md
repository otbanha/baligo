A major international event, the World Water Forum, will be held in Bali in May 2024. The conference is expected to welcome 44 heads of state and their delegations, with over 50,000 participants discussing various water-related issues, including water security, disaster reduction, governance, financing, and innovation.

This grand event is bound to impact the local area, affecting airport and road traffic, hotel room demand, and more. However, the organizers are committed to ensuring smooth proceedings while incorporating local cultural wisdom, such as leading delegations to visit the water management systems and rice fields in Jatiluwih.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8553c25fe7dc3cc7fa673d7ba8aa922a.png)

Water resources are crucial for a tourist destination like Bali, with issues ranging from local water management to marine plastic pollution. It is hoped that this conference will bring new strategies for global water resource management.

The conference will take place from **May 18th to May 24th**. If you wish to avoid traffic controls and the influx of attendees, it’s advisable to steer clear of the conference dates and the days immediately before and after.
