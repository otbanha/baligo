## Is Bali Safe? Exposing the Latest 6 Tourist Scams!

Many people DM me asking, 'Will I get scammed on my first trip to Bali?' Honestly, Bali is generally very safe, and the locals are warm and friendly. However, tourist areas always have their 'tricks'. To ensure your trip is filled with only beautiful sights and no traps, be extra vigilant about these common scams in 2026.

## ⚠️ Those 'Tourist Traps' You Should Walk Away From

### **The Sleight of Hand at Money Exchange Shops**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9f72c79c18a0688540bdd57dd45f5fad.png)

Small shops with outrageously high exchange rates often use 'quick counting' tricks to skim bills from your stack. Remember, abnormally high rates almost always mean a scam. Stick to reputable shops with proper storefronts, and always count your money slowly at the counter before leaving. Don't rush to accept the money just because you feel awkward.

### **The Taxi Meter Mystery**

Some drivers may claim their meter is broken or ask you to cancel your app booking and pay in cash instead. In such cases, there's no need to argue—just find another taxi. Stick to using **Grab**, **Gojek**, or the official **Blue Bird** to ensure transparent pricing.

### **The 'New Scratch' Controversy When Returning Vehicles**

Renting a scooter is convenient, but it can be troublesome if you're accused of pre-existing damage when returning it. Always walk around the vehicle and record a full video, including all details and mileage, before taking it. Never leave your original passport with the rental shop.

### **The New Risks of Mobile Hotspots**

In recent years, strangers have been asking to borrow mobile hotspots under the pretext of having no internet access. This not only poses a risk of data leakage but also leaves a record of your connection if the other party engages in illegal activities. Maintaining politeness while refusing to share your hotspot or connect to unknown Wi-Fi is a basic step in protecting your personal privacy.
