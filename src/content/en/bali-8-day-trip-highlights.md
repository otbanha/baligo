This summer, I finally made it to Bali, a place I’ve always dreamed of visiting. This trip had many moments that matched my expectations, as well as some unexpected surprises and reflections that I’d like to share:

## **Weather**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3121af7a76e9ab9aeae87f42ed213a36.jpg)

The weather in Bali wasn’t as hot as I imagined, especially since heavy rain and bright sunshine often occur on the same day. The rain can be torrential, happening once or twice every couple of days, but the wind isn’t too strong. I recommend bringing a light jacket, as it can get chilly when it rains. When engaging in outdoor activities, always carry rain gear to avoid sudden downpours disrupting your plans. Additionally, sunscreen is essential, as the UV rays are still very strong even when the weather is unpredictable.

## **Aesthetics and Decor**

The shops, aesthetics, and decor in Bali are quite impressive, especially in some hotels and yoga studios that excel in using bamboo and woven bamboo to decorate roofs and chandeliers. While Vietnamese bamboo weaving is more intricate, Indonesian designs are more creative and grand. I particularly loved the bamboo chandeliers here, which provided a lot of inspiration for future home decor. These unique design styles not only reflect the local cultural characteristics but also convey a sense of beauty that combines nature with modernity. During your travels, don’t forget to take plenty of photos to capture these beautiful designs as inspiration for future home decoration.

## **Transportation**

Bali's traffic is indeed chaotic, with hardly any traffic lights to be seen. Motor vehicles and motorcycles share the same lanes, and dedicated sidewalks are almost nonexistent. Walking on the streets can be nerve-wracking, as you constantly worry about being hit if you're not careful. It's advisable to wear sturdy shoes, as there are also many puddles to navigate. The traffic order here is quite different from other cities, and it takes some time to adjust when you first arrive. When choosing transportation, consider using Grab or Gojek ride-hailing services—they're convenient, safe, and reduce the risks of walking.

## **Plants**

Bali's vegetation is even more lush than imagined, making it a paradise for plant lovers. Everywhere you look, there are vast stretches of greenery and unique plants, many of which you might be seeing for the first time in your life. The island's botanical gardens and nature reserves are must-visit attractions, offering not only a chance to admire rare plants but also to learn about tropical flora. Don’t forget to bring your camera along to capture these verdant landscapes.

## **Insects**

Bali is also home to many insects, moths, and ants, and the locals have a harmonious approach to coexisting with them. Many shops place offerings outside for the little critters. It’s not unusual to encounter insects during a spa session, and locals often handle them by hand 🪰 rather than killing them. This attitude toward nature is fascinating and reflects the locals' respect for life. During your travels, it’s wise to carry insect repellent, especially for outdoor activities, to avoid bites.

## **Accessory Shops**

Bali’s accessory shops are incredibly charming, such as Blink, where almost every store invites you to step inside. There are also many local designer brands that incorporate natural elements into their designs, offering great value for money. These accessories are not only beautifully crafted but also infused with local cultural essence, making them perfect souvenirs or gifts. When shopping, take the time to chat with the shop owners and learn the stories behind each piece—it adds depth to your appreciation of these unique items.

## **Transportation**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dafbbe49d363e8f35135436c504badf2.png)

Using Grab or Gojek for taxis is very convenient, and most drivers are polite. A 20-minute ride typically costs around USD$3, and for longer distances, hiring a private car is a good option. Private car services are flexible, with drivers often tailoring routes to your needs, allowing you to maximize your time and visit more attractions. Before setting off, discuss your itinerary with the driver to ensure smooth travel to each destination.

## **Yoga Apparel**

I initially thought yoga clothes here would be cheap, but they turned out to be quite expensive. Famous brands like Indigo Luna start at around $65-$100 USD, while the yoga apparel at Alchemy downstairs costs over $160 USD. At these prices, Lululemon seems like a better deal. Despite the high cost, the quality and design of these brands are excellent, so if you find a style you love, it might still be worth the investment. When shopping, it’s a good idea to compare prices across different stores to find the best fit for your style and budget.

## **Yoga Classes**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6968936fa4c0c6ca57750fc1614ccd39.jpg)

Yoga Barn offers a wide variety of classes, but the facilities are a bit outdated, which might be disappointing if expectations are high. There are many flashy classes, but fewer traditional yoga sessions. The free yoga classes at hotels, however, are quite good. If you’re interested in yoga, it’s worth trying out different studios to find the one that suits you best. The range of yoga classes here caters to everyone from beginners to advanced practitioners, meeting various skill levels and needs.

## **Food**

Bali’s cuisine mainly features pork ribs, fried noodles, and fried rice. Those who love vegetable juices will adore the fresh juices here—super fresh and perfect for two glasses a day. The ingredients are fresh, and the dishes have a unique flavor that’s worth trying. While enjoying the food, take the time to learn about the local culinary culture and savor the diverse flavors. Don’t miss out on local specialty restaurants, where you can enjoy authentic dishes and immerse yourself in the cultural atmosphere.

## **People and Services**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3215b9db05b9e4049dd8c6ef5859a350.jpg)

Most people are very polite and friendly, and it’s safe to walk around at night. The spa services here are excellent, affordable, and offer great value for money. It’s recommended to try different spas during your trip to relax and enjoy the leisurely pace of life here. The warmth and hospitality of the locals make the journey even more memorable, whether you’re at a restaurant, shop, or tourist spot. This welcoming atmosphere adds a lot of joy to the travel experience.

## Conclusion

This trip to Bali was filled with many surprises and moving moments. I hope my sharing can help everyone better prepare and enjoy the wonderful times in Bali. Whether it's exploring natural beauty, experiencing local culture, or relaxing your mind and body, Bali can meet all your needs. I hope you can also spend a beautiful time in this dreamlike place, just like I did.
