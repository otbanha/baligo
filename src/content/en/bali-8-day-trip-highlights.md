Finally made it to Bali this summer, a place I've always dreamed of visiting. This trip had many aspects that matched my expectations, along with some unexpected discoveries and reflections to share with everyone:

## **Weather**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3121af7a76e9ab9aeae87f42ed213a36.jpg)

Bali's weather isn't as hot as I imagined, with heavy rain and bright sunshine often occurring on the same day. The rain can be torrential, happening once every day or two, but the wind isn't too strong. It's advisable to bring a light jacket, as it can get chilly when it rains. When engaging in outdoor activities, remember to carry rain gear to avoid sudden downpours disrupting your plans. Additionally, sunscreen is essential, as UV rays remain intense even when the weather is unpredictable.

## **Aesthetics and Decor**

Bali's shops, aesthetics, and decor are quite impressive, especially in some hotels and yoga studios that excel at using bamboo and bamboo weaving to adorn roofs and chandeliers. Vietnamese bamboo weaving is more intricate, but Indonesian designs are more creative and grand. I particularly love the bamboo chandeliers here, which have provided plenty of inspiration for future home decor. These unique design styles not only reflect local cultural characteristics but also convey a sense of beauty that blends nature with modernity. During your travels, don't forget to take photos and document these beautiful designs as inspiration for future home decorations.

## **Transportation**

Bali's traffic is indeed quite chaotic, with hardly any traffic lights to be seen. Motor vehicles and motorcycles share the same lanes, and dedicated sidewalks are almost nonexistent, making walking on the streets a nerve-wracking experience. You constantly worry about being hit if you're not careful. It's advisable to wear sturdy shoes, as there are also many puddles around. The traffic order here is vastly different from other cities, and it takes some time to adjust when you first arrive. When choosing transportation options, it's recommended to make good use of ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek, which are both convenient and safe, reducing the risks of walking.

## **Plants**

Bali's vegetation is even more lush than imagined, making it a paradise for plant lovers. You'll find vast stretches of greenery and various exotic plants everywhere, many of which you might be seeing for the first time in your life. The botanical gardens and nature reserves here are must-visit attractions, where you can not only admire rare plants but also learn a lot about tropical flora. Don't forget to bring your camera along to capture these verdant landscapes.

## **Insects**

Bali is also home to many insects, moths, and ants. The locals have a harmonious approach to dealing with these creatures. Many shops place offerings outside for the insects to eat. It's not unusual to encounter insects in SPA centers, where locals typically catch them by hand 🪰 rather than killing them. This attitude toward natural creatures is quite fascinating and reflects the locals' respect for life. During your travels, it's advisable to carry insect repellent, especially when engaging in outdoor activities, to avoid being bitten.

## **Accessory Shops**

Bali's accessory shops are incredibly appealing, such as Blink, where almost every store makes you want to step inside. There are also many local designer brands that incorporate natural elements into their designs, offering great value for money. These accessories are not only beautifully designed but also imbued with local cultural essence, making them excellent choices for souvenirs or gifts. When purchasing accessories, engaging in conversations with the shop owners can help you understand the stories behind each piece, allowing you to appreciate their uniqueness even more.

## **Transportation**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dafbbe49d363e8f35135436c504badf2.png)

Using Grab or Gojek for taxis is very convenient, and most drivers are quite polite. A 20-minute ride costs around USD$3, and for longer distances, you can hire a private car. Private car services are very flexible, with drivers usually tailoring the route according to your needs, allowing you to make better use of your time and visit more attractions. Before setting off, you can discuss your itinerary with the driver to ensure you reach each destination smoothly.

## **Yoga Wear**

I initially thought yoga wear here would be quite affordable, but it turns out to be quite expensive. Well-known brands like Indigo Luna start at around USD$65-100, while the yoga wear at Alchemy downstairs can cost over USD$160. At these prices, it might be more cost-effective to buy Lululemon instead. Although the prices are high, the quality and design of these brands' yoga wear are excellent. If you find a style you love, it might still be worth the investment. When shopping, it's a good idea to compare several stores to find the best style and price for you.

## **Yoga Classes**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6968936fa4c0c6ca57750fc1614ccd39.jpg)

Yoga Barn offers a wide variety of classes, but the facilities are a bit outdated, perhaps due to overly high expectations. There are many flashy classes, but fewer traditional yoga sessions. The free yoga classes at hotels are actually quite good. If you're interested in yoga, try out different studios to find the best classes and instructors for you. The range of yoga classes here is extensive, catering to everyone from beginners to advanced practitioners, ensuring there's something for every level.

## **Food**

Bali's cuisine mainly features pork ribs, fried noodles, and fried rice. Those who love vegetable juices will adore the fresh juices here—super fresh, perfect for two glasses a day. The ingredients are fresh, and the dishes have a unique flavor, making them worth trying. While enjoying the food, take the time to learn about the local culinary culture and experience different taste sensations. Be sure to visit local specialty restaurants, where you can not only enjoy authentic dishes but also immerse yourself in the rich cultural atmosphere.

## **People and Services**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3215b9db05b9e4049dd8c6ef5859a350.jpg)

Most people are very polite and friendly, and it's safe to walk the streets at night. The spa services here are excellent, affordable, and offer great value for money. It's recommended to try different spas during your trip to relax and enjoy the leisurely pace of life here. The warmth and hospitality of the locals make you feel very welcome, whether you're at a restaurant, shop, or tourist attraction. This cultural atmosphere adds many wonderful memories to your journey.

This Bali trip was filled with many surprises and moving moments. I hope my sharing can help everyone better prepare and enjoy the wonderful times in Bali. Whether it's exploring natural beauty, experiencing local culture, or relaxing your body and mind, Bali can meet all your needs. I hope you can also spend a beautiful time in this dreamy place just like I did.
