Finally made it to Bali this summer, a place I've always dreamed of visiting. This trip had many moments that matched my expectations, along with some unexpected discoveries and reflections to share with everyone:

## **Weather**

![Weather](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3121af7a76e9ab9aeae87f42ed213a36.jpg)

Bali's weather wasn't as hot as I imagined, with heavy rain and bright sunshine often occurring on the same day. The rain can be torrential, happening every day or two, but the wind isn't too strong. It's advisable to bring a light jacket, as it can get chilly when it rains. When engaging in outdoor activities, remember to carry rain gear to avoid sudden downpours disrupting your plans. Additionally, sunscreen is essential, as the UV rays are still very strong even when the weather is unpredictable.

## **Aesthetics and Decor**

The shops, aesthetics, and decor in Bali are quite impressive, especially in some hotels and yoga studios that excel in using bamboo and woven bamboo to decorate roofs and chandeliers. Vietnamese bamboo weaving is more intricate, but Indonesian designs are more creative and grand. I particularly loved the bamboo chandeliers here, which provided a lot of inspiration for future home decor. These unique design styles not only reflect local cultural characteristics but also convey a sense of beauty that combines nature and modernity. During your travels, don't hesitate to take plenty of photos to capture these beautiful designs as inspiration for future home decorations.

## **Transportation**

Bali's traffic is indeed quite chaotic, with almost no traffic lights to be seen. Motor vehicles and motorcycles share the same lanes, and there are hardly any dedicated sidewalks. Walking on the streets can be nerve-wracking, as you constantly worry about being hit if you're not careful. It's advisable to wear comfortable shoes, as there are also many puddles around. The traffic order here is quite different from other cities, so it takes some time to adjust when you first arrive. When choosing transportation options, it's recommended to use ride-hailing services like Grab or Gojek, which are both convenient and safe, reducing the risk of walking.

## **Plants**

Bali's vegetation is even more lush than imagined, making it a paradise for plant lovers. You'll find vast stretches of greenery and various exotic plants everywhere, many of which you might be seeing for the first time in your life. The botanical gardens and nature reserves here are must-visit attractions, where you can not only admire rare plants but also learn a lot about tropical flora. Don't forget to bring your camera along to capture these verdant landscapes during your journey.

## **Insects**

Bali is also home to many insects, moths, and ants, and the locals have a harmonious attitude toward them. Many shops place offerings outside for the little critters. It's not uncommon to encounter insects during a spa session, and locals usually don't kill them but instead catch them by hand 🪰. This approach to nature is quite fascinating and reflects the locals' respect for life. During your trip, it's advisable to carry insect repellent, especially when engaging in outdoor activities, to avoid being bitten.

## **Accessory Shops**

Bali's accessory shops are incredibly charming, such as Blink, where almost every store makes you want to step inside. There are also many local designer brands that incorporate natural elements into their designs, offering great value for money. These accessories are not only beautifully designed but also infused with local cultural vibes, making them perfect souvenirs or gifts. When shopping for accessories, take the time to chat with the shop owners to learn the stories behind each piece, which will help you appreciate their uniqueness even more.

## **Transportation**

![Transportation](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dafbbe49d363e8f35135436c504badf2.png)

Using Grab or Gojek for taxis is very convenient, and most drivers are polite. A 20-minute ride costs around USD$3, and for longer distances, you can hire a private car. Private car services are highly flexible, and drivers usually tailor the route to your needs, allowing you to make the most of your time and visit more attractions. Before setting off, discuss your itinerary with the driver to ensure you can smoothly reach each destination.

## **Yoga Apparel**

I initially thought yoga clothes here would be cheap, but they turned out to be quite expensive. Popular brands like Indigo Luna start at around USD$65-$100, while the yoga apparel at Alchemy downstairs costs over USD$160. At these prices, it's more economical to buy Lululemon. Although the prices are high, the quality and design of these brands' yoga wear are excellent. If you find a style you love, it's worth the investment. When shopping, compare a few stores to find the best fit and price for you.

## **Yoga Classes**

![Yoga Classes](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6968936fa4c0c6ca57750fc1614ccd39.jpg)

Yoga Barn offers many classes, but the venue is a bit outdated, perhaps due to overly high expectations. There are many flashy courses, but fewer traditional yoga classes. The free yoga classes at hotels are actually quite good. If you're interested in yoga, try out different studios to find the best classes and instructors for you. The variety of yoga courses here caters to everyone from beginners to advanced practitioners, meeting different skill levels.

## **Food**

Bali's cuisine mainly features pork ribs, fried noodles, and fried rice. Those who love vegetable juices will adore the fresh juices here—super fresh, perfect for two glasses a day. The ingredients are fresh, and the dishes have unique flavors worth trying. While enjoying the food, take the time to learn about the local culinary culture and experience different tastes. Try local specialty restaurants to not only enjoy authentic dishes but also immerse yourself in the cultural atmosphere.

## **People and Services**

![People and Services](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3215b9db05b9e4049dd8c6ef5859a350.jpg)

Most people are very polite and friendly, and it's safe to walk around at night. The spa services here are excellent, affordable, and offer great value. It's recommended to try different spas during your trip to relax and enjoy the leisurely pace. The warm hospitality of the locals makes you feel very welcome, whether in restaurants, shops, or attractions. This human touch adds many beautiful memories to your journey.

## Conclusion

This trip to Bali was filled with many surprises and moving moments. I hope my sharing can help everyone better prepare and enjoy the wonderful times in Bali. Whether it's exploring natural beauty, experiencing local culture, or relaxing your body and mind, Bali can meet all your needs. I hope you can also spend a beautiful time in this dreamlike place, just like I did.
