## Complete Guide to Garuda Wisnu Kencana Cultural Park in Bali

Garuda Wisnu Kencana (GWK) Cultural Park, located in Ungasan in southern Bali, is a stunning cultural landmark combining art, mythology, and natural scenery. It features one of the world's tallest statues and is one of the best places to explore Balinese traditional culture and mythological stories.

## **The Mythological Story of Garuda Wisnu**

In Hindu mythology, Garuda is a divine bird symbolizing strength and loyalty, while Wisnu (Vishnu) is one of the three main deities and the protector god. Legend has it that Garuda fought demons to save his enslaved mother, eventually obtaining the secret of the 'Water of Life (Amrita)' and offering it to Vishnu. Deeply moved, Vishnu allowed Garuda to become his mount, and together they protect the world, symbolizing justice and peace.

![The Mythological Story of Garuda Wisnu](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8d4e8695ec97e45be528a94384b90527.jpg)

The statue at GWK Cultural Park depicts Garuda carrying Vishnu, embodying the Balinese people's reverence and dedication to their faith and culture.

***

## **The Sculptor's Remarkable Achievement**

This 122-meter-tall statue was created by renowned Indonesian artist Nyoman Nuarta. He began designing the statue in 1990, overcoming economic crises and numerous obstacles, and finally completed it in 2018. The statue consists of over 1,500 components, weighing more than 4,000 tons, with the giant Garuda statue made of copper. The outer shell used over 25,000 square meters of copper, shaped and welded with stainless steel. The GWK copper statue also serves as a lightning rod.

The entire project took nearly 30 years and cost USD$100 million. It is not only Indonesia's tallest statue and the fourth tallest in the world but also the tallest Hindu statue globally, showcasing the artist's boundless passion and perseverance in cultural preservation.

If you'd rather not sweat while walking and want to save your energy for enjoying cultural performances and taking photos, using the park's transportation options is a smart choice.

## **GWK Cultural Park Tour Route**

Planning to visit GWK Cultural Park but unsure about the tour route? Don’t worry, here’s a complete guide to navigating the park.

**Park Transportation** After arriving at the parking lot, visitors can take a free shuttle bus to two stops: Plaza Bhagawan (ticket office) or Jendela Bali Restaurant.

### **Itinerary**

![園區地圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_31624c4a0f0c3b2778d3c5262ecb6b5f.jpg)

Park Map

**1. Tirta Amerta (Holy Water Fountain Pool)** After purchasing tickets, your journey begins. The first sight that greets you is the Tirta Amerta, a water pool serving as the park's welcoming feature. According to Stephanus, the Operations Director of GWK Cultural Park, this design reflects the story of Garuda and Lord Vishnu.

**2. Plaza Kura-Kura (Turtle Plaza)** The first attraction is Turtle Plaza, a lush little garden featuring a pond, trees, and turtle statues. At the center of the pond stands a statue of Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity, where visitors can toss coins to make wishes.

**3. Plaza Wisnu (Vishnu Plaza)** Climbing the stairs leads you to Vishnu Plaza, where a statue of Lord Vishnu, the protector of the universe, stands. It's an excellent spot for photos.

Within the plaza, there is also a perennial spring from Parayan An Somakajiri. Remarkably, despite being located on high limestone terrain, this spring has never dried up and is said to have existed long before the establishment of GWK Cultural Park.

**4. Plaza Garuda (Garuda Plaza)** After admiring the perennial spring, descending the stairs brings you to Garuda Plaza. Here, a statue of Garuda's head is displayed. Garuda, the mount of Lord Vishnu, is one of the most beloved photo spots for visitors.

**5. Garuda Sineloka (Garuda Mini Theater)** Near Garuda Plaza is a mini theater showing an animated story about a young Garuda's quest for holy water. The film plays hourly from 10:30 AM to 7:30 PM.

**6. GWK Statue** To reach the GWK Statue, you can choose to walk or rent an electric vehicle. Along the way, you'll pass Festival Park, Lotus Pond, and Titi Banda Bridge.

Upon arriving at the GWK Statue, visitors with premium tickets can enter the statue's interior, ascending to the 9th and 23rd floors to enjoy panoramic views of Bali from above.

All visitors can take photos and videos in front of this 122-meter-tall statue, with two angles available for photography: front and left side.

**7. Art Performances** At the end of the tour, visitors can enjoy various art performances at the amphitheater, lotus pond, and Vishnu Plaza.

**8. Shopping & Dining** The exit area features the Kencana Souvenir Shop and ASANA Art Gallery. Visitors can also dine at the Bali Window Restaurant while enjoying the sunset view.

**Tour Duration** Visitors typically need 3 to 4 hours to fully explore all areas of the cultural park. It is recommended to allocate sufficient time to deeply experience every corner of GWK Cultural Park!
