## Bali Hit by Sudden Blackout, Entire Island Plunged into Darkness

[Provided by Han Zongliang](https://www.facebook.com/share/v/1AW8j4mkAm/)

On May 2, 2025, at 4 PM, Bali experienced a sudden blackout that left almost the entire island without power. This major outage significantly impacted local livelihoods, transportation, and the tourism industry. PT PLN (Indonesia's State Electricity Company) quickly initiated repair operations and continued to update the power supply situation.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_da277efc556c6ee025a7bfedbf8bd7e8.jpg)

## Power Company Responds: Submarine Cable Failure is the Main Cause

According to Gregorius Adi Trianto, Vice President of Corporate Communications at PLN, the blackout occurred around 4 PM local time due to a **failure in the submarine cable between Java and Bali**, causing all connected generators to Bali to cease operation.

As of 10 PM that day, approximately 80% of the areas were still experiencing power outages.

## Where Does Bali's Electricity Come From?

Bali itself has limited power generation capacity, with most of its electricity coming from Java Island through a high-voltage direct current (HVDC) submarine cable. This crucial cable serves as a vital lifeline for the island's stable power supply, and any technical failure could lead to large-scale blackouts like the current incident.

Additionally, while Bali has several small fuel and gas power stations locally, they are only suitable for emergency use or as backups and cannot meet the entire island's energy needs.

---

## Restoration Efforts Continue, Full Power Restoration Still Requires Time

PT PLN stated that hundreds of technicians with equipment have been dispatched to the affected areas to carry out phased repairs and load management. Their goal is to stabilize the system as quickly as possible and prevent similar incidents from occurring again.

Officials emphasized that full power restoration will still take time and urged the public to be patient and conserve electricity.

---

## Looking Ahead: Bali's Power System Needs Diversified Development

This blackout highlights the risks of Bali's reliance on a single power source. In the future, the Indonesian government and PLN should consider the following strategies to enhance energy resilience:

- Invest in renewable energy: Diversify energy sources such as solar, wind, and geothermal.
- Strengthen local power generation: Increase microgrids and distributed energy systems.
- Submarine cable backup design: Enhance the backup and maintenance mechanisms of main cables.

## FAQ Section

### 1. Why did Bali experience this major power outage?

The main reason was a malfunction in the submarine cable connecting Java and Bali, which caused the power generators to fail.

### 2. Where does Bali primarily get its electricity from?

Most of Bali's electricity comes from Java, transmitted via submarine cables.

### 3. Does Bali have its own power plants?

Yes, but they are relatively small and mainly serve as backup, unable to supply the entire island for extended periods.

### 4. How can similar situations be avoided in the future?

It requires diversifying power sources, enhancing local power generation facilities, and improving the grid's backup design and response capabilities.
