Finding a hotel in Ubud that offers deep jungle serenity without feeling completely cut off is tough—but Adiwana Alas Harum is hard to beat. This hotel just opened in February 2026, designed by award-winning architect Popo Danes. It sits at the start of the Campuhan Ridge Walk, overlooking Ubud's most famous jungle valley—the sacred Wos River winding through, with the silhouette of Mount Agung in the distance. With 45 suites and 10 private pool villas, each seamlessly blends Balinese traditional craftsmanship into modern design, letting you stay in a space that truly respects local culture.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784125769170-1mc6512000rk9lc30f11e_w_1280_853_r5.webp)

## 📍 Location & Surroundings

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784125820296-1.webp)

The hotel is located in the village of Keliki, north of Ubud. It feels remote, but it's actually just a 5–10 minute drive from Ubud's center. Even better, you can access the Campuhan Ridge Walk directly from the hotel's doorstep—Ubud's most classic morning hiking trail is practically your backyard. This 2-kilometer path passes the sacred Gunung Lebah Temple, flanked by deep green valleys and rice paddies, with every turn looking like a movie still. The hotel itself is in the small village of Bangkiang Sidem at the trailhead, so you don't need to queue or drive—just wake up and go.

[https://youtube.com/shorts/3PmPpoHI_6c](https://youtube.com/shorts/3PmPpoHI_6c)

The surrounding area is quiet but has everything you need. Heading toward Ubud center, Ubud Monkey Forest is a 15-minute drive, Ubud Palace is 10 minutes, and the Sacred Art Market is 15 minutes. For more adventure, Tegenungan Waterfall is nearby. Restaurants and cafes are scattered along the Campuhan Ridge, with Nari Restaurant perched above Gunung Lebah Temple offering stunning views—the hotel staff will gladly arrange it for you.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784125912524-1mc1j12000rk9rok1e992_w_1280_853_r5.webp)

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

Stepping into the lobby, you'll be amazed by the walls full of traditional Balinese carvings and natural materials—wood, stone, bamboo, rattan—every detail shows care. From downstairs, you can see the infinity pool, its clear blue water seeming to flow straight into the jungle.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784125969035-1mc6z12000rk9kvmz2d63_w_1280_853_r5.webp)

The two main restaurants each have their own character. Black Garlic Restaurant features ceilings adorned with ancient Kamasan paintings depicting traditional Balinese life, making you feel like you're dining in a cultural heritage site. Flame Bar & Grill is more relaxed, perfect for cocktail hour. Breakfast is cooked-to-order, not a buffet—the chef prepares dishes based on your preferences, whether pancakes, omelettes, or traditional Indonesian cuisine.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784126009236-1mc3612000rk9knfa3d9a_w_1280_853_r5.webp)

Tejas Spa Alas Harum is the hotel's sanctuary for relaxation, offering traditional massages along with signature Balinese therapies. The gym is open 24 hours, and instructors lead yoga classes (Wednesdays and Sundays). The hotel puts real effort into cultural activities—mandala making, boreh making (traditional herbal body scrub), jamu making (traditional herbal medicine), and Balinese traditional dance classes on Tuesdays and Fridays. If you're bringing kids, there are daily painting classes. More uniquely, the hotel leads guests on the Campuhan Ridge Walk every morning at 7 AM, saving you the trouble of Googling directions.

## 👥 Who It's For

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784126936671-unnamed.webp)

Honeymooners are the perfect guests for Adiwana—private pools, serene vibes, and romantic dining. Those seeking spiritual rejuvenation will love it too, with yoga, meditation, and cultural activities on offer. Digital nomads and long-stay travelers are also well catered for, thanks to fast WiFi and a quiet environment. Families with kids will appreciate the children's painting activities and gentle atmosphere, but be prepared for lots of stairs and uphill walks.

## The Honest Details

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784126968962-unnamed_2_.webp)

Let's also cover the downsides. While it's not far from town, the location is a bit remote—if you prefer walking to restaurants and shops, this isn't it. There are lots of stairs, so hauling large suitcases can be tiring. The restaurant serves delicious food, but prices are on the high side (by Indonesian standards); for budget eats, head into town. Some room types (especially villas) are quite far from the main building, and walking back at night can feel a bit isolated, though the hotel provides flashlights and 24-hour security, so safety isn't an issue.

Overall, Adiwana Alas Harum isn't a "cheap and high-value" hotel, but it truly justifies its price. If you're looking to deeply unwind in Ubud, soak in the jungle tranquility, and enjoy quality service, this hotel will make you want to come back again and again.
