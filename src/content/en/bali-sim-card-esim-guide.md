## Comparison of Indonesia's Top Three Telecoms—From a Traveler's Perspective

### Telkomsel (Widest Coverage, Top Choice for Travelers)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779239732579-af57c619fd217399aaa52c62626b953e.jpg)

Indonesia's largest telecom provider with about 55% market share. In all areas of Bali, including mountainous villages like Amed, Munduk, and Sidemen, Telkomsel's 4G signal is the most stable among the three.

- **4G/5G Coverage:** Full 4G coverage from Kuta to Nusa Dua's main tourist areas, with 5G hotspots in central Ubud
- **Most Popular Plan for Tourists:** Perdana Tourist SIM (prepaid SIM card), also known as **Telkomsel Tourist SIMPATI** (airport price approx. IDR 250,000–350,000, includes 15G–35G data). Can often be purchased at city mobile shops for half or a third of the price.
- **Nusa Penida Signal:** Strongest among the three providers, stable 4G from the pier to major attractions
- **Gili Islands Signal:** Signal available on Gili Trawangan, weaker on Gili Air, occasional dropouts on Gili Meno
- **Drawbacks:** About 30–40% more expensive than Indosat, airport stalls often inflate prices

**simPATI is a brand under Telkomsel, offering the same network—no need to distinguish between them.**

### Indosat Ooredoo / IM3 (Best Value for Money)

Prices are over 30% cheaper than Telkomsel, with fully adequate signal in urban tourist areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Sanur.

- **4G Coverage:** Normal in main tourist areas, but drops to 3G or no signal in remote areas like Amed and Munduk
- **Popular Plan for Tourists:** Freedom Internet plan, IDR 50,000–80,000 including 10–20GB
- **Best For:** Travelers focusing on Kuta and central Ubud, avoiding remote areas

### XL Axiata (Fast in Cities, Weak in Remote Areas)

4G speeds in Seminyak and Kuta sometimes surpass Telkomsel, but coverage boundaries are very distinct.

- **Biggest Issue:** Extremely poor signal on Nusa Penida, with no signal at some attractions
- **Reason Not Recommended for Tourists:** Signal map optimization focuses on cities, becoming unstable just outside core tourist areas
- **Only Suitable Scenario:** Staying exclusively in Kuta and Seminyak, with high signal demands and no island-hopping

**Quick Conclusion on the Three Major Providers:** As long as your itinerary isn't limited to city centers, choosing Telkomsel is always a safe bet.**

## Where to Buy Physical SIM Cards - Complete Guide

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779240020594-e5c025ea6a97e3839ab39d4ff832db68.jpg)

### 1. Buying at Bali Airport (DPS)

**Most convenient, but full of traps.**

**Location:** Official Telkomsel and Indosat booths, along with numerous unofficial vendors, are located next to the baggage carousel in the arrival hall after customs.

***

⚠️ **Scam Warning - This is Important**

Here's what Klook blogs and eSIM brand articles won't tell you:

1. **Fake "Installation Fee" (installasi fee):** Vendors may charge IDR 200,000–800,000 for setup, which is a scam. Official SIM cards are plug-and-play and don't require manual installation fees.
2. **Pushing overpriced plans with "best package for tourist" tactics:** Politely decline and ask for the standard Kartu Perdana (prepaid card) plan.

**Safe Approach:**

- Choose official booths (with Telkomsel/Indosat signage, not roadside tables)
- After inserting the SIM, dial \*888# to check your balance

***

**Installation Steps (DIY):**

1. Ensure your phone is unlocked (phones locked to a specific SIM card cannot be swapped)
2. Locate the SIM card slot and use the ejector tool to remove the old card
3. Insert the new SIM card and restart your phone
4. If the network settings don't configure automatically, manually enter the APN settings (see the tutorial in the next section)

**By the way:** For transportation arrangements from the airport to your accommodation, see [Arriving in Bali: Transportation Options from the Airport and Practical Tips for Your First Day](/blog/bali-airport-transfer-guide/), including comparisons of private cars, taxis, and ride-hailing services.

### 2. Purchase at Convenience Stores in the City

**Indomaret and Alfamart** are found on almost every street, and most outlets sell prepaid SIM cards.

- **Purchase Process:** Tell the clerk "mau beli kartu perdana Telkomsel," show your passport, and the clerk will register it for you
- **Price:** Usually 20–30% cheaper than at the airport, but with fewer data plan options
- **Notes:** Original passport required (copies may sometimes work), minors cannot purchase
- **Recommended Scenario:** When you're not in a rush, want to compare options, or if airport stalls have long queues

Note that many clerks in tourist areas are now accustomed to helping foreign travelers with SIM cards. However, there's a common pitfall: **not every convenience store can complete the 'passport实名 registration' for you!** After purchasing:

- The SIM card may not work
- You may need to register it yourself
- Or visit an official store to resolve the issue

Especially for foreign passport registration, some clerks may not be familiar with the process.

### 3. Purchase at Official Grapari Stores

Grapari is Telkomsel's official service center, suitable for long-term residents or those needing to resolve account issues.

- **Location:** Stores in Kuta, Denpasar, and Ubud. Search "Grapari Telkomsel" on Google Maps
- **Documents Required:** Original passport, personal contact information
- **Pros:** Monthly plans available (more cost-effective for stays over 30 days), customer service issues resolved on-site
- **Cons:** Need to queue and wait, possibly 30–60 minutes

## Complete Installation Guide

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779240073782-069909000_1519793545-where-to-buy-sim-card-in-ho-chi-minh.webp)

### iPhone eSIM Installation

**Method 1: QR Code Scan (Most Common)**

1. After purchasing eSIM, obtain QR Code image from Klook / Airalo App
2. On your phone, go to "Settings → Cellular → Add eSIM"
3. Select "Use QR Code" and scan the image
4. Set up "Primary SIM" (Taiwan number) and "Cellular SIM" (Bali eSIM), assign voice/data as needed

**Method 2: Manual Activation Code Entry**

1. If unable to scan, choose "Enter Details Manually"
2. Enter SM-DP+ address and activation code (found in purchase confirmation email)

**Common Issues:**

- _"Unable to add mobile plan"_: Ensure your phone is unlocked and running iOS 12.1 or later

- _"eSIM installed but no signal"_: Verify if the activation code has already been used (each QR Code can only be used once)

- _"Screenshot QR Code cannot be scanned"_: Display it directly through the Klook App instead of scanning a screenshot

### Android eSIM Installation

The process varies slightly by brand. Here's an example for Samsung:

1. Settings → Connections → SIM card manager → Add mobile plan

2. Scan the QR Code or enter manually

3. After completion, you can switch between the main SIM and eSIM

**Note:** Some Android phones (mainly US carrier versions) have locked eSIM functionality. Verify before purchasing.

**iOS Setup Path:** Settings → Cellular → Cellular Data Network → Enter in APN field
**Android Setup Path:** Settings → Connections → Mobile Networks → Access Point Names (APN) → Add New

**Common Mistakes:**

- _Still E/2G after setup_: Restart your phone once, wait 2–3 minutes for the network to reconnect
- _APN field appears gray_: Your phone may be locked by the carrier, unlock it before switching SIM cards

### Dual SIM Setup Recommendation for Keeping Taiwan Number

Use Bali SIM for internet, keep Taiwan number for calls:

1. **Set Taiwan SIM as "Voice Call" card**, turn off mobile data for Taiwan SIM (to avoid roaming fees)
2. **Set Bali SIM as "Mobile Data" card**
3. If Taiwan SIM has Wi-Fi calling feature, enable it to receive calls on Taiwan number via Wi-Fi (completely free)

## Common Disasters & Instant Solutions

**Signal Suddenly Disappears**

- First check if APN settings have been cleared (APN sometimes resets after toggling airplane mode on and off)
- Dial \*888# to check remaining data balance. If zero, top up is needed
- For Telkomsel top-up, purchase voucher at Indomaret and tell the clerk "isi ulang Telkomsel XXX ribu"

**eSIM Activation Failure**

- Confirm network environment during installation (Wi-Fi required to download eSIM profile)
- Each QR Code can only be installed once. If phone has been changed, contact eSIM brand customer service for new code
- Klook eSIM users can directly contact Chinese customer service within the App, usually responds within 1 hour

**How to Top Up When Data Runs Out**

- Physical SIM: Purchase voucher at Indomaret/Alfamart, or top up using GoPay/Dana
- eSIM: Return to purchase platform to buy another one (usually doesn't support direct top-up on original SIM, just buy new eSIM)

**What to Do When Changing Phones**

- Physical SIM: Simply remove and insert into new phone (ensure new phone is unlocked)
- eSIM: Need to delete from original phone first, then rescan QR code to install (some brands support this, Klook customer service can assist)

**Should I Turn It Off After Returning to Taiwan**

- Physical SIM: Simply remove it. Indonesian numbers usually expire automatically after 90 days of inactivity
- eSIM: Can delete eSIM profile on phone, or keep as backup (occupies one eSIM slot)
- **Important:** After returning to Taiwan, confirm Taiwan SIM has been switched back to primary data source to avoid continuing to use Bali SIM roaming charges

## FAQ

**Is a Bali eSIM the same as an Indonesia eSIM?**
Yes, since Bali is part of Indonesia, purchasing an Indonesia eSIM will work across the entire island without regional restrictions. Some brands label their plans as "Indonesia," which covers Bali, Java, and all other regions in Indonesia.

**Can I share hotspot with companions using one eSIM?**
Most eSIMs (including Klook eSIM and Airalo) support hotspot sharing, but Holafly's unlimited plans **explicitly prohibit** hotspot usage. Please check the plan terms before purchasing to avoid disputes.

**Can I buy a SIM card at the airport if I don't speak English?**
Official Telkomsel and Indosat counters at the airport provide English service, and you can also use translation apps on your phone to communicate. Simply state the number of days and approximate data usage needed, as vendors are experienced in handling tourists. However, remain vigilant during transactions—refer to the scam warning section in this article for details.

**Is it safe to buy eSIMs online with a credit card?**
Klook, Airalo, and Holafly are globally recognized platforms that use HTTPS encryption for transactions, making them as secure as regular online shopping. It's recommended to use a credit card that offers no foreign transaction fees (such as Richart) for purchases.

**Which is more cost-effective: Wi-Fi router or SIM card?**
For trips shorter than 5 days with 3 or more people, dividing the cost of a Wi-Fi router per person might be cheaper. However, for trips longer than 5 days or with only 1–2 people, SIM cards or eSIMs are almost always more economical, and you won't need to worry about the router's battery life. For a full comparison between Wi-Fi routers and SIM cards in Bali, please refer to [this article](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/).

**Is eSIM or physical SIM more cost-effective for group travel?**
For 2 people or fewer, purchasing individual eSIMs is the most convenient option. For groups of 3 or more, sharing a Telkomsel physical SIM with hotspot enabled is the cheapest solution (around USD$0.65 per person for a IDR 120,000 SIM split three ways). However, hotspot sharing drains battery faster, so bring a power bank.

**Will there be signal on Gili Trawangan?**
Telkomsel provides 4G signal near the Gili Trawangan pier, but the signal is intermittent inland. Signal on Gili Air and Gili Meno is extremely weak and unreliable. Most accommodations offer Wi-Fi, so it's recommended to use Wi-Fi for important communications.

**Can I install an eSIM before passing through customs at the airport?**
Yes. eSIM installation requires Wi-Fi, and airport waiting areas offer free Wi-Fi. It's recommended to **install the eSIM profile before departing Taiwan**, but set the activation time to begin upon arrival in Indonesia. This way, you can switch to airplane mode and disable your Taiwan SIM upon landing, and the eSIM will be ready to use immediately.

**Can I buy a physical SIM card with a passport copy?**
Officially, the original passport is required, but some vendors accept passport photos displayed on your phone. It's advisable to carry your original passport or keep a high-resolution scanned copy in your phone's photo album as a backup.

**Are Telkomsel and simPATI the same?**
Yes, simPATI is a prepaid card brand under Telkomsel, using the exact same Telkomsel network towers with no difference in signal or coverage. Tourist SIMs sold at airports are often branded as simPATI or Kartu As, so there's no need to distinguish between them.

**Can I still use my Indonesian SIM card after returning to Taiwan?**
Technically, yes, but the rates will be charged according to Indonesian telecom roaming, which is very expensive. Immediately switch back to your Taiwan SIM upon returning, or disable mobile data on your Indonesian SIM to avoid unexpected charges.

**Can I still make calls after running out of data?**
For Telkomsel and Indosat prepaid SIM cards, voice calls and SMS balances are calculated separately from data usage. Even if your data runs out, you can still make voice calls (applicable to local Indonesian numbers) as long as your SIM has voice credit.
