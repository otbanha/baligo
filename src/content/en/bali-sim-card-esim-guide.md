## Comparison of Indonesia's Top 3 Telecom Providers—From a Traveler's Perspective

### Telkomsel (Widest Coverage, Top Choice for Travelers)

Indonesia's largest telecom provider, with a market share of around 55%. In various areas of Bali, including mountainous villages like Amed, Munduk, and Sidemen, Telkomsel's 4G signal is the most stable among the three providers.

- **4G/5G Coverage:** Full 4G coverage from Kuta to Nusa Dua's main tourist areas, with 5G hotspots in central Ubud
- **Most Popular Plan for Tourists:** Perdana Tourist SIM (airport price around IDR 80,000–120,000, includes 14–30 days of data)
- **Nusa Penida Signal:** Strongest among the three providers, stable 4G from the pier to major attractions
- **Gili Islands Signal:** Signal available on Gili Trawangan, weaker on Gili Air, occasional dropouts on Gili Meno
- **Drawbacks:** About 30–40% more expensive than Indosat, airport kiosks often inflate prices

**simPATI is a brand under Telkomsel, offering the same network, so no need to distinguish between them.**

### Indosat Ooredoo / IM3 (Best Value for Money)

Prices are over 30% cheaper than Telkomsel, and the signal is sufficient in urban tourist areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Sanur.

- **4G Coverage:** Normal in main tourist areas, but drops to 3G or no signal in remote areas like Amed and Munduk
- **Popular Plan for Tourists:** Freedom Internet plan, IDR 50,000–80,000 includes 10–20GB
- **Best For:** Travelers focusing on Kuta and central Ubud, not venturing to remote areas

### XL Axiata (Fast in Cities, Weak in Remote Areas)

Sometimes offers faster 4G speeds than Telkomsel in Seminyak and Kuta, but coverage boundaries are very distinct.

- **Biggest Issue:** Extremely poor signal in Nusa Penida, with no signal at some attractions
- **Not Recommended for Tourists:** Signal map optimization focuses on cities, becomes unstable outside core tourist areas
- **Only Suitable For:** Staying exclusively in Seminyak, requiring high signal quality, and not visiting islands

**Quick Conclusion for the Three Providers: As long as your itinerary isn't limited to city centers, choosing Telkomsel is always a safe bet.**

## Where to Buy Physical SIM Cards—Complete Guide

### 1. Bali Airport (DPS) Purchase

**Most convenient, but full of pitfalls.**

**Location:** After clearing immigration in the arrival hall, official Telkomsel and Indosat booths are located near the baggage carousel, along with numerous unofficial vendors.

**Price Range:** Official booths IDR 80,000–150,000 (approx. USD$5–10), including 14–30 days of data; unofficial vendors may charge up to IDR 300,000–500,000.

**Currency Conversion Reference:** IDR 10,000 ≈ USD$0.70 (2026 exchange rate reference, please confirm before departure)

---

⚠️ **Scam Alert – This is Important**

Here's what Klook blogs and eSIM brand articles won't tell you:

1. **Fake 'Installation Fee' (installasi fee)**: Vendors may claim an IDR 200,000–800,000 setup fee, but this is a scam. Official SIM cards are automatically configured upon insertion and don't require manual installation fees.
2. **Secretly Activating Monthly Plans**: When handing your phone to a vendor for setup, they might secretly activate a 'Telkomsel Halo' monthly plan, leaving you with unexpected charges after returning to Taiwan.
3. **Pushing Overpriced Plans with 'Best Package for Tourist' Tactics**: Politely decline and ask for the standard Kartu Perdana (new SIM card) plan.

**Safe Practices:**
- Choose official stalls (with Telkomsel/Indosat signage, not roadside tables)
- Insert the SIM card yourself; don't let vendors handle your phone for more than 30 seconds
- After inserting the card, dial *888# to check your balance

2. Locate the SIM card slot and use an ejector tool to remove the old card

## Complete Installation Guide

### iPhone eSIM Installation

**Method 1: QR Code Scan (Most Common)**
1. After purchasing eSIM, obtain QR Code image from Klook / Airalo App
2. On your phone, go to "Settings → Cellular → Add eSIM"
3. Select "Use QR Code" and scan the image
4. Set up "Primary SIM" (Taiwan number) and "Cellular SIM" (Bali eSIM), assigning voice/data as needed

**Method 2: Manual Activation Code Entry**
1. If scanning fails, select 'Manually Enter Details'
2. Enter SM-DP+ address and activation code (found in purchase confirmation email)

**Common Errors:**
- *'Cannot Add Mobile Plan'*: Ensure phone is unlocked and iOS version is 12.1 or higher
- *eSIM Installed But No Signal*: Confirm activation code hasn't been used before (each QR Code can only be used once)
- *Screenshot QR Code Won't Scan*: Display directly via Klook App, don't scan from a screenshot

### Android eSIM Installation

Paths vary slightly by brand, using Samsung as an example:
1. Settings → Connections → SIM Card Manager → Add Mobile Plan
2. Scan QR Code or enter manually
3. After completion, you can switch between main SIM and eSIM

**Note:** Some Android phones (mainly US carrier versions) have eSIM functionality locked, confirm before purchasing.

### Physical SIM Card Replacement + APN Setup

**If automatic setup fails after replacement, manually configure APN as follows:**

| Carrier | APN Name | Username | Password |

**iOS Setup Path:** Settings → Mobile Service → Mobile Data Network → Enter in APN field
**Android Setup Path:** Settings → Connections → Mobile Networks → Access Point Names (APN) → Add

**Common Errors:**
- *Still E/2G After Setup*: Reboot once, wait 2–3 minutes for network to reconnect
- *APN Field Grayed Out*: Phone may be carrier-locked, unlock before changing SIM

### Dual SIM Setup Recommendations for Keeping Your Taiwan Number

Using a Bali SIM for internet while retaining your Taiwan number for calls:
1. **Set your Taiwan SIM as the "Voice Call" card**, and disable mobile data on it (to avoid roaming charges).
2. **Set your Bali SIM as the "Mobile Data" card**.
3. If your Taiwan SIM supports Wi-Fi calling, enable it to receive calls to your Taiwan number via Wi-Fi (completely free).

## Common Issues & Quick Fixes

**Sudden Signal Loss**
- First check if APN settings were cleared (switching airplane mode on/off sometimes resets APN)
- Dial *888# to check data balance. If zero, top up
- Telkomsel top-ups can be purchased at Indomaret. Tell the clerk "isi ulang Telkomsel XXX ribu"

**eSIM Activation Failed**
- Ensure a stable Wi-Fi connection during installation (required to download eSIM profile)
- Each QR Code can only be installed once. If you’ve changed your phone, contact the eSIM brand’s customer service for a new code
- Klook eSIM users can directly contact Chinese-speaking customer service within the app, usually receiving a response within 1 hour

**How to Top Up When Data Runs Out**
- Physical SIM: Purchase a voucher at Indomaret / Alfamart or top up using GoPay / Dana
- eSIM: Return to the purchase platform and buy a new one (most eSIMs don’t support direct top-ups; you’ll need to buy a new eSIM)

**What to Do When Changing Phones**
- Physical SIM: Simply remove and insert it into the new phone (ensure the new phone is unlocked)
- eSIM: You’ll need to delete it from the original phone and reinstall it by scanning the QR code again (some brands support this; Klook customer service can assist)

**Should I Turn It Off After Returning to Taiwan?**
- Physical SIM: Remove it; the Indonesian number usually expires after 90 days of inactivity
- eSIM: You can delete the eSIM profile from your phone or keep it as a backup (occupying one eSIM slot)
- **Important:** After returning to Taiwan, ensure your Taiwan SIM is set as the primary data source to avoid roaming charges from the Bali SIM

## FAQ

**Is Bali eSIM the Same as Indonesia eSIM?**  
Yes, Bali is within Indonesia, so purchasing an Indonesia eSIM allows full island usage without regional restrictions. Some brands label their plans as "Indonesia," covering Bali, Java, and all other regions in Indonesia.

**Can I Share Hotspot with Others Using One eSIM?**  
Most eSIMs (including Klook eSIM and Airalo) support hotspot sharing, but Holafly’s unlimited plans **explicitly prohibit** hotspot usage. Check the plan’s terms before purchasing to avoid disputes.

**Can I Buy a SIM at the Airport Counter Without Speaking English?**  
Official Telkomsel and Indosat counters at the airport offer English services, and translation apps can help with communication. Simply state the duration and approximate data usage needed, as vendors are experienced with tourists. However, stay alert during transactions; see the scam warning section in this article for details.

**Is It Safe to Buy eSIM Online with a Credit Card?**  
Klook, Airalo, and Holafly are globally recognized platforms using HTTPS encryption for transactions, making them as secure as general online shopping. It’s recommended to use a Taiwan credit card with no foreign transaction fees (e.g., Richart) for purchases.

**Wi-Fi Router vs SIM Card: Which is More Cost-Effective?**  
For trips under 5 days with 3 or more people, splitting the cost of a Wi-Fi router might be cheaper. However, for trips over 5 days or with only 1–2 people, a SIM card or eSIM is almost always more economical, and you won’t have to worry about the router’s battery life. For a detailed comparison of Wi-Fi routers and SIM cards in Bali, refer to our [Bali Wi-Fi vs SIM Card Guide](/bali-wifi-vs-sim/).

**Is eSIM or Physical SIM Better for Groups?**  
For 2 people or fewer, buying individual eSIMs is the most convenient option. For groups of 3 or more, sharing a Telkomsel physical SIM card with hotspot is the most cost-effective solution (around USD$0.65 per person for a IDR 120,000 card split among three). However, hotspot sharing drains battery faster, so bring a power bank.

**Will I Have Signal on Gili Trawangan?**  
Telkomsel provides 4G signal near the Gili Trawangan pier, but the signal is intermittent inland. On Gili Air and Gili Meno, the signal is extremely weak and unreliable. Most accommodations offer Wi-Fi, so it’s recommended to use Wi-Fi for important communications.

**Can I Install eSIM Before Clearing Customs at the Airport?**  
Yes. Installing an eSIM requires Wi-Fi, and airport waiting areas offer free Wi-Fi. It’s best to **install the eSIM profile before departing from Taiwan**, but set the activation time to begin once you arrive in Indonesia. This way, when you land, switch to airplane mode and turn off your Taiwan SIM, and the eSIM will be ready to use immediately.

**Can I Buy a Physical SIM Card with a Passport Copy?**  
Officially, the original passport is required, but some vendors accept passport photos displayed on your phone. It’s recommended to carry your original passport or keep a high-resolution scanned copy in your phone’s photo album as a backup.

**Are Telkomsel and simPATI the Same?**  
Yes, simPATI is a prepaid card brand under Telkomsel, using the exact same Telkomsel network infrastructure. There’s no difference in signal or coverage. Tourist SIM cards sold at the airport are often labeled as simPATI or Kartu As, so there’s no need to distinguish between them.

**Can I Use an Indonesian SIM Card Back in Taiwan?**  
Technically, yes, but it will be charged at Indonesian roaming rates, which are very expensive. Immediately switch back to your Taiwan SIM or disable mobile data on your Indonesian SIM to avoid unexpected charges.

**Can I Make Calls After Running Out of Data?**  
With Telkomsel and Indosat prepaid SIM cards, voice calls and SMS balances are separate from data usage. Even if your data runs out, you can still make voice calls (to local Indonesian numbers) as long as your SIM has voice credit.
