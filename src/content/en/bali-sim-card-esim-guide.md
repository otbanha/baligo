## Comparison of Indonesia’s Top Three Telecoms—From a Traveler’s Perspective

### Telkomsel (Widest Coverage, Top Choice for Travelers)

![Telkomsel (Widest Coverage, Top Choice for Travelers)](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779239732579-af57c619fd217399aaa52c62626b953e.jpg)

Indonesia's largest telecom provider, with a market share of around 55%. In various areas of Bali, including mountainous villages like Amed, Munduk, and Sidemen, Telkomsel's 4G signal is the most stable among the three major providers.

- **4G/5G Status:** Full 4G coverage from Kuta to Nusa Dua's main tourist areas, with 5G hotspots in central Ubud
- **Most Popular Plan for Travelers:** Perdana Tourist SIM (prepaid SIM card), also known as **Telkomsel Tourist SIMPATI** (airport price approx. IDR 250,000–350,000, includes 15G–35G data). Local mobile shops often sell it at half or one-third the price.
- **Nusa Penida Signal:** Strongest among the three providers, with stable 4G from the pier to major attractions
- **Gili Islands Signal:** Coverage on Gili Trawangan, weaker on Gili Air, occasional dropouts on Gili Meno
- **Drawbacks:** About 30–40% more expensive than Indosat, airport vendors often inflate prices

**simPATI is a brand under Telkomsel, offering the same network—no need to distinguish between them.**

### Indosat Ooredoo / IM3 (Best Value for Money)

Prices are over 30% cheaper than Telkomsel, with fully adequate signal in urban tourist areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Sanur.

- **4G Status:** Normal in main tourist areas, but drops to 3G or no signal in remote areas like Amed and Munduk
- **Popular Plan for Travelers:** Freedom Internet plan, IDR 50,000–80,000 for 10–20GB
- **Best For:** Travelers focusing on Kuta and central Ubud, avoiding remote regions

### XL Axiata (Fast in Cities, Weak in Remote Areas)

In Seminyak and Kuta, XL's 4G speeds sometimes surpass Telkomsel, but coverage boundaries are very distinct.

- **Biggest Issue:** Extremely poor signal on Nusa Penida, with no coverage at some attractions
- **Why Not Recommended for Travelers:** Signal optimization focuses on cities, becoming unstable outside core tourist areas
- **Only Suitable Scenario:** Staying exclusively in Kuta and Seminyak, with high signal demands and no plans to visit outer islands

**Quick Conclusion on the Three Providers: As long as your itinerary isn't limited to city centers, choosing Telkomsel is a safe bet.**

## Where to Buy Physical SIM Cards—Complete Guide

![Where to Buy Physical SIM Cards—Complete Guide](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779240020594-e5c025ea6a97e3839ab39d4ff832db68.jpg)

### 1. Buy at Bali Airport (DPS)

**Most convenient, but also the most pitfalls.**

**Location:** After clearing customs in the arrival hall, official Telkomsel and Indosat booths are located next to the baggage carousel, along with numerous unofficial vendors.

***

⚠️ **Scam Warning—This is Important**

This is something Klook blogs and eSIM brand articles won’t tell you:

1. **Fake "Installation Fee"**: Vendors may claim an installation fee of IDR 200,000–800,000, which is a scam. Official SIM cards are automatically configured upon insertion and do not require manual installation fees.
2. **Pushing Expensive Plans with "Best Package for Tourist" Pitch**: Politely decline and ask directly for the Kartu Perdana (prepaid card) standard plan.

**Safe Practices:**

- Choose official booths (with Telkomsel / Indosat signage, not roadside tables)
- After inserting the SIM, dial \*888# to check your balance and confirm

***

**Installation Steps (DIY):**

1. Ensure your phone is unlocked (SIM-locked phones cannot swap cards)
2. Locate the SIM card slot and use the ejector tool to remove the original card
n3. Insert the new SIM card and restart your phone
4. If the network isn’t automatically configured, manually enter the APN settings (see the next section for guidance)

**By the way:** For transportation arrangements from the airport to your accommodation, check out [Arriving in Bali: Airport Transfer Options and First-Day Tips](/blog/bali-airport-transfer-guide/), which includes comparisons of private cars, taxis, and ride-hailing services.

### 2. Buying at Convenience Stores in the City

**Indomaret and Alfamart** are found on almost every street, and most outlets sell prepaid SIM cards.

- **Purchase Process:** Tell the clerk "mau beli kartu perdana Telkomsel," show your passport, and the clerk will register it
- **Price:** Usually 20–30% cheaper than at the airport, but with fewer data plan options
- **Notes:** Original passport required (copies may sometimes work), minors cannot purchase
- **Recommended Scenario:** If you’re not in a hurry, want to compare options, or if airport booths have long queues

It's worth noting that many staff in tourist areas are now quite accustomed to helping foreign travelers with SIM cards. However, there's a common pitfall for tourists: **not every convenience store can assist you with passport verification registration**! After purchasing:

- The SIM card might not work
- You may need to register it yourself
- Or visit an official store for assistance

Especially for foreign passport registration, some staff members aren't very familiar with the process.

### 3. Purchasing at Official Grapari Stores

Grapari is Telkomsel's official service center, ideal for long-term residents or those needing to resolve account issues.

- **Locations:** Stores in Kuta, Denpasar, and Ubud — search "Grapari Telkomsel" on Google Maps
- **Required documents:** Original passport, personal contact information
- **Advantages:** Monthly plans available (more cost-effective for stays over 30 days), immediate resolution of customer service issues
- **Disadvantages:** Possible wait times of 30–60 minutes

## Complete Installation Guide

![Complete Installation Guide](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779240073782-069909000_1519793545-where-to-buy-sim-card-in-ho-chi-minh.webp)

### iPhone eSIM Installation

**Method 1: QR Code Scan (Most Common)**

1. After purchasing the eSIM, obtain the QR Code image from the Klook / Airalo App
2. On your phone, go to 'Settings → Cellular → Add eSIM'
3. Select 'Use QR Code' and scan the image
4. Set up 'Primary SIM' (Taiwan number) and 'Cellular SIM' (Bali eSIM), assigning voice / data as needed

**Method 2: Manual Activation Code Entry**

1. If scanning isn't possible, choose 'Enter Details Manually'
2. Input the SM-DP+ address and activation code (found in the purchase confirmation email)

**Common Errors:**

- _'Unable to Add Cellular Plan'_: Ensure your phone is unlocked and iOS version is 12.1 or above
- _eSIM Installed but No Signal_: Verify if the activation code has already been used (each QR Code can only be used once)
- _Screenshot QR Code Won't Scan_: Display directly through the Klook App instead of scanning a screenshot

### Android eSIM Installation

Paths vary slightly by brand, using Samsung as an example:

1. Settings → Connections → SIM Card Manager → Add Mobile Plan
2. Scan QR Code or Enter Manually
3. After completion, switch between primary SIM and eSIM as needed

**Note:** Some Android phones (mainly U.S. carrier versions) have locked eSIM functionality. Confirm compatibility before purchasing.

[Check if your phone supports eSIM](https://saily.com/zh-tw/esim-supported-devices/)

### Physical SIM Card Replacement + APN Settings

**If automatic setup fails after replacement, manually configure APN as follows:**

| Carrier | APN Name | Username | Password |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Telkomsel | `internet` | (blank) | (blank) |
| Indosat IM3 | `indosatgprs` | (blank) | (blank) |

**iOS Setup Path:** Settings → Mobile Data → Mobile Data Network → Enter APN
**Android Setup Path:** Settings → Connections → Mobile Networks → Access Point Names (APN) → Add

**Common Errors:**

- _Still E/2G after setup:_ Restart your phone and wait 2–3 minutes for the network to reconnect
- _APN field appears gray:_ Your phone may be carrier-locked; unlock it before switching SIM cards

### Dual SIM Setup Recommendations for Taiwan Numbers

Using a Bali SIM for Internet While Keeping Your Taiwan Number for Calls:

1. **Set your Taiwan SIM to 'Voice Calls Only'** and turn off mobile data for the Taiwan SIM (to avoid roaming charges)
2. **Set your Bali SIM to 'Mobile Data'**
3. If your Taiwan SIM supports Wi-Fi calling, enable it to receive calls to your Taiwan number via Wi-Fi (completely free)

## Common Disasters & Instant Solutions

**Signal Suddenly Disappears**

- First, check if the APN settings have been reset (sometimes APN resets after toggling airplane mode on and off)
- Dial \*888# to check your remaining data balance. If it's zero, you'll need to top up
- For Telkomsel top-ups, you can purchase vouchers at Indomaret. Tell the clerk "isi ulang Telkomsel XXX ribu"

**eSIM Activation Failed**

- Check your internet connection during installation (Wi-Fi is required to download the eSIM profile)
- Each QR Code can only be installed once. If you've changed phones, contact the eSIM brand's customer service to get a new code
- Klook eSIM users can directly contact Chinese-speaking customer service within the app, usually receiving a response within 1 hour

**How to Top Up When Data Runs Out**

- Physical SIM: Purchase vouchers at Indomaret/Alfamart or top up using GoPay/Dana
- eSIM: Return to the purchase platform to buy a new one (usually doesn't support direct top-ups on the same SIM, just buy a new eSIM)

**What to Do When Changing Phones**

- Physical SIM: Simply remove and insert into new phone (make sure new phone is unlocked)
- eSIM: Need to delete from original phone and re-scan to install (some brands support this, Klook customer service can assist)

**Should I Deactivate After Returning to Taiwan**

- Physical SIM: Just remove it. Indonesian numbers usually expire after 90 days of inactivity
- eSIM: You can delete the eSIM profile from your phone or keep it as backup (takes up one eSIM slot)
- **Important:** After returning to Taiwan, make sure your Taiwan SIM is set as the primary data source to avoid roaming charges through your Bali SIM

## FAQ

**Is Bali eSIM the same as Indonesia eSIM?**
Yes, Bali is within Indonesia, so purchasing an Indonesia eSIM will work across the entire island without regional restrictions. Some brands label their plans as "Indonesia," which covers Bali, Java, and all other regions in Indonesia.

**Can I share hotspot with others using one eSIM?**
Most eSIMs (including Klook eSIM and Airalo) support hotspot sharing, but Holafly's unlimited plans **explicitly prohibit** hotspot usage. Please check the plan terms before purchasing to avoid disputes.

**Can I buy a SIM card at the airport if I don’t speak English?**
Official Telkomsel and Indosat counters at the airport offer English services, and you can also communicate using translation apps. Simply state the number of days and approximate data usage needed—vendors are experienced with tourists. However, stay alert during transactions; see the scam warning section in this article for details.

**Is it safe to buy eSIMs online with a credit card?**
Klook, Airalo, and Holafly are globally recognized platforms that use HTTPS encryption, making transactions as secure as regular online shopping. It’s recommended to use a credit card with no foreign transaction fees (e.g., Richart) for purchases.

**Which is more cost-effective: Wi-Fi router or SIM card?**
For trips under 5 days with 3 or more people, a Wi-Fi router divided per person might be cheaper. However, for trips over 5 days or with only 1–2 people, a SIM card or eSIM is almost always more economical, and you won’t have to worry about the router’s battery life. For a full comparison, refer to our article: [Bali Wi-Fi Router vs SIM Card Guide](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/).

**Is eSIM or physical SIM more cost-effective for groups?**
For 2 people or fewer, buying individual eSIMs is the most convenient. For groups of 3 or more, sharing a Telkomsel physical SIM with hotspot is the cheapest option (around USD$0.65 per person for IDR 120,000 divided among three). However, hotspot sharing drains battery faster, so bring a power bank.

**Is there signal on Gili Trawangan?**
Telkomsel has 4G signal near the Gili Trawangan pier, but the signal is intermittent inland. Gili Air and Gili Meno have very weak, unreliable signals. Most accommodations offer Wi-Fi, so it’s recommended to use Wi-Fi for important communications.

**Can I install eSIM before passing customs at the airport?**
Yes. eSIM installation requires Wi-Fi, and airport lounges provide free Wi-Fi. It’s best to **install the eSIM profile before departing Taiwan**, but set the activation time to start upon arrival in Indonesia. This way, you can switch to airplane mode, disable your Taiwan SIM, and use the eSIM immediately upon landing.

**Can I buy a physical SIM card with a passport copy?**
Officially, the original passport is required, but some vendors accept passport photos displayed on your phone. It’s recommended to carry your original passport or keep a high-resolution scanned copy in your phone’s photo album as a backup.

**Are Telkomsel and simPATI the same?**
Yes, simPATI is a prepaid card brand under Telkomsel, using the exact same Telkomsel network towers, so there’s no difference in signal or coverage. Tourist SIMs sold at the airport are often labeled as simPATI or Kartu As, so there’s no need to distinguish between them.

**Can I still use my Indonesian SIM card after returning to Taiwan?**
Technically, yes, but the rates will be charged as international roaming by the Indonesian telecom provider, which is very expensive. After returning to Taiwan, immediately switch back to your Taiwanese SIM or disable mobile data on the Indonesian SIM to avoid unexpected charges.

**Can I still make calls after using up my data?**
For Telkomsel and Indosat prepaid SIM cards, voice calls and SMS balances are calculated separately from data usage. Even if your data runs out, as long as your SIM has voice credit, you can still make voice calls (applicable to local Indonesian numbers).
