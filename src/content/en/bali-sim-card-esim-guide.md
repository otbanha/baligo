## Comparison of Indonesia’s Top 3 Telecom Providers—From a Traveler’s Perspective

### Telkomsel (Widest Coverage, Top Choice for Travelers)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779239732579-af57c619fd217399aaa52c62626b953e.jpg)

Indonesia’s largest telecom provider, with a market share of around 55%. In Bali, including mountainous villages like Amed, Munduk, and Sidemen, Telkomsel’s 4G signal is the most stable among the three providers.

- **4G/5G Status:** Full 4G coverage from Kuta to Nusa Dua's main tourist areas, with 5G hotspots in central Ubud
- **Most Popular Plan for Tourists:** Perdana Tourist SIM (prepaid SIM card), also known as **Telkomsel Tourist SIMPATI** (airport price approx. IDR 250,000–350,000, includes 15–35GB data). Often available at 1/2 or 1/3 the price in city mobile shops.
- **Nusa Penida Signal:** Strongest among the three providers, stable 4G from the pier to major attractions
- **Gili Islands Signal:** Signal available on Gili Trawangan, weaker on Gili Air, occasional dropouts on Gili Meno
- **Drawback:** About 30–40% more expensive than Indosat, with frequent price inflation at airport stalls

**simPATI is a brand under Telkomsel, offering the same network—no need to distinguish between them.**

### Indosat Ooredoo / IM3 (Best Value for Money)

Prices are over 30% cheaper than Telkomsel, with fully adequate signal in urban tourist areas like Kuta, Seminyak, and Sanur.

- **4G Status:** Normal in main tourist areas, but drops to 3G or no signal in remote areas like Amed and Munduk
- **Popular Plan for Tourists:** Freedom Internet plan, IDR 50,000–80,000 for 10–20GB
- **Best For:** Travelers focusing on Kuta and central Ubud, avoiding remote regions

### XL Axiata (Fast in Cities, Weak in Remote Areas)

4G speeds in Seminyak and Kuta sometimes surpass Telkomsel, but coverage boundaries are very distinct.

- **Biggest Issue:** Extremely poor signal in Nusa Penida, with no signal at some attractions
- **Reason Not Recommended for Tourists:** Signal map optimization focused on cities, unstable outside core tourist areas
- **Only Suitable Scenario:** Staying exclusively in Kuta and Seminyak, with high signal demands and no island hopping

**Quick Conclusion for the Three Major Providers:** As long as your itinerary isn't limited to city centers, choosing Telkomsel won't go wrong.**

## Where to Buy Physical SIM Cards — Complete Guide

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779240020594-e5c025ea6a97e3839ab39d4ff832db68.jpg)

### 1. Buying at Bali Airport (DPS)

**Most convenient, but full of traps.**

**Location:** After clearing customs in the arrival hall, official Telkomsel and Indosat booths are located next to the baggage carousels, along with numerous unofficial vendors.

***

⚠️ **Scam Warning — This is Important**

Here’s what Klook blogs and eSIM brand articles won’t tell you:

1. **Fake "Installation Fee" (installasi fee):** Vendors may claim an IDR 200,000–800,000 setup fee — this is a scam. Official SIM cards are automatically configured upon insertion; no manual installation fee is needed.
2. **Pushing expensive plans with "best package for tourist" tactics:** Politely decline and ask for the Kartu Perdana (prepaid card) standard plan.

**Safe Approach:**

- Choose official booths (with Telkomsel / Indosat signage, not roadside tables)
- After inserting the SIM, dial \*888# to check your balance and confirm

***

**Installation Steps (DIY):**

1. Ensure your phone is unlocked (phones locked to a specific SIM cannot switch cards)
2. Locate the SIM card slot and use the ejector tool to remove the original card
3. Insert the new SIM card and restart your phone
4. If the network settings don’t configure automatically, manually enter the APN settings (see the next section for guidance)

**By the way:** For transportation arrangements from the airport to your accommodation, check out [Arriving in Bali: Transportation Options and Practical Tips for Your First Day](/blog/bali-airport-transfer-guide/), which includes comparisons of private transfers, taxis, and ride-hailing services.

### 2. Buying from Convenience Stores in the City

**Indomaret and Alfamart** are found on almost every street, and most outlets sell prepaid SIM cards.

- **Purchase Process:** Tell the clerk "mau beli kartu perdana Telkomsel," show your passport, and the clerk will activate it
- **Price:** Typically 20–30% cheaper than at the airport, but with fewer data plan options
- **Note:** A physical passport is required (sometimes a copy works), and minors cannot purchase
- **Recommended Scenario:** If you’re not in a hurry, want to compare options, or the airport stalls have long queues

Be aware that many clerks in tourist areas are now accustomed to helping foreign travelers with SIM cards. However, there’s a common pitfall: **not every convenience store can complete the "passport实名 registration" for you!** After purchasing:

- The SIM card might not work
- You may need to register it yourself
- Or visit an official store to resolve the issue

Especially for foreign passport registration, some clerks may not be familiar with the process.

### 3. Purchasing from Official Grapari Stores

Grapari is Telkomsel’s official service center, ideal for long-term residents or those needing to resolve account issues.

- **Locations:** Stores available in Kuta, Denpasar, and Ubud. Search "Grapari Telkomsel" on Google Maps.
- **Required documents:** Original passport, personal contact information
- **Pros:** Monthly plans available (more cost-effective for stays over 30 days), on-the-spot resolution of customer service issues
- **Cons:** May require waiting in line, potentially for 30–60 minutes

## Complete Installation Guide

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779240073782-069909000_1519793545-where-to-buy-sim-card-in-ho-chi-minh.webp)

### iPhone eSIM Installation

**Method 1: QR Code Scanning (Most Common)**

1. After purchasing the eSIM, obtain the QR Code image from the Klook / Airalo App
2. On your phone, go to "Settings → Cellular → Add eSIM"
3. Select "Use QR Code" and scan the image
4. Set up "Primary SIM" (Taiwan number) and "Cellular SIM" (Bali eSIM), assigning voice / data as needed

**Method 2: Manual Activation Code Entry**

1. If scanning is not possible, select "Enter Details Manually"
2. Enter the SM-DP+ address and activation code (found in the purchase confirmation email)

**Common Mistakes:**

- _"Unable to Add Mobile Plan"_: Ensure your phone is unlocked and running iOS 12.1 or above

- _"eSIM Installed but No Signal"_: Verify if the activation code has already been used (each QR Code can only be used once)

- _"Screenshot QR Code Cannot Be Scanned"_: Display it directly in the Klook App, don't scan after taking a screenshot

### Android eSIM Installation

Paths may vary slightly by brand. Using Samsung as an example:

1. Settings → Connections → SIM Card Manager → Add Mobile Plan

2. Scan the QR Code or enter manually

3. After completion, you can switch between the main SIM and eSIM

**Note:** Some Android phones (mainly US carrier versions) have locked eSIM functionality. Confirm before purchasing.

**iOS Setup Path:** Settings → Cellular → Cellular Data Network → Enter in APN field
**Android Setup Path:** Settings → Connections → Mobile Networks → Access Point Names (APN) → Add New

**Common Mistakes:**

- _Still E/2G after setup_: Restart your phone once, wait 2–3 minutes for the network to reconnect
- _APN field appears gray_: Your phone may be locked by the carrier, unlock it before switching SIM cards

### Dual SIM Setup Recommendation for Keeping Taiwan Number

Use Bali SIM for internet, keep Taiwan number for calls:

1. **Set Taiwan SIM as "Voice Call" card**, turn off mobile data for Taiwan SIM (to avoid roaming fees)
2. **Set Bali SIM as "Mobile Data" card**
3. If Taiwan SIM has Wi-Fi calling, enable it to receive calls via Wi-Fi (completely free)

## Common Disasters and Immediate Solutions

**Signal Suddenly Disappears**

- First, check if the APN settings have been cleared (sometimes APN resets after toggling airplane mode on and off)
- Dial \*888# to check your data balance. If it's zero, you'll need to top up
- For Telkomsel top-ups, you can purchase vouchers at Indomaret. Tell the cashier "isi ulang Telkomsel XXX ribu"

**eSIM Activation Failure**

- Ensure a stable internet connection during installation (Wi-Fi is required to download the eSIM profile)
- Each QR Code can only be used once. If you've changed phones, contact the eSIM brand's customer service for a new code
- Klook eSIM users can directly contact Chinese-speaking customer service within the app, usually receiving a response within 1 hour

**How to Top Up When Data Runs Out**

- Physical SIM: Purchase vouchers at Indomaret / Alfamart, or top up using GoPay / Dana
- eSIM: Return to the purchase platform to buy a new one (usually, direct top-ups on the original SIM aren't supported; just buy a new eSIM)

**What to Do When Changing Phones**

- Physical SIM: Simply remove and insert it into the new phone (ensure the new phone is unlocked)
- eSIM: You'll need to delete it from the original phone and scan the QR code again to install (some brands support this; Klook customer service can assist)

**Should I Turn It Off After Returning to Taiwan**

- Physical SIM: Just remove it. Indonesian numbers typically expire after 90 days of inactivity
- eSIM: You can delete the eSIM profile from your phone or keep it as a backup (it occupies one eSIM slot)
- **Important:** After returning to Taiwan, ensure your Taiwan SIM is set as the primary data source to avoid roaming charges from the Bali SIM

## FAQ

**Is a Bali eSIM the same as an Indonesia eSIM?**
Yes, since Bali is part of Indonesia, purchasing an Indonesia eSIM will work across the entire island without regional restrictions. Some brands label their plans as "Indonesia," which covers Bali, Java, and other regions across the country.

**Can I share a hotspot with others using one eSIM?**
Most eSIMs (including Klook eSIM and Airalo) support hotspot sharing, but Holafly's unlimited plans **explicitly prohibit** hotspot usage. Please check the plan terms before purchasing to avoid disputes.

**Can I buy a SIM card at the airport if I don’t speak English?**
Official Telkomsel and Indosat counters at the airport offer English services, and you can also use translation apps to communicate. Simply state the number of days and approximate data usage needed, as vendors are experienced with tourists. However, remain cautious during transactions—refer to the scam warning section in this article for details.

**Is it safe to buy an eSIM online with a credit card?**
Klook, Airalo, and Holafly are globally recognized platforms that use HTTPS encryption, making transactions as secure as typical online shopping. It’s recommended to use a TWD credit card with no foreign transaction fees (like Richart) for purchases.

**Which is more cost-effective: a Wi-Fi router or a SIM card?**
For trips under 5 days with 3 or more people, a Wi-Fi router divided per person might be cheaper. However, for trips over 5 days or with only 1–2 people, a SIM card or eSIM is almost always more economical, and you won’t have to worry about the router’s battery life. For a detailed comparison, refer to our [Bali Wi-Fi Router vs SIM Card Guide](/blog/bali-sim-card-esim-roaming-guide/).

**Is an eSIM or a physical SIM card better for group travel?**
For 2 people or fewer, buying individual eSIMs is the most convenient option. For groups of 3 or more, sharing a Telkomsel physical SIM card with hotspot enabled is the cheapest solution (IDR 120,000 split among three people costs around USD$1 per person). However, hotspot sharing drains battery faster, so bring a power bank.

**Will there be signal on Gili Trawangan?**
Telkomsel provides 4G signal near the Gili Trawangan pier, but the signal is intermittent inland. Gili Air and Gili Meno have very weak, unreliable signals. Most accommodations offer Wi-Fi, so it’s recommended to use Wi-Fi for important communications.

**Can I install an eSIM before passing through customs at the airport?**
Yes. Installing an eSIM requires Wi-Fi, which is available in airport waiting areas. It’s best to **install the eSIM profile before departing from Taiwan**, but set the activation time for when you arrive in Indonesia. Once you land, turn on airplane mode and disable your Taiwan SIM—your eSIM will be ready to use immediately.

**Can I buy a physical SIM card with a passport copy?**
Officially, the original passport is required, but some vendors accept a photo of your passport on your phone. It’s recommended to carry your original passport or keep a high-resolution scanned copy in your phone’s photo album as a backup.

**Is Telkomsel the same as simPATI?**
Yes, simPATI is a prepaid card brand under Telkomsel, using the exact same Telkomsel network towers with no difference in signal or coverage. Tourist SIM cards sold at airports are often branded as simPATI or Kartu As, so there's no need to distinguish between them.

**Can I still use my Indonesian SIM card after returning to Taiwan?**
Technically, yes, but the rates will be charged according to Indonesian telecom roaming, which is very expensive. Please switch back to your Taiwan SIM immediately upon return, or disable mobile data on your Indonesian SIM to avoid unexpected charges.

**Can I still make calls after running out of data?**
For Telkomsel and Indosat prepaid SIM cards, voice calls and SMS balances are calculated separately from data usage. Even if your data runs out, you can still make voice calls if your SIM has a voice balance (applicable to local numbers within Indonesia).
