Bali, a picturesque vacation destination, attracts visitors from around the world. When planning your trip to this island, you'll inevitably face an important question: Should you hire a private car or use ride-hailing services? In this article, I'll share our experiences with both options during our Bali trip to help you make the best choice.

The safest and most convenient transportation methods in Bali are "private car hire" and "ride-hailing services." Private car hire means you have a driver at your disposal for a set period (usually 10 or 5 hours) to take you wherever you want to go. Ride-hailing services, on the other hand, function like taxis, using apps like Gojek or Grab for point-to-point transportation.

## Pros and Cons of Private Car Hire

https://youtu.be/0fqEvAzAy9A

1. **Customized Itinerary:** Private car hire offers a tailored travel experience, allowing you to arrange your schedule according to your preferences and needs, with flexible timing for visiting attractions, giving you full control over your travel pace.
2. **Professional Guide:** Most private car drivers are local guides familiar with the area's culture and attractions, providing you with more professional tour services.
3. **Pre-Trip Planning:** The driver will discuss itinerary arrangements with you to ensure your journey goes smoothly.
4. **Comfort:** Private car hire offers a private and comfortable space, allowing you to relax during your travels without external disturbances.
5. **No Waiting:** When you finish visiting a site, a simple call or message will have your driver ready to take you to the next destination, offering exclusive VIP treatment.
6. **Thoughtful Service:** Most private car drivers can also help look after your children, watch your luggage while you explore, and quickly arrange for any sudden cravings or shopping needs during the trip.
7. **Photography:** If you're lucky, some drivers might be willing to take photos for you, and some are quite skilled at it. However, it's worth noting that this isn't part of their job description, so consider it a bonus if you encounter such a driver.
8. **Cons:** Higher cost, typically around 600,000 IDR (USD$40) or more for a 10-hour rental (including driver and fuel).

## Pros and Cons of Ride-Hailing Services

https://youtube.com/shorts/wnhyqUekdW0

1. **Cost-Effective:** Hailing a ride (app-based only) is usually more economical than hiring a private car, especially for short trips or when you need transportation to and from a single destination.
2. **Instant Availability:** You can hail a ride anytime, anywhere, without needing to book in advance, making it ideal for last-minute transportation needs.
3. **Flexibility:** You can change your itinerary and destination on the go, without being tied to a pre-arranged schedule.
4. **Avoid Traffic:** There’s also a "motorbike taxi" option, where you ride on the back of a motorcycle. This is the least affected by traffic congestion.
5. **Drawbacks of Hailing a Ride:**
   You might have to wait outdoors in the heat for the vehicle to arrive, which could take anywhere from 5 to 30 minutes.
   Most drivers are concentrated in busy areas, so if you’re in or heading to a remote location, it might be harder to find a willing driver.
   Most drivers aren’t chatty, and some don’t speak English.
   They can’t provide services like "suddenly craving mangosteen," "wanting to try snake fruit for the first time," or "knowing where to buy small scissors."

## Private Car or Hailing a Ride? How to Choose?

If your itinerary involves long-distance travel, there might be fewer drivers willing to accept your ride request. This is because they’d be leaving their familiar area and risking an empty return trip. In such cases, hiring a private car might be a better option. It saves you time and energy, allowing you to focus more on enjoying your trip without constantly hailing and waiting for rides.

**You can refer to/apply the following formula to choose the most cost-effective/safe transportation method:**

- Need to exchange currency, get a SIM card, or go to the supermarket upon arrival in Bali ▶ Private car
- Visiting two or more destinations in a day ▶ Private car
- The day’s itinerary includes long-distance "cross-region" travel ▶ Private car
- Popular photo spots (with long queues) ▶ Private car
- Moving within busy areas ▶ App-based ride
- Short trips to a single destination and back ▶ App-based ride
- Heading to non-busy areas ▶ Private car
- Solo female traveler prioritizing safety ▶ Private car
- Traveling with two or more people ▶ Private car (costs are more affordable when split)
- Traveling with kids and needing extra care ▶ Private car
- Want someone to take group photos ▶ Private car
- Changing accommodations/flight departures/luggage in the car while visiting spots and dining ▶ Private car
- Solo traveler moving short distances in the city ▶ App-based motorbike
- Unstable weather (rainy season) ▶ Private car
- Staying in suburban/remote areas ▶ Private car
- Don’t want to wait/need VIP treatment ▶ Private car
- Want to chat/need basic explanations ▶ Private car
- Want to meet local Balinese friends ▶ Private car

## FAQ

Choosing a driver recommended by travel groups adds an extra layer of safety for solo travelers.

### 1. How much does a private car cost?

The cost of hiring a private car varies based on the driver’s language skills, the length of the trip, and the duration. Generally, a 10-hour day costs around USD$40.

### 2.How long does it take to get a ride through the app?

The waiting time for a ride is usually around 10 minutes, but it might be slightly longer during peak hours.

### 3. Do I need to bring a private driver when visiting attractions? Do I need to cover the driver's meal expenses?

Generally, no. In most cases, the driver will wait nearby until you need their service. You don't need to cover the driver's meal expenses; they will handle it themselves.

### 4. Which ride-hailing app is cheaper?

Usually, Gojek tends to be slightly cheaper.

### 5. Can I hail a taxi on the street?

Yes, but it's not recommended. There's a high chance of encountering drivers who overcharge.
