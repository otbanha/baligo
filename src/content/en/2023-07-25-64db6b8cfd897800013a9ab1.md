Staying overnight at Bali Safari is absolutely fascinating!

While swimming, you might occasionally encounter elephants on duty, leading guests from 'Bali Safari' towards you (elephant rides require an additional fee). The complimentary fruit basket (apples, mangoes, bananas) provided with your stay makes a perfect gift for your new elephant friends.

The 'Bali Safari' zoo also offers a 'Night Safari' experience, which includes a buffet dinner. Enjoy the show while dining in an outdoor setting. Hotel guests can watch the performance for free.

After dinner, embark on a cage ride to see the animals. Due to exhaustion, I skipped this part, but if you're interested, remember to book a package that includes 'Night Safari' for better savings.

The only restaurant in 'Mara River Safari Lodge', 'Tsavo Lion Restaurant', features majestic lions roaming outside the glass windows. Watching lions while having breakfast is utterly adorable (melting). Hotel guests enjoy complimentary breakfast here, while non-guests must pay extra to enter.

During this stay, both dinner (paid) and breakfast (complimentary) were at 'Tsavo Lion Restaurant'. The lions are very active at night but sleepy in the morning.

Additionally (the perks are endless, absolutely satisfying), exclusive intimate encounters are arranged for hotel guests at fixed times, bringing animals right in front of you for photos and touches.

At breakfast time (08:00), a chameleon (?) personally visits the restaurant to accompany your meal. After breakfast (09:00), I met Mr. Elephant strolling in the small garden by the hotel entrance for an intimate encounter without queuing, truly delightful.

Moreover, hotel guests enjoy unlimited access to the 'Bali Safari' park for two days (check-in & check-out days). You can even take a nap in your hotel bed between animal shows, super relaxing.

The 'Bali Safari' park isn't vast. Riding the air-conditioned Safari Bus (Safari Journey) allows you to see most of the park's animals, complemented by various spectacular animal shows.

The following are definitely worth including in your itinerary:

**Check-in day:**

> 14:00-15:00 Elephant Bath (Get up close and watch elephants bathe)

> 16:00-16:15 Animal Show (Must-see! Adorable animals coming from all directions)

> 16:30-16:50 Elephant Show (A performance intertwined with historical storytelling)

**Check-out day:**

> 09:00-10:00 Elephant Encounter Mara (Elephants personally guard the hotel entrance)

> 11:15-11:35 Big Cat Show (Must-see! Tigers climbing trees and swimming in water—quite fascinating)

In addition to the above, the park also features a freshwater aquarium, water park, and land park, though these are less appealing and can be skipped.

For non-locals, the ticket prices for Bali Safari are quite steep. If time permits, consider staying overnight at Mara River Safari Lodge within Bali Safari. **The accommodation plus ticket package offers much better value than purchasing tickets separately.** Mara River Safari Lodge:
