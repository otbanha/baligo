In [our previous article](/blog/2023-11-13-65504084fd897800011c51f2/), we discussed how to safely bring your drone to Indonesia/Bali. Today, we will delve deeper into the rules and regulations you need to follow when flying a drone in Indonesia/Bali.

The most important aspect is undoubtedly '****safety****'. Drone flight safety concerns not only yourself but also others. Therefore, there are several local regulations you must adhere to:

First, the flight altitude of the drone must not exceed 150 meters. This is not only to ensure the safety of aerial flights but also to avoid interference with other aircraft or natural ecosystems.

Second, do not fly drones within 15 kilometers of any major airport. This regulation is to ensure that drones do not interfere with normal air traffic, thereby preventing safety hazards.

Third, please do not fly drones at night. Visibility is lower at night, making such operations not only dangerous but also likely to cause disturbances to those around you.

Additionally, never fly drones in crowded or densely populated areas. This not only may cause panic but also increases the risk of flight accidents.

Finally, do not fly over temples in Bali. These places are extremely important to local residents, and respecting local culture and religious beliefs is basic courtesy.

The purpose of these regulations is to ensure everyone's safety while also hoping that drone flying can be a pleasurable activity. Any behavior that ignores these rules may face fines or even criminal charges.

Flying drones is a fascinating experience, yet we must remember that with this enjoyment comes great responsibility and respect. Adhering to regulations isn't just an obligation - it's our way of showing respect for the local community and environment. Let's ensure everyone can enjoy this wonderful activity while protecting this enchanting paradise.
