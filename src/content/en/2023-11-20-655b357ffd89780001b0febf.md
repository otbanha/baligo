In [our previous article](/blog/2023-11-13-65504084fd897800011c51f2/), we discussed how to safely bring your drone to Indonesia/Bali. Today, we will delve into the rules and regulations you need to follow when flying drones in Indonesia/Bali.

The most important aspect is undoubtedly **safety**. Drone flight safety concerns not only yourself but also others. To ensure this, there are several local regulations you must adhere to:

First, drones must not fly higher than 150 meters. This is to ensure airspace safety and avoid interference with other aircraft or natural ecosystems.

Second, do not fly drones within 15 kilometers of any major airport. This rule ensures that drones do not interfere with normal air traffic, preventing potential safety hazards.

Third, avoid flying drones at night. Low visibility during nighttime makes such operations dangerous and can easily disturb those around you.

Additionally, never fly drones in crowded or densely populated areas. This can cause panic and increases the risk of flight accidents.

Lastly, do not fly drones over temples in Bali. These sites hold great significance for local residents, and respecting local culture and religious beliefs is a basic courtesy.

These regulations are in place to ensure everyone's safety while allowing drone flying to remain an enjoyable activity. Any disregard for these rules may result in fines or even criminal charges.

Drone flying is a fascinating experience, but we must remember that responsibility and respect are equally important behind the fun. Following these regulations isn't just an obligation - it's our way of showing respect for the local community and environment. Let's ensure everyone can enjoy this wonderful activity while protecting this enchanting paradise.
