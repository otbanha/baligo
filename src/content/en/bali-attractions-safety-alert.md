**Key takeaway**: **Diamond Beach on Nusa Penida has strong rip currents, and swimming or wading is officially prohibited** (a tourist was once swept away by waves but rescued with a rope); **Stay well away from the cliff edge at Bingin Beach in Uluwatu** (a tourist died after falling from the cliff). Both are popular photo spots, so maintain a safe distance and don't risk danger for the perfect shot.

Recently, two serious accidents occurred at two famous attractions in Bali—Diamond Beach on Nusa Penida and Bingin Beach in Uluwatu—prompting local rescue teams to launch emergency operations.

![佩尼達島這麼美麗的沙灘卻暗藏危機！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_99cb39ec89e9c64fa76b4dda3a453eef.jpg)

This beautiful beach on Nusa Penida hides hidden dangers!

On August 14, at Diamond Beach on Nusa Penida, a tourist was swept into the sea by waves, creating a life-threatening situation. Fortunately, a French tourist on site reacted quickly, working with others to use a long rope to pull the victim back to shore, averting a tragedy. Diamond Beach is known for its crystal-clear bay, but the waters are extremely dangerous due to strong, unpredictable rip currents and powerful swells that appear calm on the surface. Local officials repeatedly stress that swimming or even wading in shallow water is strictly prohibited to prevent accidents.

On the same day, a tragedy struck at Bingin Beach in Uluwatu. A tourist fell from the famous cliff top and died. The body was first spotted by a French tourist, and rescue teams rushed to the scene. However, due to the steep cliff base, they couldn't safely retrieve the body until the next morning using specialized equipment. The victim was identified as a 51-year-old American. It's believed he parked his rented scooter on the cliff top at the Batu Jaran section of Bingin Beach before the accident.

These two incidents have sparked widespread concern among locals and tourism operators about safety. While Nusa Penida officials have pledged to enhance safety measures at tourist spots, local politicians and industry leaders are urging the government to speed up the process and establish stricter safety regulations to protect visitors' lives. They believe relevant authorities need to strengthen cooperation and coordination to create unified safety standards for these popular attractions, preventing similar tragedies.

As one of the world's most famous travel destinations, Bali attracts countless visitors to explore its stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. However, these incidents serve as a reminder that while enjoying Bali's beauty, travelers must remain vigilant at all times, follow local safety guidelines, and ensure their own well-being. Local authorities and relevant agencies should also continue to monitor and improve safety facilities at tourist attractions, providing a safer travel environment for visitors.
