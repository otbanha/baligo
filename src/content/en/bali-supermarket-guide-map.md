## 🛒 What to Buy at Bali Supermarkets? Must-Have Shopping List

When you step into the supermarket, what should you grab? According to the content from [Xiao Jie Indonesia](https://www.youtube.com/@j_indonesia), here are some essential items you shouldn’t miss:

### 🛑 Must-Buy Essentials for Your Trip

- **Local Indonesian Mosquito Repellent:** Mosquito repellent brought from Taiwan might not work on local mosquitoes! Indonesian repellent is formulated for Southeast Asia, affordable, and highly effective. You can choose between lotion and spray types.
- **Essential Medications (ask the staff for recommendations):**
    - Panadol
    - Cold medicine (Obat flu)
    - Diarrhea medication (Obat diare)
    - Pain relief patches (Salonpas)
- **Sunscreen and Aloe Vera Gel:** With Bali’s outdoor activities, sunscreen is cheaper locally than in Taiwan. If you get sunburned, grab a bottle of **Aloe Vera Gel** to quickly soothe the burn.
- **European Adapter and Bottled Water:** Bali uses the European two-round-pin plug (220V). Buying an adapter at the supermarket is cheap, and pairing it with your multi-port extension cord will suffice.

👉 Quick Reference: [Bali Travel Voltage/Plug Guide: Charging Drones, DSLRs, and Laptops (Including Universal Adapter Tips)](/blog/bali-voltage-adapter-tips/)

- **Tap Water is Not Safe to Drink:** Some beach hotels have desalinated water with an unbearable taste, and hotels usually only provide two small bottles per day. It’s recommended to buy large bottles of water at the supermarket. Trusted brands include **Amidis, Cleo, Aqua.**

### 🍪 Snacks, Souvenirs, and Instant Noodles

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779190258001-78dbd5473ae771c1f842dd0b00c2319e.jpg)

- **Kusuka Cassava Chips (Singkong):** Indonesian snacks are world-famous! These cassava chips have a better texture than potato chips, are rich in fiber, and contain only 60% of the calories. A must-buy for chip lovers watching their weight.
- **Nut-Based Souvenirs:**
    - **Mr. P Peanuts/Cashews:** The blue honey flavor is highly recommended locally.
    - **Bali Garlic Peanuts:** Rich in garlic flavor, these are a heavenly treat for nut-loving friends.
- **Pop Mie Cup Noodles:** A great late-night companion while traveling. The cup comes with a fork/spoon, and all you need is hot water to enjoy. Available in various flavors, perfect for trying something new.
- **Melt-in-Your-Mouth Chocolate:** Indonesian chocolate is affordable and delicious. Pure chocolate, chocolate wafer rolls, and filled biscuits are highly recommended.

### ☕ Coffee, Tea, and Alcoholic Beverages

- **Indonesia's Top Five Coffees:** Indonesia is a major coffee exporter. In supermarkets, you can find **Bali Kintamani Coffee**, Toraja, Aceh Gayo, Flores Bajawa, and the world's most expensive Kopi Luwak (cat poop coffee).
- **Bintang Beer:** Indonesia's largest beer brand, it's smooth and easy to drink. Besides the regular beer (4.5% alcohol), there's also a lemon/orange flavor with only 2% alcohol (tastes like fruit soda), and even a non-alcoholic version that mimics beer flavor!
- **Cooling Miracle Water (Indonesian Rhino Water/Tiga Kaki):** A natural herbal water with a rhino logo on the label, it's colorless and tasteless like plain water but has amazing cooling effects. It also comes in fruit flavors like orange and grape.
- **Teh Botol Bottled Tea:** Known as "Indonesia's Coca-Cola," it's made from natural green tea, jasmine, water, and sugar, with no preservatives and high in catechins.
- **Luwak White Koffie:** Convenient and affordable bottled coffee (Nestlé foil packs cost about USD$0.30), this white coffee is highly recommended.

### 🥑 Bali's Specialty Fruits

- **Salak (Snake Fruit):** A unique Indonesian fruit with a snake-like skin. Rich in nutrients and high in potassium, it's known as the "memory fruit" and offers beauty, eye health, and digestive benefits.
- **Avocado:** At about USD$2 per kilogram (roughly half the price in Taiwan), if preparing it yourself at the supermarket is inconvenient, you can always order a rich avocado juice (Avocado Juice) at local restaurants!

## 🧸 Hidden Gem Area: Clothing and Souvenirs Without Haggling

If you visit large supermarkets (like Bintang Supermarket), you'll often find sections for clothing, toys, and souvenirs. Here, you can buy tropical resort wear, accessories, bikinis, and Bali-themed souvenirs. The advantage of buying souvenirs at supermarkets is that **haggling is neither required nor allowed**, making it transparent and fair for travelers who aren't comfortable bargaining!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779190670051-mixcollage-19-may-2026-06-26-pm-7181.jpg)

**【Important Reminder】Bring Your Own Shopping Bag:** To promote environmental protection, major supermarkets and stores in Bali **do not provide plastic bags**, so remember to carry a reusable bag when you go shopping!
