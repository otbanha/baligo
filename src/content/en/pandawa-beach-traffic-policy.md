Pandawa Beach in Uluwatu, located at the southernmost tip of Bali, is often referred to as the "Blue Highway" due to its stunning limestone cliffs and beautiful white sand beaches. This area has become a must-visit destination for an increasing number of tourists. However, with the surge in visitors over the past year and a half, local authorities have decided to adjust the access methods to ensure smoother and safer traffic for both pedestrians and vehicles.

![峇里島新景點 Tanah Barak](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7bfc289c1a511e6bc49e67cf8c4bb07d.jpg)

Bali's New Attraction: Tanah Barak

Tanah Barak is the most popular entrance to Pandawa Beach. Visitors walking from the parking lot to the beach must pass through a narrow cliffside path, which has become a hotspot for photos. To enhance pedestrian safety, private vehicles are now prohibited from using this path. Instead, authorities have introduced a shuttle service, which not only protects pedestrians but also reduces traffic congestion in the area, making the environment appear cleaner.

Local official Ni Luh Hepi Wiradani explained that this policy was developed based on feedback from tourists. "Many visitors expressed a desire for more order in Tanah Barak, with fewer vehicles disrupting their experience. Therefore, we adjusted the traffic flow to make everyone's visit more enjoyable."

![Tanah Berak 一點不輸給日本著名景點「立山黑部」](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8129df44716b2513f37dca46a64237cd.jpg)

Tanah Barak Rivals Japan's Famous Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route

Now, whether arriving by motorcycle or car, visitors must park at the Lelangon parking lot and then proceed to the beach either on foot or by shuttle. The ticket price remains unchanged at IDR 25,000 per person, applicable to both domestic and international tourists.

The new system has been in operation for a few days and has received positive feedback, with both tourists and the local community expressing satisfaction with the new measures. "Since the policy was implemented, visitors have reported feeling more at ease without the interference of vehicles," said a spokesperson.

The shuttle service can transport up to 800 visitors daily between the parking lot and the beach. For those wishing to photograph the cliffside path, walking remains the best option. During peak tourist seasons, Pandawa Beach can welcome over 2,000 visitors daily. What was once a "hidden gem" has now become a must-visit destination for both local and international travelers.

The management team of Pandawa Beach is also rapidly adjusting their management methods to cope with the increasing number of tourists. For instance, during this year's Eid al-Fitr, the daily number of visitors to Pandawa Beach reached 2,500 to 3,000, posing a significant challenge for the management.

Local leaders are also considering new zoning plans, aiming to divide the beach into several specialized areas, including a public beach zone, water sports zone, beach club zone, dining zone, and a private beach area reserved for private events such as weddings and proposals.
