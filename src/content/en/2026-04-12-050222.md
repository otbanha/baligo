Recently in our Facebook group, someone asked: 'Do I need to bring my own showerhead when traveling to Bali? Should I use bottled water for brushing my teeth?' The original poster mentioned that online information is quite polarized and wanted to hear from people who have actually been there. The comment section was extremely active, with various experiences shared - different body types, different types of accommodations, and stories of both 'getting sick' and 'being fine'.

I've compiled nearly 40 comments into three key points to give you a much-needed boost (or a heads-up) if you're feeling anxious.

### 🔹 Conclusion One: **Showerhead——Most people say 'not necessary,' but the filter in Ubud/homestays tends to discolor**

- **Most comments**: No need to change the showerhead, it's fine to shower directly.
- **But some experiences**: Bringing a filtered showerhead to **Ubud or guesthouses**, the filter noticeably turned yellow with impurities. Staying at **hotels**, the filter remained clean.
- **Netizen's test**: Someone posted a photo of "the filter after three nights in Ubud", and it was really yellow.
- **Conclusion**: If you're staying at high-end hotels or resorts, the water quality is usually OK. If staying in **Ubud area villas, guesthouses, or if you have sensitive skin**, bringing a filtered showerhead as insurance is recommended, as it doesn't take up much space.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775969361098-shower_head.jpg)

### 🔹 Conclusion Two: **Brushing Teeth and Rinsing Mouth—Half Mineral Water, Half Tap Water, the Key Lies in 'Constitution'**

- **Bottled Water Camp**: Rinse with bottled water after brushing for peace of mind.
- **Tap Water Camp**: Brush directly with tap water, no discomfort.
- **Harsh Reality**: Some netizens shared that **even using bottled water to brush teeth in the same hotel, a friend still got 'dirty water sickness'**; others mentioned eating street food in India without issues but getting diarrhea in Taiwan. **It really depends on the person.**
- **Recommendations**:
    - Sensitive stomach, first-time visitors, traveling with elderly or children → **stick to brushing with bottled water**.
    - Iron stomach, frequent visitors, staying at good hotels → most say it's fine.

### 🔹 Conclusion Three: **Ice Cubes, Street Vendors, and Restaurant Drinks - High-Risk Items**

- **Ice**: Many say it's fine to drink beverages with ice from restaurants, but some netizens warn: "Avoid adding ice to drinks" as basic self-protection.
- **Street food stalls**: Universally recognized as the biggest gamble. Some have fallen victim to a roadside iced Americano, only experiencing issues after returning to Taiwan. While some eat grilled corn or noodles from stalls without issues, high-risk groups should steer clear.
- **Water in five-star hotels**: A netizen shared that the bathwater in a five-star hotel had black particles, prompting them to use bottled water for brushing teeth throughout their stay. **Star ratings don't guarantee water filtration quality**.

### 🧳 Essential Travel Kit Checklist (Crowdsourced Version)

- ✅ **Before and during your trip**: Take **probiotics** on an empty stomach every morning
- ✅ **Brushing teeth and rinsing mouth**: Mineral water is the safest option
- ✅ **Showering**: Bring a filtered showerhead (especially if staying in Ubud/guesthouses)
- ✅ **Drinks**: Ice in restaurants is fine, skip roadside stalls; canned drinks are safest
- ✅ **Stomach medicine**: Bring from Taiwan (e.g., Zhang Guozhou stomach powder, anti-diarrheal medicine...)
- ✅ **If you get sick**: [**Check out this quick self-help guide**](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2026-01-02-69523427fd89780001caa652/)

### ✍️ My Summary

**Bali's water quality issue has no standard answer - it all depends on your stomach.**
If you're prone to worry or traveling with family, **bring an extra filter, use bottled water for brushing teeth, and avoid street food** to ensure peace of mind throughout your trip.
If you have an iron stomach and a carefree attitude, relax and enjoy - most people don't encounter problems.
**The key is: pack some stomach medicine, then fully enjoy Bali!** 🌴
