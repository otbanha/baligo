Buying souvenirs during an overseas trip is a way to make those precious memories last forever. When you visit the beautiful island of Bali, not only can you enjoy stunning beaches and unique culture, but you can also bring back a variety of souvenirs to share this unforgettable journey with family and friends.

## Bali Cultural Handicrafts

Bali's handicrafts are renowned worldwide for their exquisite craftsmanship and unique designs. These handicrafts include:

- **Wood Carvings**: Wood carving is one of Bali's representative crafts. These carvings range from deities and animals to everyday items, all filled with intricate details and vitality, making them excellent choices for home decor.
- **Stone Carvings**: Similar to wood carvings, Bali's stone carvings also showcase the artisans' exceptional skills. You can find these beautiful pieces in temples, gardens, or indoor decorations.

![砂岩雕刻的迷你石雕](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_edcff6e24f592177be3b581f0d4042eb.png)

Miniature stone carvings made from sandstone

- **Textiles**: Bali's textiles are famous for their vibrant colors and traditional patterns. Whether it's sarongs, scarves, or tablecloths, they all let you experience the local charm.
- **Handicrafts**: Such as bracelets, necklaces, and other handwoven accessories.
- **Incense**: Bring the scent of Bali back home. For detailed information, check [here](/blog/2025-07-23-6880c98dfd89780001510ee2/).
- 👜 **Bali Rattan Bags**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0da8524d97ec3c0a0ab9714a64bb7364.png)

These handmade rattan bags are known for their simple, natural, and design-savvy appeal. Whether it's a small shoulder bag or a large tote, they are highly practical. Prices start from **Rp 50,000, with basic models around Rp 150,000, and larger or more intricately designed styles reaching up to Rp 500,000**, making them a popular choice for both gifting and personal use.

## Local Delicacies

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_08d17f1959db86dd24612c06e5c7ebca.png)

If you're a food enthusiast, Bali's specialty foods are a must-try. Bringing home some local delicacies not only extends the joy of your trip but also allows you to share the pleasure with friends and family.

- **Coffee**: Bali's coffee is renowned for its unique flavor and high quality. Particularly, coffee from Kintamani carries a subtle fruity aroma, beloved by tourists. Additionally, Luwak coffee is a famous product of Bali, available in various brands at almost any supermarket. When purchasing, be cautious as there's a high chance of encountering counterfeit products given the scale and rarity of production.
- **Spices**: Bali offers a wide variety of spices, from chili, white pepper, bay leaves, turmeric to cloves. These spices not only enhance the flavor of cooking but also serve as practical souvenirs.

> Further reading: [Bali's Spice Culinary Journey](https://youtu.be/TOKdzLYqbo0)

### Bali Banana Cake (Available at specialty stores)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_89fb934188f4958e5800573a89e936ff.jpg)

[Provided by Xiao K Man Man] In Indonesia, the deliciousness of traditional pastries is well-known. Recently, a special pastry—Bali Banana Cake—has been strongly recommended on Xiaohongshu. With its unique flavor and excellent texture, this cake has quickly become a favorite among tourists and locals alike.

Bali Banana offers a variety of flavors for you to choose from based on your preferences, or you can try them all:

1. Chocolate Banana: A perfect blend of rich chocolate and sweet banana, delivering a decadent taste.
2. Original Banana: A classic banana flavor, pure and natural, leaving you craving more.
3. Blueberry Cheese: A unique flavor experience combining the tangy sweetness of blueberries with the richness of cheese.
4. Milky Cheese: Overflowing with creamy aroma and rich cheese flavor, perfect for dairy lovers.

While buying souvenirs in Bali can sometimes be overwhelming, Bali Banana is undoubtedly a satisfying choice. I once searched for this cake at the airport but found that the international terminal didn’t carry it. I finally managed to buy it in the domestic terminal. If you have enough time, I recommend purchasing it at the Kuta branch to avoid the hassle at the airport.

Bali Banana cakes are soft, smooth, and infused with a natural aroma that makes it hard to stop at just one bite. The cake isn’t overly greasy or sweet, perfectly balancing texture and flavor—it’s truly a 'must-try' treat.

### Salak (Snake Fruit) (Available at supermarkets and fruit stalls)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5246f0141cf9c98d5f703e5b8a69248c.png)

[Lam Tak Wai] Brought a lot back to Hong Kong! Among Bali’s many unique souvenirs, Salak (pronounced 'Sah-lak' in Indonesian) is a distinctive and must-try option. Named for its snake-like scaly skin, Salak has a sweet and juicy flesh that’s loved by both tourists and locals.

The best variety is Salak Gula Pasir (Sugar Snake Fruit), which is exceptionally sweet with a delicate texture and a hint of honey flavor, making it a favorite for many.

Not only is Salak delicious, but it’s also rich in vitamins and antioxidants, helping to boost immunity and aid digestion. Its convenient packaging makes it easy to carry, making it a healthy and tasty gift that allows friends and family to experience Bali’s unique charm.

On your next trip to Bali, be sure to bring some Salak Gula Pasir to share the tropical sweetness with loved ones, adding an extra layer of joy to your travel memories.

**PS. Fresh fruits cannot be brought into Taiwan (dried fruits are allowed)!**

### Bali Prawn Roll (Available at airport duty-free shops or roadside stalls)

![左圖：國際機場免稅店購入；右圖：一般市面上的包裝](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e0757a52b3124d5feb27bfb33aeed06d.png)

[Provided by Fong Fong] Bali Prawn Roll, also known as Sumpia Udang, is a must-try delicacy. This snack is beloved by both tourists and locals for its unique flavor and crispy texture.

The main ingredient of Bali Prawn Roll is fresh shrimp meat, which is carefully processed, mixed with spices and seasonings, then wrapped in thin wonton skins and fried. Each bite offers a rich shrimp flavor accompanied by the crispy texture of the wonton skin, making it an instant favorite.

This snack is not only delicious but also highly portable, making it perfect for enjoying on the go. Bali Prawn Roll is often brought home as a souvenir to share with family and friends, allowing them to savor the unique flavors of Bali. Fong Fong purchased it at the airport duty-free shop and brought it back home, where everyone who received it as a gift couldn't stop praising it!

### Pia Agung (Specialty shop near the airport in Canggu)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3917493dbcbee0bb3a0c799f6c1a57ae.png)

[Provided by Anna Wei] Pia Agung is a Balinese pastry that originated from Chinese mooncakes in Guangdong. Over the years, it has evolved and incorporated local flavors, becoming a popular dessert in Bali today. Loved by both locals and tourists, Pia Agung is a must-have during festivals and a top choice for souvenirs.

Pia Agung comes in a variety of flavors to cater to different preferences. Classic flavors include mung bean, cheese, chocolate cheese, chocolate, milk cheese, tiramisu, cappuccino, and durian. These flavors retain the traditional essence of mooncakes while incorporating Indonesian ingredients, offering delightful surprises with every bite.

These pastries feature a crispy outer layer and a sweet, rich filling that melts in your mouth, making them irresistible. Whether paired with a cup of coffee or enjoyed as an afternoon snack, Pia Agung is an excellent choice.

### **Chocolate Monggo**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a91fa60eb71f580eac358c12a830f694.jpg)

**Chocolate Monggo** is a **Belgian Artisan Chocolatier** based in Bali. The founder, originally a Belgian engineer, moved to Bali and started creating island-inspired chocolates using local spices and organic cocoa.

**Features:**

- Made with premium local Indonesian cocoa beans
- Bean-to-bar artisanal chocolate
- Variety of flavors: pure cocoa, dark chocolate with mango, etc.
- Beautifully packaged, perfect for gifts

**Quality:** Clearly labeled cocoa percentage, a mid-to-high-end chocolate brand, one of Bali's most popular chocolate souvenirs.

**Where to buy:** The Ubud main store has the most complete selection with a glass workshop to observe the production process; also available in Seminyak and Kuta.

**Personal recommendation:** If you prefer less sweet options, try the pure cocoa flavor—you won't be disappointed. Regretted not buying more after returning to Taiwan! (Recommended by [Everything is Myself·Soul Awakening](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/17AMwFcBD5/))
