**Travel Dates: June 28 - July 4**

## Accommodation

We chose two fantastic villas for this trip. It was the first time our whole family stayed in a villa together, and the kids were thrilled and excited about this unique experience.

### Seminyak Stay

We stayed at The Dusun Villa, which boasts an excellent location near Jalan Kayu Aya Street (Editor's Note: What’s special about this street? Check out [Xiao Jie’s introduction](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/oz_QUWvCK_8)).

The pool is clean and spacious, and the villa offers golf cart rental services to Seminyak Square, which is very convenient for families with children. The villa's interior is elegantly designed and comfortable, featuring a spacious living room and outdoor dining area. Children can play freely in the garden while parents enjoy a relaxing time.

### **Ubud Accommodation**

We stayed at Tanah Gajah, a Resort by Hadiprana

Here, you can book a Kecak Dance performance without having to squeeze through crowds. The stay includes a lavish afternoon tea and breakfast. The resort has a relaxed atmosphere, and the staff are friendly and especially kind to children. The entire resort is nestled among rice fields and lotus ponds, offering breathtaking views that make you feel like you're in a paradise.

## Travel Sharing

### **Day 1:**

![金巴蘭（Ya-yun Chen提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_93a92d62cee0cfc04cdeeadfa545bb24.jpg)

Jimbaran (provided by Ya-yun Chen)

- After arriving in Bali, we exchanged currency and headed straight to Jimbaran to enjoy a sunset seafood dinner at Sunshine88 restaurant. The sunset view here was breathtaking, and savoring fresh seafood with the sea breeze made for a wonderful start to our journey. The kids had a blast playing on the beach while the adults enjoyed the perfect combination of delicious food and stunning scenery.

## Tips

- **Pre-trip Tipping Culture Insights:**
- - Daily private driver: IDR 50,000~100,000
  - Luggage: IDR 10,000/50,000 (approx. USD$0.30/1.60)
  - Bedside/Restaurant: IDR 10,000/20,000 (approx. USD$0.30/0.60)
  - Massage: Pre-tip 10%
  - "KEEP THE CHANGE": No need for exact change, as locals often struggle with small denominations below IDR 10,000.

- **Villa Accommodation Suggestion:** If traveling with water-loving kids, strongly consider staying in a Villa. You can arrange with your driver to rent large inflatables from local vendors or book through Klook—your kids will have a blast!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a1dc699ccad5ec44b57b56c8328810f8.jpg)

> Klook [Pool Float Rental Service](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=775806&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Factivity%2F4837-pool-float-rental-bali%2F)

- **Transportation Tip:** July and August are peak seasons, and traffic often gets congested after 11:30 AM. It's recommended to plan no more than 2-3 attractions per day for a fulfilling experience.
- **Nusa Penida Experience:** Although the roads are bumpy, the kids treated it like a bumper car ride, making it especially fun. Since our children are elementary school age, we didn't participate in snorkeling activities, but just a day trip to the west coast was well worth it.

The local culture and charm of Bali are truly captivating. Whether it's the temples, water activities, or natural landscapes, everything has a unique allure. This trip gave us a deeper understanding and appreciation of Bali, and we look forward to visiting again!
