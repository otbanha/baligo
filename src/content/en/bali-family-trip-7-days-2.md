### Bali Family Travel Itinerary & Experience

**Travel Dates: June 28 - July 4**

## Accommodation

We chose two amazing villas for this trip, and it was our first time staying in a villa as a family. The kids were thrilled and excited about this unique accommodation experience.

### Seminyak Accommodation

We stayed at The Dusun Villa, which boasts an excellent location near Jalan Kayu Aya Street (Editor's note: What's special about this street? Check out [Xiao Jie's introduction](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/oz_QUWvCK_8)).

The pool was clean and spacious, and the villa offered a golf cart reservation service to Seminyak Square, which was very convenient for families with children. The villa's interior was elegantly designed and comfortable, featuring a spacious living room and outdoor dining area. The kids could play freely in the garden while the parents enjoyed a relaxing time.

### **Ubud Accommodation**

We stayed at Tanah Gajah, a Resort by Hadiprana

Here, you can book a Kecak Dance performance without having to jostle through crowds. The stay includes a lavish afternoon tea and breakfast. The resort has a relaxed atmosphere with friendly staff who are especially kind to children. The entire resort is nestled among rice fields and lotus ponds, offering breathtaking views that make you feel like you're in a paradise.

## Itinerary Sharing

### **Day 1:**

![金巴蘭（Ya-yun Chen提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_93a92d62cee0cfc04cdeeadfa545bb24.jpg)

Jimbaran (provided by Ya-yun Chen)

- After arriving in Bali, we exchanged currency and headed straight to Jimbaran to enjoy a seafood dinner at sunset. We chose Sunshine88 Restaurant. The sunset view here was breathtaking, and enjoying fresh seafood with the sea breeze made for a perfect start to our trip. The kids had a blast playing on the beach while the adults savored the perfect combination of delicious food and stunning scenery.

### **Day 4:**

![包車一日遊（Ya-yun Chen提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f2a109172a9a3e812e741f773c5dcd9c.jpg)

Private Car Tour (provided by Ya-yun Chen)

- We had a full-day private car tour focusing on temple culture. We visited Pura Tanah Lot (tickets purchased on-site), a temple perched on a rock by the sea, surrounded by water during high tide, creating a spectacular view.
- Next, we headed to the beautiful Jatiluwih Rice Terraces, where the layered green rice fields resembled a stunning painting. We had lunch at Jatiluwih Resto and took a leisurely walk along the terraced paths, soaking in the tranquility and beauty of nature.
- In the afternoon, we visited Ulun Danu Beratan Temple, located in the middle of a lake, offering picturesque scenery perfect for photography.
- We also stopped by Handara Gate, but upon arrival, we were told we'd have to wait an hour for photos, so we decided to skip it.

## Reminders

- **Pre-trip Tipping Culture Insights:**
- - Daily private driver: 50,000~100,000 IDR
  - Luggage: 10,000/50,000 IDR (approx. USD$0.65/1.60)
  - Bedside/Restaurant: 10,000/20,000 IDR (approx. USD$0.65/1.30)
  - Pre-tip 10% for massages
  - "KEEP THE CHANGE": No need for exact change, as locals often struggle with small denominations below 10,000 IDR.

- **Villa Accommodation Suggestion:** If traveling with kids who love water, strongly consider staying in a Villa. You can arrange with your private driver to rent large inflatables from local vendors or book through Klook—your kids will have a blast.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a1dc699ccad5ec44b57b56c8328810f8.jpg)

> Klook [Large Pool Float Rental Service](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=775806&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Factivity%2F4837-pool-float-rental-bali%2F)

- **Transportation Tip:** July and August are peak seasons, and traffic often gets congested after 11:30 AM. It's recommended to plan no more than 2-3 attractions per day for a fulfilling experience.
- **Nusa Penida Experience:** Although the roads are bumpy, the kids found it fun, treating it like a bumper car ride. Since our children are all elementary school age, we didn’t join any snorkeling tours, but the West Coast day trip alone was well worth it.

The local culture and charm of Bali are truly captivating. Whether it’s the temples, water activities, or natural landscapes, everything has its unique allure. This trip deepened our understanding and love for Bali, and we look forward to visiting again!
