## **What is All Indonesia? A New Concept in One-Stop Entry Systems**

On July 24, 2025, the Indonesian government officially launched a trial of the "All Indonesia" system at Soekarno-Hatta International Airport in Jakarta. This system is an integrated digital customs platform that allows incoming travelers to complete customs declarations, health declarations, and other procedures in advance.

![系統有印尼文/英文/中文（簡體）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8cabc11e49da2d9a08d43081608493cc.png)

The system is available in Indonesian/English/Chinese (Simplified)

Previously, these processes required filling out separate forms, often leading to repetitive tasks and time-consuming issues for travelers. All Indonesia consolidates these processes into a single platform, allowing pre-filling up to three days before arrival, with an average completion time of just 2.5 minutes.

---

## **Streamlining Processes, Enhancing Efficiency, Boosting Tourism and Investment**

According to Agus Harimurti Yudhoyono (AHY), Minister of Infrastructure and Regional Development Coordination, this system not only simplifies entry but also aims to attract more international tourists and foreign investments.

> "We aim to provide a highly efficient and internationally standard entry experience while maintaining Indonesia's national security," he emphasized during the press conference.

Additionally, the government encourages public participation in the testing phase, using feedback to continuously optimize the system, and plans to conduct a series of educational and promotional activities before nationwide implementation.

---

## **Information Security and High Traffic Handling Become Core Focus**

Regarding security measures, Minister of Immigration and Corrections Agus Andrianto stated that All Indonesia has undergone penetration testing and stress testing by ITSA BSSN and external agencies.

The system utilizes Sclron technology to prevent data duplication, while also preparing backup systems and expanding Indonesian data center capacity to meet future demands from large numbers of travelers.

---

## **Current Trial Scope and Future Applications**

Currently, the system is only available to **Indonesian citizens traveling internationally** (WNI), with Garuda Indonesia serving as the pilot airline.

According to notification from Indonesian government departments, Indonesia will open its borders to international tourists using the new entry declaration platform [All Indonesia] starting **August 18** for arrivals in **Jakarta** and **Bali**.

All passengers are kindly requested to complete their online declaration before boarding and save/screenshot the completed QR CODE on their phones to ensure smoother entry procedures. Thank you for your cooperation:

👉 <https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/>

In the future, entering Indonesia will be simplified to a single platform for all applications and declarations; after implementing [All Indonesia], there will be no need to separately declare on ECD/SSHP websites. Entry procedures for Bali will be streamlined from [the original four steps](/blog/2025-01-11-67821654fd89780001f932b5/) to three.

## **5 Frequently Asked Questions FAQ**

### Is All Indonesia Mandatory?

Currently, during the trial period, it is only available for **specific flights for Indonesian citizens**. It is expected to be universally applicable to all international flights once officially launched.

### Do I Need to Complete the Declaration Before Arrival?

Yes, travelers can complete the online declaration up to three days before arriving in Indonesia to expedite the entry process.

### Does the System Support Mobile Devices?

Yes, travelers can fill out the declaration form on the official website through their mobile browser.

### What If I Encounter a System Error?

The system has built-in backup mechanisms and provides customer support to promptly assist travelers with technical issues. For any questions related to filling out the declaration form, please refer to [our FAQ](/blog/2025-08-14-689db40ffd89780001eb2f3e/).

### Is the Tourism Tax Not Integrated?

No. Because the tourism tax is only collected in Bali and does not apply nationwide.
