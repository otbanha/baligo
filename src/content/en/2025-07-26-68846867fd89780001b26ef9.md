## **What is All Indonesia? A New Concept in One-Stop Entry Systems**

On July 24, 2025, the Indonesian government officially launched a trial of the "All Indonesia" system at Jakarta's Soekarno-Hatta International Airport. This system is an integrated digital customs platform that enables incoming travelers to complete customs declarations, health declarations, and other procedures in advance.

![系統有印尼文/英文/中文（簡體）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8cabc11e49da2d9a08d43081608493cc.png)

The system is available in Indonesian/English/Simplified Chinese

Previously, these processes required filling out separate forms, often leading to repetitive tasks and time-consuming issues for travelers. All Indonesia consolidates these processes into a single platform, allowing pre-filling up to three days before arrival, with an average completion time of just 2.5 minutes.

---

## **Streamlining Processes, Boosting Efficiency, Driving Tourism and Investment**

According to Minister of Infrastructure and Regional Development Coordination Agus Harimurti Yudhoyono (commonly known as AHY), this system not only simplifies entry but also attracts more international tourists and foreign investment.

> "We aim to provide a highly efficient entry experience that meets international standards while maintaining Indonesia's national security," he emphasized during the press conference.

Additionally, the government encourages public participation during the testing phase, using feedback to continuously optimize the system, with plans for a series of educational and promotional activities before nationwide implementation.

---

## **Information Security and High Traffic Handling Become Core Focus**

Regarding security, Minister of Immigration and Corrections Agus Andrianto stated that All Indonesia has undergone penetration testing and stress testing by ITSA BSSN and external agencies.

The system utilizes Sclron technology to prevent data duplication, while also preparing backup systems and expanding Indonesian data center capacity to meet future high-volume traveler demands.

---

## **Current Pilot Participants and Future Application Scope**

Currently, the system is only available to **Indonesian citizens (WNI) traveling on international flights**, with Garuda Indonesia as the pilot airline.

As notified by Indonesian government departments, Indonesia will open its borders to international tourists in **Jakarta** and **Bali** starting **August 18**, using the new entry declaration platform [All Indonesia].

All passengers are kindly requested to complete their online declaration before boarding and save/screenshot the completed declaration QR CODE on their phones to ensure a smoother entry process. Thank you for your cooperation:

👉 <https://allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id/>

In the future, entering Indonesia will be simplified to one platform for all applications and declarations; after the implementation of [All Indonesia], there will be no need to separately declare on ECD/SSHP websites. Entry to Bali will be simplified from [the original four steps](/blog/2025-01-11-67821654fd89780001f932b5/) to three.

## **5 Frequently Asked Questions FAQ**

### **Is All Indonesia Mandatory?**

Currently during the trial period, it is only available for **Indonesian citizens on specific flights**. It is expected to be universally applicable to all international flights once officially launched.

### **Do I Need to Complete the Declaration Before Arrival?**

Yes, travelers can complete the online declaration up to three days before arriving in Indonesia to expedite the immigration process.

### **Does the System Support Mobile Devices?**

Yes, travelers can fill out the declaration form on the official website using their mobile browser.

### **What If I Encounter a System Error?**

The system has built-in redundancy mechanisms and provides customer support to promptly assist with any technical issues travelers may encounter. For questions related to filling out the declaration form, please refer to [our FAQ](/blog/2025-08-14-689db40ffd89780001eb2f3e/).

### **Is the Tourism Tax Integrated?**

No. Because the tourism tax is only collected in Bali and does not apply nationwide.
