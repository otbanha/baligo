### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: New Tourism Infrastructure and Special Economic Zone Transformation

- **Major Breakthrough in Northern Mountains! Mount Batur Cable Car Project Officially Approved**:
To revitalize tourism in central and northern Bali, the provincial government officially approved a landmark tourism infrastructure project on May 20th—a new cable car route will be built at the summit of **Mount Batur in Kintamani**. Officials stated that this project will not only significantly improve transportation connectivity in northern and central Bali but also aims to create a world-class tourist attraction. In the future, visitors will be able to enjoy panoramic views of the volcanic landscape and Lake Batur from the comfort of small cabins.
- **Beyond Tourism! Kura Kura Bali SEZ Transforms into a Regional Financial Hub**:
On May 20th, the Indonesian Ministry of Finance and the central government unveiled an ambitious strategic blueprint to transform the **Kura Kura Bali Special Economic Zone (SEZ)** into a major international financial hub. The SEZ will focus on sustainable finance, digital financial services, cross-border capital flows, and investment management. Officials emphasized the introduction of modern international regulatory frameworks, enhanced legal certainty, and transparency, leveraging Bali's global reputation to attract financial institutions worldwide. This initiative aims to open up new high-net-worth business opportunities beyond traditional tourism.
- **New Benchmark in Sports Tourism: Inaugural Bali Tourism Run to Debut in June**:
The first-ever **Bali Tourism Run 2026**, jointly organized by the Bali Tourism Board and the Indonesian Travel Agents Association (ASITA), has been officially announced to take place on **June 21st** at the UNESCO World Heritage Site—the **Jatiluwih Rice Terraces**. To protect the local ecological and cultural landscape, the event is strictly limited to 2,000 participants. Organizers also announced that the **Jatiluwih Festival** will be held on June 20th, combining sports, traditional village life, and local cuisine to promote low-density, high-quality sustainable tourism.

### 📄 Topics of Interest for Visitors: Crowd Alerts and Luxury Accommodation Recovery

- **International Sports Event This Week: Red Bull Cliff Diving World Series**:
The globally anticipated **Red Bull Cliff Diving World Series** will take place this weekend across the island. Sports tourism expert Nyoman Sujantara reminded officials and visitors that while Bali is well-prepared in terms of natural branding, large-scale events heavily rely on the rapid movement of athletes, officials, and spectators. Visitors heading to popular coastal destinations are advised to depart early and be mindful of potential traffic congestion around event areas.
- **Revitalization of Nusa Dua Peninsula and Return of Luxury Resorts**:
In preparation for upcoming international conferences and business events, Nusa Dua Special Zone General Manager I Made Agus Dwiatmika announced comprehensive landscape beautification and public facility upgrades in the **Peninsula Island** area to reinforce its position as a world-class high-quality tourism destination. Meanwhile, a renowned all-inclusive luxury resort favored by families and high-end travelers recently completed renovations and reopened, signaling a strong recovery in the southern zone's luxury tourism market.
