Starting February 2024, visitors to Bali will be required to pay a $10 (or 150,000 IDR) tourism tax in addition to visa fees. However, many travelers have failed to comply with this regulation, prompting local authorities to consider penalties for violators. One proposed measure is to deny entry to tourists who have not paid the tourism tax.

---

Challenges and Enforcement Issues with Bali's Tourism Tax System

In 2023, the Bali government introduced Local Regulation No. 6, requiring foreign tourists to pay a tourism tax, which can be paid online in advance. However, only about 40% of travelers have fulfilled this obligation, while the remaining 60% have evaded payment.

Main Reasons:

1. Lack of Clear Reminders: Apart from a few banners, there are almost no other prompts in the airport arrival area guiding tourists to pay the tax.
2. Airport Does Not Check Tax QR Codes: Customs or airport staff currently do not require tourists to show their QR codes, allowing them to pass through checks without any hindrance.

Currently, only 300 billion IDR in tourism tax is collected annually, highlighting significant loopholes in the enforcement process.

---

Government Plans to Strengthen Enforcement

The Bali Provincial Government and the Regional Representative Council (DPRD) plan to revise tourism tax regulations, focusing on:

1. **Defining Specific Sanctions**:
2. - The specific terms of the travel ban still require further research to ensure transparency and fairness in implementation.
3. **Improving the Inspection System**:
4. - Plans are in place to conduct QR code checks at airports, but measures must be taken to avoid long queues at entry points.
   - Currently, some tourist attractions already require visitors to present QR codes, and this model may be expanded.
5. **Excluding Hotel Tax Collection**:
6. - Since many foreign tourists opt for villas or non-hotel accommodations, relying on hotels to collect tourism taxes is not feasible.

### Tjokorda Bagus Pemayun, Head of the Bali Tourism Board, stated: "The system design must be perfected to ensure the comprehensive implementation of the tourism tax."

---

## Future Plans

Given the current situation, the government has no immediate plans to increase the tourism tax. The focus will be on improving the collection and enforcement systems. In 2025, the Bali Regional Representative Council and the provincial government will discuss amendments to relevant local regulations during a plenary session, aiming to effectively address existing issues and enhance tax compliance rates.

Additionally, authorities hope to strengthen publicity and education efforts to ensure tourists understand the importance of paying the tourism tax.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

### **1. Why is the Bali Tourism Tax Necessary?**

The Bali Tourism Tax aims to support local infrastructure and improve tourism-related facilities, ensuring sustainable tourism development.

### **2. How to Pay the Bali Tourism Tax?**

Travelers can pay in advance through an online platform and obtain a QR code as proof of payment. Some tourist attractions also require the presentation of this QR code.

### 3. What are the consequences of not paying the tourism tax?

The government is considering implementing travel bans or other sanctions for those who fail to pay, but specific measures are still under study.

### 4. Will hotels assist in paying the tourism tax?

There is currently no such arrangement, and it is recommended that travelers complete the payment on online platforms.

### 5. How will the tourism tax funds be used?

As of now, detailed usage of the funds has not been announced, but the tax is expected to be used for enhancing tourism infrastructure and environmental protection measures.
