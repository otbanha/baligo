### Starting February 2024, visitors to Bali will be required to pay a $10 (or 150,000 IDR) tourist tax in addition to visa fees. However, many travelers have failed to comply with this regulation, prompting local authorities to discuss potential penalties for violators. One proposed measure is to ban tourists who haven’t paid the tax from entering Bali.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_858d130a6da34f4a9f0e9e9766d18106.jpg)

---

## Challenges and Enforcement Issues with Bali’s Tourist Tax System

Bali’s government introduced Local Regulation No. 6 in 2023, requiring foreign tourists to pay a tourist tax, which can be paid online in advance. However, only about 40% of travelers have fulfilled this obligation, while the remaining 60% have evaded payment.

### **Main Reasons:**

1. **Lack of Clear Reminders**: Aside from a few banners, there are almost no other prompts in the airport arrival area guiding tourists to pay the tax.
2. **Airport Not Checking Tax QR Codes**: Customs or airport staff currently do not require travelers to show their QR codes, allowing them to pass through checks without any obstacles.

Currently, only 300 billion IDR in tourist taxes is collected annually, highlighting significant loopholes in the enforcement process.

---

## Government Plans to Strengthen Enforcement

The Bali Provincial Government and the Regional Representative Council (DPRD) plan to revise the tourist tax regulations, focusing on:

1. **Define Specific Sanction Measures**:
2. - The specific terms of the travel ban require further study to ensure transparency and fairness in implementation.
3. **Improve Inspection Systems**:
4. - Plans are in place to implement QR code checks at airports, but measures must ensure they do not cause long queues at entry points.
   - Some tourist attractions already require visitors to present QR codes, and this model may be expanded.
5. **Exclude Hotel Tax Collection Plan**:
6. - Since many foreign tourists choose to stay in villas or other non-hotel accommodations, relying on hotels to collect tourism taxes is not feasible.

### Bali Tourism Agency Head Tjokorda Bagus Pemayun stated: "The system design must be perfected to ensure the comprehensive implementation of the tourism tax."

---

## Future Plans

Based on the current situation, the government has no immediate plans to increase the tourism tax. The focus will be on improving the collection and enforcement systems. In 2025, the Bali Regional Representative Council and the provincial government will discuss amendments to local regulations during a plenary session, aiming to effectively address existing issues and improve tax compliance rates.

Additionally, authorities hope to enhance awareness and education to ensure tourists understand the importance of paying the tourism tax.

## FAQ

### **1. Why is it necessary to pay the Bali tourism tax?**

The Bali tourism tax aims to support local infrastructure and improve tourism-related facilities, ensuring sustainable tourism development.

### **2. How can I pay the Bali tourism tax?**

Travelers can pay in advance through an online platform and receive a QR code as proof of payment. Some tourist attractions also require this QR code to be presented.

### **3. What are the consequences of not paying the tourism tax?**

The government is considering implementing travel bans or other sanctions for those who fail to pay, but specific measures are still under study.

### **4. Will hotels assist in paying the tourism tax?**

There is currently no such arrangement, and it is recommended that travelers complete the payment through online platforms.

### **5. How will the tourism tax funds be used?**

As of now, detailed usage of the funds has not been announced, but the tax is expected to be used for improving tourism infrastructure and environmental protection measures.
