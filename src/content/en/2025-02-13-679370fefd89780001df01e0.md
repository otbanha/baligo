## Transportation

Transportation was one of my top concerns during this trip to Bali with my mother. Since many warned that taxi drivers in Bali often overcharge tourists, I decided to use Grab for rides. My previous experience with Grab in Thailand was excellent, and Bali was no exception. However, the situation at Bali's airport was chaotic. As soon as we stepped out, we were swarmed by taxi drivers aggressively offering their services, even claiming to be Grab drivers and promising cheaper rates. This was particularly frustrating when traveling with an elderly parent.

My advice is to head left immediately after exiting the airport, where you'll find a small parking lot where Grab drivers usually wait for passengers. This helps avoid harassment from other taxi drivers and speeds up locating your ride. However, some drivers may still approach you during the wait—simply tell them firmly that you've already booked a ride.

During our stay in Bali, we relied almost entirely on Grab for transportation. Grab's advantage is its abundance of vehicles; you can usually get a ride within three minutes, whether at a tourist spot or a restaurant. Most drivers speak decent English and are friendly, and fares are strictly based on the app's pricing, eliminating any risk of being overcharged. The only minor issue is the scarcity of small change in Bali. Sometimes, we didn't have enough small bills and simply told the driver to keep the change, which was usually just a few cents. The drivers were always grateful.

That said, Bali's traffic can be a headache 🤣. There's no clear distinction between fast and slow lanes, and motorcycles weave through cars unpredictably. Watching motorcycles dart through tight spaces can make you feel like a collision is imminent. If you're brave, you might try Grab's motorcycle service, especially during peak hours when car traffic can be unbearable. Once, we traveled 20 kilometers between two locations, and it took a full two hours—both amusing and frustrating.

Overall, Grab is a reliable transportation option in Bali, especially for travelers with elderly family members. It's convenient and safe. Just remember that Bali's traffic can be chaotic, so exercise extra caution, particularly when crossing streets, as motorcycles and cars move differently than in Taiwan.

## Private Car Hire

During our Bali trip, hiring a private car was one of the most hassle-free and convenient transportation options we chose. We found our driver through recommendations in an online community, and the reviews were excellent. After experiencing it firsthand, I can confidently say it was worth recommending. Here are the detailed insights from our private car hire experience for your reference.

### Booking and Communication:

We communicated with the driver via Line, and the process was smooth. First, we informed him about our desired itinerary and the duration of the car hire. Based on our needs, he provided suggestions, listed the total cost, and detailed the itinerary sequence. Once confirmed, he reached out the day before to ensure there were no changes to the plan. The best part was that payment was made only after completing the day's itinerary, eliminating any concerns about prepayment.

### Itinerary and Cost:

Our main goal for hiring the car was to explore the Ubud area, and we also asked the driver to drop us off at another hotel afterward. The total car hire duration was 10 hours, costing approximately USD$39, which I found very reasonable. Additionally, we asked the driver to help arrange activities like ATV rides, river tubing, and swings. He included the costs of these activities in the total price, which we confirmed beforehand. Payment for everything was made in one go after all activities were completed, making it very convenient.

### Driver's Recommendation:

During our private car tour, the driver casually asked if we'd like to try Kopi Luwak (civet coffee), emphasizing it's a specialty of Bali and only costs USD$3 per cup, making it well worth a try. Intrigued by the affordable price and our curiosity, we agreed. The driver then took us to a specialized Kopi Luwak plantation.

### Plantation Tour and Tasting:

Upon entering the plantation, the staff enthusiastically explained the process of making Kopi Luwak, highlighting how their coffee is natural, organic, and 'naturally processed' by civets. They guided us through the plantation, showing the relationship between civets (actually palm civets) and coffee beans, before leading us to the tasting area.

The tasting experience was quite interesting. In addition to Kopi Luwak, they offered a variety of other coffees and herbal teas for sampling. Each beverage was accompanied by detailed explanations about its flavor profile or health benefits. After completing the tasting session, it indeed felt a bit awkward not to purchase something.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b47855e8d20e3f0b3936512c44f670a2.jpg)

### Controversy Surrounding Kopi Luwak:

However, while tasting, I recalled the controversies surrounding Kopi Luwak online. Many reports indicate that these coffees aren't entirely naturally produced; numerous civets are kept in cages and force-fed coffee beans, which isn't animal-friendly. Additionally, Kopi Luwak is extremely expensive. Although the tasting cup was only USD$3, the actual purchase price might deter many.

### Purchase Pressure and Actual Experience:

During the tasting, the staff enthusiastically promoted their products right in front of us, creating a somewhat pressured atmosphere. We ended up buying a small packet of mangosteen tea for about USD$13, which wasn't too expensive but not exactly cheap either. As for the Kopi Luwak, it did have a subtle sweetness and smooth texture, but it didn't strike me as a 'must-buy.' If you're not particularly passionate about coffee or uninterested in such commercialized tours, you can simply tell your private driver to skip this activity, saving both time and money.

### Experience Summary:

The civet coffee experience is considered a signature activity in Bali, but the hidden animal welfare issues and commercialized approach can be off-putting. If you're not particularly interested in coffee or don't want to spend too much time on shopping-oriented activities, it's recommended to communicate with your driver in advance to skip this part. After all, Bali has many more worthwhile sights and activities to explore. Time is precious, so why not save it for creating even more beautiful memories!

## Food

Food was definitely one of the highlights that left a lasting impression on me during this Bali trip! Bali's culinary culture is incredibly diverse, especially the local specialties, which are truly unforgettable. Below is a detailed sharing of my dining experiences, hoping to provide some references for everyone.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d7966ba428f0a4c7761ad02df03437bc.jpg)

### Dirty Duck Rice and Pork Ribs:

First, I highly recommend Bali's 'Dirty Duck Rice' and 'Pork Ribs'. Dirty Duck Rice is a traditional Balinese dish where the duck is marinated with spices for a long time and then cooked until tender. Paired with the local spiced rice, it offers a rich and layered flavor that keeps you coming back for more. As for the Pork Ribs, they are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, with a sweet and savory sauce that's not greasy at all, making it perfect for meat lovers.

Interestingly, our driver told us that although the majority of Indonesia's population is Muslim and avoids pork, over 85% of Bali's population practices Hinduism, so you can find many pork dishes here, which is quite rare in other parts of Indonesia. Additionally, Bali's lifestyle is relatively free, unlike Islamic regions where there are many dress or dietary restrictions, making our trip even more relaxed and enjoyable. The driver also mentioned that schools in Bali finish at noon, which made me feel that the pace of life here is really leisurely—it sounds amazing 🤣.

### Traditional Indonesian Cuisine:

While Dirty Duck Rice and Pork Ribs amazed us, traditional Indonesian dishes didn't quite hit the mark for our taste buds. We even signed up for a cooking class, spending a morning learning to make six or seven Indonesian dishes. There were also a few Korean tourists in the class, and after tasting, everyone found the dishes too salty and oily, ending up only taking a bite or two before leaving the rest untouched. However, the process of participating in the cooking class was still fun, as it was a unique travel experience to personally engage in the making of local cuisine.

## Currency Exchange

When traveling in Bali, currency exchange is an aspect that requires special attention, especially since the local currency amounts are very large, and it's easy to get confused. Below are my currency exchange experiences from this trip, hoping to help everyone handle money matters more smoothly.

### Bringing USD is Most Convenient:

It is recommended to ****exchange USD in Taiwan first, then convert it to Indonesian Rupiah in Bali****. This is because the exchange rate for USD in Bali is relatively stable, and all currency exchange offices accept USD. This time, we brought USD to Bali and paid for the visa-on-arrival fee directly with USD at the airport, asking the staff to give change in Indonesian Rupiah, so we had some local currency right after landing.

### Airport vs. City Currency Exchange:

Although there are currency exchange offices at the airport, the rates are usually not as favorable as those in the city. Therefore, we only exchanged a small amount of Indonesian Rupiah to cover transportation costs from the airport to the city, and exchanged the rest at reputable currency exchange offices in the city.

### Amount Notation and Conversion Tips:

The denominations of Indonesian Rupiah are very large, often reaching hundreds of thousands or even millions, which might be confusing for first-time visitors. Locals commonly use "K" to represent the last three zeros in the amount, for example, 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah is written as 50K. The simplest way to convert between Indonesian Rupiah and USD is to multiply the number before "K" by 2, then add a little more, to get an approximate amount in USD. For example, 50K Indonesian Rupiah is roughly a little over USD$3, making calculations very convenient and helping to avoid mistakes when shopping or paying.

### Precautions:

When exchanging money, always choose reputable currency exchange offices and avoid street-side stalls to prevent counterfeit bills or unfair exchange rates. Additionally, it's best to count the money on the spot and confirm the amount before leaving. Some exchange offices may charge fees, so it's advisable to clarify this before proceeding.

## Accommodation

This Bali accommodation experience made me deeply realize the importance of choosing the right location and surrounding environment. Below are some insights from my stay, hoping to help you make more suitable choices when planning your trip.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a547a653ea0227a0f9908d938d74da36.jpg)

### Mosquito Issue:

First, I must remind everyone that Bali has a lot of mosquitoes, and they are particularly large! This might be due to the humid climate and the fact that hotels and villas often plant many flowers and trees to create a tropical atmosphere, which unfortunately also becomes a breeding ground for mosquitoes. Even in higher-end villas, mosquitoes can still find their way inside. Therefore, **mosquito repellent is an absolute must-have item**. I recommend carrying it with you at all times, especially during dusk and dawn when mosquitoes are most active.

*****Quick Reference:*****

[What Essential Medicines Should You Bring for an Indonesia/Bali Trip? How to Buy Mosquito Repellent/Sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/)

### Breakfast Service:

Although the mosquito issue was somewhat bothersome, the hotels and guesthouses we stayed at performed exceptionally well in terms of breakfast. The breakfast options were diverse, ranging from local specialties to Western-style breakfasts, and the quality was consistently high. I believe breakfast is crucial when staying in Bali because the areas around our accommodations had almost no convenience stores or food stalls. If breakfast wasn't satisfying, we would have to wait until lunchtime to find something to eat. Therefore, I suggest paying special attention to whether breakfast is provided when choosing accommodations, as it can make your trip much more convenient.

### Choosing the Right Location:

The biggest mistake in our accommodation choice was staying in a place far from the bustling areas, with almost no shops or restaurants nearby. Every time we went out, we had to call for a ride, which was very inconvenient. Bali's attractions are scattered, and transportation isn't very convenient. If your accommodation isn't in a bustling area, the daily transportation costs can add up significantly. Each time we called for a ride, it cost us around USD$40, and once we were out, it was impossible to return to fetch something or rest, which somewhat limited our itinerary planning.

Therefore, ****it's highly recommended to consider the convenience of the surrounding area when choosing accommodation****. If you enjoy a lively atmosphere, you might want to stay in bustling areas like Kuta, Seminyak, or Ubud, where there are plenty of restaurants, shops, and evening entertainment options. If you opt for a more secluded villa or resort, be prepared to rely on Grab delivery or private car services for your daily needs.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0391757a9b3ffecaea7406bc888c42e0.jpg)

### Accommodation Insights:

While Bali's accommodation options are stunning, mosquito issues and location choices are important factors to consider. Make sure to bring plenty of mosquito repellent and think about the convenience of your surroundings when selecting a place to stay. Staying in remote areas might increase transportation costs and inconvenience, so keep this in mind when planning your trip. We hope these tips help you find the perfect accommodation in Bali, making your journey even more enjoyable!
