## Bali's Firefly Garden Wins Eco-Tourism Award

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e0baadbdee967562986d7a819549be8b.jpg)

Located in Taro Village, Bali, the Firefly Garden, also known as the 'Firefly Conservation Home,' recently won an eco-tourism award during Indonesia's 80th Independence Day celebrations. Founded by local residents, this attraction has earned high praise for its environmental conservation and community involvement.

***

## A Model of Conservation and Tourism

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3d7022976ea005fa26f775014ebf0974.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8d0cf183d7ade2531fe06e968399fcc4.jpg)

Fireflies are considered bioindicators of environmental health, being highly sensitive to pollution. The garden features a small laboratory dedicated to studying firefly reproduction, life cycles, and behavior, while promoting organic farming to reduce chemical pesticide use. Manager Wayan Wardika aims to expand the fireflies' activity radius to 2 kilometers through collaboration with local farmers, requiring 27 hectares of farmland to achieve this goal.

***

## Community and Nature in Harmony

Co-founder Wayan Gede Ardika shares that the conservation effort began with experiments in organic farming. The use of natural fertilizers improved soil and water quality, inadvertently attracting fireflies and evolving into an eco-tourism site. This initiative has not only boosted community income but also provided healthier food sources for locals.

## A Look at Indonesia's Eco-Innovation Award Winners

In addition to the Firefly Garden, other award-winning projects include:

- **Best Innovation Award**: Led by Komang Wiratama Putra, the 'Raja Prani Farmers Group' focuses on improving cultivation methods for high-value onion crops.
- **Marine and Fisheries Award**: Ketut Adi Putra's invention, the 'Jukung Sky Boat', helps local fishermen improve fishing efficiency.

### 1. Where is the Bali Firefly Garden located?

Located in Taro Village near Ubud, it's an eco-tourism site managed by locals and not far from the city center.

### 2. Why is firefly conservation important?

Fireflies are highly sensitive to pollution, and their presence symbolizes the health and balance of the ecosystem.

Main activities include nighttime firefly tours, romantic dinner packages, and organic farm visits.
