The moment the plane's tires gently touched the runway at Ngurah Rai Airport, our six-day Bali adventure officially began. This was not just an escape from daily life, but a meticulously orchestrated sensory feast filled with human warmth. From the spiritual heart of Ubud in the rainforest, to the majestic cliffs of Uluwatu, and finally immersing in the transparent waters of Lembongan Island, we captured the multifaceted beauty of Bali in six days. This travelogue will detail the journey's highlights and share the key to a smooth trip—a trustworthy local guide.

## Arrival in Ubud: A Lesson in Trust Starting at the Airport

At 1:30 PM on December 12th, the tropical sun warmly embraced us as we stepped off the plane. Exiting customs, we immediately spotted the hotel's driver holding a sign with our names. We had previously requested currency exchange through the hotel, and the driver was well-prepared. As we drove away from the airport, his first words were not small talk but valuable local advice: 'You'll see many currency exchange places along the way. Remember, those with exceptionally good rates are best avoided.' His sincere eyes and simple English explanation instantly eased our unease in a foreign land. Following his recommendation, we successfully exchanged currency at a modest shop frequented by locals. The 90-minute drive to Ubud passed quickly with casual conversation and our first glimpses of the scenic route. The pre-agreed fare of USD$26 was hassle-free, setting a reassuring tone for the journey.

We stayed at **Kubu Tropis Hotel**. Upon arrival, the genuine smiles of the staff and the refreshing cold towels immediately washed away the fatigue from our flight. Though the hotel isn’t large, every detail reflects thoughtfulness: we once happened to witness staff meticulously scrubbing the pool, which explains why the water is always crystal clear. The room is infused with a relaxing essential oil fragrance, and the open wardrobe and dedicated clothesline on the balcony are incredibly practical for travelers. The only downside is the fixed showerhead, which rendered our portable filtered showerhead useless. The hotel’s location is excellent, with Ubud Palace just a 15 to 20-minute walk away, and surrounded by restaurants and shops, making evening strolls for food particularly convenient. This hotel became our comfortable base for exploring the Ubud area during our first three days.

## Core Three Days: Professional Depth and Exploration

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_00f2cd7be9f879ad828534427edb9114.jpg)

The next three days were the highlight of the trip. I entrusted the itinerary entirely to the highly recommended guide **Ani (Si Febri)** and her husband, the driver. Her popularity was such that I had to "go against the grain": I contacted her to confirm her availability before even purchasing our flight tickets in Taiwan, akin to booking a slot with a star director.

This early trust paid off in spades. Ani wasn't just a driver, but a professional travel consultant. When I shared my ideal itinerary with her, she carefully evaluated it and offered practical advice: 'Ubud gets very congested in the evening, so we should leave earlier for the sunset viewing.' 'The volcano sunrise and paragliding are very weather-dependent, so let's not rush to book tickets. We can decide on the morning itself, and I can help you purchase them on-site.' She always anticipated blind spots that travelers might miss and patiently explained the reasons. Moreover, she transformed into an itinerary magician, proactively suggesting: 'The road we're taking will pass by Tirta Empul Temple, which is quite convenient. Would you like to stop by?' This flexibility and thoughtfulness added delightful surprises to our travel framework.

Our driver for these three days (arranged by Ani's team) was equally impressive. Due to our adventurous spirit, we exceeded the standard 10-hour car rental limit on two days (with reasonable overtime fees), but the driver always maintained a smile, without complaint, and even voluntarily helped us with our heavy luggage. Their professionalism made us willingly give extra tips at the end of the trip as a direct expression of gratitude.

## Epilogue: The Blue of Nusa Lembongan and the Ease of the Return Journey

As our journey neared its end, we spent a day exploring **Nusa Lembongan**. We purchased round-trip boat tickets in advance on Klook (around USD$13 per person) and deliberately chose different boat companies for a bit of variety. The island's turquoise waters and the roaring waves at 'Devil's Tear' added the final vibrant touch to our Bali photo album.

On our last day, we returned from Nusa Lembongan to **Sanur Port**. Heeding online advice about inconvenient transportation around the port, we contacted Ani again to arrange a one-way transfer to **Beachwalk Shopping Center** in Kuta. This decision proved to be spot-on. The driver was waiting punctually outside the pier, and we even asked him to make a quick stop at Bali Banana for some last-minute shopping. The roughly 40-minute ride cost around USD$19, but the priceless convenience of not having to drag our luggage around made it all worthwhile.

## Conclusion: More Than Just a One-Time Wonder

Six days flew by in the blink of an eye. Looking back on this journey, the beauty of Bali lies not only in its volcanic landscapes, oceans, temples, and rice terraces but also in the people we encountered along the way. The sincerity of the hotel staff, the kindness of our driver, and the professionalism and thoughtfulness of Ani's team wove a warm safety net, allowing us to explore and feel without any worries.

If you ask me what the greatest takeaway was, I would say it was learning the art of 'entrusting.' By handing over our itinerary to a trusted local expert, she was able to use her knowledge and network to transform an ordinary trip into a surprising and in-depth experience. We’ve already started to miss it and are certain this won’t be the last time. Bali, until we meet again; Ani, we’ll be counting on you for our next adventure!
