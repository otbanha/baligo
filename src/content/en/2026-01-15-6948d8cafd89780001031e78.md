The moment the plane's tires gently touched the runway at Ngurah Rai Airport, our six-day Bali adventure officially began. This was not just an escape from daily life, but a meticulously orchestrated sensory feast filled with human warmth. From the spiritual heart of Ubud in the rainforest to the majestic cliffs of Uluwatu, and finally immersing in the transparent waters of Lembongan Island, we collected multiple facets of Bali in six days. This travelogue will detail the itinerary and share the key to a smooth journey—a trustworthy local guide.

## Arrival in Ubud: A Lesson in Trust Starting at the Airport

At 1:30 PM on December 12th, the tropical sun warmly embraced us as we stepped off the plane. Exiting customs, we immediately spotted the hotel's driver holding a sign with our names. We had informed the hotel in advance about needing to exchange currency, and the driver was well-prepared. As we drove away from the airport, his first words weren't small talk but a valuable local tip: 'You'll see many currency exchange places along the way. Remember, don't go into the ones with exceptionally good rates.' His sincere eyes and simple English explanation instantly alleviated our unease in a foreign land. Following his recommendation, we successfully exchanged currency at a modest shop frequented by locals. The ninety-minute drive to Ubud passed quickly with casual conversation and our first glimpses of the scenic route. The pre-agreed USD$26 fare was undisputed, setting a reassuring tone for the trip.

We stayed at **Kubu Tropis Hotel**. Upon arrival, the staff's genuine smiles and cold towels immediately washed away the fatigue of the flight. The hotel, though small, was thoughtfully designed: we once witnessed staff meticulously scrubbing the pool, which explained why the water was always crystal clear. The room was filled with relaxing essential oil scents, and the open wardrobe and balcony clothesline were extremely practical for travelers. The only downside was the fixed showerhead, rendering our portable filtered showerhead useless. The hotel's prime location allowed us to reach Ubud Palace in a fifteen to twenty-minute walk, with numerous restaurants and shops nearby, making evening strolls for food particularly convenient. This hotel became our comfortable base for exploring Ubud for the first three days.

## Core Three Days: Professional Deep Exploration

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_00f2cd7be9f879ad828534427edb9114.jpg)

The next three days were the highlight of this trip. I entrusted my itinerary entirely to **Ani (Si Febri)**, a highly recommended tour guide from online travel communities, and her husband, who served as our private driver. Her popularity was such that I had to 'go against the grain' — I contacted her to confirm her availability before even purchasing my flight tickets from Taiwan, much like booking a slot with a star director.

This early trust paid off immensely. Ani wasn’t just a tour guide; she was a professional itinerary consultant. When I sent her my ideal schedule, she carefully evaluated it and offered practical advice: 'Ubud gets very congested in the evening, so we should leave earlier for the sunset.' 'The volcano sunrise and paragliding are very weather-dependent, so let’s not rush to book tickets. We’ll decide on the morning itself, and I can help you purchase them on-site.' She always anticipated blind spots that travelers might overlook and patiently explained her reasoning. Additionally, she acted as an itinerary magician, suggesting: 'The road you’re taking will pass by Tirta Empul Temple — it’s on the way. Would you like to stop by?' Her flexibility and thoughtfulness added delightful surprises to our trip.

The drivers arranged by Ani's team over these three days were equally commendable. Due to our heightened enthusiasm, we exceeded the standard 10-hour car rental limit on two days (with reasonable overtime fees), yet the drivers remained cheerful and uncomplaining, even voluntarily helping us with our heavy luggage. Their professionalism led us to willingly give extra tips at the end of our journey as a direct expression of our gratitude.

## Epilogue: The Blues of Nusa Lembongan and the Ease of the Return Journey

As our journey drew to a close, we spent a day hopping over to **Nusa Lembongan**. We pre-booked round-trip ferry tickets via Klook (under USD$13 per person) and deliberately chose different ferry companies to add variety to our experience. The island's azure seas and skies, along with the dramatic waves at 'Devil's Tears,' added the final vibrant strokes to our Bali travelogue.

https://youtube.com/shorts/exdiGPLkVjw

On our final day, returning from Nusa Lembongan to **Sanur Port**, we heeded online advice about the inconvenient transport around the port area and contacted Ani again to arrange a one-way transfer to **Beachwalk Shopping Center** in Kuta. This decision proved to be absolutely correct. The driver waited punctually outside the pier, and we even asked him to make a detour to Bali Banana for some last-minute shopping. The approximately 40-minute ride cost around USD$19, but the ease it provided us while dragging our luggage was priceless.

## Conclusion: More Than Just a One-Time Wonder

Six days passed in the blink of an eye. Looking back, the beauty of Bali lies not only in the spectacular landscapes of volcanoes, oceans, temples, and rice terraces but also in the people we met along the way. The sincerity of the hotel staff, the kindness of the drivers, and the professionalism and thoughtfulness of Ani's team collectively wove a warm safety net, allowing us to explore and feel without any worries.

If asked what my greatest takeaway was, I would say it's learning the art of 'entrustment.' By handing over our itinerary to a trustworthy local expert, she was able to use her knowledge and network to transform an ordinary trip into a deeply surprising experience. We have already started to miss it and are certain this won't be our last time. Bali, until we meet again; Ani, we'll be counting on you for our next adventure!
