> The following content is adapted from fellow traveler [Liao Yiwen](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/1CjoK9BEFX/)'s travelogue:

The moment the plane's tires gently touched the runway at Ngurah Rai Airport, our six-day Bali adventure officially began. This was not just an escape from daily life, but a meticulously orchestrated sensory feast filled with human warmth. From the spiritual center of Ubud in the rainforest, to the majestic cliffs of Uluwatu, and finally to the transparent waters of Lembongan Island, we spent six days capturing the multifaceted beauty of Bali. This travelogue will detail the highlights of our itinerary and share the key to a smooth journey—a trustworthy local guide.

## Arrival in Ubud: A Lesson in Trust Starting at the Airport

At 1:30 PM on December 12th, the tropical sun warmly embraced us as we stepped off the plane. Exiting customs, we immediately spotted the hotel's driver holding a sign with our names. We had previously informed the hotel of our need to exchange currency, and the driver was well-prepared. As we drove away from the airport, his first words were not small talk but a valuable local tip: 'You'll see many currency exchange places along the way. Remember, don't go into the ones with exceptionally good rates.' His sincere eyes and simple English explanation of the risks instantly eased our anxieties in this unfamiliar land. Following his recommendation, we successfully exchanged currency at a modest shop frequented by locals. The ninety-minute drive to Ubud flew by as we chatted and admired the scenery. The pre-agreed fare of USD$26 was undisputed, setting a reassuring tone for our journey.

We stayed at **Kubu Tropis Hotel**. Upon arrival, the staff's genuine smiles and cold towels immediately washed away the fatigue of our flight. Though the hotel is small, it exudes thoughtfulness: we once witnessed staff meticulously cleaning the pool, which explains why the water is always crystal clear. The room was filled with relaxing essential oil scents, and the open wardrobe and balcony clothesline were extremely practical for travelers. The only downside was the fixed showerhead, rendering our portable filter showerhead useless. The hotel's prime location is a fifteen to twenty-minute walk from Ubud Palace, surrounded by restaurants and shops, making evening strolls and meals particularly convenient. This hotel became our comfortable base for exploring Ubud over the first three days.

## Core Three Days: Professional and In-Depth Exploration

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_00f2cd7be9f879ad828534427edb9114.jpg)

The next three days were the highlight of this trip. I entrusted my itinerary entirely to **Ani (Si Febri)**, a highly recommended tour guide from online travel communities, and her husband, who served as our private driver. Her popularity was such that I had to 'go against the grain'—contacting her to confirm her availability before even purchasing my flight tickets in Taiwan, akin to booking a slot with a celebrity director.

This early trust was rewarded beyond measure. Ani wasn't just a tour guide; she was a professional itinerary consultant. When I sent her my ideal schedule, she would carefully evaluate it and offer practical advice: 'Ubud city center gets very congested in the evening; we should leave earlier for the sunset viewing.' 'The volcano sunrise and paragliding are highly weather-dependent; let's not rush to book tickets. We can decide on the spot in the morning, and I can help you purchase them then.' She always anticipated blind spots that travelers might miss and patiently explained the reasons. Moreover, she would transform into an itinerary magician, suggesting: 'The route we're taking will pass by Tirta Empul Temple; it's on the way, would you like to visit?' Her flexibility and thoughtfulness added delightful surprises to our trip.

Our driver for these three days (arranged by Ani's team) was equally impressive. As we were in high spirits, we exceeded the standard 10-hour car rental limit on two days (with reasonable overtime charges), but the driver always maintained a smile, never complained, and even proactively helped us with our heavy luggage. ## Epilogue: The Blue of Nusa Lembongan and the Ease of the Return Journey

At the end of our journey, we spent a day hopping over to **Nusa Lembongan**. We booked round-trip ferry tickets in advance on Klook (around USD$13 per person) and deliberately chose different ferry companies to add some variety to the experience. The island's azure seas, blue skies, and the surging waves at "Devil's Tears" added the final vibrant strokes to our Bali album.

https://youtube.com/shorts/exdiGPLkVjw

On the last day, we returned from Nusa Lembongan to **Sanur Port**. Heeding online advice about the inconvenient transportation around the port, we contacted Ani again to arrange a one-way transfer service to **Beachwalk Shopping Center** in Kuta. This decision proved to be absolutely correct. The driver was waiting punctually outside the pier, and we even asked him to make a detour to Bali Banana for some last-minute shopping. The roughly 40-minute ride cost around USD$19, but what we gained was the most precious "ease" while dragging our luggage.

## Conclusion: More Than Just One Beautiful Experience

Six days passed in the blink of an eye. Looking back on this journey, the beauty of Bali lies not only in the feast of landscapes—volcanoes, oceans, temples, and rice terraces—but also in the "people" we encountered along the way. The sincerity of the hotel staff, the kindness of the drivers, and the professionalism and thoughtfulness of Ani's team collectively wove a warm safety net, allowing us to explore and feel without any worries.

If you ask me what the greatest takeaway was? I think it was learning the art of "entrusting." By handing over our itinerary to a trustworthy local expert, she was able to use her knowledge and network to transform an ordinary trip into a surprising and in-depth experience. We have already started to miss it and are certain this won't be the last time. Bali, until we meet again; Ani, we'll be counting on you for our next trip!
