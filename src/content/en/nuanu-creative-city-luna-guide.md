Nuanu Creative City is a 44-hectare creative complex located in Tabanan, Bali (south of Tanah Lot Temple), founded in 2020 by Russian tech entrepreneur Sergey Solonin with a total investment of USD$100 million. The ambitious project's name, **Nuanu**, derives from Balinese, meaning **'in progress,'** symbolizing the evolving and developing nature of this creative city. Solonin initiated the project after relocating to Bali five years ago, aiming to create an ecosystem where art, technology, and nature coexist harmoniously.

## Founding Philosophy and Principles

Nuanu Creative City is rooted in the traditional Balinese philosophy of **Tri Hita Karana**, which emphasizes the harmonious balance between humans and gods, humans and others, and humans and nature. The landmark **THK Tower** (Tri Hita Karana Tower), designed by architect Arthur Mamou Mani and constructed using reclaimed wood, embodies this philosophy. This spiral tower is not only a visual focal point but also symbolizes the spiritual core of Nuanu.

## Sustainability and Environmental Commitment

The most notable feature of Nuanu Creative City is its unwavering commitment to environmental sustainability. Across its 44-hectare site, developers have pledged to preserve two-thirds (approximately 70%) as natural landscape, with only 30% allocated for construction. Specific environmental initiatives include:

- **Tree Planting Program**: Planting 15,000 trees in educational areas to enhance biodiversity and sequester carbon
- **Waste Management**: Recycling 70% of waste generated within the complex
- **Ecosystem Protection**: Preserving large trees and relocating them within the site when necessary
- **Mangrove Conservation**: Protecting and expanding the mangrove ecosystem by replanting mangrove saplings and native coastal tree species
- **Zero-Emission Transition**: Gradually adopting zero-emission vehicles to reduce carbon footprint

These measures set Nuanu apart from the widespread overdevelopment seen in Bali, positioning it as a model project for sustainable development.

## Community Engagement and Global Vision

Nuanu is not just a physical space but a vibrant creative community. Each project partner commits 5% of their monthly revenue to the Nuanu Social Fund, which supports reforestation or social initiatives. The fund has already backed several community projects, such as establishing a computer education center in Beraban Village and preserving traditional Balinese music.

Solonin envisions Nuanu's future as: "Over the next decade, Nuanu will become a dynamic global hub, attracting the world's most innovative minds to live, collaborate, and visit. It will be a thriving community for visionaries, artists, and changemakers, living and working in thoughtfully designed spaces that inspire creativity."

Nuanu Creative City began operations in July 2024, hosting a series of international events including the Suara Art Music Festival, CoinFest Web3 Festival, and DEEP Wellness Festival, further cementing its status as a crossroads of innovation and culture.

This creative city represents a new model of urban development, demonstrating that modern development can address contemporary issues while respecting local traditions, inviting people to join in the journey toward a sustainable future. As one observer noted, Nuanu is not just a tourist destination in Bali but a "space to live, work, and create—a city of the future."
