## **1. Indonesia Customs Regulations on Meat Products**

Indonesia has **strict regulations** on the import of meat and related products, primarily to prevent the spread of animal diseases. Therefore, baby food containing pork or chicken may be considered high-risk and could be confiscated.

### ✅ **Key Regulations:**

- **Restricted Meat Products**: Whether canned, soft-packaged, frozen, or ready-to-eat, any product **containing pork or chicken** falls under restricted categories.
- **Avoid Bringing**: It’s recommended to **avoid** bringing such baby food or opt for **meat-free alternatives** like pure vegetable or fruit-based options.

### ⚠️ **Risks of Non-Compliance:**

- **Penalties or Confiscation**: If you fail to **declare** these items and they are discovered by customs, you may face **fines, confiscation**, or even denial of entry.

## **2. Declaration and Customs Inspection Process**

If you really need to bring meat-based baby food, be sure to follow these steps to reduce the risk of being stopped.

### 📌 **How to Declare Correctly:**

1. **Fill Out the Arrival Card**: On the Indonesian arrival card provided during your flight, **honestly check** the declaration box if you’re carrying meat products.
2. **Inform Customs Upon Arrival**: After landing at Bali Airport, proceed through the **red declaration channel** and actively inform customs officers about the items you’re carrying.
3. **Prepare Documentation**: Bring the **original packaging** of the baby food and ensure it has **clear ingredient labeling** (preferably in English or Indonesian) for customs inspection.

### 📦 **Packing Tips:**

- **Small Original Packages:** Only bring a **small amount** of snacks needed for the trip, and keep them in their original packaging to avoid loose, opened items.
- **Baby Medical Certificate:** If your baby has special dietary needs, ask your doctor to issue an **English medical certificate** to facilitate communication with customs.

## **III. Alternative Solutions for Baby Food**

To avoid being stopped at customs, consider these safer and more convenient options to ensure your baby's dietary needs are met.

### 🛒 **Purchasing Locally in Bali**

Bali's **supermarkets and pharmacies** offer a variety of international and local brands of baby food. Here are common places to shop:

- **Pepito Supermarket** (found in major tourist areas)
- **Guardian** (similar to a drugstore, offering baby products)
- **Coco Supermarket** (common in southern Bali and Ubud)

Available products include:

- Ready-to-eat baby meals (mostly fruit and vegetable purees)
- Snacks like rice crackers and oatmeal bars
- Baby formula and drinks

### 🧳 **Portable Alternative Foods**

If you're concerned about your baby adapting to local food, you can bring the following **meat-free** and easily portable snacks:

- **Plain rice crackers, rice cereal** (no meat ingredients)
- **Fruit puree, vegetable puree** (avoid those containing meat or high protein)
- **Instant cereal powder** (such as oatmeal, pumpkin porridge)

## **IV. Additional Friendly Reminders**

1. **Check Regulations in Advance**: Before departure, we recommend contacting the **Indonesian Consulate** or **Indonesian Agricultural Quarantine Agency** in your area to confirm the latest food import policies and avoid unexpected issues.
2. **Airline Policies**: Some airlines have specific policies regarding infant food. Please check with your airline beforehand to confirm allowable items and quantities.
3. **Utilize Online Shopping Services**: If you're concerned about finding suitable baby food locally, you can also purchase items in advance through Indonesian e-commerce platforms (such as **Tokopedia** or **Shopee Indonesia**) and have them delivered to your hotel.
