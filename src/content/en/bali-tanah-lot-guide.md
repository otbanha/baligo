Located in the Tabanan region of Bali, Tanah Lot Temple sits on a small rocky island in the sea, making it one of Bali's most iconic religious landmarks. Here, visitors can marvel at the stunning sunsets and the waves crashing against the rocky shore, while also enjoying a variety of activities that attract travelers from around the world. This article provides a detailed introduction to the history of Tanah Lot, activities to enjoy, tide information, and accommodation recommendations, ensuring your Bali trip is unforgettable.

## 1. The Historical Background and Religious Significance of Tanah Lot

The history of Tanah Lot dates back to the 16th century, when it was established by the Javanese priest Nirartha. Legend has it that Nirartha was drawn to the spiritual aura of this small island during his travels and decided to build a temple dedicated to the sea god, seeking blessings for the local fishermen and villagers. Since then, Tanah Lot has become an integral part of local faith and culture.

The temple's architecture is simple yet powerful, especially during high tide when the entire structure appears to float on the sea, creating a mystical and solemn atmosphere. During low tide, visitors can walk closer to the temple to admire its intricately carved details. As an important religious site in Bali, Tanah Lot also hosts special ceremonies, the most significant being the "Piodalan" festival, which takes place four days after the Balinese "Hari Raya Kuningan" celebration. Visitors can witness the locals' devout religious practices during this time.

## 2. Exploring Popular Activities at Tanah Lot

Tanah Lot is not just a site for admiring ancient architecture; it also offers a variety of activities for visitors to enjoy. Here are some must-try experiences:

1. **Admire the Beauty of Tanah Lot and the Rocky Island**
   The rocky island where Tanah Lot is located is full of natural beauty, appearing and disappearing with the tides. Many visitors choose to walk closer to the temple during low tide to witness the dramatic sight of waves crashing against the rocks. Known as "Tanah Lot," this unique island is an ideal spot for capturing Bali's scenic beauty.
2. **Observe the Sacred Snakes at the Snake Temple**
   Another highlight is the "Sacred Snake Cave" near the temple, home to black-and-yellow snakes with flat, fish-like tails, known locally as "Ular Suci" (Sacred Snakes). These snakes are believed to be incarnations of Nirartha, the temple's founder, and are said to guard the temple. Visitors can observe these snakes under the guidance of local guides, experiencing the locals' reverence for these sacred creatures.
3. **Capture the Dreamy Sunset at Tanah Lot**
   The sunset at Tanah Lot is considered one of Bali's best. As the sun dips below the horizon, the temple is bathed in golden light, creating a serene and romantic atmosphere. To secure the best vantage point for the "golden sunset," it's recommended to arrive early and find the perfect spot. This magical moment attracts many photography enthusiasts, making it an excellent time for capturing stunning shots.

## Travel Tips: Ticket Information, Tide Check & Nearby Accommodation

Before visiting Tanah Lot, understanding ticket and transportation details can make your trip smoother.

Tanah Lot Official Website: [www.tanahlot.id](https://www.tanahlot.id/)

The latest 2024 ticket prices for Tanah Lot are:

**Indonesian Citizens**

- Adults: 30,000 IDR
- Children: 20,000 IDR

**Foreign Visitors**

- Adults: 75,000 IDR (approx. USD$5)
- Children: 40,000 IDR (approx. USD$2.6)

Parking fees are also applicable: Motorcycles 3,000 IDR, Cars 5,000 IDR, and Large Buses 10,000 IDR. The temple is open daily from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM.

The best time to visit Tanah Lot usually depends on the tide. Travelers are advised to check the [Tanah Lot Tide Schedule](https://www.tideschart.com/Indonesia/Bali/Tabanan/Weekly/) before their visit. This allows you to plan your trip during low tide, enabling a closer view of the temple and avoiding the dangers of high tide.

It's worth noting that during peak hours at sunset, the area gets quite crowded. To avoid the crowds, it's recommended to arrive early.
