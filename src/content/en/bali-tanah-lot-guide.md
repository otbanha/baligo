Situated in the Tabanan region of Bali, Tanah Lot Temple stands on a small rocky island in the sea, making it one of Bali's most symbolic religious landmarks. Not only can visitors admire the breathtaking sunsets and the waves crashing against the rocky shore, but there are also numerous activities to experience, drawing travelers from across the globe. This article will delve into the history of Tanah Lot, activities to enjoy, tide information, and accommodation tips, making your Bali adventure even more memorable.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_36a21198d37ccb139f68e96a327f9b33.jpg)

## 1. Historical Background and Religious Significance of Tanah Lot

The history of Tanah Lot dates back to the 16th century, when it was reportedly established by the Javanese priest Nirartha. During his travels, Nirartha was captivated by the spiritual aura of this small island in the sea and built a temple dedicated to the sea god to seek blessings for the local fishermen and villagers. Since then, Tanah Lot has become an integral part of the local faith and culture.

The architecture of Tanah Lot is simple yet powerful, especially during high tide when the temple appears to float on the sea, creating a mystical and solemn atmosphere. During low tide, visitors can walk to the front of the temple to admire its intricately carved structure. As an important religious site in Bali, Tanah Lot also hosts special religious ceremonies, the most significant being the "Piodalan" festival, which usually takes place four days after the Balinese "Hari Raya Kuningan." Visitors can witness the locals' devout religious practices during these events.

## 2. Popular Activities to Explore at Tanah Lot

Tanah Lot is not just a place to admire ancient architecture; it also offers a variety of activities for visitors to enjoy. Here are some must-try experiences:

1. **Admire the Beauty of Tanah Lot and the Rocky Island**
   The rocky island where Tanah Lot is located is full of natural beauty, appearing and disappearing with the tides. Many visitors choose to walk to the temple during low tide to witness the awe-inspiring sight of waves crashing against the rocks. Known as "Tanah Lot," this unique island is an ideal spot for capturing Bali's scenic beauty.
2. **Witness the Mystical Sacred Snakes at the Snake Temple**
   Another highlight is the "Sacred Snake Cave" near the temple, home to sacred snakes known locally as "Ular Suci." These black and yellow snakes with flat, fish-like tails are believed to be incarnations of Nirartha, the temple's founder, and are said to guard the temple. Visitors can observe these snakes under the guidance of local guides, experiencing the locals' reverence for these sacred creatures.
3. **Capture the Dreamy Sunset at Tanah Lot**
   The sunset at Tanah Lot is considered one of the best in Bali. As the sun gradually dips below the horizon, the entire temple is bathed in golden light, creating a serene and romantic atmosphere. To secure the best spot for viewing the "golden sunset," it's recommended to arrive early and find the perfect vantage point. This moment attracts many photography enthusiasts, making it an excellent time for capturing stunning shots.

## Travel Tips: Ticket Info, Tide Schedules & Nearby Accommodations

Before visiting Tanah Lot, understanding ticket and transportation information can make your trip smoother.

Tanah Lot official website: [www.tanahlot.id](https://www.tanahlot.id/)

Tanah Lot's latest 2024 ticket prices are:

**Indonesian Citizens**

- Adults: 30,000 IDR
- Children: 20,000 IDR

**Foreign Visitors**

- Adults: 75,000 IDR (~USD$5)
- Children: 40,000 IDR (~USD$2.6)

There are also corresponding parking fees: 3,000 IDR for motorcycles, 5,000 IDR for cars, and 10,000 IDR for large buses. The temple is open daily from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM.

The best time to visit Tanah Lot usually depends on the tides. We recommend checking the [Tanah Lot Tide Schedule](https://www.tideschart.com/Indonesia/Bali/Tabanan/Weekly/) before your visit. This way, you can plan to arrive during low tide for a closer view of the temple and avoid the dangers of high tide.

It's worth noting that during peak hours at sunset, the area gets quite crowded. If you want to avoid the crowds, we recommend arriving early.
