Before traveling to Bali, have you ever wondered: ****Should I exchange my New Taiwan Dollars directly for Indonesian Rupiah locally, or should I first convert to USD in Taiwan and then exchange for Rupiah in Bali****? This seemingly small decision could actually give you a significant boost to your travel budget, allowing you to enjoy more delicious meals, buy souvenirs, or indulge in several luxurious spa treatments!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ffa373a8db89c151ec8c9eeb03265b9e.png)

In today's article, I'll use ****real exchange rate data from August 13, 2025**** to show you which currency exchange method is most cost-effective and why exchanging New Taiwan Dollars abroad often puts you at a disadvantage. After reading, you'll be able to easily avoid currency exchange pitfalls and make every dollar count.

## ****Scenario Comparison****

Today (August 13, 2025), you have New Taiwan Dollars to use in Bali, and there are two exchange methods:

1. ****First exchange to USD in Taiwan, then exchange for Indonesian Rupiah in Bali****
2. ****Directly exchange New Taiwan Dollars for Indonesian Rupiah in Bali****

---

### ****Method One: Exchange to USD in Taiwan → Exchange for Indonesian Rupiah in Bali****

- Today's USD exchange rate at Taiwanese banks: ****1 USD = 30.23 TWD****
- Exchange rate at authorized currency exchange offices in Bali: ****1 USD = 16,000 IDR****

Calculation:

1. 1 TWD can be exchanged for ****1 ÷ 30.23 = 0.03307 USD****
2. 0.03307 USD × 16,000 IDR = ****approximately 529 IDR****

****Result: 1 TWD ≈ 529 IDR****

![2025/08/13匯率](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9c0a43971a1d8eeb5148d6d517e5b279.jpg)

Exchange rate as of 2025/08/13

---

### ****Method Two: Directly Exchange TWD for IDR in Bali****

- Bali exchange rate: ****1 TWD = 350 IDR****

****Result: 1 TWD = 350 IDR****

---

## ****Differences and Reasons****

- Method One (exchange to USD first) yields ****529 IDR/TWD****
- Method Two (direct exchange) only yields ****350 IDR/TWD****
- Difference: ****529 − 350 = 179 IDR****, exchanging 10,000 TWD results in a difference of ****1.79 million IDR****!

The main reasons for this difference:

1. ****TWD is a weak currency in the international forex market****, with low demand in many countries, resulting in poor exchange rates.
2. Some exchange offices in Bali****do not accept TWD****, or offer rates so poor they effectively act as hidden fees.
3. USD is a mainstream international currency, ensuring better exchange rates at any exchange office.

---

💡 ****Key Recommendation:****

When traveling to Bali, ****it's much more cost-effective and convenient to exchange USD in Taiwan first, then convert to Indonesian Rupiah locally.****

Of course, if you have other channels (like Indonesian stores in Taiwan), you can inquire there too, but usually the exchange rates aren't better.
