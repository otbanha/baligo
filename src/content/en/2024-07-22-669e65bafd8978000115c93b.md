Bali, a charming tourist destination, attracts countless visitors each year to explore its beautiful beaches, rich culture, and unique natural landscapes. However, with the continuous development of tourism, Bali faces growing traffic congestion issues. To solve this problem, the Bali government has proposed an innovative plan: constructing a coastal highway connecting Ngurah Rai International Airport with Bali's popular beaches.

![現有的峇里島海上公路供一般汽車行走](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e73b8adcaedae5e6573d9951d1f786f1.jpg)

The existing coastal road in Bali is open to regular vehicles

**Plan Overview**

The so-called 'coastal highway' is actually a seaside tram line designed to provide a fast and convenient transportation method connecting the airport and popular tourist areas in western Bali such as Canggu and Kuta. This plan will not only significantly reduce travel time for tourists but also help alleviate traffic pressure on existing roads.

**Project Details**

This coastal highway is not a traditional road but a route specifically designed for electric buses or trams, which will be built along existing beaches. Surya Suamba, head of the Public Works and Spatial Planning Department (PUPR) in Badung Regency, stated that the focus of this plan is first to improve the beaches and then construct an 8-meter-wide road exclusively for electric buses or trams.

The plan has now entered the spatial adjustment phase. The coastal highway will connect the following locations:

- Gilimanuk – Denpasar
- Gilimanuk – Tabanan
- Tabanan – Kuta
- Kuta – Padang Bai
- Kuta – Tanah Lot

These highways will significantly improve traffic flow and infrastructure in Bali, providing more convenient transportation options for both local residents and tourists.

**Phase One Construction**

In the initial phase of the project, the focus will be on the restoration and rehabilitation of beaches. This includes replenishing sand lost to erosion and ensuring beach stability. Subsequently, a dedicated electric bus or tram route will be constructed, offering a fast and eco-friendly transportation option.

**Potential Challenges and Solutions**

While the plan is promising, it also faces several challenges. Coastal erosion is one of them, and to address this, the construction team will introduce the latest technologies and materials to ensure long-term beach stability. Additionally, the impact of tides and waves must be considered, and a series of measures will be implemented during construction to mitigate these natural phenomena.

**Environmental Impact Assessment**

Any large-scale construction project inevitably affects the local environment. To address this, the Badung provincial government will conduct a comprehensive environmental impact assessment to ensure no permanent damage is done to the local ecosystem. Measures will also be taken to protect marine ecosystems, such as establishing artificial reefs and seagrass beds.

**Social and Economic Impact**

This plan will not only enhance the experience for tourists but also bring economic benefits to local communities. With improved transportation, more tourists will be able to easily access various parts of Bali, thereby boosting local tourism and related industries.

**Alternative Solutions**

While the sea highway project is highly appealing, traditional roads remain a viable alternative. The Badung provincial government is also considering different alternatives, including constructing more conventional roads along the beaches. Each option has its pros and cons, and the government will make the final decision based on practical circumstances and technical feasibility.

**Construction Timeline**

According to the current plan, the first phase of the project is expected to be completed by 2025. The entire project will be carried out in multiple phases, each with specific goals and timelines. The government aims to complete the entire project within the next few years, providing more convenient transportation options for tourists and local residents.

**Funding Sources and Budget**

This ambitious project is estimated to cost around IDR 90 billion. The funding will primarily come from government allocations, while also seeking cooperation and investment from private enterprises. Proper allocation and effective management of funds will be crucial to the project's success.

**Government Support and Collaboration**

The Badung provincial government has strongly supported this plan and is actively seeking collaboration with private enterprises. This cooperative model will not only ensure the smooth progress of the project but also promote the overall development of Bali.

**Impact on Tourists**

For tourists, this project will bring numerous conveniences. Whether international visitors or domestic travelers, everyone will be able to quickly reach Bali's major attractions via this sea highway. Additionally, the introduction of electric buses or trams will enhance the overall experience for tourists, allowing them to enjoy Bali's beauty more comfortably.

**Future Outlook**

With the advancement of the sea highway project, Bali is set to welcome a more convenient and modern transportation network. This will not only help alleviate current traffic pressures but also lay a solid foundation for Bali's long-term development. In the future, the government plans to further expand this transportation network, connecting more tourist attractions and enhancing the overall travel experience.

**Conclusion**

Bali's sea highway project is an innovative and promising initiative that will not only improve local transportation conditions but also bring new opportunities for tourism and the local economy. As the project progresses, we look forward to seeing Bali become more convenient and prosperous in the future.
