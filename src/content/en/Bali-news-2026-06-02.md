### 📰 Today's Highlights: Weak Rupiah and Expansion of Asia-Australia Direct Flights

- **Continued Weakness of the Indonesian Rupiah Unexpectedly Boosts Peak Season Tourism Spending**:
Due to international geopolitical uncertainties, the Indonesian Rupiah continues to fluctuate at low levels against the US dollar and other foreign currencies. Economic and tourism experts note that while the Rupiah's weakness causes inflationary pressures for local tourism businesses reliant on imports, it significantly enhances the purchasing power of international tourists arriving in Bali just before the peak season (especially those holding US dollars and Australian dollars), expected to stimulate local spending on luxury villas, fine dining, and customized car rentals.
- **Avoiding Middle East Transit Risks! Tourism Ministry Collaborates with Airlines to Accelerate Asia-Australia Direct Flight Network**:
Due to ongoing conflicts in the Middle East potentially disrupting and delaying long-haul flights to Europe and America (requiring transit in Dubai, Doha, or Abu Dhabi), Indonesia's Tourism Minister confirmed today that the government is implementing an active strategy to 'diversify passenger source risks.' The focus will be on promoting direct flights from nearby Asia-Australia markets, with **Virgin Australia scheduled to launch its 'Canberra-Bali' direct route on June 22**; simultaneously, the Tourism Ministry is coordinating with airlines from India, Vietnam, and other countries to open more direct flights to Bali in the coming months, ensuring high inbound tourist numbers.

### 📄 Tourist Concerns: Provincial Sports Festival Crowds and Southern 3.0-Meter Waves Warning

- **Provincial Student Sports Festival (Porjar Bali 2026) Kicks Off on June 5, Thousands Expected in Two Major Cities**:
The Education and Sports Bureau issued a joint announcement today, confirming that the 2026 Bali Student Sports Festival will take place from **June 5 to 10**. Unlike previous years, all venues this year will be **precisely located in Denpasar and Tabanan**. Tabanan alone announced today that it will send a delegation of 801 people (including 648 student athletes, coaches, and medical teams) to various venues. Tourists planning to travel near major sports venues in Denpasar and Tabanan in early June are advised to be aware of potential traffic congestion due to local crowds.
- **Southern Waters Issue 3.0-Meter Waves and Strong Winds Warning, Affecting Ferries and Fishing Boats**:
The Indonesian Meteorological Agency (BMKG) issued an immediate safety warning for coastal and marine areas today. Meteorological data indicates that **southern waters off Bali (Perairan Selatan Pulau Bali) are expected to experience strong waves up to 3.0 meters high**. Authorities particularly warn that when southeast winds reach 21 knots and wave heights exceed 2.5 meters, ferries traveling to outer islands face extremely high navigation risks; for general offshore fishing boats and small speedboats, wave heights exceeding 1.25 meters are dangerous. Tourists planning to take speedboats to Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Penida, or go diving today or tomorrow are strongly advised to confirm with boat companies if schedules are delayed due to rough seas.

**Today's island-wide land weather is sunny, with comfortable conditions in central and southern mountainous areas. However, given the warning of up to 3.0-meter waves in southern waters today, it is recommended to adjust water activities and instead visit Ubud in the central region to experience stunning waterfalls and mountain scenery.**
