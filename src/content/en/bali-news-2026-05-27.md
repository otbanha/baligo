Update: 2026/05/27

## 1. Malaysian Tourist Injured on Mount Rinjani Airlifted to Bali Hospital 🚁

Indonesia's National Search and Rescue Agency (Basarnas) confirmed that a Malaysian female tourist suffered a serious fall while hiking Mount Rinjani in West Nusa Tenggara. Due to bad weather delaying the rescue operation, the injured tourist was airlifted by helicopter to a Bali hospital on Tuesday morning (May 26). After receiving the report, rescue personnel responded quickly and successfully completed the aerial evacuation as soon as weather conditions permitted, transporting the patient to Inmedika Hospital in Sanur, Bali for further medical care.

## 2. World Bank Warns Bali Faces Five Key Challenges 🌍

The World Bank has issued a stern warning to Bali, highlighting five critical challenges that, if not addressed promptly and effectively, could pose long-term risks. These issues include wastewater treatment and recycling, waste management, traffic mobility, clean water supply, and electricity supply in certain areas. Bali Governor Wayan Koster stated that infrastructure development has a direct impact on Bali's tourism sustainability and emphasized that the local government is promoting a clean and healthy environment through policies such as restricting single-use plastics and strengthening waste management at the source.

## 3. Shark Scare Resolved in Nusa Dua Waters - Bali's Beaches Confirmed Safe 🏖️

Resort officials in Nusa Dua, Bali have confirmed that the waters in the area are safe, responding to reports of a alleged shark sighting on May 22 (Friday). Following enhanced monitoring and beach patrols over the weekend, authorities confirmed that there are no sharks in the vicinity and no risk to tourists. Nusa Dua's ITDC has requested the water police and coast guard to continue routine patrols and monitoring of coastal areas to ensure tourist safety.
