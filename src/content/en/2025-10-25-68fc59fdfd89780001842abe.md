While Bali is famous for its Hindu temples, the island is also home to several Chinese Buddhist temples that exude Eastern charm. These temples not only reflect the historical presence of Chinese immigrants in Bali but also serve as unique spots for travelers seeking tranquility and cultural fusion.

Below, we introduce six must-visit temples/monasteries in Bali👇

***

### 🏮 **1. Vihara Dharmayana | Kuta**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c97c1a79a14cdb7a094a888ce573dae2.jpg)

Tucked away in the bustling streets of Kuta, **Vihara Dharmayana** is one of Bali's oldest Buddhist temples, established in 1876 with over 200 years of history. The temple is surrounded by a small pond inhabited by turtles, while the main hall features vibrant red pillars, dragon sculptures, and large red lanterns, exuding a strong Chinese ambiance. The serene atmosphere here contrasts sharply with the lively beach outside, making it an ideal spot to find a moment of peace amidst Kuta's hustle and bustle.

***

### 🌿 **2. Vihara Dharma Giri Temple | Tabanan, West Bali**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2b5cbc2609a02a0e190331f413afd26.jpg)

Located in the mountainous region of Tabanan in West Bali, **Vihara Dharma Giri Temple** is best known for its **giant reclining Buddha statue**. The temple is divided into three levels, each with distinct designs and functions, and features tranquil gardens and viewing platforms. With its fresh air and beautiful scenery, visitors can freely wander around and immerse themselves in the peaceful Buddhist atmosphere. It’s a must-visit destination for many travelers exploring West Bali.

***

### 🕊 **3. Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery | Banjar, North Bali**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c227915a15c932da1e5530467e36dfcc.jpg)

Situated in Banjar, North Bali, **Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery** was built in the 1960s and is one of **Bali’s largest Buddhist temples**. This temple also functions as an active monastery where monks practice daily rituals. The architecture blends Balinese traditions with Buddhist styles, offering views of Lovina’s coastline. It’s not only a pilgrimage site for Buddhists but also an ideal place for travelers to meditate and escape the noise of everyday life.

***

### 🕍 **4. Vihara Buddha Dharma | Seminyak**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_202f5bcbe1e52ec63840a101dcb7620e.jpg)

Located in Seminyak, **Vihara Buddha Dharma** was completed in 2007 and is a **modern and contemporary Buddhist temple**. Although it has a relatively short history, it has become an important place of worship for both local and international Buddhists. The temple is clean, bright, and bustling with incense, making it a convenient and serene spot for travelers seeking spiritual cleansing in Seminyak.

***

### 🏯 **5. Vihara Buddha Guna | Nusa Dua**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c74636fc69cbff76a2df2c287979e8ac.gif)

Situated in Nusa Dua, **Vihara Buddha Guna** is part of the renowned **Puja Mandala multi-religious complex**. This complex symbolizes Indonesia's spirit of religious harmony, housing five religious sites: a Hindu temple, a Catholic church, a Protestant church, a mosque, and this Buddhist temple. Vihara Buddha Guna features elegant architecture, fragrant incense, and a solemn atmosphere, embodying Bali's ethos of "coexistence of different faiths."

***

### 🏮 **6. Vihara Satya Dharma Temple | Benoa Harbour**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2f1db13b9d1b0b61ad8eeede51d4507.jpg)

Located near **Benoa Harbour**, **Vihara Satya Dharma Temple** is one of Bali's most magnificent Chinese Buddhist temples. The temple's grand architectural style, with its soaring eaves, crimson pillars, and golden statues, creates a visually stunning effect that immerses visitors in a rich Chinese cultural atmosphere.

The temple primarily worships **Mazu (Heavenly Holy Mother)** and also enshrines various Guanyin Bodhisattvas and guardian deities. It serves as a significant religious center for the local Chinese community during major festivals such as Lunar New Year, Ghost Festival, and Guanyin's Birthday.

The temple is also ideal for photography and quiet contemplation—morning light bathes the red tiles and golden Buddha statues, creating a particularly solemn scene. Due to its proximity to the harbor, many cruise passengers visit before or after their voyages to pray for a safe and smooth journey.

***

## 💡 **Tips for Visiting Buddhist Temples:**

- Dress modestly before entering the temple.
- Avoid pointing the soles of your feet toward Buddha statues or altars.
- Maintain a peaceful mindset and avoid loud noises.
- Traditionally, women are advised not to enter the temple during menstruation or within six weeks after childbirth.
