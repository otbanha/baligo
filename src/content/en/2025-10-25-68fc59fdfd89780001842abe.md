### 🏮 **1. Vihara Dharmayana | Kuta**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c97c1a79a14cdb7a094a888ce573dae2.jpg)

Tucked away in the bustling streets of Kuta, **Vihara Dharmayana** is one of Bali's oldest Buddhist temples, established in 1876 with over 200 years of history. The temple is surrounded by a small pond inhabited by turtles, while the main hall features vibrant red pillars, dragon sculptures, and large red lanterns, exuding a strong Chinese ambiance. This serene sanctuary offers a stark contrast to the lively beach outside, making it an ideal spot to find tranquility amidst Kuta's hustle and bustle.

### 🌿 **2. Vihara Dharma Giri Temple | Tabanan, West Bali**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2b5cbc2609a02a0e190331f413afd26.jpg)

Located in the mountainous region of Tabanan in West Bali, **Vihara Dharma Giri Temple** is most famous for its **giant reclining Buddha statue**. The temple is divided into three levels, each with distinct designs and functions, and features peaceful gardens and viewing platforms. With its fresh air and beautiful scenery, visitors can leisurely stroll through the temple grounds, soaking in the Buddhist atmosphere. It’s a must-visit destination for those exploring West Bali.

### 🕊 **3. Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery | Banjar, North Bali**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c227915a15c932da1e5530467e36dfcc.jpg)

Situated in Banjar, North Bali, **Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery** was built in the 1960s and is one of **Bali's largest Buddhist temples**. This temple also functions as an active monastery, where monks practice daily rituals. The architecture blends traditional Balinese and Buddhist styles, offering views of Lovina’s coastline. It’s not only a pilgrimage site for Buddhists but also an ideal place for travelers seeking meditation and escape from the noise of everyday life.

### 🕍 **4. Vihara Buddha Dharma | Seminyak**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_202f5bcbe1e52ec63840a101dcb7620e.jpg)

Located in Seminyak, **Vihara Buddha Dharma** was established in 2007 and is a **modern and contemporary Buddhist temple**. Despite its relatively short history, it has become an important place of worship for both local and international Buddhists. The temple is clean, bright, and bustling with incense, making it a convenient and peaceful spot for travelers seeking spiritual cleansing in Seminyak.

### 🏯 **5. Vihara Buddha Guna | Nusa Dua**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c74636fc69cbff76a2df2c287979e8ac.gif)

Situated in Nusa Dua, **Vihara Buddha Guna** is part of the renowned **Puja Mandala multi-religious complex**. This complex symbolizes Indonesia’s spirit of religious harmony, housing five religious sites: a Hindu temple, Catholic church, Protestant church, mosque, and this Buddhist temple. Vihara Buddha Guna features elegant architecture, fragrant incense, and a solemn, peaceful atmosphere, embodying Bali’s ethos of coexistence among different faiths.

### 🏮 **6. Vihara Satya Dharma Temple | Benoa Harbour, Bali**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2f1db13b9d1b0b61ad8eeede51d4507.jpg)

Located next to **Benoa Harbour, Vihara Satya Dharma Temple is one of the most magnificent Chinese Buddhist temples in Bali**. The temple's architecture is grand and impressive, with its upturned eaves, vermilion walls and pillars, and golden statues creating a stunning visual spectacle. Stepping inside, you’ll immediately feel immersed in the rich cultural atmosphere of the Chinese community.

The temple primarily worships **Mazu (Heavenly Holy Mother)** and also enshrines various Guanyin Bodhisattvas and guardian deities. It serves as an important religious center for the local Chinese community, hosting major festivals such as Lunar New Year, Ghost Festival, and Guanyin’s Birthday.

This temple is also ideal for photography and quiet contemplation—morning light bathes the red tiles and golden Buddha statues, creating a particularly solemn scene. Due to its proximity to the harbor, many cruise passengers visit before or after their voyages to pray for a safe and smooth journey.

## 💡 **Tips for Visiting the Temple:**

- Dress modestly before entering the temple.
- Avoid pointing your feet toward Buddha statues or altars.
- Maintain a peaceful mindset and avoid loud noises.
- Traditionally, women during menstruation or within six weeks postpartum are advised not to enter temples.
