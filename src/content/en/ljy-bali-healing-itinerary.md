### 🛂 Immigration Process Was Surprisingly Smooth

This time, I handled almost everything online in advance, so the immigration process was shockingly fast.

#### 1️⃣ Electronic Visa (B1)

I applied for the electronic visa before departure, which cost around USD$32.
Upon arrival, I went straight through the e-gate, and the actual clearance took about 1 minute—no queues at all.

If you're visiting Bali during peak season, I highly recommend applying for the electronic visa in advance. Waiting in line for Visa on Arrival (VOA) can sometimes make you question your life choices 😆

#### 2️⃣ Electronic Customs Declaration

This is also recommended to fill out ahead of time.
You can complete the online declaration up to three days before departure. Just save the QR code screenshot on your phone, and scan it on-site to finish.

#### 3️⃣ Tourism Tax

I paid the tourism tax online in advance, which was about USD$10.
Many processes in Bali are gradually becoming digitized, and handling things ahead of time really saves a lot of time.

### 🌅 First Stop: Straight to Jimbaran for Sunset

After arriving, I didn’t waste any time and headed straight to Jimbaran.

There are many places in Bali to watch the sunset, but the scene in Jimbaran with its "orange-red sky + low-flying planes" is truly iconic.
Especially when sitting on the beach, watching the sky slowly shift from bright blue to gold, then orange, and finally burning across the entire horizon, you can’t help but feel: "Ah, I’ve really made it to Bali."

If you take this China Airlines flight, you still have a chance to catch the sunset in Seminyak.
But if you take EVA Air, since the arrival time is a bit later, I personally think it's safer to stay in Jimbaran. Otherwise, you might hit the evening rush hour traffic on the way to Seminyak.

## 🏠 Day 1 Accommodation｜Seminyak Pelangi Bali Hotel & Spa

The first night was spent at Pelangi Bali Hotel & Spa in Seminyak.

For about USD$65 per night for two people, I think it's quite reasonable given the location.
The biggest advantage is—you can walk directly to the beach after dropping off your luggage.

No need to call a taxi or move around anymore. In the evening, take a stroll to a beachfront restaurant for dinner, listen to the sound of the waves, and feel the sea breeze. That feeling of 'the journey has officially begun' is truly comforting.

## Day 2｜Morning Beach Walk, Beach Club, Charity Visit, and Finally Slowing Down in Sanur 🌊

The rhythm of the second day was exactly my ideal Bali mode.
No cramming in attractions, no rushing from morning till night, but switching between the beach, coffee bars, charity spaces, and the slow pace of Sanur.

***

### 🌞 The Morning Beach in Seminyak is More Charming Than During the Day

Around 6 in the morning, stepping out from the hotel's back door leads directly to the beach.

This is exactly why I love staying at beachfront hotels. There's no need to plan special activities; simply waking up and being able to stroll on the sand first thing in the morning is already worth it.

Seminyak Beach in the early morning is completely different from at night. There's no loud music or crowds of tourists, just the sound of waves, morning joggers, and a group of foreigners already out surfing.

And I have to say, the 'density of surfing hunks' at Seminyak in the morning is really high 🤣 Many people hit the water before dawn, and the beach vibe is super chill. Watching everyone carrying their surfboards into the sea gives you a sense of 'This is the real Bali daily life.'

***

### 🍸 Atlas Beach Club｜The Legendary World's Largest Beach Club

During the day, we visited the highly talked-about Atlas Beach Club.

Atlas Beach Club

It's known as the 'World's Largest Beach Club,' and seeing it in person truly astounds you with its scale. The entire space is massive, featuring pools, DJ areas, restaurants, and lounge sections, almost resembling a small resort.

Thanks to our collaboration with TUL BLUE BERAWA, staying in a suite or above grants free entry, including prime front-row lounge seating and a welcome drink. The front-row area makes a huge difference, offering direct ocean views, waves, music, people, and sunset—creating a strong ambiance.

However, I personally feel that Atlas leans more towards being a 'lively party-style Beach Club'.
If you enjoy music, a vibrant atmosphere, and places that are great for photos, it will be very suitable; if you prefer a quiet and relaxing type of place, you might enjoy other beach clubs more.

***

### 🍰 Afternoon Tea at Kempinski Nusa Dua｜The Ocean View Really Adds a Lot

In the afternoon, I went to Kempinski in Nusa Dua for afternoon tea.

The Apurva Kempinski Bali

I highly recommend booking a seaside seat in advance here.
Sitting by the sea is a completely different experience compared to the regular indoor seats.

While sipping tea and gazing at the Indian Ocean, the entire pace slows down.
Moreover, Kempinski's architectural design is quite imposing, blending traditional Balinese elements with a modern style, making many corners great for photos.

The afternoon tea costs around USD$23 per person, which I think is reasonable considering the hotel's standard and the view.

***

### 💚 Bali Life Foundation｜The Most Memorable Stop of This Journey

What left the deepest impression in the afternoon wasn’t a scenic spot, but our visit to the Bali Life Foundation.

Bali Life Foundation

The foundation opens for visits every Friday from 4 PM to 7 PM.
The campus isn’t very large and currently houses around 50 children.

When we arrived, we were just about to unload the rice and cooking oil we had brought for donation.
A few young boys immediately ran over to help.

What shocked me the most was—
those kids, probably only five or six years old, effortlessly carried 10-kilogram bags of rice by themselves.

The scene was quite impactful.
It suddenly hits you that some children grow up in environments vastly different from the world we’re accustomed to.

Yet, they weren’t somber.
Instead, they kept laughing, chatting, and even proactively helped welcome everyone.

The foundation gathers for dinner at 6 PM, and visitors are welcome to join.
It feels less like a mere 'tour' and more like briefly stepping into their daily lives.

They also run a kindergarten offering half-day to full-day childcare services.
Many foreign parents who come to Bali for surfing or work send their kids here, and this income helps support the foundation’s operations.

I personally really like this model.
It’s not entirely reliant on donations but strives to establish a sustainable cycle for long-term operation.

***

### 🏠 Stay at a Beachfront Hotel in Sanur

In the evening, we moved to Sanur and checked into Respati Beach Hotel.

Respati Beach Hotel

This hotel embodies the classic Sanur beachfront style. It’s not extravagant, but the location is incredibly comfortable.

For around USD$65 per night for two people, you’re right by the beach, and it’s just a five-minute walk to the mall, making it very convenient for daily needs.

Compared to Seminyak and Canggu, Sanur has a much slower pace. It’s less about parties and more about strolling, relaxing, and embracing a slower way of life.

***

### 🛍️ Icon Bali Mall｜The Trendy Seaside Mall

After dropping off our luggage, we headed to Icon Bali Mall for some shopping.

ICON Bali Mall

This new mall in Sanur has been generating a lot of buzz lately.
Its biggest feature is the 'ocean views'.

Many spots offer direct views of the sea, and with its modern space and strong air conditioning (laughs), it has become a must-visit place for many coming to Sanur.

I also picked up some fragrances from Secret Garden while I was there.
Indonesia actually has many affordable fragrance brands, some with tropical scents that make perfect souvenirs to bring home.

## Day 3｜A Slow Day in Sanur, So Gentle It's Hard to Leave 🌿

On the third day, I didn't plan any rigid itinerary.
After waking up naturally, I decided to spend the whole day in Sanur.

Many first-time visitors to Bali focus on Seminyak, Canggu, and Ubud, but I've always loved the 'slow beach vibe' of Sanur.
It's not overly noisy or touristy, and feels more like a place where you can truly live.

***

### 🌅 Sanur Beach Walk｜The Morning Sea Breeze is Truly Gentle

In the morning, I headed straight to Sanur Beach for a walk.

Sanur Beach is quite different from Bali's west coast. The waves here are calmer, and in the early morning, the sea surface even has a mirror-like quality.

There's no Beach Club music or crowded scenes. Instead, you'll find people strolling, cycling, or simply sitting by the sea, lost in thought.

I really enjoy the morning air in Sanur. The sea breeze isn't strong but rather gentle and soothing, naturally slowing you down.

Sometimes, the most healing moments of travel aren't about visiting numerous attractions but simply sitting by the sea, gazing at the horizon.

***

### 🧸 Smile Shop｜A Stop with More Meaning Than Shopping

This time, I even brought some dolls from Taiwan to donate.

This 'Smile Shop' is actually a second-hand charity store operated by the Bali Smile Foundation.

Yayasan Senyum Bali

Yayasan Senyum Bali

This foundation has been helping local Indonesian children with cleft lips, palates, and facial deformities by raising funds for medical treatment and arranging surgeries, hoping to bring smiles back to their faces.

I’ve always admired this type of charitable organization. What they do often isn’t just short-term aid but something that truly impacts a child’s future.

Moreover, when you come here, you’ll find the overall atmosphere very warm and pressure-free. It feels more like pure support.

***

### 🧶 A Warm Cross-National Crochet Gathering

This time, I happened to join a very special cross-national crochet gathering.

People from different countries came together, stitch by stitch, crocheting 'breast prostheses.'

These handmade crocheted prostheses will all be donated to relevant associations for use by women post-mastectomy. Compared to regular prostheses, these crocheted ones are lighter, softer, and more skin-friendly.

The atmosphere was actually quite quiet. Everyone focused on their crocheting, some chatting, some sharing stories. There weren’t many overly emotional words, but a sense of gentleness gradually built up.

Sometimes, the most precious memories from travel aren’t necessarily visiting impressive landmarks but rather these small, genuine human connections.

***

### 💆‍♀️ My Go-To Spa in Sanur

Of course, I scheduled a massage for the afternoon 🤣

This time, I revisited The Nest Boutique Spa, a place I almost always return to when I'm here.

The Nest Boutique Spa

Sanur has so many massage places, but I’ve always felt that this one maintains a consistently high quality. The ambiance is comfortable, and the pressure is just right—it doesn’t feel like a rushed tourist trap.

And let’s be honest, sometimes you really need a massage during a Bali trip. After all the walking, sun exposure, and traveling, a good massage can completely rejuvenate you.

***

### 🛍️ Post-Massage Shopping Spree

After the massage, feeling completely refreshed, I headed straight to the mall to continue shopping 🤣

Sanur is becoming more and more convenient these days.
Unlike before when many people thought it was 'a bit old-fashioned,' now there are more and more large shopping malls, restaurants, and cafes, while still maintaining its original slow-paced vibe.

***

## 💡 Why I’m Growing to Love Sanur

The location of the hotel I stayed at this time was seriously impressive—almost unreal.

Massage shops, the beach, shopping malls, money changers—almost everything was within a 3-minute walk 👍👍

This level of convenience is actually really important in Bali.
Because many places may seem close, but in reality, traffic can make you question your life choices.

And Sanur has a big advantage—
it has great amenities, but the overall atmosphere remains relaxed.

Especially if it’s not your first time in Bali and you’re starting to feel like skipping the daily sightseeing, just wanting to find a comfortable place to unwind, stroll, eat, and get massages, I’d highly recommend spending a few days in Sanur.

## Day 4｜Riding a Scooter Through the Mountain Roads, from Tirta Empul Temple to Ubud and Sanur 🛵

On the fourth day, we fully embraced the scooter travel mode.
We left Sanur promptly at 7 a.m., heading straight for central Bali.

Honestly, riding a scooter in Bali gives you a truly special sense of freedom.
Along the way, you’ll pass through small villages, rice terraces, temples, and mountain roads. Sometimes, even just stopping at a red light and seeing roadside offerings, breakfast stalls, or students riding to school makes you think, 'Ah, this is the real daily life of Bali.'

However, a quick reminder: if you’re not used to riding scooters or aren’t familiar with the rhythm of Southeast Asian traffic, it’s much easier to hire a private driver 🤣

***

### 🙏 Tirta Empul Temple｜Coming early makes a huge difference

Our first stop was Tirta Empul Temple.

Tirta Empul Temple

We arrived very early, so the crowd wasn’t too bad.
I’ve seen many online photos of long queues, but coming in the morning makes the experience much more comfortable, whether it’s taking photos or participating in the purification ritual.

Tirta Empul Temple is most famous for its holy water purification.
Many locals and tourists line up to perform cleansing rituals under the different water spouts.

The atmosphere there is actually very serene.
Even with quite a few tourists around, you can still feel a very calming energy.

But I have to seriously remind you of one thing:

⭐️ No hairdryer.
⭐️ No hairdryer.
⭐️ No hairdryer.

After getting my hair wet, I really had to let it air dry 🤣
Good thing I brought my own towel, otherwise riding with wet hair in the wind would have been a nightmare.

***

### 🧱 Penglipuran Village | A Traditional Village Full of Life

Continuing north, we arrived at Penglipuran Village.

Penglipuran Village

This place has long been known as one of the cleanest villages in the world.
The overall environment is meticulously maintained, and it retains many traditional Balinese architectural features.

Unlike typical tourist spots, this is still a functioning village where people live.
Many homes have small shops in front selling drinks, snacks, and souvenirs, giving it a very down-to-earth vibe.

I really enjoyed the pace here.
There’s no need to do anything special—just take your time walking and observing.

Some shops even sell local snacks, making it a great place to stop and rest if you’re tired from riding.

***

### ☕ Tegalalang Rice Terrace | TIS Cafe's View is Truly Stunning

After leaving the village, we rode along the mountain road towards the Tegalalang Rice Terrace.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace

This time, we headed straight to TIS Cafe, a favorite spot for photos.

TIS Cafe

Many people call this place an 'Instagrammable cafe,' but honestly, the view here is genuinely impressive. Sitting facing the rice terraces, watching the layers of green valleys stack up, it's easy to lose yourself in thought.

And the mountain breeze feels incredibly refreshing. It's completely different from the hot, sticky air by the sea.

I personally think this kind of place is perfect for a mid-motorcycle trip break. Have a cup of coffee, enjoy the mountain view, and give your butt a rest 🤣

***

### 🍛 Classic Ubud Lunch｜Bebek Tepi Sawah Dirty Duck Rice

We headed straight for lunch to try Ubud's iconic dirty duck rice.

Bebek Tepi Sawah

Their Crispy Duck is seriously impressive.
The skin is fried to a perfect crisp, the meat stays juicy, and even the bones are fragrant.

Pair it with Indonesian chili sauce, and it’s downright sinful.

But let me warn you—
the chili sauce is seriously spicy 🤣🤣

If you usually eat mild spice, I recommend starting slow, or you might spend the rest of the day searching for water.

***

### 💦 Tegenungan Waterfall｜Going Down is Fun, Coming Up is Exhausting 🤣

After lunch, we headed south on our way back and made a quick detour to Tegenungan Waterfall.

Tegenungan Waterfall is one of the more accessible waterfalls in Bali.
You don't need to hike for hours, but you do have to descend into the valley.

As you approach the waterfall, the mist hitting your face is truly awe-inspiring.
The sound of the waterfall is so loud that it's almost impossible to hear someone talking next to you.

What makes Bali's waterfalls unique is that many of them aren't the 'thin and long' type, but rather have a thick, powerful flow of water.

The only issue is...

Climbing back up is quite tiring 😆
Especially after eating dirty duck rice and then tackling the stairs, it feels like a test of endurance.

***

### 🍦 Massimo Gelato｜Always a Queue

After returning to Sanur in the evening, of course, we had to eat again 🤣

This time, we went to buy Massimo Gelato.

這次跑去買 Massimo 冰淇淋。

Massimo Italian Restaurant

They always have a queue, no matter if it's day or night. There's always a crowd at the door.

But it must be said, there's indeed something remarkable about it. They offer a wide variety of flavors, and you can try almost all of them.

It's cheap, the scoops are big, and the flavors are rich.

It's easy to think 'I'll just have one scoop' when ordering, but you end up quietly getting two scoops in the end 🤣

***

### 🛍️ After showering back at the hotel, we continued our shopping spree in the evening

After showering and changing clothes at the hotel, we walked to the neighboring Icon Bali Mall in the evening.

ICON Bali Mall

I’ve been coming here almost every day these past few days 🤣. Straight into 'online shopping mode.'

Sanur is really becoming more and more convenient these days. Many things you don’t need to specially go to Kuta or Seminyak for—you can already find them all here.

And most importantly—no traffic jams. This point is really crucial.

## Day 5｜Chasing dolphins at dawn, feeding monkeys on the mountain roads, and ending perfectly with Cuca 🐬

The past few days have been more relaxed travel, but Day 5 was completely a 'day of burning through life' 🤣

We left Sanur at 3 AM, rushing all the way north to Lovina. Although I was really exhausted to the point of questioning life, the moment I saw the dolphins, I instantly thought, 'Okay, it was worth it.'

***

### 🐬 Chasing Dolphins at Dawn｜The sunrise over Lovina Beach was truly magical

We arrived at Lovina Beach before the sky even began to lighten.

Lovina Beach

Then we boarded a traditional Jukung boat to chase dolphins.

Honestly, waking up at dawn was brutal.
Especially after the long drive to North Bali in the middle of the night — I was practically a zombie.

But shortly after we set sail, dolphins suddenly started appearing.

And not just one or two.
An entire pod kept leaping out of the water right beside the boat.

Everyone on board screamed and gasped almost simultaneously 🤣
That sight instantly woke me up.

Plus, the sunrise slowly rising over the horizon, turning the sky from deep blue to orange-red, created an almost surreal atmosphere.

But a word of warning:

🌊 Remember to bring a change of clothes and pants.
The waves will definitely splash into the boat.

Especially if you’re sitting in the front row — you’re almost guaranteed to get soaked 🤣

***

### 🐒 Feeding Monkeys on the Mountain Road | The Monkeys Here Are Much Gentler

On the way back, we stopped at a roadside spot to feed the monkeys.

There are monkeys in many places in Bali, but the ones here are noticeably much gentler.
Unlike the ones in Uluwatu, where some monkeys will directly snatch glasses or phones 🤣

There's a small fee on-site (though I honestly can't remember how much 😆), and you can buy food to feed them.

Some monkeys even sit politely waiting for food, completely different from the 'gangster monkey vibe' in Uluwatu.

***

### ☕ Mountain Coffee Park | A Rest Stop on the Way Back

Then, on the way back, we stopped by a mountain coffee park for a break.

This type of coffee garden is quite common in Bali's mountainous areas,
with the biggest feature being that almost all teas and coffees are available for free tasting.

From ginger tea and herbal tea to various flavored coffees, they have it all.

Of course, the most famous is still Kopi Luwak. A cup costs around 50,000 IDR.

You can also see the coffee bean processing and production process on-site, and there are plenty of teas and coffees available for purchase.

I personally think this kind of place is perfect as a pit stop during long journeys. Sit down, enjoy the breeze, take in the mountain views, and you'll feel much more relaxed.

***

### 🍫 Jungle Gold Bali｜A Place Where Chocolate Lovers Lose Control

Then we continued on to the chocolate factory 🤣

Jungle Gold Bali

The most dangerous part is—

Almost all the chocolates are available for free tasting 😆😆

And it's not just any ordinary tasting. Many flavors have a tropical vibe, like coconut, sea salt, and various spices.

Ended up just wanting to 'take a look,' but walked out with a bag in hand 🤣

***

### 🍛 Randomly Found Babi Guling Roast Pork Rice

For lunch, instead of seeking out a famous spot, we randomly stopped at a Babi Guling place on the side of the road.

Babi Guling

Sometimes the best food in Bali isn’t necessarily at the high-rated spots on Google Maps.

Crispy pork skin, juicy shredded pork, paired with spicy sauce—it’s truly sinful.

And these kinds of places are usually very local. Sitting next to people eating, mostly locals, gives you that 'today I’m really not in a tourist area' feeling.

***

### 💤 Back to the Hotel and Immediately Passed Out

After returning to Sanur, the first thing I did was take a shower.

After washing off the sea salt, sweat, and the exhaustion from a day under the sun and wind, I fell asleep instantly.

I’m not exaggerating. It was the kind of ‘passed out as soon as I lay down’ level 🤣

I didn’t schedule any activities for the afternoon, simply using the time to recover from the exhaustion of the past few days.

***

### 🍽️ Cuca｜Still Amazed Every Time I Visit Bali

After a good nap, I headed out promptly at 6 PM.

The highlight of tonight’s dinner was Cuca, a place I almost always revisit whenever I’m in Bali.

Cuca Restaurant Bali

It’s not the flashy type of fine dining, but rather the kind that makes you think, ‘Every dish is thoughtfully designed.’

Their creative dishes, cocktails, and desserts are all truly top-notch.

Moreover, the overall atmosphere is very comfortable—not too formal, yet still refined.

After chasing dolphins at dawn, long-distance travel, and navigating mountain roads, ending the day with a slightly tipsy dinner felt like the perfect conclusion to this birthday blind-box trip.

Looking back at Day 5 now, I really feel like:

"How on earth did I survive that day? 🤣"

This Bali trip was actually quite different from what I’m used to. There was no rushing to visit attractions or cramming the schedule full.

More often, it was about lounging by the beach, riding through mountain roads, chatting with strangers, visiting foundations, relaxing with massages, and enjoying many unexpected little surprises.

Some places weren’t even the so-called 'hot spots,' but now, looking back, they’ve become the most memorable parts.

And I’m increasingly realizing—the true charm of Bali seems to go far beyond those Instagrammable moments.

It's only when you start to slow down that you gradually begin to feel the warmth, rhythm, and sense of life.

This birthday blind box trip was probably:

Half exhausting myself, half healing; 
Half running around crazily, half recharging 🤣

But because of this, it turned into a trip with a strong aftertaste, one that makes you want to keep reminiscing about it.
