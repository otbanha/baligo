### 🛂 Immigration process was smoother than expected

This time, I handled almost everything online in advance, so the immigration process was surprisingly fast.

#### 1️⃣ Electronic Visa (B1)

I applied for the electronic visa before departure, costing around USD$32.
After landing, I went straight to the e-gate channel, and the actual clearance took about 1 minute with zero waiting in line.

If you're visiting Bali during peak season, I really recommend getting the e-visa in advance—queuing for VOA on-site can sometimes make you question your life choices 😆

#### 2️⃣ Electronic Customs Declaration

I also recommend filling this out in advance.
You can submit it online up to three days before departure. Just save the QR code screenshot on your phone, and scan it on-site when you arrive.

#### 3️⃣ Tourism Tax

I also paid the tourism tax online in advance, around USD$10.
Nowadays, many processes in Bali are gradually going digital, and handling things early really saves a lot of time.

### 🌞 Seminyak Beach at Dawn, Even More Enchanting Than by Day

Around six in the morning, I walked straight out the back door of the hotel onto the sand.

This is exactly why I love staying at a beachfront hotel. No need to plan a thing—just waking up and stepping onto the sand for a stroll is already worth it.

Seminyak Beach in the early morning is a completely different world from the night. No thumping music, no crowds of tourists—just the sound of waves, a few morning joggers, and a handful of surfers already paddling out.

And I have to say, the 'surfer hottie density' on Seminyak Beach in the morning is seriously high 🤣. Many of them hit the water before dawn, and the whole vibe is super chill. Watching everyone carry their boards into the sea, you get that feeling: 'This is the real Bali daily life.'

### 🍸 Atlas Beach Club｜The World's Largest Beach Club, They Say

During the day, I checked out the much-talked-about Atlas Beach Club.

![Atlas Beach Club｜傳說中的全球最大 Beach Club](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1781014745875-atlas2.jpg)

Photo courtesy of Atlas Beach Club

It's earned the title of 'the world's largest beach club,' and seeing it in person, the scale is truly staggering. The entire space is massive—from the pool and DJ area to the restaurant and lounge zones, it feels almost like a small resort.

This time, thanks to a collaboration with TUL BLUE BERAWA, guests staying in suites or above can enter for free, and are even given front-row sofa seating and a welcome drink. The front-row area makes a huge difference—you get a direct ocean view, with the waves, music, crowds, and sunset all around you, creating an incredibly immersive atmosphere.

That said, I personally feel Atlas is more of a 'lively party-style beach club.' If you love music, a vibrant vibe, and photogenic spots, it’s a great fit; if you prefer a quiet, relaxing escape, you might enjoy other beach clubs more.

### 💚 Bali Life Foundation｜The most profound stop on this trip

The most memorable part of the afternoon wasn't actually the sights, but the visit to the Bali Life Foundation.

https://youtube.com/shorts/ljVgfCUTOyg

Courtesy of Lai Jiaying, Bali Life Foundation

The foundation opens for预约 visits every Friday from 4:00 to 7:00 PM. The campus is actually quite small, currently housing about 50 children.

When we arrived, we were about to unload the rice and cooking oil we brought for donation. A few young boys immediately ran over to help.

What shocked me the most was that these kids, probably only five or six years old, each carried a 10-kilogram bag of rice on their own.

That image was quite striking. It suddenly makes you realize that some children's upbringing is vastly different from the world we're used to.

But at the same time, they weren't gloomy. Instead, they kept laughing, chatting, and even took the initiative to greet everyone.

![Bali Life Foundation｜這趟旅程最深刻的一站](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1781014986137-bali_life_foundation2.jpg)

Courtesy of Lai Jiaying

The foundation has dinner together at 6:00 PM, and visitors are welcome to stay and join. Compared to a simple 'tour,' it felt more like briefly stepping into their lives.

They also run a kindergarten offering half-day to full-day childcare services. Many foreign parents who come to Bali for surfing or work send their children here, and this income helps subsidize the foundation's operations.

I really like this model. It's not entirely reliant on donations, but instead strives to build a cycle that can sustain itself long-term.

In the morning, I went straight for a walk on Sanur Beach.

I really love the morning air in Sanur. The sea breeze isn't strong—it's gentle and soothing, naturally slowing you down.

### 🧶 A Heartwarming International Crochet Gathering

This time, I happened to join a very special cross-border crochet gathering.

People from different countries came together, stitch by stitch, crocheting 'breast forms.'

![一場很溫暖的跨國鉤針聚會](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1781015375794-lady_bumps.jpg)

Photo by Lai Jiaying

These handmade crochet breast forms will all be donated to relevant associations for post-mastectomy women. Compared to regular prosthetics, these knitted ones are lighter, softer, and more skin-friendly and comfortable.

The atmosphere on-site was actually quite quiet. Everyone focused on their crocheting, some chatting, others sharing stories—no overly sentimental words, but a gentle warmth that slowly builds up.

Sometimes the most precious travel memories aren't necessarily about visiting amazing attractions, but rather these small, genuine human connections.

## Day 4｜Riding a Scooter Through Mountain Roads, from Tirta Empul Temple All the Way to Ubud and Sanur 🛵

Day four kicked off with scooter travel mode.
We set off from Sanur sharp at 7 AM, riding straight into central Bali.

Honestly, riding a scooter in Bali gives you a unique sense of freedom.
Along the way, you pass through small villages, rice terraces, temples, and mountain roads. Sometimes, just stopping at a red light and seeing roadside offerings, breakfast stalls, or students riding to school makes you think: 'Ah, this is the real Bali daily life.'

But a heads-up: if you're not used to riding a scooter or unfamiliar with Southeast Asian traffic rhythms, it's much easier to just hire a driver 🤣

### 🙏 Tirta Empul Temple｜Coming early in the morning makes a huge difference

First stop, straight to Tirta Empul Temple.

![聖泉寺｜一大早來真的差很多](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1781015518073-tirta_empul_3.jpg)

Tirta Empul Temple — Photo by Lai Jiaying

We arrived early, so the crowds were still manageable. I'd seen plenty of online photos with long queues, but coming in the morning made a huge difference — both for photos and for the purification ritual experience.

Tirta Empul is famous for its holy water purification. Many locals and tourists line up at different spouts to perform the cleansing ritual.

The atmosphere was actually quite serene. Even with a fair number of visitors, there's a very peaceful energy in the air.

But I have to give you a serious heads-up:

⭐️ No hair dryers. ⭐️ No hair dryers. ⭐️ No hair dryers.

Your hair will just have to air-dry after getting wet 🤣 Luckily, I brought my own towel, or I'd have been miserable riding a scooter in the wind.

I really enjoyed the pace here. There's no need to do anything special—just walk slowly and take it all in.

A few shops also sell local snacks, making them a perfect spot to sit and rest if you're tired from riding.

### 💦 Tegenungan Waterfall｜Thrilling to go down, exhausting to come up🤣

After eating, we headed south back, making a detour to Tegenungan Waterfall along the way.

This waterfall is one of the more accessible ones in Bali. You don't need to hike for ages through the mountains, but you still have to descend into the valley.

As you approach the waterfall, the mist hitting you is truly stunning. The roar of the falls is so loud that you can barely hear anyone standing next to you.

What's special about Bali's waterfalls is that many aren't the 'thin and tall' type—they have a thick, powerful flow.

The only problem is...

Climbing back up is really tiring😆 Especially after eating dirty duck rice, the stairs feel like a test of life.

https://youtu.be/XRR7ddLAiV4

### 🧘‍♀️ Taman Beji Griya Waterfall｜Five-Stage Holy Water Purification Ritual

Our first stop is the increasingly popular purification sanctuary—Taman Beji Griya Waterfall.

![‍ Taman Beji Griya Waterfall｜五階段聖水淨化儀式](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1781016977848-beji.jpg)

Taman Beji Griya Waterfall (Photo by Lai Jiaying)

It's best known for the 'Five-Stage Holy Water Purification Ritual (Melukat).'

Unlike typical waterfalls you visit on your own, the entire process here is guided, from prayers and cleansing to the different stages of holy water blessings—full of ritualistic meaning.

Even if you're not religious, you can still feel how deeply Balinese culture values purification and blessings.

For women joining the ritual, it's easiest to wear a swimsuit underneath.

Beyond Melukat, the site offers various other Balinese healing experiences, such as blessing ceremonies and energy healing sessions, though most require an additional fee.

> [Taman Beji Griya # Soul Retreat & Self-Healing Experience](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1299850&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F75759-retreat-self-healing-purification-trip-bali-aga-taman-beji-griya%2F%3Fspm%3DActivity.SearchSuggest_LIST%26clickId%3Df36411cfec)

When we arrived, I realized this was an experience that tourists could never easily access.

The driver showed me around a traditional Balinese house, introducing the family shrine, the courtyard, and the living spaces of the family members.

Completely different from the Bali you see in hotels and tourist spots.

This is real life.

The driver's mother runs a small shop.

Even though we couldn't really communicate in the same language, you could still feel the overwhelming warmth.

Sometimes the most precious travel memories aren't the sights, but the people.
