## Preparations Before Departure

The goal of this trip was Nusa Penida, an island known for its stunning landscapes. Departing from Bali, we chose to take a boat from Sanur. The new port in Sanur is labeled as 'Sanur Harbour' on Google Maps. The boarding facilities are quite modern, eliminating the worry of getting wet while boarding, which is a great relief for travelers with luggage. We opted for lightweight backpacks, though many passengers carried carry-on suitcases. However, these suitcases are placed at the front of the boat and exposed to the sun during the journey. Upon arrival, you might have to wait under the sun for the crew to unload the luggage, so it's advisable to bring a sun hat and sunscreen.

## Experience During the Voyage

The boat ride takes about 30 to 45 minutes, which might be challenging for those prone to seasickness. The waves make the boat rock significantly, so it's recommended to take motion sickness medication beforehand. Personally, I had no issues and even enjoyed the journey. The sea breeze was refreshing, and I quickly fell asleep on the boat, finding the voyage quite relaxing.

## Arrival at Nusa Penida

Upon arriving at Nusa Penida, we immediately saw a fee collection point at the entrance to the left of the dock. According to locals, this fee is used to maintain the island's environment and facilities, while residents are exempt. Remember to arrange your shuttle in advance, as the port area is quite congested. We waited about 30 minutes to leave the port - a distance that would take only 2 minutes to walk, but vehicle traffic was heavy. However, the locals seemed accustomed to this situation, patiently waiting for vehicles to clear, demonstrating their calm and polite nature.

## The Challenges of Island Transportation

The roads on Nusa Penida are quite narrow, making passing another vehicle a thrilling experience, often requiring pulling over to let the other vehicle pass. Although the roads are paved, they're uneven, making the ride feel like a rollercoaster full of excitement. This journey gave us a full appreciation of the island's transportation challenges, but also added to the fun and sense of adventure.

## Exploring the Island's Beauty and Cuisine

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d8505be114d780f2f4c578a7fcfc798d.png)

The scenery of Nusa Penida is absolutely not to be missed. Whether it's the famous Kelingking Beach or Angel's Billabong, both are breathtaking. These spots not only boast magnificent natural landscapes but also crystal-clear waters and spectacular cliff views that are unforgettable.

Additionally, the island's cuisine is a highlight. We sampled local seafood dishes, which were fresh and delicious, whetting our appetites. Particularly, the local grilled fish and seafood platters are distinctive delicacies, not only tasty but also very reasonably priced.

## Overnight Options on Nusa Penida

Visiting Nusa Penida is absolutely worth it, and we recommend staying for a night or two rather than just doing a day trip. This will give you extra time to fully experience this enchanting island. While a day trip is a great way to see the most popular tourist spots, staying longer allows you more time to explore and discover hidden gems.

## Reflections on the Return Journey

After a delightful few days on Nusa Penida, we prepared to take the boat back to Bali. For the return trip, we once again opted for light luggage, which made our journey more relaxed and enjoyable. Although the boat ride was still bumpy, we had grown accustomed to the sensation and even began to enjoy this maritime adventure.

## Conclusion

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_bc858943113e1373efd0c30989c2c3c2.png)

Our trip to Nusa Penida left us deeply impressed by the grandeur of nature and the warmth of the locals. Whether it was the thrill of sailing on the sea or the enjoyment of the island's scenic beauty and cuisine, everything was unforgettable. We are very grateful for the information shared by fellow travelers beforehand, and this trip has been incredibly rewarding. We hope this travelogue proves helpful to you.
