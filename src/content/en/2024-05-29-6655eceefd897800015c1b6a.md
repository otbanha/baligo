## Pre-Trip Preparations

This trip's destination is Nusa Penida, an island renowned for its breathtaking landscapes. Departing from Bali, we opted to take a boat from Sanur. The new port in Sanur is labeled as 'Sanur Harbour' on Google Maps. The boarding facilities are now well-developed, eliminating the worry of getting your feet wet when boarding—a great relief for travelers with luggage. We chose to travel light with backpacks, though many passengers brought carry-on suitcases. However, these suitcases are placed on the front deck of the boat, exposed to the sun during the journey. Upon arrival, you’ll need to wait for the boat crew to unload the luggage, which might leave you under the scorching sun for a while. Therefore, it’s advisable to bring a sun hat and sunscreen.

## In-Flight Experience

The boat ride takes about 30 to 45 minutes, but it can be a bit challenging for those prone to seasickness. The waves make the boat rock quite a bit, so it's advisable to take motion sickness medication beforehand. Personally, I had no issues with it and actually enjoyed the journey. With the sea breeze gently blowing, I quickly fell asleep on the boat, making the trip a relaxing experience for me.

## Arriving at Nusa Penida

Upon arriving at Nusa Penida Island, we immediately noticed a fee collection point to the left of the dock entrance. According to locals, this fee is used to maintain the island's environment and facilities, and residents are exempt. Remember to arrange your shuttle in advance, as the port area is quite congested. We waited about 30 minutes before leaving the port — a distance that would take only 2 minutes to walk, but the constant flow of vehicles made it chaotic. However, the locals seemed accustomed to this, patiently waiting for the traffic to clear, showcasing their calm and courteous demeanor.

## Navigating Bali's Transportation Challenges

The roads on Nusa Penida are quite narrow, making it quite thrilling when two vehicles pass each other, often requiring one to pull over and wait for the other to go first. Although the roads are paved, they are far from smooth, and riding in a car feels like being on a roller coaster, full of excitement. This journey gave us a full appreciation of the island's transportation challenges, but it also added to the fun and sense of adventure of our trip.

## Exploring the Island's Scenic Beauty and Culinary Delights

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d8505be114d780f2f4c578a7fcfc798d.png)

The scenery of Nusa Penida is absolutely unmissable. Whether it's the famous Kelingking Beach or Angel's Billabong, both are truly breathtaking. These attractions not only boast magnificent natural landscapes but also crystal-clear waters and spectacular cliff views that will leave you enchanted.

Moreover, the island's cuisine is a major highlight. We sampled local seafood dishes that were fresh and delicious, truly whetting our appetites. Particularly noteworthy were the local grilled fish and seafood platters, which are both distinctive delicacies. Not only are they incredibly flavorful, but they're also very reasonably priced.

## Overnight Options on Nusa Penida

Visiting Nusa Penida is absolutely worthwhile, and it's recommended to stay for a night or two rather than opting for a day trip. This will give you extra time to fully experience this enchanting island. While a day trip is a great way to see the most popular tourist spots, staying longer allows you more time to explore and uncover hidden treasures.

## The Journey Back

After spending a delightful few days on Nusa Penida, we were ready to board the boat back to Bali. For the return trip, we once again opted for light luggage, which made our journey even more relaxed and enjoyable. Although the boat ride was still bumpy, we had grown accustomed to the sensation and even began to savor this maritime adventure.
