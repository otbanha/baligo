## The Cliff Crack Crisis: Origins and Development

---

The issue of cracks in Uluwatu Temple's cliff dates back to 1992, when a powerful earthquake struck Bali, causing significant structural damage to the cliff. Although the local government recognized the risks, for 32 years, they only limited visitor numbers without implementing substantial reinforcement measures.

Over the years, authorities have continuously monitored the cracks through drone surveillance and expert assessments. However, by September 2023, monitoring results indicated signs of crack expansion, prompting the government to urgently initiate restoration plans to ensure the temple's long-term safety.

## Restoration Plan: From Traditional Rituals to Engineering Works

---

Before officially commencing construction, the local government held the "Mulang Pakelem" religious ceremony to pray for the smooth progress of the project and divine blessings. I Wayan Adi Arnawa, Secretary of Badung Regency, stated, "We hope that through collective efforts, we can ensure the safety and stability of Uluwatu Temple."

The restoration project is led by the Badung Public Works and Spatial Planning Department (PUPR), with specific construction tasks including:

修復工程由 ****巴東公共工程與空間規劃部（PUPR）**** 領導，具體施工內容包括：

✔ ****Fill cracks and reinforce the foundation structure****

✔ ****Install 6-meter-high support piles at the base of the cliff****

✔ ****Construct access roads to the base of the cliff****

The project received government funding of ****7.86 billion Indonesian Rupiah (approximately £395,000)**** and has been designated as a ****top-priority infrastructure initiative**** for the local area.

---

## ****Challenges of Wave Erosion and Reinforcement Measures****

****Water Resources Department Head A.A. Rama Putra noted**** that one of the ****biggest challenges**** faced by the project is the ****pressure of marine erosion****. Typically, Bali's waves move from ****west to east****, but recently, the ****impact of southern waves**** has accelerated cliff erosion, further weakening the rock structure.

To mitigate the effects of erosion, the government has deployed ****2,800 tetrapods**** as wave barriers, placed approximately ****10 meters**** offshore. Additionally, restoration methods include:

✔ ****Filling rock cracks with specialized concrete****

✔ ****Spraying reinforcing agents to strengthen vulnerable areas****

✔ ****Installing metal mesh for additional support****

As of now, out of the planned restoration of ****1,100 square meters of cliff surface****, ****500 square meters**** have been reinforced.

---

## ****Construction Delays and Environmental Controversy****

The project was originally scheduled for completion by ****December 31, 2024****, but due to complex construction conditions, the timeline has been extended by ****50 days****, with the latest estimated completion date set for ****February 2025****.

However, this project is not without controversy. Community members have expressed concerns about the restoration process, as the construction team ****removed portions of the limestone cliff****, leading to ****the collapse of nearby rock walls****, further exacerbating environmental issues.

Additionally, ****the Indonesian Attorney General's Office launched an investigation in October 2024****, questioning whether the project had obtained formal approval for the ****Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)****. If it is confirmed that construction began without an assessment, it could involve ****significant regulatory violations****.

More worryingly, some online videos show that after excavating the cliff, the construction team ****directly dumped excess limestone into the sea****, raising further concerns about ****marine ecosystem destruction****.
