## The Cliff Crack Crisis: Origins and Development

---

The issue of cracks in Uluwatu Temple's cliff dates back to 1992, when a powerful earthquake struck Bali, causing noticeable structural damage to the cliff. Although local authorities recognized the risk, they only limited visitor numbers over the next 32 years without implementing substantial reinforcement measures.

Over the years, authorities have monitored the cracks through drone surveillance and expert assessments. However, by September 2023, monitoring results indicated signs of widening cracks, prompting the government to urgently initiate restoration plans to ensure the temple's long-term safety.

## Restoration Plan: From Traditional Rituals to Construction Work

---

Before officially commencing construction, the local government held a "Mulang Pakelem" religious ceremony to pray for the smooth progress of the project and divine blessings. I Wayan Adi Arnawa, Secretary of Badung Regency, stated: "We hope that through collective efforts, we can ensure the safety and stability of Uluwatu Temple."

The restoration project is led by the Badung Public Works and Spatial Planning Department (PUPR), with specific construction tasks including:

修復工程由 **巴東公共工程與空間規劃部（PUPR）** 領導，具體施工內容包括：

✔ **Fill cracks and reinforce foundation structures**

✔ **Install 6-meter-high support piles at the base of the cliff**

✔ **Construct access roads to the base of the cliff**

The project received government funding of **IDR 7.86 billion (approximately USD 500,000)** and was designated as a **top-priority infrastructure initiative** for the region.

---

## **Challenges of Wave Erosion and Reinforcement Measures**

**Water Resources Department Head A.A. Rama Putra noted** that one of the **biggest challenges** of the project is the **pressure of marine erosion**. Typically, Bali's waves move from **west to east**, but recently, the **impact of southern waves** has accelerated cliff erosion, further weakening the rock structure.

To mitigate erosion effects, the government has deployed **2,800 tetrapods** as wave barriers, placed approximately **10 meters** offshore. Additionally, restoration methods include:

✔ **Filling rock cracks with specialized concrete**

✔ **Spraying reinforcement agents to strengthen vulnerable areas**

✔ **Installing metal mesh for additional support**

As of now, **1,100 square meters of the cliff surface** planned for restoration have been reinforced, with **500 square meters** already completed.

---

## **Construction Delays and Environmental Controversies**

The project was originally scheduled for completion by **December 31, 2024**, but due to complex construction conditions, the timeline has been extended by **50 days**, with the latest estimated completion date set for **February 2025**.

However, the project has not been without controversy. Community members have expressed concerns about the restoration process, as the construction team **removed parts of the limestone cliff**, leading to **collapses in nearby rock walls** and exacerbating environmental issues.

Additionally, **the Indonesian Attorney General's Office launched an investigation in October 2024**, questioning whether the project had obtained formal approval for its **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)**. If it is confirmed that construction began without the assessment, it could constitute a **major violation**.

More alarmingly, some online videos show that after excavating the cliff, the construction team **directly dumped excess limestone into the sea**, further raising concerns about **marine ecosystem damage**.

## **Conclusion: The Future of Uluwatu Temple**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_63da293a2e7ca055c3aa81845d8a3e2e.jpg)

Despite facing **numerous challenges and controversies**, the Bali government and expert teams are actively advancing the restoration plan to ensure the **structural safety of Uluwatu Temple**. This is not only crucial for preserving religious and cultural heritage but also has a profound impact on the **sustainable development of local tourism**.

As the project enters its final stages, people are hopeful that **this historic temple** will **regain its vitality in 2025**, offering a **safe and stable pilgrimage and tourism experience** for decades to come.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

### **1. Why is Uluwatu Temple so important?**

Uluwatu Temple is **one of Bali's six holiest temples**, with a history spanning over **a thousand years**, serving as a significant landmark for local Hindu beliefs.

### **2. When is the restoration project expected to be completed?**

The project is currently scheduled for completion in **February 2025**, delayed by **50 days** from the original plan.

### **3. Does the restoration work affect tourism activities?**

Certain areas are restricted, but Uluwatu Temple **remains open to visitors**. Please follow official announcements for the latest updates.

### **4. Is the project causing environmental impact?**

Reports indicate that some limestone was **directly dumped into the ocean**, and the government is currently under **investigation by Indonesia's Attorney General** to determine if environmental laws were violated.
