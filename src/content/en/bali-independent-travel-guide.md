Bali, a land of stunning scenery, draws travelers from around the world. In our Facebook group, [Chihming Lai](https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100008235455950)—not a travel professional or agency employee—makes a special trip to Bali every six months to unwind, soaking up the sun, beer, beaches, and bikinis. For him, Bali's unique slow-living vibe is its greatest charm. Below, he generously shares his firsthand travel experiences and practical tips.

(The following content is adapted and republished with permission from [Chihming Lai](https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100008235455950).)

## **Pre-Departure Preparations:**

Before you go, he recommends **applying for an e-Visa online**. With a Taiwan passport, you can use facial recognition at immigration for fast clearance without queuing.

After collecting your luggage and before exiting the airport, you'll need to have your **electronic customs declaration form** ready. Be sure to complete it in advance—just scan it at the checkpoint to save significant time. This is crucial.

As for the **tourist tax**, he feels it's a personal choice whether to pay. He personally supports traffic improvement policies and eco-friendly activities like sea turtle releases, contributing to Bali's tourism. It's only about USD$10—the cost of a few beers.

## **Upon Arrival in Bali:**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e13870a67da8a8539a2df155af5a63e5.jpg)

1. Don’t overpack your itinerary—since you’re only passing through, it’s better to slow down, embrace the local slow-living vibe, wander aimlessly, sip a cold drink, and give your soul a breather.
2. Right now, Bali offers a relatively peaceful environment, and at least the beaches are much less polluted.
3. When asked about must-see spots, he suggests that while Monkey Forest and Ubud Palace are worth a visit, five minutes is enough—no need to linger. Instead, cross the road from the palace to Ubud Market, where you might find more fun wandering around. As for the rice terraces, they’re visible along the way, so you can choose to visit them or not. In short, the journey itself is the scenery—just focus on enjoying it and relaxing.

## **Accommodation & Itinerary Planning Tips:**

1. He strongly advises villa guests not to pack their schedules too tight—that would waste the villa’s purpose and cost. A villa embodies leisure: enjoy the pool and sunshine, sip a drink, and head out only when you feel like it. Why rush out early and return at dusk? Some guests even stay just one or two nights before switching accommodations, which is too exhausting. When staying in a villa, the point is to rest, admire the scenery, and savor downtime without overexertion.
2. The length of your Bali trip should dictate your itinerary—avoid a whirlwind tour. Traffic here is often congested, roads are narrow and tricky, and hitting all the popular spots is nearly impossible.
3. He always travels independently, skipping hotels and staying in villas for the entire trip without switching. The cost of a 10-day villa stay is roughly equivalent to a 5-day, 4-night hotel stay in Taiwan. Independent travel offers more flexibility, free from package tour constraints.
4. Language barriers aren’t a big worry—a friendly smile is often the best communication tool. Plus, you can use your phone as a handy translator.
