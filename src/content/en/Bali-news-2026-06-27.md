## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: BMKG Extends Extreme Wave Safety Alert, Tourist Zones Launch Full-Scale 'Cashless Payment Transition' Inspections

- **Sea Excursions Tightened! BMKG Officially Extends 'Extreme Wind and Wave Warning' for Southern Waters and Island Routes**: In a late-night maritime bulletin issued yesterday (June 26), the Bali Meteorology, Climate, and Geophysics Agency (BMKG) announced an emergency extension of the 'Moderate to Extreme Wave Advisory' for southern waters, originally set to end this week. Maritime authorities report that affected danger zones include the southern open sea, the cliffs along Uluwatu, and the Selat Badung strait—a key route for independent travelers. In some offshore areas, breaking waves could reach 3.5 to 4.0 meters. While fixed-speed ferries from Sanur Harbor (Pelabuhan Sanur) to Nusa Penida and Nusa Lembongan on inner-sea routes remain conditionally operational, rough seas have caused intermittent severe turbulence and delays. The harbor authority issued a special warning: independent travelers planning snorkeling, diving, or speedboat trips to the islands this weekend must confirm sailing status with operators beforehand and absolutely avoid entering the water at dangerous beaches marked with red flags.

- **Cracking Down on Black Markets and Tax Evasion! Official Inspection Teams Deploy to Canggu and Seminyak, Pushing 'Cashless Digital Payment Transition' in Core Tourist Zones**: According to a joint municipal announcement from the Bali Regional Finance Office and Tourism Office yesterday, to enhance tourism market transparency and curb underground finance and tax evasion, official enforcement teams have begun deploying to core commercial areas in Canggu, Seminyak, and Ubud. They are conducting large-scale, routine on-site inspections to enforce a 'full transition to cashless digital payments' among local businesses. The focus is on ensuring that popular restaurants, boutique design villas, SPA centers, and car rental shops have all installed compliant Indonesian National QR Codes (QRIS) or electronic card terminals. Authorities are strictly checking for businesses that falsely claim 'machine malfunctions' to secretly guide international tourists into using undeclared cash for black-market transactions. Officials emphasize that this move not only secures tourism tax revenue for infrastructure improvements like traffic congestion but also significantly reduces the security risk for foreign tourists carrying large amounts of Indonesian rupiah in busy streets, which can lead to snatch thefts or pickpocketing.
