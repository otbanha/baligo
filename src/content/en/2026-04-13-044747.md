Don't worry, you're not alone!

Recently, a question on social media 'Sent from the airport on 2/13 and still haven't received it' unexpectedly brought out a lot of 'long waiting experiences' from fellow travelers. Today, we'll help you compile these real cases to let you know—**waiting for months is actually the norm!**

***

## 📬 Real reports from netizens: How long does it really take?

One netizen shared that they sent a postcard in October and received it in February the next year, waiting about 4 months.

Another said their experience from a few years ago was receiving it after 4 months.

Another mentioned that it took about almost two months to receive theirs.

Someone else sent theirs last year and it arrived in about a month and a half.

Another netizen shared that she mailed multiple postcards from Ubud Post Office in different years, and all of them arrived successfully. She specifically mentioned that when mailed simultaneously, Europe usually receives them in about a month, while Asia takes an additional half to one month longer.

***

Another person asked the front desk of a five-star hotel to mail a postcard for them when they checked out in October 2023, even paying for postage, but it still hasn't arrived.

## ✨ Tips for Successful Delivery

Based on netizens' experiences, here are some tips to increase success rates or reduce anxiety:

**Take photos first**: Snap pictures of the front of the postcard and the address before mailing — at least you'll have a digital keepsake if it doesn't arrive.

**Try to use post offices**: Some netizens shared that mailing from Ubud Post Office resulted in all postcards arriving, making it more reliable than using airport or hotel services.

**Be Prepared**: It's normal for the waiting period to take anywhere from 1.5 to 4 months, so don't panic if you don't receive it within a week or two after returning home.

**Send Separately**: If the postcard is really important, you can send it at different times and from different locations to spread the risk.
