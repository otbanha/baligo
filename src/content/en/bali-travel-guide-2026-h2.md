# 【2026 Bali Travel Outlook】From Peak Dry Season to the Onset of Rain: A Complete Guide to Festivals and Weather from July to December

**Bottom line**: The best time to visit Bali in the second half of 2026 is **July–August** (peak dry season, humidity as low as 10–20%, only 1–3 rainy days—prime time for water sports and hiking). **September–October** is still excellent for travel, though rain starts to increase. **November–December** marks the onset of the rainy season, with noticeably more rain but fewer crowds and lush green scenery—ideal for those looking to avoid the masses. For the New Year's Eve peak, book early to avoid price gouging.

Planning a trip to **Bali** in the second half of 2026? This period sees the island shift from ultra-dry bliss to a lush, colorful phase. You might enjoy the clearest waters and bluest skies in July, or welcome the fresh greenery brought by November rains.

To help you travel like a pro, we've combined climate data with local festivals to create this 'July–December Travel Outlook.'

***

# 🌦️ Climate & Seasons: From Peak Dry Season to the Rainy Season Transition

![氣候與季節：乾季巔峰到雨季預告的轉換](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780917212578-mixcollage-08-jun-2026-05-52-pm-8861.jpg)

These six months showcase Bali's full journey from the peak of the dry season to the brink of the rainy season:

- **July–August (Peak Dry Season):** The driest months of the year, with the lowest humidity (10–20%) and minimal rain (1–3 days). Comfortable temperatures (24–31°C) make for the best feeling—perfect for water activities and hiking.
- **September–October (Late Dry Season):** Plenty of sunshine remains, but rain starts to increase (2–6 days). Temperatures rise slightly (24–31°C), humidity edges up (15–30%), but it's still a great time to travel.
- **November–December (Rainy Season Prelude to Early Phase):** Rain becomes more frequent (10–15 days), humidity climbs (50–60%), while temperatures hold steady (24–31°C). This is the low season, but it has its own charm—fewer tourists, lush landscapes, and the best time to avoid crowds.

# 📅 Month-by-Month Deep Dive: Festivals & Tips to Avoid Pitfalls

## **July: A Month of Island Kites and Soaring Art**

- **Climate Overview:** July is the absolute peak of the dry season. Rainfall is extremely rare, with humidity at just 10–20% and average temperatures ranging from 24–31°C. Days are brilliantly sunny, while nights are cool and comfortable, making it the driest and most pleasant month of the year. The rare morning mountain views and excellent ocean visibility make this a dream period for outdoor enthusiasts.
- **Packing Tips:** Light, breathable cotton clothing is essential, along with high-SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+) and sunglasses. For early morning hikes, a lightweight long-sleeve sun-protection jacket is recommended.

![7 月：島嶼風箏與藝術飛揚的月份](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780922788814-7.jpg)

### **Major Festivals:**

- **July 10–12: Penglipuran Village Festival:** Held in the ancient traditional village of Penglipuran, this festival showcases Bali Aga culture, handicrafts, local cuisine, and cultural performances. Visitors can deeply experience the unique charm of this traditional village.
- **July 11–25: Festival Bali Jani (Bali Contemporary Art Festival):** A creative festival focusing on modern and contemporary Balinese art, featuring new forms of artistic collaboration and cross-disciplinary creativity.
- **July 18–19: Jatiluwih Festival:** Held at the UNESCO World Heritage Jatiluwih Rice Terraces, this event combines cultural performances, natural beauty, local agricultural products, and hiking activities. The terraces are most enchanting in the early morning light of July, perfect for a leisurely dawn stroll.
- **July 24–26: Lovina Festival:** A cultural feast in the coastal town of Lovina in North Bali, featuring stage performances, a traditional market, and a fireworks show.
- **July 26–30: Bali International Choir Festival:** An international choir competition and concert series that brings together voices from around the world.
- **July–October: Makepung Buffalo Race Season:** A traditional farming culture performance in the Jembrana region of West Bali, where buffaloes pull wooden chariots in high-speed races—a thrilling spectacle.
- **July–August: Bali Kite Festival:** Held at Sanur Beach, participants from around the world compete with giant traditional kites. July offers the most stable weather for kite flying.

### **Recommended July Travel Destinations**

- **Jatiluwih Rice Terraces:** July is the best time to view the terraces, with early morning light streaming through the rice paddies creating a dreamlike scene. Besides attending the Jatiluwih Festival, you can also take a leisurely walk, ride a scooter around the area, or join a rice field yoga session.

## **August: A Lively Season of Music Festivals and Independence Celebrations**

- **Weather Overview:** August continues the dry season with excellent weather. Expect only 1–3 rainy days, humidity at 10–20%, and average temperatures of 24–31°C. Like July, it's one of the driest months of the year. Plenty of sunshine and clear waters make it perfect for all water sports and outdoor activities. The only difference is that peak season crowds start to pour in, so early booking for accommodations and experiences is recommended.
- **Packing Tips:** Same as July: lightweight, breathable clothing, high-SPF sunscreen, and sunglasses are essential. Carry sunscreen for reapplication throughout the day.

- **8/24 Maybank Marathon:** Bali's largest road running event, including full marathon, half marathon, and short-distance categories, perfect for sports enthusiasts.

- **August: Makepung Bupati Cup (Buffalo Race):** The premier buffalo racing competition in the Jembrana region.

## **September: The Transition from Dry to Rainy Season**

- **Climate Overview:** September marks a slight shift. Rainfall increases to 2–4 days, humidity rises to 15–30%, and average temperatures remain at 24–31°C. Though still part of the dry season, clouds start to build, with occasional brief showers that come and go quickly. Mornings and evenings stay refreshing, while midday remains hot.
- **Packing Tips:** Keep your sun protection from July–August, but start carrying a lightweight foldable umbrella or rain jacket for sudden showers.
- **Avoiding Crowds:** Late September nears October, which sees the peak of the 'transition season' and more travelers. To dodge the crowds, plan your trip for early September.

- **September 4–6: Taman Ayun Barong Festival:** Held at the Taman Ayun Royal Temple, this Barong dance festival showcases Bali's most iconic traditional performances and ceremonial culture. A must-experience if you want to dive deep into Balinese dance.
- **September 5: Tumpek Kandang (Livestock Thanksgiving):** The second time in the year (first in February) that Bali thanks its livestock. You'll see heartwarming scenes of animals being bathed, decorated, and blessed in villages.
- **September 24–27: Bali International Airshow:** Showcasing the latest aircraft and aviation technology from around the world, with flight displays, exhibitions, and industry networking—perfect for tech enthusiasts.
- **September 27: Nyepi Laut (Ocean Day of Silence):** A special 'Ocean Day of Silence' observed on Bali's three main offshore islands (Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, and Nusa Ceningan). All boat services to and from the islands are suspended, so plan your itinerary well in advance.
- **September: Lembongan Surf and Music Festival:** Held on the offshore island of Nusa Lembongan, combining surfing events, live music, and beach culture.
- **September: Buleleng Fashion Festival:** Showcasing North Bali's designers and creative industries.
- **September: Parade Budaya Jembrana:** Displaying West Bali's culture, costumes, and regional specialties.

## **October: The 'Transition Season' Previews Peak Travel**

- **Climate Overview:** October marks the official shift from the dry to the rainy season. Rainfall increases to 5–9 days, humidity rises to 30–50%, and average temperatures range from 25–30°C. Days may still be sunny, but brief afternoon showers become more frequent, and cloud cover thickens. Despite this, October is part of Bali's 'Big Five' peak seasons—the 'Transition Season'—and visitor numbers start to climb noticeably.
- **Packing Tips:** In addition to sun protection and a hat, be sure to carry a foldable umbrella or lightweight raincoat. Quick-dry cotton clothing is more practical than summer wear.
- **Avoiding Crowds:** After mid-October, tourist numbers surge, and hotel and car rental prices rise accordingly. It's best to travel in early October, or opt for late September instead.

![10 月：「過渡季」的旅遊旺季預告](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780923758088-10.jpg)

### **Major Festivals:**

- **October 5-7 Nusa Penida Festival:** Held on the famous cliff-lined offshore island, this event focuses on culture, nature conservation, marine themes, and tourism promotion. You can enjoy stunning scenery while soaking up the local festive atmosphere.
- **October 10 Tumpek Wayang (Puppet Thanksgiving):** The second of two annual blessings for shadow puppets (the first is in March). This is a great opportunity to enjoy traditional shadow puppet performances.
- **October 10-11 Klungkung Heritage Festival:** Focuses on the history, culture, exhibitions, and stage performances of the Klungkung region.
- **October 10-12 Gebug Seraya Festival:** Held in Karangasem, this unique festival centers on the traditional ritual stick-fighting event 'Gebug Seraya.'
- **October 21-25 Ubud Writers & Readers Festival:** An international literary event that brings together writers, thinkers, and cultural figures from around the world for talks, discussions, and creative performances. If you're a literature lover, this festival is a must-attend.
- **October 31 Hari Raya Saraswati (Knowledge Goddess Day) & Pagerwesi (Wisdom Day):** The second annual celebration of knowledge and wisdom (the first is in April). October 31 is Knowledge Goddess Day, and November 4 is Wisdom Day, with four days between them.
- **October Tulamben Ocean Harmony Festival:** Held in the diving haven of Tulamben, this event combines marine conservation themes with water activities.
- **October Bali Hockey Festival:** A hockey sports festival or tournament.

## **November: Lush Greenery and Quiet Moments at the Start of the Rainy Season**

- **Weather Overview:** November marks the beginning of the rainy season. Rainfall increases significantly to 10–15 days, with humidity rising to 50–70% and average temperatures between 24–31°C. Rain typically falls in the afternoon and evening, while mornings remain relatively stable. The abundant moisture makes vegetation lush and vibrant, with rice paddies showing their most beautiful green hues. Correspondingly, tourist numbers drop sharply, and accommodation prices fall, making it the best time to avoid crowds.
- **Packing Tips:** Rain gear is essential. Bring a waterproof jacket, rain cover for your backpack, and water shoes. Cotton-blend, moisture-wicking clothing is more practical than pure cotton. Evenings can bring a cool breeze, so a light long-sleeved jacket comes in handy.
- **Travel Tips:** The rainy season doesn't mean you can't travel. Mornings are usually clear, with rain arriving in the afternoon and evening. Plan activities for the morning and return to your hotel for rest or a spa in the afternoon. Travel experiences during this time are often more immersive, with fewer tourists and more personalized service.

- **November Ulun Danu Festival:** Held around the Ulun Danu Temple on Lake Bratan, featuring cultural performances, rituals, and stunning natural scenery. The lake and temple create a picturesque landscape.

## **December: Mid-Rainy Season Prelude to Christmas and New Year**

- **Weather Overview:** December is the middle of the rainy season. Rainfall remains at 10–15 days (similar to November), with humidity at 60–80% and average temperatures of 24–31°C. Rain is more frequent but usually comes as afternoon showers, with mornings and evenings still relatively dry. Temperatures rise slightly, making it feel more muggy. Despite the rainy season, the end of December near Christmas and New Year sees an influx of international tourists and domestic Indonesian holidaymakers, creating the 'Nataru' (Christmas-New Year) peak season.
- **Packing Tips:** Rain gear is essential, but due to the humid heat, opt for loose, breathable clothing. Use a waterproof backpack or dry bag to protect electronics. Quick-dry clothes are more practical than long garments.
- **Avoid the Crowds:** From December 20 to January 5, it's the peak 'Nataru' holiday period. Hotel and car rental prices may double, traffic is heavy, and attractions are crowded. If you enjoy the festive buzz, join the celebrations; if you seek tranquility, avoid the period after December 20.

![12 月：雨季中期的耶誕與新年序曲](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780924308707-12.jpg)

### **Major Festivals:**

Churches and Christian communities hold prayers and celebrations. It also marks the start of the 'Nataru' holiday period.
- **December 25 – Pandawa Beach Festival:** A beach festival at Pandawa Beach, combining stage performances and festive activities with Christmas, attracting many visitors. The beautiful beach paired with traditional performances is worth experiencing.
- **December – Bali Rockin' Blues Festival:** A rock and blues music festival held on the beach, complete with food and drink stalls.
- **December – Denpasar Festival:** The year-end main city festival in Denpasar, featuring concerts, markets, and cultural programs to celebrate the end of the year and welcome the new one.
- **December – Besakih Festival:** Held near Besakih Temple, Bali's mother temple, this festival aims to promote local tourism and culture. The temple is the most sacred on the island, and during the festival, many devotees come to pray and seek blessings.

## Smart Travel Tips from Our Editors 💡

![小編的聰明旅遊筆記](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780924530878-15.jpg)

### **Seasonal Strategy:**

- **For Dry Weather & High Visibility:** July–August is the best choice overall, but it's also the most crowded and expensive. 
- **For Savings & Experience:** September to early October is the "golden transition period"—still great weather, fewer crowds, and reasonable prices. 
- **For Avoiding Crowds & Going Deeper:** November is a hidden gem. Early rainy season, lush green landscapes, few tourists, and affordable prices—perfect for in-depth travel. 
- **For Festivities & Celebrations:** December's Christmas and New Year period is a festive peak, but prices double and attractions are packed.

### **Debunking the Rainy Season Myth:**

- Rainy season ≠ all-day rain. It's usually just afternoon showers; mornings and nights stay dry. 
- Rainy season = lush green Bali. The landscapes are at their most beautiful, offering the best photo opportunities. 
- Rainy season = off-peak deals. Hotel and spa prices are negotiable, often leading to a more personalized travel experience.

### **Booking Tips:**

- For July–August and mid-to-late December, book at least three months in advance, especially in Ubud and Sanur. 
- For specific festivals (Jazz Festival, Writers Festival, Spirit Festival), book one month ahead. 
- In November, you can book last minute with more choices and better prices.

![預約建議：](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780924627221-16.jpg)

### **Transportation Tips:**

- In July–August and mid-to-late December, car rental and private driver prices rise by 20–30%, and vehicles are hard to secure. 
- January offers smooth traffic and reasonable prices—the best time for self-driving or hiring a driver. 
- During the rainy season, if your itinerary includes mountainous areas or outlying islands, allow extra time for weather-related delays.

### **Festival Etiquette:**

- Dress appropriately when attending temple ceremonies (sarong and sash are ideal). 
- Ask locals or your guide before taking photos, and respect sacred rituals. 
- Some festivals prohibit outsiders from entering the inner sanctum—please be respectful.
