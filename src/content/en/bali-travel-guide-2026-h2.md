# 【2026 Bali Travel Outlook】From Dry Season Peak to Rainy Season Arrival: A Complete Guide to Festivals & Weather from July to December

**Bottom line first**: The best departure window in the second half of 2026 is **July–August** (peak dry season, humidity as low as 10–20%, only 1–3 rainy days, prime time for water sports and hiking); **September–October** remains a great travel period but with increasing rainfall; **November–December** marks the prelude to the rainy season, with noticeably more rain but fewer tourists and lush green scenery—ideal for those looking to avoid crowds. For the New Year's peak season, book early to avoid price hikes and scams.

Planning to fly to **Bali** in the second half of 2026? This period is when the island transitions from ultimate dryness to a vibrant, wetter phase. You might enjoy the clearest waters and bluest skies of the year in July, or welcome the fresh greenery brought by November's rains.

To help you travel like a seasoned pro, we've combined climate data with local festivals to create this 'July–December Travel Outlook.'

***

# 🌦️ Climate & Seasons: Transition from Dry Season Peak to Rainy Season Preview

![氣候與季節：乾季巔峰到雨季預告的轉換](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780917212578-mixcollage-08-jun-2026-05-52-pm-8861.jpg)

These six months showcase Bali's complete journey from the peak of the dry season to the onset of the rainy season:

- **July–August (Peak Dry Season):** The driest months of the year, with the lowest humidity (10–20%) and minimal rainfall (1–3 days). Comfortable temperatures (24–31°C) make for the best overall feel—prime season for water activities and hiking.
- **September–October (Late Dry Season):** Plenty of sunshine remains, but rainfall starts to increase (2–6 days). Temperatures rise slightly (24–31°C), humidity edges up (15–30%), yet it's still an excellent travel period.
- **November–December (Rainy Season Prelude to Early Phase):** Rainfall increases significantly (10–15 days), humidity rises (50–60%), while temperatures hold steady (24–31°C). This is the low season but has its own charm—fewer tourists, lush green landscapes, and the best time to avoid crowds.

# 📅 Monthly Deep Dive: Festivals & Tips to Avoid Pitfalls

## **July: A Month of Island Kites and Soaring Art**

- **Climate Overview:** July is the absolute peak of the dry season. Rainfall is extremely rare, with humidity at just 10–20% and average temperatures ranging from 24–31°C. Days are brilliantly sunny, while nights are cool and comfortable, making it the driest and most pleasant month of the year. The rare morning mountain views and excellent ocean visibility make this a dream period for outdoor enthusiasts.
- **Packing Tips:** Lightweight, breathable cotton clothing is essential, along with high-SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+) and sunglasses. For early morning hikes, a thin, long-sleeved sun-protection jacket is recommended.

![7 月：島嶼風箏與藝術飛揚的月份](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780922788814-7.jpg)

### **Major Festivals:**

- **July 10–12: Penglipuran Village Festival:** Held in the ancient traditional village of Penglipuran, this event showcases Balinese Aga culture, handicrafts, local cuisine, and cultural performances. Visitors can deeply experience the unique charm of this traditional village.
- **July 11–25: Bali Jani Festival:** A creative festival focusing on modern and contemporary Balinese art, featuring new forms of artistic collaboration and cross-disciplinary creativity.
- **July 18–19: Jatiluwih Festival:** Set in the UNESCO World Heritage Jatiluwih Rice Terraces, this festival combines cultural performances, natural beauty, local agricultural products, and hiking activities. The terraces are most enchanting in the early morning light of July, perfect for a leisurely stroll.
- **July 24–26: Lovina Festival:** A cultural feast in the coastal town of Lovina in North Bali, featuring stage performances, a traditional market, and a fireworks show.
- **July 26–30: Bali International Choir Festival:** An international choir competition and concert series, bringing together voices from around the world.
- **July–October: Makepung Buffalo Race Season:** A traditional farming culture performance in the Jembrana region of West Bali, where buffaloes pull wooden chariots at high speed in a thrilling race.
- **July–August: Bali Kite Festival:** Held on Sanur Beach, participants from around the world compete with giant traditional kites. July offers the most stable weather for kite flying.

### **Recommended July Travel Destinations**

- **Jatiluwih Rice Terraces:** July is the best time to view the terraces, with the morning light filtering through the rice paddies creating a dreamlike scene. Besides attending the Jatiluwih Festival, you can also take a leisurely walk, rent a scooter to explore, or join a rice field yoga session.

## **August: A Lively Season of Music Festivals and Independence Celebrations**

- **Climate Overview:** August continues the dry season with excellent weather. Expect only 1–3 rainy days, humidity at 10–20%, and average temperatures of 24–31°C. Like July, it's one of the driest months of the year. With abundant sunshine and clear waters, it's perfect for all water sports and outdoor activities. The only difference is the influx of peak-season crowds, so early booking for accommodations and experiences is recommended.
- **Packing Tips:** Same as July: lightweight, breathable clothing, high-SPF sunscreen, and sunglasses are essential. Carry sunscreen for reapplication throughout the day.

## **September: The Transition from Dry to Wet Season**

- **Climate Overview:** September brings subtle changes. Rainfall increases to 2–4 days, humidity rises to 15–30%, and average temperatures stay between 24–31°C. While still part of the dry season, clouds start to build, and occasional showers appear—but they usually pass quickly. Mornings and evenings remain pleasant, with midday heat persisting.
- **Packing Tips:** Keep your sun protection from July–August, but start carrying a lightweight umbrella or rain jacket for unexpected showers.
- **Avoiding Crowds:** Late September nears October, which marks the peak of the 'transition season' with more travelers. To dodge the crowds, plan your trip for early September.

- **September 4–6: Taman Ayun Barong Festival:** A Barong dance festival held at the Taman Ayun Royal Temple, showcasing Bali's most iconic traditional performances and ceremonial culture. A must-experience event if you want to dive deep into Balinese dance.
- **September 5: Tumpek Kandang (Livestock Thanksgiving):** The second time in the year (first in February) that Bali honors its livestock. You'll see heartwarming scenes of animals being bathed, decorated, and blessed in villages.
- **September 24–27: Bali International Airshow:** Featuring the latest aircraft and aviation technology from around the world, with flight displays, exhibitions, and industry networking—perfect for tech enthusiasts.
- **September 27: Nyepi Laut (Ocean Day of Silence):** A special 'Ocean Day of Silence' observed on Bali's three main offshore islands (Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, and Nusa Ceningan). All boat traffic to and from these islands stops entirely—plan your itinerary well in advance.
- **September: Lembongan Surf and Music Festival:** Held on the offshore island of Nusa Lembongan, combining surfing competitions, live music, and beach culture.
- **September: Buleleng Fashion Festival:** Showcasing designers and creative industries from North Bali.
- **September: Parade Budaya Jembrana:** A cultural parade highlighting West Bali's traditions, costumes, and regional identity.

## **October: The ‘Transition Season’ Previews Peak Travel**

- **Climate Overview:** October marks the official shift from dry to wet season. Rainfall increases to 5–9 days, humidity rises to 30–50%, and average temperatures range from 25–30°C. Days may still be sunny, but brief afternoon showers become more frequent, with increasing cloud cover. Despite this, October is part of Bali’s ‘Big Five’ peak seasons—the ‘Transition Season’—and tourist numbers start to rise noticeably.
- **Packing Tips:** Besides sunscreen and a hat, always carry a foldable umbrella or lightweight raincoat. Cotton quick-dry clothing is more practical than summer wear.
- **Avoiding Crowds:** After mid-October, visitor numbers surge, driving up hotel and car rental prices. Aim for early October or opt for late September instead.

![10 月：「過渡季」的旅遊旺季預告](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780923758088-10.jpg)

- **October 5–7: Nusa Penida Festival:** Held on the famous cliffside island, this event focuses on culture, nature conservation, marine themes, and tourism promotion. Enjoy stunning views while soaking up the local festive atmosphere.
- **October 10: Tumpek Wayang (Shadow Puppet Thanksgiving):** The second annual blessing for shadow puppets (first in March). A great chance to watch traditional shadow puppet performances.
- **October 10–11: Klungkung Heritage Festival:** Highlights the history, culture, exhibitions, and stage performances of the Klungkung region.
- **October 10–12: Gebug Seraya Festival:** Held in Karangasem, this unique festival centers on the traditional stick-fighting ritual ‘Gebug Seraya’.
- **October 21–25: Ubud Writers & Readers Festival:** An international literary event gathering writers, thinkers, and cultural figures from around the world for talks, discussions, and creative performances. A must for literature lovers.
- **October 31: Hari Raya Saraswati (Knowledge Goddess Day) & Pagerwesi (Wisdom Festival):** The second annual celebration of knowledge and wisdom (first in April). October 31 is Knowledge Goddess Day, and November 4 is Wisdom Festival, four days apart.
- **October: Tulamben Ocean Harmony Festival:** Held in the diving haven of Tulamben, combining marine conservation themes with water activities.

## **November: Early Rainy Season's Lush Greenery and Quiet Moments**

- **Weather Overview:** November marks the start of the rainy season. Rainfall increases significantly to 10–15 days, with humidity rising to 50–70% and average temperatures ranging from 24–31°C. Rain typically falls in the afternoon and evening, while mornings remain relatively stable. The abundant moisture makes vegetation lush and vibrant, with rice paddies showing their most beautiful green hues, creating stunning landscapes. Correspondingly, tourist numbers drop sharply, and accommodation prices fall, making it the best time to avoid crowds.
- **Packing Tips:** Rain gear is essential. Bring a waterproof jacket, waterproof backpack cover, and water shoes. Cotton moisture-wicking clothing is more practical than pure cotton. Evenings can bring a cool breeze, so a light long-sleeved jacket comes in handy.
- **Travel Tips:** The rainy season doesn't mean you can't travel. Mornings are usually clear, with rain arriving in the afternoon and evening. Plan activities for the early hours and return to your hotel for rest or a spa in the afternoon. Travel experiences during this time are often more immersive, with fewer tourists and more personalized service.

- **Nov 15 Makepung Lampit (Mud Buffalo Cart Race):** A special version of buffalo racing held on muddy fields, symbolizing rural cooperation and traditional farming culture.
- **November Makepung Jembrana Cup:** The season's highlight, this is the highest-level traditional buffalo race in the Jembrana region.
- **November Ulun Danu Festival:** Held around the Ulun Danu Temple on Lake Bratan, this festival is filled with cultural performances, rituals, and natural beauty. The lake and temple create a stunning landscape.

Activities include traditional boat races, marine water sports, surfing competitions, coral planting, sea turtle releases, food festivals, music and cultural performances, and yoga classes.

## **December: Mid-Rainy Season Prelude to Christmas and New Year**

- **Climate Overview:** December is the middle of the rainy season. Rainfall remains at 10–15 days (similar to November), with humidity at 60–80% and average temperatures of 24–31°C. Rain is more frequent but typically comes as afternoon showers, with mornings and evenings still relatively dry. Temperatures rise slightly, making it feel more muggy. Despite the rainy season, late December approaches Christmas and New Year, drawing an influx of international tourists and Indonesian holidaymakers, creating the 'Nataru' (Christmas-New Year) peak season.
- **Packing Tips:** Rain gear is essential, but due to the humid heat, opt for loose, breathable clothing. Use a waterproof backpack or dry bag to protect electronics. Quick-dry clothes are more practical than long garments.
- **Avoiding Crowds:** From December 20 to January 5, it's the peak of the 'Nataru' holiday season. Hotel and car rental prices may double, traffic is congested, and attractions are crowded. If you enjoy the buzz, join the festivities; if you seek tranquility, avoid the period after December 20.

![12 月：雨季中期的耶誕與新年序曲](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780924308707-12.jpg)

### **Major Festivals:**

Churches and Christian communities hold prayers and celebrations. This also marks the start of the 'Nataru' holiday period.
- **December 25 – Pandawa Beach Festival:** A beach festival at Pandawa Beach featuring stage performances and celebrations tied to Christmas, attracting many visitors. The scenic beach combined with traditional performances makes it worth experiencing.
- **December – Bali Rockin' Blues Festival:** A music event blending rock and blues, held on the beach with food and drink stalls.
- **December – Denpasar Festival:** A year-end city festival in Denpasar featuring concerts, markets, and cultural programs, celebrating the end of the year and anticipation of the new one.
- **December – Besakih Festival:** Held near Bali's mother temple, Besakih, aimed at promoting local tourism and culture. This temple is the most sacred in Bali, drawing many pilgrims for blessings during the festival.

## Smart Travel Tips from Our Editors 💡

![小編的聰明旅遊筆記](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780924530878-15.jpg)

### **Seasonal Strategy:**

- **For dry weather and high visibility:** July-August is the best choice all year, but it's also the most crowded with the highest hotel rates.
- **For savings and experience:** Early September to early October is the "golden transition period," with still-good weather, fewer crowds, and reasonable hotel rates.
- **For avoiding crowds and deeper exploration:** November is a hidden gem month. Early rainy season, lush green scenery, few tourists, and affordable rates—perfect for in-depth travel.
- **For festivities and celebrations:** December's Christmas and New Year period is a holiday peak, but hotel rates double and attractions are packed.

### **Debunking Rainy Season Myths:**

- Rainy season ≠ all-day rain. It's usually just afternoon showers; mornings and nights remain dry.
- Rainy season = lush green Bali. The landscape is at its most beautiful, offering the richest photography opportunities.
- Rainy season = off-season deals. Hotel rates and spa prices are negotiable, often leading to a more personalized travel experience.

### **Booking Advice:**

- For July–August and mid-to-late December, be sure to book rooms at least three months in advance, especially in Ubud and Sanur.
- For specific festivals (Jazz Festival, Writers Festival, Spirituality Festival), book at least one month ahead.
- In November, you can book on short notice with more options and friendlier prices.

![預約建議：](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780924627221-16.jpg)

### **Transport Tips:**

- During July–August and mid-to-late December, car rental and private driver prices rise by 20–30%, and vehicles are hard to secure.
- In January, traffic flows smoothly and prices are reasonable, making it the best time for self-driving or hiring a driver.
- During the rainy season, if your itinerary involves mountainous areas or outlying islands, allow extra time for weather-related delays.

### **Festival Etiquette:**

- Dress appropriately when attending temple ceremonies (a sarong and sash are ideal).
- Always ask locals or your guide before taking photos, and respect sacred rituals.
- Some festivals prohibit outsiders from entering the inner sanctum—please be respectful.
