# [2026 Bali Travel Outlook] From Dry Season Peak to Rainy Season Arrival: A Complete Guide to Festivals and Weather from July to December

Thinking of flying to **Bali** in the second half of 2026? This period marks the island’s transition from ultimate dry comfort to a lush, vibrant rainy season. You might enjoy the clearest waters and bluest skies in July, or welcome the verdant renewal brought by November rains.

To help you travel like a seasoned pro, we’ve combined climate data with local festivals to craft this “July–December Travel Outlook.”

***

# 🌦️ Climate & Seasons: From Dry Season Peak to Rainy Season Transition

![Climate & Seasons: From Dry Season Peak to Rainy Season Tran](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780917212578-mixcollage-08-jun-2026-05-52-pm-8861.jpg)

These six months showcase Bali’s full journey from the peak of the dry season to the onset of the rainy season:

- **July–August (Dry Season Peak):** The driest months of the year, with the lowest humidity (10–20%) and minimal rainfall (1–3 days). Temperatures are comfortable (24–31°C), offering the best feel—perfect for water sports and hiking.
- **September–October (Dry Season End):** Still plenty of sunshine, but rainfall starts to increase (2–6 days). Temperatures rise slightly (24–31°C), humidity edges up (15–30%), but it remains a great travel period.
- **November–December (Rainy Season Prelude to Early Phase):** Rainfall becomes more frequent (10–15 days), humidity climbs (50–60%), while temperatures stay steady (24–31°C). This is the low season but has its own charm—fewer tourists, lush green landscapes, and the best time to avoid crowds.

# 📅 Monthly Deep Dive: Festivals and Tips to Avoid Pitfalls

## **July: The Month of Island Kites and Artistic Soaring**

- **Climate Overview:** July is the absolute peak of the dry season. Rainfall is minimal, with humidity at just 10–20% and average temperatures ranging from 24–31°C. Days are brilliantly sunny, and nights are cool and comfortable, making it the driest and most pleasant month of the year. Rare morning mountain views and excellent ocean visibility make this month a dream for outdoor enthusiasts.
- **Packing Tips:** Light, breathable cotton clothing is essential, along with high-SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+) and sunglasses. For early morning hikes, a lightweight long-sleeve sun-protection jacket is recommended.

![July: The Month of Island Kites and Artistic Soaring](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780922788814-7.jpg)

### **Major Festivals:**

- **July 10–12: Penglipuran Village Festival:** Held in the ancient traditional village of Penglipuran, this festival showcases Bali Aga culture, handicrafts, local cuisine, and cultural performances. Visitors can deeply experience the unique charm of this traditional village.
- **July 11–25: Bali Jani Contemporary Art Festival:** A creative festival focusing on modern and contemporary Balinese art, showcasing new forms of artistic collaboration and cross-disciplinary creativity.
- **July 18–19: Jatiluwih Festival:** Held at the UNESCO World Heritage Jatiluwih Rice Terraces, this event combines cultural performances, natural beauty, local agricultural products, and hiking activities. The early morning light on the terraces in July is most enchanting, perfect for a peaceful stroll.
- **July 24–26: Lovina Festival:** A cultural feast in the coastal town of Lovina in North Bali, featuring stage performances, a traditional market, and a fireworks show.
- **July 26–30: Bali International Choir Festival:** An international choir competition and concert series that brings together voices from around the world.
- **July–October: Traditional Buffalo Race Season (Makepung):** A traditional agricultural cultural performance in the Jembrana region of West Bali, where buffalo race at high speed pulling wooden chariots—a thrilling spectacle.
- **July–August: Bali Kite Festival:** Held on Sanur Beach, participants from around the world compete with giant traditional kites. July’s stable weather ensures the best conditions for kite flying.

### **Recommended Destinations for July**

- **Jatiluwih Rice Terraces:** July is the best time to view the terraces at their peak. The morning light filtering through the rice paddies creates a dreamlike scene. Besides the Jatiluwih Festival, you can also take a leisurely walk, ride a scooter around the area, or join a rice field yoga class.

## **August: A Lively Season of Music Festivals and Independence Celebrations**

- **Weather Overview:** August continues the dry season with excellent weather. Expect only 1–3 rainy days, humidity at 10–20%, and average temperatures of 24–31°C. Like July, it’s one of the driest months of the year. Plenty of sunshine and clear waters make it perfect for all water sports and outdoor activities. The only difference is the influx of peak-season crowds, so early booking for accommodations and experiences is recommended.
- **Packing Tips:** Same as July: lightweight, breathable clothing, high-SPF sunscreen, and sunglasses are essential. Carry sunscreen for reapplication throughout the day.

- **August 7–8: Ubud Village Jazz Festival:** One of Asia’s most important jazz events, attracting world-class jazz musicians annually. Held in Ubud with open-air concerts and stage performances, this two-night musical feast is unforgettable.
- **August 17: Indonesian Independence Day:** A national holiday in Indonesia. Nationwide celebrations include parades, fireworks, and fun competitions, creating a vibrant atmosphere. Across Bali, traditional cultural performances and congratulatory events take place.
- **August 23–28: Buleleng Festival:** The largest regional festival in North Bali, featuring concerts, cultural showcases, city events, and markets.
- **August 24: Maybank Marathon:** Bali’s largest road running event, with full marathon, half marathon, and short-distance categories, perfect for sports enthusiasts.
- **August 25: Maulid Nabi Muhammad S.A.W. (Prophet Muhammad’s Birthday):** A national holiday in Indonesia, an important day for Islamic followers, with prayers and celebrations at mosques and Muslim communities.
- **August: Tanah Lot Art and Food Festival:** Held at the iconic Tanah Lot sea temple, featuring traditional dance, music performances, handicraft markets, and local food stalls.
- **August: Makepung Bupati Cup (Buffalo Race):** The top buffalo racing competition in the Jembrana region.

## **September: The Transition from Dry to Rainy Season**

- **Weather Overview:** September marks a slight shift. Rainfall increases to 2–4 days, humidity rises to 15–30%, while average temperatures remain at 24–31°C. Though still in the dry season, clouds start to build, with occasional brief showers that come and go quickly. Mornings and evenings stay cool, while midday remains hot.
- **Packing Tips:** Keep your sun protection from July–August, but start carrying a lightweight foldable umbrella or rain jacket for sudden showers.
- **Avoiding Crowds:** Late September nears October, which peaks the 'transition season' with more travelers. To dodge the crowds, plan your trip for early September.

- **Sep 5: Tumpek Kandang (Livestock Thanksgiving)** – The second time this year (the first was in February) that Balinese tradition honors livestock. You’ll see heartwarming scenes of animals being bathed, decorated, and blessed in villages.
- **Sep 24–27: Bali International Airshow** – Featuring the latest aircraft and aviation technology from around the world, with flight displays, exhibitions, and industry networking. Perfect for tech enthusiasts.

- **September: Buleleng Fashion Festival** – Showcasing designers and creative industries from North Bali.
- **September: Parade Budaya Jembrana** – A cultural parade highlighting West Bali’s traditions, costumes, and regional identity.

## **October: The 'Transition Season' Kicks Off Peak Travel**

- **Climate Overview:** October marks the official shift from the dry to the rainy season. Rainfall increases to 5–9 days, humidity rises to 30–50%, and average temperatures range from 25–30°C. Days can still be sunny, but brief afternoon showers become more frequent, and cloud cover thickens. Despite this, October is part of Bali's 'Big Five' peak seasons—the 'transition season'—and tourist numbers start to climb noticeably.
- **Packing Tips:** Along with sun protection and a hat, be sure to carry a foldable umbrella or lightweight raincoat. Quick-dry cotton clothing is more practical than summer wear.
- **Avoid the Crowds:** After mid-October, visitor numbers surge, and prices for accommodations and private drivers rise accordingly. It's best to travel in early October or opt for late September instead.

![October: The 'Transition Season' Kicks Off Peak Travel](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780923758088-10.jpg)

### **Major Festivals:**

- **Oct 5–7: Nusa Penida Festival:** Held on the famous cliffside island, this event focuses on culture, nature conservation, marine themes, and tourism promotion. You can enjoy stunning views while soaking up the local festive atmosphere.
- **Oct 10: Tumpek Wayang (Shadow Puppet Thanksgiving):** The second time in the year to bless shadow puppets (the first is in March). A great opportunity to appreciate traditional wayang performances.
- **Oct 10–11: Klungkung Heritage Festival:** Highlights the history and culture of the Klungkung region through exhibitions and stage performances.
- **Oct 10–12: Gebug Seraya Festival:** Held in Karangasem, this unique festival centers on the traditional stick-fighting ritual 'Gebug Seraya'.
- **Oct 21–25: Ubud Writers & Readers Festival:** An international literary event bringing together writers, thinkers, and cultural figures from around the world for talks, discussions, and creative performances. A must for literature lovers.
- **Oct 31: Hari Raya Saraswati (Knowledge Goddess Day) & Pagerwesi (Wisdom Day):** The second celebration of knowledge and wisdom in the year (the first is in April). Saraswati Day falls on Oct 31, and Pagerwesi on Nov 4, four days apart.
- **October: Tulamben Ocean Harmony Festival:** Held in the diving haven of Tulamben, combining marine conservation themes with water activities.
- **October: Bali Hockey Festival:** A hockey sports festival or tournament.

## **November: Early Rainy Season’s Verdant and Quiet Moments**

- **Climate Overview:** November marks the start of the rainy season. Rainfall increases significantly to 10–15 days, with humidity rising to 50–70% and average temperatures ranging from 24–31°C. Rain typically falls in the afternoon and evening, while mornings remain relatively stable. The vegetation becomes lush and vibrant thanks to ample moisture, with rice paddies showing their most beautiful green hues, creating a stunning landscape. Correspondingly, tourist numbers drop sharply, and hotel prices fall, making it the best time to avoid crowds.
- **Packing Tips:** Make rain gear a must. Bring a waterproof jacket, a rain cover for your backpack, and water shoes. Cotton-blend moisture-wicking clothing is more practical than pure cotton. Evenings can bring a cool breeze, so a light long-sleeved jacket comes in handy.
- **Travel Tips:** The rainy season doesn’t mean you can’t travel. Mornings are usually clear, with rain arriving in the afternoon and evening. It’s best to plan activities for the morning and return to your hotel for rest or a spa in the afternoon. Travel experiences during this time often feel more immersive, as fewer tourists mean more personalized service.

## **December: Mid-Rainy Season Prelude to Christmas and New Year**

- **Weather Overview:** December is the middle of the rainy season. Rainfall remains at 10–15 days (similar to November), humidity 60–80%, average temperature 24–31°C. Rain is more frequent but typically comes as afternoon showers, with mornings and evenings still relatively dry. Temperatures rise slightly, making it feel more muggy. Despite the rainy season, the end of December near Christmas and New Year sees an influx of international tourists and Indonesian holidaymakers, creating the 'Nataru' (Christmas-New Year) peak season.
- **Packing Tips:** Rain gear is essential, but due to the humidity and heat, opt for loose, breathable clothing. A waterproof backpack or dry bag protects electronics. Quick-dry clothes are more practical than long garments.
- **Avoiding Crowds:** From December 20 to January 5, it's the peak of the 'Nataru' holiday season. Hotel and car rental prices may double, traffic is heavy, and attractions are crowded. If you enjoy the buzz, join the festivities; if you seek tranquility, avoid traveling after December 20.

![December: Mid-Rainy Season Prelude to Christmas and New Year](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780924308707-12.jpg)

### **Major Festivals:**

- **December 25 - Christmas (Kelahiran Yesus Kristus):** A national holiday in Indonesia (December 24-25 long weekend). Although Bali is predominantly Hindu, Christmas is still a public holiday. Churches and Christian communities hold prayers and celebrations. This also marks the start of the 'Nataru' holiday period.
- **December 25 - Pandawa Beach Festival:** A beach festival at Pandawa Beach, combining stage performances and festive activities with Christmas, attracting many visitors. The stunning beach paired with traditional performances makes it worth experiencing.
- **December - Bali Rockin' Blues Festival:** A music festival blending rock and blues, held on the beach with food and drink markets.
- **December - Denpasar Festival:** A year-end city festival in Denpasar featuring concerts, markets, and cultural programs, celebrating the end of the year and anticipation of the new one.
- **December - Besakih Festival:** Held near Besakih Temple, Bali's mother temple, aimed at promoting local tourism and culture. This temple is the most sacred in Bali, and during the festival, many pilgrims come to pray and seek blessings.

## Editor's Smart Travel Notes 💡

![Editor's Smart Travel Notes](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780924530878-15.jpg)

### **Season Selection Strategy:**

- **For dry weather and high visibility:** July-August is the best choice all year, but it's also the most crowded with the highest accommodation prices.
- **For savings and experience:** Early September to early October is the "golden transition period," with still-good weather, fewer tourists, and reasonable prices.
- **For avoiding crowds and depth:** November is a secret treasure month. Early rainy season, lush green landscapes, few tourists, and affordable prices make it the best time for in-depth travel.
- **For festivities and celebrations:** December's Christmas and New Year period is a holiday peak, but prices double and attractions are packed.

### **Debunking the Rainy Season Myth:**

- Rainy season ≠ all-day rain. It's usually just afternoon showers, with mornings and nights staying dry.
- Rainy season = lush green Bali. The landscapes are at their most beautiful, offering the richest photography opportunities.
- Rainy season = off-peak deals. Room rates and spa prices are negotiable, often leading to a more intimate travel experience.

### **Booking Tips:**

- For July–August and mid-to-late December, be sure to book rooms three months in advance, especially in Ubud and Sanur.
- For specific festivals (Jazz Festival, Writers Festival, Spirituality Festival), book one month ahead.
- In November, you can book on the fly with more choices and friendlier prices.

![Booking Tips:](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1780924627221-16.jpg)

### **Transportation Tips:**

- During July–August and mid-to-late December, car rental and charter prices rise by 20–30%, and vehicles are hard to secure.
- In January, traffic flows smoothly and prices are reasonable, making it the best time for self-driving or hiring a driver.
- If your rainy season itinerary includes mountainous areas or outlying islands, allow extra time for weather-related delays.

### **Festival Etiquette:**

- Dress appropriately when attending temple ceremonies (sarong and sash are ideal).
- Ask locals or your guide before taking photos, and respect sacred rituals.
- Some festivals prohibit outsiders from entering the inner sanctum—please respect that.
