## Bali Visa Extension Guide: Your Area Determines Which Immigration Office to Visit

Many travelers find that a month in Bali just isn't enough. Want to stay longer? You'll need to deal with visa extensions.

The extension process itself isn't complicated, but recently, many travelers have been running into a frustrating issue: **Which immigration office should you go to?** Bali has several immigration offices, and choosing the wrong one means a wasted trip—and potentially missing your visa deadline.

### First, Know What Visa You Have

Most tourists visiting Bali use the e-Visa on Arrival (eVOA). This visa costs IDR 500,000 (about USD$16), can be applied for online a few days before departure, is valid for 30 days, and can be extended once for an additional 30 days—allowing a maximum legal stay of 60 days in Indonesia.

### How to Extend: Online + In-Person, Both Required

Bali's visa extension uses a "Hybrid Application Process"—you first submit your application online at the official immigration website (evisa.imigrasi.go.id). After submission, you must **personally visit the designated immigration office within 48 hours (including working days)** to have your fingerprints taken, documents verified, and in some cases, attend an interview.

This system was introduced in May 2025, driven by a rise in cases where foreigners were using tourist visas to work or invest in Bali. The immigration office implemented this procedure to tighten control.

### Go to the Immigration Office Based on Where You Stay

This is the most common mistake. **The immigration office that handles your visa extension is determined by the address you provide on your application, not by your personal choice.**

The Denpasar Immigration Office covers: Denpasar City, Gianyar, Bangli, and parts of Badung Regency (Mengwi, Abiansemal, Penang). So if you're staying in Canggu, Seminyak, Legian, Kuta, Uluwatu, Sanur, Ubud, or even Kintamani, you'll all report to the Denpasar Immigration Office.

For those staying in Tabanan—areas like Kedungu, Tanah Lot, Balian, Bedugul, and Jatiluwih—you'll need to go to the **Tabanan Immigration Office**. If you're on Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Ceningan, or in eastern areas like Semarapura, head to the **Klungkung Immigration Office**. Travelers in the north and west of the island typically go to the **Singaraja Immigration Office**.

Here's a quick summary:

| Where You Stay | Corresponding Immigration Office |
| --- | --- |
| Canggu, Seseh, Seminyak, Legian, Kuta, Uluwatu, Sanur, Ubud, and even Kintamani | Denpasar Immigration Office |
| Kedungu, Tanah Lot, Balian, Bedugul, and Jatiluwih (Tabanan area) | Tabanan Immigration Office |
| Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Ceningan, and cities like Semapura | Klungkung Immigration Office |
| North and west of the island | Singaraja Immigration Office |

### Immigration Enforcement Is Stepping Up

Beyond the visa extension process updates, Bali has recently established a dedicated immigration patrol unit—the **Dharma Dewata Immigration Patrol Team**—which regularly conducts checks in popular tourist areas and expat hubs. Officials have made it clear: the goal is to prevent violations and ensure that genuine visitors who follow the rules can enjoy their time in Bali without worry.

In short, Bali is now much stricter about visa violations than before. Whether it's working on a tourist visa, freelancing, or overstaying, if you're caught, you'll face consequences.

If you're planning to stay longer in Bali, you need to understand the rules first.

I think the visa extension system is fairly reasonable overall. With online applications plus an in-person visit, the process is clear. As long as you prepare your documents in advance and plan your timing, it's not that hard to handle.

The most important thing: **When applying for a visa extension, the accommodation address you fill in must be where you're actually staying**, because this determines which immigration office you'll need to visit. For travelers planning to move around multiple places in Bali, it's best to schedule the extension during the period when you're staying the longest at one location, so you don't have to travel across districts.

Want to enjoy a full 60 days in Bali? Get these rules straight, and you can relax and savor every day. ☀️
