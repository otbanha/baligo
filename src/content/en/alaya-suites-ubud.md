## 📍 Location & Surroundings

The hotel is located on Jl. Raya Pengosekan, about 450 meters from ARMA Museum, 950 meters from Monkey Forest, 1.5 kilometers from Yoga Barn, and 1.9 kilometers from the Ubud Traditional Art Market and Ubud Palace. What makes this location special? Pengosekan is just a short distance from central Ubud but without the overwhelming commercial hustle. The area is dotted with local-style cafes and galleries, perfect for travelers who want to experience the 'real Ubud.'

Even better, the hotel is only 500 meters from its sister properties, Alaya Resort Ubud and Alaya Grounds (home to dining brands like Pison Coffee, Rayjin, Cerita Manisan, and Chon Tea). You can easily walk over for brunch or afternoon tea without worrying about finding restaurants. The entire Pengosekan area retains a strong local vibe, ideal for those who love wandering through alleyways and exploring small shops.

## 🏨 Facilities Overview

The hotel's design concept steers clear of traditional Balinese thatched-roof aesthetics. Instead, it draws inspiration from Bali's five natural elements: Water (sāgara), Earth (bhūmi), Life (jīva), Air (akasha), and Light (sūrya). Each floor is themed around a different element, expressed through carved stone, reclaimed wood, and layered textures. Walking into the hotel doesn't give you a flashy 'wow'—it invites a quiet 'ah, this is nice.'

The pool area is the heart of relaxation, featuring a main swimming pool in a serene, slow-paced setting. The gym is equipped with Technogym machines and free weights.

Dining highlights include Carbón, a restaurant inspired by Mexican and Peruvian cuisine, focusing on open-fire grilling with seasonal ingredients and Latin American flair—a rare find in Ubud. Other options include Baker Butcher Barista (light bites and coffee), Pool Bar, and Tequila & Cigar Lounge for evening drinks. Breakfast is à la carte, and guest reviews generally praise its quality.

The spa features DaLa Spa, the signature brand of the Alaya Group, with six treatment rooms centered around Bali's natural elements. Each treatment is rooted in traditional Balinese wellness wisdom and uses natural ingredients.

## 🛏️ Room Types

Suites come in two options: King Suite (one king bed) and Twin Suite (two single beds), both measuring 65 square meters. Each includes a private balcony, bathtub, and shower, with natural tones and layered textures creating a serene atmosphere. Villas are one-bedroom units spanning 100 square meters, featuring a private soaking pool overlooking Ubud's greenery, along with outdoor sun loungers and an alfresco dining area.

## 👥 Who It's For

Top choice for honeymooners—the combination of a private Villa soaking pool, couple's spa treatments, and an exquisite dinner at Carbón sets the perfect romantic mood. Also ideal for travelers who appreciate design but prefer to avoid noisy tourist spots; the Pengosekan location offers both convenience and tranquility. Slightly less suitable for families with children, as the hotel explicitly states it does not provide cribs or extra bed services.

## 💬 Guest Reviews

One guest originally booked Alaya Resort but was unexpectedly upgraded to Alaya Suites. She described the suite as spacious and the environment extremely quiet, with the large bathtub making the entire stay more relaxing. Breakfast is à la carte with unlimited servings, freshly prepared to order, and the quality was described as five-star level.

Another review meticulously listed the names of service team members—restaurant staff, spa therapists, shuttle drivers, and butlers—with specific praise for each, calling the level of personalized service 'very rare.' To receive such high-quality reviews shortly after opening clearly indicates strong service standards.
