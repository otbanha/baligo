Are you planning a vacation to Indonesia or Bali and wondering if you can bring your drone? Don't worry, the answer is yes! You can bring your drone to Bali! However, there are a few regulations you must follow before packing your drone in your luggage.

First, **batteries cannot be checked in**! You must keep the batteries in your carry-on luggage, not in your checked luggage. This prevents airlines from refusing to check in luggage containing lithium batteries due to safety concerns.

Second, the batteries must be removed from the drone and placed in a fireproof protective case to avoid the risk of fire caused by short circuits during the flight. The protective case also reduces the likelihood of the batteries being damaged by impact.

Finally, the drone body with the batteries removed can be placed in your checked luggage or carried with you in your hand luggage. If placed in checked luggage, be careful of collisions and compression that could damage the drone.

Additionally, if your drone **weighs more than 2 kilograms**, according to Indonesian regulations, you must register with the [Indonesian Ministry of Communication and Information Technology](https://hubud.dephub.go.id/hubud/website/) and obtain a permit to legally operate the drone within Indonesia.
