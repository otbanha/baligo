Bali's Ayana Bali is a sprawling resort complex beloved by tourists, spanning 90 hectares. Within this vast area, Ayana Bali offers a diverse array of facilities and activities for an unforgettable getaway. The complex features 19 restaurants and bars, 14 different pools (see [all pool images on the official website](https://www.ayana.com/bali/facilities/)), an 18-hole golf course, a kids' club, sunset yoga, the famous Rock Bar, a private beach, and, of course, unparalleled ocean views!

https://youtube.com/shorts/twUxgOqdq3E

Within Ayana Bali, there are four distinct hotel zones, each representing a different level of luxury. Think of them as four five-star resorts with varying price points and styles, located in different parts of the Ayana Bali complex. They are:

- **Ayana Resort**: The heart of Ayana, offering comfortable rooms and suites, along with numerous facilities and activities.
- **Ayana Villas**: For those seeking more privacy and luxury, Ayana Villas is an excellent choice. It offers private villas for a serene and exclusive experience.
- **Ayana Segara**: The newest addition to Ayana Resort, providing a closer connection to the ocean.
- **Rimba by Ayana**: Another outstanding option under Ayana Resort, offering diverse accommodations and facilities. Though not beachfront, it still delivers a luxurious vacation experience.

No matter which hotel zone you choose, you’ll have access to all facilities and activities within Ayana Bali, ensuring an unparalleled vacation experience. Ayana Bali is renowned not only for its luxurious accommodations and exquisite facilities but also for its stunning beachfront and exceptional service.

Let’s start with Ayana Resort.

## Ayana Resort

![Ayana Resort Ocean View Suite](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_254ad6da007ff427eb99377385b6f4c9.jpg)

Ayana Resort Ocean View Suite

Ayana Resort is the largest resort in Ayana Bali, featuring over 500 rooms and suites located in the prime area of the resort with the most convenient access. The rooms and suites range from 50 to 200 square meters, accommodating 2 to 6 guests.

The rooms and suites at Ayana Resort are decorated in traditional Balinese style, offering spacious and comfortable accommodations. Modern amenities include LCD TVs, free Wi-Fi, minibars, and some suites even feature private pools, balconies, or terraces.

Ayana Resort boasts a wide array of public facilities, including 9 restaurants, 4 swimming pools, a spa, fitness center, yoga studio, and a kids' club. Among the four accommodation options, it falls in the mid-range price category.

RIMBA by Ayana

This means RIMBA guests can easily explore everything Ayana Resort has to offer while enjoying more affordable accommodation prices.

It's particularly suitable for families with children and provides access to all of Ayana Resort's shared facilities, making it an extremely attractive option. Whether you're visiting Bali for a honeymoon, a romantic getaway, or simply looking for great value accommodation, consider staying at RIMBA by AYANA, which offers the lowest rates.

## **Bonus Highlights:**

At Ayana Resort in Bali, you're not just experiencing five-star accommodation, but a luxurious journey that engages all your senses. Here are 14 bonus highlights that will elevate your stay:

### Bonus Highlight 1: Rock Bar

As mentioned above, Rock Bar is a world-renowned sunset bar offering food, cocktails, and entertainment. Located on Jimbaran Beach as part of Ayana Resort, its name comes from its unique position perched on a low rocky cliff. Rock Bar offers unparalleled views.

![Rock Bar](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e33cd71a7fbf9ba40bb409f8a9b799d0.jpg)

Rock Bar is open to the public. All guests can enter for free, but non-Ayana guests cannot reserve tables and must queue on-site. Arrive late, and you might not get a spot!

Here are some tips for booking Rock Bar:

- Non-guests should arrive to queue around 3:30 or 4 PM.
- Guests can check available seating options through [Rock Bar's official website](https://www.ayana.com/bali/dining/rock-bar-bali/) or the [TableCheck app](https://www.tablecheck.com/en/ayana-resort-and-spa-rockbar/reserve/message?menu_lists[]=63b515a4bbd3110045eca51e&utm_source=ayanawebsite).
- Non-guests have a minimum spend requirement at Rock Bar.

Detailed introduction to Rock Bar here: [How to Reserve Jimbaran Rock Bar? Complete Experience Guide!](/blog/2024-11-03-67270573fd89780001adf758/)

### Highlight #5: SAKA Museum

In addition to its stunning natural beauty and top-tier services, Ayana Resort now boasts a cultural treasure! The newly opened SAKA Museum in 2024 is dedicated to Bali's rich culture, offering visitors a deep dive into the island's history, traditions, and art through various exhibits and interactive installations.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3d01909c96a70fef9e0966181009f5d4.jpg)

**Museum Highlights**

- **Saka Calendar:** The museum's name derives from Bali's ancient Saka calendar, which is closely tied to many of the island's festivals and rituals.
- **Cultural Artifacts:** The museum houses an extensive collection of Balinese artifacts, including sculptures, paintings, and traditional attire, showcasing the island's exquisite and unique craftsmanship.
- **Interactive Experiences:** Beyond static displays, the museum features interactive installations where visitors can engage in traditional Balinese activities, such as crafting traditional instruments or learning Balinese dance.
- **Architectural Design:** The museum's architecture blends modern and traditional elements, creating a comfortable and artistically inspiring environment for visitors.

**Why Visit Saka Museum?**

- **Deep Dive into Balinese Culture:** If you're curious about Balinese culture, Saka Museum is a must-visit destination.
- **Enrich Your Travel Experience:** Beyond enjoying the sun and beaches, visiting the museum allows you to gain a deeper understanding of this beautiful island.

Saka Museum is not only a new highlight of the Ayana Resort but also a shining gem of Balinese culture. If you're staying at Ayana, take some time to visit Saka Museum—you're sure to leave enriched.

### Bonus Highlight 14: Cashless Consumption Mode

Currently, the Ayana Bali resort has fully transitioned to a cashless payment system, offering guests enhanced security and convenience. Guests no longer need to carry large amounts of Indonesian Rupiah cash while enjoying pool or beach activities, eliminating concerns about lost wallets or dampened bills, as well as avoiding potential scams at off-site currency exchange shops. Whether through credit cards, mobile payments, or charging expenses to the room (Room Charge), this seamless payment process allows guests to immerse themselves more fully in the vacation atmosphere, uninterrupted by the hassle of handling cash.

Moreover, the fully digital payment system significantly improves the resort's operational efficiency and financial transparency. Through contactless card payments or QR code scanning, restaurant and shop checkouts become faster, and guests can clearly track every expense through electronic bills at checkout. In an era of heightened health awareness, reducing contact with physical currency also adds an extra layer of health security to the journey. This model not only aligns with modern digital travel trends but also makes the services of this top-tier resort appear more streamlined and contemporary.
