Ayana Bali is a sprawling resort complex beloved by tourists, covering 90 hectares. Within this vast area, Ayana Bali offers a wide array of facilities and activities to ensure an unforgettable holiday. The resort features 19 restaurants and bars, 14 different pools (see [all pool images on the official website](https://www.ayana.com/bali/facilities/)), an 18-hole golf course, a kids' club, sunset yoga, the famous Rock Bar, private beaches, and of course, unparalleled ocean views!

https://youtube.com/shorts/twUxgOqdq3E

Ayana Bali offers four distinct hotel zones within its complex, each representing a different level of luxury. Think of it as four five-star resorts with varying price points and styles spread across different locations within Ayana Bali. They are:

- **Ayana Resort**: The heart of Ayana, offering a range of comfortable rooms and suites, along with numerous facilities and activities.
- **Ayana Villas**: If you're seeking more privacy and luxury, Ayana Villas is an excellent choice. These private villas offer tranquility and exclusivity.
- **Ayana Segara**: The newest addition to Ayana Resort, providing a closer connection to the ocean for a unique vacation experience.
- **Rimba by Ayana**: Another superb option under Ayana Resort, offering diverse accommodations and facilities. Although not beachfront, it still delivers a luxurious vacation experience.

## Ayana Resort

![Ayana Resort Ocean View Suite](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_254ad6da007ff427eb99377385b6f4c9.jpg)

Ayana Resort Ocean View Suite

Ayana Resort is the largest resort in Ayana Bali, featuring over 500 rooms and suites located in the prime area of the resort with the most convenient access. The rooms and suites range from 50 to 200 square meters, accommodating 2 to 6 guests.

The rooms and suites at Ayana Resort are decorated in traditional Balinese style, offering spacious and comfortable accommodations. Each room is equipped with modern amenities including LCD TVs, free Wi-Fi, minibars, and more. Some suites also feature private pools, balconies, or terraces.

Ayana Resort boasts a wide array of public facilities, including 9 restaurants, 4 swimming pools, a spa, fitness center, yoga studio, kids' club, and more. Among the four accommodation options, it is moderately priced.

RIMBA by Ayana

### Bonus Highlight 5: SAKA Museum

Beyond its stunning natural landscapes and top-tier services, Ayana Resort now boasts a cultural treasure! The newly opened SAKA Museum in 2024 is dedicated to Bali's rich culture, offering visitors an immersive experience into the island's history, traditions, and art through various exhibits and interactive installations.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3d01909c96a70fef9e0966181009f5d4.jpg)

**Museum Highlights**

- **Saka Calendar:** The Saka Museum takes its name from Bali's ancient Saka calendar, which is deeply intertwined with many of the island's festivals and rituals.
- **Cultural Artifacts:** The museum houses a rich collection of Balinese artifacts, including sculptures, paintings, and traditional attire, offering visitors a glimpse into the island's exquisite and unique craftsmanship.
- **Interactive Experiences:** Beyond static exhibits, the museum features numerous interactive installations that allow visitors to engage directly with Balinese traditions, such as crafting traditional instruments or learning Balinese dance.
- **Architectural Design:** The museum's architecture blends modern and traditional elements, creating a comfortable and artistically inspiring environment for visitors.

**Why Visit the Saka Museum?**

- **Deepen Your Understanding of Balinese Culture:** If you're curious about Balinese culture, the Saka Museum is a must-visit destination.
- **Enrich Your Travel Experience:** Beyond enjoying the sun and beaches, a visit to the museum will give you a deeper appreciation of this beautiful island.

The Saka Museum is not only a new highlight of the Ayana Resort but also a shining gem of Balinese culture. If you're staying at Ayana, take some time to visit the Saka Museum—you're sure to leave enriched.

### Highlight #14: Cashless Convenience

AYANA Bali has fully transitioned to a cashless payment system, offering travelers enhanced security and convenience. Guests no longer need to carry large amounts of Indonesian Rupiah cash while enjoying pool or beach activities, eliminating concerns about lost wallets or damp bills, as well as potential scams at off-site currency exchange shops. Whether using credit cards, mobile payments, or charging expenses to the room (Room Charge), this seamless payment process allows guests to immerse themselves in the vacation atmosphere without interruptions from handling cash.

Moreover, the fully digital payment system significantly improves operational efficiency and financial transparency across the resort. With contactless card payments or QR code scanning, restaurant and shop checkouts become faster, and guests can easily track all expenses through electronic bills at checkout. In an era of heightened health awareness, reducing physical currency contact adds an extra layer of health safety to the journey. This model not only aligns with modern digital travel trends but also makes the services of this premier resort appear more streamlined and contemporary.
