Tukad Badung Riverside, also known as Tukad Badung Riverside, is one of the most successful urban renewal projects in Denpasar, Bali, under the "Green City, Friendly Citizens" initiative. This 1-kilometer-long, 4-meter-wide artificial waterway was once a murky, foul-smelling drainage ditch. However, since 2020, through collaboration between the Bali provincial government, municipal authorities, and the community, it has been thoroughly cleaned, redesigned, and transformed into a modern public space with aesthetic and community functions.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9bcc97b4941d0def4b645b5e493dc7ac.jpg)

The pathway is affectionately called "Taman Tukad Korea" because its design draws inspiration from Seoul's Cheonggyecheon. It mimics the orderly riverbank layout and lighting arrangements while incorporating local cultural elements, creating a unique blend of Eastern beauty and tropical charm.

The area is themed around "night aesthetics." After dark, LED lights form colorful ribbons along the riverbank, turning every corner—from bridges and railings to lanterns and waterfall backdrops—into a photo-worthy spot. The main waterfall area, adorned with lotus-shaped lights and a gently flowing artificial waterfall, exudes a romantic atmosphere that requires no filters.

Moreover, the riverbanks are lined with wild cherry and tropical plants, making the pathway feel like a natural garden during the rainy and blooming seasons. It's perfect for family strolls during the day and becomes a paradise for couples and photography enthusiasts in the evening.

Beyond its scenic design, Tukad Badung Riverside emphasizes "community engagement." You can sit on bamboo chairs by the river, savor hot grilled corn and satay, sip on chilled passion fruit juice, and enjoy street performers playing traditional gamelan or local pop music. Handicraft markets and street artists are also common, offering a glimpse into the city's creative pulse.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c5169911503e79727e1b2f6ff164cd5f.jpg)

In late 2023, the local government added an "Ecology Education Corner" near the Kumbasari Market bridge section, showcasing river ecology and water conservation through visuals. Future plans include outdoor stages, community performance areas, and small ecological ponds to enhance educational and tourism integration.

The pathway is well-equipped with clean public restrooms, waste sorting systems, guide signs, Wi-Fi hotspots, and basic first aid stations. For families with children, it’s a safe, car-free outdoor space. Meanwhile, young people flock to the decorated walls and reflective riverfront to shoot TikTok videos or vlogs.

The Kumbasari Art Market and Badung Market along the riverbanks are ideal for exploring daily life in Denpasar. Here, you can find local handicrafts, religious offerings, spices, textiles, and souvenirs, making it a perfect spot to experience authentic Balinese culture.

**FAQ:**

1. **Is Tukad Badung Riverside free to enter?**
   Yes, it is currently open to the public and tourists free of charge.
2. **What is the best time for photography?**
   The best time for capturing light and shadow effects is between 5:30 PM and 6:30 PM.
3. **Are food and drinks available?**
   Yes, there are several stalls along the riverbank selling Balinese snacks and fresh juices.
4. **How to get to Tukad Badung?**
   It's about a 30-minute taxi ride from the airport or just a few minutes' walk from the city center.
5. **Is it suitable for family trips?**
   Very suitable, with a dedicated children's water play area, though parental supervision is recommended.
