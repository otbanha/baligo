For all Bali enthusiasts, does the thought of traveling from the airport to Canggu (Canggu) conjure up images of being stuck in endless traffic? Great news! The Indonesian government has officially announced the construction of a 'Water Taxi' route directly from **Ngurah Rai International Airport** to **Canggu**, promising to bypass the frustrating land traffic and **reach your destination in just 30 minutes**!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775968380470-water-taxi-di-bali-hotel-bali-batiqa-hotels_ts6wi.jpg)

## 🚤 Why Build This Water Route?

According to Indonesian Transportation Minister Dudy Purwagandhi, this project aims to integrate Bali's land, sea, and air transportation systems, primarily to alleviate severe traffic congestion in tourist areas. The route between the airport and Canggu is currently one of the most congested hotspots, affecting both domestic and international tourists. During peak hours, driving can take **1 to 2 hours**, but traveling by sea will only take **30 minutes**, offering significant time savings.

## 📅 Construction and Launch Timeline?

- **Estimated Investment**: 12.1 trillion Indonesian Rupiah (approximately USD$780 million)
- **Construction Start**: August 2026
- **Completion Date**: July 2027
- **Operator**: State-owned company PT ASDP Indonesia Ferry

Feasibility studies and detailed engineering designs are currently underway, with plans to build piers and breakwaters along the route, stretching from the coastal area near the airport to Berawa Beach in Canggu.

## ⚠️ Don’t Get Too Excited Just Yet: This Isn’t the First Time a Water Taxi Has Been Proposed

Back in **August 2025**, the local government had announced that the project was in its final stages, even suggesting it would be operational by **early 2026**. Clearly, the timeline has been pushed back. However, this time a contractor has been selected, and with a state-owned company in charge, **there’s a real chance it could become a reality by 2027**.

## 💡 Current Best Alternative: Private Transfers

Until the water taxi service is operational, the most reliable way to get from the airport to Canggu is still **private transfers**. This allows you to avoid the hassle of hailing a cab and the risk of price negotiations.

## ❓ FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

**1. What will the fare for the water taxi likely be?**
Official pricing has not been announced yet. Specific numbers will only be available after the feasibility study is completed. It is generally expected to be slightly higher than regular taxis, but considering the time saved and increased comfort, it should still be attractive.

**2. Will this water taxi operate 24 hours?**
Detailed operating hours have not been announced. Considering sea conditions and nighttime navigation safety, initial operations may be limited to daytime hours. Actual details will need to wait for further official announcements.

**3. If I'm going to Bali before 2027, what's the fastest way to get from the airport to Canggu?**
The most recommended method at this stage is to book a **private transfer** (like MyBaliTrips mentioned in the article) or use ride-hailing apps like **Gojek / Grab**. To save money, you can take a taxi to a nearby transfer point and switch vehicles, but this will take longer. Avoiding peak evening hours (4:00 PM - 8:00 PM) can also reduce traffic congestion.

**4. Will the water taxi be affected by waves or weather conditions?**
Yes. Since it travels through open waters, operations may be suspended for safety reasons during heavy waves, rainstorms, or typhoons. Breakwaters will be built along the route to mitigate wave impact, but severe weather may still affect operations.

**5. Besides the airport to Canggu route, will other routes be opened in the future?**
Currently, the government is only pushing forward with this most congested route. If successful and well-received, there is potential for future expansion to other popular tourist areas, such as **Kuta, Seminyak, Nusa Dua**, and other coastal regions.

Hopefully, this water taxi project will indeed be realized by 2027 as planned, so that everyone can avoid the frustrating traffic jams when traveling to Canggu in Bali! Until then, it's best to prepare some patience (or book your transfer in advance)!
