## December in Bali: The Complete Rainy Season Travel Guide

Planning a December trip to Bali? Don't let the rainy season deter you! December temperatures range between 24–31°C, with humid conditions and brief afternoon showers. By timing your activities—beaches in the morning and indoor pursuits in the afternoon—you can still enjoy a fantastic trip.

## **_Bali's December Weather Characteristics_**

December marks the peak of the rainy season, with an average of 10–15 rainy days. However, most rainfall occurs in the afternoon and evening, leaving mornings typically clear. Plan outdoor explorations for the morning and switch to indoor activities in the afternoon.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_55fe1772410b39cbd46976df8ef08ab4.png)

Mountain areas like Munduk, Bedugul, and Kintamani offer cooler temperatures, ideal for travelers seeking relief from the humidity. Wear breathable, quick-drying clothing and stay hydrated.

## **_Travel Planning: Morning Adventures, Afternoon Relaxation_**

- **_Morning Suggestions:_** Visit the east coast or sheltered bays like Tanjung Benoa and Nusa Dua for calm waters, or explore temple courtyards and coastal walks.
- **_Afternoon Suggestions:_** If rain arrives, unwind with spa treatments, cooking classes, museum visits, art galleries, or cozy cafes.

Heads up: The southwest coast (from Kuta to Canggu) often sees marine debris during the rainy season. Consider visiting in the morning or heading to the east coast.

## **_December Festivals and Events in Bali_**

Despite the rainy season, December in Bali is filled with festive vibes.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b390e203b35621ba7e42e5a0eb18c3e2.png)

- \*\*_Denpasar Festival (late December)_\*\*: A city carnival blending dance, art, and street food, suitable for families.
- \*\*_Christmas and New Year Celebrations_\*\*: Beach clubs, nightclubs, and tourist restaurants in the southern resort areas will feature Christmas decorations, special menus, and countdown parties.

## **_Travel Tips_**

1. Start your day early and keep afternoons flexible.
2. Pause outdoor activities during heavy rain and switch to indoor attractions.
3. Be cautious of slippery paths, especially in Ubud's jungle trails.
4. Check local festival and event schedules in advance, and book popular restaurants and activities early.

## **_Frequently Asked Questions_**

**_Is December a Good Time to Visit Bali?_**

Yes, as long as you plan flexibly. Mornings are usually sunny, perfect for beach and outdoor activities.

**_Does the rainy season affect water activities?_**

Waves can be strong in open waters, but snorkeling and water sports are usually still possible in protected bays along the east coast. Check out our guides to [Sanur](/blog/2024-02-11-65bf2f75fd89780001dbf162/) and [Nusa Dua](/blog/2024-01-25-65afb7bbfd897800017023b4/).

**_What to pack for Bali in December?_**

Quick-dry clothes, a lightweight raincoat, waterproof bags, insect repellent, and sandals are essentials. See our list of [21 Must-Have Items for Bali](/blog/2023-02-12-64db6b7efd897800013a9821/).

**_Are there events during Christmas and New Year?_**

Yes! From music festivals to countdown parties, the island is full of festive vibes. Check out our [Complete Guide to Celebrating Christmas/New Year in Bali](/blog/2025-10-22-68f8711efd8978000136386b/).

**_Is it safe to bring kids to Bali in December?_**

Yes, it's safe as long as you avoid heavy rainfall periods or have a [rainy day backup plan](/blog/2024-01-06-6598c6fffd89780001047d76/).
