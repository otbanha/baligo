### 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Canggu Scraps One-Way Traffic Experiment, Returns to Two-Way Flow; Immigration Task Force Cracks Down on Foreign Influencers Using Tourist Visas

- Self-driving travelers, update your maps: Canggu's core roads have reverted to two-way traffic after a failed one-way loop experiment worsened congestion. As of yesterday, officials have cleared markings and restored full two-way flow. According to a joint public works and traffic advisory released yesterday afternoon by the Badung Regency Transportation Agency and the local police traffic division, the one-way loop experiment—implemented to ease chronic bottlenecks in Canggu's commercial core, main thoroughfares, and beach areas—actually increased congestion in nearby alleys and alternative routes, causing major inconvenience for locals and foreign travelers. After a cross-departmental traffic assessment, authorities have officially ended the one-way plan, removed related traffic signals, and restored normal two-way traffic. Officials urge travelers renting cars or scooters in Canggu to pay close attention to the updated two-way routes, avoid driving against the flow out of habit, and ensure safe self-driving.  
- Creators and influencers, take note: Immigration's elite task force is expanding social media monitoring and on-site inspections, strictly warning that even free accommodation or sponsored collaborations on a tourist visa constitute illegal work. This is a critical update for foreign creators and travel influencers working remotely on the island. Yesterday, Indonesia's Directorate General of Immigration, together with Bali's local immigration office, issued a special enforcement warning. The 100-member Dharma Dewata task force is intensifying on-site inspections in influencer hubs like Canggu and Ubud, while also monitoring social media hashtags. Immigration has firmly reiterated that foreigners entering on a Visa on Arrival (VoA) or C1 tourist visa who engage in any economically valuable promotional activities—whether or not cash changes hands, including filming short videos or tagging businesses in exchange for free hotel stays, resort meals, or spa experiences (barter)—are considered to be working illegally under Indonesian law. Offenders face visa cancellation, immediate detention, deportation, and a ban of up to ten years. Travelers with commercial filming needs are urged to apply for the correct C5A creator visa before entering the country. Do not risk breaking the law.

### 📄 Traveler Topics: Strong Dry Season Southeast Winds Bring Coastal Chills and High Wave Warnings in Southern Bali

- Travel Tip: Strong dry season southeast winds are driving temperatures below 20°C early in the morning across the island, with chilly nighttime conditions requiring windproof layers. According to the latest island-wide weather monitoring from the meteorological agency, powerful southeast monsoon winds are dominating, pushing humidity to annual lows. This week, early morning temperatures in the north-central mountains have dropped below 16°C, and even in southern coastal resort areas, lows of 18–20°C have been recorded. This typical dry season weather makes days feel crisp and dry, but nights and early mornings feel cold due to sea breezes and cool air masses. Travelers planning early morning scooter rides to watch volcanic sunrises or enjoy open-air beach dinners should dress in layers and bring a windproof jacket to stay comfortable and avoid catching a chill.  
- Beachgoers, be cautious: The southeast monsoon is driving offshore waves up to 2.5 meters, and swimming is strictly prohibited beyond red warning flags. Due to persistent strong southeast winds, the meteorological agency has issued a high-wave safety alert for coastal waters. In southern Bali waters (including Kuta, Legian, Jimbaran, and Uluwatu), offshore swells are currently between 0.5 and 2.5 meters, creating powerful, hard-to-spot rip currents near the shore. Authorities urge travelers visiting southern beaches for relaxation, surfing, or wading to strictly observe warning flags posted by lifeguards before entering the water. If a red danger flag is flying, do not swim under any circumstances. When taking photos on rocky areas, always maintain a safe distance to avoid sudden large waves that could cause injury.
