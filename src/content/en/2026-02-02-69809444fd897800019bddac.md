When visiting Ubud, Bali, besides admiring the stunning rice terraces and exploring ancient temples, you absolutely cannot miss the culinary experiences here! From traditional Indonesian cuisine to creative Western-style restaurants, from hidden rice field cafes to bustling market street food, Ubud brings together the most exciting culinary culture in Bali. This guide curates the must-try restaurants and local delicacies in Ubud, ensuring your taste buds enjoy an unforgettable Bali journey!

## Boni Bali Restaurant (Google Rating 4.3⭐) 👉🏼Map: [Immerse in Scenic Beauty](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)）

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5046f26f284c95d89a998876cafa9852.jpg)

Enjoying traditional Indonesian cuisine while overlooking the Tegallalang Rice Terrace is one of the great pleasures of visiting Bali.

Located in northeastern Bali, the Tegallalang Rice Terrace is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Spanning 2,500 hectares, the terrace consists of thousands of stepped rice fields, layered and stretching to the horizon. Under the sunlight, the fields display a stunning array of golden, green, and brown hues.

Boni Bali Restaurant is situated on the edge of the Tegallalang Rice Terrace and offers traditional Indonesian cuisine. Visitors can savor classic dishes such as Nasi Goreng, Sate Ayam, and Rendang here.
