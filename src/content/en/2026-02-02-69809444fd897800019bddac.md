When visiting Ubud in Bali, besides admiring the picturesque rice terraces and exploring ancient temples, you absolutely cannot miss the culinary experiences here! From traditional Indonesian dishes to innovative Western cuisine, from hidden cafes nestled among rice fields to bustling street food markets, Ubud gathers the most vibrant food culture in Bali. This guide selects must-try restaurants and local delicacies for you, ensuring your taste buds enjoy an unforgettable Bali journey!

## Boni Bali Restaurant (Google Rating 4.3⭐) 👉🏼 Map: [Immerse in Scenic Beauty](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1jrOaKVOZ4jpcB_kprr67w3ltg3WPbf4&usp=sharing)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5046f26f284c95d89a998876cafa9852.jpg)

Enjoying traditional Indonesian cuisine while overlooking the Tegallalang Rice Terraces is one of the great pleasures of visiting Bali.

The Tegallalang Rice Terraces, located in northeastern Bali, are a UNESCO World Heritage site. Spanning 2,500 hectares, the terraces consist of thousands of stepped rice fields, layered and stretching to the horizon. Under the sunlight, the terraces display a stunning array of colors—golden, green, and brown—creating a breathtaking view.

Boni Bali Restaurant is situated right by the Tegallalang Rice Terraces, offering traditional Indonesian dishes. Visitors can savor classic dishes such as Nasi Goreng, Sate Ayam, and Rendang here.
