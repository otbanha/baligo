Bali's famous **Sacred Monkey Forest** in Ubud has been indefinitely closed following a heartbreaking tragedy. This popular attraction, known for its natural beauty and playful monkey population, now stands eerily quiet. The incident has sparked widespread concerns about tourist safety and prompted management to accelerate safety reviews to ensure visitor security in the future.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fbf73a2a2f0e51c20cfab84446f04bd0.jpg)

## The Tragedy: Tree Collapse Causes Disaster

On Tuesday, a sudden storm caused a massive tree to collapse within the Sacred Monkey Forest, resulting in the tragic deaths of two foreign tourists. The victims were **Justine Christine** (32) from France and **Kim Hyun** (42) from South Korea. One person sustained serious injuries but is now out of danger, while five others suffered minor injuries.

Eyewitnesses recalled that strong winds accompanied by heavy rain struck suddenly. Although many visitors evacuated quickly, the massive tree uprooted by the storm still caused irreparable damage.

## Official Statement: Tree Was Healthy, Storm Unpredictable

The Sacred Monkey Forest management quickly issued an official statement, emphasizing that the incident was not caused by tree disease:

> 'We deeply regret this tragedy caused by strong winds. The fallen tree was healthy and posed no prior threat. We have consistently conducted tree monitoring and pruning to ensure the safety of visitors, staff, and animals.'

According to forest management, the accident was primarily triggered by sudden extreme weather conditions rather than any negligence in management.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_031cecfcd75e3044c50ff48bad7d4012.jpg)

## Comforting the Victims' Families: Insurance Compensation and Aftercare

**Anak Agung Bagus Bhaskara**, the General Manager of the Sacred Monkey Forest, stated that they will fully assist the victims' families and provide insurance compensation:

> "We are working with the insurance company, with compensation amounts ranging from 1 billion to 10 billion Indonesian Rupiah. Currently, the family of the South Korean victim is expected to arrive within two days, while updates regarding the French victim's family are still being confirmed."

This statement demonstrates the management's highly responsible attitude towards the victims' families, striving to alleviate their grief and distress.

## Traditional Ceremonies: Purifying the Disaster's Energy

According to Balinese tradition, when a major disaster occurs, purification ceremonies must be held to pacify ominous energies. Following the incident, **I Made Parmita**, the village head of **Padangtegal**, announced that two important ceremonies have been conducted at the site:

1. **Prayaschita Ceremony**: Includes four stages—purification, atonement, cleansing, and site preparation.
2. **Pekaruan Ceremony**: Held at **Pura Wana Temple**, aimed at dispelling negative energies and praying for future peace.

These ceremonies are crucial aspects of Balinese culture, symbolizing reverence for natural disasters and the desire to coexist harmoniously with nature.

***

## Strengthening Safety Measures: Addressing Future Risks

To prevent similar tragedies from recurring, the Sacred Monkey Forest has announced enhanced safety management and implemented the following measures:

1. **Tree Inspection and Reinforcement**: Strengthening ancient tree roots to prevent them from being uprooted during storms.
2. **Real-Time Weather Forecasts**: Providing weather alerts to remind tourists to stay safe during severe weather conditions.
3. **Temporary Closure**: The Sacred Monkey Forest is now indefinitely closed, with no reopening date announced yet.

***

## Unusual Bali Rainy Season: Frequent Disasters

The local government of Bali has noted an abnormal increase in rainfall recently, leading to frequent disasters across the island. According to the **Bali Disaster Management Agency**, there have been 50 incidents in the past three days, including **tree collapses**, **landslides**, and **flooding**. Authorities have also reminded travelers to remain vigilant during the rainy season and seek safe shelter.

Additionally, local residents believe this year's rainy season has been unusually severe, marking the worst in recent years. This phenomenon has further heightened concerns about safety management in the tourism industry.

## Conclusion: The Future of the Sacred Monkey Forest

As a iconic tourist attraction in Ubud, the indefinite closure of the Sacred Monkey Forest undoubtedly has a significant impact on the local economy. However, this tragedy also serves as a reminder for all tourism-related entities to reassess safety issues, ensuring that every visitor can safely enjoy the beauty of nature.

When planning a trip to Bali, especially during the rainy season, travelers must pay attention to local weather alerts and choose appropriate times to visit major attractions. With the efforts of management, the Sacred Monkey Forest is expected to reopen with enhanced safety measures and improvements.
