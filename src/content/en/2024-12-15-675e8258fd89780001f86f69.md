Bali's famous **Monkey Forest** in Ubud has been indefinitely closed following a tragic incident. This popular attraction, known for its natural beauty and playful monkey population, now stands eerily quiet. The event has sparked widespread concern about tourist safety and prompted management to accelerate safety reviews to ensure visitor security in the future.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fbf73a2a2f0e51c20cfab84446f04bd0.jpg)

## Tragedy: Tree Collapse Causes Disaster

On Tuesday, a sudden storm caused a massive tree to fall within the Monkey Forest, resulting in the unfortunate deaths of two foreign tourists. The victims were **Justine Christine** (32) from France and **Kim Hyun** (42) from South Korea. One seriously injured individual is now out of danger, while five others sustained minor injuries.

Eyewitnesses recounted that strong winds accompanied by heavy rain struck suddenly. While many visitors evacuated quickly, the massive tree toppled by the storm caused irreparable harm.

## Official Statement: Tree Was Healthy, Storm Unpredictable

The Monkey Forest management swiftly issued an official statement, emphasizing that the incident was not caused by tree disease:

> 'We deeply regret this tragedy caused by strong winds. The fallen tree was healthy and posed no prior threat. We have consistently monitored and pruned trees to ensure the safety of visitors, staff, and animals.'

According to forest management, the accident was primarily triggered by sudden extreme weather conditions rather than negligence.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_031cecfcd75e3044c50ff48bad7d4012.jpg)

## Comforting the Victims' Families: Insurance Compensation and Follow-up Arrangements

**Anak Agung Bagus Bhaskara**, the general manager of Monkey Forest, stated that they will fully assist the victims' families and provide insurance compensation:

> "We are working with the insurance company, and the compensation amounts range from 100 million to 1 billion Indonesian Rupiah. Currently, the families of the South Korean victims are expected to arrive within two days, while updates on the French victims' families are still being confirmed."

This statement demonstrates the management's highly responsible attitude towards the victims' families, striving to alleviate their grief and distress.

## Traditional Rituals Conducted: Purifying Disaster Energy

According to Balinese tradition, purification ceremonies must be held to appease negative energies following major disasters. After the incident, **I Made Parmita**, the village head of **Padangtegal**, announced that two important ceremonies had been conducted at the site:

1. **Prayaschita Ceremony**: Consisting of four stages - purification, atonement, cleansing, and site preparation.
2. **Pekaruan Ceremony**: Held at **Pura Wana Temple**, aiming to dispel negative energies and pray for future safety.

These rituals are a crucial part of Balinese culture, symbolizing reverence for natural disasters and the desire to live in harmony with nature.

## Strengthening Safety Measures: Addressing Future Risks

To prevent similar tragedies from recurring, the Sacred Monkey Forest has announced enhanced safety management and the implementation of the following measures:

1. **Tree Inspection and Reinforcement**: Strengthening the roots of ancient trees to prevent them from being uprooted by storms.
2. **Real-time Weather Forecasts**: Providing weather alerts to remind visitors to stay safe during adverse weather conditions.
3. **Temporary Closure**: Monkey Forest is now indefinitely closed, with reopening dates yet to be determined.

## Bali's Unusual Rainy Season: Frequent Disasters

The local government of Bali noted that recent abnormal increases in rainfall have led to frequent disasters across the region. According to statistics from the **Bali Disaster Management Agency**, 50 incidents, including **tree collapses**, **landslides**, and **floods**, have occurred in the past three days. Authorities also reminded travelers to remain vigilant and seek safe shelters during the rainy season.

Additionally, local residents believe this year's rainy season is unusually severe, the worst in recent years. This phenomenon has further heightened the tourism industry's focus on safety management.

## Conclusion: The Future of the Sacred Monkey Forest

As a iconic tourist attraction in Ubud, the indefinite closure of the Sacred Monkey Forest undoubtedly has a significant impact on the local economy. However, this tragedy also serves as a reminder for all tourism-related entities to reassess safety issues, ensuring that every visitor can safely enjoy the beauty of nature.

When planning a trip to Bali, especially during the rainy season, travelers must pay attention to local weather alerts and choose appropriate times to visit major attractions. It is believed that with the management's efforts, the Sacred Monkey Forest will reopen with a safer and more improved environment to welcome visitors once again.
