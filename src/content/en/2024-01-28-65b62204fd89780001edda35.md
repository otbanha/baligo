To ensure aviation safety and provide better service, airlines flying to Bali have established a series of luggage restrictions, allowing passengers to prepare in advance.

Most airlines have specific regulations regarding carry-on luggage. Typically, each passenger is allowed to bring one piece of carry-on luggage, with size and weight limits varying by airline. For flights to Bali, common carry-on restrictions are approximately 23cm x 36cm x 56cm, with a weight limit of around 7kg. However, please note that these regulations may change based on airline policies, so it’s best to confirm before departure.

Additionally, checked baggage limits are another important factor to consider. Each passenger is usually allowed to check a certain weight of luggage for free, with specific limits varying by airline, flight type, and booking class. For flights to Bali, typical checked baggage limits range from 23kg to 32kg. It’s advisable to review and confirm these regulations before traveling to avoid unnecessary complications.

![沒有事先了解行李規定，超過限制要繳很多錢](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_33e6c51faa3d685d5e5a87db67ffaf7c.png)

Failing to understand luggage regulations in advance can result in hefty fees for exceeding limits.

Additionally, travelers should pay attention to how excess baggage is handled. If your luggage exceeds weight or size limits, airlines typically charge extra fees, and these fees vary by carrier. Therefore, when packing, carefully consider weight and dimensions to avoid adding unnecessary expenses to your trip.

Lastly, special attention should be given to regulations for unique items like sports equipment, pets, or fragile goods. These items may have different restrictions and handling procedures compared to regular luggage. It's advisable to contact your airline in advance to understand the specific rules and prepare accordingly.
