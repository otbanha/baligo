🏞️ **Why Choose Omma Bali?**

Most waterfall spots require hiking to reach, but **Omma Bali** stands out for its convenience:

1. **Private Elevator to Day Club**: Skip the tiring downhill trek, perfect for lazy travelers and families.
2. **Stunning Views**: Enjoy the breathtaking waterfall scenery right from your seat in the restaurant.
3. **Multiple Activities**: Choose to enter the pool (minimum spend required), admire the falls, or hike down to the base for photos.

🍹 **Pricing Info at Omma Bali**

- Non-alcoholic drinks and meals are moderately priced and reasonable, but alcoholic beverages tend to be pricier 🍸.
- **Minimum Spending Rules:** Seats closer to the pool or waterfall have higher minimum spending requirements. If you're only staying briefly, there are regular seats with no minimum spending.

👣 **Tips for Easily Viewing the Waterfall**

From Omma Bali, it's just a 2-3 minute walk to the waterfall, making it very convenient. If you prefer not to move around, the seats within the restaurant offer a full view of the waterfall, where the cascading water creates a refreshing and serene atmosphere 💦.

### Bali Garden Restaurant: Savoring Crispy Duck 🍗 at Laka Leke Restaurant

If you're in Ubud looking for a tranquil spot to enjoy classic Balinese cuisine away from the crowds, Laka Leke Restaurant is the perfect choice. With its unassuming entrance, it hides a spacious Balinese garden, offering a comfortable dining experience and mouthwatering signature crispy duck.

This garden restaurant is located near our accommodation, Bali Bohemia Huts, just a 100-meter walk away. If you've just visited Monkey Forest, you can head to the restaurant from Gate 3, making it very convenient.

🏡 **Restaurant Features and Ambiance**

- **Garden Design:** The restaurant is divided into an indoor seating area in a 'traditional stilt house' and an outdoor seating area at the back. The rear seats offer views of lush green rice terraces, creating a serene atmosphere that relaxes both body and mind.
- **Tradition and Entertainment:** The restaurant occasionally offers Balinese traditional dance performances and cooking classes, but it's best to confirm the schedule with the restaurant in advance, as timings are currently irregular.
- **Avoiding Crowds:** For those who dislike the bustling crowds at Ubud's popular restaurant Bebek Bengil, enjoying crispy duck here is a quieter and more comfortable alternative.

🍴 **Dish Recommendation: Signature Crispy Duck**

- **Features:**
  Half a duck pre-marinated with spices, fried to a golden crisp. The skin is crunchy with just the right amount of fat, not greasy at all. Even the bones are easily chewable, making it irresistible.
- **Duck Texture:**
  While the duck meat itself tends to be on the drier side, pairing it with the restaurant's homemade trio of sauces enhances the overall flavor. Each sauce has a different level of spiciness, offering a fresh and delicious taste that leaves you wanting more.

🌟 **Other Food Options**

While the restaurant is most famous for its crispy duck, other dishes are also worth trying:

1. **Lemon Chicken:**
   Tender chicken paired with tangy lemon sauce and soft potato balls - flavorful and satisfying.
2. **Spaghetti Carbonara:**
   Rich and creamy sauce with perfectly cooked pasta - simple yet delightful.
3. **Nasi Campur:**
   A classic Indonesian dish with various traditional ingredients. The version here is said to be very authentic and worth trying.

🍹 **Drink Recommendations**

- **Mango Smooth:** A refreshing blend of rich mango flavor and smooth texture.
- **Coconut Juice:** Fresh coconut water straight from the source - cool and hydrating.
- **Ice Tea:** Light and refreshing, perfect with any meal.

💰 **Price Reference**

- **Crispy Duck:** 140,000 IDR (~USD$9)
- **Lemon Chicken:** 60,000 IDR (~USD$4)
- **Spaghetti Carbonara:** 60,000 IDR (~USD$4)
- **Mango Smooth:** 35,000 IDR (~USD$2)
- **Coconut Juice:** 27,000 IDR (~USD$2)
- **Ice Tea:** 18,000 IDR (~USD$1)

Laka Leke Restaurant perfectly combines traditional Balinese garden ambiance with classic cuisine. From the spacious and comfortable dining area to the distinctive traditional stilt house design and the signature crispy duck's delightful crunch, this place is definitely worth a visit.

If you want to avoid the crowds at Ubud's popular restaurants but still don't want to miss out on authentic Balinese flavors, be sure to add this understated yet impressive restaurant to your itinerary!

📍**Address and Transportation Tips:**

Located near Bali Bohemia, just a 100-meter walk from Monkey Forest Gate 3. It's recommended to visit the Monkey Forest in the morning and then walk directly to dine nearby.

❗**Important Notes**: Multilingual paper maps (including Chinese) are available upon entry. It takes about 30-60 minutes to walk through the entire park. Mornings are less crowded with more monkeys, making it ideal for an early visit.

🐒 **Tips for Interacting with Monkeys**

Although the monkeys in the forest are relatively tame, please follow these rules:

1. **Do not stare directly into the monkeys' eyes**
2. **Do not carry plastic bags or bottles** (monkeys may snatch them)
3. **Do not touch or disturb the monkeys**
4. **Feeding the monkeys is prohibited**

Look up to see monkeys leaping through the treetops, walk along forest paths lined with banyan trees, and admire the sacred Hindu temples surrounding you. The harmonious coexistence of monkeys and temples creates a deeply peaceful and healing atmosphere.

🏛️ **17th Century Temple**

Within the grounds stands a 17th-century Hindu temple. While visitors cannot enter, the moss-covered temple walls create a scene worthy of admiration.

📸 **The Secret Weapon for Monkey Selfies**

Staff in the area offer professional monkey feeding and photo services for an additional IDR 50,000 (approximately USD$3). They help capture fun moments like monkeys jumping onto your shoulders or feeding them—safe, efficient, and worth trying!

**⚠️ Note: Do not feed the monkeys yourself to avoid dangerous situations!**

**Rice Terrace Fees:**

1. Some cafés charge an entrance fee;
2. Stepping onto the rice fields may require paying a landowner fee;
3. Each section of the terrace belongs to different landowners, with separate fees.

To avoid these 'fee traps,' we recommend visiting the charming and reasonably priced TIS Cafe. With no entrance fee and equally breathtaking views, it’s perfect for those who dislike long queues and aren’t chasing Instagram fame! 😊

**《TIS Cafe》Features:**

- ✅ **No entrance fee**, with scenery rivaling famous attractions.
- 💰 **Swing prices are friendly**, just 50,000 IDR (approx. USD$3) per go.
- 🌊 **Kids can use the pool**, with a minimum spend of only 100,000 IDR/person (approx. USD$6).
- 🍽 Delicious food, affordable prices, and warm, friendly service.
- 👣 **Rice field donations are voluntary**, a one-time payment of 10,000 IDR grants access to multiple rice fields for photos (the landowners are very kind).
- 📸 Free Instagram-worthy photo setups like aerial hammocks, making it absolutely worth the visit!

⚠️ Recommendations:

- Avoid peak hours for a more peaceful experience.
- When visiting, respect local customs; donations are optional, but bringing small amounts of Indonesian Rupiah is appreciated.

### Tirta Empul Temple – A Sacred Experience of Spiritual Cleansing

**The Sacred Spring in Serenity: A Ritual of Spiritual Purification**

On the fifth day, we visited **Tirta Empul Temple (Pura Tirta Empul)**, a temple renowned for its sacred spring water, which is an important symbol of Bali's religion and culture. **Introduction to Tirta Empul Temple: Sacred Waters and Ancient Faith**

Tirta Empul Temple is named after the sacred spring that surrounds it. Inside the temple, there are more than ten different water outlets, each symbolizing a different prayer intention, such as "cleansing the soul," "warding off misfortune," "healing illnesses," and "banishing nightmares." Local devotees complete the ritual of spiritual purification by bathing in the spring before entering the temple to pray, demonstrating their devotion to the deities.

**Visitor Information and Fees**

Visitors to Tirta Empul Temple need to purchase tickets, and the fees are very affordable:

- **Adult ticket: IDR 50,000** (approx. USD$3)
- **Child ticket: IDR 25,000** (approx. USD$2)

The ticket includes the rental of a traditional colorful sarong, which is essential for entering the temple. However, please note that the colorful sarong cannot be used for entering the water. If you wish to experience the **Holy Spring Purification Ritual**, additional fees apply:

- Green sarong rental: IDR 10,000 (approx. USD$1)
- Locker rental: IDR 15,000 (approx. USD$1)

These small fees are not only affordable but also allow you to deeply immerse yourself in this unique cultural tradition.

**How to participate in the Holy Spring Purification Ritual?**

The Holy Spring Purification Ritual may seem simple, but it follows a specific order and rules:

1. **Prepare your green sarong and locker**, change your clothes, and head to the holy spring pool.
2. Facing the holy spring pool, start from the **left pool**, beginning purification from the first water spout.
3. **Skip the second and third water spouts from the end** (these are reserved for worshipping the deceased).
4. After completing purification at the left spouts, move to the **right pool**.
5. At the right pool, only worship the first water spout.

At each water spout, follow these steps to complete your prayer:

- Place your hands together in prayer and make a wish facing the water spout.
- Wet your hair with the holy water and sprinkle it over your body.

The cool spring water instantly washes away travel fatigue, leaving you feeling light in body and spirit. This is a completely new cultural experience that is definitely worth trying!

**Tourist-Friendly Yet Retaining Local Charm**

Although Tirta Empul Temple has become a popular tourist spot, with pools filled with Western visitors dressed in green sarongs participating in purification rituals, creating a fascinating scene, the temple still retains its local, unspoiled charm. The staff are warm and friendly, and there are no expensive additional charges, making visitors feel welcomed with genuine smiles and kindness.

⚠️ **Friendly Reminder: Respect Local Culture**

When experiencing the purification ritual, it's essential to maintain solemnity and respect, as many local devotees are still engaged in devout prayers and bathing. Avoid excessive photography that might disturb others.

**Recommended Extension: Gunung Kawi Temple**

If time permits, it's highly recommended to visit **Gunung Kawi Temple**, located not far from Tirta Empul Temple. This site features impressive rock carvings and stunning valley views, making it a cultural heritage site worth exploring near Ubud.

Additionally, Tirta Empul Temple is very close to **Sebatu Sanctuary Eco-Resort**, my favorite accommodation during this trip. Staying here makes itinerary planning convenient and is highly recommended!

**Experience Bali's Spiritual Cleansing**

Tirta Empul Temple is not just a photo-worthy spot in Bali but also a spiritual place where you can deeply immerse yourself in local culture. Participating in the purification ritual and bathing in the clear spring water not only relaxes your body and mind but also adds a sacred and meaningful dimension to your Bali journey.

**The Story Behind Civet Coffee**

Civet Coffee, known as **Kopi Luwak** in Indonesian and **Civet Coffee** in English, is most famous for its unique production process. Civet cats consume the coffee cherries, and after the action of digestive enzymes, they excrete the specially treated coffee beans. This process has earned it the nickname "cat poop coffee."

As one of the primary producers of Civet Coffee, Bali boasts numerous farms open for tourists to visit and taste. Most farms do not charge an entrance fee and offer free coffee and tea samples for tasting, typically referred to as a **Coffee/Tea Plantation Tour**. However, the free experience often comes with a catch—staff enthusiastically promote their products, which can feel somewhat pressuring.

**Exploring Satria Agrowisata: A Blend of Knowledge and Fun**

Upon arriving at **Satria Agrowisata**, we were greeted by a lush botanical garden at the entrance, filled with various fruit trees, coffee plants, and spice plants such as Mangosteen, Arabica Coffee, and Ginger. This garden provided us with a preliminary understanding of the raw materials used in coffee production.

Next, the farm staff introduced us to the Civet Coffee production process, explaining how coffee beans are selected and cleaned from the civet's excrement. Surprisingly, even with just one or two visitors, the staff patiently explained everything, showcasing their warmth and friendliness.

**A Surprising Experience with 13 Free Beverages**

After a brief introduction to coffee production, the staff served us **13 different beverages** for free tasting, including various coffees, teas, and specialty drinks, each with its unique flavor and benefits. Sitting under the dense forest canopy, sipping these beverages was not only a treat for the palate but also a visual delight, allowing us to relax and unwind.

**Highlight: Bali CatPoo Chino**

On a hillside, a striking sign reads 'Bali CatPoo Chino'—one of the key attractions for civet coffee! Playing on the word 'cappuccino' (cat = cat, poo = poop), it’s quite amusing. We specifically ordered a cup of civet coffee, priced at **50,000 IDR** (approximately USD$3), to taste the legendary flavor. However, for us coffee novices, it didn’t taste much different from regular black coffee. Still, it was a unique experience.

**Shopping Area & Purchasing Tips**

As the tour nears its end, you’ll arrive at the product sales area. Shelves are filled with a variety of items like coffee powder, spiced tea, dried fruits, and more, making it hard to choose. Although we’d read online reviews stating that the products taken home might not be as rich as those enjoyed on-site, we were swayed by the enthusiastic staff and bought a pack of avocado coffee powder for **130,000 IDR** (approximately USD$8). Consider it the 'admission fee' for the 'free' tour!

**Special Recommendation: Other Options**

If the high-pressure shopping environment isn’t your thing, consider visiting **Bali Pulina**. According to online information, there’s an entrance fee of **100,000 IDR** (approximately USD$6), which includes coffee and tea tasting along with snacks, offering a more relaxed atmosphere. Additionally, the scenery there is said to be quite beautiful, providing a different kind of experience.

**A Unique Memory: A Cup of Civet Coffee**

Taking a moment during our journey to savor a cup of civet coffee, even though we 'coffee amateurs' might not fully appreciate its nuances, witnessing the production process and learning the story behind the coffee beans made it a worthwhile experience. This 'world’s most expensive coffee' offers not just a taste sensation but also a glimpse into Bali’s unique culture.

☕ **Are you curious about the taste of this coffee? When in Ubud, why not try it for yourself!**

🏡 **First Impressions of the Treehouse: A Perfect Blend of Nature and Luxury**

Sebatu Sanctuary Eco-resort is a renowned eco-friendly hotel in Bali, designed with sustainability in mind, where every detail seamlessly integrates with nature. Western travelers have long listed it as a **must-stay** in Bali, but it remains relatively unknown among Asian visitors.

Upon arrival, you'll immediately feel the professionalism and thoughtfulness:

- **Welcome Service:** Staff members remember your name and greet you with juice, coffee, or beer, instantly putting you at ease.
- **Dedicated Butler:** A WhatsApp group is created for you, allowing you to handle any requests—whether it's booking breakfast, activities, or room service—with great efficiency.
- **Attention to Detail:** Every corner of the room is adorned with carefully placed greenery or flowers, from the bedside to the staircase and even the bathroom, making you feel truly at home.

🌟 **Must-Book Room: The Two-story Bamboo Pod**

The room we chose was a two-story bamboo treehouse, exuding luxury:

1. **Lower Level:**
2. - Open-plan design with views of the jungle, featuring a spacious daybed for lounging.

- The bathroom is completely open-air with stable hot water and excellent privacy. Those who want to experience outdoor bathing will definitely fall in love with it!

3. **Upper Space:**
4. - 360-degree panoramic windows with tree branches right outside, creating a dreamlike atmosphere.

- There’s also a suspended hammock—highly recommended for photo ops!

💎 **Little Surprise:** All drinks in the fridge (beer, soda) and premium chips are complimentary, and coffee and tea bags are fully stocked!

🍽 **Dining Experience: Deliciousness in Paradise**

1. **Breakfast:**
2. - You can choose to dine at the restaurant, have it delivered to your room, or enjoy a floating breakfast in the pool—all at no extra cost, just book in advance.
3. **Dinner:**
4. - The hotel restaurant offers a rich menu with fresh ingredients and reasonable prices, making it a great choice to savor Bali’s flavors.

🌿 **Activity Recommendations: Intimate Moments with Nature**

Sebatu Sanctuary is not just about the stay itself—it also offers many nature-immersive activities:

1. **Forest Picnic (Romantic Picnic for 2):**
2. - Follow the butler on a 1.5 km walk deep into the forest to enjoy a picnic basket prepared by the chef, right next to a natural waterfall pool. This two-hour escape is perfect for unwinding!
3. **SPA Experience:**
4. - The prices are very affordable, with massage beds set up in your room or outdoors, accompanied by natural breezes and the sounds of birds and insects—you’ll feel relaxed even before the massage begins.
