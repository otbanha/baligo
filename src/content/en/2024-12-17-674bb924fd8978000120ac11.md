🏞️ **Why Choose Omma Bali?**

Most waterfall spots require hiking to reach, but **Omma Bali** stands out for its convenience:

1. **Private Elevator to Day Club**: Skip the tiring downhill walk, perfect for lazy travelers and families.
2. **Stunning Views**: Enjoy the waterfall's grandeur right from your table, effortlessly soaking in nature's beauty.
3. **Multiple Activities**: Choose to enter the pool (minimum spend required), admire the waterfall, or hike down for photos.

🍹 **Omma Bali Pricing Information**

- Non-alcoholic beverages and food prices are moderately acceptable, but alcoholic drinks are relatively expensive 🍸.

If you're in Ubud looking for a serene restaurant that offers classic Balinese cuisine away from the crowds, then Laka Leke Restaurant is your perfect choice. With its understated entrance, it hides a spacious Balinese garden, providing a comfortable dining environment and mouth-watering signature crispy duck.

This garden restaurant is located near our accommodation at Bali Bohemia Huts, just a 100-meter walk away. If you've just visited the Monkey Forest, you can head to the restaurant from Gate 3, making the journey very convenient.

🏡 **Restaurant Features and Atmosphere**

- **Garden Design**: The restaurant is divided into an indoor seating area in a 'traditional stilt house' and an outdoor seating area at the back. The rear seats offer views of lush rice terraces, creating a tranquil atmosphere that relaxes both body and mind.
- **Tradition and Entertainment**: The restaurant occasionally offers Balinese traditional dance performances and cooking classes, but you need to confirm the specific times with the restaurant in advance, as the schedule for classes and performances is currently irregular.
- **Avoiding Crowds**: For those who dislike the crowded scene at Ubud's super popular restaurant Bebek Bengil, coming here to enjoy crispy duck is a quieter and more comfortable option.

🍴 **Menu Recommendation: Signature Crispy Duck (Crispy Duck)**

- **Feature:**
  Half a duck pre-marinated with spices, fried to a golden crispy perfection. The skin crackles with each bite, with just the right amount of fat—never greasy. Even the bones are easily chewable, making it impossible to stop at just one bite.
- **Duck Texture:**
  While the duck meat itself leans towards being slightly dry, it pairs wonderfully with the restaurant’s three homemade sauces. Each sauce offers a different level of spiciness, delivering a fresh and delightful flavor that leaves you licking your fingers.

🌟 **Other Food Options**

Although the restaurant is most famous for its crispy duck, the other dishes are equally impressive:

1. **Lemon Chicken:**
   Tender chicken served with a tangy lemon sauce and soft potato balls, offering both flavor and satisfaction.
2. **Spaghetti Carbonara:**
   Rich and creamy sauce with a strong cheese flavor, perfectly cooked pasta—simple yet satisfying.
3. **Nasi Campur:**
   A classic Indonesian dish mixing various traditional ingredients. The version here is said to be very authentic and worth trying.

🍹 **Drink Recommendations**

- **Mango Smooth:** A rich mango flavor with a smooth texture, perfect for cooling down.
- **Coconut Juice:** Fresh coconut juice in its purest form, refreshing and thirst-quenching.
- **Ice Tea:** Light and refreshing, ideal for pairing with any meal.

💰 **Price Reference**

- **Crispy Duck Meal:** 140,000 IDR (~ USD$9)
- **Lemon Chicken:** 60,000 IDR (~ USD$4)
- **Spaghetti Carbonara:** 60,000 IDR (~ USD$4)
- **Mango Smooth:** 35,000 IDR (~ USD$2)
- **Coconut Juice:** 27,000 IDR (~ USD$2)
- **Ice Tea:** 18,000 IDR (~ USD$1)

《Laka Leke Restaurant》 is a place that perfectly blends traditional Balinese garden ambiance with classic cuisine. Whether it’s the spacious and comfortable dining environment, the unique design of traditional stilt houses, or the crispy texture of their signature duck, this restaurant is worth a visit.

If you want to avoid the crowds at Ubud’s popular restaurants but still don’t want to miss out on classic Balinese flavors, quickly add this understated yet stunning restaurant to your itinerary!

📍**Address & Transportation Tips:**

Located near Bali Bohemia, just a 100-meter walk from Monkey Forest Gate 3. It's recommended to visit the Monkey Forest in the morning and then walk directly here for lunch.

❗**Important Notes**: Multilingual paper guides (including Chinese) are available upon entry. A full walk around the sanctuary takes about 30-60 minutes. Mornings are less crowded with more monkeys, making it ideal for an early visit.

🐒 **Tips for Interacting with Monkeys**

While the monkeys in the sanctuary are relatively tame, it's important to follow these guidelines:

1. **Do not make direct eye contact with the monkeys**
2. **Do not carry plastic bags or bottles** (monkeys will snatch them)
3. **Do not touch or disturb the monkeys**
4. **Feeding the monkeys is prohibited**

Look up and you’ll see monkeys leaping between tree branches, while beneath your feet lies a forest path covered with banyan trees. Surrounding you are sacred Hindu temples. The harmony between the monkeys and the temples creates a serene atmosphere that is incredibly soothing.

🏛️ **17th-Century Ancient Temple**

Within the grounds stands a Hindu temple built in the 17th century. Although visitors cannot enter, the moss-covered temple walls are a sight to behold and worthy of admiration.

📸 **The Secret to Monkey Selfies**

Staff in the park offer professional monkey feeding and photo services, which require an additional purchase of a 50,000 IDR (approximately USD$2) photo ticket. They help capture fun moments like monkeys jumping onto your shoulders or feeding them—safe, efficient, and worth trying.

**⚠️ Note: Do not feed the monkeys yourself to avoid dangerous situations!**

**Rice Terrace Fee Details:**

1. Some cafes may charge an entrance fee;
2. Accessing rice terrace lands may require paying a landowner fee;
3. Each section of the terraces belongs to different landowners, and fees are charged separately.

To avoid these 'fee traps,' we recommend visiting the scenic and reasonably priced TIS Cafe. Not only is there no entrance fee, but you can also enjoy equally stunning rice terrace views—perfect for those who dislike long queues and aren't chasing Instagram fame! 😊

**Highlights of TIS Cafe:**

- ✅ **No entrance fee**, with views that rival famous spots.
- 💰 **Swing prices are friendly**, costing just 50,000 IDR (approx. USD$3) per swing.
- 🌊 **Kids can use the pool**, with a minimum spend of 100,000 IDR/person (approx. USD$6).
- 🍽 Delicious meals at affordable prices, served by warm and friendly staff.
- 👣 **Optional rice terrace donation**, pay 10,000 IDR once to access multiple terraces for photos (landowners are very kind).
- 📸 Free Instagram-worthy photo spots like hanging beds—absolutely worth it!

⚠️ Tips:

- Avoid peak hours for a more peaceful experience.
- Respect local customs when visiting; donations are optional, but it's thoughtful to bring small change in Indonesian Rupiah.

**Introduction to Tirta Empul Temple: Sacred Waters and Ancient Beliefs**

Tirta Empul Temple derives its name from the sacred spring that surrounds it. Within the temple, there are over a dozen water spouts, each symbolizing different prayer intentions, such as 'cleansing the soul,' 'warding off misfortune,' 'healing illnesses,' and 'banishing nightmares.' Local devotees complete a purification bathing ritual before entering the temple to pray, demonstrating their deep reverence for the divine.

**Visiting Information and Fees**

Visitors must purchase tickets to enter Tirta Empul Temple, and the fees are quite affordable:

- **Adult ticket: 50,000 IDR** (approximately USD$3)
- **Child ticket: 25,000 IDR** (approximately USD$2)

The ticket includes the rental of a traditional colorful sarong, which is essential for entering the temple. However, note that the colorful sarong cannot be used for entering the water. If you wish to experience the **sacred spring purification ritual**, additional fees apply:

- Green sarong rental: 10,000 IDR (approximately USD$1)
- Locker rental: 15,000 IDR (approximately USD$1)

These small fees are not only affordable but also allow you to deeply immerse yourself in this unique cultural tradition.

**How to Participate in the Sacred Spring Purification Ritual?**

The sacred spring purification ritual may seem simple, but it follows a specific order and rules:

1. **Prepare the green sarong and locker**, change your clothes, and proceed to the sacred spring pool.
2. Facing the spring pool, start the purification process from the **left pool**, beginning with the first water spout.
3. **Skip the second-to-last and third-to-last spouts** (these are reserved for honoring the deceased).
4. After completing the purification at the left pool, move to the **right pool**.
5. At the right pool, only the first spout needs to be honored.

At each water spout, you should follow these steps to complete your prayer:

- Place your palms together and make a wish facing the water spout.
- Wet your hair with the holy water and sprinkle it over your entire body.

The cool spring water instantly washes away the fatigue of travel, leaving you feeling light in body, mind, and spirit. This is a completely new cultural experience that is definitely worth trying!

**Touristy Yet Retaining Its Genuine Charm**

Although Tirta Empul Temple has become a popular tourist spot, with pools filled with Western visitors wearing green sarongs for purification rituals—a rather amusing sight—the temple still retains its local, unpretentious charm. The staff are warm and friendly, and there are no expensive additional fees, making you feel welcomed with genuine smiles.

⚠️ **Friendly Reminder: Respect Local Culture**

When experiencing the purification ritual, be sure to maintain solemnity and respect, as many local devotees are still engaged in devout prayers and bathing. Avoid excessive photography that may disturb others.

**Recommended Nearby Attraction: Pura Gunung Kawi**

If time permits, it’s worth visiting **Pura Gunung Kawi**, located not far from Tirta Empul Temple. This site features impressive rock carvings and stunning valley views, making it a cultural heritage site worth exploring near Ubud.

Additionally, Tirta Empul Temple is very close to **Sebatu Sanctuary Eco-Resort**, my favorite accommodation during this trip. Staying here makes itinerary planning convenient and is highly recommended!

**Experience the Spiritual Cleansing of Bali**

Tirta Empul Temple is not just a photo spot in Bali, but a spiritual place where you can deeply immerse yourself in local culture. Participating in the purification ritual and bathing in the clear spring water not only relaxes your body and mind but also adds a sacred and meaningful touch to your Bali journey.

**The Story Behind Kopi Luwak**

Kopi Luwak, known as **Kopi Luwak** in Indonesian and **Civet Coffee** in English, is famous for its unique production process where civets eat coffee cherries, and the beans are then excreted and specially processed. This has earned it the nickname 'cat poop coffee.'

As one of the main producers of Kopi Luwak, Bali has many tourist farms where visitors can tour and taste. Most farms do not charge an entrance fee and offer free samples of coffee and tea, often referred to as **Coffee/Tea Plantation Tour**. However, the free experience often comes with a catch—staff enthusiastically promote their products, which can be a bit pressuring.

**Exploring Satria Farm: Knowledge and Fun Combined**

Upon arriving at **Satria Agrowisata**, we were greeted by a lush botanical garden filled with various fruit trees, coffee plants, and spices like Mangosteen, Arabica Coffee, and Ginger. This garden gave us a preliminary understanding of the raw materials used in coffee production.

Next, the farm staff will introduce visitors to the process of making civet coffee, explaining how coffee beans are selected and cleaned from civet droppings. Surprisingly, even if there are only one or two visitors, the staff patiently explains everything with great enthusiasm and friendliness.

**A Surprising Experience with 13 Free Drinks**

After a brief introduction to coffee production, the staff served us **13 different beverages** for free tasting, including various coffees, teas, and specialty drinks, each with unique flavors and benefits. Sitting under the lush trees sipping these drinks was not only a rich taste experience but also visually delightful, helping us relax both body and mind.

**Highlight: Bali CatPoo Chino**

On a hillside, a striking sign reads 'Bali CatPoo Chino'—one of the key attractions of civet coffee! Playing on the word 'cappuccino' (cat = cat, poo = poop), it’s quite amusing. We specifically ordered a cup of civet coffee priced at **IDR 50,000** (approximately USD $3) to taste this legendary flavor. However, for those of us who aren’t coffee connoisseurs, it didn’t taste much different from regular black coffee—just a unique experience.

**Shopping Area and Purchase Insights**

As the tour nears its end, you’ll arrive at the product sales area. Shelves are filled with a variety of items like coffee powder, spiced teas, dried fruits, and more, making it hard to choose. Although online reviews mentioned that products taken home might not taste as rich as those enjoyed on-site, we were swayed by the staff’s enthusiastic sales pitch and bought a pack of avocado coffee powder for **IDR 130,000** (approximately USD $8). Consider it the 'admission fee' for the 'free' tour!

**Special Tip: Other Options**

If the pushy shopping environment isn’t for you, consider visiting **Bali Pulina**. According to online information, there’s an entrance fee of **IDR 100,000** (approximately USD $6), which includes coffee and tea tastings along with snacks, offering a more relaxed and pleasant atmosphere. Additionally, the scenery there is said to be quite beautiful, providing a different kind of experience.

**A Unique Memory with a Cup of Civet Coffee**

Taking a moment during our journey to savor a cup of civet coffee, even for us 'coffee novices,' the flavors might be hard to distinguish. However, witnessing the production process firsthand and learning the story behind the coffee beans makes it a worthwhile experience. This cup of 'the world's most expensive coffee' offers not just a taste sensation but also a glimpse into Bali's unique culture.
