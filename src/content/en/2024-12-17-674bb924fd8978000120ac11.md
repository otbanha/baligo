### Bali Garden Restaurant: Savoring Crispy Duck 🍗 at Laka Leke Restaurant

If you're in Ubud looking for a serene spot to enjoy classic Balinese cuisine away from the crowds, then Laka Leke Restaurant is your perfect choice. With its unassuming entrance, it hides a spacious Balinese garden, offering a comfortable dining environment and mouth-watering signature crispy duck.

This garden restaurant is located near our accommodation **Bali Bohemia Huts**, just a 100-meter walk away. If you've just visited the Monkey Forest, you can head to the restaurant from Gate 3, making it very convenient.

🏡 **Restaurant Features and Atmosphere**

- **Garden Design:** The restaurant is divided into a traditional high-house indoor seating area and an outdoor seating area at the back. The back area offers a view of lush rice terraces, creating a tranquil atmosphere that relaxes both body and mind.
- **Tradition and Entertainment:** The restaurant occasionally offers traditional Balinese dance performances and cooking classes, but you'll need to confirm the specific times with the restaurant in advance, as the schedule is currently irregular.
- **Avoiding Crowds:** For those who dislike the bustling crowds at Ubud's popular restaurant Bebek Bengil, coming here to enjoy crispy duck is a quieter and more comfortable option.

🍴 **Dish Recommendation: Signature Crispy Duck**

- **Features:** 
  Half a duck pre-marinated with spices, fried to a golden crisp. The skin is crunchy with just the right amount of fat, not greasy at all. Even the bones are easy to chew, making it irresistible.
- **Duck Texture:** 
  Although the duck meat itself is a bit dry, it becomes much more flavorful when paired with the restaurant's three homemade sauces. Each sauce has a different level of spiciness, offering a fresh and delicious taste that leaves you wanting more.

🌟 **Other Food Options**

While the restaurant is most famous for its crispy duck, other dishes also shine:

1. **Lemon Chicken:** Tender chicken paired with tangy lemon sauce and soft potato balls - flavorful and satisfying.
2. **Spaghetti Carbonara:** Rich, creamy sauce with perfectly cooked pasta - simple yet delightful.
3. **Nasi Campur:** A classic Indonesian dish with a mix of traditional ingredients. The version here is said to be very authentic and worth trying.

🍹 **Drink Recommendations**

- **Mango Smoothie:** Rich mango flavor with a smooth texture, perfect for cooling off.
- **Coconut Juice:** Fresh coconut water, refreshing and hydrating.
- **Ice Tea:** Light and refreshing, pairs well with any dish.

💰 **Price Reference**

- **Crispy Duck:** 140,000 IDR (~USD$9)
- **Lemon Chicken:** 60,000 IDR (~USD$4)
- **Spaghetti Carbonara:** 60,000 IDR (~USD$4)
- **Mango Smoothie:** 35,000 IDR (~USD$2)
- **Coconut Juice:** 27,000 IDR (~USD$2)

Laka Leke Restaurant beautifully combines traditional Balinese garden ambiance with classic cuisine. Whether it's the spacious and comfortable dining environment, the unique design of traditional stilt houses, or the crispy texture of their signature duck, this place is worth a visit.

If you want to avoid the crowds at Ubud's popular restaurants but still don't want to miss out on classic Balinese flavors, add this underrated yet impressive restaurant to your itinerary!

Located near Bali Bohemia, just a 100-meter walk from Monkey Forest Gate 3. It's recommended to visit after a morning trip to the Monkey Forest and walk directly here for lunch.

🌞 **Relaxing Life Next to the Monkey Forest**

The biggest advantage of staying next to the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary is being able to leisurely enjoy the morning. Take your time with breakfast and coffee, then stroll over to the sanctuary to easily start your monkey-filled adventure.

💡 Recommendation: **Monkey Cave Expresso**

🌳 **Monkey Forest Basics**

📍**Location**: Search for **Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary** to find the main entrance.

🕘**Opening Hours**: 09:00 - 18:00 (last entry at 17:00).

💰**Ticket Prices**:

- Adults: 80,000 IDR (~USD$5)
- Children: 60,000 IDR (~USD$4)

❗**Tips**: Upon entry, you can pick up a multilingual paper guide map (including Chinese). Walking through the sanctuary takes about 30-60 minutes. Mornings are less crowded with more monkeys, making it ideal for an early visit.

🐒 **Monkey Interaction Tips**

Although the monkeys in the sanctuary are relatively gentle, please follow these rules:

1. **Do not make direct eye contact with the monkeys**
2. **Do not carry plastic bags or bottles** (monkeys may snatch them)
3. **Do not touch or disturb the monkeys**
4. **Feeding the monkeys is prohibited**

Look up to see monkeys leaping between treetops, walk along forest paths lined with banyan trees, and admire the surrounding sacred Hindu temples. The harmonious coexistence of monkeys and temples creates a serene and healing atmosphere.

🏛️ **17th-Century Ancient Temples**

Within the park stands a 17th-century Hindu temple. While visitors cannot enter, the moss-covered temple walls create a sight so captivating it's worth the visit alone.

📸 **The Secret Weapon for Monkey Selfies**

Park staff offer professional monkey feeding and photography services for an additional IDR 50,000 (approximately USD$3). They expertly capture moments like monkeys jumping onto your shoulders or feeding interactions—safe, efficient, and definitely worth trying.

## Day 5 Itinerary

### Tirta Empul Temple – A Sacred Experience of Spiritual Cleansing

**The Serenity of Tirta Empul: A Sacred Ritual for Purifying the Soul**

On the fifth day, we visited **Tirta Empul Temple (Pura Tirta Empul)**, a temple renowned for its sacred spring water and a significant symbol of Bali's religion and culture. Walking through the lush coconut grove, we were greeted by ancient stone structures and crystal-clear spring water, as if stepping into a timeless journey. The sacred spring here is regarded by locals as a sanctuary for purifying the soul, attracting countless devotees for blessings and spiritual cleansing over the centuries.

**Introduction to Tirta Empul Temple: Sacred Waters and Ancient Faith**

Tirta Empul Temple is named after its surrounding sacred spring. Inside the temple, there are more than ten different water spouts, each symbolizing a unique prayer intention, such as "cleansing the soul," "warding off misfortune," "healing illnesses," and "banishing nightmares." Local devotees often complete the purification ritual in the spring before entering the temple to pray, demonstrating their deep devotion to the deities.

**Visitor Information and Fees**

Visitors need to purchase tickets to enter Tirta Empul Temple, and the fees are quite affordable:

- **Adult Ticket: 50,000 IDR** (approximately USD$3)
- **Child Ticket: 25,000 IDR** (approximately USD$2)

The entrance fee includes the rental of a traditional colored sarong, which is essential for temple visits. Note that these sarongs are not meant to be worn in water. If you wish to experience the **Holy Spring Purification Ritual**, additional fees apply:

- Green sarong rental: 10,000 IDR (approximately USD$0.70)
- Locker rental: 15,000 IDR (approximately USD$1)

These small fees are affordable and allow you to deeply immerse yourself in this unique cultural tradition.

**How to Participate in the Holy Spring Purification Ritual?**

The Holy Spring Purification Ritual may seem simple, but it follows a specific order and rules:

1. **Prepare your green sarong and locker**, change your clothes, and head to the holy spring pool.
2. Facing the pool, start purifying from the **left pool**, beginning with the first water outlet.
3. **Skip the second-to-last and third-to-last outlets** (these are reserved for ancestral worship).
4. After completing the purification at the left outlets, move to the **right pool**.
5. At the right pool, only worship at the first outlet.

At each water outlet, follow these steps to complete your prayer:

- Place your hands together in prayer and make a wish facing the outlet.
- Wet your hair with the holy water and sprinkle it over your body.

The cool spring water instantly washes away travel fatigue, leaving you feeling light in body, mind, and spirit. This is a completely new cultural experience that is absolutely worth trying!

**Touristy Yet Still Retaining Genuine Warmth**

Although Tirta Empul Temple has become a popular tourist destination, with pools filled with Western visitors dressed in green sarongs participating in purification rituals, the scene is quite fascinating. Yet, the temple retains its local charm and warm hospitality. The staff are friendly and welcoming, with no expensive hidden fees, making visitors feel a genuine sense of kindness and smiles.

⚠️ **Gentle Reminder: Respect Local Culture**

When experiencing the purification ritual, it’s essential to remain solemn and respectful, as many local devotees are still engaged in devout prayers and bathing. Avoid excessive photography that might disturb others.

**Extended Attraction Recommendation: Gunung Kawi Temple**

If time permits, consider visiting **Gunung Kawi Temple**, located not far from Tirta Empul Temple. This site features stunning rock carvings and enchanting valley views, making it a cultural gem worth exploring near Ubud.

Additionally, Tirta Empul Temple is very close to **Sebatu Sanctuary Eco-Resort**, my favorite accommodation during this trip. Staying here makes itinerary planning convenient and is highly recommended!

**Experience Bali’s Spiritual Cleansing**

Tirta Empul Temple is not just a photo spot in Bali but also a spiritual place to deeply connect with local culture. Participating in the purification ritual and bathing in the clear spring water not only relaxes the body and mind but also adds a sacred and meaningful touch to your Bali journey.

✨ **Why not personally experience this spiritual cleansing, making your trip more than just a superficial tour!**

### The World’s Most Expensive Coffee Tour: Savor Your First Cup of Luwak Coffee

During our journey in Ubud, we embarked on an exploration of Kopi Luwak, renowned as the world's most expensive coffee. Its unique production process and distinctive flavor make it an unmissable experience for travelers in Bali. For this adventure, we chose to visit **Satria Agrowisata** farm on the island, where we witnessed firsthand the birth of this legendary coffee.

**The Story Behind Kopi Luwak**

Kopi Luwak, known as **Civet Coffee** in English, is a coffee with a unique production process. The civet cat consumes coffee cherries, and after the beans undergo enzymatic changes in its digestive system, they are excreted. This process gives rise to what is humorously referred to as 'cat poop coffee.'

Bali, one of the primary producers of Kopi Luwak, boasts numerous tourist farms where visitors can explore and taste this specialty. Most farms do not charge an entrance fee and offer free samples of coffee and tea, often referred to as a **Coffee/Tea Plantation Tour**. However, the 'free' experience often comes with a catch—staff enthusiastically promote their products, which can feel a bit pressuring.

**Exploring Satria Agrowisata: A Blend of Knowledge and Fun**

Upon arriving at **Satria Agrowisata**, we were greeted by a lush botanical garden filled with various fruit trees, coffee plants, and spices like mangosteen, Arabica coffee, and ginger. This garden provided us with a foundational understanding of the raw materials used in coffee production.

Next, the farm staff introduced us to the Kopi Luwak production process, explaining how coffee beans are selected and cleaned from the civet's excrement. Surprisingly, even with just one or two visitors, the staff patiently explained every detail, showcasing their warmth and friendliness.

**A Surprising Experience: 13 Free Beverages**

After a brief introduction to coffee production, the staff served us **13 different beverages** to sample for free, including various coffees, teas, and specialty drinks, each with its unique flavor and benefits. Sitting under the lush trees, sipping these drinks was not only a treat for the palate but also a visual delight, offering a moment of relaxation.

**Highlight: Bali CatPoo Chino**

On a hillside, a prominent sign catches the eye with its playful 'Bali CatPoo Chino' lettering – highlighting one of the island's most famous products: civet coffee! Playing on the word 'cappuccino' (cat = feline, poo = excrement), it's an amusing concept. We decided to try a cup of this legendary brew for **50,000 IDR** (approximately USD$3). While it didn't taste drastically different from regular black coffee to our untrained palates, it was certainly a unique experience.

**Shopping Area & Purchasing Tips**

As the tour concluded, we entered the merchandise area. Shelves were stocked with an array of products – coffee grounds, spiced teas, dried fruits – creating a feast for the eyes. Although online reviews had warned that products taken home might not match the intensity of the freshly brewed samples, we were swayed by the enthusiastic staff and purchased a pack of avocado coffee powder for **130,000 IDR** (around USD$8). Consider it our contribution towards the 'free' tour!

**Special Recommendation: Alternative Options**

If the high-pressure sales environment feels overwhelming, consider visiting **Bali Pulina** instead. According to online information, there's an entrance fee of **100,000 IDR** (approximately USD$6), which includes coffee and tea tastings accompanied by snacks. The atmosphere is more relaxed and enjoyable. Additionally, the scenic views there are said to be quite beautiful, offering a different kind of experience.

**A Unique Memory: A Cup of Civet Coffee**

Taking a pause during our journey to savor a cup of civet coffee, even though we 'coffee novices' might not discern its distinctive flavors, witnessing the production process and learning about the beans' backstory made it a worthwhile experience. This 'world's most expensive coffee' not only tantalized our taste buds but also introduced us to a unique aspect of Balinese culture.

☕ **Curious about the taste of this coffee? When in Ubud, why not experience it for yourself!**

**—Get up close with an active volcano, witness a magnificent sunrise, and explore black lava wonders—**

The Mount Batur sunrise trek in Kintamani connects you with nature's purity. Whether you're an avid hiker or a relaxed traveler, you'll find your own way to appreciate the breathtaking beauty of this active volcano!

**Two Ways to Experience the Volcano Adventure**

🔥 **1. Hiking Challenge: The Thrilling Sunrise Volcano Trek**

For energetic and adventurous travelers, challenge yourself with a **sunrise volcano hike**. Reaching the summit to witness the sunrise over the crater is a breathtaking experience. This journey is both thrilling and rewarding, perfect for those seeking adventure and extreme challenges ❤️‍🔥.

🚜 **2. Relaxed Enjoyment Group: Jeep Sunrise Tour**

If you're part of the 'elderly, weak, women, and children' group 🤣, or simply want to enjoy the scenery without exertion, then booking a stylish jeep for a leisurely **Black Lava Tour** is your best choice!

⏰ Itinerary Overview:

- **05:00** Jeep picks you up from your accommodation 🚜
- Drive up winding mountain roads (approx. 40-50 minutes)
- Arrive at a pavilion to prepare for the magnificent sunrise ☀️
- Enjoy the sunrise while sipping hot tea, coffee, and breakfast ☕️🍳
- Explore the Black Lava and Black Sand wonders 🪨
- Visit the lake temple, Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur 🙏
- **10:00** Return to your accommodation 🏡

🛻 **Jeep Tour Highlights**

1. **Stunning Sunrise:**  
   The pavilion at the summit offers an unparalleled panoramic view of Mount Batur. The driver doubles as a professional photographer, capturing romantic silhouettes of you with the sunrise and volcano 📸—perfect for sharing on social media!
2. **Exploring Black Lava:**  
   The post-sunrise lava adventure is exhilarating. The jeep navigates rugged volcanic terrain, bouncing up and down, making your heart race as you hold on tight 💥.
3. **Desert-like Black Sand:**  
   The Black Sand landscape resembles a miniature desert. Driving over the dunes feels like entering another world, immersing you in boundless vastness 👀.
4. **Lake Temple Visit:**  
   The final stop, Pura Segara Ulun Danu Batur, may not be as famous as Pura Ulun Danu Bratan, but its serene simplicity is equally refreshing. Entry is free, with optional donations 🙏.

💰 **Jeep Tour Cost Reference**

- **Standard Rate**: 800,000 IDR/vehicle (without breakfast) or 850,000 IDR/vehicle (with simple breakfast 🍽)
- **Price Negotiation Tip:**  
   Ask your accommodation host or driver to help negotiate; prices can often be reduced to **700,000 IDR - 750,000 IDR/vehicle** (without breakfast).
- Maximum capacity: **three adults and one child** or **two adults and two children**.

❄️ **Reminder: Bring Warm Clothing**

The Mount Batur sunrise trek requires an early morning start. As temperatures are lower in the mountainous area, it's advisable to bring warm clothing (such as a jacket, beanie, and scarf) 🧥🧢🧣, otherwise you might shiver while waiting for the sunrise.
