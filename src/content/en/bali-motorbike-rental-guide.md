Below are explanations for each comparison point:

### **Flexibility:**

> **Motorcycle Rental:** Offers the highest flexibility, allowing you to decide where to go and how long to stay at each place. It provides the utmost freedom for adventurous travelers who love to explore.

> **Private Car:** Requires pre-planning of the itinerary/attractions, but still allows you to move at your own pace.

### Price:

> **Renting a Motorcycle:** The most affordable option, costing around $3 to $5 USD per day. A budget-friendly choice for travelers.

> **Private Car Hire:** More expensive, averaging around $40 to $50 USD per day. While pricier, it offers a more comfortable and safer mode of transportation. The price includes a driver, a five-seater car, fuel, the driver's meals, and parking fees. Toll fees and entrance tickets are not included. (Check out the [Complete Guide to Private Car Hire in Bali](/blog/2024-01-12-65a0a163fd8978000115f37a/))

### Required Documents:

> **Renting a Motorcycle:** You must first obtain an international driver's license. Tourists must hold an international driver's license and ensure the vehicle has a valid STNK (vehicle registration) to comply with local regulations.

> **Private Car Hire:** No documents are required. Eliminates the need for a driver's license, making it convenient for those without a motorcycle or international license.

### Safety:

> **Renting a Motorcycle:** Safety is low due to Bali's congested traffic and reckless driving by foreigners who may have never ridden a motorcycle before. Additionally, in case of an accident, remember the 'foreigner is always guilty' rule—regardless of fault, remain humble and offer to pay for damages. If the accident causes significant property damage or injury, criminal charges may be involved. Trust me, this will be a very bad situation for you. Besides staying alert and following traffic rules, you might want to pray for safety beforehand. There have also been incidents where Taiwanese tourists experienced flat tires in remote areas, making accidents unpredictable.

> Recently, a news story on international Bali travel forums has garnered attention: a Polish couple had an accident while riding a motorcycle on Nusa Penida, resulting in one fatality and severe injuries to the other. Tragedies involving self-driving tourists in Bali are frequent, and travelers should reconsider driving themselves to avoid irreversible losses! Police also urge rental companies to ensure renters have driving skills before handing over keys and recommend tourists check their travel insurance details before riding. Many tourists rent vehicles without travel insurance or understanding coverage limits, leaving them unprotected in case of accidents.

> **Private Car Hire:** Highest safety, with professional drivers reducing all traffic-related risks.

### Police Checks:

> **Renting a Scooter:** Foreign tourists often get stopped by police for not wearing helmets or violating traffic rules, resulting in fines. Many tourists also report being asked for money without reason during random checks. Be aware of traffic laws and police inspections.

> **Private Car Hire:** Not subject to these restrictions. Eliminates the risk of being stopped by police for traffic violations or random checks.

### Too Much Sun or Rain:

> **Renting a Scooter:** Prone to sunburn or getting soaked, especially when stuck in traffic.

> **Private Car Hire:** Avoids exposure to harsh sun or rain, providing a more comfortable travel experience.

### Guide Services:

> **Renting a Scooter:** No guide services available; requires self-research for sightseeing information. Suitable for travelers who enjoy independent adventures.

> **Private Car Hire:** Drivers can offer guided tours, helping you understand Bali's culture and history. Ideal for travelers seeking deeper local insights.

### Discovering Hidden Food Gems:

> **Renting a Scooter:** Hard to find hidden local food spots; relies on personal exploration.

> **Private Car Hire:** Drivers are usually familiar with local dining options and can recommend specialty dishes, offering a richer culinary experience.

### Parking:

> **Motorbike Rental:** Parking spaces are limited, and it's not easy to find parking in busy areas. You'll need to spend more time searching for suitable parking spots.

> **Private Car Hire:** You don't need to worry about parking at all. While you go explore, the driver will handle the parking.

### Getting Lost:

> **Motorbike Rental:** Bali's roads are winding, and those unfamiliar with local conditions can easily get lost. Relying on GPS often leads to impassable routes.

> **Private Car Hire:** The driver acts as your navigator, eliminating the worry of getting lost and providing a more secure travel experience.

### Luggage Care:

> **Renting a Scooter:** You'll need to take care of your own luggage and consider how to safely secure it.

> **Private Car Hire:** The driver can help look after your luggage, allowing you to enjoy your trip with peace of mind.

### Suitable for Longer Journeys

> **Renting a Scooter:** Although Bali isn't very large, many attractions are more than an hour apart, making scooters less ideal for sightseeing.

> **Private Car Hire:** While cars may not be as nimble as scooters on the road, they offer air conditioning, scenic drives, conversation, and even naps, making them better suited for long trips.

### Professional Photographer

> **Renting a Scooter:** Traveling by scooter with just two people. Unless you bring a tripod, it can be difficult to take photos with Bali's stunning landscapes.

> **Private Car Hire:** Most Bali private car drivers have decent photography skills and know the best spots for capturing great travel photos. Usually, just by asking, they can take many photos you'll be happy with!

One reason some people hesitate to hire private drivers is their lack of English proficiency. However, many of our group members have reported that even with limited English, using Google Translate allows for easy basic communication with drivers. There's also a powerful translation app called [VoiceTra](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=jp.go.nict.voicetra&hl=zh_TW&pli=1), which supports Chinese to English and Chinese to Indonesian translations.
