**Bottom Line First**: Most Bali travelers are advised to **hire a private car** rather than rent a scooter. Renting a scooter costs around Rp 50,000–80,000 per day, offering the lowest price and highest mobility, but requires an international driver's license, carries a high risk of accidents (with serious liability for foreigners), and often involves police stops. Hiring a private car costs about Rp 600,000–800,000 per day (including driver, fuel, and parking), providing safety and comfort with no documents needed. Only travelers with scooter experience and itineraries focused on a single area should consider renting. Below is a full comparison across 12 points.

When exploring this beautiful island, **choosing your mode of travel** is a decision that requires careful thought. Should you opt for the freedom of renting a scooter, or enjoy the comfort of a private car? This 'Bali Scooter Rental Guide' takes you deep into the pros and cons of both options, helping you make an informed choice for your Bali adventure. Whether you're seeking thrills or prioritizing comfort and safety, this guide offers practical advice to ensure an unforgettable Bali trip.

## 12-Point Comparison: Private Car vs. Scooter Rental

![12項比較項目：包車 VS 租摩托車](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d4d13794f3492c137a0f6b2dd1600eb6.png)

Here's a breakdown of each comparison point:

### **Flexibility:**

- **Scooter Rental:** Offers the highest flexibility, letting you go wherever you want and stop for as long as you like. For adventurous, exploratory travelers, it provides maximum freedom.
- **Private Car:** Itinerary and spots must be planned in advance, but you can still move at your own pace.

### Cost:

- **Scooter Rental:** The lowest cost, around 50,000 to 80,000 Indonesian Rupiah per day. An economical choice for budget-conscious travelers.
- **Private Car:** Higher cost, averaging 600,000 to 800,000 Indonesian Rupiah per day. More expensive, but offers a more comfortable and safe mode of transport. The price includes a driver, a five-seater car, fuel, the driver's meals, and parking fees. Tolls and entrance fees are not included. (See [Bali Private Car Hire Guide](/en/blog/bali-private-car-hire-guide/))

### Required Documents:

1. **Renting a Scooter:** You must first apply for an International Driving Permit. Tourists need to hold an International Driving Permit and ensure the vehicle has a valid STNK (vehicle registration) to comply with local regulations.
2. **Private Car Hire:** No documents are required. This eliminates the hassle of driving permits, making it convenient for tourists who don’t have a motorcycle license or International Driving Permit.

### Safety:

- **Renting a Scooter:** Safety is very low because Bali’s traffic is congested, and many foreigners on the road have never ridden a scooter before, driving recklessly. Moreover, if an accident occurs, remember the reality that “foreigners are always at fault.” Regardless of who’s to blame, stay humble and proactively offer to pay for damages. If the accident causes significant damage to someone else’s property or injures another person, serious cases may involve criminal law. Trust me, this will put you in a very bad situation. Besides staying alert and following traffic rules, you might want to pray for safety beforehand. There have also been cases of Taiwanese tourists getting a flat tire on a scooter in remote areas—unexpected mishaps are hard to avoid.

A recent news story that sparked heated discussion on overseas Bali travel forums involved a Polish couple who had an accident while riding a scooter on the nearby island of Nusa Penida during their vacation. One died on the spot, and the other was seriously injured. Tragedies involving tourists driving themselves in Bali are all too common. Travelers should reconsider driving their own vehicles in Bali to avoid such heartbreaking outcomes. Police also urge rental companies to ensure renters have driving skills before handing over the keys and advise tourists to check their travel insurance details before riding. Many tourists don’t have travel insurance or don’t understand the scope of coverage before renting, leaving them unprotected in the event of an accident.
- **Private Car Hire:** The safest option, as a professional driver handles everything, eliminating all risks associated with traffic.

### Police Checks:

- **Renting a Scooter:** Foreign tourists on scooters often get pulled over for not wearing helmets or violating traffic rules, leading to fines. Many also encounter random checkpoints where police demand money without reason. Be mindful of traffic laws and police inspections.
- **Private Car Hire:** Not subject to this issue. It avoids the risk of being stopped by police for traffic violations or random checks.

### Extreme Sun or Rain:

- **Renting a Scooter:** You’re prone to sunburn or getting soaked, especially when stuck in traffic jams.
- **Private Car Hire:** No need to brave the scorching sun or rain, offering a more comfortable travel environment.

### Guide Service:

- **Renting a Scooter:** No guide service — you’ll need to research attractions on your own. Best for independent travelers who love adventure.
- **Private Car:** The driver can offer guided commentary, helping you understand Bali’s culture and history. Ideal for travelers who want a deeper local experience.

### Hidden Local Food Gems:

- **Renting a Scooter:** Hard to discover hidden local food spots — relies on your own exploration.
- **Private Car:** Drivers are usually familiar with local dining options and can recommend specialty dishes, offering a richer culinary experience.

### Parking:

- **Renting a Scooter:** Limited parking — difficult to find spots in busy areas. Requires extra effort to locate suitable parking.
- **Private Car:** No parking worries at all — you hop out to explore, and the driver handles parking.

### Getting Lost:

- **Renting a Scooter:** Bali’s roads are winding and unfamiliar to newcomers, making it easy to get lost. GPS often leads to impassable routes.
- **Private Car:** The driver is your built-in navigator — no need to worry about getting lost, offering a more stress-free travel experience.

### Luggage Security:

- **Renting a Scooter:** You must keep an eye on your luggage and figure out how to secure it safely.
- **Private Car:** The driver can watch your luggage, letting you explore with greater peace of mind.

### For Longer Distances

- **Renting a Scooter:** Although Bali isn't huge, many attractions are over an hour apart, making scooter rental less ideal for hopping between sights.
- **Private Car Hire:** Using a private car to visit attractions may not be as nimble as a scooter on the road, but you can enjoy air conditioning, sightseeing, chat, or even take a nap—making it much better for longer trips.

### For Photography Enthusiasts

- **Renting a Scooter:** Traveling on your own with two people on a scooter makes it hard to take photos with Bali's stunning scenery unless you bring a tripod.
- **Private Car Hire:** Most Bali private car drivers have decent photography skills and know the best spots for capturing great travel photos. Just ask, and your driver can help you take plenty of beautiful shots you'll love!

## Final Thoughts

Renting a scooter in Bali might seem cheaper at first glance, but it comes with many more considerations—and if an accident happens, you may have to cancel your plans or even extend your stay by several days. It's easy to lose more than you save. On the other hand, hiring a private driver or using Gojek offers a more convenient and worry-free travel experience.

Ultimately, you now know how to make a smart choice between renting a scooter and hiring a driver based on your preferences and needs, ensuring a smoother and more enjoyable adventure in Bali.
