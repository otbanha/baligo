# Bali Travel Hot Topics + News Roundup (2026/7/17)

Here are 5 key travel updates worth noting, covering transportation, attractions, culture, safety, and tourism industry trends—grasp them in just 2 minutes before your trip.

### ✈️ Riding a Scooter Without a Helmet: Foreign Tourists Risk Deportation

Bali police have recently reiterated a crackdown on traffic violations by foreign tourists, including riding without a helmet, driving without a license, and running red lights. For serious offenses, in addition to fines, violators may be referred to immigration authorities and face deportation. Travelers planning to rent a scooter are reminded to wear a helmet, carry a valid international driver's license, and follow local traffic rules to avoid disrupting their trip.

### 🏝️ Nusa Penida Sees More Visitors, But Hotel Occupancy Drops

The Indonesian Hotel and Restaurant Association (PHRI) notes that while the number of visitors to Nusa Penida has surged during the summer break, many opt for day trips, leaving hotel occupancy rates stagnant. To avoid crowds and enjoy the island's sunrise and sunset seascapes, consider staying overnight for a different experience from a day trip.

### 🎉 Lembongan and Nusa Penida Summer Water Activities Enter Peak Season

As July marks the start of the dry season, demand for water activities like snorkeling, diving, SUP, and kayaking surges. Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Penida, and Amed have become popular destinations. If you're planning to join marine activities, it's recommended to choose licensed operators and check daily sea conditions to avoid disruptions from wind and waves.

### ⚠️ Blue Lagoon Viral Incident Sparks Discussion, Travelers Advised to Avoid Peak Hours

Padang Bai's Blue Lagoon Beach recently went viral after a video of an interaction between a traveler and a local vendor sparked online debate. While the spot remains open and visitor numbers haven't been significantly affected, it's a reminder that popular beaches can get crowded, especially during peak hours from noon to afternoon. For a better experience, it's best to arrive early and be respectful to vendors and other travelers.

### 📊 Komodo National Park Attracts Over 200,000 Visitors in Six Months, 'Bali + Komodo' Twin-Island Trips Continue to Gain Popularity

Indonesian authorities reported that Komodo National Park welcomed over 200,000 visitors in the first half of this year. Although numbers dipped briefly in January due to weather, they quickly rebounded. More international travelers are now combining Bali and Komodo Island into a single trip. If you're planning to go, it's wise to book domestic flights and liveaboard tours well in advance to avoid sold-out situations during peak season.
