# Bali Travel Hot Topics & News Roundup (2026/7/17)

Here are 5 key travel updates worth noting, covering transportation, attractions, culture, safety, and tourism industry trends—take 2 minutes to catch up before you go.

### ✈️ Riding Scooters Without a Helmet: Foreign Tourists Risk Deportation

Bali police have recently reiterated that they will intensify crackdowns on foreign tourists violating scooter rules, including not wearing a helmet, riding without a license, and running red lights. For serious violations, in addition to fines, offenders may be referred to immigration authorities and face deportation. Travelers planning to rent a scooter are reminded to always wear a helmet, carry a valid international driver's license, and follow local traffic laws to avoid disrupting their trip.

### 🏝️ Nusa Penida Sees More Visitors, But Hotel Occupancy Drops

The Indonesian Hotel and Restaurant Association (PHRI) reports that while the number of visitors to Nusa Penida has surged during the summer break, many opt for day trips, leaving local hotel occupancy rates stagnant. To avoid crowds and enjoy the island's sunrise and sunset seascapes, consider staying overnight for a different experience from a day trip.

### 🎉 Lembongan and Nusa Penida Enter Peak Season for Summer Water Activities

As July marks the start of the dry season, demand for water activities like snorkeling, diving, SUP, and kayaking surges, with Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Penida, and Amed becoming popular destinations. If you're planning to join marine activities, it's advisable to choose licensed operators and keep an eye on daily sea condition reports to avoid disruptions from wind and waves.

### ⚠️ Blue Lagoon Viral Incident Sparks Discussion, Travelers Advised to Avoid Peak Hours

Padang Bai's Blue Lagoon Beach recently went viral online after a video of an interaction between a traveler and a local vendor sparked heated discussions. Although the spot remains open and visitor numbers haven't been significantly affected, it's a reminder that popular beaches often face seating shortages during peak hours from noon to afternoon. For a better experience, it's recommended to arrive early and respect vendors and other travelers.

### 📊 Komodo National Park Attracts Over 200,000 Visitors in Six Months; 'Bali + Komodo' Twin-Island Itineraries Continue to Gain Popularity

Indonesian authorities have reported that Komodo National Park welcomed over 200,000 visitors in the first half of this year. Although numbers briefly dipped in January due to weather, they quickly rebounded. In recent years, more international travelers are combining Bali and Komodo Island into a single trip. If you're planning to go, it's wise to book domestic flights and liveaboard tours in advance to avoid peak-season shortages.
