## Learn How to Avoid Crowds and Save Money

Alas Harum and Cretya Ubud are places where you can spend an entire day in Bali! Imagine escaping the hustle and bustle of daily life and immersing yourself in the captivating world of Cretya and Alas Harum. This destination is perfect for both adventure seekers and those looking for a peaceful retreat.

Cretya Ubud, part of Alas Harum, is an absolutely secluded, luxurious, and fun three-tiered pool club that lets you escape the noise, find tranquility, and blend into the beauty of nature.

This kind of upscale pool club usually doesn't come cheap! Let's talk about how to enjoy the luxury of Cretya Ubud's pool club without spending a fortune.

The entry fee for Alas Harum is about $3.50 USD, which grants you access to all of Alas Harum's facilities. Inside, Cretya Ubud Pool Club offers a variety of options, including cabanas, day beds, hammocks, and VIP lounge areas, where you can sip your favorite cocktails and enjoy the surrounding natural beauty. However, all of these options come with a minimum spend, ranging from $130 to $260 USD.

Yet our entire day's cost was just $20 (no minimum spend) for a poolside spot, including food and drinks—how did we manage that?

First, make sure you arrive early so you can grab the best seats! The prime spot is on the ground floor pool, directly across from the shelves and changing rooms—this is the ultimate sweet spot: shaded and with direct pool access, no need to spend over $200 on a daybed under the scorching sun. These seats have no minimum spend, meaning you don't have to pay extra!

## Reminders

Before you come, there are a few things you should know. Alas Harum is family-friendly, but Cretya is mainly for adults. The three-tiered pool area, including the bar and restaurant, is restricted to guests aged 18 and above.

In the pool area, visitors no longer need to carry their passports. However, identity checks may still occur. So, bring some form of ID, especially if you think you look too young. If you plan to book a floating breakfast at 7 AM, be prepared for the pool water to be very cold! Even under the midday sun, the water remains quite chilly. Also, there are no lockable lockers, only limited open shelves. So, it's best not to bring any valuables. And remember, shelves can get crowded during peak hours.

## Getting Around

Reaching Alas Harum and Cretya Ubud is easy—just a 15-minute drive from central Ubud. You can use Grab or Gojek to book a car or scooter for a convenient trip. We recommend not hiring a full-day driver to explore Alas Harum and Cretya. With so many activities and inviting pools, you might get so immersed that you waste valuable time on a hired car. To save both time and money, avoid having a driver wait outside, so you can fully enjoy the complete experience here.
