Engaging in water activities like diving and snorkeling is a top choice for many visitors to Bali. However, since these activities often take place at sea or on offshore islands, bringing bags on board is usually not possible, let alone having access to a safe. Thus, the question 'How should I safeguard my passport, wallet, and phone?' becomes a common concern.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_13ba81f657671652cb965ba7cbdbbb32.jpg)

No worries! Below, we've compiled **6 safe and practical methods** to help you enjoy marine activities with confidence!

## **1. Keep Your Passport in the Hotel Safe**

Your passport is your most important identification document abroad, **never take it to the beach** or any water activities. Most hotels and guesthouses provide safes; it's advisable to lock your passport, flight ticket copies, and extra cash inside.

If you need to register or participate in activities outside, carry a photocopy of your passport or keep a digital copy to avoid losing the original.

---

## **2. Use a Waterproof Bag or Waterproof Phone Pouch (Limited Use)**

If you must carry your phone or a small amount of cash, consider using a **high-quality waterproof bag (Dry Bag) or waterproof phone pouch**. These items are also readily available locally in Bali.

However, be aware: while these products are marketed as waterproof, **they are not suitable for prolonged immersion in water** or deep diving. Avoid keeping important items in them for extended periods.

---

## ****3. Utilize Lockers or Storage Boxes During Water Activities****

Most dive shops or snorkeling companies provide temporary storage spaces for participants to store items like shoes, towels, and phones. Before using them, ensure their security and avoid placing all valuables in one spot.

---

## ****4. Have a Trusted Driver? Entrust Them with Your Belongings****

If you're traveling with a private car and your driver is a ****familiar and trustworthy local****, consider leaving some valuables (like your phone or cash) in their care.

This is quite common among local travelers, especially during long trips (such as day trips to Nusa Lembongan). However, it's still recommended to first check for drivers with good reputations in community groups.

---

## ****5. Carry Passport Copies and Cloud Backups****

To prepare for emergencies, scan your passport, visa, and credit cards in advance and store them in the cloud (e.g., Google Drive, iCloud). This way, if anything gets lost, you can quickly handle reporting or replacement procedures.

---

## ****6. Travel Light, Reduce Risks****

When participating in water activities, bring only essential items like your phone, sunscreen, and a small amount of cash. Avoid wearing expensive watches, jewelry, or designer bags.

The first step to safe travel is to ****travel light and minimize temptations****!

## ****FAQ Frequently Asked Questions****

### 1. Is there a place to store belongings during diving trips?

Yes, most dive shops provide basic storage facilities like plastic bins or lockers. However, you're responsible for your own belongings, and it's advised not to bring valuables.

### 2. Can I bring my passport along?

It's strongly discouraged. If it accidentally falls into the sea or gets soaked, a damaged passport can cause significant inconvenience. Please keep it securely locked in your accommodation's safe.

### 3. Are local drivers really reliable?

Most travelers find that experienced local private drivers are usually friendly and reliable. However, it's still recommended to choose platforms with reviews and good reputation, and avoid handing over all valuables upon first meeting.

### 4. What waterproof gear should I prepare?

It's recommended to bring a dry waterproof bag, waterproof phone case, sunscreen, and amphibious shoes for convenience and practicality.

### 5. What should I do if I lose something?

It's advised to report to the local police immediately. If it's your passport, contact the Taiwan Representative Office in Indonesia (or your country's consulate) for assistance in replacement.
