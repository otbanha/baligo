Engaging in water activities like diving or snorkeling is a top choice for many travelers in Bali. However, since these activities often take place at the beach or on offshore islands, bringing bags on board is usually not possible, let alone having access to a safe. Thus, the question 'How should I store my passport, wallet, and phone?' becomes a common concern.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_13ba81f657671652cb965ba7cbdbbb32.jpg)

Don't worry! Below, we've compiled **6 safe and practical storage methods** to help you enjoy your marine activities with confidence!

## **1. Keep Your Passport in the Hotel Safe**

Your passport is your most important identification document abroad, so **never take it to the beach** or during any water activities. Most hotels and guesthouses provide safes—it's advisable to lock your passport, a copy of your flight ticket, and any extra cash inside.

If you need to register or participate in activities while out, carry a photocopy of your passport or keep a digital copy on your phone to avoid losing the original.

---

## **2. Use a Waterproof Bag or Waterproof Phone Case (Limited Use)**

If you must carry your phone or a small amount of cash, consider using a **high-quality waterproof bag (Dry Bag) or waterproof phone case**. These items are also easily available locally in Bali.

However, note that while these products are marketed as waterproof, they **are not suitable for prolonged submersion** or deep diving. Avoid keeping important items in them for extended periods.

---

## **3. Utilize Lockers or Storage Boxes During Diving Trips**

Most diving shops or snorkeling companies provide temporary storage spaces for participants to store items like shoes, towels, and phones. Before using these facilities, confirm their security and avoid placing all valuables in one location.

---

## **4. Have a Trusted Private Driver? Entrust Them with Your Belongings**

If you're traveling with a private driver who is **a familiar and trustworthy local**, consider leaving some valuables (such as your phone or cash) in their care.

This is a common practice among travelers, especially during long trips (e.g., a day trip to Nusa Lembongan). However, it's still advisable to first refer to drivers with good reputations in community forums.

---

## **5. Carry Passport Copies and Cloud Backups**

To prepare for emergencies, scan and upload your passport, visa, and credit card details to cloud storage (e.g., Google Drive, iCloud). This ensures you can quickly handle reporting or replacement procedures if anything is lost.

---

## **6. Travel Light, Reduce Risks**

When participating in water activities, carry only essentials like your phone, sunscreen, and a small amount of cash. Avoid wearing expensive watches, jewelry, or designer bags.

The first step to safe travel is **traveling light and minimizing temptations**!

## **FAQ**

### **1. Is there storage available during diving trips?**

Yes, most dive shops provide basic storage options like plastic bins or lockers. However, you are responsible for your belongings, so it's best not to bring valuables.

### **2. Can I bring my passport with me?**

Strongly not recommended. If it accidentally falls into the sea or gets soaked, passport damage can cause significant trouble. Please store it securely in your accommodation's safe.

### **3. Are local drivers really reliable?**

Most travelers find that experienced local private drivers are usually friendly and reliable. However, it's still advisable to choose platforms with reviews and good reputations, and avoid handing over all valuables upon first meeting.

### **4. What waterproof gear should I prepare?**

It's recommended to bring a dry waterproof bag, waterproof phone case, sunscreen, and amphibious shoes for convenience and practicality.

### **5. What should I do if I lose an item?**

It's advised to report the loss to the local police immediately. If you lose your passport, contact **the Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Indonesia (or your country's consulate)** for assistance with replacement.

**Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Jakarta**

- **Location**: Located on the 17th floor of Gedung Artha Graha building in Senayan, Central Jakarta (Jl. Jend. Sudirman Kav. 52-53)
- **Responsibilities**: Covers the entire territory of Indonesia (excluding East Timor), including West Java, Central Java, Jakarta Capital Region, Sumatra, Kalimantan, Papua, and most other regions

**Taipei Economic and Trade Office in Surabaya**

- **Location**: Jl. Indragiri No. 49, Surabaya, East Java
- **Responsibilities**: Specifically responsible for Eastern Indonesia, including East Java, Bali, Lombok, Sulawesi, Maluku Islands, and other islands in Eastern Indonesia
