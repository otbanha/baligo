Don't Trust Google Maps in Bali! Bali is a dream vacation destination, but for travelers who rely on Google Maps for directions, it can present some real challenges. There are two main reasons to take Google Maps with a grain of salt when planning your trip here.

![在峇里島別相信Google地圖！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e7754da22cf1ad2b6561b833de1a9148.png)

First, Google Maps may show incorrect routes. While it's a powerful navigation tool, in a place like Bali it can give inaccurate directions or even lead you down alleys too narrow for a scooter. This is often due to incomplete geographic data or changes in road construction. So when using Google Maps in Bali, it's best to treat it as a reference rather than an absolute guide.

Second, even when the route is correct, the estimated arrival time is often too optimistic. Traffic in Bali can be much heavier and more chaotic than expected, especially during peak season or rush hour. So when planning your itinerary with Google Maps, it's wise to add 50% to the estimated travel time to account for potential delays.

For example, say you're planning a trip from Kuta in southern Bali to a sight in the north. Google Maps shows the drive will take two hours. But given Bali's traffic, especially in peak season or at busy times, the roads can be far more congested and chaotic. To prepare for possible delays, it's best to add 50% to that estimate—making it three hours. That way, you'll have enough time to handle any holdups and still arrive on schedule.
