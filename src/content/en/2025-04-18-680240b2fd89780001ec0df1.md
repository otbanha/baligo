## Bali Ubud Hot Air Balloon Tour: A Family's Dreamy Aerial Date

"Mom, are we really going to fly up there?" My 5-year-old Xiao Bao clung tightly to my shirt, his eyes sparkling with excitement. My husband adjusted his camera with a smile, "Don't worry, Daddy will protect you." This was our family's most special adventure in Bali - our first hot air balloon ride in Ubud.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8dfa66b04dd9de71bb06a72e584ab9f2.jpg)

## Bali's Only Family Aerial Adventure

As the island's only tethered hot air balloon, this 23-meter-high rainbow giant is perfect for family experiences. Seeing us with our child, the staff thoughtfully brought out a small step stool: "Kids can use this to climb up first!" The wicker basket was more spacious than expected, comfortably fitting our family of three (with a total weight limit of 150kg, perfect for families with children).

## The Magic of Twilight

We chose the highly recommended 17:30 twilight flight. When the flame roared to life, Xiao Bao gasped and buried himself in his father's arms, but was soon giggling with delight at the sensation of ascent. From 50 meters above, Ubud's rice fields looked like they were covered in gold foil under the setting sun, with Mount Agung faintly visible in the distance. My husband held me with one arm and protected Xiao Bao, who was peeking out, with the other. Bali looks truly different from this perspective!

## Family-Focused Services

- Child-friendly: Staff specially explain hot air balloon principles to children, and after the flight, they award "Little Pilot Certificates." Xiao Bao proudly held his certificate for over a dozen photos.
- Family portraits: An accompanying photographer captured our moments of embrace in the air.
- Delicious dinner: A Singaporean chef prepared a special children's meal that even picky Xiao Bao enjoyed, while my husband and I savored a romantic candlelit dinner with local flavors.

## **Friendly Reminders**

- Evening flights are the most popular, so it's recommended to **check in 30 minutes early**. Otherwise, you might end up waiting until it's dark like we did (fortunately, the staff prioritized families with children).
- The basket edge reaches the chest height of a small child, so **adults need to hold onto their child tightly throughout the flight** (my husband never let go of our child's hand).
- The sound of the flames during the flight can be quite loud, so **sensitive children might want to bring earplugs** (our child was startled at first but quickly forgot the fear as they became captivated by the scenery).

These brief 10 minutes not only allowed us to witness the grandeur of Bali but also became the brightest highlight of our family trip. If romance is something shared between two people, then happiness is floating together in the skies above Ubud as a family of three.
