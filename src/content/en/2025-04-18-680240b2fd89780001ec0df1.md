## **Bali Ubud Hot Air Balloon Adventure: A Family of Three's Dreamy Sky Date**

"Mom, are we really going to fly up high?" Little Bao, 5 years old, clutched my sleeve tightly, his eyes sparkling with excitement. My husband chuckled beside us, adjusting his camera: "Don't worry, Daddy will protect you." This was our family's most special adventure in Bali — our first hot air balloon experience in Ubud.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8dfa66b04dd9de71bb06a72e584ab9f2.jpg)

## **Bali's Only Family Sky Adventure**

As the island's **only** tethered hot air balloon, this 23-meter-high rainbow giant is perfect for family experiences. Seeing us with our child, the staff thoughtfully brought out a small step stool: "Little one can step up here first!" The wicker basket was more spacious than expected, comfortably fitting the three of us (with a total weight limit of 150 kg, it's no problem for families with kids).

## **The Magic Hour at Dusk**

We chose the highly recommended **17:30 sunset flight**. When the flame roared to life, Little Bao gasped and buried his face in Daddy's arms, but the next moment he was giggling at the sensation of rising. From 50 meters up, Ubud's rice terraces looked like they were covered in gold foil under the setting sun, with Mount Agung faintly visible in the twilight. My husband held me with one arm and shielded Little Bao, who was peeking out with curiosity. Seeing Bali from this perspective is truly different!

## **Family-Friendly Services**

- **Child-Friendly**: Staff explain the principles of hot air balloons specially for kids, and after the flight, they award a "Little Pilot Certificate," which Little Bao proudly held for over a dozen photos.
- **Family Photo Shoot**: An accompanying photographer captured our moments of embrace in the sky.
- **Delicious Dinner**: A Singaporean chef prepared a special kids' meal that even picky Little Bao enjoyed, while my husband and I savored a romantic candlelit dinner infused with local flavors.

## **Travel Tips**

- Evening flights are the most popular, so **arrive 30 minutes early** to check in. Otherwise, you might end up waiting until dark like we almost did (luckily, the staff prioritized families with children).
- The basket edge reaches chest height for toddlers, so **adults need to hold onto their child tightly throughout the flight** (my husband never let go of our little one).
- The flame noise during the flight can be loud, so **sensitive children might benefit from earplugs** (our toddler was startled at first but quickly forgot about it once captivated by the views).

Those brief 10 minutes not only allowed us to witness Bali's breathtaking beauty but also became the most radiant memory of our family trip. If romance is for two, then happiness is floating together in Ubud's sky as a family of three.
