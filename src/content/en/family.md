## 🌞 What’s the Weather Like in Bali During Summer?

July and August are Bali’s dry season, with almost no rain. Even if you encounter occasional showers, they’re usually brief and won’t disrupt your plans. These months are among the most comfortable for travel, with outdoor activities almost always feasible.

https://youtu.be/MdQYUy31LiY?si=_oj6pEam_FE87pau

Average temperatures range between 27–30°C, but humidity and sunlight can make it feel hotter. If you’re traveling with kids or seniors, it’s wise to schedule a midday "heat break"—returning to the hotel or staying in air-conditioned spaces will make everyone more comfortable.

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Where is the Best Area in Bali for Families with Kids?

Among summer travelers, families with children make up a significant portion. Many resorts in Bali feature Kids Clubs and water slides as their main attractions, so parents don't need to worry about extra planning.

We've compiled two resources: "[Which Hotels Have Kids Clubs](/blog/bali-family-resorts-kids-club/)" and "[Bali Resorts with Water Slides](/blog/bali-family-resorts-water-slides/)" to make it easier for families with kids to find the right options.

As for choosing the best area, we highly recommend these three locations:

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but is actually perfect for family travel. The waves are calm, the beaches are clean, and it lacks the party crowds of Seminyak. It's just a short walk to the beach, and nights are quiet. If you're traveling with young children or elderly family members, Sanur is a reassuring choice.

### 📍 Seminyak

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867278224-cassie-gallegos-6wcwcpwmrjy.jpeg)

Seminyak's advantage lies in having "everything"—shopping, restaurants, beach clubs, and high-quality resorts are all available. If parents also want to enjoy their own vacation time without the entire trip revolving around the kids, Seminyak can balance both adult and children's needs.

## 🏡 Accommodation Options

### Private Villas are Ideal for Family Trips

True family bonding happens when multiple generations stay together!

If your summer trip involves multiple families traveling together, booking a **private villa** is a smarter and more considerate choice than reserving several hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why? Because private villas offer far more benefits than you might expect:

**✔ Stay together as a family, no need to split up**
Even if you book the same hotel, with grandparents on the third floor and kids on the seventh, just gathering everyone takes forever. A private villa means the living room, kitchen, and pool are all in one place—perfect for chatting, watching the kids play in the water, and enjoying a movie together in the evening. It truly feels like a family reunion.

**✔ High privacy, no interference from other guests**
Elderly family members dislike noisy environments, and kids might disturb others—a private villa solves all these issues. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively yours, so kids can splash around without worrying about bothering neighboring guests.

**✔ Flexible dining, easier to accommodate dietary needs**
Many elders may not enjoy Indonesian cuisine for multiple days in a row. A private villa comes with a kitchen, allowing you to cook congee, blanch vegetables, or prepare simple meals with local ingredients. Breakfast isn’t tied to hotel hours either—eat whenever suits you, making it very senior-friendly.

**✔ Great value when costs are split**
Bali offers a wide range of private villas, from three to six bedrooms or more. When the cost is divided among families, it often works out cheaper than staying at a five-star hotel, while offering more space and greater freedom.
