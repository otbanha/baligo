## 🌞 What’s the Weather Like in Bali During Summer?

July and August mark Bali’s dry season, with almost no rain. Even if you encounter occasional showers, they’re usually brief and won’t disrupt your plans. These months are among the most comfortable for travel, with outdoor activities running smoothly almost every day.

https://youtu.be/MdQYUy31LiY?si=_oj6pEam_FE87pau

Average temperatures range between 27–30°C, but humidity and sunlight can make it feel hotter. If traveling with kids or seniors, it’s wise to schedule a midday "heat break"—return to your hotel or stay in air-conditioned spaces for maximum comfort.

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Which Area in Bali is Best for Families with Kids?

Among summer travelers, families with children make up a significant portion. Many resorts in Bali feature Kids Clubs and water slides as their main attractions, saving parents the hassle of additional planning.

We’ve specifically compiled two resources: "[Which Hotels Have Kids Clubs](/blog/bali-family-resorts-kids-club/)" and "[Bali Resorts with Water Slides](/blog/bali-family-resorts-water-slides/)" to make it easier for families with kids to find the perfect spot.

As for area recommendations, here are the top three places:

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but is actually an excellent choice for family travel. The waves are calm, the beaches are clean, and there’s no party crowd like in Seminyak. It’s just a short walk to the beach, and the nights are quiet. If you have young children or elderly family members, Sanur is a reassuring choice.

### 📍 Seminyak

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867278224-cassie-gallegos-6wcwcpwmrjy.jpeg)

Seminyak’s strength lies in having "everything"—shopping, restaurants, beach clubs, and high-quality resorts are all available. If parents also want to enjoy their own vacation time without the entire itinerary revolving around the kids, Seminyak can cater to both adults and children.

## 🏡 Accommodation Choices

### Private Villas Are Ideal for Family Trips

Having multiple generations stay together is the true essence of family bonding!

If your summer trip involves multiple families traveling together, **booking a private villa** is a smarter and more thoughtful choice than reserving two or three hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why is that? Because the benefits of a private villa go far beyond what you might imagine:

**✔ Stay together as a family, no need to split up**
Even if you book rooms in the same hotel, with grandparents on the third floor and kids on the seventh, just gathering everyone can take forever. A private villa, however, keeps the living room, kitchen, and pool all in one place, allowing you to chat anytime, watch the kids play in the water, and even enjoy a movie together in the living room at night—truly capturing the essence of 'family reunion.'

**✔ High privacy, free from other guests' disturbances**
Elderly family members dislike noisy environments, and kids might worry about disturbing others—a private villa solves all these issues. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively yours, so kids can splash around without worrying about bothering neighboring guests.

**✔ Flexible dining, easier to accommodate elders' dietary needs**
Many elders may not enjoy Indonesian food for multiple days in a row. A private villa comes with a kitchen, allowing you to cook congee, blanch vegetables, or prepare simple meals with local ingredients. Breakfast isn’t tied to hotel schedules either—eat whenever you want, making it very elder-friendly.

**✔ Great value when split among everyone**
Bali offers a wide range of private villas, from three-bedroom to six-bedroom options and beyond. When the cost is divided among families, it often turns out cheaper than staying at a five-star hotel, while offering more space and greater freedom.
