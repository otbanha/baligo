## 🌞 What’s the Weather Like in Bali During Summer?

July and August are Bali’s dry season, with almost no rain. Even if you encounter occasional showers, they’re usually brief and won’t disrupt your plans. These months are among the most comfortable for travel, with outdoor activities possible almost every day.

Average temperatures range between 27–30°C, but the humidity and sunlight can make it feel hotter. If you’re traveling with kids or seniors, it’s a good idea to schedule a midday “heat break” to rest at the hotel or stay in air-conditioned spaces for comfort.

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Which Area in Bali is Best for Families with Kids?

During summer vacations, family travelers make up a significant portion of tourists. Many resorts in Bali feature Kids Clubs and water slides as their main attractions, saving parents the hassle of additional planning.

We’ve compiled two resources: "[Hotels with Kids Clubs](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-05-14-66405077fd89780001f23b72/)" and "[Bali Resorts with Water Slides](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2024-01-16-65a51edbfd89780001ffc7b2/)" to make it easier for families traveling with kids to find suitable options.

As for choosing the right area, here are our top three recommendations:

### 📍 Sanur

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867127299-_.jpeg)

Sanur is often overlooked but is actually perfect for family travel. With calm waves, clean beaches, and a lack of the party crowds found in Seminyak, it’s just a short walk to the beach and remains quiet at night. If you’re traveling with young children or elderly family members, Sanur is a safe and comfortable choice.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774867278224-cassie-gallegos-6wcwcpwmrjy.jpeg)

### 📍 Nusa Dua

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775130814476-2893e5d6-29ad-4199-8041-45c2ab39bf47.jpg)

Nusa Dua is the most hassle-free choice for a family vacation in Bali. ## 🏡 Accommodation Choices

### Villas Are the Best Choice for Family Trips

Staying together with three generations is the true essence of family reunion!

If your summer trip involves three generations or multiple families traveling together, **renting a villa** is a smarter and more considerate choice than booking two or three hotel rooms.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774868006588-e1895231-3a0b-477a-a1ae-cb01fbf71399.png)

Why is that? Because the benefits of renting a villa are far greater than you might think:

**✔ Stay Together Without Running Between Rooms**
Even if you book rooms in the same hotel, grandparents might be on the third floor while the kids are on the seventh, making it a hassle just to gather everyone. With a private villa, the living room, kitchen, and pool are all in one place, allowing you to chat anytime, watch the kids play in the water, and even enjoy a movie together in the evening—truly capturing the essence of "family togetherness."

**✔ High Privacy, Free from Other Guests' Disturbances**
Elderly family members often prefer quieter environments, while kids might worry about disturbing others—a private villa solves this perfectly. The pool, living room, and dining area are exclusively yours, so kids can splash around without worrying about bothering neighboring guests.

**✔ Flexible Dining, Easier to Cater to Elderly Dietary Needs**
Many elders might not enjoy Indonesian cuisine for multiple days in a row. A private villa comes with a kitchen, allowing you to cook congee, blanch vegetables, or prepare simple meals with local ingredients. Breakfast isn’t tied to hotel schedules either—eat whenever suits you, making it very senior-friendly.

**✔ Great Value When Split Among Families**
Bali offers a wide range of private villas, from three-bedroom to six-bedroom options. When the cost is split among families, it often turns out to be more affordable than staying at a five-star hotel, while providing more space and greater freedom.
