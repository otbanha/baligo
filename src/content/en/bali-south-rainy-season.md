When thinking of Bali, many imagine sunshine, beaches, and surfing. However, as a tropical destination, Bali also has its unique rainy season. Today, let's explore the rainfall patterns during the wet season in southern Bali.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8c004965cfd469d049ae8b0e5df25cfa.png)

According to rainfall probability charts, we can clearly see that the rainy season in southern Bali (including areas like **Kuta, Seminyak, Uluwatu, Nusa Dua, and Sanur**) mainly occurs from December to February. Around January 20th, the chance of rainfall peaks at 61%, the highest of the year. In January, there are an average of about 18 rainy days per month.

However, don't let these numbers scare you! The rainy season in Bali doesn't mean non-stop rain all day. Typically, rainfall lasts only 1-2 hours before stopping. After the rain clears, you'll still have plenty of time to enjoy the sunshine and beaches.

It's worth noting that while there may occasionally be heavy rainfall, even causing temporary flooding, such situations are not common. Most of the time, rainy season showers are more like a refreshing touch, bringing some comfort to the hot tropical climate.

From the charts, we can also observe an interesting trend: rainfall probability shows clear seasonal changes. It gradually increases from Bali's spring, peaks in January, and then slowly declines. This pattern provides a great reference for travelers planning their trips.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_482eb8e3cecbce3092b2dd1454b68823.png)

Additionally, it's worth mentioning that rainfall probability varies between northern and southern Bali. Generally, the further north you go, the higher the chance of rain. So if you're planning to visit during the rainy season, choosing southern Bali might be a good option.

In summary, even during the rainy season, southern Bali remains a charming travel destination. As long as you're well-prepared with rain gear, or choose to relax in a luxury resort or villa, you can fully enjoy the unique seasonal charm of this place. Perhaps, a brief tropical rain shower might even add some unforgettable memories to your journey!
