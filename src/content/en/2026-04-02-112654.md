## Must-Read Before You Go! Five Key Tips for Family Travel in Bali

### 🌤️ Climate & Clothing

Nusa Dua is located in southern Bali, with temperatures ranging between 26–33°C year-round. The dry season (May to October) is the most comfortable time to visit, while the rainy season (November to April) occasionally brings short afternoon showers, though they usually don’t disrupt plans.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127893271-e7cd6a46660c3237454c5f8185b741b5.jpg)

Clothing tips for kids: lightweight, breathable cotton clothes, sandals or waterproof slippers, and swimwear are essential. Sunscreen is a daily must—UV rays on the beach are stronger than you might think, and children’s delicate skin requires extra care. Reapply every two to three hours.

### 💊 Health and Safety

Before departure, pack essential medications: fever reducers, stomach medicine, anti-itch cream, and band-aids. Mosquitoes in Bali are very real, so choose a child-friendly insect repellent and remember to apply it during outdoor activities in the evening.

For drinking water, always purchase bottled water. Even for brushing your teeth, it's recommended to use mineral water to avoid stomach discomfort. Large resorts in Nusa Dua generally have excellent hygiene standards, but be cautious about the source of ice when dining out.

If engaging in water activities, make sure to prepare appropriately sized life jackets for children and confirm whether the operator provides them.

### 🚗 Transportation and Getting Around

Compared to Kuta or Seminyak, the Nusa Dua area has more orderly traffic, but getting around Bali still largely relies on private car hires. It's advisable to book a car in advance through your hotel or a trusted platform, with daily rates around 600,000–800,000 IDR (approximately USD$40–50). Drivers often double as guides, making it very convenient.

{{block:包車}}

When traveling with children, confirm whether a child safety seat is needed and arrange this in advance with the driver or hotel.

### 🍽️ Dining Options

Nusa Dua offers many high-end restaurants and hotel buffets with fresh ingredients and diverse options, so there's no need to worry about finding food for your kids. When dining out, choose busy establishments with high turnover rates to ensure food freshness.

Balinese cuisine tends to be spicy, so remember to request "No spicy, please" when ordering. Most restaurants are child-friendly, with some even offering kids' menus.

{{block:美食}}

### 🙏 Cultural Etiquette

Bali is a highly religious place, where offerings and temples can be seen everywhere. When visiting temples with children, please help them wear a sarong (most hotels provide them), and speak softly before entering sacred spaces to respect local religious customs.

Occasionally, you may see small offerings (Canang Sari) on the ground. Avoid stepping on them and remind children not to touch them casually. These are part of the daily Hindu rituals and are an important aspect of Balinese culture.

## Top 9 Family-Friendly Activities in Nusa Dua

### 1. 🐬 Splash Around at Nusa Dua Beach

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127983449-f5518c75b96f9370968adbb0ae27c709.jpg)

The waves here are very calm, making it the perfect spot for young children to play in the water. The golden sand is clean and well-maintained, and the clear, shallow waters allow kids to safely enjoy themselves. Along the beach, there are various water activity stalls where you can rent water toys. Just playing in the sand can keep kids entertained all afternoon.

### 2. 🐢 Turtle Island × Glass-Bottom Boat × Mangrove Cruise Combo Experience

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128003311-_2026-04-02_16.11.32.png)

This itinerary is, in my opinion, the best value-for-money family activity in Nusa Dua, packing three experiences into one trip that will leave your kids thoroughly satisfied.

Departing from Tanjung Benoa, you’ll first board a glass-bottom boat that glides over the crystal-clear waters. Through the glass bottom, you can see coral reefs and tropical fish swimming below—all without getting wet. Next, you’ll enter the mangrove waterways, slowly navigating through the dense mangroves, feeling as if you’ve entered another world. Finally, you’ll arrive at Turtle Island, where the kids will go wild—they can feed turtles up close, observe turtles of various sizes, and even hold baby turtles for photos. The island also features iguanas, various bird species, and other animals, making it a mini ecological education park.

The tour includes hotel transfers and is suitable for children who are not familiar with water activities, requiring no prior experience. You can conveniently and securely book through Klook.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128232527-qp1.png)

It features water slides for all ages, from dedicated areas for toddlers to adrenaline-pumping slides for adults. You can easily spend an entire day here. It’s best to visit on weekdays to avoid crowds, and don’t forget sunscreen and a change of clothes.

## Final Thoughts

For visitors, Nusa Dua is the kind of place where you can truly relax. It’s not as bustling as Kuta nor as remote as Ubud—it strikes the perfect balance of being vibrant yet not overwhelming.

Traveling with kids often comes with two worries: the kids not having enough fun and the parents not getting enough rest. Nusa Dua solves both. Kids can run on the beach, play in the pools, and marvel at cultural experiences, while parents can lounge on deck chairs, sip a Bintang beer, and finally dive into that book they’ve been putting off for months.

This is what travelers envision as the perfect family vacation in Bali.
