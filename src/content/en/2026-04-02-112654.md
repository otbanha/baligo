## Must-Read Before You Go! Top 5 Things to Know for Family Travel in Bali

### 🌤️ Climate & Attire

Located in southern Bali, Nusa Dua enjoys year-round temperatures ranging from 26–33°C. The dry season (May to October) offers the most comfortable travel conditions, while the rainy season (November to April) may bring brief afternoon showers that typically don't disrupt travel plans.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127893271-e7cd6a46660c3237454c5f8185b741b5.jpg)

Clothing tips for kids: Opt for lightweight, breathable cotton outfits, sandals or waterproof slippers, and of course, swimwear is a must. Sunscreen is an essential daily item - the UV rays at the beach are much stronger than you might think, so extra care is needed for children's delicate skin. We recommend reapplying every two to three hours.

### 💊 Health & Safety

Before departure, it's advisable to pack essential medications: fever reducers, stomach medicine, anti-itch cream, and band-aids. Mosquitoes in Bali are very real, so choose a mosquito repellent suitable for children and remember to apply it when engaging in outdoor activities in the evening.

For drinking water, please purchase bottled water. It's recommended to use mineral water even for brushing your teeth to avoid stomach discomfort. Large resorts in Nusa Dua generally maintain good hygiene standards, but be cautious about the source of ice when dining out.

If engaging in water activities, be sure to prepare appropriately sized life jackets for children and confirm whether the operator provides them.

### 🚗 Transportation & Getting Around

Compared to Kuta or Seminyak, the Nusa Dua area has more orderly traffic, but getting around Bali still largely relies on private car hire. It's recommended to book a car in advance through your hotel or a trusted platform, with daily rates around 600,000–800,000 IDR (approximately USD$40–50). The drivers often double as guides, making it a very convenient option.

{{block:包車}}

When traveling with children by car, remember to confirm if a child safety seat is required. You can arrange this in advance by contacting the driver or hotel.

### 🍽️ Dining Options

Nusa Dua boasts numerous high-end restaurants and hotel buffets, offering fresh ingredients and diverse options, so you don't have to worry about your children going hungry. When dining out, it's advisable to choose busy establishments with high turnover rates, as this ensures better food freshness.

Balinese cuisine tends to be quite spicy, so remember to request 'No spicy, please' when ordering. Most restaurants are child-friendly, with some even offering special kids' menus.

{{block:美食}}

### 🙏 Cultural Etiquette

Bali is a highly religious place, where offerings and temples can be seen everywhere. When visiting temples with children, please dress them in sarongs (most hotels provide them) and speak softly before entering sacred spaces, respecting local religious customs.

While walking, you may occasionally come across small offerings (Canang Sari) on the ground. Be careful not to step on them, and remind children not to touch them casually. These are part of the daily Hindu rituals for worshiping deities and represent an important aspect of Balinese culture.

## Top 9 Family-Friendly Activities in Nusa Dua

### 1. 🐬 Nusa Dua Beach Water Fun

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775127983449-f5518c75b96f9370968adbb0ae27c709.jpg)

The waves here are exceptionally calm, making it the perfect spot for families with young children to enjoy the water. The golden sand beach is clean and well-maintained, with crystal-clear waters and shallow areas that allow kids to play safely. Along the shore, various water activity stalls offer rentals for different water toys, and simply playing in the sand can keep children entertained for an entire afternoon.

### 2. 🐢 Turtle Island × Glass-Bottom Boat × Mangrove Cruise Combo Experience

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128003311-_2026-04-02_16.11.32.png)

This itinerary is, in my opinion, the best value-for-money family activity in Nusa Dua. It packs three experiences into one, leaving the kids thoroughly satisfied.

Starting from Tanjung Benoa, first board a glass-bottom boat that glides across the crystal-clear sea. Through the glass bottom, you can see coral reefs and tropical fish swimming below, all without getting wet. Then enter the mangrove waterways, slowly navigating through the dense mangroves, feeling as if you've entered another world. Finally, arrive at Turtle Island, the most exciting part for kids—they can hand-feed turtles, observe turtles of various sizes up close, and even hold baby turtles for photos. The conservation island also features iguanas, various bird species, and other animals, making it a miniature ecological education park.

The itinerary includes hotel transfers throughout, making it suitable for children who are not familiar with water and require no prior water experience. You can conveniently book through Klook for a hassle-free and secure experience.

### 4. 🏄 Banana Boat & Jet Ski

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775128198938-water_sports.jpg)

For slightly older kids (recommended age 8+), banana boat rides are a must-try. Several people sit together as a speedboat pulls them at high speeds, occasionally flipping over and plunging everyone into the water - endless laughter and screams ensue, making it Bali's most classic vacation activity. Jet skis are perfect for teenagers, and it's perfectly safe for parents to ride along with their children.

With water slides suitable for all ages - from dedicated kids' zones to adrenaline-pumping high-speed slides that will leave adults weak in the knees - it's easy to spend an entire day here. ## Final Thoughts

For visitors, Nusa Dua is the kind of place where you can truly relax and let your guard down. Unlike the bustling and noisy Kuta, yet not as remote as Ubud, it strikes that perfect balance of being vibrant without being overwhelming.

Traveling with kids often comes with two major concerns: the children not having enough fun, and the parents not getting enough rest. Nusa Dua solves both problems at once. Kids can run on the beach, play in the pool, and marvel at cultural experiences; while parents can lounge on deck chairs, sip a Bintang beer, and finally dive into that book they've been meaning to read for the past six months.

This is the perfect Bali family vacation that travelers dream of.
