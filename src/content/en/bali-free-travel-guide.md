## Day 3 to Day 4: Sanur or Nusa Penida

From Seminyak or Canggu, it takes about an hour by car to reach the eastern coast of southern Bali. Depending on your preferences, you can choose [Option 1] to explore the beach vibes of Sanur or [Option 2] to visit Nusa Penida and admire its stunning natural landscapes and underwater world.

### Option 1: Sanur

- Sanur is a laid-back beach town on Bali.
- Sanur boasts a tranquil and beautiful beach with a several-kilometer-long beachfront promenade, making it an ideal spot for sunbathing and leisurely walks.
- The beachfront promenade is lined with a variety of seaside restaurants, shops, and markets that blend exotic flavors with Balinese charm. You can purchase local handicrafts and souvenirs here.
- The sunrise views at Sanur Beach are spectacular. Wake up early to enjoy the sunrise and experience Bali's serenity and beauty.
- Sanur is also a popular spot for water activities. You can try kayaking, paddleboarding, windsurfing, and more.
- The pier for island hopping is located here, making it convenient to catch an early morning boat to Nusa Penida or Lembongan.

## **Day 5 to Day 6: Ubud**

- Ubud is renowned for its unique artistic and cultural atmosphere.
- Stay in a Ubud villa or boutique hotel to immerse yourself in its distinctive ambiance.
- Explore nearby attractions such as the Sacred Monkey Forest, UNESCO World Heritage-listed rice terraces, and stunning waterfalls.

## **Days 7-8: Kuta or Jimbaran**

To navigate Bali's traffic challenges, we recommend spending the last one or two nights in Kuta or Jimbaran, both conveniently located near the airport. As long as you don't stay too far north or south, you can typically reach the airport in about 30 minutes on your final day. This ensures you won't arrive too early and be bored, nor will you risk missing your flight due to traffic congestion. Whether you choose Kuta or Jimbaran, both areas offer stunning attractions to explore, providing the perfect finale to your journey.

**Option 1: Kuta**

- Kuta is one of Bali's most bustling commercial districts, featuring a variety of shops, markets, and malls. Here, you can purchase exquisite Balinese crafts, clothing, jewelry, and more.
- Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous beaches, renowned for its golden sand and impressive waves. It's a paradise for surfers and an ideal spot for sunbathing and sunset watching.
- Kuta offers a wide range of dining options, from [local traditional dishes](/blog/2023-05-02-64db6b81fd897800013a98e3/) to international cuisine. You can savor delicious meals from around the world to satisfy your palate.
- Kuta's nightlife is vibrant, with numerous bars, nightclubs, and entertainment venues. After dark, you can immerse yourself in Bali's lively party culture.
- At Kuta Beach, you can participate in surfing and other water activities. It's globally famous as a beginner-friendly surfing beach. [Watch Xiao Jie's first surfing experience here](https://youtu.be/gECdNvG16ww).

## What Not to Do vs What to Do

### How NOT to Plan Your Bali Itinerary

1. **Overcrowded and Rushed Schedule:** Avoid packing every day with too many attractions and activities. Instead, give yourself some time to relax and enjoy Bali's beautiful scenery and laid-back atmosphere. A general recommendation is to plan "no more than" four attractions/activities + lunch/dinner restaurants per day.
2. **Constantly Moving Between Areas:** While Bali isn't large, traffic congestion is a serious issue. Moving between areas can consume a lot of time and energy, leading to fatigue and inconvenience. Therefore, when planning your itinerary, try to focus on exploring and activities within one area. This will save time and energy and allow you to delve deeper into the local culture and attractions.
