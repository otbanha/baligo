## Days 3–4: Sanur or Nusa Penida

From Seminyak or Canggu, it takes about an hour by car to reach the east coast of southern Bali. Depending on your preferences, you can choose Option 1 to explore the beach vibes of Sanur, or Option 2 to head to Nusa Penida and admire its stunning natural landscapes and underwater world.

### Option 1: Sanur

- Sanur is a laid-back beach town on Bali.
- It boasts calm, beautiful beaches with a beachfront path stretching for several kilometers—perfect for sunbathing and leisurely strolls.
- The beachfront path is lined with a mix of exotic and Balinese-style seaside restaurants, shops, and markets where you can buy local handicrafts and souvenirs.
- Sanur offers spectacular sunrises; wake up early to catch the sunrise and soak in the tranquility and beauty of Bali.
- It's also a popular spot for water activities like kayaking, stand-up paddleboarding, and windsurfing.
- The ferry port to nearby islands is right here, making it easy to catch an early boat to Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan.

## **Day 5 to Day 6: Ubud**

About an hour’s drive from Sanur, you’ll reach Ubud, Bali’s most famous mountain town.

- Before heading to Ubud, take a slight detour to explore [Bali’s largest art wholesale market](/blog/kumbasari-art-market-shopping/).
- Ubud was once the capital of a Balinese kingdom and remains the island’s cultural and artistic heart.
- It’s the most culturally and artistically vibrant place in Bali.
- Stay in a resort villa or boutique hotel in Ubud to soak up its unique atmosphere and charm.
- Visit nearby attractions like the Sacred Monkey Forest, UNESCO-recognized rice terraces, and stunning waterfalls.
- Join local cultural experiences, such as Ubud craft workshops or traditional Balinese cooking classes.

## **Days 7 to 8: Kuta or Jimbaran**

To tackle Bali's traffic challenges, we recommend spending the last one or two nights of your trip in Kuta or Jimbaran. Both areas are close to the airport, and as long as you don't stay too far north or south, you can usually reach the airport within about 30 minutes on your final day. This arrangement ensures you won't arrive at the airport too early and get bored, nor worry about missing your flight due to traffic jams. Whether you choose Kuta or Jimbaran, there are plenty of amazing spots to explore, perfect for a memorable end to your journey.

**Option 1: Kuta**

- Kuta is one of Bali's busiest commercial districts, packed with shops, markets, and malls. Here you can find exquisite Balinese crafts, clothing, jewelry, and more.
- Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous beaches, known for its golden sand and spectacular waves. It's a surfer's paradise and a great spot for sunbathing and watching the sunset.
- Kuta offers a wide range of dining options, from [local traditional dishes](/blog/bali-top-10-foods/) to international cuisine, satisfying every palate.
- Kuta's nightlife is vibrant, with numerous bars, clubs, and entertainment venues. After dark, you can dive into Bali's lively party scene.
- At Kuta Beach, you can try surfing and water sports—it's world-famous as a beginner-friendly surf spot. [Video of Jay's first surfing experience](https://youtu.be/gECdNvG16ww).

## Absolutely Avoid vs. Must Do

### How NOT to Plan Your Bali Itinerary

1. **Overpacked and rushed schedule**: Avoid cramming every day with attractions and activities. Instead, give yourself time to relax and soak in Bali's beauty and laid-back vibe. A good rule of thumb is to plan no more than four attractions/activities plus lunch/dinner per day.
2. **Bouncing between regions**: Though Bali isn't huge, traffic jams are a major issue. Moving across regions can eat up a lot of time and energy, leaving you exhausted and frustrated. When planning, try to focus on one area for exploration and activities. This saves time and energy, and lets you dive deeper into the local culture and sights.
