## Day 3 to Day 4: Sanur or Nusa Penida

From Seminyak or Canggu, it takes about an hour by car to reach the eastern coast of southern Bali. Depending on your preferences, you can choose 【Option 1】 to explore the beach vibes of Sanur or 【Option 2】 to visit Nusa Penida and marvel at its stunning natural landscapes and underwater world.

### Option 1: Sanur

- Sanur is a laid-back beach town on Bali, known for its relaxed vibe.

![適合家庭度假的沙努爾攻略](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f70927bb4b24c69685305c623a8b4f7e.jpg)

- Sanur boasts a serene and beautiful beach, with a several-kilometer-long beachfront promenade, perfect for sunbathing and leisurely walks.

- The beachfront promenade in Sanur is lined with a variety of restaurants, shops, and markets that blend exotic flavors with Balinese charm, where you can purchase local handicrafts and souvenirs.

- The sunrise views at Sanur Beach are spectacular, making it worth waking up early to witness Bali's tranquility and beauty.

- Sanur is also a hotspot for water sports, offering activities like kayaking, paddleboarding, and windsurfing.

- The harbor for island hopping is located here, making it convenient to catch an early morning boat to Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan.

## **Days 5 to 6: Ubud**

About an hour's drive from Sanur, you'll arrive in Ubud, Bali's most famous mountain town.

- Before heading to Ubud, take a detour to visit [Bali's Largest Art Wholesale Market](/blog/2022-03-03-64db6b78fd897800013a9661/).
- Ubud was once the capital of Bali's ancient kingdom and remains the island's cultural and artistic heart.
- Ubud is the most uniquely artistic and culturally rich area in Bali.
- Stay in a resort villa or boutique hotel in Ubud to experience its distinctive atmosphere and charm.
- Explore nearby attractions such as the Sacred Monkey Forest, UNESCO World Heritage-listed rice terraces, and stunning waterfalls.
- Participate in local cultural experiences like Ubud's handicraft workshops or traditional Balinese cooking classes.

## **Days 7 to 8: Kuta or Jimbaran**

To tackle Bali's traffic challenges, we've arranged for the last one or two nights of the trip to be spent in Kuta or Jimbaran, both areas conveniently close to the airport. As long as you don't stay too far north or south, you can generally reach the airport in about 30 minutes on the final day. This ensures you won't arrive too early and get bored, nor worry about missing your flight due to traffic congestion. Whether you choose Kuta or Jimbaran, there are plenty of stunning attractions waiting to be explored, providing the perfect conclusion to your journey.

**Option 1: Kuta**

- Kuta is one of Bali's most bustling commercial districts, offering a variety of shops, markets, and shopping malls. Here, you can purchase exquisite Balinese crafts, clothing, jewelry, and more.
- Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous beaches, renowned for its golden sand and spectacular waves. It's a paradise for surfers and an excellent spot for sunbathing and watching sunsets.
- Kuta offers a wide range of dining options, from [local traditional dishes](/blog/2023-05-02-64db6b81fd897800013a98e3/) to international cuisine. You can savor delicious meals from around the world.
- Kuta's nightlife is vibrant, with numerous bars, nightclubs, and entertainment venues. After dark, you can immerse yourself in Bali's lively party culture.
- At Kuta Beach, you can participate in surfing and other water activities, as it's globally renowned as a beginner-friendly surfing beach. [Watch Xiao Jie's first surfing experience here](https://youtu.be/gECdNvG16ww).

## Must vs Must Not

### Don't Arrange Your Bali Itinerary Like This

1. **Avoid Overcrowded and Rushed Itineraries:** Try not to pack every day with too many attractions and activities. Instead, leave some time to relax and enjoy Bali's beautiful scenery and laid-back atmosphere. A general recommendation is to plan no more than four attractions/activities + lunch/dinner per day.
2. **Minimize Cross-Region Travel:** While Bali isn't large, traffic congestion is a serious issue. Moving between regions can consume a lot of time and energy, leading to fatigue and inconvenience. When planning your itinerary, try to focus on exploring and activities within one area. This saves time and energy while allowing you to delve deeper into the local culture and attractions.
