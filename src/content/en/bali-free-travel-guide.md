## Day 3 to Day 4: Sanur or Nusa Penida

From Seminyak or Canggu, it takes about an hour by car to reach the eastern coast of southern Bali. Depending on your preferences, you can choose [Option 1] to explore the beach vibes of Sanur or [Option 2] to visit Nusa Penida and admire its stunning natural landscapes and underwater world.

### Option 1: Sanur

- Sanur is a laid-back beach town on Bali.
- Sanur boasts a serene and beautiful beach with a several-kilometer-long beachfront promenade, perfect for sunbathing and leisurely walks.
- The beachfront promenade is lined with a variety of restaurants, shops, and markets that blend exotic flavors with Balinese charm. You can purchase local handicrafts and souvenirs here.
- The sunrise views at Sanur Beach are spectacular. Wake up early to enjoy the sunrise and experience Bali's tranquility and beauty.
- Sanur is also a popular spot for water activities. You can try kayaking, paddleboarding, windsurfing, and more.
- The harbor for island hopping is located here, making it convenient to catch an early boat to Nusa Penida or Lembongan Island.

- The harbor to Nusa Penida is located in Sanur.

## **Day 5 to Day 6: Ubud**

About an hour's drive from Sanur, you'll arrive in Ubud, Bali's most famous mountain town.

- Before heading to Ubud, take a slight detour to visit [Bali's Largest Art Wholesale Market](/blog/kumbasari-art-market-shopping/).
- Ubud was once the capital of Bali's kingdom and remains the island's cultural and artistic heart.
- Ubud is the most culturally and artistically unique place in Bali.
- Stay in a resort villa or boutique hotel in Ubud to experience its distinctive atmosphere and charm.
- Visit nearby attractions such as the Sacred Monkey Forest, UNESCO World Heritage-listed rice terraces, and stunning waterfalls.
- Participate in local cultural experiences like Ubud's handicraft workshops or traditional Balinese cooking classes.

## **Days 7 to 8: Kuta or Jimbaran**

To navigate Bali's challenging traffic conditions, we recommend spending the last one or two nights of your trip in Kuta or Jimbaran. Both areas are conveniently located near the airport, ensuring you can reach it in about 30 minutes on your final day, provided you don't stay too far north or south. This arrangement ensures you won't arrive at the airport too early and be bored, nor will you have to worry about missing your flight due to traffic congestion. Whether you choose Kuta or Jimbaran, there are plenty of stunning attractions waiting to be explored, perfect for concluding your journey.

**Option One: Kuta**

- Kuta is one of Bali's most bustling commercial districts, offering a wide variety of shops, markets, and shopping centers. Here, you can purchase exquisite Balinese crafts, clothing, jewelry, and more.
- Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous beaches, renowned for its golden sand and spectacular waves. It's a paradise for surfers and an excellent spot for tourists to enjoy sunbathing and sunset views.
- Kuta offers a diverse range of dining options, from [local traditional dishes](/blog/bali-top-10-foods/) to international cuisine. You can savor delicious meals from around the world, satisfying your taste buds.
- Kuta's nightlife is vibrant, with numerous bars, nightclubs, and entertainment venues. After dark, you can immerse yourself in Bali's lively party culture.
- At Kuta Beach, you can participate in surfing and other water activities, as it's globally renowned for being beginner-friendly. [Watch Xiao Jie's first surfing experience here](https://youtu.be/gECdNvG16ww).

## What Not to Do vs What to Do

### How Not to Plan Your Bali Itinerary

1. **Avoid Overcrowded and Rushed Schedules:** Resist the temptation to pack every day with attractions and activities. Instead, allow yourself time to relax and soak in Bali's beauty and laid-back atmosphere. A good rule of thumb is to limit yourself to no more than four attractions/activities per day, plus lunch/dinner spots.
2. **Minimize Cross-Region Travel:** While Bali isn't large, traffic congestion is a significant issue. Moving between regions can consume a lot of time and energy, leading to fatigue and inconvenience. When planning your itinerary, try to focus on exploring and engaging in activities within one area. This approach saves time and energy and allows for a deeper understanding of the local culture and attractions.
