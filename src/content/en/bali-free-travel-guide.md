**Bottom line first**: There's only one formula to avoid traffic in Bali—**'Stay where you play'**: Break your itinerary into zones (e.g., first two nights in Seminyak or Canggu, then Ubud, and finally the southern beach area). Within each zone, use Gojek/Grab for rides. Only hire a private driver on days you're moving between zones or heading to the mountains. This minimizes time stuck in traffic. Our 8-day, 7-night guide follows this logic, covering transportation, accommodation, attractions, food, and SPA—feel free to adjust based on your trip length.

Bali is a popular Indonesian destination known for its stunning beaches, rich culture, and history. However, traffic is a major headache for travelers, potentially disrupting plans or even causing you to miss flights.

To make your Bali trip smoother, we've prepared this travel guide. You can tailor it to your own schedule. We've factored in traffic when designing the itinerary, so you can enjoy Bali at your leisure.

## Day 3 to Day 4: Sanur or Nusa Penida

From Seminyak or Canggu, it takes about an hour by car to reach the east coast of southern Bali. Depending on your preferences, you can choose **Option 1** to explore the beach vibes of Sanur, or **Option 2** to head to Nusa Penida and marvel at its stunning natural landscapes and underwater world.

### Option 1: Sanur

- Sanur is a laid-back beach town on Bali.
- It boasts serene, beautiful beaches with a promenade stretching for several kilometers, perfect for sunbathing and leisurely strolls.
- The beachfront path is lined with eclectic restaurants, shops, and markets blending exotic and Balinese flavors, where you can buy local handicrafts and souvenirs.
- The sunrise over Sanur Beach is spectacular—wake up early to soak in the tranquility and beauty of Bali.
- Sanur is also a hotspot for water sports, including kayaking, stand-up paddleboarding, and windsurfing.
- The harbor for island hopping is right here, making it easy to catch an early boat to Nusa Penida or Lembongan.

## **Day 5 to Day 6: Ubud**

About an hour’s drive from Sanur, you’ll reach Ubud, Bali’s most famous mountain town.

- Before heading to Ubud, take a slight detour to explore [Bali’s largest art wholesale market](/en/blog/kumbasari-art-market-shopping/).
- Ubud was once the capital of a Balinese kingdom and remains the island’s cultural and artistic heart.
- It’s the most culturally and artistically vibrant area in Bali.
- Stay in a resort villa or boutique hotel in Ubud to soak up its unique atmosphere and charm.
- Visit nearby attractions like the Sacred Monkey Forest, UNESCO-recognized rice terraces, and stunning waterfalls.
- Join local cultural experiences, such as Ubud craft workshops or traditional Balinese cooking classes.

## **Days 7 to 8: Kuta or Jimbaran**

To tackle Bali's traffic challenges, we recommend spending the last one or two nights in Kuta or Jimbaran. Both areas are close to the airport—as long as you don't stay too far north or south, you can usually reach the airport within about 30 minutes on your final day. This arrangement ensures you won't arrive at the airport too early and get bored, nor worry about missing your flight due to traffic jams. Whether you choose Kuta or Jimbaran, there are plenty of stunning attractions to explore, providing a perfect end to your trip.

**Option 1: Kuta**

- Kuta is one of Bali's busiest commercial districts, packed with shops, markets, and malls. You can find everything from exquisite Balinese crafts and clothing to jewelry and other souvenirs.
- Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous beaches, known for its golden sand and impressive waves. It's a surfer's paradise and a great spot for sunbathing and watching the sunset.
- Kuta offers a wide range of dining options, from [local traditional dishes](/en/blog/bali-top-10-foods/) to international cuisine, satisfying every palate.
- Kuta's nightlife is vibrant, with numerous bars, clubs, and entertainment venues. After dark, you can dive into Bali's lively party scene.
- At Kuta Beach, you can try surfing and water sports—it's world-famous as a beginner-friendly surf spot. [Check out Xiao Jie's first surfing video](https://youtu.be/gECdNvG16ww).

## What NOT to Do vs. What to Do

### How NOT to Plan Your Bali Itinerary

1. **Overpacked and rushed schedule**: Avoid filling every day with too many sights and activities. Instead, give yourself time to relax and soak in Bali’s beauty and laid-back vibe. A good rule of thumb is to plan no more than four attractions/activities plus lunch/dinner per day.
2. **Bouncing between regions**: Though Bali isn’t huge, traffic jams are a real headache. Moving from one area to another can eat up a lot of time and energy, leaving you tired and frustrated. When planning your itinerary, try to focus on one region at a time to save time and energy, and to dive deeper into the local culture and attractions.
