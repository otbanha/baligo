## Day 3 to Day 4: Sanur or Nusa Penida

From Seminyak or Canggu, it's about an hour's drive to the eastern coast of southern Bali. Depending on your preferences, you can choose [Option 1] to explore the beach vibes of Sanur or [Option 2] to visit Nusa Penida and marvel at its stunning natural landscapes and underwater world.

### Option 1: Sanur

- Sanur is a laid-back beach town on Bali, exuding a relaxed charm.
- Sanur boasts serene and beautiful beaches, with a several-kilometer-long beachfront boardwalk, making it an ideal spot for sunbathing and beach strolls.
- The beachfront boardwalk in Sanur is lined with a variety of seaside restaurants, shops, and markets that blend exotic flavors with Balinese characteristics, where you can purchase local handicrafts and souvenirs.
- The sunrise views at Sanur Beach are spectacular; you can wake up early to enjoy the sunrise and experience the tranquility and beauty of Bali.
- Sanur is also a popular spot for water activities, where you can enjoy kayaking, paddleboarding, windsurfing, and more.
- The pier for island hopping is located here, making it convenient to catch an early morning boat to Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan.

### Option 2: Nusa Penida

- The harbor to Nusa Penida is located in Sanur.

## **Days 5 to 6: Ubud**

- Ubud was once the capital of Bali's ancient kingdom and remains the island's cultural and artistic heart.
- It's a place brimming with unique cultural and artistic vibes.
- Stay in Ubud's resort villas or boutique hotels to immerse yourself in its distinctive ambiance.
- Visit nearby attractions like the Sacred Monkey Forest, UNESCO-listed rice terraces, and stunning waterfalls.

## **Days 7 to 8: Kuta or Jimbaran**

To tackle Bali's traffic challenges, we recommend spending the last one or two nights in Kuta or Jimbaran, both conveniently located near the airport. As long as you don't stay too far north or south, you can typically reach the airport in about 30 minutes on your final day. This ensures you won't arrive too early and be bored, nor will you risk missing your flight due to traffic. Whether you choose Kuta or Jimbaran, both areas offer stunning attractions to explore, providing the perfect finale to your trip.

**Option 1: Kuta**

- Kuta is one of Bali's most bustling commercial areas, featuring a wide variety of shops, markets, and malls. Here, you can find exquisite Balinese crafts, clothing, jewelry, and more.
- Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most famous beaches, renowned for its golden sand and spectacular waves. It's a paradise for surfers and an ideal spot for sunbathing and watching sunsets.
- Kuta offers a diverse range of dining options, from [local traditional dishes](/blog/bali-top-10-foods/) to international cuisine. You can savor delicious dishes from around the world to satisfy your palate.
- Kuta's nightlife is vibrant, with numerous bars, clubs, and entertainment venues. After dark, you can immerse yourself in Bali's lively party culture.
- At Kuta Beach, you can try surfing, as it's globally known as a beginner-friendly surf spot. [Watch Xiao Jie's first surfing experience here](https://youtu.be/gECdNvG16ww).

## What Not to Do vs What to Do

### Don't Arrange Your Bali Itinerary Like This

1. **Avoid Overcrowded Itineraries:** Resist the temptation to pack your schedule with too many attractions and activities. Instead, allow yourself time to relax and soak in Bali's stunning scenery and laid-back atmosphere. A good rule of thumb is to plan no more than four attractions/activities plus lunch/dinner per day.
2. **Minimize Cross-Region Travel:** While Bali isn't large, traffic congestion is severe. Moving between regions can consume significant time and energy, leading to fatigue and inconvenience. When planning your itinerary, try to focus on exploring and engaging in activities within one area. This approach saves time and energy while allowing for a deeper understanding of local culture and attractions.
