## Family Travel Tips

### 1. **Climate & Clothing**

Bali has a tropical climate. During summer vacation (June to August), it's the dry season with hot weather and strong sunlight. Prepare lightweight, breathable clothing, sun hats, sunglasses, and high-SPF sunscreen for your kids. During winter vacation, Bali is in the rainy season, so be prepared for brief showers.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7c08d86c6df883cdd0f82bbf4a41a31c.png)

If you plan to watch the sunrise on a volcano, whether hiking or taking a jeep, bring warm clothing. For more details, check out [Mount Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/blog/batur-sunrise-hiking-guide/).

### 2. **Health & Safety**

- **Mosquito Protection**: Bali has many mosquitoes. Local mosquito repellent is more effective, and choose accommodations with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking Water**: Avoid tap water. Buy bottled water or use boiled water.
- **Medical Preparation**: Bring common medications like fever reducers, anti-diarrheal, motion sickness pills, and band-aids. Refer to: [Essential Medications for Bali Travel: What to Bring? How to Buy Mosquito Repellent/Sunscreen?](/blog/bali-travel-medicine-checklist/)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fe90182feb7b0f11bdd7ee503a63a11.png)

### 3. **Transportation & Mobility**

Bali's traffic can be chaotic, so it's recommended to book reputable private car services and prepare child safety seats. For younger children, avoid long car rides and choose attractions closer to your accommodation to minimize travel time.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d7d530bfe7d4a2f4a1ffbbb9f5e99b7.jpg)

### 4. **Dining Options**

Bali offers a wide variety of dining options, but children's stomachs may be more sensitive. It's advisable to choose restaurants with good hygiene standards and avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned dishes. For example, Bali's famous roast pig (babi guling) is usually very spicy and may not be suitable for kids!

Additionally, many restaurants offer children's menus, and you can also opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the flavors are kid-friendly.

**Quick Reference:**

### 5. **Cultural Etiquette**

Bali locals are friendly and welcoming, but the culture places great emphasis on etiquette. Teach your children to respect local customs, such as staying quiet when entering temples and avoiding touching religious items.

## **Eight Family-Friendly Activities in Seminyak**

Seminyak is one of Bali’s most stylish areas, ideal for families who enjoy modern amenities and beach activities.

### **1. Surfing Adventure**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_acd3def2c723eb7ba857e888a6b2173f.jpg)

Bali is one of the world’s best destinations for family-friendly surfing, especially in Seminyak’s **Seminyak Beach** and **Double Six Beach**. These spots are not only a paradise for surfers but also perfect for family fun. With gentle waves and smooth sand, it’s an ideal place for kids and parents to experience the thrill of surfing together!

Here, you’ll find many professional **family-friendly surf schools**. The instructors are not only experienced but also know how to ensure kids enjoy surfing safely. The schools provide surfing gear for the whole family, from small boards for kids to professional ones for adults—everything you need is available!

**Tips for Family Surfing:**

1. **Beginner Courses**: If it’s your family’s first time surfing, sign up for beginner lessons. Coaches will start with the basics, making it easy for everyone to get started.
2. **Family Challenges**: Parents and kids can compete to see who can stand the longest or ride the farthest, adding fun interaction!
3. **Beach Games**: If surfing gets tiring, you can build sandcastles or play frisbee on the beach, enjoying the sun and sea breeze.

Whether you're a child or an adult, you can find your own surfing rhythm here and create unforgettable family memories!

### 2. Bali's Fun Water World – Waterbom Bali

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4a40d018acfd204d09d0db5b3cc0c0dc.jpg)

**Waterbom Bali**, located about 40 minutes from Seminyak, is a massive water park and one of Asia's most popular! It's not just a great place for adults to relax, but also a dream paradise for kids! Spanning **3.8 hectares**, the park features **26** meticulously designed water slides and various fun water facilities, ensuring the whole family can enjoy themselves all day without getting bored!

**Family Fun Highlights:**

1. **Thrilling Water Slides**: Family members who love a challenge can try the high-speed slides like 'Climax' or 'Smash Down 2.0', experiencing the thrill of plunging from great heights!
2. **Relaxing Lazy River**: If you want to unwind, the whole family can float along the **Lazy River** on inflatable rings, enjoying a leisurely time.
3. **Kids' Zone**: The park also has a shallow water area and small slides specially designed for children, ensuring they can have fun safely!

**1. Is Bali suitable for traveling with infants or toddlers?**

Yes! Bali is very suitable for traveling with infants or toddlers, and you can even [hire a nanny](/blog/bali-family-travel-guide-2/).

Many restaurants offer kids' meals and family-friendly seating.

**3. Is it necessary to bring a stroller to Bali?**

If your child is young, it's recommended to bring a lightweight stroller, although some areas have uneven terrain where a baby carrier might be more practical.

**4. What are the medical facilities like in Bali?**

Major cities like Kuta, Seminyak, and Sanur have international hospitals that can handle travel emergencies.
