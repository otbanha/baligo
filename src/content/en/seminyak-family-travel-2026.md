## Family Travel Tips

### 1. Climate & What to Wear

Bali has a tropical climate. Summer (June–August) is the dry season, hot with strong sun. Pack light, breathable clothes, sun hats, sunglasses, and high-SPF sunscreen for kids. Winter is the rainy season, with brief showers likely.

![1. 氣候與穿著](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7c08d86c6df883cdd0f82bbf4a41a31c.png)

If you plan to watch sunrise from a volcano, whether hiking or taking a jeep, bring warm clothes. For more, see [Batur Sunrise Hiking Guide](/en/blog/batur-sunrise-hiking-guide/).

### 2. Health & Safety

- **Mosquito protection**: Bali has many mosquitoes. Local repellent works best; choose accommodation with mosquito nets.
- **Drinking water**: Avoid tap water; buy bottled or boiled water.
- **Medical kit**: Pack common medicines like fever reducers, anti-diarrheal, motion sickness pills, and band-aids. See: [What essential medicines to bring for Bali travel? How to buy mosquito repellent/sunscreen?](/en/blog/bali-travel-medicine-checklist/)

![2. 健康與安全](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1fe90182feb7b0f11bdd7ee503a63a11.png)

### 3. **Transportation & Getting Around**

Traffic in Bali can be chaotic, so it's best to book a reputable private car service and arrange a child safety seat. If your child is young, avoid long drives and choose attractions close to your accommodation to minimize back-and-forth travel.

![3. 交通與移動](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d7d530bfe7d4a2f4a1ffbbb9f5e99b7.jpg)

### 4. **Dining Options**

Bali offers a wide variety of restaurants, but children's stomachs can be sensitive. Choose eateries with good hygiene standards and avoid overly spicy or heavily seasoned dishes. For instance, Bali's famous babi guling (suckling pig) is often very spicy and may not be suitable for kids!

Additionally, many restaurants offer children's menus, and you can also opt for international chain restaurants to ensure the flavors are kid-friendly.

**Quick Reference:**

- [Top 10 Foods in Bali](/en/blog/bali-travel-tips/)

### 5. **Cultural Etiquette**

Balinese people are friendly and warm, but local culture places great emphasis on manners. Teach your children to respect local customs, such as staying quiet when entering temples and not touching religious objects.

## **8 Family-Friendly Activities in Seminyak**

Seminyak is one of Bali's trendiest areas, ideal for families who love modern amenities and beach activities.

### **1. Surfing Adventure**

![1.冲浪冒險之旅](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_acd3def2c723eb7ba857e888a6b2173f.jpg)

Bali is one of the world's best destinations for family surfing fun! In particular, **Seminyak Beach** and **Double Six Beach** in Seminyak are not only a paradise for surf enthusiasts but also a fantastic spot for families. The gentle slopes and calm waves make it perfect for kids and parents to enjoy surfing together!

Here, you'll find many professional **family surf schools** with instructors who are not only experienced but also skilled at helping children enjoy the thrill of surfing safely. The schools offer surf gear for the whole family, from small boards for kids to professional boards for adults—everything you need!

**Family Surfing Tips:**

1. **Beginner Lessons**: If the whole family is trying surfing for the first time, sign up for beginner lessons. The instructor will start with the basics, making it easy for everyone to get the hang of it.
2. **Family Challenge**: Parents and kids can compete to see who can stand the longest and ride the farthest, adding a fun interactive element!
3. **Beach Games**: When you're tired of surfing, build sandcastles or play frisbee on the beach, soaking up the sun and sea breeze.

Whether you're a child or an adult, everyone can find their own surfing rhythm here and create unforgettable family memories!

### 2. Bali's Ultimate Water Playground – Waterbom Bali

![2.峇里島的歡樂水世界——Waterbom Bali](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4a40d018acfd204d09d0db5b3cc0c0dc.jpg)

**Waterbom Bali** is about a 40-minute drive from Seminyak. This massive water park is one of the most popular in Asia, offering a perfect blend of relaxation for adults and pure joy for kids! Spread across **3.8 hectares**, the park features **26** thrilling water slides and a variety of fun attractions, ensuring the whole family can play all day without getting bored.

**Family-Friendly Highlights:**

1. **Thrilling Water Slides**: For those seeking an adrenaline rush, try the high-speed slides like 'Climax' or 'Smash Down 2.0' for an exhilarating plunge from above!
2. **Relaxing Lazy River**: If you want to unwind, the whole family can float along the **Lazy River** on inner tubes, enjoying a leisurely drift.
3. **Kids' Zone**: The park also has a dedicated shallow area and smaller slides for children, letting them play safely and happily!

**1. Is Bali suitable for traveling with babies or toddlers?**

Yes! Bali is very suitable for traveling with babies or toddlers, and you can even [hire a babysitter](/en/blog/bali-family-travel-guide-2/).

Many restaurants offer kids' meals and family-friendly seating.

If your child is small, it's recommended to bring a lightweight stroller. However, some areas have uneven roads, so a baby carrier may be needed.

**4. What are the medical facilities like in Bali?**

Major cities like Kuta, Seminyak, and Sanur have international hospitals that can handle travel emergencies.
