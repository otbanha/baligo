**Bottom line**: The ultimate one-day Uluwatu itinerary is — **Padang Padang Beach** (white sand surf spot) → **Karang Boma Cliff** or **Nyang Nyang Beach** (hidden gem) → **Balangan Viewpoint** (best sunset spot) → **Uluwatu Temple** for the Kecak fire dance finale. This guide is compiled from the travel vlog 'Gypsy in Sneakers', covering attractions and trendy cafés across four zones: Pecatu, Ungasan, Uluwatu, and Pandawa.

The YouTube influencer 'Gypsy in Sneakers' takes you through the must-see spots in Bali's Uluwatu area in her detailed video.

She first explains how Uluwatu, once a laid-back surf town, is now becoming Bali's hottest new travel destination. Then she breaks down the region's main zones—Pecatu, Ungasan, Uluwatu, and Pandawa—giving viewers a clearer picture of Bali's southern peninsula.

She first takes you to the famous Padang Padang Beach, which not only boasts white sand but is also a popular surf spot, even featured in the movie 'Eat, Pray, Love'.

A quick intro to Padang Padang Beach

Next up is Karang Boma Cliff, a steep and challenging yet stunning viewpoint. She then showcases the breathtaking seascape of Dreamland Beach and the remote, hidden gem Nyang Nyang Beach, often called the 'last secret paradise'.

At Balangan Viewpoint, she enjoyed what is widely considered the best sunset view in the area. Besides the beach, she also visited several popular local Instagram-worthy restaurants, such as Drifter Cafe and Nourish Cafe, to sample local cuisine.

At Uluwatu Temple, she not only admired the stunning scenery of this cliff-top temple but also witnessed the iconic traditional Kecak fire dance performance.
