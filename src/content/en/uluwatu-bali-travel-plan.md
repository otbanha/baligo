**Bottom line**: The ultimate one-day Uluwatu itinerary is — **Padang Padang Beach** (white sand surfing spot) → **Karang Boma Cliff** or **Nyang Nyang Beach** (hidden gem) → **Balangan Viewpoint** (best sunset spot) → **Uluwatu Temple** for the Kecak fire dance finale. This is compiled from the travel vlog of YouTuber 'Gypsy in Sneakers', covering attractions and trendy cafes in the four districts of Pecatu, Ungasan, Uluwatu, and Pandawa.

'Gypsy in Sneakers', a YouTube influencer, thoroughly showcases and introduces the tourist attractions of the Uluwatu area in Bali through her video.

She first explains how Uluwatu, once a laid-back surfing town, is now emerging as Bali's hottest new travel destination. She then introduces the main districts of the area, including Pecatu, Ungasan, Uluwatu, and Pandawa, giving viewers a clearer picture of Bali's southern peninsula.

She first takes viewers to the famous Padang Padang Beach, which not only boasts white sand but is also a popular surfing spot, even featured in the movie 'Eat, Pray, Love'.

Introduction to Padang Padang Beach

Next, she visits Karang Boma Cliff, a steep and towering spot that offers a challenging yet stunning view. She then showcases the breathtaking seascape of Dreamland Beach and the remote, hidden gem known as 'the last secret spot', Nyang Nyang Beach.

At Balangan Viewpoint, she enjoyed what is widely considered the best sunset view in the area. Beyond the beach, she also visited several popular local Instagram-worthy restaurants, such as Drifter Cafe and Nourish Cafe, to sample the local cuisine.

At Uluwatu Temple, she not only admired the stunning views of this cliffside temple but also witnessed the iconic traditional Kecak fire dance performance.
