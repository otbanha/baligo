If you had plans to hike Mount Agung in Bali during this period, you might want to put those plans on hold for now.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775359906886-agung.jpeg)

## Mount Agung Completely Closed from Late March to Late April

All hiking routes to Mount Agung will be closed from March 28th to April 24th this year, with reopening expected on April 25th.

The mountain was closed due to a major religious ceremony taking place at the Besakih Temple (Pura Agung Besakih) at its base - Tawur Tabuh Gentuh and Ida Bhatara Turun Kabeh, two of the most important Hindu festivals in Bali.

## Why Are Mountains Closed for Religious Ceremonies?

In Balinese belief, Mount Agung and Besakih Temple are considered as one, both sacred spaces. During major ceremonies, the entire mountain enters a 'sacred time,' prohibiting outsiders from entering or climbing.

This is not a sudden regulation, but rather a long-standing tradition in Bali. Local communities, tour guides, and tourism operators are all required to comply and refrain from attempting to go up the mountain during this period.

It's important to note - this isn't just a 'recommendation'. The consequences of forcibly entering in violation of regulations can be severe, including deportation.
