## January

### January 1st: New Year's Day (Indonesian National Holiday)

### **January 9th: Siwa Ratri (Balinese Hindu Festival)**

- January 1st, New Year's Day, is a public holiday observed worldwide, including in Indonesia/Bali, with celebrations typically peaking on December 31st during New Year's Eve festivities. During this period, Indonesians enjoy a spontaneous "Nataru" holiday—see the festival introduction at the end of December for more details.

![跨年倒數是每年元旦的高潮！在峇里島也有很多地方舉辦倒數派對喔！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_90f97b1bd0d4353fa00d0518a9fab011.png)

The countdown to midnight is the highlight of New Year's celebrations! Bali also hosts numerous countdown parties!

- January 9th marks [Siwa Ratri], one of the significant festivals in the Balinese calendar, emphasizing introspection, meditation, and seeking forgiveness. Rituals include staying awake for 36 hours, fasting for 24 hours, and maintaining silence for 12 hours. Following this period, purification ceremonies are often held on the beach, alongside rituals at major temples across the island.

## February

### **February 8th: Ascension of Prophet Muhammad (Indonesian National Holiday)**

### **February 9th - 15th: Chinese Lunar New Year Holiday**

### **February 28th - March 9th: Galungan Kuningan (Balinese Hindu Festival)**

- February 8th, 2024, commemorates the Ascension of Prophet Muhammad, a national holiday in Indonesia. This significant Islamic festival honors the Prophet's ascent to heaven. On this day, Muslims gather in mosques for prayers and worship.
- February 9th to 15th marks the Chinese Lunar New Year! Indonesia designates the first day of the lunar year (February 10th, 2024) as a national holiday. This year, it coincides with February 8th, another national holiday (Ascension of Prophet Muhammad), making February 9th a flexible holiday. Thus, friends in Indonesia can enjoy a four-day holiday during the Lunar New Year (including February 11th, Sunday)! While the week-long Chinese Lunar New Year holiday may seem unrelated to Bali, it's a peak travel season as many tourists from China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan take advantage of the long break to visit Bali!

![農曆新年假期大人放長假、小孩放寒假、還可以帶著家中長輩來峇里島避寒喔～](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_641795a1061cfdbbdd2df651788f4be8.png)

Celebrate the Lunar New Year with a long vacation for adults, winter break for kids, and bring the elders to Bali to escape the cold!

- February 28 to March 9: Galungan & Kuningan - One of Bali's most important festivals, lasting about 10 days, celebrating the creation of the universe and the triumph of good over evil. During this festival, people honor their ancestors at home, seeking their blessings, and perform rituals at temples to show respect to the deities. Galungan & Kuningan also marks the beginning of the Balinese New Year according to the Pawukon calendar, signifying the day of the universe's creation. Schools in Bali close for two weeks during this period, highlighting its significance!

## April

### **April 8 - 15: Eid al-Fitr Joint Holiday (Public Holiday)**

### **April 10: Eid al-Fitr (Indonesian Public Holiday)**

- The Eid al-Fitr holiday in Indonesia for 2024 will begin on April 8 (Friday) and continue until April 15. Eid al-Fitr is the most important holiday in Indonesia, celebrating the end of the Muslim fasting month (April 10). It is a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, similar in significance to Chinese Lunar New Year. 

In Bali, the influx of local Indonesians typically starts around April 5, filling the island with crowds. This marks the first peak season among Bali's five major high seasons. It is highly recommended to avoid traveling to Bali during this period!

## October

### **3rd - 12th: Navaratri (Bali Hindu Festival)**

### **12th: Dussehra (Bali Hindu Festival)**

- Navaratri is a Hindu festival celebrating the victory of Goddess Durga. This festival usually takes place in September or October each year, lasting for 9 days, during which people engage in fasting, prayers, flower offerings, and dances. The final day of Navaratri is Dussehra, celebrating Prince Rama's defeat of the demon king Ravana.

## November

### **Day 1: Diwali (Bali Hindu Festival)**

- Diwali is a Hindu festival celebrating the victory of light over darkness. This festival usually takes place in October or November each year, with the date determined by the Hindu calendar. In 2024, Diwali will be celebrated on November 1st.

## December

### **25th: Christmas (Indonesian National Holiday)**

### **26th: Christmas Flexible Holiday**

- December 25th is Christmas, also a national holiday in Indonesia. For Christians, Christmas is an important festival, with nearly 10% of Indonesians identifying as Christian. Since Christmas and New Year's Day are close together, many Indonesians try to take time off to combine the two holidays into one long break. This gives them the opportunity to spend more time with family and friends, and to travel to other cities or countries. Indonesians have even coined the term "Nataru," meaning "Christmas - New Year holiday," combining "Natal" (Christmas) and "tahun baru" (New Year).
