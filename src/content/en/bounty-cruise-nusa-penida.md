## What’s the Story Behind Bounty Cruise?

Bounty Cruises is one of Bali's veteran cruise companies, operating since 1999. They primarily focus on four types of services: Day Cruises, Dinner Cruises, water activities, and private charters.

The 'Day Cruise - Nusa Penida Floating Beach Club' we're discussing here is currently one of their most popular offerings.

To put it simply, the itinerary goes like this:

Depart from **Benoa Harbor** on a large catamaran → sail to the waters of **Nusa Penida** → dock at a **floating platform** (Floating Beach Club) → enjoy various water activities there → return to Bali in the afternoon.

The entire day focuses on being 'at sea,' with almost no time spent on land.

## How is this different from a typical Nusa Penida day trip? This is crucial.

I think this is where most people get confused, so let's clarify.

### A typical Nusa Penida day trip looks like this:

The usual process is: Depart from Sanur by speedboat → upon arrival, take a car → rush through attractions like Kelingking Beach, Broken Beach, Angel's Billabong, and Diamond Beach.

從 Sanur 搭快艇出發 → 上島後搭車 → 一路狂跑景點，像是 Kelingking Beach、Broken Beach、Angel's Billabong、Diamond Beach。

Simply put, this itinerary focuses on 'cliffside photography + Instagrammable spots,' with the highlight being the island's landscapes.

### But Bounty Cruise is a completely different experience

It emphasizes ocean views, water activities, snorkeling, water sports, relaxation, and a vacation vibe.

To put it plainly, it sells the experience of a 'floating beach club + water park,' rather than an in-depth exploration of Nusa Penida itself.

**So if you're expecting to snap those iconic Kelingking-style photos, this tour really isn't for you.**

## Biggest Feature: Large Cruise Ships Are Much More Comfortable

Honestly, this might be the most compelling reason to book this tour.

Anyone who's been to Nusa Penida knows—regular speedboats can really rock. They're shaky, noisy, and when the waves are big, it feels like you're on a pirate ship. Many people get seasick just from the boat ride.

Especially during the rainy season, when the sea conditions are rough, or during early morning boat trips, it can really make you question your life choices. 😂

But Bounty uses **large catamarans**, which are inherently more stable, and they come equipped with stabilizer systems, making the overall experience much better. The company also emphasizes that they meet Australian safety standards and can carry hundreds of passengers.

So this tour is particularly friendly to the following types of people:

- ✅ Elderly
- ✅ Children
- ✅ People prone to seasickness
- ✅ Those who don't enjoy thrilling speedboat rides

Just the fact that the boat is stable makes it worth the extra cost for some people.

## How's the Snorkeling Experience? Honestly, It Depends on Luck

The snorkeling spots are usually in the **Toya Pakeh waters**, which is one of the more popular snorkeling areas near Nusa Penida.

On a good day: the water is beautifully blue, visibility is great, and there are plenty of fish.

But I have to be honest—**the sea conditions around Nusa Penida are really hit or miss**. The currents here are naturally strong, so sometimes it's beautiful, sometimes the waves are rough, sometimes the water is murky, and it's easy to feel seasick.

Many travelers on Reddit have shared similar experiences: whether a day trip to Nusa Penida is enjoyable largely depends on the **weather, sea conditions, and boat schedule** that day.

So if you're prone to seasickness, not a strong swimmer, or easily nervous, **make sure to take seasickness medication beforehand**. I think this is more important than choosing which tour to join. 😅

## Is it family-friendly? I think it’s quite suitable.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779116418270-bounty-cruise-wibit-nusa-penida.webp)

Compared to other Nusa Penida tours, I’d say **Bounty Cruise is more family-friendly**, and here are three reasons why:

**First, the pace isn’t as intense.** You don’t spend the whole day driving over mountains and valleys, so kids won’t melt down and adults won’t be exhausted.

**Second, the activities are kid-approved.** Water slides, banana boats, a floating platform, kayaking—these are things kids usually don’t want to stop playing.

**Third, the boat is more stable.** This makes a huge difference. Many families end up traumatized after their first experience taking a speedboat from Sanur with kids and never want to touch Nusa Penida again. 😂

## But the downsides are also obvious, so think carefully beforehand.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779116743335-fcaf7ed9-920c-463a-b2d0-336fbe75c193-1737435892472-3ebd596edf8f61fb7ab49bd4cc2bc9c2.webp)

Having talked about so many advantages, we must also honestly discuss the drawbacks.

### 1. You Won't See Nusa Penida's Classic Attractions

This is the most important point.

This tour doesn't take you to places like Kelingking, Broken Beach, or Diamond Beach. If it's your first time visiting Bali and Nusa Penida, and you consider this tour as a 'deep exploration of Nusa Penida'—you might be disappointed.

### 2. The Price Isn't Exactly Cheap

Currently, the price is around **IDR 1.85 juta** (there's a local price for Indonesian KTP/KITAS holders).

Actually, it's a bit more expensive than the usual 'speedboat + island car tour'. The difference is—you're paying for **comfort**, not for the attractions.

### 3. It Can Get Quite Crowded

Especially during peak season, there are many Korean, Chinese, European-American families, and Indonesian domestic tourists.

It's not the kind of 'hidden gem island experience', but more of a 'lively sea club'. So if you're expecting a serene, private escape, you might find it falls short of your expectations.

## Is It Worth It? My Personal Take

Here's how I'd break it down:

### If you're this type of traveler, it's a great fit

- ✅ Don't want to deal with long travel times
- ✅ Don't want to be tortured by Nusa Penida's terrible roads
- ✅ Want to relax and unwind
- ✅ Enjoy water activities
- ✅ Traveling with kids or elderly family members
- ✅ Just want to easily capture ocean views

Then Bounty Cruise is actually a pretty good choice.

### But if it's your first time visiting Nusa Penida

I'd actually recommend — **stay on the island overnight**.

Because the truly breathtaking part of Nusa Penida is actually its land scenery. The cliffside ocean views at Kelingking Beach, Diamond Beach, and Atuh Beach are on a completely different level of awe compared to the ocean views you'll see on the cruise. 😄

## Final advice: Treat it as a 'luxury ocean experience'

Here's how I'd suggest adjusting your mindset —

**Don't** treat it as a 'Nusa Penida in-depth tour' ❌

**Do** treat it as a 'luxury day cruise club experience' ✅

With the right mindset, you'll have a great time throughout the trip.

If your goal is simply 'to leisurely enjoy the sea, take photos, and relax,' then Bounty is truly worth it.

But if it's your first time visiting Nusa Penida, I strongly recommend—**actually go ashore and explore the island**. The awe-inspiring cliffside ocean views can't be replaced by a floating platform. 😂
