## What's the Story Behind Bounty Cruise?

Bounty Cruises is a long-standing cruise company in Bali, operating since 1999. They mainly offer four types of services: Day Cruises, Dinner Cruises, water activities, and private charters.

The one we're talking about today—the "Day Cruise - Nusa Penida Floating Beach Club"—is currently one of their most popular products.

Here's the gist of the itinerary:

Depart from **Benoa Harbor** on a large catamaran → Sail to the **Nusa Penida waters** → Dock at a **floating platform** (Floating Beach Club) → Enjoy various water activities there → Return to Bali in the afternoon.

The entire day is centered on the "sea," with almost no time spent on land.

## How Is This Different from a Typical Nusa Penida Day Trip? This Is Key

I think this is where most people get confused, so let me clarify first.

### What a Typical Nusa Penida Day Trip Looks Like

The usual routine goes like this:

Depart from Sanur by speedboat → take a ride after arriving on the island → rush through attractions like Kelingking Beach, Broken Beach, Angel's Billabong, and Diamond Beach.

Simply put, it's a 'cliff photos + check-in' focused itinerary, with the highlight being **the island's landscapes**.

### But Bounty Cruise is completely different

It focuses on sea views, water play, snorkeling, marine facilities, relaxation, and a resort vibe.

To put it plainly, it sells an 'ocean club + floating water park' experience, not an in-depth exploration of Nusa Penida itself.

**So if you're expecting to snap Instagram-worthy shots like at Kelingking, this tour really isn't for you.**

## Biggest Highlight: Large Cruise Ships Are Way More Comfortable

Honestly, this might be the best reason to book this tour.

Anyone who's been to Nusa Penida knows—regular speedboats really do rock. They're bumpy, loud, and when the waves get big, it feels like riding a pirate ship. Plenty of people get seasick just from the boat ride.

Especially during the rainy season, rough seas, or early morning departures, it can be a truly miserable experience. 😂

But Bounty uses **large catamarans**, which are inherently more stable, plus they have a stabilizer system, making the whole experience much better. They also claim to follow Australian safety standards and can carry hundreds of passengers.

So this tour is especially great for:

- ✅ Seniors
- ✅ Kids
- ✅ People prone to seasickness
- ✅ Those who don't like thrilling speedboat rides

Just the fact that the boat is stable is enough to make it worth paying extra for some people.

## How's the Snorkeling Experience? Honestly, It's a Bit of a Gamble

Its snorkeling spots are usually in the **Toya Pakeh waters**, which is one of the more popular snorkeling areas near Penida.

When the weather is good: the water is crystal clear, visibility is decent, and there are plenty of fish.

But I have to be honest—**the sea conditions around Nusa Penida are really a game of luck**. The currents in this area are naturally strong, so sometimes it's stunning, sometimes the waves are rough, sometimes the water is murky, and it can even make you seasick.

Many travelers on Reddit share similar thoughts: whether a day trip to Penida is fun largely depends on the **weather, sea conditions, and boat schedules** of the day.

So if you're prone to seasickness, not a strong swimmer, or get anxious easily, remember to **take seasickness medicine beforehand**. I think that's more important than choosing which tour group. 😅

## Is it suitable for families? I'd say it's quite family-friendly

Compared to other Penida tours, I find Bounty Cruise **more suitable for families**, for three reasons:

**First, the pace is less intense.** You don't have to spend all day riding through mountains and valleys, so kids won't melt down and adults won't be exhausted.

**Second, kids will love the activities.** Water slides, banana boats, floating platforms, kayaks—these are things kids usually enjoy so much they don't want to go home.

**Third, the boat is more stable.** This makes a huge difference. Many families who take their kids on a Sanur speedboat for the first time end up with a traumatic experience and never want to touch Penida again. 😂

## But the downsides are obvious—you need to think it through first

![但缺點也很明顯，要先想清楚](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779116743335-fcaf7ed9-920c-463a-b2d0-336fbe75c193-1737435892472-3ebd596edf8f61fb7ab49bd4cc2bc9c2.webp)

After all those pros, I have to be honest about the cons.

### 1. You won’t see Nusa Penida’s iconic spots

This is the biggest point.

It’s not the kind of tour that takes you to Kelingking, Broken Beach, or Diamond Beach. If it’s your first time in Bali and your first time on Nusa Penida, and you’re expecting this to be a “deep dive into Nusa Penida”—you’ll be disappointed.

### 2. The price isn’t exactly cheap

Currently, the price is around **IDR 1.85 million** (there’s a local rate for Indonesian ID card holders).

In fact, it’s a bit pricier than the typical “speedboat + island car charter” tours. The difference is—you’re paying for **comfort**, not for sights.

### 3. It can get pretty crowded

Especially during peak season, there are plenty of Korean tourists, Chinese tourists, Western families, and Indonesian domestic travelers.

It's not that kind of 'hidden-gem island experience'—it leans more toward a 'lively sea club.' So if you're expecting a private, quiet escape, you might feel a bit let down.

## So, is it worth it? My take

Here's how I'd break it down:

### It's a great fit if you're this type of traveler

- ✅ Don't want to deal with long commutes
- ✅ Don't want to suffer through Nusa Penida's rough roads
- ✅ Just want to relax and unwind
- ✅ Love water activities
- ✅ Traveling with kids or elderly family
- ✅ Want easy, scenic ocean views

Then Bounty Cruise is actually a solid choice.

### But if it's your first time visiting Nusa Penida

I'd actually suggest—**stay overnight on the island instead.**

Because what truly makes Nusa Penida stunning is its landscape. The cliffs and ocean views at Kelingking Beach, Diamond Beach, and Atuh Beach offer a completely different level of awe compared to what you see from the cruise. 😄

## Final tip: Just treat it as a 'sea resort experience'

Here's how I'd suggest adjusting your mindset—

**Don't** treat it as a 'deep dive trip to Nusa Penida' ❌

**Do** treat it as a 'luxury sea club day trip' ✅

Get the mindset right, and you'll have a blast the whole way through.

If all you want is to 'relax by the sea, take photos, and zone out,' then Bounty is totally worth it.

But if it's your first time visiting Nusa Penida, I'd strongly recommend—**actually go ashore and explore**. The sheer awe of those cliffside ocean views is something no floating platform can replace. 😂
