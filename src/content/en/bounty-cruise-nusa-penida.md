## What is Bounty Cruise all about?

Bounty Cruises is one of Bali's veteran cruise companies, operating since 1999. They primarily offer four types of services: Day Cruises, Dinner Cruises, water sports activities, and private charters.

The 'Day Cruise - Nusa Penida Floating Beach Club' we're discussing here is currently one of their most popular products.

To put it simply, the itinerary goes like this:

Depart from **Benoa Harbor** on a large catamaran → sail to the **waters around Nusa Penida** → dock at a **floating platform** (Floating Beach Club) → enjoy various water activities there → return to Bali in the afternoon.

The entire day focuses on being 'at sea,' with almost no time spent on land.

## How is this different from a typical Nusa Penida day trip? This is crucial

I think this is where most people get confused, so let's clarify.

### Here's what a typical Nusa Penida day trip looks like:

The usual process is:

Take a speedboat from Sanur → upon arrival, transfer to a car → rush through attractions like Kelingking Beach, Broken Beach, Angel's Billabong, and Diamond Beach.

In simple terms, it's an itinerary focused on "cliff photography + Instagram spots," with the highlight being **the island's landscapes**.

### But Bounty Cruise is completely different

It emphasizes ocean views, water activities, snorkeling, sea facilities, relaxation, and a vacation vibe.

To put it plainly, it sells the experience of a "floating beach club + water park" rather than an in-depth exploration of Nusa Penida itself.

**So if you're expecting those Instagram-worthy shots at Kelingking Beach, this tour really isn't for you.**

## What exactly does the itinerary include?

According to Klook and official information, the current offerings generally include:

- Breakfast
- Buffet-style lunch
- Snorkeling
- Banana boat rides
- Kayaking
- Water slides
- Glass Bottom Boat (for underwater viewing)
- Floating Beach Club platform
- Hotel transfers in certain areas

If you opt for the upgraded package, you'll also get access to the **Wibit inflatable water park** and additional paid water activities.

Overall, it's a relaxed, "do what you want when you want" kind of pace, without being constantly rushed by a guide.

## The Biggest Advantage: Large Cruise Ships Are Much More Comfortable

Honestly, this might be the most compelling reason to book this tour.

Anyone who's been to Nusa Penida knows—regular speedboats can really rock. They're bumpy, noisy, and when the waves are big, it feels like you're on a pirate ship. Many people get seasick just from the boat ride.

Especially during the rainy season, when the sea conditions are rough, or on early morning trips, it can really make you question your life choices. 😂

But the Bounty uses a **large catamaran**, which is inherently more stable, and it’s equipped with a stabilizer system, making the overall experience much better. The company also emphasizes that it meets Australian safety standards and can carry hundreds of passengers.

So this tour is particularly friendly to the following types of people:

- ✅ Elderly
- ✅ Children
- ✅ People prone to seasickness
- ✅ Those who don’t enjoy thrilling speedboat rides

Just the fact that the boat is stable makes it worth the extra cost for some people.

## How’s the Snorkeling Experience? Honestly, It’s Luck of the Draw

The snorkeling spots are usually in the **Toya Pakeh area**, which is one of the more popular snorkeling spots near Nusa Penida.

On a good day: the water is blue, visibility is great, and there are plenty of fish.

But I have to be honest — **the sea conditions around Nusa Penida are really luck-dependent**. The currents here are naturally strong, so sometimes it's beautiful, sometimes the waves are big, sometimes the water is murky, and it's easy to get seasick.

Many travelers on Reddit have shared similar experiences: whether a day trip to Nusa Penida is enjoyable largely depends on the **weather, sea conditions, and boat schedule** that day.

So if you're prone to seasickness, not a strong swimmer, or easily nervous, **make sure to take seasickness medication beforehand**. I think this is more important than choosing which tour to join. 😅

## Is it family-friendly? I think it's quite suitable.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779116418270-bounty-cruise-wibit-nusa-penida.webp)

Compared to other Nusa Penida tours, I'd say **Bounty Cruise is more family-friendly**, for three reasons:

**First, the pace isn't as intense.** You don't have to spend the whole day riding over mountains and valleys, so kids won't melt down and adults won't be exhausted.

**Second, the activities are kid-friendly.** Water slides, banana boats, floating platforms, kayaking — kids usually don't want to go home after playing with these.

**Third, the boat is more stable.** This makes a huge difference. Many families end up traumatized after their first fast boat ride from Sanur with kids and never want to touch Nusa Penida again. 😂

## But the drawbacks are also obvious, so think carefully beforehand.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-05/1779116743335-fcaf7ed9-920c-463a-b2d0-336fbe75c193-1737435892472-3ebd596edf8f61fb7ab49bd4cc2bc9c2.webp)

Having talked about so many advantages, we must also honestly discuss the drawbacks.

### 1. You Won't See Nusa Penida's Classic Attractions

This is the main point.

It's not the kind of tour that takes you to Kelingking, Broken Beach, or Diamond Beach. If it's your first time in Bali and your first visit to Nusa Penida, and you consider this tour as a 'deep exploration of Nusa Penida'—you might be disappointed.

### 2. The Price Isn't Exactly Cheap

Currently, the price is around **IDR 1.85 juta** (there's a local price for Indonesian KTP/KITAS holders).

In fact, it's a bit more expensive than the usual 'speedboat + island car tour'. The difference is—you're paying for **comfort**, not the attractions.

### 3. It Can Get Quite Crowded

Especially during peak season, there are many Korean, Chinese, European, American families, and Indonesian domestic tourists.

It's not the kind of 'secret island experience', but more of a 'lively sea club'. So if you're expecting a serene, private retreat, there might be a bit of a mismatch.

## Is It Worth It? My Personal Take

Here's how I'd break it down:

### If You're These Types of Travelers, It's a Great Fit

- ✅ Don't want to deal with long travel times
- ✅ Don't want to suffer through Nusa Penida's rough roads
- ✅ Want to relax and unwind
- ✅ Enjoy water activities
- ✅ Traveling with kids or elderly family members
- ✅ Just want to easily capture ocean views

Then Bounty Cruise is actually a pretty good choice.

### But If It's Your First Time Visiting Nusa Penida

I'd actually recommend — **stay overnight on the island**.

Because the truly breathtaking part of Nusa Penida is actually the island's landscapes. Places like Kelingking Beach, Diamond Beach, and Atuh Beach — those cliffside ocean views are on a completely different level of awe compared to what you see on the cruise. 😄

## Final Advice: Treat It as a "Sea Vacation Experience"

Here's how I'd suggest adjusting your mindset —

**Don't** treat it as a "Nusa Penida in-depth tour" ❌

**Do** treat it as a "luxury day cruise club experience" ✅

With the right mindset, you'll have a great time throughout the trip.

If your goal is simply "I just want to leisurely enjoy the sea, take photos, and relax," then Bounty is really worth it.

But if this is your first time visiting Nusa Penida, I strongly recommend you—**actually go ashore and explore the island**. The awe-inspiring cliffside ocean views can't be replaced by a floating platform. 😂
