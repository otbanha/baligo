**Key Takeaway**: From July 1, 2026 (applicable to trips on or after this date), Bali charter **base prices remain unchanged**, with a fixed **fuel surcharge** added per trip—**IDR 50,000 for half-day, IDR 100,000 for full-day**. This ensures short trips and airport transfers aren't disproportionately affected, and the surcharge can be flexibly reduced if fuel prices drop in the future.

### 📢 Official Announcement from Bali Driver Team: Charter Service Price Adjustment (Effective July 1, 2026)

> Our decision: Base fares stay the same, with a per-trip fuel surcharge – continuing to serve you all

Dear travelers, hello!

We are a team of drivers offering charter services in Bali. In recent years, **the Indonesian Rupiah has been depreciating**, and **global fuel prices have been rising**, making our daily expenses for gas, vehicle maintenance, and even living costs much heavier. After thorough discussion and agreement, we've decided to implement a **'base charter price unchanged, with a per-trip fuel surcharge'** model starting **July 1, 2026**.

We didn't want to simply raise the base charter price, as that would be unfair to short trips and airport transfers, and it would be troublesome to lower it again if fuel prices drop. So, this is the best way to balance **your affordability** with **our livelihood**.

***

## 🔁 New Billing Method (Jointly Decided by Drivers)

- **Base Charter Price**: Unchanged (same as the previously announced price list)
- **Additional 'Fuel Surcharge' Per Trip**: A fixed amount based on charter duration
- **Total Cost** = Base Charter Price + Fuel Surcharge

By doing this, you can still clearly see the original fare, and we can subsidize the actual increase in fuel costs.

![新的收費方式（由司機們共同決定）](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-06/1781345800282-mixcollage-13-jun-2026-05-13-pm-5945.jpg)

## 📅 Effective Date and Applicable Scope
- **Effective Date**: Starting July 1, 2026
- **Applicable Scope**: All trips with a **departure date on or after July 1, 2026**

## ❓ Why a "Fuel Subsidy" Instead of a Direct Price Increase?
- A direct price increase would affect all trips (including short runs and airport transfers), which is unfair to those passengers.
- Fuel prices may drop in the future, so a subsidy offers more flexibility—if oil prices fall, we can reduce or cancel the subsidy.
- **This is the consensus among most of our drivers** – transparent, simple, and acceptable to everyone.
- Half-day charter fuel subsidy: 50,000; Full-day charter fuel subsidy: 100,000
