## Can You Snorkel Anywhere in Bali?

Although Bali’s main island is surrounded by coastline, there aren’t many spots truly suitable for snorkeling or diving. The most famous dive spots—Blue Lagoon, Amed, and Tulamben—are located in the more remote eastern part of the island, requiring a two to three-hour drive from the airport. Below is Xiao Jie’s introduction to snorkeling in Amed:

https://youtu.be/qdS8Td7QPYY?si=J-jy5ieS7-eAsl0E

To reach snorkeling or diving spots more quickly, we recommend taking a car from the airport to Sanur, staying there for a night or two, and then catching an early morning speedboat to Nusa Penida, which takes about 30 minutes. This is a more convenient option that avoids long travel times.

For those interested in what Sanur has to offer, check out Xiao Jie’s "[Sanur Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=laRb_l8BdLM)".

## Crystal Bay

![Crystal Bay](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_233401d4c81f691e18d2a6149a0d9830.jpg)

Crystal Bay

Crystal Bay is one of the most popular attractions on Nusa Penida, a secluded bay on the island's west coast with a beach stretching about 200 meters. Accessible by road, this bay is renowned not only for its picturesque palm-fringed beach but also as Nusa Penida's premier snorkeling and diving destination. The crystal-clear waters (hence the name Crystal Bay) offer exceptional underwater visibility, sometimes reaching up to 30 meters, making it a favorite among divers.

In addition to a diverse array of colorful corals and tropical fish, Crystal Bay is one of the few places in the world where you can observe the ocean sunfish (mola mola, known as manbo fish in Taiwan/Japan) up close. These massive migratory fish can weigh up to 2,000 kilograms and grow up to 3 meters in length. Divers visiting between July and October may have the chance to encounter and swim alongside these gentle giants.

## Gamat Bay

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774881880928-gamatbay1.jpeg)

Gamat Bay

Gamat Bay, along with Crystal Bay, is celebrated as one of Nusa Penida's top snorkeling spots. The shallow waters here are exceptionally clear, making it ideal for exploring the vibrant coral reef ecosystem.

At Gamat Bay, you can spot various hard corals, such as staghorn coral, flower coral, and large brain coral. Algae like anemones and sea fans thrive here as well. Small tropical fish, including butterflyfish, snappers, and surgeonfish, dart among the coral formations. Occasionally, small reef sharks can also be seen.

Another highlight is the presence of sea turtles. Different species, such as hawksbill and green turtles, are often observed in the shallow waters near the shore. From June to October, during the nesting season, you might even witness mother turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs.

Due to its proximity to the coast and calm waters, Gamat Bay is perfect for children and beginners to snorkel. Remember to wear a life jacket, goggles, and fins, and you can safely enjoy the magical world of this underwater garden.

## Manta Point

![Manta Point](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_20761005e11f28ee9b74adf674bab312.jpg)

Manta Point

Manta Point is one of the most distinctive attractions on Nusa Penida Island. At this diving spot, you can get up close and personal with the giant manta rays, offering a unique and breathtaking experience.

Known as 'devil fish,' the giant manta ray can grow up to 7 meters in length and weigh around 3000 kg—pretty impressive, right? With such size and weight, it's natural to feel apprehensive about approaching them, but don't worry. Despite their enormous size, manta rays are not dangerous at all. They are gentle giants and very friendly toward humans. There are two types of manta rays in the world: the reef manta ray and the oceanic manta ray.

The manta rays at Manta Point on Nusa Penida Island are of the reef manta ray species (Manta alfredi). This type is the second-largest species, averaging 3 to 3.5 meters in length. These beautiful creatures are filter feeders, meaning they consume plankton, crustaceans, and small fish. Although they have long tails, they are not venomous, making swimming with them very safe. In addition to their long tails, manta rays have the largest brains among bony fish. They are highly interactive with humans, making Manta Point on Nusa Penida Island the perfect spot for snorkelers and divers to capture close encounters with these majestic creatures.

A reminder: when we say 'interaction,' we don’t mean actually touching these wild animals! The main reason is to protect the manta rays' skin—they are covered in a layer of mucus that helps shield them from environmental harm. If touched by visitors, this mucus layer can be removed, increasing the risk of skin infections. Although manta rays are gentle, they are still wild animals, and touching them may startle them or provoke an instinctive reaction that could harm people. Most diving areas with manta rays have strict rules against touching marine life, and visitors should adhere to these guidelines.

If you want to encounter manta rays, the recommended months are **April to November**, especially during the dry season (April to October), when the water is clear, currents are stable, and visibility is at its best—making it the ideal time for snorkeling and diving.

Among these months, **August to October** is the peak period for encountering manta rays, as the currents bring an abundance of plankton, attracting large numbers of manta rays to feed. In southern Bali, **Manta Point** and **Manta Bay** on **Nusa Penida Island** are popular spots for observing these magnificent creatures.

Please note: The appearance of manta rays is influenced by weather and ocean currents, so sightings cannot be guaranteed 100%.

## Toyapakeh Wall Point

Toyapakeh Wall Point is actually a cliff located just below the Adiwana Warnakali Dive Center.

This dive site features a small coral plateau 4 to 6 meters deep, which is the best spot for snorkeling. Beyond the coral plateau, it divides into two layers. The first layer is about 12 to 15 meters deep, and the second layer is around 40 meters deep. This is a very popular dive site due to its clear waters and thriving coral.
