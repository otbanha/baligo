Super simple guide for recreational users – no application needed, complete with no-fly zones & practical checklist

Planning to take your drone to Bali for dreamy rice fields and sunset beaches but worried about fines or confiscation? This freshly updated 2026 guide breaks down all the regulations in plain language! No more endless searching – just save this and you're ready to go!

### Quick 30-Second Summary

- **Most travelers**: Drone **< 2kg** + recreational use → **No application needed**, fly freely!
- **Key rule**: Follow the three golden rules: 'Within line of sight, 150 meters max altitude, avoid crowds and temples.'
- Most common penalties for violations: Fines, confiscation, or even affecting future entries.

### 1. Do I Need to Apply for Permission?

**Good news**: 99% of travelers fall into the 'no application needed' category!

### 2. The Most Important 'No-Fly' Rules (Remember These 8 Points to Avoid Trouble)

- **Absolutely no flying within 15 km** of Bali's Denpasar Airport (many popular beaches are actually within this zone!)
- Maintain a distance of at least **30 meters** from people, vehicles, and boats
- Do not fly over temples (Pura), religious events, or sacred sites
- Avoid military bases, government buildings, and police stations
- Do not fly over private hotels or villas (many hotels outright prohibit it)
- Must fly **during daylight + clear visibility** – absolutely no flying at night or in rain
- Do not launch from moving vehicles or boats
- Never operate under the influence of alcohol or drugs

Key airport regulations: Official guidelines and most resources state that drones must be flown at least 15 km away from Ngurah Rai Airport in Denpasar. This effectively restricts many popular beach towns in southern Bali, such as Kuta, Seminyak, and parts of Canggu. However, some users report being able to fly as close as 5 km (depending on specific location and altitude). It’s recommended to follow official rules and use flight apps like AirMap to check local airspace in real-time.

**Cultural reminder in Bali**: Locals take their religion very seriously, and flying drones over temples may lead to being stopped. Always ask your hotel or locals first—better to show respect and avoid trouble!

### 3. Can I bring a drone into Bali?

✅ **Absolutely!**
For personal use, customs usually won’t hassle you with one drone, but make sure to:

- Carry batteries in your carry-on (never check them in)
- Check airline restrictions on battery quantity

### 4. What about commercial filming or heavier drones?

- Drones over 2kg, filming for YouTube monetization, weddings, etc. → Requires a permit
- Apply online via Indonesia’s official **SIDOPI-GO** system
- Prepare insurance and flight plans **at least 2 weeks** in advance
- Contact DGCA: hubud@dephub.go.id or +62 8 111 004 222

### 5. Practical Tips for Flying in Bali (for smoother, more stunning shots)

- **Best locations**: East Bali’s Amed, West Bali National Park, Jatiluwih rice terraces, Balangan Beach
- Consider buying a **landing pad** (sand and volcanic ash can damage your drone)
- Golden hours (sunrise, sunset) are ideal—midday sun causes harsh glare
- Be mindful of traditional kite season (especially June-August)
- Strongly recommend drone insurance (covers water damage or accidents involving people)

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I fly over hotel pools?**
A: Most hotels prohibit it, so it's best to check with the front desk first.

**Q: Can I use FPV goggles?**
A: Someone must monitor the ground situation for you; otherwise, it’s against regulations.

**Q: Will the rules suddenly change?**
A: As of 2026, there haven’t been major updates, but it’s recommended to email DGCA for confirmation before your trip.

Bali in 2026 remains quite friendly toward recreational drones—the key is to **follow the rules + respect local culture**. Fly safely, capture stunning shots, and help protect this beautiful island!
