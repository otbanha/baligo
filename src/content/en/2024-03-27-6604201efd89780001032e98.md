When planning your Bali trip, booking accommodation is one of the most crucial steps. Bali offers a wide range of luxury hotels, exquisite villas, and affordable stays, but how do you find something that fits your budget while still being comfortable? Here are some money-saving tips to help you save big when booking your accommodation in Bali.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e1458b18b30d2ce80d48b7e014546a95.png)

Want to have an amazing trip without breaking the bank? Check out these money-saving tips!

## Avoid Peak Tourist Season

First, let's start with choosing the right time to travel! Try to avoid visiting Bali during peak tourist seasons, as prices for flights, accommodations, and activities tend to skyrocket. Opting for the off-season or less busy periods can not only save you money on flights and accommodations but also provide a more peaceful travel experience.

So when is Bali's peak tourist season? Generally, the dry season with lower rainfall is the best time to visit Bali, typically from April to September, which is also the peak travel season. What are the downsides of avoiding the peak season? Check out [this link](/blog/2023-12-06-657078edfd89780001f2d88d/) to learn more about Bali's rainy season.

Besides seasonal differences, these five periods also see surges in Bali visitors, ranked by crowd/traffic levels as follows:

1. Indonesia's Eid al-Fitr holiday is a crucial time. A large number of local Indonesian tourists visit this paradise island during this approximately ten-day holiday. This is Bali's busiest peak season!
2. Christmas-New Year holidays usually attract global tourists, as this is a long holiday for most Western countries, and Indonesians will try to take time off too. Therefore, the Christmas-New Year period ranks second in terms of crowds and traffic. Here's a short video showing [the situation on the airport highway on 12/29/2023](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide/permalink/3579975912271532/).
3. Chinese New Year period is another special time, with holidays ranging from two to five days in countries like China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Singapore, South Korea, and Vietnam; Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia have a one-day holiday. The Chinese New Year holiday ranks third in attracting large numbers of tourists and causing crowded attractions.
4. The period from July 1 to September 5, coinciding with European long holidays and Northern Hemisphere summer vacations, usually creates a travel trend, with many families and students taking trips to Bali during this time. Summer vacation flight and accommodation prices are never cheap!
5. Australian school holidays and Indonesian international school holidays (one week between September 20 and October 10) also bring many Australian tourists and families from Indonesian international schools who embark on trips during this period.

During these periods, tourist numbers surge dramatically, and accommodation and attraction prices may rise due to high demand. Therefore, if planning to visit Bali during these peak seasons, it's best to book early and prepare thoroughly to ensure a pleasant, worry-free journey.

## Choose Affordable Yet High-Value Accommodation

Accommodation can take up a significant portion of your travel budget! Bali offers an overwhelming number of options, but don't get swayed by luxurious resorts. Choosing affordable yet quality stays can stretch your budget further. Additionally, consider sharing rooms with friends to not only reduce accommodation costs but also enhance the fun of your trip.

## ****Subscribe to Promotional Emails****

Subscribe to email newsletters from hotels and booking platforms to stay updated on the latest promotions and special offers. Sometimes they release limited-time deals that allow you to book at a lower price.

## ****Pay in Local Currency****

When using a credit card to pay for accommodation, prioritize settling payments in the local currency. This avoids unnecessary currency conversion fees and helps you save money to the greatest extent.
