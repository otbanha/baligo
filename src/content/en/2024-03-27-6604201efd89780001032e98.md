When planning your Bali trip, booking accommodations is one of the most important steps. Bali offers a variety of luxury hotels, boutique villas, and affordable stays, but how do you find something that fits your budget while still being comfortable? Here are some money-saving tips to help you save big when booking accommodations in Bali.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e1458b18b30d2ce80d48b7e014546a95.png)

Want to have fun without breaking the bank? Check out these money-saving hacks!

## Avoid Peak Travel Seasons

First, let's start with choosing your travel dates! Try to avoid visiting Bali during peak seasons, as flight tickets, accommodations, and activities tend to skyrocket in price. Opting for the off-season or less busy periods not only saves you money on flights and stays but also allows for a more peaceful travel experience.

So when is Bali's peak travel season? Generally, the dry season with lower chances of rainfall is the best time to visit Bali, typically from April to September, which is also Bali's peak travel season. What are the downsides of avoiding peak season? Check out [this link](/blog/2023-12-06-657078edfd89780001f2d88d/) to learn more about Bali's rainy season.

Apart from seasonal distinctions, here are five specific periods when Bali sees a surge in visitors, ranked by the level of crowds and traffic:

1. Indonesia's Eid al-Fitr holiday is a significant time. A large number of local Indonesian tourists flock to this paradise island during this roughly ten-day holiday, making it Bali's busiest peak season!
2. The Christmas-New Year holiday attracts global tourists, as it coincides with long vacations in most Western countries, and Indonesians also try to take time off. Thus, the Christmas-New Year holiday ranks second in terms of crowds and traffic. Check out this short video of [the highway situation on December 29, 2023](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide/permalink/3579975912271532/).
3. The Chinese Lunar New Year period is another special time, with holidays ranging from two to five days in countries like China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Singapore, South Korea, and Vietnam; the Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia observe a one-day holiday on the first day of the lunar year. The Lunar New Year holiday ranks third in attracting large crowds to tourist spots.
4. The period from July 1 to September 5 coincides with Europe's long holidays and the summer break for students in the Northern Hemisphere, leading to a travel surge as families and students head to Bali for vacations. Summer flight and accommodation prices are never cheap!
5. Australian school holidays and Indonesian international school holidays (a week between September 20 and October 10) also bring in many Australian tourists and families attending international schools in Indonesia, who embark on trips during this time.

During these periods, tourist numbers skyrocket, and prices for accommodations and attractions may rise due to high demand. Therefore, if you plan to visit Bali during these peak seasons, it's best to book in advance and prepare thoroughly to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.

## Choose Affordable Yet High-Value Accommodations

"Accommodation" can take up a significant portion of travel expenses! Bali offers countless accommodation options, but don't be swayed by luxurious resorts! Opting for affordable yet quality stays can stretch your budget further. Additionally, consider sharing a room with friends—this not only reduces accommodation costs but also adds to the fun of your trip.

## **Subscribe to Promotional Emails**

Subscribe to email notifications from hotels and booking websites to stay updated on the latest promotions and special deals. Sometimes they offer limited-time discounts, allowing you to book at a lower price.

## **Pay in Local Currency**

When paying for your accommodation with a credit card, always opt to settle in the local currency. This avoids unnecessary currency conversion fees, helping you save money to the fullest.
