YouTuber 'Paradise Bird' stayed at Bali's beloved Ayana Resort and shared insights into six of its 14 stunning pools in her YouTube video. Her footage highlights the unique styles and scenic beauty of these pools, captivating viewers with their charm. These pools are not just for swimming and relaxation but also serve as perfect spots for capturing beautiful photos and creating unforgettable memories.

AYANA Resort is a captivating destination located on Jimbaran Bay in southern Bali. This resort boasts its own private beach and offers a range of hotel brands at different price points, including RIMBA, SEGARA, RESORT, and VILLA, catering to travelers with varying needs and budgets. No matter which brand you choose, you'll have access to all the resort's facilities and services.

Among these, RIMBA is an excellent choice for budget-conscious travelers, allowing full access to all pools and facilities. The newly built SEGARA offers a fresh experience, providing a more private and luxurious stay. For those seeking higher-end indulgence, the VILLA option, the resort's most premium brand, delivers lavish accommodations and services.

## The Viral H2O / River Pool

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776081381855-pools-beach-ayana-river.jpeg)

Unlike the casual, no-wait photo experience of the past, the River Pool (H2O Pool) featured in the video now has new rules:

***

### 📸 The Queue Scene: Like a Timed Competition

Ayana's River Pool has strict photography rules, typically open from **6:30 AM to 9:00 AM (or 9:30 AM)**. This period feels like a 'timed, limited, numbered' photo challenge:

- **1-2 minutes per person**: Staff members are on-site with timers, and when time's up, you must step aside—creating a tense atmosphere.
- **Draw a number, wait for your turn**: You can't just show up and shoot; you must draw a number first. If you arrive around **7:45 AM**, expect to wait about **30 minutes**.
- **The waiting area feels like a photo exchange**: People nervously wait while sharing tips on angles and poses, making the scene both fun and lively. Some visitors joked that guys often scramble under the timer's pressure.

***

### 💁‍♀️ Don’t Worry About Taking Photos Alone—Staff Are Super Professional

If you're solo or worried about your companion's photography skills, don't stress—**the staff are more than happy to assist**, and they’re surprisingly good:

- They’ll guide you on poses, lighting, and even run around to capture shots from different angles.
- Many visitors later exclaimed, 'The photos turned out even better than I expected!'

***

### 🗓️ Want to Snag the Perfect Shot? Keep These Tips in Mind

Based on recent visitor experiences, follow these tips to ensure a successful photo session:

1. **Wake up early!**
Arrive between **6:30 AM and 7:30 AM**. If you show up at 8:00 AM, you might wait an hour or even miss the photo window.
2. **Where’s the most convenient place to stay?**
The River Pool is located in **Ayana's VILLAS area**. However, even if you stay at the more budget-friendly **RIMBA** or **SEGARA**, you can easily reach it via the resort's free shuttle, which runs every 10 minutes.
3. **Make the most of your 1-2 minutes**
Don’t panic when it’s your turn—plan 1-2 poses in advance. Save reference photos on your phone to show the staff for efficient communication.
4. **Photography tips**
Use **Live Photos** or burst mode to avoid missing the perfect shot due to blinking or awkward angles—there’s no second chance.

***

### ⚠️ A Friendly Reminder

The pool area can be extremely slippery. Multiple guests have already warned about being extra cautious, as some have fallen, including a young woman. While taking photos is important, safety should always come first.
