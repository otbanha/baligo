YouTuber 'Paradise Bird' stays at Bali's most popular Ayana Resort and shares in her YouTube video six of the resort's 14 stunning swimming pools. Her video showcases the unique styles and beautiful landscapes of these pools, attracting the attention and admiration of many viewers. These pools are not just places for swimming and relaxation, but also perfect spots for capturing beautiful photos and creating unforgettable memories.

AYANA Resort is a charming destination located on Jimbaran Bay in southern Bali. The resort boasts its own private beach and offers multiple hotel brands at different price points, including RIMBA, SEGARA, RESORT, and VILLA, catering to travelers with various needs and budgets. No matter which brand you choose, you can enjoy all the facilities and services within the resort.

Among them, RIMBA is a great option for budget-conscious travelers, allowing guests to fully enjoy all the pools and facilities within the resort. The newly built SEGARA offers a fresh experience, providing more privacy and a luxurious stay. For those seeking higher-end accommodations, consider staying at VILLA, the resort's top-tier hotel brand offering lavish accommodations and services.

## The Viral H2O / River Pool

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1776081381855-pools-beach-ayana-river.jpeg)

Unlike the past experience of taking photos freely without queuing at Ayana, the River Pool (H2O Pool) mentioned in the video now has new rules:

***

### 📸 The Queue Atmosphere: Like Participating in a Timed Competition

Ayana's River Pool has strict photography rules, usually open from **6:30 AM to 9:00 AM (or 9:30 AM)**. This period is like a "timed, limited, numbered" photo challenge:

- **1-2 minutes per person**: Staff will be on-site with timers, and once time's up, you must vacate the spot—creating a very tense atmosphere.
- **Draw a number first, then wait for your turn**: You can't just arrive and start shooting; you must draw a number first. If you arrive around **7:45 AM**, expect to wait about **30 minutes**.
- **The waiting area feels like a photo exchange**: While nervously waiting, people share tips on the best angles and poses, making the scene both fun and lively. Some tourists joked that the guys often get flustered by the timer.

***

### 💁‍♀️ Don't Worry About No One to Take Your Photos—The Staff Are Super Professional

If you're traveling alone or worried about your companion's photography skills, don't fret—**the staff are more than happy to assist**, and their skills are surprisingly good:

- They'll guide you on posing, finding the right lighting, and even run around to capture shots from different angles.
- Many tourists are amazed afterward, saying, "The photos turned out even better than I expected!"

***

### 🗓️ Want to Successfully Capture Stunning Photos? Keep These Tips in Mind

Based on recent visitor experiences, to successfully check in, be sure to follow these recommendations:

1. **You must wake up early!**
It's best to arrive between **6:30 - 7:30 AM**. If you arrive at 8 AM, some have waited an hour and might even miss the photo opportunity.
2. **Where to Stay for Convenience?**
The River Pool is located in the **VILLAS area of Ayana**. But even if you stay at the more budget-friendly **RIMBA** or **SEGARA**, you can easily reach it via the resort's free shuttle service, which runs every 10 minutes.
3. **Make the Most of Those Precious 1-2 Minutes**
When it's your turn, don't panic. Have 1-2 poses in mind beforehand. It's recommended to save your favorite sample photos on your phone and show them directly to the staff for the most efficient communication.
4. **Photography Tips**
It's recommended to use **Live Photos** or burst mode to avoid missing the perfect shot due to blinking or bad angles—after all, there's no chance for a retake.

***

### ⚠️ A Friendly Reminder

The poolside can be extremely slippery, as several guests have already pointed out. There have been instances where people, including women, have slipped and fallen. While capturing great photos is important, safety should always come first.
