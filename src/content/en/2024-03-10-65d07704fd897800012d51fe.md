## Is Bali a good place for a honeymoon?

Yes! Bali is one of the best honeymoon destinations, offering the following advantages:

1. **Romantic Atmosphere**: Bali is considered a romantic getaway, boasting stunning beaches, sunrise and sunset views, as well as luxurious resorts and private villas, allowing couples to spend unforgettable moments in a romantic setting.
2. **Diverse Activities**: Bali offers a wide range of activities, including water sports like snorkeling and diving, cultural experiences such as visiting temples and watching Balinese dance performances, and indulging in delicious cuisine and spa treatments.
3. **Beautiful Natural Environment**: Bali features breathtaking natural landscapes, including lush rice terraces, majestic volcanoes and waterfalls, and unique flora and fauna.
4. **Luxurious Accommodation Options**: Bali has a variety of luxurious accommodation options, from private villas to resorts and boutique hotels, allowing couples to find their ideal stay based on preferences and budget.
5. **Warm Climate**: Bali enjoys a warm tropical climate year-round, making it a perfect destination for couples to enjoy the sun and beaches at any time.

## How to get to Bali?

You can choose to take a direct flight to Bali. Bali's capital is Denpasar (airport code: DPS). Currently, China Airlines and EVA Air offer direct flights from Taiwan to Bali. Additionally, other Asian cities such as Singapore, Hong Kong, Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, and Ho Chi Minh City also have direct flights available. ## How Long Should You Stay in Bali?

For a Bali honeymoon, we recommend staying at least 10 nights to explore different regions, surrounding islands, and have time for both adventure and relaxation.

****What you can do in 10 nights:****

- Discover hidden beaches in southern Bali
- Experience art and nature in Ubud
- Watch the sunrise from Mount Batur
- Visit Nusa Penida or other nearby islands
- Enjoy spa treatments and other relaxing activities

You can adjust your itinerary based on your interests and budget. Here are some suggestions:

- If you love beaches, we recommend relaxing on some of the hidden beaches in the south.
- If you're into culture, spend more time exploring Bali's temples and villages.
- If you're drawn to natural beauty, we suggest spending extra time in Ubud.
- If you're adventurous, participate in outdoor activities like hiking, snorkeling, or surfing.

## Should I Bring Cash/Should I Tip?

In Bali, credit and debit cards are widely accepted, but it's advisable to carry some cash for emergencies, such as when dealing with street vendors, island tours, or tipping. Here are some specific recommendations:

- The only currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah. It's recommended to bring some US dollars in cash, which can be exchanged for Rupiah locally.
- Tipping is not mandatory, but if you receive good service, you can tip between 20,000 to 50,000 Rupiah.
- When dining in restaurants, the bill will include an 11% tax and a service charge ranging from 5% to 10%.
