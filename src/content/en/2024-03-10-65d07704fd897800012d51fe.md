## **Is Bali a Good Place for a Honeymoon?**

Yes! Bali is one of the best honeymoon destinations, offering the following advantages:

1. **Romantic Atmosphere**: Bali is considered a romantic getaway, featuring stunning beaches, sunrise and sunset views, as well as luxurious resorts and private villas, allowing couples to spend unforgettable moments in a romantic setting.
2. **Diverse Activities**: Bali offers a wide range of activities, including water sports like snorkeling and diving, cultural experiences such as visiting temples and watching Balinese dance performances, and indulging in delicious cuisine and spa treatments.
3. **Beautiful Natural Environment**: Bali boasts breathtaking natural landscapes, including lush rice terraces, majestic volcanoes and waterfalls, and unique flora and fauna.
4. **Luxurious Accommodation Options**: Bali has a variety of luxurious accommodation options, from private villas to resorts and boutique hotels, allowing couples to find their ideal stay based on preferences and budget.
5. **Warm Climate**: Bali enjoys a warm tropical climate, making it suitable for vacations year-round, so couples can enjoy the sun and beaches anytime.

## How to Get to Bali?

You can choose to take a direct flight to Bali. The capital of Bali is Denpasar (airport code: DPS). Currently, China Airlines and EVA Air offer direct flights from Taiwan to Bali. Additionally, other Asian cities like Singapore, Hong Kong, Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, and Ho Chi Minh City also have direct flights available. Be sure to check flight schedules and prices, and choose the option that best fits your itinerary.

## How long should you stay in Bali?

For a Bali honeymoon, we recommend staying at least 10 nights to explore different regions, surrounding islands, and have time for both adventure and relaxation.

**What you can do in 10 nights:**

- Discover hidden beaches in southern Bali
- Visit Ubud to experience art and nature
- Hike Mount Batur for sunrise views
- Explore Nusa Penida or other nearby islands
- Enjoy spa treatments and other relaxing activities

Depending on your interests and budget, you can adjust your itinerary. Here are some suggestions:

- If you love beaches, we recommend relaxing at some hidden beaches in the south.
- If you're into culture, spend more time exploring Bali's temples and villages.
- If you enjoy natural beauty, we suggest spending more time in Ubud.
- If you're adventurous, participate in outdoor activities like hiking, snorkeling, or surfing.

## Should You Bring Cash/Should You Tip?

In Bali, credit and debit cards are widely accepted, but it's advisable to carry some cash for emergencies, such as at small vendors, island tours, or tipping. Here are some specific recommendations:

- The only currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah. It's recommended to bring some US dollars in cash, which can be exchanged locally for Rupiah.
- Tipping is not mandatory, but if you receive excellent service, a tip of 20,000 to 50,000 Rupiah is appreciated.
- When dining at restaurants, an 11% tax and a 5% to 10% service charge will be added to your bill.
