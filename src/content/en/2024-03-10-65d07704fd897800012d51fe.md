## **Is Bali a Good Place for a Honeymoon?**

Yes! Bali is one of the best honeymoon destinations, offering the following advantages:

1. **Romantic Atmosphere**: Bali is considered a romantic resort destination with stunning beaches, sunrise and sunset views, and luxurious resorts and private villas, allowing couples to spend unforgettable times in a romantic setting.
2. **Diverse Activities**: Bali offers a variety of activities, including water sports like snorkeling and diving, cultural experiences such as visiting temples and watching Balinese dance performances, and enjoying rich cuisine and spa treatments.
3. **Beautiful Natural Environment**: Bali boasts breathtaking natural landscapes, including lush rice fields, majestic volcanoes and waterfalls, and unique flora and fauna.
4. **Luxurious Accommodation Options**: Bali has a wide range of luxurious accommodation options, from private villas to resorts and boutique hotels, allowing couples to find their ideal stay based on their preferences and budget.
5. **Warm Climate**: Bali enjoys a warm tropical climate, making it suitable for vacations year-round, allowing couples to enjoy the sun and beach anytime.

## How long should I stay in Bali?

For a Bali honeymoon, we recommend staying at least 10 nights to explore different regions, nearby islands, and have time for both adventure and relaxation.

**What you can do in 10 nights:**

- Discover hidden beaches in southern Bali
- Experience art and nature in Ubud
- Hike Mount Batur for sunrise
- Visit Nusa Penida or other nearby islands
- Enjoy spa treatments and other relaxing activities

Depending on your interests and budget, you can adjust your itinerary. Here are some suggestions:

- If you love beaches, we suggest relaxing at some hidden beaches in the south.
- If you're into culture, spend more time exploring Bali's temples and villages.
- If you enjoy natural beauty, we recommend spending extra time in Ubud.
- If you're adventurous, participate in outdoor activities like hiking, snorkeling, or surfing.

## Should I Bring Cash/Should I Tip?

In Bali, credit and debit cards are widely accepted, but it is advisable to carry some cash for emergencies, such as at small stalls, island tours, and tipping. Here are some specific suggestions:

- The only currency used in Bali is the Indonesian Rupiah. It is recommended to bring some US dollars in cash, which can be exchanged for Indonesian Rupiah locally.
- Tipping is not mandatory, but if you encounter good service, you can tip between 20,000 to 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah.
- When dining in restaurants, the bill will include an 11% tax and a 5% to 10% service charge.
