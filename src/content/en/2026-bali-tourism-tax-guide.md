Since February 14, 2024, Bali has implemented a new tourism policy called the "International Tourist Levy," applicable to all international visitors to Bali, regardless of age. Each person must pay IDR 150,000, equivalent to approximately USD$10. This policy aims to address Bali's traffic congestion and waste management issues.

### Starting February 14, 2024, an additional USD$10 is required for Bali trips

1. **Effective Date**: Starting February 14, 2024.
2. **Applicability**: Foreign tourists, including those arriving via direct flights to Bali or entering Bali through other Indonesian cities.
3. **Payment**: It is recommended that travelers pay online through the [Love Bali Official Website](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/home) or by downloading the "Love Bali" app before arriving in Bali. The Levy voucher will be scanned and verified at checkpoints.
4. **On-Site Payment**: The tax can also be paid at Bali International Airport's arrival terminal and Benoa Port.

The local tourism board states that the tax payment process takes no more than 23 seconds and strongly advises visitors to pay in advance.

## Do I Need to Pay Again After Island-Hopping?

Some news websites suggest that if you go island-hopping (Bali --> islands not under Bali Province jurisdiction --> return to Bali), you need to pay the tourism tax again upon returning to Bali. However, rest assured that even if your island-hopping destinations are outside Bali Province, such as the Gili Islands, Lombok, or Java, you do **not** need to pay the tourism tax again when returning to Bali! This is confirmed by the official FAQ on the Love Bali website (<https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/faq>):

## Inspection Checkpoint

Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) has officially established a 'Levy Vouchers Secondary Inspection Lane' **between the arrival hall exit and the pick-up area**. This means that even after clearing customs and collecting your luggage, before stepping out of the airport doors to meet your driver, inspectors will stop all passengers and require QR Code scanning.

- **On-site Payment**: If you cannot present the voucher, you must pay immediately via QRIS scan, credit card, or debit card.
- **Recommendation**: Be sure to save a screenshot of the voucher on your phone to avoid issues with unstable airport Wi-Fi preventing webpage access.

## Visa Exemptions from Tourism Tax/Tourism Levy

There are seven visa categories exempt from this tax, including diplomatic, official, transportation crew, student visas, temporary or permanent residence permit (KITAS and KITAP) holders, etc. Applications for tax exemption must be submitted **at least one month in advance**, and local tourism authorities will approve within a maximum of five working days.

Exempt visa holders must **submit** their application in advance and meet the requirements of the Love Bali system. If approved, the Love Bali system will send a notification and proof of tax exemption approval (in the form of a QR code) to the foreign visitor.

## Tourism Tax Payment Methods and Inspection Information:

- Tourist taxes can be paid upon arrival at Ngurah Rai International Airport or online via the LoveBali official website.
- Some hotels and resorts can also assist guests with the payment process.
- After payment, a QR code receipt will be issued, which tourists must keep for inspection.

Bali Tourism officials have begun on-site inspections at popular tourist spots, primarily at entry and exit points. Tourists who haven't paid the tax will need to pay on-site using a credit card; **cash payments are not accepted**.

## **Tips for Paying the Tourist Tax:**

1. It's recommended to use a desktop for payment to avoid system issues on mobile devices.
2. Ensure that your browser does not have ad blockers or VPN enabled, as these may prevent the website from functioning properly.
3. You can submit payment details for up to 10 people at once. Required information includes passport name, passport number, email address, and arrival date.
4. Island hopping does not require additional payment!

## Why Implement a Tourist Tax?

The outline for Bali's tourist tax implementation is as follows:

1. Bali is tired of tourists' misconduct and aims to protect its reputation.
2. Governor Wayan Koster has ordered the attachment of a code of conduct to tourists' passports and has expelled at least 136 foreigners for misconduct.
3. Bali will impose a tourist tax of approximately USD$10 per international visitor, allocated for cultural and environmental preservation.
4. While tourism has rebounded post-pandemic, tourists' misconduct has become problematic, including violating social taboos, disputes with local authorities, and traffic violations.
5. Disrespect for local customs by outsiders has caused discontent, displeasing local governments.
6. The tourist tax isn't a novel concept, with similar precedents elsewhere, but Bali's governor believes it will help improve the environment and enhance tourism quality.
