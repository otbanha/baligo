## **What is eVoA?**

eVoA (Electronic Visa on Arrival) is a convenient visa service introduced by the Indonesian government, allowing foreign travelers to apply for an electronic visa before arriving in Indonesia. This initiative aims to promote tourism and attract foreign investment by simplifying the visa process and providing a smoother entry experience for visitors.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ea63f982a4a65c2e26a1a5fcf1a94435.png)

## **Countries eligible for visa-free entry to Indonesia**

Currently, the eVoA service is available to citizens of 97 countries, including Australia, the UK, the US, New Zealand, South Korea, Japan, China, Taiwan, and India. Travelers can complete all procedures through the online system.

Countries eligible for visa-free entry are mostly ASEAN members. As of August 29, 2024, the following 13 countries have been announced for visa-free access:

- Brunei
- Philippines
- Cambodia
- Laos
- Malaysia
- Myanmar
- Singapore
- Thailand
- Vietnam
- East Timor
- Suriname
- Colombia
- Hong Kong

The next wave of countries eligible for visa-free treatment is expected to be announced by the end of January 2025.

## **The challenges of Indonesia's original system**

In the past, the visa application process was cumbersome and not fully digitized, causing inconvenience for many travelers upon entry. Starting in 2023, Indonesian Immigration Director Silmy Karim has pushed for a comprehensive digital upgrade, aiming to set a benchmark in immigration services. In collaboration with VFS Global, they launched the eVoA service.

## **Leave it to the Professionals! VFS Global Enhances Travel to Indonesia**

To improve the quality of entry visa services, the Indonesian government has appointed VFS Global as the sole partner for eVoA, offering a safer and more user-friendly application process. VFS Global is currently the primary visa application service provider for 67 countries, operating over 3,400 visa centers across 153 countries worldwide. Headquartered in Zurich and Dubai, the company is primarily owned by investment funds managed by the American private equity firm Blackstone Inc., with additional funding from the Swiss Kuoni and Hugentobler Foundation and EQT Fund.

Travelers will now only need to fill out a simple form, upload passport information, and pay the visa fee (currently IDR 500,000), after which they will receive the eVoA via email, which can be presented upon entry or used at automated gates.

VFS Global will officially launch in **early December**, coinciding with the peak travel season, when approximately one million international tourists flock to Bali annually. The Indonesian government hopes for a smooth transition to ensure that travelers' entry experiences remain unaffected. The new system also offers multilingual support, including English, Chinese, Japanese, and Korean, along with dedicated email and instant customer service to assist travelers in completing their visa applications more easily.
