## **What is an eVisa?**

The eVisa (electronic visa on arrival, abbreviated as eVoA/eVisa) refers to a visa that travelers apply for online through the official website of Indonesia's Immigration Directorate before departure. The application fee for the eVisa is the same as the visa on arrival at the airport, which is 500,000 IDR (approximately USD$16). The visa is valid for 30 days and can be extended once, allowing a maximum stay of 60 days. However, additional fees apply for credit card payments.

## eVisa Application Steps

1. [Fill out the form on the official website](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/) (<-- This is the only official link; beware of fake websites online)
2. Prepare a passport valid for at least six months
3. For general tourists visiting Indonesia or Bali, select 【B1 - Tourism (Visa On Arrival 30 Days)】. Make sure not to choose the wrong option!
4. Fill in travel information and upload your passport (photo page) and a photo (can be taken with a phone, clearly identifiable, preferably against a plain background)
5. Have a credit card ready for payment (Visa, Mastercard, or JCB cards are accepted for online payments. Up to five people can apply using one credit card. Amex cards are not accepted. The total amount after fees is Rp. 519,500)

The eVisa application should be completed within 3 days before departure. Once applied, it is valid for 90 days. If you have questions about the visa application, here is the 【[FAQ for Entering Indonesia/Bali with eVisa](https://s.id/AllInsonesia)】.

## What are the advantages of the eVisa?

The eVisa application usually takes only a few minutes to complete. The main advantage of applying for the eVisa in advance is avoiding the queue for visa on arrival after landing. Starting from 2024, travelers can also use the nearly queue-free e-gate services.
