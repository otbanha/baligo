## Itinerary Overview: The Perfect Land and Water Dual Experience

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_41c737cdbc148a72f531b97d2acd93c1.jpg)

This day trip is well-designed, combining two completely different adventure experiences: water rafting and land off-roading. The morning starts with a 2-hour rafting session on the Ayung River, followed by an Indonesian-style buffet lunch in the forest, and then a 1.5-hour ATV off-road ride in the afternoon. The entire trip runs from 8 AM to 5 PM, making for a very fulfilling day.

## Morning Experience: Exploring the Secrets of Ayung River Rafting

### Safety Briefing and Equipment Preparation

At 8:30 AM, the shuttle bus arrived punctually. I must commend their transportation arrangements—the air-conditioned vehicle was comfortable, and the 30-minute ride didn’t feel tiring at all. Upon arriving at the rafting starting point, the professional guide first gave a detailed safety briefing, including how to properly wear a life jacket, paddling techniques, and emergency procedures.

All safety equipment was complete and in good condition, including waterproof helmets, life jackets, and paddles. The guide also carefully checked each person’s gear to ensure it was worn correctly, which made us feel very secure.

### Rafting Process: A Water Adventure Full of Surprises

### 漂流過程：驚喜連連的水上冒險

The Ayung River rafting route is ingeniously designed, lasting about 2 hours with moderate difficulty. It features thrilling rapids as well as calm scenic sections. The biggest surprise was discovering several hidden waterfalls along the way! These waterfalls are nestled deep in the lush tropical rainforest, accessible only by bamboo raft - truly hidden gems that most tourists never get to see.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_78f83f6f2bd34de9e53e3f59a235f6ab.jpg)

One waterfall was particularly spectacular, about 15 meters high, with water cascading down the cliff face. In the sunlight, we could even see a rainbow forming. Our guide stopped here for about 10 minutes for us to take photos. My friend Sarah was initially nervous, but got completely excited upon seeing these beautiful sights, exclaiming in delight throughout the journey.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_12e5e214ed2fc2788efeef3a2bd801a8.jpg)

The most thrilling part of the rafting was navigating through several Class III rapids. The rubber raft sped through the rushing waters, splashing everywhere. We all got soaked but had an absolute blast! Kevin even joked that this was more exciting than any water park ride!

## Lunchtime: A Feast in the Forest

After rafting, we arrived at the dining area deep in the forest. The setting was truly stunning - surrounded by lush greenery, with fresh air and birds chirping. The open-air design made us feel like we were dining in nature's embrace.

The buffet was quite extensive, featuring classic Indonesian dishes like nasi goreng (fried rice), satay skewers, Indonesian chicken curry, various vegetable dishes, and fresh tropical fruits. The food quality was surprisingly good, especially the satay - tender meat with a fragrant peanut sauce. All four of us were thoroughly satisfied, fueling up for our afternoon ATV adventure.

Before lunch, there were shower facilities available - a thoughtful touch that allowed us to change into clean clothes and prepare for the afternoon activities.

## Afternoon Challenge: ATV Off-Road Exploration

### ATV Safety Training and Test Drive

At 1:30 PM, we arrived at the ATV base. Like in the morning, professional instructors first gave detailed safety instructions and operation guidance. Operating an ATV isn't particularly difficult - mainly controlling the throttle and brakes - but the instructors still carefully explained turning techniques and precautions for uphill/downhill riding.

Everyone gets a chance to test drive for a few minutes at the practice area to ensure familiarity with the controls before setting off. The safety gear, including helmets and boots, is of good quality.

### Countryside Adventure: Through Bali's Most Beautiful Villages

The ATV route mainly covers the Bongkasa Pertiwi and Taman areas, which preserve Bali's most authentic rural landscapes. Our route includes rice field paths, bamboo forest tunnels, traditional villages, and hill trails, each offering different scenery and challenges.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cb111d28d880b9d215e5593dce74447a.jpg)

The most memorable part was crossing the terraced rice fields, where the green rice stalks swayed in the wind, with distant mountains forming a picturesque rural scene. We also passed through several traditional Balinese villages, witnessing the locals' daily lives. The children excitedly waved at us, creating a warm atmosphere.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_284e63ef9aa86b0f3931cc37de500341.jpg)

The most thrilling part of the route was the hill trail, with some steep slopes and bumpy rocky paths. My friend Amy was a bit nervous at first but quickly adapted and even began to enjoy the sense of accomplishment from conquering the challenging terrain. The funniest moment was when Kevin almost tipped over at a muddy turn, but he managed to stabilize the ATV just in time, leaving us all laughing at his mishap.

### Natural Landscapes: Discovering Another Side of Bali

The ATV ride allowed us to explore Bali's inland areas, revealing landscapes completely different from the coast. Here, we encountered dense tropical rainforests, clear streams, and small waterfalls hidden in the valleys. The ecosystem along the way was rich, with sightings of various tropical birds, butterflies, and even a few wild monkeys.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_311b1eb06b6d96b8434a358a1fa59c69.jpg)

The most impressive moment was when we stopped by a small stream for a break and photos. The water was crystal clear, surrounded by pristine tropical vegetation without any trace of human interference. This untouched natural beauty was truly refreshing and gave us a new perspective on Bali.

## Practical Information and Tips

### Transportation Arrangements

If you have the option to include transportation in your package, I highly recommend it. The drivers are punctual and professional, eliminating any worries about transportation. It takes about 30 minutes from our hotel in Ubud.

### Essential Packing List

Based on our experience, here are the must-have items:

- Swimwear and change of clothes (at least two sets)
- Waterproof bag (to protect your phone and camera)
- Sunscreen (SPF 50 or higher)
- Towel
- Water shoes or sandals (ones that can get wet)
- Personal medications
- Cash (for souvenir photos and small expenses)
