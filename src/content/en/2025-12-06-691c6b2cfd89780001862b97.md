**Hi everyone, I'm a traveler who absolutely adores Bali, having visited during both the dry and rainy seasons multiple times. Many people hesitate when they hear 'Bali rainy season,' worrying about being caught in the rain all day and ruining their plans. But that's not the case at all! Bali during the rainy season has a unique charm—lush greenery, tranquility, and incredible value. Today, I'm sharing the ultimate Bali rainy season guide to ensure you have an amazing trip even during the wet season!**


## When is Bali's Rainy Season?

Bali's rainy season generally runs from **November to March** (sometimes starting with sporadic rains in October and ending completely by April). Here's the breakdown:

- Wettest months: December, January, February (especially January, with average rainfall exceeding 300mm)

- In 2025, the rainy season arrived early in September, but it still primarily concentrates after November.

- Rain characteristics: Mostly sudden tropical showers in the afternoon or evening, lasting 1-2 hours, while mornings and midday are usually sunny!

Temperatures remain hot (27-31°C), and while rain cools things down, the higher humidity can make it feel more stifling at times.

## Advantages of Visiting Bali During the Rainy Season (There Are So Many!)

1. **Fewer Crowds**: Popular attractions are crowd-free, making it perfect for photos — it feels like you have the whole island to yourself!
2. **Budget-Friendly**: Hotels, villas, and private pool villas often offer 30-50% discounts, and flights are 20-40% cheaper. Want to stay in a luxurious oceanfront villa without breaking the bank? Rainy season is the time!
3. **Stunning Scenery**: Rice fields, waterfalls, and forests turn lush green, and the blue skies, white clouds, and rainbows after the rain look like a full-on filter!
4. **Surfing Paradise**: Big waves on the southwest coast are perfect for advanced surfers.
5. **Fresh Air**: The rain washes away the dust, leaving the air exceptionally clean.

## The Downsides of Rainy Season (Honestly)

- Roads can be slippery, so be cautious when riding a scooter (consider hiring a car or using Gojek/Grab instead).

- Occasional localized flooding (especially in Kuta and low-lying areas).

- The sea can sometimes be murky, making it less ideal for snorkeling or capturing that crystal-clear blue water.

- Hiking (e.g., Mount Batur sunrise) carries higher risks, as you might not see the sun or the trip could be canceled.

## Must-Pack List for Rainy Season

- Lightweight raincoat or disposable poncho (more convenient than an umbrella)

- Waterproof phone case, waterproof backpack cover

- Quick-dry clothes and sandals (don't wear leather shoes, you'll get soaked)

- Non-slip sandals or waterproof shoes

- Mosquito repellent (mosquitoes are abundant during rainy season)

- Lightweight long sleeves (for temples or cooler evenings)

## Rainy Season Transportation Tips

- Be extra careful when riding scooters! Slippery roads + puddles make it easy to crash. Beginners should consider hiring a car or using Grab/GoJek.

- Southern areas (Kuta, Seminyak, Jimbaran, Uluwatu) usually get less rain, while Ubud and mountainous regions receive more.

- Don't force yourself to drive/ride during heavy rain. Just find a cafe to wait it out.

## Perfect Rainy Season Activities (Rain or Shine!!)

1. Spa Relaxation: Enjoy a massage with essential oils while listening to the rain - pure heaven! Prices are much cheaper than in the dry season.
2. Cafe Hopping: Ubud and Canggu are filled with Instagram-worthy cafes - perfect for sipping coffee and reading books while it rains.
3. Cooking/Silver Jewelry/Handicraft Classes: Indoor activities that capture the essence of Bali.
4. Shopping Spree: Explore Ubud Market, Kuta's shopping malls, and Seminyak boutiques - rainy days are perfect for shopping.
5. Yoga/Meditation/Healing Sessions: The serene atmosphere of the rainy season is ideal for spiritual recharge.
6. Waterfall Chasing: The rainy season brings the fullest waterfalls - Sekumpul and Tegenungan waterfalls are breathtaking!
7. Cultural Tours: Visit temples and watch traditional dance performances (many are indoors).

## My Honest Advice

If it's your first time in Bali, you're afraid of rain, and want to take lots of beach photos, I still recommend visiting during the dry season (April-October).

But if you've been before, are on a budget, and prefer a less crowded, more natural Bali, the rainy season will make you fall in love with a different charm! Staying in a villa listening to the rain and gazing at rice fields is incredibly healing!

Rainy season Bali isn't a 'second choice', but rather 'Bali with a different flavor'. Pack your raincoat, bring a flexible mindset, and set off! I guarantee you'll fall in love with this magical island under the rainbow after the rain, just like I did! 🌧️🌈🏝️
