****Hello everyone, I'm a traveler who's absolutely in love with Bali, having visited during both the dry and rainy seasons multiple times. Many people hesitate when they hear 'Bali rainy season,' worrying about being caught in the rain all day and having their plans ruined. But that's not the case at all! Bali during the rainy season has a unique charm—lush greenery, tranquility, and great value. Today, I'm sharing the complete Bali rainy season guide to ensure you have an amazing trip even during the wet season!****

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_792fcfe30be6848738548f0115fb1f14.png)

## When is Bali's Rainy Season?

Bali's rainy season generally runs from **November to March** (sometimes starting with sporadic rain in October and ending completely in April). Key details:

- Wettest months: December, January, February (especially January, with average rainfall exceeding 300mm)

- In 2025, the rainy season arrives early in September, but it's still concentrated after November.

- Rain characteristics: Mostly sudden tropical showers in the afternoon or evening, lasting 1-2 hours, with mornings and midday usually sunny!

Temperatures remain hot (27-31°C), and while rain cools things down, the higher humidity can make it feel more stifling at times.

## Advantages of Visiting Bali During the Rainy Season (There Are So Many!)

1. **Fewer Crowds:** Popular attractions are crowd-free, perfect for photography, making you feel like you have the whole island to yourself!
2. **Budget-Friendly:** Hotels, villas, and private pool villas often offer 30-50% discounts, and flights are 20-40% cheaper. Want to stay in a luxurious ocean-view villa without breaking the bank? The rainy season is the time!
3. **Stunning Scenery:** Rice fields, waterfalls, and forests turn incredibly lush, and the post-rain blue skies, white clouds, and rainbows look like a full-on filter!
4. **Surfing Paradise:** Big waves on the southwest coast are perfect for advanced surfers.
5. **Fresh Air:** The rain washes away the dust, leaving the air exceptionally clean.

## The Downsides of the Rainy Season (Honestly)

- Roads can be slippery, so be cautious when riding a scooter (consider hiring a car or using Grab instead).

- Occasional localized flooding (especially in Kuta and low-lying areas).

- The sea can be murky at times, not ideal for snorkeling or those seeking crystal-clear waters.

- Hiking (e.g., Mount Batur sunrise) carries higher risks and is often canceled.

## Must-Pack List for the Rainy Season

- Lightweight raincoat or disposable poncho (more convenient than an umbrella)

- Waterproof phone case and backpack cover

- Quick-dry clothes and sandals (avoid leather shoes—they’ll get soaked and ruin your day)

- Non-slip sandals or waterproof shoes

- Mosquito repellent (there are many mosquitoes during the rainy season)

- Light long-sleeved clothing (for temple visits or cooler evenings)

## Rainy Season Transportation Tips

- Be extra careful when riding a scooter! Slippery roads and puddles make it easy to fall, so beginners should consider hiring a car or using Grab/GoJek.

- The southern areas (Kuta, Seminyak, Jimbaran, Uluwatu) usually have less rain, while Ubud and mountainous regions get more.

- Don’t force yourself to drive or ride during heavy rain—just find a café to wait it out.

## Perfect Rainy Season Activities (Rain Won’t Stop You!!)

1. Spa and massage relaxation: The sound of rain paired with an aromatherapy massage is heavenly! Prices are much cheaper than in the dry season.
2. Café hopping: Ubud and Canggu are full of Instagram-worthy cafés—perfect for sipping coffee and reading while it rains.
3. Cooking classes/silver jewelry making/handicraft workshops: Indoor activities with a strong Balinese vibe.
4. Shopping spree: Visit Ubud Market, shopping malls in Kuta, or Seminyak’s boutiques—rainy days are ideal for shopping.
5. Yoga/meditation/healing sessions: The serene atmosphere of the rainy season is perfect for recharging your soul.
6. Waterfall chasing: Waterfalls are at their fullest during the rainy season—Sekumpul and Tegenungan waterfalls are breathtaking!
7. Cultural tours: Visit temples or watch traditional dance performances (many are held indoors).

## My Honest Advice

If it's your first time visiting Bali, you're afraid of rain, and want to take lots of beach photos, I'd recommend going during the dry season (April-October).

But if you've been before, are on a budget, and enjoy a quieter, more natural Bali, the rainy season will make you fall in love with a different side of the island! Staying in a villa listening to the rain and gazing at rice fields is incredibly soothing!

Bali in the rainy season isn't a 'second choice' but rather 'Bali with a different flavor'. Pack your raincoat, bring a flexible mindset, and set off! I guarantee you'll fall in love with this magical island under the rainbow after the rain, just like I did! 🌧️🌈🏝️
