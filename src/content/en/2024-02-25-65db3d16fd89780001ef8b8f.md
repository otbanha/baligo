After the pandemic, much of the online information about Bali's Sunday Markets has become outdated. However, some fantastic markets are still running in 2026. We've specially curated these genuine Sunday markets, not the ones that operate daily. Here are the Sunday markets still running in 2026:

## La Brisa Sunday Market 📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1BZB_5sqY7fAPbfZJkSKFuZI7ar1UTF8U&usp=sharing)

La Brisa Sunday Market is a stunning and vibrant market in Bali, located at Echo Beach in Canggu. Every Sunday, you can find local handmade jewelry, textiles, and beauty products here.

The name La Brisa means 'sea breeze,' and the structures here are made from recycled wood from over 500 old fishing boats, meticulously decorated. Today, La Brisa is one of Bali's most famous and sustainable eco-friendly spots, exuding authentic Balinese charm. Check out our feature: [La Brisa: How Did It Stand Out Among Tens of Thousands of Restaurants as Bali's Most Beautiful?](/blog/2023-12-04-656d19fffd897800019cb6ed/)

La Brisa Sunday Market runs every Sunday from 10 AM to 4 PM. It's an initiative supporting local farmers and small businesses, with LYD Organic Farm providing organic produce, baked goods, natural body care products, regenerative fashion, and zero-waste items.

If you're in Bali, don't miss La Brisa Sunday Market. Experience the sustainable decor, breathtaking views, delicious food, and spend a delightful time with the local community.

## Samadi Sunday Market 📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing)

Samadi Sunday Market is a popular Sunday market in the Canggu area of Bali. It's located on Jalan Padang Linjong, near Echo Beach. The market is open every Sunday from 8 AM to 4 PM.

Samadi Sunday Market is renowned for its organic produce and handmade crafts. The market features a variety of stalls selling fresh fruits, vegetables, meats, fish, and grains. Additionally, there are numerous stalls offering handmade clothing, jewelry, home goods, and souvenirs.

Samadi Sunday Market is a great place to buy local products and support local businesses. It's also a wonderful spot to relax, enjoy delicious food, and listen to music.

## Sanur Sunday Market (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing))

The monthly farmers' market held at Mercure Resort Sanur Beach is a family-friendly event designed to encourage community participation and support local businesses and artists. The market takes place on the last Sunday of each month from 10 AM to 6 PM, offering a relaxed shopping and social experience.

Each month, the market features various stalls offering a range of goods and services. From exquisite handicrafts to fashionable accessories, from gourmet food to eco-friendly products, there's something for everyone. This market is not just a shopping destination but also an ideal spot for family gatherings. You can enjoy delicious food, relax on the beautiful beach, or participate in seaside yoga classes.

Attending this market is more than just shopping; it's a way to support local businesses and artists. You can interact directly with vendors, learn the stories behind the products, and support local creativity and entrepreneurship. This not only helps the local economy but also fosters community development and prosperity.

## SUNDAZE ULUWATU MARKET (📌[Map](https://maps.app.goo.gl/wMDJDgG22d8n2JhU8))

Located at Hatch Bali Uluwatu on the southern tip of Bali, the SUNDAZE ULUWATU MARKET transforms into a vibrant bazaar every Sunday from 11 AM to 6 PM. Here, you can enjoy performances by talented acoustic musicians and savor delicious barbecues provided by local vendors. Whether you're searching for unique products or indulging in tasty snacks, this market is the perfect place to unwind and connect with friends, defining the relaxed and joyful weekend atmosphere of Uluwatu!
