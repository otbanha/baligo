After the pandemic, much online information about Bali's Sunday Markets has become outdated. However, some fantastic markets are still thriving in 2026. We've specially curated these genuine Sunday Markets, not the ones operating daily. Here are the Sunday Markets still running in 2026:

## La Brisa Sunday Market (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1BZB_5sqY7fAPbfZJkSKFuZI7ar1UTF8U&usp=sharing))

La Brisa Sunday Market is a stunning and vibrant market in Bali, located at Echo Beach in Canggu. Every Sunday, you can find local handmade jewelry, textiles, and beauty products here.

The name La Brisa means 'sea breeze,' and the architecture here uses recycled wood from over 500 old fishing boats, meticulously decorated. Today, La Brisa is one of Bali's most famous and sustainable eco-friendly spots, exuding authentic Balinese charm. Check out our feature: [Dubbed 'Bali's Most Beautiful Restaurant': How La Brisa Stands Out Among Tens of Thousands of Dining Spots?](/blog/2023-12-04-656d19fffd897800019cb6ed/)

La Brisa Sunday Market runs every Sunday from 10 AM to 4 PM. It's an initiative supporting local farmers and small businesses, with LYD Organic Farm providing organic produce, baked goods, natural body care products, regenerative fashion, and zero-waste items.

If you're in Bali, don't miss La Brisa Sunday Market to experience sustainable decor, breathtaking views, delicious food, and enjoy quality time with the local community.

## Samadi Sunday Market (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing))

Samadi Sunday Market is a popular Sunday market in Bali's Canggu area. It's located on Jalan Padang Linjong, near Echo Beach. The market is open every Sunday from 8 AM to 4 PM.

The Samadi Sunday Market is renowned for its organic produce and handmade crafts. The market features a wide variety of stalls selling fresh fruits, vegetables, meats, fish, and grains. Additionally, there are numerous stalls offering handmade clothing, jewelry, home goods, and souvenirs.

Samadi Sunday Market is an excellent place to purchase local products and support local businesses. It's also a great spot to relax, enjoy delicious food, and listen to music.

## Sunday Market Sanur (📌[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=12W9y9WtufmH2a430BAds6QtTa5TyHoE&usp=sharing))

The monthly farmers' market held at Mercure Resort Sanur Beach is a family-friendly event designed to encourage community participation and support local businesses and artists. Scheduled for the last Sunday of each month from 10 AM to 6 PM, it offers a relaxed and enjoyable shopping and social experience.

Each month's market features numerous stalls covering a wide range of goods and services. From exquisite handicrafts to fashionable accessories, from gourmet food to eco-friendly products, there's something for everyone. This market is not just a shopping destination but also an ideal place for family gatherings. You can savor delicious food, relax on the beautiful beach, or participate in seaside yoga classes.

Attending this market is more than just shopping; it's a way to support local businesses and artists. You can directly interact with vendors, learn the stories behind the products, and support local creativity and entrepreneurship. Doing so not only helps the local economy but also fosters community development and prosperity.

## SUNDAZE ULUWATU MARKET  (📌[Map](https://maps.app.goo.gl/wMDJDgG22d8n2JhU8))

Located in Hatch Bali Uluwatu at the southern tip of Bali, SUNDAZE ULUWATU MARKET transforms into a vibrant bazaar every Sunday from 11 AM to 6 PM. Here, you can enjoy performances by talented acoustic musicians and savor delicious barbecue from local vendors. Whether you're looking for unique products or tasty snacks, this market is the perfect place to unwind and connect with friends, defining Uluwatu's charm with a laid-back weekend vibe!
