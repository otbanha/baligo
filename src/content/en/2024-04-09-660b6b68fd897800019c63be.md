Bali, this picturesque land, attracts travelers from all over the world. In our Facebook group, Chihming Lai (https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100008235455950), who is not a professional traveler or travel agency employee, makes a special trip to Bali every six months to relax, enjoy the sun, beer, beaches, and bikinis. For him, the unique slow-living atmosphere of Bali is the greatest charm of this land. Below are his personal travel experiences and practical tips that he is happy to share.

(The following content is adapted and reprinted with permission from Chihming Lai (https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100008235455950))

## Preparation Before Departure:

Before departure, he suggests applying for an electronic visa online. When entering with a Taiwanese passport, you can scan your passport for facial recognition, allowing for quick clearance without queuing.

After collecting your luggage and before exiting the airport, you need to prepare an electronic customs declaration form. It is essential to complete the electronic customs declaration form in advance, as scanning it upon arrival can significantly save time during customs clearance.

As for the tourism tax, he believes it's up to the individual to decide whether to pay it or not. He personally supports environmental activities like turtle releases, despite questions about fund utilization, considering it a small contribution to environmental protection. The modest amount of USD$10 is equivalent to a few beers.

## Upon Arrival in Bali:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e13870a67da8a8539a2df155af5a63e5.jpg)

1. Don't pack your itinerary too tightly. Instead of rushing through your trip, slow down and enjoy the local pace of life. Wander aimlessly, sip a cool drink, and let your mind relax.
2. Currently, Bali offers a more peaceful environment, and beach pollution has significantly improved.
3. For those wondering which attractions to visit, he suggests that while Monkey Forest and Ubud Palace are worth seeing, five minutes is enough—no need to spend too much time there. Instead, across the street from the palace is Ubud Market, which might be more interesting to explore. Rice terraces can be seen along the way, so visiting them is optional. The journey itself is the scenery, so focus on enjoying and relaxing.

## Accommodation and Itinerary Planning Tips:

1. He strongly advises against overloading your schedule when staying in a villa. Villas are meant for relaxation—enjoy the pool, soak up the sun, and sip a drink. There's no need to rush out early and return late. Some travelers even switch accommodations after just one or two nights, which is exhausting. Staying in a villa is about resting, appreciating the scenery, and enjoying leisurely moments without overexerting yourself.
2. The length of your Bali trip should determine your itinerary. Avoid rushing through attractions. Traffic here is often congested, and roads are narrow, making it nearly impossible to visit all popular spots.
3. He always travels independently, staying in villas without switching accommodations. A 10-day villa stay costs about the same as a 5-day, 4-night hotel stay in Taiwan. Independent travel offers more flexibility than packaged tours.
4. Don't worry too much about language barriers. A friendly smile is often the best communication tool, and your phone can also serve as a handy translator.
