## 🦓 Why Choose Bali Safari?

### Unique Animal Interaction Experiences

Unlike traditional zoos, Bali Safari offers various opportunities for visitors to interact closely with animals. From feeding zebras and elephants to having breakfast with lions, every experience is full of surprises.

### Family-Friendly Environment

The park features wide, flat pathways, making it stroller-friendly. The reasonable distances between areas ensure easy transitions, even for families with young children.

### Education Meets Entertainment

Through interactive learning and engaging animal shows, children can learn about wildlife while having fun, fostering respect and love for nature.

## Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

### 1. Is Bali Safari suitable for toddlers?

Yes, Bali Safari is very suitable for toddlers. The park design takes family needs into full consideration:

- **Flat, stroller-friendly paths**: Main park roads are suitable for strollers
- **Dedicated interaction zones**: Safe animal feeding areas where young children can interact with gentle animals under supervision
- **Kids' water play area**: Shallow water park designed specifically for young children
- **Ample rest areas**: Multiple shaded seating areas and rest stops for convenient breaks
- **Overall atmosphere**: Compared to Bali's other popular attractions, it's less crowded and more peaceful, making it ideal for young children's first wildlife experience.

### 2. My child is sensitive to noise. Are there quiet areas in the park?

Yes. Although animal shows can be loud, there are several relatively quiet areas in the park:

- **Shaded garden paths**: Scenic gardens located in the park's eastern section
- **Restaurant seating areas**: Quieter during non-meal times
- **Animal viewing platforms**: Some exhibits have seating for quiet observation
- **Alternative options**: If children are particularly sensitive to noise, consider skipping animal shows and focusing on quieter activities like Safari tours and feeding experiences.

### 3. Can I bring baby food into the park?

**Yes**, Bali Safari has a lenient policy regarding baby food:

- **Allowed items**: Baby formula, special meals for young children, food for specific dietary needs
- **Quantity limits**: Reasonable personal use amounts
- **Important reminders**:
- - Outside adult food is still prohibited
    - Keep food items in a clearly marked diaper bag or baby supplies bag
    - Inform staff about medical needs or special formula foods at the entrance
    - Simple children's meals are also available for purchase in the park as backup options

### 4. Are there shaded areas in the park suitable for naps or quiet rest?

Yes, there are multiple rest stations throughout the park:

- **Gazebos**: Scattered throughout main areas, some with seating
- **Food court surroundings**: Numerous shaded seating areas
- **Scenic rest areas**: Specially placed benches near elephant and zebra exhibits
- **Thoughtful suggestion**: Bring a light blanket or nursing cover to create a more comfortable resting environment for young children.

### 5. Can we leave and re-enter the park on the same day?

**No**. Bali Safari's ticket policy is:

- **Single Entry**: All tickets (including day passes in accommodation packages) are valid for single use only
- **No Re-entry**: Once you leave, you cannot re-enter even if the day is not over
- **Preparation Tips**:
- - Prepare all essentials for young children in advance: diapers, spare clothes, sufficient water, snacks, etc.
    - Accommodation guests can take advantage of the "Two-Day Unlimited Entry" benefit, allowing free travel between the park and hotel on check-in and check-out days

### 6. What if it rains on the day of our visit?

Bali Safari remains open in most weather conditions:

- **Light Rain**: The park operates as usual
- **Safari Tour**: Vehicles are covered, so tours continue in the rain
- **Animal Shows**: Most show venues have rain shelters and will proceed as scheduled
- **Rainy Day Tips**:
- - Bring lightweight raincoats or small umbrellas (some souvenir shops in the park also sell them)
    - Prioritize activities like Safari Journey, aquarium visits, and indoor dining
    - Avoid using outdoor water parks and rides in the rain
    - Utilize indoor spaces like food courts and souvenir shops as resting points
- **Extreme Weather**: In case of heavy rain or thunderstorms, some outdoor activities may be suspended. Please follow park announcements.

### 7. Stroller Rental Service

Strollers are available for rent at the park entrance (limited quantity, first-come-first-served), but bringing your own is usually more hygienic and convenient.

### 8. Nursing Rooms and Changing Facilities

Simple changing tables are available near the main rest areas and some restrooms. Nursing mothers are advised to bring a nursing cover or use quieter corners in restaurants for nursing.

### 9. Best Times to Visit

- **Morning Hours** (9:00-12:00): Animals are more active, and the weather is cooler
- **Avoid Midday Heat**: From 12:00-14:00, consider indoor activities or returning to the hotel for a break (for accommodation guests)
- **Afternoon Hours**: Ideal for watching animal shows and participating in feeding activities

### 10. Recommendations for Accommodation and Day Trips

- **Highly Recommended for Families with Young Children**: Stay overnight to fully utilize the convenience of two-day entry and mid-day breaks
- **Day Visitors**: Arrive at opening time and plan your route in advance to minimize back-and-forth travel
