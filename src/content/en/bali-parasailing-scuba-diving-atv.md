## 5-Day Bali Free Itinerary Guide

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_403bb19aa844de08fb2031b43665cbc0.jpg)

### June 19 - June 23: Just wrapped up a relaxing and enjoyable trip to Bali

Due to deciding on the departure date too late, I originally planned to join a tour group with a travel agency, but ended up only booking flights and hotels for a free-and-easy trip. However, for me, finding attractions, restaurants, and arranging the itinerary wasn’t too difficult. Plus, everyone in this group was very enthusiastic about sharing experiences, making me feel that the most important thing was to find a reliable private driver.

### Finding a Private Driver

After some research, I saw someone in the group recommend Mang Loco. He said I could call him 'Ou Ei,' which means 'Black Ei' in Taiwanese. The key point is, he speaks Chinese! Since he used to work in Taiwan, communicating in Chinese wasn’t a problem at all. Although English is usable in Bali, I often find it hard to understand English with an Indonesian accent, so being able to communicate directly in Chinese was incredibly convenient!

### Itinerary Planning and Communication

I first drafted a rough itinerary myself and then asked Ou Ei to help fine-tune it. He also kindly gave some suggestions on the schedule. Throughout the trip, communication was very smooth. Not only could he understand our casual chats, but he could also joke around with us—this is truly the benefit of hiring a Chinese-speaking driver! He also helped us solve some unexpected issues, like changing restaurant reservations, last-minute massage plans, and even haggling with us. ## Trip Insights and Recommendations

### **Beverage Choices**

In Bali, it's best to avoid trying drinks from non-supermarkets, large restaurants, or chain stores. During our trip, a friend drank a beverage at a local eatery and ended up with stomach issues for two days. While the local drinks may look tempting, it's safer to stick to reliable options to avoid getting sick during your travels. Additionally, carrying some stomach medicine can be helpful in case of emergencies.

> [Health Tips for Bali Travel: A Guide to Drinking Water and Diarrhea in Bali](/blog/2024-01-27-65b32e1ffd8978000170519b/)

### **Bring Extra Undergarments**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b0f8826462eff2a98579a54724fb9ffa.png)

Bali's humidity is very high, and if you're engaging in water activities, you'll likely need to change clothes twice a day. I initially thought I could wash and air-dry my clothes, but due to the heavy humidity, they don't dry completely. It's a good idea to bring quick-dry clothing, especially undergarments, to ensure you have clean and comfortable clothes every day.

### **Traffic in Kuta**

Traffic in Kuta is extremely congested. If you're staying in Kuta, make sure to allocate extra time for traffic delays. The roads are narrow, and there are many vehicles, leading to daily traffic jams. If your itinerary is tight, consider changing accommodations every two days to avoid spending too much time on the road in the evenings. Additionally, try to plan some walking or cycling activities to avoid the heavy traffic.

### Accommodation

During our Bali trip, we stayed at the Grand Istana Rama Hotel for all four nights. This resort is right next to the Beachwalk Shopping Center, so every day when we returned to the hotel, as long as the mall was still open, we went shopping XD.

Just across the street is Kuta Beach, and the area is bustling with nightlife. Although this isn't the newest hotel, its rooms are spacious and clean, with complete facilities. The staff are friendly and welcoming, making us feel right at home. Especially the seafood dinners and live band performances were fantastic, allowing us to enjoy some delightful evenings. Our stay here was wonderful, offering great value for money!

### **Currency Exchange**

There are many currency exchange places in Bali. You can ask your guide or driver to take you there, which is safer and more reliable. Since exchanging money is very convenient, there's no need to exchange too much at once. For this trip, because I had collected some public funds in advance, I only exchanged USD$200 for my five-day personal expenses, and I still had some left. It's advisable to carry some USD with you for emergencies, which can also reduce the number of times you need to exchange money locally.

### **Rafting Experience**

Before starting the rafting activity in Bali, you need to walk a long mountain path (downstairs) to reach the river. On the way back, you have to climb a slope with stairs. Although the return stairs are shorter, it can still be a bit challenging for those with poor physical fitness. If you're bringing elderly or children, make sure to assess their physical condition. However, the rafting process is really exciting and fun, making it an unforgettable experience, so I highly recommend trying it.
