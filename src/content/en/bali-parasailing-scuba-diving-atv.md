### June 19 - June 23: Just Finished a Relaxing and Enjoyable Bali Trip

Due to deciding on the departure date too late, I originally planned to join a tour group with a travel agency, but ended up only booking flights and hotels for a free-and-easy trip. However, for me, finding attractions, restaurants, and arranging the itinerary wasn't too difficult, especially with the enthusiastic sharing of experiences from everyone in this group. The most important thing was finding a reliable private driver.

### Finding a Private Driver

After some research, I saw someone recommending Mang Loco in the group. He said we could call him 'Ou Ei,' which means 'Blackie' in Taiwanese. The key point is, he speaks Chinese! Since he used to work in Taiwan, basic communication in Chinese isn't a problem. Although English can be used in Bali, I often have trouble understanding English with an Indonesian accent, so being able to communicate directly in Chinese is incredibly convenient!

### Itinerary Planning and Communication

I first drafted a rough itinerary, then asked Mang Loco to help refine it. He was very thoughtful and offered some great suggestions for the schedule. Throughout our trip in Bali, communication was smooth and effortless. Not only could he understand our casual conversations, but he also joked around with us—this is definitely the advantage of hiring a Chinese-speaking driver! He also helped us solve some unexpected issues, like changing restaurant reservations, arranging last-minute massages, and even haggling for us. Below is the itinerary we planned after referencing many guides from [小傑印尼](https://www.youtube.com/@j_indonesia) on YouTube.

## Trip Insights and Recommendations

### **Beverage Choices**

In Bali, it’s best to avoid drinks from non-supermarket, non-major restaurant, or non-chain establishments. During our trip, a friend drank a beverage from a local food stall and ended up with diarrhea for two days. While the local drinks might look tempting, it’s safer to stick to reliable options to avoid getting sick during your trip. Also, consider carrying some stomach medicine with you, just in case.

> [Bali Travel Health Tips: A Guide to Drinking Water and Diarrhea in Bali](/blog/bali-travel-health-tips/)

### **Bring Extra Underwear**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b0f8826462eff2a98579a54724fb9ffa.png)

Bali is very humid, and if you’re participating in water activities, you’ll likely need to change clothes twice a day. I initially thought I could wash and dry my clothes, but due to the high humidity, they didn’t dry completely. I recommend bringing quick-dry clothing, especially underwear, to ensure you have clean and comfortable clothes every day.

### **Kuta Area Transportation**

Traffic in Kuta is notoriously congested. If you're staying in Kuta, make sure to allocate extra time for potential traffic jams. The roads are narrow and crowded with vehicles, leading to daily gridlock. If your itinerary is tight, consider changing accommodations every two days to avoid spending too much time on the road during evening returns. Additionally, try to plan activities that involve walking or cycling to bypass the heavy traffic.

### Accommodation

During our Bali trip, we stayed at the Grand Istana Rama Hotel for all four nights. The hotel is conveniently located next to the Beachwalk Shopping Center, so whenever we returned to the hotel before it closed, we would go shopping XD

https://youtu.be/IReL1ztxL7c

Just across the street lies Kuta Beach, with its vibrant nightlife nearby. Although the hotel isn't the newest, its rooms are spacious, clean, and well-equipped. The staff are friendly and welcoming, making you feel right at home. The seafood dinners and live band performances were particularly enjoyable, adding to our pleasant evenings. Our stay here was delightful, offering excellent value for money!

### Currency Exchange

There are many currency exchange places in Bali. It's safer and more reliable to have your guide or driver take you to one. Since exchanging money is very convenient, there's no need to exchange too much at once. For this trip, I had collected a public fund beforehand, so I only exchanged USD$200 for my five-day personal expenses, and I still had some left. It's advisable to carry some USD with you for emergencies, which can also reduce the number of times you need to exchange money locally.

> [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide: What currency is used in Indonesia/Bali? How to exchange money most cost-effectively? Where is the best place to exchange money?](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)

### **White Water Rafting Experience**

Before starting your white water rafting adventure in Bali, you'll need to descend a long mountain path (down stairs) to reach the river. On the way back, you'll have to climb up a slope with stairs. Although the return stairs are shorter, it can still be a bit challenging for those who aren't in great physical shape. If you're bringing elderly family members or children, make sure to assess their physical condition beforehand. However, the rafting process itself is incredibly thrilling and fun, making it a truly unforgettable experience. Highly recommended!
