### June 19 - June 23, just finished a relaxing and enjoyable Bali trip

Due to deciding on the departure date too late, I originally planned to join a tour group with a travel agency, but ended up only booking flights and hotels for a free-and-easy trip. However, for me, finding attractions, restaurants, and arranging the itinerary wasn't too difficult, plus everyone in this group was very enthusiastic about sharing experiences, making me feel that the most important thing was to find a reliable private car driver.

### Finding a Private Car Driver

After some research, I saw someone recommending Mang Loco in the group, he said we could call him 'Ou Ei', which means 'Black Ei' in Taiwanese. The key point is, he speaks Chinese! Because he used to work in Taiwan, so basically communicating in Chinese is not a problem. Although English can be used in Bali, I often couldn't quite understand the English with Indonesian accents, so being able to communicate directly in Chinese is really convenient!

## Trip Insights and Recommendations

### **Drink Choices**

In Bali, it's best to avoid trying drinks from non-supermarket, non-major restaurant, or non-chain store vendors. During our trip, a friend had a drink at a local food stall and ended up with stomach issues for two days. While local drinks may look tempting, it's safer to stick to reliable options to avoid getting sick during your trip. Additionally, carrying some stomach medicine is a good idea, just in case.

> [Bali Travel Health Tips: A Guide to Drinking Water and Diarrhea in Bali](/blog/bali-travel-health-tips/)

### **Bring Extra Underwear**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b0f8826462eff2a98579a54724fb9ffa.png)

Bali is extremely humid, and if you're engaging in water activities, you'll likely need to change clothes twice a day. I initially thought I could wash and dry clothes easily, but due to the heavy humidity, clothes don't dry completely. It's advisable to bring quick-dry clothing, especially underwear, to ensure you have clean and comfortable clothes every day.

### **Traffic in Kuta**

Traffic in Kuta is notoriously congested. If you're staying in Kuta, make sure to allocate extra time for traffic delays. The roads are narrow, and there are many vehicles, leading to daily traffic jams. If your itinerary is tight, consider changing accommodations every two days to avoid spending too much time on the road in the evenings. Also, try to include walking or cycling activities to bypass the heavy traffic.

### Accommodation

During this trip to Bali, we stayed at the Grand Istana Rama Hotel for all four nights. This resort is right next to the Beachwalk Shopping Center, so every day when we returned to the hotel, as long as the mall was still open, we went shopping XD.

Just across the street is Kuta Beach, and the area is bustling with nightlife. Although this isn’t the newest hotel, the rooms are spacious and clean, with all the necessary amenities. The staff are friendly and welcoming, making us feel right at home. Especially the seafood dinners and live band performances were fantastic, allowing us to enjoy some wonderful evenings. Our stay here was truly delightful, and the value for money was outstanding!

### **Currency Exchange**

There are many places to exchange money in Bali. You can ask your guide or driver to take you to a reliable spot for a safer experience. Since exchanging money is very convenient, there’s no need to exchange too much at once. For this trip, I had already collected some shared funds, so I only exchanged $200 for my five-day personal expenses, and I still had some left. It’s recommended to carry some USD with you for emergencies, which can also reduce the number of times you need to exchange money locally.

> [Comprehensive Guide to Currency Exchange in Indonesia/Bali: What Currency is Used? How to Get the Best Exchange Rate? Where to Exchange Money?](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)

### **Rafting Experience**

Before starting the rafting activity in Bali, you’ll need to walk down a long mountain path (stairs) to reach the river. On the way back, there’s a shorter uphill climb, but it can still be a bit challenging for those with limited stamina. If you’re traveling with elderly family members or children, make sure to assess their physical condition beforehand. However, the rafting itself is incredibly thrilling and fun, making it an unforgettable experience. I highly recommend giving it a try!
