### June 19 - June 23, Just returned from a relaxing and enjoyable trip to Bali

Due to deciding on the departure date too late, I initially planned to join a tour group with a travel agency, but ended up only booking flights and hotels for a free-and-easy trip. However, for me, finding attractions, restaurants, and arranging the itinerary wasn't too difficult, especially with the enthusiastic sharing of experiences from everyone in this group. The most important thing, I felt, was to find a reliable private driver.

### Finding a Private Driver

After some research, I found someone recommended in the group named Mang Loco, who said we could call him 'Ou Ei,' which means 'Blackie' in Taiwanese. The key point is, he speaks Chinese! Having worked in Taiwan before, he can basically communicate in Chinese without any problems. Although English is usable in Bali, I often find it hard to understand English with an Indonesian accent, so being able to communicate directly in Chinese is incredibly convenient!

## Trip Insights and Recommendations

### **Beverage Choices**

In Bali, it's best to avoid trying drinks from non-supermarket, non-major restaurant, or non-chain store vendors. During this trip, one of our friends had a drink at a local eatery and ended up with stomach issues for two days. While the local beverages might look tempting, it's safer to stick to reliable options to avoid getting sick during your trip. Also, consider carrying some stomach medicine just in case.

> [Bali Travel Health Tips: Guide to Drinking Water and Diarrhea in Bali](/blog/bali-travel-health-tips/)

### **Bring Extra Underwear**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b0f8826462eff2a98579a54724fb9ffa.png)

Bali is extremely humid, and if you're engaging in water activities, you might need to change clothes twice a day. I initially thought I could wash and air-dry my clothes, but due to the high humidity, they don't dry completely. It's a good idea to bring quick-dry clothing, especially underwear, to ensure you have clean and comfortable clothes every day.

### **Kuta Area Traffic**

Traffic in the Kuta area is notoriously congested. If you're staying in Kuta, make sure to allocate extra time for traffic delays. The roads are narrow, and there are many vehicles, leading to daily traffic jams. If your itinerary is tight, consider changing accommodations every two days to avoid spending too much time on the road in the evenings. Additionally, try to plan walking or cycling activities to bypass the heavy traffic.

### Accommodation

During our Bali trip, we stayed at the Grand Istana Rama Hotel for four nights. This resort is right next to the Beachwalk Shopping Center, so every day when we returned to the hotel, as long as the mall was still open, we went shopping XD.

Just across the street is Kuta Beach, and the area is bustling with nightlife. Although this isn't the newest hotel, the rooms are spacious and clean, and the facilities are well-equipped. The staff are friendly and welcoming, making us feel right at home. Especially the seafood dinners and live band performances were fantastic, allowing us to enjoy some delightful evenings. Our stay here was wonderful, and the value for money was exceptional!

### **Currency Exchange**

There are many currency exchange places in Bali. You can ask your guide or driver to take you there, which is safer and more reliable. Since exchanging money is very convenient, there's no need to exchange too much at once. For this trip, because I had collected some group funds in advance, I only exchanged USD$200 for my five-day personal expenses, and I still had some left. It's advisable to carry some USD with you for emergencies, which can also reduce the number of times you need to exchange money locally.

### **Rafting Experience**

Before starting the rafting activity in Bali, you need to walk a long mountain path (downstairs) to reach the river. On the way back, you have to climb up a slope with stairs. Although the return stairs are shorter, it can still be a bit challenging for those who are not physically fit. If you're bringing elderly or children, make sure to assess their physical condition. However, the rafting process is truly thrilling and fun, making it an unforgettable experience, so I highly recommend trying it.
