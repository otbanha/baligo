Bali's capital city Denpasar has recently been named one of the [Top 100 Cities in the World for 2025](https://www.forbes.com/sites/laurabegleybloom/2025/02/27/the-100-best-cities-in-the-world-to-visit-in-2025-according-to-a-new-report/), ranking 94th. This ancient city serves not only as Bali's cultural hub but also boasts rich historical heritage and a vibrant modern art scene, making it another excellent choice for travelers exploring Bali.

## The Unique Charm of Denpasar

Denpasar is not only the administrative center of Bali but also a vibrant cultural melting pot. Here, visitors can immerse themselves in the authentic essence of Bali through traditional markets, ancient temples, modern art exhibitions, and music events. Compared to other popular tourist destinations in Bali, Denpasar sees fewer tourists, offering a more localized experience away from the hustle and bustle. This allows travelers to truly appreciate the unique charm of this city.

## Global Top 100 Cities Ranking

According to Euromonitor International's rankings, Denpasar has made its debut on the list of the world's top 100 cities, excelling in various indicators such as tourism performance, cultural appeal, and sustainability. The 2025 rankings are based on 2024 data, and Denpasar's success is attributed not only to its rich cultural heritage but also to the local government's proactive efforts in promoting tourism.

## Must-Visit Attractions in Denpasar

### **Badung Market**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774614720760-badung_market.jpg)

Located in the city center, Badung Market is one of Bali's largest and oldest traditional markets. Not only is it a daily shopping destination for locals, but it's also an excellent place for visitors to experience the authentic local culture of Bali. Across from the market stands Pura Desa Temple, one of the most significant Hindu temples on the island.

### **Kumbasari Art Market**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774614765807-kumbasari.jpg)

Just across the street from Badung Market, Kumbasari Market is the perfect place to shop for traditional Balinese handicrafts and souvenirs. It's also where locals purchase items for religious ceremonies, creating a rich cultural atmosphere.

### **Bajra Sandhi Monument**

This monument is located in Puputan Renon Park, Denpasar, and was built to commemorate the heroic deeds of the Balinese people during their struggle for independence. Visitors can climb up to the viewing platform to enjoy panoramic views of the entire city.

### **Bali Museum (Museum Bali)**

The museum houses an extensive collection of Bali's historical artifacts, traditional costumes, and ancient manuscripts, making it an excellent place to delve into the island's culture and history.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f66647194cd9d3240f658a876a9ce7b7.jpg)

### **Pura Jagatnatha Temple**

This is the most important Hindu temple in Denpasar, dedicated to Sang Hyang Widhi Wasa, the supreme deity of Balinese Hinduism. The temple's architecture is unique and imbued with a profound religious atmosphere.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_36179fe2bb08dd9d6f7150e83ae45bc8.jpg)

### **Taman Tukad Korea Riverside Park**

https://youtu.be/gEcE2e4TFaM

As part of Bali's river revitalization project, Taman Tukad Korea Riverside Park has emerged as a new attraction in Denpasar. Featuring lush walking paths, relaxation areas, children's playgrounds, and an open-air performance venue, it's become a favorite spot for both locals and visitors to unwind.

### **Pasar Kreneng Night Market**

https://youtu.be/FphhiUzB7gM

As Bali's largest night market, Pasar Kreneng is the perfect place to experience local nightlife. It brings together a variety of food stalls offering everything from traditional Balinese cuisine to specialties from across Indonesia. The lively atmosphere and diverse culinary options allow visitors to fully immerse themselves in Bali's nighttime charm.

### Taman Ayun Temple: Bali's Serene Sanctuary

Located about 18 kilometers from Denpasar, Taman Ayun Water Temple was built in 1634 and once served as the royal temple of the Mengwi Kingdom. Renowned for its unique architectural style and serene ambiance, this temple was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2012.

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dksxqobud/image/upload/v1774091103/MixCollage-08-Mar-2025-11-02-AM-4254_wxvvry.jpg)

**Highlight Features**

1. **Moat and Gardens**
   The temple is surrounded by a wide moat, symbolizing sacredness and separation, complemented by meticulously designed gardens that create a serene atmosphere.
2. **Multi-tiered Meru Towers**
   The multi-tiered Meru towers within the temple represent Mount Meru, the sacred mountain in Hinduism, and are a favorite among photography enthusiasts, especially when set against a clear blue sky.
3. **Traditional Ceremonies**
   Visitors have the opportunity to witness locals performing traditional ceremonies, offering a deeper understanding of Bali's religious culture.

Taman Ayun Temple, a harmonious blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage, is a must-visit destination for exploring Bali's history and spiritual beliefs.

### Living World Denpasar

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774615058614-living_world_denpasar.jpg)

Living World Denpasar is Bali's newest shopping mall in Denpasar, combining fashion, entertainment, and dining into a popular destination for both locals and tourists. It features a mix of international and local brand stores, a modern cinema, and a diverse range of dining options, from authentic Balinese cuisine to international dishes. The mall's design is spacious and bright, incorporating traditional Balinese elements to create a comfortable shopping experience. Whether you're shopping, enjoying a meal, or spending leisure time with family and friends, Living World Denpasar is a must-visit modern attraction in Denpasar!

## Denpasar Cuisine

### Kaneshiro Resto: An All-You-Can-Eat Japanese Culinary Paradise in Denpasar

Kaneshiro Resto is a renowned Japanese all-you-can-eat hotpot restaurant in Denpasar, celebrated for its high-quality Japanese cuisine and extensive menu options that delight both locals and travelers alike. The restaurant offers a variety of classic Japanese dishes, including fresh sushi, sashimi, tempura, and grilled meats, all prepared with carefully selected ingredients to ensure exceptional taste and quality. With its comfortable ambiance and attentive service, Kaneshiro Resto is the perfect spot for sharing a delightful meal with family and friends. Whether you're a Japanese cuisine enthusiast or looking to explore diverse culinary experiences, Kaneshiro Resto promises to satisfy your palate and is a must-visit dining destination in Denpasar!

## Travel Plans for 2025

Ni Luh Putu Riyastiti, the Director of Denpasar Tourism, stated that the city aims to attract 2.1 million visitors by 2025 (for reference: Bali receives over 6 million tourists annually). To achieve this goal, the local government will actively promote Denpasar's tourism resources through social media, online marketing, and collaborations with travel industry partners. Additionally, Denpasar plans to enhance the quality of its tourist attractions and organize diverse cultural events to further boost the city's appeal.

## Bali Arts Festival

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2ab81ce50be7b72a8e74be0512b7aef.png)

The Bali Arts Festival, held annually from mid-June to mid-July, is one of Denpasar's most significant cultural events. Renowned for its grand opening parade featuring thousands of dancers and performers, the festival showcases Bali's traditional arts, music, and dance. The main venue, Taman Werdhi Budaya Arts Center located in downtown Denpasar, offers visitors an unmissable cultural experience.
