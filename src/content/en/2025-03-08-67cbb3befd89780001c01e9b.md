Denpasar, the capital of Bali, has recently been [listed among the world's top 100 cities for 2025](https://www.forbes.com/sites/laurabegleybloom/2025/02/27/the-100-best-cities-in-the-world-to-visit-in-2025-according-to-a-new-report/), ranking 94th. This ancient city is not only the cultural center of Bali but also features a wealth of historical heritage and a thriving modern art atmosphere, offering travelers another fantastic option for exploring Bali.

## The Unique Charm of Denpasar

Denpasar is not just the administrative center of Bali but also a dynamic cultural melting pot. With its traditional markets, ancient temples, modern art exhibitions, and music events, visitors can deeply experience the authentic side of Bali. Compared to other popular tourist spots on the island, Denpasar sees fewer tourists, providing a more localized experience away from the crowds, allowing travelers to truly appreciate the city's unique allure.

## The Global Top 100 Cities Ranking

According to [Euromonitor International's ranking](https://www.forbes.com/sites/laurabegleybloom/2025/02/27/the-100-best-cities-in-the-world-to-visit-in-2025-according-to-a-new-report/), Denpasar has made its debut on the list of the world's top 100 cities, excelling in metrics such as tourism performance, cultural appeal, and sustainability. The 2025 ranking is based on 2024 data, and Denpasar's success is attributed not only to its rich cultural heritage but also to the local government's proactive efforts in promoting tourism.

## Must-Visit Attractions in Denpasar

### **Badung Market**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774614720760-badung_market.jpg)

Located in the city center, Badung Market is one of the largest and oldest traditional markets in Bali. It is not only a daily shopping hub for locals but also an excellent place for visitors to experience the island's local culture. Across from the market stands Pura Desa, one of Bali's most important Hindu temples.

### **Kumbasari Art Market**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774614765807-kumbasari.jpg)

Just a street away from Badung Market, Kumbasari Market is the best place to buy traditional Balinese handicrafts and souvenirs. It's also where locals shop for religious ceremony supplies, filled with a strong cultural atmosphere.

### **Bajra Sandhi Monument**

Located in Puputan Renon Park in Denpasar, this monument was built to commemorate the bravery of the Balinese people during their struggle for independence. Visitors can climb to the viewing platform for a panoramic view of the city.

### **Bali Museum**

The museum houses a vast collection of Balinese historical artifacts, traditional costumes, and ancient manuscripts, making it an excellent place to learn about Balinese culture and history.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f66647194cd9d3240f658a876a9ce7b7.jpg)

### **Pura Jagatnatha Temple**

This is Denpasar's most important Hindu temple, dedicated to Sang Hyang Widhi Wasa, the supreme deity of Balinese Hinduism. The temple's unique architecture exudes a profound religious atmosphere.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_36179fe2bb08dd9d6f7150e83ae45bc8.jpg)

### **Taman Tukad Korea Riverside Park**

https://youtu.be/gEcE2e4TFaM

As part of Bali's river revitalization project, Taman Tukad Korea Riverside Park is an emerging attraction in Denpasar. With lush walking paths, rest areas, children's playgrounds, and open-air performance spaces, it's a great spot for both locals and tourists to relax and unwind.

### **Pasar Kreneng Night Market**

https://youtu.be/FphhiUzB7gM

As Bali's largest night market, Pasar Kreneng is the perfect place to experience local nightlife. It features a wide variety of food stalls, offering everything from traditional Balinese cuisine to specialties from across Indonesia. The lively atmosphere and diverse culinary options allow visitors to deeply immerse themselves in Bali's nighttime charm.

### Taman Ayun Temple: Bali's Serene Sanctuary

Located about 18 kilometers from Denpasar, Taman Ayun Water Temple was built in 1634 and served as the royal temple of the Mengwi Kingdom. Known for its unique architectural style and tranquil surroundings, it was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2012.

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dksxqobud/image/upload/v1774091103/MixCollage-08-Mar-2025-11-02-AM-4254_wxvvry.jpg)

**Highlights**

1. **Moat and Gardens**
   The temple is surrounded by a wide moat, symbolizing sanctity and separation, complemented by meticulously designed gardens that create a serene atmosphere.
2. **Multi-tiered Meru Towers**
   The multi-tiered Meru towers within the temple represent Mount Meru, the sacred mountain in Hinduism, and are a favorite among photography enthusiasts, especially when set against a clear blue sky.
3. **Traditional Ceremonies**
   Visitors have the opportunity to witness locals performing traditional ceremonies, offering a deeper understanding of Bali's religious culture.

Taman Ayun Water Temple combines natural beauty with cultural richness, making it a must-visit destination for exploring Bali's history and spirituality.

### Living World Denpasar

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-03/1774615058614-living_world_denpasar.jpg)

Living World Denpasar is Bali's newest shopping mall in Denpasar, blending fashion, entertainment, and dining into a popular spot for both locals and tourists. It features international and local brand stores, a modern cinema, and a diverse range of dining options, from authentic Balinese dishes to international cuisine. The mall's spacious and bright design incorporates traditional Balinese elements, providing a comfortable shopping experience. Whether you're shopping, enjoying a meal, or spending leisure time with family and friends, Living World Denpasar is a modern attraction in Denpasar not to be missed!

## Denpasar Cuisine

### Kaneshiro Resto: Denpasar's Japanese All-You-Can-Eat Paradise

Kaneshiro Resto is a renowned Japanese all-you-can-eat hotpot restaurant in Denpasar, celebrated for its high-quality Japanese cuisine and extensive menu options. It offers a variety of classic Japanese dishes such as fresh sushi, sashimi, tempura, and grilled meats, all made with carefully selected ingredients to ensure deliciousness and quality. The restaurant provides a comfortable environment and attentive service, making it perfect for sharing a meal with family and friends. Whether you're a Japanese cuisine enthusiast or looking to try a diverse culinary experience, Kaneshiro Resto will satisfy your taste buds and is a must-visit dining destination in Denpasar!

## Travel Plans for 2025

Ni Luh Putu Riyastiti, the head of Denpasar's tourism bureau, stated that Denpasar aims to attract 2.1 million tourists in 2025 (for reference, Bali receives over 6 million tourists annually). To achieve this goal, the local government will actively promote Denpasar's tourism resources through social media, online campaigns, and collaborations with tourism operators. Additionally, Denpasar plans to enhance the quality of its tourist attractions and host diverse cultural events to further boost the city's appeal.

## Bali Arts Festival

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e2ab81ce50be7b72a8e74be0512b7aef.png)

The Bali Arts Festival, held annually from mid-June to mid-July, is one of Denpasar's most significant cultural events. The festival is famous for its grand opening parade, featuring thousands of dancers and performers showcasing Bali's traditional arts, music, and dance. The main venue, Taman Werdhi Budaya Art Center, located in the heart of Denpasar, is a cultural experience not to be missed by visitors.
