## How to Use Gojek & Grab

When traveling in **Bali**, Gojek and Grab are two essential 'national apps.' Their features and interfaces are extremely similar, like two different brand versions of Uber.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3735af38f1bb9994a7ec1ec8fafe01e.png)

Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you get started quickly:

**Step 1: Register Before Your Trip (Key!)**

Both apps require an **SMS verification code (OTP)** to complete registration.

- **Tip:** Download and register with your Taiwan phone number before your trip.
- **Benefit:** Avoid getting stuck in **Bali** due to issues with local SIM cards not receiving SMS or unstable internet.

**Step 2: Link Your Credit Card (Cashless)**

While cash is accepted, it's highly recommended to link a credit card to avoid the hassle of dealing with small Indonesian rupiah change.

- **Grab:** User-friendly interface with strong international credit card support, even offering a Chinese language option.
- **Gojek:** A local Indonesian brand; if your credit card doesn't work, consider topping up GoPay at local convenience stores like Alfamart.

**Step 3: Ride-Hailing Process (Step-by-Step)**

Regardless of the app, the operation logic is essentially the same:

1. **Select Service:**

    - Bike: Ideal for solo travelers, those in a hurry (during traffic jams), or short distances. Called GoRide on Gojek and GrabBike on Grab.
    - Car: Suitable for groups, those with luggage, or those wanting air conditioning. Called GoCar on Gojek and GrabCar on Grab.

2. **Set Destination (Where to):** Enter the location name or drop a pin directly on the map.
3. **Confirm Pick-up Point:** The system will auto-locate, but it's advisable to manually check to ensure the driver can find you.
4. **Check Price:** The system will display the total fare (fixed price, no meter or haggling).
5. **Click Order/Book:** The system will match you with a driver, showing the driver's name, license plate, and location.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_74b7470a20e1026529e08d0e3f83681e.png)

**Step 4: Communicating with the Driver**

- **Built-in Chat:** Both apps have built-in chat rooms where you can send messages or photos to indicate your exact location.
- **Translation Feature:** Grab has an automatic translation feature, converting Indonesian messages from drivers into English/Chinese; Gojek may require basic English communication.
- **Common Pick-up Phrases:**

1. At the main gate: Di pintu utama
2. At XX convenient store: Di mini mart XX
3. At XX store: Di toko XX
4. Pick me up at number XX: jemput saya di nomor XX

**Step 5: Arrival and Rating**

- After getting out of the car, if you've already linked your credit card, you can simply leave.
- **Tips:** If you feel the driver provided excellent service (e.g., helping with heavy luggage or navigating around severe traffic), you can leave a small tip of 5,000–10,000 IDR on the app as a gesture of appreciation.

## Usage Restrictions

In certain areas of Bali, there are still signs opposing ride-hailing apps, primarily aimed at protecting the livelihoods of local traditional taxi and private car drivers.

To help you quickly understand, I’ve compiled the relevant information below:

### 📍 Main Restricted Areas and Situations

- Common restricted locations: Popular tourist spots like Kuta, Canggu, Ubud, Sanur, and parts of Kintamani have reportedly had bans in place since 2022.
- Controversial focus: Around Lake Batur and Songan Village, ride-hailing apps have been accused of driving down local drivers' rates, sparking strong opposition from local tourism transport associations.
- Recent policies: According to early 2025 reports, Bali authorities have introduced new regulations requiring ride-hailing drivers to hold a Bali ID (KTP) and use local license plates to protect local drivers' rights.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_11b747be41152ceaf2c885a63ca4e272.jpg)

### 🚗 Practical Impact and Recommendations for Tourists

While restrictions do exist, their overall impact on tourists is limited. You can still travel smoothly by following these tips:

1. Main areas still accessible: At airports, most hotels, and regular streets, you can still conveniently use ride-hailing apps like Grab and Gojek. They offer transparent pricing and are a practical choice for short-distance travel.
2. Avoid marked locations: If you see signs at tourist spots, temples, or restaurant entrances indicating 'No Online Taxi Pickup/Drop-off,' avoid hailing or boarding rides there (see image above).
3. Handling tips: In such situations, walk about 5-10 minutes away from the area to a nearby main road or alley before using the app to book a ride. This usually ensures a smooth booking and more reasonable pricing.
4. Alternative option: For day-long cross-regional sightseeing (e.g., from the south to Ubud or the volcanoes), booking a private car is a more common, comfortable, and hassle-free choice. Drivers often double as guides.

## ⚠️ Important Reminder

· Respect regulations: Always adhere to on-site signage. Forcing a pickup in prohibited areas may lead to disputes with local drivers.
· Safety first: If hailing a cab on the street, always choose reputable 'Bluebird Taxis' and confirm that the driver uses the meter or agrees on a fixed price to avoid being overcharged.

In summary, online ride-hailing remains a useful tool in most parts of Bali, as long as you avoid popular spots with clear prohibitions. Planning your transportation in advance will make your journey smoother.
