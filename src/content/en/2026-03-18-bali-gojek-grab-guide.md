## How to Use Gojek / Grab

When traveling in **Bali**, Gojek and Grab are two must-have 'national apps.' Their features and interfaces are extremely similar, much like two different brand versions of Uber.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3735af38f1bb9994a7ec1ec8fafe01e.png)

Below is a step-by-step guide to help you get started quickly:

**Step 1: Register Before Departure (Key!)**

Both apps require an **SMS verification code (OTP)** to complete registration.

- **Recommendation:** Download and register with your Taiwan phone number before departure.
- **Benefit:** Avoid getting stuck in **Bali** due to local SIM cards not receiving SMS or unstable internet connections.

**Step 2: Link a Credit Card (Cashless)**

While cash payment is possible, it is highly recommended to link a credit card to avoid the hassle of dealing with small Indonesian Rupiah change.

- **Grab:** The interface is user-friendly, supports international credit cards well, and even offers a Chinese interface.
- **Gojek:** As an Indonesian brand, if your credit card doesn't work, you can top up GoPay at local convenience stores like Alfamart.

**Step Three: Ride-Hailing Process (Step-by-Step)**

Regardless of which app you use, the operation logic is quite similar:

1. **Choose Service:**

    - Motorcycle (Bike): Suitable for solo travelers, those in a hurry (during traffic jams), and short distances. Called GoRide on Gojek and GrabBike on Grab.
    - Car: Suitable for groups, those with luggage, or those who want air conditioning. Called GoCar on Gojek and GrabCar on Grab.

2. **Set Destination (Where to):** Enter the location name or drop a pin directly on the map.
3. **Confirm Pick-up Point:** The system will automatically locate you, but it's recommended to manually check to ensure the driver can find you.
4. **Check Price:** The system will display the total amount (fixed price, no meter or haggling).
5. **Click Order/Book:** The system will match you with a driver, and the screen will show the driver's name, license plate, and location.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_74b7470a20e1026529e08d0e3f83681e.png)

**Step Four: Communicating with the Driver**

- **Built-in Chat:** Both apps have a built-in chat room where you can send messages or photos to indicate your exact location.
- **Translation Feature:** Grab has built-in auto-translation, converting Indonesian messages from the driver into English/Chinese; Gojek may require simple English communication.
- **Common Pick-up Phrases:**
1. At the main gate: Di pintu utama
2. At XX convenient store: Di mini mart XX
3. At XX store: Di toko XX
4. Pick me up at number XX: jemput saya di nomor XX

**Step Five: Arrival and Rating**

**第五步：抵達與評分**

- After exiting the vehicle, if your credit card is already linked, you can simply leave.
- **Tips:** If you feel the driver provided excellent service (e.g., helping with heavy luggage or navigating around severe traffic), you can show your appreciation by leaving a small tip of 5,000–10,000 IDR through the app.

## Usage Restrictions

In certain areas of Bali, there are still signs opposing ride-hailing services, primarily aimed at protecting the livelihoods of local traditional taxi and private car drivers.

To help you quickly understand, I’ve compiled the relevant information below:

### 📍 Main Restricted Areas and Situations
· Common restricted locations: Tourist hotspots such as Kuta, Canggu, Ubud, Sanur, and parts of Kintamani have been reported to have bans since 2022.
· Controversial points: Around Lake Batur and Songan Village, ride-hailing services are accused of undercutting local drivers’ rates, sparking strong opposition from local tourism transport associations.
· Recent policy: According to early 2025 reports, Bali authorities have introduced new regulations requiring ride-hailing drivers to hold a Bali ID (KTP) and use local license plates to protect local drivers’ rights.

### 🚗 Practical Impact and Recommendations for Tourists
While restrictions do exist, their overall impact on tourists is limited. You can navigate smoothly by following these tips:
1. Main areas remain accessible: At airports, most hotels, and regular streets, you can still use ride-hailing apps like Grab and Gojek. They offer transparent pricing and are convenient for short trips.
2. Avoid marked locations: If you see signs like 'No Online Taxi Pickup/Drop-off' at tourist spots, temples, or restaurant entrances, avoid hailing or boarding rides there.
3. Workaround: In such situations, walk about 5-10 minutes away from the area to a nearby main road or alley before using the app to hail a ride. This usually works smoothly and offers fairer pricing.
4. Alternative option: For day trips across regions (e.g., from the south to Ubud or volcanoes), booking a private car is a more common, comfortable, and dispute-free choice. Drivers often double as guides.

限制確實存在，但對遊客的整體影響是有限的，你可以通過以下方式順利出行：

1. 主要區域仍可使用：在機場、大多數酒店和普通街道，你依然可以正常使用 Grab 和 Gojek 這兩款叫車APP。它們價格透明，是短途移動的方便選擇。
2. 避開標誌地點：如果在旅遊景點、廟宇或餐廳門口看到類似“禁止網約車 (Online Taxi) 停靠/接客”的標誌，應避免在該處叫車或上車(上圖)。
3. 應對技巧：遇到上述情況，可以步行離開該區域約5-10分鐘，到附近的主幹道或小巷再使用APP叫車，這樣通常能順利叫到車，且價格更合理。
4. 替代方案：對於一天的跨區域觀光（例如從南部前往烏布或火山），預訂包車是更普遍、舒適且無爭議的選擇，司機通常還能兼任嚮導。
