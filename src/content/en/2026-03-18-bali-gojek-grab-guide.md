## How to Use Gojek/Grab

When traveling in **Bali**, Gojek and Grab are two must-have 'national apps.' Their features and interfaces are remarkably similar, akin to two different brand versions of Uber.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a3735af38f1bb9994a7ec1ec8fafe01e.png)

Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you get started quickly:

**Step 1: Register Before Your Trip (Key!)**

Both apps require an **SMS verification code (OTP)** to complete registration.

- **Tip:** Download the apps and register using your Taiwanese phone number before leaving (or before your trip).
- **Benefit:** Avoid getting stuck in **Bali** due to issues like local SIM cards not receiving SMS or unstable internet.

**Step 2: Link Your Credit Card (Cashless)**

While cash payment is an option, it’s highly recommended to link your credit card to avoid the hassle of handling small amounts of Indonesian Rupiah change.

- **Grab:** User-friendly interface with excellent international credit card support, even offering a Chinese language option.
- **Gojek:** A local Indonesian brand. If your credit card doesn't work, consider topping up GoPay at local convenience stores like Alfamart.

**Step 3: Ride-Hailing Process (Step-by-Step)**

Regardless of the app, the operational logic is essentially the same:

1. **Select Service:**

    - **Bike:** Ideal for solo travelers, those in a hurry (during traffic jams), or short distances. Gojek calls it GoRide, Grab calls it GrabBike.
    - **Car:** Suitable for groups, those with luggage, or anyone wanting air conditioning. Gojek calls it GoCar, Grab calls it GrabCar.

2. **Set Destination (Where to):** Enter the location name or drop a pin directly on the map.
3. **Confirm Pick-up Point:** The app will automatically locate you, but it's recommended to manually check to ensure the driver can find you.
4. **Check Price:** The app will display the total fare (fixed price, no meter or haggling).
5. **Click Order/Book:** The app will match you with a driver, displaying their name, license plate, and location.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_74b7470a20e1026529e08d0e3f83681e.png)

**Step 4: Communicating with the Driver**

- **Built-in Chat:** Both apps have a built-in chat feature where you can send messages or photos to indicate your exact location.
- **Translation Feature:** Grab has automatic translation, converting Indonesian messages from the driver into English/Chinese. Gojek may require basic English communication.
- **Common Pick-up Phrases:**

1. At the main gate: Di pintu utama
2. At XX convenient store: Di mini mart XX
3. At XX store: Di toko XX
4. Pick me up at number XX: jemput saya di nomor XX

**Step 5: Arrival and Rating**

- After exiting the vehicle, if your credit card is already linked, you can simply leave.
- **Tipping:** If you feel the driver provided excellent service (such as helping with heavy luggage or navigating around severe traffic), you can show your appreciation by leaving a small tip of 5,000–10,000 IDR through the app.

## Usage Restrictions

In certain areas of Bali, signs opposing ride-hailing services can still be seen, primarily aimed at protecting the livelihoods of local traditional taxi and charter drivers.

To help you quickly understand, I've compiled the relevant information below:

### 📍 Main Restricted Areas and Situations
· Common restricted locations: Popular tourist spots like Kuta, Canggu, Ubud, Sanur, and parts of Kintamani have reportedly had related bans since 2022.
· Controversial focus: Around Lake Batur and Songan Village, ride-hailing services are accused of undercutting local drivers' rates, sparking strong opposition from local tourism transport associations.
· Recent policy: According to early 2025 reports, Bali authorities have introduced new regulations requiring ride-hailing drivers to possess a Bali ID card (KTP) and use local license plates to protect local drivers' rights.

### 🚗 Practical Impact and Recommendations for Tourists
While restrictions do exist, their overall impact on tourists is limited. You can navigate smoothly by following these tips:
1. Main areas still accessible: You can still use Grab and Gojek apps normally at the airport, most hotels, and regular streets. They offer transparent pricing and are convenient for short trips.
2. Avoid marked locations: If you see signs like 'No Online Taxi Pickup/Drop-off' at tourist attractions, temples, or restaurant entrances, avoid hailing or boarding rides there (see image above).
3. Workaround technique: In such situations, walk about 5-10 minutes away from the area to nearby main roads or alleys before using the app to hail a ride. This usually works smoothly and offers more reasonable pricing.
4. Alternative option: For day-long cross-regional sightseeing (e.g., from the south to Ubud or volcanoes), booking a private car charter is a more common, comfortable, and dispute-free choice, with drivers often doubling as guides.

1. 主要區域仍可使用：在機場、大多數酒店和普通街道，你依然可以正常使用 Grab 和 Gojek 這兩款叫車APP。它們價格透明，是短途移動的方便選擇。
2. 避開標誌地點：如果在旅遊景點、廟宇或餐廳門口看到類似“禁止網約車 (Online Taxi) 停靠/接客”的標誌，應避免在該處叫車或上車(上圖)。

4. 替代方案：對於一天的跨區域觀光（例如從南部前往烏布或火山），預訂包車是更普遍、舒適且無爭議的選擇，司機通常還能兼任嚮導。
