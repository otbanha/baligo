### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Post-Disaster Recovery and Conservation Efforts

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1777039875036-club-med-bali-nusa-dua-terbakar_01-696x464.jpg)

- **Update on Club Med Bali Fire and Operations**: A fire broke out yesterday at Club Med Bali in Nusa Dua.
Cause of Fire: Preliminary investigations suggest the fire originated from a deep fryer in the kitchen. Staff were preparing breakfast when the oil overheated and ignited. Although employees attempted to extinguish the flames with fire extinguishers, the fire quickly spread to the wooden ceiling and thatched roof (Sirap).
Casualties: According to the latest statements from ITDC and local police, there were no injuries.
Damage: The fire destroyed approximately 5,000 square meters of the two-story building, including the Agung restaurant on the second floor, as well as the boutique, meeting rooms, and accounting office on the first floor.
- **"Row for R.O.L.E 5.0" Charity Rowing Event**: This morning (4/25), a large-scale charity rowing event took place in the waters from **Sanur to Nusa Dua**. The event aims to raise funds for the Zero Waste Center and increase awareness of marine conservation. Due to the event, there is a higher concentration of kayaks in the southern waters this morning, so please be cautious during water activities.
- **2026 Tourism Goal: Quality Over Quantity**: The Bali Tourism Board officially announced today that the target for international visitors in 2026 is set at **6.63 million**. Authorities stated they will reduce reliance on budget tours and instead promote high-end cultural and nature-focused tourism. Specific strategies will be unveiled at next week's tourism expo.

### 📄 Visitor Concerns: Weekend Crowds and Entry Reminders

- **Seminyak Weekend Party Alert**: Today marks the 13th anniversary of Seminyak's iconic Mexican restaurant, Motel Mexicola. A large crowd is expected to gather in the area from the evening. Visitors planning to dine nearby are advised to leave an hour early to avoid traffic congestion.
- **Flight and Entry Status**: Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) is operating **normally** today. Due to increased arrivals, the immigration department reminds travelers that the **USD$10 tourism tax** inspection rate has been heightened. Be sure to complete payment via "Love Bali" before arrival and save the QR Code to avoid issues downloading it at the airport due to poor signal.
- **Hotel Preventive Safety Checks**: Following the Club Med fire incident, several high-end hotels in the Nusa Dua area are enhancing internal fire safety inspections. Brief drills or security personnel movements may occur in some areas, but these will not affect guest accommodations.
