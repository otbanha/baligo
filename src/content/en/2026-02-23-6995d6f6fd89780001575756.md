📍 【Top Value Choice】April Tomb-Sweeping Day Holiday – A Spiritual Cleansing After the Calm

---

Why choose this time? It's a super smart timing! The busiest time of the year in Indonesia, the 'Eid al-Fitr' holiday, just ended around late March 2026. As Indonesians return to work and international tourists aren't as crazy as in peak season, Bali welcomes a brief but precious period of tranquility.

- Climate Status: The 'Golden Turning Point' between dry and wet seasons

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_298e8da8b8949ce68b9c9a6ea180e1af.png)

1. Farewell to the Rainy Season, Welcome the Sunshine Bali's rainy season usually ends by late March. Entering April, the chance of rain significantly decreases. Even if there are occasional showers, they are mostly in the form of 'afternoon thunderstorms,' quick to start and stop, usually clearing up within half an hour, not affecting your plans at all.

2. Lower Humidity, More Comfortable Feel Compared to the stuffy and humid feel of January and February, April's air starts to become refreshing. Although temperatures still range between 25°C - 32°C, the reduced humidity means outdoor activities won't have that sticky, suffocating feeling.

**2. 濕度下降，體感更舒適** 比起 1、2 月悶熱潮濕的感覺，4 月的空氣開始變得清爽。雖然氣溫依然維持在 **25°C - 32°C** 之間，但由於濕度降低，在戶外活動時不會有那種黏膩的窒息感。

- **Local Updates**: April 3rd is Good Friday, and April 5th is Easter, both of which are national holidays in Indonesia. While locals are on holiday, tourists can avoid the influx of visitors from across Indonesia.
- **Budget Analysis**: Airfare and accommodations often see a slight drop after Eid al-Fitr, offering great value for money. (Check out our accommodation recommendations here).

### 📍 【Relaxation First】May Labor Day — A Global Island Escape

**Why Visit During This Time?** May 1st is Labor Day, an international holiday, meaning not only are you on vacation, but **Bali** is also in holiday mode. The island's vibe during this period is incredibly relaxed and lively.

- **Climate Advantage**: May marks the official start of Bali's dry season. Rainfall decreases further compared to April, with almost every day being perfect for outdoor activities—no need for rainy-day plans. The sky is as blue as a filter, and the lower humidity makes it ideal for beach picnics and sunset watching. Starting in May, the surf conditions on Bali's west coast become ideal, making it a paradise for surfers.
- **Budget Analysis**: Airfare prices remain stable, so it's recommended to book villas in popular areas three months in advance. (Check out our no-fail accommodation recommendations here).

### 📍 Third Stop: 【Cultural Feast】June Dragon Boat Festival Holiday — A Highlight of Sun and Celebrations

**Why Visit During This Time?** If you love deep cultural experiences, June 2026 is undoubtedly Bali's most exciting month, hands down!

- **Climate Advantage**: The chance of rain is extremely low, with clear blue skies almost every day, so you won't have to worry about outdoor plans being disrupted. Thanks to the influence of Australia's cold air, Bali in June is sunny but refreshing, with cool sea breezes. Average temperatures range from **24°C to 30°C**, making it the least humid time of the year. With no rain washing away sediment, the ocean is at its clearest and bluest, making it the best time of the year for **snorkeling and diving**.
- **Local Updates**:
  - **June 17th: Galungan**: The island will be adorned with elaborate bamboo decorations (Penjor), celebrating the victory of good over evil.
  - **June 13th - July 11th: Bali Arts Festival**: A month-long arts festival takes place in Denpasar.
  - **June 18th - June 20th: Pandawa Beach Festival**: Music and market events will be held at the famous Pandawa Beach.
- **Budget Analysis**: Due to the numerous festivals, accommodations may be slightly more competitive, so it's recommended to book your preferred stay in advance. (See our community-recommended accommodations here).
