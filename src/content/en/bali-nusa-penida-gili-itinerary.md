## Before Departure, I Felt More Hesitation Than Excitement

Before leaving, I read quite a few posts about Bali, and the online reviews were pretty mixed, with no shortage of negative ones. So I didn't set off with high expectations—I just wanted to see for myself what makes this destination, praised by many yet complained about by others, so special.

Once I actually set foot in Bali, I realized that some things truly can only be understood through firsthand experience.

What this trip gave me wasn't just stunning ocean views, but a completely different travel rhythm.

There was the tranquility that slows you down and clears your mind, the vibrant island nightlife, the well-preserved traditional culture, breathtaking natural landscapes, and warm, welcoming locals. Although I didn't get to visit every spot this time, it left me with plenty of reasons to return to Bali.

***

## Nusa Penida｜A Diver's Paradise

The place that amazed me most throughout the entire trip was Nusa Penida.

Compared to mainland Bali, the pace of life here is much more laid-back, making it perfect for a few days of pure relaxation. Aside from diving, my days were spent gazing at the sea and enjoying the breeze—completely free from the pressure of a packed itinerary.

If you love diving, this place is truly worth staying a few extra days.

https://youtu.be/l5T_u1tnRHY

The chance of spotting manta rays, the stars of the underwater world, is remarkably high. Just lie still on the seabed, and you might watch them glide gracefully past you—a breathtaking sight I still vividly remember. Drift diving at other sites is equally thrilling, with well-preserved coral and incredibly rich marine life, each dive offering a new surprise.

Beyond the underwater world, the island's natural landscapes are equally unforgettable.

Angel's Billabong, Broken Beach, Kelingking Beach, and Diamond Beach each boast unique terrain and scenery. Standing on the cliff's edge overlooking the Indian Ocean truly feels like a visual feast.

As night falls, head to a beachfront restaurant to enjoy dinner while listening to a live band, with the sea breeze setting the perfect vacation mood.

***

## Gili Trawangan (Gili T) | Chill by day, party by night

https://youtube.com/shorts/FXKZWSpsrXY

After leaving Nusa Penida, I took a boat to Gili Trawangan.

As soon as I stepped off the boat, I could feel the island's completely different vibe from Nusa Penida.

There are no cars on the island—the most distinctive mode of transport is horse-drawn carriages. The entire island is filled with a joyful holiday vibe, earning it the nickname 'Party Island.'

During the day, you can stroll along the pristine white sand beaches, gazing at the crystal-clear waters, and even spot horses leisurely wandering by the shore.

As evening falls, the sunset on the west coast is an absolute must-see. If you get the chance, I recommend riding a horse along the coast to watch the sunset—the scene is truly romantic.

At night, the island's bars and restaurants start hosting live performances one after another. With delicious food, music, and lively crowds, the whole island buzzes with energy, fully showcasing the charm of a party island.

***

## Ubud | A Journey of Culture, Volcanoes, and Soul

After leaving the Gili Islands, I returned to Bali's mainland and arrived in Ubud, a place rich in cultural heritage.

The Ubud Palace showcases the profound traditions of Balinese culture, while the nearby Ubud Market is full of local life—perfect for a leisurely browse and picking out favorite souvenirs.

The next morning, before dawn, was one of the most anticipated parts of the trip.

We took a jeep to Mount Batur and greeted the sunrise at the summit. When the first rays of sunlight spilled over the volcano and the lake, the breathtaking scenery made the early morning fatigue completely worth it.

Our jeep driver was incredibly warm and enthusiastic, proactively helping us take photos along the way, leaving us with plenty of memorable shots.

After descending the mountain, head to Akasa to enjoy breakfast while taking in the views of Mount Batur. You can also see the lava formations left by volcanic eruptions up close, feeling the awe-inspiring power of nature.

https://youtube.com/shorts/lXemz_JODWo

At the end of the journey, visit Tirta Empul Temple, bringing this trip to a peaceful and fulfilling close.

## 10-Day 9-Night Itinerary Overview

**Day 1｜Arrival in Bali**
Arrive at Ngurah Rai International Airport at 2:35 PM, then head to Sanur to check in. Spend the evening strolling and shopping nearby, soaking in the relaxed seaside town vibe.

**Day 2｜West Coast of Nusa Penida**

https://youtube.com/shorts/HjaEoqBsGp4

Take a boat from Sanur to Nusa Penida, visiting Angel's Billabong, Broken Beach, and Kelingking Beach. Have dinner at Penida Colada Beach Bar, and stay at VNS Beachfront Guesthouse.

https://youtube.com/shorts/O1yqlXc6PLs

**Day 3｜Diving in Nusa Penida**
Join a scuba diving trip with Pure Dive, and have dinner at Cactus Beach Club & Restaurant.

**Day 4｜Diving + Diamond Beach**
Another scuba session with Pure Dive, then head to Diamond Beach in the afternoon. Dinner at Penida Colada Beach Bar.

**Day 5｜Crystal Bay**
Scuba diving with Pure Dive, followed by a visit to Crystal Bay. Dinner at Amarta Penida.

**Day 6｜Heading to the Gili Islands**
Take a boat to Gili Trawangan, explore the northern beaches, and enjoy a sunset horseback ride on the west coast in the evening. Check in at Quma Boutique Hotel.

https://youtube.com/shorts/wXvhx58DIps

**Day 7｜Diving at Gili T**
Scuba diving with Dive Central Gili. In the evening, hit up Pink Sunset, Kiss, and Margarita Resto for a lively night out.

**Day 8｜Leisurely Getaway**
Spend the day relaxing at the northern beaches, then check in at Villa Koru. Head back to the west coast for sunset in the evening.

**Day 9｜Return to Bali**
Take a boat to Padang Bai, then hire a private driver to visit Tegallalang Rice Terraces, TIS Cafe, Ubud Palace, and Ubud Market. Check in at Jepun Villa.

**Day 10｜Mount Batur Sunrise**
Ride a jeep to Mount Batur before dawn to catch the sunrise, then enjoy breakfast at Akasa with volcano views. Next, visit Tirta Empul Temple before heading to the airport. Arrive around 1:00 PM for check-in, wrapping up this incredible Bali adventure.

https://youtube.com/shorts/XDOnfDyylsA

**(Editor's Note)** Generally, it's not recommended to schedule far-flung spots like Mount Batur and Tirta Empul Temple on your departure day. Bali traffic can be unpredictable, with heavy congestion common during peak seasons or rush hours. Any delays or unexpected incidents could jeopardize your flight timing.

It's recommended to arrange your final day in areas closer to the airport, such as Kuta, Jimbaran, or Sanur, or book an afternoon or evening flight for a more relaxed journey.

## The Journey Ends, But the Story Doesn't

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-07/1784594813425-map.jpg)

If you ask me what left the deepest impression on this trip,

it's not any particular attraction or how many beautiful photos I took—it's rediscovering Bali.

It has its lively side and its serene side; world-class dive spots and profound cultural heritage. You can party hard on the island of revelry, or slow down in Ubud and quietly soak in life.

In the end, online reviews are just other people's experiences—everyone travels differently and feels differently. 

At least for me, these 10 days and 9 nights were more than just a trip; they were a beautiful memory of slowing down and seeing a different landscape. 

I hope this travelogue can also serve as a useful reference for you, the next traveler heading to Bali.
