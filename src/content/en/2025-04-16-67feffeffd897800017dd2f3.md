### What is the Bali Tourism Tax?

Starting February 2024, all **international travelers** entering Bali are required to pay a **150,000 IDR (approximately USD$10) tourism tax**. This fee is mainly used for:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_faa81d91ecf80644e97531f94ad11639.png)

- Protecting local culture and heritage
- Maintaining natural environments
- Improving tourism infrastructure

However, statistics since its implementation reveal that less than **40%** of travelers have actually paid the tax, highlighting the urgent need to enhance enforcement.

---

### Why Reform This Policy?

While the policy's goals are widely supported, its execution has been notably inadequate. Issues include:

- Lack of clear payment guidelines
- Absence of penalties, leaving visitors with no real incentive to pay
- Lack of transparency in how the tax is used, raising public concerns

Under mounting pressure, multiple political parties in the Bali Provincial Council have jointly supported amendments to regional regulations, aiming to strengthen the policy's legality and practicality.

---

### Strengthening Enforcement and Independent Oversight

To address tax evasion, **the Bali government plans to commission third-party operators to assist in implementing the tourism tax policy**, which will:

- Make the payment process more convenient
- Improve payment recognition for tourists
- Enable real-time tracking and transparency of funds

Additionally, the Gerindra-PSI faction has proposed establishing an **independent oversight body** responsible for managing and allocating tax revenues to ensure funds are used appropriately.

### Enhancing Communication with Tourists

According to the Indonesian Tourism Association (ASITA), many visitors are unaware of this tax. To address this issue, the government will take the following actions:

- Increase signage at airports, ports, and tourist attractions
- Send payment notifications through travel agencies
- Expand awareness campaigns via tourism apps and websites

In the future, tourists may need to **show their tax QR code more frequently**, especially at popular attractions, entry points, and ports.

Currently, tourists can pay through the [**LoveBali website**](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/) **or app**, or pay on-site at Bali's airports upon arrival. In the future, authorities may introduce more payment options to enhance convenience.

### The Shared Vision of Bali's Government and People

Bali's Vice Governor **I Nyoman Giri Prasta** stated: "We will adopt a technology-driven approach to implement a real-time information system, ensuring funds are used to enhance the welfare and happiness of the people of Bali."

The ultimate goal of this policy is to ensure that the wealth generated by tourism genuinely benefits local communities, setting a benchmark for sustainable tourism.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. Where can I pay the Bali tourism tax?

You can pay via the LoveBali App or [website](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/), or make the payment on-site at Bali's international airport.

### 2. What happens if I don't pay the tourism tax?

Currently, there are no specific penalties, but stricter checks and related restrictions may be implemented in the future.

### 3. How can I prove that I've paid the tourism tax?

After payment, you will receive a QR Code voucher. It is recommended to carry this with you during your trip and be prepared to present it when needed.

### 4. What is the actual use of the tax?

The funds are used for protecting cultural heritage, improving infrastructure, and environmental conservation. Detailed allocation will be overseen by an independent organization.

### 5. Who needs to pay this tax?

All **international tourists** entering Bali are required to pay, regardless of nationality, age, or length of stay.
