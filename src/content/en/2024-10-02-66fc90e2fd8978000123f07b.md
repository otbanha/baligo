Bali's highest peak, the sacred Mount Agung, will be temporarily closed from October 1 to November 30. During this period, neither tourists nor local residents will be allowed to enter Mount Agung, as the local community will be conducting a major religious ceremony that occurs once every decade.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7d1b23f13bb45f8f4090e3555ac1a358.png)

Mount Agung is one of Bali's most spiritual landmarks and also an active volcano. Every year, large numbers of tourists set out in the early hours to witness the spectacular sunrise from the summit. However, for Bali's Hindus, Mount Agung is not just a volcano; it is a sacred site that must be revered according to tradition and religion.

The closure includes all hiking trails, whether official or unofficial. Major local ceremonies such as Karya Tabuh Gentuh Wana Kertih, Segara Kertih, and Nubung Pedashan will be held at Pasar Agung Temple in Sebudi Village. These ceremonies occur only once every ten years, with the last one taking place in 2014.

Wayan Suara Arsana, the leader of Pasar Agung Temple, stated that all travel agencies and licensed hiking guides have been notified of the closure. He hopes that tourists will understand and respect this closure, allowing the ceremonies to be completed smoothly.

In fact, this is not the first time Mount Agung has been closed due to religious activities. In July, the mountain was temporarily closed for a full moon ceremony at Pura Besakih, the mother temple. Such closures are quite common in the area.

However, in the past, some disobedient hikers have ignored the closure and secretly climbed the mountain. Those who disrespect local culture will be required to participate in purification rituals to restore the mountain's sanctity.

While the closure of Mount Agung for these two months may be disappointing for many tourists, Bali has other famous peaks that remain open, such as Mount Batur and Mount Batukaru. If the weather is good, you can even enjoy a view of Mount Agung from the summit of Mount Batur!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1efeaab1b55ccb879580aa7b6c6fac01.png)

Additionally, when hiking, **always remember to hire a local guide**, whether you're climbing a big mountain or a small one. While Bali's hiking trails are not as high as those in the Himalayas or the Alps, the routes are challenging and poorly marked. Every month, local rescue teams are called to assist lost or injured hikers.

Therefore, for safety and to respect local culture, remember to follow the regulations and avoid attempting to climb Mount Agung during the closure period. Don't forget to hire a guide to explore Bali's other magnificent mountain landscapes together!
