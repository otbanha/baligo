Bali's highest peak, the sacred Mount Agung, will be temporarily closed from October 1st to November 30th. During this period, neither tourists nor local residents are allowed to enter Mount Agung, as the local community will be conducting a major religious ceremony that occurs once every decade.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7d1b23f13bb45f8f4090e3555ac1a358.png)

Mount Agung is one of Bali's most spiritual landmarks and also an active volcano. Every year, many tourists start their journey in the early morning to witness the magnificent sunrise from the summit. However, for Bali's Hindu community, Mount Agung is not just a volcano; it is a sacred site that must be respected according to tradition and religion.

This closure includes all hiking trails, whether official or unofficial. Major local ceremonies such as Karya Tabuh Gentuh Wana Kertih, Segara Kertih, and Nubung Pedashan will be held at Pasar Agung Temple in Sebudi Village. These ceremonies occur once every ten years, with the last one taking place in 2014.

The leader of Pasar Agung Temple, Wayan Suara Arsa, stated that all travel agencies and licensed hiking guides have been informed about the closure. He hopes that tourists will understand and respect this closure, allowing the ceremonies to proceed smoothly.

In fact, this is not the first time Mount Agung has been closed due to religious activities. In July, the mountain was temporarily closed for a full moon ceremony held at Pura Besakih, the mother temple. Such closures are quite common in the area.

However, in the past, some disobedient hikers have ignored the closure and secretly climbed the mountain. Those who disrespect the local culture will be required to participate in purification rituals to restore the mountain's sanctity.

While the closure of Mount Agung for these two months may disappoint many tourists, Bali still has other famous peaks open for hiking, such as Mount Batur and Mount Batukaru. On a clear day, you can even enjoy a beautiful view of Mount Agung from the summit of Mount Batur!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1efeaab1b55ccb879580aa7b6c6fac01.png)

Additionally, when hiking, it is essential to hire a local guide, whether you're climbing a major peak or a smaller one. While Bali's trails are not as high as those in the Himalayas or the Alps, they are challenging and poorly marked. Every month, local rescue teams are called to assist lost or injured hikers.

Therefore, for safety and to respect local culture, remember to follow the regulations and avoid the risk of climbing Mount Agung during its closure period. Don't forget to hire a guide and explore Bali's other magnificent mountain landscapes together!
