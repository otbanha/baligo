## Transportation

Transportation was one of my top concerns when traveling to Bali with my mother. Since many say Bali's taxi drivers tend to overcharge tourists, I decided to use Grab for rides before departure. My previous experience with Grab in Thailand was excellent, and Bali was no exception. However, the airport's transportation scene was chaotic. As soon as we stepped out, we were approached by a group of taxi drivers aggressively offering their services, even claiming to be Grab drivers and proposing cheaper rates. This was particularly frustrating, especially when traveling with an elderly parent.

My advice is to head left immediately after exiting the airport, where you'll find a small parking lot where Grab drivers usually wait. This avoids harassment from other taxi drivers and helps you locate your ride faster. However, some drivers may still approach you during the wait—simply tell them firmly that you've already booked a ride.

During our days in Bali, we relied almost entirely on Grab for transportation. The app's advantage is its abundance of drivers; you can usually get a ride within three minutes, whether at attractions or restaurants. Most drivers speak decent English and are friendly, with fares strictly following the app's pricing, eliminating worries about being overcharged. The only minor issue is the scarcity of small change in Bali. Sometimes, we didn't have enough small bills and simply told the driver to keep the change—usually just a few dollars—which they appreciated.

That said, Bali's traffic is truly headache-inducing 🤣. There's no clear distinction between fast and slow lanes, and motorcycles weave through traffic unpredictably. Watching them dart between cars can make you feel like a collision is imminent. If you're brave, you might try Grab's motorcycle service, especially during peak hours when car traffic can be unbearably congested. Once, we traveled 20 kilometers between two locations, and it took a full two hours—both frustrating and amusing.

Overall, Grab is a reliable transportation option in Bali, especially for those traveling with elderly family members—it's convenient and safe. Just remember that Bali's traffic can be chaotic, so exercise extra caution, particularly when crossing streets, as motorcycles and cars move differently than in Taiwan.

## Private Car Hire

During our trip to Bali, hiring a private car was one of the most convenient and stress-free transportation options we chose. Our driver was highly recommended in online forums, and after experiencing his service firsthand, I can confidently say he was worth every penny. Here’s a detailed account of our private car hire experience for your reference.

### Booking and Communication:

We contacted the driver via Line, and the communication was smooth and efficient. We first informed him about our desired itinerary and the duration of the car hire. Based on our needs, he provided suggestions, listed the total cost, and outlined the sequence of activities. The day before our trip, he reached out again to confirm the schedule. The best part? Payment was made only after the day’s activities were completed, so there was no need to worry about prepayment.

### Itinerary and Cost:

The main purpose of hiring the car was to explore the Ubud area, and afterward, we asked the driver to drop us off at our next hotel. The total car hire duration was 10 hours, costing approximately USD$39, which I found very reasonable. Additionally, the driver helped us arrange activities like ATV rides, river tubing, and swings, incorporating these costs into the final price. Payment was made in full at the end of the day, making everything hassle-free.

## Civet Coffee

During this Bali trip, our private car driver took us to a civet coffee plantation, which is one of the "classic itineraries" many tourists encounter in Bali. Although the experience felt a bit commercialized, I still wanted to share my thoughts to give you an additional reference when planning your trip.

### Driver's Recommendation:

During our private car tour, the driver casually asked if we wanted to try Kopi Luwak, emphasizing that it's a specialty of Bali and costs only USD$3 per cup, making it well worth a try. Intrigued by the affordable price and driven by curiosity, we agreed. The driver then took us to a dedicated Kopi Luwak plantation.

### Plantation Tour and Tasting:

As soon as we entered the plantation, the staff enthusiastically explained the process of making Kopi Luwak, highlighting how natural and organic their coffee is, and how it undergoes 'natural processing' by civet cats. They guided us through the plantation, showing the relationship between the civet cats (actually palm civets) and the coffee beans, before leading us to the tasting area.

The tasting experience was quite interesting. In addition to Kopi Luwak, they also prepared a variety of other coffees and herbal teas for us to sample. Each drink was accompanied by a detailed explanation, such as the flavor profile of the coffee or the benefits of the herbal tea. After tasting everything, it did feel a bit awkward not to buy something.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b47855e8d20e3f0b3936512c44f670a2.jpg)

### Controversy Surrounding Kopi Luwak:

However, while tasting, I recalled the controversy surrounding Kopi Luwak that I had read about online. Many reports indicate that these coffees aren't entirely naturally sourced, with many civet cats being kept in cages and force-fed coffee beans, which is not animal-friendly. Additionally, Kopi Luwak is extremely expensive. While the tasting cup was only USD$3, the actual purchase price might be prohibitive.

### Purchase Pressure and Actual Experience:

During the tasting, the staff enthusiastically promoted their products right in front of us, which created a bit of pressure. We ended up buying a small pack of mangosteen tea for around USD$13, which wasn't too expensive but also not exactly cheap. As for the Kopi Luwak, it did have a subtle sweetness and a smooth texture, but it didn't strike me as a 'must-buy.' If you're not particularly passionate about coffee or uninterested in such commercialized tours, you can simply tell the driver to skip this stop, saving a lot of time.

### Experience Summary:

The civet coffee experience is considered a signature activity in Bali, but the hidden animal welfare issues and commercialized approach can be a bit off-putting. If you're not particularly interested in coffee or don't want to spend too much time on such shopping-oriented activities, it's best to communicate with your driver beforehand and skip this part. After all, Bali has so many more worthwhile attractions and activities to explore. Time is precious, so why not save it for creating even more beautiful memories!

## Food

One of the standout highlights of my Bali trip was definitely the food! Bali's culinary culture is incredibly diverse, especially the local specialties, which left a lasting impression. Below is a detailed sharing of my dining experiences, hoping to provide some useful references for everyone.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d7966ba428f0a4c7761ad02df03437bc.jpg)

### Dirty Duck Rice & Pork Ribs:

First, I highly recommend Bali's 'Dirty Duck Rice' and 'Pork Ribs.' Dirty Duck Rice is a traditional Balinese dish where the duck is marinated and cooked with spices for a long time, resulting in tender meat paired with fragrant rice, creating a rich and layered flavor that keeps you coming back for more. As for the pork ribs, they are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, with a sweet and savory sauce that isn't overpowering, making it perfect for meat lovers.

Interestingly, our driver mentioned that while most of Indonesia's population is Muslim and avoids pork, over 85% of Bali's population practices Hinduism, so pork dishes are widely available here, which is quite rare in other parts of Indonesia. Additionally, Bali's lifestyle is relatively free compared to Islamic regions, with fewer restrictions on clothing or diet, making our trip more relaxed and enjoyable. Our driver also mentioned that schools in Bali end at noon, which made me feel like the pace of life here is truly leisurely—sounds amazing 🤣.

### Traditional Indonesian Cuisine:

While Dirty Duck Rice and Pork Ribs were delightful, traditional Indonesian dishes didn't quite hit the mark for us. We even signed up for a cooking class, spending a morning learning to prepare six or seven Indonesian dishes. There were a few Korean tourists in the class, and after tasting, everyone found the dishes too salty and oily, so we only took a couple of bites before stopping. However, the cooking class itself was still a lot of fun, as getting hands-on with local cuisine is a unique travel experience.

## Currency Exchange

When traveling in Bali, currency exchange is a crucial aspect to pay attention to, especially since the local currency amounts are very large, and it's easy to get confused. Below are my currency exchange experiences from this trip, hoping to help everyone handle money matters more smoothly.

### Bringing USD is Most Convenient:

It's recommended to **exchange USD in Taiwan first, then convert it to Indonesian Rupiah in Bali**. This is because the exchange rate for USD in Bali is relatively stable, and all currency exchange offices accept USD. This time, we brought USD to Bali and paid for the visa-on-arrival fee directly with USD at the airport, receiving change in Indonesian Rupiah from the staff. This way, we had some local currency right after landing.

### Airport vs. City Currency Exchange:

Although there are currency exchange offices at the airport, the rates are usually not as favorable as those in the city. Therefore, we only exchanged a small amount of Indonesian Rupiah for transportation from the airport to the city, and exchanged the rest at reputable currency exchange offices in the city.

### Amount Representation and Conversion Tips:

The denominations of Indonesian Rupiah are very large, often ranging from tens of thousands to millions, which might be confusing for first-time visitors. Locals commonly represent the last three zeros with a "K". For example, 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah is written as 50K. The simplest way to convert between Indonesian Rupiah and USD is to multiply the number before "K" by 2, then add a bit more, giving an approximate USD amount. For example, 50K Indonesian Rupiah is roughly a bit over USD$3, making it very convenient for calculations and avoiding mistakes when shopping or paying.

### Precautions:

When exchanging money, always choose reputable currency exchange offices and avoid small roadside stalls to prevent encountering counterfeit bills or unfair exchange rates. Additionally, it's best to count the money on the spot and confirm the amount before leaving. Some exchange offices may charge service fees, so it's advisable to inquire about this before proceeding with the exchange.

## Accommodation

This Bali accommodation experience deeply impressed upon me the importance of choosing the right location and surrounding environment. Below are some insights from my stay, hoping to help everyone make more suitable choices when planning their trips.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a547a653ea0227a0f9908d938d74da36.jpg)

### Mosquito Issue:

First and foremost, Bali has a lot of mosquitoes, and they are particularly large! This might be due to the humid local climate and the fact that hotels and villas often plant a lot of flowers and trees to create a tropical ambiance, which unfortunately also becomes a breeding ground for mosquitoes. Even though we stayed in a relatively upscale villa, mosquitoes still managed to fly into our room. Therefore, **mosquito repellent is an absolute must-have item**. It's recommended to carry it with you at all times, especially during dusk and dawn when mosquitoes are most active.

**Quick Reference:**

[What Essential Medicines Should You Bring for an Indonesia/Bali Trip? How to Buy Mosquito Repellent/Sunscreen?](/blog/bali-travel-medicine-checklist/)

### Breakfast Service:

Although the mosquito issue was somewhat bothersome, the hotels and guesthouses we stayed at performed exceptionally well when it came to breakfast. The breakfast options were diverse, ranging from local specialties to Western-style breakfasts, all of high quality. I believe breakfast is very important in Bali accommodations because there are hardly any convenience stores or snack stalls around where we stayed. If you didn't eat enough for breakfast, you might have to wait until lunchtime to find something to eat. Therefore, I recommend paying special attention to whether breakfast is provided when choosing accommodations, as it will make your journey much more comfortable.

### Choosing the Right Accommodation Location:

The biggest mistake in this accommodation experience was that our stay was quite far from the bustling areas, with almost no shops or restaurants nearby. Every time we went out, we had to call for a car, which was very inconvenient. Bali's attractions are scattered, and transportation isn't very convenient. If your accommodation isn't in a bustling area, the daily transportation costs can add up significantly. Each time we called for a car, it cost us around USD$40, and once we were out, it was impossible to come back to fetch something or rest, which somewhat limited our itinerary planning.

Therefore, **when choosing accommodation, it’s essential to consider the convenience of the surrounding area**. If you enjoy a lively atmosphere, you can opt to stay in bustling areas like Kuta, Seminyak, or Ubud, where there are plenty of restaurants, shops, and evening entertainment options. If you choose to stay in more remote villas or resorts, be prepared to rely on Grab food delivery or private car services for your daily needs.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0391757a9b3ffecaea7406bc888c42e0.jpg)

### Accommodation Insights:

While Bali’s accommodation options are beautiful, mosquito issues and location choices are important factors to keep in mind. Be sure to bring enough mosquito repellent and consider the convenience of your surroundings when selecting where to stay. Staying in less central areas may increase transportation costs and inconvenience, so keep this in mind when planning your itinerary. We hope these tips help you find the perfect accommodation in Bali, making your trip even more enjoyable!
