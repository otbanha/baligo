## 1. Historical Background and Religious Significance of Tanah Lot

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_36a21198d37ccb139f68e96a327f9b33.jpg)

The history of Tanah Lot dates back to the 16th century, when it was established by the Javanese priest Nirartha. During his travels, Nirartha was captivated by the spiritual aura of this small island and built a temple dedicated to the sea god to protect the local fishermen and villagers. Since then, Tanah Lot has become an integral part of local faith and culture.

The temple's architecture is simple yet powerful, especially during high tide when the entire structure appears to float on the sea, creating a mystical and solemn atmosphere. During low tide, visitors can walk to the front of the temple to admire its intricately carved structure. As an important religious site in Bali, Tanah Lot also hosts special ceremonies, the most significant being the 'Piodalan' festival, which takes place four days after the Balinese 'Hari Raya Kuningan'. Visitors can witness the devout religious activities of the locals.

## 2. Exploring Popular Activities at Tanah Lot

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_71c5ba824a21ebf14225e1a786a733aa.jpg)

Tanah Lot is not just a site for viewing ancient architecture; it also offers a variety of activities for visitors to enjoy. Here are some must-try experiences:

1. **Admire the Beauty of Tanah Lot and the Rocky Island**  
   The rocky island where Tanah Lot is located is full of natural beauty, appearing and disappearing with the tides. Many visitors choose to walk near the temple during low tide to witness the dramatic sight of waves crashing against the rocks. This unique location, known as 'Tanah Lot', is an ideal spot for capturing Bali's scenic beauty.
2. **Observe the Sacred Snakes at the Snake Temple**  
   Another unique feature is the 'Snake Cave' near the temple, home to sacred snakes known as 'Ular Suci' by the locals. These black and yellow snakes, with flat tails resembling fish, are believed to be incarnations of Nirartha, the temple's founder, and serve as guardians of the temple. Visitors can observe these snakes under the guidance of local guides, experiencing the locals' reverence for these sacred creatures.
3. **Capture the Dreamy Sunset at Tanah Lot**  
   The sunset at Tanah Lot is considered one of the best in Bali. As the sun gradually sets over the horizon, the entire temple is bathed in golden light, creating a serene and romantic atmosphere. To secure the best viewing spot for the 'golden sunset', it is recommended to arrive early and find the perfect vantage point. This moment attracts many photography enthusiasts, making it an excellent time for capturing stunning images.

1. **欣賞海神廟與海中岩島的美景**  
   海神廟所在的岩島充滿自然美景，隨著潮汐變化時隱時現。許多遊客會選擇在退潮時步行至寺廟附近，欣賞海浪拍打岩石的震撼景象。這片被稱為「Tanah Lot」的岩島，其獨特位置成為遊客拍攝峇里島風光的理想地點。
2. **觀賞蛇神廟的神秘聖蛇**  
   另一個特別之處是寺廟附近的「聖蛇洞」，裡面居住著當地人稱為「神蛇（Ular Suci）」的神聖蛇類。這些黑黃相間、尾巴扁平如魚的蛇，被認為是海神廟創建者Nirartha的化身，具有守護寺廟的神聖使命。遊客可在當地導遊指導下觀賞這些蛇，體驗當地人對神聖動物的敬畏。
3. **捕捉夕陽與海神廟的夢幻瞬間**  
   海神廟的夕陽被譽為峇里島最佳之一。隨著太陽逐漸落入海平面，整座寺廟被染上金黃色，營造出寧靜浪漫的氛圍。為了欣賞「金色夕陽」的最佳觀賞點，建議遊客提前前往並找到理想的視角。這個時刻吸引了大量攝影愛好者，是絕佳的拍攝時間。

## 3. Visiting Tips: Ticket Information, Tide Schedule, and Nearby Accommodation

Before heading to Tanah Lot Temple, understanding ticket and transportation details can make your trip smoother.

Tanah Lot Temple official website: [www.tanahlot.id](https://www.tanahlot.id/)

The latest 2024 ticket prices for Tanah Lot Temple are:

**Indonesian Citizens**

- Adults: 30,000 IDR
- Children: 20,000 IDR

**Foreign Visitors**

- Adults: 75,000 IDR (approx. NT$150 / USD$5)
- Children: 40,000 IDR (approx. NT$80 / USD$2.6)

Parking fees are also applicable: 3,000 IDR for motorcycles, 5,000 IDR for cars, and 10,000 IDR for large buses. The temple is open daily from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM.

The best time to visit Tanah Lot Temple is usually determined by the tide schedule. Travelers are advised to check the [Tanah Lot Tide Schedule](https://www.tideschart.com/Indonesia/Bali/Tabanan/Weekly/) before their visit. This allows you to plan your trip during low tide, enabling a closer view of the temple and avoiding the dangers of high tide.

It's important to note that during peak hours at sunset, the crowds can be overwhelming. To avoid the rush, it's recommended to arrive early.
