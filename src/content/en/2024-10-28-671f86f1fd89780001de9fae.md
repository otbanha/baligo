Located in the Tabanan region of Bali, Tanah Lot Temple stands on a small rocky island in the sea, making it one of Bali's most iconic religious sites. Here, visitors can marvel at the stunning sunset and the waves crashing against the rocky shore, while also enjoying a variety of activities that attract travelers from around the world. This article provides a detailed introduction to the history of Tanah Lot, activities to enjoy, tide information, and accommodation recommendations, ensuring your trip to Bali is unforgettable.

## 1. The Historical Background and Religious Significance of Tanah Lot

The history of Tanah Lot dates back to the 16th century, when it was established by the Javanese priest Nirartha. During his travels, Nirartha was captivated by the spiritual aura of this small island and built a temple dedicated to the sea god, seeking blessings for the local fishermen and villagers. Since then, Tanah Lot has become an integral part of local faith and culture.

The temple's architecture is simple yet powerful, especially during high tide when the entire structure appears to float on the sea, creating a mystical and solemn atmosphere. During low tide, visitors can walk up to the temple to admire its intricately carved structure. As an important religious site in Bali, Tanah Lot also hosts special ceremonies, the most significant being the "Piodalan" festival, which takes place four days after the Balinese "Hari Raya Kuningan" celebration. Visitors can witness the locals' devout religious practices during this time.

## 2. Popular Activities to Explore at Tanah Lot

Tanah Lot is not just a site to admire ancient architecture; it also offers a variety of activities for visitors to enjoy. Here are some must-try experiences:

1. **Admire the Beauty of Tanah Lot and the Rocky Island**
   The rocky island where Tanah Lot is located is filled with natural beauty, appearing and disappearing with the tides. Many visitors choose to walk near the temple during low tide to witness the awe-inspiring sight of waves crashing against the rocks. This unique location, known as "Tanah Lot," is an ideal spot for capturing Bali's scenic landscapes.
2. **Observe the Sacred Snakes at the Snake Temple**
   Another highlight is the "Sacred Snake Cave" near the temple, home to snakes revered by locals as "Ular Suci" (Sacred Snakes). These black-and-yellow snakes, with tails flattened like fish, are believed to be incarnations of Nirartha, the temple's founder, and are said to guard the temple. Visitors can observe these snakes under the guidance of local guides, experiencing the locals' reverence for these sacred creatures.
3. **Capture the Dreamy Sunset at Tanah Lot**
   The sunset at Tanah Lot is considered one of the best in Bali. As the sun gradually sinks below the horizon, the temple is bathed in golden light, creating a serene and romantic atmosphere. To secure the best vantage point for the "golden sunset," it is recommended to arrive early and find the perfect spot. This moment attracts many photography enthusiasts, making it an excellent time for capturing stunning shots.

## Travel Tips: Ticket Information, Tide Schedules & Nearby Accommodations

Before visiting Tanah Lot, understanding ticket and transportation details can make your trip smoother.

Tanah Lot official website: [www.tanahlot.id](https://www.tanahlot.id/)

The latest 2024 ticket prices for Tanah Lot are:

**Indonesian Citizens**

- Adults: 30,000 IDR
- Children: 20,000 IDR

**Foreign Visitors**

- Adults: 75,000 IDR (~USD$5)
- Children: 40,000 IDR (~USD$2.6)

Parking fees are also applicable: Motorcycles 3,000 IDR, Cars 5,000 IDR, and Large Buses 10,000 IDR. The temple is open daily from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM.

The best time to visit Tanah Lot usually depends on the tide schedule. Travelers are advised to check the [Tanah Lot Tide Schedule](https://www.tideschart.com/Indonesia/Bali/Tabanan/Weekly/) before their visit. This allows you to plan your trip during low tide, enabling a closer view of the temple while avoiding the dangers of high tide.

It's worth noting that during peak hours at sunset, the area gets quite crowded. To avoid the crowds, we recommend arriving early.
