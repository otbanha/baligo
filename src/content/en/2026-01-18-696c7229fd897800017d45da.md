## 1. Local "Cooling" Drinks

Bali has many traditional herbal drinks (called Loloh) that are highly effective for reducing internal heat and rehydrating:

- **Loloh Cemcem (Herbal Drink):** This is Bali's most authentic heat-relief drink, especially popular in Ubud or Penglipuran. It’s green in color, with a sweet, sour, and slightly salty taste, made from **Cemcem leaves** (a local herb), palm sugar, cinnamon, and tamarind. Locals believe it effectively alleviates "internal heat" (Panas Dalam).
- **Kelapa Muda (Fresh Coconut Water):** This is the easiest-to-find natural electrolyte source. Opt for "green coconut" (Kelapa Hijau), which has lower sugar content and high electrolytes. After drinking, ask the vendor to scrape out the coconut flesh, which has a soothing effect.
- **Jamu Kunyit Asam (Turmeric Tamarind Drink):** This bright yellow herbal drink is available in supermarkets and roadside stalls. Turmeric has anti-inflammatory properties, while tamarind helps cool the body.

## 2. Local Dietary Remedies

- **Bubur Kacang Hijau (Mung Bean Porridge):** This warm or iced mung bean porridge is commonly sold at local warungs (roadside stalls). Mung beans are widely recognized in tropical countries as a heat-relief superfood, aiding in detoxification and diuresis.
- **Tamarind Candy:** If you feel slightly nauseous or dizzy, sucking on a tamarind candy can stimulate saliva production and ease discomfort.

## 3. Unique Local Therapies

- **Boreh Herbal Body Wrap:** While traditional Boreh is more about warming and detoxifying, some SPAs offer "Cooling Boreh," which uses cucumber, aloe vera, or sandalwood to quickly cool down overheated skin and repair sunburns.
- **Balinese Massage:** After mild heatstroke, blood circulation may slow down. Once your body temperature has slightly dropped, a gentle massage can relax tense muscles (but avoid it if you’re still running a high fever).

- **Boreh 藥草敷體：** 雖然傳統的 Boreh 偏向溫熱排濕，但有些 SPA 提供「Cooling Boreh」，使用黃瓜、蘆薈或檀香，敷在發燙的皮膚上能快速降溫並修復曬傷。
- **Balinese Massage（峇里島式按摩）：** 輕微中暑後，血液循環可能變差。等體溫稍降後，進行一次輕柔的按摩可以放鬆緊繃的肌肉（但若正處於高燒狀態請避免）。

## 4. Modern Bali Travel Solution: IV Drip Therapy

In Bali's tourist areas (such as Canggu, Seminyak, or Ubud), "IV Therapy" has become extremely popular. If you're severely dehydrated and physically exhausted, professional hospitals (like **BIMC** or **Siloam**) even offer [home visit services](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1197571&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F170717-24-7-doctor-on-call-bali-at-unicare-clinic-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Def8fdeaa30), where doctors will administer an IV drip containing saline, vitamin C, and electrolytes directly to you. This can significantly alleviate heatstroke symptoms within an hour.

### 💡 Emergency Guide

If you experience **confusion, a body temperature exceeding 40°C, or an inability to sweat**, it could be a severe case of heatstroke. Seek medical attention immediately.

## Can You Get a Massage or Spa After Heatstroke?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1120e315e89abf9baad7f4e4f333bee6.png)

Whether you can get a massage or spa treatment after heatstroke depends on your **current physical condition** and the **type of treatment**. In Bali's tropical climate, improper massage techniques can worsen heatstroke symptoms, so caution is essential.

Here are detailed recommendations for massage and spa treatments after heatstroke:

### 1. When Should You Absolutely Avoid Massage?

If you're in the **acute phase** of heatstroke and experiencing the following symptoms, avoid massage at all costs:

- **Fever or high body temperature:** Massage promotes blood circulation and accelerates metabolism, which can further raise body temperature and exacerbate heat exhaustion.
- **Severe dehydration, dizziness, or vomiting:** Your body is extremely weak at this point, and even gentle massage stimulation can cause blood pressure fluctuations, potentially leading to fainting.
- **Severe sunburn:** Bali's intense sunlight often accompanies heatstroke with sunburn. Massaging damaged skin can cause excessive friction, leading to pain or inflammation.

### 2. Post-Recovery (After Body Temperature Returns to Normal) Massage Recommendations

After you've rested in a cool place, replenished fluids and electrolytes, and your body temperature has returned to normal, you may opt for a SPA session, but keep the following precautions in mind:

- **❌ Avoid Heat Therapies:** Absolutely avoid entering **saunas**, **steam rooms**, or undergoing **hot stone massages**.
- **❌ Avoid Traditional Boreh:** Traditional **Bali** Boreh herbal wraps typically contain "warming" ingredients like ginger and cloves, which are meant to expel cold but can exacerbate heatstroke symptoms.
- **✅ Choose Cooling Treatments:** Look for wraps based on **Aloe Vera**, **Cucumber**, or **Sandalwood** (Cooling Wrap). These help physically cool the skin and replenish moisture.
- **✅ Gentle Acupressure:** Light massage on the head or neck can alleviate headaches caused by heatstroke, but ensure the pressure is very gentle.

### 3. Special Reminders for SPA in Bali

Before booking a massage in Bali, it's advisable to communicate with the therapist:

1. **Inform About Your Condition:** Clearly state that you've experienced heat exhaustion ("I have heat exhaustion") and ask them to adjust the pressure or avoid heating rituals.
2. **Change Essential Oils:** Avoid oils with a spicy sensation; opt for **Peppermint**, **Lavender**, or **Lemongrass**, which can help cool and refresh.
3. **Hydrate:** Drink plenty of water before and after the SPA. Bali SPA shops usually offer ginger tea; after heatstroke, ask for **room temperature water** or **coconut water** instead.

***

**In summary:** If you're still feverish or dizzy, rest first, drink coconut water, and even consider a doctor's [home visit service](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1197571&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F170717-24-7-doctor-on-call-bali-at-unicare-clinic-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Def8fdeaa30) for an IV Drip; once your strength returns, scheduling an **Aloe Vera Soothing Treatment** would be a better choice.
