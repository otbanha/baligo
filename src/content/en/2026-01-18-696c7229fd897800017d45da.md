## 1. Local "Cooling" Drinks

Bali has many traditional herbal drinks (called Loloh) that are highly effective for cooling down and rehydrating:

- **Loloh Cemcem (Herbal Drink):** This is Bali's most authentic cooling drink, especially popular in Ubud or Penglipuran. It’s green in color, with a sweet, sour, and slightly salty taste, made from **Cemcem leaves** (a local herb), palm sugar, cinnamon, and tamarind. Locals believe it effectively relieves "internal heat" (Panas Dalam).
- **Kelapa Muda (Fresh Coconut Water):** This is the easiest-to-find natural electrolyte. Opt for "green coconut" (Kelapa Hijau), which has lower sugar content and higher electrolytes. After drinking, ask the vendor to scrape out the coconut flesh, which has a soothing effect.

- **Jamu Kunyit Asam (Turmeric Tamarind Drink):** This bright yellow herbal drink is available in supermarkets and roadside stalls. Turmeric has anti-inflammatory properties, while tamarind is excellent for cooling the body.

## 2. Local Food Remedies

- **Bubur Kacang Hijau (Mung Bean Porridge):** This warm or iced mung bean porridge is often sold at local warungs (roadside stalls). Mung beans are widely recognized in tropical countries as a cooling food that helps detoxify the body.
- **Tamarind Candy:** If you feel slightly nauseous or dizzy, eating a tamarind candy can stimulate saliva production and relieve discomfort.

## 3. Unique Local Therapies

## 3. 特色的當地療法

- **Boreh Herbal Treatment:** While traditional Boreh focuses on warming and moisture removal, some spas offer 'Cooling Boreh' using cucumber, aloe vera, or sandalwood. Applied to overheated skin, it quickly cools and repairs sun damage.
- **Balinese Massage:** After mild heatstroke, blood circulation may worsen. Once body temperature has slightly decreased, a gentle massage can relax tense muscles (but avoid if currently experiencing high fever).

## 4. Modern Bali Travel 'Solution': IV Drip

In Bali's tourist areas (like Canggu, Seminyak, or Ubud), 'IV Therapy' has become very popular. If you're severely dehydrated and extremely fatigued, professional hospitals (such as **BIMC** or **Siloam**) even offer [home services](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1197571&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F170717-24-7-doctor-on-call-bali-at-unicare-clinic-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3Def8fdeaa30), where doctors administer IV drips containing saline, vitamin C, and electrolytes directly to you. This can significantly alleviate heatstroke symptoms within 1 hour.

### 💡 Emergency Guide

If you experience **confusion, body temperature exceeding 40°C, or inability to sweat**, this may indicate severe heatstroke (Heatstroke). Seek medical attention immediately.

## Can you get a massage or spa after heatstroke?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1120e315e89abf9baad7f4e4f333bee6.png)

Whether you can get a massage or spa after heatstroke depends on your current **physical condition** and the **type of treatment**. In Bali's tropical climate, incorrect massage techniques may worsen heatstroke symptoms, so extra caution is needed.

Here are detailed recommendations for massage and spa after heatstroke:

### 1. When Should You 'Absolutely Not' Get a Massage?

If you're in the **acute phase** of heatstroke and experiencing the following symptoms, you must avoid massage:

- **Fever or High Body Temperature:** Massage promotes blood circulation and accelerates metabolism, which can further increase body temperature and exacerbate heat exhaustion.
- **Severe Dehydration, Dizziness, and Vomiting:** When the body is extremely weak, the stimulation from a massage (even if mild) can cause blood pressure fluctuations, potentially leading to fainting.
- **Severe Sunburn:** Bali's intense sunlight often accompanies heatstroke with sunburn. Massaging sunburned skin can cause excessive friction, leading to pain or inflammation.

### 2. Post-Recovery (After Body Temperature Normalizes) Massage Recommendations

Once you've rested in a cool place, replenished fluids and electrolytes, and your body temperature has returned to normal, you can opt for a SPA session, but with the following precautions:

- **❌ Avoid Heat Therapy:** Absolutely avoid entering **saunas**, **steam rooms**, or undergoing **hot stone massages**.
- **❌ Avoid Traditional Boreh:** Traditional Balinese Boreh herbal wraps often contain warming ingredients like ginger and cloves, which are meant to dispel cold but can worsen heatstroke symptoms.
- **✅ Choose Cooling Treatments:** Look for wraps based on **Aloe Vera**, **Cucumber**, or **Sandalwood** (Cooling Wrap). These help physically cool the skin and replenish moisture.
- **✅ Gentle Acupressure:** Light massage on the head or neck can alleviate headaches caused by heatstroke, but the pressure must be kept gentle.

### 3. Special Reminders for SPA in Bali

Before booking a massage in Bali, it's advisable to communicate with the therapist:

1. **Inform About Your Condition:** Clearly tell them you've experienced heat exhaustion ("I have heat exhaustion") and ask them to adjust the pressure or avoid heating rituals.
2. **Change Essential Oils:** Avoid oils with a spicy sensation. Opt for **Peppermint**, **Lavender**, or **Lemongrass**, which also help cool the body through scent.
3. **Hydrate:** Drink plenty of water before and after the SPA. Balinese SPA shops usually offer ginger tea, but after heatstroke, ask for **room-temperature water** or **coconut water** as an alternative.
