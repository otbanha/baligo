## Bali: Where Majestic Volcanoes Meet Serene Coastlines

Located in the northeastern corner of Bali, Amed and Tulamben offer a tranquil escape far removed from the island's bustling tourist hubs. Here, you won't find the lively energy of Kuta or the artistic vibe of Ubud. Instead, these peaceful fishing villages are surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes, creating a serene paradise unlike anywhere else on the island.

![阿貢火山（Gunung Agung）  峇里島最高、最具宗教與文化意義的活火山，海拔約 3,031 公尺；上古火山殘留的 Gunung Abang，海拔約 2,151 公尺；巴杜爾火山（Gunung Batur）  是一座活火山錐，海拔約 1,717 公尺](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_38b4f9c2c400c72b9f94875f5af8aeaa.jpg)

Mount Agung (Gunung Agung), Bali's tallest and most culturally significant active volcano, stands at approximately 3,031 meters above sea level. The remnants of an ancient volcano, Gunung Abang, reaches about 2,151 meters in elevation. Mount Batur (Gunung Batur), an active volcanic cone, rises to around 1,717 meters.

https://youtube.com/shorts/Hce-BK5I-NU

From this serene black sand coastline, you can take in the breathtaking views of three of Bali's iconic volcanoes: Mount Agung, Mount Abang, and Mount Batur. When the clouds disperse, all three volcanoes come into view simultaneously, transporting you to a mythical world.

The journey from Ngurah Rai International Airport to here takes approximately 2.5 to 3.5 hours by car. Along the way, you'll pass through villages, forests, and rice terraces, making it a scenic and enriching road trip.

The most captivating aspects of Amed and Tulamben lie in their crystal-clear waters and diverse underwater ecosystems. This region is a paradise for diving enthusiasts, offering suitable spots for both seasoned divers and beginners alike.

### Jukung Sunrise/Sunset Chasing & Sea Fishing

https://youtube.com/shorts/0f-eAQ78no4

In addition to diving and snorkeling, there's one unique experience you absolutely cannot miss — taking a ride on a traditional local fishing boat called a **Jukung**. These small wooden boats with outriggers on both sides have been used by local fishermen for generations and offer the most authentic way to explore the Amed coastline.

In the early morning hours, many visitors choose to board a Jukung boat to **chase the sunrise**. As the boat slowly glides away from the shore, you'll witness the sun gradually rising from the horizon, casting golden rays across the sea. The entire world is bathed in a soft, warm orange hue, creating a moment of tranquility and grandeur that is truly unforgettable.

As evening approaches, you can experience the **Jukung Sunset Cruise**. Unlike the crisp morning air during sunrise, the sea breeze at sunset is gentle and slightly cool. As the sun slowly sinks behind the volcanoes, it paints the sky and sea in hues of pink, purple, gold, and orange, creating an utterly dreamlike scene.

If you're interested in fishing village culture, you might want to try a **sea fishing experience**. Many local fishermen take tourists out to sea to fish, offering a firsthand experience of their daily rhythm. After catching fish, you can have them cooked at a local restaurant for a truly 'freshly caught and cooked' seafood dinner.

### Sunset Point（📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing)）

Due to the unique topography, it's quite magical how Sunset Point faces directly westward.

In Amed, the most popular spot to enjoy the sunset is undoubtedly **Sunset Point Amed Bali**. Perched on a hillside, this viewing platform offers an unparalleled vantage point, showcasing the entire coastline with views stretching to distant volcanoes and quaint fishing villages. It's no wonder this spot has become a 'sunset sanctuary' in the hearts of many travelers.

As the sun slowly dips behind the volcano, the sky transforms from deep blue to hues of orange-red and soft purple-pink, reflecting beautifully on the ocean's surface, creating a dreamlike scene. The serene surroundings, free from commercial hustle, make it a perfect place to truly unwind and connect with nature.

Near the viewpoint, there are several small cafes and bars where you can sip on a cold drink while quietly admiring the sunset, enjoying a peaceful and leisurely moment. The last time Xiao Jie visited, he went to this spot. While the food was just average, the view was absolutely priceless!

If you visit Uluwatu, don't forget to reserve an evening for Sunset Point - the sunset here is absolutely worth the wait.

## Authentic Fishing Village Cuisine

Amed and Tulamben are traditional fishing villages, so fresh seafood naturally takes center stage on the dining tables here.

### 9 Famous Restaurants in This Area (📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing))

**1. Warung Agung Amed**

A cozy family-style restaurant offering a fusion of Chinese, Indonesian, and seafood dishes. Popular recommendations include the daily fresh fish, corn fritters, and fish curry, along with must-try desserts like caramelized pineapple ice cream and black rice pudding. Occasionally, the owner treats guests to live music performances!

**2. Warung Enak Amed**

A beloved local and tourist spot offering affordable dining with delicious Balinese pancakes, BBQ, and freshly grilled fish. The lava cake is a hidden gem of a dessert - highly recommended for a solo treat!

**3. Restaurant Bukit Segara**

This oceanfront restaurant specializes in daily fresh catches and diverse Asian-European fusion cuisine. While prices lean towards the higher end, the exceptional service and quality, combined with stunning views, make it a worthwhile dining investment.

**4. Galanga Amed**

A cozy Asian fusion restaurant offering Balinese, Indonesian dishes, and healthy smoothie bowls. With a relaxed atmosphere, it's a great spot for lunch, dinner, and desserts.

**5. di Lipah Warung**

A traditional seafood bistro, known for its signature dish Pepe Ikan (fish grilled in coconut leaves), and its desserts are also highly praised. While there's no scenic view, the food and warm service leave a lasting positive impression.

**6. Gusto Resto Amed**

A cozy family-style restaurant specializing in Mediterranean-Indonesian fusion cuisine. Highly recommend the fresh salads, rendang chicken, and pan-seared tuna steak - an excellent choice for group dining.

**7. Green Melon Warung**

Known for its healthy cooking style, this place offers fresh ingredients at affordable prices. Highly recommend the BBQ tuna and squid dishes, paired with Balinese salad. The adorable cone-shaped rice wrapped in banana leaves is a must-try. Cash only.

**8. Trattoria Amed**

An authentic Italian restaurant with a romantic atmosphere and excellent service. Their pizzas are extremely popular, complemented by a professional wine cellar and well-maintained restroom facilities, making it a paradise for pizza lovers.

**9. Sails Restaurant**

Boasting stunning sea views reminiscent of a Greek island, this spot is highly recommended for its banana leaf-wrapped fish, lamb patties, and frozen margaritas. The atmosphere is refreshing, the staff is friendly, and they even offer complimentary shuttle service.

## Transportation

When planning your trip to Amed, it's essential to arrange transportation in advance. Located in the northeastern corner of Bali, Amed is quite far from the airport or southern tourist areas like Kuta and Seminyak, with a driving time of approximately 2.5 to 3.5 hours. We recommend booking a **private car transfer** for convenience.

Please note that most drivers operating in southern Bali are reluctant to make one-way trips to Amed and return empty-handed, especially during peak tourist seasons when it's harder to find transportation. Therefore, the best approach is to hire a local driver from Amed itself. Not only are they more willing to provide service, but they're also familiar with the area's routes.

## Essential Things to Know Before You Go

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1c77b7d525e0ff7d6cce396d75da372e.jpg)

https://youtube.com/shorts/XS1j72uEtTY

Since Amed and Tulamben are relatively remote areas with less developed infrastructure compared to the southern tourist regions, travelers must be well-prepared before setting out:

- **Cash is king**: ATMs are scarce in the area and may not accept foreign cards. It's advisable to withdraw enough cash before hiking or diving activities.
- **Bring basic medications**: Local medical facilities are limited, so it's wise to carry simple remedies like stomach medicine, painkillers, and mosquito repellent.
- **Book transportation in advance**: If you plan to hire a private car or driver, make reservations ahead of time.
- **Limited internet access**: Signal can be unstable in some areas, so consider getting a local SIM card or portable WiFi.
- **Protect marine ecosystems**: Avoid touching coral and use reef-safe sunscreen to help preserve this precious marine environment.

## FAQ Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Amed and Tulamben suitable for beginner divers?**

Indeed, the sea conditions here are stable with gentle currents, making it particularly suitable for beginner divers. Local diving schools also offer introductory courses.

**Can I go directly from Ubud or Kuta?**

Yes, but it's recommended to hire a private car for the trip. The journey takes approximately 2.5 to 3 hours, and you can arrange stops at other attractions along the way.

**What's the best time to visit?**

The dry season (May to October) is the best time to visit, with sunny weather and crystal-clear waters perfect for water activities.

**Do local restaurants offer vegetarian options?**

Most restaurants can accommodate vegetarian diets, though options may be limited. It's advisable to inform them in advance and confirm.

**What other attractions are nearby?**

If you have some extra time, I highly recommend visiting these three stunning nearby attractions:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9422f8946b1dbb2f0592bc5416a32b91.png)

- **Lempuyang Temple (Gates of Heaven)**: Perched on the mountainside, this temple is renowned for its majestic volcanic backdrop and sacred stone gate, making it a paradise for photography enthusiasts.
- **Tirta Gangga**: Once a royal water garden, this enchanting site features stone carvings and koi fish in its pools, creating a picturesque setting perfect for leisurely strolls and photography.
- **Ujung Water Palace**: A historic palace blending European and Balinese architectural styles, surrounded by tranquil pools and lush greenery, offering a serene atmosphere steeped in history.
