## The Perfect Blend of Bali's Volcanic Majesty and Serene Coastlines

Located in the northeast corner of Bali, Amed and Tulamben offer a tranquil escape far removed from the bustling tourist hubs. Here, you won’t find the lively energy of Kuta or the artistic vibe of Ubud. Instead, you’ll discover a peaceful fishing village atmosphere surrounded by breathtaking natural landscapes.

![阿貢火山（Gunung Agung）  峇里島最高、最具宗教與文化意義的活火山，海拔約 3,031 公尺；上古火山殘留的 Gunung Abang，海拔約 2,151 公尺；巴杜爾火山（Gunung Batur）  是一座活火山錐，海拔約 1,717 公尺](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_38b4f9c2c400c72b9f94875f5af8aeaa.jpg)

Mount Agung: Bali’s tallest and most culturally significant active volcano, standing at approximately 3,031 meters; Gunung Abang: remnants of an ancient volcano, reaching about 2,151 meters; Mount Batur: an active volcanic cone, standing at around 1,717 meters.

From the serene black sand coastline, you can take in views of three of Bali’s iconic volcanoes: Mount Agung, Mount Abang, and Mount Batur. When the clouds clear, all three peaks come into view, creating a scene straight out of a mythical world.

The journey from Ngurah Rai International Airport to this area takes about 2.5 to 3.5 hours, passing through villages, forests, and rice terraces—a scenic road trip in itself.

## Dive into the Underwater World and Dance with Sunrise and Sunset

What makes Amed and Tulamben truly captivating is their crystal-clear waters and diverse marine ecosystems. This is a paradise for divers, whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner just starting out.

### Scuba Diving in Tulamben

The USAT Liberty Wreck in Tulamben is one of the world's most renowned dive sites. With moderate depth and excellent visibility, you can dance alongside colorful tropical fish while admiring this historic wreck that has been embraced by the ocean.

### Jukung Sunrise/Sunset Chasing and Sea Fishing

Beyond diving and snorkeling, there's one unique experience you absolutely can't miss—sailing on a traditional local fishing boat called **Jukung**. These small wooden boats with outriggers have been used by local fishermen for generations and are the most authentic way to explore Amed's coastline.

In the early morning, many visitors choose to board a Jukung to **chase the sunrise**. As the boat slowly moves away from the shore, you'll witness the sun rising from the horizon, casting golden light over the sea and painting the world in soft, warm hues of orange. The tranquility and grandeur of that moment are unforgettable.

As evening approaches, you can experience the **Jukung Sunset Cruise**. Unlike the fresh morning air, the sea breeze at sunset is gentle with a hint of coolness. As the sun slowly sinks behind the volcano, it paints the sky and sea in shades of pink, purple, gold, and orange, creating an incredibly dreamy scene.

If you're interested in fishing village culture, you can also opt for the **Sea Fishing Experience**. Many local fishermen take tourists out to sea to fish, allowing you to experience their daily rhythm firsthand. After catching fish, local restaurants can prepare them for you, offering a truly 'catch-and-cook' seafood dinner.

In Amed, the most popular spot to enjoy the sunset is undoubtedly **Sunset Point Amed Bali**. This hillside viewing platform offers an unparalleled vantage point, providing a panoramic view of the coastline and distant views of the volcano and fishing village. It's a 'sunset sanctuary' for many travelers.

As the sun slowly descends behind the volcano, the sky transitions from deep blue to shades of orange and pink, reflecting beautifully on the sea. The serene surroundings, free from commercial distractions, make it a place where you can truly connect with nature.

Near the viewing platform, there are a few small cafes and bars where you can sip on a cold drink while quietly admiring the sunset, enjoying a peaceful and leisurely moment. The last time I visited, I went [here](https://www.sunsetpointamedbali.com/). The food was average, but the view was priceless!

If you visit Amed, don't forget to dedicate an evening to Sunset Point. The sunset here is definitely worth the wait.

## Fishing Village Cuisine

Amed and Tulamben are traditional fishing villages, so fresh seafood naturally takes center stage on the dining table here.

### 9 Popular Restaurants in the Area (📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing))

**1. Warung Agung Amed**

A cozy family-style restaurant offering a fusion of Chinese, Indonesian, and seafood dishes. Popular recommendations include the daily fresh fish, corn fritters, and fish curry. Don’t miss the caramelized pineapple ice cream and black rice pudding for dessert. Sometimes, the owner even performs live music!

**2. Warung Enak Amed**

A budget-friendly spot loved by both locals and tourists, serving delicious Balinese pancakes, BBQ, and freshly grilled fish. The Lava Cake is a hidden gem of a dessert—perfect for indulging solo!

**3. Restaurant Bukit Segara**

A seaside restaurant specializing in daily fresh catches and a variety of Asian-European fusion dishes. While prices are on the higher side, the exceptional service, quality, and stunning views make it a worthwhile dining experience.

**4. Galanga Amed**

A small and charming Asian fusion restaurant offering Balinese, Indonesian, and healthy smoothie bowl options. With its relaxed atmosphere, it’s a great spot for lunch, dinner, or dessert.

**5. di Lipah Warung**

A traditional seafood bistro, their signature dish is Pepe Ikan (fish grilled in banana leaves), and their desserts are highly praised. While there's no scenic view, the food and warm service leave a lasting impression.

**6. Gusto Resto Amed**

A cozy family-style restaurant specializing in Mediterranean-Indonesian fusion cuisine. Highly recommend their fresh salads, rendang chicken, and pan-seared tuna steak—a great choice for group dining.

**7. Green Melon Warung**

Known for healthy cooking, using fresh ingredients at affordable prices. Must-tries include BBQ tuna and squid dishes, paired with Balinese salad, and their adorable cone-shaped rice wrapped in banana leaves. Cash only.

**8. Trattoria Amed**

An authentic Italian restaurant with a romantic ambiance and excellent service. Their pizzas are a crowd favorite, and with a professional wine cellar and well-maintained facilities, it's a paradise for pizza lovers.

**9. Sails Restaurant**

Boasting stunning sea views, it feels like a Greek island getaway. Must-try dishes include fish wrapped in banana leaves, lamb patties, and frozen margaritas. The atmosphere is refreshing, the staff is friendly, and they offer complimentary shuttle service.

### **Special Recommendation: Warung Bobo**

This restaurant is a hidden gem recommended by locals. While unassuming in appearance, the dishes are truly impressive. Specializing in refined Indonesian-Asian fusion cuisine, each plate is thoughtfully presented with distinct layers of flavor while maintaining traditional essence.

From seared tuna to Balinese-style fried rice and grilled chicken with special sauce, every dish is so delicious that you'll want to return. Surprisingly, the prices are very affordable, offering excellent value for money. It's the kind of place you'll want to recommend to friends after just one visit.

### **Café Recommendation: Rimba Café**

Rimba Café is a rare find in Amed, combining natural aesthetics with creative light meals. Surrounded by greenery, the atmosphere is fresh and relaxing. Known for its handmade desserts, smoothie bowls, coffee, and healthy light meals, it's a favorite among travelers and yoga enthusiasts.

The coffee here rivals city-level quality, paired with homemade baked goods like banana cake or coconut pudding that will make you a regular. They also offer vegetarian and plant-based options, making it an ideal spot for brunch or an afternoon break.

If you're looking for a place in Amed to slow down and enjoy a good cup of coffee, **Rimba Café** is an excellent choice.

### **Fine Dining Recommendation: Dining Experience at Asmat Restaurant**

Dining at **Asmat Restaurant (Asmat Restaurant at Mathis Lodge Amed)**, located on the hillside of Amed, is more than just a meal—it's a feast for both the eyes and the palate. Situated between the mountains and the coast, the restaurant offers breathtaking views, making it a dream location for dates and special occasions. The exclusive romantic dinner set allows you to savor exquisite Western cuisine and handcrafted cocktails while immersed in the stunning sunset and coastal scenery, each bite an extension of love.

In the morning, you can enjoy an unparalleled **floating tray breakfast** by the infinity pool, starting your day leisurely under the tropical sun. For those seeking a private dining experience, you can book a **grill set menu** for two, where the chef prepares fresh ingredients on-site, accompanied by cool evening breezes and starlit skies, creating unforgettable nights in this tropical paradise. Here, it's not just about great food but also a deep connection with nature and each other.

## Transportation

Transportation to Amed requires advance planning as it's located in the northeastern corner of Bali, about 2.5 to 3.5 hours drive from the airport or southern tourist areas like Kuta and Seminyak. It's recommended to book a private car transfer directly.

Special reminder: Most drivers operating in the south are reluctant to make one-way trips to Amed and return empty, especially during peak tourist seasons. Therefore, the best approach is to find a local private car driver from Amed, who is more willing to serve and familiar with the area.

## Essential Pre-Trip Notes

Since Amed and Tulamben are relatively remote with less developed infrastructure compared to southern tourist areas, travelers must prepare well before departure:

- Cash is king: Few ATMs in the area, and not all support foreign cards. It's advisable to withdraw enough cash before hiking or diving activities.
- Bring basic medications: Local medical facilities are limited; carry simple stomach medicine, painkillers, mosquito repellent, etc.
- Arrange transportation in advance: If you need a private car or driver, it's best to book early.
- Limited internet: Some areas have unstable signals; consider preparing a SIM card or portable WiFi.
- Protect marine ecology: Do not touch corals and use reef-safe sunscreen products, treating this precious ocean with kindness.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Amed and Tulamben suitable for beginner divers?**

Yes, the sea conditions here are stable with gentle currents, making it particularly suitable for diving beginners. Local dive schools also offer introductory courses.

**Can I go directly from Ubud or Kuta?**

Yes, but we recommend hiring a private car. The journey takes about 2.5 to 3 hours, and you can arrange stops at other attractions along the way.

**What's the best time to visit?**

The dry season (May to October) is the best time to visit, with sunny weather and clear waters perfect for water activities.

**Do local restaurants offer vegetarian options?**

Most restaurants can provide vegetarian options, but choices are limited. We recommend informing them in advance and confirming.

**What other attractions are nearby?**

If you have extra time, I highly recommend visiting these three stunning attractions:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ed589a98d59d6d3d1de01faf3eb0b822.jpg)

- **Lempuyang Temple**: Perched on the mountainside, this temple is renowned for its majestic volcanic backdrop and sacred stone gates, making it a paradise for photography enthusiasts.
- **Tirta Gangga**: A former royal water garden, its stone sculptures and koi fish create a mesmerizing scene, perfect for leisurely strolls and photography.
- **Ujung Water Palace**: An ancient palace blending European and Balinese architectural styles, surrounded by pools and lush greenery, offering a serene and historically rich atmosphere.
