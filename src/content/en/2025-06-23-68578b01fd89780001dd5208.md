## The Perfect Blend of Bali's Volcanic Majesty and Serene Coastline

Located in Bali's northeast corner, Amed and Tulamben offer a world away from the bustling tourist areas. Here, you won't find the lively atmosphere of Kuta or the artistic vibe of Ubud, but instead, a peaceful fishing village ambiance surrounded by stunning natural landscapes.

![阿貢火山（Gunung Agung）  峇里島最高、最具宗教與文化意義的活火山，海拔約 3,031 公尺；上古火山殘留的 Gunung Abang，海拔約 2,151 公尺；巴杜爾火山（Gunung Batur）  是一座活火山錐，海拔約 1,717 公尺](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_38b4f9c2c400c72b9f94875f5af8aeaa.jpg)

Mount Agung: Bali's tallest and most culturally significant active volcano, standing at 3,031 meters; Mount Abang: A remnant of an ancient volcano at 2,151 meters; Mount Batur: An active volcanic cone at 1,717 meters.

https://youtube.com/shorts/Hce-BK5I-NU

From this tranquil black sand coastline, you can take in views of three of Bali's famous volcanoes: Mount Agung, Mount Abang, and Mount Batur. When the clouds part, all three volcanoes come into view simultaneously, creating a scene straight out of a mythical world.

The journey from Ngurah Rai International Airport takes approximately 2.5 to 3.5 hours, passing through villages, forests, and rice terraces - a scenic road trip in itself.

## Dive into the Underwater World, Dancing with Sunrise and Sunset

The most captivating aspects of Amed and Tulamben are undoubtedly their crystal-clear waters and diverse underwater ecosystems. This is a paradise for diving enthusiasts, whether you're a seasoned diver or a beginner just starting out, you'll find waters that suit your level.

### Scuba Diving in Tulamben

The USAT Liberty Wreck in Tulamben is one of the world's renowned diving sites. With moderate depth and excellent visibility, you can dance alongside colorful tropical fish while admiring this historical wreck that has been embraced by the ocean.

### Jukung: Chasing Sunrises, Sunsets, and Fishing Adventures

https://youtube.com/shorts/0f-eAQ78no4

Apart from diving and snorkeling, there’s one unique experience you absolutely can’t miss — setting sail on a traditional **Jukung** boat. These small wooden boats with dual outriggers have been used by local fishermen for generations and are the most authentic way to explore the Amed coastline.

In the early morning, many travelers choose to board a Jukung and **chase the sunrise**. As the boat slowly glides away from the shore, you’ll witness the sun rising above the horizon, casting golden light across the sea. The world is bathed in soft, warm hues of orange, creating a moment of tranquility and awe that’s unforgettable.

In the evening, you can experience a **Jukung sunset cruise**. Unlike the crisp morning air, the sea breeze at sunset is gentle and slightly cool. As the sun slowly sinks behind the volcano, it paints the sky and sea in shades of pink, purple, and gold, creating a truly magical scene.

If you’re interested in fishing village culture, you can also opt for a **fishing adventure**. Many local fishermen take visitors out to sea, allowing you to experience their daily rhythm firsthand. After catching fish, you can have them cooked at a local restaurant for a truly fresh "catch-to-table" seafood dinner.

### Sunset Point (📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing))

![由於地形的關係，很神奇的是Sunset Point這個角度面向西邊](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5b312da0651b1112f4c52963c74fa9ee.jpg)

Due to the unique terrain, Sunset Point offers a magical westward-facing view.

In Amed, the most popular spot to watch the sunset is undoubtedly **Sunset Point Amed Bali**. This hillside viewpoint offers an unparalleled vantage point, overlooking the entire coastline and offering distant views of the volcano and small fishing villages. It’s a "sunset sanctuary" in the hearts of many travelers.

As the sun slowly dips behind the volcano, the sky transforms from deep blue to shades of orange, red, and pink, reflecting beautifully on the sea. The serene surroundings, free from commercial distractions, make this a place where you can truly connect with nature and find peace.

Near the viewpoint, there are several small cafes and bars where you can sip on a cold drink while quietly admiring the sunset, enjoying a peaceful and leisurely moment. The last time Xiao Jie visited, he went [here](https://www.sunsetpointamedbali.com/). The food was average, but the view was priceless!

If you come to Amed, don't forget to save an evening for Sunset Point—the sunset here is absolutely worth the wait.

## Village-Style Seafood Cuisine

Amed and Tulamben are traditional fishing villages, so fresh seafood naturally takes center stage on the dining tables here.

### 9 Famous Restaurants in the Area (📍[Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/d/u/0/edit?mid=1IrHQ5679D68HUAXlXWyqhgnvITAONfE&usp=sharing))

**1. Warung Agung Amed**

A cozy family-style restaurant offering a fusion of Chinese, Indonesian, and seafood dishes. Popular recommendations include the daily fresh fish, corn fritters, and fish curry, along with must-try desserts like caramel pineapple ice cream and black rice pudding. Sometimes, the owner even performs live music!

**2. Warung Enak Amed**

A budget-friendly restaurant loved by both locals and tourists, serving delicious Balinese pancakes, BBQ, and freshly grilled fish. The lava cake is a hidden gem of a dessert—perfect for indulging solo!

**3. Restaurant Bukit Segara**

A seaside restaurant specializing in daily fresh catches and diverse Asian-European cuisine. While prices are mid-to-high range, the exceptional service, quality, and stunning views make it a worthwhile dining investment.

**4. Galanga Amed**

A cozy and intimate Asian fusion restaurant offering Balinese, Indonesian dishes, and healthy smoothie bowls. With a relaxed atmosphere, it's a great spot for lunch, dinner, or dessert.

**5. di Lipah Warung**

A traditional seafood eatery known for its signature Pepe Ikan (fish grilled in coconut leaves) and highly praised desserts. While lacking scenic views, the delicious food and warm service leave a lasting impression.

**6. Gusto Resto Amed**

A comfortable family-style restaurant featuring Mediterranean-Indonesian fusion cuisine. Recommended dishes include fresh salads, rendang chicken, and pan-seared tuna steak, making it an excellent choice for group dining.

**7. Green Melon Warung**

Known for its healthy cooking, this spot offers fresh ingredients at affordable prices. Must-tries include BBQ tuna and squid dishes paired with Balinese salad, and the adorable banana leaf cone-shaped rice. Cash only.

**8. Trattoria Amed**

An authentic Italian restaurant with a romantic atmosphere and excellent service. Their pizzas are incredibly popular, complemented by a professional wine cellar and well-maintained restroom facilities, making it a paradise for pizza lovers.

**9. Sails Restaurant**

Boasting stunning sea views, it feels like a Greek island getaway. Highly recommend the fish wrapped in banana leaves, lamb patties, and frozen margaritas. The atmosphere is refreshing, the staff is friendly, and they even offer complimentary shuttle service.

### **Café Recommendation: Rimba Café**

Rimba Café is a rare find that combines natural aesthetics with creative light fare. Surrounded by greenery, the atmosphere is fresh and relaxing. Known for its handmade desserts, smoothie bowls, coffee, and healthy light meals, it's a favorite among travelers and yoga enthusiasts.

The coffee here rivals city-level quality, paired with homemade baked goods like banana cake or coconut milk pudding, making it an instant favorite. They also offer vegetarian and plant-based options, making it an ideal spot for brunch or a relaxing afternoon.

If you're looking for a place in Amed to slow down and enjoy a good cup of coffee, **Rimba Café** is definitely a great choice.

## Transportation

Planning transportation to Amed in advance is recommended, as Amed is located in the northeastern corner of Bali, far from the airport or southern tourist areas (like Kuta and Seminyak), requiring a drive of about 2.5 to 3.5 hours. It’s best to book a **private car transfer**.

A special reminder: **Most drivers operating in the south are reluctant to make a one-way trip to Amed and return empty-handed**, especially during peak tourist seasons. ## Essential Pre-Trip Notes

https://youtube.com/shorts/XS1j72uEtTY

Since Amed and Tulamben are relatively remote, their infrastructure isn't as developed as the southern tourist areas. Therefore, travelers should prepare well before heading out:

- **Cash is king**: ATMs are scarce in the area, and foreign cards may not always work. It's advisable to withdraw enough cash before hiking or diving activities.
- **Bring basic medications**: Local medical facilities are limited, so carry essentials like stomach medicine, painkillers, and insect repellent.
- **Book transportation in advance**: If you need a private car or driver, it's best to arrange it ahead of time.
- **Limited internet**: Signal can be unstable in some areas, so consider getting a SIM card or portable WiFi.
- **Protect marine life**: Avoid touching coral and use reef-safe sunscreen to treat this precious ocean with care.

## FAQ

**Are Amed and Tulamben suitable for beginner divers?**

Yes, the sea conditions here are stable with gentle currents, making it particularly suitable for diving beginners. Local dive schools also offer introductory courses.

**Can I go directly from Ubud or Kuta?**

Yes, but it's recommended to hire a private car. The journey takes about 2.5 to 3 hours, and you can arrange stops at other attractions along the way.

**What is the best time to visit?**

The dry season (May to October) is the best time to visit, with sunny weather and clear waters perfect for water activities.

**Do local restaurants offer vegetarian options?**

Most restaurants can provide vegetarian options, but the choices are limited. It's advisable to inform them in advance and confirm.

**What other attractions are nearby?**

If you have more time, we recommend visiting these three stunning attractions in the surrounding area:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ed589a98d59d6d3d1de01faf3eb0b822.jpg)

- **Lempuyang Temple (Gates of Heaven)**: Located on the mountainside, famous for its majestic volcano backdrop and sacred stone gates, it's a paradise for photography enthusiasts.
- **Tirta Gangga**: A former royal water garden, the stone sculptures and koi fish in the pools create a mesmerizing scene, making it an excellent spot for strolling and photography.
- **Ujung Water Palace**: An ancient palace blending European and Balinese styles, surrounded by pools and greenery, offering a tranquil and historically rich atmosphere.
