### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Law Enforcement & Long-term Development

- **Police Bust International Online Gambling Ring**: Bali police held a press conference in Denpasar yesterday (4/29), announcing the dismantling of a cross-border online gambling organization linked to Cambodia. A large number of computer servers and mobile phones were seized on-site. Additionally, 35 Indian nationals involved in the case have been formally handed over to prosecutors, demonstrating the authorities' determination to combat transnational crime.
- **Latest Progress on Metro (LRT/MRT) Construction**: As Bali's most transformative project to date, the first phase of the "Bali City Metro" (Airport—Kuta—Canggu) is actively underway. Currently, **tunnel boring machines (TBM) are fully operational**, and to comply with cultural and religious regulations, the rail system is being excavated 30 meters underground, aiming for completion by 2028 to alleviate traffic congestion in the south.
- **North Bali Airport Reinstated as National Priority**: The central government has reaffirmed its commitment to prioritizing the new airport project in Buleleng, aiming to redistribute tourist traffic to the north and address the imbalance caused by over-tourism in the south.

### 📄 Topics of Interest for Tourists: Visa Extensions & Entry Checks

- **New "Mandatory Assignment" System for Visa Extensions**: The Immigration Office reminded today (4/30) that visa extensions now require in-person "hybrid applications." The key point is that tourists **cannot choose their processing office**; the system will assign one based on their residential address. For example, tourists staying near Tanah Lot will be assigned to the newly established **Tabanan Immigration Office**, while those on islands like Nusa Penida will need to go to **Klungkung**.

Those applying for a 60-day C1 visa will need proof of financial means (over USD 2,000).
