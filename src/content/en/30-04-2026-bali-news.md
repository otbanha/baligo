## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Law Enforcement and Long-term Development

- **Police Bust Cross-border Online Gambling Ring**: Bali police held a press conference in Denpasar yesterday (4/29), announcing the dismantling of a cross-border online gambling criminal organization linked to Cambodia. Authorities seized a large number of computer servers and mobile phones at the scene. Meanwhile, 35 Indian nationals involved in the case have been formally handed over to the prosecutor's office, demonstrating a firm commitment to combating transnational crime.
- **LRT/MRT Project Latest Progress**: As the most transformative project in Bali's history, the first phase of the 'Bali Urban Metro' (Airport—Kuta—Canggu) is advancing steadily. **Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs) are now fully operational**. To comply with cultural and religious regulations, the rail system is being excavated 30 meters underground, with a target completion date of 2028 to alleviate southern traffic congestion.
- **North Bali Airport Reinstated as National Priority**: The central government has reaffirmed the new airport project in Buleleng as a priority, aiming to address the current imbalance of overtourism in the south by dispersing visitor traffic to the north.

## 📄 Tourist Hot Topics: Visa Extensions and Entry Checks

- **New 'Mandatory Assignment' System for Visa Extensions**: The immigration office reminded today (4/30) that visa extensions now require an in-person 'hybrid application'. The key point is that **tourists cannot choose their own office**; the system will assign one based on your residential address. For example, tourists staying near Tanah Lot will be assigned to the newly established **Tabanan Immigration Office**, while those on outlying islands (like Nusa Penida) must go to **Klungkung**.
