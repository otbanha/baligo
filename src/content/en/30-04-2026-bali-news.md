### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Law Enforcement and Long-term Development

- **Police Bust International Online Gambling Ring**: Bali police held a press conference in Denpasar yesterday (4/29), announcing the dismantling of a cross-border online gambling organization linked to Cambodia. A large number of computer servers and mobile phones were seized on-site. Additionally, 35 Indian nationals involved in the case have been formally handed over to prosecutors, demonstrating the authorities' determination to combat transnational crime.
- **Latest Progress on Metro (LRT/MRT) Construction**: As Bali's most transformative project to date, the first phase of the "Bali City Metro" (Airport—Kuta—Canggu) is progressing actively. Currently, **Tunnel Boring Machines (TBM) are fully operational**, and to comply with cultural and religious regulations, the rail system is being excavated 30 meters underground, aiming for completion in 2028 to alleviate traffic congestion in the south.
- **North Bali Airport Reinstated as National Strategy**: The central government has reaffirmed its priority on the new airport project in Buleleng, aiming to redistribute tourist flow to the north and address the imbalance caused by over-saturation in the south.

### 📄 Visitor Concerns: Visa Extensions and Entry Checks

- **New "Mandatory Assignment" System for Visa Extensions**: The Immigration Office reminded today (4/30) that visa extensions now require in-person "hybrid applications." The key point is that visitors **cannot choose their processing office**; the system will assign one based on their residential address. For example, visitors near Tanah Lot will be assigned to the newly established **Tabanan Immigration Office**, while those on islands like Nusa Penida will need to go to **Klungkung**.
