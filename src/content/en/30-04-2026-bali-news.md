### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Law Enforcement & Long-term Development

- **Police Bust International Online Gambling Ring**: Bali police held a press conference in Denpasar yesterday (4/29), announcing the dismantling of a cross-border online gambling organization linked to Cambodia. A large number of computer servers and mobile phones were seized at the scene. Additionally, 35 Indian nationals involved in the case have been formally handed over to prosecutors, demonstrating the determination to combat transnational crime.
- **Latest Progress on Metro (LRT/MRT) Construction**: As Bali's most transformative project to date, the first phase of the "Bali City Metro" (Airport—Kuta—Canggu) is actively underway. Currently, **tunnel boring machines (TBM) are fully operational**, and to comply with cultural and religious regulations, the track system is being excavated 30 meters underground, aiming for completion in 2028 to alleviate southern traffic congestion.
- **North Bali Airport Reinstated as National Strategy**: The central government has reaffirmed the priority of the new airport project in Buleleng, aiming to redistribute tourist flow to the north and address the imbalance caused by over-tourism in the south.

### 📄 Topics of Concern for Tourists: Visa Extensions & Entry Checks

- **New "Mandatory Assignment" System for Visa Extensions**: The Immigration Bureau reminded today (4/30) that visa extensions now require a "hybrid application" process in person. The key point is that tourists **cannot choose their own office**, as the system will assign one based on their residential address. For example, tourists staying near Tanah Lot will be assigned to the newly established **Tabanan Immigration Office**, while those on islands like Nusa Penida will need to go to **Klungkung**.

Those applying for a 60-day C1 visa need proof of financial means (over USD 2,000)**.
