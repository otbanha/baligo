I want to give back to the community by sharing my experience. In March, I embarked on an 8-day solo trip to Bali. Since there isn't much information online about women traveling alone in Bali, I decided to share my itinerary and insights from this solo journey.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6152213cb48cec104b8463302a71617d.jpg)

In fact, you can see many foreign women traveling alone in Bali. Based on my experience, as long as you maintain some basic precautions, such as not getting into strangers' cars and avoiding eye contact or conversation with people who harass women on the streets, most locals are warm, friendly, and safe.

Let me share my 8-day solo travel itinerary in Bali:

## **Saturday, March 17**

**09:15-14:45** Arrived in Bali by flight. I had pre-booked airport transfer service on Klook, and the driver waited patiently at the airport for 2 hours. After getting in the car, I asked the driver to take me to a money changer first, so I went directly to BMC Money Changer. Then, I headed to [The Eight](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=23144116) resort where I stayed for the night.

> Further reading: [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/bali-currency-exchange-guide/)

Went to a nearby supermarket to buy mosquito repellent, adapters, sunglasses, and summer swimwear. I bought a set of summer swimwear at [niconico swimwear](https://niconicoswimwear.com/). The staff there were friendly and didn't push for sales, and they had a wide variety of styles.

On Nusa Penida, you can only hire a private car. I booked my ferry ticket + private car on Klook and was very satisfied overall. The car company contacted me in advance to confirm the itinerary and helped communicate with the driver. After disembarking, the driver met me and even helped carry my luggage. Throughout the trip, he was very attentive as a guide. When I wanted to climb down to Kelingking Beach, he followed me all the way down—I’m truly grateful to him. At each stop, he took photos and reported back to the company, which made me, a solo female traveler, feel much safer. I’m really thankful for the driver’s help, from carrying luggage to accompanying me for photos—he was very responsible.

## **Final Thoughts**

In summary, I found Bali to be an ideal destination for solo travel and photography. The spacious attractions allowed me to set up my tripod freely and take selfies using a remote control. Except for airport transfers and the Nusa Penida car hire, almost all my beautiful photos were taken by myself with a tripod. Even in scenarios where setting up a tripod wasn't possible, like uneven floors or waterfalls, locals often offered to take photos at a reasonable price, which was very thoughtful.

Bali's natural scenery is breathtaking! This solo trip gave me an unforgettable experience and allowed me to witness the warm hospitality of the locals. Solo travel isn't scary; instead, it lets you fully immerse yourself in nature and exotic atmospheres. I would love to visit this dreamy island again if given the chance! Looking forward to sharing more travel stories with everyone.
