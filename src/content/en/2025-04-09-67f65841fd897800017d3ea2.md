Many friends planning their Bali trip panic when they see weather forecasts showing "⚡ Thunderstorms ☔ 60% chance of rain," exclaiming, "Oh no, did I choose the wrong time to visit Bali? Will my entire vacation be ruined?"

Don't worry—this kind of weather is very common in Bali, but it doesn't mean you'll be stuck in the rain all day. Today, let’s break down how to correctly interpret Bali’s weather forecasts and address some super common misconceptions!

![看到類似這樣的天氣先不要昏倒! 請看下面的解釋](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_686bf4324352ed644f971ec89d46d424.jpg)

Don’t faint when you see weather like this! Check out the explanation below.

---

## ☁ Misconception 1: 60% Chance of Rain = It Will Rain 60% of the Day?

**Wrong:** Many people see "60% chance of rain" and assume it means "it will rain 60% of the day," but this is a misunderstanding.

👉 **Correct Interpretation:** A 60% chance of rain means there’s a 60% probability that it will rain at some point in the forecasted area. In simpler terms, it might just be a brief afternoon thunderstorm that comes and goes quickly, without ruining your entire day.

---

## 🌧 Misconception 2: Daily Thunderstorms Mean It Will Pour All Day?

**Wrong:** Bali’s weather forecasts often show thunderstorms or rain, making it seem like the whole week will be wet and gloomy. However, this is another common misconception in tropical regions.

👉 **Understand this: Afternoon showers are common in Bali, especially during the rainy season (approximately November to March each year)**, but these rains usually come and go quickly, often lasting just 30 minutes to an hour, followed by sunshine and even rainbows 🌈!

📌 **Fun fact: In meteorology, if 'even a single drop of rain falls during a period,' the forecast will show 'rain' or 'thunderstorms.'**

This means that even if it only rains for a brief minute during the day, the weather icon will still display a thunderstorm symbol, which can easily mislead travelers!

👉 So don’t cancel beach trips, snorkeling, or outdoor activities just based on the icon—check the hourly forecast for more accuracy!

## 📸 Pro tip: Make good use of the weather forecast timeline

As shown in this screenshot, sometimes forecasts include 'every three hours' weather updates, allowing travelers to plan accordingly:

- Which time periods might have rain (e.g., 2 PM to 5 PM)
- Which time periods are ideal for outdoor activities or photography (e.g., 8 AM to noon)

👉 **Tip: Schedule beach trips and outdoor activities for the morning!**

## 🌴 Quick summary: Don’t let weather icons scare you!

Bali's weather changes rapidly, and the forecasts shown on mobile apps tend to be overly cautious. Don't let 'thunderstorm icons filling the screen' scare you away from planning your trip. Instead, learn to:

✅ Check the probability of rain

✅ Check the timing of rainfall

✅ Prepare a lightweight raincoat & maintain an optimistic attitude 😄
