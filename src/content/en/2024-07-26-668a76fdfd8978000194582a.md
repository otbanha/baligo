### June 19 - June 23: Just wrapped up a relaxing and enjoyable trip to Bali

Since we decided on the departure date quite late, we initially planned to join a tour group with a travel agency. However, in the end, we only managed to book flights and hotels for a free-and-easy trip. For me, finding attractions, restaurants, and planning the itinerary wasn’t too difficult, especially with the enthusiastic sharing of experiences from everyone in this group. The most important thing, I felt, was finding a reliable private driver.

### Finding a Private Driver

After some research, I came across a recommendation for Mang Loco in the group. He said we could call him 'Ou Ei,' which means 'Black Ei' in Taiwanese. The key point is that he speaks Chinese! Having worked in Taiwan before, he can basically communicate in Chinese without any issues. Although English is usable in Bali, I often struggle to understand English with an Indonesian accent, so being able to communicate directly in Chinese was incredibly convenient!

### Itinerary Planning and Communication

I drafted a rough itinerary myself and then asked Ou Ei to help refine it. He also kindly offered some suggestions for the schedule. Throughout the trip, communication was smooth. He not only understood our casual chats but could also joke around with us—this is the advantage of hiring a Chinese-speaking driver! He also helped us solve some unexpected issues, like changing restaurant reservations, last-minute massage requests, and even haggling with us. ## Trip Insights and Recommendations

### **Beverage Choices**

In Bali, it's best to avoid trying drinks from non-supermarket, non-major restaurant, or non-chain establishments. During this trip, a friend of ours drank a beverage at a local eatery and ended up with diarrhea for two days. While the local drinks may look tempting, it's safer to stick to reliable options to avoid getting sick during your trip. Additionally, carrying some medication for stomach issues is a good idea, just in case.

> [Bali Travel Health Tips: A Guide to Drinking Water and Diarrhea in Bali](/blog/2024-01-27-65b32e1ffd8978000170519b/)

### **Bring Extra Undergarments**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b0f8826462eff2a98579a54724fb9ffa.png)

Bali is extremely humid, and if you're engaging in water activities, you'll likely need to change clothes twice a day. I initially thought I could wash and air-dry my clothes, but due to the high humidity, they don't dry completely. It's advisable to bring quick-dry clothing, especially undergarments, to ensure you have clean and comfortable clothes every day.

### **Traffic in Kuta**

Traffic in Kuta is notoriously congested. If you're staying in Kuta, make sure to allocate extra time for traffic delays. The roads are narrow, and there are many vehicles, leading to daily traffic jams. If your itinerary is tight, consider changing accommodations every two days to avoid spending too much time on the road in the evenings. Additionally, try to plan activities that involve walking or cycling to bypass the heavy traffic.

### Accommodation

During this trip to Bali, we stayed at the Grand Istana Rama Hotel for four nights. This resort is right next to the Beachwalk Shopping Center, so every day when we returned to the hotel, as long as the mall was still open, we went shopping XD.

Just across the street is Kuta Beach, with vibrant nightlife nearby. Although this isn't the newest hotel, its rooms are spacious and clean, and the facilities are well-equipped. The staff are friendly and welcoming, making us feel right at home. Especially the seafood dinners and live band performances were fantastic, allowing us to enjoy some delightful evenings. Our stay here was wonderful, offering great value for money!

### **Currency Exchange**

There are many currency exchange places in Bali. You can ask your guide or driver to take you there, which is safer and more reliable. Since exchanging money is very convenient, there's no need to exchange too much at once. For this trip, because I had collected some shared funds in advance, I only exchanged $200 for my five-day personal expenses, and I still had some left. It's advisable to carry some USD with you for emergencies, which can also reduce the number of times you need to exchange money locally.

### **Rafting Experience**

Before starting the rafting activity in Bali, you need to walk a long mountain path (downstairs) to reach the river. On the way back, you have to climb a slope with stairs. Although the return stairs are shorter, it can still be a bit challenging for those who are not physically fit. If you're bringing elderly or children, be sure to assess their physical condition. However, the rafting process is truly thrilling and fun, making it an unforgettable experience, so I highly recommend trying it.
