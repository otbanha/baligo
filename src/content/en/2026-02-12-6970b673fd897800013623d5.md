From January 11 to 17, 2026, our family embarked on a laughter-filled trip to Bali. Before departure, many friends warned me about the rainy season and traffic congestion. Now that the journey is over, it's time to share my real experiences and feedback.

First, regarding the **traffic congestion** everyone is concerned about, my conclusion is: **It’s much better than expected!** With a large group, we opted for a 14-seater minibus. Throughout the trip, we only encountered slow traffic once for about two hours when entering tourist-heavy areas. The rest of the time, we moved smoothly. This made me realize that **choosing private transfers and planning routes wisely** can effectively avoid most traffic nightmares.

![暴雨來襲，趕快開車離開海邊！！經過一些低窪地區，路面都變成河流](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f4f9f96d6c813ec68ba40960047d0ffa.jpg)

When the rainstorm hit, we quickly drove away from the beach! Passing through some low-lying areas, the roads turned into rivers.

Next, let’s talk about the **challenges and surprises of the rainy season**. Yes, during our seven-day trip, we experienced rain on five days intermittently. However, the rainy season isn’t all inconvenience—it brought several benefits: cooler and more comfortable temperatures, lush greenery, and fewer tourists, avoiding crowded scenes. Of course, sudden downpours required quick adaptation (like the scene in the attached photo where we 'fled' from the beach during a flood, now just a funny memory 🤣🤣). Interestingly, weather forecasts seem unreliable. For example, in Kuta, despite receiving a **storm warning**, we mostly enjoyed sunny days during our two-day stay, with only occasional evening showers. This taught us that in Bali, rather than worrying about the weather, it’s better to keep the itinerary flexible. Our strategy was to always have **rainy day alternatives** ready, such as indoor attractions, museums, galleries, or markets, letting the sound of rain become a soothing background to our journey.

> **Quick Reference:** [Bali Rainy Season: What to Do When It Rains in Bali? 60 Rainy Day Alternatives](/blog/2024-01-06-6598c6fffd89780001047d76/)

Of course, there were some adjustments needed during this trip: city traffic isn’t pedestrian-friendly, crossing streets felt like playing a real-life 'Frogger'; and the countless zeros on Indonesian Rupiah notes nearly overheated my brain’s CPU during currency conversions ♨️

## Sharing Our Itinerary:

### **Day 1: Arrival in Ubud, A Relaxing Start at Our Villa**

After a long flight, we smoothly landed in Bali. The immigration process was quicker than expected, and after collecting our luggage, we met up with our pre-booked private driver. As we drove away from the airport's hustle and bustle, the scenery gradually shifted from modern buildings to lush fields and intricate shrines, reminding us that we had truly arrived on the "Island of the Gods."

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_872fd38d85050291bdc0602b42d36dd9.jpg)

Upon arriving at our booked **Villa** in Ubud, the fatigue of the journey was instantly washed away by the view before us. Nestled amidst greenery, the villa featured a private pool and a spacious gazebo. As night fell, accompanied by the natural symphony of chirping insects and croaking frogs, we concluded our relaxing first day, recharging for the adventures ahead.

### **Day 4: Exploring the Rustic Villages and Coastal Scenery of the East Coast**

Today, we ventured to the eastern part of Bali to experience a more pristine and tranquil side of the island.

- **Tenganan Pegringsingan Village:** This is a well-preserved ancient village (Bali Aga) with a unique social structure and traditions. The streets are neatly arranged, exuding a serene and solemn atmosphere. We witnessed villagers weaving the famous double-sided cloth "Geringsing," showcasing intricate craftsmanship and earthy colors. Free from commercial hustle, the village is steeped in history, making it feel like a journey through time.
- **Tirta Gangga Water Palace:** Next, we visited the former royal water palace. This is a **paradise for photography enthusiasts!** The exquisite ponds, fountains, stone carvings, and water lilies create a picturesque scene, complemented by the sight of plump koi fish. Hopping across the stepping stones in the water is a fun experience for both adults and children. The entire garden radiates a zen-like tranquility and refreshing charm.

## **Travel Memo and Practical Tips**

Based on our firsthand experience, we've summarized a few tips for friends planning to visit in the future:

1. **Currency Exchange:** Locally, **the exchange rate for USD to IDR is usually better than using TWD or other currencies**. It's advisable to carry USD cash and exchange it at legitimate money changers in the city. **Quick Reference:**[Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide: What currency is used in Indonesia/Bali? How to exchange money for the best rate? Where is the best place to exchange money?](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)
2. **Internet:** The **DJB physical SIM card** we used had very stable signals, ensuring smooth internet throughout the trip for navigation, communication, and information lookup, proving to be quite reliable. **Quick Reference:**[【Essential for Bali Travel】How to set up Bali internet/SIM card/eSIM?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)
3. **Shopping Comparison:** **Supermarket prices aren't necessarily cheaper than those at small vendors**, especially for souvenirs. Vendors usually allow bargaining, while supermarkets have fixed prices. It's recommended to compare between the two based on the type of goods before purchasing.
