First, regarding the most concerning issue - traffic congestion - my conclusion is: it's much better than expected! Our group was quite large, so we opted for a private 14-seater minibus. Throughout the trip, we only encountered slow-moving traffic for about two hours when entering tourist-dense urban areas. Most of the time, we moved smoothly. This experience taught me that choosing private transportation and carefully planning routes can effectively avoid most traffic nightmares in Bali.

Next, let’s talk about **the challenges and surprises of the rainy season**. Yes, during our seven-day trip, we experienced intermittent rainfall on five of those days. However, the rainy season isn’t all inconvenience—it actually brought several benefits: the temperature was cooler and more comfortable, the greenery was exceptionally lush and fresh, and there were fewer tourists, avoiding the usual crowds. Of course, sudden downpours did require some quick thinking (like the one in the attached photo that forced us to flee from the beach in a hurry—now it just seems funny 🤣🤣). Interestingly, weather forecasts here seem to be more of a suggestion than a certainty. For example, while in Kuta, we received a **heavy rain warning**, but during our two days there, it was mostly sunny with only occasional evening showers. This taught us that in Bali, it’s better to stay flexible with your plans rather than worry about the weather. Our strategy was to always have **rainy-day alternatives** ready, such as switching to indoor attractions, museums, art galleries, or markets, keeping our itinerary adaptable. The sound of rain ended up becoming a soothing white noise backdrop to our journey.

## Sharing Our Itinerary:

### **Day 1: Arrival in Ubud, A Relaxing Prelude at the Villa**

After a long flight, we landed smoothly in Bali. The immigration process was quicker than expected, and after collecting our luggage, we met our pre-booked private driver. As we drove away from the airport's hustle, the scenery gradually shifted from modern buildings to lush fields and intricate shrines, reminding us that we had truly arrived on the 'Island of the Gods.'

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_872fd38d85050291bdc0602b42d36dd9.jpg)

When we arrived at the **Villa** we had booked in Ubud, the fatigue of the journey was instantly washed away by the view before us. The villa was nestled in lush greenery, complete with a private pool and an open-air gazebo. The children cheered at the sight of the pool, while the adults quickly unloaded the luggage, eager to brew some tea and sit in the gazebo to soak in Ubud’s tranquil atmosphere. As night fell, accompanied by the symphony of insects and frogs, we ended our first day in relaxed comfort, recharging for the adventures ahead.

### **Day 4: Exploring the Rustic Villages and Coastal Scenery of the East Coast**

On this day, we ventured to the eastern part of Bali to experience a more primitive and serene side.

- **Tenganan Pegringsingan Village:** This is a well-preserved ancient village (Bali Aga) with a unique social structure and traditions. The village streets are neatly arranged, exuding a serene and solemn atmosphere. We witnessed villagers weaving the famous double-sided "Geringsing" cloth, showcasing intricate craftsmanship and earthy colors. There’s no commercial hustle here, just a profound sense of history—strolling through feels like a journey through time.
- **Tirta Gangga Water Palace:** Next, we visited the former royal water palace. This is a **photographer’s paradise**! The exquisite ponds, fountains, stone carvings, and water lilies create a harmonious scene, and you can spot plump koi fish in the ponds. Jumping across the stepping stones in the water is a fun experience for both adults and children. The entire garden is filled with Zen-like tranquility and coolness, making it utterly charming.
- **Bukit Asah Desa Bugbug Seaside:** Finally, we headed to this hidden gem on the east coast. Instead of a sandy beach, it’s a grassy hillside (Bukit) overlooking the sea. The views are incredibly expansive, offering a glimpse of the azure waters and rocky reefs. Many locals and tourists leisurely picnic and fly kites here, creating a relaxed atmosphere. We quietly waited for the sunset, experiencing a coastal vibe entirely different from Kuta Beach.

## **Travel Memo and Practical Tips**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2a859b507a199d6500bc5d3d47023c43.png)

Based on our firsthand experience, we’ve summarized a few tips for friends planning to visit in the future:

1. **Currency Exchange:** In Bali, **the exchange rate for USD to Indonesian Rupiah is usually better than using NT$ or other currencies**. It’s recommended to bring USD cash and exchange it at reputable money changers in the city. **Quick Reference:** [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide: What Currency is Used in Indonesia/Bali? How to Get the Best Exchange Rate? Where to Exchange Money?](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)
2. **Internet:** The **DJB physical SIM card** we used provided stable signals throughout the trip, ensuring smooth navigation, communication, and information searches. It was highly reliable. **Quick Reference:** [Bali Travel Essentials: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card/eSIM?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)
