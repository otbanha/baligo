From January 11 to 17, 2026, our family embarked on a laughter-filled trip to Bali. Before departure, many friends warned me about the rainy season and traffic congestion. Now that the journey is over, it's time to share my real experiences and feedback.

First, regarding the **traffic congestion** everyone is concerned about, my conclusion is: **it's much better than expected!** With a larger group, we opted for a 14-seater minibus. Throughout the trip, we only encountered slow-moving traffic for about two hours when entering tourist-heavy urban areas. The rest of the time, we moved smoothly. This made me realize that **choosing private transfers and planning routes wisely** can effectively avoid most traffic nightmares.

![暴雨來襲，趕快開車離開海邊！！經過一些低窪地區，路面都變成河流](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f4f9f96d6c813ec68ba40960047d0ffa.jpg)

When heavy rain hit, we quickly drove away from the beach! Passing through some low-lying areas, the roads turned into rivers.

Next, let's talk about the **challenges and surprises of the rainy season**. Yes, we experienced intermittent rain on five out of seven days. However, the rainy season isn't all inconvenience—it brings several benefits: cooler and more comfortable temperatures, lush greenery, and fewer tourists, avoiding crowded situations. Of course, sudden downpours require quick adaptation (like the scene in the attached photo where we 'fled' from the beach due to flooding, now just a funny memory 🤣🤣). Interestingly, weather forecasts can only be taken as references. For example, in Kuta, despite receiving a **heavy rain warning**, we mostly enjoyed sunny days during our two-day stay, with only occasional evening showers. This taught us that in Bali, rather than worrying about the weather, it's better to maintain flexibility in our plans. Our strategy was to always have **rainy day alternatives** ready, such as indoor attractions, museums, art galleries, or markets, keeping our itinerary flexible while the sound of rain became a soothing background soundtrack.

Of course, there were some aspects of the trip that required adjustment: urban traffic isn't very pedestrian-friendly, crossing streets felt like playing a real-life 'Frogger' game; and the countless zeros on Indonesian Rupiah bills nearly overloaded my brain's CPU during currency conversions ♨️

## Sharing Our Itinerary:

### **Day 1: Arrival in Ubud, a Relaxing Prelude at the Villa**

After a long flight, we smoothly landed in Bali. The customs process was quicker than expected, and after collecting our luggage, we met up with our pre-booked private car driver. As we drove away from the airport's hustle, the scenery gradually shifted from modern buildings to lush fields and intricate shrines, reminding us that we had truly arrived on the "Island of the Gods."

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_872fd38d85050291bdc0602b42d36dd9.jpg)

Upon arriving at our booked **Villa** in Ubud, the fatigue from the journey was instantly washed away by the view before us. The Villa was nestled in greenery, featuring a private pool and an open-air gazebo. The children cheered at the sight of the pool, while the adults busied themselves with unpacking, eager to brew a pot of tea and sit in the gazebo to soak in Ubud's unique tranquility. As night fell, accompanied by the natural symphony of insects and frogs, we concluded our relaxing first day, recharging for the adventures ahead.

This day's itinerary was full of contrasts and surprises, taking us from forests to rice terraces, sacred trees to palaces, experiencing Bali's multifaceted charm.

We strictly adhered to the "no eating, no eye contact, no touching" rule, observing their behaviors from a distance. The kids were both nervous and thrilled. Fortunately, the monkeys here were much tamer! Still, it was a vivid lesson in respecting wildlife.

### **Day 4: Exploring the Rustic Villages and Coastal Scenery of the East Coast**

Today, we’ll venture to the eastern part of Bali to experience a more pristine and tranquil side of the island.

- **Tenganan Pegringsingan Village:** This is a well-preserved ancient village (Bali Aga) with a unique social structure and traditions. The village streets are neatly arranged, exuding a serene and solemn atmosphere. We witnessed villagers weaving the famous double-sided cloth "Geringsing," a complex craft with ancient, earthy colors. Here, there’s no commercial hustle—just a profound sense of history. Walking through the village feels like a journey through time.
- **Tirta Gangga Water Palace:** Next, we visited the former royal water palace. This is a **photographer’s paradise**! The intricate ponds, fountains, stone carvings, and water lilies create a picturesque scene, with plump koi fish swimming in the ponds. Hopping across the stone steps in the water is a fun experience for both adults and children. The entire garden radiates Zen-like tranquility and coolness, making it utterly enchanting.

The views are incredibly expansive, offering a panoramic look at the azure waters and rocky reefs. Many locals and tourists leisurely picnic and fly kites here, creating a relaxed and carefree atmosphere. We quietly waited for the sunset, soaking in a coastal vibe entirely different from Kuta Beach.

## **Travel Notes and Practical Tips**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2a859b507a199d6500bc5d3d47023c43.png)

Based on our firsthand experience, we've summarized a few tips for friends planning to visit in the future:

1. **Currency Exchange:** Locally, **the exchange rate for USD to Indonesian Rupiah is usually better than using NT$ or other currencies**. We recommend bringing USD cash and exchanging it at reputable money changers in the city. **Quick Reference:** [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide: What currency is used in Indonesia/Bali? How to get the best exchange rate? Where to exchange money?](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)
2. **Internet:** We used **DJB's physical SIM card**, and the signal was very stable throughout the trip. Navigation, communication, and information searches were smooth and reliable. **Quick Reference:** [Essential for Bali Travel: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card/eSIM?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)
3. **Shopping Comparison:** **Supermarket prices aren't always cheaper than street vendors**, especially for souvenirs. Vendors often allow bargaining, while supermarkets have fixed prices. We recommend comparing prices between the two before purchasing.
