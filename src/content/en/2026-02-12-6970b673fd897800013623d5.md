From January 11 to 17, 2026, our multi-generational family embarked on a laughter-filled trip to Bali. Before departure, many friends warned me about the rainy season and traffic congestion. Now that the journey has ended, it's time to share my real experiences and feedback.

![暴雨來襲，趕快開車離開海邊！！經過一些低窪地區，路面都變成河流](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f4f9f96d6c813ec68ba40960047d0ffa.jpg)

First, regarding the most concerning issue - traffic congestion - my conclusion is: it's much better than expected! Our group was quite large, so we opted for a private 14-seater minibus. Throughout the trip, we only encountered slow-moving traffic for about two hours when entering tourist-dense urban areas. Most of the time, we moved smoothly. This experience taught me that choosing private transportation and carefully planning routes can effectively avoid most traffic nightmares in Bali.

When the rainstorm hit, we quickly drove away from the beach!! Passing through some low-lying areas, the roads turned into rivers.

Next, let's talk about the challenges and surprises of the rainy season. Yes, during our seven-day trip, we experienced intermittent rain on five days. However, the rainy season isn't entirely inconvenient - it actually brought several benefits: cooler and more comfortable temperatures, exceptionally lush and fresh greenery, and fewer tourists, avoiding crowded situations. Of course, sudden rainstorms required quick adaptation (like the one shown in the attached photo where we 'fled in panic' from the beach due to flooding - now it's just funny 🤣🤣). Interestingly, weather forecasts seem to be only for reference. For example, in Kuta, despite receiving a heavy rain warning, we mostly enjoyed sunny weather during our two days there, with only occasional evening showers. This taught us that in Bali, it's better to maintain flexibility rather than worry about the weather. Our strategy was to always have rainy day alternatives ready, such as switching to indoor attractions, museums, art galleries, or markets, keeping our itinerary flexible. The sound of rain became white noise accompanying our journey.

## Sharing Our Itinerary:

### Day 1: Arrival in Ubud, A Relaxing Start at the Villa

After a long flight, we landed smoothly in Bali. The immigration process was quicker than expected, and after collecting our luggage, we met our pre-booked private driver. As we drove away from the airport's hustle, the scenery gradually shifted from modern buildings to lush fields and intricate shrines, reminding us that we had truly arrived on the 'Island of the Gods.'

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_872fd38d85050291bdc0602b42d36dd9.jpg)

Upon arriving at our booked Villa in Ubud, the fatigue of the journey was instantly washed away by the view before us. The Villa was nestled in greenery, featuring a private pool and an open-air pavilion. The children cheered at the sight of the pool, while the adults busied themselves unloading luggage, eager to brew a pot of tea and sit in the pavilion to soak in Ubud's unique tranquility. As night fell, accompanied by the natural symphony of insects and frogs, we ended our relaxed and enjoyable first day, recharging for the adventures ahead.

The children were curious about this 'queueing in water' ritual, and we took the opportunity to explain its religious significance, making it a valuable cultural experience.

We strictly followed the 'no eating, no eye contact, no touching' rule and observed their behavior from a distance. The kids were both nervous and thrilled. Thankfully, the monkeys here were much tamer! Still, it was a vivid lesson in respecting wildlife.

### ****Day 4: Exploring the Rustic Villages and Coastal Scenery of the East Coast****

On this day, we ventured to the eastern part of Bali to experience a more primitive and serene side.

- ****Tenganan Pegringsingan Village:**** This is a well-preserved ancient village (Bali Aga) with a unique social structure and traditions. The streets are neatly arranged, exuding tranquility and solemnity. We witnessed villagers weaving the famous double-sided cloth "Geringsing," showcasing intricate craftsmanship and ancient colors. There's no commercial hustle here, only a profound sense of history. Walking through the village felt like a journey through time.

- ****Tirta Gangga Water Palace:**** Next, we visited the former royal water palace. This is a ****paradise for photography enthusiasts****! The exquisite ponds, fountains, stone carvings, and water lilies create a picturesque scene, with plump koi fish swimming in the ponds. Jumping across the stepping stones in the water is a fun experience for both adults and children. The entire garden is filled with Zen-like tranquility and refreshing vibes, making it truly enchanting.

- ****Bukit Asah Desa Bugbug Seaside:**** Finally, we headed to this hidden gem on the east coast. This isn't a sandy beach but a grassy hillside (Bukit) overlooking the sea. The view is incredibly expansive, offering a panoramic vista of the azure waters and reefs. Many locals and tourists leisurely picnic and fly kites here, creating a relaxed atmosphere. We quietly waited for the sunset here, experiencing a coastal vibe vastly different from Kuta Beach.

## Travel Notes and Practical Tips

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2a859b507a199d6500bc5d3d47023c43.png)

Based on our firsthand experience, we’ve summarized a few tips for friends planning to visit in the future:

1. **Currency Exchange:** In Bali, **the exchange rate for USD to Indonesian Rupiah is usually better than using NT$ or other currencies**. We recommend carrying USD cash and exchanging it at reputable money changers in the city. **Quick Reference:** [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide: What Currency to Use? How to Get the Best Exchange Rate? Where to Exchange Money?](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)

2. **Internet:** The **DJB physical SIM card** we used had very stable signal, ensuring smooth internet throughout the trip for navigation, communication, and information lookup. It was highly reliable. **Quick Reference:** [Bali Travel Essentials: How to Get Bali Internet/SIM Card/eSIM?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)

3. **Shopping Comparison:** **Supermarket prices aren’t always cheaper than street vendors**, especially for souvenirs. Vendors often allow bargaining, while supermarkets have fixed prices. We recommend comparing prices between the two before purchasing.

4. **Transportation:** For short trips within the city or spontaneous rides, **Gojek or Grab ride-hailing apps are very convenient and affordable**, making them a great alternative to private car hires. **Quick Reference:** [Grab or Gojek? A Comprehensive Guide to Registering, Using, and Topping Up GoPay in Indonesia/Bali](/blog/2024-10-13-670b5191fd8978000185cf37/)

5. **Mosquito and Insect Protection:** Bali has **plenty of flies and mosquitoes**, especially near fields or in the evening. **Insect repellent is an absolute daily essential**, so carry it with you and reapply regularly. **Quick Reference:** [Essential Medications/Supplies for Indonesia/Bali Travel: How to Buy Insect Repellent/Sunscreen?](/blog/2024-03-22-65fcd4a5fd89780001513c9a/)
