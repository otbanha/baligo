From January 11 to 17, 2026, our family embarked on a laughter-filled trip to Bali. Before departure, many friends warned me about the rainy season and traffic congestion. Now that the journey is over, it's time to share my real experiences and feedback.

First, regarding the **traffic congestion** everyone is concerned about, my conclusion is: **much better than expected!** With a larger group, we opted for a 14-seater minibus. Throughout the trip, we only encountered slow-moving traffic for about two hours when entering tourist-heavy areas. Otherwise, we could move around smoothly. This made me realize that **choosing private transfers and planning routes wisely** can effectively avoid most traffic nightmares.

![暴雨來襲，趕快開車離開海邊！！經過一些低窪地區，路面都變成河流](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f4f9f96d6c813ec68ba40960047d0ffa.jpg)

When heavy rain hits, quickly drive away from the beach!! Passing through some low-lying areas, the roads turned into rivers.

Next, let's talk about the **challenges and surprises of the rainy season**. Yes, we experienced rain on five out of the seven days. However, the rainy season isn't entirely inconvenient—it brings several benefits: cooler and more comfortable temperatures, lush greenery, and fewer tourists, avoiding crowded scenes. Of course, sudden downpours require quick adaptation (like the one in the photo that made us 'flee' from the beach, now just a funny memory 🤣🤣). Interestingly, weather forecasts can only be taken as references. For example, in Kuta, despite receiving a **heavy rain warning**, we mostly enjoyed sunny days with only occasional evening showers. This taught us that in Bali, instead of worrying about the weather, it's better to maintain flexibility in our plans. Our strategy was to always have **rainy day alternatives** ready, such as indoor attractions, museums, art galleries, or markets, keeping our itinerary flexible. The sound of rain became a soothing white noise accompaniment to our journey.

> **Quick Reference:** [Bali Rainy Season: What to Do When It Rains in Bali? 60 Rainy Day Alternatives](/blog/2024-01-06-6598c6fffd89780001047d76/)

Of course, there were some adjustments needed during this trip: city traffic isn't very pedestrian-friendly, crossing the street felt like playing a real-life 'Frogger' game; the countless zeros on Indonesian Rupiah bills nearly overheated my brain's CPU during conversions ♨️

## Sharing Our Itinerary:

### **Day 1: Arrival in Ubud, A Leisurely Prelude at the Villa**

After a long flight, we landed smoothly in Bali. The customs process was quicker than expected, and after collecting our luggage, we met up with our pre-booked private driver. As the car drove away from the airport's hustle and bustle, the scenery gradually shifted from modern buildings to lush fields and intricate shrines, reminding us that we had truly entered the "Island of the Gods."

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_872fd38d85050291bdc0602b42d36dd9.jpg)

Upon arriving at the **Villa** we had booked in Ubud, the fatigue of the journey was instantly washed away by the view before us. The Villa was nestled in greenery, featuring a private pool and an open-air pavilion. The children cheered at the sight of the pool, while the adults busied themselves unloading the luggage, eager to brew a pot of tea and sit in the pavilion to soak in Ubud's unique tranquility. The night ended with the natural symphony of crickets and frogs, wrapping up a relaxing first day and preparing us for the adventures ahead.

The children were curious about this "queueing in the water" ritual, and we took the opportunity to explain its religious significance, making it a valuable cultural experience.

### **Day 4: Exploring the Rustic Villages and Coastal Scenery of the East Coast**

Today, we ventured to the eastern part of Bali to experience a more pristine and tranquil side of the island.

- **Tenganan Pegringsingan Village:** This is a well-preserved ancient village (Bali Aga) with a unique social structure and traditions. The streets are neatly arranged, exuding a serene and solemn atmosphere. We witnessed villagers weaving the famous double-sided cloth "Geringsing," a complex craft with ancient colors. There’s no commercial hustle here, just a profound sense of history. Strolling through the village felt like a journey through time.
- **Tirta Gangga Water Palace:** Next, we visited the former royal water palace. This is a **paradise for photography enthusiasts!** The exquisite ponds, fountains, stone carvings, and water lilies create a picturesque scene, with plump koi fish swimming in the ponds. Jumping across the stepping stones in the water is a fun experience for both adults and children. The entire garden is filled with Zen-like tranquility and refreshing vibes, making it utterly charming.

The view is incredibly expansive, offering a panoramic vista of the azure waters and rocky reefs. Many locals and tourists leisurely picnic and fly kites here, creating a relaxed and carefree atmosphere. We quietly waited for the sunset, savoring a coastal vibe entirely different from Kuta Beach.

## **Travel Memo & Practical Tips**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2a859b507a199d6500bc5d3d47023c43.png)

Based on our firsthand experience, we've summarized the following tips for friends planning to visit in the future:

1. **Currency Exchange:** In Bali, **the exchange rate for USD to Indonesian Rupiah is usually better than using NT$ or other currencies**. It's recommended to bring USD cash and exchange it at reputable money changers in the city. **Quick Reference:** [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide: What currency is used in Indonesia/Bali? How to get the best exchange rate? Where is the best place to exchange money?](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)
2. **Internet:** We used **DJB's physical SIM card**, which provided stable signal throughout our trip, ensuring smooth navigation, communication, and information searching. It was highly reliable. **Quick Reference:** [Bali Travel Essentials: How to Get Internet/SIM Card/eSIM in Bali?](/blog/2024-03-21-65f916bbfd89780001b916e0/)
3. **Shopping Comparison:** **Supermarket prices aren't always cheaper than street vendors**, especially for souvenirs. Vendors often allow bargaining, while supermarkets have fixed prices. It's advisable to compare prices between the two before making a purchase.
4. **Transportation:** For short trips within the city or spontaneous rides, **Gojek or Grab ride-hailing apps are very convenient and affordable**, making them a great alternative to private car hires. **Quick Reference:** [Grab or Gojek?
