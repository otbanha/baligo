Bali is home to many five-star hotels that cater to the diverse needs and tastes of travelers. **Raffles Bali**, located on Jimbaran Bay, was named one of the best luxury hotels in 2024, continuing to attract visitors from around the globe to experience its unique elegance and refined atmosphere.

This accolade comes from Condé Nast Traveller, a publication long regarded as a key reference in global travel and luxury getaways. Additionally, Raffles Bali has received a Global Recognition Award, further cementing its leadership in the global luxury hotel market.

This resort is more than just a place to stay—it's a gateway to the soul of Bali. Each villa embodies Balinese craftsmanship, with furniture and artworks showcasing the island's rich cultural heritage. Raffles Bali's 32 stunning villas offer guests a unique sense of privacy and immersion in nature, a reason many travelers return year after year.

Raffles Bali is set back from the coastline, surrounded by lush greenery, and is committed to supporting local conservation efforts, ensuring that natural elements are woven into the guest experience. Half of the resort's 23-hectare property remains undeveloped, serving as a sanctuary for local flora and fauna. This dedication to preserving nature is becoming increasingly rare in Bali, especially given the island's [overdevelopment](/blog/bali-hotel-construction-ban/).

In 2024, Raffles Bali received a Global Recognition Award, placing it among the world's best luxury hotels. Not only that, but its sister property in the Maldives also earned the same honor, highlighting its dual strengths in eco-friendliness and luxury amenities. These accolades inspire the hotel to continuously innovate and enhance the guest experience, allowing every traveler to discover new adventures here—whether they seek relaxation, cultural immersion, or culinary delights.

## A Hidden Beachfront Resort in Jimbaran

Jimbaran is a safe, convenient, and often overlooked beachfront resort area in Bali, just minutes from the airport. Located on the Bukit Peninsula, it's surrounded by famous destinations like Uluwatu and Nusa Dua, and borders popular southern beach spots such as Kuta, Legian, Seminyak, and Canggu. Jimbaran offers travelers the perfect balance of seclusion and exploration.
