### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Infrastructure Tackles 'Overtourism'

- **Uluwatu Launches 'Zero Congestion' Traffic Diversion Project**: The Badung Regency Transportation Department held a progress meeting on May 17 to finalize a large-scale traffic diversion project aimed at alleviating chronic congestion in southern Pecatu and the surrounding Uluwatu area. Officials noted that the current peak-hour journey from Canggu to Uluwatu often takes over an hour. The new project will focus on widening roads and implementing one-way diversions at six major choke points, including the Toyoning intersection, with the goal of providing tourists with a seamless travel experience.
- **Overcapacity! Waste Crisis Threatens Tourism Sustainability**: Several Indonesian environmental scholars and local organizations (IWO) issued a joint warning on May 17, stating that Bali's natural carrying capacity can only support around 1.5 million people. However, the current population has reached 4.5 million, coupled with millions of tourists, pushing the waste management system to the brink of collapse. Following restrictions at the Suwung landfill, severe delays in waste collection have been reported in areas like West Denpasar. Authorities are urgently promoting village-level waste sorting (TPS3R) and transitioning to Chinese-funded waste-to-energy plants to prevent the crisis from tarnishing the island's image.
- **Targeting Central Asian Tourists, ASITA Seeks Direct Flights from Kazakhstan**: The Bali Tourism Association (ASITA) is collaborating with Indonesia's Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Ministry of Tourism to secure charter and regular flights from Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan to Bali. Official analysis indicates strong demand for tropical island destinations among high-net-worth individuals in Central Asia. If successful, these routes will reduce Bali's reliance on the European and American markets, transforming it into a layover hub for Central Asian travelers.

### 📄 Visitor Concerns: Visa Scrutiny and Latest Data

- **2026 Official Report**: Over 3,000 Deported and Blacklisted: Indonesia's Directorate General of Immigration released its enforcement summary for January 1 to May 5, 2026, on May 17. The data shows that authorities conducted 6,779 enforcement actions against foreigners in the first five months of the year, with 2,026 deported and their residence permits revoked for visa violations, and 1,323 blacklisted from entering the country.
- **'Unpaid Collaboration, Mutual Business Benefits' Also Subject to Crackdown**: The Immigration Bureau recently clarified that even 'unpaid promotions, mutual modeling, cosmetic collaborations, or influencer endorsements' without monetary transactions are considered 'work' if they hold economic or brand investment value. Foreign influencers, photographers, or freelancers on tourist visas (including e-VOA) engaging in such activities will face fines or blacklisting.
- **Emerging 'Low-Density Slow Travel' Destinations Gain Popularity**: As southern Bali becomes overdeveloped, digital nomads and high-end travelers in 2026 are increasingly flocking to Seraya (known for its iconic lighthouse), Penglipuran (the world's cleanest traditional village), and Menjangan Island. These emerging hotspots, offering 'low-density, eco-friendly villas and opportunities to integrate into local communities,' are reshaping Bali's tourism map with their appeal for long-term stays.
