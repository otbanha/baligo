**Top 10 Breathtaking Tourist Spots in East Nusa Tenggara (NTT): Exploring Southeast Indonesia's Natural Wonders**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d533378734952d93925747e329aad969.jpg)

East Nusa Tenggara (NTT) is a hidden gem of Indonesia, boasting numerous stunning natural and cultural attractions. Located in southeastern Indonesia, this province attracts visitors with its enchanting islands and diverse ecosystems. This article will take you deeper into some must-visit spots when traveling to NTT.

### **1. Komodo National Park**

Located between Sumba and Flores islands, Komodo National Park is a crucial area for protecting the world's unique Komodo dragons (Varanus komodoensis) and their habitat. The ecosystem here encompasses not only Komodo dragons but also diverse marine life and unique bird species. The park became a national park in 1980 and was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1991.

![Komodo Dragon](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4035f35ae883721f8a37d1e17b4da506.jpg)

Komodo Dragon

### **2. Labuan Bajo**

Labuan Bajo serves as the main gateway to the NTT region. With its airport and port, this town offers numerous tourist facilities, including most hotels, restaurants, and many local travel agencies.

### **3. Dintor Beach (Pantai Dintor)**

Located in Satar Ruwuk Village, Dintor Beach is famous for its white sand and azure waters, offering a perfect spot to admire the views of Mules Island (Pulau Mules) across the bay. Strolling along the beach or relaxing under the coconut trees while listening to the waves are ideal ways to enjoy this paradise.

![Pantai Dintor](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a45bbd79bc249e0ec2e29ba87dfb45e1.jpg)

Pantai Dintor

### **4. Wae Rebo Village (Desa Wae Rebo)**

Wae Rebo Village is hailed as a 'village in the clouds,' nestled in the southern highlands of Flores Island. This village attracts numerous visitors with its well-preserved traditional culture. You can admire the unique conical high houses called 'Mbaru Niang' and experience how the locals continue their ancestral cultural practices.

![Wae Rebo Village](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f1aa8ea8fadee4be64245263e1265ceb.jpg)

Wae Rebo Village

### **5. Kelimutu Crater Lakes (Danau Kelimutu)**

Kelimutu Crater Lakes are famous for their three distinct-colored lakes, a scene that once graced the Indonesian 5,000 rupiah banknote. Known for their red, blue, and white hues, these lakes are a breathtaking natural wonder that will leave you in awe after ascending the 1,631-meter-high Kelimutu Mountain.

![Kelimutu Lake](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ec3f2210b163b77d7a45379b5831c608.jpg)

Kelimutu Lake

### **6. Bukit Warinding**

The hills of NTT are equally enchanting, with Bukit Warinding being a particularly popular photography spot. This location served as a filming site for the Indonesian movie 'The Golden Staff.' The rolling hills resemble a giant lying on the earth, offering an unmissable and stunning landscape.

![Bukit Warinding](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_28567107962c2d15113de6b0c80effa8.jpg)

Bukit Warinding

### **7. Cunca Wulang Waterfall**

Cunca Wulang Waterfall, located in the Sano Nggoang area of Manggarai Barat, is a hidden paradise nestled in the mountains. The clear waters of the waterfall and the surrounding lush forest provide a tranquil natural retreat. Visitors can swim, bathe, or simply enjoy the beauty of nature here.

![Cunca Wulang Waterfall](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_59f1c014134fd6dc291ba8683b1ac596.jpg)

Cunca Wulang Waterfall

### **8. Ululoga Tourism Village**

Ululoga Tourism Village is renowned for its abundant spice resources. Visitors here can join a unique spice tour to learn about cultivating and processing local spices. From nutmeg to ginger, these spices have become an integral part of the local culture and economy.

![Wisata Ululoga Village](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b80ed869689b97337deda2976cf5c454.jpg)

Wisata Ululoga Village

### **9. Whale Hunting Tradition (Lembata)**

In Lamalera Village on Lembata Island, locals maintain a traditional whale hunting practice dating back to the 17th century, showcasing their courage and wisdom. The hunting process strictly follows regulations, targeting only specific whale species and conducted sustainably.

![Lembata](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_44768e2df8fc59cc6ba55a694f9e1499.jpg)

Lembata

### **10. Alor Island**

Alor Island is a paradise for diving enthusiasts, boasting 42 dive sites, including underwater worlds with stunning coral reefs. Additionally, the island features numerous beaches and cultural attractions, such as the famous Moko Museum and a well-preserved 500-year-old Quran, reflecting the island's rich historical heritage.

![Alor Island](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e1a60e30e65865f044391515a395da7e.jpg)

Alor Island
