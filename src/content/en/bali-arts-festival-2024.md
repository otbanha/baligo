The Bali Arts Festival is about to kick off in Bali. This is one of the island's most important cultural events. Artists, musicians, and dancers from around the world will gather to showcase their works to visitors. During this time, Bali becomes a haven for art enthusiasts. There's no need to worry about ticket costs, as all performances at the Bali Arts Festival are free. You'll have the opportunity to witness not only Bali's rich performing arts but also cultural highlights from other Indonesian islands. If you're a lover of art and culture, this is an event you absolutely cannot miss.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_90ab703b954115885ae714b8d09db502.png)

## What is the Bali Arts Festival?

The Bali Arts Festival has been held since 1979. Despite its relatively short history of forty years, it has played a crucial role in promoting Indonesian culture. During the month-long event, you can enjoy daily live performances featuring art, costumes, and handicrafts. Additionally, there are dance performances, shadow puppet shows, and music competitions. Hundreds of local and international artists take the stage, ensuring that regardless of your age, you'll find performances to enjoy during this period.

## When is the 2024 Bali Arts Festival?

The Bali Arts Festival is held annually. This cultural feast lasts approximately one month, typically starting on the second Saturday of June and concluding in mid-July. However, the exact dates vary slightly each year. In 2024, it will run from **June 15th to July 13th**.

## Theme of the Bali Arts Festival

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c32e212b2a773b031367bb0fbbad6089.jpg)

The theme for the 2024 Bali Arts Festival is "Jana Kerthi Pramaguna Wikrama," which means "Enhancing Human Dignity and Excellence." The festival's themes are usually based on one of these life-sustaining forces. Therefore, the performances and exhibitions at the festival may have philosophical underpinnings, offering a rich tapestry of thought and artistic expression.

## Where is the Bali Arts Festival celebrated?

The Bali Arts Festival will be held at the Werdhi Budaya Art Centre, a sprawling complex showcasing local artistic styles. On regular days, this tourist attraction may not see many visitors. However, by mid-June, when the Bali Arts Festival kicks off, it becomes the busiest place on the island. Dance competitions, music performances, and art exhibitions will draw visitors from around the world. Additionally, there will be open-air parade activities. The entire art center resembles a garden, featuring many Southeast Asian-style buildings with local characteristics, set in a beautiful environment. During the festival, stages and seating areas will be set up. Once the event schedule is finalized, you can choose your favorite programs and plan your time accordingly to watch and participate.

## What kind of performances can you enjoy?

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b37c1c6db8934d3aa8bc6d6630976a02.png)

### Opening Parade

The Bali Arts Festival will commence with a grand and unique cultural parade. The parade will start at Puputan Square and proceed along one of Bali's main roads. In 2024, hundreds of artists will participate, including both international artists and local artists from Bali's eight regencies. You'll see traditional Indonesian costumes and musical instruments, as well as representatives and floats from other Indonesian islands. To fully immerse yourself in the festive atmosphere, consider watching up close. For example, the sides of the road are great spots, but be prepared for the heat by taking sun protection measures in advance.

### Open-Air Theater

Don't miss the performances at the open-air theater. Bali's signature dance, known as Balinese dance, is deeply influenced by religious themes and often involves depictions of deities. Based on these elements, you'll witness various traditional Balinese dances, impromptu performances by Balinese children, and collaborative showcases of vocal, musical, and dance arts.

### Art Exhibitions

If you're looking for something beyond traditional cultural performances, this event is perfect for you. During the exhibition, there will be a series of food, floral, fashion, and handicraft stalls. You can browse these diverse stalls, savor traditional Balinese cuisine, admire exquisite local handicrafts, or purchase traditional Balinese attire. If you find something you like, it’s a great idea to buy it as a souvenir for friends.

## How to Get to the Bali Arts Festival Venue?

Denpasar, the largest city in Bali, serves as the island's public transportation hub. You can reach your destination by hiring a private car, taking a taxi, or using ride-hailing services. Besides public transport, you can also opt for renting a scooter, which is a convenient and flexible way to get around. Denpasar's city layout is unique, blending villages with historic Hindu temples. As you travel, you'll have the chance to admire ancient temples and bustling villages.

## Tips for Attending the Bali Arts Festival

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_eb580db9253ea62dc0130897433dc0e1.jpg)

### Weather and Clothing

Located on the equator, Bali has a tropical rainforest climate characterized by heat and humidity. June and July are the most comfortable months, with average temperatures ranging from 22 to 27 degrees Celsius. It's advisable to pack light and breathable clothing. Although this isn't the rainy season, it's still wise to carry an umbrella just in case.

### Sun Protection

Even though June and July offer relatively comfortable temperatures, sun protection remains essential. During the Bali Arts Festival, there are outdoor parades and activities, so bring sun-protective clothing, sunglasses, and sunscreen to shield your skin. In the heat, you may lose a lot of fluids, so carrying a water bottle is a smart choice.

### Safeguarding Personal Belongings and Children

During the month of the Bali Arts Festival, Bali becomes extremely crowded. You must always keep an eye on your valuables. When carrying cash, it's best to keep it in your bag or store it in your hotel's safe. Carry only a small amount of cash with you. If you're bringing children to experience Indonesia's exotic atmosphere, make sure they stay within your sight at all times.

### Learning the Local Language

The official languages of Bali are Indonesian and English. In most cases, English will suffice. However, it’s a good idea to learn some basic everyday Indonesian phrases just in case. For example, you can say "Terima kasih!" which means "Thank you."

### Booking Accommodation and Tickets

The Bali Arts Festival is one of Indonesia’s most popular events, attracting visitors from around the world. During this month-long festival, Bali’s population density increases significantly. It’s wise to confirm your accommodation and tickets in advance. Booking a hotel near the festival venue can save you time and transportation costs.
