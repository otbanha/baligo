When preparing to travel to Indonesia or Bali, some travelers may need to bring Chinese herbal powder and health supplements, which are crucial for their well-being. However, different countries have varying regulations regarding medicines and health supplements, especially Indonesia, which has stricter controls on medications compared to other countries. Understanding and adhering to these regulations is essential to avoid unnecessary trouble and delays.

### Indonesia's Legal Regulations on Chinese Herbal Medicine

Indonesia has strict controls on Chinese herbal medicine and preparations, especially for unidentified powder-type items brought into the country. According to the regulations of Indonesia's Food and Drug Administration, if Chinese herbal powder is carried without proper documentation, it may be considered an illegal item, leading to unnecessary complications.

### Taiwan's Restrictions on Bringing Health Supplements

Taiwan's customs also impose strict limits on bringing health supplements out of the country. Travelers can carry a maximum of 12 bottles of the same type of health supplement, with a total quantity not exceeding 36 bottles, and all items must remain in their original packaging. Exceeding this limit may require an import/export permit from the Ministry of Health and Welfare.

### Documents to Note When Bringing Chinese Herbal Powder and Health Supplements into Indonesia

Each traveler should ensure that the Chinese herbal powder and health supplements they carry are clearly labeled and accompanied by a doctor's diagnosis or relevant proof. These documents can help you explain and pass through inspections more smoothly if encountered.

### Case Study: Passenger Detained for Carrying Chinese Medicine

In 2019, a passenger was detained and forced to return after being caught carrying unlabeled Chinese herbal powder into Indonesia. This occurred because he was unable to provide any documentation, violating Indonesian regulations.

### Risks and Consequences: Legal Responsibilities of Carrying Prohibited Medicines

It is not advisable to bring Chinese herbal powders or health supplements into Bali. While you might pass customs smoothly in some cases, if you encounter strict inspections and cannot clearly explain or provide relevant documentation, it could lead to significant trouble. Indonesia has stringent regulations regarding the import of medicines and health products, especially non-Western medicines like Chinese herbs, which customs officials may not immediately recognize or deem legal. If customs suspect the items, travelers may face fines, confiscation, or even more severe legal consequences. Therefore, unless absolutely necessary, it's best to avoid carrying such products, especially if you cannot provide clear English prescriptions or official certifications.
