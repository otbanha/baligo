When preparing to travel to Indonesia or Bali, some travelers may need to bring traditional Chinese medicine powders and health supplements, which are crucial for their health. However, different countries have varying regulations regarding medicines and health supplements, especially Indonesia, which has stricter controls on medications compared to other countries. Understanding and adhering to these regulations is essential to avoid unnecessary trouble and delays.

### Indonesia's Legal Regulations on Traditional Chinese Medicine

Indonesia has strict controls on traditional Chinese medicine and herbal preparations, especially when it comes to bringing unidentified powder-type items into the country. According to the regulations of the Indonesian Food and Drug Administration, if you carry traditional Chinese medicine powders without proper supporting documents, they may be considered illegal items, leading to unnecessary complications.

### Taiwan's Restrictions on Carrying Health Supplements

Taiwan's customs also impose strict limits on carrying health supplements out of the country. Travelers can bring up to 12 bottles of the same type of health supplement, with a total limit of 36 bottles, and all items must remain in their original packaging. If the quantity exceeds this limit, you may need to apply for an import/export permit from the Ministry of Health and Welfare.

### Documents to Pay Attention to When Bringing Traditional Chinese Medicine Powders and Health Supplements into Indonesia

Each traveler should ensure that the traditional Chinese medicine powders and health supplements they carry are clearly labeled and accompanied by a doctor's prescription or relevant documentation. These documents can help you explain and clear customs more smoothly if you encounter inspections.

### Case Study: Passenger's Traditional Chinese Medicine Confiscated Upon Entry

In 2019, a passenger had their unlabeled traditional Chinese medicine powder confiscated upon entering Indonesia and was subjected to detention and forced return of the item. This occurred because they were unable to provide any documentation, violating Indonesian regulations.

### Risks and Consequences: Legal Responsibilities for Bringing Prohibited Medicines

It is not advisable to bring traditional Chinese medicine powders or health supplements into Bali. While it might be possible to pass through customs without issues in some cases, if subjected to strict inspection and unable to clearly explain or provide relevant documentation, significant trouble could arise. Indonesia has stringent regulations regarding the import of medicines and health products, especially for non-Western medicines like traditional Chinese medicine, which customs officials may not immediately recognize or verify. If customs deem the items suspicious, travelers could face fines, confiscation, or even more severe legal consequences. Therefore, unless absolutely necessary, it is recommended to avoid bringing such products, particularly if you cannot provide clear English prescriptions or official certifications.
