On our last day in Bali, we chose to stay near the airport as we were heading to Komodo Island. When we planned to take the hotel taxi to the airport, Xiao Wu was concerned that the driver might mistakenly drop us off at the international terminal. He made a special trip to the hotel early in the morning to remind the driver and ensure everything went smoothly. However, a small issue still arose (I gave the wrong amount of money because I was too relaxed 😭). Thankfully, with Xiao Wu's help, he and his wife worked hard to resolve the issue, and we eventually got our money back. Having them around in an unfamiliar place made us feel incredibly secure. When we returned to Bali after our Komodo Island trip, Xiao Wu even came to the airport to return the money and escorted us from the domestic terminal to the security entrance of the international terminal before leaving.

## Komodo Island Liveaboard Adventure

The stunning sunset during the liveaboard (provided by Tyty Chow):

Our two-day, one-night liveaboard trip to Komodo Island was truly unforgettable. The comfort of the liveaboard exceeded our expectations, with calm seas and a gentle breeze making it incredibly relaxing (though this depends on the weather conditions). The meals on board were simple yet delicious, with the grilled fish being particularly memorable.

![科摩多島船宿及美景（Tyty Chow提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a615a173436b8ee9cd7ee4f0d691c0ea.jpg)

Komodo Island liveaboard and scenic views (provided by Tyty Chow)

One of the biggest advantages of joining a liveaboard is being able to witness the sunrise at Padar Island without the hassle of shuttling back and forth like on speedboat tours. Speedboat tours usually can't accommodate sunrise viewing, but liveaboards give us the opportunity to experience Padar's magnificent sunrise. Additionally, the evening sunset and night sky left us utterly captivated.

![左：帕達爾日出/中：有成群蝙蝠飛過，可惜太暗沒拍出/右：蘭科洞穴游泳（Tyty Chow提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_501ebd9a73e18b60967656df34adeb8f.jpg)

Left: Sunrise at Padar / Center: A flock of bats flew by, but it was too dark to capture / Right: Swimming at Rangko Cave (Photo by Tyty Chow)

However, the two-day-one-night itinerary felt slightly insufficient, especially when we encountered a light afternoon rain that made sea conditions a bit unstable, slowing down the boat and reducing some activities. Based on this experience, I recommend friends interested in Komodo tours to opt for a three-day-two-night or longer itinerary, especially if you're into diving or snorkeling. A two-day trip feels a bit rushed for fully experiencing all the highlights of Komodo Island.
