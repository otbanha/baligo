## The Wonderful Experience in Bali

Here, I would like to highly recommend our private driver, Mr. Wu, in Bali. During our three days in Bali, Mr. Wu accompanied and took care of us throughout the trip. Mr. Wu is the husband of Betty Pinky and speaks fluent Chinese, so we started discussing our itinerary with him while still in Taiwan. He arranged accommodations and activities based on our needs, whether it was mountain or sea excursions or tasting local snacks, he effortlessly guided us to deeply experience the charm of Bali.

During the trip, Mr. Wu chatted with us along the way, helping us understand a lot about the local culture. His arrangements were meticulous, whether it was restaurants or activities, everything was point-to-point, allowing us to enjoy ourselves thoroughly. He even tried his best to arrange the best seats for us, capturing the best views. On our last day in Bali, as we were heading to Komodo Island, we chose to stay near the airport. When we planned to simply take the hotel taxi to the airport, Xiao Wu worried that the driver might mistakenly drop us off at the international terminal, so he came early in the morning to remind the driver and ensure everything went smoothly. However, a minor hiccup still occurred (I was so relaxed that I gave the wrong amount of money 😭), but thankfully, with Xiao Wu's help, he and his wife worked hard to resolve the issue, and we eventually got our money back. Having them around in a foreign place made us feel incredibly secure. When we returned to Bali after our Komodo Island trip, Xiao Wu even came to the airport to return the money and escorted us from the domestic terminal all the way to the international terminal's security entrance before leaving.

## Komodo Island Liveaboard Adventure

The sunset view from the liveaboard (provided by Tyty Chow):

Our two-day, one-night liveaboard trip to Komodo Island was truly an unforgettable experience. The comfort of the liveaboard exceeded our expectations, with calm seas and a gentle breeze making it incredibly relaxing (though this depends on the weather conditions at the time). The meals on board were simple but delicious, with the grilled fish being particularly memorable.

![科摩多島船宿及美景（Tyty Chow提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a615a173436b8ee9cd7ee4f0d691c0ea.jpg)

Komodo Island liveaboard and scenic views (provided by Tyty Chow)

One of the greatest advantages of joining a liveaboard is the opportunity to witness the sunrise at Padar Island without the hassle of rushing back and forth like on speedboat tours. Speedboat tours usually can't accommodate sunrise viewing, but liveaboards allow us to experience Padar's magnificent sunrise. Additionally, the evening sunsets and night skies left us utterly captivated.

![左：帕達爾日出/中：有成群蝙蝠飛過，可惜太暗沒拍出/右：蘭科洞穴游泳（Tyty Chow提供）](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_501ebd9a73e18b60967656df34adeb8f.jpg)

Left: Sunrise at Padar / Center: A flock of bats flew by, but it was too dark to capture / Right: Swimming at Rangko Cave (Photo provided by Tyty Chow)

However, the two-day-one-night itinerary felt slightly insufficient, especially when we encountered a light rain in the afternoon, causing slightly rough sea conditions and slower boat speeds, which reduced some of our planned activities. Based on this experience, I recommend friends interested in Komodo tours to opt for a three-day-two-night or longer itinerary, particularly if you're into diving or snorkeling. A two-day trip is indeed a bit rushed for fully experiencing all the highlights of Komodo Island.
