### Bali Government Confirms Rules Visitors Must Follow for Upcoming Nyepi Day

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_26e4bd90b1acdf6d7ea153ac83b7542d.jpg)

Bali's Hindu authority has released the latest guidelines on how to celebrate the upcoming Nyepi Day (Bali's Day of Silence). This important festival will take place on March 29, 2025, and all non-Hindus, foreigners, and tourists on the island must adhere to the day's regulations.

The Indonesian Hindu Council (PHDI) has issued new guidelines for the upcoming Nyepi Day celebrations. These updated guidelines have been formally communicated to the Bali provincial government and the public through a circular signed by Nyoman Kenak, PHDI Bali management official, and Putu Wirata Dwikora, PHDI secretary.

Dwikora told reporters: "Every year we issue a circular (containing PHDI decrees) to remind the community, as there are too many activities in traditional villages. The guidelines are roughly the same each year, outlining to village leaders and temple management teams how each ritual of the festival should be conducted."

The updated guidelines state that the Tumpek Wariga ceremony (which can be observed through Catur Brata Panyepian) must be completed by 06:30 WITA. Dwikora explained: "If the content is the same (overall), there is no problem. Even if there are differences in timing, what matters is having an explanation." He added: "Why no later than 06:30 WITA? According to Ida Pandita's 'Indra Pramana' (commentary), the change of day occurs at 06:00 (sunrise)."

This means that temple ceremonies conducted by local Balinese Hindus will be allowed to continue until 06:30 on Nyepi morning, although the official Nyepi Day is typically marked by the ringing of bells and gongs at 06:00 on the morning of Nyepi. The next morning at 06:00, gongs and bells will be rung again, marking the end of the silence.

### What Visitors Need to Know

Visitors planning to travel to Bali between March 28 and 30, 2025, should note that the island's normal flow of visitors will change during the festival. Starting at noon on March 28, many businesses will close to allow employees to return home for the holiday. On March 29, all businesses and public services will be closed, although emergency services will remain on duty. Many shops will also be closed on March 30, or operate with limited hours and reduced staff.

On Nyepi Day (March 29), visitors will not be able to go anywhere and are required to stay at their accommodations. Hosts will inform them about which activities are permitted and which are not. Some resorts offer silent buffets for guests and allow limited use of resort facilities, while other hotels require guests to remain in their rooms, with meals delivered to them.

Additionally, in some areas, such as the remote islands of Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, and Nusa Ceningan, electricity and WiFi connections may also be disrupted. On Nyepi Day, people typically refrain from using electricity and seeking entertainment, and many Balinese Hindus also fast on this day.

### Transportation and Itinerary Planning

Visitors will also find that they cannot land in or depart from Bali on March 29, 2025. For those wishing to visit Bali during Nyepi, it is recommended to arrive on March 27 rather than March 28. Similarly, departing on March 31 is also more convenient.

Furthermore, visitors should note that this year's Nyepi Day is very close to Eid al-Fitr, which falls on March 30 (Sunday) and March 31 (Monday). Known as Lebaran in Indonesia, Eid al-Fitr is a major holiday celebrated by millions of Muslims traveling across the country, including Bali, which is one of the most popular destinations.

### Importance of Advance Preparation

Bali visitors are advised to be aware that the island will be extremely busy from March 27 to April 2, 2025. Therefore, it is recommended to book accommodations, activities, dining reservations, taxis, and drivers as early as possible. Proper transportation planning will help avoid unnecessary hassles.

In summary, Nyepi Day in Bali is a unique and sacred festival, and visitors should respect local traditions and regulations when participating. Planning ahead and understanding the rules will help ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.

## Bali Nyepi Day Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. **What is Bali Nyepi Day?**

- Nyepi Day is one of the most important Hindu holidays in Bali, also known as "Bali's Day of Silence." On this day, both residents and tourists are required to maintain silence and cease all activities for meditation and self-reflection.

### 2. **When is Nyepi Day in 2025?**

- Nyepi Day in 2025 will be observed on March 29.

### 3. **What rules must tourists follow during Nyepi Day?**

- Tourists must stay indoors and are not allowed to go outside. The use of vehicles, entertainment activities, and noise is prohibited. Many hotels and resorts offer limited services, such as silent buffets or room service.

### 4. **Will shops and restaurants be open during Nyepi Day?**

- On March 29, all shops, restaurants, and public services will be closed, with only emergency services (such as hospitals) remaining operational. Some shops may reopen on March 30, but operating hours may be shortened.

### 5. **Will electricity and WiFi be interrupted during Nyepi Day?**

- In some remote areas, such as Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, and Nusa Ceningan, electricity and WiFi may be interrupted. Hotels and resorts in major tourist areas typically maintain basic power supply but may impose usage restrictions.

### 6. **Can I arrive or depart from Bali during Nyepi Day?**

- No. Bali's airport will be closed on March 29th, and visitors will not be able to arrive or depart on this day. It is recommended that travelers arrive before March 27th or depart after March 31st.

### 7. What activities can you do during Nyepi Day?

- Visitors typically need to spend the day quietly at their accommodations. Some resorts may offer silent buffets or allow limited use of facilities, but most activities will be suspended. It's recommended to bring books or engage in meditation to enjoy the tranquility of the day.

### 8. What is the relationship between Nyepi Day and Eid al-Fitr?

- In 2025, Nyepi Day (March 29th) is very close to Eid al-Fitr (March 30th and 31st). Eid al-Fitr is an important holiday in Indonesia, during which there will be significant movement of people, and Bali will be very busy. It is recommended that travelers plan their itinerary in advance and book accommodations and transportation.

### 9. How to prepare for Nyepi Day?

- It is recommended that travelers book accommodations, transportation, and activities in advance, especially between March 27th and April 2nd. Ensure you have enough food and entertainment (such as books or music) for Nyepi Day, and respect local traditions and regulations.

### 10. What does Nyepi Day mean for non-Hindus?

- Although Nyepi Day is a Hindu holiday, it is an opportunity for reflection and relaxation for all residents and visitors in Bali. Participating in this traditional event allows for a deeper experience of Bali's culture and spirituality.

### 11. Can you use your phone or access the internet during Nyepi Day?

- Mobile phones and WiFi generally work fine in main tourist areas, but may be interrupted in remote regions. It's recommended to download necessary information in advance and minimize electronic device usage on this day to respect local customs.

### 12. Can I take photos or videos during Nyepi Day?

- While there's no explicit prohibition against photography or videography, visitors are advised to keep a low profile on this day, avoiding flash photography or making noise to respect local residents' customs.

We hope these FAQs help you better plan your Bali Nyepi Day itinerary and enjoy this unique cultural experience!
