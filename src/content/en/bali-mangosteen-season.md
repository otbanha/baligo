Mangosteen is one of the tropical fruits extensively produced in Indonesia. This sweet and tangy fruit typically has a three-month harvest season in a given region.

However, due to Indonesia's vast territory, mangosteen harvests normally continue from November to April across different areas. In Indonesia, the mangosteen harvest season usually begins in Sumatra. Farmers in this province typically start harvesting mangosteen at the end of the year, from November to December. This is followed by Java/Bali from January to March.

The variation in mangosteen harvest seasons across Indonesia means this tropical fruit is available in markets for almost eight months of the year.

## Mangosteen in Bali

Bali is one of the main producers of mangosteen in Indonesia, accounting for 50% of the country's total production. The mangosteen varieties grown in Bali are of excellent quality with superb taste! Therefore, when visiting Bali, you must not miss the opportunity to try this delicious fruit! In Indonesian, mangosteen is called "manggis".

## Best Season to Buy Mangosteen

Like most fruits, mangosteen prices tend to be cheaper during harvest season. During harvest season, mangosteen costs about IDR 10,000 per kilogram. Known as the "Queen of Tropical Fruits", its price can increase three to four times when production decreases.

To buy fresh and affordable mangosteen in Indonesia/Bali, the best time is usually between January and April.
