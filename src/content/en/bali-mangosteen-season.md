Mangosteen is one of the tropical fruits abundantly produced in Indonesia. This sweet and tangy fruit typically has a three-month harvest season in a given region.

However, due to Indonesia's vast territory, mangosteen harvests normally span from November to April across different areas. In Indonesia, the mangosteen harvest season usually begins in Sumatra. Farmers in this province typically start harvesting mangosteens at the end of the year, from November to December. This is followed by Java and Bali from January to March.

The variation in mangosteen harvest seasons across Indonesia ensures that this tropical fruit is available in markets for nearly eight months of the year.

## Mangosteen in Bali

Bali is one of the main producers of mangosteen in Indonesia, accounting for 50% of the country's total production. The mangosteens grown in Bali are of excellent variety and have an exceptional taste! Therefore, when visiting Bali, you must not miss the opportunity to try this delicious fruit! In Indonesian, mangosteen is called "manggis".

## The Best Season to Buy Mangosteen

Like most fruits, mangosteen prices tend to be cheaper during the harvest season. During the harvest season, mangosteen costs around IDR 10,000 per kilogram. However, the price of this fruit, known as the "Queen of Tropical Fruits", can increase three to four times when production decreases.

To buy fresh and affordable mangosteen in Indonesia/Bali, the best time is typically between January and April.
