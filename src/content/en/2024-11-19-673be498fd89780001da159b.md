The Maluku Islands are located in eastern Indonesia, geographically:

- East of Sulawesi Island
- West of Papua
- Marked in yellow on the map
- Comprising multiple islands, including the provinces of North Maluku (Maluku Utara) and Maluku

Central Maluku (Maluku Tengah) is located within Maluku Province and is one of its regencies (Kabupaten). This region lies in the Maluku Islands of eastern Indonesia, known for its natural landscapes and cultural diversity, making it an ideal choice for travelers who love exploring off-the-beaten-path destinations.

---

## 1. Geography and Climate 🌦

Central Maluku encompasses multiple islands, primarily Seram Island and its surrounding smaller islands. These islands are situated in the waters where the Indian Ocean and Pacific Ocean meet, featuring a tropical rainforest climate that is warm and humid year-round, perfect for diving and marine activities.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57c6d073011f69505b098fcad7c34d6f.jpg)

---

## 2. Natural Landscapes 🏝️

Central Maluku is a paradise for nature lovers, with its pristine tropical rainforests and crystal-clear waters attracting countless adventurers. Here are some must-see natural attractions:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad1e695a7be1c5cad8bfce79b7be2668.jpg)

- **Manusela National Park**  
  Manusela National Park is a key attraction on Seram Island, boasting dense rainforests and rich biodiversity, making it a dream destination for hikers and wildlife enthusiasts. It is also home to many endemic bird and plant species.
- **Ora Beach**  
  Known as Indonesia's version of the Maldives, Ora Beach features pristine white sands, calm turquoise lagoons, and coral reefs perfect for snorkeling, making it especially suitable for water sports enthusiasts.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cf25a9f62b17efd19365e6e7abf3564.jpg)

- **Inland Tropical Rainforest**  
  The rainforest on Seram Island remains in its pristine state, perfect for hiking, camping, and observing the unique flora and fauna found only in this region.
- **Pantai Ngurtafur**  
  Features a white sandbar extending into the sea, resembling a long white ribbon. The sandbar can stretch up to 2 kilometers.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a7de8d6e803d45ae699dbe6b6fd12be9.jpg)

## 3. **Culture and History** 🛖

The Maluku Islands were once world-famous as the 'Spice Islands,' attracting Portuguese, Dutch, and British traders in the 16th century who competed to control the trade of cloves and nutmeg. Today, these islands still preserve diverse cultures and rich historical heritage.

- **Local Tribal Culture**  
  The central Maluku region is primarily inhabited by Malay and Papuan ethnic groups, each with their own traditional dances, music, and rituals, showcasing strong local cultural characteristics.
- **Spice Trade Relics**  
  Many historical sites, such as Dutch colonial buildings and spice plantations, continue to attract history enthusiasts.

---

## 4. **Transportation** 🚤

The most common way to reach central Maluku is by flying to Ambon, the capital of Maluku Province, and then taking a boat or domestic flight to Seram Island and other islands. Due to limited infrastructure in the area, travelers are advised to plan ahead.

---

## 5. **Travel Tips** 🎒

- **Best Time to Visit**: The ideal travel season is from May to October, when the weather is clear and perfect for outdoor activities.
- **Travel Experience**: It’s recommended to stay for at least 5-7 days to explore the main attractions and enjoy a leisurely island experience.
- **Essential Gear**: Since some areas are remote, it’s advisable to carry basic medical supplies and sufficient cash, as ATMs are scarce.
