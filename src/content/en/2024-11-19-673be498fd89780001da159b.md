Maluku (the Maluku Islands) is located in the eastern region of Indonesia, geographically:

- East of Sulawesi Island
- West of Papua
- Marked in yellow on the map
- Composed of multiple islands, including the provinces of North Maluku (Maluku Utara) and Maluku

Central Maluku (Maluku Tengah) is located within Maluku Province and is one of its regencies (Kabupaten). Situated in the eastern Maluku Islands of Indonesia, this area is renowned for its natural landscapes and cultural diversity, making it an ideal choice for travelers who love exploring off-the-beaten-path destinations.

---

## 1. **Geography and Climate** 🌦

Central Maluku encompasses several islands, primarily **Seram Island** and its surrounding smaller islands. These islands are nestled in the waters where the Indian Ocean and Pacific Ocean meet, experiencing a tropical rainforest climate that is warm and humid year-round, perfect for diving and marine activities.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57c6d073011f69505b098fcad7c34d6f.jpg)

---

## 2. **Natural Landscapes** 🏝️

Central Maluku is a paradise for nature lovers, with its pristine tropical rainforests and crystal-clear waters attracting countless adventurers. Here are some must-visit natural attractions:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ad1e695a7be1c5cad8bfce79b7be2668.jpg)

- **Manusela National Park**  
  Manusela National Park is a key attraction on Seram Island, featuring dense rainforests and rich biodiversity, making it a dream destination for hikers and wildlife enthusiasts. It is also home to many endemic bird species and plants.
- **Ora Beach**  
  Dubbed the "Indonesian Maldives," Ora Beach boasts pristine white sands, tranquil blue lagoons, and coral reefs perfect for snorkeling, making it especially appealing for water sports enthusiasts.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cf25a9f62b17efd19365e6e7abf3564.jpg)

- **Inland Tropical Rainforest**  
  The rainforest on Seram Island remains in its pristine state, ideal for hiking, camping, and observing many unique flora and fauna found only in this region.
- **Pantai Ngurtafur**  
  Features a white sandbar extending into the sea, resembling a long white ribbon. The sandbar can stretch up to 2 kilometers.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a7de8d6e803d45ae699dbe6b6fd12be9.jpg)

## 3. **Culture and History** 🛖

The Maluku Islands were once world-famous as the "Spice Islands," attracting Portuguese, Dutch, and British traders in the 16th century who competed to control the trade of cloves and nutmeg. Today, these islands still preserve diverse cultures and rich historical sites.

- **Local Tribal Culture**  
  The central Maluku region is predominantly inhabited by Malay and Papuan ethnic groups, each with their own traditional dances, music, and rituals, showcasing strong local cultural characteristics.
- **Spice Trade Relics**  
  Many historical sites, such as Dutch colonial buildings and spice plantations, continue to attract history enthusiasts.

---

## 4. **Transportation** 🚤

The most common way to reach central Maluku is by flying to Ambon, the capital of Maluku Province, and then taking a boat or domestic flight to Seram Island and other islands. Due to limited infrastructure in this region, travelers are advised to plan ahead.

---

## 5. **Travel Tips** 🎒

- **Best Time to Visit**: The best travel season is from May to October, when the weather is clear and ideal for outdoor activities.
- **Travel Experience**: It is recommended to stay for at least 5-7 days to explore the main attractions and enjoy leisurely island time.
- **Essential Gear**: As some areas are remote, it is advisable to carry basic medical supplies and sufficient cash, as ATMs are scarce.
