## 🗞️ Today's Highlights: International Events and Infrastructure Transformation

- **The 12th 'Bali and Beyond Travel Fair (BBTF 2026)' is Coming Soon**: This leading Indonesian travel trade show is scheduled for **May 28-30** at the Nusa Dua International Convention Center. This year's theme focuses on 'Redefining Indonesia's Culinary Journey,' aiming to boost Bali's global tourism competitiveness by showcasing its rich food culture.
- **'Waste-to-Energy (PSEL)' Facility Set to Start Construction in July**: In response to the waste crisis triggered by the closure of the Suwung landfill, the provincial government has finalized plans to begin construction of the PSEL facility in **July 2026**. The project is expected to take 18 months and will significantly enhance the island's waste processing efficiency upon completion. During this transitional period, several hotels have jointly purchased 'organic incinerators' to process their own food waste, reducing pressure on landfills.
- **Southeast Asian Sports Diplomacy Results**: ASEAN youth and sports ministers recently reached a consensus in Bali and issued the 'Bali Declaration,' aimed at strengthening regional sports cooperation and advancing the dream of a joint Southeast Asian bid for the Olympics.

## 📊 Clarifying Tourist Source Data: Malaysia vs. Australia

- **Australia Remains Bali's 'Quarterly' Leader**: According to the latest briefing from the Bali Statistics Agency on May 5, **Australia** was still the largest source of tourists to Bali in the first quarter of 2026 (January-March), accounting for over 25% of total arrivals in March.
- **Malaysia's 'Monthly' Breakthrough**: The widely discussed 'Australia fell from first place' refers to **specific statistics for March 2026**, where **Malaysia** surpassed Australia (approximately 130,000 arrivals) with around 186,000 arrivals, becoming the fastest-growing source market for all of Indonesia (including Bali) that month.
- **Conclusion on Tourist Source Structure**: Malaysia currently holds the top spot in **total arrivals across Indonesia**; however, for **Bali as a single destination**, Australia and Malaysia are in a period of intense competition for the top position.

## 📄 Topics of Concern for Tourists: Safety Warnings and New Travel Routes

- **Flood Risk Monitoring and Safety Reminders**: The Ministry of Tourism recently issued a warning that due to the influence of the 'Rossby equatorial wave,' several areas on the island have experienced extreme rainfall leading to flooding (most severe in the south). Tourists are advised to avoid areas prone to flash floods and landslides, and to consult local operators before engaging in water activities.
- **Immigration Enforcement and Case Reports**: In addition to intensifying crackdowns on illegal workers (arresting 62 more foreign violators in early May), the immigration office is currently investigating cases of online fraud and illegal brokerage involving foreign nationals. Tourists are reminded to always choose officially licensed agencies when seeking travel advice or making investments.
