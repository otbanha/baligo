Pandawa Beach in Uluwatu, Bali's southernmost tip, is often called the 'Blue Highway' because of its breathtaking limestone cliffs and beautiful white sand, attracting more and more visitors. However, due to the sharp increase in tourists over the past year and a half, local authorities have decided to modify access routes to improve traffic flow and safety for pedestrians and vehicles.

![峇里島新景點 Tanah Barak](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7bfc289c1a511e6bc49e67cf8c4bb07d.jpg)

Bali's New Attraction: Tanah Barak

Tanah Barak at Pandawa Beach is the most popular entrance. Visitors walking from the parking lot to the beach must pass through a narrow cliffside path, which has become a hotspot for photos. To enhance pedestrian safety, private vehicles are now prohibited from using this path. Instead, authorities have introduced a shuttle service, which not only protects pedestrians but also reduces traffic congestion in the area, making the environment cleaner and more organized.

Local official Ni Luh Hepi Wiradani explained that this policy was developed based on visitor feedback. 'Many tourists expressed a desire for more order at Tanah Barak, with fewer vehicles disrupting their experience. That's why we adjusted the traffic flow to make everyone's visit more enjoyable.'

![Tanah Berak 一點不輸給日本著名景點「立山黑部」](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8129df44716b2513f37dca46a64237cd.jpg)

Tanah Barak Rivals Japan's Famous Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route

Now, whether arriving by motorcycle or car, visitors must park at the Lelangon parking lot and either walk or take the shuttle to the beach. The ticket price remains unchanged at IDR 25,000 per person, applicable to both domestic and international tourists.

The new system has been in place for a few days and has received positive feedback, with both tourists and the local community expressing satisfaction. 'Since the policy was implemented, visitors have reported feeling more at ease without the interference of vehicles,' said a spokesperson.

The shuttle service can transport up to 800 visitors daily between the parking lot and the beach. For those who wish to photograph the cliffside path, walking remains the best option. During peak tourist seasons, Pandawa Beach can welcome over 2,000 visitors daily. What was once a 'hidden gem' has now become a must-visit destination for both local and international travelers.

The management team at Pandawa Beach is rapidly adjusting their approach to handle the increasing number of visitors. For instance, during this year's Eid al-Fitr holiday, Pandawa Beach saw daily visitor numbers ranging from 2,500 to 3,000, posing a significant challenge for the management.

Local leaders are also considering new zoning plans, aiming to divide the beach into several specialized areas. These include a public beach zone, a water sports zone, a beach club zone, a dining area, and a private beach section reserved for events like weddings and proposals.
