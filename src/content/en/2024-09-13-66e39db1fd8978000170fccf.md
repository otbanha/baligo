At Bali's southernmost tip in Uluwatu lies Pandawa Beach, known by some as the 'Blue Highway'. With its magnificent limestone cliffs and pristine white sand, it has become a must-visit destination for increasing numbers of tourists. However, with visitor numbers surging over the past year and a half, local authorities have decided to adjust access methods to ensure smoother and safer traffic flow for both pedestrians and vehicles.

![峇里島新景點 Tanah Barak](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_7bfc289c1a511e6bc49e67cf8c4bb07d.jpg)

Bali's New Attraction: Tanah Barak

The Tanah Barak area of Pandawa Beach is the most popular entrance point. Visitors walking from the parking lot to the beach must pass through a narrow cliffside pathway, which has become a hotspot for photos. To enhance pedestrian safety, private vehicles are now prohibited from using this pathway. Instead, authorities have introduced shuttle services that not only protect pedestrians but also reduce traffic pressure in the surrounding area, making the environment appear cleaner.

Local official Ni Luh Hepi Wiradani explained that this policy was developed based on visitor feedback. 'Many visitors expressed a desire for more orderliness in Tanah Barak, with fewer vehicles disrupting their experience. Therefore, we adjusted the traffic flow to make everyone's visit more enjoyable.'

![Tanah Berak 一點不輸給日本著名景點「立山黑部」](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8129df44716b2513f37dca46a64237cd.jpg)

Tanah Barak Rivals Japan's Famous Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route

Now, whether arriving by motorbike or car, visitors must park at the Lelangon parking lot and proceed to the beach either on foot or by shuttle. The ticket price remains unchanged at IDR 25,000 per person, applicable to both domestic and international visitors.

This new system has been operational for several days and has received positive feedback, with both tourists and the local community expressing satisfaction with the new measures. 'Since the implementation of this policy, visitors have reported feeling more at ease without the interference of vehicles,'

The shuttle service can transport up to 800 visitors daily between the parking lot and the beach. For those wishing to photograph the cliffside pathway, walking remains the best option. During peak tourist seasons, Pandawa Beach can welcome over 2,000 visitors daily. What was once a 'hidden gem' has now become a must-visit destination for both domestic and international tourists.

The management team at Pandawa Beach is quickly adapting their strategies to handle the surge in visitors. During this year's Eid al-Fitr holiday, daily visitor numbers reached between 2,500 to 3,000, presenting a significant challenge for the management.

Local leaders are also considering new zoning plans for the beach, which would divide it into several specialized areas including a public beach zone, water sports zone, beach club zone, dining area, and a private beach section reserved for events like weddings and proposals.

Pandawa Beach is located between Green Bowl Beach (see [Xiao Jie's Indonesia guide](https://youtu.be/8mzJm_UrjSg)) and Pantai Gunung Payung.
