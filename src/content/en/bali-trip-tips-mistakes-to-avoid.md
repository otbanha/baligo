This time, I want to share my travel experience in Bali with everyone. Bali, known as the 'Island of the Gods', attracts tourists from all over the world with its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. From visa processing, private car services to various attractions and restaurants, we fully experienced the local charm, though we also encountered some minor incidents. I hope my sharing can provide some reference and help to friends planning to visit Bali, making your journey smoother and more enjoyable.

## Visa:

Seeing many problems encountered by others when applying for the electronic visa (eVOA) in the group, we decided to opt for the visa on arrival. Upon arrival at the airport, we waited in line for about 15-20 minutes before it was our turn, which was acceptable. However, even though the visa processing was relatively quick, baggage claim still took some time, so we felt a bit helpless while waiting for our luggage😅. Therefore, if you choose to apply for a visa on arrival, please allow sufficient time to cope with possible delays.

## Private Car Driver:

When booking private car services, do not overly trust online reviews. We booked a service based on a recommendation from a group member in a Facebook group called 'Brother X Private Car Team', but the actual situation was not as smooth as expected🥲. The driver assigned to us, 'Special X', was not very proactive, responded to messages very slowly, and was careless in arranging the itinerary. Every time we arrived at a destination, he wouldn't notify us unless we asked.

The driver said the fee included gasoline and parking, but when we went to the Cliff Road, we were asked to pay Rp5000 for parking when purchasing tickets, and this fee was ultimately paid by us. Since the driver was always the one communicating with the ticket seller, he couldn't have been unaware of this parking fee. This made us feel that the driver was not honest, which was very dissatisfying😅.

On one occasion, when we were settling the bill, the driver informed us that due to it being a holiday, an additional Rp50,000 would be charged. This left us very confused, as I had specifically confirmed the price twice before departure, and the driver had assured us there would be no changes. This last-minute price hike left us feeling dissatisfied. Therefore, when booking private car services, it's crucial to carefully verify the price and terms to avoid encountering similar situations.

### Jimbaran:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_25e33167db824cfc14c33b417c1d0709.jpg)

Li Wallis shares

We decided not to dine on seafood by the beach in Jimbaran, as we heard the prices there are exorbitant, with some spending over Rp1 million on seafood. Instead, we ate at Waroeng Pan Yoga, where we ordered a large fish (actually two fish, possibly due to a shortage of larger ones 😅), fried calamari rings, squid skewers, and clams, along with two drinks, all for just over Rp200,000. We enjoyed our meal thoroughly, and the quality was good! However, it seems to be a Jimbaran specialty that the seafood is grilled until it's a bit charred 🤣.

## Souvenirs:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_101ac5a2786fcaa5db6ac954775e1089.jpg)

Li Wallis shares

The souvenirs at Ubud Market are almost identical, with nothing particularly enticing us to buy. On the contrary, we found more joy in browsing the supermarkets, even though the prices were slightly higher. We stumbled upon Bali Galeria Mall, which was fantastic to explore! It has a supermarket, souvenir shops, and restaurants, offering everything you could need. We initially wanted to find a post office to send postcards, but it was already closed 🥲. So, if you're planning to visit the post office, remember to go early. In the end, we bought stamps and found a mailbox at the airport.

## Other Insights

- Some restrooms require payment, and in a rush, we had to pay Rp3,000 😅😂
- Prices at the airport are very high, so it's advisable to buy all your necessities and souvenirs before heading there. For example, bubble tea costs Rp65,000, a Vietnamese baguette is Rp120,000, and a large Aqua water is Rp20,000
- Don't forget to bring an eco-bag! We only realized **Bali doesn't provide plastic bags** when shopping at the supermarket, and ended up buying an eco-bag for Rp15,000, which was really expensive 🥺.
