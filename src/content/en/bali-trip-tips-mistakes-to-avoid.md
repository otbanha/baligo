This time, I want to share my travel experience in Bali with everyone. Bali, known as the 'Island of the Gods,' attracts visitors from all over the world with its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. During this trip, we experienced everything from visa processing, private car services, to major attractions and restaurants, immersing ourselves in the local culture. Of course, we also encountered some minor hiccups. I hope my sharing can provide some reference and help for friends planning to visit Bali, making your journey smoother and more enjoyable.

## Visa:

Since we saw many people encountering issues with the electronic visa (eVOA) in group chats, we decided to opt for the visa on arrival. Upon arriving at the airport, we waited in line for about 15-20 minutes before it was our turn, which was relatively acceptable. However, even though the visa process was quick, baggage claim still took some time, leaving us feeling a bit frustrated😅. Therefore, if you choose to get a visa on arrival, please allocate sufficient time to handle possible delays.

## Private Car Driver:

When booking private car services, be sure not to overly trust online reviews. We booked a service based on recommendations from a group member in a Facebook group called 'Brother X Private Car Driver Team,' but the actual experience was not as smooth as expected🥲. The assigned driver, 'Special X,' was not very proactive, responded to messages very slowly, and was careless in arranging the itinerary. Every time we arrived at a destination, he wouldn't notify us unless we asked.

The driver said the fee included gas and parking, but when we went to the Cliff Road, we were asked to pay Rp5000 for parking when purchasing tickets, and this fee ended up being paid by us. Since the driver was always the one communicating with the ticket seller, he couldn't have been unaware of this parking fee. This made us feel that the driver was not honest, which was very dissatisfying😅.

On one occasion, when we were settling the bill, the driver informed us that due to it being a holiday, an additional Rp50,000 would be charged. This left us very confused, as I had specifically confirmed the price twice before departure, and the driver had assured us there would be no changes. This last-minute price hike left us feeling dissatisfied. Therefore, when booking private car services, it's crucial to carefully verify the price and terms to avoid encountering similar situations.

### Jimbaran:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_25e33167db824cfc14c33b417c1d0709.jpg)

Li Wallis provided

We didn't choose to eat seafood by the beach in Jimbaran because we heard the prices there are very high, with some people spending over Rp1 million on seafood. Instead, we went to Waroeng Pan Yoga for a meal. We ordered a large fish (actually two fish, probably because the big fish was out of stock 😅), fried calamari rings, squid skewers, and clams, plus two drinks, all for just over Rp200,000. We had a great time and the quality was good! However, it might be a Jimbaran specialty—the seafood was all a bit charred 🤣.

## Souvenirs:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_101ac5a2786fcaa5db6ac954775e1089.jpg)

Li Wallis provided

The souvenirs at Ubud Market were almost all the same, and nothing particularly caught our eye. On the contrary, we were happier shopping at the supermarket, even though the prices were slightly higher. We stumbled upon Bali Galeria Mall, which was a great find! It has a supermarket, souvenir shops, and restaurants—everything you could need. We originally wanted to find a post office to send postcards, but it was already closed 🥲. So, if you plan to visit the post office, remember to go early. In the end, we bought stamps at the airport and found a mailbox.

## Other Tips

- Some restrooms require payment, and since we were in a hurry, we had to pay Rp3,000 😅😂
- Prices at the airport are very high, so we recommend buying everything you need, including souvenirs, before heading there. For example, bubble tea costs Rp65,000, a Vietnamese sandwich is Rp120,000, and a large Aqua water bottle is Rp20,000.
- Don’t forget to bring a reusable bag! We only found out **Bali doesn’t provide plastic bags** when we were shopping at the supermarket, so we had to buy a reusable bag for Rp15,000—it was really expensive 🥺.
