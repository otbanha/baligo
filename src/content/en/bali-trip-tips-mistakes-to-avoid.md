This time, I want to share my travel experiences in Bali with everyone. Bali, known as the 'Island of the Gods,' attracts visitors from all over the world with its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. During this trip, we experienced everything from visa processing, private car services, to various attractions and restaurants, immersing ourselves in the local culture. Of course, we also encountered some minor incidents. I hope my sharing can provide some reference and help for those planning to visit Bali, making your journey smoother and more enjoyable.

## Visa:

Since we saw many people encountering issues with the electronic visa (eVOA) in the group, we decided to opt for the visa on arrival. After arriving at the airport, we queued for about 15-20 minutes before it was our turn, which was relatively acceptable. However, even though the visa processing was quick, baggage claim still took some time, so we felt a bit helpless while waiting for our luggage 😅. Therefore, if you choose to get a visa on arrival, please allocate sufficient time to handle possible delays.

## Private Car Driver:

When booking private car services, everyone must not be too trusting of online reviews. We booked a service based on a recommendation from a group member in a Facebook group called 'X Brother Private Car Driver Team,' but the actual situation was not as smooth as expected 🥲. The assigned driver, 'Special X,' was not very proactive, responded to messages very slowly, and was careless in arranging the itinerary. Every time we arrived at a destination, he wouldn't notify us unless we asked.

The driver said the fee included gasoline and parking fees, but when we went to the cliff road, we were asked to pay Rp5000 for parking when purchasing tickets, and this fee ultimately fell on us. Since the driver was always the one communicating with the ticket seller, he couldn't have been unaware of this parking fee. This made us feel that the driver was not honest, which was very dissatisfying 😅.

On one occasion, when we were settling the bill, the driver informed us that an additional Rp50,000 would be charged due to it being a holiday. This left us quite confused, as I had specifically confirmed the price twice before departure, and the driver had assured us there would be no changes. This last-minute price hike left us feeling dissatisfied. Therefore, when booking private car services, it's crucial to carefully verify the price and terms to avoid encountering similar situations.

### Jimbaran:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_25e33167db824cfc14c33b417c1d0709.jpg)

Provided by Li Wallis

We chose not to dine on seafood by the beach in Jimbaran, as we heard the prices there are exorbitant, with some spending over Rp1 million on seafood. Instead, we went to Waroeng Pan Yoga for a meal. We ordered a large fish (actually two fish, perhaps because they were out of large ones 😅), fried calamari rings, squid skewers, and clams, along with two drinks, all for just over Rp200,000. We enjoyed our meal thoroughly, and the quality was good! However, perhaps it's a Jimbaran specialty, but the seafood was a bit charred 🤣.

## Souvenirs:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_101ac5a2786fcaa5db6ac954775e1089.jpg)

Provided by Li Wallis

The souvenirs at Ubud Market were almost all the same, with nothing particularly catching our eye. On the contrary, we had more fun shopping at the supermarket, even though the prices were slightly higher. We stumbled upon Bali Galeria Mall, which was a great find! It has a supermarket, souvenir shops, and restaurants—everything you could need. We originally wanted to find a post office to send postcards, but it was already closed 🥲. So, if you plan to visit the post office, remember to go early. In the end, we bought stamps at the airport and found a mailbox there.

## Other Tips

- Some restrooms require payment, and in a rush, we had to pay Rp3,000 😅😂
- Prices at the airport are very high, so it's advisable to buy all necessary items and souvenirs before heading there. For example, bubble tea costs Rp65,000, a Vietnamese sandwich is Rp120,000, and a large Aqua water is Rp20,000
- Don't forget to bring a reusable bag! We only found out while shopping at the supermarket that **Bali does not provide plastic bags**, so we had to buy a reusable bag for Rp15,000, which was quite expensive 🥺.
