Today I want to share my travel experience in Bali. Known as the 'Island of the Gods,' Bali draws travelers from around the world with its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. On this trip, we experienced everything from visa processing and private driver services to major attractions and restaurants, fully immersing ourselves in the local flavor—though we did encounter a few minor hiccups. I hope my sharing can offer some useful tips and help for those planning a trip to Bali, making your journey smoother and more enjoyable.

## Visa:

Since we saw many people in the group running into issues with the eVOA (electronic visa on arrival), we decided to go with the visa on arrival instead. After arriving at the airport, we waited in line for about 15–20 minutes before it was our turn—pretty reasonable. However, even though the visa process was relatively quick, collecting our luggage still took a while, leaving us feeling a bit helpless while waiting 😅. So if you choose the visa on arrival, make sure to allow enough time for potential delays.

## Private Driver:

When booking a private driver, don't put too much trust in online reviews. We booked a service through a group recommended by a fellow traveler in a Facebook group—'Brother X Private Driver Team'—but the actual experience was far from smooth 🥲. The driver assigned to us, a certain 'Special X,' was not very proactive: he replied to messages extremely slowly, his responses were vague, and he put little effort into planning the itinerary. Every time we arrived at a destination, he wouldn't let us know unless we asked.

The driver said the fee included gas and parking, but when we went to the cliff road, we were asked to pay Rp5,000 for parking at the entrance, and we ended up covering it. Since the driver handled all communication with the ticket staff, there's no way he didn't know about this parking fee. It made us feel the driver wasn't entirely honest, which was really frustrating 😅.

Another time, on the day we settled the bill, the driver told us it was a holiday and we needed to pay an extra Rp50,000. This was very confusing because I had confirmed the price twice before departure, and the driver said there would be no changes. This last-minute price hike left us dissatisfied. So, when booking a private car service, make sure to double-check the price and terms to avoid similar issues.

We didn’t opt for seafood on Jimbaran Beach because we heard the prices are sky-high—some people spent over a million IDR on seafood. ## Souvenirs:

![Li Wallis 提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_101ac5a2786fcaa5db6ac954775e1089.jpg)

Photo by Li Wallis

The souvenirs at Ubud Market are almost all identical, with nothing that really caught our eye. On the flip side, we had more fun browsing supermarkets, even though prices were slightly higher. We stumbled upon Bali Galeria Mall, which was a fantastic find! It has a supermarket, souvenir shops, and restaurants—everything you need. We originally wanted to find a post office to send postcards, but it was already closed 🥲. So, if you’re planning to visit the post office, make sure to go early. In the end, we bought stamps at the airport and found a mailbox.

## Other Tips

- Some restrooms charge a fee—when nature calls, 3,000 IDR is a small price to pay 😅😂
- Prices at the airport are very high, so it’s best to buy all your essentials and souvenirs before heading there. For example, bubble tea costs 65,000 IDR, a Vietnamese sandwich is 120,000 IDR, and a large bottle of Aqua is 20,000 IDR
- Don’t forget to bring a reusable bag! We only found out at the supermarket that **Bali doesn’t provide plastic bags**, so we had to buy one for 15,000 IDR—ouch, that was pricey 🥺.
