**Bottom Line**: Located in Gianyar, Bali Reptile Park lets you get up close with Komodo dragons, touch giant pythons and chameleons. **Plan for about 2.5-3 hours** (many visitors stay up to 4 hours). Its biggest perk is being **right next to Bali Bird Park, sharing the same parking lot**, so you can easily visit two very different animal parks in one go. It's a great stop on a Ubud day trip or en route from Ubud to Kuta, and a solid indoor option for escaping the summer heat.

When people think of Bali, most picture endless blue oceans, golden sunsets on sandy beaches, serene rice terraces, and sacred temples. These are indeed Bali's charming sides, but if you want to add a touch of raw, wild, even 'dinosaur-era' excitement to your trip, I sincerely invite you to venture deeper into Gianyar.

There, you'll find a fantastical world rarely known to tourists—**Bali Reptile Park**.

This isn't just a zoo; it's more like a living museum showcasing the reptile ecology of the Indonesian archipelago. Even better, it's **right next to Bali Bird Park**, sharing the same parking lot. Just a few steps and you can switch scenes, from the 'silent world of reptiles' to the 'colorful paradise of birds.' With good timing, you can easily enjoy two distinct yet equally fascinating animal kingdoms in one visit.

## Why Is It Worth a Special Trip?

[https://youtu.be/Gt3_BFN1KsY?si=QwJsTRCx8zz7iTAO](https://youtu.be/Gt3_BFN1KsY?si=QwJsTRCx8zz7iTAO)

Bali Reptile Park is the island's first and only reptile-themed park. The design is thoughtfully crafted, ditching the cold iron cages and concrete floors of traditional zoos for an immersive tropical rainforest environment. Walking along shaded paths with the sound of flowing water, you'll need to search a bit to spot the residents hidden among leaves and rock crevices. This sense of 'exploration' makes the whole experience full of surprises.

The park isn't huge, but the pathways are well-designed. A thorough visit takes about **2.5 to 3 hours**, and most visitors end up staying for 4 hours. It's a perfect stop on a Ubud day trip or as a midway point when traveling from Ubud to Kuta in the south.

## A Unique Interactive Experience

This is what impressed me most about the Bali Reptile Park — the "close encounters"!

During scheduled times (or you can ask the staff), under the guidance of professional keepers, you have the chance to **touch and even drape a docile python over your shoulders**! The keeper will first explain the safety rules in detail and stay by your side throughout. When that cool, dry, and finely textured snake skin glides across your skin, the sensation is truly magical. This isn't something you can experience just anywhere — it's definitely one of the most unique "trophies" of your trip.

## Ultimate One-Day Two-Park Strategy! How to Plan Perfectly?

This is what I think is the smartest, most cost-effective way to do it!

**【Morning】Bali Reptile Park (About 3 Hours)**
I recommend arriving **right when it opens at 9:00 AM**. The weather is cooler, and the reptiles are more active. Head straight to the Komodo dragon and giant crocodile areas to snap photos before the crowds. The entire park takes about 3 hours to explore, finishing just before noon. By then, the sun is strong and you'll be tired — perfect timing to head to the next park.

【Lunch】Dining at the Park Restaurant
Both parks have their own restaurants. The reptile park's eatery is simpler, while the bird park offers more choices, including indoor air-conditioned seating. You can grab lunch here and take a break.

【Afternoon】Bali Bird Park – Recommended 3-4 Hours
After lunch, just walk next door to the bird park! This vast park is home to over 250 species and thousands of birds from the Indonesian archipelago and around the world. You can step into massive aviaries where adorable hornbills and parrots fly overhead; don't miss the scheduled parrot show and raptor show—they're spectacular! The park's landscaping is also incredibly tropical, with waterfalls, lotus ponds, and traditional Balinese architecture—every shot looks like a postcard.

## Must-Read Before You Go! FAQ

To help you plan your trip smoothly, we've compiled the most frequently asked questions:

**Q1: Do I need to buy separate tickets for the reptile park and bird park?**

**A:** Yes, the two parks are independently ticketed. Although they are right next to each other, there is no combo ticket. However, you can purchase e-tickets separately through travel platforms like Klook or KKday, which are usually cheaper than buying at the gate and save you queuing time. If you only have time for one, I'd recommend the reptile park (since it's more unique), but if you have enough time, I highly suggest visiting both—the experiences are completely different!

**Q2: How long does it take to visit both parks?**

**A:** The reptile park takes about 3 hours (including detailed viewing, photos, and interactive experiences). The bird park is larger, so allow 3-4 hours (including shows, exploring, and photos). So a 'two-park day' totals about 6-7 hours. I suggest entering at 9 AM and finishing around 4 PM—plenty of time.

**Q3: Is it suitable for kids? What's the minimum age?**

**A:** It's very suitable for a family trip! The park is very child-friendly, with smooth paths that are easy for strollers. However, I'd recommend it for kids **3 years and older**, as they can already grasp the concept of 'animals' and are less likely to be scared. For younger children (0-2 years old), they might be frightened by large reptiles (especially crocodiles and giant pythons), so parents can adjust based on the situation. During interactive experiences, the keepers will assess the child's height and bravery, and won't force participation.

**Q4: Do the snake touching and holding experiences cost extra?**

**A:** This is important! Currently (based on most visitors' experiences), **the snake touching and holding experiences are included in the ticket price, with no extra charge**! Just ask the keepers when they're around, and they'll be happy to help. However, it's a good idea to prepare a small tip (e.g., 20,000-50,000 Indonesian Rupiah, about USD$1-2) for the hardworking keepers to thank them for their expert explanations and safety care.

**Q5: What's the best time to go? Are there fixed feeding shows?**

**A:** The best time is to enter **right when the park opens at 9:00 AM**! Here are three reasons:

- **Cooler weather**: Reptiles are cold-blooded; if it's too hot, they'll hide, so they're more active in the morning.
- **Fewer crowds**: You can take photos at a leisurely pace without the crowds.
- **Avoid afternoon thunderstorms**: Bali often has brief heavy rain in the afternoon.
As for feeding shows, it's best to ask the front desk for the **exact time** when you enter (they're usually held on holidays or busy days, but not always on weekdays). Even if there's no show, just observing the animals is still fascinating.

**Q6: Is there a restaurant in the park? Can I bring my own food?**

**A:** The reptile park has a **simple café** selling drinks, light snacks, instant noodles, etc., but the selection is limited. The bird park's restaurant has a wider variety, including Indonesian dishes, Western-style meals, and air-conditioned seating. You can also **bring your own food and water** into the park, as there are no strict restrictions. I'd suggest bringing your own water bottle (you'll get thirsty in the heat) and having lunch at the bird park's restaurant for the most convenience.

**Q7: Can I take photos or videos?**

**A:** **Absolutely, and it's very welcome!** There are no areas in the park where photography is banned (except for a few marked spots, such as some venomous snake exhibits where flash might disturb the animals). However, here are a few tips:

- **Turn off the flash**: Strong flashes can startle reptiles and even harm their eyes.
- **Respect other visitors**: Don’t block the display windows for too long just to take photos.
- **During interactive experiences**: Let the keepers take the photos—they’re great at finding the best angles!

**Q8: Is it easy to get there? Is it easy to get a ride back?**

**A:** The reptile park is **not in the bustling city center**, but getting there isn’t too difficult.

- **Best option: Private car charter**. It costs about 600,000–800,000 IDR (around USD$19–$26) per day, and the driver will wait while you explore both parks—no worries at all.
- **Second best: Grab / Gojek one-way ride**. It’s usually easy to get a ride there, but **for the return trip, open the app 15–20 minutes in advance**, as there aren’t many cars in the area and you may need to wait.
- **Not recommended: Taking the bus or hailing a taxi on the street**. There’s no direct bus, and street taxis are scarce, often overcharging you.

**Q9: Do I need to buy tickets online in advance?**

**A:** I’d recommend **buying tickets in advance on platforms like Klook, KKday, or Trip.com**! Here’s why:

- **Cheaper**: Usually 10–20% less than the on-site price.
- **Skip the line**: On-site ticket purchases sometimes require queuing, while e-tickets let you scan a QR code and enter directly.
- **Avoid sellouts**: During peak season, on-site tickets might sell out (especially on holidays).
Of course, you can still pay with cash at the venue, but you’ll need to spend a bit more time in line.

**Q10: Are there other nearby attractions I can visit?**

**A:** Absolutely! The reptile park and bird park are located in the **Sukawati** area, which also has these great spots:

- **Bali Zoo**: About a 15-minute drive away, where you can have breakfast with orangutans.
- **Sukawati Art Market**: About a 10-minute drive, perfect for buying souvenirs and bargaining.
- **Goa Gajah (Elephant Cave)**: About a 15-minute drive, a historic archaeological site.
- **Tegalalang Rice Terrace**: About a 20-minute drive, offering classic Balinese rice terrace views.
You can plan a day like “morning at the reptile park + bird park → afternoon at 1–2 nearby attractions” for a very fulfilling trip!

## Travel Tips & Suggestions

1. **Attire & Protection**:
    - **Wear comfortable shoes**: Both parks require a lot of walking on stone paths and grass.
    - **Bring insect repellent**: Mosquitoes thrive in tropical areas with dense vegetation.
    - **Don't skip sunscreen**: Despite shaded areas, there are plenty of open spaces.
    - **Pack a compact umbrella**: Bali often sees brief afternoon showers—better safe than sorry.
2. **What to Bring**:
    - **Camera or phone**: The photo ops are incredible! For the bird park, a telephoto lens is recommended.
    - **Drinking water**: Vending machines and small shops are available, but bringing your own is more convenient.
    - **Some cash**: Handy for small purchases (drinks, ice cream, tips for guides).
3. **Mindset**:
    - The animals at the reptile park won't greet you like cats or dogs. Approach with patience and observation, quietly appreciating their 'static beauty.'
    - During interactive experiences, **follow the keeper's instructions exactly**—avoid sudden movements or loud noises to prevent startling the animals.

Bali Reptile Park may not be as famous as Tanah Lot or Ubud Palace, but its very 'low-key' nature preserves more educational and exploratory charm. Standing before a Komodo dragon or touching a cold python, you'll realize Bali's allure goes far beyond sun and sand—here lies a more primal, authentic Indonesia.

On your next Bali trip, consider adding this fantastical reptile world to your itinerary!
