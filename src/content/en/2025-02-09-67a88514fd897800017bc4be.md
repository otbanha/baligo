## 💡 How to Avoid Common Mistakes in Indonesia Visa Applications?

The Indonesian Immigration Bureau recently released a series of visa application guidelines, reminding foreign tourists how to avoid common mistakes to improve visa approval rates. As Indonesia's visa application process has fully transitioned to online processing, while procedures have become more convenient, error rates have also increased.

Officials stated: 'Having a visa rejected is frustrating, but many situations can be avoided! We will tell you how to improve your application success rate.'

Now, let's take a look at the most common mistakes in Indonesia visa applications and how to avoid them.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_fa817dcbf8920a031cce812823af7cc3.jpg)

---

## ✅ Core Recommendation: Use the Official eVisa Website

The Indonesian Immigration Bureau emphasizes that all applicants should apply for an eVisa only through the official website:

👉 [www.evisa.imigrasi.go.id](https://www.evisa.imigrasi.go.id/)

There are many fake websites online attempting to deceive applicants, so be sure to confirm the URL is correct. Additionally, if the website loads slowly, try turning off VPN or ad-blocking software, and it is recommended to use a computer for the best application experience.

---

## 📌 Top 5 Reasons for Indonesian Visa Application Rejection

### 1️⃣ Incomplete or Missing Documents

This is one of the most common reasons for visa rejection. If the submitted materials are incomplete, immigration officers cannot verify the applicant's eligibility, leading to the application being denied.

Solution:

- Carefully read the official requirements for the visa type
- Ensure all documents are prepared, including passport, identification, financial proof, etc.
- Double-check all required documents before submitting the application

---

### 2️⃣ Incorrect Application Form

Indonesian immigration authorities note that incorrect or inconsistent information on the application form is another common reason for rejection. Examples include:

❌ Incorrect passport number

❌ Misspelled name

❌ Wrong email address

❌ Incorrect passport issue date or expiration date

**Solution:**

- Double-check all fields before submission
- Ensure all personal information matches passport details exactly

---

### **3️⃣ Insufficient financial proof**

When applying for certain visa types (such as long-term visas), immigration authorities require applicants to provide sufficient financial proof to ensure they can support themselves during their stay in Indonesia.

**Solution:**

- Prepare bank statements showing stable financial status
- Ensure bank deposits meet the minimum visa requirements

📌 **Note:** This requirement does not apply to 'eVisa on Arrival' but is applicable to other long-term visa categories.

---

### **4️⃣ Poor travel record**

If the applicant has a history of **overstaying, violating immigration laws, or being deported**, this may affect new visa applications.

**Solutions:**

- Avoid any actions that may affect your entry record
- If you've had visa issues in the past, consult a professional visa consultant for solutions

---

### **5️⃣ Unclear Travel Purpose**

Apart from the electronic Visa on Arrival (eVisa on Arrival), other types of visas may require applicants to provide a clear travel plan, such as work visas, student visas, etc.

**Solutions:**

- Prepare a detailed travel itinerary, including hotel reservations, flight tickets, etc.
- For business visas, an invitation letter or relevant proof is required

---

## **🛂 How to Apply for Indonesia's Electronic Visa on Arrival (eVisa on Arrival)?**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_70703b99831da726cd9eeac97e780116.png)

✅ **Application Time:** Earliest application can be made **90 days before arrival**

✅ **Validity Period:** 30 days

✅ **Fee:** **IDR 500,000** (approximately USD$32)

✅ **Extension:** Can be extended for **30 days**, must be processed through the same online platform.

**📌 Reminder:** If unable to apply in person, it is recommended to seek professional assistance to ensure the application proceeds smoothly.

## **🔍 6 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

### **1️⃣ How long does it take to process an Indonesian e-Visa?**

Typically **3-7 working days**, but may take longer during peak periods.

### **2️⃣ Can I apply for an e-Visa using my mobile phone?**

Yes, but it is recommended to use a **computer** to ensure the page displays correctly and to minimize technical issues.

### **3️⃣ If my visa is denied, can I reapply?**

Yes, but make sure to correct all errors before resubmitting the application.

### **4️⃣ Can I use a VPN when applying for a visa?**

Not recommended, as this may cause website loading issues and affect the application process.

### **5️⃣ Can I change visa types within Indonesia?**

Some visa types can be changed within the country, but specific conditions must be met. It's advisable to consult with the local immigration office.
