Planning a trip to Bali? Whether you're from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, or Malaysia, you need to know the customs rules when bringing luggage into Indonesia or taking souvenirs back from Bali. This year's regulations have changed again, and a simple mistake could lead to confiscation or even fines. Let us break down everything you need to know about **Bali entry and exit rules**, plus what to watch out for when returning home.

***

## 🇮🇩 Key Bali/Indonesia Customs Rules (Applies to All Travelers)

### Prohibited Items When Entering Indonesia

#### ❌ Meat and Animal Products (Strictly Controlled)

Meat products from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia are banned from entering Indonesia. This is a common disease prevention policy across Southeast Asian customs.

**Prohibited Items:**

- Fresh or frozen meat
- Processed meats (ham, sausages, cured meat, jerky, etc.)
- Foods containing animal derivatives (products with lard, egg crepes with meat ingredients, crispy pork cookies, etc.)

**Why?** Indonesia places high importance on agricultural disease prevention and strictly prohibits the import of foreign meat to prevent the spread of diseases.

#### ❌ E-Cigarettes

Regardless of whether e-cigarettes are allowed in your home country, bringing them into Indonesia is illegal:

- Taiwan: E-cigarettes are completely banned
- Hong Kong: Strictly regulated
- Singapore: Import and sale prohibited
- Malaysia: Sale prohibited

So, **do not bring e-cigarettes into Indonesia** from anywhere, and don't even think about taking them back home.

#### ❌ Plants and Agricultural Products

- Fresh fruits and vegetables are generally prohibited from entering Bali
- Certain dried products have import restrictions
- Animal feed and seeds are prohibited

**Bali specialties you can bring home:**

- ✓ Coffee (must be well-sealed)
- ✓ Dried herbs & spices (such as vanilla, cinnamon, etc.)
- ✓ Cocoa powder (ensure ingredients and quantity are reasonable)
- ✓ Nuts (in moderate amounts, with intact packaging)

**Tip:** Ask the shop before purchasing if the item can be brought back to your country, or check the ingredient label first.

#### ⚠️ Cash Declaration Rules

When entering or leaving Indonesia, please note:

- Cash or equivalent exceeding **USD 10,000** must be declared
- This includes local currency equivalents (Taiwan dollars, Hong Kong dollars, Singapore dollars, Malaysian ringgit, etc.)
- Indonesian customs may conduct random inspections
- Failure to declare can result in confiscation or fines

### Prohibited Items When Returning from Bali

#### ❌ Glass Ampoules of Skincare or Cosmetics (Intercepted by customs worldwide)

**A common pitfall for many travelers!**

Glass ampoules are containers that require snapping off the neck to open, often used for medical injections.

**The issue:** Customs in many countries may mistake them for medical injectable drugs and confiscate them.

**Special note for travelers from Taiwan:** If you want to bring a small quantity for personal use, you must:

1. Apply for an import permit from the Food and Drug Administration **before entering**
2. Use the red declaration channel **upon arrival**
3. Have your permit ready for customs inspection

**Important warning:** If caught without declaring in Taiwan, you could face a fine of up to **NT$1,000,000 (approximately USD$32,258)**!

_Note:_ If you apply late and haven't received the permit by the time you enter, the ampoules will be held. As long as you submit the documents within one month, you can retrieve them.

**Travelers from Hong Kong:** Hong Kong allows bringing back a certain amount of cosmetics, but it's recommended to check Hong Kong customs regulations in advance.

**Travelers from Singapore and Malaysia:** Similarly, be mindful of declaring glass ampoules. It's advisable to check your respective customs regulations beforehand.

#### ❌ Controlled Medications

Depending on local regulations, certain medications are classified as controlled substances:

**Taiwan:** Weight-loss drugs like "Meng Jian Le" are controlled prescription medications, requiring an import permit from the Food and Drug Administration and proactive declaration.

**Hong Kong, Singapore, Malaysia:** Each has its own list of controlled medications. It's recommended to check local customs regulations before bringing back any medication.

**General Advice:** Having a prescription + proactively declaring = significantly reducing the risk of being caught.

## ✈️ International Airline Prohibited Items (Applicable to Travelers from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia)

Regardless of which region you're flying from to Bali, these are common regulations for all flights.

### ❌ Electronic Items Strictly Prohibited in Checked Luggage

#### Bluetooth Earphones (Most Easily Overlooked!)

Many people think, 'A tiny earphone in my luggage is fine,' but that's **completely wrong**!

**Why is it not allowed?**

- Bluetooth earphones contain lithium batteries
- Even with the charging case closed, they're in standby mode, not truly powered off
- They don't meet the checked luggage regulations for personal portable electronic devices
- If a lithium battery malfunctions in your suitcase, no one will notice → **extremely dangerous**

**Bottom line: Bluetooth earphones must be carried on board as hand luggage—never checked!**

#### Power Banks

Power banks have equally strict rules:

- **Must be carried on board** (absolutely prohibited in checked luggage)
- **Cannot be used during the flight**
- **Cannot be charged during the flight**
- **Must be placed in the seat pocket** (not in overhead bins)
- Pay attention to wattage limits and quantity restrictions

_Reason:_ In recent years, there have been frequent incidents of power banks exploding or catching fire in aircraft cabins, leading airlines worldwide to impose strict regulations.

[Can Power Banks, Portable USB Fans, and Neck Fans Be Taken on a Plane? 2025 Latest Regulations Summary](https://gobaligo.id/blog/airplane-power-bank-fan-rules-2025/)

#### ❌ Pepper Spray

Although legal in some areas, it is **absolutely prohibited on airplanes**!

- High-pressure canisters
- Contains irritants or flammable substances
- Not allowed in either checked or carry-on luggage

## 🛂 By the Way: Transiting Through or Stopping in Japan, South Korea, or Thailand

If you're traveling from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, or Malaysia to Bali with a layover, or briefly stopping in these countries with your luggage, the following rules also apply.

### Japan (Common Transit Point)

#### ⚠️ Rechargeable Curling Irons / Hair Straightening Combs (Most Confiscated Items at Airports!)

Many Asian tourists love buying these products while shopping in Japan, yet they are the most frequently confiscated items at airports.

**Prohibited Rules:**

- Built-in lithium battery
- Heating styling tool with non-removable battery
- Not allowed in carry-on or checked luggage

**Exception:** If the battery is removable or it's a plug-in model, there's no issue.

**Alternative:** Buy a plug-in curling iron, or wait until you get home.

#### ⚠️ Kincho Mosquito Repellent (commonly bought in summer)

A popular mosquito repellent in Japan, many people buy several cans. However:

**Reason for ban:**

- Aerosol can + flammable ingredients (insecticide)
- Strictly prohibited from being carried on board
- Will be confiscated whether in carry-on or checked luggage

***

### South Korea (if transiting)

#### ⚠️ Hand Warmers (new rule in 2026)

South Korea recently implemented a new regulation that many Asian travelers have been caught off guard by:

**New Rules:**

- Completely banned from checked luggage
- Only a small amount allowed in carry-on
- Already enforced at Korea's three major airports (Incheon, Busan, Jeju)

**Common Mistake:** Many people think, 'If it fits in my suitcase, it's fine,' only to have their luggage flagged during scanning, causing delays at departure and ultimately being forced to discard the items.

**Recommendation:** If you want to bring hand warmers home, keep them in your carry-on and limit the quantity.

#### ⚠️ EVE Pain Relievers (Popular in Japan but Banned in Korea)

Japan's 'EVE Pain Relievers' are widely popular in Asia, but they are **strictly prohibited from entering Korea**!

**Reason:** EVE contains an ingredient classified as an antipsychotic drug in Korea, posing risks of addiction and dependency.

**Conclusion:** Neither checked nor carry-on luggage is allowed, and even declaring them won't clear customs.

#### ⚠️ Rechargeable Curling Irons (Same as Japan's Rules)

Korea, like Japan, prohibits:

- If the battery is non-removable, it cannot be brought.
- Both checked and carry-on luggage are prohibited.

#### ⚠️ Meat and Animal Products

South Korean customs strictly regulate meat products:

- Pure meat is prohibited.
- Any product containing animal-derived ingredients with packaging that appears unsterilized is also likely to be rejected.
- Recently, a traveler had frozen egg roll wrappers containing lard confiscated and was fined upon entry.

**Checklist:** Always carefully read ingredient labels when purchasing food.

***

### Thailand (if transiting or shopping)

#### ⚠️ Fried Pork Rind Crackers (Common Snack Trap)

A common snack in Thailand, but **absolutely cannot be brought home**!

**Why?** Despite being called 'crackers,' they are actually pork rinds, which are meat products. They are banned from import in all regions.

**Extended Prohibitions:** Any meat or processed ham and sausages are also prohibited.

#### ⚠️ Cash Declaration Rules

Thai customs may randomly check your cash to ensure you are not working illegally or overstaying.

**Recommended to carry:**

- Individual: at least 20,000 THB
- Family: 40,000 THB
- Equivalent in USD is also acceptable

_Although controversial and not guaranteed to be checked, it's safer to be prepared._

## 📋 Tips to Prevent Powdered Souvenirs from Exploding

Powdered items like matcha powder, cocoa powder, and coffee powder in your luggage may explode due to high altitude pressure, impact, and compression. Opening your suitcase after landing can be a real disaster.

**Protection methods:**

- Wrap in multiple layers of plastic bags or zip-lock bags
- Prevent powder leakage
- Pay special attention to Bali coffee and cocoa powder

## 💡 Pre-Departure Checklist for Travelers from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia

### Before Packing

- [ ] Check all food ingredient labels (especially for meat and animal products)
- [ ] Electronics: Confirm whether batteries are removable or built-in
- [ ] Cosmetics: Glass ampoules require checking local declaration regulations
- [ ] Bluetooth earphones and power banks: Prepare to place in carry-on luggage
- [ ] Medications to bring back: Confirm local customs regulations for such medications in advance

### Before Heading to the Airport

- [ ] Remove old baggage tags from your suitcase (to avoid it being sent to another flight)
- [ ] Check the latest customs regulations of your destination (e.g., Hong Kong Customs, Singapore Customs, Malaysia Customs, etc.)
- [ ] Confirm the latest customs regulations at transit points (Japan, South Korea, Thailand)
- [ ] Prepare declaration documents (if carrying special items)

### Special Notes for Travelers from Taiwan

- ✓ Ampoules and controlled medications must be declared to the Food and Drug Administration
- ✓ A fine of USD$32,258 for violations is far worse than just discarding the items

### For All Travelers

- ✓ When in doubt, proactively declaring is better than being caught
- ✓ Customs regulations change every year—check again before departure
- ✓ Airline websites also have regulations; confirm in advance
- ✓ Customs rules vary by destination; make sure to check local regulations
