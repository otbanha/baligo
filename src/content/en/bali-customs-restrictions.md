**Bottom Line First**: The three biggest customs pitfalls in Bali are: ① **Upon arrival**, all meat and animal products (ham, jerky, instant noodles with meat) are strictly prohibited, with fines up to USD$32,258; ② Items containing lithium batteries like Bluetooth earphones, power banks, and hand warmers **must be checked in or carried with strict limits**—check your airline's rules before boarding; ③ **On your return trip**, meat products, fresh produce, and certain seeds in your souvenirs will also be confiscated. Here's a complete breakdown by nationality and item type.

Planning a vacation to Bali? Whether you're from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, or Malaysia, you need to pay attention to customs regulations when bringing luggage into Indonesia or taking souvenirs back from Bali. Rules have changed this year, and one slip-up could mean confiscation or even a fine. Let us give you a complete rundown of **Bali entry and exit requirements**, plus what to watch out for when heading home.

## 🇮🇩 Key Bali/Indonesia Customs Regulations (Applicable to All Travelers)

### Prohibited Items When Entering Indonesia

#### ❌ Meat and Animal Products (Strictly Controlled)

Meat products from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia are banned from entering Indonesia. This is a common disease prevention policy among Southeast Asian customs authorities.

**Prohibited Items:**

- Fresh or frozen meat
- Processed meats (ham, sausages, cured meat, jerky, etc.)
- Foods containing animal derivatives (products with lard, egg crepes with meat ingredients, savory crackers, etc.)

**Why?** Indonesia places a strong emphasis on agricultural quarantine and strictly prohibits the import of foreign meat to prevent the spread of diseases.

#### ❌ E-Cigarettes

Regardless of whether e-cigarettes are allowed in your home country, bringing them into Indonesia is illegal:

- Taiwan: E-cigarettes are completely banned
- Hong Kong: Strictly regulated
- Singapore: Import and sale are prohibited
- Malaysia: Sale is prohibited

So, **do not bring e-cigarettes into Indonesia from anywhere**, and don't even think about taking them back home.

#### ❌ Plants and Agricultural Products

- Fresh fruits and vegetables are generally prohibited from entering Bali
- Certain dried products have import restrictions
- Animal feed and seeds are banned

**Bali specialties you can bring home:**

- ✓ Coffee (must be well-sealed)
- ✓ Dried herbs & spices (such as vanilla, cinnamon, etc.)
- ✓ Cocoa powder (ensure ingredients and quantity are reasonable)
- ✓ Nuts (moderate amount, intact packaging)

**Tip:** Ask the shop if the item can be brought back to your country before purchasing, or check the ingredient label first.

#### ⚠️ Cash Declaration Rules

When entering or leaving Indonesia, note:

- Cash or equivalent exceeding **USD 10,000** must be declared
- Local currency equivalents count (including TWD, HKD, SGD, MYR)
- Indonesian customs may conduct random checks
- Failure to declare can result in confiscation or fines

### Prohibited Items When Returning from Bali

#### ❌ Glass Ampoules of Skincare or Cosmetics (Intercepted by customs worldwide)

**A common pitfall for many travelers!**

Glass ampoules are containers that require snapping off the neck to open, often used for medical injections.

**The issue:** Customs worldwide may view them as medical injection vials due to their appearance and confiscate them.

**Special note for Taiwanese travelers:** To bring a small quantity for personal use, you must:

1. **Before entry**, apply for an import permit from the Taiwan Food and Drug Administration
2. **Upon entry**, use the red declaration counter
3. Have the permit ready for customs inspection

**Important Warning:** If caught without declaring in Taiwan, you could face a **maximum fine of NT$1,000,000 (approximately USD$32,258)**!

_Note:_ If you apply late and haven't received the permit by the time you arrive, the ampoules will be held temporarily. As long as you submit the required documents within one month, you can retrieve them.

**Hong Kong Travelers:** Hong Kong allows bringing back a certain quantity of cosmetics, but it's advisable to check Hong Kong Customs regulations in advance.

**Singapore and Malaysia Travelers:** Similarly, be mindful of declaring glass ampoules, and it's recommended to confirm your respective customs regulations ahead of time.

#### ❌ Controlled Medications

Depending on local regulations, certain medications are classified as controlled substances:

**Taiwan:** Weight-loss drugs like "Monjaro" are classified as controlled prescription medications and require an import permit from the Taiwan Food and Drug Administration, along with proactive declaration.

**Hong Kong, Singapore, Malaysia:** Each has its own list of controlled medications. Before bringing any medication back, it's best to check local customs regulations.

**General Advice:** Having a prescription and proactively declaring it significantly reduces the risk of being caught.

## ✈️ International Airline Prohibited Items for Flights (Applicable to Travelers from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia)

Regardless of which region you're flying from to Bali, these are the common regulations for all flights.

### ❌ Electronic Items Strictly Prohibited in Checked Luggage

#### Bluetooth Earphones (Most Easily Overlooked!)

Many people think, 'Just a tiny earbud, tossing it in my luggage is fine,' but that's **completely wrong**!

**Why not?**

- Bluetooth earphones contain lithium batteries
- Even with the charging case closed, they're in standby mode, not truly powered off
- They don't meet the checked luggage regulations for personal portable electronic devices
- If a lithium battery malfunctions in your suitcase, no one will notice → **extremely dangerous**

**Conclusion: Bluetooth earphones can only be carried in hand luggage, absolutely not in checked baggage!**

#### Power Banks

Power bank regulations are equally strict:

- **Must be carried in hand luggage** (absolutely prohibited in checked baggage)
- **Cannot be used during the flight**
- **Cannot be charged during the flight**
- **Must be placed in the seat pocket** (not in overhead bins)
- Pay attention to power limits and quantity restrictions

_Reason:_ In recent years, there have been frequent incidents of power banks exploding or catching fire in aircraft cabins, leading airlines worldwide to impose strict restrictions.

#### ❌ Pepper Spray

Although legal in some regions, it is **absolutely prohibited on airplanes**!

- High-pressure canisters
- Contains irritants or flammable substances
- Not allowed in either checked or carry-on luggage

## 🛂 By the way: Transiting through or stopping in Japan, South Korea, or Thailand

If you're traveling from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, or Malaysia to Bali with a layover, or briefly staying in these countries with your luggage, the following rules also apply.

### Japan (Common Transit Point)

#### ⚠️ Rechargeable Curling Irons / Bang Combs (Most Confiscated Items at Airports!)

Many Asian tourists love buying these products while shopping in Japan, yet they are the most frequently confiscated items at the airport.

**Prohibited Items:**

- Built-in lithium battery
- Heat styling tools with non-removable batteries
- Not allowed in carry-on or checked luggage

**Exception:** If the battery is removable or the product is plug-in, it's fine.

**Alternative:** Buy a plug-in curling iron, or wait until you get home.

#### ⚠️ Golden Bird Mosquito Repellent (commonly bought in summer)

A common mosquito repellent in Japan, many people buy several cans. However:

**Reason for Prohibition:**

- Pressurized can + flammable ingredients (insecticide)
- Strictly banned from being carried on board
- Will be confiscated in both carry-on and checked luggage

***

### South Korea (if transiting)

#### ⚠️ Hand Warmers (new rule starting 2026)

South Korea recently implemented new regulations that have caught many Asian travelers off guard:

**New Rules:**

- Complete ban on checked luggage
- Only small quantities allowed in carry-on
- Already enforced at Korea's 3 major airports (Incheon, Busan, Jeju)

**Common Mistake:** Many assume 'if it fits in my suitcase, it's fine,' only to have their luggage flagged during scanning, causing departure delays and ultimately being forced to discard the items.

**Recommendation:** If you want to bring hand warmers home, pack them in your carry-on and keep the quantity small.

#### ⚠️ EVE Pain Relievers (Popular in Japan but Banned in Korea)

Japan's 'EVE Pain Relievers' are widely popular across Asia, but they are **strictly prohibited from entering Korea**!

**Reason:** EVE contains an ingredient classified as an antipsychotic drug in Korea, posing risks of addiction and dependency.

**Bottom Line:** Neither checked nor carry-on luggage is allowed—even declaring them won't get you through customs.

#### ⚠️ Rechargeable Curling Irons (Same Rules as Japan)

South Korea, like Japan, also bans:

- Batteries that cannot be removed are not allowed
- Both checked and carry-on luggage are prohibited

#### ⚠️ Meat and Animal Products

South Korean customs enforces very strict regulations on meat:

- Pure meat is banned
- Any product containing animal-derived ingredients with packaging that appears unsterilized is also likely to be rejected
- Recently, a tourist had frozen egg pancake wrappers containing lard confiscated and was fined

**Checklist:** Always carefully read ingredient labels when purchasing food.

***

### Thailand (if transiting or shopping)

#### ⚠️ Fried Pork Rind Crackers (a common snack trap)

A common snack in Thailand, but **absolutely cannot be brought home**!

**Why?** Despite being called 'crackers,' they are actually made from pork rind, which is a meat product. They are banned from import in all regions.

**Prohibited Items:** Any meat or processed ham/sausages are banned.

#### ⚠️ Cash Declaration Rules

Thai customs may randomly check your cash to ensure you're not working illegally or overstaying.

**Recommended to carry:**

- Individual: at least 20,000 Thai Baht
- Family: 40,000 Thai Baht
- Equivalent in USD is also acceptable

_Though controversial and not always enforced, it's safer to have it ready._

## 📋 Tips to Prevent Powder Souvenirs from Exploding

Powdered products like matcha powder, cocoa powder, and coffee powder can explode in your luggage due to high altitude pressure, impact, and compression. Opening your suitcase after landing can be a real disaster.

**Protection methods:**

- Wrap in multiple layers of plastic bags or zip-lock bags
- Prevent powder leakage
- Pay special attention to Bali coffee and cocoa powder

## 💡 Pre-Departure Checklist for Travelers from Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Malaysia

### Before Packing

- [ ] Check all food ingredient labels (especially for meat and animal products)
- [ ] Electronics: Confirm whether batteries are removable or built-in
- [ ] Cosmetics: Check local declaration rules for glass ampoules
- [ ] Prepare Bluetooth earphones and power banks for carry-on luggage
- [ ] Medications to bring back: Verify local customs regulations for such items in advance

### Before Heading to the Airport

- [ ] Remove old luggage tags from your suitcase (to avoid it being sent to another flight)
- [ ] Check the latest customs regulations of your destination (e.g., Hong Kong Customs, Singapore Customs, Malaysia Customs)
- [ ] Confirm the latest customs rules for transit points (Japan, South Korea, Thailand)
- [ ] Prepare declaration documents (if carrying special items)

### Special Notes for Travelers from Taiwan

- ✓ Declare ampoules and controlled medications to the Food and Drug Administration (TFDA)
- ✓ A fine of USD$32,258 for violations is far worse than just discarding the items

### For All Travelers

- ✓ When in doubt, proactively declaring is better than getting caught
- ✓ Customs regulations change every year—recheck before departure
- ✓ Airline websites also have rules; confirm in advance
- ✓ Customs rules vary by destination; be sure to check local regulations
