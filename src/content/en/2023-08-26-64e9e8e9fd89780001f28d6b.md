As one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations, Bali attracts countless visitors with its stunning beaches, cultural heritage, and unique atmosphere. Yet, on this breathtaking island, there lies a hidden treasure: **Penglipuran Village**, waiting to be explored.

## **The Background of Penglipuran Village**

Located in eastern Bali, far from the hustle and bustle of the city, Penglipuran Village is renowned as one of the most well-preserved traditional communities in Bali. Its significance lies in maintaining the original Balinese culture and unique way of life. The village is steeped in Balinese culture, making it a valuable treasure of the island.

### **Recognized as One of the World's Best Tourism Villages**

Since becoming an official tourism village in 1988, Penglipuran Village has been consistently listed by the **United Nations World Tourism Organization (UNWTO)** as one of the world's best tourism villages. With its core values of "safety, cleanliness, and cultural depth," it has become a model for Bali's tourism transformation.

## **Attractions and Activities in Penglipuran Village**

One of the greatest charms of Penglipuran Village lies in its well-preserved traditional architecture and layout. Here, you'll feel the passage of time, as if transported back to an ancient Balinese community. The village's winding streets and historic houses showcase the rich Balinese heritage, leaving visitors in awe.

Penglipuran Village is also a venue for traditional Balinese ceremonies and festivals. Visitors have the opportunity to participate in and observe local rituals, experiencing authentic Balinese religious culture. Additionally, you can immerse yourself in the daily life of the villagers, admire their traditional crafts and activities, and gain a deeper understanding of this unique community.

## **The Verdant Bamboo Village**

Not far from Kintamani, nestled among rolling hills and countryside, lies Penglipuran Village, a serene and charming place. The village's structure remains largely intact, with orderly stone-paved roads and ancient traditional buildings blending seamlessly with meticulously maintained gardens. This village continues to uphold traditional construction methods, particularly excelling in the use of bamboo. Nearly everything in the village, from kitchens and grand gates to furniture, roofs, and doors, is woven from abundant plant materials. Notably, many distinctive creations, such as bamboo roofs, have become unique symbols of this small community, hard to find elsewhere.

## **Eco-Friendly and Beautiful**

However, what truly stands out is the village's beauty, reflected in its cleanliness. This is because environmental awareness is an integral part of daily life here, passed down through generations. Both locals and visitors refrain from littering, and smokers are restricted to designated areas. Penglipuran Village holds the special distinction of being one of the world's three cleanest villages, alongside Giethoorn in the Netherlands and Mawlynnong in India.

In June 2025, the village was once again honored with the **Kalpataru Lestari Appreciation Award** from the Indonesian Ministry of Environment, an accolade that recognizes communities and individuals for their outstanding contributions to environmental sustainability.

**Village Head Wayan Budiarta** stated, 'This honor is the result of the villagers' long-term efforts to maintain the environment. Our culture of cleanliness has been passed down through generations.'

## **A Journey Through Time**

Visitors can explore the village year-round, but the best time to visit is around the Galungan religious festival. During these days, the village is adorned with special decorations called 'penjor,' hung on bamboo poles. On these special occasions, you can also witness young girls dressed in traditional Balinese attire, gracefully carrying offerings on their heads as they make their way to the village's Hindu temple, creating a scene reminiscent of a ceremonial stage.

## Tasting Loloh Cemcem

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cf5b527a6abdf1c3481af814009eb8d9.jpg)

Loloh Cemcem is a traditional drink from Penglipuran Village. Cemcem leaves are one of the main specialties of Penglipuran Village. Local residents turn these leaves into a healthy, refreshing beverage.

This green health drink is often thought to have a bitter taste. Many believe that cemcem leaves naturally produce bitterness when used in food. In fact, this is a misconception. When you drink Loloh Cemcem, you won't taste bitterness because the palm sugar syrup balances it out.

During the preparation process, cemcem leaves are mixed with other plants such as jatropha leaves, betel leaves, and coral tree leaves. These four types of leaves are crushed and soaked in warm water. Then, cinnamon and palm sugar syrup are added to neutralize the bitterness. To enhance the flavor, some sellers add coconut meat. Adding ice makes it even more refreshing! This is the perfect thirst-quencher in Bali's hot weather. Your thirst will vanish instantly.

Loloh Cemcem is not just a refreshing drink; it also offers numerous health benefits. For example, it can help lower high blood pressure and improve digestive issues. Because of these benefits, visitors to Penglipuran Village often try Loloh Cemcem. The drink is also easy to find. Almost every resident in Penglipuran Village sells it, usually right in front of their homes. A bottle of Loloh Cemcem is very affordable, so you can try it without worrying about the price.

## Tips for Visiting Penglipuran Village

When visiting Penglipuran Village, it is crucial to respect local culture and customs. Dressing appropriately and behaving politely will help you interact well with the villagers. Please note that photography requires permission from local residents.

We strongly recommend hiring a local guide, as this will give you a deeper understanding of the village's history and traditions. The guide will provide insightful explanations of the village's stories and answer any questions you may have.

In Penglipuran Village, you can stroll through peaceful alleys, admire traditional-style houses, meticulously trimmed gardens, and the friendly smiles of local residents. The villagers take pride in respecting the environment and culture, so the entire village remains exceptionally clean and retains its original beauty.

While exploring the village, don't forget to visit local temples and cultural heritage sites to learn about Balinese religious beliefs and traditions. Additionally, you can savor authentic Balinese cuisine at local stalls, allowing your taste buds to experience the unique flavors of this land.

Whether you're seeking cultural experiences or pure travel adventure, Penglipuran Village is a destination not to be missed. This ancient Balinese community will transport you to a unique space and time, allowing you to feel the profound depth of Balinese culture.

Entry to visit here requires a ticket. The price is $2 for Indonesians and $3 for foreigners.

## **📚FAQ Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Where is Penglipuran Village located?**

Penglipuran Village is located in Bangli Regency, Bali, Indonesia, near the active volcano Mount Batur and Lake Batur area.

### **Why is Penglipuran Village called the cleanest village in the world?**

The villagers have long practiced strict waste management and environmental protection measures, with no visible trash in the village and streets kept spotlessly clean.

### **Can I stay overnight in Penglipuran Village?**

The village mainly offers local homestays, providing simple yet comfortable accommodations. Advance reservations are required.

### **Can visitors explore the bamboo forest in Penglipuran Village?**

Yes. Parts of the bamboo forest are open to visitors, and guided tours are available to introduce its sustainable management practices.

### **Is there an entrance fee for the village?**

Yes, visitors are required to pay a small entrance fee, which serves as funding for the village's environmental conservation and maintenance efforts.
