As one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations, Bali attracts countless visitors with its stunning beaches, cultural heritage, and unique atmosphere. Yet, on this breathtaking island, there's a hidden treasure: **Penglipuran Village**, waiting to be explored.

## **The Background of Penglipuran Village**

Nestled in eastern Bali, far from the hustle and bustle of the city, Penglipuran Village is renowned as one of the best-preserved traditional Balinese communities. Its special significance lies in maintaining the original Balinese culture and unique way of life. The village is steeped in Balinese culture, making it a valuable treasure of the island.

### **Recognized as One of the World's Best Tourism Villages**

Since becoming an official tourism village in 1988, Penglipuran Village has been consistently listed by the **United Nations World Tourism Organization (UNWTO)** as one of the world's best tourism villages. With its core values of 'safety, cleanliness, and cultural depth,' it has become a model for Bali's tourism transformation.

## **Attractions and Activities in Penglipuran Village**

One of Penglipuran Village's greatest charms lies in its well-preserved traditional architecture and layout. Here, you'll feel the passage of time, as if transported back to an ancient Balinese community. The village's long streets and ancient houses showcase a rich Balinese ambiance that leaves visitors in awe.

Penglipuran Village is also a venue for traditional ceremonies and festivals in Bali. Visitors have the opportunity to participate in and observe local ceremonial activities, experiencing authentic Balinese religious culture. Additionally, you can immerse yourself in the daily lives of the villagers, appreciating their traditional crafts and activities, thereby gaining a deeper understanding of this unique community.

## **The Lush Bamboo Village**

Not far from Kintamani, nestled among rolling hills and countryside, lies Penglipuran Village, a serene and charming place. The village structure remains largely intact, with orderly stone-paved roads and ancient traditional buildings blending seamlessly with meticulously maintained gardens. This village continues traditional architectural practices, particularly excelling in the use of bamboo. Almost everything in the village, from kitchens to grand gates and doorways, furniture, roofs, and main entrances, is crafted from abundant plant materials. Notably, many distinctive creations like bamboo roofs have become unique symbols of this small community, hard to find elsewhere.

## **Eco-Friendly and Beautiful**

However, what truly stands out is the village's beauty, reflected in its cleanliness. This is because environmental consciousness is an integral part of daily life here, passed down through generations. Both locals and visitors refrain from littering, and smokers are restricted to designated areas. Penglipuran Village holds a special title, being recognized alongside Giethoorn in the Netherlands and Mawlynnong in India as one of the world's three cleanest villages.

In June 2025, the village was honored once again with the **Kalpataru Lestari Appreciation Award** by the Indonesian Ministry of Environment, an accolade specifically recognizing communities and individuals for outstanding contributions to environmental sustainability.

**Village Head Wayan Budiarta** stated, "This honor is the result of the villagers' long-term efforts in maintaining the environment. Our culture of cleanliness has been passed down through generations."

## **A Journey Through Time**

Visitors can explore the village year-round, but the best time to visit is around the Galungan religious festival. During these days, the village is adorned with special decorations called 'penjor,' hung on bamboo poles. At these special times, you can also witness young girls dressed in traditional Balinese attire, gracefully carrying offerings on their heads as they head to the village's Hindu temple, creating a scene reminiscent of a ceremonial stage.

## Tasting Loloh Cemcem

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cf5b527a6abdf1c3481af814009eb8d9.jpg)

Loloh Cemcem is a traditional drink from Penglipuran Village. Cemcem leaves are one of the main specialties of Penglipuran Village. Local residents turn these leaves into a fresh beverage that is beneficial for health.

This green, healthy drink is often thought to have a bitter taste. Many believe that cemcem leaves inherently produce a bitter flavor when made into food. In fact, this notion is incorrect. When you drink Loloh Cemcem, you won't experience bitterness because the palm sugar syrup balances out the bitter taste.

During the preparation process, cemcem leaves are mixed with other plants such as physic nut leaves, betel leaves, and coral tree leaves. These four types of leaves are crushed and then soaked in warm water. Afterward, cinnamon and palm sugar syrup are added to neutralize the bitterness of the leaves. To enhance the drink's flavor, some sellers intentionally add coconut meat. Adding ice cubes makes it even more refreshing! This is the best thirst-quenching drink in Bali's hot weather. Your thirst will vanish instantly.

Loloh Cemcem is not just a refreshing drink; it also offers numerous health benefits. For example, it can help lower high blood pressure and improve digestive issues. Because of these advantages, visitors to Penglipuran Village often try Loloh Cemcem. This drink is also easy to find. Almost all residents of Penglipuran Village sell it, usually right in front of their homes. A bottle of Loloh Cemcem is very affordable, so you can try it without worrying about the price.

## Tips for Visiting Penglipuran Village

When you visit Penglipuran Village, it is crucial to respect the local culture and customs. Dressing appropriately and behaving politely will help you interact well with the villagers. Please note that photography requires the consent of the local residents.

We strongly recommend hiring a local guide, as this will allow you to gain a deeper understanding of the village's history and traditions. The guide will be able to provide you with more profound insights, explain the village's stories, and answer any questions you may have.

In Penglipuran Village, you can stroll through the tranquil alleys, admire the traditional-style houses, meticulously trimmed gardens, and the friendly smiles of the local residents. The villagers take pride in respecting the environment and culture, which is why the entire village maintains a high level of cleanliness and pristine beauty.

While exploring the village, don't forget to visit the local temples and cultural heritage sites to learn about Balinese religious beliefs and traditions. Additionally, you can also savor authentic Balinese cuisine at the local stalls, allowing your taste buds to experience the unique flavors of this land.

Whether you're seeking cultural immersion or pure travel adventure, Penglipuran Village is a destination not to be missed. This ancient Balinese community will transport you to a unique space and time, allowing you to experience the profound depth of Balinese culture.

Entry requires a ticket. The price is $2 for Indonesians and $4 for foreigners.

## **📚FAQ**

### **Where is Penglipuran Village located?**

Penglipuran Village is located in Bangli Regency, Bali, Indonesia, near the active volcano Mount Batur and Lake Batur area.

### **Why is Penglipuran Village called the cleanest village in the world?**

The villagers have long practiced strict waste management and environmental protection measures, with no visible trash in the village and streets kept spotlessly clean.

### **Can I stay overnight in Penglipuran Village?**

The village mainly offers local homestays, providing simple yet comfortable accommodations that require advance booking.

### Can visitors explore the bamboo forest in Penglipuran Village?

Yes. Some sections of the bamboo forest are open to visitors, and guided tours are available to introduce its sustainable management practices.

### Is there an entrance fee for the village?

Yes, visitors are required to pay a small entrance fee, which serves as funding for the village's environmental conservation and maintenance efforts.
