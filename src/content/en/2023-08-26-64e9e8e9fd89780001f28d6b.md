Bali, one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations, captivates countless visitors with its stunning beaches, cultural heritage, and unique ambiance. Yet, amidst this breathtaking island lies a hidden gem: the village of **Penglipuran**, waiting to be explored by adventurous travelers.

## **Background of Penglipuran Village**

Penglipuran Village is nestled in the eastern part of Bali, far from the hustle and bustle of urban life. Renowned as one of the most well-preserved traditional communities in Bali, its significance lies in maintaining the island's authentic culture and unique way of life. Steeped in Balinese heritage, the village is a true gem and a valuable treasure of Bali.

### **Recognized as One of the World’s Best Tourism Villages**

Since being designated as an official tourism village in 1988, Penglipuran Village has consistently been listed by the United Nations World Tourism Organization (UNWTO) as one of the world's best tourism villages. With its focus on 'safety, cleanliness, and cultural richness,' it has become a model for tourism transformation in Bali.

## Attractions and Activities in Penglipuran Village

One of the greatest charms of Penglipuran Village lies in its well-preserved traditional architecture and layout. Here, you'll feel the passage of time, as if transported to an ancient Balinese community. The village's winding streets and historic houses showcase a profound Balinese essence, leaving visitors in awe.

Penglipuran Village is also a venue for traditional ceremonies and festivals in Bali. Visitors have the opportunity to participate in and observe local rituals, experiencing authentic Balinese religious culture. Additionally, you can immerse yourself in the daily lives of the villagers, appreciating their traditional crafts and activities, thereby gaining a deeper understanding of this unique community.

## **The Verdant Bamboo Village**

Not far from Kintamani, nestled amidst rolling hills and countryside, lies Penglipuran Village, a serene and charming destination. The village's structure remains remarkably intact, with orderly stone-paved roads and ancient traditional buildings seamlessly blending with meticulously manicured gardens. This village continues to uphold traditional construction methods, particularly excelling in the use of bamboo. Nearly everything in the village, from kitchens and grand gates to furniture, roofs, and doors, is intricately woven from abundant plant materials. Notably, the bamboo roofs and many other distinctive creations have become unique symbols of this small community, rarely found elsewhere.

## **Where Eco-Friendliness Meets Beauty**

However, what truly stands out is the village's beauty, which is reflected in its cleanliness. This is because environmental awareness is an integral part of daily life here, passed down through generations. Whether it's the local residents or visitors, no one litters indiscriminately, and smokers are only allowed to smoke in designated areas. Penglipuran Village holds a special distinction—it is recognized as one of the world's three cleanest villages, alongside Giethoorn in the Netherlands and Mawlynnong in India.

In June 2025, this village was once again honored with the **Kalpataru Lestari Appreciation Award** by the Indonesian Ministry of Environment, an accolade specifically recognizing communities and individuals who have made outstanding contributions to environmental sustainability.

Village Head Wayan Budiarta stated: "This honor is the result of the entire village's long-term efforts in environmental preservation. Our culture of cleanliness has been passed down through generations."

## **A Journey Through Time**

Visitors have the opportunity to explore this village year-round, but the best time to visit is during the Galungan religious festival. During these days, the village is adorned with special decorations called 'penjor,' which are hung on bamboo poles. On these special occasions, you can also witness young girls dressed in traditional Balinese attire, gracefully carrying offerings on their heads as they make their way to the village's Hindu temple, creating a scene reminiscent of a ceremonial stage.

## Tasting Loloh Cem-cem

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cf5b527a6abdf1c3481af814009eb8d9.jpg)

Loloh Cemcem is a traditional beverage originating from Penglipuran Village. Cemcem leaves are one of the primary specialties of Penglipuran. Local residents transform these leaves into a refreshing drink that offers numerous health benefits.

This green health drink is often thought to have a bitter taste. Many believe that cemcem leaves inherently produce bitterness when used in food. In fact, this is a misconception. When you drink Loloh Cemcem, you won't experience bitterness because the palm sugar syrup balances and neutralizes the bitter flavor.

During the preparation process, cemcem leaves are mixed with other plants such as jatropha leaves, betel leaves, and coral tree leaves. These four types of leaves are crushed and then placed in warm water. After that, cinnamon and palm sugar syrup are added to neutralize the bitterness of the leaves. To enhance the flavor of the drink, some vendors intentionally add coconut meat. If ice cubes are added, the taste becomes even more refreshing! This is the best thirst-quenching drink in Bali's hot weather. Your thirst will disappear instantly.

Loloh Cemcem is not just a refreshing beverage; it also offers numerous health benefits. For instance, it can help lower high blood pressure and improve digestive system issues. Thanks to these advantages, visitors to Penglipuran Village often try Loloh Cemcem. This drink is also easy to find. Nearly every resident of Penglipuran Village sells it, typically right in front of their homes. A bottle of Loloh Cemcem is also very affordable, so you can feel free to give it a try without worrying about the cost.

## Tips for Visiting Penglipuran Village

When visiting Penglipuran Village, it is crucial to respect the local culture and customs. Dressing appropriately and behaving politely will facilitate positive interactions with the villagers. Please remember to seek permission from local residents before taking photographs.

We highly recommend hiring a local guide, as this will allow you to gain a deeper understanding of the village's history and traditions. The guide will be able to provide insightful explanations of the village's stories and answer any questions you may have.

In Penglipuran Village, you can stroll through serene alleyways, admiring traditional-style houses, meticulously manicured gardens, and the friendly smiles of local residents. The villagers take great pride in respecting the environment and their culture, which is why the entire village maintains an exceptional level of cleanliness and retains its pristine beauty.

While exploring the villages, don't forget to visit the local temples and cultural heritage sites to gain insight into the religious beliefs and traditions of the Balinese people. Additionally, you can savor authentic Balinese cuisine at the local stalls, allowing your taste buds to experience the unique flavors of this enchanting land.

Whether you're seeking cultural experiences or pure travel adventure, Penglipuran Village is a destination not to be missed. This ancient Balinese community will transport you to a unique time and space, allowing you to feel the profound depth of Balinese culture.

Admission to this site requires a ticket. The price is IDR 25,000 for Indonesians and IDR 50,000 for foreigners.

## **📚FAQ Frequently Asked Questions**

### **Where is Penglipuran Village?**

Penglipuran Village is located in Bangli Regency, Bali, Indonesia, near the active Mount Batur and Lake Batur area.

### **Why is Penglipuran Village Known as the Cleanest Village in the World?**

The villagers have long practiced strict waste management and environmental protection measures, leaving no visible trash in the village, with streets as clean as new.

### **Can You Stay in Penglipuran Village?**

The village primarily offers local homestays, providing simple yet comfortable accommodations. Advance reservations are required.

### **Can You Visit the Bamboo Forest in Penglipuran Village?**

Yes. Part of the bamboo forest is open to visitors, and there are guided tours available that introduce its sustainable management practices.

### **Is there an entrance fee for the village?**

Yes, visitors are required to pay a small entrance fee, which serves as a source of funding for the village's environmental protection and maintenance efforts.
