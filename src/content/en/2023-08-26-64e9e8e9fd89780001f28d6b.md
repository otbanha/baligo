As one of Indonesia's most popular tourist destinations, Bali attracts countless visitors with its stunning beaches, cultural heritage, and unique atmosphere. However, on this breathtaking island, there is a hidden treasure: Penglipuran Village, waiting to be discovered by travelers.

## The Background of Penglipuran Village

Located in eastern Bali, Penglipuran Village is far from the hustle and bustle of the city. This village is renowned as one of the most well-preserved traditional communities in Bali, with its special significance lying in maintaining the original Balinese culture and unique way of life. The village is imbued with Balinese cultural influences, making it a valuable treasure of Bali.

### Recognized as One of the World's Best Tourism Villages

Since becoming an official tourism village in 1988, Penglipuran Village has been consistently listed by the United Nations World Tourism Organization (UNWTO) as one of the world's best tourism villages. With its core values of 'safety, cleanliness, and cultural depth,' it has become a model for Bali's tourism transformation.

## Attractions and Activities in Penglipuran Village

One of the greatest charms of Penglipuran Village lies in its well-preserved traditional architecture and layout. Here, you will feel the passage of time, as if transported back to an ancient Balinese community. The village's winding streets and ancient houses showcase the rich Balinese culture, leaving visitors in awe.

Penglipuran Village is also a venue for traditional ceremonies and festivals in Bali. Visitors have the opportunity to participate in and observe local ceremonial activities, experiencing authentic Balinese religious culture. Additionally, you can immerse yourself in the daily lives of the villagers, appreciating their traditional crafts and activities, thereby gaining a deeper understanding of this unique community.

## ****A Lush Bamboo Village****

Not far from Kintamani, nestled among rolling hills and countryside, lies Penglipuran Village, a serene and charming place. The village's structure remains largely intact, with orderly stone-paved roads and ancient traditional buildings interspersed with meticulously maintained gardens. This village continues to uphold traditional construction methods, particularly making extensive use of bamboo. Almost everything in the village, from kitchens and grand gates to furniture, roofs, and doors, is woven from abundant plant materials. Notably, many distinctive creations like bamboo roofs have become unique symbols of this small community, rarely found elsewhere.

## ****Eco-Friendly and Beautiful****

However, what is most astonishing is the village's beauty, which reflects its cleanliness. This is because environmental consciousness is an integral part of daily life here, passed down through generations. Both locals and visitors refrain from littering, and smoking is only permitted in designated areas. Penglipuran Village holds the special distinction of being one of the world's three cleanest villages, alongside Giethoorn in the Netherlands and Mawlynnong in India.

In June 2025, the village was once again honored with the ****Kalpataru Lestari Appreciation Award**** by the Indonesian Ministry of Environment, an award that recognizes communities and individuals for their outstanding contributions to environmental sustainability.

****Village Head Wayan Budiarta**** stated, 'This honor is the result of the villagers' long-term efforts to maintain the environment. Our culture of cleanliness has been passed down through generations.'

## ****A Journey Through Time****

Visitors can explore this village year-round, but the best time to visit is during the Galungan religious festival. During these days, the village is adorned with special decorations called 'penjor,' hung on bamboo poles. On these special occasions, you can also witness young girls dressed in traditional Balinese attire, gracefully carrying offerings on their heads as they make their way to the village's Hindu temple, creating a scene reminiscent of a ceremonial stage.']}

## Tasting Loloh Cem-cem

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cf5b527a6abdf1c3481af814009eb8d9.jpg)

Loloh Cemcem is a traditional drink from Penglipuran Village. Cemcem leaves are one of the main specialties of Penglipuran Village. Local residents turn these leaves into a fresh beverage that is beneficial for health.

This green health drink is often thought to have a bitter taste because many believe that cemcem leaves inherently produce bitterness when used in food. In fact, this notion is incorrect. When you drink Loloh Cemcem, you won't experience bitterness because the palm sugar syrup balances out the bitter flavor.

During the preparation process, cemcem leaves are mixed with other plants such as physic nut leaves, betel leaves, and coral tree leaves. These four types of leaves are crushed and soaked in warm water. Afterward, cinnamon and palm sugar syrup are added to neutralize the bitterness of the leaves. To enhance the drink's flavor, some sellers intentionally add coconut meat. Adding ice cubes makes it even more refreshing! It's the perfect thirst-quencher in Bali's hot weather. Your thirst will vanish instantly.

Loloh Cemcem is not just a refreshing drink; it also offers various health benefits. For example, it can help lower high blood pressure and improve digestive issues. Because of these advantages, visitors to Penglipuran Village often try Loloh Cemcem. This drink is also widely available. Almost all residents of Penglipuran Village sell it, usually right in front of their homes. A bottle of Loloh Cemcem is very affordable, so you can try it without worrying about the price.

## Tips for Visiting Penglipuran Village

When visiting Penglipuran Village, it is crucial to respect the local culture and customs. Dressing appropriately and behaving politely will help you interact well with the villagers. Please note that photography requires permission from the local residents.

We strongly recommend hiring a local guide, as this will allow you to gain a deeper understanding of the village's history and traditions. The guide will provide insightful explanations about the village's stories and answer any questions you may have.

In Penglipuran Village, you can stroll through the peaceful alleys, admire traditional-style houses, meticulously maintained gardens, and the friendly smiles of the locals. The residents take pride in respecting the environment and culture, so the entire village remains exceptionally clean and retains its original beauty.

While exploring the village, don't forget to visit the local temples and cultural heritage sites to learn about Balinese religious beliefs and traditions. Additionally, you can savor authentic Balinese cuisine at local stalls, allowing your taste buds to experience the unique flavors of this land.

Whether you're seeking cultural experiences or pure travel adventure, Penglipuran Village is a must-visit destination. This ancient Balinese community will transport you to a unique space and time, allowing you to experience the profound depth of Balinese culture.

Entry requires a ticket. Prices are $2 for Indonesians and $3 for foreigners.

## ****📚FAQ****

### ****Where is Penglipuran Village located?****

Penglipuran Village is located in Bangli Regency, Bali, Indonesia, near the active volcano Mount Batur and Lake Batur area.

### ****Why is Penglipuran Village called the cleanest village in the world?****

Villagers have long practiced strict waste management and environmental protection measures, resulting in no visible trash and immaculately clean streets throughout the village.

### ****Can I stay in Penglipuran Village?****

The village mainly offers homestays, providing simple and comfortable accommodations. Advance booking is required.

### ****Can I visit the bamboo forest in Penglipuran Village?****

Yes. Some parts of the bamboo forest are open to visitors, and guided tours are available to introduce its sustainable management practices.

### ****Is there an entrance fee for the village?****

Yes, visitors are required to pay a small entrance fee, which serves as funding for the village's environmental and maintenance efforts.
