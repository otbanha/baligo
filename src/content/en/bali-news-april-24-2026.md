## 🗞️ Today's Headlines: High-Altitude Rescue and Geological Updates

- **Uluwatu Cliff Helicopter Rescue**: Yesterday afternoon (April 23), two foreign tourists (including a 21-year-old Russian man) were stranded on the steep cliffs of **Uluwatu**. Denpasar police, in coordination with the National Search and Rescue Agency (Basarnas), deployed a helicopter for a hoist operation, successfully rescuing the stranded individuals and transporting them to a hospital. Authorities remind visitors that the terrain is dangerously steep—do not climb over barriers for photos.
- **Minor Northern Tremor, No Damage**: At 04:34 yesterday morning, a **very shallow magnitude 3.8 earthquake** struck **Buleleng**, with a depth of just 10 kilometers. According to BMKG monitoring, the tremor was confined to the northern mountainous area and was mild (MMI II). No damage has been reported across the island, and tourist itineraries remain unaffected.
- **Tourism Strategy Shifts to Central Asia**: The Indonesian Travel Agents Association (ASITA) announced today that it is working with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs to develop flight routes to **Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan**. The goal is to follow Thailand's lead in positioning Bali as a transit hub for Central Asian travelers, signaling potential new direct international flights in the coming months.

## 📄 Tourist Concerns: Immigration Monitoring and Festival Preview

- **New Task Force Fully Operational**: The 'Dharma Dewata' immigration task force has expanded its patrols across major hotspots starting today. Officials emphasize the goal is to 'monitor foreigner activities' to ensure community safety. Several foreigners have recently been deported for violations or overstaying, so tourists are reminded to carry a copy of their electronic visa at all times.
- **Next Week's Festival Preview**: Although the Bali Spirit Festival has ended, next Tuesday (April 28) marks the start of the grand [**Semarapura Festival** (Klungkung) and **FHT (Food, Hotel & Tourism Expo)**](/en/blog/2026-bali-festival-guide/#festival-semarapura-%E5%A1%9E%E9%A6%AC%E6%8B%89%E6%99%AE%E6%8B%89%E7%AF%80). Travelers interested in food and culture can plan ahead to visit Klungkung for traditional performances and art exhibitions.
- **Metro Construction Update**: The governor reiterated today that by the end of 2026, transportation links across tourist areas will be fully optimized. Drilling for the southern metro stations has entered a critical phase, so be aware of lane reductions due to construction barriers.
