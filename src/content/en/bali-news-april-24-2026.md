### 🗞️ Today's Highlights: Aerial Rescue and Geological Activity

- **Helicopter Rescue at Uluwatu Cliff**: Yesterday afternoon (4/23), two foreign tourists (including a 21-year-old Russian man) were stranded on the steep cliffs of **Uluwatu**. The Denpasar Police, in collaboration with the National Search and Rescue Agency (Basarnas), deployed a helicopter for a hoisting operation, successfully rescuing the stranded individuals and transporting them to the hospital. Authorities remind visitors that the area is perilously steep and advise against crossing barriers for photo opportunities.
- **Minor Earthquake in the North**: At 04:34 yesterday morning, a **magnitude 3.8 shallow earthquake** occurred in **Buleleng**, with an epicenter depth of just 10 km. According to BMKG monitoring, the tremor was confined to the northern mountainous region and was mild (MMI II). No damage reports have been received island-wide, and tourist activities remain unaffected.
- **Tourism Strategy Shifts to Central Asia**: The Association of Indonesian Tour and Travel Agencies (ASITA) announced today that they are collaborating with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs to open routes to **Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan**. The goal is to emulate Thailand by positioning Bali as a transit hub for Central Asian tourists, indicating potential new international direct flights in the coming months.

### 📄 Visitor Concerns: Immigration Oversight and Festival Announcements

- **New Task Force Fully Operational**: The new immigration task force, codenamed "Dharma Dewata," has expanded its patrols across major hotspots starting today. Officials emphasize its purpose is to "monitor foreigner activities" to ensure community peace. Recently, several foreigners have been immediately deported for violations or overstaying their visas, reminding tourists to carry copies of their electronic visas.
- **Next Week's Festivals**: Although the Bali Spirit Festival has concluded, next Tuesday (4/28) will see the grand [**Semarapura Festival** (Klungkung) and **FHT (Food Hotel Tourism Expo)**](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2026-01-16-696a37c5fd89780001f0790b/#festival-semarapura-%E5%A1%9E%E9%A6%AC%E6%8B%89%E6%99%AE%E6%8B%89%E7%AF%80). Visitors interested in cuisine and culture can plan ahead to attend traditional performances and art exhibitions in Klungkung.
- **Metro Project Update**: The Governor reiterated today that transportation connections across tourist areas will be fully optimized by the end of 2026. Currently, drilling operations at southern metro stations are in a critical phase, so be aware of lane reductions due to construction barriers.
