## Travel Info & Preparations

Travel Dates: May 18 - May 25

This Bali trip was a family vacation with 6 adults and 1 child (a 2-year-old boy).

### Flights

We chose China Airlines' direct flight, with round-trip tickets costing around USD$420 per person. Booking more than three months in advance secured us a great deal.

### Visa

We opted for a visa on arrival, which incurred an additional 2% fee for credit card payments. We initially considered applying for an e-visa but were deterred by the extensive paperwork required. Additionally, we heard stories of people being fined for listing 'TAIWAN' on their forms, which made us nervous about making mistakes, so we decided to go with the visa on arrival instead. The process at Bali was relatively straightforward, with multiple counters available, each handling about five people at a time. We were fortunate and completed the visa process in about ten minutes. However, our luck ran out when it came to retrieving our luggage—we waited nearly two hours, which caused us to miss the sunset at Jimbaran.

> [1-minute guide to the latest process: From landing → clearing customs → collecting luggage](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

### SIM Card

We pre-purchased two XL Telecom SIM cards from the highest-rated seller on Shopee. However, we had heard that XL's signal was generally poor, so we decided to keep some budget aside to buy another SIM card locally. As expected, XL's signal was terrible, and we could barely use the internet. Later, we bought a Telkomsel SIM card locally, which cost about USD$12 for 24GB, and finally got a stable internet connection.

### Currency Exchange

We exchanged USD in Taiwan before heading to Bali, where we then exchanged it for Indonesian Rupiah at certified money changers that provided receipts. The rates at legitimate exchange offices were quite similar, though some unlicensed shops offered slightly better rates. However, fearing scams, we stuck to the certified ones. We noticed that the exchange rates were more favorable between noon and 3 PM, often worsening later in the afternoon.

How to choose a legitimate money changer? Check out: [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)

### Airport to Jimbaran Hotel

We used Grab to book two cars, with a wait time of about 20 minutes. Upon checking, we found Grab to be cheaper than Gojek and even cheaper than the airport transfer we had pre-booked on Klook (around USD$23 for a Lion car). Considering potential delays with the visa on arrival, we decided to book on the spot. The cost for two Grab cars was about USD$13, but you have to wait for the cars to arrive, so weigh your options. In comparison, airport pickups are the most expensive option if you opt for a private car, as the driver only makes one trip and rents the car exclusively, unless you have other destinations planned for the same day.

### Itinerary

Since we wanted to visit all the famous attractions, we planned our itinerary in advance and then hired a private driver to accommodate our schedule.

## May 18th, Saturday DAY 1 Exploring Jimbaran

Our flight departed from Taiwan at 9:10 AM, and after a little over five hours, we arrived in Bali at 2:40 PM.

### FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach

Upon arrival, we headed straight to FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach to drop off our luggage. The hotel is conveniently located within walking distance of Jimbaran Beach. The rooftop features an infinity pool and a children's play area, making it an ideal spot for family vacations.

### Jimbaran Beach

We originally planned to watch the sunset at Jimbaran Beach, but due to the long wait for our luggage at the airport, we missed the beautiful sunset. However, the scenery on the beach was still delightful, and we enjoyed the local specialty—grilled corn. The corn here is sweet and creamy, leaving a lasting impression.

### Dinner - Lia Cafe

For dinner, we chose Lia Cafe, which charges an additional 10% service fee and accepts card payments. While waiting for our meal, a group of local singers performed songs based on our nationality. However, they aggressively demanded tips, and if the amount was too small, they appeared displeased, which somewhat marred our dining experience. (Editor's note: These singers are not affiliated with the restaurant. Typically, a tip of around $3 USD per group would suffice to keep everyone happy!)

## Post-Trip Reflections and Insights

This trip was primarily planned for adults, but we still allocated time each day for both kids and adults to enjoy water activities. We opted not to stay in luxurious villas or resorts since our itinerary was packed, leaving little time to fully enjoy such accommodations, so we decided not to spend extra money! Instead, we chose hotels with good reviews and high value for money.

If temples aren't particularly your interest, consider cutting down on some related activities to free up more time for massages, water fun, and shopping. However, we approached this trip with the mindset that it might be a once-in-a-lifetime experience, so we crammed all the famous spots into our schedule. Overall, it was an incredibly rich and unforgettable journey. 😆

**Family's Take**: Bali's traffic is indeed very congested. Although we managed to visit almost all the famous attractions this time, the itinerary couldn't be too tight. Except for staying in a 'haunted house,' everything else was arranged quite well. In the future, we might consider reducing some temple visits and opting for more relaxing activities instead. As for shopping, the experience of being quoted high prices and then having to haggle wasn't very pleasant.

**Would We Visit Bali Again?**: While Bali's beaches are beautiful, they slightly pale in comparison to those of other islands. However, Bali's unique cultural landscape is definitely worth experiencing! That said, the world is vast, and we're eager to explore its many corners. We might not return to Bali, but the memories we made there will forever remain in our hearts.
