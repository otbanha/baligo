## Travel Information & Preparation

Trip Dates: May 18 - May 25

This Bali trip was a family vacation, with 6 adults and 1 child (a two-year-old boy).

### Flights

We chose China Airlines' direct flight, with round-trip tickets costing approximately USD$420 per person. Booking more than three months in advance secured us a great deal.

### Visa

We opted for a visa on arrival, which incurs an additional 2% fee for credit card payments. We initially considered applying for an e-visa but decided against it due to the extensive paperwork required and concerns about potential fines for listing 'Taiwan' as our place of origin. The visa-on-arrival process in Bali was relatively straightforward, with multiple counters available and queues of about five people each. We were fortunate and completed the visa process in about ten minutes. However, our luck ran out when it came to collecting our luggage—we waited nearly two hours, which caused us to miss the sunset at Jimbaran.

### [August 2025 Indonesia Bali Entry Using 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card | 1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

### SIM Cards

We pre-purchased two XL Telecom SIM cards from the highest-rated seller on Shopee. However, we heard that XL Telecom's signal is generally poor, so we decided to keep some budget aside for alternative SIM cards. As expected, XL Telecom's signal was terrible, making internet access nearly impossible. We later bought Telkomsel SIM cards locally for approximately USD$12 (24GB), which provided stable internet connectivity.

### Currency Exchange

We exchanged USD in Taiwan and then converted it to Indonesian Rupiah in Bali. We chose authorized currency exchange offices that provided receipts and certifications. In reality, the exchange rates at legitimate exchange offices were quite similar, with only a few unlicensed shops offering slightly better rates. However, we were concerned about potential scams and opted for authorized exchanges. Through observation, we found that exchange rates were more favorable between noon and 3 PM, sometimes worsening in the afternoon.

How to choose a legitimate currency exchange? Check out: [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)

### Airport to Jimbaran Hotel

We booked two Grab cars, with a waiting time of about 20 minutes. Upon checking prices on the spot, Grab was cheaper than Gojek and also more affordable than the airport transfer we pre-booked on Klook (approximately USD$23 for a van). Considering potential delays with visa-on-arrival, we chose to book transportation on-site. The cost for two Grab cars was around USD$13, but you’ll need to wait for the cars to arrive, so plan accordingly. In comparison, airport pick-up via private car is the most expensive option, as the driver only makes one trip and cannot take on other bookings unless you have additional destinations planned for the day.

### Itinerary

Since we wanted to visit all the famous attractions, we planned our itinerary in advance and then hired a private driver to accommodate our schedule.

## May 18th, Saturday DAY 1 Exploring Jimbaran

Our flight departed from Taiwan at 9:10 AM and, after a little over five hours, we arrived in Bali at 2:40 PM.

### FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach

Upon arrival, we headed straight to FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach to drop off our luggage. The hotel is conveniently close to Jimbaran Beach, just a short walk away. The rooftop features an infinity pool and a children's play area, making it an ideal spot for family vacations.

### Jimbaran Beach

We had originally planned to watch the sunset at Jimbaran Beach, but due to a long wait for our luggage at the airport, we missed the beautiful sunset. Nevertheless, the scenery at the beach was still delightful, and we enjoyed tasting the local specialty—grilled corn. The corn here is sweet and buttery, leaving a lasting impression.

### Dinner - Lia Cafe

For dinner, we chose Lia Cafe, which charges an additional 10% service fee and accepts card payments. While waiting for our meal, a group of local singers performed songs based on our nationality. However, they aggressively demanded tips and seemed displeased if the amount was too small, which somewhat marred our dining experience. (Editor's note: These performers are not affiliated with the restaurant. Typically, a tip of around 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah would suffice to keep everyone happy!)

### Private Car Driver

We hired a private car multiple times during our trip, including on May 19th and from May 21st to May 24th. Initially, we searched online for recommended drivers, but most offered small vehicles. ## Post-Trip Reflections and Insights

This trip was primarily planned with adults in mind, but we still allocated time each day for both kids and adults to enjoy water activities. We opted not to stay in luxury villas or resorts because our itinerary was quite packed, leaving little time to fully enjoy such accommodations, so we decided to save money! Instead, we chose hotels with good reviews and high value for money.

If you're not particularly interested in temples, consider cutting some related activities from your itinerary. This will free up more time to enjoy massages, water activities, and shopping. However, we approached this trip with the mindset that it might be a once-in-a-lifetime experience, so we packed our schedule with all the famous attractions. Overall, it was a rich and unforgettable journey. 😆

**Family Members' Feedback**: The traffic in Bali is extremely congested. Although we managed to visit almost all the famous attractions, the schedule couldn't be too tight. Aside from staying in a 'haunted house,' the rest of the arrangements were quite good. In the future, we might consider reducing some temple visits and replacing them with more relaxing activities. As for shopping, the experience of being quoted high prices and then haggling wasn't particularly enjoyable.

**Would We Visit Bali Again?**: While Bali's beaches are beautiful, they fall slightly short compared to other islands. However, Bali's unique cultural landscape is definitely worth experiencing! That said, the world is vast, and we want to explore its many corners. We might not return to Bali, but the memories will stay with us forever.
