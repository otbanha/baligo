## Travel Information & Pre-Trip Preparation

Trip Dates: May 18 - May 25

This Bali trip was a family vacation, with a total of 6 adults and 1 child (a 2-year-old boy).

### Airfare

We opted for China Airlines' direct flight, with round-trip tickets costing approximately USD$420 per person. Booking more than three months in advance secured us a great deal.

### Visa

We opted for the visa on arrival, which incurs an additional 2% fee when paying by credit card. We initially considered applying for an electronic visa but were deterred by the extensive information required and reports of fines for listing 'TAIWAN' incorrectly. Concerned about making mistakes, we decided to get our visas upon arrival. The process at Bali's airport was relatively straightforward, with multiple counters handling queues of about five people each. We were fortunate and completed the visa formalities in roughly ten minutes. However, our luck ran out when it came to retrieving our luggage; we waited nearly two hours, which caused us to miss the sunset at Jimbaran.

> [1-Minute Guide to the Latest Process: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

### SIM Card

We pre-purchased two XL telecom SIM cards from the highest-rated seller on Shopee. However, hearing that XL's signal is generally poor, we decided to keep some budget aside to buy another SIM card locally. As expected, XL's signal turned out to be terrible, making internet access nearly impossible. Later, we bought a Telkomsel SIM card locally, which cost around $12 for 24GB, finally giving us stable internet connection.

### Currency Exchange

We exchanged US dollars in Taiwan before our trip and then converted to Indonesian Rupiah at authorized money changers in Bali that provided receipts. While the exchange rates at legitimate money changers were quite similar, some unauthorized shops without receipts offered slightly better rates. However, to avoid potential scams, we opted for the authorized ones. Through observation, we noticed that the exchange rates were more favorable from noon to around 3 PM, often worsening later in the afternoon.

How to choose a legitimate currency exchange? Check out our guide: [Indonesia/Bali Travel Currency Exchange Guide](/blog/2024-01-28-65b5c7e2fd89780001e96fac/)

### From Airport to Jimbaran Hotels

We used Grab to book two cars, with a waiting time of about 20 minutes. On-the-spot price comparison showed that Grab was cheaper than Gojek and also more affordable than the airport transfer we had pre-booked on Klook (around USD$23 for a Toyota Hiace). Considering potential delays with visa-on-arrival, we opted for booking cars upon arrival. The total cost for two Grab cars was approximately USD$13, though you'll need to wait for the cars to arrive - something to keep in mind. In comparison, private airport transfers are the most expensive option since the driver only makes one trip, has to rent the car, and can't take other bookings. Unless you have multiple destinations to visit that same day, private transfers aren't recommended.

### Itinerary

Since we wanted to visit all the famous attractions, we planned our itinerary in advance and then found a private driver to accommodate our schedule.

## May 18th, Saturday DAY 1 Exploring Jimbaran

The flight departed from Taiwan at 9:10 AM and, after a journey of over five hours, arrived in Bali at 2:40 PM.

### FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach

Upon arrival, we headed straight to FOX Hotel Jimbaran Beach to drop off our luggage. This hotel is conveniently located just a short walk from Jimbaran Beach. The rooftop features an infinity pool and a children's play area, making it an ideal environment for family vacations.

### Jimbaran Beach

Originally planned to watch the sunset at Jimbaran Beach, but due to the long wait for luggage at the airport, we missed the beautiful sunset. However, the scenery on the beach was still delightful, and we got to taste the local specialty - grilled corn. The corn here is sweet corn with a buttery flavor, delicious and memorable.

### Dinner - Lia Cafe

For dinner, we chose Lia Cafe, which charges an additional 10% service fee and accepts credit card payments. While waiting for our meal, a local band would sing songs based on your nationality, but they would aggressively demand tips. If you gave too little, they would visibly show their displeasure, which somewhat marred our dining experience. (Editor's note: This band is not affiliated with the restaurant. Generally, a tip of 50,000 Indonesian rupiah from your group would keep everyone happy!)

### Private Driver

During our trip, we hired a private driver multiple times, including on May 19th and from May 21st to May 24th. Initially, we searched online for recommended drivers, but most only offered smaller vehicles. Considering our group of seven people plus luggage, we needed a larger vehicle. After posting our itinerary in an English Bali community group, we finally found a driver who provided 12-hour service at a price cheaper than others offering only 8 hours. His rate included all expenses such as driver fees, fuel, tolls, and parking, and he even provided each of us with a bottle of water daily. The driver was very friendly, with 15 years of experience, and his driving skills were excellent, even on very narrow roads. His English was good, allowing him to answer our questions, but he didn’t engage in constant chatter, maintaining a professional demeanor. We were extremely satisfied with his service, and on the last day, we asked for his contact information, which he gladly provided. He even mentioned that he could introduce us to other drivers who speak Chinese if needed. For [Gdbokink Driver](https://www.facebook.com/Bokink/), it was his first time driving guests who changed areas each day, but we were very pleased with his service.

## Post-Trip Reflections and Insights

This trip was primarily planned with adults in mind, but we still allocated time each day for both kids and adults to enjoy water activities. We opted not to stay in luxurious villas or resorts since our itinerary was quite packed, leaving little time to fully enjoy such accommodations. Therefore, we decided to save money by choosing well-reviewed hotels that offered great value for money.

If temples aren't particularly your thing, consider trimming some related activities from your itinerary. This will free up more time to enjoy massages, water activities, and shopping. However, we approached this trip with a 'once-in-a-lifetime' mindset and packed our schedule with all the famous sights. Overall, it was an incredibly rich and unforgettable journey. 😆

**Family Members' Impressions**: The traffic in Bali is extremely congested. Although we managed to visit almost all the famous attractions this time, it's important not to schedule the itinerary too tightly. Apart from staying in a 'haunted house,' the rest of the arrangements were quite good. In the future, we might consider reducing some temple visits and opting for more relaxing activities instead. As for shopping, the experience of being quoted high prices and then having to haggle wasn't particularly enjoyable.

**Would We Visit Bali Again?**: While Bali's beaches are beautiful, they fall slightly short compared to other tropical islands. However, Bali's unique cultural landscape is definitely worth experiencing! That said, with such a vast world to explore, we might not return to Bali. Still, the memories of our time there will forever remain in our hearts.
