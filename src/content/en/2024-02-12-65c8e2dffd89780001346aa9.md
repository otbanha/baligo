## Nusa Penida Accommodation Guide – Frequently Asked Questions

Before diving into the best places to stay in Nusa Penida, let’s address some common questions from first-time visitors:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d5a1c2ac11e4fdfbddc7dae009cc3a0a.jpg)

### Where is the Best Area to Stay in Nusa Penida?

The best area to stay in Nusa Penida is the west coast, home to Crystal Bay, Kelingking Beach, and Angel's Billabong.

In addition to stunning beaches and breathtaking resorts, you’ll find plenty of cafes and restaurants to relax and unwind. Plus, the island’s most beautiful sunset spots are located in this area.

Is Nusa Penida Family-Friendly?

Overall, Nusa Penida is a fantastic destination for families, offering many family-friendly resorts. The island boasts beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and friendly locals.

Additionally, you can easily explore your neighborhood by scooter or on foot.

### Is Staying on Nusa Penida Worth It?

Visiting Nusa Penida is absolutely worth it, and I highly recommend staying for one or two nights rather than just taking a day trip. This allows you to truly immerse yourself in the island's charm, experiencing its raw beauty and grandeur beyond just a quick tour.

While day trips are convenient for quickly visiting popular spots, staying overnight gives you the freedom to explore hidden gems at a leisurely pace and witness the island's most ethereal moments during sunrise or sunset. Another advantage of staying overnight is avoiding the crowds that come with day tours, as many famous spots are nearly empty in the early morning or evening, offering a completely different atmosphere.

So, if your schedule allows, give yourself more time to fully experience Nusa Penida's unique allure.

### Where is the Best Nightlife on Nusa Penida?

The best nightlife on Nusa Penida is in the northern part of the island, where you'll find several beautiful cocktail bars offering happy hour deals.

Nusa Penida's nightlife isn't as bustling as Bali's, but you can still find bars to enjoy cocktails and the evening.

For example, I recommend Penida Colada Beach Bar, known for its friendly staff and delicious food, open until 11 PM.

### Is Nusa Penida Expensive?

Nusa Penida is slightly more expensive than Bali, but you can still find reasonably priced accommodations and eat cheaply at local restaurants. You can find hotels for around $50 per night and dine at local restaurants for no more than $5 or $10 per person.

### Which is Better, East or West Nusa Penida?

Overall, the most popular area is the west coast, as it is home to the island's main tourist attractions.

However, the east side is also beautiful, with fewer tourists, making it a great option as well.

### Best Places to Stay in Nusa Penida

There is no absolute best area to stay in Nusa Penida; it should be based on the attractions you want to visit on the island and setting up your accommodation base in that area.

That said, Nusa Penida is a relatively small island, and you can drive from one end to the other in just an hour, so no matter where you stay, you'll have the opportunity to visit all the tourist spots.

In addition to the best places to stay in Nusa Penida, we've listed the most popular attractions in the island's east, west, and southern regions below to help you decide which area suits you best.
