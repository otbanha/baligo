## 1. Avoid Peak Travel Seasons

First, let's start with choosing the right time to travel! Avoid visiting Bali during peak seasons, as flight tickets, accommodations, and activity prices tend to skyrocket. Opting for the off-season or less busy periods not only saves you money on flights and lodging but also offers a more tranquil travel experience.

So, when is Bali's peak travel season? Generally, the dry season, which has lower chances of rainfall, is the best time to visit Bali, typically from April to October, marking the island's busiest period. Wondering about the downsides of avoiding peak season? Check out [this link](/blog/2023-12-06-657078edfd89780001f2d88d/) to learn about Bali's rainy season.

Apart from seasonal distinctions, here are five specific periods when Bali sees a surge in visitors, ranked by the level of crowds and traffic:

1. Indonesia's Eid al-Fitr holiday is a significant time. A large number of local Indonesian tourists flock to this paradise island during this roughly ten-day holiday, making it Bali's busiest peak season!
2. The Christmas-New Year holiday attracts global tourists, as it coincides with long vacations in most Western countries, and Indonesians also try to take time off. Thus, the Christmas-New Year holiday ranks second in terms of crowds and traffic. Check out this short video of [the highway situation on December 29, 2023](https://www.facebook.com/groups/indonesia.travelguide/permalink/3579975912271532/).
3. The Chinese Lunar New Year period is another special time, with holidays ranging from two to five days in countries like China, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Singapore, South Korea, and Vietnam; the Philippines, Thailand, and Indonesia observe a one-day holiday on the first day of the lunar year. The Lunar New Year holiday ranks third in attracting large crowds to tourist spots.
4. The period from July 1 to September 5 coincides with Europe's long holidays and the summer break for students in the Northern Hemisphere, leading to a travel surge as families and students head to Bali for vacations. Summer flight and accommodation prices are never cheap!
5. Australian school holidays and Indonesian international school holidays (a week between September 20 and October 10) also bring in many Australian tourists and families attending international schools in Indonesia, who embark on trips during this time.

During these periods, tourist numbers skyrocket, and prices for accommodations and attractions may rise due to high demand. Therefore, if you plan to visit Bali during these peak seasons, it's best to book in advance and prepare thoroughly to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.

## 6. Choose the Right Transportation

For travelers, Bali offers three convenient and **direct** transportation options: renting a scooter, taking a taxi, or hiring a private car. We won't discuss bus systems that stop at every station.

Renting a scooter in Bali is ideal for travelers who want the freedom to explore independently. Taxis are better suited for short trips or those who prefer not to drive themselves. For visitors seeking a more in-depth experience and customized itineraries, hiring a private car with a driver is a great option. If you're traveling with companions, splitting the cost can make this option quite affordable. Depending on your needs and budget, you can choose the most suitable transportation method. No matter which option you select, always prioritize **safety first** to ensure your Bali trip is enjoyable!

## 9. Use Cash for Payments

In Bali, cash is the most widely accepted payment method. Make sure to carry enough cash to avoid additional fees associated with card transactions.

The drawbacks of using credit cards abroad include the following:

1. You may incur additional fees, such as foreign transaction fees, ATM withdrawal fees, and exchange rate differences.
2. You may be charged a fee by retailers if you don't pay in the local currency.
3. You might overspend if you don't keep track of your daily expenses.
4. You could face fraud risks if your credit card lacks a security chip or is stolen.

Therefore, exercise caution when using credit cards abroad.

However, carrying Indonesian rupiah often means dealing with millions (the exchange rate is approximately NT$1:IDR 500). If you prefer not to carry so much cash, consider using the e-wallet feature in the Gojek app mentioned earlier.

## 16. Tipping Appropriately

Tipping appropriately is both a cultural courtesy and a way to express gratitude for services during your travels. When visiting Bali, giving the right amount of tip can help foster friendly relations with locals and is a sign of respect for local customs. Here are some [occasions and considerations for tipping](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw) from Jay to help you make appropriate choices during your journey.

Additionally, a frequently asked question in our Bali community group is: "Can I tip in US dollars?" Here, we share some insights on this topic. In Indonesia or Bali, the only currency in circulation is the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). Therefore, if you wish to tip, please always use the local currency, the Indonesian Rupiah. Avoid using other currencies or foreign money as a means of tipping. This not only shows respect for the local culture but also conveys our sincere appreciation to the service providers.

Let's think from another perspective: imagine if in Taiwan, a foreign friend handed you foreign currency, wouldn't you find it a bit troublesome? Similarly, residents of Bali might feel the same inconvenience or even discomfort.

Therefore, to ensure that our tips accurately convey our gratitude, we should **always use Indonesian Rupiah for tipping**. This approach not only respects the locals but also better expresses our appreciation and thanks for the services received.
