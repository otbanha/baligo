## 1. Avoid Peak Travel Season

First, let's start with choosing your travel time! Avoid visiting Bali during peak season as flight tickets, accommodations, and activity prices usually skyrocket. Opting for off-season or less busy periods can save you money on flights and accommodations while offering a more peaceful travel experience.

So when is Bali's peak travel season? Generally, the dry season with lower rainfall probability is the best time to visit Bali, typically from April to October, which is Bali's peak season. What are the downsides of avoiding peak season? Check out [this link](/blog/2023-12-06-657078edfd89780001f2d88d/) to learn about Bali's rainy season.

1. Indonesia's Eid al-Fitr holiday is a crucial time. A large number of local Indonesian tourists visit this paradise island during this approximately ten-day holiday. This is Bali's busiest peak season!
2. Christmas-New Year holidays usually attract global tourists, as this is a long holiday for most Western countries, and Indonesians will try to take time off too. Therefore, the Christmas-New Year period ranks second in terms of crowds and traffic. 4. The period from July 1 to September 5, coinciding with European long holidays and Northern Hemisphere summer vacations, usually creates a travel trend, with many families and students taking trips to Bali during this time. Summer vacation flight and accommodation prices are never cheap!
5. Australian school holidays and Indonesian international school holidays (one week between September 20 and October 10) also bring many Australian tourists and families from Indonesian international schools who embark on trips during this period.

During these periods, tourist numbers surge dramatically, and accommodation and attraction prices may rise due to high demand. Therefore, if planning to visit Bali during these peak seasons, it's best to book early and prepare thoroughly to ensure a pleasant, worry-free journey.

## 9. Use Cash for Payments

In Bali, cash is the most widely accepted payment method. Make sure to carry enough cash to avoid additional fees that may arise from using credit cards.

The drawbacks of using credit cards abroad include the following points:

1. You may incur additional fees, such as foreign transaction fees, ATM withdrawal fees, and unfavorable exchange rates.
2. Retailers may charge you a fee if you don't pay in local currency.
3. You might overspend if you don't keep track of your daily expenses.
4. You could face fraud risks if your credit card lacks a security chip or gets skimmed.

Therefore, exercise caution when using credit cards abroad.

However, carrying Indonesian rupiah often means dealing with millions (NT$1 ≈ IDR500). If you prefer not to carry so much cash, consider using the e-wallet feature in the Gojek app mentioned earlier.

## 16. Tipping Appropriately

Tipping appropriately is a cultural courtesy during travel and a way to express gratitude for services. When traveling in Bali, giving appropriate tips can help you build friendly relationships with locals and is also a way to respect local customs. Here are some [occasions and considerations for tipping](https://youtu.be/B_oT905RkQw) shared by Jay, helping you make the right choices during your trip.

Additionally, a common question often raised in our Bali community is: 'Can I tip in US dollars?' Here, we share this topic with everyone. In Indonesia or Bali, the only circulating currency is the Indonesian Rupiah (Rupia/IDR). Therefore, if we wish to tip, we must use the local currency, the Indonesian Rupiah. We should avoid using other currencies or foreign money as a means of tipping. This not only shows respect for the local culture but also expresses our sincere gratitude to the service providers.

Let's think from another perspective. Imagine if in Taiwan, a foreign friend handed you foreign currency, wouldn't you find it a bit troublesome? Similarly, residents of Bali might feel the same inconvenience.

Therefore, to ensure our tips accurately convey our gratitude, we should always use Indonesian Rupiah for tipping. This not only respects the locals but also better expresses our appreciation and thanks for the services.
