> The following content was provided by fellow traveler [Zhang Pingping](https://www.facebook.com/zhang.p.ping.3/) and adapted with her permission.

This itinerary is a synthesis of recommendations from fellow travelers and my own personal experiences, aiming to provide detailed feedback. During this trip, we stayed at three different hotels, two nights each, hired a private car for three days, and had a pure airport transfer service on the last day. Below, I’ll share some key points and insights from this journey.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_31e20a9205edd79820f5422844919002.jpg)

## Driver Recommendations

We chose Fovix Ketut, a highly recommended driver from the travel community, and I sincerely recommend him to anyone in need. Despite my extremely limited English, we managed smoothly over these days, thanks to Ketut’s assistance. Here, I’d like to reassure everyone: don’t be afraid of language barriers; make good use of translation apps and the few words you know, and you’ll be fine.

> Further Reading: [【Bali Guide】What to Do If You Want to Visit Bali but Don’t Speak English Well?](/blog/2024-02-08-65c44c29fd89780001141a3e/)

One thing to remind everyone about when booking a driver service is to keep your word and not stand them up casually, as this is the foundation of mutual trust. We communicated thoroughly in advance, and my itinerary was largely adjusted by Ketut, who even recommended the Spa.

## Arriving at Bali Airport

We had already completed the customs declaration, visa, and tourism tax procedures electronically while still in Taiwan, so everything went very smoothly upon arrival at Bali Airport. The electronic customs clearance had multiple steps but was very fast, taking less than ten minutes. The only slightly frustrating part was waiting for our luggage, which took about 30 minutes. Some passengers from the same flight got their luggage much earlier, but ours got stuck on the conveyor belt, sometimes due to jams.

When going through customs, choose the lane with the fewest people, and showing the QR code will get you out of the airport in less than three minutes.

> Read more: [2026 Edition] Using 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card in Bali | Understand the Latest Process in 1 Minute: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim (/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

## Itinerary Planning and Transportation

Originally, I thought our itinerary wasn't too packed and would be relaxing, but Bali's traffic congestion really kept us out late every day. I recommend leaving plenty of time to deal with traffic conditions to avoid affecting your schedule.

## Internet Usage

My mom and I used an eSIM and a physical SIM card respectively, both from XL. It turned out that my eSIM frequently lost signal, especially when I urgently needed to use translation, which was quite frustrating—I had to rely on the few words I could recall. Meanwhile, my mom's physical SIM card worked smoothly throughout. Over the seven days, I mainly relied on hotel WiFi and ended up using less than 3GB of data.

## May 29th Private Car Itinerary

### Arrival in Bali

On this day, our itinerary started right after landing. First, we exchanged money and did some grocery shopping. Then, we went to a somewhat expensive but very famous pork rib restaurant—Naughty Nuri's—and enjoyed a delicious meal.

### Accommodation and Spa

After the meal, we headed straight to our accommodation in Ubud, [Purana Boutique Resort](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1197693). This hotel is nestled in the jungle near the center of Ubud, full of natural ambiance. Although there are quite a few bugs, the staff are very friendly, and the rooms are clean and comfortable, with particularly good beds—highly recommended! The hotel offers shuttle service to Coco Supermarket near the Sacred Monkey Forest, which is very convenient. I suggest staying here, but skip the hotel breakfast as it doesn't taste good—it's just nicely presented XD.

▶[Book Purana Boutique Resort and view more room options](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1197693)

We enjoyed two delightful spa experiences during our Ubud trip. The first was on our arrival night when our driver took us to Jaens Spa Ubud, just 2 minutes from our hotel. The spa quality was excellent and reasonably priced. The second was booked through our hotel the next day.

![張萍萍提供：Purana附近走路兩分鐘的優質jaens spa](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_22b2f40d3235b284f9cd44edc040c012.jpg)

Zhang Pingping recommends: High-quality Jaens Spa, just a 2-minute walk from Purana

### Exploring Ubud Town

On our second day, we took the hotel shuttle to Coco Supermarket and then walked to the Sacred Monkey Forest. The monkeys were quite mischievous, and we even experienced a thrilling 'monkey robbery' incident. Afterward, we continued exploring Ubud Market, casually browsing and sampling local delicacies. Although we didn't hire a car that day, our driver Ketut still cared for us, sending a message the night before to remind us of shopping precautions, concerned that we might get ripped off.

![張萍萍提供：聖猴森林、椰子專賣店](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cb95c8d7db7b619d7cedb47c7207d293.jpg)

### Uluwatu Temple

Next, we visited Uluwatu Temple. The monkeys here are quite aggressive, so Ketut accompanied us throughout like a bodyguard, sweating profusely, shooing away monkeys, and taking photos for us. We're especially grateful for him carrying my mom's heavy bag, which felt like a rock. Just moments before, we witnessed a tourist's phone being snatched by a monkey and thrown off the cliff into the sea, making us even more appreciative of Ketut's companionship and protection.

### Buying Fruits

![張萍萍提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4e911106eea2855ae9b63b329f382.jpg)

Provided by Zhang Pingping

In Bali, my favorite fruit is mangosteen. Knowing our preference, Ketut took advantage of our spa time to search several places for fresh mangosteens. He even bought them for us himself, as locals get them at half the price, but we insisted on paying him. As for durian, we didn't encounter any of good quality, and Ketut proved more professional than the vendors. He would inspect the durians, and if they weren't up to par upon opening, he would clearly communicate with the vendor on our behalf, ensuring we didn't end up with poor-quality fruit. He truly stood by our side to solve the problem.

### Seafood Dinner with Sunset Views

In the evening, Ketut took us to a highly cost-effective seafood restaurant—Menega Cafe, where we could enjoy a beautiful sunset. Ketut helped us check the bill to ensure we weren't overcharged. My mom ordered lobster, two large crabs, and a set meal that included lobster and fish, totaling around USD$100. Even with the three of us, including the driver, we couldn't finish the meal and took the leftovers with us. The restaurant staff, seeing we were full, kindly advised against serving the live fish my mom had ordered, as it would indeed be too much to finish.

### Private Driver Recommendation

Regarding private drivers, I carefully selected several candidates and was fortunate to ultimately choose Fovix Ketut. Ketut treated my mother as his own, constantly caring for her with 'Be careful, Mom' and 'How are you, Mom?' throughout the journey. In turn, my mother cared for him like her own son. In just a few days, we developed a nice bond.

Ketut is a sunny driver in his early 30s with three children. He deeply loves his wife and always beams with joy when talking about her and their daughters. He works extremely hard to serve his clients, putting in great effort to earn money for his family. I strongly recommend him to Taiwanese friends who want to practice their English skills and enjoy adventurous independent travel - you can confidently choose Fovix Ketut.

It's worth mentioning that most drivers in Bali have wives and children, and they also need to take care of their families. Sometimes, they inevitably encounter unexpected family emergencies that prevent them from working. I believe this is not their intention, so let's empathize and understand their situation!
