> The following content was provided by fellow traveler [Zhang Pingping](https://www.facebook.com/zhang.p.ping.3/) and she has agreed to our adaptation

This itinerary was mainly based on suggestions from fellow travelers in our group and my own personal experience, aiming to provide detailed feedback for everyone. During this trip, we stayed at three different hotels, two nights each, hired a private car for three days, and had a pure airport transfer service on the last day. Below, I'll share some highlights and insights from this journey.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_31e20a9205edd79820f5422844919002.jpg)

## Driver Recommendation

For this trip, we chose Fovix Ketut, a highly recommended driver from our group, and I sincerely recommend him to anyone in need. Although my English is quite poor, we managed to get through smoothly thanks to Ketut's assistance. Here, I also want to remind everyone not to be afraid of language barriers; making good use of translation apps and your limited vocabulary is sufficient.

> Further reading: [【Bali Guide】What to Do If You Want to Visit Bali but Don't Speak English Well?](/blog/2024-02-08-65c44c29fd89780001141a3e/)

One thing to remind everyone about when booking a driver service is to be trustworthy and not stand up the driver casually, as this is the foundation of mutual trust. We communicated fully beforehand, and my itinerary was basically adjusted by Ketut, even the Spa was introduced by him.

## Arriving at Bali Airport

We had already completed the customs declaration, visa, and tourism tax procedures electronically in Taiwan, so everything went very smoothly upon arrival at Bali Airport. There are many electronic customs channels, and the process was very fast, taking less than ten minutes. The only slightly frustrating part was waiting for our luggage, which took about 30 minutes. Some passengers from the same flight got their luggage much earlier, but ours got stuck on the conveyor belt, sometimes due to the belt getting jammed.

When going through customs after collecting luggage, choose the lane with the fewest people, and you can exit the airport in less than three minutes by showing the QR code.

> Further Reading: [2026 Edition] Using the 'All Indonesia' Arrival Card in Bali | Master the Latest Process in 1 Minute: From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

## Itinerary Planning and Transportation

Initially, I thought our schedule wasn't too packed and would be relaxed, but Bali's traffic congestion really kept us out late every day. I recommend leaving plenty of buffer time to deal with traffic conditions to avoid disrupting your plans.

## Internet Usage

My mom and I used an eSIM and a physical SIM card respectively, both from XL. My eSIM frequently lost connection, especially when I urgently needed translation, which was frustrating and forced me to rely on the few words I could recall. In contrast, my mom's physical SIM worked smoothly throughout. Over the seven days, I mainly relied on hotel WiFi and ended up using less than 3GB of data.

## May 29th Private Car Itinerary

### Arrival in Bali

Our day began with landing. First, we exchanged money and did some supermarket shopping. Then, we headed to Naughty Nuri's, a somewhat expensive but famous pork rib restaurant, where we enjoyed a delicious meal.

### Accommodation and Spa

After dining, we went straight to our accommodation in Ubud, [Purana Boutique Resort](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1197693). This hotel is nestled in the jungle near central Ubud, offering a natural ambiance. Although there were quite a few bugs, the staff were very friendly, and the rooms were clean and comfortable, with particularly good beds—highly recommended! The hotel provides shuttle service to the Coco supermarket near the Sacred Monkey Forest, which is very convenient. I suggest staying here, but skip the hotel breakfast as it's not tasty, despite the attractive presentation XD

▶[Book Purana Boutique Resort, view more room types](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=1197693)

During our Ubud trip, we enjoyed two delightful spa experiences. The first was on the night we arrived, when our driver took us to Jaens Spa Ubud, just two minutes from our hotel. The spa quality was excellent and reasonably priced. The second was booked through our hotel the following day.

### Exploring Ubud Town

On the second day of our stay, we took the hotel shuttle to Coco Supermarket and then walked to the Sacred Monkey Forest. The monkeys here are very mischievous, and we even experienced a startling incident where they tried to steal from us. Afterward, we continued exploring the Ubud Market, strolling around and sampling local delicacies. Although we didn’t have a private car that day, our driver Ketut was still very attentive, sending us a message the night before to remind us of shopping precautions, worried that we might get scammed.

### Uluwatu Temple

Next, we arrived at Uluwatu Temple. The monkeys here are quite aggressive, so Ketut accompanied us like a bodyguard throughout, sweating profusely, shooing away monkeys, and even taking photos for us. We were especially grateful that he carried my mom's heavy bag, which felt like a rock. Just moments before, we had witnessed a tourist's phone being snatched by a monkey and thrown off the cliff, disappearing into the sea. This made us even more appreciative of Ketut's companionship and protection.

### Buying Fruits

![張萍萍提供](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_68d4e911106eea2855ae9b63b329f382.jpg)

Provided by Zhang Pingping

In Bali, my favorite fruit is mangosteen. Knowing our preference, Ketut took advantage of our spa time to visit several places in search of fresh mangosteen. He even bought some for us himself, as locals get them at half the price, but we insisted on paying him. As for durian, we didn't come across any of good quality. Ketut was more professional than the vendors. He would inspect the durian, and if it wasn't up to par, he would clearly explain to the vendor on our behalf, ensuring we didn't end up with bad fruit. He truly stood by our side to solve problems.

### Seafood Dinner with Sunset Views

In the evening, Ketut took us to a highly cost-effective seafood restaurant—Menega Cafe, where we could enjoy a beautiful sunset. Ketut checked our bill to ensure we weren't overcharged. My mom ordered lobster and two large crabs, along with a set meal that included lobster and fish, totaling around USD$100. Even with three of us, including the driver, we couldn't finish the food and ended up taking leftovers home. The restaurant staff, seeing that we were already full, kindly advised us not to serve the live fish my mom had ordered, as it would indeed be too much to finish.

## June 4th: Transfer Back to Taiwan

On this day, we prepared to return to Taiwan. Since Ketut had to attend to his daughter's matters, he asked his brother to take us back to the airport.

I hope this feedback will be helpful to friends planning to visit Bali. Due to space limitations, many details couldn't be fully covered, so feel free to reach out for more information (o^^o). There were two restaurants that weren't part of our original plan but were recommended by Ketut on the spot, and both were excellent. Unfortunately, I don't know their names (Editor's note: We have already requested additional information from Fovix Ketut).

On the contrary, the two popular restaurants we visited—SunSun and Sacred Rice—didn't meet our expectations. The taste was mediocre, not premium, nor particularly enjoyable.

### Recommended Private Driver

Regarding private drivers, I carefully selected from several options and was fortunate to choose Fovix Ketut. Ketut treated my mother as if she were his own, constantly showing concern with phrases like 'Be careful, Mom' and 'How are you, Mom?' In turn, my mother cared for him like a son. In just a few days, we developed a nice bond.

Ketut is a cheerful driver in his early 30s with three children. He deeply loves his wife and always smiles happily when talking about her and their daughters. He works very hard to serve his clients and is dedicated to earning money to support his family. I highly recommend him to Taiwanese friends who want to practice their English skills and enjoy adventurous independent travel. You can confidently choose Fovix Ketut.

It's worth noting that most drivers in Bali have wives and children and need to take care of their families. Sometimes, they may encounter unexpected family emergencies that prevent them from working. I believe this is not their intention, so let's empathize and understand their situation!
