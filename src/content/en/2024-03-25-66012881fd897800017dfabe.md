Here's an important update for everyone - Bali's tourist tax will soon be subject to random inspections! This new policy has been in effect for five weeks, and now it's time to ensure every visitor understands and complies with this new system.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ba7288e1e422dd5682c5355339280724.jpg)

The Director of Bali Tourism Board announced this news at a recent press conference. He stated that the newly established tourism police unit will begin random checks starting March 26th, targeting popular tourist spots in Bali including Kuta Beach, Uluwatu Temple, and Tanah Lot Temple. These tourism police will patrol with their adorable canine companions, who not only assist the officers but also serve as friendly icebreakers with tourists, creating a more relaxed atmosphere.

According to the latest data, only 40% of foreign tourists have paid the tourist tax. Therefore, these random checks aim to ensure everyone pays their dues on time. While this might be a new challenge for some visitors, don't worry - the tourism board has provided various payment methods including smartphone apps, website payments, and airport payment counters. There's an option for everyone!
