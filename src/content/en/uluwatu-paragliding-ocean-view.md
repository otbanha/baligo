I've always been a scaredy-cat who wouldn't even dare to ride a roller coaster. Every time I saw photos of others up in the sky, I felt both envious and terrified. This trip to Bali, I finally mustered the courage to take on my first paragliding experience! Honestly, my hands were trembling when I booked it, and my mind kept replaying all sorts of scary scenarios: What if I fall? What if the wind is too strong? But looking back now, those worries were completely unnecessary～

Choosing Timbis Beach in Uluwatu was the perfect call! Not only is it Bali's most famous paragliding spot, but the professional instructor team and comprehensive safety measures put a first-timer like me completely at ease.

But getting ready to take on my first paragliding experience, I couldn't help feeling super nervous before departure—after all, I'd be taking off from a cliff edge. But after actually trying it, I found out—**it's really not as scary as I imagined**, and the scenery is so beautiful it makes you forget your fear!

## **🚁 Paragliding Experience Process**

1. **Hotel Pickup & Check-in**
2. - The driver arrived on time in the morning, and the drive took about an hour to Timbis Beach.

- After arriving, I signed a safety waiver, and the instructor explained the process (mostly in English, but hand gestures worked too).

3. **Gear Up & Takeoff Briefing**
4. - The instructor helped me put on internationally certified safety harnesses and a helmet, including full GoPro recording throughout.
5. **Takeoff! The Thrilling Moment at the Cliff Edge**
6. - **No running required!** The instructor led me to a slope at the cliff edge, adjusted the paraglider lines from behind, and **when the wind came, I just gave a gentle push off**—and we were instantly airborne!
7. **10 Minutes in the Air, Enjoying Unbeatable Ocean Views**
8. - The instructor controlled the direction, so I just had to enjoy the views and take photos!

- **Below me was the Indian Ocean**, the water glowing an incredible blue, and I could see the entire Uluwatu cliff line—absolutely stunning!
- The instructor asked if I wanted "**a little thrill?**" and then suddenly spun us around, giving me that roller coaster feeling—it was so much fun!

9. **Landing on the Beach, Collecting Photos & Videos**
10. - The landing was smoother than expected—once your feet touch the ground, it's all over.

    - The staff will transfer the GoPro videos and photos to your phone. **Make sure to check that they saved successfully!**

***

## **⚠️ Notes & Tips**

✅ **Before You Go**

- **What to Wear**: It's best to wear **sneakers**—no flip-flops or sandals.
- **Sun Protection**: UV rays are intense at high altitudes, so be sure to apply sunscreen!
- **Weight Limit**: You must weigh between 40-110 kg (88-242 lbs) to fly—too light or too heavy won't work.
- **Age Limit**: 5 to 66 years old

❌ **Items Not Allowed**

- **Backpacks, handbags** (they can affect balance; small items can go in your pockets).
- **Selfie sticks or any loose items**.

💡 **Other Reminders**

- **Weather-Dependent**: Paragliding relies on wind. If the wind is too light or it rains, the activity may be canceled. **It's a good idea to leave some buffer time in your schedule.**
- **If You're Scared, Tell Your Instructor**: They're experienced and can keep the flight smooth and steady.

***

### **🌟 Verdict: Safer and More Fun Than Expected!**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_967be453cf97a01700bb55bec2eb3271.jpg)

I thought it would be terrifying, but once in the air, it was **incredibly stable**—like floating gently in the sky. The instructor was super friendly and even pointed out landmarks in English. **The most breathtaking moment was seeing the Indian Ocean from above—that shade of blue is unforgettable!**

If you're into thrills, ask your instructor for spins; if you're afraid of heights, a smooth ride is just as amazing. **Highly recommended for paragliding newbies**—it's not as scary as it seems, and the views are totally worth it!

**Overall, a huge thumbs up!** Next time I'm in Bali, I'd love to fly again 🪂💙
