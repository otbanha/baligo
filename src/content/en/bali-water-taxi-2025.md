## Bali's Traffic Congestion: The Current Situation

As one of the world's most famous tourist destinations, Bali attracts millions of visitors each year. However, with the rapid growth of tourism and expanding infrastructure, traffic congestion has become one of the biggest local challenges. Whether during peak hours or the high season, the journey from the airport to Seminyak or Ubud can take hours, even though these areas are only a few dozen kilometers apart.

**Key factors contributing to traffic congestion**:

1. **Road Design**: Most roads in Bali are narrow two-lane roads that cannot handle the surge in vehicle traffic.
2. **Rising Vehicle Numbers**: Not only local residents' vehicles but also a flood of rental cars and taxis.
3. **Scattered Tourist Hotspots**: Attractions like Seminyak, Jimbaran, Ubud, and Uluwatu are spread out, creating high transportation demand.
4. **Lack of Public Transit Options**: Bali's public transportation system is currently limited, with most visitors relying on private car hires or ride-hailing services.

The direct result of traffic congestion is a decline in visitor satisfaction, especially among short-stay travelers, who may find it hard to make the most of their trip due to delays.

---

## Plan Background and Approval Process

To address this issue, Bali's local government and tourism authorities have been actively pushing for transportation infrastructure reforms in recent years. The 'Sea Expressway' plan was introduced in early 2024 and quickly gained attention. The initiative was championed by then-Badung Regent I Nyoman Giri Prasta, who proposed leveraging Bali's island geography to create new sea transportation options and reduce pressure on land routes.

After multiple rounds of expert evaluation and financial review, the project received final approval at the end of 2024 and is scheduled to officially launch in 2025.

**Key Approving Bodies:**

- Bali Provincial Government
- Indonesian Ministry of Transportation
- Indonesian Ministry of Tourism
- Multiple private shipping company partners

This project is regarded as a major milestone for future tourism development, aiming to enhance Bali's global competitiveness while improving the quality of life for local residents.

## Details of the GoBoat Water Taxi Route

![GoBoat 海上計程車路線的細節](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_758cb8328c22d3ffda18f453d04f626c.png)

This new water taxi route will feature three main stops:

1. **Berawa (in the Seminyak/Canggu area)**: A convenient boarding point for travelers visiting the popular hotspots of Seminyak and Canggu.
2. **Nusa Dua**: A hub for high-end resorts, attracting tourists seeking premium travel experiences.
3. **Ngurah Rai International Airport**: A dedicated pier is planned to allow international and domestic travelers to take a water taxi directly from the airport to key destinations.

### **Expected Travel Times and Fares**

- **Berawa to Nusa Dua**: Estimated at just 25 to 30 minutes (compared to 1–2 hours by road).
- **Berawa to Airport**: Approximately 20 minutes.
- Estimated fares will vary by distance and passenger count, with initial plans pricing each trip between 200,000 and 300,000 Indonesian rupiah (approximately USD$13–$19).

## Advantages of the New Water Taxi Route

Compared to GoBoat, the new route offers these clear advantages:

1. **Official Support and Stability**: Operated jointly by the government and several major shipping companies, ensuring better safety and service quality.
2. **Broader Stop Coverage**: Adds an airport stop, greatly benefiting international travelers.
3. **Improved Time Efficiency**: Reduces time wasted on land transportation for tourists.

Additionally, this route will effectively ease road congestion in southern Bali, especially on the main roads leading to the airport—a major boon for both locals and the tourism industry.

This new plan not only offers travelers a more convenient transportation option but will also have a profound impact on Bali's tourism sector.

1. **Elevating the High-End Travel Experience**: Routes connecting luxury resorts will attract more affluent travelers willing to spend.
2. **Extending Visitor Stays**: Convenient transportation may encourage tourists to explore more attractions, further boosting the local economy.
3. **Promoting Eco-Friendly Tourism**: By reducing land traffic pressure and cutting carbon emissions, this aligns with the concept of sustainable tourism.
