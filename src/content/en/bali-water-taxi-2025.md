## The Current Traffic Congestion Situation in Bali

As a globally renowned tourist destination, Bali attracts millions of visitors each year. However, with the rapid development of tourism and the continuous expansion of infrastructure, traffic congestion has become one of the island's most significant issues. Whether during peak hours or the tourist high season, traveling from the airport to areas like Seminyak or Ubud can take several hours, even though these destinations are only a few dozen kilometers apart.

**Main Factors Contributing to Traffic Congestion**:

1. **Road Design**: Most of Bali's roads are narrow two-lane streets, unable to accommodate the rapidly increasing volume of vehicles.
2. **Rising Number of Vehicles**: Not only are there more vehicles owned by locals, but there is also a surge in rental cars and taxis.
3. **Dispersed Tourist Hotspots**: Popular destinations like Seminyak, Jimbaran, Ubud, and Uluwatu are spread out, leading to high traffic demand.
4. **Limited Public Transportation Options**: Bali's public transportation system is relatively underdeveloped, forcing most tourists to rely on private car hires or taxis.

The direct consequence of traffic congestion is a decline in tourist satisfaction, especially among short-term visitors, as congestion may prevent them from fully enjoying their trip.

## Project Background and Approval Process

To address this issue, the local government and tourism-related departments in Bali have actively promoted reforms in transportation infrastructure in recent years. The 'Sea Expressway' project was proposed in early 2024 and quickly gained attention. Spearheaded by then-Badung Regent I Nyoman Giri Prasta, the project aims to leverage Bali's island geography by introducing a new sea-based transportation method to alleviate land traffic pressure.

After multiple rounds of expert evaluations and funding reviews, the project received final approval at the end of 2024 and is scheduled to officially launch in 2025.

**Approving Authorities**:

- Bali Provincial Government
- Indonesian Ministry of Transportation
- Indonesian Ministry of Tourism
- Multiple private shipping company partners

This initiative is considered a significant milestone for future tourism development, aiming to enhance Bali's global competitiveness while improving the quality of life for local residents.

## Details of the GoBoat Water Taxi Route

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_758cb8328c22d3ffda18f453d04f626c.png)

This new water taxi route will feature three main stops:

1. **Berawa (located in the Seminyak/Canggu area)**: Provides a convenient boarding point for tourists visiting Seminyak and Canggu.
2. **Nusa Dua**: A hub for luxury resorts, attracting numerous visitors seeking high-quality travel experiences.
3. **Ngurah Rai International Airport**: Plans include a dedicated dock, allowing international and domestic travelers to directly access major destinations via water taxi from the airport.

### **Estimated Time and Fares**

- **Berawa to Nusa Dua**: Expected to take only 25 to 30 minutes (compared to 1 to 2 hours by land).
- **Berawa to Airport**: Approximately 20 minutes.
- Fares will be adjusted based on distance and passenger numbers, with initial estimates ranging from 200,000 to 300,000 IDR per trip (about USD$13-19).

## Current GoBoat Services

### **GoBoat Current Routes**

1. **Canggu to Jimbaran**: This route is popular because it connects to Jimbaran's famous beach restaurant area.
2. **Canggu to Uluwatu**: This is an attractive option for tourists who love surfing and enjoy stunning cliff views.

## Advantages of the New Sea Taxi Route

Compared to GoBoat, the new route has the following significant advantages:

1. **Official Support and Stability**: Operated jointly by the government and several major shipping companies, ensuring greater safety and service quality.
2. **Broader Coverage**: Adding airport stops greatly facilitates international travelers.
3. **Improved Time Efficiency**: Reduces the time tourists waste on land transportation.

Additionally, this route will effectively alleviate road pressure in southern Bali, especially on the main roads leading to the airport, which is a boon for both local residents and the tourism industry.

## Potential Impact on Bali's Tourism Industry

This new initiative not only provides tourists with more convenient transportation options but will also have a profound impact on Bali's tourism industry.

1. **Enhancing High-End Tourism Experience**: Routes connecting luxury resorts will attract more high-spending luxury travelers.
2. **Extending Tourist Stay**: Convenient transportation may encourage tourists to explore more attractions, further boosting the local economy.
3. **Promoting Eco-Friendly Tourism**: By reducing land transportation pressure and carbon emissions, it aligns with sustainable tourism principles.
