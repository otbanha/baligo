✈️ **Six-Day, Five-Night Bali Self-Guided Travel Diary (6/22－6/27)**

This trip was truly a long-awaited getaway for just the two of us! 👫

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9f15f8cf86cf66e1b6c46b8c56f6c98e.png)

After entrusting our child to my mother-in-law, my wife and I packed our bags with almost 'impatient' speed, ready to board the flight to Bali. For parents who haven't traveled abroad in a while, such a relaxing vacation is especially precious, and our anticipation was through the roof.

## 📌 **Essential Reminders: Four Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we referred to many travel experts' advice and applied for Indonesia's **Online Visa on Arrival (e-VOA)** in advance. Honestly, this step is super important as it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, here's a little trap to warn you about—my wife accidentally **entered the wrong birthday date** when filling out the e-VOA 😓 As a result, we had to go through the manual Visa on Arrival channel, queuing on-site for **an hour and a half**, which was super regretful!

Besides the visa, there are three other important online procedures that are also crucial. It's recommended to handle them before boarding to avoid chaos upon arrival:

1. **Customs Declaration Form (e-CD)**
2. **Tourist Tax Payment Receipt (Tourist Levy)**
3. **Health Declaration Form (SSHP)**

All four processes above can be completed online as electronic documents, which are actually not difficult to handle. Just prepare copies of your passport, flight information, and payment method in advance. It's recommended to save the completed QR Code screenshot in your phone's photo album for faster scanning upon arrival. For detailed procedures, check here: [Understanding Bali's 2025 Entry Changes in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Entry Process - From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2023-12-07-65717449fd89780001fda950/)

Next, let's start our Bali adventure for two～🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Check-in at the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We took EVA Air's direct flight at 9:50 AM, scheduled to arrive in Bali at 3:30 PM. Although the flight was long, taking a nap on the plane helped us switch to vacation mode.

After arriving at Ngurah Rai International Airport, due to queuing for the visa on arrival, it was already late when we cleared customs. We boarded our pre-booked transfer car and arrived at **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran by 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort is Worth Staying for Two Nights!**

This hotel was truly the most anticipated stop of our trip! AYANA is a very famous resort in Bali, not only with complete facilities but also boasting stunning sea views and several unique restaurants. Among them, the legendary **Rock Bar Bali** is a must-visit—it's hailed as one of the 'Top Ten Sunset Bars in the World' and is a super popular photo spot.

Although we arrived a bit late, missing the sunset and only capturing the night scene, standing at the cliffside bar area, listening to the waves and watching the lights sway, the atmosphere was incredibly romantic💫. The food itself was decent, slightly pricey, but what you're really here for is not the dishes but the atmosphere of dancing with the sea and sky.

![ (Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

We stayed in an ocean-view room, which cost around USD$387 (NT$12,000) per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a tip: **the AYANA resort is massive**. If you opt for a cheaper room, you might end up in a more remote area, and walking to the main facilities could be exhausting. I recommend stretching your budget a bit to choose a room closer to the main facilities—it’ll make your experience much better!

The photo crowd was already there early in the morning, with a queue and a one-minute time limit per person for quick shots. But the good news is—you can queue up again! 📷

### 🍽 **Breakfast Feast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the all-you-can-eat restaurant near the AYANA lobby. Although it was slightly pricey at around USD$29.6 (NT$960) per person, the options were incredibly diverse—Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even made-to-order omelets and dessert pastries. It was a hearty meal, and we were so full that we skipped lunch entirely, saving on one meal budget 😂

A little side story: we left a **USD$2 tip** during housekeeping, and when we returned to our room in the afternoon, the housekeeping staff had prepared a small surprise for us. It was thoughtful and heartwarming, truly reflecting AYANA’s service spirit. In contrast, during our last trip to Chiang Mai, a friend left USD$10 but got nothing in return. Sometimes, luck is also a skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to the Highlands Café, Staying in a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After enjoying a hearty breakfast and a leisurely morning at AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to embark on the 'mountain and forest chapter' of our journey 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive for Coffee and Scenery**

The first stop of the day was **Montana Del Café**, a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region in northern Bali. Driving from AYANA in Jimbaran, the one-way trip took a whopping **two and a half hours**—truly a journey over mountains and through valleys.

![ (Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was worth it! Montana Del Café boasts breathtaking mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether sitting indoors or on the terrace, the scenery was like a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two slices of cheesecake, totaling around USD$16, which is quite reasonable for a scenic café. The highlight was the feeling of 'drinking coffee in the clouds'—truly priceless ✨

If you love photography or enjoy fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just be mindful of the long travel time. You can ask your driver to suggest combining it with other Ubud attractions, like volcanic hot springs, for a more efficient trip.

### 🌿 **Accommodation: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After wrapping up our highland adventure, we drove back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay turned out to be one of the most delightful surprises of our trip, perfectly embodying the 'relaxation + romance' vibe we were looking for!

![ (Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcb27348f2a4fd72f4e265beed93773.jpg)

First, this is an adults-only resort. Without the noise of children, the environment is exceptionally peaceful. The staff are friendly and attentive, from welcome drinks to luggage assistance, every detail shows their thoughtfulness.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modernity with nature, with large floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking lush mountains. You can even hear birds chirping, insects buzzing, and streams flowing, making it perfect for couples to enjoy a romantic and serene time. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so you can soak in the tub and enjoy the greenery without leaving your room—pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Staying at The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak for a Convenient and Comfortable Night**

After leaving the tranquil mountains of Ubud, we headed to the lively coastal area of **Seminyak**. This is Bali's famous fashion district, filled with restaurants, bars, boutique shops, and Instagram-worthy cafes. It's super convenient and perfect for travelers looking to experience a city resort vibe.

We stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, located in the heart of Seminyak. This villa offers incredible value for money, costing only around **USD$150 per night**. It's spacious, well-equipped, and comes with a private pool! Within walking distance, you'll find convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage parlors, so there's no need to call a ride.

## 🎉 Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club - Worth Being a Luxury Spender for a Day!

Having visited Bali so many times, we finally arranged a long-awaited trip to Finns Beach Club! As one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs, located in Canggu, the venue faces the beach and features several pools, sun loungers, bars, DJ stages, and VIP cabanas, creating an incredibly lively and energetic atmosphere.

However, online reviews are quite polarized—some find the staff indifferent and prices high, while others praise the great vibe and excellent facilities, deeming it worth a visit.

Our own experience was that if you only opt for the cheapest lounger package, you might feel overlooked. But this time, we splurged on a VIP cabana package, and although it cost a pretty penny, the treatment we received was entirely different! ✨

💡 The key to good service really is 'spending money in the right places!' When you choose a high-end cabana, security, waitstaff, and even dancers will actively greet and interact with you, elevating the atmosphere to that of a private party.

The table next to us was also in the cabana area but didn't order much or engage much, and as a result, they were largely ignored, with dancers not even glancing their way😅 So, to truly enjoy Finns, you need to embrace being a luxury spender for a day (the happy kind) to fully experience its charm!

Our short six-day, five-night Bali trip came to an end. After checking out at noon, we headed to Denpasar Airport for our flight back to Taiwan. ## 📝 **Five quick takeaways from our Bali trip that might help you:**

### ✅ **1. Make sure to get your e-VOA online visa in advance!**

Let me say it again - **the e-VOA online visa is super important!** It can save you tons of queuing time. Especially for those traveling with kids or taking red-eye flights, applying early is a sign of self-respect for your energy levels.

### 📱 **2. Always use Gojek or Grab for rides**

These two local ride-hailing apps allow credit card payments with transparent pricing. We once made the mistake of flagging down a motorcycle on the street and got ripped off - they quoted prices in USD like it was totally normal 😓. Don't try to save small money only to lose big.

Quick reference: Grab or Gojek? [Complete guide to registering, using, and topping up Gojek and GoPay in Indonesia/Bali](/blog/2024-10-13-670b5191fd8978000185cf37/)

### 🚦 **3. Traffic jams are daily life in Bali - don't overpack your itinerary!**

Especially on routes between Jimbaran, Seminyak, and Ubud, traffic is often heavy. We strongly suggest planning just 1-2 key activities per day, otherwise you'll spend your whole trip 'missing out' and it'll be exhausting.

### 💵 **4. Tipping culture exists, but don't feel pressured!**

Over 75% of Bali's population works in the service industry, and tipping appropriately is a gesture of courtesy and support. However, you may encounter situations where people use manipulative language or act entitled when asking for tips. In such cases, **you really don't need to feel obligated to tip**. Maintaining boundaries and self-respect is more important.

Quick Reference: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Different Situations?](/blog/2024-05-07-663a126bfd89780001390b63/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google It Before Deciding to Go!**

If you join a private car or guided tour, you might be 'conveniently taken' to this coffee factory. These places primarily sell expensive civet coffee and sometimes employ high-pressure sales tactics or overly pushy explanations. Whether it's worth visiting is entirely up to you! 🧐
