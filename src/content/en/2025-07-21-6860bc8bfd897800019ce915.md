✈️ **Our 6-Day, 5-Night Bali Self-Guided Trip (June 22–27)**

This trip was truly a long-awaited chance for just the two of us! 👫

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9f15f8cf86cf66e1b6c46b8c56f6c98e.png)

After entrusting our child to my mother-in-law, my wife and I packed our bags with almost 'can't-wait' speed, ready to board our flight to Bali. For parents who haven't had a chance to travel abroad in a while, this relaxing vacation was especially precious, and our excitement was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Reminder: Four Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we took advice from many travel experts and applied for Indonesia's **Electronic Visa on Arrival (e-VOA)** in advance. Honestly, this step is super important as it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, here's a little trap to watch out for—my wife **accidentally filled in the wrong birthday date** when applying for the e-VOA 😓. As a result, we had to go through the manual Visa on Arrival channel, queuing for **an hour and a half** on-site, which was super frustrating!

Besides the visa, there are three other important online procedures that are also crucial. We recommend handling them before boarding to avoid any last-minute hassles upon arrival:

1. **Customs Declaration Form (e-CD)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**
3. **Health Declaration Form (SSHP)**

All four processes above can be completed online as electronic documents, making them relatively straightforward to handle. Just make sure to prepare copies of your passport, flight information, and payment method in advance. After completion, it's recommended to save the QR Code as a screenshot in your phone's photo album for quick scanning upon arrival. For a detailed step-by-step guide, check out this link: [Understanding Bali's 2025 Entry Changes in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Entry Process - From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](/blog/2023-12-07-65717449fd89780001fda950/)

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Check-in at the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We took EVA Air's direct flight departing at 9:50 AM, scheduled to arrive in Bali at 3:30 PM. Although the flight was long, a short nap onboard helped us shift into vacation mode.

After landing at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we went through the manual visa process, and by the time we cleared customs, it was already late. We boarded our pre-booked transfer car and arrived at **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran around 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort is Worth Staying Two Nights!**

This hotel was truly the highlight of our trip! AYANA is one of Bali's most renowned resorts, offering excellent facilities, stunning ocean views, and several unique dining options. Among them, the legendary **Rock Bar Bali**—hailed as one of the "World's Top 10 Sunset Bars"—is a must-visit hotspot.

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, capturing the night view was still magical. Standing at the cliffside bar, listening to the waves and watching the lights flicker, the atmosphere was incredibly romantic💫. The food was decent but slightly pricey, though the real draw here isn't the cuisine but the enchanting ambiance of being one with the sea and sky.

![ (Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

We stayed in a sea view room, which cost around USD$387 per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a small tip: **the AYANA resort complex is huge**. If you choose a cheaper room type, you might end up in a more remote area, and walking to the main facilities could be exhausting. I recommend adding a bit more to your budget and selecting a room closer to the main facilities for a better experience!

The photo crowd was already there early in the morning, with queues and a one-minute time limit per person, but the good news is—you can queue again! 📷

### 🍽 **Eat a Hearty Breakfast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the all-you-can-eat restaurant next to the AYANA lobby. Although it was slightly pricey at around USD$29.6 per person (approximately NT$960), the options were very diverse, including local Indonesian dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even freshly made omelets and bread desserts. It was very satisfying. After eating so much, we couldn't even think about lunch, saving us a meal budget 😂

A small anecdote: we left a **USD$2 tip** during room cleaning, and when we returned to our room in the afternoon, the housekeeping auntie had prepared a small surprise for us. It was thoughtful and heartwarming, truly reflecting AYANA's service spirit. In contrast, when we were in Chiang Mai last time, a friend left USD$10 but got nothing in return. Sometimes, luck is also a kind of skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to a Highland Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After enjoying a hearty breakfast and leisurely morning at AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to embark on the "mountain chapter" of our journey 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive, Just for Coffee and Scenery**

The first stop of the day was a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin — **Montana Del Café**, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region in northern Bali. Driving from AYANA in Jimbaran took a whopping **two and a half hours** one way—truly a journey over mountains and through valleys.

![ (Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was worth it once we arrived! Montana Del Café boasts breathtaking mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether seated indoors or on the terrace, it felt like being in a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two cheesecakes, totaling around USD$16, which is reasonable for a scenic café. The feeling of "drinking coffee in the clouds" was truly priceless ✨.

If you love photography or enjoy the fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just be mindful of the long travel time. You can ask your driver to suggest combining it with other Ubud attractions, like volcanic hot springs, for a more efficient trip.

### 🌿 **Stay: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After our highland adventure, we drove back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay turned out to be one of the most delightful surprises of our trip, perfectly matching our desire for a "relaxing + romantic" vacation vibe!

![ (Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcb27348f2a4fd72f4e265beed93773.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

First, this is an adults-only resort, so without the noise of children playing, the environment is particularly serene. The staff are friendly and attentive, from the welcome drink to luggage assistance, every detail makes you feel their dedication.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modernity with nature, with large floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking lush mountains. You can even hear birds chirping, insects buzzing, and the sound of flowing streams, making it perfect for couples or spouses to enjoy a romantic and peaceful time. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so if you don't feel like going out, you can soak in the tub while enjoying the greenery—pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Stay at The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak, Enjoying a Convenient and Comfortable Night**

After leaving the tranquil mountains of Ubud, our journey took us to the lively coastal area of **Seminyak**. This is Bali's famous fashion district, filled with restaurants, bars, boutique shops, and Instagram-worthy cafes. The area is super convenient for daily needs, making it ideal for travelers looking to experience a city resort vibe.

That night, we stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, located in the heart of Seminyak. This villa offers incredible value for money—around **USD$150 per night**—but it’s spacious, fully equipped, and comes with a private pool! Within a few minutes' walk, you’ll find convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage parlors, so there’s no need to call for a ride.

## 🎉 **Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club, Being a Luxurious Plate for a Day is Worth It!**

Having visited Bali so many times, we finally arranged a long-awaited trip to **Finns Beach Club** on this day! It's one of Bali's most iconic beach clubs, located in the Canggu area. The entire venue faces the beach, featuring multiple pools, sun loungers, bars, DJ stages, and VIP lounges, creating a lively and energetic atmosphere.

However, online reviews about this place are quite polarized. Some feel that **the staff's attitude is indifferent and the prices are on the higher side**; while others believe that **the atmosphere is great, the facilities are fantastic, and it's worth a visit**.

Our own experience was—**if you only opt for the cheapest lounger package, you might indeed feel overlooked**; but this time, we gritted our teeth and upgraded to a VIP sofa lounge package. Although it cost us quite a bit for the day, the treatment we received was completely different! ✨

## 📝 **Five quick takeaways from this Bali trip that might help you:**

### ✅ **1. Make sure to get your online visa-on-arrival sorted in advance!**

Let me say it again: **the e-VOA online visa is super important!** It can save you a ton of time waiting in line. Especially if you're traveling with kids or on a red-eye flight, applying early is a sign of self-respect for your energy levels.

### 📱 **2. Always use Gojek or Grab for rides**

These two local ride-hailing apps in Indonesia allow credit card payments, offering transparent pricing and peace of mind. We once tried to flag down a motorcycle on the street for convenience, only to get ripped off with inflated USD prices and a 'take it or leave it' attitude 😓. Don't save a little money only to lose big.

Quick reference: Grab or Gojek? [Complete Guide to Registering, Using, and Topping Up Gojek and GoPay in Indonesia/Bali](/blog/2024-10-13-670b5191fd8978000185cf37/)

### 🚦 **3. Traffic jams are a daily reality in Bali — don't overpack your itinerary!**

Especially on routes like Jimbaran, Seminyak to Ubud, traffic can be brutal. We strongly recommend planning just 1~2 key activities per day, or you'll spend your trip constantly 'missing out' and feeling exhausted.

### 💵 **4. Tipping culture exists, but don't let guilt-tripping pressure you!**

Over 75% of Bali's population works in the service industry, and tipping appropriately is considered polite and supportive. However, you may encounter situations where people use manipulative language or act entitled when asking for tips. In such cases, **you really don't need to force yourself to tip**—maintaining boundaries and self-respect is more important.

Quick Reference: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/blog/2024-05-07-663a126bfd89780001390b63/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google It Before Deciding to Go!**

If you join a private car or guided tour, you might be 'conveniently taken' to this coffee factory. These places primarily sell expensive civet coffee and sometimes employ high-pressure sales tactics or overly pushy explanations. Whether it's worth visiting is up to you to decide! 🧐
