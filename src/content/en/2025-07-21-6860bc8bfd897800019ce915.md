✈️ **Six-Day, Five-Night Bali Travel Diary (6/22–6/27)**

This trip was truly a long-awaited getaway for just the two of us! 👫

After entrusting our child to my mother-in-law, my wife and I practically packed our bags in record speed, ready to board the flight to Bali. For parents who haven’t had a chance to travel abroad in a while, this relaxing vacation was especially precious, and our excitement was through the roof.

## 📌 **Practical Reminder: Three Online Procedures to Complete Before Departure**

Before this trip, we took advice from many travel experts and applied for Indonesia’s **online Visa on Arrival (e-VOA)** in advance. Honestly, this step is super important—it can save you a lot of precious waiting time.

However, here’s a little trap to watch out for—my wife **accidentally entered the wrong birthdate** while filling out the e-VOA 😓. As a result, we had to go through the manual Visa on Arrival channel, queuing for **an hour and a half** at the airport. Major regret!

Besides the visa, there are two other online procedures that are equally important. We recommend handling them before boarding to avoid any last-minute chaos upon arrival:

1. **All Indonesia Arrival Card (including e-CD Customs Declaration and SSHP Health Declaration)**
2. **Tourist Levy Payment Receipt**

All four of these processes are **electronic documents that can be completed online**, and they're actually quite straightforward to handle. Just make sure to prepare copies of your passport, flight information, and payment method in advance. After completion, it's recommended to save the QR Code as a screenshot in your phone's photo album for quick scanning upon arrival. For detailed steps, check out this guide: [Understanding Bali's 2026 Entry Changes in One Minute: Indonesia/Bali Entry Process - From Landing to Customs to Baggage Claim](https://gobaligo.id/blog/2025-08-14-689dcce7fd8978000125fc52/)

Next, it's time to begin our Bali adventure for two～🌴

## 🌅 **Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Check-in at the Dreamy AYANA Resort**

We boarded EVA Air's direct flight at 9:50 AM, scheduled to arrive in Bali at 3:30 PM. Although the flight was a bit long, taking a nap on the plane helped us switch into vacation mode.

After landing at Ngurah Rai International Airport, we went through the manual visa process, and by the time we cleared customs, it was already dark. We hopped into our pre-booked transfer car and arrived at **AYANA Resort and Spa Bali** in Jimbaran by 6 PM.

### 🏨 **AYANA Resort is Worth Staying Two Nights!**

This hotel was truly the most anticipated stop of our trip! AYANA is a renowned resort in Bali, boasting excellent facilities, stunning ocean views, and several unique restaurants. Among them, the legendary **Rock Bar Bali** is a must-visit—a super popular spot hailed as one of the 'Top 10 Sunset Bars in the World.'

https://youtu.be/lbDfKut2J7M

Although we arrived a bit late and missed the sunset, capturing the night view was still magical. Standing at the cliffside bar, listening to the waves and watching the lights sway, the atmosphere was incredibly romantic💫. The food itself was decent, albeit slightly pricey, but what you're really here for is the ambiance of dancing with the sea and sky.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_197ac68f3eeb4671e523304f84bab3d1.jpg)

We stayed in an ocean view room, which cost around USD$387 per night. While it was already quite nice, here's a small tip: **The AYANA resort complex is massive**. If you opt for a cheaper room, you might end up in a more remote area, and walking to the main facilities could be exhausting. I recommend adding a bit more to your budget and choosing a room closer to the main facilities for a better experience!

The photo crowd was already there early in the morning, with queues and a one-minute time limit per person, but the good news is—you can queue up again! 📷

### 🍽 **Fill Up on Breakfast, Skip Lunch**

For breakfast, we chose the all-you-can-eat restaurant near the AYANA lobby. Although it was slightly pricey at around USD$29.6 per person (approximately NT$960), the options were incredibly diverse, featuring Indonesian local dishes, Japanese cuisine, European cold cuts, and even freshly made omelets and pastries. It was a hearty meal, and we were so full that we skipped lunch entirely, saving on one meal budget 😂

A little anecdote: We left a **$2 tip** while housekeeping was cleaning our room, and when we returned in the afternoon, we found that the housekeeping auntie had quietly prepared a small surprise for us. It was so thoughtful and heartwarming, truly reflecting AYANA's service spirit. In contrast, during our last trip to Chiang Mai, a friend left a $10 tip but received nothing in return. I guess luck is also a kind of skill 😆

## ☕️ **Day 3: Heading to a Highland Café, Checking into a Romantic Jungle Villa in Ubud**

After enjoying a hearty breakfast and leisurely morning at AYANA, we checked out at 11:00 AM, ready to embark on the "mountain chapter" of our journey 🌿.

### 🚗 **A Long Drive for Coffee and Scenery**

The first stop of the day was a scenic café highly recommended by my cousin — **Montana Del Café**, located in the high-altitude Kintamani region in northern Bali. Driving from AYANA in Jimbaran took a whopping **two and a half hours**, truly a journey over mountains and through valleys.

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5245fa7b61c80c4a4252d2e9876cc3e0.jpg)

(Photo by Johnny Wei)

But I have to say, it was worth it once we arrived! Montana Del Café boasts stunning mountain views, with the majestic Mount Batur and its lake in the distance. Whether seated indoors or on the terrace, the scenery was like a postcard 📸. We ordered two coffees and two slices of cheesecake, totaling around USD$16, which is reasonable for a scenic café. The feeling of "drinking coffee in the clouds" was truly priceless ✨.

If you love photography or enjoy the fresh mountain air, I highly recommend adding this to your itinerary. Just be mindful of the long travel time. You can ask your driver to suggest other nearby Ubud attractions, such as volcanic hot springs, to make the trip more efficient.

### 🌿 **Accommodation: Abisena Wellness & Resort Ubud**

After our highland adventure, we drove back to central **Ubud** and checked into **Abisena Wellness & Resort**, a hidden gem nestled in the jungle. This stay was one of the most delightful surprises of our trip, perfectly matching our desire for a "relaxing + romantic" getaway!

![(Johnny Wei提供)](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0bcb27348f2a4fd72f4e265beed93773.jpg)

(Provided by Johnny Wei)

First and foremost, this is an adults-only resort. Without the playful noises of children, the environment feels exceptionally serene. The staff are warm and attentive, from the welcome drinks to luggage assistance, every detail makes you feel their dedication.

We chose the **Jungle View Villa**. The room design blends modernity with nature, with large floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking lush forests. You can even hear birds chirping, insects buzzing, and streams flowing, making it perfect for couples seeking a romantic and peaceful retreat. The villa comes with a private balcony and pool, so you can enjoy soaking in the tub while gazing at the greenery without leaving your room—pure bliss 💚

## 🏝 **Day 4: Staying at The Claremont Luxury Villas in Seminyak, Enjoying a Convenient and Comfortable Night**

After leaving the tranquil mountains of Ubud, we headed to the lively coastal area of **Seminyak**. This is Bali's famous fashion district, filled with restaurants, bars, boutique shops, and Instagram-worthy cafes. The area is super convenient, making it ideal for travelers looking to experience a city resort vibe.

That night, we stayed at **The Claremont Luxury Villas**, located in the heart of Seminyak. This villa offers incredible value for money, costing only around **USD$150 per night**, yet it’s spacious, fully equipped, and features a private pool! Within a few minutes' walk, you’ll find convenience stores, international restaurants, and massage parlors, so there’s no need to call a cab.

## 🎉 **Day 5: Pilgrimage to Finns Beach Club, Being a Luxury Plate for a Day is Worth It!**

Having visited Bali so many times, we finally arranged our long-awaited visit to **Finns Beach Club** on this day! It’s one of Bali’s most iconic beach clubs, located in Canggu. The entire venue faces the beach and features multiple pools, sun loungers, bars, DJ stages, and VIP cabanas, creating an incredibly lively and energetic atmosphere.

https://youtu.be/MpetWBEQ2Ts

However, online reviews about this place are really polarized. Some people feel that **the staff’s attitude is cold and the prices are high**, while others believe that **the atmosphere is great, the facilities are fantastic, and it’s worth a visit**.

Our own experience was this—**if you only opt for the cheapest lounger package, you might feel overlooked**. But this time, we upgraded to a VIP cabana package, and although it cost us quite a bit, the treatment we received was completely different! ✨

💡The key to good service really is **spending money in the right places**! When you choose a higher-priced cabana, the security guards, waitstaff, and even dancers will proactively greet and interact with you, instantly elevating the atmosphere to feel like a private party.

The table next to us was also in the cabana area, but perhaps they didn’t order much or didn’t engage much, and as a result, they were almost completely ignored—even the dancers didn’t glance their way 😅. So, **to truly enjoy Finns, you have to be a luxury plate for a day** (the happy kind) to fully experience its charm!

## 🛫 **Day 6: Saying Goodbye and Heading Home**

Our short six-day, five-night trip to Bali has come to an end. We checked out at 12:00 PM and headed to Denpasar Airport to catch our flight back to Taiwan. Although the flight was scheduled to depart at 4:30 PM, due to airline delays, we didn't arrive in Taiwan until **11:00 PM**, several hours later than expected. Exhaustion levels were off the charts!

But hey, that's travel for you — always full of unexpected little twists. The important thing is that we made unforgettable memories, and it was all worth it! 🎒

## 📝 **Five Key Takeaways from Our Bali Trip (Hope This Helps!)**

### ✅ **1. Apply for Your e-VOA Online Visa in Advance!**

Let me say it again: **the e-VOA online visa is super important!** It saves you a ton of time standing in line. Especially if you're traveling with kids or on a red-eye flight, applying early is a sign of self-respect for your energy levels.

### 📱 **2. Always Use Gojek or Grab for Ride-Hailing**

These two local ride-hailing apps in Indonesia allow you to pay with credit cards, offering transparent pricing and peace of mind. Once, we tried to save time by hailing a motorcycle on the street, only to get ripped off with a USD price hike and a "take it or leave it" attitude 😓. Don't try to save a little money and end up losing big.

{{block:包車}}

### 🚦 **3. Traffic Jams Are a Daily Reality in Bali — Don’t Overpack Your Itinerary!**

Especially on routes like Jimbaran to Seminyak or Ubud, traffic jams are common. We strongly recommend planning just 1\~2 key activities per day. Otherwise, you'll spend your trip constantly "missing out" and feeling stressed.

### 💵 **4. Tipping Culture: Give When Appropriate, But Don’t Feel Pressured!**

Over 75% of Bali’s population works in the service industry, so tipping moderately is a polite way to show support. However, some people may use manipulative language or act entitled when asking for tips. In such cases, **you really don’t need to force yourself to tip**. Maintaining boundaries and self-respect is more important.

Quick Reference: [Should You Tip in Bali? How Much to Tip in Various Situations?](/blog/2024-05-07-663a126bfd89780001390b63/)

### 🐰 **5. Gold Rabbit Coffee Factory? Google It Before Deciding to Visit!**

If you’re on a private car or guided tour, you’ll often be ‘conveniently’ taken to this coffee factory. These places primarily sell expensive civet coffee, and sometimes you might encounter high-pressure sales tactics or overly pushy explanations. Whether it’s worth visiting is up to you to decide. 🧐
