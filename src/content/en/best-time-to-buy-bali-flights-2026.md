If you're planning a Bali getaway or a tropical island escape soon, hold off on swiping your card for that ticket!

With international aviation fuel prices stabilizing, Taiwan's Civil Aeronautics Administration has announced a significant reduction in 'passenger fuel surcharges' for national airlines, effective **June 7, 2026**. Whether you're heading to Japan, Korea, or the Southeast Asian paradise of Bali, your flight costs will see a welcome drop.

### 📉 Latest Fuel Surcharge Prices Revealed

According to the latest CPC Corporation announcement, international aviation fuel prices have dropped sharply from $208.78 per barrel to $160.1 per barrel (the pricing tier has shifted from Level 18 down to Level 14). The CAA has also required all airlines to fully disclose the updated charges, which are as follows:

- **Short-haul routes (including Bali, Japan, Korea, Hong Kong, Macau, and Southeast Asia):** Reduced from $45 to **$35** per segment (a 22.22% decrease).
- **Long-haul routes (Europe, Americas, etc.):** Reduced from $117 to **$91** per segment.

> **How much can you actually save?** For a round-trip ticket from Taiwan to Bali (a short-haul route), each passenger will save **$20** directly on fuel surcharges alone. For a family of four, that's over **$80** in savings—enough to enjoy a delicious seaside meal in Bali!

### 💡 Travel Expert Reveals the 'Best Time to Buy Flights'

Why the rush to buy? Because fuel surcharges are based on the **ticket issuance date (the day you pay)**, not your departure date.

If you've already spotted a flight to Bali, hold off on paying until **June 7 (inclusive) or later** before swiping your card online or issuing the ticket. By avoiding these few days, you can directly benefit from the reduced rates and save money in a smarter way.

Currently, aside from Taiwan's national carriers (such as China Airlines, EVA Air, and STARLUX Airlines), other Asian airlines like Korean Air, Asiana Airlines, and the Civil Aviation Authority of the Philippines have also announced fuel surcharge reductions in June. With summer vacation travel planning in full swing, this drop in oil prices brings a tangible 'little joy' to those eager to vacation abroad.

### ✈️ Direct Flights from Taiwan to Bali: Latest Fares and Deals

Currently, direct flights from Taiwan to Bali are available with China Airlines, EVA Air, and STARLUX Airlines. Round-trip fares on full-service carriers, including baggage and meals, generally range from **USD$468 to USD$532**. With recent promotions, you might snag an even better price:

- **EVA Air**:
EVA Air has recently launched summer and peak-season travel promotions, offering **up to 17% off** on many popular international routes. If you plan early to avoid the peak summer season or opt for a flight-and-hotel package, round-trip fares start at USD$469, with additional perks like accommodation and transfer discounts from partner travel agencies.
- **STARLUX Airlines**:
STARLUX Airlines is offering special deals on island getaway trips for the second half of the year, with round-trip fares from Taipei to Bali **starting at USD$492**. For those keen on experiencing premium service and the new A321neo aircraft, keep an eye on departure dates in September and October or later.
- **China Airlines**:
China Airlines also has promotional offers on select routes. Currently, round-trip fares to Bali, including taxes, start at around USD$468. It's recommended to use the 'Low Fare Calendar' feature on China Airlines' website to pick mid-week departures (like Tuesday or Wednesday), which often yield the cheapest fares.

If you're aiming to minimize your budget, besides waiting until after June 7 to book when fuel surcharges drop, consider these two strategies:

#### 1. Budget-Friendly Choice: Consider Budget Airlines with Layovers (Save Nearly USD$300)

If you don't mind an extra stop, flying with Scoot **or** VietJet Air via Singapore or Ho Chi Minh City to Bali can be significantly cheaper.

- **Scoot**: Recently, one-way fares from Taipei to Bali in June start at around **USD$197**, with round trips coming in at just over USD$300.
- **VietJet Air**: Frequently offers super deals, with round-trip fares during promotional periods potentially falling between **USD$226 and USD$290** (excluding baggage).

#### 2. The Ultimate Strategy for Flexible Travelers: The 'Four-Segment External Ticket'

This is the favorite money-saving trick of travel experts in recent years. By using an external departure point (e.g., flying from Hong Kong, transiting through Taipei to Bali), you can bundle multiple itineraries into one ticket. Sometimes, the total price of a four-segment ticket like 'Hong Kong–Taipei–Bali' is even cheaper than a direct 'Taipei–Bali' flight, making it perfect for travelers with ample vacation time or those wanting to explore other destinations along the way.

> **Final Booking Reminder:** If you've decided to fly direct with a traditional national carrier, be sure to hold your patience and **wait until June 7, when the fuel surcharge reduction officially takes effect, before booking and paying on the official website**. This way, you can maximize the double benefit of the promotional discount and the NT$600 (approx. USD$19) savings from the reduced fuel surcharge!
