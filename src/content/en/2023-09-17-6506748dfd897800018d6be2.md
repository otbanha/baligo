Jimbaran is a beautiful beach area in Bali, known for its pristine beaches, fresh seafood, and romantic sunsets. This location offers numerous seafood restaurants where visitors can enjoy delicious meals by the sea while taking in breathtaking ocean views. Additionally, Jimbaran's proximity to Bali's international airport allows diners to watch planes take off and land while enjoying their dinner.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b188faf24c6720d2559a5a1a246a1774.jpg)

Thus, Jimbaran Beach is an ideal sunset spot with a variety of seafood restaurants to choose from. If you're craving fresh seafood, here are 11 of the best seafood restaurants in Jimbaran:

1. **Menega Cafe**: For years, Menega has been one of Jimbaran's most popular beachside cafes. While it may seem similar to other restaurants at first glance, it consistently attracts a steady stream of visitors. To secure a good table before the crowd arrives (usually around 5:30 PM), it's best to arrive early. Ordering promptly is also crucial to avoid long waits. Nearly every dish here is delicious, with highlights including giant prawns, spicy grilled snapper, and clams drenched in a flavorful garlic chili sauce. Dining at a beachside table while watching Jimbaran's sunset makes this spot a perfect romantic setting.
2. **Kisik Bar and Grill at Ayana Resort and Spa**: Located within the Ayana Resort in Jimbaran Bay, Kisik Bar and Grill offers two of our favorite things: fresh, delicious seafood and stunning views of the bay. The restaurant serves a variety of freshly caught seafood, including crab, juicy oysters, and squid. A set menu includes complimentary appetizers and your choice of seafood. Beyond seafood, the restaurant also offers meat and vegetarian options. For the best experience, we recommend dining outdoors to enjoy the view.
3. **Cuca Restaurant**: Known for its tapas, Cuca also excels in seafood dishes. Located near Kayumanis Jimbaran Inn, the restaurant offers three dining areas: a food bar, a traditional dining space, and a garden lounge. The chefs select the freshest catch daily, serving perfectly grilled prawns, smoked squid, and seafood rice, which has become a staple. We also love the open kitchen, where you can watch your meal being prepared from scratch!
4. **Sundara Bali**: While not exclusively a seafood restaurant, Sundara Bali ranks among Jimbaran's best. Located at the Four Seasons Resort, it combines five-star dining with a relaxed beach club vibe. The open kitchen and semi-outdoor dining area overlook the resort's famous infinity pool.
5. **Jimbaran Beach Club**: For fresh seafood and stunning bay views, we highly recommend dining at Jimbaran Beach Club at Kupu Kupu Jimbaran Suites. Situated right on Jimbaran Bay Beach, it offers modern dining areas and sunset lounges where you can savor delicious seafood.
6. **Nyoman Café**: Known for its affordability and simplicity, Nyoman Café serves a variety of fresh seafood prepared to order. Choose from live lobster, prawns, crab, and squid, all grilled over hot charcoal. Must-try dishes include crab with sweet soy sauce, stewed fish, and fried squid.
7. **Lia Café**: If you love seafood, beautiful views, and budget-friendly options, Lia Cafe is for you! Located away from the tourist bus crowds, this humble restaurant may look unassuming, but its seafood will win you over.
8. **Akua Bali Restaurant**: Akua is not only one of Jimbaran Bay's finest seafood restaurants but also a lifestyle venue combining a bar, beach club, and restaurant. Inspired by Bali's turquoise waters, Akua offers Spanish-inspired tapas and fresh seafood in a spacious dining area with bay views.
9. **Made Bagus Café Jimbaran**: A popular spot on Jimbaran Beach, this restaurant offers clear pricing and different combo meals, with only a 10% tax added. The food is tasty and offers good value for money.
10. **Wr Mang Ayu ex Waroenk Djadoel**: If you think Jimbaran's sunset seafood is overpriced and underwhelming, Wr Mang Ayu will change your mind. This small eatery offers affordable and delicious meals, making it a perfect spot to enjoy sunset dining.
11. **Pandan Sari Café**: Highly rated on Klook, Pandan Sari Café serves fresh crab, prawns, clams, and grilled fish with unique Indonesian spices. It's an excellent choice for families or couples looking for a relaxed beachside dining experience.

It's worth noting that while most restaurants in Jimbaran Beach are honest, there are a few unscrupulous vendors who may overcharge tourists, especially when they hear English being spoken. Here are some tips to avoid being scammed:

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_87c31a5431c311fed250d5a9e0ebe4a1.jpg)

{{block:klook}}

1. **Research Restaurants**: Before heading to Jimbaran Beach, do some online research to find well-reviewed and reputable restaurants. Reading reviews and recommendations can help you identify trustworthy spots.
2. **Ask for Prices**: Once seated, inquire about the price range or ask to see the menu to avoid unpleasant surprises.
3. **Compare Prices**: If prices seem too high, compare them with other restaurants. Significant discrepancies may indicate overcharging. A reasonable per-person cost should be around IDR 150,000–300,000 (USD$10–20).
4. **Use Translation Tools**: If you're unsure about pricing, use translation apps or tools to ensure clear communication about prices and orders.
5. **Assess On-Site**: Before choosing a restaurant, assess the location. If prices are noticeably higher than similar spots, proceed with caution.
