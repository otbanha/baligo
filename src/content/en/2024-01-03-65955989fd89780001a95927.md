From February 14, 2024, Bali will introduce a new tourism policy, the 'International Tourist Levy,' applicable to all international visitors to Bali, regardless of age. Each person will be required to pay IDR 150,000, equivalent to approximately USD$10. This policy aims to address Bali's traffic congestion and waste management issues.

## Do I Need to Pay the Tourist Tax Again When Island Hopping?

Some news websites suggest that if you island hop (Bali -> other islands not under Bali Province jurisdiction -> return to Bali), you need to pay the tourist tax again upon returning to Bali. However, rest assured that even if your island hopping destinations are outside Bali Province's jurisdiction, such as the Gili Islands, Lombok, or Java, you '**do not need**' to pay the tourist tax again when returning to Bali! This is based on our verification of the official FAQ (<https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/faq>):

## Visa Categories Exempt from Tourist Tax

There are seven visa categories exempt from this tax, including diplomatic, official, transport crew, student visas, and holders of temporary or permanent residence permits (KITAS and KITAP). Applications for tax exemption must be submitted **at least one month in advance**, and local tourism authorities will approve within a maximum of five working days.

Exempt visa holders must **submit their application in advance** and meet the requirements of the Love Bali system. If approved, the Love Bali system will send a notification and proof of tax exemption approval (in the form of a QR code) to the foreign visitor.

## Tourist Tax Payment Methods and Inspection Information

- Taxes can be paid upon arrival at Ngurah Rai International Airport or online via the LoveBali official website.
- Some hotels and resorts can also assist guests with the payment process.
- After payment, a QR code certificate will be sent, which tourists must keep for inspection purposes.

**Recent Inspection Update:** On December 4th, Bali Tourism Bureau officials conducted on-site inspections at **Tanah Lot Temple**. According to data, **60% of international tourists** had not paid the tourist tax. To strengthen management, the Tourism Bureau has been conducting regular inspections at popular tourist spots since May 2024, mainly at the entrances and exits. Tourists who have not paid the tax will need to pay on-site using a credit card, **cash payments are not accepted**.

## **Tips for Paying the Tourist Tax:**

1. It is recommended to use a desktop computer for payment to avoid system issues when using a mobile phone.
2. Ensure that your browser does not have ad-blocking or VPN functions enabled, otherwise the website may not function properly.
3. You can fill in the details of up to 10 people at once for payment. Required information includes passport name, passport number, email address, and arrival date.
4. Island hopping does not require additional payment!

## Why Implement the Tourist Tax?

The outline of Bali's implementation of the tourist tax is as follows:

1. Bali is tired of tourists' inappropriate behavior and hopes to protect its reputation.
2. Governor Wayan Koster ordered the attachment of a code of conduct to tourists' passports and has deported at least 136 foreigners for inappropriate behavior.
3. Bali will impose a tourist tax of approximately USD$10 per person on all international tourists, to be used for cultural and environmental protection.
4. Tourism has rebounded since the pandemic, but tourists' inappropriate behavior has become a problem, including violating social taboos, disputes with local authorities, and traffic violations.
5. Foreigners' lack of respect for local customs has caused dissatisfaction, and the local government is displeased.
6. The tourist tax is not the first of its kind, with similar precedents elsewhere, but Bali's governor believes it will help improve the environment and enhance the quality of tourism.
