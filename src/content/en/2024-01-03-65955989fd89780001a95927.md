Since February 14, 2024, Bali has implemented a new tourism policy called the 'International Tourist Levy,' applicable to all international visitors to Bali, regardless of age. Each person is required to pay IDR 150,000, equivalent to approximately USD$10. This policy aims to address traffic congestion and waste issues in Bali.

### Starting from February 14, 2024, traveling to Bali will cost an additional USD$10

1. **Effective Date**: Starting from February 14, 2024.
2. **Applicable Persons**: Foreign tourists; including those arriving in Bali via direct flights or entering Bali through other Indonesian cities.
3. **Payment**: It is recommended that tourists pay online via the [Love Bali official website](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/home) or by downloading the 'Love Bali' app before arriving in Bali. The Levy voucher will be scanned and verified at checkpoints.
4. **On-site Payment**: The tax can also be paid at Bali International Airport's arrival terminal and Benoa Port.

The local tourism bureau stated that the process of paying the tourism tax will not exceed 23 seconds and strongly recommends that tourists pay in advance.

## Do I need to pay again after island hopping?

Some news websites suggest that if you go island hopping (Bali ---> other islands not under Bali Province jurisdiction ---> return to Bali), you need to pay the tourism tax again upon returning to Bali. However, rest assured, even if your island hopping destinations are not under Bali Province jurisdiction, such as the Gili Islands, Lombok, or Java, you '**do not need**' to pay the tourism tax again when returning to Bali! This is according to the official website FAQ (<https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/faq>):

## Inspection Checkpoint

Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) has officially established a 'Levy Vouchers Secondary Inspection Lane' **between the arrival hall exit and the pickup area**. This means that even after clearing customs and collecting your luggage, before stepping out of the airport to meet your driver, inspectors will stop all passengers and require them to scan a QR code.

- **On-site Payment**: If you cannot present the voucher, you must pay immediately via QRIS scan, credit card, or debit card.
- **Recommendation**: Be sure to save a screenshot of the voucher on your phone to avoid issues with unstable airport Wi-Fi.

## Visa Exemptions from Tourism Tax

There are seven visa categories exempt from this tax, including diplomatic, official, transportation crew, student visas, and holders of temporary or permanent residence permits (KITAS and KITAP). Applications for tax exemption must be submitted **at least one month in advance**, and local tourism authorities will approve within a maximum of five working days.

Exempt visa holders must **submit** their application in advance and meet the requirements of the Love Bali system. If approved, the Love Bali system will send a notification and proof of tax exemption approval (in the form of a QR code) to the foreign visitor.

## Tourism Tax Payment Methods and Inspection Information:

- The tourism tax can be paid upon arrival at Ngurah Rai International Airport or online via the LoveBali official website.
- Some hotels and resorts can also assist guests with the payment process.
- After payment is completed, a QR code voucher will be sent, which tourists should keep for inspection purposes.

Bali Tourism officials have begun on-site inspections at popular tourist spots, primarily at entry and exit points. Tourists who haven't paid the tax will need to settle it on-site using a credit card — **cash payments are not accepted**.

## **Tips for Paying the Tourism Tax:**

1. It's recommended to use a desktop computer for payment to avoid system issues on mobile devices.
2. Ensure your browser doesn't have ad blockers or VPN enabled, as these may prevent the website from functioning properly.
3. You can process payments for up to 10 people at once. Required information includes passport name, passport number, email address, and arrival date.
4. Island hopping doesn't require additional payments!

## Why Implement a Tourism Tax?

Here's an overview of Bali's tourism tax implementation:

1. Bali is tired of tourists' inappropriate behavior and aims to protect its reputation.
2. Governor Wayan Koster has ordered behavior guidelines to be attached to tourists' passports and has expelled at least 136 foreigners for misconduct.
3. Bali will charge approximately USD$10 per international tourist for cultural and environmental conservation.
4. While tourism has rebounded post-pandemic, tourists' inappropriate behavior has become problematic, including violating social taboos, disputes with local authorities, and traffic violations.
5. Foreigners' lack of respect for local customs has caused dissatisfaction, displeasing local authorities.
6. The tourism tax isn't a new concept, with similar precedents elsewhere, but Bali's governor believes it will help improve the environment and enhance tourism quality.
