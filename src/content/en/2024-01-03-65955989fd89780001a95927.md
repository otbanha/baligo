Starting February 14, 2024, Bali will implement a new tourism policy called the 'International Tourist Levy,' which applies to all international visitors to Bali, regardless of age. Each person will be required to pay IDR 150,000, equivalent to approximately USD$10. This policy aims to address Bali's issues with traffic congestion and waste management.

Starting from February 14, 2024, travelers to Bali will need to pay an additional fee equivalent to USD$10.

1. Date: Starting from February 14, 2024
2. Applicable to: Foreign tourists, including those arriving in Bali via direct flights or entering Bali through other cities in Indonesia.
3. Payment: It is recommended that travelers make their payment online before arriving in Bali through the <https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/home> website or by downloading the 'Love Bali' app. The tax payment proof (Levy voucher) will be scanned and verified at border checkpoints. (Payment methods are detailed in the screenshots below)
4. The tax can also be paid at the international arrival terminal in Bali and at Benoa Port for cruise arrivals.

The local tourism bureau stated that the process of paying the tourism tax takes no more than 23 seconds, and they recommend that visitors pay in advance.

Do I Have to Pay Again for Island Hopping?

Some news websites suggest that if you island-hop (Bali ---> other islands not under Bali Province jurisdiction ---> back to Bali), you'll need to pay the tourism tax again upon returning to Bali. However, rest assured that even if your island-hopping destinations are outside Bali Province's jurisdiction, such as the Gili Islands, Lombok, or Java, you "**do not need**" to pay the tourism tax again when returning to Bali! This is according to the official FAQ on the website (<https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/faq>).

## Visa Exempt from Tourism Tax

![沒有線上繳交觀光税，下飛機後去這個櫃檯補繳](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_afc7d27a7b348f233f38f42bcaf925a0.jpg)

There are seven visa categories exempt from paying this tax, including diplomatic, official, transportation crew, student visas, and holders of temporary or permanent residence permits (KITAS and KITAP). Applications for tax exemption must be submitted at least one month in advance, and local tourism authorities will approve them within a maximum of five working days.

Visa-exempt holders must **submit in advance** and meet the requirements of the Love Bali system. If approved, the Love Bali system will send foreign tourists a notification and proof of tax-free approval (in the form of a QR code).
## Tourist Tax Payment Methods and Inspection Information:

- Tourist taxes can be paid upon arrival at Ngurah Rai International Airport or online via the LoveBali official website.
- Some hotels and resorts may also assist guests with the payment process.
- After payment is completed, a QR code voucher will be issued and sent to visitors, which must be kept for inspection purposes.

**Recent Inspection Update:** On December 4th, Bali tourism officials conducted on-site inspections at **Tanah Lot Temple**. According to the data, **60% of international tourists** had not paid the tourism tax. To strengthen management, the tourism bureau has been conducting regular inspections at popular tourist spots since May 2024, primarily focusing on the entrances and exits of these attractions. Tourists who have not paid the tax will be required to settle it on-site using a credit card, **cash payments are not accepted**.

## **A Quick Reminder About Tourist Tax:**

1. It is recommended to use a desktop computer for payment to avoid system issues that may occur during mobile transactions.
2. Ensure that your browser does not have ad-blocking or VPN features enabled, as these may prevent the website from functioning properly.
3. You can submit information for up to 10 people in a single payment. Required details include passport name, passport number, email address, and entry date.
4. No additional payment is needed for island hopping!

## Why Implement a Tourism Tax?

Here are the key points regarding Bali's implementation of the tourism tax:

1. Bali is tired of tourists' inappropriate behavior and aims to protect its reputation.
2. Governor Wayan Koster has ordered behavior guidelines to be attached to tourists' passports and has expelled at least 136 foreigners for misconduct.
3. Bali will impose a tourism tax of approximately USD$10 per person on all international visitors, to be used for cultural and environmental conservation.
4. While tourism has rebounded post-pandemic, tourists' inappropriate behavior has become an issue, including violating social taboos, disputes with local authorities, and traffic violations.
5. The lack of respect for local customs by outsiders has sparked dissatisfaction, and the local government is displeased with this.
6. The tourism tax is not a new concept, with similar precedents elsewhere, but Bali's governor believes it will help improve the environment and enhance the quality of tourism.
