## Monkey Forest

Located in the heart of Ubud's ancient town, the Monkey Forest is a popular attraction that combines wild monkey populations with Hindu temple culture. After navigating through a maze of intricate alleyways, visitors are greeted by a stretch of lush green canopy formed by towering ancient trees, creating a natural shaded corridor flanked by various tropical plants, crafting an independent oasis of greenery. Walking down the entrance steps, one can immediately spot several wild monkeys frolicking and playing in the trees.

These monkeys aren't particularly large, sporting long black or brown fur. Their agility and unpredictable movements are truly remarkable, often causing visitors to pause and watch in fascination. The most delightful sight is watching mother monkeys with their babies - the little ones either clinging to their mothers' backs or peeking out from underneath, their playful antics bringing smiles to everyone's faces. Occasionally, you might witness a sudden tussle among the monkeys in the trees, scrambling and wrestling - likely competing for food.

As they walked, they witnessed firsthand the intelligence and cunning of the monkey troop. Suddenly, a black monkey leaped down from a tree, landing precisely on a tourist. In a swift motion, it snatched a pair of valuable earrings from her ear and fled into the distance. This incident clearly shows that these monkeys have a keen 'interest' in shiny jewelry, reminding visitors to remain vigilant at all times.

Though small in size, the Monkey Forest encapsulates Ubud's unique blend of nature and religious culture, making it a stop well worth savoring in detail.

## Ubud Traditional Market & New Market

After escaping the hustle and bustle, they soon arrived at Ubud Traditional Market on foot. This is a time-honored traditional market primarily selling local fresh produce and various daily necessities. At the entrance, visitors are greeted by a long walkway lined with vendors selling clothing, handicrafts, and other souvenir items. Venturing deeper into the market, one finds numerous food stalls and vegetable vendors offering fresh ingredients like fruits, vegetables, seafood, and meats. However, due to the market's expansive and aging structure, the scorching sun beats down directly, creating a somewhat stifling atmosphere. To address this, a new market was later built nearby to provide tourists with a more comfortable shopping environment. This new market is fully enclosed, featuring bright and well-ventilated spaces, with vendors organized into themed sections such as clothing, home decor, and handicrafts. Shopping here offers a bright and pleasant atmosphere, adding an extra layer of comfort to the experience.

At the new market, they discovered several practical and interesting handicrafts, such as intricately woven fruit baskets featuring various patterns that were quite unique. They also came across numerous beautiful surf-themed wood carvings, coconut carvings, and dyed fabric wall hangings, with styles too numerous to count, exuding an exotic charm. Although the prices were slightly higher than those at the older market outside, they felt that the exquisite craftsmanship made them well worth purchasing.

## Serayu Pottery Art Studio

Leaving the bustling market behind, they headed to another renowned attraction in Ubud - Serayu Pottery. This charming spot specializes in various DIY experiences like pottery making and ceramic painting, offering a serene and delightful atmosphere.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b41e6268aabe4e4c156ff8807840bee3.jpg)

Upon entering the compound, one is immediately greeted by lush greenery and vibrant pottery jars, creating a natural ambiance reminiscent of a secluded paradise. Next to the classroom stands an exhibition hall filled with exquisite ceramic pieces painted by skilled students, serving as inspiring examples. Before the class began, the staff provided a detailed introduction to the properties of clay and key considerations in pottery making, emphasizing the importance of care from kneading to shaping. Then came the hands-on session, where participants selected simple pottery jars as their canvases. The instructor demonstrated various decorative techniques, such as glaze dripping, color blending, and pattern outlining, making the process quite fascinating. During the painting session, the host also shared the rich history and artistic essence of pottery, enriching everyone's understanding. After the class concluded, the participants' creations were sent for high-temperature firing, transforming them into enduring works of art.

The staff will mail the finished pottery pieces a few weeks later, allowing you to relive the ceramic art experience time and again.

## Alas Harum Attraction

The highlight of this trip was visiting the Alas Harum theme park. This unique recreational area combines various traditional Balinese architectural styles, tropical adventure facilities, and coffee-making experiences. At the entrance, visitors are greeted by a grand Balinese-style wooden gate adorned with exquisite carvings of shrines and statues, evoking the majestic aura of a royal palace. Beyond the gate lies a serene garden landscape composed of Balinese architectural elements, exuding a relaxed and leisurely atmosphere. The park is divided into several themed areas:

1. Experience Zone: This area features various parent-child interactive facilities, with the most eye-catching being the giant outdoor swing, claimed to be the largest in the world, standing nearly 9 meters tall. In addition, there are thrilling attractions like high-altitude zip lines and rope courses, making it an exciting and fun-filled spot.

2. Pool Area: Featuring a large semi-open recreational pool with lounge chairs along the edge, perfect for cooling off and having fun in the water. Adjacent to the pool is a spacious swim-up bar serving a variety of drinks and tropical fruits.

3. Exhibition Halls and Workshops: One area is dedicated to showcasing local coffee craftsmanship and origins, where visitors can sample authentic civet coffee. Staff members vividly explain the entire production process from harvesting and roasting to hand-brewing, making it both educational and entertaining. Another section features artisan workshops offering hands-on experiences in bamboo weaving, wood carving, and painting.

4. Performance Areas: The park features various themed performance zones, showcasing traditional art dances and percussion music among others. Daily performance schedules are available for visitors to enjoy.

Alas Harum seamlessly blends traditional culture with modern entertainment, offering a diverse and enriching experience that will leave you wanting to stay longer.
