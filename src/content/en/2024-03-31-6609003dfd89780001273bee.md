## Monkey Forest

Located in the heart of Ubud's old town, the Monkey Forest is a popular attraction that combines wild monkeys with Hindu temple culture. After navigating through a maze of winding alleys, visitors are greeted by a lush green canopy formed by towering ancient trees, creating a natural shaded corridor flanked by various tropical plants, crafting an independent oasis of tranquility. Walking down the entrance steps, one immediately spots several groups of wild monkeys frolicking in the trees.

These monkeys, not particularly large, are covered in black or brown fur and are incredibly agile and unpredictable, making them a constant source of fascination. The most delightful sight is watching mother monkeys with their babies, the little ones either clinging to their mothers' backs or peeking out from underneath, their playful antics bringing smiles to visitors' faces. Occasionally, the monkeys can be seen tumbling over each other in the branches, likely scrambling for food.

During their visit, they also witnessed the intelligence and cunning of the monkeys. A black monkey suddenly leaped from a tree, landing precisely on a tourist, swiftly snatching a pair of valuable earrings from her ears before darting away. This incident highlights the monkeys' 'interest' in shiny jewelry and serves as a reminder for visitors to remain vigilant.

Beyond the charming monkeys, the forest also houses a Hindu temple, Pura Dalem Agung Padangtegal, featuring various hand-carved stone statues and sculptures, adding a unique cultural element. Further along the path, visitors can find a small pond and fountain, with an old banyan tree standing nearby, creating a serene atmosphere. Though small, the Monkey Forest encapsulates Ubud's unique blend of nature and religious culture, making it a worthwhile stop to savor.

## Ubud Traditional & New Market

After leaving the serene Monkey Forest, they quickly walked to the Ubud Traditional Market. This long-standing market primarily sells local fresh produce and various daily necessities. The entrance features a long corridor lined with stalls selling clothing, crafts, and other souvenirs. Inside, numerous food stalls and vendors offer fresh produce like fruits, seafood, and meat. However, the market's open and somewhat dilapidated structure exposes visitors to the harsh sun, making it feel quite hot and stuffy. To meet tourists' needs for a more comfortable shopping environment, a new market was built nearby. This enclosed market is spacious and well-ventilated, with vendors organized into themed sections like clothing, home decor, and handicrafts. Shopping here is a more pleasant and comfortable experience.

In the new market, they discovered several practical and interesting handicrafts, such as intricately woven fruit baskets with various patterns, and beautiful surf-themed wood carvings, coconut carvings, and dyed fabric hangings, all exuding an exotic charm. Although prices were slightly higher than in the old market, they felt the superior craftsmanship made the items worth purchasing.

## Serayu Pottery Workshop

Leaving the bustling market behind, they headed to another renowned Ubud attraction, Serayu Pottery. This spot offers various DIY experiences like pottery making and painting, set in a serene and pleasant environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b41e6268aabe4e4c156ff8807840bee3.jpg)

Upon entering the grounds, visitors are greeted by lush greenery and vibrant pottery, creating a serene, almost otherworldly atmosphere. Next to the classroom stands an exhibition hall showcasing beautifully painted ceramic pieces crafted by students, serving as inspiring examples. Before the class begins, instructors provide a detailed introduction to the properties of clay and essential techniques, emphasizing the care needed from kneading to shaping. Then, it's time to get hands-on: participants select a simple pottery piece as their canvas, while the teacher demonstrates various decorating techniques like dripping glaze, blending colors, and outlining patterns—a truly engaging process. Throughout the session, the host shares insights into the rich history and artistic significance of pottery, enriching the experience. After the class, the completed pieces are fired at high temperatures to transform them into lasting works of art.

The staff will mail the finished pieces back in a few weeks, allowing participants to relive the pottery-making experience time and again.

## Alas Harum Attraction

The highlight of this journey was visiting Alas Harum, a multifaceted theme park that combines traditional Balinese architecture, tropical recreational facilities, and coffee-making experiences—a rare gem of an attraction. At the entrance, visitors are welcomed by a grand Balinese-style wooden gate adorned with intricately carved shrines and statues, evoking the grandeur of a royal palace. Beyond the gate lies a garden landscape filled with Balinese architectural elements, exuding a tranquil and leisurely vibe. The park is divided into several themed areas:

1. Experience Zone: This area features interactive family-friendly facilities, with the standout attraction being the world's largest outdoor swing, towering at nearly 9 meters. Other thrilling activities include zip-lining and rope courses, offering plenty of excitement.

2. Pool Zone: A large semi-open pool area equipped with lounge chairs, perfect for cooling off and relaxing. Adjacent to the pool is a spacious water bar serving a variety of drinks and tropical fruits.

3. Exhibition Halls and Workshops: One section showcases local coffee craftsmanship and origins, where visitors can sample authentic civet coffee. Staff explain the entire process from harvesting to roasting and brewing, making it both informative and engaging. Another area hosts artisan workshops for activities like bamboo weaving, wood carving, and painting.

4. Performance Zone: The park also features themed performance areas, offering daily shows such as traditional art dances and percussion performances.

Alas Harum seamlessly blends traditional culture with modern entertainment, offering a diverse and enriching experience that leaves visitors captivated.

## Leisure Time

After four days of sightseeing, they often schedule some leisure time to relax.
