## Monkey Forest

Located in the heart of Ubud, the Monkey Forest is a popular attraction that combines wild monkey colonies with Hindu temple culture. After navigating through a maze of intricate alleyways, visitors are greeted by a lush green canopy formed by towering trees, creating a natural shaded corridor flanked by various tropical plants, crafting an independent oasis of greenery. Walking down the entrance steps, one can immediately spot several wild monkey colonies frolicking in the trees.

These monkeys are not particularly large, with black or brown long fur, and are extremely agile and unpredictable, making them fascinating to watch. The most amusing sight is seeing mother monkeys with their babies, either clinging to their backs or peeking out from beneath them, their playful antics bringing smiles to visitors' faces. Occasionally, the monkeys engage in playful tussles on the branches, likely scrambling for food.

During their visit, they witnessed the intelligence and cunning of the monkeys. A black monkey suddenly leaped from a tree, accurately landing on a tourist and swiftly snatching a pair of valuable earrings from her ear before fleeing. This incident highlights the monkeys' 'interest' in shiny jewelry, reminding visitors to remain vigilant.

Apart from the adorable monkeys, the forest also houses a Hindu temple, the Pura Dalem Agung Padangtegal, featuring various hand-carved stone statues and sculptures. Following the path deeper into the forest, visitors can find a small pond and fountain, with an old banyan tree standing by the water's edge, creating a serene atmosphere. Despite its small size, the Monkey Forest encapsulates Ubud's unique natural and religious culture, making it a worthwhile stop.

## Ubud Traditional & New Market

After leaving the tranquil Monkey Forest, they quickly walked to the Ubud Traditional Market. This long-standing market primarily sells local fresh produce and daily necessities. Upon entering, visitors are greeted by a long pathway lined with stalls selling clothing, crafts, and souvenirs. Inside the market, various food stalls and vendors offer fresh ingredients like vegetables, fruits, seafood, and meat. However, the market's open and aged structure exposes visitors to the harsh sun, making it somewhat stifling. To meet the needs of tourists for a better shopping environment, a new market was built nearby. This new market is fully enclosed, spacious, and well-ventilated, with vendors organized into themed sections like clothing, home decor, and handicrafts. Shopping here is a bright and pleasant experience, offering more comfort.

In the new market, they discovered several practical and interesting handicrafts, such as woven fruit baskets with intricate patterns, and many beautiful surf-themed wood carvings, coconut carvings, and dyed fabric hangings, showcasing a variety of exotic styles. Although prices are slightly higher than in the old market, they found the craftsmanship to be exquisite, making them worth the purchase.

## Serayu Pottery

Leaving the bustling market behind, they headed to another famous Ubud attraction, Serayu Pottery, which offers various DIY experiences like pottery making and painting in a serene and pleasant environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b41e6268aabe4e4c156ff8807840bee3.jpg)

As soon as I stepped into the park, I was greeted by lush trees with intricate roots and vibrant pottery jars, creating a natural atmosphere reminiscent of a hidden paradise. Next to the classroom, there was an exhibition hall filled with exquisite ceramic pieces painted by skilled students, serving as reference examples. Before the class began, the staff gave a detailed introduction about the properties of clay and the precautions to take during the crafting process, emphasizing the care needed from kneading to shaping. Then, it was time to get hands-on. They first selected a simple pottery jar as their canvas, while the teacher demonstrated various decorating techniques such as dripping glaze, blending colors, and outlining patterns, which was quite fascinating. During the painting process, the host also shared the long history and artistic essence of pottery, enriching their knowledge. After the class, the completed pieces needed to be fired at high temperatures to become permanent works of art.

The staff will mail the finished pieces back in a few weeks, allowing participants to relive the pottery experience.

## Alas Harum Attraction

The highlight of this trip was visiting the Alas Harum theme park. This place combines various traditional Balinese architectural styles, tropical recreational facilities, and coffee-making experiences, making it a rare and diverse amusement park. At the entrance, I was greeted by a grand wooden gate adorned with intricate carvings and majestic statues, giving the impression of entering a royal palace. Beyond the gate, I was met with a garden landscape composed of Balinese architectural styles, exuding a serene and leisurely atmosphere. The park is divided into several themed areas:

1. Experience Zone: This area features various interactive facilities for families, with the most eye-catching being the giant outdoor swing, nearly 9 meters high, claimed to be the largest in the world. Additionally, there are thrilling attractions like zip lines and rope courses, offering plenty of excitement.

2. Pool Area: This consists of a large semi-open swimming pool with lounge chairs by the side, perfect for cooling off. There's also a large water bar nearby, serving a variety of drinks and tropical fruits.

3. Exhibition Hall and Workshop: One area showcases local coffee craftsmanship and origins, where visitors can taste authentic civet coffee. Staff explain the entire process from harvesting to roasting and brewing, making it both informative and engaging. Another area is a workshop offering hands-on experiences in bamboo weaving, wood carving, and painting.

4. Performance Area: The park also has different themed performance zones, featuring traditional art dances and percussion music, with varying showtimes each day for visitors to enjoy.

Alas Harum perfectly blends traditional culture with modern entertainment, offering a rich and diverse experience that leaves visitors wanting to stay longer.
