## Bali Bird Park Overview: A Tropical Paradise of Birds and Blooms

**Bottom line upfront:** Located in Gianyar Regency, Bali Bird Park spans about **2 hectares** and houses over **1,300 birds from 250 species** (including rare Indonesian, African, South American, and Australian varieties). Open since the 1990s, it remains a top nature-education attraction, perfect for a half-day family eco-interactive outing.

Nestled in central Bali's Gianyar Regency, **Bali Bird Park** has been one of the island's most beloved nature-education destinations since its opening in the 1990s. The park covers about **2 hectares** of lush tropical gardens and water features, with carefully designed habitat zones that recreate the natural environments of birds from around the world.

![峇里島鳥園簡介：鳥語花香的熱帶天堂](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_9d386245f4b2e5ff72a2aa378b5a1a8a.jpg)

Today, the park is home to over **1,300 birds representing more than 250 species**, including rare Indonesian, African, South American, and Australian varieties—making it an ideal spot for birdwatching, photography, and educational fun.

## Location and How to Get There

**Official website:** [www.balibirdpark.com](https://www.balibirdpark.com/)

**Address:** Jl. Serma Cok Ngurah Gambir, Singapadu, Sukawati, Gianyar, Bali 80582, Indonesia

**Distance guide:**

- From Ubud: about 30 minutes by car
- From Kuta, Seminyak: about 60 minutes

**Transportation Tips:**

- 🚗 **Ride-hailing apps:** Use Grab or Gojek for a quick and convenient ride.
- 🚙 **Full-day car charter:** If you also want to visit the Bali Zoo or Safari Park, consider hiring a private driver who will wait and shuttle you around.
- 🛵 **Rent a scooter:** Great for light travelers, but make sure you're familiar with Bali's roads and driving habits.

Tip: Since the park is in a suburban area, it can be hard to get a ride during peak afternoon hours. It's best to arrange a return time with your driver when you set off.

## Eight Themed Ecological Zones

Bali Bird Park is divided into eight main habitat zones, each designed to mimic the natural environment with climate-appropriate vegetation.

![八大主題生態區](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_aea4c2b914a235be16f57e09e219a799.jpg)

1. **Archipelago Zone**
   Showcasing birds from Sumatra, Kalimantan, and Java, including hornbills, golden-necked doves, and serpent eagles.
2. **Papua Rainforest Zone**
   One of the most enchanting areas, featuring the magnificent **Bird of Paradise**, cassowaries, and blue-crowned pigeons.
3. **Australian Outback Zone**
   Lively kookaburras and rainbow lorikeets fly freely here, making it a favorite interactive zone for children.
4. **African Savannah Zone**
   Displays African grey parrots, hornbills, and flamingos, along with African wetland ecosystems.
5. **South America Zone**
   A vibrant collection of macaws, toucans, and Amazon jungle birds.
6. **Bali Starling Sanctuary**
   A breeding and conservation base for Bali's national bird, the **Bali Mynah**.
7. **Bird Nursery**
   Showcasing the process of egg incubation and chick rearing, with a chance to witness baby birds hatching.
8. **Komodo Experience**
   One of the few places in Bali to observe **Komodo dragons (Varanus komodoensis)** up close.

## Must-See Star Birds

![必看明星鳥類](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_94078312d545c3cd0dd092a4eedc6eee.jpg)

Characterized by a snowy white crest and glossy black body, elegant and mysterious.

![必看明星鳥類](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f7fbcada14c7aafed7a094d8650699e5.jpg)

One of the largest owl species in the world, with prominent ear tufts that create a striking appearance.

- 🐦 **Bali Starling**

![必看明星鳥類](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_add94c706a8547ea70a305ae78e01f0a.jpg)

A symbolic bird of Bali, with white feathers and blue eye rings; extremely rare and listed as critically endangered.

## Education and Conservation Mission

![教育與保育使命](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d4c7abe03b460c0714238ead3e94ed93.jpg)

Bali Bird Park carries a vital mission in **environmental education and wildlife conservation**. The park houses research and breeding centers and collaborates with international conservation organizations to promote:

- Bali Starling captive breeding and release program
- Bird conservation education programs across Indonesia
- School group tours and eco-experience camps

The park also regularly hosts children's nature education activities, teaching kids how to care for animals and respect nature.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**1. Can I buy tickets on-site?**

Yes, but it's recommended to [book online for discounted rates](https://affiliate.klook.com/redirect?aid=116349&aff_adid=1148651&k_site=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.klook.com%2Fzh-TW%2Factivity%2F169-bali-bird-park-bali%2F%3Fspm%3DSearchResult.SearchResult_LIST%26clickId%3D8555ce542e) and fast-track entry.

**2. Can I take photos inside the park?**

Yes, you're free to take photos. If you use the park's professional photography service, an additional fee applies.

**3. How long does a typical visit take?**

About 3 to 4 hours. If you want to catch all the shows, it's best to set aside half a day.

**4. Can I bring my own food?**

No. The park has dining facilities with plenty of options.

**5. Can I really see the Bali myna?**

Yes. The park has a dedicated breeding area where visitors can observe this rare bird species up close.
