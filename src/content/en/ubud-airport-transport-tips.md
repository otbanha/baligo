Bali's traffic can be unpredictable, especially on the route from Ubud to the airport, where you might encounter religious ceremonies, sudden traffic jams, or road repairs, making this relatively short journey hard to estimate. To ensure you don't miss your flight, this article provides a complete itinerary and time management tips for traveling from Ubud to Bali International Airport, making your last day flexible and stress-free.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_db0768c5c03e273249cef15e302ea65c.jpg)

This article will guide you through every detail, from arranging transportation to managing your time, especially focusing on the route and flexible planning from Ubud to Bali International Airport, ensuring a smooth and worry-free final day of your trip.

***

## Why is the journey from Ubud to the airport hard to estimate?

The distance from Ubud to the airport is approximately 30 kilometers, and while the drive seems to take about an hour, the actual time can vary due to traffic conditions, roadwork, or religious ceremonies. Bali's roads are often affected by local religious activities, causing delays or detours. Choosing the right departure time and planning your route are key to a smooth trip.

***

## Preparations Before Departure

**Confirm your flight in advance**: Double-check your flight time the night before departure. It's generally recommended to arrive at the airport at least 3 hours before your flight to account for possible queues or unexpected delays.

**Pack your luggage**: Try to organize your luggage the day before to ensure you don't leave behind any important items in a rush. This also adds flexibility to your schedule.

**Arrange transportation**: To avoid delays in finding a ride, it's best to arrange your transportation in advance and confirm the pickup time. You can either arrange this through your hotel or use a reputable private car service to ensure a smooth journey.

***

## Route planning: Ubud → Kuta Area → Airport

Start your journey from Ubud to the Kuta area. Upon arrival, assess the remaining time. If time permits, you can engage in a brief sightseeing activity in Kuta.

***

## Avoiding Specific Areas in Kuta

Although Kuta is one of the most popular bustling areas for tourists, its main streets are mostly **one-way**, narrow, and heavily trafficked, making access quite inconvenient. The most common scenario is 'easy to get in, hard to get out.' Once you're caught in the traffic, it could significantly impact your schedule. It is advisable to avoid the areas marked in red boxes in the map during your tour in Kuta to prevent delays in reaching the airport.

## Importance of Allocating Time

It is crucial to allocate at least a 3-hour buffer for your journey. This buffer provides flexibility for your schedule and ensures you are not caught off guard in case of unexpected situations.

***

## Using Real-Time Traffic Apps

Many real-time traffic apps can help you understand current traffic conditions. Using these apps to adjust departure times and choose the best routes can save time more effectively. Even if you have a scheduled driver, it's recommended to keep the app open for reference.

But **never trust the estimated arrival time on traffic apps too much**! Because in Bali, there's a very high chance it won't be accurate!

## Conclusion: Tips to Avoid Missing Your Flight

The journey from Ubud to the airport is full of variables, but with proper preparation, flexible itinerary management, and constant use of real-time traffic apps, this trip can be smooth sailing. Pack your luggage early, leave enough time, and flexibly visit attractions, allowing you to enjoy the last moments of relaxation before ending your Bali trip.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **How long does it usually take from Ubud to the airport?**
   Normally about 1.5 to 2 hours, but it's recommended to allow 3 hours buffer to handle unexpected situations.
2. **Is it recommended to include sightseeing on the last day?**
   If time permits, consider brief sightseeing in the Kuta area, but always pay attention to traffic conditions.
3. **What real-time traffic apps are recommended?**
   Apps like Google Maps or Waze provide real-time traffic updates, helping to adjust your itinerary.
4. **Why arrange transportation in advance?**
   Bali's traffic is unstable, arranging in advance can avoid the risk of last-minute searches, ensuring no delays in departure time.
5. **How early should you arrive at the airport for international flights?**
   Generally, it's recommended to arrive at the airport 3 hours before departure for international flights to allow time for check-in. Although Bali's international airport is relatively small, more than two hours of preparation is still necessary!
6. **If the flight is at 15:30, what time should you leave Ubud?**
   Airport 2 hours + travel 3 hours, it's recommended to leave Ubud by 10:30 at the latest
7. **Any recommended areas for accommodation on the last day?**
   To avoid traffic variables on the last day, it's recommended to arrange your final Bali itinerary in Jimbaran, just south of the airport
