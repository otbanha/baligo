Bali's traffic can be unpredictable, especially on the route from Ubud to the airport, where you might encounter religious ceremonies, sudden traffic jams, or road repairs, making this relatively short journey hard to predict. To ensure you don't miss your flight, this article provides a complete itinerary and time management tips for traveling from Ubud to Bali International Airport, allowing your last day to be flexible and stress-free.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_db0768c5c03e273249cef15e302ea65c.jpg)

This article will guide you through every detail, from arranging transportation to managing your time, especially focusing on the route and flexible arrangements from Ubud to Bali International Airport, ensuring a smooth and worry-free final day of your trip.

***

## Why is the journey from Ubud to the airport hard to estimate?

The distance from Ubud to the airport is approximately 30 kilometers, and while the drive seems to take about an hour, the actual time can vary due to traffic conditions, road situations, or even religious ceremonies. Bali's roads are often affected by local religious activities, causing temporary stops or detours. Choosing the right departure time and planning your route become crucial in travel arrangements.

***

## Pre-Departure Preparation

**Confirm your flight in advance**: Double-check your flight time the night before departure. It's generally recommended to arrive at the airport at least 3 hours before your flight to account for possible queues or unexpected delays.

**Pack your luggage**: Try to organize your luggage the day before to ensure you don't leave behind important items in a rush. This also adds flexibility to your schedule.

**Arrange transportation**: To avoid delays in finding a ride, it's advisable to arrange transportation in advance and confirm the pickup time. You can choose to arrange this through your hotel or use a reputable private car service to ensure a smooth journey.

***

## Route planning: Ubud → Kuta Area → Airport

Start your journey from Ubud to the Kuta area. Upon arrival, assess your remaining time. If time permits, you can engage in a brief sightseeing activity in Kuta.

***

## Avoiding Specific Areas in Kuta

Although Kuta is one of the most popular bustling areas for tourists, its main streets are mostly **one-way** and narrow, with heavy traffic, making access quite inconvenient. The most common scenario is 'easy to get in, hard to get out.' Once caught in traffic, it could severely impact your schedule. It is advisable to avoid entering the area marked by the red box in the map during your tour of Kuta to prevent delays to the airport.

## Flexible Sightseeing Arrangements in Kuta

Near the Kuta area, you can selectively visit some attractions, shop for souvenirs, or dine at a restaurant. The area [south of the red box - north of the airport] is the most ideal location.

## The Importance of Allocating Time

It is crucial to allocate at least a 3-hour buffer for your journey. This buffer provides flexibility for your schedule and ensures you are not caught off guard in case of unexpected situations.

***

## Using Real-Time Traffic Apps

Many real-time traffic apps can help you understand current traffic conditions. Using these apps to adjust departure times and choose the best routes can save you time more effectively. Even if you have a booked driver, it's recommended to keep the app open as a reference.

However, **never trust the estimated arrival times on traffic apps too much**! Because in Bali, there's a high chance they'll be inaccurate!

## Conclusion: Tips to Avoid Missing Your Flight

The journey from Ubud to the airport is full of variables, but with proper preparation, flexible itinerary management, and constant use of real-time traffic apps, this trip can be smooth sailing. Pack early, allow plenty of time, and be flexible with sightseeing, letting you enjoy the last moments of relaxation before ending your Bali trip.

## Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

1. **How long does it take on average from Ubud to the airport?**
   Normally about 1.5 to 2 hours, but it's recommended to allow 3 hours buffer for unexpected delays.
2. **Is it recommended to include sightseeing on the last day?**
   If time permits, consider brief sightseeing in the Kuta area, but always keep an eye on traffic conditions.
3. **What real-time traffic apps are recommended?**
   Apps like Google Maps or Waze provide real-time traffic updates, helping adjust your itinerary.
4. **Why arrange transportation in advance?**
   Bali's traffic is unpredictable; arranging in advance avoids the risk of last-minute searches and ensures timely departure.
5. **How early should you arrive for international flights?**
   Typically, it's recommended to arrive 3 hours before departure for international flights to allow time for check-in. Although Bali's international airport is relatively small, more than two hours of preparation is still necessary!
6. **If the flight is at 3:30 PM, what time should you leave Ubud?**
   2 hours at the airport + 3 hours on the road, so it's recommended to leave Ubud by 10:30 AM at the latest.
7. **Any recommended areas for accommodation on the last day?**
   To avoid traffic variables on the last day, it's recommended to stay in Jimbaran, just south of the airport.
