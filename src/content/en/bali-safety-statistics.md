## **Bali’s Crime Rate Hits a Historic Low**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_26c345dd21b57218a13b8b59094e84af.jpg)

In 2024, Bali’s crime rate was just **0.07%**, marking the lowest record in all of Indonesia. Compared to **0.20%** in 2022, the data continues to decline, indicating that local security is improving year by year.

Since 2020, Bali has consistently maintained the lowest crime rate nationwide, becoming a 'safe haven' for both foreign tourists and local residents.

## **National Security Improvement Trends**

Since Indonesia’s crime victimization rate peaked at **1.11%** in 2018, the government has gradually improved security through the following measures:

- **Police Reform**: Increased transparency and efficiency in law enforcement
- **Digital Security Systems**: Faster handling of reports and crime prevention
- **Community Involvement**: Collaboration between local residents and police to maintain public order

## **Frequently Asked Questions FAQ**

### **1. Is Bali safe for foreign tourists?**

Yes. According to the 2024 Safety Index, Bali leads Indonesia in public safety, making it a secure destination for travelers.

### **2. Will recent protests in Bali affect tourism?**

No. The protests are localized events and have not impacted tourist facilities, ensuring travel safety remains unaffected.

### **3. How does Bali's crime rate compare to other regions?**

Bali's crime rate is only 0.07%, significantly lower than other provinces in Indonesia, making it the safest in the country.

### **4. Is it safe to travel at night in Bali?**

Traveling at night in major tourist areas like Ubud, Kuta, and Jimbaran is generally safe, but basic vigilance is still recommended.

### **5. How is the Indonesian government improving safety in Bali and nationwide?**

Through police reform, digitized security systems, and community participation, we collectively reduced crime rates.
