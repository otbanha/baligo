## **Is Bali Safe? Latest Public Safety Data Revealed**

**Bottom line**: Yes, it's safe. 2024 data shows Bali's safety index at **4.74/5**, making it Indonesia's safest province (national average is just 4.18), with a crime rate of only **0.07%** — a historic low (down from 0.20% in 2022). Recent demonstrations have not affected Bali, and tourist attractions and infrastructure continue to operate as usual.

Bali has once again confirmed its status as "Indonesia's safest province." In the 2024 safety index ranking, Bali topped the list with an impressive **4.74/5**, far exceeding the national average of **4.18**, demonstrating stable public security and a strong sense of social safety.

## **Bali's Crime Rate Hits a Historic Low**

![峇里島犯罪率創下歷史新低](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_26c345dd21b57218a13b8b59094e84af.jpg)

In 2024, Bali's crime rate stood at just **0.07%**, the lowest in all of Indonesia. Compared to **0.20%** in 2022, the continuous decline shows that local security is improving year by year.

Since 2020, Bali has consistently maintained the lowest crime rate in the country, becoming a "safe haven" for both international travelers and local residents.

At the same time, the number of crime victims nationwide has also been declining. In 2023, only **0.45% of the population** was affected, marking the lowest rate in the past decade.

***

## **Protests Do Not Affect Travel Safety**

Although there have been protests in some parts of Indonesia recently, they have not had a lasting impact on Bali. Tourist attractions and infrastructure continue to operate normally, ensuring visitor safety.

Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** stated: "Everything has returned to normal. Bali remains safe and welcoming, and travelers from around the world can enjoy its natural beauty, cultural experiences, and the hospitality of the locals with peace of mind."

***

## **Nationwide Improvement in Public Safety**

Since Indonesia's crime victimization rate peaked at **1.11% in 2018**, the government has gradually improved public safety through the following measures:

- **Police reform**: Enhancing transparency and efficiency in law enforcement
- **Digitalized public safety systems**: Faster case handling and crime prevention
- **Community involvement**: Local residents and police collaborate to maintain public order

By 2023, the victimization rate dropped to **0.45%**, demonstrating Indonesia's overall progress in public safety, with Bali being a prime example.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

![常見問題 FAQ](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_03141a4e39b1177082670e3423f755ac.png)

### **1. Is Bali safe for foreign tourists?**

Yes. According to the 2024 safety index, Bali leads all of Indonesia in public security, so travelers can visit with peace of mind.

### **2. Will recent demonstrations in Bali affect tourism?**

No. The demonstrations are localized incidents that have not impacted tourist facilities, and travel safety remains assured.

### **3. How does Bali's crime rate compare to other regions?**

Bali's crime rate is just 0.07%, far lower than other Indonesian provinces and the lowest in the country.

### **4. Is it safe to go out at night in Bali?**

In major tourist areas like Ubud, Kuta, and Jimbaran, it's generally safe to go out at night, though basic vigilance is still recommended.

### **5. How is the Indonesian government improving safety in Bali and across the country?

Through police reform, a digital public security system, and community participation, crime rates have been collectively reduced.
