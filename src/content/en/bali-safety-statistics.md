## **Is Bali Safe? Latest Safety Data Revealed**

Bali has once again confirmed its status as the 'Safest Province in Indonesia.' In the 2024 Safety Index evaluation, Bali scored a high **4.74/5**, far exceeding Indonesia's average of **4.18**, demonstrating a stable security environment and a high level of social safety.

Bali Ranks First in Safety Index

This index not only measures the effectiveness of law enforcement but also covers freedom of speech, the ability to prevent terrorist threats, and public trust in the police.

## **Bali's Crime Rate Hits a Historic Low**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_26c345dd21b57218a13b8b59094e84af.jpg)

In 2024, Bali's crime rate was only **0.07%**, setting the lowest record in all of Indonesia. Compared to **0.20%** in 2022, the data continues to decline, showing that local security is improving year by year.

Since 2020, Bali has consistently maintained the lowest crime rate nationwide, becoming a 'safe fortress' for both foreign tourists and local residents.

At the same time, the number of crime victims nationwide is also decreasing. In 2023, only **0.45% of the population** was affected, the lowest point in the past decade.

***

## **Protests Do Not Affect Tourism Safety**

Although there have been recent protest activities in some parts of Indonesia, they have not caused long-term impacts in Bali. Tourist attractions and infrastructure continue to operate normally, and the safety of visitors is assured.

Bali Governor **Wayan Koster** stated: "Everything has returned to normal. Bali remains safe and welcoming, allowing travelers from around the world to enjoy its natural beauty, cultural experiences, and the hospitality of the locals with peace of mind."

***

## **National Trend of Improved Public Safety**

Since Indonesia's crime victimization rate peaked at **1.11% in 2018**, the government has gradually improved public safety through the following measures:

- **Police Reform**: Increased transparency and efficiency in law enforcement
- **Digitalized Security Systems**: Faster handling of reports and crime prevention
- **Community Involvement**: Collaboration between local residents and police to maintain public order

As of 2023, the victimization rate has dropped to **0.45%**, demonstrating Indonesia's overall progress in public safety, with Bali being a prime example.

## **Frequently Asked Questions FAQ**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_03141a4e39b1177082670e3423f755ac.png)

### **1. Is Bali Safe for Foreign Tourists?**

Yes. According to the 2024 Safety Index, Bali leads all of Indonesia in terms of public security, making it a safe destination for travelers.

### **2. Will Recent Protests in Bali Affect Tourism?**

No. The protests are localized incidents and have not impacted tourist facilities, ensuring travel safety remains unaffected.

### **3. How Does Bali's Crime Rate Compare to Other Regions?**

Bali's crime rate is only 0.07%, significantly lower than other provinces in Indonesia, making it the safest in the country.

### **4. Is It Safe to Go Out at Night in Bali?**

It is generally safe to go out at night in major tourist areas like Ubud, Kuta, and Jimbaran, though basic vigilance is still recommended.

### **5. How Is the Indonesian Government Improving Security in Bali and Nationwide?**

Through police reform, digitalized security systems, and community participation, the government is working collectively to reduce crime rates.
