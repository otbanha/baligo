### 🌦️ Weather and Timing: A Survival Guide for the Transition Between Wet and Dry Seasons

These four months mark the critical transition from the wet to the dry season:

***

- **March (End of the Rainy Season):** Humidity can reach up to 80%. While rainfall begins to decrease, brief afternoon showers are still common.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_07409a02cddf0c7953f3992184b40d23.png)

- **April-May (Golden Transition Period):** The weather starts to dry up, with increasing chances of sunshine, making it the perfect time for water activities and photography.

- **June (Peak of the Dry Season):** Influenced by the cold air from the Southern Hemisphere, this month brings refreshing breezes, making it one of the most comfortable times of the year.

***

## 📅 Monthly Deep Dive: Festivals and Tips to Avoid Pitfalls

## **March: Between Extreme Bustle and Absolute Stillness**

__VID0__

- **Climate Overview:** March marks Bali's transition from the rainy season to the dry season. Humidity remains high (around 80%), and while there are still occasional showers, they tend to pass quickly. This is when the island's vegetation is at its greenest, and the rice fields are at their most picturesque.
- **What to Wear:** Lightweight, breathable cotton clothing, and carry a foldable umbrella or a light raincoat.

**Major Festivals:**

- **March 18: Ogoh-Ogoh Parade:** A island-wide exorcism ritual with a spectacular atmosphere.
- **March 19-20: Nyepi (Day of Silence):** Bali's New Year, where the entire island comes to a standstill for 24 hours, offering a profound experience of stillness.
- **Note:** Late March coincides with Indonesia's Eid al-Fitr holiday, a peak travel period to avoid! Visitors from across Indonesia flock to Bali, leading to crowded spots and skyrocketing accommodation prices. If you seek tranquility, steer clear of this week or stay in a remote resort.

**March Recommended Destinations:**

- **Denpasar:** Head to Puputan Square in the evening of March 18 to witness the grandest and most authentic Ogoh-Ogoh Parade. (Reference: [Nyepi Day Guide 2026](/blog/nyepi-day-bali-guide-2026/))
- **Uluwatu Cliff Resorts:** Stay at a resort with stunning ocean views during Nyepi (March 19-20). With minimal light pollution, it's the best spot on the island to observe the 'Nyepi Milky Way.' (Reference: [Uluwatu Accommodation Recommendations](https://s.id/uluwatu_Stay))

## **April: Awakening of Body and Soul**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cc16394692a6e8dd69ea0fab7f62c062.png)

- **Climate Overview:** Rainfall significantly decreases in April, with more sunny days. Water visibility improves, marking the start of the snorkeling and diving season. Temperatures range between 25°C - 32°C.
- Early April, during the Qingming Festival holiday, is a smart time to visit! Just after Indonesia's busiest period, Eid al-Fitr, when locals return to work and international tourists are fewer, Bali enjoys a brief but precious period of calm.
- **What to Wear:** Summer attire with a sun-protective jacket, and don't forget your swimwear.

**Major Festivals:**

- **April 15-19: Bali Spirit Festival:** An annual gathering for yoga enthusiasts worldwide, featuring yoga, dance, and music.
- **April 4: Saraswati Day:** Observe locals' reverence and rituals towards books and knowledge.

**April Recommended Destinations:**

- **Ubud:** This is the main venue for the **Bali Spirit Festival**. We recommend staying near the Monkey Forest at a yoga studio or attending workshops at **The Yoga Barn**. (For reference: [Top 10 Yoga Retreats in Ubud, Bali: Combining Yoga Courses with Spiritual Relaxation](/blog/ubud-yoga-retreats-bali/))
- **Tegallalang Rice Terraces:** With reduced rainfall in April, the sunlight filtering through the lush green terraces creates the most enchanting scenery. It's perfect for a morning stroll to avoid the heat. (For reference: [A Guide to Bali's Tegallalang and Jatiluwih Rice Terraces: Entry Fees, Best Times, and Seasons to Visit](/blog/bali-rice-terraces-comparison/))
- **Campuhan Ridge Walk:** The dry weather makes the trail less slippery, ideal for an evening walk. (For reference: [Discover the Hidden Paradise Within Walking Distance of Ubud: Campuhan Ridge Walk](/blog/ubud-campuhan-ridge-walk/))

## **May: A Golden Month for Foodies and Outdoor Enthusiasts**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b7405a76a96c0882e108c5117afe7a87.png)

- **Weather Overview:** May is highly recommended by many travel experts. The dry season officially arrives, humidity drops significantly, and the weather feels refreshingly comfortable. The sky is at its bluest, making it the perfect time for stunning photos.
- **Labor Day:** Take 4 days off (April 27-30) for a 9-day holiday exclusive to workers!
- **What to Wear:** Flaunt your summer style, but don’t forget sunglasses and high-SPF sunscreen.

{{video2}}

**Major Festivals:**

- **May 29-31: Ubud Food Festival:** A must-attend culinary feast for food lovers.
- **May 31: Waisak Day:** Experience the solemnity and tranquility of Buddhist culture.

**Recommended Destinations for May**

- **Ubud Center:** To fully enjoy the **Ubud Food Festival**, stay near the center for easy access to long lunches and chef workshops. (For reference: [Ubud Accommodation Recommendations](https://s.id/UbudResorts))
- **Water Activities:** Enjoy the best weather without the peak season crowds! (For reference: [Complete Guide to Water Activities in Bali: Beyond Snorkeling, 15 Unexpected Water Adventures](/blog/2026-bali-water-activities/))
- **Island Hopping/Snorkeling:** With calmer seas in May, it’s the best time to visit **Nusa Lembongan** or snorkel with manta rays at **Nusa Penida**. (For reference: [Nusa Lembongan Guide](/blog/nusa-lembongan-ceningan-guide/) / [Nusa Penida Guide](https://s.id/penida_guide))
- **Tulamben:** With the dry season here, underwater visibility is excellent, perfect for wreck diving. (For reference: [Complete Guide to Amed and Tulamben: Hidden Gems of Northeast Bali](/blog/amed-tulamben-diving-guide/))

## **June: The Peak of Culture and Beauty**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dd2c5771be5e78e79e23c43530cfe5c2.png)

- **Weather Overview:** June offers extremely stable weather with minimal rainfall. Cool breezes from the Southern Hemisphere bring refreshing winds, and mornings and evenings can feel slightly chilly, making it the most comfortable time for hiking (e.g., Mount Batur).
  **Quick Reference:**
- Dragon Boat Festival: Take 4 days off (June 15-18) for an epic 9-day early summer trip!
- **What to Wear:** Besides summer outfits, pack a light long-sleeve or windbreaker for cooler mornings and evenings.

**Major Festivals:**

- **Bali Arts Festival (PKB) throughout June:** Held in Denpasar, it's the ultimate venue to experience traditional dances, crafts, and parades.
- **Galungan & Kuningan Festival:** Streets are adorned with spectacular Penjor bamboo decorations, creating the most festive atmosphere.

**Recommended Travel Destinations for June**

- **Werdhi Budaya Art Centre in Denpasar:** Throughout June, the **Bali Arts Festival (PKB)** features top-notch traditional dance and theater performances every afternoon and evening. (Refer to [Denpasar Culture Guide](/blog/denpasar-culture-guide/))
- **Mount Batur:** June is the driest and coolest month of the year, perfect for hiking the volcano to catch the sunrise with a high chance of seeing the sea of clouds. (Refer to [Mount Batur and Lake Batur Guide: Sunrise Hike × Royal Cruise × Lakeside Café × Mountain Playground × Hot Springs](/blog/batur-lake-volcano-guide/))
- **Pandawa Beach:** Join the **Pandawa Beach Festival** and enjoy cultural parades and music performances by the azure sea. (How to explore the southern tip of Bali? Check out [Bukit Peninsula Adventure Guide: From Jimbaran Sunset to Uluwatu Cliff Temples: Bali's Hidden Paradise](/blog/bukit-peninsula-guide/))

## 💡 Editor's Smart Travel Notes

1. **Dressing Philosophy:** In March and April, pack raincoats and moisture-wicking clothes; in May and June, besides summer outfits, bring a light jacket as the cool night breeze might give you goosebumps. Check out [Do You Need a Jacket in Bali? Bali Packing Guide: 8 Essential Clothing Items for Your Trip](/blog/bali-packing-essentials/)
2. **Avoid Crowds:** Again, Eid al-Fitr in mid to late March is the **local peak season**, so if possible, plan your trip after April.
3. **Booking Tips:** For those interested in the Ubud Food Festival or Spirit Festival, Ubud city center gets very crowded during these events, so it's advisable to book accommodations three months in advance.
