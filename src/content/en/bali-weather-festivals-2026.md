### 🌦️ Climate and Routes: A 'Survival Guide' for the Transition from Rainy to Dry Season

These four months mark the critical transition from the rainy season to the dry season:

***

- **March (End of Rainy Season):** Humidity can reach up to 80%. While rainfall begins to decrease, brief afternoon showers are still common.
- **April-May (Golden Transition Period):** The weather starts to dry up, with increasing chances of sunshine, making it the perfect time for water activities and photography.
- **June (Peak of Dry Season):** Influenced by the cold air from the Southern Hemisphere, this month brings refreshing breezes, making it one of the most comfortable months of the year.

## 📅 Monthly Deep Dive: Festivals and Tips to Avoid Pitfalls

## **March: Between Extreme Bustle and Absolute Stillness**

***

## **3 月：在極致熱鬧與絕對靜止之間**

__VID0__

- **Climate Overview:** March marks Bali's transition from the rainy season to the dry season. Humidity remains high (around 80%), and while there are still occasional showers, they usually pass quickly. This is when the island's vegetation is at its greenest, and the rice fields are most picturesque.
- **What to Wear:** Light, breathable cotton clothing, and carry a foldable umbrella or lightweight raincoat.

**Major Festivals:**

- **March 18: Ogoh-Ogoh Parade:** A island-wide exorcism ritual with a spectacular scene.
- **March 19-20: Nyepi (Silent Day):** Balinese New Year, where the entire island comes to a standstill for 24 hours, offering a profound experience of "blank space."
- **Note:** Late March coincides with Indonesia's Eid al-Fitr holiday, a peak travel period to avoid! Visitors from across Indonesia flock to Bali, causing overcrowding and skyrocketing prices. If you seek tranquility, avoid this week or stay in a remote resort.

**Recommended Destinations for March**

- **Denpasar:** Head to Puputan Square in the evening of March 18 to witness the grandest and most authentic **Ogoh-Ogoh Parade**. (Reference: [Nyepi Day Guide 2026](/blog/2026-02-13-698f077efd89780001e7294f/))
- **Uluwatu Cliff Resorts:** Stay at a resort with stunning ocean views during **Nyepi (Silent Day)** on March 19-20. With minimal light pollution, it's the best spot on the island to observe the "Silent Day Galaxy." (Reference: [Uluwatu Accommodation Recommendations: 13 Private Villas with Ocean Views](https://s.id/uluwatu_Stay))

## **April: A Time for Awakening**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_cc16394692a6e8dd69ea0fab7f62c062.png)

- **Climate Overview:** Rainfall significantly decreases in April, and sunny days become more frequent. Water visibility improves, marking the start of the ideal season for snorkeling and diving. Temperatures range between 25°C - 32°C.
- The early April Qingming holiday is a smart time to visit! Just after Indonesia's busiest period, Eid al-Fitr, when locals return to work and international tourists are less overwhelming, Bali enjoys a brief but precious period of calm.
- **What to Wear:** Summer outfits with a sun-protective jacket, and don’t forget your swimwear.

**Major Festivals:**

- **April 15-19: Bali Spirit Festival:** An annual gathering for yoga enthusiasts worldwide, featuring yoga, dance, and music.
- **April 4: Saraswati Day:** Observe locals' reverence and rituals honoring books and knowledge.

**Recommended Destinations for April**

- **Ubud:** This is the main venue for the **Bali Spirit Festival**. It’s recommended to stay near the Sacred Monkey Forest for yoga classes or join workshops at **The Yoga Barn**. (Reference: [Top 10 Yoga Retreats in Ubud, Bali: Combining Yoga and Relaxation](/blog/2024-11-26-67451f5afd89780001f002f2/))
- **Tegallalang Rice Terraces:** With reduced rainfall in April, the sunlight filtering through the lush green terraces creates a mesmerizing scene. It’s perfect for a morning stroll to avoid the heat. (Reference: [A Guide to Bali’s Tegallalang and Jatiluwih Rice Terraces: Entry Fees, Best Times to Visit](/blog/2024-11-15-673167e4fd897800017c44a9/))
- **Campuhan Ridge Walk:** The dry weather makes the trail less slippery, ideal for an evening walk. (Reference: [A Hidden Paradise Within Walking Distance of Ubud: Campuhan Ridge Walk](/blog/2023-10-29-653dc7f3fd89780001f4f90a/))

## **May: A Golden Month for Foodies and Outdoor Enthusiasts**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b7405a76a96c0882e108c5117afe7a87.png)

- **Weather Overview:** May is highly recommended by many travel experts. The dry season officially begins, humidity drops significantly, and the weather feels refreshingly comfortable. The sky is at its bluest, making it the perfect time for Instagram-worthy photos.
- **Labor Day:** Take 4 days off (April 27-30) for a 9-day holiday exclusive to workers!
- **What to Wear:** Show off your summer style—sunglasses and high-SPF sunscreen are a must.

{{video2}}

**Major Festivals:**

- **May 29-31 Ubud Food Festival:** A must-visit for foodies, offering a feast of Indonesian cuisine.
- **May 31 Waisak Day:** Experience the solemnity and serenity of Buddhist culture.

**Top Places to Visit in May**

- **Ubud Center:** Stay near the center for easy access to the **Ubud Food Festival**, where you can enjoy long-table lunches and chef talks. (Reference: [Ubud Accommodation Recommendations](https://s.id/UbudResorts))
- **Water Activities:** Enjoy the best weather without the peak-season crowds! (Reference: [Complete Guide to Water Activities in Bali: Beyond Snorkeling, 15 Unexpected Water Adventures](/blog/2026-01-20-694122b8fd89780001f514c9/))
- **Island Adventures/Snorkeling:** With calmer seas in May, it’s the best time to visit **Nusa Lembongan** or snorkel with manta rays at **Nusa Penida**. (Reference: [Nusa Lembongan Guide](/blog/2024-10-23-66adfb9afd89780001f62df1/) / [Nusa Penida Guide](https://s.id/penida_guide))
- **Tulamben:** The dry season brings excellent underwater visibility, perfect for exploring shipwrecks. (Reference: [Bali’s Hidden Gems: Amed and Tulamben Complete Guide](/blog/2025-06-23-68578b01fd89780001dd5208/))

## **June: The Peak of Culture and Beauty**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_dd2c5771be5e78e79e23c43530cfe5c2.png)

- **Weather Overview:** June offers extremely stable weather with minimal rainfall. Cool breezes from the Southern Hemisphere bring refreshing winds, and mornings and evenings can feel slightly chilly, making it the most comfortable time for hiking (e.g., Mount Batur).
  **Quick Reference:**
- Dragon Boat Festival: Take 4 days off (June 15-18) for an epic 9-day early summer trip!
- **What to Wear:** Besides summer outfits, bring a light long-sleeve or windbreaker for cooler mornings and evenings.

**Major Festivals:**

- **Bali Arts Festival (PKB) throughout June:** Held in Denpasar, it's the ultimate venue to experience traditional dance, crafts, and parades.
- **Galungan & Kuningan Festivals:** Streets are adorned with spectacular Penjor bamboo decorations, creating the most festive atmosphere.

**Recommended Travel Destinations for June**

- **Werdhi Budaya Art Centre in Denpasar:** Throughout June, the **Bali Arts Festival (PKB)** takes place, featuring top-notch traditional dance and theater performances every afternoon and evening. (Refer to [Denpasar Guide Map](/blog/2025-03-08-67cbb3befd89780001c01e9b/))
- **Mount Batur:** June is the driest and coolest month of the year, making it ideal for climbing the volcano to watch the sunrise, with a high chance of seeing a sea of clouds. (Refer to [Mount Batur and Lake Batur Guide | Sunrise Hike × Royal Cruise × Lakeside Café × Mountain Adventure Park × Hot Springs](/blog/2025-10-09-68e786abfd89780001c1b9b7/))
- **Pandawa Beach:** Join the **Pandawa Beach Festival** and enjoy cultural parades and music performances by the turquoise sea. (How to explore Bali's southern peninsula? Check out [Bukit Peninsula Adventure Guide: From Jimbaran Sunset to Uluwatu Cliff Temple: Bali's Hidden Paradise](/blog/2024-10-31-67061b07fd897800012f87b5/))

## 💡 Editor's Smart Travel Notes

1. **Dressing Philosophy:** Prepare raincoats and moisture-wicking clothes for March and April; for May and June, besides summer outfits, bring a light jacket as the cool evening breeze might give you goosebumps. Check out [Do I Need a Jacket in Bali? Bali Packing Guide: 8 Essential Items](/blog/2025-09-22-68d13539fd89780001b06dfc/)
2. **Avoiding Crowds:** Again, the Eid al-Fitr holiday in mid-to-late March is the "**local peak season**." Unless necessary, consider moving your vacation to April or later.
3. **Booking Advice:** For those planning to attend the Ubud Food Festival or Spirit Festival, Ubud city center gets extremely crowded during these events, so it's recommended to book accommodations three months in advance.
