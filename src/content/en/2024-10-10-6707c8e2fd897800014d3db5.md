In Bali, water is not just the source of life but also a sacred element. For the Balinese, spring water, known as 'Tirta,' is considered a life-giving and purifying force that sustains all existence. Tirta plays a vital role in nearly all religious ceremonies, believed to purify the mind, body, and spirit, cleanse negative energies, and bring blessings.

If you wish to delve deeper into Bali's cultural and spiritual heritage, take the time to explore two renowned water palaces and temples - **Tirta Gangga** and **Pura Tirta Empul**. Let's discover the unique allure of these sacred sites together.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_daa2c7b876bb63a589fcdb9e5d85154d.jpg)

## Tirta Gangga: The Serene Water Palace

Tirta Gangga, also known as Tirta Gangga Water Palace or Ganges Water Garden, is nestled in the lush hills of eastern Bali. This enchanting water palace spans 1.2 hectares and was built in 1946 by the last king of Karangasem. It beautifully blends Balinese and Chinese architectural styles.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_366be6165bbc016df666dfe1b6176fbe.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8634a1b05ac12f9b0f0f63ba42b0e2f7.jpg)

The palace is divided into three levels, each offering a unique experience:

- The lowest level features a large pond filled with koi fish, adorned with statues of Hindu deities.
- The middle level is dominated by the iconic eleven-tiered Nawa Sanga fountain, symbolizing the nine directions in Balinese cosmology.
- The highest level is the sacred pool, where holy spring water flows and gathers.

Tirta Gangga is not only a visual marvel but also a place of profound spiritual significance. Visitors can:

- Stroll through the garden, stepping on stone slabs that traverse the ponds
- Swim in the sacred spring pool believed to possess divine powers
- Admire the exquisite statues and architecture, experiencing the harmony and balance of Balinese aesthetics

The best time to visit is during the dry season from April to October. Remember to dress modestly and respect local customs.

## Tirta Empul: A Sacred Place of Purification

https://youtube.com/shorts/fiYs5GKdINU

Tirta Empul Temple, known as the **Holy Spring Temple**, is located in Ubud, central Bali, and is one of the island's most spiritually significant sites. Established in 960 AD, this temple is believed to house sacred spring water created by the god Indra, renowned for its healing properties. It is famous for its role in Hindu purification rituals in Bali.

The temple is divided into three main courtyards:

- The outer courtyard, Jaba Pura, welcomes visitors with traditional stone carvings and grand gates.

The most captivating experience at Tirta Empul is participating in the 'Melukat Purification Ritual.' The purification pool features 13 spouts, each representing a different spiritual purpose. Devotees immerse themselves in the water at each spout in sequence, offering prayers to cleanse both body and mind.

It's worth noting:

- The temple is open to the public, and visitors are welcome to participate in purification ceremonies
- Sarongs and sashes are required (provided free of charge by the temple)
- The best times to visit are early morning or evening to avoid crowds
- Two spouts are reserved solely for washing the deceased and are prohibited from being used in melukat rituals
