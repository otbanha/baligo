This marks our second visit to Bali this year. From October 11th to 18th, we spent an unforgettable eight days and seven nights on this 'Island of the Gods.' We chose to return because we were deeply captivated by Bali's cultural charm, natural scenery, and authentic cuisine. During this trip, we stayed at three distinct hotels, from the bustling Kuta Beach area to the serene rice fields of Ubud, and the laid-back Jimbaran Bay, each leaving us with beautiful memories.

A special thanks to the group administrators and fellow travelers who offered advice and support when my husband suffered severe diarrhea during the trip (we’ll share how we overcame Bali Belly later). This warmth became one of the most precious takeaways from our journey.

***

## Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Starting Our Tropical Island Adventure

### Arrival and Currency Exchange

After several hours of flying, we finally set foot on Bali. The tropical air outside the airport greeted us with a faint scent of frangipani, instantly immersing us in the vacation vibe. After completing immigration procedures, our first stop was currency exchange. Rates are usually better at exchange offices in the city.

### Dinner at GOSHA x GOEMEROT

After settling in, we eagerly headed to GOSHA x GOEMEROT for our first meal in Bali. This restaurant blends modern and traditional Balinese flavors, with beautifully presented dishes and authentic tastes. On our first night abroad, we savored the delicious food while looking forward to the exciting itinerary for the next seven days.

### Staying at Aloft Bali Kuta at Beachwalk

**Accommodation Recommendation: Aloft Bali Kuta at Beachwalk**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6c4dc60752ea4cbe9373a0cde09448cf.jpg)

Tonight, we stayed at Aloft Bali Kuta, located in the Kuta area. We chose the Pool View Double room, which featured two comfortable double beds. While the room wasn't particularly spacious, the overall design was modern and stylish, with excellent cleanliness. The hotel's location was the most satisfying aspect—directly connected to the Beachwalk shopping mall, making it extremely convenient for shopping or dining. Additionally, it was close to the bustling JL. Poppies II street, just a short walk away. The hotel offered a rich and delicious breakfast, and the staff were friendly and welcoming, setting a great tone for our Bali trip.

## Day 2: Tanah Lot Temple and Leisurely Time in Seminyak

### Morning: Tanah Lot Temple

After enjoying a hearty breakfast at the hotel, we drove to one of Bali's most famous landmarks—Tanah Lot Temple. This 16th-century Hindu temple stands on a large rock in the sea, accessible by foot during low tide and surrounded by water during high tide, creating a picturesque scene. Standing on the shore, watching the waves crash against the rocks with the temple gleaming in the sunlight, the sense of awe was indescribable. We recommend visiting in the afternoon when the sunset makes Tanah Lot even more breathtaking.

**Quick Reference:** [Must-Visit Spots in Bali: Detailed Guide to Tanah Lot Temple, Tides, and Surrounding Attractions](/blog/2024-10-28-671f86f1fd89780001de9fae/)

### Noon: Lunch and Shopping at Seminyak Square

After leaving Tanah Lot, we arrived in the trendy Seminyak area. This district is filled with boutique shops, restaurants, and cafes, offering a more modern vibe compared to other parts of Bali. We strolled around Seminyak Square, enjoying a leisurely shopping experience. The area is home to many designer boutiques where you can find unique clothing, accessories, and home decor, making it perfect for travelers looking for distinctive souvenirs.

**Restaurant Recommendation: ENDO Seminyak**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57caee0de0ee79e29675903fef6d5f69.jpg)

For lunch, we dined at ENDO Seminyak, located near Seminyak Square, and it turned out to be a delightful surprise! What started as a random choice ended up being a fantastic meal. We highly recommend their Bebek Betutu (Dirty Duck) and Mie Goreng (Fried Noodles). The duck was crispy on the outside and tender inside, perfectly seasoned with aromatic spices. The fried noodles were authentically Balinese, packed with ingredients and generously portioned.

### Dinner: WAHAHA

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4be51710a26107a6190d946dc32baa54.jpg)

In the evening, we headed to WAHAHA for a pork rib dinner. Enjoying delicious food under Bali’s warm night sky was the perfect way to end a fulfilling day.

## Day 3: From Kuta to Ubud, Exploring a Different Side of Bali

### Morning: Pool Time at the Hotel

Today we took it easy, enjoying a leisurely breakfast at the hotel before deciding to make the most of the pool facilities. Splashing around in the Bali sunshine is what vacation is all about. We lounged on the poolside chairs, soaking up the Balinese sun.

### Noon: Check-out and Massage

After checking out at noon, we headed to a nearby spa for a traditional Balinese massage. Known for its essential oil techniques, the skilled masseuse applied just the right pressure. After over an hour of treatment, all travel fatigue melted away, leaving us completely relaxed in body and mind.

### Afternoon: Massimo Italian Restaurant and Gelato

Post-massage, we had lunch at Massimo Italian Restaurant, a renowned spot in Bali for authentic Italian cuisine. The dishes were true to their roots, especially the handmade pasta and pizza, transporting us straight to Italy. Don't miss their gelato - creamy and smooth, it's the perfect dessert for hot weather.

### Shopping at Grand Lucky Supermarket

**Shopping Recommendation: Grand Lucky Supermarket**

On our way to Ubud, we made a special stop at Grand Lucky Supermarket. Highly recommended by many tourists for souvenir shopping, it offers transparent and reasonable prices without the tourist markup. The supermarket has a wide variety of goods, from Balinese coffee and spices to snacks and daily necessities. We stocked up on local specialties to share with friends and family back in Taiwan.

### Check-in at KAAMALA Resort & Spa Ubud

**Accommodation Recommendation: KAAMALA Resort & Spa Ubud**

In the evening, we arrived at KAAMALA Resort & Spa in Ubud. We fell in love with this resort at first sight! We stayed in a private Villa, and when we opened the curtains the next morning, we were amazed by the breathtaking view of golden rice fields stretching before us. The gentle breeze swaying the rice stalks made us feel as if we were in a paradise far removed from the world.

The hotel offers primarily Western-style breakfasts. While the selection isn't extensive, each dish is thoughtfully prepared and tastes excellent. The resort also features an infinity pool with a stunning view of the layered rice fields. What impressed us most was the service—the staff would proactively chat with guests, checking on our stay with such warmth that it felt less like a hotel and more like visiting a friend's home.

## Day 4: Deep Exploration of Ubud, Experiencing the Essence of Balinese Culture

### Morning: Leisurely Time at the Hotel

On our first morning in Ubud, we chose to relax at the hotel, fully enjoying the privacy of our Villa and the surrounding countryside. Sitting on the balcony, gazing at the rice fields and listening to the distant chirping of birds and insects, we felt the tranquility of being far from the hustle and bustle—exactly what we came to Ubud for.

### Lunch: Bebek Tepi Sawah

**Restaurant Recommendation: Bebek Tepi Sawah**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_18d5fd887059ea02f0b30f3666e5d864.jpg)

At noon, we headed to Bebek Tepi Sawah for lunch. The name translates to 'Duck by the Rice Field,' and true to its name, the restaurant is beautifully situated beside lush rice paddies. Decorated in traditional Balinese style, it exudes a strong local charm. We chose outdoor seating, enjoying the refreshing countryside views while savoring authentic Balinese cuisine. The gentle breeze carried the scent of rice fields, making this lunch a truly delightful experience. The restaurant's signature roasted duck is a must-try, with tender meat and perfectly balanced spices.

### Afternoon: Bali Reptile Park

**Attraction Recommendation: Bali Reptile Park**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_be260f863fa3cf1d7f49877c02f31826.jpg)

After lunch, we visited the Bali Reptile Park, a must-see destination for reptile enthusiasts! The admission ticket includes a full English guided tour by park staff, which is one of the highlights of this attraction. The guides are highly professional and passionate, offering detailed explanations about the habits and characteristics of each reptile. Their engaging commentary makes the experience enjoyable even for those who might initially feel uneasy around reptiles.

The most unique part of the tour is the opportunity to touch some of the gentler reptiles, including pythons and lizards. The staff ensures safety and teaches the proper way to handle these creatures. This hands-on experience gave us a whole new understanding and appreciation for these animals. The entire visit takes about 1.5 to 2 hours, making it a perfect activity for families.

### Evening: Ubud Market Shopping

After leaving the reptile park, we headed to Ubud Market. This is the liveliest area in Ubud and the best place to buy Balinese handicrafts. The market offers a wide variety of goods, from traditional clothing, wood carvings, silver jewelry, woven bags to paintings. Prices here are negotiable, and it's recommended to start bargaining at about one-third of the vendor's initial price. Haggling is part of the fun of exploring the market. Hidden among the market's alleys are numerous cafes and small eateries, perfect for taking a break with a cup of Balinese coffee and soaking in Ubud's relaxed atmosphere.

## Day 5: Exploring Kintamani Volcano and Traditional Villages

### Morning: Kintamani and Ritatkala Café

**Attraction and Restaurant Recommendation: Kintamani & Ritatkala Café**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3432f10b8a1acb758145140d33e88ac0.jpg)

Today, we drove to the famous volcanic landscape area of Bali—Kintamani. Here, you can admire the magnificent views of Mount Batur and Lake Batur, making it one of Bali's must-visit natural attractions.

We chose to dine at Ritatkala Café, a scenic coffee shop with an excellent vantage point. We highly recommend opting for outdoor seating, where you can face the volcano and lake while enjoying your meal and marveling at nature's craftsmanship. The café's chicken wings are particularly recommended—crispy on the outside, juicy on the inside, and perfectly complemented by a special sauce. In the cool mountain climate, sipping hot coffee while gazing at the distant volcano creates an indescribable sense of tranquility.

### Afternoon: Penglipuran Village

**Attraction Recommendation: Penglipuran Village**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_534f807ca0ff379512580af984f596cc.jpg)

After leaving Mount Batur, we headed to Penglipuran Village, a well-preserved traditional Balinese village. The uniform architectural style throughout the village beautifully showcases Balinese traditional aesthetics and cultural characteristics. The most special part is that visitors can enter the homes to explore, which was a dream come true for me, as I've always been curious about the interior of traditional Balinese houses.

The streets in the village are clean and tidy, lined with bamboo groves, making it exceptionally serene to walk through. Some houses sell handicrafts or local specialties, and prices are negotiable, so it's worth trying to chat with the owners. We bought ice pops from one household, and the owner was very friendly, warmly inviting us to rest in the courtyard. Sitting in the courtyard of a traditional Balinese house, feeling the breeze and tranquility, was so relaxing that we didn't want to leave. The slow-paced life here, filled with warmth, makes it an excellent spot to experience traditional Balinese culture.

## Day 6: Exploring the Southern Coastline and Cliffs

### Morning: Pool Time at the Hotel

On our last morning in Ubud, we returned to the hotel's infinity pool to soak in the rice field views one last time. Floating in the pool, gazing at the pastoral scenery, our hearts were filled with peace and gratitude.

### After Checkout: Uluwatu Cliff Road

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a123f697405c668b110a8f2b12af913f.jpg)

After checking out, we drove to the Uluwatu area in southern Bali. Cruising along the cliffside coastal road, with the azure Indian Ocean on one side and steep limestone cliffs on the other, the breathtaking views opened our hearts. This stretch is considered one of Bali's most beautiful coastlines, with several scenic spots along the way to stop for photos - don't miss them!

### Afternoon: Tea Time at Roosterfish, Pandawa Beach

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4cfd7bde880edf48f6923248b34146d2.jpg)

We enjoyed afternoon tea at Roosterfish restaurant on Pandawa Beach. Located right by the beach, this restaurant offers stunning ocean views. Sipping cool drinks and nibbling on light snacks while sitting on the breezy terrace watching the waves lap the shore made for a simple yet perfect afternoon. Pandawa Beach's crystal-clear waters and pristine white sand make it one of Bali's more tranquil beaches, ideal for travelers seeking to escape the crowds.

### Evening: Uluwatu Temple

**Attraction Recommendation: Uluwatu Temple ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_65cdfff2716506cd7615d0c7611c9c66.jpg)

As evening approached, we visited Uluwatu Temple, one of Bali's most important religious sites perched on a cliff edge. It's also one of the best spots to watch the sunset. The temple stands majestically on a 70-meter-high cliff overlooking the endless Indian Ocean, creating a truly awe-inspiring scene.

**Important Reminder: The monkeys here are very mischievous and bold!** We witnessed firsthand how tourists had their glasses snatched by monkeys. After long-term interactions with visitors, these monkeys have learned how to quickly grab items like glasses, hats, and sunglasses, then "demand" food in exchange. Therefore, please keep your belongings secure when visiting. Use a strap for your glasses and avoid carrying shiny accessories or food to prevent attracting the monkeys' attention.

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Uluwatu Temple/Lover's Cliff Travel Guide: Must-See Highlights and Practical Tips](/blog/2025-01-18-678b1d81fd89780001f7fe4d/)

Despite the need to be cautious around monkeys, the view from Uluwatu Temple is truly breathtaking. As the sun sets, golden sunlight spreads across the sea, creating a sparkling effect, and the temple appears even more sacred and majestic in the backlight. If time permits, stay to watch the traditional Kecak Fire Dance performance, enjoying this cultural feast of dance, music, and drama in the glow of the sunset.

**Quick Reference:** [Kecak Fire Dance: Bali's Must-See Spectacular Performance (Includes Ticket Booking Info)](/blog/2024-01-29-65b6e533fd89780001f51eaf/)

### Dinner: Ulu Cliffhouse

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c2d5e6caeae53304208dc0744dfb4875.jpg)

In the evening, we dined at Ulu Cliffhouse, a restaurant also perched on the cliffside with stunning ocean views. Under the starry sky, listening to the sound of the waves while enjoying exquisite cuisine, we concluded today's exciting itinerary perfectly.

### Stay at Movenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran

**Accommodation Recommendation: Movenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran**

Today we arrived at the final hotel of our journey - Mövenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran. This is our second time staying at this resort. Perhaps because we're returning guests, we were lucky enough to be upgraded to a poolside room, where we could jump directly into the pool from our balcony. This convenience felt particularly luxurious.

The resort boasts multiple pool facilities, including a children's water play area and water volleyball court, and regularly hosts various water activities, making it perfect for families. The hotel's breakfast offers a rich and diverse selection, from Asian cuisine to Western dishes, all of excellent quality. ### Important Reminders

### ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!

At monkey-populated attractions like Uluwatu Temple and the Sacred Monkey Forest, extra caution is essential! We witnessed firsthand a tourist's glasses being snatched by a monkey. These clever creatures know how to 'negotiate' after stealing items. Recommendations:

- Secure glasses and sunglasses with straps
- Avoid carrying shiny accessories or food
- Ensure bags are fully zipped
- Maintain a safe distance from monkeys; do not attempt to touch or feed them

### ⚠️ Don't Take Bali Belly Lightly! — Our Personal Horror Story

During this trip, my husband unfortunately experienced a severe case of Bali Belly. The entire ordeal was nerve-wracking, and I'm sharing the details here in hopes that those planning a trip to Bali can learn from our experience.

**Night of Day 6: Symptoms Begin**

On the very first night we checked into the Movenpick Hotel, my husband suddenly complained of stomach pain and needed to use the restroom. That night, he made three trips to the bathroom and tossed and turned all night, getting very poor sleep. At the time, we thought it was just a mild case of adjusting to the local environment, but little did we know it was the calm before the storm.

**Morning of Day 7: Condition Worsens Rapidly**

The next morning, my husband woke up saying he had a splitting headache. When I touched his forehead, I was shocked—he was running a high fever! Soon after, the diarrhea symptoms intensified, and he couldn't leave the room to have breakfast. I had to take our child to the restaurant alone.

However, when we returned to the room after breakfast, the scene before me was utterly alarming. My husband was sitting weakly on the toilet, his upper body slumped over the sink. He told me that due to the severe stomach pain and the constant urge to use the bathroom, he didn't dare move an inch from the toilet. Our child, seeing Daddy like this, thought he had fainted and burst into tears. At that moment, I was truly panicked.

I immediately took out the emergency medicine we had brought from Taiwan and gave him painkillers, fever reducers, and anti-diarrheal medication, then let him rest. Two hours later, the fever had subsided, but the diarrhea showed no signs of improvement. Realizing the severity of the situation, I decided to seek help from a local pharmacy.

**Seeking Help at the Pharmacy**

At the pharmacy, I described my husband's symptoms to the pharmacist. The pharmacist, very professionally, recommended two medications (shown in the photo) and explained in detail how to take them and what to watch out for. I quickly bought the medicine and rushed back to the hotel to administer it to my husband.

**A Dinner Gone Wrong**

In the evening, we went to Bebek Bengil near the hotel for dinner. My husband was very cautious, only daring to drink clear soup and eat plain rice, thinking it would be safe. However, back in the room, he started having diarrhea again. I was extremely worried and didn't know what to do, so I turned to an online community for help. Thanks to the administrators and kind-hearted members, who offered valuable advice and warm concern late at night, we didn't feel completely lost in a foreign land.

**Day 8: The Difficult Journey Home**

On the morning of the eighth day, I packed some plain toast from the hotel restaurant for my husband, but he continued to have diarrhea. I started worrying whether he could endure the flight back to Taiwan. Fortunately, he said that as long as he could sit, the pain was bearable.

With trepidation, we headed to the airport. During the flight, my husband didn't dare to eat the in-flight meal and endured abdominal discomfort throughout the journey. Finally, we landed safely in Taipei, but even after returning home, he continued to suffer from stomach pain and diarrhea.

**Medical Treatment and Recovery After Returning Home**

The next morning, we immediately went to the clinic. The doctor carefully inquired about his symptoms and the course of the illness, prescribing anti-diarrheal medication and probiotics. After taking the medicine, his symptoms gradually began to ease, but he still experienced intermittent stomach pain and diarrhea.

During his recovery, my husband only dared to eat plain toast. Once, he tried drinking some soup, but it triggered diarrhea again, frightening him so much that he completely avoided trying any other foods. The entire illness lasted six days, and it wasn't until the sixth day that the diarrhea finally stopped completely.

**A Surprising 'Side Effect'**

This bout of Bali Belly caused my husband to drop from 78kg to 73kg, losing a total of 5kg! While this is definitely not a healthy way to lose weight, I guess you could say every cloud has a silver lining? My husband joked, 'When the stomach pain hit, I felt like I was about to give birth!'

**Cause Analysis and Reflection**

Afterwards, we carefully reviewed our entire trip. While my child and I were fine, only my husband fell ill. We speculated the possible causes as follows:

1. **Ice Issue**: On the 6th day at noon, my husband bought an iced coffee filled with ice cubes from Pepito supermarket. The source of ice in Bali is unclear, and this was likely the culprit.
2. **Hand Hygiene**: Men sometimes get lazy and don't wash their hands properly before meals. My child and I strictly adhere to handwashing before eating. Unfortunately, that evening we had pizza, which requires eating with hands, increasing the risk of infection.
3. **Probiotic Protection**: Throughout our 8-day trip, my child and I took probiotics every morning, which may have helped protect our digestive health. (Note: This is just personal speculation, not medical advice)

It's worth mentioning that this was our second encounter with Bali Belly this year. In January, we spent 6 days in Bali, and all three of us experienced varying degrees of diarrhea, with our child even developing a fever. However, the symptoms were relatively mild then, and we recovered quickly after taking medication. We never expected my husband's symptoms to be so severe this time, truly experiencing the power of Bali Belly.

**Advice for All Travelers**

After this painful experience, I'd like to offer the following suggestions to friends planning a trip to Bali:

1. **Prepare sufficient gastrointestinal medications before departure**: Including anti-diarrheal medicine, fever reducers/painkillers, probiotics, and stomach medicine. Don't take chances - better to have them and not need them.
2. **Absolutely avoid drinks with ice cubes**: This cannot be emphasized enough! Avoid ice in street stalls, supermarket drinks, and restaurant beverages. Also ensure bottled water is properly sealed.
3. **Practice proper hand hygiene before meals**: Wash hands thoroughly with soap or use alcohol-based hand sanitizer. Don't skip this step because it's inconvenient.
4. **Consider taking probiotics daily**: Start a few days before departure and continue throughout the trip, as it may help protect your digestive system.
5. **Be cautious with street food**: Pay attention to hygiene conditions. If the environment looks unclean, it's better to pass.
6. **Don't tough it out with severe symptoms**: If you experience high fever, severe dehydration, or persistent diarrhea, seek help from local pharmacies or go directly to a hospital. Don't wait until the next day like we did.
7. **Travel insurance is crucial**: Ensure your insurance covers overseas medical expenses to reduce financial burden if medical attention is needed.
