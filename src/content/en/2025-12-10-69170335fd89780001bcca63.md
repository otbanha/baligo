This was our second visit to Bali this year. From October 11th to 18th, we spent an unforgettable eight days and seven nights on this 'Island of the Gods.' We chose to return because we were deeply captivated by Bali's cultural charm, natural landscapes, and authentic cuisine. During this trip, we stayed at three hotels with distinct styles, ranging from the bustling Kuta Beach area to the serene rice fields of Ubud and the laid-back Jimbaran Bay, each leaving us with wonderful memories.

After several hours of flying, we finally set foot on Bali. The tropical air outside the airport greeted us with a faint scent of frangipani, instantly immersing us in the vacation vibe. ## Day 2: Tanah Lot Temple and Leisure Time in Seminyak

### Morning: Tanah Lot Temple

After enjoying a hearty breakfast at the hotel, we drove to one of Bali's most iconic landmarks—Tanah Lot Temple. This 16th-century Hindu temple stands on a massive rock in the sea. During low tide, you can walk closer to it, while at high tide, it's surrounded by water, creating a picturesque scene. Standing on the shore, watching the waves crash against the rocks and the temple shimmering in the sunlight, the sense of awe is indescribable. We recommend visiting in the late afternoon when the sunset makes Tanah Lot even more breathtaking.

**Quick Reference:** [Must-Visit Bali Attractions: Tanah Lot Detailed Guide with Tide Times and Nearby Spots](/blog/2024-10-28-671f86f1fd89780001de9fae/)

### Noon: Dining and Shopping at Seminyak Square

After leaving Tanah Lot, we headed to the trendy Seminyak area. This area is filled with boutique shops, restaurants, and cafes, making it one of Bali's more modern districts. We strolled around Seminyak Square, enjoying a leisurely shopping experience. There are many designer boutiques here where you can find unique clothing, accessories, and home decor, perfect for travelers looking for distinctive souvenirs.

**Restaurant Recommendation: ENDO Seminyak**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57caee0de0ee79e29675903fef6d5f69.jpg)

For lunch, we dined at ENDO Seminyak near Seminyak Square, and it was a delightful surprise! We chose the restaurant casually, but the food quality exceeded our expectations. We highly recommend their Bebek Betutu (Dirty Duck) and Mie Goreng (Fried Noodles). The duck was crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, with just the right blend of spices. The fried noodles were authentic Balinese-style, packed with ingredients and very filling.

### Dinner: WAHAHA

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4be51710a26107a6190d946dc32baa54.jpg)

As evening fell, we headed to WAHAHA restaurant for a pork rib dinner. Enjoying delicious food under Bali's warm night sky was the perfect way to end a fulfilling day.

## Day 3: From Kuta to Ubud, Exploring a Different Side of Bali

### Morning: Pool Time at the Hotel

With no rush today, we leisurely enjoyed breakfast at the hotel before deciding to make the most of the pool facilities. Splashing around under Bali's sunshine is what vacation is all about. Lounging by the poolside, we soaked up the sun and embraced Bali's tropical vibe.

### Noon: Check-out and Massage

After checking out at noon, we visited a nearby spa center for a traditional Balinese massage. Known for its aromatic oil massage, the skilled therapists applied just the right pressure. After over an hour of treatment, all travel fatigue vanished, leaving us completely relaxed in body and mind.

### Afternoon: Massimo Italian Restaurant and Ice Cream

Post-massage, we headed to Massimo Italian Restaurant for lunch. Renowned in Bali, this restaurant serves authentic Italian cuisine. The dishes were true to their origins, especially the handmade pasta and pizza, transporting us straight to Italy. Don't forget to try their ice cream for dessert—its creamy texture makes it the perfect treat in the tropical heat.

### Shopping at Grand Lucky Supermarket

**Shopping Recommendation: Grand Lucky Supermarket**

On our way to Ubud, we made a special stop at Grand Lucky Supermarket. This supermarket is highly recommended by many tourists for souvenir shopping, offering transparent and reasonable prices without the worry of being overcharged as tourists. The supermarket boasts a wide variety of goods, from Balinese coffee, spices, snacks to daily necessities. We purchased a number of local specialty foods here, planning to share them with friends and family back in Taiwan.

**Accommodation Recommendation: KAAMALA Resort & Spa Ubud**

## Day 4: Deep Exploration of Ubud, Experiencing the Essence of Balinese Culture

### Morning: Leisurely Time at the Hotel

On our first morning in Ubud, we chose to relax at the hotel, fully enjoying the privacy of our Villa and the surrounding rural scenery. Sitting on the balcony, gazing at the rice fields, and listening to the distant chirping of birds and insects, this sense of tranquility away from the hustle and bustle was exactly why we came to Ubud.

### Lunch at Bebek Tepi Sawah

**Restaurant Recommendation: Bebek Tepi Sawah**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_18d5fd887059ea02f0b30f3666e5d864.jpg)

At noon, we headed to Bebek Tepi Sawah for lunch. The name translates to 'Duck by the Rice Field,' and true to its name, the restaurant is beautifully situated beside lush rice paddies. Decorated in traditional Balinese style, the place exudes a strong local charm. We chose an outdoor seat, enjoying authentic Balinese cuisine while taking in the refreshing rural scenery. The gentle breeze carried the scent of rice fields, making this lunch a truly relaxing experience. Their signature roasted duck is a must-try—tender and perfectly spiced.

### Afternoon: Bali Reptile Park

**Attraction Recommendation: Bali Reptile Park**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_be260f863fa3cf1d7f49877c02f31826.jpg)

After lunch, we visited the Bali Reptile Park, a must-see for reptile enthusiasts! The ticket includes an English-guided tour, which is one of the park's standout features. The guides are professional and passionate, offering detailed explanations about the habits and characteristics of each reptile. Their engaging storytelling can even win over those who are initially hesitant about reptiles.

The highlight of the tour was the opportunity to touch some of the gentler reptiles, including pythons and lizards. The staff ensured safety and taught us the proper way to interact with them. This hands-on experience gave us a whole new perspective on these creatures. The entire visit takes about 1.5 to 2 hours, making it a great activity for families.

### Evening: Exploring Ubud Market

After leaving the reptile park, we headed to Ubud Market. This is the liveliest area in Ubud and the best place to shop for Balinese handicrafts. The market offers a wide variety of goods, from traditional clothing, wood carvings, silver jewelry, woven bags to paintings. Prices here are negotiable, and it's recommended to start bargaining at about one-third of the vendor's asking price. Enjoying the haggling process is part of the market experience. Hidden among the market's alleys are numerous cafes and small eateries, perfect for taking a break with a cup of Balinese coffee and soaking in Ubud's relaxed atmosphere.

## Day 5: Exploring Kintamani Volcano and Traditional Villages

### Morning: Kintamani and Ritatkala Café

**Attraction and Restaurant Recommendation: Kintamani & Ritatkala Café**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3432f10b8a1acb758145140d33e88ac0.jpg)

Today we drove to Kintamani, Bali's famous volcanic landscape area. Here, you can admire the magnificent views of Mount Batur and Lake Batur, making it a must-visit natural attraction in Bali.

We chose to dine at Ritatkala Café, a scenic coffee shop with an excellent view. It's highly recommended to choose an outdoor seat, facing the volcano and lake, enjoying the meal while marveling at nature's craftsmanship. The café's chicken wings are particularly recommended, with crispy skin and juicy meat, paired with a special sauce that's very tasty. In the cool mountain climate, sipping hot coffee and gazing at the distant volcano, the feeling of refreshment is indescribable.

### Afternoon: Penglipuran Village Traditional Settlement

**Attraction Recommendation: Penglipuran Village**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_534f807ca0ff379512580af984f596cc.jpg)

After leaving Mount Batur, we headed to Penglipuran Village, a well-preserved traditional Balinese settlement. The uniform architectural style throughout the village beautifully showcases Balinese traditional aesthetics and cultural characteristics. What's most special here is that visitors can enter the homes to explore, which was a dream come true for me as I've always been curious about the interior of traditional Balinese houses!

The streets in the village are clean and tidy, lined with bamboo forests, making it feel exceptionally serene as you walk through. Some homes also sell handicrafts or local products, and prices are negotiable, so don't hesitate to chat with the owners. We bought ice pops from one household, and the owner was incredibly friendly, warmly inviting us to rest in their courtyard. Sitting in the courtyard of a traditional Balinese house, feeling the breeze and tranquility, the sense of relaxation made it hard to leave. The slow-paced lifestyle and warm hospitality of the residents make this an excellent spot to experience traditional Balinese culture.

## Day 6: Exploring the Southern Coastline & Cliffside Views

### Morning: Hotel Pool Time

On our last morning in Ubud, we returned to the hotel's infinity pool to soak in the stunning rice field views. Floating in the pool while gazing at the serene countryside filled our hearts with peace and gratitude.

### After Check-out: Uluwatu Cliff Road

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a123f697405c668b110a8f2b12af913f.jpg)

After checking out, we drove to the Uluwatu area in southern Bali. Cruising along the cliffside coastal road, with the azure Indian Ocean on one side and dramatic limestone cliffs on the other, the breathtaking scenery opened our hearts. This stretch is considered one of Bali's most beautiful coastlines, with several scenic viewpoints perfect for photo stops—don't miss them!

### Afternoon: Afternoon Tea at Roosterfish, Pandawa Beach

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4cfd7bde880edf48f6923248b34146d2.jpg)

We enjoyed afternoon tea at Roosterfish on Pandawa Beach. The restaurant boasts incredible ocean views right by the beach. Sipping on a cold drink with light snacks while sitting on the breezy terrace, watching the waves lap against the shore, made for a simple yet delightful afternoon. Pandawa Beach, with its crystal-clear waters and pristine white sand, is one of Bali's quieter beaches, ideal for travelers seeking to escape the crowds.

### Evening: Uluwatu Temple

**Attraction Recommendation: Uluwatu Temple ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_65cdfff2716506cd7615d0c7611c9c66.jpg)

In the evening, we visited Uluwatu Temple, one of Bali's most important religious sites and a prime spot for sunset viewing. Perched on a 70-meter-high cliff overlooking the vast Indian Ocean, the temple is awe-inspiring.

**Important Reminder: The monkeys here are mischievous and bold!** We witnessed a tourist's glasses being snatched by a monkey. These monkeys have learned to quickly grab items like glasses, hats, and sunglasses, then 'demand' food in exchange. So, keep your belongings secure, use straps for glasses, and avoid carrying shiny accessories or food to prevent attracting their attention.

**Quick Reference:**[Bali Uluwatu Temple/Lover's Cliff Travel Guide: Must-See Highlights and Practical Tips](/blog/2025-01-18-678b1d81fd89780001f7fe4d/)

While you need to be cautious of monkeys, the view from Uluwatu Temple is truly breathtaking. As the sun sets, golden rays dance on the ocean surface, creating a shimmering spectacle, and the temple appears even more sacred and majestic against the backlight. If time permits, stay to watch the traditional Kecak Fire Dance performance, a cultural feast of dance, music, and drama set against the backdrop of the sunset.

**Quick Reference:**[Kecak Fire Dance - Bali's Must-See Spectacular Performance (with Ticketing Information)](/blog/2024-01-29-65b6e533fd89780001f51eaf/)

### Dinner: Ulu Cliffhouse

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c2d5e6caeae53304208dc0744dfb4875.jpg)

We dined at Ulu Cliffhouse in the evening, a restaurant also perched on the cliffside with stunning ocean views. Under the starry sky, listening to the waves, we enjoyed exquisite cuisine, perfectly concluding today's exciting itinerary.

**Shopping Recommendation: Krisna**

In the afternoon, we headed to Krisna Souvenir Store for a final shopping frenzy. Krisna is Bali's largest chain of souvenir shops, offering an incredibly diverse range of products from food, clothing, accessories to home goods. With transparent and reasonable pricing clearly marked, there's no need for bargaining, making it one of the best choices for purchasing souvenirs.

We bought a large quantity of coffee, cookies, chocolates, spices, and other local specialties to share with friends and family back in Taiwan. Krisna has multiple branches, each quite spacious, so we recommend allocating at least 1-2 hours for shopping.

### Dinner: Bebek Bengil (Dirty Duck Diner)

For dinner, we dined at Bebek Bengil near our hotel. This restaurant is famous for its Bebek Betutu (Dirty Duck), a Balinese chain restaurant specialty. The duck is smoked and roasted for a long time, resulting in crispy skin and tender, flavorful meat, served with spices and side dishes, offering an authentic Balinese culinary experience. On this evening nearing the end of our journey, savoring delicious Balinese cuisine filled our hearts with both reluctance and satisfaction.

### Important Reminders

### ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!

At monkey-populated sites like Uluwatu Temple and the Sacred Monkey Forest, extra caution is essential! We witnessed firsthand a tourist's glasses being snatched by a monkey. These clever creatures know how to 'negotiate exchanges' after stealing items. Recommendations:

- Secure glasses and sunglasses with straps
- Avoid carrying shiny accessories or food
- Ensure bags are fully zipped
- Maintain a safe distance from monkeys; do not attempt to touch or feed them

### ⚠️ Don't Underestimate Bali Belly! - Our Personal Painful Experience

During this trip, my husband unfortunately suffered a severe case of Bali Belly, and the entire ordeal was quite harrowing. I'm sharing the details here in hopes that it serves as a cautionary tale for those planning to visit Bali.

**Night of Day 6: Symptoms Begin**

On our first night staying at the Movenpick Hotel, my husband suddenly complained of stomach pain and needed to use the restroom. That night, he made three trips to the bathroom and tossed and turned all night, getting very poor sleep. At the time, we thought it was just a mild case of travel adjustment, but little did we know, this was just the calm before the storm.

**Morning of Day 7: The Condition Takes a Turn for the Worse**

The next morning, my husband woke up with a splitting headache. When I touched his forehead, I was shocked—he was running a high fever! Shortly after, his diarrhea worsened, and he couldn’t leave the room to have breakfast. I had to take our child to the restaurant alone.

However, when we returned to the room after breakfast, the scene before me was utterly alarming. My husband was sitting weakly on the toilet, his upper body slumped over the sink. He told me that due to the intense stomach pain and the constant need to use the bathroom, he couldn’t leave the toilet. Our child, seeing him like this, thought he had fainted and burst into tears. At that moment, I was truly panicked.

I immediately took out the emergency medicine we had brought from Taiwan and gave him painkillers, fever reducers, and anti-diarrheal medication, then let him rest. Two hours later, his fever had subsided, but the diarrhea showed no signs of improvement. Realizing the severity of the situation, I decided to seek help at a local pharmacy.

**Seeking Help at the Pharmacy**

At the pharmacy, I described my husband’s symptoms to the pharmacist. The pharmacist, very professionally, recommended two medications (shown in the photo) and explained the dosage and precautions in detail. I quickly purchased the medicine and returned to the hotel to give it to my husband.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_12d5b905724f2858e8e51a33065eb6c8.jpg)

In the afternoon, I took our child to Krisna to shop for souvenirs. On one hand, it was to distract our child and keep them from worrying too much about their dad; on the other hand, I didn’t want to waste our precious travel time. When we returned to the hotel and saw that my husband’s condition had slightly improved, we finally felt a bit more at ease.

**A Dinner Gone Wrong**

In the evening, we went to Bebek Bengil near our hotel for dinner. My husband was very cautious, only having clear soup and plain rice, thinking it would be safe. However, back in our room, he started having diarrhea again. I was extremely worried and didn't know what to do, so I posted for help in an online community. Thanks to the administrators and kind members who offered valuable advice and warm concern late at night, we didn't feel too panicked in a foreign land.

**Day 8: The Difficult Journey Home**

On the morning of the eighth day, I packed some plain toast from the hotel restaurant for my husband, but he continued to have diarrhea. I started worrying whether he could manage the flight back to Taiwan, feeling very anxious. Fortunately, my husband said he could endure the pain as long as he could sit.

So, we nervously headed to the airport. On the plane, my husband didn't dare to eat the in-flight meal and endured abdominal discomfort throughout the flight. Finally, we landed safely in Taipei, but even after returning home, my husband still suffered from abdominal pain and diarrhea.

**Post-Trip Medical Care and Recovery**

The next morning, we immediately went to the clinic. The doctor inquired in detail about the symptoms and course of the illness, prescribing anti-diarrheal medication and probiotics. After taking the medication, the symptoms gradually began to alleviate, but intermittent abdominal pain and diarrhea persisted.

During the recovery period, my husband only dared to eat plain toast. Once he tried having some soup, but it triggered diarrhea again, scaring him into avoiding any other foods. The entire illness lasted six days, and it wasn't until the sixth day that the diarrhea finally stopped completely.

**The Surprising 'Side Effect'**

This bout of Bali Belly caused my husband's weight to drop from 78 kg to 73 kg, a total loss of 5 kg! Although this is definitely not a healthy way to lose weight, it was somewhat a blessing in disguise? My husband joked, 'When the stomach pain hit, I felt like I was about to give birth!'

**Cause Analysis and Reflection**

After carefully reviewing our trip, my child and I remained healthy, but my husband fell ill. We speculate the possible reasons are as follows:

1. **Ice Issue**: On the 6th day at noon, my husband bought a freshly brewed iced coffee filled with ice from Pepito supermarket. The source of ice in Bali is unclear, and it might have been the culprit.
2. **Hand Hygiene**: Men sometimes skip thoroughly washing their hands before meals. My child and I strictly adhere to washing hands before eating. Coincidentally, we had pizza that evening, which required eating with hands, increasing the risk of infection.
3. **Probiotic Protection**: Throughout the 8-day trip, my child and I took gastrointestinal probiotics every morning, which might have protected our gut health to some extent. (Note: This is merely personal speculation, not medical advice.)

It's worth mentioning that this was our second encounter with Bali Belly this year. In January, we stayed in Bali for 6 days, and all three of us experienced varying degrees of diarrhea, with our child even having a fever. However, the symptoms were relatively mild then, and recovery was quick after medication. We didn't expect my husband's symptoms to be so severe this time, truly experiencing the power of Bali Belly.

**Advice for All Travelers**

After this painful experience, I offer the following advice to those planning to visit Bali:

1. **Prepare ample gastrointestinal medication before departure**: Including anti-diarrheal, fever-reducing and pain-relieving drugs, probiotics, and stomach medicine. It's better to be over-prepared than underprepared.
2. **Absolutely avoid drinks with ice**: This cannot be overemphasized! Avoid freshly brewed drinks from roadside stalls, supermarkets, and iced beverages in restaurants if they contain ice. Ensure mineral water bottles are sealed.
3. **Implement hand hygiene before meals**: Wash hands thoroughly with soap or use alcohol-based hand sanitizer. Don't skip this step for convenience.
4. **Consider daily probiotic intake**: Start taking probiotics a few days before departure and continue throughout the trip, which may help protect your gut.
5. **Choose roadside stalls cautiously**: Pay attention to hygiene conditions; if the environment looks unclean, it's better to skip.
6. **Don't tough it out if symptoms are severe**: If experiencing high fever, severe dehydration, persistent diarrhea, etc., seek immediate assistance from local pharmacies or go directly to a hospital. Don't wait until the next day like we did.
7. **Travel insurance is crucial**: Ensure your insurance covers overseas medical expenses to alleviate the burden in case of medical needs.
