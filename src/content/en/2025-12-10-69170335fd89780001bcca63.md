> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by fellow traveler [Pinkey Lo](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/1XMLomzb3h/):

This marks our second visit to Bali this year. From October 11th to 18th, we spent an unforgettable eight days and seven nights on this 'Island of the Gods.' The reason we chose to return was our deep attraction to Bali's cultural charm, natural landscapes, and authentic cuisine. During this trip, we stayed at three distinctively different hotels, from the bustling Kuta Beach area to the serene rice fields of Ubud and the laid-back Jimbaran coast, each leaving us with beautiful memories.

A special thanks to the group administrators and fellow travelers who offered advice and support when my husband suffered severe diarrhea during the trip (we’ll share how we overcame Bali Belly later). This warmth became one of the most precious takeaways from our journey.

## Day 1: Arriving in Bali, Starting Our Tropical Island Adventure

### Arrival and Currency Exchange

After several hours of flying, we finally set foot on Bali. The tropical air outside the airport greeted us, carrying a faint scent of frangipani flowers, instantly immersing us in the vacation vibe. After completing immigration, our first stop was to exchange currency. Rates are usually better at exchange offices in the city.

## Day 2: Tanah Lot Temple and Leisurely Time in Seminyak

### Morning: Tanah Lot Temple

After enjoying a hearty breakfast at the hotel, we drove to one of Bali’s most iconic landmarks—Tanah Lot Temple. This 16th-century Hindu temple stands on a massive rock in the sea, accessible by foot during low tide and surrounded by water during high tide, creating a picturesque scene. Standing on the shore, watching the waves crash against the rocks while the temple glistened in the sunlight was an indescribably awe-inspiring experience. We recommend visiting in the afternoon, as the sunset view of Tanah Lot is breathtaking.

**Quick Reference:** [Must-Visit Bali Attraction: Detailed Guide to Tanah Lot Temple, Tides, and Nearby Spots](/blog/2024-10-28-671f86f1fd89780001de9fae/)

### Noon: Dining and Shopping at Seminyak Square

After leaving Tanah Lot, we arrived in the trendy Seminyak area. This district is filled with boutique shops, restaurants, and cafes, making it one of Bali's more modern zones. We strolled around Seminyak Square, enjoying a leisurely shopping experience. The area is dotted with designer boutiques where you can find unique clothing, accessories, and home decor, perfect for travelers seeking distinctive souvenirs.

## Day 3: Transition from Kuta to Ubud, Exploring a Different Side of Bali

### Morning: Poolside Relaxation

Today, we decided to take it easy. After a leisurely breakfast at the hotel, we made the most of the pool facilities. Playing in the water under the Bali sun is what a vacation is all about. Lounging on the poolside chairs, we soaked up the Balinese sunshine.

### Noon: Check-out and Massage

At noon, we checked out and headed to a nearby spa for a traditional Balinese massage. Known for its essential oil massages, the skilled therapists provided just the right pressure. After over an hour of pampering, all travel fatigue vanished, leaving us completely relaxed.

### Afternoon: Massimo Italian Restaurant and Gelato

Post-massage, we lunched at Massimo Italian Restaurant, renowned in Bali for authentic Italian cuisine. The dishes were genuine, especially the handmade pasta and pizza, transporting us straight to Italy. Don’t miss their gelato—creamy and smooth, it’s the perfect treat for a hot day.

### Shopping at Grand Lucky Supermarket

**Shopping Recommendation: Grand Lucky Supermarket**

On our way to Ubud, we stopped by Grand Lucky Supermarket, a favorite among tourists for souvenirs. With transparent and fair pricing, there’s no worry about tourist markups. The supermarket offers a wide range of products, from Balinese coffee and spices to snacks and daily necessities. We stocked up on local specialties to share with friends and family back home.

## Day 4: Deep Exploration of Ubud, Experiencing the Essence of Balinese Culture

### Morning: Leisurely Time at the Hotel

On our first morning in Ubud, we chose to relax at the hotel, fully enjoying the privacy of our Villa and the surrounding countryside. Sitting on the balcony, gazing at the rice fields and listening to the distant chirping of birds and insects, this sense of tranquility far from the hustle and bustle was exactly why we came to Ubud.

### Lunch: Bebek Tepi Sawah

**Restaurant Recommendation: Bebek Tepi Sawah**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_18d5fd887059ea02f0b30f3666e5d864.jpg)

At noon, we headed to Bebek Tepi Sawah for lunch. The name of this restaurant translates to 'Duck by the Rice Field,' and it truly lives up to its name, nestled beside a beautiful rice paddy. The restaurant is decorated in traditional Balinese style, exuding a strong local vibe. We chose outdoor seating, enjoying the refreshing rural scenery while savoring authentic Balinese cuisine. A gentle breeze carried the scent of rice fields, making this lunch a delightful experience. The restaurant's signature roasted duck is a must-try, with tender meat and perfectly balanced spices.

### Afternoon: Bali Reptile Park

**Attraction Recommendation: Bali Reptile Park**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_be260f863fa3cf1d7f49877c02f31826.jpg)

After lunch, we visited the Bali Reptile Park, a must-visit for anyone who loves reptiles! The ticket includes a full English guided tour by park staff, which is one of the park's standout features. The guides are highly professional and enthusiastic, providing detailed explanations about the habits and characteristics of each reptile. Their engaging storytelling can even win over those who are initially afraid of reptiles.

The most unique part of the tour is the opportunity to touch some of the gentler reptiles, including pythons and lizards. The staff ensures safety and teaches the correct way to handle them. This close-up experience gave us a whole new understanding and appreciation for these creatures. The entire visit takes about 1.5 to 2 hours, making it perfect for families.

### Evening: Ubud Market Shopping

After leaving the reptile park, we headed to Ubud Market. This is the liveliest area in Ubud and the best place to buy Balinese handicrafts. The market offers a wide variety of goods, from traditional clothing, wood carvings, silver jewelry, woven bags to paintings. Prices here are negotiable, and it's recommended to start bargaining at about one-third of the seller's initial price. Enjoying the haggling process is part of the market experience. Hidden among the market's alleys are numerous cafes and small restaurants, perfect for taking a break with a cup of Balinese coffee and soaking in Ubud's relaxed atmosphere.

## Day 5: Exploring Kintamani Volcano and Traditional Villages

### Morning: Kintamani and Ritatkala Café

**Attraction & Restaurant Recommendation: Kintamani & Ritatkala Café**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3432f10b8a1acb758145140d33e88ac0.jpg)

Today, we drove to Bali's famous volcanic landscape area — Kintamani. Here, you can gaze upon the majestic views of Mount Batur and Lake Batur, making it a must-visit natural attraction in Bali.

We highly recommend selecting outdoor seating, facing the volcano and lake, enjoying your meal while marveling at nature's craftsmanship. The café's chicken wings are particularly recommended, with crispy skin and juicy meat, perfectly complemented by a special sauce. In the cool mountain climate, sipping hot coffee and gazing at the distant volcano creates an indescribable sense of tranquility.

**Attraction Recommendation: Penglipuran Village**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_534f807ca0ff379512580af984f596cc.jpg)

After leaving Mount Batur, we headed to Penglipuran Village, a well-preserved traditional Balinese village. The slow-paced lifestyle and warm hospitality here make it a perfect spot to experience traditional Balinese culture.

## Day 6: Exploring the Southern Coastline and Cliffside Views

### Morning: Poolside Relaxation at the Hotel

On our final morning in Ubud, we returned to the hotel's infinity pool to soak in the serene rice field views once more. Floating in the pool, gazing at the pastoral landscape, we felt a deep sense of peace and gratitude.

### Afternoon: Uluwatu Cliffside Drive

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a123f697405c668b110a8f2b12af913f.jpg)

After checking out, we drove to the Uluwatu area in southern Bali. Cruising along the cliffside coastal road, with the azure Indian Ocean on one side and rugged limestone cliffs on the other, the breathtaking scenery opened our hearts. Known as one of Bali's most beautiful coastlines, this route offers several scenic spots to stop and capture photos—don't miss them.

### Afternoon Tea at Roosterfish, Pandawa Beach

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4cfd7bde880edf48f6923248b34146d2.jpg)

We enjoyed afternoon tea at Roosterfish on Pandawa Beach. This restaurant boasts stunning ocean views right by the beach. Sipping on a cool drink, nibbling on light snacks, and sitting on the breezy terrace while watching the waves lap the shore made for a simple yet delightful afternoon. Pandawa Beach, with its crystal-clear waters and pristine white sand, is one of Bali's quieter beaches, perfect for travelers seeking to escape the crowds.

### Evening: Uluwatu Temple

**Attraction Recommendation: Uluwatu Temple ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_65cdfff2716506cd7615d0c7611c9c66.jpg)

As evening approached, we visited Uluwatu Temple, one of Bali's most significant religious sites perched on a cliff edge. This temple, standing 70 meters above the Indian Ocean, offers one of the best sunset views on the island. The temple's dramatic location overlooking the endless ocean is truly awe-inspiring.

**Important Reminder: The monkeys here are very mischievous and bold!** We witnessed firsthand a tourist's glasses being snatched by a monkey. After years of interacting with tourists, these monkeys have learned how to quickly grab items like glasses, hats, and sunglasses, then "demand" food in exchange. Therefore, when visiting, be sure to keep your belongings secure. Glasses can be secured with a strap, and try to avoid carrying shiny accessories or food to prevent attracting the monkeys' attention.

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Uluwatu Temple/Lover's Cliff Travel Guide: Must-See Highlights and Practical Tips](/blog/2025-01-18-678b1d81fd89780001f7fe4d/)

Despite the need to be cautious around monkeys, the view from Uluwatu Temple is truly breathtaking. As the sun sets, golden light spills across the sea, shimmering on the waves, and the temple appears even more sacred and majestic in the backlight. If time permits, stay to watch the traditional Kecak Fire Dance performance, enjoying this cultural feast of dance, music, and drama against the backdrop of the setting sun.

**Quick Reference:** [Kecak Fire Dance: Bali's Must-See Spectacular Performance (Includes Ticket Booking Info)](/blog/2024-01-29-65b6e533fd89780001f51eaf/)

## Day 7: Leisurely Time in Jimbaran and Shopping Spree

### Stay at Movenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran

**Accommodation Recommendation: Movenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran**

Today we arrived at the final hotel of our journey—Mövenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran. This is our second time staying at this resort. Perhaps because we are returning guests, we were lucky enough to be upgraded to a poolside room, where we could jump directly into the pool from our balcony. This convenience felt incredibly luxurious.

The resort boasts multiple pool facilities, including a children's splash area and a water volleyball court, and regularly hosts various water activities, making it perfect for families. The hotel's breakfast offers a rich and diverse selection, from Asian cuisine to Western dishes, all of excellent quality. The most delightful surprise is the daily free Chocolate Happy Hour in the afternoon, offering a variety of chocolate desserts and hot chocolate drinks—a paradise for dessert lovers!

Mövenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran is a luxurious 5-star resort in the Jimbaran area, offering a tranquil and romantic getaway option, ideal for families. The resort is located at the corner of Samasta Shopping Center, providing convenient access to amenities. The shopping center also houses a fantastic pork ribs restaurant! ### Afternoon: Krisna Shopping Spree

**Shopping Recommendation: Krisna**

In the afternoon, we headed to Krisna Souvenir Store for our final shopping frenzy. The duck is slow-roasted for hours, resulting in crispy skin and tender, flavorful meat, complemented by spices and side dishes, offering an authentic taste of Balinese cuisine. As our journey neared its end, enjoying this delicious Balinese meal filled us with both nostalgia and satisfaction.

### Important Reminders

### ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!

At monkey-populated attractions like Uluwatu Temple and the Sacred Monkey Forest, be extra cautious! We witnessed firsthand a tourist’s glasses being snatched by a monkey. These clever creatures know how to steal items and then "negotiate" for an exchange. Recommendations:

- Secure glasses or sunglasses with a strap
- Avoid carrying shiny accessories or food
- Ensure bags are fully zipped
- Maintain a safe distance from monkeys and avoid touching or feeding them

### ⚠️ Don't Underestimate Bali Belly!—Our Personal Nightmare

During this trip, my husband unfortunately experienced an extremely severe case of Bali Belly. The entire ordeal was terrifying, and I’m sharing the details here in hopes that it serves as a cautionary tale for anyone planning a trip to Bali.

**Night of Day 6: Symptoms Begin**

On our first night staying at the Movenpick Hotel, my husband suddenly complained of stomach pain and needed to use the restroom. That night, he made three trips to the bathroom and tossed and turned, barely getting any sleep. At the time, we thought it was just a mild case of traveler’s stomach, but little did we know, this was just the calm before the storm.

**Morning of Day 7: The Situation Takes a Turn for the Worse**

The next morning, my husband woke up with a splitting headache. When I touched his forehead, I was shocked—he was running a high fever! Shortly after, the diarrhea worsened, and he couldn’t leave the room to have breakfast. I had no choice but to take our child to the restaurant alone.

However, when we returned to the room after breakfast, I was completely horrified by what I saw. My husband was sitting weakly on the toilet, his upper body slumped over the sink. He told me that the intense stomach pain and constant need to use the restroom made him afraid to move even an inch away from the toilet. Our child, seeing him like this, thought he had fainted and burst into tears. At that moment, I panicked.

I immediately gave him the backup medication we had brought from Taiwan—painkillers, fever reducers, and anti-diarrheal pills—and let him rest. Two hours later, his fever had subsided, but the diarrhea showed no signs of improvement. Realizing the severity of the situation, I decided to seek help at a local pharmacy.

**Heading to the Pharmacy for Help**

At the pharmacy, I described my husband's symptoms to the pharmacist. They very professionally recommended two medications (shown in the photo) and explained in detail how to take them and what to watch out for. I quickly bought the medicine and returned to the hotel to give it to my husband.

In the afternoon, I took the kids to Krisna to shop for souvenirs. On one hand, I wanted to distract them from worrying too much about their dad; on the other hand, I didn't want to waste our precious vacation time. When we returned to the hotel and saw that my husband's condition had slightly improved, we felt a bit relieved.

**A Dinner Mistake**

In the evening, we went to Bebek Bengil near the hotel for dinner. My husband was very cautious, only daring to drink clear soup and eat plain rice, thinking this would be safe. But unexpectedly, after returning to the room, he started having diarrhea again. I was really worried and didn't know what to do, so I posted online in a community group for help. Thanks to the group's admin and kind members, who offered valuable advice and warm concern late at night, we felt less panicked in a foreign land.

**Day 8: The Difficult Journey Home**

On the morning of the eighth day, I brought some plain toast from the hotel restaurant for my husband, but he still had persistent diarrhea. I started to worry whether he could manage the flight back to Taipei, feeling very anxious. Fortunately, my husband said he could endure the pain as long as he could sit.

So, we nervously headed to the airport. On the plane, my husband didn't dare to eat the in-flight meal and endured abdominal discomfort throughout the flight. Finally, we landed safely in Taipei, but even after returning home, my husband continued to suffer from stomach pain and diarrhea.

**Medical Treatment and Recovery After Returning Home**

The next morning, we immediately went to the clinic. The doctor asked detailed questions about the symptoms and the course of the illness, prescribing anti-diarrheal medication and probiotics. After taking the medicine, the symptoms gradually began to ease, but there were still intermittent stomach pains and diarrhea.

During the recovery period, my husband only dared to eat plain toast. Once he tried drinking a bit of soup, but it caused diarrhea again, scaring him from trying any other foods. The entire illness lasted six days, and it wasn't until the sixth day that the diarrhea finally stopped completely.

**The Surprising 'Side Effect'**

This bout of Bali Belly caused my husband's weight to drop from 78 kg all the way down to 73 kg, a total loss of 5 kg! While this is definitely not a healthy way to lose weight, it can be considered a silver lining. My husband joked, 'When the stomach pain hit, I felt like I was about to give birth!'

**Cause Analysis and Reflection**

Afterwards, we carefully reviewed our entire trip. My child and I were fine, but only my husband fell ill. We speculated the possible reasons as follows:

1. **Ice Issue**: On the 6th day at noon, my husband bought a cup of freshly brewed iced coffee with lots of ice from Pepito supermarket. The source of ice in Bali is unclear, and it might have been the culprit.
2. **Hand Hygiene**: Men sometimes get lazy and don't wash their hands properly before meals. My child and I always make sure to wash our hands before eating. Coincidentally, that night we had Pizza, which requires eating with hands, increasing the risk of infection.
3. **Probiotic Protection**: During the 8-day trip, my child and I took gastrointestinal probiotics every morning, which might have protected our gut health to some extent. (Note: This is just personal speculation, not medical advice.)

It's worth mentioning that this was our second encounter with Bali Belly this year. In January, we stayed in Bali for 6 days, and all three of us experienced varying degrees of diarrhea, with our child even developing a fever. However, the symptoms were relatively mild back then, and we recovered quickly after taking medication. We didn't expect my husband's symptoms to be so severe this time, truly experiencing the power of Bali Belly.

**Advice for All Travelers**

After this painful experience, I would like to offer the following advice to friends planning to visit Bali:

1. **Prepare sufficient gastrointestinal medications before departure**: Including anti-diarrheal, fever reducers, painkillers, probiotics, and stomach medicine. Don't take chances; it's better to have them and not need them.
2. **Absolutely avoid drinks with ice**: This cannot be emphasized enough! Whether it's roadside stalls, freshly brewed drinks from supermarkets, or iced drinks at restaurants, avoid anything with ice. Also, ensure that bottled water is properly sealed.
3. **Practice hand hygiene before meals**: Wash hands thoroughly with soap or use alcohol-based hand sanitizer. Don't skip this step because it's troublesome.
4. **Consider taking probiotics daily**: Start taking them a few days before departure and continue throughout the trip, as it may help protect your gut.
5. **Be cautious with roadside stalls**: Pay attention to hygiene conditions. If the environment looks unclean, it's better to skip it.
6. **Don't tough it out if symptoms are severe**: If you experience high fever, severe dehydration, or persistent diarrhea, seek help from local pharmacies or go directly to a hospital. Don't wait until the next day like we did.
7. **Travel insurance is crucial**: Ensure that your insurance covers overseas medical expenses to alleviate the burden in case you need medical attention.
