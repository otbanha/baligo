This marks our second visit to Bali this year. From October 11th to 18th, we spent an unforgettable eight days and seven nights on this 'Island of the Gods.' We chose to return because we were deeply captivated by Bali's cultural charm, natural scenery, and authentic cuisine. During this trip, we stayed at three distinct hotels, each offering a unique experience—from the lively Kuta Beach area to the serene rice fields of Ubud, and the laid-back Jimbaran Bay. Each location left us with beautiful memories.

A special thanks to the group administrators and fellow travelers who offered advice and support when my husband suffered severe diarrhea during the trip. Your kindness helped us navigate the challenge (we’ll share how we overcame Bali Belly later). This warmth became one of the most precious takeaways from our journey.

## Day 1: Arrival in Bali, Beginning Our Tropical Island Adventure

### Arrival and Currency Exchange

After several hours of flying, we finally set foot on Bali. The tropical air outside the airport greeted us, carrying the faint scent of frangipani, instantly immersing us in the vacation vibe. After completing immigration procedures, our first stop was currency exchange. Rates are usually better at exchange offices in the city.

### Dinner at GOSHA x GOEMEROT

After settling into our accommodation, we eagerly headed to GOSHA x GOEMEROT for our first meal in Bali. This restaurant masterfully blends modern and traditional Balinese flavors, with beautifully presented dishes and authentic tastes. On our first night in this exotic destination, we savored the delicious food while anticipating the exciting itinerary awaiting us over the next seven days.

## Day 2: Tanah Lot Temple Tour and Leisurely Seminyak Time

### Morning: Tanah Lot Temple

After enjoying a hearty breakfast at the hotel, we drove to one of Bali's most iconic landmarks - Tanah Lot Temple. This 16th-century Hindu temple stands majestically on a giant rock in the sea, accessible by foot during low tide and surrounded by water during high tide, creating a picturesque scene. Standing on the shore, watching the waves crash against the rocks while the temple glistens in the sunlight, the sense of awe is indescribable. We recommend visiting in the afternoon when the sunset transforms Tanah Lot into a breathtaking spectacle.

**Quick Reference:** [Must-Visit Bali Attraction: Tanah Lot Temple Detailed Guide with Tide Information and Surrounding Attractions](/blog/2024-10-28-671f86f1fd89780001de9fae/)

### Noon: Exploring Seminyak Square

After leaving Tanah Lot, we arrived in the trendy Seminyak area. This district is filled with boutique shops, restaurants, and cafes, representing a more modern side of Bali. We strolled around Seminyak Square, enjoying a leisurely shopping experience. The area is dotted with designer boutiques offering unique clothing, accessories, and home decor, making it perfect for travelers seeking distinctive souvenirs.

**Restaurant Recommendation: ENDO Seminyak**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_57caee0de0ee79e29675903fef6d5f69.jpg)

For lunch, we dined at ENDO Seminyak near Seminyak Square, and it turned out to be a delightful surprise! Initially chosen at random, the restaurant exceeded our expectations with its exceptional food quality. We highly recommend their Bebek Betutu (Dirty Duck) and Mie Goreng (Fried Noodles). The duck was crispy on the outside and tender inside, perfectly seasoned with aromatic spices. The fried noodles were authentically Balinese, generously portioned and packed with flavorful ingredients.

### Dinner: WAHAHA

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4be51710a26107a6190d946dc32baa54.jpg)

As evening fell, we headed to WAHAHA for a pork rib dinner. Enjoying delicious food under Bali's warm night sky was the perfect way to end a fulfilling day.

## Day 3: Transitioning from Kuta to Ubud, Embracing a Different Side of Bali

### Morning: Poolside Relaxation

Today was all about taking it easy. After a leisurely breakfast at the hotel, we decided to make the most of the pool facilities. Playing in the water under Bali's sunshine truly embodies the essence of a vacation. Lounging by the pool on sunbeds, we soaked up the island's warmth and relaxed in style.

### Noon: Check-out and Massage

After checking out at noon, we headed to a nearby spa center to enjoy a traditional Balinese massage. Bali is renowned for its essential oil massages, and the therapists here are highly skilled with just the right touch. After over an hour of treatment, all the fatigue from our journey vanished, leaving us completely relaxed both physically and mentally.

### Afternoon: Massimo Italian Restaurant and Gelato

Post-massage, we had lunch at Massimo Italian Restaurant. This establishment is quite famous in Bali for its authentic Italian cuisine. The dishes were genuinely Italian, especially the handmade pasta and pizza, making us feel as though we were in Italy itself. After the meal, don't forget to try their gelato—creamy and smooth, it's the perfect dessert for the hot weather.

### Shopping at Grand Lucky Supermarket

**Shopping Recommendation: Grand Lucky Supermarket**

On our way to Ubud, we made a special detour to Grand Lucky Supermarket for some shopping. This supermarket is highly recommended by many tourists for souvenir purchases, offering transparent and fair prices without the worry of tourist markups. The supermarket boasts a wide variety of goods, from Balinese coffee, spices, snacks to daily necessities. We bought a lot of local specialties here to share with friends and family back in Taiwan.

## Day 4: Deep Exploration of Ubud, Experiencing the Essence of Balinese Culture

### Morning: Leisurely Hotel Time

On our first morning in Ubud, we chose to relax at the hotel, fully enjoying the privacy of our villa and the surrounding rural scenery. Sitting on the balcony, gazing at the rice fields while listening to the distant chirping of birds and insects, we found the peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle that we came to Ubud for.

### Lunch: Bebek Tepi Sawah

**Restaurant Recommendation: Bebek Tepi Sawah**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_18d5fd887059ea02f0b30f3666e5d864.jpg)

At noon, we headed to Bebek Tepi Sawah for lunch. The restaurant's name translates to 'Duck by the Rice Field', and it truly lives up to its name with its beautiful location beside rice paddies. Decorated in traditional Balinese style, the restaurant exudes authentic local charm. We chose outdoor seating, enjoying authentic Balinese cuisine while taking in the refreshing rural scenery. The gentle breeze carried the scent of rice fields, making this lunch a truly delightful experience. Their signature roast duck is a must-try - tender meat perfectly seasoned with spices.

### Afternoon: Bali Reptile Park

**Attraction Recommendation: Bali Reptile Park**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_be260f863fa3cf1d7f49877c02f31826.jpg)

After lunch, we headed to Bali Reptile Park, a must-visit destination for reptile enthusiasts! The admission ticket includes a full English guided tour by park staff, which is one of the park's standout features. The guides are highly professional and passionate, offering detailed and engaging explanations about the habits and characteristics of each reptile. Even those who might initially feel apprehensive about reptiles will find themselves captivated by their enthusiasm.

The most unique aspect was the opportunity to touch several docile reptiles during the tour, including pythons and lizards. The staff ensured safety and taught us the proper way to handle them. This hands-on experience gave us a whole new perspective and understanding of these creatures. The entire visit takes about 1.5 to 2 hours, making it perfect for families.

### Evening: Ubud Market Shopping

After leaving the reptile park, we headed to Ubud Market. This is the liveliest area in Ubud and the best place to buy Balinese handicrafts. The market offers a wide variety of goods, from traditional clothing, wood carvings, silver jewelry, woven bags to paintings. Prices here are negotiable, and it's recommended to start bargaining at about one-third of the vendor's initial price. Enjoying the haggling process is part of the market experience. Hidden among the market's alleys are numerous cafes and small eateries, perfect for taking a break with a cup of Balinese coffee and soaking in Ubud's relaxed atmosphere.

## Day 5: Exploring Kintamani Volcano & Traditional Villages

### Morning: Kintamani & Ritatkala Café

**Attraction & Restaurant Recommendation: Kintamani & Ritatkala Café**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_3432f10b8a1acb758145140d33e88ac0.jpg)

Today, we drove to Bali's famous volcanic landscape area—Kintamani. Here, you can admire the magnificent views of Mount Batur and Lake Batur, making it one of Bali's must-visit natural attractions.

We chose to dine at Ritatkala Café, a scenic coffee shop with an excellent vantage point. We highly recommend opting for outdoor seating, where you can face the volcano and lake while enjoying delicious food and marveling at nature's artistry. The café's chicken wings are particularly recommended—crispy on the outside, juicy on the inside, and perfectly complemented by a special sauce. Sipping hot coffee in the cool mountain air while gazing at the distant volcano creates an indescribable sense of tranquility.

### Afternoon: Penglipuran Village Traditional Village

**Attraction Recommendation: Penglipuran Village**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_534f807ca0ff379512580af984f596cc.jpg)

After leaving Kintamani, we headed to Penglipuran Village, a well-preserved traditional Balinese village. The uniform architectural style throughout the village fully showcases the traditional Balinese architectural aesthetics and cultural characteristics. What's most special here is that visitors can enter the homes for a tour, which was a dream come true for me, as I've always been curious about the interior structure of traditional Balinese houses!

The streets in the village are clean and tidy, lined with bamboo groves, making it feel exceptionally serene as you walk through. Sitting in the courtyard of a traditional Balinese house, feeling the breeze and tranquility, the relaxation was so complete that we didn't want to leave. The slow pace of life and the warm hospitality of the residents here make it an excellent place to experience traditional Balinese culture.

## Day 6: Exploring the Southern Coastline and Cliffside Views

### Morning: Pool Time at the Hotel

On our last morning in Ubud, we returned to the hotel's infinity pool to fully enjoy the view of the rice paddies. Floating in the pool, gazing at the pastoral scenery before us, our hearts were filled with peace and gratitude.

### Afternoon Checkout: Uluwatu Cliff Road

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a123f697405c668b110a8f2b12af913f.jpg)

After checking out, we drove to the Uluwatu area in southern Bali. Speeding along the cliffside coastal road, with the azure Indian Ocean on one side and steep limestone cliffs on the other, the magnificent scenery opened our hearts. This road is hailed as one of Bali's most beautiful coastlines, with several scenic spots along the way to stop and take photos—don't miss them.

### Afternoon: Pandawa Beach Roosterfish Afternoon Tea

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4cfd7bde880edf48f6923248b34146d2.jpg)

We enjoyed afternoon tea at Roosterfish restaurant on Pandawa Beach. Located right by the beach, the restaurant offers stunning ocean views. Sipping a cold drink, nibbling on light snacks, sitting on the breezy terrace watching the waves lap against the shore—this simple yet delightful afternoon was pure bliss. Pandawa Beach, with its clear waters and white sand, is one of Bali's more tranquil beaches, perfect for travelers seeking to escape the crowds.

### Evening: Uluwatu Temple

**Attraction Recommendation: Uluwatu Temple ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_65cdfff2716506cd7615d0c7611c9c66.jpg)

In the evening, we visited Uluwatu Temple, one of Bali's most important religious sites perched on a cliff edge and also one of the best spots to watch the sunset. The temple stands majestically on a 70-meter-high cliff, facing the endless expanse of the Indian Ocean.

**Important Reminder: The monkeys here are very mischievous and bold!** We witnessed firsthand a tourist's glasses being snatched by a monkey. These monkeys, having interacted with tourists for a long time, have learned how to quickly grab items like glasses, hats, and sunglasses, then 'demand' food in exchange. Therefore, please keep your belongings secure when visiting. Glasses can be secured with a strap, and try to avoid carrying shiny accessories or food to prevent attracting the monkeys' attention.

**Quick Reference:** [Bali Uluwatu Temple/Lover's Cliff Travel Guide: Must-See Highlights and Practical Tips](/blog/2025-01-18-678b1d81fd89780001f7fe4d/)

Although you need to be cautious of the monkeys, the view from Uluwatu Temple is truly breathtaking. As the sun sets, golden light spills across the ocean surface, creating a shimmering effect, and the temple appears even more sacred and majestic in the backlight. If time permits, stay to watch the traditional Kecak Fire Dance performance, a cultural feast of dance, music, and drama set against the backdrop of the sunset.

**Quick Reference:** [Kecak Fire Dance - A Must-See Spectacular Performance in Bali (with Ticket Booking Info)](/blog/2024-01-29-65b6e533fd89780001f51eaf/)

### Dinner: Ulu Cliffhouse

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_c2d5e6caeae53304208dc0744dfb4875.jpg)

In the evening, we dined at Ulu Cliffhouse, a restaurant also perched on the cliffside with stunning ocean views. Under the starry sky, listening to the sound of the waves while enjoying exquisite cuisine, it was the perfect way to end an exciting day.

Mövenpick Resort & Spa Jimbaran is a luxurious 5-star resort in the Jimbaran area, offering a serene and romantic getaway option perfect for families. ### Morning: Relaxing at the Hotel

Today, we decided to fully unwind and enjoy all the facilities the hotel has to offer. We spent the morning sunbathing by the pool, swimming, and occasionally sipping on refreshing drinks, completely immersing ourselves in vacation mode.

### Afternoon: Shopping Spree at Krisna

In the afternoon, we headed to Krisna Souvenir Shop for a final shopping frenzy. Krisna is Bali's largest chain of souvenir stores, offering an incredibly diverse range of products from food, clothing, accessories to home goods. With transparent and affordable pricing clearly marked, there's no need for haggling, making it one of the best places to shop for souvenirs.

We stocked up on plenty of coffee, cookies, chocolates, spices, and other local specialties to bring back and share with friends and family in Taiwan. Krisna has multiple branches, each quite spacious, so we recommend allocating at least 1-2 hours for shopping.

## Travel Reflections and Practical Tips

### Why We Fell in Love with Bali

This was our second visit to Bali this year, and by the end of the trip, we were already discussing the possibility of returning again. What draws us to Bali isn't just its stunning natural scenery, but also its unique cultural heritage, devout religious atmosphere, diverse culinary options, and the warm hospitality of the Balinese people.

### Important Reminders

### ⚠️ Beware of Monkeys!

At monkey-populated attractions like Uluwatu Temple and the Sacred Monkey Forest, extra caution is essential! We witnessed firsthand a tourist's glasses being snatched by a monkey. These clever creatures know how to negotiate exchanges after stealing items. Recommendations:

- Secure glasses and sunglasses with straps

- Avoid carrying shiny accessories or food

- Ensure bags are fully zipped

- Maintain a safe distance from monkeys; do not attempt to touch or feed them

On our first night at the Movenpick Hotel, my husband suddenly complained of stomach pain and needed to use the bathroom. That night, he made three trips to the toilet and tossed and turned all night, resulting in extremely poor sleep quality. At the time, we thought it was just a mild case of travel discomfort, but little did we know, this was just the calm before the storm.

**Morning of Day 7: A Sudden Turn for the Worse**

The next morning, my husband woke up with a splitting headache. When I touched his forehead, I was shocked—he was running a high fever! Shortly after, his diarrhea symptoms worsened, and he couldn't leave the room to have breakfast. I had no choice but to take our child to the restaurant first.

However, when we returned to the room after breakfast, the scene before me was utterly shocking. My husband was sitting weakly on the toilet, his upper body slumped over the sink. He told me that due to severe stomach pain and the constant need to use the bathroom, he didn't dare to leave the toilet. Our child, seeing Dad like this, thought he had fainted and burst into tears. At that moment, I was truly panicked.

I immediately took out the emergency medicine we had brought from Taiwan, gave him painkillers, fever reducers, and anti-diarrheal medication, and then let him rest. Two hours later, his fever had subsided, but the diarrhea showed no signs of improvement. Realizing the severity of the situation, I decided to seek help at a local pharmacy.

**Seeking Help at the Pharmacy**

At the pharmacy, I described my husband's symptoms to the pharmacist. The pharmacist very professionally recommended two medications (shown in the photo) and explained in detail how to take them and what to watch out for. I quickly bought the medicine and returned to the hotel to administer it to my husband.

**Day 8: The Challenging Journey Home**

On the morning of the eighth day, I packed some white toast from the hotel restaurant for my husband to eat, but he continued to suffer from diarrhea. I began to worry whether he would be able to board the flight back to Taiwan smoothly, feeling very anxious. Fortunately, my husband said that as long as he could sit, the pain was still within bearable limits.

And so, we nervously made our way to the airport. On the plane, my husband didn't dare to eat the in-flight meal, enduring abdominal discomfort throughout the entire flight. Finally, we landed safely in Taipei, but after returning home, my husband still experienced persistent abdominal pain and diarrhea.

**Medical Treatment and Recovery After Returning Home**

Early the next morning, we immediately went to the clinic. The doctor carefully inquired about the symptoms and the course of the illness, prescribing anti-diarrheal medication, probiotics, and other drugs. After taking the medication, the symptoms gradually began to ease, but there were still intermittent episodes of abdominal pain and diarrhea.

During the recovery period, my husband only dared to eat white toast. Once, he tried drinking a bit of soup, but it triggered diarrhea again, frightening him so much that he completely avoided trying other foods afterward. The entire illness lasted six days, and it wasn't until the sixth day that the diarrhea finally stopped completely.

**The Surprising 'Side Effect'**

This bout of Bali Belly caused my husband's weight to drop from 78 kg to 73 kg, a total loss of 5 kg! Although this is definitely not a healthy way to lose weight, it could be considered a blessing in disguise, right? My husband joked, 'When the stomach pain hit, I felt like I was about to give birth!'

**Cause Analysis and Reflection**

After carefully reflecting on our entire trip, my child and I remained safe and sound, while my husband unfortunately fell ill. We speculate the possible reasons are as follows:

1. **Ice Issue**: On the 6th day at noon, my husband bought a cup of freshly brewed iced coffee filled with ice cubes from Pepito Supermarket. The source of ice in Bali is unclear, and it’s highly likely that this was the culprit.
2. **Hand Hygiene**: Men sometimes skip thoroughly washing their hands before meals. My child and I, however, strictly adhere to handwashing before eating. Coincidentally, that evening we had pizza, which required eating with our hands, increasing the risk of infection.
3. **Probiotic Protection**: Throughout the 8-day trip, my child and I took gastrointestinal probiotics every morning, which might have helped protect our digestive health. (Note: This is purely personal speculation and not medical advice.)

It’s worth mentioning that this was our second encounter with Bali Belly this year. In January, we stayed in Bali for 6 days, and all three of us experienced varying degrees of diarrhea, with our child even running a fever. However, the symptoms were relatively mild back then, and we recovered quickly after taking medication. We didn’t expect my husband’s condition to be so severe this time, truly showcasing the power of Bali Belly.

**Advice for All Travelers**

After this painful experience, I’d like to offer the following advice to friends planning a trip to Bali:

1. **Prepare ample gastrointestinal medication before departure**: Include anti-diarrheal medicine, fever reducers, painkillers, probiotics, and stomach medicine. It’s better to be over-prepared than underprepared.
2. **Absolutely avoid drinks with ice cubes**: This cannot be emphasized enough! Avoid freshly made drinks from street vendors, supermarkets, or restaurants if they contain ice. Also, ensure bottled water is properly sealed.
3. **Practice thorough hand hygiene before meals**: Wash hands with soap or use alcohol-based hand sanitizer. Don’t skip this step out of convenience.
4. **Consider taking probiotics daily**: Start a few days before departure and continue throughout the trip to potentially protect your digestive system.
5. **Be cautious with street food**: Pay attention to hygiene conditions. If the environment looks unclean, it’s better to skip it.
6. **Don’t push through severe symptoms**: If you experience high fever, severe dehydration, or persistent diarrhea, seek immediate help from a local pharmacy or hospital. Don’t wait until the next day like we did.
7. **Travel insurance is crucial**: Ensure your policy covers overseas medical expenses to alleviate financial burdens in case of emergencies.
