Nusa Lembongan (also known as "Lembongan Island") and its neighboring islands—Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida—draw in a niche group of adventurous travelers with their pristine blue waters, relaxed coastal village atmosphere, and unique island experiences. However, these three islands near Bali have now become must-visit destinations for vacationers. Whether you opt for a day trip exploring Nusa Lembongan and Nusa Ceningan or a deeper two-day exploration of Nusa Penida, these islands will add endless color to your Bali getaway.

Nusa Lembongan is a small island located southeast of Bali, reachable by a 30-minute boat ride from Sanur Harbor. Covering just 8 square kilometers, the island's main economic activities are tourism and seaweed farming.

When visiting Nusa Lembongan, you'll experience a completely different, more relaxed atmosphere compared to the bustling beaches of Kuta, Seminyak, or Canggu. Here, you can choose to actively explore the island (island hopping) and participate in various activities, or simply unwind by the pool or at a restaurant at your accommodation. It's a great way to take a short break and recharge before returning to your daily routine.

## The Enchanting Attractions of Nusa Lembongan

Lembongan Island covers an area of approximately 6.15 square kilometers and can be reached by speedboat in just about 30 minutes. For travelers with limited time, it's an ideal choice as it can be fully explored in a single day, unlike Nusa Penida which requires at least two days to visit the main attractions on its east and west coasts. Lembongan is also the most developed of the three islands, offering a variety of resorts, restaurants, and beach clubs, making it easy to enjoy sunbathing on the beach.

Upon arriving at Lembongan Island, visitors are usually taken to the main areas of the island—Jungut Batu Beach or Mushroom Bay, which are home to numerous accommodations and restaurants. If you plan to stay overnight, it's essential to book your accommodation in advance. This way, you can start exploring as soon as you drop off your luggage.

## Beach Exploration

Heading south by bike, you'll discover some interesting beaches like Sandy Bay, Devil's Tear, and Dream Beach. Sandy Bay is the best spot for sunset viewing, so it's recommended to save it for last.

![Sandy Bay有個beach club](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_60e81c23058acdc6be657d2a801fb230.jpg)

Sandy Bay has a beach club

Devil's Tear, known in Chinese as 'Devil's Tear,' is a rocky cliff that meets the ocean. Standing here, you can enjoy a panoramic view of the ocean from above. When the timing is right, large waves crash against the rocks, creating a spectacular splash—a perfect Instagram photo spot. However, be cautious not to stand too close, as sudden large waves can be very powerful. There have been unfortunate incidents where people were swept into the sea, so always be careful!

Dream Beach is a popular beach outside the main area of the island and tends to be a bit crowded. A morning visit is more enjoyable. Stroll to the rocky area, where you can find interesting photo spots like rock formations or small caves. Additionally, there's a cliff diving spot here, but it's best to consult a local guide to ensure safety before diving.

## Mangrove Tour

Heading north, you'll arrive at the tranquil mangrove area of Nusa Lembongan. This area is usually less crowded than the southern regions, making it perfect for those who enjoy peace and quiet.

__VID0__

Upon arriving in Nusa Lembongan, you'll find various ways to explore this enchanting mangrove forest. You can rent a canoe and leisurely paddle through the serene waterways, soaking in the comfort of being surrounded by mangroves while listening to the natural symphony of bird calls and sea breezes. For a deeper understanding of this ecosystem, consider joining a small group guided boat tour.

Generally, when visitors arrive in Nusa Lembongan, locals enthusiastically recommend various mangrove boat tours. Opting for a small group private boat is advisable, typically accommodating 3-4 people with an experienced boatman. Negotiating the price to below IDR 100,000 per person is considered reasonable. During the half-hour journey, you can closely admire the spectacular views of the pristine mangrove forest, observe various waterbirds, and even spot crabs scuttling among the roots.

Special Tip: Visiting during **low tide** is ideal, as the mangrove roots are fully exposed, presenting the most captivating scenery. The boatman can also navigate through lower water levels, allowing you to delve deeper into this unique ecosystem.

Spending a leisurely afternoon at beachfront restaurants in Jungut Batu or Mushroom Bay is highly comfortable. There are many reasonably priced seaside restaurants here. Since these areas are also the main points of arrival and departure, with ports located here, you can observe the bustling activity throughout the day.

## Surfing

If you can't resist the allure of surfing, there are three popular surf spots near Jungut Batu Beach: **Playgrounds**, **Lacerations**, and **Shipwrecks**. You can find instructors and rental services at the surf center on Jungut Batu Beach.

## Best Choices for Diving and Snorkeling

For adventurers who love the underwater world, Nusa Lembongan and its neighboring islands Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida offer abundant diving and snorkeling spots. Mangrove Point is the closest snorkeling spot to the shore of Nusa Lembongan, perfect for travelers who want a quick glimpse of the underwater beauty. There are also multiple snorkeling and diving sites around Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, such as the famous Manta Point, where you can swim alongside giant and graceful manta rays—an unforgettable experience.

https://youtube.comhorts/a4mL69nASLY?si=UciZXsalBlU3fOjo

A half-day snorkeling trip in Nusa Lembongan, including hotel pickup, snorkeling equipment, boat fees, and three dive spots, costs approximately 150,000 IDR (USD$10) per person.

## Water Activities and Adventures

In addition to diving and snorkeling, Nusa Lembongan offers a variety of water activities to cater to all kinds of enthusiasts. You can try water skiing, stand-up paddleboarding (SUP), banana boat rides, or even experience the unique sensation of Seawalking. Additionally, you can join an island-hopping boat tour to admire the island's stunning scenery from different angles and enjoy close encounters with the ocean, feeling its charm.

## Transportation

The best way to explore Nusa Lembongan and Nusa Ceningan is by renting a bicycle or scooter. Like Nusa Penida, these islands do not have ride-hailing services like Gojek or Grab. If you prefer not to ride, another unique transportation option is the local "pick-up truck shuttle service."

![當地的 pick-up truck](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_38697b346c428fcbc1d2bff17b0e777d.jpg)

Local pick-up truck

Transportation costs in Nusa Lembongan:

- Scooter rental: 75,000～100,000 IDR per day ($4.6\~6.6 USD)
- Pick-up truck: 50,000 IDR one way ($3 USD)
- Private car: Options are limited and more expensive, costing 800,000 IDR for 8 hours ($51 USD)

Renting a scooter is not only cost-effective but also allows you to freely explore every corner of the island at your own pace. The pick-up truck shuttle service offers a fun local experience, especially suitable for those who are not comfortable riding or carrying a lot of luggage.

**Friendly reminder**: When renting a scooter, make sure the vehicle is in good condition and remember to get a receipt. If you choose the pick-up truck shuttle, it’s recommended to confirm the price and destination with the driver in advance to avoid misunderstandings.

## The Future Development of Nusa Lembongan

Nusa Lembongan is set to become Bali's most digitally advanced tourist destination. In 2024, the local government and business community launched the 'Nusa Lembongan Digital Island' initiative, aiming to enhance the tourist experience and boost local economic growth through digital payment systems. The project encompasses transportation, dining, accommodation, and retail, fully supporting cashless payments. Additionally, the Central Bank of Indonesia will provide relevant financial education to integrate digital technology into daily life. This initiative is expected to improve the quality of tourism in Nusa Lembongan, increase visitor stay durations, and promote the growth of local revenue (PAD) in the Klungkung Regency.

In the future, Nusa Lembongan will become Bali's most digitally connected tourist destination. Through the 'Nusa Lembongan Digital Island' initiative, the island will gradually implement a fully digital payment system, from booking ferry tickets and local transportation to purchasing attraction tickets, hotel stays, dining, and shopping. Travelers will find it easier to plan and arrange their itineraries. Moreover, the enhancement of digital infrastructure will address current signal blind spots, ensuring stable network connectivity across all areas. By driving these changes, Nusa Lembongan will emerge as a world-class tourist destination, extending visitor stays and integrating local communities into the digital economy, fostering regional economic growth and improved quality of life.

## Nusa Lembongan Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. Is Nusa Lembongan Worth Visiting?

Absolutely! Nusa Lembongan is beloved by visitors for its serene atmosphere, offering a quieter alternative to Bali with plenty of attractions to explore. Including nearby Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, you can easily plan a week-long trip. Nusa Lembongan boasts spectacular marine activities such as swimming with manta rays and cliff jumping. The island also features many beautiful beaches to enjoy.

### 2. **What is Nusa Lembongan famous for?**

Nusa Lembongan is known for its Yellow Bridge, seaweed farms, white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, stunning sunsets, captivating scenery, excellent cafes, and its proximity to Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida. These are the main highlights that attract visitors.

### 3. **Is it better to visit Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan?**

Both islands are worth visiting! However, if you can only choose one, Nusa Lembongan is more developed with better facilities and more activities. If time permits, it’s recommended to visit both islands to experience their unique charms.

### 4. **How long does the ferry from Bali to Nusa Lembongan take?**

The ferry ride is short, taking approximately 30 to 45 minutes from Bali to Nusa Lembongan.

### 5. **How many days should I spend in Nusa Lembongan?**

You can explore the main attractions of Nusa Lembongan in a day, but if you want to leisurely enjoy the island’s beauty, it’s recommended to stay for 3 days. If you also plan to explore Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, you can arrange for around 4 days. Staying at least one night on the island allows you to truly experience the breathtaking sunsets and relaxed island atmosphere.

### 6. **Is Nusa Lembongan suitable for family trips?**

Nusa Lembongan offers many family-friendly activities such as snorkeling, beach fun, and sunset watching, making it perfect for a trip with children. The island’s facilities are also relatively well-developed, making it suitable for family travel.

### 7. **Is Nusa Lembongan Suitable for Digital Nomads?**

Although Nusa Lembongan's internet connectivity is weaker compared to Bali, the digital infrastructure has been improving in recent years. Some areas now offer stable internet, making it suitable for short-term work or digital nomad lifestyles.

### 8. **What Are the Must-See Attractions in Nusa Lembongan?**

In addition to the Yellow Bridge and beautiful beaches, Nusa Lembongan's "Devil's Tears" is a famous natural attraction. The waves crashing against the cliffs create spectacular water sprays, making it a must-see.

### 9. **How to Get to Nusa Lembongan from Bali?**

You can take a ferry from Sanur Harbor in Bali to Nusa Lembongan. There are multiple ferries daily, making transportation very convenient.

### 10. **What Is the Best Time to Visit Nusa Lembongan?**

The dry season (April to October) is the best time to visit Nusa Lembongan. During this period, the weather is clear, and the sea is crystal-clear, perfect for outdoor activities like snorkeling and island hopping.

## Tips

1. **Book Transportation and Accommodation in Advance**: As Nusa Lembongan's tourist numbers grow yearly, it's advisable to book transportation and accommodation early to avoid last-minute difficulties.
2. **Carry Cash**: Although there are a few ATMs on the island, they may sometimes run out of cash. Carrying sufficient Indonesian Rupiah ensures a smooth trip.
3. **Use Sunscreen**: The island's sunlight is intense. Be sure to apply sunscreen and reapply frequently to avoid sunburn.
4. **Follow Environmental Rules**: Protect the marine environment by not littering and adhering to local environmental regulations, preserving this beautiful tropical paradise.
