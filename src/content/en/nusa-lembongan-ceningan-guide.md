Nusa Lembongan (also known as Lembongan Island) and its neighboring islands—Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida—attract a more niche crowd with their crystal-clear turquoise waters, laid-back coastal village atmosphere, and unique island adventure experiences. But now, these three islands off the coast of Bali have become must-visit destinations for any vacation. Whether you choose a day trip to Nusa Lembongan and Nusa Ceningan or a two-day deep dive into Nusa Penida, these islands will add endless color to your Bali getaway.

Nusa Lembongan is an island located southeast of Bali, just a 30-minute boat ride from Sanur Harbor. Covering only 8 square kilometers, its main economic activities are tourism and seaweed farming.

Arriving on Nusa Lembongan, you'll feel a completely different, relaxed atmosphere compared to the bustling beaches of Kuta, Seminyak, or Canggu. Here, you can choose to actively explore the island (island hopping) and join activities, or simply unwind by your accommodation's pool or at a restaurant. It's a great way to take a short break and recharge, allowing you to fully relax before returning to daily life.

## The Enchanting Spots of Nusa Lembongan

Spanning just 6.15 square kilometers, Nusa Lembongan is only a 30-minute speedboat ride away. For travelers short on time, it's an ideal choice—you can explore it in a single day. In contrast, Nusa Penida requires at least two days to cover the main attractions on its east and west coasts. As the most developed of the three islands, Lembongan boasts a variety of resorts, restaurants, and beach clubs, making it easy to relax and soak up the sun on the sand.

Upon arriving on Nusa Lembongan, visitors are typically dropped off at the island's main areas—Jungut Batu Beach or Mushroom Bay—which are lined with accommodations and eateries. If you plan to stay overnight, booking ahead is a must. That way, you can drop your bags and start exploring right away.

## Beach Exploration

Heading south on a scooter, you'll discover some intriguing beaches like Sandy Bay, Devil's Tear, and Dream Beach. Sandy Bay is the best spot for sunset, so save it for last.

![Sandy Bay有個beach club](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_60e81c23058acdc6be657d2a801fb230.jpg)

Sandy Bay has a beach club

Devil's Tear, known in Chinese as 'The Devil's Tear,' is a rocky cliff that meets the ocean. Standing here, you can take in sweeping panoramic sea views from above. When the timing is right, big waves crash against the rocks, sending up spray—a perfect Instagram photo op. But be careful not to get too close, as sudden large waves can be powerful; there have been several tragic incidents where people were swept into the sea. Stay safe!

Wander over to the rocky area, where you'll find interesting photo spots like rock formations or small caves. There's also a cliff jumping point here, but it's best to ask a local guide to ensure it's safe.

## Mangrove Tour

Heading north, you'll reach the tranquil mangrove area of Lembongan Island. It's generally less crowded than the southern part, making it a perfect spot for those seeking peace and quiet.

https://youtube.com/shorts/yerwXithouw?si=nDV7gK4eyEVeKb16

Once you arrive on Lembongan Island, you'll find several ways to explore this enchanting mangrove forest. You can rent a kayak and paddle leisurely through the calm waterways, soaking in the serene atmosphere surrounded by mangroves while listening to the natural symphony of birdsong and sea breezes. For a deeper understanding of this ecosystem, consider joining a small-group guided boat tour.

Typically, when visitors reach Lembongan, locals will enthusiastically recommend various mangrove boat tours. It's best to opt for a small private boat, which usually accommodates 3-4 people and comes with an experienced boatman. A reasonable price is around 100,000 Indonesian Rupiah per person or less after bargaining. During the half-hour journey, you can get up close to the stunning views of the pristine mangrove forest, spot various water birds, and even catch crabs scuttling among the roots.

A special tip: aim to visit during **low tide**, as the mangrove roots are fully exposed above the water, creating the most captivating scenery. The boatman can also navigate through shallower waters, allowing you to observe this unique ecosystem up close.

## A Leisurely Day

Spending a leisurely afternoon at a beachfront restaurant in the Jungut Batu or Mushroom Bay area is the most comfortable way to relax. There are plenty of reasonably priced seaside eateries here. Since these are also the main arrival and departure points, with the harbor located in both areas, you can observe the bustling comings and goings of people throughout the day.

![悠閒的一天](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a1f56eb2c4193e81b53d33fffa1b1f7c.jpg)

At sunset, Jungut Batu is a great spot to watch the sun go down. If you spend the whole afternoon here, transitioning from the busy activity to complete tranquility is a simple yet pure delight.

For a different perspective on the sunset, take a short hike to Panorama Point to admire the beautiful view from above.

## Surfing

If you can't resist the allure of surfing, there are three popular surf spots near Jungut Batu Beach: **Playgrounds**, **Lacerations**, and **Shipwrecks**. You can find guidance and rental services at the surf center on Jungut Batu Beach.

## Top Spots for Diving and Snorkeling

For adventurers who love the underwater world, Nusa Lembongan and its neighboring islands, Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, offer a wealth of diving and snorkeling sites. Mangrove Point is the closest snorkeling spot to the shore of Nusa Lembongan, perfect for travelers who want a quick glimpse of the underwater beauty. Around Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, there are also numerous snorkeling and diving locations, such as the famous Manta Point, where you can swim alongside graceful manta rays—an unforgettable experience.

https://youtube.com/shorts/a4mL69nASLY?si=UciZXsalBlU3fOjo

A half-day snorkeling trip on Nusa Lembongan, including hotel pickup, snorkeling gear, boat fees, and three dive spots, costs around 150,000 Indonesian rupiah (USD$10) per person.

## Water Sports and Adventures

Beyond diving and snorkeling, Nusa Lembongan offers a variety of water activities to suit every enthusiast. You can try waterskiing, stand-up paddleboarding (SUP), banana boat rides, or even the unique experience of seawalking. You can also join an island-hopping boat tour to admire the island's stunning scenery from different angles, enjoying close contact with the water and the charm of the ocean.

## Getting Around

The best way to explore Nusa Lembongan and Nusa Ceningan is by bicycle or rented scooter. Like Nusa Penida, these islands don't have ride-hailing services such as Gojek or Grab. If you'd rather not ride, another unique local transport option is the modified pickup truck shuttle service.

![當地的 pick-up truck](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_38697b346c428fcbc1d2bff17b0e777d.jpg)

Local pickup truck

Transportation costs on Nusa Lembongan:

- Scooter rental: 75,000–100,000 IDR per day (150–200 TWD / $5–$6 USD)
- Pickup truck: 50,000 IDR one way (100 TWD / $3 USD)
- Private car charter: Limited options and more expensive, around 800,000 IDR for 8 hours (1,600 TWD / $51 USD)

Renting a scooter is not only budget-friendly but also gives you the freedom to explore every corner of the island at your own pace. The pickup truck shuttle, on the other hand, offers a fun local experience, especially for those not comfortable riding a scooter or traveling with more luggage.

**Friendly reminder**: When renting a scooter, make sure to check its condition and ask for a receipt. If you choose the pickup truck shuttle, it's a good idea to ask about the price in advance and confirm the destination with the driver to avoid any miscommunication.

## The Future of Lembongan Island

Lembongan is set to become Bali's most digitally advanced tourist destination. In 2024, local government and businesses launched the 'Lembongan Digital Island' initiative, aiming to enhance the travel experience and boost the local economy through digital payment systems. The plan covers transportation, dining, accommodation, and shops, fully supporting cashless payments. Additionally, Bank Indonesia will provide financial education to integrate digital technology into daily life. This project is expected to improve tourism quality on Lembongan, increase visitor stay duration, and boost the original income (PAD) of the Klungkung regency.

In the future, Lembongan will become Bali's most digitally connected tourist destination. Through the 'Lembongan Digital Island' initiative, the island will gradually implement a fully digital payment system—from booking ferry tickets and island transport to attraction entry, hotels, restaurants, and shops—making it easier for travelers to plan and organize their trips. Moreover, strengthening digital infrastructure will address current signal dead zones, ensuring stable network connectivity across all areas. By driving these changes, Lembongan will become a world-class travel destination, extending visitor stays and integrating the local community into the digital economy, boosting economic growth and quality of life across the region.

## Lembongan FAQ

### 1. **Is Lembongan worth visiting?**

Absolutely worth it! Nusa Lembongan is beloved by travelers for its peaceful atmosphere. Compared to Bali, it's much quieter and has plenty of spots worth exploring. If you add nearby Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, you can easily fill a week-long itinerary. Nusa Lembongan offers spectacular ocean activities like swimming with manta rays and cliff jumping. The island also has many beautiful beaches to enjoy.

### 2. **What is Nusa Lembongan known for?**

Nusa Lembongan's highlights include the Yellow Bridge, seaweed farms, white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, stunning sunsets, breathtaking scenery, excellent cafes, and the nearby islands of Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida. These are all major draws for visitors.

### 3. **Is it better to visit Nusa Penida or Nusa Lembongan?**

Both islands are worth visiting! But if you can only choose one, Nusa Lembongan is more developed, with better facilities and more activities. If time allows, I recommend visiting both to experience their unique charms.

### 4. **How long is the ferry from Bali to Nusa Lembongan?**

The ferry ride is quite short—it takes about 30 to 45 minutes from Bali to Nusa Lembongan.

### 5. **How many days should I stay on Nusa Lembongan?**

You can see the main sights of Nusa Lembongan in a day, but if you want to take your time and soak in the island's beauty, I suggest staying 3 days. If you also want to explore Nusa Ceningan and Nusa Penida, plan for around 4 days. Spending at least one night on the island is essential to truly experience the stunning sunsets and laid-back island vibe.

### 6. **Is Nusa Lembongan suitable for a family trip?**

Lembongan Island offers plenty of family-friendly activities, such as snorkeling, playing on the beach, and watching the sunset, making it a great choice for a trip with kids. The island's facilities are also relatively well-developed, ideal for family vacations.

### 7. **Is Lembongan Island Suitable for Digital Nomads?**

Although the internet connection on Lembongan Island is weaker than in Bali, digital infrastructure has been improving in recent years. Some areas now have stable internet, making it suitable for short-term work or a digital nomad lifestyle.

### 8. **What Are the Must-See Attractions on Lembongan Island?**

Beyond the Yellow Bridge and beautiful beaches, Devil's Tears is a famous natural attraction on Lembongan Island. Waves crashing against the cliffs create spectacular sprays of mist, making it a must-see sight.

### 9. **How Do You Get from Bali to Lembongan Island?**

You can take a ferry from Sanur port in Bali to reach Lembongan Island. Multiple ferries run daily, making transportation very convenient.

### 10. **What Is the Best Time to Visit Lembongan Island?**

The dry season (April to October) is the best time to visit Lembongan Island. During this period, the weather is clear and the seawater is crystal clear, perfect for outdoor activities like snorkeling and island hopping.

## Tips

1. **Book Transportation and Accommodation in Advance**: As the number of visitors to Nusa Lembongan grows each year, it's wise to book your transportation and accommodation ahead of time to avoid last-minute hassles.
2. **Bring Cash**: While there are a few ATMs on the island, they sometimes run out of cash. Carrying enough Indonesian rupiah will ensure a smooth trip.
3. **Protect Yourself from the Sun**: The sun on the island is intense, so be sure to apply sunscreen and reapply regularly to avoid sunburn.
4. **Follow Eco-Friendly Rules**: Please protect the marine environment by not littering and adhering to local eco-friendly guidelines to preserve this beautiful tropical paradise.
