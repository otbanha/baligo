### Paramount Pictures Ventures into Bali: Kerthi Bali Semesta Park Set to Open in 2026

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e1514a7e3f77a9ddd98d7ba1560bd96.jpg)

Bali is entering a new era of tourism. The Kerthi Bali Semesta Park (KBS Park) project, located in the Jembrana Regency of West Bali, is now in its main construction phase and is expected to officially open in 2026. This theme park is a collaboration between Paramount Pictures and Indonesian company PT Kios Ria Kreas (KIOS), aiming to merge international entertainment brands with Balinese culture, making it Indonesia's first integrated park combining film and culture.

---

### Park Theme: A Perfect Blend of Hollywood Entertainment and Balinese Legends

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2bdc5bdefc0003786f5f2c91e844831b.jpg)

Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is designed with two main themed areas:

- **International Film and Entertainment Zone**: Showcases iconic characters and brands from Paramount, such as Nickelodeon's SpongeBob SquarePants, Dora the Explorer, and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, offering immersive experiences and family-friendly facilities.

- **Balinese Mythology and Culture Zone**: Inspired by Balinese legends like Barong and Rangda, this area uses technology and live performances to bring island myths to life, allowing visitors to experience both the thrill of Hollywood and the depth of Balinese culture.

---

Additionally, the park will feature a **Craft and Cultural Education Center**, offering workshops in traditional dance, handicrafts, and music, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in Bali's cultural essence.

### Sustainability and Eco-Design: Creating a Green Tourism Landmark

- ****Solar Power Systems****
- ****Rainwater Harvesting and Reuse Technology****
- ****Electric Vehicles****
- ****Ecological Green Zones and Conservation Areas****

Certain areas within the park will be designated as ****Green Zones****, preserving rare flora and fauna ecosystems, creating an environment where entertainment and nature coexist.

Jembrana Regent ****Kembang Hartawan**** stated: "Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is a symbol of new economy that combines tradition and innovation. We hope Jembrana becomes a model for sustainable development."

---

## ****Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway: The Lifeline Connecting Future Tourism****

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d34afd400c1c24665aedfd62011a78c5.jpg)

To support the massive tourism demand and infrastructure, the Indonesian government is simultaneously advancing the ****Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway (part of the Jagat Kerthi Bali Project)****, with over ****20% of preliminary work**** already completed.

This ****96.8-kilometer-long**** highway will connect ****Gilimanuk Port**** with the ****Mengwi area****, effectively linking West Bali with South Bali's main tourism belt. Upon completion, the travel time from Denpasar to Jembrana will be reduced from 4-5 hours to ****approximately 2 hours****, making it more convenient for visitors to access the park and surrounding areas.

Along the route, facilities will include:

- ****Rest stops and viewing platforms****
- ****Themed pavilions showcasing Balinese mythology****
- ****Electric vehicle charging stations and renewable energy facilities****

Additionally, the highway construction will also promote the ****upgrading of Gilimanuk Port****, creating new waterfront walkways and docks, connecting Bali with Java, and strengthening tourism and logistics links.

---

## ****Economic and Social Benefits: Transforming Jembrana's Future****

The Indonesian Ministry of Tourism estimates that after the opening of Kerthi Bali Semesta Park, Jembrana will attract up to 5 million visitors annually, creating over 25,000 jobs across various sectors including tourism, services, transportation, environmental management, and cultural education.

In conjunction with the park's development, Jembrana will also add:

- Two five-star hotels and resorts
- A golf course and sports academy
- The Gilimanuk waterfront promenade and new marina

This will not only boost the local tourism economy but also significantly improve residents' quality of life and upgrade infrastructure.

---

## Jembrana's New Role: A Model for Sustainable Tourism in Bali

Long considered one of Bali's least developed regions, Jembrana is set to transform into a tourism hub where culture, technology, and ecology thrive, thanks to the dual progress of Kerthi Bali Semesta Park and the Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway.

Kembang Hartawan emphasized: 'We hope this project is not just about construction but also a spiritual renewal. We aim for Jembrana to become a model where tradition and modernity coexist, and culture and nature progress together.'
