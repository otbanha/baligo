## **Paramount Pictures Enters Bali｜Kerthi Bali Semesta Park Set to Open in 2026**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e1514a7e3f77a9ddd98d7ba1560bd96.jpg)

Bali is stepping into a new era of tourism. Located in the **Jembrana Regency** of West Bali, the **Kerthi Bali Semesta Park (KBS Park)** project is now in its main construction phase and is expected to **officially open in 2026**. This theme park is a collaboration between **Paramount Pictures** and **Indonesian company PT Kios Ria Kreas (KIOS)**, aiming to merge international entertainment brands with Balinese culture, making it Indonesia's first integrated film and cultural theme park.

***

## **Park Theme: Hollywood Entertainment × Balinese Legends Perfectly Blended**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2bdc5bdefc0003786f5f2c91e844831b.jpg)

Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is designed with two main themed areas:

- **International Film and Entertainment Zone**: Showcasing iconic characters and brands under Paramount, such as **SpongeBob**, **Dora**, and **Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles** from **Nickelodeon**, creating immersive experiences and family-friendly facilities.
- **Balinese Mythology and Culture Zone**: Inspired by Balinese legends like **Barong and Rangda**, this area uses technology and stage performances to bring island myths to life, allowing visitors to experience both Hollywood thrills and the depth of Balinese culture.

Additionally, the park will feature a **Craft and Cultural Education Center**, offering workshops in traditional dance, handicrafts, and music, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the essence of Balinese culture.

***

## **Sustainability and Eco-Design: Creating a Green Tourism Landmark**

Kerthi Bali Semesta Park will be Bali's first theme park built around the core concept of **sustainable development**. The entire park will incorporate:

- **Solar Power Systems**
- **Rainwater Harvesting and Recycling Technology**
- **Electric Transportation Vehicles**
- **Ecological Green Zone Conservation Areas**

Parts of the park will be designated as **Green Zones**, preserving rare flora and fauna ecosystems, creating an environment where entertainment coexists with nature.

Jembrana Regent **Kembang Hartawan** stated: "Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is a symbol of new economy that combines tradition and innovation. We hope Jembrana becomes a model for sustainable development."

***

## **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway: The Lifeline Connecting Future Tourism**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d34afd400c1c24665aedfd62011a78c5.jpg)

To support the massive tourism demand and infrastructure, the Indonesian government is simultaneously advancing the **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway (part of the Jagat Kerthi Bali Project)**, with **over 20% of preliminary work** already completed.

This **96.8-kilometer-long** highway will connect **Gilimanuk Port** with the **Mengwi area**, effectively linking West Bali and South Bali's main tourism zones. Upon completion, travel time from Denpasar to Jembrana will be reduced from 4-5 hours to **approximately 2 hours**, making it more convenient for visitors to reach the park and surrounding areas.

Along the route, there will be:

- **Rest stops and viewing platforms**
- **Pavilions showcasing Balinese mythological stories**
- **Electric vehicle charging stations and renewable energy facilities**

Additionally, the highway construction will also promote **upgrades to Gilimanuk Port**, creating new waterfront promenades and docks, connecting Bali with Java, and strengthening tourism and logistics links.

***

## **Economic and Social Benefits: Transforming Jembrana's Future**

The Indonesian Ministry of Tourism estimates that after the opening of Kerthi Bali Semesta Park, **Jembrana could attract up to 5 million visitors annually**, creating **over 25,000 job opportunities** across various sectors including tourism, services, transportation, environmental management, and cultural education.

Simultaneously, in line with the park's development, Jembrana will also add:

- **Two five-star hotels and resorts**
- **A golf course and sports academy**
- **Gilimanuk waterfront promenade and a new pier**

This will not only boost the local tourism economy but also drive a comprehensive upgrade in residents' quality of life and infrastructure.

***

## **Jembrana's New Role: A Model for Sustainable Tourism in Bali**

Jembrana has long been considered one of Bali's slowest-developing regions, but with the dual advancement of **Kerthi Bali Semesta Park** and the **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway**, the area will transform into a **tourist hub where culture, technology, and ecology thrive together**.

Kembang Hartawan emphasized, "We hope this project is not just about construction but also a spiritual renewal. Let Jembrana become a model where tradition and modernity coexist, and culture and nature progress together."

***

## **FAQ**

**1. When will Kerthi Bali Semesta Park open?**

Officials expect it to **officially open in 2026**, with construction progressing steadily.

**2. What is the completion timeline for the Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway?**

As of now, approximately 20% of the preliminary work has been completed, with the entire project expected to be finished around 2026, coinciding with the opening of the theme park.

**3. What are the unique themes within the park?**

The park combines Paramount Pictures' cinematic characters with Balinese mythological culture, and includes educational experiences and ecological conservation areas.

**4. How will this project benefit local residents?**

It will create approximately 25,000 jobs, improving transportation, environmental conditions, and the local economy.

**5. How to get to the park from Denpasar?**

In the future, visitors can take the Gilimanuk-Mengwi Expressway directly to Jembrana, with a travel time of just about two hours.

**5. What other must-visit parks are there in Bali?**

In addition to this project, [Waterbom Bali](/blog/2025-07-28-68875c61fd8978000143d895/), [Bali Safari Park](/blog/2023-07-25-64db6b8cfd897800013a9ab1/), and [Bali Bird Park](/blog/2025-10-25-68fc1e0cfd89780001736727/) are all popular attractions not to be missed.
