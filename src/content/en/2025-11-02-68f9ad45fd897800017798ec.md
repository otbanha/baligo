## **Paramount Pictures Expands to Bali: Kerthi Bali Semesta Park Set to Open in 2026**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_6e1514a7e3f77a9ddd98d7ba1560bd96.jpg)

Bali is entering a new era of tourism. The **Kerthi Bali Semesta Park (KBS Park)** project, located in the **Jembrana Regency** of West Bali, has entered its main construction phase and is expected to **officially open in 2026**. This theme park, a collaboration between **Paramount Pictures** and **Indonesian company PT Kios Ria Kreas (KIOS)**, aims to combine international entertainment brands with Balinese culture, becoming Indonesia's first integrated park that merges film and culture.

## **Park Theme: Hollywood Entertainment × Balinese Legends**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2bdc5bdefc0003786f5f2c91e844831b.jpg)

Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is designed with two main themed areas:

- **International Film and Entertainment Zone**: Showcasing Paramount's iconic characters and brands, such as **SpongeBob SquarePants**, **Dora the Explorer**, and **Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles** from **Nickelodeon**, creating immersive experiences and family-friendly facilities.
- **Balinese Mythology and Culture Zone**: Inspired by Balinese legends like **Barong and Rangda**, using technology and stage performances to recreate island myths, allowing visitors to experience both the excitement of Hollywood and the depth of Balinese culture.

Additionally, the park will feature a **Craft and Cultural Education Center**, offering workshops in traditional dance, handicrafts, and music, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the essence of Balinese culture.

## **Sustainability and Eco-Design: Creating a Green Tourism Landmark**

Kerthi Bali Semesta Park will be Bali's first theme park built around the core concept of **sustainability**. The entire park will incorporate:

- **Solar power systems**
- **Rainwater harvesting and reuse technology**
- **Electric transportation vehicles**
- **Ecological green zones and conservation areas**

Parts of the park will be designated as **Green Zones**, preserving rare flora and fauna, creating an environment where entertainment coexists with nature.

Jembrana Regent **Kembang Hartawan** stated, "Kerthi Bali Semesta Park is a symbol of new economy that combines tradition and innovation. We hope Jembrana becomes a model for sustainable development."

## **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway: The Lifeline Connecting Bali's Future**

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d34afd400c1c24665aedfd62011a78c5.jpg)

To support the massive tourism demand and infrastructure development, the Indonesian government is simultaneously advancing the **Gilimanuk-Mengwi Highway (part of the Jagat Kerthi Bali Project)**, with **over 20% of preliminary work already completed**.

This **96.8-kilometer-long** highway will connect **Gilimanuk Port** with the **Mengwi area**, effectively linking West Bali with South Bali's main tourism zones. Once completed, the travel time from Denpasar to Jembrana will be reduced from 4-5 hours to **approximately 2 hours**, making it more convenient for tourists to explore the island and its surrounding areas.

Along the route, facilities will include:

- **Rest stops and viewing platforms**
- **Pavilions showcasing Balinese mythology**
- **Electric vehicle charging stations and renewable energy facilities**

Additionally, the highway's construction will drive the **upgrading of Gilimanuk Port**, creating new waterfront promenades and docks to enhance connectivity between Bali and Java, strengthening both tourism and logistics.

## **Economic and Social Benefits: Transforming Jembrana's Future**

The Indonesian Ministry of Tourism estimates that after the opening of Kerthi Bali Semesta Park, **Jembrana could attract up to 5 million tourists annually**, generating **over 25,000 jobs** across various sectors, including tourism, services, transportation, environmental management, and cultural education.

In conjunction with the park's development, Jembrana will also add:

- **Two five-star hotels and resorts**
- **Golf courses and sports academies**
- **Gilimanuk waterfront promenade and new docks**

This will not only boost the local tourism economy but also significantly improve residents' quality of life and upgrade infrastructure.

## **FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions**

**1. When will Kerthi Bali Semesta Park open?**

The official opening is scheduled for **2026**, with construction progressing steadily.

**2. What is the completion timeline for the Gilimanuk-Mengwi Expressway?**

As of now, approximately 20% of the preliminary work has been completed, with the project expected to be finished around 2026, coinciding with the opening of the theme park.

**3. What are the unique themes within the park?**

The park integrates characters from Paramount Pictures with Balinese mythological culture, and includes educational experiences and ecological conservation areas.

**5. How to get to the park from Denpasar?**
