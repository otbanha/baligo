## Are Gojek/Grab and Other Ride-Hailing Apps Available?

Currently, Nusa Penida does not have Grab or Gojek services, and it's unlikely they'll arrive anytime soon. Due to the island's small size and emerging tourism industry, ride-hailing apps like Gojek or Grab are not available or usable on Nusa Penida.

## Are There Many Taxis?

Taxis can be found at the port where you disembark, but elsewhere on the island—especially in more remote areas—they are hard to come by.

## How to Get from the Port to Your Hotel?

The best way to get from the port to your hotel is to arrange a transfer with your accommodation in advance. If your stay doesn't offer this service, you can take a taxi from the port. Taxis are pricey (a 10-minute ride costs around 100 IDR, about USD$6), but with limited options, bargaining is tough.

## Can I Rent a Scooter?

There are plenty of scooter rental operators on Nusa Penida, but due to the island's dangerous roads—steep hills, narrow lanes, increasing traffic from tourists, extreme heat, countless potholes, and GPS that may fail midway—this option is not recommended. Safety first on your travels!

## Can I Rent a Car Myself?

Renting a car and driving yourself around Nusa Penida is hardly possible! The roads are narrow and tricky to navigate—anyone who's been there will agree! Even experienced drivers find it challenging. A far more common approach is to hire a car with a driver (private charter), making getting around much easier. Since hiring someone to tour the island is very affordable, there's really no reason to rent a car on your own.

## Private Car Charter on Nusa Penida

The vast majority of travelers agree that hiring a private car is the best way to explore Nusa Penida. Not only do you get to tour comfortably in an air-conditioned vehicle, but you also have a driver-guide who introduces you to each destination and offers tips along the way.

You can leave beach gear, drinks, or spare clothes in the car, and the driver will keep everything safe for you. Plus, the driver knows the fastest routes between places and can tell you what you can realistically see in a day.

On Nusa Penida, a **simple private car charter** costs around IDR 700,000 to IDR 800,000 per day. This includes fuel and parking fees, but not entrance fees or tips.

Speaking of tips, giving a tip shows your appreciation for the driver and is highly appreciated. If you're satisfied with the experience, a tip of 10% to 20% is appropriate—and of course, if you can afford it, more is always welcome!
