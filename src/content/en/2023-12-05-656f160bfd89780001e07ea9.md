## **Bali Travel: On-Arrival Visa or E-Visa?**

Bali, Indonesia's most famous tourist destination, attracts visitors from around the world each year. For foreign travelers from non-ASEAN countries, entering Bali requires either an on-arrival visa or a pre-arranged e-visa. So, which option is better?

### **On-Arrival Visa**

The on-arrival visa is obtained at Bali's international airport upon arrival. The visa fee is IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$32), valid for 30 days and extendable once for a maximum stay of 60 days.

The advantage of the on-arrival visa is its simplicity. Upon arrival at Bali's international airport, travelers need only present a passport valid for at least six months, a return ticket or onward ticket to another country, and payment of IDR 500,000 in cash (specific currencies accepted, [see here](/blog/2023-01-13-64db6b7dfd897800013a978a/)) or by credit card (accepted card types [see here](/blog/2023-01-13-64db6b7dfd897800013a978a/)).

However, the downside is the unpredictable processing time, which could be quick or take several hours of waiting in line. Some travelers report that "applying for an e-visa is too troublesome, while getting an on-arrival visa at immigration only takes about ten minutes." However, **this is a matter of luck**! Others have spent over an hour waiting in line for their on-arrival visa. Those who get through quickly are indeed fortunate!

If your flight arrives simultaneously with European or American flights, most of these passengers won't have e-visas, leading to long queues at the visa payment and issuance counters. After getting the visa, you'll need to queue again for passport stamping at immigration. Whether you want to spend your precious vacation time this way is a personal decision!

### **Electronic Visa**

The electronic visa is an online visa application processed through the official website of Indonesia's Immigration Directorate General before departure. The application fee for the electronic visa is IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$32), valid for 30 days and extendable once, allowing a maximum stay of 60 days. However, additional processing fees apply for credit card payments. [See here for details](/blog/2023-01-13-64db6b7dfd897800013a978a/)

The advantage of the electronic visa is its simple application process, which can be completed within 14 days before departure by following these steps:

1. [Fill out the form on the website](https://molina.imigrasi.go.id/) (<--This is the only official website! Beware of fake sites online)
2. A passport valid for more than six months
3. Upload the passport (photo page) and a photo (a clear, recognizable photo taken with a phone is acceptable)
4. Prepare a payment credit card (Visa, Mastercard, or JCB cards are accepted for online payments. Up to five people can apply simultaneously using one credit card. Amex cards are not accepted. The total amount after processing fees is IDR 519,500)

The processing time for the electronic visa is short, usually taking only a few minutes. The benefit of obtaining the electronic visa in advance is avoiding the queue for visa-on-arrival upon landing. Starting in 2024, travelers can also use the fully automated e-gates without queuing (see [Bali's Facial Recognition Automated Immigration System Launched in 2024: Electronic Visa Holders Can Expedite Entry](/blog/2024-01-04-6596cae5fd89780001f43bff/))

Both visa-on-arrival and electronic visas have their pros and cons. Travelers can choose the option that best suits their needs and preferences. If travelers prefer not to apply for a visa in advance or spend too much time researching online visa applications, they can opt for visa-on-arrival. If travelers want to save time queuing for visa processing upon arrival, they can choose the electronic visa.
