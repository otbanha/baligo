## Bali Travel: Visa on Arrival or eVisa?

Bali is Indonesia's most famous tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world every year. For foreign travelers from non-ASEAN countries, entering Bali requires either a visa on arrival or applying for an eVisa in advance. So, which option is better?

### Visa on Arrival

A visa on arrival is obtained at the immigration counter upon arrival at Bali's international airport. The fee for a visa on arrival is 500,000 IDR (approximately USD$32), valid for 30 days and extendable once, allowing a maximum stay of 60 days.

The advantage of a visa on arrival is its simplicity. Upon arrival at Bali's international airport, travelers only need to present a passport valid for at least six months, a return ticket or onward ticket, and pay the 500,000 IDR fee in cash (specific currencies accepted, [see here](/blog/2023-01-13-64db6b7dfd897800013a978a/)) or by credit card (accepted card types [see here](/blog/2023-01-13-64db6b7dfd897800013a978a/)).

However, the downside is the unpredictable processing time. It could be quick, or you might end up waiting in line for hours. Some travelers have mentioned, "Applying for an eVisa is too troublesome, and getting a visa on arrival only took about ten minutes." However, **this is purely a matter of luck**! Others have spent over an hour waiting in line for their visa on arrival. Those who get it quickly are truly fortunate!

If your flight arrives around the same time as flights from Europe or America, many of those passengers won’t have applied for an eVisa, leading to long queues at the visa on arrival counters. After that, you’ll still need to queue again for passport stamping at immigration. Is it worth wasting so much of your precious vacation time? That’s for you to decide!

### **Electronic Visa**

The electronic visa is a visa that travelers apply for online through the official website of the Indonesian Immigration Directorate before departure. The application fee for the electronic visa is also IDR 500,000 (approximately USD$32), valid for 30 days, extendable once, allowing a maximum stay of 60 days. However, additional processing fees apply for credit card payments. [See here for more details](/blog/2023-01-13-64db6b7dfd897800013a978a/)

The advantage of the electronic visa is its simple application process. Just follow these steps within 14 days before departure:

1. [Fill out the form on the website](https://molina.imigrasi.go.id/) (<-- This is the only official website! Beware of many fake sites online)
2. Passport valid for more than six months
3. Upload passport (photo page) and photo (can be a mobile photo, as long as it clearly identifies you)
4. Prepare a credit card for payment (Visa, Mastercard, or JCB credit cards are accepted for online payment. Up to five people can apply simultaneously using one credit card. Amex cards are not accepted! With processing fees, the final amount is Rp. 519,500)

The processing time for the electronic visa is short, usually taking only a few minutes. The benefit of obtaining the electronic visa in advance is that you don't need to queue for a visa on arrival after landing. Starting in 2024, you can also use the completely queue-free electronic gates (see [Bali's Facial Recognition Automated Immigration System Launched in 2024: Electronic Visa Holders Can Expedite Immigration](/blog/2024-01-04-6596cae5fd89780001f43bff/))

Both visa on arrival and electronic visa have their pros and cons. Travelers can choose the appropriate method based on their needs and preferences. If travelers do not want to apply for a visa in advance or spend too much time researching online visa applications, they can opt for a visa on arrival. If travelers want to save time queuing for visa processing after landing, they can choose the electronic visa.
