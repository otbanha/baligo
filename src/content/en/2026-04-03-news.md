【Today's USD Exchange Rate: IDR 16,475】Today (April 3), Bali news focuses on environmental management and seasonal shifts. Addressing the island's long-standing waste issue, authorities officially announced yesterday (April 2) that the Suwung landfill will permanently close in August, with organic waste banned from entry effective immediately, aiming to promote household waste sorting and reduce environmental risks.

![2026-04-03新聞](https://images.gobaligo.id/images/2026-04/1775174286881-cdb8dc9620bf0db5effd52632c41f81d.jpg)

On the climate front, officials stated that the dry season officially begins in April, with increased solar radiation intensity and a high UV index, reminding tourists to use sun protection and increase fluid intake to prevent dehydration. Additionally, the tsunami warning issued by Indonesia yesterday following a 7.6-magnitude earthquake in Sulawesi was lifted in the evening. Bali experienced mild tremors but no damage, and ports and flight routes are operating normally. For events, the annual Ubud highlight, the Bali Spirit Festival, is confirmed to take place on April 15, boosting booking demand in central Bali.
