Have you started planning your international travels for 2024?

This guide introduces Taiwan's national holidays in 2024 and provides several leave strategies to help you enjoy the longest vacations with the least amount of leave. In 2024, Taiwan has 116 national holidays, including four consecutive holidays of three days or more: New Year's Day, Lunar New Year, Tomb Sweeping Day, and Dragon Boat Festival. Mid-Autumn Festival and Double Ten Day fall on Thursdays, so there are no long holidays.

Here are some leave strategies to help you enjoy the longest vacations with the least amount of leave:

**New Year's Day Holiday** Originally 3 days, take leave from January 2nd to January 5th to enjoy 9 days off.

**Lunar New Year Holiday** Originally 7 days, take leave from February 5th to February 7th to enjoy 12 days off. Or take leave on February 16th and 17th for 11 days off.

**228 Peace Memorial Day** Originally 1 day, take leave on February 26th, 27th, 29th, and March 1st to enjoy 9 days off.

**Tomb Sweeping Day Holiday** Originally 4 days, take leave from April 1st to April 3rd to enjoy 9 days off.

**Labor Day** Originally 1 day, take leave on April 29th, 30th, May 2nd, and 3rd to enjoy 9 days off.

**Dragon Boat Festival Holiday** Originally 3 days, take extra days off from June 11 to June 14 for a 9-day break.

**Mid-Autumn Festival** Originally 1 day, take extra days off on September 16 and September 18 to September 20 for a 9-day break.

**Double Ten Day** Originally 1 day, take extra days off from October 7 to October 9 and October 11 for a 9-day break.

Bali, this picturesque gem of the Indian Ocean, awaits your visit. By comparing festivals in Taiwan and Bali, you can experience different cultural vibes in Bali. For example, while Taiwan is on holiday, Bali might be hosting significant cultural or religious celebrations, offering you a profound cultural experience. Alternatively, it might be a peak travel season you’d want to avoid. See the table and explanations below (only overlapping holidays/festivals are listed):

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a9345467ba7d6d69b803fe56f042b3d8.png)

**Best to Avoid:** Lunar New Year is Bali’s peak travel season, attracting tourists from around the world, especially from East Asia, where Lunar New Year is considered the longest holiday of the year. February 10, the first day of the Lunar New Year, is a national holiday in Indonesia, and February 8 coincides with Ascension Day of Prophet Muhammad (also a national holiday in Indonesia). Combined with the weekend, February 8 to February 11 becomes a flexible four-day holiday. Additionally, February 14 is Indonesia’s presidential election, and many Indonesians might take February 12 and 13 off, creating a seven-day holiday. Expect heavy traffic in Bali during this time. Check out our [Bali Lunar New Year Family Travel Guide](/blog/2026-bali-lunar-new-year-guide/)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_700e68141838114c4ae9cbc8dd92b922.png)

**Worth Participating In:** February 28 to March 9, Galungan & Kuningan: This is one of Bali’s most important festivals, lasting about 10 days, celebrating the creation of the universe and the triumph of good over evil. During this festival, people honor their ancestors at home, seeking their blessings, and hold ceremonies at temples to express reverence for the gods. Schools in Bali close for two weeks during this period, highlighting its significance!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_075f5585db131960b6ac37cd4a560582.png)

**Avoid:** The 2024 Eid al-Fitr holiday in Indonesia starts on April 8 (Friday) and lasts until April 15. Eid al-Fitr is Indonesia’s most important holiday, celebrating the end of Ramadan (April 10). It’s a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, akin to our Lunar New Year. Bali usually starts getting crowded with locals around April 5, making it the first peak season of Bali’s five major travel peaks. This is a holiday you’d want to avoid visiting Bali!

**Join us:** May 1st to May 5th: The 2024 Bali Spirit Festival kicks off on May 1st and runs through May 5th. The Bali Spirit Festival is a music, wellness, and yoga festival designed to provide participants with a holistic mind-body-spirit experience. The festival takes place in Ubud. During the festival, participants can engage in yoga, dance, music, and healing workshops, and connect with people from around the world. The Bali Spirit Festival aims to create a global community that respects and cares for our environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_78e2b82008d7640eff35d35211a75671.png)

**Highly recommended:** The most exciting event in September is the Bali Carnival! This is an annual cultural festival in Bali, held every September in various locations across the island. The festival is renowned for its vibrant traditional dances, music, and art performances, attracting visitors from all over the world. In recent years, more and more handsome men and beautiful women from abroad have joined the parade! Dates to be announced.

**Join us:** October 3rd to October 12th: Navaratri is a Hindu festival celebrating the victory of Goddess Durga. This festival usually takes place in September or October and lasts for nine days, during which people engage in fasting, prayer, flower offerings, and dancing. The last day of Navaratri is Dussehra, celebrating Prince Rama's defeat of the demon king Ravana. If you choose to visit Bali around Double Ten Day, in addition to enjoying a nine-day holiday, you can also participate in the Navaratri festival, experiencing the sacred atmosphere of Hinduism and adding depth to your travels.
