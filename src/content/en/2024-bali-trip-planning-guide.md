Have you started planning your international travels for 2024?

This guide will introduce Taiwan's national holidays in 2024 and provide several vacation strategies to help you enjoy the longest holidays with the least amount of vacation days. In 2024, Taiwan has 116 national holidays, including 4 consecutive holidays of 3 days or more: New Year's Day, Lunar New Year, Tomb Sweeping Day, and Dragon Boat Festival. Mid-Autumn Festival and Double Ten Day fall on Thursdays, so there are no consecutive holidays.

Here are several vacation strategies to help you enjoy the longest holidays with the least amount of vacation days:

**New Year's Day Holiday** Originally 3 days, take January 2nd to January 5th off to enjoy 9 days.

**Lunar New Year Holiday** Originally 7 days, take February 5th to February 7th off to enjoy 12 days. Or take February 16th and 17th off to enjoy 11 days.

**228 Peace Memorial Day** Originally 1 day, take February 26th, 27th, 29th, and March 1st off to enjoy 9 days.

**Tomb Sweeping Day Holiday** Originally 4 days, take April 1st to April 3rd off to enjoy 9 days.

**Labor Day** Originally 1 day, take April 29th, 30th, May 2nd, and 3rd off to enjoy 9 days.

**Dragon Boat Festival Holiday** Originally a 3-day holiday, taking additional days off from June 11 to June 14 allows for a 9-day break.

**Mid-Autumn Festival** Originally a 1-day holiday, taking additional days off on September 16 and September 18 to September 20 allows for a 9-day break.

**Double Ten Day** Originally a 1-day holiday, taking additional days off from October 7 to October 9 and October 11 allows for a 9-day break.

Bali, this picturesque gem of the Indian Ocean, awaits your visit. By comparing Taiwan's and Bali's festivals, you can experience different cultural vibes in Bali. For instance, while Taiwan is on holiday, Bali might be hosting significant cultural or religious celebrations, offering you profound cultural experiences. Alternatively, it might be a peak tourist season you'd want to avoid. Please refer to the table and explanations below (only overlapping holidays/festivals are listed):

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a9345467ba7d6d69b803fe56f042b3d8.png)

**Best to Avoid:** Lunar New Year is Bali's peak tourist season, attracting visitors from around the world, especially from East Asia where Lunar New Year is considered the longest holiday of the year. Originally, February 10th, the first day of the Lunar New Year, is a national holiday in Indonesia, and February 8th coincides with the Ascension of Prophet Muhammad (also a national holiday in Indonesia). With the weekend, February 8th to February 11th becomes a flexible four-day holiday. Additionally, February 14th is the Indonesian Presidential Election, likely prompting many Indonesians to take February 12th and 13th off, resulting in a seven-day holiday and expected heavy traffic in Bali. ![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_700e68141838114c4ae9cbc8dd92b922.png)

**Worth Participating In:** February 28th to March 9th Galungan & Kuningan: This is one of Bali's most important festivals, lasting about 10 days, celebrating the creation of the universe and the triumph of good over evil. During this festival, people honor their ancestors at home, seeking their blessings, and perform rituals at temples to express reverence to the deities. Schools in Bali close for two weeks during this period, highlighting its significance!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_075f5585db131960b6ac37cd4a560582.png)

**Avoid:** The 2024 Indonesian Eid al-Fitr holiday will start on April 8th (Friday) and last until April 15th. Eid al-Fitr is Indonesia's most important holiday, celebrating the end of Ramadan (April 10th), but it's also a time for Indonesians to spend with family and friends, akin to our Lunar New Year. Typically, Bali starts to fill with locals from various regions around April 5th, making it the first of Bali's five peak seasons—a time best avoided for visiting Bali!

**Join us:** May 1-5, 2024: The Bali Spirit Festival will begin on May 1st and continue through May 5th. The Bali Spirit Festival is a music, wellness, and yoga festival designed to provide participants with a holistic mind-body-spirit experience. The festival takes place in Ubud. During the festival, participants can join yoga, dance, music, and healing workshops, and connect with people from around the world. The Bali Spirit Festival aims to create a global community that respects and cares for our environment.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_78e2b82008d7640eff35d35211a75671.png)

**Highly recommended:** The most exciting event in September is the 'Bali Carnival'! This is an annual cultural festival in Bali, held in various locations across the island every September. The festival is renowned for its colorful traditional dances, music, and art performances, attracting visitors from all over the world. In recent years, more and more handsome men and beautiful women from abroad have joined the parade! Dates are yet to be announced.

**Join us:** October 3-12: Navaratri is a Hindu festival celebrating the victory of Goddess Durga. This festival usually takes place in September or October each year, lasting for nine days, during which people engage in fasting, praying, offering flowers, and dancing. The last day of Navaratri is Dussehra, celebrating Prince Rama's defeat of the demon king Ravana. If you choose to visit Bali around the Double Ten holiday, besides enjoying a nine-day vacation, you can also participate in the Navaratri festival, experiencing the sacred atmosphere of Hinduism firsthand, adding depth to your travels.
