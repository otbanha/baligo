## : 🗞️ Today's Headlines: Million Coral Reef Restoration Launches in Eastern Waters, Provincial Government Kicks Off Island-Wide Tourism Zone Epidemic Prevention Battle

- **Protecting the Blue Ocean! Ministry of Environment Teams Up with Transnational Conservation Groups to Launch Large-Scale 'Million Coral Reef' Ecological Restoration in Candidasa Waters**: According to today's top story from Indonesian state news agency *Antara News*, the Ministry of Marine Affairs and Fisheries (KKP), together with WWF Indonesia, officially launched the annual environmental flagship project 'Blue Bali: Million Coral Reef Regeneration Program (Restorasi Terumbu Karang Bali)' today at Candidasa Beach, a famous diving spot in eastern Bali. The project, with an investment of tens of billions of Indonesian rupiah, uses the latest 'Spider Rock Reef Structure' low-voltage micro-current technology to implant the first batch of up to 25,000 heat-tolerant hard coral seedlings onto artificial reefs in the damaged waters of Candidasa. Marine biologists note that Candidasa's waters have suffered partial ecological bleaching due to bottom trawling and climate change. This large-scale restoration not only rebuilds rich marine fish habitats but also helps create a more vibrant underwater tourism environment for international independent travelers who love snorkeling and scuba diving.
- **Bracing for Peak Summer Crowds! Health Agency Launches Full-Scale 'Dengue Chemical Fogging' Campaign in Badung Regency and Denpasar**: According to today's municipal news from *Bali Post*, to ensure public health safety for locals and international tourists during the mid-year peak season, **the Bali Provincial Health Agency, in collaboration with Badung Regency and Denpasar City, has officially kicked off an island-wide 'Anti-Dengue (Demam Berdarah Dengue / DBD) Chemical Fogging Battle'**. Starting this morning, multiple professional spraying teams have been deployed to low-lying traditional communities, drainage areas, and expat villa clusters in major tourist hubs like **Canggu**, **Seminyak**, and Kuta for high-density chemical fogging operations. The Health Agency head reiterated that current dengue case numbers on the island are within normal fluctuation ranges, and this is a preventive measure ahead of the annual peak season to ensure no sanitary dead spots around night markets and beach clubs.

### 📄 Tourist Concerns: Benoa Port New Cruise Terminal Expansion Approved & Dry Season Mosquito Prevention Tips

- **Major Leap in Southern International Infrastructure! Ministry of Transportation Officially Approves Expansion of New International Cruise Passenger Terminal at Benoa Port**: For self-media readers planning to visit the island on high-end international cruise ships or those interested in major local transportation infrastructure, this is significant economic news. Indonesia's Ministry of Transportation today issued an official approval, **formally passing the overall plan for the 'Benoa Cruise Terminal Phase II Expansion and Deep-Water Channel Dredging Project' proposed by state-owned port operator Pelindo**. The new expansion will extend the existing cruise berth by 150 meters and dredge the approach channel and turning basin to a depth of over 12 meters. The port authority head stated that upon completion, this infrastructure will allow the world's largest cruise ships—those exceeding 150,000 gross tons—to dock directly at a dedicated international passenger corridor around the clock, eliminating the need for large ships to anchor offshore and transfer passengers via small boats. This will fully elevate Bali's capacity as a high-end maritime tourism hub.
- **Self-Media Travel Tips: How to Smartly Prevent Mosquitoes During the Dry Season and Cooperate with Community Spraying**: Given that high-density community fogging operations are underway this week in many popular tourist areas, the tourism industry offers thoughtful protection tips for independent travelers. While the island is in a refreshing dry season, localized low-lying areas with standing water can still breed mosquitoes. **💡 Three Steps for Independent Travelers to Prevent Mosquitoes: **Close doors and windows during community spraying**: If your rented private villa in **Canggu** or **Seminyak** is near a main road where white fogging operations are happening, temporarily close room doors and windows for 15 minutes and secure any food left in semi-open living areas. **Look for effective active ingredients**: When strolling on tropical beaches or heading to Ubud's jungles for dinner amid rice fields, buy mosquito repellent lotions containing DEET or Picaridin (like the popular brand Soffel) at local convenience stores (e.g., Indomaret or Alfa Mart)—these are far more effective than natural essential oils. **Properly cover indoor pool water circulation**: For families staying in private villas, if an afternoon thunderstorm hits, ask the villa butler to ensure the private pool's circulation and filtration system is running normally to prevent stagnant water and ensure a clean, safe living space.
