## Challenges and Enforcement Issues with Bali's Tourism Tax System

Bali's government introduced Regional Regulation No. 6 in 2023, requiring foreign tourists to pay a tourism tax, which can be settled online in advance. Yet, only about 40% of travelers have fulfilled this obligation, with the remaining 60% evading payment.

### **Key Reasons:**

1. **Lack of Clear Reminders**: Aside from a few banners, there are almost no prompts in the airport arrival area guiding tourists to pay the tax.
2. **No QR Code Checks at the Airport**: Customs and airport staff currently do not require passengers to show the QR code, allowing travelers to pass through without any hindrance.

Currently, only IDR 300 billion in tourism tax is collected annually, highlighting significant enforcement loopholes.

---

## Government Plans to Tighten Enforcement

The Bali Provincial Government and Regional Representative Council (DPRD) are planning to revise regulations related to the tourism tax, with key points including:

1. **Defining Specific Sanctions**:
2. - The specific terms of travel bans still require further study to ensure transparency and fairness in the implementation process.
3. **Improving the Inspection System**:
4. - Plans to implement QR code checks at the airport, but must ensure they do not cause long queues at entry points.
   - Some tourist attractions already require visitors to show QR codes, and this model may be expanded.
5. **Excluding Hotel-Based Tax Collection**:
6. - Since many foreign tourists choose to stay in villas or other non-hotel accommodations, relying on hotels to collect the tourism tax is not feasible.

### Bali Tourism Office Head Tjokorda Bagus Pemayun stated: 'The system design must be perfected to ensure the full implementation of the tourism tax.'

---

## Future Plans

Based on the current situation, the government has no immediate plans to raise the tourism tax, focusing instead on refining the collection and enforcement system. In 2025, the Bali Regional Representative Council and the Provincial Government will discuss amending relevant local regulations in a plenary session, aiming to effectively address existing issues and improve tax compliance.

Additionally, authorities hope to strengthen outreach and education to ensure tourists understand the importance of paying the tourism tax.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

### **1. Why is the Bali tourism tax necessary?**

The Bali tourism tax is designed to support local infrastructure and improvements to tourism-related facilities, ensuring sustainable tourism development.

### **2. How do I pay the Bali tourism tax?**

Travelers can pay in advance through online platforms and receive a QR code as proof of tax payment. Some tourist attractions also require this QR code for entry.

### **3. What happens if the tourism tax is not paid?**

The government is considering travel bans or other penalties for those who fail to pay, but specific measures are still under review.

### **4. Will hotels help pay the tourism tax on my behalf?**

Currently, there is no such arrangement, and travelers are advised to complete payment on online platforms.

### **5. How will the tourism tax funds be used?**

As of now, no detailed allocation of funds has been announced, but the tax is expected to be used for improving tourism infrastructure and environmental measures.
