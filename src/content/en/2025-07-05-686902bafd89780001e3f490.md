## Electronic Visa on Arrival (eVoA): Apply Before Departure, Valid for 90 Days

---

The Indonesian Electronic Visa on Arrival (eVoA) is a must-have document for most travelers heading to Indonesia, especially for foreigners entering for tourism purposes.

The eVoA must be applied for in advance on the [official Indonesian website](https://evisa.imigrasi.go.id/), and it is usually issued immediately. The visa is valid for 90 days from the issue date, but it's crucial to note that you must complete both your entry and exit procedures within these 90 days; overstaying is not permitted.

It is recommended to apply within one week before your departure to ensure processing time and avoid applying too early and missing the usage period.

## Electronic Customs Declaration (eCD): Fill Out Within Three Days Before Departure

---

## 電子報關（eCD）：出發前三天內填寫

The eCD (Electronic Customs Declaration) is the official online customs declaration form for Indonesia (https://ecd.beacukai.go.id/). All travelers, regardless of whether they have items to declare, are required to complete this form. It includes questions about carrying foreign currency, plants, animals, alcohol, cigarettes, or other items that need to be declared.

There's a clear restriction on when you can fill it out: it must be completed **within three days before departure**. If filled out earlier, the system won't allow you to select the date. After completion, you'll receive a QR Code that needs to be shown to customs officers for scanning upon arrival.

It's recommended to fill it out 2-3 days before your flight to avoid any issues that might delay your entry process.

## Health Declaration (SSHP): Fill Out Truthfully Within 3 Days Before Arrival

The SSHP (Surat Sektor Health Protocol) (https://sshp.kemkes.go.id/) is Indonesia's health declaration form for public health purposes. It includes questions about symptoms experienced in the past 21 days (such as cough or fever), contact with confirmed cases, and personal health status.

This declaration must be completed **within three days before arrival**, with truthful information provided. While most airports no longer enforce temperature checks or health certificates, this form may be reviewed again in case of pandemic changes or special circumstances.

It's advisable to save the completed SSHP as a PDF or take a screenshot for future reference.

## Tourist Levy: Pay Before Arrival, Single Entry Valid

Starting from 2024, all international travelers visiting Bali (Bali only) will be required to pay a Tourist Levy. This fee is [a management charge imposed by the Indonesian government to enhance the local tourism environment](https://lovebali.baliprov.go.id/).

The fee is **150,000 Indonesian Rupiah (approximately USD$10)**, and upon payment, you will receive a QR Code that must be presented and scanned upon entry. **Each entry requires a new payment, and the code is for single use only.**

It is recommended to complete this process one week before departure to avoid any delays at the airport that could affect your itinerary.

## Frequently Asked Questions FAQ

**Q1: Can I apply for eVoA upon arrival if I haven't done so in advance?**

A: It is recommended to apply for the online visa in advance. You can apply for a visa on arrival at the airport, but the process is slower, with longer waiting times, and there is no guarantee of successful issuance.

**Q2: Does the eCD Customs Declaration Form need to be printed?**

A: No need to print. After completing the form, you'll receive a QR Code - simply show it on your phone for scanning.

**Q3: Does the SSHP health declaration check body temperature or vaccination proof?**

A: Currently, temperature checks and vaccination proof are not mandatory in most cases. However, you need to fill in health information in the form, so it's recommended to provide accurate details and keep your documentation.

**Q4: Can I pay the tourism tax on-site?**

A: There are payment counters at Bali International Airport, but online payment is faster and more convenient, helping you avoid queues and waiting times.

**Q5: Do I need to pay the tourism tax every time I enter Bali?**

A: Yes. The tourism tax is a fee required for each entry and cannot be used multiple times.

## Tips

Before traveling to Indonesia or Bali, it's recommended to prepare the following documents and confirm their completion:

- Approved eVoA electronic visa document
- QR Code for the eCD electronic customs declaration
- Screenshot or PDF of the completed SSHP health declaration
- Proof of tourism tax payment and QR Code (required only for Bali entry)
- Return flight ticket
- Travel insurance (depending on airline or entry requirements)

With all documents ready, you can breeze through immigration and enjoy your tropical island getaway with peace of mind!
