**People:** 2 adults and 2 kids (children aged 4 and 6)

**Total Cost:** USD$5,806 for 26 days, including all flights, visas, insurance, SIM cards, accommodation, transportation, and meals. We tried to keep the budget in check, making the trip both fulfilling and affordable.

### Bali Transportation Insights:

We originally planned to hire a private driver for the entire trip in Bali, especially thinking it would be more convenient with two kids. But reality had other plans! On the first day after arriving, we were shocked by the chaotic traffic—gridlock was everywhere! 🤯 If we had stuck with a driver, all our time would have been wasted in traffic jams.

So we quickly changed our strategy and opted for renting a scooter as our main mode of transport. With a family of four, we specifically rented a larger scooter, with two adults and two kids riding together. The kids easily fell asleep on the scooter, so we sandwiched them between us for safety. The road conditions on the island aren't great, but scooters offer much more flexibility, especially when moving between different attractions—it's so much easier and quicker! 🚴‍♀️🚴‍♂️

If we need to go somewhere farther, no worries! We use ride-hailing apps like GRAB or GO-JEK, which work really well in Bali—just call and they come, super convenient.

### Trip Segment Breakdown:

**Segment 1: July 31 – August 4: Kuala Lumpur + Malacca**

- During this period, we mainly stayed in Kuala Lumpur and arranged a three-day, two-night trip to the historic city of Malacca to explore local culture and cuisine.

**Segment 2: August 4 – August 14: Bali**

- Bali's stunning scenery and diverse activities kept us captivated. This was the highlight of the entire trip, as we explored numerous attractions, including beaches, temples, and some lesser-known photo spots.

## August 5 (Mon) – Day Two in Bali Officially Begins

Today's itinerary was packed with activities. We set off early to explore several iconic spots in Bali:

1. **Tanah Lot**
Bali's most iconic seaside temple, combining stunning ocean views with majestic architecture. It was an eye-opening experience for us. The kids had a blast, especially enjoying this direct connection with nature.

> Read more: [Bali Travel Must-Visit: Tanah Lot – Detailed Guide on Tides and Nearby Attractions](/en/blog/bali-tanah-lot-guide/)

2. **Turtle Conservation and Education Center**
This conservation center is perfect for family trips, especially since kids can see newborn sea turtles up close! It offers not only hands-on interaction with turtles but also educational insights into marine ecology. Highly recommended for families with children.

Perched on a dramatic cliff, Uluwatu Temple is another must-visit spot in Bali. The scenery is breathtaking, with stunning views where the cliff meets the ocean. We also spotted monkeys here, which thrilled the kids — but be careful, as they might snatch your belongings.

## August 9 (Fri) Tirta Empul Temple + Alas Harum Bali (Rice Terraces & Luwak Coffee) + Cretya Lunch

Our first stop today was the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. This temple is renowned for its holy spring water, which local believers think can purify the mind and body. Although we are not Hindu, experiencing this unique cultural ritual felt very special. The kids were also fascinated by the mystical atmosphere and impressive architecture.

Next, we visited **Alas Harum Bali**, known not only for its stunning rice terrace views but also for tasting the local specialty **Luwak Coffee**. The terraces were breathtaking, and we took plenty of photos. The kids were curious about the terraces, while we enjoyed a cup of aromatic Luwak coffee. There are also various fun activities here, like the sky swing, making for a relaxing and enjoyable time.

For lunch, we chose to dine at **Cretya**, which offers beautiful rice terrace views and a rich menu. Not only was the food delicious, but the restaurant's ambiance also allowed us to enjoy a peaceful atmosphere—truly a feast for both the eyes and the palate.

## August 10 (Sat) Kanto Lampo Waterfall + Ubud Palace + Ubud Traditional Market

Today we visited the **Kanto Lampo Waterfall**, hidden in the jungle. The water cascades gently down the rocks, creating a beautiful natural landscape. The kids had a blast playing in the water, and the natural surroundings let us experience Bali's ecological charm. The waterfall has a moderate flow, allowing visitors to get close and feel its coolness.

https://youtube.com/shorts/w-gzis-G6OI

Next, we visited the **Ubud Palace**, located in the heart of Ubud. This ancient palace features intricate carvings and traditional Balinese architecture, making it a great place to learn about local culture. Though the palace is not large, its history and architectural beauty left a strong impression on us.

Then, we headed to the famous **Ubud Market**. While the market offers a wide variety of goods, the prices are not cheap, and the vendors generally seemed indifferent, making bargaining quite difficult. Tip: If you're just looking for souvenirs, this might not be the best choice. However, on our way back to **Puri Padma Hotel**, we discovered a small market where the prices were much more reasonable, allowing us to pick up some affordable and beautiful handmade crafts. 🎁

## August 20 (Tue) Exploring Cafes and Traditional Markets + Batuidung Hill Views

Today's itinerary is all about relaxation and leisure. We started at a stylish, design-focused café called **Konyu Coffee & Space**, whose unique decor and tranquil atmosphere instantly won us over. Their brunch is delicious, making it the perfect spot to enjoy a coffee break in a cozy corner during your travels. Whether you're a coffee lover or a brunch enthusiast, this place is well worth a visit.

Next, we headed to two local traditional markets: **Sindu Traditional Market** and **Cakranegara Traditional Market**. These markets are very local, with hardly any tourists in sight, and are filled with the everyday life of Lombok's residents. Prices are very affordable, making them ideal for picking up local fruits, vegetables, or handmade crafts. We discovered many interesting little items and enjoyed the fun of bargaining with locals, which felt authentic and close to daily life.

In the afternoon, we took on the challenge of reaching the legendary **Batuidung Hill**, known for its difficulty to access. The journey was tough, requiring a winding mountain road to get there, but once we stood at the summit, the view made every bit of effort worthwhile. This hidden hill offers breathtaking scenery and an excellent vantage point, overlooking the magnificent natural landscape. The tranquility and beauty here provided a perfect end to our day's itinerary.

![Batuidung，非常難到達的小山丘，google導不到要問在地村莊的人才知道，但爬上去景色很美](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d996aca6ecb50262a1fd75642fdd10f.jpg)
