## Trip Overview:

**Travelers:** 2 adults and 2 kids (ages 4 and 6)

**Total Cost:** USD$5,806 for 26 days, including all flights, visas, insurance, SIM cards, accommodation, transportation, and meals. We kept a close eye on the budget to make the trip both fulfilling and affordable.

### Bali Transportation Insights:

We originally planned to hire a private driver for the entire trip in Bali, especially thinking it would be more convenient with two kids. But reality had other plans! On the first day, we were shocked by the chaotic traffic—gridlock was everywhere! 🤯 If we had stuck with a driver, all our time would have been wasted in traffic jams.

So we quickly changed our strategy and opted for renting a scooter as our main mode of transport. With a family of four, we specifically rented a larger scooter, with two adults and two kids riding together. The kids easily fell asleep on the scooter, so we sandwiched them between us for safety. The road conditions on the island aren't great, but scooters offer much more flexibility, especially when hopping between different attractions—it's so much easier and faster! 🚴‍♀️🚴‍♂️

For longer distances, no worries! We used ride-hailing apps like GRAB or GO-JEK, which are very well-established in Bali—just call and they come, super convenient.

### Trip Segment Breakdown:

**Segment 1: July 31 – August 4, Kuala Lumpur + Malacca**

- During this period, we mainly stayed in Kuala Lumpur and arranged a two-night, three-day trip to the historic city of Malacca to explore its culture and cuisine.

**Segment 2: August 4 – 14, Bali**

- Bali's stunning scenery and diverse activities kept us captivated; this was the highlight of our entire journey. We explored numerous spots, including beaches, temples, and some lesser-known photo-worthy locations.

**Segment 3: August 14 – 25, Lombok + Gili T**

## August 5 (Mon) – Arrival in Bali: The Second Day of Our Trip Officially Begins

Today's itinerary was packed with activities. We set off early to explore several iconic spots in Bali:

1. **Tanah Lot**
Bali's most iconic seaside temple, combining stunning ocean views with majestic architecture. It was a feast for the eyes. The kids had a blast, especially enjoying this direct encounter with nature.

> Read more: [Must-Visit Bali Attractions: Tanah Lot – Detailed Guide on Tides and Nearby Spots](/en/blog/bali-tanah-lot-guide/)

2. **Turtle Conservation and Education Center**
This conservation center is perfect for families, especially since kids can see newborn sea turtles up close! It offers not only hands-on interaction with the turtles but also an educational experience, teaching children about marine ecosystems. Highly recommended for families with young kids.

![這個真的非常推薦帶小孩去，還有海龜放生活動（要預約付費）！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d92ac0bbf87f9c83ee3ffc125a67a551.jpg)

This is really highly recommended for bringing kids, and they also have a sea turtle release activity (requires reservation and payment)!

3. **Uluwatu Temple (Pura Uluwatu)**
Perched on a dramatic cliff, Uluwatu Temple is another must-visit spot in Bali. The scenery here is breathtaking, with stunning views where the cliff meets the ocean. We also spotted monkeys here, which thrilled the kids, but be careful—they might snatch your belongings.

4. **Relaxing Massage**
After a day of sightseeing, how could you visit Bali without trying a massage? We specifically found a local massage parlor, and the whole family enjoyed a soothing session. It was the perfect way to unwind and recharge for tomorrow's adventures.

## August 9 (Fri) Tirta Empul Temple + Alas Harum Bali (Rice Terraces & Luwak Coffee) + Lunch at Cretya

Our first stop today was the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. Known for its holy spring water, local devotees believe it has the power to purify both mind and body. Although we aren't Hindu, experiencing this unique cultural ritual felt truly special. The kids were also fascinated by the mystical atmosphere and impressive architecture.

Next, we visited **Alas Harum Bali**, renowned not only for its stunning rice terraces but also for offering a taste of local **Luwak Coffee**. The terrace views were breathtaking, and we snapped plenty of photos. The kids were curious about the terraces, while we enjoyed a fragrant cup of Luwak coffee. There were also various fun activities, like the sky swing, making for a relaxing and enjoyable time.

For lunch, we chose to dine at **Cretya**, which boasts beautiful rice terrace views and a rich menu. Not only was the food delicious, but the restaurant's ambiance allowed us to soak in a peaceful atmosphere—a true feast for both the eyes and the palate.

## August 10 (Sat) Kanto Lampo Waterfall + Ubud Palace + Ubud Traditional Market

Today we visited **Kanto Lampo Waterfall**, hidden in the jungle. The water cascades gently over rocks, creating a beautiful natural scene. The kids had a blast playing in the water, and the natural surroundings let us experience Bali's ecological charm. The waterfall's flow was moderate, allowing visitors to get close and feel its cool spray.

https://youtube.com/shorts/w-gzis-G6OI

Afterward, we toured **Ubud Palace** in the heart of Ubud. This ancient palace features intricate carvings and traditional Balinese architecture, making it a great place to learn about local culture. Though small, its history and architectural beauty left a strong impression on us.

Next, we headed to the famous **Ubud Market**. While it offers a wide variety of goods, prices weren't cheap, and the sellers were generally indifferent, making bargaining difficult. Tip: If you're just looking for souvenirs, this might not be the best spot. However, on our way back to **Puri Padma Hotel**, we found a small market with much more affordable prices, where we picked up some great handmade crafts. 🎁

When heading to Padangbai for boarding, I recommend staying nearby the night before, as traffic from either the south or Ubud to Padangbai can be highly unpredictable and prone to delays. The pier environment is very chaotic, with crowds of people, making it somewhat disorienting. Near the dock, many locals will proactively offer to help with luggage, but some may forcefully take your bags and demand tips. I encountered a similar situation here—my luggage was whisked away in an instant, and I had no choice but to give a tip to avoid unnecessary trouble. Each person typically asks for 50,000 Indonesian Rupiah in tips, but I chose to give only 10,000 Rupiah, not wanting to be dragged into a long negotiation. In most cases, they're in a hurry to help other travelers with their bags, so they won't argue for long, which helped me avoid some hassle.

## Tuesday, August 20 — Coffee Shop & Traditional Market Exploration + Batuidung Hill Views

Today's itinerary was all about relaxation. We started at a stylish, artsy coffee shop called **Konyu Coffee & Space**, which instantly won us over with its unique decor and serene atmosphere. The brunch here was delicious, making it the perfect spot to enjoy a coffee break during the trip. Whether you're a coffee lover or a brunch enthusiast, this place is well worth a visit.

Next, we visited two local traditional markets: **Sindu Traditional Market** and **Cakranegara Traditional Market**. These markets are very local, with almost no tourists in sight, offering a glimpse into the daily life of Lombok's residents. Prices were incredibly affordable, making them ideal for picking up local fruits, vegetables, or handmade crafts. We discovered many interesting trinkets and enjoyed the fun of bargaining with locals, which felt authentic and close to everyday life.

In the afternoon, we took on the challenge of reaching the elusive **Batuidung Hill**. The journey was tough, requiring navigating winding mountain roads, but when we stood at the summit, the view made every bit of effort worthwhile. This hidden hill offers breathtaking scenery with an excellent vantage point overlooking the grandeur of nature. The tranquility and beauty here provided the perfect ending to our day's adventure.
