### Bali Transportation Tips:

Originally, we planned to hire a private car for our entire trip in Bali, especially thinking it would be more convenient with two young children. However, reality often differs from expectations! Upon arrival on the first day, we were shocked by the chaotic traffic on the island—traffic jams were everywhere! 🤯 If we had insisted on using a private car, we would have wasted all our time stuck in traffic.

So, we quickly changed our strategy and decided to rent scooters as our main mode of transportation. Considering our family of four, we specifically rented a larger scooter to accommodate two adults and two children. The kids easily fell asleep on the scooter, so we sandwiched them between us for safety. The road conditions on the island weren't exactly friendly, but renting scooters offered much more flexibility, especially when moving between different attractions—it was much more convenient! 🚴‍♀️🚴‍♂️

If we encountered destinations that were farther away, we didn't worry! We used ride-hailing services like GRAB or GO-JEK, which are well-established in Bali and incredibly convenient, available at any time.

## August 9th (Friday) Tirta Empul Temple + Alas Harum Bali (Rice Terraces & Luwak Coffee) + Lunch at Cretya

Our first stop today was the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. This temple is renowned for its holy spring water, believed by local devotees to purify both mind and body. Although we are not Hindu, experiencing this unique cultural ritual firsthand felt very special. The kids were also fascinated by the temple's mystical atmosphere and impressive architecture.

Next, we visited **Alas Harum Bali**, famous not only for its stunning rice terraces but also for its local specialty, **Luwak Coffee**. The terrace views were breathtaking, and we took many beautiful photos here. The kids were curious about the terraces, while we enjoyed a fragrant cup of Luwak coffee. The place also offers various recreational facilities, like sky swings, making our time there relaxing and enjoyable.

For lunch, we chose **Cretya**, which offers beautiful rice terrace views and a rich menu. Not only was the food delicious, but the restaurant's ambiance allowed us to enjoy a peaceful atmosphere, making it a feast for both the eyes and the palate.

## August 10th (Sat) Kanto Lampo Waterfall + Ubud Palace + Ubud Traditional Market

Today we visited the **Kanto Lampo Waterfall**, hidden deep within the jungle. The water cascades gently between rocks, creating a stunning natural landscape. The kids had a blast playing in the water, and the surrounding environment allowed us to fully appreciate Bali's ecological charm. The waterfall's flow was moderate, allowing visitors to get close and feel its refreshing coolness.

Afterward, we explored the **Ubud Palace** located in the heart of Ubud. This ancient palace features intricate carvings and traditional Balinese architectural styles, making it an excellent place to learn about local culture. Although the palace is not large, its history and architectural beauty left a lasting impression on us.

Next, we visited the famous **Ubud Traditional Market**. While the market offers a wide variety of goods, the prices are not particularly cheap, and the sellers' attitudes were generally indifferent, making bargaining difficult. Pro tip: If you're just looking for souvenirs, this might not be the best place. However, on our way back to **Puri Padma Hotel**, we stumbled upon a smaller market where the prices were much more reasonable, allowing us to pick up some affordable and beautiful handmade crafts. 🎁

## August 20 (Tuesday) Coffee Shop and Traditional Market Exploration + Batuidung Hill Scenery

Today's itinerary focuses on relaxation and leisure. We started at the stylish and artistic coffee shop **Konyu Coffee & Space**, where the unique decor and tranquil atmosphere instantly won us over. The brunch here is delicious, making it the perfect spot to enjoy a coffee break during our travels. Whether you're a coffee lover or a brunch enthusiast, this place is definitely worth a visit.

Next, we headed to two local traditional markets: **Sindu Traditional Market** and **Cakranegara Traditional Market**. These markets are very local, with hardly any tourists, and are filled with the daily life of Lombok residents. The prices are very affordable, making it a great place to buy local fruits, vegetables, or handmade goods. We discovered many interesting items and enjoyed the experience of bargaining with locals, which felt authentic and close to everyday life.

In the afternoon, we took on the challenge of reaching the hard-to-access **Batuidung Hill**. The journey was tough, involving winding mountain roads, but once we reached the top, the view made all the effort worthwhile. This hidden hill offers stunning scenery with excellent views overlooking the natural landscape. The peace and beauty here provided the perfect ending to our day.

![Batuidung，非常難到達的小山丘，google導不到要問在地村莊的人才知道，但爬上去景色很美](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d996aca6ecb50262a1fd75642fdd10f.jpg)

Batuidung, a very hard-to-reach hill, not accessible via Google Maps—you have to ask locals from the village to find it, but the view from the top is beautiful.

![Batuidung，非常難到達的小山丘，但是上去後可以看到整個龍目島的山跟海，搭配日落超美](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b1fb6551e41727795ba2044cacdee1da.jpg)

Batuidung, a very hard-to-reach hill, but once you're up there, you can see the mountains and sea of Lombok, especially beautiful with the sunset.
