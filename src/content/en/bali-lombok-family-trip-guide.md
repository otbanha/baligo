## Trip Overview:

**Travelers:** 2 adults and 2 kids (ages 4 and 6)

**Total Cost:** USD$5,806 for 26 days, including all flights, visas, insurance, SIM cards, accommodation, transportation, meals, and more. We kept a tight budget to make the trip both fulfilling and affordable.

**Kuala Lumpur–Bali One-Way Flight:**

- August 4: KUL–DPS OD193 14:00–17:00

**Lombok–Kuala Lumpur One-Way Flight:**

- August 24: LOP–KUL OD367 19:45–23:00
- One-way for 4 people with 30 kg luggage, total cost USD$384.

### Bali Transportation Insights:

We originally planned to hire a private driver for the entire trip in Bali, especially thinking it would be easier with two kids—but reality had other ideas! On the first day, we were shocked by the chaotic traffic on the island—gridlock was everywhere! 🤯 If we had stuck with a driver, we would have wasted all our time stuck in traffic.

So we quickly changed tactics and opted to rent a scooter as our main mode of transport. With a family of four, we specifically rented a larger scooter, riding two adults and two kids together. The kids easily dozed off on the scooter, so we sandwiched them between us for safety. The road conditions on the island are indeed less than ideal, but renting a scooter gave us much more flexibility—especially when moving between different attractions, it was a breeze! 🚴‍♀️🚴‍♂️

If we needed to go somewhere farther, no worries! We used ride-hailing services like GRAB or GO-JEK, which run smoothly in Bali—just call and they arrive, super convenient.

### Itinerary Breakdown:

**Part 1: July 31 – August 4: Kuala Lumpur + Malacca**

- During this period, we mainly stayed in Kuala Lumpur and arranged a three-day, two-night trip to the historic city of Malacca to explore its culture and cuisine.

**Part 2: August 4 – August 14: Bali**

- Bali's stunning scenery and diverse activities kept us captivated. This was the highlight of the entire trip, as we explored numerous attractions, including beaches, temples, and some lesser-known photo spots.

## Sunday, August 4: Arrival in Bali – Scooter Rental & Dinner

On the day we arrived in Bali, our top priority was renting a scooter, which would be our main mode of transportation for the next few days. We chose a scooter because traffic in Bali is notoriously bad, with heavy congestion; riding a scooter allowed us to navigate between attractions more flexibly and save a lot of time.

That evening, we had dinner locally and then checked into **Sara Residence** in the Legian/Seminyak area. The two-bedroom unit was perfect for our family of four—spacious and comfortable, giving the kids their own little space. For families traveling with children, this kind of accommodation is very convenient.

## August 5 (Monday) – Day Two in Bali Begins

Today's itinerary was packed with excitement. We set off early to explore some of Bali's most iconic spots:

1. **Tanah Lot**
Bali's most iconic seaside temple, with stunning ocean views and majestic architecture that left us in awe. The kids had a blast here, especially loving the direct connection with nature.

> Read more: [Bali Travel Must-Visit: Tanah Lot – Detailed Guide with Tides and Nearby Attractions](/blog/bali-tanah-lot-guide/)

2. **Turtle Conservation and Education Center**
This conservation center is perfect for family trips, especially since kids can see newly hatched baby turtles up close! It offers not only opportunities to interact with turtles but also an educational aspect, teaching children about marine ecology. Highly recommended for families with young children.

![這個真的非常推薦帶小孩去，還有海龜放生活動（要預約付費）！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d92ac0bbf87f9c83ee3ffc125a67a551.jpg)

This is truly a must-visit with kids, and they also offer turtle release activities (reservation and fee required)!

https://youtube.com/shorts/9EeW2YH0iY0

3. **Pura Uluwatu (Uluwatu Cliff Temple)**
Perched on a dramatic cliff, Uluwatu Temple is another must-see attraction in Bali. The views here are breathtaking, with the stunning interplay of cliffs and ocean creating an unforgettable scene. We even spotted monkeys here, which thrilled the kids, but be careful—they might snatch your belongings!

> [Bali Uluwatu Temple / Lovers' Cliff Travel Guide: Must-See Highlights & Practical Tips](/blog/uluwatu-temple-travel-guide/)

4. **Relaxing Massage**
After a day of sightseeing, how could you visit Bali without experiencing a massage? We specifically found a local massage parlor, and the whole family enjoyed a soothing session. It was the perfect way to unwind and recharge for the next day's adventures.

## August 9 (Fri) – Tirta Empul Temple + Alas Harum Bali (Rice Terraces & Luwak Coffee) + Lunch at Cretya

Our first stop today was the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. This temple is renowned for its holy spring water, which local devotees believe has the power to purify the mind and body. Although we are not Hindu, experiencing this unique cultural ritual felt truly special. The kids were also fascinated by the mystical atmosphere and impressive architecture.

Next, we visited **Alas Harum Bali**, known not only for its breathtaking rice terraces but also for the chance to taste local **Luwak Coffee**. The terrace views were stunning, and we snapped plenty of photos. The kids were curious about the terraces, while we enjoyed a fragrant cup of Luwak coffee. The venue also offers various fun activities, like the sky swing, making for a relaxing and joyful time.

For lunch, we chose to dine at **Cretya**, where the stunning rice terrace views complemented the rich menu. Not only was the food delicious, but the restaurant's ambiance also allowed us to enjoy a serene atmosphere—truly a feast for both the eyes and the palate.

## August 10 (Sat) Kanto Lampo Waterfall + Ubud Palace + Ubud Traditional Market

Today we visited **Kanto Lampo Waterfall**, hidden in the jungle. The water cascades gently over the rocks, creating a beautiful natural landscape. The kids had a blast playing in the water, and the natural surroundings let us experience Bali's ecological charm. The waterfall has a moderate flow, allowing visitors to get close and feel its refreshing coolness.

https://youtube.com/shorts/w-gzis-G6OI

Afterward, we toured **Ubud Palace**, located in the heart of Ubud. This ancient palace features intricate carvings and traditional Balinese architecture, making it a great place to learn about local culture. Though the palace is not large, its history and architectural beauty left a strong impression on us.

Next, we visited the famous **Ubud Traditional Market**. While the market offers a wide variety of goods, the prices are not cheap, and the vendors are generally quite indifferent, making bargaining difficult. Tip: If you're just looking for souvenirs, this might not be the best choice. However, on our way back to **Puri Padma Hotel**, we discovered a small market where the prices were much more reasonable, allowing us to pick up some affordable and beautiful handicrafts. 🎁

## Monday, August 12: Bali Bird Park + Villa Kayu Lama
