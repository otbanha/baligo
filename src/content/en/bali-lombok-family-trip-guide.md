### Bali Transportation Tips:

We originally planned to hire a private driver for the entire Bali trip, especially thinking it would be easier with two kids. But reality had other ideas! On the first day, we were shocked by the chaotic traffic—gridlock was everywhere! 🤯 If we had stuck with a private driver, all our time would have been wasted in traffic jams.

So we quickly changed our strategy and opted for renting a scooter as our main mode of transport. Since we're a family of four, we specifically rented a larger scooter, with two adults and two kids riding together. The kids easily fall asleep on the scooter, so we sandwiched them between the adults for safety. The road conditions on the island aren't the friendliest, but renting a scooter gave us much more flexibility—especially when moving between different attractions, it was so much easier and more convenient! 🚴‍♀️🚴‍♂️

If we needed to go somewhere farther, no worries! We used ride-hailing apps like GRAB or GO-JEK, which work really well in Bali—just call and they come right away, super convenient.

## Sunday, August 4: Arrival in Bali – Scooter Rental & Dinner

On the day we arrived in Bali, our first priority was renting a scooter, which would be our main mode of transport for the next few days. We chose a scooter because traffic in Bali is notoriously bad, with frequent jams; riding a scooter allowed us to weave through traffic and save time getting between attractions.

That evening, we had dinner locally and then checked into **Sara Residence** in the Legian/Seminyak area. The two-bedroom unit was perfect for our family of four—spacious and comfortable, giving the kids their own space. For families traveling with children, this kind of accommodation is very convenient.

## Monday, August 5 – The Second Day of Our Bali Trip Officially Begins

Today’s itinerary was packed with activities. We set off early to explore several of Bali’s iconic attractions:

1. **Tanah Lot**
Bali’s most iconic seaside temple, combining stunning ocean views with majestic architecture, truly opened our eyes. The kids had a great time here, especially drawn to this spot’s direct connection with nature.

> Read more: [Bali Travel Must-Visit: Tanah Lot – Detailed Guide with Tides and Nearby Attractions](/blog/bali-tanah-lot-guide/)

2. **Turtle Conservation and Education Center**
This conservation center is perfect for family trips, especially since kids can see newborn sea turtles up close! It offers not only interactive opportunities with the turtles but also educational insights into marine ecology. Highly recommended for families with children.

![這個真的非常推薦帶小孩去，還有海龜放生活動（要預約付費）！](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d92ac0bbf87f9c83ee3ffc125a67a551.jpg)

This is truly a must-visit for kids, and they also offer turtle release activities (reservation and fee required)!

https://youtube.com/shorts/9EeW2YH0iY0

3. **Pura Uluwatu**
Perched on a dramatic cliff, Uluwatu Temple is another unmissable spot in Bali. The breathtaking scenery, with cliffs and ocean intertwining, creates an unforgettable sight. We also spotted monkeys here, which thrilled the kids—though we had to be careful about them snatching things.

> [Bali Uluwatu Temple / Cliffside Travel Guide: Must-See Highlights and Practical Tips](/blog/uluwatu-temple-travel-guide/)

4. **Relaxing Massage**
After a full day of sightseeing, how could we skip a massage in Bali? We found a local massage parlor, and the whole family enjoyed a soothing session. It was the perfect way to unwind and recharge for tomorrow’s adventures.

## August 9 (Fri) Tirta Empul Temple + Alas Harum Bali (Rice Terraces & Civet Coffee) + Cretya Lunch

Our first stop today was the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. This temple is renowned for its holy spring water, which local devotees believe has the power to purify the mind and body. Although we are not Hindu, experiencing this unique cultural ritual felt very special. The children were also fascinated by the mystical atmosphere and impressive architecture.

Next, we arrived at **Alas Harum Bali**, famous not only for its stunning terraced rice paddies but also for offering a taste of the local **civet coffee (Luwak Coffee)**. The rice terrace views were breathtaking, and we took plenty of photos. The kids were curious about the paddies, while we enjoyed a fragrant cup of civet coffee. There were also various fun activities, like the sky swing, making for a relaxing and enjoyable time.

For lunch, we chose to dine at **Cretya**, which offers beautiful rice terrace views and a rich menu. The food was delicious, and the restaurant's ambiance allowed us to soak in a peaceful atmosphere—truly a feast for both the eyes and the taste buds.

## August 10 (Sat) Kanto Lampo Waterfall + Ubud Palace + Ubud Traditional Market

Today, we visited the **Kanto Lampo Waterfall**, hidden in the jungle. The water cascaded gently over the rocks, creating a beautiful natural scene. The kids had a blast playing in the water, and the natural surroundings let us experience Bali's ecological charm. The waterfall had a moderate flow, allowing visitors to get close and feel its cool spray.

https://youtube.com/shorts/w-gzis-G6OI

Afterward, we toured the **Ubud Palace**, located in the heart of Ubud. This ancient palace features intricate carvings and traditional Balinese architecture, making it a great place to learn about local culture. Though the palace is not large, its history and architectural beauty left a strong impression on us.

Next, we visited the famous **Ubud Traditional Market**.
