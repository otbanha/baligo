### Bali Transportation Insights:

Initially, we planned to hire a private car for our entire stay in Bali, thinking it would be more convenient with two kids in tow. But reality often differs from imagination! Upon arrival on the first day, we were shocked by the chaotic traffic on the island—traffic jams were everywhere! 🤯 If we had insisted on using a private car, we probably would have wasted all our time stuck in traffic.

So, we quickly changed our strategy and opted to rent scooters as our main mode of transportation. Considering our family of four, we rented a larger scooter, allowing two adults and two kids to ride together. The kids easily fell asleep on the scooter, so we sandwiched them between us for safety. The road conditions on the island weren’t exactly friendly, but renting scooters offered much more flexibility, especially when moving between different attractions—it was so much easier and more convenient! 🚴‍♀️🚴‍♂️

For destinations that were farther away, we didn’t have to worry either! We used ride-hailing services like GRAB or GO-JEK, which are well-established in Bali, making it easy to get around whenever we needed.

## August 9th (Friday) Tirta Empul Temple + Alas Harum Bali (Rice Terraces & Luwak Coffee) + Lunch at Cretya

Our first stop today was the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. Known for its holy spring water, local devotees believe the water here has the power to purify the mind and body. Although we are not Hindu, experiencing this unique cultural ritual felt very special. The kids were also fascinated by the temple’s mystical atmosphere and grand architecture.

Next, we visited **Alas Harum Bali**, renowned for its stunning rice terraces and the chance to taste the local specialty, **Luwak Coffee**. The terraces were breathtaking, and we took plenty of beautiful photos. The kids were curious about the terraces, while we enjoyed a fragrant cup of Luwak coffee. The place also offers various fun facilities like sky swings, making our time here relaxing and enjoyable.

For lunch, we chose **Cretya**, which offers a beautiful view of the rice terraces and a rich variety of dishes. Not only was the food delicious, but the serene atmosphere of the restaurant provided a feast for both the eyes and the palate.

## August 10th (Sat) Kanto Lampo Waterfall + Ubud Palace + Ubud Traditional Market

Today, we visited the **Kanto Lampo Waterfall**, hidden deep within the jungle. The water cascades gently between the rocks, creating a stunning natural scene. The kids had a blast playing in the water, and the surrounding environment allowed us to fully appreciate Bali's ecological charm. The waterfall's flow was moderate, allowing visitors to get close and feel its refreshing coolness.

Next, we explored the **Ubud Palace**, located in the heart of Ubud. This ancient palace features intricate carvings and traditional Balinese architecture, making it an excellent place to learn about local culture. Although the palace is not large, its history and architectural beauty left a lasting impression on us.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Ubud Traditional Market**. While the market offers a wide variety of goods, the prices are not particularly cheap, and the vendors' attitudes can be somewhat indifferent, making bargaining difficult. Pro tip: If you're just looking for souvenirs, this might not be the best spot. However, on our way back to **Puri Padma Hotel**, we stumbled upon a smaller market where the prices were much more reasonable, allowing us to pick up some affordable handmade crafts. 🎁

## August 16 (Fri) Gili T to Bangsal + Stay at Holiday Resort Lombok

![GiliT搭公交船到Bangsal](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0fb24d029b4beb6d49d2927f573ef480.jpg)

Taking the public ferry from Gili T to Bangsal

Today we bid farewell to the beautiful **Gili T** and took a public ferry to **Bangsal**. Although the journey was relatively short, it required extra caution, as both the Gili T pier and Bangsal are known for scams. We encountered several people trying to sell us fake services, but fortunately, we had done our homework and avoided being tricked. It’s advisable to stay vigilant in these areas and not easily trust strangers.

Tonight we stayed at **Holiday Resort Lombok** in Senggigi. The hotel was impressive, with spacious rooms, beautiful garden views, and a serene beach that left our entire family very satisfied. The facilities were well-equipped, including a pool, restaurant, and spaces for family activities, offering excellent value for money. ## August 20 (Tuesday) Coffee Shops and Traditional Market Exploration + Batuidung Hill Scenery

Today's itinerary is all about relaxation and leisure. We started our day at **Konyu Coffee & Space**, a trendy café with a unique design and serene atmosphere that instantly won us over. The brunch here is delicious, making it the perfect spot to enjoy a cozy coffee break during our travels. Whether you're a coffee enthusiast or a brunch lover, this place is definitely worth a visit.

Next, we headed to two local traditional markets: **Sindu Traditional Market** and **Cakranegara Traditional Market**. These markets are very authentic, with hardly any tourists, and are filled with the daily life of Lombok residents. The prices are very affordable, making it a great place to buy local fruits, vegetables, or handmade crafts. We discovered many interesting items and enjoyed the fun of bargaining with locals, which felt genuine and close to everyday life.

In the afternoon, we took on the challenge of reaching the hard-to-access **Batuidung Hill**. The journey was tough, requiring us to navigate winding mountain roads, but the view from the top made all the effort worthwhile. This hidden hill offers stunning scenery with excellent views overlooking the majestic natural landscape. The tranquility and beauty here perfectly capped off our day.

![Batuidung，非常難到達的小山丘，google導不到要問在地村莊的人才知道，但爬上去景色很美](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d996aca6ecb50262a1fd75642fdd10f.jpg)
