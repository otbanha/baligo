### Bali Transportation Tips:

Originally, we planned to hire a private car for our entire trip in Bali, especially thinking it would be more convenient with two kids in tow. But reality often differs from expectations! Upon arrival on the first day, we were shocked by the chaotic traffic on the island—traffic jams seemed to be everywhere! 🤯 If we had insisted on using a private car, we probably would have wasted all our time stuck in traffic.

So, we quickly changed our strategy and opted to rent scooters as our main mode of transportation. Considering our family of four, we specifically rented a larger scooter to accommodate two adults and two kids. The children often fell asleep on the scooter, so we sandwiched them between us for safety. The roads on the island weren’t exactly friendly, but renting scooters offered much more flexibility, especially when moving between different attractions—it was so much easier and quicker! 🚴‍♀️🚴‍♂️

If we needed to reach a destination that was farther away, there was no need to worry! We used ride-hailing services like GRAB or GO-JEK, which are well-established in Bali and incredibly convenient—just a tap away.

## August 9th (Friday) Tirta Empul Temple + Alas Harum Bali (Rice Terraces & Luwak Coffee) + Lunch at Cretya

Our first stop today was the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**. This temple is renowned for its holy spring water, which local believers believe has the power to purify both mind and body. Although we are not Hindu, experiencing this unique cultural ritual firsthand felt very special. The kids were also fascinated by the mystical atmosphere and grand architecture.

Next, we visited **Alas Harum Bali**, known not only for its stunning rice terraces but also for its local specialty, **Luwak Coffee**. The terraces were breathtaking, and we took many beautiful photos here. The kids were very curious about the terraces, while we enjoyed a fragrant cup of Luwak coffee. There are also various recreational facilities here, such as the sky swing, making our time here relaxing and enjoyable.

For lunch, we chose to dine at **Cretya**, which offers beautiful views of the rice terraces and a rich variety of dishes. Not only was the food delicious, but the serene atmosphere of the restaurant provided a feast for both the eyes and the palate.

## August 10th (Sat) Kanto Lampo Waterfall + Ubud Palace + Ubud Traditional Market

Today we visited the **Kanto Lampo Waterfall**, hidden deep in the jungle. The water cascades gently between rocks, creating a stunning natural scene. The kids had a blast playing in the water, and the surrounding nature allowed us to fully appreciate Bali's ecological charm. The waterfall's flow was moderate, letting visitors get close and feel its refreshing coolness.

Next, we explored **Ubud Palace** in the heart of Ubud. This ancient palace features intricate carvings and traditional Balinese architecture, making it an excellent place to learn about local culture. Though the palace isn't large, its history and architectural beauty left a lasting impression on us.

Afterward, we headed to the famous **Ubud Traditional Market**. While the market offers a wide variety of goods, prices aren't particularly cheap, and vendors tend to be somewhat indifferent, making bargaining difficult. Pro tip: If you're just looking for souvenirs, this might not be the best spot. However, on our way back to **Puri Padma Hotel**, we stumbled upon a smaller market where prices were much more reasonable, allowing us to pick up some affordable, high-quality handicrafts. 🎁

## August 20 (Tuesday) Coffee Shops & Traditional Market Exploration + Batuidung Hill Scenery

Today’s itinerary is all about relaxation and leisure. We started at a trendy, artsy coffee shop called **Konyu Coffee & Space**, which instantly won us over with its unique decor and tranquil atmosphere. The brunch here is delicious, making it the perfect spot to enjoy a cozy coffee break during our travels. Whether you’re a coffee lover or a brunch enthusiast, this place is definitely worth a visit.

Next, we headed to two local traditional markets: **Sindu Traditional Market** and **Cakranegara Traditional Market**. These markets are very authentic, with hardly any tourists, offering a glimpse into the daily lives of Lombok residents. The prices are incredibly affordable, making it a great place to pick up local fruits, vegetables, or handmade goods. We discovered many interesting items and enjoyed the fun of bargaining with locals, which felt genuine and close to everyday life.

In the afternoon, we took on the challenge of reaching the legendary **Batuidung Hill**. The journey was tough, requiring us to navigate winding mountain roads, but once we reached the top, the view made all the effort worthwhile. This hidden hill offers breathtaking scenery and panoramic views of the stunning natural landscape. The peacefulness and beauty here perfectly capped off our day.

![Batuidung，非常難到達的小山丘，google導不到要問在地村莊的人才知道，但爬上去景色很美](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_5d996aca6ecb50262a1fd75642fdd10f.jpg)

Batuidung, a very hard-to-reach hill, isn’t on Google Maps—you’ll need to ask locals in the village for directions. But the climb is worth it for the stunning views.

![Batuidung，非常難到達的小山丘，但是上去後可以看到整個龍目島的山跟海，搭配日落超美](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b1fb6551e41727795ba2044cacdee1da.jpg)
