The well-known Hong Kong YouTuber "Crazy with YU Fang Ting" recently traveled to Bali, staying there for over ten days and experiencing various exciting activities. In her video, she compiled a list of five must-do activities in Bali. Let's follow her footsteps and explore the unique charm of Bali together.

## First: Surfing Experience

The first must-do activity is experiencing surfing. The trio took a surfing lesson in Canggu, which included a two-hour surfing session and the chance to witness Bali's stunning sunset. She marveled at the sunset, describing it as indescribably beautiful with distinct layers of colors, calling it the most breathtaking sunset she has ever seen.

## Second: Island Hopping

The second activity she recommends is visiting Bali's nearby island, Nusa Penida. To reach Nusa Penida faster, they stayed in Sanur, as it has a port with just a 30-minute boat ride to the island. Early the next morning, they joined a day tour to Nusa Penida. Their first stop was Crystal Bay, a secluded paradise with no Wi-Fi, where they planned to fully immerse themselves in this earthly haven and temporarily forget the world. Upon arrival, they expected a small group of 30-40 people, but surprisingly, over 150 people disembarked together, resembling a flock of ducks. However, to their surprise, they were assigned to a private small tour vehicle, with four people per group, making the experience more relaxed and comfortable.

After arriving at Nusa Penida, they leisurely enjoyed breakfast, drank beer, and played with stray dogs on the beach at Crystal Bay. Although she didn't find the beach scenery particularly outstanding, the water was as clear as Long Ke Wan. Next, they visited other famous spots like Angels Billabong and Broken Beach. Despite the crowds, the geological landscapes were still breathtakingly stunning. Finally, they arrived at a dinosaur-shaped turquoise beach where the water displayed beautiful gradients of depth, leaving them in awe.

Experiencing unique accommodations in Bali is a must. They stayed in a rustic bamboo villa in Ubud, which cost just $250 per night and came with a private pool, kitchen, and two bedrooms - excellent value for money. The villa's design is ingenious, featuring a private pool right at the entrance and a sofa area beside it perfect for small gatherings. The second floor has an independent bathroom with floor-to-ceiling windows facing a primitive shower area, creating a serene atmosphere. The terrace offers panoramic mountain views, making it an ideal spot for enjoying drinks and scenery.

## Fourth: Bali Swing

![Crazy with YU芳婷視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_34c80e7c49fa6780e9392935db7bcd05.jpg)

Crazy with YU Fangting video screenshot

Besides admiring the scenery, experiencing the swing is a must-do in Bali. They went to Aloha Ubud, where there are six swing spots for photo opportunities. The entrance fee is only $25 per person, and you can even rent various wedding dresses to take dreamy wedding photos. The staff will carefully guide you on poses and angles to capture the perfect moment. While girls can swing longer for photos, boys can only swing briefly to avoid danger. However, safety ropes ensure the safety of all visitors.

## Fifth: Instagrammable Waterfall

![Crazy with YU芳婷視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d8055c47f3c5d016b36cdd1708e918f6.jpg)

Crazy with YU Fangting video screenshot

The final stop of the trip was a hidden waterfall near Ubud. They had to hike a short mountain trail to see the magnificent waterfall panorama, which was crowded with photographers. However, the site is equipped with lockers, toilets, and changing rooms, making it very convenient. It only takes five minutes to reach the waterfall photo area. The waterfall staff are very professional and will guide you on shooting angles and poses. She was happy to tip for the best perspective. For the next photo session, she suggests bringing an extra set of clothes, as swimming in the waterfall can leave you feeling cold.

https://youtube.com/shorts/w-gzis-G6OI

The Ubud area is not only famous for its culture and art but also boasts breathtaking natural landscapes, with waterfalls being a must-visit. Three renowned waterfalls near Ubud include Tegenungan Waterfall, Kanto Lampo Waterfall, and Tukad Cepung Waterfall. Check out the 'Guide to Three Famous Waterfalls Around Ubud' [here](/blog/2023-07-19-64db6b82fd897800013a995f/).

Waterfalls are ideal for exploring the beauty of nature or capturing stunning Instagram photos. Escaping the city's hustle and bustle, wander along trails through lush jungles, as if stepping into a mysterious hidden realm. Standing before a waterfall, the splashing water and rushing currents make you feel the awe-inspiring power of nature.
