The renowned Hong Kong YouTuber "Crazy with YU Fangting" recently traveled to Bali, staying there for over ten days and experiencing various exciting activities. In her video, she compiled a list of five must-do activities in Bali. Let's follow her footsteps and explore the unique charm of Bali together.

## First: Surfing Experience

The first must-do activity is experiencing surfing. The three of them took surfing lessons in Canggu, which included a two-hour surfing session and the chance to witness Bali's stunning sunset. She marveled at the sunset, describing it as indescribably beautiful, with distinct layers of colors, calling it the most breathtaking sunset she has ever seen.

## Second: Island Hopping

The second activity she recommends is visiting Bali's offshore island, Nusa Penida. To reach Nusa Penida faster, they stayed in Sanur, where there is a pier, and it only takes a 30-minute boat ride to get there. Early the next morning, they joined a day trip to Nusa Penida. Their first stop was Crystal Bay, a secluded paradise with no Wi-Fi, where they planned to fully enjoy this slice of heaven and temporarily forget the world. Upon arrival, they expected a small group of 30 or 40 people, but surprisingly, over 150 people disembarked together, resembling a flock of ducks. However, to their surprise, they were assigned to independent small tour vehicles, with four people per group, making the experience feel relaxed and comfortable.

After arriving at Nusa Penida, they leisurely enjoyed breakfast, drank beer, and played with stray dogs on the beach at Crystal Bay. Although she didn’t find the beach scenery particularly outstanding, the water was as clear as Long Ke Wan. Next, they visited other famous spots like Angels Billabong and Broken Beach. Despite the crowds, the geological landscapes were still breathtakingly stunning. Finally, they arrived at a dinosaur-shaped beach with sapphire-blue waters, where the sea displayed beautiful gradients of depth, leaving them in awe.

Experiencing unique accommodations in Bali is a must. They stayed in a rustic bamboo villa in Ubud, which cost only USD$250 per night and came with a private pool, kitchen, and two bedrooms - excellent value for money. The villa was creatively designed, featuring a private pool right at the entrance, with a sofa area beside it perfect for hosting small parties. The second floor had an independent bathroom with floor-to-ceiling glass windows facing a rustic shower area, creating a serene atmosphere. The terrace offered panoramic mountain views, making it an ideal spot for enjoying drinks while taking in the scenery.

## Chapter 4: Bali Swing

![Crazy with YU芳婷視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_34c80e7c49fa6780e9392935db7bcd05.jpg)

Crazy with YU Fangting video screenshot

Apart from admiring the beautiful scenery, experiencing the swing is a must-do in Bali. They visited Aloha Ubud, which features six swing photo spots. The entrance fee is only USD$6 per person, and you can even rent various wedding dresses to capture dreamy bridal photos. The staff will carefully guide you on poses and angles to ensure the perfect shot. While girls can swing longer for more photos, guys are limited to just a few swings for safety reasons. However, safety ropes ensure the security of all visitors.

## Chapter 5: Instagrammable Waterfalls

![Crazy with YU芳婷視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d8055c47f3c5d016b36cdd1708e918f6.jpg)

Crazy with YU Fangting video screenshot

The final stop of their journey was a hidden waterfall in Ubud. After a short hike, they were greeted by the breathtaking panoramic view of the waterfall, though it was crowded with photographers. The site offers basic amenities like lockers, restrooms, and changing rooms, making it very convenient. It takes just five minutes to reach the photo area by the waterfall. The staff there are highly professional, guiding visitors on angles and poses. She was happy to tip for the best perspectives. For future visits, she recommends bringing an extra set of clothes, as swimming in the waterfall can leave you feeling chilly.

▶**Hidden Gem**

The Ubud area is not only famous for its culture and art but also boasts breathtaking natural landscapes, with waterfalls being a must-visit. The three renowned waterfalls near Ubud are Tegenungan Waterfall, Kanto Lampo Waterfall, and Tukad Cepung Waterfall. Check out [this guide to the three famous waterfalls around Ubud](/blog/2023-07-19-64db6b82fd897800013a995f/).

Waterfalls are ideal for exploring nature's beauty and capturing Instagram-worthy photos. Escape the city's hustle and bustle, wander along trails through lush jungles, and feel like you've entered a mysterious realm. Standing before a waterfall, the splashing water and rushing currents let you experience the awe-inspiring power of nature.
