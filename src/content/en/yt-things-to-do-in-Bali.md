The renowned Hong Kong YouTuber "[Crazy with YU Fangting](https://www.youtube.com/@crazywithyu)" recently traveled to Bali, staying there for over ten days and experiencing a variety of exciting activities. In her video, she compiled a list of five must-do activities in Bali. Let's follow her footsteps and explore the unique charm of Bali together.

## First: Surfing Experience

The first must-do activity is experiencing surfing. The three of them took surfing lessons in Canggu, which included a two-hour surfing session and the chance to witness the stunning Bali sunset. She marveled at the sunset, describing it as indescribably beautiful with distinct layers of colors, calling it the most breathtaking sunset she has ever seen.

## Second: Island Hopping

The second activity she recommends is visiting Bali's neighboring island, Nusa Penida. To get there faster, they stayed in Sanur, where there is a pier, and it only takes a 30-minute boat ride to reach Nusa Penida. Early the next morning, they joined a day tour to Nusa Penida. Their first stop was Crystal Bay, a secluded paradise with no Wi-Fi, where they planned to fully enjoy this slice of heaven and temporarily disconnect from the world. Upon arrival, they expected a small group of 30 to 40 people, but surprisingly, over 150 people disembarked together, resembling a flock of ducks. However, to their surprise, they were assigned to independent small tour vehicles, with four people per group, making the experience relaxed and comfortable.

Upon arriving at Nusa Penida, they leisurely enjoyed breakfast, beer, and played with stray dogs on the beach at Crystal Bay. Although she didn't find the beach scenery particularly outstanding, the water was as clear as Long Ke Wan. Next, they visited other famous spots like Angels Billabong and Broken Beach. Despite the crowds, the geological landscapes were still breathtakingly stunning. Finally, they arrived at a dinosaur-shaped turquoise beach where the water displayed beautiful gradients of depth, leaving them in awe.

## Third Bamboo Villa Stay

Experiencing unique accommodations is a must when in Bali. They stayed in a rustic bamboo villa in Ubud, which cost only USD$260 per night and came with a private pool, kitchen, and two bedrooms - an incredible value. The villa's design was ingenious, featuring a private pool right at the entrance and a sofa area by the pool perfect for small gatherings. The second floor had a separate bathroom with floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking a natural shower area, creating a serene atmosphere. The terrace offered stunning mountain views, making it an ideal spot for enjoying drinks while taking in the scenery.

## Chapter 4: Bali Swing

![Crazy with YU芳婷視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_34c80e7c49fa6780e9392935db7bcd05.jpg)

Crazy with YU Fangting video screenshot

Apart from admiring the beautiful scenery, experiencing the swing is a must-do in Bali. They visited Aloha Ubud, which features six swing spots perfect for photo ops. The entrance fee is just USD$6 per person, and you can even rent various wedding dresses to capture dreamy bridal photos. The staff will carefully guide you on poses and angles to ensure the best shots. While girls can swing longer for more photos, boys are limited to just a few swings to avoid any danger. However, safety ropes ensure the security of all visitors.

## Chapter 5: Instagrammable Waterfalls

![Crazy with YU芳婷視頻截圖](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_d8055c47f3c5d016b36cdd1708e918f6.jpg)

Crazy with YU Fangting video screenshot

The final stop of their journey was a hidden waterfall in Ubud. They had to hike a short mountain trail to witness the grand panorama of the waterfall, which was crowded with photographers. However, the site is equipped with lockers, restrooms, and changing rooms, making it very convenient. It only takes five minutes to reach the waterfall photo area. The waterfall staff are highly professional, guiding you on shooting angles and poses. She was happy to tip for the best vantage point. For next time, she advises bringing an extra set of clothes, as swimming in the waterfall can leave you feeling chilly.

▶**Hidden Gem**

The Ubud area is not only famous for its culture and art but also boasts breathtaking natural landscapes, with waterfalls being a must-visit. The three renowned waterfalls near Ubud are Tegenungan Waterfall, Kanto Lampo Waterfall, and Tukad Cepung Waterfall. Check out [this guide to the three famous waterfalls around Ubud](/blog/ubud-waterfalls-guide/)

Waterfalls are ideal for exploring the beauty of nature or capturing stunning Instagram photos. Escape the city noise and wander along trails through lush jungles, as if entering a mysterious hidden realm. Standing in front of a waterfall, the splashing water and rushing currents make you feel the awe-inspiring power of nature.
