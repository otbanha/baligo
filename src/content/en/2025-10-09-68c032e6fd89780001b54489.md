This time, I want to share our **8-day, 7-night scooter🛵 adventure in Bali** as a couple. Exploring every corner of the island on two wheels was both thrilling and liberating for us. The biggest takeaway and tip for riding a scooter in Bali is: **"Yield! Yield! Yield!"** Whether at narrow T-junctions or chaotic crossroads, knowing how to yield is crucial. Riding requires boldness and attentiveness—keeping pace with the traffic ahead, speeding up or slowing down as needed without hesitation ensures a safe and smooth journey. Once you master this, riding in Bali can become a truly joyful experience!

## Staying in Kuta

**Day One**: After arriving at our hotel in the afternoon, we wasted no time and rented a scooter to kick off our free-spirited adventure. We casually roamed around Kuta and Seminyak to familiarize ourselves with the roads and traffic atmosphere. For dinner, we chose the locally renowned **Wahaha Restaurant**, famous for its flavorful and tender pork ribs, perfect for a hearty meal. We stayed at **The Vira Bali Boutique Hotel**, conveniently located in Kuta near the airport, ideal for travelers just arriving. The hotel thoughtfully offers free shuttle services and complimentary coffee and snacks from 6 PM to 10 PM, allowing us to unwind after a day of travel.

**Day Two**: In the morning, we enjoyed a leisurely **half-day beach trip to Nusa Dua**. The beach here is clean, the sea water crystal clear, and the atmosphere more serene than Kuta or Canggu, making it perfect for a stroll and relaxation. In the afternoon, we visited the new large shopping mall **ICON Bali**, which combines shopping, dining, and sightseeing, offering a cool, air-conditioned retreat from the midday heat.

https://youtu.be/hdIKOX5wghU

In the evening, we made a special trip to the famous **Asoka Market Night Market** in Denpasar, Bali's largest and liveliest night market. The stalls were abundant, offering affordable and delicious local snacks. We tried roast suckling pig rice, meatball soup (Bakso), and Indonesian fried rice (Nasi Goreng), each costing just 25K IDR (less than USD$2), satisfying both our hunger and taste buds. We returned to Kuta for the night, recharging for the next day's adventures.

**Editor's Note**: **Asoka Market** is often grouped together with the neighboring **Kereneng Night Market**, forming a bustling night market area with thousands of stalls. From authentic Babi Guling (roast pork) to vibrant batik fabrics, handmade tableware, snacks, and various desserts, everything is available. Tourists start pouring in from 4 PM, entering a small universe of food and culture, making it incredibly lively.

## Ubud Accommodation

**Day 3**: This day marked the highlight of our trip. We left our large luggage at our hotel in Kuta, taking only two 7 kg carry-on bags securely fastened with cargo straps and a small backpack for a light journey. We rode our scooter straight to our accommodation in Ubud, dropped off our luggage, and officially began our itinerary. Our first stop was **Pura Ulun Danu Bratan**, located at an altitude of 1200 meters. This lakeside temple seemed to float on the water, surrounded by mountains and mist, creating a postcard-perfect scene.

![當天的水神寺，雲霧瀰漫](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_39c5eeaf14517308ba67848462ecddf9.jpg)

The temple was enveloped in mist that day.

Next, we visited the recently Instagram-famous **Bali Handara Gate**, where the symmetrical Balinese gate paired with distant green mountains made it a must-visit spot for photos. Back in Ubud, we chose **Pison Ubud** for dinner. Their signature "Ubud Dirty Duck Rice" lived up to its reputation—crispy fried duck paired with fragrant rice was rich and satisfying.

**Day 4**: The highlight of the day was the **Mount Batur Sunrise**. We woke up at 3 AM, quickly got ready, and rode our scooter to the volcano, covering the 45 km distance from Ubud in about 70 minutes. Instead of hiking, we opted for the **Sukawana Sunrise Spot** viewpoint, where we could comfortably watch the sunrise without the exhaustion of climbing. As the sun rose behind the clouds, painting the volcano and lake in golden light, we felt that the early wake-up was entirely worth it.

![巴杜爾火山旁的觀景台，清晨 5 點一開門就陸續有遊客到訪，因為地點視野極佳，是拍攝日出的熱門場景。對於不想辛苦爬山的人來說，這裡是一個輕鬆欣賞日出的好選擇，汽車貨騎機車就能直接抵達，非常方便。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cae628e2c53a24c4bfb2eac74c4ccab.jpg)

The viewpoint near Mount Batur opens at 5 AM, and visitors start arriving shortly after. With its excellent vantage point, it's a popular spot for capturing sunrise photos. For those who don’t want to hike, this is a convenient and easy way to enjoy the sunrise, as it’s accessible by car or scooter.

After watching the sunrise, we headed back to the hotel for a quick nap before starting the second part of our day. At noon, we explored **Ubud Market**, where my wife happily bought an Indonesian-style dress for just 100K IDR—a great deal and a meaningful souvenir. In the afternoon, we visited **Tegalalang Rice Terrace**, Ubud’s most iconic rice field landscape. The layered green terraces paired with swaying coconut trees make every photo look like a postcard.

## Jimbaran Accommodation

**Day Five**: Early in the morning, we headed to **Ubud Palace**. With fewer visitors at this hour, the palace felt especially tranquil, making it the ideal time to capture photos of the pure Balinese architecture and intricate carvings.

Next, we visited the famous **Tirta Empul Temple**, a sacred site for locals where many devotees come to perform purification rituals in the holy spring water. Entering the temple, the atmosphere was solemn yet peaceful, evoking a deep connection between nature and faith.

Known for its large wooden art installations, every corner is full of dramatic flair, making it a perfect spot for photos, especially beloved by women and ideal for social media check-ins.

The sunset at Uluwatu Temple is one of the most unforgettable sights in Bali for many travelers. Built on a cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean, as the sun slowly sinks into the horizon, the sky is painted with golden and orange hues, reflecting off the stunning coastline, creating a romantic and sacred atmosphere. Coupled with the famous Kecak dance performance, one can truly feel the unique cultural and natural intertwining that Bali offers.

**Day Six**: Today's itinerary was more relaxed as we visited the **Pandawa Beach area**. Once a hidden gem, it has now been developed into a complete tourist zone. Upon entering, there's a special experience not to be missed: purchasing a ticket to ride the shuttle car (30K IDR per person) to the so-called "Blue Road" of **Tanah Barak**. The most striking feature here is the towering limestone cliffs on either side, described by some as "Bali's version of Japan's Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route," but with majestic limestone instead of snow. This scene is particularly popular among young people, making it a powerful spot for photos and social media check-ins.

**第六天**：今天的行程比較悠閒，我們來到 **Pandawa Beach 園區**。這裡原本是隱藏的秘境海灘，如今已規劃成完整的遊客區域。進入園區後，有一個特別的體驗不可錯過：必須購票搭乘穿梭小車（每人 30K 印尼盾）前往號稱“藍色公路”的 **Tanah Barak**。這裡最吸引人的景觀就是兩側高聳的岩壁，有人形容它像是「峇里島版的日本立山黑部雪壁」，只不過換成了壯麗的石灰岩。這個場景在年輕人中相當受歡迎，無論拍照或打卡都十分有氣勢。

![這處壯觀的岩壁近年成為許多網紅口耳相傳的打卡景點，因為角度與光影的變化，無論怎麼拍都顯得特別好看。要前往這裡，必須先從 Pandawa Beach 入口進入，再搭乘園區內的接駁車才能抵達。沿途景色也相當迷人，抵達後更能感受到岩壁與藍天大海交織出的震撼畫面，是拍照愛好者不能錯過的美拍地點。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4f8fe37b3a9f45664aad73384dc6a559.jpg)

This spectacular cliff has become a popular Instagram spot in recent years, thanks to its ever-changing angles and lighting that make every photo look stunning. To get here, you must first enter through the Pandawa Beach entrance and then take a shuttle bus provided by the park. The scenic journey is equally captivating, and upon arrival, you'll be awestruck by the breathtaking view of the cliff blending with the blue sky and ocean. It's a must-visit location for photography enthusiasts.

As the sun slowly sets, the sea turns into a golden-orange hue, accompanied by the sound of waves and a gentle breeze, creating an enchanting atmosphere. Many visitors enjoy ordering a seafood candlelight dinner on the beach, savoring the food while watching the sun dip into the Indian Ocean. The scene is poetic and picturesque, making Jimbaran a must-visit romantic experience for couples and families.

The streets are lined with small shops selling clothes, handicrafts, and souvenirs, exuding a strong Balinese vibe. With air conditioning, it's a comfortable and relaxing place to shop.

Upon arrival, we received a blessing ceremony from a local Hindu priest, who marked our foreheads with holy rice and flowers, making it a uniquely spiritual experience.

This restaurant is famous for its grilled pork ribs, which are tender and coated in a rich sauce. The aroma was irresistible as soon as the dish arrived, making it perfect for indulging. That night, we stayed in Kuta, preparing for the final day of our journey.

**Day 8**: On our last day, we leisurely explored the Kuta area without any tight schedules. Before noon, we returned our rented scooter and headed straight to the airport, concluding our 8-day, 7-night Bali couple’s adventure. Although our trip was budget-friendly, relying mainly on scooters for transportation—quite different from the usual private car tours—it allowed us to deeply experience the authenticity and freedom of the island. For us, this journey was both economical and filled with unique adventures tailored just for us as a couple.

### Final Thoughts

This **8-day, 7-night Bali couple’s adventure** was a journey where we navigated the island on scooters, weaving through cities, rice fields, and coastlines, creating unforgettable memories. Without the luxury of private car tours, we were able to immerse ourselves in the local rhythm, stopping and exploring at our own pace. Along the way, we shared romantic moments and overcame small challenges, making the trip even more meaningful. This 'budget travel' style, for us, truly embodies the essence of travel: freedom, authenticity, and our own little adventures.
