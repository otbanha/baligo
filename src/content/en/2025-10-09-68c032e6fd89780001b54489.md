> The following content is adapted from fellow traveler [Lin Jinsheng](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/16zBggKw15/)'s travelogue:

This time, I want to share our **8-day, 7-night motorbike🛵 adventure in Bali**. Exploring every corner of the island on two wheels was an exhilarating and liberating experience for us. The biggest takeaway and tip for riding a motorbike in Bali is: **"Yield! Yield! Yield!"** Whether at narrow T-junctions or chaotic crossroads, knowing when to yield is crucial. Riding requires boldness and attentiveness—follow the rhythm of the traffic ahead, speed up when needed, slow down when necessary, and don’t hesitate. Mastering this makes riding in Bali a truly joyful experience!

## Staying in Kuta

**Day 1**: After arriving at the hotel in the afternoon, we wasted no time and rented a motorbike to kick off our free-spirited journey. We casually explored the Kuta and Seminyak areas to get familiar with the roads and traffic vibe. For dinner, we chose the well-known **Wahaha Restaurant**, famous for its flavorful and tender pork ribs, perfect for a hearty meal. We stayed at **The Vira Bali Boutique Hotel**, conveniently located in Kuta and very close to the airport, making it ideal for travelers arriving on the first day. The hotel thoughtfully offers free shuttle services and complimentary coffee and snacks from 6 PM to 10 PM, allowing guests to unwind after a busy day.

**Day 2**: In the morning, we enjoyed a leisurely **half-day beach trip to Nusa Dua**. The beach here is clean, the water is crystal clear, and the atmosphere is more serene compared to Kuta and Canggu, making it perfect for strolling and relaxing. In the afternoon, we visited the newly opened **ICON Bali**, a large shopping mall combining shopping, dining, and sightseeing features, offering a cool escape from the midday heat.

In the evening, we headed to the famous **Asoka Market night market** in Denpasar, Bali's largest and liveliest night market. The stalls were abundant, and the local street food was both affordable and delicious. We tried roast pork rice, meatball soup (Bakso), and Indonesian fried rice (Nasi Goreng), each costing only 25K IDR (less than USD$2), satisfying both our hunger and taste buds. We returned to Kuta for the night, recharging for the next day's adventures.

> Editor’s Note: **Asoka Market** is often grouped with the neighboring **Kereneng Night Market**, forming a bustling night market area with thousands of stalls. From authentic Babi Guling (roast pork) to colorful batik fabrics, handmade utensils, snacks, and various desserts, it’s all there. Tourists start pouring in from 4 PM, creating a lively universe of food and culture.

## Accommodation in Ubud

**Day 3**: This day marked the beginning of the main highlights of our itinerary. We left our large luggage at our hotel in Kuta, taking only two 7 kg carry-on bags secured tightly with cargo straps, along with a small backpack, traveling light. We rode our scooter straight to our accommodation in Ubud, settled our luggage, and then officially started our day. Our first stop was **Pura Ulun Danu Bratan**, a lakeside temple situated at an altitude of 1200 meters. This temple appears to float on the lake, surrounded by mountains and mist, creating a scene as dreamy as a postcard.

![當天的水神寺，雲霧瀰漫](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_39c5eeaf14517308ba67848462ecddf9.jpg)

The temple was enveloped in mist that day.

Next, we visited the recently popular **Bali Handara Gate**, which has become a sensation on social media. The symmetrical and solemn Balinese gate, set against the backdrop of distant green mountains, truly makes it a 'must-visit spot for Instagram'. Returning to Ubud, we chose **Pison Ubud** for dinner. Their signature 'Ubud Dirty Duck' lived up to its reputation, with crispy fried duck paired with aromatic rice, offering a rich and satisfying flavor.

**Day 4**: The highlight of the day was the **Mount Batur Sunrise**. We woke up at 3 AM, quickly prepared, and set off on our scooter. The journey from Ubud to the volcano was about 45 kilometers, taking approximately 70 minutes. This time, we opted not to hike up the mountain but instead went directly to the **Sukawana Sunrise Spot** viewpoint. Here, we could easily witness the sunrise over the volcano without the exhaustion of climbing, enjoying a more relaxed experience. As the sun rose from behind the clouds, casting golden light over the volcano and lake, we felt that all the early morning effort was truly worth it.

![巴杜爾火山旁的觀景台，清晨 5 點一開門就陸續有遊客到訪，因為地點視野極佳，是拍攝日出的熱門場景。對於不想辛苦爬山的人來說，這裡是一個輕鬆欣賞日出的好選擇，汽車貨騎機車就能直接抵達，非常方便。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cae628e2c53a24c4bfb2eac74c4ccab.jpg)

After enjoying the sunrise, we headed back to the hotel for a quick nap before starting the second part of our itinerary. The layered green terraces paired with swaying coconut trees made every shot look like a postcard.

**Day Six**: Today's itinerary was more relaxed as we visited **Pandawa Beach Park**. Once a hidden gem, it has now been developed into a complete tourist area. Upon entering the park, there's a special experience not to be missed: purchasing a ticket to ride the shuttle car (USD$1 per person) to the so-called "Blue Road" of **Tanah Barak**. The most striking feature here is the towering cliffs on both sides, described by some as "Bali's version of Japan's Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route," but with magnificent limestone instead of snow. This scene is particularly popular among young people, making it a powerful spot for photos and social media check-ins.

To reach this spot, you must first enter through Pandawa Beach and then take the park's shuttle. The journey itself is quite scenic, and upon arrival, the interplay of cliffs, blue sky, and ocean creates a breathtaking scene, making it a must-visit location for photography enthusiasts.

### Final Thoughts

This **8-day, 7-night Bali couple's trip** saw us navigating the island on scooters, weaving through cities, rice fields, and coastlines, creating many one-of-a-kind memories. While we didn’t indulge in luxurious private car tours, we were able to immerse ourselves in the local rhythm of life, stopping freely and exploring at our own pace. Along the way, we shared romantic moments and overcame small challenges together, making the trip feel even more meaningful. This "budget travel" approach, for us, truly captured the essence of travel: freedom, authenticity, and our own little adventures.
