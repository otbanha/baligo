> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by fellow traveler [Lin Jinsheng](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/16zBggKw15/):

This time, I want to share our ****8-day, 7-night scooter🛵 adventure in Bali**** as a couple. Exploring every corner of the island on two wheels was both thrilling and liberating for us. The biggest takeaway and trick for riding a scooter in Bali is: ****'Yield! Yield! Yield!'**** Whether at narrow T-junctions or chaotic crossroads, knowing how to yield is crucial. Riding requires boldness and attentiveness—follow the rhythm of the traffic ahead, speed up when needed, slow down when necessary, and don’t hesitate. This ensures a safe and smooth journey. Once you master this, scooting around Bali becomes a joyous experience!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_65f5978b6ecdb6f11de5df9993c1e7d5.jpg)

## Staying in Kuta

****Day One****: After arriving at our hotel in the afternoon, we wasted no time renting a scooter to kick off our free-spirited adventure. We first roamed around Kuta and Seminyak to familiarize ourselves with the roads and traffic vibe. For dinner, we chose the renowned ****Wahaha Restaurant****, famous for its flavorful and tender pork ribs, perfect for hearty enjoyment. We stayed at ****The Vira Bali Boutique Hotel****, conveniently located in Kuta, close to the airport—ideal for travelers arriving on their first day. The hotel thoughtfully offers free shuttle services and complimentary coffee and snacks from 6 PM to 10 PM, allowing us to unwind after a busy day.

▶[****Check Availability & Rates for The Vira Bali Boutique Hotel****](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=49780)

****Day Two****: In the morning, we enjoyed a leisurely ****half-day beach outing in Nusa Dua****. The beach here is clean, the sea crystal clear, and the atmosphere quieter than Kuta or Canggu, making it perfect for strolling and relaxing. In the afternoon, we visited the newly opened ****ICON Bali****, a large shopping mall combining retail, dining, and tourist attractions. Its air-conditioned environment is a great escape from the midday heat.

By evening, we headed to Denpasar’s famous ****Asoka Market Night Market****, Bali’s largest and liveliest night market. The stalls were abundant, offering affordable and delicious local snacks. We tried roast pork rice, meatball soup (Bakso), and Indonesian fried rice (Nasi Goreng), each priced at just 25K IDR (less than USD$2), satisfying both our hunger and taste buds. We returned to Kuta for the night, recharging for the next day’s adventures.

> Editor’s Note: ****Asoka Market**** is often grouped with the neighboring ****Kereneng Night Market****, forming a bustling night market area with thousands of stalls. From authentic Babi Guling (roast pork) to vibrant batik fabrics, handmade utensils, snacks, and desserts, it’s all here. Visitors start pouring in from 4 PM, creating a lively universe of food and culture.

## Staying in Ubud

****Day Three****: The highlight of our trip began today. We left our large luggage at our hotel in Kuta, taking only two 7kg carry-ons securely fastened with cargo straps, plus a small backpack for a light journey. We rode our scooter straight to our accommodation in Ubud, dropped off our bags, and officially started our itinerary. Our first stop was ****Pura Ulun Danu Bratan****, a lakeside temple that appears to float on the water, surrounded by mountains and mist, creating a scene straight out of a postcard.

![當天的水神寺，雲霧瀰漫](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_39c5eeaf14517308ba67848462ecddf9.jpg)

The temple was enveloped in clouds and mist that day.

Next, we visited the recently Instagram-famous ****Bali Handara Gate****. The symmetrical Balinese gate against the backdrop of distant green mountains truly makes it a 'must-visit spot for that perfect photo.' Back in Ubud, we chose ****Pison Ubud**** for dinner. Their signature 'Ubud Dirty Duck Rice' lived up to its reputation—crispy fried duck paired with spiced rice was richly flavorful and satisfying.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a56fbdda5b858cfdfdd9872de32ca5e3.jpg)

That night, we stayed at ****Pondok Naya****, a guesthouse nestled in the countryside, offering a tranquil environment surrounded by lush rice fields. We highly recommend having your own transportation to truly enjoy its serenity and convenience.

[****▶Check Availability & Prices at Pondok Naya****](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=24120992)

****Day Four****: The main event today was the ****Mount Batur Sunrise****. We woke up at 3 AM, quickly got ready, and rode our scooter for about 70 minutes over 45 kilometers from Ubud to the volcano. Instead of the strenuous hike, we opted for the ****Sukawana Sunrise Spot**** viewpoint. Here, we could comfortably watch the sunrise over the volcano without the exhaustion of climbing, adding a sense of ease to the experience. As the sun rose behind the clouds, casting golden light over the volcano and lake, every bit of the early morning effort felt worthwhile.

![巴杜爾火山旁的觀景台，清晨 5 點一開門就陸續有遊客到訪，因為地點視野極佳，是拍攝日出的熱門場景。對於不想辛苦爬山的人來說，這裡是一個輕鬆欣賞日出的好選擇，汽車貨騎機車就能直接抵達，非常方便。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1cae628e2c53a24c4bfb2eac74c4ccab.jpg)

The viewpoint by Mount Batur started welcoming visitors as early as 5 AM, thanks to its excellent vantage point for sunrise photography. It's a great alternative for those who prefer not to hike, easily accessible by car or scooter.

After enjoying the sunrise, we returned to our hotel for a bit more sleep before starting the second part of our day. At noon, we explored the ****Ubud Market****, where my wife happily purchased an Indonesian-style dress for just 100K IDR, a bargain with great souvenir value. In the afternoon, we headed to the ****Tegalalang Rice Terrace****, Ubud's most iconic rice field landscape. The terraced green fields paired with swaying coconut trees make every photo look like a postcard.

![位在德哥拉朗梯田旁的 Tis Cafe，是近年很受網紅推薦的一間餐廳。店內外都設計得相當好拍，無論是眺望梯田的座位，還是充滿度假感的泳池區，都很適合拍照打卡。餐點與飲品價格不算貴，整體氛圍輕鬆愜意，非常適合邊用餐邊欣賞梯田美景。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_22c43ec1ee9705f833914609831502fd.jpg)

Located next to the Tegallalang Rice Terraces, Tis Cafe has become a popular spot recommended by influencers in recent years. Both the interior and exterior are designed to be highly photogenic, whether it's the seats overlooking the terraces or the resort-style pool area, making it perfect for photo ops and social media posts. The food and drinks are reasonably priced, and the overall atmosphere is relaxed and pleasant, ideal for enjoying a meal while taking in the scenic views of the rice terraces.

![這次沒有在遊記裡特別提到的麝香貓咖啡小園區，其實也蠻值得順道一訪。一杯麝香貓咖啡約 60K，而進入園區門票一人 25K，還能享用五款咖啡與五款茶的試喝體驗。園區裡設有不少可愛又有特色的拍照角落，不僅能品嚐咖啡，還能拍下幾張美照留念，是一個小而精緻、氣氛悠閒的景點。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_8fb3813a96cbc0f980efa9461b8dc629.jpg)

The small civet coffee plantation, which wasn't specifically mentioned in the travelogue, is actually worth a visit. A cup of civet coffee costs around USD$2, and the entrance fee is USD$0.80 per person, which includes a tasting experience of five types of coffee and five types of tea. The plantation features many cute and unique photo corners, allowing visitors not only to savor coffee but also to capture some beautiful photos. It's a small yet refined and leisurely spot.

![烏布有一處隱藏景點，因為「烏布攻略」的分享而開始受到關注。這裡原本是一間陶瓷工坊，即使不購買陶瓷，也只需付 10K 的入場費，就能在園區裡拍照。場景設計獨特、色彩繽紛，拍出來的效果相當夢幻，尤其深受女性遊客喜愛，被譽為烏布的美拍小秘境。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_1a757e29d355a841cea2b0d608e6177e.jpg)

[Ubud has a hidden gem that has started gaining attention thanks to 'Ubud Guide' shares. Originally a ceramic workshop, even if you don't purchase ceramics, you only need to pay USD$0.30 as an entrance fee to take photos in the area. The scene designs are unique and colorful, creating dreamy photo effects, especially beloved by female visitors, earning it the title of Ubud's photo paradise.](https://s.id/ubud100)

For dinner, we chose the popular and affordable local restaurant ****Sun Sun Warung****. The portions are generous, and the prices are very reasonable, making it perfect for a satisfying meal and a small treat during the trip. That night, we stayed again at [Pondok Naya](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=24120992) in Ubud, falling asleep amidst the serene night view of the rice fields.

## Jimbaran Accommodation

****Day Five****: Early in the morning, we headed to ****Ubud Palace****. At this hour, there were few tourists, and the palace was exceptionally quiet, making it the perfect time for photography to capture the pure Balinese architectural and carving details.

> Quick Reference: [【Must-Visit Spots in Ubud】Ubud Palace: A Treasure of Balinese Culture and History｜Opening Hours, Tickets, Performance Times, Nearby Attractions, Day Trip Recommendations](/blog/2024-11-05-6728cc00fd89780001c9bbdc/)

Next, we visited the famous ****Tirta Empul Temple****, a sacred place for locals where many devotees come to perform purification rituals in the holy spring. Upon entering the temple, the atmosphere was solemn and peaceful, allowing us to feel a deep connection with nature and faith.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_2e29af9eca891ff9b6e50c096a6ddc9b.jpg)

> Quick Reference: [【2025】Complete Guide to Tirta Empul Temple: Bali's Most Sacred Purification Water Temple Experience](/blog/2025-06-18-6852cdc2fd89780001e0b7ad/)

After the visit, we rode another 3.5 kilometers to the emerging Instagram hotspot ****Sari Timbul by Kubu Bali****. Known for its various large wooden art installations, every corner is full of dramatic appeal, making it a highly photogenic spot, especially popular among women, and a perfect place for social media check-ins.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4d3debb3b405f5d1a6a333a89cd8b363.jpg)

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_ee40d5d487407ad015868390bd05b4e4.jpg)

This place was originally a ceramic workshop. Even if you don't purchase ceramics, you can pay an entrance fee of USD$3 to take photos in the area. The unique scene design and vibrant colors create dreamy photo effects, making it particularly popular among female tourists. It's hailed as Ubud's hidden gem for beautiful photography.

For more information about Sari Timbul by Kubu Bali, refer to the '[Ubud Guide](https://s.id/ubud100)' which covers over 100 attractions.

After finishing our Ubud itinerary, we returned to our hotel in Kuta to retrieve our large luggage, then rode to Jimbaran to check into The Jimbaran Villa by Ini Vie Hospitality. This villa features a private pool and tranquil surroundings, making it especially suitable for couples or honeymooners.

▶[Check Availability & Rates for The Jimbaran Villa by Ini Vie Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=5730375)

After a short rest, we headed to the magnificent Uluwatu Temple in the evening. Perched atop a cliff, it offers breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean. As the sun sets, the sky and sea blend into one, creating a mesmerizing scene. We also enjoyed the classic Kecak Fire Dance, accompanied by chanting and flickering flames, making for a culturally rich and visually stunning experience. We returned to the villa, concluding a fulfilling day.

![烏魯瓦圖神廟的夕陽，是許多旅人心中最難忘的峇里島景致之一。神廟依山而建，懸崖直落印度洋，當夕陽緩緩沉入海平面時，整片天空染上金黃與橘紅色，映照著壯麗的海岸線，氣氛既浪漫又神聖。此時若再搭配著著名的卡洽舞表演，更能感受到峇里島獨有的文化與自然交織的震撼。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_b65d1bc305a0ade0de1f4849c0754f0b.jpg)

The sunset at Uluwatu Temple is one of the most unforgettable scenes in Bali for many travelers. Built on a cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean, as the sun slowly dips below the horizon, the sky turns golden and orange, casting a romantic and sacred atmosphere over the coastline. Pairing this with the famous Kecak Dance performance enhances the unique cultural and natural blend that Bali offers.

> Quick Reference: [Bali's Top 6 Must-See Kecak Fire Dance Locations: A Visual and Cultural Feast](/blog/2024-12-12-675ad354fd89780001c09f24/)

Day 6: Today's itinerary was more relaxed as we visited Pandawa Beach. Once a hidden gem, it's now a fully developed tourist area. Upon entering, don't miss the special experience of taking a shuttle (USD$2 per person) to Tanah Barak, known as the 'Blue Road.' The most striking feature here is the towering limestone cliffs on both sides, often compared to Japan's Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route, but with majestic limestone instead of snow. This spot is particularly popular among young people for its photogenic and impressive scenery.

![這處壯觀的岩壁近年成為許多網紅口耳相傳的打卡景點，因為角度與光影的變化，無論怎麼拍都顯得特別好看。要前往這裡，必須先從 Pandawa Beach 入口進入，再搭乘園區內的接駁車才能抵達。沿途景色也相當迷人，抵達後更能感受到岩壁與藍天大海交織出的震撼畫面，是拍照愛好者不能錯過的美拍地點。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_4f8fe37b3a9f45664aad73384dc6a559.jpg)

This spectacular cliff has recently become a trending spot among influencers due to its varying angles and lighting, making every photo look stunning. To get here, you must enter through Pandawa Beach and take the shuttle provided by the park. The journey itself is scenic, and upon arrival, the interplay of cliffs, blue sky, and ocean creates a breathtaking backdrop, making it a must-visit for photography enthusiasts.

Quick Reference: [Bali Batu Barak/Tanah Barak: A Stunning Journey Along the Blue Road/Cliff Road](/blog/2025-03-06-67c9a234fd897800014b5a11/)

In the afternoon, we returned to our villa in Jimbaran, choosing to do nothing but relax by the private pool, soaking in the vacation vibe. As dusk approached, we headed to **The Cuisine Bali** at **Jimbaran Beach** for dinner. Known for its romantic seafood candlelit dinners, the tables are set right on the sand, accompanied by the sound of waves and the evening glow, creating a perfect atmosphere. As the sea breeze gently brushed past and the candlelight flickered, the moment we raised our glasses together became one of the most unforgettable memories of our trip.

![金巴蘭海灘的夕陽，被許多人視為峇里島最浪漫的時刻之一。當太陽緩緩落下，海面被染成一片金橘色，伴隨著海浪聲與微風，氣氛特別迷人。許多遊客喜歡在沙灘上點一桌海鮮燭光晚餐，一邊享受美食，一邊看夕陽沉入印度洋，畫面如詩如畫，也讓金巴蘭成為情侶和家人旅行時不可錯過的浪漫體驗。](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_0954d921cc8095489a396a1ade15bdf2.jpg)

The sunset at Jimbaran Beach is considered one of Bali's most romantic moments. As the sun slowly sets, the sea turns into a golden-orange hue, accompanied by the sound of waves and a gentle breeze, creating an enchanting atmosphere. Many visitors enjoy a seafood candlelit dinner on the beach, savoring delicious food while watching the sun dip into the Indian Ocean. The scene is poetic and picturesque, making Jimbaran a must-visit romantic experience for couples and families.

> Quick reference: [Top 10 Seafood Restaurants in Jimbaran, Bali](/blog/2023-09-17-6506748dfd897800018d6be2/)

We stayed again at **[The Jimbaran Villa by Ini Vie Hospitality](https://www.agoda.com/partners/partnersearch.aspx?pcs=1&cid=1961347&hid=5730375)**, continuing to enjoy the serene nightscape of Jimbaran.

**Day 7**: In the morning, we returned to Kuta to drop off our luggage at the hotel and then wandered around the town. The streets are lined with small shops selling clothes, handicrafts, and souvenirs, exuding a distinct Balinese charm. We also visited **Beachwalk Shopping Center**, one of Kuta's liveliest malls, featuring various international brands and restaurants. With air conditioning, it was a comfortable and relaxing place to explore.

In the afternoon, we timed our visit to **Tanah Lot Temple** to coincide with low tide, as it's only during this time that you can wade through the rocky passageway to reach the temple. Upon arrival, we participated in a Hindu blessing ceremony conducted by a local priest, receiving sacred rice and flowers on our foreheads—a unique and special experience.

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_e865be61feb283663c6b4e451024b0a6.jpg)

Quick reference: [Complete Guide to Tanah Lot Temple: Tide Times and Nearby Attractions](/blog/2024-10-28-671f86f1fd89780001de9fae/)

In the evening, we dined at the highly-rated **Naughty Nuri's Warung Seminyak**. Famous for its grilled pork ribs, the meat was tender, and the sauce was rich and flavorful. The aroma was irresistible as soon as the dish arrived, making it perfect for indulging. We spent the night in Kuta, preparing for the final day of our journey.

**Day 8**: On our last day, we leisurely strolled around Kuta without any packed itinerary. Before noon, we returned our rented scooter and headed straight to the airport, concluding our 8-day, 7-night Bali adventure as a couple. Although we opted for a budget-friendly trip, relying mainly on scooters instead of private cars, it allowed us to experience the authenticity and freedom of the island in a deeper way. For us, this journey was both economical and filled with adventurous memories unique to our relationship.

### Final Thoughts

This ****8-day, 7-night Bali couple's trip**** was an unforgettable adventure. We rode scooters through cities, rice fields, and coastlines, creating unique memories. While we didn't opt for luxurious private car tours, we embraced the local lifestyle, stopping freely and exploring at our own pace. Along the way, we shared romantic moments and overcame small challenges together, making our journey more meaningful. This 'budget travel' style truly embodied the essence of travel for us: freedom, authenticity, and our own little adventures.

I've chosen to travel by scooter in ****Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, Laos, and Sri Lanka****. This method is cost-effective, convenient, and liberating, helping me avoid overpriced transportation fees. Over the years, I've accumulated plenty of experience. The biggest downside for me is enduring the scorching sun, which often leads to tanning or sunburn. However, the benefits of controlling my travel pace and exploring freely far outweigh these drawbacks.

However, I want to remind friends planning to ride scooters in Bali: ****While scooters are convenient, they also come with risks****. Accidents in a foreign country, whether hitting someone or being hit, can lead to complicated aftermaths, including high medical costs or compensations, making the experience regrettable. These risks must be carefully considered. Don't overlook safety and security just to save a bit on transportation. If you choose this path, be bold yet cautious, always prioritizing safety to truly enjoy your journey.
