> The following content is adapted from a travelogue shared by group member [Lin Jinsheng](https://www.facebook.com/share/p/16zBggKw15/):

This time, I want to share our **8-day, 7-night scooter🛵 adventure in Bali**. Exploring every corner of the island on two wheels was both thrilling and liberating for us. The biggest takeaway and trick for riding a scooter in Bali is: **"Yield! Yield! Yield!"** Whether at narrow T-junctions or busy crossroads, knowing when to yield is crucial. Riding requires boldness and attentiveness—keeping pace with the traffic ahead, speeding up or slowing down as needed without hesitation. This ensures a safe and smooth journey. Once you master this trick, riding in Bali becomes a truly enjoyable experience!

## Accommodation in Kuta

**Day 1**: After arriving at the hotel in the afternoon, we wasted no time and rented a scooter to kick off our free-spirited adventure. We casually explored the Kuta and Seminyak areas to familiarize ourselves with the roads and traffic atmosphere. For dinner, we chose the renowned **Wahaha Restaurant**, famous for its flavorful and tender pork ribs, perfect for hearty enjoyment. We stayed at **The Vira Bali Boutique Hotel**, conveniently located in Kuta and very close to the airport, making it ideal for travelers arriving on the first day. The hotel thoughtfully offers free shuttle services and complimentary coffee and snacks from 6 PM to 10 PM, allowing guests to relax after a busy day.

**Day 2**: In the morning, we enjoyed a leisurely **half-day beach trip to Nusa Dua**. The beach here is clean, the sea water is clear, and the atmosphere is more tranquil than Kuta or Canggu, making it perfect for strolling and unwinding. In the afternoon, we visited the newly opened **ICON Bali**, a large shopping mall that combines shopping, dining, and sightseeing features. The air-conditioned mall environment is also a great escape from the midday heat.

In the evening, we made a special trip to the famous **Asoka Market Night Market** in Denpasar, the largest and liveliest night market in Bali. The stalls are abundant, and the local street food is both affordable and delicious. We ordered roast pork rice, meatball soup (Bakso), and Indonesian fried rice (Nasi Goreng), each costing only 25K IDR (less than USD$2), satisfying both our hunger and taste buds. We returned to Kuta for the night, recharging for the next day's adventures.

> Editor's Note: **Asoka Market** is often grouped with the neighboring **Kereneng Night Market**, forming a super bustling night market area with thousands of stalls. From authentic Babi Guling (roast pork) to colorful batik fabrics, handmade utensils, snacks, and various desserts, everything is available. Tourists start pouring in from 4 PM, entering a small universe of food and culture, bustling with excitement.

## Staying in Ubud

**Day 3**: This day marked the start of the main highlights of our itinerary. We left our large luggage at our hotel in Kuta, taking only two 7 kg carry-ons secured tightly with cargo straps, along with a small backpack, traveling light. We rode our scooter straight to our accommodation in Ubud, dropped off our luggage, and officially began our day. Our first stop was **Pura Ulun Danu Bratan**, a lakeside temple situated at an altitude of 1,200 meters. This temple appears to float on the lake, surrounded by mountains and mist, creating a dreamy postcard-like scene.

![當天的水神寺，雲霧瀰漫](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_39c5eeaf14517308ba67848462ecddf9.jpg)

The temple was enveloped in clouds and mist that day.

Next, we visited the recently Instagram-famous **Bali Handara Gate**. The symmetrical and majestic Balinese gate, paired with the distant green mountains, truly makes it a must-visit spot for that perfect photo. Returning to Ubud, we chose **Pison Ubud** for dinner. Their signature 'Ubud Dirty Duck' lived up to its reputation—crispy fried duck paired with aromatic rice, rich and satisfying.

**Day 4**: The highlight of the day was the **Mount Batur Sunrise**. We woke up at 3 AM, quickly got ready, and rode our scooter to the volcano, covering about 45 kilometers in roughly 70 minutes. Instead of hiking, we opted for the **Sukawana Sunrise Spot** viewpoint, where we could comfortably watch the sunrise without the strenuous climb. As the sun rose behind the clouds, casting golden light over the volcano and lake, we felt that the early wake-up was entirely worth it.

The layered green terraces paired with swaying coconut trees make every photo look like a postcard.

**Day Six**: Today's itinerary was more relaxed as we visited the **Pandawa Beach area**. Once a hidden gem, it has now been developed into a complete tourist zone. Upon entering, don't miss the special experience of taking a shuttle ride (IDR 30K per person) to the so-called "Blue Road" of **Tanah Barak**. The most striking feature here is the towering cliffs on both sides, described by some as "Bali's version of Japan's Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route," but with magnificent limestone instead of snow. This scene is particularly popular among young people, making it a great spot for photos and social media check-ins.

### Final Thoughts

This **8-day, 7-night Bali couple's free-and-easy trip** was filled with unforgettable memories as we navigated the island on a scooter, weaving through cities, rice fields, and coastlines. While we didn’t opt for luxurious private car tours, we embraced the local rhythm of life, stopping freely and exploring at our own pace. Along the way, we shared romantic moments and overcame small challenges together, making the journey feel even more meaningful. For us, this 'budget-friendly' approach truly captured the essence of travel: freedom, authenticity, and our own little adventure.

I’ve also traveled by scooter in **Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, Laos, and Sri Lanka**. This method is cost-effective, convenient, and liberating, helping me avoid overpriced transportation fees. Over the years, I’ve gained plenty of experience, and while the biggest downside is enduring the scorching sun—often resulting in tanning or sunburn—the benefits of controlling my travel pace and exploring freely far outweigh the drawbacks.

However, I’d like to offer a special reminder to those planning to ride scooters in Bali: **While scooters are convenient, they also come with risks**.
