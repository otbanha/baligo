If you visit Bali, don't miss the Kecak Fire Dance performance! It's an unforgettable experience!

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f8c6296d8f2f09b45c9843546731b432.jpg)

## A Heart-Stirring Performance

Here's a brief introduction to this dramatic and awe-inspiring Kecak Fire Dance:

A visit to Bali isn't complete without witnessing some of its most captivating traditional performances. Alongside the Barong and Janger dances, another dazzling show you absolutely shouldn't miss is the traditional Kecak Fire Dance, known in Chinese as "卡恰火舞". This is one of Bali's artistic masterpieces, presented in the form of dance and musical theater.

The most famous Kecak Fire Dance in Bali takes place at the open-air theater on the cliffs overlooking the sea in Uluwatu, performed at sunset. The performance relies entirely on natural daylight. Starting at dusk, the story continues until nightfall, illuminated only by flickering bamboo torches.

What makes this dance particularly unique is that it uses no artificial backdrop and involves no musical instruments. The focus is entirely on a concentric circle of about 50-60 men, shirtless and wearing only traditional Balinese sarongs, sitting cross-legged around a set of torches in the center.

Unlike other traditional Balinese performances that are typically accompanied by a traditional "gamelan" orchestra, the Kecak Fire Dance features only a male choir representing the monkey army, continuously chanting "Cak! Cak! Cak!" or "Keh-Chak" in polyrhythmic tones throughout most of the performance. This astonishing vocal orchestra is led by a soloist who directs the high and low pitches while also serving as the narrator. Over time, this creates a dramatic wall of sound against which the performance unfolds.

## The Storyline

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_f2406be4ab4d6ea6026d450f8c08440e.jpg)

The Kecak Fire Dance performance at Uluwatu is a traditional Balinese dance drama that showcases the island's unique cultural heritage. The story is primarily derived from the Indian epic, the Ramayana. The performance focuses on the tale of Prince Rama's heroic rescue of his wife, Sita.

Here's a brief summary of the story: Prince Rama lives happily with his beautiful wife, Sita, until one day she is kidnapped by the evil demon king Ravana. To rescue his wife, Prince Rama enlists the help of the monkey god Hanuman and his army of monkeys. Amidst flames and dance, Rama ultimately defeats Ravana, saves Sita, and brings her safely home.

During the performance, actors dressed in traditional costumes chant "chak-chak-chak" as a backdrop, incorporating fire elements to create a mystical atmosphere. This fire dance not only tells the legendary tale of heroism but also allows the audience to experience the unique cultural charm of Bali.

## The Origins of Kecak Fire Dance

![raw-image](https://images.gobaligo.id/vocus/vocus_a406dcf7d7e0aeedce7bcc6494100b63.jpg)

The Kecak Fire Dance is said to have originated from an ancient Balinese ritual called Sanghyang, which was a form of exorcism or spirit expulsion where dancers entered a trance. The dance first appeared in 1930 when Balinese dancer Wayan Limbak collaborated with German painter Walter Spies to incorporate the legendary story of the Ramayana into a dramatic version of the Sanghyang performance. They took this innovative creation on a world tour, which popularized the dance.

The Kecak Fire Dance is regularly performed in many locations across Bali. However, the best place to witness this spectacular performance is Uluwatu, where two dance shows are held daily against the backdrop of a stunning sunset.
